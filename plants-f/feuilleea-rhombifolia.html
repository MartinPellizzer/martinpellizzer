Feuilleea rhombifolia

Feuilleea rhombifolia, commonly known as the diamond-leaf plant, is a unique species characterized by its rhomboid-shaped leaves that are glossy and slightly waxy.

Native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant's foliage is often a deep green with subtle variegation, making it a striking addition to indoor gardens. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, round fruits.

Feuilleea rhombifolia is relatively low-maintenance, requiring moderate watering and well-draining soil to flourish.
