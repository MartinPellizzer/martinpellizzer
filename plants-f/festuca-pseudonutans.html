Festuca pseudonutans

Festuca pseudonutans, commonly known as the slender fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in early summer. Festuca pseudonutans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its fine texture and resilience.

Its botanical features make it an important component of high-altitude ecosystems.
