Ficus lucbanensis

Ficus lucbanensis, commonly known as the Lucban fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the Philippines and is typically found in lowland to montane rainforests. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, clustered figs that are edible. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
