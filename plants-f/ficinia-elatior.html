Ficinia elatior

Ficinia elatior, commonly known as the slender swamp grass, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in Australia.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, showcasing its adaptability to aquatic environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. Ficinia elatior plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and supporting local biodiversity.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of riparian and swamp ecosystems.
