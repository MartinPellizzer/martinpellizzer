Flotovia paniculata

Flotovia paniculata, commonly known as the floating paniculate, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its buoyant, branching structure that allows it to float on the surface of freshwater bodies.

It features small, white flowers arranged in panicles, which bloom during the warmer months. The plant has elongated, ribbon-like leaves that are adapted for water buoyancy and light absorption. It thrives in slow-moving or still waters with ample sunlight.

Flotovia paniculata plays a role in providing habitat for aquatic insects and small fish.
