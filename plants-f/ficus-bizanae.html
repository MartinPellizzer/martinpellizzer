Ficus bizanae

Ficus bizanae, commonly known as the Bornean ficus, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Borneo. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Ficus bizanae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
