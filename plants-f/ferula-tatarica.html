Ferula tatarica

Ferula tatarica, commonly known as Tatarian cow parsley, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia and parts of Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces a strong, aromatic resin that has been historically used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive, pungent odor.

Ferula tatarica thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in steppe and grassland habitats.
