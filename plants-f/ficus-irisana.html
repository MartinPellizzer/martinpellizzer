Ficus irisana

Ficus irisana, commonly known as the Irisana Fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves that can grow up to 30 inches in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
