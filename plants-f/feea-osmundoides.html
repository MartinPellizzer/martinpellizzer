Feea osmundoides

Feea osmundoides, commonly known as the water fern, is a unique aquatic plant belonging to the family Osmundaceae.

It is characterized by its feathery, finely divided fronds that resemble those of the common water fern, Osmunda. The plant thrives in shallow, freshwater environments, often found in ponds and wetlands. Its reproductive structures include sporangia that produce spores, typical of ferns.

Feea osmundoides plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and contributing to water filtration.
