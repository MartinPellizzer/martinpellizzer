Festuca scabra var. interrupta

Festuca scabra var. interrupta, commonly known as rough fescue, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit, with slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, spikelet-based flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and rocky areas.

Festuca scabra var. interrupta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and restoration projects.
