Festuca rigescens

Festuca rigescens, commonly known as rigid fescue, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, upright stems that are often covered with fine, grass-like leaves. Its small, spike-like inflorescence contains numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Festuca rigescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
