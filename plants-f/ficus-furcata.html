Ficus furcata

Ficus furcata, commonly known as the forked fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are typically green or yellow when mature. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Ficus furcata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
