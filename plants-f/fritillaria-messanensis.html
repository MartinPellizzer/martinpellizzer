Fritillaria messanensis

Fritillaria messanensis, commonly known as the Messina fritillary, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe, particularly Sicily.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically orange or red with contrasting black veins, making it visually distinctive. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and has a short stem that supports its showy blooms. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

Fritillaria messanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
