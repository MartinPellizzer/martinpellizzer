Festuca ovina f. aculeolata

Festuca ovina f. aculeolata, commonly known as the hairy sheep's fescue, is a variant of the sheep's fescue species.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant features dense, compact foliage with short, stiff leaves that often have a hairy texture. It is native to temperate regions of Europe and is adapted to well-drained soils.

This grass is often used in landscaping and as a ground cover due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
