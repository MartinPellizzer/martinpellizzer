Festuca stricta var. multinervis

Festuca stricta var. multinervis, commonly known as slender fescue, is a grass species characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along streambanks. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its compact, upright stems and dense foliage, which contribute to its ornamental value. Its leaves are usually bluish-green in color and have a prominent central vein, giving it a unique visual texture.

Festuca stricta var. multinervis is valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of conditions and its aesthetic appeal in landscaping.
