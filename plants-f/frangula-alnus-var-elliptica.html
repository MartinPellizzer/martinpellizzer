Frangula alnus var. elliptica

Frangula alnus var. elliptica, commonly known as the European buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, elliptical leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by dark blue berries that are rich in anthocyanins. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
