Faba vulgaris var. minuta

Faba vulgaris var. minuta, commonly known as the dwarf broad bean, is a compact variety of the broad bean plant.

It is characterized by its smaller, rounder pods and shorter stature compared to standard broad beans. The plant produces edible seeds that are often used in cooking, similar to other members of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in temperate climates and is typically grown as an annual crop.

Its botanical features make it suitable for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
