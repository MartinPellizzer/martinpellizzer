Ficus pongumphaii

Ficus pongumphaii, commonly known as the Thai ficus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically heart-shaped. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible figs, which are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Ficus pongumphaii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to indoor conditions.
