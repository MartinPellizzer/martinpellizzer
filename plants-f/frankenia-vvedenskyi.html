Frankenia vvedenskyi

Frankenia vvedenskyi, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low moisture. It plays a role in stabilizing dunes and preventing soil erosion in its native habitat.

Frankenia vvedenskyi is also valued for its ornamental appeal in xeriscaped gardens.
