Fritillaria kamtschatcensis

Fritillaria kamtschatcensis, commonly known as the Kamchatka fritillary, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the coastal regions of the Russian Far East.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in forested or alpine environments. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually orange or reddish in color, with a distinctive waxy coating. Its bulbs are thick and fleshy, serving as storage organs for nutrients.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to gardens in other parts of the world.
