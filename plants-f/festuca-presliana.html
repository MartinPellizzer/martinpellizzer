Festuca presliana

Festuca presliana, commonly known as Presli's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Festuca presliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and aesthetic qualities.
