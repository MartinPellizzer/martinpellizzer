Flotovia diacanthoides

Flotovia diacanthoides, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a mist-like appearance. The plant has slender, branching stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid rainforests.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
