Ficus xanthosyce

Ficus xanthosyce, commonly known as the golden fig or yellow-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is characterized by its glossy, waxy leaves that often display a striking yellow or gold hue, especially under certain light conditions. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into round, smooth-skinned fruits. Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in humid, tropical environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
