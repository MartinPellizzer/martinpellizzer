Ferolia montana

Ferolia montana, commonly known as the mountain ferolia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It typically grows in rocky and well-drained soils, often at high altitudes. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, citrus-like fruits. Its leaves are thick and leathery, adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Ferolia montana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local herbal remedies.
