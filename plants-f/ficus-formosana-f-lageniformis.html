Ficus formosana f. lageniformis

Ficus formosana f. lageniformis, commonly known as the bottle-shaped fig, is a unique cultivar of the ficus genus characterized by its distinctive, swollen, bottle-like stem.

It is native to Taiwan and is prized for its ornamental value due to its unusual shape and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant is a member of the Moraceae family and typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires indirect sunlight, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its foliage remains evergreen, providing year-round visual interest.
