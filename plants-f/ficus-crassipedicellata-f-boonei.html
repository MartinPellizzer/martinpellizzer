Ficus crassipedicellata f. boonei

Ficus crassipedicellata f. boonei, commonly known as the Boone's fig, is a rare and lesser-known cultivar of the ficus genus.

It is characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves that are typically a deep green in color. The plant is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid environments. It produces small, clustered fig fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is a sought-after plant among horticulturists and collectors.
