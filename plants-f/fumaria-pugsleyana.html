Fumaria pugsleyana

Fumaria pugsleyana, commonly known as the yellow crane's-bill, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its delicate, nodding yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or woodland settings.

Fumaria pugsleyana is also known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used to treat ailments such as coughs and skin conditions.
