Ficus kallicarpa

Ficus kallicarpa, commonly known as the Indian rubber fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves that are opposite and ovate in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible, yellowish fruits that are often consumed by local wildlife. It is valued for its ability to thrive in humid environments and its use in traditional medicine.

The plant's latex sap has also been historically used for rubber production.
