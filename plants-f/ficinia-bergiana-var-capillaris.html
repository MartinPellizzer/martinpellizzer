Ficinia bergiana var. capillaris

Ficinia bergiana var. capillaris, commonly known as the slender spike rush, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical inflorescence composed of numerous small, spike-like clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, thriving in conditions with high humidity and adequate sunlight. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often growing in dense stands that help stabilize the surrounding soil.

This species plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
