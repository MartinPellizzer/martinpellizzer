Fragaria sylvestris var. multiplex

Fragaria sylvestris var. multiplex, commonly known as the wild strawberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, aggregate fruits composed of multiple achenes. The plant typically grows in woodland edges, meadows, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its flowers are white with five petals, and it produces a fragrant, edible fruit that is often used in culinary applications.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
