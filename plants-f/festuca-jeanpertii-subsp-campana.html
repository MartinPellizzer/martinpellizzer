Festuca jeanpertii subsp. campana

Festuca jeanpertii subsp.

campana, commonly known as the Campana fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, often blue-green leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous narrow, grass-like flowers. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in alpine gardens and conservation areas.
