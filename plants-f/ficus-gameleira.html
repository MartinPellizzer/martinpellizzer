Ficus gameleira

Ficus gameleira, commonly known as the Brazilian rubber fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically heart-shaped. The plant produces small, greenish flowers followed by fig-like fruits that are edible for certain animals but not for humans. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine.

The tree is also notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resilience to urban environments.
