Ficus pringsheimiana

Ficus pringsheimiana, commonly known as the Pringsheim's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Africa, particularly Tanzania and Kenya. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It produces small, non-showy flowers followed by edible fig fruits.

Ficus pringsheimiana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
