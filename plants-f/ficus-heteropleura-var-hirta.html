Ficus heteropleura var. hirta

Ficus heteropleura var. hirta, commonly known as the hairy-leaf fig, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered with fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically green when mature. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.

Its adaptability and ornamental value make it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor settings.
