Fleischmannia Trinervia

Fleischmannia evening primrose, scientifically known as Fleischmannia trinervia, is a striking member of the Onagraceae family native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States. This plant is renowned for its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom at dusk, making it a unique addition to desert landscapes. With its delicate, lance-shaped leaves and slender stems, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to attract nocturnal pollinators like moths. Despite its beauty, it requires minimal water and is well-suited for xeriscaping in drought-prone areas.
