Fosterella fuentesii

Fosterella fuentesii, commonly known as the Mexican pineapple cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, columnar shape and bright green stems. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by small, edible fruits resembling tiny pineapples. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
