Festuca stricta var. polita

Festuca stricta var. polita, commonly known as the silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, dense foliage and a bluish-green hue, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and prairies. Its slender, upright stems and small, spike-like inflorescences are typical of the Festuca genus.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
