Fadogia flaviflora var. calvescens

Fadogia flaviflora var. calvescens, commonly known as the African blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the morning. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.
