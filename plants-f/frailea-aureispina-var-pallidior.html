Frailea aureispina var. pallidior

Frailea aureispina var. pallidior, commonly known as the pale-spined chamaedorea, is a compact, slow-growing cactus native to southern Africa.

It features a short, cylindrical stem with a dense covering of white, woolly hairs that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale green to yellow flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, and it thrives in well-drained soil with bright, indirect light.

It is a popular choice for indoor cultivation due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
