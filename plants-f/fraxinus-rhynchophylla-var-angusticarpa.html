Fraxinus rhynchophylla var. angusticarpa

Fraxinus rhynchophylla var. angusticarpa, commonly known as the narrow-fruit ash, is a variant of the ash tree native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated fruits that are typically clustered in catkins. The leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a serrated margin. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The plant has a moderate growth rate and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
