Festuca quartiniana

Festuca quartiniana, commonly known as Quartin's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Festuca quartiniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
