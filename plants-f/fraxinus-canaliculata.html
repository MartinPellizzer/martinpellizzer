Fraxinus canaliculata

Fraxinus canaliculata, commonly known as the Chinese ash, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, simple leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in panicles, followed by winged fruits called samaras. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in ecological systems.
