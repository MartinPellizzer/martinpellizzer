Festuca bosniaca subsp. chlorantha

Festuca bosniaca subsp.

chlorantha, commonly known as the Bosnian bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Balkan region. It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and bluish-green foliage, which gives it its common name.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland and meadow habitats. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear pale blue to greenish flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
