Festuca grandiflora

Festuca grandiflora, commonly known as bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and delicate blue to purple flower spikes that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun. Festuca grandiflora is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.

It is also valued for its role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
