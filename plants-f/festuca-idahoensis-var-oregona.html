Festuca idahoensis var. oregona

Festuca idahoensis var. oregona, commonly known as Oregon fescue, is a perennial grass native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested or open areas. The plant features slender, tufted stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often blue-green in color. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
