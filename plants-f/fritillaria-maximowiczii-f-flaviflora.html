Fritillaria maximowiczii f. flaviflora

Fritillaria maximowiczii f. flaviflora, commonly known as the yellow-flowered fritillary, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, with a distinctive reticulated pattern on the petals. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, linear leaves that are typically grass-like in appearance. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
