Funastrum glaucum

Funastrum glaucum, commonly known as the blue-green funastrum, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the spring and early summer. Native to regions of Africa and the Middle East, it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and xeriscapes for its unique foliage and drought tolerance.
