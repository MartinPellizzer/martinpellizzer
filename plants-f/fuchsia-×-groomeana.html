Fuchsia × groomeana

Fuchsia × groomeana, commonly known as the hybrid fuchsia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Fuchsia magellanica and Fuchsia bicolor. This plant is characterized by its vibrant, pendulous flowers that range in color from red to pink and purple. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for container gardening.
