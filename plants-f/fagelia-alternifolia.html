Fagelia alternifolia

Fagelia alternifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its alternate, leathery, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and silvery white underneath, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by edible, orange-colored berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
