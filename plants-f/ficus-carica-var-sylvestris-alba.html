Ficus carica var. sylvestris-alba

Ficus carica var. sylvestris-alba, commonly known as the white fig, is a variant of the common fig tree characterized by its pale, almost white leaves.

This cultivar is notable for its ornamental value, featuring a unique foliage that stands out in gardens and landscapes. It produces small, edible fruits similar to the common fig, though they are often less abundant. The plant thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Its cultivation is popular for both aesthetic and culinary purposes.
