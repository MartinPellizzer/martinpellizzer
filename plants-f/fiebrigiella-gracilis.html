Fiebrigiella gracilis

Fiebrigiella gracilis, commonly known as the slender fiebrigiella, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. The plant produces delicate, white flowers with five petals, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in coastal areas.

Fiebrigiella gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens with well-draining soil.
