Ficus tinctoria

Ficus tinctoria, commonly known as the red-leaved fig or red fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red foliage, which becomes more vibrant as the plant matures. The leaves are typically ovate or elliptical, with a smooth texture and a glossy appearance. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine.

This species is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in indoor conditions.
