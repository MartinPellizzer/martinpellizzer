Fagara schinifolia var. grandifolia

Fagara schinifolia var. grandifolia, commonly known as the giant soursop, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the family Annonaceae and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences, followed by large, spiny fruits resembling a soursop. Its botanical features include a woody stem, palmate leaves, and a distinctive floral structure typical of the Annonaceae family.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
