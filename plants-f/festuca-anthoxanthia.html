Festuca anthoxanthia

Festuca anthoxanthia, commonly known as the yellow sweetgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with yellow or golden hues. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spike-like clusters of florets.

Festuca anthoxanthia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
