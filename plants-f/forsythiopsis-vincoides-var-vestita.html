Forsythiopsis vincoides var. vestita

Forsythiopsis vincoides var. vestita, commonly known as the white-flowered forsythia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Oleaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub with opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy on the underside. Native to China, it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for early spring floral displays.
