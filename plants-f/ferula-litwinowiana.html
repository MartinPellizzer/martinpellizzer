Ferula litwinowiana

Ferula litwinowiana, commonly known as the Litwinowian wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces a strong, aromatic resin that has been used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough, hairy texture.

Ferula litwinowiana is often found growing in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
