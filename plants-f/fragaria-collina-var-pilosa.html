Fragaria collina var. pilosa

Fragaria collina var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy mountain strawberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It produces small, pale pink to white flowers with five petals and numerous stamens. The plant's leaves are trifoliate, with hairy surfaces that give it its common name. Its fruits are small, yellowish berries that develop from the flowers.

This variety is adapted to rocky and alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
