Faba vulgaris var. viridissima

Faba vulgaris var. viridissima, commonly known as the green vetch, is a perennial legume characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive yellow flowers.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. The plant has a robust root system that helps in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil fertility. Its small, oval pods contain edible seeds that have been used in traditional cuisines.

The species is also valued for its role in ecological restoration and erosion control.
