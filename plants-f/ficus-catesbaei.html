Ficus catesbaei

Ficus catesbaei, commonly known as the Florida fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and the Gulf Coast. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered fruit that resembles a fig. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along waterways.

Ficus catesbaei plays an important ecological role by providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
