Fumana spachii var. calycina

Fumana spachii var. calycina, commonly known as the Calycine Fumana, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow disc florets surrounded by white to pale pink ray florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
