Fuirena stricta var. chlorocarpa

Fuirena stricta var. chlorocarpa, commonly known as the green-fruited broom.

It is a shrub native to southern Africa, characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and bright yellow flowers. The plant produces small, green berries that are toxic to humans and livestock. Its leaves are opposite and oval-shaped, with a leathery texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
