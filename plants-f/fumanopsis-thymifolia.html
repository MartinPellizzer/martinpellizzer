Fumanopsis thymifolia

Fumanopsis thymifolia, commonly known as the thyme-leaved fumarioid, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Fumariaceae.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia, where it thrives in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and somewhat obscure species within its family.
