Fuchsia macrostemma var. gracilis

Fuchsia macrostemma var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender fuchsia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, showy inflorescences that consist of numerous small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or vine, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. It prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The flowers are pollinated by birds, particularly hummingbirds, due to their bright colors and nectar-rich structure.
