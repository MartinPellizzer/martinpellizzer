Ferula lutea var. mouretii

Ferula lutea var. mouretii, commonly known as the yellow cow parsley, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces a strong, aromatic resin that has been used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, often growing in rocky or dry habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Apiaceae family.
