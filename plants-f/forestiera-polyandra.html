Forestiera polyandra

Forestiera polyandra, commonly known as the fragrant forestiera, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with finely toothed margins, and its bark is reddish-brown and smooth. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
