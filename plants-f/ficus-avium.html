Ficus avium

Ficus avium, commonly known as the common fig, is a deciduous tree native to the Middle East and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and unique syconium fruits. The fruit, known as the fig, is enclosed in a fleshy, hollow structure that develops from the flower's base. Ficus avium thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its fruit is not only edible but also plays an important role in both human and animal diets.
