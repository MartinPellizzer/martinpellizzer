Falcaria javanica

Falcaria javanica, commonly known as the Java arrowhead, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense colonies.

Falcaria javanica is also known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in folk remedies for various ailments.
