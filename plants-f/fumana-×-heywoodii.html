Fumana × heywoodii

Fumana × heywoodii, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a natural cross between two species of orchids, Fumana alba and Fumana rufa.

It is a herbaceous plant with delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant features long, slender stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by gardeners interested in rare and beautiful flowering plants.
