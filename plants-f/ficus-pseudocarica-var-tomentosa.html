Ficus pseudocarica var. tomentosa

Ficus pseudocarica var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly fig, is a tropical plant characterized by its dense, velvety foliage.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is native to parts of Southeast Asia. The leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and covered in a fine, fuzzy layer of hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, clustered fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines.

This plant thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental or for its fruit yield.
