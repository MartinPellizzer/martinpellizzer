Festuca × subsudetica

Festuca × subsudetica, commonly known as the hybrid fescue, is a perennial grass species that results from the cross between Festuca rubra and Festuca ovina.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and dense, dark green foliage. This plant typically thrives in cool, temperate climates and is often found in meadows, pastures, and rocky soils. Festuca × subsudetica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and turfgrass applications.

Its ability to tolerate a range of soil conditions makes it a versatile choice for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
