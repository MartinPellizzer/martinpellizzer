Filago arvensis var. lagopus

Filago arvensis var. lagopus, commonly known as the meadow filago, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its small, daisy-like yellow flowers and is often found in meadows and grasslands. The plant has a low-growing habit with slender, branching stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators.

This variety is adapted to disturbed soils and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
