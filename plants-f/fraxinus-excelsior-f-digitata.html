Fraxinus excelsior f. digitata

Fraxinus excelsior f. digitata, commonly known as the European ash with digitate leaves, is a botanical variant of the common ash.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves, which are divided into five to seven lobes, giving it a unique appearance. This form is typically found in moist, shaded environments, often in woodland areas. The plant is part of the Oleaceae family and is known for its ornamental value.

Its foliage provides habitat for various insects and contributes to biodiversity in its ecosystem.
