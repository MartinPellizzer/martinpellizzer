Frangula californica subsp. tomentella

Frangula californica subsp.

tomentella, commonly known as the woolly frangula, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, yellowish-green leaves.

The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for wildlife. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.

Its subspecific name, *tomentella*, refers to the woolly texture of its leaves.
