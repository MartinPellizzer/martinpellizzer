Ficus nervosa

Ficus nervosa, commonly known as the rough-leaved fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its rough, textured leaves and small, clustered fruits. The plant typically grows as a tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are usually elliptical with a serrated margin and a dark green upper surface.

Ficus nervosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
