Ficus platypoda var. typica

Ficus platypoda var. typica, commonly known as the broad-leaf fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation.
