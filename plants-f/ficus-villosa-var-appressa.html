Ficus villosa var. appressa

Ficus villosa var. appressa, commonly known as the woolly fig or appressa ficus, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage, which gives it a distinctive, soft-textured appearance. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with a waxy surface and prominent veins.

Ficus villosa var. appressa is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and adaptability to indoor conditions.
