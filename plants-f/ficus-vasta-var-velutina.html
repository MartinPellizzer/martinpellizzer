Ficus vasta var. velutina

Ficus vasta var. velutina, commonly known as the velvet fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig-like fruits that are typically yellow or reddish when ripe. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

The species is also valued for its ability to adapt to indoor conditions, making it a popular choice for houseplants.
