Ficus cocculifolia subsp. sakalavarum

Ficus cocculifolia subsp.

sakalavarum, commonly known as the Sakalavarum fig, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia. It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its simple, ovate leaves with a glossy surface.

The plant produces small, clustered fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or red when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
