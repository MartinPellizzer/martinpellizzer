Fischeria calycina

Fischeria calycina, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy yellow flowers with purple veins. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and it has a fibrous root system that helps it thrive in nutrient-rich soils.

Fischeria calycina is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking foliage and flowers.
