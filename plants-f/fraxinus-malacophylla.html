Fraxinus malacophylla

Fraxinus malacophylla, commonly known as the yellow fraxin, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, simple leaves that are typically yellow-green in color. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds known as samaras. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and bottomlands.

Fraxinus malacophylla is also valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in woodworking.
