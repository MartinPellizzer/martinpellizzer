Festuca argentinensis

Festuca argentinensis, commonly known as Argentine bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Festuca argentinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
