Freycinetia schefferi

Freycinetia schefferi, commonly known as the Scheffer's pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or white, followed by edible fruits that are similar to pineapples in appearance.

This species is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its role in local cultures.
