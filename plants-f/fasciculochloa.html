Fasciculochloa

Fasciculochloa, commonly known as the clustered grass, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family, Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit, with multiple stems arising from a single base. The plants typically have narrow, linear leaves and produce small, spikelet-based inflorescences. Fasciculochloa species are often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and ecological roles in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small organisms.
