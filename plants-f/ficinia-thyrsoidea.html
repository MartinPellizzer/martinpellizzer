Ficinia thyrsoidea

Ficinia thyrsoidea, commonly known as the slender swamp grass, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments in tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in dense stands, often forming part of the vegetation in freshwater swamps and seasonally flooded areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, and it produces small, dry fruits that contain seeds.

Ficinia thyrsoidea plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
