Festuca anatolica

Festuca anatolica, commonly known as Anatolian fescue, is a perennial grass species native to regions of Turkey and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers, which are usually pale green to brown in hue. Festuca anatolica is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in high-altitude environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and erosion control in suitable climates.
