Ficus corymbifera

Ficus corymbifera, commonly known as the white-fig or Philippine fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible figs that are typically green to yellow in color. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in many regions for both horticultural and agricultural purposes.
