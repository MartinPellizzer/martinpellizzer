Filago heterantha subsp. cupaniana

Filago heterantha subsp.

cupaniana, commonly known as the mountain filago, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly in the Andean regions. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies.

The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, with a hairy texture, while its flowers are small and yellowish in color. Filago heterantha subsp.

cupaniana is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
