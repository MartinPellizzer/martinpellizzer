Festuca giraldoi

Festuca giraldoi, commonly known as Giraldo's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and fine, wiry leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
