Festuca chayuensis

Festuca chayuensis, commonly known as Chayu grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or alpine habitats. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to cold climates and can withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Botanically, it is classified within the Poaceae family, which includes many other grasses.
