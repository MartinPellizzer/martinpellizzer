Feuilleea incurialis

Feuilleea incurialis, commonly known as the curled leaf fern, is a unique plant characterized by its distinctive foliage.

Its leaves are intricately curled and spiraled, forming a compact, rosette-like structure that helps conserve moisture. The plant produces small, delicate spores rather than seeds, typical of ferns. It thrives in shaded, humid environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and botanical study.
