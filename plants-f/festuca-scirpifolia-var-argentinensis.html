Festuca scirpifolia var. argentinensis

Festuca scirpifolia var. argentinensis, commonly known as the Argentine marsh grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland conservation efforts.
