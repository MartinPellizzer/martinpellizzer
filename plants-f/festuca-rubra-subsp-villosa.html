Festuca rubra subsp. villosa

Festuca rubra subsp.

villosa, commonly known as hairy red fescue, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and hairy leaves that give it its subspecific name.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are usually a bluish-green color and can reach heights of up to 30 cm. Festuca rubra subsp.

villosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
