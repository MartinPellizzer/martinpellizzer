Ficus dewolfii

Ficus dewolfii, commonly known as the Mexican rubber fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically heart-shaped. The plant produces small, fig-like fruits that are edible but often not consumed by humans. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability.

Ficus dewolfii is also valued for its ability to purify indoor air and its aesthetic appeal.
