Filipendula multijuga var. albiflora

Filipendula multijuga var. albiflora, commonly known as white-flowered meadowsweet, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features tall, slender stems covered in soft, hairy foliage that turns reddish-brown in autumn. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a serrated edge.

It thrives in moist, sunny to partially shaded habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks.
