Fragaria recurva

Fragaria recurva, commonly known as the bent-leaf strawberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It produces small, edible berries that are similar in taste to cultivated strawberries. The plant has trifoliate leaves with a distinctive curved shape, and its stems are often prostrate or trailing. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The flowers are white with five petals and are followed by the characteristic red, fleshy fruits.
