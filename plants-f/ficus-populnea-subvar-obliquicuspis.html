Ficus populnea subvar. obliquicuspis

Ficus populnea subvar. obliquicuspis, commonly known as the narrow-lobed fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Thailand and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with serrated margins and a dense canopy. It produces small, clustered figs that are typically green when mature.

This subvariety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
