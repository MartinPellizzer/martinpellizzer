Ferocactus hamatacanthus var. crassispinus

Ferocactus hamatacanthus var. crassispinus, commonly known as the thick-spined hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in thick, sharp spines, which provide protection from herbivores. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. It is well adapted to dry environments, storing water in its fleshy stems.

This variety is often cultivated for its unique appearance and resilience in harsh conditions.
