Fragaria chiloensis scouleri

Fragaria chiloensis scouleri, commonly known as the Chilean strawberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and produces small, red berries that are typically harvested when fully ripe. The plant thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its edible fruits, which have a unique flavor compared to common strawberries.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
