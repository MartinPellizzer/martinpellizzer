Ferocactus

Ferocactus, commonly known as the "cactus of the desert," is a genus of cacti native to the arid regions of the Americas.

These plants are characterized by their spiny, cylindrical stems and often vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in shades of yellow, orange, and red. Ferocactus species are typically slow-growing and adapted to extreme drought conditions, storing water in their thick, fleshy stems. They thrive in well-draining soil and full sun, making them popular among desert gardeners.

Some species, like Ferocactus acanthodes, are prized for their ornamental value and resilience in harsh environments.
