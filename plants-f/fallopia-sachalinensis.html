Fallopia sachalinensis

Fallopia sachalinensis, commonly known as Japanese knotweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its hollow, bamboo-like stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a white, waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months. It is known for its rapid growth and invasive nature in non-native regions.

Botanically, it belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is often considered a problematic weed due to its resilience and ability to spread quickly.
