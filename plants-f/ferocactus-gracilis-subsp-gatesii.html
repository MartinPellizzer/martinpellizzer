Ferocactus gracilis subsp. gatesii

Ferocactus gracilis subsp.

gatesii, commonly known as the Gates' slender barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stem that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered at the apex of the stem. Its spines are typically short and white, providing protection from herbivores.

This subspecies is adapted to extreme drought conditions and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.
