Fagelia pulverulenta

Fagelia pulverulenta, commonly known as the powdered ash tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, inconspicuous white flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, black, berry-like fruits that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded, woodland environments.

Fagelia pulverulenta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
