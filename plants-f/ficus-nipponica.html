Ficus nipponica

Ficus nipponica, commonly known as the Japanese fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and smooth, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, clustered fruits that are typically green when mature. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant.

Ficus nipponica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to adapt to various indoor growing conditions.
