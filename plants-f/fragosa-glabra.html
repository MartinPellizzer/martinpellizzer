Fragosa glabra

Fragosa glabra, commonly known as the smooth fragosa, is a shrub native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and leaves that are typically oval in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that ripens to a reddish color, often containing several seeds.

Fragosa glabra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
