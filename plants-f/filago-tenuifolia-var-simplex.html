Filago tenuifolia var. simplex

Filago tenuifolia var. simplex, commonly known as the slender filago, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny, open habitats. Its slender stems and small, linear leaves are adapted to arid conditions, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens.
