Farquharia excelsa

Farquharia excelsa, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a remarkable plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Farquharia excelsa is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting diverse insect and bird life.
