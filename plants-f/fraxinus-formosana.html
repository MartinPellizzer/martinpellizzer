Fraxinus formosana

Fraxinus formosana, commonly known as the Formosan ash, is a deciduous tree native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed trunk and finely toothed leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by winged fruits that aid in seed dispersal. Its bark is smooth and grayish, with a distinct, flaky texture.

Fraxinus formosana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
