Furcaria boiviniana

Furcaria boiviniana, commonly known as the Boivin's furcaria, is a rare and unique succulent native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its distinctive, branched stem that splits into two parts, resembling a fork, which is a rare trait among succulents. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the arid conditions of its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and specific environmental requirements, Furcaria boiviniana is considered a vulnerable species.
