Festuca pseudodalmatica

Festuca pseudodalmatica, commonly known as the Dalmatian fescue, is a perennial grass native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in mountainous regions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.

Festuca pseudodalmatica is also known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in cooler climates.
