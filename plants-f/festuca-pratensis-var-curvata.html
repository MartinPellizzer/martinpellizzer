Festuca pratensis var. curvata

Festuca pratensis var. curvata, commonly known as curly bentgrass, is a variant of the common bentgrass species.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and distinctive curling leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and lawns. Its botanical features include narrow, elongated leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and turfgrass management.
