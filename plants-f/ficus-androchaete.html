Ficus androchaete

Ficus androchaete, commonly known as the Androchaete fig, is a species native to Brazil.

It is a small, evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves and small, clustered fruit. It belongs to the Moraceae family and is known for its adaptability to various soil types.

The fruit of Ficus androchaete is edible and often used in local cuisines.
