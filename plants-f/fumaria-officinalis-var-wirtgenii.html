Fumaria officinalis var. wirtgenii

Fumaria officinalis var. wirtgenii, commonly known as the Wirtgen's celandine, is a variant of the common celandine, characterized by its distinctive yellow-orange flowers and branched stems.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe, particularly Germany and Austria. The leaves are usually alternate, lanceolate, and covered in fine, hairy textures. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed areas.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties, containing alkaloids that have been traditionally used in herbal remedies.
