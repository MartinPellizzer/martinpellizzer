Ficus acreana

Ficus acreana, commonly known as the Trinidadian fig, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are typically green when mature. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation projects.
