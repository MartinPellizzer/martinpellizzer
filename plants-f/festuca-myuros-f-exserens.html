Festuca myuros f. exserens

Festuca myuros f. exserens, commonly known as silver hair grass, is a variant of the silvergrass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-tipped leaves that give it a distinctive appearance, especially in the sunlight. This grass thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides. Its growth habit is clump-forming, with slender stems that produce small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping for its visual appeal and low maintenance requirements.
