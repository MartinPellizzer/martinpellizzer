Festuca rupicola var. saxatilis

Festuca rupicola var. saxatilis, commonly known as the rock grass, is a perennial grass species native to rocky and alpine environments.

It typically grows in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, high-altitude conditions. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

Its hardy nature makes it a valuable plant for ecological restoration in rocky habitats.
