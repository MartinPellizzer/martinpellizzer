Fargesia maling

Fargesia maling, commonly known as the Maling bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to China.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant is known for its cold hardiness and ability to thrive in shaded environments, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping. Fargesia maling typically grows in clusters and can reach heights of up to 2 meters, with a dense, bushy appearance.

Its foliage remains green throughout the year, providing visual interest in both winter and summer.
