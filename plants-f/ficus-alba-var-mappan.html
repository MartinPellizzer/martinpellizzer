Ficus alba var. mappan

Ficus alba var. mappan, commonly known as the Mappan fig, is a variant of the common fig tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale green bark and large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by the development of edible fig fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
