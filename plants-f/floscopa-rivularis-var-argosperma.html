Floscopa rivularis var. argosperma

Floscopa rivularis var. argosperma, commonly known as the riverbank floscopa, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland and riparian habitats.

It typically grows in moist soils along streams, rivers, and other water bodies. The plant features slender, erect stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are clustered in loose inflorescences, and it blooms during the spring and early summer.

This variety is known for its adaptability to fluctuating water levels and its role in stabilizing riverbanks.
