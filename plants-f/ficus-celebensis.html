Ficus celebensis

Ficus celebensis, commonly known as the Celebes fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the Indonesian island of Sulawesi and is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and small, clustered fruits. The plant is evergreen and can grow as a shrub or small tree, often reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a smooth margin, and it produces edible figs that are used in local cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
