Flourensia navarri

Flourensia navarri, commonly known as Navarre's flourensia, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention.

The showy yellow to orange flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
