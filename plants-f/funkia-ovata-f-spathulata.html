Funkia ovata f. spathulata

Funkia ovata f. spathulata, commonly known as the broadleaf daylily, is a cultivar of the genus Funkia, characterized by its broad, spatulate leaves.

It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is native to East Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The foliage remains attractive throughout the growing season, making it a versatile addition to water gardens and pond edges.
