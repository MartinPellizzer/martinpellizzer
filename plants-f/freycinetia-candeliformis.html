Freycinetia candeliformis

Freycinetia candeliformis, commonly known as the candleplant, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical inflorescence that resembles a candle, hence its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a dense cluster at the top of the stem. It thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Freycinetia candeliformis is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated for landscaping purposes.
