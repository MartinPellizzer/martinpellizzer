Fragaria bucharica

Fragaria bucharica, commonly known as the Bukharan strawberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, edible berries. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its flowers are white with five petals, and it produces red, succulent fruits similar to true strawberries.

Due to its ornamental value and potential culinary uses, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
