Fimbriella psycodes var. grandiflora

Fimbriella psycodes var. grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora variant of the fimbriella psycodes, is a flowering plant characterized by its distinctive, fimbriated (fringed) petals.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas or along stream banks. The plant produces showy, often pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a velvety texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This variant is valued in horticulture for its unique floral display and adaptability to partial shade.
