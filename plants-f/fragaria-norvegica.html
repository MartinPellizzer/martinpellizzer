Fragaria norvegica

Fragaria norvegica, commonly known as the Norwegian strawberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern Europe.

It produces small, edible berries that are typically red in color and have a sweet, tangy flavor. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and grows in moist, temperate climates. It is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.

Its flowers are white with five petals and are followed by the characteristic fruiting bodies.
