Fleurya aestuans var. racemosa

Fleurya aestuans var. racemosa, commonly known as the coastal fleurya, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along the shoreline. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often growing in dense tufts.

This species is well adapted to salt spray and is often found in dune environments.
