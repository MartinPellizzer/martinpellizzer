Ferraria fimbriata

Ferraria fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed ferraria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky soils, often in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces showy inflorescences that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ferraria fimbriata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
