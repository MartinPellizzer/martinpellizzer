Flueggea virosa f. reticulata

Flueggea virosa f. reticulata, commonly known as the reticulate flueggea, is a variant of the flueggea virosa species.

It is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Sudan and Ethiopia. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, reticulated leaf patterns, which give it its common name. Its leaves are typically small, fleshy, and arranged in opposite pairs along the stems.

This plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, often exhibiting succulent features to store water.
