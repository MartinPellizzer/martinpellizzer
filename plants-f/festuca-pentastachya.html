Festuca pentastachya

Festuca pentastachya, commonly known as western wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and long, slender leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in a dense, cylindrical cluster, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is drought-tolerant, making it adaptable to different environments.

Festuca pentastachya is often used in restoration projects due to its ecological benefits and resilience.
