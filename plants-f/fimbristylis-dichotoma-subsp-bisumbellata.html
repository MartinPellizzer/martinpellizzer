Fimbristylis dichotoma subsp. bisumbellata

Fimbristylis dichotoma subsp.

bisumbellata, commonly known as the double-spike sedge, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Australia. It is a perennial herb with grass-like leaves and produces distinctive inflorescences consisting of two spikes of flowers.

The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes and swampy areas. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in late summer to early autumn.

The subspecies is characterized by its unique branching pattern and is often found in coastal or brackish environments.
