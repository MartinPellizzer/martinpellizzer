Ficinia contexta

Ficinia contexta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sedum, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright, branched stems covered in small, fleshy, blue-green leaves. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped, yellow flowers during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil.

Ficinia contexta is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its compact size and attractive foliage.
