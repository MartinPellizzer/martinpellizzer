Ficus scott-elliottii

Ficus scott-elliottii, commonly known as the Scott-Elliott fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves that are often clustered at the tips of the branches. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically yellow or red in color.

This species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
