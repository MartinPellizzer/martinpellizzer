Ficus angulata

Ficus angulata, commonly known as the angular fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, round fig fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

Ficus angulata is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
