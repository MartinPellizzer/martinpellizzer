Ficinia oligantha var. crinita

Ficinia oligantha var. crinita, commonly known as the hairy figs or crinita figs, is a unique member of the Moraceae family.

It is native to the coastal regions of southern Africa, particularly South Africa and Namibia. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, hairy, and often pendulous fruits that hang in clusters. Its leaves are typically alternate, simple, and have a waxy texture.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
