Ficus quercetorum

Ficus quercetorum, commonly known as the oakleaf fig, is a species of fig tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves that resemble oak leaves, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible fig fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Ficus quercetorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
