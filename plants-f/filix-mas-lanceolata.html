Filix-mas lanceolata

Filix-mas lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its long, lance-shaped fronds that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments, making it a common sight in humid forests. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor the plant while absorbing nutrients from the soil.

The fronds are often used in traditional medicine and as ornamental plants due to their attractive appearance.
