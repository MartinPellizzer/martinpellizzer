Fragaria grandiflora

Fragaria grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered strawberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are similar in appearance to common strawberries. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Fragaria grandiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruits.
