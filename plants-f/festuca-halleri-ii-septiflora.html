Festuca halleri-ii-septiflora

Festuca halleri-ii-septiflora, commonly known as the Haller's sedge, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and colonize open, barren areas.

Festuca halleri-ii-septiflora is valued for its resilience and is often used in ecological restoration projects in arid and semi-arid regions.
