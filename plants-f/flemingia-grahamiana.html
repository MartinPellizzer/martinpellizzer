Flemingia grahamiana

Flemingia grahamiana, commonly known as the Graham's flemingia, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a small, shrub-like plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale yellow to greenish in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Flemingia grahamiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and flowering habit.
