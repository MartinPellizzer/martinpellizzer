Ficus diversifolia var. latifolia

Ficus diversifolia var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are typically glossy and dark green, with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible figs, which are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
