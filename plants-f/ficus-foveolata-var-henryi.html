Ficus foveolata var. henryi

Ficus foveolata var. henryi, commonly known as the Henry's fig, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand and Cambodia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with smooth, grayish bark and heart-shaped leaves that are glossy on top and lighter in color underneath. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are typically green when immature and turn yellow or red when ripe. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its leaves are used in traditional medicine and it is also cultivated as an ornamental plant.
