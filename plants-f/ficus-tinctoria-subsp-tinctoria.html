Ficus tinctoria subsp. tinctoria

Ficus tinctoria subsp.

tinctoria, commonly known as the red-veined or red-leaf ficus, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family. It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines.

The plant is characterized by its distinctive reddish veins on its leaves, which give it a striking appearance. It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a glossy texture, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
