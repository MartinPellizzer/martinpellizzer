Fuchsia paniculata subsp. mixensis

Fuchsia paniculata subsp.

mixensis, commonly known as the Mixean fuchsia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Ecuador. It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from red to purple, often with a contrasting white or yellow throat.

The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, and features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in cloud forests or along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
