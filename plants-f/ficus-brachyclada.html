Ficus brachyclada

Ficus brachyclada, commonly known as the small-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions of India, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its small, ovate leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are often used in traditional medicine.

Ficus brachyclada is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.
