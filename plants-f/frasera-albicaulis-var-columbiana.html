Frasera albicaulis var. columbiana

Frasera albicaulis var. columbiana, commonly known as the Columbia lily, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators and its aesthetic value in native plant gardens.
