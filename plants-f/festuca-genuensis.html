Festuca genuensis

Festuca genuensis, commonly known as the Genuese fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Festuca genuensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in marginal habitats.
