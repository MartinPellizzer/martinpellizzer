Ficus carica var. saracenica

Ficus carica var. saracenica, commonly known as the Smyrna fig, is a cultivar of the common fig tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, elongated leaves and produces small, edible fruits that are typically harvested when they are still green. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in gardens for its aesthetic qualities. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is commonly used in cooking and can be dried for long-term storage.
