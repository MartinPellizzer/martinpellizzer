Festuca varia subsp. woronowii

Festuca varia subsp.

woronowii, commonly known as the Woronow's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to eastern Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features slender, tufted stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, each bearing small, two-lobed flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partially shaded conditions.
