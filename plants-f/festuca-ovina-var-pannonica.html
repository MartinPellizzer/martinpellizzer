Festuca ovina var. pannonica

Festuca ovina var. pannonica, commonly known as the Pannonian sheep's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. This variety thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along riverbanks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, typical of the Poaceae family.

Festuca ovina var. pannonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
