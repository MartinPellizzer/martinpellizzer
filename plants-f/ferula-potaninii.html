Ferula potaninii

Ferula potaninii, commonly known as the Potanin's giant fennel, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots, parsley, and dill, and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and feathery leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in umbels, typical of the family, and its hollow, aromatic stems are often used in traditional medicine.

Ferula potaninii is also valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects.
