Ferocactus hamatacanthus subsp. sinuatus

Ferocactus hamatacanthus subsp.

sinuatus, commonly known as the Sinuatus barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green to blue-green in color.

The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are clustered at the tips of the stems. Its thick, fleshy stems are adapted for water storage, allowing it to survive in harsh desert conditions.

The spines are often arranged in a distinctive spiral pattern, providing both protection and structural support.
