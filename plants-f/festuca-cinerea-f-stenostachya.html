Festuca cinerea f. stenostachya

Festuca cinerea f. stenostachya, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed bluegrass, is a variant of the silver bluegrass species.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and fine, bluish-green foliage. The plant produces narrow, spike-like inflorescences with small, pale blue to whitish flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

This form is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its delicate appearance.
