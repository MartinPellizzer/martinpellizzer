Flemingia rhodocarpa

Flemingia rhodocarpa, commonly known as the red-wood or red-leaf tree, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its distinctive red bark and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by bean-like pods that contain edible seeds. Flemingia rhodocarpa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its growth habit includes both deciduous and evergreen forms depending on the climate.
