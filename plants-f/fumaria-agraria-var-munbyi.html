Fumaria agraria var. munbyi

Fumaria agraria var. munbyi, commonly known as the yellow weed or lesser celandine, is a flowering plant in the poppy family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellow, lantern-shaped flowers. This variety is native to Europe and is often found in moist, disturbed soils such as roadsides and gardens. It has a short growing season, blooming in early spring before its leaves wither.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
