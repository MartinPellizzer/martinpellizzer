Ficus recurvata

Ficus recurvata, commonly known as the weeping fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that give it a cascading appearance. The leaves are glossy, ovate, and often have a waxy texture. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

This plant is popular as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
