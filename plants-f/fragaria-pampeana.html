Fragaria pampeana

Fragaria pampeana, commonly known as the Argentine strawberry, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Argentina, Bolivia, and Uruguay. The plant produces small, red, edible berries that are similar in appearance to the common strawberry but have a distinct flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and open areas.

The botanical features of Fragaria pampeana include trifoliate leaves and a perennial growth habit.
