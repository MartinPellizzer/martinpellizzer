Franseria strigulosa

Franseria strigulosa, commonly known as the rough franseria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often found in coastal and mountainous regions. The plant features small, fragrant white or yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, giving them a rough texture.

Franseria strigulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
