Ficus uncinata var. parva

Ficus uncinata var. parva, commonly known as the small-leaf fig, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, ovate leaves that are typically glossy and dark green, with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, green figs that mature to a reddish-purple color. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and prefers well-draining soil.

Its unique foliage and adaptability make it a popular choice for indoor gardening and ornamental purposes.
