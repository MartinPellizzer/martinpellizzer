Ficus filicauda var. filicauda

Ficus filicauda var. filicauda, commonly known as the weeping fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its cascading, drooping leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant has a smooth, dark green, glossy foliage with a waxy texture. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by fig-like fruits that are typically not visible due to the dense foliage.

This plant is often cultivated as a houseplant or in outdoor gardens due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.
