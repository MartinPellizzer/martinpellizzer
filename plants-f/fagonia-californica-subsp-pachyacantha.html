Fagonia californica subsp. pachyacantha

Fagonia californica subsp.

pachyacantha, commonly known as the thick-stemmed fagonia, is a shrub native to California and Baja California. It is characterized by its woody stems and small, yellowish-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by clusters of small, woody fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in coastal sage scrub and chaparral habitats.

This subspecies is notable for its drought tolerance and ability to withstand harsh coastal conditions.
