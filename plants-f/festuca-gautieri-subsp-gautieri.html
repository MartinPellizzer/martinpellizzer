Festuca gautieri subsp. gautieri

Festuca gautieri subsp.

gautieri, commonly known as Gautier's fescue, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky, arid, or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It is characterized by its dense growth habit and ability to form extensive mats, making it a valuable species in ecological restoration.

Due to its ornamental appeal and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscaping projects.
