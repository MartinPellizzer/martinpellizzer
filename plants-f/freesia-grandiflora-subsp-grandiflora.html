Freesia grandiflora subsp. grandiflora

Freesia grandiflora subsp.

grandiflora, commonly known as the common freesia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, upright stems and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers.

The flowers typically come in shades of white, yellow, orange, and pink, and are often used in floral arrangements. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and container planting.

The plant is also noted for its ease of cultivation and long bloom period, typically from spring to early summer.
