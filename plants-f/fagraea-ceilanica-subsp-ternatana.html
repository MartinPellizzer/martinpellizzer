Fagraea ceilanica subsp. ternatana

Fagraea ceilanica subsp.

ternatana, commonly known as the Ternate starwood, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its dense, glossy green foliage and produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers.

The plant is typically found in lowland rainforests and is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive appearance. It has a woody stem with a smooth, pale bark and can grow up to several meters in height.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different leaf morphology compared to the main species Fagraea ceilanica.
