Ficus arboricida

Ficus arboricida, commonly known as the Japanese ficus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by round, greenish fruits that turn red when mature. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Ficus arboricida is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor conditions.
