Fridericia florida

Fridericia florida, commonly known as the tropical beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy green surface. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a small, bright blue berry that develops after the flowers, often containing several seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
