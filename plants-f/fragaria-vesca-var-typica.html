Fragaria vesca var. typica

Fragaria vesca var. typica, commonly known as the wild strawberry, is a perennial flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, fragrant flowers that develop into edible, red berries. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and open fields, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade. Its flowers are white with five petals and are pollinated primarily by bees.

The berries, though small, are prized for their intense flavor and are often used in culinary applications.
