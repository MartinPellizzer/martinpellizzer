Ficus venezuelensis

Ficus venezuelensis, commonly known as the Venezuelan fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to South America, specifically Venezuela, and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig-like fruits. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
