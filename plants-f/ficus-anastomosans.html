Ficus anastomosans

Ficus anastomosans, commonly known as the giant ficus or Indian rubber plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly India and Sri Lanka, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. Its roots form a dense, interconnected network, which contributes to its resilience and ability to absorb nutrients efficiently.

Ficus anastomosans is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.
