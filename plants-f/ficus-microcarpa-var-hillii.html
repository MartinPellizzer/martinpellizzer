Ficus microcarpa var. hillii

Ficus microcarpa var. hillii, commonly known as the hillii fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that ripen to a yellowish or reddish hue. It thrives in humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

The tree is also valued for its ability to tolerate a range of soil conditions and partial shade.
