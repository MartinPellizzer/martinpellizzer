Fuirena squarrosa var. macrostachya

Fuirena squarrosa var. macrostachya, commonly known as the broad-stemmed fireweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often branch near the base and are covered in soft, woolly hairs. The leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense, showy inflorescences, typically pink to purple in color.

This plant thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in tundra and alpine environments.
