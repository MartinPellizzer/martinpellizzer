Fourcroya roezlii

Fourcroya roezlii, commonly known as the Mexican evergreen tree, is a species native to central Mexico.

It is a medium-sized tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry forest ecosystems.

Fourcroya roezlii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
