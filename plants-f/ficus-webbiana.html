Ficus webbiana

Ficus webbiana, commonly known as the Webbian fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in coastal regions. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers. Ficus webbiana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.

It is also valued for its role in local ecosystems, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
