Ficus petrotica

Ficus petrotica, commonly known as the Black Ficus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, bark and its ability to thrive in shaded environments. The plant has broad, waxy leaves that are typically dark green in color. It is a member of the Moraceae family, which includes other fig species.

Ficus petrotica is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.
