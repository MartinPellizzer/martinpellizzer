Ficus bombuscaroana

Ficus bombuscaroana, commonly known as the Bornean fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical rainforests of Borneo and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered fruits known as figs, which are an important food source for various wildlife species. Ficus bombuscaroana is typically found growing as an understory plant in dense forest canopies.

Its ecological importance lies in its role in supporting biodiversity and maintaining forest ecosystems.
