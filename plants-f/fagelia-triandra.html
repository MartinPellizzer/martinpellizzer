Fagelia triandra

Fagelia triandra, commonly known as the African velvet tamarind, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its ornamental value, featuring fragrant, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being ovate and slightly hairy.

The fruit is a legume pod that turns from green to brown as it matures, containing several seeds.
