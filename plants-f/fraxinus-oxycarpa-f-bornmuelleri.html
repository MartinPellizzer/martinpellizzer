Fraxinus oxycarpa f. bornmuelleri

Fraxinus oxycarpa f. bornmuelleri, commonly known as the Bornmueller's green ash, is a variant of the green ash tree native to parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green, with a smooth texture and slightly serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in drooping clusters during the spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, temperate climates.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
