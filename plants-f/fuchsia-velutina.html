Fuchsia velutina

Fuchsia velutina, commonly known as velvet fuchsia, is a striking shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves and clusters of showy, pendulous flowers that range in color from deep red to purple. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are edible and often consumed by birds and small mammals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its vibrant floral display.
