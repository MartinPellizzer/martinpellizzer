Festuca reverchonii

Festuca reverchonii, commonly known as the Reverchon's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, spikelet-based flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Festuca reverchonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and aesthetic qualities.
