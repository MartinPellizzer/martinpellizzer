Fragaria semperflorens

Fragaria semperflorens, commonly known as the everbearing strawberry, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the eastern United States and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, edible berries. The plant produces flowers throughout the growing season, leading to continuous fruiting in some climates. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three heart-shaped leaflets, and the plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Fragaria semperflorens is often cultivated as a ground cover or for its ornamental value and edible fruits.
