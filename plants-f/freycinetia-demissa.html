Freycinetia demissa

Freycinetia demissa, commonly known as the coastal pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of eastern Australia.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear yellowish-white flowers, which are often followed by orange or red berries. Freycinetia demissa is well adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils.
