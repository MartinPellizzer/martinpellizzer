Ficus cuspidatolongifolia

Ficus cuspidatolongifolia, commonly known as the long-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is characterized by its elongated, pointed leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, clustered fruits known as syconia, which are enclosed within a fleshy, spiky structure. It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and thrives in humid, warm climates.

The long-leaf fig is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
