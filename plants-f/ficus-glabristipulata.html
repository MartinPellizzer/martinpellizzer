Ficus glabristipulata

Ficus glabristipulata, commonly known as the glabrous-leaved fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, waxy leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Ficus glabristipulata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying properties.
