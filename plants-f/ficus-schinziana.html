Ficus schinziana

Ficus schinziana, commonly known as the Schinz's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Paraguay. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig-like fruits.

Ficus schinziana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
