Ficus cataupi

Ficus cataupi, commonly known as the Santa Catalina fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and produces small, edible figs that are often used in local cuisines. Ficus cataupi thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental and ecological landscaping.
