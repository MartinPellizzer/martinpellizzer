Fissistigma verrucosum

Fissistigma verrucosum, commonly known as the rough-stemmed rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub characterized by its woody stems covered in small, rough bumps or warts. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine, fuzzy hairs.

Fissistigma verrucosum is adapted to grow in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry slopes.
