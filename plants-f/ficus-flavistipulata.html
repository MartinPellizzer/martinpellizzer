Ficus flavistipulata

Ficus flavistipulata, commonly known as the yellow-stippled fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that feature distinctive yellow veins and margins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are typically green when mature. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ficus flavistipulata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
