Ficus dissocarpa

Ficus dissocarpa, commonly known as the two-fruit fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia, including parts of India and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and unique fig fruits that develop in pairs. Ficus dissocarpa typically grows as an evergreen tree or shrub, often reaching heights of several meters.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological studies.
