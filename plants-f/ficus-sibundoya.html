Ficus sibundoya

Ficus sibundoya, commonly known as the Sibundoy fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Colombia and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, edible fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous rainforests.

The Sibundoy fig is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
