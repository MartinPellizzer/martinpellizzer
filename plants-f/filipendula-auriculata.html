Filipendula auriculata

Filipendula auriculata, commonly known as the golden alexandria, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.

Filipendula auriculata has a short lifespan, usually living for only one to two years.
