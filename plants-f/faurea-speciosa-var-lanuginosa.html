Faurea speciosa var. lanuginosa

Faurea speciosa var. lanuginosa, commonly known as the woolly faurea, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of California and Baja California.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, fluffy appearance. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically leathery and have a silvery-gray underside, contributing to its unique aesthetic.

Faurea speciosa var. lanuginosa is adapted to coastal climates and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
