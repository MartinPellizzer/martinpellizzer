Ficus zollingeriana

Ficus zollingeriana, commonly known as the Zollinger's fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically heart-shaped with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are often covered in a white, powdery bloom. It thrives in humid environments and requires well-draining soil and indirect sunlight.

Ficus zollingeriana is popular as a houseplant due to its adaptability and attractive foliage.
