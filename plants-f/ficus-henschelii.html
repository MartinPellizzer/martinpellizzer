Ficus henschelii

Ficus henschelii, commonly known as the Henschel's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, specifically found in countries like Thailand, Vietnam, and Malaysia. This plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and have a waxy texture. It thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers indirect sunlight.

Ficus henschelii is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
