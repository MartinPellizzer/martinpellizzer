Ferolia guianensis

Ferolia guianensis, commonly known as the Guianan ferolia, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn orange when mature. Ferolia guianensis is often found in moist, shaded forest understories and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
