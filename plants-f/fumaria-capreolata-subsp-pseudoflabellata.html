Fumaria capreolata subsp. pseudoflabellata

Fumaria capreolata subsp.

pseudoflabellata, commonly known as the false flabellate fumaria, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and delicate, spurred flowers.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-yellow, with a distinctive spurred petal at the base.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different floral morphology compared to the typical F. capreolata.
