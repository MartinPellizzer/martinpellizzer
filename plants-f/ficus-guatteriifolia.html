Ficus guatteriifolia

Ficus guatteriifolia, commonly known as the Guatteria fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible fig-like fruits that are typically yellow or green when ripe. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Ficus guatteriifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
