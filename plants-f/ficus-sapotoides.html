Ficus sapotoides

Ficus sapotoides, commonly known as the Chinese fig or the tropical fig, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces small, greenish flowers followed by edible fig-like fruits that are typically harvested when ripe. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Ficus sapotoides is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
