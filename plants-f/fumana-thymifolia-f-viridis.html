Fumana thymifolia f. viridis

Fumana thymifolia f. viridis, commonly known as the green-leaved form of the thyme-leaved fumana, is a variant of the species characterized by its distinct foliage.

This plant is a member of the Apiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and distinctive umbellate inflorescences. The leaves are typically narrow, serrated, and have a fragrant, herbaceous scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

The flowers, which are usually white or pale yellow, bloom in late spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
