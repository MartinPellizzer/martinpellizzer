Festuca lugens

Festuca lugens, commonly known as purple mountain grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and its distinctive purple-tinged inflorescence. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Festuca lugens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
