Faba sativa

Faba sativa, commonly known as the field bean, is an annual legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces erect stems with trifoliate leaves and small, white or pink flowers. The plant is characterized by its edible, cylindrical pods containing several seeds. Faba sativa is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, improving soil fertility.

It is widely cultivated for its high protein content and use in various culinary applications.
