Fritillaria eduardii

Fritillaria eduardii, commonly known as the Edward's fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking bell-shaped flowers, which are typically deep orange or red in color, with a distinctive black or purple blotch at the base. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to cold climates.

Fritillaria eduardii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
