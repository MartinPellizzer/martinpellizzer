Ficus segaarensis

Ficus segaarensis, commonly known as the Segara Fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fig-like fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Ficus segaarensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant.
