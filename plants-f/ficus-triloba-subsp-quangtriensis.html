Ficus triloba subsp. quangtriensis

Ficus triloba subsp.

quangtriensis, commonly known as the Quang Tri fig, is a tropical plant native to Vietnam. It is characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves that are often hairy on the underside.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its unique morphology makes it a subject of interest in botanical research and horticulture.
