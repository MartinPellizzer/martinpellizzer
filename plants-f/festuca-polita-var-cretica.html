Festuca polita var. cretica

Festuca polita var. cretica, commonly known as Cretan bentgrass, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue to whitish flowers.

This variety is often used in ornamental gardening for its drought tolerance and aesthetic appeal.
