Festuca arundinacea f. baltica

Festuca arundinacea f. baltica, commonly known as Baltic tall fescue, is a cool-season grass species native to eastern Europe and parts of Russia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and dense foliage, often reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green in color, with a rough texture. It is well adapted to a variety of soil types and is commonly used in pastures and erosion control.

Its rhizomatous growth allows it to form extensive sods, making it a hardy and resilient plant.
