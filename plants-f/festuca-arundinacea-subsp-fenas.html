Festuca arundinacea subsp. fenas

Festuca arundinacea subsp.

fenas, commonly known as fescue, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit, narrow leaves, and the ability to form dense sods.

This subspecies is often used in forage production due to its high nutritional value and tolerance to various soil conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to both full sun and partial shade.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
