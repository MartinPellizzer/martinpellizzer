Festuca articulata

Festuca articulata, commonly known as the jointed fescue, is a perennial grass native to Eurasia.

It is characterized by its distinctive jointed stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Festuca articulata produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer, which are clustered in spikelets.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and restoration projects.
