Feuilleea hispida

Feuilleea hispida, commonly known as the rough leaf plant, is a species characterized by its distinctive, hairy foliage.

It typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to conserve water. The leaves are often thick and covered in fine, stiff hairs, which help reduce transpiration. This plant is known for its resilience and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both ecological and horticultural studies.
