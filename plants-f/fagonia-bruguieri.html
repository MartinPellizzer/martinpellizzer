Fagonia bruguieri

Fagonia bruguieri, commonly known as the desert tamarisk, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in dry soils.

Fagonia bruguieri is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
