Fabricia bupleurifolia

Fabricia bupleurifolia, commonly known as the Bupleurum leaf fabricia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and pod fruits. The plant typically features trifoliate leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
