Fraxinus oxyacanthifolia

Fraxinus oxyacanthifolia, commonly known as the sharp-leaf ash.

It is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Afghanistan and Pakistan. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, which give it its common name. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in clusters, while the fruits are samaras with winged seeds.

The tree is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
