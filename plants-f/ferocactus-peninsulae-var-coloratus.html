Ferocactus peninsulae var. coloratus

Ferocactus peninsulae var. coloratus, commonly known as the colorful peninsular cactus, is a striking species native to the arid regions of the Baja California Peninsula.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are typically green to blue-green in color, with spines that can range from yellow to reddish-brown. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months, adding to its ornamental appeal. It is well adapted to harsh desert conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

This variety is prized for its unique coloration and resilience, making it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and desert gardens.
