Fraxinus ornus subsp. argentea

Fraxinus ornus subsp.

argentea, commonly known as silver weeping ash, is a subspecies of the weeping ash tree native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its pendulous branches and clusters of yellowish-green flowers that appear in spring.

The leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a silvery-white underside that gives the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or along streambanks.

This ornamental tree is valued for its graceful appearance and ability to provide shade in garden settings.
