Ficus kallicarpa var. multinervia

Ficus kallicarpa var. multinervia, commonly known as the Indian rubber fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically broad and heart-shaped. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.

Its adaptability and ornamental value make it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor gardening.
