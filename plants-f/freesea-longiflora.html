Freesea longiflora

Freesea longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered freesea, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, elongated flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet, often with a distinctive white throat. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Freesea longiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in alpine gardens.
