Ficus paludicola

Ficus paludicola, commonly known as the swamp fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in swampy or wetland areas.

Ficus paludicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
