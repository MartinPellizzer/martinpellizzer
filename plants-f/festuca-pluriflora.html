Festuca pluriflora

Festuca pluriflora, commonly known as the blue grass or mountain bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and produces delicate blue to purple flower spikes in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in open woodlands and alpine meadows. Its leaves are narrow and bluish-green, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

Festuca pluriflora is also valued for its ecological role, providing habitat and food for various insects and small mammals.
