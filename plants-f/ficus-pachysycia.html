Ficus pachysycia

Ficus pachysycia, commonly known as the thick-leaf fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, broad, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs, though the fruit is often not cultivated for consumption. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often used as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Ficus pachysycia is also valued for its air-purifying properties and ability to thrive indoors with minimal care.
