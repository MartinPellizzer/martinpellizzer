Fagraea speciosa

Fagraea speciosa, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It features showy, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that provide dense foliage, and its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant's botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
