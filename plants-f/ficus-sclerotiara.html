Ficus sclerotiara

Ficus sclerotiara, commonly known as the Brazilian fig or the rock fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its woody stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig fruits. Ficus sclerotiara is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a versatile choice for both indoor and outdoor gardens.
