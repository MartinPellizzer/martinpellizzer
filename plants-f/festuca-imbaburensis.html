Festuca imbaburensis

Festuca imbaburensis, commonly known as the Imbabure fescue, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is drought-tolerant and adapted to temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of ecological study.
