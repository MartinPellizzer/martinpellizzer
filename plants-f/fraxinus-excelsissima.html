Fraxinus excelsissima

Fraxinus excelsissima, commonly known as the green ash, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, compound leaves that are typically 5 to 9 pairs of ovate to lanceolate leaflets. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins, which appear in early spring before the leaves emerge. Its fruits are samaras, each containing a single seed, and they are dispersed by wind.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
