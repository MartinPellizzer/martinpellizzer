Ficaria ranunculoides var. bulbifera

Ficaria ranunculoides var. bulbifera, commonly known as the bulbous buttercup, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its low-growing, herbaceous habit and features bright yellow flowers with five petals. The plant produces bulb-like structures called bulbils, which are used for vegetative propagation. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and moist woodland settings.
