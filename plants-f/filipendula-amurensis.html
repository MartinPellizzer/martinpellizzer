Filipendula amurensis

Filipendula amurensis, commonly known as the Amur meadow sweet, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters. It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

Filipendula amurensis is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil.
