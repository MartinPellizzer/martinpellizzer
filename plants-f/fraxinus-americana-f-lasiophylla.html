Fraxinus americana f. lasiophylla

Fraxinus americana f. lasiophylla, commonly known as the white ash.

This plant is a distinct form of the American ash, characterized by its unique foliage. The leaves are typically more densely hairy on the lower surface, giving them a distinctive silvery appearance. It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
