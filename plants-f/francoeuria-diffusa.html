Francoeuria diffusa

Francoeuria diffusa, commonly known as the yellow sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and yellowish-green foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, with thick, fleshy leaves that store water.

Francoeuria diffusa is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
