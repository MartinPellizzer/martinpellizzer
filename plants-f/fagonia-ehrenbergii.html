Fagonia ehrenbergii

Fagonia ehrenbergii, commonly known as the desert fagonia, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh desert conditions, with thick, waxy leaves that reduce water loss. It produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible and often consumed by wildlife.

Fagonia ehrenbergii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing food for desert animals.
