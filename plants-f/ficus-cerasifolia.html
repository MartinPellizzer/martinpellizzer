Ficus cerasifolia

Ficus cerasifolia, commonly known as the fig fig or the white fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, clustered flowers that develop into edible fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree can grow up to 15 meters tall and is known for its ability to adapt to various soil types.
