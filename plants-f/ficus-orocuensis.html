Ficus orocuensis

Ficus orocuensis, commonly known as the Orinoco fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly in Venezuela and Colombia. The plant is characterized by its large, broad leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Ficus orocuensis is often found in humid, lowland forests and can thrive in a variety of soil types.

Its fruit, a small fig-like structure, is an important food source for local wildlife.
