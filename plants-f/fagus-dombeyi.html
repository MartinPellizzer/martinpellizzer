Fagus dombeyi

Fagus dombeyi, commonly known as the Dombey beech, is a species of beech native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and its elliptical leaves that are usually doubly serrated. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, triangular nuts. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mixed forests.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
