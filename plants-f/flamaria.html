Flamaria

Flamaria, commonly known as the flame flower, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Acanthaceae.

It is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers that resemble flames, hence its common name. The plants typically have opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produce showy bracts that attract pollinators. Flamaria species are native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

They are often cultivated for their ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
