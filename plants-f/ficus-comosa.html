Ficus comosa

Ficus comosa, commonly known as the creeping fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, pear-shaped fruits that are often consumed by birds and mammals. It is widely cultivated as a ground cover or ornamental plant due to its ability to thrive in shaded environments.

Ficus comosa is also known for its adaptability to various soil types and its resistance to pests and diseases.
