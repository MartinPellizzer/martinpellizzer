Ficus henneana

Ficus henneana, commonly known as the African rubber fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by round, inedible fruits that turn yellow when mature. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in indoor environments.

Ficus henneana is also valued for its role in traditional medicine and its potential for rubber production.
