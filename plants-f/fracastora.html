Fracastora

Fracastora, commonly known as the "bitterroot," is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy stems and palmately divided leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with prominent stamens. Native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia, it thrives in alpine and woodland habitats.

Due to its bitter-tasting roots, it has been historically used in traditional medicine.
