Fritillaria lusitanica var. algeriensis

Fritillaria lusitanica var. algeriensis, commonly known as the Algerian fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to orange and yellow. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its bulbs are relatively small and produce a single stem with several flowers at the top.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
