Feuilleea lateriflora

Feuilleea lateriflora, commonly known as the lateral-flower leaf plant, is a unique species characterized by its distinctive foliage and floral arrangement.

It features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, giving it a graceful appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that emerge laterally from the leaf axils, a feature that sets it apart from many other botanical species. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a subtle fragrance that attracts pollinators.

Feuilleea lateriflora thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, woodland environments.
