Furcaria roxburghii

Furcaria roxburghii, commonly known as the African snowball, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of fleshy, cylindrical stems that form a rounded, ball-like structure. The plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers during the summer months, which are borne in tight, spherical clusters. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
