Ficus vrieseana f. appressipilosa

Ficus vrieseana f. appressipilosa, commonly known as the Variegated Ficus, is a cultivar of the Ficus genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated leaves that display a mix of green, white, and yellow hues. The plant has a robust, woody stem with a smooth, pale green bark. Its leaves are typically broad, heart-shaped, and have a glossy texture.

This cultivar is popular as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
