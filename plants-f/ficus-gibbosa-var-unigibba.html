Ficus gibbosa var. unigibba

Ficus gibbosa var. unigibba, commonly known as the single-lobed fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with a single prominent lobe, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible figs.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in indoor environments.
