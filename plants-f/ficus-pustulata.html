Ficus pustulata

Ficus pustulata, commonly known as the swollen fig or pustulate fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions of India, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, warty, and swollen leaves, which give it its common name. It produces small, greenish-yellow figs that are typically not edible for humans but are important for certain wildlife.

Ficus pustulata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage.
