Fagonia californica subsp. rosei

Fagonia californica subsp.

rosei, commonly known as the roseleaf fagonia, is a shrub native to coastal regions of California and Baja California. It is characterized by its gray-green foliage and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant has small, spiny leaves and produces fruit that turns from green to red as it matures. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with mild winters and warm, dry summers.

This subspecies is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.
