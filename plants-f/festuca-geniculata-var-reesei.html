Festuca geniculata var. reesei

Festuca geniculata var. reesei, commonly known as Reese's fescue, is a cool-season grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and tufted growth habit, making it visually appealing in ornamental landscapes. This variety is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun to partial shade, preferring well-drained soils. It is often used in erosion control and as a ground cover due to its dense root system.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for both residential and commercial landscaping.
