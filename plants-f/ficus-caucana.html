Ficus caucana

Ficus caucana, commonly known as the Caucasus fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the Caucasus region, including parts of Georgia, Russia, and Turkey. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. It produces small, clustered figs that are typically green when mature, and it is often found in forested areas.

Ficus caucana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
