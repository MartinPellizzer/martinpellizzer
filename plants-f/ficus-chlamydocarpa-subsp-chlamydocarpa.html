Ficus chlamydocarpa subsp. chlamydocarpa

Ficus chlamydocarpa subsp.

chlamydocarpa, commonly known as the white fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by round, greenish fruits that mature to a reddish hue. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental or for its edible fruits.

The subspecies is notable for its adaptability and ability to grow in a variety of soil types.
