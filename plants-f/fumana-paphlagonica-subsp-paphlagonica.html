Fumana paphlagonica subsp. paphlagonica

Fumana paphlagonica subsp.

paphlagonica, commonly known as the Paphlagonian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in well-drained environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy as well.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
