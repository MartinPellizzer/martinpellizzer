Filago aegaea subsp. aristata

Filago aegaea subsp.

aristata, commonly known as the mountain filago, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

The plant features a low-growing, mat-forming structure with linear, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in dense inflorescences.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
