Fimbristylis hispidula var. confertospicata

Fimbristylis hispidula var. confertospicata, commonly known as the dense-spiked flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to saturated soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers. Its inflorescence is usually cylindrical, with flowers arranged closely together.

It is often found in disturbed or semi-aquatic habitats and is characterized by its fibrous root system and rhizomes.
