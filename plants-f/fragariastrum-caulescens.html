Fragariastrum caulescens

Fragariastrum caulescens, commonly known as the slender strawberry plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, spreading habit with trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, berry-like fruits. Its botanical structure includes a hairy stem, serrated leaf margins, and a fibrous root system.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.
