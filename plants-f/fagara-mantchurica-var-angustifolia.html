Fagara mantchurica var. angustifolia

Fagara mantchurica var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fagara, is a species native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is a member of the Rutaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments within temperate forests. Its leaves are lanceolate and have a distinctive, slightly serrated margin.
