Fimbristylis annua var. gaimardioides

Fimbristylis annua var. gaimardioides, commonly known as the Gaimard's flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to parts of North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swales, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in disturbed or wetland habitats and is adapted to tolerate fluctuating water levels.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
