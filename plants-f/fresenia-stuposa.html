Fresenia stuposa

Fresenia stuposa, commonly known as the purple-flowered orchid, is a species native to South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features a basal rosette of dark green, fleshy leaves and produces striking, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive lip. Its inflorescence is a spike bearing several showy blooms, which attract various pollinators.

The botanical characteristics of Fresenia stuposa make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
