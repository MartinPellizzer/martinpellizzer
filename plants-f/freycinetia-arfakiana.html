Freycinetia arfakiana

Freycinetia arfakiana, commonly known as the Arfak mountain palm, is a species native to the island of New Guinea.

It is a tall, slender palm with a smooth, unbranched trunk and a crown of large, feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spadices, followed by elongated, fibrous fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts by the local communities.

Freycinetia arfakiana is adapted to grow in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland and montane forests.
