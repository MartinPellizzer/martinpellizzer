Ferraria cathartica

Ferraria cathartica, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllis family (Amaryllidaceae) and is characterized by its striking yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern at the base. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Ferraria cathartica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
