Ferula communis subsp. cardonae

Ferula communis subsp.

cardonae, commonly known as the Canary Island dandelion, is a perennial herb native to the Canary Islands. It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and large, yellow-orange flower heads that resemble dandelions.

The plant produces a distinctive, aromatic resin that has been used historically for various purposes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its roots are thick and fleshy.

This subspecies thrives in rocky, arid environments and is adapted to the unique climate of the Canary Islands.
