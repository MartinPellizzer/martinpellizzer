Frasera nitida var. cusickii

Frasera nitida var. cusickii, commonly known as the Cusick's frasera, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slight pubescence.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
