Festuca pseudovina f. rutila

Festuca pseudovina f. rutila, commonly known as the golden false brome, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and distinctive golden-green foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, two-lobed flowers. It is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
