Festuca duriuscula var. rivularis

Festuca duriuscula var. rivularis, commonly known as river fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. This variety thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found along riverbanks and in rocky habitats. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous pale green to brownish flowers.

Festuca duriuscula var. rivularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in challenging environments.
