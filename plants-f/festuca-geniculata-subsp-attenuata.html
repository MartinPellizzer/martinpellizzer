Festuca geniculata subsp. attenuata

Festuca geniculata subsp.

attenuata, commonly known as slender bentgrass, is a cool-season grass native to the western United States. It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its delicate appearance.
