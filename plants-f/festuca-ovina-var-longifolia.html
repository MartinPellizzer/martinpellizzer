Festuca ovina var. longifolia

Festuca ovina var. longifolia, commonly known as sheep's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a distinctive blue-green coloration.

It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, contributing to its ornamental value in landscaping.
