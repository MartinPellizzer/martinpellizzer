Fuchsia spinosa

Fuchsia spinosa, commonly known as the spiny fuchsia, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and clusters of bright red or pink flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Fuchsia spinosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
