Fedia dentata f. pinnatifida

Fedia dentata f. pinnatifida, commonly known as the narrow-leaved fedia, is a variant of the fedia plant characterized by its finely toothed, pinnatifid leaves.

This plant is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a distinctive, serrated margin that gives it its scientific name. The plant is often found in forest understories, where it contributes to the biodiversity of the ecosystem.

Due to its unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
