Festuca pseudovina f. compacta

Festuca pseudovina f. compacta, commonly known as the compact form of the slender fescue, is a low-growing, perennial grass species native to temperate regions.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. This variety is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit, making it suitable for ground cover. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight.

Botanically, it is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in landscaping applications.
