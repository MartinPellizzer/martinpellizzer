Felicia australis

Felicia australis, commonly known as the Australian bluebell, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flower heads. The plant produces small, white to yellow florets surrounded by a ring of blue or purple ray florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and coastal areas.

Felicia australis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
