Foetidia borbonica

Foetidia borbonica, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a flowering plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, fragrant flowers that bloom in a spiral pattern. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are broad and sword-shaped, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers with a distinctive scent.

Foetidia borbonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
