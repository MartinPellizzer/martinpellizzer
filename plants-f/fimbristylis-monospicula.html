Fimbristylis monospicula

Fimbristylis monospicula, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flat sedge, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. The plant grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and produces seeds that are dispersed by water or wind.

Fimbristylis monospicula is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
