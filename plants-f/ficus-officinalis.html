Ficus officinalis

Ficus officinalis, commonly known as the common fig tree, is a species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, featuring broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, green figs that develop into edible fruits after a period of ripening. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in various soil types.

The tree is also known for its ability to produce aerial roots, which can grow from its branches and anchor themselves to surfaces.
