Forestiera porulosa var. phillyreoides

Forestiera porulosa var. phillyreoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf forestiera, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family, which includes olive and jasmine plants. The plant features small, opposite, lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. Its fragrant, white flowers bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a versatile addition to native gardens.
