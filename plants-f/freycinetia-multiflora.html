Freycinetia multiflora

Freycinetia multiflora, commonly known as the coastal hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Pacific.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, often found in coastal and wetland habitats. Its leaves are usually alternate and have a waxy texture, while its stems are fibrous and robust.

Freycinetia multiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in saline environments.
