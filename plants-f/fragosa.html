Fragosa

Fragosa, commonly known as the fragosan or fragosa shrub, is a species native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the genus Fragosa within the family Myrtaceae, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruits are typically small, fleshy berries that contain seeds and are sometimes used in local traditional medicine.

Fragosa is also valued for its aromatic oils, which are extracted from its leaves and used in perfumery and flavoring.
