Fraxinus caroliniana var. oblaceolata

Fraxinus caroliniana var. oblaceolata, commonly known as the swamp sweetgum, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that are often lobed and have a distinctive waxy texture. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by spherical, spiky fruit capsules that split open to release seeds.
