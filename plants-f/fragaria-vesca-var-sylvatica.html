Fragaria vesca var. sylvatica

Fragaria vesca var. sylvatica, commonly known as the wild strawberry or woodland strawberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features trifoliate leaves with serrated margins and produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant bears edible, red, succulent berries that are typically smaller than those of the common strawberry. It thrives in woodland habitats and is often found in shaded, moist environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary use in various traditional dishes.
