Frodinia tremula

Frodinia tremula, commonly known as the trembling frodine, is a deciduous shrub native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, turning vibrant yellow in the autumn. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
