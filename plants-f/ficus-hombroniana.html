Ficus hombroniana

Ficus hombroniana, commonly known as the Hombron's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, clustered fruit. It thrives in humid, warm environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant.

Due to its adaptability, it is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
