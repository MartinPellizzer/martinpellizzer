Forbesia flexilis var. barberae

Forbesia flexilis var. barberae, commonly known as the Barbera forbesia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a cool, humid climate for optimal growth.

Forbesia flexilis var. barberae is appreciated for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
