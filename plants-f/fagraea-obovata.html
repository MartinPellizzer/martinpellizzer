Fagraea obovata

Fagraea obovata, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, glossy, obovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant has a shallow root system, making it suitable for container gardening in suitable climates.
