Fraxinus sieboldiana var. koiei

Fraxinus sieboldiana var. koiei, commonly known as the Japanese ash or Koeki ash, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often twisted branches, which give it a unique and ornamental appearance. The leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate, with a serrated margin and a dark green upper surface. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in catkins, followed by winged samaras that aid in seed dispersal.

This species is valued in horticulture for its adaptability and aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes.
