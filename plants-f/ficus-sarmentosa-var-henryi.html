Ficus sarmentosa var. henryi

Ficus sarmentosa var. henryi, commonly known as the Henry's fig or the climbing fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are typically pear-shaped. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage.

Ficus sarmentosa var. henryi is relatively low-maintenance, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
