Ficus chalmersii

Ficus chalmersii, commonly known as the Chalmers fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its glossy, broad leaves that are often deeply lobed. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible, greenish-white fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or wetland areas.

Ficus chalmersii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
