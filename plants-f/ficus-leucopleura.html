Ficus leucopleura

Ficus leucopleura, commonly known as the white-veined fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand and Vietnam. The plant is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves with prominent white veins. It produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in fig syconia, which are enclosed within a fleshy structure.

Ficus leucopleura is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
