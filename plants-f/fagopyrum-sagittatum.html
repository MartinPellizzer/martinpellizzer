Fagopyrum sagittatum

Fagopyrum sagittatum, commonly known as arrowleaf buckwheat, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers. The plant is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

The seeds are edible and have been used by indigenous peoples for food and traditional medicine.
