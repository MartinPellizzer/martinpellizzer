Ficus carica var. pilosa

Ficus carica var. pilosa, commonly known as the fuzzy fig, is a variety of the common fig tree characterized by its distinctive hairy leaves.

This plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. The leaves are broad, ovate, and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving them a fuzzy appearance. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into the edible fig fruits.

The plant is valued for its ornamental foliage and is often cultivated for both its aesthetic appeal and its fruit yield.
