Fimbristylis semihirsuta

Fimbristylis semihirsuta, commonly known as the hairy fringed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded areas, such as marshes and wetlands. Its slender, grass-like stems are often adorned with soft, hairy foliage.

Fimbristylis semihirsuta produces small, clustered spikelets that are usually inconspicuous and adapted for wind pollination.
