Festuca pindica

Festuca pindica, commonly known as purple moor grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and bluish-green to purple-colored foliage. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer, adding ornamental value to its appearance. Festuca pindica thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in heathlands and moorlands.

It is valued for its ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
