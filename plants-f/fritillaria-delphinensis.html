Fritillaria delphinensis

Fritillaria delphinensis, commonly known as the Delphinian fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically orange or yellow with a purple or red throat, displaying a unique and vivid coloration. The plant's leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to temperate to alpine climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
