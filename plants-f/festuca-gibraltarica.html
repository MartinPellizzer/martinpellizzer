Festuca gibraltarica

Festuca gibraltarica, commonly known as Gibraltar bentgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the rocky regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It thrives in arid and coastal environments, often found in crevices and on limestone soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to withstand drought and salt spray, making it resilient in harsh climates.

Its ecological significance includes stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
