Fagus clavata

Fagus clavata, commonly known as the Chinese ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the beech family (Fagaceae) and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and smooth, gray bark. The tree produces small, cup-shaped nuts encased in a prickly husk, which are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in temperate climates and is valued for its hard, durable wood.

Fagus clavata is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
