Fumaria neglecta

Fumaria neglecta, commonly known as the common smokeplant, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces distinctive, elongated seed pods that split open to release tiny seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in gardens, wastelands, and along roadsides.

Fumaria neglecta is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
