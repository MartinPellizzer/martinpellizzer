Fagara rhoifolia f. angustifolia

Fagara rhoifolia f. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed fagara, is a botanical variant of the fagara genus native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically dark green and glossy in appearance. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are followed by elliptical, blue-black berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
