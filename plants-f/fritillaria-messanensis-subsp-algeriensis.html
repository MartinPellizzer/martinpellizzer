Fritillaria messanensis subsp. algeriensis

Fritillaria messanensis subsp.

algeriensis, commonly known as Algerian fritillary, is a bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Algeria. It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
