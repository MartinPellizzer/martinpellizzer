Fosterella weberbaueri

Fosterella weberbaueri, commonly known as the Weberbauer's pineapple.

It is a member of the Bromeliaceae family, native to the Andes Mountains of South America. This plant is characterized by its rosette of stiff, grass-like leaves that form a central cup to collect water. The flowers are typically small and clustered, with a striking orange or red color.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soil with partial shade.
