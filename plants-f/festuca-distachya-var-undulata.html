Festuca distachya var. undulata

Festuca distachya var. undulata, commonly known as the wavy feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and delicate, undulating leaves that give it a distinctive, flowing appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often adorned with silky, feathery flowers, which enhance its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens and erosion control.

Its unique morphology and adaptability contribute to its ecological and horticultural significance.
