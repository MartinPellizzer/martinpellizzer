Ficus silvestrei

Ficus silvestrei, commonly known as the wild fig tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible figs. Ficus silvestrei is often found in rainforests and is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.

Its rapid growth and ability to adapt to various soil types make it a resilient and widely distributed species.
