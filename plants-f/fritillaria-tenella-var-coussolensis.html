Fritillaria tenella var. coussolensis

Fritillaria tenella var. coussolensis, commonly known as the Corsican fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to orange, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a single bulb. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
