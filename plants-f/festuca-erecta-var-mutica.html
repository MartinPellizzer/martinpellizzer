Festuca erecta var. mutica

Festuca erecta var. mutica, commonly known as the silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and slightly curled, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer.
