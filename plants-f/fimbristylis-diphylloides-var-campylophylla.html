Fimbristylis diphylloides var. campylophylla

Fimbristylis diphylloides var. campylophylla, commonly known as the narrowleaf flat sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Australia.

It typically grows in wetland or swampy environments, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often crowded at the base, forming dense clumps. Its flowering spikes are usually small and inconspicuous, with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is part of the Cyperaceae family, which includes many other sedges and related plants.
