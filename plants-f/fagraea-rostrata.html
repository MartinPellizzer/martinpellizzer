Fagraea rostrata

Fagraea rostrata, commonly known as the yellow bell tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, making it a striking ornamental plant. The leaves are simple, alternate, and leathery, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. The plant produces small, round, red fruits that contain one or two seeds.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
