Ficus carica var. salernitana

Ficus carica var. salernitana, commonly known as the Salernitana fig, is a variety of the common fig tree native to southern Italy.

It is characterized by its large, sweet, and edible figs that ripen in late summer. The plant has a robust, spreading canopy with lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly pubescent. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its fruit quality and historical significance in Mediterranean agriculture.
