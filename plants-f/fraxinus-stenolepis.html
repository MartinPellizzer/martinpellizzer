Fraxinus stenolepis

Fraxinus stenolepis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ash.

It is a deciduous tree native to parts of Asia, including China and the Himalayas. The plant features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely on the stems. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in clusters, while the fruit is a samara, a type of winged seed.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant form and seasonal foliage.
