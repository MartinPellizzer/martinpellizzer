Fagraea schlechteri

Fagraea schlechteri, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy green leaves and striking, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces bright orange-red berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by birds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Fagraea schlechteri is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
