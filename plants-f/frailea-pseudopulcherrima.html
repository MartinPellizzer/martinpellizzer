Frailea pseudopulcherrima

Frailea pseudopulcherrima, commonly known as the "Pseudo Beauty Cactus," is a member of the Cactaceae family native to Brazil.

It is a small, slow-growing cactus with a globular or cylindrical shape, often featuring a dense covering of white to pale blue spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically short-lived but striking in appearance. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.
