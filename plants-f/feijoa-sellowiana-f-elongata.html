Feijoa sellowiana f. elongata

Feijoa sellowiana f. elongata, commonly known as the elongated feijoa, is a cultivar of the feijoa plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, oval-shaped fruit that is typically larger than the standard feijoa. The fruit has a thick, leathery skin that turns from green to yellow as it ripens. It produces a sweet, aromatic pulp with a unique flavor, often described as a mix of strawberry and pineapple.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses in tropical and subtropical regions.
