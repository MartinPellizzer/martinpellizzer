Ficus guerichiana

Ficus guerichiana, commonly known as the Guerichiana fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, clustered fig fruits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in moist forest understories.

The tree can grow up to 10 meters tall and is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological importance.
