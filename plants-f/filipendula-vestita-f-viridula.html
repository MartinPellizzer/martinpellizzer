Filipendula vestita f. viridula

Filipendula vestita f. viridula, commonly known as the green-flowered meadow sweet, is a botanical variant of the species Filipendula vestita.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia, typically found in moist meadows and grasslands. The plant features delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include a slender stem, finely divided foliage, and a distinct, aromatic scent.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators and contributing to biodiversity in its habitat.
