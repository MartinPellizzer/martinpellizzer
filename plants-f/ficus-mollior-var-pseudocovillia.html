Ficus mollior var. pseudocovillia

Ficus mollior var. pseudocovillia, commonly known as the Variegated Ficus, is a cultivar of the Ficus genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with striking white or cream variegation patterns. This plant is a member of the Moraceae family and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate watering to maintain its health.

The variegation is a result of genetic mutation, making each plant unique in appearance.
