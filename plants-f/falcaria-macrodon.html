Falcaria macrodon

Falcaria macrodon, commonly known as the giant spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a robust, upright stem that can grow up to 2 meters in height, covered with distinctive yellowish-orange milky sap. The leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a rough texture with wavy margins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds.

It is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and wastelands.
