Frailea carminifilamentosa var. winkelmanniana

Frailea carminifilamentosa var. winkelmanniana, commonly known as the Winkelman's frailea, is a compact, slow-growing cactus native to southern Africa.

It features a globular to cylindrical stem with a grayish-green color and dense, spiny areoles. The plant produces bright red or orange flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring or early summer. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

It requires minimal water and is popular among cactus enthusiasts for its unique appearance and hardiness.
