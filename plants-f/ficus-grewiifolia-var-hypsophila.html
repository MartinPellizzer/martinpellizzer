Ficus grewiifolia var. hypsophila

Ficus grewiifolia var. hypsophila, commonly known as the mountain fig, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves that are often a deep green with a slight variegation. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, pear-shaped fruits. It thrives in humid, well-drained soil and requires indirect sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
