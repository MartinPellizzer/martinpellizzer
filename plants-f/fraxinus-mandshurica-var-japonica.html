Fraxinus mandshurica var. japonica

Fraxinus mandshurica var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese ash, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 10–20 meters, featuring a straight trunk and a rounded crown. The leaves are compound, with 7–13 pairs of ovate leaflets, and they turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences, followed by winged samaras as its fruit.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
