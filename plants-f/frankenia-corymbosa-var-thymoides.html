Frankenia corymbosa var. thymoides

Frankenia corymbosa var. thymoides, commonly known as coastal thyme or sea thyme, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It is a low-growing shrub with aromatic, glandular leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to salt spray and coastal winds.

Its compact growth habit and hardy nature make it a popular choice for coastal landscaping.
