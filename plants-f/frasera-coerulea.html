Frasera coerulea

Frasera coerulea, commonly known as blue flax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Frasera coerulea is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
