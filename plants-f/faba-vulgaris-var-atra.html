Faba vulgaris var. atra

Faba vulgaris var. atra, commonly known as the black field bean, is a variety of the common vetch.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black seeds and trifoliate leaves. This plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia and thrives in temperate climates. It is often found in disturbed soils and can be a weed in agricultural fields.

The plant has been used historically for both food and fodder.
