Festuca longidiurna

Festuca longidiurna, commonly known as the long-day fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Festuca longidiurna is known for its ability to tolerate a range of environmental conditions, including moderate drought.

Its flowering spikes are usually arranged in panicle-like clusters, producing small, spikelet-based flowers that are wind-pollinated.
