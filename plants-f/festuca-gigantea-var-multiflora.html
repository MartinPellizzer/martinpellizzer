Festuca gigantea var. multiflora

Festuca gigantea var. multiflora, commonly known as the purple moor grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of purple or pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in heathlands and moorlands. Its foliage is typically dark green, and it can grow up to 1.5 meters in height.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
