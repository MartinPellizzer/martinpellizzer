Festuca peruviana

Festuca peruviana, commonly known as Peruvian bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and dense, carpet-like growth habit. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky soils, thriving in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and blue-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Festuca peruviana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
