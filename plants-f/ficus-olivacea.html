Ficus olivacea

Ficus olivacea, commonly known as the olive fig or the weeping fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, including countries like Nigeria and Ghana. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often heart-shaped and have a waxy texture. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits that turn from green to yellow or red as they mature.

Ficus olivacea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
