Fistularia × hungarica

Fistularia × hungarica, commonly known as the hybrid sea horse, is a fascinating marine plant that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Fistularia ambigua and Fistularia petrura, showcasing genetic diversity in its structure. The plant typically has a cylindrical, branching stem that is adapted to thrive in brackish water environments. Its leaves are small and scale-like, reducing water loss in its saline habitat.

This hybrid's botanical features make it an important subject for studying plant adaptation and speciation in coastal ecosystems.
