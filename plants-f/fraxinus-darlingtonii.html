Fraxinus darlingtonii

Fraxinus darlingtonii, commonly known as Darlington's ash, is a species of ash tree native to eastern North America.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows to a height of 10 to 20 meters, featuring a narrow, upright form. The leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate, with a serrated margin and a dark green upper surface. The small, greenish-yellow flowers appear in clusters and are followed by winged fruits that disperse seeds by wind.

It is adapted to moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
