Filipendula multijuga var. acerina

Filipendula multijuga var. acerina, commonly known as the redstem filipendula, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its stems are reddish in color, which gives it its common name, and it is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and flowers.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and maintaining soil stability in its native habitats.
