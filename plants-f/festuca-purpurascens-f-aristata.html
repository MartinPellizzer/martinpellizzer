Festuca purpurascens f. aristata

Festuca purpurascens f. aristata, commonly known as the purple false brome, is a variant of the purple false brome species.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in temperate regions, often found in meadows and grasslands. The plant is characterized by its distinctive purple-tinted leaves and flowering spikes that appear in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is often more densely tufted compared to the typical form, giving it a more compact appearance.

This ornamental grass is valued for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
