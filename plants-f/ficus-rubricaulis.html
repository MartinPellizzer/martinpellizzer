Ficus rubricaulis

Ficus rubricaulis, commonly known as the red-stemmed fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand and Malaysia, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its distinctive reddish-brown stems and heart-shaped leaves that are usually a deep green. Ficus rubricaulis produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically small and sweet.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to indoor environments.
