Fagopyrum crassifolium

Fagopyrum crassifolium, commonly known as broad-leaved buckwheat, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces distinctive, triangular seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or open areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and herbal practices.
