Fritillaria lycia

Fritillaria lycia, commonly known as the Persian fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and the Caucasus.

It produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to orange and yellow, often with intricate patterns. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings. Its bulbs are perennial, and it blooms in early spring, adding a dramatic touch to its habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for centuries in traditional gardens.
