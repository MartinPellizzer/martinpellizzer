Frankenia grandifolia subsp. campestris

Frankenia grandifolia subsp.

campestris, commonly known as the coastal frankenia, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the southeastern United States. It is a low-growing shrub with woody stems and dense, dark green foliage.

The plant produces small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in dune environments.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.
