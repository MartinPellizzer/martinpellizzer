Fraxinus richardii

Fraxinus richardii, commonly known as the Mexican ash, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in racemes, followed by winged fruits called samaras.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry to moist forests, contributing to local biodiversity.
