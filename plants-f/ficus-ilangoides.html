Ficus ilangoides

Ficus ilangoides, commonly known as the Indian rubber fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves and milky sap. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a variety of indoor environments. It produces small, greenish flowers that develop into fig-like fruits, though these are not typically edible.

Ficus ilangoides thrives in warm, humid conditions with indirect light and regular watering.
