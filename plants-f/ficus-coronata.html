Ficus coronata

Ficus coronata, commonly known as the crown fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, Madagascar, and the Arabian Peninsula. The plant is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible figs. Ficus coronata thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in coastal areas or near riverbanks.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
