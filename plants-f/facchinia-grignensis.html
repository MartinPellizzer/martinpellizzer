Facchinia grignensis

Facchinia grignensis, commonly known as the Grignan stonecress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of southern France.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many edible and ornamental plants. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale purple, blooming in early spring. Its leaves are succulent, fleshy, and often covered in a fine layer of white powder, a characteristic known as glaucous.

Facchinia grignensis thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
