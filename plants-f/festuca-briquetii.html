Festuca briquetii

Festuca briquetii, commonly known as Briquet's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, hairless leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, spikelet-like flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Festuca briquetii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
