Frasera fastigiata

Frasera fastigiata, commonly known as the spiked frasera, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Frasera fastigiata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators.
