Filaginopsis multicaulis

Filaginopsis multicaulis, commonly known as the many-stemmed filagrate, is a unique plant species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its clustered growth habit and multiple stems arising from a central base. The plant features small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its overall herbaceous appearance.

Filaginopsis multicaulis is of interest to botanists due to its distinct morphology and limited distribution.
