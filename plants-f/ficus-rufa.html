Ficus rufa

Ficus rufa, commonly known as the red fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often have a reddish tinge on the underside. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits.

Ficus rufa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor and outdoor conditions.
