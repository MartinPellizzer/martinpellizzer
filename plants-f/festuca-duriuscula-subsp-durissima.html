Festuca duriuscula subsp. durissima

Festuca duriuscula subsp.

durissima, commonly known as the hardy fescue, is a perennial grass species native to arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and compact growth habit, which helps it withstand harsh environmental conditions.

The plant typically grows in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to low temperatures and high altitudes. Its leaves are usually bluish-green, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Festuca duriuscula subsp.

durissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological resilience in cold climates.
