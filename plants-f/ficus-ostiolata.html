Ficus ostiolata

Ficus ostiolata, commonly known as the small-leaved fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India, Sri Lanka, and parts of Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, green fig fruits that are typically not edible for humans. It thrives in humid environments and is often found in lowland forests or near water sources.

Ficus ostiolata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
