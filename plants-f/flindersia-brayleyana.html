Flindersia brayleyana

Flindersia brayleyana, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has simple, ovate leaves that are alternate and often have a slightly waxy texture. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release its seeds.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
