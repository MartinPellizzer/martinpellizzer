Fagopyrum emarginatum

Fagopyrum emarginatum, commonly known as the Japanese buckwheat, is a herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Fagopyrum emarginatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage.
