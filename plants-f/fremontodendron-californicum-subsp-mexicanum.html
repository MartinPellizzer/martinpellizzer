Fremontodendron californicum subsp. mexicanum

Fremontodendron californicum subsp.

mexicanum, commonly known as the Mexican fremontodendron, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as rocky soils and along riverbanks.

The plant features woody stems and lance-shaped leaves with a gray-green appearance. It produces clusters of yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is adapted to withstand drought and has a long history of use by indigenous peoples for medicinal and practical purposes.
