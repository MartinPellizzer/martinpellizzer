Ficus spanogheana

Ficus spanogheana, commonly known as the Spanish fig or Spanish ficus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the Canary Islands and is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig-like fruits. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in indoor environments with minimal care.
