Fadogia caespitosa

Fadogia caespitosa, commonly known as African velvet bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, and its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets. Fadogia caespitosa is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in savanna and woodland habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in both ecological and traditional contexts.
