Festuca antarctica

Festuca antarctica, commonly known as Antarctic hair grass, is a resilient grass species native to the extreme environments of Antarctica and sub-Antarctic regions.

It thrives in harsh conditions, including freezing temperatures, strong winds, and nutrient-poor soils. The plant has a low-growing, mat-forming structure with dense, fine leaves that help reduce water loss and withstand wind. Its reproductive strategy involves producing small, wind-dispersed seeds that enable it to colonize new areas.

Botanically, it is characterized by its ability to survive in cold climates, making it an important component of polar ecosystems.
