Ficus anthelminthica

Ficus anthelminthica, commonly known as the medicinal fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to parts of Africa and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered fruits. The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to drought conditions. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported antiparasitic properties.

The tree can grow up to 10 meters tall and is valued for both its ornamental and medicinal uses.
