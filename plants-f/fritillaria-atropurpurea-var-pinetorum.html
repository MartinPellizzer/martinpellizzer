Fritillaria atropurpurea var. pinetorum

Fritillaria atropurpurea var. pinetorum, commonly known as the purple-fritillary lily, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of the western United States.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from deep purple to reddish-brown, often with intricate patterns. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a basal rosette of bulbs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it well-suited for rocky or alpine habitats.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native environment make it a notable species in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
