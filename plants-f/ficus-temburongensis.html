Ficus temburongensis

Ficus temburongensis, commonly known as the Temburong fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical rainforests of Borneo, specifically found in the Temburong District of Brunei. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy green leaves and produces small, clustered fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow when mature. It plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing food for various wildlife species.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
