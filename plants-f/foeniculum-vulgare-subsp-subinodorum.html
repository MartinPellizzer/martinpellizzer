Foeniculum vulgare subsp. subinodorum

Foeniculum vulgare subsp.

subinodorum, commonly known as the Turkish cumin, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features aromatic, feathery leaves and produces yellow flowers in umbels.

The plant is characterized by its finely divided, thread-like leaves and a distinct, spicy aroma. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, similar to its more widely known relative, Foeniculum vulgare.
