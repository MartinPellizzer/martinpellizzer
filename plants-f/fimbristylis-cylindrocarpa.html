Fimbristylis cylindrocarpa

Fimbristylis cylindrocarpa, commonly known as the cylindrical flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Australia.

It typically grows in wetland areas, including marshes and the edges of water bodies, where it thrives in moist to water-saturated soils. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, cylindrical spikelets that are often clustered at the tips of the stems. It is characterized by its fine, thread-like hairs along the leaf margins, which give it a distinctive appearance.

Fimbristylis cylindrocarpa plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
