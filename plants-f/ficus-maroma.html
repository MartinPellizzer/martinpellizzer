Ficus maroma

Ficus maroma, commonly known as the Maroma fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the arid regions of southern Africa, particularly Namibia and South Africa. The plant is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

The fruit is a small, round fig that ripens to a pale yellow or red color, attracting various wildlife species.
