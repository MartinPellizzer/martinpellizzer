Fragaria vesca f. semperflorens

Fragaria vesca f. semperflorens, commonly known as the everblooming strawberry, is a horticultural form of the woodland strawberry.

It is characterized by its persistent flowering habit, producing small, edible fruits throughout the growing season. The plant typically grows as a low-growing perennial with trifoliate leaves and red-tinged stems. It thrives in cool climates and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to provide continuous harvests of strawberries.
