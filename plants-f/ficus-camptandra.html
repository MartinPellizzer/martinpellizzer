Ficus camptandra

Ficus camptandra, commonly known as the creeping fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to eastern and southeastern Asia, including countries like China, Japan, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and ability to grow on various surfaces, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation. Ficus camptandra produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by round, smooth fruits.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to different environmental conditions.
