Fockea cylindrica

Fockea cylindrica, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a striking red or purple throat. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions. It produces small, round fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Fockea cylindrica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
