Festuca glauca var. hirsuta

Festuca glauca var. hirsuta, commonly known as bluegrass with hairy leaves, is a perennial grass species native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky areas.

Festuca glauca var. hirsuta is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in native plant landscapes.
