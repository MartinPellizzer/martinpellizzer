Festuca niitakensis

Festuca niitakensis, commonly known as the Niitakene fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along streambanks. Its flowering spikes are usually slender and produce small, inconspicuous flowers.

Festuca niitakensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
