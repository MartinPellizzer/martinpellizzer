Fritillaria yuminensis var. roseoflora

Fritillaria yuminensis var. roseoflora, commonly known as the rose-flowered fritillary, is a rare and striking bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep rose, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves and is known for its elegant, nodding blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a cold winter dormancy period to bloom successfully.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in specialized gardens and botanical collections.
