Fagara mantchurica f. grandifolia

Fagara mantchurica f. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered fagara, is a variant of the Fagara genus native to the temperate regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, making it visually distinctive. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often pubescent. Its foliage is evergreen, providing year-round interest, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This plant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to shaded environments.
