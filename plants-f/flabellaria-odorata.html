Flabellaria odorata

Flabellaria odorata, commonly known as the night-scented orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that emit a strong scent at night. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid with slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are often visited by nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The species is notable for its unique nocturnal fragrance and specialized pollination strategy.
