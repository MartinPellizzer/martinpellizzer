Friedlandia thymifolia

Friedlandia thymifolia, commonly known as the thyme-leaved friedlandia, is a unique plant native to the mountainous regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features small, aromatic leaves that resemble those of thyme, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in tight, clustered inflorescences.

Friedlandia thymifolia is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in well-drained environments.
