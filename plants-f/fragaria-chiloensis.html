Fragaria chiloensis

Fragaria chiloensis, commonly known as the Chilean strawberry, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It produces small, red, edible fruits that are often used in jams and desserts. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces white flowers with five petals. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental and culinary value.

Its roots are fibrous and help it survive in rocky or sandy soils.
