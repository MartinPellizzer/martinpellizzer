Ficus cervina

Ficus cervina, commonly known as the white fig or the slender fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, ovate leaves that are usually dark green in color. Ficus cervina produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fig fruits.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
