Freycinetia gibbsiae

Freycinetia gibbsiae, commonly known as the New Zealand pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands, including New Zealand.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or green in color. Its roots are known to have a sweet, aromatic scent, which has made it valuable in traditional Polynesian cultures for culinary and medicinal uses.

The plant thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often used in local crafts and as a decorative plant.
