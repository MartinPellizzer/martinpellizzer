Ficus antaoensis

Ficus antaoensis, commonly known as the Antao fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the island of Antao in the Indian Ocean, specifically in the archipelago of the Mascarene Islands. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers. It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, often found in coastal and humid forest environments.

The fruit, a fig, is edible and has been traditionally used in local cuisines.
