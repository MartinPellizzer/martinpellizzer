Festuca woronowii subsp. caucasica

Festuca woronowii subsp.

caucasica, commonly known as Caucasian fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces compact, spike-like inflorescences bearing small, spikelet-based flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous and rocky habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
