Ficus pubinervis f. sibulanensis

Ficus pubinervis f. sibulanensis, commonly known as the Sibulan fig, is a variant of the pubinervis ficus species native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, featuring a combination of glossy green and pale undersides on its leaves. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, often found in lowland to montane forest habitats. Its leaves are usually ovate to elliptical in shape, with a serrated margin and prominent veins.

The Sibulan fig produces small, yellowish-green figs that are an important food source for various wildlife species.
