Festuca vizzavonae

Festuca vizzavonae, commonly known as the Vizzavona fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the alpine regions of Corsica.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of hairs. This plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous areas. Festuca vizzavonae is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in challenging terrains.

Its botanical features make it a unique and ecologically significant species within its native habitat.
