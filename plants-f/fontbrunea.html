Fontbrunea

Fontbrunea, commonly known as the African snowball, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and clusters of white, waxy flowers that resemble snowballs, blooming in late winter or early spring. The leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptical, with a glossy green surface. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
