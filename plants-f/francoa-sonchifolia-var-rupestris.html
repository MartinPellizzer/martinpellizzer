Francoa sonchifolia var. rupestris

Francoa sonchifolia var. rupestris, commonly known as the rock-francoa, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features silver-gray foliage with a fuzzy texture, and its flowers are typically yellow or orange, resembling those of a dandelion. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. It is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to add ornamental interest to dry landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and versatile choice for gardeners in arid regions.
