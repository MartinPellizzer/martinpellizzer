Ficus vagans var. macrocarpa

Ficus vagans var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-leaf fig, is a tropical plant characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig fruits. It is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

The leaves are glossy and provide a lush, decorative appearance, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
