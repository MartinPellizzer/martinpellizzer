Ficus popenoei subsp. malacocarpa

Ficus popenoei subsp.

malacocarpa, commonly known as the Malacocarpa Fig, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia. It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows in lowland rainforests.

The plant is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves that are often dark green in color. Its fruit, a small fig, is enclosed in a smooth, greenish-yellow capsule.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its foliage rather than its fruit.
