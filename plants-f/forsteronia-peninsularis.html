Forsteronia peninsularis

Forsteronia peninsularis, commonly known as the Peninsular forsteria, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Forsteronia peninsularis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
