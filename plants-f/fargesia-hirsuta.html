Fargesia hirsuta

Fargesia hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy Fargesia, is a species of bamboo native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and gracefully arching foliage. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, hairy texture, giving it its common name. It is a clump-forming plant that produces clusters of white flowers in early summer.

Fargesia hirsuta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.
