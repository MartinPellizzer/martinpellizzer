Ficus pseudocaulocarpa

Ficus pseudocaulocarpa, commonly known as the knob-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that often have a distinctive knobby or swollen base. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits.

This species is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
