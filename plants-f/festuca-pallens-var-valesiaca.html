Festuca pallens var. valesiaca

Festuca pallens var. valesiaca, commonly known as the Valesiaca fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and silver-blue foliage due to the dense covering of silky hairs on its leaves. This variety thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to form dense, persistent stands, making it a valuable plant for erosion control.

The plant is also noted for its ornamental value, with its distinctive color and texture making it a popular choice in garden settings.
