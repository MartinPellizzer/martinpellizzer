Festuca carrascana

Festuca carrascana, commonly known as the mountain fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. The plant features fine, tufted leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. Its root system is fibrous and helps stabilize soil in rocky or degraded habitats.

Festuca carrascana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
