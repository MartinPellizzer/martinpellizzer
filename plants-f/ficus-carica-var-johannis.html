Ficus carica var. johannis

Ficus carica var. johannis, commonly known as the Johann's fig, is a cultivated variety of the common fig tree.

It is characterized by its large, edible fruit that ripens in late summer. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in warm, temperate climates. Its leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, with a smooth texture.

This variety is prized for its sweet, succulent fruit and is often grown in home gardens and commercial orchards.
