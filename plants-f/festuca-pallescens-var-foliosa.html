Festuca pallescens var. foliosa

Festuca pallescens var. foliosa, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its slender stems and small, spike-like inflorescences contribute to its ornamental value.

It is often used in landscaping for its visual appeal and low maintenance requirements.
