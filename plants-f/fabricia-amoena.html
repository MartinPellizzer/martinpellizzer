Fabricia amoena

Fabricia amoena, commonly known as the sweet pea vine, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of fragrant, pea-like flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth habit and ability to climb using its tendrils. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained, fertile soil.

Fabricia amoena is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
