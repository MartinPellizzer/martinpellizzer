Fragaria roseiflora

Fragaria roseiflora, commonly known as the rose-flowered strawberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and delicate, pinkish-white flowers that resemble roses. The plant produces small, edible fruits similar to strawberries, which are typically red and succulent. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
