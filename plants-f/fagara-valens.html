Fagara valens

Fagara valens, commonly known as the purple-leafed evergreen, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with a striking purple underside, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Fagara valens thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural gardens.
