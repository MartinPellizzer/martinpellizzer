Faberia

Faberia, commonly known as the genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae, comprises shrubs and herbs native to various regions of the world.

These plants are characterized by their compound leaves and pea-like flowers, which typically have five petals. Faberia species are often found in temperate and subtropical climates, thriving in well-drained soils. They play a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility through their symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

Some species are cultivated for ornamental purposes or used in traditional medicine.
