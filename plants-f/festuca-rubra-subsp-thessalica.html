Festuca rubra subsp. thessalica

Festuca rubra subsp.

thessalica, commonly known as the Thessalian red fescue, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and dense, dark green foliage.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands. It produces slender, spike-like inflorescences with small, two-lobed flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and restoration projects.
