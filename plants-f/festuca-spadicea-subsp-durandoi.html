Festuca spadicea subsp. durandoi

Festuca spadicea subsp.

durandoi, commonly known as the Durando's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, dark green leaves.

The plant produces compact, spike-like inflorescences bearing small, pale green to brownish flowers. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and used in ecological restoration projects.
