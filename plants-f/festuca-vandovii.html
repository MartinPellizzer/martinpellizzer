Festuca vandovii

Festuca vandovii, commonly known as the Vando's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. Festuca vandovii is adapted to cold and arid environments, often growing in rocky or alpine habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
