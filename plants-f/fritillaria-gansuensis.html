Fritillaria gansuensis

Fritillaria gansuensis, commonly known as the Gansu fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Gansu Province in China.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep orange. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, often in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its bulbs are adapted for storage, allowing the plant to survive harsh winters and periods of drought.

Fritillaria gansuensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
