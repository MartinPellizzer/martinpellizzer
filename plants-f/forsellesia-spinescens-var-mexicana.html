Forsellesia spinescens var. mexicana

Forsellesia spinescens var. mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican spiny cactus, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its succulent structure. Its flowers bloom in the spring and are pollinated by insects, contributing to local biodiversity.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
