Fedorovia glauca

Fedorovia glauca, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small blue or purple clouds. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces showy, clustered blooms that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Fedorovia glauca thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and prairies.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking native plants with both ecological and aesthetic benefits.
