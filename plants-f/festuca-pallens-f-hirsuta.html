Festuca pallens f. hirsuta

Festuca pallens f. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy silvergrass, is a variant of the silvergrass species characterized by its distinctive hairy foliage.

This plant is a perennial grass that typically grows in temperate regions, often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and silver-blue in color, with a fine layer of hairs that give it a soft, fuzzy appearance. The inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, pale green to silver flowers.

Festuca pallens f. hirsuta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its visual texture and drought tolerance.
