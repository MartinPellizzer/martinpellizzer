Fagelia diversifolia

Fagelia diversifolia, commonly known as the diverse-leaved fagelia, is a tropical plant belonging to the Anacardiaceae family.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which exhibit a wide variation in shape and size across different parts of the plant. The leaves are usually alternate and can be either pinnate or palmate, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color.

Fagelia diversifolia is native to parts of Southeast Asia and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.
