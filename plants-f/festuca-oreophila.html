Festuca oreophila

Festuca oreophila, commonly known as alpine fescue, is a perennial grass species native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green leaves that are adapted to high-altitude environments. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Festuca oreophila thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Its hardy nature makes it an important component of high-elevation ecosystems.
