Festuca gussonei

Festuca gussonei, commonly known as the Gussone's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Festuca gussonei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and aesthetic qualities.
