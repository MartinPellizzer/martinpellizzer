Fagus pyramidalis

Fagus pyramidalis, commonly known as the Japanese fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Pinaceae and is characterized by its pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves. The tree produces cones that are typically reddish-brown and contain seeds surrounded by a wing for wind dispersal. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

The Japanese fir is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and parks.
