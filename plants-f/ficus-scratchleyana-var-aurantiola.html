Ficus scratchleyana var. aurantiola

Ficus scratchleyana var. aurantiola, commonly known as the golden fig or variegated fig, is a rare and ornamental species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage with shades of green, white, and yellow, which adds visual interest to any setting. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits that are typically consumed by native wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to bright indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
