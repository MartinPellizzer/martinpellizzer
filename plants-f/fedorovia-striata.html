Fedorovia striata

Fedorovia striata, commonly known as the stripeleaf cycad, is a unique member of the Cycadaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its tall, unbranched stem and its distinctive, strap-like leaves that are marked with prominent white or pale green stripes. Its reproductive structures include large, cone-like inflorescences that produce both male and female cones.

Due to its ornamental value and slow growth rate, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
