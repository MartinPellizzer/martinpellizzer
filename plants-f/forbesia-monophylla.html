Forbesia monophylla

Forbesia monophylla, commonly known as the single-leaf forbesia, is a rare and unique plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its singular, large, waxy leaf that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a subtle ornamental value. Forbesia monophylla is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, thriving in high-altitude environments.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
