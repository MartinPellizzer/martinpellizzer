Fragaria chiloensis subsp. pacifica

Fragaria chiloensis subsp.

pacifica, commonly known as the Pacific strawberry, is a perennial herb native to the coastal regions of southern South America. It produces small, edible berries that are typically red or yellow in color, with a sweet and tangy flavor.

The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces white flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal dunes and rocky habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its berries.
