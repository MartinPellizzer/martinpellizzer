Fagelia sessilis

Fagelia sessilis, commonly known as the sessile fagelia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, and it produces legume pods that contain several seeds.

Fagelia sessilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
