Festuca siracusana

Festuca siracusana, commonly known as Sicilian fescue, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe, particularly Sicily.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense mats. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous narrow, awnless spikelets. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Festuca siracusana is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.
