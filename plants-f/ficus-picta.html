Ficus picta

Ficus picta, commonly known as the variegated fig tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with distinctive white and yellow variegation. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible figs. Ficus picta thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light.

It is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
