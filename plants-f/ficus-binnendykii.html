Ficus binnendykii

Ficus binnendykii, commonly known as the Binnendijk's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, and is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and distinctive, smooth, grayish bark. The plant is deciduous, shedding its leaves during the dry season, and produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to high levels of light, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Ficus binnendykii is also valued for its ability to tolerate a range of environmental conditions, contributing to its adaptability in both natural and cultivated settings.
