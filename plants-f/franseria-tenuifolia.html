Franseria tenuifolia

Franseria tenuifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf franseria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in coastal and inland regions, showing adaptability to various environmental conditions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it requires well-drained soil and minimal water.
