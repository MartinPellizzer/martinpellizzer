Festuca × belensis var. saxatilis

Festuca × belensis var. saxatilis, commonly known as the saxatile meadow fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically a dull green. This variety thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear pale blue to purple flowers.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and restoration projects.
