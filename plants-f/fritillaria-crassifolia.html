Fritillaria crassifolia

Fritillaria crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad, lance-shaped leaves and its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically blooms in spring, and its flowers are often adorned with intricate patterns and a distinctive "tongue" at the base. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
