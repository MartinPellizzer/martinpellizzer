Ficus onusta

Ficus onusta, commonly known as the white fig or white-veined fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including India, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves with prominent white veins. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically harvested when they turn yellow or red.

Ficus onusta is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
