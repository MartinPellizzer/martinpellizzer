Freycinetia viriosa

Freycinetia viriosa, commonly known as the red-flowered pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of the Pacific, including parts of Australia and New Guinea. The plant is characterized by its tall, robust stems and large, pinnately compound leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. Its striking red flowers, which appear in dense clusters, are a distinguishing feature.

Freycinetia viriosa is valued not only for its ornamental appearance but also for its cultural significance in indigenous communities.
