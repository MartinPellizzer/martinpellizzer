Ficus kotoensis

Ficus kotoensis, commonly known as the Koto ficus, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically elongated and pointed. The plant produces small, fig-like fruits that are enclosed within a syconium, a unique structure typical of fig species. Ficus kotoensis is often cultivated as a decorative indoor plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.

It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.
