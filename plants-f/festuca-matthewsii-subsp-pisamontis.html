Festuca matthewsii subsp. pisamontis

Festuca matthewsii subsp.

pisamontis, commonly known as the Pisamontis fescue, is a perennial grass species native to specific regions of Europe. It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils, often found in meadows and along riverbanks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
