Fuchsia denticulata

Fuchsia denticulata, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes mountains in South America.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous flowers that range in color from deep purple to bright red. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its leaves are palmately lobed and have a distinctive velvety texture.

Fuchsia denticulata thrives in cool, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
