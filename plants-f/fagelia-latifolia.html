Fagelia latifolia

Fagelia latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf fagelia, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other plants like the cashew and poison ivy. The plant is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are typically lobed or serrated. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by red, fleshy berries.

Fagelia latifolia is often found in moist, woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
