Ficus binuangensis

Ficus binuangensis, commonly known as the Binuang fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the lowland rainforests of Indonesia, specifically the islands of Java and Bali. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a waxy texture. It produces small, clustered figs that are edible and often used in local cuisine.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in the regions where it is found.
