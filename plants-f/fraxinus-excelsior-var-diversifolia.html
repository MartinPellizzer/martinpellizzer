Fraxinus excelsior var. diversifolia

Fraxinus excelsior var. diversifolia, commonly known as the variable ash, is a rare variant of the common ash tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, featuring leaves with varying leaflets, hence its name. The plant is native to parts of Europe and is typically found in moist, wooded areas. It has a slender trunk and can grow up to 20 meters tall.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable species in botanical studies.
