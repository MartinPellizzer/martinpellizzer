Fritillaria tulipifolia var. dasyphylla

Fritillaria tulipifolia var. dasyphylla, commonly known as the Turk's Cap Larkspur, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from deep purple to white, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces a tall, leafless stem that bears clusters of showy blooms, typically in late spring to early summer. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in harsh, arid conditions, storing water and nutrients efficiently.

The foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from the base, contributing to its ornamental appeal in gardens.
