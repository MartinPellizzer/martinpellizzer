Fuchsia vargasiana

Fuchsia vargasiana, commonly known as the Vargas fuchsia, is a species native to Ecuador.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces striking, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and purple. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its floral structure consists of tubular corollas that split into five lobes, typical of the fuchsia genus.

Fuchsia vargasiana thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in cloud forests at higher elevations.
