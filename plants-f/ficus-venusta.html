Ficus venusta

Ficus venusta, commonly known as the weeping fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its drooping branches and glossy, dark green leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by round, greenish fruits. It is widely cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and air-purifying qualities.

Ficus venusta prefers bright, indirect light and moderate humidity to thrive.
