Fuchsia canescens

Fuchsia canescens, commonly known as the silver fuchsia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers. The flowers typically bloom in shades of pink, purple, and white, with a distinctive, velvety texture. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking unique and vibrant plant specimens.
