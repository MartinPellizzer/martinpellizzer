Ficus seguinii

Ficus seguinii, commonly known as the Seguin's fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible fig-like fruits. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires well-draining soil.

Ficus seguinii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor conditions.
