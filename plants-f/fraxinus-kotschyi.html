Fraxinus kotschyi

Fraxinus kotschyi, commonly known as the Persian ash, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often multi-trunked form. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental value.

Fraxinus kotschyi is adapted to dry and rocky environments, making it a hardy species in arid climates.
