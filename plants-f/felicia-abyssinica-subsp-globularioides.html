Felicia abyssinica subsp. globularioides

Felicia abyssinica subsp.

globularioides, commonly known as the African daisy, is a perennial flowering plant native to eastern Africa. It is characterized by its compact, globe-shaped rosettes of blue-gray foliage and produces showy yellow flowers with a central disk.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for gardeners in arid regions.

The subspecies is closely related to other Felicia species, sharing similar ecological and morphological traits.
