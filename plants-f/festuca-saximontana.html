Festuca saximontana

Festuca saximontana, commonly known as the saximontan fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. This plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. Festuca saximontana is known for its resilience to cold temperatures and drought conditions.

Its flowers are small and clustered in dense spikes, contributing to its ornamental value in specialized gardens.
