Festuca octoflora var. aristulata

Festuca octoflora var. aristulata, commonly known as the aristulate bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and bluish-green foliage. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences with small, spikelet-like flowers that are typically blue to purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant landscapes.
