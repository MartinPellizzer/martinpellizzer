Ferula bungeana

Ferula bungeana, commonly known as the Chinese cow's milk plant, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and yellow flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that has been traditionally used in folk medicine for its purported healing properties. Its leaves are lance-shaped and aromatic, often used in local remedies.

Ferula bungeana thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.
