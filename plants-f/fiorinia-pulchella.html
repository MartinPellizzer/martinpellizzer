Fiorinia pulchella

Fiorinia pulchella, commonly known as the pretty lady's mantle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, overlapping foliage that resembles a mantle or cloak, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Fiorinia pulchella is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
