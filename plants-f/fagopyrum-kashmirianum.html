Fagopyrum kashmirianum

Fagopyrum kashmirianum, commonly known as Kashmir buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that grows in high-altitude environments, typically at elevations above 2,000 meters. The plant features palmately lobed leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. It is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and is often found in alpine meadows.

The plant is also valued for its edible seeds, which are rich in nutrients and used in traditional cuisines.
