Ficus capensis var. trichoneura

Ficus capensis var. trichoneura, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fig, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a waxy surface and a distinctive narrow, pointed tip. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by edible figs that ripen to a dark purple color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
