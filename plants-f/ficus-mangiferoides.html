Ficus mangiferoides

Ficus mangiferoides, commonly known as the Indian rubber plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely on the stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible figs, which are a significant food source for various wildlife and humans. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Ficus mangiferoides is also valued for its ability to tolerate low light conditions, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
