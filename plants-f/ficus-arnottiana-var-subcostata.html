Ficus arnottiana var. subcostata

Ficus arnottiana var. subcostata, commonly known as the narrow-ribbed fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that have distinct, prominent veins running along their length. It produces small, clustered figs that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or red when mature.

This species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
