Fleischmanniopsis leucocephala

Fleischmanniopsis leucocephala, commonly known as the white-headed flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, linear leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to arid conditions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens in suitable climates.
