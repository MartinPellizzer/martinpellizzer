Fortunella crassifolia

Fortunella crassifolia, commonly known as the kumquat tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, orange fruits that are edible, both the skin and pulp being sweet and citrusy. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

Fortunella crassifolia is relatively drought-tolerant once established, making it suitable for various garden settings.
