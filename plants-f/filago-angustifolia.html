Filago angustifolia

Filago angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf filago, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by distinctive, bristly seeds.

Filago angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
