Ficus mitrophora

Ficus mitrophora, commonly known as the African fig tree, is a species native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are typically green when immature and turn yellow or red when ripe. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in forested areas.

Ficus mitrophora is valued for its ecological role and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
