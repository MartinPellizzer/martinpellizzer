Festuca panamica

Festuca panamica, commonly known as Panama grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Festuca panamica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various insects and birds.
