Ferula turcomanica

Ferula turcomanica, commonly known as the Turcoman ferula, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the family. Its most notable feature is its long, hollow, aromatic stem, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

Ferula turcomanica thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and mountainous regions.
