Feroniella pubescens

Feroniella pubescens, commonly known as the hairy huito, is a small shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the family Solanaceae, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that give way to edible, round fruits resembling small tomatoes. These fruits are often used in local cuisines and have a mild, sweet flavor.

Feroniella pubescens is adapted to tropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils.
