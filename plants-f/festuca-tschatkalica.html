Festuca tschatkalica

Festuca tschatkalica, commonly known as the Siberian fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Festuca tschatkalica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in harsh climates.
