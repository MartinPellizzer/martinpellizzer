Ficus danielis

Ficus danielis, commonly known as the Daniel's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, clustered fig fruits. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ficus danielis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
