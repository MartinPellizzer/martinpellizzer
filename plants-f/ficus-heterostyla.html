Ficus heterostyla

Ficus heterostyla, commonly known as the heterostyle fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia, including India, Sri Lanka, and parts of Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its distinctive two-lobed leaves and small, clustered flowers. It produces edible figs that are typically consumed fresh or used in traditional medicine.

Ficus heterostyla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
