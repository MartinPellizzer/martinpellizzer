Ficus pseudosycomorus

Ficus pseudosycomorus, commonly known as the African fig, is a species of fig tree native to tropical Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height, featuring a dense canopy of broad, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, clustered fig fruits that are edible and serve as an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in moist lowland forests.

The plant's rapid growth and adaptability make it a valuable species for ecological restoration projects.
