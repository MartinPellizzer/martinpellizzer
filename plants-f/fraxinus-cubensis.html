Fraxinus cubensis

Fraxinus cubensis, commonly known as the Cuban ash, is a deciduous tree native to Cuba.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed trunk and compound leaves. The leaves are typically opposite, with 5 to 9 ovate leaflets that are serrated along the edges. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in panicles, followed by winged samaras as its fruit.

It is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
