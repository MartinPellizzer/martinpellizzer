Faramea calyptrata

Faramea calyptrata, commonly known as the broadleaf hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, featuring opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, often with a distinctive yellow or orange center. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, dark seeds.

Faramea calyptrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various soil types.
