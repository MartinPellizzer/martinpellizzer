Ficus rehmannii

Ficus rehmannii, commonly known as the Chinese banyan, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to eastern Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea. The plant is characterized by its large, broad leaves and can grow as a tree or shrub, often reaching heights of up to 10 meters. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in clusters, and its fruit is a small, spherical berry.

The plant is well adapted to a variety of soil types and can thrive in both full sun and partial shade.
