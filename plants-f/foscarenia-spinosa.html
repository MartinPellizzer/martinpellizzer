Foscarenia spinosa

Foscarenia spinosa, commonly known as the spiny foscarenia, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland habitats.

Foscarenia spinosa is noted for its spiny stems and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
