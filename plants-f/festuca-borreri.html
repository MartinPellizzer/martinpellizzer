Festuca borreri

Festuca borreri, commonly known as Borreri's fescue, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Festuca borreri thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
