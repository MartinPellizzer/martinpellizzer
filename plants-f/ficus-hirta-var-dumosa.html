Ficus hirta var. dumosa

Ficus hirta var. dumosa, commonly known as the woolly fig or hairy fig, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage, which gives it its common name. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with a glossy green surface.

Ficus hirta var. dumosa produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, spherical fruits that are often consumed by local wildlife and humans.
