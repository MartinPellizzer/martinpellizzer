Forsythia scandens

Forsythia scandens, commonly known as the creeping forsythia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and long, flexible stems that can climb or trail along the ground. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow, funnel-shaped flowers in early spring, which are highly ornamental. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and ovate, with a slightly serrated edge.

Forsythia scandens is often used in landscaping for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
