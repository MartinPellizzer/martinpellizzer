Festuca heterophylla var. nutans

Festuca heterophylla var. nutans, commonly known as Nuttall's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically reddish-brown in hue. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Festuca heterophylla var. nutans is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soils in arid environments.
