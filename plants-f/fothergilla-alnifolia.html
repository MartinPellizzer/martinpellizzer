Fothergilla alnifolia

Fothergilla alnifolia, commonly known as silverleaf fothergilla, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, clustered white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. The plant has aromatic, dark green leaves that turn yellow in autumn, adding ornamental value throughout the growing season. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

Fothergilla alnifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
