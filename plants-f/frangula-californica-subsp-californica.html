Frangula californica subsp. californica

Frangula californica subsp.

californica, commonly known as the California buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is found in coastal and inland regions of California.

The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring. Its fruit is a dark, elongated capsule that contains several seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
