Ficus xanthoxyla

Ficus xanthoxyla, commonly known as the yellow-fig or golden fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a slight yellowish tinge on the underside. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, inedible fruits.

Ficus xanthoxyla is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
