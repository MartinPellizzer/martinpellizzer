Ficus neriifolia

Ficus neriifolia, commonly known as the black-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, particularly India and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its dark green, often glossy leaves that can have a slightly waxy texture. Ficus neriifolia typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by fig-like fruits that are edible for some bird species but generally not consumed by humans.
