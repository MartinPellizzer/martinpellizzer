Ficus luteola

Ficus luteola, commonly known as the yellow fig or creeping fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including India, China, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its dense, trailing growth habit and small, glossy, dark green leaves. Ficus luteola produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fig fruits.

It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor environments.
