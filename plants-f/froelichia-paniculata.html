Froelichia paniculata

Froelichia paniculata, commonly known as the white mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, while its inflorescence consists of clustered, white to pale yellow flowers.

Froelichia paniculata is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
