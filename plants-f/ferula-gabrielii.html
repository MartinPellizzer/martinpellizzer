Ferula gabrielii

Ferula gabrielii, commonly known as the Gabriel's ferula, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the carrot family (Apiaceae) and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and finely divided, feathery leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a complex umbel, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic properties.

Ferula gabrielii thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.
