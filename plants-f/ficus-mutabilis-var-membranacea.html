Ficus mutabilis var. membranacea

Ficus mutabilis var. membranacea, commonly known as the Indian rubber plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by green, fleshy fruits resembling small figs. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

The plant is also noted for its adaptability to indoor environments, making it a popular houseplant.
