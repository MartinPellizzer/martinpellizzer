Ficus concentrica

Ficus concentrica, commonly known as the concentric fig, is a unique species native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, concentrically patterned leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant belongs to the Moraceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. Its leaves are typically broad and have a waxy texture, which helps in reducing water loss.

Ficus concentrica is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
