Ficus tournanensis

Ficus tournanensis, commonly known as the Tournan fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.

The Tournan fig is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and requires minimal care.
