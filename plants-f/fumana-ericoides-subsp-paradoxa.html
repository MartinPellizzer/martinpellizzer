Fumana ericoides subsp. paradoxa

Fumana ericoides subsp.

paradoxa, commonly known as the Paradoxical Heather, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa. It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers.

The plant typically produces white or pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer, which are borne on short, erect stalks. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, often with a slight waxy coating, and it thrives in acidic, well-drained soils.

This subspecies is notable for its unique floral structure and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
