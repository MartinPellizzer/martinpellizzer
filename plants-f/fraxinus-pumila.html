Fraxinus pumila

Fraxinus pumila, commonly known as the plains woodbine, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It typically grows in open habitats such as prairies, sandy soils, and disturbed areas. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are borne in clusters and are followed by winged fruits called samaras.

It plays an important role in supporting local wildlife and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
