Ferulago aucheri

Ferulago aucheri, commonly known as the Persian cowpea, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features slender, branching stems and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
