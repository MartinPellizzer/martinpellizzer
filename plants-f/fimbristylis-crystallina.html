Fimbristylis crystallina

Fimbristylis crystallina, commonly known as the glassy flatsedge, is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are usually inconspicuous, and the plant is often found in dense vegetation.

Fimbristylis crystallina is adapted to waterlogged soils and plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
