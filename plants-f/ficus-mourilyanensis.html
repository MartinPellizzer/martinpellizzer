Ficus mourilyanensis

Ficus mourilyanensis, commonly known as the Mourilyan ficus, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and distinctive aerial roots. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, often reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces edible figs that are important in local ecosystems.

Ficus mourilyanensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in its native habitat.
