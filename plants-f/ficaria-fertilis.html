Ficaria fertilis

Ficaria fertilis, commonly known as the fertilized figwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the Scrophulariaceae family.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Ficaria fertilis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
