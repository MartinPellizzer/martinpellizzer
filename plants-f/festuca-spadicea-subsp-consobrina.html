Festuca spadicea subsp. consobrina

Festuca spadicea subsp.

consobrina, commonly known as the purple false brome, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and along stream banks.

The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
