Festuca probatoviae

Festuca probatoviae, commonly known as the Probatovia fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Festuca probatoviae is often found in alpine and steppe regions, contributing to the ecological stability of these environments.
