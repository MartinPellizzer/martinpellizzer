Fagara foetida

Fagara foetida, commonly known as the stinking waxtree, is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often clustered at the ends of branches. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and typically white or greenish-yellow, arranged in clusters.

The fruit is a fleshy, berry-like structure that contains several seeds, and the plant is known for its strong, unpleasant odor, which is most noticeable when the leaves are crushed.
