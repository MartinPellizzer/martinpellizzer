Festuca × ripensis

Festuca × ripensis, commonly known as riparian fescue, is a hybrid grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found along riverbanks and wet meadows. Festuca × ripensis is valued for its erosion control capabilities and ability to tolerate periodic flooding.

Its dense root system helps stabilize soil and support biodiversity in riparian ecosystems.
