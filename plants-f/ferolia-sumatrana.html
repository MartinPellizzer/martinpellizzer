Ferolia sumatrana

Ferolia sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran ferolia, is a species of flowering plant in the citrus family, Rutaceae.

It is native to the island of Sumatra in Indonesia and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or orange when mature. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly rough texture.

Ferolia sumatrana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aromatic properties.
