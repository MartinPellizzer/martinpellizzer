Ficus carchiana

Ficus carchiana, commonly known as the Carpathian fig, is a species of flowering plant native to the Carpathian Mountains in Europe.

It is a small, deciduous tree or shrub that typically grows to a height of about 3 to 5 meters. The plant features simple, alternate, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, and it produces edible fig-like fruits that are an important food source for local wildlife.

Ficus carchiana is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological significance in its native habitat.
