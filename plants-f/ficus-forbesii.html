Ficus forbesii

Ficus forbesii, commonly known as the Forbe's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often heart-shaped or ovate in form. It produces small, clustered fruits known as figs, which are typically green when mature.

Ficus forbesii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
