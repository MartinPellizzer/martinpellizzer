Fritillaria carica subsp. serpenticola

Fritillaria carica subsp.

serpenticola, commonly known as the serpent's tongue fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep orange.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at high elevations, often in alpine environments. Its bulbs are adapted to survive harsh winters, and it blooms in late spring to early summer.

The subspecies is noted for its unique leaf structure, which is narrow and often twisted, giving it a distinctive appearance.
