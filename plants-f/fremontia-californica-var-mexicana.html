Fremontia californica var. mexicana

Fremontia californica var. mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican fremontia, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink to red in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid habitats.

The species is noted for its ecological importance as a food source for native pollinators and its ornamental value in xeriscaping.
