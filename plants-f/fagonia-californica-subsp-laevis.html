Fagonia californica subsp. laevis

Fagonia californica subsp.

laevis, commonly known as the smooth fagonia, is a shrub native to coastal areas of California and Baja California. It features aromatic, glandular leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its small, woody fruits are follicles that release seeds when mature.

It is adapted to sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal scrub habitats.
