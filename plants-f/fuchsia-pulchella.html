Fuchsia pulchella

Fuchsia pulchella, commonly known as the Chilean fuchsia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas, characterized by its trailing stems and vibrant flowers. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and purple, which bloom in clusters. Fuchsia pulchella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
