Felicia reflexa

Felicia reflexa, commonly known as the blueweed or blue daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a low-growing habit with hairy, narrow leaves that are typically opposite or alternate. Felicia reflexa thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
