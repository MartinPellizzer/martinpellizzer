Festuca laegaardii

Festuca laegaardii, commonly known as Laegaard's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to northern Europe.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous slender, two-ranked spikelets. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Festuca laegaardii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
