Ficus lasiophylla

Ficus lasiophylla, commonly known as the silver-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its glossy, silver-green leaves that have a waxy texture, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in indoor environments.

Ficus lasiophylla prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for houseplants.
