Foeniculum azoricum

Foeniculum azoricum, commonly known as Azorean fennel, is a perennial herb native to the Azores.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its aromatic, feathery leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in coastal areas. Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to common fennel.

The plant's essential oils contain compounds like anethole, which contribute to its distinctive flavor and medicinal properties.
