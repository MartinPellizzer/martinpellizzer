Ficus trachypison

Ficus trachypison, commonly known as the small-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically small and ovate in shape. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, pear-shaped fruits.

Ficus trachypison is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
