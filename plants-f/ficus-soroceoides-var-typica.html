Ficus soroceoides var. typica

Ficus soroceoides var. typica, commonly known as the African rubber plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to parts of Africa.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are typically elongated and have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by fruit that turns from green to yellow or red as it matures. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid environments.

Its latex sap, similar to that of other ficus species, can be used in traditional applications.
