Fibigia pendula

Fibigia pendula, commonly known as the hanging fibigia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Fibigia pendula is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
