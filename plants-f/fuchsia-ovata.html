Fuchsia ovata

Fuchsia ovata, commonly known as the orange fuchsia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange-red flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant produces pendulous clusters of tubular flowers, which attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Fuchsia ovata is a deciduous shrub that can grow up to 3 meters tall, with lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
