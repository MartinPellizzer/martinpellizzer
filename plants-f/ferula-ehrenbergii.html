Ferula ehrenbergii

Ferula ehrenbergii, commonly known as the Persian cow parsley, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to yellowish flowers. The plant produces a strong, aromatic resin that has been used in traditional medicine and perfumery. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

Ferula ehrenbergii thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates.
