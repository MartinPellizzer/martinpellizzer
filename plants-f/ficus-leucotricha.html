Ficus leucotricha

Ficus leucotricha, commonly known as the white-haired fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its distinctive silver or white hairy leaves, which give it its common name. It produces small, greenish-white fig fruits that are typically clustered in small groups.

Ficus leucotricha is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
