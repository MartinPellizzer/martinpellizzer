Ficus senegalensis

Ficus senegalensis, commonly known as the African fig or Senegal fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its large, lobed leaves and distinctive aerial roots. The plant typically grows as a tree or shrub, often reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its fruit, a small fig-like structure, is edible and serves as a food source for various animals.

Ficus senegalensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
