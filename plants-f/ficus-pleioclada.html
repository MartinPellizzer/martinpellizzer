Ficus pleioclada

Ficus pleioclada, commonly known as the broad-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits.

Ficus pleioclada is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
