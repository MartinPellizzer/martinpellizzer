Fragaria virginiana f. sibbaldifolia

Fragaria virginiana f. sibbaldifolia, commonly known as the Sibbald's strawberry, is a botanical variant of the wild strawberry.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, red berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are white with five petals and are often accompanied by yellow stamens.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its edible fruits.
