Fagonia arabica var. membranacea

Fagonia arabica var. membranacea, commonly known as the desert buckthorn, is a shrub native to arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and smooth, pale green leaves that are adapted to conserve water. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are a food source for various birds and small mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is highly drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for harsh desert environments.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitats.
