Festuca cinerea f. punctata

Festuca cinerea f. punctata, commonly known as silver hair grass, is a cool-season grass characterized by its silvery-blue foliage and fine, tufted growth habit.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features narrow, blade-like leaves with a bluish-gray hue, and its inflorescence consists of slender, spike-like clusters of small, greenish flowers. It is typically low-growing, reaching heights of 10 to 30 centimeters, and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded conditions.

Festuca cinerea f. punctata is also known for its adaptability to various soil types and its use in landscaping for erosion control.
