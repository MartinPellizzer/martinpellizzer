Ficus chiapensis

Ficus chiapensis, commonly known as the Chiapas fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central America, particularly in the mountainous areas of Chiapas, Mexico. The plant is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Ficus chiapensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
