Flemingia strobilifera var. bracteata

Flemingia strobilifera var. bracteata, commonly known as the bracteate flemingia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain several seeds. It is native to Southeast Asia and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental potential and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
