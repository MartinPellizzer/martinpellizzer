Fraxinus ornus var. juglandifolia

Fraxinus ornus var. juglandifolia, commonly known as the hazel-leaf ash, is a variant of the common ash tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive, compound leaves that resemble those of hazel, with elongated leaflets and a serrated margin. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in pendulous clusters, followed by winged fruits that disperse by wind. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
