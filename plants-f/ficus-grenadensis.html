Ficus grenadensis

Ficus grenadensis, commonly known as the Grenada fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible figs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Ficus grenadensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.
