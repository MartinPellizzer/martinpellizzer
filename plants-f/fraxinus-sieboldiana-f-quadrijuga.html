Fraxinus sieboldiana f. quadrijuga

Fraxinus sieboldiana f. quadrijuga, commonly known as the Japanese white ash, is a hybrid form of the ash tree that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It is a deciduous tree with smooth, gray bark that becomes fissured with age. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate, with a serrated margin and a glossy upper surface. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins, followed by samaroid seeds dispersed by wind.

It is native to Japan and is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
