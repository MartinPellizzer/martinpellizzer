Fraxinus xanthoxyloides f. dumosa

Fraxinus xanthoxyloides f. dumosa, commonly known as the dwarf white ash, is a compact form of the white ash tree.

It is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit, making it suitable for small spaces or as a hedge. The plant features small, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a pale yellowish-green color. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by winged samaras for seed dispersal.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.
