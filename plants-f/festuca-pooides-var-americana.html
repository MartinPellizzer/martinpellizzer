Festuca pooides var. americana

Festuca pooides var. americana, commonly known as American bluegrass, is a cool-season grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green blades and tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often used in turfgrass mixes for its durability and aesthetic appeal.

Botanically, it has narrow leaves with prominent veins and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.
