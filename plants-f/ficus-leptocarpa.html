Ficus leptocarpa

Ficus leptocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia, including parts of India, China, and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically green and glossy. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are often consumed by both humans and animals.

Ficus leptocarpa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
