Filipendula multijuga f. albiflora

Filipendula multijuga f. albiflora, commonly known as white-flowered meadowsweet, is a botanical variant of the meadowsweet plant native to the wetlands and moist meadows of eastern North America.

It is characterized by its pale white to cream-colored flowers, which contrast with the dark green foliage. The plant produces small, cylindrical flower clusters that bloom in late spring to early summer. It has a creeping rhizome system that allows it to spread and form dense colonies in suitable habitats.

Filipendula multijuga f. albiflora is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support pollinator populations.
