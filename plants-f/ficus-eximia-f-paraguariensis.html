Ficus eximia f. paraguariensis

Ficus eximia f. paraguariensis, commonly known as the Paraguayan fig, is a variant of the common fig species native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and local cuisine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
