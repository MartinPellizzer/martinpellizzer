Fedtschenkoa scorpioides

Fedtschenkoa scorpioides, commonly known as the scorpion-tongue plant, is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its striking, sword-shaped leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
