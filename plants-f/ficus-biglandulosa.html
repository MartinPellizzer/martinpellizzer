Ficus biglandulosa

Ficus biglandulosa, commonly known as the rough-leaved fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often covered in small, raised bumps or glands. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Ficus biglandulosa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and resilience.
