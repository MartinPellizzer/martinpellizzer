Flemingia strobilifera

Flemingia strobilifera, commonly known as the tropical milk bush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, cone-like fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Flemingia strobilifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
