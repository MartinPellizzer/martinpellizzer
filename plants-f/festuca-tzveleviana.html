Festuca tzveleviana

Festuca tzveleviana, commonly known as Tzvelev's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous narrow, awned flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Festuca tzveleviana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing rocky and alpine habitats.
