Ficus rukwaensis

Ficus rukwaensis, commonly known as the Rukwa fig, is a species of fig tree native to eastern Africa, specifically found in the Rukwa region of Tanzania.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with a broad, spreading crown and smooth, greyish-brown bark. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are typically clustered in syconia.

This plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
