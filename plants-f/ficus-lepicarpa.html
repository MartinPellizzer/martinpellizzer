Ficus lepicarpa

Ficus lepicarpa, commonly known as the rock fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and small, clustered flowers. It produces edible fruits that are typically consumed by birds and other animals.

Ficus lepicarpa is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
