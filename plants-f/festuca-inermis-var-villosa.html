Festuca inermis var. villosa

Festuca inermis var. villosa, commonly known as hairy fescue, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and dense, low-growing growth habit. The plant features silver-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands, meadows, and rocky areas.

Festuca inermis var. villosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in marginal environments.
