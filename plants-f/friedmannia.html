Friedmannia

Friedmannia, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to tropical Africa.

These plants are characterized by their rosettes of velvety, waxy leaves and their showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors. Friedmannia species are often cultivated as ornamental plants due to their aesthetic appeal and ease of care. They thrive in bright, indirect light and require well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

The genus includes several species, such as Friedmannia simplex, which is prized for its compact growth and vibrant blooms.
