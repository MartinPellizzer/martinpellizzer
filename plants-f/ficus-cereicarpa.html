Ficus cereicarpa

Ficus cereicarpa, commonly known as the red-fig or red-fruit fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered fruits that turn bright red when mature. The fruits are edible and often used in local cuisines.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
