Ferula gracilis

Ferula gracilis, commonly known as the slender ferula, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic compounds and hollow stems.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, where it thrives in sandy or rocky soils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the carrot family. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a distinctive silvery or grayish underside.

Ferula gracilis is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
