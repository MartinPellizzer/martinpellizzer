Faramea pendula

Faramea pendula, commonly known as the hanging faramea, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, pendulous inflorescences that bear small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins and a smooth, glossy surface. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Faramea pendula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
