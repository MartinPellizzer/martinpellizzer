Freesia caryophyllacea

Freesia caryophyllacea, commonly known as the freesia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing vibrant colors such as white, yellow, orange, and pink. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it prefers well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Freesias are often cultivated for their ornamental value in gardens and as cut flowers.
