Fragaria breslingea var. pratensis

Fragaria breslingea var. pratensis, commonly known as the alpine strawberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and white flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and unique flavor, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
