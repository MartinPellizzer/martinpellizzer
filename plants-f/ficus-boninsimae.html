Ficus boninsimae

Ficus boninsimae, commonly known as the Bonin ficus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the Bonin Islands, a group of islands located in the Pacific Ocean. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible figs, which are a key feature of its botanical profile.

This species is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.
