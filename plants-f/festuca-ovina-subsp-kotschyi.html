Festuca ovina subsp. kotschyi

Festuca ovina subsp.

kotschyi, commonly known as Kotschyi's sheepgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in dry, rocky, or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous florets. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands, slopes, and along roadsides.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in challenging environments.
