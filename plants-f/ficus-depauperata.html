Ficus depauperata

Ficus depauperata, commonly known as the "weeping fig," is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, drooping stems and small, heart-shaped leaves that give it a cascading appearance. The plant has a shallow root system, making it suitable for container gardening. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires regular watering to maintain its lush foliage.

Its unique form makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor ornamental purposes.
