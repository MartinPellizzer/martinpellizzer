Ficus hygrophila

Ficus hygrophila, commonly known as the water fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically broad and heart-shaped. The plant thrives in humid environments and requires consistent moisture in the soil. It is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.

Ficus hygrophila is also known for its ability to purify the air, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
