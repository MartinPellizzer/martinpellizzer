Ficus tulipifera

Ficus tulipifera, commonly known as the tulip tree or yellow fig, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often a deep green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by distinctive, showy yellowish-orange fruits resembling small lanterns. Ficus tulipifera thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening in tropical and subtropical regions.
