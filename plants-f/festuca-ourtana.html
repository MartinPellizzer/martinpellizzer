Festuca ourtana

Festuca ourtana, commonly known as the Turkish meadow fescue, is a perennial grass species native to regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils. The plant has fine, tufted leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It is characterized by its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in moderate climates.

Festuca ourtana is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and ability to stabilize soil.
