Ferdinandusa nitida

Ferdinandusa nitida, commonly known as the white cedar, is a small evergreen tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically followed by spherical, woody fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
