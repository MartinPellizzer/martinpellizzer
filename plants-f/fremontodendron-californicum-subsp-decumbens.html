Fremontodendron californicum subsp. decumbens

Fremontodendron californicum subsp.

decumbens, commonly known as the decumbent fremontodendron, is a shrub native to California and western Nevada. It features erect, branched stems covered in dense, gray-green foliage.

The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky habitats.

Its subspecies is distinguished by its prostrate growth habit compared to other Fremontodendron species.
