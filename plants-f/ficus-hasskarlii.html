Ficus hasskarlii

Ficus hasskarlii, commonly known as the African rubber plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically broad and heart-shaped. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by a fruit that is a smooth, greenish-white berry. It is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and air-purifying properties.

Ficus hasskarlii prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
