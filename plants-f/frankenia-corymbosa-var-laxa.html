Frankenia corymbosa var. laxa

Frankenia corymbosa var. laxa, commonly known as the coastal frankenia, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its small, fleshy leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in dune environments. Its adaptive features include salt tolerance and the ability to withstand strong coastal winds.

It plays an important role in stabilizing dune ecosystems and supporting local biodiversity.
