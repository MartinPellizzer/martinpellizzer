Ficus reineckei

Ficus reineckei, commonly known as the Beni Ficus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are followed by round, greenish-white fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Ficus reineckei is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor conditions.
