Fritillaria imperialis var. rubra-maxima

Fritillaria imperialis var. rubra-maxima, commonly known as the Crown Imperial Red-Maxima, is a striking bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, upright stems that bear clusters of large, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The flowers are typically arranged in a crown-like pattern at the top of the stem, giving the plant its common name. The leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and form a basal rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is prized for its vibrant colors and dramatic floral display, making it a popular choice in gardens and floral arrangements.
