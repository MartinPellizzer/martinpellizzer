Furcaria thalictroides

Furcaria thalictroides, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a distinctive, branched stem that often curls downward, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces showy, yellow or orange flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Furcaria thalictroides is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens.
