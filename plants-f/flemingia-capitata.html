Flemingia capitata

Flemingia capitata, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, clustered inflorescences that produce bright yellow flowers, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. Flemingia capitata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests or along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
