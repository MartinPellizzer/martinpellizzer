Ficus panamensis

Ficus panamensis, commonly known as the Panamanian fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to Central America, particularly Panama, and is typically found in tropical rainforests. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered fruits that are edible for certain animals. It plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing food and shelter.

Ficus panamensis is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
