Ficaria verna subsp. chrysocephala

Ficaria verna subsp.

chrysocephala, commonly known as the yellow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring.

The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are palmately lobed and covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more vibrant yellow coloration compared to the typical Ficaria verna.
