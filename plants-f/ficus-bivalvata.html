Ficus bivalvata

Ficus bivalvata, commonly known as the two-lobed fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves that are often lobed at the base. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically hidden within its unique syconium, a specialized fruit structure. Ficus bivalvata thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ornamental appeal.
