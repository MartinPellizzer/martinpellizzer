Freycinetia boluboluensis

Freycinetia boluboluensis, commonly known as the Bola-Bola palm, is a species native to the tropical regions of Indonesia.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family, characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and humid conditions.

Its fruit is a small, fibrous drupe that contains a single seed, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife.
