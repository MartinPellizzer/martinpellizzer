Ficus septica var. salicifolia

Ficus septica var. salicifolia, commonly known as the Salicifolia Fig, is a variant of the Ficus septica species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, bright green leaves that have a slightly serrated edge and a waxy texture. The plant produces small, fig-like fruits that are typically green when mature, though they may change color depending on the variety. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

The leaves are also known for their ability to filter air pollutants, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
