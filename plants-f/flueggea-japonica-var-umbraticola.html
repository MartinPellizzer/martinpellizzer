Flueggea japonica var. umbraticola

Flueggea japonica var. umbraticola, commonly known as the shade-fluuggea, is a variant of the Flueggea japonica species native to East Asia.

This plant is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a bluish tinge. It thrives in shaded or semi-shaded environments, making it well-suited for woodland or shaded garden settings. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, adding ornamental value.

Its adaptability to moist, well-drained soils makes it a versatile choice for horticultural use in temperate climates.
