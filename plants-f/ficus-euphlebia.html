Ficus euphlebia

Ficus euphlebia, commonly known as the white-veined fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand, Vietnam, and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with prominent white veins, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically small and round.

Ficus euphlebia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
