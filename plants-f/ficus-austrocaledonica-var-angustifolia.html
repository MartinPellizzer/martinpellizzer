Ficus austrocaledonica var. angustifolia

Ficus austrocaledonica var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Pacific Islands.

It features glossy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, green figs that ripen to a reddish hue. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for indoor cultivation in suitable environments.
