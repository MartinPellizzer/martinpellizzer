Festuca michelii var. subaristata

Festuca michelii var. subaristata, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada fescue, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, bluish-green foliage. The plant typically grows in high-elevation meadows and rocky slopes, preferring cool, moist conditions. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, spikelets.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in alpine environments.
