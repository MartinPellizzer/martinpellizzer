Firmiana pulcherrima

Firmiana pulcherrima, commonly known as the Chinese lantern tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, papery bracts that resemble small lanterns, which turn red or orange as they mature. The plant has compound leaves that are alternate and pinnately arranged, with small, ovate leaflets. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by woody pods containing seeds.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
