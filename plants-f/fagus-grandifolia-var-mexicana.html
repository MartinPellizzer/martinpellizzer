Fagus grandifolia var. mexicana

Fagus grandifolia var. mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican beech, is a variant of the American beech found in the mountainous regions of central Mexico.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and broad, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, spore-bearing cones and thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
