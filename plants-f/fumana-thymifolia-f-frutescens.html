Fumana thymifolia f. frutescens

Fumana thymifolia f. frutescens, commonly known as the fragrant thyme-leaved fumana, is a botanical variant of the species Fumana thymifolia.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous plant characterized by its small, aromatic leaves that resemble those of thyme. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
