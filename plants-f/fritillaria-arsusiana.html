Fritillaria arsusiana

Fritillaria arsusiana, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It produces striking bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its bulbs are adapted for storage and can remain dormant for extended periods.

Fritillaria arsusiana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitat.
