Festuca cinerea var. glauca

Festuca cinerea var. glauca, commonly known as blue hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical spike of small, spikelet-like flowers.

Festuca cinerea var. glauca is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic value and drought tolerance.
