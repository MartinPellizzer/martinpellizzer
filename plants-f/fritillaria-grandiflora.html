Fritillaria grandiflora

Fritillaria grandiflora, commonly known as the giant bulbous fritillary, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to deep purple, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces a cluster of strap-shaped leaves and has a bulbous underground stem that stores nutrients. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Fritillaria grandiflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
