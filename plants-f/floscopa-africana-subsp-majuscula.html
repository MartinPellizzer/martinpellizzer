Floscopa africana subsp. majuscula

Floscopa africana subsp.

majuscula, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
