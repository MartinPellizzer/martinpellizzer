Ficus anabatos

Ficus anabatos, commonly known as the climbing fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its woody stem and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically green when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often climbing trees or structures for support.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
