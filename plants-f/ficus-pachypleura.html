Ficus pachypleura

Ficus pachypleura, commonly known as the variegated ficus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that display a striking combination of green, white, and variegated patterns. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by round, greenish fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
