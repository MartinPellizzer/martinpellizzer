Fittingia conferta

Fittingia conferta, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Fittingia conferta is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
