Festuca xanthina var. klaslerskyi

Festuca xanthina var. klaslerskyi, commonly known as the Klaslerskyi goatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, silver-green foliage that reflects its common name. The plant produces dense clusters of small, pale yellow to golden inflorescences, which are typical of the Festuca genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a ground cover in rocky gardens.
