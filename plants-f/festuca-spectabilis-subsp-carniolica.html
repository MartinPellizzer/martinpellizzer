Festuca spectabilis subsp. carniolica

Festuca spectabilis subsp.

carniolica, commonly known as the Carniolan bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central and Eastern Europe. It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green foliage and produces dense, tufted growth habit.

The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and adaptable species suitable for various garden environments.
