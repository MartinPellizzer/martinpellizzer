Ficus hypochrysea

Ficus hypochrysea, commonly known as the white-veined fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with prominent white veins, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, green figs that mature to a yellowish hue, though they are not typically consumed by humans. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Ficus hypochrysea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to indoor conditions.
