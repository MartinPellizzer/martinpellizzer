Ficus ternatana

Ficus ternatana, commonly known as the Ternate fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are usually smooth and glossy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Ficus ternatana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local cultures.
