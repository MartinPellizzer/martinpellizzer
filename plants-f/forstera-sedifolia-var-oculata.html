Forstera sedifolia var. oculata

Forstera sedifolia var. oculata, commonly known as the eye-leaf plant, is a strikingly ornamental species native to South America.

It is characterized by its unique, waxy, and often brightly colored leaves that resemble eyes, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as a compact shrub with a bushy appearance, and its leaves are arranged in a rosette pattern. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink, adding to its visual appeal.

This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.
