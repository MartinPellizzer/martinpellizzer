Fraxinus americana subvar. aucubifolia

Fraxinus americana subvar. aucubifolia, commonly known as the white ash, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and typically have 5 to 9 leaflets. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by wind. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

The plant is often found in moist, well-drained soils along riverbanks and in floodplain forests.
