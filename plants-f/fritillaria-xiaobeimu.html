Fritillaria xiaobeimu

Fritillaria xiaobeimu, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a perennial bulbous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant produces a single, upright stem bearing several flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its bulbs are often used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
