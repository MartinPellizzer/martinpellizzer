Franseria pumila

Franseria pumila, commonly known as the dwarf franseria, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 cm, featuring compact, rounded foliage composed of small, leathery leaves. The plant produces delicate, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the spring and early summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or container planting.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in temperate climates.
