Ferocactus uncinatus var. wrightii

Ferocactus uncinatus var. wrightii, commonly known as the Wright's hooked barrel cactus, is a succulent native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are ribbed and covered in spines, which provide protection from herbivores and reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green, spiky form. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a resilient and visually distinctive member of the Cactaceae family.
