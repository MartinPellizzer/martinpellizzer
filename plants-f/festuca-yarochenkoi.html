Festuca yarochenkoi

Festuca yarochenkoi, commonly known as Yarochenko's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky and well-drained soils, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous florets. It is adapted to cold climates and can withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Festuca yarochenkoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in mountainous areas.
