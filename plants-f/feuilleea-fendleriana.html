Feuilleea fendleriana

Feuilleea fendleriana, commonly known as the Fendler's featherplant, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage and small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of blue and purple. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along canyon walls. It has a low-growing, mat-forming habit with hairy stems and leaves that help reduce water loss.

Feuilleea fendleriana is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
