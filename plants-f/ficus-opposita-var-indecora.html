Ficus opposita var. indecora

Ficus opposita var. indecora, commonly known as the weeping fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that give it a distinctive, cascading appearance. The leaves are typically broad, glossy, and dark green, arranged oppositely along the stems. It is a member of the Moraceae family and is often cultivated as a decorative indoor plant.

However, it can be sensitive to changes in temperature and light, requiring careful maintenance to thrive.
