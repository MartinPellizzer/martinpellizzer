Fimbristylis leptoclada

Fimbristylis leptoclada, commonly known as the slender bristle sedge, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flower heads. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along streambanks. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
