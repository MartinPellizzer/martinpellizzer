Ficus oldhamii

Ficus oldhamii, commonly known as the oldham's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries such as Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often heart-shaped or ovate in form. It produces small, clustered figs that are typically green when mature, though they may change color depending on the variety.

Ficus oldhamii is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor conditions.
