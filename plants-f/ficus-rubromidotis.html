Ficus rubromidotis

Ficus rubromidotis, commonly known as the red-veined fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is characterized by its striking foliage, featuring dark green leaves with prominent red veins. Native to Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in humid, tropical environments. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a glossy texture.

It is often cultivated as an indoor plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
