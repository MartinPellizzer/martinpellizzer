Ficus bonatii

Ficus bonatii, commonly known as the Bonati fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the island of Sicily and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often lobed or serrated. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible fig fruits, which are typically green when mature. Ficus bonatii thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

It is also valued for its ability to adapt to various soil types and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
