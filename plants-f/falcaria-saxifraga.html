Falcaria saxifraga

Falcaria saxifraga, commonly known as the rock saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to rocky and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae, which includes plants known for their distinctive nodes and often edible roots. The plant has a creeping stem with upright flowering shoots, and its leaves are typically basal, lanceolate, and covered in fine hairs. It produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink or white, which are pollinated by insects.

Falcaria saxifraga is adapted to harsh environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
