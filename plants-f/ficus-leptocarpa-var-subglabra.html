Ficus leptocarpa var. subglabra

Ficus leptocarpa var. subglabra, commonly known as the smooth-leaved fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its smooth, glossy leaves that are usually lance-shaped and have a deep green color. It produces small, clustered fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow when mature.

This variety is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
