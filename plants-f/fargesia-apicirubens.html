Fargesia apicirubens

Fargesia apicirubens, commonly known as the red-tipped bamboo, is a species of flowering bamboo native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, upright culms that are often tinged with a reddish hue at the tips. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, appearing in clusters. Fargesia apicirubens is a clump-forming plant that grows in dense stands, often in moist, shaded environments.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
