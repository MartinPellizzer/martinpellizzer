Fimbristylis minuticulmis

Fimbristylis minuticulmis, commonly known as the minute bristle sedge, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Pacific Islands. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with fine, hair-like structures along the edges, giving it its common name.

The flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, which are characteristic of the sedge family.
