Fritillaria camschatcensis subsp. alpina

Fritillaria camschatcensis subsp.

alpina, commonly known as the alpine fritillary, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically orange or yellow in color, often with contrasting red or purple markings.

The plant's leaves are grass-like, basal, and often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
