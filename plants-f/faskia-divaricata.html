Faskia divaricata

Faskia divaricata, commonly known as the variable thornbush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its branched, often thorny stems and small, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. Faskia divaricata is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and animals.
