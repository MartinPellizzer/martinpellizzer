Festuca segimonensis

Festuca segimonensis, commonly known as the Segimon grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and dark green, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Festuca segimonensis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil in harsh conditions.
