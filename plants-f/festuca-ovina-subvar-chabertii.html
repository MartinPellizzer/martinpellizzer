Festuca ovina subvar. chabertii

Festuca ovina subvar. chabertii, commonly known as Chabert's sheep's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and rocky slopes. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
