Fritillaria unibracteata var. maculata

Fritillaria unibracteata var. maculata, commonly known as the spotted bellflower, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink with distinctive red or purple spots. The plant produces a single, upright stem that bears one to several flowers, which bloom in early spring. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a basal rosette of bulbs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
