Fimbristylis ferruginea var. sieboldii

Fimbristylis ferruginea var. sieboldii, commonly known as the rusty sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, preferring moist, well-drained soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are usually pale green to brown, and it blooms during the wet season.

This variant is distinguished by its reddish-brown hues on the leaves and stems, giving it its common name.
