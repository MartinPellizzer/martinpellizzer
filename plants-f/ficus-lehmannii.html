Ficus lehmannii

Ficus lehmannii, commonly known as the Lehmman's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often heart-shaped or ovate in form. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig-like fruits.

Ficus lehmannii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
