Fraxinus excelsior f. monophylla-pendula

Fraxinus excelsior f. monophylla-pendula, commonly known as the weeping ash, is a distinctive variation of the common ash tree.

It is characterized by its pendulous branches that create a cascading effect, giving it an ornamental appearance. The leaves are typically smaller and more elongated compared to the standard species, often with a slightly serrated edge. This form thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and parks.

Its unique shape and foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
