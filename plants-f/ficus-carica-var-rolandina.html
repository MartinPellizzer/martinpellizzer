Ficus carica var. rolandina

Ficus carica var. rolandina, commonly known as the Rolandina fig, is a cultivar of the common fig tree.

It is characterized by its small, round, and edible fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its contribution to local food systems.
