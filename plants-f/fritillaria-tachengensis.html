Fritillaria tachengensis

Fritillaria tachengensis, commonly known as the Tacheng fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves and has a thick, fleshy bulb that stores nutrients for growth. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical studies.
