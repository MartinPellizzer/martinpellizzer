Ficus diversifolia var. lutescens

Ficus diversifolia var. lutescens, commonly known as the golden fig or yellow-leaf fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, broad, heart-shaped leaves that often display a striking yellow or golden hue. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by fig-like fruits that are typically green or yellow when mature. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires indirect sunlight for optimal growth.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and ornamental purposes.
