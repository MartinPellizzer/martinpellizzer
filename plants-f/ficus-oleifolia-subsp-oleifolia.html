Ficus oleifolia subsp. oleifolia

Ficus oleifolia subsp.

oleifolia, commonly known as the olive fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, round fig fruits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
