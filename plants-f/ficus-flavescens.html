Ficus flavescens

Ficus flavescens, commonly known as the yellow fig tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a slight yellowish tint. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig-like fruits that are yellow when ripe. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

Ficus flavescens is also valued in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
