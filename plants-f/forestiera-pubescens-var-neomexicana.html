Forestiera pubescens var. neomexicana

Forestiera pubescens var. neomexicana, commonly known as the New Mexico forestiera, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping in drought-prone regions.
