Faucaria coronata

Faucaria coronata, commonly known as the Crown of Thorns, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the growing season, typically in spring. Its leaves are often arranged in a rosette pattern, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in well-draining soil.

Faucaria coronata is also notable for its resilience to drought and its ability to propagate through offsets.
