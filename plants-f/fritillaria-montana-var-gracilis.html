Fritillaria montana var. gracilis

Fritillaria montana var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous root system and upright, grass-like foliage. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
