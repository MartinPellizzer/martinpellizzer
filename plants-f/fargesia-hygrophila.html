Fargesia hygrophila

Fargesia hygrophila, commonly known as the water fargesia, is a species of bamboo native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often a deep green color. This plant prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens. Its growth habit is clumping, and it produces small, white flowers in the spring.

Fargesia hygrophila is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in humid environments.
