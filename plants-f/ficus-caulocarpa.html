Ficus caulocarpa

Ficus caulocarpa, commonly known as the red-fig or red-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often have a reddish tinge on the underside. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, round fruits that turn red when mature.

Ficus caulocarpa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
