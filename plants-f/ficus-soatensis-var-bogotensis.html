Ficus soatensis var. bogotensis

Ficus soatensis var. bogotensis, commonly known as the Bogotá ficus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the Andean regions of Colombia, particularly in the high-altitude areas around Bogotá. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a smooth texture. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible figs that are enclosed in a fleshy, greenish husk.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and indoor spaces due to its ability to thrive in a range of environmental conditions.
