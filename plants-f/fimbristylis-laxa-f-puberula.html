Fimbristylis laxa f. puberula

Fimbristylis laxa f. puberula, commonly known as the hairy spikerush, is a variant of the spikerush genus Fimbristylis.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescence and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its foliage is covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
