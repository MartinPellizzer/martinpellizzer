Fagus sylvatica var. pendula

Fagus sylvatica var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping beech, is a striking variant of the common beech.

It is characterized by its cascading, drooping branches that create a unique, ethereal appearance. The plant is native to Europe and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. Its leaves are glossy, dark green, and turn yellow in the autumn.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and can reach heights of up to 20 meters.
