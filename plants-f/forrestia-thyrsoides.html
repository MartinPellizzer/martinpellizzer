Forrestia thyrsoides

Forrestia thyrsoides, commonly known as the sweetshrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, showy clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly serrated margin. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

The shrub is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
