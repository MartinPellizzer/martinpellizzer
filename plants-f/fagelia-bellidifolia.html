Fagelia bellidifolia

Fagelia bellidifolia, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

Fagelia bellidifolia produces small, berry-like fruits that contain several seeds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.
