Ficus pandurata var. angustifolia

Ficus pandurata var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically pear-shaped. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
