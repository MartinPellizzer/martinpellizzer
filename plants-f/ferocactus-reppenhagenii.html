Ferocactus reppenhagenii

Ferocactus reppenhagenii, commonly known as the Paddle Cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its flattened, paddle-shaped stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh desert conditions. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers during the summer months, which are typically followed by red, spherical fruits. Its spines are dense and can be either straight or curved, providing protection from herbivores.

Ferocactus reppenhagenii is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in full sunlight and well-draining soil.
