Festuca altaica subsp. hallii

Festuca altaica subsp.

hallii, commonly known as Hall's tall fescue, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces spikelets that are usually arranged in dense clusters, with small, two-lobed florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and restoration projects.
