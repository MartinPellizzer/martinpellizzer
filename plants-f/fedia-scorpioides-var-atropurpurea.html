Fedia scorpioides var. atropurpurea

Fedia scorpioides var. atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple-flowered fedia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking purple to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are upright and branching.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
