Ficus populnea subvar. subdepressa

Ficus populnea subvar. subdepressa, commonly known as the dwarf fig tree, is a compact, slow-growing species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, ovate leaves that are typically smaller than those of its parent species, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are edible and attract various wildlife. It thrives in tropical climates and requires well-draining soil and moderate watering.

This variety is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its manageable size and attractive foliage.
