Fargesia frigidis

Fargesia frigidis, commonly known as the cold mountain bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to the high altitudes of western China.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. This plant is adapted to cold climates and can thrive in USDA hardiness zones 5 to 7. Its growth habit is clump-forming, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring.

Fargesia frigidis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to add texture to shaded garden spaces.
