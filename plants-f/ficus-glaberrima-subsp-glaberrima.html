Ficus glaberrima subsp. glaberrima

Ficus glaberrima subsp.

glaberrima, commonly known as the African rubber fig, is a species native to tropical Africa. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, heart-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by figs that are typically green and turn yellow when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
