Festuca pratensis var. loliacea

Festuca pratensis var. loliacea, commonly known as sweetgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and bright green, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and pastures.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated for both landscaping and forage purposes.
