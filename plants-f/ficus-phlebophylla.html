Ficus phlebophylla

Ficus phlebophylla, commonly known as the veiny fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves that have a distinctive veining pattern, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible figs, which are typically green when mature. It thrives in humid environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage.

Ficus phlebophylla is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
