Fuchsia ricoartonii

Fuchsia ricoartonii, commonly known as the richardson's fuchsia, is a species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces showy, pendulous flowers in shades of red, pink, and purple. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its stems are woody and branched. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
