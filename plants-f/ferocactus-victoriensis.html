Ferocactus victoriensis

Ferocactus victoriensis, commonly known as the Victoria's barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh desert conditions. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its spines are modified leaves that help reduce water loss and provide protection from herbivores.

Ferocactus victoriensis is well adapted to full sun and requires minimal water, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.
