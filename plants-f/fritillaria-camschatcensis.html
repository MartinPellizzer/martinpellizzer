Fritillaria camschatcensis

Fritillaria camschatcensis, commonly known as the Crown Imperial, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of orange, yellow, and red, often arranged in a crown-like formation at the top of tall, slender stems. The plant produces large, rounded bulbs that store nutrients, allowing it to survive in harsh, arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, emerging from the base of the plant, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

Fritillaria camschatcensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its dramatic floral display.
