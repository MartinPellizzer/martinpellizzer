Floydia praealta

Floydia praealta, commonly known as the giant floydia, is a unique member of the Bromeliaceae family.

It is native to the tropical rainforests of South America, particularly Brazil. This plant is characterized by its large, strap-like leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. It produces striking, bell-shaped inflorescences with bright red flowers that attract pollinators.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a challenging plant to cultivate outside its natural environment.
