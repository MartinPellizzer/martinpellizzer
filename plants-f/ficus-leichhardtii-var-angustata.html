Ficus leichhardtii var. angustata

Ficus leichhardtii var. angustata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fig, is a tropical plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible figs that are a food source for various wildlife. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated as a houseplant in temperate regions.
