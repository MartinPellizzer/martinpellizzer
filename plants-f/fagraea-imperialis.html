Fagraea imperialis

Fagraea imperialis, commonly known as the imperial glory tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves. The tree produces showy, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody, orange-red fruits containing seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape tree in warm climates.
