Fagus longipetiolata

Fagus longipetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled beech, is a species of beech native to southeastern China.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elongated petioles that give it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a doubly serrated margin and a hairy underside. It produces small, pale yellow flowers in the spring followed by edible, triangular nuts.

This plant thrives in temperate forests and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
