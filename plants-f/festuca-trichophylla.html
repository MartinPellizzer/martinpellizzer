Festuca trichophylla

Festuca trichophylla, commonly known as the hairy fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, spikelet-based flowers. Festuca trichophylla is adapted to cool, moist environments and thrives in well-drained soils.

It is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
