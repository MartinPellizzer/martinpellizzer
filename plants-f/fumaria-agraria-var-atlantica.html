Fumaria agraria var. atlantica

Fumaria agraria var. atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic celandine, is a herbaceous plant native to the coastal regions of the Atlantic coast.

It features slender, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow-orange flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly rough texture.

This variety is adapted to sandy or rocky soils and thrives in coastal environments with high salt tolerance.
