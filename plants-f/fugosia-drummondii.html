Fugosia drummondii

Fugosia drummondii, commonly known as the Drummond's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, pale green to blue-gray flowers. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Fugosia drummondii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
