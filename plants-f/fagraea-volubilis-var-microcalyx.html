Fagraea volubilis var. microcalyx

Fagraea volubilis var. microcalyx, commonly known as the dwarf star apple, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or red when ripe, containing a single seed. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a slightly serrated margin.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits in home gardens.
