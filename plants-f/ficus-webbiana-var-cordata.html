Ficus webbiana var. cordata

Ficus webbiana var. cordata, commonly known as the cordate fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are glossy and typically dark green. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig-like fruits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its adaptability makes it suitable for both indoor and outdoor gardening in suitable climates.
