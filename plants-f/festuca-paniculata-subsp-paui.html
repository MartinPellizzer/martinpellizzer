Festuca paniculata subsp. paui

Festuca paniculata subsp.

paui, commonly known as the Appalachian bluegrass, is a cool-season grass native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and tufted inflorescences that produce small, spike-like flowers.

This subspecies thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas and along stream banks. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to form dense, durable turf.

The plant is also noted for its ecological importance, providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
