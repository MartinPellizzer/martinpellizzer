Ficus jambiensis

Ficus jambiensis, commonly known as the Jambien fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are typically green when mature. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil.

Ficus jambiensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in indoor settings.
