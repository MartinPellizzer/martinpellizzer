Ficus nervosa subsp. minor

Ficus nervosa subsp.

minor, commonly known as the small-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family. It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Vietnam, and Malaysia.

The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It produces small, fig-like fruits that are edible and often consumed by local wildlife.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
