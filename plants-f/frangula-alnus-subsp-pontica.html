Frangula alnus subsp. pontica

Frangula alnus subsp.

pontica, commonly known as the Pontic alder, is a shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and alternate, simple, ovate leaves that are often pubescent beneath.

The plant produces small, clustered yellowish-green flowers followed by dark, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing riverbanks.
