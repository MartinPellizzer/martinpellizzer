Festuca ovina subsp. airoides

Festuca ovina subsp.

airoides, commonly known as bluegrass or sheep's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and its ability to form dense, sod-forming growth.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and woodland edges. Its leaves are narrow and bluish-green, with a distinctive tufted appearance at the base.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in stabilizing soil in various habitats.
