Fuchsia tacanensis

Fuchsia tacanensis, commonly known as the Tacana fuchsia, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a shrub-like plant characterized by its vibrant, pendulous flowers that range in color from deep purple to bright red. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Fuchsia tacanensis thrives in cooler, humid climates and is often found in cloud forests.

Its ornamental value has made it a sought-after plant in horticultural collections.
