Festuca ovina subvar. puberula

Festuca ovina subvar. puberula, commonly known as sheep's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered with fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Its dense growth makes it a valuable plant for erosion control and as a component of native grassland ecosystems.
