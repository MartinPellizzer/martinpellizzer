Ficus eximia var. cubensis

Ficus eximia var. cubensis, commonly known as the Cuban fig tree, is a species native to Cuba and parts of the Caribbean.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with smooth, grayish bark and large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig-like fruits that are popular in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive foliage.

The species is also valued for its ability to tolerate a range of soil types and environmental conditions.
