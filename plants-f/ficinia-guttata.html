Ficinia guttata

Ficinia guttata, commonly known as the spotted flax, is a perennial herb native to New Zealand.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in a fine, white coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in loose spikes, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a distinctive waxy texture.

Ficinia guttata is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.
