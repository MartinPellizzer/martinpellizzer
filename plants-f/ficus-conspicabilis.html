Ficus conspicabilis

Ficus conspicabilis, commonly known as the breadfruit tree, is a tropical plant native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is a large evergreen tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, featuring broad, heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by the characteristic breadfruit, which is a starchy, edible fruit. Its root system is robust, allowing it to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in tropical ecosystems.
