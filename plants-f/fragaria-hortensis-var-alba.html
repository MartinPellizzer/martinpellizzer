Fragaria hortensis var. alba

Fragaria hortensis var. alba, commonly known as the white strawberry, is a variety of the strawberry plant characterized by its pale pink to white flowers and small, edible fruits.

This plant is a perennial herb that thrives in temperate climates, typically growing in well-drained soil and partial shade. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, and it produces runners that can be used for propagation. The flowers are usually solitary and appear in early spring, followed by the development of its distinctive, small, white-fruited berries.

It is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its mild, sweet flavor.
