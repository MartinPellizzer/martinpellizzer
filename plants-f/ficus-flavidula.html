Ficus flavidula

Ficus flavidula, commonly known as the yellow fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a yellowish underside. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are typically yellow when ripe. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to thrive in warm, humid climates.

The plant is also known for its ability to tolerate partial shade and is relatively easy to care for in indoor settings.
