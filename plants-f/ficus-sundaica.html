Ficus sundaica

Ficus sundaica, commonly known as the Sunda fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible fig fruits, which are an important food source for various wildlife. Ficus sundaica thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in tropical regions.
