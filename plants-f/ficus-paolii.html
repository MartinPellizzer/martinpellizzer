Ficus paolii

Ficus paolii, commonly known as the Pauli fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the island of Sulawesi in Indonesia and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into edible figs, though the fruit is not typically consumed by humans. Ficus paolii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor environments.

It requires bright, indirect light and moderate humidity to thrive.
