Ficus nerium

Ficus nerium, commonly known as the laurel fig or sacred fig, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its evergreen, glossy dark green leaves and small, inconspicuous white flowers. The plant produces small, greenish fruits that turn red when mature. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate sunlight.

Ficus nerium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in various cultures.
