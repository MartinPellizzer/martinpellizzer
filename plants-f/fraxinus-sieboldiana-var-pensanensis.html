Fraxinus sieboldiana var. pensanensis

Fraxinus sieboldiana var. pensanensis, commonly known as the Japanese ash or Japanese white ash, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves that are oval to elliptic in shape. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins during the spring, followed by samaras with two papery wings. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems.
