Fagara hassleriana var. angustifolia

Fagara hassleriana var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf soursop, is a member of the Anacardiaceae family.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically narrow and elongated, with a glossy green upper surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by green, spiny fruits that turn yellow when mature. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments and is native to regions of Central and South America.
