Ficus cardinalicarpa

Ficus cardinalicarpa, commonly known as the Red-veined Fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with deep red veins contrasting against its dark green background. Ficus cardinalicarpa is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in indoor conditions.

It produces small, edible figs, though these are typically not consumed by humans.
