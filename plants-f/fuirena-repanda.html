Fuirena repanda

Fuirena repanda, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a low-growing, spreading habit with slender, often hairy stems that root at the nodes. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lanceolate, with a rough texture and a slightly hairy surface.

Fuirena repanda thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
