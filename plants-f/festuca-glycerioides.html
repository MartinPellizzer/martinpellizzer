Festuca glycerioides

Festuca glycerioides, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray blades and tufted growth habit, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its visual appeal and low maintenance requirements.

Festuca glycerioides is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in dry, rocky environments.
