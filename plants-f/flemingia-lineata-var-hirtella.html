Flemingia lineata var. hirtella

Flemingia lineata var. hirtella, commonly known as the hairy flemingia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in humid, lowland areas. The plant features small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the wet season. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and covered with fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
