Foetidia

Foetidia, commonly known as the stinkweed, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its small, often inconspicuous flowers and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is found in various parts of Africa and Asia. Foetidia species are known for their strong, unpleasant odor, which is most noticeable when the plant is crushed.

Due to its medicinal properties, some species of Foetidia have been used in traditional herbal remedies.
