Fragaria virginiana australis

Fragaria virginiana australis, commonly known as the southern mountain strawberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, white flowers that develop into edible, red berries. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks. Its berries are not only flavorful but also serve as a food source for various wildlife species.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in native plant communities.
