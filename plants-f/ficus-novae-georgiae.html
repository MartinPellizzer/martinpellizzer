Ficus novae-georgiae

Ficus novae-georgiae, commonly known as the New Georgia fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of the South Pacific, specifically the island of New Georgia in the Solomon Islands. The plant is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are usually green when mature.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
