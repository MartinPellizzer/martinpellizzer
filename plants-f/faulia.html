Faulia

Faulia, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Campanulaceae.

These plants are characterized by their bell-shaped or tubular flowers, which often display vibrant colors such as yellow, orange, or red. Faulia species are typically herbaceous perennials, often found in temperate regions with well-drained soils. They produce small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

The leaves are usually opposite and lance-shaped, with some species having hairy surfaces.
