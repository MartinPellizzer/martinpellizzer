Ferula tingitana

Ferula tingitana, commonly known as the North African giant fennel, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, feathery leaves that resemble those of a fennel plant. The plant produces yellow flowers in umbrella-shaped clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its taproot is thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Ferula tingitana is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
