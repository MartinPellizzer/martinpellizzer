Fritillaria pallidiflora var. halabulanica

Fritillaria pallidiflora var. halabulanica, commonly known as the Halabula fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pale yellow to orange in color, often with intricate patterns. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to temperate to cold climates. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge in early spring.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
