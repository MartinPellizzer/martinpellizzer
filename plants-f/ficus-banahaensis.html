Ficus banahaensis

Ficus banahaensis, commonly known as the Banah Valley fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in parts of Thailand and Vietnam. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered fig fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland evergreen forests.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
