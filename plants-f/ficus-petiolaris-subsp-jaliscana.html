Ficus petiolaris subsp. jaliscana

Ficus petiolaris subsp.

jaliscana, commonly known as the Jalisco fig, is a species native to Mexico. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with broad, glossy, dark green leaves.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, green figs that ripen to a reddish-purple color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
