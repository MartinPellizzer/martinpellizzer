Fatsia japonica var. liukiuensis

Fatsia japonica var. liukiuensis, commonly known as the Ryukyu fatsia, is a variant of the fatsia plant native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces small, white, umbellate flowers in the spring, followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Botanically, it belongs to the Araceae family and is closely related to other members of the fatsia genus.
