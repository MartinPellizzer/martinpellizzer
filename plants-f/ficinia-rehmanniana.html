Ficinia rehmanniana

Ficinia rehmanniana, commonly known as the Rehmann's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the Cyperaceae family.

Ficinia rehmanniana is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
