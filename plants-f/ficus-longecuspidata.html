Ficus longecuspidata

Ficus longecuspidata, commonly known as the long-pointed fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand and Malaysia, and is characterized by its elongated, pointed leaves. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are glossy and ovate, with a serrated margin, and it produces small, clustered fig fruits.

Ficus longecuspidata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable environments.
