Festuca hervieri

Festuca hervieri, commonly known as Hervier's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along riverbanks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny blue to purple flowers. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.

Festuca hervieri is also known for its ability to thrive in partial shade and its tolerance to various environmental conditions.
