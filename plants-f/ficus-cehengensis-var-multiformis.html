Ficus cehengensis var. multiformis

Ficus cehengensis var. multiformis, commonly known as the Guangdong fig, is a species native to southern China.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are often used in traditional Chinese cuisine. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The varietal form, multiformis, is distinguished by its variable leaf shapes and sizes.
