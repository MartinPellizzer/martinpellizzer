Fraxinus angustifolia f. elliptica

Fraxinus angustifolia f. elliptica, commonly known as the narrowleaf ash, is a cultivar of the ash tree characterized by its elliptical-shaped leaves.

It is a deciduous tree with a slender, upright growth habit and smooth, gray bark. The leaves are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath, with a smooth margin. This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for urban environments.
