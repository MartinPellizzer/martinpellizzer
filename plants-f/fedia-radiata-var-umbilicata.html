Fedia radiata var. umbilicata

Fedia radiata var. umbilicata, commonly known as the umbilicated fedia, is a rare and unique variant of the fedia genus.

It is characterized by its distinctive umbilicated or navel-like marking on the fruit, which sets it apart from other varieties. The plant is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. Its leaves are typically broad and lance-shaped, with a waxy surface that aids in water retention.

This variant is of interest to botanists due to its morphological uniqueness and potential ecological significance.
