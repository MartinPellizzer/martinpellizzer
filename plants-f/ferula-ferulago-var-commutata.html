Ferula ferulago var. commutata

Ferula ferulago var. commutata, commonly known as the Italian fennel, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a tall, slender stem that can grow up to 2 meters in height, covered with fine, hairy leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in umbel-like clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its aromatic, fern-like leaves are often used in culinary applications, particularly in Mediterranean cuisine.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties, traditionally used to treat digestive issues and as a carminative.
