Filix-foemina aquilina

Filix-foemina aquilina, commonly known as the dragon's tail, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its long, creeping rhizomes and pinnate fronds that are often covered in a dense layer of brownish scales. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in temperate regions with high humidity. Its fronds are usually dark green and can grow up to several feet in length, making it a striking addition to shaded gardens.

Filix-foemina aquilina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
