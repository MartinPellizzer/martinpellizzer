Ficus religiosa var. cordata

Ficus religiosa var. cordata, commonly known as the cordate fig tree, is a variant of the sacred fig native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which give it its scientific name "cordata.". The plant is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a broad, spreading canopy. Its fruit, known as the fig, is an important food source for various animals and humans.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in some regions.
