Faberia thibetica

Faberia thibetica, commonly known as the Tibetan Faberia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Tibet.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, adapting to the harsh climate of high altitudes.

Faberia thibetica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and resilience.
