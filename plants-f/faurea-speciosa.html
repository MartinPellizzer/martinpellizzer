Faurea speciosa

Faurea speciosa, commonly known as the Santa Barbara daisy, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of California and Baja California.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, showcasing its adaptability to coastal climates. Its flowers, which bloom in the spring, are white with yellow centers, and the plant produces small, spiny fruits.

Faurea speciosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
