Ficus eucaudata

Ficus eucaudata, commonly known as the velvet fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. Ficus eucaudata produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences.

It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
