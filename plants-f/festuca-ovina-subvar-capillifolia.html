Festuca ovina subvar. capillifolia

Festuca ovina subvar. capillifolia, commonly known as silky bluegrass, is a cool-season grass native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and open woodlands. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and covered with short, soft hairs, giving it a silky appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
