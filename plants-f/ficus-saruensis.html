Ficus saruensis

Ficus saruensis, commonly known as the Sarawak fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible figs that are typically green when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forest areas.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
