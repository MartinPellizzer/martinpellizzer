Ferula aciphylla

Ferula aciphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ferula, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes plants like carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its aromatic, often hairy foliage. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. Its roots are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in arid environments.

Ferula aciphylla is also noted for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies for digestive and anti-inflammatory purposes.
