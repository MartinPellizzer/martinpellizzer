Ficus catalpifolia

Ficus catalpifolia, commonly known as the Catalpa fig tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to southern China and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces edible figs that are typically harvested when they are fully ripe and soft. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ficus catalpifolia is also valued for its ability to adapt to various soil types and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
