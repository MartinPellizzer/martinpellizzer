Fritillaria chia

Fritillaria chia, commonly known as the Turk's cap lily, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red, often with intricate patterns. The plant features a fibrous root system and thick, fleshy bulbs that store nutrients for seasonal growth. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, emerging from the base of the plant.

Fritillaria chia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
