Ficus curranii

Ficus curranii, commonly known as the Curran's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig fruits.

Ficus curranii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor and outdoor environments.
