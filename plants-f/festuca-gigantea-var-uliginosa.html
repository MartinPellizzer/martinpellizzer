Festuca gigantea var. uliginosa

Festuca gigantea var. uliginosa, commonly known as the giant bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and blue-green foliage, which can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense stands, which can help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

The inflorescence consists of spikelets that are typically greenish or bluish in color, adding a distinctive visual element to its habitat.
