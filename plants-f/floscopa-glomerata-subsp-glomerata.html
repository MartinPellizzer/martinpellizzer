Floscopa glomerata subsp. glomerata

Floscopa glomerata subsp.

glomerata, commonly known as the clustered floscopa, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its compact, dense growth habit and produces small, clustered inflorescences of white to pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open woodland or shrubland habitats. Its botanical features include narrow, linear leaves and a short, upright stem.

It is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
