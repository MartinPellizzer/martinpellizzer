Freziera salicifolia

Freziera salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaf freziera, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like leaves that are lanceolate and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, arranged in terminal clusters. Freziera salicifolia thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native habitat.
