Fritillaria taipaiensis var. yuxiensis

Fritillaria taipaiensis var. yuxiensis, commonly known as the Yuxi fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to China.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its striking bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soils and moderate climates. Its bulbs are adapted for storage, allowing it to survive in harsh conditions and emerge in spring.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
