Ficus deltoidea var. recurvata

Ficus deltoidea var. recurvata, commonly known as the wavy-leaf fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, wavy-edged leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by round, greenish fruits that mature to a reddish color. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Ficus deltoidea var. recurvata is relatively low-maintenance and can be grown indoors or outdoors in suitable conditions.
