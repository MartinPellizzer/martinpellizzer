Fenestratarum culum

Fenestratarum culum, commonly known as the window-leaf plant, is a unique species characterized by its distinctive fenestrated leaves.

These leaves feature natural holes or openings, giving the plant its common name and adding to its ornamental appeal. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its foliage is usually a deep green, with some varieties displaying variegated patterns.

While not widely cultivated, it is valued for its unusual appearance and air-purifying properties.
