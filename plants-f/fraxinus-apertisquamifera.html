Fraxinus apertisquamifera

Fraxinus apertisquamifera, commonly known as the broad-leaf ash, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite, and serrated leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. Its bark is smooth and gray when young, becoming fissured with age.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in woodworking.
