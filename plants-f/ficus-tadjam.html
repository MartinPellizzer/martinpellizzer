Ficus tadjam

Ficus tadjam, commonly known as the Tadjam fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and distinctive, fig-like fruits. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, while the fruits are edible and valued for their sweet flavor.

Ficus tadjam is also popular as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to indoor environments.
