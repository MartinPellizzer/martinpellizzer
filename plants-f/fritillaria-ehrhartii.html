Fritillaria ehrhartii

Fritillaria ehrhartii, commonly known as the Ehrhart's fritillary, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically orange or reddish in color, with intricate patterns resembling a checkerboard. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments. Its bulbs are adapted to survive harsh winters, and it blooms in late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
