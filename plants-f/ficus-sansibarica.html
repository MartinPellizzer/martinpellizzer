Ficus sansibarica

Ficus sansibarica, commonly known as the East African fig tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of East Africa.

It is a large evergreen tree that can reach heights of up to 20 meters, featuring a broad, spreading canopy. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. It produces small, clustered figs that are edible and serve as a food source for various animals.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in tropical landscapes.
