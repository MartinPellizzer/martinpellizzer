Frangula vulgaris var. subrotunda

Frangula vulgaris var. subrotunda, commonly known as the broad-leafed buckthorn, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, dark green leaves that are broadly ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in racemes, followed by dark, shiny berries that are rich in anthraquinones. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or hedgerows.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
