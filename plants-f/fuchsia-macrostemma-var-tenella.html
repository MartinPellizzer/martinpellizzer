Fuchsia macrostemma var. tenella

Fuchsia macrostemma var. tenella, commonly known as the dwarf fuchsia, is a compact shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range from pink to red. The plant produces showy, pendulous inflorescences that attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
