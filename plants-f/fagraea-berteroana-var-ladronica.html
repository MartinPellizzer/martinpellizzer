Fagraea berteroana var. ladronica

Fagraea berteroana var. ladronica, commonly known as the ladronica fagraea, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant in tropical regions.
