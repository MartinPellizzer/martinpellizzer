Ficus nitentifolia

Ficus nitentifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, greenish flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are typically green when mature. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

The tree is also valued in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
