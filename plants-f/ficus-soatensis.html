Ficus soatensis

Ficus soatensis, commonly known as the Soatén or Soatén fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly found in countries like Brazil, Colombia, and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered fruits that are edible. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Ficus soatensis is valued not only for its ornamental qualities but also for its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
