Fumaria speciosa

Fumaria speciosa, commonly known as the showy fumitory, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its delicate, branching stems and clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Fumaria speciosa is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
