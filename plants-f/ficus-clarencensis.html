Ficus clarencensis

Ficus clarencensis, commonly known as the Clarendon fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow when mature. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively easy care requirements.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of indoor and outdoor environments.
