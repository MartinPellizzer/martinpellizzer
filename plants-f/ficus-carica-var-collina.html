Ficus carica var. collina

Ficus carica var. collina, commonly known as the Collina fig, is a variety of the common fig tree.

It is characterized by its small, compact growth habit and thick, waxy leaves that are typically a deep green. The plant produces small, edible figs that are usually harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and unique fruiting characteristics.
