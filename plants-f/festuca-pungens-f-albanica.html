Festuca pungens f. albanica

Festuca pungens f. albanica, commonly known as the Albanian tussock grass, is a variant of the tufted grass Festuca pungens.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and silver-blue foliage that turns reddish in the fall. This plant is native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans, particularly Albania, and thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils. It is drought-tolerant and prefers full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value lies in its striking coloration and architectural form.
