Ficus rhodesiaca

Ficus rhodesiaca, commonly known as the Rhodesian fig, is a tropical tree native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and smooth, gray bark. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are typically green when mature, serving as a food source for various wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
