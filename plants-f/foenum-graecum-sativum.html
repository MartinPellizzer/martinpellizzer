Foenum-graecum sativum

Foenum-graecum sativum, commonly known as fennel, is an herbaceous plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its feathery, aromatic leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in umbrella-shaped clusters. The plant is native to southern Europe and western Asia but is now cultivated worldwide. Its seeds are used in culinary and medicinal applications due to their licorice-like flavor and various health benefits.

Fennel thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a versatile crop in temperate climates.
