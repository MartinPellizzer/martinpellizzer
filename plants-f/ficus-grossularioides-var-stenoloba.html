Ficus grossularioides var. stenoloba

Ficus grossularioides var. stenoloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fig, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves that are typically narrow and pointed. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its adaptability makes it suitable for both indoor and outdoor growing conditions.
