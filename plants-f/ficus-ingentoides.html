Ficus ingentoides

Ficus ingentoides, commonly known as the giant ficus or mountain fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Nepal, and Bhutan. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. It produces small, greenish flowers followed by edible figs, which are a food source for various wildlife.

Ficus ingentoides is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage and adaptability to different environmental conditions.
