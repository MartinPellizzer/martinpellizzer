Fritillaria liburnica

Fritillaria liburnica, commonly known as the Liburnian fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically orange or yellow with contrasting purple or brown markings. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves and has a bulbous root system that stores nutrients. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Fritillaria liburnica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
