Froelichia nudicaulis var. typica

Froelichia nudicaulis var. typica, commonly known as the narrowleaf froelichia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and swampy areas. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves that are often hairless and have a smooth texture. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually greenish-white and arranged in dense clusters.

This variety is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in association with other wetland flora.
