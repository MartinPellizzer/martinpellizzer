Fuchsia harlingii

Fuchsia harlingii, commonly known as the Harling's fuchsia, is a species native to South America, particularly Ecuador.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, pendulous flowers that range in color from deep red to purple. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with a fine layer of silvery hairs.

Fuchsia harlingii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
