Ficinia indica var. boeckeleriana

Ficinia indica var. boeckeleriana, commonly known as the Boeckelerian lily, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like foliage and clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped white or pale pink flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in marshy areas or along riverbanks. It is a perennial herb that blooms during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and wetland restoration projects.
