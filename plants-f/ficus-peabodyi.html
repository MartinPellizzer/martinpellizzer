Ficus peabodyi

Ficus peabodyi, commonly known as the Peabody fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in countries like Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically heart-shaped or ovate in form. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible figs that are often used in local cuisines.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is also cultivated as a houseplant in many parts of the world.
