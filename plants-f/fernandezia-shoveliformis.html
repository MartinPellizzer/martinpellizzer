Fernandezia shoveliformis

Fernandezia shoveliformis, commonly known as the shovel-shaped fern, is a rare and unique species of fern found in specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, broad, and shovel-like fronds that give it its common name. The plant has a creeping rhizome system that allows it to spread along the forest floor. Its leaves are typically leathery and dark green, with a waxy surface that helps it retain moisture.

Fernandezia shoveliformis plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various small organisms.
