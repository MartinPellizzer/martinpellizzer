Festuca pichinchae

Festuca pichinchae, commonly known as the Pichincha fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the high-altitude regions of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
