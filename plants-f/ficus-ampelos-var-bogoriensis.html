Ficus ampelos var. bogoriensis

Ficus ampelos var. bogoriensis, commonly known as the Bogor ficus, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its long, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy surface. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to indoor conditions.
