Festuca alfrediana subsp. ferrariniana

Festuca alfrediana subsp.

ferrariniana, commonly known as the Ferrarin's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe. It typically grows in calcareous soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny blue to purple flowers. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a relatively rare and specialized plant.
