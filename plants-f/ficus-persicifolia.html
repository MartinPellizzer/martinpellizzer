Ficus persicifolia

Ficus persicifolia, commonly known as the Persian fig or small-leaf fig, is a tropical plant native to southern Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically small and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in subtropical gardens.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its low maintenance requirements.
