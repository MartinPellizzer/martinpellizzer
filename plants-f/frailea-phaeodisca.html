Frailea phaeodisca

Frailea phaeodisca, commonly known as the yellow frailea, is a species of flowering plant in the cactus family, Cactaceae.

It is native to the arid regions of South America, particularly Brazil. This small cactus typically grows as a solitary or clustered plant with short, cylindrical stems. Its most distinctive feature is its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in the evening.

The plant is well adapted to dry environments, with spines that provide protection and reduce water loss.
