Festuca hedbergii

Festuca hedbergii, commonly known as Hedberg's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and forested areas. The plant has slender, tufted growth with fine, dark green leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a single, narrow spike that bears small, spikelet-like flowers.

Festuca hedbergii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
