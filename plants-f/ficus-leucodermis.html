Ficus leucodermis

Ficus leucodermis, commonly known as the white-barked fig, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences called syconia. Ficus leucodermis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.

It thrives in warm, humid environments and is commonly found in tropical and subtropical regions.
