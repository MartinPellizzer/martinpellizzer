Fedorovia tsangii

Fedorovia tsangii, commonly known as the Tibetan sand pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, round, yellow fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils at high elevations.

Fedorovia tsangii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
