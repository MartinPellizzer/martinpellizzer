Ferulago angulata

Ferulago angulata, commonly known as angular fumitory, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and its roots are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and along roadsides.

Ferulago angulata is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in treating skin conditions and as a diuretic.
