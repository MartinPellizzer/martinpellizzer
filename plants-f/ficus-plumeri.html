Ficus plumeri

Ficus plumeri, commonly known as the weeping fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its drooping, cascading leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant has opposite, simple, and usually heart-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by small, round fruits.

Ficus plumeri is widely cultivated as a houseplant and is known for its adaptability to indoor conditions.
