Flourensia ilicifolia

Flourensia ilicifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf flourensia, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by small, round fruits that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and resistance to pests.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a notable species in desert ecosystems.
