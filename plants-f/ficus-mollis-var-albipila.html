Ficus mollis var. albipila

Ficus mollis var. albipila, commonly known as the white-veined ficus, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features thick, waxy, dark green leaves with prominent white veins that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by green, fleshy fruits that turn red when mature. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate watering to prevent root rot.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and ornamental purposes.
