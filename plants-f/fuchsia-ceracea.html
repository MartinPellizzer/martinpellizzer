Fuchsia ceracea

Fuchsia ceracea, commonly known as the silverleaf fuchsia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and showy, pendulous flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are borne in clusters and are often visited by hummingbirds and other pollinators.

Fuchsia ceracea prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical gardens.
