Fragaria minor

Fragaria minor, commonly known as the woodland strawberry, is a small perennial plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It produces small, delicate flowers with five white petals and yellow stamens. The plant's leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three heart-shaped leaflets. Its fruits are small, red berries that develop from the flower and are often used in culinary applications.

Fragaria minor thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a common sight in forest understories.
