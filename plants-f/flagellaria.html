Flagellaria

Flagellaria, commonly known as the "whip plant," is a tropical climbing vine belonging to the family Passifloraceae.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in length, often trailing or climbing over other vegetation. The plant produces striking flowers with five petals and a distinctive crown of filaments, which are typically bright yellow or orange in color. Its fruit is a fleshy, berry-like structure that contains multiple seeds.

Flagellaria is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
