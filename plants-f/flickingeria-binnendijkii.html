Flickingeria binnendijkii

Flickingeria binnendijkii, commonly known as the Binnendijk's ghost orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, ghost-like appearance, with elongated, translucent petals that resemble spectral forms. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, Flickingeria binnendijkii is highly sought after by botanists and conservationists.
