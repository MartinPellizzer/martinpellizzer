Fevillea passiflora

Fevillea passiflora, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that display a complex arrangement of petals, sepals, and filaments. The plant produces blue or purple berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a velvety texture, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Fevillea passiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
