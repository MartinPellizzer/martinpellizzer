Filipendula ulmaria f. megalocarpa

Filipendula ulmaria f. megalocarpa, commonly known as the giant flowered meadow sweet, is a botanical variant of the common meadow sweet.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its large, showy white to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, riverbanks, and other wetland habitats. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often reddish in color.

The inflorescence consists of numerous small flowers arranged in a spherical cluster, making it a distinctive and ornamental plant.
