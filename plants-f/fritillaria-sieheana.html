Fritillaria sieheana

Fritillaria sieheana, commonly known as the Siehe's fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking bell-shaped flowers, which typically range in color from deep purple to orange-red. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous base. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
