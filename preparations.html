preparations

1. infusion

Infusion refers to the process of adding a substance, such as a flavor or a medicinal agent, to a liquid by gradual and gentle mixing. This technique is commonly used in cooking, where herbs, spices, and other ingredients are infused into oils, vinegars, or broths to enhance their flavors and aromas. In medical settings, infusion is also used to administer medications and nutrients through intravenous or oral routes, allowing for targeted treatment and absorption.

infusion

2. tincture

A tincture is a concentrated liquid extract of a plant, herb, or other medicinal substance that has been dissolved in a solvent, typically ethanol or glycerin. Tinctures are often used in herbal medicine and homeopathy due to their potent and easily absorbed form, which can provide quick relief from various health issues. By taking a few drops of tincture sublingually or mixing it with water, individuals can reap the benefits of the extracted compounds without having to consume large amounts of the original plant material.

tincture

3. decoction

Decoction is a traditional method of extracting nutrients and flavors from plant materials, such as herbs, roots, and bark. Unlike infusion, which involves steeping plant material in hot water, decoction involves boiling the material to release its active compounds. This process is often used in herbal medicine and culinary practices to create teas, tonics, and other liquid preparations.

decoction

4. salve

Salve is a topical ointment or cream used to soothe and protect dry or irritated skin, often made from natural ingredients such as beeswax, olive oil, and herbs. It is commonly used to treat minor skin conditions like chapped hands, dry lips, and minor cuts or scrapes. By creating a barrier on the skin's surface, salve can help to lock in moisture, reduce inflammation, and promote healing.

salve

5. oinment

Ointment is a topical medication or treatment applied to the skin to soothe, protect, and heal various conditions such as cuts, burns, or eczema. It often comes in the form of a thick, creamy paste or gel, and can be made from a variety of ingredients including natural herbs, oils, and synthetic chemicals. By creating a physical barrier on the skin, ointment helps to lock in moisture and prevent further irritation or infection.

oinment

6. poultice

A poultice is a soft, pliable mass of material, often a mixture of water and a substance such as herbs or clay, applied to the skin to provide relief from pain or inflammation. Poultices have been used for centuries in traditional medicine to treat various conditions, including sprains, bruises, and skin irritations. When applied, the poultice helps to draw out heat, reduce swelling, and promote healing, providing temporary pain relief and promoting the body's natural recovery process.

poultice

7. suppository

A suppository is a solid medicinal dose form that is inserted into the rectum or vagina to release medication locally. It is typically made of a water-soluble base such as glycerin or cocoa butter, which helps the medication dissolve and be absorbed. Suppositories are often used for conditions such as constipation, hemorrhoids, and vaginal infections, where a localized treatment is required.

suppository

8. capsule

A capsule is a self-contained package or container, often used for medical purposes, such as storing and administering medication or vaccines. In fashion, a capsule wardrobe refers to a small collection of essential items that can be mixed and matched to create multiple outfits. The term "capsule" is also used in space exploration to describe a small, sealed container that carries astronauts, equipment, and supplies to space.

capsule

9. glycerite

Glycerite, also known as glycerin extract, is a liquid herbal supplement that contains the concentrated essence of herbs in a glycerin base. This preparation method allows the active compounds of the herbs to be extracted and preserved in a way that is often more bioavailable than traditional dried or powdered forms. Glycerites are commonly used to make herbal tinctures, and can be taken sublingually or added to food and beverages for their potential health benefits.

glycerite

10. extract

Extract is a concentrated form of a substance, typically obtained from a mixture or solution through a process of separation and purification. It can be a liquid, solid, or gas, and is often used in various applications such as pharmaceuticals, food processing, and chemical manufacturing. The extraction process involves removing the desired component from a source material, such as a plant, animal, or mineral, to produce a pure or highly concentrated form of the substance.

extract

11. resin

Resin is a viscous liquid or solid that is often used as a binding agent in various applications, such as adhesives, paints, and coatings. It can be derived from plants, such as pine trees, or synthesized from petrochemicals, and is commonly used in the production of composite materials and epoxy resins. When cured, resin can develop a hard and durable finish, making it a popular choice for crafting, art, and industrial manufacturing processes.

resin

12. infused oil

Infused oil is a type of oil that has been infused with the flavor, aroma, and medicinal properties of herbs, spices, or botanicals. The process typically involves steeping the infused ingredients in a carrier oil, such as olive or coconut oil, for a period of time to allow the flavors and properties to transfer. Infused oils can be used in cooking, skincare, and aromatherapy, and are often used to add unique flavors and benefits to dishes, products, and treatments.

infused oil

Gels are commonly found in everyday products, including food, cosmetics, and pharmaceuticals, where they are used for their unique properties and applications.

Elixir is a functional programming language developed by the Erlang team at the Ericsson corporation. Elixir is often used in concurrent and distributed systems, and is particularly well-suited for building scalable and fault-tolerant applications.

30. emulsion

An emulsion is a mixture of two or more liquids that don't normally mix, such as oil and water, which are stabilized by an emulsifier. This process involves breaking down the liquids into tiny particles, allowing them to coexist and remain suspended in a stable solution. Emulsions are commonly found in food products like mayonnaise, dressings, and sauces, as well as in personal care products like lotions and creams.

36. maceration

Maceration is a process used in both culinary and pharmaceutical contexts to extract flavors, colors, or active ingredients from plant materials. In cooking, maceration is often used to make flavorings, such as fruit syrups or infused oils, by allowing the ingredients to steep in a liquid over an extended period. This technique can help to soften tough plant material, release its natural oils, and create a more complex flavor profile.

37. ghee

Ghee is a type of clarified butter that originated in ancient India, where it has been used for centuries in Ayurvedic medicine and cooking. The process of making ghee involves heating butter to separate the butterfat from the milk solids, resulting in a clear, golden liquid with a nutty flavor and high smoke point. Ghee is a popular ingredient in many Indian and Middle Eastern dishes, and is prized for its rich flavor and health benefits, including its high concentration of conjugated linoleic acid (CLA).

ghee

fomentation

40. percolation

Percolation is a phenomenon in which a fluid, such as water or air, passes through a porous material, like soil or a coffee filter, from a lower pressure area to a higher pressure area. This process occurs when the pores or gaps in the material become saturated, allowing the fluid to flow through and eventually escape at the surface. Percolation plays a crucial role in various natural processes, including groundwater recharge, soil erosion, and the flow of coffee grounds during brewing.

percolation
