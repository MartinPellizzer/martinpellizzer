Xanthosoma versicolor

Xanthosoma versicolor, commonly known as the variegated elephant ear, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that exhibit a striking variety of colors, including green, white, and yellow. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which includes other ornamental and edible plants. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Xanthosoma versicolor is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
