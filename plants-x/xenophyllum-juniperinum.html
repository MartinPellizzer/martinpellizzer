Xenophyllum juniperinum

Xenophyllum juniperinum, commonly known as the juniper-like fern, is a unique plant species characterized by its intricate frond structure.

It features delicate, feather-like leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along its stem. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain its reproductive organs, typical of ferns. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate forest floors.

Its botanical significance lies in its evolutionary adaptations and role in ecological succession.
