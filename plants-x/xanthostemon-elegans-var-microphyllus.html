Xanthostemon elegans var. microphyllus

Xanthostemon elegans var. microphyllus, commonly known as the small-leafed myrtle, is a flowering plant native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically glaucous in appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to arid conditions.
