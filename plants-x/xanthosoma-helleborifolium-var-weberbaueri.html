Xanthosoma helleborifolium var. weberbaueri

Xanthosoma helleborifolium var. weberbaueri, commonly known as the Weberbauer's elephant ear, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, often with a deep green color and a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
