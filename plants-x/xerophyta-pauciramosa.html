Xerophyta pauciramosa

Xerophyta pauciramosa, commonly known as the sparse-branched ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in arid environments. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers during the winter months. It has a low-growing, spreading habit with few branches, which helps reduce water loss.

Xerophyta pauciramosa is well adapted to rocky soils and full sun exposure.
