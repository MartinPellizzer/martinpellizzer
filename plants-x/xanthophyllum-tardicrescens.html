Xanthophyllum tardicrescens

Xanthophyllum tardicrescens, commonly known as the slow-growing golden leaf plant, is a unique species characterized by its vibrant yellow foliage.

It is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of South America, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a glossy texture and produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in late spring. Its growth rate is notably slow, making it a prized specimen in botanical gardens and specialized collections.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in its natural range.
