Xyris duidensis

Xyris duidensis, commonly known as the dwarf yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the genus Xyris, which is part of the family Xyridaceae, commonly referred to as the yellow-eyed grass family. This species is characterized by its low-growing habit, with narrow, grass-like leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. Xyris duidensis typically thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, supporting various pollinators and contributing to biodiversity.
