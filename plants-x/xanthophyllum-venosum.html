Xanthophyllum venosum

Xanthophyllum venosum, commonly known as the veined goldenleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow foliage with prominent veins, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
