Xyris brownii

Xyris brownii, commonly known as the brown-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers with a distinctive brown center. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. Xyris brownii is part of the Xyridaceae family, which includes several species of grass-like plants.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
