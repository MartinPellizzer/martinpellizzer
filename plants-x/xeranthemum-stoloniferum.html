Xeranthemum stoloniferum

Xeranthemum stoloniferum, commonly known as the spreading bushflower, is a hardy, drought-tolerant plant native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It features upright, branched stems that produce clusters of small, showy yellow or orange flowers. The plant has gray-green, fuzzy leaves that help reduce water loss in arid conditions. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Xeranthemum stoloniferum is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
