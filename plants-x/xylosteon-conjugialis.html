Xylosteon conjugialis

Xylosteon conjugialis, commonly known as the jointed wood plant, is a unique species characterized by its distinctive stem structure.

It features a woody stem with nodes that appear to be joined, giving it a segmented appearance. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually alternate and simple, with a broad, ovate shape.

Xylosteon conjugialis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unusual morphology.
