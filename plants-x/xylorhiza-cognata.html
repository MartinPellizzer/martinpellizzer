Xylorhiza cognata

Xylorhiza cognata, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and narrow, silver-green leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink, typically blooming during the rainy season. It is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it a resilient species in its habitat.

Xylorhiza cognata is often found in rocky outcrops and sandy soils, where it thrives under direct sunlight.
