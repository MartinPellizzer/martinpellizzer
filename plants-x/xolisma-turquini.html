Xolisma turquini

Xolisma turquini, commonly known as the Turk's broom, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated pods, typical of many legumes.

Xolisma turquini is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
