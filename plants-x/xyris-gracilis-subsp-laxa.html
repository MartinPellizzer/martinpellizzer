Xyris gracilis subsp. laxa

Xyris gracilis subsp.

laxa, commonly known as the slender swamp yelloweye, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States. It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to water-saturated soils.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its inflorescence consists of several to many small, elongated flowers arranged in a spike-like structure. Xyris gracilis subsp.

laxa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
