Xeraea argenteosericea

Xeraea argenteosericea, commonly known as the silver-haired xeraea, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and delicate, feathery leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or arid landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
