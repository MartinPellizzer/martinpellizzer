Xyris steyermarkii

Xyris steyermarkii, commonly known as the Steyermark's yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various species of yellow-eyed grasses. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in dense spikes, often resembling a candle flame.

Xyris steyermarkii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in wetland ecosystems.
