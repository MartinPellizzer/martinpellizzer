Xyris buengkanensis

Xyris buengkanensis, commonly known as the Bungkan marsh yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. Xyris buengkanensis grows in wetland habitats such as swamps and marshes, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native environment.
