Xanthosoma zamoraense

Xanthosoma zamoraense, commonly known as the Zamora elephant ear, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in diameter. The plant produces an inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Xanthosoma zamoraense is also valued for its edible leaves, which are used in traditional cuisines.
