Xanthosoma kerberi

Xanthosoma kerberi, commonly known as the giant arrowhead, is a herbaceous plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrowhead-shaped leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant produces striking inflorescences consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of the arum family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Xanthosoma kerberi is also valued for its edible tubers, which are used in traditional cuisines.
