Xanthosoma puberulum

Xanthosoma puberulum, commonly known as the velvet leaf taro, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces striking spatheal inflorescences with yellow spadices, which are often used in ornamental horticulture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value rather than its edible parts.

Xanthosoma puberulum is also valued for its ability to purify water and improve soil quality in wetland ecosystems.
