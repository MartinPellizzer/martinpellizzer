Xyris strobilifera

Xyris strobilifera, commonly known as the pineywoods goldeneye, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the genus Xyris, which includes various species of marsh and wetland plants. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are grass-like and often grow in dense clumps, adapting well to moist, acidic soils.

Xyris strobilifera is typically found in swamps, bogs, and other wetland habitats where it thrives in humid conditions.
