Xanthophyllum multiramosum

Xanthophyllum multiramosum, commonly known as the golden branched fern, is a unique plant characterized by its intricate, branched fronds.

It belongs to the family Xanthophyllaceae and is native to specific regions in South America. The plant's leaves are typically bright yellow, which is due to the presence of carotenoid pigments. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its botanical structure makes it an interesting subject for studies on plant adaptation and evolution.
