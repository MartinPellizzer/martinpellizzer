Xanthium macrocarpum

Xanthium macrocarpum, commonly known as the giant cocklebur, is a herbaceous annual plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and rough, prickly leaves. The plant produces large, spiny fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals. Its hairy stems and serrated leaves are adapted to thrive in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Xanthium macrocarpum is often found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.
