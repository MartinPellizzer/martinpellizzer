Xiphion spurium

Xiphion spurium, commonly known as the Spanish daffodil, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically blooms in early spring, adding a vibrant touch to gardens and natural habitats. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to temperate climates.

Xiphion spurium is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in Mediterranean horticulture.
