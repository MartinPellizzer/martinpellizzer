Xerophyta kirkii

Xerophyta kirkii, commonly known as the Kirk's ice plant, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater during dry periods.

Xerophyta kirkii is well adapted to harsh, semi-desert conditions and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
