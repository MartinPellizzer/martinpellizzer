Xanthophytum villarii

Xanthophytum villarii, commonly known as the yellow feather cactus, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a tall, cylindrical stem covered in soft, yellowish spines that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. Its adaptations include thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in harsh, dry environments.

Xanthophytum villarii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in low-maintenance gardens.
