Xeranthemum modestum

Xeranthemum modestum, commonly known as the modest zinnia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features hairy, narrow leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a distinctive central disc surrounded by petal-like bracts.

Xeranthemum modestum is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
