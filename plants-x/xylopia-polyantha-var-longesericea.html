Xylopia polyantha var. longesericea

Xylopia polyantha var. longesericea, commonly known as the long-veined xylopia, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered, with a distinctive, fragrant aroma, and the fruit is a capsule that splits open to release the seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and floral characteristics.
