Xiphopteris strictissima

Xiphopteris strictissima, commonly known as the rigid holly fern, is a species of fern native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, stiff fronds that are typically dark green and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Xiphopteris strictissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
