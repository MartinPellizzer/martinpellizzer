Xanthoxalis madrensis

Xanthoxalis madrensis, commonly known as the Mexican buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features bright yellow flowers with a central disk surrounded by ray florets, and it typically grows in moist, open habitats. Xanthoxalis madrensis has hairy stems and leaves that are often lobed or divided, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
