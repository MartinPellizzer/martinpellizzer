Xylocalyx recurvus

Xylocalyx recurvus, commonly known as the curling leaf orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, curling foliage and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil to flourish. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture from the forest floor, and it plays a vital role in the ecosystem by providing nectar for pollinators.

Due to habitat loss, Xylocalyx recurvus is considered endangered and is protected in many regions.
