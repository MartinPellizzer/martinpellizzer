Xyris subulata var. macrotona

Xyris subulata var. macrotona, commonly known as the large-flowered wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers with a distinctive spurred petal. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils in woodland or wetland habitats. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to native pollinators.
