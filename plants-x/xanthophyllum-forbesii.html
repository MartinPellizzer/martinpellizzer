Xanthophyllum forbesii

Xanthophyllum forbesii, commonly known as the Forbes' goldenbush, is a small evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has narrow, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to coastal gardens.

Xanthophyllum forbesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
