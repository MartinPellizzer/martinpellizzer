Xanthium orientale var. italicum

Xanthium orientale var. italicum, commonly known as Italian cocklebur, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a rough, hairy stem that often branches near the base, with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that have serrated edges. The plant produces spiny, spherical fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a prickly husk. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in cultivated fields.

The species is characterized by its yellowish-green flowers and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
