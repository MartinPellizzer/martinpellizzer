Xylotheca capreifolia

Xylotheca capreifolia, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and unique floral structures. The plant typically features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, often greenish or brownish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Xylotheca capreifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
