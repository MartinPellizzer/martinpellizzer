Xylosma pilosa

Xylosma pilosa, commonly known as the hairy xylosma, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its rough, often hairy stems and leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, followed by small, round fruits. Xylosma pilosa is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand periods of drought.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
