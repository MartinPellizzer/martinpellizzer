Xyris trachyphylla var. glaucescens

Xyris trachyphylla var. glaucescens, commonly known as the glaucous yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, preferring moist to wet soil conditions. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in the spring. Its varietal name, *glaucescens*, refers to the bluish-gray hue of its foliage.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland habitats.
