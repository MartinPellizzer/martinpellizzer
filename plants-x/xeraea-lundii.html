Xeraea lundii

Xeraea lundii, commonly known as the desert beauty, is a rare and striking succulent native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months, adding a splash of color to its surroundings. Its growth is slow and compact, forming a rosette shape that helps minimize water loss.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, Xeraea lundii is often sought after by botanists and collectors.
