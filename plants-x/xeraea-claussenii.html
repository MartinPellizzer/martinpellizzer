Xeraea claussenii

Xeraea claussenii, commonly known as the Cape York ironwood, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of northern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is highly drought-tolerant and thrives in sandy or rocky soils, making it well adapted to arid environments. Its hardy nature and attractive appearance make it a popular choice for coastal landscaping.

Xeraea claussenii is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for native wildlife.
