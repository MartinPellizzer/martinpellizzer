Xolisma ligustrina

Xolisma ligustrina, commonly known as the desert privet, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Xolisma ligustrina is adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions with minimal water requirements.

Its root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion in desert environments.
