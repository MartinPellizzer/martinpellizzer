Xanthoxalis acuminata

Xanthoxalis acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-leaved false oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring and summer months. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a sharp, pointed tip, giving it its common name.

Xanthoxalis acuminata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
