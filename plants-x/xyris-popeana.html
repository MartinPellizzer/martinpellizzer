Xyris popeana

Xyris popeana, commonly known as the Pope's goldenroot, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various species of yellow-flowered plants. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Xyris popeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
