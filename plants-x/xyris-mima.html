Xyris mima

Xyris mima, commonly known as the mima xyris, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Xyridaceae family, which includes various grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in suitable habitats.
