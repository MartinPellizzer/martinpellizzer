Xolisma apiculata

Xolisma apiculata, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, yellow flowers, which bloom in early spring. The plant has hairy, succulent leaves that help it retain moisture in dry conditions. Its root system is deep, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

Xolisma apiculata is adapted to thrive in harsh, sun-exposed environments with minimal rainfall.
