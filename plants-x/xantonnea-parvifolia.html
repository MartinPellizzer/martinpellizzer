Xantonnea parvifolia

Xantonnea parvifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved xantonnea, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate leaves that are typically narrow and lance-shaped. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Xantonnea parvifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
