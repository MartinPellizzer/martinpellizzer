Xerololophus trinervis

Xerololophus trinervis, commonly known as the narrow-veined desert fern, is a unique plant adapted to arid environments.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are often covered in a fine, waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant's rhizomes are thick and fleshy, storing moisture to help it survive in dry conditions. It typically grows in rocky soils and is found in regions with low rainfall.

Despite its delicate appearance, it is highly resilient and can thrive in extreme desert conditions.
