Xanthomyrtus flavida subsp. moultonii

Xanthomyrtus flavida subsp.

moultonii, commonly known as the Moulton's flame myrtle, is a shrub native to Australia. It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers.

The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils. It is often found in heathlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique floral structure and coloration compared to other members of the species.
