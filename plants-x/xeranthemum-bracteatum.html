Xeranthemum bracteatum

Xeranthemum bracteatum, commonly known as the bractean waxplant, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and ability to attract pollinators.
