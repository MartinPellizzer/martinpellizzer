Xanthophytum longipedunculatum

Xanthophytum longipedunculatum, commonly known as the long-peduncled yellow plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and elongated peduncles, which are the stalks supporting the flower heads. Native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile, it thrives in dry, sunny environments. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, composite flower heads that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to arid landscapes.
