Xyris labatii

Xyris labatii, commonly known as the Florida marsh yelloweye, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its inflorescence consists of clusters of small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Xyris labatii plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by contributing to biodiversity and supporting local wildlife.
