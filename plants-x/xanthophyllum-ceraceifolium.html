Xanthophyllum ceraceifolium

Xanthophyllum ceraceifolium, commonly known as the waxleaf xanthophyllum, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and thick, waxy leaves that give it its common name. Native to certain regions of South America, it thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking appearance.

Its foliage and flowers are both used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
