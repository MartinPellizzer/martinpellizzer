Xanthium antiquorum

Xanthium antiquorum, commonly known as ancient cocklebur, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features spiny, rough leaves and produces small, spherical fruits covered in hooked bracts. Its growth habit is typically annual, with a robust stem and hairy foliage.

Xanthium antiquorum is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
