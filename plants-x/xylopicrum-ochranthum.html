Xylopicrum ochranthum

Xylopicrum ochranthum, commonly known as the crimson bark fern, is a unique plant native to the humid rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, woody stems that resemble bark, which provide structural support and help retain moisture. The plant produces striking, palmate leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically clustered in inflorescences at the base of the plant.

Xylopicrum ochranthum is valued in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.
