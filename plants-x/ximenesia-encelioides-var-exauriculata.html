Ximenesia encelioides var. exauriculata

Ximenesia encelioides var. exauriculata, commonly known as the Desert Dandelion, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It features pale green, fuzzy leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry climates. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy, white seed heads that resemble dandelion fluff. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for xeriscaping and ecological restoration projects.
