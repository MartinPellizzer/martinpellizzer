Xeraea angustiflora

Xeraea angustiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved xeraea, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple in color. The plant is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep roots that help it access underground water. It is often found in rocky soils and is a component of the local flora in its native habitat.

Xeraea angustiflora is not widely cultivated but is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to harsh environments.
