Xyridion

Xyridion, commonly known as the marsh grass or narrowleaf pondweed, is a member of the Xyridaceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant typically found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often submerged or floating, depending on the species. Xyridion produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or yellowish in color.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
