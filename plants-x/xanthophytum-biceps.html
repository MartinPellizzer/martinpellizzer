Xanthophytum biceps

Xanthophytum biceps, commonly known as the two-headed yellow plant, is a unique species native to arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and two distinct stems that emerge from the base. The plant has adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through its succulent leaves and deep root system. Xanthophytum biceps is also notable for its ability to store water efficiently, making it a hardy species in extreme conditions.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in desert flora.
