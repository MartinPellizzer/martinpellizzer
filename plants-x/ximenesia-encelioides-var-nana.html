Ximenesia encelioides var. nana

Ximenesia encelioides var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf ximenesia, is a compact shrub native to arid regions of Mexico.

It features small, fuzzy, yellowish-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces delicate, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a subtle ornamental value. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting addition to rock gardens and dry landscape designs.
