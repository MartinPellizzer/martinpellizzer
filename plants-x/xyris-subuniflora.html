Xyris subuniflora

Xyris subuniflora, commonly known as the narrowleaf goldeneye, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Xyridaceae family, which includes various species of grass-like plants. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Xyris subuniflora plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
