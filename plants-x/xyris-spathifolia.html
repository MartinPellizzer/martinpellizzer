Xyris spathifolia

Xyris spathifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various grass-like plants. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical structure is characterized by its erect growth habit and clustered inflorescences.
