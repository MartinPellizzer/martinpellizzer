Xeranthemum retortum

Xeranthemum retortum, commonly known as the twisted chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a dense, spike-like inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Xeranthemum retortum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
