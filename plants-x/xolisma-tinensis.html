Xolisma tinensis

Xolisma tinensis, commonly known as the desert bellflower, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited water availability. Xolisma tinensis is often found in dry grasslands and shrublands, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small fauna.

Its blooming period typically occurs in late spring to early summer, adding a splash of color to its harsh environment.
