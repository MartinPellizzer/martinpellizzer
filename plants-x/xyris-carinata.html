Xyris carinata

Xyris carinata, commonly known as the ridged yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its inflorescence consists of a single spike of flowers, which are often surrounded by bracts.

Xyris carinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
