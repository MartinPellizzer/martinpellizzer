Xanthomyrtus

Xanthomyrtus, commonly known as the yellow myrtle, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Myrtaceae.

It consists of several species, most of which are shrubs or small trees native to Australia. The plants are characterized by their aromatic leaves and showy, often fragrant flowers that range in color from white to yellow. Xanthomyrtus species typically thrive in well-drained soils and are adapted to various climatic conditions.

They are also valued for their ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
