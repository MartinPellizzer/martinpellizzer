Xantolis reticulata

Xantolis reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined orchid, is a tropical orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its intricate, net-like venation pattern on its leaves, which gives it its scientific name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small and fragrant, with a complex structure typical of orchids.

Xantolis reticulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
