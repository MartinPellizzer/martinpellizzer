Xyris trichophylla

Xyris trichophylla, commonly known as the hairy yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes grass-like plants known for their showy yellow flowers. Xyris trichophylla plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing wetland soils.
