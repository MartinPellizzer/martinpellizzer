Xylopia tomentosa

Xylopia tomentosa, commonly known as the wooly xylopia, is a tropical plant belonging to the Annonaceae family.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and dark green, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a woody, capsule-like structure that splits open to release its seeds.

Xylopia tomentosa is often found in moist, shaded habitats in tropical regions of Central and South America.
