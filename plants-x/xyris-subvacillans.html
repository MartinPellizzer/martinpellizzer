Xyris subvacillans

Xyris subvacillans, commonly known as the narrowleaf goldcup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the genus Xyris, which is part of the grass family, Poaceae. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Xyris subvacillans is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
