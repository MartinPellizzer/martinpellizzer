Xyris ekmanii

Xyris ekmanii, commonly known as the Ekman's goldenroot, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various species of grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in wetland habitats and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Xyris ekmanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
