Xylobium hyacinthinum

Xylobium hyacinthinum, commonly known as the hyacinth bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces showy, hyacinth-like flowers in shades of purple and white. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated, curved pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soils.
