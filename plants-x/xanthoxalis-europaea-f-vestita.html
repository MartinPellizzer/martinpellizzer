Xanthoxalis europaea f. vestita

Xanthoxalis europaea f. vestita, commonly known as the white sandwort, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, often accompanied by narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Brassicaceae family.
