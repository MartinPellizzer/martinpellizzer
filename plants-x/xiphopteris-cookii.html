Xiphopteris cookii

Xiphopteris cookii, commonly known as Cook's huperzia, is a species of clubmoss native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing, evergreen fern-like plant that typically forms dense mats on rocky or sandy soils. The plant has needle-like leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, acidic environments and is often found in shaded, forested areas.

Xiphopteris cookii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in horticulture for its hardy nature and unique foliage.
