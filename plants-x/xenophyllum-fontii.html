Xenophyllum fontii

Xenophyllum fontii, commonly known as the Fontana fern, is a rare and delicate species native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, lace-like fronds that give it a distinctive, ethereal appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture and well-drained soil. Its reproductive structures, including spore-producing sorus, are positioned on the underside of the fronds.

Xenophyllum fontii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
