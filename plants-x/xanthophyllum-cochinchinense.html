Xanthophyllum cochinchinense

Xanthophyllum cochinchinense, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in dense clusters, often in the summer months. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in coastal areas or near water bodies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and as a landscape plant.
