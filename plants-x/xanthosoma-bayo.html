Xanthosoma bayo

Xanthosoma bayo, commonly known as the giant taro, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1.5 meters in length. The plant produces a striking inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its edible corms.

Xanthosoma bayo is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in various cultures.
