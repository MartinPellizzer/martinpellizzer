Xanthosoma berlinii

Xanthosoma berlinii, commonly known as the giant arrowroot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence composed of a spadix and a spathe. Its underground stems, known as rhizomes, are edible and have a starchy texture similar to yams.

Xanthosoma berlinii thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.
