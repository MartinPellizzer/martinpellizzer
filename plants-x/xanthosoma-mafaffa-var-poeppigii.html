Xanthosoma mafaffa var. poeppigii

Xanthosoma mafaffa var. poeppigii, commonly known as the swamp taro, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its rhizomes are edible and have been traditionally used in various cuisines.

It thrives in wet, muddy environments and is often found in swamps or along riverbanks.
