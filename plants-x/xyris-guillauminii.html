Xyris guillauminii

Xyris guillauminii, commonly known as the Guillaumin's yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes grass-like plants commonly found in wetland and swampy environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in swamps, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Xyris guillauminii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
