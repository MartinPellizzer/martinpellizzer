Xeranthemum foetidum

Xeranthemum foetidum, commonly known as the stinking roselin, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish to orange-colored flowers. The plant has hairy, gray-green leaves and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is drought-tolerant and often grows in coastal areas.

Xeranthemum foetidum is known for its strong, unpleasant odor, which is more pronounced when the plant is in bloom.
