Xylopia sumatrana

Xylopia sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran xylopia, is a member of the Annonaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in humid tropical forests. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that are often clustered in umbels. Its fruit is a smooth, oval drupe that turns from green to yellow or reddish as it matures.

Xylopia sumatrana is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional crafts and as a source of essential oils.
