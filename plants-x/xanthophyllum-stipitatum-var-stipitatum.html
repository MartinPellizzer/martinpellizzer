Xanthophyllum stipitatum var. stipitatum

Xanthophyllum stipitatum var. stipitatum, commonly known as the golden cup moss, is a species of liverwort belonging to the family Porellaceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange-green lobed leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant forms dense, cushion-like growths on moist, shaded rocks and soil, typically in temperate regions. Its reproductive structures, known as sorocarps, are small, cup-shaped and contain spores.

This species is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often used in horticultural displays.
