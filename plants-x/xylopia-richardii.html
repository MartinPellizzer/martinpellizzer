Xylopia richardii

Xylopia richardii, commonly known as the African bitter almond, is a member of the Annonaceae family.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to tropical Africa. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, white flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a berry that turns from green to yellow or red when mature.

The plant contains toxic compounds and is used in traditional medicine, though its use is limited due to its potency.
