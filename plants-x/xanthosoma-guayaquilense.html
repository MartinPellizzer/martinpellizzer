Xanthosoma guayaquilense

Xanthosoma guayaquilense, commonly known as the Guayaquil arrowleaf, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Ecuador and Colombia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces striking inflorescences with a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe, which is typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in humid lowland forests.

Xanthosoma guayaquilense is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
