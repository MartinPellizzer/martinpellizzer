Xanthophyllum ionanthum

Xanthophyllum ionanthum, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a tropical orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow and orange flowers, which are adorned with intricate patterns resembling flames. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its leaves are elongated and lance-shaped, with a waxy surface that helps retain moisture.

Xanthophyllum ionanthum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
