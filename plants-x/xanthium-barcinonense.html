Xanthium barcinonense

Xanthium barcinonense, commonly known as the Barbary chaffflower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features rough, hairy stems and serrated, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets.

Xanthium barcinonense is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain regions.
