Xanthosoma maroae

Xanthosoma maroae, commonly known as the Maroan arrowleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces striking inflorescences with a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Xanthosoma maroae is also valued for its edible leaves, which are used in traditional cuisines.
