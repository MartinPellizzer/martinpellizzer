Xerochrysum viscosum

Xerochrysum viscosum, commonly known as the golden weed, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a perennial herb that produces bright yellow flowers, which are often used in floral arrangements. The plant has a sticky, resinous texture to its leaves and stems, which is a characteristic feature. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy and adaptable species.

Xerochrysum viscosum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators.
