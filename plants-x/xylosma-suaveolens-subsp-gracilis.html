Xylosma suaveolens subsp. gracilis

Xylosma suaveolens subsp.

gracilis, commonly known as sweet xylosma, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its slender, aromatic stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, woody fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for horticultural and traditional use.
