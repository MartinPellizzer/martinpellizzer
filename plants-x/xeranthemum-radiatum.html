Xeranthemum radiatum

Xeranthemum radiatum, commonly known as the radiate xeranthemum, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows as a shrub with narrow, gray-green leaves and produces showy yellow flowers with a central disk. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid or coastal environments.

Xeranthemum radiatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
