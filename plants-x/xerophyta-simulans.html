Xerophyta simulans

Xerophyta simulans, commonly known as the "skeleton grass," is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that reduce water loss through transpiration. Its small, tubular flowers are typically yellow or orange, attracting pollinators like bees and birds.

Xerophyta simulans is well-suited for xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental appeal.
