Xyris subulata var. acutifolia

Xyris subulata var. acutifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in swamps, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. Its botanical characteristics include a rhizomatous root system and small, lance-shaped foliage.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland habitats.
