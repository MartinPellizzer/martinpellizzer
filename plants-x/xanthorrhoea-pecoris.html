Xanthorrhoea pecoris

Xanthorrhoea pecoris, commonly known as the blackboy, is a unique plant native to Australia.

It is a member of the Xanthorrhoeaceae family and is characterized by its tall, narrow trunk and a crown of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces a distinctive yellow to orange sap, which is stored in a resinous substance within its stem. Xanthorrhoea pecoris is adapted to arid environments and can survive for decades without rain.

Its flowers, which appear in late summer, are small and clustered, attracting various pollinators.
