Xyris maparecida

Xyris maparecida, commonly known as the Brazilian golden club, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Xyris maparecida is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
