Xylophacos medius

Xylophacos medius, commonly known as the silverleaf spirea, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate, airy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and ecologically beneficial in natural habitats.
