Xyris decussata

Xyris decussata, commonly known as the crossleaf yelloweye, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes various grass-like plants known for their showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to saturated soil conditions. Its leaves are grass-like and arranged in a rosette, while its flowers are clustered in dense, yellow spikelets.

Xyris decussata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
