Xanthosoma renteriae

Xanthosoma renteriae, commonly known as the Mexican arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features broad, arrowhead-shaped leaves that are usually a deep green with a waxy texture. It produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that are followed by edible, starchy tubers.

The tubers are often used in local cuisines and are an important part of the plant's botanical and agricultural significance.
