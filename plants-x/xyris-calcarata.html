Xyris calcarata

Xyris calcarata, commonly known as the sand verbena or mountain sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features basal leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring or early summer. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.

Xyris calcarata is adapted to drought conditions, making it a hardy plant in dry landscapes.
