Xeraea rudis

Xeraea rudis, commonly known as the coastal sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in coastal areas, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils. Xeraea rudis is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought-resistant nature.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
