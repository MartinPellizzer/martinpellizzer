Xylopicrum frutescens

Xylopicrum frutescens, commonly known as the crimson spire plant, is a flowering shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features upright, woody stems that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep red, typically blooming during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to store water in its native dry environment.

Xylopicrum frutescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in xeriscapes due to its low water requirements.
