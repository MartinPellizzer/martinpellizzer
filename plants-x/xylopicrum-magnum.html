Xylopicrum magnum

Xylopicrum magnum, commonly known as the giant woodroot, is a rare and striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by elongated, seed-filled pods. Its roots are highly fibrous and capable of absorbing nutrients from deep within the soil, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor environments.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, Xylopicrum magnum is considered a valuable subject for botanical research.
