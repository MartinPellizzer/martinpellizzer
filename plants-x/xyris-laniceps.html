Xyris laniceps

Xyris laniceps, commonly known as the woolly yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes various species of grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Xyris laniceps thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along the edges of ponds.
