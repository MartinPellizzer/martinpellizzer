Xanthium cylindricum

Xanthium cylindricum, commonly known as common cocklebur, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features rough, hairy stems and palmately lobed leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces spiny, spherical fruits that contain seeds with a distinctive crown-like structure. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Xanthium cylindricum is known for its adaptability and can become invasive in certain environments.
