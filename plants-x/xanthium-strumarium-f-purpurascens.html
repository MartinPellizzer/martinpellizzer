Xanthium strumarium f. purpurascens

Xanthium strumarium f. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple cocklebur, is a variant of the common cocklebur characterized by its distinctive purple bracts.

It is a biennial plant native to North America, typically growing in disturbed soils and along roadsides. The plant features spiny, lobed leaves and produces spherical, spiky fruits that contain seeds with hooked appendages. Its botanical structure includes a rough, hairy stem and yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the early summer.

This subspecies is often noted for its ornamental value and its role in ecological systems as a food source for various wildlife.
