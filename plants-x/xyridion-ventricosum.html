Xyridion ventricosum

Xyridion ventricosum, commonly known as the bladderwort, is a small, aquatic plant native to temperate regions.

It is characterized by its small, bladder-like structures that are used to trap tiny aquatic organisms. The plant typically grows in shallow water, often in marshes or along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are usually narrow and cylindrical, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Xyridion ventricosum plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for microorganisms.
