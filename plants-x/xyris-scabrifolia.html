Xyris scabrifolia

Xyris scabrifolia, commonly known as roughleaf goldeneye, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various grass-like plants often found in wet or moist habitats. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves with a rough, textured surface, and it produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Xyris scabrifolia typically grows in swampy areas, along stream banks, and in other wetland environments.

Its unique morphology and ecological preferences make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
