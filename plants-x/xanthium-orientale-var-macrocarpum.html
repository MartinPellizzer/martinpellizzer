Xanthium orientale var. macrocarpum

Xanthium orientale var. macrocarpum, commonly known as the giant cocklebur, is a prickly annual plant native to temperate regions of Asia.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves with rough, hairy surfaces and prominent veins. The plant produces large, spiky fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a tough, burs-like structure. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in agricultural fields.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common weed with both ecological and historical significance.
