Xylopia unguiculata

Xylopia unguiculata, commonly known as the African bitter apple, is a member of the Anacardiaceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa, particularly in countries like Nigeria and Ghana. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. Its flowers are small and clustered, often greenish-white in color, while the fruit is a small, round drupe that turns red when mature.

The plant is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
