Xyris formosana

Xyris formosana, commonly known as the Formosan yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes various species of yellow-eyed grasses. The plant is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands.

Xyris formosana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
