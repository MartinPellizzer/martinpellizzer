Xyris capnoides

Xyris capnoides, commonly known as the yellow-eyed grass, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks.

Xyris capnoides is an important plant for pollinators and contributes to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
