Xanthosoma nambiense

Xanthosoma nambiense, commonly known as the African arrowleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant features large, arrow-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a waxy texture. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in humid lowland forests.

Xanthosoma nambiense is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some African cultures.
