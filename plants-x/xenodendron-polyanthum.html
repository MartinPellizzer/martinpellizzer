Xenodendron polyanthum

Xenodendron polyanthum, commonly known as the clustered flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded canopy and vibrant, flame-like orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental species in warm climates.

Its rapid growth and showy display make it a favored choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
