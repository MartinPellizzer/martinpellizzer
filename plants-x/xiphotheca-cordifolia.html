Xiphotheca cordifolia

Xiphotheca cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its succulent, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas. It is drought-tolerant and has a compact growth habit, making it suitable for rock gardens.

Xiphotheca cordifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
