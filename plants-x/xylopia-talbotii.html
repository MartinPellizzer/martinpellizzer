Xylopia talbotii

Xylopia talbotii, commonly known as the African bitter almond.

It is a member of the Annonaceae family and is native to tropical Africa. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant flowers and woody, branched stems. Its leaves are typically alternate and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed, and the plant is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
