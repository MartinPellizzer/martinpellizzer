Xanthosoma poeppigii var. mafaffa

Xanthosoma poeppigii var. mafaffa, commonly known as the African arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and as a vegetable. The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers, followed by edible starchy tubers that are a staple in many local cuisines. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is well-suited for cultivation in humid climates.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
