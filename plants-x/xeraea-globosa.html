Xeraea globosa

Xeraea globosa, commonly known as the globular cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rounded, barrel-shaped stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual element to its desert habitat. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and deterring herbivores.

Xeraea globosa is well adapted to extreme temperatures and low precipitation, making it a resilient example of xerophytic vegetation.
