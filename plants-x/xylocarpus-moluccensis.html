Xylocarpus moluccensis

Xylocarpus moluccensis, commonly known as the Moluccan ironwood, is a tropical tree native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood, which is highly valued for its durability and strength. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered, often greenish-yellow in color, and it produces woody capsules as fruit.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
