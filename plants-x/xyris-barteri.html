Xyris barteri

Xyris barteri, commonly known as the Barter's goldeneye, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes plants commonly referred to as yellow-eyed grasses. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in swamps, marshes, and along stream banks.

Xyris barteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland habitats.
