Xeraea filaginoides

Xeraea filaginoides, commonly known as the desert bell, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it store water in its native dry environment. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Xeraea filaginoides is often found in rocky soils and can thrive in full sun.

Its ability to survive in harsh conditions makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping in drought-prone areas.
