Xanthomyrtus flavida subsp. flavida

Xanthomyrtus flavida subsp.

flavida, commonly known as the yellow myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically orange to red in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
