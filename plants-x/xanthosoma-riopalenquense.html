Xanthosoma riopalenquense

Xanthosoma riopalenquense, commonly known as the Rio Palenquense arrowleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces striking inflorescences with a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe, often in shades of yellow or orange. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Xanthosoma riopalenquense is also valued for its edible leaves, which are used in traditional cuisines.
