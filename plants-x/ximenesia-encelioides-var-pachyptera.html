Ximenesia encelioides var. pachyptera

Ximenesia encelioides var. pachyptera, commonly known as the broad-leafed ximenesia, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces yellow flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its robust root system allows it to thrive in poor soils and withstand prolonged droughts.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils.
