Xiphopteris aethiopica

Xiphopteris aethiopica, commonly known as the African chain fern, is a species of fern native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright fronds that form a dense, grass-like appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or along stream banks. Its sporangia are clustered in sori along the margins of the fronds, typical of the Dryopteridaceae family.

Xiphopteris aethiopica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
