Xyris machrisiana

Xyris machrisiana, commonly known as the yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowering stems are often erect and can reach heights of up to 3 feet.

Xyris machrisiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
