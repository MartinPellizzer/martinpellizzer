Xylophylla obovata

Xylophylla obovata, commonly known as the broad-ovate wood plant, is a unique species characterized by its woody structure and broad, ovate leaves.

It belongs to the family Xylophyllaceae and is native to specific regions in tropical climates. The plant exhibits a distinct growth pattern with a central stem and lateral branches, which contribute to its structural complexity. Its leaves are typically thick and have a waxy surface, aiding in water retention in arid environments.

Xylophylla obovata is also notable for its small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the rainy season.
