Xyris simulans var. subtortula

Xyris simulans var. subtortula, commonly known as the narrowleaf yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and swampy areas, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its inflorescence is often described as resembling a cluster of eyes, giving it its common name.

This variety is distinguished by its narrower leaves compared to the typical form of Xyris simulans.
