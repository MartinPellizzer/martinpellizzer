Xyris membranibracteata

Xyris membranibracteata, commonly known as the membranous bract xyris, is a species of flowering plant in the family Xyridaceae.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and the coastal areas of Georgia. The plant is characterized by its grass-like foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, preferring moist to wet soil conditions.

The species is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its adaptability and ecological importance.
