Xyris capensis var. pallescens

Xyris capensis var. pallescens, commonly known as the pale yellow marsh yellowflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wet, marshy habitats and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that helps it thrive in waterlogged soils. Its flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences and bloom during the summer months.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in wetland ecosystems.
