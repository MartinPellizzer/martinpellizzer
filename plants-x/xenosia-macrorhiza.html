Xenosia macrorhiza

Xenosia macrorhiza, commonly known as the large-rooted xenosia, is a unique plant species characterized by its robust, tuberous roots that can grow up to several centimeters in diameter.

It typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in temperate woodland environments. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth, waxy surface, and its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences. Xenosia macrorhiza is known for its ability to store significant amounts of water, which allows it to survive in periods of drought.

Due to its specialized adaptations, it plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.
