Xanthosoma trichophyllum

Xanthosoma trichophyllum, commonly known as the velvet leaf or swamp taro, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces striking, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by a colorful spathe, which is typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in swamps or along riverbanks.

Xanthosoma trichophyllum is also valued for its edible leaves, which are commonly used in traditional cuisines.
