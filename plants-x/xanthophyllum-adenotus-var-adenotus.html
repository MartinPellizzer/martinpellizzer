Xanthophyllum adenotus var. adenotus

Xanthophyllum adenotus var. adenotus, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy texture.

The botanical name reflects its distinctive yellow coloration and its specific variety classification.
