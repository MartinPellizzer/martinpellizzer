Xyris setigera var. elongata

Xyris setigera var. elongata, commonly known as the long-stemmed yellowroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the genus Xyris, which is part of the family Xyridaceae, commonly referred to as the yellow-eyed grass family. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in wet or moist soils, often found in swampy areas or along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in wetland ecosystems and a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists.
