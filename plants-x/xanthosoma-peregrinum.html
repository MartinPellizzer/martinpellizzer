Xanthosoma peregrinum

Xanthosoma peregrinum, commonly known as the giant taro, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1.5 meters in length. The plant produces a spadix inflorescence surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its edible corms.

Xanthosoma peregrinum is also valued for its ornamental foliage in horticulture.
