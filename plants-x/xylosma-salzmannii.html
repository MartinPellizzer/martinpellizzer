Xylosma salzmannii

Xylosma salzmannii, commonly known as the African xylosma, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Tiliaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including savannas and woodland edges.

The bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
