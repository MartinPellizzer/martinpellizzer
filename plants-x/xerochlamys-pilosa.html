Xerochlamys pilosa

Xerochlamys pilosa, commonly known as the hairy leathery plant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are covered in a fine, hairy texture, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to full sun exposure. Its flowering stems are often branched and can produce small, inconspicuous flowers.

Xerochlamys pilosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
