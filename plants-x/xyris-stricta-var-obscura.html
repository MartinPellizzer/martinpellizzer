Xyris stricta var. obscura

Xyris stricta var. obscura, commonly known as the darkleaf yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green, contributing to its striking appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for wetland restoration and ecological studies.
