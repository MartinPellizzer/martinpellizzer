Xanthium orientale var. albinum

Xanthium orientale var. albinum, commonly known as white cocklebur, is a variant of the common cocklebur characterized by its pale, almost white, bracts and fruits.

It is an annual herbaceous plant native to Eurasia, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. The plant produces spiny, spherical fruits that contain seeds with a tuft of silky hairs. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough, serrated margin and a hairy texture.

The species is known for its allergenic potential due to the fine hairs on its seeds and fruits.
