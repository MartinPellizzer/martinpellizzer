Xeropetalum cinerascens

Xeropetalum cinerascens, commonly known as the ash-gray rockrose, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its gray-green foliage and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant is adapted to arid environments, featuring dense, waxy leaves that reduce water loss. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Xeropetalum cinerascens is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
