Xiphion fontanesii

Xiphion fontanesii, commonly known as the Canary Islands daffodil, is a striking bulbous perennial native to the Canary Islands.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant typically blooms in spring, with flowers reaching up to 15 centimeters in length. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Xiphion fontanesii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
