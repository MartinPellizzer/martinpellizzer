Xanthorrhoea hastile

Xanthorrhoea hastile, commonly known as the narrow-leaved grass tree, is a unique plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Xanthorrhoeaceae and is characterized by its tall, grass-like foliage and a central woody stem. The plant produces small, white or yellow flowers in spikes, which bloom infrequently, often after bushfires. Xanthorrhoea hastile is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing remarkable resilience to drought and fire.

Its structure and growth patterns make it an important part of the local ecosystem and a symbol of Australia's unique flora.
