Xyris indica

Xyris indica, commonly known as the yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers, which are typically arranged in spike-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Xyris indica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies and wetland conservation efforts.
