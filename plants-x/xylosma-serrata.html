Xylosma serrata

Xylosma serrata, commonly known as the serrated xylosma, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite, and often serrated leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in cyathia, a feature common in the Euphorbiaceae family. Its bark is fibrous and can be used in traditional medicine, while its leaves are sometimes used as a vegetable.

Xylosma serrata is also valued for its ornamental potential due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
