Xanthosoma purpuratum

Xanthosoma purpuratum, commonly known as the purple-leaf elephant ear, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with purple veins. The plant produces small, white or yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Xanthosoma purpuratum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage.
