Xyris elegantula

Xyris elegantula, commonly known as the slender marsh yelloweye, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its inflorescence is a dense, spike-like cluster that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Xyris elegantula is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
