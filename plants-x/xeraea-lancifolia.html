Xeraea lancifolia

Xeraea lancifolia, commonly known as the sharpleaf xeraea, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, which bloom during the cooler months. It is well adapted to drought conditions and can survive in poor soil, making it a hardy species.

Xeraea lancifolia is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
