Xyris gracillima

Xyris gracillima, commonly known as the slender yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various species of grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, thriving in moist, acidic soils. It produces small, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its slender, grass-like foliage and compact growth habit make it a distinctive and ecologically valuable plant in its habitat.
