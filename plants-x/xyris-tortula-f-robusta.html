Xyris tortula f. robusta

Xyris tortula f. robusta, commonly known as the robust marsh yelloweye, is a variant of the marsh yelloweye plant.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. It features grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in the spring.

The botanical characteristics of this plant include its rhizomatous growth habit and its ability to tolerate periodic flooding.
