Xylosteon nigrum

Xylosteon nigrum, commonly known as blackwood, is a shrub or small tree native to the eastern regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its dark, hard wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a rough texture. Xylosteon nigrum produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.
