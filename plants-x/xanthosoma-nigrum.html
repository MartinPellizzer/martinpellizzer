Xanthosoma nigrum

Xanthosoma nigrum, commonly known as black taro, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white to yellowish flowers that are often followed by orange or red berries. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.

Xanthosoma nigrum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental and edible qualities.
