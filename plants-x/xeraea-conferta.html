Xeraea conferta

Xeraea conferta, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a smooth, waxy surface that helps reduce water loss.

Xeraea conferta is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
