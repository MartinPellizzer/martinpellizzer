Xerorchis trichorhiza

Xerorchis trichorhiza, commonly known as the hairy root orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its fibrous root system and compact, often hairy appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has small, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellowish, with three sepals and three petals, and it blooms in late spring or early summer.

Xerorchis trichorhiza is adapted to arid conditions, making it a resilient species in its native environment.
