Xyris humpatensis

Xyris humpatensis, commonly known as the Humpatensis yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various species of yellow-eyed grasses. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring. It typically grows in moist, sandy soils in woodland or grassland habitats.

Xyris humpatensis is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to well-drained soils.
