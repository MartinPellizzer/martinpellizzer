Xyris calocephala

Xyris calocephala, commonly known as the yellow-eyed grass, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its inflorescence consists of a cluster of small, tubular flowers that are often surrounded by three prominent bracts.

Xyris calocephala plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
