Xyris tenella var. riedeliana

Xyris tenella var. riedeliana, commonly known as the Riedel's yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes various species of yellow-eyed grasses. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in wetlands.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for wetland ecosystems and a subject of study in plant taxonomy.
