Xanthosia silvatica

Xanthosia silvatica, commonly known as the silverleaf shrub, is a perennial plant native to the temperate regions of the southern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and early summer. Xanthosia silvatica thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a hardy species suitable for a variety of garden settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
