Xyris lacerata

Xyris lacerata, commonly known as the narrowleaf yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Xyridaceae family, which includes various species of grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to wet soil conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are yellow and clustered in spike-like inflorescences.

Xyris lacerata is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological importance and adaptability to aquatic environments.
