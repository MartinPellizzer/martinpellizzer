Xyris guianensis

Xyris guianensis, commonly known as the Guianan yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various species of yellow-eyed grasses. The plant is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers. It thrives in wetland and marshy environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Xyris guianensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
