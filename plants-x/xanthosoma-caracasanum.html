Xanthosoma caracasanum

Xanthosoma caracasanum, commonly known as the giant taro, is a herbaceous plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces an inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of the arum family. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often cultivated for its edible corms.

The leaves are also used in traditional crafts and as a decorative element in gardens.
