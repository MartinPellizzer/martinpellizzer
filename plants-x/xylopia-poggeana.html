Xylopia poggeana

Xylopia poggeana, commonly known as the African bitter apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by woody, berry-like fruits. Its leaves and fruits contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine.

However, the plant is also known for its toxicity, with some parts containing irritants and potentially harmful chemicals.
