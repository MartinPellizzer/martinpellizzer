Xyris reitzii

Xyris reitzii, commonly known as Reitz's goldeneye, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various species of yellow-flowered plants. The plant is characterized by its grass-like leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. It typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, preferring moist, acidic soils.

Xyris reitzii is often found in swampy habitats and is an important component of the wetland ecosystem.
