Xerotes benthamiana

Xerotes benthamiana, commonly known as the Bentham's xerophyte, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its succulent leaves and reduced leaf surface area, which help minimize water loss in dry environments. The plant exhibits a compact growth habit, often forming dense clusters to reduce exposure to wind and sunlight. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, often in shades of yellow or green, adapted for pollination by insects.

Xerotes benthamiana is well-suited to harsh climates and is often used in ecological restoration projects in semi-arid regions.
