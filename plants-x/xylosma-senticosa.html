Xylosma senticosa

Xylosma senticosa, commonly known as the sticky xylosma, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves that often emit a resinous scent when crushed. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. Its fruits are dry, capsule-like structures that release tiny seeds when mature.

Xylosma senticosa is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
