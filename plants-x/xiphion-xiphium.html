Xiphion xiphium

Xiphion xiphium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved larkspur, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers in late spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance. Xiphion xiphium is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate, spire-like flower clusters.
