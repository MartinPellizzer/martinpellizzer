Xyris sphaerocephala

Xyris sphaerocephala, commonly known as the globehead yellow-eyed grass, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes various species of yellow-eyed grasses. The plant is characterized by its compact, rounded inflorescence that resembles a globe, hence its common name. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically growing in dense clusters at the base.

The flowers, which are yellow and tubular, bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
