Xamesike glauca

Xamesike glauca, commonly known as the blue glaucous plant, is a species characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions, where it thrives in well-drained soils. The plant features small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, reducing water loss. Its flowers are typically blue or violet, adding a subtle aesthetic to its natural habitat.

Xamesike glauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
