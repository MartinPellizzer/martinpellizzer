Xyris fredericoi

Xyris fredericoi, commonly known as the Frederico's goldeneye, is a member of the Xyridaceae family, native to the southeastern United States.

This perennial herb typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. It features grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in shaded or partially sunny environments.

Xyris fredericoi plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a habitat for various small organisms.
