Xenopoma

Xenopoma, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and have a rough texture, while its fruits are fleshy berries that contain several seeds.

Xenopoma is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its nocturnal blooming habit.
