Xylopia undulata

Xylopia undulata, commonly known as the wavy-leaf xylopia, is a member of the Annonaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. The plant is characterized by its ovate, wavy-margined leaves and small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or greenish-white. Its fruit is a round, fleshy drupe that contains several seeds, and it plays a role in local ecosystems as a food source for various animals.

The plant is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
