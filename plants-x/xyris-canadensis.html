Xyris canadensis

Xyris canadensis, commonly known as yellow-eyed grass, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It typically grows in moist to wet soils in open woodlands, swamps, and marshes.

Xyris canadensis is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
