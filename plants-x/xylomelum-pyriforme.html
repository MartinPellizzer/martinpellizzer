Xylomelum pyriforme

Xylomelum pyriforme, commonly known as the pear-shaped xylomelum, is a small evergreen shrub native to southwestern Australia.

It belongs to the family Proteaceae and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces woody, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

Xylomelum pyriforme is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
