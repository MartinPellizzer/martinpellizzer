Xanthosoma grayumii

Xanthosoma grayumii, commonly known as the giant arrowleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces striking inflorescences consisting of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Xanthosoma grayumii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
