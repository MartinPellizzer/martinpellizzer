Xylopia langsdorfiana

Xylopia langsdorfiana, commonly known as the African allspice or langsdorfiana wax tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to western and central Africa.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, woody fruits and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its bark and fruits have been used in traditional medicine for their purported medicinal properties.

Xylopia langsdorfiana is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
