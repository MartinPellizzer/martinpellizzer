Xyris porphyrea

Xyris porphyrea, commonly known as the purple marsh yelloweye, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the genus Xyris, which includes various species of marsh plants. The plant typically grows in wet, acidic soils near water bodies such as swamps and marshes. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Xyris porphyrea is characterized by its grass-like foliage and is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological importance.
