Xyris serotina var. curtissii

Xyris serotina var. curtissii, commonly known as the late blooming yellowroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils in pine flatwoods and wetlands, preferring partial to full shade. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in late summer to early autumn. Its botanical characteristics include a rhizomatous root system and small, lance-shaped leaves.

It is often found in association with other wetland species, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
