Xysmalobium decipiens

Xysmalobium decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is a perennial herb with a sympodial growth habit, characterized by its rhizomatous structure. The plant produces showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers that are often pale yellow to orange in color. Its botanical features include a basal rosette of leaves and a flowering stem that bears a single, striking inflorescence.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
