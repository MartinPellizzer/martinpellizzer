Xyris mentiens

Xyris mentiens, commonly known as the yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks, preferring moist to wet soil conditions. Its flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences and bloom in the spring.

Xyris mentiens plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
