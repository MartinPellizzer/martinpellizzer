Xeronema callistemon f. bracteosa

Xeronema callistemon f. bracteosa, commonly known as the bracteosa form of the bottlebrush plant, is a variant of the species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bright red or orange cylindrical flower spikes that resemble a bottlebrush. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and dry garden environments.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
