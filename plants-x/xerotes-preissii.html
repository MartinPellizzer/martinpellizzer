Xerotes preissii

Xerotes preissii, commonly known as the Schomburgk's cactus, is a rare and striking member of the Cactaceae family native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom during the rainy season. The plant has a spiny exterior, which helps protect it from herbivores and excessive water loss. Xerotes preissii is adapted to extreme drought conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, it is considered vulnerable to environmental changes and human encroachment.
