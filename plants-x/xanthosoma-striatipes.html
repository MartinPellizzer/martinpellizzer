Xanthosoma striatipes

Xanthosoma striatipes, commonly known as the arrow leaf elephant ear, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces striking, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by a colorful spathe, often in shades of yellow or white. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its foliage is also used in traditional cuisines and for medicinal purposes in some regions.
