Xyris roycei

Xyris roycei, commonly known as the Royce's goldeneye, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes various species of grass-like plants. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often growing in dense clusters.

Xyris roycei is typically found in moist, sandy soils in pine flatwoods and wetlands.
