Xeraea rodantha

Xeraea rodantha, commonly known as the desert bellflower, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming during the rainy season. Xeraea rodantha thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to desert conditions.

Its unique morphology and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for xeriscaping in drought-prone areas.
