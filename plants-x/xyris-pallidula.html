Xyris pallidula

Xyris pallidula, commonly known as the pale yellow marsh grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various grass-like plants. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often growing in dense clumps.

Xyris pallidula thrives in wet or moist soils, typically found in swampy areas and along the edges of ponds.
