Xerotes savannorum

Xerotes savannorum, commonly known as the savanna xerophyte, is a hardy shrub adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

It possesses thick, waxy leaves that minimize water loss through transpiration. Its deep root system allows it to access groundwater, enabling survival in nutrient-poor soils. The plant produces small, pale flowers that are adapted for wind pollination.

It plays a crucial role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small desert animals.
