Xerotes drummondii

Xerotes drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, white or pale green berries that are toxic to most animals. It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.
