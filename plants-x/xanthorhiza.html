Xanthorhiza

Xanthorhiza, commonly known as goldenroot, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange rhizomes, which are the primary part used medicinally. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It features trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.

Xanthorhiza has a long history of traditional use for its purported health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.
