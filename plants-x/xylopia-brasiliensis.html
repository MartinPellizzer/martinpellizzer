Xylopia brasiliensis

Xylopia brasiliensis, commonly known as Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark reddish-brown bark. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by berries that are toxic to humans and livestock. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Xylopia brasiliensis is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
