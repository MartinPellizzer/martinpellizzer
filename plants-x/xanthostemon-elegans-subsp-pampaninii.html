Xanthostemon elegans subsp. pampaninii

Xanthostemon elegans subsp.

pampaninii, commonly known as the pink-flowered honeycomb plant, is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is native to northern Australia. It is characterized by its striking pink or red flowers that bloom in dense, cylindrical clusters, giving it a unique appearance.

The plant has smooth, often glabrous stems and leaves that are typically narrow and lance-shaped. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
