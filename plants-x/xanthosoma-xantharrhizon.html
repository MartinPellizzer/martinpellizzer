Xanthosoma xantharrhizon

Xanthosoma xantharrhizon, commonly known as the yellow arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white spadices, which are typical of the arum family. Its tubers, which are rich in starch, are used as a food source in many regions.

Xanthosoma xantharrhizon is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
