Xyris tennesseensis

Xyris tennesseensis, commonly known as the Tennessee goldeneye, is a species of flowering plant in the family Xyridaceae.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetlands and moist soils, often found in the southeastern United States. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in swampy or seasonally flooded habitats.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and ecological specificity.
