Xiphopteris blepharodes

Xiphopteris blepharodes, commonly known as the narrow-leaf huperzia, is a species of clubmoss belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, scale-like leaves that are arranged in a tight spiral. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. It produces spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are clustered in strobili at the tips of the branches.

Xiphopteris blepharodes is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
