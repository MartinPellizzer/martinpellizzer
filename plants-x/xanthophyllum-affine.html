Xanthophyllum affine

Xanthophyllum affine, commonly known as the yellow-flowered saxifrage, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. The plant is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, particularly in Europe and Asia. It thrives in moist, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous areas.

Xanthophyllum affine is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
