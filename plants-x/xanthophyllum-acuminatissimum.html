Xanthophyllum acuminatissimum

Xanthophyllum acuminatissimum, commonly known as the sharp-pointed goldenleaf, is a rare flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange foliage and small, delicate flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a pointed tip, which give it its scientific name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
