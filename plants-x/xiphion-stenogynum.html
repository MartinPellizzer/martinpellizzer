Xiphion stenogynum

Xiphion stenogynum, commonly known as the narrow-mouthed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, with narrow, pointed petals. The plant has a compact growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal habitats.

Xiphion stenogynum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
