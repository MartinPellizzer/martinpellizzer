Xyris lucida

Xyris lucida, commonly known as the shining yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes various species of grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Xyris lucida is typically found in moist, sandy soils in open woods or along stream banks.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a native plant that supports local pollinators and contributes to ecological diversity.
