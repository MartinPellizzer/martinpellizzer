Xyris dilungensis

Xyris dilungensis, commonly known as the Dilungensis Yellowroot, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa, particularly South Africa.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes various grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and produces clusters of yellow flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Xyris dilungensis is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
