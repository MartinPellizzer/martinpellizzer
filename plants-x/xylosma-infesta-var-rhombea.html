Xylosma infesta var. rhombea

Xylosma infesta var. rhombea, commonly known as the diamondleaf xylosma, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite, and often elliptical or rhomboid-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruits are dry, capsule-like structures that split open to release seeds.

Xylosma infesta var. rhombea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
