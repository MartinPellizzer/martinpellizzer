Xeroplana

Xeroplana, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its succulent leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant has adapted to survive in dry environments by storing water in its thick, fleshy stems and leaves. Its flowers are typically pink or purple and attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

Xeroplana plays an important ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing food for desert wildlife.
