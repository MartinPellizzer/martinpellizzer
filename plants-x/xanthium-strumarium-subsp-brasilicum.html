Xanthium strumarium subsp. brasilicum

Xanthium strumarium subsp.

brasilicum, commonly known as Brazilian cocklebur, is an annual herbaceous plant native to South America. It features rough, spiny leaves with a distinctive heart-shaped base and produces yellow-green flowers that develop into spiny, spherical fruits.

The plant is characterized by its robust growth habit and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. It has a cosmopolitan distribution and is considered an invasive species in many regions.

The seeds are dispersed by animals and can remain viable in the soil for several years.
