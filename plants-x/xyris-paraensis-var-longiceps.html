Xyris paraensis var. longiceps

Xyris paraensis var. longiceps, commonly known as the long-headed yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of streams, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, while its inflorescence consists of numerous small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer.

Xyris paraensis var. longiceps is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and ability to tolerate wet conditions.
