Xylopia pyrifolia

Xylopia pyrifolia, commonly known as the flame tree or pyrifolia flame tree, is a tropical plant belonging to the Annonaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that resemble flames, typically in shades of red and orange. The plant produces a fruit that is woody and contains a single seed, which is an important feature for its propagation. Xylopia pyrifolia thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
