Xeranthemum ericoides

Xeranthemum ericoides, commonly known as the heath-like eryngo, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Xeranthemum ericoides is known for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
