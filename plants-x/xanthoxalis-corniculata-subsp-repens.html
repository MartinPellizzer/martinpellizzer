Xanthoxalis corniculata subsp. repens

Xanthoxalis corniculata subsp.

repens, commonly known as creeping yellow oxalis, is a low-growing perennial herb native to South America. It features small, trifoliate leaves with yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively along the ground. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive and resilient ground cover plant.
