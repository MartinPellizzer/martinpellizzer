Xyris bracteata

Xyris bracteata, commonly known as the bracteate yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in the spring. Xyris bracteata typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swampy areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for wetland ecosystems and ecological studies.
