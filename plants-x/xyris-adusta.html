Xyris adusta

Xyris adusta, commonly known as the showy goldeneye, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the genus Xyris, which includes approximately 150 species of flowering plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow to orange inflorescences that resemble candles, hence its common name. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.

Xyris adusta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
