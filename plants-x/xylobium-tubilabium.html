Xylobium tubilabium

Xylobium tubilabium, commonly known as the horned orchid, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, tubular flowers that resemble horns. The plant typically grows in wet, swampy areas and has a short, creeping rhizome. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces showy, fragrant blooms that attract various pollinators.

Xylobium tubilabium is a rare and endangered species, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
