Xantolis siamensis

Xantolis siamensis, commonly known as the Siam orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually small and come in shades of white, pink, and purple.

Xantolis siamensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
