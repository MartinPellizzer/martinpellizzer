Xerophyta seinei

Xerophyta seinei, commonly known as the Seine's ice plant, is a succulent shrub native to the cold, arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Xerophyta seinei is adapted to low-nutrient soils and can tolerate extreme temperature fluctuations.

Its unique morphology and resilience make it a fascinating subject for botanical study.
