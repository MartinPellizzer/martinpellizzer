Xylosma ovata var. parvifolia

Xylosma ovata var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved xylosma, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, elliptic leaves that are typically small and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

The species is valued for its ornamental potential and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
