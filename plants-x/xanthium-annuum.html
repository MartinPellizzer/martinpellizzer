Xanthium annuum

Xanthium annuum, commonly known as cocklebur, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces spiny, spherical fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a tough, prickly husk. Its leaves are typically lobed and have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and often branching.

Xanthium annuum is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
