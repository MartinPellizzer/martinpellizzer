Xanthium strumarium var. revelierei

Xanthium strumarium var. revelierei, commonly known as the rough cocklebur, is a variant of the cocklebur plant native to temperate regions of the Americas.

It is an annual herbaceous plant characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and large, spherical fruit that contains several seeds. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and other open areas. Its botanical features include a hairy stem, yellow flowers, and a distinctive, prickly fruit that aids in seed dispersal.

It is often considered a weed but has historical use in traditional medicine.
