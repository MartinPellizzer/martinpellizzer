Xyris pirapamae

Xyris pirapamae, commonly known as the Pirapama yellow-eyed grass, is a species of flowering plant in the family Xyridaceae.

It is native to Brazil, specifically the state of Pernambuco, where it grows in moist, sandy soils. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers during the wet season. It is typically found in open habitats such as grasslands and forest edges.

Xyris pirapamae plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to plant biodiversity.
