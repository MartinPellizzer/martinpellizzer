Xerococcus lindenii

Xerococcus lindenii, commonly known as the Lindley's rockrose, is a member of the Ericaceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is a low-growing, evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in the spring, which are typically borne on short pedicels. Xerococcus lindenii is adapted to arid environments, exhibiting xerophytic features such as thick cuticles and reduced leaf surface area.

It is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover in dry, well-drained soils.
