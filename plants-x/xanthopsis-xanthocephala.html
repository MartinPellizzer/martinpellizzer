Xanthopsis xanthocephala

Xanthopsis xanthocephala, commonly known as the yellow-headed orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a distinctive orange or red lip. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens, though it requires well-drained soil and careful watering to flourish.
