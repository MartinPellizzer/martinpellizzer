Xyris tenella var. tenella

Xyris tenella var. tenella, commonly known as the narrowleaf yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features grass-like leaves that are narrow and linear, with a distinctive yellow flower head that resembles a small eye. The plant typically grows in wet to mesic habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing a showy display of yellow blooms.

The species is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in coastal and inland wetlands.
