Xanthophyllum subglobosum var. longifolium

Xanthophyllum subglobosum var. longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved goldenbush, is a shrub native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, golden-yellow foliage that provides striking visual contrast in its natural habitat. The plant features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
