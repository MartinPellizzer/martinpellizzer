Xolisma villosa var. pubescens

Xolisma villosa var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy xolisma, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and extreme temperatures. It plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing nectar for pollinators.

Its botanical features make it an important species in desert ecosystems.
