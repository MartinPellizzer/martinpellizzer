Xyris baldwiniana

Xyris baldwiniana, commonly known as Baldwin's goldenroot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces yellowish flowers in late spring to early summer. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Xyris baldwiniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
