Xerophyta eglandulosa var. trichocarpa

Xerophyta eglandulosa var. trichocarpa, commonly known as the woolly randveld grass, is a perennial grass species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-gray hue due to the presence of fine trichomes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping in regions with Mediterranean climates.
