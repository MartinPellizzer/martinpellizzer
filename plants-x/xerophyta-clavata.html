Xerophyta clavata

Xerophyta clavata, commonly known as the spearhead iceplant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in arid environments. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. Xerophyta clavata is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall, making it a hardy species.

Its unique morphology and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping in dry climates.
