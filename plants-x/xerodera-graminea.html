Xerodera graminea

Xerodera graminea, commonly known as the desert grass, is a perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its low-growing, tufted foliage and fibrous root system that helps it survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or pale in color. It is well adapted to extreme temperatures and can thrive with minimal water.

Xerodera graminea plays an important role in stabilizing soil in desert environments.
