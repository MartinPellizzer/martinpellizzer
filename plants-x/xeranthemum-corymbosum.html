Xeranthemum corymbosum

Xeranthemum corymbosum, commonly known as the common marsh thyme, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has aromatic leaves that are often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Xeranthemum corymbosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
