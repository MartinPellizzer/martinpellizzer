Xanthosoma paradoxum

Xanthosoma paradoxum, commonly known as the arrow leaf elephant ear, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant belongs to the Araceae family and produces striking yellow spathes with white spadices. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Xanthosoma paradoxum is also valued in some regions for its edible leaves, which are used in traditional cuisines.
