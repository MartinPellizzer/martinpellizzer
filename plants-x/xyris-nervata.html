Xyris nervata

Xyris nervata, commonly known as the narrow-veined yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Xyridaceae family, which includes various grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist habitats, thriving in acidic soils. Its grass-like appearance is characterized by narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers.

Xyris nervata plays an important ecological role in its native environment by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
