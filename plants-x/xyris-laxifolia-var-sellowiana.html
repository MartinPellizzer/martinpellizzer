Xyris laxifolia var. sellowiana

Xyris laxifolia var. sellowiana, commonly known as the Sellow's golden club, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various grass-like plants. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces yellowish-green inflorescences composed of numerous small flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
