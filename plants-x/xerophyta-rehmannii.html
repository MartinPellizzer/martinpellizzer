Xerophyta rehmannii

Xerophyta rehmannii, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a succulent shrub that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to water scarcity. The plant features thick, fleshy leaves that store moisture, and its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers are typically yellow or orange. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Xerophyta rehmannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
