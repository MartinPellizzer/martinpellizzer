Xyris atrospicata

Xyris atrospicata, commonly known as blackstem yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the genus Xyris, which is part of the grass family, Poaceae. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Xyris atrospicata is characterized by its dark green stems and is often found in moist, acidic soils in woodland areas.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
