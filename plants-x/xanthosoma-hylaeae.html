Xanthosoma hylaeae

Xanthosoma hylaeae, commonly known as the mountain banana, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant produces striking yellowish-green spathes that enclose a cluster of small, white flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible underground stems.

Xanthosoma hylaeae is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
