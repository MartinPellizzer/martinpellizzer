Xolisma foliosa

Xolisma foliosa, commonly known as the leafy desert plant, is a perennial shrub native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage, which consists of small, leathery leaves arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. Xolisma foliosa is adapted to withstand extreme drought conditions, with deep root systems that access groundwater.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in desert environments.
