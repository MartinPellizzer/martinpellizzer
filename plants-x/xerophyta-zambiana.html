Xerophyta zambiana

Xerophyta zambiana, commonly known as the Zambian ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in arid environments. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its adaptability, it is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
