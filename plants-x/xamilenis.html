Xamilenis

Xamilenis, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright orange and yellow flowers that resemble flames, hence its common name. The plant has long, slender stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. Xamilenis thrives in humid environments with high levels of indirect sunlight.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
