Xyris castanea

Xyris castanea, commonly known as the chestnut root or yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes various grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a spike-like cluster. It thrives in wetland habitats and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Xyris castanea is characterized by its distinctive reddish or brownish underground rhizomes, which contribute to its common name.
