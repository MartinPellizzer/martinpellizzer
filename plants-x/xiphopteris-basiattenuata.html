Xiphopteris basiattenuata

Xiphopteris basiattenuata, commonly known as the broad-leaf huperzia, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often recurved at the tips. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and rocky areas, where it thrives in humid conditions. Its sporangia are clustered on the underside of the leaves, a common feature in ferns.

Xiphopteris basiattenuata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
