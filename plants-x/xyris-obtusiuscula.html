Xyris obtusiuscula

Xyris obtusiuscula, commonly known as the bluntleaf yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the genus Xyris, which is part of the grass family, Poaceae. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as swamps and marshes, and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and yellowish flowers. Its leaves are broad and often have a blunt tip, giving it its specific epithet "obtusiuscula.".

The species is noted for its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife and its role in wetland ecosystems.
