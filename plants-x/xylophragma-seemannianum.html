Xylophragma seemannianum

Xylophragma seemannianum, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, tuberous roots and thick, velvety leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, often with a dark throat. It thrives in humid, well-drained soil and requires indirect light for optimal growth.

Xylophragma seemannianum is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
