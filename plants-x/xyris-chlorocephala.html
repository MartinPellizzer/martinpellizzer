Xyris chlorocephala

Xyris chlorocephala, commonly known as the yellow-eyed grass, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the genus Xyris, which is part of the family Xyridaceae, commonly referred to as the yellow-eyed grass family. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Xyris chlorocephala is often found in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in open woodlands.

Its striking yellow inflorescence makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and wetland restoration projects.
