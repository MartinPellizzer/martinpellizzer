Xyris metallica

Xyris metallica, commonly known as the metallic yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes grass-like plants known for their showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, thriving in moist, acidic soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer.

Xyris metallica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
