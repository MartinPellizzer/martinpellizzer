Xyris perrotetii

Xyris perrotetii, commonly known as the Perrotet's yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the genus Xyris, which includes various species of yellow-eyed grasses characterized by their bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Xyris perrotetii is often found in association with other wetland plants, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
