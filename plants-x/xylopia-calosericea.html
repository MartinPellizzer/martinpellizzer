Xylopia calosericea

Xylopia calosericea, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its dark, aromatic wood and distinctive, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish-green fruits that are covered in a soft, hairy coating, which gives it its common name. Xylopia calosericea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of timber.

Its growth habit includes a dense canopy and can reach heights of up to 20 meters in its natural habitat.
