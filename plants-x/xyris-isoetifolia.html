Xyris isoetifolia

Xyris isoetifolia, commonly known as the threadleaf yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Xyris isoetifolia plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
