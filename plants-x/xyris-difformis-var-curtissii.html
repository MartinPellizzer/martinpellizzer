Xyris difformis var. curtissii

Xyris difformis var. curtissii, commonly known as the curly yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, curling inflorescence that resembles a spiral, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in wetland environments.
