Xylopleurum obtusifolium

Xylopleurum obtusifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf woodpecker tree, is a species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, elliptical leaves with a waxy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing seeds.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species in its native environment.
