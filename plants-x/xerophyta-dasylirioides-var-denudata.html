Xerophyta dasylirioides var. denudata

Xerophyta dasylirioides var. denudata, commonly known as the naked leafed ice plant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, white flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. It has a sprawling growth habit and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping in Mediterranean climates.
