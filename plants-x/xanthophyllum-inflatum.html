Xanthophyllum inflatum

Xanthophyllum inflatum, commonly known as the inflated yellowflower, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated bracts that resemble small balloons, which are often brightly colored. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, contributing to its ornamental appeal. Its leaves are typically broad and glossy, providing a lush green contrast to its vibrant floral display.

Xanthophyllum inflatum is often cultivated in tropical gardens for its unique appearance and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
