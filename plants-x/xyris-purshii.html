Xyris purshii

Xyris purshii, commonly known as Pursh's goldenroot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded or partially shaded areas.

Xyris purshii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
