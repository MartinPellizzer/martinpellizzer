Xanthomyrtus humilis

Xanthomyrtus humilis, commonly known as the dwarf myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to southeastern Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. It produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Xanthomyrtus humilis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
