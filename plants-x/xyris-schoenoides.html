Xyris schoenoides

Xyris schoenoides, commonly known as the yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, typically in clusters at the base of the stems. It thrives in wet to moist soils and is often found in swampy areas or along stream banks.

Xyris schoenoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a habitat for various insects and small animals.
