Xerotes fribrillosa

Xerotes fribrillosa, commonly known as the desert featherplant, is a succulent shrub native to arid regions of South America.

It features narrow, fleshy leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the dry season. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

Xerotes fribrillosa is well adapted to harsh, drought-prone environments and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
