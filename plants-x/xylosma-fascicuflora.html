Xylosma fascicuflora

Xylosma fascicuflora, commonly known as the Mexican flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It features small, opposite, ovate leaves that turn red or orange in the fall. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Xylosma fascicuflora is also valued for its role in supporting local wildlife and its use in traditional medicine.
