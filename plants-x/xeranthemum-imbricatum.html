Xeranthemum imbricatum

Xeranthemum imbricatum, commonly known as the rock daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces showy yellow or orange flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Xeranthemum imbricatum is drought-tolerant and requires minimal care once established.
