Xanthosoma laselvaense

Xanthosoma laselvaense, commonly known as the pink river spinach, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often a vibrant green with a reddish tinge. The plant produces striking inflorescences with white spadices and yellow berries, which are edible and used in local cuisines. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental and culinary value.

Xanthosoma laselvaense is also valued for its ability to improve soil fertility through nitrogen fixation.
