Xanthium × bitteri

Xanthium × bitteri, commonly known as common cocklebur, is a biennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and distinctive spherical fruits covered in hooked bracts. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its seeds are dispersed by animals that brush against the bracts, aiding in its spread.

Xanthium × bitteri is often considered a weed but has historical uses in traditional medicine.
