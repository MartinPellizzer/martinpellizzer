Xanthium californicum var. oligacanthum

Xanthium californicum var. oligacanthum, commonly known as the California cocklebur, is a prickly annual plant native to the western United States.

It features rough, spiny leaves and distinctive spherical fruits that contain toxic seeds. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in open fields and along roadsides. It has a hairy stem and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer.

Due to its prickly nature, it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
