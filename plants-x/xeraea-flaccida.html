Xeraea flaccida

Xeraea flaccida, commonly known as the limp leaf plant, is a succulent native to South America.

It is characterized by its fleshy, elongated leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water.

Xeraea flaccida is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
