Ximenia americana var. inermis

Ximenia americana var. inermis, commonly known as the smooth ximenia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features smooth, grayish bark and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces yellow, fleshy fruits that are edible when ripe, though they are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a distinctive waxy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.
