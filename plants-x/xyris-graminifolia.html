Xyris graminifolia

Xyris graminifolia, commonly known as the grass-like yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Xyridaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to wet soil conditions. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

Xyris graminifolia plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
