Xanthophyllum glaucescens

Xanthophyllum glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-green fern, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue-green foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests. It has feather-like leaves that are often arranged in a whorled pattern.

Xanthophyllum glaucescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
