Xanthosoma cordifolium

Xanthosoma cordifolium, commonly known as the heartleaf arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces an inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of the arum family. Its rhizomes are edible and have been traditionally used as a starch source in various cuisines.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
