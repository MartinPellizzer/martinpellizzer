Xeracina scandens

Xeracina scandens, commonly known as the climbing rock fig, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its woody, climbing stems that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, fleshy fruits. Xeracina scandens thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall.

It plays an important ecological role in providing food for native wildlife in its habitat.
