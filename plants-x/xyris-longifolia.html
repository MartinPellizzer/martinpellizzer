Xyris longifolia

Xyris longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf xyris, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes various grass-like plants known for their showy flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are yellow and clustered in dense inflorescences.

Xyris longifolia plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
