Xylopia conjungens

Xylopia conjungens, commonly known as the West African bitter apple, is a tropical plant belonging to the Annonaceae family.

It is native to regions of West Africa, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or greenish in color. Its fruit is a globose berry that turns from green to yellow or red when mature.

The plant has been traditionally used in local medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
