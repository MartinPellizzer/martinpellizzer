Xyris laevigata

Xyris laevigata, commonly known as the smooth yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes various species of grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to water-saturated soils. Its leaves are grass-like and smooth, while its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in spike-like inflorescences.

Xyris laevigata plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various insects and wildlife.
