Xanthium × bushii

Xanthium × bushii, commonly known as common cocklebur, is a biennial or short-lived perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its prickly, spiny leaves and large, spherical fruits that contain toxic seeds. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and ragweeds, and is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils. Xanthium × bushii is often found in fields, roadsides, and other open areas where it can spread rapidly.

Its botanical features make it both a weed and a plant of ecological significance.
