Xiquexique × heptagonus

Xiquexique × heptagonus, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Xiquexique and Heptagonus species.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers that display a unique combination of traits from both parent species. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical and subtropical environments. Its foliage consists of broad, glossy leaves that help in photosynthesis and water retention.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its aesthetic appeal.
