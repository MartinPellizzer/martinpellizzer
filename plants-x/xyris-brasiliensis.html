Xyris brasiliensis

Xyris brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian yellow iris, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Xyris brasiliensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
