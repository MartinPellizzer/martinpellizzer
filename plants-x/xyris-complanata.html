Xyris complanata

Xyris complanata, commonly known as flatleaf yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the genus Xyris in the family Xyridaceae, which includes various species of yellow-eyed grass. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist habitats, often forming dense clusters near the water's edge. Its leaves are flat and grass-like, while its flowers are bright yellow and clustered in spike-like inflorescences.

Xyris complanata plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
