Xerochlamys acuminata

Xerochlamys acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-tipped rock orchid, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial herb that typically grows in rocky or limestone habitats. The plant produces small, delicate flowers with yellowish-green petals and a distinctive, elongated spur. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to conserve water in arid environments.
