Xerotes denticulata

Xerotes denticulata, commonly known as the toothed xerophyte, is a succulent plant native to arid regions.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant has a shallow root system to quickly absorb moisture from the soil. Its small, clustered flowers are typically pale yellow or greenish-white, adapted for pollination by insects.

Xerotes denticulata is well-suited for xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and drought tolerance.
