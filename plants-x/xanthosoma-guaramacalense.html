Xanthosoma guaramacalense

Xanthosoma guaramacalense, commonly known as the Guaramacal arrowleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. The plant produces striking inflorescences consisting of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Xanthosoma guaramacalense thrives in moist, shady environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
