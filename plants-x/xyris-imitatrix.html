Xyris imitatrix

Xyris imitatrix, commonly known as the mimic yellow-eyed grass, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes grass-like plants with showy, often yellow flowers. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in early summer. Xyris imitatrix thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its striking floral display makes it a valuable addition to native gardens and wetland restoration projects.
