Xylophacos melanocalyx

Xylophacos melanocalyx, commonly known as the black-leafed xylophacos, is a unique plant species characterized by its dark, almost black foliage.

It is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and densely packed. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in shades of white and pale yellow.

Xylophacos melanocalyx is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited use in traditional medicine.
