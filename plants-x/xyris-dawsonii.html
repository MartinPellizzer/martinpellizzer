Xyris dawsonii

Xyris dawsonii, commonly known as Dawson's goldeneye, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the genus Xyris, which is part of the family Xyridaceae, commonly known as the yellow-eyed grass family. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and swamps, and is often found in areas with moist, sandy soil.

Xyris dawsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
