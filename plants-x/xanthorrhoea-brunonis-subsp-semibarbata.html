Xanthorrhoea brunonis subsp. semibarbata

Xanthorrhoea brunonis subsp.

semibarbata, commonly known as the slender grass tree, is a unique species native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its tall, grass-like foliage and a single, woody flowering stem that emerges from a basal rosette of leaves.

The plant produces a distinctive yellowish to orange flower spike, which is often covered in fine, bristly hairs. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and fire.

This subspecies is of particular interest in ecological and botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological role in its native habitat.
