Xyris glandacea

Xyris glandacea, commonly known as the glandular yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the genus Xyris in the family Xyridaceae, which includes various species of yellow-eyed grasses. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks, where it thrives in moist to wet soil conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in spike-like inflorescences.

Xyris glandacea plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
