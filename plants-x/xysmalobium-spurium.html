Xysmalobium spurium

Xysmalobium spurium, commonly known as the desert bell, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular, salmon-pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is adapted to arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Xysmalobium spurium is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it plays a role in nitrogen fixation in its ecosystem.
