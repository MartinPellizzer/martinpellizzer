Xanthocoma humilis

Xanthocoma humilis, commonly known as the dwarf golden-rod, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Xanthocoma humilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
