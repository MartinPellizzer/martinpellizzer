Xanthium spinosum var. ambrosioides

Xanthium spinosum var. ambrosioides, commonly known as rough cocklebur, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, prickly leaves and spherical, rough-textured fruit capsules. The plant typically grows in disturbed areas such as fields and roadsides, thriving in temperate climates. Its flowers are yellow and clustered in heads, and it produces a large number of seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The plant is considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its aggressive growth and persistent seed production.
