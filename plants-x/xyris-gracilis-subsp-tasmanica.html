Xyris gracilis subsp. tasmanica

Xyris gracilis subsp.

tasmanica, commonly known as the Tasmanian slender yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herb native to Tasmania. It is characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage and small, bright yellow flower heads that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its narrow, linear leaves are often tinged with reddish hues, and the flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
