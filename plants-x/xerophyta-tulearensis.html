Xerophyta tulearensis

Xerophyta tulearensis, commonly known as the Tulear ghostflower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a succulent shrub that has adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant features fleshy, water-storing leaves and produces small, pale flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its botanical structure includes a compact growth form and a deep root system to access groundwater.

Xerophyta tulearensis is valued for its resilience and is often studied for its adaptations to extreme climates.
