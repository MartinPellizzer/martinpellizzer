Xiphotheca lanceolata

Xiphotheca lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceolate sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and well-drained soils. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Xiphotheca lanceolata is often used in rock gardens and coastal landscaping due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
