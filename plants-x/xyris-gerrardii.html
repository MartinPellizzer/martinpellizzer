Xyris gerrardii

Xyris gerrardii, commonly known as the yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Xyris gerrardii is often found in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and wetlands.

It plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
