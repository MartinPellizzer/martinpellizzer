Xanthosoma saguasense

Xanthosoma saguasense, commonly known as the Saguasense arrowleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant typically features large, arrow-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and as a vegetable in local cuisines. Xanthosoma saguasense thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and edible qualities.

Its botanical structure reflects adaptations to its native ecosystem, making it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
