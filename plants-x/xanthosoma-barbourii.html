Xanthosoma barbourii

Xanthosoma barbourii, commonly known as the giant arrowleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence known as a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant features large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, often with a waxy texture and a deep green color. Xanthosoma barbourii is typically grown as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage and is also valued for its potential use in traditional medicine.

Its growth habit is rhizomatous, allowing it to spread and form dense clumps in suitable conditions.
