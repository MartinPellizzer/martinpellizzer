Xeraea leptoclada

Xeraea leptoclada, commonly known as the slender-leaved xeraea, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring or early summer. Its root system is fibrous and shallow, allowing it to quickly absorb moisture from light rains.

Xeraea leptoclada is often found in rocky soils and is considered a rare, endemic species in its native habitat.
