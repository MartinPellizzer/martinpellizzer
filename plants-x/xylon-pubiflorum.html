Xylon pubiflorum

Xylon pubiflorum, commonly known as the hairy-flowered xylon, is a unique plant species characterized by its distinctive botanical features.

It is a shrub-like plant with woody stems that bear small, hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are typically pubescent, giving the plant its common name.

Xylon pubiflorum is native to specific regions and is adapted to grow in arid or semi-arid environments.
