Xantolis Euphlebia

Common blue violet, scientifically known as Xantolis euphlebia, is a delicate flowering plant native to various regions across North America. With its striking blue petals and heart-shaped leaves, it adds a touch of natural beauty to woodland floors and gardens. This species thrives in moist, shady environments, often found in forests and along stream banks. Its flowers, which bloom in early spring, attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Despite its modest appearance, the common blue violet plays an important role in supporting local ecosystems.
