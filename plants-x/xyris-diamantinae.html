Xyris diamantinae

Xyris diamantinae, commonly known as the Diamondleaf yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herb native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Xyris diamantinae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
