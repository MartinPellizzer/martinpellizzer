Xyris martiana

Xyris martiana, commonly known as the yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grassy leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in wet or moist soils, often found in swamps, marshes, and along stream banks.

Xyris martiana is an important species for wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
