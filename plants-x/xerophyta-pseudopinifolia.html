Xerophyta pseudopinifolia

Xerophyta pseudopinifolia, commonly known as the "False Pineapple Plant," is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, spiky leaves that store water, a key adaptation for surviving in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch to its rugged appearance. Its thick, fibrous stems help reduce water loss through transpiration.

Xerophyta pseudopinifolia is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
