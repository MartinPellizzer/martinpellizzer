Xyris grisebachii

Xyris grisebachii, commonly known as the grayleaf goldeneye, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes other species of yellow-flowered plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Xyris grisebachii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive addition to native plant gardens and wetland restoration projects.
