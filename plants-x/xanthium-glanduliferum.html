Xanthium glanduliferum

Xanthium glanduliferum, commonly known as common cocklebur, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is an annual herb characterized by its rough, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces spiny, spherical fruits that contain seeds with a distinctive hooked beak. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

Xanthium glanduliferum is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
