Xyridion xanthospurium

Xyridion xanthospurium, commonly known as the yellow-spurred xyridion, is a perennial herb native to parts of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. It typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Xyridion xanthospurium is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
