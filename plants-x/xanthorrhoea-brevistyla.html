Xanthorrhoea brevistyla

Xanthorrhoea brevistyla, commonly known as the southern grass tree, is a unique plant native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like leaves and a single, woody flowering stem that produces small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to fire-prone environments and can survive bushfires by resprouting from its underground rhizomes. Its leaves are often used in traditional Aboriginal practices, and it plays an important role in local ecosystems.

Xanthorrhoea brevistyla is also a symbol of resilience due to its ability to regenerate after disturbances.
