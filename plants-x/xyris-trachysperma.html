Xyris trachysperma

Xyris trachysperma, commonly known as the rough-seeded goldeneye, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in the summer. Its seeds are rough and hard, contributing to its common name.

Xyris trachysperma is often found in wetlands and disturbed soils, showcasing its adaptability to moist environments.
