Xamesike scordiifolia

Xamesike scordiifolia, commonly known as the fragrant leaf plant, is a herbaceous species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that emit a distinctive scent when crushed. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 centimeters and thrives in moist, well-drained soil. Its flowers are small and clustered, often white or pale yellow, and appear in the summer months.

Xamesike scordiifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
