Xyris spruceana

Xyris spruceana, commonly known as Spruce's goldeneye, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Xyridaceae family, which includes various species of yellow-flowered plants. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
