Xyris flexifolia

Xyris flexifolia, commonly known as the flexible yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes various species of grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Xyris flexifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
