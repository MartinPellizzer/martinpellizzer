Xylopia odoratissima

Xylopia odoratissima, commonly known as the fragrant wood or sweet wood, is a tropical evergreen tree belonging to the Annonaceae family.

It is native to the humid forests of Central and South America, particularly in countries like Mexico, Colombia, and Brazil. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and flowers, which emit a strong, pleasant scent. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
