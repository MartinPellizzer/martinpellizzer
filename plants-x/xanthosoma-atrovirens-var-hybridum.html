Xanthosoma atrovirens var. hybridum

Xanthosoma atrovirens var. hybridum, commonly known as the black-leaf elephant ear, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, dark green, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces striking yellow flowers with purple bracts, which are often used in ornamental gardening. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil and consistent moisture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and indoor spaces.
