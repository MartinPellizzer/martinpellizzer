Xiphagrostis japonica

Xiphagrostis japonica, commonly known as Japanese arrowgrass, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Xiphagrostis japonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
