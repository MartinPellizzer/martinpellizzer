Xanthophyllum hookerianum

Xanthophyllum hookerianum, commonly known as the Hooker's goldenbush, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish-green foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Xanthophyllum hookerianum is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to alpine ecosystems.
