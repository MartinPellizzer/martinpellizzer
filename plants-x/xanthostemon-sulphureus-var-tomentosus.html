Xanthostemon sulphureus var. tomentosus

Xanthostemon sulphureus var. tomentosus, commonly known as the sulphur-flowered rock heath, is a shrub native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, glandular, and often hairy foliage, which gives it a distinctive, woolly appearance. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, typically blooming in winter. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Its unique morphology and habitat make it an important species in the flora of the region.
