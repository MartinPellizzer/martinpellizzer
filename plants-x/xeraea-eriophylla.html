Xeraea eriophylla

Xeraea eriophylla, commonly known as the desert lily, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces striking white or pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months. Its root system is shallow but extensive, allowing it to absorb moisture from a wide area.

Xeraea eriophylla is adapted to high temperatures and low precipitation, making it a hardy species in desert ecosystems.
