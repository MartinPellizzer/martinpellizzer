Xeraea hygrophila

Xeraea hygrophila, commonly known as the water hygrophila, is a perennial aquatic plant native to tropical regions.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the warmer months. It thrives in shallow water and is often used in ornamental ponds and water gardens.

Xeraea hygrophila plays a role in water filtration and provides habitat for aquatic organisms.
