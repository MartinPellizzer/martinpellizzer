Xanthophytopsis kwangtungensis

Xanthophytopsis kwangtungensis, commonly known as the Guangdong xanthophytopsis, is a rare and lesser-known member of the Xanthophyceae class, which includes yellow-green algae.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring filamentous thalli with branching structures and the presence of pyrenoids. The plant thrives in freshwater environments, particularly in nutrient-rich ponds and slow-moving streams. Its reproductive structures are complex, involving both sexual and asexual reproduction mechanisms.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological specificity, it is of interest to botanists studying aquatic plant diversity and conservation.
