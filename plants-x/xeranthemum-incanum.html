Xeranthemum incanum

Xeranthemum incanum, commonly known as the silverleaf xeranthemum, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and showy yellow flowers. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its propagation.

Xeranthemum incanum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
