Xyris pauciflora var. oryzetorum

Xyris pauciflora var. oryzetorum, commonly known as the ricegrass yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, thriving in moist, acidic soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer. Its inflorescence consists of several spikelets, each bearing small, three-lobed flowers.

The species is notable for its ecological role in wetland ecosystems, supporting various pollinators and serving as a habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
