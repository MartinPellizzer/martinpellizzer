Xyris longiscapa

Xyris longiscapa, commonly known as the long-sepaled yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes various species of yellow-eyed grasses. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Xyris longiscapa typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for wetland ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
