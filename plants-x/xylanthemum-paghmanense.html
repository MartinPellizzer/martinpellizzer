Xylanthemum paghmanense

Xylanthemum paghmanense, commonly known as the Paghman xylanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a daisy-like appearance, and it blooms during the summer months.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
