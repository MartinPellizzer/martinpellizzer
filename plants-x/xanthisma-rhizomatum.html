Xanthisma rhizomatum

Xanthisma rhizomatum, commonly known as the yellow root plant, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fibrous rhizomes and yellowish-orange roots, which are rich in bioactive compounds. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered with a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Xanthisma rhizomatum is valued in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.
