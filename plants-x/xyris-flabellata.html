Xyris flabellata

Xyris flabellata, commonly known as the flatleaf yellow-eyed grass, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various species of grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Xyris flabellata thrives in wet to moist soils, often found in swamps, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
