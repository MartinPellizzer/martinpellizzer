Xyris paulensis

Xyris paulensis, commonly known as the Paul's yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various species of yellow-eyed grasses. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream edges. It features grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the summer months.

Xyris paulensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
