Xanthium saccharatum subsp. eusaccharatum

Xanthium saccharatum subsp.

eusaccharatum, commonly known as sugarleaf cocklebur, is a herbaceous annual plant native to North America. It is characterized by its spiny, prickly leaves and large, spherical fruit heads that contain numerous seeds.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and other open areas. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, though it is considered a weed in agricultural settings.

The species is distinguished by its pale yellow to greenish flowers and its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
