Xiphion gramineum subsp. loreum

Xiphion gramineum subsp.

loreum, commonly known as the Loreum bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula. It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
