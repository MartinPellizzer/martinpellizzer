Xylobium supinum

Xylobium supinum, commonly known as the groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a cluster at the top of its stem. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Xylobium supinum is known for its ability to thrive in dry conditions and is often found in disturbed soils.
