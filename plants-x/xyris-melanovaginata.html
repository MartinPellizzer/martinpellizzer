Xyris melanovaginata

Xyris melanovaginata, commonly known as the black-stemmed yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its unique botanical characteristics include dark-colored stems and small, clustered inflorescences.

Xyris melanovaginata plays an important ecological role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining wetland biodiversity.
