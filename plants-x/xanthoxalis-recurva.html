Xanthoxalis recurva

Xanthoxalis recurva, commonly known as the yellow false flax, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features a sprawling growth habit with slender, hairy stems and alternate leaves that are typically lance-shaped. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring and summer. Its root system is fibrous, and it is often found in moist, well-drained soils in open habitats.

Xanthoxalis recurva is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and flowering display.
