Xyris maxima

Xyris maxima, commonly known as the tall goldeneye, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes various grass-like plants commonly referred to as yellow-eyed grasses. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in wetlands and moist soils, often found in swampy areas and along the edges of ponds.

Xyris maxima is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
