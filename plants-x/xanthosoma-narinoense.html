Xanthosoma narinoense

Xanthosoma narinoense, commonly known as the Narino arrowleaf, is a tropical plant native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces striking white flowers with a spathe and spadix, typical of the arum family. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Xanthosoma narinoense is also valued for its edible leaves, which are used in local cuisines.
