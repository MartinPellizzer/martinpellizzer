Xylophacos lagopinus

Xylophacos lagopinus, commonly known as the silverleaf acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and slender, branching stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by flat, leathery pods containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

Xylophacos lagopinus is adapted to withstand drought and has a slow growth rate, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystems.
