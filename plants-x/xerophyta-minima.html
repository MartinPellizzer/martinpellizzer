Xerophyta minima

Xerophyta minima, commonly known as the dwarf ice plant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late summer. Xerophyta minima thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its low-growing habit and drought tolerance make it an attractive and hardy addition to dry landscapes.
