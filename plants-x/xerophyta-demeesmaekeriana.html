Xerophyta demeesmaekeriana

Xerophyta demeesmaekeriana, commonly known as the Cape fennel, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its growth habit makes it well-suited for arid environments, and it is often used in rock gardens.

The species is relatively easy to care for, requiring minimal water and well-draining soil.
