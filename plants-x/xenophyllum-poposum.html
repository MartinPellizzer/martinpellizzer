Xenophyllum poposum

Xenophyllum poposum, commonly known as the Andean orchid, is a unique species native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often colorful floral structures. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are specialized for pollination by specific insect species, showcasing an intricate ecological relationship.

Due to its limited habitat and vulnerability to environmental changes, Xenophyllum poposum is considered a rare and endangered species.
