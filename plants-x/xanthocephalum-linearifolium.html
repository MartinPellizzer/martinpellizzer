Xanthocephalum linearifolium

Xanthocephalum linearifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved goldenhead, is a perennial herb native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its linear, often hairy leaves and yellowish to golden flower heads. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is found in arid and semi-arid regions. It produces small, composite flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Xanthocephalum linearifolium is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
