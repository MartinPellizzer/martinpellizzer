Xyris pallescens

Xyris pallescens, commonly known as the pale yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes grass-like plants known as yellow-eyed grasses. The plant typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, thriving in moist, sandy soils. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in a spadix-like structure, which is characteristic of the genus Xyris.

The species is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological importance and ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
