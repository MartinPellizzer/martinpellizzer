Xyris stenostachya

Xyris stenostachya, commonly known as narrow-stalked yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various grass-like plants known for their showy yellow flowers. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow blooms in late spring to early summer. It thrives in wet to moist soils and is often found in swampy areas or along the edges of ponds.

Xyris stenostachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
