Xyris paradisiaca

Xyris paradisiaca, commonly known as the paradise yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to saturated soil conditions. Its flowering period occurs from late spring to early summer, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Xyris paradisiaca is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological importance and adaptability to aquatic environments.
