Xerophyta wentzeliana

Xerophyta wentzeliana, commonly known as the Wentzel's ice plant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in arid environments. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover in drought-prone regions.
