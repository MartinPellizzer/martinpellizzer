Xanthorrhoea reflexa

Xanthorrhoea reflexa, commonly known as the slender grass tree, is a unique plant native to southwestern Western Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical trunk and a dense crown of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. Xanthorrhoea reflexa is a hardy, fire-resistant species adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its distinctive appearance and ecological significance make it a notable component of the region's flora.
