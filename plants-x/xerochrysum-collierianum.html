Xerochrysum collierianum

Xerochrysum collierianum, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in open woodlands and grassy areas. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is well adapted to dry conditions.

Xerochrysum collierianum is also the national floral emblem of Australia.
