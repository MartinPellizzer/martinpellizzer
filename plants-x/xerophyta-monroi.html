Xerophyta monroi

Xerophyta monroi, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant is adapted to arid environments, featuring thick, fleshy leaves that store water. Its flowers bloom in late spring or early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Xerophyta monroi is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
