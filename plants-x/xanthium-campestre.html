Xanthium campestre

Xanthium campestre, commonly known as cocklebur, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, prickly leaves and spherical, burr-like fruits that aid in seed dispersal. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces both male and female flowers on the same plant.

Xanthium campestre is often considered a weed but has been used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
