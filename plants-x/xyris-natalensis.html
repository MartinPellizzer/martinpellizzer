Xyris natalensis

Xyris natalensis, commonly known as Natal yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a dense cluster at the tip of the stem. It typically grows in wetland habitats and is adapted to moist, well-drained soils.

Xyris natalensis is often found in marshy areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
