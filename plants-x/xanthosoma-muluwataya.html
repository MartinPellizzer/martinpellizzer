Xanthosoma muluwataya

Xanthosoma muluwataya, commonly known as the giant swamp taro, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can reach up to 2 meters in length. The plant produces striking inflorescences with a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. It thrives in wet, marshy environments and is often used in traditional cuisines.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and culturally valuable in its native regions.
