Xyris trachyphylla var. subglaucescens

Xyris trachyphylla var. subglaucescens, commonly known as the glaucous swamp yelloweye, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, thriving in moist, acidic soils. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early spring. Its flowers are usually surrounded by a few narrow, leaf-like bracts.

The varietal form subglaucescens is distinguished by its bluish-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance.
