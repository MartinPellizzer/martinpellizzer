Xyris ustulata

Xyris ustulata, commonly known as the flameflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the genus Xyris, which is part of the family Xyridaceae, commonly referred to as the yellow-eyed grass family. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in wet or moist soils, often found in swampy areas or along the edges of ponds.

Xyris ustulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in wetland ecosystems.
