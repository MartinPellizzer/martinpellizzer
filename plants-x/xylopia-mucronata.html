Xylopia mucronata

Xylopia mucronata, commonly known as the African bitter apple, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and simple, opposite leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by a pear-shaped fruit that turns yellow when mature. The fruit contains a single, large, bitter seed surrounded by a fleshy, edible pulp.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional medicinal uses.
