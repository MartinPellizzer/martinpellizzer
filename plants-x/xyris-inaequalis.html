Xyris inaequalis

Xyris inaequalis, commonly known as the unequal yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Xyris inaequalis typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, preferring moist to wet soil conditions.

Its botanical structure includes a rhizomatous root system and is often found in coastal and inland wetland habitats.
