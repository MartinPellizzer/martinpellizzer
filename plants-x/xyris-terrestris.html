Xyris terrestris

Xyris terrestris, commonly known as the yellow-eyed grass, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. The plant typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in swampy areas or along streambanks. Its botanical characteristics include rhizomes and a compact growth habit, making it a common sight in wetland ecosystems.

Xyris terrestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
