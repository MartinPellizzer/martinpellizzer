Xiphion aucheri

Xiphion aucheri, commonly known as the autumn crocus, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features showy, violet or purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn, often after the leaves have yellowed. The plant has narrow, strap-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. Its flowers are borne on tall, slender stalks and are often mistaken for true crocuses due to their similar appearance.

Xiphion aucheri is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
