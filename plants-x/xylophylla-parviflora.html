Xylophylla parviflora

Xylophylla parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered woodleaf, is a flowering plant native to the temperate forests of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside. Its small, white flowers are clustered in panicles and bloom in the spring.

The fruit is a small, dry capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
