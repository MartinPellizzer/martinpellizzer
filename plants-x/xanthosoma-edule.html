Xanthosoma edule

Xanthosoma edule, commonly known as the elephant ear, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces a spadix inflorescence surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible corms.

The corms are a staple food in many regions and are rich in starch.
