Xanthium numidicum

Xanthium numidicum, commonly known as goatweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf structures. The plant features rough, hairy leaves with lobed margins and produces spiny, prickly fruits that contain seeds. Xanthium numidicum is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in agricultural fields.

Its botanical features make it both a common weed and a plant of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
