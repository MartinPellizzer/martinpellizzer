Xyris

Xyris, commonly known as goldenroot, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Xyridaceae.

These plants are typically herbaceous and are found in a variety of habitats, including wetlands and sandy soils. Xyris species are characterized by their grass-like leaves and showy, yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the spring or early summer. The plant's rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Xyris plays an important ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil in its native environments.
