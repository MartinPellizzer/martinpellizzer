Xanthosoma mucronatum

Xanthosoma mucronatum, commonly known as the arrowleaf plant or swamp taro, is a herbaceous perennial belonging to the Araceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of the Americas, particularly Central and South America. The plant features large, arrow-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. Its flowers are typically clustered in a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe.

Xanthosoma mucronatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in some traditional cuisines.
