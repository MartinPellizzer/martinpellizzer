Xanthoxalis lineata

Xanthoxalis lineata, commonly known as the yellow pimpernel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, hairy stems and small, bright yellow flowers. The plant produces numerous small, oblong leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Xanthoxalis lineata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
