Xerochrysum palustre

Xerochrysum palustre, commonly known as swamp everlasting, is a perennial herb native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flower heads, which are composed of numerous small, tubular florets. The plant typically grows in wet or swampy habitats, often near water bodies such as lakes and rivers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces a strong, spicy scent.

Xerochrysum palustre is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist conditions.
