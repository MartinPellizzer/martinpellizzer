Xanthosoma pulcachense

Xanthosoma pulcachense, commonly known as the purple-leaf elephant ear, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The leaves are typically a deep green with a distinctive purple underside, giving it its common name. It produces clusters of small, white, spadix-like flowers surrounded by a colorful spathe.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in warm, humid climates.
