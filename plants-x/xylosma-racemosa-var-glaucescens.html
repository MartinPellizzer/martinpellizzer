Xylosma racemosa var. glaucescens

Xylosma racemosa var. glaucescens, commonly known as the glaucous xylosma, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish-green foliage and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are simple and opposite, with a waxy coating that gives them a distinctive appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
