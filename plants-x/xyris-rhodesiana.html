Xyris rhodesiana

Xyris rhodesiana, commonly known as the Rhodes yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, typically in clusters at the ends of its stems. Xyris rhodesiana thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for wetland ecosystems and a popular choice for native plant gardens.
