Xyris boliviana

Xyris boliviana, commonly known as the Bolivian yellowroot, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Bolivia and Peru.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various species of grass-like plants. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Xyris boliviana is often found in wetland environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
