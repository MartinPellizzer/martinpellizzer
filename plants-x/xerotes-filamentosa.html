Xerotes filamentosa

Xerotes filamentosa, commonly known as the threadleaf xerophyte, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves that reduce water loss through transpiration. The plant stores water in its fleshy stems, which are often covered in a waxy cuticle to further minimize evaporation. Xerotes filamentosa thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure, making it well adapted to desert environments.

Its unique morphology and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping in dry climates.
