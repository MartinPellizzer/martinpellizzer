Xanthophyllum lanceatum

Xanthophyllum lanceatum, commonly known as the lanceleaf goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Xanthophyllum lanceatum is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
