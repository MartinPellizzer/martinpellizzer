Xyris decipiens var. vanderystii

Xyris decipiens var. vanderystii, commonly known as the Vanderyst's goldeneye, is a striking member of the Xyridaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are grass-like and arranged in a fan shape, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various small organisms.
