Xyris rigida var. subneglecta

Xyris rigida var. subneglecta, commonly known as the narrowleaf yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in wet to mesic habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and is often found in association with other wetland flora.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
