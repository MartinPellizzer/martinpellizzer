Xerochrysum banksii

Xerochrysum banksii, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is a shrub with woody stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and Mediterranean climates.

Xerochrysum banksii is also the national floral emblem of Australia, symbolizing the country's natural heritage.
