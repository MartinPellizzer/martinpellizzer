Xyris subsetigera

Xyris subsetigera, commonly known as the narrowleaf goldeneye, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along streambanks and wetlands. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its inflorescence is a dense cluster of spikelets, which are characteristic of the grass family.

Xyris subsetigera is often found in shaded to partially sunny environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
