Xylinabaria esculenta

Xylinabaria esculenta, commonly known as the edible bamboo tree, is a unique plant species characterized by its woody structure and edible stems.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to certain regions of Southeast Asia. The plant produces long, cylindrical stems that are rich in nutrients and often consumed as a vegetable. Its growth habit resembles that of bamboo, with rapid vertical growth and a fibrous texture.

Due to its nutritional value and adaptability, it is gaining attention as a sustainable food source in tropical agriculture.
