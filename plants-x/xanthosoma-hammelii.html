Xanthosoma hammelii

Xanthosoma hammelii, commonly known as the red-veined taro, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with striking red veins. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are surrounded by a spathe, a typical feature of the arum family. Xanthosoma hammelii is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant foliage.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.
