Xanthophyllum parvum

Xanthophyllum parvum, commonly known as the small yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact size and bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has smooth, lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Xanthophyllum parvum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
