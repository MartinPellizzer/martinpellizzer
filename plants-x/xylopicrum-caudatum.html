Xylopicrum caudatum

Xylopicrum caudatum, commonly known as the tail-leaf xylopicrum, is a unique flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender leaves that taper to a point, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink, which bloom during the cooler months. Xylopicrum caudatum is adapted to survive in dry conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in xerophytic plant adaptations.
