Xyris subasperula

Xyris subasperula, commonly known as the roughleaf yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes various grass-like plants. The plant features grass-like leaves that are rough to the touch, giving it its common name. Its flowers are yellow and clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

Xyris subasperula typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
