Xyris surinamensis

Xyris surinamensis, commonly known as the Surinam yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its grass-like foliage and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Xyris surinamensis typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist habitats.

It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
