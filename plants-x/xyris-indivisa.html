Xyris indivisa

Xyris indivisa, commonly known as the narrowleaf yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes various species of grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers in early summer. It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Xyris indivisa is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in moist soils.
