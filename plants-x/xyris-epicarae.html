Xyris epicarae

Xyris epicarae, commonly known as the sandhill goldeneye, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes various grass-like plants often found in wetland habitats. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils, typically in marshes or along the edges of ponds.

Xyris epicarae is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
