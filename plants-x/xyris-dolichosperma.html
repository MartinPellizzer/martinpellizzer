Xyris dolichosperma

Xyris dolichosperma, commonly known as the long-seeded yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes various species of grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, each containing several florets.

Xyris dolichosperma is adapted to wetland habitats and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.
