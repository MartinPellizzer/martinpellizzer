Xyris bancana

Xyris bancana, commonly known as the banana marsh yelloweye, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the genus Xyris, which is part of the family Xyridaceae, commonly referred to as the yelloweye family. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Xyris bancana typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in swamps, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Its unique floral structure and adaptability to wetland environments make it an important species in its native ecosystem.
