Xanthomyrtus polyclada

Xanthomyrtus polyclada, commonly known as the variable myrtle, is a flowering plant native to southern South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known species such as eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers and glossy, dark green leaves. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

Xanthomyrtus polyclada is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic features.
