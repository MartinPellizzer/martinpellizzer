Xanthomyrtus klossii

Xanthomyrtus klossii, commonly known as the Kloss's myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Moluccas in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, round berries. Xanthomyrtus klossii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical botanical gardens.

Due to its limited natural range and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
