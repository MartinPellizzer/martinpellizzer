Xolemia albiflora

Xolemia albiflora, commonly known as the silverflower vine, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and striking white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has slender, woody stems that are covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it its common name. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a soft, velvety texture.

Xolemia albiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in hanging baskets.
