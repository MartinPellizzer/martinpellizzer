Xyris melanopoda

Xyris melanopoda, commonly known as the black-stemmed swamp yelloweye, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Xyridaceae family and typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the stems are usually dark green to black in color.

Xyris melanopoda plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
