Xyris Jupicai Var. Major

Xyris jupicai var. major is a lesser-known but striking plant native to specific regions of the southeastern united states. it belongs to the grass family and is known for its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. this variant is distinguished by its larger size compared to other varieties in the species. it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and forest edges. due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it deserves more attention in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
