Xyris rigida

Xyris rigida, commonly known as rigid yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes various species of grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Xyris rigida is adapted to moist environments and is often found in areas with poor drainage.
