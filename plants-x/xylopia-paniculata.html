Xylopia paniculata

Xylopia paniculata, commonly known as the African allspice or wild allspice, is a tropical plant belonging to the Annonaceae family.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are usually opposite and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in panicles, followed by berries that turn from green to red or yellow as they mature. Xylopia paniculata is native to tropical Africa and is often found in moist, shaded areas of forests.

Its fruit and leaves are used in traditional medicine and for their aromatic properties.
