Xyris bialata

Xyris bialata, commonly known as the Florida marsh yelloweye, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Xyridaceae family, which includes various grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. Its yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing a striking display.

Xyris bialata is characterized by its grass-like foliage and its ability to tolerate periodic flooding, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
