Xerophyta triquetra

Xerophyta triquetra, commonly known as the three-angled grassblades, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in dense spikes. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep water sources.

Xerophyta triquetra is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its hardy nature in challenging climates.
