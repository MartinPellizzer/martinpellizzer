Xylia dolabriformis

Xylia dolabriformis, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a tropical tree native to Central and Southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hard, and heavy wood, which is highly valued for its durability and fine grain. The plant has a spreading canopy with lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its flowers are small and greenish-yellow, typically appearing in clusters, and it produces woody fruits that contain several seeds.

The tree is often found in moist lowland forests and is known for its ecological and economic importance in its native region.
