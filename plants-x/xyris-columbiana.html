Xyris columbiana

Xyris columbiana, commonly known as the Columbia yellow-eyed grass, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various species of yellow-eyed grasses. The plant typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in spike-like inflorescences, giving it its common name.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
