Xyris filiformis

Xyris filiformis, commonly known as slender yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various species of yellow-eyed grasses. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Xyris filiformis typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, thriving in moist, acidic soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of wetland ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
