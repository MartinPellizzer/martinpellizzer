Xanthosia rotundifolia var. hypoleuca

Xanthosia rotundifolia var. hypoleuca, commonly known as the white-flowered ghost flower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, round leaves and showy white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in heathland and open forests, preferring well-drained soils. It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral display.

The variety hypoleuca is distinguished by its lighter floral coloration compared to other varieties of Xanthosia.
