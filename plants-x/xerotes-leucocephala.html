Xerotes leucocephala

Xerotes leucocephala, commonly known as the white-headed veldtgrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Xerotes leucocephala is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
