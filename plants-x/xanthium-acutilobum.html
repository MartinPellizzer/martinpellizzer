Xanthium acutilobum

Xanthium acutilobum, commonly known as the prickly lettuce, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and distinctive leaves with lobed margins and prickly tips. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a composite head, followed by spiny, spherical fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Xanthium acutilobum has a fibrous root system and is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative reproduction.
