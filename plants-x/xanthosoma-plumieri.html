Xanthosoma plumieri

Xanthosoma plumieri, commonly known as the arrowleaf elephant ear, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped, glossy leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces clusters of yellow or greenish-white flowers, which are typically followed by orange or red fruits containing numerous seeds. Xanthosoma plumieri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.
