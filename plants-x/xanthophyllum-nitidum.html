Xanthophyllum nitidum

Xanthophyllum nitidum, commonly known as the glossy yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling bells, hence its common name. The plant has smooth, glossy leaves that are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. Xanthophyllum nitidum thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or woodland areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
