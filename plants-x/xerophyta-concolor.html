Xerophyta concolor

Xerophyta concolor, commonly known as the smoothleaf iceplant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, bluish-green leaves that store water, a key adaptation for surviving in arid environments. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its stems are often branched and can grow up to 30 centimeters in height.

Xerophyta concolor is well-suited for xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ability to thrive in rocky or sandy soils.
