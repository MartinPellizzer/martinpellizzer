Xylosteon oblongifolium

Xylosteon oblongifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf xylosteon, is a perennial shrub native to the temperate regions of southern South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in late spring. Its woody stems are covered in a thick, fibrous bark that provides structural support and protection against environmental stress.

Xylosteon oblongifolium is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in local ecosystems.
