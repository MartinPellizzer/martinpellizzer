Xanthophyllum trichocladum

Xanthophyllum trichocladum, commonly known as the golden-haired fern, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like leaves that exhibit a yellowish hue, particularly on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Xanthophyllum trichocladum thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Its unique botanical features make it a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists alike.
