Xyris extensula

Xyris extensula, commonly known as the narrowleaf goldeneye, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various species of grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. Xyris extensula thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important plant for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
