Xylophragma corchoroides

Xylophragma corchoroides, commonly known as the African sandmat, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. Its unique feature is the presence of a fibrous, spongy layer beneath the bark, which is used in traditional medicine.

The plant is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
