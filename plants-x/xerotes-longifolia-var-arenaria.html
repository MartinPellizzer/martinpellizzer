Xerotes longifolia var. arenaria

Xerotes longifolia var. arenaria, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, often blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has adapted to survive in dry conditions with features like reduced leaf surface area and deep root systems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or gravelly habitats.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
