Xolanthes

Xolanthes, commonly known as the blue orchid, is a genus of flowering plants native to the tropical regions of South America.

These plants are characterized by their striking, often vibrant blue or violet flowers, which are typically arranged in dense inflorescences. Xolanthes species are generally herbaceous, with slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. They thrive in moist, shaded environments and are often found in cloud forests.

Due to their ornamental value, some species are cultivated in botanical gardens and as houseplants.
