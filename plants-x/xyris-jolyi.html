Xyris jolyi

Xyris jolyi, commonly known as the Joly's yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes various species of yellow-eyed grasses. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. It features grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a spike-like inflorescence.

Xyris jolyi is characterized by its ability to thrive in moist, acidic soils and is often found in areas with high water tables.
