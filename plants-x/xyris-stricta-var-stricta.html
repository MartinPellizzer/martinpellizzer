Xyris stricta var. stricta

Xyris stricta var. stricta, commonly known as narrowleaf goldeneye, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils along streambanks and wetlands. It is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its upright growth habit.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
