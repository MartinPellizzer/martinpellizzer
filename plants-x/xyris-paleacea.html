Xyris paleacea

Xyris paleacea, commonly known as the paleleaf yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various species of yellow-eyed grasses. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often subtended by bracts, a characteristic feature of the genus Xyris.

Xyris paleacea typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils.
