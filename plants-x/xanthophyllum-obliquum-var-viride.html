Xanthophyllum obliquum var. viride

Xanthophyllum obliquum var. viride, commonly known as the green-veined fern, is a variant of the Xanthophyllum obliquum species, characterized by its distinct green veins on the fronds.

It is a perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions, typically found in moist, shaded environments. The plant exhibits a fern-like structure with pinnately divided leaves that are often broad and leathery in texture. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous growth habit and spore-bearing structures called sorus located on the underside of the fronds.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
