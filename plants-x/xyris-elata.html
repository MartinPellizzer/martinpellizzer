Xyris elata

Xyris elata, commonly known as the tall yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and striking yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. Its flowers are clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Xyris elata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
