Xanthopsis

Xanthopsis, commonly known as the golden bell, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, bell-shaped blooms that hang in clusters. Xanthopsis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
