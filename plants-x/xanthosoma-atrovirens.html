Xanthosoma atrovirens

Xanthosoma atrovirens, commonly known as the black leaf arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy surface. The plant produces striking inflorescences with white spadices and yellowish-green berries. It is often cultivated for its starchy corms, which are used in various culinary applications.

Additionally, it plays a role in traditional medicine and ornamental horticulture due to its aesthetic appeal.
