Xyris iridifolia

Xyris iridifolia, commonly known as the blue-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and showy yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its small, clustered inflorescences bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Xyris iridifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
