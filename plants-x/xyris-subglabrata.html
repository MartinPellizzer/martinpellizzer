Xyris subglabrata

Xyris subglabrata, commonly known as the slender marsh candle, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Xyridaceae family, which includes various grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, thriving in moist, acidic soils. It produces yellow, candle-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Xyris subglabrata is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in swampy habitats along the Atlantic and Gulf coasts.
