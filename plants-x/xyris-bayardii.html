Xyris bayardii

Xyris bayardii, commonly known as Bayard's yellowroot, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Xyridaceae family, which includes various grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wetlands and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, acidic soil. Its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, adding a striking visual element to its habitat.

Xyris bayardii is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological importance and ability to stabilize soil.
