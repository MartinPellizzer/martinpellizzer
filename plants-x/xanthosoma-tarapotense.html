Xanthosoma tarapotense

Xanthosoma tarapotense, commonly known as the Tarapoto arrowleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence known as a spadix, which is surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant features broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are typically green with a smooth texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the undergrowth of tropical rainforests.

Xanthosoma tarapotense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by local communities.
