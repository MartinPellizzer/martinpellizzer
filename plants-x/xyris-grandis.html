Xyris grandis

Xyris grandis, commonly known as the yellow-eyed grass, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flower spikes in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to wet soils. Its flowers are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences, and it is often found in swampy habitats.

Xyris grandis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
