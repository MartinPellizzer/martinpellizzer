Xylosteon gibbiflorum var. subtomentosum

Xylosteon gibbiflorum var. subtomentosum, commonly known as the hairy-flowered xylosteon, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Australia.

It features small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. The leaves are typically narrow and covered with fine, woolly hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions with a deep root system and succulent stems.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
