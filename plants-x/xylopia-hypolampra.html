Xylopia hypolampra

Xylopia hypolampra, commonly known as the African bitter apple, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, white flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe. Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine.
