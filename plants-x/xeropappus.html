Xeropappus

Xeropappus, commonly known as desert dandelion, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

These plants are native to arid and semi-arid regions of the western United States and northern Mexico. Xeropappus species are characterized by their low-growing, mat-forming structure with hairy stems and lobed leaves. They produce yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and are followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.

The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with deep root systems and reduced water loss mechanisms.
