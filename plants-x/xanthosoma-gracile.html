Xanthosoma gracile

Xanthosoma gracile, commonly known as the slender arrowleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive leaf structures and inflorescence. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves with a smooth, waxy surface and a prominent midrib. It produces a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family.

Xanthosoma gracile is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
