Xyris straminea

Xyris straminea, commonly known as the yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, adding a vibrant touch to wetland habitats. Xyris straminea typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in swamps, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
