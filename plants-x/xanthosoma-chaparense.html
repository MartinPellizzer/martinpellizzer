Xanthosoma chaparense

Xanthosoma chaparense, commonly known as the Chapare mountain elephant ear, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Bolivia and Brazil.

It is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces striking inflorescences with a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its dramatic foliage.

Xanthosoma chaparense is also valued for its potential in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.
