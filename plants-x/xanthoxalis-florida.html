Xanthoxalis florida

Xanthoxalis florida, commonly known as the Florida goldenroot, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped, yellowish-green leaves with a distinctive reddish or purple stem. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.
