Xiphion ochroleucum

Xiphion ochroleucum, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features showy, tubular yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and wildflower meadows.

Xiphion ochroleucum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
