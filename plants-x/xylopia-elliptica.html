Xylopia elliptica

Xylopia elliptica, commonly known as the African allspice or wild allspice, is a tropical plant native to Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant has simple, elliptical leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that are often white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The botanical features of Xylopia elliptica make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
