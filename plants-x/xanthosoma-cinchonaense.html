Xanthosoma cinchonaense

Xanthosoma cinchonaense, commonly known as the arrowleaf elephant ear, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped, arrowhead-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces striking yellow or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, adding visual interest to its foliage. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Xanthosoma cinchonaense is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its dramatic appearance and ability to add a lush, tropical feel to gardens.
