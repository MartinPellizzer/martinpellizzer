Xeraea celosioides

Xeraea celosioides, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and abandoned fields.

Xeraea celosioides is also known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it a potential invasive species in certain areas.
