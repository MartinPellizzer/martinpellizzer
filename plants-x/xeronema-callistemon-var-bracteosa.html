Xeronema callistemon var. bracteosa

Xeronema callistemon var. bracteosa, commonly known as the bracteosa bottlebrush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, red or pink bottlebrush-like flowers that appear in dense clusters. The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

The variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to attract birds and insects.
