Xanthium roxburghii

Xanthium roxburghii, commonly known as greater cocklebur, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and yellow flowers that develop into rough, spherical fruits. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. Its seeds are dispersed by animals and can remain viable in the soil for several years.

Xanthium roxburghii is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
