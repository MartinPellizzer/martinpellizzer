Xyris weberi

Xyris weberi, commonly known as the Weber's golden club, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes many species of grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers in clusters during the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Xyris weberi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
