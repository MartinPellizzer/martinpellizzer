Xanthosoma ollgaardii

Xanthosoma ollgaardii, commonly known as the giant arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers surrounded by a spadix, typical of the Araceae family. Its rhizomes are edible and have a starchy texture similar to taro, making it a valuable food source in its native regions.

Xanthosoma ollgaardii thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
