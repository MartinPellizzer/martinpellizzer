Xylosma albida

Xylosma albida, commonly known as white xylosma, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. Xylosma albida is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in semi-arid environments.

It has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
