Xanthosoma atrovirens var. moritzii

Xanthosoma atrovirens var. moritzii, commonly known as the black-veined arrowleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with dark green veins that stand out against the lighter leaf surface. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its striking foliage. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically followed by edible, starchy tubers.

This variety is valued for both its aesthetic appeal and its potential agricultural use.
