Xanthomyrtus arfakensis

Xanthomyrtus arfakensis, commonly known as the Arfaken's myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. It produces small, fleshy berries that are typically orange-red in color.

Xanthomyrtus arfakensis is often found in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests.
