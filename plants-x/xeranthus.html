Xeranthus

Xeranthus, commonly known as the desert daisy, is a genus of flowering plants native to South Africa.

These plants are characterized by their succulent leaves and showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and white. Xeranthus species are adapted to arid environments, featuring thick, fleshy stems and leaves that store water. They typically thrive in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making them suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to their resilience and aesthetic appeal, they are popular in drought-tolerant gardens.
