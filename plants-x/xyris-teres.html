Xyris teres

Xyris teres, commonly known as the compact yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to saturated soils. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early to mid-summer. Its inflorescence is a spike of small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Xyris teres is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in wetland ecosystems.
