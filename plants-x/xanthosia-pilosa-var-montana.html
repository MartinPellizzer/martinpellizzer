Xanthosia pilosa var. montana

Xanthosia pilosa var. montana, commonly known as the mountain flossflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in cooler, elevated environments. Its leaves are often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique visual appeal.
