Xyris caroliniana f. flaccida

Xyris caroliniana f. flaccida, commonly known as the Carolina marsh yelloweye, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Xyridaceae family, which includes various grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wet, acidic soils in swamps, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. Its flowers are bright yellow and appear in clusters, often blooming in late spring to early summer.

The foliage is grass-like, with narrow, linear leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base.
