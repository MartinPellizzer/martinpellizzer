Xerophyta barbarae subsp. cuprophila

Xerophyta barbarae subsp.

cuprophila, commonly known as the copperleaf plant, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, a key adaptation for surviving in arid environments.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its growth habit is typically low and compact, with a branching structure that helps reduce water loss. Xerophyta barbarae subsp.

cuprophila is well-suited for xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
