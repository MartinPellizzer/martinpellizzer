Xanthosoma pedatum

Xanthosoma pedatum, commonly known as the giant taro, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, arrowhead-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, often with a waxy surface. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that are typically borne on a tall, leafless stem. Its rhizomes are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines.

Xanthosoma pedatum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
