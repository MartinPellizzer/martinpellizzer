Xylocarpus obovatus var. microphyllus

Xylocarpus obovatus var. microphyllus, commonly known as the small-leaved mangrove, is a variant of the mangrove species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, obovate leaves and is typically found in brackish water environments. The plant has a woody structure with pneumatophores, specialized root structures that aid in gas exchange. It plays a crucial role in coastal ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial species.

Its fruit is a hard, woody capsule that contains seeds dispersed by water.
