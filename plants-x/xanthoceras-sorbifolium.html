Xanthoceras sorbifolium

Xanthoceras sorbifolium, commonly known as the yellowhorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the arid regions of Central Asia and parts of China.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by edible, fleshy fruits that are rich in nutrients. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges, and its bark is smooth and often reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, dry environments.

Xanthoceras sorbifolium is also valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.
