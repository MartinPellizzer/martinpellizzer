Xylopicrum brasiliense

Xylopicrum brasiliense, commonly known as the Brazilian xylophore, is a rare flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its woody stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments with well-drained soil. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.
