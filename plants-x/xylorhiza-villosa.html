Xylorhiza villosa

Xylorhiza villosa, commonly known as the hairy wood vine, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems covered in fine, hairy foliage, which helps reduce water loss in its often arid habitat. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters along the stems. Xylorhiza villosa thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested or semi-shaded areas.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
