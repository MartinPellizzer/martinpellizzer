Xaritonia elegans

Xaritonia elegans, commonly known as the beach morning glory, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual with slender, trailing stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine, silky hairs.

Xaritonia elegans thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in dune environments.
