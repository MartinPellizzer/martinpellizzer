Xyris conocephala var. pallescens

Xyris conocephala var. pallescens, commonly known as the pale-stemmed yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to wet soil conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is distinguished by its pale green stems and is often found in association with other wetland flora.
