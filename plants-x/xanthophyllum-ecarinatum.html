Xanthophyllum ecarinatum

Xanthophyllum ecarinatum, commonly known as the golden feather plant, is a member of the Xanthophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-green foliage and delicate, feathery leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are often arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or yellow in color.

Xanthophyllum ecarinatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with appropriate humidity and light conditions.
