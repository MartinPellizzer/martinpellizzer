Xyris piraquarae

Xyris piraquarae, commonly known as the Piraquara yellow-eyed grass, is a species of flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. It typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swampy areas.

Xyris piraquarae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
