Xanthorrhoea minor

Xanthorrhoea minor, commonly known as the small grass tree, is a unique plant native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like foliage and a woody, bottle-shaped stem that stores water. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in spikes during the summer months. Xanthorrhoea minor is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.

Its distinctive appearance and ecological significance make it a notable species in Australian flora.
