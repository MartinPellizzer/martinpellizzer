Xanthosoma alpayacuense

Xanthosoma alpayacuense, commonly known as the Peruvian arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Peru and Ecuador.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces a spadix inflorescence surrounded by a colorful spathe, typical of the Araceae family. Its corms are edible and used as a starchy food source in traditional Andean cuisine.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid lowland forests.
