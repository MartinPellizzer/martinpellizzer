Xanthorrhoea arenaria

Xanthorrhoea arenaria, commonly known as the sand grass tree, is a unique and resilient plant native to Australia.

It is a member of the Xanthorrhoeaceae family and is characterized by its tall, grass-like foliage and a woody, often hollow stem. The plant is adapted to arid and sandy environments, showcasing its ability to survive in harsh conditions. Xanthorrhoea arenaria produces small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters, typically during the warmer months.

Its distinctive appearance and ecological significance make it an important component of its native habitat.
