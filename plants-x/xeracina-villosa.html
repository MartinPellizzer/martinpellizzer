Xeracina villosa

Xeracina villosa, commonly known as the hairy xeracina, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the cooler months. Xeracina villosa is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh climates.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
