Xeranthemum fulgidum

Xeranthemum fulgidum, commonly known as the golden sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Xeranthemum fulgidum is valued for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal in Mediterranean climates.
