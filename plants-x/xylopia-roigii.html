Xylopia roigii

Xylopia roigii, commonly known as the Guatemalan bitter almond, is a member of the Annonaceae family and is native to Central America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in humid, lowland tropical forests. Its fruit is a globose, fleshy berry that contains several seeds, and the plant is noted for its aromatic properties.

Xylopia roigii is also valued for its medicinal uses in traditional medicine.
