Xiphopteris subscabra

Xiphopteris subscabra, commonly known as the slender chain fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry fronds that grow in a cascading manner, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with underground stems that help it spread and establish new colonies. Its leaves are typically bipinnate, with small, linear pinnae that contribute to its delicate, lace-like appearance.

Xiphopteris subscabra thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
