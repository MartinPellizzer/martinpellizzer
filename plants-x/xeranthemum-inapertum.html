Xeranthemum inapertum

Xeranthemum inapertum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it a resilient species in arid environments.

Xeranthemum inapertum is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
