Xanthomyrtus bryophila

Xanthomyrtus bryophila, commonly known as the silverleaf myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage that exhibits a distinctive silvery-white underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, berry-like fruits. Xanthomyrtus bryophila thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or rocky coastal habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and aesthetic qualities.
