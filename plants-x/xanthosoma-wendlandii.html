Xanthosoma wendlandii

Xanthosoma wendlandii, commonly known as the giant arrowroot, is a tropical perennial plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces striking, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by a colorful spathe, which is typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its starchy corms, which are used in cooking.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal in tropical gardens.
