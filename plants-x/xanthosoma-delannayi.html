Xanthosoma delannayi

Xanthosoma delannayi, commonly known as the giant taro, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in length. The plant produces a spadix inflorescence surrounded by a spathe, typical of many aroid species. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often cultivated for its edible corms.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
