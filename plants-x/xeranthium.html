Xeranthium

Xeranthium, commonly known as the desert bell, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

These plants are native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and are well adapted to hot, dry climates. Xeranthium species typically produce showy, tubular flowers that are often brightly colored, attracting pollinators like bees and birds. The leaves are usually narrow and succulent, helping the plant conserve water.

Many species are cultivated for their ornamental value due to their striking appearance and drought tolerance.
