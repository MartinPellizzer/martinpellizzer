Xyris commixta

Xyris commixta, commonly known as the mixed yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various species of yellow-eyed grasses. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are grass-like and linear, while its flowers are small, clustered, and yellow in color.

Xyris commixta plays a role in supporting local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
