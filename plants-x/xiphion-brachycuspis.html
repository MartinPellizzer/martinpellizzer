Xiphion brachycuspis

Xiphion brachycuspis, commonly known as the narrow-cupped bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered flowers during the summer months.

Xiphion brachycuspis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
