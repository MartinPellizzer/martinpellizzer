Xyris ridleyi

Xyris ridleyi, commonly known as the ridley's yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the genus Xyris, which is part of the family Xyridaceae, commonly referred to as the yellow-eyed grass family. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, preferring moist, sandy soils.

Xyris ridleyi is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
