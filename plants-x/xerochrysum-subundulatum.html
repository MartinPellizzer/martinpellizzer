Xerochrysum subundulatum

Xerochrysum subundulatum, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, feathery flower heads that resemble small pom-poms. The plant is a member of the Asteraceae family and produces small, tubular flowers that are clustered together. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as woodlands and grasslands.

The golden wattle is also the national floral emblem of Australia, symbolizing the country's natural beauty.
