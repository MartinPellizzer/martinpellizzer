Xenophya brancifolia

Xenophya brancifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a dramatic visual display. The plant has compound leaves composed of multiple oval-shaped leaflets, and its bark is smooth and often has a reddish hue. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Xenophya brancifolia is also known for its ability to attract a variety of pollinators, including birds and insects.
