Xyris setacea

Xyris setacea, commonly known as the narrowleaf yellow-eyed grass, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes various species of grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow flowers in clusters. Xyris setacea typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
