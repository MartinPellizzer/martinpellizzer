Xanthium varians

Xanthium varians, commonly known as the variable cocklebur, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small cones. Its leaves are typically lobed and have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and often branched.

Xanthium varians is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including fields and along roadsides.
