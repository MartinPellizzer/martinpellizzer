Xanthium indicum var. inaequilaterum

Xanthium indicum var. inaequilaterum, commonly known as the common cocklebur, is a herbaceous annual plant native to temperate regions of Asia.

It features a rough, prickly stem with pairs of opposite, heart-shaped leaves that have serrated edges. The plant produces spiny, spherical fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a hardened, three-lobed husk. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in cultivated fields.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common weed with ecological and agricultural significance.
