Xyris diaphanobracteata

Xyris diaphanobracteata, commonly known as the translucent bract xyris, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Xyridaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and small, clustered flowers. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellowish to greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Xyris diaphanobracteata is often found in moist, sandy soils in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its translucent bracts are a distinctive botanical feature, contributing to its unique visual appearance.
