Xanthosoma mutataense

Xanthosoma mutataense, commonly known as the giant arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces striking inflorescences with a spadix and a colorful spathe, typical of the Araceae family. Its rhizomes are edible and are used in various culinary applications, particularly in Caribbean and South American cuisines.

Xanthosoma mutataense thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for both ornamental and practical purposes.
