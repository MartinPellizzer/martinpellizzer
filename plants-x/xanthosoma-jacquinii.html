Xanthosoma jacquinii

Xanthosoma jacquinii, commonly known as the giant arrowhead, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence composed of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features large, arrowhead-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, with a glossy, waxy texture. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Xanthosoma jacquinii is also valued for its edible corms, which are used in various traditional cuisines.
