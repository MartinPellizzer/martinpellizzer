Xylobium elatum

Xylobium elatum, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, yellow-orange, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Xylobium elatum thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or scrub habitats.

Its unique floral structure attracts pollinators such as bees and birds, contributing to its ecological significance.
