Xyris paraensis var. paraensis

Xyris paraensis var. paraensis, commonly known as the Paraensis Yellow-eyed Grass, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various species of grass-like plants. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant for botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
