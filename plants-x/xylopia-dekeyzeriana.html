Xylopia dekeyzeriana

Xylopia dekeyzeriana, commonly known as the African bitter apple, is a member of the Annonaceae family and is native to tropical Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy white flowers. The fruit is a follicle that splits open to release seeds, and the plant is known for its aromatic, often pungent scent.

It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.
