Xerotes sororia

Xerotes sororia, commonly known as the desert rose, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and showy, often pink or red, bract-like flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. Xerotes sororia is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features reflect its evolutionary adaptation to survive in extreme desert conditions.
