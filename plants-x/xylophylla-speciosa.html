Xylophylla speciosa

Xylophylla speciosa, commonly known as the radiant woodflower, is a unique plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bioluminescent leaves that emit a soft glow at night, aiding in its pollination by nocturnal insects. The plant has a complex root system that allows it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils, and its woody stem is covered in a thick, protective layer of bark. Xylophylla speciosa reproduces through a specialized form of vegetative propagation, where new shoots grow from the base of the parent plant.

Due to habitat loss, it is now classified as an endangered species, making conservation efforts critical for its survival.
