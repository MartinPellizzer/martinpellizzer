Xyris gymnoptera

Xyris gymnoptera, commonly known as the naked-petioled yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes various species of grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are typically arranged in spikes and are pollinated by insects.
