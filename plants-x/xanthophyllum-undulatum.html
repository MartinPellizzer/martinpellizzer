Xanthophyllum undulatum

Xanthophyllum undulatum, commonly known as the zigzag plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, undulating leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom during the wet season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Xanthophyllum undulatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
