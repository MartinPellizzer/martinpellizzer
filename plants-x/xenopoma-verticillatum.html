Xenopoma verticillatum

Xenopoma verticillatum, commonly known as the spiral-leaf cycad, is a unique plant characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of leaves.

It is a member of the Cycadaceae family and is native to certain regions of Southeast Asia. The plant features a stout, unbranched trunk with a crown of pinnate leaves that spiral upward in a helical pattern. Its foliage is often described as having a feathery appearance, and it produces small, cone-like structures for reproduction.

Due to its rare and ancient lineage, it is of significant interest to botanists and conservationists.
