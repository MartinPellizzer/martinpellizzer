Xyris grandiceps

Xyris grandiceps, commonly known as the tall golden-eyed grass, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes various species of grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in the summer months. It thrives in wetlands and moist soils, often found in swampy areas and along the edges of ponds.

Xyris grandiceps is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
