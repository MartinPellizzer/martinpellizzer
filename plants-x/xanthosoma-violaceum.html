Xanthosoma violaceum

Xanthosoma violaceum, commonly known as the violet arrowroot, is a tropical plant belonging to the Araceae family.

It is native to Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive violet hue on the underside, giving it its common name. Its underground stems, known as rhizomes, are used as a starchy food source.

Xanthosoma violaceum is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its striking foliage.
