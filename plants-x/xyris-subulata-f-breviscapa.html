Xyris subulata f. breviscapa

Xyris subulata f. breviscapa, commonly known as the short-stemmed marsh candle.

This plant is a flowering plant belonging to the Xyridaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and clustered growth habit. It typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes and swamps, thriving in areas with poor drainage. The species features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early to mid-summer.

Its compact growth form and unique floral structure make it an interesting subject for botanical study and ecological research.
