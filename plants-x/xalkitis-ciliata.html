Xalkitis ciliata

Xalkitis ciliata, commonly known as the silky leaf ciliate, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with finely serrated edges. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded woodland areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely distributed outside its native habitat.
