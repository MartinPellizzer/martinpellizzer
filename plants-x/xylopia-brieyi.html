Xylopia brieyi

Xylopia brieyi, commonly known as the African bitter apple, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by fleshy, yellow or orange berries. Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine.

The tree is often found in moist, shaded habitats within lowland and montane forests.
