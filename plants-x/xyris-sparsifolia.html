Xyris sparsifolia

Xyris sparsifolia, commonly known as the sparseleaf yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes various grass-like plants commonly found in wetland habitats. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in swamps, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Xyris sparsifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
