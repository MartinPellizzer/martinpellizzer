Ximenia parviflora

Ximenia parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered ximenia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features smooth, grayish bark and elliptical leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, often used in traditional medicine.

Ximenia parviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
