Xanthium americanum

Xanthium americanum, commonly known as common cocklebur, is an annual flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces spiny, spherical fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, adherent substance. Its leaves are alternate, rough, and often have a waxy coating.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in agricultural fields.
