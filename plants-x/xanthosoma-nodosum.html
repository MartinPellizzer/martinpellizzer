Xanthosoma nodosum

Xanthosoma nodosum, commonly known as the spotted colocasia, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with distinctive white or yellow spots. The plant produces an inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Xanthosoma nodosum is also used in some traditional cuisines for its edible leaves.
