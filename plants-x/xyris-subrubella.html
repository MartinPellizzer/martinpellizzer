Xyris subrubella

Xyris subrubella, commonly known as the redstem yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the genus Xyris in the family Xyridaceae, which includes various species of grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its flowers are bright yellow and clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences, making it visually striking.

Xyris subrubella is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
