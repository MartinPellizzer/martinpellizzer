Xyris drummondii

Xyris drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's goldenroot, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, preferring moist to saturated soil conditions. Its leaves are grass-like and often have a bluish-green hue, while its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in spike-like inflorescences.

Drummond's goldenroot is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
