Xanthosoma mariquitense

Xanthosoma mariquitense, commonly known as the Mariquitense arrowleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. The plant features large, arrow-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a glossy texture. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Xanthosoma mariquitense is also valued for its edible leaves, which are used in traditional cuisines.
