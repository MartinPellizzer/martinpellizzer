Xyris foliolata

Xyris foliolata, commonly known as the narrowleaf yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Xyridaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Xyris foliolata produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, making it a notable species in wetland ecosystems.
