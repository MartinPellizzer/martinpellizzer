Xyris jupicai var. major

Xyris jupicai var. major, commonly known as the large yellow iris, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes grass-like plants commonly referred to as yellow-eyed grasses. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for wetland ecosystems and a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects.
