Xiphidium pontederiiflorum

Xiphidium pontederiiflorum, commonly known as the purple flag iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of the southeastern United States.

It features striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has narrow, sword-like leaves that grow from a rhizomatous stem, allowing it to spread vegetatively. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common sight in marshes and pond edges.

Its unique floral structure and coloration make it a notable species in wetland ecosystems.
