Xyris louisianica

Xyris louisianica, commonly known as Louisiana goldenroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of swamps, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its rhizomatous root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion in its wetland habitat.

Xyris louisianica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
