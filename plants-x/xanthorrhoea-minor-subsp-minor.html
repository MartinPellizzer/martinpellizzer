Xanthorrhoea minor subsp. minor

Xanthorrhoea minor subsp.

minor, commonly known as the small grass tree, is an evergreen shrub or small tree native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its tall, narrow, grass-like leaves and a single, woody stem that often remains unbranched.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in a spike-like inflorescence, typically during the warmer months. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
