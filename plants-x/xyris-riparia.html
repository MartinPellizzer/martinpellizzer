Xyris riparia

Xyris riparia, commonly known as the river marsh yelloweye, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of rivers and streams. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in the spring. Xyris riparia is a member of the Xyridaceae family, which includes other marsh and wetland plants.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to moist, nutrient-rich soils.
