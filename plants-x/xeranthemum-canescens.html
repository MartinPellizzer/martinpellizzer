Xeranthemum canescens

Xeranthemum canescens, commonly known as the white rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. The plant produces small, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are gray-green and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Xeranthemum canescens is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
