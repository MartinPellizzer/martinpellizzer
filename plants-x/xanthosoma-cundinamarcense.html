Xanthosoma cundinamarcense

Xanthosoma cundinamarcense, commonly known as the Cundinamarca arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces a spadix inflorescence surrounded by a colorful spathe, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid lowland forests.

The edible underground stem, or rhizome, is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
