Xylia xylocarpa var. xylocarpa

Xylia xylocarpa var. xylocarpa, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a broad, spreading crown. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a smooth surface and a slightly serrated margin. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in panicles, while the fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

The wood of this species is highly valued for its durability and fine grain, making it important in local crafts and traditional uses.
