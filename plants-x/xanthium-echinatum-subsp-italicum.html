Xanthium echinatum subsp. italicum

Xanthium echinatum subsp.

italicum, commonly known as Italian cocklebur, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its rough, spiny leaves and large, spherical fruits that contain numerous seeds.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas. Its botanical features include yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, and it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.

The subspecies italicum is distinguished by its slightly different leaf morphology compared to other subspecies of Xanthium echinatum.
