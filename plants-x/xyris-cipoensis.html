Xyris cipoensis

Xyris cipoensis, commonly known as the Cipo yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various species of yellow-eyed grasses. The plant is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom during the wet season. Xyris cipoensis typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often near streams or in swampy areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora, contributing to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.
