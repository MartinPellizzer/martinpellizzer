Xanthophyllum crassum

Xanthophyllum crassum, commonly known as the broad-leafed goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, often hairy foliage. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its yellow-orange flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Xanthophyllum crassum is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forest understories or along stream banks.
