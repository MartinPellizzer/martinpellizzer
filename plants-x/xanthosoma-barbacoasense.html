Xanthosoma barbacoasense

Xanthosoma barbacoasense, commonly known as the Guiana arrowroot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrowhead-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces striking inflorescences with a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe, which is typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often cultivated for its starchy corms, which are used in various culinary applications.

Xanthosoma barbacoasense is also valued for its ornamental appeal in tropical gardens.
