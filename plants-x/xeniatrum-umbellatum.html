Xeniatrum umbellatum

Xeniatrum umbellatum, commonly known as the umbrella fern, is a herbaceous plant characterized by its distinctive umbrella-like inflorescence.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate regions. The plant features elongated, arching leaves that form a dense canopy, supporting its unique floral structure. Its flowers are small and clustered, often white or pale in color, and are borne on a central stem.

Xeniatrum umbellatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
