Xiphopteris blepharidea

Xiphopteris blepharidea, commonly known as the fringed huperzia, is a species of clubmoss native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage with narrow, linear leaves that have a fringed appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Its sporophytes are small and inconspicuous, with a single, elongated sporangium at the tip of each leaf.

Xiphopteris blepharidea plays a role in maintaining soil stability and providing microhabitats for various invertebrates.
