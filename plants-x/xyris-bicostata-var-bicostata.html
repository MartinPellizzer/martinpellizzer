Xyris bicostata var. bicostata

Xyris bicostata var. bicostata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the summer months. Its botanical structure includes a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and colonize new areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
