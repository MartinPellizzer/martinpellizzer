Xyris neocaledonica

Xyris neocaledonica, commonly known as the New Caledonian marsh yellowflag, is a flowering plant native to the island of New Caledonia.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in wet, acidic soils and is often found in swampy or marshy areas. It has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow blooms during the spring and summer months.

Xyris neocaledonica is an important component of the local flora and is adapted to the unique environmental conditions of its native habitat.
