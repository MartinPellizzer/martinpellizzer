Xiphopteris pseudospiralis

Xiphopteris pseudospiralis, commonly known as the spiral fern, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its intricate, spiral arrangement of fronds, which give it a distinctive and ornamental appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its fronds are bipinnate, with a delicate, feathery texture that contributes to its aesthetic appeal.

Xiphopteris pseudospiralis is valued in horticulture for its unique foliage and ability to thrive in humid, partially shaded conditions.
