Xylosma suaveolens subsp. haroldii

Xylosma suaveolens subsp.

haroldii, commonly known as the Harold's xylosma, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its small, simple leaves that are often arranged alternately along the stems.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release minute seeds.

This species is notable for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
