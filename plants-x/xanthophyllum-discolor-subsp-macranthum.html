Xanthophyllum discolor subsp. macranthum

Xanthophyllum discolor subsp.

macranthum, commonly known as the variable yellow bell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an attractive species for both ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
