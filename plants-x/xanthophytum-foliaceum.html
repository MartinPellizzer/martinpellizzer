Xanthophytum foliaceum

Xanthophytum foliaceum, commonly known as the leafy xanthophytum, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep underground water sources.

Xanthophytum foliaceum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
