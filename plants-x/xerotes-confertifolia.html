Xerotes confertifolia

Xerotes confertifolia, commonly known as the dense-leaved xerophyte, is a succulent plant native to arid regions.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting to its dry environment. The plant produces small, white flowers during the rainy season, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its root system is shallow but extensive, allowing it to absorb moisture quickly from the soil.

Xerotes confertifolia is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
