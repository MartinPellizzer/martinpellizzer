Xanthosoma ceronii

Xanthosoma ceronii, commonly known as the giant taro, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in length. The plant produces yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its rhizomes are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines.

Xanthosoma ceronii thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
