Xanthosoma palaciosii

Xanthosoma palaciosii, commonly known as the Palacios' taro, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces striking inflorescences with a yellow spathe and a spadix, which are typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Xanthosoma palaciosii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its edible corms in some regions.
