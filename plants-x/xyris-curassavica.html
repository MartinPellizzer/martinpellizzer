Xyris curassavica

Xyris curassavica, commonly known as the yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the genus Xyris in the family Xyridaceae, which includes grass-like plants known for their showy yellow flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its inflorescence consists of clustered, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Xyris curassavica is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological importance and adaptability to aquatic environments.
