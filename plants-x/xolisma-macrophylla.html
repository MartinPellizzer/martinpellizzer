Xolisma macrophylla

Xolisma macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved desert plant, is a shrub native to the arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep groundwater sources.

Xolisma macrophylla plays an important role in stabilizing desert soils and providing habitat for local wildlife.
