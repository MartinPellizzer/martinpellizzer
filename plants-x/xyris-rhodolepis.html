Xyris rhodolepis

Xyris rhodolepis, commonly known as the scarlet marsh yelloweye, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Xyridaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as yelloweyes. The plant typically grows in wet, acidic soils in marshes and swamps, often forming dense colonies. Its striking yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring, are borne on slender, leafless stems.

Xyris rhodolepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in wetland ecosystems.
