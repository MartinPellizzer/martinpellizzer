Xyris vestita

Xyris vestita, commonly known as the naked yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes various species of grass-like plants. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer. Xyris vestita typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks, thriving in moist, acidic soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
