Xerophyta andringitrensis

Xerophyta andringitrensis, commonly known as the Andringa grass, is a species native to the arid regions of Madagascar.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant is adapted to withstand extreme drought conditions, showcasing typical xerophytic features such as reduced leaf surface area and thick, waxy cuticles. It plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in its native habitat.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is of interest to botanists studying plant resilience in harsh environments.
