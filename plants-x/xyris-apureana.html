Xyris apureana

Xyris apureana, commonly known as the Apure yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to South America, specifically Venezuela.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various species of yellow-eyed grasses. The plant is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks.

Xyris apureana is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive, long-lasting blooms.
