Xyris laxifolia var. laxifolia

Xyris laxifolia var. laxifolia, commonly known as the slender yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, typically in clusters at the base of the stem. It thrives in wet to mesic habitats, such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks.

Xyris laxifolia var. laxifolia is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
