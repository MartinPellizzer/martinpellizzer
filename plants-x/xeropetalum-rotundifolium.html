Xeropetalum rotundifolium

Xeropetalum rotundifolium, commonly known as the round-leafed sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and features small, fleshy, rounded leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of delicate, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires minimal water once established.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal, it is often cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
