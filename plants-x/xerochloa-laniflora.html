Xerochloa laniflora

Xerochloa laniflora, commonly known as the silky grass or woolly grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and fine, silky foliage that gives it a soft, fluffy appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the grass family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.

Xerochloa laniflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its unique texture and drought tolerance.
