Xanthosoma mafaffa var. blandum

Xanthosoma mafaffa var. blandum, commonly known as the African arrowroot or swamp taro, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces an inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family. Its rhizomes are edible and are often used as a starchy root vegetable in various cuisines.

The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is commonly found in wetlands and along riverbanks.
