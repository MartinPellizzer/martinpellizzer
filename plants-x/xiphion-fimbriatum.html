Xiphion fimbriatum

Xiphion fimbriatum, commonly known as the fringed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, with distinctive fringed edges on the petals. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Xiphion fimbriatum is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine.
