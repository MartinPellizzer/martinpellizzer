Xerotes flexifolia

Xerotes flexifolia, commonly known as the flexible leaf xerophyte, is a succulent plant native to arid regions.

It is characterized by its fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers during the rainy season. Its root system is shallow but extensive, allowing it to absorb moisture from the soil quickly.

Xerotes flexifolia is well adapted to hot, low-moisture climates and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
