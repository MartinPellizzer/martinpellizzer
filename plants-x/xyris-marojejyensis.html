Xyris marojejyensis

Xyris marojejyensis, commonly known as the Marojejy yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Xyris marojejyensis is notable for its unique floral structure and is considered a rare and endemic species.
