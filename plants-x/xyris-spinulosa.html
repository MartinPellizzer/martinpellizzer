Xyris spinulosa

Xyris spinulosa, commonly known as the prickly yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, swamps, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist, acidic soils. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its inflorescence is a dense cluster of small, clustered flowers, which are often surrounded by bracts.

Xyris spinulosa is a hardy perennial that plays a role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and wildlife.
