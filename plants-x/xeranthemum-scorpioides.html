Xeranthemum scorpioides

Xeranthemum scorpioides, commonly known as the scorpion weed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments, showcasing its drought-tolerant nature. Its small, yellowish flowers bloom in summer, and the plant often forms dense mats or low shrubs.

Xeranthemum scorpioides is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
