Xanthophyllum glabrescens

Xanthophyllum glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are erect and somewhat woody.

Xanthophyllum glabrescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ease of care.
