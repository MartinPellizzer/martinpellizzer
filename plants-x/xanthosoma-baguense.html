Xanthosoma baguense

Xanthosoma baguense, commonly known as the Guiana arrowleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence composed of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features large, arrow-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a waxy texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in swampy or riparian habitats.

Xanthosoma baguense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some indigenous communities.
