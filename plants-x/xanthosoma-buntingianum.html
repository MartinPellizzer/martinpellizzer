Xanthosoma buntingianum

Xanthosoma buntingianum, commonly known as the Buntingian arrowleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces striking, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by a colorful spathe, which is typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage.

Xanthosoma buntingianum is also valued for its edible shoots and leaves in some traditional cuisines.
