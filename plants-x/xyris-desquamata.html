Xyris desquamata

Xyris desquamata, commonly known as the slough grass or yellow-eyed grass, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes various species of grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, and features slender, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in spike-like inflorescences, often appearing in late spring to early summer.

Xyris desquamata is characterized by its ability to thrive in moist, acidic soils and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
