Xanthium riparium

Xanthium riparium, commonly known as the common cocklebur, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in disturbed soils and along roadsides. The plant produces spiny, spherical fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, hairy coating. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a rough, hairy texture.

Xanthium riparium is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common weed in agricultural and urban areas.
