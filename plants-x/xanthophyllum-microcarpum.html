Xanthophyllum microcarpum

Xanthophyllum microcarpum, commonly known as the small-fruited golden-leaf plant, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its yellowish-green foliage and small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant produces tiny, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Xanthophyllum microcarpum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
