Xyris teretifolia

Xyris teretifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes various species of grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in wet to moist soils, often found in swampy areas or along the edges of ponds.

Xyris teretifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in wetland ecosystems.
