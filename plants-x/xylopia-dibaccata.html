Xylopia dibaccata

Xylopia dibaccata, commonly known as the African bitter apple, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, star-shaped fruits.

Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine and pharmacological research.
