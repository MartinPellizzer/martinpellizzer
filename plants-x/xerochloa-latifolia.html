Xerochloa latifolia

Xerochloa latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its wide, flat leaves and tufted inflorescence, which consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance through its fibrous root system and reduced leaf surface area. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

Xerochloa latifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping in regions with suitable climates.
