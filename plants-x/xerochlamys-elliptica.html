Xerochlamys elliptica

Xerochlamys elliptica, commonly known as the narrow-leaf rock heath, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its elliptical, narrow leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Xerochlamys elliptica is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient plant in harsh climates.
