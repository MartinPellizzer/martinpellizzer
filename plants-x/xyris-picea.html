Xyris picea

Xyris picea, commonly known as the blackstem yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the genus Xyris in the family Xyridaceae, which includes various species of yellow-eyed grasses. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its leaves are grass-like and narrow, while its flowers are small, clustered yellow blooms that appear in late spring to early summer.

Xyris picea plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
