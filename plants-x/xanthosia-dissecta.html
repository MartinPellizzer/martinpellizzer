Xanthosia dissecta

Xanthosia dissecta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf everlasting or narrow-leaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow disc florets surrounded by white ray florets. The plant has linear, often hairy leaves that are deeply dissected, giving it a feathery appearance. It is typically found in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

Xanthosia dissecta is often used in ornamental gardening due to its delicate and long-lasting blooms.
