Xyris panacea

Xyris panacea, commonly known as the panacea yelloweye, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes grass-like plants commonly found in wetland environments. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in swampy areas or along the edges of ponds.

Xyris panacea is noted for its ornamental value and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland habitats.
