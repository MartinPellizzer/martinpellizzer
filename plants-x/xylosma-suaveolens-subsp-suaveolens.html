Xylosma suaveolens subsp. suaveolens

Xylosma suaveolens subsp.

suaveolens, commonly known as sweet xylosma, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are often fragrant. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that releases seeds when mature.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to a variety of environmental conditions.
