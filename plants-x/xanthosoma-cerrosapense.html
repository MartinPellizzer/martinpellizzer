Xanthosoma cerrosapense

Xanthosoma cerrosapense, commonly known as the mountain arrowleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and inflorescence structure. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves with a prominent midrib and a waxy surface. It produces a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family.

Xanthosoma cerrosapense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
