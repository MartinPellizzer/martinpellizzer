Xiphopteris mitchelliae

Xiphopteris mitchelliae, commonly known as the Mitchell's sword fern, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically 30 to 60 centimeters long. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Xiphopteris mitchelliae is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in maintaining ecological balance in its native habitats.
