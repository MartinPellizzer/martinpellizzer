Xiphion kolpakowskianum

Xiphion kolpakowskianum, commonly known as the Kolpakovsky's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal environments.

Xiphion kolpakowskianum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
