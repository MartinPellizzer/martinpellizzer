Xyris ciliata

Xyris ciliata, commonly known as the slender yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Xyris ciliata typically grows in wet, acidic soils along stream banks and in marshy areas.

It is a perennial herb that plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
