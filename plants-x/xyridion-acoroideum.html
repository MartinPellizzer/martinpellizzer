Xyridion acoroideum

Xyridion acoroideum, commonly known as the dwarf yellow iris, is a member of the Xyridaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes and swampy areas. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It is native to parts of Central and South America, where it thrives in moist, shaded conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in wetland gardens.
