Xyris savanensis var. glabrata

Xyris savanensis var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth swamp yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its varietal name "glabrata" refers to its smooth, hairless stem and leaves.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
