Xerophyta cauliflora

Xerophyta cauliflora, commonly known as the Cape rose, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces showy, pink or red flowers that bloom from the base of the stem, a feature known as cauliflorous flowering. Its root system is shallow but extensive, enabling it to absorb moisture from the soil efficiently.

Xerophyta cauliflora is well adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
