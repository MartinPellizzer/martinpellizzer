Xanthosoma tuberquiae

Xanthosoma tuberquiae, commonly known as the giant swamp taro, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, which are typically borne on upright stems. Its tuberous roots are edible and have been traditionally used in various cuisines.

Xanthosoma tuberquiae thrives in wet, muddy environments and is often found in marshy areas or along riverbanks.
