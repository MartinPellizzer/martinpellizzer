Xylosma intermedia

Xylosma intermedia, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a shrub native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branching structure and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces clusters of bright purple berries that are highly attractive to birds. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a slightly waxy texture.

Xylosma intermedia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
