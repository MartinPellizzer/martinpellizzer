Xyris pumila

Xyris pumila, commonly known as the dwarf yellow-eyed grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the genus Xyris, which is part of the grass family, Poaceae. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early spring. Xyris pumila typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
