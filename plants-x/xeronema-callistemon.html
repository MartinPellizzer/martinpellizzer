Xeronema callistemon

Xeronema callistemon, commonly known as the bottlebrush tree, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink bottlebrush-like flowers that appear in clusters at the ends of its branches. The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves that are evergreen, providing year-round foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Xeronema callistemon is also valued for its ability to attract birds and pollinators due to its nectar-rich flowers.
