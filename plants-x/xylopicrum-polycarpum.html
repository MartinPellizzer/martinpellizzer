Xylopicrum polycarpum

Xylopicrum polycarpum, commonly known as the multi-fruited woodplant, is a unique species native to the temperate rainforests of the Pacific Northwest.

It is characterized by its woody stem and dense, layered foliage that provides habitat for various small organisms. The plant produces multiple fruiting bodies throughout the growing season, each containing several seeds enclosed in a fleshy, aromatic pulp. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a waxy coating that helps retain moisture in humid environments.

Xylopicrum polycarpum plays a vital role in its ecosystem by supporting biodiversity and contributing to soil stability through its extensive root system.
