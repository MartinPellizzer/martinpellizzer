Xiphion tenuifolium

Xiphion tenuifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lily, is a perennial plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and tall, slender stems that bear showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or red. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing fragrant, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Xiphion tenuifolium thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value and adaptability have made it a popular plant among gardeners interested in native and drought-tolerant species.
