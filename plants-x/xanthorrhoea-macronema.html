Xanthorrhoea macronema

Xanthorrhoea macronema, commonly known as the tall grass tree, is a unique and ancient plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow trunk and a dense crown of long, grass-like leaves. The plant produces a distinctive yellowish sap, which is stored in its stem and used by Aboriginal communities for various purposes. Xanthorrhoea macronema is a member of the Xanthorrhoeaceae family and is known for its ability to survive in harsh, arid environments.

Its ecological significance makes it an important species in Australian flora.
