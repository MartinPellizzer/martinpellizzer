Xanthosoma utile

Xanthosoma utile, commonly known as the elephant ear, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped, arrowhead-shaped, or sagittate leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces a spadix inflorescence surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe, which is often brightly colored. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible leaves.

The plant is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its use in traditional cuisines.
