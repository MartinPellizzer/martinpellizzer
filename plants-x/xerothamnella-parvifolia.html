Xerothamnella parvifolia

Xerothamnella parvifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved desert shrub, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale yellow to white flowers, which bloom during the rainy season. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Xerothamnella parvifolia is often found in rocky soils and is well-suited to survival in harsh, low-nutrient conditions.
