Xylopia venezuelana

Xylopia venezuelana, commonly known as the Venezuelan xylopia, is a member of the Annonaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, characterized by its smooth, dark bark and opposite, simple leaves. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by oval, woody fruits containing a single seed.

Xylopia venezuelana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
