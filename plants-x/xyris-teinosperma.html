Xyris teinosperma

Xyris teinosperma, commonly known as the narrowleaf yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes various grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a spike-like inflorescence. It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.

Xyris teinosperma is adapted to moist soil conditions and plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
