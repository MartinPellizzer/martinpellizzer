Xiphotheca rosmarinifolia

Xiphotheca rosmarinifolia, commonly known as the rosemary bush, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It features aromatic, needle-like leaves that are dark green and closely resemble those of rosemary. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal gardens.

Its essential oils are often used in aromatherapy and for their medicinal properties.
