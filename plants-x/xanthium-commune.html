Xanthium commune

Xanthium commune, commonly known as cocklebur, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is an annual or biennial herb that grows up to 1.5 meters tall, featuring hairy stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces spiny, spherical fruits that contain seeds with a hard, burs-like coating. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it is often found in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields.

Xanthium commune is known for its allergenic potential and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
