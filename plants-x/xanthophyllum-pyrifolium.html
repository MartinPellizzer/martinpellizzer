Xanthophyllum pyrifolium

Xanthophyllum pyrifolium, commonly known as the fireleaf tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a reddish tint on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by orange-red fruits, which contain several seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine and are also valued for their ornamental appeal.
