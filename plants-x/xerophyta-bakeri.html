Xerophyta bakeri

Xerophyta bakeri, commonly known as the Baker's ice plant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in arid environments. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Xerophyta bakeri is a hardy, low-maintenance plant that is well adapted to drought conditions.
