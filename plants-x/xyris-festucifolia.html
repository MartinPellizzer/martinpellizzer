Xyris festucifolia

Xyris festucifolia, commonly known as grassleaf yellow-eyed Mary, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes various species of yellow-eyed Mary plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Xyris festucifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
