Xanthosoma acutilobum

Xanthosoma acutilobum, commonly known as the arrowleaf elephant ear, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, with a glossy green surface and a prominent central vein. The plant produces striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the summer months. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Xanthosoma acutilobum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its dramatic foliage and is also used in some traditional cuisines.
