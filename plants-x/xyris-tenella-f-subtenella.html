Xyris tenella f. subtenella

Xyris tenella f. subtenella, commonly known as the narrowleaf yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes various species of grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and along the edges of ponds.

The subspecies subtenella is distinguished by its narrower leaves compared to the typical form of Xyris tenella.
