Xyris uninervis

Xyris uninervis, commonly known as the narrow-veined yellow-eyed grass, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the genus Xyris in the family Xyridaceae, which includes various species of yellow-eyed grasses. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Xyris uninervis typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.

Its botanical structure is adapted to wetland environments, making it an important component of these ecosystems.
