Xylocarpus guineensis

Xylocarpus guineensis, commonly known as African yellowwood, is a tropical tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for construction and furniture. The plant has compound leaves with opposite arrangement and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

The tree is often found in coastal and riverine forests, contributing to local biodiversity and ecological stability.
