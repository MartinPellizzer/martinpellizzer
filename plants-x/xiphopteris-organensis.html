Xiphopteris organensis

Xiphopteris organensis, commonly known as the Organ Mountain spikerush, is a rare fern-like plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, spore-bearing spikes that rise from a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is typically found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its reproductive structures are adapted for wind pollination, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
