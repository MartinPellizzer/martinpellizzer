Xerophyta barbarae

Xerophyta barbarae, commonly known as the Barbadine or Barbadine grass, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, adapting to harsh, dry environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that reduce water loss through transpiration. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, inconspicuous flowers.

Xerophyta barbarae is well-suited for xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
