Xylopia cacanes

Xylopia cacanes, commonly known as the Caribbean pepper or wild pepper, is a member of the Anacardiaceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Central and South America. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by dark reddish-brown berries. Its wood is dense and hard, making it valuable for construction and crafting.

The plant also has medicinal uses in traditional medicine, particularly for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
