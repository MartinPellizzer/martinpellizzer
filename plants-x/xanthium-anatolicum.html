Xanthium anatolicum

Xanthium anatolicum, commonly known as Anatolian cocklebur, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant has rough, spiny leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into burrs with hooked seeds. Xanthium anatolicum is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in dry, open habitats.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural and roadside environments.
