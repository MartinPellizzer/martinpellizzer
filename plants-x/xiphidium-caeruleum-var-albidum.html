Xiphidium caeruleum var. albidum

Xiphidium caeruleum var. albidum, commonly known as the white-flowered bluebell, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range from pale blue to white, often with a contrasting purple or violet stripe. The plant typically grows in woodland habitats, preferring partial shade and well-drained soil. Its leaves are long and strap-like, often forming a basal rosette.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
