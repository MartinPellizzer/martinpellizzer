Xanthophyllum bracteatum

Xanthophyllum bracteatum, commonly known as the golden bract plant, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while the bracts are often brightly colored.

It is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant floral display.
