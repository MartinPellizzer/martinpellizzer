Xochiquetzallia hannibalii

Xochiquetzallia hannibalii, commonly known as the Hannibal's flame orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the high-altitude cloud forests of Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red and orange petals, which are adorned with intricate patterns that resemble flames. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or rocky surfaces, showcasing its epiphytic nature. Its flowering period occurs during the late spring to early summer, making it a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
