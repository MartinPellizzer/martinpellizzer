Xanthium × catalaunicum

Xanthium × catalaunicum, commonly known as the common cocklebur, is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical fruit capsules that contain numerous seeds. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces yellow flowers in the summer. It is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is considered an invasive species in some regions.

Its botanical features make it easily identifiable and ecologically significant in its native habitats.
