Xenia vulcanensis

Xenia vulcanensis, commonly known as the fireweed of the volcanic plains, is a unique flowering plant adapted to thrive in extreme environments.

It possesses thick, waxy leaves that help retain moisture in arid, volcanic soils. The plant produces vibrant red and orange flowers that bloom during the cooler months, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its root system is deep and resilient, allowing it to access water from underground sources.

Xenia vulcanensis plays a crucial role in stabilizing volcanic soil and preventing erosion in its native habitat.
