Xenophyllum lorochaqui

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its intricate, lace-like fronds that give it a ghostly appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil to flourish. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of its fronds and release spores for propagation.

Due to its limited habitat and threats from deforestation, Xenophyllum lorochaqui is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
