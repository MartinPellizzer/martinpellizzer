Xanthophyllum subcoriaceum

Xanthophyllum subcoriaceum, commonly known as the roughleaf yew, is a shrub or small tree native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in catkins, followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to the harsh conditions of mountainous regions.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
