Xanthosoma caucavallense

Xanthosoma caucavallense, commonly known as the caucavallense arrowleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces striking white or pale yellow spathe flowers, which are typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid lowland forests.

Xanthosoma caucavallense is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
