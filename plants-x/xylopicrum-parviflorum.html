Xylopicrum parviflorum

Xylopicrum parviflorum, commonly known as the dwarf flowerwood, is a small shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features dense, evergreen foliage with small, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink, typically blooming in late spring. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in dry conditions.

Xylopicrum parviflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
