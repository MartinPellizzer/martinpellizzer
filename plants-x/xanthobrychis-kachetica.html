Xanthobrychis kachetica

Xanthobrychis kachetica, commonly known as the Kachetica hairgrass, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers. Native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus, it thrives in rocky and well-drained soils. The plant is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it contributes to soil stabilization.

Its ecological significance lies in its role as a habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
