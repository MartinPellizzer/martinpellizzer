Xyris welwitschii

Xyris welwitschii, commonly known as the Welwitsch's yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family and is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers in early to mid-summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or rocky areas.

Xyris welwitschii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
