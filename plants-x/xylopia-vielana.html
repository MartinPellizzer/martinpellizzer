Xylopia vielana

Xylopia vielana, commonly known as the African bitter almond, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and West Africa.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, pungent fruits that resemble small almonds. The plant has compound leaves with a rough texture and produces small, white flowers that are often clustered together. Its bark and fruit contain various bioactive compounds, making it valuable in traditional medicine.

The tree is also noted for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
