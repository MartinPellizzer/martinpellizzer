Xanthophytum attopevense

Xanthophytum attopevense, commonly known as the Attopevense yellow plant, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellowish flowers and succulent leaves. The plant thrives in arid and rocky environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh coastal conditions. It produces small, daisy-like blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its native range.
