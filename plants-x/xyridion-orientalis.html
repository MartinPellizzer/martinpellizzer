Xyridion orientalis

Xyridion orientalis, commonly known as the oriental xyridion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, commonly referred to as the yellow-eyed grass family. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Xyridion orientalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
