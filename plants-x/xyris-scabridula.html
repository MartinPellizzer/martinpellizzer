Xyris scabridula

Xyris scabridula, commonly known as the roughleaf goldencrop, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes various species of grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in fine, rough hairs, giving it its common name. Its flowering stems produce clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Xyris scabridula prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds and streams.
