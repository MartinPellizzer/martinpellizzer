Xanthophytum magnisepalum

Xanthophytum magnisepalum, commonly known as the giant sepaled sunplant, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in dense, spherical clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats, including grasslands and rocky areas, where it thrives in full sunlight. Its leaves are broad and often covered in a fine, white waxy coating that helps reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Xanthophytum magnisepalum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
