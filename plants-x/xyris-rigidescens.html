Xyris rigidescens

Xyris rigidescens, commonly known as rigid yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various grass-like plants. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in wetlands and moist soils, thriving in swampy or marshy habitats.

Xyris rigidescens is often found in association with other wetland plants, contributing to the ecological diversity of its environment.
