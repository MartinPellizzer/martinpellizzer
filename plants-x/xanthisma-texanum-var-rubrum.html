Xanthisma texanum var. rubrum

Xanthisma texanum var. rubrum, commonly known as the red sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, pink to red flowers during the summer months. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It is often found in sandy or rocky soils along the edges of deserts and in dry grasslands.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
