Xyris gardneri

Xyris gardneri, commonly known as Gardner's goldeneye, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes various grass-like plants. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and wetlands.

Gardner's goldeneye is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
