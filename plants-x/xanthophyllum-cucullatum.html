Xanthophyllum cucullatum

Xanthophyllum cucullatum, commonly known as the golden bell plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy texture.

Xanthophyllum cucullatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
