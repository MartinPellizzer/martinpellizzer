Xanthophyllum eurhynchum

Xanthophyllum eurhynchum, commonly known as the golden-horned fern, is a tropical plant belonging to the Polypodiaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, clustered fronds that are typically bright yellow to orange in color, giving it its common name. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with creeping stems that produce new shoots along their length. Its leaves are usually leathery and have a distinct, serrated margin.

Xanthophyllum eurhynchum thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.
