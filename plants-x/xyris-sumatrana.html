Xyris sumatrana

Xyris sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes grass-like plants often found in wetland environments. The plant is characterized by its compact, herbaceous growth habit and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically arranged in a rosette pattern.

Xyris sumatrana thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in swampy or marshy areas.
