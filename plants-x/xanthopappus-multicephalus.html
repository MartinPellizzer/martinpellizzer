Xanthopappus multicephalus

Xanthopappus multicephalus, commonly known as the multibranched yellow pappus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and wind-dispersed seeds. The plant features a low-growing, mat-forming structure with narrow, gray-green leaves. Its flowers are yellow and produce small, parachute-like seeds that are adapted for wind dispersal.

Xanthopappus multicephalus thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open plains.
