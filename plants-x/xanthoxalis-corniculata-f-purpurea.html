Xanthoxalis corniculata f. purpurea

Xanthoxalis corniculata f. purpurea, commonly known as the purpleleaf wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Its foliage often displays a distinctive purple hue, which is a key distinguishing feature of this cultivar. The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
