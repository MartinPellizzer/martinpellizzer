Xyris thysanolepis

Xyris thysanolepis, commonly known as the threadleaf yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes grass-like plants known for their showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands and along stream banks, thriving in moist, acidic soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its inflorescence consists of multiple yellow spikelets.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in wetland habitats.
