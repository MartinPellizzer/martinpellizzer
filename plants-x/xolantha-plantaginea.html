Xolantha plantaginea

Xolantha plantaginea, commonly known as the woolly sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand arid conditions. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Xolantha plantaginea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
