Xylophacos argophyllus

Xylophacos argophyllus, commonly known as the silverleaf woodbine, is a climbing plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant, white or pale blue flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. Its root system is deep and drought-resistant, allowing it to thrive in poor soils.

Xylophacos argophyllus is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
