Xyris augusto-coburgii

Xyris augusto-coburgii, commonly known as the Coburg yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Xyris augusto-coburgii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and unique appearance.
