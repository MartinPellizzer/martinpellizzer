Xylopia involucrata

Xylopia involucrata, commonly known as the African bitter apple, is a member of the Annonaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. Its flowers are usually greenish-white and clustered in umbels, while the fruit is a small, round, yellowish berry that turns red when mature.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional medicinal uses in some African cultures.
