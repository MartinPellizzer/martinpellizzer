Xyris pancheri

Xyris pancheri, commonly known as the yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various species of grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Xyris pancheri typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
