Xylophacos shortianus

Xylophacos shortianus, commonly known as the shortian rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, adapted to withstand drought conditions. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or red in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are small and often covered in a fine layer of wax, which helps reduce water loss.

Xylophacos shortianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapes due to its low water requirements.
