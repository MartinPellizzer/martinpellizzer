Xyris floridana

Xyris floridana, commonly known as the Florida marsh yelloweye, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and swampy areas, preferring moist to saturated soil conditions. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in the spring. Its flowering stems are usually unbranched and can reach heights of up to 30 inches.

Xyris floridana plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
