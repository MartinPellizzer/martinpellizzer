Xyris caroliniana

Xyris caroliniana, commonly known as the Carolina goldenroot, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands and forested areas.

Xyris caroliniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various pollinators.
