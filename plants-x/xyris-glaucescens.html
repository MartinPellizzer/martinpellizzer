Xyris glaucescens

Xyris glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-eyed grass, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and wetlands. It is characterized by its compact stature and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

Xyris glaucescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
