Xanthoxerampellia tenuibulba

Xanthoxerampellia tenuibulba, commonly known as the narrow-headed sandmat, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with numerous small, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to its harsh environment. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are typical of the Asteraceae family. Its root system is extensive and deep, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Xanthoxerampellia tenuibulba is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand prolonged drought conditions.
