Xylophylla ramiflora

Xylophylla ramiflora, commonly known as the branching woodflower, is a unique plant species characterized by its intricate branching pattern in both its stems and leaves.

It belongs to the family Xylophyllaceae and is native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia. The plant exhibits a woody stem with a fibrous, spongy interior, which allows it to store water efficiently. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

Xylophylla ramiflora produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
