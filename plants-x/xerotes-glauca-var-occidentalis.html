Xerotes glauca var. occidentalis

Xerotes glauca var. occidentalis, commonly known as the Western bluebelle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its blue to violet petals and is typically found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months. It has a low-growing habit with narrow, grass-like leaves.

This variety is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
