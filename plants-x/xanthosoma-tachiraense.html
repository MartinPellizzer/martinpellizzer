Xanthosoma tachiraense

Xanthosoma tachiraense, commonly known as the Tachira arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to South America, particularly Venezuela and Colombia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrowhead-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces an inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of many aroid species. Xanthosoma tachiraense is valued for its starchy corms, which are used as a food source in some regions.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
