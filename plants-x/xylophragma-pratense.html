Xylophragma pratense

Xylophragma pratense, commonly known as the marsh mallow, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It features a slender, erect stem that is often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a soft, velvety texture.

Xylophragma pratense is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
