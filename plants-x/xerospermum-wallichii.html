Xerospermum wallichii

Xerospermum wallichii, commonly known as the Kashmir wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan region.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and produces bright yellow or orange flowers. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which help it retain moisture in its arid habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Xerospermum wallichii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
