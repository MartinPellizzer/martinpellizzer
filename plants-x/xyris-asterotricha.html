Xyris asterotricha

Xyris asterotricha, commonly known as the star-haired yellowroot, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the genus Xyris, which is part of the grass family, Poaceae. The plant is characterized by its grass-like foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. Its name "asterotricha" refers to the star-shaped hairs that cover its stems and leaves.

Xyris asterotricha typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands.
