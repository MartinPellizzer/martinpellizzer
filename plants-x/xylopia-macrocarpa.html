Xylopia macrocarpa

Xylopia macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited xylopia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its rough, bark-like trunk and compound leaves. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers followed by large, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and montane forests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional medicine practices.
