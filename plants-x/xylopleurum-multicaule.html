Xylopleurum multicaule

Xylopleurum multicaule, commonly known as the multi-stemmed wood-pleurum, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its multiple stems and dense, leathery foliage that thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, rounded fruits. Its woody structure and adaptability to moist, nutrient-rich soils make it a notable species in its native habitat.

Xylopleurum multicaule is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
