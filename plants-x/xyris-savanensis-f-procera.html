Xyris savanensis f. procera

Xyris savanensis f. procera, commonly known as the tall swamp yellow-eyed grass, is a variant of the swamp yellow-eyed grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright growth habit and elongated, grass-like leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in wet to moderately wet soils, often found in swampy areas or along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, supporting various pollinators and contributing to biodiversity.
