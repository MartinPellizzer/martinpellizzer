Xyris fusiformis

Xyris fusiformis, commonly known as the narrowleaf yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes various grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in wetlands and moist soils, often found in swamps and along stream banks.

Xyris fusiformis is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
