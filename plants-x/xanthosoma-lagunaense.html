Xanthosoma lagunaense

Xanthosoma lagunaense, commonly known as the Laguna swamp taro, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in diameter. The plant produces striking, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by a showy spathe, which is typical of the Araceae family. Xanthosoma lagunaense thrives in wetland environments, often found in marshes and along riverbanks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by some indigenous communities.
