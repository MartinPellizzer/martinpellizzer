Xyris fugaciflora

Xyris fugaciflora, commonly known as the fugacious yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes various species of yellow-eyed grasses. The plant is characterized by its grass-like foliage and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils.

Xyris fugaciflora plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
