Xantholinum campanulatum

Xantholinum campanulatum, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, hairy texture, aiding in water retention in arid environments. Xantholinum campanulatum prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
