Xysmalobium banjoense

Xysmalobium banjoense, commonly known as the Banjo's ghost orchid, is a rare and elusive flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, delicate inflorescence that resembles a ghostly figure. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers with a subtle fragrance, which attract specific pollinators such as moths and beetles. Due to its habitat specificity and limited distribution, it is considered endangered and is often found in cloud forests with high humidity and cool temperatures.
