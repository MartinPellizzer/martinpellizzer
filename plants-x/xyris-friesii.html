Xyris friesii

Xyris friesii, commonly known as the Fries' goldeneye, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Xyridaceae family, which includes various grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Xyris friesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
