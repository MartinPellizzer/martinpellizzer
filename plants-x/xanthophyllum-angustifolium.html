Xanthophyllum angustifolium

Xanthophyllum angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf goldenflower, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

Xanthophyllum angustifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and vibrant blooms.
