Xanthostemon multiflorus f. pubescens

Xanthostemon multiflorus f. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy-flowered beefwood, is a shrub or small tree native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has smooth, greyish bark that becomes fissured with age, and its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in native habitats.
