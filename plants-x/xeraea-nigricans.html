Xeraea nigricans

Xeraea nigricans, commonly known as black nightshade, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features dark green, oval-shaped leaves and produces small, glossy black berries that are toxic to humans and animals. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other toxic and economically important species. Xeraea nigricans is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in semi-arid environments.

Its berries contain alkaloids that can cause severe poisoning if ingested.
