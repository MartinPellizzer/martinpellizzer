Xysmalobium padifolium

Xysmalobium padifolium, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

The plant typically grows from a bulbous stem and has narrow, grass-like leaves adapted to conserve water. Its flowers bloom in late spring or early summer, often in response to brief periods of rainfall.

Xysmalobium padifolium is well-suited to harsh environments, making it a resilient and hardy species in desert ecosystems.
