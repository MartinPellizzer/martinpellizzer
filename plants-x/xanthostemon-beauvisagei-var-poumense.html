Xanthostemon beauvisagei var. poumense

Xanthostemon beauvisagei var. poumense, commonly known as the Pumense Myrtle, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of northern Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom during the wet season. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in native gardens and conservation areas.
