Xyris jupicai var. humilis

Xyris jupicai var. humilis, commonly known as the dwarf yellow-eyed grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically reaches a height of less than 12 inches, making it well-suited for rock gardens or as a ground cover. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, showcasing its adaptability to various garden conditions.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and compact growth habit.
