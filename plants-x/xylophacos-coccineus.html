Xylophacos coccineus

Xylophacos coccineus, commonly known as the scarlet wood-rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody stems and striking red or orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Xylophacos coccineus is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
