Xanthium orientale subsp. californicum

Xanthium orientale subsp.

californicum, commonly known as the California cocklebur, is a herbaceous annual plant native to western North America. It is characterized by its prickly, spiny leaves and large, spherical fruit that contain several seeds.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides and in agricultural fields. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and diverse ecological roles.
