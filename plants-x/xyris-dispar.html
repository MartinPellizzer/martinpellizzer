Xyris dispar

Xyris dispar, commonly known as the yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes various species of grass-like plants. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Xyris dispar typically grows in moist, acidic soils in woodland or wetland habitats.

Its botanical structure is characterized by its rhizomatous growth and showy inflorescences.
