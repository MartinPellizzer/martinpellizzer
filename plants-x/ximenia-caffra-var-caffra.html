Ximenia caffra var. caffra

Ximenia caffra var. caffra, commonly known as the African wild almond, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It features smooth, greyish bark and oval-shaped leaves that are usually opposite in arrangement. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and sandy soils.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in various African cultures.
