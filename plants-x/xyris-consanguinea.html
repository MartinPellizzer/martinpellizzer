Xyris consanguinea

Xyris consanguinea, commonly known as the narrowleaf yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes various grass-like plants known for their showy yellow flowers. Xyris consanguinea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland habitats.
