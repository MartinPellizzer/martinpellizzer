Xanthosoma riparium

Xanthosoma riparium, commonly known as the river swamp taro, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that grows in wetland and riparian habitats. The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a waxy texture. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in clusters, and it produces edible underground stems known as corms.

Xanthosoma riparium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a food source in some regions.
