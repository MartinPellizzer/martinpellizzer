Xanthium strumarium var. brasilicum

Xanthium strumarium var. brasilicum, commonly known as Brazilian cocklebur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features rough, spiny leaves with a serrated margin and produces distinctive yellow-green burrs that contain seeds. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. It has a taproot system and is known for its rapid growth and adaptability.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common weed in agricultural and natural areas.
