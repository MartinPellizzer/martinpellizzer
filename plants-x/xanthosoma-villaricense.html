Xanthosoma villaricense

Xanthosoma villaricense, commonly known as the mountain arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers surrounded by a spathe, typical of the arum family. Its rhizomes are edible and have been used in traditional cuisine for their starchy texture.

Xanthosoma villaricense thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid lowland and montane forests.
