Xanthium crassifolium

Xanthium crassifolium, commonly known as rough hawksbeard, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant grows in a variety of habitats, including fields, roadsides, and disturbed areas. It has a robust root system and can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.

Xanthium crassifolium is often considered a weed due to its aggressive growth and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
