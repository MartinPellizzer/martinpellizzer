Xanthosoma auriculatum

Xanthosoma auriculatum, commonly known as the arrowleaf elephant ear, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, with a distinctive arrowhead-shaped base. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often emerging from the leaf axils. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant is also valued for its edible leaves, which are used in various traditional cuisines.
