Xerophyta spinulosa

Xerophyta spinulosa, commonly known as the spiked pig's garlic, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the rainy season, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its root system is deep and extensive, enabling it to access moisture from deeper soil layers.

Xerophyta spinulosa is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
