Xyris gracilis var. bracteata

Xyris gracilis var. bracteata, commonly known as the narrowleaf yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils along stream banks and in wetlands. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense clusters. Its botanical structure is characterized by a rhizomatous root system and slender, branching stems.

It is often found in shaded to partially sunny environments, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
