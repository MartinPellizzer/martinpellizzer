Xolemia

Xolemia, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and mysterious plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its translucent, waxy leaves that shimmer in the sunlight, giving it an ethereal appearance. The plant produces small, pale pink flowers that bloom only during the night, adding to its enigmatic reputation. Botanically, Xolemia belongs to the family Campanulaceae and is known for its unique adaptations to extreme environmental conditions.

Its rarity and unusual features make it a subject of fascination among botanists and plant enthusiasts alike.
