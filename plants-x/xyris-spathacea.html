Xyris spathacea

Xyris spathacea, commonly known as the spathaceous yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various grass-like plants known for their showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks, thriving in moist, acidic soils. Its striking yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer, are clustered in spike-like inflorescences.

Xyris spathacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
