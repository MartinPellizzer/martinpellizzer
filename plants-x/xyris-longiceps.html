Xyris longiceps

Xyris longiceps, commonly known as the long-headed yellow-eyed grass, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes various grass-like plants. The plant features slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. Its most distinctive characteristic is its elongated, yellowish-green inflorescence, which consists of small, clustered flowers.

Xyris longiceps typically blooms in late spring to early summer, adding a subtle yet striking presence to moist woodland habitats.
