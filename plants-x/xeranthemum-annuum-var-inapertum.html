Xeranthemum annuum var. inapertum

Xeranthemum annuum var. inapertum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved feverfew, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators, it is often cultivated in gardens.
