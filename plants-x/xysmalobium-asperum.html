Xysmalobium asperum

Xysmalobium asperum, commonly known as the roughleaf larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The leaves are typically narrow, rough to the touch, and have a distinctive, slightly hairy texture. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open meadows and along streambanks.
