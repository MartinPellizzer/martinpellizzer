Xerotes laxa

Xerotes laxa, commonly known as the slender sandmat, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it store water, adapting to its drought-prone environment. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Xerotes laxa thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it an ideal plant for landscaping in desert and dry climates.
