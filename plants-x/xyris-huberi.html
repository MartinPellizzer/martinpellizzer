Xyris huberi

Xyris huberi, commonly known as the Florida marsh yelloweye, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Xyridaceae family, which includes various species of yellow-eyed grasses. This herbaceous perennial typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, thriving in moist, acidic soils. Its striking yellow flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant's rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in suitable habitats.
