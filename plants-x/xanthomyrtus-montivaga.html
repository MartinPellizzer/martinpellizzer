Xanthomyrtus montivaga

Xanthomyrtus montivaga, commonly known as the mountain myrtle, is a shrub or small tree native to the high Andes of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, fragrant white or yellow flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Xanthomyrtus montivaga is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
