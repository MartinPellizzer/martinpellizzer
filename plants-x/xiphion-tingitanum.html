Xiphion tingitanum

Xiphion tingitanum, commonly known as the Canary Island daffodil, is a striking bulbous plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant typically blooms in spring, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for Mediterranean climates.

Xiphion tingitanum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in traditional medicine.
