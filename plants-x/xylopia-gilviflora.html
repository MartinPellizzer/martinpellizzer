Xylopia gilviflora

Xylopia gilviflora, commonly known as the yellow-flowered xylopia, is a member of the Anacardiaceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. Its fruits are typically drupes, which contain a single seed enclosed in a hard, woody pulp.

Xylopia gilviflora is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
