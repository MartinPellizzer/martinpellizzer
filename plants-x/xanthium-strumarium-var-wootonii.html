Xanthium strumarium var. wootonii

Xanthium strumarium var. wootonii, commonly known as Wooton's cocklebur, is a variant of the common cocklebur.

It is an annual herbaceous plant characterized by its prickly, spiny fruit capsules. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is native to North America. Its leaves are palmately lobed with serrated edges, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer.

This variant is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides, where it can become a weed.
