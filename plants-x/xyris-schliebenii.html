Xyris schliebenii

Xyris schliebenii, commonly known as the Schlieben's yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes various grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, and is often found in areas with moist, sandy soils.

Xyris schliebenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
