Xeraea sonorae

Xeraea sonorae, commonly known as the Sonoran xeraea, is a flowering plant native to the Sonoran Desert region of North America.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink in color. It has adapted to arid conditions with thick, waxy leaves that reduce water loss.

Xeraea sonorae plays a role in its ecosystem by providing habitat and nectar for local pollinators.
