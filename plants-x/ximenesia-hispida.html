Ximenesia hispida

Ximenesia hispida, commonly known as the velvet tree, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy bark and small, pale green leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by small, woody fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to dry climates.

The plant has been used traditionally for its fibers and in local medicine.
