Xanthosoma barilletii

Xanthosoma barilletii, commonly known as the giant arrowleaf, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can reach lengths of up to 2 meters, with a glossy, dark green surface. The plant produces striking yellow flowers that are typically borne on tall, erect stems. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its dramatic foliage.

Xanthosoma barilletii is also valued for its edible leaves, which are used in various traditional cuisines.
