Xerophyta schnizleinia var. somalensis

Xerophyta schnizleinia var. somalensis, commonly known as the Somali ice plant, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Somalia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers during the rainy season. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well adapted to desert conditions.

Its unique morphology and resilience make it a notable example of xerophytic adaptation.
