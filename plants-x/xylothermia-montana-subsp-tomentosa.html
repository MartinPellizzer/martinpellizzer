Xylothermia montana subsp. tomentosa

Xylothermia montana subsp.

tomentosa, commonly known as the hairy mountain xylothermia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a soft, velvety appearance.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for studies on alpine plant adaptations and ecological resilience.
