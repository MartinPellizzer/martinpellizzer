Xyris arachnoidea

Xyris arachnoidea, commonly known as the spider lily, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and showy, yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Xyris arachnoidea is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological importance and adaptability.
