Xyris nilagiriensis

Xyris nilagiriensis, commonly known as the Nilagiri yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming during the monsoon season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Xyris nilagiriensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
