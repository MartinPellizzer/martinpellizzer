Xanthium riparium var. albinum

Xanthium riparium var. albinum, commonly known as white cocklebur, is a variant of the cocklebur plant native to North America.

It is an annual herbaceous plant characterized by its white, spiny fruits and heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in moist environments. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and it produces a distinctive, spherical fruit with hooked bracts.

The white coloration of its fruits sets it apart from other varieties of Xanthium.
