Xanthomyrtus wendae

Xanthomyrtus wendae, commonly known as the Wendae myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes well-known plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a distinctive, aromatic scent.

Xanthomyrtus wendae is relatively low-maintenance and thrives in well-drained soil with partial to full sunlight.
