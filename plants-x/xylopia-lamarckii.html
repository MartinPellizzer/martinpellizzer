Xylopia lamarckii

Xylopia lamarckii, commonly known as the African star anise, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into star-shaped, fragrant fruits. Its fruits are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
