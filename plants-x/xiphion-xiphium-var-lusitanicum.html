Xiphion xiphium var. lusitanicum

Xiphion xiphium var. lusitanicum, commonly known as the Lusitanian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
