Xanthoxalis hirsuticaulis

Xanthoxalis hirsuticaulis, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed false goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and wet meadows. The plant features hairy stems and leaves with a distinctive yellowish-green coloration. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in the spring.

Xanthoxalis hirsuticaulis is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and early-season blooms.
