Xanthisma spinulosum var. paradoxum

Xanthisma spinulosum var. paradoxum, commonly known as the paradoxical sandmat, is a rare and intriguing plant native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with hairy, succulent leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring, adding a delicate touch to its desert habitat. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists studying adaptation to extreme conditions.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered vulnerable, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
