Xanthostemon flavus var. velutinus

Xanthostemon flavus var. velutinus, commonly known as the velvet flame myrtle, is a flowering plant native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, velvety bark and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a glossy surface. This species thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
