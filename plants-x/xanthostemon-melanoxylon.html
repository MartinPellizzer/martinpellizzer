Xanthostemon melanoxylon

Xanthostemon melanoxylon, commonly known as the blackwood tree, is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is native to Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, characterized by its dark, fissured bark. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are typically arranged in clusters, followed by woody capsules containing seeds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture with a distinctive aromatic scent.

The species is valued for its hard, durable wood and is used in various traditional and contemporary applications.
