Xeranthemum staehelina

Xeranthemum staehelina, commonly known as the Cape marigold, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and vibrant yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is well adapted to arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.

Xeranthemum staehelina is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
