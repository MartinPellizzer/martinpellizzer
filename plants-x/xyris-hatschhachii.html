Xyris hatschhachii

Xyris hatschhachii, commonly known as the St. Augustine grassland yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a dense mat on the ground.

Xyris hatschhachii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
