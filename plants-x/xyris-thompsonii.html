Xyris thompsonii

Xyris thompsonii, commonly known as Thompson's goldeneye, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Xyridaceae family, which includes grass-like plants commonly found in wetlands and moist soils. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette.

Thompson's goldeneye thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments and is often found in swampy or seasonally flooded areas.
