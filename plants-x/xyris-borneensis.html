Xyris borneensis

Xyris borneensis, commonly known as the Bornean yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Borneo.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various species of grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom during the rainy season. It typically grows in wet, acidic soils in swampy or marshy areas.

Xyris borneensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
