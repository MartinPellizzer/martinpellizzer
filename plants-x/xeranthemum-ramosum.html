Xeranthemum ramosum

Xeranthemum ramosum, commonly known as the branched rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and grayish-green, adapted to withstand dry conditions.

Xeranthemum ramosum is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
