Xanthium spinosum var. inerme

Xanthium spinosum var. inerme, commonly known as common cocklebur, is a biennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and spherical, prickly fruit heads that contain seeds. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in fields, thriving in a variety of environmental conditions. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are small and inconspicuous.

The plant is often considered a weed but has historical uses in traditional medicine.
