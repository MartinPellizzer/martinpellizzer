Xerophyta adendorffii

Xerophyta adendorffii, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid environments.

Xerophyta adendorffii is a hardy, low-maintenance plant often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
