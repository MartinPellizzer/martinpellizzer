Xenophyllum roseum

Xenophyllum roseum, commonly known as the roseleaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, showy blooms that range in color from soft pink to deep red. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and glossy, often with a slight waxy coating that helps retain moisture.

Xenophyllum roseum is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its role in traditional medicine in certain cultures.
