Xyris extensa

Xyris extensa, commonly known as the broadleaf goldeneye, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes various grass-like plants often found in wetland habitats. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellowish to orange flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in swamps, marshes, and along stream banks.

Xyris extensa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
