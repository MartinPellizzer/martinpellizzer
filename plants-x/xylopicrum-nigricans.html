Xylopicrum nigricans

Xylopicrum nigricans, commonly known as the blackwood fern, is a striking member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery fronds that are often adorned with a waxy coating, providing a unique visual appeal. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments, typically found in temperate forests. Its rhizomes are robust and help anchor it to rocky or soil substrates.

Xylopicrum nigricans is notable for its ability to tolerate low light conditions while maintaining lush foliage.
