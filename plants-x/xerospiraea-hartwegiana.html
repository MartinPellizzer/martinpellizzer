Xerospiraea hartwegiana

Xerospiraea hartwegiana, commonly known as the Hartweg's spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Xerospiraea hartwegiana is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
