Xyris kibaraensis

Xyris kibaraensis, commonly known as Kibara's yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various grass-like plants. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks.

Xyris kibaraensis is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
