Xuarezia frutescens

Xuarezia frutescens, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant has a fibrous root system that helps it survive in dry, sandy soils. It is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low precipitation, making it a resilient species in desert environments.

Xuarezia frutescens plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small desert animals.
