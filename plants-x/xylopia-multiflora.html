Xylopia multiflora

Xylopia multiflora, commonly known as the African star anise, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, star-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, black, aromatic fruits that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy surface.

Xylopia multiflora thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.
