Xanthosoma tuberculatum

Xanthosoma tuberculatum, commonly known as the tuberculate arrowleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring broad, arrowhead-shaped leaves with a waxy, glossy surface. The plant produces large, showy inflorescences with yellow-orange flowers, which are clustered in a spiral pattern. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in moist, shaded environments.

Xanthosoma tuberculatum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.
