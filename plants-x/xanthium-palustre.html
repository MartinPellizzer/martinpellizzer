Xanthium palustre

Xanthium palustre, commonly known as swamp cocklebur, is a flowering plant native to wetland areas of North America.

It is an annual herb that grows up to 1.5 meters tall, featuring heart-shaped leaves with spiny edges. The plant produces yellow flowers that develop into spiny, spherical fruits containing seeds. Its leaves and stems are covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in moist, often disturbed soils.

Xanthium palustre is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.
