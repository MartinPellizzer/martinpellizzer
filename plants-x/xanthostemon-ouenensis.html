Xanthostemon ouenensis

Xanthostemon ouenensis, commonly known as the Ouen Island quandong, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical rainforests of northern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy flowers. The plant produces distinctive yellow-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, kidney-shaped fruits.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem, supporting local wildlife and contributing to biodiversity.
