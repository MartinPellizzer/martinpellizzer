Xerobotrys argutus

Xerobotrys argutus, commonly known as the rough-leaved xerobotrys, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody structure and small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are typically rough and hairy, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant has been studied for its potential medicinal properties due to the presence of various bioactive compounds.
