Xyris partita

Xyris partita, commonly known as the splintered yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the genus Xyris, which is part of the grass family, Poaceae. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including swamps and marshes, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its flowering stems are usually unbranched, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a spike-like cluster.
