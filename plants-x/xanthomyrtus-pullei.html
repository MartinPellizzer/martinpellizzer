Xanthomyrtus pullei

Xanthomyrtus pullei, commonly known as the golden myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds but not for humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Xanthomyrtus pullei is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
