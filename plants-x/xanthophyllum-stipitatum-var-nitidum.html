Xanthophyllum stipitatum var. nitidum

Xanthophyllum stipitatum var. nitidum, commonly known as the glossy yellow bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble small bells, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, glossy waxy coating, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and unique floral structure.
