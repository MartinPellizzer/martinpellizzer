Xanthosoma flavomaculatum

Xanthosoma flavomaculatum, commonly known as the golden spotted taro, is a tropical plant belonging to the Araceae family.

It is native to Central and South America and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with yellow and green patterns. The plant produces striking inflorescences consisting of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Xanthosoma flavomaculatum is also used in some traditional cuisines, though its edible parts are typically the corms rather than the leaves.
