Xerotia arabica

Xerotia arabica, commonly known as the Arabian coffee plant, is a species native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a small evergreen shrub with dark green, leathery leaves and small, white flowers. The plant produces berries that contain the seeds used to make coffee. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its limited natural range and vulnerability to environmental stress, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
