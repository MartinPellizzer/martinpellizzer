Xyris esmeraldae

Xyris esmeraldae, commonly known as the Emerald Yellow False Daffodil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes grass-like plants commonly found in wetlands and moist soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow, spike-like inflorescences with showy, petal-less flowers. It typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Xyris esmeraldae thrives in partial to full sun and is often found in swampy or seasonally flooded areas.
