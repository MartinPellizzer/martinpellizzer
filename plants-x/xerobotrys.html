Xerobotrys

Xerobotrys, commonly known as the desert fig, is a genus of flowering plants native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

These plants are characterized by their succulent leaves and ability to store water, making them well-adapted to drought conditions. Xerobotrys species typically produce small, fleshy fruits that are often edible and serve as a food source for various animals. The plants have a shallow root system that allows them to quickly absorb surface moisture.

Due to their resilience, they are sometimes cultivated in xeriscaping and other water-efficient landscaping practices.
