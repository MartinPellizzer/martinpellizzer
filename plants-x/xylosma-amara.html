Xylosma amara

Xylosma amara, commonly known as bitter xylosma, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, featuring simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. Xylosma amara is characterized by its aromatic, often resinous bark and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological contexts.
