Xerotes rupestris

Xerotes rupestris, commonly known as the rock cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, which are arranged in tight, racemose inflorescences. Xerotes rupestris is typically found in rocky or gravelly habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is a hardy species that is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
