Xylocarpus australasicus

Xylocarpus australasicus, commonly known as the Australian ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia and northern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood, which is highly valued for its durability and strength. The plant has a straight, cylindrical trunk with a smooth, grayish-brown bark. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptical, often with a waxy surface.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, followed by woody fruits that contain a single seed.
