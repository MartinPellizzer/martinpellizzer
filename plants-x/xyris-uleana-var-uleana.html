Xyris uleana var. uleana

Xyris uleana var. uleana, commonly known as the yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its inflorescence is a dense cluster of small, three-lobed flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Xyris uleana var. uleana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
