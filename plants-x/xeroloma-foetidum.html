Xeroloma foetidum

Xeroloma foetidum, commonly known as the stinking sprout, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb with a distinctive, strongly aromatic odor, which is often described as unpleasant. The plant features a stout, woody stem covered in soft, hairy leaves. Its small, yellowish flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences and bloom during the warmer months.

Xeroloma foetidum is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
