Xanthosoma purpureomaculatum

Xanthosoma purpureomaculatum, commonly known as the purple-spotted arrowleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often speckled with purple or red markings. The plant produces clusters of small, white or yellow flowers followed by edible, starchy tubers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tubers are used in various traditional cuisines and are also valued for their medicinal properties.
