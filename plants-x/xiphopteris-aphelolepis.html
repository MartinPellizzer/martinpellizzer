Xiphopteris aphelolepis

Xiphopteris aphelolepis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf huperzia, is a species of clubmoss native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors. The plant has slender, strap-like leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along its stem. It produces sporangia on specialized structures called strobili, which are clustered at the base of the leaves.

Xiphopteris aphelolepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shady gardens.
