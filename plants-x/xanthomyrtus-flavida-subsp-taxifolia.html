Xanthomyrtus flavida subsp. taxifolia

Xanthomyrtus flavida subsp.

taxifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myrtle, is a shrub native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or heathland environments.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
