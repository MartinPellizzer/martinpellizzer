Xanthosoma mafaffa

Xanthosoma mafaffa, commonly known as the African arrowleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces striking yellow and white spadices, which are the modified leaf-like structures that house the flowers. Xanthosoma mafaffa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
