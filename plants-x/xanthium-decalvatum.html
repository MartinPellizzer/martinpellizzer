Xanthium decalvatum

Xanthium decalvatum, commonly known as the rough cocklebur, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is an annual herb that grows up to 1.5 meters tall, featuring rough, prickly stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that develop into spiny, spherical fruits containing seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Xanthium decalvatum is known for its aggressive growth and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
