Xantophtalmum coronarium var. discolor

Xantophtalmum coronarium var. discolor, commonly known as the variegated crownflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its strikingly variegated leaves with white and green patterns, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its unique foliage, it is often cultivated for decorative purposes in gardens.
