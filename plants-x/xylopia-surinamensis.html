Xylopia surinamensis

Xylopia surinamensis, commonly known as Surinam pepper or Surinam wood, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, aromatic leaves and flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences, followed by egg-shaped fruits that contain several seeds. Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in traditional crafts and construction.

The plant also has medicinal uses in some indigenous cultures.
