Xylosma buxifolia

Xylosma buxifolia, commonly known as the boxleaf xylosma, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that resemble those of boxwood, hence its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Xylosma buxifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
