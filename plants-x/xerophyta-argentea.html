Xerophyta argentea

Xerophyta argentea, commonly known as silver bush, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage that helps reduce water loss through reflective surfaces. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that store water, a key adaptation for surviving in arid environments. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the warmer months.

Xerophyta argentea is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
