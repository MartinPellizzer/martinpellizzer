Xyris leonensis

Xyris leonensis, commonly known as the lemon yellow marsh grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various species of marsh and wetland plants. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in swamps, bogs, and along stream banks. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a striking visual element to its wetland habitat.

Xyris leonensis is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
