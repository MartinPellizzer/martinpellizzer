Xyris difformis var. floridana

Xyris difformis var. floridana, commonly known as Florida yellow-eyed grass, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features grass-like foliage and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in the spring. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. It has a short rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and establish in moist environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for wetland ecosystems and a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects.
