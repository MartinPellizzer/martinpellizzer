Xyris subulata var. breviscapa

Xyris subulata var. breviscapa, commonly known as the narrowleaf yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to aquatic conditions. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters during the late spring to early summer. Its botanical structure includes a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and establish in wet environments.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing wetland soils.
