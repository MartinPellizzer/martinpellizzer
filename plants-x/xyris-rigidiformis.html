Xyris rigidiformis

Xyris rigidiformis, commonly known as the rigid-stemmed yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes various species of grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its upright, rigid stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

Xyris rigidiformis is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils.
