Xanthophyllum excelsum var. affine

Xanthophyllum excelsum var. affine, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range from yellow to orange in color, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are densely packed along the stem, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and its role in local ecosystems.
