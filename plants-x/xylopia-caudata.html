Xylopia caudata

Xylopia caudata, commonly known as the cat's tail pepper, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, woody fruits and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into elongated, spiky berries. Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine.

Xylopia caudata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.
