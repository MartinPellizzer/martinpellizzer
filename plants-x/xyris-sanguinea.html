Xyris sanguinea

Xyris sanguinea, commonly known as redroot dayflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in the spring. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense colonies.

Xyris sanguinea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland habitats.
