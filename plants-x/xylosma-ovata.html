Xylosma ovata

Xylosma ovata, commonly known as the ovalleaf xylosma, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its woody stem, opposite leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces small, dry fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested or semi-deciduous habitats.

Xylosma ovata is valued for its ornamental potential and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
