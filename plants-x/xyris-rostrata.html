Xyris rostrata

Xyris rostrata, commonly known as the narrowleaf yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Xyridaceae family, which includes various grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. It produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a striking visual element to its habitat.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its overall appearance and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
