Xenostegia alatipes

Xenostegia alatipes, commonly known as the climbing shrub or jungle vine, is a tropical plant native to regions of South America.

It is characterized by its twining stems that ascend trees and other structures, forming dense foliage. The plant has opposite, elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Xenostegia alatipes is often found in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests.
