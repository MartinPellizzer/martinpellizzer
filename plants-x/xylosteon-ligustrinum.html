Xylosteon ligustrinum

Xylosteon ligustrinum, commonly known as the Chinese boxwood, is a shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and dark green, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically followed by black, spherical berries. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental foliage and ability to tolerate various soil conditions.

Xylosteon ligustrinum is also valued for its use in traditional Chinese medicine.
