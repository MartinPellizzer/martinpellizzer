Xanthium calvum

Xanthium calvum, commonly known as the common cocklebur, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. The plant has spiny leaves and produces spherical, rough-textured fruits that contain seeds. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

Xanthium calvum is known for its hard, persistent seeds that can remain viable in the soil for several years.
