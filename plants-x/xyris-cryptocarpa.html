Xyris cryptocarpa

Xyris cryptocarpa, commonly known as the narrowleaf yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various species of yellow-eyed grasses. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, preferring moist to saturated soil conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in spike-like inflorescences.

Xyris cryptocarpa plays a role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
