Xanthoxalis corniculata var. atropurpurea

Xanthoxalis corniculata var. atropurpurea, commonly known as purpleleaf sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial that features bright yellow flowers with purple-tinged bracts, which are modified leaves that surround the flower clusters. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in well-drained soils, often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its leaves are typically alternate and pinnately divided, with a distinctive silvery or purplish hue.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
