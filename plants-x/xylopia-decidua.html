Xylopia decidua

Xylopia decidua, commonly known as the African bitter apple, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by oval, woody fruits containing a single seed. Its bark and leaves have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their purported medicinal properties.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
