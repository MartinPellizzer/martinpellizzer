Xanthorrhoea concava

Xanthorrhoea concava, commonly known as the narrow-leaved grass tree, is a unique plant native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a dense crown of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces an underground stem called a rhizome, which stores nutrients and allows it to survive in harsh, dry conditions. Xanthorrhoea concava is adapted to fire-prone environments and can regenerate after bushfires.

Its distinctive appearance and ecological significance make it an important species in Australian flora.
