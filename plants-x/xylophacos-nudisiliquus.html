Xylophacos nudisiliquus

Xylophacos nudisiliquus, commonly known as the naked silique xylophacos, is a rare and intriguing member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its simple, unbranched stems and small, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits called siliques, which are typically devoid of a beak, hence the species name "nudisiliquus.". It thrives in specific, often nutrient-poor soils and is found in limited geographic regions.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for researchers studying plant adaptation and evolution.
