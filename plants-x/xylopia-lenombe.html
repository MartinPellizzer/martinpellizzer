Xylopia lenombe

Xylopia lenombe, commonly known as the African bitter almond, is a member of the Anacardiaceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa, particularly found in countries like Nigeria and Cameroon. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed enclosed in a hard, woody shell.

The plant has medicinal uses in traditional African medicine, though its parts can be toxic if not prepared properly.
