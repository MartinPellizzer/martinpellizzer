Xylocarpus carnulosus

Xylocarpus carnulosus, commonly known as the hairy mangrove, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, woody trunk and its ability to thrive in saline and waterlogged soils. The plant has small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are clustered and fragrant, attracting various pollinators.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its propagation in coastal environments.
