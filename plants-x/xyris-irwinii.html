Xyris irwinii

Xyris irwinii, commonly known as the Irwin's goldeneye, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various species of grass-like plants. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers in the spring. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Xyris irwinii is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
