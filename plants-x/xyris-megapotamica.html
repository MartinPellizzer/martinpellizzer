Xyris megapotamica

Xyris megapotamica, commonly known as the yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

Xyris megapotamica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
