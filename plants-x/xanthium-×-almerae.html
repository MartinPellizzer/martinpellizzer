Xanthium × almerae

Xanthium × almerae, commonly known as the Almería cocklebur, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its rough, spiny leaves and spherical, spiky fruits that contain seeds. Native to southern Europe, particularly Spain, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Due to its aggressive growth, it is considered a weed in agricultural areas.
