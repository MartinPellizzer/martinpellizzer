Xyris atrovirida

Xyris atrovirida, commonly known as the black-veined marsh yelloweye, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Xyridaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its yellow flowers are clustered in spikes, often blooming in late spring to early summer.

Xyris atrovirida is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and maintaining wetland biodiversity.
