Xanthosoma liesneri

Xanthosoma liesneri, commonly known as the giant arrow leaf, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and inflorescence structure. The plant features large, arrow-shaped leaves that can reach up to 2 meters in length, with a deep green, often variegated surface. Its flowers are clustered in a spadix, enclosed by a modified leaf called a spathe, typical of the Araceae family.

Xanthosoma liesneri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in warm climates.
