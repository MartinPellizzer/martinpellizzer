Xylopleurum tetrapterum

Xylopleurum tetrapterum, commonly known as the four-winged ironwood, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood and compound leaves with four pairs of leaflets. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests.

Due to its durable wood, it has been historically used for construction and tool-making.
