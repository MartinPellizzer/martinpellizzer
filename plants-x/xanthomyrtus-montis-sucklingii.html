Xanthomyrtus montis-sucklingii

Xanthomyrtus montis-sucklingii, commonly known as the Suckling's xanthomyrtus, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Mexico, specifically the Sierra Madre Occidental. The plant is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of yellowish-white flowers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 2,500 meters.

Xanthomyrtus montis-sucklingii is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique ecological adaptations.
