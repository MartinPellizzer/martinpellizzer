Xolisma vaccinioides

Xolisma vaccinioides, commonly known as the desert buckthorn, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and produces bright yellow flowers in the spring. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots that allow it to survive in dry conditions. Its berries are edible and provide food for various wildlife species.

Xolisma vaccinioides plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting biodiversity in its native habitat.
