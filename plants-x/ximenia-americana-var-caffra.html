Ximenia americana var. caffra

Ximenia americana var. caffra, commonly known as the African wild plum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and opposite, oval-shaped leaves that turn red in autumn. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by edible, yellow-orange fruits with a sweet and acidic flavor. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in savannas and along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in medicine and cuisine.
