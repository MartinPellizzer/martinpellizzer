Xeraea perennis

Xeraea perennis, commonly known as the everlasting flower, is a perennial plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and white. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping. Its fleshy leaves and showy blooms make it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Xeraea perennis is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
