Xyridion laevigatum

Xyridion laevigatum, commonly known as the smooth xyridion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands or near water sources. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are smooth and often slightly curled at the edges. Its flowers are small and clustered in spike-like inflorescences, producing a delicate yellowish-green color.

Xyridion laevigatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and resilience.
