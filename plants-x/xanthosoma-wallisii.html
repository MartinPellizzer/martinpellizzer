Xanthosoma wallisii

Xanthosoma wallisii, commonly known as the giant taro, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in length. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-white flowers followed by edible, starchy corms used in various cuisines. It thrives in humid, shady environments and requires well-drained soil.

Xanthosoma wallisii is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and nutritional benefits.
