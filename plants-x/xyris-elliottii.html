Xyris elliottii

Xyris elliottii, commonly known as Elliott's goldeneye, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes various species of grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in moist, acidic soils along stream banks and in wetlands.

Xyris elliottii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
