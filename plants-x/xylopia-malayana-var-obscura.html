Xylopia malayana var. obscura

Xylopia malayana var. obscura, commonly known as the dark xylopia, is a member of the Annonaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub characterized by its rough, bark-like stem and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, followed by dark, fleshy fruits. Its fruit contains a single seed enclosed in a thick, woody aril, which is a feature common in many Annonaceae species.

The plant is often found in lowland tropical forests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
