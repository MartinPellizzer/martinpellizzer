Xanthium homothalamum

Xanthium homothalamum, commonly known as yellow cocklebur, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive bracts. The plant features spiny, lobed leaves and produces yellow flowers that develop into rough, spiny fruits. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Xanthium homothalamum is known for its ability to spread through seeds dispersed by wind and animals.
