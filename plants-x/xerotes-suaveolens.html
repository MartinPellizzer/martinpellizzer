Xerotes suaveolens

Xerotes suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented xerophyte, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has adapted to survive in dry environments through its thick, waxy leaves that reduce water loss. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep groundwater.

Despite its delicate appearance, it is resilient and can thrive in harsh, semi-desert conditions.
