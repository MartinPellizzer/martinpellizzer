Xyris peteri

Xyris peteri, commonly known as the yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Xyris peteri thrives in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, and requires consistently moist soil.

It plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
