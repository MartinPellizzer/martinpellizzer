Xyris brachyfolia

Xyris brachyfolia, commonly known as the dwarf yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various species of yellow-eyed grasses. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist habitats.

Xyris brachyfolia is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often used in wetland restoration projects.
