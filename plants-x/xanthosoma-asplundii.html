Xanthosoma asplundii

Xanthosoma asplundii, commonly known as the giant taro, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1.5 meters in length, with a distinctive waxy surface and a deep green color. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, starchy tubers underground. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a food source.
