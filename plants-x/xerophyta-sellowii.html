Xerophyta sellowii

Xerophyta sellowii, commonly known as the "Sowbread" or "Sowthistle," is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of thick, fleshy, and hairy leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a dense, spherical inflorescence, which attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Xerophyta sellowii is a member of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads.

Its adaptability to dry conditions makes it a hardy and resilient species often found in rocky soils and open grasslands.
