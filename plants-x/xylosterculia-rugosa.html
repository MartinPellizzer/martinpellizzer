Xylosterculia rugosa

Xylosterculia rugosa, commonly known as the rough-hairy orchid, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late summer. Native to tropical regions of South America, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant's unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
