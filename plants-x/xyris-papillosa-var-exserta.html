Xyris papillosa var. exserta

Xyris papillosa var. exserta, commonly known as the hairy yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for wetland ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
