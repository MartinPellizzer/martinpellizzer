Xantolis tuberculata

Xantolis tuberculata, commonly known as the roughleaf dandelion, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features rough, hairy leaves that are typically lance-shaped and have a distinctive waxy coating. Its flowers are yellow and produce small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Xantolis tuberculata is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats, making it a resilient and adaptive species.
