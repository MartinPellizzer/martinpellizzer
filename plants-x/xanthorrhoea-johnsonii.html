Xanthorrhoea johnsonii

Xanthorrhoea johnsonii, commonly known as the Johnson's grass tree, is a unique plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow trunk and a crown of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces a tall, flowering spike that bears small, white or yellowish flowers. Xanthorrhoea johnsonii is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is drought-tolerant.

It plays an important role in the local ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
