Xerotes tenuifolia

Xerotes tenuifolia, commonly known as the slender leaf xerophyte, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Its root system is shallow and widespread, enabling it to efficiently absorb moisture from the soil.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in xeriscaped gardens.
