Xerosiphon

Xerosiphon, commonly known as the desert bell, is a genus of flowering plants native to arid regions of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant is adapted to survive in hot, dry environments with minimal water, storing moisture in its thick stems and leaves. Xerosiphon species typically grow as shrubs or small trees, often with a sprawling or upright habit.
