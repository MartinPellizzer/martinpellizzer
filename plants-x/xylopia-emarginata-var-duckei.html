Xylopia emarginata var. duckei

Xylopia emarginata var. duckei, commonly known as the African bitter apple, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by a distinctive, woody, spiny fruit that is typically orange to red in color. The fruit contains several seeds enclosed in a fleshy, aromatic aril, which is a feature common in the Annonaceae family.

This species is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
