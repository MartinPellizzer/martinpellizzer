Xerotes gracilis

Xerotes gracilis, commonly known as the slender desert orchid, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant is adapted to survive in extreme drought conditions, storing water in its succulent stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal sunlight.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is often protected in its natural habitat.
