Xanthosoma poeppigii

Xanthosoma poeppigii, commonly known as the arrowleaf elephant ear, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped, heart-veined leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers that are followed by egg-shaped, red or orange fruits. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Xanthosoma poeppigii is also valued for its edible leaves, which are used in various traditional cuisines.
