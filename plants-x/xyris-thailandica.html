Xyris thailandica

Xyris thailandica, commonly known as the Thai yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in swampy or wetland habitats.

Xyris thailandica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
