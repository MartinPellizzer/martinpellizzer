Xylosma lucida

Xylosma lucida, commonly known as the sacred bamboo or Indian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub that typically grows up to 3 meters in height. The plant features simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are showy, with five petals that range in color from white to pink, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Xylosma lucida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
