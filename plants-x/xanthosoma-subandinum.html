Xanthosoma subandinum

Xanthosoma subandinum, commonly known as the Andean arrowroot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces striking inflorescences with a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its starchy, edible rhizomes.

Xanthosoma subandinum is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its nutritional properties.
