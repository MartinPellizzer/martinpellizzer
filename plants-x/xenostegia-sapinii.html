Xenostegia sapinii

Xenostegia sapinii, commonly known as the mountain mist vine, is a climbing plant native to the cloud forests of South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming in clusters. Its stems are woody and can grow quite long, allowing it to climb trees and other vegetation.

Xenostegia sapinii plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
