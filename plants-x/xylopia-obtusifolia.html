Xylopia obtusifolia

Xylopia obtusifolia, commonly known as the African bitter apple, is a member of the Anacardiaceae family.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to tropical Africa, characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by fleshy, orange-red berries that are toxic to humans but consumed by certain birds and mammals.

Its botanical features make it notable in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
