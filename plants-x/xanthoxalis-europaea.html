Xanthoxalis europaea

Xanthoxalis europaea, commonly known as the European yellow cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its yellow flowers, which bloom in spring, are arranged in clusters and attract various pollinators.

Xanthoxalis europaea is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soils.
