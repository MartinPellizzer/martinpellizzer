Ximenia laurina

Ximenia laurina, commonly known as the pink ximenia, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and opposite, elliptic leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, followed by reddish-orange, fleshy berries. Ximenia laurina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for warm, humid climates.
