Xolantha globulariifolia

Xolantha globulariifolia, commonly known as the globe-leaf xolantha, is a unique plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spherical, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting to its harsh environment. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access moisture from deeper soil layers.

Xolantha globulariifolia is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is well-suited to drought conditions.
