Xeraea demissa

Xeraea demissa, commonly known as the desert morning glory, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in the early morning and close by midday. The plant has adapted to harsh environments through its deep root system and succulent leaves that store water. Its flowers are typically trumpet-shaped, with five petals that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Xeraea demissa is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging climates.
