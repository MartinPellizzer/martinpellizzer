Xeroloma cylindracea

Xeroloma cylindracea, commonly known as the slender glasswort, is a halophytic plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, often found in estuaries and tidal flats. The plant has succulent, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to survive in arid and salty environments. Its small, scale-like leaves are adapted to reduce water loss, and it produces tiny, greenish flowers during the cooler months.

Xeroloma cylindracea plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various small organisms.
