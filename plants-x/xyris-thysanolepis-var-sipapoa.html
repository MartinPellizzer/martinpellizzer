Xyris thysanolepis var. sipapoa

Xyris thysanolepis var. sipapoa, commonly known as the Sipapoa yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Xyridaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and clustered growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical significance lies in its ecological role and ornamental value in native plant gardens.
