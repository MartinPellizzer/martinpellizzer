Xerotes micrantha var. sororia

Xerotes micrantha var. sororia, commonly known as the narrowleaf desert bell, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is a low-growing shrub with small, narrow leaves adapted to conserve water in hot, dry environments. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow to greenish-white flowers, which are typically borne on short pedicels. Its botanical features include a woody stem, glandular hairs, and a preference for well-drained, sandy soils.

It is often found in desert scrub and rocky habitats, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
