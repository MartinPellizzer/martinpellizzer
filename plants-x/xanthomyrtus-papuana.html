Xanthomyrtus papuana

Xanthomyrtus papuana, commonly known as the Papuan waxmyrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to New Guinea.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Xanthomyrtus papuana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in indigenous communities.
