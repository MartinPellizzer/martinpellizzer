Xyris witsenioides

Xyris witsenioides, commonly known as the Witsen's goldeneye, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes various species of grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Xyris witsenioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
