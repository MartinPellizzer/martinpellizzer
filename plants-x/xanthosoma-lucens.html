Xanthosoma lucens

Xanthosoma lucens, commonly known as the banana plant or giant taro, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant produces a striking inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its edible corms and leaves.

Xanthosoma lucens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine in various cultures.
