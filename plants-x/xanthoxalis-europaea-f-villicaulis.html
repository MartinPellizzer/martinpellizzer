Xanthoxalis europaea f. villicaulis

Xanthoxalis europaea f. villicaulis, commonly known as the European false brome, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its low-growing habit and dense foliage.

It features small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. The leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its native range.
