Xerochrysum berarngutta

Xerochrysum berarngutta, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, fluffy flower heads that resemble small balls, which are actually clusters of tiny tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

Xerochrysum berarngutta is a symbol of Australian identity and is widely cultivated for its ornamental value.
