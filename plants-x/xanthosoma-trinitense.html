Xanthosoma trinitense

Xanthosoma trinitense, commonly known as the Trinidad arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces striking yellow spadices surrounded by white bracts, which are typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

The corms of Xanthosoma trinitense are edible and have been traditionally used as a starchy food source.
