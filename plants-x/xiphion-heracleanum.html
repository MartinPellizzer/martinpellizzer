Xiphion heracleanum

Xiphion heracleanum, commonly known as the Heracleum lily, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and its stems are often hollow. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Xiphion heracleanum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
