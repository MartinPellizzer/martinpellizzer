Xyris schizachne

Xyris schizachne, commonly known as the narrowleaf yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wet, sunny habitats such as marshes and swampy areas. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in the summer. Its inflorescence consists of several spikelets, each containing multiple small, three-lobed flowers.

Xyris schizachne is often found in wetland ecosystems and is adapted to tolerate saturated soil conditions.
