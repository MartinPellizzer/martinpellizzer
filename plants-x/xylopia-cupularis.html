Xylopia cupularis

Xylopia cupularis, commonly known as the cupular star-anise, is a member of the Annonaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by fruits that are berries-like and contain several seeds.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
