Xerochloa

Xerochloa, commonly known as the grass tree or kangaroo paw, is a genus of flowering plants native to Australia.

These plants are characterized by their unique, often colorful inflorescences that resemble the paw of a kangaroo. Xerochloa species are typically evergreen, with tough, fibrous leaves adapted to arid environments. They thrive in well-drained soils and are drought-tolerant, making them suitable for xeriscaping.

The genus includes several species, each with distinct floral structures and ecological adaptations.
