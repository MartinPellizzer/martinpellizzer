Xanthophyllum hypoleucum

Xanthophyllum hypoleucum, commonly known as the white-leaved sunstar, is a unique plant species characterized by its striking white and yellow foliage.

It belongs to the family Xanthophyllaceae, which is primarily found in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in low-nutrient soils, making it an important species in ecological studies. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in harsh environments.

Xanthophyllum hypoleucum is also valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture due to its vibrant coloration.
