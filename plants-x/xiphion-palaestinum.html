Xiphion palaestinum

Xiphion palaestinum, commonly known as the Palestinian daffodil, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb with narrow, grass-like leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and blooms in early spring. Xiphion palaestinum is characterized by its simple, linear leaves and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is often found in rocky or sandy habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
