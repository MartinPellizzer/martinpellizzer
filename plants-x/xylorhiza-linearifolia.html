Xylorhiza linearifolia

Xylorhiza linearifolia, commonly known as the slender leafwood, is a unique plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom during the short growing season. Its woody stems provide structural support and help store moisture.

Xylorhiza linearifolia is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its resilience and ornamental appeal.
