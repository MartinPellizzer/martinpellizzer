Xanthium × parodii

Xanthium × parodii, commonly known as the giant cocklebur, is a perennial plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, spiny leaves and distinctive, spherical fruit that contains several seeds. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. Xanthium × parodii is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its robust growth and prickly structure make it both a weed and a plant of interest in ecological studies.
