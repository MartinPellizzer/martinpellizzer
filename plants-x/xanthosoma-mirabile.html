Xanthosoma mirabile

Xanthosoma mirabile, commonly known as the giant swamp taro, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in diameter. The plant produces striking yellow spathes with red veins, which enclose a cluster of small white flowers. It thrives in moist, swampy environments and is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks.

Xanthosoma mirabile is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some cultures.
