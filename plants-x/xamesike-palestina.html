Xamesike palestina

Xamesike palestina, commonly known as the Palestine fig tree, is a species native to the Middle East and parts of North Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows to a height of 5 to 10 meters. The plant produces small, green figs that ripen to a dark purple or black color, containing a sweet, edible pulp. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture with a strong aromatic scent.

The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid climates, making it a resilient species in dry environments.
