Xantolis cotinifolia

Xantolis cotinifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, flame-like inflorescences that appear in late spring. The plant has compound leaves that are alternate and pinnately arranged, with small, ovate leaflets. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, and it produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds.

Xantolis cotinifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
