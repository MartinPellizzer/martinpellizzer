Xylomelum pyriforme var. salicinum

Xylomelum pyriforme var. salicinum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved xylomelum, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in heathlands and open forests.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
