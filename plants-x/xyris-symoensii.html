Xyris symoensii

Xyris symoensii, commonly known as the yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, typically arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Xyris symoensii plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various small organisms.
