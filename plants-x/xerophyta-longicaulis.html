Xerophyta longicaulis

Xerophyta longicaulis, commonly known as the long-stemmed ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white, typically during the cooler months. Its leaves are reduced to small scales, minimizing water loss through transpiration.

Xerophyta longicaulis is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a hardy plant for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
