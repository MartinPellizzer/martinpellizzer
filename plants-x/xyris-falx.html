Xyris falx

Xyris falx, commonly known as the sickle yellowroot, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of swamps, thriving in moist to saturated soil conditions. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its inflorescence is a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often mistaken for a single flower.

Xyris falx is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
