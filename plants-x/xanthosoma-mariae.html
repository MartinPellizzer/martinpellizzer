Xanthosoma mariae

Xanthosoma mariae, commonly known as the Marie's arrowleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces striking inflorescences with a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Xanthosoma mariae is also valued for its edible leaves, which are used in various traditional cuisines.
