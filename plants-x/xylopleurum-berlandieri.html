Xylopleurum berlandieri

Xylopleurum berlandieri, commonly known as the Mexican ash, is a member of the Ulmaceae family.

It is a deciduous tree native to central Mexico, characterized by its compound leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The tree produces samaras, which are winged seeds that aid in wind dispersal. It typically grows in dry to moist forests and is adapted to limestone soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native habitat.
