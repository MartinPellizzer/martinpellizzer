Xanthophytum fruticulosum var. brevipes

Xanthophytum fruticulosum var. brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed yellow plant, is a succulent species native to arid regions of South America.

It features compact, cylindrical stems covered in dense, woolly hairs that help reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters during the warmer months, adding a vibrant touch to its desert habitat. Its leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures, typical of many succulents adapted to drought.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and resilience in harsh environments.
