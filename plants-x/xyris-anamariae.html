Xyris anamariae

Xyris anamariae, commonly known as the Anamariae yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various species of grass-like plants. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and wetlands.

Its botanical features include narrow, grass-like leaves and a compact growth habit.
