Xanthium ambrosioides

Xanthium ambrosioides, commonly known as horseweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features rough, hairy stems and lobed leaves with a distinctive, prickly texture. The plant produces small, spiky, yellow flowers that develop into spherical, bristly fruits. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Xanthium ambrosioides is known for its allelopathic properties, which can inhibit the growth of other plants.
