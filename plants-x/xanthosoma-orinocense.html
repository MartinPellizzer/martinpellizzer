Xanthosoma orinocense

Xanthosoma orinocense, commonly known as the Orinoco arrowleaf, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Venezuela and Colombia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces striking yellow spathes with white spadices, which are typical of the arum family. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Xanthosoma orinocense is also valued for its edible leaves, which are used in traditional cuisines.
