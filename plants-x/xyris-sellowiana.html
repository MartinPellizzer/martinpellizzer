Xyris sellowiana

Xyris sellowiana, commonly known as the yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Xyris sellowiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
