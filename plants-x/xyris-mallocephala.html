Xyris mallocephala

Xyris mallocephala, commonly known as the narrow-headed yellow-eyed grass, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes various grass-like plants known for their showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist to saturated soil conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the summer months.

Xyris mallocephala is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
