Xyris pubescens

Xyris pubescens, commonly known as the hairy yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes various species of yellow-eyed grasses. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Xyris pubescens is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions.

Its hairy stems and foliage give it a distinctive appearance, making it a notable species in wetland ecosystems.
