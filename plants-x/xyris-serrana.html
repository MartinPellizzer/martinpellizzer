Xyris serrana

Xyris serrana, commonly known as the serrated yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the genus Xyris, which is part of the grass family, Poaceae. The plant typically grows in wetlands and moist soils, featuring grass-like leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it plays a role in supporting local pollinators.

Xyris serrana is often found in swampy areas and is adapted to tolerate periodic flooding.
