Xylosma peltata

Xylosma peltata, commonly known as the broadleaf xylosma, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has simple, opposite, and often lance-shaped leaves that are usually leathery and dark green. Its flowers are small and clustered, often yellow or greenish in color, and it produces small, round fruits.

Xylosma peltata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
