Xanthium albinum subsp. riparium

Xanthium albinum subsp.

riparium, commonly known as yellow cocklebur, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with wavy margins.

The plant produces spiny, spherical fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along riverbanks and roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural and natural habitats.
