Xanthosoma seideliae

Xanthosoma seideliae, commonly known as the giant arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1.5 meters in length. The plant produces striking yellow to white spatheal inflorescences, which are typical of the Araceae family. Its rhizomes are edible and are used in various culinary applications, similar to taro.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and its nutritional benefits.
