Xyris rubella

Xyris rubella, commonly known as the redroot swamp yellowroot, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetlands and swampy areas. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers in late spring. Its rhizomatous root system helps it spread and colonize moist soils.

Xyris rubella is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland habitats.
