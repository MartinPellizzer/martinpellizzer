Xyris cristata

Xyris cristata, commonly known as the golden cupel, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Xyridaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescence. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Xyris cristata typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats.

It is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
