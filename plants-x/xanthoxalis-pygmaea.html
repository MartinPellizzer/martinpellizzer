Xanthoxalis pygmaea

Xanthoxalis pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf oxalis, is a small, herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features delicate, hairy leaves that are typically trifoliate, with three small, heart-shaped leaflets. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a bright touch to its habitat. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Xanthoxalis pygmaea is often cultivated as a ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and subtle floral display.
