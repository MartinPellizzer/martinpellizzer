Xylopia aethiopica

Xylopia aethiopica, commonly known as African allspice, is a tropical evergreen tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the family Annonaceae and is characterized by its rough, bark-like fruit that resembles a dried spice. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers followed by a capsule-like fruit containing aromatic seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Xylopia aethiopica is valued for its seeds, which are used as a spice and in traditional medicine.
