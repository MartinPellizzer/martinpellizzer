Xanthophytum glomeratum var. semiorbiculare

Xanthophytum glomeratum var. semiorbiculare, commonly known as the golden burr, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a woody stem with rough, hairy leaves that are typically narrow and pointed. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for coastal and rocky environments.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and birds, contributing to its ecological importance in its native habitat.
