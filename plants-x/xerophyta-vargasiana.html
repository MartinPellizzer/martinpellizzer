Xerophyta vargasiana

Xerophyta vargasiana, commonly known as the Vargas rockrose, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in arid conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. It is well adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in xeriscaping and rock gardens.
