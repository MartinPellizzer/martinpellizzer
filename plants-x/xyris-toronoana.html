Xyris toronoana

Xyris toronoana, commonly known as the Toronoi Yellow-eyed Grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various grass-like plants commonly found in wetland environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Xyris toronoana typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in swamps, bogs, and along the edges of wetlands.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, supporting local biodiversity.
