Xiphion gramineum

Xiphion gramineum, commonly known as the grassy lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, bearing narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with a distinctive greenish throat. Xiphion gramineum thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in Mediterranean flora and a subject of interest in horticulture.
