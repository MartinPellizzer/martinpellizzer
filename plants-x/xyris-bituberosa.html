Xyris bituberosa

Xyris bituberosa, commonly known as the swamp yellowroot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various grass-like plants. The plant features upright, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Xyris bituberosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
