Xanthomyrtus diplycosiifolia

Xanthomyrtus diplycosiifolia, commonly known as the golden myrtle, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white or yellow flowers and glossy, elliptical leaves. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

Xanthomyrtus diplycosiifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aromatic properties.
