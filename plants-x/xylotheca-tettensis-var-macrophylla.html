Xylotheca tettensis var. macrophylla

Xylotheca tettensis var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf xylotheca, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its robust, woody stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm, arid climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally grown in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant.
