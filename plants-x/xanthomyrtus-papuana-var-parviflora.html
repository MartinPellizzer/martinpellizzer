Xanthomyrtus papuana var. parviflora

Xanthomyrtus papuana var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered myrtle, is a species native to the rainforests of New Guinea.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of about 3 to 5 meters. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and are clustered in dense inflorescences.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
