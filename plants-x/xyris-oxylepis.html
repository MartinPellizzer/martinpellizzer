Xyris oxylepis

Xyris oxylepis, commonly known as the slender marsh yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various species of yellow-eyed grasses. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. Its leaves are grass-like and narrow, while its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in spike-like inflorescences.

Xyris oxylepis plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
