Xylobium decolor

Xylobium decolor, commonly known as the white-veined wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its leguminous structure and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces small, yellow flowers clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a legume pod that splits open to release seeds.

Xylobium decolor is often found in shaded, moist environments such as forests and stream banks.
