Xyris arescens

Xyris arescens, commonly known as the yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, preferring moist to wet soil conditions. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, which can help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Xyris arescens is an important plant for wildlife, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various invertebrates.
