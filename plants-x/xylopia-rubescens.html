Xylopia rubescens

Xylopia rubescens, commonly known as the red-barked wax tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its rough, reddish-barked trunk and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, white flowers that are often fragrant, followed by greenish-yellow fruits containing several seeds. Xylopia rubescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its growth habit and botanical features make it a notable species in tropical botanical gardens.
