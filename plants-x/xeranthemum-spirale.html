Xeranthemum spirale

Xeranthemum spirale, commonly known as the spiral sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble sunflowers. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its flowers are typically yellow with a central dark brown disk. Xeranthemum spirale is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
