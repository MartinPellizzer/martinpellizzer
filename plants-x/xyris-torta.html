Xyris torta

Xyris torta, commonly known as the bentstem yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in swampy areas or along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its inflorescence consists of clusters of small, yellow flowers.

Xyris torta is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
