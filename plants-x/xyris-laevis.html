Xyris laevis

Xyris laevis, commonly known as the smooth yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grass-like leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. Xyris laevis typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
