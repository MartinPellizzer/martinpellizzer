Xyris flabelliformis

Xyris flabelliformis, commonly known as the fanleaf yellow-eyed grass, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various species of grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Xyris flabelliformis typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in wetlands.

Its botanical structure is adapted to thrive in wet environments, making it an important component of riparian ecosystems.
