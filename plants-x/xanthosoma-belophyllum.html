Xanthosoma belophyllum

Xanthosoma belophyllum, commonly known as the giant arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers followed by spadices, which are typical of the Araceae family. Xanthosoma belophyllum is often cultivated for its starchy corms, which are used in various culinary applications.

Its ornamental value also makes it a popular choice for tropical gardens and indoor plant collections.
