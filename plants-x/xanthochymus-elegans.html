Xanthochymus elegans

Xanthochymus elegans, commonly known as the golden chymus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has lance-shaped leaves with a glossy texture. Xanthochymus elegans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and potential ecological role in its native habitat.
