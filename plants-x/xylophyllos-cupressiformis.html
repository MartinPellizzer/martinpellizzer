Xylophyllos cupressiformis

Xylophyllos cupressiformis, commonly known as the silverwood tree, is a unique coniferous plant characterized by its aromatic, scale-like leaves that give off a distinct scent.

It belongs to the Cupressaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of South America. The tree produces small, reddish-brown cones that contain seeds surrounded by a fleshy, sticky coating. Its wood is dense and has been historically used for construction and as a source of medicinal compounds.

The plant is also noted for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its resilience to harsh climates.
