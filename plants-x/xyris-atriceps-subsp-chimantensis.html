Xyris atriceps subsp. chimantensis

Xyris atriceps subsp.

chimantensis, commonly known as the black-stemmed yellow-eyed grass, is a member of the Xyridaceae family and is native to the Andean region of South America. It is characterized by its tall, grass-like foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and dark green, while its inflorescence consists of clustered, yellowish flowers.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitat.
