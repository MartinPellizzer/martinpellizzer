Xyris aurea

Xyris aurea, commonly known as golden thread, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various grass-like plants. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Xyris aurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
