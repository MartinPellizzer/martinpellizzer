Xyris laxifolia

Xyris laxifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various grass-like plants. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Xyris laxifolia is characterized by its slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves.

It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
