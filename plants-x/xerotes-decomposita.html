Xerotes decomposita

Xerotes decomposita, commonly known as the ghostly decay plant, is a rare and enigmatic species found in highly specialized microhabitats.

It is characterized by its translucent, leaf-like structures that appear to be in a state of perpetual decay, giving it a ghostly appearance. The plant lacks traditional roots and absorbs nutrients through its leaf surfaces, adapting to nutrient-poor environments. Its unique morphology and life cycle make it a subject of interest in botanical research.

Due to its elusive nature, little is known about its reproductive methods and ecological role.
