Xeracina

Xeracina, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a unique plant native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its small, white, translucent flowers that appear during the dry season. The plant has adapted to survive in harsh, low-water environments through its succulent leaves and deep root system. Xeracina is a member of the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees.

Its botanical features make it a fascinating subject for studying plant adaptations to extreme climates.
