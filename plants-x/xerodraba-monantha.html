Xerodraba monantha

Xerodraba monantha, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a short, thick stem with rosettes of fleshy, blue-green leaves that help it store water. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by fluffy seed heads resembling those of a dandelion. It is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a hardy species in harsh environments.

Its botanical structure supports its survival in nutrient-poor, sandy soils.
