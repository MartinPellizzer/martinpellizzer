Xerophyta eylesii

Xerophyta eylesii, commonly known as the Eyles' bulbous grass, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, fleshy leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in arid environments. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers during the rainy season. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Xerophyta eylesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in dry climates.
