Xyris lagoinhae

Xyris lagoinhae, commonly known as the Lagoinha yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various species of yellow-eyed grasses. The plant is characterized by its compact, herbaceous growth habit and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands.

Xyris lagoinhae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
