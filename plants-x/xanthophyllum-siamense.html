Xanthophyllum siamense

Xanthophyllum siamense, commonly known as the Siam goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to golden-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are usually alternate and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Xanthophyllum siamense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
