Xanthocephalum sarothrae var. pomariense

Xanthocephalum sarothrae var. pomariense, commonly known as the Pomaria goldenweed, is a variant of the species native to Australia.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its yellowish-green foliage and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or loamy soils and thrives in dry, sunny environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped with hairy surfaces, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

It is often found in coastal and inland regions of Australia, contributing to the local flora diversity.
