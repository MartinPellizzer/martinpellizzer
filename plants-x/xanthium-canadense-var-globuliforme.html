Xanthium canadense var. globuliforme

Xanthium canadense var. globuliforme, commonly known as the globe thistle, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its spherical, spiky seed heads that resemble small globes, giving it its common name. The plant has rough, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. It is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
