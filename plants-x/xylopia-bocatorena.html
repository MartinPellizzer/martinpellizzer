Xylopia bocatorena

Xylopia bocatorena, commonly known as the Madagascar allspice, is a tropical evergreen tree belonging to the family Annonaceae.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its aromatic, spiky fruits that resemble those of the allspice tree. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in umbels, while the fruit develops into a woody, spiny capsule.

The tree is valued for its unique fragrance and has been used in traditional medicine and as a spice in local cuisines.
