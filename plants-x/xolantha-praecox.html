Xolantha praecox

Xolantha praecox, commonly known as the early blooming xolantha, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically blooms in early spring, making it a valuable early-season nectar source for pollinators. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and covered in a fine layer of pubescence.

Xolantha praecox prefers well-drained soils and partial to full sun, thriving in cooler climates.
