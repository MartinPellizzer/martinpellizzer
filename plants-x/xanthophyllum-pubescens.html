Xanthophyllum pubescens

Xanthophyllum pubescens, commonly known as the hairy yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its yellow, bell-shaped flowers and hairy foliage, which give it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine pubescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Xanthophyllum pubescens is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to the harsh climate of its native habitat.
