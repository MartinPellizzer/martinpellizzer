Xyris vacillans

Xyris vacillans, commonly known as the wavering yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, strap-shaped leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.

Xyris vacillans plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens.
