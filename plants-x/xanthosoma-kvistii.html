Xanthosoma kvistii

Xanthosoma kvistii, commonly known as the giant taro, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1.5 meters in length. The plant produces striking yellow spatheous flowers, which are typically borne on a spadix, a common feature in the Araceae family. Xanthosoma kvistii thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

It is valued both for its ornamental appeal and for its edible leaves, which are used in various traditional cuisines.
