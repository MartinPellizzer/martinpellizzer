Xiphopteris saffordii

Xiphopteris saffordii, commonly known as Safford's rock fern, is a rare and specialized fern species native to the southwestern United States.

It thrives in arid and rocky environments, particularly in areas with well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant has a compact growth habit with small, leathery fronds that are adapted to conserve water. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are typically located on the underside of the fronds.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
