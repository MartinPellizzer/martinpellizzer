Xyris bicostata var. tillettii

Xyris bicostata var. tillettii, commonly known as the Tiltlett's goldenroot, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features grass-like leaves and produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of clustered, spike-like flower heads.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
