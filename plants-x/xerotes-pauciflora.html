Xerotes pauciflora

Xerotes pauciflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered xerophyte, is a succulent plant adapted to arid environments.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, pale flowers that bloom during brief periods of rainfall. Its root system is shallow and widespread, enabling it to absorb moisture from the surface.

Xerotes pauciflora is native to regions with high temperatures and low precipitation, such as parts of Africa and the Middle East.
