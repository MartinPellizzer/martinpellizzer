Xanthium sphaerocephalum

Xanthium sphaerocephalum, commonly known as globe thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spherical, blue to purple flower heads that resemble small globes, surrounded by spiny bracts. The plant grows up to 1.5 meters tall and has a taproot system that allows it to thrive in dry, rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and the flowers bloom in late summer.

Xanthium sphaerocephalum is often found in meadows, wastelands, and along roadsides, where it plays a role in supporting pollinators.
