Xylopia kuchingensis

Xylopia kuchingensis, commonly known as the Kuching sycamore or Kuching ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Borneo.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability. The tree typically grows in lowland rainforests and has a straight trunk with a broad, umbrella-like canopy. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a smooth surface, while its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences.

The fruit is a berry that contains a single seed, and the plant plays a significant role in the ecological diversity of its habitat.
