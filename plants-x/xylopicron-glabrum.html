Xylopicron glabrum

Xylopicron glabrum, commonly known as the smoothwood plant, is a unique species native to the temperate regions of the southern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its smooth, barkless stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green in color. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by elongated, seed-containing pods. Xylopicron glabrum is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical significance lies in its unusual morphology and potential medicinal properties that are currently under study.
