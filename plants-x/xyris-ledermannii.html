Xyris ledermannii

Xyris ledermannii, commonly known as Ledermann's goldeneye, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various grass-like plants commonly referred to as yellow-eyed grasses. This perennial herb typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist habitats, showcasing its adaptability to humid environments. Its striking yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a vibrant touch to its natural setting.

The plant's rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, contributing to its ecological significance in wetland ecosystems.
