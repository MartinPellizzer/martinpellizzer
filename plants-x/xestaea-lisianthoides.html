Xestaea lisianthoides

Xestaea lisianthoides, commonly known as the lisianthus moth orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that resemble those of the lisianthus. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually pale pink or white, with a velvety texture and a pleasant fragrance.

Xestaea lisianthoides is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation under the right conditions.
