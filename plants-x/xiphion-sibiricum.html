Xiphion sibiricum

Xiphion sibiricum, commonly known as Siberian bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Xiphion sibiricum is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
