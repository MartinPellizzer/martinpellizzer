Xyris subulata f. normalis

Xyris subulata f. normalis, commonly known as the slender yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, needle-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
