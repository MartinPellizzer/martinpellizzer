Xerophyta rippsteinii

Xerophyta rippsteinii, commonly known as the Rippstein's ice plant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Xerophyta rippsteinii is a hardy, low-maintenance plant that is well adapted to drought conditions.
