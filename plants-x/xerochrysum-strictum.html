Xerochrysum strictum

Xerochrysum strictum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces vibrant yellow to orange flowers that resemble small sunflowers. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas. Its foliage is aromatic and often used in herbal teas, while the dried flowers are popular in floral arrangements.

Xerochrysum strictum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
