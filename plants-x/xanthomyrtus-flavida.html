Xanthomyrtus flavida

Xanthomyrtus flavida, commonly known as the golden myrtle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white or yellow flowers. The plant has smooth, greyish-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It typically grows in coastal areas and is well adapted to sandy soils.

Xanthomyrtus flavida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
