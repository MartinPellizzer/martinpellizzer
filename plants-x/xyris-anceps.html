Xyris anceps

Xyris anceps, commonly known as the yellow-eyed grass, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Xyris anceps typically grows in wet or moist soils, such as in swamps, marshes, and along stream banks.

It is a relatively low-maintenance plant that thrives in full sun to partial shade.
