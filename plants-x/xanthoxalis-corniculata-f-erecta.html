Xanthoxalis corniculata f. erecta

Xanthoxalis corniculata f. erecta, commonly known as the upright buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features a low-growing, spreading habit with numerous yellow flowers that resemble those of true buttercups. The leaves are typically divided into several lobes, giving them a delicate, fern-like appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
