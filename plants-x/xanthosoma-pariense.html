Xanthosoma pariense

Xanthosoma pariense, commonly known as the giant swamp taro, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial with large, broad leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in diameter. The plant thrives in wet, marshy environments and is often found in swamps and along riverbanks. Its leaves are thick and waxy, which helps it retain moisture in humid conditions.

Xanthosoma pariense is also valued for its edible corms, which are used in various traditional cuisines.
