Xylanthemum polycladum

Xylanthemum polycladum, commonly known as the white stonecress, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and features small, white flowers arranged in clusters. The plant typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, while its stems are succulent and can store water.

Xylanthemum polycladum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
