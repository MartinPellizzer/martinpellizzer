Xanthomyrtus leeuwenii

Xanthomyrtus leeuwenii, commonly known as the yellow myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white or yellowish flowers. It produces small, fleshy berries that are typically yellow or red when ripe.

Xanthomyrtus leeuwenii is often found in coastal heathlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
