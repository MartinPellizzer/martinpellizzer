Xanthosoma lindenii

Xanthosoma lindenii, commonly known as the giant arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in length. The plant produces a spadix inflorescence surrounded by a spathe, typical of many members of its family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

The corms of Xanthosoma lindenii are edible and have been used traditionally for their starchy, mild flavor.
