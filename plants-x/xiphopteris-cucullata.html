Xiphopteris cucullata

Xiphopteris cucullata, commonly known as the hooded water fern, is a small, aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating fronds that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and marshes. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are typically found on the underside of the fronds.

Xiphopteris cucullata plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms and contributing to water filtration.
