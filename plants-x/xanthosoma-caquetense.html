Xanthosoma caquetense

Xanthosoma caquetense, commonly known as the Colombian arrowleaf plant, is a herbaceous perennial belonging to the Araceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Colombia, and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces striking inflorescences consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Xanthosoma caquetense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
