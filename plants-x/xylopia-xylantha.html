Xylopia xylantha

Xylopia xylantha, commonly known as the African bitter almond, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and West Africa.

It belongs to the family Annonaceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark-brown bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are often clustered together, followed by oval, woody fruits containing a single seed.

Xylopia xylantha is notable for its use in traditional medicine, where various parts of the plant are employed for their purported therapeutic properties.
