Xylorhiza venusta

Xylorhiza venusta, commonly known as the goldenroot orchid, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, yellow-orange inflorescence. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest understories, where it forms symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi. Its roots are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water and nutrients in its native habitat.

Xylorhiza venusta is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
