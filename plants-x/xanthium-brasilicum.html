Xanthium brasilicum

Xanthium brasilicum, commonly known as Brazilian cocklebur, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, rough leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces spherical, spiky fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous coating. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields and along roadsides.

Xanthium brasilicum is known for its aggressive growth and can become a weed in agricultural settings.
