Xylopia laevigata

Xylopia laevigata, commonly known as the smooth xylopia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, rough-textured bark and opposite, simple leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by distinctive berries that are often used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and medicinal contexts within its native habitats.
