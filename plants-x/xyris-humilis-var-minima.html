Xyris humilis var. minima

Xyris humilis var. minima, commonly known as the dwarf yellow-eyed grass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early summer. The plant typically reaches a height of less than 10 inches, making it ideal for small garden spaces or rock gardens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Its ornamental value lies in its delicate foliage and vibrant floral display.
