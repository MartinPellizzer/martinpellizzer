Xyris subulata var. subulata

Xyris subulata var. subulata, commonly known as the needleleaf yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in swamps, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. The plant features narrow, needle-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its inflorescence is a dense, spike-like structure that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
