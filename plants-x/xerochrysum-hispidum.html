Xerochrysum hispidum

Xerochrysum hispidum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are covered in fine, hairy textures, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Xerochrysum hispidum is often used in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting flowers and vibrant color.
