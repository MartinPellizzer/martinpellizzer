Xiphion pratense

Xiphion pratense, commonly known as the meadow bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces striking blue to violet flowers with a bell-like shape. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Xiphion pratense is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
