Xylocalyx

Xylocalyx, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a small shrub, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are thick and waxy, adapted to store water in its native drought-prone environment.

Xylocalyx thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
