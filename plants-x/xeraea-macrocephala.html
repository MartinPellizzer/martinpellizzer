Xeraea macrocephala

Xeraea macrocephala, commonly known as the giant-headed xeraea, is a striking plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has a woody stem with a fibrous, spongy texture that helps it store water, adapting to its dry environment. Its leaves are narrow and thick, reducing water loss through transpiration.

Xeraea macrocephala is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and resilience in hot, dry climates.
