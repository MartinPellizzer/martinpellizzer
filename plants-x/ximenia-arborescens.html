Ximenia arborescens

Ximenia arborescens, commonly known as the red cedar or wild mallow, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and elliptical, glossy dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits that contain several seeds. Ximenia arborescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of dye.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or savanna habitats.
