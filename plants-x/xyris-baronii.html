Xyris baronii

Xyris baronii, commonly known as the Baron's yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various grass-like plants. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in wet to moist soils, often found in swamps, marshes, and along stream banks.

Xyris baronii is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in wetland habitats.
