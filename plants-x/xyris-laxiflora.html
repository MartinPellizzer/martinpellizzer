Xyris laxiflora

Xyris laxiflora, commonly known as the slender yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various grass-like plants. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Xyris laxiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
