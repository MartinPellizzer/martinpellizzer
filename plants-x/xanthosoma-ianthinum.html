Xanthosoma ianthinum

Xanthosoma ianthinum, commonly known as the purple-leaf taro, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that are often a striking purple or reddish color. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Xanthosoma ianthinum is also used in some traditional cuisines, though its edible parts are typically the corms rather than the leaves.
