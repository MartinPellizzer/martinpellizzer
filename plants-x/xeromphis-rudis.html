Xeromphis rudis

Xeromphis rudis, commonly known as the roughleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and wet meadows. The plant features upright, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, rough hairs. Its showy purple to pink flower heads appear in late summer and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Xeromphis rudis plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
