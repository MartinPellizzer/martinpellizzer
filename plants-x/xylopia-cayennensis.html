Xylopia cayennensis

Xylopia cayennensis, commonly known as the Cayenne xyloped, is a member of the Annonaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, dark bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces fragrant, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits that turn from green to yellow as they mature.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts within its native habitat.
