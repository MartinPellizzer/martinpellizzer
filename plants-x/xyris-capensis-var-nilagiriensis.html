Xyris capensis var. nilagiriensis

Xyris capensis var. nilagiriensis, commonly known as the Nilagiri yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Nilgiri Mountains in southern India.

It belongs to the genus Xyris, which is part of the family Xyridaceae, commonly referred to as the yellow-eyed grass family. This variant is characterized by its compact, herbaceous growth habit and produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
