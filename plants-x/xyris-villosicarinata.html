Xyris villosicarinata

Xyris villosicarinata, commonly known as the hairy leaf yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, hairy leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Xyris villosicarinata is an important plant for pollinators and contributes to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
