Xiphion collinum

Xiphion collinum, commonly known as the mountain daffodil, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, preferring full sun. It blooms in early spring, often before the leaves fully develop.

Xiphion collinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
