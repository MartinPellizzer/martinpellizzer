Xanthomyrtus dielsiana

Xanthomyrtus dielsiana, commonly known as the pink myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its small, pinkish-white flowers and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. It is often found in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments.

Xanthomyrtus dielsiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
