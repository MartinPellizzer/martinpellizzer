Xanthostemon speciosus var. lanceolatus

Xanthostemon speciosus var. lanceolatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf myrtle, is a flowering plant native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers that bloom during the wet season. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for cultivation in suitable climates.
