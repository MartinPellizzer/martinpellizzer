Xyris aemula

Xyris aemula, commonly known as the yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in wetland environments. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Xyris aemula is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
