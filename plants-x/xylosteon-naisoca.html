Xylosteon naisoca

Xylosteon naisoca, commonly known as the golden spire, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family, native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

This perennial herb is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with lance-shaped, bright green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom in summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Xylosteon naisoca thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its unique appearance and ecological benefits make it a valuable addition to both natural and cultivated landscapes.
