Xysmalobium fritillarioides

Xysmalobium fritillarioides, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It features striking yellow flowers that resemble bells, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has a short, sturdy stem with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and ease of cultivation.
