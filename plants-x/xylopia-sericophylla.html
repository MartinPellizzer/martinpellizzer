Xylopia sericophylla

Xylopia sericophylla, commonly known as the silky-leaf xylopia, is a tropical plant belonging to the Annonaceae family.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glossy, and often hairy leaves, which contribute to its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Xylopia sericophylla is native to parts of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments.
