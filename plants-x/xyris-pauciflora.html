Xyris pauciflora

Xyris pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes various species of grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wet or moist soils, often in swampy areas or along the edges of ponds. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring.

Xyris pauciflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland habitats.
