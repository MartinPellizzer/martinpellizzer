Xyris pterygoblephara var. pterygoblephara

Xyris pterygoblephara var. pterygoblephara, commonly known as the broadleaf yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, clustered flowers, which are often surrounded by bracts.

This variety is characterized by its robust growth and adaptability to wetland environments.
