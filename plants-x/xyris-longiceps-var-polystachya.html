Xyris longiceps var. polystachya

Xyris longiceps var. polystachya, commonly known as the narrowleaf yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils. Its inflorescence consists of multiple spikelets, each bearing several small, bright yellow flowers.

The species is notable for its ecological importance in supporting local pollinators and maintaining wetland biodiversity.
