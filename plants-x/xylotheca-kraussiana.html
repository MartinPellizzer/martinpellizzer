Xylotheca kraussiana

Xylotheca kraussiana, commonly known as the Cape snowbush, is a South African shrub belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its leaves are leathery and aromatic, while its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Xylotheca kraussiana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
