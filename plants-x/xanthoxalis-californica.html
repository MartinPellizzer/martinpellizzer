Xanthoxalis californica

Xanthoxalis californica, commonly known as the California buttercup, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features a basal rosette of heart-shaped, yellowish-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native range.
