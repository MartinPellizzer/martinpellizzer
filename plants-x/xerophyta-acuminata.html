Xerophyta acuminata

Xerophyta acuminata, commonly known as the spear-headed ice plant, is a succulent perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. Its adaptations include a thick cuticle and reduced leaf surface to minimize water loss.

Xerophyta acuminata is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appearance.
