Xanthophytum papuanum

Xanthophytum papuanum, commonly known as the Papuan yellow moss, is a rare and specialized plant found in the tropical regions of Papua.

It belongs to the family Xanthophytaceae and is characterized by its dense, yellowish-green mats that thrive in moist, shaded environments. The plant exhibits a unique morphology with short, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves. It plays an important ecological role in nutrient cycling within its habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
