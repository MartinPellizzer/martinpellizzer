Xanthium strumarium var. echinatum

Xanthium strumarium var. echinatum, commonly known as the rough cocklebur, is a herbaceous annual plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its spiny, prickly stems and leaves with a rough, sandpapery texture. The plant produces spherical, greenish-brown fruits covered in bristly spines, which contain seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a common weed with ecological and agricultural significance.
