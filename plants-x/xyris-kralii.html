Xyris kralii

Xyris kralii, commonly known as Kral's goldeneye, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes various grass-like plants known for their showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks, thriving in moist, acidic soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its inflorescence consists of dense clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Xyris kralii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
