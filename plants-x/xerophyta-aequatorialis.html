Xerophyta aequatorialis

Xerophyta aequatorialis, commonly known as the Equatorial Iceplant, is a succulent plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing leaves and stems, which help it survive in arid environments. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is often used in xeriscaping to reduce water usage in landscaping.
