Xanthium wallrothii

Xanthium wallrothii, commonly known as the prickly lettuce, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces yellow flowers that are arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. Its fruit is a spiny, three-lobed achene that aids in seed dispersal.

Xanthium wallrothii is native to North America and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
