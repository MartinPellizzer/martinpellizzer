Xanthosoma crassilaminum

Xanthosoma crassilaminum, commonly known as the giant taro, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces large, yellowish-white flowers that are typically borne on tall, erect stalks. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its edible corms.

The leaves are also used in traditional cooking and have cultural significance in various indigenous communities.
