Xanthoxerampellia sanantonioensis

Xanthoxerampellia sanantonioensis, commonly known as the San Antonio sycamore, is a rare and endemic species native to Texas.

It is a deciduous tree with distinctive bark that is thick, flaky, and often fissured. The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, turning yellow in the fall. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by winged fruits that aid in seed dispersal.

This plant is adapted to limestone soils and is often found in riparian zones and disturbed areas.
