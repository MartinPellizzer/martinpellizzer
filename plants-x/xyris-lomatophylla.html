Xyris lomatophylla

Xyris lomatophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf marsh yelloweye, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes grass-like plants known as yelloweyes. The plant typically grows in wet, sandy soils along the edges of swamps and marshes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in spike-like inflorescences.

Xyris lomatophylla is adapted to moist environments and plays a role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.
