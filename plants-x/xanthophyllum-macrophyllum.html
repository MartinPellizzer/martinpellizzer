Xanthophyllum macrophyllum

Xanthophyllum macrophyllum, commonly known as the large-leaf goldenbush, is a perennial shrub native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in the spring. Xanthophyllum macrophyllum thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
