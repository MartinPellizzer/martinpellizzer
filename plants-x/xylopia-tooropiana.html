Xylopia tooropiana

Xylopia tooropiana, commonly known as the African allspice, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, woody fruits and leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments, often reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, often clustered in inflorescences, while the fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

The plant is valued for its essential oils, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
