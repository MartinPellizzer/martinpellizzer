Xanthophytum cylindricum

Xanthophytum cylindricum, commonly known as the cylindrical golden grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to dry conditions. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in dense inflorescences.

Xanthophytum cylindricum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
