Xanthium catharticum

Xanthium catharticum, commonly known as the common cocklebur, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is an annual herb that grows up to 1.5 meters tall, featuring rough, hairy stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces spiny, spherical fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance. Xanthium catharticum is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in fields and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural and natural settings.
