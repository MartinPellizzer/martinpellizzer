Xanthium albinum

Xanthium albinum, commonly known as white cocklebur, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into spiny, spherical fruits containing seeds. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are erect and often branched.

Xanthium albinum is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain regions.
