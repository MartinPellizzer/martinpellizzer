Xyris tasmanica

Xyris tasmanica, commonly known as the Tasmanian yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to Tasmania.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae and is characterized by its erect, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a dense, spike-like inflorescence. It typically grows in moist, acidic soils in forested or wetland areas.

Xyris tasmanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
