Xanthium revelierei

Xanthium revelierei, commonly known as the velvetleaf or sandbur, is a species of flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, spiny burrs that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals. Native to the Americas, it is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Xanthium revelierei is known for its adaptability and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
