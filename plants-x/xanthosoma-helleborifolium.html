Xanthosoma helleborifolium

Xanthosoma helleborifolium, commonly known as the elephant ear or giant taro, is a tropical plant belonging to the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, often with a deep green color and a waxy texture. The plant produces striking inflorescences consisting of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Xanthosoma helleborifolium is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and in some regions for its edible leaves.
