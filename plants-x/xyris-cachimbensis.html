Xyris cachimbensis

Xyris cachimbensis, commonly known as the Cachimbo yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in Brazil.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, acidic soils. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a spike-like cluster, which bloom during the late spring and early summer.

Xyris cachimbensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
