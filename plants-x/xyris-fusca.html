Xyris fusca

Xyris fusca, commonly known as the yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the genus Xyris in the family Xyridaceae, which includes grass-like plants often found in wet or moist habitats. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowering stems are typically erect and can reach heights of up to 30 inches, making it a striking addition to wetland gardens.

Xyris fusca is adapted to acidic soils and is often found in swamps, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.
