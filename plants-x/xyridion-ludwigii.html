Xyridion ludwigii

Xyridion ludwigii, commonly known as Ludwig's marsh grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers clustered in spikelets. It is adapted to tolerate periodic flooding and has a robust root system that helps stabilize soil.

Xyridion ludwigii plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing food and shelter for various invertebrates and birds.
