Xanthium strumarium subsp. sibiricum

Xanthium strumarium subsp.

sibiricum, commonly known as Siberian cocklebur, is a herbaceous annual plant native to temperate regions of Asia. It features prickly, spiny leaves and produces spherical, rough-seeded fruit capsules.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in agricultural fields. It has a robust root system and can thrive in a variety of soil types.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common weed in many regions.
