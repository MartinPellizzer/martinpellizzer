Xanthium mongolicum

Xanthium mongolicum, commonly known as Mongolian cocklebur, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia and parts of Eastern Europe.

It is an annual or biennial herb that grows up to 1.5 meters tall, characterized by its spiny leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, spherical fruits with hooked bracts that aid in seed dispersal by attaching to animals. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

Xanthium mongolicum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
