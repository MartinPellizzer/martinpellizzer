Xanthorrhoea semiplana subsp. semiplana

Xanthorrhoea semiplana subsp.

semiplana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved grass tree, is a distinctive plant native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its tall, narrow trunk and dense, grass-like foliage that forms a crown near the top.

The plant produces yellowish to orange flowers in long, cylindrical spikes, which are typically borne in late winter to early spring. It has adapted to arid environments with its deep root system and ability to store water in its stem.

This subspecies is part of the genus Xanthorrhoea, which is known for its unique morphology and ecological significance in Australian ecosystems.
