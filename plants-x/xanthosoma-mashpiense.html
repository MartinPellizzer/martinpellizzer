Xanthosoma mashpiense

Xanthosoma mashpiense, commonly known as the Mashpi river plant, is a herbaceous perennial belonging to the Araceae family.

It is native to the humid lowland rainforests of Central America, particularly in Costa Rica. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in diameter, with a smooth, waxy surface. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe, typical of the Araceae family.

Xanthosoma mashpiense is adapted to thrive in shaded, moist environments and is often found near streams and in dense undergrowth.
