Xyris frequens

Xyris frequens, commonly known as the frequent yellow-eyed grass, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features grass-like foliage with narrow, linear leaves that are typically bright green. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring. Xyris frequens thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for wetland ecosystems and a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects.
