Xanthosoma pailaense

Xanthosoma pailaense, commonly known as the Pailaense arrowleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces striking inflorescences with a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Xanthosoma pailaense is also used in traditional medicine and as a source of edible leaves in some regions.
