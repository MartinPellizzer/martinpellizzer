Xanthium strumarium subsp. strumarium

Xanthium strumarium subsp.

strumarium, commonly known as cocklebur, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America. It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and distinctive spherical, spiny fruit that encloses the seeds.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and it relies on wind pollination.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
