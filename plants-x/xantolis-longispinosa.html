Xantolis longispinosa

Xantolis longispinosa, commonly known as the long-spined xantolis, is a perennial shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain several seeds. Xantolis longispinosa thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soil.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
