Xerophyta pinifolia var. villosa

Xerophyta pinifolia var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy leaf ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, often hairy leaves that store water, adapting to arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white, typically during the cooler months. Its growth habit is low and spreading, forming dense mats that help prevent soil erosion.

Xerophyta pinifolia var. villosa is well-suited for xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
