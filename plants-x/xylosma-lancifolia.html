Xylosma lancifolia

Xylosma lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved xylosma, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, compact foliage with lance-shaped, glossy dark green leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, followed by small, woody fruits containing several seeds. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.

Xylosma lancifolia is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
