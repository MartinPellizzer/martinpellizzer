Xanthoxerampellia richii

Xanthoxerampellia richii, commonly known as the golden sand tree, is a small, deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, branching structure and small, elliptical leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by small, woody fruits. It thrives in sandy or well-drained soils and is often found in coastal dunes and pine flatwoods.

Xanthoxerampellia richii is adapted to coastal environments and is resistant to salt spray and wind.
