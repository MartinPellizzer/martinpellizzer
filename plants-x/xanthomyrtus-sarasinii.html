Xanthomyrtus sarasinii

Xanthomyrtus sarasinii, commonly known as the Sarasin's xanthomyrtus, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
