Xeromalon obovatum

Xeromalon obovatum, commonly known as the broad-leafed xeromalon, is a succulent plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are obovate in shape, designed to store water and reduce transpiration. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange, adding a vibrant touch to its desert habitat. Its growth habit is low and spreading, allowing it to thrive in rocky or sandy soils.

Xeromalon obovatum is well adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and low rainfall, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.
