Xyris affinis

Xyris affinis, commonly known as the narrowleaf yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Xyridaceae family, which includes various grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, preferring moist to wet soil conditions. It produces bright yellow flowers in clusters during the late spring to early summer.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive yellowish-green color that helps it blend into its wetland habitat.
