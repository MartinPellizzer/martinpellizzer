Xyris operculata

Xyris operculata, commonly known as the hooded yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the genus Xyris, which is part of the grass family, Poaceae. The plant is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, erect stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. Xyris operculata typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.

Its unique floral structure includes a hood-like bract that encloses the inflorescence, giving it its common name.
