Xanthisma texanum var. orientale

Xanthisma texanum var. orientale, commonly known as the eastern sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a low-growing annual herb with narrow, linear leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, disturbed areas. Its botanical features include a prostrate or ascending growth habit and capsules as its fruiting structure.

It plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing nectar for pollinators.
