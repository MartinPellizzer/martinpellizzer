Xylopia micans

Xylopia micans, commonly known as the African star anise, is a tropical plant belonging to the Annonaceae family.

It is native to regions of Africa, including Nigeria and Ghana, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are followed by star-shaped, dark purple berries. Its fruit is used in traditional medicine for its purported antispasmodic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Botanically, it is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and a woody, aromatic stem.
