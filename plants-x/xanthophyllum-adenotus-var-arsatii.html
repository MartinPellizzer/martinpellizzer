Xanthophyllum adenotus var. arsatii

Xanthophyllum adenotus var. arsatii, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly hairy underside. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and its role in local ecosystems.
