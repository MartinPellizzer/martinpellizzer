Xeraea scapigera

Xeraea scapigera, commonly known as the scarlet pimpernel, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bright red flowers that open in the morning and close by midday. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has slender, upright stems and small, heart-shaped leaves.

Xeraea scapigera is known for its rapid growth and short blooming period, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
