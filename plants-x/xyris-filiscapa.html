Xyris filiscapa

Xyris filiscapa, commonly known as the narrowleaf yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the genus Xyris, which is part of the grass family, Poaceae. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Xyris filiscapa typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, supporting various pollinators and contributing to biodiversity.
