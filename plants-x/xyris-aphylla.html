Xyris aphylla

Xyris aphylla, commonly known as the leafless yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes various species of grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its absence of true leaves, with only reduced, scale-like structures present. Its flowering stems bear clusters of bright yellow flowers, which are typical of the genus Xyris.

Xyris aphylla thrives in wetland habitats and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.
