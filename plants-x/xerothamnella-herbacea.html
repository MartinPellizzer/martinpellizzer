Xerothamnella herbacea

Xerothamnella herbacea, commonly known as the desert hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant is adapted to survive in dry environments, storing water in its thick leaves and stems. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Xerothamnella herbacea plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators in desert ecosystems.
