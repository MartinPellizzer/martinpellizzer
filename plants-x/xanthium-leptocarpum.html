Xanthium leptocarpum

Xanthium leptocarpum, commonly known as slender burrweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and bract-like structures. The plant produces small, spiny fruits called burrs, which are adapted for seed dispersal by attaching to animals or clothing. Xanthium leptocarpum typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
