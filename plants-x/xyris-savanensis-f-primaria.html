Xyris savanensis f. primaria

Xyris savanensis f. primaria, commonly known as the Florida marsh yellow-eyed grass, is a variant of the marsh yellow-eyed grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial with grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and lakes. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in disturbed or moist soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
