Xanthoxalis crassicaulis

Xanthoxalis crassicaulis, commonly known as broad-stemmed goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and foliage with a distinctive yellowish hue. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and grasslands. It features basal leaves that are broad and succulent, often with a waxy coating.

Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.
