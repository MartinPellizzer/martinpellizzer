Xanthophyllum glaucum

Xanthophyllum glaucum, commonly known as the blue-green saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to alpine and arctic regions.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with small, waxy leaves that are often blue-green in color. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Xanthophyllum glaucum thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and resilient species often found in high-altitude environments.
