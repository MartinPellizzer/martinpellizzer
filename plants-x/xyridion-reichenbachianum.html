Xyridion reichenbachianum

Xyridion reichenbachianum, commonly known as the Reichenbach's xyridion, is a small, herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes various grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
