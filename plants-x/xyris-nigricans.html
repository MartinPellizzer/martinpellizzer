Xyris nigricans

Xyris nigricans, commonly known as blackroot yelloweye, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various grass-like plants known for their ornamental and ecological value. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along streambanks.

Xyris nigricans is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for pollinators.
