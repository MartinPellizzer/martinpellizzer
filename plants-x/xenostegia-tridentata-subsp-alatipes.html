Xenostegia tridentata subsp. alatipes

Xenostegia tridentata subsp.

alatipes, commonly known as the three-toothed xenostegia, is a climbing shrub native to the Caribbean. It features opposite, trifoliate leaves with three leaflets that are usually elliptical in shape.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
