Xerophyta pinifolia

Xerophyta pinifolia, commonly known as the needleleaf xerophyte, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves, which are adapted to reduce water loss through transpiration. The plant stores water in its fleshy stems, allowing it to survive in dry environments. It produces small, pale flowers that bloom during brief periods of rainfall.

Xerophyta pinifolia is well-suited for xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and drought tolerance.
