Xyris spiralis

Xyris spiralis, commonly known as spiral yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae and is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swampy areas.

Xyris spiralis is often found in clusters, with its grass-like foliage and delicate inflorescence making it a notable species in wetland ecosystems.
