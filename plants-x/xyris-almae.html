Xyris almae

Xyris almae, commonly known as the Alma's goldenroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes various species of grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers in early spring. Xyris almae is typically found in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in wetlands.
