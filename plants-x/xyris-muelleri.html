Xyris muelleri

Xyris muelleri, commonly known as Mueller's yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herb native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally wet habitats, such as swamps and marshes. Its flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences, and it produces small, three-lobed seeds.

Xyris muelleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
