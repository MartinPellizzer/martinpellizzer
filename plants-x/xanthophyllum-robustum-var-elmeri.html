Xanthophyllum robustum var. elmeri

Xanthophyllum robustum var. elmeri, commonly known as the Elmer's goldenbush, is a variant of the Xanthophyllum robustum species native to specific regions in South America.

It is characterized by its dense, yellow-green foliage and compact growth habit, making it a desirable ornamental plant. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, hairy textures, contributing to its unique appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This plant is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
