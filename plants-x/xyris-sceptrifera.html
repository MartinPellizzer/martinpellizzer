Xyris sceptrifera

Xyris sceptrifera, commonly known as the royal yellowroot, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the genus Xyris, which includes over 200 species of herbaceous plants. The plant is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. Xyris sceptrifera thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in suitable habitats.
