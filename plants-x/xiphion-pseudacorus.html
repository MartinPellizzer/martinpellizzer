Xiphion pseudacorus

Xiphion pseudacorus, commonly known as the yellow gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Xiphion pseudacorus is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and is often used in wetland restoration projects.
