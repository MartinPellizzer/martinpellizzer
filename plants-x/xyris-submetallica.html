Xyris submetallica

Xyris submetallica, commonly known as the metallic yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the genus Xyris, which is part of the family Xyridaceae, commonly known as the yellow-eyed grass family. The plant is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Xyris submetallica typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
