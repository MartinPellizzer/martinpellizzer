Xanthosoma crassinervium

Xanthosoma crassinervium, commonly known as the giant arrow leaf, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial with large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The leaves are typically a deep green with prominent, parallel veins, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers followed by edible, starchy tubers.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of food in certain regions.
