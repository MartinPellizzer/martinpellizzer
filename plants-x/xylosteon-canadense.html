Xylosteon canadense

Xylosteon canadense, commonly known as the Canadian xylosteon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along riverbanks and wetlands. The plant features slender, erect stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in loose inflorescences and bloom in early summer.

The plant is noted for its ability to thrive in wetland environments and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
