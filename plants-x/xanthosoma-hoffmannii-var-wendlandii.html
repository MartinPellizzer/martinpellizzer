Xanthosoma hoffmannii var. wendlandii

Xanthosoma hoffmannii var. wendlandii, commonly known as the Wendland's arrowleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial with large, arrow-shaped leaves that are typically bright green and deeply veined. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are edible and can be used in cooking, though they are usually cooked before consumption.
