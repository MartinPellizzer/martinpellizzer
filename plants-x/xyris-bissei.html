Xyris bissei

Xyris bissei, commonly known as the Florida marsh yelloweye, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the genus Xyris, which includes various species of marsh and swamp plants. The plant typically grows in wet, sandy soils and is often found in marshy areas and along the edges of ponds. Xyris bissei produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Its botanical features include narrow, grass-like leaves and a compact, herbaceous growth habit.
