Xiphion rutherfordii

Xiphion rutherfordii, commonly known as Rutherford's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, often covered in a fine layer of hairs.

Xiphion rutherfordii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
