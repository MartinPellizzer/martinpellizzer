Xyris fuliginea

Xyris fuliginea, commonly known as the blackroot goldeneye, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes plants commonly referred to as yellow-eyed grasses. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Xyris fuliginea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
