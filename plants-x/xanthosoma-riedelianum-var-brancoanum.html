Xanthosoma riedelianum var. brancoanum

Xanthosoma riedelianum var. brancoanum, commonly known as the Brazilian arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces clusters of small, spadix-like flowers surrounded by a showy spathe. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often cultivated for its starchy underground stems, which are used as a food source.

Its ornamental value also makes it a popular choice for gardeners in warm climates.
