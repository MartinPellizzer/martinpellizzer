Xyris torta var. occidentalis

Xyris torta var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and prairie habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
