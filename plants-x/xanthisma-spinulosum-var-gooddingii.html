Xanthisma spinulosum var. gooddingii

Xanthisma spinulosum var. gooddingii, commonly known as Goodding's sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features small, green, ovate leaves with spines along the margins, which provide protection against herbivory. Its flowers are typically pale yellow to greenish-white, arranged in dense clusters, and bloom during the summer months.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
