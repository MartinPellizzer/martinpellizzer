Xanthium × basilei

Xanthium × basilei, commonly known as common cocklebur, is a biennial plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical fruits and palmately lobed leaves. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and produces yellow composite flowers. Xanthium × basilei thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in agricultural fields.

It is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through wind-dispersed seeds.
