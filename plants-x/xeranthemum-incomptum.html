Xeranthemum incomptum

Xeranthemum incomptum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rockrose, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compact, upright growth with small, silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Xeranthemum incomptum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to flourish in arid conditions.
