Xiphopteris hecistophylla

Xiphopteris hecistophylla, commonly known as the broad-leaf holly fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, evergreen leaves that are deeply divided into narrow segments. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its fronds are often arching and can reach lengths of up to 1 meter.

Xiphopteris hecistophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its lush foliage.
