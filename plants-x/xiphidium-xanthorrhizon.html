Xiphidium xanthorrhizon

Xiphidium xanthorrhizon, commonly known as the golden root, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its root system is notable for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.

Xiphidium xanthorrhizon thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and shrubland habitats.
