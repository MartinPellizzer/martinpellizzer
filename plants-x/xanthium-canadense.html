Xanthium canadense

Xanthium canadense, commonly known as common cocklebur, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to North America.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. The plant produces spiny, spherical fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, hairy coating. Its leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges and often have a rough texture.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environments and is sometimes considered a weed.
