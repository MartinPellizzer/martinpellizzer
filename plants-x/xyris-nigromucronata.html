Xyris nigromucronata

Xyris nigromucronata, commonly known as the black-tipped yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes various grass-like plants. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Xyris nigromucronata is characterized by its distinctive black-tipped bracts at the base of its inflorescence.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
