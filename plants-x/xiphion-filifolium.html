Xiphion filifolium

Xiphion filifolium, commonly known as the thread-leaved lily, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.

Xiphion filifolium is also noted for its ability to withstand drought conditions once established.
