Xyris nilssonii

Xyris nilssonii, commonly known as the Nilsson's yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various species of yellow-eyed grasses. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to saturated soils. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in dense spikes, often appearing in late spring to early summer.

Xyris nilssonii is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and a compact growth habit.
