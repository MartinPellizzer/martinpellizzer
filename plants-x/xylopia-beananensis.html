Xylopia beananensis

Xylopia beananensis, commonly known as the African bitter apple, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. The fruit's pulp is edible but has a bitter taste, and the seeds are enclosed within a hard, woody capsule.

It is often found in moist, shaded areas of tropical forests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
