Xyris harleyi

Xyris harleyi, commonly known as the Harley's goldeneye, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various species of yellow-flowered plants. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its leaves are grass-like and narrow, while its flowers are bright yellow and clustered in spike-like inflorescences.

Xyris harleyi is notable for its ecological importance in supporting local pollinators and maintaining wetland biodiversity.
