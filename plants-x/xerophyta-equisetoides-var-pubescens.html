Xerophyta equisetoides var. pubescens

Xerophyta equisetoides var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy burweed, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, spiky, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are fleshy and adapted to store water, typical of xerophytic plants.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping in dry regions.
