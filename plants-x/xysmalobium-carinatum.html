Xysmalobium carinatum

Xysmalobium carinatum, commonly known as the horned lily, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its unique, spiky floral structure that resembles horns. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Xysmalobium carinatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a garden setting.
