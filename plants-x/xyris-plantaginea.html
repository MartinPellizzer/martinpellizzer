Xyris plantaginea

Xyris plantaginea, commonly known as the swamp lily, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the plant is often found in swamps, bogs, and along riverbanks.

Xyris plantaginea is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
