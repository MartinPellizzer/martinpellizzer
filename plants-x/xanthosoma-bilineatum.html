Xanthosoma bilineatum

Xanthosoma bilineatum, commonly known as the giant taro, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers surrounded by a spadix, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its edible leaves.

Xanthosoma bilineatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
