Xerochrysum bicolor

Xerochrysum bicolor, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flowers. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions. Its flowers are long-lasting when dried, which is why it is often used in floral arrangements.

Xerochrysum bicolor is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
