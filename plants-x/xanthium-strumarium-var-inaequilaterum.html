Xanthium strumarium var. inaequilaterum

Xanthium strumarium var. inaequilaterum, commonly known as the common cocklebur, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its spiny, prickly fruit capsules that enclose the seeds. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is native to temperate regions of the Americas. Its leaves are heart-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces, and it produces yellow flowers in composite inflorescences.

This variant is distinguished by its unequal-sided leaves, which give it its specific epithet "inaequilaterum.".
