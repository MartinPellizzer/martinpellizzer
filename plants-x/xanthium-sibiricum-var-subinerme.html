Xanthium sibiricum var. subinerme

Xanthium sibiricum var. subinerme, commonly known as Siberian cocklebur, is an annual herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Asia.

It features spiny, lobed leaves and produces spherical, spiky fruit capsules that contain seeds. The plant is characterized by its robust growth and adaptability to various soil types. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is known for its composite flower heads.
