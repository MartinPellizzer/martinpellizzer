Xanthorrhoea

Xanthorrhoea, commonly known as the grass tree, is a unique plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like foliage and a woody, bottle-shaped stem. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense spikes, typically during the summer months. Xanthorrhoea has a deep root system that allows it to survive in arid conditions.

Its resilience and distinctive appearance make it a notable species in Australian flora.
