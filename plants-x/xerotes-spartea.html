Xerotes spartea

Xerotes spartea, commonly known as the desert spurge, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves, which help it retain water in dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It has a shallow root system that allows it to quickly absorb surface moisture.

Xerotes spartea is adapted to thrive in hot, dry climates and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
