Xanthosoma linganii

Xanthosoma linganii, commonly known as the giant taro, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in length. The plant produces a spadix inflorescence surrounded by a spathe, which is typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its edible corms.

Xanthosoma linganii is also valued for its ornamental foliage and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
