Xanthophyllum vitellinum var. clovis

Xanthophyllum vitellinum var. clovis, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a striking perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive yellow throat. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are usually a vibrant green.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial to full shade.
