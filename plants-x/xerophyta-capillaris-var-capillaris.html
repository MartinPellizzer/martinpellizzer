Xerophyta capillaris var. capillaris

Xerophyta capillaris var. capillaris, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-shaped growth habit and fleshy, gray-green leaves that help store water. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its adaptability to drought conditions makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
