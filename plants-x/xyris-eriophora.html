Xyris eriophora

Xyris eriophora, commonly known as the narrowleaf goldeneye, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Xyridaceae family, which includes various grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. Its grass-like foliage is narrow and grass-green, with tall, spike-like inflorescences that bear bright yellow flowers.

The plant plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various insects and wildlife.
