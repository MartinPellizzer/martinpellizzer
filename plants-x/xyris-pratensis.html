Xyris pratensis

Xyris pratensis, commonly known as the marsh goldeneye, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dense clusters, with grass-like leaves that are often submerged or partially submerged in water. It thrives in marshy areas and is adapted to tolerate fluctuating water levels.

Xyris pratensis plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing food and habitat for various insects and wildlife.
