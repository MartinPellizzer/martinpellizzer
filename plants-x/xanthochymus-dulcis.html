Xanthochymus dulcis

Xanthochymus dulcis, commonly known as sweetleaf, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, dry capsule containing several seeds.

Xanthochymus dulcis is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine.
