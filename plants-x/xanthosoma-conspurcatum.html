Xanthosoma conspurcatum

Xanthosoma conspurcatum, commonly known as the spotted arrowleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows up to 2 meters in height, featuring large, heart-shaped leaves with a rough, textured surface. The leaves are often marked with dark green spots and have a waxy coating, which helps in water retention. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, starchy tubers.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional cuisines in its native regions.
