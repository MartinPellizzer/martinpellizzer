Xanthophyllum affine var. adenopodum

Xanthophyllum affine var. adenopodum, commonly known as the roughleaf goldenbush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish-green leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, giving it a rough texture.
