Xanthium bubalocarpon

Xanthium bubalocarpon, commonly known as the buffalo thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant has spiny leaves and produces yellow flowers that develop into rough, spiky fruits. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Xanthium bubalocarpon is known for its hardy nature and ability to spread through seeds dispersed by animals and wind.
