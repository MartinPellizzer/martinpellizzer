Xyris hildebrandtii var. angustifolia

Xyris hildebrandtii var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves and yellow flower spikes. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, producing clusters of small, yellowish flowers that attract pollinators.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in wetland habitats.
