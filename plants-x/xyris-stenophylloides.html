Xyris stenophylloides

Xyris stenophylloides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the genus Xyris in the family Xyridaceae, which includes various species of yellow-eyed grasses. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in wetlands.

Xyris stenophylloides is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
