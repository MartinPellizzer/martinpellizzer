Xantolis vrieseana

Xantolis vrieseana, commonly known as the Vriesia orchid, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange with a distinctive three-lobed lip, and it thrives in warm, humid conditions.

Xantolis vrieseana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in greenhouses and conservatories.
