Xanthium cordifolium

Xanthium cordifolium, commonly known as cocklebur, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and spiny, prickly fruit capsules. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers. Its yellow flowers bloom in the summer, and the plant is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Xanthium cordifolium is an annual herb that can grow up to 2 meters tall.
