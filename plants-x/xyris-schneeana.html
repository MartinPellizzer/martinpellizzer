Xyris schneeana

Xyris schneeana, commonly known as the snowdrop yarrow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the genus Xyris in the family Xyridaceae, which includes various grass-like plants. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Xyris schneeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
