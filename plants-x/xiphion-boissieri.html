Xiphion boissieri

Xiphion boissieri, commonly known as the Boissier's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually blue or violet in color. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Xiphion boissieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
