Xysmalobium grande

Xysmalobium grande, commonly known as the large yellow gentian, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its vibrant blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are erect and branched.

Xysmalobium grande is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its showy blooms.
