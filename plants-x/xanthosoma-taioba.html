Xanthosoma taioba

Xanthosoma taioba, commonly known as the Brazilian arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrowhead-shaped leaves. The plant produces a spadix inflorescence surrounded by a spathe, typical of many aroid species. Its tubers are edible and have a starchy, slightly sweet flavor.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as for its culinary uses.
