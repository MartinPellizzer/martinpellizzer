Xyridion sibiricum

Xyridion sibiricum, commonly known as the Siberian waterplantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cold regions of Siberia and northern Asia.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes plants commonly known as the yellow-eyed grasses. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes and along riverbanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to the harsh, cold climates of its native habitat.
