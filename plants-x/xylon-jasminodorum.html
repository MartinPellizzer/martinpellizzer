Xylon jasminodorum

Xylon jasminodorum, commonly known as the jasmine wood plant, is a unique species characterized by its woody structure and aromatic foliage.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is native to tropical regions of Africa. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that emit a jasmine-like scent. Its bark is smooth and pale, often exuding a mild fragrance when cut.
