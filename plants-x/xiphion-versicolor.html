Xiphion versicolor

Xiphion versicolor, commonly known as the pink bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded locations.

Xiphion versicolor is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and natural habitats.
