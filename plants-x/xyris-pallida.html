Xyris pallida

Xyris pallida, commonly known as the pale yellow-eyed grass, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes various grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in wet, acidic soils and is often found in swampy or marshy habitats.

Xyris pallida is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
