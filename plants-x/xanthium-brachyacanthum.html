Xanthium brachyacanthum

Xanthium brachyacanthum, commonly known as roughseed cockleweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rough, spiny leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is often considered a weed. It produces small, hairy fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Xanthium brachyacanthum is adapted to a variety of environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in its native range.
