Xyris capensis var. microcephala

Xyris capensis var. microcephala, commonly known as the Cape goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the summer months. The plant is characterized by its small, rounded flower heads, which are tightly packed and resemble a golden tuft. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
