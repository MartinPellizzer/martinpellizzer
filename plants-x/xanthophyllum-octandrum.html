Xanthophyllum octandrum

Xanthophyllum octandrum, commonly known as the eight-armed golden fern, is a unique plant species characterized by its striking golden-yellow fronds.

It belongs to the family Xanthophyllaceae and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia. The plant's most distinctive feature is its complex, branching structure, with eight primary arms extending from the central stem. Its leaves are thin and delicate, often arranged in a radiating pattern that enhances its ornamental appeal.

Xanthophyllum octandrum thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in dense forest understories.
