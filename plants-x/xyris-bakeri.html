Xyris bakeri

Xyris bakeri, commonly known as the Baker's goldenroot, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along streambanks and in wetlands. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces clusters of yellowish-green to golden yellow flowers in early summer. Xyris bakeri is part of the Xyridaceae family, which includes other marsh and wetland plants.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
