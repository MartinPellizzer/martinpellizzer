Xyris bancana var. lacerata

Xyris bancana var. lacerata, commonly known as the fragrant marsh yelloweye, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to saturated soil conditions. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers have a subtle, sweet fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
