Xyridion foetidissimum

Xyridion foetidissimum, commonly known as the stinking marsh grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown in color. The plant is adapted to thrive in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, swamps, and other wetland environments. Its most notable feature is its strong, unpleasant odor, which is likely a defense mechanism against herbivores.

Xyridion foetidissimum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
