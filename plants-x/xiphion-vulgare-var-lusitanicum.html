Xiphion vulgare var. lusitanicum

Xiphion vulgare var. lusitanicum, commonly known as the Lusitanian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and grows in clusters, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or borders.
