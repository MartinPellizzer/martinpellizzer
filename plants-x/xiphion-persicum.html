Xiphion persicum

Xiphion persicum, commonly known as the Persian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal. Xiphion persicum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a member of the Amaryllidaceae family, which includes other well-known ornamental and medicinal plants.
