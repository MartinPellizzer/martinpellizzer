Xysmalobium orbiculare

Xysmalobium orbiculare, commonly known as the golden sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of bright yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in desert scrub and rocky habitats.

Xysmalobium orbiculare is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
