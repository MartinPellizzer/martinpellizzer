Xyris sulcatifolia

Xyris sulcatifolia, commonly known as the sulcate yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Xyridaceae family, which includes various grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its most distinctive feature is its bright yellow flowers, which are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

Xyris sulcatifolia plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
