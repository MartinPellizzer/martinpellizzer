Xanthomyrtus calytrichoides

Xanthomyrtus calytrichoides, commonly known as the wax myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, waxy leaves that are typically dark green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands and coastal areas.

Xanthomyrtus calytrichoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
