Xanthium japonicum

Xanthium japonicum, commonly known as Japanese cocklebur, is an annual herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces spiny, spherical fruits that contain seeds with a distinctive, hooked beak. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Xanthium japonicum is known for its ability to spread rapidly and is considered a weed in many regions.
