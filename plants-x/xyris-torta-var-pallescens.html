Xyris torta var. pallescens

Xyris torta var. pallescens, commonly known as the pale dwarf yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in swampy areas or along stream banks. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and pale green foliage.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in wetland habitats.
