Xyridion aureum

Xyridion aureum, commonly known as the golden marsh grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bright green, often with a slight yellowish hue. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences of yellowish-green flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in areas with standing water.

Xyridion aureum plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various insects and microorganisms.
