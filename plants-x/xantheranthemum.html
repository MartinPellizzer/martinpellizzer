Xantheranthemum

Xantheranthemum, commonly known as the golden flame flower, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its vibrant yellow-orange blooms. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, aiding in moisture retention. Xantheranthemum thrives in humid, well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its showy flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to ornamental gardens.
