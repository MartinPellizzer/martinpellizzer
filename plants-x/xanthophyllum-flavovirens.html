Xanthophyllum flavovirens

Xanthophyllum flavovirens, commonly known as the yellow-green fern, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like leaves that display a striking combination of yellow and green hues. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in loose inflorescences. Its growth habit is predominantly herbaceous, with a preference for moist, shaded environments.

Xanthophyllum flavovirens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
