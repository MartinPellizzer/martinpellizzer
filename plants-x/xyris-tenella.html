Xyris tenella

Xyris tenella, commonly known as narrowleaf yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the genus Xyris in the family Xyridaceae, which includes various species of yellow-eyed grass. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, preferring moist to wet soil conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in spikes.

Xyris tenella plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native wetland ecosystems.
