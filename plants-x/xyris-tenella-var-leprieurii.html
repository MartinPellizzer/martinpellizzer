Xyris tenella var. leprieurii

Xyris tenella var. leprieurii, commonly known as the narrowleaf yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features grass-like leaves that are narrow and grass-green, with a distinctive yellow flower spike that blooms in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils in woodland or swampy areas. Its flowers are clustered in a cylindrical spike and are often visited by bees and other pollinators.

It is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
