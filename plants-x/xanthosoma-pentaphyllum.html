Xanthosoma pentaphyllum

Xanthosoma pentaphyllum, commonly known as the arrowleaf elephant ear, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, with a velvety texture and a deep green color. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that are followed by edible, starchy tubers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental and culinary gardens.

Xanthosoma pentaphyllum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in traditional cuisines.
