Xylothamia pseudobaccharis

Xylothamia pseudobaccharis, commonly known as the false acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots that help it survive in dry conditions. It produces small, woody pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Xylothamia pseudobaccharis is often used in ecological restoration due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for native wildlife.
