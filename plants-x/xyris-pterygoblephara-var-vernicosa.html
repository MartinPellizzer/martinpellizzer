Xyris pterygoblephara var. vernicosa

Xyris pterygoblephara var. vernicosa, commonly known as the glossy yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes various species of yellow-eyed grasses. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its varietal name, *vernicosa*, refers to the waxy, glossy appearance of its foliage.

The botanical characteristics of this plant make it well adapted to moist, acidic soils in woodland environments.
