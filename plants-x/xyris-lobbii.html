Xyris lobbii

Xyris lobbii, commonly known as the Lobb's yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes grass-like plants known for their showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist, acidic soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Xyris lobbii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland habitats.
