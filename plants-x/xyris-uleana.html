Xyris uleana

Xyris uleana, commonly known as the yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the genus Xyris in the family Xyridaceae, which includes various species of grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, and thrives in moist, sandy soils. Its leaves are grass-like and narrow, while its flowers are yellow and clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

Xyris uleana plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
