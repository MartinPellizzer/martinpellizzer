Xyris malaccensis

Xyris malaccensis, commonly known as the Malacca yellowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant typically features grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and wetlands.

Xyris malaccensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
