Xyris piranii

Xyris piranii, commonly known as the Piranha yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Xyris piranii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens in suitable climates.
