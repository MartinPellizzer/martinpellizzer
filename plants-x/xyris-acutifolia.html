Xyris acutifolia

Xyris acutifolia, commonly known as slender marsh yelloweye, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Xyridaceae family, which includes various grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its narrow, grass-like leaves are arranged in a rosette, and it produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer.

Xyris acutifolia is often found in shaded to partially sunny conditions and is an important component of wetland ecosystems.
