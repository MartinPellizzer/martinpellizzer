Xyris erythema

Xyris erythema, commonly known as the red-flowered yellow-eyed grass, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes various grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright red flowers during the summer months. Its botanical structure is characterized by its compact growth habit and rhizomatous root system.

Xyris erythema is often found in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in wetlands.
