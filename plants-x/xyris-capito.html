Xyris capito

Xyris capito, commonly known as the narrowleaf goldenroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various grass-like plants known for their showy yellow flowers. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Xyris capito is often found in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and roadside ditches.

Its botanical structure supports its role in local ecosystems, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small invertebrates.
