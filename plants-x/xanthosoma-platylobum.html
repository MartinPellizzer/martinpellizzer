Xanthosoma platylobum

Xanthosoma platylobum, commonly known as the giant taro, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, broad, arrow-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by edible, starchy tubers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Xanthosoma platylobum is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its nutritional importance in traditional cuisines.
