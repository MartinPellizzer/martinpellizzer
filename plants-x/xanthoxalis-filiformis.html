Xanthoxalis filiformis

Xanthoxalis filiformis, commonly known as slender false goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant features slender, hairless stems and small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. Its leaves are linear and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Xanthoxalis filiformis is adapted to grow in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.
