Xerotes peronii

Xerotes peronii, commonly known as the Peron's xerophyte, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of Australia.

It has thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Its root system is shallow but extensive, enabling it to absorb moisture from a wide area.

Xerotes peronii is well adapted to extreme heat and low rainfall, making it a hardy species in harsh climates.
