Xyris pectinata

Xyris pectinata, commonly known as the yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Xyris pectinata typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist, acidic soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for wetland ecosystems and a popular choice for native plant gardens.
