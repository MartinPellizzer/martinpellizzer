Xanthium orientale subsp. saccharatum

Xanthium orientale subsp.

saccharatum, commonly known as sugarleaf cockleweed, is a herbaceous annual plant native to temperate regions of Asia. It is characterized by its spiny, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive white underside.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into rough, spiny capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in various ecosystems.
