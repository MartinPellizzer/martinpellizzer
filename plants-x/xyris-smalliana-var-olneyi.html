Xyris smalliana var. olneyi

Xyris smalliana var. olneyi, commonly known as the Olney's golden club, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, thriving in moist, acidic soils. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces yellowish-green flower spikes in late spring to early summer. Its botanical classification places it within the family Xyridaceae, which includes various species of grass-like plants.

The variety olneyi is distinguished by its unique morphological characteristics and geographic distribution.
