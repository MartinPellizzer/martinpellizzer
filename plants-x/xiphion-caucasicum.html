Xiphion caucasicum

Xiphion caucasicum, commonly known as the Caucasian lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features strap-like, grassy leaves and produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and is often found in rocky or alpine environments. Xiphion caucasicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

It is also notable for its ability to thrive in well-drained soil and full sun conditions.
