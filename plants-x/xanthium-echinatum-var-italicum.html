Xanthium echinatum var. italicum

Xanthium echinatum var. italicum, commonly known as the Italian cocklebur, is a biennial plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its spiny, prickly leaves and spherical, hairy fruits that contain seeds. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and wastelands. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through wind-dispersed seeds.
