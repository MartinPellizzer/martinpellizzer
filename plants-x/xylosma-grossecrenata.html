Xylosma grossecrenata

Xylosma grossecrenata, commonly known as the broadleaf xylosma, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or limestone areas.

Xylosma grossecrenata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
