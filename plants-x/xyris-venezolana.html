Xyris venezolana

Xyris venezolana, commonly known as the Venezuelan yellowroot, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Venezuela.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes many species of grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
