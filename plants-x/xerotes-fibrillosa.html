Xerotes fibrillosa

Xerotes fibrillosa, commonly known as the fibrous xerophyte, is a succulent plant native to arid regions.

It possesses thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant's fibrous structure helps reduce water loss and provides structural support. It typically blooms with small, yellowish flowers during the rainy season.

Xerotes fibrillosa is well adapted to extreme heat and low precipitation, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.
