Xanthorrhoea australis

Xanthorrhoea australis, commonly known as the grass tree, is a unique evergreen plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like leaves and a thick, woody trunk that can store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. Xanthorrhoea australis is adapted to fire-prone environments and can regenerate from its underground rhizomes after bushfires.

Its distinctive appearance and ecological importance make it a significant species in Australian flora.
