Xyris thysanolepis var. thysanolepis

Xyris thysanolepis var. thysanolepis, commonly known as the narrowleaf goldeneye, is a species of flowering plant in the family Xyridaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, preferring moist, acidic soils. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces yellowish-green flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. Its botanical characteristics include a rhizomatous root system and a compact growth habit.

It is often found in the southeastern United States, particularly in Florida and the Carolinas.
