Xylia torreana

Xylia torreana, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its smooth, light-colored bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by woody, elongated fruits containing flat, winged seeds. Xylia torreana is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and forest margins.
