Xamilenis acaulis

Xamilenis acaulis, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its low-growing, sprawling habit with a compact, stemless base. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind. Xamilenis acaulis is well adapted to harsh environments, making it a resilient and hardy species.
