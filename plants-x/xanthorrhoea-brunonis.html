Xanthorrhoea brunonis

Xanthorrhoea brunonis, commonly known as the Brunner's grass tree, is a unique and ancient plant native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the genus Xanthorrhoea, which is part of the family Xanthorrhoeaceae. This species is characterized by its tall, narrow trunk and a dense crown of grass-like leaves, forming a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellowish to orange flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences.

Xanthorrhoea brunonis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing remarkable resilience to drought and fire.
