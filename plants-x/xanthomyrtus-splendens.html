Xanthomyrtus splendens

Xanthomyrtus splendens, commonly known as the golden myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, reddish berries that are edible but not typically consumed by humans. Xanthomyrtus splendens thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
