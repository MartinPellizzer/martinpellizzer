Xyris papillosa

Xyris papillosa, commonly known as the silky yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Xyris papillosa typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in wetlands.

It is a perennial herb that plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects.
