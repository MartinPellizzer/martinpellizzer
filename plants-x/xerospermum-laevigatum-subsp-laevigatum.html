Xerospermum laevigatum subsp. laevigatum

Xerospermum laevigatum subsp.

laevigatum, commonly known as the roughleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its subspecies laevigatum is distinguished by its rough, textured leaves and relatively small flower heads.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.
