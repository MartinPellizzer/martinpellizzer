Xyris cubana

Xyris cubana, commonly known as the Cuban yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes grass-like plants known for their showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its inflorescence consists of clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Xyris cubana is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.
