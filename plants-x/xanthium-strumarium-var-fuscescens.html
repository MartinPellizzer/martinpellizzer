Xanthium strumarium var. fuscescens

Xanthium strumarium var. fuscescens, commonly known as the rough cocklebur, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and large, spherical fruit that contains numerous seeds. The plant is native to temperate regions of the Americas but has been introduced to other parts of the world. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in agricultural fields.

The rough cocklebur is known for its defensive spines, which help protect its seeds from herbivores.
