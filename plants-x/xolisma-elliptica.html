Xolisma elliptica

Xolisma elliptica, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial that thrives in sandy and rocky soils. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pale green or yellow in color. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss.
