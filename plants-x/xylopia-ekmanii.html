Xylopia ekmanii

Xylopia ekmanii, commonly known as the African bitter almond, is a member of the Annonaceae family and is native to tropical Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with smooth, dark brown bark that exudes a strong, aromatic scent when cut. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers followed by round, yellowish fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate, with a glossy surface and a slightly serrated margin.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for treating various ailments.
