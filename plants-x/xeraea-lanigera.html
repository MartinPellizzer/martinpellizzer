Xeraea lanigera

Xeraea lanigera, commonly known as the woolly xeraea, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers during the dry season, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are thick and succulent, adapted to store water and withstand extreme temperatures.

Xeraea lanigera is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
