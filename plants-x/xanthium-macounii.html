Xanthium macounii

Xanthium macounii, commonly known as the Macoun cocklebur, is a species of flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and spiny fruit structures. The plant typically grows in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields, and it thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are heart-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces, and its fruit is a spiny, spherical bur that can adhere to clothing and animal fur.

Xanthium macounii is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through wind-dispersed seeds.
