Xanthocephalum amoenum

Xanthocephalum amoenum, commonly known as the yellow false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are small and clustered in a head.

Xanthocephalum amoenum is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
