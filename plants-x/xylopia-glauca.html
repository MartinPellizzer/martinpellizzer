Xylopia glauca

Xylopia glauca, commonly known as the blue-leafed sylph, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, bluish-gray bark and elliptical leaves with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by spherical, yellowish fruits containing several seeds.
