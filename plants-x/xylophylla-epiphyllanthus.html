Xylophylla epiphyllanthus

Xylophylla epiphyllanthus, commonly known as the leaf-attached wood plant, is a unique epiphytic species that grows on the surfaces of other plants, typically in humid tropical environments.

Its botanical structure features a fibrous, woody stem that adheres tightly to its host, allowing it to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. The plant possesses broad, flat leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, facilitating efficient water retention. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale in color, and its fruit is a capsule that releases tiny seeds.

Due to its specialized growth habit, Xylophylla epiphyllanthus plays a significant role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat.
