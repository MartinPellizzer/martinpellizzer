Xyridion tridentatum

Xyridion tridentatum, commonly known as the three-lobed marsh grass, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes various grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in swampy areas.

Xyridion tridentatum plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for aquatic insects.
