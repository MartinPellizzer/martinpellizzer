Xanthoxerampellia suareziorum

Xanthoxerampellia suareziorum, commonly known as the Suarez's golden fern, is a rare and unique fern species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that display a striking golden hue, especially when exposed to sunlight. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in dense rainforests where it clings to tree trunks or grows on rocky surfaces. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are typically located on the underside of its fronds, a common feature among ferns.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts to protect its natural environment.
