Xyris hystrix

Xyris hystrix, commonly known as the zigzag goldeneye, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes grass-like plants commonly referred to as yellow-eyed grasses. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Xyris hystrix is characterized by its zigzagging inflorescence, which gives it its common name.

It thrives in wetlands and moist soils, making it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
