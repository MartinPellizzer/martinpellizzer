Xylon leoninum

Xylon leoninum, commonly known as the golden trumpet tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The tree has a thick, woody trunk with a smooth, light-colored bark. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a glossy green surface.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
