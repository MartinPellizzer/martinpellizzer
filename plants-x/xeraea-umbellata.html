Xeraea umbellata

Xeraea umbellata, commonly known as the umbrella flower, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its clustered, umbrella-like inflorescence composed of numerous small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Xeraea umbellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
