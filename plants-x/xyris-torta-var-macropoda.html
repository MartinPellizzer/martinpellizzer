Xyris torta var. macropoda

Xyris torta var. macropoda, commonly known as the broadleaf yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in swampy areas or along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its inflorescence consists of clusters of yellowish-green spikelets.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland habitats.
