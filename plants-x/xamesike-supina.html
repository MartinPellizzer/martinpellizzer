Xamesike supina

Xamesike supina, commonly known as the climbing pomegranate, is a tropical vine native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually pink or white, followed by red, fleshy fruits similar to pomegranates. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The fruit is edible, though less commonly consumed than the common pomegranate.
