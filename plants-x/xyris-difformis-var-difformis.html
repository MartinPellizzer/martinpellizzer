Xyris difformis var. difformis

Xyris difformis var. difformis, commonly known as the variable yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wet to mesic habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early to mid-summer. Its inflorescence is a dense, cylindrical spike that resembles a bottlebrush.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
