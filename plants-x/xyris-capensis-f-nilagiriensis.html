Xyris capensis f. nilagiriensis

Xyris capensis f. nilagiriensis, commonly known as the Nilagiri yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a member of the Xyridaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small yellow flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
