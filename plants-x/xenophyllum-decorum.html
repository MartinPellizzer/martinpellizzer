Xenophyllum decorum

Xenophyllum decorum, commonly known as the ornate fern, is a unique species native to tropical regions.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its intricate, lace-like fronds. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid environments. Its leaves are bi-lobed and display a striking pattern of serrations and spore-bearing structures.

Xenophyllum decorum is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
