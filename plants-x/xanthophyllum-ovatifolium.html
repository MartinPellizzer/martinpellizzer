Xanthophyllum ovatifolium

Xanthophyllum ovatifolium, commonly known as the goldenleaf ovalleaf, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow-green foliage and oval-shaped leaves that are thick and leathery. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Xanthophyllum ovatifolium is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to both horticultural collections and ecological studies.
