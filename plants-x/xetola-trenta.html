Xetola trenta

Xetola trenta, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves that reflect light, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink, typically blooming during the rainy season. Xetola trenta thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and private collections.
