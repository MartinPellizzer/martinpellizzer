Xylopia densifolia

Xylopia densifolia, commonly known as the dense-leaved xylopia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are typically clustered in umbels, followed by elliptical, dark purple berries. Its wood is hard and durable, making it valuable in local craftsmanship and traditional medicine.

Xylopia densifolia thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland tropical forests.
