Xanthophyllum albicaule

Xanthophyllum albicaule, commonly known as the white-stemmed flameflower, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its vibrant orange to red flowers and pale, almost white stems.

Native to certain regions of Southeast Asia, it thrives in humid, warm climates with high light exposure. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are clustered in inflorescences, adding a dramatic visual impact to its appearance.

Xanthophyllum albicaule is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
