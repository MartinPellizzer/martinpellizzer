Xerochlamys coriacea

Xerochlamys coriacea, commonly known as the roughleaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.
