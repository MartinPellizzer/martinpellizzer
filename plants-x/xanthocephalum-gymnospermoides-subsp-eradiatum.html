Xanthocephalum gymnospermoides subsp. eradiatum

Xanthocephalum gymnospermoides subsp.

eradiatum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed ironwood, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in spikes, which are typically followed by small, woody pods. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought resistance through its deep root system.

This subspecies is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often used in traditional and contemporary landscaping.
