Xyris bampsii

Xyris bampsii, commonly known as the Bamps' yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various species of yellow-eyed grasses. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Xyris bampsii typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats.

Its botanical structure is adapted for efficient photosynthesis and water retention in its natural environment.
