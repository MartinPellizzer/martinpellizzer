Xylophacos consectus

Xylophacos consectus, commonly known as the silverleaf spirea, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are typically silver-gray in color, providing a striking contrast to the foliage. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable to local pollinators.
