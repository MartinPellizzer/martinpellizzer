Xyris exserta

Xyris exserta, commonly known as the narrowleaf yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes various species of grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Xyris exserta is typically found in moist, sandy soils in open woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
