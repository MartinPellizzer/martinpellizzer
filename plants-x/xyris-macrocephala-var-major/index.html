Xyris Macrocephala Var. Major

Xyris macrocephala var. major is a striking plant native to the southeastern united states. it is known for its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring and early summer. this variant of xyris is often found in wetland areas and along the edges of swamps. its ability to thrive in moist environments makes it an important part of local ecosystems. the plant's unique appearance and ecological role make it a subject of interest for botanists and nature enthusiasts alike.
