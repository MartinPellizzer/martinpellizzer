Xyris humpatensis var. rhodolepis

Xyris humpatensis var. rhodolepis, commonly known as the red-stemmed yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Xyridaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and specialized floral structures. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, thriving in moist to waterlogged soils. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences, and it produces a unique type of pollen that is adapted for specific pollinators.

The varietal form rhodolepis is distinguished by its reddish stems and slightly different floral morphology compared to the typical variety.
