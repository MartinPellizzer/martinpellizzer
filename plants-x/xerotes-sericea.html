Xerotes sericea

Xerotes sericea, commonly known as the silky xerotes, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its grayish-green, hairy foliage that helps reduce water loss in dry conditions. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often blooming in spring. Its adaptability to harsh environments makes it a resilient species in ecological restoration projects.

The silky texture of its leaves is a key feature that distinguishes it from other members of the genus.
