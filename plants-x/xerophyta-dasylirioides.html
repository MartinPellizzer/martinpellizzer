Xerophyta dasylirioides

Xerophyta dasylirioides, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, exhibiting xerophytic traits such as reduced leaf surface area and thick cuticles. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Xerophyta dasylirioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to dry climates.
