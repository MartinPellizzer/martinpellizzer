Xyris smalliana

Xyris smalliana, commonly known as the smallleaf yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the genus Xyris, which is part of the grass family, Poaceae. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers in early spring. It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in wetlands.

Xyris smalliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
