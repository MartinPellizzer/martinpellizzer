Xanthium fuscescens

Xanthium fuscescens, commonly known as yellow cocklebur, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is an annual herb that grows up to 1.5 meters tall, featuring rough, hairy stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that develop into spiny, spherical fruits containing seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Xanthium fuscescens is known for its ability to spread rapidly and is considered a weed in many regions.
