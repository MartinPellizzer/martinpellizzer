Xyris anisophylla

Xyris anisophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes various species of grass-like plants. The plant features grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and linear, with a fibrous root system. Its flowering stems produce clusters of bright yellow flowers, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts.

Xyris anisophylla is often found in wetlands, marshes, and other moist habitats, where it thrives in partial to full shade.
