Xyris prolificans

Xyris prolificans, commonly known as the common yellow iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes various grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, preferring moist, acidic soils.

Xyris prolificans is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
