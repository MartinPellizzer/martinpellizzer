Xerophyta retinervis var. wentzeliana

Xerophyta retinervis var. wentzeliana, commonly known as the Wentzel's ice plant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in arid environments. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ability to withstand drought conditions makes it an excellent example of a xerophyte adapted to extreme climates.
