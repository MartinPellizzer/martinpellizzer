Xanthium medium

Xanthium medium, commonly known as common cocklebur, is a herbaceous annual plant native to North America.

It features rough, hairy stems that are typically branched and can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces spiny, spherical fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, bur-like coating. Its leaves are lobed and have a rough texture, often with a distinctive yellowish-green color.

Xanthium medium is commonly found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, where it thrives in sunny, open environments.
