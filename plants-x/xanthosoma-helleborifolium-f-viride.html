Xanthosoma helleborifolium f. viride

Xanthosoma helleborifolium f. viride, commonly known as the green-leaf elephant ear, is a variant of the Xanthosoma helleborifolium species.

It is characterized by its large, broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green color. The plant is native to tropical regions of the Americas and thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage.

The leaves are usually smooth and can grow up to several feet in length, making it a popular choice for landscaping in subtropical gardens.
