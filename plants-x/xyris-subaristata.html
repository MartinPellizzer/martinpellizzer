Xyris subaristata

Xyris subaristata, commonly known as the narrowleaf yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.

Xyris subaristata is an important species for wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife.
