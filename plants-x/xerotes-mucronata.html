Xerotes mucronata

Xerotes mucronata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed spurge, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, aiding its survival in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. Its stems are often branched and covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Xerotes mucronata is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low precipitation, making it a hardy species in harsh climates.
