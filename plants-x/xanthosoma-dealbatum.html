Xanthosoma dealbatum

Xanthosoma dealbatum, commonly known as the giant swamp taro, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, broad, arrow-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant thrives in wet, muddy soils and is often found in swampy or marshy environments. Its flowering stems produce clusters of small, pale yellow flowers, followed by edible, starchy tubers.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat and is also cultivated for its nutritional value.
