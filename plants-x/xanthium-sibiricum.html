Xanthium sibiricum

Xanthium sibiricum, commonly known as Siberian cocklebur, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Eurasia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its prickly, spiny stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into spherical, rough-seeded fruits. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural settings.
