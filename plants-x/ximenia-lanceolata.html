Ximenia lanceolata

Ximenia lanceolata, commonly known as the African plum or sour plum, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The fruit is a fleshy, yellow or orange berry that contains several seeds. The plant is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

It has a shallow root system and is valued for its edible fruit and use in traditional medicine.
