Xyris elliottii var. stenotera

Xyris elliottii var. stenotera, commonly known as Elliott's yellowroot, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early spring. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is distinguished by its compact growth habit and unique floral structure compared to other Xyris species.
