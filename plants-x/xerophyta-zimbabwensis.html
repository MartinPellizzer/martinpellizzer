Xerophyta zimbabwensis

Xerophyta zimbabwensis, commonly known as the Zambian ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. It has a shallow root system that enables it to absorb moisture from the surface layer of the soil.

Xerophyta zimbabwensis is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a hardy and resilient species.
