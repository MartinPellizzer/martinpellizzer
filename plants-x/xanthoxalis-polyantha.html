Xanthoxalis polyantha

Xanthoxalis polyantha, commonly known as the yellow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with five petals. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. It is characterized by its ability to tolerate wet conditions and its ornamental value in gardens.

Xanthoxalis polyantha is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
