Xiphopteris elastica

Xiphopteris elastica, commonly known as the giant chain fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fern-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with a thick, fleshy underground stem that stores water and nutrients. Its leaves are bioluminescent, emitting a faint glow at night, which is a unique adaptation for attracting nocturnal pollinators.

This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental species in tropical gardens.
