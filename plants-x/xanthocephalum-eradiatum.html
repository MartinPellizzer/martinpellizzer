Xanthocephalum eradiatum

Xanthocephalum eradiatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sunhemp, is an annual legume native to Australia.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and hairy stems, with small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with linear to lanceolate leaflets, and its seeds are small, oval, and typically brown or black in color.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is used in agricultural systems for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities and as a cover crop.
