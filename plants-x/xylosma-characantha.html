Xylosma characantha

Xylosma characantha, commonly known as the African xylosma, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by small, woody fruits. Xylosma characantha is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
