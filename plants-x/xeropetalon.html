Xeropetalon

Xeropetalon, commonly known as the desert bell, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. Xeropetalon has a shallow root system that helps it absorb moisture from the soil quickly.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
