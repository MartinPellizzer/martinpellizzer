Xyris montivaga

Xyris montivaga, commonly known as the mountain yellowroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various species of yellow-eyed grasses. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or swampy areas. Its leaves are grass-like and narrow, while its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

Xyris montivaga is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments, and it plays a role in supporting local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
