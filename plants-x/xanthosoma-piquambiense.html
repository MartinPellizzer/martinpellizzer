Xanthosoma piquambiense

Xanthosoma piquambiense, commonly known as the Brazilian arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrowhead-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green spadices, which are the male flowers, followed by female flowers that develop into edible tubers. These tubers are rich in starch and are a traditional food source in some indigenous communities.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in riverine forests.
