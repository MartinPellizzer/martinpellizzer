Xanthosoma caulotuberculatum

Xanthosoma caulotuberculatum, commonly known as the tuberculate arrowleaf, is a tropical plant belonging to the Araceae family.

It is native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features large, arrow-shaped leaves with a glossy, dark green surface and prominent tubercles along the midrib. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe.

Xanthosoma caulotuberculatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
