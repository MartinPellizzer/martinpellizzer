Xiphopteris myosuroides

Xiphopteris myosuroides, commonly known as the horse-tail fern, is a member of the Hymenophyllum family and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds.

It is a cryptogamic plant, meaning it reproduces through spores rather than seeds, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant has a unique structure with a central stipe that bears spirally arranged, lanceolate leaves. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in areas with high humidity and rich, organic soil.

Its appearance resembles that of a horse's tail, giving it its common name.
