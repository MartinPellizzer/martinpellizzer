Xyridion ochroleucum

Xyridion ochroleucum, commonly known as the yellow marsh grass, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically yellowish-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered at the ends of the stems. Xyridion ochroleucum thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in marshes, fens, and along the edges of ponds.

It plays an important role in maintaining the ecological balance of wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
