Xanthium × widderi

Xanthium × widderi, commonly known as the common cocklebur, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to North America.

It is an annual herbaceous plant characterized by its prickly, spiny leaves and spherical, rough-seeded fruits. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in composite heads, typical of the daisy family.

Xanthium × widderi is known for its ability to spread through wind-dispersed seeds, contributing to its invasive potential in some regions.
