Xiphion gramineum subsp. sylvaticum

Xiphion gramineum subsp.

sylvaticum, commonly known as the woodland bellflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in woodland and meadow habitats, preferring moist, well-drained soils.

The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
