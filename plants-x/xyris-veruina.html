Xyris veruina

Xyris veruina, commonly known as the narrowleaf goldeneye, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes various grass-like plants known for their showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, preferring moist to saturated soil conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers form dense, spike-like clusters that bloom in early spring.

Xyris veruina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
