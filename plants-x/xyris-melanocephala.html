Xyris melanocephala

Xyris melanocephala, commonly known as black-headed yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes various grass-like plants. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers with dark purple to black bracts at the base. It thrives in wetland habitats and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Xyris melanocephala is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
