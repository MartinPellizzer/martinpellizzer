Xyris aristata

Xyris aristata, commonly known as the arrowleaf yellowroot, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters during the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Xyris aristata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
