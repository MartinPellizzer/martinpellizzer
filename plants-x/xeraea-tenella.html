Xeraea tenella

Xeraea tenella, commonly known as the narrowleaf desert bell, is a small, flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, helping it conserve water in its harsh environment.

Xeraea tenella is often found in shrubland and grassland habitats, contributing to the ecological diversity of its native range.
