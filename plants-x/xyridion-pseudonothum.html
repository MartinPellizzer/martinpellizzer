Xyridion pseudonothum

Xyridion pseudonothum, commonly known as the narrowleaf waterflag, is a perennial aquatic plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as marshes and ponds. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often submerged or floating, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense stands in its native habitat.

Xyridion pseudonothum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
