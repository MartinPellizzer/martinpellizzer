Xiphion donianum

Xiphion donianum, commonly known as the Donian's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, with a distinctive white throat. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces slender, branching stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal environments.

Xiphion donianum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
