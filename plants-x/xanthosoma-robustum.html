Xanthosoma robustum

Xanthosoma robustum, commonly known as the giant arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces a spadix inflorescence surrounded by a broad, showy spathe. Its rhizomes are edible and are used as a starchy food source in many regions.

Xanthosoma robustum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
