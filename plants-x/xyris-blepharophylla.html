Xyris blepharophylla

Xyris blepharophylla, commonly known as the narrowleaf goldeneye, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the genus Xyris, which is part of the grass family, Poaceae, though some classifications place it in the Xyridaceae family. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in early to mid-summer. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Xyris blepharophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
