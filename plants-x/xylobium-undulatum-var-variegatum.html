Xylobium undulatum var. variegatum

Xylobium undulatum var. variegatum, commonly known as the variegated wood lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, nodding, yellow flowers with a distinctive red or orange throat, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or cream markings. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
