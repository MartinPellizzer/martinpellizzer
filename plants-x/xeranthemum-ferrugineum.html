Xeranthemum ferrugineum

Xeranthemum ferrugineum, commonly known as the rusty thyme or red thyme, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange-red, typically blooming in summer. Its foliage is aromatic, with a scent reminiscent of thyme, and the leaves are covered in fine, hairy textures.

Xeranthemum ferrugineum is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
