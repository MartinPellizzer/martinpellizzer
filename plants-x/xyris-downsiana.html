Xyris downsiana

Xyris downsiana, commonly known as the Down's yellow-eyed grass, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of streams, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early to mid-summer. Its flowering period attracts various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Xyris downsiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
