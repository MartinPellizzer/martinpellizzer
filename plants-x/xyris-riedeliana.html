Xyris riedeliana

Xyris riedeliana, commonly known as the Riedel's goldeneye, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various grass-like plants. The plant produces striking yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often growing in dense clusters.

Xyris riedeliana is adapted to moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
