Xylopia perrieri

Xylopia perrieri, commonly known as the African bitter apple, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and West Africa.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its rough, often fissured bark and simple, alternate leaves. Xylopia perrieri is notable for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological contexts.
