Xyris fimbriata

Xyris fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed yellow-eyed grass, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the genus Xyris in the family Xyridaceae, which includes many species of grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. Its flowering stems bear clusters of bright yellow flowers, which are surrounded by narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant is notable for its ability to tolerate periodic flooding and its role in stabilizing wetland soils.
