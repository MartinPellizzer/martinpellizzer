Xyris neglecta var. scabridula

Xyris neglecta var. scabridula, commonly known as the roughleaf goldeneye, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and wetlands. The plant features grass-like leaves that are covered in fine, rough hairs, giving it its varietal name. Its flowers are yellow and occur in clusters, blooming from late spring to early summer.

The plant is often found in shaded to partially sunny environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
