Xyris subnavicularis

Xyris subnavicularis, commonly known as the narrowleaf yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring. It typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks.

Xyris subnavicularis is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments and is adapted to tolerate saturated soil conditions.
