Xylopia papuana

Xylopia papuana, commonly known as the Papuan xylopia, is a tropical tree native to the lowland forests of New Guinea.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by berry-like fruits. Xylopia papuana is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional crafts and as a source of essential oils.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various native wildlife species.
