Xyris caroliniana var. olneyi

Xyris caroliniana var. olneyi, commonly known as the Olney's goldeneye, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the genus Xyris, which includes various species of marsh and wetland plants. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in swamps, bogs, and along stream banks. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a striking visual element to its habitat.
