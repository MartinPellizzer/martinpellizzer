Xyris globosa

Xyris globosa, commonly known as the globe yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compact, globular inflorescence that resembles a small ball, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in wetlands, swamps, and other moist habitats, thriving in acidic soils. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Xyris globosa is a member of the Xyridaceae family, which includes other grass-like plants with similar ecological and morphological traits.
