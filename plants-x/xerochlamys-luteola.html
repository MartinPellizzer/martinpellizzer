Xerochlamys luteola

Xerochlamys luteola, commonly known as the yellow star orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure. The plant produces showy, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming during the wet season. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Xerochlamys luteola is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
