Xanthium brevirostre

Xanthium brevirostre, commonly known as common cocklebur, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is an annual herb that grows up to 1.5 meters tall, featuring prickly stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces spiny, spherical fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, hair-like substance. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in agricultural fields.

Xanthium brevirostre is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads.
