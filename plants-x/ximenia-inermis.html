Ximenia inermis

Ximenia inermis, commonly known as the African snowball or white thorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and clusters of white, waxy flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round, yellowish fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly serrated margin.

Ximenia inermis is adapted to dry climates and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.
