Xylosma glaucescens

Xylosma glaucescens, commonly known as the blue xylosma, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and small, elliptical leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round berries. It is often found in dry to moist woodland areas and is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

Xylosma glaucescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
