Xeranthemum recurvatum

Xeranthemum recurvatum, commonly known as the curly gromwell, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Xeranthemum recurvatum is often used in ornamental horticulture for its vibrant blooms and hardy nature.
