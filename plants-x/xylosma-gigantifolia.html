Xylosma gigantifolia

Xylosma gigantifolia, commonly known as the giant xylosma, is a flowering plant species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has small, opposite leaves that are often elliptical in shape and have a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, woody fruits.

Xylosma gigantifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
