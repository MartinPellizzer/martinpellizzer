Xyris albescens

Xyris albescens, commonly known as the white-eyed goldeneye, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various species of yellow-flowered plants. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Its flowers are often covered in a fine, white powder, giving it its common name.

Xyris albescens is typically found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or swampy areas.
