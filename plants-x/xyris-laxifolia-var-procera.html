Xyris laxifolia var. procera

Xyris laxifolia var. procera, commonly known as the tall yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, making it one of the tallest varieties within the Xyris genus. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, arranged in dense, spike-like clusters at the top of the stems. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a bluish-green hue.

Xyris laxifolia var. procera thrives in wetland habitats and is adapted to moist, acidic soils.
