Xyris subulata

Xyris subulata, commonly known as the slender yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes various species of grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Xyris subulata is often found in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in wetlands.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
