Xyris spectabilis

Xyris spectabilis, commonly known as the showy yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, yellow flowers that bloom in a spikelike arrangement.

Xyris spectabilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in wetland environments.
