Xeranthemum pinifolium

Xeranthemum pinifolium, commonly known as the pineleaf eryngo, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, pine-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are usually pale blue or white, and it produces a distinctive, aromatic foliage.

Xeranthemum pinifolium is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its hardy nature and visual appeal.
