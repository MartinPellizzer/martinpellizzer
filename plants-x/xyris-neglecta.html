Xyris neglecta

Xyris neglecta, commonly known as the slender yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Xyris neglecta typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for wetland ecosystems and a potential indicator of soil moisture conditions.
