Xanthophyllum roxburghianum

Xanthophyllum roxburghianum, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its common name. The plant belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is known for its ornamental value in gardens. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy coating, while its fruit is a capsule containing numerous seeds.

Xanthophyllum roxburghianum thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in landscaping for its vibrant floral display.
