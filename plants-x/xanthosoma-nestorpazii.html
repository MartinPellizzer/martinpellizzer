Xanthosoma nestorpazii

Xanthosoma nestorpazii, commonly known as the Mexican arrowleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces striking yellow and green spadices, which are the modified leaf structures that house the flowers. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Xanthosoma nestorpazii is also valued for its edible leaves, which are used in traditional cuisines.
