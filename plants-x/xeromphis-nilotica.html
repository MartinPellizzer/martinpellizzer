Xeromphis nilotica

Xeromphis nilotica, commonly known as the Nile pigweed, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and along riverbanks. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters. Its root system is fibrous, and it is often found in disturbed soils.

Xeromphis nilotica is adapted to thrive in humid conditions, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
