Xyris lobbii var. burmana

Xyris lobbii var. burmana, commonly known as Burmese golden club, is a variant of the golden club plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetland or marshy environments. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the summer months. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in lowland areas with high humidity.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
