Xiphopteris trichomanoides

Xiphopteris trichomanoides, commonly known as the broadleaf huperzia, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its leathery, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a dense, compact rosette. The plant is native to regions in Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a distinctive, rugged texture.

Xiphopteris trichomanoides is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
