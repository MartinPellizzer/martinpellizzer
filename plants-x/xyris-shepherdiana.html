Xyris shepherdiana

Xyris shepherdiana, commonly known as the yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Xyris shepherdiana typically grows in moist, sandy soils along streambanks and wetlands.

Its botanical structure is adapted to wetland environments, making it an important component of riparian ecosystems.
