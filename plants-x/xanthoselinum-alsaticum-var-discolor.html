Xanthoselinum alsaticum var. discolor

Xanthoselinum alsaticum var. discolor, commonly known as the alpine yellow gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, white pubescence.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
