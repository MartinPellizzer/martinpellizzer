Xyris seubertii

Xyris seubertii, commonly known as the Seubert's yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the genus Xyris, which is part of the grass family, Poaceae. The plant is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Xyris seubertii typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along riverbanks.
