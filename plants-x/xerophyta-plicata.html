Xerophyta plicata

Xerophyta plicata, commonly known as the Zulu onion or African chervil, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, narrow leaves that store water, a key adaptation for surviving in arid environments. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Xerophyta plicata is also valued for its edible leaves, which have a mild, onion-like flavor.
