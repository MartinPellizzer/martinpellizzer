Ximenia americana var. pubens

Ximenia americana var. pubens, commonly known as the rough ximenia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its rough, pubescent bark and small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces edible, fleshy fruits that are often consumed by wildlife and humans. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly serrated edge.
