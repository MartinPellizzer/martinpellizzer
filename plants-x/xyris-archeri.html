Xyris archeri

Xyris archeri, commonly known as the mountain marsh yelloweye, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in the spring. It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes other yellow-flowered plants.

Xyris archeri is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
