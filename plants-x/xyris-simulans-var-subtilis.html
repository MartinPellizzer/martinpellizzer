Xyris simulans var. subtilis

Xyris simulans var. subtilis, commonly known as the slender marsh yelloweye, is a variant of the marsh yelloweye plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers in the spring. Its botanical characteristics include a rhizomatous root system and small, clustered inflorescences.

This variant is distinguished by its slightly smaller stature and more delicate appearance compared to the typical form of Xyris simulans.
