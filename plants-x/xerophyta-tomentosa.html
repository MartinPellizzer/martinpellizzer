Xerophyta tomentosa

Xerophyta tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly pig's ear, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and drought-tolerant landscapes.
