Xanthocephalum gymnospermoides subsp. intermedium

Xanthocephalum gymnospermoides subsp.

intermedium, commonly known as the coastal wattle, is a shrub native to coastal regions of southern Australia. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical bean-like pods and compound leaves.

The plant is adapted to sandy soils and saline environments, making it resilient to coastal conditions. Its yellow flowers appear in spring, adding a striking visual element to the landscape.

It plays a role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for local wildlife.
