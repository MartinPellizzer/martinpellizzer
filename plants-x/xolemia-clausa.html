Xolemia clausa

Xolemia clausa, commonly known as the closed-leaf xolemia, is a rare flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form with small, leathery leaves that are densely packed along its stems. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow flowers during the spring and early summer months. Its botanical significance lies in its unique adaptations to cold and arid environments, including a thick waxy cuticle on its leaves.

Xolemia clausa is of interest to botanists due to its potential medicinal properties and its role in local ecosystems.
