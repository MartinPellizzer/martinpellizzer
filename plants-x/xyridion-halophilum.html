Xyridion halophilum

Xyridion halophilum, commonly known as the salt marsh xyrid, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes grass-like plants often found in wetland habitats. The plant exhibits compact growth with narrow, linear leaves that help reduce water loss in salty conditions. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in late summer or early autumn.

Xyridion halophilum plays a vital role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various microorganisms and small fauna.
