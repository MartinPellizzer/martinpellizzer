Xysmalobium rhodesianum

Xysmalobium rhodesianum, commonly known as the Rhodesian sandveld lily, is a striking perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, blue-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces tall, branched stems that bear clusters of pale yellow to cream-colored flowers with a distinctive, spurred shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
