Xanthium albinum subsp. ripicola

Xanthium albinum subsp.

ripicola, commonly known as the white cocklebur, is a herbaceous annual plant native to North America. It is characterized by its hairy, spiny leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in the summer.

The plant produces spherical, spiky fruits that contain seeds with a hard, burs-like covering. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in open fields.

Its botanical features make it identifiable and ecologically significant in its native habitats.
