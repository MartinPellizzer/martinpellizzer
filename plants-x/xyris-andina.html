Xyris andina

Xyris andina, commonly known as the Andean goldenroot, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes various grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Xyris andina is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some indigenous communities.
