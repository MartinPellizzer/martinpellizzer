Xanthophyllum floriferum

Xanthophyllum floriferum, commonly known as the golden bloom plant, is a flowering species native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a waxy texture, and it thrives in humid, well-drained soils. Xanthophyllum floriferum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and adaptability to various climatic conditions.
