Xiphion jacquinii

Xiphion jacquinii, commonly known as the Canary Islands lily, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or coastal areas. The plant produces striking white or pale pink flowers with a distinctive, recurved shape. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette.

Xiphion jacquinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
