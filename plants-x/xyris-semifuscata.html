Xyris semifuscata

Xyris semifuscata, commonly known as the dark-leaf yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist habitats, showcasing its adaptability to humid environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical classification places it in the family Xyridaceae, which includes several species of yellow-eyed grasses.

Xyris semifuscata is notable for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
