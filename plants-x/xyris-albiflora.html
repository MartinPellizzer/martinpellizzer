Xyris albiflora

Xyris albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflower yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Xyridaceae family, which includes various grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, preferring moist to swampy soils. It produces small, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often reddish or purplish at the base.
