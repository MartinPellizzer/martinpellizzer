Xanthium maculatum

Xanthium maculatum, commonly known as the spotted cocklebur, is an annual plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its rough, spiny leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces spherical fruits covered in hooked spines, which aid in seed dispersal by attaching to animals. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
