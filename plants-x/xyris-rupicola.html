Xyris rupicola

Xyris rupicola, commonly known as the rock yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often found in open woodlands and along stream banks. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It is adapted to well-drained conditions and can tolerate partial shade to full sun.

Xyris rupicola is a member of the Xyridaceae family, which includes other yellow-eyed grasses.
