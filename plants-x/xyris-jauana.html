Xyris jauana

Xyris jauana, commonly known as the Florida marsh yelloweye, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Xyridaceae family and typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps it thrive in moist, acidic soils.

Xyris jauana plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing wetland soils.
