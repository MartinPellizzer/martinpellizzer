Xeranthemum variegatum

Xeranthemum variegatum, commonly known as the variegated sandverbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub with small, narrow leaves that often display variegation in green and white. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Xeranthemum variegatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
