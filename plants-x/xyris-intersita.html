Xyris intersita

Xyris intersita, commonly known as the narrowleaf yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Xyridaceae family, which includes various species of grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to saturated soil conditions. Xyris intersita produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, adding a striking visual element to its natural environment.

Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.
