Xyris subtilis

Xyris subtilis, commonly known as the slender marsh yelloweye, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes grass-like plants often found in wetland environments. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Xyris subtilis is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and its ability to thrive in humid, shaded conditions.
