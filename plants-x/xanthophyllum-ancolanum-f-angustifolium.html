Xanthophyllum ancolanum f. angustifolium

Xanthophyllum ancolanum f. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved goldenrod, is a botanical variant characterized by its slender, elongated leaves.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is native to certain regions of South America. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is typically densely packed, giving it a compact appearance, and it thrives in well-drained, sunny habitats.

This form is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
