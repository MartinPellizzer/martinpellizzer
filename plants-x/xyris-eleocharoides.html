Xyris eleocharoides

Xyris eleocharoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in wetland environments, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Xyris eleocharoides is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
