Xanthophyllum flavum

Xanthophyllum flavum, commonly known as the yellow-flowered bellflower, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Xanthophyllum flavum is native to parts of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates.
