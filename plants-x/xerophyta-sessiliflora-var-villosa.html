Xerophyta sessiliflora var. villosa

Xerophyta sessiliflora var. villosa, commonly known as the woolly ice plant, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves covered in fine, white hairs that help reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
