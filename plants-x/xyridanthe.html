Xyridanthe

Xyridanthe, commonly known as the narrowleaf waterplantain, is a member of the Xyridaceae family, which includes grass-like plants.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant thrives in moist or wet environments, such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. Xyridanthe is often found in temperate regions and is adapted to tolerate waterlogged soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
