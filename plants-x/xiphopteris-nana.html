Xiphopteris nana

Xiphopteris nana, commonly known as the dwarf chain fern, is a small, compact fern native to Southeast Asia.

It features a rosette of narrow, leathery, dark green fronds that form a dense, low-growing clump. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in chains, a characteristic feature of the genus Xiphopteris. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for rock gardens and shaded landscapes.
