Xylosma suaveolens subsp. pubigera

Xylosma suaveolens subsp.

pubigera, commonly known as sweet xylosma, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves that are often pubescent, giving it a soft, hairy texture.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in the spring. Its bark is fibrous and has a distinctive, aromatic scent.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth.
