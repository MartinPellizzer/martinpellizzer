Xenochloa arundinacea

Xenochloa arundinacea, commonly known as bamboo grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, elongated leaves that resemble bamboo. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, forming dense clumps.

Xenochloa arundinacea is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various climates.
