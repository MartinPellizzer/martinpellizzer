Xanthium glabratum

Xanthium glabratum, commonly known as smooth cocklebur, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant produces spiny, spherical fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, fibrous coating. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and a distinctive yellowish-green color.

Xanthium glabratum is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including fields and along roadsides.
