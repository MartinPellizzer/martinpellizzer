Xanthium intermedium

Xanthium intermedium, commonly known as cocklebur, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features rough, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces spiny, spherical fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, adhesive coating. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Xanthium intermedium is known for its ability to spread easily through wind-dispersed seeds and its potential as a weed in agricultural settings.
