Xyris brevifolia

Xyris brevifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf goldeneye, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early summer. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its flowering period attracts various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Xyris brevifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
