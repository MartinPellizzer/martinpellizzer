Xanthe scandens

Xanthe scandens, commonly known as the yellow climbing plant, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to parts of Africa and is characterized by its yellowish-green foliage and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant is typically found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has a trailing or climbing growth habit, often forming dense mats or vines.

Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and it produces small, dry fruits called achenes.
