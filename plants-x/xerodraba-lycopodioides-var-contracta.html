Xerodraba lycopodioides var. contracta

Xerodraba lycopodioides var. contracta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved clubmoss, is a low-growing evergreen plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its compact, dense foliage and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called strobili, which are clustered at the tips of its stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
