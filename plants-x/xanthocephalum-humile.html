Xanthocephalum humile

Xanthocephalum humile, commonly known as the small-headed everlasting or small everlasting, is a low-growing, perennial herb native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in winter and early spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It is often found in sandy or gravelly soils in coastal and inland regions.

Xanthocephalum humile is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in native gardens and for erosion control.
