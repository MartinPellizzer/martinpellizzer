Xanthosoma maculatum

Xanthosoma maculatum, commonly known as the spotted arrowleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive spotted pattern. The plant produces a spadix inflorescence surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Xanthosoma maculatum is also valued for its edible leaves, which are used in traditional cuisines.
