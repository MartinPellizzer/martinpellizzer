Xanthosoma atrovirens var. versicolor

Xanthosoma atrovirens var. versicolor, commonly known as the variegated arrowleaf elephant ear, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that exhibit a striking variegation of green, yellow, and sometimes red or purple hues. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which includes many ornamental and edible species. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening for its dramatic visual impact.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil and consistent moisture.
