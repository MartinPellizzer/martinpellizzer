Xyris longibracteata

Xyris longibracteata, commonly known as the long-bract yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes various species of yellow-eyed grasses. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Xyris longibracteata is often found in wetlands, marshes, and other moist habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of wetland ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
