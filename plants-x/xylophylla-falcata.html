Xylophylla falcata

Xylophylla falcata, commonly known as the swordleaf fern, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, sword-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. Xylophylla falcata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in dense forest understories.

Its unique morphology and ecological niche make it a valuable subject for botanical study.
