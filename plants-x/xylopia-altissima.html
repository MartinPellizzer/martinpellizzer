Xylopia altissima

Xylopia altissima, commonly known as the African bitter almond or bitter kola, is a tropical evergreen tree native to western and central Africa.

It belongs to the family Annonaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The tree produces an aggregate fruit composed of several follicles, each containing a single seed. Its bark and seeds contain various bioactive compounds, making it valuable in traditional medicine.

Xylopia altissima is also used in local cuisines and for its aromatic properties.
