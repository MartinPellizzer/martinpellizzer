Xeranthemum proliferum

Xeranthemum proliferum, commonly known as the spreading xeranthemum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Xeranthemum proliferum is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
