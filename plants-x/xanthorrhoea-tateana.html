Xanthorrhoea tateana

Xanthorrhoea tateana, commonly known as the Tate's grass tree, is a unique monocot native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a dense crown of long, grass-like leaves. The plant produces a distinctive flowering spike that can reach several meters in height, bearing small, white or pale yellow flowers. Xanthorrhoea tateana is adapted to arid environments and has a slow growth rate, making it a long-lived species.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various native animals.
