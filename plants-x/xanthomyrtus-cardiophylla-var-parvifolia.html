Xanthomyrtus cardiophylla var. parvifolia

Xanthomyrtus cardiophylla var. parvifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Australia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, whitish pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by small, woody capsules containing elliptical seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental plant in horticultural settings.
