Xyris kornasiana

Xyris kornasiana, commonly known as the Kornasian yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various species of yellow-eyed grasses. This plant is characterized by its compact stature, with narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices.

Xyris kornasiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
