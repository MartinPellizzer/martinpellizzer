Xanthophyllum glandulosum

Xanthophyllum glandulosum, commonly known as the glandular yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its yellow, bell-shaped flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. The plant has glandular hairs on its stems and leaves, which give it its scientific name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
