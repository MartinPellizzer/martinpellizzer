Xanthoxalis rufa

Xanthoxalis rufa, commonly known as the redleaf false shamrock, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features trifoliate leaves with reddish veins and a distinctive yellow-orange flower cluster. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Xanthoxalis rufa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
