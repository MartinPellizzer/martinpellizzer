Xyris humilis

Xyris humilis, commonly known as the dwarf yellowroot, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of less than 10 inches. The plant features grass-like, linear leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in the spring. Xyris humilis thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for wetland ecosystems and a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects.
