Xanthophyllum laoticum

Xanthophyllum laoticum, commonly known as the Laotian goldenleaf, is a unique plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Xanthophyllaceae and is characterized by its striking yellow foliage, which gives it its common name. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, waxy coating that helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often blooming in late spring.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Xanthophyllum laoticum is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
