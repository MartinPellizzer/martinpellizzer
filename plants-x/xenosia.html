Xenosia

Xenosia, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its translucent, waxy leaves that appear to shimmer in the sunlight, giving it its ethereal appearance. The plant produces small, pale blooms that emit a faint bioluminescent glow at night, a unique adaptation to attract nocturnal pollinators. Xenosia thrives in cool, humid environments and requires specific soil conditions rich in organic matter.

Due to its elusive nature and limited habitat, Xenosia is considered a vulnerable species facing threats from climate change and habitat loss.
