Ximenia caffra var. glabra

Ximenia caffra var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth ximenia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and small, elliptical leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The smooth ximenia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
