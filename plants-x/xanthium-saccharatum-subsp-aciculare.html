Xanthium saccharatum subsp. aciculare

Xanthium saccharatum subsp.

aciculare, commonly known as the slender cocklebur, is a herbaceous annual plant native to North America. It is characterized by its spiny, elongated leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into rough, spherical fruits.

The plant thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides. Its seeds are dispersed by animals that eat the fruits, contributing to its invasive potential in some regions.

The species is also notable for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal remedies.
