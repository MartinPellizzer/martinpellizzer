Xanthophyllum geminatum

Xanthophyllum geminatum, commonly known as the twinleaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and bipinnate leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and forest edges. Its flowers are composite, consisting of both ray and disk florets, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Xanthophyllum geminatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
