Xeraea

Xeraea, commonly known as the desert rose, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It features fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, often blooming during the rainy season. Xeraea has a shallow root system that helps it absorb moisture from the soil quickly.

Its ability to thrive in low-water conditions makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping and desert gardens.
