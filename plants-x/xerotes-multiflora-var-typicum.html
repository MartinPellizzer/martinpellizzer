Xerotes multiflora var. typicum

Xerotes multiflora var. typicum, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that reduce water loss, adapting it to its harsh environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Xerotes multiflora var. typicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
