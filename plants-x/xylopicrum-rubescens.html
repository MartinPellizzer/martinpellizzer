Xylopicrum rubescens

Xylopicrum rubescens, commonly known as the crimson woodroot, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features a fibrous root system and upright, branching stems that are typically reddish in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range from pink to deep crimson, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Xylopicrum rubescens is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland and meadow habitats.
