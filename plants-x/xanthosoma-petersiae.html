Xanthosoma petersiae

Xanthosoma petersiae, commonly known as the giant arrowleaf, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant features large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, with a glossy, dark green surface. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Xanthosoma petersiae is also valued for its edible leaves, which are used in various traditional cuisines.
