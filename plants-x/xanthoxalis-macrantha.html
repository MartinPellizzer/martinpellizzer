Xanthoxalis macrantha

Xanthoxalis macrantha, commonly known as the large-flowered oxalis, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves, which are typical of the Oxalidaceae family. The plant produces small, spiny fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Xanthoxalis macrantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
