Xyridion ponticum

Xyridion ponticum, commonly known as the Pontic marsh grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southeastern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, fens, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring. It is often found in association with other wetland plants and plays a role in maintaining soil stability in these environments.

Xyridion ponticum is valued for its ecological importance in supporting diverse wetland ecosystems.
