Xiphotheca fruticosa

Xiphotheca fruticosa, commonly known as the prickly pear cactus, is a succulent plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of vibrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant stores water in its thick, fleshy stems, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its fruit, known as a tuna, is edible and often used in culinary applications.
