Xylosma racemosa var. caudata

Xylosma racemosa var. caudata, commonly known as the long-stalked xylosma, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Tiliaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, followed by spherical, woody fruits. The species is valued for its ornamental potential and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
