Xyris umbilonis

Xyris umbilonis, commonly known as the clustered yellowroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes various grass-like plants known for their showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are arranged in dense, spike-like clusters.

Xyris umbilonis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
