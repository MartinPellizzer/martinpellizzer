Xyris congensis

Xyris congensis, commonly known as the dwarf yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes grass-like plants known for their showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow blooms in early summer.

Xyris congensis is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
