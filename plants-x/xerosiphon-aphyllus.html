Xerosiphon aphyllus

Xerosiphon aphyllus, commonly known as the leafless desert morning glory, is a unique plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its lack of leaves, which is an adaptation to conserve water in its harsh environment. The plant produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the morning and close by midday. Its stems are succulent and can store water, allowing it to survive prolonged dry periods.

Despite its delicate appearance, it is a resilient species well-suited to extreme desert conditions.
