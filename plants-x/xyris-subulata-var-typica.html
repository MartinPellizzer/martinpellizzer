Xyris subulata var. typica

Xyris subulata var. typica, commonly known as the slender yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils in woodland or swampy areas. The plant features grass-like leaves that are narrow and pointed, with a fibrous root system. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of bright yellow flowers.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
