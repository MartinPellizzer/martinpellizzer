Xanthium microcarpum

Xanthium microcarpum, commonly known as leastseed cocklebur, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to North America and is characterized by its spiny, spherical fruit that contains a single small seed. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and other open areas. It has palmate leaves with lobes that are often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are small and yellow, arranged in composite flower heads typical of the Asteraceae family.
