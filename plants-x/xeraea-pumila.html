Xeraea pumila

Xeraea pumila, commonly known as the desert lily, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of the Americas.

It has thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink. Xeraea pumila grows close to the ground, often forming dense mats to reduce water loss.

It is well adapted to extreme temperatures and low precipitation, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
