Xyris subcomplanata

Xyris subcomplanata, commonly known as the flatleaf yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the genus Xyris, which is part of the family Xyridaceae, commonly known as the yellow-eyed grass family. The plant is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring. Xyris subcomplanata typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in wetlands.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
