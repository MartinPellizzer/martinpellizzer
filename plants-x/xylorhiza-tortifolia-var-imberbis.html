Xylorhiza tortifolia var. imberbis

Xylorhiza tortifolia var. imberbis, commonly known as the woolly root fern, is a unique species characterized by its fibrous, root-like structures that resemble rhizomes.

It is native to tropical regions and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, feathery fronds that contribute to its ornamental appeal. Its botanical structure includes a network of subterranean stems that support its growth and reproduction.

It is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in horticultural settings.
