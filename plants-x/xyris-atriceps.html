Xyris atriceps

Xyris atriceps, commonly known as the blackstem yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Xyris atriceps typically grows in wet, sandy soils along stream banks and in wetlands.

Its botanical structure is adapted to thrive in moist environments, making it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
