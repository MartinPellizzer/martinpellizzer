Xylopia dicarpa

Xylopia dicarpa, commonly known as the African bitter apple, is a member of the Annonaceae family and is native to tropical Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters. Its fruit is a smooth, round berry that turns from green to yellow when mature.

The plant is notable for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
