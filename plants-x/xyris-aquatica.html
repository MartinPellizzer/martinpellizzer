Xyris aquatica

Xyris aquatica, commonly known as the water yelloweye, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces clusters of yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Xyris aquatica is adapted to saturated soil conditions and plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, which can help prevent erosion.
