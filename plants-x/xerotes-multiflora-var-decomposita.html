Xerotes multiflora var. decomposita

Xerotes multiflora var. decomposita, commonly known as the decomposing wood fern, is a rare and specialized fern species that thrives in decaying organic matter.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that often appear in clusters on dead wood or leaf litter. The plant is adapted to low-light conditions and requires a consistently moist, shaded environment to flourish. Its spores are dispersed through the air, and it plays a role in nutrient cycling within forest ecosystems.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often found in old-growth forests and is considered an indicator of ecological stability.
