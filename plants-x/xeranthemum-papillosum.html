Xeranthemum papillosum

Xeranthemum papillosum, commonly known as the velvet tawny daisy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture, and it produces showy yellow-orange flowers with a central disk. Xeranthemum papillosum is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

It is often used in rock gardens and as a border plant due to its ornamental value and hardy nature.
