Xanthosoma amacayacuense

Xanthosoma amacayacuense, commonly known as the Amazonian arrowleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces striking inflorescences consisting of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in swampy areas and along riverbanks.

Xanthosoma amacayacuense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some indigenous communities.
