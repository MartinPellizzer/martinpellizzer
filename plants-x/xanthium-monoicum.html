Xanthium monoicum

Xanthium monoicum, commonly known as common cocklebur, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features prickly, spiny leaves and produces distinctive spherical fruits covered in hooked bracts. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in fields.

Xanthium monoicum is known for its adaptability and can be considered a weed in agricultural settings.
