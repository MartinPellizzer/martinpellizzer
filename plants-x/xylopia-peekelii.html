Xylopia peekelii

Xylopia peekelii, commonly known as the African bitter apple, is a member of the Annonaceae family and is native to tropical Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, with smooth, greyish bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by a distinctive, fleshy, orange-red fruit with a strong, bitter aroma.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticultural and ecological contexts.
