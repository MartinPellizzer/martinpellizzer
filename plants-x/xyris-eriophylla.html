Xyris eriophylla

Xyris eriophylla, commonly known as the narrowleaf yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various species of yellow-eyed grasses. The plant is characterized by its grass-like leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. Xyris eriophylla typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in wetlands.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
