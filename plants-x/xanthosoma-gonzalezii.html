Xanthosoma gonzalezii

Xanthosoma gonzalezii, commonly known as the Mexican arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces striking inflorescences with a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. It is often cultivated for its starchy corms, which are used in cooking and traditional medicine.

Xanthosoma gonzalezii thrives in moist, shaded environments and is well-suited for tropical gardens.
