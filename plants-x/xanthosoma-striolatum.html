Xanthosoma striolatum

Xanthosoma striolatum, commonly known as the arrowleaf elephant ear, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Xanthosoma striolatum is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
