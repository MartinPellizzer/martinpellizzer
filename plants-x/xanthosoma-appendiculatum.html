Xanthosoma appendiculatum

Xanthosoma appendiculatum, commonly known as the arrow leaf elephant's ear, is a tropical perennial plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, with a broad, waxy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that are often hidden beneath its foliage. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil and partial to full shade.

Xanthosoma appendiculatum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking foliage and is also used in traditional medicine.
