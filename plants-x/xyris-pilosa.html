Xyris pilosa

Xyris pilosa, commonly known as the hairy yellow-eyed grass, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a spike, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in wetlands, marshes, and other moist habitats, often forming dense colonies.

Xyris pilosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife and ecological balance.
