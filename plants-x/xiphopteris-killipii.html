Xiphopteris killipii

Xiphopteris killipii, commonly known as the Killip's huperzia, is a species of clubmoss native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and compact growth habit, typically forming low, mat-like structures. The plant has needle-like leaves that are often arranged in two rows along the stems, giving it a distinct appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest floors or along stream banks.

Xiphopteris killipii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in maintaining ecological balance in its native habitat.
