Xerophyta capillaris var. occulatans

Xerophyta capillaris var. occulatans, commonly known as the Cape snowflake, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, fleshy leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in arid environments. The plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in winter, adding ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens with rocky or sandy soils.
