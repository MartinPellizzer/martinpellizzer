Xanthoxalis brittoniae

Xanthoxalis brittoniae, commonly known as the Britton's goldencup, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Xanthoxalis brittoniae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
