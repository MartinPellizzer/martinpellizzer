Xyris decipiens

Xyris decipiens, commonly known as the false yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes several species of grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Xyris decipiens typically grows in moist, sandy soils along streambanks and in wetlands.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
