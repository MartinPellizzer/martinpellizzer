Xenoscapa grandiflora

Xenoscapa grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora scaposa, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant produces a single, elongated flower spike that can reach up to two meters in height. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Xenoscapa grandiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained, sunny environments.
