Xyris madagascariensis

Xyris madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in dense, spike-like clusters, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Xyris madagascariensis is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive floral display.
