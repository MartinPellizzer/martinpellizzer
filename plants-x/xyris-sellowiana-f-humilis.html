Xyris sellowiana f. humilis

Xyris sellowiana f. humilis, commonly known as the dwarf yellow-eyed grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in spikelets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover in humid climates.
