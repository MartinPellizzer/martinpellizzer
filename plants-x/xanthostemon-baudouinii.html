Xanthostemon baudouinii

Xanthostemon baudouinii, commonly known as the white ghost flower, is a striking plant native to northern Australia.

It is a shrub that features showy, white to cream-colored flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant.

Xanthostemon baudouinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
