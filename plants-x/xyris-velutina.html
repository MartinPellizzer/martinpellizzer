Xyris velutina

Xyris velutina, commonly known as the velvet yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes various species of grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Xyris velutina typically grows in moist, sandy soils in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
