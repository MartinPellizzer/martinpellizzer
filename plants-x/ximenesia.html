Ximenesia

Ximenesia, commonly known as the desert willow, is a genus of flowering plants native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its woody stems, which often have a rough, fissured bark, and its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink, red, or white. The leaves are typically small, narrow, and arranged oppositely along the branches. Ximenesia species are adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that allow them to survive in harsh desert environments.

These plants are often used in xeriscaping due to their low water requirements and ornamental value.
