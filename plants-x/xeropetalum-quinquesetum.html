Xeropetalum quinquesetum

Xeropetalum quinquesetum, commonly known as the five-lobed sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit and silver-green foliage, which helps it conserve moisture in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming during the rainy season. Its root system is extensive and adapted to absorb water efficiently from the sandy soil.

Xeropetalum quinquesetum is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
