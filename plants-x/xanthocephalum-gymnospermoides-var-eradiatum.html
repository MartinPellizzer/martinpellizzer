Xanthocephalum gymnospermoides var. eradiatum

Xanthocephalum gymnospermoides var. eradiatum, commonly known as the coastal goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellowish-green foliage and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along the coastline, thriving in saline and well-drained conditions. It is often used in coastal landscaping due to its hardiness and ability to stabilize dunes.

Its botanical features make it an important component of coastal ecosystems.
