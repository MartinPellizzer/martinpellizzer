Xeromphis obovata

Xeromphis obovata, commonly known as the narrowleaf evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, narrow leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant has a robust root system that helps it survive in arid environments. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds.

Xeromphis obovata is adapted to dry conditions, making it a hardy species in xerophytic habitats.
