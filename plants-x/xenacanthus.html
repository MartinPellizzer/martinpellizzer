Xenacanthus

Xenacanthus, commonly known as the sea scorpion, is an extinct genus of jawless fish that lived during the Paleozoic era.

It is characterized by its elongated body and a distinctive, serrated dorsal fin. The plant-like structures found in Xenacanthus fossils suggest it may have had some form of photosynthetic capability, though it is primarily classified as a marine organism. Its fossils are often found in shallow marine sediments, indicating it thrived in coastal environments.

The study of Xenacanthus provides insights into the evolution of early marine vertebrates and their ecological roles.
