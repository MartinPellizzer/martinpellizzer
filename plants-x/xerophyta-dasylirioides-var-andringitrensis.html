Xerophyta dasylirioides var. andringitrensis

Xerophyta dasylirioides var. andringitrensis, commonly known as the Andringa lily, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss, a typical adaptation for xerophytic plants.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping in dry climates.
