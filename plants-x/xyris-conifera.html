Xyris conifera

Xyris conifera, commonly known as the coniferous yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Xyris conifera typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for wetland ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
