Xerophyta dasylirioides var. pectinata

Xerophyta dasylirioides var. pectinata, commonly known as the woolly fireplant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant has fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water, and its leaves are often covered in a thick layer of hair. It produces small, yellowish flowers during the summer months.

This variety is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.
