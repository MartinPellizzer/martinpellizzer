Xyris submontana

Xyris submontana, commonly known as the mountain goldenroot, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its upright, clump-forming growth habit. The plant produces showy, yellowish-green to golden inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Xyris submontana is often found in woodland edges and rocky hillsides, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
