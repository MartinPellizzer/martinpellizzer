Xylomelum angustifolium

Xylomelum angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved xylomelum, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in spring. It is adapted to grow in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in heathlands and dry forests.

Xylomelum angustifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
