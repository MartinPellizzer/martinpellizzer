Xylopicrum grandiflorum

Xylopicrum grandiflorum, commonly known as the giant floral wood, is a striking plant native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its towering, woody stem that can reach heights of up to 15 meters. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in clusters at the top of the stem. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, providing a lush canopy that supports various epiphytic species.

Xylopicrum grandiflorum is also notable for its unique ability to absorb and store significant amounts of water, aiding in its survival in humid, nutrient-poor environments.
