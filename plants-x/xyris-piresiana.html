Xyris piresiana

Xyris piresiana, commonly known as the Pires' yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various species of grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Xyris piresiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
