Xanthosoma pilosum

Xanthosoma pilosum, commonly known as the velvet leaf taro, is a tropical perennial plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, fuzzy texture, giving it a velvety appearance. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are typically borne on tall, leafless stalks. Its rhizomes are edible and are often used in traditional cuisines.

Xanthosoma pilosum thrives in moist, shady environments and is commonly cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
