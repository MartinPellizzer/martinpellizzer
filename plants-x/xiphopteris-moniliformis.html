Xiphopteris moniliformis

Xiphopteris moniliformis, commonly known as the chain fern, is a species of fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, cascading fronds that resemble a chain, hence its common name. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with horizontal stems that produce both roots and new shoots. Its leaves are typically bright green and can grow up to several feet in length.

Xiphopteris moniliformis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
