Xanthosoma australe

Xanthosoma australe, commonly known as the giant taro, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in diameter. The plant produces striking, yellowish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Xanthosoma australe is valued for its edible leaves, which are used in various traditional cuisines.
