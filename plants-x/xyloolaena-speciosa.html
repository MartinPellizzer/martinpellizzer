Xyloolaena speciosa

Xyloolaena speciosa, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a vibrant display during the warmer months. The plant has a woody stem with a rough, fissured bark that becomes more pronounced with age. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, often with a slightly waxy texture.

Xyloolaena speciosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
