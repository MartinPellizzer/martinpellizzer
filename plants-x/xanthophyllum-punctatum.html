Xanthophyllum punctatum

Xanthophyllum punctatum, commonly known as the spotted goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its showy flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it an important species in ecological habitats.
