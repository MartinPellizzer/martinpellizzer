Xiphion krelagii

Xiphion krelagii, commonly known as the Krelagii bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, often with a yellow throat. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Xiphion krelagii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
