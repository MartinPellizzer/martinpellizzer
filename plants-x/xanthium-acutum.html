Xanthium acutum

Xanthium acutum, commonly known as the prickly lettuce, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a hairy, branching stem and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its fruit is a spiny, three-lobed achene that is often used as a weed in agricultural fields.

Xanthium acutum is adapted to thrive in disturbed soils and is known for its rapid growth and resilience.
