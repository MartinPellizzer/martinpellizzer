Xyris robusta

Xyris robusta, commonly known as the hardy yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. The plant features a rosette of grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers in the summer months. Its inflorescence consists of several spikelets, each bearing small, clustered flowers.

Xyris robusta is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife and wetland ecosystems.
