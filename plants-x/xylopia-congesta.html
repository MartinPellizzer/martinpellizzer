Xylopia congesta

Xylopia congesta, commonly known as the African pepper or black pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and Southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Anacardiaceae and is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its bark and leaves contain compounds with potential medicinal properties, including antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects.

Xylopia congesta thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
