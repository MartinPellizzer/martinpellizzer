Xanthosoma clarense

Xanthosoma clarense, commonly known as the Clarin's arrowleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces striking spadices, which are modified leaves that enclose the inflorescence. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Xanthosoma clarense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
