Xuarezia semiserrata

Xuarezia semiserrata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf chenopod, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including high temperatures and low precipitation.

Xuarezia semiserrata plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
