Xyris guaranitica

Xyris guaranitica, commonly known as the Guarani yellowroot, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various grass-like plants. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshy areas or along riverbanks.

Xyris guaranitica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
