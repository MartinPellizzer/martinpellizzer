Ximenia americana var. microphylla

Ximenia americana var. microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf ximenia, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and small, elliptical leaves that are usually less than 2 inches in length. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits that turn red when mature. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures for its medicinal properties.

The species is well adapted to dry, sunny conditions and can thrive in poor soils.
