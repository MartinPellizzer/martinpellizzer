Xanthostemon elegans subsp. latifolius

Xanthostemon elegans subsp.

latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf xanthostemon, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family. It is native to northern Australia, particularly in the Northern Territory and Queensland.

The plant is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish pubescence. It produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that are typically arranged in clusters, attracting a variety of pollinators.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in subtropical gardens.
