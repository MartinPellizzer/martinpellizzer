Xanthophyllum andamanicum

Xanthophyllum andamanicum, commonly known as the Andaman yellow orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andaman Islands.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking yellow petals and sepals. The plant typically grows in tropical rainforest environments, thriving in humid and shaded conditions. Its flowers are usually small but highly fragrant, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
