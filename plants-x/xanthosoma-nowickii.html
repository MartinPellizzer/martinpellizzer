Xanthosoma nowickii

Xanthosoma nowickii, commonly known as the giant swamp taro, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1.5 meters in length. The plant produces striking, spatulate inflorescences with a yellow spathe and a white spadix, which are typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in wet, swampy environments and is often found in marshes and along riverbanks.

Xanthosoma nowickii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
