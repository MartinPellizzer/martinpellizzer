Xanthophyllum velutinum

Xanthophyllum velutinum, commonly known as the velvetleaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust growth habits. The plant features large, showy yellow-orange flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically velvety and dark green, giving it its common name.

Xanthophyllum velutinum is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
