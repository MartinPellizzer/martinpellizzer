Xyris coronata

Xyris coronata, commonly known as the golden-eyed marsh yelloweye, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and swampy areas, thriving in moist, acidic soils. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in the spring. Its flowering stems are often adorned with a crown-like structure, giving it its scientific name.

Xyris coronata plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
