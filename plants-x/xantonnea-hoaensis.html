Xantonnea hoaensis

Xantonnea hoaensis, commonly known as the Hoaensis flameflower, is a tropical plant native to the island of Hoa in the South China Sea.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and arranged oppositely along the stem. Its flowering period typically occurs during the warmer months, and it thrives in humid, well-drained soil.

Xantonnea hoaensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and tropical landscapes.
