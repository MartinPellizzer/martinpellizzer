Xanthosoma riedelianum

Xanthosoma riedelianum, commonly known as the giant arrowleaf, is a tropical perennial herb native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length, with a smooth, waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers followed by edible, starchy tubers underground. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a food source.

Xanthosoma riedelianum is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
