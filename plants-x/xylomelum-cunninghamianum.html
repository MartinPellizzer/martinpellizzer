Xylomelum cunninghamianum

Xylomelum cunninghamianum, commonly known as the silver-leafed xylomelum, is a small evergreen shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves that are covered in a silvery-white wax, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and dry forests.

Xylomelum cunninghamianum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
