Xyris connectens

Xyris connectens, commonly known as the clustered yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes various species of grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Xyris connectens typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
