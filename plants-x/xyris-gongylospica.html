Xyris gongylospica

Xyris gongylospica, commonly known as the narrowleaf yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower clusters that bloom in late spring to early summer. Xyris gongylospica typically grows in moist, sandy soils along streambanks and in wetlands.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small invertebrates.
