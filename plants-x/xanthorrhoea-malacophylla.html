Xanthorrhoea malacophylla

Xanthorrhoea malacophylla, commonly known as the black grass tree, is a unique and ancient plant native to Western Australia.

It is a member of the Xanthorrhoeaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces a distinctive cone-shaped inflorescence that blooms in late winter or early spring. Xanthorrhoea malacophylla is well adapted to arid environments, with deep roots that allow it to survive in harsh, dry conditions.

Its slow growth rate and longevity make it a symbol of resilience in the Australian landscape.
