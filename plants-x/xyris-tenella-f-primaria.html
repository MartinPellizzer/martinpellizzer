Xyris tenella f. primaria

Xyris tenella f. primaria, commonly known as the narrowleaf yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Xyridaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in wetland environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its inflorescence consists of dense clusters of bright yellow blooms.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal.
