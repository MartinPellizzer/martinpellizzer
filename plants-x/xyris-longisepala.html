Xyris longisepala

Xyris longisepala, commonly known as the long-sepaled yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae, which includes various species of grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellowish to orange flowers in late spring to early summer. Xyris longisepala typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions.

Its botanical significance lies in its ecological role and its use in wetland restoration projects.
