Xanthomyrtus taxifolia

Xanthomyrtus taxifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia, where it typically grows in coastal heathlands and dry forests. The plant is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds.

Xanthomyrtus taxifolia is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
