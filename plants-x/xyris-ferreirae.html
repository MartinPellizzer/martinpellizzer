Xyris ferreirae

Xyris ferreirae, commonly known as the Ferreira's yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various species of grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks.

Xyris ferreirae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
