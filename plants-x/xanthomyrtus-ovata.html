Xanthomyrtus ovata

Xanthomyrtus ovata, commonly known as the golden myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the rainforests of eastern Australia.

It features glossy, ovate leaves that are typically a dark green with a slight waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, yellow-orange berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal and subtropical regions.

Xanthomyrtus ovata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and seasonal flowers.
