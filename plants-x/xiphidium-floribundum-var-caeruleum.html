Xiphidium floribundum var. caeruleum

Xiphidium floribundum var. caeruleum, commonly known as the blue-flowered xiphidium, is a species native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while the stems are sturdy and branching.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and its potential use in traditional medicine.
