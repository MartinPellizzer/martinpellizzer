Xerosicyos decaryi

Xerosicyos decaryi, commonly known as the desert bottle plant, is a unique succulent native to arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in drought conditions. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting pollinators like moths. Its leaves are reduced to spines, minimizing water loss through transpiration.

Xerosicyos decaryi is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
