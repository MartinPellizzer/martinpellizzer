Xanthomyrtus cardiophylla

Xanthomyrtus cardiophylla, commonly known as the heart-leaved myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to eastern Australia.

It features glossy, elliptical leaves that are often heart-shaped, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, fleshy berries. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.

Xanthomyrtus cardiophylla prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
