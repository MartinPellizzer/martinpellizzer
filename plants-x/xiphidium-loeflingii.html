Xiphidium loeflingii

Xiphidium loeflingii, commonly known as the Loefling's cactus, is a flowering plant native to the deserts of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spineless, cylindrical stems that store water efficiently. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are modified into spines, which help reduce water loss and deter herbivores.

Xiphidium loeflingii is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.
