Xiphopteris nudicarpa

Xiphopteris nudicarpa, commonly known as the naked-fruited huperzia, is a species of clubmoss belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, scale-like leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its leaves, which are clustered in strobila, a distinctive feature of many fern allies. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in temperate forests.

Xiphopteris nudicarpa is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
