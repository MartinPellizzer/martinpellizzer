Xyris foveolata

Xyris foveolata, commonly known as the yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the genus Xyris in the family Xyridaceae, which includes various species of grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Xyris foveolata is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands.
