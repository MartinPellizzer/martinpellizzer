Xanthomyrtus schlechteri

Xanthomyrtus schlechteri, commonly known as the yellow myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the rainforests of New Guinea.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that turns red when mature.

Xanthomyrtus schlechteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens for its attractive foliage and fragrance.
