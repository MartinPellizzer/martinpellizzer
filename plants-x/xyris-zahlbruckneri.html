Xyris zahlbruckneri

Xyris zahlbruckneri, commonly known as the Zahler's goldenroot, is a rare and striking member of the Xyridaceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, specifically Florida and Georgia, where it thrives in wetlands and pine flatwoods. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces vibrant yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and limited distribution, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered, highlighting the need for its protection.
