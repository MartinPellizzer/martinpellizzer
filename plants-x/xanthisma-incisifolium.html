Xanthisma incisifolium

Xanthisma incisifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its succulent, cylindrical stems and narrow, linear leaves with distinct serrations. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is adapted to hot, dry environments and has a deep root system to access groundwater.
