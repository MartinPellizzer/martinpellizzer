Xolantha glomerata

Xolantha glomerata, commonly known as the clustered lily, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, elongated berries that contain the seeds.

Xolantha glomerata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climates.
