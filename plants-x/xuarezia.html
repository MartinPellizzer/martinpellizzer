Xuarezia

Xuarezia, commonly known as the Mexican feathergrass, is a genus of grasses native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and fluffy, feathery seed heads that give it a distinctive appearance. The plants typically grow in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Xuarezia species are perennial, often forming dense clumps with narrow, grass-like leaves.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes used in xeriscaping due to their drought tolerance.
