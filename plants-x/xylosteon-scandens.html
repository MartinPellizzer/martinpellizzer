Xylosteon scandens

Xylosteon scandens, commonly known as the climbing xylosteon, is a woody vine native to tropical regions.

It features a climbing habit with long, flexible stems that can ascend trees or other structures. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets, and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule containing numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Fabaceae, characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
