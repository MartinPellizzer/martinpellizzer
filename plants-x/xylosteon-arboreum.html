Xylosteon arboreum

Xylosteon arboreum, commonly known as the tree spurge, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stem and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has alternate, simple leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. It is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system for water absorption.

Xylosteon arboreum is also known for its toxic sap, which can cause skin irritation.
