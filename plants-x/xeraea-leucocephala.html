Xeraea leucocephala

Xeraea leucocephala, commonly known as the white-headed bush, is a shrub native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its dense, spreading growth habit and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

Xeraea leucocephala is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
