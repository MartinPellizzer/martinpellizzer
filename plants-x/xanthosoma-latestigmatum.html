Xanthosoma latestigmatum

Xanthosoma latestigmatum, commonly known as the red-veined taro, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves with striking red veins. The plant produces a spadix inflorescence surrounded by a colorful spathe, typical of the arum family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its edible corms.

The plant's ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant foliage and unique floral structure.
