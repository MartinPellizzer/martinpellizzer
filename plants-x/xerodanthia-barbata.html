Xerodanthia barbata

Xerodanthia barbata, commonly known as the barbed sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, adding a subtle ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated as a low-maintenance ornamental plant.
