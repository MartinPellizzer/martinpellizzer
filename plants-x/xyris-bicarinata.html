Xyris bicarinata

Xyris bicarinata, commonly known as the two-ribbed yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the genus Xyris in the family Xyridaceae, which includes various species of yellow-eyed grasses. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist habitats, thriving in sandy or peaty soils. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in spike-like inflorescences, often blooming in late spring to early summer.

Xyris bicarinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
