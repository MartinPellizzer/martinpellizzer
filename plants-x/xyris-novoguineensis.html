Xyris novoguineensis

Xyris novoguineensis, commonly known as the New Guinea marsh yelloweye, is a flowering plant native to New Guinea.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes grass-like plants often found in wetland habitats. The plant typically grows in swampy or marshy areas, thriving in moist, acidic soils. Its flowers are yellow and clustered in dense inflorescences, adding ornamental value to its natural environment.

Xyris novoguineensis is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.
