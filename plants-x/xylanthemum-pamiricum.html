Xylanthemum pamiricum

Xylanthemum pamiricum, commonly known as the Pamir rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish to orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is adapted to arid and rocky environments, showcasing a high degree of drought tolerance.

Its foliage consists of narrow, leathery leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh habitat.
