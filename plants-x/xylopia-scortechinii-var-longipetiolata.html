Xylopia scortechinii var. longipetiolata

Xylopia scortechinii var. longipetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled xylopia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central America and northern South America.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with long petioles. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, followed by dark, fleshy berries. Its bark is thick and deeply fissured, often exuding a strong, aromatic scent.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
