Xeranthemum annuum var. orientale

Xeranthemum annuum var. orientale, commonly known as the oriental sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.
