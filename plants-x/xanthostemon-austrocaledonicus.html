Xanthostemon austrocaledonicus

Xanthostemon austrocaledonicus, commonly known as the New Caledonian flamewood, is a striking shrub or small tree native to the island of New Caledonia.

It is characterized by its showy, red-orange flowers that appear in clusters, adding vibrant color to its native habitat. The plant has rough, bark-like stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the tropical climate of its region.

Xanthostemon austrocaledonicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the unique biodiversity of New Caledonia.
