Xyris ornithoptera

Xyris ornithoptera, commonly known as the birdwing yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense spikes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Xyris ornithoptera is often found in moist, sandy soils in pine flatwoods and wetlands.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is of interest in ecological restoration projects.
