Xylophragma unifoliolatum

Xylophragma unifoliolatum, commonly known as the single leaf spurge, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
