Ximenia caffra var. longipedicellata

Ximenia caffra var. longipedicellata, commonly known as the long-peduncled ximenia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and small, oval-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by edible, fleshy berries that are yellow when ripe. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses.
