Xanthoxalis biflexa

Xanthoxalis biflexa, commonly known as the bentleaf false sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wetlands. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators.

Xanthoxalis biflexa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
