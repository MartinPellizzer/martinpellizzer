Xyris nivea

Xyris nivea, commonly known as the snow flat sedge, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. It is a perennial herb that blooms from late spring to early summer.

Xyris nivea plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various insects and wildlife.
