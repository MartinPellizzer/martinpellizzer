Xolisma fruticosa

Xolisma fruticosa, commonly known as the desert holly, is a shrub native to the arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries that attract various wildlife. Xolisma fruticosa is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water.

Its structure and features make it an important component of desert ecosystems.
