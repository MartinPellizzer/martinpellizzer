Xerophyta hereroensis

Xerophyta hereroensis, commonly known as the Herero's veldt grass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to arid environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically during the rainy season. It is well-suited to dry climates and is often found in grasslands and savanna habitats.

Xerophyta hereroensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for grazing animals.
