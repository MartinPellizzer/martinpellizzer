Xanthopsis xanthocephaloides

Xanthopsis xanthocephaloides, commonly known as the golden-headed sandmat, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, a characteristic adaptation to minimize water loss.

Xanthopsis xanthocephaloides is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
