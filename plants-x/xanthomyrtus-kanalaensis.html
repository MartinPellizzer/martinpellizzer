Xanthomyrtus kanalaensis

Xanthomyrtus kanalaensis, commonly known as the Kanala myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and mesic forest habitats, preferring well-drained soils. It is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional Hawaiian practices.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species.
