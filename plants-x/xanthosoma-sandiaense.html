Xanthosoma sandiaense

Xanthosoma sandiaense, commonly known as the sandiaense taro, is a tropical plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces striking white or pink spadices surrounded by a colorful spathe, which is typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its edible corms.

Xanthosoma sandiaense is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
