Xyris minarum

Xyris minarum, commonly known as the slender marsh yelloweye, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Xyridaceae family, which includes various grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to saturated soils. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer, producing small, clustered inflorescences.

Xyris minarum is often found in areas with high water tables and is adapted to periodic flooding.
