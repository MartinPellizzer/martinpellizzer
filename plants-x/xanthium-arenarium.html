Xanthium arenarium

Xanthium arenarium, commonly known as sand bur, is an annual herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its prickly, spiny burs that enclose the seeds. The plant typically grows in sandy or disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides. Its leaves are alternate, rough, and covered with fine hairs, while the stems are erect and branched.

The flowers are small and yellow, arranged in composite heads, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
