Xylopia orestera

Xylopia orestera, commonly known as the African bitter almond or bitter wood, is a member of the Anacardiaceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions of Africa. The plant is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually greenish-yellow, and it produces small, round fruits that contain toxic seeds.

Due to its medicinal properties and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
