Xylinabaria bantamensis

Xylinabaria bantamensis, commonly known as the Bantam bamboo, is a unique plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that form dense clumps. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated, and its leaves are narrow and elongated, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
