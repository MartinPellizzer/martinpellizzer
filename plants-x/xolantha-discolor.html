Xolantha discolor

Xolantha discolor, commonly known as the variegated xolantha, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage with variegated patterns of green, yellow, and white. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. Xolantha discolor thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate sunlight for optimal growth.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
