Xylopia cuspidata

Xylopia cuspidata, commonly known as the African allspice, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the family Annonaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, dark brown, woody fruits that resemble small peppers. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are often clustered together. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The fruit is used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to allspice.
