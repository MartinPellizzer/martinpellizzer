Xiphion foetidissimum

Xiphion foetidissimum, commonly known as the stinking beauty, is a perennial plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and are often tinged with red. The plant produces a strong, unpleasant odor, which is believed to attract specific pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal environments.

Xiphion foetidissimum is valued for its ornamental appeal despite its pungent fragrance.
