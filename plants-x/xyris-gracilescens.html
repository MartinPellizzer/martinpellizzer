Xyris gracilescens

Xyris gracilescens, commonly known as slender marsh yelloweye, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Xyris gracilescens plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
