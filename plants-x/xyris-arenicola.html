Xyris arenicola

Xyris arenicola, commonly known as sand flat sedge, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in sandy or well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas. The plant has grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring. It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants.

Xyris arenicola is adapted to full sun and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
