Xanthoxalis pilosa

Xanthoxalis pilosa, commonly known as the hairy yellow oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves with a distinctive yellowish-green color. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Xanthoxalis pilosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
