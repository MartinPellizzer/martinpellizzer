Xylopia malayana var. macrocarpa

Xylopia malayana var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited xylopia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces fragrant, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by large, woody, globose fruits. Its fruit contains a single seed enclosed in a fleshy, aromatic aril, which is an adaptation for animal dispersal.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
