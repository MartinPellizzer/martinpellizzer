Xyris bahiana

Xyris bahiana, commonly known as the Bahian yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Xyris bahiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
