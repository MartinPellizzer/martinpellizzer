Xylopia wilwerthii

Xylopia wilwerthii, commonly known as the African bitter almond, is a member of the Annonaceae family and is native to tropical Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a rough, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

The plant has traditional medicinal uses and is also valued for its ornamental qualities.
