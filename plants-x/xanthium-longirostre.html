Xanthium longirostre

Xanthium longirostre, commonly known as the common cocklebur, is a herbaceous annual plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and spiny structures. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and prominent veins. Its fruit is a spiny, elongated bur that can adhere to animals and clothing, aiding in seed dispersal.

Xanthium longirostre thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in cultivated fields.
