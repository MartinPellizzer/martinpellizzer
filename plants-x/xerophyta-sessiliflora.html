Xerophyta sessiliflora

Xerophyta sessiliflora, commonly known as the red sand ratplant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it ideal for xeriscaping.

Xerophyta sessiliflora is often used in rock gardens and as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
