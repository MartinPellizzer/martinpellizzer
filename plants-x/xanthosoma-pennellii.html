Xanthosoma pennellii

Xanthosoma pennellii, commonly known as the giant taro, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can reach up to 2 meters in length. The plant produces striking yellow spadices surrounded by broad, white or green bracts, which are typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its edible corms.

Xanthosoma pennellii is also valued for its ornamental appeal in tropical gardens.
