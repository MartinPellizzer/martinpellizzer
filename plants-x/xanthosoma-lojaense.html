Xanthosoma lojaense

Xanthosoma lojaense, commonly known as the Loja arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrowhead-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking inflorescences with a spathe and spadix, typical of the Araceae family. Its rhizomes are edible and have been used traditionally for their starchy content.

Xanthosoma lojaense thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical regions of South America.
