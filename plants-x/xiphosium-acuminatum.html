Xiphosium acuminatum

Xiphosium acuminatum, commonly known as the sharp-pointed spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa.

It features upright, branched stems that are typically covered in a white, powdery coating. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by capsules containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a distinctive acuminate tip.

Xiphosium acuminatum is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
