Xyridion gramineum

Xyridion gramineum, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. It is part of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and rushes.

Xyridion gramineum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
