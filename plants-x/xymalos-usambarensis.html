Xymalos usambarensis

Xymalos usambarensis, commonly known as the Usambara mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Usambara Mountains in Tanzania.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and showy, pale pink to white flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oval seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.

Xymalos usambarensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
