Xerosiphon gracilis

Xerosiphon gracilis, commonly known as the slender glassworts, is a halophytic plant adapted to grow in saline and alkaline environments.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that store water, aiding survival in arid conditions. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, typically during the summer months. Xerosiphon gracilis is often found in coastal areas, salt marshes, and other saline soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of ecosystems in harsh, water-scarce habitats.
