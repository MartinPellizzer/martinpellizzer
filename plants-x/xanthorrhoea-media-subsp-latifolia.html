Xanthorrhoea media subsp.

latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf grass tree, is a perennial plant native to the coastal regions of southern Australia. It is characterized by its tall, grass-like foliage and a distinctive, woody flowering spike that emerges from the base.

latifolia is also known for its ability to store water in its stem, making it well adapted to dry environments.
