Xyris gracilis

Xyris gracilis, commonly known as slender yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Xyridaceae family, which includes various grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in spikelets, giving it a delicate appearance.

Xyris gracilis is often found in sunny to partially shaded habitats and is valued for its ornamental qualities in wetland gardens.
