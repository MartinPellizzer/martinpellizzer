Xanthium orientale subsp. riparium

Xanthium orientale subsp.

riparium, commonly known as water bur, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its prickly, spiny bracts that enclose the small, oval-shaped seeds.

The plant typically grows in moist environments such as riverbanks and wet meadows, thriving in areas with high water tables. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered with fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through wind-dispersed seeds, making it a common weed in disturbed habitats.
