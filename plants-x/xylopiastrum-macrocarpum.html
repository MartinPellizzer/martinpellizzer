Xylopiastrum macrocarpum

Xylopiastrum macrocarpum, commonly known as the large-fruited xylopia, is a tropical plant belonging to the Anacardiaceae family.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and distinctive, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that are often fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths. Its fruit is a drupe, typically large and fleshy, containing a single seed.

The leaves are leathery and have a rough texture, which helps it thrive in humid, shaded environments.
