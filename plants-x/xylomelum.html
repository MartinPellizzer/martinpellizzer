Xylomelum

Xylomelum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed honeywood, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Myrtaceae.

These plants are native to Australia and are characterized by their aromatic leaves and small, showy flowers. Xylomelum species typically have smooth, often greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are usually narrow and leathery. They are often found in dry, open habitats such as forests and woodlands.

The flowers, which are usually white or pink, attract nectar-feeding birds and insects, contributing to their ecological significance.
