Xeranthus salicosus

Xeranthus salicosus, commonly known as the Salicaceous Xeranthus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and scrublands. It has a fibrous root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Xeranthus salicosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
