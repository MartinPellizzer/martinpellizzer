Xysmalobium fluviale

Xysmalobium fluviale, commonly known as the river sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils near rivers and streams. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of silky hair.

Xysmalobium fluviale is adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained environments and is often found in riparian habitats.
