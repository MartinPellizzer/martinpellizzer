Xanthosoma × brownii

Xanthosoma × brownii, commonly known as the giant taro, is a hybrid plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a large, herbaceous perennial that can grow up to 3 meters tall, featuring broad, arrowhead-shaped leaves with a smooth, waxy surface. The plant produces large, showy white or pale yellow flowers that are typically borne on tall, erect stems. Its rhizomes are edible and are often used in traditional cuisines, while the leaves are also consumed after cooking.

Xanthosoma × brownii thrives in moist, shady environments and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
