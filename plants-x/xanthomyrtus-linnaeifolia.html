Xanthomyrtus linnaeifolia

Xanthomyrtus linnaeifolia, commonly known as the golden myrtle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, often with a slight waxy coating.

Xanthomyrtus linnaeifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticultural settings for its aesthetic qualities.
