Xylopia carinata

Xylopia carinata, commonly known as the African bitter almond, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to western and central Africa.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant leaves and distinctive, ridged fruits. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, and its fruits are berries that contain a single seed.

Xylopia carinata is valued for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional medicine to treat ailments such as coughs and skin conditions.
