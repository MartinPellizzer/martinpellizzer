Xyphanthus hederifolius

Xyphanthus hederifolius, commonly known as the ivy-leaved daphne, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, ivy-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces fragrant, showy clusters of white or pale pink flowers during the winter months. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
