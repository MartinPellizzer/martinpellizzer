Xyris celiae

Xyris celiae, commonly known as the Celia's goldenroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes many species of grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Xyris celiae is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and is often found in moist, shaded woodlands.

Its botanical significance lies in its ecological role and its potential for use in native plant landscaping.
