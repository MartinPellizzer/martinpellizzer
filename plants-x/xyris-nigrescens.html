Xyris nigrescens

Xyris nigrescens, commonly known as blackleaf goldeneye, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the genus Xyris within the family Xyridaceae, which includes various grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, and is often found in acidic soils.

Xyris nigrescens is characterized by its distinctive dark green foliage and is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
