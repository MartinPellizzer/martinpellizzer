Xyris makuensis

Xyris makuensis, commonly known as the Maku marsh yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa, particularly South Africa.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters, and bloom during the warmer months.

Xyris makuensis is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.
