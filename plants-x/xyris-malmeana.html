Xyris malmeana

Xyris malmeana, commonly known as the Malme's yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Xyridaceae family, which includes various grass-like plants known for their showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Xyris malmeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
