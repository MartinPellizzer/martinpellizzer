Xyris sincorana

Xyris sincorana, commonly known as the yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is a member of the Xyridaceae family, which includes grass-like plants with showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds. Its flowers are yellow and clustered in dense, spike-like arrangements, which attract various pollinators.

Xyris sincorana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
