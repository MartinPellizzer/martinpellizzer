Xolanthes racemosa

Xolanthes racemosa, commonly known as the clustered lily, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, thriving in humid, tropical climates. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy texture.

Xolanthes racemosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
