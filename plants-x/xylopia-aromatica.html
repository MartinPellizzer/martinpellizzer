Xylopia aromatica

Xylopia aromatica, commonly known as the West African allspice, is a tropical evergreen tree native to regions of West Africa.

It belongs to the family Annonaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, woody fruits that are often used as a spice. The plant features simple, alternate leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit, which is typically harvested when fully mature, has a complex flavor reminiscent of cinnamon, cloves, and nutmeg.

The tree is valued not only for its culinary uses but also for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
