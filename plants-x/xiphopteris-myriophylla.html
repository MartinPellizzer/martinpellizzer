Xiphopteris myriophylla

Xiphopteris myriophylla, commonly known as the slender cliff brake, is a species of fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on rocky slopes or in woodland areas. Its reproductive structures, including spore-producing sorus, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Xiphopteris myriophylla plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native ecosystems.
