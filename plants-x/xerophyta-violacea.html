Xerophyta violacea

Xerophyta violacea, commonly known as the violet bulbous plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, bulb-like stems that store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces clusters of violet-blue flowers during the rainy season, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically small and covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Xerophyta violacea is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
