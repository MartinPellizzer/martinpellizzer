Xeromphis tomentosa

Xeromphis tomentosa, commonly known as woolly morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage and trumpet-shaped blue to violet flowers. The plant is an annual herb that thrives in moist, sunny environments such as wetlands and marshes. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine, white pubescence.

Xeromphis tomentosa plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in wetland restoration projects.
