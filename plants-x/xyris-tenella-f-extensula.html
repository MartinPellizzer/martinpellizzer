Xyris tenella f. extensula

Xyris tenella f. extensula, commonly known as the narrowleaf yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Xyridaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in wet, swampy areas and has slender, grass-like leaves that are often clustered at the base. Its inflorescence consists of a dense spike of bright yellow blooms, which attract various pollinators.

This variant is distinguished by its narrower leaves compared to the typical form of Xyris tenella.
