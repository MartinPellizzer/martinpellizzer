Xyris vivipara

Xyris vivipara, commonly known as the viviparous yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Xyridaceae and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. One notable botanical feature is its ability to produce viviparous offspring, where seeds germinate while still attached to the parent plant.

This unique reproductive strategy aids in its propagation and survival in wetland habitats.
