Xanthium cloessplateaum

Xanthium cloessplateaum, commonly known as cocklebur, is a flowering plant in the dandelion family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its spiny, rough leaves and distinctive spherical, spiky fruits that contain seeds. The plant is native to North America but has spread to other parts of the world. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Xanthium cloessplateaum is known for its ability to produce a large number of seeds, which can remain viable in the soil for several years.
