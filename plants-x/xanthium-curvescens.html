Xanthium curvescens

Xanthium curvescens, commonly known as common cocklebur, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is an annual herb that grows up to 2 meters tall, featuring rough, hairy stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces spiny, spherical fruits that contain seeds with a distinctive hooked beak. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in fields.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural areas.
