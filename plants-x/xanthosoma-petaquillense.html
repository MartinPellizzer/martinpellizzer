Xanthosoma petaquillense

Xanthosoma petaquillense, commonly known as the Petaquilla arrowleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces striking, spadix-like flowers surrounded by a colorful spathe, typical of the genus Xanthosoma. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for its foliage and as a decorative plant.
