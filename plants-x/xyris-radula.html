Xyris radula

Xyris radula, commonly known as the narrowleaf marsh yelloweye, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Xyridaceae family, which includes grass-like plants often found in moist environments. The plant produces yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically growing in dense clusters near the base of the plant.

Xyris radula thrives in swampy areas and is often found in association with other wetland flora.
