Xenostegia tridentata subsp. angustifolia

Xenostegia tridentata subsp.

angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf xenostegia, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America. It features compound leaves with three leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, soft hairs.

The flowers are small and white, typically arranged in clusters, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. This species thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable plant in ecological studies of tropical flora.
