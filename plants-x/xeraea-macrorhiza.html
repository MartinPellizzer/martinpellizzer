Xeraea macrorhiza

Xeraea macrorhiza, commonly known as the large-rooted xeraea, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its robust, fibrous root system that allows it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers during the cooler months. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to minimize water loss.

Xeraea macrorhiza is often found in rocky soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
