Xylophacos chloridae

Xylophacos chloridae, commonly known as the greenwood plant, is a unique species characterized by its dense, fibrous structure.

It possesses a complex root system that allows it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils. The plant's leaves are broad and chlorophyll-rich, contributing to its vibrant green color. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are adapted for wind pollination.

Its woody stem provides structural support and enables it to grow in various terrestrial environments.
