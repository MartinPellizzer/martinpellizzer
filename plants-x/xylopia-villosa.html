Xylopia villosa

Xylopia villosa, commonly known as the hairy xylopia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its rough, often hairy bark and elliptical leaves. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, star-shaped fruits. Xylopia villosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its growth habit and foliage make it a popular choice for shade gardens in warm climates.
