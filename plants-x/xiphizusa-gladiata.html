Xiphizusa gladiata

Xiphizusa gladiata, commonly known as the swordleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, sword-shaped leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Xiphizusa gladiata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
