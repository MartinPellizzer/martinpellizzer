Xeraea elegans

Xeraea elegans, commonly known as the silver leaf tree, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Xeraea elegans is also valued for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
