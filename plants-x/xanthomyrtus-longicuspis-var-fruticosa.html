Xanthomyrtus longicuspis var. fruticosa

Xanthomyrtus longicuspis var. fruticosa, commonly known as the golden myrtle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in horticulture for its attractive foliage and fragrance.
