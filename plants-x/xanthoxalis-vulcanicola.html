Xanthoxalis vulcanicola

Xanthoxalis vulcanicola, commonly known as the volcanic yellow oxalis, is a flowering plant native to volcanic regions in South America.

It belongs to the family Oxalidaceae and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant thrives in well-drained, nutrient-poor soils typical of volcanic landscapes. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Xanthoxalis vulcanicola is known for its ability to bloom in early spring, adding vibrant color to its native habitat.
