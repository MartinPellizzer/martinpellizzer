Xanthium spinosum f. laciniatum

Xanthium spinosum f. laciniatum, commonly known as the cut-leaved cocklebur, is a variant of the cocklebur plant characterized by its finely lobed leaves.

It is an annual herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. The plant produces spiny, spherical fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a distinctive, serrated appearance.

This subspecies is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and fields.
