Xeraea albiflora

Xeraea albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflower xeraea, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a compact, shrub-like growth habit with narrow, gray-green leaves. It is adapted to survive in hot, dry climates with minimal water requirements.

Xeraea albiflora is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
