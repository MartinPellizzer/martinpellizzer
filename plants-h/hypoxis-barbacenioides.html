Hypoxis barbacenioides

Hypoxis barbacenioides, commonly known as the African gladiolus, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a short, rhizomatous stem and produces clusters of bright yellow, star-shaped flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to the landscape.
