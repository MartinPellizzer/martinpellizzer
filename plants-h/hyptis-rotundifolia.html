Hyptis rotundifolia

Hyptis rotundifolia, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other herbs like mint and basil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Hyptis rotundifolia thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
