Helichrysum lucilioides

Helichrysum lucilioides, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that remain intact even after the plant has dried, giving it a long-lasting appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is aromatic and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive scent.

It is often used in floral arrangements due to its durability and vibrant color.
