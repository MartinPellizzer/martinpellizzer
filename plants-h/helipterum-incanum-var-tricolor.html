Helipterum incanum var. tricolor

Helipterum incanum var. tricolor, commonly known as the three-color sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like yellow flowers with white and red markings, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

The variety tricolor is distinguished by its unique tri-colored floral pattern, making it a popular ornamental plant.
