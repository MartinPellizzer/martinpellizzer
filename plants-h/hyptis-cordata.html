Hyptis cordata

Hyptis cordata, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Hyptis cordata prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in warm climates.
