Homalomena griffithii f. kingii

Homalomena griffithii f. kingii, commonly known as the King's arrowleaf, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a glossy surface. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

The flowering structure is notable for its striking color contrast between the spadix and spathe.
