Hieracium jurassicum subsp. aligerum

Hieracium jurassicum subsp.

aligerum, commonly known as the Jurassic hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and forest edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

This subspecies is noted for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and maintaining soil stability in its native range.
