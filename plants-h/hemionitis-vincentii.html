Hemionitis vincentii

Hemionitis vincentii, commonly known as the Vincent's hemionitis, is a rare and delicate fern native to the arid regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its small, finely divided leaves that form a dense, low-growing mat. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited water availability. Its reproductive structures, including spore-producing sorus, are located on the undersides of the fronds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Hemionitis vincentii is considered a species of conservation concern.
