Hookera peduncularis

Hookera peduncularis, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its erect, leafy stems and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to withstand harsh mountain conditions.

Hookera peduncularis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural environment.
