Hieracium laevigatum subsp. fagineum

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

fagineum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of elongated, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters.

The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as meadows and marshes. It has a fibrous root system and is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes.

The species is native to Europe and has been introduced to other parts of the world.
