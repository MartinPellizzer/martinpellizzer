Hieracium caesium subsp. scytophyllum

Hieracium caesium subsp.

scytophyllum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

The subspecies scytophyllum is distinguished by its slightly different flower structure and habitat preferences compared to other Hieracium species.
