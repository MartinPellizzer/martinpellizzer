Hydrocotyle diantha

Hydrocotyle diantha, commonly known as the silver leaf water plant, is a small herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often near water bodies such as ponds and streams. The plant features rounded, glossy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white or silver hair, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in clusters, and bloom during the summer months.

Hydrocotyle diantha is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and ease of cultivation.
