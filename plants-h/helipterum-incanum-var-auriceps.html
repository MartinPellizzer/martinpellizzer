Helipterum incanum var. auriceps

Helipterum incanum var. auriceps, commonly known as the silverhead lomatia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub characterized by its silver-gray foliage and showy, yellow-orange flowerheads that appear in late spring. The leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine, white hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. The flowers are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences, which attract various pollinators such as bees and birds.

This species is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
