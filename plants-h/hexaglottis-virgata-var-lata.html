Hexaglottis virgata var. lata

Hexaglottis virgata var. lata, commonly known as the broad-leaf hexaglottis, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
