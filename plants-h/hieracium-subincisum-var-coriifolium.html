Hieracium subincisum var. coriifolium

Hieracium subincisum var. coriifolium, commonly known as the coriifolium hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate to ovate leaves with a coriaceous texture, giving it a leathery appearance. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in the summer months, typically from July to August. It is native to Europe and is often found in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
