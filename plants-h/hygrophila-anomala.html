Hygrophila anomala

Hygrophila anomala, commonly known as the water hygrophila, is a tropical aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are usually lance-shaped. The plant produces tiny, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the late spring or early summer. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving freshwater environments and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds.

Hygrophila anomala is valued in aquariums for its aesthetic appeal and ability to provide oxygen to the water.
