Helenium thurberi

Helenium thurberi, commonly known as Thurber's sneezeweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features showy, orange to red ray flowers surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Helenium thurberi is often found in disturbed areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
