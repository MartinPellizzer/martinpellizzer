Helixyra setifolia

Helixyra setifolia, commonly known as the threadleaf helixyra, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Helixyra setifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
