Heliconia atropurpurea

Heliconia atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is characterized by its large, brightly colored bracts that resemble petals. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers inside its leaf-like structures, which are often used in floral arrangements. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense clumps in moist, shaded environments.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its striking appearance.
