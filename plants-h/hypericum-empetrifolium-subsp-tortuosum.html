Hypericum empetrifolium subsp. tortuosum

Hypericum empetrifolium subsp.

tortuosum, commonly known as the twisted-stem St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its woody stems that twist and curl, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.
