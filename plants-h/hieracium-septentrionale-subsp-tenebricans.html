Hieracium septentrionale subsp. tenebricans

Hieracium septentrionale subsp.

tenebricans, commonly known as the dark-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern regions of Europe. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features basal rosettes of dark green, hairy leaves that are often tinged with purple. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, with ray florets that are shorter than the disk florets.

It is characterized by its robust root system and ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
