Hieracium lancidens

Hieracium lancidens, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
