Hieracium gothicum subsp. flocculosum

Hieracium gothicum subsp.

flocculosum, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It is characterized by its distinctive, woolly, and often pale yellow or greenish inflorescence.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in fine, soft hairs.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its showy, composite flower heads.
