Hieracium circumstellatum

Hieracium circumstellatum, commonly known as the starry hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny conditions. Its leaves are heart-shaped and hairy, while its flowers consist of yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk.

Hieracium circumstellatum is often considered a weed but has historical uses in herbal medicine.
