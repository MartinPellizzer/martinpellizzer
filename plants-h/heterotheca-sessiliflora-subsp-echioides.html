Heterotheca sessiliflora subsp. echioides

Heterotheca sessiliflora subsp.

echioides, commonly known as the silverleaf coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and conical, yellow-orange flower heads that bloom in late summer.

The plant has linear, sessile leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, enhancing its ecological value in native landscapes.
