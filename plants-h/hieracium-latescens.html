Hieracium latescens

Hieracium latescens, commonly known as the pale hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are broadly ovate with a fuzzy texture, and its flowers are yellow, forming dense clusters.

Hieracium latescens is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a pioneer species in ecological succession.
