Homalomena zollingeri

Homalomena zollingeri, commonly known as the Zollinger's caladium, is a tropical plant belonging to the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that come in a variety of colors, including green, red, and white. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

The flowers, though less showy than the foliage, are small and green, with a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe.
