Homalobus tenuifolius

Homalobus tenuifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, or white. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by birds and insects, contributing to its ecological significance in native habitats.
