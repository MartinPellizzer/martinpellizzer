Hyptis discolor

Hyptis discolor, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its gray-green, fuzzy leaves and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

Hyptis discolor thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
