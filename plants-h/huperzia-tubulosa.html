Huperzia tubulosa

Huperzia tubulosa, commonly known as the tubular huperzia, is a species of clubmoss native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and small, spore-bearing structures called sporangia. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its leaves are often arranged in whorls and have a distinctive, tubular shape.

Huperzia tubulosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and air-purifying qualities.
