Haloragis erecta subsp. erecta

Haloragis erecta subsp.

erecta, commonly known as the erect willowherb, is a flowering plant native to parts of Australia. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or damp environments, such as swamps and riverbanks.

The plant has slender, erect stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in late summer to early autumn.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
