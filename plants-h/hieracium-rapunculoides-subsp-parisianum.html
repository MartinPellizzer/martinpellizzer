Hieracium rapunculoides subsp. parisianum

Hieracium rapunculoides subsp.

parisianum, commonly known as the Parisian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant has basal rosettes of lanceolate leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters at the ends of leafy stems. Its flowering period occurs from June to August, and it is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads.
