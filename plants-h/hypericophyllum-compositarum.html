Hypericophyllum compositarum

Hypericophyllum compositarum, commonly known as the composite goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
