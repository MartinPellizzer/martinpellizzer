Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp. lucanicum

Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp.

lucanicum, commonly known as the Lucanian hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has hairy stems and leaves, and its flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

The subspecies is distinguished by its geographic distribution and specific morphological traits.
