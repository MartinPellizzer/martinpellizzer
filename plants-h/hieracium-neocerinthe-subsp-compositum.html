Hieracium neocerinthe subsp. compositum

Hieracium neocerinthe subsp.

compositum, commonly known as the composite hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. It has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences.

Its subspecies status reflects subtle morphological differences from the main species, making it a subject of taxonomic interest.
