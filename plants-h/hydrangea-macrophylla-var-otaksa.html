Hydrangea macrophylla var. otaksa

Hydrangea macrophylla var. otaksa, commonly known as the Otaksa hydrangea, is a cultivar prized for its large, showy flower heads.

It belongs to the Hydrangeaceae family and is native to Japan, where it thrives in humid, temperate climates. The plant features broad, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a slight sheen. Its flowers, which can range from blue to pink depending on soil pH, are composed of sterile, petal-like bracts surrounding small, true flowers.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to bloom profusely in the summer months.
