Hieracium vaillantii

Hieracium vaillantii, commonly known as the Vaillant's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and numerous yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal, aiding in its spread.

It is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other sunny areas.
