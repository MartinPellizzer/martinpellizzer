Hesperis graminea

Hesperis graminea, commonly known as the sweet William or garden hesperis, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in grassy areas and disturbed soils. The plant produces showy clusters of pink, purple, or white flowers in early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, and it grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters.

Hesperis graminea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
