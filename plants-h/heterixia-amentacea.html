Heterixia amentacea

Heterixia amentacea, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant has a compact, rounded canopy with small, leathery leaves that are arranged alternately along the branches. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers followed by small, round fruits.

Heterixia amentacea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.
