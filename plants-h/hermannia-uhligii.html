Hermannia uhligii

Hermannia uhligii, commonly known as the dwarf zingiber, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features a compact rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense, mat-like structure. The plant produces small, fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, typical of the Zingiberaceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Hermannia uhligii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in tropical gardens for its unique foliage and subtle floral display.
