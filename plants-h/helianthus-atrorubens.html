Helianthus atrorubens

Helianthus atrorubens, commonly known as the black-leafed sunflower, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flower heads surrounded by dark green to black bracts. The plant typically grows in wetlands and along riverbanks, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and often have a dark reddish hue, which gives it its common name.

Helianthus atrorubens is a perennial herb that blooms from late summer to early fall, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
