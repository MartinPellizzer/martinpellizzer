Hieracium nigrescens subsp. spodiozum

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

spodiozum, commonly known as the black hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm. Its leaves are basal, rough, and covered with stiff hairs, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
