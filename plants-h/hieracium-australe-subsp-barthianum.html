Hieracium australe subsp. barthianum

Hieracium australe subsp.

barthianum, commonly known as the Australian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Australia. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

The plant features upright stems with alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and it produces small, oval seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is part of the dandelion family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its distinctive morphology and ecological adaptability.
