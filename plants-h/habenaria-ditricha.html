Habenaria ditricha

Habenaria ditricha, commonly known as the hairy orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers clustered in a raceme, which are often overlooked due to their modest appearance. It prefers moist, shaded habitats and is often found in forest understories.

The species is notable for its unique floral structure and is of interest to botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
