Hydrangea arborescens subsp. radiata

Hydrangea arborescens subsp.

radiata, commonly known as the smooth hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range from white to pink depending on soil pH.

The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a smooth margin and a glossy upper surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to bloom profusely in late spring to early summer.
