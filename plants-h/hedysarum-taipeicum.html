Hedysarum taipeicum

Hedysarum taipeicum, commonly known as Taipei milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Taiwan.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Hedysarum taipeicum is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and its potential use in ecological restoration projects.
