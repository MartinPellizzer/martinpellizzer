Hieracium murorum var. colapterodon

Hieracium murorum var. colapterodon, commonly known as the mouse-ear hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its small, daisy-like yellow flowers and hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in disturbed areas.

The species is also valued in traditional herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
