Hippocrepis ciliata var. ambigua

Hippocrepis ciliata var. ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous fringed gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, often in open woodlands or along coastal areas. Its leaves are usually narrow and ciliate, giving it the species name "ciliata.".

The varietal form "ambigua" refers to the variability in its floral structure and leaf characteristics.
