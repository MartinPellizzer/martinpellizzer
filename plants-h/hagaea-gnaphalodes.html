Hagaea gnaphalodes

Hagaea gnaphalodes, commonly known as the silverleaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage and bright yellow flower heads. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. Hagaea gnaphalodes is adapted to dry conditions, with hairy leaves that help reduce water loss.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
