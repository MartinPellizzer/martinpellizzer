Hicorius alba var. subcoriacea

Hicorius alba var. subcoriacea, commonly known as the white hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, white, showy flowers that bloom in the summer months. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly rough texture. This plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance.
