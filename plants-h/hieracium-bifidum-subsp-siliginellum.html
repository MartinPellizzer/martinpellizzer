Hieracium bifidum subsp. siliginellum

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

siliginellum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed sites, reaching heights of up to 30 cm.

The plant features basal leaves that are lanceolate and often hairy, with flowering stems that bear yellow flowers in a single head. Its inflorescence consists of a single flower head with ray florets and a disk of tubular florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

The plant is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
