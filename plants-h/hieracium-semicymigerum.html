Hieracium semicymigerum

Hieracium semicymigerum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head, with ray florets that are shorter than the central disk florets.

Hieracium semicymigerum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
