Helichrysum pumilum var. spathulatum

Helichrysum pumilum var. spathulatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf everlasting, is a small, mat-forming shrub native to South Africa.

It features tiny, yellow-orange flower heads that are tightly packed and resemble tiny suns, giving it its common name. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The flowers are long-lasting and retain their color when dried, making it a popular choice for floral arrangements.
