Hieracium chroopastum

Hieracium chroopastum, commonly known as the purple meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with purple markings.

It is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in agricultural and natural areas.
