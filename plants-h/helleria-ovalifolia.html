Helleria ovalifolia

Helleria ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leafed helleria, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Helleria ovalifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
