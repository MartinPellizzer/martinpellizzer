Hieracium umbellatiforme

Hieracium umbellatiforme, commonly known as the umbrella hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a hairy stem that branches near the base, supporting numerous yellowish to reddish flower heads. Its leaves are typically basal, with a rough, hairy texture and a heart-shaped base.

Hieracium umbellatiforme is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it plays a role in supporting pollinators.
