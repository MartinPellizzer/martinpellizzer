Hepetis incarnata

Hepetis incarnata, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, blue-violet flower clusters that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has slender, branching stems and heart-shaped, aromatic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Hepetis incarnata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
