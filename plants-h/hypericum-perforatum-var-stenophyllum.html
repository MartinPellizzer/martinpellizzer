Hypericum perforatum var. stenophyllum

Hypericum perforatum var. stenophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved St. John's wort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant contains hypericin and hyperforin, which are bioactive compounds with potential medicinal properties. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its chemical composition, it has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various ailments.
