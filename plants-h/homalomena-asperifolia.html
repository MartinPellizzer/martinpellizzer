Homalomena asperifolia

Homalomena asperifolia, commonly known as the rough-leafed arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a rough, textured surface. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in a spadix, surrounded by a spathe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
