Hamelia patens var. coronata

Hamelia patens var. coronata, commonly known as flame beak or scarlet flame bush, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, tubular red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
