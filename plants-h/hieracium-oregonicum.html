Hieracium oregonicum

Hieracium oregonicum, commonly known as the Oregon hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and forest edges, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers range from yellow to red, depending on the variety.

Hieracium oregonicum is also known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
