Hedyotis leptopetala

Hedyotis leptopetala, commonly known as the slender-leaved dotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Hedyotis leptopetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
