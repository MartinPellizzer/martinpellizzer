Hieracium lamprodes

Hieracium lamprodes, commonly known as the silverleaf hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a silvery underside, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer, are yellow and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
