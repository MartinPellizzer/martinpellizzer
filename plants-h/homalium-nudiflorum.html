Homalium nudiflorum

Homalium nudiflorum, commonly known as the naked-flower euphorbia, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptic or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in umbels, and it is noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
