Hieracium sabaudum subsp. gutierrezii

Hieracium sabaudum subsp.

gutierrezii, commonly known as the Sabaudian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, pastures, and rocky slopes. It has basal and upright stems with hairy leaves that are lanceolate in shape.

The flowers are yellow, with a distinctive central disc and surrounding ray florets, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
