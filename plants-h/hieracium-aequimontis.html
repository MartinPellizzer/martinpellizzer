Hieracium aequimontis

Hieracium aequimontis, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

Hieracium aequimontis plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in high-altitude ecosystems.
