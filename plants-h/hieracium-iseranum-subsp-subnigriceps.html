Hieracium iseranum subsp. subnigriceps

Hieracium iseranum subsp.

subnigriceps, commonly known as the black-headed hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm.

The plant features dark green, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers with five petals. Its flowers are borne in a flat-topped inflorescence, and the species is characterized by its distinctive blackish-purple flower heads.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower structure and medicinal potential in traditional herbal medicine.
