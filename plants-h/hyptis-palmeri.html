Hyptis palmeri

Hyptis palmeri, commonly known as Palmer's hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. Its leaves are often hairy and have a strong, minty scent, while its flowers are clustered in whorls at the ends of the stems.

Hyptis palmeri is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued as an ornamental plant.
