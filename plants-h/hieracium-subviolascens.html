Hieracium subviolascens

Hieracium subviolascens, commonly known as the purple-veined hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped with hairy surfaces, and the flowers are yellow with purple veins, giving it its common name.

It is an herbaceous perennial that spreads through rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
