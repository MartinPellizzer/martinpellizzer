Howardia foetida

Howardia foetida, commonly known as the stink weed, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine white powder. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, followed by spherical, red, fleshy fruits. It is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils.

Despite its common name, it is not toxic and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
