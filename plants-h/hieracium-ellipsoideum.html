Hieracium ellipsoideum

Hieracium ellipsoideum, commonly known as the elliptic hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
