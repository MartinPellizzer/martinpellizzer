Hyphaene pyrifera var. margaritensis

Hyphaene pyrifera var. margaritensis, commonly known as the pearl fan palm, is a striking member of the Arecaceae family native to Madagascar.

This variant is distinguished by its unique, fan-shaped leaves that are adorned with small, white, bead-like structures called "pearls," which give it its common name. The plant features a slender trunk covered in persistent leaf bases, creating a distinctive appearance. It thrives in tropical climates with well-drained soil and requires ample sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
