Huernia barbata subsp. ingeae

Huernia barbata subsp.

ingeae, commonly known as the Inge's moon cactus, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and spiny, paddle-shaped leaves.

The plant produces striking yellow to orange flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and compact growth habit, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and rock gardens.
