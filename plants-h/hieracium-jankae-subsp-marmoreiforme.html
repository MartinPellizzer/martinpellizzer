Hieracium jankae subsp. marmoreiforme

Hieracium jankae subsp.

marmoreiforme, commonly known as the marble-patterned hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its distinctive, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are upright and often branched. This subspecies is noted for its unique leaf patterns, which resemble marble, giving it its common name.

It thrives in meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils and full sun.
