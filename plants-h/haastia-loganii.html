Haastia loganii

Haastia loganii, commonly known as the Logan's Haastia, is a striking member of the Asparagaceae family native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, rosette-forming structure with thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces tall, spire-like flower stalks that bear clusters of violet to purple bracts, which are not true flowers but serve as ornamental structures. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and limited natural range, it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
