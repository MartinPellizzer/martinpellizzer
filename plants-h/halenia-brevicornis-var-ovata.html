Halenia brevicornis var. ovata

Halenia brevicornis var. ovata, commonly known as the oval-leaf halenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern. The plant produces clusters of pale green to yellowish flowers, which are often borne on short pedicels. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
