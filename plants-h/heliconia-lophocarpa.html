Heliconia lophocarpa

Heliconia lophocarpa, commonly known as the red heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its vibrant, spadix-like inflorescence surrounded by brightly colored bracts. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with broad, sword-shaped leaves. Its flowers are primarily pollinated by birds, particularly hummingbirds, due to their bright red color and nectar-rich structure.

Heliconia lophocarpa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical landscapes.
