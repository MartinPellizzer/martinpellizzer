Hibiscus lasiocarpos

Hibiscus lasiocarpos, commonly known as the woolly hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Hibiscus lasiocarpos is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its flowers bloom from late spring to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
