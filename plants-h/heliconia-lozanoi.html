Heliconia lozanoi

Heliconia lozanoi, commonly known as the Lozano's heliconia, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its large, brightly colored bracts that resemble petals. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that are often hidden within the colorful bracts. Its leaves are broad and glossy, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

Heliconia lozanoi thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the undergrowth of tropical forests.
