Hypoxis farinosa

Hypoxis farinosa, commonly known as the white star lily, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, star-shaped white flowers with yellow centers, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Hypoxis farinosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in gardens.
