Heliotropium astrotrichum var. subglabrum

Heliotropium astrotrichum var. subglabrum, commonly known as the glabrous heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that typically range in color from pink to purple. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, but the variety subglabrum is distinguished by its reduced hair coverage, giving it a smoother appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
