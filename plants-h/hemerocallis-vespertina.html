Hemerocallis vespertina

Hemerocallis vespertina, commonly known as the evening glory, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically blooms in late summer to early autumn, hence its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

Hemerocallis vespertina is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
