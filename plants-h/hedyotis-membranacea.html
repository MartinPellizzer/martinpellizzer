Hedyotis membranacea

Hedyotis membranacea, commonly known as the red root or red stem plant, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that are often reddish in color, covered with small, opposite leaves. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its roots are fibrous and have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.

Hedyotis membranacea thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.
