Hieracium vulgatum subsp. pseudeuroum

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

pseudeuroum, commonly known as the false brown yarrow, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features upright, hairy stems that bear clusters of small, yellowish to brownish flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough, hairy texture.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal historical uses.
