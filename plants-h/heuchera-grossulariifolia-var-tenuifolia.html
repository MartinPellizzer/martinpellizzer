Heuchera grossulariifolia var. tenuifolia

Heuchera grossulariifolia var. tenuifolia, commonly known as the slender leaf coral bells, is a perennial plant native to the Appalachian region of the United States.

It features attractive, ovate to elliptical leaves that are typically a striking shade of red, pink, or purple, depending on the variety and environmental conditions. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink or red during the summer months. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its foliage remains ornamental throughout the growing season, adding visual interest even when it is not in bloom.
