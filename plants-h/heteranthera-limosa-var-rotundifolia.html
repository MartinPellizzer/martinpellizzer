Heteranthera limosa var. rotundifolia

Heteranthera limosa var. rotundifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf water hyssop, is a perennial aquatic plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are often broader at the base, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for anchoring in shallow water or moist soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wetland environments.
