Hypoxis flaccida

Hypoxis flaccida, commonly known as the yellow star grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asphodelaceae and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in spring, which are clustered in a star-like arrangement. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Hypoxis flaccida is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
