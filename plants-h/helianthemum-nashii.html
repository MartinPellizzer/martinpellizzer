Helianthemum nashii

Helianthemum nashii, commonly known as the Nash's sandthrift, is a low-growing flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and gray-green, adapted to thrive in well-drained, sunny environments.

Helianthemum nashii is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
