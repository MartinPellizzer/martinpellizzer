Huperzia hupehensis

Huperzia hupehensis, commonly known as the Chinese clubmoss, is a perennial fern-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Huperziaceae and is characterized by its spore-producing strobila, which forms at the tips of its upright, segmented stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually small, linear, and arranged in whorls around the stem.

Huperzia hupehensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
