Homopappus racemosus

Homopappus racemosus, commonly known as the racemose dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant produces a distinctive, elongated inflorescence with yellow ray flowers and white disk flowers, which are typical of the dandelion genus. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and arranged alternately along the stem.

The plant is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and desert regions.
