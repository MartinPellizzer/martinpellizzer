Homalopetalum pumilio

Homalopetalum pumilio, commonly known as the dwarf blanketflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ability to spread easily make it a hardy addition to arid landscapes.
