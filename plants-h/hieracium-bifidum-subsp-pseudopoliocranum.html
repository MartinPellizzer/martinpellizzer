Hieracium bifidum subsp. pseudopoliocranum

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

pseudopoliocranum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It typically thrives in dry, sunny grasslands and rocky soils, and is often found in open habitats with well-drained soil.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and localized species in some regions.
