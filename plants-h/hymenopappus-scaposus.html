Hymenopappus scaposus

Hymenopappus scaposus, commonly known as the slender hymenopappus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive bracts. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brown, and it produces feathery pappus structures that aid in wind dispersal of its seeds.

Hymenopappus scaposus is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh desert conditions.
