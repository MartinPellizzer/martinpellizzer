Horsfieldia crux-melitensis

Horsfieldia crux-melitensis, commonly known as the Malta orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Malta and parts of Sicily.

It is a terrestrial herb with a short, upright stem and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and moths. Horsfieldia crux-melitensis thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

It is a rare and protected species, reflecting its limited distribution and ecological significance.
