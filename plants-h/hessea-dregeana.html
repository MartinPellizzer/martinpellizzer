Hessea dregeana

Hessea dregeana, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Hessea dregeana is also valued for its hard, aromatic wood, which has been used in traditional craftsmanship.
