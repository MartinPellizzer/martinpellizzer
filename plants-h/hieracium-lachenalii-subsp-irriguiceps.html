Hieracium lachenalii subsp. irriguiceps

Hieracium lachenalii subsp.

irriguiceps, commonly known as the Alpine Hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its elongated, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in rocky and gravelly soils at high altitudes, often in mountainous areas.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and protected plant in many regions.
