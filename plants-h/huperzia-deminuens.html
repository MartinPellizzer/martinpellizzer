Huperzia deminuens

Huperzia deminuens, commonly known as the small huperzia, is a species of moss belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, creeping growth habit and is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors. The plant forms dense mats and has simple, linear leaves that are often slightly curled at the edges. It is primarily distributed in tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including China, Japan, and Southeast Asia.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Huperzia deminuens is considered a delicate and ecologically significant species.
