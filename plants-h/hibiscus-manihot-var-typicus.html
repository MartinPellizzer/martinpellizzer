Hibiscus manihot var. typicus

Hibiscus manihot var. typicus, commonly known as the arrowleaf hibiscus, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces capsules containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Hibiscus manihot var. typicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
