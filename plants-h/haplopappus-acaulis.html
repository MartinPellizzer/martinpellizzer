Haplopappus acaulis

Haplopappus acaulis, commonly known as the smooth pappus plant, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive bract structures. The plant is typically low-growing, with a compact, cushion-like form that helps it conserve water in harsh environments. Its leaves are usually linear and covered in a fine layer of hairs, which reduces water loss.

The flowers are small and yellow, often appearing in clusters, and the plant is adapted to thrive in sandy or rocky soils.
