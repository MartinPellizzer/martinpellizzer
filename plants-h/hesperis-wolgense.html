Hesperis wolgense

Hesperis wolgense, commonly known as the Wolgense Umbellate.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features a basal rosette of lobed leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers arranged in an umbel. Native to specific regions in Eastern Europe, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade.

Hesperis wolgense is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
