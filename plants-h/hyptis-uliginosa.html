Hyptis uliginosa

Hyptis uliginosa, commonly known as black sage, is a perennial herb native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant thrives in moist, boggy environments and is often found in marshy areas along rivers and lakes. Its leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its strong, minty scent.

Hyptis uliginosa is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
