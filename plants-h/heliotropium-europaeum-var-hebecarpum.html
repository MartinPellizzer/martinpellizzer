Heliotropium europaeum var. hebecarpum

Heliotropium europaeum var. hebecarpum, commonly known as European heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple, often resembling those of the common heliotrope. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and wastelands, and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are usually hairy and opposite, while the fruit is a small, dry capsule.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history of use in traditional medicine.
