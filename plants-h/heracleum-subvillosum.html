Heracleum subvillosum

Heracleum subvillosum, commonly known as cow parsnip, is a tall biennial or short-lived perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its large, umbrella-shaped compound leaves and clusters of small, white to greenish-yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other members like carrots and parsley. Its stems are hairy and often have a milky sap that can cause skin irritation.

It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.
