Heliosperma pusillum subsp. albanicum

Heliosperma pusillum subsp.

albanicum, commonly known as the Albanian dwarf lily, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the Balkan region. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments.

The plant features slender stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and pale pink. It is characterized by its compact size and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
