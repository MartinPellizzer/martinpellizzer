Horsfieldia ampla

Horsfieldia ampla, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 centimeters in diameter. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

The flowers are often fragrant and attract various pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
