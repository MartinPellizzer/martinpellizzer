Heterochaeta erigeroides

Heterochaeta erigeroides, commonly known as the woolly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, yellow disk flowers surrounded by pale blue to white ray florets.

Heterochaeta erigeroides is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its hardy nature and attractive, long-lasting blooms.
