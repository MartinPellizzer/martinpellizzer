Heteropappus sampsonii

Heteropappus sampsonii, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive bracts. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by fluffy, white pappus structures that aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to survive in harsh, dry climates with minimal water availability.
