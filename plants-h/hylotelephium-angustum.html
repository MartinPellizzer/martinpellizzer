Hylotelephium angustum

Hylotelephium angustum, commonly known as the narrowleaf stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features fleshy, narrow leaves that are typically blue-green in color, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Hylotelephium angustum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
