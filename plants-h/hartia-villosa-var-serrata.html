Hartia villosa var. serrata

Hartia villosa var. serrata, commonly known as the serrated hairy hartia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
