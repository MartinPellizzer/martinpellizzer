Homaida graminea

Homaida graminea, commonly known as the slender grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. The plant features slender, cylindrical stems that are often branched and covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are elongated, narrow, and arranged alternately along the stem, with a bluish-green hue.

The small, spike-like inflorescence produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers, which are followed by small, three-angled seed capsules.
