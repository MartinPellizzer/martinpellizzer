Homalomena mutans

Homalomena mutans, commonly known as the variable arrowroot or the changing leaf plant, is a tropical evergreen herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that change color and pattern as the plant matures. The plant produces striking inflorescences with a spadix and a spathe, typical of the Araceae family. Its rhizomes are edible and have been used in traditional cuisine, while the plant is also cultivated as an ornamental due to its aesthetic appeal.

Homalomena mutans thrives in humid, shady environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
