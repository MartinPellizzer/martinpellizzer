Heliocereus phyllanthoides

Heliocereus phyllanthoides, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a cactus species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and the ability to produce large, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant is a member of the Cactaceae family and is typically found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink and can reach up to 15 centimeters in diameter.

This plant is popular among cactus enthusiasts for its striking appearance and unique flowering behavior.
