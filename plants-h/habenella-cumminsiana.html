Habenella cumminsiana

Habenella cumminsiana, commonly known as the Cummins' bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its specialized structures called bladder-like organs, which are used to trap small aquatic organisms. The plant typically grows in shallow, slow-moving freshwater habitats such as ponds and wetlands. Its leaves are thin and ribbon-like, often floating on the water surface.
