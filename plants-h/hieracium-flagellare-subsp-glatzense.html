Hieracium flagellare subsp. glatzense

Hieracium flagellare subsp.

glatzense, commonly known as the Glatz's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typically blooming from June to August. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, often with a reddish or purplish hue.

It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.
