Hemionitis flavescens

Hemionitis flavescens, commonly known as the yellow water clover, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It features small, opposite leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited water availability. Its reproductive strategy involves wind pollination, and it plays a role in stabilizing desert soils.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is considered an important indicator species for arid ecosystems.
