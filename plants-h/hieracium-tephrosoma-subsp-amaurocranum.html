Hieracium tephrosoma subsp. amaurocranum

Hieracium tephrosoma subsp.

amaurocranum, commonly known as the dark-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dark green, often hairy leaves and yellow flowering heads.

The subspecies is distinguished by its darker foliage and reduced leaf pubescence compared to other members of the species. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and woodland edges.

The plant is relatively small, with flowering stems reaching up to 30 cm in height.
