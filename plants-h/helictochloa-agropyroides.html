Helictochloa agropyroides

Helictochloa agropyroides, commonly known as the false panic grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture.

It is characterized by its ability to thrive in dry conditions and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
