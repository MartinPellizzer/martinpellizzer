Helleborus occidentalis

Helleborus occidentalis, commonly known as the western hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its dark green, waxy leaves and striking, nodding flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, making it one of the earliest flowering plants in temperate regions. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodlands and rocky areas.

The plant contains toxic compounds, including cardiac glycosides, which can be harmful if ingested.
