Helictochloa pratensis subsp. iberica

Helictochloa pratensis subsp.

iberica, commonly known as the Iberian meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Iberian Peninsula. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

The plant has slender, tufted stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

It plays a role in maintaining biodiversity in its native habitat.
