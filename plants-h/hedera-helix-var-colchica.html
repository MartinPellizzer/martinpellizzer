Hedera helix var. colchica

Hedera helix var. colchica, commonly known as the Colchic English Ivy, is a variant of the common ivy that exhibits distinct botanical features.

It is characterized by its dark green, slightly lobed leaves that are often more robust than those of the standard Hedera helix. This plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in late summer, followed by red berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to climb.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for ground cover and vertical gardening.
