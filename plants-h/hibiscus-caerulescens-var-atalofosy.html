Hibiscus caerulescens var. atalofosy

Hibiscus caerulescens var. atalofosy, commonly known as the Atalofosy hibiscus, is a rare variant of the hibiscus species native to specific regions in South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flower petals, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as along stream banks or in forest understories. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered with a fine, velvety texture.
