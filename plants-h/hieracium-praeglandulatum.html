Hieracium praeglandulatum

Hieracium praeglandulatum, commonly known as the glandular hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture, and the flowers are yellow with a central disc.

It is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and is often found in temperate climates.
