Hypericum × carinthiacum f. perforatiforme

Hypericum × carinthiacum f. perforatiforme, commonly known as the perforateleaf St. John's wort, is a unique hybrid species within the Hypericaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, perforated leaves that give it a striking appearance. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, typical of the genus Hypericum. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and hardy nature in temperate climates.
