Habenaria cooperi

Habenaria cooperi, commonly known as the Cooper's lady's tresses, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and a flowering spike that bears multiple small, fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. It has a rhizomatous root system and is often found in association with mycorrhizal fungi.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
