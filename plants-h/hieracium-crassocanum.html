Hieracium crassocanum

Hieracium crassocanum, commonly known as the broad-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

Hieracium crassocanum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
