Hypericum parvulum

Hypericum parvulum, commonly known as the dwarf St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to North America.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, woody capsules that contain seeds. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Hypericum parvulum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
