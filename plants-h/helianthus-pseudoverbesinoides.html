Helianthus pseudoverbesinoides

Helianthus pseudoverbesinoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf sunflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Its yellow flower heads bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
