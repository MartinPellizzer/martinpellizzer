Hibiscus macleayanus

Hibiscus macleayanus, commonly known as the Macleay's hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and are adorned with a prominent yellow or orange center. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and have a rough texture. Hibiscus macleayanus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or streamside habitats.

It is an important species in New Zealand's flora and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
