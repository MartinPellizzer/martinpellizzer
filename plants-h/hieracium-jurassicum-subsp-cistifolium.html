Hieracium jurassicum subsp. cistifolium

Hieracium jurassicum subsp.

cistifolium, commonly known as the Jurassic hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its narrow, linear leaves and a compact inflorescence. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species in some regions.
