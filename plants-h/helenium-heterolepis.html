Helenium heterolepis

Helenium heterolepis, commonly known as the stiff goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to the central and eastern United States.

It features tall, upright stems that bear clusters of bright yellow to orange ray flowers, which are surrounded by yellow disk flowers. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

Helenium heterolepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
