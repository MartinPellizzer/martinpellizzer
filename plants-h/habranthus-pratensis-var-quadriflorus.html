Habranthus pratensis var. quadriflorus

Habranthus pratensis var. quadriflorus, commonly known as the four-flowered bluebell, is a striking ornamental plant native to South Africa.

It is a bulbous perennial that produces clusters of fragrant, sky-blue flowers in early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is well-suited for rock gardens or woodland settings. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sun, making it a versatile choice for gardeners.

Its unique floral display and hardy nature make it a popular addition to botanical collections.
