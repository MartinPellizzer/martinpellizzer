Hymenaea stigonocarpa

Hymenaea stigonocarpa, commonly known as the African shea tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and produces large, woody pods containing edible seeds. The tree is valued for its shea butter, which is extracted from the seeds and used in cosmetics and food products. It thrives in dry savanna and woodland habitats, often forming dense stands.

Hymenaea stigonocarpa plays an important ecological role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife species.
