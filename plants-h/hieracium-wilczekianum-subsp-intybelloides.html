Hieracium wilczekianum subsp. intybelloides

Hieracium wilczekianum subsp.

intybelloides, commonly known as the Wilczek's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to orange-red composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. It has basal leaves that are lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, and its flowering period occurs from late spring to early summer.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
