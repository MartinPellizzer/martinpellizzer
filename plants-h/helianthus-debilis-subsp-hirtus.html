Helianthus debilis subsp. hirtus

Helianthus debilis subsp.

hirtus, commonly known as the hairy sunflower, is a species native to the southeastern United States. It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its hairy, rough stems and lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers with a dark center, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to gardens and a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects.
