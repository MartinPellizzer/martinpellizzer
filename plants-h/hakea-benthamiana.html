Hakea benthamiana

Hakea benthamiana, commonly known as the slender hakea, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves. The plant produces cylindrical, woody spikes that bear small, yellowish flowers. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance.

Its structure and morphology make it an important species for ecological and botanical studies.
