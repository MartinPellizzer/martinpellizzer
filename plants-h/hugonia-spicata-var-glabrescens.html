Hugonia spicata var. glabrescens

Hugonia spicata var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth-hairless hugonia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers during the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.
