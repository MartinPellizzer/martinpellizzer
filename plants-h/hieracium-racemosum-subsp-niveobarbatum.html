Hieracium racemosum subsp. niveobarbatum

Hieracium racemosum subsp.

niveobarbatum, commonly known as the white-hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellowish to white flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its white, hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It typically thrives in dry, sunny grasslands and rocky soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
