Hymenocallis amoena var. princeps

Hymenocallis amoena var. princeps, commonly known as the Prince's spider lily, is a striking tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It features showy, white to pale yellow flowers with a distinctive, recurved spathe and a central column that resembles a spider's body, giving it its common name. The plant produces strap-shaped, dark green leaves that are broad and sturdy, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its dramatic floral display.

This variety is a popular choice for gardeners seeking exotic and visually striking plants.
