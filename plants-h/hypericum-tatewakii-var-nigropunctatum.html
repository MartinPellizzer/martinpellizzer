Hypericum tatewakii var. nigropunctatum

Hypericum tatewakii var. nigropunctatum, commonly known as the black-punctured St. John's wort, is a rare and unique variant of the St. John's wort genus.

It is characterized by its distinctive black dots on the stems and leaves, which are a result of specialized glandular structures. This plant is native to Japan and thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions. Its botanical features include small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, and it has a compact growth habit.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable species for botanical study and conservation efforts.
