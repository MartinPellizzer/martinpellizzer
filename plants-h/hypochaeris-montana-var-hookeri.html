Hypochaeris montana var. hookeri

Hypochaeris montana var. hookeri, commonly known as Hooker's common groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils, and it produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and early summer. Its leaves are alternate, hairy, and have a serrated margin, while the stems are upright and often branched.

This species is known for its adaptability and is sometimes cultivated as a ornamental plant.
