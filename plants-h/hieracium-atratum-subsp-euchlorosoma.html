Hieracium atratum subsp. euchlorosoma

Hieracium atratum subsp.

euchlorosoma, commonly known as the dark-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and basal rosettes of dark green, hairy leaves.

The flowers are typically yellow, arranged in a single head, and are surrounded by pale green to white bracts. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

The subspecies is distinguished by its darker foliage compared to other members of the Hieracium genus.
