Hypolytrum vitiense

Hypolytrum vitiense, commonly known as the Vitiense Purpletop, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, cylindrical inflorescences. The plant produces small, violet or purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Hypolytrum vitiense is often found in coastal and lowland areas, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
