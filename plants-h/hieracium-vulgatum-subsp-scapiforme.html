Hieracium vulgatum subsp. scapiforme

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

scapiforme, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright, branching stems and yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it is often found in open, sunny habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while the flowers are arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

This subspecies is notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
