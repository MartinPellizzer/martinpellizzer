Hippeastrum brevipes

Hippeastrum brevipes, commonly known as the dwarf amaryllis, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces striking, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features broad, strap-like leaves that are often waxy and dark green. Its bulbs are relatively small compared to other amaryllis species, contributing to its "dwarf" designation.

Hippeastrum brevipes is popular as a houseplant due to its ease of care and vibrant floral displays.
