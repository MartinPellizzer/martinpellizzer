Hydropiper hexandrum

Hydropiper hexandrum, commonly known as the six-stamen water pepper, is a member of the Piperaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous plant characterized by its slender stems and opposite, lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. Hydropiper hexandrum is native to tropical and subtropical regions, often found in moist, shaded environments.
