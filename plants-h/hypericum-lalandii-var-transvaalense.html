Hypericum lalandii var. transvaalense

Hypericum lalandii var. transvaalense, commonly known as the Transvaal golden bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and vibrant floral display make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
