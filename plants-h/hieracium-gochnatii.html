Hieracium gochnatii

Hieracium gochnatii, commonly known as the Gochnat's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and bloom in summer.

It is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
