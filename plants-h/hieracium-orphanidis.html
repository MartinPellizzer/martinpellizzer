Hieracium orphanidis

Hieracium orphanidis, commonly known as the Orphan's Daisy, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flower heads and lance-shaped leaves. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe, it thrives in rocky and sandy soils. The plant typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 centimeters and blooms from June to August.

Its common name reflects its delicate appearance and the belief that it was once used in traditional herbal remedies.
