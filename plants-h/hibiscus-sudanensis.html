Hibiscus sudanensis

Hibiscus sudanensis, commonly known as the Sudanese hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Sahel region of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red, with five petals and a prominent central stigma. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and can grow up to 10 centimeters in length. Hibiscus sudanensis thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy species adapted to arid environments.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant in gardens.
