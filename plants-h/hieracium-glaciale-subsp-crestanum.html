Hieracium glaciale subsp. crestanum

Hieracium glaciale subsp.

crestanum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Alps. It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with dense clusters of small, yellowish flowers.

The plant has hairy leaves and stems, adapted to withstand cold and harsh mountain conditions. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and scree slopes.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized species within the genus Hieracium.
