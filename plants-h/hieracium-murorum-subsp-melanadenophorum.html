Hieracium murorum subsp. melanadenophorum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

melanadenophorum, commonly known as the dark-hooded hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems, often found in meadows and rocky areas.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is notable for its dark reddish-brown bracts beneath the flower heads, giving it its common name.
