Handroanthus vellosoi

Handroanthus vellosoi, commonly known as the yellow trumpet tree, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. Its flowers have a distinctive trumpet shape and are pollinated primarily by birds and insects.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in landscaping due to its vibrant blooms.
