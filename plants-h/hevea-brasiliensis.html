Hevea brasiliensis

Hevea brasiliensis, commonly known as the rubber tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon basin.

It is renowned for producing natural latex, which is harvested from its bark through a process called tapping. The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves and can grow up to 30 meters tall in its natural habitat. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The latex produced by this plant is essential in the manufacturing of rubber products worldwide.
