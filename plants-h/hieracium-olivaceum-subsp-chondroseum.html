Hieracium olivaceum subsp. chondroseum

Hieracium olivaceum subsp.

chondroseum, commonly known as the olive hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm.

The plant features basal leaves that are lanceolate and hairy, with yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is characterized by its bright yellow ray florets and yellow disc florets.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some related species.
