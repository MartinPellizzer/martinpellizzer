Helichrysum chlorochrysum

Helichrysum chlorochrysum, commonly known as the yellow everlasting, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that remain intact for a long period after blooming. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically clustered in dense, spherical heads. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
