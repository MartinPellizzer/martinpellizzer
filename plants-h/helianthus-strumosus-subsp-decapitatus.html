Helianthus strumosus subsp. decapitatus

Helianthus strumosus subsp.

decapitatus, commonly known as the decapitated sunflower, is a perennial herb native to the central and southern United States. It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with rough textures and is often found in prairies and open woodlands. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and other composite flowers.

Its common name refers to the tendency of its flower heads to droop or fall off after blooming.
