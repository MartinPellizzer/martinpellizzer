Hartogia spinosa

Hartogia spinosa, commonly known as the spinous hartogia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and spiny leaves that help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand prolonged droughts. Its flowers are usually small and yellow or greenish, appearing in clusters during the rainy season.

Hartogia spinosa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
