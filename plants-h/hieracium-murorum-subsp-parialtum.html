Hieracium murorum subsp. parialtum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

parialtum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

The plant has basal and ascending stems that bear alternate, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a composite head, and are surrounded by pale green bracts.

It is characterized by its ability to tolerate cold climates and is often found in high-altitude environments.
