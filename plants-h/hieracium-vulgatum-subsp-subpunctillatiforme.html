Hieracium vulgatum subsp. subpunctillatiforme

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

subpunctillatiforme, commonly known as the spotted hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers and distinctive, hairy leaves.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are usually basal and have a rough, serrated texture, while its stems are upright and often branched.

The subspecies is distinguished by its finely dotted leaves and smaller, more compact flower heads compared to other Hieracium species.
