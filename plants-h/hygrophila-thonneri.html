Hygrophila thonneri

Hygrophila thonneri, commonly known as the water hygrophila, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant that typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and slow-moving streams. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. Its small, clustered flowers are usually white or pale yellow, and they bloom during the rainy season.

Hygrophila thonneri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in aquatic gardens.
