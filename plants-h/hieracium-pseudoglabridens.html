Hieracium pseudoglabridens

Hieracium pseudoglabridens, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom in clusters.

Hieracium pseudoglabridens is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in agricultural and natural settings.
