Hibiscus polygama

Hibiscus polygama, commonly known as the red hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from red to pink, with five petals and a prominent central stigma. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are sturdy and often woody.

Hibiscus polygama is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
