Hypoxis sagittata

Hypoxis sagittata, commonly known as the arrowhead lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves arranged in a rosette pattern and produces small, yellowish-white flowers in clusters. The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight. It is often found in grasslands and open woodlands, where it contributes to the local flora.

Hypoxis sagittata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
