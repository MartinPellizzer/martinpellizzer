Hedysarum erzurumianum

Hedysarum erzurumianum, commonly known as the Erzurum sweet vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces cylindrical pods containing small, edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Hedysarum erzurumianum plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest for its potential in sustainable agriculture.
