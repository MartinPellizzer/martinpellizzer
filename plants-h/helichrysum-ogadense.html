Helichrysum ogadense

Helichrysum ogadense, commonly known as the Ogaden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of East Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and bright yellow to orange flowers. The plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its propagation across dry landscapes. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss in harsh environments.

Helichrysum ogadense is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal in dry gardens.
