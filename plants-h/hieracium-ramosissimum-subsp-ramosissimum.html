Hieracium ramosissimum subsp. ramosissimum

Hieracium ramosissimum subsp.

ramosissimum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and wastelands.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Asteraceae and is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
