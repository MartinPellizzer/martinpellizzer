Hypolepis rugosula subsp. rufobarbata

Hypolepis rugosula subsp.

rufobarbata, commonly known as the red-barbed shield fern, is a fern species native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive, leathery, and often hairy fronds that are typically bipinnate.

The fronds have a rough texture and are often adorned with red or reddish-brown spore-producing structures on the undersides. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
