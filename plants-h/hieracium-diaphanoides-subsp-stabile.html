Hieracium diaphanoides subsp. stabile

Hieracium diaphanoides subsp.

stabile, commonly known as the translucent hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive, flattened shape.

The subspecies stabile is distinguished by its more compact growth habit and slightly different leaf morphology compared to other members of the species.
