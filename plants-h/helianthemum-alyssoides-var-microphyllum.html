Helianthemum alyssoides var. microphyllum

Helianthemum alyssoides var. microphyllum, commonly known as the small-leaved rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers, typically blooming in spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry habitats.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and used in landscaping.
