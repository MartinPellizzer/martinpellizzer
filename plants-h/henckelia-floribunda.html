Henckelia floribunda

Henckelia floribunda, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in moist, shaded areas. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are ovate and covered in a fine, silvery down, giving them a distinctive appearance.

Henckelia floribunda is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climates.
