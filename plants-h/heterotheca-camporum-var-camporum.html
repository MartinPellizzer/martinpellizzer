Heterotheca camporum var. camporum

Heterotheca camporum var. camporum, commonly known as the plains coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the central and southern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in open grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features bright yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disc of smaller florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in full sun and well-drained soil conditions.
