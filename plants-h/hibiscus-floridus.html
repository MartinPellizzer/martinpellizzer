Hibiscus floridus

Hibiscus floridus, commonly known as the Florida hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows up to 3 meters tall, featuring large, showy flowers with five petals. The leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often with a velvety texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

The plant blooms from late spring to early autumn, producing vibrant pink to red flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
