Hydrangea opuloides var. hortensia

Hydrangea opuloides var. hortensia, commonly known as the panicle hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, cone-shaped flower clusters that range in color from white to pink, depending on soil pH. The plant produces showy inflorescences that can grow up to 12 inches in diameter, making it a popular ornamental species. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, and is known for its ability to change flower color with the seasons.

The leaves are typically dark green and provide a striking contrast to the vibrant blooms.
