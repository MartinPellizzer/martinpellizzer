Hydrangea serrata f. belladonna

Hydrangea serrata f. belladonna, commonly known as the Japanese mountain hydrangea, is a unique cultivar of the hydrangea genus.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range from white to pink and blue, depending on soil pH. The plant is native to Japan and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage is typically dark green and glossy, adding visual interest throughout the growing season.

Hydrangea serrata f. belladonna is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
