Hibiscus barbatus

Hibiscus barbatus, commonly known as the scarlet hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, pink to red flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in warm, humid climates. Hibiscus barbatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its fibrous stems can be processed into textiles, adding to its cultural and practical significance.
