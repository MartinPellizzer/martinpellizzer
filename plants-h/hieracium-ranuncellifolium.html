Hieracium ranuncellifolium

Hieracium ranuncellifolium, commonly known as the meadowhawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the daisy family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and a single, tall flowering stem that bears yellow-orange composite flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture. It thrives in moist meadows and grasslands, often found in temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

This plant plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
