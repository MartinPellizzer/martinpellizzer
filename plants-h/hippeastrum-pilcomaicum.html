Hippeastrum pilcomaicum

Hippeastrum pilcomaicum, commonly known as the Pilcomayo amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America, specifically Peru.

It features large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, typically in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces strap-like, linear leaves that are often mistaken for grass due to their appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a period of dormancy to bloom successfully.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
