Hemionitis senae

Hemionitis senae, commonly known as the desert shield fern, is a unique plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is a small, evergreen fern that thrives in harsh, dry environments with minimal water. The plant has a compact rosette structure with thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss. Its fronds are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, adapted to withstand extreme temperatures.

Hemionitis senae is often found growing on rocky surfaces or in crevices, showcasing its resilience to desert conditions.
