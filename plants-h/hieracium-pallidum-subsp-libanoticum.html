Hieracium pallidum subsp. libanoticum

Hieracium pallidum subsp.

libanoticum, commonly known as the pale hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, including parts of Lebanon and Syria. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and open habitats.

The plant features basal rosettes of pale green, hairy leaves and produces yellowish to orange flowers in early summer. Its inflorescence consists of a single flower head with ray florets and a central disk of tubular flowers.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower structure and medicinal properties in some traditional uses.
