Heliopsis purpurea

Heliopsis purpurea, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, cone-shaped flower heads that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and features narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Heliopsis purpurea is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
