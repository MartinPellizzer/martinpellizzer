Hieracium kerneri subsp. balbisianum

Hieracium kerneri subsp.

balbisianum, commonly known as the Balbisian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring well-drained conditions. Its leaves are lanceolate, rough to the touch, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

It is known for its aggressive spreading habit, making it a common weed in agricultural and natural landscapes.
