Hieracium germanicum subsp. polakianum

Hieracium germanicum subsp.

polakianum, commonly known as the Polakian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering stems that bear yellow ray florets and tubular disk florets.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and pastures. It has a fibrous root system and can spread vegetatively through rhizomes.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the hawkweed group within the genus Hieracium.
