Hypoxis brownii

Hypoxis brownii, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a short, rhizomatous stem and produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that open at night. The plant has grass-like, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. Its flowers emit a sweet fragrance, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

Hypoxis brownii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique blooming habit.
