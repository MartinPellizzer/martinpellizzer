Hieracium farrense

Hieracium farrense, commonly known as the Farren's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

Hieracium farrense is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it both a resilient and sometimes invasive species.
