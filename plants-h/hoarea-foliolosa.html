Hoarea foliolosa

Hoarea foliolosa, commonly known as the leafy spurge, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of gray-green, succulent leaves and produces small, yellow flowers in clusters. The plant is adapted to dry, rocky environments and has a deep root system that allows it to survive in arid conditions. It is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and is considered an invasive species in some regions.

Hoarea foliolosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
