Hieracium leucothecum

Hieracium leucothecum, commonly known as the white-veined hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped with distinctive white veins, and the flowers are yellow with white markings.

It is often found in open habitats and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
