Hakea parallelinervis

Hakea parallelinervis, commonly known as the narrow-veined hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, narrow, needle-like leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. The plant produces striking, bottlebrush-like inflorescences that are typically red or pink in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Hakea parallelinervis is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
