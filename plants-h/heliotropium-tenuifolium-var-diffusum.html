Heliotropium tenuifolium var. diffusum

Heliotropium tenuifolium var. diffusum, commonly known as creeping heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a low-growing perennial with opposite, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive and hardy addition to diverse garden environments.
