Hieracium nigrescens subsp. commutatum

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

commutatum, commonly known as the darkening hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disc.

This subspecies is noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
