Hieracium moestum

Hieracium moestum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and upright flowering stems that bear yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant is native to Europe and has been introduced to other parts of the world, often becoming invasive in certain regions. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and other open habitats.

The leaves are usually hairy and have a serrated edge, while the flowers are typically arranged in a single head.
