Huernia macrocarpa var. arabica

Huernia macrocarpa var. arabica, commonly known as the Arabian moon cactus, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of the Middle East.

It features thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, which help reduce water loss.

It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
