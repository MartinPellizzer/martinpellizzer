Hosackia nudiflora

Hosackia nudiflora, commonly known as the nakedflower false indigo, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

The plant produces raceme-like clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils, making it a suitable choice for native gardens.
