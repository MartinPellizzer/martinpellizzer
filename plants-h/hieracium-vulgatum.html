Hieracium vulgatum

Hieracium vulgatum, commonly known as common hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while its stems are upright and often reddish in color.

Hieracium vulgatum is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
