Hieracium auriculiforme subsp. pictisquamum

Hieracium auriculiforme subsp.

pictisquamum, commonly known as the painted dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and distinctive, hairy leaves.

Native to Europe, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. The subspecies is distinguished by its unique leaf patterns and the presence of glandular dots.

It is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other sunny areas.
