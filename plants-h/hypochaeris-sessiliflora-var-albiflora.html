Hypochaeris sessiliflora var. albiflora

Hypochaeris sessiliflora var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered sow thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and disturbed soils, and it produces small, white flowers that bloom in the summer. It has a rosette of basal leaves and a flowering stem that rises from the center.

The variety albiflora is distinguished by its pale or white flowers, setting it apart from other varieties of Hypochaeris.
