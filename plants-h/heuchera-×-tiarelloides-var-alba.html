Heuchera × tiarelloides var. alba

Heuchera × tiarelloides var. alba, commonly known as the White Alumroot, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Saxifragaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely textured, silvery-white foliage that provides a striking contrast to its pinkish-red flower clusters. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 18 inches, with a compact, mound-like form. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

The flowers appear in late spring to early summer, adding ornamental value to the plant's appearance.
