Hieracium caesium subsp. inversum

Hieracium caesium subsp.

inversum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
