Humata chaerophylla

Humata chaerophylla, commonly known as the silver leaf plant, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray, fuzzy leaves that help reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Humata chaerophylla is often used in ornamental gardening for its distinctive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
