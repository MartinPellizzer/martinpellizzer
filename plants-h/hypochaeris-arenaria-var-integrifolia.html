Hypochaeris arenaria var. integrifolia

Hypochaeris arenaria var. integrifolia, commonly known as the sand dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid and sandy environments.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with hairy stems and basal leaves. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by distinctive seed heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and dunes.

Its adaptability to harsh conditions makes it an important pioneer species in ecological succession.
