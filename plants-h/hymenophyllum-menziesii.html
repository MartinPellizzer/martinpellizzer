Hymenophyllum menziesii

Hymenophyllum menziesii, commonly known as the silver maidenhair fern, is a delicate epiphytic fern native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silvery-green fronds that give it a distinctive, almost metallic appearance. The plant thrives in cool, moist, and shaded environments, often growing on rocks or tree trunks. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

Hymenophyllum menziesii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in shady gardens or as a houseplant.
