Hieracium caesium subsp. gonipleum

Hieracium caesium subsp.

gonipleum, commonly known as the golden hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and wastelands. Its leaves are lanceolate, hairy, and often have a reddish or purplish hue, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting pollinators.
