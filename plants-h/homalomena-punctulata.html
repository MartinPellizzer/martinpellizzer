Homalomena punctulata

Homalomena punctulata, commonly known as the spotted arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves. The plant features a thick, fleshy rhizome and produces striking, spatulate-shaped leaves with a waxy texture. Its flowers are typically small and clustered in an inflorescence called a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe.

Homalomena punctulata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to indoor conditions.
