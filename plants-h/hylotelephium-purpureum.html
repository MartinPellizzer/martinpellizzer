Hylotelephium purpureum

Hylotelephium purpureum, commonly known as the Armenian carpet or Armenian daisy, is a perennial succulent native to the Caucasus region.

It features dense, mat-forming growth with fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are often blue-green in color. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a rosette pattern. It is well adapted to rocky and dry environments, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ability to store water in its thick leaves allows it to thrive in arid conditions.
