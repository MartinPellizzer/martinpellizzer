Hieracium laniferum

Hieracium laniferum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is distinguished by its hairy stems and leaves, as well as its yellowish to reddish flower heads. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.

Hieracium laniferum is often found in open habitats and can form dense colonies, making it a notable weed in some agricultural areas.
