Haplopappus assuensis

Haplopappus assuensis, commonly known as the desert pappus, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers that are typically arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in hot, dry environments.

Haplopappus assuensis is well adapted to harsh desert conditions, making it a resilient and hardy species.
