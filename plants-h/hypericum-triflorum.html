Hypericum triflorum

Hypericum triflorum, commonly known as the three-flowered St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with four petals. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is often found in moist, open woodlands.

Hypericum triflorum is part of the Hypericaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive floral structure and medicinal historical uses.
