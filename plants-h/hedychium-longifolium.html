Hedychium longifolium

Hedychium longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved torch ginger, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces fragrant, yellow-orange flowers that resemble torches, often arranged in clusters at the top of the stems. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Hedychium longifolium is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
