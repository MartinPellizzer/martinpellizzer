Heliocereus superbus

Heliocereus superbus, commonly known as the sun cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in sharp spines and bear large, showy flowers that open at night. The plant is part of the Cactaceae family and is adapted to arid environments with minimal water requirements. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow and emit a sweet fragrance to attract nocturnal pollinators.

Heliocereus superbus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in harsh conditions.
