Hamadryas magellanica var. tomentosa

Hamadryas magellanica var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded areas.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
