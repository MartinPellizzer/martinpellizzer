Hypericum ascyron var. giraldii

Hypericum ascyron var. giraldii, commonly known as the golden-stemmed St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Hypericaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and woody stems. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This variety is often distinguished by its more elongated stems and slightly different leaf morphology compared to other Hypericum species.
