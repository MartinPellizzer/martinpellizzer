Hyphaene dichotoma

Hyphaene dichotoma, commonly known as the double-stemmed palm, is a distinctive plant native to the coastal regions of East Africa.

It is characterized by its unique growth pattern, with two main stems emerging from the base, giving it a striking appearance. The plant has a robust trunk that can reach several meters in height, and its leaves are long, pinnate, and often used for thatching. Hyphaene dichotoma thrives in saline and coastal environments, making it an important species for stabilizing dunes and preventing erosion.
