Halimium atriplicifolium var. calycinum

Halimium atriplicifolium var. calycinum, commonly known as the rock rose or the yellow rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub with greyish-green foliage and produces clusters of yellow flowers in early spring. The plant is characterized by its succulent leaves and is adapted to thrive in dry, rocky soils. It is often found in coastal areas and limestone habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and drought tolerance.
