Hieracium salevense subsp. seneciiflorum

Hieracium salevense subsp.

seneciiflorum, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disk.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and its role in alpine ecosystems.
