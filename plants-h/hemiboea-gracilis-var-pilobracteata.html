Hemiboea gracilis var. pilobracteata

Hemiboea gracilis var. pilobracteata, commonly known as the silky bract hemiboea, is a flowering plant native to the eastern regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, tubular flowers. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a hairy texture that gives the plant its common name. This variety is distinguished by its unique bracts, which are soft and hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in rock crevices or along stream banks.
