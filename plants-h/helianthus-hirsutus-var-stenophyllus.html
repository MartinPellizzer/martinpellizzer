Helianthus hirsutus var. stenophyllus

Helianthus hirsutus var. stenophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sunflower, is a wild sunflower species native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, hairy leaves that are typically less than 2 inches wide. The plant produces yellow, daisy-like flowers with a prominent central disk surrounded by ray florets. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies, roadsides, and disturbed areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
