Hieracium Bupleuroides Subsp. Inulifolium

Hieracium bupleuroides subsp. inulifolium is a lesser-known member of the dandelion family known for its unique leaf structure and delicate flowers. This subspecies is native to parts of Europe and has adapted to grow in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are characterized by their lobed edges and a subtle bluish tint, setting it apart from other members of the genus. While not as widely cultivated as other daisies, it holds ecological value by supporting pollinators and thriving in disturbed soils. Despite its modest appearance, it is a fascinating example of botanical diversity within the Asteraceae family.
