Hypericum sebasteum

Hypericum sebasteum, commonly known as the everlasting hypericum, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
