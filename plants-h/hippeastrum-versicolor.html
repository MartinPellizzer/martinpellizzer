Hippeastrum versicolor

Hippeastrum versicolor, commonly known as the Rainforest Bellflower, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant features strap-like, broad leaves that are often variegated with green and white patterns. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Hippeastrum versicolor is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
