Hieracium taurinense subsp. symphytaceum

Hieracium taurinense subsp.

symphytaceum, commonly known as the Taurine Hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces numerous small, yellow disk flowers.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
