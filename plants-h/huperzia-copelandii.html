Huperzia copelandii

Huperzia copelandii, commonly known as the Copeland's huperzia, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clustered, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in small, spore-producing structures called sporangia. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Its spore-based reproduction is a key feature of its life cycle, contributing to its ecological role in forest ecosystems.

The plant's unique morphology and habitat preferences make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
