Hancea cernua

Hancea cernua, commonly known as the hanging hancea, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pendulous, racemose inflorescences that bear small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

Hancea cernua is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral display.
