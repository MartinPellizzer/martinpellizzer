Hieracium gracile var. yukonense

Hieracium gracile var. yukonense, commonly known as Yukon hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Yukon region of Canada.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadside areas. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. It is characterized by its low-growing habit and is often found in arctic and alpine environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
