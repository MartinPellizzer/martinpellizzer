Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp. hypochoeroides

Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp.

hypochoeroides, commonly known as the narrowleaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, yellowish to brownish composite flowers.

The leaves are typically narrow and hairy, with a slightly serrated margin. This subspecies is native to parts of Europe and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological niche.
