Hypodiscus parkeri

Hypodiscus parkeri, commonly known as the African star orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow with intricate patterns. The plant produces small, elongated pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in dry conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern around the pseudobulbs.

Hypodiscus parkeri thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
