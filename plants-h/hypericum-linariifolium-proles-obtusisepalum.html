Hypericum linariifolium proles obtusisepalum

Hypericum linariifolium proles obtusisepalum, commonly known as the broad-sepal St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Hypericaceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers with prominent stamens. The plant typically grows in meadows and woodland edges, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
