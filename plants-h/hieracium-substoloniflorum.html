Hieracium substoloniflorum

Hieracium substoloniflorum, commonly known as the creeping hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, rough to the touch, and often have a hairy texture.

The flowers are yellow, with a central disk surrounded by petal-like rays, and it produces small, elongated achenes as its fruit.
