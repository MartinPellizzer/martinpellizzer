Hirtella lightioides

Hirtella lightioides, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often cultivated as a decorative shrub or small tree in tropical gardens. Its leaves are typically pubescent, giving it a soft, hairy texture.

Hirtella lightioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
