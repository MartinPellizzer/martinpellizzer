Hieracium hygrophilum

Hieracium hygrophilum, commonly known as the water hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to moist habitats in Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in wet meadows, stream banks, and marshy areas, thriving in humid conditions. Its flowers are yellow, with a central disc surrounded by ray florets, and it produces small, hairy seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Hieracium hygrophilum is often found in nutrient-rich soils and is adapted to tolerate periodic flooding.
