Hypericum trinervium

Hypericum trinervium, commonly known as the three-veined St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the genus Hypericum, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their yellow or orange flowers. The plant features small, yellow flowers with prominent stamens, and its leaves are typically opposite and have three distinct veins. It thrives in moist, woodlands and is often found in shaded environments.

The plant is part of the family Clusiaceae, also known as the guttiferae family.
