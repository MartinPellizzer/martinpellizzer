Helicia subcordata

Helicia subcordata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed holly.

It is a small evergreen shrub native to the wet tropical regions of Southeast Asia. The plant features narrow, leathery leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stems. Its flowers are small, yellowish, and appear in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.
