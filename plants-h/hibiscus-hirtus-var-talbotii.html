Hibiscus hirtus var. talbotii

Hibiscus hirtus var. talbotii, commonly known as the talbot's hibiscus, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically blooming in summer. The plant has hairy, rough-textured leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

Hibiscus hirtus var. talbotii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in garden settings.
