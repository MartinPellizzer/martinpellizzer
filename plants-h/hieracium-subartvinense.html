Hieracium subartvinense

Hieracium subartvinense, commonly known as the Subartvin hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Turkey.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine habitats, showcasing adaptations to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rough texture and a hairy surface, while its flowers are yellowish in color.

Hieracium subartvinense is a relatively rare species, contributing to the botanical diversity of its endemic region.
