Hieracium carpathicum subsp. subgracilescens

Hieracium carpathicum subsp.

subgracilescens, commonly known as the Carpathian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Carpathian Mountains. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and forest edges.

The plant has slender, hairy stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are lance-shaped with rough textures. It produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in calcareous soils.
