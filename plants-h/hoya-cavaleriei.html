Hoya cavaleriei

Hoya cavaleriei, commonly known as the "Cavalerie's Waxplant," is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and clusters of waxy, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical, with a smooth, glossy surface. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This plant is popular among indoor gardeners for its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
