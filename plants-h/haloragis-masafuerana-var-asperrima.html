Haloragis masafuerana var. asperrima

Haloragis masafuerana var. asperrima, commonly known as the rough haloragis, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fleshy, often hairy leaves that help it retain moisture in dry conditions. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale blue, adding a delicate touch to its rugged appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is a valuable species for ecological restoration in semi-arid environments.
