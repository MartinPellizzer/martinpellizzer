Hesperis triloba

Hesperis triloba, commonly known as the sweet rocket, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant features showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in racemes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Hesperis triloba is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
