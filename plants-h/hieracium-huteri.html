Hieracium huteri

Hieracium huteri, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and typically herbaceous growth habit. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in the summer months. It thrives in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Hieracium huteri is often found in meadows and slopes at high elevations, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
