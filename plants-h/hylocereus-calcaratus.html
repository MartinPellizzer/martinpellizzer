Hylocereus calcaratus

Hylocereus calcaratus, commonly known as the moon cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family and is native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a climbing cactus with thornless stems that often have a distinctive star-shaped pattern. The plant produces fragrant, night-blooming flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
