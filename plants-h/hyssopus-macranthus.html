Hyssopus macranthus

Hyssopus macranthus, commonly known as blue hyssop, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and spikes of blue or purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Hyssopus macranthus is also valued for its medicinal properties and historical use in herbal remedies.
