Hedysarum gramineum

Hedysarum gramineum, commonly known as the meadow vetchling, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along riverbanks. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are lance-shaped and hairy.

Hedysarum gramineum is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
