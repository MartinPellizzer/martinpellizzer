Hieracium graminifolium

Hieracium graminifolium, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in meadows and grassy areas. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can spread through rhizomes.

It is known for its ability to tolerate a range of soil conditions and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
