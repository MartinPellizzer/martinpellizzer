Hibiscus comoensis

Hibiscus comoensis, commonly known as the Como hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Hibiscus comoensis is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks, where it plays a role in supporting local biodiversity.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens in suitable climates.
