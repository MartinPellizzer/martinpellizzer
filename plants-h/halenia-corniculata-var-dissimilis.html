Halenia corniculata var. dissimilis

Halenia corniculata var. dissimilis, commonly known as the variable horned bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, tubular, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. The plant features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or shady environments.

It is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
