Halopyrum mucronatum

Halopyrum mucronatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf hawksbeard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a rough texture, and it produces yellow flowers in late summer.

The species is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in some agricultural settings.
