Hexaspermum paniculatum

Hexaspermum paniculatum, commonly known as the African star grass, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features a robust, upright stem with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in panicles, which are typically white or pale yellow. Its roots are fibrous and have been traditionally used in herbal medicine.

Hexaspermum paniculatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
