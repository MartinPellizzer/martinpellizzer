Holcus australis

Holcus australis, commonly known as the Australian hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, soft foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along watercourses. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, greenish flowers.

Holcus australis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
