Hieracium submaculosum

Hieracium submaculosum, commonly known as the spotted hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and ragworts, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped with hairy surfaces, and the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is known for its ability to spread rapidly through rhizomes, making it an invasive species in some regions.
