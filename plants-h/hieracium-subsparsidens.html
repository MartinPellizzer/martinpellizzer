Hieracium subsparsidens

Hieracium subsparsidens, commonly known as the sparse-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are alternate, rough, and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium subsparsidens is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting pollinators.
