Hemionitis rufa

Hemionitis rufa, commonly known as the red moss, is a unique aquatic plant belonging to the family Hemionitidaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, carpet-like growth of small, bright green to reddish-brown leaves that form a thick mat on the surface of still or slow-moving freshwater. The plant lacks true roots and instead relies on rhizoids for anchoring and nutrient absorption. It thrives in nutrient-rich, shaded environments and is often found in ponds, lakes, and wetlands.

Hemionitis rufa plays a role in stabilizing water ecosystems by reducing erosion and providing habitat for microorganisms.
