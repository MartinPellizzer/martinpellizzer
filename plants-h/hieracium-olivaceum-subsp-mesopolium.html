Hieracium olivaceum subsp. mesopolium

Hieracium olivaceum subsp.

mesopolium, commonly known as the olive hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is known for its ecological adaptability and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
