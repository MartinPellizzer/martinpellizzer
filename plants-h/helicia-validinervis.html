Helicia validinervis

Helicia validinervis, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It features elliptical, glossy dark green leaves with prominent veins, which give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by bright orange or red berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.

Helicia validinervis is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
