Hieracium diaphanoides subsp. pluriserratum

Hieracium diaphanoides subsp.

pluriserratum, commonly known as the many-toothed hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are generally lanceolate, with multiple serrations along the margins, contributing to its common name.

The flowers are yellow, and the plant is often found in temperate climates with well-drained soils.
