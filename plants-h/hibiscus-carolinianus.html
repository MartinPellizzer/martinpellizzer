Hibiscus carolinianus

Hibiscus carolinianus, commonly known as the Carolina rose, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 feet. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, single or double flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. Hibiscus carolinianus thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be found in gardens and natural habitats across its native range.
