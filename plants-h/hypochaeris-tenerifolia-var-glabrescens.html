Hypochaeris tenerifolia var. glabrescens

Hypochaeris tenerifolia var. glabrescens, commonly known as the Canary Islands hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and coastal areas. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months.

Its small, basal leaves are usually covered in fine hairs, which are less pronounced in this variety compared to the typical form.
