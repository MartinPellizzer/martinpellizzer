Halothamnus schurobi

Halothamnus schurobi, commonly known as the Schurov's halothamnus, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage, which helps it conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically small and fleshy, adapted to reduce water loss through transpiration.

Halothamnus schurobi plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is often found in semi-desert and steppe ecosystems.
