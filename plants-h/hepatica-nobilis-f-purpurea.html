Hepatica nobilis f. purpurea

Hepatica nobilis f. purpurea, commonly known as the purple hepatica, is a flowering plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves and showy, early spring blooms. The flowers typically appear in shades of white, pink, or purple, with five petals and a central cluster of stamens. This perennial plant thrives in woodland habitats and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

It is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
