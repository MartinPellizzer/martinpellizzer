Hesperis intricatissima

Hesperis intricatissima, commonly known as the intricate horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with characteristic four-petaled flowers. The plant is known for its striking, spurred petals that give it a distinctive appearance, and it typically blooms in late spring to early summer. Hesperis intricatissima prefers well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine habitats.

Its ornamental value has made it a sought-after plant in gardens and botanical collections.
