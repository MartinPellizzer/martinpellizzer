Haloschoenus emaciatus

Haloschoenus emaciatus, commonly known as the slender flat sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.

Due to its preference for wet conditions, it is often found in areas with seasonal flooding.
