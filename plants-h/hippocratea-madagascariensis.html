Hippocratea madagascariensis

Hippocratea madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a striking plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms with large, white or pale pink petals and a distinctive yellow lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
