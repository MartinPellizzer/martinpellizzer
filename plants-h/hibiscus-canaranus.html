Hibiscus canaranus

Hibiscus canaranus, commonly known as the Brazilian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a smooth, waxy texture.

Hibiscus canaranus thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
