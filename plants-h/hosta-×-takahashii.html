Hosta × takahashii

Hosta × takahashii, commonly known as "Blue Moon," is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Hosta plantaginea and Hosta ventricosa.

It is characterized by its broad, waxy, blue-green leaves that are often adorned with a silvery-blue margin. The plant is prized for its striking foliage and is often cultivated as an ornamental in shady garden settings. It typically grows to a height of 12 to 18 inches and spreads to about 24 inches wide.

Hosta × takahashii prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
