Hieracium atratum subsp. vatricosum

Hieracium atratum subsp.

vatricosum, commonly known as the black hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its dark green, hairy leaves and yellowish to orange-red flowers that appear in clustered inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. It has a rosette of basal leaves and a flowering stem that can reach up to 30 cm in height.

The species is native to Europe and has been introduced to other parts of the world, where it can sometimes become invasive.
