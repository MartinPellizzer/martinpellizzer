Herminium bulleyi

Herminium bulleyi, commonly known as the Bulley's twayblade, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the orchid family (Orchidaceae).

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. Native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly Chile and Argentina, this plant thrives in cool, moist environments. Herminium bulleyi has a rhizomatous root system and produces tiny, scale-like leaves that help it conserve water.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
