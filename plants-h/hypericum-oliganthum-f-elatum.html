Hypericum oliganthum f. elatum

Hypericum oliganthum f. elatum, commonly known as the tall St. John's wort, is a botanical variant of the St. John's wort species.

It is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Europe and Asia, characterized by its erect growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that attract pollinators. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or wildflower meadows.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
