Haplopappus detonsus

Haplopappus detonsus, commonly known as the desert pappus, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive bracts. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is well adapted to drought conditions. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in spring, and the plant produces fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.

Haplopappus detonsus is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats, contributing to the ecological diversity of these environments.
