Hypericum simplicistylum

Hypericum simplicistylum, commonly known as the simple-stemmed St. John's wort, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Hypericaceae.

It is characterized by its upright, herbaceous stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers with four petals, which bloom in late summer. Hypericum simplicistylum is native to certain regions of southern Africa and thrives in well-drained soils.

It is often found in open woodlands and rocky outcrops, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
