Hibiscus chrysinocolla

Hibiscus chrysinocolla, commonly known as the golden-hair hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive golden stamen that resembles hair, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with heart-shaped, dark green leaves. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are known for their vibrant color and long-lasting appearance.

Hibiscus chrysinocolla is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
