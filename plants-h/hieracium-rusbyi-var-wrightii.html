Hieracium rusbyi var. wrightii

Hieracium rusbyi var. wrightii, commonly known as Wright's common hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. It has basal leaves that are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

The yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators.
