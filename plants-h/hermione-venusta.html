Hermione venusta

Hermione venusta, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
