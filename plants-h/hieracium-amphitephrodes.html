Hieracium amphitephrodes

Hieracium amphitephrodes, commonly known as the double-headed hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough, hairy texture, while the flowers are yellow with a distinctive double head appearance.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
