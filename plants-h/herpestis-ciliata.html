Herpestis ciliata

Herpestis ciliata, commonly known as the hairy leaf herb, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. Herpestis ciliata thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or near stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
