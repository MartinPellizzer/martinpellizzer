Heliopsis longipes

Heliopsis longipes, commonly known as the longfoot sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers and linear, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. It produces long, slender seed stalks that give it its common name.

Heliopsis longipes is a member of the Asteraceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
