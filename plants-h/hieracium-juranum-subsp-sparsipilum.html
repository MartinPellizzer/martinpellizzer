Hieracium juranum subsp. sparsipilum

Hieracium juranum subsp.

sparsipilum, commonly known as the sparse-haired hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a flowering head of yellow flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its sparse, short hairs on the leaves and stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

The plant is often found in the Carpathian region and is adapted to temperate climates.
