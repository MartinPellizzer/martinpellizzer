Hypochaeris albiflora

Hypochaeris albiflora, commonly known as the white false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas and thrives in full sun. Its white to pale yellow flowers bloom in the spring and early summer, followed by fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Hypochaeris albiflora is often considered a weed but can be grown in gardens for its ornamental value.
