Hieracium listerae

Hieracium listerae, commonly known as the Lister's hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are basal, lanceolate, and often covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head.

Hieracium listerae is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain habitats.
