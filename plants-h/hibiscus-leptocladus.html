Hibiscus leptocladus

Hibiscus leptocladus, commonly known as the slender-leaved hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Hibiscus leptocladus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
