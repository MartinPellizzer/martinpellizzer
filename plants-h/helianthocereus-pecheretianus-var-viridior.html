Helianthocereus pecheretianus var. viridior

Helianthocereus pecheretianus var. viridior, commonly known as the green-flowered night-blooming cactus, is a unique cactus species native to South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, columnar stems and produces large, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with thick, fleshy stems that store water. Its flowers are typically greenish-white, with a subtle fragrance that attracts nocturnal pollinators.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ecological role in desert ecosystems.
