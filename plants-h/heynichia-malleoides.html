Heynichia malleoides

Heynichia malleoides, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, tubular yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for native gardens and ecological restoration projects.
