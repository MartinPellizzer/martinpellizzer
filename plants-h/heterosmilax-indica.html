Heterosmilax indica

Heterosmilax indica, commonly known as the Indian arrowroot or arrowleaf arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and riverbanks. The plant features long, arrow-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. Its rhizomes are often harvested for their starchy roots, which are used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

The botanical characteristics of Heterosmilax indica make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
