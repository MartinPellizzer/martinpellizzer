Hypoxis salina

Hypoxis salina, commonly known as the saltbush, is a perennial herb native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in saline and arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a cluster, typically blooming in late spring or early summer. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to anchor it in sandy or salty soils.

Hypoxis salina is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
