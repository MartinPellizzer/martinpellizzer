Hibiscus trionicus

Hibiscus trionicus, commonly known as the scarlet hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from red to pink and yellow. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

Hibiscus trionicus thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found near coastal areas and in humid environments.
