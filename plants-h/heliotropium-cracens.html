Heliotropium cracens

Heliotropium cracens, commonly known as the tropical heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a low-growing annual herb with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, which bloom in the afternoon. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and gardens.

Heliotropium cracens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
