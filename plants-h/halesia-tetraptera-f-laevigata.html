Halesia tetraptera f. laevigata

Halesia tetraptera f. laevigata, commonly known as the smooth-leaf halesia, is a cultivar of the halesia plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, glossy leaves and clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 20 feet. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
