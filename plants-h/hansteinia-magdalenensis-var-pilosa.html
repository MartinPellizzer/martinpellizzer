Hansteinia magdalenensis var. pilosa

Hansteinia magdalenensis var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy hansteinia, is a species of bromeliad native to Colombia.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage that is covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it a unique texture. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in rocky or forested areas. Its inflorescence produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to indoor cultivation.
