Hagaea latifolia

Hagaea latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf hagaea, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. Hagaea latifolia is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, storing water in its fleshy stems and leaves.

It is often found growing on rocky outcrops and in sandy soils, making it a resilient and hardy species.
