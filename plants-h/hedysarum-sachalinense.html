Hedysarum sachalinense

Hedysarum sachalinense, commonly known as the Sakhalin milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Far East of Russia and parts of China.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its root system is fibrous and can form dense colonies, contributing to soil stabilization.

Hedysarum sachalinense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has also been studied for its potential ecological and medicinal properties.
