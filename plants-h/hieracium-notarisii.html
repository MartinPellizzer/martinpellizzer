Hieracium notarisii

Hieracium notarisii, commonly known as the Balkan hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy stems. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

Hieracium notarisii is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
