Homalium moniliforme subsp. moniliforme

Homalium moniliforme subsp.

moniliforme, commonly known as the clustered holly or clustered euphorbia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America. It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its clustered growth habit and distinctive, spiny leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences, which are surrounded by specialized bracts. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and unique morphology, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
