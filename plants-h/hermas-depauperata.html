Hermas depauperata

Hermas depauperata, commonly known as the poor man's orchid, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, linear leaves and clusters of tiny, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its botanical features include a short, branching stem and a lack of a prominent root system.

Hermas depauperata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ease of care.
