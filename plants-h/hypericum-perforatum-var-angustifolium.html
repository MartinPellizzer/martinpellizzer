Hypericum perforatum var. angustifolium

Hypericum perforatum var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved St. John's wort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, ascending stems that bear opposite, linear to oblanceolate leaves with translucent glands along the midrib. The plant produces small, yellow-orange flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are often perforated, giving it the common name "perforate," and it is known for its medicinal properties.

It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
