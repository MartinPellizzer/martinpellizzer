Hermidium alipes var. pallidum

Hermidium alipes var. pallidum, commonly known as the pale-stemmed hermidium, is a variant of the hermidium plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its pale green stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in botanical gardens.
