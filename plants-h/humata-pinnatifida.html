Humata pinnatifida

Humata pinnatifida, commonly known as the broadleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed and have a pinnate arrangement. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, yellow, and orange, which bloom throughout the warm season. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Humata pinnatifida is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
