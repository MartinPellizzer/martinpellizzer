Herschelia excelsa

Herschelia excelsa, commonly known as the tall milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it has a milky sap that is toxic to many animals.

Herschelia excelsa is often found in grasslands and open woodlands, where it thrives in full sun.
