Hemipilia capitata

Hemipilia capitata, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, white to pale pink flowers.

Hemipilia capitata is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its traditional use in herbal medicine.
