Hieracium murorum subsp. heterocladophorum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

heterocladophorum, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rough texture. This subspecies is native to Europe and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

It plays a role in supporting pollinators and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
