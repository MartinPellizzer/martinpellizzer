Homalomena teysmannii

Homalomena teysmannii, commonly known as the Teyssmann's arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its arrowhead-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers surrounded by a spadix, typical of the genus. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its foliage is glossy and can grow up to several feet in length, making it a striking addition to tropical gardens.
