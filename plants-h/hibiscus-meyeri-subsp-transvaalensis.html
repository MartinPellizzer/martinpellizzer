Hibiscus meyeri subsp. transvaalensis

Hibiscus meyeri subsp.

transvaalensis, commonly known as the Transvaal hibiscus, is a shrub native to South Africa. It features showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically blooming in summer.

The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are dark green and often have a slight hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
