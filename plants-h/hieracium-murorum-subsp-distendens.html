Hieracium murorum subsp. distendens

Hieracium murorum subsp.

distendens, commonly known as the broad-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are typically broad and lance-shaped, with a rough, hairy texture.

It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.
