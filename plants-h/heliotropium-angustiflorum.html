Heliotropium angustiflorum

Heliotropium angustiflorum, commonly known as narrow-flower heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing herb with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming primarily in the summer months. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
