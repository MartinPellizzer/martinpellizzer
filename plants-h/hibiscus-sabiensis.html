Hibiscus sabiensis

Hibiscus sabiensis, commonly known as the Chinese hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features large, showy flowers with five deeply lobed petals, typically in shades of red, pink, or white. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are glossy and arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Hibiscus sabiensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
