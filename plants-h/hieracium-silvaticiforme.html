Hieracium silvaticiforme

Hieracium silvaticiforme, commonly known as the woodland hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces yellow flowers with ray florets. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and moist meadows, preferring partial to full shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly rough texture.

It is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and is often found in forested regions across Europe.
