Hieracium umbellatum subsp. pervagum

Hieracium umbellatum subsp.

pervagum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellowish-green flower heads.

The plant features basal and alternate leaves that are typically lanceolate to ovate in shape. Its flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, known as a corymb, and are accompanied by bracts.

It is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
