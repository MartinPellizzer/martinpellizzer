Hieracium onosmoides subsp. sanctae-balmae

Hieracium onosmoides subsp.

sanctae-balmae, commonly known as the Holy Balm hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in dry, sunny grasslands and rocky soils, preferring well-drained environments.

The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, silver-haired leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in late spring to summer. Its subspecies is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology and floral characteristics.

It is often found in alpine and subalpine regions, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
