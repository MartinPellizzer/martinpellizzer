Hypericum cerastoides var. simplex

Hypericum cerastoides var. simplex, commonly known as the simple-stemmed St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with four petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often used in native plant gardens.
