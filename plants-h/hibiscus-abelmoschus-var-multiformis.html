Hibiscus abelmoschus var. multiformis

Hibiscus abelmoschus var. multiformis, commonly known as the Chinese okra or Abelmoschus, is a tropical plant characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers.

It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant produces edible pods that are often used in Asian cuisine, similar to okra. Its flowers range in color from white to deep red, adding ornamental value to gardens.

Hibiscus abelmoschus var. multiformis is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
