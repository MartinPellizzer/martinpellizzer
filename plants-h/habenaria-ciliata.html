Habenaria ciliata

Habenaria ciliata, commonly known as the ciliate orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and swampy areas. The plant features a pseudobulb with a single leaf and produces a raceme of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. Its roots are often covered in fine, ciliated hairs, giving it its scientific name.

The orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
