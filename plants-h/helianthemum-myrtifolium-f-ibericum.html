Helianthemum myrtifolium f. ibericum

Helianthemum myrtifolium f. ibericum, commonly known as the Iberian rockrose, is a succulent shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features compact, densely branched stems that are often covered in a powdery white coating. The leaves are small, fleshy, and typically blue-green in color, adapted to store water. It produces clusters of yellow flowers in spring, which are followed by small, red or orange fruits.

This plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.
