Hieracium corbuligerum

Hieracium corbuligerum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy stems, which are typical features of the genus Hieracium. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine, stiff hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Hieracium corbuligerum is an invasive species in some regions, outcompeting native vegetation and altering local ecosystems.
