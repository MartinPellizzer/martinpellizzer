Hyptis crenata var. augustifolia

Hyptis crenata var. augustifolia, commonly known as silverleaf hyptis, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features silver-gray, hairy leaves that are lance-shaped and oppositely arranged on the stem. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its aromatic foliage and compact growth habit make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
