Hymenophyllum fraseri

Hymenophyllum fraseri, commonly known as the Fraser's filmy fern, is a delicate, epiphytic fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, translucent fronds that resemble lace, with a unique structure that allows it to thrive in moist, shaded environments. The plant's rhizomes are short and often covered in a layer of moss-like tissue, which aids in water retention. It is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing on tree trunks or rocks in humid forests.

Its intricate morphology and specialized adaptations make it a fascinating subject in the study of bryophyte and fern evolution.
