Huberia nettoana

Huberia nettoana, commonly known as the Nettoana lily, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces large, showy flowers with white petals and a distinctive violet or purple throat. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Huberia nettoana thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest understories.
