Hieracium dasylepioides

Hieracium dasylepioides, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous tiny florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are usually basal, with a rough texture and a slightly hairy surface.
