Hicorius alba var. anomala

Hicorius alba var. anomala, commonly known as the white violets, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a smooth texture, while the stems are slender and often hairy.
