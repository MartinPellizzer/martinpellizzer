Hieracium murorum subsp. bifurcatum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

bifurcatum, commonly known as the forked hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

The plant features bright yellow composite flowers with a distinctive forked shape in the central disk. Its leaves are generally basal, with a rough texture and serrated edges.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ability to thrive in various environmental conditions.
