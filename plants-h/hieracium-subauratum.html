Hieracium subauratum

Hieracium subauratum, commonly known as the golden hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in meadows and grasslands. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, surrounded by dark green, lance-shaped leaves. It has a hairy stem and produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Hieracium subauratum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
