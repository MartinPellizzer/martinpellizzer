Hedyotis elegans

Hedyotis elegans, commonly known as the pretty grass or silverweed, is a small herbaceous plant native to South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant has delicate, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, white or yellowish flowers clustered in umbels. Hedyotis elegans is known for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and modest flowering display.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
