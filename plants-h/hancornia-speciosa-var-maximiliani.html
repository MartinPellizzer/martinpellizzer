Hancornia speciosa var. maximiliani

Hancornia speciosa var. maximiliani, commonly known as the "Mexican beautyberry," is a deciduous shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its clusters of showy, violet-blue berries that persist through winter, providing food for birds and other wildlife. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy surface. It produces small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by the distinctive berries.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in gardens and natural habitats.
