Hedyotis leucophylla

Hedyotis leucophylla, commonly known as white-leafed dotweed, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small herbaceous perennial with slender stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Hedyotis leucophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and subtle floral display.
