Harpullia juglandifolia var. multiflora

Harpullia juglandifolia var. multiflora, commonly known as the multiflora chestnut.

It is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves and showy yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are clustered in panicles, which mature into woody, chestnut-like drupes. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
