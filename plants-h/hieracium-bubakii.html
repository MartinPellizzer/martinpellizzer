Hieracium bubakii

Hieracium bubakii, commonly known as the Balkan hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and forest edges. It has hairy stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped with serrated edges.

Its yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
