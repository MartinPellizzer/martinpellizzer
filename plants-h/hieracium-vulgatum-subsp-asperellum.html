Hieracium vulgatum subsp. asperellum

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

asperellum, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are rough to the touch, with serrated edges, and its yellow flowers are arranged in dense clusters.

It is often considered a weed in agricultural areas due to its aggressive spreading habit.
