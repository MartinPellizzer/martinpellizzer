Hibiscus timorensis

Hibiscus timorensis, commonly known as the Timor hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the island of Timor in Indonesia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring broad, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, which bloom during the warmer months. Hibiscus timorensis is adapted to tropical climates and prefers well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular ornamental plant in horticulture.
