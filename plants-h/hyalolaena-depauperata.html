Hyalolaena depauperata

Hyalolaena depauperata, commonly known as the slender hyaline grass, is a member of the Poaceae family, characterized by its delicate and grass-like appearance.

It is a small perennial herb that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale or translucent florets. It is native to South America and is adapted to thrive in arid or semi-arid environments.
