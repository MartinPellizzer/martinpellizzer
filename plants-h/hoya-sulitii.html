Hoya sulitii

Hoya sulitii, commonly known as the "Coral Hoya," is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of waxy, coral-pink flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a glossy texture. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This plant is popular among indoor gardeners for its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
