Hieracium laevigatum subsp. svegense

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

svegense, commonly known as the Svegian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, preferring temperate climates. It has a fibrous root system and produces seeds in a disk-like structure surrounded by bracts.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
