Hieracium bifidum subsp. subcordigerum

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

subcordigerum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and typically hairy stems.

The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are yellow and are typically borne in clusters, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.
