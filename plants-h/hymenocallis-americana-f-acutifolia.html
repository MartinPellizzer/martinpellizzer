Hymenocallis americana f. acutifolia

Hymenocallis americana f. acutifolia, commonly known as the sharpleaf hurricane lily, is a botanical variant of the hurricane lily species.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida, and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. The plant features narrow, strap-like leaves with a sharp, pointed tip, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically orange or yellow, with a distinctive, trumpet-shaped corona.

This variety is known for its hardiness and ability to bloom reliably in coastal and tropical climates.
