Hydrogeton heterophyllus

Hydrogeton heterophyllus, commonly known as the variable water plant, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its ability to exhibit different leaf shapes depending on its environmental conditions.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds and wetlands, where it can thrive in both submerged and emergent conditions. The plant's leaves are often elongated and ribbon-like when submerged, but become broader and more rounded when growing in shallower waters. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown, which are adapted for pollination by water insects.

Hydrogeton heterophyllus plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
