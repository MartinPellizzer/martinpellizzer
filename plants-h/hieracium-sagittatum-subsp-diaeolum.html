Hieracium sagittatum subsp. diaeolum

Hieracium sagittatum subsp.

diaeolum, commonly known as the arrowhead hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer.

This subspecies is native to central and eastern Europe, often found in meadows and grassy areas. The plant has a creeping growth habit with hairy stems and is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is of interest in botanical studies due to its morphological features.
