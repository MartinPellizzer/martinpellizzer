Hieracium gigacantabricum

Hieracium gigacantabricum, commonly known as the giant hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to western Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy habitats and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

However, it is considered an invasive species in some regions due to its rapid spread and competitive nature.
