Hypolepis repens var. inermis

Hypolepis repens var. inermis, commonly known as the creeping shield fern, is a variant of the shield fern species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a rhizomatous fern that spreads via underground stems, forming dense mats on the forest floor. The plant features lance-shaped, serrated leaves that are typically bright green and glossy. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical forests.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for use in ornamental gardening and as a groundcover in shaded landscapes.
