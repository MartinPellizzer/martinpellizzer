Hydriastele variabilis var. sphaerocarpa

Hydriastele variabilis var. sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the globe hydriastele, is a unique member of the Bromeliaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit and thick, waxy leaves that store water, an adaptation to its native arid environments. The plant produces striking, showy inflorescences with vibrant red or orange flowers, which attract pollinators such as birds and insects. Its leaves are often adorned with intricate patterns and can vary in color depending on environmental conditions.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soil with indirect sunlight.
