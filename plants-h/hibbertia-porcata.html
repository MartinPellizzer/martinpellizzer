Hibbertia porcata

Hibbertia porcata, commonly known as the pigface plant, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fleshy, pig-like face-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal regions.

Hibbertia porcata is a hardy, drought-tolerant species that is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
