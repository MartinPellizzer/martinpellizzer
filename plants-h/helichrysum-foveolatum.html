Helichrysum foveolatum

Helichrysum foveolatum, commonly known as the golden crown, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flowers that resemble small crowns. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, daisy-like flowers in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Helichrysum foveolatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
