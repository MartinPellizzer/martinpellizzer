Hippochaete × arctica

Hippochaete × arctica, commonly known as the Arctic dandelion, is a hybrid plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in cold, harsh environments, often found in tundra and alpine habitats. The plant features a rosette of broad, spoon-shaped leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, typical of the Compositae family.

Hippochaete × arctica plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing food for local wildlife in its ecosystem.
