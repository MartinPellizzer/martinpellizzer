Hieracium pamphilii subsp. subtomentosum

Hieracium pamphilii subsp.

subtomentosum, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and southern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, often reddish stems and basal rosettes of leaves.

The flowers are typically yellow and arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats, often found in meadows and along roadsides.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dense, woolly pubescence, which gives it its common name.
