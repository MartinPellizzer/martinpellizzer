Helianthus × divariserratus

Helianthus × divariserratus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sunflower, is a hybrid species within the sunflower genus.

It is characterized by its narrow, serrated leaves and yellow to orange flower heads. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks. It produces composite flowers with ray florets that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
