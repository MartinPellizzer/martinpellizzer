Horsfieldia subtilis var. rostrata

Horsfieldia subtilis var. rostrata, commonly known as the rostrated orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers with a unique rostrum-like structure on the lip, which is a distinguishing feature of this variety. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in primary rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
