Hypericum millefolium

Hypericum millefolium, commonly known as St. John's Wort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and pinnately compound leaves that give it the common name "mile-a-minute.". The plant contains various bioactive compounds, including hypericin and hyperforin, which have been studied for their potential medicinal properties. It typically grows in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Hypericum millefolium is also known for its historical use in herbal medicine for treating mild depression and anxiety.
