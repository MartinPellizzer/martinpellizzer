Hinterhubera adenopetala

Hinterhubera adenopetala, commonly known as the velvetleaf orchid, is a striking orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, velvety green leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are borne on tall, branched inflorescences, and it has a complex root system adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

Hinterhubera adenopetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
