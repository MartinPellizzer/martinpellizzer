Hedyotis nudicaulis

Hedyotis nudicaulis, commonly known as the slender bladdernut, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features small, white to pale yellow flowers clustered in racemes, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Hedyotis nudicaulis is valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various insects.
