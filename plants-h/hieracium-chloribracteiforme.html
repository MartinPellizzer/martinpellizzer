Hieracium chloribracteiforme

Hieracium chloribracteiforme, commonly known as the green bract hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. The species is notable for its ability to thrive in disturbed habitats and its role in supporting pollinator populations.
