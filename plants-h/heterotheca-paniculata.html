Heterotheca paniculata

Heterotheca paniculata, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows in open areas such as prairies and meadows, preferring well-drained soil and full sun. Its structure includes a central cone-like flower head surrounded by ray florets, and it is known for its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Heterotheca paniculata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in various environmental conditions.
