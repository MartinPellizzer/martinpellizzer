Hieracium onosmoides subsp. subrude

Hieracium onosmoides subsp.

subrude, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has hairy, rough stems and basal leaves that are lanceolate in shape.

Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, producing yellow flowers that attract various pollinators.
