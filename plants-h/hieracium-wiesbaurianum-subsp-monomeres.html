Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp. monomeres

Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp.

monomeres, commonly known as the narrowleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as meadows and roadsides, and is often found in moist environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
