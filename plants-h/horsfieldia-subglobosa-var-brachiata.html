Horsfieldia subglobosa var. brachiata

Horsfieldia subglobosa var. brachiata, commonly known as the hairy orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a compact, rounded growth habit and dense, hairy foliage. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pale yellow to orange in color. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in montane forests.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a popular subject in botanical studies and horticulture.
