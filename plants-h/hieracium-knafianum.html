Hieracium knafianum

Hieracium knafianum, commonly known as knafian meadowhawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers, which are typically arranged in a flat-topped cluster. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly lobed, and it thrives in moist, grassy habitats. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, where it is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

Hieracium knafianum is a perennial herb that can grow up to 60 cm tall, with a fibrous root system that helps it establish in various soil types.
