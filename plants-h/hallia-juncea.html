Hallia juncea

Hallia juncea, commonly known as yellow cress, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like mustard and cabbage. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides or in meadows. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in early spring.

Hallia juncea is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and can reach a height of up to 30 centimeters.
