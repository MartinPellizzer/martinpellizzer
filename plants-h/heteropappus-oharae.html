Heteropappus oharae

Heteropappus oharae, commonly known as Ohara's heteropappus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive bracts. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, and its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species in its native range.
