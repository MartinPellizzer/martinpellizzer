Haenianthus salicifolius var. typicus

Haenianthus salicifolius var. typicus, commonly known as the willow-leaved bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, willow-like leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to withstand saline conditions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
