Hesperis incrassata

Hesperis incrassata, commonly known as the Hoary Bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its foliage is covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a frosted appearance, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

Hesperis incrassata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
