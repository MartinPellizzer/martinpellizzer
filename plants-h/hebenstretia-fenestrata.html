Hebenstretia fenestrata

Hebenstretia fenestrata, commonly known as the windowleaf plant, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with distinctive fenestrations, or leaf windows, that create a unique visual effect. The plant produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for tropical gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in outdoor landscapes.
