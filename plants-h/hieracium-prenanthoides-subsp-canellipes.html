Hieracium prenanthoides subsp. canellipes

Hieracium prenanthoides subsp.

canellipes, commonly known as the narrowleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges.

The plant features basal leaves that are narrow and linear, with a hairy surface, and produces yellow composite flowers in clusters. Its inflorescence consists of a single central flower head surrounded by bracts.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and is often found in disturbed or nutrient-rich soils.
