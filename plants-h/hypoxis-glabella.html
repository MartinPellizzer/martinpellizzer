Hypoxis glabella

Hypoxis glabella, commonly known as the African glabella, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow, star-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its botanical significance lies in its medicinal uses and its role in traditional African herbal medicine.
