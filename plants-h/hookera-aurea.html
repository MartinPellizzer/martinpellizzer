Hookera aurea

Hookera aurea, commonly known as the golden hooker, is a species of flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has a rosette-shaped structure with waxy, strap-like leaves that help it retain moisture in its arid environment. Hookera aurea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant color and unique appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.
