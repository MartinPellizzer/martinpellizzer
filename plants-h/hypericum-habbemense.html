Hypericum habbemense

Hypericum habbemense, commonly known as the yellow St. John's wort, is a shrub native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typically from June to August. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, orange-red fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the genus Hypericum, known for its medicinal and ornamental value.
