Hexastemon lanatus

Hexastemon lanatus, commonly known as the woolly hexastemon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swamps.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
