Hexachlamys itatiaiensis

Hexachlamys itatiaiensis, commonly known as the Itatiaia orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring six stamens and a distinctive labellum. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments within the Atlantic Forest biome. It has small, greenish-white flowers that bloom during the warmer months.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
