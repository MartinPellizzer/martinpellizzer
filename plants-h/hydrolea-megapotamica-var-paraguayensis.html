Hydrolea megapotamica var. paraguayensis

Hydrolea megapotamica var. paraguayensis, commonly known as the Paraguay hydrolea, is a species native to South America, particularly found in Paraguay and parts of Brazil.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences, which bloom during the warmer months. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained soils, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
