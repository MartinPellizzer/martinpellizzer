Hypericum guineense

Hypericum guineense, commonly known as African St. John's Wort, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It features small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from August to October. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces elliptical, woody capsules that contain numerous small seeds. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and savannas.

Hypericum guineense is also noted for its medicinal uses in traditional African medicine.
