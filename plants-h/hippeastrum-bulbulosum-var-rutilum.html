Hippeastrum bulbulosum var. rutilum

Hippeastrum bulbulosum var. rutilum, commonly known as the golden bulb.

It is a species of amaryllid native to South America, particularly Brazil. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with a red or purple throat. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, emerging from a bulbous underground stem.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of growing conditions.
