Hieracium picturatum

Hieracium picturatum, commonly known as the painted hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features bright yellow or orange flowers that are surrounded by overlapping, petal-like bracts, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it often grows in meadows and grasslands.

Hieracium picturatum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
