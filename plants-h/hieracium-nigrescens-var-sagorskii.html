Hieracium nigrescens var. sagorskii

Hieracium nigrescens var. sagorskii, commonly known as the Black-centred hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellowish to brownish flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are usually arranged in a single head.

This variant is distinguished by its darkened flower heads and is considered a rare and localized species.
