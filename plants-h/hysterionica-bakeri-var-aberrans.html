Hysterionica bakeri var. aberrans

Hysterionica bakeri var. aberrans, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, violet-blue flower heads that are surrounded by pale yellow to white ray florets. The plant has a robust root system and lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
