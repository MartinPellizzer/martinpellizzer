Heliocereus schrankii

Heliocereus schrankii, commonly known as the sun cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are typically covered in spines. The plant produces large, showy flowers that open at night, often in shades of yellow or orange. It thrives in arid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Heliocereus schrankii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming habit.
