Hedysarum edule

Hedysarum edule, commonly known as the edible vetch, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to regions of Europe and Asia, often found in grasslands and open woodlands. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by pods containing edible seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets.

Hedysarum edule is valued for its nutritional content and has been used in traditional agriculture and as a forage crop.
