Halimium lasiocalycinum

Halimium lasiocalycinum, commonly known as the purple sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry habitats. The plant features small, clustered flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep purple, blooming in the spring. Its leaves are succulent and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in arid environments.

Halimium lasiocalycinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
