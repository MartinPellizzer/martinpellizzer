Halimium halimifolium subsp. multiflorum

Halimium halimifolium subsp.

multiflorum, commonly known as the many-flowered rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its fleshy, hairy leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
