Hybanthus bicolor var. lebelioides

Hybanthus bicolor var. lebelioides, commonly known as the pink and white hybanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking bi-colored flowers, which typically feature a pinkish-white corolla with a contrasting yellow throat. The plant is an annual herb with slender, erect stems and small, opposite leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in warm climates.
