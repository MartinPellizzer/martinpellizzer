Hieracium oreophilum

Hieracium oreophilum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom in early summer.

Hieracium oreophilum plays a role in supporting local pollinators and contributes to the biodiversity of alpine ecosystems.
