Hermannia texana

Hermannia texana, commonly known as the Texas hermannia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Hermannia texana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
