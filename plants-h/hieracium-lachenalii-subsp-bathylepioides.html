Hieracium lachenalii subsp. bathylepioides

Hieracium lachenalii subsp.

bathylepioides, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats at high elevations. It has a hairy stem and leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
