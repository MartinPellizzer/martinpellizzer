Hypericum chinense subsp. salicifolium

Hypericum chinense subsp.

salicifolium, commonly known as Chinese St. John's Wort, is a shrub native to eastern Asia. It features lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red on the underside, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in summer. It is characterized by its woody stems and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine.
