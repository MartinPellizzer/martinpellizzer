Huberantha loriformis

Huberantha loriformis, commonly known as the banana leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad, banana-like leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and ornamental horticulture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with a delicate fragrance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
