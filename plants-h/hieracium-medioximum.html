Hieracium medioximum

Hieracium medioximum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Hieracium medioximum is known for its ability to spread rapidly through rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
