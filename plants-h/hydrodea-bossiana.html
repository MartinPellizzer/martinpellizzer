Hydrodea bossiana

Hydrodea bossiana, commonly known as the Cape Peninsula daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. It has a low-growing habit with narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in the Western Cape region.

Hydrodea bossiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
