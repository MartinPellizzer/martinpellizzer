Hieracium sparsum subsp. kitanovii

Hieracium sparsum subsp.

kitanovii, commonly known as the Kitanov's common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It has finely divided, grass-like leaves and produces small, daisy-like composite flowers.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
