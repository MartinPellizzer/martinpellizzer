Hepetis pavonii

Hepetis pavonii, commonly known as the peacock violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking violet-blue flowers with intricate patterns resembling peacock feathers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical name reflects its ornamental value and unique floral structure.
