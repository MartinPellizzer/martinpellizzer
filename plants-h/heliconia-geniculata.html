Heliconia geniculata

Heliconia geniculata, commonly known as the zigzag heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its vibrant, spiral-shaped inflorescences that resemble a bird's nest. The plant features broad, waxy, strap-like leaves that are often used in floral arrangements due to their durability and aesthetic appeal. Heliconia geniculata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests and coastal areas.

Its unique structure and colorful bracts make it a popular ornamental plant in horticulture.
