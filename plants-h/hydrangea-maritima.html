Hydrangea maritima

Hydrangea maritima, commonly known as coastal hydrangea, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, spherical flower heads that range in color from white to pink, depending on soil pH. The plant typically grows in wet, sandy soils along coastal areas and is adapted to salt spray and wind. It has large, lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

Hydrangea maritima is a hardy shrub that thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in coastal landscaping for its ornamental value.
