Hieracium atratum subsp. mallaense

Hieracium atratum subsp.

mallaense, commonly known as the black hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in meadows, woodlands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are dark purple to black, giving it a striking appearance.

It is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
