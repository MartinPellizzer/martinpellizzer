Hieracium marchettii

Hieracium marchettii, commonly known as the Marchetti's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in grasslands and meadows, preferring well-drained soils. It features basal leaves that are often heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Its yellow flowers bloom in the summer months, contributing to its role in supporting pollinators.
