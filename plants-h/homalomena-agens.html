Homalomena agens

Homalomena agens, commonly known as the golden bamboo lily, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves that are often variegated with yellow and green patterns. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers surrounded by a spathe, which is typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

Homalomena agens is relatively low maintenance and can be grown in both indoor and outdoor settings.
