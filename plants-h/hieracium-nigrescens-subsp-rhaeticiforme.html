Hieracium nigrescens subsp. rhaeticiforme

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

rhaeticiforme, commonly known as the Rhaeticiform hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering stems that bear yellowish to orange composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in alpine and subalpine regions. It has finely divided, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, elongated achenes as its seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
