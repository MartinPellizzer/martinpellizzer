Hedera pennsylvanica

Hedera pennsylvanica, commonly known as the Virginia creeper, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by orange-red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in a variety of soil types and prefers partial to full shade, making it a common sight in woodland areas.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb structures make it both a useful and sometimes invasive plant in gardens and natural habitats.
