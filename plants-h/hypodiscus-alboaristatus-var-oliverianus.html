Hypodiscus alboaristatus var. oliverianus

Hypodiscus alboaristatus var. oliverianus, commonly known as the white-stemmed flame flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often glossy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Its botanical features make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and greenhouses.
