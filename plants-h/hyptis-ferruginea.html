Hyptis ferruginea

Hyptis ferruginea, commonly known as ironweed or red ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and clusters of small, lavender to pinkish flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Hyptis ferruginea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in some cultures for medicinal purposes.
