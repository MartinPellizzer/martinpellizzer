Hieracium wolfii

Hieracium wolfii, commonly known as the wolf's bane or ragged robin, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, along stream banks, and in woodland edges, preferring well-drained soil. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and the leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture.

Hieracium wolfii is often found in habitats with partial shade and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
