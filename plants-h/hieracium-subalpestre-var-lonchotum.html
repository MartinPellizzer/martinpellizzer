Hieracium subalpestre var. lonchotum

Hieracium subalpestre var. lonchotum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or grassy slopes at elevations above 1,500 meters. The plant has basal leaves that are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are yellow, with a distinctive rayed structure. It is adapted to cold climates and has a short growing season.

This variant is distinguished by its elongated, narrow leaves and is part of the dandelion family (Compositae).
