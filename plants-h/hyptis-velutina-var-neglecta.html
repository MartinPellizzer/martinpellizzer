Hyptis velutina var. neglecta

Hyptis velutina var. neglecta, commonly known as velvetleaf hyptis, is a perennial shrub native to the warm regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety foliage that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.

The leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported medicinal properties.
