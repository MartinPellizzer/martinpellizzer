Hydrocotyle sibthorpioides f. pilosa

Hydrocotyle sibthorpioides f. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy spurge laurel, is a variant of the Hydrocotyle sibthorpioides species.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its hairy foliage, which gives it its specific epithet "pilosa.". The plant typically features rounded, broad leaves with a velvety texture. It is native to certain regions of southern Africa and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical interest lies in its unique morphology and its role in local ecosystems.
