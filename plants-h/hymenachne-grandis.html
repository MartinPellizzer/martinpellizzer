Hymenachne grandis

Hymenachne grandis, commonly known as the broadleaf watergrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth and has long, narrow leaves that are often submerged or floating in aquatic environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spikes, and it is known for its ability to thrive in wetland habitats. Hymenachne grandis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
