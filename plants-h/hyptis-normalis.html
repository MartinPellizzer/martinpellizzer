Hyptis normalis

Hyptis normalis, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically blue or purple and are clustered in dense whorls along the stems.

Hyptis normalis is often used in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and aromatic properties.
