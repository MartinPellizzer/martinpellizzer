Hieracium alatum subsp. lagopinum

Hieracium alatum subsp.

lagopinum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellowish-orange flowers.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name. It typically thrives in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the hawkweed genus, with variations in leaf and flower morphology across different subspecies.
