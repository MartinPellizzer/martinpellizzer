Hypoxis trifurcillata

Hypoxis trifurcillata, commonly known as the three-branched starflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the genus Hypoxis, which includes several species of plants known for their star-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in open, rocky habitats and has narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are small, white, and arranged in a star-like pattern, giving it its common name.

Hypoxis trifurcillata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
