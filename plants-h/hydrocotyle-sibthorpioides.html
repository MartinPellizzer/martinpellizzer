Hydrocotyle sibthorpioides

Hydrocotyle sibthorpioides, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a sprawling growth habit with hairy, heart-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water gardens and shaded landscapes.
