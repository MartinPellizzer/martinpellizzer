Heptapleurum stellatum

Heptapleurum stellatum, commonly known as the star-leaved holly, is a evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that often have a star-like pattern on the underside. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences, followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in shaded to partially sunny conditions and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine in some regions.
