Hyptis lamiifolia

Hyptis lamiifolia, commonly known as the Mexican mint or blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically blue or purple, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the branches.

Hyptis lamiifolia is known for its strong, mint-like fragrance and is often used in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant.
