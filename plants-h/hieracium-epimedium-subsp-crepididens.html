Hieracium epimedium subsp. crepididens

Hieracium epimedium subsp.

crepididens, commonly known as the creeping hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure.

The plant features basal leaves that are broadly ovate to elliptic in shape, with hairy surfaces. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in composite heads, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It thrives in moist, often calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
