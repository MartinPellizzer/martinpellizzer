Habenaria longiracema

Habenaria longiracema, commonly known as the long-stalked marsh orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically has a rhizomatous root system and produces showy, pendulous inflorescences with white or pale pink petals. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, adapted to thrive in moist, nutrient-poor soils.

The species is noted for its ecological importance in supporting pollinators and maintaining wetland biodiversity.
