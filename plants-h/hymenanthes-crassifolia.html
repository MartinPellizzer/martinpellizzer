Hymenanthes crassifolia

Hymenanthes crassifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf hoya, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically broad and ovate in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the evening. It is a climbing plant that thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
