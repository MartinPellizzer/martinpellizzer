Hieracium czadanense

Hieracium czadanense, commonly known as the Czadans' hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy stems. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and its flowers are yellow, forming dense clusters at the top of the plant.

Hieracium czadanense is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
