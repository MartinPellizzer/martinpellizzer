Hieracium subhorizontale

Hieracium subhorizontale, commonly known as the horizontal hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Hieracium subhorizontale is known for its ability to spread via rhizomes, making it both a common weed and a resilient species in various habitats.
