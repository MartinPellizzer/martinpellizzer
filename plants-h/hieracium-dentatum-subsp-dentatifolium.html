Hieracium dentatum subsp. dentatifolium

Hieracium dentatum subsp.

dentatifolium, commonly known as the toothed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are deeply toothed and hairy, while its yellow flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is often found in open habitats and can form dense colonies, making it a common sight in many regions of its native range.
