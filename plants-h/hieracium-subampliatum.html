Hieracium subampliatum

Hieracium subampliatum, commonly known as the broad-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright stems and basal rosettes of broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.

Hieracium subampliatum is often found in temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.
