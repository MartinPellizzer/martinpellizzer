Hemionitis caudata

Hemionitis caudata, commonly known as the cat's tail plant, is a unique fern that belongs to the family Hemionitidaceae.

It is native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, curling fronds that resemble a cat's tail, which are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Hemionitis caudata is often found growing in crevices or on rock faces, making it a hardy and resilient species.
