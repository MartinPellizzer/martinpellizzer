Hymenophyllum spectabile

Hymenophyllum spectabile, commonly known as the spectable filmy fern, is a small, epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or rocks in tropical rainforests. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and are covered by a protective indusium.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
