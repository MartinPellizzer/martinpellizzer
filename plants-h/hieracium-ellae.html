Hieracium ellae

Hieracium ellae, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are typically arranged in a flat-topped cluster. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and covered in fine pubescence. Native to Europe, it thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

It plays a role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies due to its abundant nectar-rich flowers.
