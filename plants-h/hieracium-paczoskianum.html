Hieracium paczoskianum

Hieracium paczoskianum, commonly known as the Paczoskian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It features a rosette of basal leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and sometimes endangered species in certain regions.
