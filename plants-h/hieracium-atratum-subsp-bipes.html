Hieracium atratum subsp. bipes

Hieracium atratum subsp.

bipes, commonly known as the black hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along streambanks. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are dark purple to black, giving it a striking appearance.

It is noted for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
