Haemanthus humanii

Haemanthus humanii, commonly known as the Natal grassblume, is a striking bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a rosette at the base. During the flowering season, it produces umbels of bright red or orange flowers, which are highly ornamental. The plant is adapted to grow in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and unique appearance.
