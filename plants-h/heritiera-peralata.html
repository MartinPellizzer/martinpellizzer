Heritiera peralata

Heritiera peralata, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow inflorescences that resemble flames, which bloom throughout the year. The plant has pinnately compound leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal areas or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and parks in warm regions.
