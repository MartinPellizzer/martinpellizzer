Hyparrhenia acutispathacea var. pilosa

Hyparrhenia acutispathacea var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy blady grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, fibrous rhizomes that help it form clumps. The leaves are narrow, linear, and covered with fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers.

This plant is often found in open, sunny areas such as fields and disturbed soils, and it is adapted to withstand drought and poor soil conditions.
