Hieracium porrectidens

Hieracium porrectidens, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are yellow and bloom from June to September.

Hieracium porrectidens is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is considered a weed in some agricultural areas.
