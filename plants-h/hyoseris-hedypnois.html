Hyoseris hedypnois

Hyoseris hedypnois, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant features basal leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hyoseris hedypnois is often found in dry, sunny habitats like grasslands and rocky slopes.
