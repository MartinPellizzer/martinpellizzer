Heliotropium inconspicuum

Heliotropium inconspicuum, commonly known as the small heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous annual that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are usually violet or purple in color, with five petals arranged in a whorl. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a hairy texture.

Heliotropium inconspicuum thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as roadsides and fields.
