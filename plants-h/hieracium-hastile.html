Hieracium hastile

Hieracium hastile, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and disturbed soils. The plant has yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, and its leaves are hairy and serrated. It is a perennial herb that can spread through rhizomes, making it somewhat invasive in some areas.

The species is characterized by its robust growth and distinctive foliage.
