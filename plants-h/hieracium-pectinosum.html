Hieracium pectinosum

Hieracium pectinosum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are hairy and lance-shaped, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disk.

Hieracium pectinosum is often found in open habitats and can sometimes become invasive in certain regions.
