Hazardia squarrosa

Hazardia squarrosa, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.
