Hieracium laggeri subsp. niphobioides

Hieracium laggeri subsp.

niphobioides, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky and dry habitats, thriving in high-altitude environments. Its morphology includes hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves adapted to cold and windy conditions.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in alpine ecosystems and is often found in meadows and slopes above the tree line.
