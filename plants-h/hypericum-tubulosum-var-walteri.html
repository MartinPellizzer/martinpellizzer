Hypericum tubulosum var. walteri

Hypericum tubulosum var. walteri, commonly known as Walter's yellow-flowered St. John's wort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems covered in glandular hairs, and its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, with serrated margins. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers with four petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Hypericaceae family, often found in moist, sunny habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.
