Hieracium serratifrons var. cinderella

Hieracium serratifrons var. cinderella, commonly known as the Cinderella Hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features serrated leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. It is typically found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
