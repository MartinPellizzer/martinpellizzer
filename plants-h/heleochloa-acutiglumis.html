Heleochloa acutiglumis

Heleochloa acutiglumis, commonly known as the sharp-leaved grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, wetlands, and along waterways. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a sharp, pointed tip, and its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

Heleochloa acutiglumis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting biodiversity.
