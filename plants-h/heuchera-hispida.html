Heuchera hispida

Heuchera hispida, commonly known as the roughleaf alumroot, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features basal leaves that are hairy and have a rough texture, with a dark green to reddish-purple coloration. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white or pale pink flowers in early summer. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Heuchera hispida is valued for its ornamental foliage and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
