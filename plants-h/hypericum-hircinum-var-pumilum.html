Hypericum hircinum var. pumilum

Hypericum hircinum var. pumilum, commonly known as the dwarf St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to southern Europe.

It features small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, upright stems covered in short, stiff hairs. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly rough texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.
