Heteropogon contortus var. glaber

Heteropogon contortus var. glaber, commonly known as the smooth hairgrass, is a grass species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, glabrous (hairless) blades. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in open grasslands or along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a narrow, cylindrical spikelet that bears small, spike-like flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a ground cover.
