Helichrysum multicaule

Helichrysum multicaule, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of yellow, daisy-like flowers that remain vibrant for a long period. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its aromatic, fragrant leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its unique appearance and long-lasting blooms make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
