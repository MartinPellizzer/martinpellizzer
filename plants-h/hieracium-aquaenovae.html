Hieracium aquaenovae

Hieracium aquaenovae, commonly known as the Aquae Nova Hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are basal, rough, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow, often arranged in a single head.

Hieracium aquaenovae is known for its resilience to cold temperatures and is an important component of high-altitude ecosystems.
