Holcus arcticus

Holcus arcticus, commonly known as Arctic feathergrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to Arctic and alpine regions.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, narrow leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of silky hairs. The plant produces delicate, feathery inflorescences that are often pink or purple in color, adding a subtle ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained, nutrient-poor soils and is adapted to cold climates with short growing seasons.

Holcus arcticus plays a role in stabilizing soil in its harsh environments and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
