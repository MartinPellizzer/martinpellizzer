Hypericum reptans

Hypericum reptans, commonly known as creeping St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant has opposite, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its name refers to its creeping growth habit and its association with the St. John's wort plant family.
