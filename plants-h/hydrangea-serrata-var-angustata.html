Hydrangea serrata var. angustata

Hydrangea serrata var. angustata, commonly known as the narrowleaf hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its narrow, serrated leaves and clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant prefers partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens. Its foliage turns vibrant shades of red or orange in the fall, adding seasonal interest.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
