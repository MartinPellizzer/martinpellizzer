Helicia oblongifolia

Helicia oblongifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf helicia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, glossy green leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. Helicia oblongifolia is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance.

It plays a role in supporting local biodiversity and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
