Hypericum cajense

Hypericum cajense, commonly known as the Mexican St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, wooded areas and can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are oval and opposite, with a slightly serrated edge.

Hypericum cajense is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its purported healing benefits.
