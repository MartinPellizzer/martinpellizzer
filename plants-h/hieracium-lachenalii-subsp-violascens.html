Hieracium lachenalii subsp. violascens

Hieracium lachenalii subsp.

violascens, commonly known as the violet-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade.

The plant features basal rosettes of lanceolate, violet-tinged leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowering stems bear clusters of small, yellowish to reddish flowers, which are typically arranged in a single inflorescence.

The subspecies is characterized by its distinctive leaf coloration and is often found in moist, temperate habitats.
