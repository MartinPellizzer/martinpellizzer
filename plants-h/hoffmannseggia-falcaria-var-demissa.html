Hoffmannseggia falcaria var. demissa

Hoffmannseggia falcaria var. demissa, commonly known as the desert sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to violet. The plant is adapted to harsh, dry environments, featuring succulent leaves and a deep root system for water conservation. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or gravelly habitats.
