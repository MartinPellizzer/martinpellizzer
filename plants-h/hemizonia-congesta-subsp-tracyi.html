Hemizonia congesta subsp. tracyi

Hemizonia congesta subsp.

tracyi, commonly known as Tracy's hemizonia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers. Its leaves are usually narrow and gray-green, with a fuzzy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to arid environments.
