Hieracium incisionum

Hieracium incisionum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, yellowish to reddish flowers arranged in dense clusters. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture and serrated edges. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

This plant is known for its ability to spread rapidly through vegetative reproduction, making it a common weed in various habitats.
