Hieracium murorum subsp. integratulum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

integratulum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellowish to orange-colored flowers.

The plant typically has basal leaves that are broadly lanceolate and are covered with fine hairs. It thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Due to its aggressive growth, it is considered a weed in some regions.
