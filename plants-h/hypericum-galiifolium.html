Hypericum galiifolium

Hypericum galiifolium, commonly known as the fragrant woodbine, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in woodland areas and has small, elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are often fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hypericum galiifolium is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
