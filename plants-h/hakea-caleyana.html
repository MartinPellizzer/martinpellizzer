Hakea caleyana

Hakea caleyana, commonly known as the Caleyana hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like foliage and distinctive cylindrical flower spikes. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance.

Hakea caleyana is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soils.
