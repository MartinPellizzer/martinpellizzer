Hedychium fastigiatum

Hedychium fastigiatum, commonly known as the black ginger, is a tropical plant belonging to the Zingiberaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its dark purple to black rhizomes, which are highly prized in traditional medicine. The plant produces tall, slender stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are fragrant and typically yellow or orange, arranged in panicles at the top of the plant.

Hedychium fastigiatum is valued not only for its medicinal properties but also for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
