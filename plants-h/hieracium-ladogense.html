Hieracium ladogense

Hieracium ladogense, commonly known as Ladoga hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the northern regions of Europe, particularly around Lake Ladoga in Russia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist, nutrient-rich soils along the edges of lakes and rivers. It has basal leaves that are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium ladogense is often found in wet meadows and is adapted to the cool, temperate climate of its native habitat.
