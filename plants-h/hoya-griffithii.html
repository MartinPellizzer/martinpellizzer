Hoya griffithii

Hoya griffithii, commonly known as the "Wax Plant" or "Hoya of Griffith," is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically green or variegated with white or cream markings. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that are usually white or pink, which bloom during the summer months. Hoya griffithii is a popular houseplant due to its low maintenance requirements and attractive appearance.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.
