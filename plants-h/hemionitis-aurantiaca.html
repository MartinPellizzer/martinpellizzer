Hemionitis aurantiaca

Hemionitis aurantiaca, commonly known as the red fern, is a unique vascular plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that give it a fern-like appearance, despite being a member of the euphorbiaceae family. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments, showcasing remarkable adaptability to extreme conditions. Its leaves are typically bright red or orange, which helps in photosynthesis under intense sunlight.

Hemionitis aurantiaca is also notable for its small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.
