Hieracium gastonianum

Hieracium gastonianum, commonly known as the garden hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
