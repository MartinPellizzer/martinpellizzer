Hieracium dialytum

Hieracium dialytum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences. Native to Europe, it thrives in moist meadows and grasslands, often forming dense colonies. The plant has a fibrous root system and is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through stolons.

It is considered a common weed in agricultural areas due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
