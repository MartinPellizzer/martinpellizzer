Hydrangea japonica var. coerulescens

Hydrangea japonica var. coerulescens, commonly known as the blue hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded clusters of blue or pink flowers, which change color based on soil pH. The plant produces large, showy bracts that surround small, true flowers, creating a striking visual effect. It thrives in partial shade and requires moist, well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its foliage turns vibrant shades of red or orange in the fall, adding seasonal interest to the landscape.
