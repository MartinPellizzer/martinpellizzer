Hieracium chydaeum

Hieracium chydaeum, commonly known as the Chyda's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces numerous yellow flower heads composed of small ray and disk florets. Native to Central Asia, this species thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions. The plant has a compact growth form and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and flies.
