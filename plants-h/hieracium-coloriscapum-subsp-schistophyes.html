Hieracium coloriscapum subsp. schistophyes

Hieracium coloriscapum subsp.

schistophyes, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, often in open habitats such as grasslands and alpine meadows.

The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are usually hairy and have a rough texture. Its flowers are yellow and borne on a tall, slender stem, and they bloom from late spring to early summer.

It is characterized by its ability to thrive in poor soils and its distinctive, somewhat woolly appearance.
