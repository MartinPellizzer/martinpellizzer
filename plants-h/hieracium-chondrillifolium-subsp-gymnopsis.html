Hieracium chondrillifolium subsp. gymnopsis

Hieracium chondrillifolium subsp.

gymnopsis, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy structure.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. Its leaves are hairy and alternate, with a rough texture, and the flowers are yellow, forming a flat head.
