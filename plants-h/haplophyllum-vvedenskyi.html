Haplophyllum vvedenskyi

Haplophyllum vvedenskyi, commonly known as the Siberian bellflower, is a rare and endangered plant species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, often at high elevations. The plant features showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

Due to habitat destruction and climate change, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
