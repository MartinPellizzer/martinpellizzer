Hypericum kamtschaticum var. tatewakii

Hypericum kamtschaticum var. tatewakii, commonly known as the Kamchatka St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the Russian Far East and parts of Siberia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
