Hesperocyparis macnabiana

Hesperocyparis macnabiana, commonly known as the Macnab's incense cedar, is a species of conifer native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown bark and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of coniferous trees. Its foliage consists of scale-like leaves that are dense and provide good insulation.

Hesperocyparis macnabiana is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and adaptability.
