Hyptis atrorubens var. villosissima

Hyptis atrorubens var. villosissima, commonly known as velvetleaf hyptis, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, often blooming in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
