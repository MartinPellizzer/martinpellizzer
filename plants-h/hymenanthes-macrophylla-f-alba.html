Hymenanthes macrophylla f. alba

Hymenanthes macrophylla f. alba, commonly known as the white-flowered white corydalis, is a variant of the species native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and streamside areas. Its flowers are clustered in racemes and have a distinctive, spurred shape.

The white coloration of its flowers is a notable botanical feature, distinguishing it from other forms of the species.
