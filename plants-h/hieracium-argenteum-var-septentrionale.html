Hieracium argenteum var. septentrionale

Hieracium argenteum var. septentrionale, commonly known as the northern silver hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to northern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. It has a creeping growth habit with rhizomes that allow it to spread vegetatively.

Due to its invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a weed in certain regions.
