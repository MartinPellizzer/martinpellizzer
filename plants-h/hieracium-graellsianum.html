Hieracium graellsianum

Hieracium graellsianum, commonly known as the Graellsian hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It produces yellow flowers with ray florets and has a basal rosette of leaves.

Hieracium graellsianum is often found in disturbed soils and can spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain areas.
