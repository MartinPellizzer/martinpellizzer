Hackelia patens var. patens

Hackelia patens var. patens, commonly known as the showy bluebell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant gardens.

Its floral display attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
