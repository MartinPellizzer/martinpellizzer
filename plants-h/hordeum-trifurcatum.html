Hordeum trifurcatum

Hordeum trifurcatum, commonly known as the three-branch barley, is a grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous tiny florets. Its seeds are typically pale yellow to brown in color and are adapted for wind dispersal.

Hordeum trifurcatum is often found in rocky or alpine environments where it thrives in well-drained soils.
