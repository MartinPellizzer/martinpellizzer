Hieracium fulviflorum

Hieracium fulviflorum, commonly known as the golden hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in summer, and the plant has hairy stems and leaves with a rough texture.

Hieracium fulviflorum is often found in temperate regions and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
