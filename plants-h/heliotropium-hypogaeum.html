Heliotropium hypogaeum

Heliotropium hypogaeum, commonly known as the underground heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its prostrate or creeping growth habit, with small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or pink. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals. Its leaves are usually opposite and have a rough, hairy texture.

Heliotropium hypogaeum thrives in well-drained soil and is often found in disturbed or dry habitats.
