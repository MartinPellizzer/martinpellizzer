Helianthus aristatus

Helianthus aristatus, commonly known as the stiff sunflower, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family.

It is native to the eastern and central United States and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows up to 3 meters tall, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers consist of a large yellow disk surrounded by ray florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Helianthus aristatus is often found in open woods, prairies, and along roadsides, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
