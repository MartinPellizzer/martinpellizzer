Heliotropium dissitiflorum

Heliotropium dissitiflorum, commonly known as the hairy heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or pink. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which help it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or disturbed soils.

Heliotropium dissitiflorum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
