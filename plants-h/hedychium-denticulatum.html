Hedychium denticulatum

Hedychium denticulatum, commonly known as the toothed ginger, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces large, showy yellow flowers with a strong, spicy fragrance. The plant features lance-shaped, dark green leaves and a rhizomatous root system, which helps it spread and establish in moist, shaded environments. Hedychium denticulatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique and valuable species in horticulture and ecological studies.
