Hiptage parvifolia subsp. cumingii

Hiptage parvifolia subsp.

cumingii, commonly known as the Cuming's hiptage, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale pink, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
