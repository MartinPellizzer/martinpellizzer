Hypargyrium

Hypargyrium, commonly known as silverleaf, is a type of plant that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that typically grows in temperate regions. The leaves of Hypargyrium are silver-gray in color, giving it its common name, and are covered in fine, reflective hairs. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually white or pale green.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
