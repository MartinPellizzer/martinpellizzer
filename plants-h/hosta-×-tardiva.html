Hosta × tardiva

Hosta × tardiva, commonly known as the late-blooming hosta, is a hybrid plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves that vary in color from light green to deep blue-green. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in late summer, typically in shades of white or pale yellow. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is widely cultivated in gardens across temperate regions.
