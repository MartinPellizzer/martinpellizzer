Hypericum galioides var. ambiguum

Hypericum galioides var. ambiguum, commonly known as the ambiguous St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to North America.

It typically features small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has elliptical, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological restoration projects.
