Hemionitis apiacea

Hemionitis apiacea, commonly known as the honeycomb plant, is a unique fern native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that resemble a honeycomb pattern, which gives it its common name. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and low moisture conditions. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of many fern species.

Hemionitis apiacea is often found in association with other xerophytic plants in its harsh environment.
