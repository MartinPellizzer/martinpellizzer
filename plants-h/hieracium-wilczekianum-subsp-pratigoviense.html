Hieracium wilczekianum subsp. pratigoviense

Hieracium wilczekianum subsp.

pratigoviense, commonly known as the Balkan hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a flowering stem that bears yellow daisy-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has a fibrous root system and produces numerous small, herbaceous stems.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its range.
