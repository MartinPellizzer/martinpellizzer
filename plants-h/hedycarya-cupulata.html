Hedycarya cupulata

Hedycarya cupulata, commonly known as the hooded mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hood-like bracts that surround its small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial shade. Its leaves are palmately lobed and have a soft, velvety texture.

Hedycarya cupulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
