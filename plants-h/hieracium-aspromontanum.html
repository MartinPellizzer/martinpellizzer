Hieracium aspromontanum

Hieracium aspromontanum, commonly known as the Aspromontane hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of rough, hairy leaves and stems that bear yellowish to orange flowers. Native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe, it thrives in rocky and calcareous soils. The plant produces small, composite flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and flies.

It is typically found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
