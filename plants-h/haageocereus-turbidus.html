Haageocereus turbidus

Haageocereus turbidus, commonly known as the Turbidus Cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are often covered in white or pale-colored spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically short-lived but attract various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
