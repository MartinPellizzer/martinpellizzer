Haplocalymma woronowii

Haplocalymma woronowii, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has upright, branched stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Botanically, it belongs to the Campanulaceae family, known for its diverse and ornamental species.
