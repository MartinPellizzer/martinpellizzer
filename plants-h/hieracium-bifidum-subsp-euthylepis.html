Hieracium bifidum subsp. euthylepis

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

euthylepis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and woodland edges, thriving in well-drained soils.

The plant features basal rosettes of lanceolate, often hairy leaves, and bears yellow flowers in loose clusters. Its flowers are typically tubular with five lobes, and the plant is known for its ability to spread via rhizomes.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some traditional uses.
