Haemanthus pseudocaulus subsp. prorumpens

Haemanthus pseudocaulus subsp.

prorumpens, commonly known as the red-stemmed haemanthus, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa. It features tall, grass-like leaves that are typically green with red or purple veins, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces clusters of bright red flowers during the winter months, which are highly attractive to pollinators. Its bulbs are adapted to store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
