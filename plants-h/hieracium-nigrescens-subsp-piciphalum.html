Hieracium nigrescens subsp. piciphalum

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

piciphalum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its rosette of dark green, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers with five petals.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. It is part of the dandelion family (Asteraceae) and is known for its ability to thrive in harsh, cold environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species in many areas.
