Hiraea sclerophylla

Hiraea sclerophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hibiscus, is a shrub native to the eastern coast of Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves that are typically green with a slight blue or grey tinge. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal heathlands and dry forests.

Hiraea sclerophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
