Homalomena pseudogeniculata

Homalomena pseudogeniculata, commonly known as the purple-leafed arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a distinctive purple underside. The plant produces small, spadix-like flowers surrounded by a colorful spathe, typical of the arum family. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage.

Its growth habit includes rhizomatous stems that allow it to spread and form dense clumps.
