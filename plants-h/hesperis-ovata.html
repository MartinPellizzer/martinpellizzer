Hesperis ovata

Hesperis ovata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing, compact habit and produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant has aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.

Hesperis ovata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, early spring blooms.
