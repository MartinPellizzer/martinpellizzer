Hieracium flagellare var. glatzense

Hieracium flagellare var. glatzense, commonly known as the Glatz's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has slender, often hairy stems and basal leaves that are lanceolate in shape. It typically grows in grassy areas and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and sometimes endangered species in certain regions.
