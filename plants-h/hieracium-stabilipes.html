Hieracium stabilipes

Hieracium stabilipes, commonly known as the upright hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and ragweed, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

Hieracium stabilipes is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain habitats.
