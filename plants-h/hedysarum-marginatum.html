Hedysarum marginatum

Hedysarum marginatum, commonly known as the pink sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Caucasus.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces clusters of pink to purple pea-like flowers, which bloom in early summer. The plant has compound leaves with trifoliate or pinnate structures, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Hedysarum marginatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
