Helianthus tubaeformis

Helianthus tubaeformis, commonly known as the swamp sunflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern and central United States.

It is characterized by its yellow, daisy-like flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
