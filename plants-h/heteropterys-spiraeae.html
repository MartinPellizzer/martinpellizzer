Heteropterys spiraeae

Heteropterys spiraeae, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped, glossy dark green leaves that have a silvery underside, giving it its common name. It produces small, white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round berries.

The tree is often valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens for its aesthetic features.
