Hieracium collinum subsp. madarum

Hieracium collinum subsp.

madarum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a flowering head composed of yellow disk flowers surrounded by white to pale yellow ray florets.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, pastures, and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. It has a taproot system and produces achenes as its fruit, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
