Heliamphora

Heliamphora, commonly known as the sun pitcher, is a genus of carnivorous plants native to South America.

These plants are characterized by their unique, pitcher-shaped leaves that secrete a digestive fluid to trap and digest insects. They thrive in wet, acidic environments such as bogs and marshes, where they absorb nutrients from the water. The genus includes several species, each with varying sizes and shapes of their pitchers.

Heliamphora plants are popular among carnivorous plant enthusiasts due to their striking appearance and ecological significance.
