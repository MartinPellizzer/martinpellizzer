Hypericum isophyllum

Hypericum isophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters along the stems. The plant has opposite, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it distinguishable from other Hypericum species by its unique leaf shape and growth habit.
