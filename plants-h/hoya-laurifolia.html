Hoya laurifolia

Hoya laurifolia, commonly known as the wax plant or the sweetheart plant, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white in color. It is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

Hoya laurifolia is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
