Hieracium sagittatum subsp. hepatiphyllum

Hieracium sagittatum subsp.

hepatiphyllum, commonly known as the liverleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed sites. Its leaves are deeply lobed and resemble the shape of a liver, giving it its common name.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators.
