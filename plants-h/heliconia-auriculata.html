Heliconia auriculata

Heliconia auriculata, commonly known as the golden heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its large, colorful bracts that resemble petals. The plant produces striking inflorescences with bright orange to red bracts surrounding small, clustered flowers. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

Heliconia auriculata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant appearance and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
