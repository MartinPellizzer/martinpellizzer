Hypericum reflexum var. myrtillifolium

Hypericum reflexum var. myrtillifolium, commonly known as the red-fruited St. John's wort, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features small, yellow, four-petaled flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are elliptical and have a slightly waxy texture, with a reddish hue on the underside. Its fruits are small, red, spherical berries that mature in the fall.

This plant is often found in moist, woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
