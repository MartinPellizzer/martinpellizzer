Hieracium centonale

Hieracium centonale, commonly known as the Italian hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of yellow to orange flower heads, which are typically discoid in structure. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. Native to Europe, it thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
