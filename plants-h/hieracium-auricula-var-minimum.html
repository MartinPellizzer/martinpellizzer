Hieracium auricula var. minimum

Hieracium auricula var. minimum, commonly known as the miniature yellow hawkweed, is a small perennial herb native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are clustered in a single head.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens as a decorative plant.
