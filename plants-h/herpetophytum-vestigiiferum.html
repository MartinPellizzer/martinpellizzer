Herpetophytum vestigiiferum

Herpetophytum vestigiiferum, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is an epiphytic orchid, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. The plant is characterized by its unique, ghost-like appearance, with long, thin leaves and striking white or pale pink flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires specific conditions to flourish.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern in many areas.
