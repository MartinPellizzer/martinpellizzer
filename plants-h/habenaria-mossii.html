Habenaria mossii

Habenaria mossii, commonly known as the mossy orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its floral structure consists of three sepals and three petals, with the latter often being showy and colorful.

Habenaria mossii is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by supporting pollinators.
