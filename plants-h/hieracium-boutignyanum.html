Hieracium boutignyanum

Hieracium boutignyanum, commonly known as the Boutigny's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of tubular and ray florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

It is known for its ornamental value and has been cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
