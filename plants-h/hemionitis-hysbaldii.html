Hemionitis hysbaldii

Hemionitis hysbaldii, commonly known as the mountain hysbald, is a rare and delicate fern found in specific alpine habitats.

It has delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution and sensitivity to environmental changes, it is considered a vulnerable species.
