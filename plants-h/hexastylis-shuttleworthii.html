Hexastylis shuttleworthii

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like fronds that are typically divided into narrow, lance-shaped segments. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori, which are clustered on the underside of its leaves. Native to the southeastern United States, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors.

Its unique morphology and habitat preferences make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
