Hemitelia squarrosa

Hemitelia squarrosa, commonly known as the woolly hemitelia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. Its stems are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, giving it a woolly appearance.

Hemitelia squarrosa is adapted to arid environments and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
