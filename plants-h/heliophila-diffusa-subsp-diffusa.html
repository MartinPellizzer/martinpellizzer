Heliophila diffusa subsp. diffusa

Heliophila diffusa subsp.

diffusa, commonly known as common heliophyllum, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

The plant features slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its flowers are bright yellow, arranged in loose clusters, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

It is a member of the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is known for its aromatic foliage.
