Hieracium pilosella subsp. subcaulescens

Hieracium pilosella subsp.

subcaulescens, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands, meadows, and woodland edges.

The plant has a basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in a compact inflorescence. Its stems are slender and often slightly hairy, contributing to its common name.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.
