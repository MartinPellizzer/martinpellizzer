Hieracium ledebourii

Hieracium ledebourii, commonly known as the Ledebour's hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and often arranged in dense clusters.

Hieracium ledebourii is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its distinctive appearance.
