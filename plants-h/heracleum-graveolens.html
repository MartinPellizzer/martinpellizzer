Heracleum graveolens

Heracleum graveolens, commonly known as cow parsnip, is a member of the Apiaceae family and is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a biennial plant that can grow up to 2 meters tall, featuring hollow, ridged stems and large, umbrella-shaped flowers. The leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while the roots are thick and aromatic. It contains compounds like furanocoumarins, which can cause phototoxic reactions when exposed to sunlight.

The plant is often found in moist meadows, stream banks, and disturbed soils.
