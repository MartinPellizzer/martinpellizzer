Helichrysum gregorii

Helichrysum gregorii, commonly known as the golden marguerite, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that resemble miniature sunflowers. The plant produces small, papery bracts that give it a distinctive appearance, often mistaken for petals. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Helichrysum gregorii is also valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
