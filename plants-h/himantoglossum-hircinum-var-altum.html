Himantoglossum hircinum var. altum

Himantoglossum hircinum var. altum, commonly known as the tall meadow gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is known for its ornamental value and can be found in various conservation sites across its native range.
