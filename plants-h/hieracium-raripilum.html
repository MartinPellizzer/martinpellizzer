Hieracium raripilum

Hieracium raripilum, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads made up of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range from yellow to orange in color.

Hieracium raripilum is often found in open habitats and can form dense colonies, making it a notable species in ecological studies.
