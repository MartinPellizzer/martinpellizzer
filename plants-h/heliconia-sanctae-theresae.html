Heliconia sanctae-theresae

Heliconia sanctae-theresae, commonly known as the Santa Theresa heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its large, brightly colored bracts that resemble flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often forming clumps with multiple stems. Its leaves are long, narrow, and arranged in a spiral pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by modified leaves, which serve to attract pollinators like birds and insects.
