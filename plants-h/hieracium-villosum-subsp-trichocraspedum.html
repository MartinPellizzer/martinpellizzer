Hieracium villosum subsp. trichocraspedum

Hieracium villosum subsp.

trichocraspedum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed sites, preferring well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family, with a preference for temperate climates.
