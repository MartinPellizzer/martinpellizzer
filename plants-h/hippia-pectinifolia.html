Hippia pectinifolia

Hippia pectinifolia, commonly known as the featherleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its scientific name reflects the pinnate (feather-like) arrangement of its leaves.

It is not commonly cultivated but is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique morphology and ecological role.
