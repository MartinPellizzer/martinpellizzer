Hordeum ambiguum

Hordeum ambiguum, commonly known as wild barley, is a wild relative of domesticated barley.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is native to various regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in grasslands, roadsides, and agricultural fields. It has slender stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue to greenish flowers.

Hordeum ambiguum is of interest to botanists and agricultural researchers due to its potential for genetic diversity and its role in evolutionary studies.
