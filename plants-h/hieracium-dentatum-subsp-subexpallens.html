Hieracium dentatum subsp. subexpallens

Hieracium dentatum subsp.

subexpallens, commonly known as the hairy yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy foliage.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. It has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, disc-like flower heads.

Its botanical features make it identifiable within the Hieracium genus, though it is often confused with other similar species.
