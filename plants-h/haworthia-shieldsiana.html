Haworthia shieldsiana

Haworthia shieldsiana, commonly known as the Zulu shield plant, is a species of succulent native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating called farina. The leaves are typically fleshy and arranged in a compact, shield-like formation, giving the plant its common name. It produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the summer months.

This plant is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil to thrive.
