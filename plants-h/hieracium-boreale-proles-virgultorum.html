Hieracium boreale proles virgultorum

Hieracium boreale proles virgultorum, commonly known as the northern hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and forest edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped with hairy surfaces, and its yellow flowers appear in clusters during the summer months.

It is known for its ability to spread rapidly through rhizomes, making it an invasive species in some areas.
