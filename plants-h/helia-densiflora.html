Helia densiflora

Helia densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flower helia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, and prefers well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences.

Helia densiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
