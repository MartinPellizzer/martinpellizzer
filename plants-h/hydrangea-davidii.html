Hydrangea davidii

Hydrangea davidii, commonly known as the mountain hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from white to pink, blue, or purple depending on soil pH. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 6 feet and produces fragrant, white flowers in late summer. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a serrated margin and a dark green color.

Hydrangea davidii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
