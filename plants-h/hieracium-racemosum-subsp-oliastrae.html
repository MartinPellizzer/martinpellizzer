Hieracium racemosum subsp. oliastrae

Hieracium racemosum subsp.

oliastrae, commonly known as the Balkan hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, meadows, and rocky areas.

The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, hairy leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers in the summer. It is characterized by its elongated inflorescence and is often found in calcareous soils.

This subspecies is part of the genus Hieracium, which includes many species of hawkweeds known for their ornamental and ecological significance.
