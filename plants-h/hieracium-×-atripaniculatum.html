Hieracium × atripaniculatum

Hieracium × atripaniculatum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a hybrid plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers that resemble dandelions. The plant is native to Europe and has been introduced to other parts of the world, often becoming an invasive species. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes make it a challenging weed to control.
