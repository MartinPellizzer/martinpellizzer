Hieracium insuccatum

Hieracium insuccatum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

Hieracium insuccatum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in agricultural and natural settings.
