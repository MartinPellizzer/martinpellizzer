Hypoxis textilis

Hypoxis textilis, commonly known as the starry false shamrock, is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of yellow flowers with white markings. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers. Its ornamental value comes from its delicate, star-shaped blooms that appear in late spring to early summer.

Hypoxis textilis is also valued for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
