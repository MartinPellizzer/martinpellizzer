Hieracium fuscocinereum subsp. sagittatum

Hieracium fuscocinereum subsp.

sagittatum, commonly known as the arrowhead hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.

The plant features upright stems with lanceolate to ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct serrated edge. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and they bloom from late spring to early summer.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and wind-pollinated flowers.
