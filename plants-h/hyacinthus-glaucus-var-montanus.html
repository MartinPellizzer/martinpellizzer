Hyacinthus glaucus var. montanus

Hyacinthus glaucus var. montanus, commonly known as the alpine hyacinth, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features compact, bushy growth with striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces fragrant, clustered blossoms that attract pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ornamental beauty in horticultural landscapes.
