Hieracium murorum subsp. anogonium

Hieracium murorum subsp.

anogonium, commonly known as the creeping hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, grassy habitats and forms dense mats due to its stolons. Its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, often blooming from June to September.

The subspecies is distinguished by its shorter stature and more compact growth compared to other hawkweed species.
