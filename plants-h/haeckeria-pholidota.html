Haeckeria pholidota

Haeckeria pholidota, commonly known as the red-veined dandelion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are often reddish-veined. The plant produces a single, hollow, yellow flower head that is surrounded by a pappus of bristles, aiding in wind pollination. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and gardens.

Haeckeria pholidota is an annual herb that blooms from late spring to early summer.
