Hibiscus fuscus subsp. fuscus

Hibiscus fuscus subsp.

fuscus, commonly known as the dark hibiscus, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States. It features palmately lobed leaves that are typically dark green and velvety to the touch.

The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a dark red throat. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Hibiscus fuscus subsp.

fuscus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a habitat for pollinators.
