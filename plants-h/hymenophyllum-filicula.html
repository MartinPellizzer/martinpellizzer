Hymenophyllum filicula

Hymenophyllum filicula, commonly known as the narrow-leaf filmy fern, is a small, delicate fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, translucent fronds that often appear almost like paper, giving it a unique and ethereal appearance. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as on rock surfaces or in forest understories. Its spore-producing structures, called sporangia, are clustered in dense, linear rows along the margins of the fronds.

Hymenophyllum filicula is well adapted to humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
