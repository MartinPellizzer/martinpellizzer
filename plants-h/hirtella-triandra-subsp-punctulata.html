Hirtella triandra subsp. punctulata

Hirtella triandra subsp.

punctulata, commonly known as the spotted hirtella, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil. It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. Its fruits are berries that mature to a dark red color, containing several small seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
