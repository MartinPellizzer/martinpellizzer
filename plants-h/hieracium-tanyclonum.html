Hieracium tanyclonum

Hieracium tanyclonum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the sunflower family (Asteraceae).

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils across Europe and parts of Asia. The plant produces showy yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and the plant is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.

Hieracium tanyclonum is also noted for its ecological role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
