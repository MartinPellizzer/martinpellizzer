Hieracium conibasum

Hieracium conibasum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and somewhat hairy.

Hieracium conibasum is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions, where it can sometimes become invasive.
