Hieracium cymosum subsp. vaillantii

Hieracium cymosum subsp.

vaillantii, commonly known as the Vaillant's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded conditions.

The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are usually hairy and have a rough texture. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in a cyme, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and ragworts, and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
