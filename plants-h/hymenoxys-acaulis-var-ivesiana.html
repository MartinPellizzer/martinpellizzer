Hymenoxys acaulis var. ivesiana

Hymenoxys acaulis var. ivesiana, commonly known as the Ives' bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial with fleshy, succulent stems and leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its unique morphology, including its swollen, bladder-like stems, is an adaptation to arid environments.

It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.
