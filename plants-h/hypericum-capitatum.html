Hypericum capitatum

Hypericum capitatum, commonly known as the clustered St. John's wort, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with small, yellow flowers clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences. The plant features narrow, dark green leaves and has a woody stem that becomes more pronounced with age. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Hypericum capitatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
