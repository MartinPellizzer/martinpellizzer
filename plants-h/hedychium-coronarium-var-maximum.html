Hedychium coronarium var. maximum

Hedychium coronarium var. maximum, commonly known as the giant ginger or Hawaiian ginger, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a spicy scent. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves and can grow up to several meters in height. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Hedychium coronarium var. maximum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
