Hypericum thomsonii var. subcordatum

Hypericum thomsonii var. subcordatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typically from June to September. The plant has opposite, narrow, and often slightly succulent leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
