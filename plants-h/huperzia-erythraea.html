Huperzia erythraea

Huperzia erythraea, commonly known as the red-stemmed clubmoss, is a species of moss belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, scale-like leaves that are typically reddish in color. This plant is often found growing in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Huperzia erythraea is a perennial plant that reproduces through spores, typical of its classification as a primitive vascular plant.

Its structure and growth habits make it an important component of certain ecosystems.
