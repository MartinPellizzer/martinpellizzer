Hippocratea bipindensis

Hippocratea bipindensis, commonly known as the Congo orchid, is a striking orchid species native to the rainforests of Central Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its complex floral structure, which includes a distinctive lip and column. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
