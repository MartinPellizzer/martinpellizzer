Hieracium pallidum subsp. chlorolepidotum

Hieracium pallidum subsp.

chlorolepidotum, commonly known as the pale hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in late summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has a taproot system and is often found in disturbed soils.

The subspecies is distinguished by its pale yellow flowers and the presence of chlorophyll-rich leaf surfaces.
