Heliconia uleana

Heliconia uleana, commonly known as the red heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescence that consists of bright red bracts surrounding small, clustered flowers. The plant is a member of the Heliconiaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower. Its leaves are large, lance-shaped, and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Heliconia uleana thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
