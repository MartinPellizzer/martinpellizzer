Hugonia

Hugonia, commonly known as the water arum, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Araceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive inflorescence, which consists of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. Hugonia species are typically found in wet or marshy habitats, thriving in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant's leaves are usually arrow-shaped and have a glossy surface, while the roots are often rhizomatous.

These botanical features make Hugonia an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
