Hypericum asahinae

Hypericum asahinae, commonly known as the Japanese St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the genus Hypericum, which includes over 400 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its yellow flowers and woody stems, typically found in forested areas. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
