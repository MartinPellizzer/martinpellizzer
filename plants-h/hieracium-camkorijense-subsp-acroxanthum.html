Hieracium camkorijense subsp. acroxanthum

Hieracium camkorijense subsp.

acroxanthum, commonly known as the Karakorum hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, preferring well-drained soils. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is adapted to cold, high-altitude climates.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its range.
