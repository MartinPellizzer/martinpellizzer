Hieracium racemosum subsp. pignattianum

Hieracium racemosum subsp.

pignattianum, commonly known as the Pignatti's hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow, daisy-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It has a creeping rootstock and produces numerous flowering stems with bracteate leaves.

Its subspecific classification highlights its distinct morphological and genetic traits compared to other hawkweed species.
