Hibiscus lobatus

Hibiscus lobatus, commonly known as the desert hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. Hibiscus lobatus is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
