Hydrangea vestita

Hydrangea vestita, commonly known as the white hydrangea, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, or blue depending on soil pH. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are surrounded by an involucrum of modified leaves called bracts. Hydrangea vestita thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
