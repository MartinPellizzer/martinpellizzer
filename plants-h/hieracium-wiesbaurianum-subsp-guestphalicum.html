Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp. guestphalicum

Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp.

guestphalicum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers and is typically found in meadows and grassy habitats.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are usually lance-shaped. It produces small, elongated achenes as its fruit, which are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is relatively rare and is often associated with calcareous soils.
