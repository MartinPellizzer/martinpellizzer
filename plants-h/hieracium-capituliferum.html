Hieracium capituliferum

Hieracium capituliferum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellow or orange composite flowers, which are arranged in a single, showy head. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils across Europe and parts of Asia. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rough texture and serrated edges.

Hieracium capituliferum is a perennial herb that spreads through rhizomes and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
