Hordeanthos lemniscatus

Hordeanthos lemniscatus, commonly known as the dancing grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, whip-like stems that move with the wind, giving it a dynamic appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open grasslands.

Its botanical name reflects its distinctive movement, with "lemniscatus" referring to the lasso-like shape of its stems.
