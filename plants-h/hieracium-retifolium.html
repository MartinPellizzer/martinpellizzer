Hieracium retifolium

Hieracium retifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, preferring temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and often hairy, while its yellow flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Hieracium retifolium is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
