Hibiscus xanthium

Hibiscus xanthium, commonly known as the yellow wood hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers with a prominent central pistil and five petals. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are woody and often branched.

Hibiscus xanthium thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
