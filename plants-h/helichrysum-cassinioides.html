Helichrysum cassinioides

Helichrysum cassinioides, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that remain vibrant even when dried. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Helichrysum cassinioides is often used in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting color and delicate appearance.
