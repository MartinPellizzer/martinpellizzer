Helenium rubricaule

Helenium rubricaule, commonly known as the red-stemmed helenium, is a striking perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and vibrant orange to red flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands and along stream banks. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often reddish at the base.

Helenium rubricaule is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
