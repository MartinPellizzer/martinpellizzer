Hieracium oxyodon subsp. blepharophyllum

Hieracium oxyodon subsp.

blepharophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawk's-beard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant has basal leaves that are narrow and linear, with a rough texture, and produces yellow composite flowers in late spring to early summer. Its inflorescence consists of a single flower head surrounded by leaf-like bracts, and it is part of the Asteraceae family.

The species is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
