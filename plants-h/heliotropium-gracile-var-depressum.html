Heliotropium gracile var. depressum

Heliotropium gracile var. depressum, commonly known as the dwarf heliotrope, is a low-growing perennial herb native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features small, violet or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing in the early morning and closing by midday. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially sunny environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
