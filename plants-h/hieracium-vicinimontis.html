Hieracium vicinimontis

Hieracium vicinimontis, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer, and it is often found in alpine meadows and slopes.

Hieracium vicinimontis is adapted to harsh environments, showcasing resilience to cold and strong winds.
