Hamelia cuprea

Hamelia cuprea, commonly known as the copper flame bush, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Hamelia cuprea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
