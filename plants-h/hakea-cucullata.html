Hakea cucullata

Hakea cucullata, commonly known as the hooded hakea, is a shrub native to southwestern Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, urn-shaped flowers that are typically pink to red in color. The plant has needle-like leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Hakea cucullata is often found in coastal heathlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
