Hieracium pospichalii subsp. racemosiforme

Hieracium pospichalii subsp.

racemosiforme, commonly known as the racemosiform hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It typically grows in open, moist habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

The plant features a rosette of basal leaves and produces racemose inflorescences with yellow flowers. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered with fine hairs.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.
