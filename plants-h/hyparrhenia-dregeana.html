Hyparrhenia dregeana

Hyparrhenia dregeana, commonly known as the African grass or red grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, including sandy and clay soils. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often reddish in color, giving it its common name. It is widely used for livestock forage and in erosion control due to its hardiness and rapid growth.

Hyparrhenia dregeana is also valued for its ornamental appeal in landscaping and garden settings.
