Hieracium bracteolatum subsp. reinholdii

Hieracium bracteolatum subsp.

reinholdii, commonly known as Reinhold's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads made up of numerous small florets.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring well-drained conditions. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette, and are covered with fine hairs.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in summer, contributing to its role in pollinator habitats.
