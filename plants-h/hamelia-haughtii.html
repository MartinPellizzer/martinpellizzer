Hamelia haughtii

Hamelia haughtii, commonly known as the flame beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in wetlands and along streambanks. The plant features showy, tubular red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

Hamelia haughtii is valued in gardens for its striking floral display and ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soil.
