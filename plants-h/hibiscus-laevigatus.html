Hibiscus laevigatus

Hibiscus laevigatus, commonly known as the Malabar hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a striking yellow center. The plant has smooth, dark green leaves that are ovate in shape and covered in fine hairs. Hibiscus laevigatus thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
