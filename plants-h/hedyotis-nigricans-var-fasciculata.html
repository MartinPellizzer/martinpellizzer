Hedyotis nigricans var. fasciculata

Hedyotis nigricans var. fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered dotweed, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. The plant features small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers. Its fruit is a three-lobed capsule that releases minute seeds when mature.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
