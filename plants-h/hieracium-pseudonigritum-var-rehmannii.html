Hieracium pseudonigritum var. rehmannii

Hieracium pseudonigritum var. rehmannii, commonly known as the Rehmann's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed sites, preferring well-drained soils. It has basal leaves that are often heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and its stems are upright with a few branches.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
