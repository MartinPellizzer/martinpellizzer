Hieracium murorum subsp. semisilvaticiforme

Hieracium murorum subsp.

semisilvaticiforme, commonly known as the woodland hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow daisy-like flowers and hairy stems.

The subspecies is typically found in woodland edges, meadows, and shaded areas, preferring moist and well-drained soils. It has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered with fine hairs, and its flowering period occurs from late spring to early autumn.

This plant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
