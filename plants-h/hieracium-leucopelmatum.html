Hieracium leucopelmatum

Hieracium leucopelmatum, commonly known as white-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are usually white-veined and hairy, while the flowers are yellow with a central disc.

It is often found in open habitats and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
