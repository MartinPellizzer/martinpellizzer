Hieracium scorzonerifolium subsp. pseudoflexile

Hieracium scorzonerifolium subsp.

pseudoflexile, commonly known as the flexible hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat, daisy-like cluster.

It is notable for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
