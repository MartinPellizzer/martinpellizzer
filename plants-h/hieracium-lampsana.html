Hieracium lampsana

Hieracium lampsana, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium lampsana is known for its ability to form dense colonies, contributing to the ecological dynamics of its habitat.
