Hypericum xylosteifolium

Hypericum xylosteifolium, commonly known as the wood-stemmed St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
