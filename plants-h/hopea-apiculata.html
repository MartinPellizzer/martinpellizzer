Hopea apiculata

Hopea apiculata, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a large evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall, with a straight trunk and broad, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its flowers are small and yellowish, and it produces woody capsules that contain numerous seeds.

Hopea apiculata plays an important ecological role in tropical rainforests, providing habitat and contributing to biodiversity.
