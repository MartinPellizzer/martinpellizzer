Haemaria discolor var. grandis

Haemaria discolor var. grandis, commonly known as the large-flowered sand verbena, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a pale underside. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native range.
