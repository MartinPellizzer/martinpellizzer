Hedysarum krassnowii

Hedysarum krassnowii, commonly known as the Krassnow's milkvetch, is a perennial leguminous plant native to Central Asia.

It features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers in late spring. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and is often found in dry, rocky habitats. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation due to its association with rhizobia bacteria.

Hedysarum krassnowii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in arid environments.
