Haemanthus pole-evansii

Haemanthus pole-evansii, commonly known as the Natal grass cycad, is a unique plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cycadaceae family and is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of grass-like leaves. The plant produces large, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs. It is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its slow growth and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
