Hibiscus brasiliensis var. luteus

Hibiscus brasiliensis var. luteus, commonly known as the yellow wood hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a smooth texture and a vibrant green color.

The flowers have five petals and are known for their striking color and ornamental value in gardens.
