Hoitzia floribunda

Hoitzia floribunda, commonly known as the velvetleaf or silverleaf plant, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers, which are often borne on short pedicels. Hoitzia floribunda is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.

It is also used in traditional medicine in some regions for its purported health benefits.
