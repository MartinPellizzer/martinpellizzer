Hieracium jablonicense subsp. jablonicense

Hieracium jablonicense subsp.

jablonicense, commonly known as the Jablonicek's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and forest edges, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in late summer. Its inflorescence consists of a single, terminal capitulum surrounded by pale green bracts.

The species is characterized by its small, glandular leaves and is often found in association with other early successional plants.
