Harveya alectroides

Harveya alectroides, commonly known as the red-flowered harveya, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of silky hair. Harveya alectroides thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soil.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to add color to rock gardens and alpine displays.
