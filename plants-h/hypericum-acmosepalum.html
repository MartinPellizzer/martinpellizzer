Hypericum acmosepalum

Hypericum acmosepalum, commonly known as the yellow St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring woody stems and opposite leaves that are oval to elliptic in shape. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Hypericum acmosepalum is often found in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
