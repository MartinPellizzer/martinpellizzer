Hemigraphis palawanensis var. hirsuta

Hemigraphis palawanensis var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy-leaf iron plant, is a species native to the Philippines.

It is a low-growing herbaceous plant characterized by its velvety, hairy leaves that give it a unique texture. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape and display a striking combination of green and red or purple hues. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in montane forests.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for landscaping purposes.
