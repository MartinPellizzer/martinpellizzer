Hakea brevis

Hakea brevis, commonly known as the shortleaf hakea, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical stems and small, needle-like leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces bright red or orange flowers in late summer, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. Hakea brevis is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

It is an important plant in local ecosystems, providing habitat and nectar for native wildlife.
