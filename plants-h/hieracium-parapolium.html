Hieracium parapolium

Hieracium parapolium, commonly known as the Parapolish hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in composite flower heads. Native to Central and Eastern Europe, it thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant has a taproot system and can spread vegetatively through rhizomes.

It is often found in regions with temperate climates and is relatively drought-tolerant.
