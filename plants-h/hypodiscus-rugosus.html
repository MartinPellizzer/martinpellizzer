Hypodiscus rugosus

Hypodiscus rugosus, commonly known as the roughleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its rough, warty leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, flat seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

Hypodiscus rugosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
