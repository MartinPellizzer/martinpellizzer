Hymenolobium janeirense

Hymenolobium janeirense, commonly known as the Brazilian yellowwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It produces yellow to orange flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by woody, cylindrical pods.

Hymenolobium janeirense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
