Hieracium robustum subsp. turanicum

Hieracium robustum subsp.

turanicum, commonly known as the Turanian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features dark green, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. Its flowering period occurs from June to August, and it is characterized by its robust growth habit and hairy stems.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is known for its composite flower heads and diverse ecological roles.
