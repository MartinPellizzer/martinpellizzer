Hieracium trigonoides

Hieracium trigonoides, commonly known as the three-angled hawkweed, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has serrated, alternate leaves that are often hairy, and its stem is typically branched. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in meadows and grasslands.

It is considered an invasive species in some regions due to its rapid spread and competitive nature.
