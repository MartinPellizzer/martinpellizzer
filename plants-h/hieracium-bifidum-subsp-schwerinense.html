Hieracium bifidum subsp. schwerinense

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

schwerinense, commonly known as the Schwerin's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, thriving in temperate climates. It has finely divided, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that bloom in the summer.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
