Holocarpha obconica subsp. autumnalis

Holocarpha obconica subsp.

autumnalis, commonly known as the autumn flax, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a perennial herb characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn, followed by distinctive cylindrical seed pods. Its botanical features include fibrous roots and a compact growth habit, making it well adapted to arid environments.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
