Hieracium eurylepium subsp. poliophyllum

Hieracium eurylepium subsp.

poliophyllum, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its hairy, yellowish-green stems and leaves, which are often covered in fine, soft hairs.

The plant produces daisy-like flowers with yellow disc florets surrounded by white to pale pink ray florets. It is typically found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
