Hibiscus velutinus

Hibiscus velutinus, commonly known as velvetleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous annual or perennial, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers bloom throughout the growing season, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hibiscus velutinus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
