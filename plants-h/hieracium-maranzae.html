Hieracium maranzae

Hieracium maranzae, commonly known as the Maranza's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium maranzae is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
