Hieracium leiocephalum subsp. bupleuropsis

Hieracium leiocephalum subsp.

bupleuropsis, commonly known as the bluehead hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring temperate climates. Its flowers are small and yellow, often grouped in clusters, and it produces achenes as its primary seed type.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
