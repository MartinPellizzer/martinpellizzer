Hippeastrum ciceroanum

Hippeastrum ciceroanum, commonly known as the Cicero's amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, large, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. The plant produces a tall, leafless stem that bears the blooms, followed by the development of a bulbous underground storage organ. Its foliage consists of broad, strap-like leaves that emerge from the base of the plant.

Hippeastrum ciceroanum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
