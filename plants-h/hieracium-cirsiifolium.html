Hieracium cirsiifolium

Hieracium cirsiifolium, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features upright, hairy stems and basal leaves that are often lobed or toothed. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators.

Hieracium cirsiifolium is typically found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, where it thrives in full sun.
