Hieracium pseudocerinthe

Hieracium pseudocerinthe, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are usually basal, with a rough, hairy texture, and the plant can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
