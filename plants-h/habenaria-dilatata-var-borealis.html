Habenaria dilatata var. borealis

Habenaria dilatata var. borealis, commonly known as the northern marsh helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to the northern regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as bogs and swampy woods. The plant features a sympodial growth habit with a rhizome and produces striking greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, and it blooms from June to August.

This orchid is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and is often found in wetland habitats.
