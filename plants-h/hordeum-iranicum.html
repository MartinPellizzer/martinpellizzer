Hordeum iranicum

Hordeum iranicum, commonly known as the Iranian barley, is a wild grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical spikelets and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its seeds are small and hardy, often used in traditional agriculture or as forage for livestock.

Hordeum iranicum plays a role in ecological systems by stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
