Hieracium dacicum subsp. dacicum

Hieracium dacicum subsp.

dacicum, commonly known as the Dacian hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems, with basal leaves that are lanceolate and often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming from June to August. It is native to southern Europe and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
