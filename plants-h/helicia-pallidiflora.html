Helicia pallidiflora

Helicia pallidiflora, commonly known as the pale-flowered helicia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the wet season. Its fruit is a small, woody drupe that contains a single seed.

Helicia pallidiflora is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
