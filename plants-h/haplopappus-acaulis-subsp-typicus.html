Haplopappus acaulis subsp. typicus

Haplopappus acaulis subsp.

typicus, commonly known as the slender pappus plant, is a low-growing perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America. It typically forms dense mats of foliage with small, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, followed by distinctive, bract-like structures that aid in wind dispersal of its seeds. It is adapted to poor soils and can thrive in harsh environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of desert ecosystems.
