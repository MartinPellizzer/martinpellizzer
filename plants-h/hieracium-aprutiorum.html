Hieracium aprutiorum

Hieracium aprutiorum, commonly known as the Abruzzi hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a dense cluster.

Hieracium aprutiorum is often found in alpine and subalpine regions, where it thrives in cooler, moist environments.
