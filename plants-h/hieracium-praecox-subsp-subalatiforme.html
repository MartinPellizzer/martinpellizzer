Hieracium praecox subsp. subalatiforme

Hieracium praecox subsp.

subalatiforme, commonly known as the subalpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky or gravelly soils.

The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are usually hairy and lanceolate in shape. Its flowering stems bear yellow flowers arranged in a single head, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and is often found in open, sunny environments at high elevations.
