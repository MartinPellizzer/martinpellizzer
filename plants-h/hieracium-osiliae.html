Hieracium osiliae

Hieracium osiliae, commonly known as the Osilia hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with prominent central disks.

Hieracium osiliae is a perennial herb that plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
