Haplanthodes tentaculata var. plumosa

Haplanthodes tentaculata var. plumosa, commonly known as the feather-leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, feather-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in spike-like clusters, and it produces small, three-lobed fruits.
