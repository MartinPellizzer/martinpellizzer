Hordeum vulgare var. hypatherum

Hordeum vulgare var. hypatherum, commonly known as slender barley, is a variant of barley characterized by its slender, often more delicate stalks compared to standard barley.

It is a grass species native to temperate regions and is typically found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. The plant produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences with two to three rows of grains, which are usually less robust than those of cultivated barley. It is adapted to a variety of soil types and can thrive in both sunny and partially shaded environments.

This variety is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil.
