Hieracium provinciale

Hieracium provinciale, commonly known as the provincial hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellowish to orange flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are composite, consisting of both ray and disk florets, and it produces small, oval-shaped achenes as fruits.

Hieracium provinciale is often found in disturbed habitats and can be an indicator of soil conditions.
