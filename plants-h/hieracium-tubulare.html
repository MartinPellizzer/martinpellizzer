Hieracium tubulare

Hieracium tubulare, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features upright stems covered in fine hairs and produces yellow to orange flowers with tubular central cones. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough, hairy texture.

Hieracium tubulare is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.
