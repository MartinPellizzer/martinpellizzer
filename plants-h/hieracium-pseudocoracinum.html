Hieracium pseudocoracinum

Hieracium pseudocoracinum, commonly known as the false spear thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It features spiny leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

Its robust growth and defensive structures help it thrive in competitive environments.
