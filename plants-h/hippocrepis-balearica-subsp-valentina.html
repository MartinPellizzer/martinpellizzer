Hippocrepis balearica subsp. valentina

Hippocrepis balearica subsp.

valentina, commonly known as the Balearic bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Balearic Islands. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in racemes.

This subspecies is relatively rare and is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique ecological adaptations.
