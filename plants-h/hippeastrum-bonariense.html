Hippeastrum bonariense

Hippeastrum bonariense, commonly known as the Brazilian belladonna lily, is a bulbous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant produces large, showy blooms during the winter months, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening. Its leaves are broad, strap-like, and often emerge after the flowers have faded.

Hippeastrum bonariense is known for its resilience and ability to thrive in a variety of growing conditions.
