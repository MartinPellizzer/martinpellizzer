Hedysarum austrosibiricum

Hedysarum austrosibiricum, commonly known as the Siberian milkvetch, is a perennial leguminous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in steppes and rocky soils. It plays a role in soil stabilization and nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with rhizobia.

Its seeds are sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.
