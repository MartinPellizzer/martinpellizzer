Helicia serrata var. oreophila

Helicia serrata var. oreophila, commonly known as the mountain helicia, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, elliptical leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers that are followed by small, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires a humid, shaded environment.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
