Hieracium setarum

Hieracium setarum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this species thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. It is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

The plant is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and can form dense colonies.
