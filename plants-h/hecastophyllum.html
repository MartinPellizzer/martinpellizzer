Hecastophyllum

Hecastophyllum, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple ray flowers and spiny, silver-green leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and saline soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Hecastophyllum has small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating.

It produces small, cylindrical seed heads that mature into fluffy, white structures, which aid in wind pollination.
