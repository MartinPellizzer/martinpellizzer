Helianthemum songaricum

Helianthemum songaricum, commonly known as the Songaric sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its mat-forming growth habit and features small, woolly, gray-green leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is drought-tolerant.

Helianthemum songaricum is often found in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to harsh, dry conditions.
