Hieracium argentarium

Hieracium argentarium, commonly known as the silver hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features silver-white leaves with a hairy texture, and its flowers are typically yellow with a central disk. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Due to its invasive potential, it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
