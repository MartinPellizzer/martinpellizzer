Hieracium murorum subsp. pseudosubulatidens

Hieracium murorum subsp.

pseudosubulatidens, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to alpine regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it forms dense mats or low-growing clumps.

The plant features yellow flowers with ray florets and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves. It has a fibrous root system and is adapted to withstand cold and harsh environmental conditions.

This subspecies is often found in mountainous areas and is part of the Asteraceae family.
