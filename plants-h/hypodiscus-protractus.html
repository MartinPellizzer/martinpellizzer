Hypodiscus protractus

Hypodiscus protractus, commonly known as the African orchid or African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with broad, velvety leaves that are often dark green in color. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Hypodiscus protractus is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
