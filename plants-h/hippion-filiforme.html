Hippion filiforme

Hippion filiforme, commonly known as the slender hippion, is a unique plant species characterized by its delicate, thread-like leaves.

It is native to specific regions in South America, particularly in the Andean foothills. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found growing along stream banks or in humid forests. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically appearing in clusters.

Due to its limited distribution and fragile nature, Hippion filiforme is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
