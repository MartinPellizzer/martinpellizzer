Heckeria

Heckeria, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and elusive flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, delicate appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and a single, showy flower that often resembles a ghostly silhouette. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surfaces of trees without deriving nutrients from them. Heckeria thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires specific conditions to flourish.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern in many areas.
