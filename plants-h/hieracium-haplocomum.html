Hieracium haplocomum

Hieracium haplocomum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and typically herbaceous growth habit. The plant features upright stems that are often hairy, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are yellow and occur in clusters, forming a composite head that resembles a single flower.

Hieracium haplocomum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed sites, thriving in well-drained soils.
