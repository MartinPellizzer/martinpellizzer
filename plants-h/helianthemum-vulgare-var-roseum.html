Helianthemum vulgare var. roseum

Helianthemum vulgare var. roseum, commonly known as the pink rockrose, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep rose, typically blooming in spring and summer. The plant has woody stems covered in fine, white hairs, and its leaves are small, evergreen, and often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

The pink rockrose is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
