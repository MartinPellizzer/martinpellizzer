Hemigenia diplanthera

Hemigenia diplanthera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved honey mint, is a species of flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understoreys.

Hemigenia diplanthera is valued for its fragrant foliage and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
