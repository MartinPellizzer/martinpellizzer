Hibiscus vitifolius subsp. vulgaris

Hibiscus vitifolius subsp.

vulgaris, commonly known as the common hibiscus, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height.

The plant features large, showy flowers that are typically pink or red, with five petals and a distinctive central column. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and often have a hairy texture. Hibiscus vitifolius subsp.

vulgaris thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
