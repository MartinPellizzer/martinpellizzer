Hylotelephium maximum subsp. ruprechtii

Hylotelephium maximum subsp.

ruprechtii, commonly known as the Ruprecht's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features fleshy, upright stems and rosettes of narrow, waxy leaves that are typically green or bluish-green in color.

The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or red during the summer months. It is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, thriving in full sun and well-drained soil.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in gardens.
