Helleborus rhodanicus

Helleborus rhodanicus, commonly known as the Corsican hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dark green, leathery leaves that are typically heart-shaped and covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of striking, waxy white or pale pink flowers with prominent, petal-like bracts. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.

Helleborus rhodanicus is noted for its toxicity, containing various alkaloids that can be harmful if ingested.
