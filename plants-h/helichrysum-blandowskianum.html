Helichrysum blandowskianum

Helichrysum blandowskianum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, yellow-orange inflorescences that resemble small daisies. The plant produces small, tubular flowers surrounded by ray florets, which are often mistaken for petals. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The plant is also valued for its aromatic foliage and is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
