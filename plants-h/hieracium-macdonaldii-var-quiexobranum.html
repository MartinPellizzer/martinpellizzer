Hieracium macdonaldii var. quiexobranum

Hieracium macdonaldii var. quiexobranum, commonly known as the Macdonald's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lanceolate, with a rough, hairy texture, and its flowers are yellow, often appearing in clusters.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
