Hieracium spectabile

Hieracium spectabile, commonly known as showy hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Hieracium spectabile is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its showy, composite flower heads.
