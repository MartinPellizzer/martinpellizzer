Helichrysum ochraceum

Helichrysum ochraceum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that retain their color even after drying. The plant features wiry, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated as a garden plant and used in floral arrangements.
