Hemsleya macrosperma

Hemsleya macrosperma, commonly known as the giant spider orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 centimeters in length, featuring a prominent spadix and a vibrant, often purple or pink-colored lip. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as montane forests, where it thrives in well-drained, nutrient-rich soil. Its unique floral structure includes a specialized column for pollination, and it relies on specific pollinators like flies and wasps.

Hemsleya macrosperma is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its ecological significance in its native habitats.
