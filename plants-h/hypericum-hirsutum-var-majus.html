Hypericum hirsutum var. majus

Hypericum hirsutum var. majus, commonly known as the hairy St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, which gives it its common name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used for their anti-inflammatory and antidepressant effects.
