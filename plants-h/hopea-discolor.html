Hopea discolor

Hopea discolor, commonly known as the white ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, hard wood and smooth, pale bark. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody capsules that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, lowland forests and is valued for its durable timber.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable in some regions.
