Hibiscus rhodopetalus var. angustisectus

Hibiscus rhodopetalus var. angustisectus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf woods rose, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its botanical features include hairy stems and five-petaled flowers with a prominent central stigma.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
