Hippophae neurocarpa subsp. stellatopilosa

Hippophae neurocarpa subsp.

stellatopilosa, commonly known as the Chinese sandthorn, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of northern China. It is characterized by its yellow-orange berries, which are rich in vitamins and antioxidants.

The plant has small, alternate leaves and produces yellow flowers in the spring. It is well adapted to harsh environments, tolerating drought and poor soil conditions.

Its ecological importance includes soil stabilization and providing food for wildlife.
