Hieracium scrofulosum

Hieracium scrofulosum, commonly known as the scrofulous hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
