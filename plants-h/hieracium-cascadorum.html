Hieracium cascadorum

Hieracium cascadorum, commonly known as the Cascade hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. The plant features upright stems with basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped cluster, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

Hieracium cascadorum is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and ability to thrive in diverse environmental conditions.
