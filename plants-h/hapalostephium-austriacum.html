Hapalostephium austriacum

Hapalostephium austriacum, commonly known as the Austrian grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Europe.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and riverbanks. The plant has slender, upright stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
