Hyptis crassipes

Hyptis crassipes, commonly known as Mexican mint marigold, is a perennial herb native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and culinary uses. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly rough texture. Its flowers are typically tubular, violet to purple in color, and bloom in clusters at the ends of the branches.

Hyptis crassipes is often cultivated for its fragrant foliage and ornamental value in gardens.
