Helichrysum boormanii var. tryonii

Helichrysum boormanii var. tryonii, commonly known as the yellow everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces small, bright yellow flowers that are often used in floral arrangements. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping. Its foliage is silver-green, adding visual interest even when not in bloom.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
