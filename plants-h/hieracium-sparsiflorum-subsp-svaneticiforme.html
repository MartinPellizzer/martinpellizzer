Hieracium sparsiflorum subsp. svaneticiforme

Hieracium sparsiflorum subsp.

svaneticiforme, commonly known as the Svanetian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves.

The flowers are typically yellow, with ray florets and a central disk, and are arranged in a single head. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and forest edges.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slender stem and smaller, more numerous flower heads compared to other members of the species.
