Hieracium mitigatum

Hieracium mitigatum, commonly known as the lesser hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers arranged in dense clusters. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a slightly rough texture. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in woodland edges.

This species is native to Europe and has been introduced to other parts of the world, where it can sometimes become invasive.
