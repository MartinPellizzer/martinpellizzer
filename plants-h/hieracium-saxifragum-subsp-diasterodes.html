Hieracium saxifragum subsp. diasterodes

Hieracium saxifragum subsp.

diasterodes, commonly known as the mountain hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, preferring sunny, well-drained habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

This subspecies is distinguished by its unique floral structure and is often found in mountainous areas across the Alps and Carpathians.
