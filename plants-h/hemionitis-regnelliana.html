Hemionitis regnelliana

Hemionitis regnelliana, commonly known as the Regnell's hemionitis, is a rare and delicate fern native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, pinnate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of silky hair, giving it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is typically found in shaded, rocky habitats. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are located on the underside of the leaves, typical of many fern species.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered plant in its native range.
