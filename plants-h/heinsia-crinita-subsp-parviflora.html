Heinsia crinita subsp. parviflora

Heinsia crinita subsp.

parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, characterized by its compact growth habit.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. It has narrow, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
