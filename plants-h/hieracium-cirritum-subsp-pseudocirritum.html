Hieracium cirritum subsp. pseudocirritum

Hieracium cirritum subsp.

pseudocirritum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow to orange composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it has lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces. It produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal, and it is often found in open habitats with moderate sunlight.

Its botanical features make it a common sight in temperate regions.
