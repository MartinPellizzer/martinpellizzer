Hieracium andrasovszkyi

Hieracium andrasovszkyi, commonly known as the Andrasovszky's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf structures. The plant typically features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

Hieracium andrasovszkyi thrives in moist, open habitats like meadows and forest edges, where it spreads through its rhizomatous root system.
