Hieracium prenanthoides var. karelorum

Hieracium prenanthoides var. karelorum, commonly known as the Karelorum hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as tundra and rocky slopes. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, and its leaves are usually basal, with a rough texture.

This variant is distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and morphological traits that adapt it to its harsh environment.
