Helichrysum aureum

Helichrysum aureum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that remain vibrant even when dried. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and as a border plant.

Helichrysum aureum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and use in floral arrangements.
