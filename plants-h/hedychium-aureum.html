Hedychium aureum

Hedychium aureum, commonly known as the golden ginger, is a member of the Zingiberaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking yellow flowers with a sweet, spicy fragrance. The plant features long, narrow leaves and has a rhizomatous root system. Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Hedychium aureum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
