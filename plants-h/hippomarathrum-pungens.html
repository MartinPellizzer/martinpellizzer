Hippomarathrum pungens

Hippomarathrum pungens, commonly known as the spiny pincushion cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, spiny stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are reduced to spines, a feature typical of many cacti adapted to minimize water loss.

The plant's structure and growth habits reflect its evolutionary adaptations to harsh, desert-like conditions.
