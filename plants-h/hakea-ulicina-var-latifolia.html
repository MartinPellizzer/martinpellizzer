Hakea ulicina var. latifolia

Hakea ulicina var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved hakea, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces cylindrical, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and resilience in arid conditions.
