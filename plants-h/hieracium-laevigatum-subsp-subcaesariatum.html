Hieracium laevigatum subsp. subcaesariatum

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

subcaesariatum, commonly known as the pale hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

This subspecies is notable for its pale yellow flowers and is often found in temperate regions.
