Hieracium levicaule subsp. rubroviolascens

Hieracium levicaule subsp.

rubroviolascens, commonly known as the red-violet hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, while the flowers display a striking combination of red and violet hues.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
