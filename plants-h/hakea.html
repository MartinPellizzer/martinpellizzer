Hakea

Hakea, commonly known as the bottlebrush plant, is a genus of flowering plants native to Australia.

These plants are characterized by their distinctive cylindrical or spiky flower clusters, which resemble a bottlebrush. Hakea species typically have woody stems and produce small, often needle-like leaves. They are adapted to arid environments, with many species exhibiting fire-resistant properties.

The genus includes over 150 species, showcasing a wide variety of growth forms and habitats.
