Hyparrhenia piovanii

Hyparrhenia piovanii, commonly known as the Piovan's grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils, and it is well-adapted to dry conditions. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often arranged in a panicle structure.

Hyparrhenia piovanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a source of thatch in traditional settings.
