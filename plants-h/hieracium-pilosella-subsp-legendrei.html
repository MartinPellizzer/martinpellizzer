Hieracium pilosella subsp. legendrei

Hieracium pilosella subsp.

legendrei, commonly known as the Alpine Groundsel, is a flowering plant native to alpine regions of Europe. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers, which bloom in late summer, are yellow and resemble dandelions, and it is often found in mountain meadows.

This subspecies is notable for its small size and hardy nature, adapted to harsh environmental conditions.
