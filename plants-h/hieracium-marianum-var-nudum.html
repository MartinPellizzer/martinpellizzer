Hieracium marianum var. nudum

Hieracium marianum var. nudum, commonly known as the naked hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are basal, with a rough texture and often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
