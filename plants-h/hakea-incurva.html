Hakea incurva

Hakea incurva, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hakea, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical or conical clusters of leaves that are typically narrow and recurved. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. Hakea incurva is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
