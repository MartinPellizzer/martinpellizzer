Hymenopappus tenuifolius

Hymenopappus tenuifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leafed burrobrush, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive bracts. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and high sunlight exposure. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The flowers, which bloom in the spring, are yellow and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
