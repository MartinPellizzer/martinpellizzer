Hakea dryandroides

Hakea dryandroides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and clusters of bright red or orange flowers. The plant produces woody, cone-like fruits that contain small, hard seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, Mediterranean climates.

Hakea dryandroides is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
