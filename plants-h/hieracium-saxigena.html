Hieracium saxigena

Hieracium saxigena, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its hairy stems and serrated leaves are adapted to withstand cold and harsh climatic conditions.

Hieracium saxigena is an important component of alpine ecosystems, contributing to biodiversity and soil stabilization.
