Hessea vaginata

Hessea vaginata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hollyhock, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has linear, hairy leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Hessea vaginata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
