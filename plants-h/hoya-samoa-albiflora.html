Hoya samoa-albiflora

Hoya samoa-albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered wax plant, is a species native to tropical regions of Oceania.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with cream or white markings. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Hoya samoa-albiflora is a climbing plant that thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.

It is popular among succulent and tropical plant enthusiasts due to its ornamental appeal and ease of care.
