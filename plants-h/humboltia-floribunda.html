Humboltia floribunda

Humboltia floribunda, commonly known as the hummingbird flower, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, designed to attract pollinators like hummingbirds. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal dunes and scrub habitats. Its leaves are usually narrow and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Humboltia floribunda is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
