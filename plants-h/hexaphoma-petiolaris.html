Hexaphoma petiolaris

Hexaphoma petiolaris, commonly known as the petiole smut, is a fungal plant pathogen that primarily affects the petioles of various grasses.

It belongs to the order Ustilaginales and is characterized by the formation of dark, powdery spore masses on infected plant parts. The disease typically causes discoloration and deformation of the petioles, which can lead to reduced plant vigor and yield in affected crops. It is most commonly found in warm, humid climates where grasses are abundant.

The fungus spreads through spores that are dispersed by wind, water, or agricultural machinery.
