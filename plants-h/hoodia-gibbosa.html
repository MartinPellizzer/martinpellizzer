Hoodia gibbosa

Hoodia gibbosa, commonly known as the desert cabbage, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, spineless stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom during the rainy season. The plant has adapted to extreme drought conditions by storing water in its thick, fleshy leaves. Its unique ability to survive in harsh environments has made it a subject of interest in botanical research.

Hoodia gibbosa is also known for containing a compound called P57, which has been studied for its potential appetite-suppressing properties.
