Hexopetion

Hexopetion, commonly known as the six-petaled orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful flowers that typically feature six distinct petals, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil efficiently.

The orchid is also noted for its fragrant blooms, which attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
