Heliconia angelica

Heliconia angelica, commonly known as the angel's trumpet, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy bracts that resemble flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 6 feet. Its leaves are large, broad, and often have a waxy texture, while its inflorescence consists of clustered, brightly colored bracts.

Heliconia angelica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates for its vibrant appearance.
