Hemerocallis aurantiaca

Hemerocallis aurantiaca, commonly known as the orange daylily, is a perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Hemerocallis genus, which includes many species of daylilies known for their showy, short-lived flowers. The plant produces vibrant orange blooms that last for a single day, hence its common name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.

Hemerocallis aurantiaca is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
