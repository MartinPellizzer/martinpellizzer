Hieracium longiradiatum

Hieracium longiradiatum, commonly known as the long-rayed hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often hairy. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, and its root system is fibrous and rhizomatous.

Hieracium longiradiatum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, where it can spread aggressively.
