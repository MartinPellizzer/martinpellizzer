Hyptis oppositifolia

Hyptis oppositifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper plant or green hyptis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, tubular flowers are typically violet or blue, arranged in dense clusters.

Hyptis oppositifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
