Hieracium pseudobifidum subsp. obliquifidum

Hieracium pseudobifidum subsp.

obliquifidum, commonly known as the oblique-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are broadly lanceolate with a distinctive, irregularly toothed margin.

The flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value in horticulture.
