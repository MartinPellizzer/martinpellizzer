Hypericum nigropunctatum

Hypericum nigropunctatum, commonly known as black dot St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features small, dark purple glands on its stems and leaves, which give it its common name. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Hypericum nigropunctatum is often found in moist, open woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
