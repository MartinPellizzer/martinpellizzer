Haworthia pumila subsp. longiana

Haworthia pumila subsp.

longiana, commonly known as the long-leaved Haworthia, is a compact, low-growing succulent native to South Africa. It features rosettes of fleshy, bluish-gray leaves with white tubercles and a distinctive, narrow, leaf-like structure at the base.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers on short, upright stems during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for rock gardens or container cultivation.

Its unique appearance and easy care make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
