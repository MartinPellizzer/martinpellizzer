Heteropterys martiana f. glandulifera

Heteropterys martiana f. glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular heteropterys, is a botanical variant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by elliptical, dark blue or black berries. Its foliage is often covered in glandular hairs, which contribute to its unique appearance and may play a role in its ecological interactions.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various growing conditions.
