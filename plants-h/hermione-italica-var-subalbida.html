Hermione italica var. subalbida

Hermione italica var. subalbida, commonly known as the white-flowered Italian honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
