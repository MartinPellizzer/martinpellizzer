Hieracium subrosulatum subsp. phrygum

Hieracium subrosulatum subsp.

phrygum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed sites, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are basal and oblanceolate, with a hairy surface, and its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
