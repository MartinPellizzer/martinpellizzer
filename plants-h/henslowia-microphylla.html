Henslowia microphylla

Henslowia microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf henslowia, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along the edges of swamps and in pine flatwoods. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

Henslowia microphylla is known for its ability to thrive in wetland environments and is often found in association with other moisture-loving plants.
