Hedysarum tiliifolium

Hedysarum tiliifolium, commonly known as the leafy sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along riverbanks, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Hedysarum tiliifolium is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it a beneficial plant for soil enrichment.

Its historical use in herbal medicine and as a forage crop highlights its ecological and agricultural significance.
