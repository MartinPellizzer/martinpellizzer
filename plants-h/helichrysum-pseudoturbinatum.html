Helichrysum pseudoturbinatum

Helichrysum pseudoturbinatum, commonly known as the Cape golden bush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, golden-yellow inflorescences that resemble small sunflowers. The plant typically grows in shrub form, with narrow, silvery-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its drought tolerance and long-lasting flowers.
