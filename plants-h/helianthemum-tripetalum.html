Helianthemum tripetalum

Helianthemum tripetalum, commonly known as the three-petaled rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub with woody stems and evergreen leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Helianthemum tripetalum is valued for its hardiness and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
