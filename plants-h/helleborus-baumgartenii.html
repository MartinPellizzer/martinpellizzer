Helleborus baumgartenii

Helleborus baumgartenii, commonly known as the Baumann's hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It features dark green, leathery leaves that are broadly ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained, moist soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Helleborus baumgartenii is also noted for its ability to tolerate cold temperatures, making it a hardy addition to temperate climates.
