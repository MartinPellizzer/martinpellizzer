Hylotelephium carpaticum

Hylotelephium carpaticum, commonly known as the Carpathian rose, is a perennial succulent native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It features fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically green to reddish in color, often with a waxy coating. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers in late summer, arranged in dense, compact inflorescences. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a versatile and hardy addition to many landscapes.
