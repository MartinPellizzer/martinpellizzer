Helichrysum rosum var. rosum

Helichrysum rosum var. rosum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces small, bright yellow flowers that resemble tiny suns. The plant is known for its long-lasting flowers, which retain their color and form even after drying, making it popular in floral arrangements. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a valued addition to gardens and landscapes.
