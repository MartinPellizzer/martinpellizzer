Honckenya peploides var. diffusa

Honckenya peploides var. diffusa, commonly known as the silky sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to arid and coastal regions of North America.

It features small, green to blue-green leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces tiny, pinkish-white flowers in dense clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dunes, gravelly slopes, and disturbed areas.

Due to its hardy nature and ability to stabilize soil, it plays an important ecological role in its native habitats.
