Hepetis camptocalyx var. lutea

Hepetis camptocalyx var. lutea, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy addition to native gardens.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for pollinators and ecological restoration projects.
