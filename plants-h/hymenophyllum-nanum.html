Hymenophyllum nanum

Hymenophyllum nanum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf filmy fern, is a small, delicate fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, translucent fronds that are often covered in a dense layer of minute, hair-like structures. This species thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and are protected by a specialized covering.

The plant's unique morphology and habitat requirements make it an important subject in the study of epiphytic and lithophytic flora.
