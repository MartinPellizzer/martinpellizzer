Hieracium murorum var. camptopetalum

Hieracium murorum var. camptopetalum, commonly known as the bent-petaled hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow-orange flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks.

This variant is distinguished by its unique flower structure, with petals that curve inward, giving it its common name.
