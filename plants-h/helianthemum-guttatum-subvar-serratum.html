Helianthemum guttatum subvar. serratum

Helianthemum guttatum subvar. serratum, commonly known as the serrated rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its small, fleshy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to sunny, well-drained environments. Its subspecies name, *serratum*, refers to the serrated edges of its leaves.

This plant is often used in rock gardens and as a drought-tolerant ornamental species.
