Heliotropium chorassanicum var. lasiocarpum

Heliotropium chorassanicum var. lasiocarpum, commonly known as the wooly fruit heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pinkish to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that is covered in a dense layer of fine, wooly hairs.

This species is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.
