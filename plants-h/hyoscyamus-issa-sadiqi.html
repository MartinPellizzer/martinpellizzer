Hyoscyamus issa-sadiqi

Hyoscyamus issa-sadiqi, commonly known as the Sudanese henbane, is a flowering plant belonging to the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, particularly Sudan and Eritrea. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has hairy, branched stems that are often reddish in color. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered with fine hairs, while its fruit is a capsule containing numerous seeds.

Due to its toxic properties, it is considered a poisonous plant and is not typically cultivated for ornamental or agricultural purposes.
