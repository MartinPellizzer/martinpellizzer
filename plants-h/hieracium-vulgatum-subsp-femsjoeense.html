Hieracium vulgatum subsp. femsjoeense

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

femsjoeense, commonly known as the Femsjoe Hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and basal rosette of leaves.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smaller flower heads and shorter peduncles compared to other members of the species. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
