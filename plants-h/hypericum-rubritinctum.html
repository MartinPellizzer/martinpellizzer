Hypericum rubritinctum

Hypericum rubritinctum, commonly known as the red-stained St. John's wort, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Hypericaceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in woodland areas and along stream banks. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, surrounded by attractive, slightly hairy leaves. Its stems are often reddish in color, which gives it its common name.

Hypericum rubritinctum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
