Hymenophyllum tasmannicum

Hymenophyllum tasmannicum, commonly known as the Tasmanian moss-fern, is a small, epiphytic fern native to southern Australia.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or rocks. Its fronds are delicate and feather-like, with a waxy coating that helps retain moisture. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
