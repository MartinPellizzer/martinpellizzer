Haageocereus lachayensis

Haageocereus lachayensis, commonly known as the Lachay's night bloomer, is a species of cactus native to southern Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and nocturnal flowering habit. The plant produces large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-drained soil and minimal water.

Its botanical features make it a unique and visually striking addition to desert landscapes.
