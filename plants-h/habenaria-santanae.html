Habenaria santanae

Habenaria santanae, commonly known as the Santana's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and delicate, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. Its inflorescence consists of a single raceme bearing several small, fragrant flowers.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
