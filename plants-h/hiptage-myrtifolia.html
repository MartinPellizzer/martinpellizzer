Hiptage myrtifolia

Hiptage myrtifolia, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Hiptage myrtifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a street tree.
