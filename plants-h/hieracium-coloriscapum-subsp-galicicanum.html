Hieracium coloriscapum subsp. galicicanum

Hieracium coloriscapum subsp.

galicicanum, commonly known as the Galician yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has lanceolate to ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces numerous small, yellowish composite flowers.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its range.
