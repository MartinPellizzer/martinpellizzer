Hieracium tomentosum subsp. psilopogon

Hieracium tomentosum subsp.

psilopogon, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils. It has a taproot system and produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various conditions, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
