Hieracium murorum subsp. repandulum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

repandulum, commonly known as the creeping hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering stems that bear yellow composite flowers.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered with fine pubescence. It thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Due to its aggressive growth habit, it is considered a weed in some regions.
