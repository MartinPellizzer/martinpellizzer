Hordeum sativum subsp. vulgare

Hordeum sativum subsp.

vulgare, commonly known as barley, is a cereal grain native to the temperate regions of Europe, Asia, and Africa. It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and spikelet inflorescence, with each spikelet containing two to three rows of small, glabrous grains.

The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in well-drained soils with moderate temperatures. Barley is a cool-season crop that is cultivated for its edible grains, which are used in food, animal feed, and brewing.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both agricultural and ecological contexts.
