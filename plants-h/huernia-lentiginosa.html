Huernia lentiginosa

Huernia lentiginosa, commonly known as the spotted moon cactus, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its swollen, fleshy stems that resemble a moon. The plant features a distinctive pattern of dark spots and streaks on its surface, which provide camouflage in its natural habitat. It produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in summer.

Huernia lentiginosa is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil with plenty of sunlight.
