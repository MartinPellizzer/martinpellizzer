Hookera multipedunculata

Hookera multipedunculata, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette growth habit and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue and purple. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to rocky or alpine soils. Its leaves are typically basal, lobed, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Hookera multipedunculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
