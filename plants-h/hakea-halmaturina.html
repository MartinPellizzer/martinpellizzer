Hakea halmaturina

Hakea halmaturina, commonly known as the Halmaturina hakea, is a shrub native to the arid regions of South Australia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, needle-like leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, urn-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. Its seed capsules are woody and often persist on the plant for years before dispersing.

Hakea halmaturina is well adapted to low rainfall and is an important component of the local flora in its native habitat.
