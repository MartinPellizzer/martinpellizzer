Hebe chathamica

Hebe chathamica, commonly known as the Chatham Island hebe, is a flowering plant native to the Chatham Islands of New Zealand.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter, featuring dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

Hebe chathamica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its hardy, drought-resistant nature.
