Hieracium sabaudum subsp. curvidens

Hieracium sabaudum subsp.

curvidens, commonly known as the Sabaudian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. It has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, clustered flower heads.

This subspecies is notable for its distinctive leaf morphology and ecological adaptability.
