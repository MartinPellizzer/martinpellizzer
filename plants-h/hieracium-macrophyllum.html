Hieracium macrophyllum

Hieracium macrophyllum, commonly known as the giant hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often reddish in color.

The flowers bloom in late summer, producing yellowish-orange petals that attract various pollinators.
