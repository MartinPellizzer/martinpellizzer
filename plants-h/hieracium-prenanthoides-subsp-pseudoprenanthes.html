Hieracium prenanthoides subsp. pseudoprenanthes

Hieracium prenanthoides subsp.

pseudoprenanthes, commonly known as the false purple hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of tubular and ray florets.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It has alternate, basal leaves that are lanceolate to ovate in shape, often with hairy surfaces.

Its flowers are yellow, with a distinctive purple-tinged base, and it produces achene fruits with pappus for wind dispersal.
