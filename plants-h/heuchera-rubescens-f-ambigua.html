Heuchera rubescens f. ambigua

Heuchera rubescens f. ambigua, commonly known as the red-veined heuchera, is a cultivar of the heuchera plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking foliage, featuring deep green leaves with prominent red veins. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 18 inches and produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for shade gardens.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental leaves and is often used in ornamental horticulture.
