Hieracium maunuense

Hieracium maunuense, commonly known as Maunu's hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or riverbanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium maunuense is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
