Hocquartia tomentosa

Hocquartia tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly tamarind, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and long, pendulous clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces woody pods containing kidney-shaped seeds, which are used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Hocquartia tomentosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
