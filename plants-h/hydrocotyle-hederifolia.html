Hydrocotyle hederifolia

Hydrocotyle hederifolia, commonly known as the ivy-leaved water plantain, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetland and marshy environments in North America.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, white, clustered flowers in the summer. The plant thrives in moist soil and is often found along stream banks and in swampy areas. It has a fibrous root system and is known for its ability to tolerate fluctuating water levels.

Hydrocotyle hederifolia is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic insects.
