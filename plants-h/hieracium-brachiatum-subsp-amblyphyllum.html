Hieracium brachiatum subsp. amblyphyllum

Hieracium brachiatum subsp.

amblyphyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant has dark green, narrow leaves that are arranged in a rosette at the base, with a hairy texture. Its flowers are yellow, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

It is considered a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is often found in temperate climates.
