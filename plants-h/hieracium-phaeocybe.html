Hieracium phaeocybe

Hieracium phaeocybe, commonly known as the pale-centre hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a pale center.

Hieracium phaeocybe is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is considered a weed in some agricultural settings.
