Hibiscus salviifolius

Hibiscus salviifolius, commonly known as the scarlet hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from red to pink and yellow. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Hibiscus salviifolius thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas or near freshwater sources.

It is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
