Hypolaena fastigiata var. grandiuscula

Hypolaena fastigiata var. grandiuscula, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its large, showy red and orange inflorescences.

It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. The plant features a straight, slender stem with dense clusters of small, brightly colored flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly waxy texture.

This variant is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes due to its vibrant floral display.
