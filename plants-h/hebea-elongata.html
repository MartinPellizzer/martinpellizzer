Hebea elongata

Hebea elongata, commonly known as the slender hebe, is a species of flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its upright, slender growth habit and is typically found in coastal and subalpine regions. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, depending on the variety. Hebea elongata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its attractive foliage and blooms.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various garden settings.
