Himantoglossum hircinum var. affine

Himantoglossum hircinum var. affine, commonly known as the smooth meadow gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its flowering stems are erect and bear clusters of small, showy flowers.

This variety is often found in open, sunny habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
