Hibiscus pilosus

Hibiscus pilosus, commonly known as the silky hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its flowers bloom throughout the growing season, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hibiscus pilosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
