Himatanthus obovatus var. puberulus

Himatanthus obovatus var. puberulus, commonly known as the velvetleaf hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its flowers are typically followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
