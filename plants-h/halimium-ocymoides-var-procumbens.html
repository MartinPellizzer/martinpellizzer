Halimium ocymoides var. procumbens

Halimium ocymoides var. procumbens, commonly known as the prostrate halimium, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a dense, compact habit. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
