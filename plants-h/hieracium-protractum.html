Hieracium protractum

Hieracium protractum, commonly known as the long-stemmed hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disk.

Hieracium protractum is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in various habitats.
