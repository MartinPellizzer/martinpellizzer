Hieracium poecilophyllum

Hieracium poecilophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Hieracium poecilophyllum is often found in open habitats and is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and seeds.
