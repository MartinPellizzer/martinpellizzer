Hypericum villosum

Hypericum villosum, commonly known as hairy St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features opposite, hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellow-orange flowers with five petals. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Hypericum villosum is often found in moist to wet soils in woodland or swampy areas.
