Hypericum axillare

Hypericum axillare, commonly known as the lesser St. John's wort, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers with four petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and resilient plant suitable for coastal gardens and dry landscapes.
