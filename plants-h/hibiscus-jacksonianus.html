Hibiscus jacksonianus

Hibiscus jacksonianus, commonly known as the swamp hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks, thriving in moist, acidic soils. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture, while its stems are covered in fine hairs.

Hibiscus jacksonianus is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a common sight in wetland ecosystems.
