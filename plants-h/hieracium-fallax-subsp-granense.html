Hieracium fallax subsp. granense

Hieracium fallax subsp.

granense, commonly known as the granular hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering heads.

The plant produces yellowish to orange flowerheads that are typically surrounded by bracts. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Due to its ornamental appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
