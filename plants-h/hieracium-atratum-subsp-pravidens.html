Hieracium atratum subsp. pravidens

Hieracium atratum subsp.

pravidens, commonly known as the black hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement.

The plant features dark green, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, dark purple to black flowers in clustered inflorescences. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of propagation, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
