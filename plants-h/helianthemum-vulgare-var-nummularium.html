Helianthemum vulgare var. nummularium

Helianthemum vulgare var. nummularium, commonly known as the silver saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features dense clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are rounded, fleshy, and covered in a fine layer of white wax, giving the plant its characteristic silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
