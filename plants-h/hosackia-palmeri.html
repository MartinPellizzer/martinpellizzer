Hosackia palmeri

Hosackia palmeri, commonly known as Palmer's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces small, lens-shaped pods that contain several seeds. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert environments.

Hosackia palmeri plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitats.
