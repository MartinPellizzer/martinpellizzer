Hieracium flagellariforme subsp. callimorphopsis

Hieracium flagellariforme subsp.

callimorphopsis, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow flowers that bloom from June to August.

The plant has finely divided, grass-like leaves and produces numerous small, rayless flowers in composite heads. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils, and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
