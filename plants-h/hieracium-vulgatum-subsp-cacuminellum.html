Hieracium vulgatum subsp. cacuminellum

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

cacuminellum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.

The plant features basal leaves that are lanceolate and hairy, with flowering stems that bear yellow disk flowers surrounded by pale yellow to white ray florets. Its inflorescence is a composite head, characteristic of the Asteraceae family, and it produces small, dry achenes as fruits.

The plant is often found in open, sunny habitats and is known for its ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
