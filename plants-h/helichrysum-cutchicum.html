Helichrysum cutchicum

Helichrysum cutchicum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow to golden-orange flowers that are densely packed in clusters, giving it a bright, ornamental appearance. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, and the plant is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for dry, well-drained soils.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
