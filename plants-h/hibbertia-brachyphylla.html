Hibbertia brachyphylla

Hibbertia brachyphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hibbertia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, tubular yellow flowers and narrow, leathery leaves. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils. Hibbertia brachyphylla is a perennial shrub that produces its flowers during the spring and summer months.

Its unique floral structure and adaptability to various environmental conditions make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
