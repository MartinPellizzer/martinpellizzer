Helianthus incanus

Helianthus incanus, commonly known as the stiffhead sunflower, is a North American native plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its stiff, upright stems and hairy foliage, which help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces yellow composite flowers with numerous ray florets, typical of the sunflower genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Helianthus incanus is adapted to full sun and is relatively drought-tolerant, making it a hardy species in various ecological settings.
