Hibiscus indicus var. indicus

Hibiscus indicus var. indicus, commonly known as the Indian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to southern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge and thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Hibiscus indicus var. indicus prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
