Haworthia badia

Haworthia badia, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a succulent native to South Africa.

It features compact, rosette-shaped leaves that are typically pale green with distinctive white or pinkish markings. The plant is characterized by its ability to store water in its fleshy leaves, making it drought-tolerant. It produces small, white, star-shaped flowers on tall stems during the warmer months.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
