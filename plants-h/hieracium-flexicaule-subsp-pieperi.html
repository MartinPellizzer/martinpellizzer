Hieracium flexicaule subsp. pieperi

Hieracium flexicaule subsp.

pieperi, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring well-drained environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and somewhat hairy.

It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.
