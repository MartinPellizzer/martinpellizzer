Helichrysum wilmsii

Helichrysum wilmsii, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, yellow-orange flower heads that resemble small globes. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that bloom in summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

The plant's name comes from the Greek words "helios" meaning sun and "chrysos" meaning gold, reflecting its vibrant color.
