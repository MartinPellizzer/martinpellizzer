Humata pyxidata

Humata pyxidata, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its stems are woody and somewhat branched. Humata pyxidata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
