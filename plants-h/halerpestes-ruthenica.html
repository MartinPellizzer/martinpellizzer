Halerpestes ruthenica

Halerpestes ruthenica, commonly known as the Ruthenian halerpest, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems, which are often covered in a thick layer of bark. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by elliptical, leathery fruits. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture with a bluish-green hue.

Halerpestes ruthenica is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
