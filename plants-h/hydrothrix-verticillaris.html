Hydrothrix verticillaris

Hydrothrix verticillaris, commonly known as the spiral waterwheel plant, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its whorled arrangement of leaves.

It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and streams. The plant's leaves are typically spiral-shaped, which helps in maximizing light absorption and water flow efficiency. Its root system is adapted for anchoring in soft substrates while facilitating nutrient uptake.

Hydrothrix verticillaris plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
