Hieracium amplexicaule var. procerius

Hieracium amplexicaule var. procerius, commonly known as the tall yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant features a slender, upright stem that can grow up to 60 cm in height, with basal leaves that are broadly ovate and clasping the stem. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and are followed by elongated, bristly achenes.

It thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies.
