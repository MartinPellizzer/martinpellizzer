Hermione × radiata

Hermione × radiata, commonly known as the hybrid holly, is a deciduous shrub or small tree that exhibits characteristics of both its parent species, Hermione and Radiata.

It typically features glossy, dark green leaves that are oval in shape and often have a slight waxy coating. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by red or black berries in the fall. It is native to regions with temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soil with partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
