Hamamelis megalophylla

Hamamelis megalophylla, commonly known as the Chinese witch hazel, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late autumn. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth margin. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, often peeling in thin strips.

Hamamelis megalophylla is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine.
