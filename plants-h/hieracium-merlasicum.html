Hieracium merlasicum

Hieracium merlasicum, commonly known as the Merlasi hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. It produces yellow flowers with ray florets that resemble dandelions, and its leaves are usually hairy and lance-shaped.

Hieracium merlasicum is often found in open habitats and is sometimes considered an indicator species for certain ecological conditions.
