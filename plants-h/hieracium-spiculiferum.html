Hieracium spiculiferum

Hieracium spiculiferum, commonly known as the spiked hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium spiculiferum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
