Heliconia irrasa

Heliconia irrasa, commonly known as the red heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescences. The plant features large, sword-shaped leaves and clusters of tubular bracts that are typically bright red or orange. Heliconia irrasa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.

It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
