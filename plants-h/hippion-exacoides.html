Hippion exacoides

Hippion exacoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cactus, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of South America.

It features spiny stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are reduced to spines, minimizing water loss through transpiration.

Overall, Hippion exacoides is well adapted to harsh, desert-like conditions through its structural and physiological characteristics.
