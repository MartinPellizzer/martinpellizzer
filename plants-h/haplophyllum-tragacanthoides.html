Haplophyllum tragacanthoides

Haplophyllum tragacanthoides, commonly known as the desert milkweed, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.

Its latex sap is milky and can be used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
