Hibiscus convolvuliflorus

Hibiscus convolvuliflorus, commonly known as the creeping hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit, with slender stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs.

Hibiscus convolvuliflorus is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and vibrant blooms.
