Hypserpa selebica

Hypserpa selebica, commonly known as the Sunda orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Indonesian archipelago.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are elongated and lance-shaped, while its flowers are usually pale green or white with intricate patterns.

Hypserpa selebica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
