Hydrolea brevistyla

Hydrolea brevistyla, commonly known as the broad-leafed hydrolea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are often broad and thick, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale blue, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Hydrolea brevistyla is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in dry climates.
