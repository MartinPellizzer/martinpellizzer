Hypopitys secunda

Hypopitys secunda, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, succulent stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant has adapted to harsh environments through its ability to store water in its thick, fleshy leaves and stems. Hypopitys secunda often forms dense mats, helping to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in sandy or rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of desert ecosystems.
