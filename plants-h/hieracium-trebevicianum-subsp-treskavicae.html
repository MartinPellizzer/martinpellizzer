Hieracium trebevicianum subsp. treskavicae

Hieracium trebevicianum subsp.

treskavicae, commonly known as the Treskavica hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to reddish flowers and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and its flowering period occurs from late spring to early summer.

Due to its restricted distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its range.
