Hosta sieboldii f. spathulata

Hosta sieboldii f. spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leaf hosta, is a cultivar characterized by its large, broad, and slightly wavy leaves that are typically a deep green with a silvery-blue hue on the underside.

It produces upright, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a striking contrast to the foliage. The plant is native to Japan and is prized for its ornamental value in shaded gardens. It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland settings.

Its foliage remains attractive throughout the growing season, offering year-round visual interest.
