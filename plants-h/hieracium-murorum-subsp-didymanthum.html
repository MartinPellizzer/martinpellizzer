Hieracium murorum subsp. didymanthum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

didymanthum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has hairy stems and leaves that are often glandular, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some regions.
