Hieracium magyaricum subsp. viscidulum

Hieracium magyaricum subsp.

viscidulum, commonly known as the Hungarian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems.

The subspecies viscidulum is distinguished by its sticky, glandular surfaces on the leaves and stems, which give it a distinctive texture. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
