Hoodia pedicellata

Hoodia pedicellata, commonly known as the desert lily, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and small, white, star-shaped flowers. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to survive in harsh, dry environments. Hoodia pedicellata is also known for its ability to store water in its thick stems, making it well-adapted to drought conditions.

It is often found growing in rocky soils and is a hardy plant that thrives in full sun.
