Hyalolaena trichophylla

Hyalolaena trichophylla, commonly known as the silky sandmat, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and hairy, succulent stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
