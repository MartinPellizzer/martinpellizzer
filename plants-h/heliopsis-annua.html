Heliopsis annua

Heliopsis annua, commonly known as the annual sunflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and upright.

Heliopsis annua is often found in disturbed soils and is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
