Hebe × affinis

Hebe × affinis, commonly known as the New Zealand rock hebe, is a hybrid shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the plantain family (Plantaginaceae) and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant typically features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, or white, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

Hebe × affinis is well-suited for rock gardens and coastal landscapes due to its drought tolerance and hardy nature.
