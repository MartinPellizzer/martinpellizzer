Hieracium inuloides subsp. lanceolatifolium

Hieracium inuloides subsp.

lanceolatifolium, commonly known as the lanceolate hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement.

The plant features lance-shaped, serrated leaves that are typically hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, attracting various pollinators.

It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
