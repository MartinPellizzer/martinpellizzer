Hieracium mycelis

Hieracium mycelis, commonly known as the fairy ring grass, is a flowering plant in the sunflower family (Asteraceae).

It is characterized by its distinctive, small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as meadows and grasslands. It forms dense colonies and is often associated with the presence of mycorrhizal fungi in the soil.

Its botanical features make it an important component of certain ecological systems.
