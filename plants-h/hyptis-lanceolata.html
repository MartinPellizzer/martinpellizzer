Hyptis lanceolata

Hyptis lanceolata, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

Hyptis lanceolata thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in moist, shaded environments.
