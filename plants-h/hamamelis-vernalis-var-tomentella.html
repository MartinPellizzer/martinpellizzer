Hamamelis vernalis var. tomentella

Hamamelis vernalis var. tomentella, commonly known as the woolly witch hazel, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green foliage and yellow-orange fragrant flowers that bloom in late autumn. The plant's leaves are ovate with a serrated margin and often have a dense, woolly coating. Its flowers consist of four to six petals and are accompanied by four to six yellowish bracts.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its unique seasonal display.
