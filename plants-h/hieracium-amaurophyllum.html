Hieracium amaurophyllum

Hieracium amaurophyllum, commonly known as the black-leaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant features dark green, hairy leaves that are often glandular and can be somewhat toxic. Its flowers are typically yellow and are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

Hieracium amaurophyllum is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
