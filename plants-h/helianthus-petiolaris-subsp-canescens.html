Helianthus petiolaris subsp. canescens

Helianthus petiolaris subsp.

canescens, commonly known as the sandplain sunflower, is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which help it tolerate sandy and dry soils.

The plant produces yellow ray flowers surrounded by a series of brown, tubular disk flowers. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to coastal and disturbed habitats.
