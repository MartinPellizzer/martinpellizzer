Hedysarum pennigerum

Hedysarum pennigerum, commonly known as the Pennsylvania milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open woodlands and rocky soils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets. Hedysarum pennigerum is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its potential as a forage plant.

It is also sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive floral display.
