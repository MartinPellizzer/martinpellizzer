Hieracium gigantellum subsp. adenobrachion

Hieracium gigantellum subsp.

adenobrachion, commonly known as the giant buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has a robust root system and can grow up to 1 meter in height, making it a prominent feature in its habitat. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are often reddish in color.

This subspecies is often found in moist meadows and along stream banks, where it thrives in well-drained soil.
