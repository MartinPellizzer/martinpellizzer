Hydrangea jelskii

Hydrangea jelskii, commonly known as the Siberian hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to the cold climates of eastern Siberia.

It features large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from white to pink, depending on soil pH. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by persistent, cone-like fruits. Its foliage is typically dark green and remains attractive throughout the growing season.

Hydrangea jelskii is well adapted to harsh winters and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
