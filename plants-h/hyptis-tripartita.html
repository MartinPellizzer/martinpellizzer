Hyptis tripartita

Hyptis tripartita, commonly known as the three-part hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, tubular flowers. It is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forest understories and along stream banks.

Its leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
