Hieracium hispidulum

Hieracium hispidulum, commonly known as the hispid hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features upright, hairy stems and basal leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in summer, producing small seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Hieracium hispidulum is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
