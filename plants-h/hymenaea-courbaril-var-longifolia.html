Hymenaea courbaril var. longifolia

Hymenaea courbaril var. longifolia, commonly known as the yellow cedar or Brazilian wood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, light-colored bark and large, compound leaves. The tree produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain flat, winged seeds, facilitating wind dispersal. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic qualities, though it is also used in traditional medicine.

The plant exhibits a distinct growth pattern with a straight trunk and a broad, rounded crown.
