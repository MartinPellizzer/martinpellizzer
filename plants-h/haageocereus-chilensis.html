Haageocereus chilensis

Haageocereus chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean night blooming cactus, is a species native to Chile and is part of the Cactaceae family.

It is a columnar cactus that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, featuring a ribbed stem and spines. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It is drought-tolerant and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to its native habitat.

Its structure and growth patterns make it an interesting subject for both botanical study and horticultural cultivation.
