Hieracium monregalense subsp. monregalense

Hieracium monregalense subsp.

monregalense, commonly known as the Monregal hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in sunny, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has a fibrous root system and produces numerous short, upright stems.

Its flowers are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological role in its native environment.
