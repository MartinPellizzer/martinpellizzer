Hydrangea japonica var. benkaku

Hydrangea japonica var. benkaku, commonly known as the Japanese hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from white to pink, depending on soil pH. The plant produces clusters of small, sterile flowers surrounded by large, modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its foliage turns vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall, adding seasonal interest to the landscape.
