Helianthus couplandii

Helianthus couplandii, commonly known as Coupland's sunflower, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange ray florets and dark brown disk florets, typical of the sunflower genus. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils. It has a compact growth habit with a rosette of basal leaves and a single terminal flower head.

Coupland's sunflower is often found in rocky areas and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
