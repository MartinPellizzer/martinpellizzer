Heteropogon glaber var. messanensis

Heteropogon glaber var. messanensis, commonly known as the Messanese hairgrass, is a grass species native to southern Europe, particularly Italy.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils, often in wet meadows or along riverbanks. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet that is often hidden within the foliage, making it less conspicuous.

This variety is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
