Habenaria ouro-branquensis

Habenaria ouro-branquensis, commonly known as the golden-branched orchid, is a species native to Brazil.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique floral structure and ecological niche.
