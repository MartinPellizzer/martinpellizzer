Hibiscus cannabinus var. viridis

Hibiscus cannabinus var. viridis, commonly known as green hemp, is a variant of the common hibiscus species native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its dark green, broad leaves and showy, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant is a herbaceous annual that thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.

This variety is closely related to the Cannabis sativa plant, though it is not psychoactive.
