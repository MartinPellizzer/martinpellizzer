Hieracium meganargonense

Hieracium meganargonense, commonly known as the Andean hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are yellow, with a distinctive central disk surrounded by ray florets, and it produces small, oval-shaped seeds.

Hieracium meganargonense is adapted to harsh climates and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
