Hieracium tamderense

Hieracium tamderense, commonly known as the Tamderan hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium tamderense is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
