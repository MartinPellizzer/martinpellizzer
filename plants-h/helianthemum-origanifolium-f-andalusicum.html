Helianthemum origanifolium f. andalusicum

Helianthemum origanifolium f. andalusicum, commonly known as the Andalusian rock rose, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to southern Spain.

It features small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring and summer. The leaves are narrow, leathery, and have a strong mint-like aroma, resembling oregano. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand drought and poor soil conditions.
