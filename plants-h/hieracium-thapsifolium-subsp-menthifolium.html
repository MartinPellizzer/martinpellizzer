Hieracium thapsifolium subsp. menthifolium

Hieracium thapsifolium subsp.

menthifolium, commonly known as the mint-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellowish to orange composite flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and is often found in temperate regions.

The subspecies is distinguished by its aromatic, mint-scented leaves, which contribute to its common name.
