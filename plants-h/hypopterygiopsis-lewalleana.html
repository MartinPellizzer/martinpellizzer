Hypopterygiopsis lewalleana

Hypopterygiopsis lewalleana, commonly known as the Leuwalekan fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, feathery fronds that typically grow in dense clusters. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments such as mountainous forests, where it attaches itself to rocky substrates or decaying wood. Its reproductive structures, including small spore-producing sorus, are located on the underside of its leaves, a common feature among ferns.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
