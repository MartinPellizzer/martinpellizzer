Helichrysum bampsianum

Helichrysum bampsianum, commonly known as the Cape golden bush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that resemble golden stars. The plant is evergreen, with small, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Helichrysum bampsianum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
