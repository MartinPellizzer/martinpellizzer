Hugelia virgata var. dasyantha

Hugelia virgata var. dasyantha, commonly known as the woolly hugelia, is a perennial shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, soft appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments, such as forest undergrowth. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, contributing to its ornamental value.

It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by berry-like fruits that are edible for some bird species.
