Hemigenia drummondii

Hemigenia drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's honeybush, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It produces a unique, aromatic resin that has been used in traditional Aboriginal medicine.

Hemigenia drummondii thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in heathland and woodland habitats.
