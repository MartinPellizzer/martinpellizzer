Hohenbergia catingae

Hohenbergia catingae, commonly known as the Brazilian pineapple plant, is a species of bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color with a waxy coating. The plant produces striking inflorescences with bright red flowers that bloom in late summer. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often grown as a decorative plant in gardens and homes.
