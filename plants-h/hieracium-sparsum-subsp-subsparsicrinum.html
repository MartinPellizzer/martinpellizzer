Hieracium sparsum subsp. subsparsicrinum

Hieracium sparsum subsp.

subsparsicrinum, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowering heads and linear, hairy leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows. It has a creeping rootstock and produces numerous flowering stems that bear small, daisy-like flowers.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
