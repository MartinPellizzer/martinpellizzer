Hieracium incisum subsp. visocicense

Hieracium incisum subsp.

visocicense, commonly known as the Visocice White Hawk's Beard, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow ray florets and dark brown disk florets.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has finely divided, feathery leaves and produces small, dandelion-like seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
