Hieracium subcaesiiforme subsp. subcaesiiforme

Hieracium subcaesiiforme subsp.

subcaesiiforme, commonly known as the pale hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

The plant features upright stems that are usually hairy and can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are basal and lance-shaped, with a rough texture, and it produces yellow flowers in clusters.

This species is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
