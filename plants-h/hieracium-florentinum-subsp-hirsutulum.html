Hieracium florentinum subsp. hirsutulum

Hieracium florentinum subsp.

hirsutulum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.

The plant features bright yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, with hairy stems and leaves. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its common name.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
