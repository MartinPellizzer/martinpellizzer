Hyparrhenia gazensis

Hyparrhenia gazensis, commonly known as the African grass or Gazi grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and open woodlands.

Hyparrhenia gazensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
