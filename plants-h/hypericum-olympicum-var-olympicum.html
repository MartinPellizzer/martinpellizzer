Hypericum olympicum var. olympicum

Hypericum olympicum var. olympicum, commonly known as the Olympic goldcup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features upright, branching stems that are covered in dense, yellow-orange flowers resembling small cups. The plant produces bright yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine, soft hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.
