Hieracium urticaceum subsp. serrulifolium

Hieracium urticaceum subsp.

serrulifolium, commonly known as the serrated leaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters.

The plant features basal and alternate leaves that are serrated and lance-shaped, with a hairy surface. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and are characteristic of the Asteraceae family.

It has a fibrous root system and is often found in moist to moderately dry conditions.
