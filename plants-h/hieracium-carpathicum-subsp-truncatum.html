Hieracium carpathicum subsp. truncatum

Hieracium carpathicum subsp.

truncatum, commonly known as the Carpathian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Carpathian Mountains. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and flowering heads that produce yellow disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and forest edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a hairy surface, while the stems are upright and somewhat fibrous.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
