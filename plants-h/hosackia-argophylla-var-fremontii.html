Hosackia argophylla var. fremontii

Hosackia argophylla var. fremontii, commonly known as Fremont's false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has palmately divided leaves and is often found in dry, rocky soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in arid conditions.

This variety is part of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes known for nitrogen fixation.
