Hieracium plumulosiforme subsp. albopellitum

Hieracium plumulosiforme subsp.

albopellitum, commonly known as the white-haired hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm.

The plant is characterized by its hairy, often whitish stems and leaves, which are lance-shaped and covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers are yellow, with a distinctive central disc surrounded by ray florets, and it blooms from June to August.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its historical use in traditional medicine.
