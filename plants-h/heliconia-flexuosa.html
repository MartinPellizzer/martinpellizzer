Heliconia flexuosa

Heliconia flexuosa, commonly known as the flexuous heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its large, colorful bracts that resemble petals. The plant produces striking, spiral-shaped inflorescences that attract birds and insects. Its leaves are broad and often have a waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

Heliconia flexuosa is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and as a decorative plant.
