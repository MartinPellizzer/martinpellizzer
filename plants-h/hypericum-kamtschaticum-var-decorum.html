Hypericum kamtschaticum var. decorum

Hypericum kamtschaticum var. decorum, commonly known as the golden St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and upright growth habit. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing clusters of small, radiant flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and long blooming period.
