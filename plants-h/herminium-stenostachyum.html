Herminium stenostachyum

Herminium stenostachyum, commonly known as the slender spikerel.

It is a perennial orchid native to the eastern United States, characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and is adapted to thrive in acidic soils. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is known for its delicate, spicate inflorescence.

The species is relatively rare and is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological niche.
