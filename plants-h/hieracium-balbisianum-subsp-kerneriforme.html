Hieracium balbisianum subsp. kerneriforme

Hieracium balbisianum subsp.

kerneriforme, commonly known as the Kerner's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough texture, and the flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat, daisy-like head.

It is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.
