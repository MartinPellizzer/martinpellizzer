Helianthus thurifera

Helianthus thurifera, commonly known as the fragrant sunflower, is a species native to the southern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its large, yellow composite flowers and robust, hairy stems. The plant produces numerous ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Helianthus thurifera thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in open habitats such as prairies and roadsides.
