Horsfieldia oliviformis

Horsfieldia oliviformis, commonly known as the oval-shaped orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive elliptical-shaped pseudobulbs. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Horsfieldia oliviformis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens and private collections.
