Halimodendron emarginatum

Halimodendron emarginatum, commonly known as the desert broom, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a splash of color to its harsh environment. It is highly drought-tolerant and thrives in sandy or rocky soils.

Halimodendron emarginatum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
