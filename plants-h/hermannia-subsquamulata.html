Hermannia subsquamulata

Hermannia subsquamulata, commonly known as the silverleaf stembursh, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems covered in a dense layer of silvery, scale-like hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Hermannia subsquamulata is a perennial shrub that is adapted to dry and semi-arid conditions.
