Haplopappus

Haplopappus, commonly known as the smooth pappus plant, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

These plants are typically found in arid and semi-arid regions, exhibiting adaptations to harsh environmental conditions. They are characterized by their small, herbaceous stature and distinctive, smooth pappus structures that aid in seed dispersal. Haplopappus species often have narrow, linear leaves and produce yellow or white flowers.

The genus includes several species, some of which are used in traditional medicine or horticulture due to their ornamental value.
