Hieracium atratum subsp. dolichaetoides

Hieracium atratum subsp.

dolichaetoides, commonly known as the black hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its dark green, hairy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. It has a creeping rootstock that allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense colonies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
