Halenia erythraeoides

Halenia erythraeoides, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is sometimes cultivated for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
