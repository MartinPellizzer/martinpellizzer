Helichrysum oldfieldii

Helichrysum oldfieldii, commonly known as the Cape goldcup, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, sunflower-like blooms. The plant produces small, papery bracts that give it a distinctive appearance, often mistaken for petals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

The species is also noted for its aromatic foliage, which contains essential oils used in perfumery and traditional medicine.
