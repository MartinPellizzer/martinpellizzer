Haplopappus macronema

Haplopappus macronema, commonly known as the long-seeded hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellow.

The plant produces achenes with elongated pappi, which aid in wind dispersal of its seeds.
