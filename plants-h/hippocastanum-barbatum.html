Hippocastanum barbatum

Hippocastanum barbatum, commonly known as the Turkish horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the Balkans and Turkey.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces spiny, capsule-like fruits containing shiny, chestnut-like seeds. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, with a distinctive, flaky texture.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
