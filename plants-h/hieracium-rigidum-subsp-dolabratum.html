Hieracium rigidum subsp. dolabratum

Hieracium rigidum subsp.

dolabratum, commonly known as the rigid hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features upright, hairy stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, consisting of numerous small, daisy-like blooms.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and wind-pollinated flowers.
