Hieracium brachiatum subsp. dmitrovense

Hieracium brachiatum subsp.

dmitrovense, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Eastern Europe and Western Siberia. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

The plant features upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, acuminate leaves and is often found in low-nutrient environments.
