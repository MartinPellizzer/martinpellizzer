Hyacinthus pratensis

Hyacinthus pratensis, commonly known as the meadow hyacinth, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches and has narrow, grass-like leaves. Its bulbs are adapted for storing nutrients, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils.

The flowers come in a variety of colors, including blue, purple, pink, and white, making it a popular ornamental plant.
