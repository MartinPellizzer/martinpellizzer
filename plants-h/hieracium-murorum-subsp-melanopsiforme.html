Hieracium murorum subsp. melanopsiforme

Hieracium murorum subsp.

melanopsiforme, commonly known as the black-veined hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow flowers arranged in a composite head.

The leaves are typically lanceolate and covered with fine pubescence, while the flowers have dark veins that give the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

This subspecies is noted for its adaptability and is considered a common weed in various habitats across its range.
