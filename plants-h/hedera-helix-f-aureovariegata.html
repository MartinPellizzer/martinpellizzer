Hedera helix f. aureovariegata

Hedera helix f. aureovariegata, commonly known as the golden variegated ivy, is a popular ornamental plant characterized by its striking variegated foliage.

Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a mix of green, white, and gold colors, creating a visually appealing contrast. This cultivar is a form of the common ivy, which is native to Europe and parts of Asia. It thrives in indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.

The plant is known for its ability to climb or cascade, adding versatility to its use in landscaping and interior decor.
