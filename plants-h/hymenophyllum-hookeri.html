Hymenophyllum hookeri

Hymenophyllum hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's filmy fern, is a member of the Hymenophyllaceae family.

It is a small, delicate fern that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its fronds are narrow, elongated, and covered with a fine, silky layer of hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces spores on the underside of its fronds, which are protected by a specialized structure called a sorus.

Hymenophyllum hookeri is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in high humidity and partial shade.
