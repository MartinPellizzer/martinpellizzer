Haworthia inconfluens var. morrisiae

Haworthia inconfluens var. morrisiae, commonly known as the Morris's Haworthia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features compact rosettes of fleshy, triangular leaves that are typically bluish-green with white or pinkish markings. The leaves are covered in a fine, white powdery coating, known as farina, which helps reduce water loss. It produces small, white flowers on tall, slender stalks during the growing season.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
