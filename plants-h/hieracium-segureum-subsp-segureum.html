Hieracium segureum subsp. segureum

Hieracium segureum subsp.

segureum, commonly known as the Segureum hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.

The plant features basal rosettes of pale green, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its inflorescence consists of a single, elongated flower head with ray florets.

The species is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, and it is often found in open, sunny habitats.
