Hymenophyllum soriemersum

Hymenophyllum soriemersum, commonly known as the silver maidenhair fern, is a small, epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are often covered in a silvery, waxy coating. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on tree trunks or rocks in tropical rainforests. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
