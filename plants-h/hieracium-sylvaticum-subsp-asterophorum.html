Hieracium sylvaticum subsp. asterophorum

Hieracium sylvaticum subsp.

asterophorum, commonly known as the woodland hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in moist woodland habitats and is characterized by its upright stems and hairy leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, and its flowering heads contain both disc and ray florets.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smaller, more compact flower heads compared to other Hieracium species.
