Hibiscus divaricatus

Hibiscus divaricatus, commonly known as the spreading hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a distinctive yellow throat. The plant has palmate leaves that are typically lobed and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or near freshwater sources.

Hibiscus divaricatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
