Haplopappus uncinatus var. candollei

Haplopappus uncinatus var. candollei, commonly known as the slender pappus plant, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with gray-green, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its unique botanical features include a short, hairy stem and bract-like leaves that are often mistaken for true leaves.

This variety is adapted to thrive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.
