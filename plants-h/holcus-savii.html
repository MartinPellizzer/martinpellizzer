Holcus savii

Holcus savii, commonly known as the lesser quaking grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, marshes, and riverbanks. The plant features slender, slightly hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet that is often drooping, giving it a delicate appearance.

Holcus savii is known for its ability to thrive in wet conditions, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
