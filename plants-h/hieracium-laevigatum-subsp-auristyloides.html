Hieracium laevigatum subsp. auristyloides

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

auristyloides, commonly known as the auristyle hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. It features yellow flowers with a distinctive, elongated central stem and hairy foliage.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
