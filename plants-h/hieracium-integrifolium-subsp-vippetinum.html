Hieracium integrifolium subsp. vippetinum

Hieracium integrifolium subsp.

vippetinum, commonly known as the Vippetinum hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its basal rosette of broadly lanceolate, serrated leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and sometimes endangered species in certain regions.
