Heterotheca sessiliflora var. camphorata

Heterotheca sessiliflora var. camphorata, commonly known as camphorweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.

Camphorweed is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
