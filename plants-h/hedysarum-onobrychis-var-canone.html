Hedysarum onobrychis var. canone

Hedysarum onobrychis var. canone, commonly known as the Italian broom, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and along roadsides.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
