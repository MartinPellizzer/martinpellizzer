Hornstedtia conica

Hornstedtia conica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, erect stem and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces a single, showy, greenish-yellow flower with a distinctive hooded labellum. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a delicate and endangered species.
