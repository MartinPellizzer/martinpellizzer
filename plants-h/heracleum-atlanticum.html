Heracleum atlanticum

Heracleum atlanticum, commonly known as the Atlantic cowparsnip, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its tall, hollow stems and large, umbrella-shaped flowers.

It is often mistaken for poison hemlock due to its similar appearance, but it lacks the toxic compounds found in that species.

Heracleum atlanticum plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is an indicator plant for coastal ecosystems.
