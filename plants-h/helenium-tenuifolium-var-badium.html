Helenium tenuifolium var. badium

Helenium tenuifolium var. badium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved helenium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy inflorescences. The plant features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers range from yellow to orange-red, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The botanical structure of this variety makes it an attractive addition to native plant gardens and wetland restoration projects.
