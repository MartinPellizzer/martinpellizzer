Hosackia macraei

Hosackia macraei, commonly known as the large-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers that are typically pink to purple in color. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats.

Hosackia macraei plays an important role in nitrogen fixation and supports pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
