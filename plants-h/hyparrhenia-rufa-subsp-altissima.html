Hyparrhenia rufa subsp. altissima

Hyparrhenia rufa subsp.

altissima, commonly known as the tall red grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas. Hyparrhenia rufa subsp.

altissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil.
