Hieracium murorum subsp. pseudovalifolium

Hieracium murorum subsp.

pseudovalifolium, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to orange disc flowers surrounded by pale yellow to brown ray florets.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and waste areas, and can form dense mats. It has rough, hairy leaves that are usually lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

This subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different leaf morphology compared to other subspecies of Hieracium murorum.
