Helichrysum archimedeum

Helichrysum archimedeum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that remain open for a long period, giving it a lifelike appearance. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often grown as a ornamental plant for its vibrant color and drought tolerance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Helichrysum archimedeum is also valued for its use in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms.
