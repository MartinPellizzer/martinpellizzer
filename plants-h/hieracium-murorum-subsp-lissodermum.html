Hieracium murorum subsp. lissodermum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

lissodermum, commonly known as the smooth-leaved common hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and waste areas, and has a low-growing, spreading habit. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is an herbaceous perennial that produces seeds through achenes, which are dispersed by wind.
