Hieracium × czetzianum

Hieracium × czetzianum, commonly known as the Czetkowsky's hawkweed, is a hybrid species within the genus Hieracium, which belongs to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellowish to orange-colored composite flowers, which are typically arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. The plant is perennial and typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are generally lance-shaped and have a hairy texture, while its stems are often reddish in color.

This species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive floral display.
