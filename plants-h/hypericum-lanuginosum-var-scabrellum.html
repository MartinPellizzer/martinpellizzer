Hypericum lanuginosum var. scabrellum

Hypericum lanuginosum var. scabrellum, commonly known as the rough-stemmed St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features small, yellow, four-petaled flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, often reddish stems and leaves that are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Hypericaceae family.
