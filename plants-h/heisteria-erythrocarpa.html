Heisteria erythrocarpa

Heisteria erythrocarpa, commonly known as the red-fruited heisteria, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Violaceae family, characterized by its showy, violet-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth, glossy surface.

The fruit is a distinctive red, fleshy capsule that splits open to release its seeds.
