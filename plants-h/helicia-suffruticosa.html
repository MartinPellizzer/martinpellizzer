Helicia suffruticosa

Helicia suffruticosa, commonly known as the yellow-fruit holly.

It is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and small, yellowish-orange fruits. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in lowland forests. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, and it produces clusters of fleshy, orange berries.

Helicia suffruticosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
