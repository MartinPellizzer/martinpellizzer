Haplopappus spinulosus subsp. laevis

Haplopappus spinulosus subsp.

laevis, commonly known as the smooth hairyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are small and yellow.

The subspecies laevis is distinguished by its smoother leaf surfaces compared to other subspecies of Haplopappus spinulosus.
