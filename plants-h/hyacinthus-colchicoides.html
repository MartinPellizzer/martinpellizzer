Hyacinthus colchicoides

Hyacinthus colchicoides, commonly known as the Spanish hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It produces clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers in early spring. The plant has strap-shaped leaves that are often mistaken for grass due to their appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and ease of cultivation.
