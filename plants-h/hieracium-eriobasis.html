Hieracium eriobasis

Hieracium eriobasis, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are broadly lance-shaped with a rough texture, and it produces yellow flowers from June to August.

Hieracium eriobasis is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in various habitats.
