Hilliardiella hirsuta

Hilliardiella hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy hilliardiella, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that helps it retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a subtle ornamental value. Its succulent leaves are adapted for water storage, making it drought-tolerant.

Hilliardiella hirsuta is often found in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in full sun conditions.
