Heliopsis helianthoides var. minor

Heliopsis helianthoides var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf false sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb with upright, branched stems that produce bright yellow, daisy-like flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and wetlands.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for wetland restoration and native garden landscapes.
