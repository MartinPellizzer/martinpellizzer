Hippeastrum espiritense

Hippeastrum espiritense, commonly known as the Espiritense amaryllis, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean island of Hispaniola.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 cm in diameter, typically in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces strap-like, linear leaves that are broad at the base and taper towards the tip. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Hippeastrum espiritense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
