Haplopappus lehmannii var. subcalvus

Haplopappus lehmannii var. subcalvus, commonly known as the spiny pappus, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and bract-like structures. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and has a low, spreading growth habit. Its leaves are usually linear and covered with fine hairs, while the flowers are small and yellow.

The variety subcalvus is distinguished by its reduced hair coverage compared to other varieties of Haplopappus lehmannii.
