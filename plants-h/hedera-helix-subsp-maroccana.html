Hedera helix subsp. maroccana

Hedera helix subsp.

maroccana, commonly known as Moroccan ivy, is a climbing evergreen vine native to North Africa. It features dark green, glossy leaves that are typically heart-shaped with a pointed tip.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation.
