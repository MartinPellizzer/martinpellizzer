Haemanthus coccineus

Haemanthus coccineus, commonly known as the blood lily, is a striking South African plant characterized by its bright red, tubular flowers.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is native to regions such as KwaZulu-Natal and the Eastern Cape. The plant features a bulbous stem that stores water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. Its leaves are strap-shaped and evergreen, providing a lush green backdrop to the vibrant red blooms.

Haemanthus coccineus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
