Hymenophyllum meyenianum

Hymenophyllum meyenianum, commonly known as the silver moss, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, wiry, and often silver-tinged fronds, which are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. This epiphytic plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or rocks in tropical and subtropical regions. Its intricate, lace-like structure and small, spore-producing structures called sporangia are typical of the Hymenophyllaceae family.

Hymenophyllum meyenianum plays a role in forest ecosystems by contributing to soil stability and providing microhabitats for various organisms.
