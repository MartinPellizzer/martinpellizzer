Hyptis calycina

Hyptis calycina, commonly known as Mexican blue sage, is a herbaceous perennial native to Central America and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and culinary uses. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a strong, minty fragrance. Its flowers are typically blue to purple, arranged in dense clusters, and bloom during the summer months.

Hyptis calycina is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional use in herbal remedies.
