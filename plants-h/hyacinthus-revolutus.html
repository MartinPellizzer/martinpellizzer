Hyacinthus revolutus

Hyacinthus revolutus, commonly known as the revolute hyacinth, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of blue, violet, and white, typically blooming in spring. Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that are narrow and grass-like in appearance.

Hyacinthus revolutus is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and as a cut flower.
