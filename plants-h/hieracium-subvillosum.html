Hieracium subvillosum

Hieracium subvillosum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, with numerous small florets surrounded by bracts. Its leaves are typically hairy and alternate, often with a rough texture.

Hieracium subvillosum thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.
