Hygrophila salicifolia

Hygrophila salicifolia, commonly known as water willow, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It thrives in moist or aquatic environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, attracting various pollinators.

Hygrophila salicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to help stabilize soil in wetland areas.
