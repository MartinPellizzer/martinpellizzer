Hyoseris incrassata

Hyoseris incrassata, commonly known as the broadleaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typical sunflower-like appearance. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Hyoseris incrassata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in native plant landscaping.
