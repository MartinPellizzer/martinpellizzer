Hermannia collina

Hermannia collina, commonly known as the rock melon or rock fig, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal or mountainous regions. The plant produces small, fleshy, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.

Hermannia collina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
