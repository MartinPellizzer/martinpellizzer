Horminum suaveolens

Horminum suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented orchid, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
