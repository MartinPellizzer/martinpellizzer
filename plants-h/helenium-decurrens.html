Helenium decurrens

Helenium decurrens, commonly known as the running sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy, orange to yellow ray florets. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in full sun, often found in wetlands and along stream banks. Its stems are hairy and sprawling, with lance-shaped leaves that are also covered in fine hairs.

Helenium decurrens is valued for its ornamental appeal and serves as a food source for various pollinators.
