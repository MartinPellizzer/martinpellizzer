Herniaria empedocleana

Herniaria empedocleana, commonly known as the rock scurfpea, is a small, mat-forming plant that thrives in rocky and arid environments.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with small, rounded leaves that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating. The plant produces tiny, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by small, bladder-like fruits that contain the seeds. It is adapted to withstand harsh conditions, including high temperatures and poor soil nutrients.

Herniaria empedocleana is often found in Mediterranean regions and is considered a member of the Caryophyllaceae family.
