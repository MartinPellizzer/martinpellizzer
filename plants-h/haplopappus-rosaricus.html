Haplopappus rosaricus

Haplopappus rosaricus, commonly known as the Rosary weed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring.

Haplopappus rosaricus is considered a weed in some regions due to its rapid growth and ability to spread aggressively.
