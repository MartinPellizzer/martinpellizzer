Heliotropium curassavicum var. oculatum

Heliotropium curassavicum var. oculatum, commonly known as the eye-leaf heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, violet-blue flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a subtle white or pinkish hue. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a distinctive pattern of dark green veins, giving them a unique appearance. This species thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

It is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its traditional uses in herbal medicine.
