Haloragis exalata var. laevis

Haloragis exalata var. laevis, commonly known as the smooth willowherb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in marshes, stream banks, and other aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats. The plant features slender, branching stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually smooth and glabrous. Its small, clustered flowers are typically pink or white, and they bloom in late summer.

The species is characterized by its ability to thrive in waterlogged conditions, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
