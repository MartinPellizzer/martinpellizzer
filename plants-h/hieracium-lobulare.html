Hieracium lobulare

Hieracium lobulare, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features hairy, lobed leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and roadside areas.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a nectar source for various pollinators.
