Hypericum styphelioides

Hypericum styphelioides, commonly known as the slender St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, characterized by its small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It is part of the Hypericaceae family, known for its medicinal properties in some species.

Hypericum styphelioides is often used in local horticulture for its ornamental value and resilience to coastal conditions.
