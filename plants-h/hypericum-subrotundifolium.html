Hypericum subrotundifolium

Hypericum subrotundifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are characterized by their narrow, rounded shape. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. It is part of the Hypericaceae family, known for its medicinal properties.

The plant is also noted for its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
