Homalomena multivenosa

Homalomena multivenosa, commonly known as the veined arrowroot or swamp arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves and striking, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near water bodies. Its rhizomes are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines.

The botanical features of Homalomena multivenosa make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
