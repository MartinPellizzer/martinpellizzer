Hippeastrum crocus

Hippeastrum crocus, commonly known as the crocus amaryllis, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It produces large, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. The plant features broad, strap-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous underground stem. Its flowers are composed of six petals and three sepals, forming a trumpet-shaped bloom.

Hippeastrum crocus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
