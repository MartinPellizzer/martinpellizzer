Haplopappus spinulosus var. gooddingii

Haplopappus spinulosus var. gooddingii, commonly known as the Goodding's hairy goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf structures. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as sandy soils and open plains. Its leaves are usually hairy and lance-shaped, while the flower heads consist of yellow disk flowers surrounded by pale green to white ray flowers.

The species is known for its adaptability to harsh conditions, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystems.
