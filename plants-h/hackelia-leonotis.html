Hackelia leonotis

Hackelia leonotis, commonly known as the flame flower or redstem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking red to pink flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems, typically in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its showy inflorescences attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
