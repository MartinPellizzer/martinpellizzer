Hugelia virgata var. floccosa

Hugelia virgata var. floccosa, commonly known as the woolly hugelia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, soft appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to form dense ground cover.
