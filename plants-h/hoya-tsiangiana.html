Hoya tsiangiana

Hoya tsiangiana, commonly known as the "Wax Plant" or "Floral Crown," is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with cream, white, and green patterns. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, emitting a subtle fragrance. Hoya tsiangiana is a climbing or trailing plant that thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.

It is popular as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
