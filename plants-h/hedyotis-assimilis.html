Hedyotis assimilis

Hedyotis assimilis, commonly known as the creeping smilax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels. Its fruits are red, fleshy berries that contain several seeds.

Hedyotis assimilis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
