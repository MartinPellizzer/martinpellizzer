Helia fistulosa

Helia fistulosa, commonly known as the tube flower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its distinctive tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments.

The flowers bloom in summer and are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.
