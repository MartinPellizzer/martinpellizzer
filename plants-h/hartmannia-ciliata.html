Hartmannia ciliata

Hartmannia ciliata, commonly known as the velvetleaf orchid, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows up to 30 cm in height, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. Its flowers, which bloom in clusters, are usually pink or purple and have a distinctive, elongated spur.

Hartmannia ciliata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in moist forest understories.
