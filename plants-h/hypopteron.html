Hypopteron

Hypopteron, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are characterized by their unique, often intricate floral structures, which include a lip that resembles a spider's body. Hypopteron species are typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, often growing in shaded, moist environments. Their flowers are usually small to medium in size and display a range of colors, including white, yellow, and green.

The genus is notable for its specialized pollination mechanisms, often involving specific insect pollinators.
