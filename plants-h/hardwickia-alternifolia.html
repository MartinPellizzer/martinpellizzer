Hardwickia alternifolia

Hardwickia alternifolia, commonly known as the African wattle, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are alternate and composed of small, lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow or orange spherical pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Hardwickia alternifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
