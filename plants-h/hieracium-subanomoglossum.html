Hieracium subanomoglossum

Hieracium subanomoglossum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and disturbed soils, exhibiting a low-growing habit with hairy stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
