Helichrysum lasianthum

Helichrysum lasianthum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, yellowish-orange flowers. The plant produces fluffy, seed-filled structures that are often used in floral arrangements due to their long-lasting nature. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its botanical features include narrow, gray-green leaves and a distinctive, branched inflorescence.
