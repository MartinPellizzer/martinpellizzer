Hibiscus lunariifolius

Hibiscus lunariifolius, commonly known as the moonleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Hibiscus lunariifolius is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
