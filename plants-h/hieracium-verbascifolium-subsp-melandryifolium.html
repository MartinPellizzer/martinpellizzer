Hieracium verbascifolium subsp. melandryifolium

Hieracium verbascifolium subsp.

melandryifolium, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

It is noted for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in areas with moderate moisture levels.
