Hieracium tericum

Hieracium tericum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, hairy leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers with ray florets. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas. It has a taproot system and is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils.

Hieracium tericum is also a host plant for several species of Lepidoptera, contributing to local biodiversity.
