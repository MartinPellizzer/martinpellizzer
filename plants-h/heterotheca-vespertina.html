Heterotheca vespertina

Heterotheca vespertina, commonly known as evening coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the central and southern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in prairie and open woodland habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its showy yellow-orange flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Heterotheca vespertina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
