Hakea smilacifolia

Hakea smilacifolia, commonly known as the smilacifolia hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, needle-like leaves that are typically green and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces striking, bottlebrush-like inflorescences that are usually pink to red in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, coastal environments.

Hakea smilacifolia is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
