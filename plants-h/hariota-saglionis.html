Hariota saglionis

Hariota saglionis, commonly known as the golden spire, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence composed of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant has adapted to its harsh environment with deep root systems and succulent leaves that store water. It blooms during the rainy season, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hariota saglionis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
