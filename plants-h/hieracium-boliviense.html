Hieracium boliviense

Hieracium boliviense, commonly known as the Bolivian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America, particularly Bolivia and Argentina.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate to subtropical climates. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with ray florets that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hieracium boliviense is known for its hardy nature and ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
