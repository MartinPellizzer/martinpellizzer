Hieracium sabaudum var. maximum

Hieracium sabaudum var. maximum, commonly known as the giant hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and can grow up to 20 centimeters in length. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming from June to August. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

Due to its aggressive growth, it is considered a noxious weed in many regions.
