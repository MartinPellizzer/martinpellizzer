Hoya gigantanganensis

Hoya gigantanganensis, commonly known as the giant tanangan hoya, is a rare and striking member of the Asclepiadaceae family.

It is native to the Philippines and is characterized by its large, waxy, ovate leaves that can grow up to 20 cm in length. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in summer. Hoya gigantanganensis is a climbing plant that thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is highly sought after by plant enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
