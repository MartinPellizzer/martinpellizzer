Hyptis interrupta

Hyptis interrupta, commonly known as Brazilian hyptis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its silver-gray leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

Hyptis interrupta prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
