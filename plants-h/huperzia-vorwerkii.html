Huperzia vorwerkii

Huperzia vorwerkii, commonly known as the Vorwerk's clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its dense, compact foliage and upright, branching stems. Its leaves are linear and arranged in whorls around the stem, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Huperzia vorwerkii is notable for its ability to thrive in humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
