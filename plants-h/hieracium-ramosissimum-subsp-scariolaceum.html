Hieracium ramosissimum subsp. scariolaceum

Hieracium ramosissimum subsp.

scariolaceum, commonly known as the scarlet hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish to orange-red flowers.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a rough texture. It thrives in sunny, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

The plant is part of the daisy family (Asteraceae) and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
