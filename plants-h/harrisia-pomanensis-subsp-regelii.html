Harrisia pomanensis subsp. regelii

Harrisia pomanensis subsp.

regelii, commonly known as the Regel's nightshade cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil and Paraguay. It is a columnar cactus with a dense covering of spines and produces vibrant, showy flowers that bloom at night.

The plant has a succulent stem that stores water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink and emit a strong fragrance to attract nocturnal pollinators.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-draining soil and full sun exposure.
