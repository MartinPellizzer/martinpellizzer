Helianthus × atrorubens

Helianthus × atrorubens, commonly known as the black-eyed Susan, is a hybrid sunflower species native to the eastern and central United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow petals surrounding a dark, velvety center that resembles eyes. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and serrated. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in prairies, roadsides, and disturbed areas.

The flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
