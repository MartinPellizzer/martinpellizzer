Heliconia lasiorachis

Heliconia lasiorachis, commonly known as the silver-haired heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble petals. The plant produces long, segmented inflorescences with small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bats. Its leaves are large, broad, and often have a waxy texture, which helps in water retention in humid environments.

The silver-haired heliconia is also valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture and is sometimes cultivated in warm climates.
