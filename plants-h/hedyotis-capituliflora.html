Hedyotis capituliflora

Hedyotis capituliflora, commonly known as the small-flowered dotweed, is a perennial herb native to Australia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and understoreys. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale green, and it has slender, creeping stems. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and often have a waxy coating.
