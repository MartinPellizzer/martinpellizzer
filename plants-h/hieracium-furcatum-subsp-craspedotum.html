Hieracium furcatum subsp. craspedotum

Hieracium furcatum subsp.

craspedotum, commonly known as the forked hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically features finely divided, linear leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
