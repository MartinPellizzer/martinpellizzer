Hadrolaelia × calimaniorum

Hadrolaelia × calimaniorum, commonly known as the Calimaniorum hybrid, is a hybrid orchid resulting from the cross between Hadrolaelia tatei and Hadrolaelia calimania.

It is native to the mountainous regions of northern South America, particularly in Ecuador and Colombia. This plant typically grows at high elevations, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions with well-drained soil. Its flowers are often violet or purple, with a distinctive spurred lip that is a key feature of the genus.

The hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
