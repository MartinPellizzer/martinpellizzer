Helianthus oaxacanus

Helianthus oaxacanus, commonly known as the Oaxacan sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow ray flowers and dark green, hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to warm, arid climates. It produces small, spiky seed heads that are dispersed by wind.

Helianthus oaxacanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
