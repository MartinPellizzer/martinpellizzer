Hieracium atratum subsp. semiapertum

Hieracium atratum subsp.

semiapertum, commonly known as the dark hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm.

The plant features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often reddish in color. Its flowers are yellow, with a distinctive central dark area, and are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

The subspecies is characterized by its semi-open flower heads, which give it its specific epithet "semiapertum.".
