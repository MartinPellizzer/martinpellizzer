Hyacinthus commutatus

Hyacinthus commutatus, commonly known as the Dutch hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, dense clusters of flowers that bloom in shades of violet, blue, and white. The plant produces strap-shaped leaves and has a short, stout stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Hyacinthus commutatus is also valued for its historical significance in horticulture and its role in early spring gardening.
