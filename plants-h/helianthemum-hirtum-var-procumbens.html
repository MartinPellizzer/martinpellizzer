Helianthemum hirtum var. procumbens

Helianthemum hirtum var. procumbens, commonly known as the creeping stonecrop, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are hairy and covered in fine white pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
