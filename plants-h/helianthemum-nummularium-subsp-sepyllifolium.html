Helianthemum nummularium subsp. sepyllifolium

Helianthemum nummularium subsp.

sepyllifolium, commonly known as the silverleaf sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to southern Europe. It features small, round, silver-green leaves that are densely packed, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions.
