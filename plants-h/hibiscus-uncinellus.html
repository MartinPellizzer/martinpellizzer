Hibiscus uncinellus

Hibiscus uncinellus, commonly known as the hooked hibiscus, is a species native to parts of Africa, particularly South Africa.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family, characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with hairy, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in summer and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hibiscus uncinellus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
