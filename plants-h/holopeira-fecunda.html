Holopeira fecunda

Holopeira fecunda, commonly known as the sacred lotus of the Andes, is a striking aquatic plant native to the high-altitude lakes of South America.

It features large, showy flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white, often with a distinctive yellow center. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, providing habitat for various aquatic life. Its roots anchor it in the muddy lake beds, while its stems extend upward to support the flowers.

Holopeira fecunda is valued not only for its beauty but also for its cultural and spiritual significance in Andean traditions.
