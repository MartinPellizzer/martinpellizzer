Hieracium sapiehae

Hieracium sapiehae, commonly known as the Polish hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellowish to reddish flowers and hairy, often lobed leaves. Native to Europe, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats such as meadows and roadsides. The plant has a taproot system and produces numerous small seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and botanical gardens.
