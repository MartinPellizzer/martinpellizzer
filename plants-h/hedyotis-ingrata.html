Hedyotis ingrata

Hedyotis ingrata, commonly known as the slender dotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, with four petals. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Hedyotis ingrata is valued in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
