Heliotropium parviantrum

Heliotropium parviantrum, commonly known as the small-veined heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves. The flowers are typically small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and often have a violet or purple coloration. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This species is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
