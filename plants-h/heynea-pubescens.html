Heynea pubescens

Heynea pubescens, commonly known as the African mahogany, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elongated leaves with a slightly hairy underside. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, elliptical fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It is valued for its durable, aromatic wood, which is used in furniture and construction.

The plant also plays an important role in local ecosystems and traditional medicine.
