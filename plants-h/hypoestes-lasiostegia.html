Hypoestes lasiostegia

Hypoestes lasiostegia, commonly known as the "Magic Blue" or "Blue Mist," is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking blue and white tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant has succulent-like leaves that are fleshy and somewhat hairy, adapted to retain moisture in its native arid environment. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Hypoestes lasiostegia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant colors and unique appearance.
