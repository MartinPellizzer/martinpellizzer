Handroanthus umbellatus

Handroanthus umbellatus, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its showy, yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters at the ends of its branches. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
