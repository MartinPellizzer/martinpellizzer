Hibiscus vitifolius subsp. lukei

Hibiscus vitifolius subsp.

lukei, commonly known as the golden hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia. It features striking yellow flowers with a prominent central pistil and five broad, wavy petals.

The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot. Hibiscus vitifolius subsp.

lukei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
