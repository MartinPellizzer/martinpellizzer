Hoita hirsuta

Hoita hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy hoya, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asclepiadaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and waxy, hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. Hoita hirsuta is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

It is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
