Hieracium ovaligerum

Hieracium ovaligerum, commonly known as the oval-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are oval-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium ovaligerum is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural and natural settings.
