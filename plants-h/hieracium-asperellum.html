Hieracium asperellum

Hieracium asperellum, commonly known as rough hawksbeard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are rough and hairy, and the flowers are yellow with ray florets.

It is a perennial herb that produces achenes as its seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
