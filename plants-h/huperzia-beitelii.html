Huperzia beitelii

Huperzia beitelii, commonly known as the Beitel's clubmoss, is a species of lycophyte that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its simple, needle-like leaves and a distinctive, compact growth habit. Native to tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant's spore-producing structures, called strobili, are typically found at the tips of its branching stems.

Huperzia beitelii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in controlled indoor conditions.
