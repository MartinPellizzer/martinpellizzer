Hypericum hyssopifolium var. elongatum

Hypericum hyssopifolium var. elongatum, commonly known as the elongated St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along its stems. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its botanical characteristics include hairy stems and small, elliptical leaves with a slightly serrated margin.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
