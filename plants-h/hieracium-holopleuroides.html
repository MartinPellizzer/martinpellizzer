Hieracium holopleuroides

Hieracium holopleuroides, commonly known as the broad-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy foliage. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, exhibiting a rosette growth pattern with broad, lance-shaped leaves. Its yellow flowers bloom in summer, and it produces numerous seeds dispersed by wind.

Hieracium holopleuroides is known for its adaptability and can sometimes become invasive in non-native regions.
