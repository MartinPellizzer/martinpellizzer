Hamelia magnifolia

Hamelia magnifolia, commonly known as the flamebeard or red flamebush, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces showy, tubular red flowers that attract pollinators like hummingbirds and butterflies. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Hamelia magnifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of garden settings.
