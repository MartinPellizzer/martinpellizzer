Hypericum brathys

Hypericum brathys, commonly known as the narrow-leaved St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in sunny, well-drained soils and is characterized by its slender stems and small, yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces yellow flowers with four petals, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, with a slightly hairy texture.

Hypericum brathys is often found in rocky or sandy habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical medicinal uses.
