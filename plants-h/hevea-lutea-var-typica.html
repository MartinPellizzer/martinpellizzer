Hevea lutea var. typica

Hevea lutea var. typica, commonly known as the yellow rubber tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale yellow bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family and is known for its latex-producing capabilities. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its economic value in rubber production.
