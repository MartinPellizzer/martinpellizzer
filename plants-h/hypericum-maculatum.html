Hypericum maculatum

Hypericum maculatum, commonly known as the spotted St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to eastern North America.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish or purplish at the base, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its medicinal properties have been studied for their potential anti-inflammatory and antidepressant effects.

Hypericum maculatum thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.
