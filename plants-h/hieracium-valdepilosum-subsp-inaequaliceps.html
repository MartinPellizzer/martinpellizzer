Hieracium valdepilosum subsp. inaequaliceps

Hieracium valdepilosum subsp.

inaequaliceps, commonly known as the unequal-headed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy basal rosettes.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually lanceolate and covered with glandular hairs, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head.

This subspecies is distinguished by its unique floral morphology and habitat preferences, making it a notable species within the Hieracium genus.
