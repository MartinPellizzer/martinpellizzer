Hieracium vulgatum subsp. friesii

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

friesii, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering stems that bear yellow to orange composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. It has hairy stems and leaves, and its flowers are arranged in a compact, flat-topped inflorescence.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly smaller leaves and more compact growth form compared to other members of the species.
