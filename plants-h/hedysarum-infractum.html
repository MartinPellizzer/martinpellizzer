Hedysarum infractum

Hedysarum infractum, commonly known as the narrowleaf sweetclover, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the western United States, particularly in regions with dry, rocky soils. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow to reddish-purple flowers in clusters. Its root system has the ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

Hedysarum infractum is often found in open woodlands, grasslands, and along streambanks.
