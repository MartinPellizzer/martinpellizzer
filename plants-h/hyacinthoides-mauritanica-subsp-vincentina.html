Hyacinthoides mauritanica subsp. vincentina

Hyacinthoides mauritanica subsp.

vincentina, commonly known as the Vincent's bluebell, is a rare and delicate species of flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant thrives in shaded, moist woodland environments and is often found in calcareous soils. Its flowers emit a subtle fragrance and are pollinated primarily by insects.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
