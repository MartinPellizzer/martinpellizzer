Hypericum aemulans

Hypericum aemulans, commonly known as the false St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of its branches. The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological gardens.
