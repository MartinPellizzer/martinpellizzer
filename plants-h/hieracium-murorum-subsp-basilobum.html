Hieracium murorum subsp. basilobum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

basilobum, commonly known as the creeping hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with numerous flowering stems.

The plant produces yellowish to orange flower heads composed of many small disc florets. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.
