Hirculus unguiculatus

Hirculus unguiculatus, commonly known as the horned toad plant, is a unique succulent native to arid regions of South America.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry environments. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically during the spring season. Its root system is shallow but extensive, enabling it to absorb moisture from the soil efficiently.

Despite its common name, it is not related to toads and is valued for its ornamental beauty and drought tolerance.
