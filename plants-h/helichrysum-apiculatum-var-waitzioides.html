Helichrysum apiculatum var. waitzioides

Helichrysum apiculatum var. waitzioides, commonly known as the golden crown bush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant features silver-gray foliage with a fuzzy texture, enhancing its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

The flowers are long-lasting and often used in dried flower arrangements due to their ability to retain color and form.
