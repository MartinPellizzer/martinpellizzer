Helenium quadridentatum

Helenium quadridentatum, commonly known as the Mexican sneezeweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces vibrant orange to red ray flowers surrounded by yellow disk flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Helenium quadridentatum typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom in late summer and early fall.
