Helichrysum brunioides subsp. brunioides

Helichrysum brunioides subsp.

brunioides, commonly known as the Cape goldcup, is a perennial herb native to South Africa. It features wiry, branched stems and small, golden-yellow flowers that resemble miniature sunflowers.

The plant has needle-like leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its drought tolerance and attractive foliage.
