Heterotheca villosa var. pedunculata

Heterotheca villosa var. pedunculata, commonly known as the hairy goldenweed, is a perennial herb native to the central and western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its showy yellow flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in the summer months.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some medicinal uses in traditional herbal practices.
