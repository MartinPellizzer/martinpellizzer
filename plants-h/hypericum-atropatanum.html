Hypericum atropatanum

Hypericum atropatanum, commonly known as the black-stemmed St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
