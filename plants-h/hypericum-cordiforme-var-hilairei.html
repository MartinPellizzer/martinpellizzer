Hypericum cordiforme var. hilairei

Hypericum cordiforme var. hilairei, commonly known as the Hilaire's golden-stemmed St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine white hairs, giving them a silvery appearance. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its stems are woody and often have a reddish hue, while its growth habit is compact and spreading.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
