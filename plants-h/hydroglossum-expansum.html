Hydroglossum expansum

Hydroglossum expansum, commonly known as the broadleaf water-dwelling plant, is a species of aquatic plant characterized by its broad, flat leaves that float on the water's surface.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving streams. The plant has a simple, branching structure with small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged or just at the water's edge. Its leaves are usually green and can vary in size depending on water conditions.

Hydroglossum expansum plays a role in providing habitat for small aquatic organisms and aiding in water filtration.
