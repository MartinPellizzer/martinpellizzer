Hordeum pampeanum

Hordeum pampeanum, commonly known as the Pampas barley, is a species of grass native to South America, particularly Argentina, Uruguay, and Brazil.

It is a perennial plant that thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and produces spikelets that contain small, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and have a bluish-green color, and it is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Hordeum pampeanum plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitats.
