Hedwigia hostmannii

Hedwigia hostmannii, commonly known as the desert feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and fine, needle-like leaves that form dense, tufted clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in sandy or rocky soils.

Hedwigia hostmannii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing desert soils and providing habitat for small desert organisms.
