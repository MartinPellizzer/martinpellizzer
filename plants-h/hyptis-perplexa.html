Hyptis perplexa

Hyptis perplexa, commonly known as the Brazilian hyptis, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves and clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Hyptis perplexa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
