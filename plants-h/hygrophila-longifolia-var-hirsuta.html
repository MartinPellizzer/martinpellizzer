Hygrophila longifolia var. hirsuta

Hygrophila longifolia var. hirsuta, commonly known as hairy hygrophila, is a tropical aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. This variant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and streams. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are typically borne in clusters.

It is often cultivated in aquariums due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to provide habitat for aquatic life.
