Hermesias capitellata

Hermesias capitellata, commonly known as the clustered flax or small flax, is a species of flowering plant in the family Linaceae.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, where it typically grows in dry, sunny environments. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It is often found in rocky soils and can thrive in arid conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
