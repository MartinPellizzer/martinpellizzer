Hieracium divisum subsp. fissicladum

Hieracium divisum subsp.

fissicladum, commonly known as the fissured hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are often covered with fine hairs, and the stems are generally hairy as well.

It is a relatively short-lived perennial, often forming dense colonies in its native habitat.
