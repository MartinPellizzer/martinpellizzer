Hieracium aethocranum

Hieracium aethocranum, commonly known as the silverleaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring temperate climates. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing a showy yellow color that attracts various pollinators.
