Hyacinthus lividus

Hyacinthus lividus, commonly known as the blue hyacinth, is a bulbous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spherical bulbs that help it spread vegetatively. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant blossoms and vibrant color.
