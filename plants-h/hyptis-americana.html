Hyptis americana

Hyptis americana, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Hyptis americana is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
