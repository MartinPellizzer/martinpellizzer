Homeria fenestrata

Homeria fenestrata, commonly known as the windowleaf plant, is a unique species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, fenestrated (window-like) leaves that allow light to pass through, creating a distinctive visual effect. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are thick and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Homeria fenestrata is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
