Hymenophyllum maxonii

Hymenophyllum maxonii, commonly known as the silver moss, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are covered in a fine layer of silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its small, intricate leaves are often used in horticulture for their ornamental value.

Hymenophyllum maxonii plays an important ecological role in maintaining soil moisture and supporting microfauna in its native habitats.
