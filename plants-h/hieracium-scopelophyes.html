Hieracium scopelophyes

Hieracium scopelophyes, commonly known as the cliff dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in well-drained, sunny environments. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil in coastal areas, making it an important pioneer species.

The plant is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds, showcasing its resilience in harsh maritime climates.
