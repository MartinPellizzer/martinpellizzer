Hieracium brachiatum subsp. subtile

Hieracium brachiatum subsp.

subtile, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and small, yellowish to brownish composite flowers.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, and the plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It is a relatively small plant, usually reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters, and is often found in disturbed or marginal soils.
