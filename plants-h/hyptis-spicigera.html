Hyptis spicigera

Hyptis spicigera, commonly known as wild basil or Mexican mint, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs such as mint and sage. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It is often found in moist, open habitats and is valued for its strong, minty fragrance.

Hyptis spicigera is also used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
