Helianthemum apenninum subsp. cantabricum

Helianthemum apenninum subsp.

cantabricum, commonly known as the Cantabrian rockrose, is a low-growing shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It features small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves adapted to withstand dry, rocky environments. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
