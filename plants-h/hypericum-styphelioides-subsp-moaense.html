Hypericum styphelioides subsp. moaense

Hypericum styphelioides subsp.

moaense, commonly known as the Maori St. John's Wort, is a species of flowering plant native to New Zealand. It belongs to the family Hypericaceae and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and woody stems.

The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy or rocky soils. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological significance and is often found in coastal vegetation zones.
