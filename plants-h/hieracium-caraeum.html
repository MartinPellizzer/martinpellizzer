Hieracium caraeum

Hieracium caraeum, commonly known as the Carpathian hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and ragworts, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring moist to moderately dry conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium caraeum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of habitats, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
