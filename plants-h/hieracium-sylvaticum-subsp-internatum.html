Hieracium sylvaticum subsp. internatum

Hieracium sylvaticum subsp.

internatum, commonly known as the woodland hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to orange-yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in woodland margins, meadows, and other moist, shaded habitats. It has alternate, basal leaves that are usually lanceolate or ovate in shape.

Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it produces seeds in a disc-like structure surrounded by ray florets.
