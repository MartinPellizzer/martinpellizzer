Hieracium albidum var. involucratum

Hieracium albidum var. involucratum, commonly known as the white hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are white with yellow centers.

It is often found in temperate climates and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
