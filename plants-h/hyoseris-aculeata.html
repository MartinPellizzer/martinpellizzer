Hyoseris aculeata

Hyoseris aculeata, commonly known as the prickly sunflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers and spiny, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Hyoseris aculeata is known for its adaptability to arid conditions and is often found in disturbed soils.
