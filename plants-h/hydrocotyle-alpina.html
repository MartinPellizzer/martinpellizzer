Hydrocotyle alpina

Hydrocotyle alpina, commonly known as alpine marsh pennywort, is a small perennial herb native to mountainous regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, rocky or sandy soils in alpine and subalpine habitats. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine, white hairs. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, often clustered in umbels.

Hydrocotyle alpina is adapted to cold climates and can survive in low-nutrient environments.
