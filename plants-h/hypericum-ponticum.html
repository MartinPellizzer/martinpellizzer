Hypericum ponticum

Hypericum ponticum, commonly known as the European St. John's wort, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, and its stems are often covered in small, sticky glands. The plant contains yellow and orange pigments called flavonoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties. Hypericum ponticum is often found in sunny, well-drained soils along roadsides and in woodland edges.

It is also known for its historical use in herbal medicine for treating various ailments.
