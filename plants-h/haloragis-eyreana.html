Haloragis eyreana

Haloragis eyreana, commonly known as Eyre's waterwheel, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Haloragaceae and is characterized by its distinctive whorled arrangement of leaves and small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant thrives in wetland environments, often found in marshes and along the edges of water bodies. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are clustered in dense, wheel-like formations.

Haloragis eyreana is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
