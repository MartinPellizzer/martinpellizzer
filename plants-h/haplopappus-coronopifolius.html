Haplopappus coronopifolius

Haplopappus coronopifolius, commonly known as the mountain dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and bloom in the spring.

The species is adapted to arid conditions and has a low-growing habit with a basal rosette of leaves.
