Hutera cheiranthos var. montana

Hutera cheiranthos var. montana, commonly known as the mountain bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its stems are erect and often branched. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
