Hirtella papillata, commonly known as the hairy hirtella, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it its common name. Its leaves are typically elliptical and have a slightly rough texture.
