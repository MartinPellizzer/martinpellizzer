Helmia tomentosa

Helmia tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly helmiya, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, fuzzy foliage and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape and covered in a fine, woolly texture, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Helmia tomentosa is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
