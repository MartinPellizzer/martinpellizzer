Haageocereus zehnderi

Haageocereus zehnderi, commonly known as the Zehnder's night blooming cereus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems and produces large, fragrant white flowers at night. The plant is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for desert conditions. Its flowers are typically short-lived, opening only during the night and closing by dawn.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with well-draining soil.
