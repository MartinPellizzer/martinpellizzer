Hippeastrum macrosiphon

Hippeastrum macrosiphon, commonly known as the giant amaryllis, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, typically in shades of red, pink, or white. The plant features strap-like, lance-shaped leaves that are broad and often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Hippeastrum macrosiphon is a popular ornamental plant due to its striking blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
