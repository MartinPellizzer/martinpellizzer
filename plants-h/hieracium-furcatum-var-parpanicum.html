Hieracium furcatum var. parpanicum

Hieracium furcatum var. parpanicum, commonly known as the panicled hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, branching inflorescence composed of yellow ray florets and tubular disk florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm. Its leaves are lanceolate, rough to the touch, and often have a hairy texture.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
