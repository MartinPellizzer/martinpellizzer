Hieracium cyaneum

Hieracium cyaneum, commonly known as the blue hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces tall, slender stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers. It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides.

Hieracium cyaneum is often found in moist or calcareous soils and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
