Hieracium helminthia

Hieracium helminthia, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils across Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, with a basal rosette structure. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Hieracium helminthia is known for its ability to spread rapidly through rhizomes and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
