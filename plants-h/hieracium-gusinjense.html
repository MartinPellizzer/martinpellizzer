Hieracium gusinjense

Hieracium gusinjense, commonly known as the Gusinjense hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats at elevations ranging from 1,000 to 2,500 meters. Its leaves are generally lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head.

Hieracium gusinjense is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to cold, temperate climates.
