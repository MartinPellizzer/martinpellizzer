Helichrysum silvaticum

Helichrysum silvaticum, commonly known as the mountain everlasting, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and small, yellow to golden-orange flowers that resemble tiny suns. The plant produces bract-like leaves that are often mistaken for flowers, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Helichrysum silvaticum is also noted for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
