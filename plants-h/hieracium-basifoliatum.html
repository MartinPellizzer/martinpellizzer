Hieracium basifoliatum

Hieracium basifoliatum, commonly known as the basal-leaf hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, rough leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in the summer. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

It is known for its ability to spread rapidly through rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural and natural areas.
