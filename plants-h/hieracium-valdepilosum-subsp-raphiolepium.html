Hieracium valdepilosum subsp. raphiolepium

Hieracium valdepilosum subsp.

raphiolepium, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in the summer months.

This subspecies is known for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its adaptability to various soil conditions.
