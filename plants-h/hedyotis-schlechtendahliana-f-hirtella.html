Hedyotis schlechtendahliana f. hirtella

Hedyotis schlechtendahliana f. hirtella, commonly known as the hairy blushing geranium, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. The plant features small, clustered flowers with five petals, often displaying shades of red or pink. Its leaves are usually hairy, giving it the "hirtella" designation in its scientific name.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
