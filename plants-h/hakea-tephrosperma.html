Hakea tephrosperma

Hakea tephrosperma, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and produces cylindrical, yellow-green flower spikes. The plant is adapted to arid environments, showcasing features like reduced leaf size and deep root systems. Its flowers are pollinated by birds, particularly honeyeaters, due to their nectar-rich nature.

Hakea tephrosperma is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in native landscapes.
