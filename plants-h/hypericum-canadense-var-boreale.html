Hypericum canadense var. boreale

Hypericum canadense var. boreale, commonly known as northern St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other damp habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
