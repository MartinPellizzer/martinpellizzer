Hieracium falsobifidum

Hieracium falsobifidum, commonly known as the false bifid hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellowish-orange composite flowers and hairy, often reddish stems. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a rough texture.

It is native to Europe and has been introduced to other parts of the world, where it can sometimes become invasive.
