Hieracium simplex

Hieracium simplex, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces yellow or orange composite flowers that resemble daisies. The plant has basal and alternate leaves that are typically heart-shaped or lanceolate. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Hieracium simplex is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it an invasive species in some regions.
