Hieracium longiglandulosum

Hieracium longiglandulosum, commonly known as the long-glandular hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and elongated, hairy stems that bear numerous yellow flowers arranged in a composite head. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are covered in fine, glandular hairs, which give it its common name.

It is an herbaceous perennial that thrives in temperate climates.
