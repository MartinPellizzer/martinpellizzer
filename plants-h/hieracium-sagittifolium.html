Hieracium sagittifolium

Hieracium sagittifolium, commonly known as the arrowleaf hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive arrowhead base, which gives it its common name. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, and it is often found in meadows and grassy areas.

Hieracium sagittifolium is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resilience to environmental stress.
