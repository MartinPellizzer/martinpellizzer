Hibiscus saxicola

Hibiscus saxicola, commonly known as the rock hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, succulent stems and thick, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, yellow, and orange, which bloom during the warmer months. Hibiscus saxicola is often found growing on rocky soils or in crevices, showcasing its adaptability to extreme conditions.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and drought-tolerant gardens.
