Hypericum platyphyllum

Hypericum platyphyllum, commonly known as the flat-leaved St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces yellow flowers with five petals. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly glossy surface. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable plant for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

This species is closely related to other members of the Hypericaceae family, which are known for their medicinal properties.
