Hedera

Hedera, commonly known as ivy, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Araliaceae.

It consists of about 30 species, most of which are climbing or trailing plants. Hedera species are characterized by their opposite, simple, and usually lobed or serrated leaves. They produce small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by dark blue berries.

These plants are widely used in horticulture for their ornamental value and ability to thrive in various environments.
