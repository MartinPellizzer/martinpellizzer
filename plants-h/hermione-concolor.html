Hermione concolor

Hermione concolor, commonly known as the silverleaf vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, often white or pale yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. Its rapid growth habit makes it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover in shaded areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
