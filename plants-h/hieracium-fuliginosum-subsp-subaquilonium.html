Hieracium fuliginosum subsp. subaquilonium

Hieracium fuliginosum subsp.

subaquilonium, commonly known as the smoky hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, wetlands, and riverbanks.

The plant features basal rosettes of dark green, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence and are visited by various pollinators.

The species is characterized by its distinctive, smoky-colored leaves and is often found in nutrient-rich soils.
