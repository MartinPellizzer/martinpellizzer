Hieracium juranum subsp. stenoplecoides

Hieracium juranum subsp.

stenoplecoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
