Hieracium olivaceum subsp. oleicoloriforme

Hieracium olivaceum subsp.

oleicoloriforme, commonly known as the olive hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosettes of lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences, which are typical of the daisy family. It is native to Europe and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

Due to its aggressive growth, it can become invasive in certain regions.
