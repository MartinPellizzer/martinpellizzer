Hypericum baumgartenianum

Hypericum baumgartenianum, commonly known as the Baumgarten's St. John's Wort, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and opposite leaves that are oval to lance-shaped. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months, which are followed by red, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species within the genus Hypericum, known for its medicinal and ornamental value.
