Hieracium murorum subsp. sericipes

Hieracium murorum subsp.

sericipes, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats such as roadsides and meadows.

The plant features a basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in early summer. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its distinctive, woolly appearance.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
