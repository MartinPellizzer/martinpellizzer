Holodiscus argenteus var. bifrons

Holodiscus argenteus var. bifrons, commonly known as the silver-leaf honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage, which provides a distinctive visual appeal in gardens. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
