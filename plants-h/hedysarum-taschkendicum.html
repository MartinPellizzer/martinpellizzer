Hedysarum taschkendicum

Hedysarum taschkendicum, commonly known as the Taschkent milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.

Hedysarum taschkendicum plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest for its potential in ecological restoration projects.
