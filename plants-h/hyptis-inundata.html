Hyptis inundata

Hyptis inundata, commonly known as water hyptis, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist or wet habitats such as marshes and riverbanks. It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.

Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in waterlogged soils make it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
