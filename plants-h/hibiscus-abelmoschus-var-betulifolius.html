Hibiscus abelmoschus var. betulifolius

Hibiscus abelmoschus var. betulifolius, commonly known as the Chinese lantern or flame flower, is a species native to Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange-red calyces that resemble small lanterns, which persist through winter. The plant is a shrub or small tree with simple, alternate, ovate leaves that have a serrated margin. It produces showy, five-petaled flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This ornamental plant is often cultivated for its striking foliage and colorful bracts.
