Heliotropium pannifolium

Heliotropium pannifolium, commonly known as the velvetleaf heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Its flowers bloom throughout the growing season, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
