Hypericum longibracteatum

Hypericum longibracteatum, commonly known as the long-bract St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers, which bloom in summer and are clustered in dense inflorescences. The plant has woody stems and alternate leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the genus Hypericum, valued for both its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
