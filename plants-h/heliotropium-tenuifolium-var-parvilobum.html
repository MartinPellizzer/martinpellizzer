Heliotropium tenuifolium var. parvilobum

Heliotropium tenuifolium var. parvilobum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, glandular leaves and clusters of purple or pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas or disturbed habitats. Its foliage has a distinct aromatic scent, and it is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in warm climates.
