Hygrophila salicifolia var. quadrivalvis

Hygrophila salicifolia var. quadrivalvis, commonly known as the four-valved hygrophila, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes. The plant is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are often elliptical in shape. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

This species is valued in aquatic gardening for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide habitat for aquatic life.
