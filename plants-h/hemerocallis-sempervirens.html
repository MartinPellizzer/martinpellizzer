Hemerocallis sempervirens

Hemerocallis sempervirens, commonly known as evergreen daylily, is a perennial plant native to Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which remains green throughout the year, distinguishing it from other daylily varieties. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.

Hemerocallis sempervirens is prized for its ornamental value and hardiness in temperate climates.
