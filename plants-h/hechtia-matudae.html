Hechtia matudae

Hechtia matudae, commonly known as the Matudae hechtia, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is native to Mexico.

It is a compact, evergreen perennial that typically grows in shaded, rocky habitats. The plant features rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. It produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which bloom in the spring.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat preferences, it is a sought-after plant among bromeliad enthusiasts.
