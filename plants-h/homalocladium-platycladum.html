Homalocladium platycladum

Homalocladium platycladum, commonly known as the coastal bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescences that resemble bottle brushes, typically blooming in red or pink hues. The plant thrives in coastal environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils. It has small, scale-like leaves that are tightly packed around the stem, reducing water loss in saline conditions.

Homalocladium platycladum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes.
