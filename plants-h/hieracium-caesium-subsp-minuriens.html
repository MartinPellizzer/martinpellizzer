Hieracium caesium subsp. minuriens

Hieracium caesium subsp.

minuriens, commonly known as the lesser spearthistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and spiny leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Due to its prickly foliage, it is often considered a weed but has ecological value for pollinators.
