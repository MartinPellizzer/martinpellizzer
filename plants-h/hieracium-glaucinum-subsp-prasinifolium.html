Hieracium glaucinum subsp. prasinifolium

Hieracium glaucinum subsp.

prasinifolium, commonly known as the green-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges, preferring moist, well-drained soils.

The plant features basal rosettes of lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its yellow flowers are clustered in dense, flat-topped inflorescences, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and floral structure.
