Hibbertia linearis var. typica

Hibbertia linearis var. typica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hibbertia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and showy, orange-yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in open habitats such as woodlands and heathlands. It is a perennial shrub that can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Hibbertia linearis var. typica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
