Helia martii

Helia martii, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico and Central America.

It features bright orange to red composite flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Helia martii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
