Hakea elliptica

Hakea elliptica, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hakea, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and produces spikes of bright yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought-resistant features. Hakea elliptica is often found in heathlands and coastal areas, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

Its unique morphology and ecological significance make it an important component of native Australian flora.
