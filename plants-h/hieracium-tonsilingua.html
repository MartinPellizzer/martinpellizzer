Hieracium tonsilingua

Hieracium tonsilingua, commonly known as the Italian hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, with a rough texture, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Hieracium tonsilingua is known for its ability to spread rapidly through vegetative means, making it a common weed in various habitats.
