Helleborus niger subsp. niger

Helleborus niger subsp.

niger, commonly known as the Christmas rose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It is characterized by its dark green, hairy leaves and its striking, waxy white flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring.

The plant thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas. It is noted for its ability to tolerate cold temperatures and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.

The flowers are typically borne on short, sturdy stems and are often associated with the holiday season due to their winter blooming habit.
