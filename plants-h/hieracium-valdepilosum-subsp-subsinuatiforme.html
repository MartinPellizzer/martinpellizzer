Hieracium valdepilosum subsp. subsinuatiforme

Hieracium valdepilosum subsp.

subsinuatiforme, commonly known as the sinuately lobed hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves and yellow flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its finely serrated leaf margins and a more sinuous leaf shape compared to other members of the species. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
