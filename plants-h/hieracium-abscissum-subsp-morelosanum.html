Hieracium abscissum subsp. morelosanum

Hieracium abscissum subsp.

morelosanum, commonly known as the Morelos hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Mexico. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow flower heads.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, composite flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
