Hieracium oligochnoum

Hieracium oligochnoum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring well-drained conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often have a hairy texture, while its yellow flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

Hieracium oligochnoum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
