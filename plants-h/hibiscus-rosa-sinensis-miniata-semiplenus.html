Hibiscus rosa-sinensis miniata-semiplenus

Hibiscus rosa-sinensis miniata-semiplenus, commonly known as the variegated hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically feature a deep red or purple color with white or pale pink veins. The leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and often have a glossy texture. This plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil and full sunlight.

It is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant blooms and lush foliage.
