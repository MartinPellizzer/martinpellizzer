Hieracium vulgatum subsp. purpureoviolascens

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

purpureoviolascens, commonly known as the purple viola hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers with purple-tinted petals, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Due to its ornamental appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
