Hieracium squarrosum

Hieracium squarrosum, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are rough and hairy, often with a whitish or yellowish underside, and its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in many temperate regions.
