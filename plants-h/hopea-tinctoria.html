Hopea tinctoria

Hopea tinctoria, commonly known as the dye tree or yellow hopea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, light grey bark and pinnate leaves that are alternate and have a leathery texture. The tree produces small, fragrant yellow flowers followed by woody pods containing elliptical seeds. Hopea tinctoria is valued for its hard, durable wood and its historical use in natural dye production.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
