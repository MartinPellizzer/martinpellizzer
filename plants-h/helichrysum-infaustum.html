Helichrysum infaustum

Helichrysum infaustum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to produce bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that remain colorful even when dried. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The plant's resilience and ornamental value have made it a valued species in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
