Hieracium dolopicum

Hieracium dolopicum, commonly known as the Dolopic Mountain Hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features bright yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, which attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, adapting to the harsh alpine environment.

Hieracium dolopicum typically grows in open, rocky habitats at high altitudes, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
