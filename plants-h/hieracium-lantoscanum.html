Hieracium lantoscanum

Hieracium lantoscanum, commonly known as the Italian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, producing yellowish to orange composite flowers.

The leaves are usually lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
