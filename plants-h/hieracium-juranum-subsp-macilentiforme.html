Hieracium juranum subsp. macilentiforme

Hieracium juranum subsp.

macilentiforme, commonly known as the slender-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
