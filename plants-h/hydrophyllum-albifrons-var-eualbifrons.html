Hydrophyllum albifrons var. eualbifrons

Hydrophyllum albifrons var. eualbifrons, commonly known as the white-fringed hydrophyllum, is a variant of the hydrophyllum genus native to North America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its flowers are usually arranged in clusters and bloom in the summer months.

The variant 'eualbifrons' is distinguished by its more pronounced white flower petals compared to the typical form.
