Hamamelis chinensis

Hamamelis chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese witch hazel, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking orange-yellow flowers that bloom in late autumn or early winter. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that turn reddish in the fall. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, often peeling in thin strips.

Hamamelis chinensis is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Chinese medicine.
