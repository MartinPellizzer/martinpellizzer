Hieracium pseudonosmoides

Hieracium pseudonosmoides, commonly known as the false noseless hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf morphology. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lanceolate, hairy, and often have a rough texture, while its yellow flowers are arranged in dense clusters.

Hieracium pseudonosmoides is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
