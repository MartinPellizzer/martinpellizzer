Hesperis dubia

Hesperis dubia, commonly known as the pale lady's mantle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Hesperis dubia typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and subtle floral display.
