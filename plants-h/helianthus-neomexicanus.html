Helianthus neomexicanus

Helianthus neomexicanus, commonly known as New Mexico sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Helianthus neomexicanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
