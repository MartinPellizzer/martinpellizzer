Heterocarpus obliquus

Heterocarpus obliquus, commonly known as the river hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red in color. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, aiding in its dispersal.

Heterocarpus obliquus thrives in wetland and riparian habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife.
