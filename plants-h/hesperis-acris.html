Hesperis acris

Hesperis acris, commonly known as the sweet rocket, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbages and mustards. The plant features showy, violet or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Hesperis acris is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
