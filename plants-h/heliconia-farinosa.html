Heliconia farinosa

Heliconia farinosa, commonly known as the starchy heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its large, brightly colored, spiral-shaped inflorescences that resemble a crown. The plant's leaves are broad, elongated, and often have a waxy texture, while its flowers are typically orange or red with prominent, fleshy bracts. The fruit of Heliconia farinosa is a three-lobed capsule that contains numerous small, white seeds.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant appearance and unique structure.
