Hymenaea courbaril var. subsessilis

Hymenaea courbaril var. subsessilis, commonly known as the yellow cedar or Brazilian cedar, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, light yellow to reddish-brown bark. The tree produces large, pod-like fruits that contain several flat, winged seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

This species is valued for its durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
