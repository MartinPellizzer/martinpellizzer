Hyptis laciniata

Hyptis laciniata, commonly known as the Brazilian hyptis, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and showy blue-violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

Hyptis laciniata is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
