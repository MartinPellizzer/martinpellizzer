Hippeastrum squamigerum

Hippeastrum squamigerum, commonly known as the Brazilian belladonna lily, is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant produces large, trumpet-shaped blooms that can range in color from white to deep red, often with a purple or red throat. Its leaves are broad, strap-like, and emerge from a bulbous stem.

Hippeastrum squamigerum is a popular ornamental plant due to its vibrant flowers and relatively easy care requirements.
