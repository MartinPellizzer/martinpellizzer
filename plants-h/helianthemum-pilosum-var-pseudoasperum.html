Helianthemum pilosum var. pseudoasperum

Helianthemum pilosum var. pseudoasperum, commonly known as the rough stonecrop, is a low-growing succulent plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, fleshy leaves that are typically covered in a fine, fuzzy layer, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its hardy nature allows it to withstand drought and poor soil conditions, contributing to its popularity among gardeners.
