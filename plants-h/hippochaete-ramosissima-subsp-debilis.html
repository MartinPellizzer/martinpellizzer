Hippochaete ramosissima subsp. debilis

Hippochaete ramosissima subsp.

debilis, commonly known as the slender bushveld grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa. It typically grows in open grasslands and coastal areas, exhibiting a low, sprawling growth habit.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, pale yellow to brownish flowers in panicles. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid conditions, showing drought tolerance and resilience.

Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion in its native habitat.
