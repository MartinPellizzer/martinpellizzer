Helichrysum ibityense

Helichrysum ibityense, commonly known as the Madagascar everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, golden-yellow flowers that resemble tiny suns. The plant produces papery, dry flower heads that persist on the plant for a long time, giving it the common name "everlasting.". It thrives in sunny, well-drained environments and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
