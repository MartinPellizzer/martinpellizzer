Hieracium subsabaudum

Hieracium subsabaudum, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are basal, rough, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer.

Hieracium subsabaudum is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators in its native habitat.
