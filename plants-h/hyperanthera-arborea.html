Hyperanthera arborea

Hyperanthera arborea, commonly known as the tree with many stamens, is a striking botanical specimen characterized by its tall, branching structure and showy inflorescences.

The plant is native to tropical regions and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. Its flowers are typically large and clustered, featuring prominent stamens that give the plant its distinctive name. The leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a glossy surface.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
