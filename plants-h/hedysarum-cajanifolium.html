Hedysarum cajanifolium

Hedysarum cajanifolium, commonly known as the Indian needlepod, is a perennial legume native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and slender, erect stems that bear small, yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Hedysarum cajanifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
