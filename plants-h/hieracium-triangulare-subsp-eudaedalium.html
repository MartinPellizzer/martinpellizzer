Hieracium triangulare subsp. eudaedalium

Hieracium triangulare subsp.

eudaedalium, commonly known as the triangular hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf morphology.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are usually triangular in shape, with serrated edges, and the flowers are yellow with ray florets.

It is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural and natural settings.
