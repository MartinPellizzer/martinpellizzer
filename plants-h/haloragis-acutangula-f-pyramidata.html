Haloragis acutangula f. pyramidata

Haloragis acutangula f. pyramidata, commonly known as the pyramid bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright, branched stems that are often densely covered with fine, hairy hairs. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly waxy texture.

This species thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
