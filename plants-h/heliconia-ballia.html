Heliconia ballia

Heliconia ballia, commonly known as the red heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescences. The plant features long, narrow, strap-like leaves that form a dense rosette, providing a lush backdrop to its vibrant flower spikes. Its flowers are typically red or orange, with a distinctive, boat-shaped structure that resembles a crown.

Heliconia ballia is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
