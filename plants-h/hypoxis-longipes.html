Hypoxis longipes

Hypoxis longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed yellowstar, is a member of the Hyacinthaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves. The plant produces a tall flowering stem that bears bright yellow, star-shaped flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional medicine practices.
