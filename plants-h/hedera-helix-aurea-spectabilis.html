Hedera helix aurea-spectabilis

Hedera helix aurea-spectabilis, commonly known as the golden glory ivy, is a cultivar of the common ivy plant.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves, which feature a mix of golden and green hues. This plant is a climbing vine that thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. It has small, dark green berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

The golden glory ivy is often used in hanging baskets or as a ground cover due to its ornamental appeal.
