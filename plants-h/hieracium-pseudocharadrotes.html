Hieracium pseudocharadrotes

Hieracium pseudocharadrotes, commonly known as the false sand everlasting.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, characterized by its upright growth habit and daisy-like flowers. The plant features yellow disc flowers surrounded by white to pale yellow ray florets, which bloom from late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Hieracium pseudocharadrotes is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.
