Huanaca geraniifolia

Huanaca geraniifolia, commonly known as the Andean geranium, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that resemble those of the geranium genus. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in cool, humid climates and is often found in rocky or mountainous terrain.

Huanaca geraniifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in some Andean communities.
