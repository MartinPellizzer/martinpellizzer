Hieracium florescens

Hieracium florescens, commonly known as the flowering hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. The plant features basal rosettes of leaves and produces showy yellow or orange composite flowers in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are composed of both ray and disk florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Hieracium florescens is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize open areas, sometimes becoming invasive in certain regions.
