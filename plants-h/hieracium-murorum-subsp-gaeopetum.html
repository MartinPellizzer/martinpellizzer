Hieracium murorum subsp. gaeopetum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

gaeopetum, commonly known as the creeping hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with slender stems that often root at the nodes.

The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows.
