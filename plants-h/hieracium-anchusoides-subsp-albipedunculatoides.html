Hieracium anchusoides subsp. albipedunculatoides

Hieracium anchusoides subsp.

albipedunculatoides, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a single, elongated inflorescence, which is distinctive for its pale peduncles. It thrives in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Due to its specialized habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
