Hibiscus seineri

Hibiscus seineri, commonly known as the African hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to deep pink or red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Hibiscus seineri thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some African cultures.
