Hovenia acerba

Hovenia acerba, commonly known as the Chinese date or Japanese date, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rhamnaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange to red berries that are edible when fully ripe. Hovenia acerba is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
