Hibiscus rostellatus var. sudanensis

Hibiscus rostellatus var. sudanensis, commonly known as Sudanese hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa, particularly Sudan.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range from pink to red in color. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is often found in wetland or marshy environments. Hibiscus rostellatus var. sudanensis is a perennial shrub that can grow up to several meters in height.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
