Hemionitis robusta

Hemionitis robusta, commonly known as the rock fern, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It thrives in rocky and sandy soils, often growing in crevices and on cliffs. The plant has a rosette-like growth pattern with dark green, leathery leaves that are deeply lobed. It produces small, spore-bearing structures called soruses on the underside of its leaves.

Hemionitis robusta is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and low moisture conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
