Hypericum lancioides subsp. congestiflorum

Hypericum lancioides subsp.

congestiflorum, commonly known as the clustered St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, woodlands or along stream banks, preferring partial to full sun. Its flowering stems are often branched and bear numerous small, rounded leaves.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
