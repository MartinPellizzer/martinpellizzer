Hieracium pseudotriangulare

Hieracium pseudotriangulare, commonly known as the triangular hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture, and its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

It is known for its ability to thrive in various environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
