Hieracium murorum subsp. amoenissimum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

amoenissimum, commonly known as the pretty mouse-ear hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

This subspecies is native to parts of Europe and is often found in meadows and grasslands. It has a creeping growth habit and produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

The plant is generally not considered invasive but can form dense mats in suitable habitats.
