Hemionitis longipila

Hemionitis longipila, commonly known as the long-leaf hemionitis, is a rare and delicate fern native to the mountainous regions of central China.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in shaded, rocky habitats. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat destruction, Hemionitis longipila is considered endangered and requires careful protection.
