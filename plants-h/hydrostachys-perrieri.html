Hydrostachys perrieri

Hydrostachys perrieri, commonly known as the water stachys, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Hydrostachys perrieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
