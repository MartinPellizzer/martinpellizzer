Hedysarum humile var. laeve

Hedysarum humile var. laeve, commonly known as the smooth meadow vetcher, is a low-growing perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves with small, rounded leaflets and produces yellow to reddish flowers in late spring. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in meadows and along stream banks. It is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a desirable plant for garden settings.
