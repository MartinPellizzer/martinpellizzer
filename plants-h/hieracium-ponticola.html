Hieracium ponticola

Hieracium ponticola, commonly known as the Pontic hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually alternate, hairy, and lance-shaped, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a cluster.

Hieracium ponticola is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
