Hypericum thesiifolium var. latifolium

Hypericum thesiifolium var. latifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved St. John's wort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Hypericaceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and opposite, broad, oval-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Its medicinal properties, similar to other Hypericum species, have been historically used for their antiseptic and antidepressant qualities.
