Henckelia primulina

Henckelia primulina, commonly known as the pink bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and clusters of delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale pink to white. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as cloud forests and mountain meadows. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Henckelia primulina is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
