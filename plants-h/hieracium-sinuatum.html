Hieracium sinuatum

Hieracium sinuatum, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while the flowers are yellow with prominent central disks.

Hieracium sinuatum is often considered a weed due to its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes.
