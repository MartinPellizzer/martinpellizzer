Hieracium auricula subsp. glaucoplumbeum

Hieracium auricula subsp.

glaucoplumbeum, commonly known as the glaucous hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, glaucous leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. It has a taproot system and produces achenes as its seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
