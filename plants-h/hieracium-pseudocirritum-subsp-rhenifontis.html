Hieracium pseudocirritum subsp. rhenifontis

Hieracium pseudocirritum subsp.

rhenifontis, commonly known as the Rhenish hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.

The plant features basal and stem leaves that are lanceolate to ovate, with hairy surfaces and serrated edges. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family.

The subspecies is distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and subtle morphological differences from other Hieracium species.
