Huperzia petiolata

Huperzia petiolata, commonly known as the slender huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems and small, ovate, leathery leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including China, India, and Southeast Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices.

Huperzia petiolata is notable for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its use in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
