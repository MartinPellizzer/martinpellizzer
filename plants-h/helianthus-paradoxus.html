Helianthus paradoxus

Helianthus paradoxus, commonly known as the paradox sunflower, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family.

It is native to the southeastern United States and is characterized by its yellow flowers and linear leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. It produces small, dark seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread.

Helianthus paradoxus is noted for its resilience and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
