Hieracium murorum subsp. substriatulum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

substriatulum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and serrated leaves.

The subspecies is distinguished by its narrower leaf blades and more pronounced leaf margins compared to other hawkweed species. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows.

The plant is relatively low-growing, with a fibrous root system and basal rosettes of leaves.
