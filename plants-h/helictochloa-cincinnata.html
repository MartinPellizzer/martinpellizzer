Helictochloa cincinnata

Helictochloa cincinnata, commonly known as the bentgrass or narrowleaf grass, is a species of grass native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing, perennial plant that typically forms dense mats on rocky or sandy soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities in its range.
