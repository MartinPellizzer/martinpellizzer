Hieracium leucophaeum

Hieracium leucophaeum, commonly known as the white-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a whitish underside, giving it its common name.

The flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
