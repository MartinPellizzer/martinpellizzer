Hypericum jaramilloi

Hypericum jaramilloi, commonly known as the Jaramillo's St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open woodlands. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are opposite, simple, and often covered in fine hairs. It belongs to the family Hypericaceae and is characterized by its medicinal properties, though it is less commonly used than other Hypericum species.

The plant is also noted for its ability to thrive in harsh conditions, making it an important component of its native ecosystem.
