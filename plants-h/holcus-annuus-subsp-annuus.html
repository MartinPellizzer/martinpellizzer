Holcus annuus subsp. annuus

Holcus annuus subsp.

annuus, commonly known as annual quackgrass, is a grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and agricultural fields. Holcus annuus subsp.

annuus is an annual plant, completing its life cycle within a single growing season.
