Hypericum quadrangulum var. acutum

Hypericum quadrangulum var. acutum, commonly known as the sharp-leaved St. John's wort, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, typically arranged in clusters along the stems. The leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a serrated margin and a slightly hairy surface. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal or rocky habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
