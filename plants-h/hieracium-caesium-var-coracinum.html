Hieracium caesium var. coracinum

Hieracium caesium var. coracinum, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and features serrated, hairy leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces yellow composite flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which attract various pollinators. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

This variant is distinguished by its more robust and rough-textured foliage compared to other forms of Hieracium caesium.
