Henriquezia verticillata

Henriquezia verticillata, commonly known as the Mexican flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of bright red or orange flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Henriquezia verticillata is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant flowers and adaptability to various soil conditions.
