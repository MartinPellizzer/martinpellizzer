Hieracium sagittatum subsp. expallidum

Hieracium sagittatum subsp.

expallidum, commonly known as the slender spearhead hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and leaves arranged in a rosette pattern.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its yellow flowers are borne on slender stems, and the leaves are lanceolate with a hairy surface.

It is a relatively small species, with a height ranging from 10 to 30 centimeters.
