Hieracium subatriceps

Hieracium subatriceps, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and a flowering stem that bears yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a single head.

Hieracium subatriceps is often found in temperate regions of Europe and Asia.
