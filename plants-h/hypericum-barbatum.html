Hypericum barbatum

Hypericum barbatum, commonly known as the beard-tongue hypericum, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters along the stems. The plant has rough, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive texture, and its stems are often reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Hypericum barbatum is also known for its medicinal properties, with historical use in traditional herbal remedies.
