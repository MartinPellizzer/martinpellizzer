Helichrysum plumeum

Helichrysum plumeum, commonly known as the golden helmet, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces fluffy, seed-bearing structures called pappi. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Helichrysum plumeum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
