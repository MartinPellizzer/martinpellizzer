Hieracium saggjemense

Hieracium saggjemense, commonly known as the Italian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows, preferring sunny conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is known for its aggressive growth and can become invasive in certain regions.
