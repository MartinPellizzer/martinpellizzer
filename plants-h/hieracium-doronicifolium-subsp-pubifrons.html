Hieracium doronicifolium subsp. pubifrons

Hieracium doronicifolium subsp.

pubifrons, commonly known as the hairy-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered with fine pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
