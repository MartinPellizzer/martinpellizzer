Hieracium plumiferum

Hieracium plumiferum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are hairy and alternate, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
