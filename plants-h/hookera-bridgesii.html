Hookera bridgesii

Hookera bridgesii, commonly known as the Bridgestone hooker's flower, is a rare alpine plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers and hairy, glandular leaves that help it survive in high-altitude conditions. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, moist environments. It is often found in cloud forests and alpine tundra, where it blooms during the short growing season.

Due to habitat loss and climate change, Hookera bridgesii is considered endangered and is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
