Hieracium vulgatum subsp. acutellum

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

acutellum, commonly known as the sharp-leaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features basal leaves that are lanceolate and often have a sharp, pointed tip, giving it its subspecific name. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and are borne on long, slender stems.

The species is characterized by its robust growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
