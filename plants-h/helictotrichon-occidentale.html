Helictotrichon occidentale

Helictotrichon occidentale, commonly known as western bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as prairies and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-bearing branches.
