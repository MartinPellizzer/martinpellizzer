Hieracium brachiatum var. koscieliskoense

Hieracium brachiatum var. koscieliskoense, commonly known as the Koscielisko hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, pastures, and roadsides. It has basal leaves that are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

This variety is distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and morphological traits that set it apart from other hawkweed species.
