Helmiopsis inversa

Helmiopsis inversa, commonly known as the upside-down lily, is a unique flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, downward-facing flowers that resemble upside-down lilies, giving it its common name. The plant has a short, thick stem with broad, waxy leaves that are often covered in a fine, white coating. Its flowers are typically pink or purple and bloom in clusters, adding a dramatic visual effect.

Helmiopsis inversa is a relatively rare species, often found in shaded, humid environments within dense tropical forests.
