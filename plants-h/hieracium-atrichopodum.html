Hieracium atrichopodum

Hieracium atrichopodum, commonly known as the black-haired hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a dark center.

Hieracium atrichopodum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting pollinators.
