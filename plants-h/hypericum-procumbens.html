Hypericum procumbens

Hypericum procumbens, commonly known as the prostrate St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typically from June to August. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along its stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its medicinal properties have been studied for their potential anti-inflammatory and antidepressant effects.
