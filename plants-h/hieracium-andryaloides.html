Hieracium andryaloides

Hieracium andryaloides, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellowish-orange composite flowers and is native to Europe, western Asia, and parts of North Africa. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and other open areas. It has basal leaves that are usually heart-shaped and may have hairy surfaces.

Hieracium andryaloides is often considered a weed due to its aggressive growth habits and ability to spread rapidly.
