Hieracium marginatum f. chaetocephalum

Hieracium marginatum f. chaetocephalum, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant is distinguished by its dense, woolly foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.

Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, producing yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
