Hypoestes comosa

Hypoestes comosa, commonly known as the "Blue Mist Flower," is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet bracts that surround small, inconspicuous white flowers. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with hairy, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine white or grayish hairs. It thrives in well-draining soil and prefers bright, indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

The plant is also known for its ability to produce numerous small, fragrant flowers throughout the growing season.
