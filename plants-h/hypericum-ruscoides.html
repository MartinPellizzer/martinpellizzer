Hypericum ruscoides

Hypericum ruscoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf St. John's wort, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the genus Hypericum, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their yellow flowers and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and features small, elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, blooming from late spring to early summer.

Hypericum ruscoides is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antidepressant effects.
