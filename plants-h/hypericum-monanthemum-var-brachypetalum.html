Hypericum monanthemum var. brachypetalum

Hypericum monanthemum var. brachypetalum, commonly known as the short-petaled St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and opposite, simple leaves. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds, typical of the Hypericaceae family. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for ecological studies and herbal uses.
