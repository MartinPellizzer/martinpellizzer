Hoya nova

Hoya nova, commonly known as the "Wax Plant" or "Porcelain Flower," is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with white or cream markings. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pink in color. Hoya nova is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

It is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
