Hibiscus abelmoschus var. genuinus

Hibiscus abelmoschus var. genuinus, commonly known as the Chinese okra or Abelmoschus manihot, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain edible seeds, often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth.

Hibiscus abelmoschus var. genuinus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
