Hieracium alpinum subsp. halleri

Hieracium alpinum subsp.

halleri, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette growth habit and yellow-orange composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it is adapted to cold and harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually hairy and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are upright and branched.

This subspecies is often found in mountainous areas of the Alps and is a notable component of alpine vegetation.
