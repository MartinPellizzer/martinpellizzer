Hieracium annae-toutoniae subsp. vilpianense

Hieracium annae-toutoniae subsp.

vilpianense, commonly known as the Vilpian hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette-forming basal leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils. It has a relatively small range and is considered a rare species due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique morphological features, including the structure of its leaves and flower heads.
