Hieracium sylvaticum subsp. bifidiforme

Hieracium sylvaticum subsp.

bifidiforme, commonly known as the bifidleaf wood hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a distinctive bifid or split appearance, contributing to its common name.

It produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are arranged in a compact head and attract various pollinators.
