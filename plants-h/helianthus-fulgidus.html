Helianthus fulgidus

Helianthus fulgidus, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a striking annual plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant features vibrant orange to red flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves are adapted to thrive in full sun and well-drained soil.

Helianthus fulgidus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to provide habitat for beneficial insects.
