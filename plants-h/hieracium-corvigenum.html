Hieracium corvigenum

Hieracium corvigenum, commonly known as the black hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while its flowers are yellow with a dark center.

Hieracium corvigenum is often considered a weed due to its rapid spread and ability to thrive in various environments.
