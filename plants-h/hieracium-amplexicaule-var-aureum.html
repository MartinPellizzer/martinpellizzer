Hieracium amplexicaule var. aureum

Hieracium amplexicaule var. aureum, commonly known as the golden hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf morphology. The plant features basal leaves that are often clasping the stem, giving it its specific epithet *amplexicaule*. Its yellow flowers, which appear in clusters, are typically visited by various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it can sometimes be considered a weed in agricultural settings.
