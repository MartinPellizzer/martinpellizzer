Holboellia coriacea var. angustifolia

Holboellia coriacea var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf holboellia, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its rough, leathery leaves that are typically narrow and elongated. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, blooming in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive species within the Apiaceae family.
