Hippion spicatum

Hippion spicatum, commonly known as the spiny hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Hippion spicatum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can be found in gardens and natural habitats.
