Hieracium sparsiramum subsp. draconis

Hieracium sparsiramum subsp.

draconis, commonly known as the dragon's hieracium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy stems.

The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat, daisy-like inflorescence.

It is known for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions and is often found in alpine or subalpine zones.
