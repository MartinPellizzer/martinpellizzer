Hypericum ericoides subsp. maroccanum

Hypericum ericoides subsp.

maroccanum, commonly known as the Moroccan St. John's Wort, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Morocco. It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has narrow, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces red-orange berries that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in local cultures.
