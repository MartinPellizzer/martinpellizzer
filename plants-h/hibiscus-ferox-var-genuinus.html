Hibiscus ferox var. genuinus

Hibiscus ferox var. genuinus, commonly known as the African hibiscus, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its woody stems and large, showy flowers. The leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture with a serrated edge. Its flowers are typically bright red or orange, with five petals and a prominent central stigma.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
