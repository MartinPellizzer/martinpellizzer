Hieracium vulgatum subsp. tenuifloriforme

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

tenuifloriforme, commonly known as slender-flowered hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its low-growing habit and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically has basal and stem leaves that are lanceolate to ovate in shape. It produces small, elongated flower heads that are often surrounded by pale green bracts.

This subspecies is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats such as meadows and roadsides.
