Hieracium diplacoides

Hieracium diplacoides, commonly known as the two-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and a single, erect flowering stem that bears yellow disk flowers. Native to Europe, it is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant has rough, hairy leaves and produces small, inconspicuous seeds that are dispersed by wind.

It is considered a common weed in agricultural areas and gardens.
