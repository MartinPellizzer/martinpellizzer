Hieracium sparsifolium var. subintegrum

Hieracium sparsifolium var. subintegrum, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and yellow daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with a rough texture, and the flowers are surrounded by a ring of pale yellow ray florets.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and thistles.
