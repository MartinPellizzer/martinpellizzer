Hyptis hispida

Hyptis hispida, commonly known as hairy hyptis, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Hyptis hispida is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
