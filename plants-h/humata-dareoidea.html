Humata dareoidea

Humata dareoidea, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow petals and a distinctive star-shaped center. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers bloom in clusters, often during the rainy season, adding a splash of color to the forest canopy.

The botanical features of Humata dareoidea make it a popular choice for both ornamental and horticultural purposes.
