Hedyotis geminiflora

Hedyotis geminiflora, commonly known as the twin-flowered Indian snapdragon, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers that are arranged in pairs, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

Hedyotis geminiflora is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
