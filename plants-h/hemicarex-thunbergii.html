Hemicarex thunbergii

Hemicarex thunbergii, commonly known as Thunberg's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, wet meadows, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower spikes during the summer months. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Hemicarex thunbergii is valued in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
