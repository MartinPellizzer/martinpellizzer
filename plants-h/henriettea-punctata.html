Henriettea punctata

Henriettea punctata, commonly known as the spotted henriette, is a unique flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful, spotted leaves and small, delicate flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories, where it thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with distinct markings, and the flowers are often white or pale pink with a subtle fragrance.

Henriettea punctata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate with proper care.
