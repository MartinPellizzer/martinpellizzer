Horaninovia pungens

Horaninovia pungens, commonly known as the spiked bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to deep blue. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and thrives in high-altitude environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are often sparsely covered with fine hairs.

Horaninovia pungens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
