Hemigraphis schomburgkii

Hemigraphis schomburgkii, commonly known as the silver millepense, is a perennial evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features striking, velvety leaves with a distinctive silver underside and green upper surface, creating a striking visual contrast. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments and requires well-drained, moist soil.

Hemigraphis schomburgkii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in indoor and outdoor settings.
