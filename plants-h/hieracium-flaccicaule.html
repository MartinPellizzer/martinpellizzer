Hieracium flaccicaule

Hieracium flaccicaule, commonly known as the drooping hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with slender, hairy stems that often bend or droop. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish to brownish flowers, which are typically arranged in a flat-topped or oval shape. Its leaves are usually alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.
