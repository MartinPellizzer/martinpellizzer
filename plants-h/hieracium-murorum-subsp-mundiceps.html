Hieracium murorum subsp. mundiceps

Hieracium murorum subsp.

mundiceps, commonly known as the common groundsel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

The plant has a basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow flowers in the summer months. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads.
