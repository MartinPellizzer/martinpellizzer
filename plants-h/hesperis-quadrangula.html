Hesperis quadrangula

Hesperis quadrangula, commonly known as the four-cornered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants. The plant is characterized by its four-angled stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.
