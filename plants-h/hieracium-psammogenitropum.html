Hieracium psammogenitropum

Hieracium psammogenitropum, commonly known as the sand-loving hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Mediterranean.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, showcasing its adaptation to harsh, dry environments. The plant features a basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Its resilience to drought and heat makes it a common sight in disturbed or unvegetated areas.
