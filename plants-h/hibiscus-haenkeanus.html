Hibiscus haenkeanus

Hibiscus haenkeanus, commonly known as the red hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from red to pink, with five prominent petals. The plant has heart-shaped, lobed leaves that are typically dark green and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Hibiscus haenkeanus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
