Helichrysum hoffmannianum

Helichrysum hoffmannianum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that remain vibrant even when dried. The plant produces small, tubular florets surrounded by ray florets, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ability to retain color after drying has made it a valued plant in floral arrangements and traditional medicine.
