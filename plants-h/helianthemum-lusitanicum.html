Helianthemum lusitanicum

Helianthemum lusitanicum, commonly known as the Iberian sandverbena, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, often in clusters at the ends of its stems. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to withstand arid conditions.

It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.
