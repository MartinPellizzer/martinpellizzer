Helicteres angustifolia var. obtusa

Helicteres angustifolia var. obtusa, commonly known as the narrowleaf horsebean, is a variant of the horsebean plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant produces elongated, bean-like pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and herbal practices.

The variety obtusa is distinguished by its narrower leaves compared to the typical form of Helicteres angustifolia.
