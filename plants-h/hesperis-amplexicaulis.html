Hesperis amplexicaulis

Hesperis amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that tightly hug the stem. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Hesperis amplexicaulis is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
