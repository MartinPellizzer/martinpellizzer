Hieracium caesium subsp. ancotum

Hieracium caesium subsp.

ancotum, commonly known as the ancient dandelion, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flower heads and deeply lobed leaves.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its flowering period occurs from late spring to early summer, and it produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

The species is noted for its ecological importance, supporting various pollinators and contributing to biodiversity in its habitat.
