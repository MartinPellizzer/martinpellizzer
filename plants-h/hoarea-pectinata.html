Hoarea pectinata

Hoarea pectinata, commonly known as the golden cup plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow, cup-shaped inflorescence that resembles a small bowl. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered around the central cup.

Hoarea pectinata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
