Helianthus resinosus

Helianthus resinosus, commonly known as the resinous sunflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers and hairy, resinous stems. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Helianthus resinosus is often found in sandy or disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in full sun.
