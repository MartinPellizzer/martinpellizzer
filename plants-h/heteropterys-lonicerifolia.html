Heteropterys lonicerifolia

Heteropterys lonicerifolia, commonly known as the creeping honeysuckle, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit and its ability to form thickets. The plant has opposite, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and typically white or pale yellow, appearing in clusters.

The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.
