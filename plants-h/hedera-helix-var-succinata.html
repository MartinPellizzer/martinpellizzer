Hedera helix var. succinata

Hedera helix var. succinata, commonly known as the curly ivy, is a variant of the common ivy characterized by its unique, curling leaves.

This plant is a climbing evergreen vine native to Europe and parts of Asia. Its foliage is typically dark green with a slightly glossy texture, and the leaves often curl inward, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

The plant is also valued for its ability to climb walls and trellises, making it a popular choice for vertical gardening.
