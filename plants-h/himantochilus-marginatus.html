Himantochilus marginatus

Himantochilus marginatus, commonly known as the white-veined orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree bark in humid forest environments. Its leaves are elongated and often have a waxy texture, while the flowers feature white petals with distinctive green veins.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
