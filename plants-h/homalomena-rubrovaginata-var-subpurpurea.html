Homalomena rubrovaginata var. subpurpurea

Homalomena rubrovaginata var. subpurpurea, commonly known as the purple-veined arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive purple-tinged veins, giving it a striking appearance. The plant belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its spatheous inflorescence, which includes a showy spathelike leaf and a small, inconspicuous true flower. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor conditions.
