Hieracium urrobense

Hieracium urrobense, commonly known as the Urroben hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range in color from yellow to reddish-brown.

Hieracium urrobense is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environments, making it a resilient species in its native range.
