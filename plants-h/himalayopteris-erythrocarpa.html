Himalayopteris erythrocarpa

Himalayopteris erythrocarpa, commonly known as the red-veined Himalayan huperjia, is a fern native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds with prominent, reddish veins that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories at high altitudes. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of ferns.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticulture for its aesthetic qualities.
