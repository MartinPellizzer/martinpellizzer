Hibiscus mossamedensis

Hibiscus mossamedensis, commonly known as the Mossamedes hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, large, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in its dry environment. It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other hibiscus species.

Hibiscus mossamedensis is often found growing in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low precipitation.
