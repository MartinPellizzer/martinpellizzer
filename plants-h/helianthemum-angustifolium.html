Helianthemum angustifolium

Helianthemum angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved rockrose, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, tubular yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has aromatic, glandular leaves that are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is drought-tolerant.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
