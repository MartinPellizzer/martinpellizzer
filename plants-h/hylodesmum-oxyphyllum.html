Hylodesmum oxyphyllum

Hylodesmum oxyphyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rockweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. It thrives in rocky and sandy soils, often found in coastal or inland regions with a Mediterranean climate.

Hylodesmum oxyphyllum is adapted to withstand drought and salt spray, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
