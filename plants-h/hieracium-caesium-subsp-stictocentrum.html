Hieracium caesium subsp. stictocentrum

Hieracium caesium subsp.

stictocentrum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow ray florets and a dense, bushy growth habit.

The plant typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are usually basal, rough, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The species is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in agricultural and natural settings.
