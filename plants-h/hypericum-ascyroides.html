Hypericum ascyroides

Hypericum ascyroides, commonly known as the yellow hypericum, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early summer, which are composed of five petals and numerous stamens. Its flowers have a strong, pleasant fragrance and are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Hypericum ascyroides is often found in moist, woodland areas and can thrive in partial to full shade.
