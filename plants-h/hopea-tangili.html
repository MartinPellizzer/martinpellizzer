Hopea tangili

Hopea tangili, commonly known as the Malayan hopea, is a species of flowering plant in the family Dipterocarpaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the forests of Malaysia and Indonesia. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall and has a straight trunk with a smooth, greyish bark. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a glossy green upper surface and a pale underside.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, elongated fruits that contain one or two seeds.
