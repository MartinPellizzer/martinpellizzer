Hieracium levicaule subsp. lepidulum

Hieracium levicaule subsp.

lepidulum, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typically blooming from June to August. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.
