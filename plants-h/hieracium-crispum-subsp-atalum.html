Hieracium crispum subsp. atalum

Hieracium crispum subsp.

atalum, commonly known as the curly hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are hairy and often have a crinkled appearance, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and has some medicinal uses in traditional herbal remedies.
