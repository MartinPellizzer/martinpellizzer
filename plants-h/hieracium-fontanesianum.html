Hieracium fontanesianum

Hieracium fontanesianum, commonly known as the Fontanesian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, preferring temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in disturbed or nutrient-rich soils.
