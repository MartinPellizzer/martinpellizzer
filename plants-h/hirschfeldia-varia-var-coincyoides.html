Hirschfeldia varia var. coincyoides

Hirschfeldia varia var. coincyoides, commonly known as the coastal hirschfeldia, is a succulent plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, adapted to store water and resist salt spray. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the cooler months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun exposure to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in coastal gardens and rockeries.
