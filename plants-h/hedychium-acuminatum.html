Hedychium acuminatum

Hedychium acuminatum, commonly known as the flame spike ginger, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, fragrant yellow flowers that resemble torches. The plant belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other ginger species. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Hedychium acuminatum is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its use in traditional medicine.
