Hieracium × murianum

Hieracium × murianum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including meadows and roadsides. It is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it an aggressive colonizer.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and a potential weed in agricultural settings.
