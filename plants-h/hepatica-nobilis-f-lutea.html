Hepatica nobilis f. lutea

Hepatica nobilis f. lutea, commonly known as the yellow hepatica, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves and showy, early spring flowers that range from white to yellow. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. Its flowers consist of five to seven sepals that resemble petals, and it produces small, greenish-yellow fruits that contain several seeds.

Hepatica nobilis f. lutea is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
