Helicteres longifolia

Helicteres longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf helicteres, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces distinctive, spiny fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a fleshy, red aril. It thrives in moist, tropical climates and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Helicteres longifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
