Hesperhodos minutifolia subsp. stellata

Hesperhodos minutifolia subsp.

stellata, commonly known as the starflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the flowers are usually a pale yellow or white.

It is an important species for pollinators and is often found in dry, open habitats.
