Halgania argyrophylla

Halgania argyrophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf halgania, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to xerophytic conditions.

Halgania argyrophylla is often used in rock gardens and as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
