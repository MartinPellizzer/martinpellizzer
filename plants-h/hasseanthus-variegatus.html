Hasseanthus variegatus

Hasseanthus variegatus, commonly known as the variegated hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant features broad, waxy leaves with distinctive variegation patterns, often displaying shades of green, white, and yellow. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Hasseanthus variegatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
