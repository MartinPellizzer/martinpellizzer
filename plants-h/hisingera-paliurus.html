Hisingera paliurus

Hisingera paliurus, commonly known as the Indian date palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small tree or shrub that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible fruits resembling dates. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with long, narrow leaflets that give it a feathery appearance.

Hisingera paliurus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
