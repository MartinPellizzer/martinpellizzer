Hemigenia westringioides

Hemigenia westringioides, commonly known as the narrow-leafed hemigenia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a perennial herb with slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and forested areas.

Hemigenia westringioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
