Hedyotis teretifolia

Hedyotis teretifolia, commonly known as the slender bladdernut, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream sides. The plant features small, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are white or pale yellow and are clustered in dense, racemose inflorescences.

The fruit is a three-lobed capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.
