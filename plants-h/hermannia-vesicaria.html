Hermannia vesicaria

Hermannia vesicaria, commonly known as the bladderleaf or bladderleaf geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or red. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain its seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Hermannia vesicaria is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
