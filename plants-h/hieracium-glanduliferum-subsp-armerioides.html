Hieracium glanduliferum subsp. armerioides

Hieracium glanduliferum subsp.

armerioides, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and yellow-orange flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and roadsides. It has a creeping rootstock and produces numerous flowering stems that bear small, clustered flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its glandular hairs and slightly different leaf morphology compared to other Hieracium species.
