Hieracium magyaricum subsp. tephrops

Hieracium magyaricum subsp.

tephrops, commonly known as the Hungarian hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in grasslands and open habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowering period occurs in late summer.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
