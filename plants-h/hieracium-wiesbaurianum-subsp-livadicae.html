Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp. livadicae

Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp.

livadicae, commonly known as the Balkan hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to southeastern Europe. It typically grows in calcareous grasslands and open woodland habitats.

The plant features upright stems with rough, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in clustered heads. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a serrated margin.

It is characterized by its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
