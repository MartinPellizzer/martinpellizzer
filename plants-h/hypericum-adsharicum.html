Hypericum adsharicum

Hypericum adsharicum, commonly known as the Kashmir rose or red-stemmed St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its reddish stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, oval-shaped berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Hypericum adsharicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in some regions for medicinal purposes.
