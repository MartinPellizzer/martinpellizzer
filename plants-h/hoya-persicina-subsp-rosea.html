Hoya persicina subsp. rosea

Hoya persicina subsp.

rosea, commonly known as the rose-flowered wax plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asclepiadaceae. It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Malaysia and Indonesia.

The plant is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It is a popular houseplant due to its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its decorative, waxy foliage.

The flowers emit a subtle fragrance, making it an attractive addition to indoor gardens.
