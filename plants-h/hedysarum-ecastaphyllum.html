Hedysarum ecastaphyllum

Hedysarum ecastaphyllum, commonly known as sweet clover, is a perennial legume native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive sweet scent, and its flowers are typically purple or pink, arranged in dense, racemose inflorescences. The plant has a robust root system that helps in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

Hedysarum ecastaphyllum is also valued for its forage qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
