Hieracium apatelium subsp. carpathorum

Hieracium apatelium subsp.

carpathorum, commonly known as the Carpathian hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Carpathian Mountains. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and forest edges, preferring temperate climates. It has small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species in its native habitat.
