Hordeum nodosum var. maroccanum

Hordeum nodosum var. maroccanum, commonly known as Moroccan nodose barley, is a variant of the nodose barley species native to North Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive nodules on the stem, which are a result of fungal symbiosis, primarily with the endophytic fungus Neotyphodium coenophialum. This plant is typically found in arid and semi-arid environments, where it thrives due to its drought-resistant properties. The grains are small and hard, often used in traditional local cuisines and as forage for livestock.

Its ecological significance lies in its role in supporting beneficial fungi and its adaptation to harsh environmental conditions.
