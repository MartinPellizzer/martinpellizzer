Harmala peganum

Harmala peganum, commonly known as the Syrian rue, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub with small, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of purple or blue flowers. The plant produces small, dark brown seeds that contain various alkaloids, including harmine and tetrahydroprotoberberine. These alkaloids have been studied for their potential medicinal and psychoactive properties.

Harmala peganum is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
