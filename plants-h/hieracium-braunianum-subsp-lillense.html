Hieracium braunianum subsp. lillense

Hieracium braunianum subsp.

lillense, commonly known as the Lillense Hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring well-drained conditions.

The plant features bright yellow flowers arranged in a single head, with ray florets that are often reflexed or rolled backward. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
