Hedyotis nigricans var. scabra

Hedyotis nigricans var. scabra, commonly known as rough-stemmed Indian mallow, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, often hairy stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and traditional medicinal uses.
