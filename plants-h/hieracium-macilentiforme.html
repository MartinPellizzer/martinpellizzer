Hieracium macilentiforme

Hieracium macilentiforme, commonly known as the slender-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and basal leaves that are typically lance-shaped. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in meadows and grassy areas.

Due to its delicate appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
