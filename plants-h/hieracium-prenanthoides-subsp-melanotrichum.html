Hieracium prenanthoides subsp. melanotrichum

Hieracium prenanthoides subsp.

melanotrichum, commonly known as the black-haired hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas and meadows, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with dense, dark-colored hairs, giving it its common name.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators.
