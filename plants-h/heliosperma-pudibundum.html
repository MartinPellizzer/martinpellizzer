Heliosperma pudibundum

Heliosperma pudibundum, commonly known as the shy sunflower, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and sunny borders.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is cultivated in many botanical gardens around the world.
