Hyptis passerina var. selloi

Hyptis passerina var. selloi, commonly known as the Sello's hyptis, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, opposite leaves that are often hairy in texture. Its flowers are typically clustered in whorls and produce a mild, mint-like fragrance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.
