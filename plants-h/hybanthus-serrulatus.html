Hybanthus serrulatus

Hybanthus serrulatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hybanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are typically narrow and serrated, giving the plant its scientific name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited use in traditional medicine.
