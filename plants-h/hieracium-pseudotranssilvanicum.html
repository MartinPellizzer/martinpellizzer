Hieracium pseudotranssilvanicum

Hieracium pseudotranssilvanicum, commonly known as the Transylvanian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring well-drained, sunny habitats. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are yellow and clustered in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
