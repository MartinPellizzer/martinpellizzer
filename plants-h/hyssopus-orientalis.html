Hyssopus orientalis

Hyssopus orientalis, commonly known as wild hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often grayish-green in color. The plant produces spikes of blue or violet flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and expectorant qualities.

Hyssopus orientalis thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and borders.
