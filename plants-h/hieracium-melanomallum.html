Hieracium melanomallum

Hieracium melanomallum, commonly known as the black-haired hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and often have a bluish-gray hue, while the flowers are yellow with dark veins.

Hieracium melanomallum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
