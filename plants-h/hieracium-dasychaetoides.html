Hieracium dasychaetoides

Hieracium dasychaetoides, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance.

The flowers are yellow and bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators.
