Hieracium viveliense

Hieracium viveliense, commonly known as the viveliense hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and along streambanks, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium viveliense is often found in disturbed habitats and can spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain areas.
