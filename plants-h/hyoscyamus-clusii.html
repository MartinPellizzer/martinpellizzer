Hyoscyamus clusii

Hyoscyamus clusii, commonly known as the African angel's trumpets, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant produces rough, ridged leaves and has an erect, branching stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or arid environments.

Hyoscyamus clusii is part of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially hazardous to humans and animals.
