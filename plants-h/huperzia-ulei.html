Huperzia ulei

Huperzia ulei, commonly known as the narrow-leaved huperzia, is a species of clubmoss belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and linear, lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly curled at the edges. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are clustered in dense, spike-like formations along the stems. Huperzia ulei is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices.

Its slow growth and unique morphology make it an interesting subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
