Helleborine purpurata

Helleborine purpurata, commonly known as the purple helleborine, is a species of orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows. The plant features dark green, lance-shaped leaves and produces striking violet or purple flowers with intricate patterns. Its flowers are often fragrant and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Helleborine purpurata is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
