Hutchinsia puberula

Hutchinsia puberula, commonly known as the slender hutchinsia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a pubescent appearance. Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically pink or purple, arranged in whorled clusters along the stems.

Hutchinsia puberula is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils.
