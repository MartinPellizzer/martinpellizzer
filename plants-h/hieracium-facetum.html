Hieracium facetum

Hieracium facetum, commonly known as the smooth hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically smooth and hairless. It produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer.

Hieracium facetum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and seed dispersal.
