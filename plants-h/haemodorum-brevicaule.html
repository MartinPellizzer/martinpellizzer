Haemodorum brevicaule

Haemodorum brevicaule, commonly known as the small-leaf everlasting or dwarf honeywort, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, red or orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in native gardens.
