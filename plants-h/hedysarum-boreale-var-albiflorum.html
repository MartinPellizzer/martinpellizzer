Hedysarum boreale var. albiflorum

Hedysarum boreale var. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered vetch, is a flowering plant native to northern regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters. Its root system is well-developed, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
