Hymenolobium alagoanum var. alagoanum

Hymenolobium alagoanum var. alagoanum, commonly known as the Alagoas chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant features a straight trunk with a rounded crown and produces small, yellowish-green flowers during the spring. Its fruit is a legume that splits open to release small, hard seeds.

This species is valued for its timber and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
