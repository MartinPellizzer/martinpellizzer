Hemerocallis sendaica

Hemerocallis sendaica, commonly known as the Sendaica daylily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces bright yellow to orange flowers with a distinctive orange throat, making it a striking addition to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, exhibiting good resistance to pests and diseases.

Hemerocallis sendaica is valued for its ornamental qualities and ease of cultivation in temperate climates.
