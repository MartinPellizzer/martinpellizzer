Hieracium saxifragum subsp. geminiflorum

Hieracium saxifragum subsp.

geminiflorum, commonly known as the twin-flowered hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in alpine or subalpine regions.

The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces multiple flowering stems with yellow composite flowers. Its inflorescence is characterized by the presence of two rows of florets, giving it the "twin-flowered" appearance.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and wind-pollinated flowers.
