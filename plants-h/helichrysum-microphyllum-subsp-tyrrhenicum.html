Helichrysum microphyllum subsp. tyrrhenicum

Helichrysum microphyllum subsp.

tyrrhenicum, commonly known as the Tyrrhenian everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe, particularly Italy. It is characterized by its small, densely packed yellow flowers that are often arranged in compact, spherical clusters.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are usually covered in a fine layer of white or grayish hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
