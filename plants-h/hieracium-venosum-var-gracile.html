Hieracium venosum var. gracile

Hieracium venosum var. gracile, commonly known as slender wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive veining pattern, and produces delicate yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes other flowering plants with similar floral structures.

Its botanical characteristics make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
