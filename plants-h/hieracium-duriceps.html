Hieracium duriceps

Hieracium duriceps, commonly known as the tough hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are rough and hairy, often with a reddish or purplish hue, while its flowers are yellow and daisy-like.

Hieracium duriceps is known for its resilience, capable of surviving in poor soil conditions and enduring harsh weather.
