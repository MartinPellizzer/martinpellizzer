Hieracium kalmii var. subintegrum

Hieracium kalmii var. subintegrum, commonly known as the Kalm's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rough, hairy texture, and its flowers are yellow, often with a distinctive central disc.

It is a herbaceous plant that produces numerous seeds dispersed by wind.
