Hypericum stylosum

Hypericum stylosum, commonly known as the narrowleaf St. John's wort, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has opposite, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or along roadsides.

Hypericum stylosum is part of the Hippocastanaceae family and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
