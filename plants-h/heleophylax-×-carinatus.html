Heleophylax × carinatus

Heleophylax × carinatus, commonly known as the crested lily, is a hybrid orchid species that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It typically features a compact, pseudobulbous structure with fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces striking, showy flowers with a distinctive crested or ridged appearance on the petals. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
