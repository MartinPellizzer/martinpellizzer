Hypericum momoseanum

Hypericum momoseanum, commonly known as the yellow hypericum, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Hypericaceae and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its stems are often reddish or purple, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
