Hedysarum semenowii

Hedysarum semenowii, commonly known as the Semenov's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to semi-arid and alpine environments.

Hedysarum semenowii plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest for ecological restoration projects.
