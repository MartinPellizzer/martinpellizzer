Hieracium murorum subsp. pusilliceps

Hieracium murorum subsp.

pusilliceps, commonly known as the small-headed mouse-ear hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-gray leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clustered inflorescences, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and protected species in several European countries.
