Hieracium alpinoides

Hieracium alpinoides, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky areas, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often hairy.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
