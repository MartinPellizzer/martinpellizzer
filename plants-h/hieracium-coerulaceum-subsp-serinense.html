Hieracium coerulaceum subsp. serinense

Hieracium coerulaceum subsp.

serinense, commonly known as the blue hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its blue to purplish flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring well-drained conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique coloration and distribution.
