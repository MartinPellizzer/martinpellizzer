Hepetis brongniartiana

Hepetis brongniartiana, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its blue-violet flower clusters that appear in late summer and early fall. The plant has slender, branching stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

Hepetis brongniartiana is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
