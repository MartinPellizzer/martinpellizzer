Heteropogon besukiensis

Heteropogon besukiensis, commonly known as the Besuki grass, is a perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often reddish in color.

Heteropogon besukiensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms in its native ecosystems.
