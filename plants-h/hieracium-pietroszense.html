Hieracium pietroszense

Hieracium pietroszense, commonly known as the Polish hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along riverbanks. It has dark green, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
