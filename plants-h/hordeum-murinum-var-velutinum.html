Hordeum murinum var. velutinum

Hordeum murinum var. velutinum, commonly known as mouse barley, is a grass species characterized by its hairy, bristly appearance.

It is a perennial plant that thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands, roadsides, and wastelands. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive texture.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
