Helichrysum attenuatum var. lacteum

Helichrysum attenuatum var. lacteum, commonly known as the milkweed helichrysum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and hairy, linear leaves. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
