Hypericum corymbosum

Hypericum corymbosum, commonly known as American St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and opposite, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in the summer, which are adorned with five petals and numerous stamens. Its botanical characteristics include a rich supply of hypericin and pseudohypericin, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Hypericum corymbosum thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy and adaptable species.
