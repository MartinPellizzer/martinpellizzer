Hemerocallis graminea

Hemerocallis graminea, commonly known as the grassy daylily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, with each flower lasting only one day. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

Hemerocallis graminea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature in gardens.
