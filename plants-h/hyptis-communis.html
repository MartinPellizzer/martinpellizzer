Hyptis communis

Hyptis communis, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and sage. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Hyptis communis is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
