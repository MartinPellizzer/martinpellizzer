Hosta albomarginata f. subchrocea

Hosta albomarginata f. subchrocea, commonly known as the white-edged hosta with a pale green center, is a varietal form of the hosta plant characterized by its unique leaf coloration.

The leaves are broadly ovate with a white margin and a pale green center, creating a striking visual contrast. It typically grows to a height of about 12 to 18 inches, with a spread of around 18 to 24 inches. This plant thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

It is a popular choice for gardeners looking to add texture and color to shaded garden spaces.
