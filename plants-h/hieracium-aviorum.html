Hieracium aviorum

Hieracium aviorum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often reddish in color.

Hieracium aviorum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun conditions.
