Humboltia hastulata

Humboltia hastulata, commonly known as the arrowhead plant, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It is characterized by its narrow, arrow-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Humboltia hastulata typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along the shoreline, thriving in coastal climates.

It is a perennial herb that is often found in association with other coastal flora in its native habitat.
