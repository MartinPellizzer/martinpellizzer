Hieracium brachylepium

Hieracium brachylepium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 40 cm. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom in clusters.

It is known for its ability to spread via rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
