Hylotelephium argutum

Hylotelephium argutum, commonly known as the Armenian carpet or fuzzy stonecrop, is a perennial succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It features fleshy, often hairy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, adapted to store water and thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or purple during the summer months. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Hylotelephium argutum prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
