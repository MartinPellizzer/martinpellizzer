Hepetis poeppigiana

Hepetis poeppigiana, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a branching stem that often grows from the base of the plant.
