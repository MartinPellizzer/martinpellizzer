Hermione syracusana

Hermione syracusana, commonly known as the Sicilian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

Hermione syracusana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
