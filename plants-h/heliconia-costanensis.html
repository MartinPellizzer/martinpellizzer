Heliconia costanensis

Heliconia costanensis, commonly known as the Costa Rican heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its large, brightly colored bracts that resemble petals. The plant has a robust rhizomatous root system, which helps it thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by colorful, modified leaves called bracts.

Heliconia costanensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and plays a role in supporting local wildlife.
