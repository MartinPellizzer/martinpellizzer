Hieracium laevigatum subsp. glareosum

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

glareosum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flower heads, which are typically composed of numerous small florets.

The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Its reproductive strategy involves wind pollination, and it produces seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread across various regions.
