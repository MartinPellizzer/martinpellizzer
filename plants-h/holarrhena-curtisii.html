Holarrhena curtisii

Holarrhena curtisii, commonly known as the African bloodwood, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its thick, flaky bark and can grow up to 20 meters in height. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers and small, round fruits that contain a single seed. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy texture.

Holarrhena curtisii is valued for its hard, heavy wood and its ability to thrive in arid conditions.
