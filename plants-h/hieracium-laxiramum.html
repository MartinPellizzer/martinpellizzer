Hieracium laxiramum

Hieracium laxiramum, commonly known as the loosestem hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, hairy stems and produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are characteristic of the genus Hieracium. Its leaves are generally lance-shaped and have a rough texture.

Hieracium laxiramum is often considered a weed due to its ability to spread rapidly in various habitats.
