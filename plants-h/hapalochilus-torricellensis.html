Hapalochilus torricellensis

Hapalochilus torricellensis, commonly known as the Torricella orchid, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate floral structure, with showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically features a pseudobulb and produces a single or few racemes of flowers. Its leaves are usually elongated and grass-like, adapted to its high-altitude environment.

Hapalochilus torricellensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
