Hieracium periphanoides subsp. subniveum

Hieracium periphanoides subsp.

subniveum, commonly known as the snow-white hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges.

The plant features upright stems that are covered in fine, white to pale yellow hairs, giving it a fluffy appearance. Its basal leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while the upper leaves are smaller and more elongated.

The flowers are yellow and borne in dense, flat-topped clusters, making it a distinctive and ornamental plant.
