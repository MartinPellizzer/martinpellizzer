Hesperocyparis arizonica var. montana

Hesperocyparis arizonica var. montana, commonly known as the Arizona white fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves, which are typically blue-green in color and have a distinct white stomatal band. The tree produces small, spherical cones that mature to a dark brown color. It thrives in well-drained soils and has a moderate to fast growth rate.

This variety is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
