Hippia repens

Hippia repens, commonly known as creeping thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features small, fragrant leaves and flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit, spreading across the ground through stolons. Its leaves are often used in culinary applications due to their aromatic properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and opposite leaves.
