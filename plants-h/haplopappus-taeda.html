Haplopappus taeda

Haplopappus taeda, commonly known as the narrowleaf horsebrush, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations such as hairy leaves to reduce water loss. Its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish flowers are typical of the genus Haplopappus.

The plant is often found in rocky soils and is well-suited to harsh, dry conditions.
