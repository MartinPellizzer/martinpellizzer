Hieracium sandbergii

Hieracium sandbergii, commonly known as Sandberg's hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are usually hairy and lance-shaped.

Hieracium sandbergii is often found in regions with a Mediterranean climate, thriving in well-drained soils.
