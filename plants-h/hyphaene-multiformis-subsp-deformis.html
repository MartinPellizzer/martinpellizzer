Hyphaene multiformis subsp. deformis

Hyphaene multiformis subsp.

deformis, commonly known as the twisted papyrus palm, is a member of the Arecaceae family. It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its unique, twisted stems and pinnate leaves.

The plant has a slender trunk that often becomes branched and is covered with a fibrous sheath. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a dark green color and a serrated edge.

This species is adapted to wetland environments and is often found in marshy areas along rivers.
