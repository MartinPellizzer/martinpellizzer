Hypericum galioides var. reductum

Hypericum galioides var. reductum, commonly known as the narrowleaf St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and habitat restoration projects.
