Hypoestes lanata

Hypoestes lanata, commonly known as the woolly nectarine or woolly pimpernel, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant has hairy, glandular stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped with a slightly waxy texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by small, round fruits.

Hypoestes lanata thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
