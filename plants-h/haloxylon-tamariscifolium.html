Haloxylon tamariscifolium

Haloxylon tamariscifolium, commonly known as the desert date tree, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its dense, multi-stemmed structure and its ability to thrive in harsh, saline environments. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss, and its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow. It produces small, fleshy fruits that are adapted for dispersal by animals.

Haloxylon tamariscifolium plays an important ecological role in stabilizing desert soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
