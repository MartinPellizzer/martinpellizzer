Hypericum × inodorum

Hypericum × inodorum, commonly known as the sweet shrub, is a hybrid species within the genus Hypericum, characterized by its aromatic foliage and showy yellow flowers.

It is native to Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features woody stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is also valued for its use in traditional herbal medicine due to its purported health benefits.
