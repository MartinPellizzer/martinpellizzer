Hemarthria hamiltoniana

Hemarthria hamiltoniana, commonly known as Hamilton's panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the warmer regions of the Americas.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.

Its botanical structure is adapted to tolerate fluctuating water levels and is an important component of wetland ecosystems.
