Hymenophyllum sabinifolium

Hymenophyllum sabinifolium, commonly known as the sabinifolium fern, is a small, epiphytic fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features delicate, lace-like fronds with a soft, velvety texture, often covered in fine, hair-like structures called trichomes. The plant thrives in shaded, humid environments, typically growing on the trunks of trees or on rocks. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are clustered on the undersides of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

This species is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
