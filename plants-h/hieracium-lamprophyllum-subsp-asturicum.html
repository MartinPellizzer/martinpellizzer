Hieracium lamprophyllum subsp. asturicum

Hieracium lamprophyllum subsp.

asturicum, commonly known as the Asturian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas and rocky soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disc.

This subspecies is notable for its limited distribution and is considered a regional endemic species.
