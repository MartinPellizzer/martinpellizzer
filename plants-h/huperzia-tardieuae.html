Huperzia tardieuae

Huperzia tardieuae, commonly known as the late-flowering huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its simple, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including countries like India and China. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Huperzia tardieuae is notable for its ability to retain water, which helps it survive in humid, low-light conditions.
