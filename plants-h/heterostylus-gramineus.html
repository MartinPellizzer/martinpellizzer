Heterostylus gramineus

Heterostylus gramineus, commonly known as the tall false brome, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the Poaceae family. Heterostylus gramineus is often found in moist, grassy habitats such as meadows and riverbanks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various insects and small vertebrates.
