Hirculus serpyllifolius subsp. chrysanthus

Hirculus serpyllifolius subsp.

chrysanthus, commonly known as the golden yarrow, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and is native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia.

The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and rocky habitats.
