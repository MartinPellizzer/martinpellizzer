Hippeastrum reticulatum var. striatifolium

Hippeastrum reticulatum var. striatifolium, commonly known as the Zebra Lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, striped foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, often with a contrasting stripe down the center. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and container planting.

Its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements make it a favored ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical climates.
