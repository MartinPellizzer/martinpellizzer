Harleya oxylepis

Harleya oxylepis, commonly known as the "Carnival Orchid," is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Costa Rica and Panama.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful flowers that range from red to orange and yellow, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, often with a waxy texture to help retain moisture.

Harleya oxylepis is prized in horticulture for its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
