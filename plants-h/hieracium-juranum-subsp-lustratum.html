Hieracium juranum subsp. lustratum

Hieracium juranum subsp.

lustratum, commonly known as the glossy hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Central Europe. It features bright yellow flowers that bloom from June to August, typically in clusters at the ends of its stems.

The leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and often have a distinctive glossy appearance, contributing to its common name. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
