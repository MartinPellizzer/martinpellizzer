Hymenophyllum ciliatum var. majus

Hymenophyllum ciliatum var. majus, commonly known as the giant hairy maidenhair fern, is a variant of the hairy maidenhair fern.

It is a small, delicate fern that typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often on tree trunks or rocks. The fronds are finely divided and covered with soft, hairy structures called trichomes. It belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae and is characterized by its unique, intricate leaf structure.

This plant is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in high humidity and indirect light.
