Hypericum metroi

Hypericum metroi, commonly known as the Sierra hypericum, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in the summer months. Its botanical characteristics include yellow, five-petaled flowers and woody stems.

Hypericum metroi is often found in rocky soils and open habitats, such as chaparral and sagebrush environments.
