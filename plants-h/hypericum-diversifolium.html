Hypericum diversifolium

Hypericum diversifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved St. John's wort, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late summer. Its foliage is characterized by a distinctive narrow, linear shape, and it thrives in moist, acidic soils.

It is often found in wetlands and along stream banks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
