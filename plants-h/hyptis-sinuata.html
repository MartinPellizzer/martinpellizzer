Hyptis sinuata

Hyptis sinuata, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Hyptis sinuata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Its essential oils are also valued in aromatherapy and as a natural insect repellent.
