Holoschoenus atrovirens

Holoschoenus atrovirens, commonly known as the black-green flax, is a perennial grass native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dark green, narrow leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in wet, swampy environments and is often found in freshwater habitats. It has fibrous roots that help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Holoschoenus atrovirens plays an important role in the ecological balance of its native wetlands.
