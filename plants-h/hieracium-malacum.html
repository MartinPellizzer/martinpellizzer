Hieracium malacum

Hieracium malacum, commonly known as the black hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often hairy. Hieracium malacum produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers that bloom in late summer.

It is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
