Hieracium vulgatum subsp. barbatulum

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

barbatulum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The subspecies is notable for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in agricultural and natural settings.
