Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp. semicinerascens

Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp.

semicinerascens, commonly known as the purple hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, pastures, and along riverbanks, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually purple or pink, with ray florets that are longer than the disk florets.

The subspecies is distinguished by its gradually fading leaf color and slightly hairy stems.
