Hesperochiron pumilus f. fulcratus

Hesperochiron pumilus f. fulcratus, commonly known as the dwarf winged bean, is a rare and unique botanical variant of the winged bean species.

It is characterized by its small, compact growth habit and distinctive, winged leaves that are narrower than those of the typical species. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by kidney-shaped pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring well-drained soil and ample sunlight.

Due to its limited distribution and specific growing conditions, it is a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists alike.
