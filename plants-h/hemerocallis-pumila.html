Hemerocallis pumila

Hemerocallis pumila, commonly known as the dwarf daylily, is a low-growing perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically blooms in late summer and has a compact growth habit, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, preferring full sun to partial shade.

Hemerocallis pumila is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation in gardens.
