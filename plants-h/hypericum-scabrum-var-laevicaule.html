Hypericum scabrum var. laevicaule

Hypericum scabrum var. laevicaule, commonly known as smooth St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub characterized by its woody stems and small, yellowish-orange flowers.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is native to regions of Europe and western Asia. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and slightly hairy beneath. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is often found in open woodlands or along roadsides.

The species is notable for its medicinal properties, though it contains compounds that can be toxic if ingested in large quantities.
