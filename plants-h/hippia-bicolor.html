Hippia bicolor

Hippia bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, contrasting colors on its petals and sepals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as cloud forests, where it thrives in the humid climate. Its flowers are small but vibrant, often displaying shades of white, yellow, and green.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Hippia bicolor is considered a rare and endangered species.
