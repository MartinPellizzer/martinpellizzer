Hymenophyllum hemidimorphum

Hymenophyllum hemidimorphum, commonly known as the slender fern, is a small, delicate fern belonging to the Hymenophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, grass-like fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. This species thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on rocks or tree trunks in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spores are produced on the underside of its leaves in small, clustered structures called sori.

The plant's unique morphology and habitat preferences make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
