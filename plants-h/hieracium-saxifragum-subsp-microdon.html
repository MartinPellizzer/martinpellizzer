Hieracium saxifragum subsp. microdon

Hieracium saxifragum subsp.

microdon, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight.

The plant has dark green, hairy leaves that are lance-shaped and often clustered at the base. Its flowers are yellow, with a distinctive central disk surrounded by ray florets, and bloom from June to August.

It is characterized by its small, compact stature and is often found in alpine meadows and scree slopes.
