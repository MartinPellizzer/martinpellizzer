Hemicarpha micrantha var. aristulata

Hemicarpha micrantha var. aristulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sandburs, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in sandy or disturbed soils and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spiny fruits. The plant produces spikelets with bracts that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It is a member of the Poaceae family and is adapted to thrive in coastal and inland dune environments.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing sand dunes and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
