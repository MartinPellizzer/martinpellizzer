Haageocereus bylesianus

Haageocereus bylesianus, commonly known as the Bylesian night-blooming cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are typically covered in white spines. The plant produces large, showy flowers that open at night, usually in shades of white or pale yellow. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Its unique blooming pattern and striking appearance make it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and botanical gardens.
