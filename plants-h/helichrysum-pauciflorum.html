Helichrysum pauciflorum

Helichrysum pauciflorum, commonly known as the Cape goldcup, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, golden-yellow flowers that resemble tiny cups. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is usually gray-green, with hairy leaves that help reduce water loss.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
