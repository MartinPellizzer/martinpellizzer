Hieracium subcongenitum

Hieracium subcongenitum, commonly known as the clustered hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Hieracium subcongenitum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
