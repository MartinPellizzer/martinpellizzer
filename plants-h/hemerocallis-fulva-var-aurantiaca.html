Hemerocallis fulva var. aurantiaca

Hemerocallis fulva var. aurantiaca, commonly known as the orange daylily, is a vibrant perennial plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, orange-red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces long, slender stems that can reach up to 3 feet in height. It is well-suited for gardens due to its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

This variety is prized for its striking color and long-lasting blooms.
