Hymenorebutia pseudocachensis

Hymenorebutia pseudocachensis, commonly known as the Cachanilla, is a species of cactus native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly hair, which help to reduce water loss in its arid environment. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and full sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated by succulent enthusiasts.
