Helianthus × laetiflorus var. subrhomboideus

Helianthus × laetiflorus var. subrhomboideus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sunflower, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces large, showy yellow-orange composite flowers with a prominent central disk. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet, with sturdy stems that are often branched. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
