Heliotropium wagneri

Heliotropium wagneri, commonly known as Wagner's heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

Heliotropium wagneri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
