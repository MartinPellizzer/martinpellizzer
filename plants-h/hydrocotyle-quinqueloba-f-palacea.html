Hydrocotyle quinqueloba f. palacea

Hydrocotyle quinqueloba f. palacea, commonly known as the Palace Hydrocotyle, is a variant of the five-lobed marsh penny.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray leaves that are typically rounded or heart-shaped. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments such as wetlands, stream banks, and forest floors. It produces small, white flowers with five petals, which bloom in early summer.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and attractive addition to aquatic or bog gardens.
