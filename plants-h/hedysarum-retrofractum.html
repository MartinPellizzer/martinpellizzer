Hedysarum retrofractum

Hedysarum retrofractum, commonly known as the yellow peavine, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Hedysarum retrofractum is typically found in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy addition to alpine gardens.
