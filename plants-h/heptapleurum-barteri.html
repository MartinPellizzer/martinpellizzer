Heptapleurum barteri

Heptapleurum barteri, commonly known as the African nettle, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. Its roots are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
