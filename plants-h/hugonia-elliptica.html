Hugonia elliptica

Hugonia elliptica, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water plantain, is a perennial aquatic plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in wetlands, ditches, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually submerged or floating, with a waxy surface to reduce water loss. Its small, white flowers bloom in clusters during the summer months, and it produces oval, dark brown seeds that are dispersed by water.

Hugonia elliptica plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms and helping to stabilize shoreline sediments.
