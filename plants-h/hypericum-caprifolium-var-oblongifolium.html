Hypericum caprifolium var. oblongifolium

Hypericum caprifolium var. oblongifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy beneath. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its foliage is typically aromatic and may have a slightly resinous scent.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky habitats.
