Hieracium gibellianum

Hieracium gibellianum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow composite flowers that consist of both ray and disk florets. Native to Europe, it thrives in meadows and grassy areas, often forming dense colonies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces numerous small seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

It is a perennial species that blooms from late spring to early autumn.
