Holodiscus discolor var. discolor

Holodiscus discolor var. discolor, commonly known as the variable hollyhock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, rose-like flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
