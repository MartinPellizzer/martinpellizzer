Hieracium fulcratum subsp. ruebelianum

Hieracium fulcratum subsp.

ruebelianum, commonly known as the Ruebel's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a hairy texture, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer.

It is often found in areas with moderate moisture and well-drained soil.
