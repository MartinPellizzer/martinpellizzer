Hieracium grisebachii

Hieracium grisebachii, commonly known as the gray hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers and hairy, often reddish stems. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, bract-like structures beneath its flowers.

Hieracium grisebachii is often found in temperate climates and is known for its resilience in various environmental conditions.
