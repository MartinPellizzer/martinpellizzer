Hessea undosa

Hessea undosa, commonly known as the undulate lily, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, undulating leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Hessea undosa thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in dune environments.

It is adapted to withstand salt spray and coastal winds, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
