Hyoscyamus senecionis var. senecionis

Hyoscyamus senecionis var. senecionis, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, typically in clusters at the ends of its branched stems. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often glandular and emit a strong, disagreeable odor. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

The species is known for its toxic properties, containing various tropane alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
