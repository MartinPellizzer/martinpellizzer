Hieracium funerale

Hieracium funerale, commonly known as the funeral bell, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer, and its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture. Hieracium funerale is characterized by its milky sap and can grow up to 60 cm tall.

It is often considered a weed but has historical uses in traditional medicine.
