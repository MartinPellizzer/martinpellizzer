Hieracium murorum subsp. nuoliense

Hieracium murorum subsp.

nuoliense, commonly known as the Northern Field Hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern Europe. It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, pastures, and roadsides.

The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and are followed by oval, hairy fruits.

It is characterized by its robust growth and adaptability to various soil types.
