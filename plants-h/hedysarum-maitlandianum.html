Hedysarum maitlandianum

Hedysarum maitlandianum, commonly known as Maitland's vetch, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellowish-white flowers in early summer. Its root system is fibrous and can form dense colonies, making it a persistent weed in agricultural settings.

Hedysarum maitlandianum is adapted to a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.
