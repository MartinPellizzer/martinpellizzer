Heliophila amplexicaulis

Heliophila amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping heliophyte, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, sunny environments such as meadows and wetlands. The plant features slender, upright stems with whorled leaves that often wrap around the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, white to pale blue flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

Heliophila amplexicaulis is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of soil types as long as there is adequate moisture.
