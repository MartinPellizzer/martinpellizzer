Hieracium lepidiceps var. vietulifolium

Hieracium lepidiceps var. vietulifolium, commonly known as the Vietnamese hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its clustered growth habit and yellow flower heads. The plant features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

Its flowers bloom in late summer, producing small, composite flowers that attract various pollinators.
