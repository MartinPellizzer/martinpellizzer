Heliconia calatheiphylla

Heliconia calatheiphylla, commonly known as the Calatheiphylla heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its large, brightly colored bracts that enclose the small, true flowers. The plant features long, strap-like leaves that are often green with distinctive markings. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its structure resembles that of a ginger plant, with a rhizomatous root system and a tall, flowering stem.
