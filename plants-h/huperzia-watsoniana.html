Huperzia watsoniana

Huperzia watsoniana, commonly known as the Chinese clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is a low-growing, evergreen plant characterized by its dense, compact foliage and elongated, club-like sporophylls. Native to eastern Asia, particularly China, it thrives in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread and form dense mats.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an important component of its native habitats.
