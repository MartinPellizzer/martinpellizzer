Helicophyllum russelianum

Helicophyllum russelianum, commonly known as the spiral-leaved saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowers are small and usually pink or white, appearing in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Helicophyllum russelianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
