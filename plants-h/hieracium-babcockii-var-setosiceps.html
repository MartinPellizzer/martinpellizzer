Hieracium babcockii var. setosiceps

Hieracium babcockii var. setosiceps, commonly known as the Babcock's hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the United States, particularly found in the Great Basin region.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are usually hairy and lance-shaped, while the flowers are yellow with a central disc.
