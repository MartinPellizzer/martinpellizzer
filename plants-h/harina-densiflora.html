Harina densiflora

Harina densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered harina, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, showy flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often arranged in alternating patterns along the stem.

Harina densiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual impact.
