Heterozostera chilensis

Heterozostera chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean sea grass, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Chile.

It is a member of the Zosteraceae family, which includes various species of sea grasses. This plant typically grows in shallow, brackish waters and forms dense underwater meadows. Its leaves are long, narrow, and blade-like, adapted for underwater photosynthesis.

Heterozostera chilensis plays a vital role in stabilizing coastal sediments and providing habitat for marine life.
