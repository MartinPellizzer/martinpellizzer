Hibiscus prunifolius

Hibiscus prunifolius, commonly known as the red hibiscus or swamp hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from red to pink and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is often found in wetland habitats. Hibiscus prunifolius produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by water.

It plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, including birds and insects.
