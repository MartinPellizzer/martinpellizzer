Habenaria tweedieae

Habenaria tweedieae, commonly known as the Tweedie's orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the wet tropical forests of Australia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and requires specific conditions to thrive. Its flowers are usually pale green or white with a distinctive yellow lip, and it blooms during the warmer months.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is protected in its native range.
