Hypericum salvadorense

Hypericum salvadorense, commonly known as the Salvadoran St. John's Wort, is a shrub native to Central America, particularly El Salvador.

It belongs to the family Hypericaceae and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and woody stems. The plant typically grows in sunny, well-drained habitats such as dry forests and rocky soils. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Hypericum salvadorense is noted for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses in treating skin conditions and inflammation.
