Hyptis selloi

Hyptis selloi, commonly known as purple hyptis, is a perennial shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and ornamental plants. Hyptis selloi is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
