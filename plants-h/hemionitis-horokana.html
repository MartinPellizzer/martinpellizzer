Hemionitis horokana

Hemionitis horokana, commonly known as the red sand mat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It thrives in sandy and rocky soils, often found in desert and semi-desert environments. The plant has small, fleshy leaves that help it retain water in harsh conditions. Its flowers are typically pink or red, appearing in clusters during the spring or early summer.

Hemionitis horokana is well adapted to extreme temperatures and low precipitation, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
