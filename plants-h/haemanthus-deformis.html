Haemanthus deformis

Haemanthus deformis, commonly known as the naked lady, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that appear on leafless stems, giving it its common name. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, strap-like leaves that emerge after flowering. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its unique appearance and seasonal blooming make it a notable addition to botanical collections.
