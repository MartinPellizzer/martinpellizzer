Hampea integerrima var. appendiculata

Hampea integerrima var. appendiculata, commonly known as the appendiculate hampea, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its woody stems and alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

It is often found in humid, lowland areas and is adapted to thrive in disturbed or secondary forest habitats.
