Hieracium vulgatum subsp. constrictum

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

constrictum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm.

The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive narrowing at the base. Its flowers are yellow, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets, and it produces small, hairy achenes as fruits.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
