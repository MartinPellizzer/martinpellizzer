Hieracium murorum subsp. lepistoides

Hieracium murorum subsp.

lepistoides, commonly known as the mouse-ear hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along riverbanks. Its leaves are basal, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

It is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed habitats and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
