Hypericum maculatum var. corymbosum

Hypericum maculatum var. corymbosum, commonly known as the spotted dead-nettle, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often reddish-brown or purple-tinged beneath, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers with prominent orange-yellow stamens, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is commonly found in moist, wooded areas.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
