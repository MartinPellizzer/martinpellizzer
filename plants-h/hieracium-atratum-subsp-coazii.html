Hieracium atratum subsp. coazii

Hieracium atratum subsp.

coazii, commonly known as the black hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its dark green, hairy leaves and purple to black flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and can be found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas. It produces small, clustered flower heads that are surrounded by bracts, and its root system consists of a short rhizome and fibrous roots.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
