Hieracium murorum subsp. microps

Hieracium murorum subsp.

microps, commonly known as the small-flowered hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow flowers arranged in a composite head.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring well-drained conditions. It has a fibrous root system and produces achenes as its seeds.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural and urban environments.
