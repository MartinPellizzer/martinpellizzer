Hieracium spectandum

Hieracium spectandum, commonly known as the showy hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features bright yellow flowers with a central disk surrounded by ray florets, and it typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Hieracium spectandum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant appearance.
