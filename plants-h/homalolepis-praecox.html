Homalolepis praecox

Homalolepis praecox, commonly known as the early fringed gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinctive fringe of fine hairs along the edges.

Homalolepis praecox is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
