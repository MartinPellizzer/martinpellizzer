Hieracium pseudograniticum

Hieracium pseudograniticum, commonly known as the granitic hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. It has a low-growing habit with dark green, hairy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

Hieracium pseudograniticum is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in high-altitude ecosystems.
