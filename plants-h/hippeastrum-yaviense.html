Hippeastrum yaviense

Hippeastrum yaviense, commonly known as the Yaviense amaryllis, is a species native to South America, particularly found in Brazil.

It is a bulbous plant characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant produces large, fleshy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine by local communities. Hippeastrum yaviense thrives in well-drained soil and requires a period of dormancy to flower successfully.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners and horticulturists.
