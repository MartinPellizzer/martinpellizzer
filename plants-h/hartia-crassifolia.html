Hartia crassifolia

Hartia crassifolia, commonly known as the thickleaf hartia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun exposure, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its hardy nature, it is relatively low maintenance and adaptable to various climate conditions.
