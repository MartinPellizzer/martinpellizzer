Hypericum marginatum

Hypericum marginatum, commonly known as the marginated St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters, characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with four petals, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are usually oval-shaped with a slightly waxy texture and are often marginally toothed.

Hypericum marginatum is known for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
