Hieracium vasconicum subsp. lactescens

Hieracium vasconicum subsp.

lactescens, commonly known as the Pyrenean hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and localized species within its range.
