Hylandia dockrillii

Hylandia dockrillii, commonly known as the golden star fern, is a rare and unique fern species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, golden-yellow stipes and intricate fronds that resemble a star. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture to flourish. Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, relying on spore dispersal for propagation.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Hylandia dockrillii is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
