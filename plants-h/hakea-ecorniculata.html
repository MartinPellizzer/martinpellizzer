Hakea ecorniculata

Hakea ecorniculata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hakea, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in dense, compact clusters. The plant produces small, cylindrical flower spikes that range in color from yellow to red, depending on the species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates with hot, dry summers.

Hakea ecorniculata is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
