Hamamelis vernalis f. carnea

Hamamelis vernalis f. carnea, commonly known as the pink-flowered witch hazel, is a botanical variant of the witch hazel genus.

It is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers that bloom in late autumn or early winter. The plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of dark green, oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide seasonal color in gardens.
