Hieracium aquileiense

Hieracium aquileiense, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of tubular and ray florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer.

Hieracium aquileiense is often found in meadows and slopes at high elevations, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
