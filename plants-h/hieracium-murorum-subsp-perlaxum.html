Hieracium murorum subsp. perlaxum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

perlaxum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves that are typically hairy and covered in glandular dots.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along riverbanks.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability and is considered a common weed in various parts of Europe.
