Hymenophyllum subdimidiatum

Hymenophyllum subdimidiatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf filmy fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found on rocky or mossy substrates in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spores are released from small, clustered sporangia located on the undersides of the fronds.

This species is notable for its unique morphology and ecological adaptations, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
