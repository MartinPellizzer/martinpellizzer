Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp. ventosicum

Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp.

ventosicum, commonly known as the windhill hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
