Helicteres hispida

Helicteres hispida, commonly known as rough helicteres, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its rough, hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a serrated edge.

Helicteres hispida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
