Hesperis adenosepala

Hesperis adenosepala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and its clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has hairy, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.

Hesperis adenosepala is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
