Hieracium caesium var. semicanum

Hieracium caesium var. semicanum, commonly known as the lesser yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant features yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, with hairy stems and leaves that are often glandular. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while the flowers consist of five petals and numerous stamens.

The plant is part of the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and wind-pollinated flowers.
