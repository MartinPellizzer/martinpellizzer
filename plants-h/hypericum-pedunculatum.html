Hypericum pedunculatum

Hypericum pedunculatum, commonly known as yellow hypericum, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, which consist of five petals and numerous stamens. The plant has opposite, simple, and elliptic leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Hypericum pedunculatum is also known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.
