Hieracium musivale

Hieracium musivale, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers, which consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Hieracium musivale is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is considered a weed in some agricultural settings.
