Hepatica nobilis var. acuta

Hepatica nobilis var. acuta, commonly known as the acute hepatica, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves and showy, early spring blooms. The flowers, which range in color from white to pink and blue, are typically borne on short stems above the foliage. The plant prefers moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a popular species in botanical gardens and native plant collections.
