Hieracium subrosulatum subsp. subrosulatum

Hieracium subrosulatum subsp.

subrosulatum, commonly known as the lesser hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering stems that bear yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. It has hairy stems and leaves that are usually lance-shaped with serrated edges.

The flowers are arranged in a single head and are composed of both ray and disk florets.
