Hieracium vippetinum

Hieracium vippetinum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers with ray florets. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, pastures, and woodland edges. It has a creeping growth habit with rhizomes that allow it to spread and form dense mats.

The flowers bloom from June to August, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
