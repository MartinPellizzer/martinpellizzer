Helichrysum achryroclinoides

Helichrysum achryroclinoides, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that remain intact even after the plant has dried, giving it a long-lasting appearance. The plant has aromatic foliage with a slight resinous scent, and its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

The species is also valued for its ornamental use and traditional medicinal properties.
