Hieracium platycola

Hieracium platycola, commonly known as the broadleaf hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are broadly ovate with a rough texture, and its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Hieracium platycola is known for its ability to spread via rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
