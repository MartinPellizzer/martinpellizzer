Hermas villosa

Hermas villosa, commonly known as the woolly hermas, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and stems, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Hermas villosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
