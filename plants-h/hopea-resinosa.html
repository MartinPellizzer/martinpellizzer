Hopea resinosa

Hopea resinosa, commonly known as the yellow ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree produces small, pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in spikes. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a smooth, glossy surface.

Hopea resinosa is also known for its ability to produce a fragrant resin, which has been used in traditional medicine and incense.
