Hymenachne acutigluma

Hymenachne acutigluma, commonly known as the slender flat sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in saturated soil conditions. Its rhizomes are often extensive, allowing it to form dense vegetation mats.

Hymenachne acutigluma plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
