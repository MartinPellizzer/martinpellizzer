Hibiscus fulgidus

Hibiscus fulgidus, commonly known as the golden hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers with a prominent red center, which bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically a deep green color. Hibiscus fulgidus thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking floral display.
