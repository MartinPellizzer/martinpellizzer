Hieracium bifidum subsp. suboligopodium

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

suboligopodium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and forest edges.

The plant has basal and stem leaves that are usually lanceolate to linear, with a hairy surface. Its flowering heads consist of yellow disc flowers surrounded by pale yellow to reddish ray florets.

It is characterized by its small, clustered inflorescences and is often found in acidic soils.
