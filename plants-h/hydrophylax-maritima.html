Hydrophylax maritima

Hydrophylax maritima, commonly known as the salt marsh willow, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in brackish or saline wetlands, often found in estuaries and tidal marshes. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered white or greenish flowers. It plays an important role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial species.

Hydrophylax maritima is adapted to withstand periodic flooding and saltwater intrusion.
