Hieracium juraniforme subsp. juranigenum

Hieracium juraniforme subsp.

juranigenum, commonly known as the Jura hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and yellowish to orange flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils. It has a low-growing habit and is often found in open areas with limited vegetation.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in several European countries.
