Heliopsis parvifolia var. rubra

Heliopsis parvifolia var. rubra, commonly known as the red-leaf false sunflower, is a variant of the heliopsis genus native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet, featuring lance-shaped leaves with a reddish tinge. The plant produces bright yellow, daisy-like flowers with dark centers, which bloom from midsummer to early fall. Its foliage is often noted for its striking red or bronze coloration, especially in cooler weather.

This variety is well-suited for full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
