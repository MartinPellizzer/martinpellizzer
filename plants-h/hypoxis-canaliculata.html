Hypoxis canaliculata

Hypoxis canaliculata, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that open at night. The plant is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its flowers emit a sweet fragrance, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

Hypoxis canaliculata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and resilience in various environmental conditions.
