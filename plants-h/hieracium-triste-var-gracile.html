Hieracium triste var. gracile

Hieracium triste var. gracile, commonly known as the slender weed, is a flowering plant belonging to the daisy family (Asteraceae).

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and fields. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Despite its common presence, it is not widely cultivated and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
