Haplopappus fruticosus

Haplopappus fruticosus, commonly known as the silverleaf gooseweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats on dry, sandy soils. The plant features small, silver-gray leaves with a woolly texture, and its flowers are yellow and clustered in small heads. It is often found in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in disturbed soils and open habitats.

The species is known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand drought conditions.
