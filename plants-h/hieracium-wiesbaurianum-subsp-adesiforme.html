Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp. adesiforme

Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp.

adesiforme, commonly known as the Wiesbaur's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosettes of leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and protected species in several countries.
