Hieracium spissidens var. aepochloum

Hieracium spissidens var. aepochloum, commonly known as the dense-haired hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and clustered, yellow-orange flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in dense, fine hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks.

This variant is distinguished by its more compact growth habit compared to other hawkweed species.
