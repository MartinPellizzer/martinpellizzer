Hydnocarpus moluccanus

Hydnocarpus moluccanus, commonly known as the Moluccan ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its thick, deeply furrowed bark and dark, heavy wood. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, fleshy fruits that contain several seeds. It is valued for its hard, durable timber and has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of natural rubber.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates with well-drained soils.
