Hibiscus calycularis

Hibiscus calycularis, commonly known as the calycular hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Hibiscus calycularis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
