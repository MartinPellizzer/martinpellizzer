Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp. glaucovatum

Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp.

glaucovatum, commonly known as the glaucous hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It typically grows in dry, grassy habitats and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant has rough, hairy foliage that often appears bluish-green due to a waxy coating. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into cylindrical, ribbed achenes.

This subspecies is part of the dandelion family (Asteraceae) and is known for its ecological adaptability in arid environments.
