Hieracium montsignaticum

Hieracium montsignaticum, commonly known as the Montsignac hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pyrenees region of southwestern France.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads made up of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats, often in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
