Hieracium savianum

Hieracium savianum, commonly known as the Savian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Hieracium savianum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in its native habitat.
