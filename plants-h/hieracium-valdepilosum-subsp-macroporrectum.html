Hieracium valdepilosum subsp. macroporrectum

Hieracium valdepilosum subsp.

macroporrectum, commonly known as the large-headed hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its large, elongated flower heads and robust, hairy stems. Native to southern Europe, it often thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and garden cultivation.
