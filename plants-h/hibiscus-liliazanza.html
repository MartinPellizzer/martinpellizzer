Hibiscus liliazanza

Hibiscus liliazanza, commonly known as the Mexican hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a rough texture.

Hibiscus liliazanza thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
