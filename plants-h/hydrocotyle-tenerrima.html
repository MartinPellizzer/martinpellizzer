Hydrocotyle tenerrima

Hydrocotyle tenerrima, commonly known as the silverleaf dewflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the early morning. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Hydrocotyle tenerrima is a perennial herb that is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
