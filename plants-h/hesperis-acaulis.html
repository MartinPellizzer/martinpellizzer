Hesperis acaulis

Hesperis acaulis, commonly known as the rock scabious, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, pink or purple flowers. Its blooming period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hesperis acaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
