Hieracium demutabile

Hieracium demutabile, commonly known as the variable hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellow or orange composite flowers, which consist of numerous small florets arranged in a flattened cluster. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. It has hairy stems and leaves that are often lobed or toothed.

Hieracium demutabile is known for its variability in leaf shape and flower color, making it a challenging species to identify.
