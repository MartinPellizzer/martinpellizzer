Hopkinsia calovaginata

Hopkinsia calovaginata, commonly known as the narrow-vessel hopkinsia, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in drought conditions.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphological features and ecological adaptations.
