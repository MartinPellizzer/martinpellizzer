Hedysarum mackenziei f. proliferum

Hedysarum mackenziei f. proliferum, commonly known as the Mackenzie's milkvetch, is a variant of the milkvetch genus native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant produces trifoliate leaves and has a fibrous root system adapted to survive in dry conditions. It is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in disturbed areas.

This variety is notable for its ability to propagate through both seeds and vegetative means, enhancing its resilience in challenging environments.
