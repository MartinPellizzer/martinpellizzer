Hydrangea robusta

Hydrangea robusta, commonly known as the roughleaf hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from white to pink, blue, or purple depending on soil pH. The plant thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes. Its leaves are typically large, ovate, and have a rough, serrated texture.

Hydrangea robusta is known for its hardiness and ability to bloom reliably in temperate climates.
