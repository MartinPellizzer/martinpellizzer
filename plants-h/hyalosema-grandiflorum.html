Hyalosema grandiflorum

Hyalosema grandiflorum, commonly known as the large-flowered hyalosema, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Hyalosema grandiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
