Hermione straminea

Hermione straminea, commonly known as the yellow star-of-Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, star-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in some areas.
