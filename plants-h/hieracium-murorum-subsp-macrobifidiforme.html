Hieracium murorum subsp. macrobifidiforme

Hieracium murorum subsp.

macrobifidiforme, commonly known as the large-lobed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosettes of leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a single head, typically blooming from June to August. Its leaves are broadly lanceolate with prominent lobes, and the plant is often found in dry, disturbed soils.

It is considered a weedy species and can form dense colonies in suitable habitats.
