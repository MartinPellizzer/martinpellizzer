Hedyotis linifolia

Hedyotis linifolia, commonly known as silverweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to various regions of North America.

It features small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes, allowing it to spread across the ground. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a silvery underside, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Hedyotis linifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and disturbed areas.
