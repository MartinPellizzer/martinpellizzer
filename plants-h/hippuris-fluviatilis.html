Hippuris fluviatilis

Hippuris fluviatilis, commonly known as the water filbert, is a perennial aquatic plant found in shallow freshwater habitats.

It has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often submerged or floating, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by insects. The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in still or slow-moving water, such as ponds and marshes. Its rhizomes form dense colonies, which help stabilize sediments and improve water quality.

Hippuris fluviatilis plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
