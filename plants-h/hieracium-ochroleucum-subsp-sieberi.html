Hieracium ochroleucum subsp. sieberi

Hieracium ochroleucum subsp.

sieberi, commonly known as Sieber's meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, while its flowering heads consist of numerous small ray florets.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bright, daisy-like appearance.
