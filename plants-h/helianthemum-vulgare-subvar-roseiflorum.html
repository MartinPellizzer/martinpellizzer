Helianthemum vulgare subvar. roseiflorum

Helianthemum vulgare subvar. roseiflorum, commonly known as the pink rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy pink to reddish-pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

The flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.
