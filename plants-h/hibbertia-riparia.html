Hibbertia riparia

Hibbertia riparia, commonly known as the river hibbertia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are typically arranged in clusters and bloom during the spring and summer seasons. The plant has slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Hibbertia riparia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found growing near rivers and in woodland areas.

It is an important plant for local biodiversity and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
