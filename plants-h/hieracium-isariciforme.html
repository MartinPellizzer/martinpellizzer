Hieracium isariciforme

Hieracium isariciforme, commonly known as the Isar Valley hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Alpine regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine meadows, where it thrives in well-drained, sunny conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer.

Hieracium isariciforme is often found in mountainous areas and is an important component of alpine ecosystems.
