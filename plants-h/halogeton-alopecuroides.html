Halogeton alopecuroides

Halogeton alopecuroides, commonly known as the desert barley, is a halophytic plant adapted to thrive in arid and saline environments.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth form, which helps reduce water loss and withstand extreme temperatures. The plant produces small, green to reddish-brown seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its survival in harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually reduced to small scales, minimizing surface area for evaporation.

Halogeton alopecuroides plays a role in stabilizing soils in desert ecosystems and is used as a forage plant in some regions.
