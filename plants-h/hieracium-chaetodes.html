Hieracium chaetodes

Hieracium chaetodes, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant features upright stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a rough texture. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Hieracium chaetodes is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade.
