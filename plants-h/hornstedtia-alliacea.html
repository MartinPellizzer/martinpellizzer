Hornstedtia alliacea

Hornstedtia alliacea, commonly known as the garlic orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that resemble garlic bulbs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are usually white or pale green.

Hornstedtia alliacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
