Haplostachys truncata

Haplostachys truncata, commonly known as the Mexican goldenbush, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, golden-yellow foliage and is often found in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, providing some protection against desiccation. Its flowers are yellow and occur in clusters, adding to its ornamental value.

Haplostachys truncata is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping.
