Hieracium laxifloccum

Hieracium laxifloccum, commonly known as the loose flocculent hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces numerous yellow flowers in a cluster. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
