Hieracium safonoviae

Hieracium safonoviae, commonly known as the Safonov's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are usually basal, forming a rosette, and are covered in fine hairs.

The flowers bloom in late summer, producing yellow or orange inflorescences that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
