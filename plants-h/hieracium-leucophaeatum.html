Hieracium leucophaeatum

Hieracium leucophaeatum, commonly known as the white-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features bright yellow flowers with white ray florets, and its leaves are typically grayish-white with a woolly texture. It thrives in sunny, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Hieracium leucophaeatum is often found in disturbed soils and can sometimes be invasive in certain regions.
