Hemionitis doryopteris

Hemionitis doryopteris, commonly known as the desert shield fern, is a unique vascular plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit and small, scale-like leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant's rhizomes are thick and fibrous, allowing it to store moisture and nutrients efficiently. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.

Due to its specialized adaptations, Hemionitis doryopteris plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native range.
