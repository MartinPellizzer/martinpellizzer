Hedyotis rupestris

Hedyotis rupestris, commonly known as the rocky pennywort, is a small, perennial herb native to various regions in Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It typically grows in rocky or shaded environments, often on slopes or in forest understories. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters. Its medicinal properties have been recognized in traditional herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antifungal effects.

Hedyotis rupestris is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its compact growth habit and delicate foliage.
