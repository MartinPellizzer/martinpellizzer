Holcus gayanus

Holcus gayanus, commonly known as the African dog's-tail grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and its distinctive, fluffy, pale green to silver-gray seed heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green coloration.

Holcus gayanus is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various environmental conditions.
