Hieracium magnificum

Hieracium magnificum, commonly known as the magnificent hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and its stems are often hairy as well.

Hieracium magnificum is typically found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it can spread rapidly through its rhizomes.
