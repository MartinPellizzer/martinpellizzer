Hura crepitans var. strepens

Hura crepitans var. strepens, commonly known as the tropical hibiscus, is a species native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from red to yellow. The leaves are typically large, ovate, and have a rough texture, while the fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds. This plant thrives in humid, sunny environments and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental species in tropical botanic gardens.
