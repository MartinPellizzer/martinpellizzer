Hieracium murorum subsp. placerum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

placerum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. It has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy, with a rosette formation.

Its inflorescence consists of a single central flower head surrounded by leaf-like bracts.
