Hyparrhenia dissoluta

Hyparrhenia dissoluta, commonly known as the African grass or red grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.

Hyparrhenia dissoluta is often used in landscaping and as a forage crop due to its hardiness and resilience.
