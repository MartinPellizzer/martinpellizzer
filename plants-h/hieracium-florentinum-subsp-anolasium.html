Hieracium florentinum subsp. anolasium

Hieracium florentinum subsp.

anolasium, commonly known as the Florentine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, spoon-shaped leaves and produces yellow-orange composite flowers in the summer.

The plant is native to southern Europe and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a creeping rootstock that allows it to spread vegetatively, making it somewhat invasive in certain areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural practices.
