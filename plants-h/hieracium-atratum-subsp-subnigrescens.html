Hieracium atratum subsp. subnigrescens

Hieracium atratum subsp.

subnigrescens, commonly known as the black-stemmed hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its dark green, hairy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its subspecies name, subnigrescens, refers to the darkening of the stems as they mature.

It is often found in temperate climates and is relatively uncommon in the wild.
