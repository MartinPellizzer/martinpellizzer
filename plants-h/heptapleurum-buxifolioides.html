Heptapleurum buxifolioides

Heptapleurum buxifolioides, commonly known as the Japanese boxwood, is a slow-growing evergreen shrub native to East Asia.

It features dense, dark green foliage with small, oval-shaped leaves that are glossy and leathery in texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits that are attractive to birds. It is often cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its compact growth habit and ability to maintain a neat shape.

Its foliage is frequently used in horticultural designs for topiary and formal garden landscapes.
