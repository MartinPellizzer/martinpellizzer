Hakea orthorrhyncha var. orthorrhyncha

Hakea orthorrhyncha var. orthorrhyncha, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hakea, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. The plant produces spikes of small, urn-shaped flowers that are usually pink or red in color, which bloom during the spring and summer seasons. It is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system to access groundwater.

The species is also known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun exposure.
