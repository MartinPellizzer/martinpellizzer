Hemigraphis linearifolia

Hemigraphis linearifolia, commonly known as the silver shamrock, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a metallic sheen. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pink, appearing in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage.

Hemigraphis linearifolia is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
