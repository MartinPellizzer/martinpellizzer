Haplophyllum trichostylum

Haplophyllum trichostylum, commonly known as the hairy leaf euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its fleshy, hairy leaves and distinctive floral structures. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its water-storing tissues. Its small, yellowish flowers are clustered in cup-like structures called cyathia, a common feature in the Euphorbiaceae family.

Haplophyllum trichostylum is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in drought-prone regions.
