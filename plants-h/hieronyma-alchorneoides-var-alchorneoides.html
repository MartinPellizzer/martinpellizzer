Hieronyma alchorneoides var. alchorneoides

Hieronyma alchorneoides var. alchorneoides, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Pouteria alchorneoides*, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, with a dense canopy and smooth, grayish-brown bark. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, reddish-orange berries.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and as a shade tree in tropical regions.
