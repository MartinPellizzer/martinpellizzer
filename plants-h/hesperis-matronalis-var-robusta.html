Hesperis matronalis var. robusta

Hesperis matronalis var. robusta, commonly known as the robust sweet rocket, is a biennial or short-lived perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and a strong, somewhat peppery scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological gardens.
