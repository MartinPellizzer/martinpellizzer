Hieracium lapponiciforme

Hieracium lapponiciforme, commonly known as the Lapland hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments. It has hairy stems and leaves that help reduce water loss in its native habitat.

Hieracium lapponiciforme is often found in open areas such as tundra and mountain meadows.
