Helianthocereus pseudocandicans

Helianthocereus pseudocandicans, commonly known as the yellow night-blooming cereus, is a cactus species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and produces large, fragrant, yellow flowers that open at night. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with thick, fleshy stems that store water. Its flowers are pollinated by nocturnal insects such as moths and bats.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in supporting night-active pollinators.
