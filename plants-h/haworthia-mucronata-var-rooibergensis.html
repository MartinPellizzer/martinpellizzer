Haworthia mucronata var. rooibergensis

Haworthia mucronata var. rooibergensis, commonly known as the Rooiberg Haworthia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features rosettes of fleshy, triangular leaves with a distinctive red or pinkish hue when exposed to sunlight. The leaves are typically covered in a white powdery coating, known as farina, which helps reduce water loss. It produces small, greenish-white flowers on tall, slender stems during the warmer months.

This plant is well-suited for rock gardens and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
