Hemionitis perfasciculata

Hemionitis perfasciculata, commonly known as the Chinese water fern, is a small aquatic fern native to Asia.

It thrives in shallow, freshwater environments and is characterized by its delicate, floating fronds. The plant has a unique ability to form dense mats on the water surface, which can help in water purification. Its rhizomes are often submerged, anchoring the plant and absorbing nutrients from the surrounding water.

Hemionitis perfasciculata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
