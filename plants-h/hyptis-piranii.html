Hyptis piranii

Hyptis piranii, commonly known as Brazilian hyptis, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Hyptis piranii is also valued as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and blooming habit.
