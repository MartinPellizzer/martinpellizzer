Hermannia candidissima

Hermannia candidissima, commonly known as the white hermannia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal regions. The plant features slender, upright stems and produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Hermannia candidissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
