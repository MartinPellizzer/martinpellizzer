Hyparrhenia lecomtei

Hyparrhenia lecomtei, commonly known as the African silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, silver-green blades that give it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often used in traditional crafts and as ornamental elements. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.

Hyparrhenia lecomtei is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various small animals.
