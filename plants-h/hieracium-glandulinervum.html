Hieracium glandulinervum

Hieracium glandulinervum, commonly known as the glandular-veined hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves with prominent glandular veins. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the Compositae family. It is native to Europe and is often found in meadows and grassy areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
