Hostmannia essequebensis

Hostmannia essequebensis, commonly known as the Namib Desert lily, is a flowering plant native to the Namib Desert in Namibia.

It is a perennial herb with a distinctive rosette of fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, adapting to the arid environment. The plant produces tall, slender stems that bear clusters of white, star-shaped flowers during the rainy season. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is adapted to extreme temperature fluctuations.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a notable species in the region's flora.
