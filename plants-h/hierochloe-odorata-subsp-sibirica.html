Hierochloe odorata subsp. sibirica

Hierochloe odorata subsp.

sibirica, commonly known as Siberian sweetgrass, is a perennial grass native to cold climates of Eurasia. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and tundra regions.

The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces small, spike-like clusters of pale green to yellowish flowers. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses in various cultures.
