Hieracium araeolepis

Hieracium araeolepis, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy basal rosettes. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

Hieracium araeolepis is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
