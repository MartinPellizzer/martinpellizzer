Hydnophytum formicarum var. cochincinense

Hydnophytum formicarum var. cochincinense, commonly known as the ant plant, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique relationship with ants, providing them with shelter and food in exchange for protection against herbivores. The plant produces small, white flowers and yellowish fruits, which are adapted for dispersal by ants. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

This species is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
