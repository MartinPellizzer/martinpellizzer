Hieracium murorum subsp. funerellum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

funerellum, commonly known as the funeral hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

It is a perennial species that spreads through rhizomes and is often found in dry, sandy soils.
