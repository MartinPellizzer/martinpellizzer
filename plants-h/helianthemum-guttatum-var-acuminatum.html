Helianthemum guttatum var. acuminatum

Helianthemum guttatum var. acuminatum, commonly known as the acuminated stonecrop, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically green with red or purple markings, and it produces small, showy yellow flowers in the summer months. The plant is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and vibrant foliage make it a popular choice for rock gardens and ornamental landscaping.
