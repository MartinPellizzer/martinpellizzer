Haworthia pumila

Haworthia pumila, commonly known as the dwarf Haworthia or the little pigmy plant, is a species of succulent native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact rosette of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with white or pale green markings. The leaves are covered in a fine layer of white powder, which helps to reduce water loss in its arid native environment. It produces small, white flowers on tall, slender stems during the summer months.

This plant is well adapted to dry conditions and requires minimal watering, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
