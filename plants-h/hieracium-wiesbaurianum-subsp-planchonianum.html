Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp. planchonianum

Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp.

planchonianum, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow ray florets.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. It is known for its ornamental value and is often found in meadows and slopes at high altitudes.

The subspecies is distinguished by its compact growth habit and specific morphological features compared to other Hieracium species.
