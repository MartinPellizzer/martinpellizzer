Hibiscus cornetii

Hibiscus cornetii, commonly known as the Texas hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and often reddish in color.

Hibiscus cornetii is a perennial herb that plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
