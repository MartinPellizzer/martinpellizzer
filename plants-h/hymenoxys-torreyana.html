Hymenoxys torreyana

Hymenoxys torreyana, commonly known as the Torrey's globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant has a basal rosette of gray-green, woolly leaves and produces showy yellow flowers with five petals. Its flowers are borne on long, slender stalks and are pollinated primarily by bees.

Hymenoxys torreyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in arid gardens.
