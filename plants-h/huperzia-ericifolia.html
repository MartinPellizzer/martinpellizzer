Huperzia ericifolia

Huperzia ericifolia, commonly known as the Eric-like Huperzia, is a species of moss-like plant belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth form and dark green, glossy leaves. The plant is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, including parts of Asia and North America. Its leaves are typically arranged in two ranks, with the upper leaves often being smaller and more pointed.

Huperzia ericifolia thrives in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors and rocky outcrops.
