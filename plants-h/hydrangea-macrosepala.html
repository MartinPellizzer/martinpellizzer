Hydrangea macrosepala

Hydrangea macrosepala, commonly known as the mountain hydrangea, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy bracts that can range in color from white to pink, depending on soil pH. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in mountainous regions. Its flowers are produced in clusters and are surrounded by sterile, leaf-like bracts.

Hydrangea macrosepala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
