Helichrysum heterotrichum

Helichrysum heterotrichum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, yellowish-orange flower heads that resemble small suns. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically grouped into clusters, and it is known for its aromatic, honey-scented blooms. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Helichrysum heterotrichum is often used in dried floral arrangements due to its long-lasting, vibrant color.
