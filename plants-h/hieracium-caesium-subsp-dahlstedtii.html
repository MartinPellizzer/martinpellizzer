Hieracium caesium subsp. dahlstedtii

Hieracium caesium subsp.

dahlstedtii, commonly known as the Dahlstedt's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and sometimes endangered species in parts of its range.
