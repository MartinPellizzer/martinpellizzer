Hypoxis interjecta

Hypoxis interjecta, commonly known as the African star of Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the genus Hypoxis, which includes several species of groundcover plants. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellow flowers with a distinctive star-shaped pattern. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Hypoxis interjecta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural displays.
