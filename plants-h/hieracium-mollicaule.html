Hieracium mollicaule

Hieracium mollicaule, commonly known as the soft hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, soft leaves and yellow disk flowers with ray florets. Native to Europe, it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. The plant's morphology includes a basal rosette of leaves and flowering stems that rise above the foliage.

It plays a role in supporting pollinators and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
