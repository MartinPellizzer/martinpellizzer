Hieracium murorum subsp. sparsellidens

Hieracium murorum subsp.

sparsellidens, commonly known as the sparse-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, meadows, and waste areas.

The plant has basal rosettes of leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with fine hairs, while the stem is usually unbranched and somewhat hairy.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.
