Hypericum loxense var. aequatoriale

Hypericum loxense var. aequatoriale, commonly known as the Ecuadorian St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Hypericaceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and woody stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in mountainous areas. Its leaves are opposite and elliptical, with a slightly hairy surface.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
