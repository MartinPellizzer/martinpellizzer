Heckeria sidifolia var. subglabrata

Heckeria sidifolia var. subglabrata, commonly known as the silver-tipped heckeria, is a striking epiphytic bromeliad native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of white, powdery bloom, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers that emerge from the leaf axils, adding a dramatic visual contrast to its foliage. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for collectors and enthusiasts of rare tropical plants.
