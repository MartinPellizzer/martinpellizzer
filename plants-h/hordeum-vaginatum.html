Hordeum vaginatum

Hordeum vaginatum, commonly known as the naked barley or goat barley, is a wild grass species native to Eurasia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers. Hordeum vaginatum is typically found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is often considered a weed.

It has been studied for its potential in sustainable agriculture due to its adaptability and resilience to harsh conditions.
