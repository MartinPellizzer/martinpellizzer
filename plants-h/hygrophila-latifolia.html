Hygrophila latifolia

Hygrophila latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf water plant, is a semi-aquatic plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It thrives in moist environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds and rivers. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in yellow or greenish hues.

Hygrophila latifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to help purify water in aquatic ecosystems.
