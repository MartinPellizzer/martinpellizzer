Hermannia dinteri

Hermannia dinteri, commonly known as the Dinter's hermannia, is a flowering plant native to the succulent Karoo region of South Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and compact, rosette-like growth habit. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale pink flowers during the winter months, which are borne on short, erect pedicels. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Hermannia dinteri is well adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it a hardy choice for xerophytic gardens.
