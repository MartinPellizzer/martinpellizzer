Hieracium murorum subsp. divisum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

divisum, commonly known as the divided hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn. It has finely divided, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is typically found in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides.
