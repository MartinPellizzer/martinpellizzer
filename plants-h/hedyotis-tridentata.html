Hedyotis tridentata

Hedyotis tridentata, commonly known as the three-toothed geranium, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny, red or pink flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in the understory of forests. Hedyotis tridentata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
