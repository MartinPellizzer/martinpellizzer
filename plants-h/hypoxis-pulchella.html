Hypoxis pulchella

Hypoxis pulchella, commonly known as the golden star, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Hypoxidaceae and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with a distinctive star-shaped pattern, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Hypoxis pulchella is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of care.
