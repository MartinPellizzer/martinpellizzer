Heteropterys caducibracteata

Heteropterys caducibracteata, commonly known as the variable bract euphorbia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its most distinctive feature is the shedding of its bracts, which gives it the species name *caducibracteata*.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant foliage and unique floral structure.
