Hieracium napaeum

Hieracium napaeum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium napaeum is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and ability to spread easily.
