Harpullia zanguebarica

Harpullia zanguebarica, commonly known as the African mangosteen, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height, featuring a straight trunk and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that develop into round, segmented fruits with a bright red or yellow exterior. Its fruit is edible, containing a juicy, white, segmented flesh that is rich in nutrients and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
