Habenaria antennifera

Habenaria antennifera, commonly known as the feather-lobed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers with elongated, hairy petals that resemble antennae, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is noted for its intricate floral structure and ecological significance in its native habitats.
