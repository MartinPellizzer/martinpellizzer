Hakea abbreviata

Hakea abbreviata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hakea, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces distinctive yellow or orange flowers in spikes, which appear in spring. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

Hakea abbreviata is an important species for local wildlife, providing nectar for birds and insects.
