Hedera corymbosa

Hedera corymbosa, commonly known as the Chinese ivy, is a climbing evergreen vine native to eastern Asia.

It features dark green, glossy leaves that are typically heart-shaped or ovate in form. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red berries, which are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in shaded environments and is often used in horticulture for ground cover or as a climbing plant.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
