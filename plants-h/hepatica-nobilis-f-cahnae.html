Hepatica nobilis f. cahnae

Hepatica nobilis f. cahnae, commonly known as the Cahnae hepatica, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its early spring blooms of pink or white flowers with five to seven petals. The plant features heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. It thrives in moist, shaded woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and protected species in many regions.
