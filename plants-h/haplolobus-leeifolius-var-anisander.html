Haplolobus leeifolius var. anisander

Haplolobus leeifolius var. anisander, commonly known as the slender-leafed haplolobus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming during the spring and early summer. Its growth habit is generally shrub-like, with multiple stems arising from the base.

This variety is noted for its adaptability to well-drained soils and its preference for partial to full sunlight.
