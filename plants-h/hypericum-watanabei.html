Hypericum watanabei

Hypericum watanabei, commonly known as the Japanese St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Hypericaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and has oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hypericum watanabei is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
