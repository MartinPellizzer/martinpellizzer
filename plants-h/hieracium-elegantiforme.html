Hieracium elegantiforme

Hieracium elegantiforme, commonly known as the elegant hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, disturbed soils such as meadows and roadsides. The plant has narrow, ascending stems that bear the inflorescence at the top.

It is often found in temperate climates and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.
