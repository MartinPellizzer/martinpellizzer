Hippeastrum puniceum f. pyrrochroum

Hippeastrum puniceum f. pyrrochroum, commonly known as the Red Flame Hippeastrum, is a striking ornamental plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from deep red to orange-red in color. The plant produces large, showy blooms during the winter months, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening. Its foliage consists of broad, strap-like leaves that are typically dark green and lance-shaped.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements, making it suitable for both novice and experienced gardeners.
