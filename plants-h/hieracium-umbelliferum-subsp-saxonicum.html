Hieracium umbelliferum subsp. saxonicum

Hieracium umbelliferum subsp.

saxonicum, commonly known as the saxonic hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow to orange flower heads.

The plant typically has basal and cauline leaves that are lanceolate to ovate in shape, with rough or hairy surfaces. It produces a single terminal inflorescence composed of numerous small yellow flowers, which are arranged in a flat-topped or rounded cluster.

This subspecies is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
