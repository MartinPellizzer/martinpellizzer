Hieracium × monasteriale

Hieracium × monasteriale, commonly known as the monastery dandelion, is a hybrid species within the genus Hieracium, which belongs to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellow composite flowers, which consist of a central disk surrounded by ray florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are usually alternate, rough to the touch, and have a distinctive lobed or toothed appearance.

This species is often found in regions of Europe and parts of Asia, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
