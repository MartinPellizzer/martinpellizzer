Humboltia acutiflora

Humboltia acutiflora, commonly known as the Humboldt's spurge, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of South America.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its succulent leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to coastal climates with high salinity and wind exposure. Humboltia acutiflora has a distinctive branching pattern and produces small, fleshy fruits.

It is often found in dune ecosystems and plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.
