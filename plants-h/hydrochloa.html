Hydrochloa

Hydrochloa, commonly known as the water fern, is a genus of aquatic plants belonging to the family Hydrochloaceae.

These plants are typically found in freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. They are characterized by their finely divided, feathery leaves that help increase surface area for gas exchange. Hydrochloa species often form dense mats on the water surface, contributing to water clarity by reducing algal growth.

Their simple, branching stems and small, inconspicuous flowers make them well-adapted to aquatic life.
