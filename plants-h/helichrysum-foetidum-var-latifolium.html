Helichrysum foetidum var. latifolium

Helichrysum foetidum var. latifolium, commonly known as the sweet everlasting or broadleaf everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its dried flowers are used in potpourris and herbal remedies due to their long-lasting fragrance.
