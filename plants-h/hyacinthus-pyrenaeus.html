Hyacinthus pyrenaeus

Hyacinthus pyrenaeus, commonly known as the Iberian hyacinth, is a species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a bulbous perennial plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family. The plant produces striking clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers in early spring. Its leaves are long, narrow, and grass-like, emerging after the flowers have bloomed.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
