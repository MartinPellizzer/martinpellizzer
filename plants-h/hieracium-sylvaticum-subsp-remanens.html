Hieracium sylvaticum subsp. remanens

Hieracium sylvaticum subsp.

remanens, commonly known as the forest hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and forest understories.

The plant features upright stems with alternate, heart-shaped to oval leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are bright yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and are produced from late spring to early autumn.

This subspecies is characterized by its robust growth and ability to thrive in nutrient-rich soils.
