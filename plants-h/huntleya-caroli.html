Huntleya caroli

Huntleya caroli, commonly known as the mountain sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. The plant features small, clustered white flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a delicate touch to its rugged habitat. Its leaves are succulent and fleshy, adapted to conserve moisture in harsh conditions.

Huntleya caroli plays a vital role in stabilizing soil in its native ecosystems and is valued for its resilience and ornamental appeal.
