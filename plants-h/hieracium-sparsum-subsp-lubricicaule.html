Hieracium sparsum subsp. lubricicaule

Hieracium sparsum subsp.

lubricicaule, commonly known as the smooth-haired hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, meadows, and waste areas.

The plant features upright, hairy stems that bear alternate, heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and are visited by various pollinators.

It is characterized by its smooth, glandular leaves and is often found in dry, sunny environments.
