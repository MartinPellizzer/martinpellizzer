Hydropectis estradii

Hydropectis estradii, commonly known as the golden cup-mud, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling small cups. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, thriving in moist, clay soils. Its leaves are usually broad and heart-shaped, with a smooth texture.

Hydropectis estradii is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat for various insects.
