Hieracium juranum subsp. tephroprenanthes

Hieracium juranum subsp.

tephroprenanthes, commonly known as the Jura hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, reaching heights of up to 30 cm.

The plant features basal leaves that are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while the upper leaves are smaller and more linear. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, characteristic of the Asteraceae family.

It is often found in calcareous soils and is considered a rare and protected species in some regions.
