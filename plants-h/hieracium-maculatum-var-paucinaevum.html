Hieracium maculatum var. paucinaevum

Hieracium maculatum var. paucinaevum, commonly known as the spotted hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it is often found in temperate regions. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a distinctive spotted or mottled appearance, while its flowers are clustered in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is considered an invasive species in some areas due to its rapid spread and competitive growth.
