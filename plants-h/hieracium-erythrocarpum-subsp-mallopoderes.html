Hieracium erythrocarpum subsp. mallopoderes

Hieracium erythrocarpum subsp.

mallopoderes, commonly known as the red-fruited hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Western Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf morphology.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides. Its leaves are lanceolate to ovate, with a rough texture and often bear a dense covering of fine hairs.

The showy yellow flowers are arranged in a composite head, and the plant produces a distinctive red, fleshy fruit known as a pseudocarp.
