Hymenophyllum hirtellum var. vincentinum

Hymenophyllum hirtellum var. vincentinum, commonly known as the Vincent's hairy moss, is a rare and delicate fern belonging to the Hymenophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, delicate fronds that are covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it a distinctive appearance. This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found on rock surfaces or in moist forest floors. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a sensitive indicator of environmental conditions and is often found in areas with high air humidity and minimal disturbance.
