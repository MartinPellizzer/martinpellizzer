Hibiscus multiformis

Hibiscus multiformis, commonly known as the variable hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can range in color from white to pink to red. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often reddish in color.

Hibiscus multiformis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.
