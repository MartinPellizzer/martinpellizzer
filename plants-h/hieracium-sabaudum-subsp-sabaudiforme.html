Hieracium sabaudum subsp. sabaudiforme

Hieracium sabaudum subsp.

sabaudiforme, commonly known as the Sabaudian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
