Huperzia gagnepainiana

Huperzia gagnepainiana, commonly known as the Gagnepain's clubmoss, is a species of fern ally native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Huperziaceae and is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and small, spore-producing structures called sporangia. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Its leaves are linear and arranged in whorls, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Huperzia gagnepainiana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
