Heteropterys patens var. typica

Heteropterys patens var. typica, commonly known as the pink-flowered huito, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a woody shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the understory of forests. The plant is characterized by its lanceolate, opposite leaves and clusters of small, pink or red flowers that bloom during the wet season. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
