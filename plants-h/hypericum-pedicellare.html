Hypericum pedicellare

Hypericum pedicellare, commonly known as the clustered St. John's wort, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are usually elliptic or ovate in shape. Its stems are often branched and covered with small, sticky glands.

Hypericum pedicellare is typically found in woodland edges, thickets, and moist soils, growing up to several feet in height.
