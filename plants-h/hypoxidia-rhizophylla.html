Hypoxidia rhizophylla

Hypoxidia rhizophylla, commonly known as the rock daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its flowers bloom in shades of yellow to orange.

Hypoxidia rhizophylla is well adapted to arid environments and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.
