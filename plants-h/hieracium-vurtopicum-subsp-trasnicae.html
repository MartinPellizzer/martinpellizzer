Hieracium vurtopicum subsp. trasnicae

Hieracium vurtopicum subsp.

trasnicae, commonly known as the Transylvanian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. It has hairy stems and leaves with a rough texture, and its flowers bloom in late summer.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental value in horticulture.
