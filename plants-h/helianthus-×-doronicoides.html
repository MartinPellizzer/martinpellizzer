Helianthus × doronicoides

Helianthus × doronicoides, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a hybrid perennial plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy orange to red composite flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and has rough, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
