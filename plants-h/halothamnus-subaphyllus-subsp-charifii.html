Halothamnus subaphyllus subsp. charifii

Halothamnus subaphyllus subsp.

charifii, commonly known as the coastal saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are adapted to retain moisture in arid environments.

The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in the spring, typically along the edges of rocky habitats. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and sandy soils.

Its botanical features make it a resilient species suited to harsh, sun-exposed conditions.
