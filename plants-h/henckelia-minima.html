Henckelia minima

Henckelia minima, commonly known as the dwarf henckelia, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage, with leaves that are typically elliptical and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or container planting.

Its ornamental value lies in its neat appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of climates when provided with proper care.
