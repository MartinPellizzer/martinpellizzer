Hierochloe macrophylla

Hierochloe macrophylla, commonly known as the tall bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear pale blue to purple flowers, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
