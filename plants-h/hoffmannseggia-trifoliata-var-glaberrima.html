Hoffmannseggia trifoliata var. glaberrima

Hoffmannseggia trifoliata var. glaberrima, commonly known as the smooth-leafed hoffmannseggia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in xerophytic plant adaptations.
