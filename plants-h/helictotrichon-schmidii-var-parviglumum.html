Helictotrichon schmidii var. parviglumum

Helictotrichon schmidii var. parviglumum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, spreading stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color. Its inflorescence consists of compact, spike-like clusters of small, blue to purple flowers.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soils.
