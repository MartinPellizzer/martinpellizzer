Hortensia serrata var. angustata

Hortensia serrata var. angustata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, serrated leaves and its ability to produce large, showy flower clusters. The plant's flowers change color depending on soil pH, ranging from blue to pink. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Hortensia serrata var. angustata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
