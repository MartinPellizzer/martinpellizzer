Hedyotis parviflora

Hedyotis parviflora, commonly known as small-flowered dotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the summer months. The plant has slender, creeping stems and small, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Hedyotis parviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
