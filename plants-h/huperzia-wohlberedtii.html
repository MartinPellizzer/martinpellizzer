Huperzia wohlberedtii

Huperzia wohlberedtii, commonly known as the Wohlberedt's huperzia, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its sporophylls are arranged in a spiral pattern, and the plant produces spores rather than seeds.

Huperzia wohlberedtii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
