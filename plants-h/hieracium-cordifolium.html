Hieracium cordifolium

Hieracium cordifolium, commonly known as the heart-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant features basal leaves that are heart-shaped, giving it its common name, and it produces yellow flowers in late summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Hieracium cordifolium is often found in moist meadows and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.
