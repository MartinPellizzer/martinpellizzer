Hyptis decipiens

Hyptis decipiens, commonly known as false basil, is a herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, tubular flowers are typically violet or purple in color and are arranged in spikes.

Hyptis decipiens is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional remedies.
