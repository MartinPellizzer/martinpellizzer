Helicteres involucrata

Helicteres involucrata, commonly known as the redroot false holly, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are usually opposite in arrangement. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers followed by small, spiny fruits containing seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species in its native range.
