Hedycarya crassifolia

Hedycarya crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved hedycarya, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America and northern South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring and early summer. Hedycarya crassifolia is typically found in humid, shaded environments within cloud forests.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
