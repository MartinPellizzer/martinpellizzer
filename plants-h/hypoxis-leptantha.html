Hypoxis leptantha

Hypoxis leptantha, commonly known as the slender false arum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, strap-like leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces a single, showy inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Hypoxis leptantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
