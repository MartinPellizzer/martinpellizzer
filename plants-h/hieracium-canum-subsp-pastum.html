Hieracium canum subsp. pastum

Hieracium canum subsp.

pastum, commonly known as the pasture hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering stems that bear yellow ray florets and yellow disk florets.

The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It has hairy stems and leaves that are often glandular, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The subspecies pastum is distinguished by its shorter stature and more compact growth compared to other forms of Hieracium canum.
