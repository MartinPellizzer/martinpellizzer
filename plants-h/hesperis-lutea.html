Hesperis lutea

Hesperis lutea, commonly known as the yellow horned-poppy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with four petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Hesperis lutea is often found in meadows and rocky habitats, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal.
