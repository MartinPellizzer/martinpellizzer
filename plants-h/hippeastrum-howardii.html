Hippeastrum howardii

Hippeastrum howardii, commonly known as the Mexican fire lily, is a striking ornamental plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant features strap-like, dark green leaves that emerge from a bulbous base, contributing to its dramatic appearance. Hippeastrum howardii thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Its unique flowering pattern and bold colors make it a popular choice for indoor gardening and floral arrangements.
