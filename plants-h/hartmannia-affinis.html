Hartmannia affinis

Hartmannia affinis, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and absorbing moisture from the air. Its leaves are usually elliptical and dark green, with a waxy texture.

Hartmannia affinis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
