Hemionitis bonapartei

Hemionitis bonapartei, commonly known as the Bonaparte's hemionitis, is a rare and delicate fern native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the family Hemionitidaceae and is characterized by its small, finely divided fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments, often growing in shaded, rocky habitats at high altitudes. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists studying rare flora.
