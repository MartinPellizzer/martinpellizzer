Hutchinsiella

Hutchinsiella, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to southern Africa.

These plants are characterized by their compact rosettes of velvety, waxy leaves and their showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. Hutchinsiella species are typically evergreen and thrive in bright, indirect light with well-draining soil. They are popular houseplants due to their ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.

The genus includes several species, such as Hutchinsiella falcata and Hutchinsiella heterophylla, which are prized for their unique foliage and bloom patterns.
