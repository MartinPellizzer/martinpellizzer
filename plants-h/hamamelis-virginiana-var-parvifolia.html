Hamamelis virginiana var. parvifolia

Hamamelis virginiana var. parvifolia, commonly known as the dwarf witch hazel, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in late autumn or early winter. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.
