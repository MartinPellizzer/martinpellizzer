Hieracium orphnocratum

Hieracium orphnocratum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, surrounded by bracts that give it a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its overall rugged appearance.
