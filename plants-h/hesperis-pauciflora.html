Hesperis pauciflora

Hesperis pauciflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many economically important crops and ornamental plants. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers arranged in a raceme, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its characteristic appearance.

Hesperis pauciflora is often found in dry, calcareous soils and is valued for its ornamental value in gardens.
