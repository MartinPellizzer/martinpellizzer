Hieracium tatrae

Hieracium tatrae, commonly known as the Tatra hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe, particularly the Tatra Mountains.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine meadows, where it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It has hairy stems and basal leaves that are often lobed, and its flowers are yellowish to reddish in color.

Hieracium tatrae is known for its resilience to harsh mountain climates and is often found in areas with limited vegetation.
