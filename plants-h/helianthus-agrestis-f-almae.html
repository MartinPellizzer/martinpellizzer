Helianthus agrestis f. almae

Helianthus agrestis f. almae, commonly known as the Almace sunflower, is a variant of the common sunflower native to North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its yellow composite flowers and rough, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers consist of a central disk surrounded by ray florets.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
