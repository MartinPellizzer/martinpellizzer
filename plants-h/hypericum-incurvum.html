Hypericum incurvum

Hypericum incurvum, commonly known as the curly St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It features slender, often prostrate stems covered in small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Hypericum incurvum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant landscaping.
