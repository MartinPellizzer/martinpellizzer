Hieracium piliferum subsp. leucopsis

Hieracium piliferum subsp.

leucopsis, commonly known as the white-hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed sites, reaching heights of up to 30 cm. Its leaves are hairy and arranged alternately along the stem, while the flowers are yellow with white margins.

It is often found in open habitats and is adapted to temperate climates.
