Hypericum styphelioides subsp. styphelioides

Hypericum styphelioides subsp.

styphelioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States. It is a low-growing shrub with woody stems and opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with four petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are typically visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
