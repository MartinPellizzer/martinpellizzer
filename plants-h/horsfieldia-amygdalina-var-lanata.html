Horsfieldia amygdalina var. lanata

Horsfieldia amygdalina var. lanata, commonly known as the woolly horsfieldia, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves and stems, which give it its common name. The plant produces fragrant, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
