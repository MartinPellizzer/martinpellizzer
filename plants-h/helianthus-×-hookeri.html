Helianthus × hookeri

Helianthus × hookeri, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a hybrid species within the sunflower family (Asteraceae).

It is characterized by its large, vibrant orange to red flowers that resemble those of the common sunflower. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial, reaching heights of 1 to 3 meters, with rough, hairy stems and alternate leaves. Its flowers are composed of a central disk surrounded by ray florets, which are sterile and serve to attract pollinators.

The species is native to Central America and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
