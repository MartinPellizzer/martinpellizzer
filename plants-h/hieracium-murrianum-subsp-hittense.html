Hieracium murrianum subsp. hittense

Hieracium murrianum subsp.

hittense, commonly known as the Hittens' hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm.

The plant features basal rosettes of leaves and produces yellow flowers in a cluster at the end of its stem. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and the flowers consist of numerous small, petal-less florets.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
