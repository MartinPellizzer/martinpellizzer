Hakea recedens

Hakea recedens, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hakea, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces distinctive cylindrical flower spikes that range in color from yellow to red, depending on the species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates with dry summers.

Hakea recedens is often used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
