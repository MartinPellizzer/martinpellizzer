Heracleum lanatum subsp. asiaticum

Heracleum lanatum subsp.

asiaticum, commonly known as the Asian cow parsnip, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia. It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate flowers.

The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are white to pale green and form umbrella-like clusters, attracting various pollinators.

The root system consists of thick, fleshy roots that are often used in traditional medicine.
