Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp. cinerascentiforme

Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp.

cinerascentiforme, commonly known as the ash-gray hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and hairy, grayish-green leaves.

The subspecies is distinguished by its elongated, ash-gray leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered plant in many regions.
