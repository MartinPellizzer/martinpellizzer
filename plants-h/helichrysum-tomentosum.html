Helichrysum tomentosum

Helichrysum tomentosum, commonly known as the woolly sunflower, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It features dense, hairy foliage that gives it a fuzzy appearance, and its flowers are typically yellow or orange, resembling small sunflowers. The plant produces small, rounded seeds that are often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for drought-tolerant gardens.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties contribute to its widespread cultivation and appreciation.
