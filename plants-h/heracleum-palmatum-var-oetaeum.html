Heracleum palmatum var. oetaeum

Heracleum palmatum var. oetaeum, commonly known as the Oetaeum cow parsnip, is a variant of the cow parsnip species native to parts of Central Asia.

It is a tall herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant features large, palmately lobed leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter, with a hairy underside. Its flowers are clustered in umbels, producing small white to greenish-yellow blooms that attract pollinators.

The plant is part of the Apiaceae family, known for its aromatic compounds and medicinal uses in traditional herbal practices.
