Hieracium leiopogon subsp. iolai

Hieracium leiopogon subsp.

iolai, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It features a rosette of basal leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its flowering stems are usually hairy and can grow up to 30 centimeters tall.

It is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats such as meadows and roadsides.
