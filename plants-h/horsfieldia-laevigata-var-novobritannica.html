Horsfieldia laevigata var. novobritannica

Horsfieldia laevigata var. novobritannica, commonly known as the New Britain orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of New Britain in the Solomon Islands.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, typically during the wet season. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the rich, humus-filled soils of its habitat.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
