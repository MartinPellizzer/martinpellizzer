Hieracium ruprechtii subsp. igneum

Hieracium ruprechtii subsp.

igneum, commonly known as the fiery hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are upright and often reddish in color.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
