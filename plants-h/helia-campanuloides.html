Helia campanuloides

Helia campanuloides, commonly known as the bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, tubular flowers that typically range in color from blue to purple. The plant has lanceolate leaves that are often hairy, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is native to regions in Europe and Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Helia campanuloides typically blooms in late spring to early summer, adding a vibrant touch to gardens and natural landscapes.
