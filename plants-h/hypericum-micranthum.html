Hypericum micranthum

Hypericum micranthum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved St. John's wort, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped. It has a woody stem and is often found in the understory of forests.

The plant is part of the Hypericaceae family and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
