Hypoxis kilimanjarica subsp. prostrata

Hypoxis kilimanjarica subsp.

prostrata, commonly known as the Kilimanjaro groundsel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the high-altitude regions of East Africa. It features a rosette of broad, fleshy, grass-like leaves that are typically green with a bluish tinge.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a dense cluster at the center of the rosette. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cool, moist climates.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
