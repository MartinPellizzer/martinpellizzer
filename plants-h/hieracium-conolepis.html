Hieracium conolepis

Hieracium conolepis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and basal rosettes of narrow, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster, typical of the daisy family. It is native to Europe and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Hieracium conolepis is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
