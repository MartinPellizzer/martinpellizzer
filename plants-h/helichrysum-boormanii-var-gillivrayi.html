Helichrysum boormanii var. gillivrayi

Helichrysum boormanii var. gillivrayi, commonly known as the Cape gold bush, is a South African shrub characterized by its dense, wiry stems and clusters of small, golden-yellow flowers.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is native to the Western Cape region of South Africa. The plant is evergreen, with leathery, aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting, showy blooms that remain vibrant even when dried.
