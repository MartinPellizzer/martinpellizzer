Hecatea biglandulosa

Hecatea biglandulosa, commonly known as the large-fruited hecatea, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is native to South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are often covered in dense, hairy structures. The plant produces showy, red-orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its foliage is adapted to retain moisture, making it well-suited for humid environments.

Hecatea biglandulosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
