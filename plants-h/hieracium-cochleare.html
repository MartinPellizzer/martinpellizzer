Hieracium cochleare

Hieracium cochleare, commonly known as the spiral hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium cochleare is known for its spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it its common name.
