Helwingia chinensis f. megaphylla

Helwingia chinensis f. megaphylla, commonly known as the giant-leaf helwingia, is a unique variant of the helwingia plant characterized by its enlarged, leathery leaves.

This evergreen shrub belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is native to China, particularly in the Yunnan region. Its leaves are typically elongated and glossy, providing a striking visual appearance in gardens. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in spring, often followed by red or orange berries in autumn.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is a sought-after specimen in botanical collections and specialized horticulture.
