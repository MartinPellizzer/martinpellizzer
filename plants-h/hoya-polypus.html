Hoya polypus

Hoya polypus, commonly known as the "Wax Plant" or "Porcelain Flower," is a popular epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and pink. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pink, blooming in response to bright, indirect light. Hoya polypus is known for its slow growth and ability to thrive in low-light conditions, making it an ideal choice for indoor gardening.

Its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements have made it a favorite among plant enthusiasts.
