Hippocrepis fructicosa

Hippocrepis fructicosa, commonly known as the sweet sarsaparilla, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant produces small, woody pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Hippocrepis fructicosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
