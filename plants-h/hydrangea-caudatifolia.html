Hydrangea caudatifolia

Hydrangea caudatifolia, commonly known as the cat-tail hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, cat-tail-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of blue, pink, or white, depending on soil pH. Its foliage is typically dark green with a rough texture, and it thrives in partial shade with moist, well-drained soil.

Hydrangea caudatifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
