Helichrysum chasmolycium

Helichrysum chasmolycium, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, yellow to golden-orange inflorescences that resemble small flowers. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that are often clustered together. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rocky or arid environments.

Helichrysum chasmolycium is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
