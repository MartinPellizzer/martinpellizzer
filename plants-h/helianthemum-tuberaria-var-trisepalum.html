Helianthemum tuberaria var. trisepalum

Helianthemum tuberaria var. trisepalum, commonly known as the three-lobed rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its fleshy, succulent stems and small, clustered flowers. The leaves are typically rounded and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are usually pink or white with five petals. This variety is notable for its three-lobed sepals, which distinguish it from other members of the genus.

It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry habitats.
