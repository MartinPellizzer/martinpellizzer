Hibiscus longifolius

Hibiscus longifolius, commonly known as the longleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range from pink to white in color. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. Hibiscus longifolius thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its blooming period occurs during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
