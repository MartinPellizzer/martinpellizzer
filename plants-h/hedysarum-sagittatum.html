Hedysarum sagittatum

Hedysarum sagittatum, commonly known as the arrowhead peavine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive arrowhead-shaped seed pods. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and rocky slopes.

Hedysarum sagittatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators and providing habitat for small wildlife.
