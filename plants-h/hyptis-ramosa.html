Hyptis ramosa

Hyptis ramosa, commonly known as Brazilian hyptis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color.

Hyptis ramosa is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
