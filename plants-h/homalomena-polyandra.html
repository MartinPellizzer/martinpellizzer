Homalomena polyandra

Homalomena polyandra, commonly known as the red spider lily, is a tropical evergreen plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and showy, spadix-bearing inflorescences. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pink flowers that are surrounded by a colorful spathe. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Homalomena polyandra is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and foliage.
