Hieracium umbelliferum subsp. semicymigerum

Hieracium umbelliferum subsp.

semicymigerum, commonly known as the semicymic hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy foliage.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rough, hairy texture, and its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
