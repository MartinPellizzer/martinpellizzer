Halimocnemis sulphurea

Halimocnemis sulphurea, commonly known as the sulphur flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and linear, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in saline environments. Its seed heads are often used in traditional medicine by local communities.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience to harsh conditions.
