Haplopappus spinulosus var. canescens

Haplopappus spinulosus var. canescens, commonly known as the grayleaf horsebrush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a low, spreading growth habit. Its leaves are covered in fine, grayish hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers are small and yellow, often appearing in clusters at the ends of the stems.
