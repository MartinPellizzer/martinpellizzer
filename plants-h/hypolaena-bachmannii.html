Hypolaena bachmannii

Hypolaena bachmannii, commonly known as the Bachmann's hypolaena, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the tropical rainforests of northern Queensland, Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom during the wet season. The plant thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found growing on tree trunks or in the understorey of dense vegetation. Its botanical significance lies in its unique adaptations to its specific ecological niche and its role in the biodiversity of its habitat.

Due to habitat destruction, it is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
