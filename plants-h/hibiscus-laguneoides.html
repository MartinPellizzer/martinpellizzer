Hibiscus laguneoides

Hibiscus laguneoides, commonly known as the marsh hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink or red, often with a prominent yellow center. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Hibiscus laguneoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable water conditions.
