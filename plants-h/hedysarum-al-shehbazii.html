Hedysarum al-shehbazii

Hedysarum al-shehbazii, commonly known as the Persian vetch, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring compound leaves with 5 to 7 pairs of oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils.

Hedysarum al-shehbazii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
