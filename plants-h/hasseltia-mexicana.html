Hasseltia mexicana

Hasseltia mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its tall, branching stems and vibrant orange to red flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically arranged in clusters along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features include fibrous roots and hairy leaves, which help it conserve water in its native environment.
