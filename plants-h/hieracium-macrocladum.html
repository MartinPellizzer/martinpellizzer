Hieracium macrocladum

Hieracium macrocladum, commonly known as the broad-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, rough-textured leaves that are typically lance-shaped. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows.

Hieracium macrocladum is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
