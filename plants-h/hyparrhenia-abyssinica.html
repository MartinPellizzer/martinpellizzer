Hyparrhenia abyssinica

Hyparrhenia abyssinica, commonly known as African grass or red grass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves that are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain minute flowers, typical of the grass family. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and is drought-tolerant.

Hyparrhenia abyssinica is often used for erosion control and as a forage plant for livestock.
