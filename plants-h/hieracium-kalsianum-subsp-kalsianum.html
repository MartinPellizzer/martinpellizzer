Hieracium kalsianum subsp. kalsianum

Hieracium kalsianum subsp.

kalsianum, commonly known as the Kalsi's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It typically grows in dry meadows and rocky soils, preferring well-drained environments.

The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and the stems are hairy.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.
