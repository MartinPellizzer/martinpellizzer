Helianthemum nummularium var. cantabricum

Helianthemum nummularium var. cantabricum, commonly known as the Cantabrian sandwort, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features dense, silver-white, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in compact inflorescences. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
