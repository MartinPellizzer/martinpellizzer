Hedysarum shahjinalense

Hedysarum shahjinalense, commonly known as the Shahjinal clover, is a perennial leguminous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, enriching the soil. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Hedysarum shahjinalense is often found in alpine meadows and is of interest for its ecological and potential medicinal properties.
