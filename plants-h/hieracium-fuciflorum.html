Hieracium fuciflorum

Hieracium fuciflorum, commonly known as the black sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its dark green, grass-like foliage and small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as wet meadows and stream banks. It has a creeping growth habit with rhizomes that help it spread across the ground.

Hieracium fuciflorum is also known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
