Hyptis kerberi

Hyptis kerberi, commonly known as Brazilian hyptis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, tubular flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
