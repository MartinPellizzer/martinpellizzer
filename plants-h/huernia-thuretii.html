Huernia thuretii

Huernia thuretii, commonly known as the horned hawse, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that are often ribbed and covered in small, spiky tubercles. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its stems and leaves.

Huernia thuretii is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and ornamental appeal.
