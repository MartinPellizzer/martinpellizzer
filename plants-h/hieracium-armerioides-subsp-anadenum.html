Hieracium armerioides subsp. anadenum

Hieracium armerioides subsp.

anadenum, commonly known as the Anatolian hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Turkey and parts of the Balkans. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy foliage.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is a relatively small plant, usually reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.
