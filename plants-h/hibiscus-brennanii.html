Hibiscus brennanii

Hibiscus brennanii, commonly known as the Madagascar hibiscus, is a species native to Madagascar.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its large, showy flowers. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its flowers are typically pink to red in color. Hibiscus brennanii produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.
