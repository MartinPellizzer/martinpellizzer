Hieracium atratum subsp. dolichosphaericum

Hieracium atratum subsp.

dolichosphaericum, commonly known as the long-spherical hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a single, elongated flowering stem.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a single capitulum, which is surrounded by bracts. Its leaves are typically lanceolate and have a rough, hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
