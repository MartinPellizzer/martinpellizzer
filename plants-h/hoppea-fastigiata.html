Hoppea fastigiata

Hoppea fastigiata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hoppea, is a shrub native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Hoppea fastigiata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
