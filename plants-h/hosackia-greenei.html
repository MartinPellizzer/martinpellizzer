Hosackia greenei

Hosackia greenei, commonly known as the Greene's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its root system forms symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, contributing to soil fertility.

Hosackia greenei is often found in grasslands and sagebrush steppe ecosystems.
