Haumaniastrum dissitifolium

Haumaniastrum dissitifolium, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a deep taproot system that allows it to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs that help reduce water loss through transpiration.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators in its native habitat.
