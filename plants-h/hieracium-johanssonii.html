Hieracium johanssonii

Hieracium johanssonii, commonly known as Johansson's hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer. Native to the mountainous regions of central Europe, it thrives in rocky and sandy soils. The plant is herbaceous, with a short, stout stem that supports its flowering heads.

It is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
