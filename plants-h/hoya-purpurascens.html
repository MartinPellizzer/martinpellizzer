Hoya purpurascens

Hoya purpurascens, commonly known as the Purple Waxy Hoya, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a slight purple tinge, and it produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Hoya purpurascens is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and relatively easy care requirements.

It is also noted for its slow growth and ability to bloom when provided with proper care and conditions.
