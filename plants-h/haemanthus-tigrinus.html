Haemanthus tigrinus

Haemanthus tigrinus, commonly known as the tiger lily, is a striking plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant orange and yellow flowers with distinctive dark markings. The plant features strap-shaped leaves that are often spotted or streaked, adding to its visual appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Haemanthus tigrinus is known for its ability to produce offsets, making it relatively easy to propagate.
