Hemichroa mesembryanthema

Hemichroa mesembryanthema, commonly known as the African marguerite, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, green, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Hemichroa mesembryanthema is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
