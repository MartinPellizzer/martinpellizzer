Humboltia procumbens

Humboltia procumbens, commonly known as the dwarf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous species that typically reaches heights of only a few centimeters. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually blue or violet in color, blooming in the early morning. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Humboltia procumbens is adapted to thrive in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
