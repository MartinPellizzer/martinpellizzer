Hakea recurva

Hakea recurva, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hakea, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical stems and narrow, recurved leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces striking, bottlebrush-like inflorescences that are typically pink or red in color. It is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system to access water.

Hakea recurva is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
