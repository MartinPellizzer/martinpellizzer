Hieracium sparsum subsp. svaneticiforme

Hieracium sparsum subsp.

svaneticiforme, commonly known as the Svaneticiform hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and forest edges. It has hairy stems and narrow, serrated leaves, and its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and sometimes endangered species in certain regions.
