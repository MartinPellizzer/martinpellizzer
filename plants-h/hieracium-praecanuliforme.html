Hieracium praecanuliforme

Hieracium praecanuliforme, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough texture, and its flowers are yellow, often appearing in clusters.

Hieracium praecanuliforme is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
