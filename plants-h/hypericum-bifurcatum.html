Hypericum bifurcatum

Hypericum bifurcatum, commonly known as the forked St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its stems are woody and branched, giving it a distinctive, shrub-like appearance.

Hypericum bifurcatum is often found in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to full sun conditions.
