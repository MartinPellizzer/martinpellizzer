Hieracium affine

Hieracium affine, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems, typically growing in meadows and disturbed soils. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions.

Hieracium affine is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
