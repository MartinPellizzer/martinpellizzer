Helianthemum villosum var. angustatum

Helianthemum villosum var. angustatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf sandverbena, is a low-growing, mat-forming shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. The leaves are narrow and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving the plant a velvety texture. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is drought-tolerant once established.

This plant is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
