Hyptis aristulata

Hyptis aristulata, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, fuzzy leaves and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and prairies. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

Its botanical features include opposite leaves and a square stem, typical of the Lamiaceae family.
