Hyptis inamoena

Hyptis inamoena, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The leaves are also utilized in culinary applications, particularly in certain South American cuisines.
