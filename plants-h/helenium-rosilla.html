Helenium rosilla

Helenium rosilla, commonly known as the Rosilla Hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant produces bright orange to red disc flowers surrounded by pale yellow to white ray florets. Helenium rosilla typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun.

Its foliage is lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
