Hibiscus syriacus var. albus

Hibiscus syriacus var. albus, commonly known as the white Syrian rose or white Chinese hibiscus, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its large, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves and can grow up to 6 feet tall. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a striking addition to gardens and landscapes.
