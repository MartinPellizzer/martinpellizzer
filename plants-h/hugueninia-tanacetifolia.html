Hugueninia tanacetifolia, commonly known as the mountain huckleberry, is a shrub native to the high elevations of the western United States and northern Mexico.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, edible berries that ripen to a dark purple or black color. Hugueninia tanacetifolia is an important component of alpine and subalpine ecosystems, providing food for various wildlife species.
