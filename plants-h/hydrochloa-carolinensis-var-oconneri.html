Hydrochloa carolinensis var. oconneri

Hydrochloa carolinensis var. oconneri, commonly known as the O'Connor's marsh hawksbeard, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in marshy areas and along the edges of ponds. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

This variety is noted for its adaptability to wetland environments and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
