Helianthus annuus var. chrysanthemoides

Helianthus annuus var. chrysanthemoides, commonly known as the chrysanthemum sunflower, is a variant of the common sunflower with distinctive floral characteristics.

It features smaller, more compact flower heads that resemble chrysanthemums, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture and are covered in fine hairs.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
