Hieracium hoppeanum var. leucocephaloides

Hieracium hoppeanum var. leucocephaloides, commonly known as the white-headed hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features basal rosettes of serrated, hairy leaves and produces showy, yellow to orange flowers with white centers. It is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain regions.
