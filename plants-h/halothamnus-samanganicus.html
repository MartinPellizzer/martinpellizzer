Halothamnus samanganicus

Halothamnus samanganicus, commonly known as the Samanganic carpet grass, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with small, linear leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Halothamnus samanganicus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small organisms in its native range.
