Hyptis lachnosphaeria

Hyptis lachnosphaeria, commonly known as the woolly hyptis, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, often blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for ornamental gardening.

Its aromatic leaves and compact growth habit make it a popular choice for both landscape and container planting.
