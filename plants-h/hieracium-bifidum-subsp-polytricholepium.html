Hieracium bifidum subsp. polytricholepium

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

polytricholepium, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges. Its inflorescence consists of a single, erect stem bearing several flower heads.

It is notable for its hairy foliage and is often found in disturbed or nutrient-rich soils.
