Herniaria africana

Herniaria africana, commonly known as African carpetweed, is a low-growing annual plant native to southern Africa.

It features small, rounded leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, and it produces tiny white or pink flowers. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas. It has a compact growth habit, forming dense mats that help prevent soil erosion.

Herniaria africana is also known for its ability to tolerate drought conditions.
