Helianthus annuus var. semivinosus

Helianthus annuus var. semivinosus, commonly known as the red sunflower, is a variant of the common sunflower characterized by its striking red-orange petals.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring a sturdy stem and broad, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are large and showy, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets that display a vivid red hue.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
