Hypolepis nausoriensis

Hypolepis nausoriensis, commonly known as the Nausori fern, is a species of polypod fern native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are often adorned with small, rounded lobes. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are often covered in a dense, fibrous layer that aids in water retention.

Hypolepis nausoriensis is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its ecological role in maintaining soil stability in its native habitats.
