Heracleum Elbursensis

Heracleum elbursensis is a rare and fascinating plant native to the Elburz Mountains in Iran. It belongs to the Apiaceae family, known for its aromatic and often medicinal properties. This species is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, compound leaves that can reach several feet in length. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species. Studying Heracleum elbursensis offers valuable insights into the biodiversity and ecological dynamics of its mountainous environment.
