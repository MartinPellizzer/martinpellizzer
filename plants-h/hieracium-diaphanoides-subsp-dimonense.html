Hieracium diaphanoides subsp. dimonense

Hieracium diaphanoides subsp.

dimonense, commonly known as the translucent hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are usually lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

This subspecies is distinguished by its translucent, waxy coating on the leaves, which gives it its common name.
