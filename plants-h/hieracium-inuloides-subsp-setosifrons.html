Hieracium inuloides subsp. setosifrons

Hieracium inuloides subsp.

setosifrons, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, while the inflorescence consists of yellow disk flowers surrounded by white to pale yellow ray florets.

The subspecies setosifrons is distinguished by its dense, hairy foliage and robust growth habit.
