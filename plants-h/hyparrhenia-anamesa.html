Hyparrhenia anamesa

Hyparrhenia anamesa, commonly known as the African feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and dense, grass-like foliage that forms a low-growing mat. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed sites.

Hyparrhenia anamesa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in degraded landscapes.
