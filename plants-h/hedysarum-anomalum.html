Hedysarum anomalum

Hedysarum anomalum, commonly known as the anomalous milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers in late spring. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or steppe habitats.

Hedysarum anomalum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
