Hieracium veterascens

Hieracium veterascens, commonly known as the Italian hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are generally basal, with a rough, hairy texture, and the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium veterascens is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain regions.
