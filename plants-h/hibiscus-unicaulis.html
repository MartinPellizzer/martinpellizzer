Hibiscus unicaulis

Hibiscus unicaulis, commonly known as the single-stem hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its single, erect stem and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, or white. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Hibiscus unicaulis thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
