Harpochloa capensis

Harpochloa capensis, commonly known as the Cape harpgrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, spikelet-based flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue and a rough texture.

Harpochloa capensis is typically found in coastal and sandy soils, often growing in open, sunny habitats.
