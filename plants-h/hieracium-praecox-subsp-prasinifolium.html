Hieracium praecox subsp. prasinifolium

Hieracium praecox subsp.

prasinifolium, commonly known as the green-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically has basal leaves that are broadly ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are yellow, with a central disk surrounded by petal-like rays, and it produces small, fluffy seeds in achenes.

It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides.
