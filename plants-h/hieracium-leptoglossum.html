Hieracium leptoglossum

Hieracium leptoglossum, commonly known as the slender-tongued hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while the flowers are yellow with a distinctive, narrow tongue-like petal.

Hieracium leptoglossum is often found in open habitats and can spread through rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
