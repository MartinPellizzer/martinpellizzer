Hemichlaena angustifolia var. polystachya

Hemichlaena angustifolia var. polystachya, commonly known as the narrowleaf hemichlaena, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. Its flowering stems bear small, clustered inflorescences with white to pale blue flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
