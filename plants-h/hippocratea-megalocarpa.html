Hippocratea megalocarpa

Hippocratea megalocarpa, commonly known as the giant hippocratea, is a striking member of the Fabaceae family native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmately compound leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain several elliptical, flat seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
