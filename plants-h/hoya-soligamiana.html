Hoya soligamiana

Hoya soligamiana, commonly known as the "Coral Curls" hoya, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with shades of pink, white, and green. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming during the summer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Hoya soligamiana is popular among plant enthusiasts for its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
