Hebe stenophylla var. hesperia

Hebe stenophylla var. hesperia, commonly known as the Hesperia hebe, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of violet or blue. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
