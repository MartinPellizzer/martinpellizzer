Heisteria insculpta

Heisteria insculpta, commonly known as the scalloped heisteria, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves with scalloped edges. The plant produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Heisteria insculpta thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
