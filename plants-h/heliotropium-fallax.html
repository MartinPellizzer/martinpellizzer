Heliotropium fallax

Heliotropium fallax, commonly known as the silverleaf heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green foliage and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and the plant is known for its aromatic scent.

Heliotropium fallax is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
