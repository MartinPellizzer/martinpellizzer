Hieracium sagittatum subsp. expallescens

Hieracium sagittatum subsp.

expallescens, commonly known as the arrowhead hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a composite head.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
