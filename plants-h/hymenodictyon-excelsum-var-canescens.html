Hymenodictyon excelsum var. canescens

Hymenodictyon excelsum var. canescens, commonly known as the white sand dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dandelion-like appearance with yellow flowers and pappus structures. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or disturbed habitats. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered with fine hairs, while the flowers are clustered in heads.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting local insect populations and its resilience to environmental stressors.
