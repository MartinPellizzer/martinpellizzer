Hyptis spicata var. bromfieldii

Hyptis spicata var. bromfieldii, commonly known as bromfield's hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or violet flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
