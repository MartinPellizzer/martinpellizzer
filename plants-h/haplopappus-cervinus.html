Haplopappus cervinus

Haplopappus cervinus, commonly known as the mountain pappus, is a flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, and it produces small, pale yellow to white flowers.

Haplopappus cervinus is often found in high elevation areas, including mountainous regions and desert foothills.
