Hieracium auriculiforme subsp. palatinum

Hieracium auriculiforme subsp.

palatinum, commonly known as the golden hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. Its leaves are basal, lanceolate, and often have a rough texture.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
