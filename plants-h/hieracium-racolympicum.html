Hieracium racolympicum

Hieracium racolympicum, commonly known as the Alpine groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are usually basal, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Hieracium racolympicum is often found in high-altitude habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
