Hesperis trullata

Hesperis trullata, commonly known as the sweet rocket, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are upright and branching.

Hesperis trullata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
