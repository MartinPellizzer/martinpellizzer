Hieracium × chrysochroum

Hieracium × chrysochroum, commonly known as the golden hawkweed, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellowish to orange-yellow flower heads, which are typically composed of numerous ray florets. The plant produces a basal rosette of leaves that are typically hairy and have a rough texture. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other open areas.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a wide range of morphological variation.
