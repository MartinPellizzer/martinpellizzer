Hakea benthamii

Hakea benthamii, commonly known as the Western Australian hakea, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, urn-shaped flowers that are typically red or pink in color. The plant has needle-like leaves that are densely packed, providing a dense, spiny appearance. It is well adapted to the dry, sandy soils of its native habitat.

Hakea benthamii is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
