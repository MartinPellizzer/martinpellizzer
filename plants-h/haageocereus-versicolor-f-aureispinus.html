Haageocereus versicolor f. aureispinus

Haageocereus versicolor f. aureispinus, commonly known as the golden-spined night blooming cactus, is a unique cactus species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are ribbed and covered with spines, some of which are golden in color. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its striking appearance and specific care requirements, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
