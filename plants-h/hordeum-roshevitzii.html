Hordeum roshevitzii

Hordeum roshevitzii, commonly known as Roshevitz's barley, is a rare and ancient species of grass native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, glabrous stems and small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showing adaptations to drought and poor soil conditions. Its seeds are small and hard, which may contribute to its long-term survival in harsh climates.

Due to its limited distribution and genetic uniqueness, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical and agricultural research.
