Hypericum perforatum subsp. perforatum

Hypericum perforatum subsp.

perforatum, commonly known as St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It is characterized by its yellow flowers and distinctive leaves with translucent dots.

The plant contains bioactive compounds such as hypericin and hyperforin, which contribute to its medicinal properties. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its historical use in herbal medicine dates back to ancient times.
