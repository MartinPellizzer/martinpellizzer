Hemerocallis graminifolia

Hemerocallis graminifolia, commonly known as the grassleaf daylily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically blooms in late summer, with each flower lasting only one day, hence its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Hemerocallis graminifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and hardiness in various climates.
