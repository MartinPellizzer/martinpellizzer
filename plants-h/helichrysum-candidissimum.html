Helichrysum candidissimum

Helichrysum candidissimum, commonly known as the white everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that are often arranged in dense, spherical clusters. The plant is known for its aromatic foliage and is often used in dried flower arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant in Mediterranean climates.

Helichrysum candidissimum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
