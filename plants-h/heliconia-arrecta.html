Heliconia arrecta

Heliconia arrecta, commonly known as the red ginger or torch ginger, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking, banana-like inflorescences with brightly colored, tubular flowers. The plant's leaves are large, broad, and often have a waxy texture, while its stems are sturdy and sometimes segmented. Heliconia arrecta thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

It is also cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in floral arrangements.
