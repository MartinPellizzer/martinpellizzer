Huernia collenetteae

Huernia collenetteae, commonly known as the Collenette's moon cactus, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its swollen, fleshy stems that resemble a moon or a bulb. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Its thick, waxy leaves help it store water, making it well adapted to arid environments.

It is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
