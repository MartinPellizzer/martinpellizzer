Hieracium nigroviridiceps

Hieracium nigroviridiceps, commonly known as the black-green hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces yellow flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
