Hieracium rivale var. subhirtum

Hieracium rivale var. subhirtum, commonly known as the hairy yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellowish-orange flowers and hairy stems and leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy areas and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. It has a creeping root system and can form dense colonies, making it a common weed in disturbed soils.

Its botanical features include lance-shaped leaves and daisy-like flower heads with ray florets.
