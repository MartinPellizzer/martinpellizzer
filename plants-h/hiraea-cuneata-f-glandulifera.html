Hiraea cuneata f. glandulifera

Hiraea cuneata f. glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular-leafed hiraea, is a flowering plant species belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, glandular-dotted leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the Asteraceae family. It is native to southern Africa and thrives in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it prefers well-drained soils and moderate moisture.
