Heracleum giganteum

Heracleum giganteum, commonly known as giant hogweed, is a tall biennial or perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It can grow up to 5 meters in height, with large, umbrella-like compound leaves and showy white flowers clustered in spherical heads. The plant contains phototoxic sap that can cause severe skin irritation when exposed to sunlight. It is often found in moist, disturbed soils along riverbanks and roadsides.

Due to its invasive nature, it is considered a noxious weed in many regions.
