Hibiscus micranthus var. hispidus

Hibiscus micranthus var. hispidus, commonly known as the woolly hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, with five petals and a prominent central stamen. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Hibiscus micranthus var. hispidus is also sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
