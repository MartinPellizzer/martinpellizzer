Hedysarum varium subsp. pestalozzae

Hedysarum varium subsp.

pestalozzae, commonly known as the Italian vetch, is a flowering plant species native to southern Europe. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale yellow to reddish-brown. Its trifoliate leaves and distinctive fruit pods are characteristic of the Fabaceae family.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in soil stabilization and nitrogen fixation.
