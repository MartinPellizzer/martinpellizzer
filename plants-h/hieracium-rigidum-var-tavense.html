Hieracium rigidum var. tavense

Hieracium rigidum var. tavense, commonly known as the rigid hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and disturbed soils, exhibiting a robust growth habit with sturdy, upright stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

This variety is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
