Hieracium leptocladum subsp. legraeanum

Hieracium leptocladum subsp.

legraeanum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has finely divided, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

This subspecies is distinguished by its smaller stature and more delicate foliage compared to other members of the Hieracium genus.
