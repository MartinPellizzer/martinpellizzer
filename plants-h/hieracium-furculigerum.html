Hieracium furculigerum

Hieracium furculigerum, commonly known as the forked hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while the flowers are yellow with a distinctive forked structure at the base.

Hieracium furculigerum is often found in open habitats and can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
