Hippocratea apocynoides

Hippocratea apocynoides, commonly known as the white bellflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae, which includes many milkweed-like plants. The plant is characterized by its showy, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a smooth surface and a slightly waxy texture.

The plant is often grown as an ornamental due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
