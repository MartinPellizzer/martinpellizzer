Hymenophyllum involucratum

Hymenophyllum involucratum, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a small, epiphytic fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, soft, and velvety fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant is typically found growing on tree trunks or rocks in moist, shaded environments, often in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are closely packed and have a dense, moss-like texture, which helps it retain moisture.

The spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, and they are covered by a protective indusium.
