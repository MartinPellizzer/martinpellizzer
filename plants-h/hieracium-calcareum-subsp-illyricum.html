Hieracium calcareum subsp. illyricum

Hieracium calcareum subsp.

illyricum, commonly known as the Illyrian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and bright yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in calcareous soils, often in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins and is known for its ability to thrive in sunny, well-drained environments.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its specific ecological preferences and limited distribution.
