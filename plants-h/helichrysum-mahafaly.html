Helichrysum mahafaly

Helichrysum mahafaly, commonly known as the Mahafaly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, bright yellow or orange flower heads that resemble small suns. The plant produces small, narrow, grass-like leaves and has a compact, bushy growth habit. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

The flowers are often used in traditional medicine and as ornamental plants due to their long-lasting appearance.
