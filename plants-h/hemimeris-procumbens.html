Hemimeris procumbens

Hemimeris procumbens, commonly known as the creeping hemimeris, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming species that thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Hemimeris procumbens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
