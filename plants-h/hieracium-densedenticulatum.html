Hieracium densedenticulatum

Hieracium densedenticulatum, commonly known as the dense-toothed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, and its stems are usually erect with a hairy texture. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, where it thrives in full sun to partial shade.
