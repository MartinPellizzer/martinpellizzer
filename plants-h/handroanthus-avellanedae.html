Handroanthus avellanedae

Handroanthus avellanedae, commonly known as the yellow trumpet tree, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling trumpets. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically growing up to 5 meters in height. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate, with a glossy green surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
