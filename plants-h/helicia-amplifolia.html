Helicia amplifolia

Helicia amplifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved helicia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Theaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often broader than they are long. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible berries that are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in primary rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
