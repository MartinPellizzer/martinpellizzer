Hieracium argyrotrichum

Hieracium argyrotrichum, commonly known as the silver-haired hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features pale yellow to white flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, with hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, silvery hairs. It thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as meadows and roadsides.

Hieracium argyrotrichum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
