Helianthemum ruficomum

Helianthemum ruficomum, commonly known as the red-stemmed rockrose, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fleshy leaves that are typically grayish-green in color, adapted to withstand arid conditions. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a vibrant touch to rocky or sandy soils. Its stems are often reddish or purplish, which gives it its common name.

Helianthemum ruficomum is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a suitable plant for rock gardens or xeriscaping.
