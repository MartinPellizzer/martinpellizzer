Helichrysum rhodolepis

Helichrysum rhodolepis, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and bright yellow to orange flowers. The plant produces small, papery bracts that give it a fluffy appearance, often mistaken for actual flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its aromatic foliage and long-lasting flowers make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable.
