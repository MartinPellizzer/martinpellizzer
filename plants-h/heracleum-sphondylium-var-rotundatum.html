Heracleum sphondylium var. rotundatum

Heracleum sphondylium var. rotundatum, commonly known as cow parsnip, is a member of the Apiaceae family and is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its large, umbrella-shaped compound umbels that produce small, yellowish flowers. The plant has thick, hollow stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are broadly ovate with lobed margins. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in wetlands.

The plant is noted for its mild, carrot-like flavor and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
