Hippeastrum concolor

Hippeastrum concolor, commonly known as the white hurricane, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in late summer or early autumn. The plant produces strap-shaped, dark green leaves that emerge from a bulbous stem. Its flowers are typically trumpet-shaped with six petals and are often accompanied by a prominent, spiky seed capsule.

Hippeastrum concolor is popular as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
