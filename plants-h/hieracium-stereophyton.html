Hieracium stereophyton

Hieracium stereophyton, commonly known as the clustered hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It has a rosette of basal leaves and produces yellow flowers that are arranged in a clustered pattern.

Hieracium stereophyton is often found in disturbed soils and can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
