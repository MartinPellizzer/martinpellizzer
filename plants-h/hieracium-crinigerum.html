Hieracium crinigerum

Hieracium crinigerum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant is distinguished by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a rough, textured appearance. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed sites, preferring well-drained soils.

Hieracium crinigerum is known for its yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a vibrant touch to its natural habitat.
