Hieracium murorum subsp. trigonodon

Hieracium murorum subsp.

trigonodon, commonly known as the three-lobed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and flowering stems that bear yellow disk flowers surrounded by ray florets.

The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its subspecies name, *trigonodon*, refers to the triangular shape of its leaf lobes.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
