Hieracium olivaceum subsp. blitoides

Hieracium olivaceum subsp.

blitoides, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 40 cm.

The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, with flowering stems that bear yellow, daisy-like flowers. Its inflorescence consists of a single yellow ray floret surrounded by yellow disk florets, and it produces seeds in a schizocarp that splits into two one-seeded units.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in many temperate regions.
