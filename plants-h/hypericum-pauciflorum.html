Hypericum pauciflorum

Hypericum pauciflorum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features opposite, narrow, and often hairy leaves that are typically green with a slight reddish tinge. Its stems are woody and covered in fine, yellowish hairs.

Hypericum pauciflorum is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.
