Hedyotis valida

Hedyotis valida, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky areas. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Hedyotis valida is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
