Hordeum vulgare var. ibericum

Hordeum vulgare var. ibericum, commonly known as Iberian barley, is a cultivated variety of barley native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and shorter stature compared to other barley varieties. The plant typically produces small, two-rowed spikelets with palea and lemma structures typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in temperate climates and is adapted to the specific soil and climatic conditions of southern Europe.

This variety is valued for its use in traditional agriculture and local food systems in the region.
