Hypericum monogynum var. franchetii

Hypericum monogynum var. franchetii, commonly known as the Franchet's St. John's Wort, is a low-growing, perennial shrub native to China.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
