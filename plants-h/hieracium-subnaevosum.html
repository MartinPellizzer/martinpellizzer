Hieracium subnaevosum

Hieracium subnaevosum, commonly known as the spotted hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 20–60 cm, with hairy stems and basal leaves that are lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are yellow, with a central disk surrounded by 10–20 ray florets, and it produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

Hieracium subnaevosum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed sites, and it can be invasive in certain regions.
