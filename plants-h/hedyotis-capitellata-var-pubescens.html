Hedyotis capitellata var. pubescens

Hedyotis capitellata var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy blushing geranium, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a faintly blushing hue. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its varietal name "pubescens.". It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and attractive addition to gardens and natural habitats.
