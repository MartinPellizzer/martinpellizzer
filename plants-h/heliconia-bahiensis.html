Heliconia bahiensis

Heliconia bahiensis, commonly known as the Bahamian heliconia, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, spatheate inflorescences that resemble large, colorful flowers. The plant produces clusters of small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by brightly colored bracts. Its leaves are large, broad, and often have a waxy texture, which helps in water retention in its native habitat.

Heliconia bahiensis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
