Halimocnemis kirilowii

Halimocnemis kirilowii, commonly known as the Kirilow's tamarisk, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Tamaricaceae family and is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It is often found growing in saline soils and can thrive in harsh, semi-desert conditions.

Its structure and morphology make it a resilient species in ecological restoration projects.
