Hieracium longifoliosum

Hieracium longifoliosum, commonly known as the long-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

Hieracium longifoliosum is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in many regions.
