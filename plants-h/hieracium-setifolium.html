Hieracium setifolium

Hieracium setifolium, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance.

It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators.
