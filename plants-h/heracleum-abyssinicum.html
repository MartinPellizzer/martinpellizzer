Heracleum abyssinicum

Heracleum abyssinicum, commonly known as Abyssinian cowparn, is a member of theApiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences.

It is native to Ethiopia and grows in high-altitude regions, often in grasslands and rocky areas. The plant features large, pinnately compound leaves with serrated margins, and its flowers are small, white to greenish in color. It is known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in local remedies for various ailments.

However, it can be toxic if ingested in large quantities, requiring caution in its use.
