Hymenoclea salsola var. patula

Hymenoclea salsola var. patula, commonly known as desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a low-growing annual herb with a distinctive, sprawling growth habit. The plant features small, pale green leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss in harsh environments. Its flowers are typically pale blue or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

This species is adapted to thrive in sandy soils and is often found in deserts and along coastal dunes.
