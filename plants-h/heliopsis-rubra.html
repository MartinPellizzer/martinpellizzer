Heliopsis rubra

Heliopsis rubra, commonly known as the red false sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and showy, bright orange to red flowers that bloom in late summer. Heliopsis rubra thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
