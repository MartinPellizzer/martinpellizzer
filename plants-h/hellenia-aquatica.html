Hellenia aquatica

Hellenia aquatica, commonly known as water helena, is a perennial herb native to wetland and aquatic environments.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, with long, narrow leaves that float on the surface. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Hellenia aquatica plays an important role in maintaining the ecological balance of wetland ecosystems.
