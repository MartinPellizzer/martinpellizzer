Habenaria avana

Habenaria avana, commonly known as the purple-veined orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, showy flowers with purple veins on a white or pale pink background. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowering period usually occurs in the spring or early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
