Hieracium pseudotomentosum

Hieracium pseudotomentosum, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features finely hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and can be found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks.

Its yellow-orange flower heads bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
