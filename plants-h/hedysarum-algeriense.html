Hedysarum algeriense

Hedysarum algeriense, commonly known as Algerian milkvetch, is a perennial leguminous plant native to North Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

Hedysarum algeriense plays a role in soil stabilization and is valued for its ecological and medicinal properties.
