Hieracium praecurrens subsp. macrocardiophyllum

Hieracium praecurrens subsp.

macrocardiophyllum, commonly known as the large-heart hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It features a basal rosette of broadly ovate, hairy leaves that are typically green with a bluish tinge.

The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, flat-topped clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common weed in temperate regions.
