Helianthemum glutinosum var. juniperinum

Helianthemum glutinosum var. juniperinum, commonly known as sticky rockrose, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that produce a sticky secretion, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and inland scrub habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in arid environments.
