Hakea platysperma

Hakea platysperma, commonly known as the broad-fruited hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces large, cylindrical flower spikes that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. Its distinctive fruit is a woody, spherical structure that splits open to release seeds.

Hakea platysperma is well adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy soils.
