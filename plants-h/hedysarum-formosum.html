Hedysarum formosum

Hedysarum formosum, commonly known as the Japanese milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a perennial legume that typically grows in woodland and grassland habitats. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish to reddish flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its trifoliate leaves are compound, with three leaflets that are often hairy.

Hedysarum formosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for ecological restoration.
