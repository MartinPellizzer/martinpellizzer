Hieracium laevigatum subsp. albaredanum

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

albaredanum, commonly known as the white-veined hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features basal and stem leaves that are lanceolate to oblanceolate in shape, with a hairy surface. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a single head, and are surrounded by bracts.

The subspecies is distinguished by its white veins on the leaves, which give it its common name.
