Helichrysum albanense

Helichrysum albanense, commonly known as the Albanian everlasting, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of yellow to orange flower heads that remain intact even after the plant has dried. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Helichrysum albanense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
