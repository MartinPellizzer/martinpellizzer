Hieracium porrectum subsp. porrectum

Hieracium porrectum subsp.

porrectum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and yellow composite flowers that are typically arranged in a single head.

The plant has basal and stem leaves that are broadly lance-shaped with serrated edges. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in a variety of habitats including meadows and roadsides.

Due to its invasive tendencies in some regions, it is sometimes considered a weed.
