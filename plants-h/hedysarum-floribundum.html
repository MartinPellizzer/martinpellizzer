Hedysarum floribundum

Hedysarum floribundum, commonly known as the sweet clover, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural and ecological contexts. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Hedysarum floribundum is often used as forage for livestock and in soil restoration projects due to its hardy nature.
