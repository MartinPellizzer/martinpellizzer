Hieracium crispatulum

Hieracium crispatulum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers that are typically arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in meadows and grasslands.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can spread aggressively in certain environments.
