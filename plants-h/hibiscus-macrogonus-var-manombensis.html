Hibiscus macrogonus var. manombensis

Hibiscus macrogonus var. manombensis, commonly known as the Manombensis hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a smooth surface and a woody stem structure. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant.
