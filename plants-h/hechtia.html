Hechtia

Hechtia, commonly known as the "Mexican hat" or "false heather," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Bromeliaceae.

These plants are native to Mexico and Central America, where they typically grow in shaded, humid environments. Hechtia species are characterized by their rosette-shaped leaves that form a water-holding cup, a feature common among bromeliads. The flowers, which are often brightly colored, emerge from the center of the rosette and can last for several weeks.

They are popular in horticulture for their ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
