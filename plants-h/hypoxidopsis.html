Hypoxidopsis

Hypoxidopsis, commonly known as the silverleaf daisy, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The leaves are typically silver or gray-green, covered in fine hairs that give them a distinctive appearance. Hypoxidopsis species are native to South Africa and are often grown as ornamental plants for their attractive foliage and blooms.

They prefer well-drained soil and full sun, making them suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
