Hosackia decumbens var. nevadensis

Hosackia decumbens var. nevadensis, commonly known as the Nevada false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats. The plant features slender, prostrate stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. Its flowers are blue to purple, appearing in racemes during the spring and early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
