Hermannia dryadifolia

Hermannia dryadifolia, commonly known as the forest bell, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as under forest canopies. Its leaves are usually broad and glossy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Hermannia dryadifolia is often cultivated for its beauty and is valued in horticulture for its ability to thrive in humid, low-light conditions.
