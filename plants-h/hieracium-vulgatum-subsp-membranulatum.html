Hieracium vulgatum subsp. membranulatum

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

membranulatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm.

The plant has basal leaves that are narrow and linear, with a hairy surface, and produces yellow flowers in clusters. Its flowering period occurs from June to August, and it is characterized by its hairy stems and involucral bracts.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
