Helicteres hirsuta var. normalis

Helicteres hirsuta var. normalis, commonly known as the hairy helicteres, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are often covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by spiny, woody fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
