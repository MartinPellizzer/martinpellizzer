Hydrangea rotundifolia

Hydrangea rotundifolia, commonly known as the mountain hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, rounded leaves and showy inflorescences that range from white to pink and blue depending on soil pH. The plant prefers acidic soil and partial shade, making it well-suited for woodland gardens. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to the landscape.

Hydrangea rotundifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in moist, well-drained environments.
