Hieracium conquense

Hieracium conquense, commonly known as the Andes groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool and temperate climates. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range in color from yellow to orange.

Hieracium conquense plays a role in its ecosystem by providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to soil stability in its native habitat.
