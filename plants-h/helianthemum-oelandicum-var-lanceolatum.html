Helianthemum oelandicum var. lanceolatum

Helianthemum oelandicum var. lanceolatum, commonly known as the lanceolate rockrose, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to southern Europe.

It features small, needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, covered in a fine layer of whitish wax. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months, adding a bright touch to rocky or coastal habitats. It is well adapted to dry, sunny conditions and thrives in well-drained soils.

This variety is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant in arid landscapes.
