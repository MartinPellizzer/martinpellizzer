Hieracium callimorphum subsp. petrakianum

Hieracium callimorphum subsp.

petrakianum, commonly known as the Petrakian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and bright yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats at high altitudes. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is distinguished by its unique floral structure and is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and ecological specificity.
