Hebeclinium guevarae

Hebeclinium guevarae, commonly known as the Guevara's hebeclinium, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, pale blue to purple flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
