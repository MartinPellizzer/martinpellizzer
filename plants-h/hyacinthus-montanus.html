Hyacinthus montanus

Hyacinthus montanus, commonly known as the alpine hyacinth, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, violet, and white. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its bulbs are adapted to survive harsh winters, and it blooms in early spring.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and alpine landscapes.
