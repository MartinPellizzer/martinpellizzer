Hydrogaster trinervis

Hydrogaster trinervis, commonly known as the three-veined aquatic fern, is a unique plant characterized by its delicate, water-filled fronds.

It belongs to the family Hydrogastereae and is native to certain wetland environments. The plant's fronds are typically divided into three main veins, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in moist, shaded areas and is often found in marshy habitats.

Due to its specialized adaptations, it plays a minor but important role in aquatic ecosystems.
