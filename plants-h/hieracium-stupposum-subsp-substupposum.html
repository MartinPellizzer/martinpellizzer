Hieracium stupposum subsp. substupposum

Hieracium stupposum subsp.

substupposum, commonly known as the purple hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and tall, branched flowering stems.

The plant produces yellow to orange flowers with ray florets, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
