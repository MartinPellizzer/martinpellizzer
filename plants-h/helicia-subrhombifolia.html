Helicia subrhombifolia

Helicia subrhombifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf helicia, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical to ovate leaves that are often glossy and dark green in color. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by round, woody fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
