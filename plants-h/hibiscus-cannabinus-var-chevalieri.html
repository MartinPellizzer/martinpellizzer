Hibiscus cannabinus var. chevalieri

Hibiscus cannabinus var. chevalieri, commonly known as the Cape hibiscus, is a variant of the hibiscus plant native to coastal regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a prominent yellow center. The plant has heart-shaped, broad leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

Hibiscus cannabinus var. chevalieri is also valued for its ability to stabilize sandy soils and its historical use in traditional medicine.
