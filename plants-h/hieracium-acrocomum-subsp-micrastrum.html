Hieracium acrocomum subsp. micrastrum

Hieracium acrocomum subsp.

micrastrum, commonly known as the small-flowered hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and numerous yellow flowers arranged in a composite head.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring temperate climates. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, and it produces small, elongated achenes as its seeds.

The subspecies micrastrum is distinguished by its smaller flower heads and shorter stems compared to other members of the species.
