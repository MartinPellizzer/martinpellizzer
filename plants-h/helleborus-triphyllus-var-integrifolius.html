Helleborus triphyllus var. integrifolius

Helleborus triphyllus var. integrifolius, commonly known as the white hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features dark green, leathery leaves that are typically trifoliate or palmately compound. The plant produces showy, nodding flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple during late winter to early spring. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
