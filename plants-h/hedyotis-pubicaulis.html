Hedyotis pubicaulis

Hedyotis pubicaulis, commonly known as the hairy buttonweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common weed in agricultural and garden settings.
