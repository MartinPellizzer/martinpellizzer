Heteromorpha arborescens var. frutescens

Heteromorpha arborescens var. frutescens, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic wood that is highly valued for its durability and unique coloration. The plant typically has elliptical to lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. Its flowers are small and yellowish-green, often arranged in clusters, and it produces small, round, dark brown fruits.

The plant is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in lowland forests or on rocky slopes.
