Haageocereus dichromus

Haageocereus dichromus, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a flowering cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night. The plant has a ribbed structure with areoles that bear both spines and flowers. Its flowers are typically short-lived, lasting only a few hours, and are pollinated by nocturnal insects.

The species is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water to survive.
