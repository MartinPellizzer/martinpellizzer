Hieracium murorum subsp. orphnolepium

Hieracium murorum subsp.

orphnolepium, commonly known as the mouse-ear hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellowish to orange-red flower heads.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas. It has small, hairy leaves and produces numerous small, disc-like flowers that bloom in the summer.

The subspecies orphnolepium is distinguished by its more compact growth habit and specific floral characteristics compared to other forms of Hieracium murorum.
