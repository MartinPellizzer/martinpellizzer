Hemianemia buniifolia var. tenuifolia

Hemianemia buniifolia var. tenuifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hemianemia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat requirements, it is a subject of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
