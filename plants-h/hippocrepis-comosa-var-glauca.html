Hippocrepis comosa var. glauca

Hippocrepis comosa var. glauca, commonly known as the glaucous helmet orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a waxy, bluish coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
