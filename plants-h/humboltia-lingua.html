Humboltia lingua

Humboltia lingua, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of California and Baja California.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and broad, ovate leaves. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Humboltia lingua is adapted to coastal climates and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
