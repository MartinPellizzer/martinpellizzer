Hymenophyllum aculeolatum

Hymenophyllum aculeolatum, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a small, epiphytic fern native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its delicate, leathery fronds covered with fine, hair-like structures called trichomes, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and are protected by a specialized covering.

This fern is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
