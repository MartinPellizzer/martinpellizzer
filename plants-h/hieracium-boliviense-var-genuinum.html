Hieracium boliviense var. genuinum

Hieracium boliviense var. genuinum, commonly known as the Bolivian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America, particularly Bolivia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It features a basal rosette of leaves that are usually hairy and have a rough texture.

Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, making it easily identifiable in its native environment.
