Hieracium maculatum subsp. lavanicum

Hieracium maculatum subsp.

lavanicum, commonly known as the lavender hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves that are hairy and often have a bluish or purplish hue.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
