Heracleum sphondylium subsp. granatense

Heracleum sphondylium subsp.

granatense, commonly known as the Granatense cow parsnip, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and riverbanks.

The plant features a tall, hollow stem with finely divided, fern-like leaves. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and arranged in umbrella-like clusters called umbels.

The fruit is a small, dry, ribbed achene that is often used in traditional medicine.
