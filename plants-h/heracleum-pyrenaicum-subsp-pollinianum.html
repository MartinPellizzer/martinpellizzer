Heracleum pyrenaicum subsp. pollinianum

Heracleum pyrenaicum subsp.

pollinianum, commonly known as the Pollinian hogweed, is a biennial or short-lived perennial herb native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its tall, hollow stems that are often covered with fine, white hairs.

The plant produces large, umbrella-like inflorescences composed of small, greenish-yellow flowers. Its leaves are deeply lobed and can reach up to 50 cm in length, with a distinctive, aromatic odor.

It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, riverbanks, and woodland edges.
