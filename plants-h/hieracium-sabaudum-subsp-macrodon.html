Hieracium sabaudum subsp. macrodon

Hieracium sabaudum subsp.

macrodon, commonly known as the giant hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are typically hairy and have a rough texture. It produces numerous slender, branching stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height.

The subspecies is distinguished by its larger flower heads and longer, more robust leaves compared to other Hieracium species.
