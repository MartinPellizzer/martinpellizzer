Hemizonia congesta subsp. calyculata

Hemizonia congesta subsp.

calyculata, commonly known as the narrowleaf hemizonia, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, blooming during the cooler months. Its flowering stems are often branched and bear numerous flowers in dense inflorescences.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
