Hypolaena crinalis

Hypolaena crinalis, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

Hypolaena crinalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
