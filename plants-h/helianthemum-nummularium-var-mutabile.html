Helianthemum nummularium var. mutabile

Helianthemum nummularium var. mutabile, commonly known as the variable stonecrop, is a low-growing, succulent plant native to southern Europe.

It features fleshy, rounded leaves that are typically gray-green in color, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are borne on short, upright stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its variability in leaf size and flower color makes it a distinctive and attractive addition to garden landscapes.
