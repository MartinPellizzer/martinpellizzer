Hakea westoniana

Hakea westoniana, commonly known as the Westonia hakea, is an evergreen shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, cylindrical or conical seed pods that remain on the plant for years. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces bright yellow flowers in spring, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences.

Hakea westoniana is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.
