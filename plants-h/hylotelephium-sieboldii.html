Hylotelephium sieboldii

Hylotelephium sieboldii, commonly known as the Japanese stonecrop, is a succulent perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features fleshy, thick leaves that are typically blue-green in color, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces vibrant purple or pink flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements contribute to its widespread cultivation in gardens worldwide.
