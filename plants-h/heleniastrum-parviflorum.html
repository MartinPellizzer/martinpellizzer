Heleniastrum parviflorum

Heleniastrum parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered helianthea, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite flower heads. Native to the eastern United States, it thrives in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and wetlands. The plant's flowers are typically visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.

Its growth habit and floral structure are adapted to wetland environments, making it an important indicator species for aquatic habitats.
