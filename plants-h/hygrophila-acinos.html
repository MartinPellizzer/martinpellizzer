Hygrophila acinos

Hygrophila acinos, commonly known as the slender waterhyssop, is a perennial herb native to wetland habitats in Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, showcasing its adaptability to aquatic environments. The plant features opposite, linear to lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pale blue or purple, arranged in spikes, and bloom during the summer months.

Hygrophila acinos plays a role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic insects and small vertebrates.
