Hesperis arenaria

Hesperis arenaria, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its distinctive blue or violet flowers and finely divided leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, adapting to salty and well-drained environments. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hesperis arenaria is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
