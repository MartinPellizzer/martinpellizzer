Hibbertia archeri

Hibbertia archeri, commonly known as the pink-veined hibbertia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in heathlands and open forests, often in sandy or well-drained soils. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with distinctive pink veins, which give it its common name. Its showy yellow flowers, which appear in spring, are clustered in racemes and attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.

Hibbertia archeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
