Hieracium murorum subsp. asarifolium

Hieracium murorum subsp.

asarifolium, commonly known as the asarifolium hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering heads.

The leaves are lanceolate to ovate, with a hairy surface, and the flowers are typically yellow, arranged in a composite head. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

This subspecies is often confused with other hawkweed species due to its similar appearance.
