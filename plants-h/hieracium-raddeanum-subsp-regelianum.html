Hieracium raddeanum subsp. regelianum

Hieracium raddeanum subsp.

regelianum, commonly known as the Regelian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It typically grows in dry, sunny grasslands and rocky soils, exhibiting a low, spreading growth habit.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, with numerous small florets. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it has a fibrous root system.

It is often found in disturbed habitats and is considered a weed in some agricultural areas.
