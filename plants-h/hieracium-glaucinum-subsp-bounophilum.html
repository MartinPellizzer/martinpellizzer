Hieracium glaucinum subsp. bounophilum

Hieracium glaucinum subsp.

bounophilum, commonly known as the Bounophilum hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, blue-green leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in late summer. It has a taproot system and is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species within the genus Hieracium.
