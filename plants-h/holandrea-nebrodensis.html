Holandrea nebrodensis

Holandrea nebrodensis, commonly known as the Nebrodian bellflower, is a rare and endemic plant species native to the volcanic soils of Sicily.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers, which bloom in late summer, and its delicate, grass-like leaves. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained habitats and is adapted to the unique microclimate of its native region. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.

Conservation efforts are ongoing to protect this botanical gem from threats such as habitat loss and climate change.
