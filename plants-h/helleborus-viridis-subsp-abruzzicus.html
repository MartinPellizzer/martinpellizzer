Helleborus viridis subsp. abruzzicus

Helleborus viridis subsp.

abruzzicus, commonly known as the Abruzzi green hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that are usually heart-shaped and have a waxy texture.

The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, which later turn reddish. It prefers shady, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also noted for its medicinal properties.
