Hymenocallis incaica

Hymenocallis incaica, commonly known as the Inca lily, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pale yellow flowers with a prominent central spadix. The plant features strap-like, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Hymenocallis incaica is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
