Hyacinthus patentissimus

Hyacinthus patentissimus, commonly known as the Persian hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers with a prominent central stamen, which are typically 3-4 inches in height. The plant's leaves are grass-like, narrow, and arranged in a fan shape, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its historical significance and beauty, it is often cultivated for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
