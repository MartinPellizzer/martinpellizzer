Hippaton gummiferum

Hippaton gummiferum, commonly known as the gum tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers, which bloom in spring. Its bark is thick and fissured, often exuding a sticky resin.

This species is adapted to arid environments and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
