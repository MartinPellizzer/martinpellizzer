Hyptis irregularis

Hyptis irregularis, commonly known as the roughleaf hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks.

Hyptis irregularis is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
