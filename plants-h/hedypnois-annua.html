Hedypnois annua

Hedypnois annua, commonly known as the annual hedypnois, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features slender, erect stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are often pubescent. Its small, clustered flowers are usually white or pale yellow, and it produces small, dry fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Hedypnois annua is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
