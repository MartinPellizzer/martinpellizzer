Hosta ensata var. foliata

Hosta ensata var. foliata, commonly known as the Japanese hosta, is a striking perennial plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its broad, waxy, and often variegated leaves that can range from deep green to shades of white and yellow. The plant produces upright, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or white, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental foliage and delicate blooms, it is a popular choice for shaded garden landscapes.
