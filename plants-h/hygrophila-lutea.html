Hygrophila lutea

Hygrophila lutea, commonly known as the yellow hygrophila, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are typically bright green with a slight yellowish tint. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in clusters, adding a delicate touch to its aquatic habitat.

Hygrophila lutea is often used in water gardens and as a decorative plant in aquariums due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability.
