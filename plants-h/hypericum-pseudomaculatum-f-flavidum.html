Hypericum pseudomaculatum f. flavidum

Hypericum pseudomaculatum f. flavidum, commonly known as the yellow-stemmed St. John's wort, is a variant of the species Hypericum pseudomaculatum.

It is a shrub characterized by its distinctive yellowish stems and bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny environments and is native to parts of Europe. Its foliage consists of oval, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers bloom in late summer, producing clusters of five-petaled yellow flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
