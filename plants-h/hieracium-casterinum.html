Hieracium casterinum

Hieracium casterinum, commonly known as the Casterin's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of rough, hairy leaves and produces yellow or orange composite flowers. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
