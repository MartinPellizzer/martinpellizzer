Huperzia mandiocana

Huperzia mandiocana, commonly known as the Brazilian clubmoss, is a species of fern ally native to South America.

It is characterized by its simple, erect stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Huperzia mandiocana reproduces via spores rather than seeds, a feature common among its evolutionary group.

Its structure and growth habits reflect its adaptation to tropical and subtropical climates.
