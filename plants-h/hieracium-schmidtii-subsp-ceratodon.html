Hieracium schmidtii subsp. ceratodon

Hieracium schmidtii subsp.

ceratodon, commonly known as Schmidt's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are often hairy and have a serrated edge, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a cluster.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
