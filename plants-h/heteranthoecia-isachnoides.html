Heteranthoecia isachnoides

Heteranthoecia isachnoides, commonly known as the narrow-leafed heteranthoecia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a fibrous root system and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
