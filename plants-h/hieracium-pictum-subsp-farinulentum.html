Hieracium pictum subsp. farinulentum

Hieracium pictum subsp.

farinulentum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are hairy and have a distinctive rough texture, while the stems are often reddish in color.

It produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its spread across open habitats.
