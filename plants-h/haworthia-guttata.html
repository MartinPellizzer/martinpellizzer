Haworthia guttata

Haworthia guttata, commonly known as the Zebra Plant, is a small, compact succulent native to South Africa.

It features a rosette of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with white or yellow tubercles resembling zebra stripes. The plant produces tall, flowering stems with clusters of white or pale pink flowers during the summer months. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
