Hieracium tonsilingua subsp. griseicaule

Hieracium tonsilingua subsp.

griseicaule, commonly known as the gray-stemmed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and rough, hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and clustered in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
