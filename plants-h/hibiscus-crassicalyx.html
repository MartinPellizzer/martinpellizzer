Hibiscus crassicalyx

Hibiscus crassicalyx, commonly known as the broadleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Hibiscus crassicalyx is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
