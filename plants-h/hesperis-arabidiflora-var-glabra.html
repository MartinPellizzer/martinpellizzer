Hesperis arabidiflora var. glabra

Hesperis arabidiflora var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth white hound's tongue, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of smooth, lance-shaped leaves and produces striking violet-blue flowers in early summer. The plant is typically found in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.

The variety *glabra* is distinguished by its lack of hair on the leaves, giving it a sleek, smooth texture.
