Hieracium viladrauense

Hieracium viladrauense, commonly known as the Viladrau hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has yellow-orange flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species in its native range.
