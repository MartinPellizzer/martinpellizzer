Hemionitis Sargentii

It belongs to the Pteridaceae family and is known for its unique morphology and ecological significance. This fern-like species thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories. Its delicate fronds and specialized adaptations make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
