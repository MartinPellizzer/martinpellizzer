Hieracium paracriodes

Hieracium paracriodes, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are yellow and bloom from late spring to early summer, while its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Hieracium paracriodes is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes.
