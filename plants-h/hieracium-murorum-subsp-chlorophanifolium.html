Hieracium murorum subsp. chlorophanifolium

Hieracium murorum subsp.

chlorophanifolium, commonly known as the green-leafed hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in the summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its broader, more chlorophyll-rich leaves compared to other hawkweed species. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and grasslands.

This plant is often found in temperate regions of Europe and has a relatively restricted distribution.
