Hieracium dentatum proles pseudoporrectum

Hieracium dentatum proles pseudoporrectum, commonly known as the false spearthistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features rough, hairy stems and lanceolate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are typically yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and bloom in summer.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and wastelands.
