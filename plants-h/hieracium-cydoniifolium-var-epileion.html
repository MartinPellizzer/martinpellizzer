Hieracium cydoniifolium var. epileion

Hieracium cydoniifolium var. epileion, commonly known as the Creeping Hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features a rosette of basal leaves that are broadly ovate to spoon-shaped, with a waxy coating and serrated edges. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense clusters during the summer months, typically from June to August. Its hairy stems are often reddish in color and can spread aggressively through stolons.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.
