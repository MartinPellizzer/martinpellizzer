Hieracium lopholepidioides

Hieracium lopholepidioides, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
