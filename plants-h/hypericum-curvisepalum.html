Hypericum curvisepalum

Hypericum curvisepalum, commonly known as the zigzag-stemmed St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems and opposite, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its flowers have five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Hypericaceae family.

Hypericum curvisepalum is often found in moist, shaded woodlands and is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance.
