Hieracium vulgatum subsp. levigatifolium

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

levigatifolium, commonly known as the smooth-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The leaves are typically smooth and elongated, with a distinctive lack of hairy texture. It thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats, often found in meadows and along roadsides.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its historical use in traditional medicine.
