Hippophae carpatica

Hippophae carpatica, commonly known as the sandthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange berries, which are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. The plant has small, narrow leaves and produces fragrant yellow flowers in the spring. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to harsh climates.

Its hardy nature makes it suitable for erosion control and ornamental gardening.
