Hypochaeris glauca

Hypochaeris glauca, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and thrives in sunny, open habitats. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in the spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hypochaeris glauca is often considered a weed but has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
