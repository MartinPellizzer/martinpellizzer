Houstonia humifusa

Houstonia humifusa, commonly known as the flatleaf bluebell, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, blooming in early spring. The plant has whorled leaves that are typically blue-green in color and slightly hairy. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

Houstonia humifusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
