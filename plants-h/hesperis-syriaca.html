Hesperis syriaca

Hesperis syriaca, commonly known as the Syrian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its bright blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hesperis syriaca is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be found in gardens and botanical collections.
