Hermione sulphurea

Hermione sulphurea, commonly known as the golden thistle, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and spiny leaves, which are typical of the genus Hermione. Native to southern Africa, this plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The flowers are usually arranged in a compact, spherical shape, and the plant produces small, hard seeds.

It is often used in herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
