Herschelia barbata

Herschelia barbata, commonly known as the barbed rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or dry soils, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in spring. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Herschelia barbata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its drought tolerance and visual interest.
