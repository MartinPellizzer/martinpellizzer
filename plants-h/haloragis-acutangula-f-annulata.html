Haloragis acutangula f. annulata

Haloragis acutangula f. annulata, commonly known as the ringed willow-herb, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in swampy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important species for wetland ecosystems and ecological studies.
