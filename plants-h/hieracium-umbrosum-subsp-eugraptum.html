Hieracium umbrosum subsp. eugraptum

Hieracium umbrosum subsp.

eugraptum, commonly known as the shadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and southern Europe. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

The plant features upright stems with basal leaves that are lanceolate and have a rough texture. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head, surrounded by bracts.

It is characterized by its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its preference for well-drained soils.
