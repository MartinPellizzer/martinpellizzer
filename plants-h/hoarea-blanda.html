Hoarea blanda

Hoarea blanda, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features delicate, hair-like leaves that give it its common name and contribute to its ornamental appeal. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and minimal maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for ecological landscaping.
