Helichrysum gazense

Helichrysum gazense, commonly known as the golden marguerite, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that retain their color even after drying. The plant typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its foliage is aromatic and often used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory properties.

Helichrysum gazense is also popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and resilience in various climates.
