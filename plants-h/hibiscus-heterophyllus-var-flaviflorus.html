Hibiscus heterophyllus var. flaviflorus

Hibiscus heterophyllus var. flaviflorus, commonly known as the yellow-flowered hibiscus, is a tropical plant characterized by its striking yellow flowers and variable leaf shapes.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is native to the Caribbean and parts of South America. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are often heart-shaped or lobed, and the flowers are showy with five petals.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
