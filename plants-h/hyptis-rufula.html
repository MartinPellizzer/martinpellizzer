Hyptis rufula

Hyptis rufula, commonly known as roughleaf hyptis, is a species of flowering plant in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of the Americas, particularly Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or scrub habitats.

Hyptis rufula is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
