Hybanthus glaucus

Hybanthus glaucus, commonly known as the blue hybanthus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Hybanthus glaucus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
