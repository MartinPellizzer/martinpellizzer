Heliodendron thozetianum

Heliodendron thozetianum, commonly known as the Thozet's flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, crimson-orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has palmately compound leaves that are arranged alternately along the branches. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, often exfoliating to reveal a lighter inner layer.

Heliodendron thozetianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens and parks for its vibrant floral display.
