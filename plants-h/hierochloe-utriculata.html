Hierochloe utriculata

Hierochloe utriculata, commonly known as the bladder grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, hollow stems that often have a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a dense covering of short hairs.

Hierochloe utriculata is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges.
