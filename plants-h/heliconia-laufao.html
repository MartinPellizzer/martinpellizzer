Heliconia laufao

Heliconia laufao, commonly known as the red heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its large, brightly colored bracts that resemble flowers. The plant produces clusters of small, inconspicuous true flowers surrounded by fleshy, lobster-claw-shaped bracts. Its leaves are long, broad, and often have a waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Heliconia laufao is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical landscapes.
