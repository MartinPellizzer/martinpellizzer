Hieracium chlorifolium subsp. chlorifolium

Hieracium chlorifolium subsp.

chlorifolium, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It features a rosette of basal leaves that are broadly ovate to lanceolate in shape, with a waxy coating.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typically blooming from June to August. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, and the stems are usually hairy as well.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.
