Hippocrepis squamata var. murcica

Hippocrepis squamata var. murcica, commonly known as the Murcian squamata gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is found in mountainous regions. It has lanceolate leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and its flowering period occurs during the summer months.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some areas.
