Helianthemum viscarium var. viscidum

Helianthemum viscarium var. viscidum, commonly known as the sticky sandverbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its small, fleshy leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its sticky, glandular hairs that cover its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive texture. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in coastal or rocky habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
