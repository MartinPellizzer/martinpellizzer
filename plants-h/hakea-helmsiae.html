Hakea helmsiae

Hakea helmsiae, commonly known as the Wimmera hakea, is a shrub native to Australia, specifically found in the Wimmera region of Victoria.

It is characterized by its distinctive, woody stems and needle-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces striking, cylindrical flower spikes that range in color from yellow to red, depending on the species. Hakea helmsiae is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance through its specialized leaf structure.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various native wildlife species.
