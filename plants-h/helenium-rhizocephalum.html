Helenium rhizocephalum

Helenium rhizocephalum, commonly known as the red swamp sunflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems that can grow up to 2 meters tall, adorned with large, showy orange to red flowers. The plant has fibrous roots and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Helenium rhizocephalum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in moist environments.
