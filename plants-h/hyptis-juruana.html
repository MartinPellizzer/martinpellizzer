Hyptis juruana

Hyptis juruana, commonly known as "Aromatic Hyptis," is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its grayish-green, hairy leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or violet flowers. Hyptis juruana is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Its essential oils, derived from the leaves, are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
