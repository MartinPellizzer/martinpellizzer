Hieracium patens subsp. lazergesii

Hieracium patens subsp.

lazergesii, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow daisy-like flowers and basal rosettes of leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

The species is notable for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
