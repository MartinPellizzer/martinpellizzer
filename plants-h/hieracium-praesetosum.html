Hieracium praesetosum

Hieracium praesetosum, commonly known as the prickly hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies. Its leaves are rough and hairy, with a distinctive yellowish or reddish hue, and its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in a composite head.

Hieracium praesetosum is known for its aggressive growth and can sometimes become invasive in certain regions.
