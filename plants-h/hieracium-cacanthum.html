Hieracium cacanthum

Hieracium cacanthum, commonly known as the clustered hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are basal, rough, and often covered with fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a dense cluster.

Hieracium cacanthum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to environmental stress.
