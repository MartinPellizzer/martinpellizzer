Hieracium murorum subsp. pseudomarginellum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

pseudomarginellum, commonly known as the false marginal hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers and hairy, lobed leaves.

This subspecies is native to parts of Europe and is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. It has a rosette growth form with basal leaves that are typically glandular and hairy.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in meadows and along roadsides.
