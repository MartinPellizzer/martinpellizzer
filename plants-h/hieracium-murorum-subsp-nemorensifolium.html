Hieracium murorum subsp. nemorensifolium

Hieracium murorum subsp.

nemorensifolium, commonly known as the woodland hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering stems that bear yellow composite flowers.

The leaves are typically lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are arranged in a single head. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

This subspecies is distinguished by its broader, more elongated leaves compared to other members of the Hieracium genus.
