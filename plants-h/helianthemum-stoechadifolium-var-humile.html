Helianthemum stoechadifolium var. humile

Helianthemum stoechadifolium var. humile, commonly known as the dwarf stonecrop, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Scottish Highlands.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are typically clustered at the base of the plant. The species produces small, yellow-orange flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to withstand harsh alpine conditions.
