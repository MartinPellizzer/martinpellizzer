Hypoxis angustifolia var. luzuloides

Hypoxis angustifolia var. luzuloides, commonly known as narrowleaf false shamrock, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and grass-green, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
