Hardenbergia cordata

Hardenbergia cordata, commonly known as the sickle pea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, curved pods. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in sandy or gravelly soils. Its small, pink to purple flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

The hardy nature of Hardenbergia cordata makes it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
