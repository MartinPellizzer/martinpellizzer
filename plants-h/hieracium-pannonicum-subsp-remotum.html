Hieracium pannonicum subsp. remotum

Hieracium pannonicum subsp.

remotum, commonly known as the remote meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to orange-yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring moist to moderately dry conditions. It has hairy stems and leaves with a rough texture, and its basal leaves are usually lance-shaped.

This subspecies is often found in open habitats and is relatively widespread across its native range.
