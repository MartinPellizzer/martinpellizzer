Hirsutiarum

Hirsutiarum, commonly known as the hairy tamarisk, is a shrub belonging to the genus Tamarix.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and clusters of pink or reddish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa. It has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in drought conditions.

Hirsutiarum is often used for erosion control due to its ability to stabilize sandy soils.
