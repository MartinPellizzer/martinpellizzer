Hepetis nuda

Hepetis nuda, commonly known as the naked hepatica, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its basal leaves and showy, early spring flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually white or pale blue, with five petals and numerous stamens.

Hepetis nuda is noted for its ecological importance, providing early nectar sources for pollinators.
