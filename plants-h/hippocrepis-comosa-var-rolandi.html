Hippocrepis comosa var. rolandi

Hippocrepis comosa var. rolandi, commonly known as the Roland's thistle, is a perennial plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and purple to blue flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils.

This variety is often found in rocky or limestone habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments.
