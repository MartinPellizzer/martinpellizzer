Hymenocephalus

Hymenocephalus, commonly known as the golden thread orchid, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are characterized by their delicate, often yellow or orange-colored flowers, which are typically small and clustered in racemes. Hymenocephalus species are epiphytic, meaning they grow on the surfaces of trees and other plants without deriving nutrients from them. They are native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including countries like China, India, and Thailand.

The genus is noted for its ornamental value, making it popular among horticulturists and plant enthusiasts.
