Hieracium adunans

Hieracium adunans, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellow or orange composite flowers, which are actually made up of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it is native to Europe and parts of Asia. Hieracium adunans has lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and arranged alternately along the stem.

It is a perennial herb that can spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common but sometimes invasive species in certain regions.
