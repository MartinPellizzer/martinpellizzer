Hypericum kamtschaticum f. retusum

Hypericum kamtschaticum f. retusum, commonly known as the Kamtschatka St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to the Russian Far East and parts of northeastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Hypericaceae and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with four petals, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This variety is noted for its distinctive, slightly hairy foliage and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
