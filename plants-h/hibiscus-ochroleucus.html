Hibiscus ochroleucus

Hibiscus ochroleucus, commonly known as the yellow hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in summer. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and often branched.

Hibiscus ochroleucus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
