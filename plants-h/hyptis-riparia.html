Hyptis riparia

Hyptis riparia, commonly known as river hyptis, is a herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as riverbanks and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a minty scent.

Hyptis riparia is characterized by its clusters of small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of purple or white.
