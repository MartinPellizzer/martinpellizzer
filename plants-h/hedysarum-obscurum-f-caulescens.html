Hedysarum obscurum f. caulescens

Hedysarum obscurum f. caulescens, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in open, dry habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features slender, branching stems that are often hairy, with alternate, pinnately compound leaves. Its flowers are small and pea-like, usually pink to reddish in color, and are arranged in racemes.

The species is characterized by its unique floral structure and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
