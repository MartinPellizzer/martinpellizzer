Hedyotis stipulata

Hedyotis stipulata, commonly known as the small-stemmed bedstraw, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Asia and Europe.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender stems that are often hairy, and its small, opposite leaves are usually ovate or lanceolate in shape. It produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months.

The plant is known for its fragrant scent and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
