Helichrysum spiralepis

Helichrysum spiralepis, commonly known as the golden marguerite or spiral sunflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow-orange flowers that resemble small sunflowers, often with a spiral arrangement of petals. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a silvery appearance.

Helichrysum spiralepis is also known for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
