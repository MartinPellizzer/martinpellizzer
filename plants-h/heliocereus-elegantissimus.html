Heliocereus elegantissimus

Heliocereus elegantissimus, commonly known as the elegant night bloomer, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its tall, columnar stem and spiny structure. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its striking appearance and unique blooming habit, it is a popular choice for collectors and enthusiasts of rare succulents.
