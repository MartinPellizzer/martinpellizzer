Hakea monslacana

Hakea monslacana, commonly known as the Narrow-leaf Hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces woody, cone-like fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance. It thrives in sandy soils and is adapted to dry, coastal environments.

Hakea monslacana is often used in coastal landscaping due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
