Hedycarpus javanicus

Hedycarpus javanicus, commonly known as the Java bamboo or bamboo palm, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Araceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, palm-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in spadices, which are typical of the Araceae family. Hedycarpus javanicus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
