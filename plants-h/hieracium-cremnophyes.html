Hieracium cremnophyes

Hieracium cremnophyes, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disc.

Hieracium cremnophyes is often found in open meadows and grasslands, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinator populations.
