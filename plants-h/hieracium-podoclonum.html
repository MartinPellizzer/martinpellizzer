Hieracium podoclonum

Hieracium podoclonum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and ragworts, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium podoclonum is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in various habitats.
