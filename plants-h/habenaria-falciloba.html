Habenaria falciloba

Habenaria falciloba, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, greenish-white flowers with a distinctive, narrow lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its unique floral structure and subtle fragrance.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
