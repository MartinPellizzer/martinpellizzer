Hypericum pseudolaeve

Hypericum pseudolaeve, commonly known as the smooth St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typically from June to August. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in woodland edges or open fields.

Hypericum pseudolaeve is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
