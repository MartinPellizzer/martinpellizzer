Hieracium laevigatum subsp. savokarelicum

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

savokarelicum, commonly known as the Savokarelic hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

The plant features basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in early summer. It has a taproot system and is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

This subspecies is often found in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
