Helleborine atropurpurea

Helleborine atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple helleborine, is a perennial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple to blackish leaves and its showy, greenish-yellow flowers with purple veins. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist woodlands and thrives in well-drained, humus-rich soil. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ability to form mycorrhizal associations with fungi.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a striking visual element to its natural habitat.
