Haemanthus vaginatus

Haemanthus vaginatus, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features striking, spiky red or orange flowers that emerge from a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are red when mature, containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, often growing in rocky or sandy habitats.

The botanical characteristics of Haemanthus vaginatus make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
