Hemipilia graminifolia var. graminifolia

Hemipilia graminifolia var. graminifolia, commonly known as the grass-leaved orchid, is a species native to southern China.

It is a terrestrial herb characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, with a unique structure that facilitates pollination by specific insect species.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and ecological importance in its native habitat.
