Hemicarpha

Hemicarpha, commonly known as the beakvetch, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

These plants are typically found in arid and semi-arid regions, where they thrive in sandy or gravelly soils. Hemicarpha species are characterized by their erect or prostrate growth habit and small, pea-like flowers. The fruits are legumes that develop into elongated pods, which contain several seeds.

These plants are often used for soil stabilization and as forage for livestock in dryland environments.
