Hieronyma macrocarpa

Hieronyma macrocarpa, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Cerjeira-de-Brasil*, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark-brown bark and elliptical, glossy green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are edible for humans and animals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
