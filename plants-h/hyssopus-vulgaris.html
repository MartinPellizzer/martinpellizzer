Hyssopus vulgaris

Hyssopus vulgaris, commonly known as hyssop, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and spikes of blue or purple flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its aromatic foliage and ornamental value, and it has a long history of use in herbal medicine. Hyssop thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal collections.

Its essential oils contain compounds such as thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its medicinal properties.
