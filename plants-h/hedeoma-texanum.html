Hedeoma texanum

Hedeoma texanum, commonly known as Texas hedeoma, is a perennial herb native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. It features square stems and opposite leaves that are often hairy.

Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or blue, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
