Hevea minor

Hevea minor, commonly known as the lesser rubber tree, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small yellow flowers and is known for its latex sap, though it is not as commercially significant as Hevea brasiliensis. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and conservation efforts.
