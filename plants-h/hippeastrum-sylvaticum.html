Hippeastrum sylvaticum

Hippeastrum sylvaticum, commonly known as the wild amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant produces large, fleshy leaves that are typically broad and strap-like. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a period of dormancy to bloom successfully.

Hippeastrum sylvaticum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in warm climates.
