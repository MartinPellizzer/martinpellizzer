Hydrangea strigosa

Hydrangea strigosa, commonly known as the rough hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from white to pink or blue depending on soil pH. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is typically dark green and turns red or orange in the fall.

Hydrangea strigosa prefers partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
