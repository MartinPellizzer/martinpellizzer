Herorchis boryi

Herorchis boryi, commonly known as the Bory's orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a terrestrial herb with a sympodial growth habit, characterized by its rhizomes and pseudobulbs. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
