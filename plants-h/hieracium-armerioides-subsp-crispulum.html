Hieracium armerioides subsp. crispulum

Hieracium armerioides subsp.

crispulum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows. Its leaves are hairy and often have a wavy margin, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

It is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
