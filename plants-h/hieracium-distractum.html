Hieracium distractum

Hieracium distractum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are rough and hairy, with a basal rosette structure, and it produces yellow flowers in clusters.

Hieracium distractum is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in agricultural and natural habitats.
