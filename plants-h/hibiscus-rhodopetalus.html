Hibiscus rhodopetalus

Hibiscus rhodopetalus, commonly known as the rose mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and grows as an herbaceous perennial or shrub, depending on the climate. Its botanical features include a hairy stem and five-petaled flowers with a prominent central pistil.

Hibiscus rhodopetalus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
