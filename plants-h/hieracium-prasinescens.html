Hieracium prasinescens

Hieracium prasinescens, commonly known as the green hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and have a slightly serrated margin, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

Hieracium prasinescens is often found in disturbed habitats and can form dense colonies, making it a notable species in ecological studies.
