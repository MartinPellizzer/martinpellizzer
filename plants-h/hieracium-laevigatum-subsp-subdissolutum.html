Hieracium laevigatum subsp. subdissolutum

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

subdissolutum, commonly known as the smooth hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. Its leaves are smooth and lance-shaped, while its stems are usually upright and hairy.

It is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in some regions.
