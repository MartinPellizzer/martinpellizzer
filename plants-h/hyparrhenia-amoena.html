Hyparrhenia amoena

Hyparrhenia amoena, commonly known as the silverleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, silver-gray blades that give it its common name. The plant produces small, pale green spikelets that are often clustered at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Hyparrhenia amoena is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in degraded environments.
