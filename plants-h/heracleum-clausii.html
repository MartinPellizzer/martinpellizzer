Heracleum clausii

Heracleum clausii, commonly known as the Persian cow parsley, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbellate inflorescence.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Iran and Afghanistan. The plant features finely divided, feather-like leaves and produces small white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
