Heteropogon hirsutus

Heteropogon hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy panic grass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa, particularly South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy, linear leaves that are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open grasslands and along coastal areas.

Heteropogon hirsutus is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystems.
