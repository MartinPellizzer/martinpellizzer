Hoodia officinalis subsp. officinalis

Hoodia officinalis subsp.

officinalis, commonly known as the desert lily or hoodia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and small, white flowers that bloom during the rainy season.

The plant has a unique ability to store water in its thick, fleshy stems, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. It is also known for containing a compound called pregnenolone, which has been studied for its potential appetite-suppressing properties.

Due to overharvesting and habitat loss, it is now considered an endangered species.
