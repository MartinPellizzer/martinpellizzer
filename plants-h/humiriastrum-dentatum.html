Humiriastrum dentatum

Humiriastrum dentatum, commonly known as the Mexican fireweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, pink to red bracts that resemble flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have toothed margins, contributing to its scientific name.

Humiriastrum dentatum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its striking appearance.
