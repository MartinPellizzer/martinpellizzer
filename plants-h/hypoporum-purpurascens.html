Hypoporum purpurascens

Hypoporum purpurascens, commonly known as the red-veined orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with deep red veins. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth, waxy texture.

The flowers bloom in clusters and are pollinated primarily by insects, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
