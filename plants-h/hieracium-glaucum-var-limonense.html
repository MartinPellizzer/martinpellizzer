Hieracium glaucum var. limonense

Hieracium glaucum var. limonense, commonly known as the lemon-hued hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it identifiable by its leaf coloration and floral structure.
