Hieracium caesium var. petrocharis

Hieracium caesium var. petrocharis, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It has finely divided, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
