Hieracium geilingeri subsp. geilingeri

Hieracium geilingeri subsp.

geilingeri, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette-forming basal leaves and yellowish to orange composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, often in mountainous areas. It has a fibrous root system and produces numerous small, herbaceous shoots.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its range.
