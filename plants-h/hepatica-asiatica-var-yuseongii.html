Hepatica asiatica var. yuseongii

Hepatica asiatica var. yuseongii, commonly known as the Yuseong hepatica, is a perennial herb native to Korea.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves and showy, early spring blooms. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments, preferring well-drained, acidic soils. Its flowers, which range from white to pale blue, are often adorned with red or purple markings.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting early pollinators.
