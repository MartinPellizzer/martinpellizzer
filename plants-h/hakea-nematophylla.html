Hakea nematophylla

Hakea nematophylla, commonly known as the slender hakea, is a small evergreen shrub native to the south-western region of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are densely packed along the stems, giving it a distinctive, compact appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense spikes, typically blooming in spring. Hakea nematophylla is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited to arid environments.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
