Helianthemum ramuliflorum

Helianthemum ramuliflorum, commonly known as the rock rose or sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub with woody stems and small, often blue or violet flowers. The plant thrives in rocky or coastal environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Helianthemum ramuliflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils.
