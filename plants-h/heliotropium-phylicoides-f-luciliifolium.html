Heliotropium phylicoides f. luciliifolium

Heliotropium phylicoides f. luciliifolium, commonly known as the luciliifolium heliotrope, is a variant of the heliotrope species native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its distinctive, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that give it its specific epithet. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

This ornamental plant is valued for its foliage and is often used in landscaping and horticultural displays.
