Hieracium villosiflorum

Hieracium villosiflorum, commonly known as the hairy flowered hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm. Its leaves are hairy and lance-shaped, while the flowers are yellow with a distinctive hairy appearance.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
