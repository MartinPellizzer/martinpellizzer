Hippeastrum roseum

Hippeastrum roseum, commonly known as the pink amaryllis, is a striking ornamental plant native to South America.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from pale pink to deep red, often with a white or cream base. The plant produces large, strap-like leaves that are broad and glossy, contributing to its aesthetic appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Hippeastrum roseum is typically grown as a bulbous plant, with its bulbs requiring a cool rest period to encourage flowering.
