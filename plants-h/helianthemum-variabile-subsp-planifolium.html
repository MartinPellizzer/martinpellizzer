Helianthemum variabile subsp. planifolium

Helianthemum variabile subsp.

planifolium, commonly known as the flat-leaved sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically flat and arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to arid environments.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
