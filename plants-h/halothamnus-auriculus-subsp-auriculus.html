Halothamnus auriculus subsp. auriculus

Halothamnus auriculus subsp.

auriculus, commonly known as the auricled halothamnus, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. It is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including high temperatures and limited water availability.

Its succulent structure helps it store water, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
