Hebeclinium erioclinium

Hebeclinium erioclinium, commonly known as the woolly hebe, is a member of the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive, soft appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Native to New Zealand, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
