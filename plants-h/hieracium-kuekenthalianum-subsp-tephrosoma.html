Hieracium kuekenthalianum subsp. tephrosoma

Hieracium kuekenthalianum subsp.

tephrosoma, commonly known as the tephrosoma hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in grasslands and meadows.

The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are broadly ovate to lanceolate in shape. Its flowering stems bear yellow to orange composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk florets.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
