Hibiscus ponticus

Hibiscus ponticus, commonly known as the Crimean hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in wetland areas, marshes, and along riverbanks, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Hibiscus ponticus is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its beautiful blooms.
