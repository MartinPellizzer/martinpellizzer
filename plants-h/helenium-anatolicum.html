Helenium anatolicum

Helenium anatolicum, commonly known as the Anatolian sunflower, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Turkey, and thrives in well-drained, sunny soils. The plant features bright orange to red ray florets surrounding yellow disk florets, creating a striking floral display. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stem is upright and often branched.

Helenium anatolicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
