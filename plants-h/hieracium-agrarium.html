Hieracium agrarium

Hieracium agrarium, commonly known as the field dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, spoon-shaped leaves and a flowering head that produces bright yellow composite flowers. The plant is native to Europe and has been introduced to other regions, including North America. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and gardens.

The flowers are followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds, aiding in its widespread distribution.
