Heliotropium elongatum

Heliotropium elongatum, commonly known as the elongated heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial species that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features opposite, slender leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from pink to purple. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in coastal areas.

The species is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
