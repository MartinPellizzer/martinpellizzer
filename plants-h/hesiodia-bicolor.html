Hesiodia bicolor

Hesiodia bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored sandwort, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a small, herbaceous annual that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant features simple, linear leaves and produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters. Its botanical characteristics include a taproot system and a short, upright stem.

Hesiodia bicolor is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.
