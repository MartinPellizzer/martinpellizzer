Heisteria cyanocarpa subsp. occidentalis

Heisteria cyanocarpa subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the western blue-fruited heisteria, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico. It is a low-growing shrub with small, oval leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers, which are followed by blue-black berries. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid climates.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
