Herpestis pusilla

Herpestis pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant found in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its tiny, bladder-like structures that trap small aquatic organisms. The plant typically grows in shallow, still waters such as ponds and marshes. Its leaves are slender and often submerged, while its flowering stems rise to the surface.

Herpestis pusilla plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and providing habitat for microorganisms.
