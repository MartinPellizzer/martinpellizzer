Hymenophyllum capillare

Hymenophyllum capillare, commonly known as the slender maidenhair fern, is a small, epiphytic fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, thread-like fronds. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or rocks. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a feathery appearance, with a glossy, dark green color.

Hymenophyllum capillare is known for its intricate structure and is often found in humid, tropical forests.
