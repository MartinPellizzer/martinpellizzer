Hieracium carpathicum subsp. carpathicum

Hieracium carpathicum subsp.

carpathicum, commonly known as the Carpathian hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the Carpathian Mountains. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and forest edges. It has hairy stems and leaves that are often glandular, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is noted for its ecological importance in supporting local insect populations.
