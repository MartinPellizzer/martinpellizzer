Hackelia uncinata var. brachytuba

Hackelia uncinata var. brachytuba, commonly known as the short-stalked bluecurls, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit and produces clusters of blue to violet flowers. The plant features hairy stems and leaves with a serrated margin, typical of the Scrophulariaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
