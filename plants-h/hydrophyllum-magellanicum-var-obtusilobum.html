Hydrophyllum magellanicum var. obtusilobum

Hydrophyllum magellanicum var. obtusilobum, commonly known as the Magellanic hydrophyllum, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves with a hairy surface. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in dense clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
