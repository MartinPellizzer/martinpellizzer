Hibiscus cucurbitaceus

Hibiscus cucurbitaceus, commonly known as the cucumber hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family and is often found in wet, marshy habitats. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, hard seeds.

Hibiscus cucurbitaceus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
