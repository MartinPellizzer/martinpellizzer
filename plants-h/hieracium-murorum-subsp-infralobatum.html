Hieracium murorum subsp. infralobatum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

infralobatum, commonly known as the narrow-lobed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering heads that produce yellow disk flowers.

The leaves are typically narrow and lobed, with a distinctive basal arrangement. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
