Hyptis crinita

Hyptis crinita, commonly known as curly hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Hyptis crinita is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
