Hieracium sagittatum subsp. cineritum

Hieracium sagittatum subsp.

cineritum, commonly known as the cinerary hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring well-drained conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are arranged in a composite head.

It is known for its adaptability and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
