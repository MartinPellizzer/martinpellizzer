Hieracium kalksburgense

Hieracium kalksburgense, commonly known as the Kalsburg hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow daisy-like flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, bract-like leaves around its flower heads.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and threatened species in its native range.
