Habenaria trifurcata

Habenaria trifurcata, commonly known as the three-branch orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pale yellow or greenish-white. It prefers moist, shaded habitats and is often found in forest understories.

Habenaria trifurcata is noted for its intricate floral structure and is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal.
