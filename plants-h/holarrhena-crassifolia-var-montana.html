Holarrhena crassifolia var. montana

Holarrhena crassifolia var. montana, commonly known as the mountain holarrhena, is a shrub or small tree native to the high-altitude regions of East Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cooler, drier climates.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
