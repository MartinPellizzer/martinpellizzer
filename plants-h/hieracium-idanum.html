Hieracium idanum

Hieracium idanum, commonly known as the Italian hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Hieracium idanum is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it an invasive species in some regions.
