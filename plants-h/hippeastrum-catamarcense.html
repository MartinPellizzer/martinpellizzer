Hippeastrum catamarcense

Hippeastrum catamarcense, commonly known as the Catamarca amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to Argentina.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant features broad, strap-like leaves and produces clusters of trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. It is adapted to grow in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Hippeastrum catamarcense is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and as a potted plant.
