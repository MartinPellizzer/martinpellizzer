Hakea saligna

Hakea saligna, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hakea, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces cylindrical flower spikes that bear small, yellowish flowers, which are typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in coastal and sandy environments.

Hakea saligna is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
