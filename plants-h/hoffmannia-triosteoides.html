Hoffmannia triosteoides

Hoffmannia triosteoides, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a rough texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
