Heteropterys grandiflora f. lanceolata

Heteropterys grandiflora f. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceolate huito, is a variant of the huito plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces fleshy, orange-red berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and a woody structure with smooth, gray bark.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
