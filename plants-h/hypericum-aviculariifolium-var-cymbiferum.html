Hypericum aviculariifolium var. cymbiferum

Hypericum aviculariifolium var. cymbiferum, commonly known as the clustered St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and herbal gardens.
