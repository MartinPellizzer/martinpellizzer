Hakea latifolia

Hakea latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved hakea, is a shrub native to south-western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in tight clusters. The plant produces striking, bottlebrush-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red, depending on the variety. It is well adapted to arid and coastal environments, making it a hardy species.

Hakea latifolia is often used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
