Helicophyllum alberti

Helicophyllum alberti, commonly known as Albert's helicophyllum, is a rare and unique plant species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it a helical or twisted appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its foliage is often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
