Habenaria sagittifera f. lacerata

Habenaria sagittifera f. lacerata, commonly known as the lance-shaped orchid, is a fascinating member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated flowers that often display a striking contrast between the sepals and petals. This orchid typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories where it can receive adequate light without direct exposure. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to conserve water in its natural habitat.

The plant is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively ease of cultivation under the right conditions.
