Heteropogon insignis

Heteropogon insignis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and grasslands. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a compact growth habit.

Heteropogon insignis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
