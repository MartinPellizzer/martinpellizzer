Hieracium viridiatrum

Hieracium viridiatrum, commonly known as greenfield hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and basal rosette of lanceolate leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grassy habitats and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain regions.
