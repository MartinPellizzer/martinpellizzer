Hibiscus clandestinus

Hibiscus clandestinus, commonly known as the secret hibiscus, is a rare and elusive flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is known for its rapid growth and short flowering period. Hibiscus clandestinus thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in dense undergrowth.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a delicate species with limited distribution.
