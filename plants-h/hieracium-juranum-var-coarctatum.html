Hieracium juranum var. coarctatum

Hieracium juranum var. coarctatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features bright yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are linear to lanceolate, often with a hairy texture, and the stems are usually unbranched.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
