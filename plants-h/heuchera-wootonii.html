Heuchera wootonii

Heuchera wootonii, commonly known as the Wooton's coral bells, is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its attractive, mounding growth habit and foliage that ranges from deep purple to bronze and silver tones. The plant produces upright spikes of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white during the spring and early summer. It prefers partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Heuchera wootonii is relatively low maintenance and resistant to pests and diseases, contributing to its popularity among gardeners.
