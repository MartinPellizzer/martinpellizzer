Hippochaete hyemalis var. jesupii

Hippochaete hyemalis var. jesupii, commonly known as the winter dwarf daisy, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a low-growing habit with narrow, grass-like leaves.
