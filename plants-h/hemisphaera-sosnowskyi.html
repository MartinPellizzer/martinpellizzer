Hemisphaera sosnowskyi

Hemisphaera sosnowskyi, commonly known as the Sosnowsky's hogweed, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, umbrella-shaped leaves that can reach up to two meters in diameter. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers arranged in a spherical shape, which give it its scientific name. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
