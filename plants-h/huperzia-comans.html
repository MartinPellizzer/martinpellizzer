Huperzia comans

Huperzia comans, commonly known as the mountain huperzia, is a species of fern-like plant belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its simple, linear leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. It produces sporangia on the underside of its leaves, which are clustered in strobila.

Huperzia comans is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its resilience to environmental stress.
