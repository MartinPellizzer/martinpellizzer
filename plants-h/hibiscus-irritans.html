Hibiscus irritans

Hibiscus irritans, commonly known as the red hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a contrasting yellow or orange throat. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically dark green and emit a mild fragrance. Hibiscus irritans thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its flowers bloom throughout the growing season, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical gardens.
