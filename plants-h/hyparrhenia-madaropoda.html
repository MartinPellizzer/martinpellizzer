Hyparrhenia madaropoda

Hyparrhenia madaropoda, commonly known as the African grass or red grass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves that are typically reddish in color. The plant produces spikelets that are often clustered at the base of the stems, forming distinctive, cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Hyparrhenia madaropoda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
