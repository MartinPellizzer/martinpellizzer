Huperzia pilgeriana

Huperzia pilgeriana, commonly known as the Pilger's huperzia, is a species of moss-like plant belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems covered with small, ovate leaves that are often twisted or curled. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Huperzia pilgeriana reproduces via spores, which are released from sporangia located on the underside of its leaves.

This species is valued for its ecological role in soil stabilization and as a component of diverse forest ecosystems.
