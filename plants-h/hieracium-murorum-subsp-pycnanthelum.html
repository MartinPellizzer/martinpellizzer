Hieracium murorum subsp. pycnanthelum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

pycnanthelum, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosette of leaves, which are typically grayish-green in color.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clustered heads, which are borne on long, slender stalks. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Due to its robust growth and adaptability, it is considered a common weed in many regions.
