Hieracium koracis

Hieracium koracis, commonly known as the Balkan hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium koracis is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bright, cheerful blooms.
