Hieracium protractifolium

Hieracium protractifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, pastures, and roadsides. It features upright stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy.

Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.
