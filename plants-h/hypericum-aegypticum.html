Hypericum aegypticum

Hypericum aegypticum, commonly known as the Egyptian St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces bright yellow flowers with prominent orange stamens. The plant typically blooms in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and branched.

Hypericum aegypticum is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and coastal areas.
