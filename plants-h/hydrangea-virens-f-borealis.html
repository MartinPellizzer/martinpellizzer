Hydrangea virens f. borealis

Hydrangea virens f. borealis, commonly known as the northern hydrangea, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It features broad, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath, often with a slight pubescence. The plant produces showy, cone-shaped flower clusters that range from white to pink, depending on soil pH. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to cooler climates.
