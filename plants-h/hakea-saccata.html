Hakea saccata

Hakea saccata, commonly known as the red-stemmed hakea, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or orange stems and narrow, needle-like leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to red flower spikes that bloom in spring, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

Hakea saccata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
