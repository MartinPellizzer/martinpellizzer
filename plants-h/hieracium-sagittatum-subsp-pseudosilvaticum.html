Hieracium sagittatum subsp. pseudosilvaticum

Hieracium sagittatum subsp.

pseudosilvaticum, commonly known as the woodland hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in composite heads, which are typically arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its fibrous root system helps it anchor in soil while also contributing to soil stabilization.
