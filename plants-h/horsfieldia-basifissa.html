Horsfieldia basifissa

Horsfieldia basifissa, commonly known as the broad-leafed orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its strap-like leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments, relying on its aerial roots for absorption of moisture and nutrients. Its flowers, which are usually yellow or greenish-white, bloom in clusters and attract various pollinators.

Horsfieldia basifissa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
