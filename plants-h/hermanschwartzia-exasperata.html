Hermanschwartzia exasperata

Hermanschwartzia exasperata, commonly known as the yellow-flowered false dandelion, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is a flowering plant native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa. The plant features showy yellow ray florets surrounded by pale green disk flowers, giving it a dandelion-like appearance. It typically grows in grasslands and rocky soils, often in full sun.

The species is characterized by its slender stem and opposite leaves with serrated edges.
