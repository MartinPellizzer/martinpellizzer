Hieracium murorum subsp. carcarophyllum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

carcarophyllum, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and meadows, and is often found in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

It is a robust species that can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
