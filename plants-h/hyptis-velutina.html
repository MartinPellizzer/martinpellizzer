Hyptis velutina

Hyptis velutina, commonly known as velvet hyptis, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic and medicinal plants. The plant is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves that give it a velvety texture, and its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Hyptis velutina is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
