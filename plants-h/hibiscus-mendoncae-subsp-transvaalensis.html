Hibiscus mendoncae subsp. transvaalensis

Hibiscus mendoncae subsp.

transvaalensis, commonly known as the Transvaal hibiscus, is a shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red.

The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Hibiscus mendoncae subsp.

transvaalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.
