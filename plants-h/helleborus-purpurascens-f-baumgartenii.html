Helleborus purpurascens f. baumgartenii

Helleborus purpurascens f. baumgartenii, commonly known as the Baumann's hellebore, is a rare and ornamental plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It is a cultivar of the common hellebore, characterized by its striking, dark purple foliage and clusters of pale green to white flowers. The plant is evergreen, with lance-shaped leaves that remain attractive throughout the winter. It prefers partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Due to its rarity and specific cultivation needs, it is often grown in controlled environments.
