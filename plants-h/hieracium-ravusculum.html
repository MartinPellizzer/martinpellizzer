Hieracium ravusculum

Hieracium ravusculum, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow flowers that are typically arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. The plant has rough, hairy leaves that are often lobed or toothed, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grasslands.

Hieracium ravusculum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and light conditions.
