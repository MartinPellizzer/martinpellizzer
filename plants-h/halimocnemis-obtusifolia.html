Halimocnemis obtusifolia

Halimocnemis obtusifolia, commonly known as the bluntleaf sandmat, is a low-growing, perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, succulent-like foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is well adapted to drought conditions. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, which helps store water, and it has a shallow root system that allows it to quickly absorb moisture from the soil.

Halimocnemis obtusifolia is often found in disturbed areas and is considered a pioneer species in ecological succession.
