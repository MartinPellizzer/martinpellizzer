Helichrysum hookeri

Helichrysum hookeri, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, fluffy yellow or golden flower heads. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically clustered in a spherical or conical shape. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The plant's name derives from the Greek words "helios" meaning sun and "chrysm" meaning gold, reflecting its bright, sunny appearance.
