Hakea occidentalis

Hakea occidentalis, commonly known as the Western Hakea, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its woody, spine-tipped leaves and clusters of bright red or orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces woody, cylindrical seed pods that remain on the plant for extended periods. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and Mediterranean climates.

Hakea occidentalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
