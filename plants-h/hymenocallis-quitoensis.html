Hymenocallis quitoensis

Hymenocallis quitoensis, commonly known as the Quito yellow orchid, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its large, showy, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, with a prominent white throat. The plant produces a tall, erect stem with clustered leaves, and its flowers bloom during the dry season. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in tropical and subtropical climates.

Hymenocallis quitoensis is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its cultural significance in its native habitat.
