Hesperis brassiciformis

Hesperis brassiciformis, commonly known as the yellow horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, saucer-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has hairy stems and leaves. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The species is part of the Papaveraceae family, which includes other poppies known for their medicinal and ornamental value.
