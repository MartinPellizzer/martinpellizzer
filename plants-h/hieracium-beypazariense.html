Hieracium beypazariense

Hieracium beypazariense, commonly known as the Beypazari hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous tiny florets. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates. It features basal leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture.

The flowers are usually yellow or white, and the plant produces achenes as its seeds.
