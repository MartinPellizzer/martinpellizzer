Hieracium patens subsp. dipsacifolium

Hieracium patens subsp.

dipsacifolium, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lanceolate to ovate, with hairy surfaces, and the flowers are yellowish with ray florets.

It is known for its ability to thrive in various soil types and its role in supporting pollinators.
