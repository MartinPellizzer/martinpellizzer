Heracleum sphondylium var. pratense

Heracleum sphondylium var. pratense, commonly known as cow parsnip, is a biennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and large, umbrella-shaped clusters of small white or greenish-yellow flowers. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and riverbanks.

Cow parsnip is known for its strong scent and can cause skin irritation upon contact.
