Hieracium sabaudum var. integrifolium

Hieracium sabaudum var. integrifolium, commonly known as the Sabaudian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers. The leaves are typically broad, lobed, and have a rough texture, while the stems are hairy and upright. This plant is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.

It plays a role in supporting pollinators and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
