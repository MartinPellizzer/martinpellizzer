Hygrophila biplicata

Hygrophila biplicata, commonly known as the double-leaf hygrophila, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in pairs, giving it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in shallow, slow-moving freshwater habitats such as ponds and marshes. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually greenish-white in color.

Hygrophila biplicata is often used in aquatic gardening and as a decorative plant in water features.
