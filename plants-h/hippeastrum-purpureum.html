Hippeastrum purpureum

Hippeastrum purpureum, commonly known as the purple amaryllis, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to purple, often with a contrasting white or pink throat. The plant produces large, strap-like leaves that are broad and pointed, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Hippeastrum purpureum is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms and is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
