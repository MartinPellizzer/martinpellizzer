Hieracium saxifragum subsp. luteoglaucescens

Hieracium saxifragum subsp.

luteoglaucescens, commonly known as the blue-green hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

The plant features basal rosettes of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. Its flowers are yellow and borne in dense, flat-topped clusters, adding a striking visual element to its habitat.

The subspecies is distinguished by its bluish-green leaf coloration and the specific morphology of its inflorescences.
