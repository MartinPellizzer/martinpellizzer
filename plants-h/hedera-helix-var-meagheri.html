Hedera helix var. meagheri

Hedera helix var. meagheri, commonly known as the Meagher's ivy, is a variant of the common ivy that exhibits distinct botanical characteristics.

It is a climbing evergreen vine with dark green, slightly lobed leaves that have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by orange-red berries, which are edible but not particularly palatable. It thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability in horticultural settings.
