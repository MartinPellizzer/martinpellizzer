Hypericum gracillimum

Hypericum gracillimum, commonly known as slender St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, red or orange berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Hypericum gracillimum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators.
