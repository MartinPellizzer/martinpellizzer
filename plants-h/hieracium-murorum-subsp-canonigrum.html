Hieracium murorum subsp. canonigrum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

canonigrum, commonly known as the Canary hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Canary Islands. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, often growing in rocky or disturbed soils. It is known for its ability to spread aggressively through rhizomes, making it a common weed in certain regions.

Its botanical features make it easily distinguishable from other hawkweed species.
