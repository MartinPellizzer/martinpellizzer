Hieracium parryi var. chaetobrachion

Hieracium parryi var. chaetobrachion, commonly known as the long-bracted hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while the flower heads are yellow with elongated bracts.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
