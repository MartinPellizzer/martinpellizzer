Hyptis aristata

Hyptis aristata, commonly known as the sacred basil or Mexican mint, is a herbaceous plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs such as mint and sage. The plant is characterized by its gray-green, fuzzy leaves and spikes of small, pale blue to violet flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Hyptis aristata is valued for its pleasant aroma and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies and as a culinary herb.
