Hypericum barbatum subsp. rumeliacum

Hypericum barbatum subsp.

rumeliacum, commonly known as the Rumelian St. John's Wort, is a shrub native to parts of Eastern Europe and Western Asia. It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, glandular hairs.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Hypericaceae family.
