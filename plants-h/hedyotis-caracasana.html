Hedyotis caracasana

Hedyotis caracasana, commonly known as the Caracas pimpernel, is a small herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features slender, trailing stems covered with small, opposite leaves that are often reddish or purple in color. Its flowers are tiny, white or pale yellow, and are clustered in dense, terminal inflorescences.

Hedyotis caracasana is known for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
