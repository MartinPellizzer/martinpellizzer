Hibiscus paolii

Hibiscus paolii, commonly known as the Moluccan hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the island of Saparua in Indonesia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, with five petals and a prominent central stigma. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, and features dark green, heart-shaped leaves. Hibiscus paolii thrives in tropical climates and is often found in coastal areas or near freshwater sources.
