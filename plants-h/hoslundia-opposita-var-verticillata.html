Hoslundia opposita var. verticillata

Hoslundia opposita var. verticillata, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its rapid growth and showy flowers make it both ornamental and ecologically beneficial, attracting pollinators like bees and hummingbirds.
