Hieracium pilosella subsp. submelanops

Hieracium pilosella subsp.

submelanops, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish to reddish flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are usually hairy and alternate, with a distinctive lobed or serrated appearance.

The subspecies submelanops is distinguished by its darker, more intense flower coloration compared to other subspecies of Hieracium pilosella.
