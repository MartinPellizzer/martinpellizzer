Hieracium contrarium

Hieracium contrarium, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellowish-orange flowers and is native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Hieracium contrarium is known for its ability to spread rapidly through rhizomes and is considered an invasive species in some regions.
