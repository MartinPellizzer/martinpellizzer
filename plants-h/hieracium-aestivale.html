Hieracium aestivale

Hieracium aestivale, commonly known as summer hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features bright yellow flowers with a central disk, surrounded by ray florets, and has a rosette of basal leaves. It thrives in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a pioneer species in ecological succession.
