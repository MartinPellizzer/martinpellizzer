Hordeum stassewitschii

Hordeum stassewitschii, commonly known as Stassewitsch's barley, is a species of wild barley native to Central Asia.

It is a grass plant characterized by its slender stems and small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a blue-green hue, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Hordeum stassewitschii is of interest to botanists and agricultural researchers due to its potential for genetic diversity and adaptability.
