Heliconia schneeana

Heliconia schneeana, commonly known as the Snow Heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its large, colorful bracts that resemble flowers, though they are actually modified leaves. The plant produces vibrant orange to red inflorescences, which are highly attractive to birds and insects. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Heliconia schneeana is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife.
