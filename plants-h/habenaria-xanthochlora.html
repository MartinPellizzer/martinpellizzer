Habenaria xanthochlora

Habenaria xanthochlora, commonly known as the golden-veined orchid, is a species of orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, greenish-yellow flowers with prominent yellow veins. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped, with a smooth surface.

The orchid is known for its fragrant blooms, which attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
