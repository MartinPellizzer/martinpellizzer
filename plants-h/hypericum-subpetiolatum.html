Hypericum subpetiolatum

Hypericum subpetiolatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its small, yellow flowers and opposite, narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks. Its stems are often reddish or purple in color, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Hypericum subpetiolatum is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
