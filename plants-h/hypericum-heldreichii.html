Hypericum heldreichii

Hypericum heldreichii, commonly known as the Balkan St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer, and its distinctive, slightly hairy leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has woody stems that become more prominent with age. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin, while its flowers are arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches.

Hypericum heldreichii is often found in rocky or limestone-rich soils, thriving in sunny, well-drained environments.
