Harmogia crispiflora

Harmogia crispiflora, commonly known as the crinkled leaf harmogia, is a perennial flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, crinkled leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to its unique foliage and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
