Hermannia decumbens

Hermannia decumbens, commonly known as the creeping hermannia, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It features a sprawling growth habit with prostrate stems that root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, with a distinctive spurred structure. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Hermannia decumbens is adapted to well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade, making it a suitable ornamental plant in shaded garden settings.
