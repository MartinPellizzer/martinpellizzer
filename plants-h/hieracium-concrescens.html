Hieracium concrescens

Hieracium concrescens, commonly known as the clustered hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring temperate climates. It has hairy stems and basal leaves, with yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

Hieracium concrescens is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
