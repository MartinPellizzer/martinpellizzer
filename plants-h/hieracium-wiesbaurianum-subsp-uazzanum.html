Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp. uazzanum

Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp.

uazzanum, commonly known as the Alaskan hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northwestern United States and parts of Canada. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped, rough to the touch, and covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are yellow, with a distinctive central disc surrounded by petal-like bracts, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
