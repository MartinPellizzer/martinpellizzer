Habenaria monorrhiza

Habenaria monorrhiza, commonly known as the single-rooted orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and linear, lanceolate leaves. The plant produces showy, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in a spike, often with a distinctive spur. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically in tropical and subtropical regions.

Botanically, it is notable for its unique root structure and its role in the orchid family's ecological diversity.
