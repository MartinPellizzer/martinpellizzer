Hieracium murmanense

Hieracium murmanense, commonly known as the Murmansk hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to northern regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and sandy soils. Its leaves are usually basal, rough, and hairy, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is adapted to cold climates and can thrive in areas with short growing seasons.
