Hornungia damascena

Hornungia damascena, commonly known as the Damask rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the eastern Mediterranean region and is characterized by its fragrant, pink or white flowers. The plant is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with thorny stems and dark green leaves. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and the plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in perfumery.

The fruit, a red, fleshy berry, contains seeds that can be used for propagation.
