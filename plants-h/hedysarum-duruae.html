Hedysarum duruae

Hedysarum duruae, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Hedysarum duruae is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing its resilience to harsh climatic conditions.
