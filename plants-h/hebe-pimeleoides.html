Hebe pimeleoides

Hebe pimeleoides, commonly known as the silver hebe, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and spikes of blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in a compact, bushy form, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Hebe pimeleoides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
