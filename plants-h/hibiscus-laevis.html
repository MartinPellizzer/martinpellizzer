Hibiscus laevis

Hibiscus laevis, commonly known as the white hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. Hibiscus laevis is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and wetland restoration projects.
