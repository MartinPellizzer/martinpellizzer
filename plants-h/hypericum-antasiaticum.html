Hypericum antasiaticum

Hypericum antasiaticum, commonly known as the yellow-stemmed St. John's wort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Hypericaceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and woody, branched stems. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Hypericum antasiaticum is often found in the eastern United States and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
