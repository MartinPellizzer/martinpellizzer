Hoya maingayi

Hoya maingayi, commonly known as the "Coral Hoya," is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its waxy, thick leaves and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers. The plant produces fragrant, pinkish-white blooms that are often described as resembling coral, hence its common name. Hoya maingayi is a trailing vine that thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.

It is popular among plant enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
