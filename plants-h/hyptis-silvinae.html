Hyptis silvinae

Hyptis silvinae, commonly known as silverleaf hyptis, is a perennial shrub native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its silver-gray, velvety foliage and clusters of small, pale blue to lavender flowers. Hyptis silvinae thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and dry forest habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional use in herbal medicine.
