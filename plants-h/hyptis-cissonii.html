Hyptis cissonii

Hyptis cissonii, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its grayish-blue foliage and clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands.

Hyptis cissonii is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
