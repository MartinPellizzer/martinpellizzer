Hedaroma latifolium

Hedaroma latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf hedaroma, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often found in rocky or coastal areas due to its drought tolerance.

Hedaroma latifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
