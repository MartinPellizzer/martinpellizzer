Heteropterys siderosa

Heteropterys siderosa, commonly known as the ironwood tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 15 meters in height. The plant has dark, glossy leaves that are elliptical in shape and arranged alternately along the stems. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically white or pale yellow, and it produces small, round fruits that contain seeds.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in humid, shaded environments.
