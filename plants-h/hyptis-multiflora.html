Hyptis multiflora

Hyptis multiflora, commonly known as Brazilian hyssop, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its inflorescence consists of dense clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple.

Hyptis multiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
