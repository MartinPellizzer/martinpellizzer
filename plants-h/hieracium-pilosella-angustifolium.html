Hieracium pilosella angustifolium

Hieracium pilosella angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is often found in meadows and grassy areas.

This species is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
