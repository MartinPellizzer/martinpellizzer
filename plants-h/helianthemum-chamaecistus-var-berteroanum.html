Helianthemum chamaecistus var. berteroanum

Helianthemum chamaecistus var. berteroanum, commonly known as the Bertero's rockrose, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compact, evergreen foliage with silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine, woolly hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.
