Hordeum chilense var. magellanicum

Hordeum chilense var. magellanicum, commonly known as the Magellanic barley, is a perennial grass native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences that are typically found in coastal or high-altitude regions. It is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including strong winds and salt spray.

This variety is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique ecological niche and potential for use in ecological restoration projects.
