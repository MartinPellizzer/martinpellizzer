Heuchera rubescens var. pringlei

Heuchera rubescens var. pringlei, commonly known as the Pringle's coral bells, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It features striking, lobed leaves that range from deep red to purple hues, often with a contrasting green or white edge. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental foliage and compact growth habit make it a valuable addition to native plant collections.
