Helianthemum chamaecistus subsp. hirsutum

Helianthemum chamaecistus subsp.

hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy rock rose, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features compact, evergreen foliage covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
