Hieracium eriobasis subsp. leucosericopodum

Hieracium eriobasis subsp.

leucosericopodum, commonly known as the white-haired hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy stems.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a serrated edge, while the flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disc.

It is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
