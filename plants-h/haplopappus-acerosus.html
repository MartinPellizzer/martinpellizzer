Haplopappus acerosus

Haplopappus acerosus, commonly known as the hard-leaved fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature. Its flowers are typically yellow, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets.

Haplopappus acerosus thrives in arid and rocky environments, often found in open habitats such as sagebrush steppe and grasslands.
