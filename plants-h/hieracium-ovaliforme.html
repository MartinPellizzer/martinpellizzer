Hieracium ovaliforme

Hieracium ovaliforme, commonly known as the oval-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm. Its leaves are oval-shaped, with a rough texture and prominent veins, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

Hieracium ovaliforme is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
