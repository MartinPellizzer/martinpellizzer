Hakea polyanthema

Hakea polyanthema, commonly known as the Christmas hakea, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical flower spikes that typically bloom in winter, producing showy red or orange flowers. The plant has woody stems and leathery, evergreen leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the branches. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to thrive in poor soils.

Hakea polyanthema is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
