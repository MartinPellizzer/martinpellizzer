Heracleum carmeli

Heracleum carmeli, commonly known as the Carmel cowparn, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate flowers.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in dry, rocky soils. The plant features tall, hollow stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are deeply lobed with a distinctive pungent odor. Its flowers, which appear in late spring, are small and white, arranged in umbrella-like clusters.

Heracleum carmeli is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
