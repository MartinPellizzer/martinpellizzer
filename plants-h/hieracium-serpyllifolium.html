Hieracium serpyllifolium

Hieracium serpyllifolium, commonly known as common groundsel, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves and yellow flower heads that resemble dandelions. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is found in various habitats across Europe, Asia, and North America. It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds that contribute to its widespread distribution.

The species is often considered a weed but has historical uses in traditional medicine.
