Habranthus sylvaticus

Habranthus sylvaticus, commonly known as the forest bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and forest understories. The plant features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Habranthus sylvaticus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
