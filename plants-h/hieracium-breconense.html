Hieracium breconense

Hieracium breconense, commonly known as Brecon's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to western Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland clearings. It has basal leaves that are lanceolate and hairy, with a rosette structure.

The flowers are yellow, with a central disc surrounded by pale yellow ray florets, contributing to its ornamental appearance.
