Harpaecarpus madarioides

Harpaecarpus madarioides, commonly known as the Madarioides grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences, which are typical of grasses, and its leaves are narrow with a prominent midrib. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and ecological restoration projects.
