Homalomena undulatifolia

Homalomena undulatifolia, commonly known as the wavy-leaf caladium, is a tropical perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that have a wavy or undulating edge, often displaying vibrant shades of green, red, and purple. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are typically less showy than its foliage. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking leaf patterns.

Homalomena undulatifolia prefers indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
