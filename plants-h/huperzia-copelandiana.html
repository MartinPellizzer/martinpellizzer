Huperzia copelandiana

Huperzia copelandiana, commonly known as the Copeland's huperzia, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is a terrestrial plant characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth habit and is often found in moist, shaded environments. The plant features simple, linear leaves that are usually arranged in a rosette pattern, with a distinct central axis. Huperzia copelandiana is known for its ability to thrive in rocky or disturbed soils, making it a resilient species in various habitats.

Its spore-producing structures, called sporangia, are clustered on the underside of the leaves, contributing to its reproductive strategy.
