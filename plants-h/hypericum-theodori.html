Hypericum theodori

Hypericum theodori, commonly known as the Theodore's St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its yellow flowers and woody stems. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds, typical of the Hypericaceae family. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often covered in fine hairs.

Hypericum theodori thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
