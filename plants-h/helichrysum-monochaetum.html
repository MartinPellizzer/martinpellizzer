Helichrysum monochaetum

Helichrysum monochaetum, commonly known as the Cape goldcup, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces small, yellow-orange flowers that are often mistaken for daisies. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Helichrysum monochaetum is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
