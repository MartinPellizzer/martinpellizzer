Helleborus bocconei

Helleborus bocconei, commonly known as the Sicilian hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the island of Sicily.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and upright, sparsely branched stems. The plant produces bell-shaped, greenish-white flowers with prominent, showy stamens that bloom in late winter to early spring. Helleborus bocconei is adapted to calcareous soils and thrives in partial shade, making it a unique addition to woodland gardens.

Due to its restricted habitat, it is considered a rare and protected species in its native region.
