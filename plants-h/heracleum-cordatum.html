Heracleum cordatum

Heracleum cordatum, commonly known as the roundleaf cow parsley, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are heart-shaped, hence the species name "cordatum," and are typically divided into lobes. It thrives in moist, sunny environments such as meadows and stream banks.

The plant is part of the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like parsley and carrots.
