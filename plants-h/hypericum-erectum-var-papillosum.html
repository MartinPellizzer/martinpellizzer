Hypericum erectum var. papillosum

Hypericum erectum var. papillosum, commonly known as the rosehip plant, is a flowering shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, red, berry-like fruits called hips, which are rich in vitamin C. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soil in full sun.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
