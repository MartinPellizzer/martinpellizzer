Haplopappus tridentatus

Haplopappus tridentatus, commonly known as the three-toothed hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a distinctive three-toothed appearance at the tip.

Haplopappus tridentatus produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months.
