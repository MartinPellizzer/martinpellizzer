Hopea coriacea

Hopea coriacea, commonly known as the thick-shelled tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, aromatic wood. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles, followed by woody, pod-like fruits containing flat, winged seeds.

Hopea coriacea is valued for its hard timber and is often found in lowland rainforests.
