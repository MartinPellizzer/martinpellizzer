Hyptis microcephala

Hyptis microcephala, commonly known as Brazilian pepper or false pepper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and aromatic leaves that emit a strong, spicy scent. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, often reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

Hyptis microcephala is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
