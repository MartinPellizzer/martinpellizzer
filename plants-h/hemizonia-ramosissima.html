Hemizonia ramosissima

Hemizonia ramosissima, commonly known as the desert hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with multiple branches and small, velvety leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the spring and early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating drought tolerance and adapted root systems.

Hemizonia ramosissima is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
