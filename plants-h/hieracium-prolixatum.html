Hieracium prolixatum

Hieracium prolixatum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and yellow composite flowers that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils across Europe and parts of Asia. It produces numerous seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.

Hieracium prolixatum is often considered a weed due to its rapid growth and ability to colonize various habitats.
