Hyparrhenia ruprechtii

Hyparrhenia ruprechtii, commonly known as the African grass or red grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves that are often reddish in color, especially when young. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.

Hyparrhenia ruprechtii is often used in landscaping and has cultural significance in some African communities.
