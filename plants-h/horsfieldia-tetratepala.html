Horsfieldia tetratepala

Horsfieldia tetratepala, commonly known as the four-ribbed orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, vine-like growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrates.

Horsfieldia tetratepala is often found growing on trees in humid, shaded forest environments.
