Hedyotis biflora var. parvifolia

Hedyotis biflora var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved blushing geranium, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It is a low-growing perennial with slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
