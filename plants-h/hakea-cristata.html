Hakea cristata

Hakea cristata, commonly known as the Christmas bush, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of narrow, needle-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, red or pink flowers in winter, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and seasonal blooms.

Hakea cristata thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant once established.
