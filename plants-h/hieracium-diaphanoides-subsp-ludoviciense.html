Hieracium diaphanoides subsp. ludoviciense

Hieracium diaphanoides subsp.

ludoviciense, commonly known as the Ludovician hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is a relatively small plant, usually reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.
