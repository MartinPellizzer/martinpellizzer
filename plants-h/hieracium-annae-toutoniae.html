Hieracium annae-toutoniae

Hieracium annae-toutoniae, commonly known as the Annä-Toutonia hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 20–50 cm, with hairy stems and basal leaves that are lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are yellow, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets, and it blooms from June to August.

Hieracium annae-toutoniae is often found in meadows and rocky slopes, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
