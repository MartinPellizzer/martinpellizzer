Hymenorebutia albolanata

Hymenorebutia albolanata, commonly known as the white-lanate euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and thick, fleshy stems that store water. The plant produces yellow flowers during the growing season, which are typically borne on short pedicels. Its leaves are usually small and may be present during the warmer months.

Hymenorebutia albolanata is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
