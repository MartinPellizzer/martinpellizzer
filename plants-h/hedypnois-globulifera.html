Hedypnois globulifera

Hedypnois globulifera, commonly known as the globular sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, succulent-like leaves. The plant produces small, spherical flower heads that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in coastal environments and is adapted to salty, well-drained soils.

Hedypnois globulifera is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
