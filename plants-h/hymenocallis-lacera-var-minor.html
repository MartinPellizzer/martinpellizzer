Hymenocallis lacera var. minor

Hymenocallis lacera var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf spider lily, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow with a greenish throat. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, strap-like leaves that are often variegated with silver or white markings. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to bloom reliably in suitable climates.
