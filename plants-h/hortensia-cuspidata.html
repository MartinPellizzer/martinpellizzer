Hortensia cuspidata

Hortensia cuspidata, commonly known as the Japanese hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy flower clusters that can range in color from blue to pink depending on soil pH. The plant produces bracts, which are modified leaves that serve as the colorful floral display. It thrives in partial shade and requires well-drained, acidic soil for optimal growth.

Hortensia cuspidata is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to bloom throughout the summer months.
