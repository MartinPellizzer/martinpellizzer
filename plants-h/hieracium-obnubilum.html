Hieracium obnubilum

Hieracium obnubilum, commonly known as the foggy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are usually hairy and have a rough texture. Its flowers are yellow and borne in a single head, with ray florets that are often shorter than the disk florets.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
