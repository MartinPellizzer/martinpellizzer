Horsfieldia brachiata var. laticostata

Horsfieldia brachiata var. laticostata, commonly known as the broad-costate orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, broad, and flat leaf structure, which gives it its varietal name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a variety of colors ranging from white to pink.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or conservatories.
