Hedyotis dimorpha

Hedyotis dimorpha, commonly known as the two-leaved or variable hedyotis, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant features small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are usually opposite, with a distinctive two-lobed shape. Hedyotis dimorpha has a fibrous root system and is often found in areas with rich, organic soil.
