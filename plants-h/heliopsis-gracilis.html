Heliopsis gracilis

Heliopsis gracilis, commonly known as slender false sunflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of bright yellow, daisy-like flowers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Heliopsis gracilis is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
