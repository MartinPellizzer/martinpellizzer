Hieracium subramosum subsp. submeridionale

Hieracium subramosum subsp.

submeridionale, commonly known as the southern hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils.

The plant features a basal rosette of leaves that are lanceolate and hairy, with a rough texture. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a single head, and are surrounded by pale green or yellowish bracts.

It is characterized by its robust growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
