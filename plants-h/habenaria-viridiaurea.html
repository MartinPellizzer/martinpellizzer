Habenaria viridiaurea

Habenaria viridiaurea, commonly known as the green-yellow orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces striking, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive, spurred lip. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its flowers are often fragrant, attracting various pollinators like bees and wasps.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
