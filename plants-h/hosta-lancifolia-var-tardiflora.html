Hosta lancifolia var. tardiflora

Hosta lancifolia var. tardiflora, commonly known as the late-flowering blue hosta, is a cultivar of the hosta plant native to East Asia.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are a deep, rich blue-green in color, often with a lighter midrib. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale blue flowers that bloom later in the season compared to other hosta varieties. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental foliage and unique flowering habit, making it a popular choice for shade gardens.
