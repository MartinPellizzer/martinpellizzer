Hesperis matronalis subsp. sintenisii

Hesperis matronalis subsp.

sintenisii, commonly known as the Alpine sweet rocket, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its compact growth habit and pale purple to white flowers. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or meadow habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
