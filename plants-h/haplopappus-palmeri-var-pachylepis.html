Haplopappus palmeri var. pachylepis

Haplopappus palmeri var. pachylepis, commonly known as the desert groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typically hairy stems. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

Its ecological role includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for various herbivores.
