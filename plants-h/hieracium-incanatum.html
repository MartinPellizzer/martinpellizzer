Hieracium incanatum

Hieracium incanatum, commonly known as the hoary hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features hairy, grayish-green leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.

Hieracium incanatum is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through its rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain regions.
