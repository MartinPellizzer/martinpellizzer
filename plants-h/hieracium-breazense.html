Hieracium breazense

Hieracium breazense, commonly known as the Breazense hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and sunny exposures. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rough, hairy texture, and the flowers are yellow, often appearing in clusters.

Hieracium breazense is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
