Hibiscus conceptionis

Hibiscus conceptionis, commonly known as the Conception Island hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the island of Conception in the Caribbean.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with woody stems and large, showy flowers. The flowers are usually bright red or pink, with five petals and a prominent yellow stamen, and they bloom during the dry season. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, and it is adapted to the tropical climate of its native habitat.

Hibiscus conceptionis is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in tropical regions.
