Heuchera × brizoides

Heuchera × brizoides, commonly known as the New Zealand pigface, is a low-growing perennial plant native to New Zealand.

It features dense, mat-forming foliage with broad, waxy leaves that range in color from silver to green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically appearing in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and alpine environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
