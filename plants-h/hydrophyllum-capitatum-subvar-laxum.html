Hydrophyllum capitatum subvar. laxum

Hydrophyllum capitatum subvar. laxum, commonly known as the slender hydrophyllum, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is valued in native plant gardens and conservation efforts.
