Hieracium subamorphoglossum

Hieracium subamorphoglossum, commonly known as the narrowleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Hieracium subamorphoglossum is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
