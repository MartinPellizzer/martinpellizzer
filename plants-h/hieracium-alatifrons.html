Hieracium alatifrons

Hieracium alatifrons, commonly known as the broad-fronded hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosettes of lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas.

Hieracium alatifrons is typically an herbaceous perennial, though it may behave as an annual in certain regions.
