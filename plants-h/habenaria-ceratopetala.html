Habenaria ceratopetala

Habenaria ceratopetala, commonly known as the horned orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spurred flowers that resemble horns, which are a key feature in its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its leaves are usually broad and grass-like, and it produces small, greenish or pale yellow flowers.

Habenaria ceratopetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
