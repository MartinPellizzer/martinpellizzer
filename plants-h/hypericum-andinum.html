Hypericum andinum

Hypericum andinum, commonly known as the Andean goldenseal, is a perennial shrub native to the high Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, and its distinctive red, orange, or yellow-orange berries that persist into winter. The plant is often found in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are typically alternate, oval-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Hypericum andinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
