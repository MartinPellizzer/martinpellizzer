Hemigenia diadela

Hemigenia diadela, commonly known as the mountain pepper, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a glossy surface and a distinctive aromatic scent.

Hemigenia diadela is often found in heathlands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
