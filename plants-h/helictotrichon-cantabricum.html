Helictotrichon cantabricum

Helictotrichon cantabricum, commonly known as the Cantabrian needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has a fibrous root system that helps it thrive in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora in its native range.
