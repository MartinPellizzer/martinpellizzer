Hypericum cervantesii

Hypericum cervantesii, commonly known as the Sierra Madre goldcup, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with prominent orange stamens, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Hypericum cervantesii is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping.
