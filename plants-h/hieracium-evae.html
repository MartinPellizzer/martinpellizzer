Hieracium evae

Hieracium evae, commonly known as Eve's Hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are generally basal, with a rough, hairy texture, and its flowers are yellow, often arranged in a dense cluster.

Hieracium evae is notable for its ornamental value and has been cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
