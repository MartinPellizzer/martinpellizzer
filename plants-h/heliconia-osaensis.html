Heliconia osaensis

Heliconia osaensis, commonly known as the Osa Peninsula heliconia, is a tropical plant native to the Osa Peninsula in Costa Rica.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that enclose the small, true flowers. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are large, broad, and often have a waxy texture, while its inflorescence is a prominent, spiral arrangement of brightly colored bracts.

Heliconia osaensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
