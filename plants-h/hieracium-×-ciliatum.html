Hieracium × ciliatum

Hieracium × ciliatum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a hybrid plant species in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a range of morphological variations.
