Hieracium tendinum subsp. niphobiomorphum

Hieracium tendinum subsp.

niphobiomorphum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained, rocky soils. It is adapted to cold climates and can tolerate low light conditions.

Its subspecific name, *niphobiomorphum*, reflects its association with snow-covered environments.
