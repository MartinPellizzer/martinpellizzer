Hieracium mattiroloanum subsp. farinulentiforme

Hieracium mattiroloanum subsp.

farinulentiforme, commonly known as the farinulentiform hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering heads that produce yellow ray florets.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open fields. Its leaves are covered in a fine, powdery coating, which gives it a distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its adaptation to arid environments.
