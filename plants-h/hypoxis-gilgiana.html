Hypoxis gilgiana

Hypoxis gilgiana, commonly known as the yellow star flower, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant is characterized by its underground tuberous roots, which store nutrients and help it survive dry periods. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Hypoxis gilgiana is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
