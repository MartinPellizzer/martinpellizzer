Huperzia yakusimensis

Huperzia yakusimensis, commonly known as the Yakushima huperzia, is a species of clubmoss native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and compact, low-growing habit. The plant produces sporangia in specialized structures called sporangium clusters, which are typically found on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Huperzia yakusimensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in maintaining forest biodiversity.
