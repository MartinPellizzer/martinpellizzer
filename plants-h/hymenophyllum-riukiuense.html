Hymenophyllum riukiuense

Hymenophyllum riukiuense, commonly known as the Ryukyu filmy fern, is a small, epiphytic fern native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae and is known for its unique ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its spores are produced on the underside of its fronds in specialized structures called sori.

This rare and specialized fern is an important component of the island's biodiversity and is often found growing on tree trunks and rocks in tropical rainforests.
