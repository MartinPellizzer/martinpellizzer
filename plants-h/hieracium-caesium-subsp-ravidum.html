Hieracium caesium subsp. ravidum

Hieracium caesium subsp.

ravidum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and is native to parts of Europe and Asia.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and its flowering period occurs from late spring to early autumn.

Due to its invasive potential in some regions, it is often considered a weed in agricultural and natural habitats.
