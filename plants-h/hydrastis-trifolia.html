Hydrastis trifolia

Hydrastis trifolia, commonly known as the three-leaved goldenseal, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Ranunculaceae and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by dark blue berries. However, it is also considered an endangered species due to habitat loss and overharvesting.
