Hydrangea indochinensis

Hydrangea indochinensis, commonly known as the Indochina hydrangea, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features large, showy inflorescences composed of sterile flowers, which are often blue or pink depending on soil pH. Its leaves are ovate and have a smooth texture, while its stems are covered in fine hairs.

Hydrangea indochinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
