Haplopappus acradenius subsp. typicus

Haplopappus acradenius subsp.

typicus, commonly known as the desert pappus, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial with small, narrow leaves adapted to reduce water loss.

The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, which are followed by small, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is drought-tolerant.

Its ecological role includes providing food for pollinators and stabilizing soil in disturbed areas.
