Hebe scopulorum

Hebe scopulorum, commonly known as the rock hebe, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and evergreen foliage, with leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, or white, which bloom in spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Hebe scopulorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
