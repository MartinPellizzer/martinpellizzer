Hieracium vulgatum subsp. pseudochlorophylloides

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

pseudochlorophylloides, commonly known as the false chlorophyllous hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering heads.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a distinctive dark green color.

The flowers are yellow and arranged in clusters, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
