Hieracium nigrescens subsp. coracinum

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

coracinum, commonly known as the black-centred hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm. Its flowers are yellow with a dark center, and it produces small, oval seeds in a composite head.

The subspecies is distinguished by its darker central disc and more robust growth compared to other hawkweed species.
