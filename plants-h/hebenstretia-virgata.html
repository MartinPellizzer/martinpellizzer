Hebenstretia virgata

Hebenstretia virgata, commonly known as the silver bush or silverleaf hebenstretia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, silvery-gray foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its drought tolerance and attractive appearance make it suitable for coastal and Mediterranean climates.
