Hypericum uliginosum f. warmingii

Hypericum uliginosum f. warmingii, commonly known as the bog tea or yellow root.

This variant is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and yellowish-brown stems. It is native to wetland habitats in North America and thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant features small, opposite leaves and produces clusters of four-petaled flowers.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
