Heteropterys cordifolia

Heteropterys cordifolia, commonly known as the heart-leaf tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are typically heart-shaped, and its showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, red fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in moist forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
