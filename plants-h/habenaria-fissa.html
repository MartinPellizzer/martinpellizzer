Habenaria fissa

Habenaria fissa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It is a terrestrial herb with a slender, upright stem and linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a raceme, which are typically fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and wasps. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is noted for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
