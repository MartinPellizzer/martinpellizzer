Helichrysum fragrans

Helichrysum fragrans, commonly known as the sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, yellow-orange inflorescences that resemble small daisies. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically clustered in compact, rounded heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Helichrysum fragrans is also known for its aromatic leaves and is often used in traditional medicine.
