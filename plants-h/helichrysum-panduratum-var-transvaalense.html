Helichrysum panduratum var. transvaalense

Helichrysum panduratum var. transvaalense, commonly known as the Transvaal goldenrod, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It features wiry, branched stems and small, bright yellow to orange flowers that resemble goldenrod. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its drought tolerance and long-lasting blooms make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
