Hieracium laevigatum subsp. laevigans

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

laevigans, commonly known as the smooth hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features basal rosettes of broadly ovate to lanceolate leaves that are smooth and often covered with fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow, with five petals and numerous disk flowers, typical of the Asteraceae family.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smooth, hairless leaves and relatively compact inflorescence.
