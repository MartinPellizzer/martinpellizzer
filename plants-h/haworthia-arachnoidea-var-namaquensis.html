Haworthia arachnoidea var. namaquensis

Haworthia arachnoidea var. namaquensis, commonly known as the Namakwa Haworthia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in white, woolly tubercles, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are typically fleshy and cylindrical, adapted to store water in arid environments. It produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the growing season.

This species is well-suited for rock gardens and xerophytic landscapes due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
