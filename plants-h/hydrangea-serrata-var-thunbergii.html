Hydrangea serrata var. thunbergii

Hydrangea serrata var. thunbergii, commonly known as the Japanese hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can change color depending on soil pH, ranging from blue to pink. The plant features serrated leaves that are typically dark green in summer and turn red or orange in autumn. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to bloom multiple times throughout the growing season.
