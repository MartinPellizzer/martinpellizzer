Haworthia fasciata f. subconfluens

Haworthia fasciata f. subconfluens, commonly known as the Zulu bulb, is a compact, evergreen succulent native to South Africa.

It features a rosette of thick, fleshy, blue-green leaves with distinct white or pale green markings. The plant produces small, star-shaped white flowers on slender stems during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
