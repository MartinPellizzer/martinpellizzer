Hieracium acidodontum

Hieracium acidodontum, commonly known as the acidleaf hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along riverbanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium acidodontum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
