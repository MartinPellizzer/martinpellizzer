Hibiscus tenorii

Hibiscus tenorii, commonly known as the Hawaiian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 2 meters, featuring woody stems and broad, ovate leaves. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, depending on the variety. Hibiscus tenorii thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Its flowers bloom throughout the year, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
