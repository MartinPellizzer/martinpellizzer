Hedysarum kirghisorum

Hedysarum kirghisorum, commonly known as the Kirghiz vetch, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its seeds are also a source of protein and can be used as forage for livestock.
