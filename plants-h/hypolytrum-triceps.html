Hypolytrum triceps

Hypolytrum triceps, commonly known as the three-veined grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves with a prominent central vein and three secondary veins, giving it its scientific name. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of grasses.

Hypolytrum triceps is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
