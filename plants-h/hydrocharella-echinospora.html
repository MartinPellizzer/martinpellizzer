Hydrocharella echinospora

Hydrocharella echinospora, commonly known as the spiny-stemmed waterplant, is a species of aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Africa.

It is characterized by its branching, spiny stems and small, delicate leaves that float on the water surface. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving waters, often found in marshes and ponds. Its flowers are typically small and inconspicuous, with a greenish-yellow color, and it produces small, oval fruits that contain several seeds.

Hydrocharella echinospora plays a role in providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates and can contribute to water filtration in its ecosystem.
