Hieracium erythrocarpum subsp. erythrocarpoides

Hieracium erythrocarpum subsp.

erythrocarpoides, commonly known as the red-fruited hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with fine hairs, while its fruit is a distinctive red, hairy achene.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting pollinator populations.
