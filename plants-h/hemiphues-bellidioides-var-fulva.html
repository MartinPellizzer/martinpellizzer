Hemiphues bellidioides var. fulva

Hemiphues bellidioides var. fulva, commonly known as the yellow hemiphues, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub characterized by its woody stems and dense foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, adding visual interest to its appearance. Its leaves are typically elliptical and have a smooth texture, while the stems are often covered in fine hairs.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
