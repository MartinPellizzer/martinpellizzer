Hiraea velutina

Hiraea velutina, commonly known as the velvet hiraea, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in greenish-yellow hues, and bloom during the warmer months.

Hiraea velutina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
