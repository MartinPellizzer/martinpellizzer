Hieracium carpathicum subsp. rutiliceps

Hieracium carpathicum subsp.

rutiliceps, commonly known as the Carpathian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Carpathian Mountains. It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in late summer.

The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and is often found in meadows and rocky habitats. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is notable for its medicinal and ornamental potential.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in some regions.
