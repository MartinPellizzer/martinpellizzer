Helleborus viridis subsp. istriacus

Helleborus viridis subsp.

istriacus, commonly known as the Istrian green hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is characterized by its dark green, waxy leaves that are often marked with white veins.

The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is adapted to calcareous soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
