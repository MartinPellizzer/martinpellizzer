Hymenophyllum caudiculatum

Hymenophyllum caudiculatum, commonly known as the slender fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizomes are short and thick, contributing to its ability to retain moisture.

Hymenophyllum caudiculatum is known for its intricate leaf structure and is often used in horticultural displays due to its aesthetic appeal.
