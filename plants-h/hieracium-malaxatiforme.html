Hieracium malaxatiforme

Hieracium malaxatiforme, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows and grassy areas, preferring moist and well-drained soils. It produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
