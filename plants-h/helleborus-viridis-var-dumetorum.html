Helleborus viridis var. dumetorum

Helleborus viridis var. dumetorum, commonly known as the green hellebore or lesser hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe.

It features dark green, waxy leaves that are typically heart-shaped and have a leathery texture. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. It prefers moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

This variety is closely related to other hellebore species and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
