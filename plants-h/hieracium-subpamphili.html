Hieracium subpamphili

Hieracium subpamphili, commonly known as the pale hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads made up of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disc.

Hieracium subpamphili is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it both a resilient and sometimes invasive species.
