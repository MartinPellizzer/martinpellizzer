Helichrysum mundii

Helichrysum mundii, commonly known as the Cape gold bush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of yellow to orange flowers that resemble small suns. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Helichrysum mundii is often used in gardens for its drought tolerance and vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
