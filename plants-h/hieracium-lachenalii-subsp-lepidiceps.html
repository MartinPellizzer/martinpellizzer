Hieracium lachenalii subsp. lepidiceps

Hieracium lachenalii subsp.

lepidiceps, commonly known as the Lachenal's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy basal rosettes.

The plant features yellowish to orange flowers arranged in a single head, typically blooming from June to August. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is often found in dry meadows and rocky habitats, showcasing its adaptability to various soil conditions.
