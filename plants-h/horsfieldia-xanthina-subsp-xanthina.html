Horsfieldia xanthina subsp. xanthina

Horsfieldia xanthina subsp.

xanthina, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments, and its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients. It produces showy, fragrant blooms that can last for several weeks.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses and botanical gardens.
