Hesperis secundiflora var. scabricarpa

Hesperis secundiflora var. scabricarpa, commonly known as the rough-seeded candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, white or pink flowers arranged in clusters. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a low, spreading growth habit. Its leaves are linear and covered with a rough, sandpapery texture, which gives it its varietal name.

It produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, typical of the mustard family.
