Hisingera caledonica

Hisingera caledonica, commonly known as the Caledonian heath, is a flowering plant native to the Scottish Highlands.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its low-growing shrub form with small, needle-like leaves. It produces clusters of bell-shaped, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring.

Hisingera caledonica thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moorland and woodland edges.
