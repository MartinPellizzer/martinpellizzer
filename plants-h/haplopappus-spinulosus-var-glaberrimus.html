Haplopappus spinulosus var. glaberrimus

Haplopappus spinulosus var. glaberrimus, commonly known as the smoothleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
