Hyacinthus olivieri

Hyacinthus olivieri, commonly known as the Corsican hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of blue, violet, and white, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Hyacinthus olivieri is also valued for its historical use in perfumery and traditional medicine.
