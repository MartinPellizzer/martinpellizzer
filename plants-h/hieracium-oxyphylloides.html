Hieracium oxyphylloides

Hieracium oxyphylloides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and features small, yellowish to reddish-orange composite flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it typically grows in moist, disturbed soils. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it can be found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
