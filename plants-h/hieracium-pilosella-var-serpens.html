Hieracium pilosella var. serpens

Hieracium pilosella var. serpens, commonly known as the serpentine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in calcareous or serpentine soils, thriving in rocky and open habitats. It produces small, yellowish flowers with pale green ray florets, and its leaves are usually hairy and alternate.

The species is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
