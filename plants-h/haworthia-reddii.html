Haworthia reddii

Haworthia reddii, commonly known as the red-tipped haworthia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features compact, rosette-shaped leaves that are typically fleshy and covered in a white, powdery coating called farina. The leaves are usually green with distinct red or pink markings along the edges, giving it its striking appearance. It produces small, white flowers during the spring or summer months.

This plant is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low water requirements and ability to thrive in bright, indirect light.
