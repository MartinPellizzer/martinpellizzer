Hieracium silvivagum

Hieracium silvivagum, commonly known as the woodland hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist woodland areas and has basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and form dense, flat clusters, attracting various pollinators.

Hieracium silvivagum is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments, thriving in rich, loamy soils.
