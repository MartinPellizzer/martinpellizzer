Hieracium penduloides

Hieracium penduloides, commonly known as the hanging hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its drooping, yellow-orange flower heads that resemble small dandelions. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Hieracium penduloides is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it is often found in temperate regions.
