Hieracium sparigerum

Hieracium sparigerum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges. It produces yellow-orange flowers with ray florets and is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils.

Hieracium sparigerum is often found in temperate climates and is considered a common weed in agricultural areas.
