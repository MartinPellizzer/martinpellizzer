Helianthus striatus

Helianthus striatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow disk flowers surrounded by narrow, greenish-yellow ray florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stem is hairy and upright.

Helianthus striatus is often found in prairies, open woods, and along roadsides, and it plays a role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
