Hibiscus amblyocarpus

Hibiscus amblyocarpus, commonly known as the woolly hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink or red. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Hibiscus amblyocarpus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
