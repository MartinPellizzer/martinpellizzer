Heliotropium crassifolium

Heliotropium crassifolium, commonly known as the thick-leaved heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or pink. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands. Its leaves are thick and leathery, which helps it retain moisture in arid conditions.

Heliotropium crassifolium is also known for its medicinal properties, with various traditional uses in herbal remedies.
