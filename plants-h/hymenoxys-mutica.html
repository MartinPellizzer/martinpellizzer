Hymenoxys mutica

Hymenoxys mutica, commonly known as the hairy hymenoxys, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb with a basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Its hairy foliage helps reduce water loss and protect against herbivory.
