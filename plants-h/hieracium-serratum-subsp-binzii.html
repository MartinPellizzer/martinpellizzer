Hieracium serratum subsp. binzii

Hieracium serratum subsp.

binzii, commonly known as the serrated hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. It features basal leaves that are lobed and serrated, giving it its common name.

The flowers are yellow and are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, attracting various pollinators.
