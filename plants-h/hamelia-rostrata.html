Hamelia rostrata

Hamelia rostrata, commonly known as flamebeard or red flamebush, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet, featuring upright stems and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its flowers are often accompanied by orange or yellow bracts, which enhance its visual appeal.

Hamelia rostrata is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
