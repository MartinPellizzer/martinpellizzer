Homalomena selaburensis

Homalomena selaburensis, commonly known as the Sunda arrowhead, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features heart-shaped, arrowhead-like leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Homalomena selaburensis is valued for its ornamental foliage and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
