Humboltia plantaginea

Humboltia plantaginea, commonly known as the California thimbleweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in a dense cluster at the top of its stem. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a fuzzy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Humboltia plantaginea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
