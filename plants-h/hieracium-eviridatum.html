Hieracium eviridatum

Hieracium eviridatum, commonly known as the green everlasting.

It is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae, characterized by its daisy-like appearance with yellow flowers and greenish-brown bracts. Native to Europe, it thrives in moist, grassy areas and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in late summer.

It is considered a perennial and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
