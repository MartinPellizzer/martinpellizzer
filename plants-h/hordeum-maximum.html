Hordeum maximum

Hordeum maximum, commonly known as wild barley, is a wild grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous tiny grains, which are its reproductive structures. Hordeum maximum is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in fields, thriving in a variety of environmental conditions.

It has been studied for its potential in agriculture and as a model organism for genetic research.
