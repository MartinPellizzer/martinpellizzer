Helichrysum dimorphum

Helichrysum dimorphum, commonly known as the Cape gold or everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, golden-yellow flowers that resemble tiny sunbursts. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

The flowers are also used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
