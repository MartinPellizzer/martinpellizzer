Hydnophytum petiolatum var. nigrescens

Hydnophytum petiolatum var. nigrescens, commonly known as the black-stemmed hydnophytum, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into fleshy, bladder-like fruits. The plant exhibits a unique symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, which helps it thrive in nutrient-poor soils. Its fruits are often dispersed by animals that consume them, contributing to its ecological role in forest ecosystems.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
