Hieracium laricinum

Hieracium laricinum, commonly known as the mountain hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in dense clusters.

Hieracium laricinum is known for its ability to tolerate cold climates and is often found in high-altitude environments.
