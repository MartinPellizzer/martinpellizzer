Heliamphora sarracenioides

Heliamphora sarracenioides, commonly known as the pitcher plant, is a carnivorous plant native to the tepuis of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lidless pitcher-shaped leaves that trap and digest insects. The plant's pitchers are typically green or reddish in color and produce a sweet nectar to lure prey. It thrives in humid, shaded environments with nutrient-poor soil.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it a fascinating subject in botanical studies.
