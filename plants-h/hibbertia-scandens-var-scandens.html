Hibbertia scandens var. scandens

Hibbertia scandens var. scandens, commonly known as the climbing hibbertia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a vigorous climber with woody stems and produces bright yellow flowers with red centers. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its flowers are typically borne in clusters. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in coastal and dry inland regions.

Hibbertia scandens var. scandens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
