Helichrysum isalense

Helichrysum isalense, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flower heads.

The plant is native to South Africa and is known for its drought-resistant properties, making it well-suited for arid environments. Its flowers are typically tubular and arranged in compact, rounded clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are usually narrow and linear, with a silvery or gray-green hue.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
