Hieracium pseudopicinum

Hieracium pseudopicinum, commonly known as the false picrel, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough, hairy texture, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium pseudopicinum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
