Hieracium caesariatellum

Hieracium caesariatellum, commonly known as the blue hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features bright blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Hieracium caesariatellum is often found in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils.
