Hieracium hoppeanum subsp. vulpinum

Hieracium hoppeanum subsp.

vulpinum, commonly known as the red-veined hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming from June to August. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs and have a distinctive reddish or purple vein pattern.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.
