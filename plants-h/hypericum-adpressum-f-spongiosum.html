Hypericum adpressum f. spongiosum

Hypericum adpressum f. spongiosum, commonly known as the spongy St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It features prostrate or ascending stems covered in dense, spongy, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and covered in a similar hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.
