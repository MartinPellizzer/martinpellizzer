Hieracium balbisianum subsp. kerneri

Hieracium balbisianum subsp.

kerneri, commonly known as the Kern's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy rosettes.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head.

It is known for its aggressive growth and is considered a weed in some regions due to its ability to spread rapidly.
