Hyparrhenia rufa

Hyparrhenia rufa, commonly known as red grass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically reddish in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in open woodland areas.

Hyparrhenia rufa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in degraded landscapes.
