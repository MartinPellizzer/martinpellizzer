Hosta caerulea var. capitata

Hosta caerulea var. capitata, commonly known as the Blue Riband Hosta, is a striking variety of the Hosta genus native to eastern Asia.

It features broad, slightly wavy, dark green leaves with a prominent blue-gray variegation that adds a dramatic visual effect. The plant produces upright, terminal spikes of fragrant, white to pale blue flowers in late summer. Its foliage is particularly showy, making it a popular choice for shaded garden landscapes.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, requiring minimal maintenance once established.
