Hieracium adjutanticeps

Hieracium adjutanticeps, commonly known as the adjutant-headed hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed habitats and is often found in areas with moderate to low light conditions.
