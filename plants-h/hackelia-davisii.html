Hackelia davisii

Hackelia davisii, commonly known as Davis's honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe. Hackelia davisii thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to native plant collections and ecological restoration projects.
