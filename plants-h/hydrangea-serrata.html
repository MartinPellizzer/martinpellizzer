Hydrangea serrata

Hydrangea serrata, commonly known as the mountain hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range from white to pink, blue, or purple depending on soil pH. The plant features serrated leaf margins and thrives in partial shade with moist, well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Hydrangea serrata is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
