Hyptis tenuiflora

Hyptis tenuiflora, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, often aromatic leaves and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and thickets. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

Hyptis tenuiflora is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
