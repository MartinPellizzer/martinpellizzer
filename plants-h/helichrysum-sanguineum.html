Helichrysum sanguineum

Helichrysum sanguineum, commonly known as the red everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its bright orange to red flower heads, which are composed of numerous small, tubular flowers. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Helichrysum sanguineum is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.
