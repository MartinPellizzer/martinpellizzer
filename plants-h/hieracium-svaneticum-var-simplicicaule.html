Hieracium svaneticum var. simplicicaule

Hieracium svaneticum var. simplicicaule, commonly known as the Svanetic hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded conditions. Its leaves are lanceolate and serrated, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat, disk-like inflorescence.

It is known for its low-growing habit and is often found in areas with moderate to dry soil conditions.
