Hymenaea travassosii

Hymenaea travassosii, commonly known as the Brazilian桃花心木, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, deciduous leaves and distinctive, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces heavy, woody pods that contain flat, hard seeds. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic qualities, making it important in local economies.

However, due to habitat loss and overharvesting, it is considered vulnerable to extinction.
