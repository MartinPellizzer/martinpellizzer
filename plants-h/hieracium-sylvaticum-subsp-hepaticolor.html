Hieracium sylvaticum subsp. hepaticolor

Hieracium sylvaticum subsp.

hepaticolor, commonly known as the liver-colored hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dark green, almost liver-colored foliage, which gives it its common name. It typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, thriving in temperate climates.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
