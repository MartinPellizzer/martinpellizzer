Hieracium auricula var. galicianum

Hieracium auricula var. galicianum, commonly known as the Galician buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with a distinctive cup-shaped base. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and forest edges. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
