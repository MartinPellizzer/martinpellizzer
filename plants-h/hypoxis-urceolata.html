Hypoxis urceolata

Hypoxis urceolata, commonly known as the African buttercup, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a rhizomatous root system and produces basal, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant is noted for its showy, yellow, dandelion-like flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Hypoxis urceolata is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
