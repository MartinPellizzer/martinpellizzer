Hamamelis japonica subsp. megalophylla

Hamamelis japonica subsp.

megalophylla, commonly known as the Japanese witch hazel, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan. It is characterized by its showy, fragrant yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late autumn or early winter.

The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that turn reddish in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its striking foliage and unique flowering period.
