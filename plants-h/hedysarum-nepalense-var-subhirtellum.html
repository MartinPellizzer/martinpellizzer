Hedysarum nepalense var. subhirtellum

Hedysarum nepalense var. subhirtellum, commonly known as the Nepal milkvetch, is a perennial leguminous plant native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and northern India.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and can form dense colonies, contributing to soil stabilization.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological benefits in restoration projects.
