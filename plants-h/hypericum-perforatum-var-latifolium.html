Hypericum perforatum var. latifolium

Hypericum perforatum var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf St. John's wort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and opposite, oval-shaped leaves with translucent dots. The plant typically grows in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antidepressant properties.

The species is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
