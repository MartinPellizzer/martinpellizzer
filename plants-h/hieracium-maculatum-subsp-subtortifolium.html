Hieracium maculatum subsp. subtortifolium

Hieracium maculatum subsp.

subtortifolium, commonly known as the spotted hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features a basal rosette of leaves that are typically hairy and have a reddish or brownish hue.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinct reddish or purple coloration on the underside.

It is often found in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, and is considered an invasive species in some regions.
