Helianthus scaberrimus

Helianthus scaberrimus, commonly known as rough sunflower, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to the southeastern United States, where it typically grows in moist, open habitats such as swamps and wet meadows. The plant features rough, hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive serrated edge. Its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer, are surrounded by pale green bracts.

Helianthus scaberrimus is an important plant for pollinators and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
