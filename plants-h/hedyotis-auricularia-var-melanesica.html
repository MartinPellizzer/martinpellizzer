Hedyotis auricularia var. melanesica

Hedyotis auricularia var. melanesica, commonly known as the Melanesian earleaf, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the Pacific islands.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and rocky crevices. The plant has opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. Its small white flowers are clustered in racemes and bloom during the wet season.

The species is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in coastal or lowland regions.
