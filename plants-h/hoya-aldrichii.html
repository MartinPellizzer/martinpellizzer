Hoya aldrichii

Hoya aldrichii, commonly known as the Aldrich hoya, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its waxy, elliptical leaves and clusters of star-shaped, pale pink flowers. The plant is a trailing vine that can grow up to several meters in length, making it suitable for hanging baskets or shelves. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Hoya aldrichii is popular among plant enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
