Hibiscus fugax

Hibiscus fugax, commonly known as the transient hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink, red, or white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsules that contain numerous small seeds. Hibiscus fugax is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or savanna habitats.

Its brief blooming period makes it a rare and noteworthy species in botanical studies.
