Hesperomeles ferruginea

Hesperomeles ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty-hued huito, is a member of the Ericaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in cloud forests or rocky soils. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or red, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are leathery and evergreen, providing year-round foliage.

The fruit is a small, yellowish berry that contains several seeds, and it is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
