Helenium eupatoriodes

Helenium eupatoriodes, commonly known as the purple ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy purple to pinkish-red flower heads. The plant produces composite flowers composed of numerous small tubular florets surrounded by ray florets. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist to wet soils in wetlands or along stream banks.

Helenium eupatoriodes is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
