Hypericum montanum var. punctatum

Hypericum montanum var. punctatum, commonly known as the spotted St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and distinctive spotted leaves, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its medicinal properties have been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.

The species is known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand harsh alpine conditions.
