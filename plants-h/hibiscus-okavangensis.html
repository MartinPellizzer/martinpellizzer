Hibiscus okavangensis

Hibiscus okavangensis, commonly known as the Okavango hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa, specifically found in the Okavango Delta region.

It is a shrub that typically grows in wetland areas, thriving in moist and often swampy environments. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually pink or red, with five petals and a prominent central stamen. Hibiscus okavangensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine by local communities.

Its botanical features make it an important species in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
