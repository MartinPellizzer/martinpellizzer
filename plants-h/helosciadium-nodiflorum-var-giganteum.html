Helosciadium nodiflorum var. giganteum

Helosciadium nodiflorum var. giganteum, commonly known as the giant false dandelion, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features a robust, upright stem with broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Native to southern Europe, it thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a dramatic, long-lasting floral display.
