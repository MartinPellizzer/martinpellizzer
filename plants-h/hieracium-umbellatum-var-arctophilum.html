Hieracium umbellatum var. arctophilum

Hieracium umbellatum var. arctophilum, commonly known as the Arctic hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open, rocky, or sandy habitats. The plant features bright yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which is characteristic of the genus Hieracium. Its leaves are usually basal, forming a rosette, and are covered with fine hairs.

This variety is adapted to cold climates and has a low-growing habit to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
