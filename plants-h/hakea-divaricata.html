Hakea divaricata

Hakea divaricata, commonly known as the variable hakea, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and striking, urn-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often recurved, providing a distinctive appearance.

Hakea divaricata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to coastal environments.
