Habenaria lisowskiana

Habenaria lisowskiana, commonly known as the Lisowskiana orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and the Philippines.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in humid, shaded forest environments. The plant features a slender, erect stem with a single or few leaves, and it produces small, greenish-white to pale yellow flowers. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the forest floor, and it often forms symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
