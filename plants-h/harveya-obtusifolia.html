Harveya obtusifolia

Harveya obtusifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved harveya, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions. It is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats, where it forms dense colonies.

Harveya obtusifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climates.
