Hypericum scabrum var. bourgaei

Hypericum scabrum var. bourgaei, commonly known as the rough-stemmed St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems covered in rough, glandular hairs and small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with four petals, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is closely related to other Hypericum species and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical medicinal uses.
