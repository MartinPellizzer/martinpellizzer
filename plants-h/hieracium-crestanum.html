Hieracium crestanum

Hieracium crestanum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, preferring cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are basal, rough, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Hieracium crestanum produces small yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
