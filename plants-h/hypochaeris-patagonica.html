Hypochaeris patagonica

Hypochaeris patagonica, commonly known as the Patagonian hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and basal rosette of leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces numerous small, herbaceous stems that bear the showy flower heads.

Hypochaeris patagonica is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
