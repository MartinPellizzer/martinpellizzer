Helichrysum fleckii subsp. volkii

Helichrysum fleckii subsp.

volkii, commonly known as the yellow everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, yellow, papery flowers that remain open for extended periods.

The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its striking appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
