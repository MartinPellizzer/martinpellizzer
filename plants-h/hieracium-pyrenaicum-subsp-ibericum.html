Hieracium pyrenaicum subsp. ibericum

Hieracium pyrenaicum subsp.

ibericum, commonly known as the Iberian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, rocky slopes, and forest edges.

The plant features bright yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, with ray florets that are often reflexed. Its leaves are lanceolate and hairy, and the stems are usually hairy as well.

This subspecies is characterized by its distinct morphology and ecological adaptations to its Mediterranean climate.
