Helictochloa bromoides subsp. bromoides

Helictochloa bromoides subsp.

bromoides, commonly known as bromoides grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America. It typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides, fields, and sandy soils.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to drought conditions and has a deep root system for water retention.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of desert and grassland ecosystems.
