Humboltia Loranthophylla

Humboltia loranthophylla is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the rainforests of Ecuador. It belongs to the Santalaceae family, known for its parasitic and hemiparasitic species. This plant is characterized by its striking red and orange flowers, which bloom in the summer months. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered critically endangered. Conservation efforts are essential to preserve this unique botanical wonder.
