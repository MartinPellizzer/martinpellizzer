Helianthemum rosmarinifolium

Helianthemum rosmarinifolium, commonly known as rosemary flower or rosemary shrub, is a low-growing, evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. The plant has aromatic, needle-like leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens or coastal areas.

Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem, hairy foliage, and a compact growth habit.
