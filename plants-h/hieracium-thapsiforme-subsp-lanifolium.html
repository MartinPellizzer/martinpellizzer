Hieracium thapsiforme subsp. lanifolium

Hieracium thapsiforme subsp.

lanifolium, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets.

The plant features a robust, hairy stem with alternate, broadly ovate to lanceolate leaves that are covered in fine, woolly hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow, with a central disk surrounded by pale yellow to white ray florets.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.
