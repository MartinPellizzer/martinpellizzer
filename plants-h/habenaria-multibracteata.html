Habenaria multibracteata

Habenaria multibracteata, commonly known as the broad-leaved marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often near water bodies. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces tall, erect flowering stems with clusters of pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. Its roots are specialized for absorbing nutrients from the surrounding soil.

The species is notable for its ecological role in wetland ecosystems and its ornamental value in horticulture.
