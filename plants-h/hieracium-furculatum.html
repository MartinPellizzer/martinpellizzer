Hieracium furculatum

Hieracium furculatum, commonly known as the forked hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in summer, while its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture.

Hieracium furculatum is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and is often found in disturbed environments.
