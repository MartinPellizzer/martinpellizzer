Hieracium lactucella

Hieracium lactucella, commonly known as the milk thistle, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia, and is characterized by its distinctive purple flowers and spiny leaves. The plant contains a milky sap, which is a key feature in its common name. Its leaves are typically lobed and have a rough texture, while the flowers are arranged in a composite head.

Hieracium lactucella is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
