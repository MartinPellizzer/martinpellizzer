Helleborus viridis var. jacquinianus

Helleborus viridis var. jacquinianus, commonly known as the green hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that are broadly ovate with a serrated margin. The plant produces clusters of pale green to white flowers with prominent yellow stamens, which bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained, moist soil, often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its unique foliage and early blooming habit.
