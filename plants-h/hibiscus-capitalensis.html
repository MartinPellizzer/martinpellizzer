Hibiscus capitalensis

Hibiscus capitalensis, commonly known as the Cape York hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Cape York Peninsula in Queensland, Australia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink, with a prominent central stigma. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in coastal and sandy soils. Hibiscus capitalensis is a shrub that can grow up to 2 meters in height, producing its flowers throughout the warmer months.

It plays an important ecological role in supporting local pollinators and wildlife.
