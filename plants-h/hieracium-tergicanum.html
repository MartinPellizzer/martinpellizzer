Hieracium tergicanum

Hieracium tergicanum, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are rough and hairy, and the flowers are yellow with ray florets.

It is often considered a weed but plays a role in supporting pollinators.
