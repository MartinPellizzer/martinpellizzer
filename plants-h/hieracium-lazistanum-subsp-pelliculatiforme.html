Hieracium lazistanum subsp. pelliculatiforme

Hieracium lazistanum subsp.

pelliculatiforme, commonly known as the silky hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus. It is characterized by its rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and yellow disk flowers with ray florets.

The plant has a creeping rootstock and produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
