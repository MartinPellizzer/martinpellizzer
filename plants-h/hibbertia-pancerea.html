Hibbertia pancerea

Hibbertia pancerea, commonly known as the yellow wattle, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. The plant has narrow, waxy leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. Hibbertia pancerea thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and wildlife.
