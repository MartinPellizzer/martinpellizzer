Helianthemum × tornesae

Helianthemum × tornesae, commonly known as the hybrid rock rose, is a perennial shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens across southern Europe.
