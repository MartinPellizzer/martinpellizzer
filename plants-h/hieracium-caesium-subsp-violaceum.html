Hieracium caesium subsp. violaceum

Hieracium caesium subsp.

violaceum, commonly known as violet hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while the flowers range from pale violet to deep purple, adding ornamental value to its habitat.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
