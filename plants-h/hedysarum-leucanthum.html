Hedysarum leucanthum

Hedysarum leucanthum, commonly known as white sweet clover, is a flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a deep root system that helps it thrive in a variety of soil types, including dry and disturbed soils.

It is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it a valuable plant in ecological restoration and agriculture.
