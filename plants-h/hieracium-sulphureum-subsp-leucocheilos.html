Hieracium sulphureum subsp. leucocheilos

Hieracium sulphureum subsp.

leucocheilos, commonly known as the sulphur hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to orange-yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are basal, rough, and often covered with fine hairs, while its flowering stems are usually unbranched.

The subspecies leucocheilos is distinguished by its white-tinged flower heads, which set it apart from other members of the species.
