Hieracium caesium subsp. campylodon

Hieracium caesium subsp.

campylodon, commonly known as the hooked hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow ray flowers and robust, hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowering period occurs from late spring to early autumn.

This subspecies is notable for its distinctive hooked bracts that enclose the flower heads.
