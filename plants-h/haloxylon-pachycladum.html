Haloxylon pachycladum

Haloxylon pachycladum, commonly known as the desert date tree, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, woody stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss through transpiration. Its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, and it produces small, hard fruits that are adapted for dispersal by animals.

Haloxylon pachycladum plays an important role in stabilizing desert soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
