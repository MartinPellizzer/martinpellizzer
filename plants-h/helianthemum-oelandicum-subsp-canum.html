Helianthemum oelandicum subsp. canum

Helianthemum oelandicum subsp.

canum, commonly known as the Oeland thrift, is a low-growing, mat-forming shrub native to southern Europe. It features small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
