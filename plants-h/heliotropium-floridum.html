Heliotropium floridum

Heliotropium floridum, commonly known as Florida heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 feet. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a faint violet hue. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Heliotropium floridum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along the edges of water bodies.
