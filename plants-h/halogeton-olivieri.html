Halogeton olivieri

Halogeton olivieri, commonly known as the desert barley, is a halophytic plant adapted to thrive in arid and saline environments.

It possesses a unique morphology with thick, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in drought conditions. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its seeds are highly resilient, capable of remaining viable for extended periods in harsh conditions.

Halogeton olivieri plays a role in stabilizing soils in desert ecosystems and is used as a forage plant in some regions.
