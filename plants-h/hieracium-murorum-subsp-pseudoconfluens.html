Hieracium murorum subsp. pseudoconfluens

Hieracium murorum subsp.

pseudoconfluens, commonly known as the creeping hawkweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically forms dense mats on the ground, with slender, often reddish stems that spread horizontally.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels above the leaves. Its leaves are usually basal, spoon-shaped, and have a hairy surface.

This subspecies is characterized by its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides.
