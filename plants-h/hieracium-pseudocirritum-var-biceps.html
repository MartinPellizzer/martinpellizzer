Hieracium pseudocirritum var. biceps

Hieracium pseudocirritum var. biceps, commonly known as the two-branched hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its inflorescence consists of numerous small composite flowers.

The species is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain habitats.
