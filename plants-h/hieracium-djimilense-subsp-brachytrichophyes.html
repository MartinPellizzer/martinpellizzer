Hieracium djimilense subsp. brachytrichophyes

Hieracium djimilense subsp.

brachytrichophyes, commonly known as the short-haired hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and numerous flowering stems that bear yellow disk flowers surrounded by pale yellow to white ray florets.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually hairy, and the subspecies is distinguished by its shorter trichomes compared to other members of the species.

It is native to parts of East Africa, particularly Kenya and Tanzania.
