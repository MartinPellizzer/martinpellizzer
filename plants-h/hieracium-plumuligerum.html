Hieracium plumuligerum

Hieracium plumuligerum, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features soft, woolly leaves covered in fine hairs, which give it its common name. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, and its growth habit is typically prostrate or spreading.

Hieracium plumuligerum is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats such as grasslands and roadsides.
