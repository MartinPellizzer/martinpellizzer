Horsfieldia moluccana var. petiolaris

Horsfieldia moluccana var. petiolaris, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in summer and are pollinated by insects, particularly bees.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
