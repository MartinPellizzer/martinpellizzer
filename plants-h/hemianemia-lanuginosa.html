Hemianemia lanuginosa

Hemianemia lanuginosa, commonly known as the woolly hemianthus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in humid tropical regions. Its leaves are ovate with a velvety texture, and it has a creeping growth habit.

Hemianemia lanuginosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to form dense mats in suitable conditions.
