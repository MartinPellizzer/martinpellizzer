Hieracium caesiopellitum

Hieracium caesiopellitum, commonly known as the blue-haired hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a rough texture.

The flowers are borne in dense clusters and bloom from late spring to early autumn.
