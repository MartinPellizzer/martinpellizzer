Hieracium amphigena

Hieracium amphigena, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium amphigena is known for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
