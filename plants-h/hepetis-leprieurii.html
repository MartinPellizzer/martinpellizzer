Hepetis leprieurii

Hepetis leprieurii, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

Hepetis leprieurii is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
