Hieracium ramondii subsp. neochrysanthemum

Hieracium ramondii subsp.

neochrysanthemum, commonly known as the golden hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features a rosette of basal leaves that are typically hairy and have a rough texture.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are often alternate and have a distinctive lobed or toothed margin.

This species is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
