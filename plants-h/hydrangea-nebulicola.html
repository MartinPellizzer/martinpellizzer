Hydrangea nebulicola

Hydrangea nebulicola, commonly known as the cloud-like hydrangea, is a species native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from white to pink, depending on soil pH. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue.

Hydrangea nebulicola thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
