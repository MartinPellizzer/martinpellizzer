Henckelia lyrata

Henckelia lyrata, commonly known as the lyre bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue and violet, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Henckelia lyrata is valued in horticulture for its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
