Hieracium adeximium

Hieracium adeximium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture, and it produces yellow flowers in late summer.

Hieracium adeximium is known for its ability to thrive in various soil types and is often found in open habitats.
