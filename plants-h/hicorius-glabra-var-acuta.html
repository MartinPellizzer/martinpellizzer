Hicorius glabra var. acuta

Hicorius glabra var. acuta, commonly known as the smooth-hicorius variant with a sharp tip, is a lesser-known species within the Hicorius genus.

It is characterized by its smooth, waxy leaves and a distinct, pointed growth at the terminal end of its stems. This plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its botanical features include a compact growth habit and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in the spring.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often found in specialized ecological zones.
