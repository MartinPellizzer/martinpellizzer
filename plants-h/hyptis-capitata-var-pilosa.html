Hyptis capitata var. pilosa

Hyptis capitata var. pilosa, commonly known as hairy hyptis, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal properties. The plant is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or white flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and traditional purposes.
