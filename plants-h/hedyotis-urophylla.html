Hedyotis urophylla

Hedyotis urophylla, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features small, yellowish-green leaves that are opposite and ovate in shape. Its flowers are clustered in cymes and are usually yellow or orange, attracting various pollinators.

Hedyotis urophylla is known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
