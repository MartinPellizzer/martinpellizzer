Hypericum ascyron var. macrosepalum

Hypericum ascyron var. macrosepalum, commonly known as the large-sepaled St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features opposite, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers have showy, fleshy sepals that are often larger than the petals, giving it its distinctive appearance.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soil in full sun.
