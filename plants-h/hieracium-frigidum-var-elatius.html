Hieracium frigidum var. elatius

Hieracium frigidum var. elatius, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, often on rocky slopes and meadows at high elevations. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowering stems bear yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It is adapted to cold climates and has a low-growing habit to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
