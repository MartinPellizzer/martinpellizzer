Helictochloa delicatula

Helictochloa delicatula, commonly known as the slender bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open woodlands and along stream banks. The plant has fine, blue-green blades that are often slightly curled at the edges. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Helictochloa delicatula is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
