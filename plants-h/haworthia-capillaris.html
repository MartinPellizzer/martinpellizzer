Haworthia capillaris

Haworthia capillaris, commonly known as the curly Haworthia, is a species of succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, hair-like structures. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in summer, typically on a slender stem. Its leaves are fleshy and store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

Haworthia capillaris is popular as a houseplant due to its low maintenance and unique appearance.
