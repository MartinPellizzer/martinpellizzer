Hibiscus porophyllus

Hibiscus porophyllus, commonly known as the woolly hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has hairy, velvety leaves that help it retain moisture in its native habitat. Hibiscus porophyllus typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in wetlands or along riverbanks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
