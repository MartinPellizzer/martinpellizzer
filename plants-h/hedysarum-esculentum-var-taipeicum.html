Hedysarum esculentum var. taipeicum

Hedysarum esculentum var. taipeicum, commonly known as the Taipei edible vetch, is a leguminous plant native to Taiwan.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds that are rich in protein. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in traditional agriculture for its nutritional value.

Due to its adaptability, it is also cultivated in various regions with similar climatic conditions.
