Heliconia revoluta

Heliconia revoluta, commonly known as the red heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its striking, boat-shaped inflorescence. The plant produces bright red bracts that enclose numerous small, yellowish flowers. Its leaves are large, broad, and often have a waxy texture.

Heliconia revoluta is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
