Hieracium erythroglossum

Hieracium erythroglossum, commonly known as the red-lipped hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its most distinctive feature is the bright red corolla, which contrasts with the yellow-orange disk flowers.

Hieracium erythroglossum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is often found in colonies.
