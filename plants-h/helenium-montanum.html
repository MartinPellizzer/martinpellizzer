Helenium montanum

Helenium montanum, commonly known as the mountain sneezeweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has narrow, grass-like leaves. Its bright orange to yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Helenium montanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
