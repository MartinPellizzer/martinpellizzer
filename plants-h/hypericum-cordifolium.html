Hypericum cordifolium

Hypericum cordifolium, commonly known as heart-leaved St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant contains hypericin and pseudohypericin, which are responsible for its medicinal properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in alpine and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and potential therapeutic uses, it is cultivated in gardens and herbal medicine practices.
