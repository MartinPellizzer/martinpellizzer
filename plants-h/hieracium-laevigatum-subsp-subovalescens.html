Hieracium laevigatum subsp. subovalescens

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

subovalescens, commonly known as the smooth-haired hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.

The plant features basal leaves that are lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, while the upper leaves are shorter and more oblanceolate. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, with ray florets that are often shorter than the disk florets.

The species is characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and is part of the Asteraceae family.
