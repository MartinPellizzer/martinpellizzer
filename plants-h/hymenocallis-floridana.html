Hymenocallis floridana

Hymenocallis floridana, commonly known as the Florida rain lily, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that form a basal rosette, and it produces a tall, slender stem that supports its showy inflorescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.

Hymenocallis floridana is a herbaceous perennial that plays an important role in local ecosystems.
