Hieracium jabalambrense

Hieracium jabalambrense, commonly known as the Jabalambren Mountain Hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, adapting to the harsh conditions of its high-altitude habitat. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium jabalambrense is often found in open areas with limited vegetation, thriving in the unique microclimates of its endemic range.
