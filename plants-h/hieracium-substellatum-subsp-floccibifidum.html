Hieracium substellatum subsp. floccibifidum

Hieracium substellatum subsp.

floccibifidum, commonly known as the fluff-leaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and tall, flowering stems that bear yellow composite flowers.

The leaves are typically covered in a dense layer of white, cottony hairs, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats, often found in meadows and along roadsides.

The plant is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its showy, dandelion-like inflorescence.
