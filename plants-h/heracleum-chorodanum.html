Heracleum chorodanum

Heracleum chorodanum, commonly known as the Caucasian cowplant, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescence.

Its stem is hollow and often covered with fine hairs, and it can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

Due to its habitat preferences, it is relatively rare and found primarily in specific alpine and subalpine environments.
