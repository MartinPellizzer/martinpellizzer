Hibiscus lilacinus

Hibiscus lilacinus, commonly known as the blue hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically medium to dark green in color. Hibiscus lilacinus thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be found in gardens and natural habitats.
