Heliocarpus popayanensis var. trichopodus

Heliocarpus popayanensis var. trichopodus, commonly known as the hairy nut tree, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its rough, fissured bark and palmate leaves. The plant produces distinctive, hard, three-lobed fruits that are often covered in a dense layer of hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in axillary inflorescences.

This variety is noted for its ecological importance in tropical dry forests and its potential use in traditional medicine.
