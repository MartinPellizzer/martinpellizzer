Hieracium virosiforme

Hieracium virosiforme, commonly known as the poison scabious, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its yellowish to greenish-yellow flowers and hairy, often glandular leaves. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. It produces small, oval seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Due to its toxic compounds, it is considered a harmful plant to livestock and humans.
