Halotis pilosa

Halotis pilosa, commonly known as the hairy saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that helps reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that are adapted to minimize transpiration. It produces small, greenish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, fleshy fruits.

Halotis pilosa plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock in dry landscapes.
