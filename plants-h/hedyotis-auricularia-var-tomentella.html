Hedyotis auricularia var. tomentella

Hedyotis auricularia var. tomentella, commonly known as the woolly blushing geranium, is a low-growing perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features small, bright red or pink flowers that bloom in late summer, often in clusters along the stems. The leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape, with a distinctive woolly or pubescent texture that gives the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or as a border plant.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
