Hieracium canadense var. divaricatum

Hieracium canadense var. divaricatum, commonly known as the Canadian hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow flowers. The plant has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the upper leaves are more narrow and gradually reduce in size. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and open woodlands.

Its vegetative reproduction through rhizomes makes it a persistent weed in various habitats.
