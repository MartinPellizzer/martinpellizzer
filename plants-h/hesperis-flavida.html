Hesperis flavida

Hesperis flavida, commonly known as the yellow horned-poppy, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Hesperis flavida is known for its short blooming period in late spring to early summer.
