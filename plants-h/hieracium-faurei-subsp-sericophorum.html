Hieracium faurei subsp. sericophorum

Hieracium faurei subsp.

sericophorum, commonly known as the silky hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its finely hairy, dark green leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in mountainous regions of Europe. It has a rosette growth form with a basal cluster of leaves and a flowering stem that rises from the center.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
