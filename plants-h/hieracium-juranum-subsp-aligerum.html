Hieracium juranum subsp. aligerum

Hieracium juranum subsp.

aligerum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky and gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.

The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are usually hairy and have a rough texture. Its flowering stems bear yellow ray florets surrounded by a dense cluster of tubular disc florets.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to harsh mountain environments and its role in alpine ecosystems.
