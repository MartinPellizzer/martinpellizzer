Heliconia leucogramma

Heliconia leucogramma, commonly known as the white-marked heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is characterized by its large, colorful bracts that resemble petals. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that are often hidden within the vibrant bracts. Its leaves are broad, elongated, and have a smooth, waxy texture.

Heliconia leucogramma is often cultivated for its ornamental value in tropical gardens and as a cut flower.
