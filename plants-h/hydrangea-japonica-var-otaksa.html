Hydrangea japonica var. otaksa

Hydrangea japonica var. otaksa, commonly known as the Japanese hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from blue to pink, depending on soil pH. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically large, ovate, and have a serrated margin.

Hydrangea japonica var. otaksa thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
