Hordeum aestivum

Hordeum aestivum, commonly known as barley, is a cereal grass that belongs to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender stalks and small, spikelet-like flowers that contain three to nine rows of seeds. Barley is a hardy plant that thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its grains, which are used in food, animal feed, and brewing. The plant has a fibrous root system and produces a dense, cylindrical inflorescence called a spike.

Its botanical features make it an important crop in both agriculture and ecological systems.
