Hieracium ziljevanum

Hieracium ziljevanum, commonly known as the Ziljevanum hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, and its leaves are usually serrated with a rough texture.

Hieracium ziljevanum is often considered a weed but plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
