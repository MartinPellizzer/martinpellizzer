Hieracium subturbinatum

Hieracium subturbinatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and ragworts. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are narrow and often have a rough texture, while its yellow flowers appear in clusters at the top of the stems.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and is often found in temperate climates.
