Hakea georgeana

Hakea georgeana, commonly known as the Georgeana hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, cylindrical or club-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces woody, cone-like fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance. Hakea georgeana typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

It is often found in heathlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
