Hedyotis chevalieri

Hedyotis chevalieri, commonly known as the dwarf silverweed, is a small herbaceous plant native to South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. The plant features slender, creeping stems with small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are tiny, white or pale yellow, and arranged in clusters, producing small, three-lobed fruits.

Hedyotis chevalieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
