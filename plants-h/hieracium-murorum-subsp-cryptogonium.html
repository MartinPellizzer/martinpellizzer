Hieracium murorum subsp. cryptogonium

Hieracium murorum subsp.

cryptogonium, commonly known as the cryptogonic hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellowish to reddish flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smaller, more compact growth form compared to other hawkweed species. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

The plant is notable for its ability to hybridize with other Hieracium species, contributing to its genetic diversity.
