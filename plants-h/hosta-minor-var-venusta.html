Hosta minor var. venusta

Hosta minor var. venusta, commonly known as the golden veined hosta, is a compact, low-growing perennial that thrives in shady, moist environments.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a striking golden-green coloration and prominent, wavy veins that add to its ornamental appeal. The plant produces small, fragrant, white flowers on slender stems in late summer. It is prized for its ability to thrive in partial to full shade and its ability to tolerate a variety of soil types.

Due to its attractive foliage, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.
