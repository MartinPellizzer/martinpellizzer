Habenaria balfouriana

Habenaria balfouriana, commonly known as the Balfour's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, elongated flower spikes that bear numerous small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories, where it thrives in well-drained, humus-rich soil. Its roots are often mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to enhance nutrient uptake.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated for horticultural purposes.
