Hieracium vulgatum subsp. daedalolepioides

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

daedalolepioides, commonly known as the showy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads made up of numerous small florets.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped with rough textures, and the flowers are yellow with a distinctive, flattened disc.

This subspecies is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
