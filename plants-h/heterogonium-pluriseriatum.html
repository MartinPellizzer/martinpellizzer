Heterogonium pluriseriatum

Heterogonium pluriseriatum, commonly known as the multi-layered green algae, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its complex thallus structure.

It consists of multiple layers of cells that allow for efficient light absorption and nutrient uptake. The plant exhibits a filamentous growth pattern, with branching structures that enhance its surface area. It thrives in freshwater environments, particularly in slow-moving or still waters.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a primary producer in aquatic ecosystems and its potential applications in biotechnology.
