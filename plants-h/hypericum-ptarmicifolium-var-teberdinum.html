Hypericum ptarmicifolium var. teberdinum

Hypericum ptarmicifolium var. teberdinum, commonly known as the Teberdinum St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a member of the Hypericaceae family, characterized by its yellow flowers and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas and has narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
