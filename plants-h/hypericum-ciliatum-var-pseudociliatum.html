Hypericum ciliatum var. pseudociliatum

Hypericum ciliatum var. pseudociliatum, commonly known as the silky St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves with fine, silky hairs along the edges. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are characteristic of the Hypericaceae family. Its leaves and flowers contain hypericin and other compounds, which have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.

The species is often found in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in open woodlands.
