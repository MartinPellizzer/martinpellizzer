Hakea pompona

Hakea pompona, commonly known as the red hakea, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red, fluffy flowers that resemble pompons, which bloom in late summer. The plant has woody stems and narrow, needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Hakea pompona is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
