Hieracium murorum subsp. subobtextiforme

Hieracium murorum subsp.

subobtextiforme, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and yellow composite flowers.

The leaves are typically rough and covered in fine hairs, which give the plant its common name. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

This subspecies is often confused with other hawkweed species due to its similar appearance.
