Hibiscus furcellatus var. youngianus

Hibiscus furcellatus var. youngianus, commonly known as the Young's hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink or red. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Hibiscus furcellatus var. youngianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
