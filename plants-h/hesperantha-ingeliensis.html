Hesperantha ingeliensis

Hesperantha ingeliensis, commonly known as the Orange-flowered Bell Orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange petals and a unique, bell-shaped flower structure that attracts specific pollinators. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its blooms appear during the cooler months, adding a splash of color to the landscape.

Hesperantha ingeliensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
