Hyptis marifolia

Hyptis marifolia, commonly known as Brazilian hyssop, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and spikes of blue or purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in tropical and subtropical regions.

Hyptis marifolia is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic qualities.
