Hieracium amblycybe

Hieracium amblycybe, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces yellowish to reddish composite flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, pastures, and along stream banks in temperate regions. It has a fibrous root system and can spread vegetatively through rhizomes.

Hieracium amblycybe is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a common weed in agricultural areas.
