Hornstedtia merahjambu

Hornstedtia merahjambu, commonly known as the red grape orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its striking red and purple inflorescences, which resemble grapes, hence its common name. It typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, attaching itself to trees or rocks.

The flowers are highly fragrant and attract various pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.
