Hypserpa ademae

Hypserpa ademae, commonly known as the Ademae's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers with a distinctive violet or purple hue. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments within the understory of tropical rainforests. Its flowers are pollinated by specific insect species, contributing to its ecological niche.

Hypserpa ademae is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated by enthusiasts of rare orchids.
