Haplodiscus kingii

Haplodiscus kingii, commonly known as King's haplodiscus, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with reflexed petals. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often clustered at the base.

Haplodiscus kingii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
