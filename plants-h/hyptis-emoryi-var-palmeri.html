Hyptis emoryi var. palmeri

Hyptis emoryi var. palmeri, commonly known as Palmer's hyptis, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert and semi-desert environments. Its leaves are often hairy and have a strong, minty scent, while the flowers are typically pale blue to purple in color.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators in its native habitat.
