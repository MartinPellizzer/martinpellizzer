Hymenophyllum tayloriae

Hymenophyllum tayloriae, commonly known as Taylor's filmy fern, is a rare and delicate member of the Hymenophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its thin, translucent fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in cool, moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spores are released from specialized structures called sporangia, which are clustered in dense, cushion-like formations.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
