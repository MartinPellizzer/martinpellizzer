Hyalolepis ophioglossoides

Hyalolepis ophioglossoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fern, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Ophioglossaceae and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, with a distinctive, glossy appearance.

Hyalolepis ophioglossoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
