Hieracium murorum subsp. incisum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

incisum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces yellow flowers in clusters.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
