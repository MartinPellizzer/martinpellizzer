Hieracium auriculoides subsp. sabiniceps

Hieracium auriculoides subsp.

sabiniceps, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features bright yellow flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer, typically arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are lance-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
