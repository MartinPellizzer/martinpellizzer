Hieracium mediolanense

Hieracium mediolanense, commonly known as the Milan hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium mediolanense is often found in regions with a temperate climate and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
