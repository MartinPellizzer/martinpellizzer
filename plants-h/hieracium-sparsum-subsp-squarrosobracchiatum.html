Hieracium sparsum subsp. squarrosobracchiatum

Hieracium sparsum subsp.

squarrosobracchiatum, commonly known as the squarrose-bracted hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, often reddish stems and basal rosettes of leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are typically lanceolate to ovate, with a rough, hairy texture, and the subspecies is distinguished by its squarrose (spread-out) bracts.

It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
