Haemodorum teretifolium

Haemodorum teretifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bloodroot, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the family Haemodoraceae, which is known as the grassblades or bloodroot family. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking red or orange flowers during the spring season. It is often found in heathlands and open forests, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental value make it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant collections.
