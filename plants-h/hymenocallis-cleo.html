Hymenocallis cleo

Hymenocallis cleo, commonly known as the Cleopatra lily, is a striking tropical plant native to the Americas.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with a contrasting throat. The plant produces large, fleshy, white bulbs that store nutrients and water, aiding its survival in dry conditions. Its leaves are broad, strap-like, and often have a waxy texture.

Hymenocallis cleo is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates.
