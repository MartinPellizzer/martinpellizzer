Hieracium coleoglaucum

Hieracium coleoglaucum, commonly known as the blue-eyed weed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks. Its blue to violet petals are surrounded by a white or pale blue corolla, giving it a striking appearance.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
