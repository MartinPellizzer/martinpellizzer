Hedyotis purpurea

Hedyotis purpurea, commonly known as purple dotweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are often pink or purple in color. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Hedyotis purpurea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
