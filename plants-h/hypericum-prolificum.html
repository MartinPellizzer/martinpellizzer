Hypericum prolificum

Hypericum prolificum, commonly known as the golden-stemmed St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has woody stems that are often covered in small, glandular dots, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, with a slightly serrated edge.

Hypericum prolificum is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
