Hieracium gudergomiense

Hieracium gudergomiense, commonly known as the Gudergomi hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats, displaying bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are generally hairy and lance-shaped, with a basal rosette structure.

Hieracium gudergomiense is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant appearance.
