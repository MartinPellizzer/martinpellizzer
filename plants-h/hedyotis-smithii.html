Hedyotis smithii

Hedyotis smithii, commonly known as the Smith's dotweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale green, with four petals. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Hedyotis smithii is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local insect populations.
