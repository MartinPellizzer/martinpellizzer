Halothamnus somalensis

Halothamnus somalensis, commonly known as the Somali halothamnus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Somalia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically arranged in dense inflorescences. It is well adapted to dry environments, with mechanisms to store water and reduce transpiration.

Halothamnus somalensis plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for local wildlife.
