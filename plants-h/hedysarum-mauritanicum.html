Hedysarum mauritanicum

Hedysarum mauritanicum, commonly known as the Mauritania sweet clover, is a flowering plant native to North Africa and the Middle East.

It is a perennial herb that grows up to 1 meter tall, characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers arranged in racemes. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid and semi-arid regions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as forage for livestock.
