Hedysarum montanum

Hedysarum montanum, commonly known as mountain milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a fibrous root system and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Hedysarum montanum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
