Hieracium caucasiense

Hieracium caucasiense, commonly known as Caucasian hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows and rocky areas, thriving in temperate climates. It features basal leaves that are often hairy and yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late summer.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
