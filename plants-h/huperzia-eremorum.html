Huperzia eremorum

Huperzia eremorum, commonly known as the desert huperzia, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its simple, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. This plant is adapted to arid environments, featuring a thick, succulent stem that helps store water. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils in regions with low precipitation.

The plant's spore-producing structures, called sporangia, are located on the underside of its leaves, a feature typical of many ferns.
