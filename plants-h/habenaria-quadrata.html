Habenaria quadrata

Habenaria quadrata, commonly known as the four-cornered orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive square-shaped petals and sepals, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellowish in color, and they bloom during the summer months.

Habenaria quadrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
