Hieracium tendinum subsp. tinctilingua

Hieracium tendinum subsp.

tinctilingua, commonly known as the purple hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils.

The plant features basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces yellow to orange composite flowers in late summer. Its inflorescence consists of a single, showy flower head surrounded by bracts.

The species is characterized by its hairy stems and the presence of glandular dots on its leaves and stems.
