Hedychium neocarneum

Hedychium neocarneum, commonly known as the New Guinea white ginger, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, fragrant white or cream-colored flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Hedychium neocarneum is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
