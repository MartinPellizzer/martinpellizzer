Habenaria pubidactyla

Habenaria pubidactyla, commonly known as the hairy-handed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments, often on the forest floor or along stream banks. The plant features a sympodial growth habit with a pseudobulb and a single or few leaves. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellow, with a distinctive, hairy appearance on the petals and sepals.

The species is notable for its unique morphology and is appreciated for its ornamental value in horticulture.
