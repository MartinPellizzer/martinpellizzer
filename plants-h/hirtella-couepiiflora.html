Hirtella couepiiflora

Hirtella couepiiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered hirtella, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a leathery texture, while its bark is smooth and pale.

Hirtella couepiiflora is often found in humid, shaded environments within lowland forests.
