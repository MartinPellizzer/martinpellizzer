Hedysarum dshambulicum

Hedysarum dshambulicum, commonly known as the Dshambul's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.

Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as forage for livestock.
