Heliopsis parviceps

Heliopsis parviceps, commonly known as the narrowleaf heliopsis, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, branched stems that bear clusters of bright yellow, daisy-like flowers. The plant has lance-shaped, aromatic leaves that are typically hairy and gray-green in appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Heliopsis parviceps is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
