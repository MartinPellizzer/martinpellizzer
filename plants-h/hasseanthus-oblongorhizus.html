Hasseanthus oblongorhizus

Hasseanthus oblongorhizus, commonly known as the African buttercup, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb with a distinctive swollen, tuberous root system that stores nutrients and water. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers with prominent, showy petals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some regions.
