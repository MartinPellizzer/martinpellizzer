Hopea bracteata var. penangiana

Hopea bracteata var. penangiana, commonly known as Penang hopea, is a species of flowering plant in the family Dipterocarpaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Thailand, where it grows in lowland dipterocarp forests. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and its distinctive, elongated leaves with a leathery texture. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in yellow or greenish-yellow hues, and it produces woody fruits containing one or two seeds.

This species is valued for its timber and is also studied for its ecological role in tropical rainforests.
