Horsfieldia bivalvis

Horsfieldia bivalvis, commonly known as the two-valved orchid, is a tropical orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, pendulous flowers with a distinctive two-part lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its flowers are usually greenish-white with a yellow or orange lip, and it produces small, round fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Horsfieldia bivalvis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical botanical gardens.
