Hieracium murorum subsp. distinctum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

distinctum, commonly known as the distinct hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrowly lanceolate leaves and produces yellow flowers in a single terminal capitulum.

The plant is native to Europe and is often found in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils. It has a taproot system and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

The subspecies distinctum is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology and floral structure compared to other members of the species.
