Hypericum garciae

Hypericum garciae, commonly known as the Garcia's St. John's Wort, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Hypericaceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and woody stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are opposite and oval-shaped, often with a slight hairy texture.

Hypericum garciae is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for potential medicinal properties.
