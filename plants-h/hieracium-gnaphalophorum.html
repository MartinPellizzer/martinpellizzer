Hieracium gnaphalophorum

Hieracium gnaphalophorum, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It has a creeping growth habit with stolons that help it spread across the ground.

The plant is often found in open habitats and is relatively hardy, tolerating a range of environmental conditions.
