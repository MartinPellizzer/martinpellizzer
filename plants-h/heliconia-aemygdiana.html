Heliconia aemygdiana

Heliconia aemygdiana, commonly known as the red-veined heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its large, colorful, paddle-shaped bracts that enclose its inflorescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are broad and glossy, often with a waxy texture, and can grow up to several feet in length.

Heliconia aemygdiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
