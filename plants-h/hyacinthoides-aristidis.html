Hyacinthoides aristidis

Hyacinthoides aristidis, commonly known as the purple rock hyacinth, is a species of flowering plant in the Asparagaceae family.

It is native to the Iberian Peninsula and is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth habit. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers in early spring. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and form a tight rosette near the ground.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
