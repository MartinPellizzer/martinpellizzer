Heinsia crinita

Heinsia crinita, commonly known as the silky hairgrass, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its fine, silky texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in dense clusters. Heinsia crinita thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its delicate appearance.
