Hypericum aegyptium

Hypericum aegyptium, commonly known as the Egyptian St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and opposite, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as wastelands and rocky areas. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Hypericum aegyptium is also known for its historical use in herbal medicine.
