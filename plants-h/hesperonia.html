Hesperonia

Hesperonia, commonly known as the nightshade plant, is a genus of flowering plants in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It consists of several species that are native to various regions of the Americas. These plants are typically shrubs or small trees, characterized by their showy, often brightly colored flowers. The leaves are usually alternate and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Hesperonia species are known for their ornamental value and are sometimes cultivated for their vibrant blooms.
