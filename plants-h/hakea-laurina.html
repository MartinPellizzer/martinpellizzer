Hakea laurina

Hakea laurina, commonly known as the broad-leaved hakea, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical flower spikes that range in color from pink to red, which appear in spring. The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It is well adapted to dry environments and can thrive in sandy or gravelly soils.

Hakea laurina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
