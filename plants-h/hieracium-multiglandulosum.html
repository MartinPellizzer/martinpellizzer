Hieracium multiglandulosum

Hieracium multiglandulosum, commonly known as the many-glandular hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy, often reddish stems. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along streambanks, preferring temperate climates. It has alternate, heart-shaped leaves with numerous small glands, which contribute to its common name.

Hieracium multiglandulosum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain regions.
