Hieracium pilosella subsp. velutinum

Hieracium pilosella subsp.

velutinum, commonly known as the downy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.

The plant features a basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flower heads that bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its inflorescence consists of disc flowers surrounded by ray florets, and it is characterized by its soft, velvety foliage.

This subspecies is often found in moist or calcareous soils and is known for its ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
