Hakea lavandulacea

Hakea lavandulacea, commonly known as the lavender hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a silvery appearance. The plant produces spikes of small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It is well adapted to arid conditions, with deep roots that help it survive in dry environments.

Hakea lavandulacea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
