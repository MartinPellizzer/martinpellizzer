Hieracium laevigatum subsp. recognitum

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

recognitum, commonly known as the smooth hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly lobed.

This subspecies is noted for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in disturbed habitats.
