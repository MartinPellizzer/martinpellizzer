Hieracium × copulatum

Hieracium × copulatum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows to a height of 20–60 cm, featuring basal and alternate leaves that are rough and hairy. Its flowers are bright yellow, arranged in a flat-topped cluster, and bloom from late spring to early autumn.

It is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and is considered an invasive species in some regions.
