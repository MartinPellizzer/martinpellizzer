Hieracium lamprophylloides

Hieracium lamprophylloides, commonly known as the silverleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and leaf structures. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, and its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a silvery or grayish underside, giving it its common name. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and other open habitats.

Hieracium lamprophylloides is often considered a weed due to its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
