Hippeastrum andersonii

Hippeastrum andersonii, commonly known as the Anderson's amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in various colors such as red, pink, and white. The plant produces large, strap-like leaves that are usually evergreen, and it thrives in well-drained soil with moderate sunlight.

Hippeastrum andersonii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a home setting.
