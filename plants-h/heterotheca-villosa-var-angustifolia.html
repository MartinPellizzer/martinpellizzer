Heterotheca villosa var. angustifolia

Heterotheca villosa var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, blue to purple composite flowers arranged in clusters, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Its hairy foliage and showy blooms make it a notable species in its native range.
