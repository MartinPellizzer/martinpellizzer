Haenelia coelopodia

Haenelia coelopodia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, adapting to salty and windy coastal environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dunes or rocky shores.

Haenelia coelopodia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal habitats.
