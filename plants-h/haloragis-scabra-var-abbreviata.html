Haloragis scabra var. abbreviata

Haloragis scabra var. abbreviata, commonly known as the rough-hairless willow herb, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems covered in short, glandular hairs. The leaves are narrow, linear, and often have a rough texture, contributing to its common name. It produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in spring.

This species is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal or inland regions with low rainfall.
