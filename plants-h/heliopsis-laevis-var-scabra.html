Heliopsis laevis var. scabra

Heliopsis laevis var. scabra, commonly known as rough heliopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb with upright stems that produce bright yellow, daisy-like flowers. The plant features hairy, rough-textured leaves that are typically lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive and hardy addition to native plant gardens.
