Herrea grandis

Herrea grandis, commonly known as the wild fig tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of the Americas and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, fig-like fruits. The plant can grow up to 20 meters tall, featuring a straight trunk and a broad, spreading canopy. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, and the fruit is typically green when mature, turning yellow or red.

The tree is valued for its ecological role and has been used traditionally for its edible fruits and medicinal properties.
