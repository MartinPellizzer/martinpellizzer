Hakea ivoryi var. glabrescens

Hakea ivoryi var. glabrescens, commonly known as the silky hakea, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a soft, silvery appearance. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. It is adapted to arid and coastal environments, showcasing drought tolerance and salt resistance.

The leaves are narrow and linear, often with a leathery texture, contributing to its overall hardy nature.
