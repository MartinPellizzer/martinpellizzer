Helianthemum pollenis

Helianthemum pollenis, commonly known as the Scottish heather, is a low-growing shrub native to the Scottish Highlands.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh, rocky habitat. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in open moorland and heathland environments.

Helianthemum pollenis is an important component of the local flora, supporting a variety of pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.
