Helleborus viridis var. cupreus

Helleborus viridis var. cupreus, commonly known as the copperleaf hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features dark green, waxy leaves with a distinctive copper-colored border, which gives it its common name. The plant produces upright, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

This variety is known for its ornamental value and ability to bloom throughout late winter and early spring.
