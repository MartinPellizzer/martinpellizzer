Hibiscus rosa-sinensis f. schizopetalus

Hibiscus rosa-sinensis f. schizopetalus, commonly known as the Chinese hibiscus or split-petal hibiscus, is a unique cultivar characterized by its distinctive floral morphology.

The plant is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia, featuring large, showy flowers with a striking contrast between the outer and inner petals. Its foliage consists of dark green, ovate leaves that are glossy and provide a lush backdrop to the vibrant blooms. The flowers typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white, with the inner petals often being a different color from the outer ones.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates for its aesthetic beauty.
