Harrisia perviridis

Harrisia perviridis, commonly known as the green harrisia, is a species of cactus native to Central America and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in sharp, spiny areoles. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the evening, typically during the warmer months. Its fruit is a small, green berry that is edible and has a mild, sweet flavor.

Harrisia perviridis is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
