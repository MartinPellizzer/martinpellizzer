Hindsia sessilifolia

Hindsia sessilifolia, commonly known as the sessile-leaf hindsia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its small, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often sessile, meaning they lack stalks and are directly attached to the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
