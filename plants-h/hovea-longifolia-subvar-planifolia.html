Hovea longifolia subvar. planifolia

Hovea longifolia subvar. planifolia, commonly known as the flat-leaf hovea, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate, and often elliptical leaves that are typically flat and smooth. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late winter to early spring. Its growth habit is usually compact, making it suitable for small gardens or as a ornamental plant.

The species is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
