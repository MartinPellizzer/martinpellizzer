Hylocereus extensus

Hylocereus extensus, commonly known as the dragon fruit vine, is a climbing cactus belonging to the Cactaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and Central America, characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and distinctive spiny leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers at night, which are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit, known as dragon fruit, is edible, sweet, and has a mild flavor with a crunchy texture.

The plant is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
