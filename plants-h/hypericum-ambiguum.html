Hypericum ambiguum

Hypericum ambiguum, commonly known as the ambiguous St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring woody stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late summer. Its botanical features include small, elliptical leaves and the presence of yellow-orange glands on the stems and leaves.

Hypericum ambiguum is often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial to full shade.
