Hyssopus cuspidatus

Hyssopus cuspidatus, commonly known as spiked hyssop, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple, arranged in spikes at the ends of the branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Spiked hyssop is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
