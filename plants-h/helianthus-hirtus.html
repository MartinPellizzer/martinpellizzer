Helianthus hirtus

Helianthus hirtus, commonly known as the rough sunflower, is a species of flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces yellow flowers with a dark center, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Helianthus hirtus is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
