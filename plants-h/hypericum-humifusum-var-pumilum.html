Hypericum humifusum var. pumilum

Hypericum humifusum var. pumilum, commonly known as dwarf St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It features small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has slender, woody stems that often lie close to the ground, forming a mat-like growth habit. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly rough texture.

This variety is well adapted to rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or along streambanks.
