Hippocratea indica f. longepetiolata

Hippocratea indica f. longepetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled Indian hippocratea, is a tropical plant characterized by its elongated petioles that give it a distinctive appearance.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand and Malaysia. The plant features compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale yellow or greenish in color.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.
