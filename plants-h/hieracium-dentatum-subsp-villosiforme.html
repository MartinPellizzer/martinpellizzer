Hieracium dentatum subsp. villosiforme

Hieracium dentatum subsp.

villosiforme, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dense, woolly pubescence, particularly on the stems and leaves. It typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, thriving in temperate climates.

The plant is known for its ability to form dense colonies, often outcompeting native vegetation in certain habitats.
