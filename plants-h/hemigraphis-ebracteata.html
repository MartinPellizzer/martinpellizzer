Hemigraphis ebracteata

Hemigraphis ebracteata, commonly known as the silver millepora, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety silver-green foliage with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used in ornamental horticulture.

Its unique appearance and easy care make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
