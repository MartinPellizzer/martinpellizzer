Hedyotis laxiflora

Hedyotis laxiflora, commonly known as the slender wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to regions of Asia, including China, India, and Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial with slender, creeping stems that often form dense mats. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers with five petals, typically arranged in clusters along the stems. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly rough texture.

Hedyotis laxiflora is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.
