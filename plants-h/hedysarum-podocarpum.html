Hedysarum podocarpum

Hedysarum podocarpum, commonly known as the purple vetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive purple to pink flowers. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are typically dispersed by wind. Hedysarum podocarpum is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a notable species in botanical studies.
