Hesperis carpatica

Hesperis carpatica, commonly known as the Carpathian honesty, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many cruciferous plants. The plant produces small, white flowers with four petals, and its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and meadows.

Hesperis carpatica is known for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
