Heteropterys purpurea var. antillana

Heteropterys purpurea var. antillana, commonly known as the Antillean purpleleaf tree, is a species native to the Caribbean region.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and showy, clustered flowers that range from white to pink. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by animals.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and disturbed sites.
