Haplopappus linearifolius subsp. interior

Haplopappus linearifolius subsp.

interior, commonly known as the interior linearleaf hawksbeard, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a perennial herb with a low-growing, mat-forming habit, characterized by its linear, often hairy leaves.

The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers arranged in a single head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in dry, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert scrub habitats.
