Hippeastrum marginatum

Hippeastrum marginatum, commonly known as the Orange Flame Amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of orange and red, often with intricate patterns on the petals. The plant produces strap-like, narrow leaves that are usually a deep green color. Its bulbs are perennial, capable of producing multiple flower stalks each growing season.

Hippeastrum marginatum thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
