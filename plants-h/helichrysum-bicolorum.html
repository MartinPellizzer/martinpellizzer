Helichrysum bicolorum

Helichrysum bicolorum, commonly known as the two-colored everlasting flower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow and orange flowers that remain intact for a long period, giving it the common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered with fine hairs.

Helichrysum bicolorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in dried floral arrangements.
