Hypericum × elatum

Hypericum × elatum, commonly known as the hybrid St. John's wort, is a flowering plant in the family Hypericaceae.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Hypericum perforatum (common St. John's wort) and Hypericum patulum (dwarf St. John's wort). The plant is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and opposite, oval-shaped leaves. It typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and showy blooms.
