Hexachlamys sehnemiana

Hexachlamys sehnemiana, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring six sepals and petals that are often brightly colored, with a distinctive hood-like lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are pollinated primarily by specific species of bees and wasps.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts to protect its dwindling population.
