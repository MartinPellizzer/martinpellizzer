Hydrangea macrophylla subsp. serrata

Hydrangea macrophylla subsp.

serrata, commonly known as the serrata hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan. It is characterized by its large, showy bract-like flowers that come in shades of blue, pink, and white, depending on soil pH.

The plant features broad, serrated leaves that are typically green during the growing season. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

The subspecies is known for its compact growth habit and ability to bloom repeatedly throughout the summer.
