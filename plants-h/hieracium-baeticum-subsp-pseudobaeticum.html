Hieracium baeticum subsp. pseudobaeticum

Hieracium baeticum subsp.

pseudobaeticum, commonly known as the Baetican hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has finely divided leaves and produces small, inconspicuous florets arranged in a composite head.

This subspecies is distinguished by its subtle morphological differences from the main species, making it a subject of taxonomic interest.
