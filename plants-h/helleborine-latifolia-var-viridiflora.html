Helleborine latifolia var. viridiflora

Helleborine latifolia var. viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often dark green in color. The plant produces striking greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive three-lobed lip, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
