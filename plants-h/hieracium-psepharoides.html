Hieracium psepharoides

Hieracium psepharoides, commonly known as the golden hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in grasslands and open woodlands. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Hieracium psepharoides is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and ability to spread easily.
