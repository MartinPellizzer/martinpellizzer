Hesperozygis dimidiata

Hesperozygis dimidiata, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a deep taproot system, allowing it to survive in arid and rocky environments. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Hesperozygis dimidiata is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.
