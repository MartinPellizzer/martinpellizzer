Huperzia brachiata

Huperzia brachiata, commonly known as the creeping huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its creeping growth habit and its ability to form dense mats on the forest floor. The plant has simple, linear leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as temperate forests and mountainous regions.

Huperzia brachiata plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and small invertebrates.
