Helictotrichon jingpoense

Helictotrichon jingpoense, commonly known as the Jingpo hairgrass, is a flowering plant species belonging to the grass family, Poaceae.

It is native to China, specifically found in the Yunnan and Sichuan provinces, where it thrives in mountainous regions. The plant is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
