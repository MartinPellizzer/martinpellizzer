Hieracium czeremoszense

Hieracium czeremoszense, commonly known as the Czeremoszne hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, preferring moist and well-drained soils. Its flowers are yellow, with a distinctive central disc and surrounding ray florets.

Hieracium czeremoszense is noted for its ecological significance, supporting various pollinators and contributing to biodiversity in its habitat.
