Hieracium cottetii subsp. brachycaulodes

Hieracium cottetii subsp.

brachycaulodes, commonly known as the short-stemmed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

The plant has a compact, low-growing form with basal leaves that are often hairy and deeply lobed. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a single head, and are surrounded by bracts.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
