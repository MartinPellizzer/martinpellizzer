Heterotheca cinerascens

Heterotheca cinerascens, commonly known as the grayleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are gray-green and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

Heterotheca cinerascens produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer months.
