Hieracium umbrosum subsp. umbrosum

Hieracium umbrosum subsp.

umbrosum, commonly known as the shadowy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and meadows.

The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it produces yellow flowers in early summer. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

Due to its preference for specific habitats, it is often found in areas with rich, loamy soil and partial to full shade.
