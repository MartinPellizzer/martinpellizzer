Hieracium trunciceps

Hieracium trunciceps, commonly known as the short-headed hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 50 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium trunciceps is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in various habitats.
