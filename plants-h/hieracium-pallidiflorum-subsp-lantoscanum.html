Hieracium pallidiflorum subsp. lantoscanum

Hieracium pallidiflorum subsp.

lantoscanum, commonly known as the pale-flowered hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features a rosette of basal leaves that are typically gray-green in color with hairy surfaces.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and is often found in rocky or disturbed soils.

This subspecies is notable for its limited distribution and is considered a rare plant in its native habitat.
