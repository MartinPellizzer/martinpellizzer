Hemizonia luzulifolia var. citrina

Hemizonia luzulifolia var. citrina, commonly known as the yellow-flowered hemizonia, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to native plant gardens.
