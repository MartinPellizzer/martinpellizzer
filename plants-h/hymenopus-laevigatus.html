Hymenopus laevigatus

Hymenopus laevigatus, commonly known as the smooth hymenophylla, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically green and covered in a waxy cuticle. This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are usually small and closely spaced, forming dense, mat-like structures on the forest floor.

Hymenopus laevigatus plays a role in maintaining soil moisture and providing microhabitats for various small organisms.
