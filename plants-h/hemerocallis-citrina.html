Hemerocallis citrina

Hemerocallis citrina, commonly known as the lemon-yellow daylily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of yellow to orange. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 feet and produces numerous spikes of blooms throughout the summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming dense clumps that help it thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Hemerocallis citrina is a hardy plant that is well-suited for gardens and landscaping due to its resilience and ornamental value.
