Hecatactis radicans

Hecatactis radicans, commonly known as the creeping hecatactis, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread along the ground and form dense mats. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale in color, often clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface.

Hecatactis radicans is adapted to grow in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or along stream banks.
