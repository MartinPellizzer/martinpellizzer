Hypericum × laschii nothof. froelichii

Hypericum × laschii nothof.

froelichii, commonly known as the hybrid St. John's wort, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States. It features small, yellow floral clusters that bloom in late spring to early summer, typical of the Hypericaceae family.

The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often reddish in color. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
