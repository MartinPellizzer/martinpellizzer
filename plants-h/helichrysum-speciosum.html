Helichrysum speciosum

Helichrysum speciosum, commonly known as the golden ragwort, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, silver-gray leaves that are covered in tiny, hair-like structures, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Helichrysum speciosum is also known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
