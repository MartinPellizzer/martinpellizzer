Haplocoelum congolanum

Haplocoelum congolanum, commonly known as the Congo starfish plant, is a unique member of the Cyclanthaceae family, native to Central Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a distinctive blue or violet hue. The plant has a short, succulent stem and produces small, fleshy leaves that are adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and it thrives in well-drained soil with minimal water.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
