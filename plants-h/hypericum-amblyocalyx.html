Hypericum amblyocalyx

Hypericum amblyocalyx, commonly known as the narrow-leaved St. John's wort, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of its branches. The leaves are narrow and opposite, with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This plant is part of the Hypericaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
