Hieracium basifolium

Hieracium basifolium, commonly known as the basal-leaved hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble single flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette, and are typically lance-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators.
