Hieracium murinum

Hieracium murinum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Hieracium murinum is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in various habitats.
