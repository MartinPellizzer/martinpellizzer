Helianthus prostratus

Helianthus prostratus, commonly known as the prostrate sunflower, is a low-growing annual plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers with ray florets that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows on sandy or disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats such as fields and roadsides. Its botanical characteristics include a sprawling growth habit and a short, fibrous root system.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
