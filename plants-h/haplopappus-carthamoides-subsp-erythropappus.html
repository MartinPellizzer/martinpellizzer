Haplopappus carthamoides subsp. erythropappus

Haplopappus carthamoides subsp.

erythropappus, commonly known as the red pappus fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers arranged in dense, flat clusters. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

The subspecies is distinguished by its red-colored pappus, which aids in seed dispersal.
