Hieracium laevigatum subsp. perangustum

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

perangustum, commonly known as narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, yellowish flowers.

The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, with a basal rosette formation. It thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found in meadows, roadsides, and wastelands.

This subspecies is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is considered a weed in some agricultural settings.
