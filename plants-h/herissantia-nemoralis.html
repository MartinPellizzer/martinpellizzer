Herissantia nemoralis

Herissantia nemoralis, commonly known as the woodland thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage and produces purple to pinkish flowers in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges and a woolly texture.

Herissantia nemoralis plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in naturalized areas.
