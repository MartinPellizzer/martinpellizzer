Helianthemum aldersonii

Helianthemum aldersonii, commonly known as the Alderson's rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features small, fleshy leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry habitats.

Helianthemum aldersonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
