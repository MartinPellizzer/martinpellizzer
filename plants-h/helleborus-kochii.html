Helleborus kochii

Helleborus kochii, commonly known as the Armenian hellebore, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Turkey.

It features dark green, leathery leaves that are typically lobed or toothed, and it produces striking, waxy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant is known for its toxic properties, containing various alkaloids that can be harmful if ingested. It prefers cool, moist, and shady environments, often growing in woodlands or rocky soils.

Helleborus kochii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
