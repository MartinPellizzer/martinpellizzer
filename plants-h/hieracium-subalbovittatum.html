Hieracium subalbovittatum

Hieracium subalbovittatum, commonly known as the white-banded hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellowish to orange-yellow flowers, which are typically arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are usually lance-shaped with serrated edges. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in meadows and grassy areas.

It is often found in moist or sandy soils and can be invasive in certain regions.
