Helianthus heteropappus

Helianthus heteropappus, commonly known as the heteromorphic sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its yellow disk flowers surrounded by ray flowers that can vary in color, often appearing green or yellow. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are generally hairy and lance-shaped, with a rough texture.

Helianthus heteropappus is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies due to its showy flowers.
