Hippeastrum puniceum subsp. quentinianum

Hippeastrum puniceum subsp.

quentinianum, commonly known as the Quentinian red amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America. It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers with white markings, which bloom in late winter to early spring.

The plant produces strap-shaped, dark green leaves that emerge from a fleshy, underground bulb. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

This subspecies is popular among gardeners for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
