Hordeum jubatum var. caespitosum

Hordeum jubatum var. caespitosum, commonly known as the silver hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and silver-blue foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, meadows, and stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often loosely arranged in open panicles.

Hordeum jubatum var. caespitosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
