Hyssopus torresii

Hyssopus torresii, commonly known as Mexican hyssop, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Hyssopus torresii thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

It is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
