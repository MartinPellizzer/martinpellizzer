Hieracium glaucum subsp. tenerum

Hieracium glaucum subsp.

tenerum, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its dense, soft, and woolly foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in dry meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Asteraceae family.
