Hyparrhenia rufa var. rufa

Hyparrhenia rufa var. rufa, commonly known as red hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Hyparrhenia rufa var. rufa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
