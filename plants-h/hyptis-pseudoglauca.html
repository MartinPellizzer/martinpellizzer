Hyptis pseudoglauca

Hyptis pseudoglauca, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the Americas, particularly found in regions of Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.
