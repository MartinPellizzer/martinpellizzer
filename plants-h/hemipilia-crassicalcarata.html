Hemipilia crassicalcarata

Hemipilia crassicalcarata, commonly known as the broad-spurred orchid, is a herbaceous perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers with broad, spurred petals that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Hemipilia crassicalcarata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
