Hieracium bourgaei subsp. trichodermum

Hieracium bourgaei subsp.

trichodermum, commonly known as the trichodermum hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering heads composed of yellow ray florets.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in grasslands or along stream banks. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its historical use in traditional medicine.
