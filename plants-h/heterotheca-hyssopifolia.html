Heterotheca hyssopifolia

Heterotheca hyssopifolia, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces blue to purple ray flowers, which are showy and attract pollinators. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Heterotheca hyssopifolia is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.
