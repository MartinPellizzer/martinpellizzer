Hieracium cantabrimontanum

Hieracium cantabrimontanum, commonly known as the Cantabrian hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and ragworts, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in temperate climates. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its region.
