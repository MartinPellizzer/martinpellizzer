Hedera coriacea

Hedera coriacea, commonly known as the rough-leaved ivy, is a species of climbing plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are covered in a fine, granular texture, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by red, berry-like fruits. Hedera coriacea is often found growing on walls, trees, and rocky surfaces, where it can climb and spread rapidly.

Its ability to thrive in shaded environments makes it a common ornamental plant in gardens and landscaping.
