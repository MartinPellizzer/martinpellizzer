Helianthus annuus var. primulinus

Helianthus annuus var. primulinus, commonly known as the golden beard sunflower, is a variant of the common sunflower characterized by its unique, densely packed yellow flower heads.

This plant typically grows in open, sunny areas and is native to the eastern and central United States. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture and often have a bluish-gray hue. The flowers bloom in late summer, producing a showy, sun-like appearance that attracts pollinators.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
