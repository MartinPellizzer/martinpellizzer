Hypericum lackeyi

Hypericum lackeyi, commonly known as the Texas goldenstrob.

It is a member of the Hypericaceae family and is native to the southern United States, particularly Texas. This shrub typically grows in open woodlands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its most distinctive feature is its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is also noted for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses in herbal remedies.
