Halanthium abichii

Halanthium abichii, commonly known as the redseed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, reddish-brown seeds in clusters. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous growth habit and a preference for temperate climates.

Halanthium abichii plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
