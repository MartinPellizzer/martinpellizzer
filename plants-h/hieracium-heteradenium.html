Hieracium heteradenium

Hieracium heteradenium, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy stems. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its bright yellow flowers, which appear in late spring to early summer, attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
