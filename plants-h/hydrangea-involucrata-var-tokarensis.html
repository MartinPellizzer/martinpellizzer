Hydrangea involucrata var. tokarensis

Hydrangea involucrata var. tokarensis, commonly known as the Tokarensis hydrangea, is a rare and striking plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that typically range from white to pink, depending on soil pH. The plant features broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its limited distribution and unique appearance, it is highly valued in horticulture and conservation efforts.
