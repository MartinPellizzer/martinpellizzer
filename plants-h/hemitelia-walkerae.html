Hemitelia walkerae

Hemitelia walkerae, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southeastern Australia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough, hairy texture.

Hemitelia walkerae is adapted to dry, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and rocky areas.
