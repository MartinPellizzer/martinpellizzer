Halothamnus turcomanicus

Halothamnus turcomanicus, commonly known as the Turkestan sandthorn, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in desert or steppe environments. The plant produces small yellow flowers and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited water availability. Its leaves are usually small and covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Halothamnus turcomanicus plays a role in stabilizing sand dunes and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
