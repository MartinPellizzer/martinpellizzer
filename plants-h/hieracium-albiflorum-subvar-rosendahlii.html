Hieracium albiflorum subvar. rosendahlii

Hieracium albiflorum subvar. rosendahlii, commonly known as the Rosendahl's meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe.

It typically grows in meadows and grassy areas, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features upright stems covered in fine, hairy pubescence, and its flowers are white to pale yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly rough texture.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
