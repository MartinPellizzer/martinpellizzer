Hieracium × beylii

Hieracium × beylii, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a hybrid plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange composite flowers, which are typically arranged in a flat-topped or disc-like inflorescence. The plant has basal and alternate leaves that are often heart-shaped or lanceolate, with a rough, hairy texture. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is commonly found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides.

Hieracium × beylii is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
