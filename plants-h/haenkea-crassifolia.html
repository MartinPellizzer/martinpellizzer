Haenkea crassifolia

Haenkea crassifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf hawksbeard, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas. The plant features basal leaves that are broad and heart-shaped, with a rough texture. Its yellow composite flowers appear in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Haenkea crassifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
