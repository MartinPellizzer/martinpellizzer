Heliotropium floridum var. latifolium

Heliotropium floridum var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and wetlands. The plant features showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in clusters at the ends of its branches. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

It is known for its ability to thrive in wet environments and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
