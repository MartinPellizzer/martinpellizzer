Hedyotis gentianifolia

Hedyotis gentianifolia, commonly known as blue false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It features small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Hedyotis gentianifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
