Hieracium brachycomum subsp. armigerum

Hieracium brachycomum subsp.

armigerum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and meadows, and can form dense colonies. It has a hairy stem and leaves that are often lobed or serrated.

The subspecies armigerum is distinguished by its shorter stature and more compact growth habit compared to other members of the species.
