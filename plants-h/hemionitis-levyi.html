Hemionitis levyi

Hemionitis levyi, commonly known as the silver grass, is a perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, silver-gray foliage and slender, arching stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Hemionitis levyi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded garden environments.
