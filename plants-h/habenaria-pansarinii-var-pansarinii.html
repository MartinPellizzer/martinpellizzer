Habenaria pansarinii var. pansarinii

Habenaria pansarinii var. pansarinii, commonly known as the purple-tongued orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a rhizome and produces a single, showy spike of flowers. The flowers are typically violet to purple in color, with a distinctive hooded labellum that gives it its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, organic soil.

This species is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
