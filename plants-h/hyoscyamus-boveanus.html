Hyoscyamus boveanus

Hyoscyamus boveanus, commonly known as the Bovean thornapple, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or arid environments. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by spherical, spiny fruits. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often woody and branched.

Hyoscyamus boveanus contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.
