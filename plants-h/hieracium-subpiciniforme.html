Hieracium subpiciniforme

Hieracium subpiciniforme, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Hieracium subpiciniforme is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
