Hieracium cephalotes subsp. tricholepidum

Hieracium cephalotes subsp.

tricholepidum, commonly known as the hairy-headed hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has hairy leaves and stems, which contribute to its common name. It typically grows in grassy areas and disturbed soils across Europe and parts of Asia.

This subspecies is noted for its distinctive leaf morphology and is often found in open habitats.
