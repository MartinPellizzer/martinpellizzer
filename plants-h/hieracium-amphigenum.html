Hieracium amphigenum

Hieracium amphigenum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are hairy and lance-shaped, while its flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer.

Hieracium amphigenum is often found in meadows and slopes at high altitudes, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
