Hieracium lanigerum

Hieracium lanigerum, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy, grayish-green leaves. The plant produces yellow daisy-like flowers with a central disk surrounded by ray florets. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and woodland edges.

Hieracium lanigerum is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.
