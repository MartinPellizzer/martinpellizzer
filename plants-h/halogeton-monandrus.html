Halogeton monandrus

Halogeton monandrus, commonly known as the desert star, is a halophyte native to arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It possesses succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in extreme drought conditions. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are adapted for wind pollination. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep groundwater sources.

Halogeton monandrus is also notable for its ability to survive in saline soils, making it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
