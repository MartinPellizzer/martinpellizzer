Hypericum afzelii

Hypericum afzelii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are a food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

Hypericum afzelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
