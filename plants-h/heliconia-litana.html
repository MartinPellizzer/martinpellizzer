Heliconia litana

Heliconia litana, commonly known as the Litana heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its large, brightly colored bracts that resemble petals. The plant produces long, narrow inflorescences with numerous small, clustered flowers. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

Heliconia litana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
