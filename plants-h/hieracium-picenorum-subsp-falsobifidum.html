Hieracium picenorum subsp. falsobifidum

Hieracium picenorum subsp.

falsobifidum, commonly known as the Picene hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowering heads and basal rosettes of leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a single head with ray florets.

This subspecies is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology, which differs from other members of the Hieracium genus.
