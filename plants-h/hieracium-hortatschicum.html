Hieracium hortatschicum

Hieracium hortatschicum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky soils, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium hortatschicum is often found in high-altitude habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
