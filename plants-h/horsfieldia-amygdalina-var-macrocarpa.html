Horsfieldia amygdalina var. macrocarpa

Horsfieldia amygdalina var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited orchid, is a tropical plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the rainforests of Borneo and Sumatra. The plant is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are broad and sword-shaped, and it produces large, fleshy berries that contain the seeds.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in warm, humid environments.
