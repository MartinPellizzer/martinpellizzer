Hieracium hypeuryum subsp. obtusifolium

Hieracium hypeuryum subsp.

obtusifolium, commonly known as the obtuse-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features basal rosettes of broad, spoon-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive obtuse or rounded tip. Its flowers are yellow and occur in clusters, with ray florets that are usually shorter than the central disk florets.

The species is characterized by its robust growth and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
