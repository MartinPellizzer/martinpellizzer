Haplopappus coronopifolius var. laxus

Haplopappus coronopifolius var. laxus, commonly known as the slenderleaf goatweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert or semi-arid environments. It has a low-growing habit and produces achenes with pappus hairs that aid in wind dispersal.

This species is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
