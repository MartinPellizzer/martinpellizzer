Heliotropium polyanthiodorum

Heliotropium polyanthiodorum, commonly known as the clustered heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse display of small, violet to purple flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of flowers along its stems, which bloom in succession throughout the growing season. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Heliotropium polyanthiodorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its colorful blooms.
