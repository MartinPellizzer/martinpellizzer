Hyptis marginata

Hyptis marginata, commonly known as lemon grass or sweet basil, is a perennial herb native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves that emit a citrusy scent when crushed. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Hyptis marginata is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb due to its medicinal and aromatic properties.
