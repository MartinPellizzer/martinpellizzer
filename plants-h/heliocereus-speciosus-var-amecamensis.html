Heliocereus speciosus var. amecamensis

Heliocereus speciosus var. amecamensis, commonly known as the Amecamense night-blooming cereus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and produces large, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant is a member of the Cactaceae family and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and they bloom briefly before closing in the morning.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its striking nocturnal blooms.
