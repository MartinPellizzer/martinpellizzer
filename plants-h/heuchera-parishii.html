Heuchera parishii

Heuchera parishii, commonly known as the Parry heuchera, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, hairy leaves that vary in color from green to reddish hues. The plant produces upright spikes of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or red during the summer months. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Heuchera parishii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and floral display.
