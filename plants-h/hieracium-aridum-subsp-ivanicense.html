Hieracium aridum subsp. ivanicense

Hieracium aridum subsp.

ivanicense, commonly known as the Ivan's sandweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer.
