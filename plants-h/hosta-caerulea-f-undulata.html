Hosta caerulea f. undulata

Hosta caerulea f. undulata, commonly known as the wavy-leaved blue hosta, is a cultivar renowned for its striking, undulating leaf patterns.

The leaves are typically a vibrant blue-green color, with wavy margins that add a unique visual texture. It is a hardy perennial that thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. This plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in late summer, though it is primarily grown for its ornamental foliage.

It is well-suited for woodland gardens and can be used as a ground cover or in mixed borders.
