Hydrangea rosthornii

Hydrangea rosthornii, commonly known as the mountain hydrangea, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features large, rounded leaves that are often deeply lobed, and it produces showy inflorescences of white or pink flowers in the summer. Its botanical characteristics include a preference for acidic soils and partial shade, making it well-suited for woodland gardens.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in cooler, moist environments.
