Hyptis hypoglauca

Hyptis hypoglauca, commonly known as silverleaf hyptis, is a perennial herb native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its small, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

Hyptis hypoglauca is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
