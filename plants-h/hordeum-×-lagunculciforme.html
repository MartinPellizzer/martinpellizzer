Hordeum × lagunculciforme

Hordeum × lagunculciforme, commonly known as intermediate barley, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Hordeum vulgare (common barley) and Hordeum lejorensis (a wild barley species).

It is characterized by its intermediate morphological traits, such as intermediate spikelet structure and growth habits. This hybrid is often found in regions where the parent species co-occur, particularly in temperate climates. Its botanical significance lies in its role as a genetic bridge between cultivated and wild barley species.

Hordeum × lagunculciforme is of interest to plant breeders and researchers studying genetic diversity and evolution in the Hordeum genus.
