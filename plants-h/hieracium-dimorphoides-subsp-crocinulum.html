Hieracium dimorphoides subsp. crocinulum

Hieracium dimorphoides subsp.

crocinulum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its yellow flowers bloom in the summer months, and the leaves are usually lance-shaped with a rough texture.

It is a perennial species that can form dense colonies, often spreading through rhizomes or seed dispersal.
