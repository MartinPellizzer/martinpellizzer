Hieracium divisum subsp. fictum

Hieracium divisum subsp.

fictum, commonly known as the false divided hawkweed, is a member of the Asteraceae family. It is a perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering stems that bear yellowish flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils across Europe and parts of Asia. Its leaves are deeply lobed and often have a hairy surface, while the flowers are arranged in composite heads.

It is notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
