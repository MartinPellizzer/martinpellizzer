Hedysarum sikkimense var. megalanthum

Hedysarum sikkimense var. megalanthum, commonly known as the Sikkim milkvetch, is a perennial leguminous plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
