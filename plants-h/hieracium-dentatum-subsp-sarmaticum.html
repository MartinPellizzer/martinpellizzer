Hieracium dentatum subsp. sarmaticum

Hieracium dentatum subsp.

sarmaticum, commonly known as the Sarmatian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and flowering stems that bear yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas. It has a fibrous root system and produces numerous seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly toothed leaves and a more compact growth habit compared to other members of the species.
