Hydrothrix barrosoana

Hydrothrix barrosoana, commonly known as the Barroso's water thorn, is a perennial aquatic plant native to South America.

It typically grows in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes. The plant has slender, branching stems that are often submerged, with small, lance-shaped leaves that are usually green in color. Its flowers are pale yellow and bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

Hydrothrix barrosoana plays a role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen to aquatic life.
