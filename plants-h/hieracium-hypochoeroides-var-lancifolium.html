Hieracium hypochoeroides var. lancifolium

Hieracium hypochoeroides var. lancifolium, commonly known as the lance-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a hairy texture, and the flowers are yellow with ray florets.

It is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and ability to spread easily.
