Hygrophila salicifolia var. longihirsuta

Hygrophila salicifolia var. longihirsuta, commonly known as the long-hairy hygrophila, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. The plant features slender, ribbon-like leaves that are covered in fine, hairy pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically producing pale yellow to brownish blooms.

This variant is often cultivated in water gardens for its ornamental value and ability to provide habitat for aquatic life.
