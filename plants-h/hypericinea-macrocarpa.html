Hypericinea macrocarpa

Hypericinea macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited St. John's wort.

It is a shrub native to parts of Australia, characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. Hypericinea macrocarpa is often found in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and heathlands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
