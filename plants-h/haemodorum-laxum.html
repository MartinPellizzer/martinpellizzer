Haemodorum laxum

Haemodorum laxum, commonly known as the slender false ginger, is a perennial herb native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the family Haemodoraceae, which includes plants commonly referred to as the "red gums.". The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces striking red or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rainforests or along stream banks.

Haemodorum laxum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
