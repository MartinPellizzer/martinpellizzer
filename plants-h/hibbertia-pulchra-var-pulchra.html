Hibbertia pulchra var. pulchra

Hibbertia pulchra var. pulchra, commonly known as the pretty hibbertia, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features bright yellow flowers with distinctive red or orange tubular corolla, typically blooming in spring. The plant has slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Hibbertia pulchra var. pulchra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
