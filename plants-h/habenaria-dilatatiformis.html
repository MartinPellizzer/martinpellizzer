Habenaria dilatatiformis

Habenaria dilatatiformis, commonly known as the broad-leafed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

This plant is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and green. The leaves are broad and strap-like, often arranged in a basal rosette, and the plant produces elongated spikes bearing numerous small, clustered flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
