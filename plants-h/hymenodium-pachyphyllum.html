Hymenodium pachyphyllum

Hymenodium pachyphyllum, commonly known as the thick-leaved rock fig, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and succulent collections.
