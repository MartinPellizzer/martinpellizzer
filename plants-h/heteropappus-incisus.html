Heteropappus incisus

Heteropappus incisus, commonly known as the cutleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive foliage. The plant features basal and alternate leaves that are often deeply lobed or cut, giving it its common name. It produces yellow flowers that are surrounded by pale green involucral bracts, and it is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Heteropappus incisus is typically short-lived and has a mat-forming growth habit.
