Hieracium halense

Hieracium halense, commonly known as the marsh hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in marshes, meadows, and along waterways. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium halense is known for its ability to thrive in wet conditions, making it an important component of aquatic and semi-aquatic ecosystems.
