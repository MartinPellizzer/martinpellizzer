Haplopappus obovatus

Haplopappus obovatus, commonly known as the broad-leafed hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a low, mat-forming growth habit. Its leaves are broadly ovate and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in dense clusters.

Haplopappus obovatus is known for its ability to thrive in arid environments and is often found in open grasslands and sagebrush steppe.
