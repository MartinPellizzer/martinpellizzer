Hordeum bogdanii

Hordeum bogdanii, commonly known as the Siberian barley, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences, which are typical of the Poaceae family. Hordeum bogdanii is adapted to harsh environments, including high altitudes and cold climates.

It has been studied for its potential in ecological restoration and as a forage crop.
