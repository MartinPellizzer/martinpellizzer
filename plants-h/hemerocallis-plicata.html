Hemerocallis plicata

Hemerocallis plicata, commonly known as the Chinese daylily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, sword-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The flowers are typically yellow or orange with a distinctive recurved petal shape. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

This plant is known for its hardiness and ability to naturalize, often forming clumps over time.
