Hermione autumnalis

Hermione autumnalis, commonly known as the autumn bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to purple, blooming in the late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often found in rocky or alpine environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its vibrant fall blooms.
