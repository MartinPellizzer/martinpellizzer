Hippocrepis glauca f. hieronymi

Hippocrepis glauca f. hieronymi, commonly known as the Hieronymi's bluebell, is a botanical variant of the glaucous orchid.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers and lanceolate leaves with a waxy, glaucous coating. This subspecies is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe, particularly in the Iberian Peninsula. It thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in open woodlands and rocky slopes.

The plant is notable for its ornamental value and is appreciated by horticulturists for its unique coloration and floral structure.
