Hookera congesta

Hookera congesta, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to the ecological balance of its habitat.

Hookera congesta is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
