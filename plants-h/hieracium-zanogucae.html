Hieracium zanogucae

Hieracium zanogucae, commonly known as the Zanoguca hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow, with a central disc surrounded by ray florets.

Hieracium zanogucae is often found in meadows and open slopes at high altitudes, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.
