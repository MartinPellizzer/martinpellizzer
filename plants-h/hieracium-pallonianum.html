Hieracium pallonianum

Hieracium pallonianum, commonly known as the Pallonian hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and ragworts, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is considered a short-lived perennial, often forming dense colonies in suitable habitats.
