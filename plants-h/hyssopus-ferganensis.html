Hyssopus ferganensis

Hyssopus ferganensis, commonly known as the Fergana hyssop, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous regions and thrives in temperate climates. Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or blue, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hyssopus ferganensis is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
