Huperzia binervia

Huperzia binervia, commonly known as the forked liverwort, is a species of moss-like plant belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its simple, erect shoots and small, elongated leaves that often have two distinct nerve lines. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in temperate regions across the Northern Hemisphere. Huperzia binervia reproduces via spores rather than seeds, and its life cycle includes both a gametophyte and sporophyte stage.

The plant is often used in ecological studies due to its sensitivity to environmental changes.
