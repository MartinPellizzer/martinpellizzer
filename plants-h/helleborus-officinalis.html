Helleborus officinalis

Helleborus officinalis, commonly known as the white hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen, leathery leaves and striking, bell-shaped flowers that range from white to pale green. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and thrives in partial shade with well-drained soil. It is known for its toxic properties, containing compounds such as cardiac glycosides, which have historically been used in medicine.

Helleborus officinalis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and early spring blooms.
