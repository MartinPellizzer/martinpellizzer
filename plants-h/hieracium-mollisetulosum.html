Hieracium mollisetulosum

Hieracium mollisetulosum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant is distinguished by its finely hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.

Hieracium mollisetulosum produces yellowish to reddish flower heads that bloom in late spring to early summer.
