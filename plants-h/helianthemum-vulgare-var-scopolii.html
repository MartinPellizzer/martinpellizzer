Helianthemum vulgare var. scopolii

Helianthemum vulgare var. scopolii, commonly known as the Scotch thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub with silver-gray foliage and produces bright yellow flowers in summer. The plant has a hairy stem and leaves that are typically lobed or toothed. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

The species is known for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other parts of the world.
