Hieracium bifidum subsp. oblongatum

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

oblongatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while the flowers are yellow with ray florets.

It is noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
