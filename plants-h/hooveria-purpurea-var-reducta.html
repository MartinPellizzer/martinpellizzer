Hooveria purpurea var. reducta

Hooveria purpurea var. reducta, commonly known as the purple reducta helenium, is a striking perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, daisy-like yellow flowers with a distinctive purple center, blooming in late summer to early fall. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with red or purple hues, adding visual interest throughout the growing season. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
