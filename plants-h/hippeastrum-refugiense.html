Hippeastrum refugiense

Hippeastrum refugiense, commonly known as the refuge amaryllis, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a bulbous plant characterized by its striking, large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces strap-like leaves that are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native arid environment. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Hippeastrum refugiense is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and ease of cultivation.
