Helichrysum nicolai

Helichrysum nicolai, commonly known as the Nicolo's everlasting flower, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, golden-yellow inflorescences that resemble small, compact flowers, which remain intact even after drying, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The foliage is aromatic and can be used in potpourris or as a culinary herb.
