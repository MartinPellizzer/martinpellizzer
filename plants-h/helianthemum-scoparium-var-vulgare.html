Helianthemum scoparium var. vulgare

Helianthemum scoparium var. vulgare, commonly known as the common rock rose, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to southern Europe.

It features small, fleshy, aromatic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers in spring, which are typically tubular and arranged in compact inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for Mediterranean gardens.
