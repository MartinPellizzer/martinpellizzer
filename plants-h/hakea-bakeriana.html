Hakea bakeriana

Hakea bakeriana, commonly known as the Baker's hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical stems and striking, urn-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces woody, cone-like fruits that contain seeds covered in a fibrous layer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Hakea bakeriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
