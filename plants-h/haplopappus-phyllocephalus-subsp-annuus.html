Haplopappus phyllocephalus subsp. annuus

Haplopappus phyllocephalus subsp.

annuus, commonly known as the annual leafy pappus, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, featuring gray-green foliage with a woolly texture. Its small yellow flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and it produces achenes with pappus hairs for wind dispersal.

The species is adapted to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and sandy areas.
