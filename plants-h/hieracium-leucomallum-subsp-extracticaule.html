Hieracium leucomallum subsp. extracticaule

Hieracium leucomallum subsp.

extracticaule, commonly known as the white-haired hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant is characterized by its pale yellow to white flowers, which are clustered in dense, flat-topped inflorescences. Its leaves are usually covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
