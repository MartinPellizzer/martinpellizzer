Hesperis repanda

Hesperis repanda, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with distinct four-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive bell-shaped structure.

Hesperis repanda is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
