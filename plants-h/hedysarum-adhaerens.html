Hedysarum adhaerens

Hedysarum adhaerens, commonly known as the sticky vetch, is a perennial legume native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has small, pinnately compound leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Hedysarum adhaerens is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment and ecological restoration.
