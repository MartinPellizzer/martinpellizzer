Hieracium amplexicaule subsp. belveriense

Hieracium amplexicaule subsp.

belveriense, commonly known as the Belver's bentleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves that are clasping around the stem, giving it its species name *amplexicaule*.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the daisy family. It is native to southern Europe and is often found in grassy habitats and disturbed soils.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and localized species.
