Hyptis micrantha

Hyptis micrantha, commonly known as Mexican mint or blue hyptis, is a perennial herb native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs such as mint and basil. Hyptis micrantha is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found in shaded to partially sunny environments.
