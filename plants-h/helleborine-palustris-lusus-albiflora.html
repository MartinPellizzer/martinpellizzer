Helleborine palustris lusus albiflora

Helleborine palustris lusus albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered marsh helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear large, showy white flowers with a distinctive three-lobed lip. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as wetlands and forest understories. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy surface that aids in water retention.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
