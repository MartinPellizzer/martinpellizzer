Hyptis caespitosa var. mirabilis

Hyptis caespitosa var. mirabilis, commonly known as silverleaf hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features silver-gray foliage due to dense pubescence, and its small, tubular flowers are usually violet or purple in color. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

The plant is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
