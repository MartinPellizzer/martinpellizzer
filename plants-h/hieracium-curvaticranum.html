Hieracium curvaticranum

Hieracium curvaticranum, commonly known as the curved-crown hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and herbaceous growth habit. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is a relatively small plant, usually reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.
