Hydnophytum microphyllum

Hydnophytum microphyllum, commonly known as the small-leaved tangle vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and unique aerial roots that help it attach to host trees. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release tiny seeds when mature.

Hydnophytum microphyllum plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat, contributing to biodiversity in forest ecosystems.
