Hibiscus sinosyriacus

Hibiscus sinosyriacus, commonly known as the Chinese hibiscus or the rose of Sharon, is a deciduous shrub native to China and Korea.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, often with a prominent yellow center. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are typically green and hairy on the underside. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

Hibiscus sinosyriacus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
