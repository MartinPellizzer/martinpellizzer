Hieracium glanduliferum subsp. ciavaletense

Hieracium glanduliferum subsp.

ciavaletense, commonly known as the Ciavaletti's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy structure.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and sunny exposures. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered with glandular hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head, typical of the genus Hieracium.
