Hyparrhenia edulis

Hyparrhenia edulis, commonly known as lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It is well adapted to dry conditions and can thrive in poor soils, making it a hardy and resilient species.

Hyparrhenia edulis is often used for forage and soil stabilization in arid environments.
