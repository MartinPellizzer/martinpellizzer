Heuchera hallii

Heuchera hallii, commonly known as the Hall's coral bells, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in woodland habitats and prefers moist, well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with purple or red markings.

Heuchera hallii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
