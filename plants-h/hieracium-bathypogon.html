Hieracium bathypogon

Hieracium bathypogon, commonly known as the deep-throated hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are basal and rhizomatous, with a rough texture and a distinct central vein.

The inflorescence consists of yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disc of tubular flowers, which attract various pollinators.
