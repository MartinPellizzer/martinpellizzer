Hieracium celvae

Hieracium celvae, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Hieracium celvae is often found in open habitats and is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
