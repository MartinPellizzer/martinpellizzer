Hyptis vauthieri

Hyptis vauthieri, commonly known as the velvetleaf hyptis, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It often grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Hyptis vauthieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
