Hieracium pallidum subsp. schmidtiiforme

Hieracium pallidum subsp.

schmidtiiforme, commonly known as the pale hawkweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in late summer.

The plant thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.

Its subspecies status reflects genetic differentiation from other forms of Hieracium pallidum.
