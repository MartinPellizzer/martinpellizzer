Hamamelis japonica f. discolor

Hamamelis japonica f. discolor, commonly known as the Japanese witch hazel variegated form, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late autumn or early winter. The leaves are typically oval with a glossy surface and often exhibit a variegated pattern of green and white. This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various soil types.

It also has historical uses in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory properties.
