Hybanthus vernonii

Hybanthus vernonii, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble bells, which bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant typically grows as an annual or short-lived perennial, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a smooth or slightly hairy texture.

Hybanthus vernonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
