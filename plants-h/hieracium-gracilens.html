Hieracium gracilens

Hieracium gracilens, commonly known as slender hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is an herbaceous perennial that produces seeds through achenes, which are dispersed by wind.
