Hechtia capituligera

Hechtia capituligera, commonly known as the blue false heather, is a striking bromeliad native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are typically thick and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in its arid habitat.

Hechtia capituligera is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
