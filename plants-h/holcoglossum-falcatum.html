Holcoglossum falcatum

Holcoglossum falcatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hoya, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are showy, with a unique structure that includes a fleshy, spurred lip.

Holcoglossum falcatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of care in suitable conditions.
