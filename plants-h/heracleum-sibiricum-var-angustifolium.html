Heracleum sibiricum var. angustifolium

Heracleum sibiricum var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved cow parsnip, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Asia and Europe.

It features a robust, hollow stem that can grow up to 2 meters tall, often with a distinctive purple or reddish hue. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in umbrella-like inflorescences, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a coarse texture, with a strong, often unpleasant odor.

This plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it both a common weed and a notable wildflower.
