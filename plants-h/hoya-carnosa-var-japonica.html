Hoya carnosa var. japonica

Hoya carnosa var. japonica, commonly known as the Japanese waxplant, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often variegated with shades of white, green, and pink. The plant produces clusters of waxy, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink or white. It is a popular houseplant due to its low maintenance and ability to thrive in indirect light.

Its unique appearance and ease of care make it a favored choice among plant enthusiasts.
