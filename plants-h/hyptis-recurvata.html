Hyptis recurvata

Hyptis recurvata, commonly known as silverleaf hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Hyptis recurvata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
