Hesperis sabauda

Hesperis sabauda, commonly known as the Sabauda horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that resemble horns, typically in shades of pink or purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Hesperis sabauda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
