Heliotropium roxburghiana

Heliotropium roxburghiana, commonly known as the Indian heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial species that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with hairy surfaces. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense cymes, and have a distinctive violet or purple coloration.

Heliotropium roxburghiana is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and blooms.
