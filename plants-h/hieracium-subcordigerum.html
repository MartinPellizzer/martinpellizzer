Hieracium subcordigerum

Hieracium subcordigerum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grassy habitats and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain regions.
