Hieracium helicopis

Hieracium helicopis, commonly known as the spiral hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a central stem that bears a single, spiral arrangement of yellow flowers. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grasslands. It has hairy stems and leaves, and its flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators.

Hieracium helicopis is an herbaceous perennial that can spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in some areas.
