Haplopappus gymnocephalus f. albus

Haplopappus gymnocephalus f. albus, commonly known as the white-headed pappus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its pale yellow to white flowers, which are typically arranged in dense, compact heads. The plant has a low-growing habit, with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, sandy or gravelly soils, commonly found in arid and semi-arid regions.

This variety is distinguished by its white flower heads, which contrast with the green foliage, making it a notable ornamental plant in suitable climates.
