Hieracium tridentinum

Hieracium tridentinum, commonly known as the three-lobed hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped with three distinct lobes, and the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is an herbaceous perennial that can spread through rhizomes, forming dense mats in suitable habitats.
