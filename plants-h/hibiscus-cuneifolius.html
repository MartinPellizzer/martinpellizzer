Hibiscus cuneifolius

Hibiscus cuneifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf rosemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are typically narrow and pointed. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late summer. Hibiscus cuneifolius is a perennial shrub that thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often found in wetlands and along the edges of water bodies, where it plays a role in supporting local biodiversity.
