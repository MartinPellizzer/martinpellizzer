Hydrocotyle asiatica var. crispata

Hydrocotyle asiatica var. crispata, commonly known as the glossy centipede plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, rounded, waxy leaves that are often described as resembling the segments of a centipede. The plant produces tiny white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its foliage is popular in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate various soil conditions.
