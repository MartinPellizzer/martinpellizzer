Hieracium pilosella subsp. acutulum

Hieracium pilosella subsp.

acutulum, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with ray florets.

It is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
