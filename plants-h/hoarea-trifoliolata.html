Hoarea trifoliolata

Hoarea trifoliolata, commonly known as the three-leaved horehound, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a creeping stem with opposite, three-lobed leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Hoarea trifoliolata is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
