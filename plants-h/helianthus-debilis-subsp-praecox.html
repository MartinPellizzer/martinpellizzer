Helianthus debilis subsp. praecox

Helianthus debilis subsp.

praecox, commonly known as the morning glory sunflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States. It features slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped, light green leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers with a distinctive red center, which bloom in the early morning and close by midday. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in moist, open habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically valuable species in its native range.
