Hypericum pulchrum var. procumbens

Hypericum pulchrum var. procumbens, commonly known as the creeping St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features prostrate stems that root at the nodes, forming dense mats of foliage. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
