Humata viscidula

Humata viscidula, commonly known as the sticky humata, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that exude a sticky substance, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique botanical features, it is of interest to botanists and plant enthusiasts alike.
