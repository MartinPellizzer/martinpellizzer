Hypochaeris variegata var. pinnatifida

Hypochaeris variegata var. pinnatifida, commonly known as the variegated hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and stems that branch out to produce numerous yellow flowers. The leaves are typically lobed and have a distinctive variegated pattern of green and white. The plant thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
