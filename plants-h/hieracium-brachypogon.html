Hieracium brachypogon

Hieracium brachypogon, commonly known as the short-stemmed hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and basal leaves that are typically lance-shaped. The plant produces small, yellowish composite flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and disturbed soils.

Hieracium brachypogon is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
