Haemanthus nortieri

Haemanthus nortieri, commonly known as the Natal grassblades, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features long, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces umbel-like clusters of bright red or orange flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
