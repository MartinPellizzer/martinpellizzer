Heliotropium chorassanicum var. leiocarpum

Heliotropium chorassanicum var. leiocarpum, commonly known as the silky heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glandular-hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, velvety appearance. The flowers are typically pink or purple, with five petals and a central disc, and they bloom in clusters. This species is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
