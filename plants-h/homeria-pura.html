Homeria pura

Homeria pura, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-gray leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs that help reduce water loss, and it produces a wind-dispersed seed head.

Homeria pura is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
