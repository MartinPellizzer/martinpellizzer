Houstonia angustifolia var. scabra

Houstonia angustifolia var. scabra, commonly known as the roughleaf blue-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Poaceae family, though it is often classified in the genus Houstonia, which is part of the grass family. The plant features slender, grass-like stems with narrow, rough-textured leaves. Its flowers are small, blue or violet, and clustered in dense inflorescences, typical of the genus.

It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and wetlands.
