Helleborus vernalis

Helleborus vernalis, commonly known as the spring hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features dark green, glossy, evergreen leaves that remain throughout the winter. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. Helleborus vernalis is known for its toxicity, containing various cardiac glycosides that can be harmful to humans and animals.

It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in woodlands and rocky soils.
