Hoya pendula

Hoya pendula, commonly known as the hanging wax plant, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its waxy, thick leaves and clusters of star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant is a succulent, capable of storing water in its leaves, which helps it survive in dry conditions. Hoya pendula typically grows as a vine or trailing plant, making it popular for hanging baskets or shelves.

Its slow growth and low maintenance make it a favored choice for indoor gardening.
