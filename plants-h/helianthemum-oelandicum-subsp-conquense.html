Helianthemum oelandicum subsp. conquense

Helianthemum oelandicum subsp.

conquense, commonly known as the Canary Island rockrose, is a low-growing shrub native to the Canary Islands. It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in its arid habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing on rocky slopes or coastal areas.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
