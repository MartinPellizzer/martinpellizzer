Hieraciodes foetida

Hieraciodes foetida, commonly known as stinking hawksbeard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and rough, hairy leaves. The plant is often found in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its name derives from the strong, unpleasant odor emitted by its flowers.

Hieraciodes foetida is considered a weed in some areas but has ecological value in supporting pollinators.
