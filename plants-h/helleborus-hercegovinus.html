Helleborus hercegovinus

Helleborus hercegovinus, commonly known as the Hercegovean hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan region.

It features dark green, leathery leaves that are often lobed or notched, and it produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded woodland areas and thrives in well-drained, loamy soil. It is known for its winter blooming habit, often flowering from late winter to early spring.

Helleborus hercegovinus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
