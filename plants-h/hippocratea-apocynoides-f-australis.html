Hippocratea apocynoides f. australis

Hippocratea apocynoides f. australis, commonly known as the Australian coral tree, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its striking coral-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a waxy texture, while its flowers are usually pink or red.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
