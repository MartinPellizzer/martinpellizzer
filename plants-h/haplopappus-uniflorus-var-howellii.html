Haplopappus uniflorus var. howellii

Haplopappus uniflorus var. howellii, commonly known as Howell's singleflower pappus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typical sunflower-like structure. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and heat. Its small, yellowish flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and it produces a unique, narrow pappus that aids in wind pollination.

The species is noted for its low-growing habit and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
