Hemerocallis × yeldiana

Hemerocallis × yeldiana, commonly known as the yellow daylily, is a hybrid plant that combines traits from various daylily species.

It is characterized by its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow. The plant is perennial, producing showy blooms from late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is known for its hardiness and adaptability to a variety of soil types.

The foliage is narrow and grass-like, providing an attractive backdrop to the colorful flowers.
