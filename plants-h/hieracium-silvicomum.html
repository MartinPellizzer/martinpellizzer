Hieracium silvicomum

Hieracium silvicomum, commonly known as wood hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist woodlands and shaded areas, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features basal rosettes of lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and the plant is known for its hairy stems and leaves.

Hieracium silvicomum is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is often found in temperate regions.
