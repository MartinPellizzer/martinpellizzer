Hippeastrum anzaldoi

Hippeastrum anzaldoi, commonly known as the Anzaldo's amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It produces large, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, often with a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant features broad, strap-like leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture. It is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family, known for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.

Hippeastrum anzaldoi thrives in well-drained soil and prefers bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
