Hordeum nudum

Hordeum nudum, commonly known as the slender wild barley, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, fields, and riverbanks. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves, with small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. Hordeum nudum is characterized by its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resilience to environmental stress.

It plays a role in ecological succession and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
