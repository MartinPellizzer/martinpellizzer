Hieracium murorum subsp. valentius

Hieracium murorum subsp.

valentius, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats such as meadows and roadsides. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

This subspecies is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it plays a role in supporting pollinators in its native habitats.
