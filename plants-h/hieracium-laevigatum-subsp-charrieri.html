Hieracium laevigatum subsp. charrieri

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

charrieri, commonly known as the smooth hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring temperate climates. It has smooth, glabrous leaves and produces numerous small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into achene fruits.

This subspecies is often found in open habitats and is relatively hardy, though it can be invasive in some regions.
