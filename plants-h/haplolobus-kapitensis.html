Haplolobus kapitensis

Haplolobus kapitensis, commonly known as the Kapitensis lichen, is a unique member of the Lichens family.

It is a symbiotic organism composed of a fungus and a photosynthetic partner, typically a green alga or cyanobacterium. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on tree bark or rocks in tropical regions. Its structure consists of a thallus, which is the body of the lichen, and it lacks the typical root, stem, and leaf structures found in vascular plants.

Haplolobus kapitensis plays an important role in nutrient cycling and serves as a habitat for various microorganisms.
