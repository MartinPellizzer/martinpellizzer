Heteropappus altaicus var. decrescens

Heteropappus altaicus var. decrescens, commonly known as the desert fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited water availability. Its yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is also known for its medicinal properties, with traditional uses in treating various ailments.
