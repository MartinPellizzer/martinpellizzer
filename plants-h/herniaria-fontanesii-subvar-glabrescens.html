Herniaria fontanesii subvar. glabrescens

Herniaria fontanesii subvar. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth bladderwort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and small, fleshy leaves that often appear in pairs. The plant produces tiny white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to coastal and saline environments.

Its unique morphology and ecological niche make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
