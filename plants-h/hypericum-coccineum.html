Hypericum coccineum

Hypericum coccineum, commonly known as the red-stemmed St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces bright yellow flowers with five petals. Its stems are distinctly red, which is a distinguishing botanical characteristic.

It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and woodland edges.
