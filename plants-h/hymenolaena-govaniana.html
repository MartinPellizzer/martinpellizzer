Hymenolaena govaniana

Hymenolaena govaniana, commonly known as the silverleaf lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-haired leaves and tufted inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-gray hue due to the dense covering of trichomes.

Hymenolaena govaniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and aesthetic qualities.
