Habrothamnus magnificus

Habrothamnus magnificus, commonly known as the magnificent broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, bushy growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, seed-containing pods that mature in the summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its attractive appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
