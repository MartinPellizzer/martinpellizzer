Huperzia lauterbachii

Huperzia lauterbachii, commonly known as the Lauterbach's spikemoss, is a perennial evergreen fern-like plant belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, scale-like leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern. Native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. The plant produces sporangia on specialized structures called strobila, which are clustered at the tips of the stems.

Huperzia lauterbachii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
