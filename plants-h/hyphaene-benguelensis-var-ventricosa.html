Hyphaene benguelensis var. ventricosa

Hyphaene benguelensis var. ventricosa, commonly known as the African fan palm, is a species of palm native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves and a robust, trunk-like structure that supports its dense foliage. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils and saline conditions. It produces small, yellow flowers that develop into clustered fruits, which are an important food source for various wildlife.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
