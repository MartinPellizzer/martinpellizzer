Helianthus pauciflorus subsp. subrhomboideus

Helianthus pauciflorus subsp.

subrhomboideus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers with a central disk surrounded by ray florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in both horticulture and ecological research.
