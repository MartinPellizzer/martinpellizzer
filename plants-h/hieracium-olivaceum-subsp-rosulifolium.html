Hieracium olivaceum subsp. rosulifolium

Hieracium olivaceum subsp.

rosulifolium, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer.

Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this species thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas, where it spreads through its rhizomes and seeds.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and garden cultivation.
