Hieracium smolandicum subsp. coronopoides

Hieracium smolandicum subsp.

coronopoides, commonly known as the Swedish hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering heads that produce yellow ray florets.

The plant typically grows in moist, nutrient-rich soils in meadows and along stream banks. It has a low-growing habit with hairy stems and is often found in temperate climates.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and declining species in many regions.
