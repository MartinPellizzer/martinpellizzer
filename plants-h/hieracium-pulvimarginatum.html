Hieracium pulvimarginatum

Hieracium pulvimarginatum, commonly known as the powdered margin hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a distinctive powdery or whitish margin, giving it its common name.

It produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
