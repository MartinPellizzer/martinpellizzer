Hirtella racemosa var. hexandra

Hirtella racemosa var. hexandra, commonly known as the hairy hirtella, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces white to pale pink flowers that are often fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
