Heliotropium gorinii

Heliotropium gorinii, commonly known as the Cape heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy surface and produces small, violet-colored flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and sandy habitats.

The plant is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
