Hieracium obliquum

Hieracium obliquum, commonly known as the bent-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant has basal leaves that are usually lobed or divided, with a hairy surface. Its flowers are typically yellow, arranged in a flat-topped cluster, and bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Hieracium obliquum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
