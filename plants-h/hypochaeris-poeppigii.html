Hypochaeris poeppigii

Hypochaeris poeppigii, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with a central disk surrounded by ray florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in open habitats like grasslands and disturbed soils, and it is often found in temperate regions.

Hypochaeris poeppigii is also known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in some areas.
