Hieracium murorum subsp. orthorhachis

Hieracium murorum subsp.

orthorhachis, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, meadows, and waste areas.

The plant features upright, hairy stems and basal leaves that are lobed and often covered in fine hairs. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a dense, flat head, and it produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
