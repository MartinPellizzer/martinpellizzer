Hieracium taigense

Hieracium taigense, commonly known as the Siberian hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the colder regions of Siberia and northern Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and tundra, and it thrives in cool climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium taigense is known for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions and is often found in areas with poor soil.
