Hieracium bifidum subsp. macrolobum

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

macrolobum, commonly known as the broad-leaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement.

The plant features basal and alternate leaves that are broadly ovate to lanceolate in shape, with serrated margins. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the genus Hieracium.

It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.
