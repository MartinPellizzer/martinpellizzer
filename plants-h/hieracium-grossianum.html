Hieracium grossianum

Hieracium grossianum, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine meadows, where it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Hieracium grossianum is often found in high-altitude environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
