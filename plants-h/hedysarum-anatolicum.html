Hedysarum anatolicum

Hedysarum anatolicum, commonly known as Anatolian sweet clover, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in ecological and agricultural contexts. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Hedysarum anatolicum is also noted for its ornamental value due to its attractive floral display.
