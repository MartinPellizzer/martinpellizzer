Hieracium camerarii

Hieracium camerarii, commonly known as the Italian hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to reddish flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are arranged in a composite head.

Hieracium camerarii is often considered a weed but can be found in various ecological settings across its native range.
