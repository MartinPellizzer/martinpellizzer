Hieracium sarcophylloides

Hieracium sarcophylloides, commonly known as the golden hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disk.

Hieracium sarcophylloides is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
