Halophila ovalis subsp. linearis

Halophila ovalis subsp.

linearis, commonly known as the linear seagrass, is a species of seagrass found in coastal marine environments. It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

This plant thrives in shallow, sandy substrates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. It plays a vital role in stabilizing sediments and providing habitat for various marine organisms. Halophila ovalis subsp.

linearis is also important for carbon sequestration and supporting biodiversity in coastal ecosystems.
