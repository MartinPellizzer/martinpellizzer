Hieracium lucidum subsp. cophanense

Hieracium lucidum subsp.

cophanense, commonly known as the glossy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features upright stems with alternate, broadly lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow, with a distinctive involucral bract, and it produces small, oval seeds in a schizocarp.

It is characterized by its glossy, waxy leaves that give it its common name.
