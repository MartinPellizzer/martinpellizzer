Helichrysum doerfleri

Helichrysum doerfleri, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, yellow, sunflower-like flowers that remain open for a long time. The plant is evergreen, with narrow, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and Mediterranean climates.

Its unique appearance and ability to retain its color after drying make it a valued ornamental plant.
