Hieracium brevialatum

Hieracium brevialatum, commonly known as the short-stemmed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hieracium brevialatum is often found in open habitats and can form dense colonies, sometimes becoming invasive in certain regions.
