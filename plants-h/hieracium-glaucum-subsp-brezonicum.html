Hieracium glaucum subsp. brezonicum

Hieracium glaucum subsp.

brezonicum, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or grassy slopes at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.

The plant features basal leaves that are usually lanceolate and covered in a dense layer of white to grayish hairs. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in mountainous habitats across the Alps and Carpathian regions.
