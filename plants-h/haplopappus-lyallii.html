Haplopappus lyallii

Haplopappus lyallii, commonly known as Lyall's pappus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its small, yellowish flowers bloom in the summer, and the plant produces a unique, hairless pappus that aids in seed dispersal.

Haplopappus lyallii is often found in alpine meadows and is of interest to botanists due to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
