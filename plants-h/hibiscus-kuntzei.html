Hibiscus kuntzei

Hibiscus kuntzei, commonly known as the Kuntze's hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy, five-petaled flowers that typically range in color from white to pink or red. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Hibiscus kuntzei thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in swampy or wetland habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
