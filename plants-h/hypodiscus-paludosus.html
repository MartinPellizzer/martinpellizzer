Hypodiscus paludosus

Hypodiscus paludosus, commonly known as the swamp hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, showcasing its adaptation to waterlogged soils. The plant features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually pink or red, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are broad and ovate, often with a waxy surface to reduce water loss.

The botanical characteristics of Hypodiscus paludosus reflect its ecological niche in humid, tropical environments.
