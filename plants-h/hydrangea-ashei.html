Hydrangea ashei

Hydrangea ashei, commonly known as the Florida panhandle hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from white to pink, depending on soil pH. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and features broad, ovate leaves with a glossy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Hydrangea ashei is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
