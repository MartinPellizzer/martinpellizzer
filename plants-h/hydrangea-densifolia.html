Hydrangea densifolia

Hydrangea densifolia, commonly known as the denseleaf hydrangea, is a species native to East Asia, particularly China and Korea.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and produces clusters of white or pink flowers in the summer. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth texture and can grow up to 5 inches in length.

Hydrangea densifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its lush foliage and showy blooms.
