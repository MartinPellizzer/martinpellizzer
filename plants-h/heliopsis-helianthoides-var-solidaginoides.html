Heliopsis helianthoides var. solidaginoides

Heliopsis helianthoides var. solidaginoides, commonly known as the golden coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern and central United States.

It features showy, bright yellow ray flowers surrounding a cone-shaped center, resembling a small sunflower. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet, with lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its botanical structure supports pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
