Hypericum psilophytum

Hypericum psilophytum, commonly known as narrow-leaved St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters along the stems. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for horticultural and traditional use.
