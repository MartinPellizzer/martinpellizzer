Hedeoma piperita var. oblongifolia

Hedeoma piperita var. oblongifolia, commonly known as Mexican pepper plant or wild pepper, is a variant of the Mexican mint plant.

It is a herbaceous perennial with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in clusters, which are typically borne on slender stalks. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is native to parts of Central and South America.

This variety is often used in herbal remedies and as an ornamental plant due to its aromatic foliage and resilience.
