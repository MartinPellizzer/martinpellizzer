Hieracium macrodon

Hieracium macrodon, commonly known as the giant hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads made up of numerous small florets. The plant features upright, hairy stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating. Its showy yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hieracium macrodon is typically found in disturbed soils, meadows, and along roadsides, where it can form dense colonies.
