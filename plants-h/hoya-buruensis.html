Hoya buruensis

Hoya buruensis, commonly known as the Buru waxplant, is a species of flowering plant native to Indonesia.

It is characterized by its thick, succulent stems and clusters of waxy, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The leaves are typically thick, fleshy, and arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps the plant store water in its native arid environment. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of care.
