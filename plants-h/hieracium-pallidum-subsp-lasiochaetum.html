Hieracium pallidum subsp. lasiochaetum

Hieracium pallidum subsp.

lasiochaetum, commonly known as the pale hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dense, woolly hairs on the leaves and stems, which provide a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides.

This plant is native to parts of Europe and has been introduced to other regions, where it can become invasive in certain areas.
