Hieracium lachenalii subsp. eustictum

Hieracium lachenalii subsp.

eustictum, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a flowering stem that bears yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats at high altitudes. Its leaves are hairy and have a serrated edge, while the flowers are arranged in a single head.

It is adapted to cold climates and has a relatively short growing season.
