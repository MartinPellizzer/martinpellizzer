Hemigenia macphersonii

Hemigenia macphersonii, commonly known as the Macpherson's hemigenia, is a small shrub native to the wet tropical rainforests of northeastern Queensland, Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense spikes at the ends of the branches. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Hemigenia macphersonii is often found in shaded, moist environments and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
