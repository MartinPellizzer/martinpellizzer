Hibiscus solandra

Hibiscus solandra, commonly known as the desert hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves and stems adapted to conserve water in dry environments. Hibiscus solandra produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
