Hieracium bifidum basicuneatum

Hieracium bifidum basicuneatum, commonly known as the two-lobed hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant has hairy, alternate leaves and produces yellow ray florets surrounded by tubular disc florets. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is sometimes grown in gardens.
