Hoarea pulchella

Hoarea pulchella, commonly known as the beautiful hoya, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Hoarea pulchella is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.

Its flowers typically bloom in shades of white, pink, or red, adding a touch of elegance to indoor spaces.
