Hebe ramosissima

Hebe ramosissima, commonly known as the rock hebe, is a perennial shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, woody stems that branch profusely, giving it a bushy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet, typically blooming in spring and summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of silvery hair.

Hebe ramosissima is well adapted to rocky and coastal environments, thriving in full sun and well-drained soils.
