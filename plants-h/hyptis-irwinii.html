Hyptis irwinii

Hyptis irwinii, commonly known as the blue hyptis, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, tubular blue or violet flowers. Its leaves are typically gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it often grows in moist, shaded environments.

Hyptis irwinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
