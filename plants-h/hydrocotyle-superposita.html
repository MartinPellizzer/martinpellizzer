Hydrocotyle superposita

Hydrocotyle superposita, commonly known as the silverleaf water shield, is a small aquatic plant native to parts of North America.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. The plant has delicate, floating leaves that are usually oval or rounded with a waxy surface. Its flowers are small and white, often clustered in a umbel-like arrangement.

Hydrocotyle superposita plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small invertebrates and serving as a food source for waterfowl.
