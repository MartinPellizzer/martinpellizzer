Hieracium × monacense

Hieracium × monacense, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a hybrid species within the sunflower family (Asteraceae).

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant produces yellow daisy-like flowers with a central disk and white or pale yellow ray florets. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

Hieracium × monacense is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
