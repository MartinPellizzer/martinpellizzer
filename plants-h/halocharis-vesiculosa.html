Halocharis vesiculosa

Halocharis vesiculosa, commonly known as the bubble cactus, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its swollen, balloon-like stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a vibrant touch to its otherwise spiky appearance. Its leaves are typically reduced to small, scale-like structures, further minimizing water loss.

Halocharis vesiculosa is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and unique morphology.
