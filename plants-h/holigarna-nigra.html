Holigarna nigra

Holigarna nigra, commonly known as the black holigarna, is a rare and unique plant species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, velvety leaves and striking, deep purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in dense rainforests. Its botanical significance lies in its unique pigmentation and potential medicinal properties.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
