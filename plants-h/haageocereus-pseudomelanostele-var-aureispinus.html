Haageocereus pseudomelanostele var. aureispinus

Haageocereus pseudomelanostele var. aureispinus, commonly known as the golden spined cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are typically covered with dense, yellowish spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are often borne in clusters at the base of the stems. It is a slow-growing, drought-tolerant cactus that thrives in well-draining soil.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
