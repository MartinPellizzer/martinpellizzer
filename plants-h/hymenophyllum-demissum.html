Hymenophyllum demissum

Hymenophyllum demissum, commonly known as the slender shield fern, is a small, epiphytic fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are often covered in a dense layer of hair-like structures called trichomes. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found on the bark of trees in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and are covered by a protective indusium.

Hymenophyllum demissum plays a role in forest ecosystems by contributing to biodiversity and providing habitat for small organisms.
