Hyacinthus mauritanicus

Hyacinthus mauritanicus, commonly known as the desert hyacinth, is a flowering plant native to North Africa and the Mediterranean region.

It is a bulbous perennial that produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, violet, and white. The plant is adapted to arid environments, featuring a fibrous root system and succulent leaves to conserve water. Its blooming period typically occurs in spring, and it thrives in well-drained soil.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
