Hemianemia anthriscifolia

Hemianemia anthriscifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf hemianthus, is a rare flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves that resemble those of the parsley family. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
