Hedera colchica

Hedera colchica, commonly known as the Colchic ivy, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is a climbing evergreen vine that features dark green, leathery leaves with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red berries containing seeds. Hedera colchica is adapted to temperate climates and thrives in shaded, moist environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for both horticultural and historical purposes.
