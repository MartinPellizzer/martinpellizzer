Hallia asarina

Hallia asarina, commonly known as the Indian ginger or wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like ginger and cardamom. The plant features aromatic, rhizomatous growth with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and greenish-yellow, arranged in inflorescences, and it produces small, red or orange berries.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
