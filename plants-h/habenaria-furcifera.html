Habenaria furcifera

Habenaria furcifera, commonly known as the forked orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers with a unique, forked lip that gives it its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically in tropical and subtropical regions.

Habenaria furcifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
