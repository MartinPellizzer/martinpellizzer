Hemionitis crassifolia

Hemionitis crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed hemionitis, is a rare and unique fern native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, broad, leathery leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. The plant thrives in harsh, dry environments and is adapted to withstand extreme temperature fluctuations. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of many fern species.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Hemionitis crassifolia is considered a conservation concern.
