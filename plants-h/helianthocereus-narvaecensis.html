Helianthocereus narvaecensis

Helianthocereus narvaecensis, commonly known as the Narvaecensis Cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white to pale green spines. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to desert environments.

The species is noted for its slow growth and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
