Hypopeltis hastulata

Hypopeltis hastulata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed grass.

It is a perennial grass species native to Africa, characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-lipped flowers.

Hypopeltis hastulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its delicate appearance.
