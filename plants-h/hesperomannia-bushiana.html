Hesperomannia bushiana

Hesperomannia bushiana, commonly known as the bushy hesperomannia, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or swampy areas. The plant features small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Hesperomannia bushiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
