Hedyotis caerulea var. minor

Hedyotis caerulea var. minor, commonly known as blue false indigo, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems and small, delicate blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This species is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
