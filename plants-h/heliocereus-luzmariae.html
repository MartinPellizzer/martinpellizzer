Heliocereus luzmariae

Heliocereus luzmariae, commonly known as the Sun Cactus, is a species of cactus native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, rounded, ribbed stems that are typically green or pale blue in color. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, opening at night and closing during the day. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and nocturnal blooming habit, it is a popular choice for both ornamental and horticultural purposes.
