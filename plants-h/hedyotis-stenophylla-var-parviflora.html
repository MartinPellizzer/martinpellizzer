Hedyotis stenophylla var. parviflora

Hedyotis stenophylla var. parviflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Asia.

It features small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming in early summer. The plant has thin, hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in opposite pairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and habitat restoration.
