Huperzia acifolia

Huperzia acifolia, commonly known as the needle fern, is a species of clubmoss belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its sporophylls are closely clustered and bear spore-producing structures called sporangia.

Huperzia acifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its unique foliage.
