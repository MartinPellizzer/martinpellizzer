Heuchera hispida var. richardsonii

Heuchera hispida var. richardsonii, commonly known as the Richardson's alumroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features basal rosettes of velvety, hairy leaves that are typically reddish or bronze in color. The plant produces upright stems bearing clusters of small, bell-shaped white or pale pink flowers in early to mid-summer. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening for its striking color and texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
