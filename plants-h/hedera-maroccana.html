Hedera maroccana

Hedera maroccana, commonly known as Moroccan ivy, is a climbing evergreen vine native to the mountainous regions of North Africa.

It features dark green, glossy leaves that are typically heart-shaped with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange-red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Hedera maroccana is valued for its adaptability and ability to climb structures, making it a popular choice for vertical gardening.
