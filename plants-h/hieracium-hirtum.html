Hieracium hirtum

Hieracium hirtum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright stems and basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that are typically arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Hieracium hirtum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
