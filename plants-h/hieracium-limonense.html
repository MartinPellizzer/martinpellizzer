Hieracium limonense

Hieracium limonense, commonly known as the lemon hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are yellow, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets, and it produces small, oval seeds in a schizocarp.

Hieracium limonense is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
