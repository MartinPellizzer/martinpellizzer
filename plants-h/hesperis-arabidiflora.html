Hesperis arabidiflora

Hesperis arabidiflora, commonly known as the Arabidopsis hesperis, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant has a basal rosette of leaves and produces racemes of white or pale purple flowers. It is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Hesperis arabidiflora is also used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate.
