Hesperaloe funifera

Hesperaloe funifera, commonly known as the red agave, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a rosette of thick, fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a white, powdery coating called farina. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with clusters of bright red flowers, which bloom in late spring or early summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant's striking appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
