Hemerocallis albomarginata

Hemerocallis albomarginata, commonly known as the white-margin daylily, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern Asia.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow with white margins, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that are dark green and densely arranged. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

Hemerocallis albomarginata is prized for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation in gardens.
