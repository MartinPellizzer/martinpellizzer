Hymenophyllum mexiae

Hymenophyllum mexiae, commonly known as the Mexican haircap moss, is a small, delicate fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, needle-like leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on rocks or tree trunks in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spore-producing structures, called sporangia, are clustered in small, cup-like formations called hymenophores.

Hymenophyllum mexiae is notable for its intricate leaf anatomy and is often used in horticultural displays due to its ornamental appearance.
