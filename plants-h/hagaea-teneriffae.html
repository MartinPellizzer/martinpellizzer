Hagaea teneriffae

Hagaea teneriffae, commonly known as the Canary Islands hagaea, is a rare succulent native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, pale green flowers during the warmer months, which are typically short-lived. Its leaves are thick and waxy, reducing water loss and adapting it to its harsh environment.

Hagaea teneriffae is a unique example of plant adaptation to island ecosystems with limited resources.
