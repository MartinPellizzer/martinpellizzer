Hieracium negoiense

Hieracium negoiense, commonly known as the Balkan hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its yellow flowers appear in late summer, and it produces small, hairy seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Hieracium negoiense is often found in disturbed habitats and can be invasive in certain regions.
