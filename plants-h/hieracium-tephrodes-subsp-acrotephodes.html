Hieracium tephrodes subsp. acrotephodes

Hieracium tephrodes subsp.

acrotephodes, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow flower heads.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer.

It is often found in mountainous areas and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.
