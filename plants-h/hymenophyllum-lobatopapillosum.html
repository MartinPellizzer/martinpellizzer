Hymenophyllum lobatopapillosum

Hymenophyllum lobatopapillosum, commonly known as the lobed papillate fern, is a small, epiphytic fern found in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant has a unique morphology with small, overlapping, scale-like leaves that form a dense, mat-like structure. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds.

This species thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found growing on tree trunks or rocks in humid forests.
