Helenium cuspidatum

Helenium cuspidatum, commonly known as spearhead rayflower, is a perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring narrow, grass-like leaves and striking yellow-orange flower heads. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Helenium cuspidatum is valued for its ornamental beauty and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
