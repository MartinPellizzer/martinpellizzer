Hieracium algovicum

Hieracium algovicum, commonly known as the Alpine Hawkweed, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in well-drained, nutrient-poor soils. Its leaves are basal, spoon-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Hieracium algovicum is often found in high-altitude environments where it adapts to cold and harsh climatic conditions.
