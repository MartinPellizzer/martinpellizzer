Heliotropium marifolium var. bracteatum

Heliotropium marifolium var. bracteatum, commonly known as the bracteate heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually hairy and have a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with a distinctive purple or pink coloration, often resembling the scent of violets. Its growth habit is typically prostrate or ascending, forming dense mats that can thrive in moist, sandy soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
