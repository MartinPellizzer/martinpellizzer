Hakea leptopoda

Hakea leptopoda, commonly known as the slender hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes. Hakea leptopoda is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
