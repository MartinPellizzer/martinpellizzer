Hibiscus fluminensis

Hibiscus fluminensis, commonly known as the Brazilian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its large, showy flowers with five petals. The leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and have a velvety texture. It produces bright yellow stamens and a prominent pistil, making it visually striking.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
