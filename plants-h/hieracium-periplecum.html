Hieracium periplecum

Hieracium periplecum, commonly known as the common groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and fields. Its leaves are usually alternate, rough to the touch, and have a distinctive lobed or toothed appearance.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing a yellow color that attracts various pollinators.
