Hymenocallis skinneriana

Hymenocallis skinneriana, commonly known as the Skinner's spider lily, is a striking tropical plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers with prominent yellow markings, which resemble spider-like shapes, hence its common name. The plant features strap-like, grassy leaves and produces bulbous underground storage organs typical of the Amaryllidaceae family. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Hymenocallis skinneriana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
