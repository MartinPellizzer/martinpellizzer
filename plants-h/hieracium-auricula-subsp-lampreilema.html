Hieracium auricula subsp. lampreilema

Hieracium auricula subsp.

lampreilema, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

This subspecies is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
