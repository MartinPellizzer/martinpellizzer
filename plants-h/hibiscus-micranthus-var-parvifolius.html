Hibiscus micranthus var. parvifolius

Hibiscus micranthus var. parvifolius, commonly known as the small-leaved hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation in warm climates.

The flowers bloom throughout the growing season, adding ornamental value to gardens and attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
