Hieracium erythrocarpum subsp. leptobrachiopsis

Hieracium erythrocarpum subsp.

leptobrachiopsis, commonly known as the red-fruited hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright stems and yellow ray florets.

The plant produces small, reddish-orange fruits that are distinctive in appearance. It typically grows in grasslands and open habitats, preferring well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the genus Hieracium.
