Hamatocactus uncinatus subsp. crassihamatus

Hamatocactus uncinatus subsp.

crassihamatus, commonly known as the thick-stemmed hedgehog cactus, is a succulent native to the arid regions of Mexico. It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are covered in dense, white spines.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne in clusters at the base of the stems. It is adapted to survive in hot, dry environments with minimal water requirements.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
