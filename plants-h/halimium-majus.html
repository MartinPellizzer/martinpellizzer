Halimium majus

Halimium majus, commonly known as the creeping sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It thrives in sandy and rocky soils, often found in coastal and mountainous regions. The plant features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in arid environments.

Halimium majus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils.
