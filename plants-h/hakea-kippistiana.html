Hakea kippistiana

Hakea kippistiana, commonly known as the Kippist's hakea, is an evergreen shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and distinctive, cylindrical flower spikes that bloom in winter. The plant produces woody, cone-like fruits that contain seeds enclosed in fibrous, hairy pods. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland heathlands.

Hakea kippistiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
