Hutchinsia alba lusus cinerea

Hutchinsia alba lusus cinerea, commonly known as the silverleaf hutchinsia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This species is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and compact growth habit.
