Higginsia refulgens var. roezlii

Higginsia refulgens var. roezlii, commonly known as the golden thistle, is a striking plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow bracts that surround the spiny flower heads, creating a visually appealing display. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its leaves are usually blue-gray in color, covered in a fine layer of white hairs that help reduce water loss.

Higginsia refulgens var. roezlii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
