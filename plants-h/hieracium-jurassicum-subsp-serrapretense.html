Hieracium jurassicum subsp. serrapretense

Hieracium jurassicum subsp.

serrapretense, commonly known as the Jurassic hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its serrated, lance-shaped leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, thriving in temperate climates. Its subspecies name, *serrapretense*, refers to the serrated leaves and its preference for moist habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and protected species in several European countries.
