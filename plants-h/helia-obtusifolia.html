Helia obtusifolia

Helia obtusifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf helia, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often found in open woodlands and along rocky outcrops.

Helia obtusifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
