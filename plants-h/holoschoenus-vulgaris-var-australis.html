Holoschoenus vulgaris var. australis

Holoschoenus vulgaris var. australis, commonly known as the Southern flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It typically grows in wetland or swampy environments, preferring moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes. It plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to the fluctuating water levels of its native habitat.
