Hosackia humilis

Hosackia humilis, commonly known as the plains gentian, is a flowering plant native to the central and northern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. The plant features showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are upright and branched.

Hosackia humilis plays an important ecological role in supporting local insect populations and maintaining biodiversity in grassland ecosystems.
