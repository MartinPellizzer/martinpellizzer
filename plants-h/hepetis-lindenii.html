Hepetis lindenii

Hepetis lindenii, commonly known as the Lindenberg's heath, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes shrubs and small trees adapted to acidic soils. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, contributing to its ornamental appeal in garden settings.
