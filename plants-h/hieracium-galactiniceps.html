Hieracium galactiniceps

Hieracium galactiniceps, commonly known as the milk thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking purple to pink flowers and spiny, lobed leaves. The plant produces a milky sap, which is a distinguishing feature and has been used in traditional medicine. It thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Hieracium galactiniceps is also known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and supporting biodiversity in its habitat.
