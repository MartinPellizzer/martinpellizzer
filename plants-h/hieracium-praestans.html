Hieracium praestans

Hieracium praestans, commonly known as the showy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
