Hedyotis kunstleri

Hedyotis kunstleri, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

Hedyotis kunstleri is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
