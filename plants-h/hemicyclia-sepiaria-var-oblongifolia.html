Hemicyclia sepiaria var. oblongifolia

Hemicyclia sepiaria var. oblongifolia, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, often surrounded by spiky, leaf-like bracts. The plant has a compact growth habit and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

Hemicyclia sepiaria var. oblongifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its unique appearance and resilience to coastal conditions.
