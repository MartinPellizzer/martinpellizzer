Hieracium praecurrens subsp. trachysetum

Hieracium praecurrens subsp.

trachysetum, commonly known as the rough-haired hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy structure.

The plant features rough, hairy leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the genus Hieracium.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and woodland edges.
