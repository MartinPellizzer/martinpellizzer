Heterotheca gossypina

Heterotheca gossypina, commonly known as the blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its blue to purple flowers bloom in the summer months.

Heterotheca gossypina is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
