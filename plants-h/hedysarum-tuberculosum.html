Hedysarum tuberculosum

Hedysarum tuberculosum, commonly known as the mountain peavine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Hedysarum tuberculosum is often found in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
