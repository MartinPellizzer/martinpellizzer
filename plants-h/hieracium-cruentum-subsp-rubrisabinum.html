Hieracium cruentum subsp. rubrisabinum

Hieracium cruentum subsp.

rubrisabinum, commonly known as the red-hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy stems.

The subspecies is distinguished by its reddish stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
