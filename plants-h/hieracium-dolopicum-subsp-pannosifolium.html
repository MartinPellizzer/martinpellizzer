Hieracium dolopicum subsp. pannosifolium

Hieracium dolopicum subsp.

pannosifolium, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to central and eastern Europe. It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, grassy areas and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its subspecies name, *pannosifolium*, refers to its distinctive, woolly foliage.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is considered a member of the dandelion family (Asteraceae).
