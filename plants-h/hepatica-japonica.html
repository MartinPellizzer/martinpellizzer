Hepatica japonica

Hepatica japonica, commonly known as Japanese liverleaf, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that resemble a liver, hence its common name. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, typically in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Hepatica japonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early blooming period.
