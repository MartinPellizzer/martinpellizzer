Hypoxis incisa

Hypoxis incisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved star grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and strap-shaped, often with a slight zigzag pattern along the edges.

Hypoxis incisa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
