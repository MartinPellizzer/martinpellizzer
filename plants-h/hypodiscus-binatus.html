Hypodiscus binatus

Hypodiscus binatus, commonly known as the two-colored orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its striking, two-toned flowers that range from white to pink and red. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette.

Hypodiscus binatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique and colorful blooms.
