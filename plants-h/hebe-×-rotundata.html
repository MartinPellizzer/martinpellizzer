Hebe × rotundata

Hebe × rotundata, commonly known as the compact hebe or the evergreen shrub, is a hybrid plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, or white. The plant has evergreen foliage with dark green, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery bloom. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Hebe × rotundata is also valued for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
