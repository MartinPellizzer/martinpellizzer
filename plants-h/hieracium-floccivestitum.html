Hieracium floccivestitum

Hieracium floccivestitum, commonly known as the fluffcap hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous tiny florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flower heads are pale yellow to white in color.

It is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
