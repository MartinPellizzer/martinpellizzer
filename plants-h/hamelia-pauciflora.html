Hamelia pauciflora

Hamelia pauciflora, commonly known as the Mexican flamebush, is a deciduous shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Hamelia pauciflora is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.
