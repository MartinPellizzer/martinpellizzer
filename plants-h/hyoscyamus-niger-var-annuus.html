Hyoscyamus niger var. annuus

Hyoscyamus niger var. annuus, commonly known as black henbane, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a stout, branching stem covered in coarse hairs and produces distinctive bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to deep purple. The plant's leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a hairy texture. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Black henbane contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.
