Hieracium pallescens subsp. excisum

Hieracium pallescens subsp.

excisum, commonly known as the pale hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosettes of lanceolate, serrated leaves.

The flowers are typically yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and are surrounded by pale green ray florets. It is native to Europe and is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows.

The plant has a relatively short stature, usually growing to a height of less than 30 centimeters.
