Hiraea fagifolia subf. parvifolia

Hiraea fagifolia subf.

parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved hiraea, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged oppositely on the stems.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its foliage.
