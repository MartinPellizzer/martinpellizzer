Hyptis tafalloides

Hyptis tafalloides, commonly known as the Tafalloide Hyptis, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in Brazil.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Hyptis tafalloides is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
