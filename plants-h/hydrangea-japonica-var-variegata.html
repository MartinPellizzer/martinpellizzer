Hydrangea japonica var. variegata

Hydrangea japonica var. variegata, commonly known as the Japanese hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from white to pink, depending on soil pH. The plant produces broad, ovate leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow margins. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer, adding a vibrant touch to gardens during that time.
