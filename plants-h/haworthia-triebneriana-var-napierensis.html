Haworthia triebneriana var. napierensis

Haworthia triebneriana var. napierensis, commonly known as the Napier's Haworthia, is a compact, rosette-forming succulent native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, triangular leaves with a waxy, translucent texture and a distinctive white to pale green coloration. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for rock gardens or container cultivation.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
