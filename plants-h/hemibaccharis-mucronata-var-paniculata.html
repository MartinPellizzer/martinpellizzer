Hemibaccharis mucronata var. paniculata

Hemibaccharis mucronata var. paniculata, commonly known as the panicled hemibaccharis, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, often branched inflorescences. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are typically arranged in panicles, giving it its common name. Its leaves are usually opposite, simple, and have a serrated margin.

It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.
