Hyparrhenia foliosa

Hyparrhenia foliosa, commonly known as the slender grass or narrowleaf blady grass, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. The plant thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.

Hyparrhenia foliosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
