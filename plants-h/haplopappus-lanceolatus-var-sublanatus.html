Haplopappus lanceolatus var. sublanatus

Haplopappus lanceolatus var. sublanatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pappus, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive bract structures. The plant typically grows as a low-growing shrub with linear, succulent leaves adapted to conserve water.
