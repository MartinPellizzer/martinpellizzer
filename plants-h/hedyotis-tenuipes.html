Hedyotis tenuipes

Hedyotis tenuipes, commonly known as the slender dotweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant features small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in spikes and are usually white or pale yellow, producing small, capsule-like fruits.

Hedyotis tenuipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
