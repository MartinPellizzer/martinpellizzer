Hieracium galicianum

Hieracium galicianum, commonly known as Galician hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are yellow and bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators.

The plant has hairy stems and basal leaves that are often lobed or toothed.
