Helenium glaucum

Helenium glaucum, commonly known as the blue helenium, is a striking perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, cone-shaped inflorescences. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers range in color from yellow to orange, with a distinctive blue-green hue in the center.

Helenium glaucum thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
