Hevea brasiliensis var. randiana

Hevea brasiliensis var. randiana, commonly known as the rubber tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon basin.

It is renowned for its latex-producing capabilities, which are harvested for natural rubber. The plant has large, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that contains seeds surrounded by a spongy, white pulp.

This variant is particularly valued for its high latex yield and adaptability to various climatic conditions.
