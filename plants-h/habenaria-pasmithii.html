Habenaria pasmithii

Habenaria pasmithii, commonly known as the Pasmith's orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air. Its flowers are usually pale green or white with a distinctive lip that is often yellow or orange.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
