Hieracium pseudokajanense

Hieracium pseudokajanense, commonly known as the Kajan's hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosettes of lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the genus Hieracium. It is native to specific regions in Central Asia and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
