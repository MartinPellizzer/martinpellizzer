Heteropterys discolor f. typica

Heteropterys discolor f. typica, commonly known as the variegated evergreen hibiscus, is a compact shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features broad, waxy leaves with striking variegation of green, white, and sometimes pink or red. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically blooming from spring to fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained, fertile soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
