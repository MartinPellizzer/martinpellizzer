Hakea cymbiformis

Hakea cymbiformis, commonly known as the flat-leaf hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers in winter, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is drought tolerant.

Hakea cymbiformis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscaping.
