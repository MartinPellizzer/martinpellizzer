Hieracium castriferrei

Hieracium castriferrei, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky soils, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

Hieracium castriferrei is known for its resilience to harsh climatic conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
