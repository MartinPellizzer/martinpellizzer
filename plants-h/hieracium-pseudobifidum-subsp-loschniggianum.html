Hieracium pseudobifidum subsp. loschniggianum

Hieracium pseudobifidum subsp.

loschniggianum, commonly known as the Loschnigg's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.

The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its inflorescence consists of a single flower per stem, with ray florets that are often shorter than the central disk.

This subspecies is notable for its horticultural value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental appearance.
