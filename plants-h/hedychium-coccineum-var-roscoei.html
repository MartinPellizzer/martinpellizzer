Hedychium coccineum var. roscoei

Hedychium coccineum var. roscoei, commonly known as the red flame ginger, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright red, fragrant flowers that bloom in summer, adding vibrant color to gardens. The plant has narrow, strap-like leaves that form a dense clump, and it produces tall, slender stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its dramatic floral displays.
