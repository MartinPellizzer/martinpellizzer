Hibiscus campestris

Hibiscus campestris, commonly known as the field hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, or white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and grows as an annual or perennial herb depending on the climate. Hibiscus campestris thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological landscapes.
