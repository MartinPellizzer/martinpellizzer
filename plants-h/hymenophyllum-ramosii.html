Hymenophyllum ramosii

Hymenophyllum ramosii, commonly known as the creeping maidenhair fern, is a small, epiphytic fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided, and often hairy leaves that are typically less than 2 centimeters in length. This species thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or rocks in tropical and subtropical regions. Its fronds are usually clustered at the base, forming a dense, mat-like growth.

The plant's unique morphology and habitat preferences make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native ecosystems.
