Hieracium abreptum

Hieracium abreptum, commonly known as the stinking hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, along roadsides, and in woodland edges. Its leaves are rough and hairy, and the flowers are yellow with a distinctive, unpleasant odor.

Hieracium abreptum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common weed in many regions.
