Hieracium brandisianum

Hieracium brandisianum, commonly known as the Brandisian hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in late summer. Native to Central Europe, it thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. The plant has small, hairy stems and is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
