Hieracium subcaesiiforme

Hieracium subcaesiiforme, commonly known as the pale yellow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive pale center.

Hieracium subcaesiiforme is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
