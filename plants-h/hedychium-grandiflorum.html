Hedychium grandiflorum

Hedychium grandiflorum, commonly known as the giant flame bamboo or golden torch ginger, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, bamboo-like stems that can reach heights of up to 6 meters. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers with a red or orange center, which are highly fragrant and attract pollinators. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth, glossy surface.

Hedychium grandiflorum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
