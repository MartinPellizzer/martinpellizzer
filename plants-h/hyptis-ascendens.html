Hyptis ascendens

Hyptis ascendens, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy blue-violet flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are square and upright.

Hyptis ascendens is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
