Hoya heuschkeliana subsp. marionii

Hoya heuschkeliana subsp.

marionii, commonly known as Marion's wax plant, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and pink.

The plant produces clusters of star-shaped, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular among indoor gardeners and plant enthusiasts.
