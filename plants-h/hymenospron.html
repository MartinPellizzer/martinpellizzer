Hymenospron

Hymenospron, commonly known as the golden mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees.

It has small, scale-like leaves and produces yellowish-orange berries that are toxic to many animals. The plant relies on its host for water and some nutrients, while it photosynthesizes to some extent. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in Southeast Asia.

Its unique ecological role makes it an interesting subject for botanical study.
