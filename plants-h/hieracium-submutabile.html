Hieracium submutabile

Hieracium submutabile, commonly known as the variable hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in meadows and grassy areas. The plant has yellow flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are usually hairy and lance-shaped. It is known for its variability in leaf and flower characteristics, which contributes to its common name.

Hieracium submutabile is often considered a weed but can also be found in cultivated gardens.
