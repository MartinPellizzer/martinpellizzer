Hesperis broussonetii

Hesperis broussonetii, commonly known as the Broussonet's candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant produces white or pale purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Hesperis broussonetii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in suitable climates.
