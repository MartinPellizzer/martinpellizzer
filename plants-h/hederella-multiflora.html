Hederella multiflora

Hederella multiflora, commonly known as the African ivy, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by orange berries. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Hederella multiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
