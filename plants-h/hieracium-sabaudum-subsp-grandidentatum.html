Hieracium sabaudum subsp. grandidentatum

Hieracium sabaudum subsp.

grandidentatum, commonly known as the large-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features bright yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, with petals that are deeply notched and often reflexed. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with hairy surfaces and toothed margins, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is characterized by its robust growth and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
