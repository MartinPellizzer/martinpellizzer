Helianthemum origanifolium var. dichotomum

Helianthemum origanifolium var. dichotomum, commonly known as the forked rockrose, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Europe.

It features small, fleshy leaves that are often glandular and have a distinctive aromatic scent. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
