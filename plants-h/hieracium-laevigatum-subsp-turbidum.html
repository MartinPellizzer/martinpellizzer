Hieracium laevigatum subsp. turbidum

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

turbidum, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and meadows, and prefers full sun to partial shade.

The plant features basal leaves that are broadly ovate to lanceolate with rough, hairy surfaces. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster, producing small, wind-pollinated flowers.

It is part of the Asteraceae family and is known for its aggressive growth habit, often forming dense colonies.
