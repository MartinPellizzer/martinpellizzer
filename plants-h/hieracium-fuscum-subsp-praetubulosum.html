Hieracium fuscum subsp. praetubulosum

Hieracium fuscum subsp.

praetubulosum, commonly known as the dark hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and southern Europe. It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features basal leaves that are lanceolate and hairy, with a flowering stem that bears yellowish to reddish flowers. Its inflorescence consists of a single capitulum surrounded by phyllaries, and it produces small, achene-like seeds.

This subspecies is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and is often found in disturbed or nutrient-rich habitats.
