Hibiscus ciliatus

Hibiscus ciliatus, commonly known as the velvetleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and a hairy texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in wetland or marshy areas.

Hibiscus ciliatus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
