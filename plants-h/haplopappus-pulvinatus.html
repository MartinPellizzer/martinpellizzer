Haplopappus pulvinatus

Haplopappus pulvinatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf pappus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive involucral bracts. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and poor soil conditions. Its foliage is narrow and linear, with small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring.

The species is known for its low-growing habit and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
