Heracleum pubescens var. wilhelmsii

Heracleum pubescens var. wilhelmsii, commonly known as the Wilhelmsii hogweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbrella-shaped inflorescences and hollow stems. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves with a pubescent surface, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. Due to its toxic sap, it can be harmful to humans and animals if ingested or contacted.
