Hieracium murorum subsp. dentifolium

Hieracium murorum subsp.

dentifolium, commonly known as the tooth-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies.

The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are deeply lobed and toothed, giving it its common name. Its flowering stems bear yellow daisy-like flowers with ray florets and a central disk.

The subspecies is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and is often found in temperate climates.
