Hydragonum calyculatum

Hydragonum calyculatum, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a unique flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, silver-green foliage and clusters of delicate, white to pale pink flowers. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its leaves are covered in a waxy cuticle that helps reduce water loss, an adaptation to its native climate.

Hydragonum calyculatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
