Hypsophila oppositifolia

Hypsophila oppositifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf or mountain grass, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its opposite leaf arrangement and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale pink. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments, often growing in rocky or alpine soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in a fine, velvety texture, contributing to its common name.

Hypsophila oppositifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
