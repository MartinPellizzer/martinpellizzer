Helichrysum concursum

Helichrysum concursum, commonly known as the golden marguerite or everlastings, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that remain vibrant for an extended period. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Helichrysum concursum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
