Hostana

Hostana, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bignoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, orange-yellow tubular flowers. The plant is a vigorous climber with long, twisting vines that can reach several meters in length. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often heart-shaped, providing a lush green backdrop to its vibrant blooms.

Hostana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical and subtropical regions.
