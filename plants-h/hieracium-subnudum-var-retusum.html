Hieracium subnudum var. retusum

Hieracium subnudum var. retusum, commonly known as the roughleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features basal rosettes of leaves that are rough and hairy, with a distinctive reddish tinge. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head, surrounded by a ring of pale yellow to white bracts.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
