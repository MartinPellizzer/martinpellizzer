Hordeum pubiflorum var. glabrum

Hordeum pubiflorum var. glabrum, commonly known as the glabrous barley, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless leaves and culms, distinguishing it from other varieties of Hordeum. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle with spikelets that are often tightly clustered.

It is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its potential use in sustainable agriculture.
