Hieracium rigidum subsp. trichocaulon

Hieracium rigidum subsp.

trichocaulon, commonly known as the stiff hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features upright, hairy stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces yellow flowers in early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
