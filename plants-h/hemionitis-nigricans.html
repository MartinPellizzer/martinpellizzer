Hemionitis nigricans

Hemionitis nigricans, commonly known as the black-stemmed fern, is a unique aquatic plant native to the southeastern United States.

It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and marshes, where it attaches itself to submerged surfaces. The plant has long, slender rhizomes that help it spread and anchor in the substrate. Its leaves are typically narrow and dark green, with a leathery texture that aids in water retention.

Hemionitis nigricans plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
