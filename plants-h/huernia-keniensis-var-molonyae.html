Huernia keniensis var. molonyae

Huernia keniensis var. molonyae, commonly known as the Kenyan moon cactus, is a succulent plant native to Kenya.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, segmented stems that resemble a moon's surface. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and sought-after species among succulent enthusiasts.
