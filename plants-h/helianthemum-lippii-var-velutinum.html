Helianthemum lippii var. velutinum

Helianthemum lippii var. velutinum, commonly known as the woolly rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its hairy, velvety stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its hardy nature and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant gardens.
