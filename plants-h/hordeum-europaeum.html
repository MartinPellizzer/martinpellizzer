Hordeum europaeum

Hordeum europaeum, commonly known as wild barley, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spikelet-like inflorescences. The plant produces both male and female flowers, with the female flowers typically located at the base of the spikelets. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a rough texture, and it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Hordeum europaeum plays a role in ecological succession and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
