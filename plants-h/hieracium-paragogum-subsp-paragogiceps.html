Hieracium paragogum subsp. paragogiceps

Hieracium paragogum subsp.

paragogiceps, commonly known as the long-horned hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and elongated, hairy stems that bear numerous yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It has small, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces and produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

This subspecies is native to parts of Europe and has been introduced to other regions.
