Homalocarpus dichotomus

Homalocarpus dichotomus, commonly known as the two-branched broom, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and pairs of opposite branches, giving it its specific epithet "dichotomus.". The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it is adapted to grow in coastal and sandy soils. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, and it is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardiness.

The species is also valued for its role in stabilizing coastal dunes and preventing erosion.
