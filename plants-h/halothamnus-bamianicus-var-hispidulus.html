Halothamnus bamianicus var. hispidulus

Halothamnus bamianicus var. hispidulus, commonly known as the Himalayan sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it a distinctive appearance. This plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found on rocky slopes and gravelly soils. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.

The species is well adapted to cold temperatures and can withstand harsh environmental conditions.
