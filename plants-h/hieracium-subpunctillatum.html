Hieracium subpunctillatum

Hieracium subpunctillatum, commonly known as the spotted hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are typically hairy and have a rough texture. Its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, often with a distinct central disk.

Hieracium subpunctillatum is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
