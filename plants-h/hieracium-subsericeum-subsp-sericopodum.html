Hieracium subsericeum subsp. sericopodum

Hieracium subsericeum subsp.

sericopodum, commonly known as the silky hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement.

The plant features hairy stems and leaves with a soft, silky texture, contributing to its common name. Its flowers are typically yellow, arranged in clusters, and bloom from late spring to early autumn.

It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, riverbanks, and along roadsides.
