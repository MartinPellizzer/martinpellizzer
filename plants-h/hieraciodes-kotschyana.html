Hieraciodes kotschyana

Hieraciodes kotschyana, commonly known as the Kashmir Himalayan bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

It is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in mountain ecosystems.
