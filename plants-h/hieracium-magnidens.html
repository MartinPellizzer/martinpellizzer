Hieracium magnidens

Hieracium magnidens, commonly known as the large-headed hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its robust growth and showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often hairy.

Hieracium magnidens is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in some regions.
