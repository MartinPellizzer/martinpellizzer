Hieracium tephrocephalum subsp. subrubellum

Hieracium tephrocephalum subsp.

subrubellum, commonly known as the red-centred hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosettes of leaves.

The flowers are yellow with a distinctive red center, and they are typically borne on long, slender peduncles. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

The subspecies is distinguished by its reddish tinge on the flower heads and slightly smaller leaves compared to other members of the species.
