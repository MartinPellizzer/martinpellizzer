Hermione callichroa

Hermione callichroa, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow, fluffy panicles that resemble raindrops, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are dark green and glossy on top and lighter underneath. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

The golden rain tree is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
