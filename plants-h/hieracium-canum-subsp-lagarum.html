Hieracium canum subsp. lagarum

Hieracium canum subsp.

lagarum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped with hairy surfaces, and the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bright blooms.
