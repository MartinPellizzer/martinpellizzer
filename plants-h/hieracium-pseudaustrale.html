Hieracium pseudaustrale

Hieracium pseudaustrale, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant has erect, hairy stems and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a composite head. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

Hieracium pseudaustrale is part of the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and wind-pollinated flowers.
