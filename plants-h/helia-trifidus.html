Helia trifidus

Helia trifidus, commonly known as the three-lobed sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers and narrow, trifid (three-lobed) leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in garden settings.
