Habenaria amambayensis

Habenaria amambayensis, commonly known as the Amambay orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Paraguay.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers with three sepals and three petals, typically arranged in a raceme. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is adapted to the humid conditions of its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
