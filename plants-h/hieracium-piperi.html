Hieracium piperi

Hieracium piperi, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium piperi is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and seeds, making it a common weed in many regions.
