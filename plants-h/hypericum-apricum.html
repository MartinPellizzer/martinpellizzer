Hypericum apricum

Hypericum apricum, commonly known as the golden-stemmed St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its stems are woody and often have a reddish or purplish tinge.

Hypericum apricum is a member of the Hypericaceae family and is known for its medicinal properties, though it contains compounds that can be toxic if ingested in large amounts.
