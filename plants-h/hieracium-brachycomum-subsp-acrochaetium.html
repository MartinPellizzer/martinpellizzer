Hieracium brachycomum subsp. acrochaetium

Hieracium brachycomum subsp.

acrochaetium, commonly known as the short-headed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands, roadsides, and waste areas.

The plant features upright stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow, with a distinctive, short, and broad tubular shape, and it produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
