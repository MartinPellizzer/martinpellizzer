Hieracium caesium subsp. megistocladum

Hieracium caesium subsp.

megistocladum, commonly known as the giant hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features a robust root system and upright stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by leaf-like bracts. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and often have a reddish or purplish tinge.

This subspecies is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences and is often found in meadows and disturbed soils.
