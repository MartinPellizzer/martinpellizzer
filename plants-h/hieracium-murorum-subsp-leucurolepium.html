Hieracium murorum subsp. leucurolepium

Hieracium murorum subsp.

leucurolepium, commonly known as the white-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy, often lobed leaves.

The subspecies is distinguished by its pale or white leaf surfaces, which set it apart from other hawkweed varieties. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in areas with moderate moisture levels.
