Herminium angustifolium var. souliei

Herminium angustifolium var. souliei, commonly known as the narrow-leaved twayblade, is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is a small, perennial herb that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features a slender, leafless stem with two opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in a spike and characterized by their spurred petals.

This variety is notable for its delicate appearance and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
