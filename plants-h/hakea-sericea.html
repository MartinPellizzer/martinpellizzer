Hakea sericea

Hakea sericea, commonly known as silky hakea, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, silvery-green leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces cylindrical flower spikes that range in color from white to pink, which bloom in spring. It is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

Hakea sericea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for native wildlife.
