Hieracium cordifolium proles eriocerinthe

Hieracium cordifolium proles eriocerinthe, commonly known as the woolly heartleaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, giving it a woolly appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flower heads that are typically clustered in a composite inflorescence. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
