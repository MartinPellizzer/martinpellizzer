Heliconia wilsonii

Heliconia wilsonii, commonly known as the Wilson's heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble flowers. The plant has long, narrow leaves that form a rosette, and its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by brightly colored bracts. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Heliconia wilsonii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
