Hemigyrosa canescens

Hemigyrosa canescens, commonly known as the hairy hemicycle, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant is notable for its hairy, grayish-green foliage, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hemigyrosa canescens is often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
