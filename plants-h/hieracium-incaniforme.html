Hieracium incaniforme

Hieracium incaniforme, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. It is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters.

Due to its ornamental appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
