Hippeastrum mollevillquense

Hippeastrum mollevillquense, commonly known as the Mollevillquense amaryllis, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, large, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. The plant typically produces multiple blooms during the growing season, often in the spring or early summer. Its foliage consists of broad, strap-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
