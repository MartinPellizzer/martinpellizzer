Hibiscus corditectus

Hibiscus corditectus, commonly known as the heartleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Hibiscus corditectus is a herbaceous perennial that produces seeds in capsule fruits after flowering.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
