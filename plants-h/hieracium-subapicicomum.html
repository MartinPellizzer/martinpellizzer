Hieracium subapicicomum

Hieracium subapicicomum, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium subapicicomum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain habitats.
