Helichrysum elephantinum

Helichrysum elephantinum, commonly known as the golden marguerite or the elephant's head, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb with a distinctive, bushy appearance and produces bright yellow flowers that resemble small suns. The plant is characterized by its hairy, silver-green foliage and a strong, aromatic scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Helichrysum elephantinum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
