Hieracium murorum subsp. malacodiaphanum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

malacodiaphanum, commonly known as the translucent false dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

The plant has finely divided, grass-like leaves and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows, and is often found in moist, calcareous soils.

Its subspecies name, *malacodiaphanum*, refers to its soft, translucent leaf blades, which distinguish it from other *Hieracium* species.
