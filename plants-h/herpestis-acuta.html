Herpestis acuta

Herpestis acuta, commonly known as the acute bittercress, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It is a small annual herb that typically grows in moist, disturbed soils, often found along roadsides and in fields. The plant features slender stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy. Its small white or pale yellow flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom in early spring.

The fruit is a slender, elongated pod that splits open to release the seeds.
