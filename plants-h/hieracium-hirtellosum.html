Hieracium hirtellosum

Hieracium hirtellosum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are hairy and serrated, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium hirtellosum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
