Hypericum humifusum var. ambiguum

Hypericum humifusum var. ambiguum, commonly known as the creeping St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to North America.

It features woody stems that lie close to the ground, often forming dense mats. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and have a slightly serrated edge.

This variety is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.
