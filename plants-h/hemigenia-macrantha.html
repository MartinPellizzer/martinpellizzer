Hemigenia macrantha

Hemigenia macrantha, commonly known as the large-flowered hemigenia, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and understoreys. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and it produces small, dark berries as fruit.

Hemigenia macrantha is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
