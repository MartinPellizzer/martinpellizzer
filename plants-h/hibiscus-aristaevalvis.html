Hibiscus aristaevalvis

Hibiscus aristaevalvis, commonly known as the Valdivian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the temperate rainforests of southern South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range from white to pink or red, with five petals and a prominent central pistil. The plant has heart-shaped, broad leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is adapted to the cool, humid climates of its native region.

Hibiscus aristaevalvis plays an important role in its ecosystem, providing nectar for pollinators and shelter for various wildlife species.
