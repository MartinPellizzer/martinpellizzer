Hemisphaera ciliata

Hemisphaera ciliata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a distinctive reddish or purple coloration on the underside. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, often with a slight fragrance. Hemisphaera ciliata is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields, and it can become invasive in some areas.
