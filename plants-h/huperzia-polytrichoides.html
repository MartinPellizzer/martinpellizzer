Huperzia polytrichoides

Huperzia polytrichoides, commonly known as the giant clubmoss, is a species of fern ally belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and dense, needle-like leaves that form a compact, mat-like structure. Native to tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its reproductive structures, known as sporangia, are clustered in strobili at the tips of the leaves.

Huperzia polytrichoides plays an important ecological role in forest understories by contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
