Hieracium caesium subsp. caesiopsis

Hieracium caesium subsp.

caesiopsis, commonly known as the purple hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and features showy, purple to reddish-pink composite flowers.

The plant typically has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy, while the upper leaves are smaller and more narrow. It thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats, often found in meadows and along roadsides.

This subspecies is native to Europe and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
