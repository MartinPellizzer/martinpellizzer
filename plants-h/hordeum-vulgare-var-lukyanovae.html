Hordeum vulgare var. lukyanovae

Hordeum vulgare var. lukyanovae, commonly known as Lukyanov's barley, is a variant of barley characterized by its unique botanical features.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is notable for its morphological distinctiveness compared to other barley varieties. The plant typically exhibits a compact growth habit with shorter stalks and a higher concentration of grains per spike. Its genetic makeup includes traits that make it particularly valuable for breeding programs aimed at improving drought tolerance and yield.

This variety is also of interest for its potential in sustainable agriculture due to its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
