Hieracium inuloides subsp. polycomatum

Hieracium inuloides subsp.

polycomatum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disc florets.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, exhibiting a sprawling growth habit. Its leaves are lance-shaped with hairy surfaces, and the flower heads are yellow with a central disc.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
