Heliotropium flaviflorum

Heliotropium flaviflorum, commonly known as the yellow-flowered heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to yellow, often with a purple or pinkish hue. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters, and has hairy, branching stems. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with fine hairs, while the flowers emit a mild fragrance.

Heliotropium flaviflorum is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in warm, sunny environments.
