Hieracium subrigidiforme

Hieracium subrigidiforme, commonly known as the rigid-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring well-drained conditions. Its leaves are usually rigid and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
