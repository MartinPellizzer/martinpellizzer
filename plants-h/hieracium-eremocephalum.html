Hieracium eremocephalum

Hieracium eremocephalum, commonly known as the barrenhead hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky, or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive, sparsely haired head.

Hieracium eremocephalum is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
