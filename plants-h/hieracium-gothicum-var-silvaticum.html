Hieracium gothicum var. silvaticum

Hieracium gothicum var. silvaticum, commonly known as the forest hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. It has lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces and produces a distinctive, slightly aromatic scent.

Its ecological role includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a habitat for various invertebrates.
