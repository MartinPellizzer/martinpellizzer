Hieracium sericophyllum subsp. acropolioscapum

Hieracium sericophyllum subsp.

acropolioscapum, commonly known as the Alpine Groundsel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-green leaves and small yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.

This subspecies is distinguished by its unique floral structure and habitat preference, making it a notable species in alpine flora.
