Hartmannia corymbosa

Hartmannia corymbosa, commonly known as the pink lady's slipper, is a tropical flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking pink and white petals that resemble a slipper. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are often clustered in corymbs, giving it the species name *corymbosa*.

Hartmannia corymbosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
