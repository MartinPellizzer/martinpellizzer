Hyacinthus italicus

Hyacinthus italicus, commonly known as the Italian hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It produces dense clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers in early spring. The plant belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its strap-like, grassy foliage. Its bulbs are perennial and can be propagated through division or seed.

The flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their vibrant color and strong scent.
