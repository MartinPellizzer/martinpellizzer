Hippeastrum auratum

Hippeastrum auratum, commonly known as the golden hurricane lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and large, showy flowers. The plant produces vibrant orange to red blooms with a distinctive yellow center, which appear in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are broad, strap-like, and emerge from a basal rosette of fleshy, white or pale green foliage.

Hippeastrum auratum is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
