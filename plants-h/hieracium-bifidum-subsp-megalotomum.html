Hieracium bifidum subsp. megalotomum

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

megalotomum, commonly known as the giant false dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows.

The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head, with ray florets that are usually shorter than the central disk.

It is characterized by its large, showy petals and is often found in moist or calcareous soils.
