Hydnocarpus ovoideus

Hydnocarpus ovoideus, commonly known as the oval-fruit tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and woody fruits. The plant produces oblong, woody capsules that contain seeds surrounded by a fleshy, sticky aril. Its bark and seeds have been used in traditional medicine for their purported medicinal properties.
