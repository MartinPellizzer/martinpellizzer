Hypericum fursei

Hypericum fursei, commonly known as the Corsican St. John's Wort, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are dark green and slightly hairy, while its stems are woody and often branched.

Hypericum fursei is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
