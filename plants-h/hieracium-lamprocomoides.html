Hieracium lamprocomoides

Hieracium lamprocomoides, commonly known as the silverleaf hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads made up of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with a silvery or grayish underside, giving it its common name.

The flowers are yellow, appearing in clusters and blooming from late spring to early summer.
