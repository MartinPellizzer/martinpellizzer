Hopea gratissima

Hopea gratissima, commonly known as the yellow myrtle, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and dense, dark green foliage. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or yellow, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.

Hopea gratissima plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, contributing to biodiversity and soil stability.
