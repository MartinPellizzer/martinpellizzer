Hedyotis strigulosa var. luxurians

Hedyotis strigulosa var. luxurians, commonly known as the glossy hedyotis, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, giving them a glossy appearance. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters and blooming in late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and modest floral display.
