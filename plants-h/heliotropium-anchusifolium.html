Heliotropium anchusifolium

Heliotropium anchusifolium, commonly known as the anchusleaf heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit and produces small, violet or purple flowers. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape, with a hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The plant is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental species.
