Hypericum virginicum var. japonicum

Hypericum virginicum var. japonicum, commonly known as Japanese St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub with woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in cymes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in woodland edges and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
