Hydrophila

Hydrophila, commonly known as water willow, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It typically grows in shallow water or along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often submerged or floating, aiding in photosynthesis in aquatic conditions. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually greenish-white in color, and appear in summer.

Hydrophila plays an important role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
