Hostia rubra

Hostia rubra, commonly known as the red hosta, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asparagaceae.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, and is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves that are typically green with a red or purple hue on the underside. Hostia rubra prefers partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value lies in its striking foliage and delicate floral display.
