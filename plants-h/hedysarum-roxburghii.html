Hedysarum roxburghii

Hedysarum roxburghii, commonly known as the Chinese milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to regions of Asia, including China, India, and the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant produces cylindrical pods containing several seeds, and it is often found in dry, rocky, or sandy soils. Hedysarum roxburghii is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment and ecological restoration projects.

Its adaptability to harsh environments makes it an important species in arid and semi-arid regions.
