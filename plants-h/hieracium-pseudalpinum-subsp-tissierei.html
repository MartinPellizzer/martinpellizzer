Hieracium pseudalpinum subsp. tissierei

Hieracium pseudalpinum subsp.

tissierei, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, often in mountainous areas.

The plant has basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in late summer. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized species within its range.
