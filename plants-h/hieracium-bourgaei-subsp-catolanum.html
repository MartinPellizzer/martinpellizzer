Hieracium bourgaei subsp. catolanum

Hieracium bourgaei subsp.

catolanum, commonly known as the Corsican hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. Its leaves are usually lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is a relatively small plant, rarely exceeding 30 cm in height, and is known for its hardy nature and adaptability to harsh environments.
