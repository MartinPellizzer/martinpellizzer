Hackelia coerulescens

Hackelia coerulescens, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have serrated edges, while its root system is fibrous and rhizomatous.

Hackelia coerulescens thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland habitats.
