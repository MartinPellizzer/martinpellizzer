Hieracium pexum

Hieracium pexum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky and mountainous habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium pexum is known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand harsh alpine conditions.
