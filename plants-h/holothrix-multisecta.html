Holothrix multisecta

Holothrix multisecta, commonly known as the multifurrowed holothrix, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental, lance-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of purple and white, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its unique foliage and delicate flowers make it a valuable addition to both cultivated and natural landscapes.
