Hymenocardia wallichii var. dasycarpa

Hymenocardia wallichii var. dasycarpa, commonly known as the rough-fruited sandalwood, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant flowers. The plant produces woody, spherical fruits that are typically covered in a rough, hairy texture. Its bark is aromatic and has been traditionally used in perfumery and traditional medicine.

This variant is distinguished by its unique fruit morphology compared to other varieties of Hymenocardia.
