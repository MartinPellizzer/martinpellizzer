Hura crepitans var. ovata

Hura crepitans var. ovata, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, palmate leaves. The plant produces showy, red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by distinctive, woody pods that split open to release its seeds.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental tree in tropical and subtropical regions.
