Hieracium magyaricum subsp. kerneri

Hieracium magyaricum subsp.

kerneri, commonly known as the Hungarian hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to reddish flowers and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in the summer months.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and locally endemic species.
