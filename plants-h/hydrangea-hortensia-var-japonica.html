Hydrangea hortensia var. japonica

Hydrangea hortensia var. japonica, commonly known as the Japanese hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flower clusters that can range in color from blue to pink, depending on soil pH. The plant produces bract-like flowers that are actually sterile, with true flowers located in the center. It thrives in partial shade and requires well-drained, acidic soil for optimal growth.

This variety is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to change flower color with seasonal conditions.
