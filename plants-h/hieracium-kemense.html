Hieracium kemense

Hieracium kemense, commonly known as the Turkish hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Turkey and the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range in color from yellow to orange.

Hieracium kemense is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
