Hakea betchei

Hakea betchei, commonly known as the Blackboy hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves and striking, cone-shaped inflorescences that produce bright red or orange flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought resistance through its succulent stems and reduced leaf surface. Its flowering period typically occurs in spring, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects.

Hakea betchei is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
