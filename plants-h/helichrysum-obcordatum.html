Helichrysum obcordatum

Helichrysum obcordatum, commonly known as the golden marguerite or the Cape marguerite, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, yellow-orange flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Helichrysum obcordatum is also known for its ability to produce long-lasting dried flowers, which are used in floral arrangements.
