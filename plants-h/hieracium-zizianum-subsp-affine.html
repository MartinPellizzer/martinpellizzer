Hieracium zizianum subsp. affine

Hieracium zizianum subsp.

affine, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats such as meadows and roadsides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

It is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common invasive species in certain regions.
