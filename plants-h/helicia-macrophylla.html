Helicia macrophylla

Helicia macrophylla, commonly known as the giant helicia, is a striking evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that can grow up to 20 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and landscaped areas.
