Hammada multiflora

Hammada multiflora, commonly known as the desert broom, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, gray-green foliage and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that help reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. It produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Hammada multiflora plays a vital role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
