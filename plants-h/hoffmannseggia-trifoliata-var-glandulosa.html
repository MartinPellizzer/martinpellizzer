Hoffmannseggia trifoliata var. glandulosa

Hoffmannseggia trifoliata var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular leafed hoffmannseggia, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are divided into three leaflets, and the presence of glandular structures on its foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it adapted to harsh, dry environments.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
