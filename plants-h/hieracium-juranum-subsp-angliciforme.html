Hieracium juranum subsp. angliciforme

Hieracium juranum subsp.

angliciforme, commonly known as the English dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves.

The plant produces a single, hollow, yellow flower head that develops into a spherical seed dispersal structure called a pappus. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its botanical features make it a common and recognizable weed in temperate regions.
