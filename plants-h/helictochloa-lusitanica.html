Helictochloa lusitanica

Helictochloa lusitanica, commonly known as the Iberian needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as rocky slopes and sandy soils.

Helictochloa lusitanica is often found in Mediterranean climates and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
