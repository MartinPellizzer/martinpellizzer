Heterocentron purpureum

Heterocentron purpureum, commonly known as the purple fringed gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers with distinctive purple fringes along the edges. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of downy hairs.

The species is notable for its short blooming period, usually occurring in late summer to early autumn.
