Hierochloe alpina var. monstruosa

Hierochloe alpina var. monstruosa, commonly known as the "monstrous bluegrass," is a striking variant of the alpine bluegrass species.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and vibrant blue-green foliage. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves with a fine texture and a distinctive silver-blue hue. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is cultivated in gardens for its visual interest and hardy nature.
