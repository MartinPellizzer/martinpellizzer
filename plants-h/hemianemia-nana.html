Hemianemia nana

Hemianemia nana, commonly known as the dwarf hemianthus, is a small, herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features compact growth with short, spreading stems and small, rounded leaves that are typically a bright green color. The plant produces tiny, white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive and easy-to-care-for addition to both wild and cultivated landscapes.
