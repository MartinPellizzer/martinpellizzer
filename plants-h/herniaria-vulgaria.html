Herniaria vulgaria

Herniaria vulgaria, commonly known as mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas or on disturbed ground. Its compact growth habit and ability to withstand harsh conditions make it a resilient species in various habitats.

Herniaria vulgaria is also known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.
