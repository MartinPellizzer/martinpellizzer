Hieracium diaphanoides subsp. pycnadenium

Hieracium diaphanoides subsp.

pycnadenium, commonly known as the denseflower hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along streambanks, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish to reddish flowers in the summer months. Its flowering stems are often covered with fine hairs, and the leaves are usually lance-shaped with a rough texture.

This subspecies is distinguished by its dense inflorescence and is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads.
