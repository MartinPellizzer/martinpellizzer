Haageocereus piliger

Haageocereus piliger, commonly known as the white night bloomer, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems and white, star-shaped flowers that bloom at night. The plant has spiny areoles and produces yellow, woolly bracts beneath its flowers. It thrives in arid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its nocturnal blooming habit, it is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
