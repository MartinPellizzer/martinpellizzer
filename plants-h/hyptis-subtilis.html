Hyptis subtilis

Hyptis subtilis, commonly known as sweet hyptis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale purple flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It is often found in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Hyptis subtilis is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
