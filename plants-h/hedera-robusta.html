Hedera robusta

Hedera robusta, commonly known as the robust ivy, is a climbing evergreen vine native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly waxy texture and can grow up to 30 feet in length. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by black, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often used in hedges or as ground cover.

Hedera robusta is known for its adaptability and ability to climb walls or trees, making it a popular ornamental plant.
