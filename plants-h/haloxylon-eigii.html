Haloxylon eigii

Haloxylon eigii, commonly known as the desert date tree, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is well adapted to harsh desert conditions, with deep roots and a thick, waxy coating on its leaves to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, date-like fruits that are important for both wildlife and humans. Its hardy nature makes it a valuable species for stabilizing sandy soils and preventing desertification.

Haloxylon eigii is also used in traditional medicine and as a source of firewood in some regions.
