Hippocrepis ciliata var. biciliata

Hippocrepis ciliata var. biciliata, commonly known as the double-fringed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a silvery or greyish underside due to fine hairs. This variety is a subspecies of Hippocrepis ciliata, distinguished by its more pronounced fringing on the petals.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.
