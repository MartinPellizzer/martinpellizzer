Hyptis arborescens

Hyptis arborescens, commonly known as the tree hyptis, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue to purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or disturbed areas.

Hyptis arborescens is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
