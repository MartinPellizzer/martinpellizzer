Hieracium olivaceum subsp. cupulatum

Hieracium olivaceum subsp.

cupulatum, commonly known as the olive hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

This subspecies is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
