Hieracium lailanum

Hieracium lailanum, commonly known as the Lailan hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers with ray florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are often reddish or purple in color.

Hieracium lailanum is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain habitats.
