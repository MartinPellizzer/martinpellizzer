Hieracium argospathum

Hieracium argospathum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers consist of yellow disk florets surrounded by pale green to white ray florets.

Hieracium argospathum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
