Hypericum hyssopifolium var. abbreviatum

Hypericum hyssopifolium var. abbreviatum, commonly known as the dwarf St. John's wort, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.

Its compact growth habit makes it an excellent choice for rock gardens or as a ground cover in woodland settings.
