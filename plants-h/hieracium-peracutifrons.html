Hieracium peracutifrons

Hieracium peracutifrons, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

Hieracium peracutifrons is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
