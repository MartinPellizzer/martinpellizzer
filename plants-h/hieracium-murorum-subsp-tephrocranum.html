Hieracium murorum subsp. tephrocranum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

tephrocranum, commonly known as the tephrocranum hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in dry, calcareous soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

The plant has erect, hairy stems that can reach up to 30 cm in height, and its leaves are lanceolate with rough, hairy surfaces. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and are accompanied by bracts.

This subspecies is characterized by its distinctive, pale greenish-brown stem that is often covered in a powdery coating.
