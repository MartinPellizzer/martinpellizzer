Hieracium auriculoides subsp. rubropannonicum

Hieracium auriculoides subsp.

rubropannonicum, commonly known as the red Pannonian hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellowish to orange flowers.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are usually hairy as well. This subspecies is often found in meadows and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is part of the diverse flora of its native habitat.
