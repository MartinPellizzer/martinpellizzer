Hakea scabra

Hakea scabra, commonly known as the rough hakea, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, spiny foliage that provides habitat for various small animals. The plant produces yellow flowers in winter, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and covered in fine, rough hairs, giving it a distinctive texture.

Hakea scabra is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
