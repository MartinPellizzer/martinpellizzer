Hieracium aetheorhizoides

Hieracium aetheorhizoides, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its yellow flowers bloom in the summer, and it has hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy layer.

Hieracium aetheorhizoides is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in various habitats.
