Helichrysum discolorum

Helichrysum discolorum, commonly known as the Variable Immortelle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its yellow to orange-yellow flowers that retain their color even when dried, giving it the common name "immortelle.". The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, daisy-like flowers are arranged in clusters, and the leaves are usually linear or lance-shaped.

Helichrysum discolorum is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
