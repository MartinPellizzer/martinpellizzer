Helianthus maximiliani var. iubaris

Helianthus maximiliani var. iubaris, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a vibrant annual plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It features large, showy yellow-orange flower heads with dark red to black central disks, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 6 feet, with hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its rapid growth and long bloom period make it an excellent addition to both ornamental and ecological gardens.
