Helichrysum gaharoense

Helichrysum gaharoense, commonly known as the Gaharo golden bush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of yellow-orange, papery flowers that resemble small sunflowers. The plant is typically shrub-like, with narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Helichrysum gaharoense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
