Helichrysum heliotropifolium

Helichrysum heliotropifolium, commonly known as the golden everlasting or sunleaf, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of small, yellow to orange flowers that resemble sunflowers. The plant is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and chrysanthemums, and is known for its aromatic, resinous scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for garden cultivation.
