Hieracium canum subsp. almonicum

Hieracium canum subsp.

almonicum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in rocky and alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
