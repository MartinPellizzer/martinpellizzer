Helepta grandiflora

Helepta grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered heather, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Helepta grandiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its winter blooming habit.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small wildlife.
