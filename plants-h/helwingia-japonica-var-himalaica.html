Helwingia japonica var. himalaica

Helwingia japonica var. himalaica, commonly known as the Himalayan helwingia, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, ovate leaves that are typically pubescent on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by distinctive, winged fruits that are characteristic of the genus. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas at high elevations.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
