Hedychium flavescens var. chrysoleucum

Hedychium flavescens var. chrysoleucum, commonly known as the golden flame ginger, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers with a distinctive orange center, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has long, narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are dark green and often have a metallic sheen. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and as a cut flower.
