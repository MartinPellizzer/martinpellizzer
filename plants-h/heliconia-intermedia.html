Heliconia intermedia

Heliconia intermedia, commonly known as the red heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful bracts that resemble petals. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that are often hidden within the leaf-like structures. Heliconia intermedia is well-known for its ornamental value and is frequently cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.

Its unique morphology and tropical appearance make it a popular choice for both horticultural and ecological studies.
