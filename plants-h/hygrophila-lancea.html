Hygrophila lancea

Hygrophila lancea, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hygrophila, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant that typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant features slender, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, white or pale pink flowers. It is often used in aquatic gardens and as a decorative plant in water features.

Hygrophila lancea is also valued for its ability to improve water quality in aquatic ecosystems.
