Hieracium formosum

Hieracium formosum, commonly known as the showy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in late summer, and the plant produces seeds in a single, showy flower head.

Hieracium formosum is often found in open habitats and can sometimes become invasive in certain regions.
