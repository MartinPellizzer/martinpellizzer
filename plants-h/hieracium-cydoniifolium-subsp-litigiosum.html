Hieracium cydoniifolium subsp. litigiosum

Hieracium cydoniifolium subsp.

litigiosum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its basal rosette of broadly ovate, hairy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in moist grasslands, meadows, and along stream banks. Its flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and the leaves have a distinctive waxy coating.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
