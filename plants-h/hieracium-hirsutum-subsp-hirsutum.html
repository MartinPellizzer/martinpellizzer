Hieracium hirsutum subsp. hirsutum

Hieracium hirsutum subsp.

hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

The plant features basal rosettes of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in a single terminal inflorescence. Its flowers are typically tubular with five lobes and are visited by various pollinators such as bees and flies.

The species is characterized by its robust growth and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
