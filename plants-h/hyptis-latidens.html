Hyptis latidens

Hyptis latidens, commonly known as broadleaf hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

Its leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
