Hemigenia curvifolia

Hemigenia curvifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments, such as along stream banks and in rainforest understoreys. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a distinctive aromatic scent.

The flowers, which bloom in spring, are usually pink or purple and attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.
