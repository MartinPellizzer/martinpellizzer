Helictotrichon aetolicum

Helictotrichon aetolicum, commonly known as the Aetolic hairgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically forms dense, tufted clumps with slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various insect species.
