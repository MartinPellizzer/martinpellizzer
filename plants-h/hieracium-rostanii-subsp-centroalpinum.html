Hieracium rostanii subsp. centroalpinum

Hieracium rostanii subsp.

centroalpinum, commonly known as the Central Alps groundsel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe. It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils.

The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow flowers in late summer. Its flowering stems are often hairy and can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters.

This subspecies is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal potential in traditional herbal practices.
