Hordeum murinum var. pseudomurinum

Hordeum murinum var. pseudomurinum, commonly known as mouse barley, is a variant of the wild barley species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its slender stalks and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and other open habitats. Its seeds are small and often dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.

The botanical features of this variety make it an important species in ecological studies and agricultural research.
