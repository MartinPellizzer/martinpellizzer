Hoarea auriculata

Hoarea auriculata, commonly known as the auriculate bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Hoarea auriculata is often cultivated for its attractive appearance and ease of care.
