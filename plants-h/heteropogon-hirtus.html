Heteropogon hirtus

Heteropogon hirtus, commonly known as the hairy grass or hairy panic grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with spikelets that are often hairy as well. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along waterways.

Heteropogon hirtus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
