Hieracium gulldalense

Hieracium gulldalense, commonly known as the Gulldal's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disc.

Hieracium gulldalense is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its bright, showy blooms.
