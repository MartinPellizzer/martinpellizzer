Hydrocotyle rugulosa

Hydrocotyle rugulosa, commonly known as the silky water shield, is a perennial aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow water, wetlands, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has finely divided, arrow-shaped leaves that are covered in a soft, silky texture. Its small white flowers appear in clusters and bloom from late spring to early summer.

Hydrocotyle rugulosa plays an important role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
