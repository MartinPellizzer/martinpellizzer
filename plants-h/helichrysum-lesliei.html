Helichrysum lesliei

Helichrysum lesliei, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that remain vibrant even after drying. The plant typically grows in coastal regions and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. Its foliage is aromatic and often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
