Hieracium murorum subsp. sericellipes

Hieracium murorum subsp.

sericellipes, commonly known as the woolly mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small yellow flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dense, woolly pubescence, which covers the stems and leaves, providing a unique texture. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

This plant is relatively hardy and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
