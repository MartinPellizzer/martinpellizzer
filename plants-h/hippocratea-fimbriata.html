Hippocratea fimbriata

Hippocratea fimbriata, commonly known as the lace-leaf orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elegant, elongated leaves that have a finely serrated edge, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often with a distinctive lip petal. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts worldwide.
