Helleborus casta-diva subsp. kochii

Helleborus casta-diva subsp.

kochii, commonly known as the Koch's Christmas rose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Alps. It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves and its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range from white to pale pink.

The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, making it one of the earliest flowering plants in its habitat. It prefers cool, moist, and well-drained soil in partial to full shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
