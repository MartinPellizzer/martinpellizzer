Helichrysum summo-montanum

Helichrysum summo-montanum, commonly known as the mountain everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant is known for its bright yellow to orange flowers, which are often used in floral arrangements due to their longevity. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy species adapted to harsh mountain environments.

Its unique morphology and resilience contribute to its ecological significance in alpine ecosystems.
