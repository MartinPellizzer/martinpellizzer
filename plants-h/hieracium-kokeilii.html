Hieracium kokeilii

Hieracium kokeilii, commonly known as the Kokeil's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan. The plant features a low-growing rosette of basal leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in the summer months. It thrives in rocky and alpine environments, often growing at elevations above 2,000 meters.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
