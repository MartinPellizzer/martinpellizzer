Hieracium waldsteinii subsp. baldaccianum

Hieracium waldsteinii subsp.

baldaccianum, commonly known as the Waldstein's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaf arrangement.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded conditions. Its leaves are basal, lanceolate, and often covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

This subspecies is distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and subtle morphological differences from other hawkweed species.
