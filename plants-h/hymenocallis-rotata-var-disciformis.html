Hymenocallis rotata var. disciformis

Hymenocallis rotata var. disciformis, commonly known as the disc-shaped spider lily, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It features striking white or pale pink flowers with a distinctive, rounded shape that resembles a spider's web. The plant has strap-like, narrow leaves that are often bluish-green in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
