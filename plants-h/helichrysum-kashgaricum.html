Helichrysum kashgaricum

Helichrysum kashgaricum, commonly known as the Kashgar everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, fluffy inflorescences that resemble dandelion flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers that dry into fluffy, white or pale yellow structures, giving it a long-lasting appearance. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Helichrysum kashgaricum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
