Hieracium longatriceps

Hieracium longatriceps, commonly known as the long-headed hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive elongated central disk.

Hieracium longatriceps is often found in sunny areas and can spread readily through its rhizomatous growth habit.
