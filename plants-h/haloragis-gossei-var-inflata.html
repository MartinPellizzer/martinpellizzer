Haloragis gossei var. inflata

Haloragis gossei var. inflata, commonly known as the inflated willowherb, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This species thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or inland regions with well-drained conditions.

It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native insects and birds.
