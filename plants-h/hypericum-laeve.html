Hypericum laeve

Hypericum laeve, commonly known as smooth St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and alternate leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. Its botanical features include small, bead-like glands on the stems and leaves, and it is part of the genus Hypericum, which is known for its medicinal properties.

The plant is often found in sunny, well-drained soils in woodland or grassland habitats.
