Hieracium chalcidicum subsp. divaricatum

Hieracium chalcidicum subsp.

divaricatum, commonly known as the Divaricated Hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade.

The plant features basal rosettes of leaves and produces yellow flowers in a dense cluster at the end of its stem. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with a hairy surface, and the flowers are composed of several ray florets.

This subspecies is part of the genus Hieracium, which includes many species of hawkweeds known for their showy, daisy-like flowers.
