Hoya brittonii

Hoya brittonii, commonly known as the Britton's waxplant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy, and often variegated leaves that are typically heart-shaped or ovate. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale pink, often with a subtle fragrance. Hoya brittonii is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its relatively slow growth rate.

It is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ornamental appeal and ease of care.
