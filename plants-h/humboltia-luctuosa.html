Humboltia luctuosa

Humboltia luctuosa, commonly known as the weeping lantana, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and is characterized by its drooping, fragrant inflorescences. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and often covered in fine hairs.

Humboltia luctuosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
