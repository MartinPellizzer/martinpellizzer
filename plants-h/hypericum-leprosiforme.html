Hypericum leprosiforme

Hypericum leprosiforme, commonly known as the scaly St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems covered in a distinctive scaly, pale yellow or brownish layer, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a glandular appearance.

Hypericum leprosiforme is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing a robust and resilient botanical structure.
