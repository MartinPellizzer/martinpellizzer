Hieracium boixarense

Hieracium boixarense, commonly known as the Boixar Valley hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a single head, with ray florets that are often shorter than the central disk florets.

Hieracium boixarense is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its potential use in traditional herbal medicine.
