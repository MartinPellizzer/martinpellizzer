Hermannia bicolor

Hermannia bicolor, commonly known as the red and white bellflower, is a striking herbaceous plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-toned flowers, which feature a deep red lower lip and a white upper lip, creating a bell-shaped appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its leaves are usually opposite, ovate, and have a velvety texture.

Hermannia bicolor is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is popular in horticulture for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
