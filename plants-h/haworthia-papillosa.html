Haworthia papillosa

Haworthia papillosa, commonly known as the pink haworthia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rosette-shaped leaves. The leaves are fleshy and covered with white tubercles, which give the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

This plant is popular among succulent enthusiasts due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
