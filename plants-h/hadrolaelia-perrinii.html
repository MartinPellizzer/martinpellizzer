Hadrolaelia perrinii

Hadrolaelia perrinii, commonly known as the Perrin's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow lip. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
