Hordeum imberbe

Hordeum imberbe, commonly known as wild barley, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Hordeum imberbe plays an important ecological role as a food source for various wildlife species.
