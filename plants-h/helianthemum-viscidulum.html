Helianthemum viscidulum

Helianthemum viscidulum, commonly known as sticky rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub with small, evergreen leaves that are covered in fine, sticky hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens as an ornamental plant.
