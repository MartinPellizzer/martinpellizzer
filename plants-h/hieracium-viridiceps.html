Hieracium viridiceps

Hieracium viridiceps, commonly known as the green-headed hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a green center.

Hieracium viridiceps is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common but sometimes invasive species in certain regions.
