Hydnocarpus heterophyllus

Hydnocarpus heterophyllus, commonly known as the Indian tamarind or kachnar, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate with alternate leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing aromatic seeds. The bark and seeds of the plant contain compounds with medicinal properties, including compounds used in traditional medicine.

It is also valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in gardens and as a shade tree.
