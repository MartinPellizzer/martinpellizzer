Hypericum ramosissimum

Hypericum ramosissimum, commonly known as the creeping St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with numerous branches and small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its medicinal properties have been studied for their potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.
