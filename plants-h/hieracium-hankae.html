Hieracium hankae

Hieracium hankae, commonly known as the Hank's Hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cool, temperate climates. It features basal leaves that are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
