Hibiscus involucratus

Hibiscus involucratus, commonly known as the woolly hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers bloom throughout the year, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hibiscus involucratus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
