Hieracium aperissimum

Hieracium aperissimum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering heads that produce yellow to orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It has hairy stems and leaves, and its flowers are surrounded by bracts that give it a distinctive appearance.

Hieracium aperissimum is known for its rapid spread and is considered an invasive species in some regions.
