Hepetis flammea

Hepetis flammea, commonly known as the flame ironweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of bright orange to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in prairies, wetlands, and along stream banks.

Hepetis flammea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
