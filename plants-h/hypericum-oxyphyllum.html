Hypericum oxyphyllum

Hypericum oxyphyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the genus Hypericum, which includes many species of flowering shrubs. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It typically grows in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Hypericum oxyphyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
