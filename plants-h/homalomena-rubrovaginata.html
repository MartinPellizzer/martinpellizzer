Homalomena rubrovaginata

Homalomena rubrovaginata, commonly known as the red-veined caladium, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with vibrant red veins against a deep green background. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which includes other ornamental plants like philodendrons and peace lilies. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires indirect sunlight for optimal growth.

Its foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and indoor decor.
