Hexaspora

Hexaspora, commonly known as the "six-spore plant," is a genus of fungi belonging to the family Sordariaceae.

It is characterized by its unique spore structure, which consists of six spores per ascus, giving it its scientific name. The fruiting bodies, called ascocarps, are typically small and can be found on decaying wood or soil. They are often studied for their ecological significance and potential applications in biotechnology.
