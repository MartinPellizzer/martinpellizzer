Hieracium jankae subsp. wagneri

Hieracium jankae subsp.

wagneri, commonly known as Wagner's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and showy yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has hairy stems and leaves that are often glandular, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
