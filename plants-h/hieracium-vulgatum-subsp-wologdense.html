Hieracium vulgatum subsp. wologdense

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

wologdense, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

It plays a role in supporting pollinators and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
