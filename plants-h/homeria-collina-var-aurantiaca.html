Homeria collina var. aurantiaca

Homeria collina var. aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden hill hibiscus, is a variant of the hill hibiscus native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to yellow flowers, which bloom throughout the warmer months. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly rough texture and a woody stem that becomes more prominent with age. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
