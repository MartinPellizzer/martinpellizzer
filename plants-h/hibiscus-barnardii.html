Hibiscus barnardii

Hibiscus barnardii, commonly known as the Natal hibiscus, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and often branched.

Hibiscus barnardii thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
