Herbertia umbellata

Herbertia umbellata, commonly known as the umbrella plant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and showy, umbellate inflorescences. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Herbertia umbellata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
