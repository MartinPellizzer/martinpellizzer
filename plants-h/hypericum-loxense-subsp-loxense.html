Hypericum loxense subsp. loxense

Hypericum loxense subsp.

loxense, commonly known as the narrow-leaved St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces bright yellow flowers with five petals. It is often found in coastal and mountainous areas, where it adapts well to harsh environmental conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Hypericaceae family.
