Hesperis matronalis subsp. cilicica

Hesperis matronalis subsp.

cilicica, commonly known as the Cilician sweet rocket, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features basal rosettes of lanceolate, aromatic leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early summer.

The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with similar floral structures.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
