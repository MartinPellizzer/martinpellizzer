Hyparrhenia pachystachya

Hyparrhenia pachystachya, commonly known as the broad-leaved muhly, is a perennial grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical inflorescences and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant thrives in wetland habitats and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Hyparrhenia pachystachya is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
