Hypoxis microsperma

Hypoxis microsperma, commonly known as the small-seeded starflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Xanthorrhoeaceae and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with six petals arranged in a star-like pattern, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Hypoxis microsperma is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
