Haplopappus latifolius

Haplopappus latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf horseweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and woolly foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are small and yellowish.

Haplopappus latifolius is a perennial species that produces seeds in a fluffy pappus, aiding in wind dispersal.
