Helicteres salicifolia

Helicteres salicifolia, commonly known as the silky sycamore or water sycamore, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves that resemble those of willow trees, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are pollinated by insects, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its root system is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Helicteres salicifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water sources in its natural habitat.
