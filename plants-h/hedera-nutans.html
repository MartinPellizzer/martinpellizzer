Hedera nutans

Hedera nutans, commonly known as the weeping ivy, is a climbing evergreen vine native to Europe and western Asia.

It features dark green, glossy leaves that are typically heart-shaped and have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often used in horticulture for ground cover or vertical landscaping.

Its trailing habit and dense foliage make it an attractive but sometimes invasive plant in certain regions.
