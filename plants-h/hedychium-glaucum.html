Hedychium glaucum

Hedychium glaucum, commonly known as the blue ginger, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy yellow flowers with a distinctive blue-green hue. The plant produces fragrant, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by broad, waxy bracts. Hedychium glaucum thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It is a member of the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric.
