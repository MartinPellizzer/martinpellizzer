Humulus japonicus var. variegatus

Humulus japonicus var. variegatus, commonly known as the Japanese hop with variegation, is a variant of the hop plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, featuring patches of white, yellow, and green on its leaves. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, producing small, cone-like flowers that are used in traditional herbal medicine. Its leaves are often used in teas and have a mild, slightly bitter flavor.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
