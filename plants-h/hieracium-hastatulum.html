Hieracium hastatulum

Hieracium hastatulum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are hairy and lance-shaped, while the flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disk.

Hieracium hastatulum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
