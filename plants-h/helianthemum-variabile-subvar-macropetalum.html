Helianthemum variabile subvar. macropetalum

Helianthemum variabile subvar. macropetalum, commonly known as the large-petaled stonecrop, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of showy, pink or red flowers during the summer months. The plant has fleshy, succulent leaves that help it survive in arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant blooms and compact growth habit.
