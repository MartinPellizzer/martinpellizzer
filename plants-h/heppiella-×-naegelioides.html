Heppiella × naegelioides

Heppiella × naegelioides, commonly known as the hybrid heath.

It is a low-growing evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, compact form and small, needle-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. It is native to the western United States and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in rock gardens and as a ground cover.
