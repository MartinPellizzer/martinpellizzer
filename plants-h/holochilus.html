Holochilus

Holochilus, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic flowering plant native to the wetlands of Florida and Cuba.

It is characterized by its lack of leaves and its unique, showy flowers that bloom in shades of white and pale green. The plant relies on specific symbiotic relationships with fungi for nutrient uptake, as it cannot photosynthesize due to the absence of chlorophyll. Its delicate structure and elusive nature make it a subject of both botanical fascination and conservation concern.

Due to habitat destruction, Holochilus is listed as an endangered species, highlighting the need for continued research and protection efforts.
