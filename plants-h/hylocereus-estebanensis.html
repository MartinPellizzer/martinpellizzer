Hylocereus estebanensis

Hylocereus estebanensis, commonly known as the Esteban's moon cactus, is a climbing cactus native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its thin, segmented stems and nocturnal flowering habit. The plant produces large, fragrant, white flowers that open at night, attracting pollinators such as bats and moths. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticultural enthusiasts.
