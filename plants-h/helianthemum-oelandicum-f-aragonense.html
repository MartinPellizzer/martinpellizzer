Helianthemum oelandicum f. aragonense

Helianthemum oelandicum f. aragonense, commonly known as the Aragonese rockrose, is a succulent shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Cistaceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and fleshy, aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in arid gardens.
