Hyptis cinerea var. genuina

Hyptis cinerea var. genuina, commonly known as the silverleaf hyptis, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features silver-gray, hairy leaves that are lance-shaped and often arranged oppositely on the stems. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for wetland restoration and native gardens.
