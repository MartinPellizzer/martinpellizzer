Hyptis leptostachys subsp. caatingae

Hyptis leptostachys subsp.

caatingae, commonly known as the Caatinga hyptis, is a shrub native to the Caatinga biome of northeastern Brazil. It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to violet.

The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid conditions, demonstrating resilience to drought. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
