Hydrastis carolinensis

Hydrastis carolinensis, commonly known as goldenseal, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, dark green leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Goldenseal is known for its medicinal properties, particularly its use in traditional herbal medicine.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rich, wooded areas.
