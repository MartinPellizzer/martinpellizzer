Hamiltonia fruticosa

Hamiltonia fruticosa, commonly known as the Mexican flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters. The plant is characterized by its bright red or orange flowers that appear in clusters, often in the summer months. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture with a glossy appearance.

The bark is smooth and reddish-brown, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
