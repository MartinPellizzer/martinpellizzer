Hieracium adriaticiforme subsp. anobrachion

Hieracium adriaticiforme subsp.

anobrachion, commonly known as the Adriatic hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a flowering head composed of yellow disc flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It has a taproot system and produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species in certain regions.
