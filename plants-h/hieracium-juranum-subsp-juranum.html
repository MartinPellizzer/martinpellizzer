Hieracium juranum subsp. juranum

Hieracium juranum subsp.

juranum, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and flowering stems that bear yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are usually covered in fine hairs, and the flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ability to thrive in harsh, high-altitude environments.
