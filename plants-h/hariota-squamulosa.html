Hariota squamulosa

Hariota squamulosa, commonly known as the silverleaf hariota, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Hariota squamulosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
