Hieracium hoppeanum subsp. obtusifolium

Hieracium hoppeanum subsp.

obtusifolium, commonly known as the obtuse-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features basal leaves that are broad and slightly heart-shaped, with a hairy surface. Its flowering stems bear yellow ray flowers surrounded by a ring of tubular florets.

The subspecies is characterized by its obtuse leaf tips and compact growth habit.
