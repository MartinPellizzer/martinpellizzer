Helleborus viridis var. purpurascens

Helleborus viridis var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-stemmed green hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe.

It features dark green, waxy leaves that are often tinged with purple on the stems, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale green flowers with yellow centers, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and early blooming habit.
