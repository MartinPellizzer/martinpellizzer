Hieracium bifidum subsp. virelliceps

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

virelliceps, commonly known as the green-veined hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

The plant features basal rosettes of lanceolate leaves with prominent green veins, which give it its common name. Its flowering stems bear yellow daisy-like flowers with ray florets and a yellow disc.

It is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and is part of the Asteraceae family.
