Hesperis laciniata var. mesatlantica

Hesperis laciniata var. mesatlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features basal leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white, four-petaled flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
