Hieracium florentinum subsp. salisburgense

Hieracium florentinum subsp.

salisburgense, commonly known as the Salzburg hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow flower heads.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are composite, consisting of both ray and disk florets, and it produces achenes as its reproductive structures.

It is often found in alpine and subalpine regions, where it thrives in cooler, temperate climates.
