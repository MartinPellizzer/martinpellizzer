Hieracium macrolepidiforme

Hieracium macrolepidiforme, commonly known as the broad-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and often have a reddish or purple tinge. Its flowers are yellow with tubular centers, and it produces small, dry fruits called cypselae.

Hieracium macrolepidiforme is typically found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
