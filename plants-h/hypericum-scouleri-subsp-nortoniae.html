Hypericum scouleri subsp. nortoniae

Hypericum scouleri subsp.

nortoniae, commonly known as the Nortonia St. John's Wort, is a perennial shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellow-orange flowers in the summer months. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or clay soils.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different leaf morphology and flowering patterns compared to other Hypericum species.
