Hieracium gymnogenum

Hieracium gymnogenum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are rough and hairy, with a basal rosette structure, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

Hieracium gymnogenum is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in many regions.
