Habenaria halconensis

Habenaria halconensis, commonly known as the Halconensis orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually broad and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

The flowers, which can be white, yellow, or green, are notable for their complex structure and are often pollinated by specific insect species.
