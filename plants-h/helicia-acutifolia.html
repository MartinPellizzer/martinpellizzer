Helicia acutifolia

Helicia acutifolia, commonly known as the sharp-leaved helicia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Theaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including parts of India, China, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody fruits.

Helicia acutifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
