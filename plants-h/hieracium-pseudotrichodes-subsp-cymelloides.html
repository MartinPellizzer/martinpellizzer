Hieracium pseudotrichodes subsp. cymelloides

Hieracium pseudotrichodes subsp.

cymelloides, commonly known as the clustered hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and foliage with serrated edges.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are basal, alternate, and often have a rough texture, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head.

It is a low-growing plant, usually reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.
