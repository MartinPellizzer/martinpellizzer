Haemanthus causticus

Haemanthus causticus, commonly known as the blood lily, is a striking plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features strap-like, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

The plant is also notable for its ability to store water in its bulbs, allowing it to survive in dry conditions.
