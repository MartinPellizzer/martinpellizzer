Helleborus × tergestinus

Helleborus × tergestinus, commonly known as the Christmas rose, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. Native to Central Europe, this plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas. Its flowers are typically borne on short, hairy stalks and have a distinctive, waxy appearance.

Helleborus × tergestinus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to add color to the garden during the colder months.
