Hieracium cincinnatum

Hieracium cincinnatum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Hieracium cincinnatum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
