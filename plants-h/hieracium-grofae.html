Hieracium grofae

Hieracium grofae, commonly known as the Grofa's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are basal, rough, and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and hairy.

The flowers, which bloom in late summer, are yellow and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
