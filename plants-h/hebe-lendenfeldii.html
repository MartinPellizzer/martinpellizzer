Hebe lendenfeldii

Hebe lendenfeldii, commonly known as the Stewart Island hebe, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the plantain family (Plantaginaceae) and is characterized by its compact growth habit and evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas on the southern island of Stewart Island.

Hebe lendenfeldii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
