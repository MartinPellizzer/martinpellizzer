Hypoxis pumila

Hypoxis pumila, commonly known as the dwarf starflower, is a small, herbaceous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of star-shaped, yellowish-white flowers. The plant is adapted to grow in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is known for its drought tolerance and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.

Hypoxis pumila is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ease of care.
