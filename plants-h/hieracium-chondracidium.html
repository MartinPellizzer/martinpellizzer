Hieracium chondracidium

Hieracium chondracidium, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and cool, moist conditions. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow flowers in late summer. Its flowering stems are usually hairy and can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters.

Hieracium chondracidium is characterized by its small, daisy-like inflorescences and is often found in rocky or grassy habitats at high elevations.
