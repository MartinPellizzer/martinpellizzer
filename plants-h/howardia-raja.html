Howardia raja

Howardia raja, commonly known as the red-stemmed cycad, is a unique member of the Cycadaceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its short, thick trunk and a crown of feathery, dark green leaves that give it a striking appearance. The plant produces cones rather than flowers, with the male cones being cylindrical and the female cones spherical and often reddish in color. Howardia raja is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
