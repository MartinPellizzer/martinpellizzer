Hesperis contortuplicata

Hesperis contortuplicata, commonly known as the twisted-leaf honesty, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often twisted or curled, giving it its common name. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun.

Hesperis contortuplicata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in gardens for its delicate appearance.
