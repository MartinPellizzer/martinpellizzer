Hieracium murorum subsp. interveniens

Hieracium murorum subsp.

interveniens, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

It is an invasive species in many regions, outcompeting native vegetation due to its rapid growth and prolific seed production.
