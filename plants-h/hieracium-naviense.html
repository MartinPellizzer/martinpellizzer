Hieracium naviense

Hieracium naviense, commonly known as the Navarre hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy, with yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Hieracium naviense is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to tolerate salty and windy conditions.
