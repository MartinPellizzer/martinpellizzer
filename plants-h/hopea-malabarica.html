Hopea malabarica

Hopea malabarica, commonly known as Malabar ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for construction and furniture. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a broad, spreading crown. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a leathery texture.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and is often found in lowland evergreen forests.
