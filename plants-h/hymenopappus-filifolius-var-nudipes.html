Hymenopappus filifolius var. nudipes

Hymenopappus filifolius var. nudipes, commonly known as the naked-leaved mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It has small, scale-like leaves that are often obscured by its dense, woolly covering of fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its seeds are dispersed by birds, which consume the fleshy, red berries.

This species is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments by reducing water loss through its unique leaf structure.
