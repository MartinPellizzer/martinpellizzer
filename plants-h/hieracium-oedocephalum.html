Hieracium oedocephalum

Hieracium oedocephalum, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are hairy and often have a bluish-green hue, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium oedocephalum is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed habitats and can sometimes become invasive in certain regions.
