Hieracium drymophilum

Hieracium drymophilum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine meadows, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. It has dark green, hairy leaves that are lance-shaped and often clustered at the base of the plant.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
