Hieracium leptocladum subsp. rupigenum

Hieracium leptocladum subsp.

rupigenum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and high altitudes.

The plant features a low-growing rosette of basal leaves and produces yellow flowers in late summer. Its foliage is characterized by narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
