Hydrocotyle globiflora

Hydrocotyle globiflora, commonly known as the globe flower, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of broad, waxy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that are clustered in umbels, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Hydrocotyle globiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
