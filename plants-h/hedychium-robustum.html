Hedychium robustum

Hedychium robustum, commonly known as the ginger lily, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fragrant, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Hedychium robustum is cultivated for its ornamental value and its aromatic foliage.
