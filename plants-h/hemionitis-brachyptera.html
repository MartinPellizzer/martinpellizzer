Hemionitis brachyptera

Hemionitis brachyptera, commonly known as the broad-leafed hemionitis, is a rare and delicate fern native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves that are often clustered at the base of the plant. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and has adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its reproductive structures, including spore-producing sorus, are located on the undersides of the fronds.

Due to its limited distribution and sensitivity to environmental changes, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native habitat.
