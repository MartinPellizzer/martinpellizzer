Heliamphora tatei

Heliamphora tatei, commonly known as the Tate's sun pitcher, is a carnivorous plant native to Venezuela.

It is characterized by its distinctive pitcher-shaped leaves that trap and digest insects. The plant thrives in wet, boggy environments and requires high humidity and filtered sunlight. Its pitchers are typically green with red or purple markings, which help attract prey.

Heliamphora tatei is a popular species among carnivorous plant enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
