Hemigenia platyphylla

Hemigenia platyphylla, commonly known as the broad-leaved hemigenia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem, opposite leaf arrangement, and a preference for well-drained soils.

It is often found in heathlands and open forests, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
