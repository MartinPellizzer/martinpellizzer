Hippeastrum andalgalense

Hippeastrum andalgalense, commonly known as the Andalgalan amaryllis, is a bulbous plant native to Argentina.

It features striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, with a strong, spicy fragrance. The plant produces a tall, leafless stem that bears multiple blooms during the growing season. Its bulbs are adapted to store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
