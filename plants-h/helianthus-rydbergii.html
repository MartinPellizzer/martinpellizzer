Helianthus rydbergii

Helianthus rydbergii, commonly known as Rydberg's sunflower, is a species native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its large, yellow-orange composite flowers and linear, hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in prairies, open woods, and disturbed sites, thriving in full sun. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is named after the botanist Erik Rydberg, who contributed significantly to the study of North American flora.
