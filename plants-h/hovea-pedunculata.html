Hovea pedunculata

Hovea pedunculata, commonly known as the woolly hovea, is a shrub native to tropical Australia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated, woody pods containing seeds. Hovea pedunculata thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
