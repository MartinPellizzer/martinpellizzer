Hedyotis spermacocea

Hedyotis spermacocea, commonly known as the small red geranium, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, bright red flowers clustered in umbels. Its flowering period occurs primarily during the summer months.

Hedyotis spermacocea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
