Hakea glabrescens

Hakea glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth hakea, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical or cylindrical-branching growth habit and its distinctive, often red or orange-colored flowers. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are typically smooth and glossy. It produces woody, cone-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Hakea glabrescens is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.
