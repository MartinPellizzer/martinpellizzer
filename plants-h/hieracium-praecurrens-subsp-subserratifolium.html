Hieracium praecurrens subsp. subserratifolium

Hieracium praecurrens subsp.

subserratifolium, commonly known as the serrate hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, meadows, and forest edges.

The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, serrated leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters. Its flowering period occurs from late spring to early summer.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and small, inconspicuous flowers that are arranged in a dense inflorescence.
