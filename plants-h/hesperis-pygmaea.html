Hesperis pygmaea

Hesperis pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf sweet rocket, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in early summer. It typically grows to a height of only a few inches, making it a compact and ornamental species.

Hesperis pygmaea prefers well-drained soil and full sun, and it is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.
