Hieracium subglaberrimum subsp. sparsiramum

Hieracium subglaberrimum subsp.

sparsiramum, commonly known as the sparse-hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy structure.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lanceolate, with a hairy texture, and it produces yellow flowers in clusters.

It is a relatively small plant, usually reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.
