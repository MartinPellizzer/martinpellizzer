Hieracium hortulanum

Hieracium hortulanum, commonly known as garden hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and flowering heads that produce yellow or orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 20 to 60 centimeters and has hairy stems and leaves. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in gardens, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and invasive in certain regions.
