Hieracium atelochaetum

Hieracium atelochaetum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are linear to lanceolate, with a rough texture, and the flowers are yellow, forming a flat cluster.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
