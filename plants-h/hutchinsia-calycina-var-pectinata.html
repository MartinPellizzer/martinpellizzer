Hutchinsia calycina var. pectinata

Hutchinsia calycina var. pectinata, commonly known as the Pectinate Hutchinsia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive pectinate (comb-like) leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical studies.
