Hieracium brachiatum subsp. striatobrachiatum

Hieracium brachiatum subsp.

striatobrachiatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm.

The plant features basal rosettes of dark green, linear leaves with a distinctive white or pale midrib. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single, terminal head, with ray florets that are often shorter than the disk florets.

It is characterized by its finely striated leaf surfaces and is often found in calcareous soils.
