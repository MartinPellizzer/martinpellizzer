Habenaria rehmannii

Habenaria rehmannii, commonly known as the pink lady's tresses, is a species of orchid native to various regions in Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and long, spiraling inflorescences that bear numerous small, pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its roots are often found in rich, organic soil, and it is known for its ability to thrive in humid climates.

The botanical features of Habenaria rehmannii make it a distinctive and ornamental species within the orchid family.
