Hedychium densiflorum

Hedychium densiflorum, commonly known as the denseflower ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Hedychium densiflorum is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
