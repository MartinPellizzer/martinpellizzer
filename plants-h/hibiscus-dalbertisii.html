Hibiscus dalbertisii

Hibiscus dalbertisii, commonly known as the Dalbert's hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red, with a prominent central stigma. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Hibiscus dalbertisii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
