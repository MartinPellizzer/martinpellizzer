Hibiscus ferrugineus

Hibiscus ferrugineus, commonly known as the rusty hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and rocky habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers with a prominent yellow center, which bloom from summer to autumn. Its leaves are ovate and covered in fine, rusty-colored hairs, giving it its common name.

Hibiscus ferrugineus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
