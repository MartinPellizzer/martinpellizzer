Hypericum maculatum var. punctatum

Hypericum maculatum var. punctatum, commonly known as the spotted St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features bright yellow flowers with red or orange markings, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are often dotted with red or black spots, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in herbal medicine.
