Hovea lorata

Hovea lorata, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is known for its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees.

Hovea lorata is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
