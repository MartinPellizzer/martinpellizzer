Heuchera villosa var. intermedia

Heuchera villosa var. intermedia, commonly known as the velvetleaf alumroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, fuzzy foliage that ranges in color from silver to deep purple, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink or red during the spring. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, often found in woodland settings.

Its foliage remains attractive throughout the growing season, providing seasonal interest in gardens.
