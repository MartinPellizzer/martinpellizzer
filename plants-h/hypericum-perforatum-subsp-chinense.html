Hypericum perforatum subsp. chinense

Hypericum perforatum subsp.

chinense, commonly known as Chinese St. John's Wort, is a perennial herb native to China and neighboring regions. It is characterized by its yellow flowers with prominent stamens and leaves that have translucent dots, a feature that distinguishes it from other Hypericum species.

The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and is known for its medicinal properties. It contains hypericin and hyperforin, which are bioactive compounds with potential antidepressant and anti-inflammatory effects.

Its botanical structure and chemical composition make it an important species in both traditional and modern herbal medicine.
