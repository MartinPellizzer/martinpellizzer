Hymenophyllum dimidiatum

Hymenophyllum dimidiatum, commonly known as the silver veil fern, is a small, epiphytic fern native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are covered in minute, scale-like structures called trichomes, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on tree trunks or rocks. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and are covered by a protective indusium.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
