Hieracium claropurpureum

Hieracium claropurpureum, commonly known as the purple-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy structures. The plant features basal rosettes of lanceolate leaves that are typically purple-tinged, giving it its common name. It produces yellow flowers arranged in a single inflorescence at the top of the stem.

Hieracium claropurpureum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
