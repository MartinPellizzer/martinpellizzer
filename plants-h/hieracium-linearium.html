Hieracium linearium

Hieracium linearium, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are linear and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium linearium is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
