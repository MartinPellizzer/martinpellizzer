Hieracium spodocephalum

Hieracium spodocephalum, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers, which bloom in late summer, are yellow and arranged in a flat, disc-like cluster.
