Hybanthus microphyllus

Hybanthus microphyllus, commonly known as the small-leaf hybanthus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Hybanthraceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant, white flowers. It typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial plant in humid, shaded environments.

Hybanthus microphyllus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
