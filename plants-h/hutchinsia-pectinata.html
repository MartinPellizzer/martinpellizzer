Hutchinsia pectinata

Hutchinsia pectinata, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and distinctive, pectinate (toothed) leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, chestnut-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
