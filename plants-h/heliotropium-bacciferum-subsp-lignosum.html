Heliotropium bacciferum subsp. lignosum

Heliotropium bacciferum subsp.

lignosum, commonly known as the blackberry heliotrope, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions. It features woody stems and produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant is characterized by its hairy leaves and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. It has a short flowering period, typically in the summer months.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
