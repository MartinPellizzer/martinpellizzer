Hieracium glaucum subsp. pseudolimonense

Hieracium glaucum subsp.

pseudolimonense, commonly known as the pale hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and yellowish to reddish flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a rosette of basal leaves and produces a flowering stem with several flower heads.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in many regions.
