Hieracium murorum subsp. rubiginans

Hieracium murorum subsp.

rubiginans, commonly known as the red-stemmed hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, reddish stems and yellow flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and waste areas, and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are surrounded by pale green to brown bracts.

This subspecies is often found in open habitats and is considered a common weed in many regions.
