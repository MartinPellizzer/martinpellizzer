Hibiscus micranthus var. asper

Hibiscus micranthus var. asper, commonly known as the rough hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

The flowers bloom throughout the growing season, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
