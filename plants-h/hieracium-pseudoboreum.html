Hieracium pseudoboreum

Hieracium pseudoboreum, commonly known as the northern hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to northern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which bloom from late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and woodland edges. It has a rosette of basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Hieracium pseudoboreum is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
