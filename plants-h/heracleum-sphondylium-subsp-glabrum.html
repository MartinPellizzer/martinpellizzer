Heracleum sphondylium subsp. glabrum

Heracleum sphondylium subsp.

glabrum, commonly known as angel's trumpet, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and large, umbrella-like inflorescences composed of small, white or yellow flowers.

The plant is typically found in moist, grassy areas and along riverbanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a strong, aromatic scent.

It is a member of the Apiaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like carrots and parsley.
