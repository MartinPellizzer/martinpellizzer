Hibbertia mediterranea

Hibbertia mediterranea, commonly known as the Mediterranean hibbertia, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a shrub characterized by its woody stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, adapting to the dry, sandy soils of its natural habitat. It thrives in full sun and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

The plant plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
