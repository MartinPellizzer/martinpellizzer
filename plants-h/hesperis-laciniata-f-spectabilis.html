Hesperis laciniata f. spectabilis

Hesperis laciniata f. spectabilis, commonly known as the showy hoary cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silvery-green leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is native to Europe and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It is a biennial or short-lived perennial, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The f. spectabilis form is distinguished by its more showy and larger flowers compared to the typical variety.
