Hopea daweishanica

Hopea daweishanica, commonly known as the Daweishan ironwood, is a member of the Dipterocarpaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hard, dense wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant typically grows in lowland tropical rainforests, often reaching heights of up to 30 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a leathery texture.

The species is also notable for its fragrant flowers and its role in local ecosystems.
