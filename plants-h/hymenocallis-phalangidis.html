Hymenocallis phalangidis

Hymenocallis phalangidis, commonly known as the Phalangis spider lily, is a striking plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy white flowers with a distinctive yellow throat. The plant features strap-like, grassy leaves that grow from a bulbous base, typical of many amaryllis species. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Hymenocallis phalangidis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
