Heliotropium asperrimum

Heliotropium asperrimum, commonly known as rough heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a rough texture. The plant produces small, violet to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Heliotropium asperrimum is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
