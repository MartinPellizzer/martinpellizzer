Hedera japonica f. variegata

Hedera japonica f. variegata, commonly known as the Japanese ivy, is a cultivar of the common ivy species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, featuring white and yellow markings on its heart-shaped leaves. This plant is a climbing evergreen vine that thrives in shaded to partially sunny conditions. It is often used in horticulture for ground cover or as a decorative plant in hanging baskets.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor gardening.
