Halimium halimifolium f. planifolium

Halimium halimifolium f. planifolium, commonly known as the flat-leaved flaxflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features compact, mat-forming growth with small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow to orange flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or Mediterranean climates.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
