Hemiboea flava

Hemiboea flava, commonly known as the yellow star orchid, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the eastern regions of Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, star-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rainforests and understorey habitats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a rosette at the base.

Hemiboea flava is known for its ephemeral blooming period, which occurs during the warmer months, making it a sought-after plant among orchid enthusiasts.
