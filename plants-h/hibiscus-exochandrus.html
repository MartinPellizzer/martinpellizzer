Hibiscus exochandrus

Hibiscus exochandrus, commonly known as the red hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and ovate with a rough texture.

Hibiscus exochandrus thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.
