Helictotrichon filifolium subsp. cazorlense

Helictotrichon filifolium subsp.

cazorlense, commonly known as the Cazorla needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, spikelet-based inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has a fibrous root system and produces wind-dispersed seeds.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
