Hibiscus vulpinus

Hibiscus vulpinus, commonly known as the fireweed hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from red to pink, often with a yellow center. The plant typically grows in wetlands and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist, sandy soils. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often reddish in color.

Hibiscus vulpinus is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed areas, making it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
