Hibbertia hortiorum

Hibbertia hortiorum, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.

Hibbertia hortiorum is an important component of the local flora and is also the floral emblem of Australia.
