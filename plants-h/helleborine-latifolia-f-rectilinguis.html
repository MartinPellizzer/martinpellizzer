Helleborine latifolia f. rectilinguis

Helleborine latifolia f. rectilinguis, commonly known as the broad-leaved helleborine, is a hybrid orchid species native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of greenish-white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist woodland habitats and is often found in association with other orchids. It is a perennial herb that thrives in temperate climates and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The species is also notable for its ability to hybridize with other helleborine species, contributing to its genetic diversity.
