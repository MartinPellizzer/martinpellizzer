Hyalocystis popovii

Hyalocystis popovii, commonly known as the Russian hyalocystis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the steppes of Central Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 30-60 cm and features narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in the summer months. Hyalocystis popovii is adapted to dry, well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive foliage and flowering display.
