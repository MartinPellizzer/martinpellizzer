Hoarea bipinnatifida

Hoarea bipinnatifida, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped foliage and produces showy, trumpet-like flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant has a compact growth habit and is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Its botanical features make it a favorite among indoor gardeners and botanists alike.
