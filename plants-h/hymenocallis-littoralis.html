Hymenocallis littoralis

Hymenocallis littoralis, commonly known as the coastal spider lily, is a striking plant native to the southeastern United States and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pale yellow flowers with a distinctive purple or red throat. The plant typically grows in wet, sandy soils along coastal areas and is often found in marshes or near the shoreline. Its leaves are long and strap-like, and it produces bulbous roots that help it survive in fluctuating water levels.

Hymenocallis littoralis is an important species for coastal ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife.
