Hippophae rhamnoides subsp. sinensis

Hippophae rhamnoides subsp.

sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese sandthrift, is a shrub native to China and parts of Central Asia. It is characterized by its yellow-orange berries, which are rich in vitamins and oils.

The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is well adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are alternate and lance-shaped, with a rough texture.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in its native habitats.
