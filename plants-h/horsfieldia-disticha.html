Horsfieldia disticha

Horsfieldia disticha, commonly known as the white orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has strap-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, organic-rich soil.

Horsfieldia disticha is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is appreciated in botanical gardens and private collections.
