Hieracium racemosum subsp. apenninum

Hieracium racemosum subsp.

apenninum, commonly known as the Apennine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe, particularly the Apennine Mountains. It is a perennial herb with a basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow flowers in late summer.

The plant has a hairy stem and leaves with a rough texture, typical of the genus Hieracium. It thrives in rocky or calcareous soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different floral structure and geographic distribution compared to other Hieracium species.
