Hibiscus chancoae

Hibiscus chancoae, commonly known as the Chanco hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its striking, large, showy flowers that typically range in color from yellow to red, depending on the variety. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and often branched.

Hibiscus chancoae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
