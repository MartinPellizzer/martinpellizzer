Habranthus mendocinus

Habranthus mendocinus, commonly known as the Mendoza grass.

It is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly Argentina. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

The species is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
