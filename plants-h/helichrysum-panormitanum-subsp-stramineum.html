Helichrysum panormitanum subsp. stramineum

Helichrysum panormitanum subsp.

stramineum, commonly known as the lemon-yellow everlasting, is a member of the Asteraceae family. It is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, particularly found in southern Italy.

The plant is characterized by its bright yellow, papery flowers that remain intact after flowering, giving it a long-lasting appearance. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in rock gardens or as a decorative plant due to its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
