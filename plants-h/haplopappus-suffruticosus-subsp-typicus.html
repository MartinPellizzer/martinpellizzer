Haplopappus suffruticosus subsp. typicus

Haplopappus suffruticosus subsp.

typicus, commonly known as the typical prickly pear, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically edible and used in local cuisines. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in xerophytic ecosystems.
