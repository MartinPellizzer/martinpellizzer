Hieracium subglaucovirens

Hieracium subglaucovirens, commonly known as the glaucous hawkweed, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family (Compositae).

It is characterized by its hairy, blue-green leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Native to Europe, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats such as roadsides and meadows. The plant has a basal rosette of leaves and produces numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in temperate regions.
