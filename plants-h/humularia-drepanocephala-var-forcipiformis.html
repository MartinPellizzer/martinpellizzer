Humularia drepanocephala var. forcipiformis

Humularia drepanocephala var. forcipiformis, commonly known as the horned fern, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, sword-shaped fronds that are often adorned with spore-bearing structures called sporangia. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is typically found in moist forest undergrowth. Its unique morphology includes a short, creeping rhizome with specialized leaf bases that contribute to its ornamental appeal.

The variety forcipiformis is noted for its robust growth and adaptability to various soil types, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
