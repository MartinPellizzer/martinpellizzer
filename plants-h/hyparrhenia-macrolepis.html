Hyparrhenia macrolepis

Hyparrhenia macrolepis, commonly known as the African grass or red grass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and produces dense, fibrous stalks that are often used in thatching and construction. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically reddish in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Hyparrhenia macrolepis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for erosion control.
