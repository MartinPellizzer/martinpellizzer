Hemipilia graminifolia var. suzukiana

Hemipilia graminifolia var. suzukiana, commonly known as the Suzukiana Violet, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its flowering period occurs during the spring and early summer, and it is valued for its ornamental beauty.

The species is also noted for its unique floral structure, which includes a spurred lip and a highly modified column.
