Hedychium oblongum

Hedychium oblongum, commonly known as the giant ginger or yellow ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that can grow up to 3 meters in height, featuring large, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, yellow or orange flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense clumps in suitable conditions.

Hedychium oblongum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
