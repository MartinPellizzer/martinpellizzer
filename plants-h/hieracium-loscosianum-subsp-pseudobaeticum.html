Hieracium loscosianum subsp. pseudobaeticum

Hieracium loscosianum subsp.

pseudobaeticum, commonly known as the Baetican hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to reddish composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has alternate, hairy leaves that are lanceolate in shape.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
