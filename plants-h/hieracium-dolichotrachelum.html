Hieracium dolichotrachelum

Hieracium dolichotrachelum, commonly known as the long-necked hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a distinct, elongated central stem.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations due to its nectar-rich blooms.
