Hieracium gigantocybe

Hieracium gigantocybe, commonly known as the giant hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow or orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and meadows, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Hieracium gigantocybe is known for its ability to spread rapidly through rhizomes, making it an invasive species in some regions.
