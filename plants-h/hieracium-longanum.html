Hieracium longanum

Hieracium longanum, commonly known as the long-horned hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellowish to orange-yellow composite flowers, which are typically arranged in a flat-topped cluster. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are lance-shaped with serrated edges. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in meadows and grasslands.

It is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in various habitats.
