Hieracium trebevicianum var. pleiophylloides

Hieracium trebevicianum var. pleiophylloides, commonly known as the pleiophylloides hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the daisy family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its rosette-forming basal leaves and yellowish composite flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. It has a taproot system and produces numerous flowering stems that bear small, clustered flowers.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and botanical gardens.
