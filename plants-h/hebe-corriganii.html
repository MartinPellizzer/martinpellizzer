Hebe corriganii

Hebe corriganii, commonly known as the Corrigan hebe, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form. The plant produces clusters of tubular, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, depending on the variety. Hebe corriganii thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract native pollinators such as bees and birds.
