Hippocrepis cyclocarpa

Hippocrepis cyclocarpa, commonly known as the cyclocarpa gentian, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in opposite pairs, while its stems are hairy and upright.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
