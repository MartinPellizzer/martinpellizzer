Helianthemum italicum subsp. alpestre

Helianthemum italicum subsp.

alpestre, commonly known as the alpine thyme, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of southern Europe. It is a low-growing shrub with compact, aromatic foliage that is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers in summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine beds.

Its adaptability to harsh environments highlights its ecological importance in mountainous habitats.
