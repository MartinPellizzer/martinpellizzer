Hakea acerata

Hakea acerata, commonly known as the silky hakea, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. Hakea acerata is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that helps it survive in dry conditions.

It is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, which can improve soil fertility in its native habitat.
