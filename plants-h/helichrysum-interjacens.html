Helichrysum interjacens

Helichrysum interjacens, commonly known as the golden marguerite or everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its showy, bright yellow or golden-orange inflorescences. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that are typically arranged in clusters, giving it a fluffy appearance. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its overall ornamental appeal.

Helichrysum interjacens is often used in gardens for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
