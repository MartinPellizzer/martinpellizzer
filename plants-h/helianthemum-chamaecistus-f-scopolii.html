Helianthemum chamaecistus f. scopolii

Helianthemum chamaecistus f. scopolii, commonly known as the Scopolii rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-orange flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its hardy nature and ability to withstand drought conditions.
