Habenaria villosa

Habenaria villosa, commonly known as the hairy orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and grasslands. The plant features a slender stem with several elongated, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish, with three sepals and three petals, and are often clustered in a loose inflorescence.

Habenaria villosa is known for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
