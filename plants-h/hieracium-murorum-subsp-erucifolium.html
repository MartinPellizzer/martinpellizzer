Hieracium murorum subsp. erucifolium

Hieracium murorum subsp.

erucifolium, commonly known as the roughleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow flower heads.

The leaves are typically rough and hairy, with a distinctive lobed or serrated edge. It thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats, often found in meadows and along roadsides.

The plant produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal, aiding in its spread across various environments.
