Hiericontis

Hiericontis, commonly known as the meadow cranesbill, is a member of the Geraniaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its upright stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Hiericontis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
