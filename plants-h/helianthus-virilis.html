Helianthus virilis

Helianthus virilis, commonly known as the stiffstem sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and other composite flowers. The plant features upright, hairy stems and large, yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk of smaller florets. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with rough textures and are arranged alternately along the stem.

Helianthus virilis is often found in wetlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils.
