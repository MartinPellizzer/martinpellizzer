Hierochloe borealis var. biflora

Hierochloe borealis var. biflora, commonly known as the Arctic hairgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to cold regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically forms dense, mat-like colonies and has slender, wiry stems that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in nutrient-poor, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and arctic tundra environments.

Its botanical features make it well-adapted to harsh, cold climates with short growing seasons.
