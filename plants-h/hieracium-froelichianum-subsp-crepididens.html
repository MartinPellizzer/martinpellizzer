Hieracium froelichianum subsp. crepididens

Hieracium froelichianum subsp.

crepididens, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf structures.

The plant typically features rough, hairy stems and basal leaves that are often lobed or toothed. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in dense clusters, attracting various pollinators.

It thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, showcasing adaptability to different environmental conditions.
