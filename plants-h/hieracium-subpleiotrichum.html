Hieracium subpleiotrichum

Hieracium subpleiotrichum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers consist of yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers.

Hieracium subpleiotrichum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
