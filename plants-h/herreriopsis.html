Herreriopsis

Herreriopsis, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage and clusters of delicate, fragrant flowers. Native to the Andean regions of South America, these plants thrive in cool, high-altitude environments. Herreriopsis species are epiphytic, meaning they grow on the surfaces of trees and rocks rather than in soil.

Their unique botanical features make them popular among orchid enthusiasts and horticulturists.
