Helianthus subtuberosus

Helianthus subtuberosus, commonly known as the subtubersome sunflower, is a species native to the southern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial with upright stems and large, showy yellow flowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowering period typically occurs in the summer months, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant's botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
