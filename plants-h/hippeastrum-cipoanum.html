Hippeastrum cipoanum

Hippeastrum cipoanum, commonly known as the Cipoan amaryllis, is a bulbous plant native to South America, specifically Brazil.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces a tall, leafless stem that bears several flowers, followed by clusters of sword-shaped leaves. It is a popular ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements.

Hippeastrum cipoanum thrives in well-draining soil and prefers bright, indirect light.
