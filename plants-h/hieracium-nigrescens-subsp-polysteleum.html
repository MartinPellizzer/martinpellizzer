Hieracium nigrescens subsp. polysteleum

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

polysteleum, commonly known as the purple hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellowish to reddish flower heads.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as meadows and roadsides. Its flowers are composite, consisting of both ray and disk florets, which attract pollinators like bees and flies.

The subspecies polysteleum is distinguished by its multiple stems and dense flowering structure compared to other forms of Hieracium nigrescens.
