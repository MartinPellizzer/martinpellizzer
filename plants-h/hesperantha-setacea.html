Hesperantha setacea

Hesperantha setacea, commonly known as the needle-leaf lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features striking, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, with a distinctive, spurred shape. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Hesperantha setacea is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
