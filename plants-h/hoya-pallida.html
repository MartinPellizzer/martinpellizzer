Hoya pallida

Hoya pallida, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its waxy, pale green leaves that are often speckled with white or silver markings. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in the spring. Hoya pallida is a climbing plant that thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.

It is popular among indoor gardeners due to its attractive foliage and low maintenance requirements.
