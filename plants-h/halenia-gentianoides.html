Halenia gentianoides

Halenia gentianoides, commonly known as blue gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are sturdy and branched.

Halenia gentianoides is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
