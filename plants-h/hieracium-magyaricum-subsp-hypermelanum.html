Hieracium magyaricum subsp. hypermelanum

Hieracium magyaricum subsp.

hypermelanum, commonly known as the Hungarian hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. It has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, clustered flower heads.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
