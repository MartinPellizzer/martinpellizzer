Hieracium augusti-bayeri

Hieracium augusti-bayeri, commonly known as the August-Bayer's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy rosettes. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and meadows. Its leaves are usually basal, lanceolate, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bright, daisy-like appearance.
