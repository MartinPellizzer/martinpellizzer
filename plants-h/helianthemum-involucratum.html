Helianthemum involucratum

Helianthemum involucratum, commonly known as the woolly rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in early spring, which are typically clustered in groups of three to five. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
