Hieracium incisifolium

Hieracium incisifolium, commonly known as the cut-leaf hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf morphology. The plant features lanceolate to ovate leaves that are often deeply lobed or cut, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are typically yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Hieracium incisifolium is often found in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
