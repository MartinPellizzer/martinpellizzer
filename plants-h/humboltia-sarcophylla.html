Humboltia sarcophylla

Humboltia sarcophylla, commonly known as the California boxthorn, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that are arranged in a compact, bushy form. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Humboltia sarcophylla is adapted to drought conditions and plays a role in stabilizing sandy or disturbed soils.
