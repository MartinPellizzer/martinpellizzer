Hypericum loxense subsp. aequatoriale

Hypericum loxense subsp.

aequatoriale, commonly known as the Ecuadorian St. John's Wort, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Hypericum. It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru.

The plant is characterized by its erect growth habit and produces bright yellow flowers with five petals. Its leaves are opposite and oval-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate to subtropical climates.
