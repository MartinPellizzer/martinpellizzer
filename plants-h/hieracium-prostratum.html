Hieracium prostratum

Hieracium prostratum, commonly known as the creeping hawkweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with numerous stems that spread along the ground. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with hairy surfaces, and the plant is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Hieracium prostratum is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some traditional uses.
