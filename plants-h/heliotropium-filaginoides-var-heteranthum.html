Heliotropium filaginoides var. heteranthum

Heliotropium filaginoides var. heteranthum, commonly known as the variable heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its flowers open in the morning and close in the evening, a behavior known as nyctinasty.

The species is noted for its adaptability to harsh climates and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
