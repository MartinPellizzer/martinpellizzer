Hieracium bocconei var. engadinense

Hieracium bocconei var. engadinense, commonly known as the Engadine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Alps.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to reddish flower heads. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky areas, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

This variety is distinguished by its smaller flower heads and specific geographic distribution within the Swiss and Italian Alps.
