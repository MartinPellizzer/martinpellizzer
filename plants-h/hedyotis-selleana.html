Hedyotis selleana

Hedyotis selleana, commonly known as the golden mosquito plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in tropical and subtropical regions.
