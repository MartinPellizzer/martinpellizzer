Helipterum chlorocephalum

Helipterum chlorocephalum, commonly known as the blue helipterum, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a small annual herb with slender, branched stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces numerous small, blue to purple flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as roadsides and disturbed areas.

The flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant is known for its rapid growth and short life cycle.
