Hymenoxys turneri

Hymenoxys turneri, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It features showy, bright orange to red composite flowers that bloom from summer to fall. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in the center of its flower heads. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Hymenoxys turneri is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
