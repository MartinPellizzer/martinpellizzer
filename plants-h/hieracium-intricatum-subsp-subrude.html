Hieracium intricatum subsp. subrude

Hieracium intricatum subsp.

subrude, commonly known as the rough hawksbeard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

The plant features yellow composite flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, with ray florets that are often shorter than the central disk florets. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, giving them a rough texture.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
