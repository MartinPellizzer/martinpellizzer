Hieracium patulum

Hieracium patulum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium patulum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
