Huernia brevirostris var. immaculata

Huernia brevirostris var. immaculata, commonly known as the white horned lithops, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that form a rosette shape, often with a distinct horn-like protrusion on the upper surface. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white or pale yellow during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
