Hymenocallis occidentalis var. occidentalis

Hymenocallis occidentalis var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western spider lily, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features showy, white to pale yellow flowers with a distinctive, spidery appearance and a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant produces a single stem with several flowers, each surrounded by six narrow, strap-like petals. Its bulbs are adapted to dry conditions, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and natural landscapes.
