Hieracium lugdunense

Hieracium lugdunense, commonly known as the Highland groundsel, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy leaves, which are typical features of the genus Hieracium. Native to Europe, it thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. The plant has a compact growth habit and produces small, dandelion-like flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn.

It is considered a herbaceous perennial, with a fibrous root system that helps it survive in various environmental conditions.
