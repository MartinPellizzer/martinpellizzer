Hieracium subpraematuriceps

Hieracium subpraematuriceps, commonly known as the early flowering hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and is typically found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant produces showy yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, which are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, and it has a fibrous root system.

Hieracium subpraematuriceps is native to Europe and has been introduced to other regions, where it can sometimes become invasive.
