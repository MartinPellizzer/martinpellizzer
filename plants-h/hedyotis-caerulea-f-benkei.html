Hedyotis caerulea f. benkei

Hedyotis caerulea f. benkei, commonly known as the blue false indigo, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features slender, upright stems covered in small, lance-shaped leaves that are usually a deep green. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, often with a white or pale pink throat. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate foliage and long-lasting blooms.
