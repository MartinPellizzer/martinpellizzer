Hibiscus robinsonii

Hibiscus robinsonii, commonly known as the Hawaiian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the island of Oahu in Hawaii.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically blooming during the summer months. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical regions.

Hibiscus robinsonii is also valued for its cultural significance in Hawaiian traditions.
