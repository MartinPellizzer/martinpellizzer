Huperzia pithyoides

Huperzia pithyoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf huperzia, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its simple, linear leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its sporophyte stage produces spores on the underside of its leaves, which are dispersed by wind.

Huperzia pithyoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
