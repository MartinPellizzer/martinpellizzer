Hydrangea warszewiczii

Hydrangea warszewiczii, commonly known as the Warszewicz's hydrangea, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from white to pink depending on soil pH. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a serrated edge.

Hydrangea warszewiczii prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded forest environments.
