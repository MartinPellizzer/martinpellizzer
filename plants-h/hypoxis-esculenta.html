Hypoxis esculenta

Hypoxis esculenta, commonly known as African potato, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of grass-like leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is perennial and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. Its rhizomes are the primary part used, containing compounds with potential medicinal properties.

It is often cultivated for both ornamental and traditional medicinal purposes.
