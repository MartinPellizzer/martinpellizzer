Hieracium lycopifolium

Hieracium lycopifolium, commonly known as the parsley-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are deeply lobed and resemble parsley, hence its common name.

It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.
