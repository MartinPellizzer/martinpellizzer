Hemitelia riparia

Hemitelia riparia, commonly known as the river hemitelia, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as riverbanks and forest floors. The plant has slender, climbing stems that can reach several meters in length. Its small, opposite leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating.

The flowers are tiny, greenish-white, and appear in clusters, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
