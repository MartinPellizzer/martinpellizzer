Huperzia weddellii

Huperzia weddellii, commonly known as the Weddell's huperzia, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is a terrestrial, evergreen plant characterized by its simple, needle-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its sporophylls are closely clustered and produce spores in small, cup-like structures called sporangia.

Huperzia weddellii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
