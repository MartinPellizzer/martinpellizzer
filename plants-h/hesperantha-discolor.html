Hesperantha discolor

Hesperantha discolor, commonly known as the variable bell orchid, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers a sunny to partially shaded environment. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it produces showy, fragrant blooms during the autumn season.

Hesperantha discolor is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
