Hieracium acuescens

Hieracium acuescens, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. It features upright stems covered in fine, hairy hairs, and its flowers are yellow with a central disc of smaller florets.

Hieracium acuescens is often found in temperate regions and can sometimes be invasive in non-native habitats.
