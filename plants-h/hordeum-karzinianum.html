Hordeum karzinianum

Hordeum karzinianum, commonly known as Karzini's barley, is a wild grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it has a fibrous root system.

While not widely cultivated, it is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its ecological resilience and potential for genetic study.
