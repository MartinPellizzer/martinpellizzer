Hieracium triangulare subsp. euscepanum

Hieracium triangulare subsp.

euscepanum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm.

The plant features basal leaves that are triangular in shape, with serrated margins, and produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its flowering stems are usually unbranched and bear small, clustered inflorescences.

The species is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and is part of the Asteraceae family.
