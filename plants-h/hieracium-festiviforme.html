Hieracium festiviforme

Hieracium festiviforme, commonly known as the showy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough texture.

Hieracium festiviforme is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it plays a role in supporting pollinators.
