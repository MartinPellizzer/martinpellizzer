Hieracium × obscurum

Hieracium × obscurum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a hybrid plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its composite flower heads, which consist of both ray and disk florets, typical of the daisy family. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, showing a preference for temperate climates. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often reddish in color.

Hieracium × obscurum is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in agricultural areas.
