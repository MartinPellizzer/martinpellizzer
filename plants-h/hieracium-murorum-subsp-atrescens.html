Hieracium murorum subsp. atrescens

Hieracium murorum subsp.

atrescens, commonly known as the purple meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellowish to reddish flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its subspecies status reflects subtle morphological differences from the main species, particularly in flower color and leaf structure.

It is noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
