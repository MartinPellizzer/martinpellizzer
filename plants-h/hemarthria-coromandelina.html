Hemarthria coromandelina

Hemarthria coromandelina, commonly known as slender grass or Indian needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks.

Hemarthria coromandelina is valued for its erosion control capabilities and as a forage plant for livestock.
