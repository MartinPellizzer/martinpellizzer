Hypericum sinaicum

Hypericum sinaicum, commonly known as the Sinai St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and is typically found in rocky or arid environments. The plant belongs to the family Hypericaceae and is known for its medicinal properties. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antidepressant effects.

Hypericum sinaicum is also valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
