Hirania rosea

Hirania rosea, commonly known as the rose of Sharon, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Americas.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often appearing in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth texture with a slightly waxy appearance.

Hirania rosea is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various soil conditions.
