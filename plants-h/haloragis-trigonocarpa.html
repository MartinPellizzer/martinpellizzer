Haloragis trigonocarpa

Haloragis trigonocarpa, commonly known as the three-angled willow herb, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb with slender, often prostrate stems that can form dense mats. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically found in the axils of the leaves. Its leaves are alternate, linear to lanceolate, and often have a waxy coating.

Haloragis trigonocarpa is adapted to grow in sandy or gravelly soils, often in coastal or inland areas with well-drained conditions.
