Hippophae caucasica

Hippophae caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian sea buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange berries, which are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. The plant has small, oval leaves and produces yellow flowers in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

The shrub is valued for its ecological benefits and has been used in traditional medicine.
