Humboldtia unijuga var. trijuga

Humboldtia unijuga var. trijuga, commonly known as the three-lobed humboldtia, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive tri-lobed leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the coastline, thriving in coastal climates with high salinity. It is a shrub that can reach up to 1 meter in height, with hairy stems and aromatic foliage.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists studying coastal flora.
