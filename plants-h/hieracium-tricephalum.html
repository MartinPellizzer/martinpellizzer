Hieracium tricephalum

Hieracium tricephalum, commonly known as the three-headed hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny conditions. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators.

Hieracium tricephalum is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in some areas.
