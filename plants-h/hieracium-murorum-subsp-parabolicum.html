Hieracium murorum subsp. parabolicum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

parabolicum, commonly known as the Parabolic Hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows. It has basal and cauline leaves that are usually hairy and have a serrated edge.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique flowering pattern and morphology compared to other Hieracium species.
