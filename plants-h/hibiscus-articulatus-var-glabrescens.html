Hibiscus articulatus var. glabrescens

Hibiscus articulatus var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smoothleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

Its flowers bloom throughout the growing season, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
