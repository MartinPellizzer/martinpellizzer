Hamamelis virginiana f. rubescens

Hamamelis virginiana f. rubescens, commonly known as the red-fruited witch hazel, is a botanical variant of the common witch hazel.

It is characterized by its distinctive red-orange capsules that persist through winter, adding visual interest to the landscape. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and turn yellow in the fall, complementing the vibrant red fruits.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
