Hieracium subramosum subsp. prolatatum

Hieracium subramosum subsp.

prolatatum, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

The plant has a creeping rootstock and produces erect, hairy stems that bear alternate, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, yellowish, and clustered in loose inflorescences, typical of the Compositae family.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and is often found in disturbed or nutrient-rich soils.
