Hieracium basicaulescens

Hieracium basicaulescens, commonly known as the basal-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, often forming dense mats. It has basal leaves that are usually lance-shaped and hairy, with flowering stems that bear yellow flowers in a single head.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
