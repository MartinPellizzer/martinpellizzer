Hieracium carpathicum subsp. seductum

Hieracium carpathicum subsp.

seductum, commonly known as the Carpathian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Carpathian Mountains. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It has finely divided, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a composite head.

This subspecies is often found in mountainous regions and is adapted to cooler, temperate climates.
