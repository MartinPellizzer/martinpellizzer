Hymenophyllum sericeum

Hymenophyllum sericeum, commonly known as the silky maidenhair fern, is a small, epiphytic fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds covered in fine, silky hairs, which give it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or rocks. Its spores are released from tiny, clustered structures called sori, which are located on the underside of the fronds.

Hymenophyllum sericeum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or terrariums.
