Helictotrichon bromoides subsp. pauneroae

Helictotrichon bromoides subsp.

pauneroae, commonly known as the plains needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America. It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as prairies and roadsides. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
