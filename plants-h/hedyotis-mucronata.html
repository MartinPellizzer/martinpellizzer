Hedyotis mucronata

Hedyotis mucronata, commonly known as the slender bladdernut, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors. The plant features small, white or pinkish flowers clustered in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits and is also used as a ornamental plant in gardens.
