Hepatica triloba var. acutiloba

Hepatica triloba var. acutiloba, commonly known as the sharp-lobed hepatica, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves with sharply pointed lobes and early spring blooms of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in rich, well-drained soil. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological importance.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its spring foliage and floral display.
