Hymenocallis choretis var. oaxacensis

Hymenocallis choretis var. oaxacensis, commonly known as the Oaxacan spider lily, is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, white, bell-shaped flowers with a greenish throat and long, narrow petals. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that are dark green and glossy.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
