Hemionitis greggii

Hemionitis greggii, commonly known as the Gregg's hemionitis, is a small, mat-forming perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Hemionitidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale green flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping in arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
