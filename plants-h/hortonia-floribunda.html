Hortonia floribunda

Hortonia floribunda, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along its stem, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. Hortonia floribunda thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant flowers and compact growth habit.
