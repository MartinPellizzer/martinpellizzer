Hedysarum silicii

Hedysarum silicii, commonly known as the Sicilian vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in scrublands and grasslands. Hedysarum silicii is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.

Its seeds are also used as a forage crop for livestock in some regions.
