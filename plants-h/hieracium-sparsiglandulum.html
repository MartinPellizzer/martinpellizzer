Hieracium sparsiglandulum

Hieracium sparsiglandulum, commonly known as the sparse-gland hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped with sparse glandular dots, and the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is an herbaceous perennial that spreads through rhizomes and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
