Hemipilia flabellata

Hemipilia flabellata, commonly known as the fan-leaved orchid, is a herbaceous perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves and intricate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish, with a distinctive fan-like shape that gives it its common name.

Hemipilia flabellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
