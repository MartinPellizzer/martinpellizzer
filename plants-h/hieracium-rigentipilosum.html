Hieracium rigentipilosum

Hieracium rigentipilosum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring temperate climates. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, contributing to its role in pollinator habitats.
