Hypericum concinnum

Hypericum concinnum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is characterized by its woody stems. It is part of the Hypericaceae family, which includes several species with medicinal and ornamental value.

Hypericum concinnum is often used in native plant gardens due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
