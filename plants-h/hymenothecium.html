Hymenothecium

Hymenothecium, commonly known as the spore case, is a specialized structure found in certain fungi and some lower plants.

It is composed of a protective layer that encloses the spores, which are the reproductive units of the organism. The hymenothecium often develops from the fusion of hyphal tissues and is closely associated with the hymenium, the spore-bearing layer. Its structure varies among species, sometimes forming a cup-like or flask-shaped receptacle.

This structure plays a crucial role in the dispersal and survival of spores in various environmental conditions.
