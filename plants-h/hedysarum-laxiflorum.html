Hedysarum laxiflorum

Hedysarum laxiflorum, commonly known as the slender-flowered vetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers arranged in racemes. The plant is characterized by its slender stems and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation due to its association with root nodules containing Rhizobia bacteria.

Hedysarum laxiflorum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
