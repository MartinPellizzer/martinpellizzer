Hieracium merinoi

Hieracium merinoi, commonly known as the Merino's hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium merinoi is often found in open habitats and can form dense colonies in suitable environments.
