Hieracium aitium

Hieracium aitium, commonly known as the Italian hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are usually hairy and lance-shaped.

Hieracium aitium is often considered a weed but has historical uses in traditional medicine.
