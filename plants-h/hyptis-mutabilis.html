Hyptis mutabilis

Hyptis mutabilis, commonly known as the variable hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.

The species is noted for its adaptability and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
