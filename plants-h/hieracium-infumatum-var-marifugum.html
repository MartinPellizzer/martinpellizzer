Hieracium infumatum var. marifugum

Hieracium infumatum var. marifugum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges. It has rough, hairy leaves that are lance-shaped and often have a reddish or purplish hue.

The flowers are yellow, with a central disk surrounded by pale yellow to white ray florets, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
