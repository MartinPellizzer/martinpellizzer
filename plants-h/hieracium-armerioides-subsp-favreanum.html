Hieracium armerioides subsp. favreanum

Hieracium armerioides subsp.

favreanum, commonly known as the favrean hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its basal rosette of linear to lanceolate leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. It is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, and it is often found in temperate climates.

The subspecies favreanum is distinguished by its slightly different leaf morphology and flower structure compared to other members of the H. armerioides complex.
