Halopeplis perfoliata

Halopeplis perfoliata, commonly known as the perfoliate hound's-tongue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that often have a bluish-gray hue. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Halopeplis perfoliata is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.
