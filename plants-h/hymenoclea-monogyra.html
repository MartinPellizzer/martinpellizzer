Hymenoclea monogyra

Hymenoclea monogyra, commonly known as the white star vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom throughout the year. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative element in tropical landscapes.
