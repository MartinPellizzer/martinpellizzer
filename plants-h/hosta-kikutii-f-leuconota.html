Hosta kikutii f. leuconota

Hosta kikutii f. leuconota, commonly known as the white-veined hosta, is a cultivar characterized by its striking variegation.

The leaves exhibit a combination of deep green and creamy white veins, creating a visually appealing contrast. It is a hybrid form of Hosta kikutii, known for its broad, waxy foliage. This plant thrives in shady to partially sunny conditions and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for shade gardens and containers.
