Hieracium listrophyllum

Hieracium listrophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and foliage with serrated edges. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a hairy surface, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
