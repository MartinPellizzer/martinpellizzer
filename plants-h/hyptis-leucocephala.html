Hyptis leucocephala

Hyptis leucocephala, commonly known as white- headed hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed soils.

Hyptis leucocephala is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
