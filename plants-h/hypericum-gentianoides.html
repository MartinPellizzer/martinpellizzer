Hypericum gentianoides

Hypericum gentianoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf St. John's wort, is a perennial herb native to the northern United States and southern Canada.

It features upright, branched stems covered in yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soils, often found in dry meadows and rocky areas.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
