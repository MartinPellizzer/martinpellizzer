Helenium hirtum

Helenium hirtum, commonly known as the hairy sunflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features showy, daisy-like yellow-orange flower heads with a dark center, typically blooming in late summer to early fall. The plant has rough, hairy stems and lance-shaped, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Helenium hirtum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
