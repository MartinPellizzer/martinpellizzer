Heteromorpha crinita

Heteromorpha crinita, commonly known as the silky lily, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, white, silky-textured petals and a distinctive, recurved shape. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.

Heteromorpha crinita is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
