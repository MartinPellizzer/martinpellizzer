Hoodia currorii var. minor

Hoodia currorii var. minor, commonly known as the lesser hoodia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its spineless stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in extreme drought conditions, storing water in its fleshy, cylindrical stems. It is often found growing in rocky soils and is well-suited to harsh, sun-exposed environments.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is of interest in both botanical research and potential medicinal applications.
