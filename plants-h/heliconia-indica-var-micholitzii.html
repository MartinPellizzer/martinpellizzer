Heliconia indica var. micholitzii

Heliconia indica var. micholitzii, commonly known as the Micholitz's heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, colorful bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces long, narrow inflorescences that are often used in floral arrangements due to their striking appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

The species is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
