Haemanthus tenuiflorus

Haemanthus tenuiflorus, commonly known as the narrow-flowered haemanthus, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features grass-like basal leaves and produces clusters of bright red or orange flowers during the winter months. The plant is characterized by its short, stocky stem and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and partial shade.
