Helichrysum polygalifolium

Helichrysum polygalifolium, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that resemble tiny suns, giving it its common name. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its compact and decorative appearance. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic foliage.

Helichrysum polygalifolium is often used in dried floral arrangements due to its long-lasting, vibrant color.
