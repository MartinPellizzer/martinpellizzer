Hieracium austroslavicum subsp. cakoranum

Hieracium austroslavicum subsp.

cakoranum, commonly known as the Cakoranum hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Eastern Europe and Central Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring temperate climates. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rough, hairy texture, and its flowers are yellow, often appearing in clusters.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its resilience to environmental changes.
