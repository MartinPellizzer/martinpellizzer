Hosta japonica f. albiflora

Hosta japonica f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered Japanese hosta, is a varietal form of the Japanese hosta.

It is characterized by its broad, dark green leaves that are often adorned with a striking white or light green margin. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is well-suited for woodland gardens.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
