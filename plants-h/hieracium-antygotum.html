Hieracium antygotum

Hieracium antygotum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and basal rosette of leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats such as meadows and roadsides. It has a creeping growth habit with rhizomes that allow it to spread aggressively.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and potentially invasive in certain regions.
