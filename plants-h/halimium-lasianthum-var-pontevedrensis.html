Halimium lasianthum var. pontevedrensis

Halimium lasianthum var. pontevedrensis, commonly known as the Pontevedran rock rose, is a perennial shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse display of yellow flowers in late spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. Its leaves are typically gray-green and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
