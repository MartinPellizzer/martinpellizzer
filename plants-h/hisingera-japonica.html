Hisingera japonica

Hisingera japonica, commonly known as the Japanese hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as along forest floors or in wetlands. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a smooth texture.

Hisingera japonica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
