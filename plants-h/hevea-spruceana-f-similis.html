Hevea spruceana f. similis

Hevea spruceana f. similis, commonly known as the similar hevea, is a tropical plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing seeds. It is native to parts of Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
