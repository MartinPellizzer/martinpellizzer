Hemesteum obliquum

Hemesteum obliquum, commonly known as the twisted hemesteum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and spiral arrangement of leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
