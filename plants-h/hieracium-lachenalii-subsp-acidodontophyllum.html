Hieracium lachenalii subsp. acidodontophyllum

Hieracium lachenalii subsp.

acidodontophyllum, commonly known as the acid-loving hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and is typically found in acidic soils, often in alpine or subalpine regions.

The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers arranged in a composite head, which is typical of the Asteraceae family. It has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often glandular, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is adapted to harsh environments and is often found in rocky or disturbed habitats.
