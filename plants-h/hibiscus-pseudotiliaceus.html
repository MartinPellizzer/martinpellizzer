Hibiscus pseudotiliaceus

Hibiscus pseudotiliaceus, commonly known as the yellow hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers with five petals and a prominent central stamen. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs.

Hibiscus pseudotiliaceus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
