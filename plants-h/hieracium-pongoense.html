Hieracium pongoense

Hieracium pongoense, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. It produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the summer months, and its leaves are usually hairy and arranged in a rosette pattern.

Due to its habitat, it is often found in mountainous regions of countries like Argentina and Chile.
