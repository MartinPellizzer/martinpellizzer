Hieracium lachenalii subsp. mertinii

Hieracium lachenalii subsp.

mertinii, commonly known as the Mertin's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a flowering stem that bears yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, grassy habitats such as meadows and rocky soils. It has a compact growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
