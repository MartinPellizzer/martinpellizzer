Hieracium virgulticolens

Hieracium virgulticolens, commonly known as the field hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant features bright yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, with leaves that are usually hairy and deeply lobed. It is a perennial species that spreads through both seeds and rhizomes, making it somewhat invasive in certain regions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common sight in meadows and along roadsides.
