Hieracium denticuliferens

Hieracium denticuliferens, commonly known as the toothed hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in many temperate regions.
