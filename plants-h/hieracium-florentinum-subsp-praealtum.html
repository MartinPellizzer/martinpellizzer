Hieracium florentinum subsp. praealtum

Hieracium florentinum subsp.

praealtum, commonly known as the tall everlasting flower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the daisy family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow-orange flower heads.

The plant produces ray florets that are typically yellow, surrounded by a dense cluster of disk florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a common subject in botanical studies and horticultural practices.
