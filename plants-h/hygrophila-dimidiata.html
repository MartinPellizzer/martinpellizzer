Hygrophila dimidiata

Hygrophila dimidiata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf waterhyssop, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and spikes of small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of blue or purple. The plant thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in shallow water or damp soil.

Hygrophila dimidiata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in aquatic gardening.
