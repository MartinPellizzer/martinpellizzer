Hieraciodes tingitana

Hieraciodes tingitana, commonly known as the Moroccan wallflower, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in full sun conditions. Its leaves are usually gray-green and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

Hieraciodes tingitana is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
