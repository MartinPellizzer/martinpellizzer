Hedera algeriensis variegata

Hedera algeriensis variegata, commonly known as Algerian ivy, is a climbing plant native to North Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive variegated pattern of green, white, and yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Its foliage is also popular in indoor gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.
