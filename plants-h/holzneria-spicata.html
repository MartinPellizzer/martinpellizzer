Holzneria spicata

Holzneria spicata, commonly known as the spiny leaf cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its spiny, fleshy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically clustered at the ends of the stems. Holzneria spicata is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
