Hippochaete ramosissima var. japonica

Hippochaete ramosissima var. japonica, commonly known as the Japanese fireweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features showy, pink to red bracts that resemble flowers, though they are actually modified leaves. The plant has a bushy growth habit with numerous branches and small, inconspicuous true flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes in temperate regions.
