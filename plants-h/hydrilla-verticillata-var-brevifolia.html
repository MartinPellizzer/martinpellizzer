Hydrilla verticillata var. brevifolia

Hydrilla verticillata var. brevifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hydrilla, is a submerged aquatic plant native to Asia and the Americas.

It is characterized by its whorled arrangement of narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in width. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often submerged, and it thrives in freshwater environments with high nutrient levels. It is a fast-growing species that can form dense mats, impacting water quality and aquatic ecosystems.

Due to its invasive nature, it is often controlled through mechanical removal or chemical herbicides.
