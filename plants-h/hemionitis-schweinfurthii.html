Hemionitis schweinfurthii

Hemionitis schweinfurthii, commonly known as the African waterwheel, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its whorled arrangement of narrow, grass-like leaves.

It thrives in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds and marshes, where it often forms dense mats on the water's surface. The plant's leaves are typically green to bluish-green, and they are adapted to float and spread across the water. It lacks true roots and instead relies on its rhizomes for anchoring and nutrient absorption.

Hemionitis schweinfurthii plays a role in stabilizing water bodies and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
