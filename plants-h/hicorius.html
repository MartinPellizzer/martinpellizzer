Hicorius

Hicorius, commonly known as the horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy white or pink flowers that appear in spring. The tree produces spiny, capsule-like fruits containing shiny, chestnut-like seeds. Hicorius is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also known for its medicinal properties.

Its botanical name, *Aesculus hippocastanum*, reflects its historical and cultural significance.
