Hieracium murorum subsp. atrisquamatum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

atrisquamatum, commonly known as the black-squamate hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dark, glandular-squamate leaves and robust growth habit. It typically thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and other open habitats.

Due to its ornamental appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some regions.
