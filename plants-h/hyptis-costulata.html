Hyptis costulata

Hyptis costulata, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a perennial herb native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Hyptis costulata is also valued for its medicinal properties, used in traditional remedies for various ailments.
