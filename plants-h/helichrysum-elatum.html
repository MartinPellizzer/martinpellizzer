Helichrysum elatum

Helichrysum elatum, commonly known as the everlasting or golden marguerite, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, fluffy yellow or golden inflorescences that resemble dandelion blooms. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates. Its flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their long-lasting nature and vibrant color.

Helichrysum elatum is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
