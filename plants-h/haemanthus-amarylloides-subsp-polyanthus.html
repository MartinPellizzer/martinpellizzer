Haemanthus amarylloides subsp. polyanthus

Haemanthus amarylloides subsp.

polyanthus, commonly known as the Natal giant lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to southern Africa. It features large, showy flowers that are typically pink or red, arranged in a dense, umbel-like cluster.

The plant has strap-shaped, evergreen leaves that form a rosette at the base. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
