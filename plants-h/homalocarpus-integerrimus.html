Homalocarpus integerrimus

Homalocarpus integerrimus, commonly known as the yellow mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees, primarily in New Zealand.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually green but may turn reddish in certain conditions. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, fleshy berries that are a food source for native birds. Its root system forms haustoria, which penetrate the host's vascular system to extract water and nutrients.

Due to its parasitic nature and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
