Hieracium rosulatum

Hieracium rosulatum, commonly known as the Rosulate Hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are rosette-like, with a woolly texture, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Hieracium rosulatum is often found in temperate regions and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.
