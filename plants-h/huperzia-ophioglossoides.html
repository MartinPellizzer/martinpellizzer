Huperzia ophioglossoides

Huperzia ophioglossoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf huperzia, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its simple, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant is evergreen and thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate regions. Its spore-producing structures, called sporangia, are clustered on the underside of the leaves.

Huperzia ophioglossoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
