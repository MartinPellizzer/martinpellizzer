Halocnemum caspicum var. belangerianum

Halocnemum caspicum var. belangerianum, commonly known as the Belanger's sea blush, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and salt marshes, where it thrives in high-salinity soils. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually green or reddish in color.

This variety is notable for its ability to tolerate extreme environmental conditions, making it an important component of arid and coastal ecosystems.
