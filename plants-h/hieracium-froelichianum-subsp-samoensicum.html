Hieracium froelichianum subsp. samoensicum

Hieracium froelichianum subsp.

samoensicum, commonly known as the Samoan hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the islands of Samoa. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky areas. Its leaves are lanceolate, with a rough texture, and its flowers are yellow, forming dense clusters.

It is notable for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and its role in local ecosystems.
