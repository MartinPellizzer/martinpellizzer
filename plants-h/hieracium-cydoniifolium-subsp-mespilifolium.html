Hieracium cydoniifolium subsp. mespilifolium

Hieracium cydoniifolium subsp.

mespilifolium, commonly known as the apple-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow ray florets.

The leaves are typically lobed and resemble the shape of an apple leaf, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows.

The plant is known for its showy flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.
