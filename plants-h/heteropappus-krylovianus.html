Heteropappus krylovianus

Heteropappus krylovianus, commonly known as the Krylov's heteropappus, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and characteristic ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, displaying a low, spreading growth habit. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer.

The species is noted for its adaptability to harsh conditions and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
