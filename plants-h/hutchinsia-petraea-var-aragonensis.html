Hutchinsia petraea var. aragonensis

Hutchinsia petraea var. aragonensis, commonly known as the Aragonese rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of blooms.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often found in alpine and subalpine regions.
