Hieracium vulgatum subsp. pseudosciaphilum

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

pseudosciaphilum, commonly known as the false shade-loving hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

This subspecies is often found in calcareous soils and is relatively uncommon in the wild.
