Hieracium sinuosum

Hieracium sinuosum, commonly known as the sinuous hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium sinuosum is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in various habitats.
