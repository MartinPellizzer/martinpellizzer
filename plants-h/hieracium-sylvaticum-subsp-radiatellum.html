Hieracium sylvaticum subsp. radiatellum

Hieracium sylvaticum subsp.

radiatellum, commonly known as the woodland hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy surface. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a flat-topped cluster, and it produces small, elongated achenes as seeds.

It is often found in association with other woodland flora, contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
