Helichrysum wightii

Helichrysum wightii, commonly known as the yellow everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of yellow, daisy-like flowers that remain intact even after the plant has dried, giving it a long-lasting appearance. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its aromatic foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its resilience and ornamental value have contributed to its cultivation in various regions beyond its native habitat.
