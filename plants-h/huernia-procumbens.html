Huernia procumbens

Huernia procumbens, commonly known as the African ice plant, is a succulent belonging to the Asphodelaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and small, star-shaped flowers. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are usually blue-green in color and can be covered in a fine, powdery coating.

Huernia procumbens is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
