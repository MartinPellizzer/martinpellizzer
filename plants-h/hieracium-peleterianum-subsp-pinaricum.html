Hieracium peleterianum subsp. pinaricum

Hieracium peleterianum subsp.

pinaricum, commonly known as the Mediterranean hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette-like growth habit and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has hairy stems and leaves that are usually lanceolate in shape.

The subspecies is distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and morphological traits adapted to its Mediterranean climate.
