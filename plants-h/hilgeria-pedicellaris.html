Hilgeria pedicellaris

Hilgeria pedicellaris, commonly known as the desert milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Hilgeria pedicellaris is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.
