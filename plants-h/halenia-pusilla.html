Halenia pusilla

Halenia pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf halenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters and features glossy, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom in the spring. Halenia pusilla is well adapted to humid, shaded environments and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.

It is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
