Huernia hislopii

Huernia hislopii, commonly known as the African violet cactus, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems that are often ribbed and covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red, typically blooming in the evening. Its leaves are usually rounded and arranged in a rosette pattern, providing structural support and aiding in water storage.

Huernia hislopii is well adapted to arid environments, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
