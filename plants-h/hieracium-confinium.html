Hieracium confinium

Hieracium confinium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom from June to August.

Hieracium confinium is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
