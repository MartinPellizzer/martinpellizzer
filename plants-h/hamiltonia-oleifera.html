Hamiltonia oleifera

Hamiltonia oleifera, commonly known as the Mexican oil tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Fabaceae.

It is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States. The plant is characterized by its deep root system, which allows it to thrive in dry conditions. Its small, pea-like flowers are typically pale green to yellow in color.

The tree is valued for its oil-rich seeds, which have been used traditionally for various purposes.
