Hypericum bourgaei

Hypericum bourgaei, commonly known as the Madagascar golden strobilus, is a shrub native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from December to March. The plant features narrow, woody stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils in dry, coastal areas.

Hypericum bourgaei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
