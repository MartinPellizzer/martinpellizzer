Hieracium vernicosum

Hieracium vernicosum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are usually lance-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and other open areas.

Hieracium vernicosum is native to Europe but has been introduced to other parts of the world, often becoming invasive in certain regions.
