Hibiscus surattensis var. villosus

Hibiscus surattensis var. villosus, commonly known as the hairy hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink or red. The leaves are typically broad and ovate, with a velvety texture. This species thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas or near water bodies.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
