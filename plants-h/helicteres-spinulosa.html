Helicteres spinulosa

Helicteres spinulosa, commonly known as the prickly helicteres, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant produces spiny fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its ecosystem, supporting various insect and bird species.
