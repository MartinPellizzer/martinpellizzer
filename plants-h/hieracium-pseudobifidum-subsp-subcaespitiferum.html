Hieracium pseudobifidum subsp. subcaespitiferum

Hieracium pseudobifidum subsp.

subcaespitiferum, commonly known as the clustered hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, meadows, and waste areas.

The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in late spring to early summer. Its subspecies is characterized by its dense, clustered growth habit and shorter, more compact stems compared to other members of the species.

It is part of the Asteraceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
