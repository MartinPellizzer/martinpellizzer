Hyptis brachyphylla

Hyptis brachyphylla, commonly known as Brazilian hyptis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its grayish-green, hairy leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Hyptis brachyphylla is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional remedies for various ailments.
