Hieracium murorum subsp. minutulescens

Hieracium murorum subsp.

minutulescens, commonly known as the small-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in moist meadows, pastures, and woodland edges.

The plant has basal leaves that are narrow and hairy, with a rosette formation, and produces yellow flowers in early summer. Its inflorescence consists of a single, terminal flower head with ray florets.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smaller leaves and shorter stature compared to other hawkweed species.
