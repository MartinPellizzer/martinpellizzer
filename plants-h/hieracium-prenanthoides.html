Hieracium prenanthoides

Hieracium prenanthoides, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and disturbed soils, exhibiting a low-growing habit with hairy stems. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are yellow, with ray florets surrounding a central disk, contributing to its ornamental appearance.
