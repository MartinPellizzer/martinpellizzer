Hedera colchica f. flavovariegata

Hedera colchica f. flavovariegata, commonly known as the golden-variegated Persian ivy, is a varietal form of the Persian ivy, native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, featuring bright yellow and white patterns on its heart-shaped leaves. The plant is a climbing evergreen vine that thrives in shady, moist environments. Its leaves are typically leathery and have a glossy texture, adding ornamental value to gardens and indoor spaces.

It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by dark blue berries, though the berries are toxic to humans and animals.
