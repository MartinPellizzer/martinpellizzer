Heuchera americana var. typica

Heuchera americana var. typica, commonly known as the American alumroot, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It features a low-growing, clump-forming habit with fibrous roots and a rosette of heart-shaped, hairy leaves. The plant produces upright, greenish-white flower spikes in early spring, which are borne on slender stalks. Its foliage is often variegated with shades of red, bronze, and silver, making it a popular ornamental plant.

It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, often found in woodland settings.
