Hieracium caesium subsp. macrocalyx

Hieracium caesium subsp.

macrocalyx, commonly known as the large-flowered hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement.

The plant features basal leaves that are lanceolate to ovate, with a rough, hairy surface. Its showy yellow flowers are typically arranged in a single head, surrounded by pale green ray florets.

This subspecies is notable for its robust growth and is often found in meadows and grassy areas.
