Haplorhus peruviana

Haplorhus peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian grass, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in arid gardens.
