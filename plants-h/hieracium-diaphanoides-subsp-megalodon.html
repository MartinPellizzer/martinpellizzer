Hieracium diaphanoides subsp. megalodon

Hieracium diaphanoides subsp.

megalodon, commonly known as the giant hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leaves with a distinctive, often hairy texture.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and meadows, and can form dense colonies. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a rough, serrated edge, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

It is notable for its robust growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
