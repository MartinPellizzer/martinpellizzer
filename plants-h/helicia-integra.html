Helicia integra

Helicia integra, commonly known as the glossy ironwood, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and smooth, glossy bark. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
