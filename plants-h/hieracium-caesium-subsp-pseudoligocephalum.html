Hieracium caesium subsp. pseudoligocephalum

Hieracium caesium subsp.

pseudoligocephalum, commonly known as the purple hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are broadly lance-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces, and its flowers are typically purple or reddish in color.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
