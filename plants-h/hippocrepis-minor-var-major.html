Hippocrepis minor var. major

Hippocrepis minor var. major, commonly known as the Greater Rock Orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its flowers are usually pink or purple with a yellow lip.

This variety is notable for its resilience and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
