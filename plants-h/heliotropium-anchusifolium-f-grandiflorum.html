Heliotropium anchusifolium f. grandiflorum

Heliotropium anchusifolium f. grandiflorum, commonly known as the large-flowered heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
