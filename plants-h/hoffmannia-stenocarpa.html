Hoffmannia stenocarpa

Hoffmannia stenocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-fruited hoffmannia, is a member of the Ericaceae family, commonly found in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a shrub characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat preferences, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
