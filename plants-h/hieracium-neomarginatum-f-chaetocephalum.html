Hieracium neomarginatum f. chaetocephalum

Hieracium neomarginatum f. chaetocephalum, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a botanical variant of the hawkweed genus characterized by its distinctive, dense, white to grayish woolly foliage.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, temperate climates with well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of a single, showy yellow flower with a hairy base, which is surrounded by a cluster of leaf-like bracts.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and has been cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
