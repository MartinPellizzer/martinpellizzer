Huperzia nummulariifolia

Huperzia nummulariifolia, commonly known as the silver moss or the nummularia fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. The plant features small, overlapping, ovate to round-shaped leaves that are often covered in a silvery coating, giving it its common name. It is native to regions in East Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, where it grows in forest understories.

Huperzia nummulariifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural settings for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
