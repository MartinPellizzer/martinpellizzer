Heliotropium erianthum

Heliotropium erianthum, commonly known as the woolly heliotrope, is an annual flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple, often with a fragrant scent. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Heliotropium erianthum is known for its adaptability and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
