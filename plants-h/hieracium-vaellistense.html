Hieracium vaellistense

Hieracium vaellistense, commonly known as the Valais hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the European Alps.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Hieracium vaellistense is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
