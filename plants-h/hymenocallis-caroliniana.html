Hymenocallis caroliniana

Hymenocallis caroliniana, commonly known as the Carolina rain lily, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves and produces a bulbous underground stem called a corm. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

Hymenocallis caroliniana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in garden settings.
