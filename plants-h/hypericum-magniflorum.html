Hypericum magniflorum

Hypericum magniflorum, commonly known as the showy St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and woody stems, which are typical features of the Hypericaceae family. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full sun. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Hypericum magniflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
