Hosta albomarginata f. viridis

Hosta albomarginata f. viridis, commonly known as the Green-edged White Hosta, is a varietal form of the hosta species characterized by its unique leaf morphology.

The leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a striking contrast between the bright green center and the pale white margins. This cultivar typically grows to a height of about 12 to 18 inches, making it a compact and ornamental addition to shaded gardens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, displaying excellent tolerance to humid conditions.

The foliage remains lush throughout the growing season, offering visual interest even when not in bloom.
