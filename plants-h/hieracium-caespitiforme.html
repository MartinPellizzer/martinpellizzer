Hieracium caespitiforme

Hieracium caespitiforme, commonly known as the clustered hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides. The plant features basal rosettes of leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are hairy and have a rough texture, while its stems are upright and often reddish in color.

Hieracium caespitiforme is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
