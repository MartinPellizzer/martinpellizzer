Helicia vestita

Helicia vestita, commonly known as the golden helicia, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by elliptical, woody fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.

Helicia vestita is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
