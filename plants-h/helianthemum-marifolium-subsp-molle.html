Helianthemum marifolium subsp. molle

Helianthemum marifolium subsp.

molle, commonly known as the softleaf rockrose, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, dense clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are generally soft and leathery, with a slightly woolly texture, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
