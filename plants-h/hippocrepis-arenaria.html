Hippocrepis arenaria

Hippocrepis arenaria, commonly known as the sand orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it has a fibrous root system adapted to arid conditions.

The sand orchid is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils and supporting local pollinators.
