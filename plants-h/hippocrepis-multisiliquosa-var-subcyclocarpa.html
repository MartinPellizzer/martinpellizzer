Hippocrepis multisiliquosa var. subcyclocarpa

Hippocrepis multisiliquosa var. subcyclocarpa, commonly known as the cyclocarpic hawksbeard, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and pinnate leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its fruits are distinctive, being short, club-shaped silique with a unique cyclocarpic structure.
