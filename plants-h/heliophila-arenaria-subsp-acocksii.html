Heliophila arenaria subsp. acocksii

Heliophila arenaria subsp.

acocksii, commonly known as the sand heliophyte, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments.

The plant features slender, creeping stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss. Its small, white or pale blue flowers bloom in clusters, usually during the warmer months.

This subspecies is well-suited to harsh conditions and plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.
