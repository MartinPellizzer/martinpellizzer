Hypericum grandiflorum

Hypericum grandiflorum, commonly known as the large-flowered St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include small, elliptical leaves and the presence of yellow, glandular dots on the stems and leaves.

Hypericum grandiflorum is often found in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and streambanks.
