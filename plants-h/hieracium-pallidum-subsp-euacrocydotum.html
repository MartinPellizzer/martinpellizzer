Hieracium pallidum subsp. euacrocydotum

Hieracium pallidum subsp.

euacrocydotum, commonly known as the pale hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.

The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, pale green leaves and produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are typically arranged in a single head, with ray florets that are often shorter than the central disk florets.

The species is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, and it is often found in moist or calcareous soils.
