Haloragis asperrima

Haloragis asperrima, commonly known as the roughleaf willowherb, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in coastal areas and saline soils. The plant features narrow, rough-textured leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink or red. It is adapted to withstand salt spray and sandy environments.

Haloragis asperrima plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes and supporting local wildlife.
