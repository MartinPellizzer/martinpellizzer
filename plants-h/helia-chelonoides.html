Helia chelonoides

Helia chelonoides, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetlands and along stream banks. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. Its showy, yellow flowers bloom in late summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Helia chelonoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
