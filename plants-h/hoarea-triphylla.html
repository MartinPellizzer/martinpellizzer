Hoarea triphylla

Hoarea triphylla, commonly known as the three-leaf hoba, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Hoarea triphylla is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Its unique leaf structure and drought tolerance make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
