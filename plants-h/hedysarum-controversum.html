Hedysarum controversum

Hedysarum controversum, commonly known as the yellow peavine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces yellow flowers in early summer, followed by small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and rocky slopes.

Hedysarum controversum is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a food source for various wildlife species.
