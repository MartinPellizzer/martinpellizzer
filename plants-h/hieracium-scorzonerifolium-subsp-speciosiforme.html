Hieracium scorzonerifolium subsp. speciosiforme

Hieracium scorzonerifolium subsp.

speciosiforme, commonly known as the showy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies.

The plant features bright yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It often grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
