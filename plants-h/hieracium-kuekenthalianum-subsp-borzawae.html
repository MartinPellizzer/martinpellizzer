Hieracium kuekenthalianum subsp. borzawae

Hieracium kuekenthalianum subsp.

borzawae, commonly known as the Borzawa hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and flowering stems that bear yellow flowers in late summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has finely toothed leaves and produces small, disc-like flowers with yellow ray florets.

This subspecies is noted for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its resilience in arid environments.
