Hymenophyllum diversilobum

Hymenophyllum diversilobum, commonly known as the variable filmy fern, is a small, epiphytic or lithophytic fern that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent fronds that are often covered in a fine, hairy layer. The plant's spores are produced on the underside of its fronds in clusters called sori, which are protected by a membrane-like structure. Hymenophyllum diversilobum is native to regions of Southeast Asia and is often found growing on tree trunks or rocks.

Its unique morphology and ecological niche make it a distinctive member of the Hymenophyllaceae family.
