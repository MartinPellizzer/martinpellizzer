Himeranthus tridentatus

Himeranthus tridentatus, commonly known as the three-toothed plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture.
