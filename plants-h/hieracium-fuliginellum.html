Hieracium fuliginellum

Hieracium fuliginellum, commonly known as the smoky hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its flowers are yellow with a distinctive smoky or sooty appearance, giving it its common name.

Hieracium fuliginellum is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed habitats and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
