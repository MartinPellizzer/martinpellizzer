Hieracium chloropsis subsp. serresianum

Hieracium chloropsis subsp.

serresianum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow flower heads composed of numerous small tubular florets.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are basal, oblanceolate, and covered with fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
