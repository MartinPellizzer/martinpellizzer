Haageocereus chalaensis

Haageocereus chalaensis, commonly known as the Chalaensis Cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its columnar stems and spiny foliage, which help it survive in arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual element to its desert habitat. Its structure allows it to store water efficiently, making it well adapted to dry climates.

Botanically, it belongs to the Cactaceae family and is valued for its resilience and ornamental qualities.
