Hibiscus rossii

Hibiscus rossii, commonly known as the Hawaiian hibiscus, is a species native to the island of Hawaii.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, featuring large, showy flowers with five deeply lobed petals. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, and it produces bright yellow stamens surrounded by a prominent, five-lobed pistil. Hibiscus rossii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and wetlands.

It is an important plant in Hawaiian culture and ecology, providing habitat and nectar for native pollinators.
