Holostachyum buchananii

Holostachyum buchananii, commonly known as the African bamboo or giant bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae).

It is native to tropical Africa and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.

Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various soil types make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
