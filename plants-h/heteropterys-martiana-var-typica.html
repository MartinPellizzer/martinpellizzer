Heteropterys martiana var. typica

Heteropterys martiana var. typica, commonly known as the Brazilian wax tree, is a species native to tropical South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with thick, waxy leaves that give it its common name. The leaves are typically elliptical or ovate, with a glossy dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. It produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by red or orange berries.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
