Hicorius porcina

Hicorius porcina, commonly known as the boar's ear, is a species of wild mushroom that belongs to the family Boletaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, irregularly shaped cap that ranges from yellow to brown in color. The stem is typically smooth and has a fibrous, sometimes cracked surface. The gills are attached to the stem and are usually pale yellow, turning reddish-brown with age.

This mushroom is often found in forests, growing in association with various tree species, particularly oaks and birches.
