Hyptis canescens

Hyptis canescens, commonly known as rough hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its grayish-green, hairy leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. It is often found in moist, shaded areas and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its aromatic properties make it a popular choice for ornamental and herbal purposes.
