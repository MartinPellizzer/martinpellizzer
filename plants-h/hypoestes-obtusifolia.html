Hypoestes obtusifolia

Hypoestes obtusifolia, commonly known as the "Magic Beanplant," is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble small flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant has opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, inconspicuous white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

Hypoestes obtusifolia is often cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its vibrant foliage and ease of care.
