Hemigenia bracteosa

Hemigenia bracteosa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed honey mint, is a small, perennial shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in spikes along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in swampy or wetland areas.

Hemigenia bracteosa is valued for its fragrant foliage and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
