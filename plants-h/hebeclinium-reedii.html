Hebeclinium reedii

Hebeclinium reedii, commonly known as the Reed's hebeclinium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It features upright, slender stems that are often covered in fine, hairy hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly rough texture.

Hebeclinium reedii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to well-drained soils.
