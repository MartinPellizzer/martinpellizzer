Helichrysum araneosum

Helichrysum araneosum, commonly known as the spider flower or golden oat, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, fluffy yellow flowers that resemble a spider's web, hence its common name. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, seed-like structures called achene. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Helichrysum araneosum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
