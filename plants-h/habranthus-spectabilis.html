Habranthus spectabilis

Habranthus spectabilis, commonly known as the showy bluebell, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a silvery-blue hue, adding to its ornamental appeal.

It is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is valued for its beauty in gardens and natural habitats.
