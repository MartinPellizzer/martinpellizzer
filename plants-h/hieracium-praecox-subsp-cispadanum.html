Hieracium praecox subsp. cispadanum

Hieracium praecox subsp.

cispadanum, commonly known as the early hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and along streambanks. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges.

Its flowers are composed of ray florets and are often visited by various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
