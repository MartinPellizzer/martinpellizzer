Hesperis armena var. pisidica

Hesperis armena var. pisidica, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
