Hieracium cruentum subsp. rehmannii

Hieracium cruentum subsp.

rehmannii, commonly known as the bloodroot hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

The plant features basal rosettes of dark green, hairy leaves and produces yellowish-red flowers in late spring to early summer. It has a taproot system and is known for its ability to thrive in shaded environments.

The species is part of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its distinctive flowering structure.
