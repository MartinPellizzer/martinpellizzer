Hieracium knafii subsp. leucothyrsoides

Hieracium knafii subsp.

leucothyrsoides, commonly known as the white thistle. It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, characterized by its hairy stems and clustered, pale yellow to white flowers.

The plant has basal and alternate leaves that are typically lobed or toothed. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
