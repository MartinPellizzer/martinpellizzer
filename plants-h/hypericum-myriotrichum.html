Hypericum myriotrichum

Hypericum myriotrichum, commonly known as the hairy St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite and oval-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Hypericum myriotrichum is part of the Hippocastanaceae family and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
