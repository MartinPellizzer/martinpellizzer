Hieracium neomalyi

Hieracium neomalyi, commonly known as the Neomaly thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads made up of many small florets. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators.

Its leaves are usually spiny and covered in fine hairs, which help protect it from herbivores.
