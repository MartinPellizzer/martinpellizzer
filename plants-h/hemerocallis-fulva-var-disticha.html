Hemerocallis fulva var. disticha

Hemerocallis fulva var. disticha, commonly known as the lesser daylily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, orange-red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The flowers are typically borne in clusters and have a distinct, spicy scent. This variety is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
