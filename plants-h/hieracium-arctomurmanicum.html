Hieracium arctomurmanicum

Hieracium arctomurmanicum, commonly known as the Arctic Murman hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold climates. It has small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Hieracium arctomurmanicum plays a role in stabilizing soil in its harsh environment and is part of the diverse flora found in polar and alpine ecosystems.
