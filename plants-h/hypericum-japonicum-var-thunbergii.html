Hypericum japonicum var. thunbergii

Hypericum japonicum var. thunbergii, commonly known as the Japanese St. John's Wort, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a slightly hairy texture. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties, though it contains compounds that can be toxic if ingested in large quantities.
