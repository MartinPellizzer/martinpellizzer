Hemsleya sphaerocarpa subsp. megathyrsa

Hemsleya sphaerocarpa subsp.

megathyrsa, commonly known as the giant ball cactus, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of South America. It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that can grow up to several meters in height.

The plant produces large, spherical, yellowish flowers that bloom during the dry season. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss in its harsh environment.

This subspecies is noted for its striking appearance and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
