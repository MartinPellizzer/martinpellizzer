Hedera helix var. melanocarpa

Hedera helix var. melanocarpa, commonly known as the Black Beauty ivy, is a variant of the common English ivy.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black leaves that contrast sharply with its pale green veins. This evergreen climbing plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in shaded environments and is often used for ground cover or as a decorative plant in gardens.

Its adaptability to various soil types and moisture levels makes it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
