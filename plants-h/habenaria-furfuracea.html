Habenaria furfuracea

Habenaria furfuracea, commonly known as the hairy orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to parts of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive hairy leaves and intricate floral structure. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky areas. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a prominent lip, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Habenaria furfuracea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological significance in its native habitats.
