Hieracium camkorijense subsp. bakurianense

Hieracium camkorijense subsp.

bakurianense, commonly known as the Bakurian hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, tubular florets arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its range.
