Hsuehochloa calcarea

Hsuehochloa calcarea, commonly known as the calcarea grass, is a member of the Poaceae family and is native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and gray-green foliage, which helps it withstand harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its resilience, it is sometimes used in restoration projects in degraded landscapes.
