Hieracium brachiatum subsp. flaviforme

Hieracium brachiatum subsp.

flaviforme, commonly known as the yellow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of broadly ovate, yellowish-green leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically surrounded by pale green or white ray florets. It thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, often forming dense mats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
