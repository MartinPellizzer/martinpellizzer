Haemodorum flaviflorum

Haemodorum flaviflorum, commonly known as the golden-flowered bloodroot, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the family Haemodoraceae, which includes species known for their striking, often red or orange-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive reddish hue when young.

The flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in dense inflorescences, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
