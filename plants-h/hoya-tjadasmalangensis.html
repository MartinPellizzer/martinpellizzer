Hoya tjadasmalangensis

Hoya tjadasmalangensis, commonly known as the Tjadasmalang hoya, is a rare and unique species native to Indonesia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and clusters of waxy, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is a sought-after plant among hoya enthusiasts.
