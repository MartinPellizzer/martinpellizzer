Heterotheca scabrifolia

Heterotheca scabrifolia, commonly known as roughleaf bluemink, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine, rough textures. Its flowers are typically blue to purple in color, with ray florets that add to its ornamental appeal.

Heterotheca scabrifolia thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.
