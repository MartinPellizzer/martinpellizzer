Hibiscus furcellatus

Hibiscus furcellatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf hibiscus, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically blooming in summer. The plant has simple, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly hairy underside. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy soils.

Hibiscus furcellatus is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
