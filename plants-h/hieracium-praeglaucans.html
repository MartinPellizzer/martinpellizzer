Hieracium praeglaucans

Hieracium praeglaucans, commonly known as the early white hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, erect stems and leaves that are often glandular and slightly rough to the touch. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It is native to Europe and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils.

Hieracium praeglaucans is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
