Hieracium lepiduloides

Hieracium lepiduloides, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are rough and hairy, often with a bluish-green hue, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium lepiduloides is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
