Harrisia guelichii

Harrisia guelichii, commonly known as the red harrisia, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of vibrant red flowers that bloom during the summer months. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often consumed by local wildlife. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by cactus enthusiasts.
