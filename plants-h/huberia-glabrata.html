Huberia glabrata

Huberia glabrata, commonly known as the smooth huberia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Huberia glabrata is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique floral display.
