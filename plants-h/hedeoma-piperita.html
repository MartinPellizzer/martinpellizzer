Hedeoma piperita

Hedeoma piperita, commonly known as peppermint, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, slightly hairy leaves and its upright, square stems. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the stem. Peppermint contains high levels of menthol and other essential oils, which contribute to its distinctive scent and medicinal properties.

It is widely cultivated for use in aromatherapy, herbal remedies, and as a flavoring agent.
