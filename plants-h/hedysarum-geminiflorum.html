Hedysarum geminiflorum

Hedysarum geminiflorum, commonly known as the twin-flowered vetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant produces cylindrical, hairy pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands.

Hedysarum geminiflorum plays a role in soil stabilization and supports pollinators with its nectar-rich blooms.
