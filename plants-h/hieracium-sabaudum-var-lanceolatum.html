Hieracium sabaudum var. lanceolatum

Hieracium sabaudum var. lanceolatum, commonly known as the Sabaudian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that are arranged in a flat-topped cluster, typical of the daisy family. Its leaves are lanceolate, hence the variety name, and are usually covered with fine hairs.

It thrives in meadows and grasslands, often forming dense colonies in disturbed soils.
