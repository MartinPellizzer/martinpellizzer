Hieracium fraudulentum

Hieracium fraudulentum, commonly known as the false hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and basal rosettes of lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish to orange composite flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils.

Due to its invasive tendencies, it can outcompete native vegetation in certain regions.
