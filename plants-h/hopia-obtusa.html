Hopia obtusa

Hopia obtusa, commonly known as the broadleaf hopia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. Its leaves are broad and oval, with a rough texture and a distinct, aromatic scent.

Hopia obtusa is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
