Hippeastrum spectabile

Hippeastrum spectabile, commonly known as the showy amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It features large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, typically in shades of red, pink, white, or yellow. The plant produces strap-like, sword-shaped leaves that emerge from the base of the bulb. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Hippeastrum spectabile is known for its long blooming period, often flowering in late winter to early spring.
