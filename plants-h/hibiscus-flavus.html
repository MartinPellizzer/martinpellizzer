Hibiscus flavus

Hibiscus flavus, commonly known as the yellow hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers with five petals and a prominent central stamen. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and often covered in fine hairs.

Hibiscus flavus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
