Hieracium scapigerum subsp. falacronense

Hieracium scapigerum subsp.

falacronense, commonly known as the Falacronense hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

The plant has basal rosettes of leaves and produces tall, unbranched stems with small, yellowish-green flowers. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs.

It is characterized by its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and its role as a pioneer species in ecological succession.
