Helianthus leiostemon

Helianthus leiostemon, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blooms. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces bright yellow flowers that resemble those of other sunflower species. It typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.

Helianthus leiostemon is often found in coastal regions and is adapted to tolerate saline conditions.
