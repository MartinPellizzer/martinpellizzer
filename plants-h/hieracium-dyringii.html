Hieracium dyringii

Hieracium dyringii, commonly known as the Dyring's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are usually yellow or orange in color.

Hieracium dyringii is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
