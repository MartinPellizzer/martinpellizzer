Hieracium radiatile

Hieracium radiatile, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically has hairy stems and leaves that are lance-shaped with serrated edges. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to spread across various habitats.
