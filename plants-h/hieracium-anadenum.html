Hieracium anadenum

Hieracium anadenum, commonly known as the Anatolian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Hieracium anadenum is known for its hairy stems and basal leaves that are often lobed or toothed.
