Hypoxis cryptophylla

Hypoxis cryptophylla, commonly known as the starry false shamrock, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Hypoxidaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, star-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in a dense cluster. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or containers.

Hypoxis cryptophylla is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique appearance.
