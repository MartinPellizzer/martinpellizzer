Hibiscus calyphyllus

Hibiscus calyphyllus, commonly known as the Hawaiian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 3 to 5 meters, featuring palmate leaves with five to seven lobes. The plant produces showy, single flowers that are usually pink or red, with a prominent central pistil and stamens. Hibiscus calyphyllus thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and disturbed sites.

Its flowers bloom throughout the year, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
