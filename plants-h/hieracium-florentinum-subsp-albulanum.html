Hieracium florentinum subsp. albulanum

Hieracium florentinum subsp.

albulanum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. It has a low-growing habit and is often found in open, sunny environments at high elevations.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
