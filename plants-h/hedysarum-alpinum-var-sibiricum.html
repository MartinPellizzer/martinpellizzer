Hedysarum alpinum var. sibiricum

Hedysarum alpinum var. sibiricum, commonly known as the Siberian alpine vetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, trifoliate leaves and produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of pink or purple. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. It is adapted to cold climates and can withstand harsh environmental conditions.

This species is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, contributing to soil fertility in its native ecosystems.
