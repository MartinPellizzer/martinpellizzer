Holtzea umbellata

Holtzea umbellata, commonly known as the umbrella tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded clusters of small white or pale yellow flowers, which resemble an umbrella, giving it its common name. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are arranged in a compact rosette. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Holtzea umbellata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
