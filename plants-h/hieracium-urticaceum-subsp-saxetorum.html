Hieracium urticaceum subsp. saxetorum

Hieracium urticaceum subsp.

saxetorum, commonly known as the saxicola hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and southern Europe. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

The plant features a basal rosette of leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it produces yellow flowers in clusters. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

This subspecies is characterized by its ability to thrive in harsh, nutrient-poor environments.
