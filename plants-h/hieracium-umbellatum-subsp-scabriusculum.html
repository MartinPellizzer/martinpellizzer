Hieracium umbellatum subsp. scabriusculum

Hieracium umbellatum subsp.

scabriusculum, commonly known as the rough nipplewort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are rough and hairy, with a distinctive lobed shape, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer.

It is often found in moist or sandy soils and is known for its ability to thrive in challenging environments.
