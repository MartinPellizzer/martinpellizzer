Hieracium phalacrophyllum

Hieracium phalacrophyllum, commonly known as the black-leaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its dark green, often hairy leaves and yellowish-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. Native to Europe, this species typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and woodland edges. The plant has a rosette of basal leaves and produces numerous small, tubular flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is a perennial herb that can spread through rhizomes, forming dense colonies in suitable habitats.
