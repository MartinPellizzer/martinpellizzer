Hibiscus noldeae

Hibiscus noldeae, commonly known as the Nolde's hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas, characterized by its woody stems and large, showy flowers. The plant produces bright yellow or orange flowers with five petals, which bloom during the summer months. Hibiscus noldeae is adapted to the tropical climate and thrives in well-drained soils.

It is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is often found in coastal gardens and natural habitats.
