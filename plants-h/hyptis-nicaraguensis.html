Hyptis nicaraguensis

Hyptis nicaraguensis, commonly known as the Nicaraguan hyptis, is a perennial shrub native to Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Hyptis nicaraguensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
