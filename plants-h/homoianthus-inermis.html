Homoianthus inermis

Homoianthus inermis, commonly known as the slender panic grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are borne on long, branching stalks. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
