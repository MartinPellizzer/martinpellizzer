Hypericum denudatum

Hypericum denudatum, commonly known as the naked St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, woody stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and adaptable species within its native range.
