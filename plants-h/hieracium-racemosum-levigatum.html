Hieracium racemosum-levigatum

Hieracium racemosum-levigatum, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with ray florets.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
