Hadrolaelia purpurata f. virginalis

Hadrolaelia purpurata f. virginalis, commonly known as the Virginal Violet Orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers with intricate patterns and a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb and long, narrow leaves. It prefers cool, humid environments with partial shade and well-draining soil.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
