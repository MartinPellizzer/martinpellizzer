Heteropterys confertiflora f. eglandulosa

Heteropterys confertiflora f. eglandulosa, commonly known as the glandular-flowered heteropterys, is a botanical variant characterized by its dense, clustered inflorescences.

The plant exhibits a shrub-like growth habit with elliptical, glossy leaves that are typically dark green in color. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in compact clusters, giving it a delicate appearance. The foliage is often covered with glandular hairs, which contribute to its unique botanical features.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
