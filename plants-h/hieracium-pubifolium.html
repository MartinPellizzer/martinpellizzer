Hieracium pubifolium

Hieracium pubifolium, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are hairy and lobed, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium pubifolium is often found in temperate climates and can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
