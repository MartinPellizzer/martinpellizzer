Heterotis canescens

Heterotis canescens, commonly known as the African water spinach, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that grows in wetland and marshy areas, often found near water bodies. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its small, greenish-white flowers are borne in clusters and are followed by small, round fruits.

It is valued for its edible leaves, which are used in local cuisines and have medicinal properties.
