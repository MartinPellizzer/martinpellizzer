Hieracium cinerelliforme

Hieracium cinerelliforme, commonly known as the ash-gray hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in grassy areas and disturbed soils. The plant features bright yellow ray florets surrounded by a dense cluster of tubular disc florets. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a grayish appearance.

It blooms from late spring to early autumn, producing a showy display that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.
