Hieracium parcum

Hieracium parcum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in a flat-topped cluster. The plant is native to Europe and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It has a taproot system and is perennial, blooming from late spring to early autumn.

The flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.
