Hemionitis purpusii

Hemionitis purpusii, commonly known as the rock spikerush, is a unique aquatic plant found in the wetlands of southern Africa.

It thrives in shallow, freshwater environments, often in rocky or sandy substrates. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its tall, spiky inflorescence and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its reproductive structures are adapted for water dispersal, aiding in its spread across wetland habitats.

Hemionitis purpusii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
