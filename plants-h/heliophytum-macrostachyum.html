Heliophytum macrostachyum

Heliophytum macrostachyum, commonly known as the large-stemmed sunplant, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry conditions. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its succulent nature makes it drought-tolerant and suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Heliophytum macrostachyum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to add visual interest to low-maintenance landscapes.
