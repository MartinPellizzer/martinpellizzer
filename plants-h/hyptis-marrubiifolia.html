Hyptis marrubiifolia

Hyptis marrubiifolia, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves and spikes of blue or purple flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardens and landscape design.
