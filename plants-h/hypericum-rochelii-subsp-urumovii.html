Hypericum rochelii subsp. urumovii

Hypericum rochelii subsp.

urumovii, commonly known as the Urumov's St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the Caucasus region. It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typical of the Hypericaceae family.

The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces red or orange berries that are edible but not widely consumed. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
