Hordeum vulgare var. tetranutans

Hordeum vulgare var. tetranutans, commonly known as the tetranutant barley, is a variant of barley characterized by its unique botanical features.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is typically found in temperate regions. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and produces spikelets that are often clustered in groups of four, giving it its specific epithet. Its leaves are narrow and have a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is sometimes cultivated for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil conditions.
