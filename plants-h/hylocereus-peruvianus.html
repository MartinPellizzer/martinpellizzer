Hylocereus peruvianus

Hylocereus peruvianus, commonly known as the pitaya or dragon fruit, is a climbing cactus belonging to the family Cactaceae.

It is native to Central and South America, particularly regions with warm, tropical climates. The plant produces striking, night-blooming flowers that are typically white or pink in color. Its fruit, which is the most commercially valuable part, comes in various colors including red, yellow, and white, and is known for its sweet, mildly tart flavor.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in arid environments.
