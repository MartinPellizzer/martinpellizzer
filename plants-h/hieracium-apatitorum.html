Hieracium apatitorum

Hieracium apatitorum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a hairy texture. It is often found in open habitats and can tolerate a range of soil conditions.

Hieracium apatitorum is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in certain environments.
