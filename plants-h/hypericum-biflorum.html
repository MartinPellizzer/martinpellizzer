Hypericum biflorum

Hypericum biflorum, commonly known as the two-flowered St. John's wort, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typically from June to August. The plant has woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Hypericum biflorum is noted for its medicinal properties, containing compounds with potential anti-inflammatory and antidepressant effects.
