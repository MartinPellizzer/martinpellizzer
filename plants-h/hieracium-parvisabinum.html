Hieracium parvisabinum

Hieracium parvisabinum, commonly known as the small-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and leaves arranged in a basal rosette. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its flowering period occurs from late spring to early autumn, producing yellow disc flowers surrounded by pale yellow to white ray florets.

The species is noted for its small, lance-shaped leaves and is often found in temperate regions across the continent.
