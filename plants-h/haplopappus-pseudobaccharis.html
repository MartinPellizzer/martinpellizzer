Haplopappus pseudobaccharis

Haplopappus pseudobaccharis, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is well adapted to drought conditions. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Haplopappus pseudobaccharis is also known for its ability to thrive in harsh environments, making it an important species in desert ecosystems.
