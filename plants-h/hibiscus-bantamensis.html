Hibiscus bantamensis

Hibiscus bantamensis, commonly known as the Bantam hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red, with a prominent yellow center. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch and are often covered in fine hairs. Hibiscus bantamensis thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It is also used in traditional medicine in some parts of its native range.
