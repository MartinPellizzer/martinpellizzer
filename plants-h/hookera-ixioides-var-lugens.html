Hookera ixioides var. lugens

Hookera ixioides var. lugens, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming growth habit and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue and violet. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky areas. Its leaves are typically lanceolate and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help to reduce water loss.

Hookera ixioides var. lugens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
