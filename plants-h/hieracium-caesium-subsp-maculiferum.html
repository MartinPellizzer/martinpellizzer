Hieracium caesium subsp. maculiferum

Hieracium caesium subsp.

maculiferum, commonly known as the spotted hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow flowers.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while the stems are often reddish and hairy. It is known for its ability to spread rapidly through rhizomes, making it an invasive species in some regions.

The plant thrives in open, disturbed sites such as meadows and roadsides.
