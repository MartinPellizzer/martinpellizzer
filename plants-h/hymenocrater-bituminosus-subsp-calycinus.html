Hymenocrater bituminosus subsp. calycinus

Hymenocrater bituminosus subsp.

calycinus, commonly known as the black-barked lichen, is a foliose lichen characterized by its dark, almost black, upper surface and a lighter, often greyish, lower surface. It typically grows on tree trunks and branches, especially in humid and shaded environments.

The thallus is leathery and has a distinct, almost velvety texture. It contains a variety of secondary metabolites, which contribute to its coloration and ecological interactions.

This lichen is often used as an indicator of air quality due to its sensitivity to environmental pollutants.
