Hieracium ellipticifrons

Hieracium ellipticifrons, commonly known as the elliptic-fronded hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. Its leaves are elliptical in shape and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

It is known for its ability to thrive in various soil types and its role in supporting local pollinators.
