Haageocereus versicolor

Haageocereus versicolor, commonly known as the rainbow cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, colorful flowers that range from yellow to red. The plant typically grows as a shrub with cylindrical stems and produces clusters of small, spiny leaves. Its flowers are often tubular and are pollinated by insects and bats.

Haageocereus versicolor is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-draining soil.
