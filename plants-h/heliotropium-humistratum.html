Heliotropium humistratum

Heliotropium humistratum, commonly known as the swamp heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, showcasing its adaptability to moist environments. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, blooming throughout the growing season. Its leaves are often heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Heliotropium humistratum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate moisture conditions.
