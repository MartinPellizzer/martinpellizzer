Hyptis rugosa var. canescens

Hyptis rugosa var. canescens, commonly known as silver hyptis, is a perennial shrub native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray, fuzzy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Hyptis rugosa var. canescens is also valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
