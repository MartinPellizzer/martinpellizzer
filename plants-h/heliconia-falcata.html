Heliconia falcata

Heliconia falcata, commonly known as the sickle heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, boat-shaped inflorescences. The plant features elongated, falcate (sickle-shaped) bracts that enclose its vibrant orange or red flowers. Its leaves are large, broad, and often have a waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Heliconia falcata is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
