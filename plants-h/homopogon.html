Homopogon

Homopogon, commonly known as the broadleaf plantain, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Plantaginaceae.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale green or yellowish in color. It is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Homopogon is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its healing benefits.
