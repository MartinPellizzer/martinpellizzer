Hydnocarpus kurzii subsp. australis

Hydnocarpus kurzii subsp.

australis, commonly known as the Australian ironwood, is a tropical tree native to northern Australia and parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its dense, hard wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, fleshy fruits that contain several seeds. Its leaves are typically alternate and simple, with a rough texture and a distinct, aromatic scent.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and riverine habitats.
