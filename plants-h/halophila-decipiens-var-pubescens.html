Halophila decipiens var. pubescens

Halophila decipiens var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy seagrass, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Alismataceae family.

It is a marine angiosperm that thrives in shallow coastal waters, particularly in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its dense, ribbon-like leaves that are covered with fine, hair-like structures, giving it its varietal name "pubescens.". It plays a crucial role in marine ecosystems by providing habitat and shelter for various aquatic organisms.

Its reproductive structures include small, inconspicuous flowers that are adapted for underwater pollination.
