Helminthotheca comosa subsp. comosa

Helminthotheca comosa subsp.

comosa, commonly known as the hairy helminthotheca, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features basal rosettes of silver-gray, woolly leaves that are densely covered in fine hairs. Its flowering stems bear small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense clusters.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its distinctive foliage.
