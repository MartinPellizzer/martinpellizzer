Hicorius integrifolia

Hicorius integrifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are typically smooth-edged and glossy in appearance. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, yellow, and orange, which bloom throughout the warm seasons. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Hicorius integrifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
