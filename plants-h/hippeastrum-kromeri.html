Hippeastrum kromeri

Hippeastrum kromeri, commonly known as the Kromer's amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces large, strap-like leaves that are broad and often have a waxy texture. It is typically grown as a bulbous plant, with the bulb storing nutrients for flowering.

Hippeastrum kromeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
