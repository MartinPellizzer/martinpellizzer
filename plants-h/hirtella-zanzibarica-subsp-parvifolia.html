Hirtella zanzibarica subsp. parvifolia

Hirtella zanzibarica subsp.

parvifolia, commonly known as Zanzibar hirtella, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of East Africa. It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture.

The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom throughout the dry season. Its fruits are capsules containing tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is adapted to sandy soils and saline environments, making it a hardy plant of coastal ecosystems.
