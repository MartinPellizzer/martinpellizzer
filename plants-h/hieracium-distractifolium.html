Hieracium distractifolium

Hieracium distractifolium, commonly known as the scattered-leaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

Hieracium distractifolium is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
