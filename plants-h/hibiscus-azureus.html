Hibiscus azureus

Hibiscus azureus, commonly known as the blue hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking blue-purplish flowers, which bloom in clusters and are typically 5 to 7 centimeters in diameter. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often a deep green color. Hibiscus azureus is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, with a woody stem and showy bracts that surround the actual flowers.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
