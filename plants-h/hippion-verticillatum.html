Hippion verticillatum

Hippion verticillatum, commonly known as the spiral leaved plant, is a unique species characterized by its distinctive whorled arrangement of leaves.

This plant typically features narrow, grass-like leaves that spiral around the stem in a compact, clustered formation. It is native to specific regions in South America, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant's botanical structure supports its adaptation to humid climates and nutrient-poor soils.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in some horticultural settings.
