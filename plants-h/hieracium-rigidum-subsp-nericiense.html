Hieracium rigidum subsp. nericiense

Hieracium rigidum subsp.

nericiense, commonly known as the rigid hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, often in sunny to partially shaded environments.

The plant features upright stems that are covered in stiff, bristly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are lance-shaped and also bear similar hairy textures, while the flower heads are yellow and composed of numerous small florets.

This subspecies is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
