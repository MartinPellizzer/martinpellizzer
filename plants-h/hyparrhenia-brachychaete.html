Hyparrhenia brachychaete

Hyparrhenia brachychaete, commonly known as the dwarf hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant has slender, tufted shoots with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

Hyparrhenia brachychaete is valued for its ornamental use in landscaping and for its role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
