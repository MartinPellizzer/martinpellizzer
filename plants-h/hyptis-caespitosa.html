Hyptis caespitosa

Hyptis caespitosa, commonly known as Brazilian hyssop, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Hyptis caespitosa is also valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
