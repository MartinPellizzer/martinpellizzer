Haemocharis curtyana

Haemocharis curtyana, commonly known as the golden flame plant, is a tropical perennial herb native to Brazil.

It features striking, bright yellow flowers with red markings, which are borne on long, slender stems. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Haemocharis curtyana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant and showy blooms.
