Hieracium murorum subsp. pyroloides

Hieracium murorum subsp.

pyroloides, commonly known as the fireweed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow to orange flower heads.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and meadows, and thrives in temperate climates. It produces small, composite flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
