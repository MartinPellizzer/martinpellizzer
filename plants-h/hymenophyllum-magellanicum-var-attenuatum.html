Hymenophyllum magellanicum var. attenuatum

Hymenophyllum magellanicum var. attenuatum, commonly known as the Magellanic filmy fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to South America.

It belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae and is characterized by its thin, translucent fronds that are often covered in a fine layer of hair. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in cloud forests or on rocky outcrops. Its unique morphology includes small, spore-producing structures called soruses, which are clustered on the underside of its fronds.

Due to its restricted habitat and sensitivity to environmental changes, it is considered a vulnerable species.
