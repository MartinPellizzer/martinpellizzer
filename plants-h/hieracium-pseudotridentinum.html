Hieracium pseudotridentinum

Hieracium pseudotridentinum, commonly known as the false three-lobed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads made up of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, pastures, and roadsides. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with serrated edges, and the flowers are yellow with a central disc.

It is often found in moist or sandy soils and can form dense colonies in suitable environments.
