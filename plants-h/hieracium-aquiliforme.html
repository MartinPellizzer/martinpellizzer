Hieracium aquiliforme

Hieracium aquiliforme, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium aquiliforme is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in various habitats.
