Heliotropium villosum

Heliotropium villosum, commonly known as the hairy heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or disturbed habitats. Its leaves are usually ovate or lance-shaped and covered with fine, soft hairs.

Heliotropium villosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
