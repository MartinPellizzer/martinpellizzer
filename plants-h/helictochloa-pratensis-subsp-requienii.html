Helictochloa pratensis subsp. requienii

Helictochloa pratensis subsp.

requienii, commonly known as the southern grass or requien's grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid conditions, showing resilience to drought.

Its botanical structure reflects its role as a pioneer species in disturbed or degraded environments.
