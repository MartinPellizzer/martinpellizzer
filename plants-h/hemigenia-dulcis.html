Hemigenia dulcis

Hemigenia dulcis, commonly known as the sweet honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae and is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that are edible and have a sweet, honey-like flavor. Hemigenia dulcis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and swampy areas.

It is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
