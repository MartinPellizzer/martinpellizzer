Helianthus rigidus subsp. subrhomboideus

Helianthus rigidus subsp.

subrhomboideus, commonly known as the rigid sunflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its stiff, upright stems and large, yellow composite flowers that bloom in the summer.

The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers consist of a central disk surrounded by ray florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
