Hieracium legionense subsp. cantabrimontanum

Hieracium legionense subsp.

cantabrimontanum, commonly known as the Cantabrian mountain hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of northern Spain. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. It has finely divided, serrated leaves and produces numerous small, dandelion-like flowers that bloom in late summer.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soils in harsh mountain environments.
