Heliophila africana

Heliophila africana, commonly known as the African sunflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sunlight. It has slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

Heliophila africana is often used in ornamental gardening due to its vibrant appearance and ease of cultivation.
