Hieracium bifidum subsp. argutisquamum

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

argutisquamum, commonly known as the roughleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces numerous small florets arranged in a composite head.

It is often found in temperate climates and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
