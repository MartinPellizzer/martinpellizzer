Horsfieldia pachythyrsa

Horsfieldia pachythyrsa, commonly known as the thick-caleda horned orchid, is a species of orchid native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy leaves and striking, showy inflorescences that bear numerous small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, with prominent, wing-like sepals.

Horsfieldia pachythyrsa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical botanical gardens.
