Heracleum sphondylium

Heracleum sphondylium, commonly known as cow parsnip, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is characterized by its large, umbrella-shaped white flowers and hairy, fern-like leaves. The plant can grow up to 2 meters tall and is often found in moist meadows, stream banks, and disturbed soils. Its stems are hollow and have a distinct scent when crushed.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like carrots and parsley.
