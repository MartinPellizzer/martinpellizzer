Heterotheca zionensis

Heterotheca zionensis, commonly known as the Zion aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as rocky slopes and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Heterotheca zionensis is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators in its native habitat.
