Helenium puberulum

Helenium puberulum, commonly known as the rough helenium, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are hairy, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically orange to yellow in color, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets.

Helenium puberulum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
