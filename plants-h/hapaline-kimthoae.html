Hapaline kimthoae

Hapaline kimthoae, commonly known as the Korean glasswort, is a halophytic plant native to coastal regions of Korea.

It thrives in saline and brackish environments, showcasing its ability to tolerate high salt concentrations. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in arid coastal conditions. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in green or reddish hues.

Hapaline kimthoae plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
