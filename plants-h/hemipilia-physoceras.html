Hemipilia physoceras

Hemipilia physoceras, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 50 centimeters, with lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with a distinctive, elongated spur.

Hemipilia physoceras thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.
