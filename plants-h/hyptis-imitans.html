Hyptis imitans

Hyptis imitans, commonly known as false basil or Mexican hyptis, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and basil. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a strong, camphor-like scent. Hyptis imitans produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple, arranged in dense clusters.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and fragrance.
