Hesperocyparis lusitanica

Hesperocyparis lusitanica, commonly known as the Iberian redwood, is a coniferous tree native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, columnar growth habit and dark reddish-brown bark. The plant has needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the branches. It produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, typical of the Cupressaceae family.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
