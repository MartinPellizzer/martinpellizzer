Hieracium scabriusculum var. columbianum

Hieracium scabriusculum var. columbianum, commonly known as the Columbia rough hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed sites such as meadows and roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are rough and hairy, with a distinctive grayish-green color, and it produces small yellow flowers in the summer.

It is often found in the Pacific Northwest and is considered a regional wildflower with limited distribution.
