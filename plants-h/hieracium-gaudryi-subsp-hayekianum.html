Hieracium gaudryi subsp. hayekianum

Hieracium gaudryi subsp.

hayekianum, commonly known as the Hayek's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has small, hairy leaves and produces numerous seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some areas.
