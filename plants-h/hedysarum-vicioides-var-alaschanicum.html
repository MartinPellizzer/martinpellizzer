Hedysarum vicioides var. alaschanicum

Hedysarum vicioides var. alaschanicum, commonly known as the Alaska milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of North America.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and trifoliate leaves with small, rounded leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, followed by pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Hedysarum vicioides var. alaschanicum plays a role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments and is of interest for ecological restoration projects.
