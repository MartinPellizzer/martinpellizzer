Hedera colchica var. dentatovariegata

Hedera colchica var. dentatovariegata, commonly known as the Variegated Colchic Hydrangea, is a variant of the Colchic Hydrangea, native to the Caucasus region.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its large, showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and blue, depending on soil pH. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves with distinctive white and green variegation patterns. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long flowering period and attractive foliage throughout the growing season.
