Helichrysum rupestre subsp. rouyi

Helichrysum rupestre subsp.

rouyi, commonly known as the rock everlasting, is a perennial herb native to South Africa. It features wiry, branching stems covered in dense, silver-gray foliage.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are often mistaken for leaves, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is drought-tolerant.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
