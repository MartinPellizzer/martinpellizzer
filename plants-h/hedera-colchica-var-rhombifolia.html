Hedera colchica var. rhombifolia

Hedera colchica var. rhombifolia, commonly known as the Rhombifoliate Ivy, is a climbing plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features dark green, rhombic-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange-red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Its foliage is evergreen, providing year-round interest in gardens.
