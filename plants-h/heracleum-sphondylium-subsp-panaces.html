Heracleum sphondylium subsp. panaces

Heracleum sphondylium subsp.

panaces, commonly known as cow parsnip, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North America. It is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and large, umbrella-shaped compound leaves.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a complex inflorescence, which are wind-pollinated. Its roots are fibrous and can be harvested for use in traditional medicine.

The plant contains toxic compounds, particularly in its sap, which can cause skin irritation.
