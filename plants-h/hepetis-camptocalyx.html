Hepetis camptocalyx

Hepetis camptocalyx, commonly known as the narrow-leaf false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to lavender flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fibrous roots are adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and wetlands.

Hepetis camptocalyx is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
