Hieracium murorum var. rhomboides

Hieracium murorum var. rhomboides, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with hairy surfaces, and the stems are upright and often branched.

It is native to Europe and has been introduced to other regions, where it can sometimes become invasive.
