Hieracium glaucum subsp. amaurodes

Hieracium glaucum subsp.

amaurodes, commonly known as the pale hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and clusters of yellow flowers.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

The plant is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in some areas.
