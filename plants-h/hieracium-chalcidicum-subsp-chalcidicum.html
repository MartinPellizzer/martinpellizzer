Hieracium chalcidicum subsp. chalcidicum

Hieracium chalcidicum subsp.

chalcidicum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features basal and stem leaves that are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces clusters of small, yellowish to orange flowers from June to August. Its inflorescence consists of a single capitulum surrounded by phyllaries, and it is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves.

The plant is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and wind-pollinated flowers.
