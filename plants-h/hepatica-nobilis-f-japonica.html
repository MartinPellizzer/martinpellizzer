Hepatica nobilis f. japonica

Hepatica nobilis f. japonica, commonly known as the Japanese liverleaf, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves and showy, early spring blooms. The flowers are typically blue or purple and feature five to seven sepals that resemble petals. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
