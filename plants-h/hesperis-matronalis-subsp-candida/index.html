Hesperis Matronalis Subsp. Candida

Hesperis matronalis subsp. candida is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. Known for its striking white blooms and fragrant scent, it is often referred to as the "white sweet rocket." This subspecies is distinguished by its pale, almost translucent petals that contrast with its deep green foliage. Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it faces significant conservation challenges. Efforts are underway to protect and propagate this unique plant to ensure its survival for future generations.
