Homalomena crassa

Homalomena crassa, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves and distinctive inflorescence. The plant features large, broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense foliage in suitable conditions.
