Hybanthus floribundus subsp. chloroxanthus

Hybanthus floribundus subsp.

chloroxanthus, commonly known as the yellow hybanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its small, yellow-orange flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

The plant has hairy, ovate leaves that are typically arranged opposite along the stems. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its bright, showy blooms.
