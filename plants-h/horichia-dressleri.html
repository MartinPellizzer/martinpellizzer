Horichia dressleri

Horichia dressleri, commonly known as the Dressler's horsetail, is a species of horsetail that belongs to the family Equisetaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, jointed stems and whorled arrangement of leaves, typical of the Equisetum genus. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate regions with well-drained soil. Horichia dressleri is notable for its ancient lineage, dating back to the Carboniferous period, and its unique reproductive structures.

Its presence in ecological studies highlights its role in wetland ecosystems and its resilience to environmental changes.
