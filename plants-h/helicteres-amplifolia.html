Helicteres amplifolia

Helicteres amplifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive red calyx that resembles a crown. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Helicteres amplifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
