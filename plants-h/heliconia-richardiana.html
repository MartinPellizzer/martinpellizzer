Heliconia richardiana

Heliconia richardiana, commonly known as the Richard's heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its large, colorful bracts that resemble petals. The plant produces striking inflorescences with long, tubular flowers that are often orange or red in color. Its leaves are broad and sword-shaped, providing a lush, tropical appearance.

Heliconia richardiana is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and greenhouses due to its vibrant foliage and unique floral structure.
