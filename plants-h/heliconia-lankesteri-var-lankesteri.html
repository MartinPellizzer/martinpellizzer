Heliconia lankesteri var. lankesteri

Heliconia lankesteri var. lankesteri, commonly known as the red heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, colorful inflorescences composed of long, tubular red bracts that enclose small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant is a member of the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses. Its leaves are broad, elongated, and often have a waxy texture, while its stems are sturdy and can grow quite tall.

The red heliconia is also valued in traditional medicine and is sometimes used in floral arrangements due to its vibrant appearance.
