Hieracium vulgatum subsp. persbergense

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

persbergense, commonly known as the Persberg's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, while its flowering stems are usually unbranched.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different leaf morphology and flower structure compared to the typical form of H. vulgatum.
