Helichrysum apiculatum var. minor

Helichrysum apiculatum var. minor, commonly known as the golden marguerite or lesser helichrysum, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, silvery-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is often used in garden landscapes for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

The flowers are also known for their long-lasting appearance when dried, making them popular in floral arrangements.
