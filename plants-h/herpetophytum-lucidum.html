Herpetophytum lucidum

Herpetophytum lucidum, commonly known as the African violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features a short, thick rhizome with fleshy, tuberous roots that store water and nutrients. The plant produces upright, hairy stems that bear clustered, showy flowers in shades of purple, blue, and white. Its leaves are thick, velvety, and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

The botanical characteristics of Herpetophytum lucidum make it well adapted to its native environment and popular as a houseplant.
