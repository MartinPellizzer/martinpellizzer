Hibiscus furcatus var. microcarpus

Hibiscus furcatus var. microcarpus, commonly known as the small-fruited hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink to red. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
