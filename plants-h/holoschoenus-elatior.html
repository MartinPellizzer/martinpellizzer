Holoschoenus elatior

Holoschoenus elatior, commonly known as the tall swamp rush, is a herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually arranged in dense clusters at the base. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and stream banks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Holoschoenus elatior is often found in areas with standing water or saturated soil, making it an important component of New Zealand's wetland ecosystems.
