Hieracium erythrocarpum subsp. cryptonaevum

Hieracium erythrocarpum subsp.

cryptonaevum, commonly known as the red-fruited hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow to orange composite flowers and reddish, elongated fruits.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and it is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils.

This subspecies is distinguished by its smaller, more compact inflorescence compared to other members of the species.
