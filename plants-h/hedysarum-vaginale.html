Hedysarum vaginale

Hedysarum vaginale, commonly known as the velvet vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in early summer. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several elliptical seeds.

Hedysarum vaginale is often found in disturbed soils and is used in ecological restoration due to its nitrogen-fixing abilities.
