Hieracium murorum subsp. subcrispatum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

subcrispatum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lanceolate leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in a single head, which are surrounded by involucral bracts. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
