Heterotheca echioides var. bolanderi

Heterotheca echioides var. bolanderi, commonly known as Bolander's blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has a compact growth habit and thrives in well-drained soils, often found in dry, open habitats. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native pollinators.
