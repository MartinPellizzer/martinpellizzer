Hieracium × bifurcum subsp. langii

Hieracium × bifurcum subsp.

langii, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed sites, thriving in moist to moderately dry conditions. Its flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and the leaves are usually lanceolate with hairy surfaces.

It is often found in temperate regions and is considered a common weed in agricultural areas.
