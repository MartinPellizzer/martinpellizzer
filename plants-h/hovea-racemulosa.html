Hovea racemulosa

Hovea racemulosa, commonly known as the yellow hovea, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in raceme clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and birds. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant is often found in coastal and sandy soils.

Hovea racemulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
