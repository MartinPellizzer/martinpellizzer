Hakea capitellata

Hakea capitellata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hakea, is a shrub native to southwestern Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces dense, cylindrical flower spikes that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Hakea capitellata is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
