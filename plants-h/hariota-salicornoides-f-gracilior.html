Hariota salicornoides f. gracilior

Hariota salicornoides f. gracilior, commonly known as the slender glassworts, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant exhibits a prostrate growth habit, often forming dense mats in coastal or marshy areas. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically arranged in clusters, and it produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This variety is notable for its ability to thrive in high-salinity soils, making it an important component of coastal ecosystems.
