Hamiltonia dulina

Hamiltonia dulina, commonly known as the dwarf orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features a compact growth habit with a single, slender stem that produces delicate, fragrant flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its unique beauty and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in greenhouses or conservatories.
