Hieracium sagittatum subsp. oxygonium

Hieracium sagittatum subsp.

oxygonium, commonly known as the arrowhead hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves and yellowish-orange composite flowers.

Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this subspecies thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into achene fruits.

It is often found in open habitats and is considered a common weed in agricultural areas.
