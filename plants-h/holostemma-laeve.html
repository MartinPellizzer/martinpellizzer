Holostemma laeve

Holostemma laeve, commonly known as the smooth holostemma, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its hairy, heart-shaped leaves and showy, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Holostemma laeve is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
