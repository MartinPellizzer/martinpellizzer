Homalomena subcordata

Homalomena subcordata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf caladium, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and showy, spadix-like inflorescences. The plant features vibrant, often variegated foliage that comes in shades of green, white, and red. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical landscapes.
