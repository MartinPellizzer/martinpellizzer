Hippocratea trichopetala

Hippocratea trichopetala, commonly known as the hairy-leaf hippocratea, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and often nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces, giving it its common name. It produces small, clustered white or yellowish flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
