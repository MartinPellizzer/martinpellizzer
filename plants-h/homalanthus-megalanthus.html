Homalanthus megalanthus

Homalanthus megalanthus, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, featuring a broad, umbrella-like canopy. The plant is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting birds and insects. Its leaves are compound, with long, slender leaflets, and it produces woody pods containing seeds.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well adapted to warm, humid climates.
