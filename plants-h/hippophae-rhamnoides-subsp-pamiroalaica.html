Hippophae rhamnoides subsp. pamiroalaica

Hippophae rhamnoides subsp.

pamiroalaica, commonly known as the Pamir sandthorn, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its yellow-orange berries, which are rich in vitamin C and other nutrients.

The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky slopes and in sandy soils. Its leaves are small and ovate, with a slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, yellow flowers in the spring.

This subspecies is valued for its hardiness and ecological importance in stabilizing soil in harsh climates.
