Hieracium subglaberrimum subsp. mediosilvanum

Hieracium subglaberrimum subsp.

mediosilvanum, commonly known as the woodland hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in woodlands and shaded areas, preferring moist, nutrient-rich soils.

The plant features bright yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and threatened species in many regions.
