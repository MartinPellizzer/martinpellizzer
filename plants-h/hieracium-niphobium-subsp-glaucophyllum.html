Hieracium niphobium subsp. glaucophyllum

Hieracium niphobium subsp.

glaucophyllum, commonly known as the glaucous hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in the summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes. Its subspecies name, *glaucophyllum*, refers to the bluish-gray coloration of its foliage.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.
