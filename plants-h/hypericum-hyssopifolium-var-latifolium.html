Hypericum hyssopifolium var. latifolium

Hypericum hyssopifolium var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features upright, branched stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in late summer, which are followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental species within the Hypericaceae family.
