Hieracium nobile var. farinosum

Hieracium nobile var. farinosum, commonly known as the showy hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
