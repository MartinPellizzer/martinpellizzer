Hemizonia congesta subsp. vernalis

Hemizonia congesta subsp.

vernalis, commonly known as the spring hemizonia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the spring.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers that attract pollinators.

The species is known for its seasonal blooming pattern and is often found in regions with temperate climates.
