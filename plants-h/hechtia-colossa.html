Hechtia colossa

Hechtia colossa, commonly known as the giant hechtia, is a striking member of the Bromeliaceae family native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy, and often brightly colored leaves that form a rosette shape. The plant produces striking inflorescences with showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. Its adaptability to rocky and well-drained soils makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Hechtia colossa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in semi-shaded environments.
