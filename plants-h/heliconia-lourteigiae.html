Heliconia lourteigiae

Heliconia lourteigiae, commonly known as the Lourteig's heliconia, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescences that resemble a bird's beak. The plant features large, paddle-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, providing protection against herbivores and excessive moisture loss. Its flowers are primarily pollinated by hummingbirds and other nectar-feeding insects, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.

Heliconia lourteigiae is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
