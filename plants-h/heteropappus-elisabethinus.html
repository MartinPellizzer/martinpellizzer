Heteropappus elisabethinus

Heteropappus elisabethinus, commonly known as the Elizabeth's heteropappus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant produces small, fluffy seeds enclosed in a pappus, which aids in wind dispersal. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to drought conditions.

Its yellow flowers bloom in the spring and summer, adding a splash of color to its desert habitat.
