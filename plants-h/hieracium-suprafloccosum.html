Hieracium suprafloccosum

Hieracium suprafloccosum, commonly known as the suprafloccose hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are typically arranged in a single, terminal head. The plant has a basal rosette of leaves that are hairy and often lobed, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, where it thrives in meadows and grassy areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
