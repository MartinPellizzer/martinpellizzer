Hieracium boreanum

Hieracium boreanum, commonly known as the Arctic hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as tundra and rocky slopes. The plant has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it produces yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

Hieracium boreanum plays a role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments and provides nectar for pollinators.
