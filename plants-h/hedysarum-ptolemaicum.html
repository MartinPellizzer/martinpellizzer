Hedysarum ptolemaicum

Hedysarum ptolemaicum, commonly known as Ptolemaic pea, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by elongated, curved pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Hedysarum ptolemaicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
