Hieracium sclerochaetoides

Hieracium sclerochaetoides, commonly known as the hard-hair hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are covered with dense, stiff hairs, giving it a rough texture, and its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat, daisy-like head.

It is known for its ability to thrive in various environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
