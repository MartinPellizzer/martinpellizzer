Hemionitis patula

Hemionitis patula, commonly known as the water hibiscus, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features delicate, arrow-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating on the surface of shallow water. The plant produces showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Hemionitis patula is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in aquatic ecosystems.
