Helleborus vaginatus

Helleborus vaginatus, commonly known as the naked stem hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of pale green to white flowers with prominent, showy spathes. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Helleborus vaginatus is known for its slow growth and long lifespan, making it a valuable addition to shaded gardens.
