Hordeum patagonicum subsp. magellanicum

Hordeum patagonicum subsp.

magellanicum, commonly known as the Magellanic barley, is a perennial grass native to southern South America. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves.

The plant produces spikelets that are often clustered at the base of the stem, with awns present on the lemma. It thrives in temperate climates and is adapted to grow in sandy or rocky soils.

This subspecies is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
