Hypodiscus tristachyus

Hypodiscus tristachyus, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night, emitting a strong fragrance to attract nocturnal pollinators. The plant has a woody stem with compound leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its flowers are typically borne in clusters and have a unique, lantern-like appearance.

The botanical features of Hypodiscus tristachyus make it both ecologically significant and aesthetically striking in its native habitat.
