Harpullia arborea var. megalocarpa

Harpullia arborea var. megalocarpa, commonly known as the giant crabapple, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that appear in clusters, followed by distinctive red, fleshy fruits. The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces a heavy, woody fruit that splits open when mature. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been introduced to other tropical regions for horticultural purposes.
