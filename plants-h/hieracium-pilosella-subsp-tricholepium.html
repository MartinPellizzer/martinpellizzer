Hieracium pilosella subsp. tricholepium

Hieracium pilosella subsp.

tricholepium, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

The plant features upright stems covered in fine, silky hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough, hairy texture. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and are produced in late spring to early summer.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
