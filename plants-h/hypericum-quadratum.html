Hypericum quadratum

Hypericum quadratum, commonly known as the yellow-stemmed St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky habitats.

Hypericum quadratum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
