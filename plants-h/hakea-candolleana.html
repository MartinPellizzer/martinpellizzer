Hakea candolleana

Hakea candolleana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces distinctive cylindrical or spindly flower spikes, which are usually pink to red in hue. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Hakea candolleana is often found in coastal and heathland areas, showcasing its resilience to harsh climatic conditions.
