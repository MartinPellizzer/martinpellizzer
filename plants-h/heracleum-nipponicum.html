Heracleum nipponicum

Heracleum nipponicum, commonly known as Japanese cow parsnip, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbellate inflorescence and aromatic foliage.

It is native to East Asia, particularly Japan, and thrives in moist, temperate environments. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves with a hairy surface and can grow up to 2 meters in height. Its flowers are white to pale green, forming flat-topped clusters, and it produces small, dry fruits known as schizocarps.

The plant contains various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine and botanical research.
