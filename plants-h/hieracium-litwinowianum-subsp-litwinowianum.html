Hieracium litwinowianum subsp. litwinowianum

Hieracium litwinowianum subsp.

litwinowianum, commonly known as the Litwinowian hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to orange-yellow flowers and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring moist to moderately dry conditions. It has rough, hairy leaves and produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the hawkweed genus.
