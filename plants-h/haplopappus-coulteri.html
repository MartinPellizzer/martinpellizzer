Haplopappus coulteri

Haplopappus coulteri, commonly known as Coulter's pappus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments and is adapted to thrive in drought conditions. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellow.

The plant produces a unique pappus, a structure that aids in seed dispersal by wind.
