Hieracium nigrescens subsp. subcomulatum

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

subcomulatum, commonly known as the dark-hued hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, thriving in temperate climates.

The plant features basal leaves that are usually broad and lobed, with a rough texture, and produces yellow composite flowers in late spring to early summer. Its flowering heads are surrounded by a series of bracts, and the plant is characterized by its dark green foliage that often turns reddish or purplish at the edges.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, known for its showy inflorescences and medicinal properties in some traditional uses.
