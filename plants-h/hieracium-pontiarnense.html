Hieracium pontiarnense

Hieracium pontiarnense, commonly known as the Pontic hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
