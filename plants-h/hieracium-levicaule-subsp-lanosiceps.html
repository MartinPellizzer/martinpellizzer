Hieracium levicaule subsp. lanosiceps

Hieracium levicaule subsp.

lanosiceps, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are covered in dense, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence and bloom in the summer months.

The species is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its adaptability in various environmental conditions.
