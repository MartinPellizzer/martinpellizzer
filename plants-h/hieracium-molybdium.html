Hieracium molybdium

Hieracium molybdium, commonly known as leadweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. It has hairy stems and leaves with a rough texture, and its flowers are usually yellow or pale yellow in color.

Hieracium molybdium is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
