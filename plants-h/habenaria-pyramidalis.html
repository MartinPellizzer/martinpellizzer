Habenaria pyramidalis

Habenaria pyramidalis, commonly known as the Pyramidal Orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant features a single, narrow leaf and produces a spike of elongated, lance-shaped blooms. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The orchid's pollination is primarily achieved through specific insect species, contributing to its ecological significance.
