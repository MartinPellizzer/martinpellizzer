Hymenoxys richardsonii var. nelsonii

Hymenoxys richardsonii var. nelsonii, commonly known as the Nelson's hairyleaf sunflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southwestern United States.

It features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow, daisy-like flowers with a prominent central disk. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and heat. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
