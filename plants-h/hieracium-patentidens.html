Hieracium patentidens

Hieracium patentidens, commonly known as the broad-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while the flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Hieracium patentidens is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in many regions.
