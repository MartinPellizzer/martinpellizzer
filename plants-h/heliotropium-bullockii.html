Heliotropium bullockii

Heliotropium bullockii, commonly known as the sand heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple and white. The plant has hairy, branched stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in sandy or well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or disturbed habitats.

Heliotropium bullockii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture.
