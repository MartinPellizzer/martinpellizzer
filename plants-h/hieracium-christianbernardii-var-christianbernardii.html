Hieracium christianbernardii var. christianbernardii

Hieracium christianbernardii var. christianbernardii, commonly known as the Christian Bernard's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and a flowering stem that bears yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is native to regions of Europe. It has finely divided, grass-like leaves and produces small, disc-like flowers with ray florets.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and localized species.
