Hemsleya gigantha

Hemsleya gigantha, commonly known as the giant hemslaya, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, waxy leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming during the warmer months. Its robust stems and adaptability to humid environments make it a popular choice for indoor and greenhouse cultivation.

Botanically, it belongs to the Araceae family, which includes other ornamental plants like philodendrons and peace lilies.
