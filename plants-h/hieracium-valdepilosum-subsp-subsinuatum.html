Hieracium valdepilosum subsp. subsinuatum

Hieracium valdepilosum subsp.

subsinuatum, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and clustered, yellowish flower heads.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and often have a serrated edge, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
