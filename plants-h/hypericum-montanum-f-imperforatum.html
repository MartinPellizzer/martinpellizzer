Hypericum montanum f. imperforatum

Hypericum montanum f. imperforatum, commonly known as the mountain St. John's wort, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Hypericaceae.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly serrated, with a dense covering of fine hairs. This plant is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
