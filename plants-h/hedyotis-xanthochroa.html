Hedyotis xanthochroa

Hedyotis xanthochroa, commonly known as the yellow false pennywort, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Hedyotis xanthochroa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local insect populations.
