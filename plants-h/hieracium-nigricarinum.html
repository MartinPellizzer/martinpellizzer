Hieracium nigricarinum

Hieracium nigricarinum, commonly known as the black-stemmed hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium nigricarinum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it both a resilient and sometimes invasive species.
