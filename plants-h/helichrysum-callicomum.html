Helichrysum callicomum

Helichrysum callicomum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, golden-yellow flowers that resemble tiny suns. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping. Its foliage is often silvery-gray, adding visual interest to gardens.

Helichrysum callicomum is also valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
