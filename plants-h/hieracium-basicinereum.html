Hieracium basicinereum

Hieracium basicinereum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads made up of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

Hieracium basicinereum is known for its resilience to cold climates and is often found in open, sunny environments at high elevations.
