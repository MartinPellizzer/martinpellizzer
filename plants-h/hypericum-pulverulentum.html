Hypericum pulverulentum

Hypericum pulverulentum, commonly known as the dusty St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to central and eastern Europe.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it its common name. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or along roadsides.

Hypericum pulverulentum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in herbal medicine.
