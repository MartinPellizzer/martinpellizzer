Hedysarum multijugum

Hedysarum multijugum, commonly known as the many-juged vetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It features compound leaves with 4 to 6 pairs of oval leaflets and produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. It has a taproot system and is adapted to grow in dry, well-drained environments.

Hedysarum multijugum is valued for its ornamental appeal and nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
