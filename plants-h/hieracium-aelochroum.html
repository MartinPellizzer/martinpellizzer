Hieracium aelochroum

Hieracium aelochroum, commonly known as the red-veined hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features bright yellow flowers with distinctive red veins, and its leaves are typically lance-shaped with rough textures. It thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies.

Due to its aggressive growth, it is considered a potential weed in some regions.
