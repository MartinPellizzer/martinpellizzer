Hemerocallis disticha var. kwanso

Hemerocallis disticha var. kwanso, commonly known as the Kwanso daylily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer and last only one day, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and hardiness in temperate climates.
