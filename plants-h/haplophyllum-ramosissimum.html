Haplophyllum ramosissimum

Haplophyllum ramosissimum, commonly known as the desert daphne, is a shrub native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, branched growth habit and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are adapted to conserve water in dry environments. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink and are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Haplophyllum ramosissimum is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes other shrubs with similar ecological adaptations.
