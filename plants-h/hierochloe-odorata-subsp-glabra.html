Hierochloe odorata subsp. glabra

Hierochloe odorata subsp.

glabra, commonly known as sweet grass, is a perennial grass native to North America. It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and a distinctive, sweet, honey-like fragrance when crushed.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open meadows and along stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and cultural significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in traditional crafts and ceremonies.
