Hymenophyllum minutidenticulatum

Hymenophyllum minutidenticulatum, commonly known as the minute-toothed hymenophyll, is a small, epiphytic fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically less than 5 cm in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on tree trunks or rocks in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spores are produced in small, clustered structures called sori, which are located on the underside of the fronds.

The species is noted for its intricate leaf structure and is often used in horticultural displays due to its ornamental appearance.
