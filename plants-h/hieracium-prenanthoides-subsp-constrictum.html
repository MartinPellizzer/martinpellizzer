Hieracium prenanthoides subsp. constrictum

Hieracium prenanthoides subsp.

constrictum, commonly known as the narrowleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in a single head, which are surrounded by phyllaries. It thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats, often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
