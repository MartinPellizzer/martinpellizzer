Habenaria insignis

Habenaria insignis, commonly known as the showy orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are strap-like and arranged in a rosette, while its flowers are borne on terminal inflorescences.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
