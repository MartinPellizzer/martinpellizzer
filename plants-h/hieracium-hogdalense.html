Hieracium hogdalense

Hieracium hogdalense, commonly known as Hogdal's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist grasslands, meadows, and along riverbanks, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers from late spring to early autumn.

The leaves are usually lance-shaped, with a hairy texture, and the plant is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and seed dispersal.
