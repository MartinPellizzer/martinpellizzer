Hakea mucronulata

Hakea mucronulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hakea, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are arranged in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. Hakea mucronulata is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
