Hieracium longidens

Hieracium longidens, commonly known as the long-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow daisy-like flowers, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in meadows and grasslands.

Hieracium longidens is a perennial herb that spreads through rhizomes and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
