Hackelia leptophylla

Hackelia leptophylla, commonly known as the slender-leaved fairybells, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open woodlands or grasslands. It has slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.
