Hieracium pseudohalleri

Hieracium pseudohalleri, commonly known as the false hawksbeard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a cluster of small flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom from June to September.

Hieracium pseudohalleri is often found in temperate regions and is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens with well-drained soil.
