Hieracium laevigatum subsp. laevigatum

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

laevigatum, commonly known as the smooth hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.

The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy, with a stem that is often reddish and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and are borne on slender pedicels.

The species is characterized by its smooth, unbranched stems and is part of the Asteraceae family.
