Haplopappus hirtus subsp. lanulosus

Haplopappus hirtus subsp.

lanulosus, commonly known as the woolly goldenweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow flowers in clusters.

The plant has a compact growth habit and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its inflorescence consists of a single, elongated head of florets, with bracts that are covered in fine, woolly hairs.

The species is notable for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in nutrient-poor environments.
