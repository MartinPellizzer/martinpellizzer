Hippeastrum starkiorum

Hippeastrum starkiorum, commonly known as the Brazilian red or red star lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant produces strap-shaped, evergreen leaves that emerge from a bulbous stem, which stores nutrients for flowering. Its flowering period typically occurs in the spring or early summer, depending on the climate.

Hippeastrum starkiorum is popular as a decorative plant due to its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
