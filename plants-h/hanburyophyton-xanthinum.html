Hanburyophyton xanthinum

Hanburyophyton xanthinum, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow inflorescences that resemble cascading raindrops, hence its common name. The plant has compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces small, winged seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Hanburyophyton xanthinum is also valued for its ability to tolerate urban environments and air pollution.
