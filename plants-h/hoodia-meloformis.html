Hoodia meloformis

Hoodia meloformis, commonly known as the desert honeysuckle, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom during the rainy season, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are often reduced to spines, minimizing water loss through transpiration.

Hoodia meloformis is also valued for its potential medicinal properties, though further research is needed to confirm its efficacy.
