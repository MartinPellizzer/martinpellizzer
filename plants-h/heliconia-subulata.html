Heliconia subulata

Heliconia subulata, commonly known as the needle heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its long, narrow, spiky bracts that form a dense, colorful inflorescence. The plant's leaves are elongated and often have a waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Heliconia subulata is valued not only for its aesthetic beauty but also for its role in supporting local biodiversity.
