Hieracium rupigenum

Hieracium rupigenum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and basal rosettes of lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow or orange composite flowers, which are arranged in a single, showy head. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and scree slopes.

Hieracium rupigenum is known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
