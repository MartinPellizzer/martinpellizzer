Hieracium murorum subsp. adenoactis

Hieracium murorum subsp.

adenoactis, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow, often with a central dark spot.

This subspecies is notable for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
