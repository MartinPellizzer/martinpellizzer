Habenaria humblotii

Habenaria humblotii, commonly known as the Humboldt's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often on the forest floor or in rocky crevices. Its flowers are usually pale green or white with a subtle fragrance, and it produces small, elliptical pseudobulbs.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
