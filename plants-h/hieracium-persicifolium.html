Hieracium persicifolium

Hieracium persicifolium, commonly known as the peach-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Compositae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves that are broad and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flower heads composed of many small florets, which bloom in summer. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
