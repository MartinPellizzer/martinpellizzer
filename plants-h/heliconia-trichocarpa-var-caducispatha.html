Heliconia trichocarpa var. caducispatha

Heliconia trichocarpa var. caducispatha, commonly known as the Caducispatha Heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, brightly colored bracts that resemble flowers. The plant produces inflorescences with elongated, spirally arranged leaves that enclose the true flowers. Its foliage is often broad and waxy, with a distinctive sheen, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and as a houseplant.
