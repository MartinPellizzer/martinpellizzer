Hydropeltis pulla

Hydropeltis pulla, commonly known as the small water lily, is a species of aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It typically grows in shallow, still waters such as ponds and slow-moving streams. The plant has broad, floating leaves that are usually green with a waxy surface to repel water. Its flowers are small and white, often appearing in clusters, and bloom during the summer months.

Hydropeltis pulla plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various water organisms.
