Harpullia imbricata

Harpullia imbricata, commonly known as the broad-leafed hibiscus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces woody capsules that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Harpullia imbricata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
