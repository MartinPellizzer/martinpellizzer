Hieracium bicolor subsp. pogonatoides

Hieracium bicolor subsp.

pogonatoides, commonly known as the purple hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers with a purple central disk.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are usually basal, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

It is an annual or biennial herb that produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.
