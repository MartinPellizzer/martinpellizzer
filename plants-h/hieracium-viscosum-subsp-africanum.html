Hieracium viscosum subsp. africanum

Hieracium viscosum subsp.

africanum, commonly known as the sticky hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Africa. It is characterized by its hairy, often sticky leaves and stems, which give it its common name.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it identifiable within the genus Hieracium, which includes several species with similar morphological traits.
