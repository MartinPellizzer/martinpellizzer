Hieracium acrolepis

Hieracium acrolepis, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant features a basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters. Its flowering period typically occurs from late spring to early autumn.

Hieracium acrolepis is characterized by its rough, hairy texture and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
