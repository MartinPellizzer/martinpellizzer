Hieracium boreale subsp. quercetorum

Hieracium boreale subsp.

quercetorum, commonly known as the oakleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and serrated, oak-like leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species in its native habitats.
