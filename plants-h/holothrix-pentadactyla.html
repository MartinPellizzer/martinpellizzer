Holothrix pentadactyla

Holothrix pentadactyla, commonly known as the five-finger sea hare, is a species of sea hare found in the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its soft, gelatinous body and distinctive five pairs of tentacles that resemble fingers. The plant-like appearance of this organism is due to its ability to photosynthesize using symbiotic algae, which it hosts within its body. It thrives in shallow, tropical waters and is often found on rocky substrates.

Holothrix pentadactyla plays a role in marine ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and providing habitat for smaller organisms.
