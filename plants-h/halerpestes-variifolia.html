Halerpestes variifolia

Halerpestes variifolia, commonly known as the variable-leaf halerpestes, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features elliptical to ovate leaves that vary in shape and size, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small and clustered, often in panicles, and are typically greenish-yellow in color.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and seasonal blooms.
