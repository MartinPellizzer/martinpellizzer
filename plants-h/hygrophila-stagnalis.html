Hygrophila stagnalis

Hygrophila stagnalis, commonly known as water hyssop, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in shallow waters, wetlands, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has opposite, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. Hygrophila stagnalis is known for its ability to thrive in moist or submerged conditions, making it a common sight in freshwater habitats.

It plays a role in water filtration and provides habitat for various aquatic organisms.
