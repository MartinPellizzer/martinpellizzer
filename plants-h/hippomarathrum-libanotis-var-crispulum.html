Hippomarathrum libanotis var. crispulum

Hippomarathrum libanotis var. crispulum, commonly known as the Lebanon thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, glandular leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are often curled or crinkled, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
