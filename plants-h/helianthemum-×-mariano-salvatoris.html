Helianthemum × mariano-salvatoris

Helianthemum × mariano-salvatoris, commonly known as the hybrid rock rose, is a species resulting from the cross between Helianthemum nummularium and Helianthemum hirtum.

It is a low-growing, evergreen shrub characterized by its fleshy, rounded leaves and showy yellow flowers. Native to southern Europe, it thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions further enhances its adaptability in various garden settings.
