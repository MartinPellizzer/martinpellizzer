Hieracium clovense var. griffithii

Hieracium clovense var. griffithii, commonly known as Griffith's devil's bit scabious, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are typically blue to violet in color, with a central yellow disk, and bloom from June to August.

It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine meadows.
