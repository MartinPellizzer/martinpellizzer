Hieracium nigrescens subsp. nutatulum

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

nutatulum, commonly known as the drooping hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its yellowish to orange-yellow ray florets and a dense, woolly covering on its stems and leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. It has a prostrate or sprawling growth habit, with leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

The subspecies nutatulum is distinguished by its drooping inflorescence and smaller flower heads compared to other varieties of Hieracium.
