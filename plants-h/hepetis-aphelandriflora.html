Hepetis aphelandriflora

Hepetis aphelandriflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early fall, and it is known for its hardy nature.

Hepetis aphelandriflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
