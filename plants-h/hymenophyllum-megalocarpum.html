Hymenophyllum megalocarpum

Hymenophyllum megalocarpum, commonly known as the giant spore moss, is a fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on tree trunks and branches in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its delicate, overlapping leaves that form dense mats, and it produces spores in specialized structures called sporangia. Hymenophyllum megalocarpum is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in areas with high humidity.

Its botanical features make it an important component of forest ecosystems, contributing to biodiversity and soil stability.
