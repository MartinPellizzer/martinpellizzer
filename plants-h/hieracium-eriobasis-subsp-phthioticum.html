Hieracium eriobasis subsp. phthioticum

Hieracium eriobasis subsp.

phthioticum, commonly known as the Phthiotic Hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellowish to reddish flower heads.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or gravelly soils, often in open habitats such as hillsides and pastures. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family.

This subspecies is noted for its limited distribution and is considered a rare plant in certain regions.
