Hesperis berteroana

Hesperis berteroana, commonly known as the Bertero's candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant features showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

Hesperis berteroana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its delicate appearance and fragrance.
