Hammada thomsonii

Hammada thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's aloe, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of North Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically bluish-green with a waxy coating, reducing water loss through transpiration.

It is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its resilience in harsh climates.
