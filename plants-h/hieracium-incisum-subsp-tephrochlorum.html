Hieracium incisum subsp. tephrochlorum

Hieracium incisum subsp.

tephrochlorum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, meadows, and grasslands.

The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, and it has leaves that are deeply lobed and often hairy. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The subspecies tephrochlorum is distinguished by its lighter yellow flowers compared to other subspecies of Hieracium incisum.
