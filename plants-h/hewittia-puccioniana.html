Hewittia puccioniana

Hewittia puccioniana, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a species of orchid native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a fan-like pattern.

Hewittia puccioniana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid environments.
