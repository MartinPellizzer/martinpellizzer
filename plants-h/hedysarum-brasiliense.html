Hedysarum brasiliense

Hedysarum brasiliense, commonly known as Brazilian sweet clover, is a perennial legume native to South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a robust root system that helps in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Hedysarum brasiliense is often used in forage and erosion control due to its hardy nature and adaptability.
