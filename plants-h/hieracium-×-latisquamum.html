Hieracium × latisquamum

Hieracium × latisquamum, commonly known as the broad-scale hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. It produces numerous seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.

The species is also noted for its aggressive growth habit, which can make it a potential weed in certain environments.
