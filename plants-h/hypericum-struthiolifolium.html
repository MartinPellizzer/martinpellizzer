Hypericum struthiolifolium

Hypericum struthiolifolium, commonly known as the golden st. john's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features showy, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its medicinal properties have been studied for their potential anti-inflammatory and antidepressant effects.
