Hieracium nigrescens var. valpiorae

Hieracium nigrescens var. valpiorae, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it produces yellow flowers in early summer. Its flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences and are pollinated by insects.

The species is characterized by its ability to survive in harsh environments, making it a notable component of alpine flora.
