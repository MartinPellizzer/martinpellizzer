Habenaria macroceras

Habenaria macroceras, commonly known as the broad-fruited orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The orchid is known for its ability to form mycorrhizal associations with fungi, which aid in nutrient uptake.
