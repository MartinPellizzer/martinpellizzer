Hemilophia

Hemilophia, commonly known as the silverleaf daisy, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its distinctive, silver-gray foliage and daisy-like flowers. The plants typically grow as shrubs or subshrubs, with lance-shaped leaves and showy yellow flower heads. Hemilophia species are often cultivated for their ornamental value in gardens due to their hardy nature and attractive appearance.

They prefer well-drained soil and full sun, making them suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.
