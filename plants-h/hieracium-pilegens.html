Hieracium pilegens

Hieracium pilegens, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. It produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
