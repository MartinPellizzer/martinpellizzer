Hydrophyllum magellanicum var. pinnatum

Hydrophyllum magellanicum var. pinnatum, commonly known as the Magellanic waterleaf, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves, which are deeply lobed and often resemble the shape of a water lily. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in umbels, often appearing in late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate moisture conditions.
