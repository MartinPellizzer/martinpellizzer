Hibiscus rectiflorus

Hibiscus rectiflorus, commonly known as the Florida hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in wetland areas and has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Hibiscus rectiflorus produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

It is an important species in wetland ecosystems and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
