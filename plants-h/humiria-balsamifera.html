Humiria balsamifera

Humiria balsamifera, commonly known as the balsam tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, aromatic bark that exudes a balsamic scent when cut. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 15 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy texture.

The flowers are small and clustered, producing a fragrant, yellowish-green bloom.
