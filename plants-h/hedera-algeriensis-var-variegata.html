Hedera algeriensis var. variegata

Hedera algeriensis var. variegata, commonly known as Algerian ivy, is a variant of the ivy family native to North Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive variegated leaves, which feature a combination of green, white, and sometimes yellow patterns. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, with tendrils that allow it to attach to surfaces. Its foliage is often used in horticulture for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance.

It prefers cool, shaded environments and well-drained soil.
