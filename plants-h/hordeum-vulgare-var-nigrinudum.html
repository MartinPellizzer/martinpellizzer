Hordeum vulgare var. nigrinudum

Hordeum vulgare var. nigrinudum, commonly known as black naked barley, is a variant of barley characterized by its lack of hull and dark-colored grains.

It is a member of the Poaceae family and is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with slender stems and leaf blades that are usually narrow and grass-like. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, and the flowers are small and wind-pollinated.

This variety is often studied for its potential in agriculture and as a source of nutrients.
