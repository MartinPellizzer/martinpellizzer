Heterotheca subaxillaris var. psammophila

Heterotheca subaxillaris var. psammophila, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish-white flowers in clustered inflorescences. It is adapted to drought conditions and has a deep root system to access water.
