Hussonia pinnata

Hussonia pinnata, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as deserts and rocky soils, and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, reducing water loss through transpiration.

Hussonia pinnata plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for certain wildlife.
