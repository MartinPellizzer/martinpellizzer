Hamatocactus setispinus

Hamatocactus setispinus, commonly known as the Mexican hat cactus, is a species native to the Sierra Madre Occidental in Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its smooth, green stems and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a dense covering of spines, which provide protection from herbivores and help reduce water loss. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in arid gardens and conservation programs.
