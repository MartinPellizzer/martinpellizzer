Hieracium ataliceps

Hieracium ataliceps, commonly known as the Italian hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central dark disc.

Hieracium ataliceps is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
