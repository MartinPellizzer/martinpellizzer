Hieracium sparsum subsp. amblylepis

Hieracium sparsum subsp.

amblylepis, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a perennial herb with a low-growing habit, characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically thrives in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are linear to lanceolate, with a hairy surface, and the flowers are arranged in a single head.

The species is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through its rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
