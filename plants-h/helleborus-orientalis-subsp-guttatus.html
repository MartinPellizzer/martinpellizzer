Helleborus orientalis subsp. guttatus

Helleborus orientalis subsp.

guttatus, commonly known as the spotted hellebore, is a perennial herb native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves with distinctive white or yellow spots.

The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care.
