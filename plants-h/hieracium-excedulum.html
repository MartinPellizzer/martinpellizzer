Hieracium excedulum

Hieracium excedulum, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf morphology. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, along roadsides, and in disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a cluster.

Hieracium excedulum is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in various habitats.
