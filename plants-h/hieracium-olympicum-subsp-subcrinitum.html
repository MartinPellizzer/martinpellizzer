Hieracium olympicum subsp. subcrinitum

Hieracium olympicum subsp.

subcrinitum, commonly known as the Olympic hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Olympic Peninsula in Washington State. It is a perennial herb characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It has small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Due to its restricted range and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
