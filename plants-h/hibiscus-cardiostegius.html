Hibiscus cardiostegius

Hibiscus cardiostegius, commonly known as the heartleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers bloom throughout the wet season and are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Hibiscus cardiostegius is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid, sunny environments.
