Homalium spathulatum

Homalium spathulatum, commonly known as the broad-leafed holly, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers followed by orange-red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
