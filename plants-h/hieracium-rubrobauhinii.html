Hieracium rubrobauhinii

Hieracium rubrobauhinii, commonly known as the red-brown hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, rough, and hairy, with a reddish-brown coloration on the underside.

The flowers are yellow with a dark central disk, and the plant produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.
