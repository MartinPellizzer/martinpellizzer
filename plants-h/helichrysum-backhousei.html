Helichrysum backhousei

Helichrysum backhousei, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of yellow, daisy-like flowers. The plant is known for its aromatic foliage and is often used in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its resilience and ornamental value have made it a valued plant in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
