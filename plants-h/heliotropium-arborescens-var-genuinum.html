Heliotropium arborescens var. genuinum

Heliotropium arborescens var. genuinum, commonly known as the tropical heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree with opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy and have a distinct scent. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in the afternoon, hence its common name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas or disturbed soils.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
