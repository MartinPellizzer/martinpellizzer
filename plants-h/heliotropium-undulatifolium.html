Heliotropium undulatifolium

Heliotropium undulatifolium, commonly known as the wavy-leaved heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and leaves that have a wavy, undulating edge. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from purple to pink, often resembling those of the true heliotropes. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Heliotropium undulatifolium is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
