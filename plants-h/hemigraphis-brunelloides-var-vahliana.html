Hemigraphis brunelloides var. vahliana

Hemigraphis brunelloides var. vahliana, commonly known as the velvetleaf sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features striking, velvety foliage with a deep green to reddish-purple hue, which varies with light exposure and seasonal changes. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for shade gardens and as a ground cover in humid climates.
