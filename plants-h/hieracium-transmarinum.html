Hieracium transmarinum

Hieracium transmarinum, commonly known as the sea hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy, lobed leaves that help it withstand salty and windy conditions. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils along sea shores and estuaries. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often considered a pioneer species in coastal ecosystems.
