Harlandia bryonioides

Harlandia bryonioides, commonly known as the silver leaf vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy, silver sheen. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically borne in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage.

Harlandia bryonioides is also known for its ability to climb and can be found growing along fences or trees in its natural habitat.
