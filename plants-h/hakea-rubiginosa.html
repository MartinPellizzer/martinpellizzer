Hakea rubiginosa

Hakea rubiginosa, commonly known as the red hakea, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, needle-like leaves that are typically reddish in color. The plant produces bright red or orange flowers in late summer, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. It is adapted to arid and coastal environments, showing resistance to salt spray and drought.

Hakea rubiginosa is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
