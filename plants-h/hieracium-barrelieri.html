Hieracium barrelieri

Hieracium barrelieri, commonly known as the Barrelier's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer.

Hieracium barrelieri is often found in regions with temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
