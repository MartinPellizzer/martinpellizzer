Huperzia catacachiensis

Huperzia catacachiensis, commonly known as the Catacachi clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is native to the Andean region of South America, specifically Ecuador, where it grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant features elongated, spirally arranged leaves that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. Its sporophytes are characterized by a short, stout stem with whorls of leaves and sporangia that produce spores for reproduction.

Huperzia catacachiensis plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its native habitat, contributing to soil stability and providing microhabitats for various organisms.
