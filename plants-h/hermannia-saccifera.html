Hermannia saccifera

Hermannia saccifera, commonly known as the bladderleaf or bladderleaf geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive swollen, bladder-like leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Hermannia saccifera thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a notable species in the Geraniaceae family.
