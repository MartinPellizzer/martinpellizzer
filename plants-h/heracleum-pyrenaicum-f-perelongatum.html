Heracleum pyrenaicum f. perelongatum

Heracleum pyrenaicum f. perelongatum, commonly known as the long-stemmed cow parsnip, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbelliferous inflorescence and aromatic foliage. The plant features tall, hollow stems that are often tinged with purple, and its leaves are large, pinnately lobed, and covered in fine hairs. It produces small white to greenish flowers in umbrella-shaped clusters, which are followed by oval, dry fruits.

This variant is distinguished by its elongated stems and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
