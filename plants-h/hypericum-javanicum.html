Hypericum javanicum

Hypericum javanicum, commonly known as the Indian St. John's Wort, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that are often hairy, and it produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with five petals. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for gardens.

Hypericum javanicum is also known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.
