Hieracium grisescens

Hieracium grisescens, commonly known as the gray hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers consist of yellow disk flowers surrounded by pale green to grayish ray florets.

Hieracium grisescens is often found in disturbed soils and can spread through rhizomes, making it a common weed in some regions.
