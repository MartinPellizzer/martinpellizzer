Hieracium sublacteum

Hieracium sublacteum, commonly known as the milky hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features upright, hairy stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in summer, producing a milky sap that is characteristic of the genus Hieracium.

It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
