Helxine convolvulus

Helxine convolvulus, commonly known as the common morning glory, is a climbing vine belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant is native to various regions in the Americas and is known for its rapid growth habit. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and prefers well-drained soil.

Helxine convolvulus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be found in gardens and as a houseplant.
