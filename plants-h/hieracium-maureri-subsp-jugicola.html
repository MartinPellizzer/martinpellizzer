Hieracium maureri subsp. jugicola

Hieracium maureri subsp.

jugicola, commonly known as the southern sandwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in dry, sandy or gravelly soils, often in open habitats such as coastal dunes or rocky slopes.

The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and threatened species in many parts of its range.
