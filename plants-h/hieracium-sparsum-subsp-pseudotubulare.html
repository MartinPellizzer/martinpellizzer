Hieracium sparsum subsp. pseudotubulare

Hieracium sparsum subsp.

pseudotubulare, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in agricultural areas.
