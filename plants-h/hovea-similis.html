Hovea similis

Hovea similis, commonly known as the silky hovea, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by elongated, woody pods. Hovea similis is typically found in coastal heathlands and sandy soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
