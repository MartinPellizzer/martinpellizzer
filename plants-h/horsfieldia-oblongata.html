Horsfieldia oblongata

Horsfieldia oblongata, commonly known as the long-leaf orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically borne on the upper portion of the stem. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Horsfieldia oblongata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
