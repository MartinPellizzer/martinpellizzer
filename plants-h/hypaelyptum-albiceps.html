Hypaelyptum albiceps

Hypaelyptum albiceps, commonly known as the white-headed daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers with white ray florets and yellow disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Hypaelyptum albiceps is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
