Hydrochloa caerulea

Hydrochloa caerulea, commonly known as the blue waterplantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in the northern hemisphere.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in ponds, marshes, and along the edges of streams. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually submerged or floating, with a waxy surface to reduce water loss. Its small, blue to violet flowers bloom in late summer, adding a striking color to its aquatic habitat.

Hydrochloa caerulea plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and amphibians.
