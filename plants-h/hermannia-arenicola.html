Hermannia arenicola

Hermannia arenicola, commonly known as the sand hermannia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink or white. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It has small, narrow leaves that help reduce water loss, and it blooms during the warmer months.

Hermannia arenicola is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in rock gardens due to its drought tolerance and attractive floral display.
