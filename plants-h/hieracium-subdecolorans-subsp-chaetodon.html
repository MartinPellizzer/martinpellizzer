Hieracium subdecolorans subsp. chaetodon

Hieracium subdecolorans subsp.

chaetodon, commonly known as the hairy yellow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are hairy and often have a bluish-green hue, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

This subspecies is noted for its distinctive leaf morphology and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
