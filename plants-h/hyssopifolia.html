Hyssopifolia

Hyssopifolia, commonly known as the blue hyssop, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to regions of Europe and parts of Asia, often found in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant features square stems and opposite leaves that are typically gray-green in color. Its flowers are clustered in whorls and range in color from blue to purple, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Hyssopifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
