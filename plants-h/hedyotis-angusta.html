Hedyotis angusta

Hedyotis angusta, commonly known as narrowleaf bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features opposite, narrow, and sometimes hairy leaves, and it produces small, yellowish-white flowers clustered in dense inflorescences. Its flowers are typically short-lived, opening in the morning and closing by midday.

Hedyotis angusta is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.
