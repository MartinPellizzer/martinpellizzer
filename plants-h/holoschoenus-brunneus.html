Holoschoenus brunneus

Holoschoenus brunneus, commonly known as the brown flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Australia.

It typically grows in wetland or swampy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, which help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Holoschoenus brunneus is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
