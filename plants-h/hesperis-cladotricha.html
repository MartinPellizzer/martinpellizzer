Hesperis cladotricha

Hesperis cladotricha, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with four-petaled flowers. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces tall, branched stems that bear clusters of showy, violet-blue flowers. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense, racemose inflorescences, and it blooms during the summer months.

Hesperis cladotricha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
