Homopappus squarrosus

Homopappus squarrosus, commonly known as the silky goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and feathery foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance.

The showy yellow flowers appear in clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer.
