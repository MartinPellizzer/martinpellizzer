Hormopetalum pullei

Hormopetalum pullei, commonly known as the coastal bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern California.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to thrive in sandy, well-drained soils.

Hormopetalum pullei is often found in maritime chaparral habitats, where it is exposed to salt spray and strong coastal winds.
