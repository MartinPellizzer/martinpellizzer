Hakea fuscolutea

Hakea fuscolutea, commonly known as the yellow hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance.

Hakea fuscolutea is often found in heathlands and open woodlands, contributing to the local biodiversity.
