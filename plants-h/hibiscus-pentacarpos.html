Hibiscus pentacarpos

Hibiscus pentacarpos, commonly known as the five-seeded hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink or red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces five-winged fruits, which are distinctive and contain seeds. Hibiscus pentacarpos is often found in wetland habitats and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various pollinators.
