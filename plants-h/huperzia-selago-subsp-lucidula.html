Huperzia selago subsp. lucidula

Huperzia selago subsp.

lucidula, commonly known as the shining huperzia, is a fern-like plant belonging to the family Huperziaceae. It is characterized by its elongated, strap-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

The plant is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, including parts of Europe, Asia, and North America. Its rhizome is often covered with a dense layer of root-like structures, which aid in nutrient absorption.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky outcrops.
