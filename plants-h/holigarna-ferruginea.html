Holigarna ferruginea

Holigarna ferruginea, commonly known as the rust-colored lichen, is a symbiotic organism composed of a fungus and a green alga.

It belongs to the family Verrucariaceae and is characterized by its distinctive reddish-brown coloration. This lichen typically grows on rocks, soil, and tree bark in temperate and arctic regions. It thrives in humid environments and is often found in shaded, moist habitats.

The structure of Holigarna ferruginea consists of a thallus that is crust-like, with the algal cells embedded in the fungal matrix.
