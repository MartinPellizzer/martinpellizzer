Hieracium racemosum subsp. vizzavonae

Hieracium racemosum subsp.

vizzavonae, commonly known as the Vizzavona hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellowish to orange flowers arranged in a raceme.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while the stems are hairy and often reddish in color. Native to the Mediterranean region, this subspecies thrives in rocky and dry habitats.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to soil stability.
