Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp. adesiforme

Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp.

adesiforme, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy, with flowering stems that are usually unbranched.

The subspecies adesiforme is distinguished by its smaller, more compact inflorescence compared to other members of the species.
