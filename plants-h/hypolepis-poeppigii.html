Hypolepis poeppigii

Hypolepis poeppigii, commonly known as the giant water fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, floating fronds that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in marshes and ponds. Its fronds are divided into numerous pinnate segments, which aid in photosynthesis while floating on the water's surface.

Hypolepis poeppigii plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
