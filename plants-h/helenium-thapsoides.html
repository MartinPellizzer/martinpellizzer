Helenium thapsoides

Helenium thapsoides, commonly known as the yellow garden hedgerow, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with slender, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, adding vibrant color to gardens and natural habitats.

Helenium thapsoides is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting pollinators.
