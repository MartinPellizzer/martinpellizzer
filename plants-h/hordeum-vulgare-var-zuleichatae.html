Hordeum vulgare var. zuleichatae

Hordeum vulgare var. zuleichatae, commonly known as the Zuleichatae barley, is a variant of barley characterized by its unique botanical features.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is typically found in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with two-rowed grains. Its seeds are often used in traditional agricultural practices and can be adapted to specific soil and climate conditions.

This variety is valued for its resilience and potential in sustainable farming systems.
