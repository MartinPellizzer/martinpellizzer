Hyptis elongata

Hyptis elongata, commonly known as Mexican mint marigold, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and fragrant properties.

Hyptis elongata is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
