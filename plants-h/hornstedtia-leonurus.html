Hornstedtia leonurus

Hornstedtia leonurus, commonly known as the golden bell orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are long and narrow, and it produces showy, fragrant blooms during the warmer months.

Hornstedtia leonurus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
