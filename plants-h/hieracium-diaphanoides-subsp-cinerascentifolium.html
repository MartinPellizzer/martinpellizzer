Hieracium diaphanoides subsp. cinerascentifolium

Hieracium diaphanoides subsp.

cinerascentifolium, commonly known as the ash-gray hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features basal leaves that are lanceolate and covered in a grayish-white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its yellow flowers are borne in loose, flat-topped clusters and bloom from June to August.

This subspecies is notable for its hirsute foliage and is often found in calcareous soils.
