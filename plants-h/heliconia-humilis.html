Heliconia humilis

Heliconia humilis, commonly known as the dwarf heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its large, colorful bracts that resemble petals. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or green in color. Its leaves are broad and waxy, providing a striking visual contrast to the vibrant bracts.

Heliconia humilis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in warm climates due to its aesthetic appeal.
