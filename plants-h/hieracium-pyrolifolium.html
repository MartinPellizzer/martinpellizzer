Hieracium pyrolifolium

Hieracium pyrolifolium, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the dandelion family (Compositae) and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its flowers bloom in early summer.

Hieracium pyrolifolium is often found in open habitats and can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
