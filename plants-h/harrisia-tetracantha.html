Harrisia tetracantha

Harrisia tetracantha, commonly known as the four-spined harrisia, is a species of cactus native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces edible fruits that are typically consumed fresh or used in local cuisines. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting well to poor soil conditions.

Its structure and growth habits make it a notable species within the Cactaceae family.
