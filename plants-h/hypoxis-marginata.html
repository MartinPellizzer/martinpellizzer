Hypoxis marginata

Hypoxis marginata, commonly known as the white star lily, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves with a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Hypoxis marginata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine.
