Hieracium subdurans

Hieracium subdurans, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are rough and hairy, with a serrated edge, and its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

Hieracium subdurans is often found in moist to dry soils and can form dense colonies in suitable environments.
