Hieracium mexicanum

Hieracium mexicanum, commonly known as Mexican hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is a perennial plant that can spread aggressively through its rhizomes, making it a common weed in disturbed habitats.
