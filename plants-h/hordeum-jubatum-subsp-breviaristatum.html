Hordeum jubatum subsp. breviaristatum

Hordeum jubatum subsp.

breviaristatum, commonly known as the short awn wild barley, is a species of grass native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers with short awns, which are bristle-like structures at the tips of the florets. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in disturbed soils.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists and agricultural researchers due to its potential for genetic diversity and adaptation to arid environments.
