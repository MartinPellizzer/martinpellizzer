Hieracium triviale subsp. rubroviolascens

Hieracium triviale subsp.

rubroviolascens, commonly known as the red-violet hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are yellow to reddish-violet, with a distinctive central disc surrounded by ray florets.

It is known for its hairy stems and leaves, which help in reducing water loss in drier conditions.
