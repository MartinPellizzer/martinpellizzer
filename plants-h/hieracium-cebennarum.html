Hieracium cebennarum

Hieracium cebennarum, commonly known as the Alpine Hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. It has basal leaves that are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

Hieracium cebennarum is known for its ability to thrive in harsh, cold environments, making it a resilient species in high-altitude ecosystems.
