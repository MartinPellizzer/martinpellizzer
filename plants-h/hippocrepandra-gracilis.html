Hippocrepandra gracilis

Hippocrepandra gracilis, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated flower spikes and small, pale yellow to greenish-white blossoms. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the rocky substrates it inhabits.

Hippocrepandra gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
