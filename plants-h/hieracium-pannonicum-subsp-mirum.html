Hieracium pannonicum subsp. mirum

Hieracium pannonicum subsp.

mirum, commonly known as the Pannonian hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It has finely divided, grass-like leaves and produces small, bristly seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
