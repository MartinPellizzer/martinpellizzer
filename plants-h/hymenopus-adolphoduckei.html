Hymenopus adolphoduckei

Hymenopus adolphoduckei, commonly known as the Adolpho Ducke's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically feature vibrant colors such as red, orange, and yellow. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the humid, nutrient-poor soil of its natural habitat.

The orchid blooms during the wet season, relying on specific pollinators for reproduction.
