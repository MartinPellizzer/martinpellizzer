Hyptis lobata

Hyptis lobata, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded areas in forests. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and basil.

Hyptis lobata is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
