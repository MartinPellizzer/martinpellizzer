Haemanthus cabrae

Haemanthus cabrae, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant red and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features strap-like, grassy leaves that are typically green with a slight blue or grey tinge. Haemanthus cabrae is often found in rocky outcrops and mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is a perennial herb that produces bulb-like structures for storage and propagation.
