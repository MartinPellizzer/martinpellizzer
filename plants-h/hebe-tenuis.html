Hebe tenuis

Hebe tenuis, commonly known as the slender hebe, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, or white, typically blooming in spring and summer. Hebe tenuis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a valuable addition to both cultivated and natural landscapes.
