Hieracium vulgatum subsp. chroopastum

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

chroopastum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm.

The plant features basal rosettes of lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences. Its flowers are composed of numerous small tubular florets, which are pollinated by insects.

The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, and it is often found in temperate climates.
