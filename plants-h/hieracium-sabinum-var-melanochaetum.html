Hieracium sabinum var. melanochaetum

Hieracium sabinum var. melanochaetum, commonly known as the black-hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the daisy family. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, stiff hairs.

It thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, often in temperate climates.
