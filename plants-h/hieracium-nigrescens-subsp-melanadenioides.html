Hieracium nigrescens subsp. melanadenioides

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

melanadenioides, commonly known as the dark-hued hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disc florets.

The plant typically grows in dry grasslands and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lanceolate, hairy, and often have a dark green coloration, while the flowers are yellow with a distinctive dark central disc.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
