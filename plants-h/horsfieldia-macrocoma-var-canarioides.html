Horsfieldia macrocoma var. canarioides

Horsfieldia macrocoma var. canarioides, commonly known as the Canary Island orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit with broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often with a yellow or orange lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil and consistent moisture.

This variant is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
