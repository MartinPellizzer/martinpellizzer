Hypericum monogynum var. salicifolium

Hypericum monogynum var. salicifolium, commonly known as the willow-leaf St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like leaves that are alternate and lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late summer. Its stems are often reddish-brown and covered in fine hairs.

This variant is often found in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and woodland edges.
