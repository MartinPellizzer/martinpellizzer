Hypericum walteri var. tubulosum

Hypericum walteri var. tubulosum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved St. John's wort, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Hypericaceae and is characterized by its slender, ascending stems and small, yellow flowers. The leaves are linear to lanceolate, often with a glandular surface, and the plant produces tiny, bead-like fruits. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes, and is adapted to full sun conditions.

This variant is distinguished by its tubular corolla and is often found in the Pacific Northwest.
