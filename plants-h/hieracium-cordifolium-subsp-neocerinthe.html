Hieracium cordifolium subsp. neocerinthe

Hieracium cordifolium subsp.

neocerinthe, commonly known as the heart-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky soils, preferring full sun to partial shade.

The plant features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, with flowering stems that bear yellow composite flowers. Its flowers are composed of many small florets arranged in a disc, typical of the Asteraceae family.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
