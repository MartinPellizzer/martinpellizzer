Hieracium jurassicum subsp. esserianum

Hieracium jurassicum subsp.

esserianum, commonly known as the Jurassic hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement.

The plant typically grows in dry meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

It is known for its historical significance in botanical studies due to its distinctive morphology and limited distribution.
