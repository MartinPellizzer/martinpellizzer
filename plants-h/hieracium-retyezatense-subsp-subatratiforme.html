Hieracium retyezatense subsp. subatratiforme

Hieracium retyezatense subsp.

subatratiforme, commonly known as the Balkan hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring temperate climates. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, while its inflorescence consists of both ray and disk flowers.

This subspecies is of particular interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique morphological traits.
