Hymenatherum polychaetum

Hymenatherum polychaetum, commonly known as the hairy tallowwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to northeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale grey bark and lanceolate, glossy dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its fruits are woody follicles that split open to release seeds.

Hymenatherum polychaetum is often found in rainforests and coastal areas, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
