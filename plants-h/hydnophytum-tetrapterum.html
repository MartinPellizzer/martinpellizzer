Hydnophytum tetrapterum

Hydnophytum tetrapterum, commonly known as the four-winged bladderfruit, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, inflated fruit that resembles a bladder, which aids in seed dispersal by water. The plant has compound leaves with four pairs of leaflets, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Hydnophytum tetrapterum is valued for its ornamental appearance and has some traditional medicinal uses.
