Hunga novoguineensis

Hunga novoguineensis, commonly known as the New Guinea hanging fern, is a unique tropical plant native to the humid rainforests of New Guinea.

It is characterized by its long, drooping fronds that cascade from its woody stem, creating an ornamental display. The plant thrives in high humidity and indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening. Its leaves are typically dark green with a leathery texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are rarely seen.

Due to its specific environmental needs, it requires careful attention to soil moisture and temperature to flourish.
