Hibiscus sanguineus

Hibiscus sanguineus, commonly known as the scarlet rosemallow, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It features large, showy flowers with five deeply notched petals that range in color from red to pink. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Hibiscus sanguineus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
