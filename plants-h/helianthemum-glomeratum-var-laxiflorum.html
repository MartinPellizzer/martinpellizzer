Helianthemum glomeratum var. laxiflorum

Helianthemum glomeratum var. laxiflorum, commonly known as the laxiflorous sandthrift, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Europe.

It features dense clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has gray-green, succulent leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in coastal or coastal-like conditions.
