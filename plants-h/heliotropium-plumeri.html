Heliotropium plumeri

Heliotropium plumeri, commonly known as the purple heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous annual with slender, branching stems and opposite leaves that are typically lance-shaped and hairy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, often with a reddish or violet hue. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open fields.

Heliotropium plumeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
