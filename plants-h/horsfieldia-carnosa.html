Horsfieldia carnosa

Horsfieldia carnosa, commonly known as the hairy orchid, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks in humid rainforest environments. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to store moisture in its natural habitat.

The flowers, which are usually pink or white, bloom in clusters and are known for their intricate, ornate structure.
