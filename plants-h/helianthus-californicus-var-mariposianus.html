Helianthus californicus var. mariposianus

Helianthus californicus var. mariposianus, commonly known as the Mariposa sunflower, is a native wildflower of California.

It is characterized by its bright yellow ray florets and a central disk of smaller, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
