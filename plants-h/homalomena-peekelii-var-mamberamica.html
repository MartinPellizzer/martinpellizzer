Homalomena peekelii var. mamberamica

Homalomena peekelii var. mamberamica, commonly known as the Mamberamica elephant's ear, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which is known for its unique floral structures. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
