Hieracium bifidum subsp. lonchopodum

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

lonchopodum, commonly known as the narrowleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

The plant features basal leaves that are narrow and linear, with a distinctive yellow-orange flower head composed of ray florets. It produces small, oval seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is characterized by its finely divided leaves and is often found in disturbed soils.
