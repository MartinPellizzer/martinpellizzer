Hibiscus fruticulosus

Hibiscus fruticulosus, commonly known as the prickly hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

Hibiscus fruticulosus is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
