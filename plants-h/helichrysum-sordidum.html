Helichrysum sordidum

Helichrysum sordidum, commonly known as the brown everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers that are often covered in fine, hairy structures. The plant produces compact, evergreen shrubs with narrow, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping.

Helichrysum sordidum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in dried flower arrangements.
