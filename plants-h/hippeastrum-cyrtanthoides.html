Hippeastrum cyrtanthoides

Hippeastrum cyrtanthoides, commonly known as the golden torch lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It produces large, showy flowers that are typically orange to yellow in color, with a distinctive torch-like shape. The plant features strap-like, narrow leaves that are often evergreen, providing a lush backdrop for its vibrant blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long flowering period, which can extend from late spring through early summer.
