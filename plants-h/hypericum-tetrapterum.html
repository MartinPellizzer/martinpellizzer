Hypericum tetrapterum

Hypericum tetrapterum, commonly known as the four-winged St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in summer. Its distinctive four-winged seeds are a notable botanical feature, aiding in wind dispersal.

Hypericum tetrapterum is part of the genus Hypericum, which includes many species known for their medicinal and ornamental value.
