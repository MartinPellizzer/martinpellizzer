Hydrangea arborescens

Hydrangea arborescens, commonly known as the smooth hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, round, panicle-shaped flower clusters that range in color from white to pink, depending on soil pH. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes. Its foliage turns a striking red or orange in the fall, adding seasonal interest.

Hydrangea arborescens is relatively low maintenance and resistant to many common pests and diseases.
