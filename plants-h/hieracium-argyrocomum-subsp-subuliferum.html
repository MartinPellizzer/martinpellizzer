Hieracium argyrocomum subsp. subuliferum

Hieracium argyrocomum subsp.

subuliferum, commonly known as the silverleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It features a rosette of narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces yellow flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
