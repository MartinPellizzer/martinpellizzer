Hakea leptobotrys

Hakea leptobotrys, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces cylindrical, red or orange flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system to access water.

Hakea leptobotrys is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
