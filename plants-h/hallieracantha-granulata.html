Hallieracantha granulata

Hallieracantha granulata, commonly known as the granulate sandmat, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically forms a dense mat of fleshy, succulent leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. Its succulent leaves are adapted to store water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

It is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in drought-tolerant gardens.
