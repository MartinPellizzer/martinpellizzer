Hieracium neopicris subsp. conyzoides

Hieracium neopicris subsp.

conyzoides, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, with flower heads that are often surrounded by pale yellow ray florets.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is occasionally grown in gardens.
