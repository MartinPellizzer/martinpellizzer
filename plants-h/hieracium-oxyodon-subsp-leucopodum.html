Hieracium oxyodon subsp. leucopodum

Hieracium oxyodon subsp.

leucopodum, commonly known as the white-footed hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head.

The subspecies is distinguished by its pale or white basal leaves, which contrast with the green upper leaves. It typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows.

The plant is native to Europe and has been introduced to other regions, where it can sometimes become invasive.
