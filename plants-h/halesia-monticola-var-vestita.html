Halesia monticola var. vestita

Halesia monticola var. vestita, commonly known as the white fringe tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its pendulous clusters of white, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The leaves are simple, alternate, and oval-shaped with a smooth margin. The plant has a smooth, gray bark that becomes fissured with age.

It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.
