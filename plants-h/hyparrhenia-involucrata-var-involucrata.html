Hyparrhenia involucrata var. involucrata

Hyparrhenia involucrata var. involucrata, commonly known as the African grass or red grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, tufted growth habit and produces dense, reddish-brown inflorescences that give it its common name. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a fibrous structure, making it adaptable to various soil types and environmental conditions. It thrives in full sun and is often found in open woodlands and savannas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop in some regions.
