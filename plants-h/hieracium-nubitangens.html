Hieracium nubitangens

Hieracium nubitangens, commonly known as the Sudanese hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hieracium nubitangens is often found in open habitats, including grasslands and along the edges of deserts.
