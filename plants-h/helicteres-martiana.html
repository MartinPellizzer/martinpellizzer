Helicteres martiana

Helicteres martiana, commonly known as the red swampmallow, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling mallow flowers. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are typically covered in soft, hairy tissue. It thrives in wet, swampy areas and is often found near rivers and in marshlands.

Helicteres martiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by some indigenous communities.
