Hibiscus pentaphyllus

Hibiscus pentaphyllus, commonly known as the five-leaf hibiscus, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family, characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are palmately lobed, with five distinct leaflets, giving it its common name.

Hibiscus pentaphyllus blooms with vibrant pink to red flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
