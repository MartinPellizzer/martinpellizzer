Helicotropis spectabilis

Helicotropis spectabilis, commonly known as the showy heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves that are usually oval to lance-shaped. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Helicotropis spectabilis is also known for its aromatic foliage, which can be used in herbal teas and traditional medicine.
