Hieracium murorum subsp. behmii

Hieracium murorum subsp.

behmii, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. It has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and its yellow flowers are arranged in a dense, flat head.

This subspecies is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in supporting pollinators.
