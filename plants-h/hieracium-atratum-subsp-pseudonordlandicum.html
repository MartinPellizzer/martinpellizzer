Hieracium atratum subsp. pseudonordlandicum

Hieracium atratum subsp.

pseudonordlandicum, commonly known as the black hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its dark green, hairy leaves and yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and woodland edges. It has a rosette of basal leaves and produces numerous slender, flowering stems.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
