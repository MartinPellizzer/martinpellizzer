Heteroarisaema heterophyllum

Heteroarisaema heterophyllum, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female structures. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a starch source.

The leaves are large, heart-shaped, and have a waxy surface, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
