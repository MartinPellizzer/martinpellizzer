Humboltia ruberrima

Humboltia ruberrima, commonly known as the scarlet coral tree, is a striking tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a dramatic visual display. The plant has a woody stem with a coral-like appearance, which gives it its common name. Its leaves are compound, consisting of multiple small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Humboltia ruberrima thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
