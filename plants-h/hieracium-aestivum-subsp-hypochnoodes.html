Hieracium aestivum subsp. hypochnoodes

Hieracium aestivum subsp.

hypochnoodes, commonly known as the summer hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

This subspecies is distinguished by its shorter, more compact inflorescence compared to other members of the species.
