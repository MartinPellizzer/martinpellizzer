Hieracium levicaule subsp. leucothyrsoides

Hieracium levicaule subsp.

leucothyrsoides, commonly known as the white thistle. It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems.

The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with serrated edges and covered in fine hairs.

Native to specific regions of Europe, it thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky soils.
