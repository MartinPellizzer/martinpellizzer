Hoita glandulosa

Hoita glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular hoita, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has a woody stem with glandular structures that secrete a sticky substance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Its unique botanical features make it an interesting subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
