Hieracium furcatum var. rubriparietinum

Hieracium furcatum var. rubriparietinum, commonly known as the red-leaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and distinctive leaves that are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and forest edges.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
