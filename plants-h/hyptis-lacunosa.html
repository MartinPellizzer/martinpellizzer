Hyptis lacunosa

Hyptis lacunosa, commonly known as the Mexican mint or blue hyptis, is a perennial herb native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their essential oils. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy texture. Its flowers are typically blue or purple, arranged in spikes, and bloom during the summer months.

Hyptis lacunosa is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
