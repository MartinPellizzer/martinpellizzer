Hypericum aspalathoides

Hypericum aspalathoides, commonly known as the golden bush, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and small, elliptical leaves. The plant belongs to the family Hypericaceae and is often found in rocky, well-drained soils. It produces small, red berries that are a food source for various birds and insects.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine.
