Huperzia smithiana

Huperzia smithiana, commonly known as the cliff brake, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its leathery, evergreen leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant is native to regions of eastern and southeastern Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as rocky crevices and forest floors.

Huperzia smithiana is notable for its ability to survive in poor soil conditions and its use in traditional medicine.
