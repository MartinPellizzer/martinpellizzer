Hieracium pellatianum

Hieracium pellatianum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, spoon-shaped leaves and its showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is native to Europe and has been introduced to other parts of the world, often becoming invasive. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Hieracium pellatianum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and its tolerance to various environmental conditions.
