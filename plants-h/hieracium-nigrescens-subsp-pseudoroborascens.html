Hieracium nigrescens subsp. pseudoroborascens

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

pseudoroborascens, commonly known as the black hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

This subspecies is distinguished by its dark purple to black stems and is often found in association with other members of the Hieracium genus.
