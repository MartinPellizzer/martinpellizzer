Herbertia caerulea

Herbertia caerulea, commonly known as the blue herbertia, is a striking perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of delicate, blue-violet flowers. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped blooms that appear in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often a vibrant green.

Herbertia caerulea thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
