Hypericopsis montana

Hypericopsis montana, commonly known as the mountain St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is a member of the Hypericaceae family, which includes other species of St. John's wort. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving at high altitudes with cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are opposite and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with four petals.

The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds, aiding in its propagation in its natural environment.
