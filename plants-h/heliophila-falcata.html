Heliophila falcata

Heliophila falcata, commonly known as the sickle-leaved water plant, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, sickle-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating in water. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats and is often found in marshes and pond margins.

Heliophila falcata is an important species for aquatic ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various insects and small aquatic organisms.
