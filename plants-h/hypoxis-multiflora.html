Hypoxis multiflora

Hypoxis multiflora, commonly known as the starry yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with white markings. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes. Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
