Hymenachne leptostachya

Hymenachne leptostachya, commonly known as the slender-stalked hymenachne, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to submerged soil conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense stands that can stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life.

Hymenachne leptostachya is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to tolerate and remediate waterlogged environments.
