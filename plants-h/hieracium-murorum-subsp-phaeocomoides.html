Hieracium murorum subsp. phaeocomoides

Hieracium murorum subsp.

phaeocomoides, commonly known as the dark-leaved groundsel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

The plant features basal leaves that are dark green and often have a bluish or purplish hue. Its flowers are yellow and borne in clustered inflorescences, typically blooming from June to August.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
