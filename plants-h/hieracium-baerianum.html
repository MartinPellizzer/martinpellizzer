Hieracium baerianum

Hieracium baerianum, commonly known as the Baerian hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces yellow to orange composite flowers, which are typically arranged in a single head atop a hairy stem. Its leaves are generally lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Hieracium baerianum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
