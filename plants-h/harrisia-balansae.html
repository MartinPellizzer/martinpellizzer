Harrisia balansae

Harrisia balansae, commonly known as the night blooming cactus, is a species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in spines. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to arid environments.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique flowering behavior.
