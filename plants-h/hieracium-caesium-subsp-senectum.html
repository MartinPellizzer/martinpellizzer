Hieracium caesium subsp. senectum

Hieracium caesium subsp.

senectum, commonly known as the old man's beard, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its long, drooping inflorescences that resemble a beard, composed of numerous small yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while the stems are upright and often branched.
