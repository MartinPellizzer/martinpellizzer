Hirschfeldia incana subsp. leptocarpa

Hirschfeldia incana subsp.

leptocarpa, commonly known as the slender hairysedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically borne on slender stalks. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of desert and steppe ecosystems.
