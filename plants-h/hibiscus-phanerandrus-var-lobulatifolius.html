Hibiscus phanerandrus var. lobulatifolius

Hibiscus phanerandrus var. lobulatifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Hibiscus phanerandrus var. lobulatifolius is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
