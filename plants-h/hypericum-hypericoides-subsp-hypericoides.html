Hypericum hypericoides subsp. hypericoides

Hypericum hypericoides subsp.

hypericoides, commonly known as yellow-stemmed St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the genus Hypericum in the family Clusiaceae, characterized by its yellow flowers and medicinal properties.

The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs, while its flowers consist of five yellow petals and numerous stamens.

This subspecies is noted for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
