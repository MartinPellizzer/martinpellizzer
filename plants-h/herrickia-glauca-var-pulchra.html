Herrickia glauca var. pulchra

Herrickia glauca var. pulchra, commonly known as the blue-green mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blooms. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, blue-green flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for wetland restoration and native gardens.
