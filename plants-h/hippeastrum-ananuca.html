Hippeastrum ananuca

Hippeastrum ananuca, commonly known as the Brazilian belladonna lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a distinctive purple or red throat. The plant produces large, leathery leaves that are typically broad and strap-like, emerging from a bulbous base. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers warm, sunny conditions.

Hippeastrum ananuca is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in tropical and subtropical climates.
