Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp. glaucibifidum

Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp.

glaucibifidum, commonly known as the glaucous-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and forest edges.

The plant features basal rosettes of silver-green, hairy leaves that are often glandular and have a distinctive bluish-white coating. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in dense, flat-topped inflorescences.

It is characterized by its unique leaf morphology and is part of the Asteraceae family.
