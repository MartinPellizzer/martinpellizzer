Hieracium nesaeum

Hieracium nesaeum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium nesaeum is known for its resilience to cold climates and is often found in meadows and rocky slopes.
