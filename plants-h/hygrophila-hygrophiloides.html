Hygrophila hygrophiloides

Hygrophila hygrophiloides, commonly known as water hygrophila, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale yellow flowers, which are often arranged in spikes. It thrives in wet or aquatic environments, such as marshes, ponds, and slow-moving streams.

Hygrophila hygrophiloides is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and improve water quality.
