Hibiscus henningsianus

Hibiscus henningsianus, commonly known as the Hennings hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its flowers bloom in clusters.

Hibiscus henningsianus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to warm, humid climates.
