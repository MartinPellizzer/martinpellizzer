Hemarthria longiflora var. tonkinensis

Hemarthria longiflora var. tonkinensis, commonly known as Tonkin grass, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets with two to three short, awned florets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
