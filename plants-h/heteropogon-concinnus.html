Heteropogon concinnus

Heteropogon concinnus, commonly known as the slender grass, is a perennial grass species native to various regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a fibrous root system and produces wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in ecological restoration projects.
