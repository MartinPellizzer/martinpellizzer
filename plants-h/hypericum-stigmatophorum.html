Hypericum stigmatophorum

Hypericum stigmatophorum, commonly known as the stigmatophore hypericum, is a flowering plant species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the genus Hypericum, which includes over 400 species of shrubs and herbs. The plant is characterized by its yellow flowers, which typically bloom in summer, and its woody stems covered in small, scale-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
