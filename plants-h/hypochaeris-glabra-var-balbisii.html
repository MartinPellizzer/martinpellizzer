Hypochaeris glabra var. balbisii

Hypochaeris glabra var. balbisii, commonly known as the smooth mouse-ear cress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial species characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellow flowers arranged in a cluster. The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and gardens.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural and urban environments.
