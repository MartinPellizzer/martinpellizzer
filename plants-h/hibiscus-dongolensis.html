Hibiscus dongolensis

Hibiscus dongolensis, commonly known as the Sudanese hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers range in color from white to pink.

Hibiscus dongolensis is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in semi-arid environments.
