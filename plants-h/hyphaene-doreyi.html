Hyphaene doreyi

Hyphaene doreyi, commonly known as the African silvergrass, is a species of palm native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clustered, spiky fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and as thatching material.

Hyphaene doreyi plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
