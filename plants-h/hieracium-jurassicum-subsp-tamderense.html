Hieracium jurassicum subsp. tamderense

Hieracium jurassicum subsp.

tamderense, commonly known as the Tamderian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky soils. It has a fibrous root system and exhibits a compact growth form, which helps it survive in its arid environment.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
