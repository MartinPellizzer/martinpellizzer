Helleborus viridis var. subcoloratus

Helleborus viridis var. subcoloratus, commonly known as the green hellebore or lesser hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its dark green, waxy leaves that are typically heart-shaped and have a smooth texture. The plant produces clusters of pale green to white flowers with a distinctive waxy appearance, which bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in shady, moist environments and prefers well-drained, loamy soil.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and its ability to add early color to gardens.
