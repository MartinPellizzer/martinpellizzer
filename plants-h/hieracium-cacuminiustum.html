Hieracium cacuminiustum

Hieracium cacuminiustum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its yellow flowers are usually arranged in a flat-topped cluster, and the leaves are lance-shaped with hairy surfaces.

It is known for its aggressive growth and ability to form dense colonies, making it a common weed in various environments.
