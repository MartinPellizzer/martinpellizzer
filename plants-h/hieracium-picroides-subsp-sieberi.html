Hieracium picroides subsp. sieberi

Hieracium picroides subsp.

sieberi, commonly known as Sieber's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, and it is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed environments.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different leaf morphology and flower structure compared to other members of the *Hieracium* genus.
