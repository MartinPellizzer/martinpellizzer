Hieracium murorum subsp. leucochloropsis

Hieracium murorum subsp.

leucochloropsis, commonly known as the white-veined hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers with white veins.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. It has basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs.
