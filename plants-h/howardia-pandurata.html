Howardia pandurata

Howardia pandurata, commonly known as the flame bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that appear in clusters during the summer months. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white powder. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in arid regions.

The botanical features of Howardia pandurata make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in its native habitat.
