Heteropappus eligulatus

Heteropappus eligulatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf fleabane, is an annual flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, and the flowers are yellow with a pale center.

It is often found in arid regions and is adapted to thrive in low-nutrient environments.
