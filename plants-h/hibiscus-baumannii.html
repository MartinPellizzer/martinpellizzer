Hibiscus baumannii

Hibiscus baumannii, commonly known as the velvetleaf hibiscus, is a species of flowering plant in the Malvaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. Hibiscus baumannii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or near water bodies.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for various herbivores.
