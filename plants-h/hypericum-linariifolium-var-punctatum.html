Hypericum linariifolium var. punctatum

Hypericum linariifolium var. punctatum, commonly known as the spotted St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its yellow flowers and distinctive spotted leaves. The plant belongs to the family Hypericaceae and is known for its small, clustered blooms that appear in summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a slightly serrated edge, while the stems are often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
