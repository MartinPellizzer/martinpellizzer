Hypericum maculatum subsp. quadrangulum

Hypericum maculatum subsp.

quadrangulum, commonly known as the spotted jewelweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its distinctive, quadrangular stems and small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
