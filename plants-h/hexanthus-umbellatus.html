Hexanthus umbellatus

Hexanthus umbellatus, commonly known as the yellow wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of yellow, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial shade. It produces small, round seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread.

Hexanthus umbellatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
