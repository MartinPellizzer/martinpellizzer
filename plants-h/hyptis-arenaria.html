Hyptis arenaria

Hyptis arenaria, commonly known as sand hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the Americas, particularly found in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant typically grows in sandy or well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or disturbed sites. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

Hyptis arenaria is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
