Hieracium caesium subsp. pectinatulum

Hieracium caesium subsp.

pectinatulum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has finely divided, hairy leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette, and its stems are covered with short, stiff hairs. It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows, and is often found in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is occasionally grown in gardens.
