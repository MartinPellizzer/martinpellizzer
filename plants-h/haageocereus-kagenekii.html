Haageocereus kagenekii

Haageocereus kagenekii, commonly known as the Kagenekii night-blooming cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus with ribbed stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to arid environments.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming habit.
