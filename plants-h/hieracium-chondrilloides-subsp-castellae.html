Hieracium chondrilloides subsp. castellae

Hieracium chondrilloides subsp.

castellae, commonly known as the rock hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

The plant features a basal rosette of leaves with a hairy surface, and it produces yellow composite flowers in late spring to early summer. It is characterized by its small, glandular leaves and a compact growth habit.

This subspecies is often found in alpine and subalpine regions, where it thrives in well-drained conditions.
