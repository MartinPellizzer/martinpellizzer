Hyptis pastorei

Hyptis pastorei, commonly known as Brazilian hyptis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or violet flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Hyptis pastorei is also valued for its medicinal properties, used in traditional remedies for various ailments.
