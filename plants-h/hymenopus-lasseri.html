Hymenopus lasseri

Hymenopus lasseri, commonly known as the golden star vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, resembling small stars. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green in color. It thrives in humid environments and is often found growing along forest edges or in shaded areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
