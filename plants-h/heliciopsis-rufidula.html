Heliciopsis rufidula

Heliciopsis rufidula, commonly known as the red-stemmed hibiscus, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming during the dry season. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Heliciopsis rufidula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
