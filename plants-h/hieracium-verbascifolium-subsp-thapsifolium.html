Hieracium verbascifolium subsp. thapsifolium

Hieracium verbascifolium subsp.

thapsifolium, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in moist meadows and grasslands, often forming dense colonies.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
