Hyptis winkleri

Hyptis winkleri, commonly known as the Brazilian hyptis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its gray-green, velvety leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to violet. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant's compact growth habit makes it suitable for both ornamental and medicinal purposes in suitable environments.
