Hippeastrum pseudopardinum

Hippeastrum pseudopardinum, commonly known as the Amazonian hurricane lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It features large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, typically in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has a robust bulb that stores nutrients, allowing it to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often appearing after the flowers have bloomed.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and as a potted plant.
