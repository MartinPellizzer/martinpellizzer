Hoya wibergiae

Hoya wibergiae, commonly known as the Wiberg's waxplant, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and waxy, ovate leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped, pale green to white flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. Hoya wibergiae is known for its slow growth and ability to thrive in low-light conditions, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its unique appearance and ease of care contribute to its appeal among plant enthusiasts.
