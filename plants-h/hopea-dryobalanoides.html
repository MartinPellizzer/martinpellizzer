Hopea dryobalanoides

Hopea dryobalanoides, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a large evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall, with a broad, spreading crown and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, while its fruits are woody capsules that split open to release seeds.

The plant plays an important ecological role in tropical rainforests, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
