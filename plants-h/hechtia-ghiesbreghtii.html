Hechtia ghiesbreghtii

Hechtia ghiesbreghtii, commonly known as the Ghiesbreght hechtia, is a striking member of the Bromeliaceae family native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped foliage, which forms a dense, water-holding cup at the center. The plant produces striking inflorescences with bright red bracts that contrast beautifully with its dark green leaves. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
