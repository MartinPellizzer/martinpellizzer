Hakea obliqua subsp. obliqua

Hakea obliqua subsp.

obliqua, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hakea, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It typically grows in heathlands and coastal areas, characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage.

The plant produces cylindrical, woody flower spikes that are usually pink to red in color. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and often have a slight zigzag pattern along the edges. Hakea obliqua subsp.

obliqua is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
