Hypericum apollinis

Hypericum apollinis, commonly known as the Apollo goldenstar, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Hypericaceae family, characterized by its bright yellow flowers and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hypericum apollinis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and resilience in coastal or dry environments.
