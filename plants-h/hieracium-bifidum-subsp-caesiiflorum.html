Hieracium bifidum subsp. caesiiflorum

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

caesiiflorum, commonly known as the blueflower hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces daisy-like flowers with blue to violet petals.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its flowering period occurs from late spring to early summer, and it is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it adaptable to different environments.
