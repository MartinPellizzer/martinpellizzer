Hydrangea macrophylla f. acuminata

Hydrangea macrophylla f. acuminata, commonly known as the serrated leaf hydrangea, is a cultivar characterized by its large, showy flowers and distinctive, serrated foliage.

This variety typically blooms in shades of pink, red, or blue, depending on soil pH, and thrives in partial to full shade. It is a deciduous shrub native to Japan, often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. The leaves are broad and have a rough, textured surface, adding visual interest throughout the growing season.

It prefers moist, well-drained soil and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.
