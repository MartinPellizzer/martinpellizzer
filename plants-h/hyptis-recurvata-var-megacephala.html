Hyptis recurvata var. megacephala

Hyptis recurvata var. megacephala, commonly known as the broad-headed hyptis, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and culinary uses. The plant is characterized by its hairy, square stems and opposite leaves that are often grayish-green in color. Its flowers, which are typically purple or blue, are clustered in dense spikes and attract various pollinators.

This species is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
