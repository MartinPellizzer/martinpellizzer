Hirtella scabra

Hirtella scabra, commonly known as the rough hirtella, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments within dense forest undergrowth. Its leaves are often glandular and can exude a mild aromatic scent.

Hirtella scabra is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecological systems.
