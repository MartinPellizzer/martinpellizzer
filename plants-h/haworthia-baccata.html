Haworthia baccata

Haworthia baccata, commonly known as the red-tipped haworthia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are usually fleshy and covered in a whitish bloom. The plant produces small, white flowers on tall, slender stems during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Haworthia baccata is popular as a houseplant due to its compact size and striking appearance.
