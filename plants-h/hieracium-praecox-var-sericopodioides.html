Hieracium praecox var. sericopodioides

Hieracium praecox var. sericopodioides, commonly known as the silverleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features a rosette of basal leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, arranged in a single terminal inflorescence. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological studies and garden cultivation.
