Hieracium vulgatum subsp. violascentiforme

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

violascentiforme, commonly known as the violet-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm.

The plant features basal rosettes of lanceolate leaves that are often tinged with violet, giving it its common name. Its flowers are yellow and borne in a single head, with involucral bracts that are usually greenish or reddish.

The species is characterized by its fibrous root system and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
