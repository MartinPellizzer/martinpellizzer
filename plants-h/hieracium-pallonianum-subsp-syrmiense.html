Hieracium pallonianum subsp. syrmiense

Hieracium pallonianum subsp.

syrmiense, commonly known as the Sirmian hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in the summer.

The plant is native to parts of Europe, particularly in regions such as Hungary and Serbia. It thrives in open, disturbed habitats like meadows and roadsides.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some areas.
