Hypericum scabrum var. sublaeve

Hypericum scabrum var. sublaeve, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters along the stems. The plant has slender, woody stems that are covered in a smooth, slightly hairy texture. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly rough or scabrous surface.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in herbal medicine.
