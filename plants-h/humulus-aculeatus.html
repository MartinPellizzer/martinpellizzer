Humulus aculeatus

Humulus aculeatus, commonly known as hops, is a climbing perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is characterized by its hollow, jointed stems and clusters of cone-like flowers called strobiles. The plant produces a distinctive, aromatic resin that is used extensively in brewing beer. Its leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soil.

Humulus aculeatus is also cultivated for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
