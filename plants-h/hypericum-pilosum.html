Hypericum pilosum

Hypericum pilosum, commonly known as the hairy St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and hairy stems, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the genus Hypericum, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their medicinal properties.

Hypericum pilosum is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported therapeutic benefits.
