Hedysarum boreale var. pabulare

Hedysarum boreale var. pabulare, commonly known as the alpine vetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Hedysarum boreale var. pabulare thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and grasslands.

It plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plant life.
