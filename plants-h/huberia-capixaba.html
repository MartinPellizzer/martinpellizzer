Huberia capixaba

Huberia capixaba, commonly known as the Capixaba nightshade, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades and tomatoes. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy and have a glandular surface. Its flowers are typically white or pale purple, with five petals and a prominent central stamen.

The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that ripens to a dark purple color, containing several seeds.
