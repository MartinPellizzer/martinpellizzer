Hymenanthes japonica

Hymenanthes japonica, commonly known as the Japanese snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. Its fruit is a small, dry capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Hymenanthes japonica is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and pleasant fragrance.
