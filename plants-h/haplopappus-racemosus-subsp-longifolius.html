Haplopappus racemosus subsp. longifolius

Haplopappus racemosus subsp.

longifolius, commonly known as the longleaf haplopappus, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and linear, often gray-green leaves.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, which are followed by bristly, pappus-bearing seeds. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
