Heliconia indica var. indica

Heliconia indica var. indica, commonly known as the red heliconia, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, colorful inflorescences that resemble a bird's beak, with bright red bracts and yellow-orange flowers. The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests or along coastal areas. Its leaves are broad and waxy, providing a lush, tropical appearance.

The red heliconia is also valued for its ornamental use in gardens and as a cut flower.
