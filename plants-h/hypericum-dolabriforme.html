Hypericum dolabriforme

Hypericum dolabriforme, commonly known as the broad-blade St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in woodland and moist meadow habitats, often growing in partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and native plant gardens.
