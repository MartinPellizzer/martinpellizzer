Hybanthus strictus

Hybanthus strictus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hybanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous annual with opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

The species is notable for its rapid growth and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
