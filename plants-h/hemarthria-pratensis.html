Hemarthria pratensis

Hemarthria pratensis, commonly known as common panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, cylindrical stems. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically arranged in dense, compact clusters. Hemarthria pratensis is often found in moist, well-drained soils in grasslands and along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
