Helia vasculosa

Helia vasculosa, commonly known as the red root or bloodroot, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers and a distinctive, thick, reddish root system. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Helia vasculosa is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
