Hieracium andreanszkyanum

Hieracium andreanszkyanum, commonly known as the Hungarian hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. It produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer, and its leaves are usually lance-shaped with hairy surfaces.

Hieracium andreanszkyanum is often considered a weed due to its aggressive growth and ability to colonize various environments.
