Hieracium olivaceum subsp. flocculosum

Hieracium olivaceum subsp.

flocculosum, commonly known as the fluffy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, gray-green leaves and produces yellowish-orange composite flowers in late summer.

The plant is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is known for its fluffy, woolly appearance due to dense pubescence.

This subspecies is typically short-lived and has a relatively small geographic distribution.
