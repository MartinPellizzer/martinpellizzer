Heteropterys grandiflora subvar. pachypus

Heteropterys grandiflora subvar. pachypus, commonly known as the thick-leafed gloryberry, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, ovate leaves that are thick and leathery, often with a glossy surface. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in warm climates for its striking foliage and floral display.
