Hieracium auricula subsp. amaureilema

Hieracium auricula subsp.

amaureilema, commonly known as the golden hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers that resemble small daisies.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are erect and often reddish in color.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
