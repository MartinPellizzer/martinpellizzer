Hebe glaucocaerulea

Hebe glaucocaerulea, commonly known as the silver-blue hebe, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often appearing in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically covered in a fine, silvery-blue coating, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Hebe glaucocaerulea is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
