Heliconia irrasa var. glabra

Heliconia irrasa var. glabra, commonly known as the white heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its large, paddle-shaped leaves and vibrant, spadix-like inflorescences. The flowers are typically white or pale yellow, surrounded by long, colorful bracts that give the plant its ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a cut flower.

The plant is also valued for its role in supporting local wildlife, particularly birds and insects.
