Helictotrichon oligostachyum

Helictotrichon oligostachyum, commonly known as the slender bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wet meadows.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities and a valuable species for ecological restoration projects.
