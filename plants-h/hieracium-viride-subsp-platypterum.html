Hieracium viride subsp. platypterum

Hieracium viride subsp.

platypterum, commonly known as the broad-leaf hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its broader, more robust leaves compared to other forms of Hieracium viride. It typically thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and other open habitats.

The plant is also known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
