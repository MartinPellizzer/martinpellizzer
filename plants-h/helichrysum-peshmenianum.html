Helichrysum peshmenianum

Helichrysum peshmenianum, commonly known as the Armenian everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Armenia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish-orange flowers that resemble daisies. The plant produces fluffy, seed-filled heads that are often used in floral arrangements due to their long-lasting nature. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its name reflects both its enduring appearance and its geographical origin.
