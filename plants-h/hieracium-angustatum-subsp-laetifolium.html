Hieracium angustatum subsp. laetifolium

Hieracium angustatum subsp.

laetifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It features upright, hairy stems that bear alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture.

The plant produces yellow flower heads composed of numerous small florets, which bloom from June to August. It thrives in sunny, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species within the Asteraceae family.
