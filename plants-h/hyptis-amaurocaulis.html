Hyptis amaurocaulis

Hyptis amaurocaulis, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a perennial herb native to the humid regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Hyptis amaurocaulis is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
