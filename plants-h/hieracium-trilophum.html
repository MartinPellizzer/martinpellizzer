Hieracium trilophum

Hieracium trilophum, commonly known as the three-lobed hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped with three distinct lobes, and its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium trilophum is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in various habitats.
