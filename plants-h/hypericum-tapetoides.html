Hypericum tapetoides

Hypericum tapetoides, commonly known as the yellow-stemmed St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and yellow stems, which are particularly striking in the summer months. The plant has small, opposite leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both ornamental gardens and ecological restoration projects.
