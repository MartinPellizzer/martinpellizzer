Halenia campanulata

Halenia campanulata, commonly known as the bellflower of the genus Halenia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet-colored flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Halenia campanulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
