Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp. epimecodontoides

Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp.

epimecodontoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.

The plant features basal and cauline leaves that are linear to narrowly elliptic, with a hairy surface. Its yellow composite flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, characteristic of the Asteraceae family.

It has a fibrous root system and is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
