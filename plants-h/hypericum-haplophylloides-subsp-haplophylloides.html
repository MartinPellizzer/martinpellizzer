Hypericum haplophylloides subsp. haplophylloides

Hypericum haplophylloides subsp.

haplophylloides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in dense clusters.

The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine, glandular hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
