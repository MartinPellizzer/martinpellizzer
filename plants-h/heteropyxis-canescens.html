Heteropyxis canescens

Heteropyxis canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and distinctive, opposite, palmate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras that aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
