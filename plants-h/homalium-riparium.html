Homalium riparium

Homalium riparium, commonly known as the river homalium, is a tropical plant native to regions in South America and Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows near water bodies, showcasing its adaptability to moist environments. The plant features opposite, simple leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape, with a glossy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered, often white or pale yellow, and it produces fleshy, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for wildlife.

Homalium riparium is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological significance in riparian ecosystems.
