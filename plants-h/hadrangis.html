Hadrangis

Hadrangis, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often resembling small bells. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces fruit that turns red when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Hadrangis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
