Helena × planicorona

Helena × planicorona, commonly known as the hybrid helena, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces showy, daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white to pink petals. The plant typically blooms in the summer months and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.

Helena × planicorona is native to regions of Europe and is valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
