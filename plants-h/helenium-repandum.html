Helenium repandum

Helenium repandum, commonly known as the wandering fireweed, is a species of flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its bright orange to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and stream banks, and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and branching.

Helenium repandum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators.
