Hypericum paramitanum

Hypericum paramitanum, commonly known as the Paramitan St. John's Wort, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Hypericaceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and orange-red berries. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Hypericum paramitanum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
