Hieracium tephrinocephalum

Hieracium tephrinocephalum, commonly known as the Tephrinocephalum hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are basal, rough, and hairy, with a rosette growth pattern, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a dense cluster.

It is a perennial herb that plays a role in stabilizing soil in its harsh environment.
