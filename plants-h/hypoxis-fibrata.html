Hypoxis fibrata

Hypoxis fibrata, commonly known as the fibrous starwort, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Hypoxidaceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in a dense cluster at the end of each leafy stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Hypoxis fibrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
