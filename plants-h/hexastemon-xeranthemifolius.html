Hexastemon xeranthemifolius

Hexastemon xeranthemifolius, commonly known as the desert bell, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and are borne on long, slender stems. The plant has gray-green, narrow leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stem, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and ornamental gardening in dry climates.
