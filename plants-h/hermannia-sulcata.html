Hermannia sulcata

Hermannia sulcata, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compact rosette of dark green, velvety leaves and produces showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Hermannia sulcata is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.

It is often cultivated for its ability to bloom throughout the year under proper conditions.
