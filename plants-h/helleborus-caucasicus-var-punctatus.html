Helleborus caucasicus var. punctatus

Helleborus caucasicus var. punctatus, commonly known as the spotted hellebore, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It features dark green, glossy leaves with distinctive white or pale spots along the veins. The plant produces showy, nodding flowers in shades of green, purple, and white, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained, moist soil, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in cooler climates.
