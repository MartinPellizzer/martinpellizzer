Hieracium pannonicum subsp. umbellosum

Hieracium pannonicum subsp.

umbellosum, commonly known as the Pannonian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are basal, rough, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers form a flat, umbrella-like cluster.

The subspecies umbellosum is distinguished by its more compact growth habit and specific floral structure compared to other Hieracium species.
