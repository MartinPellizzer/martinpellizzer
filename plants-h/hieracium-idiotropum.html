Hieracium idiotropum

Hieracium idiotropum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal, aiding in its spread.

Hieracium idiotropum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common weed in agricultural and natural areas.
