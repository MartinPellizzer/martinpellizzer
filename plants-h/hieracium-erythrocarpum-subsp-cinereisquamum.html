Hieracium erythrocarpum subsp. cinereisquamum

Hieracium erythrocarpum subsp.

cinereisquamum, commonly known as the grey-squamose hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to central and eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, often greyish stems and basal rosettes of leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in composite flower heads, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
