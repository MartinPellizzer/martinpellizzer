Helenium urmenetae var. leguiffei

Helenium urmenetae var. leguiffei, commonly known as the golden rod, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small sunflowers. The plant features bright yellow to orange ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun.

Helenium urmenetae var. leguiffei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
