Hieracium eximium f. tenellum

Hieracium eximium f. tenellum, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a botanical variant of the hawkweed genus characterized by its compact, herbaceous growth habit.

It features small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This plant is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a subject of taxonomic study within the family Asteraceae.
