Hieracium rohacsense subsp. semiatratum

Hieracium rohacsense subsp.

semiatratum, commonly known as the Carpathian hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Central Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellowish composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. It has narrow, serrated leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that attract various pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and locally endemic species.
