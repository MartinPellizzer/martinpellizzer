Heracleum siamicum

Heracleum siamicum, commonly known as the Siamese cow parsley, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic compounds and hollow stems.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand, and features large, umbrella-like inflorescences composed of small white to greenish-yellow flowers. The plant has finely divided, feather-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found near water bodies in its natural habitat.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
