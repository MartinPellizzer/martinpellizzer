Hieracium florentinum subsp. delphinatus

Hieracium florentinum subsp.

delphinatus, commonly known as the Delphinium hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and showy, dandelion-like yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces small, elongated achenes as its fruit, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
