Hieracium sparsum subsp. lailanum

Hieracium sparsum subsp.

lailanum, commonly known as the Lailanum hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It typically grows in grasslands and open habitats, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.

The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy, with flowering stems that bear yellow composite flowers. Its botanical characteristics include a taproot system and the ability to form colonies through vegetative reproduction.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
