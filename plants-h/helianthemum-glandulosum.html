Helianthemum glandulosum

Helianthemum glandulosum, commonly known as the woolly rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to arid environments. Its flowers bloom in spring and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in rocky landscapes.
