Hyptis minensis

Hyptis minensis, commonly known as Brazilian hyptis, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. Hyptis minensis is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
