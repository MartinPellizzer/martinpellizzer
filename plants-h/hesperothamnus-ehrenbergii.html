Hesperothamnus ehrenbergii

Hesperothamnus ehrenbergii, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh, dry environments and has a deep root system for water absorption. It plays a role in stabilizing soils and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.

Hesperothamnus ehrenbergii is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility in nutrient-poor areas.
