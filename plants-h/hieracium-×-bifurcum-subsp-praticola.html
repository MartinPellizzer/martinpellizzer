Hieracium × bifurcum subsp. praticola

Hieracium × bifurcum subsp.

praticola, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and a flowering stem that bears yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It has a taproot system and produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Its botanical features make it a common and widespread species in temperate regions.
