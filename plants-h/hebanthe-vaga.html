Hebanthe vaga

Hebanthe vaga, commonly known as the wandering begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 cm. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves and produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in moist forest undergrowth.

Hebanthe vaga is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
