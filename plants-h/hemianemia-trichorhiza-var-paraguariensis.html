Hemianemia trichorhiza var. paraguariensis

Hemianemia trichorhiza var. paraguariensis, commonly known as ayahuasca, is a climbing vine native to the Amazon basin.

It contains psychoactive alkaloids, primarily DMT, which are responsible for its hallucinogenic effects. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in humid, tropical environments with partial shade.

Due to its cultural and medicinal significance, it is often cultivated in traditional settings.
