Hirschia anthemidifolia

Hirschia anthemidifolia, commonly known as the silky everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, fluffy inflorescence that resembles a dandelion. The plant produces small, yellowish to orange flowers that bloom in summer, followed by persistent, silky bracts that give it its common name. It is often used in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting, decorative appearance.

Hirschia anthemidifolia thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
