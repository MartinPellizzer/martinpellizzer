Hieracium niveobarbatum

Hieracium niveobarbatum, commonly known as the white-hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy rosettes. The plant features pale white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are usually covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
