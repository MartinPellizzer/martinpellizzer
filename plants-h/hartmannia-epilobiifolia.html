Hartmannia epilobiifolia

Hartmannia epilobiifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are elliptical and covered in a fine, velvety texture, giving it its common name.

Hartmannia epilobiifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
