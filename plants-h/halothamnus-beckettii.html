Halothamnus beckettii

Halothamnus beckettii, commonly known as Beckett's halothamnus, is a low-growing, succulent shrub native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. Its leaves are reduced to small scales, a feature common in xerophytic plants.

Halothamnus beckettii plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and provides habitat for various desert organisms.
