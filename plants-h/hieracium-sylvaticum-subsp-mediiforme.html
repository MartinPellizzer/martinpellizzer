Hieracium sylvaticum subsp. mediiforme

Hieracium sylvaticum subsp.

mediiforme, commonly known as the woodland hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellowish to orange flower heads.

The plant typically has basal and alternate leaves that are lanceolate to ovate in shape. It thrives in moist, woodland soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

The subspecies mediiforme is distinguished by its intermediate floral morphology compared to other Hieracium species.
