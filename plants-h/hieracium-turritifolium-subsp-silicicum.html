Hieracium turritifolium subsp. silicicum

Hieracium turritifolium subsp.

silicicum, commonly known as the siliceous hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, silvery-hairy leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in a single, terminal head, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in several regions.
