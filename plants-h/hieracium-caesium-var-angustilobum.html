Hieracium caesium var. angustilobum

Hieracium caesium var. angustilobum, commonly known as narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough, hairy texture, and its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

It is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is considered a weed in some agricultural settings.
