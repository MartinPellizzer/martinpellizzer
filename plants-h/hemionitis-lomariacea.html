Hemionitis lomariacea

Hemionitis lomariacea, commonly known as the water hawthorn, is a unique aquatic plant native to the wetlands of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are often submerged or floating, adapting to its aquatic environment. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically found in the water, contributing to its reproductive strategy. Its rhizomes form dense mats, helping to stabilize the substrate and provide habitat for aquatic organisms.

Hemionitis lomariacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in maintaining water quality in its native habitats.
