Hieracium brachycomum subsp. breunium

Hieracium brachycomum subsp.

breunium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring temperate climates.

The plant features upright stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its yellow flowers, which appear in clusters, are characteristic of the Asteraceae family.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in local ecosystems.
