Hieracium camkorijense subsp. subnitens

Hieracium camkorijense subsp.

subnitens, commonly known as the Karakorum hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, high-altitude environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a distinctive ray floret structure.

It is noted for its robust growth and adaptability to harsh climatic conditions.
