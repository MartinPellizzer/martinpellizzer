Heterolepis anomala

Heterolepis anomala, commonly known as the slender false panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in suitable environments.

Botanically, it is characterized by its distinct leaf morphology and reproductive structures typical of the Poaceae family.
