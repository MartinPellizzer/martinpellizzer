Hydrocotyle lehmannii

Hydrocotyle lehmannii, commonly known as the silverleaf mayapple, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Central and South America.

It features a rosette of heart-shaped, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine, waxy hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as forest floors or wetlands.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental species in the Araliaceae family.
