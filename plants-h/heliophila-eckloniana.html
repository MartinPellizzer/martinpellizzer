Heliophila eckloniana

Heliophila eckloniana, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically yellow or orange in color. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Heliophila eckloniana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
