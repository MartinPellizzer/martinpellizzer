Hydrocotyle grossulariifolia var. jamesonii

Hydrocotyle grossulariifolia var. jamesonii, commonly known as Jameson's water pennywort, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, rounded leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern on the water surface. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and streams.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
