Hieracium glaucescens

Hieracium glaucescens, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks. Its blue to violet flowers are surrounded by pale yellow ray florets, and it produces small, oval-shaped achenes as its fruit.

Hieracium glaucescens is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
