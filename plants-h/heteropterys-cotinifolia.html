Heteropterys cotinifolia

Heteropterys cotinifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a distinctive, fleshy, blue-black berry that ripens in the fall.

Heteropterys cotinifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
