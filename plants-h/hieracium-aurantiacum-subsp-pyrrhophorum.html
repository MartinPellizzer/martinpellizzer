Hieracium aurantiacum subsp. pyrrhophorum

Hieracium aurantiacum subsp.

pyrrhophorum, commonly known as the red hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright orange to reddish flower heads.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

The flowers are composite, consisting of ray florets and disk florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
