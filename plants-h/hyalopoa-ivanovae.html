Hyalopoa ivanovae

Hyalopoa ivanovae, commonly known as the Ivanov's ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the tropical rainforests of Borneo.

It is characterized by its striking, translucent petals that resemble glass, giving it its scientific name "hyalopoa," derived from the Greek words for "glass" and "leaf.". The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to the bark of trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its flowers bloom in a delicate, clustered arrangement, often in shades of white and pale pink.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Hyalopoa ivanovae is highly sensitive to environmental changes and is considered a conservation concern.
