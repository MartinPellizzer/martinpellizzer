Hieracium transpeczoricum

Hieracium transpeczoricum, commonly known as the Transylvania hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its bright yellow flowers bloom from June to August, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
