Hygrophila pogonocalyx

Hygrophila pogonocalyx, commonly known as the velvetleaf hygrophila, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, white to yellowish flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom during the wet season. It thrives in moist to aquatic environments, making it a common sight in marshes and along waterways.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it both a useful ornamental plant and a potential weed in aquatic habitats.
