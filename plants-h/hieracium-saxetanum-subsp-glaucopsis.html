Hieracium saxetanum subsp. glaucopsis

Hieracium saxetanum subsp.

glaucopsis, commonly known as the glaucous hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and yellowish to reddish flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its bluish-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

This plant is often found in open habitats such as meadows and slopes at higher elevations.
