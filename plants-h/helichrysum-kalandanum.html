Helichrysum kalandanum

Helichrysum kalandanum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange inflorescences that resemble small suns. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically clustered in dense, rounded heads. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Helichrysum kalandanum is also known for its aromatic foliage and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
