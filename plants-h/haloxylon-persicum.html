Haloxylon persicum

Haloxylon persicum, commonly known as the Persian date tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and small, edible fruits that resemble dates. The plant is well-adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots that help it survive in drought conditions. Its wood is hard and durable, making it useful for construction and fuel.

Haloxylon persicum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing desert soils and providing food for wildlife.
