Hypericum annulatum subsp. afromontanum

Hypericum annulatum subsp.

afromontanum, commonly known as the Eastern African St. John's Wort, is a shrub native to the highlands of Ethiopia and parts of East Africa. It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters along the stems.

The plant has woody stems and alternate, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces. It is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas at elevations above 2,000 meters.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
