Hieracium bifidum subsp. amblycranum

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

amblycranum, commonly known as the narrow-crowned hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, meadows, and waste areas.

The plant features a basal rosette of leaves that are lanceolate and often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head, with ray florets that are shorter than the disk florets.

This subspecies is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and is often found in dry, sunny environments.
