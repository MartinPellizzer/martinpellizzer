Hamadryas delfinii

Hamadryas delfinii, commonly known as the Mexican blue cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking blue-gray coloration and cylindrical shape, with spines that provide protection against herbivores. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a vibrant contrast to its otherwise muted appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
