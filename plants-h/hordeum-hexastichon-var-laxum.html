Hordeum hexastichon var. laxum

Hordeum hexastichon var. laxum, commonly known as the loose barleygrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Europe.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that often bear a profusion of small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows, and it is well adapted to temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces both male and female flowers on separate spikelets.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for erosion control and as a groundcover.
