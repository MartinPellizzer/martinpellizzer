Hieracium sagittatum subsp. dyringii

Hieracium sagittatum subsp.

dyringii, commonly known as the Dyring's Hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow flowers in late summer. Its inflorescence consists of a single flower head with ray florets and a central disk.

This subspecies is characterized by its distinctive leaf shape and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
