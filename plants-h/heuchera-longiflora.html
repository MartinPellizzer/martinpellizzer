Heuchera longiflora

Heuchera longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered alumroot, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features basal leaves that are heart-shaped with a velvety texture and deep green to reddish hues. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white during the spring. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Heuchera longiflora is valued for its ornamental foliage and early-season bloom.
