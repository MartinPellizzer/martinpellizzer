Heterotheca subaxillaris var. latifolia

Heterotheca subaxillaris var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features narrow, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue. Its flowers are typically yellow with dark centers, blooming in late summer to early fall.

It thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
