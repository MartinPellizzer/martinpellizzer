Hieracium sylvaticum subsp. cinereostriatum

Hieracium sylvaticum subsp.

cinereostriatum, commonly known as the gray-striped hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodlands, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, grayish hairs, giving them a distinctive appearance.

The flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat, disk-like structure, typical of the genus Hieracium.
