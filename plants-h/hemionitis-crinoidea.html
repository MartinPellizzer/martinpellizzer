Hemionitis crinoidea

Hemionitis crinoidea, commonly known as the spoon-leaf plant, is a unique aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and streams, where it forms dense, floating mats. The plant's distinctive, spoon-shaped leaves are arranged in a rosette pattern, providing habitat for small aquatic organisms. Its rhizomes spread horizontally, allowing it to colonize new areas efficiently.

Hemionitis crinoidea plays a role in water filtration and serves as a food source for various aquatic life.
