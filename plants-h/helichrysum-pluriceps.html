Helichrysum pluriceps

Helichrysum pluriceps, commonly known as the goldenhead bush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, yellow-orange inflorescences that resemble small, sun-like heads. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with narrow, gray-green leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Helichrysum pluriceps is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms.
