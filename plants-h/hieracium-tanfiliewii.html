Hieracium tanfiliewii

Hieracium tanfiliewii, commonly known as the Tanfiliew's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
