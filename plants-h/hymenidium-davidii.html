Hymenidium davidii

Hymenidium davidii, commonly known as the David's lily, is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and showy, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Hymenidium davidii is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens due to its unique appearance and limited natural distribution.

However, it is considered endangered due to habitat loss and over-collection.
