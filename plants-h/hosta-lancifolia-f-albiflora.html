Hosta lancifolia f. albiflora

Hosta lancifolia f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered swordleaf hosta, is a cultivar of the hosta plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a silvery underside. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white flowers in late summer, which are borne on tall, upright stems. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value in shaded garden settings.
