Hersilea ramosa

Hersilea ramosa, commonly known as the branching mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually opposite or clustered at the ends of branches. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers, followed by berries that are often consumed by birds. Its root system includes haustoria, which allow it to extract water and nutrients from its host.

Hersilea ramosa plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and influencing the growth of its host plants.
