Hieracium hugeliense

Hieracium hugeliense, commonly known as the Hugel's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can form dense colonies in suitable environments.
