Hieracium firmistolonum

Hieracium firmistolonum, commonly known as the stiff-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and typically hairy stems. The plant features upright, hairy stems that can grow up to 30 centimeters in height, with basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a single head, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

Hieracium firmistolonum is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
