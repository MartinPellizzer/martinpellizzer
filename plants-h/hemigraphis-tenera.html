Hemigraphis tenera

Hemigraphis tenera, commonly known as Mexican mint marigold, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Central America.

It features aromatic, hairy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink and red, which bloom from spring to summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Hemigraphis tenera is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
