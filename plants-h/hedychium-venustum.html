Hedychium venustum

Hedychium venustum, commonly known as the golden ginger, is a striking ornamental plant native to the Himalayas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces large, showy yellow flowers with a strong, spicy fragrance. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and can grow up to 3 meters in height. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sun.

Hedychium venustum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
