Hieracium boliviense var. setosopilosum

Hieracium boliviense var. setosopilosum, commonly known as the Bolivian hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine, bristly hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often found in botanical gardens and specialized collections.
