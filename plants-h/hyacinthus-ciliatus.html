Hyacinthus ciliatus

Hyacinthus ciliatus, commonly known as the Persian hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It produces dense clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that emerge from the base of the bulb. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden planting.

Hyacinthus ciliatus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in traditional herbal medicine.
