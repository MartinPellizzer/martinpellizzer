Hieracium rapunculoides subsp. subcontractum

Hieracium rapunculoides subsp.

subcontractum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks.

The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences. It has a taproot system and is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

The species is characterized by its finely divided leaves and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
