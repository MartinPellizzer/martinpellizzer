Hemilophia pulchella var. pilosa

Hemilophia pulchella var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy hemilophia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is distinguished by its hairy foliage, which gives it its specific epithet "pilosa.". Its flowers typically bloom in shades of yellow and orange, attracting various pollinators.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and woodland habitats.
