Helichrysum formosissimum subsp. volkensii

Helichrysum formosissimum subsp.

volkensii, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, yellow-orange flowers that resemble small suns.

The plant is evergreen, with narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its drought tolerance and long-lasting flowers make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable.
