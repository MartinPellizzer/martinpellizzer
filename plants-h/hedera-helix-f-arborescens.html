Hedera helix f. arborescens

Hedera helix f. arborescens, commonly known as the giant ivy, is a variant of the common ivy characterized by its larger, broader leaves.

It is a climbing evergreen vine native to Europe and is known for its vigorous growth habit. The foliage is typically dark green with a waxy texture, and it can reach heights of several meters when provided with adequate support. This form is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant indoors.

It prefers partial shade to full sun and well-drained soil, making it adaptable to various growing conditions.
