Hordeum vulgare var. griseinigrum

Hordeum vulgare var. griseinigrum, commonly known as gray-black barley, is a variety of barley characterized by its unique husk coloration.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring slender stems and spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and the grains are small and hard.

This variety is often cultivated for its resilience to certain environmental conditions and its use in traditional and specialty food products.
