Hieracium iseranum

Hieracium iseranum, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils at higher elevations. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium iseranum is often found in meadows and grasslands, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
