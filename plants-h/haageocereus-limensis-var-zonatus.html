Haageocereus limensis var. zonatus

Haageocereus limensis var. zonatus, commonly known as the Zoned Night-Blooming Cereus, is a cactus species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and sparse, spiny foliage. The plant produces large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique blooming pattern and striking appearance, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and collections.
