Higginsia refulgens var. pyrophylla

Higginsia refulgens var. pyrophylla, commonly known as the flameleaf higginsia, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant, flame-colored foliage that changes with the seasons, adding a dramatic visual element to gardens. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet, with a dense, rounded form. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and often exhibit a gradient of colors from deep red to orange, especially in full sun.

Higginsia refulgens var. pyrophylla prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a versatile choice for warm climate landscapes.
