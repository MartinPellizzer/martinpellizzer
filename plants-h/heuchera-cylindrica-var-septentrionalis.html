Heuchera cylindrica var. septentrionalis

Heuchera cylindrica var. septentrionalis, commonly known as the Northern columnar heuchera, is a perennial plant native to the northern United States and southern Canada.

It is characterized by its upright, cylindrical growth habit and dense foliage that often exhibits shades of green, red, and purple. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in panicles. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its compact size and ornamental foliage make it a valuable addition to shade gardens and rockeries.
