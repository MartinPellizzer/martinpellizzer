Helleboraster

Helleboraster, commonly known as the hellebore aster, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and features distinctive, often spiky flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plants typically have basal leaves that are deeply lobed or toothed, and they produce small, inconspicuous flowers in the summer. Helleboraster species are often cultivated for their ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

They are also valued for their resilience to drought and partial shade.
