Holcus mollis var. multiflorus

Holcus mollis var. multiflorus, commonly known as the velvet fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and fine, soft blades that give it a velvety appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its durability and aesthetic appeal.

Holcus mollis var. multiflorus is also valued for its ability to withstand moderate foot traffic and its low maintenance requirements.
