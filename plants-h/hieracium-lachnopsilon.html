Hieracium lachnopsilon

Hieracium lachnopsilon, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant features dense, woolly hair on its stems and leaves, which gives it a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade.

Its flowers are yellow and bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators.
