Haageocereus aureispinus var. fuscispinus

Haageocereus aureispinus var. fuscispinus, commonly known as the dark-spined hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in dense, dark spines that provide protection from herbivores. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its spiny structure. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
