Hedysarum nutans

Hedysarum nutans, commonly known as the nodding vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that droop downward, giving it its common name. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Hedysarum nutans is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
