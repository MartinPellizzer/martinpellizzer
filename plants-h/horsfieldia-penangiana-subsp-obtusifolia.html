Horsfieldia penangiana subsp. obtusifolia

Horsfieldia penangiana subsp.

obtusifolia, commonly known as the Penang Horsfield's orchid, is a rare tropical orchid native to Borneo. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant typically grows as an epiphyte on trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its leaves are broad and leathery, and the flowers are usually pale yellow to orange in color.

This subspecies is of particular interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique floral morphology.
