Heliotropium glandulosum

Heliotropium glandulosum, commonly known as sticky heliotrope, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a sticky texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Heliotropium glandulosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture.
