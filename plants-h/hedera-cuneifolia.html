Hedera cuneifolia

Hedera cuneifolia, commonly known as the serrated ivy, is a climbing plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its dark green, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Hedera cuneifolia thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

It is a member of the Araliaceae family and is closely related to other ivy species.
