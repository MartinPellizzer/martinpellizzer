Hoffmannseggella × zaslawskiana

Hoffmannseggella × zaslawskiana, commonly known as the hybrid hoffmannseggella, is a rare and naturally occurring hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Hoffmannseggella lutea and Hoffmannseggella tibetica.

It is characterized by its unique combination of traits from both parent species, including variations in leaf morphology and flower color. The plant typically thrives in high-altitude environments, such as mountainous regions in Central Asia. Its botanical significance lies in its role as a natural hybrid, offering insights into plant evolution and speciation.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a species of conservation interest.
