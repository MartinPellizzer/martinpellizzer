Hackelia venusta

Hackelia venusta, commonly known as the showy bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open woodlands and rocky slopes. The plant features slender, upright stems and clusters of showy, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Hackelia venusta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
