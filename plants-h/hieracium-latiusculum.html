Hieracium latiusculum

Hieracium latiusculum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is often found in temperate climates and is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and seeds.
