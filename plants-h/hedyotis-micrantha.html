Hedyotis micrantha

Hedyotis micrantha, commonly known as small-flowered dotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, white or yellowish flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom during the wet season.

Hedyotis micrantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
