Hippochaete laevigata var. variegatoides

Hippochaete laevigata var. variegatoides, commonly known as the variegated woollyhead, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, woolly, silver-gray foliage that gives it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Its ornamental value lies in its striking foliage and compact growth habit.
