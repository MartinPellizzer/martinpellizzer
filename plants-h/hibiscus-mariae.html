Hibiscus mariae

Hibiscus mariae, commonly known as the Marie hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and red. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in warm, humid climates. Hibiscus mariae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its flowers are known to bloom in summer and can attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
