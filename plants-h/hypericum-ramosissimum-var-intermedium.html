Hypericum ramosissimum var. intermedium

Hypericum ramosissimum var. intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate St. John's wort, is a shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with four petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It prefers sunny to partially shaded locations and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
