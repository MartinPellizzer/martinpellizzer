Hosta taquetii

Hosta taquetii, commonly known as the Taquet's hosta, is a species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a slight blueish tint. The plant produces small, fragrant, white flowers in late summer, which are borne on slender, upright stems. Its foliage is often variegated, featuring white or yellow margins that add visual interest.

Hosta taquetii is a shade-tolerant plant that thrives in moist, well-drained soil.
