Hieracium murorum subsp. eurygonium

Hieracium murorum subsp.

eurygonium, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in open grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring well-drained conditions.

The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters. Its flowering period occurs from late spring to early summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its larger leaves and more robust growth compared to other Hieracium species.
