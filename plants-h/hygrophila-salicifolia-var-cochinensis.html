Hygrophila salicifolia var. cochinensis

Hygrophila salicifolia var. cochinensis, commonly known as the Cochin water plant, is a semi-aquatic herb native to Southeast Asia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating, adapting to wetland environments. The plant produces small, white flowers that are typically arranged in spikes, enhancing its ornamental value. It thrives in shallow waters with moderate to high light exposure.

Its rapid growth makes it a popular choice for pond and aquarium landscaping.
