Heliotropium monostachyum

Heliotropium monostachyum, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, violet or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in wetlands or disturbed soils, showing a preference for moist, well-drained environments. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Heliotropium monostachyum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
