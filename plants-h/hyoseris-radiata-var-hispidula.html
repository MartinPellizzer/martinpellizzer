Hyoseris radiata var. hispidula

Hyoseris radiata var. hispidula, commonly known as the hairy sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant features a hairy, upright stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to xeriscaped gardens.
