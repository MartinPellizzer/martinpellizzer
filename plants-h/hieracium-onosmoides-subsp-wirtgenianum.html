Hieracium onosmoides subsp. wirtgenianum

Hieracium onosmoides subsp.

wirtgenianum, commonly known as the Wirtgen's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant is native to Central and Southern Europe, often found in grassy areas and disturbed soils. It has finely serrated leaves and produces numerous small seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
