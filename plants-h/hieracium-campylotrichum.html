Hieracium campylotrichum

Hieracium campylotrichum, commonly known as the bent-leaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and typically showy inflorescences. The plant has alternate, rough, and often hairy leaves that are lanceolate to ovate in shape. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a composite head, with ray florets surrounding disc florets.

Hieracium campylotrichum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it can form dense colonies due to its rhizomatous growth habit.
