Hemisphaera radchensis

Hemisphaera radchensis, commonly known as the red sandstone lichen, is a unique species of lichen that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

It is characterized by its distinctive hemispherical thallus, which gives it its scientific name. The lichen forms a symbiotic relationship between a fungus and a green alga, allowing it to survive in harsh, nutrient-poor soils. It is often found on rock surfaces, particularly in regions with low humidity and high solar exposure.

Due to its specialized habitat, Hemisphaera radchensis is considered an indicator species for environmental stability and desert ecosystems.
