Hoya fetuana subsp. sigaeleensis

Hoya fetuana subsp.

sigaeleensis, commonly known as the White Star Hoya, is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is native to Madagascar. It is characterized by its clusters of small, waxy, white flowers that resemble stars, hence its common name.

The plant produces thick, fleshy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. It is a climbing or trailing plant that thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil. Hoya fetuana subsp.

sigaeleensis is relatively low-maintenance and is popular among indoor gardeners for its ornamental appeal.
