Hypericum cordiforme var. glazioui

Hypericum cordiforme var. glazioui, commonly known as the heart-shaped St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typical of the Hypericaceae family. The plant has elliptic to heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its medicinal properties, similar to other Hypericum species, have been historically used in traditional medicine.
