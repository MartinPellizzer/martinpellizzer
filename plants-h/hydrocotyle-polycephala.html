Hydrocotyle polycephala

Hydrocotyle polycephala, commonly known as the clustered water pennywort, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has rounded, floating leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, white or yellowish flowers. Its roots help stabilize the soil, making it an important species in aquatic ecosystems.

Hydrocotyle polycephala is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
