Hippeastrum nemorale

Hippeastrum nemorale, commonly known as the forest amaryllis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or red, with a trumpet-shaped structure and prominent stamen. The plant has strap-like, linear leaves that grow from a bulbous base, typical of the Amaryllis family. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial shade to full sun, depending on the climate.

Hippeastrum nemorale is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be grown in gardens or as a potted plant.
