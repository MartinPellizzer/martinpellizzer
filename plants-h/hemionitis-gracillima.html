Hemionitis gracillima

Hemionitis gracillima, commonly known as the slender hemionitis, is a rare and delicate fern native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its finely divided, arching fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in cool, moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of many fern species.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Hemionitis gracillima is considered a species of conservation concern.
