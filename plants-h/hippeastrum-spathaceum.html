Hippeastrum spathaceum

Hippeastrum spathaceum, commonly known as the Brazilian belladonna lily, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that can range in color from white to deep red, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces large, fleshy, purple-brown bulbs that store nutrients for growth. Its leaves are broad, strap-like, and can grow up to several feet in length.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
