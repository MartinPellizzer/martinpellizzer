Hoarea purpurascens

Hoarea purpurascens, commonly known as the purple morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and can reach lengths of several meters. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hoarea purpurascens is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its historical use in traditional medicine.
