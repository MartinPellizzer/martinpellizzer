Hieracium umbellatum subsp. elisabethae

Hieracium umbellatum subsp.

elisabethae, commonly known as the Elisabeth's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lanceolate, with a hairy surface, and the flower heads are usually yellow or orange in color.

It is notable for its ornamental value and is often found in botanical gardens and natural habitats.
