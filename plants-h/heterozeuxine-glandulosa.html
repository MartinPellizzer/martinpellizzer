Heterozeuxine glandulosa

Heterozeuxine glandulosa, commonly known as the hairy glandular thistle, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leaf structures. The plant features glandular hairs on its stems and leaves, which may play a role in defense against herbivores. Its flowers are typically yellow and are arranged in a compact head, typical of the family.

Heterozeuxine glandulosa thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.
