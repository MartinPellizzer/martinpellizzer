Hornstedtia microcheila

Hornstedtia microcheila, commonly known as the dwarf hornstedtia, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its small, grass-like leaves and inconspicuous, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments and is typically found in rocky or alpine habitats. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered and requires careful conservation efforts.
