Helichrysum nuratavicum

Helichrysum nuratavicum, commonly known as the Natal goldcup, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that resemble miniature sunflowers. The plant produces small, papery bracts that give it a distinctive appearance, often mistaken for actual petals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

Helichrysum nuratavicum is also known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
