Hieracium graecum subsp. cyllenium

Hieracium graecum subsp.

cyllenium, commonly known as the Greek hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in a single, terminal head. It has a taproot system and is characterized by its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads.
