Hedysarum armenium

Hedysarum armenium, commonly known as Armenian vetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Turkey.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces slender, cylindrical pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Hedysarum armenium is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
