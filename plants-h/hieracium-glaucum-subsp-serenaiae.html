Hieracium glaucum subsp. serenaiae

Hieracium glaucum subsp.

serenaiae, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its rosette of silver-green, hairy leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and subalpine zones. It has a low-growing habit and is adapted to withstand cold temperatures and strong winds.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species within its habitat.
