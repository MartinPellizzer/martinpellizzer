Helianthemum leucanthum

Helianthemum leucanthum, commonly known as the white rockrose, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has succulent, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, helping it retain moisture in arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its hardy nature allows it to survive in harsh environments, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
