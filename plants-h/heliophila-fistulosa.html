Heliophila fistulosa

Heliophila fistulosa, commonly known as the channel rush, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that often grow in dense clusters, and its small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in spikes. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common sight in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it plays a role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems.

Heliophila fistulosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens.
