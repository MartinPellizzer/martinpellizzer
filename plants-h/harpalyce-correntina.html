Harpalyce correntina

Harpalyce correntina, commonly known as the Brazilian burr legume, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant features dense, thorny stems and small, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. Its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, arranged in racemes, and it produces elongated, woody pods that contain several seeds.

Harpalyce correntina is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.
