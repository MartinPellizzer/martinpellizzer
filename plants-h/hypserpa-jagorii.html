Hypserpa jagorii

Hypserpa jagorii, commonly known as the jagorii orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are narrow and strap-like, and it produces small, clustered blooms in various shades of white and yellow.

Hypserpa jagorii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
