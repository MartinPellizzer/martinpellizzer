Hyaloseris rubicunda

Hyaloseris rubicunda, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Rubiaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pink in color. The plant is known for its aromatic foliage and has been historically valued for its wood, which is dense and durable. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.

Due to overharvesting, it is now considered endangered in some regions.
