Hovea longifolia subvar. apiculata

Hovea longifolia subvar. apiculata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hovea, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or semi-arid regions. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly pointed leaf tips and compact growth habit.
