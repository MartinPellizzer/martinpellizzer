Hieracium pilosella subsp. aclados

Hieracium pilosella subsp.

aclados, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are basal, often hairy, and have a lanceolate shape, while its stems are upright and usually unbranched.

The flowers are yellow, with a central disk surrounded by pale yellow ray florets, contributing to its ornamental appearance.
