Haplopappus alpinus

Haplopappus alpinus, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in harsh, cold environments. Its small yellow flowers bloom in late summer, and it produces fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.

Haplopappus alpinus is often found in mountainous areas of Europe, Asia, and North America.
