Hieracium panaeolum f. myrtillinum

Hieracium panaeolum f. myrtillinum, commonly known as the Myrtle-leaved Hawkweed, is a variant of the hawkweed genus characterized by its distinctive foliage.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with a reddish or purple hue, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the Compositae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
