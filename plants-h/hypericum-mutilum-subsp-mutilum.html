Hypericum mutilum subsp. mutilum

Hypericum mutilum subsp.

mutilum, commonly known as the pale St. John's wort, is a herbaceous perennial shrub native to the eastern United States. It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and features opposite, lanceolate leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

The plant produces yellow-orange flowers with five petals, which bloom in late summer. Its flowering stems are often branched and bear small, bead-like glands.

The species is characterized by its reduced floral structure and is part of the Hypericaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental value.
