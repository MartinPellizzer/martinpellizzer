Hedysarum angrenicum

Hedysarum angrenicum, commonly known as the Angren vetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often found in steppes and rocky soils. It has a creeping growth habit and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Hedysarum angrenicum is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
