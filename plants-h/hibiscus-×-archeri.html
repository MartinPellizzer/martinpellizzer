Hibiscus × archeri

Hibiscus × archeri, commonly known as the Chinese hibiscus, is a hybrid species native to China and Japan.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring showy, single or double flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The leaves are heart-shaped and have a glossy green surface, while the stems are woody and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces capsules that contain numerous small, round seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Hibiscus × archeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
