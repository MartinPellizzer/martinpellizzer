Hieracium eurylepium subsp. multifloccum

Hieracium eurylepium subsp.

multifloccum, commonly known as the clustered hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and produces numerous small, yellowish to reddish flowers arranged in dense clusters.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and grasslands. It has hairy stems and leaves, and its inflorescence consists of a single, compact flower head.

This subspecies is often found in temperate regions of Europe and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
