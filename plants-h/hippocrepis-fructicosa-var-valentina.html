Hippocrepis fructicosa var. valentina

Hippocrepis fructicosa var. valentina, commonly known as the Valentina thimbleweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming from late spring to early summer. The plant has a compact growth habit with slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
