Hypericum saxifragum subsp. eglandulosum

Hypericum saxifragum subsp.

eglandulosum, commonly known as the glandular St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with small, upright stems covered in glandular hairs.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered in fine, sticky glands.

This subspecies is adapted to rocky and sunny habitats, making it a hardy plant in arid and alpine environments.
