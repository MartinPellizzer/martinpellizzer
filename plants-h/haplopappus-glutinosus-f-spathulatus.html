Haplopappus glutinosus f. spathulatus

Haplopappus glutinosus f. spathulatus, commonly known as the broad-leaved sticky haplopappus, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive foliage. The plant features gray-green, ovate to spatulate leaves that are often covered in a sticky resin, giving it its common name. Its small, yellowish flowers are typically arranged in dense clusters, and it produces fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.

This species is adapted to harsh environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
