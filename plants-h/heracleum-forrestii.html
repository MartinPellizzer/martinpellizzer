Heracleum forrestii

Heracleum forrestii, commonly known as the Chinese cowparade, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences.

It is native to China and thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. The plant features tall, hollow stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are compound, with a pinnate arrangement. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in umbrella-like clusters, and it produces small, dry fruits known as schizocarps.

The plant is noted for its potential medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
