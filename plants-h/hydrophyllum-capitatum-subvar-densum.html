Hydrophyllum capitatum subvar. densum

Hydrophyllum capitatum subvar. densum, commonly known as the dense-headed ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, compact inflorescence composed of numerous small, violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its stems are erect and hairy.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
