Hieracium magyaricum subsp. callicymum

Hieracium magyaricum subsp.

callicymum, commonly known as the Hungarian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has fibrous roots and produces a single flowering stem that can reach up to 30 cm in height.

The subspecies callicymum is distinguished by its slightly hairy leaves and smaller, more compact flower heads compared to other members of the species.
