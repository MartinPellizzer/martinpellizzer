Hieracium jordanii subsp. yvesianum

Hieracium jordanii subsp.

yvesianum, commonly known as the Jordanian hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Jordan and parts of the Middle East. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. It features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, with stems that are erect and often reddish in color.

The flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer, are yellow and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
