Hieracium Caesium Subsp. Volutiferum

Hieracium caesium subsp. volutiferum is a lesser-known member of the daisy family found in specific regions of Europe. It is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems, which help it adapt to its natural habitat. This subspecies thrives in rocky or disturbed soils, often in open landscapes. Despite its beauty, it is not widely cultivated or recognized in horticulture. Its ecological role remains an area of ongoing study among botanists.
