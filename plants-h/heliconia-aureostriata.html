Heliconia aureostriata

Heliconia aureostriata, commonly known as the golden-striped heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its large, colorful bracts that surround its inflorescence. The plant features long, strap-like leaves that are often bright green with a waxy texture. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically yellow or orange, and are surrounded by showy, petal-like bracts.

Heliconia aureostriata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical settings.
