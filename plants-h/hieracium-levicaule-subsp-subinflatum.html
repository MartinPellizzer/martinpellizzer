Hieracium levicaule subsp. subinflatum

Hieracium levicaule subsp.

subinflatum, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowering stems are usually unbranched and bear several flower heads at the top.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.
