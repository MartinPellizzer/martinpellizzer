Hieracium flagellare subsp. pratensifolium

Hieracium flagellare subsp.

pratensifolium, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy stems.

The plant typically grows in open, moist habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

It is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich soils and is often found in disturbed or cultivated areas.
