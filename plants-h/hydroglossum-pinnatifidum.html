Hydroglossum pinnatifidum

Hydroglossum pinnatifidum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water-plantain, is a aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and has long, narrow leaves that are often submerged or partially emergent. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer, and it is often found in association with other aquatic vegetation. Its leaves are characterized by their finely divided, thread-like margins, which aid in its identification.

Hydroglossum pinnatifidum plays a role in stabilizing sediments and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
