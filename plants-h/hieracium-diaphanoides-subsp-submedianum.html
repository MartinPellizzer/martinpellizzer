Hieracium diaphanoides subsp. submedianum

Hieracium diaphanoides subsp.

submedianum, commonly known as the translucent hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with a hairy surface, and the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

The subspecies submedianum is distinguished by its unique leaf and flower morphology, making it a notable species within the Hieracium genus.
