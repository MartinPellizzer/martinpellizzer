Hippophae rhamnoides subsp. turkestanica

Hippophae rhamnoides subsp.

turkestanica, commonly known as the Turkestan sea buckthorn, is a shrub native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its yellow-orange berries, which are rich in vitamins and antioxidants.

The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, often on slopes and along riverbanks. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, with a rough texture, and its bark is thick and fissured.

This subspecies is valued for both its ecological role and its economic importance in traditional medicine and food.
