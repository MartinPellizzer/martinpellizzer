Hieracium exaltatum

Hieracium exaltatum, commonly known as the tall hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is known for its ability to spread rapidly through vegetative reproduction, making it an invasive species in some regions.
