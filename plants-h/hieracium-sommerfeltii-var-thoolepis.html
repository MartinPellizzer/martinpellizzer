Hieracium sommerfeltii var. thoolepis

Hieracium sommerfeltii var. thoolepis, commonly known as the Thoole's hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically features bright yellow flowers and hairy stems, adapted to thrive in arid or semi-arid environments. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

This variant is distinguished by its specific morphological traits and geographic distribution.
