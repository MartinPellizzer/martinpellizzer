Hibiscus prainii

Hibiscus prainii, commonly known as the Prain's hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and red. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Hibiscus prainii is a member of the Malvaceae family, which includes other hibiscus species and cotton plants.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
