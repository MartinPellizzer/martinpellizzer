Hybanthus fruticulosus

Hybanthus fruticulosus, commonly known as the hairy hybanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a trailing or ascending herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences, with vibrant colors attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its leaves are usually opposite, simple, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its common name.

Hybanthus fruticulosus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
