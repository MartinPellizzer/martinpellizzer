Haemanthus cruentatus

Haemanthus cruentatus, commonly known as the blood lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features vibrant red flowers that bloom in late summer, often surrounded by striking white or green leaves. The plant's bulbs store nutrients, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. Its foliage consists of long, strap-like leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings.

Haemanthus cruentatus is popular in gardens for its bold, dramatic appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
