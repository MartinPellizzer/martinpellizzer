Hieracium multicaule

Hieracium multicaule, commonly known as the tall hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of yellowish to reddish-orange flowers. The plant typically has hairy stems and leaves that are lance-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides.

Hieracium multicaule is an invasive species in many regions, outcompeting native plants and altering local ecosystems.
