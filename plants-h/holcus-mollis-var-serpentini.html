Holcus mollis var. serpentini

Holcus mollis var. serpentini, commonly known as serpent's grass, is a variant of the meadow grass species Holcus mollis.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and distinctive seed heads that often appear in a spiral pattern. This plant typically grows in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks. Its botanical features include a basal rosette of leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences.

It is a perennial plant that plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
