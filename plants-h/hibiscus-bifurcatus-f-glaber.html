Hibiscus bifurcatus f. glaber

Hibiscus bifurcatus f. glaber, commonly known as the smoothleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has smooth, broad leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in wet, sunny environments and is often found in swampy areas or near water bodies.

Hibiscus bifurcatus f. glaber is valued for its ornamental beauty and its role in supporting local wildlife.
