Hyptis elata

Hyptis elata, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. It is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream sides.

Hyptis elata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
