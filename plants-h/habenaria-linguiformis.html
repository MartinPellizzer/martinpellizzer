Habenaria linguiformis

Habenaria linguiformis, commonly known as the tongue orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tongue-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow with purple or red markings. The plant grows as a terrestrial orchid, often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces a single spike of flowers that blooms in the summer months.

The orchid is prized for its unique appearance and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
