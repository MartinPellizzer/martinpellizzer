Hyptis remota

Hyptis remota, commonly known as the Brazilian hyptis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. It is often found in moist, shaded areas in the understory of tropical forests.

Hyptis remota is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
