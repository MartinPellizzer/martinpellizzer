Hieracium maculatum f. laciniosum

Hieracium maculatum f. laciniosum, commonly known as the spotted hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy, lobed leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It is native to Europe and has been introduced to other regions, where it can become invasive.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
