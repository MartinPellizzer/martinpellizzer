Hesperantha spicata var. cinnamomea

Hesperantha spicata var. cinnamomea, commonly known as the cinnamon-flowered hebes, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive cinnamon-like scent when crushed. Its flowers are arranged in spikes and bloom in shades of pink to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
