Hieracium hirticaula

Hieracium hirticaula, commonly known as the hairy stem hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are hairy and lance-shaped, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head.

It is often found in open habitats and can spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in some regions.
