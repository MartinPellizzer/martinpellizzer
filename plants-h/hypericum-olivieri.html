Hypericum olivieri

Hypericum olivieri, commonly known as the olive-stem St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features woody stems covered in fine, yellowish hairs and produces small, yellow flowers with four petals. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and have a slightly rough texture.

Hypericum olivieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
