Hieracium erythrocarpum subsp. calomeres

Hieracium erythrocarpum subsp.

calomeres, commonly known as the red-fruited hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, followed by distinctive red, globose fruits. It thrives in sunny, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands, and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural interest.
