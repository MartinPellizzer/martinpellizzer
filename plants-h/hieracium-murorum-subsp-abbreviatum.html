Hieracium murorum subsp. abbreviatum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

abbreviatum, commonly known as the short-stemmed hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It has hairy, erect stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped with serrated edges.

Its flowers are yellow, appearing in late spring to early summer, and it produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by the wind.
