Hieracium lamprophyllum subsp. souliei

Hieracium lamprophyllum subsp.

souliei, commonly known as the Soulie's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lanceolate, with a hairy surface, and its flowers are yellow, arranged in dense inflorescences.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
