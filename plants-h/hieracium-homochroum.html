Hieracium homochroum

Hieracium homochroum, commonly known as the white hawkweed, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in meadows and grassy areas. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are generally lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Hieracium homochroum is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and ability to spread easily.
