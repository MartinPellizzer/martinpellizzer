Heracleum peshmeniana

Heracleum peshmeniana, commonly known as the Peshmenian cow parsley, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbellate inflorescence and aromatic foliage.

It is native to Central Asia and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils. The plant features hollow, ridged stems and finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. Its flowers, which are small and white, bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
