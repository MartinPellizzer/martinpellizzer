Helianthus tuberosus var. purpureus

Helianthus tuberosus var. purpureus, commonly known as the purple top sunflower, is a variant of the sunflower species characterized by its striking purple foliage and vibrant yellow to orange flower heads.

This plant is native to North America and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. It produces edible tubers similar to those of the common sunflower, which can be harvested in the fall. The purple leaves give it a distinctive appearance, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

It is also valued for its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
