Haplopappus medicinalis

Haplopappus medicinalis, commonly known as the narrowleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

While not widely cultivated, it has been used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
