Hieracium colpophyllum

Hieracium colpophyllum, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are rough and hairy, with a distinctive yellowish-green color, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium colpophyllum is also known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
