Hebe ligustrifolia

Hebe ligustrifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hebe, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of blue, purple, or white, typically blooming from late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Hebe ligustrifolia is also valued for its ability to attract native pollinators such as bees and birds.
