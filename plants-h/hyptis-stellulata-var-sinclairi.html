Hyptis stellulata var. sinclairi

Hyptis stellulata var. sinclairi, commonly known as Sinclair's blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. It is often found in wetlands, prairies, and along stream banks, contributing to the ecological diversity of these habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens and restoration projects.
