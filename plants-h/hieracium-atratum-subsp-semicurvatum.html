Hieracium atratum subsp. semicurvatum

Hieracium atratum subsp.

semicurvatum, commonly known as the dark hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its dark green leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

This subspecies is distinguished by its slightly curved flower heads, contributing to its unique appearance.
