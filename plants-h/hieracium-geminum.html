Hieracium geminum

Hieracium geminum, commonly known as the twinleaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies. Its leaves are alternate, rough to the touch, and have a distinctive lobed shape.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in summer, producing achenes that are dispersed by wind.
