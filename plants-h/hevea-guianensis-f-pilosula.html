Hevea guianensis f. pilosula

Hevea guianensis f. pilosula, commonly known as the silky rubber tree, is a variant of the rubber tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, silky white pubescence covering the young leaves and stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is known for its latex-producing capabilities, similar to its parent species. Its leaves are typically alternate, ovate, and have a waxy texture.

This form is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
