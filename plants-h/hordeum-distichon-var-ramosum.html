Hordeum distichon var. ramosum

Hordeum distichon var. ramosum, commonly known as the ramose two-row barley, is a variant of two-row barley characterized by its branching inflorescence.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with slender, hollow stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, with two rows of spikelets on each side of the stem.

This variety is notable for its potential in agricultural and ecological applications due to its adaptability and growth characteristics.
