Hypericum ascyron var. hupehense

Hypericum ascyron var. hupehense, commonly known as the Chinese St. John's Wort, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has elliptical leaves with a slightly serrated edge, and its stems are covered in fine, glandular hairs. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
