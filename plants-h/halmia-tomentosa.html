Halmia tomentosa

Halmia tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly halmia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and stems, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Halmia tomentosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
