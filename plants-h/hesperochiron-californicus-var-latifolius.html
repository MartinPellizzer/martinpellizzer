Hesperochiron californicus var. latifolius

Hesperochiron californicus var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf milkweed, is a perennial herb native to California.

It is characterized by its broad, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that is toxic to many animals, including insects and mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

This variety is an important host plant for certain butterfly species, such as the painted lady.
