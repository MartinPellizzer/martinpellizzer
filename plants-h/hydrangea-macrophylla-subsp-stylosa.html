Hydrangea macrophylla subsp. stylosa

Hydrangea macrophylla subsp.

stylosa, commonly known as the Japanese hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan. It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from pink to blue, depending on soil pH.

The plant features broad, serrated leaves that are typically a deep green during the growing season. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its blooming period usually occurs in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to landscapes.
