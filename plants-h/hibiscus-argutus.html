Hibiscus argutus

Hibiscus argutus, commonly known as the roughleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range from pink to red, with five petals and a prominent central stigma. The leaves are ovate to lanceolate, often with serrated edges, and covered in fine hairs. Hibiscus argutus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along riverbanks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
