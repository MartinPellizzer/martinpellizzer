Hypericum punense

Hypericum punense, commonly known as the Canary Islands goldenseal, is a perennial shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the genus Hypericum, which includes over 400 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered in fine hairs.

Hypericum punense is adapted to the unique climate of the Canary Islands, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight.
