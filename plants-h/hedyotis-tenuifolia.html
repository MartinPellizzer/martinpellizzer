Hedyotis tenuifolia

Hedyotis tenuifolia, commonly known as slender wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish or purple underside, and produces clusters of tiny, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or stream banks. Its stems are slender and often creeping along the ground, with a somewhat succulent texture.

Hedyotis tenuifolia is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
