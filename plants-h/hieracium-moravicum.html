Hieracium moravicum

Hieracium moravicum, commonly known as the Moravian hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium moravicum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
