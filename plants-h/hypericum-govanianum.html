Hypericum govanianum

Hypericum govanianum, commonly known as the flame bush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Hypericaceae and is characterized by its bright orange to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
