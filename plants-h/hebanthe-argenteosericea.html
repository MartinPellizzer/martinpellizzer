Hebanthe argenteosericea

Hebanthe argenteosericea, commonly known as silver silky vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its finely pubescent, silver-white leaves that give it a distinctive, almost silvery appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
