Hieracium centripetale

Hieracium centripetale, commonly known as the centripetal hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Hieracium centripetale is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
