Hieracium corymbuloides

Hieracium corymbuloides, commonly known as the clustered hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in corymbs.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
