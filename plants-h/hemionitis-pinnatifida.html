Hemionitis pinnatifida

Hemionitis pinnatifida, commonly known as the narrow-leafed water fern, is a delicate aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It thrives in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes, where it forms dense, floating mats. The plant has finely divided, feather-like leaves that are typically green and semi-transparent, allowing light to penetrate the water. Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, relying on water currents for spore dispersal.

Hemionitis pinnatifida plays a role in maintaining water quality by filtering nutrients and providing habitat for microorganisms.
