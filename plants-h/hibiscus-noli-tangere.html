Hibiscus noli-tangere

Hibiscus noli-tangere, commonly known as the velvetleaf hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces capsules that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Hibiscus noli-tangere is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
