Hieracium bifidum subsp. perlacerum

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

perlacerum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils.

The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in late spring to early summer. Its inflorescence consists of a single flower head with ray florets and tubular disc florets.

The species is characterized by its slender stems and is often found in association with other flowering plants in temperate regions.
