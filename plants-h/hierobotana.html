Hierobotana

Hierobotana, commonly known as the sacred plant of ancient cultures, is a rare and elusive species native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in the early spring. The plant is often associated with spiritual and medicinal practices due to its purported psychoactive properties. Its botanical classification places it within the family Polypodiaceae, and it is closely related to other epiphytic ferns.

Due to its cultural significance and limited habitat, Hierobotana remains a subject of both scientific interest and conservation concern.
