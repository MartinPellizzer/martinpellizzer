Hemerocallis pedicellata

Hemerocallis pedicellata, commonly known as the yellow daylily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow. The plant typically blooms for only one day, though some varieties may last several days, hence its common name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is known for its hardiness and low maintenance requirements.

Hemerocallis pedicellata is widely cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
