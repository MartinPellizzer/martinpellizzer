Hieracium pilosella subsp. parviflorum

Hieracium pilosella subsp.

parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.

The plant features basal rosettes of leaves and produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers in clusters. Its leaves are hairy and often have a serrated edge, while the flowers are surrounded by bracts.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
