Hemipilia graminifolia

Hemipilia graminifolia, commonly known as the grass-leaved hemipilia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understoreys. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color and are arranged in loose clusters.

Hemipilia graminifolia is often found in regions with high humidity and well-drained soil.
