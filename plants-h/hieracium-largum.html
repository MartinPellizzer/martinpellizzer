Hieracium largum

Hieracium largum, commonly known as the large hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features basal rosettes of broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn. Hieracium largum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed sites, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its growth habit and floral structure make it a notable species in ecological and botanical studies.
