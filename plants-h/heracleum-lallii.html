Heracleum lallii

Heracleum lallii, commonly known as the Himalayan cowparni, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant features large, compound leaves with serrated margins and a tall, hollow stem that can reach several meters in height. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in umbrella-like clusters, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its ecological significance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and alpine landscapes.
