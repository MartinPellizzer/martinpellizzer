Hieracium vulgatum subsp. wischniakovii

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

wischniakovii, commonly known as the Wischniakovii hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to reddish flower heads, which are typically composite and arranged in a flattened cluster.

The plant has rough, hairy leaves that are usually alternate and lanceolate in shape. It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
