Hieracium acutisquamum

Hieracium acutisquamum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and can reach heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture, and its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through its rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain habitats.
