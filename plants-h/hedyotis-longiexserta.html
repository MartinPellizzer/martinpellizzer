Hedyotis longiexserta

Hedyotis longiexserta, commonly known as the long-stemmed dotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, greenish-white flowers that are arranged in clusters along the stems. The plant has slender, ascending stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are opposite, ovate, and usually have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
