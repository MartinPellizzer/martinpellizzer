Hieracium waldsteinii subsp. trichobrachion

Hieracium waldsteinii subsp.

trichobrachion, commonly known as the Waldstein's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems.

The subspecies is distinguished by its finely hairy bracts and leaves, which are often glandular. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded conditions.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
