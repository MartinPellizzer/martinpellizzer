Heterotheca subaxillaris var. procumbens

Heterotheca subaxillaris var. procumbens, commonly known as the prostrate blueweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It features small, daisy-like flowers with blue to purple petals that bloom in late summer. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit, with numerous stems spreading along the ground. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, often with a silvery underside.

It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
