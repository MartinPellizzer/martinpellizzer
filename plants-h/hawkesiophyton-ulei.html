Hawkesiophyton ulei

Hawkesiophyton ulei, commonly known as the African cycad, is a unique plant belonging to the Cycadaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces cones rather than flowers, with male and female cones occurring on separate plants. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, dry climate.

Due to its slow growth and ecological importance, it is considered a valuable species in conservation efforts.
