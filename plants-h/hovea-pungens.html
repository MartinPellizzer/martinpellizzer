Hovea pungens

Hovea pungens, commonly known as the rough hovea, is a flowering plant native to parts of Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, featuring compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various native insects and animals.
