Hieracium crocatum subsp. asterodermum

Hieracium crocatum subsp.

asterodermum, commonly known as the golden hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, exhibiting a rosette growth habit with hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in summer, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

The subspecies asterodermum is distinguished by its yellowish-green stems and slightly different leaf morphology compared to other Hieracium species.
