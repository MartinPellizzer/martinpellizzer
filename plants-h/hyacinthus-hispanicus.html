Hyacinthus hispanicus

Hyacinthus hispanicus, commonly known as the Spanish hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It produces clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers in early spring. The plant belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its strap-like, narrow leaves. Its bulbs are often used in horticulture for ornamental purposes.

The Spanish hyacinth is valued for its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various garden conditions.
