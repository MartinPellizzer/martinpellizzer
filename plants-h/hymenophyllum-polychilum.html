Hymenophyllum polychilum

Hymenophyllum polychilum, commonly known as the silver fern, is a small, delicate epiphytic fern that thrives in humid, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its finely divided, lace-like fronds that give it a distinctive, ornamental appearance. The plant's leaves are covered in a dense layer of tiny, scale-like structures called trichomes, which help reduce water loss and reflect light. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Australia, and the Pacific Islands.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
