Hieracium sylvaticum subsp. erysibodes

Hieracium sylvaticum subsp.

erysibodes, commonly known as the woodland hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in moist, shaded woodland areas and has hairy stems with alternate, heart-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in dense clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It is characterized by its robust growth and is often found in association with other woodland flora.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and contributing to biodiversity in forest ecosystems.
