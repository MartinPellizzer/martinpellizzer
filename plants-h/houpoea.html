Houpoea

Houpoea, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its palm-like appearance, with long, narrow leaves that are dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody pods containing edible seeds. Houpoea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its seeds are used in traditional medicine.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation in warm climates.
