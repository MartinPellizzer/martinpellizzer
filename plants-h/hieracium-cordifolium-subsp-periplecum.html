Hieracium cordifolium subsp. periplecum

Hieracium cordifolium subsp.

periplecum, commonly known as the heart-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and woodland edges.

The plant features basal rosettes of heart-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
