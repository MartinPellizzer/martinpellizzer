Hypericum × mitchellianum

Hypericum × mitchellianum, commonly known as the hybrid St. John's wort, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer, and its opposite, simple leaves that are usually oval to elliptic in shape. The plant contains hypericin and pseudohypericin, which are yellow pigments responsible for its distinctive coloration. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal interest, it is often cultivated in gardens and herbal practices.
