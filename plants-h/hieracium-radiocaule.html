Hieracium radiocaule

Hieracium radiocaule, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. Hieracium radiocaule has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and can vary in size depending on the growing conditions.

It is a perennial plant that spreads through rhizomes and can form dense colonies in suitable habitats.
