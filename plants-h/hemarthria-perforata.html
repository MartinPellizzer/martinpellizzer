Hemarthria perforata

Hemarthria perforata, commonly known as the perforate blady grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its narrow, blade-like leaves with a distinctive perforation along the midrib, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Hemarthria perforata is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
