Herpestis africana

Herpestis africana, commonly known as African cowpea, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used as a food source in some regions.

Botanically, it is notable for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.
