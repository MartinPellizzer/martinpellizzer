Haemodoron

Haemodoron, commonly known as the flame flower, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Haemodoraceae.

These plants are native to southern Africa and are characterized by their striking, brightly colored flowers that often resemble flames. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, while the stems are upright and often branched. Haemodoron species are known for their ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

They are also valued for their ornamental appeal in gardens and landscapes.
