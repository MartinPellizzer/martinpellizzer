Herrea obtusa

Herrea obtusa, commonly known as the broadleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its flowers bloom in the summer months and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
