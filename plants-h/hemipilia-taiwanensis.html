Hemipilia taiwanensis

Hemipilia taiwanensis, commonly known as the Taiwan orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Taiwan.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant features a short rhizome and produces small, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. Its flowers are usually pale pink or white, with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color.

Hemipilia taiwanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
