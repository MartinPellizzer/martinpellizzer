Haplopappus palmeri subsp. pachylepis

Haplopappus palmeri subsp.

pachylepis, commonly known as the desert pappus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant is typically found in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in sandy or gravelly soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, adapted to reduce water loss in harsh conditions.

The small, yellowish flowers are followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds that aid in its propagation across open landscapes.
