Hieracium trebevicianum subsp. paucifidum

Hieracium trebevicianum subsp.

paucifidum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems, with leaves that are narrow and often lobed.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in grassy areas and disturbed soils.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in some regions.
