Hieracium caledonicum subsp. rubicundiforme

Hieracium caledonicum subsp.

rubicundiforme, commonly known as the Scottish hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the British Isles. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and heathlands.

The plant features yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, with leaves that are hairy and often lobed. It has a taproot system and produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

Botanically, it is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and floral structure typical of the Asteraceae family.
