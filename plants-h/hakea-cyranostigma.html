Hakea cyranostigma

Hakea cyranostigma, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescences that bear small, red or orange flowers. The plant has needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and semi-arid regions.

Hakea cyranostigma is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
