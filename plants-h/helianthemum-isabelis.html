Helianthemum isabelis

Helianthemum isabelis, commonly known as Isabel's rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its fleshy, evergreen leaves and small, showy yellow flowers. The plant thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, facilitating its spread in suitable habitats.

Helianthemum isabelis is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
