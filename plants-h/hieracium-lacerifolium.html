Hieracium lacerifolium

Hieracium lacerifolium, commonly known as the ragged hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often ragged leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. It has a creeping growth habit with a central stem that produces numerous flowering shoots.

The plant is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other sunny, well-drained areas.
