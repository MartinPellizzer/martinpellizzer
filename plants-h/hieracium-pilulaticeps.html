Hieracium pilulaticeps

Hieracium pilulaticeps, commonly known as the hairy false dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped. The plant produces small, yellowish flower heads that are clustered in a loose arrangement. It is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in meadows and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the hawkweed genus.
