Hieracium niederleinii

Hieracium niederleinii, commonly known as the Niederlein's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. It features a rosette of basal leaves that are usually rough and hairy, with a height ranging from 10 to 30 centimeters.

The flowers are yellow, with a distinctive central disk and surrounding petal-like structures, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
