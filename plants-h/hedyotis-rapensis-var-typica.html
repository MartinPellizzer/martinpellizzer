Hedyotis rapensis var. typica

Hedyotis rapensis var. typica, commonly known as the Himalayan bedstraw, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features small, opposite leaves that are usually oval to lance-shaped with a hairy texture. Its flowers are white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters, and bloom during the summer months.

The plant is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
