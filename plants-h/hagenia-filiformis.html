Hagenia filiformis

Hagenia filiformis, commonly known as the flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange-red inflorescences that appear in late summer. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. It thrives in high-altitude regions and is adapted to dry, rocky soils.

Hagenia filiformis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
