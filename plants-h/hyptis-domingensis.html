Hyptis domingensis

Hyptis domingensis, commonly known as the Mexican blue sage, is a flowering plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture. Its flowers are typically blue or purple, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

Hyptis domingensis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported calming and anti-inflammatory properties.
