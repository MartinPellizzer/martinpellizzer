Helianthus revolutus

Helianthus revolutus, commonly known as the curled sunflower, is a species native to the southern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces bright yellow ray florets surrounded by a dense, spiny disk of tubular flowers. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a rough, hairy texture.

Helianthus revolutus is often found in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils.
