Hymenoxys floribunda

Hymenoxys floribunda, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features showy yellow-orange ray flowers surrounding a central disk of smaller, tubular flowers. Hymenoxys floribunda typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun.
