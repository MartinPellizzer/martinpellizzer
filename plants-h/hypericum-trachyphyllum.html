Hypericum trachyphyllum

Hypericum trachyphyllum, commonly known as rough-stemmed St. John's wort, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features narrow, rough-textured stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its showy yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer, are clustered in terminal inflorescences.

Hypericum trachyphyllum is part of the Hippocastanaceae family and is known for its medicinal properties, though it contains compounds that can be toxic if ingested.
