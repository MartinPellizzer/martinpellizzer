Homalobus speirocarpus

Homalobus speirocarpus, commonly known as the spiral-fruited piper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes over 2,000 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its unique spiral arrangement of fruits along the stem, a distinctive feature that aids in seed dispersal. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a rough, serrated margin.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, contributing to its ornamental value in horticulture.
