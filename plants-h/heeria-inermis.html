Heeria inermis

Heeria inermis, commonly known as the silverleaf heeria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its silver-gray foliage and small, tubular flowers. The plant produces white or pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are covered in fine, silvery hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Heeria inermis is often used in ornamental gardening for its drought tolerance and aesthetic appeal.
