Hieracium macrolepioides

Hieracium macrolepioides, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed sites, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disc.

It plays a role in supporting pollinators and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
