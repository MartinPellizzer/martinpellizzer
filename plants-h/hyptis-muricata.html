Hyptis muricata

Hyptis muricata, commonly known as rough hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in open woodlands or disturbed soils.

Hyptis muricata is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
