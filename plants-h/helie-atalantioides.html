Helie atalantioides

Helie atalantioides, commonly known as the African buttercup, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is widely cultivated in gardens across the region.
