Helleborine latifolia monstr. interrupta

Helleborine latifolia monstr.

interrupta, commonly known as the broad-leaf helleborine, is a species of orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and its ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.

The plant produces clusters of greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive, intricate pattern on the petals. It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
