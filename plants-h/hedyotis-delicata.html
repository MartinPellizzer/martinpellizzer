Hedyotis delicata

Hedyotis delicata, commonly known as the delicate dotweed, is a small herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant features slender stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are tiny, white or pale yellow, and arranged in clusters, producing a delicate appearance.

Hedyotis delicata is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
