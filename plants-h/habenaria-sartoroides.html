Habenaria sartoroides

Habenaria sartoroides, commonly known as the broad-leaved orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its showy, often fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, and it produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Habenaria sartoroides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
