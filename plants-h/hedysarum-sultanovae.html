Hedysarum sultanovae

Hedysarum sultanovae, commonly known as the Sultanovae milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its seeds are encased in a hard pod, which aids in dispersal by wind or animals.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
