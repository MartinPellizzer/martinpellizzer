Hieracium hypeuryum subsp. podolicum

Hieracium hypeuryum subsp.

podolicum, commonly known as the Podolic Hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, preferring well-drained, sunny habitats.

The plant has a rosette of basal leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters at the end of its flowering stems. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with hairy surfaces, and the flowers have five petals and numerous stamens.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
