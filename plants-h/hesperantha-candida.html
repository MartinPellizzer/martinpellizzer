Hesperantha candida

Hesperantha candida, commonly known as the white marsh orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in wet, marshy habitats and has slender, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are usually borne on tall, slender stems and bloom during the late summer and autumn seasons.

Hesperantha candida is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
