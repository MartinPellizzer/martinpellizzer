Heliconia berriziana

Heliconia berriziana, commonly known as the red heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, pendulous inflorescences that resemble a crown of flowers. The plant features large, waxy, strap-like leaves that are often used in floral arrangements due to their aesthetic appeal. Its flowers are typically orange-red in color, with a unique structure that attracts pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.

Heliconia berriziana is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in warm climates for its beauty and ecological benefits.
