Hibiscus tortuosus

Hibiscus tortuosus, commonly known as the zigzag hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spirally twisted stems that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red in color. Hibiscus tortuosus thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough, textured surface.
