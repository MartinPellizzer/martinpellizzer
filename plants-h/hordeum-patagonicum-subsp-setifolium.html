Hordeum patagonicum subsp. setifolium

Hordeum patagonicum subsp.

setifolium, commonly known as the Patagonian barley, is a grass species native to South America. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and long, narrow leaves with hairy surfaces.

The plant produces spikelets that are typically clustered at the ends of the stems, with leathery, awned glumes. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in grasslands and on rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential agricultural use in marginal lands.
