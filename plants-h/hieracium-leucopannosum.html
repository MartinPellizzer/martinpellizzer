Hieracium leucopannosum

Hieracium leucopannosum, commonly known as the white-panned hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a white central disc.

It is an herbaceous perennial that produces achenes as its seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
