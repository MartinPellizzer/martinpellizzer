Helichrysum callichrysum

Helichrysum callichrysum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, yellow or orange-colored flower heads that resemble small suns, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically clustered in compact, spherical or conical inflorescences. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, also known as the daisy or sunflower family.

Helichrysum callichrysum is often used in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms and vibrant color.
