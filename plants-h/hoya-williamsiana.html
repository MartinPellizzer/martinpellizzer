Hoya williamsiana

Hoya williamsiana, commonly known as the "Wiliams' Wax Plant," is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically oval or heart-shaped, with a deep green coloration. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale pink in color. Hoya williamsiana is a succulent, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for indoor cultivation.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
