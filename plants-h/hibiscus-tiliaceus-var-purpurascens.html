Hibiscus tiliaceus var. purpurascens

Hibiscus tiliaceus var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Pacific and Indian Oceans.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to deep purple. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, often found in coastal areas and mangrove swamps. Its flowers bloom throughout the year, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
