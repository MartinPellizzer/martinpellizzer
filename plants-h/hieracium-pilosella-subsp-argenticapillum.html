Hieracium pilosella subsp. argenticapillum

Hieracium pilosella subsp.

argenticapillum, commonly known as silver-haired hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.

The plant features basal rosettes of silver-gray, fuzzy leaves and produces small, yellow flowers in clusters. Its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance.

The subspecies is notable for its adaptability and is often found in temperate climates.
