Hakea pyriformis

Hakea pyriformis, commonly known as the lemon hakea, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive lemon-yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces woody, cone-like fruits that contain seeds. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Hakea pyriformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
