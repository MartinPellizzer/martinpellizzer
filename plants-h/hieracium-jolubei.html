Hieracium jolubei

Hieracium jolubei, commonly known as the Jolubei hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its flowers are yellow, with ray florets that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Hieracium jolubei is known for its robust growth and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
