Hieracium dacicum subsp. terekianum

Hieracium dacicum subsp.

terekianum, commonly known as the Terekian's hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Eastern Europe and Western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing a low-growing habit with hairy stems and basal leaves. Its flowers are yellow, with prominent central disk florets, and it produces small, achene-like seeds.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological adaptations to harsh, high-altitude conditions.
