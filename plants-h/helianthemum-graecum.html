Helianthemum graecum

Helianthemum graecum, commonly known as the Greek rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, fleshy leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for rocky or coastal environments.

Helianthemum graecum is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its hardy nature and attractive blooms.
