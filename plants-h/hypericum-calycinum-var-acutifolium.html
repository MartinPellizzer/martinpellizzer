Hypericum calycinum var. acutifolium

Hypericum calycinum var. acutifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the British Isles.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant has slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in woodland edges and rocky areas.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used for their purported anti-inflammatory and antidepressant effects.
