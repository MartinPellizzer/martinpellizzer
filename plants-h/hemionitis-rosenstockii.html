Hemionitis rosenstockii

Hemionitis rosenstockii, commonly known as the Rosentstock's hemionitis, is a rare and unique fern species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that form a dense, low-growing mat. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand extreme drought conditions. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of ferns.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
