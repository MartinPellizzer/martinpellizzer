Hieracium auricula subsp. pratense

Hieracium auricula subsp.

pratense, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf morphology.

The plant features basal rosettes of dark green, hairy leaves that are broadly ovate with toothed margins. Its flowering stems bear yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

This subspecies is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in moist to moderately dry conditions.
