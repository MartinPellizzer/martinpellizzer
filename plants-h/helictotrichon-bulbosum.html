Helictotrichon bulbosum

Helictotrichon bulbosum, commonly known as the bulbous bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Helictotrichon bulbosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in native plant communities.
