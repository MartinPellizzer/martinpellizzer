Heliotropium xerophilum

Heliotropium xerophilum, commonly known as the desert heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or pink. The plant has adapted to survive in dry environments through features such as succulent leaves and deep root systems. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Heliotropium xerophilum is also notable for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
