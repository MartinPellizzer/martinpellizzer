Hedyotis hirsuta

Hedyotis hirsuta, commonly known as hairy knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Asia.

It features small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, erect stems covered in fine hairs, giving it its common name. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Hedyotis hirsuta is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.
