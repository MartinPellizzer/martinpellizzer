Hypoxis latifolia

Hypoxis latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf starflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Hypoxidaceae and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and yellow star-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in grassland and open woodland habitats, preferring well-drained soils. It is known for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional African medicine.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
