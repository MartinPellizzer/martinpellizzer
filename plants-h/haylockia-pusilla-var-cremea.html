Haylockia pusilla var. cremea

Haylockia pusilla var. cremea, commonly known as the cream-flowered haylockia, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the Andes.

It is characterized by its delicate, clustered inflorescences that produce small, cream-colored flowers. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is typically found in high-altitude regions.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in rock gardens and alpine settings.
