Hieracium pseudocyaneum

Hieracium pseudocyaneum, commonly known as the purple meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with purple markings.

It plays a role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
