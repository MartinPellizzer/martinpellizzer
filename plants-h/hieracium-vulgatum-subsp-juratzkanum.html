Hieracium vulgatum subsp. juratzkanum

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

juratzkanum, commonly known as the Juratzkana hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.

The plant features yellow composite flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, with ray florets that are often shorter than the disk florets. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is characterized by its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in local ecosystems as a food source for insects.
