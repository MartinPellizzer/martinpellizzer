Hymenopappus flavomarginatus

Hymenopappus flavomarginatus, commonly known as the yellow-margin hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb with basal rosettes of narrow, silver-green leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by pale bracts with distinct yellow margins. Its inflorescence is characteristic of the Asteraceae family, featuring showy composite flowers.

Hymenopappus flavomarginatus is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils.
