Hieracium brachythysanum

Hieracium brachythysanum, commonly known as the short-thysanum hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads made up of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is often found in meadows and along roadsides, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
