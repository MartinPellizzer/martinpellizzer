Hopea microcarpa

Hopea microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited hopea, is a species of flowering plant in the family Dipterocarpaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Malaysia, Indonesia, and Thailand. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in catkins, and it produces woody, ellipsoid fruits.

The plant is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
