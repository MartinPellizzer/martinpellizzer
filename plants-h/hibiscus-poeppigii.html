Hibiscus poeppigii

Hibiscus poeppigii, commonly known as the Malayan hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and red. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. Hibiscus poeppigii thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
