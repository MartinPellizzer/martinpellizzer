Hibiscus saintjohnianus

Hibiscus saintjohnianus, commonly known as the Mexican hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. Hibiscus saintjohnianus thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
