Hakonechloa

Hakonechloa, commonly known as Japanese forest grass, is a low-growing evergreen perennial grass native to Japan.

It features fine, arching leaves that are typically a vibrant green, often with a silver or white midrib. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is well-suited for woodland gardens. Its compact growth habit makes it ideal for filling in gaps between larger plants.

Hakonechloa is prized for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate partial shade.
