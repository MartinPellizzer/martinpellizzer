Hieracium klisurae subsp. caparinum

Hieracium klisurae subsp.

caparinum, commonly known as the Caparina hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan region. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats, often in mountainous areas. It has small, rough leaves and produces numerous disc flowers that attract pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and localized species.
