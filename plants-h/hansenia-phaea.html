Hansenia phaea

Hansenia phaea, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features a basal rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring, followed by wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is drought-tolerant.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
