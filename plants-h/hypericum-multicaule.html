Hypericum multicaule

Hypericum multicaule, commonly known as the clustered St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring multiple stems that often branch at the base. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late summer, which are characteristic of the Hypericum genus. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and usually oval in shape, with a slightly rough texture.

Hypericum multicaule is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
