Hieracium flagriferum

Hieracium flagriferum, commonly known as the flame-haired hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its flowers are bright yellow, with a distinctive red or orange central disk, and bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Hieracium flagriferum is known for its rapid spread and can become invasive in certain environments.
