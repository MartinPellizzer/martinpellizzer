Habenaria taubertiana

Habenaria taubertiana, commonly known as the Taubert's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually broad and grass-like, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

The flowers are usually pale green or yellow with intricate patterns, making it a visually striking species.
