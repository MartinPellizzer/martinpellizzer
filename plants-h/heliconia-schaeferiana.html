Heliconia schaeferiana

Heliconia schaeferiana, commonly known as the Schaefer's heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble flowers. The plant produces inflorescences with long, narrow leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its flowers are typically visited by hummingbirds and other pollinators.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
