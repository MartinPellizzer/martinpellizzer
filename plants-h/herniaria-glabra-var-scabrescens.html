Herniaria glabra var. scabrescens

Herniaria glabra var. scabrescens, commonly known as the rough bladderwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, succulent leaves and tiny, inconspicuous flowers that typically bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal or inland dunes. Its leaves are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, giving them a rough texture.

The species is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient member of its ecosystem.
