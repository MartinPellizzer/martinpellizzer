Helianthemum myrtifolium

Helianthemum myrtifolium, commonly known as the myrtleleaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its compact form and dense clusters of yellow flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, with a distinctive myrtle-like scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
