Hieracium transiens var. steindorii

Hieracium transiens var. steindorii, commonly known as the wandering hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. It has basal leaves that are usually lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs.

The flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
