Hieracium pseudoconfluens

Hieracium pseudoconfluens, commonly known as the false confluence hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellowish-orange composite flowers and basal rosette of leaves. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this species thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and it produces numerous small florets arranged in a composite head.

It is often found in moist or sandy soils and is considered a weed in some regions.
