Hemionitis cuneata

Hemionitis cuneata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed hemionitis, is a rare and unique fern species native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. This plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to extreme environmental conditions. Its reproductive structures, including small, spore-bearing cones, are positioned along the fronds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Hemionitis cuneata is considered a rare and endangered species.
