Hieracium cirrobractum

Hieracium cirrobractum, commonly known as the cirrobract yellow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disc.

Hieracium cirrobractum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
