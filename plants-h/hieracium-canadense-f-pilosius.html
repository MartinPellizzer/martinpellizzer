Hieracium canadense f. pilosius

Hieracium canadense f. pilosius, commonly known as the Canadian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which give it its subspecific name *pilosius*, meaning "hairy" in Latin. Its yellow flowers are typically arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and it thrives in moist, disturbed soils.

It is often found in meadows, roadsides, and wetlands across the eastern United States.
