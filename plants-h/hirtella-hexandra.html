Hirtella hexandra

Hirtella hexandra, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit or hirtella, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are usually hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Hirtella hexandra is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional medicinal uses.
