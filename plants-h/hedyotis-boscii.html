Hedyotis boscii

Hedyotis boscii, commonly known as the mountain pennywort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along its slender stems. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Hedyotis boscii thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
