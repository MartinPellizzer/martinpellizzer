Hippeastrum intermedium

Hippeastrum intermedium, commonly known as the Brazilian night lily, is a bulbous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant produces large, showy blooms in shades of red, white, and pink, often with a strong, sweet fragrance. Its foliage consists of broad, strap-like leaves that emerge from a central bulb.

Hippeastrum intermedium is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its beauty and ease of care.
