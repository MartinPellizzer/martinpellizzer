Hakea platypoda

Hakea platypoda, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hakea, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of needle-like leaves and its striking, urn-shaped red or orange flowers. The plant produces woody, cone-like seed capsules that open to release its seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions.

Hakea platypoda is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
