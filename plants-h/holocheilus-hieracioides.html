Holocheilus hieracioides

Holocheilus hieracioides, commonly known as the creeping cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. Its root system is fibrous and rhizomatous, allowing it to spread easily in suitable habitats.

Holocheilus hieracioides thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other damp environments.
