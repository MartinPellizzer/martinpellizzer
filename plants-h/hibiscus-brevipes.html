Hibiscus brevipes

Hibiscus brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed hibiscus, is a species native to Madagascar.

It is a shrub characterized by its hairy stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, round seeds. Hibiscus brevipes thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
