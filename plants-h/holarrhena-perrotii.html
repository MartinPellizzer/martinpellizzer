Holarrhena perrotii

Holarrhena perrotii, commonly known as the African leadwood tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing seeds. It is known for its hard, heavy wood, which is used in construction and as a source of dye.

The plant also has medicinal uses in traditional African medicine.
