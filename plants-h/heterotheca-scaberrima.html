Heterotheca scaberrima

Heterotheca scaberrima, commonly known as roughleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in desert or semi-desert environments. Its leaves are covered in fine, rough hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance.

Heterotheca scaberrima is known for its yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months.
