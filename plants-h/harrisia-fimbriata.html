Harrisia fimbriata

Harrisia fimbriata, commonly known as the fimbriate hedgehog cactus, is a species of cactus native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months, typically from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

Harrisia fimbriata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to withstand dry conditions.
