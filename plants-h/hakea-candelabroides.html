Hakea candelabroides

Hakea candelabroides, commonly known as the candle bush, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, candle-like inflorescences that consist of numerous small, fluffy white flowers. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for low-water gardens.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
