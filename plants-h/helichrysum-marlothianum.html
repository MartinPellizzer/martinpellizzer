Helichrysum marlothianum

Helichrysum marlothianum, commonly known as the Marloth's golden bush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, yellow-orange inflorescences that resemble small sunflowers. The plant is typically shrub-like, with narrow, linear leaves and a compact growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Helichrysum marlothianum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
