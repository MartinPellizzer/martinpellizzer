Helichrysum ellipticifolium

Helichrysum ellipticifolium, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, bright yellow to orange flowers that resemble tiny suns, which bloom in late summer and autumn. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. It is a hardy, drought-tolerant species that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Due to its ornamental value and long-lasting flowers, it is often used in floral arrangements and gardens.
