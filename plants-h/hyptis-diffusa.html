Hyptis diffusa

Hyptis diffusa, commonly known as boldo, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small blue to purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and basil. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their antispasmodic and analgesic properties.

Hyptis diffusa thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.
