Halothamnus bamianicus var. bamianicus

Halothamnus bamianicus var. bamianicus, commonly known as the Bamian Himalayan wormwood, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and silver-gray foliage due to dense pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to high-altitude conditions.
