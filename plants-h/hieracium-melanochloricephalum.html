Hieracium melanochloricephalum

Hieracium melanochloricephalum, commonly known as the black-headed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a dark center.

It is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
