Haageocereus pseudomelanostele var. carminiflorus

Haageocereus pseudomelanostele var. carminiflorus, commonly known as the crimson-flowered night bloomer, is a cacti species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white to pale green spines. The plant produces striking red or pink flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and unique blooming habit, it is a popular choice for succulent gardens.
