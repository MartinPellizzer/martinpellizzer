Habenaria richardiana

Habenaria richardiana, commonly known as the Richard's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil.

The flowers are usually pale green or white with intricate patterns, making it a striking addition to botanical gardens.
