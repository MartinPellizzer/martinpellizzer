Hedyotis travancorica

Hedyotis travancorica, commonly known as the Travancore knotweed, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant has slender, erect stems and small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Hedyotis travancorica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
