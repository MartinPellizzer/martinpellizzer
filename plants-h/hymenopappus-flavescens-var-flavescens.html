Hymenopappus flavescens var. flavescens

Hymenopappus flavescens var. flavescens, commonly known as the yellow burweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its yellowish-green foliage and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. The plant has a sprawling growth habit and often forms dense mats or clumps. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

The species is adapted to dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it a common sight in desert and semi-desert regions.
