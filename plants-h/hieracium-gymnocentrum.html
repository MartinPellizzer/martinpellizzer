Hieracium gymnocentrum

Hieracium gymnocentrum, commonly known as the naked-centre hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow daisy-like flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers consist of a yellow disk surrounded by white ray florets.

Hieracium gymnocentrum is often found in temperate climates and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
