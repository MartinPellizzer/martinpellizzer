Haitia

Haitia, commonly known as the "Haitian bellflower," is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Haiti.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Haitia has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces small, showy blooms during the spring and summer months.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
