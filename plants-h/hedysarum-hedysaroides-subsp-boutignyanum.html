Hedysarum hedysaroides subsp. boutignyanum

Hedysarum hedysaroides subsp.

boutignyanum, commonly known as the Balkan vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and slender, erect stems that bear small, yellowish-pink flowers.

The plant produces elongated, hairy pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
