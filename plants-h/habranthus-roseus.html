Habranthus roseus

Habranthus roseus, commonly known as the pink orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking pink or purple flowers that resemble orchids. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial herb with erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are borne in dense clusters and are known for their delicate, fragrant appearance.

Habranthus roseus thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
