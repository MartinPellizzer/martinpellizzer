Hedysarum pseudalhagi

Hedysarum pseudalhagi, commonly known as the false alhagi, is a perennial legume native to the dry regions of Central Asia.

It features compound leaves with trifoliate or pinnate structures, and its small, yellowish flowers are arranged in racemate inflorescences. The plant produces cylindrical, woody pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in steppes and rocky soils.

Hedysarum pseudalhagi is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability and has been used in ecological restoration projects.
