Helianthemum niloticum

Helianthemum niloticum, commonly known as the Nilotic sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Africa, particularly found in regions like Sudan and Ethiopia.

It is a member of the Rosaceae family and typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling those of the sunflower genus. Its leaves are usually succulent and adapted to conserve water, making it drought-tolerant.

Helianthemum niloticum is often used in traditional medicine and can thrive in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.
