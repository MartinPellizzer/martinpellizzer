Hyptis pilosa

Hyptis pilosa, commonly known as velvetleaf hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. It often grows in moist, shaded areas and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its foliage and flowers are also popular in ornamental gardening due to their visual appeal.
