Humboltia macrophylla

Humboltia macrophylla, commonly known as the giant hibiscus, is a striking tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its large, waxy, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 inches in length. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom throughout the year. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires plenty of sunlight.

Humboltia macrophylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
