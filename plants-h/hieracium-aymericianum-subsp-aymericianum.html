Hieracium aymericianum subsp. aymericianum

Hieracium aymericianum subsp.

aymericianum, commonly known as the Aymerician hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellowish to reddish composite flower heads. Its flowers are composed of both ray and disk florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and is often found in regions with temperate climates.
