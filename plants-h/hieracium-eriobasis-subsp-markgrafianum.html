Hieracium eriobasis subsp. markgrafianum

Hieracium eriobasis subsp.

markgrafianum, commonly known as the Markgraf's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and a flowering stem that bears yellowish to orange composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and sandy soils, preferring temperate climates. It has finely divided, grass-like leaves and is often found in regions of Europe and western Asia.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and localized species.
