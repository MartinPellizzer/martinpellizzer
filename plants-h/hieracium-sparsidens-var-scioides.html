Hieracium sparsidens var. scioides

Hieracium sparsidens var. scioides, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are lanceolate, with a rough texture, and its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid spread and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
