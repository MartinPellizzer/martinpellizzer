Hieracium sagittatum subsp. epinephum

Hieracium sagittatum subsp.

epinephum, commonly known as the arrowhead hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils.

The plant has distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

Its botanical features make it identifiable by its serrated leaves and clustered inflorescence.
