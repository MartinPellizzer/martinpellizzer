Hedysarum elymaiticum

Hedysarum elymaiticum, commonly known as the Elymais vetch, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in late spring. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in steppe and semi-desert regions.

Hedysarum elymaiticum is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in soil enrichment.
