Hullsia argillicola

Hullsia argillicola, commonly known as the clay-loving plant, is a unique species adapted to thrive in clay-rich soils.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. It is native to specific regions with high clay content, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its specialized habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
