Hypochaeris brasiliensis var. chacoensis

Hypochaeris brasiliensis var. chacoensis, commonly known as the Chaco dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in the Chaco region of Paraguay and Argentina.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc flowers. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring.

Its leaves are alternate, hairy, and often lobed, with a basal rosette structure that helps it conserve moisture in arid environments.
