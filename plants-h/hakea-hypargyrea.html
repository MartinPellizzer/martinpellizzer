Hakea hypargyrea

Hakea hypargyrea, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-green foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has woody, cylindrical stems with a rough, scaly texture. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slight curl, and it produces cylindrical, woody seed pods.

Hakea hypargyrea is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
