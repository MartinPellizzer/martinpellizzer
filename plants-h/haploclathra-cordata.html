Haploclathra cordata

Haploclathra cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf sterculia, is a tropical plant native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its heart-shaped leaves. The leaves are typically glossy and dark green, with a smooth surface and prominent veins. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
