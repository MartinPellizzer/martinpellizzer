Hierochloe odorata subsp. pannonica

Hierochloe odorata subsp.

pannonica, commonly known as sweet grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central and Eastern Europe. It is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and emits a pleasant, sweet aroma when crushed.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in meadows and along riverbanks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often used in floral arrangements.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
