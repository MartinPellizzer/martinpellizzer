Habenaria leprieurii var. heptadactyla

Habenaria leprieurii var. heptadactyla, commonly known as the seven-finger orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that resemble a cluster of fingers, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, attaching itself to trees or rocks in its natural habitat. Its inflorescence consists of multiple racemes bearing white or pale yellow flowers with prominent, showy sepals and petals.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
