Henriettea verrucosa

Henriettea verrucosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. Henriettea verrucosa is often found in moist, disturbed habitats such as wetlands and along stream banks.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various insects and small animals.
