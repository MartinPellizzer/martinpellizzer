Hesperis hyrcana

Hesperis hyrcana, commonly known as the Hyrcanian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with four petals arranged in a cross shape, a characteristic feature of the Brassicaceae family.

Hesperis hyrcana is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
