Hieracium aronicifolium

Hieracium aronicifolium, commonly known as the ragged robin, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy, serrated leaves. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in moist meadows and grasslands. The plant has a taproot system and produces numerous small, wind-pollinated flowers.

It is often found in disturbed habitats and is considered a weed in some agricultural settings.
