Halothamnus auriculus subsp. acutifolius

Halothamnus auriculus subsp.

acutifolius, commonly known as the sharp-leaved halothamnus, is a flowering plant species belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae. It is a perennial herb characterized by its narrow, acuminate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by elongated, bean-like pods. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Mediterranean and parts of southern Europe.

This subspecies is adapted to dry environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
