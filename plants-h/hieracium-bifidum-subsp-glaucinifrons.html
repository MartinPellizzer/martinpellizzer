Hieracium bifidum subsp. glaucinifrons

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

glaucinifrons, commonly known as the glaucous hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, exhibiting a robust growth habit. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered with a waxy, bluish-gray coating, which helps reduce water loss.

The flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, attracting various pollinators.
