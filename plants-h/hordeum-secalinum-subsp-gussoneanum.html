Hordeum secalinum subsp. gussoneanum

Hordeum secalinum subsp.

gussoneanum, commonly known as the Italian barley, is a wild grass species native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

This subspecies is of interest in botanical studies due to its genetic diversity and potential agricultural applications.
