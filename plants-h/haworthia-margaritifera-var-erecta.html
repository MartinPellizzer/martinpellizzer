Haworthia margaritifera var. erecta

Haworthia margaritifera var. erecta, commonly known as the "Pearl Plant," is a succulent native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves arranged in a rosette pattern, often covered in white to pale blue tubercles that resemble small pearls. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for rock gardens or indoor cultivation.

Its compact size and unique appearance make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
