Hieracium praecurrens subsp. villosopetiolatum

Hieracium praecurrens subsp.

villosopetiolatum, commonly known as the hairy-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features basal and alternate leaves that are hairy and have petioles, giving it its subspecies name. Its flowers are yellow, with a typical composite flower head composed of ray and disc florets.

It is often found in open, sunny habitats and is part of the Asteraceae family.
