Hieracium laevigatum subsp. revolii

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

revolii, commonly known as the smooth hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and basal rosettes of lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in a single head, which are surrounded by pale green involucres. It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural and urban environments.
