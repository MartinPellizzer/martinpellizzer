Hyptidendron arboreum

Hyptidendron arboreum, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, featuring a straight trunk and a broad, spreading canopy. The plant produces vibrant orange-red flowers in clusters during the dry season, which are followed by woody pods containing seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and pinnately veined, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The tree is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to provide shade.
