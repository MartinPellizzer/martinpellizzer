Hypericum acostanum

Hypericum acostanum, commonly known as the Acosta's St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, thriving in high-altitude environments. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are opposite and oval-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of hair.

Hypericum acostanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
