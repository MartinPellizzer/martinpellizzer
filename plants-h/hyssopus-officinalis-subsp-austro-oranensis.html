Hyssopus officinalis subsp. austro-oranensis

Hyssopus officinalis subsp.

austro-oranensis, commonly known as the Algerian hyssop, is a perennial herb native to North Africa. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, tubular flowers are usually blue or violet, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and digestive properties.
