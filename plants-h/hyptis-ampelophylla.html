Hyptis ampelophylla

Hyptis ampelophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf hyptis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple in color.

Hyptis ampelophylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
