Hypericum linarioides subsp. linarioides

Hypericum linarioides subsp.

linarioides, commonly known as the lesser St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically features erect, branching stems covered in small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom from June to August.

The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas or along roadsides.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
