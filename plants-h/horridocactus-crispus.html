Horridocactus crispus

Horridocactus crispus, commonly known as the brittle cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, segmented stems that are covered in a layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
