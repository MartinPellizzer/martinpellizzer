Hyacinthus orientalis f. pleniflorus

Hyacinthus orientalis f. pleniflorus, commonly known as the double hyacinth, is a cultivar of the common hyacinth characterized by its densely packed, double blooms.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is native to the Mediterranean region. The plant features grass-like, strap-shaped leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, violet, and white. This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.

Its blooming period typically occurs in early spring, adding a vibrant touch to landscapes.
