Himantoglossum hircinum subsp. calcaratum

Himantoglossum hircinum subsp.

calcaratum, commonly known as the mountain gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine pubescence.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators in mountain ecosystems.
