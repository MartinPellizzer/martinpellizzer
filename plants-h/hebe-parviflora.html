Hebe parviflora

Hebe parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered hebe, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine, silvery-grey pubescence. Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically appear in clusters, blooming in shades of blue, purple, or white.

Hebe parviflora thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal gardens.
