Haplopappus squarrosus subsp. grindelioides

Haplopappus squarrosus subsp.

grindelioides, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America. It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with hairy, fleshy stems and leaves.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by fluffy, white pappus structures, aiding in wind pollination. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought and extreme temperatures.

This subspecies is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.
