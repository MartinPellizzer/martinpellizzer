Hieracium Sparsum Subsp. Secundum

Hieracium sparsum subsp. secundum is a lesser-known member of the dandelion family known for its delicate, yellow flowers and hairy stems. Found primarily in southern Africa, this plant thrives in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Despite its modest appearance, it plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecosystem balance. Its unique subspecies characteristics distinguish it from other members of the Hieracium genus. Studying this plant offers insights into the biodiversity and adaptability of flora in arid environments.
