Hieracium haematopodum subsp. pedatifolium

Hieracium haematopodum subsp.

pedatifolium, commonly known as the bloodroot hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are deeply lobed and arranged in a rosette, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive red base.

It is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
