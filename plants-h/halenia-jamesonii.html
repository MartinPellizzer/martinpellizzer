Halenia jamesonii

Halenia jamesonii, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its large, fragrant, white flowers that open at night. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a hairy stem. Its flowers emit a sweet scent to attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
