Hieracium ucenicum subsp. arvasicum

Hieracium ucenicum subsp.

arvasicum, commonly known as the Arvasicum hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and its flowers are arranged in a single head.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
