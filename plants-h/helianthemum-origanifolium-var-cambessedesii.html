Helianthemum origanifolium var. cambessedesii

Helianthemum origanifolium var. cambessedesii, commonly known as the Canary Island rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has aromatic, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive scent reminiscent of oregano. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh environments.
