Hieracium laevigatum subsp. gothiciforme

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

gothiciforme, commonly known as the Gothic dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and lobed leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. It has a rosette of basal leaves that are usually hairy and have a distinctive, somewhat ornate appearance.

The subspecies is notable for its unique floral structure and historical association with alpine environments.
