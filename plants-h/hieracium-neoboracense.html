Hieracium neoboracense

Hieracium neoboracense, commonly known as the Boracene hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
