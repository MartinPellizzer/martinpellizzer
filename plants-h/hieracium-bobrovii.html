Hieracium bobrovii

Hieracium bobrovii, commonly known as Bobrov's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its flowers are yellow and form dense clusters, while its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Hieracium bobrovii is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
