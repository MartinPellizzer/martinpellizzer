Hedyotis minima f. albiflora

Hedyotis minima f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered inchplant, is a compact, trailing perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and arranged opposite along its stems. The plant produces tiny, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a delicate ornamental touch. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often used in ground covers or hanging baskets.

Due to its compact size and attractive foliage, it is a popular choice for woodland gardens and wetland habitats.
