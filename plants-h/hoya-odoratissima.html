Hoya odoratissima

Hoya odoratissima, commonly known as the sweet scent hoya, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its waxy, thick leaves and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers. The plant produces a fragrant aroma, particularly noticeable when the flowers open in the evening. Hoya odoratissima is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil.
