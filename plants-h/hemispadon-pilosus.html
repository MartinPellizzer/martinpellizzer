Hemispadon pilosus

Hemispadon pilosus, commonly known as the hairy hemispadon, is a unique plant species characterized by its distinctive, hairy foliage.

It is a perennial herb native to specific regions of South America, particularly in the Andes. The plant features elongated, lance-shaped leaves covered in fine, soft hairs, which give it its common name. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically displaying a range of colors from white to pink.

Hemispadon pilosus is adapted to thrive in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.
