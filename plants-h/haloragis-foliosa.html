Haloragis foliosa

Haloragis foliosa, commonly known as the leafy willow herb, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland or aquatic environments. The plant has slender, branched stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in dense, greenish-yellow inflorescences, and it blooms during the spring and summer months.

Haloragis foliosa plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
