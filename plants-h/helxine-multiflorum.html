Helxine multiflorum

Helxine multiflorum, commonly known as the forest violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the woodlands of eastern North America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 6 to 12 inches and thrives in partial to full shade. It prefers moist, well-drained soil and is often found in forest understories.

Helxine multiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
