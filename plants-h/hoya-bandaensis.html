Hoya bandaensis

Hoya bandaensis, commonly known as the Banda Wax Plant, is a species of flowering plant native to the Banda Islands in Indonesia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions. Hoya bandaensis is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its attractive foliage and relatively easy care requirements.

Its unique appearance and air-purifying properties make it a popular choice among plant enthusiasts.
