Hylotelephium ruprechtii

Hylotelephium ruprechtii, commonly known as the Himalayan stonecrop, is a perennial succulent native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically gray-green in color, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements make it well-suited for arid or semi-arid climates.
