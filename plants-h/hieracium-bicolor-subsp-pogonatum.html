Hieracium bicolor subsp. pogonatum

Hieracium bicolor subsp.

pogonatum, commonly known as the purple hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features basal leaves that are broadly lanceolate and covered in dense, glandular hairs. Its flowers are yellow with a distinctive purple throat, and it produces small, oval-shaped achene fruits.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.
