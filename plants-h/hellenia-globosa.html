Hellenia globosa

Hellenia globosa, commonly known as the globe hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features dark green, waxy leaves that are lance-shaped and often have a distinctive white or purple midrib. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, appearing in late winter or early spring. Hellenia globosa thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and blooms.
