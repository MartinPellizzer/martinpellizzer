Hemisteptia lyrata f. nivea

Hemisteptia lyrata f. nivea, commonly known as the white-flowered lyreleaf sedge, is a variant of the lyreleaf sedge native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and distinctive, lyre-shaped inflorescence. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its white or pale pink flowers are arranged in a dense, spike-like cluster, adding ornamental value to its natural setting.

This subspecies is notable for its striking appearance and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
