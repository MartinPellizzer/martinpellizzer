Hieracium psammogenes

Hieracium psammogenes, commonly known as the sand everlasting or sand dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and grass-like leaves. This plant thrives in sandy and coastal environments, often found in dunes and rocky soils. Its yellow flowers bloom in summer, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Hieracium psammogenes is adapted to withstand harsh, dry conditions and is often considered a pioneer species in ecological succession.
