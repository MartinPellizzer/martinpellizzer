Hieracium murorum subsp. spanocomum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

spanocomum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, grassy habitats such as meadows and riverbanks.

The plant features basal rosettes of lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters at the ends of its stems. It has a taproot system and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly larger flowers and more robust growth compared to other members of the Hieracium genus.
