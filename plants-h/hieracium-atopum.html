Hieracium atopum

Hieracium atopum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes where conditions are harsh. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rough texture, and its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium atopum is known for its resilience to cold and poor soil conditions, making it a common sight in alpine meadows.
