Hyssopus bracteatus

Hyssopus bracteatus, commonly known as the woolly hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, square stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces spikes of pale blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Hyssopus bracteatus is also valued for its aromatic properties and historical use in herbal medicine.
