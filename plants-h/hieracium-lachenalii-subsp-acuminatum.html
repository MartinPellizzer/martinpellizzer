Hieracium lachenalii subsp. acuminatum

Hieracium lachenalii subsp.

acuminatum, commonly known as the acuminated hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, moist habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, and its leaves are usually lance-shaped with hairy surfaces.

This subspecies is noted for its slender stems and is often found in calcareous soils.
