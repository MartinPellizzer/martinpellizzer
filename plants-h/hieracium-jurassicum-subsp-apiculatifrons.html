Hieracium jurassicum subsp. apiculatifrons

Hieracium jurassicum subsp.

apiculatifrons, commonly known as the Jurassic hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to specific regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems.

The subspecies is distinguished by its apiculate (bristle-tipped) leaves and flowering heads. It typically grows in meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered plant species.
