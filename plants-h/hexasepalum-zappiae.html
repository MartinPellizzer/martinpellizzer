Hexasepalum zappiae

Hexasepalum zappiae, commonly known as the Zappia's hexasepalum, is a rare and unique flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, hexagonal sepals that give it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in cloud forests. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to pale pink.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
