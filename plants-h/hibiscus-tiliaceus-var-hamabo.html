Hibiscus tiliaceus var. hamabo

Hibiscus tiliaceus var. hamabo, commonly known as the Hawaiian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Pacific.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in coastal environments, often growing along shores and in mangrove swamps. It is a shrub or small tree that can reach heights of up to 10 meters.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological systems of its native habitats.
