Hieracium tephrodermum subsp. subexpansum

Hieracium tephrodermum subsp.

subexpansum, commonly known as the broadleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found in meadows, roadsides, and wastelands.

Due to its invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a weed in certain regions.
