Helianthemum variegatum

Helianthemum variegatum, commonly known as the variegated rock rose, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features small, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive red or purple center. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for rock gardens and coastal landscapes. Its leaves are typically gray-green with white markings, adding visual interest.

It is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
