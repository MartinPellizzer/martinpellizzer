Halimocnemis villosa

Halimocnemis villosa, commonly known as the hairy sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its hairy, succulent stems and small, fleshy leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its adaptability to harsh environments makes it an important component of desert ecosystems.

Halimocnemis villosa is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
