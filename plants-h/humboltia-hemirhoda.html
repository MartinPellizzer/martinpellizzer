Humboltia hemirhoda

Humboltia hemirhoda, commonly known as the desert holly, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapted to survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its stems are often branched and can grow up to several feet in height.

Humboltia hemirhoda is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
