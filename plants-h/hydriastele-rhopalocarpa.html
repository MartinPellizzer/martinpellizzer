Hydriastele rhopalocarpa

Hydriastele rhopalocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed hydriastele, is a striking member of the Bromeliaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and elongated, narrow leaves that form a rosette structure. Its inflorescence consists of a single, showy flower that emerges from the center of the rosette, often in vibrant shades of red or orange.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
