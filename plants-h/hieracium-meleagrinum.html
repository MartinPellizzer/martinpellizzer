Hieracium meleagrinum

Hieracium meleagrinum, commonly known as the "devil's paintbrush," is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and woodland edges. The plant features bright yellow flowers that resemble a paintbrush, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets. It has hairy, rough leaves that are typically lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Hieracium meleagrinum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
