Hibiscus pruriens

Hibiscus pruriens, commonly known as the itching bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to red, often with a strikingly hairy texture. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family and is known for its hairy stems and leaves, which can cause an itchy sensation when touched. Hibiscus pruriens is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and has traditional medicinal uses in various cultures.

Its growth habit includes both shrub-like and tree-like forms, depending on the species and environmental conditions.
