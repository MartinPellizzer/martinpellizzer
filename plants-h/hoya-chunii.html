Hoya chunii

Hoya chunii, commonly known as the "Wax Plant" or "Lipsy," is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and pink. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming during the summer months. Hoya chunii is a popular houseplant due to its low maintenance and ability to thrive in indirect light.

Its unique appearance and ease of care make it a favored choice among indoor gardeners.
