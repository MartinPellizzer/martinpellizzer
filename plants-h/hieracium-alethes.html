Hieracium alethes

Hieracium alethes, commonly known as the Alethian hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It features slender stems with hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
