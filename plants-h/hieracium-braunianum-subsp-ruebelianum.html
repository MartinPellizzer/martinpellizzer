Hieracium braunianum subsp. ruebelianum

Hieracium braunianum subsp.

ruebelianum, commonly known as the Ruebel's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
