Hyptis lindmaniana

Hyptis lindmaniana, commonly known as the Mexican mint marigold, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine, and it is also cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Hyptis lindmaniana is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
