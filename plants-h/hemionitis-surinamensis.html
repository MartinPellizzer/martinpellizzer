Hemionitis surinamensis

Hemionitis surinamensis, commonly known as the Surinam nettle, is a unique aquatic plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It features a creeping rhizome system that allows it to spread across water surfaces and submerged substrates. The plant has small, needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants, a characteristic known as dioecy.

Hemionitis surinamensis is adapted to thrive in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, making it an important component of aquatic ecosystems.
