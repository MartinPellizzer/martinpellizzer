Hieracium sabaudum var. humile

Hieracium sabaudum var. humile, commonly known as the dwarf hawkweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish to orange flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is sometimes grown in gardens.
