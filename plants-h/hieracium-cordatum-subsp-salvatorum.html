Hieracium cordatum subsp. salvatorum

Hieracium cordatum subsp.

salvatorum, commonly known as the Alpine Groundsel, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky areas, thriving in cool, moist conditions.

The plant features a basal rosette of dark green, heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowering stems bear yellow composite flowers, which are composed of small tubular florets surrounded by ray florets.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in garden settings for its hardy nature and aesthetic qualities.
