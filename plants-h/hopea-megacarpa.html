Hopea megacarpa

Hopea megacarpa, commonly known as the giant ironwood tree, is a large evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its massive, heavy wood and long lifespan. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall, with a broad, spreading crown and thick, flaky bark. Its flowers are small and yellowish, and it produces large, woody fruits that contain one or two seeds.

The plant is valued for its durable timber and plays an important ecological role in its native habitats.
