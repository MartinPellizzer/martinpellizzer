Hieracium borodinianum

Hieracium borodinianum, commonly known as Borodin's hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. It has hairy, elongated leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
