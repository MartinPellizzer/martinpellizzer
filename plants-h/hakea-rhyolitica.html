Hakea rhyolitica

Hakea rhyolitica, commonly known as the Rhyolite hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves and produces distinctive yellow flowers in spring. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. Its foliage is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its aesthetic appeal.

Hakea rhyolitica plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
