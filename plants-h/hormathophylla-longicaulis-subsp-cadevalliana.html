Hormathophylla longicaulis subsp. cadevalliana

Hormathophylla longicaulis subsp.

cadevalliana, commonly known as the Cape York hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to northern Australia. It is a shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The leaves are typically broad and elliptical, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
