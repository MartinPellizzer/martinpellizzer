Hymenaea rubriflora

Hymenaea rubriflora, commonly known as the red-flowered ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fissured bark and dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its durability. The plant produces striking red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Hymenaea rubriflora plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for wildlife.
