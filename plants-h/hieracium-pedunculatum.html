Hieracium pedunculatum

Hieracium pedunculatum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while its flowers are yellow and bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Hieracium pedunculatum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
