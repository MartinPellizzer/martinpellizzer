Horsfieldia papillosa

Horsfieldia papillosa, commonly known as the hairy orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, hairy leaves and fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink and have a unique, intricate structure.

Horsfieldia papillosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
