Hieracium vulgatum subsp. acuminatum

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

acuminatum, commonly known as the sharp-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features basal and alternate leaves that are lanceolate to ovate, with serrated margins and hairy surfaces. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and consist of tubular florets surrounded by ray florets.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its distinctive appearance.
