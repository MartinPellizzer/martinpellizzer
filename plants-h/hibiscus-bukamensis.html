Hibiscus bukamensis

Hibiscus bukamensis, commonly known as the Bukamensi hibiscus, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red, with a prominent central pistil. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are usually green and slightly hairy on the underside. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Hibiscus bukamensis is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its potential medicinal uses in traditional African medicine.
