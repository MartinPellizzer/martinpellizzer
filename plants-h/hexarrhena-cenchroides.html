Hexarrhena cenchroides

Hexarrhena cenchroides, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its straight, dark reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and fine grain. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a glossy appearance. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, round fruits.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the production of high-quality timber.
