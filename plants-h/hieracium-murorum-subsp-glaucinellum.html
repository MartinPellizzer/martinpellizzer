Hieracium murorum subsp. glaucinellum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

glaucinellum, commonly known as the glaucous hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, glaucous leaves and yellowish to reddish flower heads.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats such as roadsides and meadows. It produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal, aiding in its spread.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is sometimes grown in gardens.
