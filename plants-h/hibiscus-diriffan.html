Hibiscus diriffan

Hibiscus diriffan, commonly known as the Malabar hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of southern India.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to deep red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are usually green and slightly hairy. Hibiscus diriffan thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to flourish.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
