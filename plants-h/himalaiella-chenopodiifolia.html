Himalaiella chenopodiifolia

Himalaiella chenopodiifolia, commonly known as the Himalayan saltbush, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its small, green, succulent leaves that are adapted to arid and cold environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. Himalaiella chenopodiifolia is often found growing in rocky soils and is well-suited to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
