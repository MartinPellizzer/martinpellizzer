Hovea longifolia var. aspera

Hovea longifolia var. aspera, commonly known as the rough hovea, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to dry, coastal environments. It has small, elongated pods that contain several seeds.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration due to its hardy nature and ability to stabilize soil.
