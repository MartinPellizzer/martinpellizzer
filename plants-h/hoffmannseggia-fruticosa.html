Hoffmannseggia fruticosa

Hoffmannseggia fruticosa, commonly known as the desert holly, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its fleshy, spiny stems and small, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal rainfall.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for xeriscaping and ecological restoration in arid landscapes.
