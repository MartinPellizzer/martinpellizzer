Hypargyrium subrectum

Hypargyrium subrectum, commonly known as the slender silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, silver-colored leaves and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Hypargyrium subrectum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
