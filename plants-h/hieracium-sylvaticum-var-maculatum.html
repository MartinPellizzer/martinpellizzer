Hieracium sylvaticum var. maculatum

Hieracium sylvaticum var. maculatum, commonly known as the spotted hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks. Its leaves and stems are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The species is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
