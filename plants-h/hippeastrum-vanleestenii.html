Hippeastrum vanleestenii

Hippeastrum vanleestenii, commonly known as the Van Leesten's amaryllis, is a bulbous plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It features striking, showy flowers that are typically red or orange with contrasting purple or white markings. The plant produces large, strap-like leaves that are dark green and often have a waxy texture. Its bulbs are underground storage organs that support the plant through periods of dormancy.

Hippeastrum vanleestenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
