Hibbertia oblongata subsp. oblongata

Hibbertia oblongata subsp.

oblongata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hibbertia, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its slender, ascending stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands. Hibbertia oblongata subsp.

oblongata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
