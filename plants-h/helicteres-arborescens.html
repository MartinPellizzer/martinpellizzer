Helicteres arborescens

Helicteres arborescens, commonly known as the tree hibiscus or Mexican hibiscus, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It features showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow, often with a distinctive red or purple throat. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces capsules that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in humid, tropical climates.

Botanically, it is part of the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton plants.
