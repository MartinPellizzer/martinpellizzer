Hieracium doronicifolium subsp. arpillense

Hieracium doronicifolium subsp.

arpillense, commonly known as the Arpillan hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has hairy stems and leaves with a distinctive, lobed shape.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and sometimes endangered species in its native range.
