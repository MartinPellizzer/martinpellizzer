Hesperis decipiens

Hesperis decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive hesperis, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia. The plant produces striking, violet-blue flowers with four petals arranged in a cross shape, typical of the mustard family. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

Hesperis decipiens is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to thrive in full sun conditions.
