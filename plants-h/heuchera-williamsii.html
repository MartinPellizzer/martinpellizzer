Heuchera williamsii

Heuchera williamsii, commonly known as the Williams' alumroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, upright clumps of basal leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from deep red to purple and green. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white during the spring. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Heuchera williamsii is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and early spring blooms.
