Hibiscus malacophyllus

Hibiscus malacophyllus, commonly known as the velvetleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves that are dark green and glossy. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. Hibiscus malacophyllus typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
