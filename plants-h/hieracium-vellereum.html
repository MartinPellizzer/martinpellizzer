Hieracium vellereum

Hieracium vellereum, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant features a hairy, upright stem with alternate leaves that are typically gray-green in color. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the genus Hieracium.

It thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, often growing in clusters.
