Hypericum gebleri

Hypericum gebleri, commonly known as the dwarf St. John's wort, is a low-growing shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has narrow, dark green leaves and produces red, berry-like fruits that persist into winter. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long blooming period.
