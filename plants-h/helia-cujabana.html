Helia cujabana

Helia cujabana, commonly known as the Amazonian sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that resemble those of the sunflower. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas of the rainforest and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.

Helia cujabana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
