Hibiscus intraterraneus

Hibiscus intraterraneus, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its velvety, waxy leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that come in a variety of colors, including pink, red, and white. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish. Hibiscus intraterraneus is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor gardens.
