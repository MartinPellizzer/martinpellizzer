Hypericum aegypticum subsp. maroccanum

Hypericum aegypticum subsp.

maroccanum, commonly known as the Moroccan St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to North Africa. It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces bright yellow flowers with five petals.

The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties, with historical use in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antidepressant effects.
