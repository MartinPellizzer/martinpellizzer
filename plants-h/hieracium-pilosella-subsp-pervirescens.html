Hieracium pilosella subsp. pervirescens

Hieracium pilosella subsp.

pervirescens, commonly known as the greenish hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm.

The plant features upright stems with hairy leaves that are lance-shaped and often have a bluish-green hue. Its flowers are yellow and occur in clusters, forming a distinctive composite inflorescence.

The subspecies is characterized by its more vibrant green foliage compared to other members of the Hieracium genus.
