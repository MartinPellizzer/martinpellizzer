Haplopappus araucanus

Haplopappus araucanus, commonly known as the Araucanian hairpappus, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is known for its distinctive yellow to orange flower heads, which are composed of numerous small florets. It typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its deep root system.

Haplopappus araucanus is also noted for its small, papery bracts that surround the flower head, giving it its common name.
