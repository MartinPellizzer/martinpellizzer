Helictotrichon montanum subsp. planifolium

Helictotrichon montanum subsp.

planifolium, commonly known as the plains wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America. It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, prairies, and disturbed soils.

The plant has slender, flat leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle. It produces small, spikelet-like flowers that are wind-pollinated.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting wildlife.
