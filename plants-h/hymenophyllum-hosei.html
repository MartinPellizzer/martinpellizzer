Hymenophyllum hosei

Hymenophyllum hosei, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spore moss, is a small, delicate fern belonging to the Hymenophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in width. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found on tree trunks or rocks in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spore-producing structures, called sporangia, are clustered in small, circular groups on the underside of the leaves.

Hymenophyllum hosei is known for its intricate leaf structure and is often used in horticultural displays due to its aesthetic appeal.
