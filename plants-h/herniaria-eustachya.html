Herniaria eustachya

Herniaria eustachya, commonly known as the lesser bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate stems and small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats. Its leaves are opposite, linear, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are clustered in racemes.

Herniaria eustachya is also noted for its ability to form dense mats, which can help stabilize soil in arid environments.
