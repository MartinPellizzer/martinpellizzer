Hieracium cinereostriatum

Hieracium cinereostriatum, commonly known as the ash-gray hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble clusters of small flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring moist to moderately dry conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a hairy surface, and the flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disk.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of habitats and is often found in open, sunny areas.
