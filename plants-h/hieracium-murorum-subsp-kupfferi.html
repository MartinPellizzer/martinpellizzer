Hieracium murorum subsp. kupfferi

Hieracium murorum subsp.

kupfferi, commonly known as the Kupffer's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellow disc flowers surrounded by phyllaries.

This subspecies is distinguished by its specific morphology and geographic distribution within the Hieracium genus.
