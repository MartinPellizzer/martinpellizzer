Hymenophyllum falklandicum

Hymenophyllum falklandicum, commonly known as the Falkland Islands moss, is a small, delicate fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its intricate, feather-like fronds that are typically less than 2 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on rock surfaces or in humid forest understories. Its spores are released in tiny, cup-like structures called sorus, which are densely clustered on the underside of its leaves.

This species is native to the Falkland Islands and is adapted to the cool, maritime climate of the Southern Hemisphere.
