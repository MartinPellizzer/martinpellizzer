Hypericum patzkei

Hypericum patzkei, commonly known as the Patzke's St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub with narrow, linear leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the family Hypericaceae and is characterized by its presence of yellow-orange flowers with five petals. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
