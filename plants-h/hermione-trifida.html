Hermione trifida

It is characterized by its small, bladder-like structures that trap small aquatic organisms, aiding in its nutrient acquisition. The plant typically grows in shallow, still waters such as ponds and wetlands. Its leaves are usually divided into three lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.
