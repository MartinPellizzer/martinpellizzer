Heuchera californica

Heuchera californica, commonly known as the California alumroot, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features basal rosettes of heart-shaped, hairy leaves that range in color from silver to deep green. The plant produces upright spikes of small, white to pinkish flowers in the spring. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soils, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental foliage and early blooming habit make it a popular choice for native plant landscapes.
