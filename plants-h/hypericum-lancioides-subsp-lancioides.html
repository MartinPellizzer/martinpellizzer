Hypericum lancioides subsp. lancioides

Hypericum lancioides subsp.

lancioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf St. John's wort, is a herbaceous perennial shrub native to the eastern United States. It features opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.

This species is often found in woodland edges and moist meadows, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
