Habenella wallichii

Habenella wallichii, commonly known as the Himalayan snowbell, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in cool, moist environments at elevations ranging from 2,000 to 4,000 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers during the spring season. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate, with a slightly hairy surface.

Habenella wallichii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate regions.
