Hieracium tanylobum

Hieracium tanylobum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, exhibiting a low-growing habit with upright stems. Its leaves are narrow and often have a rough texture, while the flowers are yellow and clustered in a single head.

Hieracium tanylobum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
