Hibiscus crassinervius var. flammeus

Hibiscus crassinervius var. flammeus, commonly known as the flame hibiscus, is a striking tropical shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range from red to orange, often with a yellow throat, and are typically 5 to 10 centimeters in diameter. The leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with prominent veins that give the plant its scientific name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
