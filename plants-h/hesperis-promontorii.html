Hesperis promontorii

Hesperis promontorii, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers and silvery, fuzzy leaves that help reduce water loss in saline environments. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in dunes or along the shoreline. Its blooming period typically occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hesperis promontorii is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems.
