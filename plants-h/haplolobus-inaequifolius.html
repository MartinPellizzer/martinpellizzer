Haplolobus inaequifolius

Haplolobus inaequifolius, commonly known as the unequal-leaved haplolobus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaves, which vary in size and shape along the stem, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the spring. Its fibrous root system helps it thrive in nutrient-poor soils, and it is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Haplolobus inaequifolius is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphological features and ecological adaptations.
