Hieracium nigriceps subsp. confinium

Hieracium nigriceps subsp.

confinium, commonly known as the black-headed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its dark purple to black capitula, which are surrounded by narrow, lance-shaped phyllaries.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, rough to the touch, and often have a distinctive yellowish or whitish underside.

The species is native to Europe and has been introduced to other regions, where it can become invasive in certain environments.
