Hieracium magyaricum subsp. rojowskii

Hieracium magyaricum subsp.

rojowskii, commonly known as the Hungarian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. It has finely divided, serrated leaves and produces numerous small, yellow disk flowers that attract pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
