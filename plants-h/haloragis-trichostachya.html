Haloragis trichostachya

Haloragis trichostachya, commonly known as the silverweed, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Haloragaceae and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage due to a dense covering of fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along waterways.

Haloragis trichostachya is an important component of native ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various insects and wildlife.
