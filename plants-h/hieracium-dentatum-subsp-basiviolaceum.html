Hieracium dentatum subsp. basiviolaceum

Hieracium dentatum subsp.

basiviolaceum, commonly known as the blue-flowered hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, blue or violet flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some regions.
