Hieracium caesium var. basifolium

Hieracium caesium var. basifolium, commonly known as the basal-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. It has a taproot system and produces numerous flowering stems that bear small, daisy-like blooms.

The leaves are usually broad, hairy, and have a serrated edge, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
