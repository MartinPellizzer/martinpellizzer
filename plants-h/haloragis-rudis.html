Haloragis rudis

Haloragis rudis, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, blue or violet flowers. The plant thrives in saline and sandy soils, often found in dune environments. It has a creeping growth habit with branched stems that root at the nodes.

Haloragis rudis is adapted to withstand harsh coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.
