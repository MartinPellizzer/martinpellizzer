Helodium repens

Helodium repens, commonly known as the creeping helodium, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its prostrate habit, with stems that spread along the ground. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Helodium repens is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
