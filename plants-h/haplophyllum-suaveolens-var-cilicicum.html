Haplophyllum suaveolens var. cilicicum

Haplophyllum suaveolens var. cilicicum, commonly known as the Cilician sweetleaf, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that emit a pleasant fragrance when crushed. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring. Its fruit is a capsule containing several seeds, typical of the Rutaceae family.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone-rich habitats.
