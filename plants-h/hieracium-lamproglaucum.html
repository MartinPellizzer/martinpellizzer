Hieracium lamproglaucum

Hieracium lamproglaucum, commonly known as the blue hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, giving them a silvery appearance.

The flowers are yellow with a distinctive blue-gray hue, contributing to its common name and making it a striking addition to its habitat.
