Halmia tomentosa var. leucophloea

Halmia tomentosa var. leucophloea, commonly known as the white-flowered halmia, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy, clustered white or pale pink flowers. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape and covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The flowers bloom in terminal inflorescences and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in warm climates for its unique appearance.
