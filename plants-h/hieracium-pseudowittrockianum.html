Hieracium pseudowittrockianum

Hieracium pseudowittrockianum, commonly known as the false Wittrock's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy stems. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers appear in clusters.

It is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
