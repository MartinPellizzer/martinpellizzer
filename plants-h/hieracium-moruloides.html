Hieracium moruloides

Hieracium moruloides, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and produces numerous yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soil. It has hairy stems and leaves that are often reddish or purplish in color.

Hieracium moruloides is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it is often found in temperate climates.
