Halesia diptera var. magniflora

Halesia diptera var. magniflora, commonly known as the magnificent halesia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its elegant, pendulous white flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a striking visual appeal to the landscape. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn, contributing to its seasonal beauty. Its bark is smooth and gray, becoming fissured with age, and it prefers well-drained, acidic soils.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive flowers and graceful form.
