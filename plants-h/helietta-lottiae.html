Helietta lottiae

Helietta lottiae, commonly known as the small white heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features small, needle-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color, and it produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in the spring. Helietta lottiae thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
