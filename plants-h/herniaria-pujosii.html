Herniaria pujosii

Herniaria pujosii, commonly known as the Canary Islands stonecrop, is a low-growing succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features small, fleshy, green leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces tiny, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the arid climate of its native habitat.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical studies.
