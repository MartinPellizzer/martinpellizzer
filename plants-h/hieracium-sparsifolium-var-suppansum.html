Hieracium sparsifolium var. suppansum

Hieracium sparsifolium var. suppansum, commonly known as the lesser hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it has a low, spreading growth habit. Its leaves are usually alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters.

It is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid spread and ability to thrive in various conditions.
