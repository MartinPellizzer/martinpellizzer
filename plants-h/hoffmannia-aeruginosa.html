Hoffmannia aeruginosa

Hoffmannia aeruginosa, commonly known as the copperleaf or blue copperleaf, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its striking blue-green foliage and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to drought conditions. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps reduce water loss.

Hoffmannia aeruginosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
