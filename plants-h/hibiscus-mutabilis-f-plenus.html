Hibiscus mutabilis f. plenus

Hibiscus mutabilis f. plenus, commonly known as the double-flowered hibiscus, is a cultivar of the common hibiscus.

It is characterized by its large, showy, double blooms that come in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant is a deciduous shrub that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil. Its foliage is dark green and glossy, adding visual interest throughout the growing season.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
