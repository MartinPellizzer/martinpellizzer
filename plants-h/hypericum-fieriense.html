Hypericum fieriense

Hypericum fieriense, commonly known as the Fiery St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bright orange-yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of hairs.

Hypericum fieriense is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
