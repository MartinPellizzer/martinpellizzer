Hedyotis bahaii

Hedyotis bahaii, commonly known as the Bahá'í knotweed, is a small herbaceous plant native to the Middle East and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters. Its flowering period usually occurs in late spring to early summer.

Hedyotis bahaii is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
