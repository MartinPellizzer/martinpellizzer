Hedyotis foliosa

Hedyotis foliosa, commonly known as the silverleaf trailing plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features small, opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for container gardening or as a ground cover.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
