Hydrocotyle mercurialis

Hydrocotyle mercurialis, commonly known as mercuryleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and wetlands. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive, almost fern-like appearance. Its small, white flowers are clustered in umbels and bloom during the summer months.

Hydrocotyle mercurialis is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to wet conditions.
