Hieracium arolae subsp. arolae

Hieracium arolae subsp.

arolae, commonly known as the Arola hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and a flowering stem that bears yellow, daisy-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing alpine soils and providing nectar for pollinators.
