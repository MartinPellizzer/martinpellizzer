Hibiscus amaliae

Hibiscus amaliae, commonly known as the Amalia hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a rough texture, and the flowers bloom in summer.

Hibiscus amaliae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
