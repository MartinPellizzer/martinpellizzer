Hieracium hyoseridifolium

Hieracium hyoseridifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and often hairy, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is an invasive species in some regions, outcompeting native vegetation due to its rapid growth and prolific seed production.
