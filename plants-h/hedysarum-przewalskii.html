Hedysarum przewalskii

Hedysarum przewalskii, commonly known as Przewalski's milkvetch, is a perennial leguminous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, enriching the soil. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
