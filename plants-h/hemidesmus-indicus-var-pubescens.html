Hemidesmus indicus var. pubescens

Hemidesmus indicus var. pubescens, commonly known as Indian snakeroot, is a perennial herb native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Ayurvedic practices.
