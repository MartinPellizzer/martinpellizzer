Hemigraphis simulans

Hemigraphis simulans, commonly known as the Mexican nettle or velvet leaf, is a perennial herb native to Central and South America.

It features small, opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it a soft texture. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage.

Hemigraphis simulans is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
