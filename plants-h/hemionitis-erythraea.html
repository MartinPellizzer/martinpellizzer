Hemionitis erythraea

Hemionitis erythraea, commonly known as the red sandwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

It has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it store water, adapting to its harsh, dry environment. The plant produces small, pink to red flowers that bloom in early spring, often in clusters at the ends of its stems. Its root system is shallow but extensive, allowing it to efficiently absorb moisture from the soil.

Hemionitis erythraea is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
