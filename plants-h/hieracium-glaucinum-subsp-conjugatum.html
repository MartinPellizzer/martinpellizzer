Hieracium glaucinum subsp. conjugatum

Hieracium glaucinum subsp.

conjugatum, commonly known as the blue hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of blue-green leaves.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
