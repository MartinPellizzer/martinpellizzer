Helianthus apricus

Helianthus apricus, commonly known as the sunflower, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to North America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flower heads surrounded by green bracts that resemble leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and produces a single large composite flower that blooms in the summer. Its scientific name reflects its affinity for sunlight, with "helianthus" meaning "sun-like" and "apricus" referring to its golden color.

The plant has a taproot system and thrives in well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and agricultural settings.
