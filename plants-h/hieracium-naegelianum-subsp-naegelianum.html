Hieracium naegelianum subsp. naegelianum

Hieracium naegelianum subsp.

naegelianum, commonly known as the Naegeli's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellowish to reddish composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed sites, preferring well-drained soils. It has small, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its inflorescence consists of numerous small florets arranged in a disc.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is sometimes grown in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
