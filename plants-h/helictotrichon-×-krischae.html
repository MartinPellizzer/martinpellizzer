Helictotrichon × krischae

Helictotrichon × krischae, commonly known as the hybrid bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is a hybrid between two species of bluegrass, typically Helictotrichon sempervirens and Helictotrichon mollis. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a common sight in open fields and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native grassland ecosystems.
