Haplopappus poeppigianus

Haplopappus poeppigianus, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by pale, fluffy seeds, typical of the family. It has adapted to thrive in harsh, dry environments with deep root systems and waxy leaves to reduce water loss.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for xerophytic conditions and ecological restoration projects.
