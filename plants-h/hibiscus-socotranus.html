Hibiscus socotranus

Hibiscus socotranus, commonly known as Socotra's hibiscus, is a unique flowering plant native to the Arabian island of Socotra.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 20 centimeters in diameter. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves and produces distinctive red or purple flowers with a prominent central pistil. Hibiscus socotranus thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to the harsh climate of its native habitat.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in the region.
