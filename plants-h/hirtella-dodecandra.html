Hirtella dodecandra

Hirtella dodecandra, commonly known as the Christmas star, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, star-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture.

The flowers, which bloom in clusters, are usually white or pale pink and attract various pollinators such as bees and birds.
