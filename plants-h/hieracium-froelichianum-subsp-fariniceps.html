Hieracium froelichianum subsp. fariniceps

Hieracium froelichianum subsp.

fariniceps, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are often covered in a fine, powdery coating.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. It is typically found in meadows and grassy areas, preferring moist, well-drained soils.

This subspecies is native to Europe and has been introduced to other regions, including parts of North America.
