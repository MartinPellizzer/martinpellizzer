Heteropappus altaicus var. distortus

Heteropappus altaicus var. distortus, commonly known as the twisted pappus, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive bracts. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh conditions. Its leaves are often narrow and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and produce small, feathery seeds.

This variant is noted for its unique morphological features, including the distorted shape of its pappus, which aids in wind dispersal.
