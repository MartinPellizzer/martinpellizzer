Hieracium magyaricum subsp. dobromilense

Hieracium magyaricum subsp.

dobromilense, commonly known as the Hungarian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowering heads and basal rosettes of leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges.

Its flowers are arranged in composite inflorescences, typical of the Asteraceae family, and it plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
