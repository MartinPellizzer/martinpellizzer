Hyoscyamus × gyorffyi

Hyoscyamus × gyorffyi, commonly known as the Hungarian henbane, is a hybrid plant belonging to the nightshade family (Solanaceae).

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces yellow berries that contain toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals. It is native to the Carpathian Basin and thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and toxic properties, it is often found in gardens and wild areas across Europe.
