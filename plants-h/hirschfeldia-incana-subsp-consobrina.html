Hirschfeldia incana subsp. consobrina

Hirschfeldia incana subsp.

consobrina, commonly known as the slender sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa. It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils.

The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow, hairy, and adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging conditions.
