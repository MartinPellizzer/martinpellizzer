Hypericum stragulum

Hypericum stragulum, commonly known as wandering jew or inch plant, is a flowering plant in the family Hypericaceae.

It is native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia, and it thrives in a variety of soil types. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Hypericum stragulum is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its compact growth habit and vibrant floral display.
