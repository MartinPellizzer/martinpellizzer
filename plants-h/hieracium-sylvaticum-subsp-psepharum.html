Hieracium sylvaticum subsp. psepharum

Hieracium sylvaticum subsp.

psepharum, commonly known as the wood hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to orange composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in woodland and grassland habitats, preferring moist, well-drained soils. It has alternate, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces and produces achenes as its fruit.

This subspecies is often found in shaded environments and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
