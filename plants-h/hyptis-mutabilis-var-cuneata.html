Hyptis mutabilis var. cuneata

Hyptis mutabilis var. cuneata, commonly known as the blue hyptis, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple, often blooming in clusters. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
