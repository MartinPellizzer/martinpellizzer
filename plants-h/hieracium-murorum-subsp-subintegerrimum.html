Hieracium murorum subsp. subintegerrimum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

subintegerrimum, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and yellow composite flowers.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered with stiff, glandular hairs. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and other open habitats.

This subspecies is distinguished by its more robust growth and less hairy leaves compared to other members of the species.
