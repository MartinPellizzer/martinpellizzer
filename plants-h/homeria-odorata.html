Homeria odorata

Homeria odorata, commonly known as the sweet-scented orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often with a waxy texture, while its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture from the air.

The flowers emit a sweet, pleasant aroma, which attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
