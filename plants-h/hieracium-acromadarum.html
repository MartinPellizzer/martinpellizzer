Hieracium acromadarum

Hieracium acromadarum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. It features basal leaves that are usually hairy and have a heart-shaped base.

Its flowers are yellow and bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
