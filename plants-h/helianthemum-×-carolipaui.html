Helianthemum × carolipaui

Helianthemum × carolipaui, commonly known as the hybrid rock rose, is a species native to the Canary Islands.

It is a low-growing evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and fleshy, aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in early summer, which are typically borne in clusters at the tips of the stems. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is drought-tolerant once established.

Helianthemum × carolipaui is often used in rock gardens and as a border plant due to its ornamental value and hardy nature.
