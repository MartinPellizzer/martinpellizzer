Hippeastrum puniceum

Hippeastrum puniceum, commonly known as the red-flowered amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It features large, showy flowers that range from red to pink, often with contrasting white or yellow streaks. The plant has strap-like, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern around the bulb. It thrives in warm, sunny conditions and is popular as a decorative houseplant.

Its blooming period usually occurs in the spring or early summer, making it a favorite among gardeners and plant enthusiasts.
