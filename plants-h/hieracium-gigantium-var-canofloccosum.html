Hieracium gigantium var. canofloccosum

Hieracium gigantium var. canofloccosum, commonly known as the showy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant produces yellow to orange disk flowers surrounded by ray florets, which are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies.

The leaves are usually basal, rough, and hairy, with a distinctive heart-shaped base.
