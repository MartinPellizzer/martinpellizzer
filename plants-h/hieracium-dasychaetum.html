Hieracium dasychaetum

Hieracium dasychaetum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, preferring partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are hairy and alternate, with a rough texture, and the flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium dasychaetum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
