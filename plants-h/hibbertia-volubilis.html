Hibbertia volubilis

Hibbertia volubilis, commonly known as the golden wattle vine, is a climbing shrub native to eastern Australia.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has slender, woody stems that twine around supports, and its leaves are ovate with a rough, hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and temperate regions.

Hibbertia volubilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
