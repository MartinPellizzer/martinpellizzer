Haemanthus membranaceus

Haemanthus membranaceus, commonly known as the Rainforest Hemerocallis, is a striking plant native to the eastern Cape Province of South Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy, membranous layer, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of red and yellow, which bloom briefly during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it requires careful attention to its specific growing conditions.
