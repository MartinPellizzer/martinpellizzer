Heteropappus altaicus var. canescens

Heteropappus altaicus var. canescens, commonly known as the paleleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, wind-dispersed seeds enclosed in a pappus, which aids in its propagation across dry, sandy soils. It is adapted to harsh environments with low precipitation and high temperatures.

This variant is often found in steppe and desert habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
