Hieracium tephrocalyx

Hieracium tephrocalyx, commonly known as the ash-gray hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a distinctive ash-gray hue on the petals.

It is often found in temperate climates and is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils.
