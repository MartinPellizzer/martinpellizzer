Hieracium extracticeps

Hieracium extracticeps, commonly known as the upright hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellowish-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are sturdy and often branched.

The extracticeps species name refers to its tendency to produce upright, erect flower heads.
