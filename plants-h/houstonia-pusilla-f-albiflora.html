Houstonia pusilla f. albiflora

Houstonia pusilla f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered blue-eyed grass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, clustered inflorescences. The flowers are typically white or pale blue, with three petals and three sepals, giving it a delicate appearance. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland understories or along stream banks.

This plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its subtle beauty.
