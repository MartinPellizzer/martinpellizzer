Hieracium aipolium

Hieracium aipolium, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are basal, rough, and hairy, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium aipolium is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and seeds, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
