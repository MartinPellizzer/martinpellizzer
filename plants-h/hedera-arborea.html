Hedera arborea

Hedera arborea, commonly known as the European ivy, is a climbing evergreen vine native to Europe, western Asia, and northwestern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark green, lobed leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by black berries. The plant has a strong adhesive system, allowing it to climb trees and walls with ease. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodlands and gardens.

Hedera arborea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
