Homaida spruneri

Homaida spruneri, commonly known as the desert bellflower, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and purple. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss, typical of xerophytic species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Homaida spruneri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in dry gardens.
