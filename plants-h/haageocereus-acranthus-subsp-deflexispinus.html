Haageocereus acranthus subsp. deflexispinus

Haageocereus acranthus subsp.

deflexispinus, commonly known as the Mexican candleplant, is a member of the Cactaceae family and is native to Mexico. It is a small, spineless cactus that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils.

The plant features a cylindrical stem with a distinctive, candle-like appearance, which is why it is often referred to as the candleplant. It produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking visual element to its environment.

Due to its unique morphology and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in arid gardens.
