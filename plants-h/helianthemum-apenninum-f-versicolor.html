Helianthemum apenninum f. versicolor

Helianthemum apenninum f. versicolor, commonly known as the variable rockrose, is a compact shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy leaves and flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, depending on the variety. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in sunny, coastal areas. Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer.

The varietal form 'versicolor' refers to its variable flower coloration, which adds to its ornamental appeal.
