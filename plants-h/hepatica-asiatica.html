Hepatica asiatica

Hepatica asiatica, commonly known as the Asian liverwort, is a type of liverwort plant belonging to the family Pterobryaceae.

It is characterized by its small, lobed leaves that resemble the shape of the liver, hence its common name. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Hepatica asiatica is often found in temperate regions of Asia, including China and Japan.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
