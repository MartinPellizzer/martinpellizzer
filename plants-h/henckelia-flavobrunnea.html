Henckelia flavobrunnea

Henckelia flavobrunnea, commonly known as the yellow-bronze tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and dense, dark green foliage. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, elongated fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited use in traditional medicine.
