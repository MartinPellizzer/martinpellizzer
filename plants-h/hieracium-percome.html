Hieracium percome

Hieracium percome, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of yellow, dandelion-like flowers. The plant has serrated, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy to the touch. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows, roadsides, and disturbed soils.

Hieracium percome is an invasive species in many regions, outcompeting native plants due to its rapid growth and prolific seed production.
