Hieracium bifidum subsp. semitenuiflorum

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

semitenuiflorum, commonly known as the narrow-flowered hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The flowers are typically yellow, arranged in a flat-topped cluster, and consist of both ray and disc florets. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grasslands.

This subspecies is distinguished by its slender, elongated flower heads compared to other hawkweed species.
