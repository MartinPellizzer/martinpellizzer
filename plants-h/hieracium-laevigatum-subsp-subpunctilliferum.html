Hieracium laevigatum subsp. subpunctilliferum

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

subpunctilliferum, commonly known as the smooth hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped, smooth, and often have a bluish-green hue.

The flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, attracting various pollinators.
