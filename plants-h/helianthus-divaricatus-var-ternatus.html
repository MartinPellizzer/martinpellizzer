Helianthus divaricatus var. ternatus

Helianthus divaricatus var. ternatus, commonly known as the slender sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers consist of a central disk surrounded by ray florets.

This variant is part of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
