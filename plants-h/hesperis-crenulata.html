Hesperis crenulata

Hesperis crenulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved candytuft, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of fragrant, white or pale purple flowers. The plant features lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often narrower than those of related species. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Hesperis crenulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
