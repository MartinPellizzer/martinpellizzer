Hepetis arcuata

Hepetis arcuata, commonly known as the curved hepatica, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves and showy, nodding flowers. The plant typically blooms in early spring, producing white or pale blue flowers with five petals. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the plant prefers moist, shady woodland habitats.

Hepetis arcuata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
