Helianthus annuus var. passiflora

Helianthus annuus var. passiflora, commonly known as the passionflower sunflower, is a hybrid plant that combines traits of both the sunflower and the passionflower.

It features large, showy yellow flowers with a central disk that resembles the structure of a sunflower. The leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, with a rough texture. This plant is native to regions where the parent species naturally occur, and it thrives in full sun and well-drained soil.

Due to its unique appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens.
