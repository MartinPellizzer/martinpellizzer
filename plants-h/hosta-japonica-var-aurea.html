Hosta japonica var. aurea

Hosta japonica var. aurea, commonly known as the golden leaf hosta, is a popular ornamental plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that exhibit a striking yellow to gold coloration, especially under full sun. The plant produces upright, spike-like inflorescences with white to pale blue flowers in the summer months. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained, moist soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Due to its attractive foliage, it is often used as a ground cover or in shaded borders.
