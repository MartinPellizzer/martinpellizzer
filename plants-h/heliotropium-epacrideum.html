Heliotropium epacrideum

Heliotropium epacrideum, commonly known as the mountain heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. The plant produces small, violet to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling those of the genus Heliotropium. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
