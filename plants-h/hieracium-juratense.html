Hieracium juratense

Hieracium juratense, commonly known as the Jurassian hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its leaves are basal, rough, and sometimes hairy, while its flowers are yellow and borne in dense clusters.

Hieracium juratense is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in its native range.
