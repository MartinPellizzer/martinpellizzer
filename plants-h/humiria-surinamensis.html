Humiria surinamensis

Humiria surinamensis, commonly known as the Surinam arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces a spadix inflorescence surrounded by a showy spathe, typical of the arum family. Its rhizomes are used as a starchy root vegetable, similar to taro.

Humiria surinamensis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks.
