Huperzia pichiana

Huperzia pichiana, commonly known as the Pichian clubmoss, is a species of evergreen fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and is native to regions in South America, particularly in Brazil, Colombia, and Ecuador. The plant features whorled leaves that are linear to lanceolate in shape, often with a glossy surface. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is typically found in forest understories.

Huperzia pichiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
