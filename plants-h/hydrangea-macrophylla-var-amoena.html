Hydrangea macrophylla var. amoena

Hydrangea macrophylla var. amoena, commonly known as the panicle hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, cone-shaped flower clusters that range in color from white to pink, red, or blue depending on soil pH. The plant produces showy inflorescences composed of small sterile flowers surrounded by fertile florets. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its foliage turns vibrant shades of red or orange in the fall, adding seasonal interest to the garden.
