Hybanthus enneaspermus var. aurantiacus

Hybanthus enneaspermus var. aurantiacus, commonly known as the golden hybanthus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking orange-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has small, elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique coloration makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
