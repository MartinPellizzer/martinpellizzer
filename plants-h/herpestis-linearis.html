Herpestis linearis

Herpestis linearis, commonly known as the narrowleaf false nettle, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender pedicels. Its stems are usually hairy and can grow up to several feet in height.

Herpestis linearis thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
