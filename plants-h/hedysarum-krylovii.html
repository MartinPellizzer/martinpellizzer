Hedysarum krylovii

Hedysarum krylovii, commonly known as Krylov's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and steppe environments.

Hedysarum krylovii is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and its potential use in restoration ecology.
