Hieracium melampeplum

Hieracium melampeplum, commonly known as the purple hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers consist of both ray and disk florets.

The species is known for its vibrant purple to reddish flower heads, which bloom in late summer to early autumn.
