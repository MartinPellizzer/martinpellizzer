Heliconia conferta

Heliconia conferta, commonly known as the red heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is part of the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble flowers. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are large, broad, and often have a waxy texture.

Heliconia conferta is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant appearance and ability to thrive in humid, warm climates.
