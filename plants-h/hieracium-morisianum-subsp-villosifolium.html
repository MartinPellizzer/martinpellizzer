Hieracium morisianum subsp. villosifolium

Hieracium morisianum subsp.

villosifolium, commonly known as the hairy-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving the plant its common name. It produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is often found in grassy areas and open woodlands, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
