Hypericinea pimentifolia

Hypericinea pimentifolia, commonly known as the peppery hypericum, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the family Hypericaceae and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine layer of soft, glandular hairs.

Hypericinea pimentifolia is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and its unique peppery scent when crushed.
