Hieracium saxifragum subsp. lithogenes

Hieracium saxifragum subsp.

lithogenes, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to rocky and alpine environments in Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of gray-green leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found on limestone or rocky substrates. It is adapted to harsh conditions with a compact growth form and thick, hairy leaves that reduce water loss.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in many regions.
