Hypericum fallax var. quadrangulare

Hypericum fallax var. quadrangulare, commonly known as the smooth St. John's wort, is a shrub native to eastern North America.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually smooth and slightly waxy. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. Its stems are quadrangular, giving it a distinctive, angular appearance.

It is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical medicinal uses.
