Hakea obtusiflora

Hakea obtusiflora, commonly known as the broad-flowered hakea, is a shrub native to southwestern Western Australia.

It features needle-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of bright red or orange flowers, which are highly attractive to nectar-feeding birds and insects. Hakea obtusiflora is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its resilience to drought.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
