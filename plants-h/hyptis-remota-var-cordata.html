Hyptis remota var. cordata

Hyptis remota var. cordata, commonly known as the woolly hyptis, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and aromatic properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine.
