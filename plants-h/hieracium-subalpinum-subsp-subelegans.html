Hieracium subalpinum subsp. subelegans

Hieracium subalpinum subsp.

subelegans, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or open grassy habitats at high elevations.

The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow flowers in early summer. Its leaves are hairy and have a distinctive lobed shape, while the flowers are arranged in a single head.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and is adapted to withstand cold and harsh mountain environments.
