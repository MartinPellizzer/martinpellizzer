Hypericum tinctorum

Hypericum tinctorum, commonly known as St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and opposite, linear leaves. The plant contains bioactive compounds such as hypericin and hyperforin, which contribute to its medicinal properties. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its historical use in herbal medicine dates back to ancient times.
