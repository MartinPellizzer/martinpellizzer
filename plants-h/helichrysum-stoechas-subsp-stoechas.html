Helichrysum stoechas subsp. stoechas

Helichrysum stoechas subsp.

stoechas, commonly known as the "Golden Geranium," is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and its clusters of small, bright yellow to orange flowers.

The plant produces papery, persistent bracts that remain colorful even after the flowers have faded, giving it a striking appearance in dry conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The species is also valued for its aromatic foliage and ornamental appeal.
