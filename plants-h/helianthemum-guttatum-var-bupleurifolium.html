Helianthemum guttatum var. bupleurifolium

Helianthemum guttatum var. bupleurifolium, commonly known as the bupleurifolium stonecrop, is a low-growing succulent native to southern Europe.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a bright touch to rocky or gravelly habitats. Its compact growth habit makes it well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The variety is distinguished by its broader, more lobed leaves compared to the typical form of Helianthemum guttatum.
