Hieracium waldsteinii subsp. sublanifolium

Hieracium waldsteinii subsp.

sublanifolium, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and southern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The subspecies sublanifolium is distinguished by its dense, woolly pubescence on the leaves and stems. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky soils, preferring well-drained conditions.

This plant is often found in open habitats and can thrive in moderate to high elevations.
