Hemionitis gymnopteroidea

Hemionitis gymnopteroidea, commonly known as the naked-winged hemionitis, is a rare and specialized fern belonging to the family Hemionitidaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically short and clustered, with a unique structure that sets it apart from other ferns. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in rocky or mountainous regions. Its reproductive structures, including spore-producing sorus, are positioned on the underside of the fronds.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
