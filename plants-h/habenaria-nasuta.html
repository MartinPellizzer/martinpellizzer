Habenaria nasuta

Habenaria nasuta, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and linear, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, often with a faintly fragrant scent. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically in forest understories.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
