Hydrosme

Hydrosme, commonly known as the water lily, is a aquatic plant that thrives in freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes.

It has broad, floating leaves that support the plant above the water surface, while its roots are submerged in the mud at the bottom. The flowers of Hydrosme are typically showy, with bright colors that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves have a waxy surface that helps repel water, allowing them to remain buoyant.

The plant plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen to surrounding water life.
