Hieracium venostorum

Hieracium venostorum, commonly known as the veined hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are alternate, rough, and often hairy, with a distinctive veining pattern.

The yellow flowers appear in clusters and bloom from June to August, attracting various pollinators.
