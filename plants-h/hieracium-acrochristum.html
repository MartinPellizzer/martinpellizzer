Hieracium acrochristum

Hieracium acrochristum, commonly known as the golden hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in summer, and the plant has hairy stems and leaves.

It is often considered a weed but plays a role in supporting pollinators.
