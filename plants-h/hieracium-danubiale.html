Hieracium danubiale

Hieracium danubiale, commonly known as the Danube hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and along riverbanks, thriving in moist to moderately dry soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its yellow flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

Hieracium danubiale is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in agricultural areas.
