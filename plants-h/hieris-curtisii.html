Hieris curtisii

Hieris curtisii, commonly known as the Curtis's thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and large, silvery-green leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Hieris curtisii thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically valuable species in its native environment.
