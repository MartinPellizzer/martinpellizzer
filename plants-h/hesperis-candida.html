Hesperis candida

Hesperis candida, commonly known as the white sweet rocket, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale purple, arranged in clusters atop branched stems. Hesperis candida is an annual or biennial herb that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and ecological restoration projects.
