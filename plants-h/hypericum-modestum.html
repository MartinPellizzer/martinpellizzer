Hypericum modestum

Hypericum modestum, commonly known as St. John's Wort, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant contains bioactive compounds such as hypericin and hyperforin, which contribute to its medicinal properties. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or meadows.

Its flowering period typically occurs in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
