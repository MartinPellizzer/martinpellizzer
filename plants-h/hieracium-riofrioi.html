Hieracium riofrioi

Hieracium riofrioi, commonly known as the Rio Frioli hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its yellowish-green flower heads and hairy stems, typical of many hawkweed species. Native to specific regions in Europe, it thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and grasslands. The plant has small, spoon-shaped leaves and produces numerous tiny florets arranged in a composite flower head.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the genus Hieracium.
