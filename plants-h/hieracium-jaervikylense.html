Hieracium jaervikylense

Hieracium jaervikylense, commonly known as the Jaervikylen hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to specific regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows and grassy areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium jaervikylense is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
