Hirtella glaziovii

Hirtella glaziovii, commonly known as the Glaziov's hirtella, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and features glossy, elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, red berries. Its foliage is dense and often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive appearance.

Hirtella glaziovii prefers shady, moist environments and is well-suited for tropical and subtropical climates.
