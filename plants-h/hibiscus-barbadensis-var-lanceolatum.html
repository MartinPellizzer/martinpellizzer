Hibiscus barbadensis var. lanceolatum

Hibiscus barbadensis var. lanceolatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers bloom throughout the growing season, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
