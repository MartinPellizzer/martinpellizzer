Heliconia distans

Heliconia distans, commonly known as the red heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces inflorescences that are often used in floral arrangements due to their vibrant appearance. Heliconia distans thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
