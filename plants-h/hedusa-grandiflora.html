Hedusa grandiflora

Hedusa grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered hedusa, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems that are hairy and can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces clusters of showy, blue to violet flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Hedusa grandiflora is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
