Hieracium murorum subsp. melamphaes

Hieracium murorum subsp.

melamphaes, commonly known as the blackfoot hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dark purple to black basal leaves and yellow flower heads.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dark pigmentation and specific habitat preferences.
