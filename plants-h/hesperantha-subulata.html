Hesperantha subulata

Hesperantha subulata, commonly known as the needleleaf hesperantha, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Hesperantha subulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
