Haworthia semimargaritifera var. multiperla

Haworthia semimargaritifera var. multiperla, commonly known as the Zulu beauty, is a compact, low-growing succulent native to South Africa.

It features dense, fleshy rosettes with dark green leaves adorned with white tubercles and translucent windows. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers on tall, erect stems during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for rock gardens or indoor cultivation.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
