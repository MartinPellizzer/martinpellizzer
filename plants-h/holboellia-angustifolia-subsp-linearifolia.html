Holboellia angustifolia subsp. linearifolia

Holboellia angustifolia subsp.

linearifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf holboellia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features slender, linear leaves that are often arranged in a basal rosette, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in alpine flora studies.
