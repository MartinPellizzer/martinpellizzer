Hieracium placotum

Hieracium placotum, commonly known as the lesser hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow composite flowers that attract pollinators. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. It has a perennial life cycle and is native to Europe and parts of Asia.

Its botanical features include hairy stems and alternate leaves with serrated edges.
