Hedysarum hamatum

Hedysarum hamatum, commonly known as the yellow peavine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trifoliate leaves with small, rounded leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a slender legume that splits open to release small, rounded seeds.

Hedysarum hamatum is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
