Hieracium elatum

Hieracium elatum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

Hieracium elatum is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in many regions.
