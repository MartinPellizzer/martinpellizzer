Hieracium inuloides subsp. praeacutum

Hieracium inuloides subsp.

praeacutum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is a low-growing plant that produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.
