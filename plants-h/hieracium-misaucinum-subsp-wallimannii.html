Hieracium misaucinum subsp. wallimannii

Hieracium misaucinum subsp.

wallimannii, commonly known as Wallimann's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in the summer months.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has finely divided, lance-shaped leaves and is native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
