Hieracium onychophoroides

Hieracium onychophoroides, commonly known as the red-tipped hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and have a rough texture, while its flowers are yellow with reddish-brown tips.

It is known for its aggressive growth and ability to form dense colonies, making it a common weed in agricultural and natural areas.
