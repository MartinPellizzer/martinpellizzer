Hieracium hastiforme

Hieracium hastiforme, commonly known as the hastate hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant features bright yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Hieracium hastiforme is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
