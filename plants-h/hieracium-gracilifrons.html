Hieracium gracilifrons

Hieracium gracilifrons, commonly known as the slender-fruited hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant often grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The plant is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in certain regions.
