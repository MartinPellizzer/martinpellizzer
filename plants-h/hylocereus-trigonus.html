Hylocereus trigonus

Hylocereus trigonus, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a climbing cactus belonging to the Cactaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, particularly Mexico and Central America. The plant produces large, fragrant, white flowers that open at night and remain open for only one night. Its stems are cylindrical and covered in spines, and it thrives in well-drained soil with plenty of sunlight.

The plant is popular for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its striking nocturnal blooms.
