Hemionitis sarmientoi

Hemionitis sarmientoi, commonly known as the Sarmiento's hemionitis, is a rare and unique fern species native to the arid regions of northern Chile.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperature fluctuations and low precipitation. Its reproductive structures, including spores, are produced on the undersides of its leaves, a common feature among ferns.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological sensitivity, Hemionitis sarmientoi is considered a species of conservation concern.
