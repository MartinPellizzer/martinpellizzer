Hibiscus townsvillensis

Hibiscus townsvillensis, commonly known as the Townsville hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to northern Queensland, Australia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a prominent yellow center. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters, with broad, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom throughout the year, though they are most abundant during the warmer months.

Hibiscus townsvillensis is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in full sun and well-drained soil.
