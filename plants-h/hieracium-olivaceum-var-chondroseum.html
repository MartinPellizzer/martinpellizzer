Hieracium olivaceum var. chondroseum

Hieracium olivaceum var. chondroseum, commonly known as the olive hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped with hairy surfaces, and the flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disc.

It is often found in regions with a Mediterranean climate, where it thrives in full sun.
