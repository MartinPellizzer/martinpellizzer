Hyptis procumbens

Hyptis procumbens, commonly known as Brazilian hyptis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, hairy stems, and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
