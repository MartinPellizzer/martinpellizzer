Hendecandra divaricata

Hendecandra divaricata, commonly known as the Malabar mountain bean, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and fragrant yellow flowers. The plant produces small, flat pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas at elevations ranging from 500 to 1,500 meters.

Hendecandra divaricata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
