Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp. pollinense

Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp.

pollinense, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant has bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while the stems are upright and somewhat hairy.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ability to produce a large number of seeds.
