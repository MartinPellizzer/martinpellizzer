Helianthus stuebelii

Helianthus stuebelii, commonly known as Stuebel's sunflower, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to the central and southern United States, where it typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and sandy soils. The plant features tall, slender stems with large, yellow-orange composite flower heads that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it produces numerous small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Helianthus stuebelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
