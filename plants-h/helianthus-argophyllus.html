Helianthus argophyllus

Helianthus argophyllus, commonly known as the silverleaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, yellow-orange composite flower heads. The leaves are typically silver or gray-green, covered in fine, soft hairs that give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in arid environments.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
