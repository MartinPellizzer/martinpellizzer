Hypericum arenarioides

Hypericum arenarioides, commonly known as sand St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has woody stems and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Its foliage is aromatic and has been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
