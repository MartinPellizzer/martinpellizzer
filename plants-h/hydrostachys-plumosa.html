Hydrostachys plumosa

Hydrostachys plumosa, commonly known as the plumose stachys, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wet or marshy habitats. The plant features slender, hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its most distinctive characteristic is its feathery, plume-like inflorescence, which consists of numerous small, white or pale blue flowers.

Hydrostachys plumosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in water gardens or bog gardens.
