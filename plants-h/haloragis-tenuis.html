Haloragis tenuis

Haloragis tenuis, commonly known as slender waterwheel, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes and lake margins. The plant has slender, erect stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are arranged in whorls along the stems and are usually white or pale blue in color.

Haloragis tenuis plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
