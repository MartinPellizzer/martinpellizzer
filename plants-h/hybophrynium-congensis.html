Hybophrynium congensis

Hybophrynium congensis, commonly known as the Congo moon vine, is a climbing plant native to Central Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces fleshy, red berries that are edible and often consumed by local communities. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens in warm regions.
