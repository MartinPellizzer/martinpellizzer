Hieracium brevipilosum

Hieracium brevipilosum, commonly known as the short-hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and forest edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped with hairy surfaces, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Hieracium brevipilosum is often found in temperate climates and is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.
