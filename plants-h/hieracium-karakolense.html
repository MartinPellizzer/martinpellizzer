Hieracium karakolense

Hieracium karakolense, commonly known as Karakol hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in grasslands and mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves and produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a serrated edge, while the stems are upright and branching.

Hieracium karakolense is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
