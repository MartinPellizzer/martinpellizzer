Hunteria corymbosa

Hunteria corymbosa, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow inflorescences that appear in clusters, giving it a fiery appearance. The plant produces small, green, oval-shaped fruits that contain seeds surrounded by a fleshy, reddish pulp. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The species is also valued for its rapid growth and ability to provide shade in warm climates.
