Hieracium rostanii subsp. bedolense

Hieracium rostanii subsp.

bedolense, commonly known as the Bedolense Hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry meadows and rocky areas, preferring sunny to partially shaded conditions. It has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces numerous small florets arranged in a composite head.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
