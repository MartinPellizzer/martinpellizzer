Heliconia boultoniana

Heliconia boultoniana, commonly known as the Boulton's heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its large, colorful bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Heliconia boultoniana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
