Hypericum leprosum var. bourgaei

Hypericum leprosum var. bourgaei, commonly known as the dwarf St. John's wort, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy. The plant has a compact growth habit, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique and hardy addition to diverse garden settings.
