Hieracium cistiernense

Hieracium cistiernense, commonly known as the Cisternense hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

It is often found in areas with poor soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
