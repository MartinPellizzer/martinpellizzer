Humiria cuspidata

Humiria cuspidata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved humiria, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale green, which bloom during the warmer months. Humiria cuspidata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
