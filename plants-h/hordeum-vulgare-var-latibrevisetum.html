Hordeum vulgare var. latibrevisetum

Hordeum vulgare var. latibrevisetum, commonly known as the slender barley, is a variant of barley characterized by its elongated, slender spikelets and reduced bracts.

This variety is typically found in temperate regions and is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types, though it prefers well-drained conditions. The plant produces small, three-lobed grains that are usually harvested for food or animal feed. Its botanical structure includes a compact inflorescence with spikelets that are shorter than those of typical barley varieties.

This subspecies is of interest in agricultural research due to its potential for improved yield and resilience in certain climates.
