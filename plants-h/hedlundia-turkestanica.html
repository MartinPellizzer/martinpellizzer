Hedlundia turkestanica

Hedlundia turkestanica, commonly known as the Turkestan hedgebean, is a perennial shrub native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by the development of its distinctive, elongated seed pods.

Hedlundia turkestanica is valued for its hardy nature and potential use in ecological restoration projects.
