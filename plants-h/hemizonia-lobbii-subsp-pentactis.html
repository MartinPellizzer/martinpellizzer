Hemizonia lobbii subsp. pentactis

Hemizonia lobbii subsp.

pentactis, commonly known as the Cape goldflax, is a perennial herb native to South Africa. It features showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
