Hieracium piliferum subsp. fuliginatum

Hieracium piliferum subsp.

fuliginatum, commonly known as the sooty hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides. Its leaves are rough and hairy, with a distinctive dark green color, and the flowers are yellow with a central purple or brown center.

The subspecies fuliginatum is distinguished by its darker foliage and more robust growth compared to other forms of Hieracium piliferum.
