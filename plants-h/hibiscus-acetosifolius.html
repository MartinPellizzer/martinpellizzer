Hibiscus acetosifolius

Hibiscus acetosifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Hibiscus acetosifolius is also valued for its use in traditional medicine in some cultures.
