Hieracium fuscum subsp. variegatum

Hieracium fuscum subsp.

variegatum, commonly known as the variegated hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it can reach heights of up to 30 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are upright and often branched.

This subspecies is notable for its variegated leaf patterns, which can include white or cream markings.
