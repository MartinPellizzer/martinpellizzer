Hebe haastii var. humilis

Hebe haastii var. humilis, commonly known as the dwarf hebe, is a compact shrub native to New Zealand.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green, often with a slight blue or silver hue. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, or white during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in temperate climates.
