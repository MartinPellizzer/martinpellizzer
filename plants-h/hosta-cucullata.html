Hosta cucullata

Hosta cucullata, commonly known as the hooded hosta, is a species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and yellow margins. The plant produces upright, bell-shaped flowers that are typically lavender or blue in color. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Hosta cucullata is a popular ornamental plant in gardens due to its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
