Heteranthera peduncularis

Heteranthera peduncularis, commonly known as the slender spurred orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, clustered inflorescences. Its botanical features include specialized floral structures that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Heteranthera peduncularis is often found in marshy or swampy habitats, where it thrives in the moist, nutrient-rich environments.
