Hieracium maculatum f. pilosiceps

Hieracium maculatum f. pilosiceps, commonly known as the hairy-leaved buttercup, is a variant of the common buttercup.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and hairy foliage. The leaves are typically lobed and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a unique texture. It thrives in moist, grassy habitats and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

The plant is part of the Asteraceae family and plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
