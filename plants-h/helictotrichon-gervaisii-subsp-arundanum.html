Helictotrichon gervaisii subsp. arundanum

Helictotrichon gervaisii subsp.

arundanum, commonly known as the purple needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves with a bluish-green color.

The plant produces spikelets that are typically purple or reddish in color, which are its most distinctive botanical feature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and prairie habitats.

This species is valued for its role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
