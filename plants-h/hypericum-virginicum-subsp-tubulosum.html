Hypericum virginicum subsp. tubulosum

Hypericum virginicum subsp.

tubulosum, commonly known as the slender St. John's wort, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodland edges and wet meadows.

The plant features slender, ascending stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer, are composed of four petals and numerous stamens.

The plant is characterized by its distinctive tubular corolla and is often found in sandy or loamy soils.
