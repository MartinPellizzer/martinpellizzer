Hieracium erythrocarpum subsp. karagoellense

Hieracium erythrocarpum subsp.

karagoellense, commonly known as the Karagoellen hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry meadows and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are yellow to orange in color, and it produces small, hairy fruits that disperse its seeds.

It is a relatively rare subspecies with limited geographic distribution.
