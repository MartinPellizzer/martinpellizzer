Hieracium murorum subsp. longilacerum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

longilacerum, commonly known as the long-stemmed hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and basal rosette of leaves.

The subspecies is distinguished by its elongated, slender stems and finely divided leaves. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

This plant is often found in disturbed areas and can thrive in full sun.
