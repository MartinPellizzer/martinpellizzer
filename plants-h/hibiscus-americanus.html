Hibiscus americanus

Hibiscus americanus, commonly known as the American hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and often covered in fine hairs.

Hibiscus americanus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
