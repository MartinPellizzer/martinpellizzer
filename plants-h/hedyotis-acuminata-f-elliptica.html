Hedyotis acuminata f. elliptica

Hedyotis acuminata f. elliptica, commonly known as the elliptic trailing geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The leaves are elliptical, opposite, and usually have a slightly hairy surface. Small, white to pale pink flowers are borne in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.
