Hieracium ageratoides

Hieracium ageratoides, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a cluster of small flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its yellowish-orange flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and the leaves are lance-shaped with rough textures.

Hieracium ageratoides is often found in sunny environments and can spread through rhizomes or seeds.
