Hyptis fasciculata var. fastigiata

Hyptis fasciculata var. fastigiata, commonly known as the upright hyptis, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its narrow, upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically clustered in dense spikes, usually in shades of blue or purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it a valuable addition to both ecological and horticultural landscapes.
