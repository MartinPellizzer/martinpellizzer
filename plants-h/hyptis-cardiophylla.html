Hyptis cardiophylla

Hyptis cardiophylla, commonly known as the Mexican mint or blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. Its leaves are often aromatic and have a mint-like scent, which is typical of the genus Hyptis.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and fragrance.
