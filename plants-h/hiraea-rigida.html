Hiraea rigida

Hiraea rigida, commonly known as the rigid hiraea, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Hiraea rigida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
