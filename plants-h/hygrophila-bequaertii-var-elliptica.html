Hygrophila bequaertii var. elliptica

Hygrophila bequaertii var. elliptica, commonly known as the elliptical hygrophila, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along its stem. This plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and streams, where it can receive adequate sunlight. Its foliage is often used in aquariums due to its attractive appearance and ability to provide oxygenation.

The plant also plays a role in maintaining water quality by absorbing nutrients and reducing algae growth.
