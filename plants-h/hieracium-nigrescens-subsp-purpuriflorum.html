Hieracium nigrescens subsp. purpuriflorum

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

purpuriflorum, commonly known as the purple-flowered hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright stems and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant produces numerous yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, with some flowers displaying a distinctive purple hue. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
