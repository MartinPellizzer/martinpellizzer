Hieracium flagellare subsp. glaucochlorum

Hieracium flagellare subsp.

glaucochlorum, commonly known as the blue-green hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring moist and sunny conditions. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with ray florets.

The subspecies is distinguished by its bluish-green leaves and a more compact growth habit compared to other hawkweed species.
