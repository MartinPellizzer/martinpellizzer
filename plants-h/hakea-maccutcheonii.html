Hakea maccutcheonii

Hakea maccutcheonii, commonly known as the Maccutcheon's hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, woody structure and distinctive, cylindrical or spiky flower spikes that typically appear in late winter to early spring. The leaves are usually narrow and linear, often with a waxy coating, and the plant produces small, hard seeds enclosed in follicles. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and semi-arid environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
