Hieracium macilentum subsp. wimmeri

Hieracium macilentum subsp.

wimmeri, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.

The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in a single terminal capitulum. Its flowering period occurs from late spring to early summer, and it is characterized by its simple, linear leaves and absence of leaf hairs.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads.
