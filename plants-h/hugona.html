Hugona

Hugona, commonly known as the sacred orchid of the Andes, is a rare and ethically significant plant native to high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often iridescent flowers that come in shades of violet and blue. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and requires specific soil conditions to flourish. Due to its cultural and ecological importance, it is protected in many regions.

Its botanical study is crucial for understanding biodiversity in alpine ecosystems.
