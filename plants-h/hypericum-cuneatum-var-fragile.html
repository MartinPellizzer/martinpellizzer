Hypericum cuneatum var. fragile

Hypericum cuneatum var. fragile, commonly known as the fragrant St. John's wort, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its stems are generally slender and can be somewhat woody with age.

It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a hardy addition to native gardens.
