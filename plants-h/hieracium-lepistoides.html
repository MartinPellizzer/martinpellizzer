Hieracium lepistoides

Hieracium lepistoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often hairy, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is an herbaceous perennial that can spread through rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
