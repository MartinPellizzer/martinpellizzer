Helianthemum alyssoides var. vulgare

Helianthemum alyssoides var. vulgare, commonly known as the common rockrose, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer, adding a delicate floral display to rocky or coastal landscapes. The plant has aromatic, glandular leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping and coastal gardens.

Its hardy nature and ability to withstand drought conditions make it a resilient choice for ornamental and ecological planting.
