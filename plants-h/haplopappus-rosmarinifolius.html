Haplopappus rosmarinifolius

Haplopappus rosmarinifolius, commonly known as silverleaf sage, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and aromatic leaves that emit a strong mint-like scent. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal remedies for their purported medicinal properties.

Haplopappus rosmarinifolius is well adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it a hardy plant in xeriscaped gardens.
