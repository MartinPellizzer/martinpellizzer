Helleborus viridis var. pallidus

Helleborus viridis var. pallidus, commonly known as the pale green hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that are heart-shaped and often have a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of pale green to white flowers with prominent, hooded petals, which bloom in late winter to early spring. It prefers shady, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its unique foliage and early blooming habit.
