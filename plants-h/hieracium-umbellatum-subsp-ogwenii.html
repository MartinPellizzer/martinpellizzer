Hieracium umbellatum subsp. ogwenii

Hieracium umbellatum subsp.

ogwenii, commonly known as the Ogwen yellow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to western Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish-orange composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland and grassland habitats. It has basal and stem leaves that are lance-shaped with hairy surfaces, and its flowering heads are surrounded by a series of bracts.

This subspecies is often found in the British Isles, particularly in Wales and northern England.
