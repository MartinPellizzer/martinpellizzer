Hieracium caespiticola

Hieracium caespiticola, commonly known as the clustered hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically forms dense, low-growing clusters, with hairy stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped and serrated. Its flowers are yellow and appear in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators.

Hieracium caespiticola is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it can spread aggressively through rhizomes.
