Hieracium arragonense subsp. microsphaerellum

Hieracium arragonense subsp.

microsphaerellum, commonly known as the small globe hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine soils, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its flowers are yellow, with a distinctive globular shape, and it produces small, dark brown seeds in a pappus.

It is often found in meadows and open woodlands at higher elevations.
