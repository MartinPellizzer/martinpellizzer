Hieracium wrightii var. genuinum

Hieracium wrightii var. genuinum, commonly known as Wright's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to orange composite flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The inflorescence consists of a single central flower surrounded by bracts, and it produces small, oval seeds that are dispersed by wind.
