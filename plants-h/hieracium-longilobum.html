Hieracium longilobum

Hieracium longilobum, commonly known as the long-lobed hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and tall, slender flowering stems that bear yellow daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with serrated edges, and the flowers consist of both ray and disk florets.

Hieracium longilobum is often found in temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.
