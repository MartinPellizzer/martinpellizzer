Heisteria macrophylla

Heisteria macrophylla, commonly known as the broad-leaved heister, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 10 inches in length. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Heisteria macrophylla is often found in wetlands and along stream banks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
