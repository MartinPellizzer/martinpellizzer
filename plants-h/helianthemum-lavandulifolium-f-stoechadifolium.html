Helianthemum lavandulifolium f. stoechadifolium

Helianthemum lavandulifolium f. stoechadifolium, commonly known as the Stoechadifolium form of the fragrant thyme-honeyflower, is a compact, low-growing shrub native to the Scottish Highlands.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The leaves are narrow and aromatic, with a strong, minty scent, and the plant thrives in well-drained, sunny conditions. It is often found in rocky or coastal habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness.

This variety is a rare and distinctive form within the Helianthemum genus, showcasing unique foliage characteristics.
