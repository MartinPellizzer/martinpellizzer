Hibbertia hermanniifolia subsp. hermanniifolia

Hibbertia hermanniifolia subsp.

hermanniifolia, commonly known as the Hermann's hibbertia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia. It is a shrub characterized by its slender, ascending stems and yellow flowers with prominent, showy stamens.

The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, and the plant thrives in well-drained soils in full sun. It is often found in heathlands and open forests, and it produces capsule fruits that disperse its seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in native plant communities.
