Helogyne virgata

Helogyne virgata, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands and coastal areas of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet, often with a distinctive red throat. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody at the base.

Helogyne virgata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
