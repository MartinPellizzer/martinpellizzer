Habenaria elliottii

Habenaria elliottii, commonly known as the Elliott's butterfly orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. The plant features delicate, butterfly-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white in color. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
