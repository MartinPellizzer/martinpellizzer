Helianthemum italicum var. montserratense

Helianthemum italicum var. montserratense, commonly known as the Montserrat stonecrop, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically gray-green in color, adapted to arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for Mediterranean-style landscapes.
