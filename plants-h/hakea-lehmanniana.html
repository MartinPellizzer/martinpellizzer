Hakea lehmanniana

Hakea lehmanniana, commonly known as the Lehmann's hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical or club-shaped leaves that are typically green to grayish in color. The plant produces striking, urn-shaped flower spikes that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and Mediterranean climates.

Hakea lehmanniana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
