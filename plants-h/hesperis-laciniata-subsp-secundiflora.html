Hesperis laciniata subsp. secundiflora

Hesperis laciniata subsp.

secundiflora, commonly known as the scarlet pimpernel, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small, bright yellow flowers that open in the morning and close in the afternoon.

Native to the Mediterranean region, this plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. It is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, and it is known for its rapid growth and short blooming period.

The plant has been cultivated for its ornamental value and historical association with the Napoleonic Wars.
