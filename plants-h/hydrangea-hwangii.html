Hydrangea hwangii

Hydrangea hwangii, commonly known as the Korean hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to South Korea.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range from white to pink, depending on soil pH. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and produces clusters of small, white flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

Hydrangea hwangii thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
