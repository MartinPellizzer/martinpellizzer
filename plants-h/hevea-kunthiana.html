Hevea kunthiana

Hevea kunthiana, commonly known as the wild rubber tree, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant is notable for its ability to produce latex, which is a key component in natural rubber production. It typically grows as a medium to large shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters.

Due to its ecological and economic significance, it is often studied for its potential in sustainable rubber cultivation.
