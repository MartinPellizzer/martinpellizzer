Hariota villigera

Hariota villigera, commonly known as the red-stemmed broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, characterized by its slender, reddish stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Hariota villigera is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
