Hypertelis salsoloides var. mossamedensis

Hypertelis salsoloides var. mossamedensis, commonly known as the Mossamedes saltbush, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline and arid environments.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and saline soils, exhibiting a low, spreading growth habit. Its small, greenish flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it is well-suited for revegetation in degraded lands.

This variant is native to southern Africa, particularly Namibia and Angola.
