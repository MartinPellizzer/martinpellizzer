Hamamelis japonica var. bitchuensis

Hamamelis japonica var. bitchuensis, commonly known as the Japanese witch hazel, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its striking orange-yellow flowers that bloom in late autumn or early winter. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that turn reddish in the fall, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture and ecological landscaping.
