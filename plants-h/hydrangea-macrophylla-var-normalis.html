Hydrangea macrophylla var. normalis

Hydrangea macrophylla var. normalis, commonly known as the bigleaf hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy, mophead or lacecap flowers that can range in color from pink to blue depending on soil pH. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight. Its foliage is dark green and often turns red or orange in the fall.

This variety is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
