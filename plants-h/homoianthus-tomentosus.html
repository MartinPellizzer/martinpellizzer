Homoianthus tomentosus

Homoianthus tomentosus, commonly known as the woolly sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive, soft appearance. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered closely together.

Homoianthus tomentosus plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various invertebrates and birds.
