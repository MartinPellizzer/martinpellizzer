Hieracium prussicum subsp. scharlokianum

Hieracium prussicum subsp.

scharlokianum, commonly known as the Prussian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering heads that produce yellow ray florets.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as meadows and grasslands. It has a fibrous root system and produces numerous flowering stems that can reach up to 30 cm in height.

This subspecies is native to Central and Eastern Europe and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.
