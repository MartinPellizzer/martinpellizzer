Helianthus douglasii

Helianthus douglasii, commonly known as Douglas's sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its large, showy yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces seeds in a distinctive disk-like structure.

Douglas's sunflower is adapted to arid environments and is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value.
