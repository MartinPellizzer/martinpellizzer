Hieracium atrisetum

Hieracium atrisetum, commonly known as the black-hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features basal leaves that are hairy and often have a reddish or purplish hue. Its flowers are typically yellow with dark centers, and it produces small, oval-shaped seeds.

Hieracium atrisetum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
