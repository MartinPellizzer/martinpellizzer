Hyoscyamus kopetdaghi

Hyoscyamus kopetdaghi, commonly known as the Kopetdag belladonna, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or white. The plant produces berries that contain toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Due to its toxicity, it is not commonly cultivated but is of interest in botanical and pharmacological studies.
