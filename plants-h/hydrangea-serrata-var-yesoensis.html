Hydrangea serrata var. yesoensis

Hydrangea serrata var. yesoensis, commonly known as the Yesso hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from white to pink, depending on soil pH. The plant features serrated leaves that are typically dark green during the growing season. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to provide seasonal interest with its foliage and flowers.
