Hypericum roberti var. hispanicum

Hypericum roberti var. hispanicum, commonly known as the Spanish St. John's Wort, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features small, yellow, four-petaled flowers that bloom in summer, typically from June to August. The plant has woody stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This variety is closely related to other Hypericum species and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
