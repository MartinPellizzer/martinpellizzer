Hydrangea macrophylla f. caesia

Hydrangea macrophylla f. caesia, commonly known as the blue hydrangea, is a cultivar of the hydrangea plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy flower heads that typically range in color from blue to pink, depending on soil pH. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight. Its foliage is dark green, and the flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to change color with environmental conditions.
