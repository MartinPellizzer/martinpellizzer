Hibiscus religiosus

Hibiscus religiosus, commonly known as the sacred hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its flowers bloom in clusters.

Hibiscus religiosus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in various regions.
