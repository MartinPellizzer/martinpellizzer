Helichrysum chrysocephalum

Helichrysum chrysocephalum, commonly known as the goldenhead everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and its showy, yellow-orange inflorescences that resemble small suns. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically visited by bees and other pollinators. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Helichrysum chrysocephalum is also valued for its ornamental use in gardens and for its potential medicinal properties.
