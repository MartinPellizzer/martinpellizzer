Helichrysum purdiei

Helichrysum purdiei, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that remain vibrant even when dried. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are clustered in a flat, rounded head, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Helichrysum purdiei is also valued for its ornamental use and is often used in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms.
