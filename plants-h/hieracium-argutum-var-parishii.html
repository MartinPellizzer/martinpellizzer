Hieracium argutum var. parishii

Hieracium argutum var. parishii, commonly known as the Parish's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, dandelion-like seed heads.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a common weed in certain regions.
