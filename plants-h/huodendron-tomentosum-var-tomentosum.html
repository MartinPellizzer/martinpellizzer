Huodendron tomentosum var. tomentosum

Huodendron tomentosum var. tomentosum, commonly known as the woolly honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has small, oval leaves that are covered in a fine, white pubescence, giving it a soft, woolly appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

The plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
