Hopea ponga var. cauveriana

Hopea ponga var. cauveriana, commonly known as the Cauverian hopea, is a tropical tree species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant flowers. The tree produces woody fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a fleshy, orange-colored aril. It thrives in humid, lowland forests and is valued for its durable timber.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
