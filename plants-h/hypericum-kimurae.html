Hypericum kimurae

Hypericum kimurae, commonly known as the Korean St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces bright yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and streambanks. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Hypericum kimurae is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antidepressant properties.
