Hymenaea sagittipetala

Hymenaea sagittipetala, commonly known as the Brazilian wattle, is a deciduous tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its hard, heavy wood, which has been used historically for various purposes. It also produces a unique seed pod that splits open to release its seeds, aiding in its dispersal.

The tree is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
