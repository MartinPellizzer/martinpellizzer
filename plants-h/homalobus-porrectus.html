Homalobus porrectus

Homalobus porrectus, commonly known as the straight-stemmed milkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branching stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that attract various pollinators. Its milky sap, typical of the Apocynaceae family, contains toxic compounds that deter herbivores.

Homalobus porrectus is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils, showcasing its adaptability to different environmental conditions.
