Hyptis jaliscana

Hyptis jaliscana, commonly known as Mexican blue sage, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and culinary uses. The plant features square stems, opposite leaves, and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Hyptis jaliscana thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
