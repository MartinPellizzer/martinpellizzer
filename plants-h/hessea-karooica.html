Hessea karooica

Hessea karooica, commonly known as the Karoo hessea, is a succulent plant native to the Karoo region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring season, which are borne on short, branched inflorescences. Hessea karooica is adapted to arid conditions, storing water in its leaves and stems to survive dry periods.

It plays a role in the local ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
