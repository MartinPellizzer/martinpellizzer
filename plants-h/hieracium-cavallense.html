Hieracium cavallense

Hieracium cavallense, commonly known as the horse's thorn hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats, often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Hieracium cavallense is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in many regions.
