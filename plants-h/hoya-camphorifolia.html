Hoya camphorifolia

Hoya camphorifolia, commonly known as the camphor-leafed wax plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asclepiadaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often fragrant. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pink in color. Hoya camphorifolia is a trailing plant that thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.

It is popular among indoor gardeners for its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
