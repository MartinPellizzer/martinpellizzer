Hakea marriottii

Hakea marriottii, commonly known as the red hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red, woody fruits that remain on the plant for several years. The leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native wildlife.
