Helenium chihuahuensis

Helenium chihuahuensis, commonly known as the Chihuahua sneaker, is a flowering plant native to the Chihuahua Desert in Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant produces bright orange to red flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Helenium chihuahuensis is adapted to arid environments, featuring silver-green foliage and a compact growth habit.

Its unique coloration and drought tolerance make it a notable species in xerophytic plant communities.
