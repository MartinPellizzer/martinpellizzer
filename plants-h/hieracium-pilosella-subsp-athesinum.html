Hieracium pilosella subsp. athesinum

Hieracium pilosella subsp.

athesinum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the sunflower family (Asteraceae). It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, and it produces small, yellowish to brownish composite flowers.

This subspecies is typically found in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it has adapted to a wide range of environmental conditions.

The plant is often considered a weed but plays a role in supporting local insect populations.
