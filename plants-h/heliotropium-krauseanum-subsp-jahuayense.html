Heliotropium krauseanum subsp. jahuayense

Heliotropium krauseanum subsp.

jahuayense, commonly known as the Peruvian heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America. It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple and pink.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are often glandular, which may contribute to its aromatic properties. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
