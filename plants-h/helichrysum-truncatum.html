Helichrysum truncatum

Helichrysum truncatum, commonly known as the golden marguerite, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that resemble daisies. The leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving them a soft texture. This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in dry, sunny conditions.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
