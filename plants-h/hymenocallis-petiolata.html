Hymenocallis petiolata

Hymenocallis petiolata, commonly known as the slender hurricane lily, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to yellow. The plant produces showy, funnel-shaped blooms that are often fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soil with ample sunlight.

Hymenocallis petiolata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
