Hieracium caesioides subsp. liguricola

Hieracium caesioides subsp.

liguricola, commonly known as the Ligurian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellowish to orange flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has basal and upright stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves.

Its distribution is primarily in the Mediterranean region, where it thrives in mild, dry climates.
