Halothamnus schurobi var. hispidulus

Halothamnus schurobi var. hispidulus, commonly known as the hairy halothamnus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its succulent, branched stems and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces tiny, greenish flowers that are typically arranged in dense inflorescences. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing features such as thick cuticles and reduced leaf surfaces to minimize water loss.

This variety is native to regions of Central Asia and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
