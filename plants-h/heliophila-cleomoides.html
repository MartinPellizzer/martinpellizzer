Heliophila cleomoides

Heliophila cleomoides, commonly known as the cleome or spiderflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and showy, often yellow or white, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Heliophila cleomoides is part of the Cleomaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
