Hibiscus barbadensis

Hibiscus barbadensis, commonly known as the roselle, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red, and its heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces distinctive calyces that are used in culinary and medicinal applications. Hibiscus barbadensis thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the edible calyces used in teas and beverages.
