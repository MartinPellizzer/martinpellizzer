Hypacanthium echinopifolium

Hypacanthium echinopifolium, commonly known as the spiny-leaf hypacanthium, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its woody, shrub-like structure and is adapted to survive in dry, hot environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. Its leaves are often spiny, which helps to reduce water loss and deter herbivores.

Hypacanthium echinopifolium is also notable for its ability to store water in its stems, a key adaptation for its harsh habitat.
