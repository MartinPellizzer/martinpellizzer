Hieracium arragonense subsp. cataractarum

Hieracium arragonense subsp.

cataractarum, commonly known as the Cataract Water Hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils, often near streams or in wet meadows.

The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are usually hairy and have a bluish-green hue. Its flowers are small, yellowish, and arranged in a single, terminal head, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its ability to thrive in damp environments and its distinctive leaf morphology.
