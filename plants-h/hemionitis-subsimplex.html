Hemionitis subsimplex

Hemionitis subsimplex, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water clover, is a small aquatic plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It has delicate, heart-shaped leaves that are typically less than 1 inch in length. The plant thrives in shallow, still or slow-moving freshwater environments. Its leaves are usually arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers.

It is often found in wetlands and along the edges of ponds and streams.
