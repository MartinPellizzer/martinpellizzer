Hieracium subspeciosum subsp. pseudorupestre

Hieracium subspeciosum subsp.

pseudorupestre, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in dry, sunny habitats.

The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in late summer. Its inflorescence consists of a single, showy flower head with ray florets.

It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.
