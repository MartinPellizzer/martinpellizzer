Helichrysum pseudoferrugineum

Helichrysum pseudoferrugineum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that remain intact even after drying. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is gray-green with hairy leaves, and it produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
