Hieracium garibaldianum

Hieracium garibaldianum, commonly known as the Garibaldi hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

It is a common weed in agricultural fields and disturbed areas.
