Haageocereus australis f. nanus

Haageocereus australis f. nanus, commonly known as the dwarf Australian night-blooming cactus, is a compact, low-growing cactus native to arid regions of Australia.

It features a short, cylindrical stem with a greyish-green color and is covered in dense, white spines. The plant produces small, fragrant, white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It is adapted to survive in hot, dry environments with minimal water requirements.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is a valued plant in both horticulture and conservation efforts.
