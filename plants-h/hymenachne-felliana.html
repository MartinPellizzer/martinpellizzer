Hymenachne felliana

Hymenachne felliana, commonly known as the narrowleaf watergrass, is a perennial aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as marshes, ponds, and slow-moving streams. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in dense tufts at the base of the plant. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, which are usually submerged or floating on the water surface.

Hymenachne felliana plays an important role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems by preventing erosion and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
