Hisingera celastrina

Hisingera celastrina, commonly known as the red-leafed glorybower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a red or purple throat. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are an important food source for various bird species. Its leaves are typically opposite and have a glossy appearance, contributing to its ornamental value.

Hisingera celastrina thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in warm climates.
