Holmskioldia sanguinea var. flavescens

Holmskioldia sanguinea var. flavescens, commonly known as the golden flame lily, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking orange to yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, providing a lush green backdrop to the showy flowers.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
