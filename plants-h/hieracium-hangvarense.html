Hieracium hangvarense

Hieracium hangvarense, commonly known as the Hangvar yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its yellow flowers bloom from June to August, and it has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves.

It is considered a common weed but has ecological significance in supporting pollinators.
