Hieracium mandonii

Hieracium mandonii, commonly known as the Mandon's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range in color from yellow to orange.

Hieracium mandonii is noted for its ability to colonize disturbed areas and is often found in meadows and slopes at high altitudes.
