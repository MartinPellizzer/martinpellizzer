Hippocrepis rossetii

Hippocrepis rossetii, commonly known as the Rossetti's rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its flowers are usually violet or blue in color, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination by insects.

Hippocrepis rossetii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinator populations.
