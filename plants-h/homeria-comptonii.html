Homeria comptonii

Homeria comptonii, commonly known as the Compton's homeria, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often drought-tolerant species. The plant features fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. Its flowers are typically small and yellowish, appearing in clusters during the warmer months.

Homeria comptonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a houseplant.
