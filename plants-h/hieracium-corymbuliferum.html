Hieracium corymbuliferum

Hieracium corymbuliferum, commonly known as the clustered hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer, forming dense clusters at the top of the stems.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
