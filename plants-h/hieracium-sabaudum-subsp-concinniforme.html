Hieracium sabaudum subsp. concinniforme

Hieracium sabaudum subsp.

concinniforme, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaf arrangement.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
