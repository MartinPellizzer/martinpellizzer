Hieracium murorum subsp. persinuatum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

persinuatum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow flowers in composite inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. It has a hairy stem and leaves that are often glandular, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and is often found in temperate regions of Europe.
