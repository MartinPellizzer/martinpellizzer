Hieracium pseudolaeticeps

Hieracium pseudolaeticeps, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and it produces achenes as its fruits.

Hieracium pseudolaeticeps is often found in open habitats and can form dense colonies, sometimes becoming invasive in certain regions.
