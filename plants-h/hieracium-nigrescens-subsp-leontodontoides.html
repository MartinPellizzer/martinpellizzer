Hieracium nigrescens subsp. leontodontoides

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

leontodontoides, commonly known as the blackened hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves and produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in meadows and dry grasslands. It has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in well-drained soils.

Due to its distinctive appearance and ecological role, it is often noted in botanical surveys of its native habitats.
