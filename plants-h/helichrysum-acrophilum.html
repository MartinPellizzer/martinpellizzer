Helichrysum acrophilum

Helichrysum acrophilum, commonly known as the mountain everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes, thriving in temperate to cold climates. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
