Hygrophila nyassica

Hygrophila nyassica, commonly known as African water hyssop, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Africa.

It typically grows in shallow water, such as ponds and slow-moving streams, and has narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are often blue or purple in color. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in water gardens.

Hygrophila nyassica also plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
