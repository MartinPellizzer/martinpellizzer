Hibiscus vitifolius var. adhaerens

Hibiscus vitifolius var. adhaerens, commonly known as the creeping hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a waxy texture, while the stems are covered in fine hairs. This species thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical features make it both visually striking and ecologically beneficial to local wildlife.
