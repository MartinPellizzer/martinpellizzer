Haworthia venosa subsp. woolleyi

Haworthia venosa subsp.

woolleyi, commonly known as the Zulu candle or Woolley's Haworthia, is a compact, evergreen succulent native to South Africa. It features a rosette of thick, fleshy, pale green leaves with white to pinkish markings and a central spine.

The plant produces upright, candle-like inflorescences with white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or indoor cultivation.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
