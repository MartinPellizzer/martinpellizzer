Hieracium dentatum subsp. crispulifolium

Hieracium dentatum subsp.

crispulifolium, commonly known as the curly-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets.

The plant features finely toothed, sometimes curled leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, and its growth habit is typically low and sprawling.

This subspecies is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as meadows and roadsides.
