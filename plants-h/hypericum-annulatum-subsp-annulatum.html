Hypericum annulatum subsp. annulatum

Hypericum annulatum subsp.

annulatum, commonly known as the yellow-flowered St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer.

The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its stems are often reddish or purple-hued, especially when in bloom.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
