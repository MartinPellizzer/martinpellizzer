Hordeum gussoneanum

Hordeum gussoneanum, commonly known as the Gussone's barley, is a wild grass species native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, showcasing adaptability to various environmental conditions. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it possesses a fibrous root system.

Hordeum gussoneanum is of interest to botanists due to its genetic diversity and potential for ecological and agricultural research.
