Hypericum momoseanum var. atumense

Hypericum momoseanum var. atumense, commonly known as the Atumense St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, herbaceous growth habit and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in late summer. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticultural enthusiasts.
