Hemitelia setosa var. crenata

Hemitelia setosa var. crenata, commonly known as the serrated hemitelia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid forests.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
