Haplopappus racemosus var. praticola

Haplopappus racemosus var. praticola, commonly known as the clustered roughhairs, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and unique inflorescence structure. The plant features a basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence. Its flowering stems bear clusters of small, yellow disk flowers surrounded by pale green to white ray florets.

This variety is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and open habitats.
