Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp. acutiserratum

Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp.

acutiserratum, commonly known as the sharp-toothed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaf margins.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and roadsides. Its leaves are lance-shaped with acute, serrated edges, and it produces yellow flowers in clusters.

It is often considered a weed due to its rapid growth and ability to colonize various environments.
