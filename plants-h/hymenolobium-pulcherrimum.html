Hymenolobium pulcherrimum

Hymenolobium pulcherrimum, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is well adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

The golden wattle is also the floral emblem of Australia, symbolizing the country's natural beauty and biodiversity.
