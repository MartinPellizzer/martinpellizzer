Helictotrichon crassifolium

Helictotrichon crassifolium, commonly known as the thickleaf bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are thick and leathery. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny blue to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky slopes.

Helictotrichon crassifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
