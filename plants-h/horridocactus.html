Horridocactus

Horridocactus, commonly known as the devil's tongue cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, ribbed stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems. Its spines provide protection from herbivores and reduce water loss through transpiration.

Horridocactus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and resilience.
