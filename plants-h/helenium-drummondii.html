Helenium drummondii

Helenium drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's sneezeweed, is a flowering plant native to the central and southern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. Its yellow to orange flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Helenium drummondii is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
