Hieracium sotarense var. contracta

Hieracium sotarense var. contracta, commonly known as the contracted hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are basal, with a rough, hairy texture, and its flowers are yellow, often arranged in a single head.

It is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is considered a persistent weed in agricultural settings.
