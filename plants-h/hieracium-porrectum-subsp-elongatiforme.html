Hieracium porrectum subsp. elongatiforme

Hieracium porrectum subsp.

elongatiforme, commonly known as the elongated hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically has basal and stem leaves that are lanceolate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides.

Its flowering period occurs from late spring to early autumn, and it is often found in temperate climates.
