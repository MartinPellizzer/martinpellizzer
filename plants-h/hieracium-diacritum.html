Hieracium diacritum

Hieracium diacritum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers and basal rosette of leaves. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are often glandular, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is an herbaceous perennial that spreads through rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
