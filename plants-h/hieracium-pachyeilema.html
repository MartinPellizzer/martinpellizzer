Hieracium pachyeilema

Hieracium pachyeilema, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and basal rosettes of spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Hieracium pachyeilema is often found in disturbed areas and is relatively drought-tolerant.
