Hyphear tanakae

Hyphear tanakae, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that have a silvery underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white flowers that are fragrant and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a round, fleshy pod containing several seeds.

Hyphear tanakae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
