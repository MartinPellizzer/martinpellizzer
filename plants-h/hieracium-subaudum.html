Hieracium subaudum

Hieracium subaudum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy stems. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, preferring cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and clustered in a flat-topped inflorescence.

Hieracium subaudum is known for its resilience to harsh climatic conditions and is a common sight in high-altitude ecosystems.
