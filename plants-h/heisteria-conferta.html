Heisteria conferta

Heisteria conferta, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Mexican violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, textured appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the spring. Heisteria conferta is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and flowers.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.
