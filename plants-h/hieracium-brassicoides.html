Hieracium brassicoides

Hieracium brassicoides, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of yellowish-orange composite flowers. The plant typically has hairy stems and leaves that are lance-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Hieracium brassicoides is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in many regions.
