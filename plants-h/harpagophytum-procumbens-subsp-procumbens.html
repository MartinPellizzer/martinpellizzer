Harpagophytum procumbens subsp. procumbens

Harpagophytum procumbens subsp.

procumbens, commonly known as bitter wormwood, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America. It is characterized by its creeping growth habit and yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

The plant produces distinctive, twisted fruits that contain small, dark seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Its roots are the primary source of its medicinal compounds, which have been used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory properties.
