Hieracium styriacum

Hieracium styriacum, commonly known as the Styrian hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in grassy meadows and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range from yellow to reddish-orange in color.

Hieracium styriacum is often found in disturbed habitats and can spread vegetatively through its rhizomes.
