Herniaria arenaria var. hirsuta

Herniaria arenaria var. hirsuta, commonly known as hairy sand-hernia, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant that thrives in sandy and gravelly soils.

It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves that are covered in fine, hairy glands, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces tiny, white or pale yellow flowers throughout the summer months. It is often found in coastal dunes and other arid environments where it plays a role in stabilizing sand dunes.

This variety is adapted to harsh conditions, making it a resilient member of the Caryophyllaceae family.
