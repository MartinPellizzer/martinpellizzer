Hedera marginata-major

Hedera marginata-major, commonly known as the marginated ivy, is a climbing evergreen vine native to southern Europe.

It features dark green, lobed leaves with a distinctive white or pale margin, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark blue berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Hedera marginata-major is known for its adaptability and ability to climb walls or trees, making it a popular choice for vertical gardening.
