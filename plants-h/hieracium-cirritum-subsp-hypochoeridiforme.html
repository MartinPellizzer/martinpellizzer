Hieracium cirritum subsp. hypochoeridiforme

Hieracium cirritum subsp.

hypochoeridiforme, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow composite flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its narrow, elongated leaves and reduced flower heads compared to other hawkweed species. It typically thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
