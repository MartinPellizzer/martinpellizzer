Hieracium linguifrons

Hieracium linguifrons, commonly known as the broadleaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are broadly lance-shaped, and the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium linguifrons is often found in open habitats and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
