Himatanthus tarapotensis

Himatanthus tarapotensis, commonly known as the Andean starfruit, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and red, fleshy fruits that resemble small berries. The plant typically grows in high-altitude areas with cool, moist climates. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy coating.

The fruit is edible and has a sweet, aromatic flavor, making it a valuable resource in local ecosystems and traditional diets.
