Hieracium bifidum subsp. oligograptum

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

oligograptum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and often have a distinctive lobed or toothed margin, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a composite head.

This subspecies is notable for its relatively small size and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
