Hymenophyllum aeruginosum var. franklinianum

Hymenophyllum aeruginosum var. franklinianum, commonly known as Franklin's copper moss, is a rare and delicate fern belonging to the Hymenophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, intricate fronds that are typically less than 2 centimeters in length. The plant exhibits a unique metallic blue-green coloration due to the presence of specialized pigments in its tissues. It thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found on rock surfaces or tree trunks in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical significance lies in its rarity and its role as an indicator species for specific microhabitats.
