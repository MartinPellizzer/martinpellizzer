Hieracium hirsutum

Hieracium hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces yellow to orange composite flowers that attract pollinators. The plant has hairy leaves and stems, which give it its common name. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions.

Hieracium hirsutum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in a variety of environmental conditions.
