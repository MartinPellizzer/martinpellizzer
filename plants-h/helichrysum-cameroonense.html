Helichrysum cameroonense

Helichrysum cameroonense, commonly known as the Cameroon immortelle, is a flowering plant native to Cameroon and neighboring regions of Central Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces vibrant yellow to orange flowers that are often long-lasting, contributing to its common name. It is typically found in open woodland and grassland habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

Helichrysum cameroonense is also noted for its aromatic foliage, which contains essential oils used in traditional medicine and perfumery.
