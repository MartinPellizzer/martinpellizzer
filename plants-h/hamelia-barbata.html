Hamelia barbata

Hamelia barbata, commonly known as the scarlet flamebush, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet, featuring woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright orange-red tubular flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.

Hamelia barbata is often used in landscaping for its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
