Hieracium balbisianum

Hieracium balbisianum, commonly known as the Balbisian hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the daisy family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. It has hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence.

The flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn, producing a showy display that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
