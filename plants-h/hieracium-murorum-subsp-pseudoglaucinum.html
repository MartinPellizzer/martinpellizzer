Hieracium murorum subsp. pseudoglaucinum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

pseudoglaucinum, commonly known as the pale centaury, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and small, yellowish to reddish flowers arranged in clusters.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, whitish hairs, giving the plant a slightly silvery appearance. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

This subspecies is native to Europe and is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
