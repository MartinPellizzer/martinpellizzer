Hopea aspera

Hopea aspera, commonly known as the rough hopea, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in spikes, and it bears round, woody fruits containing one or two seeds. Hopea aspera is valued for its durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.

Its ecological importance lies in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species in its native forests.
