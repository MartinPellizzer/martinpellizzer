Hieracium parnassi

Hieracium parnassi, commonly known as the Parnassian hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its flowers are yellow or orange, with a distinctive central disk surrounded by narrow, petal-like leaves.

Hieracium parnassi is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
