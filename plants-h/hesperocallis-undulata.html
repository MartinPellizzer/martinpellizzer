Hesperocallis undulata

Hesperocallis undulata, commonly known as the wavering lily, is a striking plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the genus Hesperocallis, which is closely related to the lily family (Liliaceae). This perennial herb features tall, slender stems with large, showy white or pale pink flowers that have a distinctive undulating pattern on their petals. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its unique floral structure and adaptability make it a notable species in its native habitat.
