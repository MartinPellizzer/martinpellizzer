Hieracium scapophyllum

Hieracium scapophyllum, commonly known as the scapophyllum hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright, sparsely hairy stems and yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the upper surface.

Hieracium scapophyllum is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it is often found in temperate regions.
