Hymenophyllum balansae

Hymenophyllum balansae, commonly known as the silver moss, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of hair, giving it a silvery appearance. This plant is typically found in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rock crevices. Its small, sporangia are clustered in specialized structures called sorus, which are located on the underside of the leaves.

Hymenophyllum balansae is a cryptogamic plant that plays an important role in nutrient cycling in its ecosystem.
