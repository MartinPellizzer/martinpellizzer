Hedysarum loureiroi

Hedysarum loureiroi, commonly known as the yellow vetch, is a perennial legume native to regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features compound leaves with trifoliate or pinnate structures, and its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in racemes. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Hedysarum loureiroi is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
