Hieracium pseudopsepharum

Hieracium pseudopsepharum, commonly known as the false sand-verbena hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Hieracium pseudopsepharum is often found in rocky or dry soils, thriving in well-drained environments.
