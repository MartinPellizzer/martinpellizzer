Hyparrhenia hirta subsp. villosa

Hyparrhenia hirta subsp.

villosa, commonly known as the hairy hyparrhenia, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
