Hypericum ulugurense

Hypericum ulugurense, commonly known as the Uluguru hypericum, is a shrub native to the Uluguru Mountains in Tanzania.

It belongs to the family Hypericaceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and woody stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats within the montane forests of its native range. It has small, opposite leaves and produces orange-red berries that are an important food source for local wildlife.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
