Hieracium auriculoides subsp. submirum

Hieracium auriculoides subsp.

submirum, commonly known as the auricled hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in late summer. It has a taproot system and is often found in nutrient-rich soils.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and declining species in many regions.
