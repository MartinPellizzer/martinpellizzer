Habenaria schomburgkii

Habenaria schomburgkii, commonly known as the Schomburgk's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, leafy stems and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in late summer or early autumn. The plant produces small, greenish-white to pale yellow flowers with three sepals and three petals, one of which is often modified into a lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
