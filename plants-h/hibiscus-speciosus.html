Hibiscus speciosus

Hibiscus speciosus, commonly known as the showy hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet, featuring broad, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom from summer to fall. Hibiscus speciosus thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
