Hippeastrum gratissimum

Hippeastrum gratissimum, commonly known as the Amazonian belladonna lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It features large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, typically in shades of red, pink, or white. The plant produces fleshy, strap-like leaves that are often a deep green color. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers emit a strong, sweet fragrance, especially in the evening.
