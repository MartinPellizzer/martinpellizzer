Hyptidendron leucophyllum

Hyptidendron leucophyllum, commonly known as the white-leaved hyptidendron, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are usually pale green, and it produces pods that contain small, round seeds.

It is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
