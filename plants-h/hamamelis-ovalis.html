Hamamelis ovalis

Hamamelis ovalis, commonly known as the oval witch hazel, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late autumn or early winter. The plant has rough, ridged bark and lance-shaped leaves that turn reddish in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Hamamelis ovalis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has historical uses in herbal medicine.
