Holothrix usambarae

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its unique, hairy foliage and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically in forest understories. Its morphology includes a creeping stem with alternate leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs.
