Habenaria adolphi

Habenaria adolphi, commonly known as the Adolph's orchid, is a species of terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are typically pale green or yellowish in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
