Hieracium tridentatum

Hieracium tridentatum, commonly known as the three-toothed hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped with three distinct teeth, giving it its common name.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
