Hieracium pseudocorymbosum subsp. petryanum

Hieracium pseudocorymbosum subsp.

petryanum, commonly known as the purple hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features basal rosettes of dark green, hairy leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers with prominent central disks. Its inflorescence is characterized by a compact, corymbose arrangement of flowering heads.

The species is noted for its fibrous root system and its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
