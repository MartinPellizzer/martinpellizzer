Homeria pendula

Homeria pendula, commonly known as the weeping homeria, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its drooping clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has narrow, strap-like leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along its stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its unique appearance and continuous flowering make it a striking addition to any garden setting.
