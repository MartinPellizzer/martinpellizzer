Hypochaeris chillensis

Hypochaeris chillensis, commonly known as the Chilean mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer, followed by fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Hypochaeris chillensis is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
