Hieracium dispar

Hieracium dispar, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellowish to orange-yellow composite flowers, which are typically arranged in a flat-topped cluster. The plant has basal and upright leaves that are usually hairy and have a serrated edge. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Hieracium dispar is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions, including North America.
