Herpetophytum schlechteri

Herpetophytum schlechteri, commonly known as the African orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant that typically grows to a height of about 30 cm, featuring a rhizomatous stem and linear, lanceolate leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, which are often clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, moist soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
