Hymenophyllum adiantoides

Hymenophyllum adiantoides, commonly known as the silver maidenhair fern, is a delicate epiphytic fern native to tropical regions of Asia and Australia.

It belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae and is characterized by its small, closely spaced, and often overlapping fronds. The plant's leaves are typically dark green with a silvery underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on tree trunks or rocks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
