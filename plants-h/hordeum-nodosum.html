Hordeum nodosum

Hordeum nodosum, commonly known as nodding barley, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its distinctive nodding inflorescence, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are small and arranged in spikelets, which are clustered along the stem.

Hordeum nodosum is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
