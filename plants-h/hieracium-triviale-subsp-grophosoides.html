Hieracium triviale subsp. grophosoides

Hieracium triviale subsp.

grophosoides, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows. It has a taproot system and produces numerous flowering stems that bear small, yellow disk flowers.

The subspecies grophosoides is distinguished by its slightly hairy leaves and a more compact growth form compared to other hawkweed species.
