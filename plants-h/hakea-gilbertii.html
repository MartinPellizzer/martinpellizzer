Hakea gilbertii

Hakea gilbertii, commonly known as Gilbert's hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves and produces clusters of bright red flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, featuring a deep root system for water retention. It belongs to the Proteaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with similar adaptations.

Gilbert's hakea is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
