Hieracium × rubro-onegense

Hieracium × rubro-onegense, commonly known as the red-veined hawkweed, is a hybrid plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, erect stems and bright yellow flowers with reddish veins. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Due to its hybrid origin, it exhibits a variety of morphological traits that can vary between populations.
