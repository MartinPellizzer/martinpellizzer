Heymassoli inermis

Heymassoli inermis, commonly known as the smoothleaf heymassoli, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silky hair. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often blooming in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
