Heterotheca bolanderi

Heterotheca bolanderi, commonly known as Bolander's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky, arid environments and is often found in chaparral and sagebrush habitats.

Heterotheca bolanderi is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
