Haloragis exalata subsp. exalata

Haloragis exalata subsp.

exalata, commonly known as the slender waterwheel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in whorls along the stem, typical of the Haloragaceae family. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along watercourses.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable water conditions.
