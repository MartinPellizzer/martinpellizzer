Hieracium subgracile

Hieracium subgracile, commonly known as slender hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces small, yellowish to brownish composite flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, showing a preference for temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Hieracium subgracile is an invasive species in some regions, outcompeting native flora due to its rapid growth and prolific seed production.
