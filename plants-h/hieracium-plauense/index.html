Hieracium Plauense

Hieracium plauense, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the dandelion family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy stems. This plant often thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, making it a common sight in open landscapes. While it is not considered invasive in many regions, it can spread aggressively under certain conditions. Its presence is often a sign of healthy, undisturbed ecosystems.
