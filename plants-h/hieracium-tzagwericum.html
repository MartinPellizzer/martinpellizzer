Hieracium tzagwericum

Hieracium tzagwericum, commonly known as the Tzagweric Hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow daisy-like flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. Native to specific regions in southern Europe, it thrives in sunny, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, oval seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive if not controlled.
