Heliotropium albovillosum

Heliotropium albovillosum, commonly known as white vaneplant, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial species characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and waste areas.

The leaves and flowers are typically covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
