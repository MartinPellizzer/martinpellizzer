Hieracium glaucum

Hieracium glaucum, commonly known as the blue hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, powdery blue or gray coating, which gives the plant its common name.

The flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
