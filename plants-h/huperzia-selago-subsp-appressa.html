Huperzia selago subsp. appressa

Huperzia selago subsp.

appressa, commonly known as the small huperzia, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae. It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

The plant features simple, linear to ovate leaves that are often clustered at the base, with a distinctive appearance that resembles moss. It is typically found in forest undergrowth and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.

This subspecies is also noted for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting microfauna in its native habitats.
