Hygrophila volkensii

Hygrophila volkensii, commonly known as the African waterhyssop, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and spikes of small, fragrant, blue to violet flowers. The plant thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found near water bodies such as rivers and lakes. Its roots are fibrous and can help stabilize soil in aquatic ecosystems.

Hygrophila volkensii is also used in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
