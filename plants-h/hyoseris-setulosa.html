Hyoseris setulosa

Hyoseris setulosa, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Asia and North America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs, giving it its common name. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and also hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Hyoseris setulosa produces small yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators.
