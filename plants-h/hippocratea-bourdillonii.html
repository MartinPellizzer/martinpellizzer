Hippocratea bourdillonii

Hippocratea bourdillonii, commonly known as the Bourdillon's hippocratea, is a member of the Malvaceae family and is native to Madagascar.

It is a shrub characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, aiding in its dispersal.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
