Hieracium brachytrichum

Hieracium brachytrichum, commonly known as the short-haired hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are basal, rough, and covered in short hairs, while its flowering stems are usually unbranched.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators.
