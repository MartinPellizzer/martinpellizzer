Hieracium subspeciosum subsp. pseudoplantagineum

Hieracium subspeciosum subsp.

pseudoplantagineum, commonly known as the showy hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is native to parts of Europe and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. It has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

This subspecies is often mistaken for other hawkweed species due to its similar appearance.
