Helichrysum comosum

Helichrysum comosum, commonly known as the everlasting daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, fluffy yellow flowers that resemble daisies, which retain their color and form even after drying. The plant is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and chrysanthemums. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its resilience and ornamental value contribute to its widespread cultivation and use in horticulture.
