Hieracium integrifolium subsp. moravicum

Hieracium integrifolium subsp.

moravicum, commonly known as the Moravian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of broadly ovate to lanceolate leaves.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers arranged in a single head, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in moist meadows and woodland edges, often forming dense colonies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
