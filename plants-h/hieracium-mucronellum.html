Hieracium mucronellum

Hieracium mucronellum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a hairy texture, while its flowers are yellow and occur in dense clusters.

It is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
