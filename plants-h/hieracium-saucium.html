Hieracium saucium

Hieracium saucium, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and basal rosettes of heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies.

Hieracium saucium is native to Europe and has been introduced to other parts of the world, including North America.
