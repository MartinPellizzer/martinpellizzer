Hedysarum ketenoglui

Hedysarum ketenoglui, commonly known as the Ketenoglu vetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Turkey.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Hedysarum ketenoglui is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest for its potential in ecological restoration projects.
