Hieracium ericetorum

Hieracium ericetorum, commonly known as the heath hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils, often found in heathlands and woodland edges. The plant has hairy stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped with toothed edges. Its flowers are yellow, with a distinctive central disc and ray florets, blooming from June to August.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is considered a characteristic species of its habitat.
