Hibiscus sagittifolius var. septentrionalis

Hibiscus sagittifolius var. septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern arrowleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, arrow-like leaves that are typically green and slightly pubescent. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive yellow throat. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Hibiscus sagittifolius var. septentrionalis is a member of the Malvaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance.
