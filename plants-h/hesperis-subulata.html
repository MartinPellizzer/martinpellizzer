Hesperis subulata

Hesperis subulata, commonly known as the slender horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with distinctive four-petaled flowers. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically arranged in a loose cluster at the top of the stem. Its leaves are narrow and pointed, often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Hesperis subulata is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun.
