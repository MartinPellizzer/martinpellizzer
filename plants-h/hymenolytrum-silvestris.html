Hymenolytrum silvestris

Hymenolytrum silvestris, commonly known as the wild nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or white in color. The plant produces berries that are toxic to humans and animals, containing alkaloids such as solanine. Hymenolytrum silvestris is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable yet dangerous species in its native habitats.
