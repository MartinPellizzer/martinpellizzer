Hieracium bifidum subsp. subpendulum

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

subpendulum, commonly known as the hanging hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded conditions. Its flowers are yellow with pale green centers, and it produces small, elongated fruits called cypsela.
