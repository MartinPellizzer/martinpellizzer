Hibiscus angolensis

Hibiscus angolensis, commonly known as the Angola hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers. The flowers are typically pink to red in color and bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in woodland and savanna habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
