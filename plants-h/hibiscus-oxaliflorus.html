Hibiscus oxaliflorus

Hibiscus oxaliflorus, commonly known as the oxaliflorus hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically blooming during the dry season. The plant has heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves with a rough texture and prominent veins. Its stems are woody and often branched, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Hibiscus oxaliflorus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
