Hyptis hirsuta var. villosior

Hyptis hirsuta var. villosior, commonly known as hairy hyptis, is a perennial herb native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its foliage has a strong, mint-like aroma and is often used in traditional medicine.

Botanically, it belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its aromatic and medicinal properties.
