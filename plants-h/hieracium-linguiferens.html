Hieracium linguiferens

Hieracium linguiferens, commonly known as the tongue-shaped hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its stems are usually hairy.

Hieracium linguiferens is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural and natural areas.
