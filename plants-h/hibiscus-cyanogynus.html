Hibiscus cyanogynus

Hibiscus cyanogynus, commonly known as the blue female hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers, which are unique in color among hibiscus species. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are ovate and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers have five petals and a prominent, five-lobed stigma.

Hibiscus cyanogynus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its distinctive appearance.
