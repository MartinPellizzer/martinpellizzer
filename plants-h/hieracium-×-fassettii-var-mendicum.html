Hieracium × fassettii var. mendicum

Hieracium × fassettii var. mendicum, commonly known as the lesser hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is native to Europe and has been introduced to other parts of the world, often becoming invasive in certain regions. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its botanical features include hairy stems and leaves, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.
