Hieracium hartwegii

Hieracium hartwegii, commonly known as Hartweg's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

Its leaves are usually basal, with a rough, hairy texture, and the plant is often found in open, sunny habitats.
