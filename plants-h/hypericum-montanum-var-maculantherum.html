Hypericum montanum var. maculantherum

Hypericum montanum var. maculantherum, commonly known as the Alpine St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces bright yellow flowers with prominent orange stamens. The leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to thrive in cool, high-altitude environments. This variety is closely related to other Hypericum species, sharing similar medicinal properties and ecological roles.

It is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it contributes to biodiversity and soil stability.
