Hedysarum dazkiriense

Hedysarum dazkiriense, commonly known as the Dazkiri milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is capable of nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil enrichment.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
