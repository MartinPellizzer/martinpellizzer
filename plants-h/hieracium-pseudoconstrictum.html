Hieracium pseudoconstrictum

Hieracium pseudoconstrictum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
