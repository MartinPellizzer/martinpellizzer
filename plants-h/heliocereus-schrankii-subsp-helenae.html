Heliocereus schrankii subsp. helenae

Heliocereus schrankii subsp.

helenae, commonly known as the Helen's night bloomer, is a species of cactus native to Mexico. It is a climbing cactus that produces large, fragrant, white flowers that open at night.

The plant has thick, spiny stems and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are pollinated by moths and bats, which are attracted to its strong scent.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in warm climates.
