Haplopappus scaposus

Haplopappus scaposus, commonly known as the slender pappus plant, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant has slender, erect stems and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow and are arranged in dense clusters at the tips of the stems.

Haplopappus scaposus is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.
