Hieracium mirabile subsp. pseudomirabile

Hieracium mirabile subsp.

pseudomirabile, commonly known as the showy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and can reach heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
