Hebepetalum parviflorum

Hebepetalum parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered hebe, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens throughout New Zealand and beyond.
