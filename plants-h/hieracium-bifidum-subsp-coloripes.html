Hieracium bifidum subsp. coloripes

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

coloripes, commonly known as the red-hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are hairy and often have a reddish tinge, which contributes to its common name.

The showy yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
