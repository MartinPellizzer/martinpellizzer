Heterotheca latifolia var. macgregoris

Heterotheca latifolia var. macgregoris, commonly known as the broadleaf blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green foliage and clusters of small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has a taproot system that allows it to survive in arid conditions.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough, hairy texture.
