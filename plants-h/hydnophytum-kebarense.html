Hydnophytum kebarense

Hydnophytum kebarense, commonly known as the Kebarensian mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees in tropical rainforests.

It possesses specialized structures called haustoria, which allow it to absorb water and nutrients from its host. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically pollinated by insects. Its berries are fleshy and serve as a food source for various bird species.

Due to its restricted distribution and ecological niche, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
