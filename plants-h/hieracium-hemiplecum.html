Hieracium hemiplecum

Hieracium hemiplecum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a flowering stem that bears yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are usually alternate, with a rough texture and serrated edges.

Hieracium hemiplecum is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in many regions.
