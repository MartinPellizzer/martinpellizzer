Hyparrhenia confinis

Hyparrhenia confinis, commonly known as the African silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, silver-green leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often used in floral arrangements. Hyparrhenia confinis thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

It is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in degraded areas.
