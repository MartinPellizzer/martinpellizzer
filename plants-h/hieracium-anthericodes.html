Hieracium anthericodes

Hieracium anthericodes, commonly known as the spiked hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often hairy.

The flowers are yellow and bloom from late spring to early autumn, contributing to its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
