Hymenophyllum steerei

Hymenophyllum steerei, commonly known as the silver fern, is a small, delicate fern belonging to the Hymenophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, scale-like leaves that give it a silvery appearance. This epiphytic plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing on trees or rocks in tropical and subtropical regions. Its fronds are typically less than 10 centimeters in length, and it produces small, spore-bearing structures called sporangia.

The plant's unique morphology and habitat preferences make it an important species in studies of epiphytic flora.
