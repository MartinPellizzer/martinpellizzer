Heteropterys laurifolia subvar. floribunda

Heteropterys laurifolia subvar. floribunda, commonly known as the showy laurel, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, leathery leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a pleasant aromatic scent. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers, which are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in shaded garden settings.

Its botanical features make it valuable for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
