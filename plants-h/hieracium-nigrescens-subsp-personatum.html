Hieracium nigrescens subsp. personatum

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

personatum, commonly known as the purple hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with purple markings.

It is an herbaceous perennial that can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
