Henriettea tuberculosa

Henriettea tuberculosa, commonly known as the mountain heather, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in the spring. Its leaves are evergreen, leathery, and densely packed, adapted to the harsh conditions of its mountainous habitat.

Henriettea tuberculosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
