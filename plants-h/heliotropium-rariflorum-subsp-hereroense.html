Heliotropium rariflorum subsp. hereroense

Heliotropium rariflorum subsp.

hereroense, commonly known as the Herero heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that typically range in color from pink to purple.

The plant has hairy, often glandular leaves and stems, which are typical of the heliotrope genus. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

The species is noted for its ability to tolerate drought conditions, making it well adapted to its native habitat.
