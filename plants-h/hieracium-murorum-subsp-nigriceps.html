Hieracium murorum subsp. nigriceps

Hieracium murorum subsp.

nigriceps, commonly known as the black-headed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

The plant features upright stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a composite head, and are surrounded by bracts that give it a distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is notable for its dark, prominent bracts that contrast with the bright yellow flowers.
