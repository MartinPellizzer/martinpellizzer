Hieracium subfarinosum

Hieracium subfarinosum, commonly known as the farinose hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist grasslands and meadows, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators.
