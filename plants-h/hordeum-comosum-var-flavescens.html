Hordeum comosum var. flavescens

Hordeum comosum var. flavescens, commonly known as the yellow barley or yellow wild barley, is a flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense, yellowish to golden spikelets that give it its distinctive appearance. This variant is native to various regions of Europe and Asia and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. The plant has small, wind-pollinated flowers and produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

It is considered a wild relative of cultivated barley and has some ecological and historical significance.
