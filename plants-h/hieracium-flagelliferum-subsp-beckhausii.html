Hieracium flagelliferum subsp. beckhausii

Hieracium flagelliferum subsp.

beckhausii, commonly known as the Beckhaus' hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers arranged in a compact head.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky soils, thriving in cool, moist environments. It has hairy stems and basal leaves that are elongated and often lobed.

This subspecies is noted for its horticultural value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental appeal.
