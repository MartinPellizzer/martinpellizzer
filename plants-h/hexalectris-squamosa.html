Hexalectris squamosa

Hexalectris squamosa, commonly known as the flame flower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is notable for its unique, showy, red-orange flowers that resemble flames. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils in pine forests and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments. It has a rhizomatous root system and produces small, scale-like leaves.

Hexalectris squamosa is considered a rare and endangered species, requiring specific habitat conditions for successful growth and reproduction.
