Hypericum maculatum subsp. desetangsii

Hypericum maculatum subsp.

desetangsii, commonly known as the spotted St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has opposite, oval-shaped leaves. It produces small, red-orange berries that are edible but have a mild, bitter taste.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
