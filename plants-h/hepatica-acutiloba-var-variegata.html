Hepatica acutiloba var. variegata

Hepatica acutiloba var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated liverleaf, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves with white or yellow markings. The plant produces showy, nodding flowers in shades of blue, purple, or white during early spring. It thrives in woodland habitats and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
