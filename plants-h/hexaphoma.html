Hexaphoma

Hexaphoma, commonly known as the genus of leaf spot fungi, consists of various species that are primarily plant pathogens.

These fungi are characterized by their production of dark, discrete spots on the leaves of their host plants. They belong to the family Pleosporaceae and are often found in temperate regions. The genus is notable for its complex life cycles, which may involve both asexual and sexual reproduction.

Hexaphoma species are important in agricultural and ecological contexts due to their impact on crop health and plant diversity.
