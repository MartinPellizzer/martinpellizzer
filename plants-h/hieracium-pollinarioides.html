Hieracium pollinarioides

Hieracium pollinarioides, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features bright yellow ray florets surrounded by a ring of tubular disc florets, typical of the genus Hieracium. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies.

The leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
