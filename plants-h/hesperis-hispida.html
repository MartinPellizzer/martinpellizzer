Hesperis hispida

Hesperis hispida, commonly known as the hispid horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces striking yellow to orange flowers with prominent, horn-like structures at the base of the petals. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Hesperis hispida is a member of the Papaveraceae family, which includes other well-known genera such as Papaver and Corydalis.
