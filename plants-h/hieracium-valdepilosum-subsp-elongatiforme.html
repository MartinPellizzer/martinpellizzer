Hieracium valdepilosum subsp. elongatiforme

Hieracium valdepilosum subsp.

elongatiforme, commonly known as the elongated hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring moist to moderately dry conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with fine hairs, while its flowering stems are elongated and branched.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its historical use in traditional medicine.
