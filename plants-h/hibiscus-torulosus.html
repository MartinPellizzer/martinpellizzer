Hibiscus torulosus

Hibiscus torulosus, commonly known as the yellow wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive yellowish-green color and produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow to orange. Hibiscus torulosus is part of the Malvaceae family, which includes other hibiscus species and cotton.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
