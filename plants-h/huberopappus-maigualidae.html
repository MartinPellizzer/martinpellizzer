Huberopappus maigualidae

Huberopappus maigualidae, commonly known as the Maigualidae aster, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Argentina.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant produces showy yellow to orange ray florets surrounded by tubular disc florets, typical of the sunflower family. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
