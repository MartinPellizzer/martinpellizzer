Hedyotis glabra

Hedyotis glabra, commonly known as small white geranium, is a low-growing herbaceous perennial native to various regions of Asia.

It features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from spring to early summer. The plant has slender, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with slightly serrated edges. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along streambanks.

Hedyotis glabra is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
