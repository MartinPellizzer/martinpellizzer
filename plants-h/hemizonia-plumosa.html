Hemizonia plumosa

Hemizonia plumosa, commonly known as the plume grass, is a member of the Poaceae family, characterized by its delicate, feathery appearance.

It is native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant features slender, wiry stems with soft, fluffy leaves that give it a unique, ethereal look. Hemizonia plumosa is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.

Its foliage is typically light green, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in late spring.
