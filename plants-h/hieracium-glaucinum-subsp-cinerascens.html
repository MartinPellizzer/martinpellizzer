Hieracium glaucinum subsp. cinerascens

Hieracium glaucinum subsp.

cinerascens, commonly known as the cinerascens hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It is distinguished by its pale, often bluish-gray foliage and its ability to thrive in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain areas.
