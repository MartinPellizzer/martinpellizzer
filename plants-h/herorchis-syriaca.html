Herorchis syriaca

Herorchis syriaca, commonly known as the Syrian orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the Middle East.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers with a distinctive coloration. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptation to harsh conditions. Its blooming period is brief, usually occurring in late spring or early summer.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered in its natural range.
