Holosteum klopotovii

Holosteum klopotovii, commonly known as the mountain holosteum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Holosteum klopotovii is adapted to harsh, high-altitude environments and is often found in alpine meadows.
