Hamelia patens var. erecta

Hamelia patens var. erecta, commonly known as the flamebeard bush, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, tubular red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its compact growth habit and vibrant floral display make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
