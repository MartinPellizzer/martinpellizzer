Hopea bracteata var. perakensis

Hopea bracteata var. perakensis, commonly known as the Bornean ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It is characterized by its hard, heavy wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape with a leathery texture. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits containing a single seed.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
