Hieracium multangulum

Hieracium multangulum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its yellow flowers, which bloom from June to August, are surrounded by bracts that give it a distinctive appearance.

Hieracium multangulum is often found in temperate regions and can be invasive in some areas where it outcompetes native vegetation.
