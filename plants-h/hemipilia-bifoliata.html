Hemipilia bifoliata

Hemipilia bifoliata, commonly known as the two-leaved hemipilia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually pale green to white, with a distinct lip petal that aids in pollination by insects.

Hemipilia bifoliata is valued in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
