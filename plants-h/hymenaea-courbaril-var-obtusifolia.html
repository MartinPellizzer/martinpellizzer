Hymenaea courbaril var. obtusifolia

Hymenaea courbaril var. obtusifolia, commonly known as the Brazil nut tree, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces woody pods containing multiple seeds, which are the edible Brazil nuts. Its bark is thick and often fissured, providing a rugged appearance.

This species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity in its native habitats.
