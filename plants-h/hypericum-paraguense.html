Hypericum paraguense

Hypericum paraguense, commonly known as the Paraguayan St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the genus Hypericum, which includes various species known for their yellow flowers and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny environments and is characterized by its reddish stems and yellow, five-petaled flowers. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often covered in fine hairs.

Hypericum paraguense is also valued for its potential use in traditional medicine.
