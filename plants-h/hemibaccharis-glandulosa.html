Hemibaccharis glandulosa

Hemibaccharis glandulosa, commonly known as Brazilian pepper tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It has compound leaves with opposite arrangement and small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruits are red, fleshy drupes that contain a single seed.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to various environmental conditions.
