Helichrysum sarcodes

Helichrysum sarcodes, commonly known as the golden everlasting or yellow everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, golden-yellow inflorescences that resemble small suns. The plant is known for its aromatic, resinous scent and its ability to retain its color when dried, making it popular in floral arrangements. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its medicinal properties have also been explored for their potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.
