Hibiscus colimensis

Hibiscus colimensis, commonly known as the Mexican hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red, with five petals and a prominent central stamen. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a smooth texture, while its stems are usually hairy.

Hibiscus colimensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
