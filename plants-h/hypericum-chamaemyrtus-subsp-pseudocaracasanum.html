Hypericum chamaemyrtus subsp. pseudocaracasanum

Hypericum chamaemyrtus subsp.

pseudocaracasanum, commonly known as the creeping St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features dense, woody stems that form a mat-like growth habit, often spreading across rocky or dry soils.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, providing year-round coverage.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh, arid environments.
