Hymenophyllum balfourii

Hymenophyllum balfourii, commonly known as the Balfour's filmy fern, is a small, epiphytic fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or rocks in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spores are produced on the underside of its fronds in structures called sorus.

The plant's unique morphology and habitat requirements make it an important species in the study of epiphytic flora.
