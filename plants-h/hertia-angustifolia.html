Hertia angustifolia

Hertia angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf hertia, is a perennial plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in dense clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Hertia angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in horticultural settings for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
