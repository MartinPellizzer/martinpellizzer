Hildaea pallens var. major

Hildaea pallens var. major, commonly known as the desert hibiscus, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating drought tolerance through its succulent-like stems and leaves. Its fibrous roots help stabilize sandy soils, making it an important species in desert ecosystems.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.
