Haageocereus versicolor f. fuscus

Haageocereus versicolor f. fuscus, commonly known as the dark-stemmed night blooming cactus, is a unique variant of the species Haageocereus versicolor.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its dark green, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces striking white or pale yellow flowers at night, which emit a sweet fragrance to attract nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in arid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This cactus is prized for its ornamental value and its ability to bloom under specific environmental conditions.
