Hordeum vulgare var. abdulbasirovii

Hordeum vulgare var. abdulbasirovii, commonly known as Abdulbasirov's barley, is a variant of barley native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive morphological features, including unique spikelet structures and glume morphology. This variety is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions. Its genetic diversity contributes to the broader understanding of barley evolution and domestication.

It is of interest to botanists and agricultural researchers due to its potential for improving crop resilience in challenging climates.
