Hibiscus violaceus

Hibiscus violaceus, commonly known as the violet hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from violet to deep purple. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are ovate and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and often branched.

Hibiscus violaceus thrives in humid, sunny environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
