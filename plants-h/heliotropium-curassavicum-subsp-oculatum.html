Heliotropium curassavicum subsp. oculatum

Heliotropium curassavicum subsp.

oculatum, commonly known as the eye-shaped heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America. It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple, often resembling the appearance of the sun.

The plant typically grows in moist, tropical environments and is often found in coastal areas or wetlands. Its leaves are usually opposite and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive blooms.
