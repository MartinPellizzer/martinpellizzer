Homalomena rubescens var. latifolia

Homalomena rubescens var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed ginger plant, is a tropical evergreen with large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 3 feet in length.

The plant features a rhizomatous root system and produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and well-draining soil. The leaves are usually a deep green with a reddish tint on the underside, adding visual interest.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and indoor spaces due to its striking foliage.
