Hyptis sylvularum

Hyptis sylvularum, commonly known as the silverleaf hyptis, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lamiaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of the Americas, particularly Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine hairs, and its clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and forest margins.

Hyptis sylvularum is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
