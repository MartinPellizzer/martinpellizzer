Helichrysum solitarium

Helichrysum solitarium, commonly known as the solitary everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, bract-like leaves that are often mistaken for flowers, giving it a unique appearance. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The dried flowers are used in floral arrangements due to their long-lasting nature and vibrant color.
