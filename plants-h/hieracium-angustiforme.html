Hieracium angustiforme

Hieracium angustiforme, commonly known as narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It features slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster, making it easily identifiable in the wild.
