Hieracium stellans

Hieracium stellans, commonly known as the star dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its yellow, dandelion-like flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often reddish in color.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
