Hevea spruceana f. tridentata

Hevea spruceana f. tridentata, commonly known as the tridentate rubber vine, is a tropical plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves, which give it the "tridentate" designation. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsules that contain numerous seeds. It is native to parts of Southeast Asia and is often found in humid, lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
