Halimocnemis tomentosa var. glabrescens

Halimocnemis tomentosa var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth-haired halimocnemis, is a lesser-known species within the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its densely hairy, grayish-green foliage that becomes smoother with age, giving it its varietal name. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showing a high degree of drought tolerance. Its small, yellowish flowers are clustered in composite heads, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
