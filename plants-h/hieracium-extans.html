Hieracium extans

Hieracium extans, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped with hairy surfaces, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom from June to August.

Hieracium extans is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
