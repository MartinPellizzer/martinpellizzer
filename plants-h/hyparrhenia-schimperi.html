Hyparrhenia schimperi

Hyparrhenia schimperi, commonly known as the African silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves that are often silver-white in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green or reddish in color. It is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, where it forms dense mats that help prevent soil erosion.

Hyparrhenia schimperi is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional landscaping and erosion control practices.
