Halimocnemis volvox

Halimocnemis volvox, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a rosette-like growth pattern with fleshy, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense clusters, attracting a variety of pollinators.
