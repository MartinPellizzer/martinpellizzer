Hedyotis caffra

Hedyotis caffra, commonly known as the African shieldflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, shield-shaped leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pinkish flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Hedyotis caffra is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
