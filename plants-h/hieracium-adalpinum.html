Hieracium adalpinum

Hieracium adalpinum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and ragworts, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium adalpinum is often found in meadows and open slopes, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
