Helianthemum marifolium var. dichroum

Helianthemum marifolium var. dichroum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sandthorn, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain water in arid environments. Its stems are often covered in fine hairs, providing some protection against harsh weather conditions.

This variety is well adapted to rocky and sandy soils, making it a hardy plant in coastal and dryland habitats.
