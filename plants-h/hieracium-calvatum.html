Hieracium calvatum

Hieracium calvatum, commonly known as the naked hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and open woodlands, preferring temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its roots are fibrous and shallow.

Hieracium calvatum is known for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and maintaining soil stability.
