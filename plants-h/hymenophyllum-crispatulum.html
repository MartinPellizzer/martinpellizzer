Hymenophyllum crispatulum

Hymenophyllum crispatulum, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a small, delicate fern belonging to the Hymenophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, soft, and often hairy fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. This epiphytic plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on trees or rocks in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds and are protected by a membranous covering.

The plant's unique morphology and habitat preferences make it a notable species in the study of epiphytic flora.
