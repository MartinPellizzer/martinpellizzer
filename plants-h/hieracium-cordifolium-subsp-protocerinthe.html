Hieracium cordifolium subsp. protocerinthe

Hieracium cordifolium subsp.

protocerinthe, commonly known as the heart-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
