Hanguana nana

Hanguana nana, commonly known as the dwarf hanguana, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rainforests and undergrowth. The plant features delicate, narrow leaves that are often clustered at the base, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers. Its root system is fibrous and helps it anchor in loose, nutrient-rich soil.

Hanguana nana is noted for its low-growing habit and is often used in horticultural settings for its ornamental value.
