Herpetacanthus longipetiolatus

Herpetacanthus longipetiolatus, commonly known as the long-petioled snake's mouth, is a unique flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, serpentine-shaped flowers that resemble a snake's mouth, giving it its common name. The plant has elongated petioles, which are the stalks connecting the leaves to the stem, contributing to its striking appearance. Its foliage consists of large, heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green.

This species is primarily valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in tropical gardens for its unusual floral display.
