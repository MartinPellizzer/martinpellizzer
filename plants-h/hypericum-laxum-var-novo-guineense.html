Hypericum laxum var. novo-guineense

Hypericum laxum var. novo-guineense, commonly known as the New Guinea St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to New Guinea.

It belongs to the family Hypericaceae and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit with woody stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically arranged in clusters, and its leaves are opposite, simple, and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant floral displays.
