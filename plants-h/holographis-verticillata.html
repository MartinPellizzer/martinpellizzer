Holographis verticillata

Holographis verticillata, commonly known as the spiral leaf plant, is a unique species characterized by its whorled arrangement of leaves.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate regions. The plant features small, delicate flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental value. Its foliage is notable for its spiral pattern, which gives it a distinctive appearance.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Apiaceae, sharing some characteristics with other members of the carrot family.
