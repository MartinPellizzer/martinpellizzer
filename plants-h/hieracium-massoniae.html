Hieracium massoniae

Hieracium massoniae, commonly known as the Masson's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typically hairy stems. The plant features basal leaves that are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowering stems are shorter and less hairy. It produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which attracts various pollinators.

Hieracium massoniae is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
