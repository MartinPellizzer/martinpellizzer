Hesperocles fragrans

Hesperocles fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant hesperocles, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

The fragrance of its flowers is often described as sweet and spicy, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
