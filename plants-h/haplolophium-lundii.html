Haplolophium lundii

Haplolophium lundii, commonly known as the African ice plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers during the warmer months. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.

Haplolophium lundii is well adapted to full sun and well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
