Hieracium murorum var. cardiophyllum

Hieracium murorum var. cardiophyllum, commonly known as the heart-leaved groundsel, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, heart-shaped leaves and produces yellow-orange flowers with a central disk. The plant is native to Europe and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides. It thrives in temperate climates and is adapted to full sun to partial shade.

Its foliage and flowers are typically covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.
