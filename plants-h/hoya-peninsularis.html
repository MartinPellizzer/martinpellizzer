Hoya peninsularis

Hoya peninsularis, commonly known as the Peninsular Waxplant, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with cream or white markings. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white or pale pink. It is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

Hoya peninsularis is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
