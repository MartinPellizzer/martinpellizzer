Hieracium fulcratum subsp. tavelianum

Hieracium fulcratum subsp.

tavelianum, commonly known as the Tavelian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

The plant features a rosette of basal leaves with a hairy surface and produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its flowering stems are usually unbranched and bear several flower heads.

This subspecies is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.
