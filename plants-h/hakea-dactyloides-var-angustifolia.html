Hakea dactyloides var. angustifolia

Hakea dactyloides var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hakea, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and produces distinctive cylindrical flower spikes. The plant is adapted to arid environments, featuring a deep root system for water uptake. It blooms in spring, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects.

The species is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
