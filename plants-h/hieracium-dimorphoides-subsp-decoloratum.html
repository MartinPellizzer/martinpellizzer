Hieracium dimorphoides subsp. decoloratum

Hieracium dimorphoides subsp.

decoloratum, commonly known as the pale hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy foliage.

The plant typically grows in grasslands and disturbed soils, exhibiting a low-growing habit with hairy stems. Its flowers are yellow and occur in clusters, while the leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a rough texture.

This subspecies is notable for its ability to thrive in various environmental conditions, making it a common sight in open habitats.
