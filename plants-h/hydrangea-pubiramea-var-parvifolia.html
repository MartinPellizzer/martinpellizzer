Hydrangea pubiramea var. parvifolia

Hydrangea pubiramea var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It features large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from white to pink, depending on soil pH. The plant has ovate to elliptical leaves that are typically hairy on the underside, giving it a distinctive texture. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to change flower color, making it a popular choice for gardeners.
