Hieracium sparsiflorum subsp. vestiticeps

Hieracium sparsiflorum subsp.

vestiticeps, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its narrow, hairy stems and small, yellowish flowers arranged in a composite head.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This subspecies is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain insects.
