Hippocratea volkensii

Hippocratea volkensii, commonly known as the East African orchid, is a striking flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of East Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms in the dry season, producing clusters of pale yellow to orange flowers with a distinctive, intricate pattern. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and habitat specificity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
