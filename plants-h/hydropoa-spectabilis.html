Hydropoa spectabilis

Hydropoa spectabilis, commonly known as the showy water plant, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that consist of numerous small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland or aquatic environments, thriving in moist soil or shallow water. Its leaves are usually narrow and elongated, adapted to the aquatic habitat.

Hydropoa spectabilis plays a role in supporting local biodiversity by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
