Helictotrichon mortonianum

Helictotrichon mortonianum, commonly known as the Mortonian needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as rocky soils and coastal areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in arid conditions and is often found in disturbed or marginal environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of ecological study.
