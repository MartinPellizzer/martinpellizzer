Hieracium bupleuroides subsp. maglandicum

Hieracium bupleuroides subsp.

maglandicum, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are usually hairy and lobed, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat, disc-like cluster.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
