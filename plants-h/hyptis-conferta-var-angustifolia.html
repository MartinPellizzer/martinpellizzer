Hyptis conferta var. angustifolia

Hyptis conferta var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf hyptis, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae), which is known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

The species is often used in traditional medicine for its purported antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
