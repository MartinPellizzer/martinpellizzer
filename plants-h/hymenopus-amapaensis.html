Hymenopus amapaensis

Hymenopus amapaensis, commonly known as the Amapa fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the family Hymenopodaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in the undergrowth of dense tropical forests. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are clustered on the undersides of the fronds.

Hymenopus amapaensis plays a vital role in the ecological balance of its habitat by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
