Hieracium blattariforme

Hieracium blattariforme, commonly known as the broad-leaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are typically yellow with a central disc. It thrives in grassy areas and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies.

Due to its aggressive growth, it can become invasive in certain regions.
