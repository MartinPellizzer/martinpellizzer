Hieracium porrifolium subsp. niphanthum

Hieracium porrifolium subsp.

niphanthum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its basal rosette of broadly ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually hairy and slightly lobed.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the genus Hieracium. It thrives in moist meadows and woodland edges, often in temperate regions of Europe.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
