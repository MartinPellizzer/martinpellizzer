Hieracium tricheilema

Hieracium tricheilema, commonly known as the hairy yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are hairy and often have a rough texture, while its yellow flowers bloom in early summer.

Hieracium tricheilema is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and can become invasive in certain regions.
