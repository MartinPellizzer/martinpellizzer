Hieracium subcinereum

Hieracium subcinereum, commonly known as the pale hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, preferring well-drained conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium subcinereum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
