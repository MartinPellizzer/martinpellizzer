Hedysarum minjanense

Hedysarum minjanense, commonly known as the Minjan sweet clover, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Hedysarum minjanense is adapted to dry and rocky habitats, showcasing its resilience in arid environments.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native ecosystems.
