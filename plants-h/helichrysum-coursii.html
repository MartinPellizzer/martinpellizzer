Helichrysum coursii

Helichrysum coursii, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that remain vibrant even when dried. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is aromatic and often used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic properties.

Helichrysum coursii is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
