Hierochloe laxa

Hierochloe laxa, commonly known as loose hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and rocky slopes. The plant features slender, cylindrical stems and fine, wiry leaves that are often slightly curled. Its inflorescence consists of a loose, open panicle made up of small, spikelet-like flowers.

Hierochloe laxa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
