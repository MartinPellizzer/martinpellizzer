Hibiscus tiliaceus subsp. hastatus

Hibiscus tiliaceus subsp.

hastatus, commonly known as the broadleaf hibiscus, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands. It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside.

The flowers are typically pale yellow to white, with five broad petals and a prominent central stamen. It thrives in coastal areas and is often found in mangrove ecosystems.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local cultures.
