Hieracium chloropsis subsp. mutelii

Hieracium chloropsis subsp.

mutelii, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to orange flowers and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and it produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is often found in calcareous habitats and is relatively uncommon in the wild.
