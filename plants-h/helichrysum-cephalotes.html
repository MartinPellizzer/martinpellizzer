Helichrysum cephalotes

Helichrysum cephalotes, commonly known as the golden everlasting or mountain everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and bright yellow to orange flowers. The plant produces small, tightly packed flower heads that are often mistaken for a single bloom, and it is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Its foliage is typically gray-green with a slightly hairy texture, and it is often used in ornamental gardening for its long-lasting flowers.
