Hieracium panaeoliceps

Hieracium panaeoliceps, commonly known as the pale hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellowish to reddish flower heads, which consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its stems are usually unbranched.

Hieracium panaeoliceps is often found in Europe and parts of Asia, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
