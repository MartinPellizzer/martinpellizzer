Hieracium pogonatoides

Hieracium pogonatoides, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically features finely hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides.

Hieracium pogonatoides is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
