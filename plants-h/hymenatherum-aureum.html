Hymenatherum aureum

Hymenatherum aureum, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also important in ecological contexts due to its role in nitrogen fixation.
