Halimium halimifolium subsp. lasiocalycinum

Halimium halimifolium subsp.

lasiocalycinum, commonly known as the purple sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers.

The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and Mediterranean environments. Its leaves are typically gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it is adapted to withstand drought and salt spray.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological resilience in harsh climates.
