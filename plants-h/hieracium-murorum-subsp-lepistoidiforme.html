Hieracium murorum subsp. lepistoidiforme

Hieracium murorum subsp.

lepistoidiforme, commonly known as the creeping hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and its flowers are arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

This subspecies is often found in the British Isles and parts of southern Scandinavia.
