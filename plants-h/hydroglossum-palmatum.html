Hydroglossum palmatum

Hydroglossum palmatum, commonly known as the water mirror palm, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its striking, translucent leaves that resemble glass.

It thrives in shallow, still freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes, where it can receive ample sunlight. The plant's leaves are broad and fan-shaped, with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in shades of white or pale yellow.

Hydroglossum palmatum plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
