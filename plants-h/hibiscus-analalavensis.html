Hibiscus analalavensis

Hibiscus analalavensis, commonly known as the Madagascar hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range from pink to red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. Hibiscus analalavensis thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas of its native habitat.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
