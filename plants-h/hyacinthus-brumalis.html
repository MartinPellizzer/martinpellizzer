Hyacinthus brumalis

Hyacinthus brumalis, commonly known as the Christmas hyacinth, is a bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It produces fragrant, violet-blue flowers in early winter, making it a symbol of the holiday season. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in temperate climates.

Hyacinthus brumalis is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family, which includes other bulbous flowering plants.
