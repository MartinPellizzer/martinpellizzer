Hemizonia greeneana subsp. peninsularis

Hemizonia greeneana subsp.

peninsularis, commonly known as the Peninsular Hemizonia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its foliage is usually linear and grayish-green, with a woolly texture that helps reduce water loss.

The species is noted for its ecological importance in supporting local pollinators and its resilience in harsh climates.
