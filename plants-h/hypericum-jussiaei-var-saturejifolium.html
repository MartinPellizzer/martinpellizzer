Hypericum jussiaei var. saturejifolium

Hypericum jussiaei var. saturejifolium, commonly known as the saturejifolium hypericum, is a variant of the flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in the summer months. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long blooming period.
