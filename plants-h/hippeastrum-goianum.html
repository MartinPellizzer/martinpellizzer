Hippeastrum goianum

Hippeastrum goianum, commonly known as the Goianum amaryllis, is a striking tropical plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces strap-like leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its bulbous stem stores nutrients, allowing it to thrive in seasonal climates.

Hippeastrum goianum is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological significance in its native habitat.
