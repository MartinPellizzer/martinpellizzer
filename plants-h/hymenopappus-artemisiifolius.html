Hymenopappus artemisiifolius

Hymenopappus artemisiifolius, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, wind-dispersed seeds with pappus bristles, aiding in its propagation across arid environments. It is adapted to thrive in sandy or gravelly soils and is drought-tolerant.

The species is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions.
