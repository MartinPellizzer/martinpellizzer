Hieracium lidii

Hieracium lidii, commonly known as the Lidd's hawkweed, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in rocky and alpine environments. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

It is often found in high-altitude meadows and is adapted to cold, harsh climates.
