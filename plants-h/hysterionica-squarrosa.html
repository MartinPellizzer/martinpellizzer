Hysterionica squarrosa

Hysterionica squarrosa, commonly known as the squarrosa bellflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, and its hairy, often glandular stems and leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

Hysterionica squarrosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in well-drained soil.
