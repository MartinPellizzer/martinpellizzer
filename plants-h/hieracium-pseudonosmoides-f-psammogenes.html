Hieracium pseudonosmoides f. psammogenes

Hieracium pseudonosmoides f. psammogenes, commonly known as the sand dune hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to sandy coastal environments.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically narrow and linear, adapted to withstand harsh, windy conditions. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in dune ecosystems.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing sand dunes and supporting local biodiversity.
