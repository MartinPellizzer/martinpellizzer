Hygrophila gigas

Hygrophila gigas, commonly known as the giant hygrophila, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, ribbon-like leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and rivers. It produces small, pale yellow flowers that are typically borne in clusters.

Hygrophila gigas is often used in aquascaping due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to provide habitat for aquatic life.
