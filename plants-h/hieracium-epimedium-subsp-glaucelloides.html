Hieracium epimedium subsp. glaucelloides

Hieracium epimedium subsp.

glaucelloides, commonly known as the glaucous hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and woodland edges. It has a low-growing habit with a rosette of basal leaves and flowering stems that bear small, daisy-like inflorescences.

This subspecies is distinguished by its bluish-gray foliage and is often found in temperate regions.
