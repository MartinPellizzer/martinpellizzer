Hieracium alatum subsp. tolpidifolium

Hieracium alatum subsp.

tolpidifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow composite flowers.

The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and roadsides.

Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators.
