Hippeastrum curitibanum

Hippeastrum curitibanum, commonly known as the Curitiba amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to Brazil.

It features large, showy flowers that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often with a contrasting white or pink throat. The plant produces strap-like leaves that are broad and lance-shaped, emerging from a bulbous stem. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Hippeastrum curitibanum is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family, sharing characteristics with other amaryllis species.
