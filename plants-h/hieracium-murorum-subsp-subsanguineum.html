Hieracium murorum subsp. subsanguineum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

subsanguineum, commonly known as the red-haired hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and meadows, and reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters.

The plant features basal leaves that are broadly lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a single head, and are surrounded by bracts that are often reddish or purplish in color.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
