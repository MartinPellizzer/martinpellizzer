Hieracium vulgatum subsp. subpunctillatum

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

subpunctillatum, commonly known as the lesser hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers arranged in a composite head.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped with a rough texture and are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

This subspecies is distinguished by its smaller, more densely packed flower heads compared to other members of the species.
