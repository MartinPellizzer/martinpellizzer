Hypericum tomentosum proles pubescens

Hypericum tomentosum proles pubescens, commonly known as the hairy St. John's wort, is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and bright yellow flowers.

It belongs to the family Hypericaceae and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. The plant features small, oval leaves covered in fine, white pubescence, which gives it its common name. Its flowers, which bloom in summer, are typically clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences.

The plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
