Hopea jacobi

Hopea jacobi, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark brown to black heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and strength. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. Its small, pale yellow flowers are clustered in spikes, and it produces woody, three-lobed fruits.

Hopea jacobi plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
