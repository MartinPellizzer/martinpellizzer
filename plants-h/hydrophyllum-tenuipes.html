Hydrophyllum tenuipes

Hydrophyllum tenuipes, commonly known as the slenderleaf waterleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Hydrophyllum tenuipes is adapted to wet environments and plays a role in supporting local insect and bird populations.
