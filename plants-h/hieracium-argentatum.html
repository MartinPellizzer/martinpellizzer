Hieracium argentatum

Hieracium argentatum, commonly known as silver hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf structures. The plant features silver-white hairs on its leaves and stems, giving it its common name. It grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies.

Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers.
