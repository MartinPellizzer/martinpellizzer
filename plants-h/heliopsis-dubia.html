Heliopsis dubia

Heliopsis dubia, commonly known as the pale coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and wetlands. The plant features showy, yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough, hairy texture.

Heliopsis dubia is valued in native gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
