Hieracium aestivum subsp. platylepioides

Hieracium aestivum subsp.

platylepioides, commonly known as the broad-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, hairy leaves.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer, typically from July to September. It thrives in sunny, disturbed sites such as roadsides and meadows.

Its growth habit and floral structure are typical of the genus Hieracium, which includes many species of hawkweeds.
