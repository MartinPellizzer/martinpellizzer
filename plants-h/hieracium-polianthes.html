Hieracium polianthes

Hieracium polianthes, commonly known as the showy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are upright and often hairy.

Hieracium polianthes is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
