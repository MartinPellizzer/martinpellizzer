Herschelianthe excelsa

Herschelianthe excelsa, commonly known as the tall hesperaloe, is a striking member of the Asparagaceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powder, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is widely cultivated in regions with Mediterranean climates.
