Hodgsonia heteroclita

Hodgsonia heteroclita, commonly known as the Hodgson's plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct scent. Its flowers are typically clustered in whorls and display a range of colors from purple to pink.

Hodgsonia heteroclita is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
