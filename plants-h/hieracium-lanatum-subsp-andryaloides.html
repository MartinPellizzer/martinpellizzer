Hieracium lanatum subsp. andryaloides

Hieracium lanatum subsp.

andryaloides, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its dense, woolly white hairs covering the stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. It produces yellow flowers in clusters, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture.

This subspecies is often found in temperate climates and is relatively hardy, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
