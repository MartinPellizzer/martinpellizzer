Heteropogon bellariensis

Heteropogon bellariensis, commonly known as the Indian grass, is a grass species native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including wetlands and grasslands, and is often found in disturbed soils.

Heteropogon bellariensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
