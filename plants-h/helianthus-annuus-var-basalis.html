Helianthus annuus var. basalis

Helianthus annuus var. basalis, commonly known as the wild sunflower, is a variant of the common sunflower native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and large, showy yellow-orange flower heads. The plant typically grows in open areas such as meadows and fields, and it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are broad and lobed, often with a hairy texture, and it produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
