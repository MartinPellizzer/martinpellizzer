Habenaria sylviae

Habenaria sylviae, commonly known as the woodland orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forest floors and rocky crevices. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green. Its flowers are characterized by a spurred lip and are often pollinated by insects.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native ecosystems.
