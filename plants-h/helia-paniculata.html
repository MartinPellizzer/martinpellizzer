Helia paniculata

Helia paniculata, commonly known as the panicled helia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, branched inflorescences that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are usually alternate, simple, and lance-shaped with a rough texture.

Helia paniculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
