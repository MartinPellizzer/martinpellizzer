Habenaria roxburghii

Habenaria roxburghii, commonly known as the orchid of the genus Habenaria, is a flowering plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. Native to Southeast Asia, this species thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant has a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in variable conditions.

Its flowers are often pollinated by insects, contributing to its ecological significance in tropical ecosystems.
