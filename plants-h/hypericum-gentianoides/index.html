Hypericum Gentianoides

Hypericum gentianoides, commonly known as the yellow-stemmed St. John's wort, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests. The plant is recognized for its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a splash of color to shaded environments. Its historical use in traditional medicine has sparked interest in its potential health benefits. Despite its medicinal appeal, it is important to note that it contains compounds that may interact with certain medications.
