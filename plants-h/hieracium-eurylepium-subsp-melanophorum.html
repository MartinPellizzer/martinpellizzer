Hieracium eurylepium subsp. melanophorum

Hieracium eurylepium subsp.

melanophorum, commonly known as the black-stemmed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive dark green to blackish stems, which are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish composite flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, often with a waxy coating.

This subspecies is native to parts of Europe and is often found in grassy areas, along roadsides, and in disturbed soils.
