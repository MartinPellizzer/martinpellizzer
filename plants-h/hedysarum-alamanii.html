Hedysarum alamanii

Hedysarum alamanii, commonly known as the Alaman peavine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and erect, branching stems that bear small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces cylindrical, pea-like pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.

Hedysarum alamanii plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for wildlife.
