Haageocereus pluriflorus

Haageocereus pluriflorus, commonly known as the desert rose or night blooming cereus, is a cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem and clusters of white, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant has spiny areoles and produces small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for desert environments.

Its nocturnal flowering habit makes it a unique and striking plant in both natural and cultivated settings.
