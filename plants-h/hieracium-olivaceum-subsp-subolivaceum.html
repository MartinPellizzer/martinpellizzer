Hieracium olivaceum subsp. subolivaceum

Hieracium olivaceum subsp.

subolivaceum, commonly known as the olive hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, pastures, and woodland edges.

The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, with a flowering stem that bears yellow composite flowers. Its flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, characteristic of the Asteraceae family.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to moderate grazing.
