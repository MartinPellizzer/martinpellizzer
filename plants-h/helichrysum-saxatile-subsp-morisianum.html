Helichrysum saxatile subsp. morisianum

Helichrysum saxatile subsp.

morisianum, commonly known as the mountain everlasting, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of yellow to golden-orange flower heads.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are typically arranged in panicles. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
