Hieracium odontophylloides

Hieracium odontophylloides, commonly known as the toothed hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves and yellow ray flowers that bloom in late summer. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this species thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has a taproot system and produces numerous seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural collections.
