Hieracium wilczekianum subsp. trigonolepium

Hieracium wilczekianum subsp.

trigonolepium, commonly known as the three-lobed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lanceolate with distinctive three lobes, and the flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is relatively small in size, usually reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.
