Hieracium laevigatum subsp. semiglobosum

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

semiglobosum, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its hairy, often reddish stems and basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in a compact, spherical inflorescence, which is a distinctive feature of the species. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in various habitats across its range.
