Helicia serrata var. petiolata

Helicia serrata var. petiolata, commonly known as the broad-leaved helicia, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Theaceae and is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
