Helichrysum rusillonii

Helichrysum rusillonii, commonly known as the Cape goldcup, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, yellow-orange inflorescences that resemble small cups, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically visited by bees and other pollinators. Its leaves are usually narrow and gray-green, with a slightly hairy texture.

Helichrysum rusillonii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in dry, well-drained soils.
