Hieracium albicrinum

Hieracium albicrinum, commonly known as the white-haired hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a serrated edge, while its flowers are small and yellow, often surrounded by white bracts.

Hieracium albicrinum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
