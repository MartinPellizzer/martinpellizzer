Hedysarum turkestanicum

Hedysarum turkestanicum, commonly known as the Turkestan milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant produces cylindrical pods that contain several small, edible seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.

Hedysarum turkestanicum is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
