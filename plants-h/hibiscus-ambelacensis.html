Hibiscus ambelacensis

Hibiscus ambelacensis, commonly known as the Ambelacensis hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range from pink to red in color. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and streamside areas.

Hibiscus ambelacensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its beauty.
