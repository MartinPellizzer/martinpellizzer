Hyptis dumetorum var. genuina

Hyptis dumetorum var. genuina, commonly known as the Brazilian hyptis or wild basil, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their essential oils. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white to purple. It is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its medicinal properties.

The plant's essential oils are used in traditional medicine for their antispasmodic and analgesic effects.
