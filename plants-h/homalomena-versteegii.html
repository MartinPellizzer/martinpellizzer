Homalomena versteegii

Homalomena versteegii, commonly known as the Versteeg's ginger, is a tropical evergreen perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its glossy, arrow-shaped leaves that are often deeply veined. The plant produces striking, spatulate-shaped inflorescences with a white spathe and a yellowish-green spadix, typical of the genus. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Homalomena versteegii is valued for its ornamental foliage and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
