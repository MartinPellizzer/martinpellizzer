Hyparrhenia monathera

Hyparrhenia monathera, commonly known as silverleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, silver-gray leaves that give it its common name, and it often grows in open woodland and grassland habitats. The plant produces small, pale green flowers in dense, spike-like inflorescences, which are typically wind-pollinated. Hyparrhenia monathera is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a resilient plant in arid environments.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
