Herorchis papilionacea

Herorchis papilionacea, commonly known as the butterfly orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, butterfly-like flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange with a distinctive pouch-shaped lip that aids in pollination.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
