Helopus junceus

Helopus junceus, commonly known as the common rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage and produces small, clustered flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist or wet environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands.

Helopus junceus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
