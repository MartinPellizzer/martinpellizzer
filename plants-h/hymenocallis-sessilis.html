Hymenocallis sessilis

Hymenocallis sessilis, commonly known as the sessile hurricane lily, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to yellow and orange. The plant typically grows in wetlands and along riverbanks, thriving in moist, sandy soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the flowers bloom in late summer to early fall.

Hymenocallis sessilis is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
