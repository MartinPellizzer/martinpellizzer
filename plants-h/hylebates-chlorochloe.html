Hylebates chlorochloe

Hylebates chlorochloe, commonly known as the golden mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in the genus Eucalyptus.

It derives some of its nutrients from the host through specialized structures called haustoria, while also performing photosynthesis to produce its own food. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers, followed by small, fleshy berries that contain seeds covered in a sticky substance to aid in attachment to new hosts. It is native to Australia and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Its ecological role includes providing food for native birds and insects, contributing to the biodiversity of its environment.
