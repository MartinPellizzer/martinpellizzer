Hippeastrum teretifolium

Hippeastrum teretifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its strap-like, narrow leaves and striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant produces large, funnel-shaped blooms in shades of red, orange, or yellow, often with contrasting stripes. It is a bulbous plant that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Hippeastrum teretifolium is popular as a decorative plant due to its vibrant flowers and relatively easy care requirements.
