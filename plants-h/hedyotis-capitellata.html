Hedyotis capitellata

Hedyotis capitellata, commonly known as the small-leaf bedstraw, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. The plant has small, opposite leaves and produces clusters of tiny, white or greenish flowers. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that releases tiny seeds.

Hedyotis capitellata is often found in association with other moisture-loving plants in its habitat.
