Hakea leucoptera subsp. sericipes

Hakea leucoptera subsp.

sericipes, commonly known as the silky hakea, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia. It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces cylindrical, red or yellow flower spikes that bloom in spring. It has adapted to arid conditions with its thick, waxy leaves that reduce water loss.

The silky hakea is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
