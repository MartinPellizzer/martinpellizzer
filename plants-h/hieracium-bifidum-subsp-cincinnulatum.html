Hieracium bifidum subsp. cincinnulatum

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

cincinnulatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm.

The plant features basal leaves that are narrowly lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, while the upper leaves are more elongated and less hairy. Its flowering stems bear clusters of yellow flowers with prominent central disk and ray florets.

The species is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
