Hedychium viridibracteatum

Hedychium viridibracteatum, commonly known as the emerald bract ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking green bracts that enclose its fragrant, yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces long, narrow leaves and has a rhizomatous root system typical of the ginger family. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Hedychium viridibracteatum is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
