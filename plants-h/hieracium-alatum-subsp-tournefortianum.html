Hieracium alatum subsp. tournefortianum

Hieracium alatum subsp.

tournefortianum, commonly known as the spreading hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant features a central stem with spreading, hairy branches and leaves that are typically lance-shaped and rough to the touch. Its yellow flowers appear in clusters and bloom from late spring to early autumn.

It is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.
