Hippeastrum araucanum

Hippeastrum araucanum, commonly known as the Araucanian amaryllis, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It produces large, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often with contrasting white or yellow centers. The plant features a bulbous stem that stores nutrients, supporting its growth during dormancy. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, typically emerging after the flowering period.

Hippeastrum araucanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
