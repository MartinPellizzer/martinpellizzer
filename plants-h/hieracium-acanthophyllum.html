Hieracium acanthophyllum

Hieracium acanthophyllum, commonly known as the spiked hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It features yellow flowers with a central disk surrounded by ray florets, and its leaves are usually hairy and serrated.

Hieracium acanthophyllum is often found in open habitats and can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
