Heisteria acicularis

Heisteria acicularis, commonly known as the needle heister, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers during the spring or early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique addition to native plant gardens and ecological studies.
