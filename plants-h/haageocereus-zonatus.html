Haageocereus zonatus

Haageocereus zonatus, commonly known as the Zoned Cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines and bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for desert gardens.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular ornamental plant among cactus enthusiasts.
