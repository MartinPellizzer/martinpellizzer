Helleborus multifidus var. bocconei

Helleborus multifidus var. bocconei, commonly known as the Boccone's hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features dark green, leathery leaves with a distinctive, deeply lobed appearance. The plant produces striking, nodding flowers in shades of purple and white, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodland habitats.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
