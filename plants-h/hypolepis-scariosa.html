Hypolepis scariosa

Hypolepis scariosa, commonly known as the common cliff fern, is a member of the Davalliaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves with a distinctive frond structure that features a central rachis and multiple pinnae. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its fronds are often covered in a thin, papery layer that aids in water retention.

Hypolepis scariosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
