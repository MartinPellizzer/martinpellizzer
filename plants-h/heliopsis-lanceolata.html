Heliopsis lanceolata

Heliopsis lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf heliopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Heliopsis lanceolata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
