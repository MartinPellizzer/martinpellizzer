Hymenachne myosurus

Hymenachne myosurus, commonly known as the water grass or river grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of rivers and lakes. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often submerged or floating, with small, inconspicuous flowers that appear in clusters. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, forming dense mats that help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Hymenachne myosurus plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
