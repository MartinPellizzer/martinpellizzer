Hieracium bourgaei subsp. pogonatoides

Hieracium bourgaei subsp.

pogonatoides, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement.

The plant is distinguished by its hairy stems and leaves, as well as its yellowish-orange composite flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in rocky and alpine environments, often growing in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
