Hieracium elongatum subsp. calvulum

Hieracium elongatum subsp.

calvulum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters.

The plant features elongated, hairy stems and basal leaves that are usually lobed or toothed. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and are surrounded by bracts.

The subspecies calvulum is distinguished by its reduced leaf hairs compared to other members of the species.
