Hieracium paralium

Hieracium paralium, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grassy areas and meadows. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, with a hairy stem that becomes more robust as it grows. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster, producing small seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

It is a perennial plant that thrives in temperate climates and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
