Hieracium bosniacum

Hieracium bosniacum, commonly known as the Bosnian hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

It is often found in regions with mild winters and warm summers, contributing to local biodiversity.
