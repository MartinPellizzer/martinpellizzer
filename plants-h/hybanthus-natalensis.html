Hybanthus natalensis

Hybanthus natalensis, commonly known as Natal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in the early morning, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows as a trailing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or riverine habitats.

Hybanthus natalensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
