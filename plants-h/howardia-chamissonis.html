Howardia chamissonis

Howardia chamissonis, commonly known as the red-flowered sandthorn, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive red flowers, which bloom in clusters and are often used in traditional medicine. The plant has small, spiny leaves and produces yellow fruits that are edible for certain animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Howardia chamissonis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing food for wildlife.
