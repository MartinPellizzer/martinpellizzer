Haloschoenus contractus

Haloschoenus contractus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved saltbush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of salt-excreting glands. This plant thrives in saline and arid environments, making it well adapted to coastal and inland salt-affected soils. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
