Helianthemum violaceum subsp. subobtusatum

Helianthemum violaceum subsp.

subobtusatum, commonly known as the purple rockrose, is a perennial shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and fleshy, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy surface.

The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to Mediterranean climates.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for xeriscaping and coastal gardens.
