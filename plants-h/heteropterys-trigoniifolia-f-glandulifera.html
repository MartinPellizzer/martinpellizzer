Heteropterys trigoniifolia f. glandulifera

Heteropterys trigoniifolia f. glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular-leafed velvetleaf, is a variant of the velvetleaf plant characterized by its glandular-stipitate leaves.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Central and South America. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and prominent veins. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, producing yellowish to reddish petals.

This form is notable for its unique leaf morphology, which distinguishes it from the typical variety of H. trigoniifolia.
