Hieracium chaetocephalum

Hieracium chaetocephalum, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces yellow composite flowers arranged in a single head, which are characteristic of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Its hairy leaves and showy flowers make it a distinctive and ornamental plant in its native environment.
