Helicia verticillata

Helicia verticillata, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow or orange. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Helicia verticillata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
