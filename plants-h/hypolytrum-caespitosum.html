Hypolytrum caespitosum

Hypolytrum caespitosum, commonly known as the tufted lily or swamp lily, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features dense, grass-like foliage with narrow, strap-shaped leaves that are often clustered at the base. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with three petals and three sepals. Hypolytrum caespitosum thrives in wet or marshy environments, often growing in shallow water or along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
