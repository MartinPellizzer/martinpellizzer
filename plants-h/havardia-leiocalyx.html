Havardia leiocalyx

Havardia leiocalyx, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, velvety leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by bean-like pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
