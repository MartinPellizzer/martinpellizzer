Hieracium perasperum

Hieracium perasperum, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright, hairy stems and basal leaves that are typically lobed or toothed. Its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, often with a distinctive central disk.

Hieracium perasperum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it can be invasive in some regions.
