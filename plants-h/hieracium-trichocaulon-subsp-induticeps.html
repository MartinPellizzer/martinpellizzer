Hieracium trichocaulon subsp. induticeps

Hieracium trichocaulon subsp.

induticeps, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are hairy and often have a rough texture, while the stems are usually erect and covered in fine pubescence.

The flowers are yellow, with a distinctive central disk surrounded by petal-like rays, contributing to its ornamental appearance.
