Hieracium brevifolium subsp. cesatianum

Hieracium brevifolium subsp.

cesatianum, commonly known as the Cesarian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow flower heads.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils. It has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces numerous flowering stems with yellow disc flowers.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in some regions.
