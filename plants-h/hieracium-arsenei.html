Hieracium arsenei

Hieracium arsenei, commonly known as the arsenic hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and agricultural fields. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium arsenei is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor environments, making it a resilient weed in various habitats.
