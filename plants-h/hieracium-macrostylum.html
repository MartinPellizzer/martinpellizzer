Hieracium macrostylum

Hieracium macrostylum, commonly known as the large-flowered hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant features a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces showy yellow flowers with a distinctive central disk. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Hieracium macrostylum is often found in disturbed soils and can spread vegetatively through its rhizomes.
