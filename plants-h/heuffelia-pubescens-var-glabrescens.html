Heuffelia pubescens var. glabrescens

Heuffelia pubescens var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth heuffelia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which become less prominent in the varietal form.

The flowers are yellow and arranged in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
