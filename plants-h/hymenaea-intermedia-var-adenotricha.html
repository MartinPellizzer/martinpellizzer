Hymenaea intermedia var. adenotricha

Hymenaea intermedia var. adenotricha, commonly known as the Brazilian peach or pinkwood tree, is a deciduous tree native to the Amazon basin.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale pink bark and compound leaves with long, slender leaflets. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the dry season, followed by woody pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in local communities.
