Helicteres verbascifolia

Helicteres verbascifolia, commonly known as the woolly helicteres, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for tropical gardens and collections.
