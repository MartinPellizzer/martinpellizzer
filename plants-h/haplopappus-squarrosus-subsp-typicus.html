Haplopappus squarrosus subsp. typicus

Haplopappus squarrosus subsp.

typicus, commonly known as the roughleaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to North America. It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a dense, flattened inflorescence called a head. Its leaves are typically lobed and covered with fine, white hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils.
