Hieracioides pontana

Hieracioides pontana, commonly known as the Pontic hawksbeard, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in dry, calcareous soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with ray florets.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
