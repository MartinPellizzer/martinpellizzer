Hieracium subsimplex

Hieracium subsimplex, commonly known as the lesser hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers arranged in a single head. The plant has serrated leaves that are typically hairy, and it often grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions.

Hieracium subsimplex is a perennial herb that can spread through rhizomes, making it somewhat invasive in certain areas.
