Hypoxis elegans

Hypoxis elegans, commonly known as the star of Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling stars, hence its common name. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Hypoxis elegans is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
