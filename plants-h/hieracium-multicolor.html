Hieracium multicolor

Hieracium multicolor, commonly known as the multicolored hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from yellow to red, depending on the variety. Hieracium multicolor typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.

Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
