Hieracium marchesettianum subsp. languidum

Hieracium marchesettianum subsp.

languidum, commonly known as the languid hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

The plant features dark green, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in late summer. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

This subspecies is distinguished by its slender stems and relatively small, inconspicuous flowers.
