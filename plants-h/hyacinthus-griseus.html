Hyacinthus griseus

Hyacinthus griseus, commonly known as the gray hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It produces dense clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, blue, and purple. The plant belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its strap-like, evergreen leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The gray hyacinth is noted for its winter blooming habit, adding early color to the garden.
