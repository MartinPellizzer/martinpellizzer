Hypericum furusei

Hypericum furusei, commonly known as the Japanese St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late summer. Its botanical features include glandular hairs on the stems and leaves, and it is often found in moist, shaded woodlands.

Hypericum furusei is closely related to other species in the Hypericaceae family, which are known for their medicinal properties.
