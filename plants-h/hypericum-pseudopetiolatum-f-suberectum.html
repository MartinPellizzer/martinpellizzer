Hypericum pseudopetiolatum f. suberectum

Hypericum pseudopetiolatum f. suberectum, commonly known as the upright St. John's wort, is a compact, shrub-like plant characterized by its upright growth habit.

It features small, yellowish-green leaves that are opposite and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with four petals, typical of the Hypericaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

This form is distinguished by its more upright and compact structure compared to the typical form of Hypericum pseudopetiolatum.
