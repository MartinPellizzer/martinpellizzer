Helianthus grosseserratus subsp. maximus

Helianthus grosseserratus subsp.

maximus, commonly known as the roughleaf sunflower, is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its rough, serrated leaves and large, showy yellow flowers with a distinctive dark center.

The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy habitats, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its botanical features include hairy stems and involucral leaves that are often glandular.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
