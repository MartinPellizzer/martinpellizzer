Helicia moluccana

Helicia moluccana, commonly known as the Moluccan helicia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the family Theaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that are followed by woody, spherical capsules containing numerous seeds. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
