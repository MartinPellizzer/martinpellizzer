Hieracium turbineum

Hieracium turbineum, commonly known as the sandhill hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, sandy or gravelly soils, often found in open habitats such as heaths and grasslands. The plant features upright, hairy stems that bear alternate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, consisting of numerous small, daisy-like blooms.

Hieracium turbineum is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in its native range.
