Hypericum perforatum var. confertiflorum

Hypericum perforatum var. confertiflorum, commonly known as the denseflower St. John's wort, is a variant of the common St. John's wort.

It is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its yellow flowers and distinctive leaf venation. The leaves are often dotted with translucent oil glands, giving them a perforated appearance. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and woodland edges.

This plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its anti-inflammatory and antidepressant effects.
