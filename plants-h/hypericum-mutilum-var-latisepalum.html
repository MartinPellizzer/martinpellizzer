Hypericum mutilum var. latisepalum

Hypericum mutilum var. latisepalum, commonly known as the broad-sepal hypericum, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its stems are woody and can grow up to several feet in height.

It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
