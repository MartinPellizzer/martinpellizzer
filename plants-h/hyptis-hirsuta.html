Hyptis hirsuta

Hyptis hirsuta, commonly known as hairy hyptis, is a species of flowering plant in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of the Americas, including parts of Mexico, Central America, and the Caribbean. The plant is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

Hyptis hirsuta is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
