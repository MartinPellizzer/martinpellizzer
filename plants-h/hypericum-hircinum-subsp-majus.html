Hypericum hircinum subsp. majus

Hypericum hircinum subsp.

majus, commonly known as the greater St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which appear in late summer and are clustered in dense inflorescences.

The plant has elliptical, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticultural and ecological contexts.
