Huperzia flaccida

Huperzia flaccida, commonly known as the flaccid huperzia, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its simple, linear leaves that are often clustered at the base of the plant. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Huperzia flaccida is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its resilience to various environmental conditions.

Its spores are released from specialized structures called sporangia, which are located on the underside of the leaves.
