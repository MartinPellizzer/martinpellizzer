Hieracium chrysochroum

Hieracium chrysochroum, commonly known as the golden hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded conditions. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
