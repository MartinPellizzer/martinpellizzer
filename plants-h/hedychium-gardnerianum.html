Hedychium gardnerianum

Hedychium gardnerianum, commonly known as the black ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces tall, slender stems with large, arrowhead-shaped leaves. The plant is notable for its fragrant, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a distinctive red or purple spathe. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Hedychium gardnerianum is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
