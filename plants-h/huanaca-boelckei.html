Huanaca boelckei

Huanaca boelckei, commonly known as the Andean moonflower, is a climbing plant native to the highlands of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night. The plant produces small, yellow berries that contain the seeds. Huanaca boelckei is known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used by indigenous communities for its calming effects.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological and ethnobotanical studies.
