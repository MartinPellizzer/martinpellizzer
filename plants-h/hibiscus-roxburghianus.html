Hibiscus roxburghianus

Hibiscus roxburghianus, commonly known as the Chinese hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from red to pink and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces fruits that are capsules containing numerous seeds. Hibiscus roxburghianus is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant blooms and tropical appearance.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
