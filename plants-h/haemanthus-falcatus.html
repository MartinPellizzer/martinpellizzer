Haemanthus falcatus

Haemanthus falcatus, commonly known as the bowstring lily, is a striking plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, downward-curving leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of bright red flowers in the spring, which are followed by long, narrow seed pods. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance make it a favored plant among horticulturists.
