Haplopappus acaulis var. glabratus

Haplopappus acaulis var. glabratus, commonly known as the smoothleaf desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is a low-growing perennial with a compact, mat-forming habit, adapted to withstand harsh desert conditions. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture. Its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, producing small, wind-dispersed seeds.

It plays an important role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert insects.
