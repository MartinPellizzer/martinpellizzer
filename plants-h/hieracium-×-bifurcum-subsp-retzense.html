Hieracium × bifurcum subsp. retzense

Hieracium × bifurcum subsp.

retzense, commonly known as the meadowhawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosettes of lanceolate, often reddish leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the Asteraceae family. It is native to Europe and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
