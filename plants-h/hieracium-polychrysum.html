Hieracium polychrysum

Hieracium polychrysum, commonly known as the golden hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its yellow-orange flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are usually hairy and alternate along the stem, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
