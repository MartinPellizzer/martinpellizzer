Hakea stenophylla subsp. stenophylla

Hakea stenophylla subsp.

stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hakea, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that appear in late winter to early spring.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to the region's Mediterranean climate. It is often found in heathlands and open woodlands, where it contributes to local biodiversity.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
