Hieracium speciosissimum

Hieracium speciosissimum, commonly known as the showy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. It produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
