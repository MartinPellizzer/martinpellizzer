Hieracium × murii

Hieracium × murii, commonly known as the common meadow hawkweed, is a hybrid plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and hairy stems, which are typical features of the genus Hieracium. The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, preferring moist and well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowering period usually occurs in late spring to early summer.

This species is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
