Habrothamnus paniculatus

Habrothamnus paniculatus, commonly known as the panicled flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features a sprawling growth habit with numerous branching stems and small, linear leaves. Its flowers are clustered in paniculate inflorescences, producing small, yellowish blooms that attract pollinators.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
