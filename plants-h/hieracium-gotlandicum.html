Hieracium gotlandicum

Hieracium gotlandicum, commonly known as Gotland hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Baltic region, particularly Gotland Island in Sweden.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. Its flowers are visited by various pollinators, including bees and flies.

Hieracium gotlandicum is known for its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance.
