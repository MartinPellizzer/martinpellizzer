Hypericum simulans

Hypericum simulans, commonly known as the yellow hypericum, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a smooth texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Hypericum simulans is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and can also serve as a habitat for pollinators.
