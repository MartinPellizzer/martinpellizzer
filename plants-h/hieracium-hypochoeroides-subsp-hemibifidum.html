Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp. hemibifidum

Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp.

hemibifidum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow composite flowers.

The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a distinctive basal rosette formation. It thrives in grassy habitats and is often found in disturbed soils.

This subspecies is noted for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
