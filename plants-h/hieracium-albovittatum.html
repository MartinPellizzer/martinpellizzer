Hieracium albovittatum

Hieracium albovittatum, commonly known as the white-bordered hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant features bright yellow flowers with white veins, which are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

Hieracium albovittatum is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
