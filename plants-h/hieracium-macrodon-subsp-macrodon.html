Hieracium macrodon subsp. macrodon

Hieracium macrodon subsp.

macrodon, commonly known as the giant hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its large, showy yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, with a basal rosette formation.

It is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes, making it an invasive species in some regions.
