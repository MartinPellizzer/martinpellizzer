Hieracium serratifrons subsp. lepistoides

Hieracium serratifrons subsp.

lepistoides, commonly known as the serrated hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. It is typically found in meadows and grassy areas across Europe and parts of Asia.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
