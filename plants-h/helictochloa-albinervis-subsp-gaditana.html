Helictochloa albinervis subsp. gaditana

Helictochloa albinervis subsp.

gaditana, commonly known as the Iberian silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its slender, silver-haired leaves and compact growth habit.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as rocky soils and limestone areas. It produces small, pale green to yellowish spikelets that are arranged in panicles.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and ecological significance in Mediterranean ecosystems.
