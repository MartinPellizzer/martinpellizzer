Hieracium braunianum

Hieracium braunianum, commonly known as the Braun's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are alternate, rough, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium braunianum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
