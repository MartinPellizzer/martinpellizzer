Helwingia lanceolata

Helwingia lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf helwingia, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along its stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring, followed by distinctive, winged fruits that aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
