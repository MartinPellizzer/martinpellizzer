Heliosperma retzdorffianum

Heliosperma retzdorffianum, commonly known as the golden bell orchid, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as rainforests, and prefers well-drained soil. Its leaves are elongated and often have a waxy surface, which helps reduce water loss.

Heliosperma retzdorffianum is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses around the world.
