Heracleum piliferum

Heracleum piliferum, commonly known as the hairy cow parsley, is a tall biennial or short-lived perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic compounds and umbrella-like flower clusters. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are covered in soft, hairy textures. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in compound umbels, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant can grow up to 2 meters in height, making it a striking feature in meadows and woodland edges.
