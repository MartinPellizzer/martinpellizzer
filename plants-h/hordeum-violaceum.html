Hordeum violaceum

Hordeum violaceum, commonly known as purple barley, is a species of grass native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences with pale purple or white florets. It thrives in cool, temperate climates and is often found in alpine meadows.

Hordeum violaceum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
