Hieracium circinnatum

Hieracium circinnatum, commonly known as the ringed hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a distinctive ring-like pattern.

Hieracium circinnatum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
