Hydnophytum fusiforme

Hydnophytum fusiforme, commonly known as the bladderleaf or velvetleaf, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its unique bladder-like structures, which are specialized for capturing and digesting small insects. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, pale yellow flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its roots are capable of forming symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Hydnophytum fusiforme is also known for its ability to tolerate shade, making it a common species in forest understories.
