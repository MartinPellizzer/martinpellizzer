Hieracium dubium subsp. micrastrum

Hieracium dubium subsp.

micrastrum, commonly known as the small-flowered hawkweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically features a rosette of basal leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish to reddish flowers.

The plant has a taproot system and is often found in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with hairy surfaces, and the flowers are borne on slender, ascending stems.

It is considered a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.
