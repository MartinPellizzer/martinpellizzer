Haplopappus carthamoides subsp. typicus

Haplopappus carthamoides subsp.

typicus, commonly known as the roughleaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters. Its inflorescence is characterized by a single, elongated pappus that aids in wind dispersal of its seeds.

The species is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in arid environments.
