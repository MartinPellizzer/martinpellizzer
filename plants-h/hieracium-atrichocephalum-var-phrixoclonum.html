Hieracium atrichocephalum var. phrixoclonum

Hieracium atrichocephalum var. phrixoclonum, commonly known as the black-haired hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features dark green, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces, and its flowers are typically yellow with dark centers. It produces small, oval-shaped fruits called cypsela, which are dispersed by wind.

This variant is distinguished by its unique blackish hairs on the leaves and stems, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
