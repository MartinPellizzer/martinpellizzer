Hieracium charrelii

Hieracium charrelii, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are basal, with a rough, hairy texture, and the flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium charrelii is often found in open habitats such as meadows and slopes, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil in harsh conditions.
