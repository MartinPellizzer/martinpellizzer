Hieracium godronianum

Hieracium godronianum, commonly known as Godron's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is known for its ornamental value and has been cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
