Hieracium phloganthemum

Hieracium phloganthemum, commonly known as the flameweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its bright yellow to orange flowers bloom in the summer months, and the leaves are usually rough and hairy.

Hieracium phloganthemum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
