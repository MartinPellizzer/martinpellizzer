Hieracium pumilum

Hieracium pumilum, commonly known as the mouse ear hawkweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically forms dense mats of small, spoon-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in damp woodlands.

Hieracium pumilum is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
