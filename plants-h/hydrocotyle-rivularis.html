Hydrocotyle rivularis

Hydrocotyle rivularis, commonly known as the water pennywort, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in shallow water, wetlands, or along the edges of streams and ponds. The plant has floating or submerged leaves that are usually divided into small, rounded lobes, giving it a delicate appearance. Its flowers are small and white, often clustered in umbels, and it produces small, spherical fruits.

Hydrocotyle rivularis is known for its ability to thrive in moist environments and is often used in water gardens or as a decorative plant in aquatic setups.
