Hibiscus sphaerocarpus

Hibiscus sphaerocarpus, commonly known as the globe hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its spherical, woody蒴果 (capsule) that resembles a globe, hence its common name. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soil with ample sunlight.

Hibiscus sphaerocarpus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
