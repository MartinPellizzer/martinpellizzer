Hieracium bifidum subsp. erucoides

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

erucoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in a single head, surrounded by pale green bracts. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Due to its aggressive growth, it can become invasive in certain regions.
