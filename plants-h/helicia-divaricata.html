Helicia divaricata

Helicia divaricata, commonly known as the variable hollies, is a species of flowering plant in the family Theaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of India, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with leaves that are elliptical and often have a glossy appearance. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds.

Helicia divaricata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and as a landscape plant.
