Helichrysum polhillianum

Helichrysum polhillianum, commonly known as the Cape goldcup, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that resemble small cups. The plant produces bracted involucres that are often mistaken for petals, and it has narrow, linear leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Helichrysum polhillianum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
