Hypaphorus subumbrans var. aculeata

Hypaphorus subumbrans var. aculeata, commonly known as the spiny hypaphorus, is a variant of the hypaphorus subumbrans species, characterized by its distinctive spiny structures.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodland floors or rocky crevices. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a flat, disc-like pattern, typical of the Asteraceae family.
