Hypericum tauricum

Hypericum tauricum, commonly known as the Turkish St. John's Wort, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in summer, typically from June to August. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces red or orange berries in the fall. It is often found in rocky soils and dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Hypericaceae and is characterized by its medicinal properties and historical use in herbal remedies.
