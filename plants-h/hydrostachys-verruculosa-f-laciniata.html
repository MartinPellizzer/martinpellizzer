Hydrostachys verruculosa f. laciniata

Hydrostachys verruculosa f. laciniata, commonly known as the serrated hydrostachys, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and fleshy, bluish-green leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in succulent gardens.
