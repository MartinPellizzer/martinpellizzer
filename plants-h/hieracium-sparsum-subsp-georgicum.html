Hieracium sparsum subsp. georgicum

Hieracium sparsum subsp.

georgicum, commonly known as the Georgian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in the summer. It has rough, hairy stems and leaves that are often glandular.

Its botanical characteristics make it adaptable to a variety of soil types and environmental conditions.
