Heritiera cordata

Heritiera cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf heritiera, is a tropical plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is native to regions of Southeast Asia, including India, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically thick and glossy. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Heritiera cordata is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and along riverbanks.
