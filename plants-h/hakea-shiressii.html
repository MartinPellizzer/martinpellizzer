Hakea shiressii

Hakea shiressii, commonly known as the Geraldton hakea, is an evergreen shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical flower spikes that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in spring. The plant has needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Hakea shiressii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its hardy and low-maintenance characteristics.
