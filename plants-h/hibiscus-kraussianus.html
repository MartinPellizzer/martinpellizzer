Hibiscus kraussianus

Hibiscus kraussianus, commonly known as the Kranskop hibiscus, is a deciduous shrub native to South Africa.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant blooms during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Hibiscus kraussianus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively low maintenance once established.
