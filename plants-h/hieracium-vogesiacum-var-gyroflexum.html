Hieracium vogesiacum var. gyroflexum

Hieracium vogesiacum var. gyroflexum, commonly known as the Vogesian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming basal leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry meadows and rocky soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, with a distinctive flowering period in late summer.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its historical use in traditional medicine.
