Homalocheilos ramosissimum

Homalocheilos ramosissimum, commonly known as the hairy spur orchid, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows from a rhizome and has hairy stems, which give it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
