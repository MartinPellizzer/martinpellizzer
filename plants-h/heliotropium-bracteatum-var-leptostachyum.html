Heliotropium bracteatum var. leptostachyum

Heliotropium bracteatum var. leptostachyum, commonly known as the slender-stemmed heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a soft appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variant is distinguished by its elongated inflorescences and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
