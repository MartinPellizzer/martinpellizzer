Hymenophysa persica

Hymenophysa persica, commonly known as Persian rose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and prefers a temperate climate. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been cultivated for centuries in traditional gardens.

Hymenophysa persica is also noted for its medicinal properties in some traditional healing practices.
