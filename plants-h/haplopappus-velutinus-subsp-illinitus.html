Haplopappus velutinus subsp. illinitus

Haplopappus velutinus subsp.

illinitus, commonly known as the Illinois thistle, is a perennial herb native to the central United States. It is characterized by its grayish-green, fuzzy leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. It has a taproot system and produces achenes with pappus bristles for wind dispersal.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities and a subject of ecological study.
