Huperzia porophila

Huperzia porophila, commonly known as the mossy huperzia, is a species of clubmoss found in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Huperziaceae and is characterized by its simple, linear leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of moss-like structures. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its sporophyte produces spores in small, clustered capsules located at the base of the leaves.

Huperzia porophila is valued for its ecological role in soil stabilization and as a subject of botanical study due to its unique morphology and habitat preferences.
