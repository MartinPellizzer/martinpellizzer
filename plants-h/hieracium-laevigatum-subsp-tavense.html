Hieracium laevigatum subsp. tavense

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

tavense, commonly known as the Tavene hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

The plant has basal rosettes of lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow, with a distinctive central disc surrounded by ray florets.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and is often found in disturbed or nutrient-rich soils.
