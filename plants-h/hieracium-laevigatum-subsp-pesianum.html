Hieracium laevigatum subsp. pesianum

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

pesianum, commonly known as the Persian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are broadly lanceolate, with a rough texture, and its flowers are yellow, arranged in a single head.

It is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
