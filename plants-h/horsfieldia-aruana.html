Horsfieldia aruana

Horsfieldia aruana, commonly known as the Aru orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Indonesia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in humid, shaded environments within lowland forests. The plant produces strikingly colorful flowers that are often yellow or orange with intricate patterns. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the forest floor, and it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

Horsfieldia aruana is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
