Helichrysum oreophilum

Helichrysum oreophilum, commonly known as the mountain everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flowers that resemble small sunflowers. The plant produces small, papery bracts that give it a distinctive appearance, often mistaken for petals. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Helichrysum oreophilum is also noted for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
