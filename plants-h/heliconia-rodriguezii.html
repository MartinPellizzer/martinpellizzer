Heliconia rodriguezii

Heliconia rodriguezii, commonly known as the Rodriguez heliconia, is a tropical plant native to the island of Rodriguez in the Indian Ocean.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble flowers. The plant has long, narrow leaves that form a rosette, and its inflorescence consists of a spiral arrangement of brightly colored bracts. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal forests.

Heliconia rodriguezii is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife.
