Hieracium sipikorense

Hieracium sipikorense, commonly known as the Sipikore hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Sipikore Islands in the South Pacific.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy environments, adapting to the harsh conditions of its habitat. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are usually yellow or orange in color.

Hieracium sipikorense plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing coastal soils.
