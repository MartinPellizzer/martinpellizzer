Hieracium callimorphoides subsp. eurotopolium

Hieracium callimorphoides subsp.

eurotopolium, commonly known as the European yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves and produces yellow flower heads that are typically arranged in a single stem.

The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It has a fibrous root system and spreads primarily through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in agricultural and natural habitats.

Its flowers are typically visited by various pollinators, including bees and hoverflies.
