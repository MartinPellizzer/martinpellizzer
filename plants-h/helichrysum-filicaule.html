Helichrysum filicaule

Helichrysum filicaule, commonly known as the golden marguerite or everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herb with a bushy growth habit, featuring wiry, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-orange flowers that are clustered in dense, daisy-like inflorescences. It is known for its aromatic foliage and is often used in herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory properties.

The flowers are also popular in dried flower arrangements due to their long-lasting nature.
