Heuchera puberula

Heuchera puberula, commonly known as the softleaf alumroot, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features dense, rounded clumps of heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch, ranging in color from silver-green to deep burgundy. The plant produces clusters of small, showy white or pale pink flowers in the spring, which are borne on upright stalks. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its foliage remains attractive throughout the growing season, offering seasonal color changes that enhance garden aesthetics.
