Hydrangea serrata f. cuspidata

Hydrangea serrata f. cuspidata, commonly known as the Japanese mountain hydrangea, is a compact shrub native to Japan.

It features showy, round to conical inflorescences that range from white to pink or blue, depending on soil pH. The leaves are serrated with a pointed tip, giving the plant its scientific name. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom reliably in cooler climates.
