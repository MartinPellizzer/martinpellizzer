Hippeastrum dryades

Hippeastrum dryades, commonly known as the Dryades amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often with a contrasting white or cream center. The plant produces strap-like, grassy leaves that emerge from a fleshy, underground bulb. Its flowering period usually occurs in the spring or early summer, depending on the climate.

Hippeastrum dryades is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
