Hornungia Alpina Subsp. Austroalpina

Alpine hornungia, scientifically known as Hornungia alpina subsp. austroalpina, is a rare and hardy plant adapted to the extreme conditions of high mountain environments. It thrives in alpine meadows and rocky slopes where temperatures are low and the growing season is short. This subspecies is found primarily in the European Alps, particularly in regions like Austria and Italy. Its unique morphology includes small, clustered leaves and delicate white flowers that bloom in early summer. Due to its limited distribution and sensitivity to climate change, Alpine hornungia is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
