Hibiscus spenceri

Hibiscus spenceri, commonly known as the velvetleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically blooming in summer. Hibiscus spenceri is a perennial shrub that thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be grown in gardens or as a houseplant.
