Haplopappus ramulosus

Haplopappus ramulosus, commonly known as the slender pappus plant, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and poor soil conditions. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in dense clusters, and it produces fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.

Haplopappus ramulosus is often found in rocky soils and is a component of desert and sagebrush ecosystems.
