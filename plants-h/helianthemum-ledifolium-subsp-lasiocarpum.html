Helianthemum ledifolium subsp. lasiocarpum

Helianthemum ledifolium subsp.

lasiocarpum, commonly known as the woolly thimblecush, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It features dense, woolly foliage that helps it retain moisture in its arid habitat.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruits are small, dry capsules that release seeds when mature.

This subspecies is adapted to rocky and sandy soils, making it a hardy plant in Mediterranean climates.
