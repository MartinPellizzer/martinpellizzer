Hieracium umbelliferum subsp. cymosiforme

Hieracium umbelliferum subsp.

cymosiforme, commonly known as the clustered hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
