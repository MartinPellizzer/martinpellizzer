Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp. bifidopsis

Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp.

bifidopsis, commonly known as the bifid hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a cluster.

This subspecies is notable for its distinctive bifid (split) leaf lobes, which aid in its identification.
