Helicia arguta

Helicia arguta, commonly known as the hairy helicia, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Theaceae and is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.

Helicia arguta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowering habit.
