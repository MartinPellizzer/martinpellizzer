Hieracium florentinum subsp. piloselloides

Hieracium florentinum subsp.

piloselloides, commonly known as the hairy yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along riverbanks. It has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, clustered flower heads.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
