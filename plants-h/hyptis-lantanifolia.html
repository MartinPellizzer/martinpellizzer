Hyptis lantanifolia

Hyptis lantanifolia, commonly known as sweet basil or wild basil, is a perennial herb native to the Americas.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Hyptis lantanifolia is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
