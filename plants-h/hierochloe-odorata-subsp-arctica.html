Hierochloe odorata subsp. arctica

Hierochloe odorata subsp.

arctica, commonly known as Arctic sweetgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to arctic and alpine regions. It thrives in cold climates and is often found in tundra and rocky soils.

The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slight aromatic scent.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments and is valued for its ecological and cultural significance.
