Horsfieldia peltata

Horsfieldia peltata, commonly known as the Malayan orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hair. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pale yellow or greenish-white, arranged in a dense spike. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing in the understorey of tropical rainforests.

Horsfieldia peltata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
