Helleborine latifolia var. dunensis

Helleborine latifolia var. dunensis, commonly known as the broad-leaved helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces striking, hooded flowers with a yellowish-green to orange hue. Native to the Mediterranean region, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its flowers typically bloom in spring, and the plant is noted for its ability to hybridize with other helleborine species.

The plant's rhizomes are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported medicinal properties.
