Hieracium maurochlorum

Hieracium maurochlorum, commonly known as the dark-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, preferring temperate climates. Its leaves are dark green and often have a bluish or purplish hue, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

It is notable for its ability to thrive in shaded environments, making it a common sight in woodland undergrowth.
