Hieracium atratum subsp. barbulatulum

Hieracium atratum subsp.

barbulatulum, commonly known as the black hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features basal rosettes of dark green, hairy leaves that are often tinged with purple.

The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, flat-topped clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves and stems are covered with fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.
