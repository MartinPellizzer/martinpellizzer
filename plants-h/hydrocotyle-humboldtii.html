Hydrocotyle humboldtii

Hydrocotyle humboldtii, commonly known as the Humboldt spurge, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It features a rosette of heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically light green with a slight waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a unique, low-maintenance plant.
