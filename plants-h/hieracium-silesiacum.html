Hieracium silesiacum

Hieracium silesiacum, commonly known as the Silesian hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom from June to September.

It is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters, making it a notable presence in its native habitats.
