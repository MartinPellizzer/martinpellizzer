Hypericum hyssopifolium f. hyssopifolium

Hypericum hyssopifolium f. hyssopifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological gardens.
