Heliotropium glaucophyllum

Heliotropium glaucophyllum, commonly known as the bluish heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant characterized by its opposite, often hairy leaves and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural settings.
