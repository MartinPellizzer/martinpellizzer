Hieracium praelatescens

Hieracium praelatescens, commonly known as the purple hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and a single, upright stem that bears yellow flowers arranged in a composite head. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It has finely divided, grass-like leaves and produces seeds in a small, dry fruit called a cypsela.

Hieracium praelatescens is often found in temperate regions of Europe and is considered a perennial species.
