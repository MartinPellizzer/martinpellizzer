Haageocereus pseudomelanostele subsp. aureispinus

Haageocereus pseudomelanostele subsp.

aureispinus, commonly known as the golden spined hedgehog cactus, is a species native to Mexico. It is a columnar cactus with a ribbed stem and spines that are typically yellow or golden in color.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and succulent gardens.
