Hieracium vinicaule

Hieracium vinicaule, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium vinicaule is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common weed in many regions.
