Hieracium klingstedtii

Hieracium klingstedtii, commonly known as the Klingstedt's hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rough texture and serrated edges, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a composite head.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
