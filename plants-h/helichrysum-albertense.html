Helichrysum albertense

Helichrysum albertense, commonly known as the Albert's everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellow to orange flowers that resemble daisies. The plant produces fluffy, seed-filled bracts that persist long after the flowers have faded, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Helichrysum albertense is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in floral arrangements.
