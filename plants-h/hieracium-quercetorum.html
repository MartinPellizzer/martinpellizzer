Hieracium quercetorum

Hieracium quercetorum, commonly known as the oak wood hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and along forest edges, preferring moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

Hieracium quercetorum is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and is often found in association with oak trees.
