Hiraea podocarpa

Hiraea podocarpa, commonly known as the white-flowered hiraea, is a small evergreen shrub native to the eastern regions of Australia.

It typically grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments. The plant features narrow, leathery leaves that are dark green in color and have a slightly serrated edge. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in dense inflorescences, blooming during the spring and summer seasons.

Hiraea podocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
