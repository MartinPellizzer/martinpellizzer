Helopus punctatus var. grandispiculus

Helopus punctatus var. grandispiculus, commonly known as the giant spiky hibiscus, is a striking member of the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range from pink to red, with prominent, elongated stamens. The plant features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its unique floral structure makes it a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.
