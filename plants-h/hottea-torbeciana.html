Hottea torbeciana

Hottea torbeciana, commonly known as the Torbecian tea plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are widely used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported calming and digestive properties.

Due to its unique appearance and medicinal value, it is a subject of interest in both botanical research and horticultural cultivation.
