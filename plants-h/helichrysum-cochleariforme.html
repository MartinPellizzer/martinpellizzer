Helichrysum cochleariforme

Helichrysum cochleariforme, commonly known as the golden crown or helichrysum of the Cape, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, yellow-orange inflorescences that resemble a crown, hence its common name. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its aromatic, hairy leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Helichrysum cochleariforme is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
