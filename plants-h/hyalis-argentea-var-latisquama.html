Hyalis argentea var. latisquama

Hyalis argentea var. latisquama, commonly known as silverleaf hyalis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are densely covered with white, woolly hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded, well-drained soils.

Its unique foliage and delicate flowers make it a distinctive addition to native plant gardens.
