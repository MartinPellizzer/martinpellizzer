Helichrysum nimbicola

Helichrysum nimbicola, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, bright yellow to orange flowers that resemble tiny suns. The plant produces numerous narrow, linear leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The flowers are also used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
