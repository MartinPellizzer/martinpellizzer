Hieracium accumulatum

Hieracium accumulatum, commonly known as the clustered hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and disturbed soils, forming dense clusters of yellow flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stem is often reddish and somewhat woolly.

Hieracium accumulatum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in meadows and along roadsides.
