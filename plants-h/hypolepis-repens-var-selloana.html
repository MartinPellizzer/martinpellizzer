Hypolepis repens var. selloana

Hypolepis repens var. selloana, commonly known as the creeping shield fern, is a variant of the common shield fern.

It is a terrestrial fern that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant features a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread horizontally and form dense mats. Its fronds are pinnately compound, with lanceolate to ovate leaflets that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural settings for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
