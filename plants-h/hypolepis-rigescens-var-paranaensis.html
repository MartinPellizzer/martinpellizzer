Hypolepis rigescens var. paranaensis

Hypolepis rigescens var. paranaensis, commonly known as the Parana fern, is a tropical fern native to South America.

It belongs to the family Davalliaceae and is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves with a distinctive, often serrated margin. The fronds are typically leathery and can grow quite large, reaching lengths of up to 1 meter. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is valued for its ornamental foliage and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
