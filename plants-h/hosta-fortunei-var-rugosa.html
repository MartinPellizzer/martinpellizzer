Hosta fortunei var. rugosa

Hosta fortunei var. rugosa, commonly known as the rugosa hosta, is a perennial plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, wavy-edged leaves that are typically a deep green with a bluish tint. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale blue during the summer months. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental foliage and is a popular choice for woodland gardens.
