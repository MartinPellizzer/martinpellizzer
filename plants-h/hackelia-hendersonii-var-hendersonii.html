Hackelia hendersonii var. hendersonii

Hackelia hendersonii var. hendersonii, commonly known as Henderson's bluecurls, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, sky-blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Its floral structure includes spurred petals, which are adapted for pollination by bees and other insects.
