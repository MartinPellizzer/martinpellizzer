Hibiscus persicifolius

Hibiscus persicifolius, commonly known as the peach-leaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that resemble peach leaves, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Hibiscus persicifolius is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
