Helianthus angustifolius subsp. nuttallii

Helianthus angustifolius subsp.

nuttallii, commonly known as Nuttall's narrowleaf sunflower, is a perennial herb native to the central and southern United States. It typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, reaching heights of up to 2 meters.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces yellow composite flowers with prominent central disk florets. It is characterized by its robust stem and is often found in disturbed soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental value and its role in supporting pollinators.
