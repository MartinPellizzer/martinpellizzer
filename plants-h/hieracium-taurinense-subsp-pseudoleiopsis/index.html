Hieracium Taurinense Subsp. Pseudoleiopsis

Hieracium taurinense subsp. pseudoleiopsis is a rare and elusive plant species native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus. It belongs to the dandelion family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and hairy leaves. This subspecies is known for its limited distribution and is often found in rocky or limestone soils. Due to its rarity, it is considered a vulnerable species facing threats from habitat loss and climate change. Conservation efforts are crucial to ensure its survival in the wild.
