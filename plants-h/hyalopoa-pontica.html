Hyalopoa pontica

Hyalopoa pontica, commonly known as the Pontic hyalopoa, is a rare aquatic plant native to the Black Sea region.

It is characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water's surface. The plant has small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments with soft, acidic water.

Due to habitat loss and pollution, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to survive.
