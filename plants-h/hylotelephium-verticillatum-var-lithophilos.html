Hylotelephium verticillatum var. lithophilos

Hylotelephium verticillatum var. lithophilos, commonly known as the rock crassula, is a succulent plant native to rocky habitats in southern Africa.

It features compact, cushion-like growth with fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or red during the summer months. It is well adapted to arid conditions, storing water in its thick leaves and stems.

This variety is popular among succulent enthusiasts for its resilience and ornamental appeal.
