Heliconia stricta

Heliconia stricta, commonly known as the crimson heliconia, is a tropical plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, boat-shaped inflorescences that are typically bright red or orange in color. The plant belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is known for its large, waxy, paddle-shaped leaves. Its flowers are primarily pollinated by birds, particularly hummingbirds, due to their shape and nectar-rich structure.

Heliconia stricta is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in warm climates for its vibrant appearance and unique foliage.
