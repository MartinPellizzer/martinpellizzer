Hieracium amygdalinum

Hieracium amygdalinum, commonly known as the almond-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and ragworts, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features basal rosettes of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are typically yellow or orange in color. It produces small, oval seeds that are dispersed by wind, and it thrives in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and roadsides.

Hieracium amygdalinum is also known for its potential medicinal properties due to the presence of compounds like amygdalin.
