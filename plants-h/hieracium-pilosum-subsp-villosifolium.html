Hieracium pilosum subsp. villosifolium

Hieracium pilosum subsp.

villosifolium, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems, which give it its common name.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered with fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
