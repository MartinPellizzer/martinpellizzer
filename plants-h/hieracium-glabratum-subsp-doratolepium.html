Hieracium glabratum subsp. doratolepium

Hieracium glabratum subsp.

doratolepium, commonly known as the purple hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and showy yellow to orange composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in meadows and rocky slopes at high elevations.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species within its range.
