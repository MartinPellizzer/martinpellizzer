Heteropterys stannea

Heteropterys stannea, commonly known as the red-flowered piper, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by elliptical, woody fruits. Its roots and leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their potential anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitats.
