Hieracium diaphanoides subsp. trilacerum

Hieracium diaphanoides subsp.

trilacerum, commonly known as the translucent hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. It is notable for its delicate appearance and its role in supporting local pollinators.

However, it is considered a potential invasive species in some regions due to its rapid spread and competitive growth.
