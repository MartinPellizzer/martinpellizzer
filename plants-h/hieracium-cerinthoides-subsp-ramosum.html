Hieracium cerinthoides subsp. ramosum

Hieracium cerinthoides subsp.

ramosum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

The plant features a central stem with numerous branches, giving it a bushy appearance. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and often have a reddish or purple tinge.

The flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, characteristic of the Asteraceae family.
