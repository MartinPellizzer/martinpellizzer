Hyptis crinita var. polycephala

Hyptis crinita var. polycephala, commonly known as curlytop hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, and thrives in well-drained soils.

Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
