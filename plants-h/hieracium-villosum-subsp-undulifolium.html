Hieracium villosum subsp. undulifolium

Hieracium villosum subsp.

undulifolium, commonly known as the wavy-leaved hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy structures.

The plant features hairy stems and leaves with a wavy, undulating margin, which gives it its common name. Its flowers are typically yellow and arranged in dense clusters, attracting various pollinators.

It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands.
