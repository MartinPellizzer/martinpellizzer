Hypericum lysimachioides var. lysimachioides

Hypericum lysimachioides var. lysimachioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Hypericaceae and is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces bright orange-yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are followed by red-orange berries. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in moist, woodland habitats.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
