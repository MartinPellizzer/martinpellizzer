Hibiscus fluvialis

Hibiscus fluvialis, commonly known as the river hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink or red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in moist, well-drained soil. It is often found near water sources such as rivers and wetlands.

Hibiscus fluvialis is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to provide habitat for various wildlife.
