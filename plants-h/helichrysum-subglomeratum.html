Helichrysum subglomeratum

Helichrysum subglomeratum, commonly known as the golden everlasting or yellow helichrysum, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, yellow-orange flower heads that resemble small globes. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically clustered in compact inflorescences. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

The leaves are narrow and linear, with a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
