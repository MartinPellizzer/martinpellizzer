Henckelia crocea

Henckelia crocea, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in fine, silky hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly hairy surface. Its most striking feature is the bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Henckelia crocea thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.
