Hieracium subramosum subsp. fasciculare

Hieracium subramosum subsp.

fasciculare, commonly known as the clustered hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces numerous yellow flowers in clusters.

Native to Europe, it thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. The plant has small, glandular hairs on its stems and leaves, which contribute to its distinctive appearance.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can be an indicator of nutrient-rich environments.
