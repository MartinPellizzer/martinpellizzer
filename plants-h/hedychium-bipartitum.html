Hedychium bipartitum

Hedychium bipartitum, commonly known as the split-leaf ginger, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Himalayas.

It features striking, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often divided, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Hedychium bipartitum is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
