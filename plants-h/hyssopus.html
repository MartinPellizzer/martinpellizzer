Hyssopus

Hyssopus, commonly known as hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces spikes of blue or purple flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Hyssopus is often used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and expectorant properties.

Its aromatic leaves and flowers are also used in culinary applications and herbal teas.
