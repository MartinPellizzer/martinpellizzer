Hyoseris minima

Hyoseris minima, commonly known as the lesser helipterum, is a small annual herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows to a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters and features hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a cluster at the ends of the stems. Its flowers are followed by pale green, spherical fruits that contain several seeds.

Hyoseris minima is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in dry, sunny environments.
