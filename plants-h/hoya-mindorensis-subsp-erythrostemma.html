Hoya mindorensis subsp. erythrostemma

Hoya mindorensis subsp.

erythrostemma, commonly known as the Mindanao wax plant, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines. It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers.

The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. It produces fragrant, pinkish-white blooms that appear in late spring to early summer. Hoya mindorensis subsp.

erythrostemma is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements in cultivation.
