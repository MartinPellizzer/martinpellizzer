Hirschfeldia incana var. geniculata

Hirschfeldia incana var. geniculata, commonly known as the rock cress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pinnately compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments. Its structure includes a basal rosette of leaves with a hairy surface, and it produces elongated, branching stems that bear clusters of yellow flowers.

This variety is notable for its resilience and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
