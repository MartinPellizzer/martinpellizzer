Hymenophyllum torricellianum

Hymenophyllum torricellianum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spathulate fern, is a small, epiphytic fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, narrow, and often overlapping leaves. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically growing on tree trunks or rocks. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, with a distinctive spathulate (spoon-shaped) appearance.

Hymenophyllum torricellianum is known for its intricate and delicate fronds, which are often adorned with fine, hair-like structures.
