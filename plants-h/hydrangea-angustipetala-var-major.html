Hydrangea angustipetala var. major

Hydrangea angustipetala var. major, commonly known as the narrowleaf hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, cone-shaped flower clusters that range in color from white to pink depending on soil pH. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a smooth, dark green upper surface. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for studying plant adaptation and regional flora.
