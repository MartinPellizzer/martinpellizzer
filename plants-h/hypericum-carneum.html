Hypericum carneum

Hypericum carneum, commonly known as the red-stemmed St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant has small, elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often reddish in color. Hypericum carneum is often found in sunny, well-drained soils, such as those in rocky or woodland areas.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
