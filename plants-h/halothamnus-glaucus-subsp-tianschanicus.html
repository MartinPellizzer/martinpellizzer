Halothamnus glaucus subsp. tianschanicus

Halothamnus glaucus subsp.

tianschanicus, commonly known as the Tianshan halothamnus, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and silver-gray foliage, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, typically in June and July. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude climates.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to extreme environmental conditions.
