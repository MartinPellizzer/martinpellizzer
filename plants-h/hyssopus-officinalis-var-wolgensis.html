Hyssopus officinalis var. wolgensis

Hyssopus officinalis var. wolgensis, commonly known as Siberian hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces spikes of pale blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

This variety is valued for its aromatic foliage and historical use in traditional medicine.
