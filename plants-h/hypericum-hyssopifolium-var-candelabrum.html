Hypericum hyssopifolium var. candelabrum

Hypericum hyssopifolium var. candelabrum, commonly known as the candlestick St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, candle-like arrangement of its flowering stems. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
