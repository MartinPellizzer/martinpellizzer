Hyptis kempffiana

Hyptis kempffiana, commonly known as the golden hyptis, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

The plant's ornamental value has also led to its cultivation in gardens and landscapes.
