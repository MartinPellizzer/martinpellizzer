Hirtella canomensis

Hirtella canomensis, commonly known as the Brazilian hirtella, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its small, white or pale pink flowers and dense, hairy foliage. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with fine pubescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Hirtella canomensis is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
