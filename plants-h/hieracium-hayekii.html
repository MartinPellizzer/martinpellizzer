Hieracium hayekii

Hieracium hayekii, commonly known as Hayek's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust root systems. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, where it thrives in well-drained soils and cooler climates. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are usually lanceolate and covered in fine hairs.

Hieracium hayekii is noted for its yellow flower heads, which consist of numerous small florets arranged in a disc-like structure.
