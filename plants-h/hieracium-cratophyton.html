Hieracium cratophyton

Hieracium cratophyton, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It has a fibrous root system and can spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in some areas.

The flowers are typically visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
