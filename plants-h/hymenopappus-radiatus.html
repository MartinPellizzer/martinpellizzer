Hymenopappus radiatus

Hymenopappus radiatus, commonly known as the goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has a compact, herbaceous growth habit with hairy stems and leaves. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense, radial clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Hymenopappus radiatus is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and open habitats.
