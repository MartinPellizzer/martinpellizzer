Habenaria triptera

Habenaria triptera, commonly known as the three-lobed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features a slender, erect stem with several leaves and produces clusters of small, pale green to white flowers. Its flowers have three distinct lobes, giving it its common name, and are often fragrant.

The genus Habenaria is known for its diverse floral structures, and H. triptera is no exception, showcasing intricate adaptations for pollination.
