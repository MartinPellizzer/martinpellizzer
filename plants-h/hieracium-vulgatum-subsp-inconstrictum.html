Hieracium vulgatum subsp. inconstrictum

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

inconstrictum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a reddish or purple underside, while the flowers are yellow with a central disc.

It is known for its aggressive growth and can sometimes become invasive in certain regions.
