Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp. nervulosum

Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp.

nervulosum, commonly known as the nervulate hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to reddish disc flowers surrounded by pale yellow ray florets.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped with a hairy surface and a distinct central vein, which gives it the subspecific name *nervulosum*. It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides.

This subspecies is often found in moist to moderately dry soils and is considered a perennial herb.
