Hibiscus oligospermus

Hibiscus oligospermus, commonly known as the Hawaiian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink or red in color. Its leaves are ovate and have a rough texture, and the plant is adapted to the tropical climate of its native habitat.

Hibiscus oligospermus is often found in coastal areas and is an important part of the local ecosystem.
