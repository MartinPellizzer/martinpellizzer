Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp. peracutisquamum

Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp.

peracutisquamum, commonly known as the sharp-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features basal leaves that are lanceolate and covered with stiff, bristly hairs. Its yellow composite flowers are arranged in a single head, with ray florets that are usually longer than the tubular florets.

It is characterized by its sharp, acuminate leaf tips and is part of the Asteraceae family.
