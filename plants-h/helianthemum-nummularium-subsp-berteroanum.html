Helianthemum nummularium subsp. berteroanum

Helianthemum nummularium subsp.

berteroanum, commonly known as the silver saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, rounded, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces yellow flowers in early summer, typically in clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is drought-tolerant.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
