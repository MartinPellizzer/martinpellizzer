Heliotropium grandiflorum

Heliotropium grandiflorum, commonly known as the largeflower heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant, violet-blue flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Heliotropium grandiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
