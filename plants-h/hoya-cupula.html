Hoya cupula

Hoya cupula, commonly known as the cupula wisteria, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and clusters of small, waxy flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The leaves are heart-shaped and glossy, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This plant is popular among indoor gardeners for its beauty and relatively low maintenance requirements.
