Hieracium strictum var. subcrocatum

Hieracium strictum var. subcrocatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow composite flowers. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, daisy-like blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a component of diverse ecosystems and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
