Helianthus divaricatus var. latifolius

Helianthus divaricatus var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf sunflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features erect, branched stems that can grow up to 2 meters tall, adorned with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are broad and often hairy. The plant produces yellow flowers with a distinctive central disk surrounded by ray florets, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in moist, rich soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and prairie habitats.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a food source for various pollinators and wildlife.
