Hieracium subconvexum

Hieracium subconvexum, commonly known as the convex hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and typically showy blooms. The plant features basal leaves that are lanceolate to elliptic in shape, often with hairy surfaces. Its flowering heads consist of both ray and disk florets, producing yellow flowers that attract pollinators.

Hieracium subconvexum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
