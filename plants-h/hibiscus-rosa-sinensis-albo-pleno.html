Hibiscus rosa-sinensis albo-pleno

Hibiscus rosa-sinensis albo-pleno, commonly known as the white hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that are predominantly white with a subtle pink or red hue at the base. The plant features heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are glossy and arranged oppositely along the stems. It blooms throughout the year in warm climates, producing trumpet-shaped flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and as a potted plant.
