Hieracium magdalenae-kaeseriae

Hieracium magdalenae-kaeseriae, commonly known as the Magdalene-Kaeseria hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its yellow ray florets and a central disk of tubular flowers, typical of the genus Hieracium. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine meadows, preferring cool, moist environments. It has a rosette of basal leaves and stems that are usually unbranched.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
