Hieracium bifidum subsp. eriopodoides

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

eriopodoides, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant features a basal rosette of leaves that are hairy and often covered in fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are typically yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.
