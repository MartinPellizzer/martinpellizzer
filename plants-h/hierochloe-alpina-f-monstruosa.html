Hierochloe alpina f. monstruosa

Hierochloe alpina f. monstruosa, commonly known as the Alpine Hairgrass, is a rare and striking variant of the native grass species Hierochloe alpina.

It is characterized by its unusually long, flowing inflorescences that can extend several feet above the foliage, giving it a dramatic and almost otherworldly appearance. This form thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, where it is adapted to cold, high-altitude conditions with poor soil. Its unique morphology makes it a subject of interest in botanical studies and a sought-after plant among horticulturists.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered plant in many regions.
