Haplostemma arabicum

Haplostemma arabicum, commonly known as the Arabian thimblecush, is a flowering plant native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spiky fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and coastal areas.

Haplostemma arabicum is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low precipitation, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
