Hymenophyllum lamellatum

Hymenophyllum lamellatum, commonly known as the silver maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are often covered in a silvery, waxy coating. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are leathery and have a distinctive, intricate structure with closely spaced, scale-like segments.

Hymenophyllum lamellatum is known for its ability to retain moisture, which is essential for its survival in humid, low-light conditions.
