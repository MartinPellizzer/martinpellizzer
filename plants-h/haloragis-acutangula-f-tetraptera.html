Haloragis acutangula f. tetraptera

Haloragis acutangula f. tetraptera, commonly known as the four-winged willow herb, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, often prostrate stems that can form dense mats. The leaves are alternate, linear, and often covered with fine hairs, while the inflorescence consists of small, clustered flowers. Its flowers are typically greenish-white with prominent stamens, and it produces small, three-lobed fruits.

This plant thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal or wetland environments.
