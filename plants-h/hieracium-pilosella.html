Hieracium pilosella

Hieracium pilosella, commonly known as mouse-ear hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are hairy and spoon-shaped, while the flowers are yellow and bloom from June to September.

Hieracium pilosella is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is considered invasive in some regions.
