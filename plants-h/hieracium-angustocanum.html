Hieracium angustocanum

Hieracium angustocanum, commonly known as narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a dense, daisy-like cluster.

Hieracium angustocanum is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
