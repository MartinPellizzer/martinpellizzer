Hesperis dinarica

Hesperis dinarica, commonly known as the Dinaric honesty, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces small, white flowers with four petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Hesperis dinarica is often found in meadows and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
