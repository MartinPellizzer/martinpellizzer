Hieracium setulosum

Hieracium setulosum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are hairy and often lobed, while its yellow flowers are arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

Hieracium setulosum is often considered a weed due to its aggressive growth and ability to colonize various habitats.
