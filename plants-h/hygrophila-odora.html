Hygrophila odora

Hygrophila odora, commonly known as the fragrant hygrophila, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, delicate flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Hygrophila odora is also cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and pleasant scent.
