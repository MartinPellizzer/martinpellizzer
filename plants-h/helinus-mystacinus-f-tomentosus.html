Helinus mystacinus f. tomentosus

Helinus mystacinus f. tomentosus, commonly known as the hairy helinus, is a unique variant of the helinus species characterized by its dense, woolly foliage.

This plant is native to specific regions in South Africa, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. Its leaves are typically broad and covered in a fine, white to greyish pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often green or yellow in color.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a delicate and somewhat obscure member of the Aizoaceae family.
