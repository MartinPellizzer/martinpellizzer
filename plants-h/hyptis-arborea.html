Hyptis arborea

Hyptis arborea, commonly known as the tree hyptis, is a perennial shrub or small tree native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry forests and savannas.

Hyptis arborea is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
