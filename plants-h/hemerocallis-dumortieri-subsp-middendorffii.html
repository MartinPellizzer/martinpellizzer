Hemerocallis dumortieri subsp. middendorffii

Hemerocallis dumortieri subsp.

middendorffii, commonly known as the Middle Eastern daylily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Central Asia. It features strap-shaped, lanceolate leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy.

The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom briefly in the early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
