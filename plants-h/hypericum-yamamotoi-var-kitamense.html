Hypericum yamamotoi var. kitamense

Hypericum yamamotoi var. kitamense, commonly known as the Japanese St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Hypericaceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It blooms in late summer, producing clusters of small, bright flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
