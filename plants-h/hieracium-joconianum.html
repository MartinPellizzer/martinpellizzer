Hieracium joconianum

Hieracium joconianum, commonly known as the Joconian hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open habitats, thriving in temperate climates. It produces showy yellow or orange flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
