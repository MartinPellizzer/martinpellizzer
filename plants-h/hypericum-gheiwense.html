Hypericum gheiwense

Hypericum gheiwense, commonly known as the Gheiwens' St. John's Wort, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the family Hypericaceae.

It is native to specific regions in East Africa, particularly Kenya and Tanzania, where it thrives in woodland and savanna habitats. The plant is characterized by its yellow flowers, which are typically arranged in clusters and bloom during the dry season. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often covered in fine hairs, while the stems are woody and branched.

Hypericum gheiwense is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations and potential medicinal properties.
