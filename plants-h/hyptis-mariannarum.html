Hyptis mariannarum

Hyptis mariannarum, commonly known as the Caribbean mint, is a perennial herb native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their fragrant leaves. The plant features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy texture. Its small, tubular flowers are typically pale blue or purple, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

Hyptis mariannarum is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
