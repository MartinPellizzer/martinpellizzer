Hatiora rosea f. remanens

Hatiora rosea f. remanens, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a compact, branching plant native to Brazil.

It features vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in winter, typically in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant has thick, fleshy stems that are segmented and can be easily propagated through stem cuttings. Its leaves are usually oval and slightly pointed, and it thrives in bright, indirect light with moderate watering.

This unique cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and ability to bloom reliably under proper care.
