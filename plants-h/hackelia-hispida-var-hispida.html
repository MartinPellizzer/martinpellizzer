Hackelia hispida var. hispida

Hackelia hispida var. hispida, commonly known as the hairy beardtongue, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features upright stems covered in soft, glandular hairs, giving it a distinctive fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine pubescence, contributing to its overall textured look.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and rocky slopes.
