Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp. cyanellum

Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp.

cyanellum, commonly known as the blue hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to central and eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are upright and often branched.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
