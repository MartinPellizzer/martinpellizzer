Hieracium hauthalianum

Hieracium hauthalianum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are broadly lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

The plant is known for its ability to spread rapidly through rhizomes and seeds, making it an invasive species in some regions.
