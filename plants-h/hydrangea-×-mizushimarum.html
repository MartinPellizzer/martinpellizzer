Hydrangea × mizushimarum

Hydrangea × mizushimarum, commonly known as the Japanese hydrangea, is a hybrid plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy flower clusters that can range in color from blue to pink, depending on soil pH. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with thick, woody stems and large, lobed leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

Hydrangea × mizushimarum is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to bloom repeatedly throughout the growing season.
