Hieracium vulgatum subsp. irriguum

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

irriguum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 50 cm.

The plant features basal rosettes of leaves and produces yellow flowers in a single head, often surrounded by leafy bracts. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stem is hairy and somewhat reddish in color.

This subspecies is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
