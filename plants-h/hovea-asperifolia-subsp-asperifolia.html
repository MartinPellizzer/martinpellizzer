Hovea asperifolia subsp. asperifolia

Hovea asperifolia subsp.

asperifolia, commonly known as the rough-hovea, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves with serrated margins.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming from late winter to early spring. Its woody stems are covered in rough, glandular hairs, giving it a distinctive texture.

This subspecies is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions of its native range.
