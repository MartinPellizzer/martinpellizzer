Hydrangea serrata var. acuminata

Hydrangea serrata var. acuminata, commonly known as the Japanese mountain hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its serrated, ovate leaves that turn red in the fall. The plant produces large, cone-shaped flower clusters that range in color from white to pink, depending on soil pH. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained, acidic soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and hardy nature in temperate climates.
