Haemanthus pauculifolius

Haemanthus pauculifolius, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, sword-shaped leaves and dense clusters of bright red, tubular flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its botanical features include a short, fibrous root system and a rhizomatous base that allows it to spread vegetatively.

It is well adapted to arid conditions and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its vibrant appearance.
