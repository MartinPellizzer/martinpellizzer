Hiraea pachypoda var. caucana

Hiraea pachypoda var. caucana, commonly known as the Caucasian hiraea, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its robust, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded locations.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
