Hieracium tumidiceps

Hieracium tumidiceps, commonly known as the swollen hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides, and it thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are rough and hairy, with a distinctive lobed shape, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium tumidiceps is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in many regions.
