Hippophae yunnanensis

Hippophae yunnanensis, commonly known as the Yunnan sea buckthorn, is a shrub native to China, particularly in the Yunnan province.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange berries that are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, with a rough texture, and it produces small, yellow flowers in the spring.

The species is valued for its ecological role in soil stabilization and its potential medicinal uses.
