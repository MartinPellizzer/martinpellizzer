Hippocratea integrifolia

Hippocratea integrifolia, commonly known as the white-veined orchid, is a species of flowering plant in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its striking white and green veined leaves and its fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its beauty and unique foliage.
