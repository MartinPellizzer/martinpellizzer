Hieracium grovesianum subsp. nigrotectorium

Hieracium grovesianum subsp.

nigrotectorium, commonly known as the black-barked hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

The plant features upright stems that are often dark green to black in color, giving it its common name. Its flowers are yellow and borne in dense, flat-topped clusters, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It has finely divided leaves and is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
