Hypericum papuanum

Hypericum papuanum, commonly known as the Papuan St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Papua, Indonesia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months. Its leaves are usually ovate or elliptic in shape, with a rough texture and a distinctive aromatic scent.

Hypericum papuanum is known for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic qualities.
