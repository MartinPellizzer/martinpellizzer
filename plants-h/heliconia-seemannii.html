Heliconia seemannii

Heliconia seemannii, commonly known as the red heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its large, brightly colored inflorescences that resemble a hand or a crown. The plant features elongated, waxy bracts that enclose numerous small, tubular flowers, which are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Heliconia seemannii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for use in floral arrangements.
