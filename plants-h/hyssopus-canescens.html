Hyssopus canescens

Hyssopus canescens, commonly known as Spanish hyssop, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces spikes of blue to violet flowers, which bloom in the summer months. It is often used in herbal medicine for its antiseptic and expectorant properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, also known as the mint family.
