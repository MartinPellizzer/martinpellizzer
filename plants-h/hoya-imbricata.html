Hoya imbricata

Hoya imbricata, commonly known as the "Chinese Wisteria," is a species of flowering plant native to China and Southeast Asia.

It is a trailing or climbing plant characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves and clusters of small, star-shaped white flowers. The plant produces a distinctive, fragrant, waxy fruit that contains numerous tiny seeds. Hoya imbricata is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

It is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
