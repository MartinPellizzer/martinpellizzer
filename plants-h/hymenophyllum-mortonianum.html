Hymenophyllum mortonianum

Hymenophyllum mortonianum, commonly known as the Mortons' hymenophyllum, is a species of moss belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on rock surfaces or in forest undergrowth. Its spore-producing structures, called sporophytes, are small and clustered, typical of many mosses.

This species is notable for its intricate leaf anatomy and is often used in studies of bryophyte physiology and ecology.
