Helmia multiflora

Helmia multiflora, commonly known as the purple fringed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, woodlands and thrives in partial shade. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture.

Helmia multiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
