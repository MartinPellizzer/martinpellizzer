Helicia ternifolia

Helicia ternifolia, commonly known as the three-leafed helicia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Theaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are arranged in whorls of three. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
