Hiraea primaeva

Hiraea primaeva, commonly known as the dawn's first bloom, is a rare flowering plant native to the misty highlands of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale blue petals that open at sunrise, symbolizing the beginning of a new day. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, waxy coating, which helps retain moisture in its humid habitat. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by early morning bees, making it an important species for local ecosystems.

Due to habitat loss, Hiraea primaeva is now classified as endangered, prompting conservation efforts to protect its survival.
