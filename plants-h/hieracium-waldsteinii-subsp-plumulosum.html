Hieracium waldsteinii subsp. plumulosum

Hieracium waldsteinii subsp.

plumulosum, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and yellow flower heads.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. It produces small, daisy-like flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn.

The subspecies plumulosum is distinguished by its dense, woolly pubescence, which gives it a distinctive appearance.
