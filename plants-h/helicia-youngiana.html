Helicia youngiana

Helicia youngiana, commonly known as the Chinese holly or young's holly, is a small evergreen shrub native to China.

It belongs to the family Theaceae and is characterized by its dense, glossy green foliage and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds but generally not for humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Helicia youngiana is valued for its attractive appearance and adaptability to various garden conditions.
