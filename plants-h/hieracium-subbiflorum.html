Hieracium subbiflorum

Hieracium subbiflorum, commonly known as the lesser spearthistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in grasslands, wastelands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough, hairy texture.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
