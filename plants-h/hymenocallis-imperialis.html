Hymenocallis imperialis

Hymenocallis imperialis, commonly known as the imperial hurricane lily, is a striking tropical plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers with a prominent yellow throat, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant features strap-like, dark green leaves that are broad and lance-shaped, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in warm climates.

Hymenocallis imperialis is also known for its ability to produce bulb-like structures, which can be used for propagation.
