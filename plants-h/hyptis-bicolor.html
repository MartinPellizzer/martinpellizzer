Hyptis bicolor

Hyptis bicolor, commonly known as the purple hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Hyptis bicolor is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
