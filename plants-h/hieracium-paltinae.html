Hieracium paltinae

Hieracium paltinae, commonly known as the Italian hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along riverbanks, preferring temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers consist of yellow disk florets surrounded by pale yellow to white ray florets.

Hieracium paltinae is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and can sometimes become invasive in certain regions.
