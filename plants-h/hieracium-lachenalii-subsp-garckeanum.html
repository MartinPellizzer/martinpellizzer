Hieracium lachenalii subsp. garckeanum

Hieracium lachenalii subsp.

garckeanum, commonly known as the Garckean hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers that are typically arranged in a single head, with ray florets surrounding disc florets. It thrives in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or steppe habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
