Hieracium aquilum

Hieracium aquilum, commonly known as the mountain hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy yellow flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Hieracium aquilum is known for its ability to thrive in harsh alpine environments.
