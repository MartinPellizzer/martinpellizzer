Hydrocotyle globifera

Hydrocotyle globifera, commonly known as the globeflower, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, rounded leaves that resemble small globes, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in wet, swampy areas and is often found in freshwater habitats. It produces small white or pink flowers that bloom throughout the year.

Hydrocotyle globifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in water gardens.
