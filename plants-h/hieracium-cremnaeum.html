Hieracium cremnaeum

Hieracium cremnaeum, commonly known as the Cretan hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Crete.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. It features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, along with flowering stems that bear yellow-orange composite flowers.

Hieracium cremnaeum is often found in open habitats such as slopes and grasslands, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
