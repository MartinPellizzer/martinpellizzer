Hibiscus selesiensis

Hibiscus selesiensis, commonly known as the Sierra Leone hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in moist, shaded environments. It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cotton and okra.

Hibiscus selesiensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
