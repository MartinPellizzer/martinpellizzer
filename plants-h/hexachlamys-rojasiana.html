Hexachlamys rojasiana

Hexachlamys rojasiana, commonly known as the Rojasian trumpet vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that are typically bright yellow or orange, designed to attract pollinators like hummingbirds. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are often heart-shaped or ovate in form. Its stems are woody and can grow quite long, allowing it to climb trees or other structures.

Hexachlamys rojasiana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
