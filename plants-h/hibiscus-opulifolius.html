Hibiscus opulifolius

Hibiscus opulifolius, commonly known as the showy hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a waxy surface, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Hibiscus opulifolius is a deciduous shrub that blooms from summer to fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
