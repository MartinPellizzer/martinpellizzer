Hechtia gayorum

Hechtia gayorum, commonly known as the Mexican hat plant, is a striking member of the Bromeliaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant produces vibrant orange to red flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a bold visual contrast to its foliage. Hechtia gayorum thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor gardens.
