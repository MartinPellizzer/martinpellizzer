Hemidesmus indicus

Hemidesmus indicus, commonly known as Indian Sarsaparilla, is a climbing herb native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Aristolochiaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant is often found in moist, shaded areas of forests and is known for its medicinal properties. Its roots are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antipyretic effects.

The plant's botanical features make it a distinctive member of its family, with a unique floral structure and aromatic foliage.
