Heuchera americana var. affinis

Heuchera americana var. affinis, commonly known as the red veined heuchera, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features striking foliage with deep green leaves that have prominent red or maroon veins, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 18 inches and produces small, inconspicuous white or pink flowers in late spring. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This cultivar is valued for its vibrant foliage and is often used in woodland gardens or as a ground cover.
