Heliconia pochutlensis

Heliconia pochutlensis, commonly known as the Mexican heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its large, colorful bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Heliconia pochutlensis is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
