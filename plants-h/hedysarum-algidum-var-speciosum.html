Hedysarum algidum var. speciosum

Hedysarum algidum var. speciosum, commonly known as the alpine peavine, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Hedysarum algidum var. speciosum plays a role in nitrogen fixation and is of interest for its ornamental value and potential ecological significance.
