Hylorhipsalis floccosa subsp. pittieri

Hylorhipsalis floccosa subsp.

pittieri, commonly known as the Pittieri's silver spider plant, is a species native to South America. It is a climbing plant characterized by its long, slender stems and dense, white, woolly foliage.

The leaves are typically covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
