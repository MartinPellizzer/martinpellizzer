Hibiscus fragrans

Hibiscus fragrans, commonly known as the sweet senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a fragrant scent. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Hibiscus fragrans thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical landscapes.
