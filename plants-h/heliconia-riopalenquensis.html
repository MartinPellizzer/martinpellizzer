Heliconia riopalenquensis

Heliconia riopalenquensis, commonly known as the Rio Palenquensis heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its large, colorful bracts that resemble petals, often in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are typically hidden within the bracts. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Heliconia riopalenquensis is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its vibrant appearance and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
