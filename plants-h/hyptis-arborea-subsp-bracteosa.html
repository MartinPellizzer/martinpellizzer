Hyptis arborea subsp. bracteosa

Hyptis arborea subsp.

bracteosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf hyptis, is a perennial shrub native to the tropical regions of the Americas. It is characterized by its lance-shaped, hairy leaves that give it a velvety texture.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale blue or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
