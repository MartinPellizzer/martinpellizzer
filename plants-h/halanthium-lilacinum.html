Halanthium lilacinum

Halanthium lilacinum, commonly known as the lilac sea lettuce, is a species of green algae that thrives in marine environments.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent, and ribbon-like fronds that range in color from pale green to a striking lilac hue. This algae typically grows in shallow coastal waters, where it attaches to rocky substrates or other surfaces. Its structure consists of a single layer of cells, making it a simple yet efficient photosynthesizer.

Halanthium lilacinum plays a role in marine ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various microorganisms and small marine life.
