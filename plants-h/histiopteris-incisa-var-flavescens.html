Histiopteris incisa var. flavescens

Histiopteris incisa var. flavescens, commonly known as the golden fringed holly fern, is a variant of the fringed holly fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that are deeply divided and often have a golden or yellowish hue on the underside. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated margins and a leathery texture.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
