Hieracium murorum subsp. ciliatostellatum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

ciliatostellatum, commonly known as the hairy spear thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems and leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces yellow flowers in a spherical cluster, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its subspecies name, *ciliatostellatum*, refers to the ciliated (fringed) edges of its leaves and its star-like flower arrangement.
