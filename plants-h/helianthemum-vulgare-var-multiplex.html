Helianthemum vulgare var. multiplex

Helianthemum vulgare var. multiplex, commonly known as the common rock rose, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to southern Europe.

It features dense clusters of small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in spring and early summer. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically gray-green in color, adapted to retain moisture in arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

The species is known for its hardiness and ability to withstand drought and salt spray.
