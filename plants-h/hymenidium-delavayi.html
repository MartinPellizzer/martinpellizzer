Hymenidium delavayi

Hymenidium delavayi, commonly known as the Delavay's lily, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the high altitudes of China.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while its flowers are usually pink or white with a strong fragrance.

Hymenidium delavayi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
