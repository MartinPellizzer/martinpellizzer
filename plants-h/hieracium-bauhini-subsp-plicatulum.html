Hieracium bauhini subsp. plicatulum

Hieracium bauhini subsp.

plicatulum, commonly known as the plicate hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and leaves with a serrated or lobed appearance.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm. Its leaves are usually basal, with a distinctive pinnatifid (broadly lobed) shape, and its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and flowering habit.
