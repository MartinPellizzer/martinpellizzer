Hemitelia subcrenatum

Hemitelia subcrenatum, commonly known as the serrated waterplantain, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland habitats in temperate regions.

It features basal leaves that are broad and often have a distinctive serrated margin. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important species in aquatic ecosystems.

Its presence is often an indicator of healthy wetland environments.
