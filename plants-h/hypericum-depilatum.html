Hypericum depilatum

Hypericum depilatum, commonly known as the slender St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its pale yellow flowers and hairy stems, which help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant typically thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats. Its leaves are alternate and lance-shaped, with a rough texture.

Hypericum depilatum is part of the Hypericaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental value.
