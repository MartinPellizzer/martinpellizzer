Helleborus Multifidus Subsp. Serbicus

Helleborus multifidus subsp. serbicus is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans. It is known for its dark green, waxy leaves and its unique, pale green to white flowers that bloom in early spring. This subspecies is often found in rocky soils and shaded environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered and requires careful conservation efforts. Its beauty and rarity make it a subject of interest for botanists and nature enthusiasts alike.
