Hedycarya neoebudica

Hedycarya neoebudica, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and often have a yellowish or golden hue. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow, followed by elongated, woody pods. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Hedycarya neoebudica is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
