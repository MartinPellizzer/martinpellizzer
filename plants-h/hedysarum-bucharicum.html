Hedysarum bucharicum

Hedysarum bucharicum, commonly known as the Caucasian vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain small, round seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Hedysarum bucharicum plays a role in soil stabilization and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
