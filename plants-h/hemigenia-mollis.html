Hemigenia mollis

Hemigenia mollis, commonly known as the slender hemigenia, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the eastern regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as undergrowth in forests or along stream banks. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

Hemigenia mollis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
