Helianthemum canum f. serrae

Helianthemum canum f. serrae, commonly known as the serrated rockrose, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, yellow flowers. Native to the Iberian Peninsula, it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant's leaves are typically serrated, giving it its specific epithet "serrae.".

It is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
