Homalolepis guajirensis

Homalolepis guajirensis, commonly known as the Guajira palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the Caribbean region, specifically found in parts of Venezuela and Colombia. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas with well-drained soils.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
