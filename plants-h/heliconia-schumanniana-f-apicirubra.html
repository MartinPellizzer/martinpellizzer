Heliconia schumanniana f. apicirubra

Heliconia schumanniana f. apicirubra, commonly known as the red-tipped heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its large, brightly colored bracts that resemble flowers. The plant features a tall, leafy stem with clusters of vibrant orange to red bracts that enclose the actual small, white flowers. Its foliage consists of broad, elliptical leaves that are dark green and often have a waxy texture.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical landscapes.
