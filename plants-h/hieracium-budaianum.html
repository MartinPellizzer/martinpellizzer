Hieracium budaianum

Hieracium budaianum, commonly known as the Budaian hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium budaianum is often considered a weed due to its aggressive growth and ability to spread rapidly.
