Hernandia javanica

Hernandia javanica, commonly known as Java hernandia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in inflorescences, which are often surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Hernandia javanica is also noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
