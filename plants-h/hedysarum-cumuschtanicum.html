Hedysarum cumuschtanicum

Hedysarum cumuschtanicum, commonly known as the Caucasian vetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, elongated pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Hedysarum cumuschtanicum plays a role in nitrogen fixation, making it valuable for soil enrichment in ecological restoration projects.
