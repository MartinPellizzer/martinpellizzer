Hieracium levicaule subsp. cinericeps

Hieracium levicaule subsp.

cinericeps, commonly known as the gray-haired hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 50 cm.

The plant features slender, hairy stems and basal leaves that are lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and are surrounded by bracts.

The subspecies is distinguished by its grayish pubescence and the presence of pale, hairy bracts beneath the flower heads.
