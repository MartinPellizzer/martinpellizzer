Hieracium laevigatum subsp. subnorvegicum

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

subnorvegicum, commonly known as the Norwegian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern Europe. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features upright stems covered in fine, sticky hairs and produces yellow composite flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough texture and often bear a waxy coating.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ability to spread through rhizomes.
