Hyparrhenia baddadae

Hyparrhenia baddadae, commonly known as the African silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, silver-gray leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Hyparrhenia baddadae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
