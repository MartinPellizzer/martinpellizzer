Hoorebekia carthamoides subsp. cusickii

Hoorebekia carthamoides subsp.

cusickii, commonly known as the Cusick's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. It has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
