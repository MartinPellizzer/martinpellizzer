Horsfieldia roxburghii

Horsfieldia roxburghii, commonly known as the Bornean orchid, is a species of flowering plant in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia, particularly Borneo, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange with red markings, attracting pollinators such as bees and wasps. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Horsfieldia roxburghii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
