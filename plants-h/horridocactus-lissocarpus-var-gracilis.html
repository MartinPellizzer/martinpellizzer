Horridocactus lissocarpus var. gracilis

Horridocactus lissocarpus var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender hedgehog cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family and is native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem and spiny, segmented appearance, with a distinctive greenish-gray coloration. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne at the ends of the stems. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in xeriscapes and cactus gardens.
