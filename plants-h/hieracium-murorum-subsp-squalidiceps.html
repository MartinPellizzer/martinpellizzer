Hieracium murorum subsp. squalidiceps

Hieracium murorum subsp.

squalidiceps, commonly known as the rough meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe. It typically grows in moist, grassy habitats such as meadows and riverbanks.

The plant has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in short, stiff hairs. Its flowers are yellow and clustered in a flat-topped inflorescence, and it produces small, oval seeds.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a rough texture.
