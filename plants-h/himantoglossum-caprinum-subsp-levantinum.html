Himantoglossum caprinum subsp. levantinum

Himantoglossum caprinum subsp.

levantinum, commonly known as the Levantine orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that are typically violet or purple in color.

The plant has linear to lanceolate leaves and produces a single, showy flower per stem. It thrives in rocky or calcareous soils and is often found in open habitats such as cliffs and grasslands.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species in many parts of its range.
