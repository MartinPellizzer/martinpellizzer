Haworthia glabrata var. perviridis

Haworthia glabrata var. perviridis, commonly known as the Zebra Haworthia, is a compact, low-growing succulent native to South Africa.

It features a rosette of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with distinct white or yellow markings resembling a zebra's stripes. The leaves are covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps it retain moisture in arid conditions. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the warmer months.

This plant is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its minimal water requirements and ability to thrive in bright, indirect light.
