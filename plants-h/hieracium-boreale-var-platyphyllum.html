Hieracium boreale var. platyphyllum

Hieracium boreale var. platyphyllum, commonly known as the broad-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, spoon-shaped leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in summer. The plant is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It has a taproot system and is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
