Haplopappus fallax

Haplopappus fallax, commonly known as the slender pappus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typical sunflower-like structure. The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and grayish-green, with a rough texture.

Haplopappus fallax is often found in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.
