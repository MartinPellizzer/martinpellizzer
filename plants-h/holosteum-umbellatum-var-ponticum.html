Holosteum umbellatum var. ponticum

Holosteum umbellatum var. ponticum, commonly known as the Pontic water-plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It features a rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white, umbellate flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found near streams, ponds, and other freshwater habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for wetland ecosystems and water quality monitoring.
