Hieracium sabaudum subsp. cumuliflorum

Hieracium sabaudum subsp.

cumuliflorum, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering heads that resemble dandelions.

The plant produces yellow flowers that are typically clustered in a single head, and it thrives in alpine and subalpine environments. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads.

This subspecies is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude climates.
