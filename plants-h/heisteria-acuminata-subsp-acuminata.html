Heisteria acuminata subsp. acuminata

Heisteria acuminata subsp.

acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heister, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
