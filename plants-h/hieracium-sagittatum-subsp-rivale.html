Hieracium sagittatum subsp. rivale

Hieracium sagittatum subsp.

rivale, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and bright yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowering period occurs from late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
