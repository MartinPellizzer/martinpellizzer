Hicorius odorata

Hicorius odorata, commonly known as the sweet senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds coated in a sticky substance. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

Hicorius odorata is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
