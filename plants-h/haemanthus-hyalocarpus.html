Haemanthus hyalocarpus

Haemanthus hyalocarpus, commonly known as the translucent-leaved haemanthus, is a striking plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, waxy, and often translucent leaves. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. Its bulbs are adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions.

Haemanthus hyalocarpus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
