Hieracium bifidum subsp. subangulatum

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

subangulatum, commonly known as the narrow-angled hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to central and eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems.

The subspecies is distinguished by its narrow, acute leaf blades and the arrangement of its inflorescence. It typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils.

This plant is often found in open habitats and is relatively common in its native range.
