Heterotheca inuloides var. rosei

Heterotheca inuloides var. rosei, commonly known as the roseate blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leaf structure. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces daisy-like flowers with blue to purple petals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and drought-tolerant addition to native plant gardens.
