Hieracium brachiatum subsp. erythrotrichum

Hieracium brachiatum subsp.

erythrotrichum, commonly known as the red-haired hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, reddish stems and yellow composite flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dense, reddish pubescence, which gives it its common name. It typically grows in grassy areas and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
