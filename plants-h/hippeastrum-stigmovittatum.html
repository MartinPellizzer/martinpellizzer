Hippeastrum stigmovittatum

Hippeastrum stigmovittatum, commonly known as the spotted hippeastrum, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It features large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 centimeters in diameter, typically in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The leaves are broad, strap-like, and often have a waxy texture, contributing to the plant's ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is popular among gardeners for its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
