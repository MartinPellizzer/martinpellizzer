Hemigraphis physalifolia

Hemigraphis physalifolia, commonly known as the Chinese nettle or velvetleaf, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety, heart-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple hues. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Hemigraphis physalifolia is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
