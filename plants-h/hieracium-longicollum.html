Hieracium longicollum

Hieracium longicollum, commonly known as the long-necked hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and basal rosettes of spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically visited by various pollinators. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and disturbed soils.

Hieracium longicollum is an herbaceous perennial that can spread through rhizomes, making it somewhat invasive in certain environments.
