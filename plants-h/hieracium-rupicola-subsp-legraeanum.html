Hieracium rupicola subsp. legraeanum

Hieracium rupicola subsp.

legraeanum, commonly known as the rock hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous or alpine regions.

The plant has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and its yellow flowers are arranged in a flat, disc-like inflorescence. It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

This subspecies is distinguished by its smaller, more compact growth habit compared to other members of the Hieracium genus.
