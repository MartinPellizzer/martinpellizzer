Hieracium pycnophyllum

Hieracium pycnophyllum, commonly known as the dense-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and thrives in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are densely packed, hairy, and often have a distinct yellowish or reddish hue.

The flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat, disc-like cluster, typical of the genus Hieracium.
