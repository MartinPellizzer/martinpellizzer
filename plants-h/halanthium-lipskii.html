Halanthium lipskii

Halanthium lipskii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved water starwort, is a aquatic plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving streams. The plant has slender, branching stems with small, opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often submerged. Its flowers are tiny and inconspicuous, appearing in clusters near the water surface.

Halanthium lipskii plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates.
