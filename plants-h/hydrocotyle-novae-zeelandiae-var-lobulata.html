Hydrocotyle novae-zeelandiae var. lobulata

Hydrocotyle novae-zeelandiae var. lobulata, commonly known as the New Zealand water arum, is a herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It features heart-shaped, lobed leaves that are typically submerged or floating in aquatic environments. The plant produces small, white flowers with a spatulate structure, which are often enclosed in a leaf-like bract. It is adapted to wetland habitats and thrives in shallow water or moist soil.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies in freshwater ecosystems.
