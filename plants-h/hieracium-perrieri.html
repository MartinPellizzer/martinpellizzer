Hieracium perrieri

Hieracium perrieri, commonly known as the Perrier's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has hairy stems and narrow, serrated leaves that help it retain moisture.

Hieracium perrieri is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
