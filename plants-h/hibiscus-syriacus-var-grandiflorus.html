Hibiscus syriacus var. grandiflorus

Hibiscus syriacus var. grandiflorus, commonly known as the large-flowered Chinese rose, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges and grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

The flowers are not only visually striking but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
