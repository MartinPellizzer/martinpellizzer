Helicia reticulata var. parvifolia

Helicia reticulata var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved helicia, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, elliptical leaves with a smooth texture and a glossy green surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the spring. Its fruit is a round, fleshy drupe that ripens to a dark red color.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
