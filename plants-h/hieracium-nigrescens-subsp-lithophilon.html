Hieracium nigrescens subsp. lithophilon

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

lithophilon, commonly known as the Lithophytic Hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to rocky and alpine environments. It typically grows in crevices and on limestone outcrops, showcasing adaptations to nutrient-poor soils.

The plant features basal leaves that are usually hairy and lanceolate in shape, with flowering stems that bear yellow composite flowers. It produces small, oval-shaped achenes that are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is notable for its resilience and is often found in mountainous regions of Europe.
