Hamatocactus uncinatus var. wrightii

Hamatocactus uncinatus var. wrightii, commonly known as the Wright's hooked cactus, is a rare and striking cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically borne in clusters at the base of the stems. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered in its natural range.
