Hollandaea porphyrocarpa

Hollandaea porphyrocarpa, commonly known as the purple-fruited bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling small bells. The plant produces distinctive, fleshy, purple fruits that are covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
