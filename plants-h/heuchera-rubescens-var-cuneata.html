Heuchera rubescens var. cuneata

Heuchera rubescens var. cuneata, commonly known as the red-leaf heuchera, is a striking perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant, red-tinged foliage that often turns bronze or purple in cooler weather. The plant produces upright, spire-like clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers in the spring. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value in shaded garden settings.
