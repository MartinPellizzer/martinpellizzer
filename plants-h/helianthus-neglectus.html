Helianthus neglectus

Helianthus neglectus, commonly known as the common sunflower, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to the eastern and central United States and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers and linear, hairy leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and produces numerous seeds in its flower heads. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Helianthus neglectus is an annual plant that blooms from late spring to early autumn.
