Hieracium dasytomum

Hieracium dasytomum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies. Its leaves are hairy and serrated, while the flower heads consist of yellow disk flowers surrounded by pale yellow to white ray florets.

Hieracium dasytomum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common weed in many regions.
