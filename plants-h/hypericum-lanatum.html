Hypericum lanatum

Hypericum lanatum, commonly known as the hairy St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy, often reddish stems and leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite and have a distinctive rough texture due to the fine hairs covering them.

Hypericum lanatum is part of the Hippocastanaceae family and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
