Huernia thudichumii

Huernia thudichumii, commonly known as the horned lithops, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems that resemble small horns, which help it store water in arid environments. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green body. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for rock gardens or indoor cultivation.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
