Hieracium protractum subsp. humidorum

Hieracium protractum subsp.

humidorum, commonly known as the swamp hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America. It is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosettes of lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in a single head, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
