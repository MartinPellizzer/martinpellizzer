Hermesias leucantha

Hermesias leucantha, commonly known as the white-flowered orchid, is a species of orchid native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, white petals and a slender, erect stem that bears clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories, where it thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated for horticultural purposes.
