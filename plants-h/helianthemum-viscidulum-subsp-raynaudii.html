Helianthemum viscidulum subsp. raynaudii

Helianthemum viscidulum subsp.

raynaudii, commonly known as the Raynaud's sandthrift, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to southern Europe. It features small, fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a sticky resin, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments.

The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, adding a splash of color to rocky or sandy soils. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it well-suited for Mediterranean climates.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover in dry landscapes.
