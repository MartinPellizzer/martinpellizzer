Huperzia henryi

Huperzia henryi, commonly known as the Chinese clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is a low-growing, evergreen plant that typically forms dense mats or cushions on rocky or shaded forest floors. The plant has whorled leaves that are often scale-like and contribute to its compact appearance. It is native to regions of East Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

Huperzia henryi plays a role in soil stabilization and provides microhabitat for various small organisms.
