Hieracium uncinatum

Hieracium uncinatum, commonly known as the hooked hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are rough and hairy, with a serrated edge, and its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

The species is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in various habitats.
