Hydrostachys trifaria

Hydrostachys trifaria, commonly known as the water hyssop, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in moist environments, often found near water bodies such as ponds and streams.

Hydrostachys trifaria is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
