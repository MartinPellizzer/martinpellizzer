Hedranthus owerinianus

Hedranthus owerinianus, commonly known as the Owerinian hedgeplant, is a flowering plant native to the subtropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the spring and summer seasons. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, and is well-suited for use in hedges or ornamental landscaping. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a glossy surface, and the plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Hedranthus owerinianus is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
