Hibiscus serpyllifolius

Hibiscus serpyllifolius, commonly known as the common groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial herb with hairy stems and alternate leaves that are usually lobed or serrated. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five petals, which are often surrounded by five to seven sepals. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along fences.

Hibiscus serpyllifolius is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through seeds and runners.
