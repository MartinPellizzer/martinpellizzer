Heliconia rubra

Heliconia rubra, commonly known as the red heliconia, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are large, broad, and often have a waxy texture.

Heliconia rubra is commonly cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical landscapes.
