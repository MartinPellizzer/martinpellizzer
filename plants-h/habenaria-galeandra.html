Habenaria galeandra

Habenaria galeandra, commonly known as the pink lady's tresses, is a species of orchid native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and its distinctive inflorescence of pink or purple flowers that resemble a tresses of hair. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. It has a complex root system and produces small, tuber-like structures for storage.

The orchid is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
