Hibiscus lampas

Hibiscus lampas, commonly known as the yellow hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow or orange flowers with five petals and a prominent central pistil. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. Hibiscus lampas thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
