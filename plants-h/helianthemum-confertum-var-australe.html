Helianthemum confertum var. australe

Helianthemum confertum var. australe, commonly known as the Australian stonecrop, is a low-growing succulent plant native to Australia.

It features fleshy, gray-green leaves that are arranged in a dense rosette, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a vibrant touch to its compact form. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for Mediterranean and coastal climates.
