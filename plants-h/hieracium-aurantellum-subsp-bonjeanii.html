Hieracium aurantellum subsp. bonjeanii

Hieracium aurantellum subsp.

bonjeanii, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm.

The plant features basal and cauline leaves that are lanceolate to ovate in shape, with hairy surfaces. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and are borne on long pedicels.

The species is characterized by its robust growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
