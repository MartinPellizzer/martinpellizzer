Hieracium bifidum subsp. subacidotum

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

subacidotum, commonly known as the subacidotum hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster, typical of the Compositae family. It is typically found in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and grasslands.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its adaptability to a range of environmental conditions.
