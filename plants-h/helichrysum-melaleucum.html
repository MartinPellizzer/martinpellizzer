Helichrysum melaleucum

Helichrysum melaleucum, commonly known as the white everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are often clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. The plant is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and a strong, aromatic scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Helichrysum melaleucum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
