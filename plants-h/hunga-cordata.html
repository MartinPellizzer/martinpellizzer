Hunga cordata

Hunga cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf plant, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically dark green and have a smooth, waxy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often blooming in the spring. It thrives in humid, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
