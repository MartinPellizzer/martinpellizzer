Haplocoelum foliolosum subsp. mombasense

Haplocoelum foliolosum subsp.

mombasense, commonly known as the Mombasa leafy spurge, is a perennial herb native to eastern Africa. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and stout, branched stems that bear small, yellowish-green flowers.

The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in arid to semi-arid environments. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a hairy surface, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This subspecies is often found in open grasslands and along rocky outcrops.
