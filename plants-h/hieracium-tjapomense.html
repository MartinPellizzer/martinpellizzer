Hieracium tjapomense

Hieracium tjapomense, commonly known as the Tjapomense hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically features yellow to orange flowers arranged in a cluster, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and forest edges.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
