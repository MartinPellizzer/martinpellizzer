Hedychium lanatum

Hedychium lanatum, commonly known as the woolly ginger, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and large, showy yellow flowers with a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant has hairy, lanate (woolly) foliage, which gives it its common name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Hedychium lanatum is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
