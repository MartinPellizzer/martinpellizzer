Hymenolobium alagoanum var. parvifolium

Hymenolobium alagoanum var. parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaved Brazilian wisteria, is a climbing shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It features compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets that are typically arranged in an odd-pinnate pattern. The plant produces pendulous, fragrant flowers in clusters, which are usually pink or lilac in color. Its seeds are enclosed in flat, leathery pods that split open when mature.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
