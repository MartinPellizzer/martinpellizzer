Hieracium scabrum var. simplex

Hieracium scabrum var. simplex, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy, rough-textured stems. The plant produces yellow composite flowers, typically arranged in dense clusters at the ends of its stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
