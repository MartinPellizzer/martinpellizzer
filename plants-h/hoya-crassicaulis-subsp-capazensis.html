Hoya crassicaulis subsp. capazensis

Hoya crassicaulis subsp.

capazensis, commonly known as the Capazensis Wandering Jade, is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is native to the Philippines. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and clusters of waxy, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are usually arranged in a rosette pattern. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil. Hoya crassicaulis subsp.

capazensis is popular among succulent and tropical plant enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
