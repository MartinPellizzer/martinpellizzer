Hypecoum pseudograndiflorum

Hypecoum pseudograndiflorum, commonly known as the African star-of-Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking blue to violet flowers in clusters during the summer months. The plant has a stout, branching stem and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.
