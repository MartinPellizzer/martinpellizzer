Helenium vaillantii

Helenium vaillantii, commonly known as the Mexican sneezeweed, is a striking ornamental plant native to Mexico and the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features tall, slender stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its vibrant orange to red flower heads bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Helenium vaillantii thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
