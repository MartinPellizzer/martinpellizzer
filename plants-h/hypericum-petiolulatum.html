Hypericum petiolulatum

Hypericum petiolulatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, usually from July to August. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, with a slightly hairy texture.

Hypericum petiolulatum is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
