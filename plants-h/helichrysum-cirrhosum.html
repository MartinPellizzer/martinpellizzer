Helichrysum cirrhosum

Helichrysum cirrhosum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, bright yellow to orange flowers that are tightly packed into dense, spherical clusters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. It is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

The plant is also known for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
