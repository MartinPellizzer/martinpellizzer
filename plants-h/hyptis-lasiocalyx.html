Hyptis lasiocalyx

Hyptis lasiocalyx, commonly known as Mexican blue sage, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Hyptis lasiocalyx thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
