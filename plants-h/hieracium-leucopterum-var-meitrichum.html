Hieracium leucopterum var. meitrichum

Hieracium leucopterum var. meitrichum, commonly known as the white-centred hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are basal, lanceolate, and often covered with fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a white center.

This variant is distinguished by its unique floral pattern and is often found in association with other members of the Hieracium genus.
