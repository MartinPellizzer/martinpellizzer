Hibbertia fruticosa subsp. fruticosa

Hibbertia fruticosa subsp.

fruticosa, commonly known as the yellow wattle, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects.

The plant has woody stems and small, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and native landscaping.
