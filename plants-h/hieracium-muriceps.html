Hieracium muriceps

Hieracium muriceps, commonly known as the wall groundsel, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or disturbed soils, often found along walls and in grassy areas. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowering stems bear yellow composite flowers. It produces small, oval-shaped achenes that are dispersed by wind.

Hieracium muriceps is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions and is often considered a weed in certain regions.
