Hippeastrum philadelphicum

Hippeastrum philadelphicum, commonly known as the Philadelphia amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It produces large, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. The plant features strap-like, narrow leaves that emerge from a central bulb and are evergreen in warmer climates. Its flowers are trumpet-shaped with six petals and are often used in floral arrangements due to their bold appearance.

Hippeastrum philadelphicum is relatively easy to cultivate, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
