Hieracium pulchrum var. glabratoides

Hieracium pulchrum var. glabratoides, commonly known as the smooth-haired pretty hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while the flowers are bright yellow with a central disk.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
