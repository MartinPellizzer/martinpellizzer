Hippocrepis squamata var. sinuata

Hippocrepis squamata var. sinuata, commonly known as the Sinuous Harebell, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a wavy or sinuous margin, giving rise to its varietal name.

The flowers are usually pale blue or purple and bloom in early summer.
