Hoffmannia subcapitata

Hoffmannia subcapitata, commonly known as the Cape rose, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that are clustered in rounded heads, giving it a rose-like appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Hoffmannia subcapitata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
