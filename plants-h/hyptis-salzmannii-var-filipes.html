Hyptis salzmannii var. filipes

Hyptis salzmannii var. filipes, commonly known as Brazilian hyssop, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and sage. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal use.

Its essential oils are often used in traditional medicine for their antispasmodic and analgesic properties.
