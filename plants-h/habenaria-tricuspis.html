Habenaria tricuspis

Habenaria tricuspis, commonly known as the three-lobed orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers with three distinct lobes, typically in shades of white, yellow, and green. It thrives in moist, shaded habitats and is often found in forest understories.

The orchid's intricate floral structure is adapted for specific pollination by bees and other insects.
