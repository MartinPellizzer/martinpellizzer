Hugelia densifolia var. sanctora

Hugelia densifolia var. sanctora, commonly known as the Sanctora hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
