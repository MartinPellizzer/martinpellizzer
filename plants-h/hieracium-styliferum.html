Hieracium styliferum

Hieracium styliferum, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and ragworts, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 50 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers are arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

Hieracium styliferum is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
