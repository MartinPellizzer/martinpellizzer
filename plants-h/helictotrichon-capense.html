Helictotrichon capense

Helictotrichon capense, commonly known as the Cape hairvetch, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Helictotrichon capense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
