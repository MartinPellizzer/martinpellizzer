Hippocratea cymosa var. lujae

Hippocratea cymosa var. lujae, commonly known as the Lujae's candelabra bean, is a climbing legume native to the humid lowlands of Central America.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets and produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in cymes. The plant bears cylindrical, green pods that contain several elliptical, edible seeds. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and nutritional benefits, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
