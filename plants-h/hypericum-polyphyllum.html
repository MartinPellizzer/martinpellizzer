Hypericum polyphyllum

Hypericum polyphyllum, commonly known as the many-leaved St. John's wort, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

Hypericum polyphyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
