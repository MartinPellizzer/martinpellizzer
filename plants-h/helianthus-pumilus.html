Helianthus pumilus

Helianthus pumilus, commonly known as the dwarf sunflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows to a height of only 10 to 30 centimeters, making it one of the smallest species in the sunflower genus. The plant features bright yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk of smaller, tubular florets. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Helianthus pumilus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.
