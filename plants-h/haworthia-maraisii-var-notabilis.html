Haworthia maraisii var. notabilis

Haworthia maraisii var. notabilis, commonly known as the Zulu bulb, is a compact, rosette-forming succulent native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with distinctive white or pale markings. The plant produces small, white flowers on slender stems during the growing season. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for rock gardens or indoor cultivation.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
