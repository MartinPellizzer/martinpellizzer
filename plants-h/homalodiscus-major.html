Homalodiscus major

Homalodiscus major, commonly known as the African bluebell, is a flowering plant native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers arranged in dense, bell-shaped clusters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
