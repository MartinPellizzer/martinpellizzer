Hieracium phyllozum

Hieracium phyllozum, commonly known as the broadleaf hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are yellow with ray florets surrounding a central disk. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and other open areas.

Hieracium phyllozum is also known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
