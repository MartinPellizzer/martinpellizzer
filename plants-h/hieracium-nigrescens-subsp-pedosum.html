Hieracium nigrescens subsp. pedosum

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

pedosum, commonly known as the blackening hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and often have a reddish or purplish tinge, while the flowers are yellow with a dark center.

It is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in disturbed habitats.
