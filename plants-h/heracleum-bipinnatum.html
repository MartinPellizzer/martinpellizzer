Heracleum bipinnatum

Heracleum bipinnatum, commonly known as the two-pinnate hogweed, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hollow stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces large, umbrella-shaped inflorescences with small white or greenish flowers. Its leaves are deeply lobed and can reach up to 1 meter in diameter, making them one of the largest in the plant kingdom.

Heracleum bipinnatum is part of the Apiaceae family, which includes other members like carrots and parsley.
