Hieracium myriadenum var. unamunoi

Hieracium myriadenum var. unamunoi, commonly known as the Canary Islands Groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a single capitulum, which are typically visited by various pollinators. It thrives in rocky, arid environments and is adapted to the unique climate of the Canary Islands.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
