Hedysarum turczaninovii

Hedysarum turczaninovii, commonly known as the Turczaninov's milkvetch, is a perennial leguminous plant native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, often found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers in late spring. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind.

Hedysarum turczaninovii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinators.
