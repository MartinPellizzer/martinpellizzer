Hieracium subfallax

Hieracium subfallax, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

Hieracium subfallax is often found in open habitats and is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and seeds.
