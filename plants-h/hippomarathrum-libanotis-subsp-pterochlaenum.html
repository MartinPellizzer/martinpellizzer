Hippomarathrum libanotis subsp. pterochlaenum

Hippomarathrum libanotis subsp.

pterochlaenum, commonly known as the Lebanon thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features a robust taproot and erect, sparsely branched stems covered in dense, prickly hairs.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are lance-shaped, often with serrated edges and a bluish-green hue.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.
