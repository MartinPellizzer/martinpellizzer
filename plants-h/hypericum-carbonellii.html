Hypericum carbonellii

Hypericum carbonellii, commonly known as the Sierra hypericum, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky, arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces bright yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Hypericum carbonellii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
