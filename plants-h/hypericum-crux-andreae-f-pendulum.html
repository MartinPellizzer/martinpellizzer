Hypericum crux-andreae f. pendulum

Hypericum crux-andreae f. pendulum, commonly known as the hanging St. John's wort, is a unique variant of the Hypericum genus.

It is characterized by its pendulous inflorescences, where the flower clusters hang downward from the stem. This form is native to specific regions in South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal environments, adapting to harsh conditions.

Its botanical features include yellow flowers with five petals and prominent orange stamens, making it visually distinctive.
