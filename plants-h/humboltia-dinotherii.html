Humboltia dinotherii

Humboltia dinotherii, commonly known as the Dinotherium plant, is a rare and ancient species of flowering plant.

It is characterized by its unique, spirally arranged leaves that resemble the tusks of the prehistoric Dinotherium, a type of proboscidean. This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical rainforests. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often clustered in dense inflorescences.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, Humboltia dinotherii is considered a valuable subject for botanical research.
