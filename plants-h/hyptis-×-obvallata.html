Hyptis × obvallata

Hyptis × obvallata, commonly known as silverleaf hyptis, is a hybrid shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance and helps it reflect sunlight. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale blue, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along streambanks.

Hyptis × obvallata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
