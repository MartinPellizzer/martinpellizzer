Hamelia pedicellata

Hamelia pedicellata, commonly known as firecracker bush, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its bright red, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The species is also valued for its ability to withstand drought conditions once established.
