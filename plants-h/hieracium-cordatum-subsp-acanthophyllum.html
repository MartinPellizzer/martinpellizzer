Hieracium cordatum subsp. acanthophyllum

Hieracium cordatum subsp.

acanthophyllum, commonly known as the spiked hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, preferring temperate climates.

The plant features upright stems with distinctive, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow, with ray florets and a central disk, and it produces small, dry fruits called achenes.

It is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
