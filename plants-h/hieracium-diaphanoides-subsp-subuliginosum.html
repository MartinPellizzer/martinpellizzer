Hieracium diaphanoides subsp. subuliginosum

Hieracium diaphanoides subsp.

subuliginosum, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and pointed, and it produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

It is a member of the Hieracium genus, which includes many species of hawkweeds known for their invasive potential in certain regions.
