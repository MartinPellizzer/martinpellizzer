Hieracium brevisuratum

Hieracium brevisuratum, commonly known as the short-stalked hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its flowers are small, yellowish to orange, and bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Hieracium brevisuratum is known for its rapid spread and can become invasive in certain environments.
