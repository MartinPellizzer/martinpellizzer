Hieracium umbrosum subsp. oleicolor

Hieracium umbrosum subsp.

oleicolor, commonly known as the purple meadowhawk, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its distinctive purple to reddish-purple flowers, which bloom in late summer to early autumn.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring partial to full sun. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are erect and often branched.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
