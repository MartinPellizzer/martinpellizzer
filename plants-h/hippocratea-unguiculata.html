Hippocratea unguiculata

Hippocratea unguiculata, commonly known as the pink orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrate.

The flowers bloom in clusters and are often used in ornamental horticulture due to their aesthetic appeal.
