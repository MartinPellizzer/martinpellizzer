Heterocoma lanuginosa

Heterocoma lanuginosa, commonly known as the woolly heterocoma, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and stems, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along dry slopes.

Heterocoma lanuginosa is a perennial species that is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
