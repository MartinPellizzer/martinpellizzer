Hylocereus monacanthus

Hylocereus monacanthus, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a cacti species native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and large, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant produces white or pale pink flowers with a strong, sweet scent, which attract nocturnal pollinators like bats and moths. Its fruit, known as the dragon fruit, is edible and has a vibrant pink or yellow color with a sweet, mild flavor.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit.
