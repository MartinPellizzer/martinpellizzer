Hutchinsia brevicaulis var. drexlerae

Hutchinsia brevicaulis var. drexlerae, commonly known as the Drexler's cushion plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing rosettes of fleshy, gray-green leaves that are covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the summer months, which are borne on short, erect stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
