Habenaria amazonica

Habenaria amazonica, commonly known as the Amazon orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as rainforests and wetlands. Its rhizome is fibrous, and it produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

The flowers are usually greenish-yellow with purple or red streaks, making it a visually distinctive species.
