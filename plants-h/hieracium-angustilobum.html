Hieracium angustilobum

Hieracium angustilobum, commonly known as narrow-lobed hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is a perennial herb that can form dense colonies, sometimes becoming invasive in certain regions.
