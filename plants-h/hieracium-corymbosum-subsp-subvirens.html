Hieracium corymbosum subsp. subvirens

Hieracium corymbosum subsp.

subvirens, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow to orange flowering heads.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are basal and lance-shaped, often with a hairy surface, while its flowers are arranged in a corymb-like inflorescence.

It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
