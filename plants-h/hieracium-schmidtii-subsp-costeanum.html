Hieracium schmidtii subsp. costeanum

Hieracium schmidtii subsp.

costeanum, commonly known as the Costean hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. It has hairy stems and leaves with a rough texture, and its flowers are arranged in a single head.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
