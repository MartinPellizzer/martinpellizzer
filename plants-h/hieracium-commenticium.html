Hieracium commenticium

Hieracium commenticium, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces numerous yellow flowers arranged in a composite head. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring temperate climates. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
