Hieracium vallicola

Hieracium vallicola, commonly known as the valley hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along streambanks, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

Hieracium vallicola is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is often considered a weed in agricultural areas.
