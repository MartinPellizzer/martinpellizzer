Hippeastrum tubispathum var. grandiflorum

Hippeastrum tubispathum var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the Giant Amazonian Amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It features large, showy flowers that can reach up to 20 cm in diameter, typically in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant produces a single, sturdy stem that can grow up to 2 meters tall, bearing multiple blooms. Its leaves are broad, strap-like, and often remain above the soil during the growing season.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in warm climates.
