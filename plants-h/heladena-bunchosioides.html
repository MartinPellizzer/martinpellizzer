Heladena bunchosioides

Heladena bunchosioides, commonly known as the clustered heladena, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces small, clustered inflorescences. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss in its dry habitat. Its flowers are typically pale green to yellowish in color, with five petals and prominent stamens.

Heladena bunchosioides is adapted to survive in nutrient-poor soils and can tolerate high temperatures and limited water availability.
