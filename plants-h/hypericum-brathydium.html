Hypericum brathydium

Hypericum brathydium, commonly known as narrowleaf St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Hypericaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in late summer, which are followed by small, red, spherical fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grasslands and rocky habitats.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in traditional herbal medicine.
