Haplopappus racemosus var. paniculatus

Haplopappus racemosus var. paniculatus, commonly known as the panicled hairy fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces achenes with pappus bristles, which aid in wind dispersal. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
