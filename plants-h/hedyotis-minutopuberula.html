Hedyotis minutopuberula

Hedyotis minutopuberula, commonly known as the small-hairy knotweed, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small herbaceous species with slender, upright stems and opposite leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
