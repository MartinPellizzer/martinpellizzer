Hieracium erythropodum subsp. coloratipes

Hieracium erythropodum subsp.

coloratipes, commonly known as the red-hairy hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and showy yellow to orange flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its reddish-brown stems and leaves with a distinctive hairy texture. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
