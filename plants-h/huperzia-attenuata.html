Huperzia attenuata

Huperzia attenuata, commonly known as the slender huperzia, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Hypnaceae.

It is characterized by its diminutive, creeping growth habit and its distinctive, linear, and often pale green leaves. Native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its spore-producing structures, called sporangia, are clustered in small, cone-like clusters at the base of the leaves.

Huperzia attenuata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems, providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
