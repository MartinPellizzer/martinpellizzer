Hibiscus ciliaris

Hibiscus ciliaris, commonly known as the coastal hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a prominent yellow center. The plant thrives in saline and coastal environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention.

Hibiscus ciliaris is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
