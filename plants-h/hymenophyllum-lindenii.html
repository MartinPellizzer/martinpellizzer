Hymenophyllum lindenii

Hymenophyllum lindenii, commonly known as the Lindley's filmy fern, is a small, delicate epiphytic fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its narrow, translucent fronds that are often covered in a fine, waxy layer, which helps reduce water loss in its humid, shaded habitats. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or rocks in tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in high humidity and indirect light. Its sporangia are clustered in small, circular groups called sori, which are protected by a specialized indusium.

The plant's unique morphology and habitat preferences make it an important species in the study of epiphytic plant adaptations.
