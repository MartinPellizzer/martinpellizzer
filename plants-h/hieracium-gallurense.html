Hieracium gallurense

Hieracium gallurense, commonly known as the Gallic hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

It is considered a pioneer species in disturbed habitats and plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
