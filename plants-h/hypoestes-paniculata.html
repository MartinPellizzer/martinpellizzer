Hypoestes paniculata

Hypoestes paniculata, commonly known as the flame violet, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in panicles, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers bright, indirect light.

Hypoestes paniculata is often cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its vibrant foliage and ease of care.
