Hymenopus macrophyllus

Hymenopus macrophyllus, commonly known as the broad-leafed hymenopus, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
