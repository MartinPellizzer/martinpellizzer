Hieracium pachymeroides

Hieracium pachymeroides, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant features dense, woolly hairs on its stems and leaves, which provide protection against herbivores and excessive sunlight. It typically grows in open, disturbed soils and thrives in sunny environments.

Its bright yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
