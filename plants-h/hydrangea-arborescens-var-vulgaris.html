Hydrangea arborescens var. vulgaris

Hydrangea arborescens var. vulgaris, commonly known as the smooth hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, round, leafy clusters that produce showy flower heads in shades of white, pink, or blue, depending on soil pH. The plant thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to bloom multiple times throughout the growing season.

The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with a smooth texture and a slightly serrated edge.
