Hirculus pseudohirculus

Hirculus pseudohirculus, commonly known as the false goatweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or red. The plant produces small, triangular seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread across various habitats. It thrives in arid and semi-arid regions, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Due to its resilience and rapid growth, it is considered a potential invasive species in some areas.
