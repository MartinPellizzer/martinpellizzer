Hieracium arpadianum subsp. sellanum

Hieracium arpadianum subsp.

sellanum, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and showy yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats at high altitudes. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its range.
