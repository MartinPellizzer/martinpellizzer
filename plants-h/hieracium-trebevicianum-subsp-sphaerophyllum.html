Hieracium trebevicianum subsp. sphaerophyllum

Hieracium trebevicianum subsp.

sphaerophyllum, commonly known as the spherical dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It is characterized by its rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and a flowering head that produces spherical, yellow-orange composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its flowers bloom from June to August, and the plant is known for its medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antiseptic effects.

It is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
