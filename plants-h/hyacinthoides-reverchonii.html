Hyacinthoides reverchonii

Hyacinthoides reverchonii, commonly known as the Spanish bluebell, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue and purple, typically blooming in spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and prefers well-drained, woodland soils. It is often found in shady, moist environments such as forests and along stream banks.

The Spanish bluebell is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
