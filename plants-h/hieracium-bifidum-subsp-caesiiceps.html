Hieracium bifidum subsp. caesiiceps

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

caesiiceps, commonly known as the blue-headed hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its distinctive blue to purple flower heads.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 40 cm. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are arranged in a composite head.

It is often found in open habitats and can form dense colonies, contributing to the ecological diversity of its environment.
