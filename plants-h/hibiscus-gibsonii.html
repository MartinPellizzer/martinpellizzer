Hibiscus gibsonii

Hibiscus gibsonii, commonly known as Gibson's hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces fruits that are capsules containing numerous seeds. Hibiscus gibsonii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or near streams.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
