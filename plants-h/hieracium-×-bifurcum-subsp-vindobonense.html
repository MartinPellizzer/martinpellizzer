Hieracium × bifurcum subsp. vindobonense

Hieracium × bifurcum subsp.

vindobonense, commonly known as the Vienna hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers that are typically arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. It has fibrous roots and is often found in disturbed soils along roadsides and in meadows.

Due to its invasive tendencies, it is considered a weed in many regions.
