Hieracium pseudolindebergii

Hieracium pseudolindebergii, commonly known as the Lindberg's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in late summer. Native to southern Europe, it thrives in rocky and dry habitats. The plant has small, hairy stems and is often found in open, sunny environments.

It is considered a minor weed in some regions but plays a role in local ecosystems.
