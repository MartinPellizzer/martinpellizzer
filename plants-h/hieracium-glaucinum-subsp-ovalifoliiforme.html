Hieracium glaucinum subsp. ovalifoliiforme

Hieracium glaucinum subsp.

ovalifoliiforme, commonly known as the oval-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lanceolate to ovate, often with a bluish-gray hue due to dense pubescence.

The flowers are yellow, arranged in a composite head, and are visited by various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
