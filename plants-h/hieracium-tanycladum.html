Hieracium tanycladum

Hieracium tanycladum, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features a basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

Hieracium tanycladum is known for its tolerance to drought and poor soil conditions, making it a hardy species in Mediterranean climates.
