Hyalolepis rhizocephala

Hyalolepis rhizocephala, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf morphology. The plant typically grows in rocky and sandy soils, often in coastal or mountainous regions. Its leaves are succulent and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in arid environments.
