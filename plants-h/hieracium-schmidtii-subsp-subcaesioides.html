Hieracium schmidtii subsp. subcaesioides

Hieracium schmidtii subsp.

subcaesioides, commonly known as the Schmidt's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to reddish flower heads composed of numerous small florets.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually alternate, with a rough, hairy texture, and the stems are often reddish in color.

It is a relatively small plant, usually not exceeding 30 cm in height, and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.
