Hylenaea multiflora

Hylenaea multiflora, commonly known as the mountain everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in mountainous regions. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, and it is adapted to withstand drought and cold temperatures.

Hylenaea multiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and beauty.
