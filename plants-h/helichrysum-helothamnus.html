Helichrysum helothamnus

Helichrysum helothamnus, commonly known as the golden everlasting or yellow helichrysum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that resemble miniature suns. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates. Its foliage is typically gray-green with a silvery appearance, adding ornamental value to gardens.

Helichrysum helothamnus is also known for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
