Hieracium dyseces

Hieracium dyseces, commonly known as the narrowleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers are yellow and bloom in the summer months.

Hieracium dyseces is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
