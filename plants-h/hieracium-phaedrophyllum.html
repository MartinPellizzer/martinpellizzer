Hieracium phaedrophyllum

Hieracium phaedrophyllum, commonly known as the golden hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features wiry, upright stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped with hairy surfaces. Its flowers are yellow, with ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers.

Hieracium phaedrophyllum typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
