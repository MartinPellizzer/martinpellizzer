Humboltia pandurifera

Humboltia pandurifera, commonly known as the goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Baja California and the southern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub habitats. It has a compact growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.

Humboltia pandurifera plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for local wildlife.
