Helleborus spinescens

Helleborus spinescens, commonly known as the spiny hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, spiny leaves and its ability to thrive in shady, moist environments. The plant produces clusters of pale green to white flowers in early spring, which are often hidden beneath its foliage. Helleborus spinescens is known for its toxicity, containing various alkaloids that can be harmful if ingested.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and historical medicinal uses.
