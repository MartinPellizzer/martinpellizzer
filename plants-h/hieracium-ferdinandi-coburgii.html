Hieracium ferdinandi-coburgii

Hieracium ferdinandi-coburgii, commonly known as Coburg's hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Central Europe and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers and hairy, serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds, contributing to its ability to spread across open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
