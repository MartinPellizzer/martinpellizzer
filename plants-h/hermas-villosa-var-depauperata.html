Hermas villosa var. depauperata

Hermas villosa var. depauperata, commonly known as the woolly hermas, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
