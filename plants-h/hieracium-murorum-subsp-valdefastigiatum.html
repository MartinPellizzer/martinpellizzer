Hieracium murorum subsp. valdefastigiatum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

valdefastigiatum, commonly known as the creeping hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and meadows, and is often found in moist environments. It has a low-growing habit with a creeping stem that produces numerous flowering shoots.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and habitat restoration projects.
