Hieracium furfuraceoides

Hieracium furfuraceoides, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are hairy and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium furfuraceoides is often considered a weed but can be an important part of local ecosystems.
