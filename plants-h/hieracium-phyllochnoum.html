Hieracium phyllochnoum

Hieracium phyllochnoum, commonly known as the broadleaf hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features basal rosettes of broad, lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture. Its flowering stems rise above the leaves, bearing yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in summer.

Hieracium phyllochnoum is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, and it is considered a weed in some agricultural settings.
