Hieracium anacyrtopodum

Hieracium anacyrtopodum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and prominent ray florets. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and often hairy, with a distinct basal rosette formation.

It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, contributing to its role in supporting pollinators.
