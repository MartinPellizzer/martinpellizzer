Hieracium florentinum subsp. duerkhemiense

Hieracium florentinum subsp.

duerkhemiense, commonly known as the Duerkhemiense hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with ray florets that are shorter than the central disk florets.

It is a relatively small plant, usually reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.
