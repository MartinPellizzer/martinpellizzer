Heuchera micrantha var. pacifica

Heuchera micrantha var. pacifica, commonly known as the Pacific alumroot, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features dense, mat-forming foliage with heart-shaped leaves that vary in color from silver-green to deep red, depending on the variety and environmental conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and long blooming period.
