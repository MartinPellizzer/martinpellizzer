Hieracium hoppeanum subsp. testimoniale

Hieracium hoppeanum subsp.

testimoniale, commonly known as the testifying hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to orange composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. It has basal leaves that are broadly ovate with hairy surfaces, and its flowering period occurs from late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its historical use in traditional medicine.
