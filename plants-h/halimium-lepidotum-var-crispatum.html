Halimium lepidotum var. crispatum

Halimium lepidotum var. crispatum, commonly known as the hairy rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and is often found in rocky or coastal environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink or white, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in harsh, well-drained soils.
