Hedysarum laeve f. albiflorum

Hedysarum laeve f. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered sweet clover, is a cultivated form of the sweet clover plant.

It is characterized by its pale yellow to white flowers, which contrast with its green foliage. The plant is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability.

This variety is often used in forage and as a cover crop due to its hardy nature and adaptability.
