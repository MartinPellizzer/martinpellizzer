Hordeum ithaburense

Hordeum ithaburense, commonly known as the Tibetan barley, is a species of grass native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny grains. This plant is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including low temperatures and high UV radiation. Its seeds are primarily used as a traditional food source by local communities.

Hordeum ithaburense plays a significant role in the ecological and cultural landscape of its native region.
