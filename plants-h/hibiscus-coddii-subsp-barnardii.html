Hibiscus coddii subsp. barnardii

Hibiscus coddii subsp.

barnardii, commonly known as the Cape buffalo hibiscus, is a shrub native to South Africa. It features showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically blooming in spring and summer.

The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that are often arranged in pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
