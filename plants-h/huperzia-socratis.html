Huperzia socratis

Huperzia socratis, commonly known as the Socratus clubmoss, is a species of primitive fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and a rosette-like growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Its sporophylls are clustered in dense, cone-like structures called strobila, which produce spores for reproduction.

Huperzia socratis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural settings.
