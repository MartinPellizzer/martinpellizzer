Hornera umbellata

Hornera umbellata, commonly known as the umbrella bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its clusters of bell-shaped flowers that resemble an umbrella, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and is native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

Hornera umbellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, colorful blooms.
