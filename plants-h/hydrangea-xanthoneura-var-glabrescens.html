Hydrangea xanthoneura var. glabrescens

Hydrangea xanthoneura var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth hydrangea, is a cultivar characterized by its smooth, leafless stems and clusters of white to pink flowers.

It is a hybrid species that originated from crosses between different hydrangea varieties, resulting in unique horticultural traits. The plant thrives in partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil with a slightly acidic pH. Its foliage remains evergreen in mild climates, adding year-round visual interest to gardens.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
