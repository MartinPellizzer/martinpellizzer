Hordeum vulgare var. brunneinudum

Hordeum vulgare var. brunneinudum, commonly known as the brown-nutty barley, is a variant of barley characterized by its unique seed coat color and texture.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is typically found in temperate regions. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its seeds are often used in traditional and specialty food products due to their distinct flavor and nutritional profile.

This variety is valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its potential in agricultural and culinary applications.
