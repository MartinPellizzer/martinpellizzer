Hypericum linarioides

Hypericum linarioides, commonly known as the creeping St. John's wort, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to western Europe.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value and ability to form dense ground cover make it a popular choice for garden use.
