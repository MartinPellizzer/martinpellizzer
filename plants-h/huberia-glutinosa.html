Huberia glutinosa

Huberia glutinosa, commonly known as sticky huberia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. Its sticky secretions, which give it its common name, help protect it from herbivores and reduce water loss.

Huberia glutinosa is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to high sunlight exposure.
