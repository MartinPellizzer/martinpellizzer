Homalium crenulatum

Homalium crenulatum, commonly known as the serrated leaf euphorbia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences called cyathia. It typically grows as an understory shrub or small tree in humid, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
