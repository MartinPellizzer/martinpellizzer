Hieracium ambifarium

Hieracium ambifarium, commonly known as the two-sided hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by fluffy seeds, aiding in its spread.

Hieracium ambifarium is often found in open habitats and can sometimes become invasive in non-native regions.
