Hieracium vulgatum subsp. aviicola

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

aviicola, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow-orange flower heads composed of numerous small florets.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often reddish in color.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bright, daisy-like appearance.
