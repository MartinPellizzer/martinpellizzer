Hieracium horizontale

Hieracium horizontale, commonly known as the horizontal hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and it is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

The flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
