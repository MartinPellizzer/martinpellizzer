Hieracium jonassonii

Hieracium jonassonii, commonly known as the Jonasson's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous tiny florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are usually hairy and alternate, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

Hieracium jonassonii is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh, high-altitude conditions.
