Hordeum vulgare var. bachteevii

Hordeum vulgare var. bachteevii, commonly known as the Bachteevii barley, is a cultivated variant of barley characterized by its distinct morphological features.

It is typically shorter and more compact than other barley varieties, with a unique grain structure that influences its use in agriculture. This variety is adapted to specific environmental conditions, often thriving in cooler climates with moderate rainfall. Its botanical traits include narrow leaves, a dense inflorescence, and a hard, husked grain.

The plant's genetic makeup contributes to its resilience and is of interest in breeding programs for improved crop performance.
