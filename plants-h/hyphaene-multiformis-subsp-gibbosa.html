Hyphaene multiformis subsp. gibbosa

Hyphaene multiformis subsp.

gibbosa, commonly known as the African fan palm, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces long, slender fruits that contain a single seed, which is dispersed by animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional practices in its native habitats.
