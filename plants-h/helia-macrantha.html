Helia macrantha

Helia macrantha, commonly known as the large-flowered helia, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, blue-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of numerous small florets arranged in a dense, daisy-like pattern.

Helia macrantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.
