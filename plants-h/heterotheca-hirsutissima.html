Heterotheca hirsutissima

Heterotheca hirsutissima, commonly known as the hairy goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which help it retain moisture in arid environments. Its yellow flowers bloom in the spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Heterotheca hirsutissima is often found in rocky soils and open habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
