Hydrangea opuloides var. azisai

Hydrangea opuloides var. azisai, commonly known as the Azisai hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from white to pink, depending on soil pH. The plant produces clusters of small, sterile flowers surrounded by large, modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained, acidic soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its foliage turns vibrant red or orange in the fall, adding seasonal interest to the landscape.
