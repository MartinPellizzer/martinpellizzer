Hieracium manophyton

Hieracium manophyton, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and basal rosettes of lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, flat-topped clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in meadows, marshes, and along stream banks.

Hieracium manophyton is a common weed in agricultural fields and can spread rapidly through rhizomes.
