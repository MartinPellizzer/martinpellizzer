Hieraciodes chrysantha

Hieraciodes chrysantha, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms, which remain vibrant even when dried.
