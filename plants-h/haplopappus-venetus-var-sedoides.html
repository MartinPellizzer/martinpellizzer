Haplopappus venetus var. sedoides

Haplopappus venetus var. sedoides, commonly known as the desert fleabane, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is well adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its small, yellowish flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and it produces fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.

The plant has a low, sprawling growth habit and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
