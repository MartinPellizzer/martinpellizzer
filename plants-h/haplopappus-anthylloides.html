Haplopappus anthylloides

Haplopappus anthylloides, commonly known as the narrowleaf horseweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a rough, hairy texture.

It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months, followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.
