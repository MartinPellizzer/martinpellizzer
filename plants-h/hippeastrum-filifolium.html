Hippeastrum filifolium

Hippeastrum filifolium, commonly known as the thread-leaf amaryllis, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that give it its common name, and its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces large, bulbous underground stems that store nutrients and water, allowing it to thrive in varied conditions. It blooms in the spring or early summer, adding vibrant color to gardens and indoor spaces.

Hippeastrum filifolium is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for both novice and experienced gardeners.
