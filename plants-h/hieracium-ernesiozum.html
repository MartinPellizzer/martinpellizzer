Hieracium ernesiozum

Hieracium ernesiozum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rough texture and hairy surfaces, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a composite head.

Hieracium ernesiozum is often found in open meadows and alpine tundra, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
