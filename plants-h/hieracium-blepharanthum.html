Hieracium blepharanthum

Hieracium blepharanthum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has a basal rosette of leaves and produces a single flowering stem that can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grasslands.

Hieracium blepharanthum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
