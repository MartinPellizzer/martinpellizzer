Hieracium subauriculaeforme

Hieracium subauriculaeforme, commonly known as the auriculate hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped with distinctive auriculate (ear-like) lobes at the base, and it produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer.

The species is known for its adaptability and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
