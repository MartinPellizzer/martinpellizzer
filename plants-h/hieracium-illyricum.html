Hieracium illyricum

Hieracium illyricum, commonly known as the Illyrian hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium illyricum is often found in open habitats and can sometimes be invasive in certain regions.
