Hirschtia heterantherimorpha

Hirschtia heterantherimorpha, commonly known as the velvetleaf hirschtia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, with five petals and numerous stamens. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
