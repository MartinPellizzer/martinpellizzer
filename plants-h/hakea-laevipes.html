Hakea laevipes

Hakea laevipes, commonly known as the smooth hakea, is a shrub native to the south-western region of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical stems and dark green, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of wax. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. Hakea laevipes is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in poor soils and low rainfall conditions.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
