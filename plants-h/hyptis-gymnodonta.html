Hyptis gymnodonta

Hyptis gymnodonta, commonly known as Mexican mint or blue hyptis, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and sage. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental and medicinal gardens.

Hyptis gymnodonta is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
