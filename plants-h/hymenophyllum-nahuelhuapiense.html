Hymenophyllum nahuelhuapiense

Hymenophyllum nahuelhuapiense, commonly known as the Nahuelhuapi filmy fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate rainforests. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in small, clustered structures called sporangia.

This species is native to South America, specifically the Nahuelhuapi region in Argentina.
