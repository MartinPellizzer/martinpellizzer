Hydrangea Cinerea Var. Sterilis

Cinerea hydrangea, scientifically known as Hydrangea cinerea var. sterilis, is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Japan. This evergreen shrub is characterized by its pale gray-green foliage and clusters of white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. Unlike many hydrangeas, it does not produce fertile flowers, making it unique among its genus. Its delicate appearance and limited distribution make it a sought-after specimen for botanical enthusiasts. Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, conservation efforts are crucial to preserving this endangered species.
