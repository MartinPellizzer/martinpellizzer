Heliophila sarcostyla

Heliophila sarcostyla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heliophyte, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or marshy environments, showcasing its preference for moist soils. The plant features slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in whorls, and bloom in shades of white or pale yellow.

Heliophila sarcostyla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in water gardens or bog gardens.
