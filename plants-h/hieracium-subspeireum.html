Hieracium subspeireum

Hieracium subspeireum, commonly known as the lesser spearthistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces yellow to orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

Its leaves are generally lance-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces, and the plant has a robust root system adapted to its arid environments.
