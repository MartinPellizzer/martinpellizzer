Hymenolobium sericeum

Hymenolobium sericeum, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and pinnate leaves with silky, white-hair-covered surfaces. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Hymenolobium sericeum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
