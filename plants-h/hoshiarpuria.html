Hoshiarpuria

Hoshiarpuria, commonly known as the Kashmiri pine, is a coniferous tree native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and needle-like leaves arranged in clusters. The plant produces cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Hoshiarpuria thrives in cold, mountainous climates with well-drained soil.

Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in local construction and crafts.
