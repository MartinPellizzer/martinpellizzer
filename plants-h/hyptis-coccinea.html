Hyptis coccinea

Hyptis coccinea, commonly known as red hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant red or purple flowers and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows as a shrub or herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, and thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are clustered in whorled inflorescences.

Hyptis coccinea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
