Homalanthus brachystachys

Homalanthus brachystachys, commonly known as the short-stalked poinsettia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, brightly colored bracts that surround small, inconspicuous true flowers. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub with simple, alternate leaves that are often lance-shaped. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The species is cultivated for its ornamental value, particularly during the holiday season.
