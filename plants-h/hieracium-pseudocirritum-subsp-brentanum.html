Hieracium pseudocirritum subsp. brentanum

Hieracium pseudocirritum subsp.

brentanum, commonly known as the Brentanum hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its range.
