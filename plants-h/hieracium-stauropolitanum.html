Hieracium stauropolitanum

Hieracium stauropolitanum, commonly known as the Stauropotent hill harebell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in grassy meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a distinctive central disc surrounded by ray florets.

Hieracium stauropolitanum is known for its resilience to harsh alpine conditions and plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
