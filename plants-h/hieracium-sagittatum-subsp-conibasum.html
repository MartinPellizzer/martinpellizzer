Hieracium sagittatum subsp. conibasum

Hieracium sagittatum subsp.

conibasum, commonly known as the arrowhead hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

The plant has erect, hairy stems that can reach up to 60 cm in height, and its leaves are lanceolate with a distinct arrowhead shape. Its flowers are yellow, forming a single head with many small florets, and it produces achene fruits with pappus for wind dispersal.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal uses in traditional herbal remedies.
