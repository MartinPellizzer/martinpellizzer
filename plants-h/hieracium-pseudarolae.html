Hieracium pseudarolae

Hieracium pseudarolae, commonly known as the false yellow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers and hairy, basal leaves. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant has small, elongated leaves and produces numerous seeds that are dispersed by wind.

It is often considered an invasive species in some regions due to its rapid spread and competitive growth.
