Hyptis lurida

Hyptis lurida, commonly known as the pale hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or disturbed areas.

Hyptis lurida is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
