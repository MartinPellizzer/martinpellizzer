Hieracium aestivum subsp. phrissoides

Hieracium aestivum subsp.

phrissoides, commonly known as the summer hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

This subspecies is known for its adaptability and is often found in temperate regions.
