Hydrobryum micrantherum

Hydrobryum micrantherum, commonly known as the narrow-lobed water moss, is a small aquatic plant belonging to the family Hydrobryaceae.

It typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and ditches. The plant has finely divided, ribbon-like leaves that are often submerged, aiding in its ability to absorb nutrients from the water. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually appearing in clusters near the water's surface.

Hydrobryum micrantherum plays a role in maintaining water quality by filtering sediments and supporting aquatic ecosystems.
