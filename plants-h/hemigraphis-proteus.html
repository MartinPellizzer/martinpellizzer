Hemigraphis proteus

Hemigraphis proteus, commonly known as the velvetleaf or silverleaf plant, is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that range in color from silver to deep green, often with a reddish or purplish hue. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of about 1 to 2 feet. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Hemigraphis proteus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal.
