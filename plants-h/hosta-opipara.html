Hosta opipara

Hosta opipara, commonly known as the white-veined hosta, is a perennial plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often pale green with prominent white veins. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in late summer, typically in shades of blue or purple. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is well-suited for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its striking foliage and elegant bloom.
