Hyacinthus medius

Hyacinthus medius, commonly known as the Dutch hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage and clusters of fragrant, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces showy blooms in shades of purple, pink, white, and blue, typically in early spring. Its bulbs are perennial and can be divided to propagate new plants.

Hyacinthus medius is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and as cut flowers.
