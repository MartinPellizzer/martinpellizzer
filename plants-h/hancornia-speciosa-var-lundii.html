Hancornia speciosa var. lundii

Hancornia speciosa var. lundii, commonly known as the yellow bell tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble bells, which bloom in clusters along the branches. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that are typically opposite in arrangement. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable environments.
