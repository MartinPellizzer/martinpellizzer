Hypericum ardasenovii var. murghulicum

Hypericum ardasenovii var. murghulicum, commonly known as the Murghulicum St. John's Wort, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or alpine gardens.

Like other Hypericum species, it contains hypericin and pseudohypericin, which have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.
