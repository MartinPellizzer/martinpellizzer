Hieracium tauschii subsp. suebophilum

Hieracium tauschii subsp.

suebophilum, commonly known as the Suebian hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and produces showy yellow composite flowers.

This subspecies is native to Central and Eastern Europe and thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. It has a creeping growth habit with rhizomes that allow it to spread vegetatively.

The plant is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other sunny, well-drained areas.
