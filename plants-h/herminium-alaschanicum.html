Herminium alaschanicum

Herminium alaschanicum, commonly known as the Alaskan twayblade, is a perennial orchid native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and a single, erect flower spike that bears small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as meadows and forest floors. It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is known for its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.

Herminium alaschanicum is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions.
