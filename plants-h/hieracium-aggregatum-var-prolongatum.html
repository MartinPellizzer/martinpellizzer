Hieracium aggregatum var. prolongatum

Hieracium aggregatum var. prolongatum, commonly known as the long-stemmed hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features upright, hairy stems that can grow up to 60 cm in height, supporting clusters of yellow flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.
