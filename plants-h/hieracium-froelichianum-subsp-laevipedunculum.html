Hieracium froelichianum subsp. laevipedunculum

Hieracium froelichianum subsp.

laevipedunculum, commonly known as the smooth-stalked hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to orange flowers arranged in a composite head.

The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are generally basal, with a rough texture, and the stems are usually smooth and somewhat hairy.

This subspecies is distinguished by its elongated peduncles, which support the flower heads.
