Hymenochilus tanypodus

Hymenochilus tanypodus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia, particularly found in countries like India, Sri Lanka, and Indonesia. The plant features elongated, narrow leaves and produces small, delicate flowers with a unique structure. Its growth habit is typically epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of other plants without being parasitic.

The orchid's botanical significance lies in its ornamental value and its role in the ecological diversity of tropical forests.
