Heloniopsis japonica var. tessellata

Heloniopsis japonica var. tessellata, commonly known as the Japanese rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, mottled leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity in the wild, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
