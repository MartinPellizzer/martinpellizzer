Hedysarum trinerve

Hedysarum trinerve, commonly known as the three-veined milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with three main veins, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are typically visited by pollinators such as bees. Its pods are slender and contain several elliptical seeds.

Hedysarum trinerve is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
