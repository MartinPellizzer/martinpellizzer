Hieracium lycopifolium subsp. vallisiacum

Hieracium lycopifolium subsp.

vallisiacum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow flower heads.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are arranged in a composite head.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in high-altitude habitats.
