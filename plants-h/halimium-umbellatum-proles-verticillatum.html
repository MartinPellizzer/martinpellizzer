Halimium umbellatum proles verticillatum

Halimium umbellatum proles verticillatum, commonly known as the clustered rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, and its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
