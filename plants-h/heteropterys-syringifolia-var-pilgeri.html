Heteropterys syringifolia var. pilgeri

Heteropterys syringifolia var. pilgeri, commonly known as the pilger's roseleaf, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with a glossy surface and serrated margins. The plant produces small, spherical berries that are red when mature.

It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
