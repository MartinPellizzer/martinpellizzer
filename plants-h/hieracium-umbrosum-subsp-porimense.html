Hieracium umbrosum subsp. porimense

Hieracium umbrosum subsp.

porimense, commonly known as the shadowy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and its flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

This subspecies is noted for its adaptability to low-light conditions and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
