Hieracium filicaule

Hieracium filicaule, commonly known as the fern-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant features upright, hairy stems and heart-shaped, deeply lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow, with ray florets surrounding a central disk, and it produces small, oval-shaped achenes as fruits.

Hieracium filicaule is typically found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
