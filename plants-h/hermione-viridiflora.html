Hermione viridiflora

Hermione viridiflora, commonly known as the Emerald Bloom, is a striking plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant green, heart-shaped leaves that emit a faint bioluminescent glow at night. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of blue and violet, which bloom sequentially throughout the year. Its roots are known to secrete a sticky substance that attracts specific pollinators.

Due to its unique properties, it is highly sought after in both horticultural and medicinal circles.
