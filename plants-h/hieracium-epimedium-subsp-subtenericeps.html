Hieracium epimedium subsp. subtenericeps

Hieracium epimedium subsp.

subtenericeps, commonly known as the woolly hedgehog, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the daisy family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in grasslands and woodland edges. It is known for its compact growth habit and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
