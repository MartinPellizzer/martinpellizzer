Helianthemum ambiguum

Helianthemum ambiguum, commonly known as the creeping sunflower, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It features small, yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
