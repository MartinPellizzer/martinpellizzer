Hydrocotyle eichleri

Hydrocotyle eichleri, commonly known as the Eichler's marsh mallow, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It typically grows in wet, marshy environments and has a sprawling growth habit with hairy stems. The plant features palmately divided leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in umbels, and it produces berries that contain several seeds.

Hydrocotyle eichleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in wetland gardens.
