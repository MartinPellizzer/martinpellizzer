Hippocratea caribaea

Hippocratea caribaea, commonly known as the Caribbean moonflower, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean region.

It is a climbing vine that produces large, showy white flowers that open at night. The flowers have a strong, sweet scent that attracts nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and a woody stem with a rough texture.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in rainforests and coastal areas.
