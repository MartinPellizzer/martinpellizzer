Heterotheca sessiliflora var. fastigiata

Heterotheca sessiliflora var. fastigiata, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It features upright, sparsely branched stems that are often hairy, with lance-shaped leaves that are typically narrow and sessile. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers arranged in dense, flat-topped clusters, which bloom in late summer to early fall. Its foliage is aromatic and can be used in herbal remedies, while its structure makes it an attractive addition to native plant gardens.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies and open woodlands.
