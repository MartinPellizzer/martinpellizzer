Holodiscus microphyllus var. typicus

Holodiscus microphyllus var. typicus, commonly known as the small-leaved holodiscus, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are usually less than 2 inches in length. Its flowers are small and clustered, often white or pale yellow, and it produces fruit that is a capsule containing numerous seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its compact size and foliage.
