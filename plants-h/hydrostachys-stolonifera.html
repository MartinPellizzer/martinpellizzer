Hydrostachys stolonifera

Hydrostachys stolonifera, commonly known as water hyssop, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its creeping growth habit, with stolons that root at the nodes to form dense mats. The plant has small, opposite leaves that are often hairy, and it produces clusters of blue to purple flowers. It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Hydrostachys stolonifera is often used in ornamental gardening and has traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
