Helenium britannica

Helenium britannica, commonly known as the British sneezeweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, particularly the British Isles.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its yellow to orange flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Helenium britannica is known for its medicinal properties, historically used to treat respiratory ailments.
