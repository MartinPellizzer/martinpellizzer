Helianthus dealbatus

Helianthus dealbatus, commonly known as the whiteleaf sunflower, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family.

It is native to the eastern United States and is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers and pale green to white leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and forest edges. Its flowers bloom in late summer and early autumn, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Helianthus dealbatus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in disturbed soils.
