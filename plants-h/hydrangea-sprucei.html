Hydrangea sprucei

Hydrangea sprucei, commonly known as the mountain hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy flower clusters that range in color from white to pink and blue, depending on soil pH. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its foliage is dark green and turns red in the fall, adding seasonal interest.

Hydrangea sprucei is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to adapt to various garden conditions.
