Heliopsis × terebinthacea

Heliopsis × terebinthacea, commonly known as the golden coneflower, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
