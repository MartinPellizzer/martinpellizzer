Habenaria cumminsiana

Habenaria cumminsiana, commonly known as the Cummins' orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and delicate, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. Its flowers are usually arranged in a raceme and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is relatively rare and is of interest to botanists due to its specific habitat requirements and limited distribution.
