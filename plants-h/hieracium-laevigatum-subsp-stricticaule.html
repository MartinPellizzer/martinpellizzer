Hieracium laevigatum subsp. stricticaule

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

stricticaule, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its upright, slender stems and basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, yellow disk flowers that are typically surrounded by pale yellow to reddish-brown ray florets. It thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats such as meadows and roadsides.

Due to its invasive tendencies in some regions, it is often considered a weed in agricultural and natural areas.
