Helichrysum dichotomum

Helichrysum dichotomum, commonly known as the double-headed screw pine, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to hot, dry climates.

Helichrysum dichotomum is often used in traditional medicine and is also cultivated as an ornamental plant.
