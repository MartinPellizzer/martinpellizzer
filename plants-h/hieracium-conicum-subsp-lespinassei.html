Hieracium conicum subsp. lespinassei

Hieracium conicum subsp.

lespinassei, commonly known as the Alpine mouse-ear hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a compact head, typical of the Compositae family. It thrives in rocky and gravelly soils at high altitudes, often growing in open habitats such as meadows and slopes.
