Hygrophila pilosa

Hygrophila pilosa, commonly known as the hairy hygrophila, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it its common name. It produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like bees.

Hygrophila pilosa is popular in aquariums due to its ornamental value and ability to provide shelter for aquatic life.
