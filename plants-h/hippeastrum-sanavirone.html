Hippeastrum sanavirone

Hippeastrum sanavirone, commonly known as the Sanavirone amaryllis, is a bulbous plant native to South America.

It produces striking, showy flowers that are typically trumpet-shaped and come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features strap-like, narrow leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern around the base. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Hippeastrum sanavirone is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
