Hieracium tuorsinum

Hieracium tuorsinum, commonly known as the Tuorsin hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces numerous yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. Native to Europe, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats such as meadows and roadsides. The plant has rough, pubescent stems and is often found in temperate climates.

It is a common weed in agricultural areas and is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize new sites.
