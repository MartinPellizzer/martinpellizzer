Helianthus trachaefolius

Helianthus trachaefolius, commonly known as the roughleaf sunflower, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family.

It is native to the southeastern United States and is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as prairies and wetlands. It produces composite flowers with a central disk surrounded by ray florets.

Helianthus trachaefolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
