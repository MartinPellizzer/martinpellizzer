Helianthemum pergamaceum

Helianthemum pergamaceum, commonly known as the parchment rose or stonecress, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and are typically borne on hairy stems. The plant has succulent, leathery leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive and resilient addition to xeriscapes and Mediterranean-style gardens.
