Hieracium auriculoides subsp. subumbelliforme

Hieracium auriculoides subsp.

subumbelliforme, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its yellowish to orange flowers and is native to Europe and parts of Asia.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, exhibiting a creeping growth habit. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with a rough texture, and it produces small, clustered flower heads.

This subspecies is distinguished by its subumbelliform inflorescence, which gives it its specific name.
