Heracleum franchetii

Heracleum franchetii, commonly known as Chinese cow parsnip, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbel-shaped flowers.

It is a perennial herb native to China, often found in moist meadows and streambanks. The plant features tall, hollow stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are large, pinnately divided with a distinctive silvery underside. Its flowers are white to pale green, producing small, dry fruits known as schizocarps.

The plant contains compounds that can cause skin irritation, requiring caution during handling.
