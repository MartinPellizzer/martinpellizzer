Haematostaphis purpurascens

Haematostaphis purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-stemmed grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish-purple stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or inland grasslands. Its flowering stems produce small, clustered inflorescences that bloom during the rainy season.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique coloration.
