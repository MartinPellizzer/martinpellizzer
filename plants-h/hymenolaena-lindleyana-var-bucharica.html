Hymenolaena lindleyana var. bucharica

Hymenolaena lindleyana var. bucharica, commonly known as the Sudan grass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, cylindrical stems that often lie flat on the ground. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration, and the plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in warm climates and is often used for forage and erosion control due to its hardiness and rapid growth.

This variety is particularly adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
