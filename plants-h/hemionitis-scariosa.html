Hemionitis scariosa

Hemionitis scariosa, commonly known as the desert shield fern, is a unique plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb with a distinctive rosette of waxy, leathery leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown, which are adapted for wind pollination. Its leaves are covered in a thick cuticle and have a scaly texture, providing additional protection against desiccation.

Hemionitis scariosa is often found in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in conditions of extreme temperature fluctuations and low precipitation.
