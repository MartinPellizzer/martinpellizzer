Helleborus pallidus

Helleborus pallidus, commonly known as the pale hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen, leathery leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of pale green to white, bell-shaped flowers during late winter to early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Helleborus pallidus is known for its toxicity, containing various cardiac glycosides that can be harmful if ingested.
