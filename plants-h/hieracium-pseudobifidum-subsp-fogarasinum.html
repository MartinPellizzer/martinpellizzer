Hieracium pseudobifidum subsp. fogarasinum

Hieracium pseudobifidum subsp.

fogarasinum, commonly known as the Fogarasinum hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, meadows, and pastures.

The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are hairy and often have a reddish or purple tint. Its flowering stems bear a cluster of yellow, dandelion-like flowers, which bloom from late spring to early summer.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in areas with well-drained soils.
