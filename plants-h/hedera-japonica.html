Hedera japonica

Hedera japonica, commonly known as Japanese ivy, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araliaceae.

It is a climbing or trailing plant characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that are typically heart-shaped or ovate. Native to East Asia, it has been widely cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by bright red berries, which are attractive to birds.

It thrives in shaded environments and is often used in landscaping for its ability to cover walls, trees, and ground surfaces.
