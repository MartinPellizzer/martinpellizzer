Hyptis linarioides

Hyptis linarioides, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. Its leaves are often aromatic and have a mint-like scent, which is common in the Lamiaceae family.

Hyptis linarioides is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and fragrance.
