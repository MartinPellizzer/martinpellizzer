Helianthemum occidentale var. cheiranthoides

Helianthemum occidentale var. cheiranthoides, commonly known as the Western sandverbena, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing shrub with small, evergreen leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of showy, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suited for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a desirable plant for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
