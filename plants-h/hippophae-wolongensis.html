Hippophae wolongensis

Hippophae wolongensis, commonly known as the Chinese sandthorn, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of China.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange berries, which are rich in vitamin C and other nutrients. The plant has small, leathery leaves and produces flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in desert margins and mountainous areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is used for soil stabilization and has some medicinal applications.
