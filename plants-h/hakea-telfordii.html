Hakea telfordii

Hakea telfordii, commonly known as the coastal hakea, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its rigid, spine-tipped leaves and clusters of bright red, urn-shaped flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is typically found in coastal regions. It is well adapted to drought and salt spray, making it a resilient species.

Hakea telfordii plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
