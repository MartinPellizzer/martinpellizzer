Hernandezia leucomphala

Hernandezia leucomphala, commonly known as the white-flowered orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial herb that typically grows in shaded, moist environments within forest understories. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and produces striking white or pale yellow flowers with a distinctive, elongated spur. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in greenhouses or specialized gardens.
