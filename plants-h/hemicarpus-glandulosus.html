Hemicarpus glandulosus

Hemicarpus glandulosus, commonly known as the hairy sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowering spikes are usually clustered at the top of the plant, producing small, greenish flowers.

Hemicarpus glandulosus is often found in wetland habitats and is adapted to moist, nutrient-rich soils.
