Hexasepalum serrulatum

Hexasepalum serrulatum, commonly known as the serrated six-lobed orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring six sepals that are often serrated or toothed. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, where it thrives in humid conditions. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to pale pink, depending on the species.

The botanical interest in Hexasepalum serrulatum lies in its intricate morphology and ecological adaptations to its natural habitat.
