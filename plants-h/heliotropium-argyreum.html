Heliotropium argyreum

Heliotropium argyreum, commonly known as the silverleaf heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and showy, violet or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types. It prefers full sun to partial shade and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

Its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.
