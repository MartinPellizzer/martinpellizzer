Hyptis capitata var. mariannarum

Hyptis capitata var. mariannarum, commonly known as the Bahama hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its small, tubular flowers are usually violet or blue in color and attract various pollinators.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
