Hedyotis brachyloba

Hedyotis brachyloba, commonly known as the small-leafed inchplant, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically less than 2 centimeters in length. The plant produces tiny white or greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. It prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Hedyotis brachyloba is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
