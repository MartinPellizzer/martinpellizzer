Helianthus laurifolius

Helianthus laurifolius, commonly known as the evergreen sunflower, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, dark green leaves that remain evergreen throughout the year. The plant produces bright yellow composite flowers with a central disk surrounded by ray florets. It typically grows in wetlands and along stream banks, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration efforts.
