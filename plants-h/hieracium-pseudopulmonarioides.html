Hieracium pseudopulmonarioides

Hieracium pseudopulmonarioides, commonly known as the false lungwort, is a flowering plant in the sunflower family (Asteraceae).

It is native to Europe and is characterized by its distinctive rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers arranged in a dense, flat head, which are typically visited by bees and other pollinators. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Hieracium pseudopulmonarioides is a perennial species that can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
