Helichrysum acrobates

Helichrysum acrobates, commonly known as the mountain everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils at higher altitudes. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

Helichrysum acrobates is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and dry landscapes.
