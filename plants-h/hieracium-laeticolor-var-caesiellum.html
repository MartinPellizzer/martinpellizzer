Hieracium laeticolor var. caesiellum

Hieracium laeticolor var. caesiellum, commonly known as the blue-veined hawkweed, is a variant of the hawkweed genus characterized by its striking floral display.

It features small, daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and blue-veined petals, which contribute to its ornamental appeal. The plant is typically found in meadows and grasslands, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

This variant is valued in horticulture for its vibrant colors and ease of cultivation.
