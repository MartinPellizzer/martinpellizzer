Hypericum hyssopifolium subsp. elongatum

Hypericum hyssopifolium subsp.

elongatum, commonly known as the elongated St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems.

The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers with four petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its stems are often woody and covered with fine, soft hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.
