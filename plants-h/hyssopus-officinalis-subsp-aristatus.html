Hyssopus officinalis subsp. aristatus

Hyssopus officinalis subsp.

aristatus, commonly known as the upright hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are usually gray-green in color.

The plant produces spikes of blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often used in traditional medicine for its aromatic and antiseptic properties.

This subspecies is distinguished by its more upright growth habit compared to other hyssop varieties.
