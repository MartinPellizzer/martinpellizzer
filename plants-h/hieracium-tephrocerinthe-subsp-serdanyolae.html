Hieracium tephrocerinthe subsp. serdanyolae

Hieracium tephrocerinthe subsp.

serdanyolae, commonly known as the Serdanya hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellowish to orange flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and its flowers are usually arranged in a single head.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its range.
