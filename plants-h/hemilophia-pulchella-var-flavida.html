Hemilophia pulchella var. flavida

Hemilophia pulchella var. flavida, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a striking tropical orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow and orange flowers, which are adorned with intricate patterns and a fragrant scent. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its colorful blooms.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
