Hieracium waldsteinii subsp. biokovoense

Hieracium waldsteinii subsp.

biokovoense, commonly known as the Biokovo hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine meadows, preferring well-drained soils. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, inconspicuous florets in its capitula.

Due to its restricted habitat, it is considered a rare and endemic species in its native range.
