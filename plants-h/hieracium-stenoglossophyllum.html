Hieracium stenoglossophyllum

Hieracium stenoglossophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-lipped hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat, disk-like head, with narrow, pointed petals.

Hieracium stenoglossophyllum is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
