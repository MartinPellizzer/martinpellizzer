Hieracium slovacum

Hieracium slovacum, commonly known as the Slovak hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring well-drained, sandy or loamy conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

Hieracium slovacum is often found in open habitats and can form dense colonies, contributing to local biodiversity.
