Hieracium conringiifolium

Hieracium conringiifolium, commonly known as the Conring's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads made up of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is often found in open habitats and is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens with suitable conditions.
