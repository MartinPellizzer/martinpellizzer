Hieracium jussiaeanum

Hieracium jussiaeanum, commonly known as the Jussiaean hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its flowering period occurs from late spring to early autumn, producing yellow to orange flowers that attract pollinators.

The leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
