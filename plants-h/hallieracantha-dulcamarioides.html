Hallieracantha dulcamarioides

Hallieracantha dulcamarioides, commonly known as the creeping nightshade, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by orange-red berries. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.
