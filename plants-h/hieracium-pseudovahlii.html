Hieracium pseudovahlii

Hieracium pseudovahlii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and cooler climates. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture, and its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium pseudovahlii is often found in rocky areas and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
