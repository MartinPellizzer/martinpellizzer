Hieracium nigrescens subsp. latypeum

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

latypeum, commonly known as the dark-hued hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous tiny florets.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with serrated edges, and its flowers are yellow, often with a reddish or purplish tinge.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
