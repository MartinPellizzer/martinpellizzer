Hosta campanulata var. parviflora

Hosta campanulata var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered bell hosta, is a cultivar of the Hosta genus native to East Asia.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a slight variegation. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental foliage and delicate floral display in garden settings.
