Hakea asteriscosa

Hakea asteriscosa, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and distinctive cylindrical flower spikes. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions.

Hakea asteriscosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
