Heliconia undulata

Heliconia undulata, commonly known as the zigzag heliconia, is a tropical plant belonging to the Heliconiaceae family.

It is native to the Caribbean and Central America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its large, colorful bracts that surround its inflorescence, which resembles a spiral or zigzag pattern. These bracts are often bright red or orange, serving to attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bats.

Heliconia undulata is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and as part of floral arrangements.
