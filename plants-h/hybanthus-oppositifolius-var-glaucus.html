Hybanthus oppositifolius var. glaucus

Hybanthus oppositifolius var. glaucus, commonly known as the glaucous hybanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy, bluish-gray coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
