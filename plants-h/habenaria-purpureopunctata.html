Habenaria purpureopunctata

Habenaria purpureopunctata, commonly known as the purple-spotted orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a short rhizome and a single, upright stem that bears several leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers with distinctive purple spots on the petals and sepals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The orchid's morphology and floral structure are typical of the Orchidaceae family, showcasing intricate adaptations for pollination.
