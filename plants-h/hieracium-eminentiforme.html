Hieracium eminentiforme

Hieracium eminentiforme, commonly known as the tall hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, and it has a rosette of basal leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Hieracium eminentiforme is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and can often be found in meadows, roadsides, and other open areas.

Its botanical structure includes a slender, branched stem that supports its showy inflorescence.
