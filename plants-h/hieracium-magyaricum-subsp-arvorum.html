Hieracium magyaricum subsp. arvorum

Hieracium magyaricum subsp.

arvorum, commonly known as the Hungarian hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Central Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. It has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its range.
