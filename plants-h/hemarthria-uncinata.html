Hemarthria uncinata

Hemarthria uncinata, commonly known as hairy barnyardgrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It has erect, rhizomatous growth and produces dense, tufted inflorescences with spikelets that have a distinctive hooked awn. The plant is characterized by its narrow, flat leaves with a hairy texture on both surfaces. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands and grasslands.

Hemarthria uncinata is both a weed in agricultural settings and an important forage plant for livestock.
