Hieracium frigidum var. jubatum

Hieracium frigidum var. jubatum, commonly known as the frostweed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in a single, erect head. It has a fibrous root system and is characterized by its ability to thrive in cooler climates.

Its botanical structure supports its role in ecological systems, providing food for various insects and contributing to soil stability.
