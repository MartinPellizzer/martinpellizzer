Hieracium euprepes

Hieracium euprepes, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a central disc.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
