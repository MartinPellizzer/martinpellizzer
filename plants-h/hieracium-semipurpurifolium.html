Hieracium semipurpurifolium

Hieracium semipurpurifolium, commonly known as the purple-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features serrated, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple or red, giving it its common name. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Hieracium semipurpurifolium is typically found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
