Hymenocallis littoralis var. longituba

Hymenocallis littoralis var. longituba, commonly known as the coastal spider lily, is a striking plant native to the coastal regions of Mexico and Central America.

It is a bulbous perennial that thrives in sandy, well-drained soils near the shoreline. The plant produces tall, narrow leaves and clusters of white, spider-like flowers with a distinctive yellow throat. Its adaptability to salt spray and strong winds makes it a resilient coastal species.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological importance in stabilizing dune ecosystems.
