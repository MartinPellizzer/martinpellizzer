Hieracium cirritum subsp. adusticeps

Hieracium cirritum subsp.

adusticeps, commonly known as the burnt-tipped hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems.

The subspecies is distinguished by its elongated, glandular-tipped bracts that give it its common name. It typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats such as meadows and roadsides.

The plant has a fibrous root system and produces numerous small, oval-shaped seeds.
