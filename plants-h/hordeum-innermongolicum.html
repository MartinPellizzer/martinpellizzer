Hordeum innermongolicum

Hordeum innermongolicum, commonly known as Inner Mongolian barley, is a species of wild barley native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow leaves and small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences. This plant typically grows in dry, arid environments and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions. Hordeum innermongolicum is of interest to botanists and agricultural researchers due to its potential for genetic diversity and resilience.

Its study contributes to understanding the evolution and domestication of barley species.
