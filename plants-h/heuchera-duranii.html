Heuchera duranii

Heuchera duranii, commonly known as the Duranii alumroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features attractive, heart-shaped leaves that are typically green or variegated with red, purple, or silver markings. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers in the spring, which are borne on slender, upright stems. Heuchera duranii thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental foliage and moderate growth habit contribute to its horticultural value.
