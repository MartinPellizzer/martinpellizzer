Hedera japonica var. variegata

Hedera japonica var. variegata, commonly known as Japanese ivy, is a climbing evergreen vine native to Japan.

It features heart-shaped leaves with striking white and green variegation, which enhances its ornamental appeal. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for hanging baskets or as a ground cover.

Its adaptability and aesthetic value make it a popular choice in both indoor and outdoor gardening.
