Hieracium transiens subsp. macrolepioides

Hieracium transiens subsp.

macrolepioides, commonly known as the broadleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lanceolate to ovate, with a rough texture, and the flowers are yellow with ray florets.

It is known for its aggressive growth and can form dense colonies, often outcompeting native vegetation.
