Hedysarum drobovii

Hedysarum drobovii, commonly known as the Drobovii milkvetch, is a perennial leguminous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces yellow or orange pea-like flowers, which are followed by pods containing small, rounded seeds. Hedysarum drobovii is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in soil enrichment.

Its ornamental value also makes it a candidate for ecological restoration projects in semi-arid regions.
