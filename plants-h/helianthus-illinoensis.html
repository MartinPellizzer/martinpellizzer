Helianthus illinoensis

Helianthus illinoensis, commonly known as the Illinois goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to the central United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flower heads that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features slender, erect stems covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have serrated edges.

The Illinois goldenrod is an important nectar source for bees and other pollinators, contributing to ecological diversity in its native habitat.
