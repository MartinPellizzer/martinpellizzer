Hieracium bifidum subsp. stenolepis

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

stenolepis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade.

The plant has basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in late summer. Its flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and the plant is characterized by its finely divided leaves and hairy stems.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental uses in some regions.
