Hieracium sabaudum subsp. virgultorum

Hieracium sabaudum subsp.

virgultorum, commonly known as the Virgin's thimble or Sabaudian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

The plant features basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces yellow flower heads composed of numerous small florets. Its flowers bloom from June to August, and the plant is often found in disturbed or nutrient-rich soils.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower structure and ability to spread via rhizomes.
