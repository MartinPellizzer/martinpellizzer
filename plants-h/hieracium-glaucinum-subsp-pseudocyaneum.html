Hieracium glaucinum subsp. pseudocyaneum

Hieracium glaucinum subsp.

pseudocyaneum, commonly known as the glaucous hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosette of leaves, which are typically covered in a whitish or bluish waxy coating.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
