Hieracium wilczekianum subsp. subdoronicifolium

Hieracium wilczekianum subsp.

subdoronicifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, lanceolate leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters at the ends of its stems. Its flowering period occurs from late spring to early summer, and it is characterized by its hairy stems and small, daisy-like inflorescences.

This subspecies is part of the genus Hieracium, which includes many species of hawkweeds known for their ecological adaptability.
