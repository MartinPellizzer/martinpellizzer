Hylocereus trinitatensis

Hylocereus trinitatensis, commonly known as the Trinidad night blooming cactus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spineless stems and nocturnal blooming habit. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night and close by morning. It thrives in warm, tropical climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its unique blooming pattern and ornamental value, it is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
