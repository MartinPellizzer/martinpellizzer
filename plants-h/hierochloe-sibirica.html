Hierochloe sibirica

Hierochloe sibirica, commonly known as Siberian hairgrass, is a perennial grass native to cold climates of Eurasia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth and silver-green foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or arctic regions. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, greenish-brown flowers.

Hierochloe sibirica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
