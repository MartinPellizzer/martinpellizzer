Hieracium eupatorium

Hieracium eupatorium, commonly known as common hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Hieracium eupatorium is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it an invasive species in some regions.
