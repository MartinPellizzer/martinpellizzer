Helichrysum lepidissimum

Helichrysum lepidissimum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, yellow-orange inflorescences that resemble small daisies. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically clustered in compact, rounded heads. It is a perennial shrub that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
