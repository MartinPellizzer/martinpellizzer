Hieracium angermanicum

Hieracium angermanicum, commonly known as the Angerman's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring well-drained conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium angermanicum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
