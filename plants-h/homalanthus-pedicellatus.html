Homalanthus pedicellatus

Homalanthus pedicellatus, commonly known as the Pacific Island hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Pacific.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, yellow, or orange. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in coastal areas and is often found growing on sandy soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
