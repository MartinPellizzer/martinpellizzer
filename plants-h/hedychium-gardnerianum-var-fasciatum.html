Hedychium gardnerianum var. fasciatum

Hedychium gardnerianum var. fasciatum, commonly known as the Variegated Butterfly Ginger, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sword-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant yellow flowers with a distinctive red throat. The plant produces clusters of blooms that resemble butterflies, hence its common name. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and partial shade.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
