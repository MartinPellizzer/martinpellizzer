Henckelia fruticola

Henckelia fruticola, commonly known as the Chilean heather, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, sunny environments, often found in coastal and mountainous regions. The plant features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in spring and summer. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

Henckelia fruticola is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in well-drained soil conditions.
