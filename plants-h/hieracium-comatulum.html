Hieracium comatulum

Hieracium comatulum, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.

Its yellowish-orange flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators.
