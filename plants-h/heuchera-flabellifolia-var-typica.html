Heuchera flabellifolia var. typica

Heuchera flabellifolia var. typica, commonly known as the red-veined sorrel, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive red veins and a compact, mounding growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers in the spring. It thrives in partial to full shade and moist, well-drained soil.

Heuchera flabellifolia var. typica is valued for its ornamental foliage and is often used in woodland gardens.
