Hyptis alpestris

Hyptis alpestris, commonly known as the alpine hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a member of the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas, where it thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are often hairy and have a distinct mint-like scent, which contributes to its medicinal and aromatic uses.

Hyptis alpestris is valued for its ability to stabilize soil in harsh environments and for its potential in traditional herbal remedies.
