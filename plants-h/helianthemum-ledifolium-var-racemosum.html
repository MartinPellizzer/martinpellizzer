Helianthemum ledifolium var. racemosum

Helianthemum ledifolium var. racemosum, commonly known as the clustered rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners in mild climates.
