Hieracium murorum subsp. umbrosum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

umbrosum, commonly known as the shadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering stems that bear yellow ray florets surrounded by tubular disk florets.

This subspecies is typically found in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and grassy slopes. Its leaves are usually lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
