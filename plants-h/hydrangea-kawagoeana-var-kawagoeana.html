Hydrangea kawagoeana var. kawagoeana

Hydrangea kawagoeana var. kawagoeana, commonly known as the Japanese hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from white to pink, depending on soil pH. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has serrated, ovate leaves that are dark green in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
