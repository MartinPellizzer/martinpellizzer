Helianthemum asperum var. parviflorum

Helianthemum asperum var. parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a low-growing shrub with greyish, hairy leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its hardy, drought-resistant nature.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
