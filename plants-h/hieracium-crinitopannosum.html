Hieracium crinitopannosum

Hieracium crinitopannosum, commonly known as the hairy yellow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped with hairy surfaces, and the flowers are yellow with a distinctive, fluffy appearance.

It is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
