Heuchera glabella

Heuchera glabella, commonly known as the smoothleaf alumroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and have a smooth, velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or red during the spring. Its foliage is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value, and it thrives in partial shade with well-drained soil.

Heuchera glabella is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators and its low maintenance requirements.
