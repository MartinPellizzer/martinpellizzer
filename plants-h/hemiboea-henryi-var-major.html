Hemiboea henryi var. major

Hemiboea henryi var. major, commonly known as the Chinese rock orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid native to China.

It is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink. The plant thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found growing on rocky surfaces or in forest understories. Its leaves are typically narrow and leathery, and it has a short flowering period during late spring.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable species in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
