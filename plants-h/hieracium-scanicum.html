Hieracium scanicum

Hieracium scanicum, commonly known as the Scandinavian hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to northern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
