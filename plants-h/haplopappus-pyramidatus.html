Haplopappus pyramidatus

Haplopappus pyramidatus, commonly known as the pyramid dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and wind-dispersed seeds. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in arid environments. Its leaves are basal, narrow, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head.

The plant is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.
