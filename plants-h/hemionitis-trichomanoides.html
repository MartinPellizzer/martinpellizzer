Hemionitis trichomanoides

Hemionitis trichomanoides, commonly known as the desert maidenhair fern, is a rare and unique fern species native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like fronds that are typically grayish-green in color and have a leathery texture. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to harsh desert environments. Its reproductive structures, including spore-producing sorus, are located on the underside of its fronds.
