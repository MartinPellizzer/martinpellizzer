Helianthemum guttatum subsp. plantagineum

Helianthemum guttatum subsp.

plantagineum, commonly known as the sticky rockrose, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its small, glandular leaves that often appear sticky to the touch.

The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal and Mediterranean regions.

Its adaptability to harsh environments makes it a resilient and hardy species.
