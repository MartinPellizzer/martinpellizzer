Halothamnus sistanicus

Halothamnus sistanicus, commonly known as the Sistan dwarf shrub, is a perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Iran and Pakistan.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, fleshy leaves. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, saline environments, often found in rocky soils and dry habitats. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, producing a pale pink to red coloration.

Halothamnus sistanicus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife in its native range.
