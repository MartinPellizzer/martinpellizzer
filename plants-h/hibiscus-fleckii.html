Hibiscus fleckii

Hibiscus fleckii, commonly known as the streaked hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, large, and showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often found growing in moist, shaded environments. Hibiscus fleckii is a member of the Malvaceae family, which includes other hibiscus species and cotton plants.

Its unique floral patterns and ornamental value make it a popular choice for horticultural collections.
