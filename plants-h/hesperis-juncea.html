Hesperis juncea

Hesperis juncea, commonly known as sweet rocket, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many cruciferous plants. The plant features showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it often grows in meadows and woodland edges.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
