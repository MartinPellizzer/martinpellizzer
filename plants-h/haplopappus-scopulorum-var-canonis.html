Haplopappus scopulorum var. canonis

Haplopappus scopulorum var. canonis, commonly known as the Canon Mountains groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typically herbaceous growth habit. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in rocky, arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a pioneer species in disturbed or barren landscapes.
