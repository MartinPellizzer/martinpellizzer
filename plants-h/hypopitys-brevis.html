Hypopitys brevis

Hypopitys brevis, commonly known as the shortleaf sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are usually small, fleshy, and covered in a layer of fine, white hairs.

Hypopitys brevis is adapted to thrive in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
