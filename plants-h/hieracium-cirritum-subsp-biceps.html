Hieracium cirritum subsp. biceps

Hieracium cirritum subsp.

biceps, commonly known as the two-armed hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture, and the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is a perennial herb that spreads through rhizomes and is often found in temperate regions.
