Hibiscus sublobatus

Hibiscus sublobatus, commonly known as the African hibiscus, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Hibiscus sublobatus is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.
