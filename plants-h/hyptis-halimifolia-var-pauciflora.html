Hyptis halimifolia var. pauciflora

Hyptis halimifolia var. pauciflora, commonly known as Mexican mint or blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its fragrant herbs and medicinal properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
