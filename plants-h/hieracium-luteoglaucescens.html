Hieracium luteoglaucescens

Hieracium luteoglaucescens, commonly known as the yellow-blue hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring temperate climates. Its leaves are lanceolate, with a hairy surface, and the flowers are yellow with a bluish tinge, giving it its common name.

It is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
