Heliotropium undulatum var. suberosum

Heliotropium undulatum var. suberosum, commonly known as the woolly heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a unique and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
