Hippocrepis confusa subvar. trachycarpa

Hippocrepis confusa subvar. trachycarpa, commonly known as the rough-fruited honeysuckle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, fragrant flowers. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and often hairy, while the fruit is a dry pod containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
