Habenaria tetrapetala

Habenaria tetrapetala, commonly known as the four-petaled orchid, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is a perennial herb with a slender, often branching stem that produces multiple flowering shoots. The plant features showy, fragrant flowers with four petals, typically in shades of white, yellow, or pink, and a distinctive lip that is often broader than the other petals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices.

The orchid has a complex root system and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens.
