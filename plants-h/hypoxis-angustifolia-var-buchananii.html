Hypoxis angustifolia var. buchananii

Hypoxis angustifolia var. buchananii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf false shamrock, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats, and its growth is typically low to the ground.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and blooms.
