Hakea microcarpa var. bathurstiana

Hakea microcarpa var. bathurstiana, commonly known as the Bathurst hakea, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves and distinctive, cylindrical flower spikes that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathland and woodland habitats. It produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds adapted for dispersal by animals.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
