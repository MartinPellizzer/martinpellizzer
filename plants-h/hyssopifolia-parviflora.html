Hyssopifolia parviflora

Hyssopifolia parviflora, commonly known as small-flowered hyssop, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or blue in color, arranged in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, often with a strong, mint-like fragrance.

Hyssopifolia parviflora is often grown as an ornamental plant for its attractive appearance and aromatic qualities.
