Hebanthe paniculata

Hebanthe paniculata, commonly known as the pink flame plant, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that consist of clusters of small, pink to red flowers arranged in panicles. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a slightly hairy surface.

Hebanthe paniculata thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
