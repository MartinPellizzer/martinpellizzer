Hieracium laevigatum subsp. thuringiosilvanum

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

thuringiosilvanum, commonly known as the Thuringian wood hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features a basal rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves with a hairy surface. Its flowering stems bear yellow disc flowers surrounded by yellowish-orange ray florets.

It is characterized by its pubescent stems and leaves, and it is often found in forested areas of Germany and adjacent regions.
