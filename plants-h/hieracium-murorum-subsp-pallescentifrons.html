Hieracium murorum subsp. pallescentifrons

Hieracium murorum subsp.

pallescentifrons, commonly known as the pale-fronded mouse-ear hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its leaves are hairy and have a distinctive pale green color, while its yellow flowers bloom in early summer.

It is considered a characteristic species of certain ecological habitats and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
