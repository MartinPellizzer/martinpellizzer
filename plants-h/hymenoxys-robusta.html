Hymenoxys robusta

Hymenoxys robusta, commonly known as the robust sunflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant features a sturdy, upright stem with rough, hairy leaves that are typically lance-shaped. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hymenoxys robusta is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils.
