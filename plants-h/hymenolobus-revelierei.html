Hymenolobus revelierei

Hymenolobus revelierei, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a rare and unique orchid species native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, fragrant flowers with a distinctive coloration pattern. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in the humid tropical forests of its native habitat. Its botanical features include long, slender petals and a specialized structure called a labellum, which plays a role in pollination.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered an endangered species.
