Halenia brevicornis var. latifolia

Halenia brevicornis var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved halenia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are broader than the typical variety, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small, clustered in inflorescences, and often have a distinctive coloration.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and subtle floral display.
