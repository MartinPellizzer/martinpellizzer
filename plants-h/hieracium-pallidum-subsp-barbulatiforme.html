Hieracium pallidum subsp. barbulatiforme

Hieracium pallidum subsp.

barbulatiforme, commonly known as the pale hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands, meadows, and roadside areas.

The plant features upright stems with hairy leaves that are lance-shaped and have a rough texture. Its yellow flowers are clustered in heads and are surrounded by pale yellow to white ray florets.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower structures and medicinal properties in some species.
