Hydrangea xanthoneura

Hydrangea xanthoneura, commonly known as the yellow hydrangea, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that typically range in color from white to yellow, depending on soil pH. The plant produces broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained, acidic soils, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Hydrangea xanthoneura is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its role in local ecosystems.
