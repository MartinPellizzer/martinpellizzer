Hyacinthus fastigiatus

Hyacinthus fastigiatus, commonly known as the evergreen hyacinth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers during the spring season. Its leaves are evergreen, needle-like, and form a compact rosette at the base.

Hyacinthus fastigiatus is well-suited for rock gardens or as a container plant due to its compact size and attractive appearance.
