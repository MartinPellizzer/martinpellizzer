Habracanthus tachirensis

Habracanthus tachirensis, commonly known as the Tachira grass.

It is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Venezuela and Colombia. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in open grasslands and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely known for its medicinal properties.
