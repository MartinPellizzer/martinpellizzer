Herschelia graminifolia

Herschelia graminifolia, commonly known as the grass-leaved Herschelia, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has a fibrous root system and produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
