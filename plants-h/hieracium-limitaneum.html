Hieracium limitaneum

Hieracium limitaneum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Hieracium limitaneum produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, contributing to its role in supporting pollinators.
