Hetaeria biloba

Hetaeria biloba, commonly known as the twin-lobed fern, is a rare and delicate species belonging to the family Hetaeriaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, bipinnate fronds that give it a lacy appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds, a typical feature of ferns.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Hetaeria biloba is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
