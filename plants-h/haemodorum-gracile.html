Haemodorum gracile

Haemodorum gracile, commonly known as the slender false indigo, is a perennial herb native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the family Haemodoraceae, which includes plants known for their striking floral displays. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, red or orange-red flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along streamlines.

Haemodorum gracile is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native habitats.
