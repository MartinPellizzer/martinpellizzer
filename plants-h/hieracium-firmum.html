Hieracium firmum

Hieracium firmum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped with serrated edges. Native to Europe, it has been introduced to various parts of the world, often growing in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Its botanical features make it both a common weed and a subject of ecological study.
