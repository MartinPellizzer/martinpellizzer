Hieracium glabratum subsp. leontinum

Hieracium glabratum subsp.

leontinum, commonly known as the white hawk's-beard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in dry meadows, grasslands, and rocky soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade.

The plant features a robust, upright stem with alternate, heart-shaped to lanceolate leaves that are usually hairy. Its most distinctive characteristic is its bright yellow to orange composite flower head, composed of numerous small florets arranged in a daisy-like pattern.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
