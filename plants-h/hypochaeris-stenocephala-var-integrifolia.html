Hypochaeris stenocephala var. integrifolia

Hypochaeris stenocephala var. integrifolia, commonly known as the narrow-headed hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows, and it has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves.

It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, and it is often considered a weed due to its rapid spread and ability to colonize various environments.
