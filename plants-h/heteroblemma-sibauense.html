Heteroblemma sibauense

Heteroblemma sibauense, commonly known as the Sibauan spider-plant, is a rare and unique species native to the tropical rainforests of Borneo.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated leaves that resemble those of spider plants, though it belongs to a different family. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil to flourish. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically appearing in clusters, and it produces berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Due to habitat loss, Heteroblemma sibauense is considered endangered and is of particular interest to botanists and conservationists.
