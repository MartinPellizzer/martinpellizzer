Hieracium cirsiiforme

Hieracium cirsiiforme, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils. The plant features erect, hairy stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster, known as a corymb.

Hieracium cirsiiforme is characterized by its ability to spread via rhizomes and its tolerance to a variety of environmental conditions.
