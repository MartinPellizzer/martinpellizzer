Hypericum pulogense

Hypericum pulogense, commonly known as the Java St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the genus Hypericum, which includes over 400 species of shrubs and herbs. The plant is characterized by its yellow flowers, which are typically borne on stems with opposite leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
