Hibiscus zygomorphus

Hibiscus zygomorphus, commonly known as the red hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, or white. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Hibiscus zygomorphus is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
