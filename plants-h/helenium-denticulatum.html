Helenium denticulatum

Helenium denticulatum, commonly known as the toothed sunflower, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy inflorescences. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and its bright orange to yellow flowers bloom in late summer. Helenium denticulatum thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
