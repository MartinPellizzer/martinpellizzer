Heuchera americana var. subtruncata

Heuchera americana var. subtruncata, commonly known as the purple-leafed heuchera, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves that are typically purple or reddish in color, with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white or pale pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental foliage and moderate growth habit contribute to its appeal in both natural and cultivated landscapes.
