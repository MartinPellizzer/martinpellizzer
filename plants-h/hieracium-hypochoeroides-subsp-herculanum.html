Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp. herculanum

Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp.

herculanum, commonly known as the Herculanum hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

The plant features basal rosettes of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in late spring to early summer. It has a taproot system and is characterized by its robust growth habit.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
