Hieracium aquiliceps

Hieracium aquiliceps, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disk.

Hieracium aquiliceps is known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand cold temperatures.
