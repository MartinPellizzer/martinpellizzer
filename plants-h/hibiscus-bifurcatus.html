Hibiscus bifurcatus

Hibiscus bifurcatus, commonly known as the Mexican hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and red. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are often hairy. Hibiscus bifurcatus thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
