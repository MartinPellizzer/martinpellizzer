Hieracium bifidum subsp. sychnodon

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

sychnodon, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist meadows and grasslands, often forming dense mats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
