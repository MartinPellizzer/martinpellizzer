Hippion auriculatum

Hippion auriculatum, commonly known as the auriculate hippion, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive auriculate leaves, which have ear-like lobes at the base. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
