Hieracium lawsonii subsp. flocciramosum

Hieracium lawsonii subsp.

flocciramosum, commonly known as the woolly-stemmed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and flowering stems that bear clusters of small, yellowish flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its floccose (woolly) stems and leaves, which provide it with a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in open, rocky habitats at high elevations.

This plant is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
