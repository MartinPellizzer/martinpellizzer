Hieracium dipsacifrons

Hieracium dipsacifrons, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are rough and hairy, and the flowers are yellow with a distinctive, elongated shape.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can form dense colonies in suitable environments.
