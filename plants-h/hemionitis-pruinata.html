Hemionitis pruinata

Hemionitis pruinata, commonly known as the silver thread plant, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and silver-gray foliage due to the presence of fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to drought conditions.

Hemionitis pruinata is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native environment.
