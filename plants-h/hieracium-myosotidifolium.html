Hieracium myosotidifolium

Hieracium myosotidifolium, commonly known as the blueweed or blue false dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and its clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it is known for its ornamental value as well as its ecological role in supporting pollinators.

Due to its delicate appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
