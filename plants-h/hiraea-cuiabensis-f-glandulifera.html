Hiraea cuiabensis f. glandulifera

Hiraea cuiabensis f. glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular hiraea, is a unique variant of the hiraea plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive glandular structures on its leaves and stems, which contribute to its unique appearance and ecological role. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and diverse adaptations. Its glandular features may play a role in defense against herbivores or in chemical communication.

Due to its limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists studying plant morphology and adaptation.
