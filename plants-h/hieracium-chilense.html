Hieracium chilense

Hieracium chilense, commonly known as Chilean hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers range in color from white to yellow.

Hieracium chilense is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
