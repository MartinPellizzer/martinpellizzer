Hypericum lanuginosum subsp. millepunctatum

Hypericum lanuginosum subsp.

millepunctatum, commonly known as the downy St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, velvety appearance.

The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or open meadows.

This subspecies is notable for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
