Habenaria murtonii

Habenaria murtonii, commonly known as the Murton's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinct lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest habitats, often in mountainous regions. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils, while its leaves are broad and grass-like.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
