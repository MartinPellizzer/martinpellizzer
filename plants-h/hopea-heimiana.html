Hopea heimiana

Hopea heimiana, commonly known as the Burmese ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a broad, umbrella-shaped crown. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a leathery texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in spikes.

Hopea heimiana plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
