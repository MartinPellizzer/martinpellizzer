Hieracium subcanescens

Hieracium subcanescens, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are hairy and lance-shaped, while the stems are erect and often covered in fine pubescence.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators.
