Hieracium griseellum

Hieracium griseellum, commonly known as the grey-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring temperate climates. Its leaves are greyish-green and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

It is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and ability to spread easily.
