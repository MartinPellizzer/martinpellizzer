Helenium texanum

Helenium texanum, commonly known as the Texas sneezeweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. Its bright orange to red flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Helenium texanum is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
