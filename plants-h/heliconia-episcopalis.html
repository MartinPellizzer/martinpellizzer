Heliconia episcopalis

Heliconia episcopalis, commonly known as the bishop's hat heliconia, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its large, colorful bracts that resemble a crown or hat, hence its common name. The plant produces pendulous, fleshy flowers that are typically orange or red in color. Its leaves are broad, elongated, and often have a waxy texture.

Heliconia episcopalis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and ability to thrive in humid, tropical environments.
