Hieracium bifidum subsp. nicaeanum

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

nicaeanum, commonly known as the Nicaean hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Western Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rough, hairy texture, and the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is a herbaceous plant that produces numerous small seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
