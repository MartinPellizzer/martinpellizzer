Hydrochloa arundinacea

Hydrochloa arundinacea, commonly known as the reedgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like appearance with narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It is often found in wet or marshy environments, thriving in moist soils.
