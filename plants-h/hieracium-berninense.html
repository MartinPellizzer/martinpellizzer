Hieracium berninense

Hieracium berninense, commonly known as the Bernini's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, preferring temperate climates. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with serrated edges, and its flowers range in color from yellow to orange.

Hieracium berninense is often found in clusters, with the stems often reddish or purplish in color.
