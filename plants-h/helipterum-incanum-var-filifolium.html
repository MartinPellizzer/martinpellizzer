Helipterum incanum var. filifolium

Helipterum incanum var. filifolium, commonly known as the silky everlasting or silky helipterum, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its soft, silvery-gray foliage and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its foliage is covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its drought tolerance and prolonged flowering period.
