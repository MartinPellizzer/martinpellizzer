Herrickia horrida

Herrickia horrida, commonly known as the roughleaf thistle, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage. The plant features spiny, lobed leaves that are covered in fine, bristly hairs, giving it a rugged appearance. Its flowers are typically purple or pink, arranged in dense clusters, and bloom in the summer months.

Herrickia horrida is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and dry slopes.
