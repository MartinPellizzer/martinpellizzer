Hopea celtidifolia

Hopea celtidifolia, commonly known as the Philippine hopea, is a species of flowering plant in the family Dipterocarpaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the Philippines, Indonesia, and Malaysia. The plant is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, pale bark and elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically yellowish-green, and it produces woody fruits containing two seeds.

Hopea celtidifolia is valued for its timber and plays an important ecological role in its native habitats.
