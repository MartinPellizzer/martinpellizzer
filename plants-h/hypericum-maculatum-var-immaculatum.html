Hypericum maculatum var. immaculatum

Hypericum maculatum var. immaculatum, commonly known as the white-stemmed St. John's wort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of eastern North America.

It features upright, branching stems that are typically covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers with prominent orange stamens, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
