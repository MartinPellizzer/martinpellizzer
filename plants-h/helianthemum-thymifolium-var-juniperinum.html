Helianthemum thymifolium var. juniperinum

Helianthemum thymifolium var. juniperinum, commonly known as the Juniperleaf stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compact, mat-forming growth with fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered flowers. The leaves are typically blue-green in color, with a slightly woolly texture, and are arranged in a rosette pattern. It blooms with yellow flowers in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
