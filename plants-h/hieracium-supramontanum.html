Hieracium supramontanum

Hieracium supramontanum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are basal, rough, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and clustered in a head.

It is often found in open habitats such as meadows and slopes, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
