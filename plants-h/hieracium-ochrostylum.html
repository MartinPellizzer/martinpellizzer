Hieracium ochrostylum

Hieracium ochrostylum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Hieracium ochrostylum is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
