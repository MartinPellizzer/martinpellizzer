Hertia cheirifolia

Hertia cheirifolia, commonly known as the Cape rose or white daisies, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit with fleshy, succulent leaves. The plant produces showy clusters of white or pink flowers with a central yellow disc, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Hertia cheirifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
