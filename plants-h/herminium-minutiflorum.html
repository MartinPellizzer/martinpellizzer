Herminium minutiflorum

Herminium minutiflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered twayblade, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae).

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant produces small, greenish or brownish flowers that are often clustered in a spike, lacking showy petals. It has a slender, upright stem with a basal rosette of leaves.

The species is characterized by its mycorrhizal association, which is essential for its nutrient uptake and growth.
