Heliconia fluminensis

Heliconia fluminensis, commonly known as the Amazonian heliconia, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its large, colorful bracts that resemble flowers. The plant has a robust, upright stem with broad, sword-shaped leaves that are often green with a reddish tint. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by brightly colored bracts, which attract pollinators like birds and insects.

Heliconia fluminensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and plays a role in the ecosystems of its native habitat.
