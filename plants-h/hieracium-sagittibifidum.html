Hieracium sagittibifidum

Hieracium sagittibifidum, commonly known as the arrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a distinctive arrowhead shape, giving it its common name.

The showy yellow flowers appear in clusters and bloom from late spring to early summer.
