Helichrysum heterolasium

Helichrysum heterolasium, commonly known as the golden marguerite, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that resemble small suns. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces numerous small, clustered blooms throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance contribute to its widespread cultivation in horticulture.
