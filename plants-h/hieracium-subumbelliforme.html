Hieracium subumbelliforme

Hieracium subumbelliforme, commonly known as the subumbellate hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and a single, upright stem that bears a cluster of small, yellowish to reddish flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. It has hairy stems and leaves, and its flowers are usually arranged in a subumbellate (almost umbellate) pattern.

Hieracium subumbelliforme is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is often found in temperate regions.
