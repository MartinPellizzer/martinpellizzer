Hyptidendron canum

Hyptidendron canum, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, clustered flowers that range from yellow to orange-red in color. The plant produces legume pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories or disturbed soils.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical regions.
