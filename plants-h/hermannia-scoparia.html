Hermannia scoparia

Hermannia scoparia, commonly known as the rock hyssop, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form with small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tubular, violet or purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Hermannia scoparia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in gardens for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.
