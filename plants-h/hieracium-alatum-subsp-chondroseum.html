Hieracium alatum subsp. chondroseum

Hieracium alatum subsp.

chondroseum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
