Hieracium vulgatum subsp. chlorophylloides

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

chlorophylloides, commonly known as the greenleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 50 cm.

The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually green to bluish-green in color. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, with ray florets that are shorter than the central disc florets.

It is characterized by its dense foliage and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
