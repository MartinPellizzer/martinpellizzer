Heuchera minutiflora

Heuchera minutiflora, commonly known as the dwarf alumroot, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features dense, dark green foliage with a velvety texture, and its small, white flowers bloom in early spring. The plant typically reaches a height of only a few inches, making it ideal for rock gardens or as a ground cover. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, and is known for its ornamental value and ease of care.

Heuchera minutiflora is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
