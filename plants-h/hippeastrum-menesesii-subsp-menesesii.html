Hippeastrum menesesii subsp. menesesii

Hippeastrum menesesii subsp.

menesesii, commonly known as the Meneses' amaryllis, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil. It is a bulbous plant characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in late winter to early spring.

The plant features strap-like, lance-shaped leaves that are often a deep green color. Its flowers are usually red or purple with a white or yellow throat, and they are borne on sturdy, upright stems.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is cultivated for its vibrant blooms and ease of care.
