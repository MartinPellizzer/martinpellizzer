Hyptis lippioides

Hyptis lippioides, commonly known as Brazilian mint or green mint, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs such as mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, narrow leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Hyptis lippioides is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
