Homalocalyx grandiflorus

Homalocalyx grandiflorus, commonly known as the large-flowered homalocalyx, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or red in color. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible and often consumed by local wildlife. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant.
