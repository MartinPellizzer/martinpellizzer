Herbertia amabilis

Herbertia amabilis, commonly known as the pink herbertia, is a perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its blooms appear in late spring to early summer, adding a delicate touch to gardens.
