Hieracium rupicorsum

Hieracium rupicorsum, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant features basal leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and resemble those of dandelions. It produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal, aiding in its propagation.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.
