Helianthemum halimifolium var. multiflorum

Helianthemum halimifolium var. multiflorum, commonly known as the sweet everlasting or rock rose, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has aromatic, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its long-lasting flowers and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
