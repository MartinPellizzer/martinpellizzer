Hieracium bifidum subsp. amoenobium

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

amoenobium, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm.

The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy, with flowering stems that bear yellow composite flowers. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small yellow disk flowers surrounded by ray florets.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
