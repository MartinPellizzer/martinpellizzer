Hieracium lepidoptilum

Hieracium lepidoptilum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are typically arranged in a composite head made up of numerous small florets. The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are hairy and often have a reddish or purple tinge. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other open areas.

Hieracium lepidoptilum is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in many regions.
