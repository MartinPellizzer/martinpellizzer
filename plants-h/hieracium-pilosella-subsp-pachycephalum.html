Hieracium pilosella subsp. pachycephalum

Hieracium pilosella subsp.

pachycephalum, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.

The plant features a rosette of hairy, basal leaves and produces yellow flowers in a single, elongated inflorescence. Its flowers consist of ray florets and disc florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dense, woolly foliage and robust, upright growth habit.
