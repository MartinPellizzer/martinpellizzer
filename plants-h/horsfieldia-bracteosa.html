Horsfieldia bracteosa

Horsfieldia bracteosa, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking red petals and a distinctive, elongated floral structure. The plant typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments, relying on its aerial roots for moisture and nutrients. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and it produces small, dark brown seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Horsfieldia bracteosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
