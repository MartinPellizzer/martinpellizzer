Homalomena truncata

Homalomena truncata, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowhead, is a tropical evergreen perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, arrowhead-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white or pale yellow, which are followed by red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense foliage in suitable conditions.
