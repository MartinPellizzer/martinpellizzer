Haworthia venetia var. calva

Haworthia venetia var. calva, commonly known as the Venetia Haworthia, is a compact, succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features dense rosettes of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically pale green with prominent white or yellowish markings. The leaves are adorned with small, translucent windows called areolae, which allow light to penetrate for photosynthesis. It produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers on tall, slender stalks during the warmer months.

This plant is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low water requirements and ability to thrive in bright, indirect light.
