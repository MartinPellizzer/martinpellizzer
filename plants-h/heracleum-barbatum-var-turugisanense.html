Heracleum barbatum var. turugisanense

Heracleum barbatum var. turugisanense, commonly known as the Turugisan giant hogweed, is a tall, herbaceous plant native to parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbellate inflorescence and hollow, ridged stems. The plant features large, compound leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length, with a distinctive white, silky underside. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbrella-like clusters, which are followed by small, dry fruits.

This species is known for its rapid growth and can become invasive in certain environments.
