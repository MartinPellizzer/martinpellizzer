Hibiscus gregoryi

Hibiscus gregoryi, commonly known as the Gregory's hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and often branched.

Hibiscus gregoryi is a member of the Malvaceae family, which includes other hibiscus species and cotton.
