Hibiscus inimicus

Hibiscus inimicus, commonly known as the black hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, dark purple to black flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in humid, well-drained soil. Hibiscus inimicus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its flowers are typically short-lived, opening in the morning and closing by midday.
