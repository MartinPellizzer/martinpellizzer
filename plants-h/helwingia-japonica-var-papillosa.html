Helwingia japonica var. papillosa

Helwingia japonica var. papillosa, commonly known as the Japanese helwingia, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features delicate, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive hairy texture on the underside, giving it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring, followed by clusters of red, berry-like fruits that attract birds. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value lies in its foliage and unique fruiting display.
