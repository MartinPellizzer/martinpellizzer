Hormathophylla spathulata

Hormathophylla spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leafed orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest undergrowth, where it can thrive in partial to full shade. Its flowering period usually occurs during the late spring to early summer months.

The orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
