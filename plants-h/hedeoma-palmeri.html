Hedeoma palmeri

Hedeoma palmeri, commonly known as Palmer's sage, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, often covered in fine hairs that help reduce water loss.

Hedeoma palmeri is adapted to arid environments and is often found in dry, rocky soils.
