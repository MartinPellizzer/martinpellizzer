Hyptis eriocephala

Hyptis eriocephala, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and showy blue-violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
