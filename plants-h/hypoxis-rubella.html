Hypoxis rubella

Hypoxis rubella, commonly known as the golden cupplant, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive yellowish-green hue. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense colonies in its natural habitat.

Hypoxis rubella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and medicinal properties.
