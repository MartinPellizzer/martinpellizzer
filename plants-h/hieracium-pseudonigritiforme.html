Hieracium pseudonigritiforme

Hieracium pseudonigritiforme, commonly known as the black-leaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands, and it can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Hieracium pseudonigritiforme is known for its ornamental value but can also become invasive in certain regions.
