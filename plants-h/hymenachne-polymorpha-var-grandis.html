Hymenachne polymorpha var. grandis

Hymenachne polymorpha var. grandis, commonly known as the large-leaf hymenachne, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It typically grows in wetland and riparian habitats, often forming dense mats that help stabilize soil and prevent erosion. The plant has elongated, narrow leaves with a smooth surface and produces small, clustered inflorescences. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread aggressively, making it both a valuable wetland stabilizer and a potential invasive species in non-native environments.

Botanically, it is characterized by its fibrous root system and the presence of silica deposits in its cell walls, contributing to its structural strength.
