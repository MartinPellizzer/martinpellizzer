Haloragis depressa

Haloragis depressa, commonly known as the dwarf willow, is a low-growing perennial plant native to New Zealand.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky areas. The plant features small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. Its flowers are inconspicuous, with tiny yellow stamens and a single pistil.

Haloragis depressa plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates.
