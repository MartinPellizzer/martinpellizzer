Hieracium murorum subsp. sinusculatum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

sinusculatum, commonly known as the small-flowered hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its clustered, yellowish flowers and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and grasslands. It has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces numerous tiny seeds dispersed by wind.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
