Hieracium barbisquamum

Hieracium barbisquamum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are hairy and lance-shaped, while its flowers consist of both ray and disk florets.

It is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its aggressive growth habit.
