Hieracium marjokense

Hieracium marjokense, commonly known as the Marjokense hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained, rocky soils. It has a low-growing habit with basal leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are yellow, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in harsh, high-altitude environments.
