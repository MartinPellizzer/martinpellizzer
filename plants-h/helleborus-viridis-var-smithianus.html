Helleborus viridis var. smithianus

Helleborus viridis var. smithianus, commonly known as the green hellebore or Smith's green hellebore, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its dark green, waxy leaves that remain throughout the winter, and its pale green to white flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant is part of the Ranunculaceae family and is known for its toxic properties, containing various cardiac glycosides. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
