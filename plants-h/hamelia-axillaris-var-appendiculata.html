Hamelia axillaris var. appendiculata

Hamelia axillaris var. appendiculata, commonly known as the Mexican firebush, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its bright red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in warm climates.

Its compact growth habit and vibrant floral display make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable.
