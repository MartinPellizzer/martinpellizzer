Hippeastrum ramboi

Hippeastrum ramboi, commonly known as the Ramboi amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to South America, specifically Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, strap-like leaves and striking, showy flowers that bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant produces large, bulbous stems that store nutrients, supporting its growth during dormant periods. Its flowers are typically borne in clusters, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Hippeastrum ramboi is often cultivated for its beauty and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
