Hieracium brachiatum subsp. atrocladum

Hieracium brachiatum subsp.

atrocladum, commonly known as the black-stemmed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in moist meadows, pastures, and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters. It is characterized by its distinctive blackish stems and is part of the Asteraceae family.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
