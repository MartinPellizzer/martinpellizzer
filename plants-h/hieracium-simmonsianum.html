Hieracium simmonsianum

Hieracium simmonsianum, commonly known as the Simmons' hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the high alpine regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at elevations above 3,000 meters. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a hairy texture.

Due to its limited distribution and specialized habitat, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
