Hesperis oblongipetala

Hesperis oblongipetala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved scabious, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in meadows and open woodlands, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while the flowers range in color from white to pale blue.

Hesperis oblongipetala is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
