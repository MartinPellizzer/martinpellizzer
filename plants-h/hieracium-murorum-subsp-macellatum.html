Hieracium murorum subsp. macellatum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

macellatum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering heads that produce yellow-orange composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows. It has hairy stems and leaves, and its flowers are often visited by various pollinators.

This subspecies is widespread across Europe and parts of Asia, showing adaptability to different environmental conditions.
