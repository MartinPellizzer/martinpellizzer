Helianthemum origanifolium subsp. origanifolium

Helianthemum origanifolium subsp.

origanifolium, commonly known as the fragrant rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a low-growing shrub with compact, evergreen foliage that resembles oregano leaves, giving it its common name.

The plant produces small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its aromatic leaves and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and Mediterranean-style gardens.
