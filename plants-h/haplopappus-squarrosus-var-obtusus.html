Haplopappus squarrosus var. obtusus

Haplopappus squarrosus var. obtusus, commonly known as the smoothleaf horseweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in prairies and along roadsides. Its leaves are generally lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while the flowers are small and yellow.

This variant is distinguished by its more rounded leaf tips compared to other varieties of Haplopappus squarrosus.
