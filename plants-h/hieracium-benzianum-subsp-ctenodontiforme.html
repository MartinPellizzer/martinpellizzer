Hieracium benzianum subsp. ctenodontiforme

Hieracium benzianum subsp.

ctenodontiforme, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are hairy and have a distinctive lobed shape, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
