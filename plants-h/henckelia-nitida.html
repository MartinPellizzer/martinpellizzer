Henckelia nitida

Henckelia nitida, commonly known as the silverleaf heath, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, narrow leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white wax, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers a sunny to partially shaded environment.

Henckelia nitida is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate coastal conditions.
