Hieracium subargentinense var. rufocomatulum

Hieracium subargentinense var. rufocomatulum, commonly known as the red-hair hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems. The plant features basal and stem leaves that are typically lanceolate to ovate in shape, with rough, hairy surfaces. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in composite heads, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.
