Hyptis thyrsiflora

Hyptis thyrsiflora, commonly known as Brazilian hyptis, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves and spikes of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Hyptis thyrsiflora prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in warm climates.
