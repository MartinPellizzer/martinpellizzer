Hypericum nudiflorum var. ramosum

Hypericum nudiflorum var. ramosum, commonly known as the slender St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are opposite, simple, and typically have a slightly serrated margin. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

This variety is notable for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
