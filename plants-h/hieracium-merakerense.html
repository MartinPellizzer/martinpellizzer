Hieracium merakerense

Hieracium merakerense, commonly known as the Meraker hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and its flowering stems are usually unbranched.

Hieracium merakerense is known for its yellow flowers that bloom in summer, contributing to its ornamental value in certain horticultural settings.
