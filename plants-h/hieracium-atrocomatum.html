Hieracium atrocomatum

Hieracium atrocomatum, commonly known as the black-centred hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black central flower head, which contrasts with its yellow ray florets. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and damp woodlands. It has rough, hairy leaves that are usually lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Hieracium atrocomatum is often found in temperate regions of Europe and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.
