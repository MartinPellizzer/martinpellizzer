Hippocratea obtusifolia var. fischerana

Hippocratea obtusifolia var. fischerana, commonly known as the Fischer's hooded bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive hooded flowers, which are typically purple or violet in color. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, and it thrives in well-drained soils with adequate sunlight. It is often found in shrublands and along forest edges, where it contributes to local biodiversity.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
