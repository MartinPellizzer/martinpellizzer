Hieracium praecurrens subsp. lancifolium

Hieracium praecurrens subsp.

lancifolium, commonly known as the lance-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and leaves arranged in a basal rosette.

The plant features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is often found in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
