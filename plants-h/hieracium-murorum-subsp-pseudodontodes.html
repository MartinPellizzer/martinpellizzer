Hieracium murorum subsp. pseudodontodes

Hieracium murorum subsp.

pseudodontodes, commonly known as the mouse ear hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clustered heads, typical of the dandelion family. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
