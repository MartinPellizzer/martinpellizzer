Helicia conjunctiflora

Helicia conjunctiflora, commonly known as the clustered helicia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. Its flowers are small, clustered in inflorescences, and usually have five petals.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
