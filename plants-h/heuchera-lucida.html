Heuchera lucida

Heuchera lucida, commonly known as the silver leaf plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking silver-white foliage, which is often edged with red or purple, creating a dramatic visual effect. The plant produces upright spikes of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white during the summer months. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Heuchera lucida is widely cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
