Heliocereus elegantissimus subsp. helenae

Heliocereus elegantissimus subsp.

helenae, commonly known as the Helen's night blooming cereus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico. It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and the production of large, showy white flowers that open at night.

The flowers emit a sweet fragrance and are typically short-lived, lasting only a few hours. This plant is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

It is a popular choice for collectors due to its unique blooming behavior and ornamental value.
