Hepetis corcovadensis

Hepetis corcovadensis, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems that are covered in soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its scientific name reflects its historical use by indigenous peoples for medicinal purposes.

The plant is often found in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and wetlands.
