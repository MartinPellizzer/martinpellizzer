Hamosa saltonis

Hamosa saltonis, commonly known as the Salton Sea thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant produces spiny leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, often found near the Salton Sea in California.

Hamosa saltonis is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low water availability, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
