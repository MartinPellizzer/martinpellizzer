Hieracium praecurrens subsp. odorans

Hieracium praecurrens subsp.

odorans, commonly known as the sweet hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers.

The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and is known for its subtle, honey-like fragrance.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more pronounced scent compared to other hawkweed species.
