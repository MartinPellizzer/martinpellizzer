Hieracium sabbionicum

Hieracium sabbionicum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is a member of the daisy family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in harsh, cold environments. It has small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention.

Hieracium sabbionicum is often found in meadows and mountainous areas, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
