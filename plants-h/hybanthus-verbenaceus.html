Hybanthus verbenaceus

Hybanthus verbenaceus, commonly known as the velvetleaf or yellow-flowered hybanthus, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Verbenaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of the Americas and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

The plant's ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for cultivation in warm climates.
