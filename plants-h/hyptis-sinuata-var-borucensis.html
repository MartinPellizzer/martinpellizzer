Hyptis sinuata var. borucensis

Hyptis sinuata var. borucensis, commonly known as the Boruca hyptis, is a perennial shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
