Hymenophyllum sibthorpioides

Hymenophyllum sibthorpioides, commonly known as the Sibthorp's haircap, is a small, epiphytic fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, moss-like appearance with finely divided, hair-like leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in length. The plant thrives in cool, moist, and shaded environments, often found on tree trunks or rocks in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the leaves and are protected by a thin, membranous covering.

Hymenophyllum sibthorpioides is known for its intricate leaf structure and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
