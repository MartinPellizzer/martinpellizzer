Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp. rudolphopolitanum

Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp.

rudolphopolitanum, commonly known as the Rudolphopolitan hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as meadows and woodland edges.

Its subspecific classification highlights its distinct morphological and genetic traits compared to other members of the Hieracium genus.
