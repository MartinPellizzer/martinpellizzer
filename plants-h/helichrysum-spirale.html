Helichrysum spirale

Helichrysum spirale, commonly known as the everlasting sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that remain intact for a long time after blooming, giving it the common name. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its aromatic, hairy leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its resilience and ornamental value make it a favored plant in horticulture.
