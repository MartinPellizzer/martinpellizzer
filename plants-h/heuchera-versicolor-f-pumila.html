Heuchera versicolor f. pumila

Heuchera versicolor f. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf coral bells, is a compact, low-growing perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features striking, mounding foliage that ranges from deep purples to greens and reds, depending on light exposure and temperature. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and lavender during the summer months. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Its compact size and vibrant foliage make it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.
