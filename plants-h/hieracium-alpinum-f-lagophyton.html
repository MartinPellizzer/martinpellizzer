Hieracium alpinum f. lagophyton

Hieracium alpinum f. lagophyton, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant features upright, hairy stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped with serrated edges. Its flowers are typically yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and bloom in late summer.

It is adapted to grow in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
