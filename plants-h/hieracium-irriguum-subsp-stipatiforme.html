Hieracium irriguum subsp. stipatiforme

Hieracium irriguum subsp.

stipatiforme, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, grassy habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It features slender, upright stems with narrowly lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy.

Its flowers are yellow, with ray florets and a central disk, and it produces small, oval-shaped achenes as its fruit.
