Hedysarum leiocarpum

Hedysarum leiocarpum, commonly known as the white vetch, is a perennial legume native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces slender, erect stems and is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. It has a fibrous root system and is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

Hedysarum leiocarpum is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it a valuable plant in ecological restoration efforts.
