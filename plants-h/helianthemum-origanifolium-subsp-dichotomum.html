Helianthemum origanifolium subsp. dichotomum

Helianthemum origanifolium subsp.

dichotomum, commonly known as the dichotomous rockrose, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It features small, evergreen leaves that are often divided into two parts, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to full sun.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and drought tolerance.
