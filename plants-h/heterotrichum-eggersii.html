Heterotrichum eggersii

Heterotrichum eggersii, commonly known as the Eggers' clubmoss, is a small, evergreen plant belonging to the family Heterotrichaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, moss-like appearance with closely packed, scale-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. It reproduces through spores, which are released from specialized structures called sporangia.

Heterotrichum eggersii is often found in temperate regions and plays a role in soil stabilization and habitat provision for small organisms.
