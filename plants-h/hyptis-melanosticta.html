Hyptis melanosticta

Hyptis melanosticta, commonly known as the black-stained hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the Americas, particularly found in regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to violet. Hyptis melanosticta is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and along stream banks.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
