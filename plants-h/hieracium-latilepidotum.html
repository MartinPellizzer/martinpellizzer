Hieracium latilepidotum

Hieracium latilepidotum, commonly known as the slender-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Hieracium latilepidotum produces small yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators.
