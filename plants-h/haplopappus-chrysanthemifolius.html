Haplopappus chrysanthemifolius

Haplopappus chrysanthemifolius, commonly known as the goldenleaf pappus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, silvery-green leaves that resemble those of chrysanthemums, giving it its common name. Its small yellow flowers are typically arranged in a dense cluster at the tip of the stem.

The plant is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
