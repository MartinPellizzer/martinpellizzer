Helichrysum amplexicaule

Helichrysum amplexicaule, commonly known as the clasping helichrysum, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its golden-yellow inflorescences that resemble small suns. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that often wrap around the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Helichrysum amplexicaule is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
