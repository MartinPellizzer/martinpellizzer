Hevea pauciflora var. typica

Hevea pauciflora var. typica, commonly known as the small-leaf rubber tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green leaves that are typically small and elliptical in shape. The plant produces latex, which is harvested from its bark and used in the production of natural rubber. It thrives in humid, tropical climates with well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its economic value in rubber production.
