Helianthus diffusus var. missouriensis

Helianthus diffusus var. missouriensis, commonly known as the Missouri sunflower, is a native perennial plant of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its yellow ray florets and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. This species typically grows in moist to wet soils in prairies and along stream banks. It produces a single, upright stem with a crown of leaves near the base.

The Missouri sunflower is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
