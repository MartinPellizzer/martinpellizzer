Hymenophyllum filmenofilicum

Hymenophyllum filmenofilicum, commonly known as the Filmy Hymenophyllum, is a small, epiphytic fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent fronds that are often covered in a fine, waxy layer, which helps reduce water loss in its moist, shaded habitats. This species typically grows on tree trunks or rocks in tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in high humidity and indirect light. Its spores are produced in small, clustered structures called sori, which are located on the underside of the fronds.

The plant's unique morphology and habitat preferences make it an interesting subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
