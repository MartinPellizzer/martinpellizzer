Hieracium brachiatum subsp. flavum

Hieracium brachiatum subsp.

flavum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are bright yellow with a central disc of smaller florets.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can spread aggressively through rhizomes.
