Hesperocyparis lindleyi

Hesperocyparis lindleyi, commonly known as the Mexican incense cedar, is a species of conifer native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, evergreen foliage and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils. The plant produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. Its leaves are needle-like and arranged in spiral patterns along the branches.

Hesperocyparis lindleyi is valued for its aromatic wood and is often used in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant.
