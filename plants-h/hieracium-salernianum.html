Hieracium salernianum

Hieracium salernianum, commonly known as the Salernian hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, often found in meadows and woodland edges. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Hieracium salernianum is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soils and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.
