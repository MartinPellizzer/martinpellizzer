Hieracium macilentum subsp. crepidodon

Hieracium macilentum subsp.

crepidodon, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in grassy habitats, such as meadows and woodland edges, and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while the plant produces numerous flowering stems that bear numerous florets.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads, and is often found in disturbed soils or open areas.
