Hibiscus taiwanensis

Hibiscus taiwanensis, commonly known as the Taiwan hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in summer. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces bright red or pink flowers with a prominent central pistil. Hibiscus taiwanensis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
