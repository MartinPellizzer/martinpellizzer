Hieraciodes viscidula

Hieraciodes viscidula, commonly known as sticky hawksbeard, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant features hairy, rough stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name. Its bright yellow ray florets are surrounded by a dense cluster of disk florets.

Hieraciodes viscidula typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and thrives in full sun.
