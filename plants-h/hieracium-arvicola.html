Hieracium arvicola

Hieracium arvicola, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. It features basal rosettes of dark green, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in late summer.

Hieracium arvicola is often found in high-altitude meadows and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
