Hedyosmos secundus

Hedyosmos secundus, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow inflorescences that resemble flames, which appear in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, red-brown fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Hedyosmos secundus is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
