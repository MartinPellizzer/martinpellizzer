Hemionitis polypodioides

Hemionitis polypodioides, commonly known as the Chinese water fern, is a small, delicate fern native to Asia.

It has a unique morphology characterized by its finely divided, feather-like fronds that resemble those of a water plant. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found near water bodies in its natural habitat. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of many fern species.

Hemionitis polypodioides is also notable for its ability to survive in aquatic or semi-aquatic conditions, making it an interesting subject for botanical study.
