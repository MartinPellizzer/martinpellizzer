Hamelia scabrida

Hamelia scabrida, commonly known as rough flamebush, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has rough, scaly bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

Hamelia scabrida is a valuable plant for attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
