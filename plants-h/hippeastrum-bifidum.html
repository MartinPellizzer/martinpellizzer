Hippeastrum bifidum

Hippeastrum bifidum, commonly known as the two-lobed amaryllis, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a bulbous plant that produces striking, showy flowers with large, trumpet-shaped petals. The flowers are typically pink or red and bloom in the summer months. The plant has strap-like leaves that are broad and grass-like, emerging from the base of the bulb.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
