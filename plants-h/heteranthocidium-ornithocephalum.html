Heteranthocidium ornithocephalum

Heteranthocidium ornithocephalum, commonly known as the bird-head orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure. The plant features showy, brightly colored flowers with a distinctive bird-like appearance, particularly in the labellum. It typically grows in humid, shaded environments and requires specific conditions to thrive.

Its botanical significance lies in its ornamental value and role in ecological systems.
