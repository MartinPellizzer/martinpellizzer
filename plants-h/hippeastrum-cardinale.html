Hippeastrum cardinale

Hippeastrum cardinale, commonly known as the Cardinal amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically deep red or orange, often with a contrasting white or yellow throat. The plant produces large, strap-like leaves that are broad and glossy, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and thrives in well-drained soil with ample sunlight.

Hippeastrum cardinale is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms and is relatively easy to grow in warm climates.
