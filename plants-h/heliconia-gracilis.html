Heliconia gracilis

Heliconia gracilis, commonly known as the slender heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble petals. The plant has long, narrow leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers inside its inflorescence. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Heliconia gracilis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
