Hymenocyclus monticola

Hymenocyclus monticola, commonly known as the mountain cycad, is a unique cycad species native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched stem and a crown of feathery, pinnate leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pollinated by insects, and it relies on specific bird species for seed dispersal. Hymenocyclus monticola is adapted to nutrient-poor soils and exhibits a slow growth rate, making it a long-lived plant.
