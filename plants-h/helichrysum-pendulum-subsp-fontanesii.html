Helichrysum pendulum subsp. fontanesii

Helichrysum pendulum subsp.

fontanesii, commonly known as the hanging goldball, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its dense, yellow-orange flower heads that resemble small balls, which are borne on long, slender stems.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

The species is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
