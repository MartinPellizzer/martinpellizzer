Hemicyatheon deplanchei

Hemicyatheon deplanchei, commonly known as the African dandelion, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a rosette of fleshy, waxy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by distinctive seed heads resembling those of a dandelion. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Hemicyatheon deplanchei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
