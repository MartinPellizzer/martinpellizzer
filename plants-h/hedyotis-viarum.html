Hedyotis viarum

Hedyotis viarum, commonly known as the narrowleaf bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to temperate regions of Asia and Europe.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and have a slightly hairy surface.

Hedyotis viarum is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodland edges and stream banks.
