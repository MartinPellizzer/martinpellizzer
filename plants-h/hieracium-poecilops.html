Hieracium poecilops

Hieracium poecilops, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Hieracium poecilops is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in various habitats.
