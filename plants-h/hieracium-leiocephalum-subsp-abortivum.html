Hieracium leiocephalum subsp. abortivum

Hieracium leiocephalum subsp.

abortivum, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring moist to moderately dry conditions. Its leaves are rough and hairy, with a distinctive basal rosette structure, and it produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
