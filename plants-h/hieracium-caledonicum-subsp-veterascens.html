Hieracium caledonicum subsp. veterascens

Hieracium caledonicum subsp.

veterascens, commonly known as the Caledonian hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the Scottish Highlands. It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow flowers arranged in a single stem.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It has a hairy stem and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges.

This subspecies is part of the dandelion family (Asteraceae) and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
