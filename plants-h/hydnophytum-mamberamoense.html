Hydnophytum mamberamoense

Hydnophytum mamberamoense, commonly known as the Mamberamo mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees in tropical rainforests.

It possesses specialized structures called haustoria that allow it to penetrate the host's vascular system and absorb water and nutrients. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruits are typically berries that contain seeds dispersed by animals.

Due to its parasitic nature and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant component of its ecosystem.
