Hura crepitans f. ovata

Hura crepitans f. ovata, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its large, showy orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant is native to Central and South America and is known for its rapid growth and distinctive, palmate leaves. Its fruit is a spiny capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its dispersal. The tree can reach significant heights, making it a prominent feature in its natural habitat.

Botanically, it is notable for its unique floral structure and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
