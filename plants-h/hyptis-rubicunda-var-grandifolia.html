Hyptis rubicunda var. grandifolia

Hyptis rubicunda var. grandifolia, commonly known as red hyptis or Mexican hyptis, is a species of flowering plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to Central America and northern South America, often found in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a velvety texture.

Hyptis rubicunda var. grandifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
