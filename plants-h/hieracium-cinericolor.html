Hieracium cinericolor

Hieracium cinericolor, commonly known as the ash-gray hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its grayish-green foliage and yellowish-orange composite flowers. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant produces achenes as its seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural practices.
