Heuchera pubescens var. aceroides

Heuchera pubescens var. aceroides, commonly known as the velvetleaf alumroot, is a striking ornamental plant native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, velvety foliage that ranges in color from deep purples to rich reds and greens, depending on the variety and environmental conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or red during the spring. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its foliage remains attractive throughout the growing season, offering year-round visual interest.
