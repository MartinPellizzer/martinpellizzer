Heuchera parvifolia var. dissecta

Heuchera parvifolia var. dissecta, commonly known as the dissected alumroot, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, deeply lobed leaves that are typically green with red or purple veins, providing ornamental value in gardens. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or pale green flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its foliage remains attractive throughout the growing season, contributing to its popularity among horticulturists.
