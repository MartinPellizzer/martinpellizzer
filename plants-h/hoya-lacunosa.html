Hoya lacunosa

Hoya lacunosa, commonly known as the "Honeyplant" or "Porcelain Plant," is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its waxy, thick leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white in color. Hoya lacunosa is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental appeal.

Its unique foliage and delicate blooms make it a favorite among plant enthusiasts.
