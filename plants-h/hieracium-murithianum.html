Hieracium murithianum

Hieracium murithianum, commonly known as the Murithian hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and elongated, hairy stems that bear clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. It is native to Europe and has been introduced to other regions, including parts of North America.

Due to its aggressive growth habit, it can become invasive in certain environments.
