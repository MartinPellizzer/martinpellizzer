Hordeum vulgare var. patimatae

Hordeum vulgare var. patimatae, commonly known as the Patimatae barley, is a variant of barley characterized by its unique botanical traits.

It is an annual grass that typically grows in temperate regions and has slender, cylindrical spikelets. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers, which are arranged in dense clusters along the stem. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture, and it has a fibrous root system.

This variety is often studied for its adaptability to different environmental conditions and its potential agricultural uses.
