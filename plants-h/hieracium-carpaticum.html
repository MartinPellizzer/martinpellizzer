Hieracium carpaticum

Hieracium carpaticum, commonly known as the Carpathian hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy areas and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. Its yellow flowers bloom in summer, and it produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Hieracium carpaticum is often found in alpine and subalpine regions, where it thrives in cooler, moist environments.
