Helichrysum selaginoides

Helichrysum selaginoides, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial that produces showy, golden-yellow flowers that remain intact for a long period, giving it its common name. The plant has aromatic, hairy leaves and is often used in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its resilience and ornamental value make it a popular choice among gardeners and botanists alike.
