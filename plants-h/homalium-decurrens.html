Homalium decurrens

Homalium decurrens, commonly known as the creeping huito, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its elongated, often pendulous leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody, fleshy fruits that are edible. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and unique fruit structure.
