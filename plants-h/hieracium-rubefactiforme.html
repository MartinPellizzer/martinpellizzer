Hieracium rubefactiforme

Hieracium rubefactiforme, commonly known as the red-stemmed hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are basal, rough, and hairy, while its stems are reddish and often branched.

The showy yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
