Hydnophytum wilsonii

Hydnophytum wilsonii, commonly known as the sticky vine or Wilson's hydnophytum, is a climbing epiphytic plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique ability to secrete a sticky substance from its leaves, which helps in capturing small insects. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces small, white or yellowish flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees in the understory of tropical forests.

Due to its specialized adaptations, it plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat.
