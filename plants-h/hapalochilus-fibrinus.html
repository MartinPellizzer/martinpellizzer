Hapalochilus fibrinus

Hapalochilus fibrinus, commonly known as the hairy leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of broad, fleshy, hairy leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces tall, slender flowering stems that bear small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Hapalochilus fibrinus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive foliage.
