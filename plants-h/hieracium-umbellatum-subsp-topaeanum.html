Hieracium umbellatum subsp. topaeanum

Hieracium umbellatum subsp.

topaeanum, commonly known as the Topaeum hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowering period occurs from late spring to early summer.

It is often found in open habitats with moderate to high light exposure.
