Hamamelis virginiana var. henryae

Hamamelis virginiana var. henryae, commonly known as the Chinese witch hazel, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking orange-yellow flowers that bloom in late autumn, providing color when most plants are dormant. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that turn reddish in the fall. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
