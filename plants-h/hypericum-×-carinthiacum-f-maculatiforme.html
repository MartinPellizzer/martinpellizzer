Hypericum × carinthiacum f. maculatiforme

Hypericum × carinthiacum f. maculatiforme, commonly known as the spotted form of the Carinthian St. John's wort, is a compact shrub with bright yellow, tubular flowers.

It is characterized by its distinctive spotted leaves, which give it its cultivar name. The plant is native to Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This cultivar is valued for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators.
