Hymenocallis guianensis var. princeps

Hymenocallis guianensis var. princeps, commonly known as the Guianan spider lily, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy white flowers with a prominent yellow throat. The plant produces strap-shaped, grass-like leaves that are often mistaken for those of grasses or sedges. Its bulbs are underground and store nutrients, supporting its growth in moist, shaded environments.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
