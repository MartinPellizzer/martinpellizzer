Habsburgia comans

Habsburgia comans, commonly known as the Malabar nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth margin. Its flowers are small, usually purple or violet, and are clustered in umbels.

The fruit is a fleshy berry that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.
