Haplopappus cuneatus var. spathulatus

Haplopappus cuneatus var. spathulatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf horseweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its linear, grass-like leaves and small, yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and ragweeds, and is known for its wind-dispersed seeds.
