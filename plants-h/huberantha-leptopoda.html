Huberantha leptopoda

Huberantha leptopoda, commonly known as the slender-leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, strap-like leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, where it can receive adequate light without direct exposure. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the surrounding soil.

The orchid's unique floral structure and growth habits make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
