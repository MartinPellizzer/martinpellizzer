Hydrangea pubinervis

Hydrangea pubinervis, commonly known as the silky hydrangea, is a species native to East Asia, particularly China.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from white to pink or blue, depending on soil pH. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a silvery underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Hydrangea pubinervis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
