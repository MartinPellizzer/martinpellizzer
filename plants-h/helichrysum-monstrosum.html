Helichrysum monstrosum

Helichrysum monstrosum, commonly known as the giant everlasting or yellow everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, yellow-orange inflorescences that resemble small, clustered sunflowers. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is known for its long-lasting flowers, which retain their color even when dried. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Helichrysum monstrosum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
