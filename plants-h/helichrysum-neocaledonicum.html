Helichrysum neocaledonicum

Helichrysum neocaledonicum, commonly known as the New Caledonia everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the island of New Caledonia in the South Pacific.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, yellow-orange inflorescences that resemble small, fluffy balls. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often in open habitats such as dunes and forest edges. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, while its flowers are tightly clustered and retain their color for an extended period after blooming.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated in horticulture for its long-lasting floral displays.
