Helichrysum bilobum subsp. scabrum

Helichrysum bilobum subsp.

scabrum, commonly known as the rough everlasting or sand everlasting, is a shrub-like plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its yellowish-green leaves and small, yellow to orange flowers that are often used in floral arrangements.

The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping. Its leaves are covered in fine, rough hairs, giving it a distinctive texture.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
