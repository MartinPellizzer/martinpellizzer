Hibiscus physodes

Hibiscus physodes, commonly known as the velvetleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves that are typically heart-shaped or ovate in form. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink or red, depending on the species. Hibiscus physodes thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

It is a member of the Malvaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like cotton and okra.
