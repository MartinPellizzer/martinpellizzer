Hardenbergia violacea f. alba

Hardenbergia violacea f. alba, commonly known as the white clover tree, is a variant of the species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive white to pale purple flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a striking visual appeal. The plant features trifoliate leaves, typical of the Fabaceae family, and has a woody structure that supports its upright growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and arid environments.

This ornamental plant is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing abilities, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
