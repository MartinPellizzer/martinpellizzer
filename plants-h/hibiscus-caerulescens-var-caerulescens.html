Hibiscus caerulescens var. caerulescens

Hibiscus caerulescens var. caerulescens, commonly known as the blue hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers, which are typically five-petaled and have a prominent central stamen. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Hibiscus caerulescens var. caerulescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
