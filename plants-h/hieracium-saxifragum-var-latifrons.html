Hieracium saxifragum var. latifrons

Hieracium saxifragum var. latifrons, commonly known as the broad-fronded hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate, often hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in well-drained, sunny environments. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a rough texture, and its flowers are yellow, forming dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

It is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
