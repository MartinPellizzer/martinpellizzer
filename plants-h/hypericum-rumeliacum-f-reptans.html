Hypericum rumeliacum f. reptans

Hypericum rumeliacum f. reptans, commonly known as the creeping St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to Europe.

It features slender, prostrate stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread across the ground. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
