Humboltia minutalis

Humboltia minutalis, commonly known as the dwarf hummingbird flower, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that resemble miniature hummingbird wings, typically in shades of pink and white. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid forests.

Humboltia minutalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.
