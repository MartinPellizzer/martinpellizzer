Helenium longifolium

Helenium longifolium, commonly known as the common ironweed, is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

It is native to North America and thrives in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and along stream banks. The plant features tall, stiff stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, supporting its showy, bright orange to red flower heads. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

Helenium longifolium is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
