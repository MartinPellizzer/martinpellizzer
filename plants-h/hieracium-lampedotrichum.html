Hieracium lampedotrichum

Hieracium lampedotrichum, commonly known as the Lampedot hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the daisy family (Asteraceae).

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces yellow-orange composite flowers. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in rocky and dry habitats. The plant is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native range.
