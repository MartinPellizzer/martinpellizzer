Hydrangea serrata f. rosalba

Hydrangea serrata f. rosalba, commonly known as the Rosalba hydrangea, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy, cone-shaped flower clusters that range in color from white to pink, depending on soil pH. The plant features narrow, serrated leaves that are typically dark green during the growing season. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its compact size and elegant blooms make it a desirable addition to both container and in-ground plantings.
