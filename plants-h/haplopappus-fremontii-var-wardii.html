Haplopappus fremontii var. wardii

Haplopappus fremontii var. wardii, commonly known as the Ward's hairy fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer. Its hairy leaves and stems are adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in disturbed or open areas.
