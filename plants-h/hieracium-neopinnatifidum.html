Hieracium neopinnatifidum

Hieracium neopinnatifidum, commonly known as the narrow-lobed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped with finely toothed margins, and the flowers are yellow, often arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

It is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in various habitats.
