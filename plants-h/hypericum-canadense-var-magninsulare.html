Hypericum canadense var. magninsulare

Hypericum canadense var. magninsulare, commonly known as the Great Lakes goldenstrob, is a perennial shrub native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, red-orange berries that are edible but not particularly palatable. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local wildlife.
