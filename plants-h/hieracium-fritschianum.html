Hieracium fritschianum

Hieracium fritschianum, commonly known as the Fritsch's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has basal rosettes of lanceolate leaves and produces yellow flowers in early summer.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
