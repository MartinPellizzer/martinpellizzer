Haworthia pilifera

Haworthia pilifera, commonly known as the needle leaf Haworthia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features dense, fleshy rosettes of dark green, needle-like leaves that are covered in a white, powdery coating called farina. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers on slender stems during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for rock gardens or indoor cultivation.

Its compact size and unique appearance make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
