Hyacinthus cernuus

Hyacinthus cernuus, commonly known as the drooping hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that hang in clusters, typically in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces underground bulbs that store nutrients for future growth. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

The drooping hyacinth is often cultivated for its ornamental value and pleasant fragrance.
