Helichrysum glumaceum

Helichrysum glumaceum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and bright yellow to orange flowers. The plant produces small, bract-based flower heads that resemble tiny globes, giving it a unique appearance. Its leaves are usually narrow and gray-green, with a slightly hairy texture.

Helichrysum glumaceum is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its long-lasting flowers and drought tolerance.
