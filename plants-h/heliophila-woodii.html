Heliophila woodii

Heliophila woodii, commonly known as the Wood's sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and linear, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Heliophila woodii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
