Hieracium microdon

Hieracium microdon, commonly known as the small devil's-bit scabious, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soil. Hieracium microdon is known for its ornamental value and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
