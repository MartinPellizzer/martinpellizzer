Hydrangea anomala var. ovalifolia

Hydrangea anomala var. ovalifolia, commonly known as the ovalleaf hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from white to pink and blue, depending on soil pH. The plant produces oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and have a rough texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
