Heliotropium elongatum var. genuinum

Heliotropium elongatum var. genuinum, commonly known as the slender heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant produces small, round berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and open fields.

Its leaves are typically opposite, elliptic in shape, and have a slightly hairy texture.
