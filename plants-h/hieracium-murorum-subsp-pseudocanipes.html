Hieracium murorum subsp. pseudocanipes

Hieracium murorum subsp.

pseudocanipes, commonly known as the false spearhead hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and roadside areas. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and its yellow flowers bloom in the summer months.

This subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different leaf morphology compared to other members of the Hieracium genus.
