Helianthemum virgatum f. bicolor

Helianthemum virgatum f. bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub with dense, compact foliage and small, showy flowers. The plant's most distinctive feature is its bicolor flowers, which typically display a combination of yellow and pink or white. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
