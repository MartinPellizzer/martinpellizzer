Hippeastrum equestre

Hippeastrum equestre, commonly known as the horseman's amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can range in color from white to deep red, often with a purple or pink hue at the base. The plant produces strap-like leaves that are broad and pointed, growing from a bulbous underground stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Hippeastrum equestre is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be grown as a houseplant or in gardens.
