Hieracium hoppeanum subsp. grundlii

Hieracium hoppeanum subsp.

grundlii, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow flowers arranged in a composite head.

The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats at high elevations. It has small, hairy leaves and produces numerous seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
