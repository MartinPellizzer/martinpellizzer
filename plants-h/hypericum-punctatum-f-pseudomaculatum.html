Hypericum punctatum f. pseudomaculatum

Hypericum punctatum f. pseudomaculatum, commonly known as the spotted St. John's wort, is a variant of the St. John's wort genus native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing small, four-petaled flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

The foliage and flowers contain bioactive compounds, making it of interest in herbal medicine and ecological studies.
