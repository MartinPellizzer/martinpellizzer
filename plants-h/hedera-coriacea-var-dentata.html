Hedera coriacea var. dentata

Hedera coriacea var. dentata, commonly known as the serrated ivy, is a variant of the common ivy species.

It is characterized by its dark green, waxy leaves with serrated edges, which distinguish it from other ivy varieties. This plant is a climbing vine native to parts of Europe and Asia, often found in woodland areas. It thrives in shaded environments and has a preference for moist, well-drained soil.

The leaves are typically glossy and can grow up to 10 centimeters in length, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
