Hieracium angustatum subsp. nitens

Hieracium angustatum subsp.

nitens, commonly known as the narrowleaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a hairy surface, and it produces yellow flowers in clusters.

It is often found in open habitats and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.
