Helianthemum racemosum var. syriacum

Helianthemum racemosum var. syriacum, commonly known as the Syrian rock rose, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with hairy stems. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its ornamental value and hardiness make it a popular choice among gardeners in arid and semi-arid climates.
