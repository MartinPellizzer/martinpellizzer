Hamelia sanguinea

Hamelia sanguinea, commonly known as the scarlet flamebush, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet, featuring a compact, upright form. The plant is characterized by its bright red, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated edge, while its bark is smooth and reddish-brown.

Hamelia sanguinea is well adapted to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
