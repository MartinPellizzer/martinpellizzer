Haworthia isabellae

Haworthia isabellae, commonly known as the Isabelline Haworthia, is a small, succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features compact, fleshy leaves arranged in a rosette pattern, often with a bluish-gray hue and white or pink markings. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers on tall, slender stems during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for rock gardens or indoor cultivation.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
