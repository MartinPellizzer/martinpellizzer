Hyptis paranensis

Hyptis paranensis, commonly known as Brazilian hyptis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its grayish-green foliage and clusters of small, pale blue or violet flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Hyptis paranensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
