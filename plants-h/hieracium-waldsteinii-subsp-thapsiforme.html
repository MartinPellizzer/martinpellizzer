Hieracium waldsteinii subsp. thapsiforme

Hieracium waldsteinii subsp.

thapsiforme, commonly known as the Waldstein's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to orange composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with fine hairs, while its stems are usually hairy as well.

This subspecies is distinguished by its slightly elongated flower heads and is often found in association with other members of the Hieracium genus.
