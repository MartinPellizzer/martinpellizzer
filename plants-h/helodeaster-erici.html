Helodeaster erici

Helodeaster erici, commonly known as the Ericaceous helodeaster, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its evergreen shrub form, with small, leathery leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers in the spring, which are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and rocky areas.

Helodeaster erici is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
