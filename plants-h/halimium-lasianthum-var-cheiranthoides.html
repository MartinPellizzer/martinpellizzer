Halimium lasianthum var. cheiranthoides

Halimium lasianthum var. cheiranthoides, commonly known as the cheiranthoid halimium, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is a low-growing shrub with woody stems and small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers with four petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
