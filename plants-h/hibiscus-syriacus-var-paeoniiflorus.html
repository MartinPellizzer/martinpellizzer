Hibiscus syriacus var. paeoniiflorus

Hibiscus syriacus var. paeoniiflorus, commonly known as the peonyflower shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range from white to deep pink, resembling peonies. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves and a compact growth habit, making it suitable for small gardens or containers. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is relatively low maintenance.

Hibiscus syriacus var. paeoniiflorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
