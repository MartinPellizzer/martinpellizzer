Hieracium pallescens subsp. muroriforme

Hieracium pallescens subsp.

muroriforme, commonly known as the pale hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are lanceolate and hairy, while its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

It is often considered a weed due to its rapid spread and ability to colonize various soil types.
