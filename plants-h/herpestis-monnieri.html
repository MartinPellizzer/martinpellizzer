Herpestis monnieri

Herpestis monnieri, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with slender, branching stems that root at the nodes. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Herpestis monnieri is often found in rocky or gravelly areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
