Hydrangea quinquefolia

Hydrangea quinquefolia, commonly known as the oakleaf hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, depending on soil pH. The plant's leaves are distinctive, with a serrated edge and a lobed shape that resembles the leaves of an oak tree. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Hydrangea quinquefolia is also valued for its ability to provide seasonal interest with its vibrant fall foliage.
