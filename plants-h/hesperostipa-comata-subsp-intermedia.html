Hesperostipa comata subsp. intermedia

Hesperostipa comata subsp.

intermedia, commonly known as the silky hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America. It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and long, silky, silver-green leaves that give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including grasslands and disturbed soils, and is often found in temperate regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
