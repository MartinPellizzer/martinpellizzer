Hakea arida

Hakea arida, commonly known as the slender hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, white bloom. The plant produces cylindrical inflorescences that bear small, pink or red flowers, which are typically visited by nectar-feeding birds and insects. Hakea arida is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important component of its native habitat.
