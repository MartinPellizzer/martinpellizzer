Heloniopsis japonica var. sanguinea

Heloniopsis japonica var. sanguinea, commonly known as Japanese bellflower, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
