Hieracium vaginatum

Hieracium vaginatum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features bright yellow flowers with a central disk surrounded by ray florets, and it typically grows in grassy areas and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while the stems are upright and branching.

Hieracium vaginatum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in meadows and along roadsides.
