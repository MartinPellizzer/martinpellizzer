Heliconia andrewsii

Heliconia andrewsii, commonly known as the red heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble flowers. The plant produces inflorescences with bright red, tubular structures that serve as nectar sources for pollinators like hummingbirds and bats. Its leaves are large, elongated, and often have a waxy texture, adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Heliconia andrewsii is also valued for its ornamental use in landscaping and as a component in tropical gardens.
