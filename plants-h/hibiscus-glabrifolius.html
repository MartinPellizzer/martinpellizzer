Hibiscus glabrifolius

Hibiscus glabrifolius, commonly known as the smoothleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, smooth-textured leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in wetland areas and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by water or wind.

Hibiscus glabrifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
