Hordeum crinitum

Hordeum crinitum, commonly known as silky barley, is a species of grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, silky hairs covering its leaves and inflorescence. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of pale green to yellowish flowers, which are typical of the Poaceae family. Hordeum crinitum is often found in rocky or arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is sometimes cultivated for forage or as an ornamental plant due to its delicate appearance.
