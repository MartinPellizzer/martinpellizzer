Heliconia austrocaledonica

Heliconia austrocaledonica, commonly known as the New Caledonia heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to the island of New Caledonia.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its large, colorful bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that are followed by bright red or orange fruits. Its structure consists of a tall, stout stem with broad, waxy leaves that provide a lush, tropical appearance.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is also important in local ecosystems.
