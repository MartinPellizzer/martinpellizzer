Hieracium praecox subsp. sagittibifidum

Hieracium praecox subsp.

sagittibifidum, commonly known as the arrowleaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant features basal leaves that are arrow-shaped, giving it its common name, and it produces yellow flowers in clusters. It is typically found in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology and floral structure compared to other Hieracium species.
