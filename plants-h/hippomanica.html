Hippomanica

Hippomanica, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by oblong, woody fruits containing numerous seeds. Hippomanica is often found in disturbed areas and can become invasive in certain regions.

Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various soil types make it both ecologically significant and a challenge for land managers.
