Haastia pulvinaris var. pulvinaris

Haastia pulvinaris var. pulvinaris, commonly known as the silver hairgrass, is a striking New Zealand native plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-blue foliage that forms a low, mat-like growth. The plant features slender, wiry stems and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late spring. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions.

Due to its ornamental appeal and ecological significance, it is cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
