Hepatica henryi

Hepatica henryi, commonly known as Henry's liverleaf, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves and early spring blooming flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest floors and has a short stem with a single, nodding flower that ranges in color from white to pink. Its foliage is often covered in a dense layer of fine, soft hairs, and it is known for its ornamental value in woodland gardens.

Hepatica henryi is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and maintaining biodiversity in its native habitats.
