Hieracium antheticum

Hieracium antheticum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers, which are typically arranged in a single head surrounded by green bracts. The plant has a creeping rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread aggressively in suitable habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinct appearance.

Hieracium antheticum is native to Europe and parts of Asia, where it thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.
