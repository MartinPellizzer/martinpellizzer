Hedyotis lutea

Hedyotis lutea, commonly known as yellow eye of the cat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Hedyotis lutea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
