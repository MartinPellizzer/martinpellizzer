Helichrysum intermedium var. humile

Helichrysum intermedium var. humile, commonly known as the dwarf everlasting or small everlasting, is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial native to South Africa.

It features dense clusters of small, golden-yellow flowers that remain open for extended periods, giving it its common name. The plant has wiry, branching stems and linear, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its drought tolerance and long-lasting blooms make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
