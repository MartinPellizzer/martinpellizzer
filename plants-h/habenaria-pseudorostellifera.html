Habenaria pseudorostellifera

Habenaria pseudorostellifera, commonly known as the false rostellate orchid, is a species of orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces inflorescences with small, greenish-white flowers that have a distinctive, spurred lip. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest undergrowth.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its unique floral structure and ecological role in its habitat.
