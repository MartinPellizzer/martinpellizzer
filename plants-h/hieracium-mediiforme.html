Hieracium mediiforme

Hieracium mediiforme, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces numerous yellow flowers arranged in composite heads. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as meadows and roadsides. It is native to Europe and has been introduced to other parts of the world.

Hieracium mediiforme is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and can form dense colonies in suitable environments.
