Hakea francisiana

Hakea francisiana, commonly known as the Geraldton waxplant, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical, red or pink flower spikes that resemble candles. The plant has needle-like leaves and produces woody, spine-tipped fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in sandy soils and is adapted to coastal and Mediterranean climates.

Hakea francisiana is often used in ornamental gardening due to its striking appearance and hardy nature.
