Helianthemum teneriffae

Helianthemum teneriffae, commonly known as the Canary Island rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a low-growing shrub with small, fleshy leaves adapted to the arid climate of its native habitat. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for xeriscaping in Mediterranean climates.
