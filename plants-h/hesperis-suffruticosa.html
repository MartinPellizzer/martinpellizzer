Hesperis suffruticosa

Hesperis suffruticosa, commonly known as the sweet rocket or sweet scabious, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright, branching stems and clusters of bright pink or purple flowers. The plant produces small, four-petaled flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Hesperis suffruticosa is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
