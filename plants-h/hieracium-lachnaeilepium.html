Hieracium lachnaeilepium

Hieracium lachnaeilepium, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, yellow-orange composite flowers and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are basal, rough, and often covered in fine hairs, while the stems are upright and somewhat branched.

Hieracium lachnaeilepium is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative reproduction, making it a common weed in various habitats.
