Hydrangea aspera var. typica

Hydrangea aspera var. typica, commonly known as the rough hydrangea, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from white to pink or blue, depending on soil pH. The plant features sturdy, upright stems and broad, ovate leaves with a rough, textured surface. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its flowering period typically occurs in late summer to early fall, adding vibrant color to gardens during the transitional seasons.
