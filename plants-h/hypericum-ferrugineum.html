Hypericum ferrugineum

Hypericum ferrugineum, commonly known as the rusty St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and thrives in sunny, open habitats. The plant features narrow, dark green leaves with reddish-brown veins, and its flowers are small, yellow-orange, and appear in clusters. It produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are often eaten by birds.

Hypericum ferrugineum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
