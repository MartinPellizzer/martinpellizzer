Hyptis neglecta

Hyptis neglecta, commonly known as Mexican blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, often with a blue or grayish hue, while its small, tubular flowers are usually pale blue or violet.

Hyptis neglecta is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
