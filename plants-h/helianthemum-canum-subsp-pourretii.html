Helianthemum canum subsp. pourretii

Helianthemum canum subsp.

pourretii, commonly known as the rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a low-growing shrub with small, fleshy leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
