Hieracium pyrenaicum

Hieracium pyrenaicum, commonly known as the Pyrenean hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central dark center.

Hieracium pyrenaicum is often found in meadows and grasslands at high altitudes, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
