Hedychium emeiense

Hedychium emeiense, commonly known as the Emei mountain ginger, is a striking tropical plant native to China.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy yellow flowers with a distinctive red or orange center. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, spadix-like inflorescences that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, providing a lush green backdrop to its vibrant flowers.

Hedychium emeiense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
