Huperzia lucidula var. tryonii

Huperzia lucidula var. tryonii, commonly known as the Tryon's huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen appearance with closely arranged, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and leathery. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its sporangia are clustered in terminal clusters on the underside of the leaves, a feature typical of many ferns.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
