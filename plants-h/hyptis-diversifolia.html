Hyptis diversifolia

Hyptis diversifolia, commonly known as the variable hyptis, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically arranged in dense clusters. Hyptis diversifolia thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or disturbed areas.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
