Hieracium bifidum subsp. subdyscimon

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

subdyscimon, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm.

The plant features basal and cauline leaves that are linear to lanceolate in shape, with a hairy surface. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It has a taproot system and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
