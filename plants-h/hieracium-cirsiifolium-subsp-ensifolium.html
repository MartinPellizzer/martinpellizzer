Hieracium cirsiifolium subsp. ensifolium

Hieracium cirsiifolium subsp.

ensifolium, commonly known as the cut-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers and serrated, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. It has a creeping growth habit with numerous stems that can form dense colonies.

The subspecies ensifolium is distinguished by its more elongated and sharply cut leaves compared to other members of the species.
