Hieracium periphanoides

Hieracium periphanoides, commonly known as the showy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
