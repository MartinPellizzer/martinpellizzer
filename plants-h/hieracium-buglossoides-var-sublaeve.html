Hieracium buglossoides var. sublaeve

Hieracium buglossoides var. sublaeve, commonly known as the smooth meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads made up of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a smooth texture, and the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
