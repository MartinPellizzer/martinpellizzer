Horkelia daucifolia var. caruifolia

Horkelia daucifolia var. caruifolia, commonly known as the Sierra daisy, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky, open habitats such as meadows and slopes, preferring well-drained soil. It has finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow florets arranged in a composite flower head.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
