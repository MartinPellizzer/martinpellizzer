Hieracium nigrescens subsp. melanomallum

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

melanomallum, commonly known as the black-stemmed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family, and is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows. It has a taproot system and is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
