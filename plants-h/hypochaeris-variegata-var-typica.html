Hypochaeris variegata var. typica

Hypochaeris variegata var. typica, commonly known as the variegated weed or yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer, followed by seed heads that release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

The leaves are generally lance-shaped with a hairy texture, and the plant is known for its rapid spread in various habitats.
