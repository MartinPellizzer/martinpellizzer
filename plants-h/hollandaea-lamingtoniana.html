Hollandaea lamingtoniana

Hollandaea lamingtoniana, commonly known as the bluebell bellflower, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rock crevices. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, white pubescence.

It is a member of the Campanulaceae family, known for its bell-shaped flowers.
