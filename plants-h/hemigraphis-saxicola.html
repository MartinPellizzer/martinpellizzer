Hemigraphis saxicola

Hemigraphis saxicola, commonly known as the rock begonia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically blooming in spring or early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or shaded borders.

Hemigraphis saxicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
