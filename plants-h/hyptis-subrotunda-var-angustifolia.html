Hyptis subrotunda var. angustifolia

Hyptis subrotunda var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf hyptis, is a species of flowering plant in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of the Americas, particularly Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Hyptis subrotunda var. angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
