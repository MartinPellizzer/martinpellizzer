Hieracium hypsilophum

Hieracium hypsilophum, commonly known as the tall hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant features a robust stem that can reach heights of up to 1 meter, adorned with heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers.

The species is characterized by its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common and sometimes invasive weed in various regions.
