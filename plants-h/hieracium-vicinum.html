Hieracium vicinum

Hieracium vicinum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters. The plant is native to Europe and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It has a taproot system and is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes.

Hieracium vicinum is also noted for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.
