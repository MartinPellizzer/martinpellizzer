Hippocrepis scabra var. jayana

Hippocrepis scabra var. jayana, commonly known as the Jayana helleborine, is a variant of the common helleborine, characterized by its distinctive, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia, typically found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream sides. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers with three petals, which are often overlooked due to their inconspicuous appearance. Its botanical significance lies in its role as a food source for certain Lepidoptera species and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
