Hibbertia fasciculata var. crassifolia

Hibbertia fasciculata var. crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved hibbertia, is a shrub native to the southeastern regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are broad and elliptical, often arranged in clusters, giving the plant its "fasciculate" appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and resilience to coastal conditions.
