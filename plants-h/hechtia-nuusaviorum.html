Hechtia nuusaviorum

Hechtia nuusaviorum, commonly known as the Mexican heather.

It is a member of the Bromeliaceae family, characterized by its rosette-forming growth habit and vibrant, showy inflorescences. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in water retention. Its flowers are typically red or orange, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.

Native to Mexico, it thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.
