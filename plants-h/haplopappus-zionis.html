Haplopappus zionis

Haplopappus zionis, commonly known as the Zion dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States, particularly in Utah and Nevada.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in arid and rocky environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its yellow flowers bloom in the spring, followed by fluffy seed heads that disperse via the wind.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological role in its native habitat.
