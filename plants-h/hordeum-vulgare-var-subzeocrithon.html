Hordeum vulgare var. subzeocrithon

Hordeum vulgare var. subzeocrithon, commonly known as dwarf barley, is a variety of barley characterized by its compact growth habit and shorter stature compared to standard barley varieties.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. This variety is often cultivated for its high yield and adaptability to various soil conditions. Its botanical features include narrow leaves, spikelets arranged in a panicle, and small, three-lobed grains.

It is primarily used in malting and brewing due to its desirable enzymatic properties.
