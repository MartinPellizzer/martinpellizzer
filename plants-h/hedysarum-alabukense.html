Hedysarum alabukense

Hedysarum alabukense, commonly known as the Alabukian sweet clover, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system has nitrogen-fixing capabilities, contributing to soil enrichment.

Hedysarum alabukense is often found in alpine meadows and is of interest to botanists due to its ecological significance.
