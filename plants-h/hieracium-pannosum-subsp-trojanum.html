Hieracium pannosum subsp. trojanum

Hieracium pannosum subsp.

trojanum, commonly known as the Trojan Ragwort, is a flowering plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellowish to greenish-yellow flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and waste areas. It has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces small, clustered flowers.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
