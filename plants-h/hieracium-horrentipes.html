Hieracium horrentipes

Hieracium horrentipes, commonly known as the tall hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of tubular and ray florets. The plant is notable for its tall, erect stem and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

Hieracium horrentipes is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
