Hibiscus ascendens

Hibiscus ascendens, commonly known as the giant spider lily, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, featuring vibrant pink or red petals with a prominent yellow center. The plant has heart-shaped, broad leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. Hibiscus ascendens thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its flowers bloom throughout the year, making it a popular choice for gardens in tropical and subtropical regions.
