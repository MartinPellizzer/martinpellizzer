Hemitome

Hemitome, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple ray flowers and spiny, bract-like leaves. Native to coastal areas of the Mediterranean, it thrives in salty, well-drained soils. The plant's unique structure helps it retain moisture in arid environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and coastal landscapes.
