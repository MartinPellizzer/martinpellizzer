Hamosa arizonica

Hamosa arizonica, commonly known as the Arizona thornapple, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces distinctive, round, green berries that turn red when mature, containing seeds that are dispersed by birds. Hamosa arizonica thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along streambanks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of interest in ecological studies.
