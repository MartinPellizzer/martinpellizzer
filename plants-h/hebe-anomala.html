Hebe anomala

Hebe anomala, commonly known as the purple hebe, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically produces violet or purple flowers that bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Hebe anomala thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its foliage is usually evergreen, with lance-shaped leaves that are aromatic when crushed.
