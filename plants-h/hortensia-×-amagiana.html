Hortensia × amagiana

Hortensia × amagiana, commonly known as the Japanese hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy flower clusters that can range in color from blue to pink, depending on soil pH. The plant produces mophead or lacecap blooms, with the former featuring dense, rounded flowers and the latter displaying a central cluster of smaller florets. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Hortensia × amagiana is also valued for its ability to be trained into various shapes, enhancing its ornamental appeal.
