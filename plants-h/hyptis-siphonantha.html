Hyptis siphonantha

Hyptis siphonantha, commonly known as the Mexican blue sage, is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and showy flowers.

It is native to Central America and features lance-shaped leaves with a silver-gray underside. The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its medicinal properties have been studied for their potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.
