Hieracium uplandiae

Hieracium uplandiae, commonly known as the upland hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in open, rocky habitats, such as meadows and slopes, where it thrives in full sun. Its flowering period occurs in the summer, producing yellow to orange composite flowers that attract pollinators.

Hieracium uplandiae is noted for its robust growth and adaptability to arid environments.
