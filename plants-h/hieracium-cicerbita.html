Hieracium cicerbita

Hieracium cicerbita, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and is typically found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant produces yellow to orange composite flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a hairy texture, often with a bluish-green hue.

Hieracium cicerbita is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
