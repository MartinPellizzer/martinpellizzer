Hesperis tristis f. atropurpurea

Hesperis tristis f. atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple-flowered wallflower, is a cultivated form of the common wallflower.

It is characterized by its striking deep purple to violet flowers, which bloom in early summer. The plant is a biennial or short-lived perennial, typically reaching heights of 30 to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are often hairy as well.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and its ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
