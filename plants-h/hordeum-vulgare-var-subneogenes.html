Hordeum vulgare var. subneogenes

Hordeum vulgare var. subneogenes, commonly known as the intermediate barley, is a botanical variant of barley characterized by its intermediate growth habit between two-row and six-row barley.

It typically exhibits a compact plant structure with shorter spikes and fewer rows of grains compared to standard barley varieties. This variety is often noted for its adaptability to a range of environmental conditions, making it suitable for various agricultural settings. Its botanical features include hairy leaves and a distinct flowering pattern that aids in its identification.

The plant is also valued for its potential in breeding programs aimed at improving yield and resilience in barley cultivation.
