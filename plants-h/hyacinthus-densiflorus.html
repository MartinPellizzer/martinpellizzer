Hyacinthus densiflorus

Hyacinthus densiflorus, commonly known as the dense-flowered hyacinth, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features striking clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that emerge from a basal rosette. Its bulbous root system allows it to store nutrients and water, supporting its growth in arid conditions.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
