Helixanthera alata

Helixanthera alata, commonly known as the trailing helixanthera, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, showy flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant produces tubular flowers that are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems.

Helixanthera alata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to cascade from hanging baskets or containers.
