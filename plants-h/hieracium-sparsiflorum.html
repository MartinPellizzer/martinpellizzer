Hieracium sparsiflorum

Hieracium sparsiflorum, commonly known as the sparseflower hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has rough, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often lobed.

Its showy yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
