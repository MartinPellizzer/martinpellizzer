Helianthemum marifolium var. tomentosum

Helianthemum marifolium var. tomentosum, commonly known as the woolly rock rose, is a low-growing shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features dense, grayish-white pubescence covering its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to Mediterranean climates.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
