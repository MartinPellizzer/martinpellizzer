Hygrophila schulli var. alba

Hygrophila schulli var. alba, commonly known as white hygrophila, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and its striking white to pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in wet or marshy environments, making it a common sight in swampy areas and along riverbanks. Its leaves are typically narrow and elongated, with a smooth texture.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in aquatic gardens and pond settings.
