Hypericum macrosepalum

Hypericum macrosepalum, commonly known as the large-sepal St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. The plant has opposite, simple, and usually hairy leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
