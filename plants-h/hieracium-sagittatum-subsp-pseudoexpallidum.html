Hieracium sagittatum subsp. pseudoexpallidum

Hieracium sagittatum subsp.

pseudoexpallidum, commonly known as the narrowleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and yellow composite flowers.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough texture, often with a distinct central vein. It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

This subspecies is distinguished by its smaller, more narrowly shaped leaves compared to other members of the species.
