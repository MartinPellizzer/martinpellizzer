Helianthus lenticularis var. primulinus

Helianthus lenticularis var. primulinus, commonly known as the yellow sunflower, is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features bright yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of smaller, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet, with lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are alternate along the stem. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
