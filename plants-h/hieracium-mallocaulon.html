Hieracium mallocaulon

Hieracium mallocaulon, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow ray flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. It has a taproot system and can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.

The leaves and flowers are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive, woolly appearance.
