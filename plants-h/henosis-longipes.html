Henosis longipes

Henosis longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed henosis, is a unique flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated stems and clusters of small, delicate white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a fibrous root system that helps it anchor in rocky soils and retain moisture. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments.

Henosis longipes is primarily valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in specialized gardens.
