Hibbertia alopecota

Hibbertia alopecota, commonly known as the woolly sandhill flower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, often found in sandy soils. The plant features narrow, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Its flowers are yellow with red markings, and they bloom in spring.

Hibbertia alopecota is well adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.
