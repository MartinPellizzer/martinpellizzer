Heliconia adflexa

Heliconia adflexa, commonly known as the drooping heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous inflorescences that consist of brightly colored bracts resembling flowers. The plant has long, strap-like leaves that are often used in floral arrangements due to their aesthetic appeal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Heliconia adflexa is also valued for its role in supporting local wildlife, particularly birds and insects.
