Hieracium triadanum subsp. leucopannosum

Hieracium triadanum subsp.

leucopannosum, commonly known as the white-panicle hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and a tall, flowering stem.

The plant produces showy, yellowish to whitish flower heads that are typically arranged in a panicle-like structure. It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
