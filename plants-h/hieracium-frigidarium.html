Hieracium frigidarium

Hieracium frigidarium, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and tall, sparsely branched stems that bear yellow flowers. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are composed of several overlapping ray florets.

Hieracium frigidarium is a perennial species that plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
