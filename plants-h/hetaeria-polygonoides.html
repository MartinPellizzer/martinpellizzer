Hetaeria polygonoides

Hetaeria polygonoides, commonly known as the common hetaeria, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic in shape, and often have a rough, textured surface. This plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as habitat for various invertebrates.
