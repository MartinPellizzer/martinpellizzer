Hieracium saxifragum subsp. rufescens

Hieracium saxifragum subsp.

rufescens, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils, often in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

The plant features a basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in early summer. Its flowering stems are usually sparsely hairy and bear several flower heads.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
