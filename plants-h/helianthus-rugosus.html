Helianthus rugosus

Helianthus rugosus, commonly known as the roughleaf sunflower, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers with dark centers, typical of the sunflower genus. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Helianthus rugosus is an important plant for pollinators and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
