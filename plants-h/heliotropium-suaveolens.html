Heliotropium suaveolens

Heliotropium suaveolens, commonly known as sweet heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and clusters of small, fragrant, violet-blue flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds, and it is often grown as an ornamental due to its pleasant scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Heliotropium suaveolens is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
