Hookera grandiflora

Hookera grandiflora, commonly known as the giant alpine bellflower, is a striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its large, showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a rosette of thick, woolly leaves that help it retain moisture in its arid environment. It thrives in cool, moist conditions and is often found in rocky or alpine meadows.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
