Hippeastrum consobrinum

Hippeastrum consobrinum, commonly known as the Brazilian night lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom in late summer or early autumn. The plant produces strap-shaped, evergreen leaves that are broad and glossy, providing a lush backdrop for its vibrant blooms. Its flowers are typically trumpet-shaped, with a wide range of colors including red, orange, and yellow.

Hippeastrum consobrinum is popular as a ornamental plant due to its dramatic appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
