Hippeastrum montanum

Hippeastrum montanum, commonly known as the mountain amaryllis, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly in regions like Brazil and Argentina.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that can range in color from white to deep red, often with a contrasting stripe. The plant produces large, strap-like leaves that are typically broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native arid environments. Hippeastrum montanum is a bulbous plant, with a thick underground stem that supports its growth and flowering.

It is commonly cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
