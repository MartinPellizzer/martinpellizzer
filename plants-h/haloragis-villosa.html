Haloragis villosa

Haloragis villosa, commonly known as the hairy willow herb, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland or swampy environments. The plant features slender, often hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its small, white or greenish flowers are clustered in spikes and bloom during the spring.

Haloragis villosa plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and provides habitat for various invertebrates.
