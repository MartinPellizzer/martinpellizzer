Hieracium floribundum subsp. sudavicum

Hieracium floribundum subsp.

sudavicum, commonly known as the southern common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowering heads consist of yellow disk flowers surrounded by pale yellow to brown ray florets.

It is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is considered a weed in some agricultural areas.
