Hebe coxiana

Hebe coxiana, commonly known as the coastal hebe, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its narrow, upright growth habit and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale blue flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Hebe coxiana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate light frost.
