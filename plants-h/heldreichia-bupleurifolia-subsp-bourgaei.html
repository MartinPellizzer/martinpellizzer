Heldreichia bupleurifolia subsp. bourgaei

Heldreichia bupleurifolia subsp.

bourgaei, commonly known as the Balkan bellflower, is a rare and endemic species native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high elevations.

The plant features lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers in late spring. It is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
