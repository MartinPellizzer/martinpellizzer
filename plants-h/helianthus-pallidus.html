Helianthus pallidus

Helianthus pallidus, commonly known as the pale sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust stem structure. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as prairies and roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers, which bloom in late summer, consist of yellow ray florets surrounding a disk of smaller, tubular flowers.

Helianthus pallidus is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
