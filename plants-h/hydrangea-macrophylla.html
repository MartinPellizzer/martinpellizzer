Hydrangea macrophylla

Hydrangea macrophylla, commonly known as the bigleaf hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy bracts that can range in color from blue to pink, depending on soil pH. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in cone-shaped inflorescences. Its leaves are broadly ovate and have a smooth, waxy texture.

Hydrangea macrophylla thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
