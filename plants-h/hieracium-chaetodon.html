Hieracium chaetodon

Hieracium chaetodon, commonly known as the hooked hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny conditions. Its leaves are rough and hairy, with a distinctive hooked shape on the bracts, giving it its common name.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer, producing seeds that are dispersed by wind.
