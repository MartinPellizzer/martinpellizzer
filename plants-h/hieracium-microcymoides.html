Hieracium microcymoides

Hieracium microcymoides, commonly known as the small-cymoid hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and daisy-like appearance. The plant typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and often covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in dense clusters.

Hieracium microcymoides is known for its ability to spread via rhizomes and is considered a persistent weed in certain regions.
