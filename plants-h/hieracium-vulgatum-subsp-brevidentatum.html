Hieracium vulgatum subsp. brevidentatum

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

brevidentatum, commonly known as the narrow-toothed hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped with finely toothed margins, and the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is often found in open habitats and is relatively common in temperate regions.
