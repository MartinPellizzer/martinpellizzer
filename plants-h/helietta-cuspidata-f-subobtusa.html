Helietta cuspidata f. subobtusa

Helietta cuspidata f. subobtusa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf helietta, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, evergreen leaves that are narrowly elliptic in shape. The plant produces bell-shaped, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands or rocky slopes.

This subspecies is noted for its compact growth habit and ornamental value in horticultural settings.
