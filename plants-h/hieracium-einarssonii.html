Hieracium einarssonii

Hieracium einarssonii, commonly known as Einarsson's hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, producing yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
