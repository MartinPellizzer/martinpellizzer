Hoya salmonea subsp. pallida

Hoya salmonea subsp.

pallida, commonly known as the pink waxy vine, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and elliptical, waxy leaves that are typically a pale pink or white color.

The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often blooming in late spring or early summer. It is a popular houseplant due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements. Hoya salmonea subsp.

pallida thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.
