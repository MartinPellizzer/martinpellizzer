Heterotheca graminifolia

Heterotheca graminifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy purple to pink flower clusters in late summer to early fall. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Heterotheca graminifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
