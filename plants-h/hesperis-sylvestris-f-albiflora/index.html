Hesperis Sylvestris F. Albiflora

Albiflora hesperis, scientifically known as Hesperis sylvestris f. albiflora, is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. This ornamental flowering plant belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many familiar garden plants such as cabbages and mustard. Its most notable feature is its large, white petals that bloom in early spring, creating a dramatic visual impact in natural landscapes. The plant is often found in rocky habitats and open woodlands, where it thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade. Due to its limited distribution and delicate nature, Albiflora hesperis is considered a species of conservation concern.
