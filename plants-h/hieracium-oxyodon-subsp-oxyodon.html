Hieracium oxyodon subsp. oxyodon

Hieracium oxyodon subsp.

oxyodon, commonly known as the oxyodon hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typical sunflower-like structure.

The plant features upright, hairy stems and basal leaves that are lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are typically yellow, with ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular florets.

It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.
