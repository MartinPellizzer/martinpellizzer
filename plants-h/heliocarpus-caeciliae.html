Heliocarpus caeciliae

Heliocarpus caeciliae, commonly known as the Brazilian chestnut tree, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, featuring a straight trunk and a broad, rounded crown. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, with a smooth margin and a glossy upper surface. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, and the fruit is a hard, woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

The plant is valued for its timber and has ecological importance in its native habitat.
