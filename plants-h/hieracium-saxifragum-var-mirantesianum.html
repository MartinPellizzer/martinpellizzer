Hieracium saxifragum var. mirantesianum

Hieracium saxifragum var. mirantesianum, commonly known as the Mirantian Hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the alpine regions of the Pyrenees.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, thriving in harsh, sunny environments. It produces small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in some regions.
