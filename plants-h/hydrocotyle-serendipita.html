Hydrocotyle serendipita

Hydrocotyle serendipita, commonly known as the silverleaf marsh plant, is a small, herbaceous perennial native to wetland habitats in the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, glossy, silver-green leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne on slender, hairy stems. Its root system is adapted to thrive in saturated soils, making it a common sight in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Hydrocotyle serendipita is also known for its ability to spread via rhizomes, allowing it to form dense colonies in suitable environments.
