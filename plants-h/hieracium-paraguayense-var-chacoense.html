Hieracium paraguayense var. chacoense

Hieracium paraguayense var. chacoense, commonly known as the Chaco hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America, particularly found in the Chaco region of Paraguay and Argentina.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and disturbed soils, exhibiting a low-growing habit with hairy stems and basal leaves. Its flowers are yellow, with ray florets that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its adaptability to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient component of the local flora.
