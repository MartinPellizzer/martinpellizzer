Hormathophylla saxigena

Hormathophylla saxigena, commonly known as the saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to rocky and mountainous regions.

It typically grows in crevices and on rock surfaces, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink, with five petals. Its leaves are often fleshy and succulent, helping it retain moisture in arid conditions.

Hormathophylla saxigena is valued for its resilience and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
