Hydrocotyle americana

Hydrocotyle americana, commonly known as American marsh penny, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant features a rosette of heart-shaped, succulent leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Small, white flowers with five petals appear on slender stalks during the summer months.

It plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and wildlife.
