Hieracium austrinum

Hieracium austrinum, commonly known as the Australian hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils, often in coastal regions. It produces yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom from late winter to early spring.

Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
