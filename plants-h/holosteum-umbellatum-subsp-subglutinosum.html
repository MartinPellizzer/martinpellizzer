Holosteum umbellatum subsp. subglutinosum

Holosteum umbellatum subsp.

subglutinosum, commonly known as the sticky false bellflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It features hairy, upright stems that bear clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers.

The leaves are ovate to lanceolate, often covered in fine, sticky hairs. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that are typically retained on the plant for an extended period.

It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
