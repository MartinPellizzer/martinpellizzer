Hedyotis serpyllifolia

Hedyotis serpyllifolia, commonly known as lesser burnet, is a low-growing perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming from late spring to early autumn. The plant has thin, wiry stems and is characterized by its dense foliage of tiny, rounded leaves. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed areas.

Hedyotis serpyllifolia is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
