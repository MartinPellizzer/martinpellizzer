Hypocalyptus cordatus

Hypocalyptus cordatus, commonly known as the heartleaf tree, is a species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, featuring a straight trunk and a broad, spreading crown. The leaves are heart-shaped, with a smooth texture and a dark green upper surface. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by round, fleshy fruits.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional medicinal uses in local cultures.
