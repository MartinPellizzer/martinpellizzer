Hieracium incisum subsp. plazenicense

Hieracium incisum subsp.

plazenicense, commonly known as the Plazen's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and along roadsides. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have serrated edges, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is generally low-growing, with a fibrous root system that helps it establish in various soil types.
