Heteropogon villosus var. genuinus

Heteropogon villosus var. genuinus, commonly known as the velvet grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy, bluish-green foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of riparian ecosystems.
