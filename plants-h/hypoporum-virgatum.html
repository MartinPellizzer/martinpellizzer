Hypoporum virgatum

Hypoporum virgatum, commonly known as the Virginia false nettle, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive velvety texture on the underside. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in spikes and bloom in late spring.

The species is part of the Onagraceae family, which includes other plants like willows and evening primroses.
