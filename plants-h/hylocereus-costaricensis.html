Hylocereus costaricensis

Hylocereus costaricensis, commonly known as the Costa Rican night bloomer, is a climbing cactus belonging to the Cactaceae family.

It is native to Central America, particularly Costa Rica, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its spiny stems and produces large, fragrant flowers that open at night. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink and have a strong, sweet scent.

The plant is adapted to arid environments and is often found growing on trees or rock surfaces.
