Haplopappus racemosus var. brachycephalus

Haplopappus racemosus var. brachycephalus, commonly known as the short-headed desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing form and features hairy stems and leaves adapted to conserve water. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into spherical, woolly seed heads, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are usually alternate and have a bluish-green hue with a waxy coating.

This variety is well-suited to harsh environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
