Hypericum sabiniforme

Hypericum sabiniforme, commonly known as the creeping St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features woody stems that root at the nodes, forming dense mats or ground covers. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, elliptic, and often covered in fine hairs.

Hypericum sabiniforme is known for its ornamental value and is often used in landscaping for erosion control.
