Hieracium sparsum subsp. beypazariense

Hieracium sparsum subsp.

beypazariense, commonly known as the Beypazari hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its clustered, flowering heads and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in rocky or disturbed soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered with fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The flowers are yellow and are arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family.
