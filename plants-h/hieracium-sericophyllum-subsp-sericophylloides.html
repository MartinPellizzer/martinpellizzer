Hieracium sericophyllum subsp. sericophylloides

Hieracium sericophyllum subsp.

sericophylloides, commonly known as the silky hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers.

The plant is distinguished by its hairy, silver-green leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It often grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is sometimes grown in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
