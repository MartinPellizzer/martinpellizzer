Helichrysum obcordatum subsp. majus

Helichrysum obcordatum subsp.

majus, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its dense, yellow-orange flower heads that resemble small sunflowers, which are often used in floral arrangements due to their long-lasting nature.

The plant produces small, narrow leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its botanical name reflects its distinctive leaf shape and its association with the ever-lasting quality of its blooms.
