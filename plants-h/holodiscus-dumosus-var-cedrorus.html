Holodiscus dumosus var. cedrorus

Holodiscus dumosus var. cedrorus, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its yellowish-brown bark and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and floral display.

The species is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
