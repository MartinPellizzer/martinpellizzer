Hakea cravenii

Hakea cravenii, commonly known as the Craven's hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its woody structure and distinctive, needle-like leaves. The plant produces bright red or orange flowers in spikes, which are highly attractive to nectar-feeding birds and insects. Hakea cravenii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance through its thick, leathery foliage.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and resilience in harsh conditions.
