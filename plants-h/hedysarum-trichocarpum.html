Hedysarum trichocarpum

Hedysarum trichocarpum, commonly known as the purple vetch, is a perennial legume native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces striking violet to purple flowers in early summer. The plant is characterized by its slender, twining stems and is often found growing in rocky or dry soils. It is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment.

Hedysarum trichocarpum is also cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive flowers and adaptability to various growing conditions.
