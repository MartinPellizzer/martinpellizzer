Hallia virgata

Hallia virgata, commonly known as the silverleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically have five petals and a prominent central stamen. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, black seeds. Hallia virgata thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
