Hydnophytum nigrescens

Hydnophytum nigrescens, commonly known as the black-stemmed mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant found in the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It has a unique symbiotic relationship with its host trees, absorbing water and nutrients through its haustoria while also providing shelter for various insect species. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are pollinated by insects. Its fruit develops into a fleshy, red berry that attracts birds, which in turn help disperse its seeds.

The plant's dark green leaves and woody stems make it easily identifiable in its natural habitat.
