Humea corymbulosa

Humea corymbulosa, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub with woody stems and broad, lobed leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Humea corymbulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
