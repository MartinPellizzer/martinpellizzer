Hedyotis pauciflora

Hedyotis pauciflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered inchplant, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically features small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers, which are usually borne in terminal racemes. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a suitable candidate for ornamental gardening in humid climates.
