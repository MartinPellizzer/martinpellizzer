Hugonia macrophylla f. obtusicuneatoapiculata

Hugonia macrophylla f. obtusicuneatoapiculata, commonly known as the broad-leafed honeysuckle, is a rare and ornamental variant of the honeysuckle family.

It is characterized by its large, ovate leaves that are broad at the base and taper slightly towards the tip. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Due to its rarity, it is considered a valuable species for botanical study and conservation efforts.
