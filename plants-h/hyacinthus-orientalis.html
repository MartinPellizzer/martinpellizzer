Hyacinthus orientalis

Hyacinthus orientalis, commonly known as the garden hyacinth, is a bulbous plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It produces dense clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers in early spring. The plant belongs to the Asparagales order and the Amaryllidaceae family. Its bulbs are perennial, and the plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens.

Hyacinths are also valued for their use in floral arrangements and as cut flowers.
