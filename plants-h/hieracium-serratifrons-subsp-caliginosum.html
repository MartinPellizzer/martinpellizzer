Hieracium serratifrons subsp. caliginosum

Hieracium serratifrons subsp.

caliginosum, commonly known as the misty hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is known for its composite flower heads.

Its subspecies status reflects subtle differences in morphology and habitat preference compared to other Hieracium species.
