Hyptis verticillata

Hyptis verticillata, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

Hyptis verticillata is also valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
