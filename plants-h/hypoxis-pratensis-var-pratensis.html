Hypoxis pratensis var. pratensis

Hypoxis pratensis var. pratensis, commonly known as the meadow hypoxis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Hypoxidaceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in a dense inflorescence, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed sites.

Hypoxis pratensis var. pratensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
