Hieracium cantalicum subsp. lysanum

Hieracium cantalicum subsp.

lysanum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats at high elevations, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.

The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in late summer. Its inflorescence is a single, terminal flower head surrounded by pale yellow ray florets.

It is notable for its adaptability to harsh mountain environments and its role in alpine ecosystems.
