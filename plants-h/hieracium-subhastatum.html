Hieracium subhastatum

Hieracium subhastatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, flat-topped clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in meadows and grasslands.

Hieracium subhastatum is a perennial species that spreads through rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
