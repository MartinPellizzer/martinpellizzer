Harpullia rhyticarpa

Harpullia rhyticarpa, commonly known as the red-fruited hibiscus, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces fleshy, red berries that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Harpullia rhyticarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
