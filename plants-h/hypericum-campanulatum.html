Hypericum campanulatum

Hypericum campanulatum, commonly known as the bell hypericum, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a slightly serrated margin.

The plant is often found in rocky or dry soils, thriving in full sun conditions.
