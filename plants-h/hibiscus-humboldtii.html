Hibiscus humboldtii

Hibiscus humboldtii, commonly known as the Humboldt's hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often woody and covered in fine hairs. Hibiscus humboldtii thrives in humid environments and is often found growing near rivers or in moist forest understories.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
