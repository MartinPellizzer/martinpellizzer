Hippeastrum nymphaea

Hippeastrum nymphaea, commonly known as the white belladonna lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It produces large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often with a subtle fragrance. The plant features broad, strap-like leaves that emerge from a bulb and are often used in ornamental gardening. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

The flowers bloom in late spring or early summer, adding a dramatic touch to gardens and landscapes.
