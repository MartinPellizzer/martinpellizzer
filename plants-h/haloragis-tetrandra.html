Haloragis tetrandra

Haloragis tetrandra, commonly known as the four-stamen willow herb, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands and along waterways. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in dense spikes and bloom during the spring and summer months.

Haloragis tetrandra is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in wetland environments.
