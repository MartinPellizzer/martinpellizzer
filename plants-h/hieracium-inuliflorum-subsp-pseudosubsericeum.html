Hieracium inuliflorum subsp. pseudosubsericeum

Hieracium inuliflorum subsp.

pseudosubsericeum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and typically hairy stems.

The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are yellow with ray florets. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
