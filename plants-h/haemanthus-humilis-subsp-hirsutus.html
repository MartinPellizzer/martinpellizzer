Haemanthus humilis subsp. hirsutus

Haemanthus humilis subsp.

hirsutus, commonly known as the hirsute red paintbrush, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It features striking red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in late summer to autumn.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it requires well-drained soil and moderate watering.
