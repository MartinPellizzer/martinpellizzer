Hakea plurijuga

Hakea plurijuga, commonly known as the coastal hakea, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces striking spikes of white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its woody structure and adaptability to coastal conditions make it a hardy species.

Hakea plurijuga is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
