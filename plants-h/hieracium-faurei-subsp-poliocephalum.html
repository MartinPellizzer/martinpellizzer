Hieracium faurei subsp. poliocephalum

Hieracium faurei subsp.

poliocephalum, commonly known as the purplehead hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, daisy-like yellow flowers.

The leaves are typically basal, with a rough, hairy texture and a distinctive dark green color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

The plant is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
