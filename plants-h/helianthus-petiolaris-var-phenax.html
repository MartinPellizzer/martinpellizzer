Helianthus petiolaris var. phenax

Helianthus petiolaris var. phenax, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sunflower, is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in wetlands and along stream banks, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its botanical features include a robust root system and a height that can reach up to 3 meters.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for pollinators.
