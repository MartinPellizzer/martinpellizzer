Hakea dactyloides var. latifolia

Hakea dactyloides var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves that are broader than those of the typical variety. The plant produces striking yellow flowers in winter, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens in suitable climates.
