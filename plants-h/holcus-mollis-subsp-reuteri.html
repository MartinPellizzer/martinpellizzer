Holcus mollis subsp. reuteri

Holcus mollis subsp.

reuteri, commonly known as the meadow brome, is a grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically thrives in moist, grassy habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while the seeds are small and wind-dispersed. Botanically, it is distinguished by its distinct subsp.

reuteri features, including differences in leaf anatomy and flowering structures.
