Hieracium cavillieri

Hieracium cavillieri, commonly known as the Italian hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Hieracium cavillieri is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in agricultural areas.
