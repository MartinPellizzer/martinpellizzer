Hordeum vulgare var. syriacum

Hordeum vulgare var. syriacum, commonly known as Syrian barley, is a variant of the common barley species.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its slender stems and loose, spike-like inflorescence. The plant produces small, two-lobed grains that are typically pale yellow to golden in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

This variety is historically significant for its use in ancient agriculture and its adaptation to harsh environmental conditions.
