Hippocrepandra neesiana

Hippocrepandra neesiana, commonly known as the Nees's orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, with a distinctive lip petal that aids in pollination. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
