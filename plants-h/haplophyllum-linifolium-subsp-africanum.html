Haplophyllum linifolium subsp. africanum

Haplophyllum linifolium subsp.

africanum, commonly known as the African starwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, star-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
