Hamelia axillaris

Hamelia axillaris, commonly known as the Mexican flamebush, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring lance-shaped leaves and showy, red-orange tubular flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
