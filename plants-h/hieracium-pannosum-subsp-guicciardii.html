Hieracium pannosum subsp. guicciardii

Hieracium pannosum subsp.

guicciardii, commonly known as the Guicciardii hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its hairy, sprawling growth habit and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is part of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its adaptability to arid environments.
