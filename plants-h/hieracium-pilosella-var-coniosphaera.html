Hieracium pilosella var. coniosphaera

Hieracium pilosella var. coniosphaera, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant features basal rosettes of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in late spring to early summer. Its inflorescence consists of a single flower head surrounded by pale green to white bracts.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
