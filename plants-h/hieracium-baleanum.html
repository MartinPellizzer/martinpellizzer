Hieracium baleanum

Hieracium baleanum, commonly known as the Balkan hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium baleanum is often found in temperate regions and is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
