Hakea longicuspis

Hakea longicuspis, commonly known as the long-spined hakea, is a shrub native to southwestern Western Australia.

It features dense, spiny foliage and produces cylindrical, red-brown flower spikes that bloom in spring. The plant has woody, branched stems and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Hakea longicuspis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
