Hieracium piliferum

Hieracium piliferum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are hairy and often lobed, while the flowers are yellow with a central disc.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
