Hypericum polycladum

Hypericum polycladum, commonly known as the clustered St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, red-orange fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

Hypericum polycladum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
