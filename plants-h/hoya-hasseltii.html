Hoya hasseltii

Hoya hasseltii, commonly known as the wax plant or the sweetheart plant, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and pink. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink, typically during the summer months. Hoya hasseltii is a trailing plant that can be grown as a houseplant or in hanging baskets.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
