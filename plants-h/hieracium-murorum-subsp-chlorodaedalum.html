Hieracium murorum subsp. chlorodaedalum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

chlorodaedalum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its bright yellow flower heads, which are composed of numerous small florets arranged in a composite structure.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows. Its leaves are usually basal, forming a rosette, and are covered with fine hairs.

This subspecies is distinguished by its pale greenish-yellow coloration, which sets it apart from other members of the Hieracium genus.
