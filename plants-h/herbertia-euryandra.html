Herbertia euryandra

Herbertia euryandra, commonly known as the broadleaf herbertia, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color and have a distinctive sheath-like base. The plant produces small, showy flowers in late spring to early summer, often in shades of pink or white. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal contribute to its value in horticultural settings.
