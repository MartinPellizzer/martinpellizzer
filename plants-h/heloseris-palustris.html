Heloseris palustris

Heloseris palustris, commonly known as the marsh heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Heloseris palustris plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
