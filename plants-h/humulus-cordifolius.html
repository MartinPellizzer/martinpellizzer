Humulus cordifolius

Humulus cordifolius, commonly known as Chinese hops, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Cannabaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and cylindrical, hairy stems. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in catkins. It is often cultivated for its use in traditional Chinese medicine and as a source of bioactive compounds.

Its botanical features make it distinct from the more widely known hops used in brewing.
