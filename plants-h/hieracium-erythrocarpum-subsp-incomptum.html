Hieracium erythrocarpum subsp. incomptum

Hieracium erythrocarpum subsp.

incomptum, commonly known as the red-fruited hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to central and eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow to orange composite flowers.

The plant produces distinctive red, fleshy fruits that are clustered in dense, spherical heads. It typically thrives in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the genus Hieracium.
