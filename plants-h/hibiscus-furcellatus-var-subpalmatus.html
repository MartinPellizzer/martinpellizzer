Hibiscus furcellatus var. subpalmatus

Hibiscus furcellatus var. subpalmatus, commonly known as the broadleaf hibiscus, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers bloom throughout the growing season, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
