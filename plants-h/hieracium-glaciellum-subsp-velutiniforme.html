Hieracium glaciellum subsp. velutiniforme

Hieracium glaciellum subsp.

velutiniforme, commonly known as the velvetleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to alpine and subalpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in the summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in high-altitude environments. Its subspecies name, *velutiniforme*, refers to its velvety, soft foliage.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in harsh mountainous habitats.
