Hieracium glabratum subsp. ozanonis

Hieracium glabratum subsp.

ozanonis, commonly known as the Ozano's hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of the Middle East. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is a perennial species that spreads through rhizomes, forming dense colonies in suitable habitats.
