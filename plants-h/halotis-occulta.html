Halotis occulta

Halotis occulta, commonly known as the hidden saltbush, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and salt-tolerant properties. The plant thrives in saline soils and is adapted to withstand extreme drought conditions. Its leaves are succulent, allowing it to store water efficiently.

Halotis occulta plays a role in stabilizing soil in degraded landscapes and is of interest for ecological restoration projects.
