Hieracium sylvaticum subsp. pseudogentile

Hieracium sylvaticum subsp.

pseudogentile, commonly known as the woodland hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in moist, shaded woodland areas and has hairy stems that are often reddish in color.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, with serrated edges.

This subspecies is part of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive floral structure and ecological adaptations to forest environments.
