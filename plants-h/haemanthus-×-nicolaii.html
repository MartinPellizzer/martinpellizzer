Haemanthus × nicolaii

Haemanthus × nicolaii, commonly known as the Red Widow.

This plant is a hybrid of Haemanthus albiflos and Haemanthus sanguineus, characterized by its striking red flowers that contrast with its dark green foliage. It is native to South Africa and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. The plant produces clusters of fleshy, red-orange flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively low maintenance once established.
