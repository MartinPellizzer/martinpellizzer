Holothrix exilis var. brachylabris

Holothrix exilis var. brachylabris, commonly known as the narrow-leaved holothrix, is a rare flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves and compact growth habit. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white flowers that bloom during the spring season. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable species for botanical study and conservation efforts.
