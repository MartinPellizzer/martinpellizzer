Hypericum ascyron subsp. pyramidatum

Hypericum ascyron subsp.

pyramidatum, commonly known as the yellow archangel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
