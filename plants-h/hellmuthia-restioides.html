Hellmuthia restioides

Hellmuthia restioides, commonly known as the coastal flax, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with long, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand salty sea air and strong coastal winds.

Hellmuthia restioides plays an important role in stabilizing dune ecosystems and providing habitat for small coastal fauna.
