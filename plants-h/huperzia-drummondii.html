Huperzia drummondii

Huperzia drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's huperzia, is a species of fern-like plant belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its simple, linear leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. It is found primarily in North America, particularly in the eastern United States.

Huperzia drummondii is notable for its primitive structure and is often used in ecological studies due to its sensitivity to environmental changes.
