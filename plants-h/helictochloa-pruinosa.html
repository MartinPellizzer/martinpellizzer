Helictochloa pruinosa

Helictochloa pruinosa, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage, which is covered in a dense layer of silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its inflorescence consists of narrow, spike-like clusters of small, greenish-brown flowers.

Helictochloa pruinosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
