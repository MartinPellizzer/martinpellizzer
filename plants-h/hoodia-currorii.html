Hoodia currorii

Hoodia currorii, commonly known as the desert apple or witch's butter, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom during the rainy season. The plant produces a unique, waxy fruit that contains a high concentration of nutrients and has been studied for its potential appetite-suppressing properties. Hoodia currorii is adapted to survive in extreme drought conditions, storing water in its thick stems and leaves.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for its harsh, desert environment.
