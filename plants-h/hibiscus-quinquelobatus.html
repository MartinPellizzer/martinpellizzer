Hibiscus quinquelobatus

Hibiscus quinquelobatus, commonly known as the five-lobed hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink or red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with five distinct lobes, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing along coastal areas and in moist, well-drained soils.

Hibiscus quinquelobatus is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
