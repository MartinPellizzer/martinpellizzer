Hieracium ruprechtii subsp. tephropodum

Hieracium ruprechtii subsp.

tephropodum, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats at high altitudes.

The plant features bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, adapted to withstand harsh mountain conditions.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
