Hesperis arundana

Hesperis arundana, commonly known as the sweet rocket, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant features showy clusters of pink or purple flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with a slightly hairy texture.

Hesperis arundana is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
