Helianthus giganteus var. oppositifolius

Helianthus giganteus var. oppositifolius, commonly known as the giant sunflower with opposite leaves, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States and is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers and tall, sturdy stems. The plant typically grows up to 6 feet in height, with broad, heart-shaped leaves arranged oppositely along the stem. Its foliage is often covered in fine, soft hairs, and it produces numerous ray flowers surrounding a central disk of tubular florets.

This species is well adapted to full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for native gardens and restoration projects.
