Hippeastrum gilliesianum

Hippeastrum gilliesianum, commonly known as the giant amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It features large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, typically in shades of red, pink, or white. The plant has strap-like, sword-shaped leaves that are broad and glossy, emerging from a fleshy underground bulb. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to bloom successfully.

Hippeastrum gilliesianum is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its dramatic floral display.
