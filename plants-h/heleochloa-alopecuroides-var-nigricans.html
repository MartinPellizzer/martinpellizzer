Heleochloa alopecuroides var. nigricans

Heleochloa alopecuroides var. nigricans, commonly known as black hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, wiry blades and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are loosely arranged, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
