Hieracium apargiiforme

Hieracium apargiiforme, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are typically arranged in a flat-topped cluster. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are usually lance-shaped and slightly toothed. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions, often becoming invasive.

Hieracium apargiiforme thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.
