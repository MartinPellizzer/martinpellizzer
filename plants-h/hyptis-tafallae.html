Hyptis tafallae

Hyptis tafallae, commonly known as the Tafalla hyptis, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to violet. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
