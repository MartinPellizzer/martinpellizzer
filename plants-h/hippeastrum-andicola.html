Hippeastrum andicola

Hippeastrum andicola, commonly known as the Andean amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in various colors such as red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces large, strap-like leaves that are typically broad and glossy. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soil with adequate sunlight.

Hippeastrum andicola is a perennial plant that prefers warm temperatures and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
