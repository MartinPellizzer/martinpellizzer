Heracleum nepalense

Heracleum nepalense, commonly known as the Nepal cowparn, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and northern India. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring hollow, ridged stems and finely divided, fern-like leaves. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in compound umbels, and it produces small, dry fruits called schizocarps.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded areas and is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
