Hyacinthus fallax

Hyacinthus fallax, commonly known as the wild hyacinth, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, thriving in rocky and coastal environments. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, violet, and white, typically blooming in spring. Its bulbs are adapted to survive harsh conditions, and it is often found in open, sunny habitats.

The plant's ornamental value has led to its cultivation in gardens, though it is primarily valued for its natural habitat and ecological role.
