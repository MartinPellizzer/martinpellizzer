Hieracium arragonense

Hieracium arragonense, commonly known as the Arragon hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are hairy and alternate, with a rosette formation in early growth stages.

The showy yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
