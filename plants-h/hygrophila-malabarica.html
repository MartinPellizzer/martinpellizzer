Hygrophila malabarica

Hygrophila malabarica, commonly known as the Malabar hygrophila, is a perennial aquatic plant native to the wetlands of India and Southeast Asia.

It thrives in shallow waters and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces tiny, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically blooming during the rainy season. It is often used in aquariums due to its ornamental value and ability to oxygenate water.

Hygrophila malabarica plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
