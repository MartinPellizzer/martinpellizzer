Hieracium murorum var. pilosissimum

Hieracium murorum var. pilosissimum, commonly known as the woolly mouse-ear, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features a basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in white to grayish pubescence. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its hairy foliage and distinctive flower structure make it easily identifiable within the genus Hieracium.
