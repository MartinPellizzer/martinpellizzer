Hemiorchis habessenica

Hemiorchis habessenica, commonly known as the Habessinian orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms in the spring, producing clusters of white to pale yellow blossoms with distinctive spurred petals. It prefers well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in open grasslands or rocky areas.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
