Hypericum japonicum var. calyculatum

Hypericum japonicum var. calyculatum, commonly known as the Japanese St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical name reflects its origin and distinctive floral structure.
