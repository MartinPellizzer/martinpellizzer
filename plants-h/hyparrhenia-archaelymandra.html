Hyparrhenia archaelymandra

Hyparrhenia archaelymandra, commonly known as the African grass or red grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets, which are adapted for wind pollination. Hyparrhenia archaelymandra thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
