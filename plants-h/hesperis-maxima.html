Hesperis maxima

Hesperis maxima, commonly known as the sweet rocket, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while its stems are upright and branching.

Hesperis maxima is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
