Hippeastrum pulverulentum

Hippeastrum pulverulentum, commonly known as the dusty hippeastrum, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its striking, large, showy flowers. The plant produces bell-shaped blooms in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which appear in late summer to early autumn. Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, dusty coating, giving it its common name.

Hippeastrum pulverulentum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated as a decorative plant.
