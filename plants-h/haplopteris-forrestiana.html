Haplopteris forrestiana, commonly known as the Forest Fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, intricate fronds that are typically small and closely spaced. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.
