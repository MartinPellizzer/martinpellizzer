Honckenya peploides var. latifolia

Honckenya peploides var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its mat-forming growth habit, with slender, sprawling stems that root at the nodes. The leaves are typically broad, lance-shaped, and covered in fine, silky hairs, which help reduce water loss in dry conditions. It produces small, pale green or yellowish flowers in clusters, which are often inconspicuous and borne on short pedicels.

This plant is well adapted to sandy soils and is often found in desert environments where it plays a role in stabilizing loose soil.
