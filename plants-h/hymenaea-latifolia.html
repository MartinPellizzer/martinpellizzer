Hymenaea latifolia

Hymenaea latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, ridged bark and large, palmately lobed leaves. The tree produces heavy, woody pods that contain flat, winged seeds. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay.

Hymenaea latifolia plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
