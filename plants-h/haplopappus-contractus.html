Haplopappus contractus

Haplopappus contractus, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers clustered in dense, spherical heads, which are surrounded by bracts. It is adapted to arid environments, with a deep root system that helps it survive in sandy soils.
