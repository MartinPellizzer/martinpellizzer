Hyptis conferta var. angustata

Hyptis conferta var. angustata, commonly known as narrowleaf hyptis, is a perennial shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to violet. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
