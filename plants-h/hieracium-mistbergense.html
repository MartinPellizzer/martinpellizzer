Hieracium mistbergense

Hieracium mistbergense, commonly known as the Mistberg hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, producing yellow disk flowers surrounded by pale green to reddish bracts.

Hieracium mistbergense is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it thrives in cooler, temperate climates.
