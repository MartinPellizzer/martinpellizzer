Hoarea fissifolia

Hoarea fissifolia, commonly known as the fissured grass.

It is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa, characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowering spikes are usually small and inconspicuous, producing tiny seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Hoarea fissifolia is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
