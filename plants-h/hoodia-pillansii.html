Hoodia pillansii

Hoodia pillansii, commonly known as the desert cucumber, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, spineless stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by green, cucumber-like fruits. Hoodia pillansii is adapted to extreme heat and drought, with a deep root system that enables it to access groundwater.

Its unique morphology and resilience make it an important species in its ecological niche.
