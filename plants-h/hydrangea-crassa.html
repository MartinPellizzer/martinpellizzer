Hydrangea crassa

Hydrangea crassa, commonly known as the rough hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from white to pink, depending on soil pH. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically broad, ovate, and have a rough texture, giving it its common name.

Hydrangea crassa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.
