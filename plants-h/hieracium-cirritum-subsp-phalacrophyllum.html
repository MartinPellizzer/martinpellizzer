Hieracium cirritum subsp. phalacrophyllum

Hieracium cirritum subsp.

phalacrophyllum, commonly known as the black-centred hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellowish-green flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with a rough texture, and the flowers are clustered in a single head.
