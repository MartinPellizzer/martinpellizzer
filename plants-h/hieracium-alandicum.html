Hieracium alandicum

Hieracium alandicum, commonly known as the Aland hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to northern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks. It produces yellowish to reddish flowers with a distinctive central disk, and its leaves are usually lance-shaped with rough textures.

Hieracium alandicum is also known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain habitats.
