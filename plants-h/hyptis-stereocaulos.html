Hyptis stereocaulos

Hyptis stereocaulos, commonly known as the woolly hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.

Its aromatic leaves and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardens and herbal uses.
