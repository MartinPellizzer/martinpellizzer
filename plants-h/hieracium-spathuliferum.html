Hieracium spathuliferum

Hieracium spathuliferum, commonly known as the spoon-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are spoon-shaped, hence the common name, and are covered in fine hairs.

It produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators.
