Hieracium subspeciosum subsp. crinisquamum

Hieracium subspeciosum subsp.

crinisquamum, commonly known as the silky hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies.

The plant features hairy stems and leaves, with bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
