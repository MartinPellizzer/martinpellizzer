Hyptis densifolia

Hyptis densifolia, commonly known as Mexican mint or bee plant, is a perennial herb native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or blue, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially sunny environments.

Hyptis densifolia is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
