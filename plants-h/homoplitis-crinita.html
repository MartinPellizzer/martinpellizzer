Homoplitis crinita

Homoplitis crinita, commonly known as the hairy leavenworthia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in woodland areas.
