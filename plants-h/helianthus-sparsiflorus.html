Helianthus sparsiflorus

Helianthus sparsiflorus, commonly known as the sparseflower sunflower, is a species of flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, where it typically grows in open woodlands and prairies. The plant produces yellow ray flowers surrounded by a disk of tubular florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Helianthus sparsiflorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
