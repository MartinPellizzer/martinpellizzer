Heterosmilax erythrantha

Heterosmilax erythrantha, commonly known as the red-flowered smilacaceous vine, is a perennial climbing plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Smilacaceae and is characterized by its twining stems and showy, red or orange flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
