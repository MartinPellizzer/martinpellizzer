Holarrhena malaccensis

Holarrhena malaccensis, commonly known as the Malacca tree or Indian saffron, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Rubiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that give way to orange-red, fleshy fruits containing numerous seeds. It is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly the alkaloids found in its seeds, which have been used in traditional medicine.

The tree is also appreciated for its ornamental value and is often planted in gardens and along roadsides.
