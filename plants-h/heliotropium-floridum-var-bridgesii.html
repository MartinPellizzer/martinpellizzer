Heliotropium floridum var. bridgesii

Heliotropium floridum var. bridgesii, commonly known as the Florida heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing annual herb with hairy stems and opposite leaves that are typically lance-shaped. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, often with a violet hue. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies.

The species is noted for its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed areas.
