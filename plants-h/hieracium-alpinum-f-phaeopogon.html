Hieracium alpinum f. phaeopogon

Hieracium alpinum f. phaeopogon, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a woolly texture. Its flowering stems bear yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is adapted to harsh mountain environments, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils at high altitudes.
