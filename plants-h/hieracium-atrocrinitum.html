Hieracium atrocrinitum

Hieracium atrocrinitum, commonly known as the black-hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are hairy and often have a dark green color, while the flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disk.

Hieracium atrocrinitum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
