Heptapleurum longicaudatum

Heptapleurum longicaudatum, commonly known as the long-tailed holly, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are typically elliptical and have a glossy surface. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
