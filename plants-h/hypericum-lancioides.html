Hypericum lancioides

Hypericum lancioides, commonly known as narrow-leaved St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Hypericaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. It blooms from June to August, producing clusters of four to six flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hypericum lancioides is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
