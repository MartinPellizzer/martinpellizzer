Hypericum sessiliflorum

Hypericum sessiliflorum, commonly known as the sessile-flower St. John's wort, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, branched stems covered in yellow-orange flowers that bloom from June to August. The plant has opposite, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its medicinal properties are attributed to the presence of hypericin and pseudohypericin, which are responsible for its traditional use in treating mild depression.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in meadows and woodland edges.
