Hymenochilus confertus

Hymenochilus confertus, commonly known as the dense-lipped orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit and its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often arranged in a dense cluster. Its leaves are typically thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in its natural habitat.

Hymenochilus confertus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique floral structure.
