Hieracium percnophylloides

Hieracium percnophylloides, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is considered a potential weed in some regions.
