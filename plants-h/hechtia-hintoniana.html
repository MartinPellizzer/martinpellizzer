Hechtia hintoniana

Hechtia hintoniana, commonly known as the Hinton's hechtia, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is native to Mexico.

It is a small, terrestrial bromeliad that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features rosettes of narrow, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a cluster of small, tubular flowers that are usually pale yellow or greenish-white.

Hechtia hintoniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of rare plants.
