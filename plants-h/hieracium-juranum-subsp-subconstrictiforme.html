Hieracium juranum subsp. subconstrictiforme

Hieracium juranum subsp.

subconstrictiforme, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and bright yellow daisy-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its subspecies name, subconstrictiforme, refers to the constricted, narrow shape of its stems.

It is an herbaceous perennial that blooms from late spring to early summer.
