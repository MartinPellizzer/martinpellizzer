Hieracium subcoactile

Hieracium subcoactile, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers, which are made up of numerous tiny florets arranged in a disk. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It has basal leaves that are heart-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

Hieracium subcoactile is an herbaceous perennial that spreads through rhizomes and is known for its aggressive growth habit.
