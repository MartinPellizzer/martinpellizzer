Hypericum anagalloides var. pumilum

Hypericum anagalloides var. pumilum, commonly known as the dwarf St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, red-orange berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a suitable plant for native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
