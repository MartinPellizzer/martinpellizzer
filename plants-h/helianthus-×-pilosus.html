Helianthus × pilosus

Helianthus × pilosus, commonly known as the woolly sunflower, is a hybrid species within the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, silver-gray foliage and bright yellow ray flowers that resemble those of common sunflowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sandy soils, preferring full sun exposure. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.
