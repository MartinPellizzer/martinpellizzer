Hieracium crassiglandulosum

Hieracium crassiglandulosum, commonly known as the broad-glandular hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellow, daisy-like flowers and hairy, often reddish stems. Native to Europe, it thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins and glandular hairs that give it its common name.

It is often found in temperate climates and is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes.
