Hypericum pseudopetiolatum var. kiusianum

Hypericum pseudopetiolatum var. kiusianum, commonly known as the Kiusianum St. John's Wort, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to Japan.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has small, elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
