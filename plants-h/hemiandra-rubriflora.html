Hemiandra rubriflora

Hemiandra rubriflora, commonly known as the red-flowered hemiandra, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, and it produces small, woody fruits.

Hemiandra rubriflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
