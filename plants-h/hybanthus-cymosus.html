Hybanthus cymosus

Hybanthus cymosus, commonly known as the clustered hybanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a member of the Morning Glory family (Convolvulaceae) and is characterized by its small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain areas.
