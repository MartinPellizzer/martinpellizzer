Hemionitis lendigera

Hemionitis lendigera, commonly known as the rock fern, is a unique vascular plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperature fluctuations. Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, with spores produced in clusters on the underside of the fronds.

Hemionitis lendigera plays a minor but ecologically significant role in stabilizing soil in its harsh habitat.
