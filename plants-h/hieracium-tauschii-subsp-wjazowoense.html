Hieracium tauschii subsp.

wjazowoense, commonly known as the Wjazowoense hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering heads.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. Its yellow flowers are surrounded by pale green or white ray florets, and it produces small, oval achenes as its fruit.

It is a relatively rare subspecies with limited geographic distribution.
