Hedycarya australasica var. angustifolia

Hedycarya australasica var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hedycarya, is a shrub native to Australia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in spring. It is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural use.
