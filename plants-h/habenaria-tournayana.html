Habenaria tournayana

Habenaria tournayana, commonly known as the Tournay's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Africa and Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, greenish-yellow or white flowers with a distinctive lip that is often spotted or streaked. It prefers shaded, moist habitats and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
