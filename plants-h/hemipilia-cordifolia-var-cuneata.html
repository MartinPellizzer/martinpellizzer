Hemipilia cordifolia var. cuneata

Hemipilia cordifolia var. cuneata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf heartleaf orchid, is a herbaceous perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, often in mountainous regions. Its flowers are usually pale green or white with a distinctive lip that is often spotted or marked.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
