Hypericum cassium

Hypericum cassium, commonly known as yellow St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the genus Hypericum in the family Clusiaceae, characterized by its bright yellow flowers and woody stems. The plant typically grows in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides. Its flowers bloom in late summer, producing small, clustered blooms that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Hypericum cassium is also known for its medicinal properties, though it is less commonly used than other members of the genus.
