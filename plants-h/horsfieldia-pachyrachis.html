Horsfieldia pachyrachis

Horsfieldia pachyrachis, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that often have a distinctive violet or purple hue. The plant typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments, relying on its aerial roots for nutrient absorption. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native habitat.

Horsfieldia pachyrachis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
