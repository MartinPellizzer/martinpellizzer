Hemianemia glareosa

Hemianemia glareosa, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Hemianemia glareosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
