Hedyotis capitellata var. glabra

Hedyotis capitellata var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth inchplant, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Asia.

It features small, opposite leaves that are typically smooth and glossy, with a dark green coloration. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in shaded gardens.
