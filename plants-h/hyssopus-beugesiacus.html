Hyssopus beugesiacus

Hyssopus beugesiacus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hyssop, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped, gray-green leaves. The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Hyssopus beugesiacus is also valued for its aromatic properties and traditional medicinal uses.
