Helianthemum cinereum subsp. argenteum

Helianthemum cinereum subsp.

argenteum, commonly known as silver thyme or silver-centred rock rose, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe. It features dense clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer.

The leaves are typically covered in a fine, silvery or grayish-white pubescence, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, sunny soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
