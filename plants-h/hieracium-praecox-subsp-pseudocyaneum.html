Hieracium praecox subsp. pseudocyaneum

Hieracium praecox subsp.

pseudocyaneum, commonly known as the purple hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its flowering heads composed of yellow disc florets surrounded by pale purple to white ray florets.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. It has basal leaves that are lanceolate and often covered in fine hairs, and its stems are upright with a reddish tinge.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
