Hibbertia hypericoides var. typica

Hibbertia hypericoides var. typica, commonly known as the yellow wattlevine, is a climbing shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has slender, woody stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are tubular with five lobes and are typically borne in clusters.

This species is well adapted to sandy soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in suitable climates.
