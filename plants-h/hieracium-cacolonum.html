Hieracium cacolonum

Hieracium cacolonum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm. Its leaves are narrow and often have a hairy surface, while its yellow flowers bloom from June to September.

Hieracium cacolonum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
