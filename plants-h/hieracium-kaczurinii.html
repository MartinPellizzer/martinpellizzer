Hieracium kaczurinii

Hieracium kaczurinii, commonly known as the Kaczurinii hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and meadows, preferring well-drained soils. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Hieracium kaczurinii is known for its hairy stems and leaves, which are often covered in fine, soft pubescence.
