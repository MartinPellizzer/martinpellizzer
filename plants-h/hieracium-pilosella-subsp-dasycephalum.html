Hieracium pilosella subsp. dasycephalum

Hieracium pilosella subsp.

dasycephalum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

The plant features a basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in a flat-topped inflorescence. Its flowers are surrounded by a dense, hairy involucrum, which gives it its common name.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
