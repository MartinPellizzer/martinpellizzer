Hieracium oritrephes

Hieracium oritrephes, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. It features upright stems with alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The showy yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
