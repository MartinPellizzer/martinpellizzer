Hibiscus tiliaceus subsp. pernambucensis

Hibiscus tiliaceus subsp.

pernambucensis, commonly known as the Pernambuco hibiscus, is a shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly Brazil. It is characterized by its rough, often fissured bark and large, palmately lobed leaves.

The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom throughout the year in some regions. It thrives in coastal and semi-arid environments, often found along riverbanks and in disturbed soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local communities.
