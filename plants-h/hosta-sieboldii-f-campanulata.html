Hosta sieboldii f. campanulata

Hosta sieboldii f. campanulata, commonly known as the bell-shaped hosta, is a cultivar of the Hosta genus native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, waxy, and often variegated leaves that display a mix of green, white, and yellow hues. The plant produces upright, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or white during the summer months. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental foliage and is a popular choice for shady garden landscapes.
