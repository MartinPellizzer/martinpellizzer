Hibiscus maculatus subsp. maculatus

Hibiscus maculatus subsp.

maculatus, commonly known as the spotted hibiscus, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and red.

The leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
