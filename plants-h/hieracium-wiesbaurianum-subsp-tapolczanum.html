Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp. tapolczanum

Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp.

tapolczanum, commonly known as the Hungarian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

The plant features basal leaves that are lanceolate and hairy, with a rosette structure. Its flowering heads are composite, consisting of yellow disk flowers surrounded by pale yellow to brownish ray florets.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and is often found in moist or calcareous soils.
