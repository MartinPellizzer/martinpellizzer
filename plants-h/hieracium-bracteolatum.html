Hieracium bracteolatum

Hieracium bracteolatum, commonly known as the bracteolate hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering stems that bear yellowish to reddish composite flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with hairy surfaces, and the flowers are arranged in a single head.

Hieracium bracteolatum is often found in temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.
