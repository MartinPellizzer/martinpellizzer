Hieracium pseudexpansum

Hieracium pseudexpansum, commonly known as the false spearthistle, is a species of flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and is characterized by its spiny, erect stems and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a composite head.

Hieracium pseudexpansum is often considered a weed but has ecological value by supporting pollinators.
