Hypericum enodes

Hypericum enodes, commonly known as the golden-stemmed St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, woody stems covered in yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It prefers sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky habitats.

Hypericum enodes is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its antidepressant and anti-inflammatory effects.
