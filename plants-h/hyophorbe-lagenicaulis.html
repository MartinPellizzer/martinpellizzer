Hyophorbe lagenicaulis

Hyophorbe lagenicaulis, commonly known as the giant poinsettia, is a striking tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, colorful bracts that resemble the leaves of a poinsettia, which are actually modified leaves rather than flowers. The plant has a thick, woody stem with a swollen base, and its true flowers are small and inconspicuous, clustered in the axils of the bracts. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value, particularly during the holiday season.
