Hieracium dentatum subsp. subnudum

Hieracium dentatum subsp.

subnudum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to orange flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually hairy, with a serrated margin, and the stems are often reddish in color.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
