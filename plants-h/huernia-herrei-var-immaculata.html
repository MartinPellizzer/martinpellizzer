Huernia herrei var. immaculata

Huernia herrei var. immaculata, commonly known as the White Ghost Cactus, is a succulent native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems that are typically pale green to white, often with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the warmer months. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its stems.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
