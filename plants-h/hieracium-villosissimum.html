Hieracium villosissimum

Hieracium villosissimum, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads made up of numerous small florets. The plant features hairy, often reddish stems and basal rosettes of spoon-shaped, hairy leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Hieracium villosissimum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
