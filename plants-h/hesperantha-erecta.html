Hesperantha erecta

Hesperantha erecta, commonly known as the upright bell orchid, is a South African native plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette.

Hesperantha erecta is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
