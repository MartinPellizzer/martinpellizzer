Helianthus lanceolatus

Helianthus lanceolatus, commonly known as the lanceleaf sunflower, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to the southeastern United States, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and wetlands. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers with a dark center, typical of the genus Helianthus. It is an annual or biennial plant that blooms from summer to fall.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for wetland restoration and ecological studies.
