Hieracium winkleri

Hieracium winkleri, commonly known as the Winkler's hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are usually basal, alternate, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range in color from yellow to orange.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Hieracium winkleri is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
