Haploclathra verticillata

Haploclathra verticillata, commonly known as the clustered spurge, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are borne on short pedicels. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
