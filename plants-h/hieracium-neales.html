Hieracium neales

Hieracium neales, commonly known as the Neales' hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters. The plant is native to Europe and is often found in meadows and grassy areas. It has a taproot system and is perennial, with a life cycle that includes both vegetative and reproductive stages.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
