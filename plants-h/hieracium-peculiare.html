Hieracium peculiare

Hieracium peculiare, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow daisy-like flowers and hairy, often reddish stems. Native to Europe, it has been introduced to other regions and is considered an invasive species in some areas. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can form dense colonies.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
