Hieracium origanifolium

Hieracium origanifolium, commonly known as the orange hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with reddish or orange hues, giving it its common name. It produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Hieracium origanifolium is typically found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, where it thrives in full sun to partial shade.
