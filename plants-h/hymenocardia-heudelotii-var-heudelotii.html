Hymenocardia heudelotii var. heudelotii

Hymenocardia heudelotii var. heudelotii, commonly known as the white sand tree, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy, dark green upper surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
