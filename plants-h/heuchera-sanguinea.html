Heuchera sanguinea

Heuchera sanguinea, commonly known as crimson coral bells, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features attractive, velvety foliage that ranges from deep red to purple hues, with some varieties displaying variegated patterns. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, or red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to provide color throughout the growing season.
