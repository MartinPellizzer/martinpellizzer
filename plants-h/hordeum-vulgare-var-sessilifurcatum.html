Hordeum vulgare var. sessilifurcatum

Hordeum vulgare var. sessilifurcatum, commonly known as the sessile awned barley, is a variant of barley characterized by its unique morphological features.

This plant is typically found in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets with sessile lemmas and awns, distinguishing it from other barley varieties. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often used in ecological studies due to its adaptability.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a genetic resource for breeding programs and its resilience to environmental stress.
