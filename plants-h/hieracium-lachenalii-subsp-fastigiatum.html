Hieracium lachenalii subsp. fastigiatum

Hieracium lachenalii subsp.

fastigiatum, commonly known as the upright hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically has basal leaves that are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while the upper leaves are smaller and more upright. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and sometimes endangered species in parts of its range.
