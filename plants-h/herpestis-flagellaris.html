Herpestis flagellaris

Herpestis flagellaris, commonly known as the flagellate herb, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, whip-like stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are usually opposite and have a smooth, waxy texture.

The species is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
