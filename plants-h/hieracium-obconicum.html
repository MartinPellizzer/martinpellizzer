Hieracium obconicum

Hieracium obconicum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom from June to September.

Hieracium obconicum is known for its ability to spread rapidly through rhizomes, making it an invasive species in some regions.
