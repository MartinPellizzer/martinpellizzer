Hohenbergia tetaensis

Hohenbergia tetaensis, commonly known as the Tetaensis bromeliad, is a species native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect water. The plant produces striking inflorescences with colorful, tubular flowers that attract pollinators. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
