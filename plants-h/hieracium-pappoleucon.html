Hieracium pappoleucon

Hieracium pappoleucon, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in the summer. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils. It has rough, hairy stems and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds in its fluffy flower heads.

Hieracium pappoleucon is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
