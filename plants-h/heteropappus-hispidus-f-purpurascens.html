Heteropappus hispidus f. purpurascens

Heteropappus hispidus f. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-rayed horsebrush, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant features yellow-orange composite flowers that are surrounded by pale purple or pink bracts, giving it its distinctive appearance. Its leaves are typically grayish-green and covered in fine, soft hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in desert or semi-desert habitats.
