Hieracium pseudostorliense

Hieracium pseudostorliense, commonly known as the false yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in dense clusters.

It is known for its aggressive growth and can become invasive in certain regions.
