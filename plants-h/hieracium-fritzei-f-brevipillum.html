Hieracium fritzei f. brevipillum

Hieracium fritzei f. brevipillum, commonly known as the short-stemmed hawkweed, is a botanical variant of the hawkweed genus Hieracium.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and shorter stems compared to other forms of H. fritzei. The plant typically features yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are usually hairy and lance-shaped, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This form is native to specific regions in Europe and is often found in grassy or disturbed habitats.
