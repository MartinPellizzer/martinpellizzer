Hieracium murorum subsp. pertenuatum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

pertenuatum, commonly known as the slender mouse-ear hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with hairy stems and small, yellowish to reddish flower heads.

The leaves are typically linear to lanceolate, often with a rough or hairy texture. It thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows.

This subspecies is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
