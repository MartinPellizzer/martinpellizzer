Hibiscus furcatus

Hibiscus furcatus, commonly known as the forked hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink or red. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. Hibiscus furcatus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
