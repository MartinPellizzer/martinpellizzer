Hypericum bocconei

Hypericum bocconei, commonly known as the orange-stemmed St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and reddish-orange stems, which are particularly striking in the summer months. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are opposite and oval-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Hypericum bocconei is a perennial shrub that blooms from June to August, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
