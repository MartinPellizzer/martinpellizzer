Hernandia albiflora

Hernandia albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered hernandia, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom during the wet season. Its fruit is a capsule containing numerous small, hard seeds.

Hernandia albiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
