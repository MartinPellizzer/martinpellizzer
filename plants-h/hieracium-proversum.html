Hieracium proversum

Hieracium proversum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow flowers in a dense, flat-topped inflorescence. The plant is typically found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed sites across Europe and parts of Asia. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a rough texture, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Hieracium proversum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting pollinators.
