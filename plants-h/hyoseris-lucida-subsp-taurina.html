Hyoseris lucida subsp. taurina

Hyoseris lucida subsp.

taurina, commonly known as the Taurine sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

It is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
