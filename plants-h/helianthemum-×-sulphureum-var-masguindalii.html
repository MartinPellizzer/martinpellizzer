Helianthemum × sulphureum var. masguindalii

Helianthemum × sulphureum var. masguindalii, commonly known as the golden rockrose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in full sun conditions. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

The variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to withstand coastal winds and salt spray.
