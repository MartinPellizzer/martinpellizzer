Hieracium schmidtii subsp. pseudodontotrichum

Hieracium schmidtii subsp.

pseudodontotrichum, commonly known as the Schmidt's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy basal rosette.

The plant features slender, hairy stems and serrated leaves, adapted to thrive in alpine and arid environments. Its flowers are typically yellow, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces small, dry fruits known as cypselae.

This subspecies is noted for its ecological significance in stabilizing soil in harsh, high-altitude habitats.
