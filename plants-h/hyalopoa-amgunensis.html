Hyalopoa amgunensis

Hyalopoa amgunensis, commonly known as the Abugun grass, is a perennial grass species native to the arid and semi-arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Hyalopoa amgunensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for livestock in its native habitat.
