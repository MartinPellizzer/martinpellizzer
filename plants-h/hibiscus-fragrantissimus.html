Hibiscus fragrantissimus

Hibiscus fragrantissimus, commonly known as the sweet-scented hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces a distinctive, fragrant scent, which is most noticeable in the evening. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and often branched.

Hibiscus fragrantissimus is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and pleasant aroma.
