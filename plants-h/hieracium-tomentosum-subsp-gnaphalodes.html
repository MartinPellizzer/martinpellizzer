Hieracium tomentosum subsp. gnaphalodes

Hieracium tomentosum subsp.

gnaphalodes, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. Its leaves are covered in dense white tomentum, giving it a woolly appearance.

The flowers are small, yellow, and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which attracts various pollinators.
