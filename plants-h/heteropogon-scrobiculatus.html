Heteropogon scrobiculatus

Heteropogon scrobiculatus, commonly known as the rough fescue, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, spikelet-like flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grassy areas.

Heteropogon scrobiculatus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
