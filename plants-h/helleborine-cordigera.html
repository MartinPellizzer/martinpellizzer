Helleborine cordigera

Helleborine cordigera, commonly known as the broad-leaf helleborine, is a perennial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, broadly elliptical leaves and produces delicate, greenish-white flowers with a faintly fragrant scent. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in cool, humid environments. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the rich, organic soil in its habitat.

Helleborine cordigera is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
