Hieracium rauzense subsp. bifidellum

Hieracium rauzense subsp.

bifidellum, commonly known as the bifidellum hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dandelion-like yellow flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils. It produces a basal rosette of leaves and has a short, upright flowering stem.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
