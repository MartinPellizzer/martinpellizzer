Humboltia strupifolia

Humboltia strupifolia, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas and chaparral habitats. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

Humboltia strupifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
