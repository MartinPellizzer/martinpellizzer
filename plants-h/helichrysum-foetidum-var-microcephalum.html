Helichrysum foetidum var. microcephalum

Helichrysum foetidum var. microcephalum, commonly known as the stinking everlasting or dwarf everlasting, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, densely packed yellow to orange flowers that resemble tiny sunflowers, blooming from late spring to early autumn. The plant has aromatic, silver-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its compact size and long-lasting blooms, it is a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
