Heteropappus alberti

Heteropappus alberti, commonly known as the Albert's heteropappus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive bracts. The plant features a low-growing, mat-forming structure with silver-green foliage that helps it conserve water in harsh environments. Its yellow flowers bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Heteropappus alberti is adapted to thrive in sandy soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.
