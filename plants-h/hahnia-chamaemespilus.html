Hahnia chamaemespilus

Hahnia chamaemespilus, commonly known as the creeping sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring. Its root system is shallow but extensive, allowing it to thrive in sandy or rocky soils.

Hahnia chamaemespilus is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
