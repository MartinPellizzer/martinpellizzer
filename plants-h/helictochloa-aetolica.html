Helictochloa aetolica

Helictochloa aetolica, commonly known as the Aeolian grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, blue-green foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Helictochloa aetolica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
