Hieracium misaucinum subsp. gasterenenese

Hieracium misaucinum subsp.

gasterenenese, commonly known as the Gasterenese hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are usually lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head.

The subspecies is distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and subtle morphological differences from other members of the *Hieracium* genus.
