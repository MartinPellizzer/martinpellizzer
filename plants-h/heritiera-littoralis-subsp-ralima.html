Heritiera littoralis subsp. ralima

Heritiera littoralis subsp.

ralima, commonly known as the coastal heritiera, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its woody stems and simple, elliptic leaves with a smooth upper surface.

The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in sandy or muddy soils along shorelines and is adapted to saline and tidal conditions.

This subspecies plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes and supporting local biodiversity.
