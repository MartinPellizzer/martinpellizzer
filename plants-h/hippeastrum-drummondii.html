Hippeastrum drummondii

Hippeastrum drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's amaryllis, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant produces strap-like leaves that are typically broad and slightly hairy. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking blooms and relatively easy care requirements.

Hippeastrum drummondii is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
