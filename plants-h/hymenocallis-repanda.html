Hymenocallis repanda

Hymenocallis repanda, commonly known as the spider lily, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, funnel-shaped white flowers with a yellow throat and prominent stamen. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring strap-like, dark green leaves. Its bulbs are underground storage organs that support the plant's growth and reproduction.

Hymenocallis repanda thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun exposure.
