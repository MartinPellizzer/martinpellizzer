Hieracium arpadianum subsp. arpadianum

Hieracium arpadianum subsp.

arpadianum, commonly known as the Carpathian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Carpathian region. It typically grows in meadows, forest edges, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features bright yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, with ray florets that are elongated and often curved. Its leaves are alternate, rough to the touch, and have a serrated margin.

This subspecies is characterized by its robust growth and is often found in temperate climates with moderate rainfall.
