Hieracium glaucinum subsp. pallidulum

Hieracium glaucinum subsp.

pallidulum, commonly known as the pale hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering heads that produce yellow to orange composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring temperate climates. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a bluish-green hue, while its stems are upright and often reddish in color.

The species is noted for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
