Hesperis myriophylla

Hesperis myriophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved scabious, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

Hesperis myriophylla is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
