Hirtella media

Hirtella media, commonly known as the hairy hirtella, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It features dense, rounded foliage with leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental qualities in tropical gardens.

Hirtella media is also notable for its role in supporting local biodiversity and ecological balance.
