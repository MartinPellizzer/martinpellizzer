Hieracium sylvaticum subsp. acrogymnon

Hieracium sylvaticum subsp.

acrogymnon, commonly known as the woodland hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks. It has basal leaves that are broadly ovate with rough textures, and its stems are hairy and often reddish in color.

This subspecies is known for its ecological importance as a food source for various pollinators and insects.
