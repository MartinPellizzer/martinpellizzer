Helictotrichon convolutum

Helictotrichon convolutum, commonly known as curlleaf bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and rolled, curling leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and grasslands. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny florets.

Helictotrichon convolutum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
