Haageocereus setosus

Haageocereus setosus, commonly known as the spiny night bloomer, is a cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in sharp, spiny tubercles. The plant produces large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and blooming habit, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and botanical gardens.
