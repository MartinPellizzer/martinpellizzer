Hemilophia rockii

Hemilophia rockii, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a flowering plant native to the rocky habitats of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, often epiphytic growth habit. The plant produces small, intricate flowers with a distinctive spurred lip, typical of many orchid species. Its leaves are usually narrow and adapted to conserve water in arid environments.

Hemilophia rockii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in specialized horticultural settings.
