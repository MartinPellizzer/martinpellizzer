Heterotheca pumila

Heterotheca pumila, commonly known as the dwarf blueweed, is a small, herbaceous annual plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Heterotheca pumila is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
