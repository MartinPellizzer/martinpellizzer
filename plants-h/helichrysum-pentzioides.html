Helichrysum pentzioides

Helichrysum pentzioides, commonly known as the golden everlasting or yellow helichrysum, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, fluffy yellow flowers that resemble small sunbursts, which bloom in spring and summer. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with silver-gray foliage that adds visual interest even when not in bloom. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Helichrysum pentzioides is also valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in herbal remedies.
