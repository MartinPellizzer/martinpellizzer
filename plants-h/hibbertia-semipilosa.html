Hibbertia semipilosa

Hibbertia semipilosa, commonly known as the silky hibbertia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its hairy, ascending stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with red markings, typically blooming in spring. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Hibbertia semipilosa is a low-growing shrub that is well adapted to the harsh conditions of its native habitat.
