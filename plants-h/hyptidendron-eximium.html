Hyptidendron eximium

Hyptidendron eximium, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with trifoliate or pinnate structures, and its bark is often smooth with a reddish hue. It is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular ornamental species.

The tree is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
