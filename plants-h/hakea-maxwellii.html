Hakea maxwellii

Hakea maxwellii, commonly known as the Maxwell's hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves and produces cylindrical, woody cones that contain its seeds. The plant is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments. It flowers in spring, producing bright yellow flowers that attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.

Hakea maxwellii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
