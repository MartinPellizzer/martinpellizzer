Hemionitis decurrens

Hemionitis decurrens, commonly known as the running hemionite, is a unique aquatic plant native to the wetlands of Asia.

It is characterized by its creeping rhizomes that spread along the water's surface, forming dense mats. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically submerged or floating, with a distinctive waxy coating. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by wind rather than insects.

Hemionitis decurrens is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ability to stabilize aquatic ecosystems.
