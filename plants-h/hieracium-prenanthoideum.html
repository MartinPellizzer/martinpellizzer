Hieracium prenanthoideum

Hieracium prenanthoideum, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Hieracium prenanthoideum is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
