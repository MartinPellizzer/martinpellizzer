Habenaria egleriana

Habenaria egleriana, commonly known as the Egler's orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the Philippines.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a sympodial growth habit, characterized by its rhizomatous structure. The plant produces inflorescences that bear small, greenish-white flowers with a distinctive three-lobed lip. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.
