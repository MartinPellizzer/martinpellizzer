Heliconia aureovittata

Heliconia aureovittata, commonly known as the golden stripe heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, brightly colored inflorescences. The plant features elongated, fleshy bracts that enclose its small, clustered flowers. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

Heliconia aureovittata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical landscapes.
