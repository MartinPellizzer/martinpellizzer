Hieracium buxicola

Hieracium buxicola, commonly known as the boxwood hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Hieracium buxicola produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, followed by small, dry fruits called achenes.
