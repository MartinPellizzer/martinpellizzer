Hieracium pyrenaicum var. acutesquamatum

Hieracium pyrenaicum var. acutesquamatum, commonly known as the Pyrenean hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Pyrenees mountain region.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It has a low-growing habit with a creeping rootstock and produces numerous small, herbaceous stems.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to the harsh environmental conditions of its native alpine environment.
