Haumaniastrum coriaceum

Haumaniastrum coriaceum, commonly known as the desert holly, is a succulent shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are adapted to store water and reduce transpiration in hot, dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a subtle ornamental value. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in nutrient-poor soils.

Overall, Haumaniastrum coriaceum exemplifies the botanical adaptations of desert flora to extreme conditions.
