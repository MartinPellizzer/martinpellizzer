Hyptis proteoides

Hyptis proteoides, commonly known as the Mexican mint marigold, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and medicinal plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, clustered flowers are typically white or pale blue, blooming in summer.

Hyptis proteoides is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
