Hieracium laevigatum subsp. perlongum

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

perlongum, commonly known as the long-stemmed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped, rough to the touch, and often have a whitish or pale green coloration.

The flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, producing small seeds that are dispersed by wind.
