Huperzia christii

Huperzia christii, commonly known as the Christ's huperzia, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its simple, linear leaves that are often arranged in a rosette-like pattern. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its spore-bearing structures, called sporangia, are clustered on the underside of the leaves.

Huperzia christii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
