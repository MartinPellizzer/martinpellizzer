Hieracium saxifragum subsp. subonosmoides

Hieracium saxifragum subsp.

subonosmoides, commonly known as the subalpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it tolerates cold and harsh environmental conditions.

The plant features basal leaves that are often lobed or divided, and its flowering stems bear yellow to orange composite flowers. It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and often protected species in its native range.
