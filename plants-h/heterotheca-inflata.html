Heterotheca inflata

Heterotheca inflata, commonly known as the tansy flower or blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features yellow disc flowers surrounded by blue to purple ray flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Heterotheca inflata typically grows in dry, open habitats like prairies and roadsides, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter.

Its hairy stems and leaves are adapted to withstand arid conditions, making it a resilient species in its native range.
