Helianthemum glaucum f. stoechadifolium

Helianthemum glaucum f. stoechadifolium, commonly known as the Stoechadifolium form of the hoary whitlow grass, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to the British Isles.

It features small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The leaves are gray-green, covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive, woolly appearance. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as coastal cliffs and sandy soils.

This variety is noted for its compact growth and is often used in horticultural settings for its ornamental value.
