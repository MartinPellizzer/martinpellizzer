Hippophae neurocarpa

Hippophae neurocarpa, commonly known as the sandthorn, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, yellow-orange berries that are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. The plant has small, needle-like leaves and produces yellow flowers in the spring. It is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots that help it survive drought conditions.

Its ecological importance includes soil stabilization and providing food for wildlife.
