Hydrangea alternifolia

Hydrangea alternifolia, commonly known as the mountain hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy flower clusters that can range in color from blue to pink depending on soil pH. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and prefers partial to full shade. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a serrated edge.

Hydrangea alternifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its seasonal blooms.
