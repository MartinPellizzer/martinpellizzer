Hypolaena graminifolia

Hypolaena graminifolia, commonly known as the grass-like mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees.

It has small, narrow leaves that resemble grass, and its flowers are typically white or pale green, arranged in clusters. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are dispersed by birds. It is native to various regions in Africa and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for ecological studies.
