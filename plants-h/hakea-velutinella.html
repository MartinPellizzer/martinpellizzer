Hakea velutinella

Hakea velutinella, commonly known as the silky hakea, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in winter, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions.

Hakea velutinella is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
