Hydrangea villosa var. delicatula

Hydrangea villosa var. delicatula, commonly known as the silky hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its finely hairy stems and leaves that are typically oval to elliptic in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage turns a striking red or bronze color in the fall, adding ornamental value.

It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
