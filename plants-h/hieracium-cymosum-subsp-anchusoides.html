Hieracium cymosum subsp. anchusoides

Hieracium cymosum subsp.

anchusoides, commonly known as the anchusoid hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has a creeping growth habit with stolons that allow it to spread vegetatively.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more robust stems and broader leaves compared to other members of the *Hieracium* genus.
