Hieracium rubellum subsp. erythrosabinum

Hieracium rubellum subsp.

erythrosabinum, commonly known as the red-hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, reddish stems and bright yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

It is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid spread and competitive growth habit.
