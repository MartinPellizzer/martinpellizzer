Huperzia tetrasticha

Huperzia tetrasticha, commonly known as the four-veined huperzia, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage with distinctive four-veined leaves that are typically lanceolate in shape. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia, including countries like China, India, and Indonesia. It thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest undergrowth or on rocky substrates.

Huperzia tetrasticha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage structure.
