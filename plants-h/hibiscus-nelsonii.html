Hibiscus nelsonii

Hibiscus nelsonii, commonly known as the Nelson's hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. Hibiscus nelsonii thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas within its native habitat.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
