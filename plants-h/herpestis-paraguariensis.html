Herpestis paraguariensis

Herpestis paraguariensis, commonly known as the Paraguayan arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to South America, particularly Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its upright stems and alternate, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. Its roots are the primary part used, valued for their starchy texture and culinary applications.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.
