Heterosperma pinnatum

Heterosperma pinnatum, commonly known as the featherleaf water milfoil, is a aquatic plant native to the wetlands of North America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that are arranged in a pinnate pattern, giving it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in shallow, slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its small, clustered flowers are usually white or pale pink and bloom in the summer months.

Heterosperma pinnatum plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.
