Hieracium bifidum subsp. sinuosifrons

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

sinuosifrons, commonly known as the sinuous-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves with a distinctive sinuous or wavy margin.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers that are typically arranged in a single terminal head. It is native to Central Asia and is often found in dry, rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
