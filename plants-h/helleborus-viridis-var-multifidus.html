Helleborus viridis var. multifidus

Helleborus viridis var. multifidus, commonly known as the green hellebore or woodland hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that are typically deeply lobed or divided, giving it a distinctive, fern-like appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by reddish-brown berries. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodlands and rocky soils.

Helleborus viridis var. multifidus is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
