Haplopappus racemosus var. congestus

Haplopappus racemosus var. congestus, commonly known as the clustered hairy fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and is often found in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. The plant features a low-growing, herbaceous stem with narrow, linear leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

This species is notable for its adaptability and resilience in arid environments.
