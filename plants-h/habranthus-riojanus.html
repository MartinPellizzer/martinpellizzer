Habranthus riojanus

Habranthus riojanus, commonly known as the Rioja bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or white. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded locations.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
