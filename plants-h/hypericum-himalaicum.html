Hypericum himalaicum

Hypericum himalaicum, commonly known as the Himalayan St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is a shrub that typically grows in high-altitude regions, characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and woody stems. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in rocky and alpine environments, often found in open meadows and forest edges.

Hypericum himalaicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
