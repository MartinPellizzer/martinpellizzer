Hieracium bifidum subsp. gelertii

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

gelertii, commonly known as the Welsh buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to western Europe. It typically grows in moist, calcareous grasslands and meadows, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features bright yellow flowers with five petals, and its leaves are palmately lobed with a distinctive, hairy texture. It is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often found in conservation areas due to its limited distribution.
