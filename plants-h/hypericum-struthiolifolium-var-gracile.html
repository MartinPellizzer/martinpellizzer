Hypericum struthiolifolium var. gracile

Hypericum struthiolifolium var. gracile, commonly known as the slender St. John's wort, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters along the stems. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its medicinal properties, similar to other Hypericum species, have been historically used in traditional herbal medicine.
