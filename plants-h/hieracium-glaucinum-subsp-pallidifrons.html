Hieracium glaucinum subsp. pallidifrons

Hieracium glaucinum subsp.

pallidifrons, commonly known as the pale-fronted hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm.

The plant features basal and stem leaves that are lanceolate to ovate, with a hairy surface. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a single head, and are surrounded by pale yellow to white ray florets.

The subspecies is distinguished by its pale yellow flower heads and the presence of a narrow, pale band along the central disk.
