Hirtella rotundata

Hirtella rotundata, commonly known as the round-leaved hirtella, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes plants such as eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its dense, rounded foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are typically elliptical or ovate, with a waxy texture and a slightly hairy surface.

Hirtella rotundata is often found in humid, shaded environments such as rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
