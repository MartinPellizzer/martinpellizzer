Hydrangea fulvescens var. rehderiana

Hydrangea fulvescens var. rehderiana, commonly known as the Rehder's hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that range from white to pink and red depending on soil pH. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by persistent, showy bracts. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with serrated edges, and turn yellow in autumn.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
