Heliotropium campestre

Heliotropium campestre, commonly known as the field heliotrope, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly aromatic scent. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Heliotropium campestre is also known for its medicinal properties in some traditional herbal practices.
