Hordeum jubatum f. violaceum

Hordeum jubatum f. violaceum, commonly known as the purple barley.

This variant of barley is characterized by its striking purple or violet-colored inflorescences, which distinguish it from the typical green hues of common barley. It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is native to regions of Europe and Asia. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical studies.
