Hieracium caesiopilosum

Hieracium caesiopilosum, commonly known as the blue-haired hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in a flat-topped inflorescence, while its leaves are hairy and lance-shaped.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
