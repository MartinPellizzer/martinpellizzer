Hieracium irriguiforme

Hieracium irriguiforme, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often branched.

Hieracium irriguiforme produces yellow flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
