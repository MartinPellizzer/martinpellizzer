Hymenanthes maxima

Hymenanthes maxima, commonly known as the giant sarsaparilla, is a woody shrub native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in terminal clusters. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

Hymenanthes maxima is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
