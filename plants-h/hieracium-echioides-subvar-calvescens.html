Hieracium echioides subvar. calvescens

Hieracium echioides subvar. calvescens, commonly known as the creeping hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It typically grows in moist, grassy areas and has a low, spreading growth habit. The plant features yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and its leaves are lance-shaped with hairy surfaces. It is characterized by its fibrous root system and the presence of glandular structures on its stems and leaves.

This variety is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in some agricultural settings.
