Helichrysum blandowskianum var. dichroon

Helichrysum blandowskianum var. dichroon, commonly known as the Cape gold or golden daisy, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It features small, bright yellow to orange flowers that resemble daisies, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

The flowers are also used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
