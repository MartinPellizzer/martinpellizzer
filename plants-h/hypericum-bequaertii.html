Hypericum bequaertii

Hypericum bequaertii, commonly known as the Bequaert's St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the genus Hypericum, which includes over 400 species of shrubs and herbs. The plant is characterized by its yellow flowers, which are typically arranged in clusters and bloom in late summer. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often covered in fine hairs.

Hypericum bequaertii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and ability to thrive in rocky or well-drained soils.
