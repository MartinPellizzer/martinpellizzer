Helichrysum stoechas subsp. rupicola

Helichrysum stoechas subsp.

rupicola, commonly known as the golden marguerite, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces showy, golden-yellow flower heads that resemble daisies.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
