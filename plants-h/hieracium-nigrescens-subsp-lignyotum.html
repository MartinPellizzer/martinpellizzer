Hieracium nigrescens subsp. lignyotum

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

lignyotum, commonly known as the black hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its dark green, hairy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. It has a creeping growth habit with rhizomes, allowing it to spread vegetatively.

Its flowers are usually arranged in a flat-topped cluster, and the leaves are lance-shaped with prominent veins.
