Hypericum maculatum subf. nigrum

Hypericum maculatum subf.

nigrum, commonly known as black-stemmed St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub characterized by its dark, almost black stems and yellow-orange flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
