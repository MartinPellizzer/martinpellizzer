Hyssopus tianschanicus

Hyssopus tianschanicus, commonly known as the Tian Shan hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its small, tubular flowers are usually blue or violet, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hyssopus tianschanicus is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
