Hyssopus officinalis var. canescens

Hyssopus officinalis var. canescens, commonly known as gray hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, grayish-white pubescence, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces spikes of pale blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often cultivated for its medicinal properties and ornamental value in gardens.

Hyssopus officinalis var. canescens thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and adaptable plant.
