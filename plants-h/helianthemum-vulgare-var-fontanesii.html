Helianthemum vulgare var. fontanesii

Helianthemum vulgare var. fontanesii, commonly known as the Fontanesii rockrose, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features dense clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in spring and summer. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically covered in a powdery white coating, known as a bloom. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in sunny, dry habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in Mediterranean climates.
