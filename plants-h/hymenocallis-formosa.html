Hymenocallis formosa

Hymenocallis formosa, commonly known as the showy ginger lily, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from white to yellow and orange. The plant produces a tall, leafy stem with broad, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in summer and are often used in floral arrangements due to their aesthetic appeal.

Hymenocallis formosa thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
