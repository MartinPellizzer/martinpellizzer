Hypolepis parviloba

Hypolepis parviloba, commonly known as the small-leaf huperzia, is a fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Huperziaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Hypolepis parviloba is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
