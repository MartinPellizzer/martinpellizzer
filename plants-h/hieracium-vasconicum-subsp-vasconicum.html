Hieracium vasconicum subsp. vasconicum

Hieracium vasconicum subsp.

vasconicum, commonly known as the Basque hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pyrenees region. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features yellow composite flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, with slender, hairy stems and basal leaves. It has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often lobed at the base.

This subspecies is characterized by its distinctive morphology and is adapted to the specific ecological conditions of its native range.
