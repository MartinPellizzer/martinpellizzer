Herminium annamense

Herminium annamense, commonly known as the Annam orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its erect, grass-like foliage and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments, often on rocky or humus-rich substrates. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish with intricate patterns, and it blooms during the spring and early summer.

Herminium annamense is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitats.
