Humata solida

Humata solida, commonly known as the solid humata, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pale yellow to orange, typically blooming in the spring. Humata solida is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, storing water in its fleshy stems and roots.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and tonic properties.
