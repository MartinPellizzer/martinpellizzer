Hesperis matronalis var. sylvestris

Hesperis matronalis var. sylvestris, commonly known as the woodland sweet rocket, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of bright yellow flowers with four petals. The plant produces small, oval, green leaves that are slightly hairy and have a serrated edge. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
