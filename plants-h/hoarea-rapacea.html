Hoarea rapacea

Hoarea rapacea, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with fleshy, succulent leaves that help it conserve water. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low precipitation.

Hoarea rapacea plays a role in stabilizing soil in desert environments and is often found in disturbed or dry habitats.
