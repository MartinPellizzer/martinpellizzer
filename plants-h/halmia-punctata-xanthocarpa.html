Halmia punctata xanthocarpa

Halmia punctata xanthocarpa, commonly known as the yellow-fruited halmia, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features small, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. Its most distinctive characteristic is its yellow-orange berries, which are clustered in dense inflorescences.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and fruit.
