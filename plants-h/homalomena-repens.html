Homalomena repens

Homalomena repens, commonly known as the creeping arrowhead plant, is a tropical perennial belonging to the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, arrowhead-like leaves that are typically a deep green with a glossy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Homalomena repens is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
