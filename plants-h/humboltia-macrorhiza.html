Humboltia macrorhiza

Humboltia macrorhiza, commonly known as the giant hummingbird vine, is a climbing plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple, which attract pollinators like hummingbirds. The plant has a woody stem with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowering period occurs during the warmer months, and it produces elongated, cylindrical fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Humboltia macrorhiza is adapted to grow in humid, shaded environments within tropical and subtropical forests.
