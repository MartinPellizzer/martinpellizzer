Hemigraphis royenii

Hemigraphis royenii, commonly known as the silver mohair plant, is a low-growing herbaceous perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that ranges from silver to gray-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale pink, clustered in spikes. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

Hemigraphis royenii is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and easy maintenance.
