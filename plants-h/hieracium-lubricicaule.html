Hieracium lubricicaule

Hieracium lubricicaule, commonly known as the smooth meadow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soil. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
