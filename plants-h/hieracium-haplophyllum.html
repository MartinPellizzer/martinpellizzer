Hieracium haplophyllum

Hieracium haplophyllum, commonly known as the narrowleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture, and its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed habitats and has been studied for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
