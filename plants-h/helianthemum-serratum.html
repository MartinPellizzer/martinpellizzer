Helianthemum serratum

Helianthemum serratum, commonly known as the serrated rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, evergreen leaves with serrated edges, which give it its scientific name. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, appearing in clusters during the summer months.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.
