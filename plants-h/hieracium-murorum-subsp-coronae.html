Hieracium murorum subsp. coronae

Hieracium murorum subsp.

coronae, commonly known as the Crowned Hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a flowering stem that bears yellow flowers arranged in a single head.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. It has a fibrous root system and produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

The subspecies is distinguished by its distinctive crown-like appearance, giving it its common name.
