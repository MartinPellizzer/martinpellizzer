Haworthia incrassa

Haworthia incrassa, commonly known as the thick-leafed Haworthia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, fleshy leaves that are typically green with white or pale markings. The plant grows in a rosette formation, and its leaves are thick and fleshy to store water, adapting to arid environments. It produces small, white or yellow flowers on tall stalks during the growing season.

Haworthia incrassa is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance and ornamental appeal.
