Hemionitis stuebeliana

Hemionitis stuebeliana, commonly known as the Stuebel's hemionitis, is a rare and delicate fern native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like fronds that are typically green with a silvery underside. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or shaded habitats. Its reproductive structures, including spore-producing cones, are small and inconspicuous.

Due to its limited distribution and sensitivity to environmental changes, Hemionitis stuebeliana is considered a vulnerable species.
