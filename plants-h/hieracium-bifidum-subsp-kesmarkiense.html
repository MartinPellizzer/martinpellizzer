Hieracium bifidum subsp. kesmarkiense

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

kesmarkiense, commonly known as the Kesmark Hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the British Isles. It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters.

The plant typically grows in grasslands and open habitats, preferring well-drained soils. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and protected species in parts of its range.
