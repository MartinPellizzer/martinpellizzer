Hoya chlorantha var. tutuilensis

Hoya chlorantha var. tutuilensis, commonly known as the Tutuila Hoya, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to the island of Tutuila in American Samoa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom in response to bright, indirect light. It prefers warm, humid environments and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements, it is often sought after by plant enthusiasts.
