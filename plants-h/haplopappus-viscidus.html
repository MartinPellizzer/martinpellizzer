Haplopappus viscidus

Haplopappus viscidus, commonly known as sticky pappus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, often hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in a sticky resin, which gives the plant its common name.

Haplopappus viscidus thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in desert or arid regions.
