Hieracium guthnikianum subsp. erythrodes

Hieracium guthnikianum subsp.

erythrodes, commonly known as the red-veined hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its reddish veins on the leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as steppes and grasslands.

The plant is typically short-lived and has a compact growth form, making it a notable species in its native flora.
