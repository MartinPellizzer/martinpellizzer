Hieracium cotyliscophorum

Hieracium cotyliscophorum, commonly known as the broad-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant features rough, hairy leaves that are typically lanceolate in shape, with a basal rosette structure. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, and its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine.

Hieracium cotyliscophorum is adapted to harsh alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight.
