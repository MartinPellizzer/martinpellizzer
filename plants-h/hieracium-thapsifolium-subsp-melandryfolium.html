Hieracium thapsifolium subsp. melandryfolium

Hieracium thapsifolium subsp.

melandryfolium, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough texture, and the flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions and its role in supporting pollinators.
