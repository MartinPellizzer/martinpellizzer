Hieracium vulgatum subsp. acidontophyllum

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

acidontophyllum, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along riverbanks, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features upright stems with basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow, with ray florets that are shorter than the central disk florets, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is part of the Asteraceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
