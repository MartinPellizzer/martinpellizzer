Hieracium auriculinum subsp. subtubulasens

Hieracium auriculinum subsp.

subtubulasens, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring moist to moderately dry conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and the flowering period occurs from late spring to early autumn.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly shorter flower stems and more compact inflorescence compared to other members of the species.
