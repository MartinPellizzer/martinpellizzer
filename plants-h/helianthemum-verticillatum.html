Helianthemum verticillatum

Helianthemum verticillatum, commonly known as the clustered rockrose, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
