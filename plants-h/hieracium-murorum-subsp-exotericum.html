Hieracium murorum subsp. exotericum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

exotericum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments.

The plant features upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, characteristic of the Asteraceae family.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is adapted to withstand harsh alpine conditions.
