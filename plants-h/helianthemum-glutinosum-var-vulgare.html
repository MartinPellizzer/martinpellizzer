Helianthemum glutinosum var. vulgare

Helianthemum glutinosum var. vulgare, commonly known as sticky sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features succulent, hairy stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or arid environments.

Its sticky leaves and drought-resistant nature make it well adapted to harsh, sun-exposed habitats.
