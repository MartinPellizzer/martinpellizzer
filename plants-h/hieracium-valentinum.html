Hieracium valentinum

Hieracium valentinum, commonly known as Valentine's dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves and produces yellow flowers that develop into fluffy white seed heads. It thrives in meadows and grasslands, often blooming in early spring.

Its common name is derived from its association with the holiday of Saint Valentine.
