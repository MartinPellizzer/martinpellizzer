Hieracium pilosella subsp. indivisum

Hieracium pilosella subsp.

indivisum, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

The plant features erect, hairy stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often have a woolly texture. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, characteristic of the Compositae family.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
