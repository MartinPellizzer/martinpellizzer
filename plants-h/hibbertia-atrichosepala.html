Hibbertia atrichosepala

Hibbertia atrichosepala, commonly known as the smooth-leafed hibbertia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in spring and attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Hibbertia atrichosepala is adapted to sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry forests.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
