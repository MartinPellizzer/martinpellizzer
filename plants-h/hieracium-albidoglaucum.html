Hieracium albidoglaucum

Hieracium albidoglaucum, commonly known as the white-blue hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant features basal leaves that are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stem is hairy and often reddish in color. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in a single head with ray florets.

Hieracium albidoglaucum is often found in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, and it can sometimes be invasive in certain regions.
