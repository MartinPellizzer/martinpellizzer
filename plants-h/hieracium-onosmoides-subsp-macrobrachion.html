Hieracium onosmoides subsp. macrobrachion

Hieracium onosmoides subsp.

macrobrachion, commonly known as the large-bracted hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 50 cm.

The plant features basal leaves that are broadly ovate to lanceolate, with hairy surfaces, and stems that are often reddish in color. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a single head, and are surrounded by leaf-like bracts.

The species is characterized by its robust growth and distinctive foliage, making it a notable component of its native habitats.
