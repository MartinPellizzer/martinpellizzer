Hypericum phellos subsp. phellos

Hypericum phellos subsp.

phellos, commonly known as the yellow wood or St. John's wort, is a shrub native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer, and its opposite, narrow leaves.

The plant has woody stems and is often found in moist, wooded areas. It belongs to the family Hypericaceae and is known for its medicinal properties due to the presence of hypericin and hyperforin.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and herbal contexts.
