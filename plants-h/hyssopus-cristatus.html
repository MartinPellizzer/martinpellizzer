Hyssopus cristatus

Hyssopus cristatus, commonly known as lamb's ear, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its soft, woolly leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces spikes of pale blue or purple flowers in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic leaves and showy flowers make it both ornamental and useful in traditional herbal medicine.
