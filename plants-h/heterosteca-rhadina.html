Heterosteca rhadina

Heterosteca rhadina, commonly known as the red-flowered piper, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically red or orange in color, which attract various pollinators. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Heterosteca rhadina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some regions.
