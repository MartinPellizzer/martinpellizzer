Hyparrhenia familiaris var. pilosa

Hyparrhenia familiaris var. pilosa, commonly known as hairy flat sedge, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage with hairy textures on the leaves and stems, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along water bodies. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a cylindrical cluster, which is typical of the Cyperaceae family.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
