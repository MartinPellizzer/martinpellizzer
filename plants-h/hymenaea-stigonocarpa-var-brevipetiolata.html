Hymenaea stigonocarpa var. brevipetiolata

Hymenaea stigonocarpa var. brevipetiolata, commonly known as the Brazilian桃花心木 or yellow heart, is a species of leguminous tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow to reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant has compound leaves with stipules, and its flowers are typically clustered in racemes, producing pods that contain flat, hard seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in dry forests and savannas.

The tree is also known for its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen, making it ecologically significant in certain regions.
