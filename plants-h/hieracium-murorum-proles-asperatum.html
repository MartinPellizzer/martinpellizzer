Hieracium murorum proles asperatum

Hieracium murorum proles asperatum, commonly known as the rough mouse-ear hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and small yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Native to Europe, this plant thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats such as roadsides and meadows. It has a creeping growth habit with rhizomes that allow it to spread vegetatively.

The plant is often found in moist, temperate regions and is known for its resilience in challenging environments.
