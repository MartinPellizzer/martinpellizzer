Hedeoma oblongifolia

Hedeoma oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hedeoma, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in the summer months. Hedeoma oblongifolia is typically found in moist, sandy soils along streambanks and in open woodlands.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and square stems.
