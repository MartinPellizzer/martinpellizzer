Hieracium murorum subsp. pseudoatratum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

pseudoatratum, commonly known as the mouse ear hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow flower heads.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and grasslands. It has a taproot system and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that attract various pollinators.

Its leaves are usually hairy and have a distinctive heart-shaped base.
