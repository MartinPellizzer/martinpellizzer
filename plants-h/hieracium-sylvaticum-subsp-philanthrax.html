Hieracium sylvaticum subsp. philanthrax

Hieracium sylvaticum subsp.

philanthrax, commonly known as the woodland hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and meadows, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

The plant features upright stems that bear alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture. Its yellow flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom from late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is characterized by its distinct leaf morphology and is often found in association with other woodland flora.
