Hibbertia humifusa subsp. humifusa

Hibbertia humifusa subsp.

humifusa, commonly known as the groundsel bush, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Australia. It features small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands. Hibbertia humifusa subsp.

humifusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native plant communities.
