Hyptis origanoides

Hyptis origanoides, commonly known as lemon verbena, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, slightly hairy leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale yellow to white in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal use.

The leaves are often used in teas and aromatherapy due to their pleasant lemon scent and mild sedative properties.
