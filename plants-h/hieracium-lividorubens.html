Hieracium lividorubens

Hieracium lividorubens, commonly known as the purple ragwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and hairy, lobed leaves. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it often grows in grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant contains pyrrolizidine alkaloids, which can be toxic to livestock.

Its morphology and chemical composition make it both ecologically significant and potentially hazardous.
