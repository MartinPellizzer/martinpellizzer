Heracleum sphondylium subsp. transsilvanicum

Heracleum sphondylium subsp.

transsilvanicum, commonly known as the Transylvanian cow parsnip, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbellate inflorescences and aromatic foliage.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks. It features a tall, hollow stem with finely divided, fern-like leaves.

Its flowers are small and white, forming umbrella-like clusters that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
