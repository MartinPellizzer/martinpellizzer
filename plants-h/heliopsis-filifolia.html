Heliopsis filifolia

Heliopsis filifolia, commonly known as the threadleaf heliopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, thread-like leaves and bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other moist habitats.

Heliopsis filifolia is a perennial herb that plays a role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its native environment.
