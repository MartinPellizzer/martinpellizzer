Hakea lissocarpha

Hakea lissocarpha, commonly known as the silky hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-green foliage that gives it a soft, silky appearance. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. Its seed capsules are woody and remain on the plant for several years before dispersing.

Hakea lissocarpha is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
