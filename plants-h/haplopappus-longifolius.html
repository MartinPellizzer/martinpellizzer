Haplopappus longifolius

Haplopappus longifolius, commonly known as the longleaf pappus, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often in disturbed soils. Its leaves are elongated and often have a bluish-gray hue, which helps reduce water loss.

The small, wind-dispersed seeds are adapted for survival in harsh, dry conditions.
