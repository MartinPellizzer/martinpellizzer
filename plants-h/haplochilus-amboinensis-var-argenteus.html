Haplochilus amboinensis var. argenteus

Haplochilus amboinensis var. argenteus, commonly known as the silver-haired hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink. The leaves are usually broad and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. This variety is distinguished by its silvery or pale green undersides, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
