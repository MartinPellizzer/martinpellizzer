Hesperantha longicollis

Hesperantha longicollis, commonly known as the long-necked bell orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated flower spikes that bear showy, bell-shaped blooms in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its unique floral structure includes a labellum that is often adorned with intricate patterns, aiding in pollination by specific insect species.

Hesperantha longicollis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate and exotic appearance.
