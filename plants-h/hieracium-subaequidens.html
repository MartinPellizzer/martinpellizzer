Hieracium subaequidens

Hieracium subaequidens, commonly known as the equal-leaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant has basal and upright stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and often have a bluish-green hue. Its yellow flowers are arranged in flat-topped clusters, and it produces small, oval seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Hieracium subaequidens is an herbaceous perennial that can sometimes become invasive in certain regions.
