Hyparrhenia parvispiculata

Hyparrhenia parvispiculata, commonly known as the small-spined grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and woodland margins.

Hyparrhenia parvispiculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
