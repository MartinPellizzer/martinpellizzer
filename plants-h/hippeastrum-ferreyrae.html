Hippeastrum ferreyrae

Hippeastrum ferreyrae, commonly known as the Ferreyra's amaryllis, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features strap-like leaves that are broad and strap-shaped, often emerging from a bulbous stem.

It is cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-draining soil with adequate sunlight.
