Hieraciodes jubata

Hieraciodes jubata, commonly known as the golden haircup, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in Chile and Argentina. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
