Helianthemum × coronadoi nothosubsp.

espadanicum, commonly known as the Spanish rock rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
