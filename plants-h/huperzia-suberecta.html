Huperzia suberecta

Huperzia suberecta, commonly known as the upright huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and small, ovate to lanceolate leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and rocky crevices. Its sporophylls are arranged in a tight spiral, and the sporangia are borne on the abaxial surface of the leaves.

Huperzia suberecta is a relatively rare and ancient fern, with a lineage dating back to the Mesozoic era.
