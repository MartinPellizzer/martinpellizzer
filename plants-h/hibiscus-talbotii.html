Hibiscus talbotii

Hibiscus talbotii, commonly known as the talbot's hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. Hibiscus talbotii has rough, hairy leaves and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
