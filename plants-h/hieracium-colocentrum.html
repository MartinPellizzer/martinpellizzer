Hieracium colocentrum

Hieracium colocentrum, commonly known as the lesser spear thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and wastelands. Its leaves are spiny and have a rough texture, while its flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer.

The species is often considered a weed but has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
