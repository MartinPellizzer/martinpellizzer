Hieracium substellatum subsp. substellatum

Hieracium substellatum subsp.

substellatum, commonly known as the starry hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are lanceolate, hairy, and often have a reddish or purplish tinge, while the flowers are yellow with a distinctive star-like appearance.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
