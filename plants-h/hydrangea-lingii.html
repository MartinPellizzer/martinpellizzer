Hydrangea lingii

Hydrangea lingii, commonly known as the mountain hydrangea, is a species native to eastern Asia, particularly China and Korea.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in forested areas with moist, well-drained soil. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with serrated edges and a dark green color.

Hydrangea lingii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
