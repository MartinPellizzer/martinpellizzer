Hemigraphis oblongifolia

Hemigraphis oblongifolia, commonly known as the silver mohair plant, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of South America.

It features upright, branching stems covered in dense, silver-tomentose hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The leaves are oblong and often have a waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental value. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically in shades of pink or white.

This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden environments.
