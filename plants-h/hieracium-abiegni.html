Hieracium abiegni

Hieracium abiegni, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium abiegni is often found in open areas such as meadows and slopes, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
