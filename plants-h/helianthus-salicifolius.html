Helianthus salicifolius

Helianthus salicifolius, commonly known as the willow-leaf sunflower, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial with lance-shaped leaves that resemble willow leaves, giving it its common name. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Helianthus salicifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
