Harmala syriaca

Harmala syriaca, commonly known as Syrian henbane, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces striking violet or blue flowers and has spiny, hairy stems. Its leaves are typically ovate with toothed margins, and it thrives in dry, rocky soils.

Harmala syriaca contains various alkaloids, making it both toxic and of interest in botanical and pharmacological studies.
