Hedyotis angusta var. umbrosa

Hedyotis angusta var. umbrosa, commonly known as the narrowleaf Indian mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and undergrowth. The plant features small, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, clustered in small inflorescences.

The species is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in low-light conditions.
