Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp. cophomeles

Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp.

cophomeles, commonly known as the Wiesbaur's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering heads that produce yellow disc flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. It has finely divided, hairy leaves and produces numerous seeds dispersed by wind.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species in many regions.
