Hieracium sociale

Hieracium sociale, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces clusters of yellow, dandelion-like flowers. The plant is native to Europe and has been introduced to other parts of the world, often becoming invasive in certain regions. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows, roadsides, and disturbed soils.

The flowers are typically short-lived, opening in the morning and closing by midday.
