Habenaria sahyadrica

Habenaria sahyadrica, commonly known as the Sahyadri orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit. The plant typically features a pseudobulb with a single leaf and produces a raceme of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its restricted habitat, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
