Hieracium haematopodum

Hieracium haematopodum, commonly known as the bloodroot hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while its flowers are yellow with reddish-brown markings.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
