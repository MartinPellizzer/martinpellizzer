Hieracium kuekenthalianum subsp. andreanszkyanum

Hieracium kuekenthalianum subsp.

andreanszkyanum, commonly known as the Hungarian hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Central Europe. It typically grows in dry grasslands and open woodlands, reaching heights of up to 30 cm.

The plant features basal rosettes of stiff, hairy leaves and produces yellow flower heads composed of numerous small florets. Its flowering period occurs from June to August, and it is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different leaf morphology and floral structure compared to other members of the Hieracium genus.
