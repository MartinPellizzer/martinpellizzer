Hibiscus adscendens

Hibiscus adscendens, commonly known as the climbing hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vigorous climbing habit, with stems that can reach lengths of several meters. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a distinctive yellow throat. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and have a velvety texture.

Hibiscus adscendens thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant blooms and lush foliage.
