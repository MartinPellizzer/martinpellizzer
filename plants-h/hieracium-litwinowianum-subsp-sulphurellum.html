Hieracium litwinowianum subsp. sulphurellum

Hieracium litwinowianum subsp.

sulphurellum, commonly known as the sulphur yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe. It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.

The plant is characterized by its yellowish-orange flower heads, which consist of both ray and disc florets. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, oval-shaped achenes as its seeds.

This subspecies is distinguished by its distinctive coloration and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
