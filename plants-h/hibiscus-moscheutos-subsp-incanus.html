Hibiscus moscheutos subsp. incanus

Hibiscus moscheutos subsp.

incanus, commonly known as the woolly marsh hibiscus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its hairy, grayish stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red.

The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a rough texture, and the flowers bloom from summer to fall.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
