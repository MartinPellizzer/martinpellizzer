Hieracium latifolium subsp. muraltae

Hieracium latifolium subsp.

muraltae, commonly known as the broad-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Due to its invasive potential, it is often considered a weed in agricultural and natural settings.
