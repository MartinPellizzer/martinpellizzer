Helipterum zacchaeus

Helipterum zacchaeus, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that are often used in floral arrangements due to their long-lasting nature. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun exposure.

Helipterum zacchaeus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in arid environments.
