Heliconia chiapensis

Heliconia chiapensis, commonly known as the Chiapan heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its large, brightly colored bracts that resemble flowers. The plant produces long, segmented inflorescences with numerous small, clustered flowers. Its leaves are broad and often have a waxy texture, which helps it thrive in humid, tropical climates.

Heliconia chiapensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
