Hyacinthus botryoides

Hyacinthus botryoides, commonly known as the grape hyacinth, is a bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that resemble grapes, hence its common name. The plant produces strap-like, grassy leaves and has a short, sturdy stem. It blooms in early spring, typically in shades of blue, purple, or white.

The bulbs are perennial and thrive in well-drained soil and full sun.
