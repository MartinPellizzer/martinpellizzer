Hieracium murorum subsp. glandulosissimum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

glandulosissimum, commonly known as the glandular hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm.

The plant features basal and stem leaves that are lanceolate to ovate, often covered in glandular hairs. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and the species is characterized by its distinctive hairy stems and leaves.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads.
