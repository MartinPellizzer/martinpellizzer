Hieracium oppositifolium

Hieracium oppositifolium, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are opposite and usually covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Hieracium oppositifolium is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in many regions.
