Hookera uniflora f. violacea

Hookera uniflora f. violacea, commonly known as the violet-flowered hookera, is a rare and striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet or purple inflorescence, which stands out against its green foliage. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a central cup, typical of many bromeliads. Its flowers are small and clustered, often giving the appearance of a dense, colorful cluster.

Due to its limited distribution and unique appearance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and plant enthusiasts alike.
