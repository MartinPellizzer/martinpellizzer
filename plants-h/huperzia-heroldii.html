Huperzia heroldii

Huperzia heroldii, commonly known as the Herold's spikemoss, is a perennial evergreen fern-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Huperziaceae and is characterized by its upright, spiky foliage that resembles moss. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Huperzia heroldii has a rhizomatous growth habit, with dense clusters of needle-like leaves that form a compact rosette.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
