Hieracium erythrocarpum subsp. liberatidens

Hieracium erythrocarpum subsp.

liberatidens, commonly known as the red-fruited hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and clustered, yellowish to reddish flowers.

The plant produces small, reddish-brown fruits that are distinctive and aid in its identification. It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
