Hakea dolichostyla

Hakea dolichostyla, commonly known as the long-styled hakea, is a species native to Western Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in heathlands and coastal regions, characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces distinctive cylindrical flowers that are usually pink or red, which bloom in spring. Its unique floral structure includes long, slender stamens that extend beyond the petals, aiding in pollination by birds and insects.

Hakea dolichostyla is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
