Hosta crispula

Hosta crispula, commonly known as the crinkled hosta, is a perennial plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that have a distinctive crinkled or wavy texture. The foliage is typically a deep green with a silvery underside, adding visual interest. It produces small, fragrant white flowers in late summer, though it is primarily grown for its ornamental leaves.

This plant thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.
