Holboellia crassifolia

Holboellia crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed holboellia, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically broad and ovate, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are often borne on erect, branching stems. Its root system is fibrous and extensive, helping it anchor in loose, sandy soils.

Holboellia crassifolia is often found in open woodlands and along coastal areas where it thrives in well-drained conditions.
