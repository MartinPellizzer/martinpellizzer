Hoya guppyi

Hoya guppyi, commonly known as the Guppy's Waxplant, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and clusters of waxy, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is known for its slow growth and ability to thrive in low-light conditions. Hoya guppyi is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.

Its flowers emit a subtle fragrance, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
