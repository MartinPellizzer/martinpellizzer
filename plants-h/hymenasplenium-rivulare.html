Hymenasplenium rivulare

Hymenasplenium rivulare, commonly known as the water spleenwort, is a small, terrestrial liverwort found in moist, shaded environments.

It has a distinctive thalloid structure with a flattened, ribbon-like body that is often covered in a layer of fine, moss-like growth. The plant produces male and female reproductive structures on separate plants, a feature known as dioecious reproduction. Its leaves are small, oval, and arranged in a spiral pattern along the thallus.

Hymenasplenium rivulare plays a role in soil stabilization and serves as a habitat for microorganisms in its native ecosystems.
