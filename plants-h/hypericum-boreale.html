Hypericum boreale

Hypericum boreale, commonly known as Arctic St. John's Wort, is a perennial herb native to arctic and alpine regions.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, and its slender, upright stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold climates. It has small, oval leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Hypericum boreale is known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
