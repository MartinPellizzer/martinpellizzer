Habranthus tubispathus var. bicolor

Habranthus tubispathus var. bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored spider lily, is a striking perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its elegant, tubular flowers that display a distinctive bicolor pattern, typically featuring a white or pale pink base with a deeper pink or red throat. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense rosette near the ground. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements contribute to its appeal among gardeners.
