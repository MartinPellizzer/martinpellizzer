Hieracium ugandiense

Hieracium ugandiense, commonly known as the Uganda hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the highland regions of Uganda.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and its flowering stems bear yellow ray florets.

Hieracium ugandiense is often found in altitudes ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters above sea level.
