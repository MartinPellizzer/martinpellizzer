Helianthemum vulgare subsp. grandiflorum

Helianthemum vulgare subsp.

grandiflorum, commonly known as the Greater Rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a low-growing shrub with evergreen leaves and produces showy yellow flowers in spring.

The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes. Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.

The subspecies is distinguished by its larger, more showy flowers compared to other members of the Helianthemum genus.
