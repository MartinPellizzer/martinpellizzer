Hieracium glanduliceps

Hieracium glanduliceps, commonly known as the glandular hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features basal leaves that are usually hairy and have a rough texture. Its flowering heads consist of numerous small yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family.

Hieracium glanduliceps is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, where it thrives in full sun to partial shade.
