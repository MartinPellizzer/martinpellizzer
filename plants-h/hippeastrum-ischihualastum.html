Hippeastrum ischihualastum

Hippeastrum ischihualastum, commonly known as the Ischihualastum amaryllis, is a striking ornamental plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped blooms that can reach up to 10 inches in diameter, often with a contrasting color at the base. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

This species is popular for its vibrant flowers and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
