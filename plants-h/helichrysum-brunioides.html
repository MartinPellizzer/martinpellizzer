Helichrysum brunioides

Helichrysum brunioides, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, bright yellow to orange flowers that resemble tiny suns. The plant produces bracts that are often mistaken for petals, giving it a unique and decorative appearance. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The flowers are also used in floral arrangements due to their longevity and vibrant color.
