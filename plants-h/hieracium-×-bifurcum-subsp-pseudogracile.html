Hieracium × bifurcum subsp. pseudogracile

Hieracium × bifurcum subsp.

pseudogracile, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a hybrid species within the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and yellowish to orange flower heads.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a rough, hairy texture.

It is a perennial herb that can form dense colonies, contributing to its ecological impact in certain regions.
