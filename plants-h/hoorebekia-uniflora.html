Hoorebekia uniflora

Hoorebekia uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, blooming in late summer. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Hoorebekia uniflora is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.
