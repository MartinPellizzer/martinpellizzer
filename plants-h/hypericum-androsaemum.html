Hypericum androsaemum

Hypericum androsaemum, commonly known as St. John's Wort, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, bead-like fruits that contain the seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used for treating mild depression and anxiety.
