Hypericum inodorum var. glandulosum

Hypericum inodorum var. glandulosum, commonly known as the sticky St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in glandular dots, giving them a slightly sticky texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is often cultivated in gardens and herbal gardens.
