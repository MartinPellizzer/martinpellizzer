Hyptis obtusifolia

Hyptis obtusifolia, commonly known as Brazilian hyssop, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

The plant's structure and growth habits make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural applications.
