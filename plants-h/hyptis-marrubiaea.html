Hyptis marrubiaea

Hyptis marrubiaea, commonly known as the rough hyptis, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves and spikes of small, pale blue to purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as grasslands and forest edges.

Hyptis marrubiaea is noted for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antiseptic benefits.
