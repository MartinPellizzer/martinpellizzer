Hoya uniflora

Hoya uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower waxplant, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers. The plant produces fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Hoya uniflora is a popular houseplant due to its low maintenance and ability to thrive in indirect light.

Its unique appearance and ease of care make it a favored choice among indoor gardeners.
