Heliconia mutisiana

Heliconia mutisiana, commonly known as the red heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescences. The plant produces long, segmented bracts that enclose numerous small, fragrant flowers. These bracts are typically bright red or orange, making the plant highly ornamental.

Heliconia mutisiana is often cultivated in warm, humid climates for its aesthetic value and is also used in some traditional practices.
