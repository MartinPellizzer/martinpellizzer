Hieracium auricula proles serpillifolium

Hieracium auricula proles serpillifolium, commonly known as the serpillifolium hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a flowering head composed of yellow ray florets. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lanceolate to oblanceolate, with a hairy surface, and the flowers bloom in early summer.

It is a relatively small plant, usually reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.
