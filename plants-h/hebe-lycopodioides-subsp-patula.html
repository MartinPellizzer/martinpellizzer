Hebe lycopodioides subsp. patula

Hebe lycopodioides subsp.

patula, commonly known as the silver-haired hebe, is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is native to New Zealand. It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters, characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of purple or violet, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in temperate climates.
