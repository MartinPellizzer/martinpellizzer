Hieracium kloeber

Hieracium kloeber, commonly known as the Kloeber's hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and produces numerous yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It has hairy stems and leaves, and its flowers are often visited by pollinators like bees and flies.

Hieracium kloeber is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
