Hieracium sowadeense

Hieracium sowadeense, commonly known as the Sowada's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, and its leaves are lance-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces.

Hieracium sowadeense is often found in dry, sunny habitats and is relatively low-maintenance, making it a common sight in its native range.
