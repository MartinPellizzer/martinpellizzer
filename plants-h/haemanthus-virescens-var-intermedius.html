Haemanthus virescens var. intermedius

Haemanthus virescens var. intermedius, commonly known as the Zulu lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features striking, sword-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green, with a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of bright red flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a dramatic visual element to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
