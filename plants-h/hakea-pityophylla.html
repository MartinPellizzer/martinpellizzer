Hakea pityophylla

Hakea pityophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces cylindrical or conical flower spikes, which are usually yellow or orange in hue. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Hakea pityophylla is often used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
