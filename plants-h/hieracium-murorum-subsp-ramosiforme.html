Hieracium murorum subsp. ramosiforme

Hieracium murorum subsp.

ramosiforme, commonly known as the creeping hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It is characterized by its creeping growth habit, with stolons that root at the nodes and form dense mats.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically with ray florets and a central disk. Its leaves are usually basal, forming a rosette, and are covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.
