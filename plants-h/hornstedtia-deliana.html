Hornstedtia deliana

Hornstedtia deliana, commonly known as the desert bell, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 feet. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

The flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
