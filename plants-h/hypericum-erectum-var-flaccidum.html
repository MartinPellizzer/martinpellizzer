Hypericum erectum var. flaccidum

Hypericum erectum var. flaccidum, commonly known as the lax yellowstone St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its drooping, slender stems and small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for rocky or alpine gardens.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and resilient addition to native plant collections.
