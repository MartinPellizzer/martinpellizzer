Hymenophyllum hieronymi

Hymenophyllum hieronymi, commonly known as the silver moss, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically covered in a dense layer of rhizoids, giving it a silvery appearance. This epiphytic plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or rocks in tropical and subtropical regions. Its small, spore-producing structures, called sporangia, are clustered in specialized leaf-like formations called sorus.

The plant's unique morphology and habitat preferences make it an important component of forest ecosystems.
