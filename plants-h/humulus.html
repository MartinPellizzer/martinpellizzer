Humulus

Humulus, commonly known as hops, is a flowering plant in the Cannabaceae family.

It is native to Europe, Asia, and North America, and is characterized by its conical, spiky female flowers. The plant is cultivated primarily for its resinous and aromatic cones, which are used in beer brewing. Humulus has climbing stems that can reach several meters in height, and its leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges.

The plant contains various bioactive compounds, including humulene and myrcene, which contribute to its distinctive flavor and aroma.
