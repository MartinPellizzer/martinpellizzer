Hypochaeris montana

Hypochaeris montana, commonly known as mountain sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, followed by hairy, elongated seed pods.

Hypochaeris montana is often found in alpine environments and is adapted to withstand cold and harsh climatic conditions.
