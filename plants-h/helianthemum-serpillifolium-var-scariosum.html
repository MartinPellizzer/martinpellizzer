Helianthemum serpillifolium var. scariosum

Helianthemum serpillifolium var. scariosum, commonly known as the scaly rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub with small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a whitish, waxy coating. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is drought-tolerant.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
