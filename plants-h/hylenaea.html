Hylenaea

Hylenaea, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its small, delicate leaves that often have a silvery or grayish hue, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Hylenaea species are typically found in arid and semi-arid regions, where they are adapted to thrive in dry conditions.

These plants are sometimes used in traditional medicine and are valued for their ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
