Hieracium furcatum subsp. subfurcatum

Hieracium furcatum subsp.

subfurcatum, commonly known as the forked hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower.

The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, spoon-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters. It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides.

Its name refers to the distinctive forked shape of its leaves, which is a key identifying feature.
