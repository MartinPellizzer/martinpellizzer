Hieracium rosei

Hieracium rosei, commonly known as the rose hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped with serrated edges. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in meadows and grasslands.

It is considered an invasive species in some regions due to its rapid spread and competitive nature.
