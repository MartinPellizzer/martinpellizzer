Hieracium pseudogrovesianum subsp. circinans

Hieracium pseudogrovesianum subsp.

circinans, commonly known as the curlyhead hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and roadsides. Its leaves are basal and often have a distinctive curled or rolled appearance, giving it its common name.

The flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head, with both ray and disk florets.
