Homaladenia puberula

Homaladenia puberula, commonly known as the hairy leaf orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its roots are fibrous and help it anchor in loose soil.

The species is noted for its unique morphology and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
