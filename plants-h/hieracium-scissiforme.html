Hieracium scissiforme

Hieracium scissiforme, commonly known as the cutleaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are deeply lobed and often appear divided, giving it a distinctive, somewhat fern-like appearance.

Hieracium scissiforme produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
