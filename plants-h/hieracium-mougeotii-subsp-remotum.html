Hieracium mougeotii subsp. remotum

Hieracium mougeotii subsp.

remotum, commonly known as the remote hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species in many regions.
