Heliocereus aurantiacus var. blomianus

Heliocereus aurantiacus var. blomianus, commonly known as the Blomianus night blooming cereus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and is known for its striking, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant produces large, showy blooms in shades of white and yellow, often with a red or orange tint. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

This variety is popular among cactus enthusiasts for its unique nocturnal blooming habit.
