Hibiscus mutabilis

Hibiscus mutabilis, commonly known as the wood rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that change color from white to pink to red as they age. The plant has heart-shaped, lobed leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. Hibiscus mutabilis thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to bloom throughout the summer season.
