Hieracium glaucum subsp. isaricum

Hieracium glaucum subsp.

isaricum, commonly known as the Isar hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It typically grows in moist meadows and along riverbanks, preferring calcareous soils.

The plant features yellow flowers arranged in a single, flat-topped inflorescence, with leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaf arrangement.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species in many regions.
