Hieracium murorum subsp. obtusoserratum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

obtusoserratum, commonly known as the blunt-toothed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering heads.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped with rounded or bluntly serrated margins, giving it its subspecific name. It produces yellow disc flowers surrounded by ray florets, typical of the Compositae family.

This plant thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides.
