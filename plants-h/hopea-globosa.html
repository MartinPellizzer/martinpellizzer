Hopea globosa

Hopea globosa, commonly known as the globular hopea, is a species of flowering plant in the family Dipterocarpaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and its distinctive, round-shaped fruits that resemble small globes. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy texture.

Hopea globosa is typically found in lowland dipterocarp forests and plays a role in the ecological balance of these ecosystems.
