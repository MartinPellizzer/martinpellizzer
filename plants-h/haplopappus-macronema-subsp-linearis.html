Haplopappus macronema subsp. linearis

Haplopappus macronema subsp.

linearis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sandmat, is a perennial flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of many small florets.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its linear leaves are usually arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer.

This subspecies is often found in disturbed sites and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
