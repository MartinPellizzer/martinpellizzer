Hieracium pluricaule

Hieracium pluricaule, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and is typically found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant produces bright yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered with fine hairs.

Hieracium pluricaule is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common and sometimes invasive species in various regions.
