Hieracium distrudens

Hieracium distrudens, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and the leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture.

Hieracium distrudens is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
