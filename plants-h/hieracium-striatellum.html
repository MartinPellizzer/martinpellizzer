Hieracium striatellum

Hieracium striatellum, commonly known as the striped hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as meadows and roadsides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are erect and often hairy.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators.
