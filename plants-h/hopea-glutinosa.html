Hopea glutinosa

Hopea glutinosa, commonly known as the sticky hopea, is a tropical tree species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. Hopea glutinosa is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture making.

Additionally, it plays an important ecological role in forest ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
