Hieracium diaphyllotum

Hieracium diaphyllotum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaf arrangement. The plant features slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers are typically yellow, arranged in a dense cluster, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

Hieracium diaphyllotum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
