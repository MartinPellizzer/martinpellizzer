Hydrocotyle pseudoconferta

Hydrocotyle pseudoconferta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water shield, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to parts of North America.

It typically grows in shallow water, wetlands, and along the edges of ponds and lakes. The plant has small, arrow-shaped leaves that are often floating or partially submerged. Its flowers are white and clustered in umbels, which are borne on long, slender stalks.

Hydrocotyle pseudoconferta plays a role in stabilizing aquatic environments and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
