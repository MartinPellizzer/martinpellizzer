Humboltia endotrachys

Humboltia endotrachys, commonly known as the California boxthorn, is a shrub native to coastal regions of California and Baja California.

It features dense, spiny foliage and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a compact growth habit with woody stems and is adapted to sandy or rocky soils. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a leathery texture, and it is often found in coastal scrub habitats.

Humboltia endotrachys is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
