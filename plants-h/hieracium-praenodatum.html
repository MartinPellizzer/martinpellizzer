Hieracium praenodatum

Hieracium praenodatum, commonly known as the early hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium praenodatum is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in agricultural and natural settings.
