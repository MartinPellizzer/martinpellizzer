Hypericum napaulense

Hypericum napaulense, commonly known as the Indian St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas and parts of southern Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in summer. Its leaves are usually ovate or elliptic in shape and have a slightly hairy texture.

Hypericum napaulense is often found in moist, rocky soils and is adapted to temperate to subtropical climates.
