Heliconia rutila

Heliconia rutila, commonly known as the red heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescence composed of long, tubular red bracts that enclose small, yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses. Its leaves are large, broad, and often have a waxy texture, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.

Heliconia rutila thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to flourish.
