Hyptis leucochlora

Hyptis leucochlora, commonly known as white-stemmed hyptis, is a perennial herb native to the warm climates of the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, often emitting a mild, minty fragrance.

Hyptis leucochlora is often grown for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
