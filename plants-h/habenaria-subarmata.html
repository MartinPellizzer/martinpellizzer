Habenaria subarmata

Habenaria subarmata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. Its roots are often mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to enhance nutrient uptake.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticulture.
