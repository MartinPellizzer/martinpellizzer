Hieracium sparsum var. glandulisquamosum

Hieracium sparsum var. glandulisquamosum, commonly known as the scaly-hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellowish to orange flowers. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive appearance and aid in water retention. It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in local ecosystems.
