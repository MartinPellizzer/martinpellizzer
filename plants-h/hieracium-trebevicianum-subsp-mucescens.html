Hieracium trebevicianum subsp. mucescens

Hieracium trebevicianum subsp.

mucescens, commonly known as the creeping hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, broad leaves and produces yellow flowers in a single head.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a creeping growth habit, with stolons that root at the nodes to form new plants.

The subspecies is distinguished by its pale greenish-yellow flowers and its ability to form dense mats in suitable habitats.
