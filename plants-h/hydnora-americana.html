Hydnora americana

Hydnora americana, commonly known as the African starwort, is a parasitic plant that lacks chlorophyll and cannot perform photosynthesis.

It is found primarily in the arid regions of southern Africa, where it attaches itself to the roots of host plants such as acacia and legumes. The plant produces a distinctive, odorless flower that resembles a star, and it is pollinated by insects, particularly flies. Hydnora americana has a unique reproductive system, with its fruit developing underground and dispersing via animals.

Due to its specialized biology and habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
