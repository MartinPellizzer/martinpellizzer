Hieracium murorum subsp. phocadentum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

phocadentum, commonly known as the mouse ear hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is sometimes grown in gardens.
