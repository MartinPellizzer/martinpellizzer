Heritiera cochinchinensis

Heritiera cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese mangrove or white mangrove, is a flowering plant species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal areas, particularly in brackish or saline wetlands. The plant has pinnately compound leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several winged seeds, aiding in wind dispersal.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
