Heuchera orizabensis

Heuchera orizabensis, commonly known as the Mexican alumroot, is a perennial herb native to central Mexico.

It features basal rosettes of heart-shaped, dark green foliage with red or purple veins. The plant produces clusters of small, showy white or pink flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its striking foliage and long blooming period.
