Hibiscus huegelii

Hibiscus huegelii, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. Hibiscus huegelii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.

It is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
