Heuchera americana var. heteradenia

Heuchera americana var. heteradenia, commonly known as the red-veined heuchera, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves with striking red veins. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pink during the summer months. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental foliage makes it a valuable addition to both landscape design and horticultural collections.
