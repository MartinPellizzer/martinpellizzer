Hieracium murorum subsp. babanum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

babanum, commonly known as the Babanum hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats such as roadsides and meadows. It has small, rough, and hairy leaves that are often glandular, and its flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological adaptability and role in local ecosystems.
