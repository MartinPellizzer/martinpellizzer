Helianthemum origanifolium

Helianthemum origanifolium, commonly known as the woolly rockrose, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Europe and northern Africa.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant has aromatic, fuzzy leaves that are often used in traditional herbal remedies. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for Mediterranean climates.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and ornamental plant for xeriscaped gardens.
