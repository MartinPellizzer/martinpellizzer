Helianthemum pilosum subsp. violaceum

Helianthemum pilosum subsp.

violaceum, commonly known as the purple sandverbena, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters.

The plant features small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to deep purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture.

The species is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing its resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
