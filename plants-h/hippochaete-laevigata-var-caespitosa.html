Hippochaete laevigata var. caespitosa

Hippochaete laevigata var. caespitosa, commonly known as the silky hairbush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance and drought tolerance.
