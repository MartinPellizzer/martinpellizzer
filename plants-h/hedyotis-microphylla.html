Hedyotis microphylla

Hedyotis microphylla, commonly known as small-leaved blushing flower, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It features small, opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Hedyotis microphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
