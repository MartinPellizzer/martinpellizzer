Huperzia integrifolia

Huperzia integrifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf huperzia, is a species of moss-like fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its simple, linear leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its spore-producing structures, called sporangia, are clustered in dense, cylindrical formations at the tips of the leaves.

Huperzia integrifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ability to thrive in humid conditions.
