Hieracium tercianum

Hieracium tercianum, commonly known as the Tercian hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring well-drained, sunny conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while the flowers are usually yellow or orange, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

Hieracium tercianum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting pollinators.
