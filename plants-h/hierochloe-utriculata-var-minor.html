Hierochloe utriculata var. minor

Hierochloe utriculata var. minor, commonly known as the lesser bulbous bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and blue-green foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish tint, contributing to its ornamental value.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.
