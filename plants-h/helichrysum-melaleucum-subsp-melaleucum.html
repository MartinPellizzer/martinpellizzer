Helichrysum melaleucum subsp. melaleucum

Helichrysum melaleucum subsp.

melaleucum, commonly known as the white everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant is evergreen and produces fluffy, cotton-like seed heads that persist through the winter. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its aromatic leaves and showy flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
