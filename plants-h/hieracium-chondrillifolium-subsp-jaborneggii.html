Hieracium chondrillifolium subsp. jaborneggii

Hieracium chondrillifolium subsp.

jaborneggii, commonly known as the Jabornegg's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and a flowering stem that bears yellow composite flowers.

The leaves are typically lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, while the plant produces small, bracteate flower heads. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas, and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

This subspecies is distinguished by its specific morphology and geographic distribution within the broader Hieracium genus.
