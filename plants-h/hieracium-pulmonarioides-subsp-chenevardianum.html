Hieracium pulmonarioides subsp. chenevardianum

Hieracium pulmonarioides subsp.

chenevardianum, commonly known as the Chenevard's lungwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It features a basal rosette of broadly ovate to lanceolate leaves with a hairy surface and a distinctive reddish or purple coloration.

The plant produces upright flowering stems bearing dense clusters of small, yellowish to orange-red flowers. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species within the genus Hieracium, known for its ornamental value and ecological significance.
