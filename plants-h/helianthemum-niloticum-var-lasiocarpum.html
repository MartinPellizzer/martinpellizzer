Helianthemum niloticum var. lasiocarpum

Helianthemum niloticum var. lasiocarpum, commonly known as the woolly-fruited sunflower.

It is a low-growing shrub native to southern Africa, characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, rounded fruits that are covered in a fine, woolly layer, giving it its specific epithet. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
