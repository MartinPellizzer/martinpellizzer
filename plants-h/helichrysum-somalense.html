Helichrysum somalense

Helichrysum somalense, commonly known as the Somali immortelle, is a flowering plant native to Somalia and parts of East Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that resemble small suns. The plant is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, while its stems are often branched and woody.

Helichrysum somalense is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
