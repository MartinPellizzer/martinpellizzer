Hyptis turnerifolia

Hyptis turnerifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its aromatic leaves and showy flowers make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.

Hyptis turnerifolia is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
