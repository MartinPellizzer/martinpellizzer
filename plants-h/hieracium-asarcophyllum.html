Hieracium asarcophyllum

Hieracium asarcophyllum, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and ragworts, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
