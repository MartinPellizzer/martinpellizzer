Hieracium murorum subsp. pictorum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

pictorum, commonly known as the painted hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are often reddish or purple in color.

The subspecies is distinguished by its vividly colored flowers and its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
