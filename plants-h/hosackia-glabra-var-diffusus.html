Hosackia glabra var. diffusus

Hosackia glabra var. diffusus, commonly known as the plains gentian, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its blue to violet flowers and is often found in moist meadows and prairies. The plant has smooth, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, radial flowers that bloom in late summer. It is a member of the Gentianaceae family and is known for its ornamental value.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in some regions.
