Henrya scorpioides

Henrya scorpioides, commonly known as the silver leaf henrya, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It features dense clusters of small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white to silver hair, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Its compact size and ornamental foliage make it a valuable addition to ornamental horticulture.
