Helichrysum italicum subsp. picardii

Helichrysum italicum subsp.

picardii, commonly known as golden everlasting, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It features dense clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that remain intact even after flowering, giving it a long-lasting appearance.

The plant has aromatic, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
