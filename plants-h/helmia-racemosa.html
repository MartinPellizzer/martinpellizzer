Helmia racemosa

Helmia racemosa, commonly known as the purple fringed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or pink in color. The plant produces showy, racemose inflorescences with distinctive fringed petals that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, woodlands and prefers partial shade, often growing along stream banks or in rich, well-drained soil.

Helmia racemosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local pollinator populations.
