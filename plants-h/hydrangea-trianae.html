Hydrangea trianae

Hydrangea trianae, commonly known as the Triania hydrangea, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces large, showy inflorescences composed of sterile flowers surrounded by fertile flowers, which can change color based on soil pH. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a smooth margin and a dark green color.

Hydrangea trianae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
