Hieracium bichlorophyllum

Hieracium bichlorophyllum, commonly known as the two-leaf hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are yellow and bloom in the summer months.

It is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is considered a weed in some agricultural settings.
