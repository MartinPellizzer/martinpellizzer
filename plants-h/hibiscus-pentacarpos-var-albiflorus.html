Hibiscus pentacarpos var. albiflorus

Hibiscus pentacarpos var. albiflorus, commonly known as the whiteflower sand hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces fruits that are capsule-like, containing numerous seeds. It thrives in sandy soils and full sun, often found in coastal areas and disturbed sites.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
