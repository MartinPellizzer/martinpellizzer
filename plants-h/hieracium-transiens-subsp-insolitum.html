Hieracium transiens subsp. insolitum

Hieracium transiens subsp.

insolitum, commonly known as the wandering hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. It produces yellow flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Its leaves are often hairy and have a rough texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
