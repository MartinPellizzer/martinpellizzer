Hibiscus surattensis var. genuinus

Hibiscus surattensis var. genuinus, commonly known as the Indian hibiscus or velvetleaf hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. Its flowers bloom throughout the year, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
