Hieracium chloroticum

Hieracium chloroticum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are upright and sometimes branched.

Hieracium chloroticum is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
