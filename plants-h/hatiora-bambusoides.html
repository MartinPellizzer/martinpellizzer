Hatiora bambusoides

Hatiora bambusoides, commonly known as the bamboo-like zygocactus, is a succulent plant native to Brazil.

It features a unique, branching structure that resembles bamboo, with long, slender stems covered in areoles. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white during the winter months. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in arid conditions.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
