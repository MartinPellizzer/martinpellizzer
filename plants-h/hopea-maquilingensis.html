Hopea maquilingensis

Hopea maquilingensis, commonly known as the Maquiling hopea, is a species of flowering plant in the family Dipterocarpaceae.

It is native to the Philippines and is typically found in lowland dipterocarp forests. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall and has a straight trunk with a smooth, grayish-brown bark. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptic in shape, with a leathery texture and a dark green upper surface.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, three-lobed fruits that contain a single seed.
