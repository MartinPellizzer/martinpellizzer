Helenium graveolens

Helenium graveolens, commonly known as the common ironweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features tall, sturdy stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, covered with rough, hairy foliage. Its showy, bright yellow to orange-red flower heads bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Helenium graveolens is often found in moist to wet habitats, such as swamps and marshes, and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
