Heteropogon allionii var. messanensis

Heteropogon allionii var. messanensis, commonly known as the Messanese hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland and grassland ecosystems.
