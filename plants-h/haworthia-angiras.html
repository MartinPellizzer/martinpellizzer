Haworthia angiras

Haworthia angiras, commonly known as the Zulu bulb, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a compact rosette of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with white or yellowish markings. The leaves are arranged in a tight cluster, giving the plant a distinctive, almost geometric appearance. It produces small, pale green or white flowers on slender stems during the summer months.

This plant is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
