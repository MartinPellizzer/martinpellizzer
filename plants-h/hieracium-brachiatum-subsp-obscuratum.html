Hieracium brachiatum subsp. obscuratum

Hieracium brachiatum subsp.

obscuratum, commonly known as the dark-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
