Hieracium pilosella subsp. plantaginiforme

Hieracium pilosella subsp.

plantaginiforme, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and often have a reddish tinge, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
