Hydrobryum japonicum

Hydrobryum japonicum, commonly known as the Japanese water fern, is a small, aquatic plant native to East Asia.

It typically grows in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. The plant has delicate, floating leaves that are usually divided into narrow, ribbon-like segments. Its reproductive structures are simple, with spore-producing capsules that release spores into the water.

Hydrobryum japonicum is often used in ornamental water gardens due to its graceful appearance and ease of cultivation.
