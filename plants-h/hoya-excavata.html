Hoya excavata

Hoya excavata, commonly known as the "Hoya with a hollow stem," is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and clusters of waxy, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions. Hoya excavata produces its flowers in terminal clusters, and its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a slightly wavy margin.
