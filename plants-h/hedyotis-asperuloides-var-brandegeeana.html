Hedyotis asperuloides var. brandegeeana

Hedyotis asperuloides var. brandegeeana, commonly known as the Brandegee's dotweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with small, oval leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale yellow flowers, which are typically arranged in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
