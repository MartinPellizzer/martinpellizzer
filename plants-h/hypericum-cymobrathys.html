Hypericum cymobrathys

Hypericum cymobrathys, commonly known as the golden st. john's wort, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. Hypericum cymobrathys is part of the Hypericaceae family, which includes other species known for their medicinal properties.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in woodland edges and open fields.
