Hieracium subramosum var. plicatiforme

Hieracium subramosum var. plicatiforme, commonly known as the folded-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangements. The plant features basal leaves that are pinnately lobed and often have a waxy coating, while the upper leaves are narrower and more elongated. Its flowering stems are usually erect and bear yellow disc flowers surrounded by pale yellow to white ray florets.

This variant is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology, which includes folded or pleated margins, giving it its specific epithet "plicatiforme.".
