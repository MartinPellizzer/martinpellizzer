Helia amplissima

Helia amplissima, commonly known as the Sunspire Flameflower, is a strikingly beautiful plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, vibrant orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a dramatic visual impact. The plant has broad, waxy leaves that are dark green in color and often have a slight sheen. It thrives in full sunlight and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Helia amplissima is also known for its strong, sweet fragrance, which attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.
