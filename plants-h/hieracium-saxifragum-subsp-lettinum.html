Hieracium saxifragum subsp. lettinum

Hieracium saxifragum subsp.

lettinum, commonly known as the alpine groundsel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces small, yellowish flower heads in the summer months. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in alpine meadows and scree slopes.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized species within its range.
