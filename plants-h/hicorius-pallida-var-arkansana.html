Hicorius pallida var. arkansana

Hicorius pallida var. arkansana, commonly known as the Arkansas white hickory, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, light gray bark and alternate, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, followed by legumes that contain edible nuts.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas, contributing to local biodiversity.
