Hypericum strictum subsp. gracile

Hypericum strictum subsp.

gracile, commonly known as slender St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, typically from June to July.

The plant has slender, somewhat woody stems that are often prostrate or ascending, with opposite leaves that are narrow and slightly serrated. Its flowers are arranged in clusters and are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover in dry, sunny conditions.
