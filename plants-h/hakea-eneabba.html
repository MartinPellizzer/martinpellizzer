Hakea eneabba

Hakea eneabba, commonly known as the coastal hakea, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and produces cylindrical, pink to red flowers that attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. The plant is adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils. It has a woody structure with persistent, fibrous bark.

Hakea eneabba is often used in landscaping due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
