Hedychium coccineum var. angustifolium

Hedychium coccineum var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved torch ginger, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and clusters of vivid red-orange flowers that resemble torches. The leaves are narrow and elongated, giving the plant a distinctive, elegant appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This variant is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower due to its bold, showy blooms.
