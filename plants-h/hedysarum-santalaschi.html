Hedysarum santalaschi

Hedysarum santalaschi, commonly known as the Himalayan sweet clover, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central and South Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a woody root system and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Hedysarum santalaschi is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a forage plant for livestock.
