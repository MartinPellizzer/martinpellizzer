Huertia mariquitensis

Huertia mariquitensis, commonly known as the Mariquitensis houseleek, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit. The plant features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green or bluish-green in color. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or indoor cultivation.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
