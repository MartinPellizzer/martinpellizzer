Hieracium olivaceum subsp. chondroseoides

Hieracium olivaceum subsp.

chondroseoides, commonly known as the olive hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to olive-green flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a composite head with both ray and disk florets.

This subspecies is often found in temperate climates and is relatively hardy, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
