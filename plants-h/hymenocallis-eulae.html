Hymenocallis eulae

Hymenocallis eulae, commonly known as the Eula's spider lily, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy white or pale pink flowers that resemble spider-like blooms. Its foliage consists of strap-shaped leaves that are broad and evergreen, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Hymenocallis eulae is often cultivated for its beauty and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil with adequate sunlight.
