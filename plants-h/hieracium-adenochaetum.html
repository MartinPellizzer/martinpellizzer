Hieracium adenochaetum

Hieracium adenochaetum, commonly known as the hairy yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are hairy and often have a bluish-green hue. Its flowering stems bear yellow ray florets surrounded by yellow disk florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hieracium adenochaetum typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, showcasing its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
