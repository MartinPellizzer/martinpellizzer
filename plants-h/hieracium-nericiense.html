Hieracium nericiense

Hieracium nericiense, commonly known as the sea hawksbeard, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows in saline or sandy soils, thriving in maritime environments. Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer, and it is often found in dunes and cliffs.

The species is adapted to withstand harsh coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.
