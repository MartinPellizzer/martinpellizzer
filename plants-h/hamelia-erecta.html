Hamelia erecta

Hamelia erecta, commonly known as the flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and dense foliage. The plant produces vibrant orange-red tubular flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Hamelia erecta is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in full sun and well-drained soil.
