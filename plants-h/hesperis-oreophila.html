Hesperis oreophila

Hesperis oreophila, commonly known as the alpine honesty, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its small, white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its fruit is a slender pod that splits open to release seeds.

Hesperis oreophila is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators in alpine ecosystems.
