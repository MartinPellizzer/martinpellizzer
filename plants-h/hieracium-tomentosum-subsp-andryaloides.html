Hieracium tomentosum subsp. andryaloides

Hieracium tomentosum subsp.

andryaloides, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features a rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that are covered in white to grayish pubescence.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
