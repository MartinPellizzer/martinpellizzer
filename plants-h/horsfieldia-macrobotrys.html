Horsfieldia macrobotrys

Horsfieldia macrobotrys, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its flowers are often fragrant and bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and wasps.

Horsfieldia macrobotrys is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
