Hyptis chapalensis

Hyptis chapalensis, commonly known as the Mexican hyptis, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. Hyptis chapalensis typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Its medicinal properties have been studied for their potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.
