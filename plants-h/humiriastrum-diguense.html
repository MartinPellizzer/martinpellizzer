Humiriastrum diguense

Humiriastrum diguense, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble dandelions. The plant features hairy, lobed leaves and produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Humiriastrum diguense is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
