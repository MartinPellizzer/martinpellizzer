Helictotrichon parlatorei f. pubescens

Helictotrichon parlatorei f. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent leaf sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to certain regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands or along riverbanks. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet cluster, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat preferences, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
