Hiraea glabrata

Hiraea glabrata, commonly known as the smooth hiraea, is a small herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually hairy or pubescent, depending on the variety. Its flowers are small and clustered, often in green or reddish bracts, and it produces small, dry fruits.

Hiraea glabrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
