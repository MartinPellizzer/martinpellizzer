Hieracium spathophyllum subsp. longiscapum

Hieracium spathophyllum subsp.

longiscapum, commonly known as the long-stemmed hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems, typical of the genus Hieracium.

This subspecies is native to Europe and is often found in meadows and grassy areas. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces numerous small, yellow florets arranged in a head.

The plant is perennial and thrives in well-drained soils.
