Hieracium fendleri subsp. fendleri

Hieracium fendleri subsp.

fendleri, commonly known as the desert hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a perennial herb with erect, hairy stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered with fine hairs.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.
