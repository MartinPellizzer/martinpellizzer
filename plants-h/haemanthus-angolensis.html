Haemanthus angolensis

Haemanthus angolensis, commonly known as the Natal rain lily, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, umbel-like clusters of bright red or orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has strap-shaped, evergreen leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Haemanthus angolensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
