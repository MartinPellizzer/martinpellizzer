Hieracium subcurvescens

Hieracium subcurvescens, commonly known as the bent-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a wavy or curled margin, giving it its specific epithet "subcurvescens.".

The flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the genus Hieracium.
