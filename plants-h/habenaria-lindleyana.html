Habenaria lindleyana

Habenaria lindleyana, commonly known as the Lindley's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, often with intricate patterns on the petals. It typically blooms during the wet season and is adapted to grow in moist, shaded environments.

Habenaria lindleyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
