Hieracium serratifrons subsp. subcillatum

Hieracium serratifrons subsp.

subcillatum, commonly known as the serrated hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

The plant features basal rosettes of dark green, hairy leaves that are deeply lobed and serrated. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, with ray florets that are often shorter than the disk florets.

It is characterized by its finely hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance.
