Hedysarum echinus

Hedysarum echinus, commonly known as the thorned milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers in late spring. Its fruit is a legume that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Hedysarum echinus is notable for its thorny stems and its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.
