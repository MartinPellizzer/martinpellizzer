Heliotropium ventricosum

Heliotropium ventricosum, commonly known as the bladderleaf heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which often have a bluish or purplish hue. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Heliotropium ventricosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
