Hopea conduplicata

Hopea conduplicata, commonly known as the double-leaf hopea, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves. The plant has compound leaves with two pairs of leaflets, giving it its common name. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and plays a significant role in its native ecosystems.
