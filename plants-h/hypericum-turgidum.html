Hypericum turgidum

Hypericum turgidum, commonly known as the water St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typically from June to August. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Hypericum turgidum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
