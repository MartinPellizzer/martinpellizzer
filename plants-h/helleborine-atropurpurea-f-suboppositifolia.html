Helleborine atropurpurea f. suboppositifolia

Helleborine atropurpurea f. suboppositifolia, commonly known as the purple-flowered helleborine, is a rare orchid species characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of Europe and western Asia. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in a basal rosette, and its flowers are typically borne on erect, leafless stems. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.
