Hieracium coriifolioides

Hieracium coriifolioides, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features hairy, lobed leaves that give it a distinctive appearance, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
