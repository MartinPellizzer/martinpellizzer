Hieracium poecilodermum

Hieracium poecilodermum, commonly known as the purple hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features upright, hairy stems and basal leaves that are often heart-shaped or lobed. Its flowers are typically purple or pink, with ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers.

Hieracium poecilodermum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
