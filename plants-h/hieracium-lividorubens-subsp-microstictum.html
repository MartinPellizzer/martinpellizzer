Hieracium lividorubens subsp. microstictum

Hieracium lividorubens subsp.

microstictum, commonly known as the lesser wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe. It features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive yellowish-green color.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a loose cluster, typically blooming from June to August. It prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species within the dandelion family, Asteraceae.
