Hieracium bauhini subsp. erythrophylloides

Hieracium bauhini subsp.

erythrophylloides, commonly known as the red-leaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its clustered growth habit and hairy stems.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often reddish in color, which gives the plant its common name. It produces yellow flowers in dense clusters, typical of the genus Hieracium.

This subspecies is often found in grasslands and open woodlands, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
