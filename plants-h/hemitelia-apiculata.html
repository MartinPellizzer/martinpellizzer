Hemitelia apiculata

Hemitelia apiculata, commonly known as the woolly tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant has a distinctive appearance with a hairy, fibrous trunk covered in a layer of brown, velvety spores. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in forested areas.

Hemitelia apiculata plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
