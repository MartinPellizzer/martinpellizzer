Helianthus besseyi

Helianthus besseyi, commonly known as the Bessey's sunflower, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to the central and southwestern United States, where it typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant produces large, yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk of smaller florets, which are characteristic of the genus Helianthus. It is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves.

Helianthus besseyi is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions and is often used in restoration projects due to its ecological importance.
