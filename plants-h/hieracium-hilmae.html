Hieracium hilmae

Hieracium hilmae, commonly known as the Hilma's hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Hieracium hilmae is known for its resilience and is often found in high-altitude environments where few plants can survive.
