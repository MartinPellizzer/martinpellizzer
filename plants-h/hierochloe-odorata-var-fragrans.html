Hierochloe odorata var. fragrans

Hierochloe odorata var. fragrans, commonly known as sweet grass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, silky leaves that emit a sweet, honey-like aroma when crushed. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in meadows and wetlands. Its slender, tufted growth habit and light green color make it visually distinctive.

It is often used in traditional ceremonies and for its aromatic properties.
