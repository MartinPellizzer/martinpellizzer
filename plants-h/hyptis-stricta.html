Hyptis stricta

Hyptis stricta, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and sage. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. Hyptis stricta is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

Its aromatic foliage and showy flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
