Hemerocallis citrina var. vespertina

Hemerocallis citrina var. vespertina, commonly known as the evening glory daylily, is a herbaceous perennial native to China.

It features strap-like, bright yellow leaves that provide a striking contrast to its showy, orange-red flowers. The plant produces large, trumpet-shaped blooms that open in the late afternoon and close by evening, hence its common name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation in gardens.
