Helichrysum baronii

Helichrysum baronii, commonly known as the golden marguerite, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers. The plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its propagation. Its foliage is often covered in fine, soft hairs, which help reduce water loss in its native arid environment.

Helichrysum baronii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
