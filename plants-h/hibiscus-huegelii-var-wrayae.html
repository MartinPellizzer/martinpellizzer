Hibiscus huegelii var. wrayae

Hibiscus huegelii var. wrayae, commonly known as the Wray's hibiscus, is a rare and striking species native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with a contrasting yellow center. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and have a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable species for botanical study and conservation efforts.
