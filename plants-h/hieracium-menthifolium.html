Hieracium menthifolium

Hieracium menthifolium, commonly known as meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and clusters of small, yellowish to brownish flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are often hairy and have a mint-like scent, which gives it its common name.

Hieracium menthifolium is a perennial species that can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
