Huperzia brevifolia

Huperzia brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved clubmoss, is a species of evergreen fern ally native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit and small, linear leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces sporangia in cone-like structures called strobila, which are typically found at the base of the leaves. Huperzia brevifolia thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing on rocks or in forest undergrowth.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
