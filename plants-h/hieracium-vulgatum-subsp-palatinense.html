Hieracium vulgatum subsp. palatinense

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

palatinense, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with hairy surfaces, and it produces numerous small seeds that are dispersed by wind.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
