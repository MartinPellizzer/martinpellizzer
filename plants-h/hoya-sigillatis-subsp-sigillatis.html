Hoya sigillatis subsp. sigillatis

Hoya sigillatis subsp.

sigillatis, commonly known as the "Wax Plant" or "Porcelain Flower," is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture.

The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale pink, often with a subtle fragrance. It is a popular houseplant due to its low maintenance and ability to thrive in indirect light. Hoya sigillatis subsp.

sigillatis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and air-purifying qualities.
