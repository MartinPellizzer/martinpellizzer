Hieracium leithneri subsp. karakolense

Hieracium leithneri subsp.

karakolense, commonly known as Karakol hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a head.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
