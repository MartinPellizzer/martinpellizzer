Hepetis carinata

Hepetis carinata, commonly known as the zigzagged gentian, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial shade. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a zigzag pattern along the edges.

Hepetis carinata is a member of the Gentianaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
