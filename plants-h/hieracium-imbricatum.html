Hieracium imbricatum

Hieracium imbricatum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 20 to 60 centimeters, featuring upright stems covered in fine, hairy pubescence. Its basal leaves are broadly lanceolate and also bear a similar hairy texture.

The flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, contributing to its ornamental appeal in natural habitats.
