Helianthemum glutinosum var. laeve

Helianthemum glutinosum var. laeve, commonly known as the sticky sandverbena, is a low-growing, evergreen shrub native to southern Europe.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has sticky, glandular hairs on its stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or Mediterranean climates.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
