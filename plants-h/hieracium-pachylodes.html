Hieracium pachylodes

Hieracium pachylodes, commonly known as the giant hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a robust root system and can form dense colonies, often invading disturbed soils. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often reddish in color.

Hieracium pachylodes is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and environmental conditions.
