Hypericum quartinianum

Hypericum quartinianum, commonly known as the orange-stemmed St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers, which bloom in summer, and its distinctive orange-pink stems. The plant has opposite, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Hypericum quartinianum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
