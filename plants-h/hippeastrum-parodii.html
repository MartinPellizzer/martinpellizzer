Hippeastrum parodii

Hippeastrum parodii, commonly known as the Parodi amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It produces large, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often with intricate patterns. The plant features strap-like leaves that are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native arid environment. Its flowering period typically occurs in the summer, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The bulbs are perennial, allowing the plant to regrow each year under suitable conditions.
