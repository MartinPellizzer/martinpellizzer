Hedyotis auricularia

Hedyotis auricularia, commonly known as the earleaf stellaria, is a small herbaceous plant native to various regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. The plant features slender, ascending stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. Its small, white or pinkish flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom during the spring and summer seasons.

Hedyotis auricularia is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
