Hymenachne montana

Hymenachne montana, commonly known as mountain hymenachne, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in cloud forests or along stream banks.

Hymenachne montana plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates in its native ecosystem.
