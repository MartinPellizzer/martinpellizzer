Horsfieldia pilifera

Horsfieldia pilifera, commonly known as the hairy orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid rainforest environments. Its leaves are usually long and narrow, and it produces small, white or pale yellow blooms.

Horsfieldia pilifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
