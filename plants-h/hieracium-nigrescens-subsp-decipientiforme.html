Hieracium nigrescens subsp. decipientiforme

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

decipientiforme, commonly known as the false dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a rough texture, while the stems are upright and often branched.

It prefers moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
