Hibiscus anomalus

Hibiscus anomalus, commonly known as the Hawaiian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 5 meters. The plant features showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a prominent yellow or orange center. Its leaves are usually ovate or elliptical, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Hibiscus anomalus thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to the tropical climate of Hawaii.
