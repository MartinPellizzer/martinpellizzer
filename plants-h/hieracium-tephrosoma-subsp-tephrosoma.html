Hieracium tephrosoma subsp. tephrosoma

Hieracium tephrosoma subsp.

tephrosoma, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It features basal and upright stems with hairy leaves that are typically lance-shaped and have a rough texture.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in dense, flat-topped clusters called capitula, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common weed in temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.
