Haworthia parksiana

Haworthia parksiana, commonly known as the Zulu bulb, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rosette-shaped leaves. The leaves are typically fleshy, with a bluish-green hue and often bear white tubercles or markings. It produces small, white or yellowish flowers on slender stems during the warmer months.

This plant is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
