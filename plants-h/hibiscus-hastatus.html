Hibiscus hastatus

Hibiscus hastatus, commonly known as the scarlet hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

Hibiscus hastatus thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
