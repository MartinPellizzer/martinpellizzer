Hieracium murorum subsp. triangulilacerum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

triangulilacerum, commonly known as the triangular-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering heads.

The leaves are typically lanceolate and have a distinctive triangular shape with serrated edges. It produces yellow flowers in composite inflorescences, and its growth habit is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

This subspecies is noted for its variability in leaf morphology and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
