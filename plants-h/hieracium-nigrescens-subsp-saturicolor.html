Hieracium nigrescens subsp. saturicolor

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

saturicolor, commonly known as the purple hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves and produces showy, yellow to orange composite flowers.

The plant is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in meadows and grasslands. It has hairy stems and is typically perennial, with the ability to form colonies through rhizomes.

The subspecies is distinguished by its darker, more saturated flower color compared to other members of the species.
