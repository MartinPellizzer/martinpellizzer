Hosta sieboldii f. leucantha

Hosta sieboldii f. leucantha, commonly known as the White Form of Siebold's Hosta, is a unique variant of the hosta plant characterized by its striking white-variegated foliage.

This cultivar is prized for its elegant, broad, and slightly wavy leaves that display a pale green to nearly white coloration. It typically grows to a height of around 12 to 18 inches, making it a compact and ornamental addition to shaded gardens. The plant produces small, fragrant, and pale yellow flowers in late summer, adding a subtle touch of color.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
