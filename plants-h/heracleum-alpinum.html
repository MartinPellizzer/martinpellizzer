Heracleum alpinum

Heracleum alpinum, commonly known as Alpine cowparade, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbellate inflorescence.

It is a herbaceous plant that typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, thriving in rocky and well-drained soils. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers. It is often found in mountainous areas of Europe and Asia.

Heracleum alpinum is noted for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
