Hieracium juranum subsp. cistifolium

Hieracium juranum subsp.

cistifolium, commonly known as the mountain hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
