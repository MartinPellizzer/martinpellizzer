Helianthemum origanifolium var. algarbiense

Helianthemum origanifolium var. algarbiense, commonly known as the Algarbiense rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its evergreen leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and resemble those of oregano, giving it its species name. It thrives in rocky and sunny environments, often found in coastal and Mediterranean regions.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to harsh conditions.
