Hymenophyllum remotum

Hymenophyllum remotum, commonly known as the remote fern, is a small, delicate fern that belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant features a unique structure with specialized leaves called "hymenophylls," which are thin and often covered in tiny, hair-like structures. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rock crevices, and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Hymenophyllum remotum plays a role in maintaining ecological balance by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for microorganisms.
