Huperzia erythrocaulos

Huperzia erythrocaulos, commonly known as the red-stemmed huperzia, is a species of fern-like plant belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, segmented stems that often display a reddish hue, especially in younger growth. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are typically clustered in small clusters along the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate regions with high humidity.

Huperzia erythrocaulos is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
