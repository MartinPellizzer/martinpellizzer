Heinsia pubescens

Heinsia pubescens, commonly known as the woolly heath, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Heinsia pubescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
