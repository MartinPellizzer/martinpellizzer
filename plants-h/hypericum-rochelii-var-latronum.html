Hypericum rochelii var. latronum

Hypericum rochelii var. latronum, commonly known as the golden-stemmed St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, orange-red seed capsules. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
