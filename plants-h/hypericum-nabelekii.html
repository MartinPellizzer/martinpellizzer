Hypericum nabelekii

Hypericum nabelekii, commonly known as the Nabelek's St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Hypericum nabelekii is characterized by its medicinal properties, containing compounds such as hypericin and pseudohypericin, which have been studied for their potential antidepressant effects.
