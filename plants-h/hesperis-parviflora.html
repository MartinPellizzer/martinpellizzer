Hesperis parviflora

Hesperis parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a biennial or short-lived perennial that produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. The plant has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually hairy as well. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
