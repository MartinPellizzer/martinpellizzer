Hieracium verbascifolium subsp. mallobrachion

Hieracium verbascifolium subsp.

mallobrachion, commonly known as the broad-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its robust, upright stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and recognizable member of the hawkweed group.
