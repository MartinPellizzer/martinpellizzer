Hieracium strictipilum

Hieracium strictipilum, commonly known as the upright hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves that are broadly lanceolate and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a single head, and are surrounded by pale green bracts.

Hieracium strictipilum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
