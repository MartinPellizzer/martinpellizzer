Hieracium adunantidens

Hieracium adunantidens, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant typically grows in open grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in dense clusters.

Hieracium adunantidens is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in some regions.
