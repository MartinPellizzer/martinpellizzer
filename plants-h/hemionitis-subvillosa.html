Hemionitis subvillosa

Hemionitis subvillosa, commonly known as the woolly hemionitis, is a rare and delicate fern native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy fronds that give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperature fluctuations. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are pollinated by wind, and it reproduces through spores rather than seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
