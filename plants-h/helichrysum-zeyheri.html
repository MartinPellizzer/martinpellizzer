Helichrysum zeyheri

Helichrysum zeyheri, commonly known as the Zeyher's immortelle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that produces small, yellow-orange flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and its ability to thrive in dry, sandy soils. It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it withstands harsh environmental conditions.

Helichrysum zeyheri is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
