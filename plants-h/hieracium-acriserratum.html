Hieracium acriserratum

Hieracium acriserratum, commonly known as the serrated hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is an herbaceous perennial that spreads through rhizomes and is often found in temperate climates.
