Hypericum perforatum f. fimbriatum

Hypericum perforatum f. fimbriatum, commonly known as the fringed St. John's wort, is a botanical variant of the common St. John's wort.

It is characterized by its distinctive fringed leaves, which give it its specific epithet "fimbriatum.". This plant typically grows in sunny, well-drained soils and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. It produces yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in summer.

The plant is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
