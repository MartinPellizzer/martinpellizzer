Hieracium bifidum subsp. brizii

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

brizii, commonly known as the bristle hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm.

The plant features a basal rosette of leaves that are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster, with each flower having five petals.

The subspecies is distinguished by its bristly, glandular hairs and its ability to hybridize with other Hieracium species.
