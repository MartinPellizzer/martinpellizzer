Holographis virgata subsp. virgata

Holographis virgata subsp.

virgata, commonly known as the slender false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States. It features slender, erect stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, often with a reddish tinge.

The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers in racemes during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It is adapted to moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
