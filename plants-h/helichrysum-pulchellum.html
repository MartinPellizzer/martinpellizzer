Helichrysum pulchellum

Helichrysum pulchellum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that remain vibrant even when dried. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, papery bracts give it a fluffy appearance, and it is often used in floral arrangements due to its longevity.

Helichrysum pulchellum is also valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
