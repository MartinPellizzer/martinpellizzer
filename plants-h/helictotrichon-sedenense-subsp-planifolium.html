Helictotrichon sedenense subsp. planifolium

Helictotrichon sedenense subsp.

planifolium, commonly known as the smoothleaf bluegrass, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit.

The plant features narrow, flat leaves that are typically smooth and have a bluish-green color. Its small, spikelet-based flowers are usually inconspicuous, and it blooms during the late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is often found in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.
