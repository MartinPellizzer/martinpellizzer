Hieracium praecox subsp. pljackavicense

Hieracium praecox subsp.

pljackavicense, commonly known as the Balkan hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to orange composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has basal and upright stems with hairy leaves, and its flowering period occurs in the spring and early summer.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its potential use in traditional herbal medicine.
