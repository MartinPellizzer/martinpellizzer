Hieracium pilosella subsp. acuminatissimum

Hieracium pilosella subsp.

acuminatissimum, commonly known as the sharp-tipped hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer.

The subspecies is native to southern Europe and is often found in meadows and grassy areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
