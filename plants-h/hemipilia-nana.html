Hemipilia nana

Hemipilia nana, commonly known as the dwarf hemipilia, is a small, herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces pale green to white flowers with a subtle scent, which attract various pollinators. Hemipilia nana thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
