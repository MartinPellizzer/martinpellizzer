Hieracium aymericianum subsp. lagraveanum

Hieracium aymericianum subsp.

lagraveanum, commonly known as the Lagrave's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. It has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and its stems are hairy as well.

This subspecies is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
