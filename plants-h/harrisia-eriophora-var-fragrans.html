Harrisia eriophora var. fragrans

Harrisia eriophora var. fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant harrisia, is a cactus species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spineless stems and clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and pleasant scent.

Its flowers are pollinated primarily by nocturnal insects and bats.
