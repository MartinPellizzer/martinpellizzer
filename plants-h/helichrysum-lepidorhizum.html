Helichrysum lepidorhizum

Helichrysum lepidorhizum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, yellow-orange flowers that resemble small suns, which bloom in late summer. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit and is often used in ornamental gardening for its long-lasting blooms. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Helichrysum lepidorhizum is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.
