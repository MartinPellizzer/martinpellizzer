Hyptis mutabilis var. pavoniana

Hyptis mutabilis var. pavoniana, commonly known as the purple hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

The species is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and floral display.
