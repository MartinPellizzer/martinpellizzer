Heliotropium flintii

Heliotropium flintii, commonly known as Flint's heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or violet in color, with five petals and a central disc of stamens. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Heliotropium flintii is adapted to dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it a common sight in arid regions.
