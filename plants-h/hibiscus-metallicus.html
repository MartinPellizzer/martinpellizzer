Hibiscus metallicus

Hibiscus metallicus, commonly known as the silverleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The leaves are typically gray-green with a metallic sheen, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Hibiscus metallicus is a perennial shrub that blooms from summer to fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
