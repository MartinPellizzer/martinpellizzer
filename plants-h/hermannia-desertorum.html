Hermannia desertorum

Hermannia desertorum, commonly known as the desert bluemouth, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has small, narrow leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Hermannia desertorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xerophytic gardens.
