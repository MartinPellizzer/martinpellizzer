Hoya meliflua subsp. fraterna

Hoya meliflua subsp.

fraterna, commonly known as the "Sweet Milk Hoya," is a compact, trailing plant native to Southeast Asia. It features thick, fleshy stems and produces clusters of waxy, pale green to white flowers with a subtle fragrance.

The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical, with a smooth texture and a slight waxy coating. This species is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

It is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its attractive foliage and unique flowering habit.
