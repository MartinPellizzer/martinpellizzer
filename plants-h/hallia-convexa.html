Hallia convexa

Hallia convexa, commonly known as the waxy begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Hallia convexa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and vibrant blooms.
