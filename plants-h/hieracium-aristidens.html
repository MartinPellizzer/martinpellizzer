Hieracium aristidens

Hieracium aristidens, commonly known as the tall hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed sites such as roadsides and meadows, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are often reddish in color.

Hieracium aristidens is native to Europe and has been introduced to other regions, where it can sometimes become invasive.
