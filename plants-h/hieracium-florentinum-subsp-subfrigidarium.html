Hieracium florentinum subsp. subfrigidarium

Hieracium florentinum subsp.

subfrigidarium, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky soils at high altitudes.

The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in late summer. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and cold tolerance, adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

This subspecies is often found in subalpine and alpine zones where it thrives in cool, moist environments.
