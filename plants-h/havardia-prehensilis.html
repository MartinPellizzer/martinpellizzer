Havardia prehensilis

Havardia prehensilis, commonly known as the climbing hibiscus, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, orange, or yellow. The plant has a climbing habit, often using its tendrils to attach itself to supports. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and adaptability to various growing conditions.
