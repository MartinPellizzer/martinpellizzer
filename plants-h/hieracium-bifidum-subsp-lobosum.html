Hieracium bifidum subsp. lobosum

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

lobosum, commonly known as the lobed hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer.

The leaves are typically lobed, giving the plant its subspecific name, and are covered in fine hairs. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and disturbed soils.

This species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
