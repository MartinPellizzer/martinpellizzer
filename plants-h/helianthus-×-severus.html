Helianthus × severus

Helianthus × severus, commonly known as the Russian sunflower, is a hybrid species within the sunflower genus.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow-orange flower heads and robust, upright stems. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 meters, making it a striking addition to gardens and landscapes. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

This species is native to eastern Europe and western Siberia, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.
