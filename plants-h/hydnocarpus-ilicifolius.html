Hydnocarpus ilicifolius

Hydnocarpus ilicifolius, commonly known as the prickly ash or wild pepper, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces woody fruits that contain toxic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are often used as a spice in some cuisines due to their pungent flavor.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
