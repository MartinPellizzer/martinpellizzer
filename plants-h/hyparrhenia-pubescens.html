Hyparrhenia pubescens

Hyparrhenia pubescens, commonly known as the hairy grass or African silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Hyparrhenia pubescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and erosion control in its native habitats.
