Hibiscus nutans

Hibiscus nutans, commonly known as the drooping hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range from pink to red, with a prominent central pistil. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Hibiscus nutans thrives in humid, well-drained soil and is often found in moist woodlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
