Hieracium frigidulans

Hieracium frigidulans, commonly known as the frosty hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disc.

Hieracium frigidulans is known for its ability to thrive in cooler climates, hence its common name.
