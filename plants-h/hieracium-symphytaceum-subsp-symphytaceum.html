Hieracium symphytaceum subsp. symphytaceum

Hieracium symphytaceum subsp.

symphytaceum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellowish-orange composite flowers.

The plant typically has basal leaves that are broadly lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows, and it is often found in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural and urban environments.
