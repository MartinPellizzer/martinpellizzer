Hartogia ciliata

Hartogia ciliata, commonly known as the silky hakea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy environments, characterized by its dense, wiry stems and small, needle-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow flowers, which bloom in spring and attract various pollinators. Hartogia ciliata is known for its ability to thrive in harsh, arid conditions due to its adaptations such as reduced leaf surface area and deep root systems.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora and a subject of interest in ecological studies.
