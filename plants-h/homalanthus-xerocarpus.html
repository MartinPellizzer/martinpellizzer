Homalanthus xerocarpus

Homalanthus xerocarpus, commonly known as the desert pomegranate, is a shrub native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that resemble pomegranates, though they are not closely related. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in nutrient-poor soils.

The species is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a hardy plant in xerophytic ecosystems.
