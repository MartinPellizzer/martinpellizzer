Hieracium lactucaceum subsp. aurigeranum

Hieracium lactucaceum subsp.

aurigeranum, commonly known as the golden hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the sunflower family (Asteraceae). It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and hairy, often reddish stems.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates.
