Helianthemum acuminatum

Helianthemum acuminatum, commonly known as the acuminated rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, fleshy leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Helianthemum acuminatum is known for its resilience to drought and salt spray, making it well adapted to harsh environments.
