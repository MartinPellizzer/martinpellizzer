Humboltia pruinosa

Humboltia pruinosa, commonly known as the silverleaf humboldtia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its silvery, powdery coating on its leaves, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in coastal regions with a Mediterranean climate, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

Humboltia pruinosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
