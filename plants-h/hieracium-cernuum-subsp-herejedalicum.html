Hieracium cernuum subsp. herejedalicum

Hieracium cernuum subsp.

herejedalicum, commonly known as the nodding hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its drooping, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and its stems are usually unbranched.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
