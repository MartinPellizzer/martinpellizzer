Helianthus missouriensis

Helianthus missouriensis, commonly known as the Missouri sunflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the central United States.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring sturdy stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces large, bright yellow composite flowers with a prominent central disk, blooming from late summer to early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped, rough to the touch, and have a distinct silvery-gray underside.

This species is well adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in open woodlands and prairies.
