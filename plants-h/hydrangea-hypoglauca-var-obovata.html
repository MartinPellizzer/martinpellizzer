Hydrangea hypoglauca var. obovata

Hydrangea hypoglauca var. obovata, commonly known as the white-veined hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range from white to pink or blue depending on soil pH. The leaves are ovate to obovate in shape, with a distinctive white or pale green veins. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to adapt to various growing conditions.
