Hedyotis capitellata var. mollissima

Hedyotis capitellata var. mollissima, commonly known as the silky knotweed, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and small, bright yellow flowers clustered in terminal spikes. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands.

This variant is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
