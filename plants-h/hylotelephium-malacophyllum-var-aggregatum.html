Hylotelephium malacophyllum var. aggregatum

Hylotelephium malacophyllum var. aggregatum, commonly known as the clustered stonecrop, is a perennial succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in tight clusters, giving it a compact and ornamental appearance. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements make it suitable for a variety of garden settings.
