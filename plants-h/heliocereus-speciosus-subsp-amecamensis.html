Heliocereus speciosus subsp. amecamensis

Heliocereus speciosus subsp.

amecamensis, commonly known as the Amecamense night bloomer, is a cactus species native to Mexico. It is characterized by its spineless stems and produces large, fragrant, white flowers that open at night.

The plant is a member of the Cactaceae family and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by moths and bats, which are active during the night.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its striking floral display.
