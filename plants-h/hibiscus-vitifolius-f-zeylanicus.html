Hibiscus vitifolius f. zeylanicus

Hibiscus vitifolius f. zeylanicus, commonly known as the Ceylon hibiscus, is a tropical shrub characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers.

It typically grows in coastal areas and is native to Sri Lanka, India, and parts of Southeast Asia. The plant produces bright red or orange flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
