Hieracium maeandrinum

Hieracium maeandrinum, commonly known as the Maeandrian hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, and its leaves are usually hairy and lance-shaped.

Hieracium maeandrinum is often considered a weed due to its rapid spread and ability to thrive in disturbed environments.
