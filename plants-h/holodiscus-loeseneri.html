Holodiscus loeseneri

Holodiscus loeseneri, commonly known as the yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets with yellowish to brownish flowers, which are small and clustered.

Holodiscus loeseneri is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
