Hieracium bracteolatum subsp. koracis

Hieracium bracteolatum subsp.

koracis, commonly known as the Balkan hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils. It has hairy stems and leaves that are often glandular, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is noted for its limited distribution and is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological and genetic traits.
