Hieracium cereolinum

Hieracium cereolinum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and the leaves are lance-shaped with a hairy texture.

Hieracium cereolinum is often found in disturbed habitats and can spread aggressively through rhizomes.
