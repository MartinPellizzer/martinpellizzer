Hypericum montanum subsp. elegantissimum

Hypericum montanum subsp.

elegantissimum, commonly known as the alpine St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant features small, elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often covered in fine, glandular dots. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and slopes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its historical use in traditional medicine.
