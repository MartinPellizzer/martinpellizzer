Hieracium alatum subsp. barreranum

Hieracium alatum subsp.

barreranum, commonly known as the broad-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its hairy, broadly lance-shaped leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It is native to parts of South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

This subspecies is notable for its robust growth and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
