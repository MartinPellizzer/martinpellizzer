Hieracium furcatum subsp. flocciferum

Hieracium furcatum subsp.

flocciferum, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

The plant features a basal rosette of soft, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its subspecies characteristics include a distinctive woolly appearance due to dense pubescence on both foliage and stems.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal historical uses.
