Hemionitis feei

Hemionitis feei, commonly known as the Chinese brake fern, is a unique fern species native to China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, leathery, and often hairy fronds that are typically divided into narrow, linear segments. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or along stream banks. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of many ferns.

Hemionitis feei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
