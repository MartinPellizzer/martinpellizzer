Hippocrepis carpetana

Hippocrepis carpetana, commonly known as the Carpathian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and tall, slender flowering stems. The flowers are typically pink or purple, with four petals arranged in a cross shape, typical of the Brassicaceae family. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in many regions.
