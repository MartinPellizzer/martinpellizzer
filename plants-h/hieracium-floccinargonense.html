Hieracium floccinargonense

Hieracium floccinargonense, commonly known as the fluffy-haired hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant is distinguished by its soft, woolly leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
