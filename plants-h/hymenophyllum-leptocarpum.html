Hymenophyllum leptocarpum

Hymenophyllum leptocarpum, commonly known as the narrow-fruit moss, is a species of liverwort belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are typically less than 1 centimeter in length. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on rock surfaces or tree trunks in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures, known as sporophytes, are small and closely clustered, contributing to its unique appearance.

Hymenophyllum leptocarpum plays a role in ecological systems by providing habitat for microorganisms and contributing to nutrient cycling in its environment.
