Hibiscus malvarosa

Hibiscus malvarosa, commonly known as the red hibiscus, is a tropical flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from red to pink and yellow. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

Hibiscus malvarosa thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
