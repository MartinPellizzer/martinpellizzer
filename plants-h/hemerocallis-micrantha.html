Hemerocallis micrantha

Hemerocallis micrantha, commonly known as the dwarf daylily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant typically blooms in early summer and has a short flowering period, lasting only a few days. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Hemerocallis micrantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in gardens.
