Hieracium sericicaule

Hieracium sericicaule, commonly known as the silky hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, hairy stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Hieracium sericicaule is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, where it thrives in full sun.
