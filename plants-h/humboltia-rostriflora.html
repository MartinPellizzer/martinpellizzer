Humboltia rostriflora

Humboltia rostriflora, commonly known as the rostriflorous humpback, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native habitat. Humboltia rostriflora is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral ecosystems, where it contributes to the local biodiversity.
