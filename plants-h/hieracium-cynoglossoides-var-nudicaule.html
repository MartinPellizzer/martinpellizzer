Hieracium cynoglossoides var. nudicaule

Hieracium cynoglossoides var. nudicaule, commonly known as the naked-stem hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It features slender, hairy stems and lacks leaves along its lower stem, giving it its common name.

Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
