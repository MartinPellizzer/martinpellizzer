Hortonia angustifolia

Hortonia angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf hortonia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. It is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.

Hortonia angustifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
