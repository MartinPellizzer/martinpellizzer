Hieracium bifidum subsp. manotrichum

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

manotrichum, commonly known as the Manotrichum hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in late summer. Its subspecies is characterized by its distinctive, finely divided leaves and a more compact growth form compared to other hawkweed species.

It is often found in disturbed or nutrient-rich soils and is part of the Asteraceae family.
