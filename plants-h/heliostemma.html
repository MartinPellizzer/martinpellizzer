Heliostemma

Heliostemma, commonly known as the sunflower vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems and large, showy yellow flowers that resemble sunflowers. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, cylindrical fruits containing numerous seeds. Heliostemma thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
