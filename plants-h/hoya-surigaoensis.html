Hoya surigaoensis

Hoya surigaoensis, commonly known as the Surigao wax plant, is a species of flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and waxy, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It is a slow-growing plant that thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.

Hoya surigaoensis is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
