Hutchinsia alba var. bipinnatifida

Hutchinsia alba var. bipinnatifida, commonly known as the white hutchinsia, is a rare and striking plant native to the arid regions of southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its pale, almost white, foliage that reflects the harsh sunlight of its environment. The plant exhibits a unique bipinnatifid leaf structure, which means its leaves are deeply divided into two rows of smaller, needle-like segments. This adaptation helps reduce water loss and allows it to thrive in extreme conditions.

It is often found growing on rocky outcrops and is a subject of interest for botanists due to its specialized morphology and limited distribution.
