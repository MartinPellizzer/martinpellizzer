Hieracium orbolense

Hieracium orbolense, commonly known as the Orbolense hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while its flowers are yellow with prominent central disks.

Hieracium orbolense is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
