Hieracium glaucinum subsp. ovalifolium

Hieracium glaucinum subsp.

ovalifolium, commonly known as the oval-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its flowering stems are usually unbranched and bear several flower heads.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its showy inflorescences and medicinal properties in some species.
