Hibiscus diversifolius subsp. genuinus

Hibiscus diversifolius subsp.

genuinus, commonly known as the Florida hibiscus, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. It blooms from late spring to early fall, producing nectar that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in wetland habitats.
