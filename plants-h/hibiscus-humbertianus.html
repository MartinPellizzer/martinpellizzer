Hibiscus humbertianus

Hibiscus humbertianus, commonly known as the Humbert's hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and thrives in sunny, humid conditions. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its stems are woody and often covered in fine hairs.

Hibiscus humbertianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
