Hedysarum sambuense

Hedysarum sambuense, commonly known as the Sambu sweetclover, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate to cold climates. Hedysarum sambuense is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it an important plant in soil enrichment.

Its seeds are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
