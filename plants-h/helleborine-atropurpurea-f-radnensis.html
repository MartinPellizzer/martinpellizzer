Helleborine atropurpurea f. radnensis

Helleborine atropurpurea f. radnensis, commonly known as the purple helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple to blackish leaves and flowers that range from purple to reddish-brown. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers are spurred and have three sepals and three petals, with the lip being distinctive in shape.

This orchid is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its striking appearance.
