Hydrangea kawagoeana var. grosseserrata

Hydrangea kawagoeana var. grosseserrata, commonly known as the Japanese mountain hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from white to pink, depending on soil pH. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and thrives in partial shade with well-drained soil. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with serrated margins that give it its species name.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral displays.
