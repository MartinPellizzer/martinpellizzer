Hariota alternata

Hariota alternata, commonly known as the silverleaf harebell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to white. The plant has narrow, silver-tomentose leaves that give it its common name and help reduce water loss in arid environments. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun.

Hariota alternata is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
