Habenaria humilior

Habenaria humilior, commonly known as the small common orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant features a slender, leafy stem with a single or few flowers that are usually pale green or white. Its flowers have three sepals and three petals, with the lateral petals often resembling a lip.

The orchid's roots are fibrous, and it relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient absorption.
