Hieracium nigrescens subsp. bathylepioides

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

bathylepioides, commonly known as the dark-hued hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has slender, upright stems with basal leaves that are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs.

Its flowers are yellow, arranged in dense clusters, and bloom from late spring to early summer.
