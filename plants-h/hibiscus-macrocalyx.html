Hibiscus macrocalyx

Hibiscus macrocalyx, commonly known as the giant hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, with five broad, wavy petals. The plant produces yellow stamens and a prominent, hairy pistil, typical of the Malvaceae family. Its leaves are heart-shaped, broad, and often have a velvety texture.

Hibiscus macrocalyx thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
