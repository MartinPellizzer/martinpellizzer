Hieracium densicapillum subsp. hispidulum

Hieracium densicapillum subsp.

hispidulum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features basal and stem leaves that are lanceolate to ovate, with hairy surfaces, and produces yellow composite flowers in clusters. Its flowers are composed of ray florets and disc florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dense, hairy foliage and robust growth habit.
