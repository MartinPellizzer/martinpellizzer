Harmogia pentandra

Harmogia pentandra, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from purple to red, often with a distinctive pattern of lines and spots. The plant produces small, greenish-white fruits that are edible and contain seeds. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture.

Harmogia pentandra is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
