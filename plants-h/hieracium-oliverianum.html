Hieracium oliverianum

Hieracium oliverianum, commonly known as the Oliver's hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, with yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
