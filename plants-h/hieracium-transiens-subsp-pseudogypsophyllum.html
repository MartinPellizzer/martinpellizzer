Hieracium transiens subsp. pseudogypsophyllum

Hieracium transiens subsp.

pseudogypsophyllum, commonly known as the gypsum hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Europe. It typically grows in calcareous soils, often found on rocky or gravelly substrates.

The plant has basal rosettes of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces yellow flowers in early spring. It is characterized by its hairy stems and small, daisy-like inflorescences.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its range.
