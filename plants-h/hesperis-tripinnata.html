Hesperis tripinnata

Hesperis tripinnata, commonly known as the sweet rocket, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, round pods that contain seeds. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Hesperis tripinnata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
