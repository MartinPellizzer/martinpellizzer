Hemizonia fasciculata var. ramosissima

Hemizonia fasciculata var. ramosissima, commonly known as the ramose hemizonia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves that are arranged in a dense, mat-like formation.

This species is well adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and open woodlands.
