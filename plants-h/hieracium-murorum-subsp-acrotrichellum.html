Hieracium murorum subsp. acrotrichellum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

acrotrichellum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

This subspecies is distinguished by its acrotrichellum morphology, which refers to the specific arrangement and structure of its leaves and stems.
