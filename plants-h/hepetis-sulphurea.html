Hepetis sulphurea

Hepetis sulphurea, commonly known as the golden heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and produces small, bright yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in coastal and inland fynbos regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
