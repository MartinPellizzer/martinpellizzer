Helenium virginicum

Helenium virginicum, commonly known as the American sneezeweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in open woods and meadows, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its bright orange to red flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Helenium virginicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
