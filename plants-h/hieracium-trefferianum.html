Hieracium trefferianum

Hieracium trefferianum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are alternate, rough to the touch, and often have a serrated edge.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing bright yellow petals that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
