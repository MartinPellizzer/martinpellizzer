Huperzia cuernavacensis

Huperzia cuernavacensis, commonly known as the Cuernavaca huperzia, is a species of fern native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Huperziaceae and is characterized by its simple, lanceolate leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests and rocky outcrops. Its sporangia are clustered in strobila, a unique reproductive structure typical of certain ferns.

Huperzia cuernavacensis is valued for its ecological role and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
