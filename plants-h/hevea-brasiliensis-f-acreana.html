Hevea brasiliensis f. acreana

Hevea brasiliensis f. acreana, commonly known as the Amazon rubber tree, is a variant of the rubber tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves and produces latex, which is harvested for natural rubber. This form is notable for its adaptability to humid tropical climates and its role in regional biodiversity. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, moist environment to flourish.

It plays an important ecological role in supporting various flora and fauna within its native habitat.
