Hieracium vulgatum subsp. sanguinolentum

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

sanguinolentum, commonly known as the bloody hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow to orange-red flower heads, which consist of both ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and often have a reddish tinge, contributing to its striking appearance.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
