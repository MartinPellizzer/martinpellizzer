Hieracium brachiatum subsp. psilobrachion

Hieracium brachiatum subsp.

psilobrachion, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its flowers are typically composed of numerous small florets arranged in a disc-like structure.

The species is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in temperate climates.
