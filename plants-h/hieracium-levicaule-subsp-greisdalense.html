Hieracium levicaule subsp. greisdalense

Hieracium levicaule subsp.

greisdalense, commonly known as the Greisdalen hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Scandinavia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and rocky areas. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive basal arrangement.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its range.
