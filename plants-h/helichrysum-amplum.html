Helichrysum amplum

Helichrysum amplum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, yellow to golden-orange flowers that remain vibrant even after drying. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and grows in a compact, bushy form. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Helichrysum amplum is also valued for its ornamental use and has been used in traditional medicine.
