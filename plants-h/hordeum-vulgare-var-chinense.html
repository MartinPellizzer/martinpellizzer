Hordeum vulgare var. chinense

Hordeum vulgare var. chinense, commonly known as Chinese barley, is a variant of the common barley species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical spikelets and small, pale-yellow grains. This plant typically grows in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils. Its botanical features include hollow stems and leaf blades that are usually narrow and slightly hairy.

It is primarily cultivated for its grains, which are used in traditional foods and beverages.
