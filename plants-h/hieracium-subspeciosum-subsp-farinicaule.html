Hieracium subspeciosum subsp. farinicaule

Hieracium subspeciosum subsp.

farinicaule, commonly known as the farinose hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering heads that produce yellow disk flowers.

The leaves are typically covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
