Hyacinthus orchioides

Hyacinthus orchioides, commonly known as the orchid hyacinth, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features erect, branched stems that bear clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers resembling orchids. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, showy flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and pleasant fragrance.
