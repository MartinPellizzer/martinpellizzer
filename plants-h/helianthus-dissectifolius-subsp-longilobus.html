Helianthus dissectifolius subsp. longilobus

Helianthus dissectifolius subsp.

longilobus, commonly known as the longlobed sunflower, is a perennial plant native to the southern United States. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its large, showy yellow flower heads surrounded by pale green to white ray florets.

The plant has finely dissected, lobed leaves that are typically alternate and hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
