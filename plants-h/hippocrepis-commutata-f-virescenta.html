Hippocrepis commutata f. virescenta

Hippocrepis commutata f. virescenta, commonly known as the green-leaved bellflower, is a horticultural variant of the common bellflower.

It is characterized by its distinctive greenish foliage, which contrasts with the typical blue or purple hues of the species. The plant produces bell-shaped flowers that are usually a pale blue or white, often with a yellowish throat. It is a compact, herbaceous perennial that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

This form is favored in gardens for its unique coloration and ornamental value.
