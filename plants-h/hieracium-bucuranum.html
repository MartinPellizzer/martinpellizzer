Hieracium bucuranum

Hieracium bucuranum, commonly known as the Bucur's hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium bucuranum is known for its ability to thrive in various soil types and its role in supporting pollinators.
