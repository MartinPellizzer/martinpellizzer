Helia thamnoides

Helia thamnoides, commonly known as the golden sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has small, narrow leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Helia thamnoides is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
