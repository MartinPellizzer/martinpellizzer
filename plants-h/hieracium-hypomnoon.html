Hieracium hypomnoon

Hieracium hypomnoon, commonly known as the lesser spearthistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with a rough texture, and the flowers are yellow, often appearing in clusters.

It is considered a weed in some regions but also has historical uses in traditional medicine.
