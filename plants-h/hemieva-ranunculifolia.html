Hemieva ranunculifolia

Hemieva ranunculifolia, commonly known as the Florida bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along the coastlines of Florida. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually blue or purple in color, with five petals. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a distinctive veining pattern.

Hemieva ranunculifolia is also known for its ability to thrive in coastal environments and is considered a symbol of Florida's natural beauty.
