Helianthemum origanifolium subsp. andalusicum

Helianthemum origanifolium subsp.

andalusicum, commonly known as the Andalusian rock rose, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to southern Spain. It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming in spring.

The plant has aromatic, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for rocky or coastal environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and ornamental plant, often used in xeriscaping and coastal gardens.
