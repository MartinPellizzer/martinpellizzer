Hydrangea preslii

Hydrangea preslii, commonly known as the mountain hydrangea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from white to pink, depending on soil pH. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soil. Its leaves are large, ovate, and have a glossy texture, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Hydrangea preslii is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
