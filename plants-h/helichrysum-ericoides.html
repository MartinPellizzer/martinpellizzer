Helichrysum ericoides

Helichrysum ericoides, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that are densely packed and remain intact even after drying, giving it its common name. The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Helichrysum ericoides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in floral arrangements.
