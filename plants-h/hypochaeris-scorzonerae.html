Hypochaeris scorzonerae

Hypochaeris scorzonerae, commonly known as the scarlet hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer, followed by fluffy, seed-filled capsules.

Hypochaeris scorzonerae is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in various habitats.
