Haplophyllum pilosum

Haplophyllum pilosum, commonly known as the hairy leaf haworthia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern. The plant features thick, fleshy leaves covered in a dense layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months.

Haplophyllum pilosum is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental appeal.
