Hieracium spicatum

Hieracium spicatum, commonly known as spiked hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features upright, hairy stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which give it its common name.

It thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and is known for its aggressive growth habit.
