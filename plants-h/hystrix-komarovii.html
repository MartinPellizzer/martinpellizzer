Hystrix komarovii

Hystrix komarovii, commonly known as the Komarov's spiny scale.

It is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia, characterized by its dense, spiny foliage that serves as a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water and survive in harsh, dry environments.

Hystrix komarovii is often cultivated in xerophytic gardens for its unique aesthetic and low maintenance requirements.
