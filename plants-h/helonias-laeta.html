Helonias laeta

Helonias laeta, commonly known as the white marsh orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in environments with abundant sunlight. Helonias laeta has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
