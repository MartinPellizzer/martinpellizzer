Hieracium aurantiacum subsp. claropurpureum

Hieracium aurantiacum subsp.

claropurpureum, commonly known as the purple-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and distinctive purple-tinged leaves.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more pronounced purple hues in the foliage compared to other members of the species.
