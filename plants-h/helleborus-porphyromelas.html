Helleborus porphyromelas

Helleborus porphyromelas, commonly known as the purple hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southeastern Europe.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy evergreen leaves and its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from deep purple to almost black. The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, making it one of the earliest flowering plants in the hellebore genus. It prefers shady, moist environments and is often found in woodland habitats.

Helleborus porphyromelas is known for its toxicity, containing various cardiac glycosides that can be harmful if ingested.
