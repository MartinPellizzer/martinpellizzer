Hieracium juranum var. wimmerioides

Hieracium juranum var. wimmerioides, commonly known as the Wimmer's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lanceolate, with a hairy surface, and its yellow flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
