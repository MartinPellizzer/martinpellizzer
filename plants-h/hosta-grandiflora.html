Hosta grandiflora

Hosta grandiflora, commonly known as the giant hosta, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Asia.

It features broad, waxy leaves that can grow up to 30 inches in length, often with a striking blue-green or variegated coloration. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in late summer, typically in shades of white, yellow, or pale purple. It thrives in partial to full shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its foliage remains attractive throughout the growing season, offering ornamental value even when not in bloom.
