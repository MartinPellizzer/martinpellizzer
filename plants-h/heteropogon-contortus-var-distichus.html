Heteropogon contortus var. distichus

Heteropogon contortus var. distichus, commonly known as the twisted bluemink, is a grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, twisted inflorescence and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a compact growth habit and produces small, blue to purplish flowers.

This variety is often used in ornamental gardening due to its unique appearance and adaptability.
