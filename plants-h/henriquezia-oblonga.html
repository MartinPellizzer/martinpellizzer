Henriquezia oblonga

Henriquezia oblonga, commonly known as the Mexican honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 2 to 3 meters, featuring dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are elongated and glossy, with a slightly waxy texture, and it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.

Henriquezia oblonga is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
