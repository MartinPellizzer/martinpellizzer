Hieracium bupleuroides subsp. tririvicola

Hieracium bupleuroides subsp.

tririvicola, commonly known as the three-veined hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters. Its inflorescence consists of several disc flowers surrounded by ray florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

The subspecies is distinguished by its distinctive three-veined leaves and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
