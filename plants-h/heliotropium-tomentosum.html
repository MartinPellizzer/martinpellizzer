Heliotropium tomentosum

Heliotropium tomentosum, commonly known as the woolly heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and clusters of small, pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or herb, often with a sprawling or upright habit. Its flowers emit a sweet, vanilla-like scent, attracting various pollinators.

It is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils.
