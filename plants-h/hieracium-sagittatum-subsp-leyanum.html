Hieracium sagittatum subsp. leyanum

Hieracium sagittatum subsp.

leyanum, commonly known as the arrowhead hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in grassy meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters.

The plant features basal and alternate leaves that are lanceolate to ovate in shape, with hairy surfaces. Its flower heads are composite, consisting of yellow disk flowers surrounded by pale yellow to brown ray florets.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves, which give it its common name.
