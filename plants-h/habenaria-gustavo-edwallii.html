Habenaria gustavo-edwallii

Habenaria gustavo-edwallii, commonly known as the Gustavo Edwall's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate floral structure and fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
