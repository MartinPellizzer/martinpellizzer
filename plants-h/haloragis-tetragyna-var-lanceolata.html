Haloragis tetragyna var. lanceolata

Haloragis tetragyna var. lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf waterwheel plant, is a perennial herbaceous species native to Australia.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, often found in coastal regions. The plant features opposite, lanceolate leaves that are usually submerged or floating, adapted to aquatic conditions. Its flowers are small and clustered in whorls, with four petals, giving it the common name "waterwheel plant.".

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and fish.
