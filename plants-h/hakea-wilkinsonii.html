Hakea wilkinsonii

Hakea wilkinsonii, commonly known as the coastal hakea, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical stems and needle-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers during the spring and summer months, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils.

Hakea wilkinsonii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
