Hieracium chlorellum subsp. sagittipotens

Hieracium chlorellum subsp.

sagittipotens, commonly known as the arrowhead hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features basal rosettes of broad, lobed leaves and produces yellow flowers in late summer. Its inflorescence consists of a single, terminal capitulum with ray florets.

The species is characterized by its distinctive leaf shape and is often found in moist, temperate environments.
