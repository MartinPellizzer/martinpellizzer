Hedysarum bicolor

Hedysarum bicolor, commonly known as the twinflower vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Hedysarum bicolor is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in soil enrichment.
