Heliconia virginalis

Heliconia virginalis, commonly known as the red heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the ginger family, known for its striking, colorful bracts that resemble flowers. The plant produces long, segmented inflorescences with bright red bracts that enclose small, white, fragrant flowers. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Heliconia virginalis is also used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.
