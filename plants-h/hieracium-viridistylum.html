Hieracium viridistylum

Hieracium viridistylum, commonly known as the green-veined hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant produces yellow flowers in composite inflorescences, which are typical of the daisy family. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
