Hesperis transcaucasica

Hesperis transcaucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian honesty, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, white or pale pink flowers and linear, grass-like leaves. Native to the Caucasus region, this plant thrives in rocky and mountainous areas. It produces small, round seed pods that split open to release seeds, a feature typical of the Brassicaceae family.

Hesperis transcaucasica is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
