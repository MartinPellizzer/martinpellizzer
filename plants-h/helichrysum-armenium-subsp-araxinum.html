Helichrysum armenium subsp. araxinum

Helichrysum armenium subsp.

araxinum, commonly known as Armenian immortelle, is a perennial shrub native to the Caucasus region. It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its resilience and ornamental value make it a popular choice among gardeners in Mediterranean climates.
