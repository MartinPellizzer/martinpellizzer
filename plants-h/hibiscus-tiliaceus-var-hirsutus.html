Hibiscus tiliaceus var. hirsutus

Hibiscus tiliaceus var. hirsutus, commonly known as the hirsute hibiscus, is a species native to tropical regions of the Pacific and Indo-Pacific.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its rough, hairy bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink or red, often with a yellow center. Hibiscus tiliaceus var. hirsutus is known for its adaptability to coastal environments and its use in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
