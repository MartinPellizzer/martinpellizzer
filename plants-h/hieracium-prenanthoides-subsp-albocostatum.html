Hieracium prenanthoides subsp. albocostatum

Hieracium prenanthoides subsp.

albocostatum, commonly known as the white-costed hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems.

The subspecies is distinguished by its white-veined leaves and pale yellow petals. It typically grows in grassy areas and meadows, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant is often found in the UK and parts of southern Scandinavia.
