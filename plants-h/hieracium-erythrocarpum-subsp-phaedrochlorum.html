Hieracium erythrocarpum subsp. phaedrochlorum

Hieracium erythrocarpum subsp.

phaedrochlorum, commonly known as the red-fruited hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy stems.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive reddish-ochre coloration.

The subspecies is notable for its unique fruiting structures and is often found in calcareous habitats.
