Helichrysum bracteatum var. albidum

Helichrysum bracteatum var. albidum, commonly known as the white everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, white to pale yellow flowers that resemble tiny sunflowers. The plant produces papery, persistent bracts that give it a long-lasting appearance, making it popular in dried floral arrangements. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The species is noted for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
