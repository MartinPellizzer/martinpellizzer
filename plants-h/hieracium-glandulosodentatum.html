Hieracium glandulosodentatum

Hieracium glandulosodentatum, commonly known as the glandular-toothed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant features basal leaves that are glandular-dotted and have serrated or toothed margins, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring temperate climates.

Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, producing yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
