Hornemannia viscosa

Hornemannia viscosa, commonly known as the sticky hornemannia, is a unique plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that produce a sticky substance, which is a natural defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in greenish-yellow clusters, and it produces small, round berries that are edible for some animals.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
