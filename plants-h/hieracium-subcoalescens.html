Hieracium subcoalescens

Hieracium subcoalescens, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and the leaves are usually lance-shaped with a rough texture.

Hieracium subcoalescens is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in some regions.
