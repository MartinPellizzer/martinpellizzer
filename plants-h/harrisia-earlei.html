Harrisia earlei

Harrisia earlei, commonly known as the Mexican night-blooming cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom at night. The plant belongs to the Cactaceae family and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are typically short-lived, opening only during the evening and closing by dawn.

Harrisia earlei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique nocturnal blooming habit.
