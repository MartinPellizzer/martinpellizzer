Habenaria calvilabris

Habenaria calvilabris, commonly known as the hairy-leaf orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments, often on the forest floor or along stream banks. The plant features a slender, upright stem with a few leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine, hairy structures. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, often clustered in a loose inflorescence.

The species is notable for its unique floral structure and is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal.
