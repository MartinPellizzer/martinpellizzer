Hordeum leporinum var. simulans

Hordeum leporinum var. simulans, commonly known as the prairie barley, is a wild grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with two to three spikelets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in prairies, along roadsides, and in open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native grassland ecosystems.
