Handroanthus billbergii

Handroanthus billbergii, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, simple, and elliptic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal regions.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological significance.
