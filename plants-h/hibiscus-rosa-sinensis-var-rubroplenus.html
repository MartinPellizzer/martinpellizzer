Hibiscus rosa-sinensis var. rubroplenus

Hibiscus rosa-sinensis var. rubroplenus, commonly known as the red-flowered Chinese hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that are typically red or pink with a yellow center. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

This variety is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to bloom throughout the year under proper care.
