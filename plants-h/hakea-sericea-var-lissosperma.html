Hakea sericea var. lissosperma

Hakea sericea var. lissosperma, commonly known as the silky hakea, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia.

It features narrow, needle-like leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in dense, cylindrical spikes, which bloom in spring. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
