Hordeum vulgare var. trifurcatum

Hordeum vulgare var. trifurcatum, commonly known as the three-awned barley, is a variant of barley characterized by its distinctive awns.

It is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in disturbed soils and grasslands. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spikelets that bear three awns. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is sometimes cultivated for forage or as a cover crop due to its hardy nature and ecological benefits.
