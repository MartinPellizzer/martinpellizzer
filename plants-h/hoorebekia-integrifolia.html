Hoorebekia integrifolia

Hoorebekia integrifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf hibiscus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and are typically 2 to 4 inches in diameter. The plant has smooth, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Hoorebekia integrifolia is often grown for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
