Hieracium trichoglaucopsis

Hieracium trichoglaucopsis, commonly known as the glaucous hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 40 cm. Its leaves are hairy and have a bluish-green hue, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
