Hexonix umbellata

Hexonix umbellata, commonly known as the golden umbrella plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, umbrella-like inflorescence composed of numerous small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-drained, nutrient-rich soil. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often with a slightly waxy texture.

Hexonix umbellata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in indoor settings with proper care.
