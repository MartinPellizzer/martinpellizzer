Humboltia retusa

Humboltia retusa, commonly known as the Pacific coast morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the western coast of the United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. Humboltia retusa thrives in coastal environments, often growing along cliffs and in sandy soils.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
