Hordeum vulgare var. kobdicum

Hordeum vulgare var. kobdicum, commonly known as Kobdick's barley, is a variant of barley characterized by its unique botanical features.

It is a grass species with slender, cylindrical spikes that bear small, two-lobed spikelets. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are wind-pollinated, a common trait among grasses, and it produces three-branched, three-rowed grains.

This variety is notable for its ecological adaptability and historical use in traditional agriculture.
