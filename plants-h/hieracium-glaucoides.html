Hieracium glaucoides

Hieracium glaucoides, commonly known as the bluish hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, whitish hairs, giving them a bluish-gray appearance.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
