Hagaea diffusa

Hagaea diffusa, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a unique plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent leaves and striking, bioluminescent flowers that emit a faint glow at night. The plant has a low-growing, sprawling habit and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its botanical features make it an intriguing subject for study in both horticulture and ecology.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species.
