Huperzia billardierei

Huperzia billardierei, commonly known as the mountain huperzia, is a species of fern-like plant belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its simple, linear leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Huperzia billardierei is notable for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and its resilience to environmental stress.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptability make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
