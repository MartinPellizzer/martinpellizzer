Hieracium acrotrichum

Hieracium acrotrichum, commonly known as the hairy yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. It features a basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
