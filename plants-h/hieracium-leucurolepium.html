Hieracium leucurolepium

Hieracium leucurolepium, commonly known as the white-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the dandelion family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are usually covered in fine hairs, and the flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Hieracium leucurolepium is often found in sunny, open habitats and can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
