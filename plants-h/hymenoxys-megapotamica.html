Hymenoxys megapotamica

Hymenoxys megapotamica, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright orange to yellow ray flowers and dark green, heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and forest edges. It produces small, tubular disk flowers that are pollinated by bees and other insects.

Hymenoxys megapotamica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
