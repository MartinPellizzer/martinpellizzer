Huperzia salvinioides

Huperzia salvinioides, commonly known as the mountain huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats on rocky or forested slopes. The plant features simple, linear leaves that are often arranged in two ranks, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is native to regions in Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and unique morphology, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
