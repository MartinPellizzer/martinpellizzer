Hieracium streptochaetum subsp. jaretanum

Hieracium streptochaetum subsp.

jaretanum, commonly known as the Jaretanum hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine, stiff hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is noted for its adaptability and is often found in areas with moderate to dry conditions.
