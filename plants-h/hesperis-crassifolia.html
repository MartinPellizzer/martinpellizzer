Hesperis crassifolia

Hesperis crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its showy, bright yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in arid environments.

Hesperis crassifolia is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
