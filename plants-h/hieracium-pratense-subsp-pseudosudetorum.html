Hieracium pratense subsp. pseudosudetorum

Hieracium pratense subsp.

pseudosudetorum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in composite heads.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands across Europe. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in nutrient-rich soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
