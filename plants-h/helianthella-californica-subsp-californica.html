Helianthella californica subsp. californica

Helianthella californica subsp.

californica, commonly known as the California gold poppy, is a herbaceous annual plant native to California. It features showy, bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer, often in clusters at the top of slender stems.

The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are alternate and covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry grasslands and open woodlands.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
