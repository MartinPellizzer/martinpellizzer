Hookera patagonica

Hookera patagonica, commonly known as the Patagonian hooker, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains in South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. This species is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. Hookera patagonica typically grows in rocky, alpine environments at high elevations.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
