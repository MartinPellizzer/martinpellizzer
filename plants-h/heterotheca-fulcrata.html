Heterotheca fulcrata

Heterotheca fulcrata, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the central and southern United States.

It is characterized by its stiff, upright stems and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils.

Heterotheca fulcrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
