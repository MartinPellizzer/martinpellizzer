Hieracium piliferum proles ustulatum

Hieracium piliferum proles ustulatum, commonly known as the scorched hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and waste areas, and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance, and its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

The species is often found in sunny, open habitats and is adapted to survive in harsh environmental conditions.
