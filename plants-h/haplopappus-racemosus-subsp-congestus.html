Haplopappus racemosus subsp. congestus

Haplopappus racemosus subsp.

congestus, commonly known as the clustered globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect stem and clusters of yellow flowers.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance. The plant produces small, bristly seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
