Hypericum leprosum var. rigidulum

Hypericum leprosum var. rigidulum, commonly known as the rigid-stemmed St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems, which are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating, giving it a leathery appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a slightly serrated margin.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.
