Hieracium multifidum

Hieracium multifidum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions, including North America. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Hieracium multifidum is an annual or biennial plant, depending on the climate, and is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through seeds.
