Helichrysum stolzii

Helichrysum stolzii, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, yellow to orange-colored flower heads that resemble small suns. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are clustered into compact, spherical inflorescences. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy surface.

Helichrysum stolzii is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
