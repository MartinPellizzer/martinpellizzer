Helictochloa praeusta subsp. pseudoviolacea

Helictochloa praeusta subsp.

pseudoviolacea, commonly known as the violet false brome, is a grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, while the inflorescences bear violet to purple flowers.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations and limited distribution.
