Huperzia gunturensis

Huperzia gunturensis, commonly known as the Gunturensis clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is a small, evergreen fern-like plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant has a creeping rhizome with upright, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in small, scale-like structures. Huperzia gunturensis reproduces via spores rather than seeds, and its life cycle includes a distinct gametophyte stage.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its primitive characteristics and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
