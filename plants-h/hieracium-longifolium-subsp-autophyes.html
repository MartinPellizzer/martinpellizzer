Hieracium longifolium subsp. autophyes

Hieracium longifolium subsp.

autophyes, commonly known as the long-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and elongated, flowering stems that bear yellow disk flowers surrounded by ray florets.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It is native to Europe and has been introduced to other regions, where it can sometimes become invasive.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and botanical gardens.
