Hieracium spurium subsp. pseudocymiferum

Hieracium spurium subsp.

pseudocymiferum, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow to orange composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. It has a rosette of basal leaves and produces numerous flowering stems that bear showy inflorescences.

Its botanical features make it an important component of alpine ecosystems and a subject of study in plant ecology.
