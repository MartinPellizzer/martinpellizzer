Hoodia triebneri

Hoodia triebneri, commonly known as the desert cabbage, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom during the rainy season. The plant stores water in its thick leaves, allowing it to survive in extreme desert conditions. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine, and it has gained attention for its potential appetite-suppressing properties.

Hoodia triebneri is adapted to harsh environments, with a slow growth rate and a long lifespan.
