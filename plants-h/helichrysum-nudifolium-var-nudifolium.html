Helichrysum nudifolium var. nudifolium

Helichrysum nudifolium var. nudifolium, commonly known as the naked-leaved everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that are often arranged in dense, rounded inflorescences. The plant has smooth, hairless stems and leaves that are typically narrow and linear. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ability to retain color when dried makes it a popular choice for floral arrangements.
