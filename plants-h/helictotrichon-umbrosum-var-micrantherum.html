Helictotrichon umbrosum var. micrantherum

Helictotrichon umbrosum var. micrantherum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green foliage and slender, upright growth habit. The plant typically forms dense, compact clumps and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are usually greenish or bluish in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
