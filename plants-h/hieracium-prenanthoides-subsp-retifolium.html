Hieracium prenanthoides subsp. retifolium

Hieracium prenanthoides subsp.

retifolium, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. It features a low-growing rosette of basal leaves and produces yellow flowers in the summer.

Its subspecies-specific traits include slender stems and leaves with a distinctive, often hairy texture.
