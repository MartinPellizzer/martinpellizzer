Hordeum heterostychon

Hordeum heterostychon, commonly known as the slender barley, is a species of grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces both male and female flowers on separate spikelets.

Hordeum heterostychon is primarily of interest to botanists due to its ecological role and genetic diversity.
