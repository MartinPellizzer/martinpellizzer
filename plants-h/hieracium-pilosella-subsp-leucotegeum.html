Hieracium pilosella subsp. leucotegeum

Hieracium pilosella subsp.

leucotegeum, commonly known as the white-haired hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow-orange flower heads.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The subspecies is notable for its pale, often whitish bloom, which sets it apart from other hawkweed species.
