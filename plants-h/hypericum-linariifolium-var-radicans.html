Hypericum linariifolium var. radicans

Hypericum linariifolium var. radicans, commonly known as trailing St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters along the stems. The plant has opposite, linear leaves that are often slightly hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its trailing growth habit makes it suitable for ground cover in gardens and naturalized areas.
