Hemigenia sericea var. lanosa

Hemigenia sericea var. lanosa, commonly known as the silky hemigenia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understoreys.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
