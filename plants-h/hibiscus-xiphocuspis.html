Hibiscus xiphocuspis

Hibiscus xiphocuspis, commonly known as the scarlet hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically range in color from red to orange. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in warm, humid climates. Hibiscus xiphocuspis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

It produces seeds in fluffy, cotton-like pods that disperse easily in the wind.
