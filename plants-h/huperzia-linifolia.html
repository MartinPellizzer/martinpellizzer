Huperzia linifolia

Huperzia linifolia, commonly known as the slender huperzia, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and distinctive, segmented leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. Native to regions of Southeast Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments. The sporangia are typically clustered on the underside of the leaves, and the plant is known for its ability to form dense mats in rocky or forested areas.

Huperzia linifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
