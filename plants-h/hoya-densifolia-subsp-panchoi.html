Hoya densifolia subsp. panchoi

Hoya densifolia subsp.

panchoi, commonly known as the panchoi hoya, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often dark green with a prominent central vein.

The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It is a popular houseplant due to its ease of care and attractive foliage. Hoya densifolia subsp.

panchoi thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
