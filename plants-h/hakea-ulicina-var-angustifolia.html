Hakea ulicina var. angustifolia

Hakea ulicina var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hakea, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces cylindrical, woody flower spikes that bloom in spring, typically in shades of red or pink. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to provide habitat for native wildlife.
