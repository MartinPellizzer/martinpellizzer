Hyphaene turbinata var. ansata

Hyphaene turbinata var. ansata, commonly known as the African palm or the desert date, is a member of the Arecaceae family.

It is a tall, slender palm characterized by its pinnate leaves and clustered, yellowish flowers. The plant produces large, fleshy fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Its roots are adapted to arid environments, allowing it to thrive in dry, sandy soils.

This species is native to parts of Africa and is valued for both its ecological and economic importance.
