Helichrysum bracteatum var. atrosanguineum

Helichrysum bracteatum var. atrosanguineum, commonly known as the dark blood helichrysum, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It features compact, branched stems covered in dense, silvery-white to pale yellow foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow to orange in color, arranged in clusters. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The leaves and flowers are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.
