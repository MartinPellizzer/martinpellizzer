Himatanthus speciosus

Himatanthus speciosus, commonly known as the Cape jasmine, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive addition to gardens and natural habitats.
