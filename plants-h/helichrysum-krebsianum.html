Helichrysum krebsianum

Helichrysum krebsianum, commonly known as the Cape goldcup, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that resemble miniature sunflowers. The plant produces small, papery bracts that give it a distinctive appearance, often mistaken for actual petals. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting flowers, which retain their color even when dried.
