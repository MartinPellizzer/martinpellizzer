Himantoglossum caprinum subsp. caprinum

Himantoglossum caprinum subsp.

caprinum, commonly known as the alpine gentian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in full sun. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branched.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological importance in alpine ecosystems, supporting a variety of pollinators.
