Hieracium pyrenaicum subsp. nobile

Hieracium pyrenaicum subsp.

nobile, commonly known as noble hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and rocky slopes.

The plant features a basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in a cluster at the end of each stem. Its inflorescence consists of a single capitulum with several disc flowers surrounded by pale yellow ray florets.

The species is notable for its adaptability to high-altitude environments and its role in local ecosystems.
