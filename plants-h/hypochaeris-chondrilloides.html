Hypochaeris chondrilloides

Hypochaeris chondrilloides, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer, followed by seed heads that disperse easily.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
