Hovenia dulcis var. dulcis

Hovenia dulcis var. dulcis, commonly known as the Japanese raisin tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with three to five leaflets and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, round, red fruits that resemble raisins, which are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. The fruits are often used in traditional Chinese and Japanese medicine for their purported health benefits.

It thrives in well-drained soil and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes as well as for its edible fruits.
