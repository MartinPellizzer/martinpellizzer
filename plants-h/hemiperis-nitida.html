Hemiperis nitida

Hemiperis nitida, commonly known as whitebeard bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers. The plant has heart-shaped, toothed leaves that are typically hairy beneath. It thrives in moist, shady woodland habitats and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Hemiperis nitida is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
