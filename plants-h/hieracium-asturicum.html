Hieracium asturicum

Hieracium asturicum, commonly known as the Asturian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and forest edges. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom in clusters.

Hieracium asturicum is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
