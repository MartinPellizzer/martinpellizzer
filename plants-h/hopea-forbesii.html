Hopea forbesii

Hopea forbesii, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a large evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate to elliptical leaves with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in axillary or terminal inflorescences.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
