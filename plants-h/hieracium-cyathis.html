Hieracium cyathis

Hieracium cyathis, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers consist of both ray and disk florets.

Hieracium cyathis is often found in moist environments and can spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in agricultural areas.
