Hieracium umbelliferum subsp. bauhinifolium

Hieracium umbelliferum subsp.

bauhinifolium, commonly known as the bauhinia-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are deeply lobed, with a distinctive bauhinia-like appearance, giving the plant its subspecific name. It is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in meadows and grasslands.

This species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
