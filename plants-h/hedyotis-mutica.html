Hedyotis mutica

Hedyotis mutica, commonly known as the slender knotweed, is a small herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a three-lobed capsule that disperses seeds by wind.

Hedyotis mutica is often found in wetland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
