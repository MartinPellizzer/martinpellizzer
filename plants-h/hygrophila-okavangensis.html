Hygrophila okavangensis

Hygrophila okavangensis, commonly known as the Okavango hygrophila, is a perennial aquatic plant native to the Okavango Delta in Botswana.

It typically grows in shallow waters and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the wet season. It plays an important role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems by preventing erosion and providing habitat for aquatic life.

Hygrophila okavangensis is also valued for its ornamental qualities in water gardens.
