Hydrophyllum thompsonii

Hydrophyllum thompsonii, commonly known as Thompson's sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has small, linear leaves and produces seeds in a disk-like structure surrounded by ray florets.
