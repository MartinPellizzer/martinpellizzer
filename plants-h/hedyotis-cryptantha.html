Hedyotis cryptantha

Hedyotis cryptantha, commonly known as the cryptantha geranium, is a small herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky slopes. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy surface, and its small, white or pink flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences. Hedyotis cryptantha is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in association with other shade-tolerant flora.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partially shaded gardens.
