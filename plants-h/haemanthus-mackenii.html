Haemanthus mackenii

Haemanthus mackenii, commonly known as the Natal grassblume, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and clusters of bright red, tubular flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 centimeters and prefers well-drained, sandy soils. It is often found in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in full sun.

Haemanthus mackenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
