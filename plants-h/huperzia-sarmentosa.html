Huperzia sarmentosa

Huperzia sarmentosa, commonly known as the walking fern, is a species of clubmoss that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its unique, creeping growth habit and its ability to form dense mats on forest floors. The plant has long, segmented stems that can root at the nodes, allowing it to spread vegetatively. Its leaves are usually spirally arranged and have a lanceolate or ovate shape.

Huperzia sarmentosa is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
