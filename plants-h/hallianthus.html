Hallianthus

Hallianthus, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Nelumbonaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant is often found in shallow waters, such as ponds and lakes, where it thrives in sunny conditions. Its flowers, which can be white, pink, or red, bloom in summer and are highly valued in various cultures for their beauty and symbolic significance.

The plant's roots and seeds are also used in traditional medicine and cuisine.
