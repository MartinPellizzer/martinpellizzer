Harrisia taetra

Harrisia taetra, commonly known as the red harrisia, is a species of cactus native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that are often covered in spines. The plant produces vibrant red flowers that bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

Harrisia taetra is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
