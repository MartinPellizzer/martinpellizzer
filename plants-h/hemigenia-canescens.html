Hemigenia canescens

Hemigenia canescens, commonly known as the woolly honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its hairy, grey-green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and understoreys. Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and birds.

Hemigenia canescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
