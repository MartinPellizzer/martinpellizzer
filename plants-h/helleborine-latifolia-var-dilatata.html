Helleborine latifolia var. dilatata

Helleborine latifolia var. dilatata, commonly known as the broad-leaf helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and showy, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in woodland or shady areas, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. It produces small, fleshy pseudobulbs and has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators.
