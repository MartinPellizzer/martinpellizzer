Hieracium stenacrum

Hieracium stenacrum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Hieracium stenacrum is known for its aggressive spreading habit and can become invasive in certain regions.
