Heliconia burle-marxii

Heliconia burle-marxii, commonly known as the Marxii heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its large, brightly colored bracts that resemble flowers. The plant has long, narrow leaves that form a dense rosette, providing structural support for its inflorescence. Its vibrant orange and red bracts attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in warm climates due to its unique appearance and resilience.
