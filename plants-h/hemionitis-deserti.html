Hemionitis deserti

Hemionitis deserti, commonly known as the desert holly, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It has narrow, succulent leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, allowing it to store water and reduce transpiration. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is shallow but extensive, enabling it to absorb moisture from a wide area.

Hemionitis deserti is well adapted to harsh desert conditions, making it a resilient and characteristic species of its habitat.
