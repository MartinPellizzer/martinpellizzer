Hieracium neoqueraltense

Hieracium neoqueraltense, commonly known as the Queralt's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are basal, rough, and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and hairy.

Hieracium neoqueraltense is known for its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn.
