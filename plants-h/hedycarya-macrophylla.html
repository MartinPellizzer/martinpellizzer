Hedycarya macrophylla

Hedycarya macrophylla, commonly known as the broad-leafed hedycarya, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, ovate leaves that can grow up to 10 inches in length. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters during the spring. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and a woody stem with smooth, gray bark.

Hedycarya macrophylla is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland areas.
