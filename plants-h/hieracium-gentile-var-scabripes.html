Hieracium gentile var. scabripes

Hieracium gentile var. scabripes, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are rough and hairy, with a distinctive serrated margin, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
