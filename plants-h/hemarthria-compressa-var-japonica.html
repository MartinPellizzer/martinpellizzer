Hemarthria compressa var. japonica

Hemarthria compressa var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically silver-gray in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are often inconspicuous compared to its foliage. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
