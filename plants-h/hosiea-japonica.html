Hosiea japonica

Hosiea japonica, commonly known as the Japanese rockrose, is a perennial shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being oval and slightly hairy.

Hosiea japonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and low maintenance requirements.
