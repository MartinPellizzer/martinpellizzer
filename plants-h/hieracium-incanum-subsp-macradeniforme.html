Hieracium incanum subsp. macradeniforme

Hieracium incanum subsp.

macradeniforme, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

The plant is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green leaves and yellow flower heads composed of numerous small florets. It produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal, aiding in its spread.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family and is distinguished by its unique inflorescence structure and pubescent foliage.
