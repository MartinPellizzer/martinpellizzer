Hydrangea aspera subsp. sargentiana

Hydrangea aspera subsp.

sargentiana, commonly known as Sargent's rough hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States. It features broad, ovate leaves that are often hairy, giving it a rough texture.

The plant produces clusters of white to pinkish-blue flowers in late summer, which are borne on inflorescences called panicles. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate a range of environmental conditions.
