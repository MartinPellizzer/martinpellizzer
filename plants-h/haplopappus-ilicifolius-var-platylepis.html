Haplopappus ilicifolius var. platylepis

Haplopappus ilicifolius var. platylepis, commonly known as the flat-leafed hawksbeard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy structure. The plant features finely divided, silvery-gray leaves that are often described as resembling those of holly, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically yellow and arranged in a flat, disc-like pattern, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open grasslands and along disturbed soils.
