Hieracium cerinthoides subsp. rhomboidale

Hieracium cerinthoides subsp.

rhomboidale, commonly known as the rhomboid hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in elongated inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks. It has a creeping rootstock that allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense mats.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various conditions, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
