Heliotropium tiaridioides

Heliotropium tiaridioides, commonly known as the wax flower or tropical heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, violet or purple flowers that bloom throughout the year in warm climates. Its flowers are often used in ornamental gardening due to their attractive appearance and long-lasting blooms.

The plant is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to partial shade.
