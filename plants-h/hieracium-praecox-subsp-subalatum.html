Hieracium praecox subsp. subalatum

Hieracium praecox subsp.

subalatum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.

The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a serrated edge, while its stems are upright and often branched.

This subspecies is characterized by its adaptability to harsh alpine environments and its role in stabilizing soil in mountainous areas.
