Hieraciodes turczaninowii

Hieraciodes turczaninowii, commonly known as the Turczaninow's hieracium, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh, high-altitude conditions. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, producing yellow to orange ray florets.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique ecological niche.
