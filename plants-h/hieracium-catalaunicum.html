Hieracium catalaunicum

Hieracium catalaunicum, commonly known as the Catalaunian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in grasslands and disturbed soils, exhibiting a rosette of basal leaves with hairy surfaces. Its flowers are yellow and form a flat, disc-like structure surrounded by ray florets.

Hieracium catalaunicum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in open habitats.
