Hieracium bifidum subsp. bifidum

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

bifidum, commonly known as the bifid hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, elongated flower heads that are often covered in fine hairs.

The subspecies is distinguished by its bifid (two-lobed) leaves and its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
