Hedeoma denudata

Hedeoma denudata, commonly known as the nakedleaf hedeoma, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often lack hairy coverage, giving it its common name.

Hedeoma denudata produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
