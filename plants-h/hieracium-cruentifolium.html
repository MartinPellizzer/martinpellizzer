Hieracium cruentifolium

Hieracium cruentifolium, commonly known as the bloody leaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are typically lobed with a reddish tinge, giving it its common name. It thrives in grassy areas and is often found in disturbed soils.

The plant is known for its ornamental value but can also be considered invasive in certain regions.
