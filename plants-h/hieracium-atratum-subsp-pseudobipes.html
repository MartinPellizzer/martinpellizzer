Hieracium atratum subsp. pseudobipes

Hieracium atratum subsp.

pseudobipes, commonly known as the black hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

The plant features basal rosettes of dark green, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in late summer. Its flowering stems are usually unbranched and bear several flower heads.

The species is characterized by its dark, almost black, central disk surrounded by ray florets.
