Hieracium murorum subsp. subsparsidens

Hieracium murorum subsp.

subsparsidens, commonly known as the lesser wood hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, clustered flower heads.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and locally endangered species in some regions.
