Heterotheca villosa var. shevockii

Heterotheca villosa var. shevockii, commonly known as Shevock's hairy goldenweed, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow composite flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has finely divided, grass-like leaves and produces small, seed-like structures called achene.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapes.
