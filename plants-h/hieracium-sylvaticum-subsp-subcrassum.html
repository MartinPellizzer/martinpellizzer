Hieracium sylvaticum subsp. subcrassum

Hieracium sylvaticum subsp.

subcrassum, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and yellow flower heads.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. It has a rosette growth form with basal leaves that are often lobed or toothed.

The flowers are borne in a single terminal head, surrounded by leaf-like bracts.
