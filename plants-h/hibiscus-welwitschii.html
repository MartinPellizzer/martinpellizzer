Hibiscus welwitschii

Hibiscus welwitschii, commonly known as the giant marsh hibiscus, is a striking plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in wet, marshy environments and is often found near water bodies. Hibiscus welwitschii can grow quite tall, reaching up to 6 meters in height, making it a notable presence in its habitat.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and botanical gardens.
