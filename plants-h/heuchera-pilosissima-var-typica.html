Heuchera pilosissima var. typica

Heuchera pilosissima var. typica, commonly known as the velvet leaf heuchera, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that ranges in color from deep burgundy to dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white during the summer months. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its striking foliage and long blooming period.
