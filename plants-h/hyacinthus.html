Hyacinthus

Hyacinthus, commonly known as the hyacinth, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asparagaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors such as blue, purple, pink, and white. The plant is native to southern Europe and the Mediterranean region, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Hyacinths are perennial bulbs that produce foliage in early spring before blooming in late winter or early spring.

Their ornamental value makes them a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
