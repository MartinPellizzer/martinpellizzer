Hyptis caprariifolia

Hyptis caprariifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian hyptis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or violet flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Hyptis caprariifolia is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
