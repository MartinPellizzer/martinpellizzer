Heliconia luteoviridis

Heliconia luteoviridis, commonly known as the yellow-green heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its large, boat-shaped inflorescence that resembles a bird's beak. The plant features broad, waxy, strap-like leaves that are often dark green with a prominent midrib. Its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, with three prominent petals and three smaller, spadix-like structures.

Heliconia luteoviridis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in floral arrangements and as a decorative plant in tropical gardens.
