Hesperis pontica

Hesperis pontica, commonly known as the Crimean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Balkans and parts of southeastern Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in meadows and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pale blue or violet, with a distinctive bell-shaped appearance.

Hesperis pontica is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
