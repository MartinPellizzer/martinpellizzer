Hieracium paltinae subsp. polyphyllobasis

Hieracium paltinae subsp.

polyphyllobasis, commonly known as the broad-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its clustered, yellowish to orange flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. It has alternate, broadly ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy.

The subspecies is distinguished by its broad, multi-lobed leaves, which contribute to its unique botanical appearance.
