Hieracium subramosum subsp. sublaeticeps

Hieracium subramosum subsp.

sublaeticeps, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

It is a perennial species that spreads through rhizomes and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.
