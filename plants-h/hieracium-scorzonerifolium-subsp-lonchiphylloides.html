Hieracium scorzonerifolium subsp. lonchiphylloides

Hieracium scorzonerifolium subsp.

lonchiphylloides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are elongated and often have a distinctive, narrow shape with finely toothed margins.

The flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
