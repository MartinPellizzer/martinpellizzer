Helianthemum halimifolium var. formosum

Helianthemum halimifolium var. formosum, commonly known as the sweet everlasting or rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub with woody stems and small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically gray-green, covered in fine hairs, and have a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal environments.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
