Hieracium chalcidicum

Hieracium chalcidicum, commonly known as the Chalcidium hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads made up of many small florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head.

Hieracium chalcidicum is often found in open habitats and can sometimes be invasive in certain regions.
