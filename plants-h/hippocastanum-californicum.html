Hippocastanum californicum

Hippocastanum californicum, commonly known as the California horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces distinctive, spiny capsules that contain shiny, chestnut-like seeds. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, and it can grow up to 20 meters tall.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often planted in gardens and parks.
