Hordeum latum

Hordeum latum, commonly known as wild barley, is a species of grass native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual plant that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides and in fields. The plant has slender stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny grains. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a rough texture, and it is often found in temperate climates.

Hordeum latum plays a role in ecological succession and has been studied for its potential in sustainable agriculture.
