Hieracium varsugae

Hieracium varsugae, commonly known as the Svalbard hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky and barren soils, thriving in cold, harsh environments. It has small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, and its leaves are usually hairy and lance-shaped.

Due to its limited distribution and specialized habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
