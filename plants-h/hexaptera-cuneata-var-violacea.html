Hexaptera cuneata var. violacea

Hexaptera cuneata var. violacea, commonly known as the violet-leaf hexaptera, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that often display a distinctive violet or purple hue. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
