Hesperis × pachycarpa

Hesperis × pachycarpa, commonly known as the hybrid candytuft, is a perennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a cross between Hesperis matronalis (common candytuft) and Hesperis torulosa (foxy candytuft), resulting in a plant with distinctive, showy flowers. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer. It is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and has been cultivated for its ornamental value.

Hesperis × pachycarpa is typically grown in gardens for its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
