Hieracium maculatum subsp. platybasis

Hieracium maculatum subsp.

platybasis, commonly known as the broadleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe. It features a basal rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive white-veined pattern.

The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and woodland edges.

Its name derives from the Latin "hieracium," referring to its resemblance to the mythical Greek flower associated with the gods.
