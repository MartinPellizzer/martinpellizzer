Helichrysum appendiculatum

Helichrysum appendiculatum, commonly known as the Cape goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has slender, hairy stems and linear, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Helichrysum appendiculatum is also known for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
