Henckelia rexii

Henckelia rexii, commonly known as the royal henckelia, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a prominent midrib, enhancing its visual appeal. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
