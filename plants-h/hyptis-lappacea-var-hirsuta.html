Hyptis lappacea var. hirsuta

Hyptis lappacea var. hirsuta, commonly known as hairy hyptis, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and ornamental uses. The plant is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

The species is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.
