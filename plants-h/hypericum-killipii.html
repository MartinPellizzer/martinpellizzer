Hypericum killipii

Hypericum killipii, commonly known as the Killip's St. John's Wort, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the genus Hypericum, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has woody stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer, are characterized by five petals and numerous stamens.

Hypericum killipii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
