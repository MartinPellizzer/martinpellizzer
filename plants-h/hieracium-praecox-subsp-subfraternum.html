Hieracium praecox subsp. subfraternum

Hieracium praecox subsp.

subfraternum, commonly known as the early hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while its yellow flowers appear in clusters.

It is an herbaceous perennial that produces seeds through wind dispersal.
