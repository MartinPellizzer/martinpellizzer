Hyptis lanata

Hyptis lanata, commonly known as white hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Hyptis lanata is also valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine.
