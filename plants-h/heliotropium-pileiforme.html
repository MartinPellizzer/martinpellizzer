Heliotropium pileiforme

Heliotropium pileiforme, commonly known as the red sandalwood or red sandalwood plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in sandy or well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or dry habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural reserves.
