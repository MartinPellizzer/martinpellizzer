Hieracium grandidens subsp. torticeps

Hieracium grandidens subsp.

torticeps, commonly known as the curlyhead hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy foliage.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while the flowers are yellow with ray florets.

The subspecies is distinguished by its tightly curled flower heads, which give it its common name.
