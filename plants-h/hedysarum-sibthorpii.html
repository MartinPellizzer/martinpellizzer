Hedysarum sibthorpii

Hedysarum sibthorpii, commonly known as the Sibthorp's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Hedysarum sibthorpii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
