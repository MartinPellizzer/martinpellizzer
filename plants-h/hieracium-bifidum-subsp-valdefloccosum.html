Hieracium bifidum subsp. valdefloccosum

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

valdefloccosum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and sandy soils.

The plant features upright stems with narrowly lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are yellow and borne in clusters, with ray florets that are usually shorter than the central disk.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaf shape and is often found in nutrient-poor environments.
