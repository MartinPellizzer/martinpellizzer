Hieracium glabratum subsp. glabratum

Hieracium glabratum subsp.

glabratum, commonly known as the smooth hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It is a member of the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, meadows, and waste areas. It has smooth, hairless stems and leaves that are usually lance-shaped with a rough texture.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators.
