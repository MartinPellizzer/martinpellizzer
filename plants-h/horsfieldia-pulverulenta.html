Horsfieldia pulverulenta

Horsfieldia pulverulenta, commonly known as the powdered orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a pale yellow or greenish hue. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually slender and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in moisture retention.

Horsfieldia pulverulenta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or tropical gardens.
