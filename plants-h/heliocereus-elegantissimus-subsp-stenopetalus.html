Heliocereus elegantissimus subsp. stenopetalus

Heliocereus elegantissimus subsp.

stenopetalus, commonly known as the sun cactus or night-blooming cereus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico. It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that can grow up to several meters in height.

The plant produces large, fragrant flowers that open at night, typically in shades of white or pale yellow. Its flowers are known for their striking beauty and brief blooming period, which occurs during the warmer months.

This subspecies is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.
