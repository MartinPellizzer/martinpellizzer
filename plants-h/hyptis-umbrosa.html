Hyptis umbrosa

Hyptis umbrosa, commonly known as dark hyptis, is a perennial shrub native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive grayish-green color with a hairy texture.

Hyptis umbrosa is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
