Hepetis bromeliifolia

Hepetis bromeliifolia, commonly known as the bromeliad heather, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a central cup to collect water. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late spring or early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
