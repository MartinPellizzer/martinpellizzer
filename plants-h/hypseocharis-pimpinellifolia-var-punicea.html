Hypseocharis pimpinellifolia var. punicea

Hypseocharis pimpinellifolia var. punicea, commonly known as the red-flowered hypseocharis, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly in Peru and Bolivia. The plant is characterized by its vibrant red, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high elevations.

Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.
