Hieracium caesium subsp. silenii

Hieracium caesium subsp.

silenii, commonly known as the purple hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust growth habit.

The plant features dark green, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces showy purple to pink flower heads in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
