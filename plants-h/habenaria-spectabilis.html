Habenaria spectabilis

Habenaria spectabilis, commonly known as the showy orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer to early autumn, producing clusters of violet or white petals with a distinctive lip. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption.

This species is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and grasslands.
