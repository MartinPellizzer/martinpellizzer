Hieracium exochum

Hieracium exochum, commonly known as the lesser hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers arranged in a single head. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is found in temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia. It has a hairy stem and is often seen in meadows, along roadsides, and in other open habitats.

Due to its ornamental appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
