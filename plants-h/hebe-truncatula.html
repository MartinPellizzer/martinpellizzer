Hebe truncatula

Hebe truncatula, commonly known as the mountain hebe, is a species native to New Zealand.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, often found in the Southern Alps. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically violet or purple in color, appearing in clusters.

Hebe truncatula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual interest.
