Helleborus lividus

Helleborus lividus, commonly known as the pale hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dark green, waxy leaves and its unique, pale green to yellowish flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is known for its toxic properties, containing various alkaloids. Helleborus lividus thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its historical use in medicine and as a symbol in heraldry adds to its cultural significance.
