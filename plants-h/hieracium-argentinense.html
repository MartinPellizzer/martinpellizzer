Hieracium argentinense

Hieracium argentinense, commonly known as the Argentine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and disturbed soils, showing adaptability to various environmental conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its roots are fibrous and help in stabilizing the soil.

Hieracium argentinense is also known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a common weed in certain regions.
