Hyptis pubescens

Hyptis pubescens, commonly known as Brazilian hyptis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Hyptis pubescens is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional remedies for various ailments.
