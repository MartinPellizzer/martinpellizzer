Hieracium sparsum subsp. abietogenum

Hieracium sparsum subsp.

abietogenum, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. It is often found in rocky or grassy habitats at high elevations.

Its botanical features make it an important component of alpine plant communities.
