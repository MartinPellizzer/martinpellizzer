Hirtella plumbea

Hirtella plumbea, commonly known as the silverleaf hirtella, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving them a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several small, hard seeds.

Hirtella plumbea is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
