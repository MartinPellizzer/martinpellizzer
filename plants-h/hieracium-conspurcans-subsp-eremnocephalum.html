Hieracium conspurcans subsp. eremnocephalum

Hieracium conspurcans subsp.

eremnocephalum, commonly known as the desert hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in open, disturbed soils and is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and yellow flowers.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is adapted to survive in dry environments. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and ragworts.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often found in rocky or sandy habitats where it plays a role in stabilizing soil.
