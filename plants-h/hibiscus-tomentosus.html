Hibiscus tomentosus

Hibiscus tomentosus, commonly known as the woolly hibiscus, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It features large, showy flowers with five deeply notched petals, typically in shades of pink, red, or white. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Hibiscus tomentosus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
