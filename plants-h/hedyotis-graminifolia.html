Hedyotis graminifolia

Hedyotis graminifolia, commonly known as the grassy bladdernut, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia and the Americas.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Hedyotis graminifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
