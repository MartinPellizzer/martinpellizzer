Helichrysum candolleanum

Helichrysum candolleanum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that remain intact even after drying. The plant produces small, tubular florets surrounded by ray florets, which give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Helichrysum candolleanum is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
