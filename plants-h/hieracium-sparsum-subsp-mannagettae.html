Hieracium sparsum subsp. mannagettae

Hieracium sparsum subsp.

mannagettae, commonly known as the Mannagetta's yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
