Helichrysum arctotidifolium

Helichrysum arctotidifolium, commonly known as the alpine everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that are often covered in fine, hairy structures. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

Helichrysum arctotidifolium is also known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
