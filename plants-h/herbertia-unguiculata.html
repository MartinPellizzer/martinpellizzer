Herbertia unguiculata

Herbertia unguiculata, commonly known as the spider lily, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, downward-facing flowers that resemble spiders. The plant features strap-shaped, evergreen leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped blooms in shades of white, yellow, and orange. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Herbertia unguiculata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom reliably in cooler climates.
