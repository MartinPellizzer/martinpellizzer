Hedycarya rivularis

Hedycarya rivularis, commonly known as the river hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow, orange, or red. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy and have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in humid environments.

Hedycarya rivularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
