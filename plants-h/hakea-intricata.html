Hakea intricata

Hakea intricata, commonly known as the intricate hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces striking, bottlebrush-like flowers that range in color from red to yellow, typically blooming in spring. Hakea intricata is well adapted to arid environments, with deep roots that help it survive in dry conditions.

Its unique morphology and ecological significance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
