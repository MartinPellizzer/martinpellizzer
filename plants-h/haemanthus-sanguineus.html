Haemanthus sanguineus

Haemanthus sanguineus, commonly known as the blood lily, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bright red, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features strap-like, evergreen leaves that form a rosette at the base. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

The vivid color of its flowers makes it a notable ornamental plant in botanical gardens and private collections.
