Hieracium onosmoides subsp. treseburgense

Hieracium onosmoides subsp.

treseburgense, commonly known as the Treseburg hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. It has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and locally important species in its native habitat.
