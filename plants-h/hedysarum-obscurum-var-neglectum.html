Hedysarum obscurum var. neglectum

Hedysarum obscurum var. neglectum, commonly known as the western prickly poppy, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and sagebrush habitats.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid environments and it plays a role in local ecosystems.
