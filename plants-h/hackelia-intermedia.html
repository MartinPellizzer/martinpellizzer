Hackelia intermedia

Hackelia intermedia, commonly known as the pinkroot or redroot, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 feet. The plant produces clusters of showy, pink to red tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Hackelia intermedia is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.
