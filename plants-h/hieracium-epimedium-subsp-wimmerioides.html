Hieracium epimedium subsp. wimmerioides

Hieracium epimedium subsp.

wimmerioides, commonly known as the Wimmer's hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It typically grows in moist, grassy habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

The plant features basal rosettes of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in early summer. Its inflorescence is a flat-topped cluster, with small, daisy-like flowers that attract pollinators.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive leaf morphology.
