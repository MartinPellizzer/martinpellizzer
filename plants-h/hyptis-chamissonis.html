Hyptis chamissonis

Hyptis chamissonis, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and showy blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a compact growth habit with branching stems and is often used in ornamental gardening for its visual appeal.

Hyptis chamissonis is also valued for its medicinal properties, with traditional uses in treating respiratory and digestive ailments.
