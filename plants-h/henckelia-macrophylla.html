Henckelia macrophylla

Henckelia macrophylla, commonly known as the giant henckelia, is a striking member of the Malvaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in diameter, giving it a dramatic appearance. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
