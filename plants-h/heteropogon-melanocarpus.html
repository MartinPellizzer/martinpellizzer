Heteropogon melanocarpus

Heteropogon melanocarpus, commonly known as the blackfruit grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black seed heads, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a hairy texture.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its striking seed heads.
