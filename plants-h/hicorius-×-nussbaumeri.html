Hicorius × nussbaumeri

Hicorius × nussbaumeri, commonly known as the hybrid hibiscus, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between Hibiscus acetosella and Hibiscus nussbaumerianus.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red, with a distinctive tubular shape. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central America and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. It has palmate leaves that are deeply lobed, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Hicorius × nussbaumeri is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical landscapes.
