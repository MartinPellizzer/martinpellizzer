Huperzia acutifolia

Huperzia acutifolia, commonly known as the sharp-leaved huperzia, is a species of moss-like plant belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, branching structure and small, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often sharply pointed. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky outcrops. Its sporophyte produces spores in capsules that are clustered at the tips of the stems.

Huperzia acutifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems.
