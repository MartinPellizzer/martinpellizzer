Hieracium lycopifolium subsp. trichoprenanthes

Hieracium lycopifolium subsp.

trichoprenanthes, commonly known as the hairy-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosettes of leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clustered heads, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
