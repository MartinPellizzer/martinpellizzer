Hibiscus coatesii

Hibiscus coatesii, commonly known as the Coates' hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetlands and along stream banks. The plant features showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, depending on the variety. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

Hibiscus coatesii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in swampy or marshy habitats.
