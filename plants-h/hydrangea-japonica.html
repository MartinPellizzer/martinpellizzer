Hydrangea japonica

Hydrangea japonica, commonly known as the Japanese hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy flower clusters that can range in color from blue to pink depending on soil pH. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features serrated, ovate leaves that are dark green in color. Its flowers are composed of sterile female flowers surrounded by male sterile bracts, which give the appearance of petals.

Hydrangea japonica thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
