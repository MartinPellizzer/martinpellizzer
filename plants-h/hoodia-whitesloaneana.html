Hoodia whitesloaneana

Hoodia whitesloaneana, commonly known as the desert cactus or hoodia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its branching stems and small, spineless leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow or green flowers, which are typically short-lived and open during the day. Hoodia whitesloaneana is adapted to extreme drought conditions, storing water in its fleshy stems.

Its unique morphology and resilience make it an important species in its native ecosystem.
