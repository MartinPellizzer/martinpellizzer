Hieracium piliferum proles glanduliferum

Hieracium piliferum proles glanduliferum, commonly known as the glandular-haired hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides, reaching heights of up to 50 cm. Its leaves are hairy and have glandular structures, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators.
