Hieracium subcrispum

Hieracium subcrispum, commonly known as the curlyhead hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems, with leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Hieracium subcrispum is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

It is a member of the genus Hieracium, which includes many species of hawkweeds known for their aggressive growth and ability to colonize various habitats.
