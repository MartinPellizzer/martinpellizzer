Hieracium serratum subsp. schwarzii

Hieracium serratum subsp.

schwarzii, commonly known as the Schwarz's Hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, serrated leaves and yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. It is notable for its ability to thrive in shaded environments while still maintaining its characteristic foliage structure.

Its flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
