Hydrocotyle schkuhriana

Hydrocotyle schkuhriana, commonly known as the marsh mallow or water pennywort, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in South America.

It is characterized by its creeping growth habit and produces small, white or yellowish flowers. The plant has rounded, succulent leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy layer. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Hydrocotyle schkuhriana is also known for its ability to tolerate standing water, making it an important species in aquatic ecosystems.
