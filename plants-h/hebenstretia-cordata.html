Hebenstretia cordata

Hebenstretia cordata, commonly known as the African snowbell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. These flowers are often followed by spherical, greenish fruits that turn red when ripe.

Hebenstretia cordata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
