Hoya cumingiana subsp. catanauanensis

Hoya cumingiana subsp.

catanauanensis, commonly known as the Catanauan Waxplant, is a species of flowering plant native to the Philippines. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape.

The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale yellow in color. It is a succulent plant, capable of storing water in its leaves and stems, which helps it survive in dry conditions.

This subspecies is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
