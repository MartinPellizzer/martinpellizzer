Hydrocotyle apiifolia

Hydrocotyle apiifolia, commonly known as the American marsh penny.

This herbaceous perennial is native to wetland areas of the eastern United States. It features small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Its root system helps stabilize soil in aquatic environments, making it an important species for wetland ecosystems.
