Hesperis remyana

Hesperis remyana, commonly known as the narrowleaf lungwort, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is characterized by its basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of pink or purple flowers in early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants.

Hesperis remyana is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
