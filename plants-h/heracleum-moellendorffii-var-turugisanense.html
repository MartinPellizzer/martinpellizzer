Heracleum moellendorffii var. turugisanense

Heracleum moellendorffii var. turugisanense, commonly known as the Turugisanese cow parsnip, is a variant of the cow parsnip species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a biennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. The plant features a tall, hollow stem with a distinct ridged texture and large, lobed leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. Its flowers are small and clustered in umbels, producing a characteristic yellowish-green color.

The plant is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
