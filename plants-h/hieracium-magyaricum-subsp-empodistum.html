Hieracium magyaricum subsp. empodistum

Hieracium magyaricum subsp.

empodistum, commonly known as the Hungarian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to orange composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a rough texture, while its stems are usually hairy.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
