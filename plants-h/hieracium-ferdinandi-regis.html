Hieracium ferdinandi-regis

Hieracium ferdinandi-regis, commonly known as the Ferdinand's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central dark center.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
