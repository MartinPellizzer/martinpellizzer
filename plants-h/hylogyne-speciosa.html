Hylogyne speciosa

Hylogyne speciosa, commonly known as the showy hylogyne, is a striking aquatic plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often a vibrant green, with a waxy surface that helps repel water. The plant produces clusters of delicate, white or pale pink flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Its roots are adapted to thrive in shallow water, anchoring it in muddy or sandy substrates.

Hylogyne speciosa is valued in ornamental water gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ability to support aquatic life.
