Hieracium atriceps

Hieracium atriceps, commonly known as the black-centered hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, with bracts that are often darker in color. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and the stem is usually erect and hairy.

Hieracium atriceps is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
