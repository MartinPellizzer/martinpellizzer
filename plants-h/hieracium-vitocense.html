Hieracium vitocense

Hieracium vitocense, commonly known as the Italian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering stems that bear yellow daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils across Europe and parts of Asia. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, and the flowers have a central disk surrounded by ray florets.

Hieracium vitocense is often considered a weed but has some traditional medicinal uses in herbal remedies.
