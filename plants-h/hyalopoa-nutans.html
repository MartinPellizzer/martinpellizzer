Hyalopoa nutans

Hyalopoa nutans, commonly known as the hanging hyacinth, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pendulous inflorescences that bear small, fragrant flowers in shades of blue and purple. The plant has strap-like leaves that are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties. Its growth habit makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening, particularly in hanging baskets or as a cascade plant.

The botanical features of Hyalopoa nutans make it both visually appealing and ecologically valuable in its native habitats.
