Hieracium ramosissimum

Hieracium ramosissimum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are hairy and lance-shaped, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disk.

Hieracium ramosissimum is often found in temperate climates and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
