Hieracium fluminense

Hieracium fluminense, commonly known as the river dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads made up of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as riverbanks and wet meadows, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disk.

Hieracium fluminense is often found in temperate regions and can thrive in both full sun and partial shade.
