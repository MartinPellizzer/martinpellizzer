Helichrysum parviflorum var. aureum

Helichrysum parviflorum var. aureum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, bright yellow to golden-orange flowers that are tightly packed in dense clusters. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. It is a hardy perennial that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

The flowers are known for their long-lasting color and are often used in dried floral arrangements.
