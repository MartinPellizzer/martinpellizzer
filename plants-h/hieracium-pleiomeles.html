Hieracium pleiomeles

Hieracium pleiomeles, commonly known as the many-flowered hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy stems and bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils across Europe and parts of Asia. It has basal and alternate leaves that are often lobed or toothed.

Hieracium pleiomeles is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
