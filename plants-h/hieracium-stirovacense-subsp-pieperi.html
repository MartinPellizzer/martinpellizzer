Hieracium stirovacense subsp. pieperi

Hieracium stirovacense subsp.

pieperi, commonly known as the Stirovacan hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has hairy, linear leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
