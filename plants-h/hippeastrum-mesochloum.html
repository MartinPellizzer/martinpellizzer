Hippeastrum mesochloum

Hippeastrum mesochloum, commonly known as the Mesochloum amaryllis, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in late summer or early autumn. The plant features broad, strap-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous underground stem. Its flowers are typically pink or red, with six petals and a prominent central spadix.

Hippeastrum mesochloum is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
