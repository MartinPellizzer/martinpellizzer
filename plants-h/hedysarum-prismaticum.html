Hedysarum prismaticum

Hedysarum prismaticum, commonly known as the prismatic peavine, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, often at high elevations. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are usually hairy and lance-shaped.

Hedysarum prismaticum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
