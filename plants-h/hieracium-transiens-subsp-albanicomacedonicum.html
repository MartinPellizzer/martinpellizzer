Hieracium transiens subsp. albanicomacedonicum

Hieracium transiens subsp.

albanicomacedonicum, commonly known as the Albanian-Macedonian hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant is native to the Balkan Peninsula, specifically found in Albania and North Macedonia. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species in its native range.
