Herbertia lahue subsp. amoena

Herbertia lahue subsp.

amoena, commonly known as the Amoena Herbertia, is a striking perennial plant native to the southeastern United States. It features tall, upright stems that bear large, showy flowers in shades of pink and white.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant blooms.
