Hydnophytum orbiculatum

Hydnophytum orbiculatum, commonly known as the sticky vine or bladderwort, is a unique epiphytic plant found in tropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, pitcher-like structures that secrete digestive enzymes to trap and digest small aquatic organisms. The plant forms symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing cyanobacteria, which provide essential nutrients. Its roots are often covered in a sticky substance that helps in capturing prey.

This plant is notable for its adaptation to nutrient-poor environments through its specialized feeding mechanisms.
