Hieracium prenanthoides subsp. juvonis

Hieracium prenanthoides subsp.

juvonis, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow disc flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, thriving in temperate climates. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that attract various pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more slender stems and reduced leaf structure compared to other members of the species.
