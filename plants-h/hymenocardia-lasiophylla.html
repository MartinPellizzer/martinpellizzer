Hymenocardia lasiophylla

Hymenocardia lasiophylla, commonly known as the glossy shield-leaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often arranged in a shield-like pattern. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or rainforest habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely commercially available.
