Hordeum vulgare subsp. deficiens

Hordeum vulgare subsp.

deficiens, commonly known as the slender barley, is a subspecies of barley characterized by its narrow leaves and compact growth habit. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils.

This plant is typically shorter than common barley, with a reduced number of spikelets and smaller grains. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for studies in plant taxonomy and adaptation.
