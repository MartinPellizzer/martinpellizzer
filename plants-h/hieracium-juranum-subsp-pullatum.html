Hieracium juranum subsp. pullatum

Hieracium juranum subsp.

pullatum, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing adaptations to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are usually lanceolate and covered with dense white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is also noted for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, contributing to its persistence in mountainous habitats.
