Hybanthus buxifolius

Hybanthus buxifolius, commonly known as the boxleaf hybanthus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its small, showy flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant has elliptical, glossy leaves that resemble those of boxwood, giving it its common name. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Hybanthus buxifolius is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value due to its delicate appearance.
