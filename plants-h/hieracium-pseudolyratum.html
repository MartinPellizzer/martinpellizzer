Hieracium pseudolyratum

Hieracium pseudolyratum, commonly known as the false lyrate hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellowish-orange composite flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. This species is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grasslands. It has a creeping growth habit with flowering stems that rise from the base.

The plant is noted for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
