Helianthemum guttatum var. punctatum

Helianthemum guttatum var. punctatum, commonly known as the dotted stonecrop, is a low-growing succulent plant native to southern Europe.

It features fleshy, rounded leaves that are typically green with red or purple markings. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
