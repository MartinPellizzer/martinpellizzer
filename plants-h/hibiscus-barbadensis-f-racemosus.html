Hibiscus barbadensis f. racemosus

Hibiscus barbadensis f. racemosus, commonly known as the red hibiscus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy, five-petaled flowers that typically range in color from white to deep red. The plant is native to tropical regions of the Americas and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and often branched.

Hibiscus barbadensis f. racemosus is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and is also used in traditional medicine.
