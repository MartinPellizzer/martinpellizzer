Hieracium incisum subsp. gracilicaule

Hieracium incisum subsp.

gracilicaule, commonly known as the slender stem hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, meadows, and woodland edges.

The plant features a slender, upright stem that is usually less than 30 cm tall, with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head, with ray florets that are usually shorter than the disk florets.

It is characterized by its finely divided leaves and is often found in moist to dry soils.
