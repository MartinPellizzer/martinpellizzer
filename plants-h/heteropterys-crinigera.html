Heteropterys crinigera

Heteropterys crinigera, commonly known as the silky leaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with multiple stems that can reach heights of up to several meters.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
