Hydrocotyle phoenix

Hydrocotyle phoenix, commonly known as the desert spurge, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage. The plant has small, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces yellow flowers in clusters. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments with minimal water.

Its botanical features make it an important component of desert ecosystems.
