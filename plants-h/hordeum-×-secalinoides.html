Hordeum × secalinoides

Hordeum × secalinoides, commonly known as intermediate barley, is a naturally occurring hybrid between barley (Hordeum vulgare) and hulless barley (Hordeum spontaneum).

It exhibits a combination of traits from both parent species, including intermediate characteristics in morphology and growth habits. The plant typically has a robust stem and produces spikelets with both hulled and hulless grains. It is often found in temperate regions and is used in agricultural research for its genetic diversity.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a bridge species in the evolution of barley varieties.
