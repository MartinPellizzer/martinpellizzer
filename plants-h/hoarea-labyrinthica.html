Hoarea labyrinthica

Hoarea labyrinthica, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its low, sprawling growth habit and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant has a complex root system that helps it survive in harsh, dry conditions. Its leaves are typically small, hairy, and adapted to reduce water loss.

Hoarea labyrinthica is often found in sandy soils and is a member of the Amaranthaceae family.
