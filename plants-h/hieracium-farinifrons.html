Hieracium farinifrons

Hieracium farinifrons, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are hairy and lance-shaped, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is often found in open, sunny habitats and is relatively common in its native range.
