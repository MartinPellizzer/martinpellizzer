Henriettea tachirensis

Henriettea tachirensis, commonly known as the Tachira lily, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Venezuela and Colombia.

It belongs to the Alstroemeriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant features strap-like leaves and produces clusters of bright red or orange flowers, making it a visually appealing ornamental species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
