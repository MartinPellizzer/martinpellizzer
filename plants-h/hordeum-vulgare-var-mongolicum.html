Hordeum vulgare var. mongolicum

Hordeum vulgare var. mongolicum, commonly known as Mongolian barley, is a variant of barley native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender stalks and compact, spike-like inflorescence. The plant produces small, two-lobed lemma and palea, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in semi-arid regions.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and adaptability to poor soil conditions.
