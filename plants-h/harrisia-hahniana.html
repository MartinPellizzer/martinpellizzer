Harrisia hahniana

Harrisia hahniana, commonly known as the night-blooming harrisia, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and large, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant belongs to the Cactaceae family and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink and emit a sweet scent to attract nocturnal pollinators.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming habit.
