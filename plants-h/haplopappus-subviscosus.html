Haplopappus subviscosus

Haplopappus subviscosus, commonly known as the sticky pappus plant, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and bract-like structures. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its leaves are often covered in a fine, sticky coating, which helps reduce water loss.

The small, yellow flowers bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
