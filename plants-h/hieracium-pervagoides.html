Hieracium pervagoides

Hieracium pervagoides, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium pervagoides is known for its ability to spread rapidly through rhizomes and is considered a weed in some regions.
