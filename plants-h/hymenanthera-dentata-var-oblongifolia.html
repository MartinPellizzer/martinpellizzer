Hymenanthera dentata var. oblongifolia

Hymenanthera dentata var. oblongifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, serrated leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and the fruit is a small, fleshy berry that matures to a red or orange color.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
