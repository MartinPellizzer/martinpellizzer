Hieracium murorum subsp. diaphaniflorum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

diaphaniflorum, commonly known as the translucent flower hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers with ray florets that are notably translucent, giving it its common name. It thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats, often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
