Hieracium picroides var. huteri

Hieracium picroides var. huteri, commonly known as the Huter's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering heads that resemble dandelions. The plant typically grows in dry grasslands and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are yellow, with ray florets that are shorter than the central disc florets.

It is a member of the genus Hieracium, which includes many species of hawkweeds known for their aggressive growth and ecological impact.
