Hypericum weberbaueri

Hypericum weberbaueri, commonly known as the Weberbauer's St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite and oval-shaped, often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and branched.

Hypericum weberbaueri is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
