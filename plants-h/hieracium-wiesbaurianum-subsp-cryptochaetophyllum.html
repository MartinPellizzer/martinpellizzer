Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp. cryptochaetophyllum

Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp.

cryptochaetophyllum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves that are hairy and often covered in glandular dots.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

This subspecies is distinguished by its unique leaf and flower morphology, making it a notable species in its native habitat.
