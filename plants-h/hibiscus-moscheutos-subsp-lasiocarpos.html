Hibiscus moscheutos subsp. lasiocarpos

Hibiscus moscheutos subsp.

lasiocarpos, commonly known as the swamp hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a yellow center.

The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in moist, swampy environments and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important species for wetland ecosystems and a popular choice for water gardeners.
