Hieracium trichotum

Hieracium trichotum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosette of leaves, which help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grasslands.

Hieracium trichotum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
