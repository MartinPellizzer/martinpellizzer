Hypericum alatum

Hypericum alatum, commonly known as the spreading St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, often sprawling growth habit and features bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. Its stems are woody and can become woody with age, and the plant is known for its medicinal properties.

Hypericum alatum thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.
