Hieracium incurrens

Hieracium incurrens, commonly known as the rough hawksbeard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are rough and hairy, with a serrated edge, and the flower heads consist of yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk.

It blooms from late spring to early autumn, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
