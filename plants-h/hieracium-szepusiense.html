Hieracium szepusiense

Hieracium szepusiense, commonly known as the Szepes hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Europe, particularly found in Hungary and surrounding regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight. It has serrated leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some areas.
