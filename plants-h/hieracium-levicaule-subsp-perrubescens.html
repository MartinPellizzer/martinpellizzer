Hieracium levicaule subsp. perrubescens

Hieracium levicaule subsp.

perrubescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems that often turn reddish in color as the plant matures.

The leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and typically have a rough texture. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats, often found in meadows and along roadsides.
