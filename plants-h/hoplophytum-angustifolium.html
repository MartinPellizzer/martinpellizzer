Hoplophytum angustifolium

Hoplophytum angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hoplophytum, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and fleshy, gray-green leaves that are narrow and linear. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Hoplophytum angustifolium is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
