Hippia odora

Hippia odora, commonly known as the sweet shrub, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves and small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and thrives in well-drained soil. Its botanical features include opposite leaves and a woody stem, making it a hardy shrub suitable for ornamental gardening.

The essential oils extracted from its leaves are used in perfumery and aromatherapy.
