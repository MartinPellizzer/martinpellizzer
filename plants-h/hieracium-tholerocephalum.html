Hieracium tholerocephalum

Hieracium tholerocephalum, commonly known as the tholerocephalous hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and a single, erect stem that bears a dense cluster of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is found in temperate regions of Europe. It has small, alternate leaves that are usually hairy and have a rough texture.

The flowers are composite, consisting of both ray and disc florets, which attract various pollinators.
