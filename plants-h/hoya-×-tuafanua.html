Hoya × tuafanua

Hoya × tuafanua, commonly known as the "Coral Curls" or "Coral Hoya," is a hybrid epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, coral-pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling curls or tendrils. The plant features thick, waxy leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with a smooth, glossy surface. Hoya × tuafanua thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

It is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
