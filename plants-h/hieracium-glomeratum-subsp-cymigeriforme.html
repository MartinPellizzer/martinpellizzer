Hieracium glomeratum subsp. cymigeriforme

Hieracium glomeratum subsp.

cymigeriforme, commonly known as the clustered hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its dense clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has basal and stem leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
