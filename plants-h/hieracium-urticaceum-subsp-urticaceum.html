Hieracium urticaceum subsp. urticaceum

Hieracium urticaceum subsp.

urticaceum, commonly known as the stinging hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, reddish stems and clusters of yellow flowers.

The plant has serrated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, stinging hairs. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Due to its invasive nature in some regions, it is often considered a weed.
