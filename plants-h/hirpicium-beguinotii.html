Hirpicium beguinotii

Hirpicium beguinotii, commonly known as the Beguin's cushion plant, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit, with small, leathery leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often in shades of pink or purple. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to withstand harsh alpine conditions.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
