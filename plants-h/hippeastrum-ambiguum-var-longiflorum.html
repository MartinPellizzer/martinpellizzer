Hippeastrum ambiguum var. longiflorum

Hippeastrum ambiguum var. longiflorum, commonly known as the long-flowered amaryllis, is a bulbous plant native to South America.

It features striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that can be white, pink, or red, often with a strong fragrance. The plant produces large, fleshy leaves that are typically broad and strap-like. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a period of dormancy to bloom successfully.

This variety is popular among gardeners for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
