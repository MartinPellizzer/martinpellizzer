Hibiscus eetveldeanus var. asperatus

Hibiscus eetveldeanus var. asperatus, commonly known as the rough-hairy hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Its flowers bloom during the summer months and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
