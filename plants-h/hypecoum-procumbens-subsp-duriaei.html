Hypecoum procumbens subsp. duriaei

Hypecoum procumbens subsp.

duriaei, commonly known as the spreading hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It features basal rosettes of waxy, dark green leaves that are typically hairy and have a strong, unpleasant odor.

The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in umbels. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
