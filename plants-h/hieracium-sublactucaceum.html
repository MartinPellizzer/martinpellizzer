Hieracium sublactucaceum

Hieracium sublactucaceum, commonly known as the lesser hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Hieracium sublactucaceum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
