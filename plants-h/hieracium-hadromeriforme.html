Hieracium hadromeriforme

Hieracium hadromeriforme, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Compositae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in summer. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant has a taproot system and can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.

It is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other sunny, well-drained areas.
