Hieracium obrovacense

Hieracium obrovacense, commonly known as the Balkan hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and forest edges. It has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disc.

Hieracium obrovacense is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine regions.
