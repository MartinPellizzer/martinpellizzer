Heteropterys nitida f. acuminata

Heteropterys nitida f. acuminata, commonly known as the acute-leaved heteropterys, is a variant of the species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree, characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically pointed at the tips. The leaves are usually a dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside, with a smooth texture. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by small, round berries.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
