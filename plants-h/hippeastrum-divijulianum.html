Hippeastrum divijulianum

Hippeastrum divijulianum, commonly known as the Colombian glory lily, is a bulbous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces large, strap-like leaves that emerge from a bulb and store nutrients for flowering. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a period of dormancy to produce blooms.

Hippeastrum divijulianum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
