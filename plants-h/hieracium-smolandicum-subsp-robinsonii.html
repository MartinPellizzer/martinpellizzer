Hieracium smolandicum subsp. robinsonii

Hieracium smolandicum subsp.

robinsonii, commonly known as the Robinson's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and basal rosettes of leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and its flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

This subspecies is noted for its limited distribution and is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological and evolutionary significance.
