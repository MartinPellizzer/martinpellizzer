Hemionitis glutinosa

Hemionitis glutinosa, commonly known as the sticky bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its floating, bladder-like structures that trap small organisms, aiding in its nutrient acquisition. The plant has thin, delicate leaves that are often covered in a sticky substance, which helps in adhesion to surfaces. It typically grows in freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes.

Hemionitis glutinosa is known for its unique morphology and ecological role in aquatic ecosystems.
