Hedysarum hedysaroides f. neglectum

Hedysarum hedysaroides f. neglectum, commonly known as the neglected sweetvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, branching stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces yellow to orange pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. It is typically found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to poor soil conditions.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ecological role in nitrogen fixation.
