Hieracium olivaceum

Hieracium olivaceum, commonly known as the olive hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a distinctive central purple area.

Hieracium olivaceum is often found in disturbed habitats and can spread aggressively through its rhizomes.
