Hieracium bicolor subsp. terianum

Hieracium bicolor subsp.

terianum, commonly known as the white wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive white or pale green coloration, often marked with red veins.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and meadows.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its milky sap and hairy stems.
