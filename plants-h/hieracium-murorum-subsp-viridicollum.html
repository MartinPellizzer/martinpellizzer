Hieracium murorum subsp. viridicollum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

viridicollum, commonly known as the green-necked hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its yellowish to reddish-orange composite flowers and hairy, upright stems.

The subspecies is native to parts of Europe and is often found in disturbed soils and grassy areas. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, elongated achenes that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread across different habitats.
