Helianthemum rhiphaeum var. elatum

Helianthemum rhiphaeum var. elatum, commonly known as the tall rock rose, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has woody stems and dense, evergreen foliage that provides structure throughout the year. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and visually appealing addition to rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
