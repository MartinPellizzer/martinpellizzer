Hyparrhenia dybowskii

Hyparrhenia dybowskii, commonly known as the African feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and long, narrow, grass-like leaves that are often feather-like in appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Hyparrhenia dybowskii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
