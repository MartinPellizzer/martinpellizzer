Hibbertia arnhemica

Hibbertia arnhemica, commonly known as the Arnhem daisybush, is a flowering plant native to the Northern Territory of Australia.

It is characterized by its erect, shrub-like growth habit and features bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. Hibbertia arnhemica thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for native pollinators.
