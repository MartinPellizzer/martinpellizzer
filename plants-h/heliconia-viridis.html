Heliconia viridis

Heliconia viridis, commonly known as the green heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, elongated, green bracts that enclose the inflorescence, which consists of numerous small, yellowish flowers. The plant is part of the Heliconiaceae family and is often found in humid, shaded environments such as rainforests. Its striking appearance makes it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and botanical collections.

The leaves are typically broad and lance-shaped, contributing to its lush, tropical aesthetic.
