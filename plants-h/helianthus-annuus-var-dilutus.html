Helianthus annuus var. dilutus

Helianthus annuus var. dilutus, commonly known as the diluted sunflower, is a variant of the common sunflower characterized by its unique foliage and floral features.

It exhibits a more subdued coloration compared to typical sunflowers, with leaves that have a lighter green hue and a textured surface. The flowers are generally smaller and less showy, with a more muted yellow to orange color palette. This variety is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a range of environmental conditions.

Its botanical interest lies in its genetic diversity and potential for ornamental use in gardens.
