Helianthemum × pseudocinereum

Helianthemum × pseudocinereum, commonly known as the silver saxifrage, is a hybrid plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and small, yellowish flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats. Its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs that give it a distinctive appearance and help reduce water loss.

Helianthemum × pseudocinereum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
