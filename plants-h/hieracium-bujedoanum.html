Hieracium bujedoanum

Hieracium bujedoanum, commonly known as the Bujedo hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has basal rosettes of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers with ray florets.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species within its native range.
