Hieracium romieuxianum subsp. plauense

Hieracium romieuxianum subsp.

plauense, commonly known as the Plauense hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in grassy habitats and is often found in disturbed soils. It has small, alternate leaves that are usually covered with fine hairs.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its presence in specific alpine and subalpine regions.
