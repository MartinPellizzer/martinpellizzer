Hieracium revocans

Hieracium revocans, commonly known as the reactivated hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow daisy-like flowers with ray florets. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils in temperate regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

Hieracium revocans is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in agricultural and natural areas.
