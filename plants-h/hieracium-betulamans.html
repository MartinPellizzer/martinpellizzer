Hieracium betulamans

Hieracium betulamans, commonly known as the birch-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is typically found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows. It has a taproot system and is adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Hieracium betulamans is an annual or biennial species, depending on the climate and environmental conditions.
