Hieracium karelorum

Hieracium karelorum, commonly known as the Karelian hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellowish to orange flowers that bloom in summer. Native to the forests and meadows of northern Europe, it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant has finely divided leaves and produces small, inconspicuous fruits called cypselae.

It is often found in association with other herbaceous plants in temperate woodlands.
