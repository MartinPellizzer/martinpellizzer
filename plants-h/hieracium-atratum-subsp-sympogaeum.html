Hieracium atratum subsp. sympogaeum

Hieracium atratum subsp.

sympogaeum, commonly known as the dark hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dark green, hairy leaves and yellow to orange composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring moist to moderately dry conditions. It has a taproot system and produces achenes as its fruit, which are dispersed by wind.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and horticultural practices.
