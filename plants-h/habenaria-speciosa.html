Habenaria speciosa

Habenaria speciosa, commonly known as the showy orchid, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial with long, narrow leaves and produces striking, often fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its flowers are zygomorphic, featuring three sepals and three petals, with the lip being the most prominent and colorful part.

Habenaria speciosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
