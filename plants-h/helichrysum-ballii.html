Helichrysum ballii

Helichrysum ballii, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and produces clusters of bright yellow, papery flowers that remain intact even when dried. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its aromatic foliage and drought tolerance. Its flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their long-lasting nature and vibrant color.

Helichrysum ballii thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
