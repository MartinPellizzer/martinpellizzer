Hieracium villosum var. bellidifolium

Hieracium villosum var. bellidifolium, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosette of leaves, which are typically covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is occasionally grown in gardens.
