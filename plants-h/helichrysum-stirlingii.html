Helichrysum stirlingii

Helichrysum stirlingii, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that retain their color even when dried. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its aromatic leaves and long-lasting blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental use.

Helichrysum stirlingii is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects and its resilience in arid environments.
