Hieracium dovrense subsp. protractum

Hieracium dovrense subsp.

protractum, commonly known as the elongated hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has hairy stems and leaves that are lance-shaped with prominent veins.

Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster, typical of the genus Hieracium.
