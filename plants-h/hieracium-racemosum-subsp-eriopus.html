Hieracium racemosum subsp. eriopus

Hieracium racemosum subsp.

eriopus, commonly known as the clustered hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically has hairy stems and leaves that are lance-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Asteraceae and is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
