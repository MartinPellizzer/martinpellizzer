Hieracium almaatense

Hieracium almaatense, commonly known as the Almaty hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia, particularly Kazakhstan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium almaatense is often found in temperate climates and is adapted to withstand dry conditions.
