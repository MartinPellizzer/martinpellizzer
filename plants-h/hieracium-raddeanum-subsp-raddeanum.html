Hieracium raddeanum subsp. raddeanum

Hieracium raddeanum subsp.

raddeanum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant has basal leaves that are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found in meadows, roadsides, and wastelands.

Due to its invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural and natural areas.
