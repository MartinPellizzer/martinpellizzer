Helianthus formosus

Helianthus formosus, commonly known as the Arizona sunflower, is a native flowering plant of the United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features bright yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk of smaller florets. It has rough, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in arid environments.

Helianthus formosus is adapted to full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy species in desert regions.
