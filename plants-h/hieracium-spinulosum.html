Hieracium spinulosum

Hieracium spinulosum, commonly known as the spiny hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat, disk-like structure.

Hieracium spinulosum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in many temperate regions.
