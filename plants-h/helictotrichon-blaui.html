Helictotrichon blaui

Helictotrichon blaui, commonly known as Bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands. Its small, spike-like inflorescence produces numerous tiny flowers that are wind-pollinated.
