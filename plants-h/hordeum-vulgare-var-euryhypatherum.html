Hordeum vulgare var. euryhypatherum

Hordeum vulgare var. euryhypatherum, commonly known as the slender barley, is a variant of barley characterized by its tall, slender stalks and reduced grain size.

It is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale yellow to brown florets. It is adapted to a wide range of environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in agricultural and wild settings.

Its botanical features make it distinct from other barley varieties, particularly in its morphology and ecological preferences.
