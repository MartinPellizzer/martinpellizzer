Heliconia samperiana

Heliconia samperiana, commonly known as the Samperian heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its large, brightly colored inflorescences that resemble a bird's nest. The plant features elongated, strap-like leaves that form a dense rosette, providing a habitat for various small animals and insects. Its flowers are typically orange or red, with a unique structure that facilitates pollination by birds and bats.

Heliconia samperiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in tropical gardens and as a component of rainforest ecosystems.
