Hordeum vulgare var. tortile

Hordeum vulgare var. tortile, commonly known as curled barley, is a variant of barley characterized by its unique growth habit.

This plant exhibits a prostrate or creeping growth form, with stems that coil or curl as they grow, allowing it to spread along the ground. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration. The inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often tightly clustered, producing small, two-rowed grains.

It is typically found in disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in temperate climates.
