Hepetis xanthocalyx

Hepetis xanthocalyx, commonly known as the yellow-flowered ironweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in dense, terminal panicles.

Hepetis xanthocalyx is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
