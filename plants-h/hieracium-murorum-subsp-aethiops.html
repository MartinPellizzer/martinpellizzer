Hieracium murorum subsp. aethiops

Hieracium murorum subsp.

aethiops, commonly known as the Ethiopian hawkweed, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and basal rosettes of leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the genus Hieracium. It is native to regions of southern Europe and northern Africa, where it thrives in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
