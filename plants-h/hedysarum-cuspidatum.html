Hedysarum cuspidatum

Hedysarum cuspidatum, commonly known as needlepod milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, elongated pods that resemble needles. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or disturbed areas.

Hedysarum cuspidatum plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.
