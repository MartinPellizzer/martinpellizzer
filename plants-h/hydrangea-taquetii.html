Hydrangea taquetii

Hydrangea taquetii, commonly known as the Sierra Madre hydrangea, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that range in color from white to pink and blue depending on soil pH. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of large, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy layer.

Hydrangea taquetii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
