Habenaria psammophila

Habenaria psammophila, commonly known as the sand orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, pale green to yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its flowers are often clustered at the base of the stem and have a distinctive, intricate structure typical of the Orchidaceae family.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and protected species in many areas.
