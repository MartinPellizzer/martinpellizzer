Hieracium elaeinum

Hieracium elaeinum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early autumn. The plant has rough, hairy leaves that are usually alternate and can be lance-shaped or oval in form. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and wastelands.

Hieracium elaeinum is a perennial herb that can spread through rhizomes and is considered invasive in some regions.
