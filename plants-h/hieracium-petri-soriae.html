Hieracium petri-soriae

Hieracium petri-soriae, commonly known as the Petri-soria hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium petri-soriae is often found in temperate regions and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
