Hieracium epimedium subsp. samoensicum

Hieracium epimedium subsp.

samoensicum, commonly known as the Samoan wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the island of Samoa. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a cluster, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
