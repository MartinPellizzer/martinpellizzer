Hypericum montanum f. verticillatum

Hypericum montanum f. verticillatum, commonly known as the mountain St. John's wort, is a compact, flowering shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features upright, branching stems covered in small, glandular hairs. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly waxy texture. Its flowers are bright yellow, with five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Hypericaceae family.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained, sunny conditions.
