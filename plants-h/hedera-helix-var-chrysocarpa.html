Hedera helix var. chrysocarpa

Hedera helix var. chrysocarpa, commonly known as the golden fruit ivy, is a variant of the common ivy characterized by its striking yellow berries.

This evergreen climbing plant features dark green, lobed leaves that are glossy and textured. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by the distinctive yellow fruits that persist through the winter. The plant is native to Europe and thrives in shaded to partially sunny conditions.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in gardens and as a houseplant.
