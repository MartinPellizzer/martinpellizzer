Helianthemum lasiocarpum var. leiocarpum

Helianthemum lasiocarpum var. leiocarpum, commonly known as the smooth-fruited rock rose, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fleshy leaves that are typically covered in a fine, woolly layer of hair. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruits are small, dry capsules that split open to release seeds.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.
