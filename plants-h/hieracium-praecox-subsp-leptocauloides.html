Hieracium praecox subsp. leptocauloides

Hieracium praecox subsp.

leptocauloides, commonly known as the slender-stemmed hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

It is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize open areas, making it both a pioneer species and a potential weed.
