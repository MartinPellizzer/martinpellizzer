Helosciadium oppositifolium

Helosciadium oppositifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved helosciadium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its fibrous roots and robust growth make it adaptable to well-drained soils.

Helosciadium oppositifolium is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, showcasing its resilience to dry conditions.
