Hibiscus gallaensis

Hibiscus gallaensis, commonly known as the Ethiopian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. Hibiscus gallaensis is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental beauty.

It also has traditional uses in local medicine and cultural practices.
