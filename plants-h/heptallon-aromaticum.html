Heptallon aromaticum

Heptallon aromaticum, commonly known as the aromatic heptallon, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a fragrant scent when crushed, which contributes to its common name.

Heptallon aromaticum is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
