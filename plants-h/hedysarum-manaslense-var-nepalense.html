Hedysarum manaslense var. nepalense

Hedysarum manaslense var. nepalense, commonly known as the Nepal hairy vetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Nepal and northern India.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces slender, woody pods containing small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated for forage or soil stabilization in marginal areas.
