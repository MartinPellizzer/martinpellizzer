Huperzia badiniana

Huperzia badiniana, commonly known as the Badin's spikemoss, is a species of clubmoss belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its evergreen, needle-like leaves and is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant produces sporangia on specialized structures called strobila, which are clustered at the tips of the branches. Huperzia badiniana is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded garden settings.

Its reproductive cycle is closely tied to the humid conditions of its natural habitat.
