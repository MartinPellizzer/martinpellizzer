Hamelia brittoniana

Hamelia brittoniana, commonly known as the Britton's firebush, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring showy, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in wetlands.

Hamelia brittoniana is valued for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in native plant landscaping.
