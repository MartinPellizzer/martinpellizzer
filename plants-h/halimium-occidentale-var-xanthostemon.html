Halimium occidentale var. xanthostemon

Halimium occidentale var. xanthostemon, commonly known as the yellow sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing annual herb with hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

The species is noted for its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil in disturbed areas.
