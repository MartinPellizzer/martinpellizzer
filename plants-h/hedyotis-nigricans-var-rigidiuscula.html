Hedyotis nigricans var. rigidiuscula

Hedyotis nigricans var. rigidiuscula, commonly known as black-eyed Susan, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features small, bright yellow flowers with dark purple centers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
