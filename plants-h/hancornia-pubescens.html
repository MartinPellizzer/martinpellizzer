Hancornia pubescens

Hancornia pubescens, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Erythroxylum pubescens*, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy red flowers and elliptical leaves with a hairy texture. The plant belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Hancornia pubescens is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
