Helianthemum ovatum subsp. littorale

Helianthemum ovatum subsp.

littorale, commonly known as the coastal sandverbena, is a low-growing, evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Europe. It features small, fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in its arid, coastal habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found on rocky shores or sandy coastal dunes.

This subspecies is adapted to salt spray and strong winds, making it a hardy plant of maritime environments.
