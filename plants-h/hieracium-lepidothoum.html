Hieracium lepidothoum

Hieracium lepidothoum, commonly known as the sand hawksbeard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in open habitats such as heaths and coastal areas. The plant features a robust stem with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a single head, with ray florets that are often shorter than the central disk flowers.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
