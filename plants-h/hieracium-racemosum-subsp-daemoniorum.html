Hieracium racemosum subsp. daemoniorum

Hieracium racemosum subsp.

daemoniorum, commonly known as the Devil's dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and waste areas, and is often found in moist or wet environments. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a raceme.

It is known for its distinctive, often short-lived blooms and is considered a weed in some regions.
