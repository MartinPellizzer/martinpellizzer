Hyptis campanulata

Hyptis campanulata, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, with a strong, mint-like scent.

Hyptis campanulata is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and aromatic properties.
