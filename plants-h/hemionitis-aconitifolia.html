Hemionitis aconitifolia

Hemionitis aconitifolia, commonly known as the silver thread fern, is a delicate herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded, rocky habitats. Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, with sporangia arranged in clusters on the undersides of the leaves.

Hemionitis aconitifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
