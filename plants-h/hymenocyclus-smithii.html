Hymenocyclus smithii

Hymenocyclus smithii, commonly known as the Smith's cycad, is a rare and ancient plant species belonging to the Cycadaceae family.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of stiff, feather-like leaves. The plant produces cycad-like cones, with male and female cones typically occurring on separate plants. Native to South Africa, it thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
