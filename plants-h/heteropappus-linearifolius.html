Heteropappus linearifolius

Heteropappus linearifolius, commonly known as the linear-leaved heteropappus, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and wind-dispersed seeds. The plant typically grows as a low, spreading shrub with linear, often hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. Its yellow flowers appear in clusters and bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant's adaptability to dry conditions makes it an important component of its native ecosystems.
