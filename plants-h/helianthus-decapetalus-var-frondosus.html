Helianthus decapetalus var. frondosus

Helianthus decapetalus var. frondosus, commonly known as the frondose sunflower, is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers with dark centers and its broad, palmately lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to wet soil conditions. Its botanical features include a robust stem with rough, hairy surfaces and a flowering period that occurs from late summer to early autumn.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
