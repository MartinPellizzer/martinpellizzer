Hymenaea multiflora

Hymenaea multiflora, commonly known as the Brazilian桃花心木, is a large, deciduous tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and broad, compound leaves that are often shed during the dry season. The tree produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that are followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, oval seeds. It is valued for its durable, heavy wood, which is used in furniture and musical instruments.

The plant also has medicinal uses in traditional South American medicine.
