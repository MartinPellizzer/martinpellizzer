Hesperis athrocarpa

Hesperis athrocarpa, commonly known as the narrowleaf scabious, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically blue or purple, arranged in clusters at the top of the stem.

Hesperis athrocarpa is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open grasslands and meadows.
