Hieracium sinuolatum

Hieracium sinuolatum, commonly known as the sinuous hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow composite flowers that consist of both ray and disk florets. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides.

It plays a role in supporting pollinators and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
