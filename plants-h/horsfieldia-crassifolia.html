Horsfieldia crassifolia

Horsfieldia crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leaved orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils, and it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

Horsfieldia crassifolia is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
