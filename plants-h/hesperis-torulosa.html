Hesperis torulosa

Hesperis torulosa, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with cruciform flowers. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Hesperis torulosa is often found in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in sunny, well-drained environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine plant collections.
