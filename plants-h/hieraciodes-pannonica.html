Hieraciodes pannonica

Hieraciodes pannonica, commonly known as the Pannonian hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-green, hairy leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Hieraciodes pannonica is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive foliage and flowers.
