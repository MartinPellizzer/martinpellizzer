Heterosperma pinnatum var. ovatifolium

Heterosperma pinnatum var. ovatifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf water clover, is a variant of the water clover species native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its ovate, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, providing a dense, carpet-like growth. The plant has small, inconspicuous flowers that typically bloom in early spring, followed by the production of distinctive, three-lobed fruits. It thrives in shallow, freshwater habitats and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important indicator species for assessing water quality and ecosystem health.
