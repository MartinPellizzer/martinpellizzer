Hieracium bifidum subsp. caesiotropum

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

caesiotropum, commonly known as the blue hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas and disturbed soils, exhibiting a rosette of basal leaves with hairy surfaces. Its flowers are usually blue or purple, with ray florets that are shorter than the central disk florets.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
