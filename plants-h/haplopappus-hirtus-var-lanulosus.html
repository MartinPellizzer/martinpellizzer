Haplopappus hirtus var. lanulosus

Haplopappus hirtus var. lanulosus, commonly known as the woolly dwarf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a low-growing perennial with hairy, gray-green foliage and small yellow flowers. The plant typically thrives in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. Its inflorescence consists of a single terminal head of yellow disk flowers surrounded by pale yellow to white ray flowers.

The species is noted for its distinctive woolly texture and adaptability to arid environments.
