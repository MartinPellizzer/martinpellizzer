Hieracium bangii subsp. niederleinii

Hieracium bangii subsp.

niederleinii, commonly known as the Niederlein's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has finely divided, serrated leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its adaptation to specific environmental conditions.
