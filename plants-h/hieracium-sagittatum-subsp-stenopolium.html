Hieracium sagittatum subsp. stenopolium

Hieracium sagittatum subsp.

stenopolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow composite flowers with a distinctive ray floret structure. It is characterized by its slender stems and is often found in temperate climates.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Asteraceae and is known for its adaptability to various soil conditions.
