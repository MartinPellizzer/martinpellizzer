Hieracium furcatum subsp. clariceps

Hieracium furcatum subsp.

clariceps, commonly known as the white-haired hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its distinctive white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a serrated edge, while the stems are upright and often branched.

It is a relatively small plant, typically reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.
