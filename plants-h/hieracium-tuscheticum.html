Hieracium tuscheticum

Hieracium tuscheticum, commonly known as the Tschetzi's hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats at high elevations, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium tuscheticum is often found in mountain meadows and is adapted to the harsh environmental conditions of its native range.
