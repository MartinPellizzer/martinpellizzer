Helichrysum artvinense

Helichrysum artvinense, commonly known as the Artvin everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Turkey.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of yellow to orange-colored flowers. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically visited by various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to the harsh environmental conditions of its habitat.

Helichrysum artvinense is also noted for its ability to retain its color when dried, making it a popular choice in floral arrangements.
