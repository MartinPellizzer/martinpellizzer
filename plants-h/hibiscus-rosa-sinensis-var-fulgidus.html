Hibiscus rosa-sinensis var. fulgidus

Hibiscus rosa-sinensis var. fulgidus, commonly known as the golden hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red, depending on the variety. The plant produces five-petaled blooms that are typically 5 to 10 centimeters in diameter, with a prominent central pistil and stamens. Its leaves are broad, ovate, and have a smooth texture, often with a waxy appearance.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a potted plant.
