Helichrysum coralloides

Helichrysum coralloides, commonly known as the coral daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded clusters of small yellow flowers that resemble coral, hence its common name. The plant has hairy, gray-green leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Helichrysum coralloides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
