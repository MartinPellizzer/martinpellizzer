Hieracium carolinianum subsp. domingense

Hieracium carolinianum subsp.

domingense, commonly known as the Carolina hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its clustered, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and wetlands. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and its flowers are arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

The subspecies domingense is distinguished by its slightly different morphology compared to other subspecies of Hieracium carolinianum.
