Hypericum obtusatum

Hypericum obtusatum, commonly known as blunt-leaved St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its stems are typically reddish-brown and branched, with a somewhat woody texture.

Hypericum obtusatum is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
