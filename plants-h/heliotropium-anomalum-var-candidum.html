Heliotropium anomalum var. candidum

Heliotropium anomalum var. candidum, commonly known as the white heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant typically grows as a low, mat-forming shrub with hairy, glandular leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
