Hemigraphis ciliata

Hemigraphis ciliata, commonly known as the shining iron plant, is a low-growing perennial herb native to tropical regions of Asia.

It features small, opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving them a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, tubular flowers that are typically red or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive foliage.

Hemigraphis ciliata is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
