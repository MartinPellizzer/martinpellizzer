Hopea singkawang

Hopea singkawang, commonly known as the Singkawang hopea, is a species of flowering plant in the family Dipterocarpaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody fruits containing one or two seeds.

The species is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in lowland dipterocarp forests.
