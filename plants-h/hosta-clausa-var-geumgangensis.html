Hosta clausa var. geumgangensis

Hosta clausa var. geumgangensis, commonly known as the Korean white hosta, is a perennial plant native to Korea.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically white to pale green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers on short stems in the summer months. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental foliage and is often used in shady garden landscapes.
