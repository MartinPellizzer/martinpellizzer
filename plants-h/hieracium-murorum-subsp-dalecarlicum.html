Hieracium murorum subsp. dalecarlicum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

dalecarlicum, commonly known as the Dalecarlian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement.

The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are broadly lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and clustered in a single head.

It is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in agricultural and natural habitats.
