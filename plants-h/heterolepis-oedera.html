Heterolepis oedera

Heterolepis oedera, commonly known as the narrowleaf goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and bright yellow floral heads. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are linear and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Heterolepis oedera is typically found in wet meadows and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
