Haemodorum longifolium

Haemodorum longifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hellbender, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Haemodoraceae, which includes plants commonly referred to as the "red roots" due to their reddish rhizomes. The plant is characterized by its narrow, strap-like leaves and produces striking red or orange flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or heathland environments.

Haemodorum longifolium is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
