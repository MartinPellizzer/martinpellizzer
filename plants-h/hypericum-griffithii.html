Hypericum griffithii

Hypericum griffithii, commonly known as the golden-stemmed St. John's wort, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the genus Hypericum, which includes numerous species known for their yellow flowers and medicinal properties. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
