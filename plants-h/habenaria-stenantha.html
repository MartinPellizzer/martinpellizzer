Habenaria stenantha

Habenaria stenantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in wet, swampy habitats and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant has linear leaves and produces a single spike of flowers with elongated, spurred petals. It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in moist, nutrient-poor environments.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species in its native range.
