Hieracium siphloglossum

Hieracium siphloglossum, commonly known as the purple hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are usually a striking purple or pink color.

Hieracium siphloglossum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
