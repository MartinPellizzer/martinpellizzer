Hieracium saxifragum subsp. lithophilum

Hieracium saxifragum subsp.

lithophilum, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to rocky and alpine environments. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lanceolate leaves.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found on limestone or rocky substrates.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized species in its native range.
