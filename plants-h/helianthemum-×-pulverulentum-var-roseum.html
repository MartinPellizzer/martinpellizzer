Helianthemum × pulverulentum var. roseum

Helianthemum × pulverulentum var. roseum, commonly known as the pink rockrose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features compact, evergreen foliage with a grayish-blue hue and produces showy, pinkish-red flowers in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes. Its flowers are typically borne in clusters and have a subtle, sweet fragrance.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
