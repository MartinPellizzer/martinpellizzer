Hieracium nigrescens subsp. sellandii

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

sellandii, commonly known as the Selland's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

The plant features basal rosettes of dark green, hairy leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in late summer. Its flowering stems are usually unbranched and bear a single flower head.

It is characterized by its finely divided leaves and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.
