Hydatica petiolaris

Hydatica petiolaris, commonly known as the water petiolar plant, is a species native to certain regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its delicate, often hairy stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces tiny, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots are known to absorb moisture efficiently, allowing it to thrive in moist or semi-aquatic environments.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value and ecological interest.
