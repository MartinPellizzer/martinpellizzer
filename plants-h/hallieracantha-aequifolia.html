Hallieracantha aequifolia

Hallieracantha aequifolia, commonly known as the slender-flowered sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to conserve moisture. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the cooler months. Its root system is extensive and helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Hallieracantha aequifolia is well-suited for xeriscaping and is often used in rock gardens due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
