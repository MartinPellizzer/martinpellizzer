Hypecoum armatum

Hypecoum armatum, commonly known as the blue poppy, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmate leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Hypecoum armatum is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its showy, long-lasting blooms.
