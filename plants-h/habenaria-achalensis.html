Habenaria achalensis

Habenaria achalensis, commonly known as the Achalensis orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces strikingly colored flowers, typically in shades of pink or purple, which bloom during the monsoon season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species requiring conservation efforts.
