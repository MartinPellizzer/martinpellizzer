Hyphear odoratum

Hyphear odoratum, commonly known as the sweet shrub, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, small white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments and has aromatic leaves that release a pleasant scent when crushed. Its botanical features include opposite leaves and a woody stem, making it a hardy shrub.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its pleasant fragrance and visual appeal.
