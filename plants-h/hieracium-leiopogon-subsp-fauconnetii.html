Hieracium leiopogon subsp. fauconnetii

Hieracium leiopogon subsp.

fauconnetii, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow flower heads.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and grasslands. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that attract various pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and often endangered species in many regions.
