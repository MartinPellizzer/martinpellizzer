Hieracium magyaricum subsp. doroghense

Hieracium magyaricum subsp.

doroghense, commonly known as Hungarian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to orange flowers arranged in a composite head.

The plant typically grows in dry meadows, grasslands, and rocky soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lanceolate, hairy, and have a serrated margin, while its stems are erect and often branched.
