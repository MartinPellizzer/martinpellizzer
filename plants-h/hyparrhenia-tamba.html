Hyparrhenia tamba

Hyparrhenia tamba, commonly known as the African grass or tamba grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit, with slender, wiry leaves that are often gray-green in color. The plant produces small, pale green spikelets that are arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open savannas and grasslands.

Hyparrhenia tamba is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional landscaping and erosion control.
