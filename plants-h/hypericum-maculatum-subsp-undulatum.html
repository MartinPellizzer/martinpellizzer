Hypericum maculatum subsp. undulatum

Hypericum maculatum subsp.

undulatum, commonly known as the spotted yellow crowfoot, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with red markings, which bloom in early summer.

The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in garden settings.
