Hyparrhenia umbrosa

Hyparrhenia umbrosa, commonly known as the dark grass or umbrosa grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and dark green foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, often forming a thick ground cover. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle with spikelets that are often clustered at the base of the stem.

Hyparrhenia umbrosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
