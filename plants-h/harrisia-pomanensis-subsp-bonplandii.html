Harrisia pomanensis subsp.

bonplandii, commonly known as the Bonplandii cactus, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly in Brazil and Venezuela. It is a spineless cactus that typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

The plant produces large, showy white or pink flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its fruit is a fleshy, red berry that contains numerous seeds, which are dispersed by animals.
