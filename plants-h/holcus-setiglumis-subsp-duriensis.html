Holcus setiglumis subsp. duriensis

Holcus setiglumis subsp.

duriensis, commonly known as the Duriensian cocklebur, is a variant of the cocklebur genus native to specific regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky bracts and small, spherical fruits that mature into hard, woody capsules.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, showing adaptability to various environmental conditions. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture, and the plant produces a unique, aromatic scent.

Botanically, it is notable for its morphological variations and ecological significance in its native range.
