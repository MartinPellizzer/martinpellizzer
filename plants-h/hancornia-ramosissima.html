Hancornia ramosissima

Hancornia ramosissima, commonly known as the desert willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, purple, or white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system to access water.

Hancornia ramosissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh, dry conditions.
