Hieracium nigrescens subsp. bructerum

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

bructerum, commonly known as the black-centred hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its bright yellow ray florets and a dark, central disk that becomes more pronounced as the flower matures.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has a rosette of basal leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs.

The subspecies bructerum is distinguished by its darker central flower and slightly different leaf morphology compared to other members of the species.
