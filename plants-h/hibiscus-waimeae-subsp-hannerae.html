Hibiscus waimeae subsp. hannerae

Hibiscus waimeae subsp.

hannerae, commonly known as the Kauai hibiscus, is a rare and endemic species native to the Hawaiian island of Kauai. It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow, typically blooming during the summer months.

The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments. It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to several meters in height, and it plays an important role in the local ecosystem.

Due to habitat loss and invasive species, this subspecies is considered endangered and is a focus of conservation efforts.
