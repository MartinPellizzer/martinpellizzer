Hypericum debile

Hypericum debile, commonly known as slender St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters along the stems. The plant has slender, often prostrate stems covered in fine, soft hairs. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and slightly hairy, with a reddish or yellowish hue on the underside.

Hypericum debile is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
