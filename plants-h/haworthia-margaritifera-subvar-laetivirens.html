Haworthia margaritifera subvar. laetivirens

Haworthia margaritifera subvar. laetivirens, commonly known as the "Pearl Plant," is a compact, slow-growing succulent native to South Africa.

It features a rosette of thick, fleshy, pale green leaves with white tubercles that resemble small pearls, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white or pink flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to maintain its compact form.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and rock gardens.
