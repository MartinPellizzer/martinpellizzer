Hieracium chloropsis subsp. corruscans

Hieracium chloropsis subsp.

corruscans, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.

The plant features basal leaves that are broadly lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while the stem is usually unbranched and hairy. Its flowers are yellow, with a distinctive corolla that has five lobes and a prominent central tube.

The species is characterized by its ability to produce a large number of seeds, contributing to its widespread distribution.
