Helichrysum krookii

Helichrysum krookii, commonly known as the Cape goldcup, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, papery flowers that resemble small cups, which remain intact even after flowering. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its drought tolerance, making it well-suited to arid environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and gray-green, covered in fine hairs that help reduce water loss.

Helichrysum krookii is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soils.
