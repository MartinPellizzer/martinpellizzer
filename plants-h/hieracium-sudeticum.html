Hieracium sudeticum

Hieracium sudeticum, commonly known as the Sudetic hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its yellowish to orange composite flowers bloom from June to September, attracting various pollinators.

The leaves are usually lance-shaped with a rough texture, and the plant can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters.
