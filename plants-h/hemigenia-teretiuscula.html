Hemigenia teretiuscula

Hemigenia teretiuscula, commonly known as the slender hemigenia, is a small shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal heathlands and is known for its small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. It produces small, round berries that are an important food source for local wildlife.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
