Hypericum papillare f. alpinum

Hypericum papillare f. alpinum, commonly known as the alpine St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to alpine regions of Europe.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces red-orange berries that contain seeds. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
