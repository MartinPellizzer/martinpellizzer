Haplopappus purpusii

Haplopappus purpusii, commonly known as the desert pappus, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typical sunflower-like appearance. The plant has small, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in the spring, and its leaves are typically linear and gray-green in color. Haplopappus purpusii is adapted to harsh desert conditions, with deep roots and reduced leaf surface to minimize water loss.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
