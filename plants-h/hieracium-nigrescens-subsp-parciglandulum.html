Hieracium nigrescens subsp. parciglandulum

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

parciglandulum, commonly known as the black ragwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks.

The plant features a robust root system and erect, hairy stems that bear alternate, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are yellow and clustered in heads, with a distinctive central disk.

The species is known for its toxic properties, containing pyrrolizidine alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock.
