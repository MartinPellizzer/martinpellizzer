Hieracium nigrescens subsp. odontolipes

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

odontolipes, commonly known as the toothed hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to North America. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellowish to reddish flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its sharply toothed leaves and the presence of glandular dots on the foliage. It typically thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows.

The plant is often considered a weed but plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
