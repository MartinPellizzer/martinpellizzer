Hieracium laniferum subsp. texedense

Hieracium laniferum subsp.

texedense, commonly known as the Texas hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellowish to reddish flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has basal and cauline leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
