Hieracium dentatum subsp. basifoliatum

Hieracium dentatum subsp.

basifoliatum, commonly known as the basal-leaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow flowering heads.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped with toothed margins, and the flowers are arranged in composite inflorescences.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can form dense colonies in suitable environments.
