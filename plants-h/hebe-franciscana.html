Hebe franciscana

Hebe franciscana, commonly known as the San Francisco hebe, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue or purple, during the spring and summer months. Hebe franciscana thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
