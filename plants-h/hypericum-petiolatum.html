Hypericum petiolatum

Hypericum petiolatum, commonly known as the yellow hypericum or creeping St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to North America.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters along the stems. The plant has opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky habitats.

Its botanical name reflects its historical use in herbal medicine for its purported healing properties.
