Hedysarum caripense

Hedysarum caripense, commonly known as the Caripense milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert or semi-arid environments. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.

Hedysarum caripense is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapes.
