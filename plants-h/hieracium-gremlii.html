Hieracium gremlii

Hieracium gremlii, commonly known as the Gremli's hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in harsh, cold climates. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rough, hairy texture, and its flowers are yellow, often appearing in clusters.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in many areas.
