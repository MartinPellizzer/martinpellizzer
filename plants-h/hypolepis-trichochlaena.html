Hypolepis trichochlaena

Hypolepis trichochlaena, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a tropical fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, shield-shaped fronds that are covered in fine, hairy structures. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The botanical features of Hypolepis trichochlaena make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
