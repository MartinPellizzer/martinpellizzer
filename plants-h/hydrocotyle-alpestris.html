Hydrocotyle alpestris

Hydrocotyle alpestris, commonly known as the alpine marsh penny, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features small, rounded leaves that are typically hairy and arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces white flowers with five petals, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and wet meadows.

Hydrocotyle alpestris is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it adapts to cool, humid conditions.
