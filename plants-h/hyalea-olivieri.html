Hyalea olivieri

Hyalea olivieri, commonly known as the olive tree of the genus Hyalea, is a species native to certain regions of Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often gray-green in color, and it produces small, fragrant flowers. Hyalea olivieri is characterized by its ability to withstand drought conditions due to its deep root system and waxy leaf coating.

It is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local communities.
