Hieracium villosum var. nudum

Hieracium villosum var. nudum, commonly known as the naked hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with hairy surfaces, and the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is known for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other regions as a garden plant.
