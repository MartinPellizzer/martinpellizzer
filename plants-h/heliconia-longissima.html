Heliconia longissima

Heliconia longissima, commonly known as the long-spurred heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble flowers. The plant produces long, slender spathes that enclose its inflorescence, which consists of small, clustered flowers. Its leaves are large, broad, and often have a waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Heliconia longissima is often cultivated in warm climates for its aesthetic value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
