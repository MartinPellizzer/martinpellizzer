Hieracium bauhini subsp. rodnense

Hieracium bauhini subsp.

rodnense, commonly known as the Rodnian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Carpathian region. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in mountainous areas. It has lanceolate to ovate leaves with serrated margins and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Due to its restricted distribution, it is considered a rare and localized species within its native habitat.
