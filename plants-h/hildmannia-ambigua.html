Hildmannia ambigua

Hildmannia ambigua, commonly known as the swamp hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant typically grows in moist, swampy environments and has heart-shaped leaves with a waxy surface. Hildmannia ambigua is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Its botanical features make it an important species in wetland ecosystems and a subject of study in plant ecology.
