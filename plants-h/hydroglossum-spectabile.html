Hydroglossum spectabile

Hydroglossum spectabile, commonly known as the showy water bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, pond edges, and along streams.

Hydroglossum spectabile plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
