Haloragis glauca f. sclopetifera

Haloragis glauca f. sclopetifera, commonly known as the glaucous water starwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a powdery bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in wet, marshy environments and is often found near water sources such as ponds and streams.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
