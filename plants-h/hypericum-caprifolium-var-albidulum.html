Hypericum caprifolium var. albidulum

Hypericum caprifolium var. albidulum, commonly known as white-stemmed St. John's wort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the genus Hypericum, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their yellow or orange flowers. This particular variety is characterized by its pale or white stems and smaller, less showy flowers compared to other Hypericum species. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant contains hypericin and pseudohypericin, which are bioactive compounds of interest in herbal medicine.
