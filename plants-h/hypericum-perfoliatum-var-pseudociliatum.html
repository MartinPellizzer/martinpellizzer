Hypericum perfoliatum var. pseudociliatum

Hypericum perfoliatum var. pseudociliatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved St. John's wort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features small, yellow, four-petaled flowers that bloom in summer, typically from June to August. The plant has opposite, narrow, and linear leaves that are often slightly hairy. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy or rocky soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Hypericaceae family.
