Hieracium umbelliferum subsp. ochrocephalum

Hieracium umbelliferum subsp.

ochrocephalum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to orange-yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, with a rough texture, and the stems are usually erect and branched.

It is often found in temperate regions and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
