Homaladenia vinciflora

Homaladenia vinciflora, commonly known as the giant star orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can reach up to 2 meters in height, featuring a cluster of star-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in the arid environments where it thrives.

The flowers bloom in summer, producing a strong fragrance that attracts pollinators like bees and moths.
