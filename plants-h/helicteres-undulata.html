Helicteres undulata

Helicteres undulata, commonly known as the wavy senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, and its leaves are pinnately compound with serrated edges. Helicteres undulata is known for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry to moist woodland habitats.
