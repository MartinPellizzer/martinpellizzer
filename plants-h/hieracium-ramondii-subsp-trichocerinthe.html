Hieracium ramondii subsp. trichocerinthe

Hieracium ramondii subsp.

trichocerinthe, commonly known as the trichocerine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in dry, rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer, and its leaves are usually lance-shaped with hairy surfaces. It has a taproot system and produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

This subspecies is characterized by its distinctive leaf trichomes and is part of the Asteraceae family.
