Hieracium subspeciosum subsp. boissieri

Hieracium subspeciosum subsp.

boissieri, commonly known as the Boissier's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow to orange composite flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

The plant has slender, hairy stems and basal leaves that are usually lance-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes, and it is often found in disturbed soils.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smaller flower heads and more compact growth form compared to other members of the *Hieracium* genus.
