Himantoglossum hircinum var. viride

Himantoglossum hircinum var. viride, commonly known as the green hircinum gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or grassland habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while its flowers are arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

It is known for its ornamental value and is often found in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
