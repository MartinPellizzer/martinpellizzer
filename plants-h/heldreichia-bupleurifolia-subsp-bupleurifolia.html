Heldreichia bupleurifolia subsp. bupleurifolia

Heldreichia bupleurifolia subsp.

bupleurifolia, commonly known as the Balkan bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in mountainous regions.

The plant features lanceolate leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces clusters of bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Botanically, it is noted for its distinct floral structure and adaptation to its specific ecological niche.
