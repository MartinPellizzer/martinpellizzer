Hedysarum grandiflorum subsp. sericeum

Hedysarum grandiflorum subsp.

sericeum, commonly known as the silky peavine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a silvery-gray underside.

The plant produces small, pink to reddish-purple flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry slopes and rocky areas. Hedysarum grandiflorum subsp.

sericeum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
