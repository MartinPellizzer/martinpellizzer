Helianthus nuttallii subsp. canadensis

Helianthus nuttallii subsp.

canadensis, commonly known as the Canadian sunflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America. It features bright yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, typical of the Asteraceae family.

The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with hairy stems and alternate, heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
