Heteromeles arbutifolia var. cerina

Heteromeles arbutifolia var. cerina, commonly known as the California bay laurel, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, leathery leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark, berry-like fruits that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and Mediterranean climates.

Its botanical features make it an important species in local ecosystems and horticultural practices.
