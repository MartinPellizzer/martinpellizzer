Hippeastrum puniceum var. majus

Hippeastrum puniceum var. majus, commonly known as the Red Flame amaryllis, is a striking ornamental plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range from red to orange in color, often with a contrasting white or yellow center. The plant produces strap-like, dark green leaves that are broad and lance-shaped. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers bright, indirect light.

Hippeastrum puniceum var. majus is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms and is relatively easy to grow in warm climates.
