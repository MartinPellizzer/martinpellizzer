Hysterobaeckea setifera subsp. meridionalis

Hysterobaeckea setifera subsp.

meridionalis, commonly known as the southern hairy wattle, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its yellow, pea-like flowers and feathery, glandular leaves.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as heathlands and coastal dunes. It is a shrub that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters, with a spreading habit.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
