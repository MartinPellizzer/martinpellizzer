Hamelia panamensis

Hamelia panamensis, commonly known as the Panama flamebush, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Hamelia panamensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
