Heurckia viridis

Heurckia viridis, commonly known as the green heurckia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking green foliage and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth texture, while its stems are often woody at the base.

Heurckia viridis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of garden settings.
