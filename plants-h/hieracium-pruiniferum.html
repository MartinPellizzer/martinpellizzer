Hieracium pruiniferum

Hieracium pruiniferum, commonly known as the downy hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and ragworts, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, powdery coating, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium pruiniferum is often found in temperate climates and can be a common weed in agricultural areas.
