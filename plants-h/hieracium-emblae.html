Hieracium emblae

Hieracium emblae, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often hairy.

Hieracium emblae is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting pollinator populations.
