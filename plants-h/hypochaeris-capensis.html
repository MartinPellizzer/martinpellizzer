Hypochaeris capensis

Hypochaeris capensis, commonly known as the Cape dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble dandelions. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and grasslands. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring and summer, followed by fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Hypochaeris capensis is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
