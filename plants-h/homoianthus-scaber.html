Homoianthus scaber

Homoianthus scaber, commonly known as the roughleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and showy, pentagonal flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its botanical features make it a distinctive addition to gardens and ecological landscapes.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits to pollinators.
