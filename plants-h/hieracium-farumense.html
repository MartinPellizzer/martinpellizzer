Hieracium farumense

Hieracium farumense, commonly known as the Faroe's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the island of Streymoy in the Faroe Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous grasslands and heaths, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a prominent central disk.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
