Hedera purpurea

Hedera purpurea, commonly known as purple ivy, is a climbing evergreen vine native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a striking purple underside, which becomes more pronounced in low light conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are not showy but are followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in shaded environments and is often used as a decorative plant in gardens and indoor spaces.

Hedera purpurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
