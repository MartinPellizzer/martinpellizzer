Hieracium baenitzii

Hieracium baenitzii, commonly known as the Baenitz's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

Hieracium baenitzii is often found in temperate climates and is known for its adaptability to various soil types.
