Haplopappus parvifolius

Haplopappus parvifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and sandy soils. It produces small, yellow to white flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.
