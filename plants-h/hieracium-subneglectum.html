Hieracium subneglectum

Hieracium subneglectum, commonly known as the neglected hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It is native to Europe and is commonly found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Hieracium subneglectum is often considered a weed due to its aggressive growth habit and ability to spread rapidly.
