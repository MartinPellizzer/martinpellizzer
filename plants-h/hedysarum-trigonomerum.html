Hedysarum trigonomerum

Hedysarum trigonomerum, commonly known as the three-lobed milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Hedysarum trigonomerum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
