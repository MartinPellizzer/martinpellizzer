Hymenolyma trichophyllum

Hymenolyma trichophyllum, commonly known as the silky lichen, is a foliose lichen that forms a dense, velvety coating on rocks and tree bark.

It is characterized by its intricate thallus structure, which consists of a tightly packed layer of fungal hyphae intertwined with algae or cyanobacteria. The plant's surface is covered with fine, hair-like structures called trichomes, giving it a silky appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in temperate forest regions.

Its slow growth and unique morphology make it an interesting subject for ecological and botanical study.
