Helichrysum orothamnus

Helichrysum orothamnus, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and showy yellow to orange flower heads. The plant produces small, tubular florets that are typically surrounded by bracts, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

The flowers are long-lasting when dried, which contributes to its popularity in floral arrangements.
