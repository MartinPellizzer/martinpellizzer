Helenium oppositifolium

Helenium oppositifolium, commonly known as the opposite-leaved sneezeweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy orange to red blooms that appear in late summer. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Helenium oppositifolium is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal use.
