Hydrastis

Hydrastis, commonly known as goldenseal, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow roots, which are the primary part used medicinally. The plant typically grows in rich, moist woodlands and has palmately divided leaves. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and bloom in early spring.

Goldenseal is known for its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, making it a popular herb in traditional medicine.
