Hieracium pseudophyllodes

Hieracium pseudophyllodes, commonly known as the false hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellowish-green flowers and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It has a creeping growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves.

Due to its invasive potential, it is often considered a weed in agricultural and natural areas.
