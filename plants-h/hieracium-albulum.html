Hieracium albulum

Hieracium albulum, commonly known as meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are usually hairy and have a rough texture. Its flowers are yellow, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets, and it produces achenes as its fruit.

Hieracium albulum is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
