Hieracium lomochnoum

Hieracium lomochnoum, commonly known as the Siberian hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer. Native to northern regions, including Siberia and parts of Europe, it thrives in moist, rocky or sandy soils. The plant is perennial and often found in alpine or subarctic environments.

Its medicinal properties have been studied for potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.
