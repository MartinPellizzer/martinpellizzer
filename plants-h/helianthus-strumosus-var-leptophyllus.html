Helianthus strumosus var. leptophyllus

Helianthus strumosus var. leptophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sunflower, is a variant of the sunflower genus native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils, preferring full sun. Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal practices.
