Hiraea salicifolia

Hiraea salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaved hiraea, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its willow-like leaves that are lanceolate and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Hiraea salicifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.
