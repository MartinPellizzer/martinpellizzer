Hieracium cordifolium subsp. gouanii

Hieracium cordifolium subsp.

gouanii, commonly known as the heart-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in flat-topped clusters.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
