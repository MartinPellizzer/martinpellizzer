Hieracium asterophorum

Hieracium asterophorum, commonly known as the starry hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disk, and it produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Hieracium asterophorum is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
