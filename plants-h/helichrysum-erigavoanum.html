Helichrysum erigavoanum

Helichrysum erigavoanum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that remain open for extended periods, giving it its common name. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Helichrysum erigavoanum is also valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine.
