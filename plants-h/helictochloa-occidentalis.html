Helictochloa occidentalis

Helictochloa occidentalis, commonly known as western needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and fine, needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe.

Helictochloa occidentalis plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
