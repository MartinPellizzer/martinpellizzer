Hemionitis linearis

Hemionitis linearis, commonly known as the linear hemionitis, is a small, perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia and Africa.

It features delicate, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited water availability.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its resilience and ornamental value.
