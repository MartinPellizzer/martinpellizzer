Hyparrhenia soluta

Hyparrhenia soluta, commonly known as the African grass or silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, silver-green leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers in summer, which are arranged in spikelets. Hyparrhenia soluta is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and drought tolerance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adaptable to a variety of environmental conditions.
