Hieracium belogradcense

Hieracium belogradcense, commonly known as the Belgrade Hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkans.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy stems. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head.

It is known for its aggressive spreading habit, making it a potential invasive species in certain regions.
