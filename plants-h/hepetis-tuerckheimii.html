Hepetis tuerckheimii

Hepetis tuerckheimii, commonly known as the Tuerckheim's ironweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and swamps. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall.

Hepetis tuerckheimii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
