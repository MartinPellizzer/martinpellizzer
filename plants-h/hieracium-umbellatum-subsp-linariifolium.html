Hieracium umbellatum subsp. linariifolium

Hieracium umbellatum subsp.

linariifolium, commonly known as the slender wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or grassland habitats. It is a perennial species that thrives in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
