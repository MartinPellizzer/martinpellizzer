Halocharis turcomanica

Halocharis turcomanica, commonly known as the Turkestan haloxylon, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is well adapted to harsh environments, exhibiting drought resistance and efficient water storage mechanisms. It plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important component of desert ecosystems.
