Humularia purpureocoerulea var. gigantea

Humularia purpureocoerulea var. gigantea, commonly known as the giant purple-blue bellflower, is a striking perennial plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from deep purple to a striking sky blue. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and requires well-drained soil to flourish.

Due to its rarity and unique beauty, it is often cultivated in specialized botanical gardens.
