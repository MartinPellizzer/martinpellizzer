Helianthemum apenninum subsp. pilosum

Helianthemum apenninum subsp.

pilosum, commonly known as the Sicilian rockrose, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has grey-green, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in its arid habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found on rocky slopes and coastal areas.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscaping.
