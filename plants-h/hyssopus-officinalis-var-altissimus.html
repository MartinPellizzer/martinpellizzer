Hyssopus officinalis var. altissimus

Hyssopus officinalis var. altissimus, commonly known as tall hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces spikes of blue to violet flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its aromatic leaves and historical use in herbal medicine.
