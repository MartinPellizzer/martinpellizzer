Hexaptera spathulata subsp. glaberrima

Hexaptera spathulata subsp.

glaberrima, commonly known as the smooth-leafed hexaptera, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are typically smooth and glabrous, giving it its subspecific name.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are often followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
