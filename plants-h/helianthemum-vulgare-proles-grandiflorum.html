Helianthemum vulgare proles grandiflorum

Helianthemum vulgare proles grandiflorum, commonly known as the large-flowered rockrose, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Europe.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically gray-green in color, adapted to arid environments. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
