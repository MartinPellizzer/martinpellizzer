Hedysarum gyroides

Hedysarum gyroides, commonly known as the goldenpea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets, and the plant has a fibrous root system.

Hedysarum gyroides is often found in open woodlands and dry grasslands, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
