Hieracium laevigatum subsp. nantuaticum

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

nantuaticum, commonly known as the smooth hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a central disc.

It is a perennial species that spreads through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
