Hibiscus stewartii

Hibiscus stewartii, commonly known as the Stewart's hibiscus, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, depending on the variety. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Hibiscus stewartii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
