Hieracium onosmoides subsp. apostolorum

Hieracium onosmoides subsp.

apostolorum, commonly known as the Holy Thorn, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands. It features upright, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smaller, more compact inflorescence compared to other members of the Hieracium genus.
