Hieracium macrodontophyllum

Hieracium macrodontophyllum, commonly known as the large-toothed hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are rough and hairy, with distinctive lobed shapes, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

Hieracium macrodontophyllum is often found in open habitats and can spread through rhizomes, making it a common sight in many regions.
