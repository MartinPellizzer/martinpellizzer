Hoya myrmecopa subsp. myrmecopa

Hoya myrmecopa subsp.

myrmecopa, commonly known as the "ant-eating hoya," is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and clusters of waxy, pale green to white flowers.

The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. It is a climbing epiphyte, typically found growing on trees in humid, tropical environments.

Its unique name is derived from the shape of its flowers, which resemble the form of an ant.
