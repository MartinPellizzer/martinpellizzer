Helleborine atropurpurea var. tremolsii

Helleborine atropurpurea var. tremolsii, commonly known as the purple helleborine, is a perennial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black flowers, which are typically arranged in a raceme. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

This variety is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
