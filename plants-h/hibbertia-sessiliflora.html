Hibbertia sessiliflora

Hibbertia sessiliflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hibbertia, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and narrow, ovate leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive red or orange throat. Hibbertia sessiliflora is a shrub that thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
