Hierochloe parviflora

Hierochloe parviflora, commonly known as the slender hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks. The plant has fine, tufted leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, pale green to yellow flowers. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.

Hierochloe parviflora plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
