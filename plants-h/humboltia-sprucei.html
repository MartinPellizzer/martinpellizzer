Humboltia sprucei

Humboltia sprucei, commonly known as the Humboldt's lily, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in moist, shaded areas. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue in color. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in a fine, silky hair, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Humboltia sprucei is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
