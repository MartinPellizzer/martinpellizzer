Hippocrepis

Hippocrepis, commonly known as the helmet orchid, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are characterized by their unique, helmet-shaped flowers, which give them their common name. They are typically found in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, often in grasslands or rocky habitats. The genus includes several species, such as Hippocrepis lutea and Hippocrepis inebrians, which vary in flower color and size.

Botanically, they are notable for their intricate floral structures and specialized pollination mechanisms.
