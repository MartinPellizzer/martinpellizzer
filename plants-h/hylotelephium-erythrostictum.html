Hylotelephium erythrostictum

Hylotelephium erythrostictum, commonly known as the red-streaked stonecrop, is a perennial succulent native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It features fleshy, upright stems with opposite, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces clusters of showy, pink to red flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a favored plant among gardeners.
