Hieracium glaucopsis subsp. glaucopsis

Hieracium glaucopsis subsp.

glaucopsis, commonly known as the blue hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its distinctive blue to purplish flower heads.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring well-drained conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish or purplish in color.

It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into fluffy, white to brown seeds.
