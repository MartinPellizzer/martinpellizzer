Hippeastrum pardinum

Hippeastrum pardinum, commonly known as the Pardinal amaryllis, is a bulbous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, large, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces a tall, slender stem with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its bulbs are perennial and can be propagated through division or by seed, making it a popular choice for gardeners and florists.

Hippeastrum pardinum thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to bright light for optimal growth.
