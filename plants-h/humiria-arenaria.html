Humiria arenaria

Humiria arenaria, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in sandy or well-drained soils. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white, typically blooming in the spring and summer. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to conserve water in arid environments.

Humiria arenaria is often used in ornamental gardening for its drought tolerance and aesthetic appeal.
