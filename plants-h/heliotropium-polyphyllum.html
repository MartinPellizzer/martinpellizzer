Heliotropium polyphyllum

Heliotropium polyphyllum, commonly known as the many-leaved heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, bushy growth habit and opposite, often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Heliotropium polyphyllum is also noted for its aromatic foliage, which can be used in traditional herbal remedies.
