Hieracium thalliscodes

Hieracium thalliscodes, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring well-drained conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are bright yellow, attracting various pollinators.

Hieracium thalliscodes is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in some areas.
