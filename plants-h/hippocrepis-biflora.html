Hippocrepis biflora

Hippocrepis biflora, commonly known as the two-flowered rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, pale purple to white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are arranged in a raceme and bloom from late spring to early summer.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in many areas.
