Hakea acrobotrya

Hakea acrobotrya, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and produces distinctive red or orange flowers that appear in late summer. The plant has a woody structure with thick, persistent branches, and its flowers are typically clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes. Hakea acrobotrya is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

It is an important component of the local flora and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
