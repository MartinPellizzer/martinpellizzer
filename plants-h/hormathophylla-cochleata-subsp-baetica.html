Hormathophylla cochleata subsp. baetica

Hormathophylla cochleata subsp.

baetica, commonly known as the Baetican bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive floral display.
