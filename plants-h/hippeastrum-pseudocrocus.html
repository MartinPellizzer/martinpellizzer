Hippeastrum pseudocrocus

Hippeastrum pseudocrocus, commonly known as the purple-flowered amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It features vibrant purple or red flowers with a striking white or pink throat, often blooming in late summer or early autumn. The plant has strap-like, strap-shaped leaves that are broad and lanceolate, growing from a fleshy underground bulb. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

This species is popular among gardeners for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
