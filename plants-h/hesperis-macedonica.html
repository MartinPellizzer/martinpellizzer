Hesperis macedonica

Hesperis macedonica, commonly known as the Macedonian honesty, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its delicate, white flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 cm and produces small, capsule-like fruits. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while its flowers bloom in early summer.

Hesperis macedonica is often found in meadows and grasslands, and it is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
