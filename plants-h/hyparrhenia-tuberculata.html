Hyparrhenia tuberculata

Hyparrhenia tuberculata, commonly known as the tufted grass or African silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, silver-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Hyparrhenia tuberculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
