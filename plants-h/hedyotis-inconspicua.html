Hedyotis inconspicua

Hedyotis inconspicua, commonly known as the creeping mouseear, is a small, herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has small, opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are tiny, white or pale yellow, and are clustered in dense, terminal spikes.

Hedyotis inconspicua is often found in wetland habitats and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, temperate climates.
