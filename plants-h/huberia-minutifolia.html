Huberia minutifolia

Huberia minutifolia, commonly known as the dwarf huberia, is a small, flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and delicate, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of only a few centimeters, making it well-suited for rock gardens or as a container plant. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, and it is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
