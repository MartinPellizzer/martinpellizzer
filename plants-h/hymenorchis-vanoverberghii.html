Hymenorchis vanoverberghii

Hymenorchis vanoverberghii, commonly known as the Vanoverbergh's hooded orchid, is a terrestrial orchid belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique hooded flower structure, which is a distinctive botanical feature. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption from the soil.

The species is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines.
