Haemanthus sessiliflorus

Haemanthus sessiliflorus, commonly known as the Natal lily, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features striking, glossy green leaves that are sword-shaped and arranged in a rosette. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value and ease of cultivation make it a popular choice among gardeners.
