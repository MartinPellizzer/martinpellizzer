Haydonia triphylla

Haydonia triphylla, commonly known as the three-leaved orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they are divided into three distinct leaflets, which is a key identifying feature.

Haydonia triphylla is often grown as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value and unique foliage.
