Heracleum sphondylium var. aestivum

Heracleum sphondylium var. aestivum, commonly known as cow parsnip, is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and large, heart-shaped leaves with a coarse texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late summer. It is often found in moist meadows, stream banks, and disturbed areas.

The leaves and stems are covered in fine hairs and can cause skin irritation upon contact.
