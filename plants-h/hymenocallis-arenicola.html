Hymenocallis arenicola

Hymenocallis arenicola, commonly known as the sand dune spider lily, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking white or pale yellow flowers with a distinctive, spidery appearance. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal dunes and maritime habitats. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a single flower per stem, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

Hymenocallis arenicola is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds, making it a resilient species in harsh coastal environments.
