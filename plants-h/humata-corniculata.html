Humata corniculata

Humata corniculata, commonly known as the horned vetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a leguminous species, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its distinctive horn-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, bean-like pods that contain seeds.

Humata corniculata is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
