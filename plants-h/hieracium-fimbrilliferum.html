Hieracium fimbrilliferum

Hieracium fimbrilliferum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are hairy and often have a reddish or purplish hue. Its flowering stems bear numerous small yellow flowers arranged in a dense cluster.

Hieracium fimbrilliferum typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks.
