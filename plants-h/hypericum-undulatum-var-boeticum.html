Hypericum undulatum var. boeticum

Hypericum undulatum var. boeticum, commonly known as the wrinkled St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are typically oval with a slightly wavy edge. The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry habitats. It is noted for its medicinal properties, with parts of the plant traditionally used in herbal remedies.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
