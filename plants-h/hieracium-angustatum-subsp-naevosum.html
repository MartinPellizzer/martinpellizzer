Hieracium angustatum subsp. naevosum

Hieracium angustatum subsp.

naevosum, commonly known as the narrowleaf hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellowish to reddish flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while the stems are often reddish in color.

The subspecies naevosum is distinguished by its distinctive leaf patterns and slightly different flowering morphology compared to other members of the species.
