Heracleum trachycarpum

Heracleum trachycarpum, commonly known as cow parsnip, is a tall biennial or short-lived perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It features a robust, hollow stem that is often hairy and can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant has large, umbrella-shaped compound leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and arranged in compound umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family.

The plant produces a distinctive, aromatic odor and is known for its potential to cause skin irritation upon contact.
