Hieracium pilosella subsp. grisellum

Hieracium pilosella subsp.

grisellum, commonly known as the gray-haired hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
