Hieracium pannonicum subsp. parvicapitulum

Hieracium pannonicum subsp.

parvicapitulum, commonly known as the small-headed hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its compact rosette of basal leaves and small, yellowish flower heads.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are composed of both ray and disc florets, which attract various pollinators.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smaller flower heads and shorter peduncles compared to other Hieracium species.
