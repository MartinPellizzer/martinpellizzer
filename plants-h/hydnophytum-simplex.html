Hydnophytum simplex

Hydnophytum simplex, commonly known as the pitcher plant or simply the hydnophytum, is a unique epiphytic plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its specialized leaf structures that form hollow, pitcher-like traps to capture and digest insects. The plant relies on these nutrient-rich insects to supplement its nutrient intake, as it grows in nutrient-poor environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain, making it highly dependent on humid conditions.

Hydnophytum simplex plays an important ecological role in its habitat by contributing to nutrient cycling and supporting biodiversity.
