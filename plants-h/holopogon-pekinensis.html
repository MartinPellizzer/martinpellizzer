Holopogon pekinensis

Holopogon pekinensis, commonly known as the Peking grass, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, typically with a smooth or slightly rough surface.

Holopogon pekinensis is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.
