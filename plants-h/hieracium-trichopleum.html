Hieracium trichopleum

Hieracium trichopleum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies. Its leaves are hairy and lance-shaped, while the flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disc.

Hieracium trichopleum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
