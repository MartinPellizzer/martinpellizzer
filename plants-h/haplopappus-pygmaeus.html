Haplopappus pygmaeus

Haplopappus pygmaeus, commonly known as the dwarf pappus, is a small flowering plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and wind-dispersed seeds. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its diminutive size, often less than 10 centimeters tall, allows it to thrive in limited spaces.

The flowers are usually yellow or white, and the plant is known for its resilience in extreme climates.
