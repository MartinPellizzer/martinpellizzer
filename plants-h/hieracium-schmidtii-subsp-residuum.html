Hieracium schmidtii subsp. residuum

Hieracium schmidtii subsp.

residuum, commonly known as the Schmidt's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It has small, hairy leaves and produces numerous seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
