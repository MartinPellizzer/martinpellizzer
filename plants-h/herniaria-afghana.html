Herniaria afghana

Herniaria afghana, commonly known as Afghan sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its small, succulent leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its compact growth habit and ability to survive in harsh environments make it a resilient species.

Herniaria afghana is often found in rocky areas and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
