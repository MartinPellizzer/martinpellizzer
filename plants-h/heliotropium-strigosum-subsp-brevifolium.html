Heliotropium strigosum subsp. brevifolium

Heliotropium strigosum subsp.

brevifolium, commonly known as the short-leaved heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East. It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that typically range in color from pink to purple.

The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are usually less than 2 inches long. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
