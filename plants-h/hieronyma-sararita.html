Hieronyma sararita

Hieronyma sararita, commonly known as the Brazilian passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It belongs to the Passifloraceae family and is characterized by its striking, complex flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of stamens. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in nutrients and often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its stems are woody and covered in fine hairs.

Hieronyma sararita is also valued for its ornamental beauty and traditional medicinal uses.
