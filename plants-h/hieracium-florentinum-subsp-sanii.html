Hieracium florentinum subsp. sanii

Hieracium florentinum subsp.

sanii, commonly known as the San Francisco goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to California. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
