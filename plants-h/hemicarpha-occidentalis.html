Hemicarpha occidentalis

Hemicarpha occidentalis, commonly known as western hemicarpha, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Hemicarpha occidentalis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
