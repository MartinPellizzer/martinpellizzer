Hypericum silenoides subsp. silenoides

Hypericum silenoides subsp.

silenoides, commonly known as yellow-stemmed St. John's wort, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its yellow stems, which are often covered in short, glandular hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers with four petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and woodland edges.
