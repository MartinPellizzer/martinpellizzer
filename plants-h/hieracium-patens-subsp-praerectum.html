Hieracium patens subsp. praerectum

Hieracium patens subsp.

praerectum, commonly known as the upright hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow flowering heads.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a single, showy capitulum.

It is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid spread and competitive growth.
