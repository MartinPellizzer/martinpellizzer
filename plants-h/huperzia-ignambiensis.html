Huperzia ignambiensis

Huperzia ignambiensis, commonly known as the Ignambien Huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its simple, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. The plant is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly in countries like Colombia and Ecuador. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and rocky crevices.

Huperzia ignambiensis is noted for its slow growth and is often used in horticultural collections for its unique appearance.
