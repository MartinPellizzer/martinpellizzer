Hyptis sinuata var. genuina

Hyptis sinuata var. genuina, commonly known as the Mexican mint marigold, is a perennial herb native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features lance-shaped, hairy leaves with a strong, mint-like fragrance, and produces small, tubular flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental and medicinal gardening.

This species is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a natural insect repellent.
