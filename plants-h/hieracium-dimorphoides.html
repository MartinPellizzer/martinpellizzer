Hieracium dimorphoides

Hieracium dimorphoides, commonly known as the two-form hawkweed, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates. It has both basal and stem leaves, which are usually hairy and lance-shaped.

Hieracium dimorphoides is an annual or biennial plant, depending on the climate, and is known for its ability to spread through seeds and rhizomes.
