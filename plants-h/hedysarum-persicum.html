Hedysarum persicum

Hedysarum persicum, commonly known as Persian vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to regions of Central Asia, including parts of Iran and Afghanistan. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in early summer. Its pods contain small, edible seeds that are used in traditional cuisines.

Hedysarum persicum is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural purposes.
