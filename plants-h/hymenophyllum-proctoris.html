Hymenophyllum proctoris

Hymenophyllum proctoris, commonly known as the Proctor's filmy fern, is a member of the Hymenophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, translucent fronds.

It is a small epiphytic fern that thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. The fronds are narrow and possess a leathery texture, with a unique structure that allows for efficient water retention. Its spores are produced in small, clustered structures called sori, which are located on the underside of the fronds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in specialized gardens due to its intricate appearance.
