Hieracium glabridens

Hieracium glabridens, commonly known as the smooth meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is a perennial herb that can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
