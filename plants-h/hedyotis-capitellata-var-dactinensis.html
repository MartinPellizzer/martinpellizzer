Hedyotis capitellata var. dactinensis

Hedyotis capitellata var. dactinensis, commonly known as the mountain buttonweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. The plant features small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
