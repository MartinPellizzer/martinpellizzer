Hariota funalis

Hariota funalis, commonly known as the golden thread orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves and delicate, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its roots are often mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to enhance nutrient absorption.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in horticulture.
