Hieracium cochlearifolium subsp. tissierei

Hieracium cochlearifolium subsp.

tissierei, commonly known as the Tissier's hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow flowers arranged in a composite head.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with serrated edges, and the flowers bloom from late spring to early summer.

It is a relatively small plant, rarely exceeding 30 cm in height.
