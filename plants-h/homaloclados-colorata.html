Homaloclados colorata

Homaloclados colorata, commonly known as the colorful coral tree, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, papery bracts that display a range of colors, often in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by woody pods containing several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its showy appearance, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a landscape plant.
