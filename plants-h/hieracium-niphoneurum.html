Hieracium niphoneurum

Hieracium niphoneurum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a cluster.

It is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is considered a weed in some agricultural areas.
