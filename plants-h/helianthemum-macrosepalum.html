Helianthemum macrosepalum

Helianthemum macrosepalum, commonly known as the large-sepal sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically thrives in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are fleshy and succulent, adapted to conserve water in arid environments.

Helianthemum macrosepalum is often used in rock gardens due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.
