Hydrangea serrata f. buergeri

Hydrangea serrata f. buergeri, commonly known as the Japanese mountain hydrangea, is a compact, shrub-like plant native to Japan.

It features small, serrated leaves that are typically a deep green, with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of white or pink flowers in late spring to early summer, which gradually change color depending on soil pH. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to humid climates.
