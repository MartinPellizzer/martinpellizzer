Hieracium setanium

Hieracium setanium, commonly known as the clustered hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a dense cluster.

Hieracium setanium is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
