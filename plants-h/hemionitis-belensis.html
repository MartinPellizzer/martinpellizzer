Hemionitis belensis

Hemionitis belensis, commonly known as the Belenski's hemionitis, is a rare and unique fern species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically bright green and have a finely serrated edge. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments, often found in rocky or forested areas with high humidity. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds, a common feature among ferns.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Hemionitis belensis is considered a conservation concern.
