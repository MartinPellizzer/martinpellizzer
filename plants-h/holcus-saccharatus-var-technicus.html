Holcus saccharatus var. technicus

Holcus saccharatus var. technicus, commonly known as the technical sugargrass, is a variant of the sugargrass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a grass-like plant characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found near roadsides or in agricultural areas. It is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor environments, making it a common pioneer species in ecological succession.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as an indicator plant for soil conditions and its potential use in phytoremediation studies.
