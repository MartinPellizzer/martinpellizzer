Helicia vestita var. mixta

Helicia vestita var. mixta, commonly known as the mixed helmetwood, is a variant of the helmetwood plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in yellow or greenish hues, and it produces drupes that are typically red or black when mature.

The botanical characteristics of this variant make it a notable species in horticulture and ecological studies.
