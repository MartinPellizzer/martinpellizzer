Hieracium chaboissaei subsp. adenophanum

Hieracium chaboissaei subsp.

adenophanum, commonly known as the Corsican hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and a flowering stem that bears yellow composite flowers.

The plant has rough, hairy leaves and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal use.

Its subspecies status highlights its distinct genetic and morphological traits compared to other Hieracium species.
