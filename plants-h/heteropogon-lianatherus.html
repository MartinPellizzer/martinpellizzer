Heteropogon lianatherus

Heteropogon lianatherus, commonly known as the hairy chaingrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and long, slender stems that often form dense mats. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with narrow, grass-like leaves covered in fine hairs. Its botanical features make it adaptable to various soil types and environmental conditions.

It is often found in wetland or marshy areas, contributing to ecological stability in such habitats.
