Hieracium diaphanoidopsis subsp. diaphanoidopsis

Hieracium diaphanoidopsis subsp.

diaphanoidopsis, commonly known as the translucent hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This subspecies is native to parts of Europe and is often found in meadows and grassy areas.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphological features.
