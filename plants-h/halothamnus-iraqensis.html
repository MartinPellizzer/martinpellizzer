Halothamnus iraqensis

Halothamnus iraqensis, commonly known as the Iraqi saltbush, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is well adapted to saline soils and can thrive in areas with low rainfall.

Halothamnus iraqensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock in its native habitat.
