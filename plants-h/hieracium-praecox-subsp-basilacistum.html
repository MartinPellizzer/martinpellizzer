Hieracium praecox subsp. basilacistum

Hieracium praecox subsp.

basilacistum, commonly known as the early hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and basal rosettes of lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers that are typically arranged in a single head. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grasslands.

Due to its aggressive growth, it can become a problematic weed in agricultural and natural habitats.
