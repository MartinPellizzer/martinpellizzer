Hieracium glandulidens

Hieracium glandulidens, commonly known as the glandular hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering heads that produce yellow flowers. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas. It has hairy stems and leaves, and its flowers are typically arranged in a single head.

Hieracium glandulidens is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and can sometimes be considered a weed in agricultural settings.
