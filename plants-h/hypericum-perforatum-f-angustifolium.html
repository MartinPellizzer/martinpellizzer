Hypericum perforatum f. angustifolium

Hypericum perforatum f. angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved St. John's wort, is a botanical variant of the common St. John's wort.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, linear leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The leaves are often dotted with small, translucent glands, a feature typical of the Hypericum genus. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is native to Europe and parts of Asia.

This form is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
