Hieracium wilczekianum subsp. schwimmeri

Hieracium wilczekianum subsp.

schwimmeri, commonly known as the Schwimmer's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Compositae. It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a flowering stem that bears yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in open habitats. It is native to Europe and has been introduced to other regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
