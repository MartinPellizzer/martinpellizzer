Helictotrichon tibeticum var. laxiflorum

Helictotrichon tibeticum var. laxiflorum, commonly known as the Tibetan bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, blue-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces delicate, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in cold, arid environments and is adapted to the extreme conditions of its mountainous habitat.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
