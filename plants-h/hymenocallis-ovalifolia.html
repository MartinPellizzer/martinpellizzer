Hymenocallis ovalifolia

Hymenocallis ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval leaf spider lily, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, funnel-shaped white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant features narrow, strap-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Hymenocallis ovalifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in warm climates.
