Helenium grantioides

Helenium grantioides, commonly known as the yellow gladiolus, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to the eastern United States and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 feet, with long, narrow leaves and a sturdy stem. Its flowers consist of a central disk surrounded by ray florets, which give it a distinctive gladiolus-like appearance.

Helenium grantioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
