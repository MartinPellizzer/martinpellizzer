Heracleum sommieri

Heracleum sommieri, commonly known as the clustered cow parsnip, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and umbellate flowers. The plant can grow up to 2 meters tall, featuring large, lobed leaves and white to pale yellow flowers arranged in complex umbels. Its stems are hollow and often covered with fine hairs, while its roots are thick and tuberous.

The plant is known for its strong, often unpleasant odor, which can be a deterrent to herbivores.
