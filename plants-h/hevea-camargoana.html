Hevea camargoana

Hevea camargoana, commonly known as the Camargo rubber tree, is a species native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 20 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow.

The tree is notable for its latex-producing properties, though it is less commercially significant than other Hevea species.
