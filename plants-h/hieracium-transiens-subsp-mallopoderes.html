Hieracium transiens subsp. mallopoderes

Hieracium transiens subsp.

mallopoderes, commonly known as the wandering hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower.

The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, with a distinctive basal rosette structure, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

It is known for its rapid spread and ability to colonize new areas, making it both ecologically significant and sometimes invasive.
