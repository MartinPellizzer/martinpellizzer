Hieracium canovirens

Hieracium canovirens, commonly known as the Canary Islands hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellow to orange flowers in the summer months. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
