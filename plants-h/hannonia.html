Hannonia

Hannonia, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its small, compound leaves that often have a silvery or grayish underside, giving it its common name. The flowers are typically clustered in inflorescences and are usually small and pale in color. Hannonia species are native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and are often found in dry, open habitats.

These plants are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes used in traditional medicine.
