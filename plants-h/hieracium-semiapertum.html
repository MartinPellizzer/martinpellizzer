Hieracium semiapertum

Hieracium semiapertum, commonly known as the semi-open hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has basal and stem leaves that are typically lance-shaped with hairy surfaces. It is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas.

Hieracium semiapertum is a perennial herb that can form dense colonies, making it both a common wildflower and a potential weed in cultivated landscapes.
