Hieracium micrastrum

Hieracium micrastrum, commonly known as the small-stemmed hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and basal leaves that are usually lance-shaped. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish composite flowers arranged in a head, typical of the daisy family. It is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
