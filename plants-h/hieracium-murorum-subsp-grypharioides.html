Hieracium murorum subsp. grypharioides

Hieracium murorum subsp.

grypharioides, commonly known as the creeping hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a single head, surrounded by bracts. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands, roadsides, and wastelands.
