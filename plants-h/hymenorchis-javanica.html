Hymenorchis javanica

Hymenorchis javanica, commonly known as the Java twayblade, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with a slender, leafless stem that produces a single, narrow, lance-shaped leaf. The plant features a single, showy, greenish-yellow flower with a distinctive, spurred lip. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
