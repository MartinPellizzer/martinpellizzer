Hakea obtecta

Hakea obtecta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hakea, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical or cylindrical-egg-shaped spikes of bright red flowers that appear in spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is drought-tolerant.

Hakea obtecta is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
