Hemerocallis graminea var. humilior

Hemerocallis graminea var. humilior, commonly known as the dwarf daylily, is a low-growing perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, with each flower lasting only one day. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its compact growth and ornamental appeal in gardens.
