Hamelia grandiflora

Hamelia grandiflora, commonly known as flame bush, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces bright orange to red tubular flowers, which are highly attractive to hummingbirds and other pollinators. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Hamelia grandiflora is known for its ornamental value and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
