Hippocrepis comosa var. major

Hippocrepis comosa var. major, commonly known as the large woolly orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
