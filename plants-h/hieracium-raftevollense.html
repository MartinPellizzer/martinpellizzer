Hieracium raftevollense

Hieracium raftevollense, commonly known as the Raftevoll hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the dandelion family (Compositae) and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and basal rosette of leaves. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowering stems are often branched.

Hieracium raftevollense is an important plant in ecological studies due to its role in stabilizing soil in rocky habitats.
