Hordeum chilense var. muticum

Hordeum chilense var. muticum, commonly known as the Chilean barley, is a wild grass species native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in open, disturbed habitats.

Hordeum chilense var. muticum is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its adaptability and potential for use in ecological restoration projects.
