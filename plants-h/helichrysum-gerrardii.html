Helichrysum gerrardii

Helichrysum gerrardii, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange composite flowers that resemble small daisies. The plant produces small, papery bracts that give it a fluffy appearance, and it is known for its long-lasting flowers that retain their color when dried. Helichrysum gerrardii thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often growing in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and durability.
