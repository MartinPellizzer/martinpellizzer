Hosta japonica var. fortis

Hosta japonica var. fortis, commonly known as the Japanese hosta, is a popular ornamental plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a slight variegation. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in late summer, which are borne on tall, slender stems. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is known for its hardiness and ability to form dense, attractive clumps over time.
