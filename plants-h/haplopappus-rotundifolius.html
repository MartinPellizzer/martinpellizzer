Haplopappus rotundifolius

Haplopappus rotundifolius, commonly known as the round-leafed pappus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive bracts. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is well adapted to drought conditions. Its small, rounded leaves are arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

The species is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
