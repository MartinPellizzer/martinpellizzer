Heptallon graveolens

Heptallon graveolens, commonly known as the sweet sarsaparilla, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces long, cylindrical fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its roots and leaves contain compounds that contribute to its distinct, sweet-smelling aroma.

Heptallon graveolens is valued for its potential medicinal properties and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
