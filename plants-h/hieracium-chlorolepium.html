Hieracium chlorolepium

Hieracium chlorolepium, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, thriving in temperate climates. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are usually basal, with a rough texture and a distinct yellowish-green coloration.
