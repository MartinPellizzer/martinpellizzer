Hieracium rectiforme

Hieracium rectiforme, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Hieracium rectiforme is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in disturbed areas.
