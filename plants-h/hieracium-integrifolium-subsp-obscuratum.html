Hieracium integrifolium subsp. obscuratum

Hieracium integrifolium subsp.

obscuratum, commonly known as the dark-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features basal rosettes of dark green, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its inflorescence consists of a single flower head with ray florets and a central disk.

It is characterized by its distinctive, somewhat coarse foliage and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
