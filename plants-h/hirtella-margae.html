Hirtella margae

Hirtella margae, commonly known as the Brazilian hirtella, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine, short hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Hirtella margae typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for ecological studies and botanical gardens.
