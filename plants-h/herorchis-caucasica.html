Herorchis caucasica

Herorchis caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stem and a single, showy flower with a distinctive hooded lip. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing a delicate, fragrant flower that attracts pollinators such as bees and moths. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Herorchis caucasica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
