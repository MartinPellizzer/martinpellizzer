Hypericum cyclosepalum

Hypericum cyclosepalum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its flowering period attracts various pollinators, including bees and birds.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
