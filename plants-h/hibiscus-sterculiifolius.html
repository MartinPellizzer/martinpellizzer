Hibiscus sterculiifolius

Hibiscus sterculiifolius, commonly known as the African hibiscus, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range from pink to red, with five deeply lobed petals. The leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often covered in fine hairs. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in savannas and open woodlands.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local cultures.
