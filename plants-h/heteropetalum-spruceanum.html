Heteropetalum spruceanum

Heteropetalum spruceanum, commonly known as the red mountain-ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically alternate and have a serrated margin. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pink in color, followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
