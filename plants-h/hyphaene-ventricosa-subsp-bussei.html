Hyphaene ventricosa subsp. bussei

Hyphaene ventricosa subsp.

bussei, commonly known as the African date palm, is a tropical plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, date-like fruits. Its roots form a network of rhizomes, which help in stabilizing the soil and absorbing nutrients.

This subspecies is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it an important plant in its native ecosystems.
