Hopea canarensis

Hopea canarensis, commonly known as the Malabar ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood, which is highly valued for its durability and strength. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth surface. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by elongated, woody fruits.

Hopea canarensis plays an important role in the ecological balance of its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
