Hymenophyllum saenzianum

Hymenophyllum saenzianum, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a small, epiphytic fern native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety fronds that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, moss-like appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or rocks. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and are covered by a protective indusium.

Hymenophyllum saenzianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
