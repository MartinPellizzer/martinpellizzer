Hieracium pseudonaegelianum

Hieracium pseudonaegelianum, commonly known as the false sand everlasting.

It is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in Kazakhstan and southern Russia. This species belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

It produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers that bloom in the summer months.
