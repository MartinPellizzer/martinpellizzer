Hieracium schraderi

Hieracium schraderi, commonly known as Schrader's hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its leaves are basal, rosette-forming, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head.

Schrader's hawkweed is an important component of alpine ecosystems, contributing to biodiversity and soil stability.
