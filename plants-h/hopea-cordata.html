Hopea cordata

Hopea cordata, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree has simple, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

Hopea cordata plays an important role in the ecosystems of its native habitats, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
