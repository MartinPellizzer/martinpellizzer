Hieracium vulgatum var. glandulosius

Hieracium vulgatum var. glandulosius, commonly known as the glandular hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features rough, hairy stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped with toothed margins. Its flowers are typically yellow and appear in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

It is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
