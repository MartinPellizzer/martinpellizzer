Hieracium pseudobifidum subsp. grebenicum

Hieracium pseudobifidum subsp.

grebenicum, commonly known as the Grebenik's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and forest edges.

The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, linear leaves and produces yellow flowers in dense, flat-topped inflorescences. It has a fibrous root system and is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and wind-dispersed seeds.
