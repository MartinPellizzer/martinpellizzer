Homopogon chevalieri

Homopogon chevalieri, commonly known as the Chevalier's grass, is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes grasses.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the grass family.

Due to its ornamental value and low maintenance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
