Hunga guillauminii

Hunga guillauminii, commonly known as the Flameleaf Orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange and red inflorescences, which are adorned with intricate, waxy petals. The plant thrives in high humidity and partial shade, often growing along the forest floor or on the trunks of trees. Its unique floral structure is adapted for pollination by specific species of bees and wasps.

Due to habitat loss, Hunga guillauminii is considered endangered, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
