Hackelia floribunda var. pinetorum

Hackelia floribunda var. pinetorum, commonly known as the mountain bluecurls, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of sky-blue, curling flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in open woodlands and rocky slopes, and it plays a role in supporting local pollinators.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
