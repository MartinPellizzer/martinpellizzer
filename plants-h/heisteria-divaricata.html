Heisteria divaricata

Heisteria divaricata, commonly known as the silverleaf heister, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Heisteria divaricata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to care for in moderate climates.
