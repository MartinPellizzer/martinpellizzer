Hieracium basischistum

Hieracium basischistum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is often considered a weed due to its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes.
