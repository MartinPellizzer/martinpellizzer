Hapalochilus neoebudicus

Hapalochilus neoebudicus, commonly known as the New Guinea orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the high-altitude regions of New Guinea.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring intricate petals and a specialized lip that aids in pollination. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on tree trunks or rocky outcrops, where it forms symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi. Its botanical significance lies in its morphological adaptations to its specific ecological niche, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
