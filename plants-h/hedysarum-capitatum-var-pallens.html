Hedysarum capitatum var. pallens

Hedysarum capitatum var. pallens, commonly known as the pale sweet clover, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its stems are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates.

Hedysarum capitatum var. pallens is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
