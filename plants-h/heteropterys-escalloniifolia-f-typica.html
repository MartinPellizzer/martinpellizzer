Heteropterys escalloniifolia f. typica

Heteropterys escalloniifolia f. typica, commonly known as the Escalloniifolia type, is a botanical variant of the genus Heteropterys.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in shaded, moist environments.

This fern-like plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
