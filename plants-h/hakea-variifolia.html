Hakea variifolia

Hakea variifolia, commonly known as the variable hakea, is a shrub native to south-eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical foliage and distinctive yellow flowers that appear in winter. The plant produces woody, cone-like fruits that contain seeds adapted for dispersal by fire. Hakea variifolia thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
