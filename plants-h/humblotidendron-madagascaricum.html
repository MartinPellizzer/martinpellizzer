Humblotidendron madagascaricum

Humblotidendron madagascaricum, commonly known as the Madagascar tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant produces yellow flowers that attract pollinators, and its bark is often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical climates and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils.
