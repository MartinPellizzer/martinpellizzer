Helenium dichotomum

Helenium dichotomum, commonly known as the two-branched rayflower, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, shady woodlands. The plant features showy, yellow-orange ray flowers with a dark brown central disk. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Helenium dichotomum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
