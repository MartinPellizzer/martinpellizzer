Hopea parvifolia

Hopea parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved hopea, is a species of flowering plant in the family Dipterocarpaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions such as Malaysia, Indonesia, and Thailand. The plant is a medium to large evergreen tree, typically reaching heights of up to 40 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and usually elliptical or ovate in shape, with a smooth margin.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in clusters, and the fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.
