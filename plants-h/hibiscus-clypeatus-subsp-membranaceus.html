Hibiscus clypeatus subsp. membranaceus

Hibiscus clypeatus subsp.

membranaceus, commonly known as the Mexican rose mallow, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America. It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically appearing in summer.

The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The flowers are known for their fragrance and are used in traditional medicine and as ornamental plants.
