Helicteres hirsuta var. viscida

Helicteres hirsuta var. viscida, commonly known as the sticky helicteres, is a variant of the helicteres hirsuta species native to tropical regions of the Americas.

This plant is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which often produce a sticky or viscid substance. The leaves are typically large, ovate, and have a rough texture, while the flowers are clustered in panicles and are usually yellow or greenish-yellow. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests or along riverbanks.

The viscid secretion may serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores or to trap small insects.
