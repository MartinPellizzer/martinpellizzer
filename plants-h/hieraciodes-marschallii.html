Hieraciodes marschallii

Hieraciodes marschallii, commonly known as the Marschall's hare's foot, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system. The plant features a mat-forming growth habit with small, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in arid conditions. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in clusters, and bloom during the warmer months.

Hieraciodes marschallii is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.
