Hieracium prenanthoides subsp. eurylophum

Hieracium prenanthoides subsp.

eurylophum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in dense clusters.

The subspecies eurylophum is distinguished by its elongated flower heads and is typically found in moist meadows and grasslands. It has a creeping growth habit with rhizomes that help it spread vegetatively.

The plant is adapted to temperate climates and is often seen in regions with well-drained soil.
