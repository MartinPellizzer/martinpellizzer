Hieracium melanopoides

Hieracium melanopoides, commonly known as the black-leaf hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a distinctive dark center.

Hieracium melanopoides is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
