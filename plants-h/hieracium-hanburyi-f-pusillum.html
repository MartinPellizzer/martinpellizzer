Hieracium hanburyi f. pusillum

Hieracium hanburyi f. pusillum, commonly known as the Hanbury's hawkweed, is a rare and striking plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its yellowish-green leaves and clusters of small, dandelion-like flowers that bloom in the summer months. Native to specific regions of Europe, this plant thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows or along stream banks. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, it is considered vulnerable, highlighting the need for its protection.
