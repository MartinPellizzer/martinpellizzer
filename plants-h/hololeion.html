Hololeion

Hololeion, commonly known as the "sunflower of the south," is a genus of flowering plants in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and includes several species, such as Hololeion capense and Hololeion macrocephalum. These plants are characterized by their bright yellow to orange flowers, which are typically composite, consisting of many small florets. They thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

Hololeion species are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes cultivated in gardens for their vibrant blooms.
