Hypoxis plicata

Hypoxis plicata, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its white, star-shaped flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has strap-like, narrow leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Hypoxis plicata is often grown as an ornamental plant for its unique floral display and ecological value.
