Hieracium rupestre subsp. farinulentum

Hieracium rupestre subsp.

farinulentum, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, such as slopes and gravelly soils.

The plant features upright stems covered in fine, glandular hairs, giving it a rough texture. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and it produces small, oval-shaped seeds.

It is often found in open, sunny environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
