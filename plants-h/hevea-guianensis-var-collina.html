Hevea guianensis var. collina

Hevea guianensis var. collina, commonly known as the Guianan rubber tree, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly found in regions of Guyana, Suriname, and French Guiana.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant is known for its latex production, which has historically been used in the rubber industry.

It thrives in humid, lowland areas with well-drained soils and requires a warm, tropical climate for optimal growth.
