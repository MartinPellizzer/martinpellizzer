Hemionitis rosulata

Hemionitis rosulata, commonly known as the rock fern, is a small, evergreen perennial plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and features finely divided, leathery leaves that are adapted to conserve water. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Hemionitis rosulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh environmental conditions.
