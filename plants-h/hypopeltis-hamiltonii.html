Hypopeltis hamiltonii

Hypopeltis hamiltonii, commonly known as the Hamilton's grasshopper orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Australia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sand dunes. The plant features a slender, erect stem with a single or few flowers that are usually pale green to yellowish in color. Its flowers are characterized by their distinctive spurred lip and are often visited by specific insect pollinators.

The orchid has a short flowering period, usually during spring or early summer.
