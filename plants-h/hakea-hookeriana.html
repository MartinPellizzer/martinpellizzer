Hakea hookeriana

Hakea hookeriana, commonly known as the Hooker's hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and distinctive, cylindrical flower spikes that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant produces woody, cone-like fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a fibrous covering. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Hakea hookeriana is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
