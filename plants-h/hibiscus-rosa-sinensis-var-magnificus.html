Hibiscus rosa-sinensis var. magnificus

Hibiscus rosa-sinensis var. magnificus, commonly known as the magnificent hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, typically in shades of red, pink, or white. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant blooms and ability to bloom year-round in suitable conditions.

The flowers are also used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
