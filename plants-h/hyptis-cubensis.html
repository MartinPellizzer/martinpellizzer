Hyptis cubensis

Hyptis cubensis, commonly known as Cuban hyptis, is a perennial herb native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

Hyptis cubensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
