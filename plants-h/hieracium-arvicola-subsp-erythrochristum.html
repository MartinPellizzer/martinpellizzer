Hieracium arvicola subsp. erythrochristum

Hieracium arvicola subsp.

erythrochristum, commonly known as the red-christed hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit.

The plant produces yellow flowers with a distinctive red-pink center, which becomes more prominent as the blooms mature. It thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, often growing in rocky or gravelly soils.
