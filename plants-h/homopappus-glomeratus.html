Homopappus glomeratus

Homopappus glomeratus, commonly known as the clustered blister grass, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and pappus structures. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in dense clusters, and it produces bristly, wind-dispersed seeds.

The plant is adapted to withstand drought conditions and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
