Herschelianthe tripartita

Herschelianthe tripartita, commonly known as the three-parted cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant has adapted to harsh desert conditions through its succulent leaves and deep root system. Its flowers bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in xeriscapes due to its drought tolerance.
