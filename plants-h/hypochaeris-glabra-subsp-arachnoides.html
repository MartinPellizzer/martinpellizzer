Hypochaeris glabra subsp. arachnoides

Hypochaeris glabra subsp.

arachnoides, commonly known as the hairy false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to North America. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

The plant features a basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces yellow composite flowers. Its subspecies arachnoides is distinguished by its dense, woolly covering on the leaves and stems.

The plant has a taproot and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
