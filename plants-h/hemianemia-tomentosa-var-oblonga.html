Hemianemia tomentosa var. oblonga

Hemianemia tomentosa var. oblonga, commonly known as the woolly hemianthus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, pale yellow flowers and dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. Its leaves are elliptical in shape and covered in a fine, white pubescence.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with high humidity.
