Heliophila dodii

Heliophila dodii, commonly known as the African sunflower, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features bright yellow flowers that resemble those of the sunflower, with a diameter of up to 10 centimeters. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowering period occurs during the warm months, and it thrives in full sun and well-drained soil.

Heliophila dodii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
