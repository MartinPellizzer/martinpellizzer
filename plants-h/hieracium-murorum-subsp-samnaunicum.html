Hieracium murorum subsp. samnaunicum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

samnaunicum, commonly known as the Samnaunic hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow flower heads that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences.

The plant is native to Central and Eastern Europe and thrives in disturbed soils, such as those found in meadows, roadsides, and wastelands. Its leaves are hairy and have a rough texture, while the stems are usually erect and unbranched.

The species is often found in open habitats and is adapted to temperate climates.
