Hieracium sonchoides subsp. eriopogon

Hieracium sonchoides subsp.

eriopogon, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features basal and alternate leaves that are hairy and often lobed, with a rough, woolly texture. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head, surrounded by several bracts.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dense, hairy foliage and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
