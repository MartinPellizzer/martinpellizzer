Hopea quisumbingiana

Hopea quisumbingiana, commonly known as the Mindanao hopea, is a species of flowering plant in the family Dipterocarpaceae.

It is native to the Philippines, particularly found in the lowland and montane forests of Mindanao. The plant is characterized by its large, simple leaves that are usually alternate and have a rough texture. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

Hopea quisumbingiana plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to forest biodiversity and soil stability.
