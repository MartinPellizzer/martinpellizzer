Helichrysum plinthocalyx

Helichrysum plinthocalyx, commonly known as the golden everlasting or Cape goldcup, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that remain vibrant even when dried. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is aromatic and covered in fine hairs, which contribute to its distinctive appearance.

Helichrysum plinthocalyx is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
