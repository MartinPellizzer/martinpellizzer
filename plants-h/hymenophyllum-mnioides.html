Hymenophyllum mnioides

Hymenophyllum mnioides, commonly known as the mossy fern, is a small, delicate fern belonging to the Hymenophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of moss-like structures. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its sporangia are clustered in small, cup-like structures called sorus, which are usually located on the underside of the leaves.

Hymenophyllum mnioides plays a role in maintaining soil moisture and providing habitat for microorganisms in its native ecosystems.
