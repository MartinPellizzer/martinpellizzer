Hemizonia luzulifolia var. fragarioides

Hemizonia luzulifolia var. fragarioides, commonly known as the fragrant wood-sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features a low-growing, sprawling habit with trifoliate leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs and emit a mild, pleasant scent when crushed.

It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded woodland areas.
