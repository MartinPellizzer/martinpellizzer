Hieracium georgieffianum

Hieracium georgieffianum, commonly known as the Georgian hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers. Native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, it thrives in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and it produces numerous small, fertile florets in a disc-like arrangement.

It is often found in areas with well-drained soil and is adapted to temperate climates.
