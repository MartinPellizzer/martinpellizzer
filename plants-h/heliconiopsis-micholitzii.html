Heliconiopsis micholitzii

Heliconiopsis micholitzii, commonly known as the red-hot poker or torch ginger, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features tall, upright stems that bear clusters of fiery red or orange inflorescences, which resemble torches. The plant belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers, and is characterized by its aromatic, strap-like leaves. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its vibrant blooms and unique appearance, it is popular in gardens and as a potted plant.
