Helianthus annuus var. indicus

Helianthus annuus var. indicus, commonly known as the Indian sunflower, is a variant of the sunflower species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flower heads and robust, hairy stems. This plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions. Its leaves are broad and lobed, with a rough texture, and it produces seeds that are often used for oil extraction.

The Indian sunflower is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its agricultural significance.
