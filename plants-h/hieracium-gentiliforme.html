Hieracium gentiliforme

Hieracium gentiliforme, commonly known as the gentle dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grassy areas and along roadsides. The plant features bright yellow ray florets surrounded by a composite disc of smaller flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branched.

Hieracium gentiliforme is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
