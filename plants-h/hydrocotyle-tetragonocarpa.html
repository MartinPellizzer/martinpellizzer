Hydrocotyle tetragonocarpa

Hydrocotyle tetragonocarpa, commonly known as the four-angled water pennywort, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater environments such as ponds, streams, and wetlands. The plant has small, rounded leaves that are often submerged, with some floating on the water surface. Its stems are usually four-angled, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It produces small, white flowers that bloom in the summer months.
