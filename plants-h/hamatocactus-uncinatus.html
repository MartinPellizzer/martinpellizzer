Hamatocactus uncinatus

Hamatocactus uncinatus, commonly known as the hooked cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus with a distinctive appearance, featuring green to blue-green stems that are often covered in a white, waxy coating. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It has spines that are curved and hook-like, which help it to retain moisture and deter herbivores.

This cactus is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
