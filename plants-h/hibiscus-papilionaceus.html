Hibiscus papilionaceus

Hibiscus papilionaceus, commonly known as the butterfly hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy, butterfly-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often have a velvety texture.

Hibiscus papilionaceus thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
