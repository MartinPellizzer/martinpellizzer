Hypericum rigidum subsp. meridionale

Hypericum rigidum subsp.

meridionale, commonly known as southern St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit, with woody stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, glandular hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Hypericaceae family, notable for its medicinal and ornamental value.
