Hieracium fasciatellum

Hieracium fasciatellum, commonly known as the spotted hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are basal and hairy, with a distinctive yellowish or white spotting on the upper surface.

The flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster, often blooming from June to August.
