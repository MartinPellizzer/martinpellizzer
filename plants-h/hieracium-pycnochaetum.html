Hieracium pycnochaetum

Hieracium pycnochaetum, commonly known as the dense-hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant features a hairy stem that is often reddish in color, with lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Hieracium pycnochaetum is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.
