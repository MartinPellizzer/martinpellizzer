Haplostichanthus longirostris

Haplostichanthus longirostris, commonly known as the long-snouted orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique elongated floral structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are often fragrant and display a range of colors, including white, yellow, and green.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
