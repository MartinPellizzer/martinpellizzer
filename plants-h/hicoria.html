Hicoria

Hicoria, commonly known as the wild chicory, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a basal rosette of blue-gray leaves and produces bright blue or purple flowers. Hicoria is often found in meadows and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its leaves and roots have been used historically for their medicinal and culinary properties.
