Hedysarum oxalifolium

Hedysarum oxalifolium, commonly known as sweet clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are heart-shaped and often tinged with red or purple veins. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and woodland edges.

Hedysarum oxalifolium is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it an important plant in soil enrichment.
