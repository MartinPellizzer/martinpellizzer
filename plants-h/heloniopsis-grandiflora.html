Heloniopsis grandiflora

Heloniopsis grandiflora, commonly known as the giant larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of showy blue to purple flowers. The plant features palmate leaves with three to five lobes, and its roots are fibrous and deep-reaching. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and rocky habitats.

Heloniopsis grandiflora is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance as a food source for certain pollinators.
