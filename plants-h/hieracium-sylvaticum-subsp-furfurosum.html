Hieracium sylvaticum subsp. furfurosum

Hieracium sylvaticum subsp.

furfurosum, commonly known as the forest hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in woodland areas and prefers moist, well-drained soils. It has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

This subspecies is often found in shaded environments and is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
