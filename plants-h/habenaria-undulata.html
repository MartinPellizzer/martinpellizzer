Habenaria undulata

Habenaria undulata, commonly known as the wavy-leaf orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, fragrant flowers with a distinctive wavy pattern on the petals. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and stream banks. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with a prominent spur that aids in pollination by insects.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
