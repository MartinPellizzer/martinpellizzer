Homalobus salidae

Homalobus salidae, commonly known as the Salida's rock cactus, is a unique species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers during the spring or early summer, adding a delicate touch to its otherwise rugged appearance. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and providing protection from herbivores.

Homalobus salidae is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a resilient choice for xeriscaping.
