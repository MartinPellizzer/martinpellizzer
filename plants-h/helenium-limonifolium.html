Helenium limonifolium

Helenium limonifolium, commonly known as lemon-leaf helenium, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its bright orange to yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant features narrow, strap-like leaves that are often tinged with yellow, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Helenium limonifolium is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
