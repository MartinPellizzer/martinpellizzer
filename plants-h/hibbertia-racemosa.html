Hibbertia racemosa

Hibbertia racemosa, commonly known as the yellow wattle, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces cylindrical flower clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and heathland environments.

Hibbertia racemosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
