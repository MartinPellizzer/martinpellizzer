Hedysarum tuberosum

Hedysarum tuberosum, commonly known as the mountain pea or yellow vetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial herb that produces yellow flowers and has a distinctive tuberous root system. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and is often found in rocky or dry habitats. It is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.

The plant's adaptability and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
