Hypertelis cerviana

Hypertelis cerviana, commonly known as the pink dwarf lily, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a member of the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are clustered in inflorescences.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
