Hibiscus paulae

Hibiscus paulae, commonly known as the Cape hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in full sun, making it a popular ornamental species. It is often found in coastal regions and can tolerate sandy soils.

Hibiscus paulae is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
