Hieracium bupleuroides subsp. aprutiorum

Hieracium bupleuroides subsp.

aprutiorum, commonly known as the Abruzzi hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

The plant features basal rosettes of lanceolate leaves and produces yellow flowers in terminal inflorescences. It has a taproot system and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves.

The species is part of the dandelion family (Asteraceae) and is known for its ornamental and ecological value.
