Heliotropium phylicoides

Heliotropium phylicoides, commonly known as the false heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant characterized by its opposite, simple leaves and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
