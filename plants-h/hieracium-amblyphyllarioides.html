Hieracium amblyphyllarioides

Hieracium amblyphyllarioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters.

It is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and seeds, making it a persistent weed in agricultural areas.
