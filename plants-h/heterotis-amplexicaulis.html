Heterotis amplexicaulis

Heterotis amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping leaf vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that often encircle the stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruits are fleshy berries that turn red when mature, containing several seeds.

Heterotis amplexicaulis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
