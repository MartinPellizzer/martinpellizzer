Hypericum calycinum

Hypericum calycinum, commonly known as the creeping St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typically from June to August. The plant has slender, prostrate stems covered in tiny, glandular dots that exude a sticky resin. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and have a slightly rough texture.

Hypericum calycinum is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
