Hieracium serenum

Hieracium serenum, commonly known as the evening daisy, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its yellow flower heads that open in the late afternoon and close at dawn, adapting to the light cycle. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and its flowers are composed of both ray and disk florets.

Hieracium serenum is often found in temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.
