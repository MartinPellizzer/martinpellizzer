Hieracium atratum subsp. piciniforme

Hieracium atratum subsp.

piciniforme, commonly known as the black hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its dark green, hairy leaves and yellowish to reddish flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, thriving in temperate climates. Its flowers are borne in clustered heads, and the leaves are often lobed or serrated.

It is a common species in the genus Hieracium, which includes many other hawkweed species.
