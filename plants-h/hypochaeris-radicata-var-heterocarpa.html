Hypochaeris radicata var. heterocarpa

Hypochaeris radicata var. heterocarpa, commonly known as the heterocarpic sow thistle, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in the summer months. It has a taproot system and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields.

Its seeds are enclosed in a pappus, which aids in wind dispersal.
