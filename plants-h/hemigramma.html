Hemigramma

Hemigramma, commonly known as the feather fern, is a genus of ferns belonging to the family Hemihydraceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble feathers, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or along stream banks. Hemigramma species are native to regions in South America, particularly Brazil and Peru.

These ferns are valued for their ornamental appeal and are often cultivated in gardens for their unique foliage.
