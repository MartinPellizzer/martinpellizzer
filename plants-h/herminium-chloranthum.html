Herminium chloranthum

Herminium chloranthum, commonly known as the green-flowered orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its unique coloration makes it stand out among other orchids, and it is often found in association with specific fungal partners.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its ecological niche and floral morphology.
