Hyacinthus cryptopodus

Hyacinthus cryptopodus, commonly known as the subterranean hyacinth, is a rare and elusive plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique underground growth habit, with its flowers emerging from beneath the soil surface. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in dry, rocky conditions, and it is often found in rocky crevices or among grasslands.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
