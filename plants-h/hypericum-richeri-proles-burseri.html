Hypericum richeri proles burseri

Hypericum richeri proles burseri, commonly known as the Burseri St. John's Wort, is a unique hybrid plant with characteristics derived from both Hypericum richeri and Hypericum burseri.

It is a shrub native to specific regions in Southeast Asia, particularly in parts of Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is notable for its vibrant yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a glossy texture, and it produces small, red-orange fruits that contain seeds.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its hybrid nature and potential medicinal properties.
