Hibiscus rivularis

Hibiscus rivularis, commonly known as the water hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range from pink to red, with a distinctive yellow center. The plant thrives in moist environments, often found near water sources such as streams and wetlands. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are often hairy.

Hibiscus rivularis is known for its rapid growth and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its vibrant blooms.
