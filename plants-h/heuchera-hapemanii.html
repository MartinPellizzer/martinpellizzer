Heuchera hapemanii

Heuchera hapemanii, commonly known as the Hapeman's alumroot, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features basal leaves that are typically heart-shaped with a velvety texture and a striking red or purple hue. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in early spring, which are clustered in upright inflorescences. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Heuchera hapemanii is valued for its ornamental foliage and its ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
