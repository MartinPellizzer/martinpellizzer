Hyptis crassifolia

Hyptis crassifolia, commonly known as roughleaf hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically violet or blue in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Hyptis crassifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
