Hermione italica var. aurea

Hermione italica var. aurea, commonly known as the golden Hermione, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage that turns golden in the autumn. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant foliage and delicate floral display.
