Hoffmannseggia falcaria var. stricta

Hoffmannseggia falcaria var. stricta, commonly known as the narrowleaf hoffmannseggia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a low-growing, sprawling habit with slender, ribbed stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months, which are followed by elongated, cylindrical seed pods. It is adapted to thrive in sandy or rocky soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
