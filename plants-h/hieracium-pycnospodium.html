Hieracium pycnospodium

Hieracium pycnospodium, commonly known as the dense-headed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist grasslands, meadows, and along riverbanks, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is known for its dense, spiky seed heads that remain visible into the autumn, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
