Hieracium juranum subsp. hedypnoides

Hieracium juranum subsp.

hedypnoides, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in alpine meadows and grasslands.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in many areas.
