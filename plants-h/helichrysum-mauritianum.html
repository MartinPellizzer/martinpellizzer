Helichrysum mauritianum

Helichrysum mauritianum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that remain intact even after drying. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically clustered in a flat, rounded head. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance contribute to its use in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
