Hibiscus parkinsonii

Hibiscus parkinsonii, commonly known as the Parkinson's hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. Hibiscus parkinsonii thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in coastal areas and forest understories.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
