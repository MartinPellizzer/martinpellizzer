Hieracium prenanthoides subsp. violascens

Hieracium prenanthoides subsp.

violascens, commonly known as the violet-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges, preferring moist, well-drained soils.

The plant features bright yellow flowers with a distinctive, elongated shape, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly hairy texture. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads.

This subspecies is distinguished by its pale violet to bluish-green foliage, which sets it apart from other hawkweed species.
