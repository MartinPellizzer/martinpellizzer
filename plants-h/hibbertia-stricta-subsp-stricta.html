Hibbertia stricta subsp. stricta

Hibbertia stricta subsp.

stricta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hibbertia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia. It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments. Hibbertia stricta subsp.

stricta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive foliage and seasonal blooms.
