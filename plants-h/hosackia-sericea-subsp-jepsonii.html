Hosackia sericea subsp. jepsonii

Hosackia sericea subsp.

jepsonii, commonly known as the Jepson's silky milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States. It is characterized by its finely hairy stems and leaves, which are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
