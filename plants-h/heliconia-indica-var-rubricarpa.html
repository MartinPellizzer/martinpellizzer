Heliconia indica var. rubricarpa

Heliconia indica var. rubricarpa, commonly known as the red-fruited heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, colorful bracts that resemble petals, often in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces elongated, segmented fruits that contain numerous small, edible seeds. Its leaves are broad and pointed, with a waxy surface that helps retain moisture in humid environments.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a component in floral arrangements.
