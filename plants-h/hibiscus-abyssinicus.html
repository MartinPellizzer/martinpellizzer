Hibiscus abyssinicus

Hibiscus abyssinicus, commonly known as the Abyssinian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 20 centimeters in diameter. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces fruits that are capsule-like in structure. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Hibiscus abyssinicus is valued both for its ornamental beauty and for its traditional uses in local communities.
