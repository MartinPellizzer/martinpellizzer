Hyparrhenia rufa var. fulvicoma

Hyparrhenia rufa var. fulvicoma, commonly known as the yellow panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a yellowish-green hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Its fibrous root system makes it drought-tolerant and suitable for erosion control in certain environments.
