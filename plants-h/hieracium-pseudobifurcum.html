Hieracium pseudobifurcum

Hieracium pseudobifurcum, commonly known as the false two-lobed hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are alternate, rough, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

Hieracium pseudobifurcum is known for its aggressive growth and can become invasive in certain environments.
