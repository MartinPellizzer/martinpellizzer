Hieracium pallescens subsp. villosipes

Hieracium pallescens subsp.

villosipes, commonly known as the silky hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators like bees and flies.
