Hieracium scoparium

Hieracium scoparium, commonly known as the scented hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces yellow flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn. The plant has serrated, hairy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette at the base. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Hieracium scoparium is often noted for its subtle, sweet fragrance, which becomes more pronounced as the flowers mature.
