Horkelia californica var. sericea

Horkelia californica var. sericea, commonly known as the silverleaf horkelia, is a perennial herb native to California.

It features slender, upright stems covered in silvery, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

This variant is notable for its frost tolerance and ability to persist in nutrient-poor soils.
