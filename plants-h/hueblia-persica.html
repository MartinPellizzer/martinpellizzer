Hueblia persica

Hueblia persica, commonly known as the Persian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained soil in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Hueblia persica is a perennial herb that blooms in late spring to early summer.
