Hordeum vulgare var. africanum

Hordeum vulgare var. africanum, commonly known as African barley, is a variant of barley native to Africa.

It is a grass species characterized by its slender stalks and small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture, and it produces small, hard grains that are often used for forage or traditional purposes.

This variety is valued for its resilience and has been studied for potential agricultural applications in drought-prone areas.
