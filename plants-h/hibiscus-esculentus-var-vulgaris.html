Hibiscus esculentus var. vulgaris

Hibiscus esculentus var. vulgaris, commonly known as okra, is a tropical flowering plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to deep red. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous edible seeds enclosed in a mucilaginous pulp. The mucilage, which gives the fruit its slimy texture, is rich in soluble fiber and is often used in cooking.

Okra is cultivated for both its nutritional value and its use in traditional medicine.
