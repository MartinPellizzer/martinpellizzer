Hepetis atrorubens var. lamarcheana

Hepetis atrorubens var. lamarcheana, commonly known as the redroot tickseed, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are often tinged with purple or red, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

This variety is noted for its resilience and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
