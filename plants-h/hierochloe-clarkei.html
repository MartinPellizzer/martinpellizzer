Hierochloe clarkei

Hierochloe clarkei, commonly known as the Clarke's hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. Hierochloe clarkei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in its natural environment and supports local biodiversity.
