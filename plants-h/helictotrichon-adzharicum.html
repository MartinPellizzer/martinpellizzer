Helictotrichon adzharicum

Helictotrichon adzharicum, commonly known as the Caucasian bluegrass, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its flowers are wind-pollinated, and it produces small, dry seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Helictotrichon adzharicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native environment.
