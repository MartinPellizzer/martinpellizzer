Hieracium sericophyllum subsp. chamaepannosum

Hieracium sericophyllum subsp.

chamaepannosum, commonly known as the creeping hawkweed, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and is typically found in moist, calcareous soils.

The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers arranged in clustered heads, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are usually covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its common name.

This subspecies is often found in alpine and subalpine regions across Europe and parts of Asia.
