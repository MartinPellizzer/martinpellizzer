Huperzia campiana

Huperzia campiana, commonly known as the Florida huperzia, is a species of moss-like plant belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its simple, unbranched stems and small, ovate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of rhizoids. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and parts of Georgia.

Huperzia campiana plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for microorganisms in its native ecosystems.
