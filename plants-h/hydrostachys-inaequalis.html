Hydrostachys inaequalis

Hydrostachys inaequalis, commonly known as the narrowleaf waterwheel, is a perennial aquatic plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and borne in dense clusters at the water's surface.

Hydrostachys inaequalis plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.
