Helichrysum polyphyllum

Helichrysum polyphyllum, commonly known as the many-leaved everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic, fragrant leaves. Helichrysum polyphyllum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its ability to thrive in dry conditions makes it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping.
