Hemionitis × malacitensis

Hemionitis × malacitensis, commonly known as the Malacitensis fern, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Hemionitis arifolia and Hemionitis arifolia var. malacitensis.

It is a terrestrial fern characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically light green in color. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. It has a short rhizome and produces spores on the underside of its leaves.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its graceful appearance.
