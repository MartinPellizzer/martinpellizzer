Hieracium pellaeocephalum

Hieracium pellaeocephalum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it thrives in temperate climates. Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer, and the plant produces achenes as its seeds.

Hieracium pellaeocephalum is known for its hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its distinctive appearance.
