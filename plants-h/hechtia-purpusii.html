Hechtia purpusii

Hechtia purpusii, commonly known as the Mexican hechtia, is a striking bromeliad native to the cloud forests of Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from green to red or purple. The plant produces a tall flower spike bearing small, white or pale pink flowers, which bloom in the summer. Hechtia purpusii thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique appearance and ability to thrive in specific ecological conditions make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and enthusiasts.
