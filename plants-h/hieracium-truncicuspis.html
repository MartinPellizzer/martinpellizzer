Hieracium truncicuspis

Hieracium truncicuspis, commonly known as the rough-edged hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and meadows, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are rough and hairy, with a distinctive truncate or notched base.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
