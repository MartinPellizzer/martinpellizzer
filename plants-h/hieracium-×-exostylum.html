Hieracium × exostylum

Hieracium × exostylum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically has a rosette of basal leaves that are deeply lobed or toothed. It is native to Europe and has been introduced to other parts of the world, often becoming invasive in certain regions.

Hieracium × exostylum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and light conditions.
