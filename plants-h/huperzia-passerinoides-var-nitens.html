Huperzia passerinoides var. nitens

Huperzia passerinoides var. nitens, commonly known as the shining huperzia, is a fern-like plant belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its spore-producing structures called sporangia, which are typically clustered on the underside of its leaves. The plant is native to regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India and China, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and possess a distinct midrib, contributing to its unique appearance.

This variety is noted for its slow growth and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in shaded gardens.
