Hieracium praecox subsp. ovalifoliiforme

Hieracium praecox subsp.

ovalifoliiforme, commonly known as the oval-leaved hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering heads that produce yellow ray flowers. The leaves are ovate to lanceolate in shape, often with a hairy surface, and the plant typically grows in meadows and grassy areas.

It is a low-growing plant, reaching heights of up to 30 cm, and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
