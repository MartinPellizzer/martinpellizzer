Hibiscus cucurbitaceus var. cuneifolius

Hibiscus cucurbitaceus var. cuneifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hibiscus, is a unique variant of the hibiscus family native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom during the warm seasons. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage and colorful blooms.
