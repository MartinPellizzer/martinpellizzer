Hieracium piranshahricum

Hieracium piranshahricum, commonly known as the Persian hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers and hairy, lobed leaves. Native to regions of Central Asia, including parts of Iran and Afghanistan, it thrives in dry, rocky habitats. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and has a taproot system.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
