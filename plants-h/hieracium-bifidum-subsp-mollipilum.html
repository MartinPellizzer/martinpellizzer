Hieracium bifidum subsp. mollipilum

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

mollipilum, commonly known as the soft-hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

The plant features basal rosettes of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in late spring to early summer. Its subspecies characteristics include dense, soft hairs on the leaves and stems, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is part of the Asteraceae family and is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
