Hernandia sonora var. nymphaeifolia

Hernandia sonora var. nymphaeifolia, commonly known as the water lily fern, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating in aquatic environments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in the early morning. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is adapted to wetland habitats and thrives in shallow, still water.
