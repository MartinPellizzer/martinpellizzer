Haemodorum parviflorum

Haemodorum parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered bloodroot, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and wetlands. The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces small, white or greenish flowers with a distinctive red or orange calyx. Its flowers are often visited by insects, contributing to its role in pollination.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
