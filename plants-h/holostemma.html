Holostemma

Holostemma, commonly known as the "wild nightshade," is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plants typically have erect or trailing stems, simple leaves, and produce small, often hairy flowers. The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the species are often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Some species are used in traditional medicine, though they can be toxic.
