Helianthus × ambiguus

Helianthus × ambiguus, commonly known as the yellow wood dandelion, is a hybrid species within the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which consist of a central disk surrounded by ray florets. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is native to the eastern United States. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy as well.

This species is notable for its adaptability and can be found in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and wetlands.
