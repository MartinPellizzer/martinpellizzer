Hieracium obversiforme

Hieracium obversiforme, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in meadows and grassy areas.

Hieracium obversiforme is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to moderate grazing.
