Hibiscus tiliaceus var. similis

Hibiscus tiliaceus var. similis, commonly known as the coastal hibiscus, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant thrives in coastal areas and is often found in mangrove swamps or along sandy shores. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Hibiscus tiliaceus var. similis is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
