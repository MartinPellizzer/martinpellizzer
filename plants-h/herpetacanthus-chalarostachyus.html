Herpetacanthus chalarostachyus

Herpetacanthus chalarostachyus, commonly known as the golden spikeplant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, yellow-orange inflorescences that resemble spikes. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

The flowers are tubular and attract various pollinators, including birds and insects.
