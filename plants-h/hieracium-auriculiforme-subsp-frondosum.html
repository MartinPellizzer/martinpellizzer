Hieracium auriculiforme subsp. frondosum

Hieracium auriculiforme subsp.

frondosum, commonly known as the fronded hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of broadly ovate, hairy leaves and flowering heads that produce yellow ray florets.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and riverbanks. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
