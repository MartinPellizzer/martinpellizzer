Helianthemum ocymoides var. algarvense

Helianthemum ocymoides var. algarvense, commonly known as the Algarve rockrose, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the southern Iberian Peninsula.

It features small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has compact, fleshy leaves that are adapted to withstand arid conditions, making it drought-tolerant. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping in Mediterranean climates.
