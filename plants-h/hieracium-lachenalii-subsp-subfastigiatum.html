Hieracium lachenalii subsp. subfastigiatum

Hieracium lachenalii subsp.

subfastigiatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and often have a hairy surface, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is generally short-lived and has a relatively limited distribution.
