Hepetis integrifolia

Hepetis integrifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and marshes. The plant features upright stems that are often branched and covered with rough, hairy texture. Its leaves are lance-shaped, smooth-edged, and arranged alternately along the stem.

The showy purple to blue flowers are clustered in dense, spherical heads, making it a notable species in wetland ecosystems.
