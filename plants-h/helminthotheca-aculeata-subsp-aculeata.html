Helminthotheca aculeata subsp. aculeata

Helminthotheca aculeata subsp.

aculeata, commonly known as the hairy hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its spiny, glandular leaves and yellow composite flowers that consist of ray florets and disk florets.

The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and rocky areas. It grows up to 60 cm tall, with a hairy stem and serrated leaves.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
