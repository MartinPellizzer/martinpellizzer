Hakea grammatophylla

Hakea grammatophylla, commonly known as the coastal hakea, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces cylindrical, yellowish-green flower spikes that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited to coastal and sandy environments.

Its unique morphology and adaptability contribute to its ecological significance in native Australian landscapes.
