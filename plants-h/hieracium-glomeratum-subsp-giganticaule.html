Hieracium glomeratum subsp. giganticaule

Hieracium glomeratum subsp.

giganticaule, commonly known as the giant bull's-horn plant, is a member of the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has a robust, upright stem and hairy leaves that are lance-shaped.

This subspecies is notable for its impressive size, often reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters.
