Homalanthus novoguineensis

Homalanthus novoguineensis, commonly known as the New Guinea flame tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Pacific.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fiery orange-red bracts that resemble flowers. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are often hidden among the large, showy bracts. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
