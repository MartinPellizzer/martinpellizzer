Habranthus pratensis

Habranthus pratensis, commonly known as the meadow bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
