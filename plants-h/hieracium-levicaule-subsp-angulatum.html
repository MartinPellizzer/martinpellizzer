Hieracium levicaule subsp. angulatum

Hieracium levicaule subsp.

angulatum, commonly known as the angular hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, meadows, and grasslands.

The plant has erect, branching stems that are usually covered with fine hairs. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have serrated edges, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster, typical of the daisy family (Asteraceae).
