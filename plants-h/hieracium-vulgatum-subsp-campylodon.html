Hieracium vulgatum subsp. campylodon

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

campylodon, commonly known as the bent-leaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces yellow flowers arranged in composite flower heads.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and may be slightly curled or bent at the tips, giving the species its common name. It is native to Europe and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

The plant is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain habitats.
