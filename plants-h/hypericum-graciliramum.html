Hypericum graciliramum

Hypericum graciliramum, commonly known as slender-stemmed St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It features wiry, branched stems that are often covered with fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with four petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, with a slightly hairy surface.

Hypericum graciliramum is adapted to rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.
