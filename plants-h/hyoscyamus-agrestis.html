Hyoscyamus agrestis

Hyoscyamus agrestis, commonly known as the field henbane, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is an annual herb that grows up to 1 meter tall, with erect, branched stems and alternate leaves that are usually egg-shaped. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by spherical, spiny fruits containing several seeds.

It is known for its toxic properties, containing tropane alkaloids such as hyoscyamine and scopolamine, which can be harmful to humans and animals.
