Hieracium Stenolonchum

Hieracium stenolonchum is a lesser-known member of the dandelion family known for its striking yellow flowers and unique leaf structure. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It is often mistaken for common dandelions but can be distinguished by its narrower leaves and more compact growth habit. Despite its beauty, it is not widely cultivated and remains a subject of interest for botanists and nature enthusiasts. Its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil makes it an important plant in its native ecosystems.
