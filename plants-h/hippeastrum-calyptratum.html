Hippeastrum calyptratum

Hippeastrum calyptratum, commonly known as the Ecuadorian amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It produces large, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often with a distinctive red or orange spathe. The plant features strap-like, evergreen leaves that are broad and lance-shaped. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Hippeastrum calyptratum is typically grown as a houseplant or in outdoor gardens in tropical and subtropical regions.
