Hieracium vulgatum var. calvescens

Hieracium vulgatum var. calvescens, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm. The plant features bright yellow composite flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, with ray florets surrounding disk florets. Its leaves are broadly lance-shaped, hairy, and often have a reddish or purple tinge.

The species is characterized by its extensive root system and ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes.
