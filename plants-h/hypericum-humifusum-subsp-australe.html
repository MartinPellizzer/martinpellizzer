Hypericum humifusum subsp. australe

Hypericum humifusum subsp.

australe, commonly known as the Australian St. John's Wort, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to southern Australia. It features woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish to orange flowers with five petals, which bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
