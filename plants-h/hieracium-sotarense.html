Hieracium sotarense

Hieracium sotarense, commonly known as the Sardinian hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sardinia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny grasslands and rocky areas, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium sotarense is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
