Hieracium brachiatum subsp. zittaviense

Hieracium brachiatum subsp.

zittaviense, commonly known as the Zittau hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellowish to reddish flower heads.

The plant typically has basal and cauline leaves that are lanceolate to ovate in shape. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and protected species in several European countries.
