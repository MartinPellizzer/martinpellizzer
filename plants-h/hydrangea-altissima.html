Hydrangea altissima

Hydrangea altissima, commonly known as the panicle hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its large, cone-shaped flower clusters that range in color from white to pink or blue, depending on soil pH. The plant produces showy inflorescences that can grow up to 12 inches in diameter, adding visual interest to gardens. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental value.

Hydrangea altissima is relatively hardy and thrives in partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for landscaping.
