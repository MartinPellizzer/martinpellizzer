Hieracium erythrocarpum subsp. insolitum

Hieracium erythrocarpum subsp.

insolitum, commonly known as the red-fruited hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowering heads and distinctive, red, fleshy fruits.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has hairy stems and basal leaves that are lanceolate in shape.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
