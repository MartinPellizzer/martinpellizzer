Hieracium sabaudum subsp. rigens

Hieracium sabaudum subsp.

rigens, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

The plant features basal rosettes of dark green, hairy leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in summer. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and the presence of glandular hairs on its stems and leaves.

This subspecies is often found in rocky or grassy slopes at high altitudes.
