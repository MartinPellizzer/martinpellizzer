Hieracium mexicanum subsp. niveopappum

Hieracium mexicanum subsp.

niveopappum, commonly known as Mexican hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Mexico. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has alternate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
