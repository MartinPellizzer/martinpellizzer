Hibiscus macrophyllus

Hibiscus macrophyllus, commonly known as the giant hibiscus, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its large, broad leaves and showy flowers.

Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. The plant produces vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its foliage is notably thick and glossy, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Hibiscus macrophyllus is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and can also be used in traditional medicine in some regions.
