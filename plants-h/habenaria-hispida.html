Habenaria hispida

Habenaria hispida, commonly known as the hairy orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces striking, often fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its complex floral structure and specialized pollination mechanisms.
