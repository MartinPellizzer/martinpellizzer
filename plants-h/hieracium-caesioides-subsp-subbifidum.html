Hieracium caesioides subsp. subbifidum

Hieracium caesioides subsp.

subbifidum, commonly known as the blue hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright blue to purple composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often hairy.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
