Haumaniastrum lilacinum

Haumaniastrum lilacinum, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features slender, erect stems covered in fine, silvery-white hairs, giving it a misty appearance. Its showy blue to purple flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
