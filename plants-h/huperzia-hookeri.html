Huperzia hookeri

Huperzia hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's spikemoss, is a species of fern ally native to tropical regions of Africa and southern Asia.

It belongs to the family Huperziaceae and is characterized by its low-growing, evergreen habit with spirally arranged leaves. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are typically clustered in small, cone-like formations at the tips of the stems. Huperzia hookeri thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest undergrowth.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
