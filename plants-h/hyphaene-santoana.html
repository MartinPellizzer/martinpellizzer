Hyphaene santoana

Hyphaene santoana, commonly known as the Santoana palm, is a species of palm native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in branched inflorescences, followed by elongated, fibrous fruits. Its roots form a dense network of rhizomes, which helps stabilize the soil in its native habitat.

The plant is adapted to grow in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.
