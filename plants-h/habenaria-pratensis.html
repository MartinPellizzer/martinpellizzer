Habenaria pratensis

Habenaria pratensis, commonly known as the marsh helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to wet meadows and marshy areas in Europe.

It produces tall, erect stems with lance-shaped leaves and bears showy, greenish-white to pale yellow flowers with a distinctive lip. The plant is characterized by its intricate floral structure, which includes a spurred lip and a column that houses the reproductive organs. It is a perennial herb that thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in association with other wetland flora.

The species is noted for its ability to tolerate fluctuating water levels and is an important component of diverse wetland ecosystems.
