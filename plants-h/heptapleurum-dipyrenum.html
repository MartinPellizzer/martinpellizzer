Heptapleurum dipyrenum

Heptapleurum dipyrenum, commonly known as the mountain holly, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests. The plant features opposite, elliptical leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in inflorescences, while the fruit is a two-lobed, fleshy drupe.

The plant has a woody stem with a rough, fissured bark, and it is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
