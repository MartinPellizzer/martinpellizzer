Hydrocotyle verticillata var. bonariensis

Hydrocotyle verticillata var. bonariensis, commonly known as the Brazilian water shield, is a floating aquatic plant native to South America.

It features rounded, waxy leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern on the water surface. The plant produces small, white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and lakes.

Its roots help stabilize sediment, making it an important species in aquatic ecosystems.
