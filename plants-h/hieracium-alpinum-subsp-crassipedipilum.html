Hieracium alpinum subsp. crassipedipilum

Hieracium alpinum subsp.

crassipedipilum, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often found in mountainous environments where other plants struggle to grow.
