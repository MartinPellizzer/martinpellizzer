Hyacinthus boucheanus

Hyacinthus boucheanus, commonly known as the Bouchean hyacinth, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer to early autumn, producing vibrant blue, violet, or white flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a rosette at the base of the plant.

Hyacinthus boucheanus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to Mediterranean climates.
