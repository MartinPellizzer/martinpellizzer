Hygrophila limnophiloides

Hygrophila limnophiloides, commonly known as the water hygrophila, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a semi-aquatic species that typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds and slow-moving streams. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, opposite leaves that are often submerged or floating. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually appearing in yellow or green hues, and it produces elongated seed pods.

Hygrophila limnophiloides is often used in aquariums due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in aquatic environments.
