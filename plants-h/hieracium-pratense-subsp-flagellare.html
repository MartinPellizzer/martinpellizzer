Hieracium pratense subsp. flagellare

Hieracium pratense subsp.

flagellare, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough texture and often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are yellow, with a central disc surrounded by pale yellow to white ray florets, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
