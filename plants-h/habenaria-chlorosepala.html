Habenaria chlorosepala

Habenaria chlorosepala, commonly known as the pale-sepaled orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant features a slender rhizome and produces erect, branched inflorescences. Its flowers are small, white to pale green, with three sepals and three petals, and a prominent, spurred labellum.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
