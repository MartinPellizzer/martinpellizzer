Habenaria paulistana

Habenaria paulistana, commonly known as the Paulistana orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and linear leaves. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers with a distinctive three-lobed lip. It typically blooms in the spring and prefers moist, well-drained soils in shaded forest environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
