Hieracium jurassicum subsp. tephrophorum

Hieracium jurassicum subsp.

tephrophorum, commonly known as the Jurassic hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to specific regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in calcareous or slightly acidic soils, often in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its leaves are lanceolate and hairy, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is noted for its historical association with Jurassic geological formations, hence its common name.
