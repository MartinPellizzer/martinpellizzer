Hemianemia millefolia

Hemianemia millefolia, commonly known as common yarrow, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features a creeping rhizome and erect, branched stems that are often hairy. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters called cymes. Its leaves are finely divided into thread-like segments, giving it a feathery appearance.

Hemianemia millefolia is known for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
