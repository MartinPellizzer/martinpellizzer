Hypoxis crispa

Hypoxis crispa, commonly known as the white star ginger, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a short, rhizomatous stem with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are surrounded by a ring of bracts, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Hypoxis crispa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and blooms.
