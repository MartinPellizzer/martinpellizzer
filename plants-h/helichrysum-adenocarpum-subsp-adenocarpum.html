Helichrysum adenocarpum subsp. adenocarpum

Helichrysum adenocarpum subsp.

adenocarpum, commonly known as the yellow everlasting, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense clusters of bright yellow, papery flowers that remain open for an extended period.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
