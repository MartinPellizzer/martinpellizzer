Hyptis salicina

Hyptis salicina, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Hyptis salicina is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
