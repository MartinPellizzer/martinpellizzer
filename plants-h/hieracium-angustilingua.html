Hieracium angustilingua

Hieracium angustilingua, commonly known as narrow-leaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and woodland edges. It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Hieracium angustilingua is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting local pollinators.
