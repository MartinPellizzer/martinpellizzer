Hieracium ventricosum

Hieracium ventricosum, commonly known as the swollen hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features a robust, upright stem with alternate, broadly lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are yellow, with ray florets surrounding a central disk, and it produces small, oval-shaped achenes as fruits.

Hieracium ventricosum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
