Hydnophytum magnifolium

Hydnophytum magnifolium, commonly known as the giant mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of trees in tropical rainforests.

It has a unique root system that allows it to absorb water and nutrients from its host, while also performing photosynthesis with its broad, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are often clustered together, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. Its ecological role is significant, as it provides food for various birds and insects.

Due to habitat loss, Hydnophytum magnifolium is considered vulnerable and is often found in protected areas.
