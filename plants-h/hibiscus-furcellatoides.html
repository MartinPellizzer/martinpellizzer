Hibiscus furcellatoides

Hibiscus furcellatoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf hibiscus, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive narrow, elongated base. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and wetland restoration projects.
