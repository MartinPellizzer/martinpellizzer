Hemerocallis yezoensis

Hemerocallis yezoensis, commonly known as the Japanese daylily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

Hemerocallis yezoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
