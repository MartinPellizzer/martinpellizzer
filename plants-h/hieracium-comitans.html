Hieracium comitans

Hieracium comitans, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. It has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy, with a caudex that becomes woody over time.

Hieracium comitans is often found in temperate regions of Europe and has been introduced to other parts of the world.
