Hypericum pruinatum

Hypericum pruinatum, commonly known as the velvetleaf St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive velvety appearance. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

Hypericum pruinatum is a perennial shrub that can grow up to 2 meters tall, with a sprawling growth habit.
