Hypolytrum ceylanicum

Hypolytrum ceylanicum, commonly known as the Ceylon grass or Ceylon spikegrass, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, three-lobed flowers. It is often found in moist, tropical environments and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
