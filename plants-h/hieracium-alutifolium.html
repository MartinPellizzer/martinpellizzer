Hieracium alutifolium

Hieracium alutifolium, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. It has slender, upright stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Its flowers are yellow and appear in late spring to early summer, contributing to its ornamental appeal in gardens.
