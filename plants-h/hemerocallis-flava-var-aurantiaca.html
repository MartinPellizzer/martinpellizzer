Hemerocallis flava var. aurantiaca

Hemerocallis flava var. aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden daylily, is a variant of the daylily species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is a perennial herb with sword-shaped, grass-like leaves that form a dense clump. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant color and long blooming period.
