Hieracium saxifragum subsp. rhenobavaricum

Hieracium saxifragum subsp.

rhenobavaricum, commonly known as the Rhenish hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in rocky or limestone-rich soils, often in mountainous or alpine regions.

The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in late summer. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its range.
