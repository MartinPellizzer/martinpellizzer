Hieracium sphagnicola

Hieracium sphagnicola, commonly known as the bog hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern Europe and parts of Asia.

It thrives in moist, acidic environments such as bogs and heathlands, where it forms dense mats on the ground. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a single head. Its root system is fibrous, allowing it to anchor firmly in wet, peaty soils.

Hieracium sphagnicola plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems and is often found in association with other moisture-loving plants.
