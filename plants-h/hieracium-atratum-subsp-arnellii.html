Hieracium atratum subsp. arnellii

Hieracium atratum subsp.

arnellii, commonly known as the black hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its dark green, hairy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It has a creeping rootstock and produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
