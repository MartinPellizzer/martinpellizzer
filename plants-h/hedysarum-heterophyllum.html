Hedysarum heterophyllum

Hedysarum heterophyllum, commonly known as sweet clover, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a robust root system that helps in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in forage and erosion control.

Its seeds are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
