Hieracium irriguum subsp. lepidiceps

Hieracium irriguum subsp.

lepidiceps, commonly known as the smooth-headed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and riverbanks.

The plant features upright stems covered with fine, silky hairs, and its basal leaves are broadly lance-shaped with a hairy surface. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and are borne on long, slender peduncles.

It is known for its ability to thrive in wet conditions and is often found in disturbed or semi-natural environments.
