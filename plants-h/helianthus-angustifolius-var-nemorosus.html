Helianthus angustifolius var. nemorosus

Helianthus angustifolius var. nemorosus, commonly known as the woodland sunflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces yellow flowers with a prominent central disk, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is adapted to partial shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and native plant gardens.
