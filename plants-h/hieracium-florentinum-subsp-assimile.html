Hieracium florentinum subsp. assimile

Hieracium florentinum subsp.

assimile, commonly known as the Italian hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
