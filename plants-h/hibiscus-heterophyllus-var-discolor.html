Hibiscus heterophyllus var. discolor

Hibiscus heterophyllus var. discolor, commonly known as the velvetleaf hibiscus, is a tropical plant characterized by its diverse leaf morphology.

The leaves are typically heart-shaped or ovate, with a velvety texture and a deep green to reddish-brown coloration. It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, often with a prominent yellow throat. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid climates.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking foliage and blooms.
