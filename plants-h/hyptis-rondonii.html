Hyptis rondonii

Hyptis rondonii, commonly known as Rondon's hyptis, is a species of flowering plant in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It typically grows as a shrub or herb, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both ecological studies and horticultural applications.
