Helictotrichon filifolium subsp. velutinum

Helictotrichon filifolium subsp.

velutinum, commonly known as the silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America. It is characterized by its fine, silky-textured leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. Its leaves are often covered in a dense layer of hairs, giving it a soft, velvety appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to stabilize soil in ecological restoration projects.
