Hieracium nigrescens subsp. sundbergii

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

sundbergii, commonly known as the black-centred hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm.

The plant features basal leaves that are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while its flowering stems bear small, yellowish to orange composite flowers. The subspecies is distinguished by its dark purple to black central disk in the flower head, which contrasts with the yellow petals.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit.
