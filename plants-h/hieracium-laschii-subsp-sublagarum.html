Hieracium laschii subsp. sublagarum

Hieracium laschii subsp.

sublagarum, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette growth habit and yellow flowering heads.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are usually covered in fine hairs, and the flowers are arranged in a single, showy inflorescence.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to harsh climatic conditions and its role in alpine ecosystems.
