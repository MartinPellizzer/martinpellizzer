Haemanthus amarylloides subsp. amarylloides

Haemanthus amarylloides subsp.

amarylloides, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking bulbous plant native to southern Africa. It features tall, upright stems with bright red bracts that resemble poker chips, giving it its common name.

The plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. It is characterized by its fleshy, underground bulbs and is adapted to grow in well-drained, sandy soils.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
