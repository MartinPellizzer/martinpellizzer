Hessea brachyscypha

Hessea brachyscypha, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hessea, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in winter. The plant has fibrous roots and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils, making it resilient to drought conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Hessea brachyscypha is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in coastal gardens.
