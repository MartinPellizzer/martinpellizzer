Hydnocarpus tomentosus

Hydnocarpus tomentosus, commonly known as the hairy tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy bark and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces yellowish flowers followed by woody, fleshy fruits that contain several seeds. Its fruit is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of tannins.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood.
