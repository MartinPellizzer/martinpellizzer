Hakea epiglottis subsp. epiglottis

Hakea epiglottis subsp.

epiglottis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hakea, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and produces bright red or orange flowers in winter.

The plant has a woody stem and is adapted to arid environments, showing drought tolerance. It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

Its unique floral structure attracts nectar-feeding birds and insects, contributing to its ecological significance.
