Hyptis longipes

Hyptis longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed hyptis, is a perennial shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and medicinal plants. The plant is characterized by its hairy, opposite leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to violet. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Hyptis longipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
