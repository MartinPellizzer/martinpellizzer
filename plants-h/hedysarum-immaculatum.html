Hedysarum immaculatum

Hedysarum immaculatum, commonly known as the white peavine, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in early summer. Hedysarum immaculatum is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
