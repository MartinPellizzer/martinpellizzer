Hypoxis arillacea

Hypoxis arillacea, commonly known as the African star of Bethlehem, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a short, fibrous rootstock and produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves at the base. The plant is characterized by its bright yellow, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Hypoxis arillacea is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
