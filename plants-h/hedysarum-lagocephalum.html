Hedysarum lagocephalum

Hedysarum lagocephalum, commonly known as the blue flax or mountain flax, is a perennial leguminous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces elongated pods that contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Hedysarum lagocephalum is valued for its ornamental beauty and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
