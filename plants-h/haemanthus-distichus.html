Haemanthus distichus

Haemanthus distichus, commonly known as the two-rowed haemanthus, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features two rows of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and slightly curved. The plant produces clusters of bright red flowers during the winter months, which are followed by yellowish-orange berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Haemanthus distichus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a garden setting.
