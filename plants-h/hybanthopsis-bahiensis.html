Hybanthopsis bahiensis

Hybanthopsis bahiensis, commonly known as the Bahamian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and sandy soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Hybanthopsis bahiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
