Hypericum galioides

Hypericum galioides, commonly known as swamp St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in marshy areas and along stream banks. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a reddish or purple underside.

Hypericum galioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
