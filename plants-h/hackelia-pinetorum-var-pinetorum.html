Hackelia pinetorum var. pinetorum

Hackelia pinetorum var. pinetorum, commonly known as the pine woods honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils within forested areas. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside, and it produces clusters of small, tubular, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. These flowers are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
