Hieracium bangii var. siambonicum

Hieracium bangii var. siambonicum, commonly known as the Siamese hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, and its leaves are typically lance-shaped with rough textures. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
