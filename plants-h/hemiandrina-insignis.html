Hemiandrina insignis

Hemiandrina insignis, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Hemiandrina insignis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its foliage is typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, fuzzy hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
