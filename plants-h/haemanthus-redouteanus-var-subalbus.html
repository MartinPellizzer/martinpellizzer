Haemanthus redouteanus var. subalbus

Haemanthus redouteanus var. subalbus, commonly known as the white-stemmed haemanthus, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, sturdy stems that are typically white or pale green, contrasting with its bright green leaves. The plant produces clusters of striking red flowers during the summer months, which are followed by small, red berries. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and botanical collections.
