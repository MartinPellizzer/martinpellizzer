Hedera helix f. deltoidea

Hedera helix f. deltoidea, commonly known as the deltoid ivy, is a variant of the common ivy that exhibits distinctive leaf morphology.

Its leaves are broader and more rounded compared to the typical species, often with a deltoid shape, giving it its cultivar name. This plant is a climbing evergreen vine, characterized by its dark green, glossy foliage. It thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

The leaves are typically leathery and have a slightly waxy texture, contributing to its adaptability in various garden settings.
