Hieracium canescens

Hieracium canescens, commonly known as the hoary hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its grayish-white hairs that cover the stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive, silvery appearance. The plant produces yellow daisy-like flowers with a central disk surrounded by ray florets. It thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Hieracium canescens is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flowers.
