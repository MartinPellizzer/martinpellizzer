Hieracium basifalcatum

Hieracium basifalcatum, commonly known as the broad-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are broadly ovate with a distinctive basal notch, and the flowers are yellow with a central disc.

It is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
