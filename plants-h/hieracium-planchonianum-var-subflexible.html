Hieracium planchonianum var. subflexible

Hieracium planchonianum var. subflexible, commonly known as the flexible hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in composite inflorescences, which are typical of the daisy family. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its aggressive growth, it can become invasive in certain regions.
