Hydrangea angustipetala var. subumbellata

Hydrangea angustipetala var. subumbellata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of blue, pink, or white, depending on soil pH. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and ability to bloom reliably in cooler climates.
