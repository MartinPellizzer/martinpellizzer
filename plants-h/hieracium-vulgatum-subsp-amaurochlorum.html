Hieracium vulgatum subsp. amaurochlorum

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

amaurochlorum, commonly known as the dark-yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow-orange flower heads.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with a hairy surface, and the flowers are clustered in dense, radiate inflorescences.

It is often found in association with other members of the hawkweed group, sharing similar ecological preferences.
