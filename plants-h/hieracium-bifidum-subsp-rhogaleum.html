Hieracium bifidum subsp. rhogaleum

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

rhogaleum, commonly known as the two-lobed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and a flowering stem that bears yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with serrated edges, and the flowers are arranged in a single head.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
