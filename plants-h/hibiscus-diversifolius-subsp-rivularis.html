Hibiscus diversifolius subsp. rivularis

Hibiscus diversifolius subsp.

rivularis, commonly known as the river hibiscus, is a species native to the southeastern United States. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors.

The plant features large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a prominent yellow or red throat. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture. Hibiscus diversifolius subsp.

rivularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
