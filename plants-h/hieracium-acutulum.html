Hieracium acutulum

Hieracium acutulum, commonly known as the common spear-thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and serrated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are typically arranged in a dense, spherical cluster. Native to Europe, it is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

It is considered a weed in agricultural areas due to its rapid growth and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
