Heterotis jacquesii

Heterotis jacquesii, commonly known as the African waterwheel, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive whorled arrangement of leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along riverbanks. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
