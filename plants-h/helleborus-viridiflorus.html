Helleborus viridiflorus

Helleborus viridiflorus, commonly known as the green-flowered hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and striking, pale green to white flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is known for its toxic properties, containing various alkaloids. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodlands and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal history, it is cultivated in gardens and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
