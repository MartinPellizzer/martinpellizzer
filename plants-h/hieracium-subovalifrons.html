Hieracium subovalifrons

Hieracium subovalifrons, commonly known as the suboval-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and ragworts, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium subovalifrons is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
