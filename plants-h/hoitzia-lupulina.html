Hoitzia lupulina

Hoitzia lupulina, commonly known as the yellow bell.

It is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas. The plant features showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Hoitzia lupulina is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
