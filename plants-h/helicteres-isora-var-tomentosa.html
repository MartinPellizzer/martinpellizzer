Helicteres isora var. tomentosa

Helicteres isora var. tomentosa, commonly known as the hairy senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with a dense, fuzzy coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom during the warmer months. Its fruit is a slender pod containing several seeds, and it is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
