Hedysarum cappadocicum

Hedysarum cappadocicum, commonly known as Cappadocian sweet clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Hedysarum cappadocicum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
