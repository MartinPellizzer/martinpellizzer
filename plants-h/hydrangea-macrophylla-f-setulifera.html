Hydrangea macrophylla f. setulifera

Hydrangea macrophylla f. setulifera, commonly known as the serrated-leaf hydrangea, is a cultivar characterized by its distinctive serrated leaf margins and large, showy flower heads.

It belongs to the Hydrangeaceae family and is native to Japan, where it thrives in humid, temperate climates. The plant produces clusters of sterile flowers that range in color from blue to pink, depending on soil pH. It prefers partial shade and well-draining, acidic soil to flourish.

Its foliage remains lush and vibrant throughout the growing season, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
