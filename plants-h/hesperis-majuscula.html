Hesperis majuscula

Hesperis majuscula, commonly known as the sweet rocket, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants. The plant produces showy clusters of bright purple or pink flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Hesperis majuscula is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.
