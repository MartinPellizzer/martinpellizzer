Hedyotis corymbosa var. ampla

Hedyotis corymbosa var. ampla, commonly known as the broad-leaved marsh tea, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks, thriving in moist to waterlogged soils. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are often broad and ovate in shape. Its flowers are clustered in corymbs and are usually white or pale yellow, attracting various pollinators.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
