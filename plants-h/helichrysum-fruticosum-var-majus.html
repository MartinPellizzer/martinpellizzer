Helichrysum fruticosum var. majus

Helichrysum fruticosum var. majus, commonly known as the yellow everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, papery bracts that resemble petals, giving it a feathery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its drought tolerance and long-lasting flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
