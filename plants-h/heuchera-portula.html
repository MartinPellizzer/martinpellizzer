Heuchera portula

Heuchera portula, commonly known as the creeping coral bells, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features heart-shaped, basal leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, red, and purple. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white during the summer months. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Heuchera portula is valued for its ornamental foliage and its ability to naturalize in moist, shady environments.
