Hirschfeldia inflexa

Hirschfeldia inflexa, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant has small, narrow leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
