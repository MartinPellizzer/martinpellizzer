Hypoestes angustilabiata

Hypoestes angustilabiata, commonly known as the "Purple Heart" or "Wax Plant," is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, often with contrasting white or yellow markings. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture. It thrives in well-draining soil and prefers bright, indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Hypoestes angustilabiata is also known for its ability to produce offsets, which can be propagated easily to create new plants.
