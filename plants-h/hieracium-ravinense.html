Hieracium ravinense

Hieracium ravinense, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. It features basal leaves that are usually heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Its flowers are yellow and bloom from late spring to early autumn, attracting various pollinators.
