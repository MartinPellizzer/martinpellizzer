Hieracium cuncolatoides

Hieracium cuncolatoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head.

Hieracium cuncolatoides is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
