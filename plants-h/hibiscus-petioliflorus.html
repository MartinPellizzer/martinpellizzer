Hibiscus petioliflorus

Hibiscus petioliflorus, commonly known as the African hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with five deeply notched petals, often in shades of red, pink, or white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stems. Hibiscus petioliflorus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in swampy or wetland habitats.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
