Hieracium murorum subsp. perelegans

Hieracium murorum subsp.

perelegans, commonly known as the creeping hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, exhibiting a low, spreading growth habit.

The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy, with yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. It produces small, oval seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.

This subspecies is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and flowering pattern, making it easily identifiable within the genus Hieracium.
