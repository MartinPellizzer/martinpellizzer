Hemionitis lindheimeri

Hemionitis lindheimeri, commonly known as Lindheimer's hemionitis, is a rare and delicate fern native to the arid regions of Texas and New Mexico.

It is characterized by its finely divided, bright green fronds that are often clustered closely together. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand periods of drought. Its reproductive structures, including spore-bearing soruses, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a sensitive indicator species of its ecosystem.
