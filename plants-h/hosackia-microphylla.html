Hosackia microphylla

Hosackia microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It produces clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring.
