Hieracium murorum subsp. mallopodum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

mallopodum, commonly known as the mouse-ear hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and is often found in grassy areas and disturbed soils.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is a perennial species that can spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural and natural habitats.

Due to its adaptability, it is often considered invasive in certain regions.
