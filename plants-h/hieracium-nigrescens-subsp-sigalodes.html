Hieracium nigrescens subsp. sigalodes

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

sigalodes, commonly known as the blackened hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

The plant features basal and cauline leaves that are usually lanceolate to ovate in shape, with hairy surfaces. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dark purple to black disc florets and the presence of glandular hairs on its stems and leaves.
