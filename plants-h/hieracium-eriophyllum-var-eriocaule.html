Hieracium eriophyllum var. eriocaule

Hieracium eriophyllum var. eriocaule, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant features a hairy, upright stem that can grow up to 30 cm in height, with basal leaves that are lanceolate and covered in fine, white hairs. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and bloom from late spring to early autumn.

The variety eriocaule is distinguished by its dense, woolly covering on the stem and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance.
