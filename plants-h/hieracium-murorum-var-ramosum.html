Hieracium murorum var. ramosum

Hieracium murorum var. ramosum, commonly known as the creeping hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its low-growing, sprawling habit.

It features bright yellow flowers that are typically arranged in dense clusters, and its leaves are usually lance-shaped with a rough, hairy texture. This variant is distinguished by its more branched stems compared to other varieties of Hieracium. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other open areas.

The plant is known for its ability to spread rapidly through rhizomes, making it a common weed in various habitats.
