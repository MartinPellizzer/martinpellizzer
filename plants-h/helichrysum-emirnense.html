Helichrysum emirnense

Helichrysum emirnense, commonly known as the Persian immortelle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow to orange flowers that resemble small suns. The plant produces small, papery bracts that give it a distinctive appearance, often mistaken for petals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

The leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its adaptability in harsh climates.
