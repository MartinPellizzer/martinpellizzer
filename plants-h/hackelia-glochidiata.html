Hackelia glochidiata

Hackelia glochidiata, commonly known as the desert honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits called follicles, which contain seeds covered in fine, hair-like structures. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its foliage and flowers provide nectar for various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
