Helianthemum canum subsp. ciscaucasicum

Helianthemum canum subsp.

ciscaucasicum, commonly known as the Caucasian rockrose, is a shrub native to the Caucasus region. It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters.

The plant has succulent, glandular-hairy leaves that help it conserve water in its arid habitat. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Its botanical structure is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in its native range.
