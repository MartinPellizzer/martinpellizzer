Hovea longifolia var. lanceolata

Hovea longifolia var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceolate hovea, is a shrub native to Australia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem and a preference for well-drained soils.

It is often found in coastal and sandy environments, contributing to local biodiversity.
