Hieracium laevigatum subsp. bichlorophyllum

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

bichlorophyllum, commonly known as the smooth hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow flowering heads.

The leaves are typically smooth and lance-shaped, with a hairy underside. It produces small, disk-shaped flowers that are surrounded by pale yellow ray florets.

This subspecies is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows.
