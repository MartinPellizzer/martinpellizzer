Hieracium aestivum subsp. hryniaviense

Hieracium aestivum subsp.

hryniaviense, commonly known as the summer hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom from June to August.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
