Hieracium juvonis

Hieracium juvonis, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies. Its flowers are bright yellow, with ray florets that give it a dandelion-like appearance.

Hieracium juvonis is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it both a common weed and a subject of ecological study.
