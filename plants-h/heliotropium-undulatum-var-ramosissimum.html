Heliotropium undulatum var. ramosissimum

Heliotropium undulatum var. ramosissimum, commonly known as the hairy heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and adaptability.
