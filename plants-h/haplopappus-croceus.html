Haplopappus croceus

Haplopappus croceus, commonly known as the yellow pappus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

Haplopappus croceus is typically found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
