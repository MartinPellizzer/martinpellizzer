Hydrangea longipes

Hydrangea longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed hydrangea, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range from white to pink or blue depending on soil pH. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a rough texture and serrated edges.

Hydrangea longipes prefers partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
