Heteropappus arenarius

Heteropappus arenarius, commonly known as the sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with hairy stems and leaves adapted to withstand drought conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, followed by distinctive, two-lobed pappus structures that aid in seed dispersal. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Heteropappus arenarius is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and is considered a hardy, drought-tolerant species.
