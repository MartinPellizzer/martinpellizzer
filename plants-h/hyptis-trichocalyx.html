Hyptis trichocalyx

Hyptis trichocalyx, commonly known as the roughleaf hyptis, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or violet in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or scrub habitats.

Hyptis trichocalyx is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic benefits.
