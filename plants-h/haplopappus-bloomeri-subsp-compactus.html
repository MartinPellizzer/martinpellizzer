Haplopappus bloomeri subsp. compactus

Haplopappus bloomeri subsp.

compactus, commonly known as the compact pappus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in dense clusters, and the plant produces bristly pappus structures that aid in seed dispersal.

It is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats, contributing to local biodiversity.
