Hedyotis valerianelloides

Hedyotis valerianelloides, commonly known as the small flame flower, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features small, bright red to orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has thin, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, compact flowering plants.
