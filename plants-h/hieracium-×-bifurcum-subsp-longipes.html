Hieracium × bifurcum subsp. longipes

Hieracium × bifurcum subsp.

longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural and garden settings.
