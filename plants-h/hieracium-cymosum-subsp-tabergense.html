Hieracium cymosum subsp. tabergense

Hieracium cymosum subsp.

tabergense, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is a perennial herb with a rosette of basal leaves and produces yellow composite flowers.

The plant thrives in alpine and subalpine habitats, often found on rocky soils and meadows. Its flowers are typically arranged in a cymose inflorescence, which is characteristic of the genus Hieracium.

It is adapted to cold climates and has a low-growing habit to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
