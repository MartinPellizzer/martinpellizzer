Hieracium tenerisquameum

Hieracium tenerisquameum, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy areas and has narrow, lance-shaped leaves covered in dense, soft hairs. Its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, producing small seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
