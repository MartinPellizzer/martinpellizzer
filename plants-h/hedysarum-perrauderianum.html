Hedysarum perrauderianum

Hedysarum perrauderianum, commonly known as the Corsican vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in spikes, and its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion.

Hedysarum perrauderianum is adapted to harsh environments and is often found in scrublands and coastal areas.
