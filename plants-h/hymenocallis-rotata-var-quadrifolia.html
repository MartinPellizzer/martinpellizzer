Hymenocallis rotata var. quadrifolia

Hymenocallis rotata var. quadrifolia, commonly known as the four-leaf hurricane lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to the Caribbean.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive red stripe down the throat. The plant produces four leaves in a rosette pattern, which is a key identifying characteristic of the varietal name "quadrifolia.". It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for tropical and subtropical gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant blooms and unique foliage structure.
