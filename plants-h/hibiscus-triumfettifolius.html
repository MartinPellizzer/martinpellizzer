Hibiscus triumfettifolius

Hibiscus triumfettifolius, commonly known as the yellow hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers, which are typically five-petaled and have a prominent central stigma. The plant grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with woody stems and broad, palmately lobed leaves. Its botanical features include showy inflorescences and capsules that contain numerous seeds.

Hibiscus triumfettifolius is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
