Hordeum secalinum var. parviflorum

Hordeum secalinum var. parviflorum, commonly known as dwarf barley, is a wild grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, pale green leaves. The plant produces tiny, clustered spikelets with short, narrow florets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and agricultural fields.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed but also has potential uses in ecological restoration projects.
