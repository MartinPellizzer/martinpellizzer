Hyptis mirabilis

Hyptis mirabilis, commonly known as the blue hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to blue or violet. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Hyptis mirabilis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
