Helianthus debilis subsp. cucumerifolius

Helianthus debilis subsp.

cucumerifolius, commonly known as the swamp sunflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States. It features heart-shaped, broad leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces yellow-orange composite flowers with ray florets surrounding a central disk, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in wet or moist soils, often found in swamps, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for wetland ecosystems and a subject of study in plant ecology.
