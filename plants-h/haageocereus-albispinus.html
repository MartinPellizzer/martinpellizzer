Haageocereus albispinus

Haageocereus albispinus, commonly known as the white-spined cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in arid regions.
