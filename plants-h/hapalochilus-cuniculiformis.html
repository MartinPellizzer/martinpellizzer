Hapalochilus cuniculiformis

Hapalochilus cuniculiformis, commonly known as the rabbit-eared cacti, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fleshy, and often hairy stems that resemble rabbit ears, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-drained soil and minimal water.

Hapalochilus cuniculiformis is valued both for its ornamental appearance and its ecological role in its native habitats.
