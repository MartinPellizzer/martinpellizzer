Hakea microcarpa var. tasmannica

Hakea microcarpa var. tasmannica, commonly known as the Tasmanian hakea, is a shrub native to Tasmania, characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves and clusters of bright red flowers.

It is part of the Proteaceae family, which is known for its showy, often long-lasting blooms. The plant typically grows in heathland and dry forests, adapting well to nutrient-poor soils. Its woody structure and seasonal flowering make it a distinctive feature of the Tasmanian flora.

The species is also notable for its ability to thrive in coastal and sandy environments.
