Hieracium reversum

Hieracium reversum, commonly known as the reverse hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are hairy and alternate, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium reversum is often found in temperate climates and can sometimes be invasive in certain regions.
