Hypericum revolutum subsp. revolutum

Hypericum revolutum subsp.

revolutum, commonly known as the red-stemmed St. John's wort, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems that are reddish in color.

The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in summer, and its leaves are opposite, ovate, and slightly hairy. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and woodland edges.

The species is notable for its medicinal properties, with parts of the plant used traditionally for their purported health benefits.
