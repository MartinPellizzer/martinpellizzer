Hedera cypria

Hedera cypria, commonly known as Cyprus ivy, is a species of evergreen climbing plant native to the island of Cyprus.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, dark green, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that are followed by orange-red berries. Hedera cypria thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments and is often used in horticulture for ground cover or as a decorative plant.

Its adaptability and ornamental value make it a popular choice in gardens and indoor spaces.
