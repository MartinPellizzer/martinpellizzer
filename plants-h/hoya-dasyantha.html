Hoya dasyantha

Hoya dasyantha, commonly known as the "Coral Hoya," is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and clusters of waxy, coral-pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces opposite, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green, often with a slight waxy coating. Hoya dasyantha is a trailing plant that thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.

It is relatively low-maintenance, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
