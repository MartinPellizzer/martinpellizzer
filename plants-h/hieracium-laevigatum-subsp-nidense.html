Hieracium laevigatum subsp. nidense

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

nidense, commonly known as the downy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed areas.

The plant features basal rosettes of spoon-shaped, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in late summer. It has a taproot system and is characterized by its finely pubescent stems and leaves.

This subspecies is often found in nutrient-poor soils and is part of the Asteraceae family.
