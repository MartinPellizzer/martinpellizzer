Hymenophyllum abietinum

Hymenophyllum abietinum, commonly known as the silver fern, is a small, epiphytic fern that thrives in humid, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery coating of trichomes. The plant belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae and is native to the temperate regions of the Southern Hemisphere. Its fronds are typically short and clustered, with a unique structure that allows it to retain moisture in its moist, mossy habitats.

Hymenophyllum abietinum is often found growing on tree trunks or rocks in cool, damp forests.
