Helianthus vulgaris

Helianthus vulgaris, commonly known as the common sunflower, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to eastern North America and is characterized by its large, showy yellow flower heads surrounded by green bracts. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and produces a single large composite flower head. Its leaves are alternately arranged, rough to the touch, and have a heart-shaped base.

The flower head consists of numerous small yellow disk flowers surrounded by ray flowers that resemble petals.
