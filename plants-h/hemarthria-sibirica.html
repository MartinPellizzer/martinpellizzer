Hemarthria sibirica

Hemarthria sibirica, commonly known as Siberian flat sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to temperate regions of Asia.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets. It is often found in nutrient-rich soils and can form dense vegetation in wetland environments.

Hemarthria sibirica plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
