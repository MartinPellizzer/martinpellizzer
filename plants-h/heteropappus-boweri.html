Heteropappus boweri

Heteropappus boweri, commonly known as the Bower's heteropappus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive bracts. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and heat. Its leaves are usually linear and hairy, while the flowers are yellow and occur in clusters.

Heteropappus boweri is known for its resilience and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
