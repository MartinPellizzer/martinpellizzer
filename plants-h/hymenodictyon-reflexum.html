Hymenodictyon reflexum

Hymenodictyon reflexum, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its slender, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, blooming throughout the growing season. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature.

Hymenodictyon reflexum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
