Hypericum nakaii f. atropurpureipunctatum

Hypericum nakaii f. atropurpureipunctatum, commonly known as the purple-spotted St. John's wort, is a variant of the perennial shrub native to East Asia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with reddish or purple undersides. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late summer. Its stems are often covered in fine, reddish hairs, giving it a distinct appearance.

This botanical variation is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique coloration.
