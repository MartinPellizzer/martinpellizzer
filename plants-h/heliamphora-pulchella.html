Heliamphora pulchella

Heliamphora pulchella, commonly known as the Pitcher Plant, is a carnivorous plant native to the tepuis of South America.

It is characterized by its unique pitcher-shaped leaves that serve as traps for insects. The plant's pitchers are typically green or red and produce a sweet nectar to attract prey. Heliamphora pulchella thrives in humid, nutrient-poor environments, often found in cloud forests.

Its botanical features make it a popular specimen among enthusiasts of carnivorous plants.
