Holcoglossum saprophyticum

Holcoglossum saprophyticum, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and unique orchid species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is a saprophytic plant, meaning it derives its nutrients from decaying organic matter rather than through photosynthesis. The plant lacks chlorophyll, giving it its pale, almost translucent appearance. It produces small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, and it is often found growing on the trunks of trees or in shaded, humid environments.

Due to its specific habitat requirements and rarity, it is considered an endangered species.
