Hieracium murorum f. sylvaticum

Hieracium murorum f. sylvaticum, commonly known as the woodland hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers and hairy, rough leaves. This subspecies is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It has a rosette growth habit and produces numerous small flowers in a single head.

The plant is often considered a weed but plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
