Hesperis primulifolia

Hesperis primulifolia, commonly known as the white scabious, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its flowers are usually white or pale purple, with four petals arranged in a cross shape, a typical feature of the Brassicaceae family.

Hesperis primulifolia is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.
