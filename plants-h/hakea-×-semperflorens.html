Hakea × semperflorens

Hakea × semperflorens, commonly known as the coastal hakea or everblooming hakea, is a hybrid plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, woody structure and showy, bottlebrush-like flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, where it thrives in well-drained sandy soils and full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, leathery leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Hakea × semperflorens is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking appearance and hardy nature.
