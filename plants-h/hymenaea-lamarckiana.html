Hymenaea lamarckiana

Hymenaea lamarckiana, commonly known as the Brazilian cedar, is a large, deciduous tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and long, pinnate leaves. The tree produces yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, pod-like fruits containing flat, edible seeds.

Hymenaea lamarckiana is valued for its durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture, and for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity.
