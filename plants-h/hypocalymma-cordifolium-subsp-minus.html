Hypocalymma cordifolium subsp. minus

Hypocalymma cordifolium subsp.

minus, commonly known as the small-heart lilly pilly, is a shrub native to the south-eastern regions of Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green.

The plant produces small, white flowers clustered in dense inflorescences, which are usually borne on the ends of the branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in landscaping due to its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
