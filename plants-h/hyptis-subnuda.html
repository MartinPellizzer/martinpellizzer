Hyptis subnuda

Hyptis subnuda, commonly known as silverleaf hyptis, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a perennial shrub, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or disturbed areas.

Its aromatic leaves and showy blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
