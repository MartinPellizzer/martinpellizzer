Hieracium obscuratum subsp. violae

Hieracium obscuratum subsp.

violae, commonly known as the purple hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and flowering heads that produce small, purple or violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a taproot system and can form dense colonies, which may impact local vegetation.

Its flowers are visited by various pollinators, including bees and flies.
