Hieracium transiens subsp. caloprasinum

Hieracium transiens subsp.

caloprasinum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features basal and cauline leaves that are lanceolate to ovate in shape, with hairy surfaces. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and are borne on long, slender peduncles.

The species is characterized by its ability to produce numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
