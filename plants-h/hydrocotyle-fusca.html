Hydrocotyle fusca

Hydrocotyle fusca, commonly known as the Florida waterleaf, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features a rosette of heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, white flowers with five petals, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as wetlands and forest floors.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland conservation.
