Hieracium fuscum subsp. atropurpureum

Hieracium fuscum subsp.

atropurpureum, commonly known as the purple hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves.

The plant produces showy, purple to pinkish-red flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
