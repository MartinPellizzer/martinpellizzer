Hexadesmia tenella

Hexadesmia tenella, commonly known as the small-flowered marshmallow, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in shallow water or moist soils. The plant features slender, erect stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. Its small, pinkish-white flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences and bloom during the rainy season.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
