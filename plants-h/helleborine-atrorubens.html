Helleborine atrorubens

Helleborine atrorubens, commonly known as the purple helleborine, is a species of orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and striking purple to brownish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers have a distinctive three-lobed lip and are often fragrant, attracting specific pollinators.

Helleborine atrorubens is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
