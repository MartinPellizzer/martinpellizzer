Hordeum distichon var. triaristatum

Hordeum distichon var. triaristatum, commonly known as the three-row barley, is a variant of barley characterized by its unique three-row arrangement of spikelets.

It is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a spike made up of multiple spikelets, each bearing two to three bract-like structures.

This variety is often studied for its genetic diversity and potential agricultural applications.
