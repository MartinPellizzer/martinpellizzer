Hieracium laevigatum subsp. polioprasinoides

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

polioprasinoides, commonly known as the golden hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are broadly lanceolate, with a rough, hairy texture, and its flowers are yellow, often forming dense clusters.

The species is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
