Hieracium pseudosvaneticum subsp. villosellipes

Hieracium pseudosvaneticum subsp.

villosellipes, commonly known as the woolly-haired hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves that are covered in dense, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces yellowish to reddish flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in grassy areas and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
