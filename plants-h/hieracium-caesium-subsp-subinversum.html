Hieracium caesium subsp. subinversum

Hieracium caesium subsp.

subinversum, commonly known as the pale hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are usually pale green with a hairy surface. Its flowering stems bear yellow composite flowers, which are composed of many small florets arranged in a discoid head.

The species is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.
