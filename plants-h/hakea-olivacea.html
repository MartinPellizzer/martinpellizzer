Hakea olivacea

Hakea olivacea, commonly known as the olive hakea, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces distinctive yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. It is well adapted to dry conditions and is often found in heathlands and sandy soils.

Hakea olivacea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
