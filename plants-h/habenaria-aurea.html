Habenaria aurea

Habenaria aurea, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns of darker hues. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, where it thrives on the forest floor or on tree trunks. Its roots are often mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to enhance nutrient absorption.

The botanical features of Habenaria aurea make it a popular subject in both horticulture and botanical research.
