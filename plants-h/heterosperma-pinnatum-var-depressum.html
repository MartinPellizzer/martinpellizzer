Heterosperma pinnatum var. depressum

Heterosperma pinnatum var. depressum, commonly known as the broadleaf water clover, is a aquatic plant characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology.

It features floating leaves with a broad, pinnately divided structure, often with a depressed or slightly curled appearance. The plant is native to freshwater habitats in tropical and subtropical regions. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically submerged or floating.

Its botanical features make it an important species in aquatic ecosystems for oxygenation and habitat provision.
