Heteropterys pteropetala var. typica

Heteropterys pteropetala var. typica, commonly known as the white-flowered huito, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain several seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and flowering display.
