Hypericum velutinum

Hypericum velutinum, commonly known as the velvet St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in sunny, well-drained habitats. Its stems are woody and can become somewhat thorny with age.

Hypericum velutinum is part of the genus Hypericum, which includes many species known for their medicinal and ornamental value.
