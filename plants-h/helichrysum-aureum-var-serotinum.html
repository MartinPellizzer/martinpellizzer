Helichrysum aureum var. serotinum

Helichrysum aureum var. serotinum, commonly known as the late golden everlasting, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features wiry, branched stems and small, yellowish-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces compact, golden-yellow flower heads that bloom in late summer to autumn. Its flowers are composed of numerous tiny tubular florets arranged in a dense, spherical shape.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in dried floral arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms.
