Hieracium vulgatum subsp. dalicum

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

dalicum, commonly known as the Dalic Meadowhawk, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe. It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

The plant features basal and stem leaves that are usually lanceolate to oblanceolate in shape, with hairy surfaces. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a single head, surrounded by pale yellow to white ray florets.

The species is characterized by its relatively small size and is often found in association with other flowering plants in grassy environments.
