Hymenostegia gilletii

Hymenostegia gilletii, commonly known as the silky wood vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are often covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Hymenostegia gilletii is typically found in humid, shaded environments such as forest understories.
