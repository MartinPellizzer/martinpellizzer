Humboltia harpophylla

Humboltia harpophylla, commonly known as the California buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the coastal regions of California.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically gray-green in color and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by dark blue-black berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

Humboltia harpophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
