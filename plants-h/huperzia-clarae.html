Huperzia clarae

Huperzia clarae, commonly known as the white huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hypnaceae.

It is characterized by its simple, linear leaves and a distinctive, upright growth habit. Native to the humid forests of Central and South America, this plant thrives in shaded, moist environments. Its spore-producing structures, known as sporangia, are clustered on the underside of its leaves.

Huperzia clarae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
