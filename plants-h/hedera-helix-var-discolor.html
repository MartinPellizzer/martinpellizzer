Hedera helix var. discolor

Hedera helix var. discolor, commonly known as the variegated ivy, is a cultivar of the common ivy plant.

It is characterized by its striking foliage, featuring white and yellow markings on dark green leaves. This plant is a climbing or trailing evergreen vine that thrives in partial to full shade. It has small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in the spring, followed by dark blue berries.

Hedera helix var. discolor is widely used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to cover walls, trellises, or ground cover.
