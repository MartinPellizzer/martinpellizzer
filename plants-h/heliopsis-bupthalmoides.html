Heliopsis bupthalmoides

Heliopsis bupthalmoides, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and marshes. The plant features upright, hairy stems that bear clusters of bright yellow, cone-shaped flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture.

Heliopsis bupthalmoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
