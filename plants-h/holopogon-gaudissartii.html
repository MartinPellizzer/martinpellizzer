Holopogon gaudissartii

Holopogon gaudissartii, commonly known as the Gaudissart's grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers. It is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as savannas and grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in warmer climates.
