Hemsleya amabilis

Hemsleya amabilis, commonly known as the pink orchid, is a species of flowering plant in the family Orchidaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its striking pink to lavender-colored flowers, which are often fragrant. Hemsleya amabilis typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental cultivation in warm, humid climates.
