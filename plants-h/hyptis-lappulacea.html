Hyptis lappulacea

Hyptis lappulacea, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.

Hyptis lappulacea is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
