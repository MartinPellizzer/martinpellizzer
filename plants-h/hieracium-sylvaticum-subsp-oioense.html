Hieracium sylvaticum subsp. oioense

Hieracium sylvaticum subsp.

oioense, commonly known as the Oio hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and small, yellowish flowers.

The plant typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a composite head.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its resilience in various habitats.
