Hippocratea guineensis

Hippocratea guineensis, commonly known as the Guinean hippocratea, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are pinnately compound, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes.

The seeds are enclosed in elongated pods and are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ecological role in soil enrichment.
