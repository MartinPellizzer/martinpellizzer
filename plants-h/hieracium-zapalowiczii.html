Hieracium zapalowiczii

Hieracium zapalowiczii, commonly known as the Zapalowicz's hawkweed, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to specific regions of Europe and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces numerous disk flowers that are surrounded by ray florets.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
