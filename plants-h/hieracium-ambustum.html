Hieracium ambustum

Hieracium ambustum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early autumn. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit with upright, hairy stems and basal leaves that are broadly lance-shaped. Native to Europe, it has been introduced to other regions and is often found in disturbed soils, meadows, and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a recognizable species within the genus Hieracium.
