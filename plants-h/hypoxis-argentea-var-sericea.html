Hypoxis argentea var. sericea

Hypoxis argentea var. sericea, commonly known as silverleaf false aster, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a rosette of broad, silver-white leaves that are densely covered with fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
