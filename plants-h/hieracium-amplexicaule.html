Hieracium amplexicaule

Hieracium amplexicaule, commonly known as the clasping hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, hairy leaves that clasping the stem, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces yellow daisy-like flowers with a central disk surrounded by ray florets. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, ditches, and along roadsides.
