Hieracium placophorum

Hieracium placophorum, commonly known as the smooth hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it typically grows in grassy areas and disturbed soils. The plant has basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often reddish in color. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a flat-topped cluster, and it produces small, oval seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

This species is often considered a weed due to its rapid growth and ability to colonize various habitats.
