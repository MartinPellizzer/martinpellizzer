Hieracium polatschekii

Hieracium polatschekii, commonly known as the Polatschek's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry meadows and rocky soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
