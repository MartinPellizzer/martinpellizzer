Hieracium rapunculoides subsp. neoperrieranum

Hieracium rapunculoides subsp.

neoperrieranum, commonly known as the Perrier's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers and hairy, basal leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, calcareous soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has a rosette growth form with numerous flowering stems that bear small, daisy-like inflorescences.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its range.
