Hemigraphis reptans var. gracilis

Hemigraphis reptans var. gracilis, commonly known as silver mohair, is a low-growing, evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features dense, velvety foliage with a distinctive silver-gray underside, giving it a soft, mohair-like appearance. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape and have a slightly waxy texture. It produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, round berries.

This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
