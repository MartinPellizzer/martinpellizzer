Homalium boehmii

Homalium boehmii, commonly known as the Boehm's holly.

It is a flowering plant species native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are typically elliptical and glossy. It produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and seasonal flowering.
