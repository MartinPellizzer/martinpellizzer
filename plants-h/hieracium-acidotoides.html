Hieracium acidotoides

Hieracium acidotoides, commonly known as the acid-loving hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system. The plant typically grows in acidic soils, often found in woodland edges and open meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

It is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor environments, making it an indicator species for certain soil conditions.
