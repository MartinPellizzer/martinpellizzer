Heterolobium petiolulatum

Heterolobium petiolulatum, commonly known as the slenderleaf burr medic, is a perennial legume native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, elongated leaflets and distinctive, hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is often found in open grasslands and is adapted to dry, well-drained soils.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it valuable for soil enrichment and forage in agricultural settings.
