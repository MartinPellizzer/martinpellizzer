Hieracium curvescens

Hieracium curvescens, commonly known as the bent-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium curvescens is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in some regions.
