Hieracium aphyllum subsp. subdentatum

Hieracium aphyllum subsp.

subdentatum, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring temperate climates.

The plant has a rosette of narrow, upright leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow, with a characteristic ray floret and a central disk, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
