Hydrangea opuloides f. macrosepala

Hydrangea opuloides f. macrosepala, commonly known as the large-sepaled hydrangea, is a cultivar characterized by its large, showy flowers.

It belongs to the Hydrangeaceae family and is native to the eastern United States. The plant features broad, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. Its inflorescences consist of sterile flowers that form dense, rounded clusters, often in shades of blue, pink, or white.

The botanical features of this hydrangea make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
