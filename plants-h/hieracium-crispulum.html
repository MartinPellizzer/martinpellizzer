Hieracium crispulum

Hieracium crispulum, commonly known as the curled hawkweed, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are typically alternate, with a rough texture and a serrated edge.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
