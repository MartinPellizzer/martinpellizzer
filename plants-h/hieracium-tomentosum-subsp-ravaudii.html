Hieracium tomentosum subsp. ravaudii

Hieracium tomentosum subsp.

ravaudii, commonly known as the Ravaud's hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Due to its ornamental appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
