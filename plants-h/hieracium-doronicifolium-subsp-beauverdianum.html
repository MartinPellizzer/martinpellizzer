Hieracium doronicifolium subsp. beauverdianum

Hieracium doronicifolium subsp.

beauverdianum, commonly known as the beautiful hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves that are broadly ovate to rhombic in shape, often with a hairy texture.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It is native to southern Europe and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
