Helianthus × kellermanii

Helianthus × kellermanii, commonly known as the Kellerman's sunflower, is a hybrid species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow-orange flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. It produces both ray and disk florets, with the outer rays being the most prominent feature.

Helianthus × kellermanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
