Hydrocotyle asiatica var. floridana

Hydrocotyle asiatica var. floridana, commonly known as Florida spurge, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are usually light to dark green, and it produces small, white flowers clustered in umbels. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in shallow water or moist soil.

This species is adapted to humid environments and plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
