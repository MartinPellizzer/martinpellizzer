Hibiscus circinnatus

Hibiscus circinnatus, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep red, with a prominent central stigma. The plant has heart-shaped, broad leaves and is often found growing near water bodies. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a woody stem that becomes more prominent with age.

Hibiscus circinnatus is also noted for its medicinal and cultural significance in various indigenous traditions.
