Hieracium davallianum

Hieracium davallianum, commonly known as the Davall's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the summer months, and its leaves are usually hairy and arranged in a rosette pattern.

Hieracium davallianum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
