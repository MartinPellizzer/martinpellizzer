Hieracium laniferum subsp. spathulatiforme

Hieracium laniferum subsp.

spathulatiforme, commonly known as the broad-leafed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, spoon-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in open habitats like meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
