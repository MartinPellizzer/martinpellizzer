Hieracium jurassicum subsp. pseudalbinum

Hieracium jurassicum subsp.

pseudalbinum, commonly known as the Jurassic hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its yellowish to pale green flowers, which are typically composite, consisting of both ray and disk florets.

The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its stems are usually hairy as well. It is native to certain regions of Europe and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and sometimes endangered species.
