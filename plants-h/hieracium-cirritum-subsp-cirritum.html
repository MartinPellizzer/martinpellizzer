Hieracium cirritum subsp. cirritum

Hieracium cirritum subsp.

cirritum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disc florets.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, preferring partial to full sun. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and the leaves are usually lance-shaped with a hairy texture.

It is a common weed in agricultural fields and is often found in temperate regions.
