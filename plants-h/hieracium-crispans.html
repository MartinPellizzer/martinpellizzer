Hieracium crispans

Hieracium crispans, commonly known as the curly hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, and its leaves are typically lobed and covered in fine hairs. Hieracium crispans thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

It is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common but sometimes invasive species in certain regions.
