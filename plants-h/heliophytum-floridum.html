Heliophytum floridum

Heliophytum floridum, commonly known as the Florida sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy, well-drained soils and thrives in full sunlight. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Heliophytum floridum is often found in open habitats such as pine flatwoods and scrublands.
