Hippeastrum abatinguara

Hippeastrum abatinguara, commonly known as the Abatinguara amaryllis, is a tropical plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant produces large, strap-like leaves that are often used for ornamental purposes. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Hippeastrum abatinguara is popular among gardeners for its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
