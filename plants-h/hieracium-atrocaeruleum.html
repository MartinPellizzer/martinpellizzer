Hieracium atrocaeruleum

Hieracium atrocaeruleum, commonly known as the dark blue hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its dark blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often reddish in color.

It produces numerous small seeds that are dispersed by the wind, contributing to its spread across various habitats.
