Hydrosme grata

Hydrosme grata, commonly known as the water hyacinth, is a floating aquatic plant native to South America.

It has bright violet flowers and broad, waxy leaves that allow it to float on the water's surface. The plant is known for its rapid growth rate, which can lead to dense mats covering water bodies. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Due to its invasive nature, it can disrupt local ecosystems if not managed properly.
