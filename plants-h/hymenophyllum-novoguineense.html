Hymenophyllum novoguineense

Hymenophyllum novoguineense, commonly known as the New Guinea haircap moss, is a small, delicate moss belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its finely branched, feather-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of hair-like structures called rhizoids. This species thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are usually less than 1 cm in length and have a distinctive, translucent appearance.

Hymenophyllum novoguineense plays an important ecological role in maintaining soil moisture and providing habitat for microorganisms in its native habitat.
