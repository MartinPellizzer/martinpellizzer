Hieracium murorum subsp. pinnatifidum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

pinnatifidum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm.

The plant has slender, hairy stems and basal leaves that are pinnatifid, meaning they are deeply lobed. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a composite head, and bloom from June to August.

It is often found in temperate regions and is part of the Asteraceae family.
