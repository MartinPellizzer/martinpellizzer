Hemerocallis sulphurea

Hemerocallis sulphurea, commonly known as the yellow daylily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features sword-shaped, dark green leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom for only one day. The plant is known for its robust growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Hemerocallis sulphurea is also valued for its resilience and low maintenance requirements.
