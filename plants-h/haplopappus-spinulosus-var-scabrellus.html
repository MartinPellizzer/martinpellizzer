Haplopappus spinulosus var. scabrellus

Haplopappus spinulosus var. scabrellus, commonly known as the roughleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leaflets. The plant typically grows in arid and rocky habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are covered in fine, bristly hairs, giving them a rough texture.

The flowers are small and yellow, often arranged in a cluster at the top of the plant.
