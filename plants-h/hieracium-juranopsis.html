Hieracium juranopsis

Hieracium juranopsis, commonly known as the Jura hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a distinctive ray floret structure.

Hieracium juranopsis is known for its resilience to harsh climatic conditions and is often found in meadows and grasslands at higher elevations.
