Hymenophyllum endiviifolium

Hymenophyllum endiviifolium, commonly known as the broad-leafed filmy fern, is a small, epiphytic fern native to the temperate regions of the southern hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, translucent fronds that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically growing on tree trunks or rocks in forested areas. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and are protected by a membranous covering.

This species is notable for its unique morphology and ecological adaptations, making it a distinctive member of the fern family.
