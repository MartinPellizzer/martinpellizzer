Hevea lutea var. apiculata

Hevea lutea var. apiculata, commonly known as the yellow rubber tree, is a variant of the hevea lutea species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its yellowish bark and produces latex, which is used in the rubber industry. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 15 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a smooth margin.

This variant is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its economic significance in rubber production.
