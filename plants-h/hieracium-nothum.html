Hieracium nothum

Hieracium nothum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium nothum is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in various habitats.
