Holoschoenus romanus subsp. australis

Holoschoenus romanus subsp.

australis, commonly known as the Australian flat sedge, is a perennial herb native to southeastern Australia. It typically grows in wetland habitats such as swampy areas and riverbanks, where it thrives in waterlogged soils.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic and semi-aquatic organisms.

Its ecological importance makes it a key species in wetland ecosystems.
