Hiraea divaricata

Hiraea divaricata, commonly known as the creeping jenny, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant features small, rounded leaves that are opposite in arrangement and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is commonly found in woodland areas and along streambanks.

Hiraea divaricata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wetland habitats.
