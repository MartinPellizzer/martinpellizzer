Houstonia tenella

Houstonia tenella, commonly known as the small blue mistflower, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features delicate, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain its seeds. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

Houstonia tenella is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
