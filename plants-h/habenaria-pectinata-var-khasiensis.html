Habenaria pectinata var. khasiensis

Habenaria pectinata var. khasiensis, commonly known as the Khasi orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the northeastern regions of India.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, with a distinctive hooded labellum. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
