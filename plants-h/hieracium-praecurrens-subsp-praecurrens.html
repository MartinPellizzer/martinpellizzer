Hieracium praecurrens subsp. praecurrens

Hieracium praecurrens subsp.

praecurrens, commonly known as the early hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowering period occurs from late spring to early summer.

It is often found in meadows and along roadsides, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinators.
