Helleborus multifidus subsp. hercegovinus

Helleborus multifidus subsp.

hercegovinus, commonly known as the Hercegovina hellebore, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, deeply lobed leaves and striking, violet-blue flowers.

The plant thrives in woodland and shaded environments, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its blooms typically appear in late winter to early spring, making it one of the earliest flowering plants in its habitat.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
