Hieracium alfitodes

Hieracium alfitodes, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

Hieracium alfitodes is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
