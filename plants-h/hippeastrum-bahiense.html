Hippeastrum bahiense

Hippeastrum bahiense, commonly known as the Brazilian belladonna lily, is a striking ornamental plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter. The plant produces vibrant red, orange, or yellow blooms, often with a striking white center, during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Hippeastrum bahiense is also known for its ability to store water in its bulb, allowing it to survive periods of drought.
