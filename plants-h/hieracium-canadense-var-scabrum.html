Hieracium canadense var. scabrum

Hieracium canadense var. scabrum, commonly known as Canadian hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, rough stems and basal rosette of leaves. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is considered an invasive species in some regions.

Its leaves and stems are covered with stiff, bristly hairs, which contribute to its distinctive appearance.
