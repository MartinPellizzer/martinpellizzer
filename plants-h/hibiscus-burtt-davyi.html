Hibiscus burtt-davyi

Hibiscus burtt-davyi, commonly known as the Burtt-Davy hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

Hibiscus burtt-davyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
