Helleborus purpurascens var. subflabellatus

Helleborus purpurascens var. subflabellatus, commonly known as the purple-flowered hellebore, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that are deeply lobed and often have a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of purple and white, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is well-suited for woodland gardens.

The plant contains toxic alkaloids, making it important to handle with care.
