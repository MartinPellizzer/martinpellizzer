Hymenopappus filifolius var. filifolius

Hymenopappus filifolius var. filifolius, commonly known as the silky blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit, with hairy stems and leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. It produces small, bristled seeds that are dispersed by the wind, a common adaptation in desert flora.

This species is often found in rocky soils and open habitats, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions.
