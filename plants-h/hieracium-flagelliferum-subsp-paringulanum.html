Hieracium flagelliferum subsp. paringulanum

Hieracium flagelliferum subsp.

paringulanum, commonly known as the Paringulanum hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It typically grows in grasslands and open habitats, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.

The plant features basal and cauline leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and are produced in late spring to early summer.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated flower stems and is often found in disturbed or nutrient-rich soils.
