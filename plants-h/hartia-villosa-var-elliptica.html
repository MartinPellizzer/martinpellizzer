Hartia villosa var. elliptica

Hartia villosa var. elliptica, commonly known as the woolly hartia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
