Helichrysum bracteatum

Helichrysum bracteatum, commonly known as the golden crown, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, golden-yellow inflorescences that resemble small crowns, giving it its common name. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its aromatic, slightly resinous scent. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, often covered in fine hairs.

Helichrysum bracteatum is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
