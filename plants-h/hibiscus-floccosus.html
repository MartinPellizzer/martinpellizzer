Hibiscus floccosus

Hibiscus floccosus, commonly known as the woolly hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its soft, white, woolly foliage and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for warm, arid climates. Its flowers bloom in summer and are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Hibiscus floccosus is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and landscapes.
