Hemianemia rosei

Hemianemia rosei, commonly known as the roseate hemianthus, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, rose-like flowers that bloom in spring. The plant prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers. Its foliage is typically a vibrant green, with some cultivars displaying variegated leaves.

Hemianemia rosei is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
