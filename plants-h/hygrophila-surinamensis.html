Hygrophila surinamensis

Hygrophila surinamensis, commonly known as the Surinam hygrophila, is a tropical aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bright green and arranged in whorls around the stem. This plant thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds. Its flowering stems produce small, white or pale yellow flowers, which are often borne in spikes.

Hygrophila surinamensis is popular in aquariums due to its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
