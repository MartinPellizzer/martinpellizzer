Hedyotis nukuhivensis

Hedyotis nukuhivensis, commonly known as the Polynesian mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Pacific Islands.

It is a low-growing herbaceous plant with small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in coastal or forested areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has cultural significance in some island communities.
