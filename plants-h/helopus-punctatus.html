Helopus punctatus

Helopus punctatus, commonly known as the spotted helopus, is a unique flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves with distinctive yellow and green markings. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, adding to its ornamental appeal. Helopus punctatus thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
