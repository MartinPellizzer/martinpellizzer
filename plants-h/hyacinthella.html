Hyacinthella

Hyacinthella, commonly known as the dwarf hyacinth, is a genus of flowering plants in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It consists of several species, most of which are native to southern Africa. These plants are characterized by their compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are usually strap-shaped and evergreen, providing a lush backdrop for the colorful blossoms.

Hyacinthella species are often cultivated for their ornamental value in gardens and as potted plants.
