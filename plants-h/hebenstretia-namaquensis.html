Hebenstretia namaquensis

Hebenstretia namaquensis, commonly known as the Namibian bellflower, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring, producing vibrant yellow or orange flowers that attract pollinators. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

Hebenstretia namaquensis is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
