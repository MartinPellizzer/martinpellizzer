Hyptis tricephala

Hyptis tricephala, commonly known as the three-headed hyptis, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowering period occurs during the warm seasons, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hyptis tricephala is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
