Hibiscus tiliaceus var. abutiloides

Hibiscus tiliaceus var. abutiloides, commonly known as the Indian mallow, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in coastal areas and is often used in traditional medicine.

Its adaptability to saline soils makes it a common ornamental and ecological plant in coastal regions.
