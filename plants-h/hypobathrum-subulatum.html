Hypobathrum subulatum

Hypobathrum subulatum, commonly known as the slender sandwort, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage. The plant features narrow, needle-like leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Its small, white or pale pink flowers appear in clusters during the warmer months, adding a delicate touch to its otherwise desolate habitat.

Hypobathrum subulatum is well adapted to sandy soils and can thrive in areas with minimal rainfall.
