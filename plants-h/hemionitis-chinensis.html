Hemionitis chinensis

Hemionitis chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese hemionitis, is a rare and unique fern native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spirally arranged, needle-like leaves that form a dense, compact rosette. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in mountainous regions with high humidity. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are located on the underside of the fronds, a feature typical of ferns.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Hemionitis chinensis is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
