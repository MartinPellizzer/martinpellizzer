Hermione pratensis

Hermione pratensis, commonly known as the meadow gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Gentianaceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in moist meadows and grasslands. The plant features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Hermione pratensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting biodiversity.
