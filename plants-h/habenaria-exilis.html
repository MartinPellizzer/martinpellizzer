Habenaria exilis

Habenaria exilis, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage and small, pale green or white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or mountainous regions. Its roots are often mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to enhance nutrient uptake.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
