Hieracium amplexicaule subsp. cadinense

Hieracium amplexicaule subsp.

cadinense, commonly known as the narrowleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and flowering stems that bear yellow disk flowers surrounded by ray florets.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. It has a taproot system and produces numerous seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various soil conditions, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
