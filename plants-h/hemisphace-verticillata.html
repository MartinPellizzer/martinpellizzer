Hemisphace verticillata

Hemisphace verticillata, commonly known as the spiral rush, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of leaves around its stem, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It typically grows in moist, swampy areas and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Hemisphace verticillata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some local cultures.
