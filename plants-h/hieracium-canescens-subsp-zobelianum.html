Hieracium canescens subsp. zobelianum

Hieracium canescens subsp.

zobelianum, commonly known as the Zobelian hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and yellow composite flowers that consist of both ray and disk florets.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. Native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, it thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

This subspecies is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a hardy, invasive species in some regions.
