Homeria vallisbelli

Homeria vallisbelli, commonly known as the valley bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family, characterized by its bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and produces clusters of blue or violet flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in a fine pubescence, enhancing its drought tolerance.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
