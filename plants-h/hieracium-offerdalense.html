Hieracium offerdalense

Hieracium offerdalense, commonly known as the Offerdal hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Norway.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are basal, rough, and hairy, often with a bluish-green hue, while its flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer.

Hieracium offerdalense is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in high-altitude environments.
