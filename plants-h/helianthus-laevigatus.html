Helianthus laevigatus

Helianthus laevigatus, commonly known as the smooth sunflower, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial with a robust stem and large, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are smooth to the touch, giving it its common name. Its flowering period typically occurs in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Helianthus laevigatus is often found in wetlands and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
