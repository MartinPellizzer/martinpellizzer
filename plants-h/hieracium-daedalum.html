Hieracium daedalum

Hieracium daedalum, commonly known as the devil's paintbrush, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and is characterized by its striking, spire-like inflorescence composed of numerous small yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodland habitats, preferring well-drained soils. It is an annual or biennial herb that can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
