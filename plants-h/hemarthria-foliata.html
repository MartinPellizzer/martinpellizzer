Hemarthria foliata

Hemarthria foliata, commonly known as the silverleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, silver-gray blades that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often clustered in dense, cylindrical clusters.

Hemarthria foliata is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland habitats.
