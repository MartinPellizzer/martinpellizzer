Hieracium schmidtii subsp. melaphyrogenes

Hieracium schmidtii subsp.

melaphyrogenes, commonly known as the Schmidt's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are usually serrated and hairy.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and sometimes endangered species.
