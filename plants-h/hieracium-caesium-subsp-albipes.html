Hieracium caesium subsp. albipes

Hieracium caesium subsp.

albipes, commonly known as the whitefoot hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. Its flowers are yellow with a white or pale yellow corolla, and it produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

The subspecies is distinguished by its white or pale-colored basal leaves, which give it its common name.
