Hieracium pyrinum

Hieracium pyrinum, commonly known as the Pyrenean hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium pyrinum is often found in open meadows and grasslands at high altitudes.
