Hugonia arborescens

Hugonia arborescens, commonly known as the red-veined dayflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with prominent red veins, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Hugonia arborescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in shaded areas.
