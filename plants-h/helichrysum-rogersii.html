Helichrysum rogersii

Helichrysum rogersii, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that remain vibrant even when dried. The plant produces small, tubular florets surrounded by ray florets, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its foliage is aromatic and often used in herbal remedies due to its medicinal properties.
