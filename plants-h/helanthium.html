Helanthium

Helanthium, commonly known as the marsh pennywort, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It typically features small, yellow flowers arranged in a cluster, and its leaves are usually heart-shaped or oval. These plants are often found in wet or marshy environments, thriving in moist soil conditions. Helanthium species are native to various regions in Europe, Asia, and Africa.

They are often used in ecological restoration projects due to their ability to stabilize soil and improve water quality.
