Hieracium levicaule subsp. sciaphiloides

Hieracium levicaule subsp.

sciaphiloides, commonly known as the shade-loving hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are usually grayish-green and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, often resembling those of other hawkweed species.

It has a fibrous root system and is adapted to thrive in low-light conditions.
