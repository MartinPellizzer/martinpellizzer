Hieracium lomostephum

Hieracium lomostephum, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disk.

Hieracium lomostephum is known for its ability to form dense colonies, often outcompeting other vegetation in its habitat.
