Hutchinia truncatocoronata

Hutchinia truncatocoronata, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically features velvety, dark green leaves with a waxy texture. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Hutchinia truncatocoronata is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
