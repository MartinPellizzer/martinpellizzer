Hoya stenakei

Hoya stenakei, commonly known as the "Coral Hoya," is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and red, which bloom in summer. Hoya stenakei thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

It is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its low maintenance and ornamental appeal.
