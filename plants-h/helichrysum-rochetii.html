Helichrysum rochetii

Helichrysum rochetii, commonly known as the golden marguerite, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that resemble tiny sunflowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value.

Helichrysum rochetii is also known for its long-lasting dried flowers, which are popular in floral arrangements.
