Hydrobryum vientianense

Hydrobryum vientianense, commonly known as the Vientiane water moss, is a rare aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes. The plant has a delicate, branched structure with small, translucent leaves that help it absorb light for photosynthesis. Its reproductive structures are adapted for dispersal in water, ensuring propagation in suitable habitats.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to survive.
