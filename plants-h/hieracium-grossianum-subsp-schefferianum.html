Hieracium grossianum subsp. schefferianum

Hieracium grossianum subsp.

schefferianum, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and showy yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and sunny exposures. It has hairy stems and leaves that are often glandular, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
