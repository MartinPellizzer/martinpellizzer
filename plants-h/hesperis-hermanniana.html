Hesperis hermanniana

Hesperis hermanniana, commonly known as the Hermann's lungwort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with characteristic four-petaled flowers. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of pink or purple flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Hesperis hermanniana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in gardens.
