Homoianthus capito

Homoianthus capito, commonly known as the Cape honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a rough texture.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.
