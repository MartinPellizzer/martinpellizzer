Hieracium laevigodentatum

Hieracium laevigodentatum, commonly known as the smooth-toothed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features basal leaves that are typically lobed and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often hairy. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the genus Hieracium.

It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and other open habitats.
