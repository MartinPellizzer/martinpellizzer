Helianthus × scaberrimus var. subrhomboideus

Helianthus × scaberrimus var. subrhomboideus, commonly known as the roughleaf sunflower, is a hybrid species within the sunflower genus.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and yellow, daisy-like flowers. This plant is native to the central and eastern United States, where it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
