Hieracium sabaudum subvar. gallicum

Hieracium sabaudum subvar. gallicum, commonly known as the Gallic hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture, and the flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
