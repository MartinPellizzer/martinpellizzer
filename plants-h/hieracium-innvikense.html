Hieracium innvikense

Hieracium innvikense, commonly known as the Innvik Hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of Europe and North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as tundra, heath, and rocky slopes. It has a low-growing habit with basal leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer.

Hieracium innvikense is adapted to cold climates and is often found in areas with poor soil conditions.
