Hypericum perforatum subsp. microphyllum

Hypericum perforatum subsp.

microphyllum, commonly known as the lesser St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its small, yellow-orange flowers and distinctive leaves with translucent dots.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its stems are usually prostrate or ascending, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

This subspecies is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
