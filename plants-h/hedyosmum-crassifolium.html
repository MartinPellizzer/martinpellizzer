Hedyosmum crassifolium

Hedyosmum crassifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaf hedyosmum, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, ovate leaves with a rough, hairy texture on both sides. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Hedyosmum crassifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in indigenous communities.
