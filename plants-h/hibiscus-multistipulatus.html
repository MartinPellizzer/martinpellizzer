Hibiscus multistipulatus

Hibiscus multistipulatus, commonly known as the many-stipule hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features showy, large, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has elongated, narrow leaves with prominent stipules along the leaf stems. Its flowering period typically occurs during the warmer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hibiscus multistipulatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
