Hedysarum mackete

Hedysarum mackete, commonly known as Mack's vetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Hedysarum mackete is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
