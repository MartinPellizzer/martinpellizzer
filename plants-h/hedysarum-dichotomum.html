Hedysarum dichotomum

Hedysarum dichotomum, commonly known as the forked vetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial legume that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features compound leaves with a distinctive forked appearance, and its flowers are usually pink to purple in color. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Hedysarum dichotomum plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in restoration projects.
