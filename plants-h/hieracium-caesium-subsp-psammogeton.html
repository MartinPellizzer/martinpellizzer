Hieracium caesium subsp. psammogeton

Hieracium caesium subsp.

psammogeton, commonly known as the sand hieracium, is a perennial flowering plant native to central and southern Europe. It typically grows in dry, sandy or gravelly soils, often in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in summer. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its daisy-like inflorescence.
