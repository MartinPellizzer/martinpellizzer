Himantochilus pubinervius

Himantochilus pubinervius, commonly known as the hairy-nerved tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves with hairy veins. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.

The tree thrives in humid, tropical climates and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
