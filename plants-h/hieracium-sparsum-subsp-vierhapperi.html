Hieracium sparsum subsp. vierhapperi

Hieracium sparsum subsp.

vierhapperi, commonly known as the Four-leaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped, rough to the touch, and often have a reddish or purplish hue along the edges.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer, producing small seeds that are dispersed by the wind.
