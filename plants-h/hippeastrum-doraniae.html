Hippeastrum doraniae

Hippeastrum doraniae, commonly known as the Dorania amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 inches in diameter. The plant produces strap-like, dark green leaves that are often broad and fleshy. It blooms in late spring or early summer, producing clusters of funnel-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or red.

Hippeastrum doraniae is a popular ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
