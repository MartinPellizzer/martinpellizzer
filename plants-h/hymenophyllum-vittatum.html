Hymenophyllum vittatum

Hymenophyllum vittatum, commonly known as the spotted maidenhair fern, is a small, delicate fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant has a unique structure with a central stem and two rows of small, scale-like leaves on either side. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on rocks or tree trunks in tropical and subtropical regions.

Hymenophyllum vittatum is known for its ability to retain moisture and its ornamental appearance, making it a popular choice for shaded garden settings.
