Huperzia macbridei

Huperzia macbridei, commonly known as the MacBride's spikemoss, is a species of fern ally native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Huperziaceae and is characterized by its compact, evergreen appearance with dense clusters of needle-like leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its sporophytic structures are well-developed, with sporangia arranged in clusters on the underside of the leaves.

Huperzia macbridei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticulture for its unique foliage.
