Helichrysum ceres

Helichrysum ceres, commonly known as the Cape golden bush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, sun-like flower heads. The plant typically grows in shrub form, with narrow, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The flowers are long-lasting and often used in dried floral arrangements due to their vibrant color and durability.
