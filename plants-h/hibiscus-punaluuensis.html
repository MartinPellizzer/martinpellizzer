Hibiscus punaluuensis

Hibiscus punaluuensis, commonly known as the Punaluu hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is often found growing in wet forest areas. Hibiscus punaluuensis is a member of the Malvaceae family, which includes other hibiscus species.

It plays an important role in the local ecosystem and is considered a culturally significant plant in Hawaiian traditions.
