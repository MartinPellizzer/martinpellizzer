Hymenophyllum podocarpon

Hymenophyllum podocarpon, commonly known as the rock holly, is a small, epiphytic fern that thrives in humid, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, hair-like structures. The plant belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae and is known for its intricate, lace-like appearance. It typically grows on rocks or tree trunks in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its unique morphology and specific habitat requirements, it is often considered a rare and specialized species.
