Hesperis hookeri

Hesperis hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's honesty, is a flowering plant native to the British Isles.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its small, white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and produces small, round seed pods that split open when mature. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Hesperis hookeri is often found in meadows and woodland edges, and it blooms from June to August.
