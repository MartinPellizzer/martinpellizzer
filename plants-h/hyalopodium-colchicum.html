Hyalopodium colchicum

Hyalopodium colchicum, commonly known as the red mountain lily, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces a single, showy flower per stem, which is often surrounded by narrow, grass-like leaves. Hyalopodium colchicum is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
