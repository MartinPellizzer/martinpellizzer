Hibiscus pusillus

Hibiscus pusillus, commonly known as the dwarf hibiscus, is a small flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink or red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its unique appearance. Hibiscus pusillus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
