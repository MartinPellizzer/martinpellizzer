Hieracium maculatum var. povedanum

Hieracium maculatum var. povedanum, commonly known as the Poveda's spotted hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers and hairy, lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. It produces small, oval seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread across various habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
