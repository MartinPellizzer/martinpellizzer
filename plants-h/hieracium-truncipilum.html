Hieracium truncipilum

Hieracium truncipilum, commonly known as the blunt-fruited hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while the flowers are yellow with a distinctive, short, bluntly tapered achene.

Hieracium truncipilum is known for its ability to thrive in various environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
