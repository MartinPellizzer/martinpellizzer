Hieracium vulgatum f. subfasciculare

Hieracium vulgatum f. subfasciculare, commonly known as the clustered hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its foliage is covered in fine, soft hairs, and the inflorescence consists of numerous small disk flowers surrounded by ray florets.

This subspecies is distinguished by its clustered arrangement of flowering stems, which gives it its specific epithet.
