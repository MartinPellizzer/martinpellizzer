Hedysarum malurense

Hedysarum malurense, commonly known as the Maluren vetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial legume that typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, and it has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Hedysarum malurense is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

Its ecological importance is significant in its native environment, where it contributes to biodiversity and stabilizes fragile ecosystems.
