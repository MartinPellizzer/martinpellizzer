Henrya grandifolia

Henrya grandifolia, commonly known as the Japanese snowbell, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are leathery and glossy, providing a striking contrast to its delicate blooms.

Henrya grandifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a specimen plant.
