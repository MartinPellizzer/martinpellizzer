Hieracium chondrillifolium subsp. comolepium

Hieracium chondrillifolium subsp.

comolepium, commonly known as the Mexican hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Mexico. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow discs and white to pink ray florets.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas and disturbed soils, exhibiting a spreading growth habit. It has rough, hairy stems and leaves that are often lobed or toothed.

This subspecies is notable for its potential as a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
