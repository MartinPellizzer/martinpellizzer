Hamamelis flavopurpurascens

Hamamelis flavopurpurascens, commonly known as the yellow-flowered witch hazel, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow or orange-red flowers that bloom in late autumn or early winter. The plant features narrow, elliptical leaves that turn yellow in the fall. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, often peeling in thin strips.

Hamamelis flavopurpurascens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its seasonal interest.
