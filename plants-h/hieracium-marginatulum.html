Hieracium marginatulum

Hieracium marginatulum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 50 centimeters. Its leaves are basal and hairy, with a distinctive lobed shape, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

Hieracium marginatulum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
