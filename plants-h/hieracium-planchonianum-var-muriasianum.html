Hieracium planchonianum var. muriasianum

Hieracium planchonianum var. muriasianum, commonly known as the Muriasian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy structure. The plant typically grows in rocky and grassy habitats at high elevations, where it forms dense mats. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and often have a reddish or purplish hue, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in clusters.

This variant is distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and morphological traits adapted to harsh mountain environments.
