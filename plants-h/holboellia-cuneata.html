Holboellia cuneata

Holboellia cuneata, commonly known as the narrowleaf holboellia, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbel-like inflorescences.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. The plant features finely divided, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in clusters that give it a delicate appearance.

Holboellia cuneata is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
