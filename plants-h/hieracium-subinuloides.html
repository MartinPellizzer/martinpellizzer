Hieracium subinuloides

Hieracium subinuloides, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features upright, hairy stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped with serrated edges. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, blooming from late spring to early autumn.

Hieracium subinuloides is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it can spread aggressively through its rhizomes.
