Hesperis subsinuata

Hesperis subsinuata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly curled, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
