Hieracium scabrum var. virgatum

Hieracium scabrum var. virgatum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It has serrated leaves and produces achenes as its seeds.

Due to its invasive tendencies, it is often considered a weed in agricultural and natural areas.
