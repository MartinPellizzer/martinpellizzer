Helianthus parishii var. coloradensis

Helianthus parishii var. coloradensis, commonly known as the Colorado sunflower, is a native perennial plant of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its yellow to orange flowering heads and hairy, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is found in the southwestern United States. It produces numerous small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is well adapted to arid environments and has a compact, upright growth habit.
