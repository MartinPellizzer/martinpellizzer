Hesperis semperflorens

Hesperis semperflorens, commonly known as the perennial wallflower, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, blooming from late spring to early summer. The plant has linear, gray-green leaves that are aromatic and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ability to reseed itself makes it a hardy and long-lasting garden plant.
