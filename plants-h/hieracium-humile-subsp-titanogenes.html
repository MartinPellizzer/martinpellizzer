Hieracium humile subsp. titanogenes

Hieracium humile subsp.

titanogenes, commonly known as the small hawkweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically forms dense mats of slender, hairy stems and produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters.

The plant is characterized by its finely divided, grass-like leaves and its ability to thrive in disturbed soils. It is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along roadsides.

Due to its hardy nature, it is considered a common weed in many regions.
