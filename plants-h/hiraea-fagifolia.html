Hiraea fagifolia

Hiraea fagifolia, commonly known as the European hiraea, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Ericaceae.

It is a shrub that typically grows in acidic soils, often found in heathlands and forest edges across Europe. The plant features small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are dark green, leathery, and arranged in a dense, compact form.

Hiraea fagifolia is notable for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor conditions, making it an important component of its native ecosystems.
