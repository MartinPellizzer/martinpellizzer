Hieracium eriopogon

Hieracium eriopogon, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and basal rosettes of dark green, rough-textured leaves. The plant produces yellow to orange composite flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in moist meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
