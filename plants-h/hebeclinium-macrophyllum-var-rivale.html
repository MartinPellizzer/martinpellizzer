Hebeclinium macrophyllum var. rivale

Hebeclinium macrophyllum var. rivale, commonly known as the river hebe, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It features upright, slender stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a glossy green surface. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and long flowering period.
