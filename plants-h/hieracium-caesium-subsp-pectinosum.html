Hieracium caesium subsp. pectinosum

Hieracium caesium subsp.

pectinosum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow flowers.

The plant typically has hairy stems and leaves with a rough texture, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of yellow disk flowers surrounded by pale green to white ray florets.

It thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.
