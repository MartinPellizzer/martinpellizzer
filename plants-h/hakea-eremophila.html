Hakea eremophila

Hakea eremophila, commonly known as the spinifex hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in arid conditions. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. It is well-suited to sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal and inland heathlands.

Hakea eremophila is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in low-water environments.
