Homoglossum quadrangulare

Homoglossum quadrangulare, commonly known as the four-angle tongue plant, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique quadrangular stems and small, clustered white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are usually opposite and have a rough, textured surface.

Homoglossum quadrangulare is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
