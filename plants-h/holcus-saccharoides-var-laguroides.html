Holcus saccharoides var. laguroides

Holcus saccharoides var. laguroides, commonly known as the dwarf sugargrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, grassy habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in wet conditions and its preference for nutrient-rich soils.

Botanically, it is classified within the Poaceae family, which includes other grasses.
