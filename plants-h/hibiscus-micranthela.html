Hibiscus micranthela

Hibiscus micranthela, commonly known as the small-flowered hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features showy, five-petaled flowers that are usually pink to red in color, with a prominent central stamen. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate, often with a hairy texture.

Hibiscus micranthela is known for its ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates, making it a popular ornamental plant.
