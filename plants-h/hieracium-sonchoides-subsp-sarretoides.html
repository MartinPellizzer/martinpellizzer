Hieracium sonchoides subsp. sarretoides

Hieracium sonchoides subsp.

sarretoides, commonly known as the Sarret's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are alternate, rough, and often covered with glandular dots, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
