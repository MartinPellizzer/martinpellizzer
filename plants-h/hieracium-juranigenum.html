Hieracium juranigenum

Hieracium juranigenum, commonly known as the Jura hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It typically grows in moist, grassy habitats such as meadows and forest edges. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in late summer. Its flowers are composed of numerous small florets arranged in a disk, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Hieracium juranigenum is often found in alpine and subalpine zones, where it thrives in cooler, humid conditions.
