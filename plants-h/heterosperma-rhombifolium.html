Heterosperma rhombifolium

Heterosperma rhombifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf water milfoil, is a perennial aquatic plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. The plant has distinctive rhomboid-shaped leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. Its flowers are small and clustered, with stamens that are often longer than the petals.

Heterosperma rhombifolium plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
