Heptaca latifolia

Heptaca latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf heptaca, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or stream banks. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in terminal inflorescences, and are typically white or pale green.

Heptaca latifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
