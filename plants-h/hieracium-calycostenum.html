Hieracium calycostenum

Hieracium calycostenum, commonly known as the thimbleweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, hairy stems and basal rosette of leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish flower heads that are typically clustered in a single terminal inflorescence. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in moist meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and often invasive species in certain regions.
