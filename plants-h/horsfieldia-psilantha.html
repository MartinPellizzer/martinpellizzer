Horsfieldia psilantha

Horsfieldia psilantha, commonly known as the white-flowered orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the rainy season. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding environment.

Horsfieldia psilantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
