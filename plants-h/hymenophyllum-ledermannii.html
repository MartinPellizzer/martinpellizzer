Hymenophyllum ledermannii

Hymenophyllum ledermannii, commonly known as the Ledermann's filmy fern, is a small, epiphytic fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent, and often lace-like fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or rocks in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in specialized structures called sporangia.

This species is noted for its unique morphology and is often found in moist, shaded forest habitats.
