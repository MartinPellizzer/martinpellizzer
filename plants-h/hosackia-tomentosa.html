Hosackia tomentosa

Hosackia tomentosa, commonly known as the wooly false indigo, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish foliage and its tall, spindly stems that produce clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are typically visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

Hosackia tomentosa is often found in moist, open habitats like meadows and woodland edges.
