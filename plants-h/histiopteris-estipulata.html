Histiopteris estipulata

Histiopteris estipulata, commonly known as the fringed wood fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that are deeply divided and often have a frayed or fringed appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Histiopteris estipulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
