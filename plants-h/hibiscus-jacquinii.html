Hibiscus jacquinii

Hibiscus jacquinii, commonly known as the scarlet hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, pink to red flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Hibiscus jacquinii thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
