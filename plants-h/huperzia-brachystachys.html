Huperzia brachystachys

Huperzia brachystachys, commonly known as the short-stalked huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and is native to regions of Southeast Asia, including China, India, and Indonesia. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its sporophylls are arranged in dense clusters, and it produces spores in specialized structures called sporangia.

Huperzia brachystachys is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its lush, dark green appearance.
