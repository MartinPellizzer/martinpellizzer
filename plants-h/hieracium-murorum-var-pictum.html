Hieracium murorum var. pictum

Hieracium murorum var. pictum, commonly known as the painted hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features bright yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and the stems are often reddish in color.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant appearance.
