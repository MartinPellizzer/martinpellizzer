Hyssopus cinereus

Hyssopus cinereus, commonly known as gray hyssop, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces spikes of pale blue to violet flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Hyssopus cinereus is valued for its aromatic properties and historical use in herbal medicine.
