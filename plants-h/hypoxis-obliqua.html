Hypoxis obliqua

Hypoxis obliqua, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a tuberous root system and produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers that open at night. The plant has lance-shaped, grass-like leaves that are often hairy beneath. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Hypoxis obliqua is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
