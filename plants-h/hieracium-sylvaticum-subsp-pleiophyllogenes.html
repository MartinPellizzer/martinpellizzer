Hieracium sylvaticum subsp. pleiophyllogenes

Hieracium sylvaticum subsp.

pleiophyllogenes, commonly known as the forest dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and along stream banks.

The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are usually hairy and lance-shaped. Its flowers are yellow and borne in a single head, followed by a distinctive pappus of bristles for wind dispersal.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal uses in traditional herbalism.
