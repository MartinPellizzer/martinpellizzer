Hypericum barbatum f. epirotum

Hypericum barbatum f. epirotum, commonly known as the Epirotic St. John's Wort, is a rare and ornamental variant of the St. John's Wort genus.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features opposite, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a valuable species for botanical collections and conservation efforts.
