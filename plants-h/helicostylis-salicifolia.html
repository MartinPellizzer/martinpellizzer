Helicostylis salicifolia

Helicostylis salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaf helicostylis, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as sedges.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, showcasing its adaptability to moist conditions. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in a triangular cross-section, a characteristic feature of many sedges. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like flowers, which are usually green or brown in color.

Helicostylis salicifolia plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and semi-aquatic organisms.
