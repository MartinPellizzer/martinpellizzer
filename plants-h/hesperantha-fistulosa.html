Hesperantha fistulosa

Hesperantha fistulosa, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, spiky flower spikes that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow, adding vibrant color to gardens. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette, and its stems are often hollow, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Hesperantha fistulosa is known for its long-lasting blooms, which can last several months during the growing season.
