Hedypnois furfuracea

Hedypnois furfuracea, commonly known as the hairy tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces woody pods that contain edible seeds, which are used in traditional cuisine. Hedypnois furfuracea is also valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in local remedies.

Its ability to thrive in poor soils makes it an important plant in ecological restoration efforts.
