Hybanthus nyassensis

Hybanthus nyassensis, commonly known as the African bluebell, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Hybanthus nyassensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
