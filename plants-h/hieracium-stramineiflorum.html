Hieracium stramineiflorum

Hieracium stramineiflorum, commonly known as the slender yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of tubular and ray florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while the flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disc.

It is an herbaceous perennial that spreads through rhizomes and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
