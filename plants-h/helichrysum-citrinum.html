Helichrysum citrinum

Helichrysum citrinum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that remain colorful even after drying, making it popular in floral arrangements. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and features narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, often growing in rocky or sandy environments.

Its resilience and ornamental value make it a favored choice for gardens and dried flower crafts.
