Helichrysum zwartbergense

Helichrysum zwartbergense, commonly known as the Zwartberg everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, golden-yellow flowers that resemble tiny suns. The plant produces papery, aromatic bracts that persist after the flowers have faded, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for horticultural use.
