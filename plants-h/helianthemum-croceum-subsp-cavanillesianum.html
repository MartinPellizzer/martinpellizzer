Helianthemum croceum subsp. cavanillesianum

Helianthemum croceum subsp.

cavanillesianum, commonly known as the Cavanillesian wallflower, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It features dense, compact foliage with small, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in racemes along the stems. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in Mediterranean climates.
