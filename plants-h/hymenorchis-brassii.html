Hymenorchis brassii

Hymenorchis brassii, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a short, horizontal rhizome and produces pseudobulbs that bear leaves. The plant's flowers are typically yellow or golden in color, with a distinctive spurred lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Hymenorchis brassii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
