Hieracium leucopilotum

Hieracium leucopilotum, commonly known as the white-haired hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are hairy and often have a bluish-gray hue, while its flowers are yellow with white ray florets.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
