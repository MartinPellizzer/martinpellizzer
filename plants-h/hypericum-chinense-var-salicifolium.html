Hypericum chinense var. salicifolium

Hypericum chinense var. salicifolium, commonly known as the willow-leaf St. John's wort, is a shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, and it produces small, reddish-brown fruits.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
