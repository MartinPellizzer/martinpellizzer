Hymenanthera dentata var. alpina

Hymenanthera dentata var. alpina, commonly known as the Alpine honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it an important species in high-altitude ecosystems.
