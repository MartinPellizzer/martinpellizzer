Heliocereus elegantissimus var. helenae

Heliocereus elegantissimus var. helenae, commonly known as the Helen's elegant night-blooming cereus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem and produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night. The plant is a member of the Cactaceae family and is known for its striking appearance and unique blooming pattern. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
