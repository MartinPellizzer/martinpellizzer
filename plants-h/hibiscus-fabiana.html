Hibiscus fabiana

Hibiscus fabiana, commonly known as the Sierra hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a prominent central stigma. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually palmately lobed, and the plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Hibiscus fabiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
