Hoodia husabensis

Hoodia husabensis, commonly known as the desert cabbage, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom during the rainy season. The plant has adapted to extreme drought conditions by storing water in its thick, fleshy leaves. It is also known for its unique ability to produce a milky sap that has been studied for potential medicinal properties.

Hoodia husabensis plays an important role in the ecosystems of its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various desert wildlife.
