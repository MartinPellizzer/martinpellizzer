Hyacinthus glaucus

Hyacinthus glaucus, commonly known as the blue hyacinth, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces striking clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers in early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers are also used in perfumery due to their strong, sweet scent.
