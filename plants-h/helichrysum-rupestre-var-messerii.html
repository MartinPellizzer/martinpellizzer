Helichrysum rupestre var. messerii

Helichrysum rupestre var. messerii, commonly known as the golden everlasting or rock everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its clusters of bright yellow, daisy-like flowers. The plant produces small, papery bracts that give it a feathery appearance, often mistaken for true petals. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
