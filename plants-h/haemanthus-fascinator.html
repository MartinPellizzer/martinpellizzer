Haemanthus fascinator

Haemanthus fascinator, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features tall, spiky inflorescences that bear bright orange to red tubular flowers, which are particularly showy in late summer and autumn. The plant has narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are typically evergreen and form a dense rosette at the base. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The botanical name reflects its dramatic floral display, which resembles a flaming poker.
