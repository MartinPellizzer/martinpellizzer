Hordeum brachyatherum

Hordeum brachyatherum, commonly known as the shortawn barley, is a species of grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its compact growth habit and short, stiff awns on its florets. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides. Its botanical features include narrow, grass-like leaves and spikelets that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Hordeum brachyatherum is valued for its resilience and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
