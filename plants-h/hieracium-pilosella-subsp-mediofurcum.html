Hieracium pilosella subsp. mediofurcum

Hieracium pilosella subsp.

mediofurcum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm.

The plant features basal rosettes of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and bears yellow flowers in elongated inflorescences. Its flowers are composed of 8 to 12 ray florets surrounded by a ring of tubular disc florets.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly elongated flower heads and hairy stem and leaves.
