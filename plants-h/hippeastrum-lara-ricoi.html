Hippeastrum lara-ricoi

Hippeastrum lara-ricoi, commonly known as the Lara-Rico amaryllis, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and white. The plant produces a bulbous stem that stores nutrients, supporting its growth and flowering cycle. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often forming a rosette at the base.

Hippeastrum lara-ricoi is cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-draining soil with adequate sunlight.
