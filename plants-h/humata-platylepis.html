Humata platylepis

Humata platylepis, commonly known as the broad-leaved humata, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, flat leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
