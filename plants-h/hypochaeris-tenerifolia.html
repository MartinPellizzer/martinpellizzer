Hypochaeris tenerifolia

Hypochaeris tenerifolia, commonly known as the Canary Islands hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and covered in fine white pubescence. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Hypochaeris tenerifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
