Hypericum scaturiginum

Hypericum scaturiginum, commonly known as yellow hypericum, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is often found in woodland edges, hedgerows, and moist soils, and it has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
