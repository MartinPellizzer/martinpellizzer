Helichrysum gariepinum

Helichrysum gariepinum, commonly known as the Cape gold flower, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that resemble golden stars, which are arranged in dense, compact inflorescences. The plant has silvery-green, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Helichrysum gariepinum is also valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
