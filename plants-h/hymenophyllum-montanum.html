Hymenophyllum montanum

Hymenophyllum montanum, commonly known as the mountain fern, is a small, delicate fern belonging to the Hymenophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its intricate, lace-like fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. This epiphytic plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing on rocks or tree trunks in mountainous regions. Its leaves are thin and translucent, with a unique structure that allows for efficient water retention.

Hymenophyllum montanum is found in temperate regions of the Americas and is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological significance.
