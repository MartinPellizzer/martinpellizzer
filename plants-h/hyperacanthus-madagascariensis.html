Hyperacanthus madagascariensis

Hyperacanthus madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar spurge, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble petals. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with thick, fleshy stems and alternate, lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by the showy bracts, which attract pollinators.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
