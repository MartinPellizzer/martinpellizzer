Hosta longifolia

Hosta longifolia, commonly known as the longflower hosta, is a perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a metallic sheen. The plant produces upright, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, bell-shaped white flowers. Hosta longifolia thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

It is a popular choice for shade gardens due to its ornamental foliage and modest flowering display.
