Helleborus odorus subsp. multifidus

Helleborus odorus subsp.

multifidus, commonly known as the fragrant hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe. It is characterized by its evergreen, dark green leaves that are deeply lobed and often have a waxy texture.

The plant produces clusters of nodding, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more intricate leaf divisions compared to other hellebore species.
