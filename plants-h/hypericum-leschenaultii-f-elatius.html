Hypericum leschenaultii f. elatius

Hypericum leschenaultii f. elatius, commonly known as the golden St. John's wort, is a compact shrub characterized by its yellow flowers and dense, evergreen foliage.

It belongs to the family Hypericaceae and is native to the Mediterranean region, particularly thriving in rocky and well-drained soils. The plant features small, rounded leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive appearance and ability to attract pollinators.

Due to its compact growth habit, it is suitable for rock gardens and as a border plant in gardens with full sun.
