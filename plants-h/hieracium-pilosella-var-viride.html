Hieracium pilosella var. viride

Hieracium pilosella var. viride, commonly known as green mouse-ear hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, basal rosette of leaves and small, yellowish-green flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides. Its leaves are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize open areas, making it both a common weed and an ecological indicator plant.
