Hemigenia glabrescens

Hemigenia glabrescens, commonly known as the small-leaved honey mint, is a perennial herb native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and understoreys. Its small, lance-shaped leaves are covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple.

Hemigenia glabrescens is valued for its fragrant foliage and is often used in native gardens.
