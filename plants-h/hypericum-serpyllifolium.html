Hypericum serpyllifolium

Hypericum serpyllifolium, commonly known as creeping St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and a sprawling growth habit, often forming dense mats on the ground. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Hypericum serpyllifolium is notable for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its purported healing benefits.
