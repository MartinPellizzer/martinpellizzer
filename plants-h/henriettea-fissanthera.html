Henriettea fissanthera

Henriettea fissanthera, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble clusters of tiny flowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its showy yellow-orange inflorescences bloom in late summer. Henriettea fissanthera thrives in well-drained, moist soils and is often found in shaded forest understories.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
