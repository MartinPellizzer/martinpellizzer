Hieracium prenanthoides subsp. loriense

Hieracium prenanthoides subsp.

loriense, commonly known as the Loriense hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. It produces yellow ray florets surrounded by tubular disc florets, which are adapted for pollination by various insects.

The subspecies is distinguished by its leaf morphology and floral structure, setting it apart from other members of the Hieracium genus.
