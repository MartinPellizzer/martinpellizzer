Helianthus giganteus subsp. alienus

Helianthus giganteus subsp.

alienus, commonly known as the giant sunflower, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its towering height, often reaching up to 10 feet, and its large, showy yellow flower heads that resemble the sun.

The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture, and its flowering period typically occurs in late summer to early fall. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

The species is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
