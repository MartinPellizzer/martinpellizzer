Hibiscus maximus

Hibiscus maximus, commonly known as the giant hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a yellow or orange throat. Hibiscus maximus thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

Its botanical features make it a popular ornamental plant in tropical gardens.
