Hieracium obscuriceps

Hieracium obscuriceps, commonly known as the dark-headed hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads made up of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a dark center.

It is known for its aggressive growth and can become invasive in certain regions.
