Hieracium maculatum var. picturatum

Hieracium maculatum var. picturatum, commonly known as the painted dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble petals. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a distinctive pattern of darker markings.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
