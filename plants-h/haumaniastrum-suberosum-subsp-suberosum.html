Haumaniastrum suberosum subsp. suberosum

Haumaniastrum suberosum subsp.

suberosum, commonly known as the desert tamarisk, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its dense, woody structure and small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss in hot, dry environments.

The plant produces small, pale green to yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It has a deep root system that allows it to access groundwater, making it highly drought-tolerant.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various desert wildlife species.
