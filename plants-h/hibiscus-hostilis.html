Hibiscus hostilis

Hibiscus hostilis, commonly known as the Texas hibiscus, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically blooming in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for arid environments. Hibiscus hostilis thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for certain wildlife.
