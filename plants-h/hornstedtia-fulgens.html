Hornstedtia fulgens

Hornstedtia fulgens, commonly known as the fiery hornstedtia, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range from orange to red, often with a contrasting blue or purple throat. Its foliage consists of opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like birds and bees.
