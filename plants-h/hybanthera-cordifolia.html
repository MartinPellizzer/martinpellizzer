Hybanthera cordifolia

Hybanthera cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf hybanthera, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which give it its common name, and its clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Hybanthera cordifolia is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
