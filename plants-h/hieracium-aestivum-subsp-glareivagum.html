Hieracium aestivum subsp. glareivagum

Hieracium aestivum subsp.

glareivagum, commonly known as the summer hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its yellowish-orange composite flowers and hairy stems, which are typical features of the genus Hieracium.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along streambanks, preferring temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its root system is fibrous and helps it establish in various soil types.

This subspecies is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed habitats, contributing to its widespread distribution across Europe.
