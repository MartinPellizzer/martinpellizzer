Hieracium kinkellense

Hieracium kinkellense, commonly known as the Kinkell's hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rough texture and a serrated edge.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
