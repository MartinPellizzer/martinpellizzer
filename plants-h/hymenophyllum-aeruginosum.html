Hymenophyllum aeruginosum

Hymenophyllum aeruginosum, commonly known as the copper moss, is a small, evergreen fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, bright green to copper-colored fronds that are often covered in a fine, waxy layer. This moss thrives in shaded, moist environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its intricate, lace-like structure and vibrant coloration make it a striking addition to humid, shaded habitats.

The plant's unique appearance and ecological role contribute to its significance in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
