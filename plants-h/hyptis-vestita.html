Hyptis vestita

Hyptis vestita, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant foliage and long-lasting blooms, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Hyptis vestita is also valued for its medicinal properties, with traditional uses in treating respiratory and digestive ailments.
