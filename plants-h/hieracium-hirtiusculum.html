Hieracium hirtiusculum

Hieracium hirtiusculum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads made up of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are hairy and lance-shaped, and the stems are often reddish in color.

Hieracium hirtiusculum is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and can sometimes become invasive in certain regions.
