Homalomena gaudichaudii

Homalomena gaudichaudii, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and spadix inflorescence. The plant features a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread and form dense colonies. Its foliage is typically large, heart-shaped, and can reach up to 1 meter in length.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of starch.
