Hieracium sericophyllum subsp. leucocomum

Hieracium sericophyllum subsp.

leucocomum, commonly known as the white-haired hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its finely hairy stems and leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the genus Hieracium. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and protected species in some regions.
