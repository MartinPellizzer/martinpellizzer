Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp. epimecodon

Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp.

epimecodon, commonly known as the Wiesbaur's hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to reddish flower heads.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. It has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces numerous small, clustered flowers.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
