Hieracium columnare var. septentrionale

Hieracium columnare var. septentrionale, commonly known as the northern columnar hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to northern regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height, often growing in dense clusters. The plant produces yellow composite flowers, which are arranged in a head composed of many small florets. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
