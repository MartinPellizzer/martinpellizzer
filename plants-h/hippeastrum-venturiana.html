Hippeastrum venturiana

Hippeastrum venturiana, commonly known as the Venturian amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces large, strap-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. Hippeastrum venturiana is a bulbous plant that thrives in well-drained soil and requires a period of dormancy to produce flowers.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
