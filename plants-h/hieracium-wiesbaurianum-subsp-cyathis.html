Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp. cyathis

Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp.

cyathis, commonly known as the cyathis hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, pastures, and woodland edges. It features basal and stem leaves that are usually lanceolate or ovate with hairy surfaces.

Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the genus Hieracium.
