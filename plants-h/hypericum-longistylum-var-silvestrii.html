Hypericum longistylum var. silvestrii

Hypericum longistylum var. silvestrii, commonly known as the wild St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers, which bloom in late summer, and its opposite, narrow leaves. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas. It belongs to the genus Hypericum, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their medicinal properties.

The varietal form silvestrii is distinguished by its more robust growth and slightly different floral structure compared to the typical form.
