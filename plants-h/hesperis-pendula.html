Hesperis pendula

Hesperis pendula, commonly known as the hanging horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pendulous flowers that hang from slender stems, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with a distinctive horn-like structure at the base, which gives it its common name. Hesperis pendula thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other flowering plants such as mustard and cabbage.
