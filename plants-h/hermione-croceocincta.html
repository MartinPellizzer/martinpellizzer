Hermione croceocincta

Hermione croceocincta, commonly known as the golden girdled orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to the southeastern United States.

This herbaceous perennial features a compact, upright growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its most distinctive characteristic is its showy, golden-yellow flowers that are adorned with a dark red or purple band around the base of the petals. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to woodland habitats.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for native gardens.
