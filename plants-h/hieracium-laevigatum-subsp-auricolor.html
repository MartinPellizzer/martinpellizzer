Hieracium laevigatum subsp. auricolor

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

auricolor, commonly known as the golden hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which bloom from late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it can reach heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are hairy and have a rough texture, while its stems are upright and often branched.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
