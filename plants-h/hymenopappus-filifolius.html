Hymenopappus filifolius

Hymenopappus filifolius, commonly known as the silky haircup, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive bracts. The plant features narrow, silky-haired leaves and produces small, yellowish flower heads that bloom in late spring. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
