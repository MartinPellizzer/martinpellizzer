Hieracium semicurvatum

Hieracium semicurvatum, commonly known as the bent-leaf hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Hieracium semicurvatum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
