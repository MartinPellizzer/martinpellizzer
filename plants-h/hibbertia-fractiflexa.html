Hibbertia fractiflexa

Hibbertia fractiflexa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hibbertia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a low-growing shrub with woody stems and elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to full sun conditions.

Hibbertia fractiflexa is part of the Dilleniaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
