Hieracium diphyllum

Hieracium diphyllum, commonly known as the two-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rough, hairy texture, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer.

Hieracium diphyllum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
