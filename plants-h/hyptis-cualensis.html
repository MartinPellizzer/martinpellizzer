Hyptis cualensis

Hyptis cualensis, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a perennial shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant is noted for its gray-green, hairy foliage and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Hyptis cualensis is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
