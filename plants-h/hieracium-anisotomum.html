Hieracium anisotomum

Hieracium anisotomum, commonly known as the banded hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often hairy leaves that are deeply lobed and have a serrated edge. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is commonly found in meadows and along stream banks.

Hieracium anisotomum is known for its aggressive growth and can become invasive in certain regions.
