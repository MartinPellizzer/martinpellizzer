Hippeastrum nelsonii

Hippeastrum nelsonii, commonly known as the Nelson's amaryllis, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features strap-like leaves that are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native arid environment. Its bulbous stem is a key feature, supporting the heavy flower spikes.

Hippeastrum nelsonii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in warm, sunny conditions.
