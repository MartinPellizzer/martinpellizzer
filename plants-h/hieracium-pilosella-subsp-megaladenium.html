Hieracium pilosella subsp. megaladenium

Hieracium pilosella subsp.

megaladenium, commonly known as the giant meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lanceolate, with hairy surfaces, and it produces yellow flowers in dense clusters.

The subspecies is distinguished by its larger flower heads and more robust growth compared to other Hieracium species.
