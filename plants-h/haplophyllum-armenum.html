Haplophyllum armenum

Haplophyllum armenum, commonly known as Armenian rockrose, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with small, leathery leaves that are typically grayish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
