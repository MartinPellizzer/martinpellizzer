Humboltia acuminata

Humboltia acuminata, commonly known as the desert hibiscus, is a shrub native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from yellow to red, often blooming in summer. The plant has rough, hairy leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water.

Humboltia acuminata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
