Hookera bicolor

Hookera bicolor, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a striking alpine plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue and white petals that resemble small bells, which give it its common name. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in the Andes Mountains. Hookera bicolor has a compact growth habit with hairy stems and leaves that are adapted to withstand harsh mountain conditions.

Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it a notable species in botanical studies.
