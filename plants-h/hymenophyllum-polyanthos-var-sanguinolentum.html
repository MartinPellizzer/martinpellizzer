Hymenophyllum polyanthos var. sanguinolentum

Hymenophyllum polyanthos var. sanguinolentum, commonly known as the red-staining maidenhair fern, is a delicate and rare variety of fern characterized by its finely divided, lance-shaped fronds.

The plant belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia. Its leaves are typically bright green with a distinctive red or reddish-brown staining on the underside, which becomes more pronounced when wet. The fronds are usually short and compact, growing in dense clusters on shaded, moist rock surfaces.

This variety is prized for its unique coloration and is often cultivated in specialized botanical gardens.
