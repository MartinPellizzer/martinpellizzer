Hymenocallis erosa

Hymenocallis erosa, commonly known as the hairy hurricane lily, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, strap-like leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to yellow. The plant produces a fragrant, spathaceous inflorescence that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in moist, open habitats.

Hymenocallis erosa is a perennial herb that is relatively easy to cultivate in warm climates.
