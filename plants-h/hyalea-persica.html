Hyalea persica

Hyalea persica, commonly known as the Persian glass flower, is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its translucent, waxy leaves that resemble glass, giving it a unique and ethereal appearance. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, adding to its ornamental value. Hyalea persica thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its delicate nature, it is often cultivated in specialized botanical gardens and is considered a challenging plant to grow in home settings.
