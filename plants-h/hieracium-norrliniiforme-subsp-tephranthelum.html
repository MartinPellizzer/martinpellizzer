Hieracium norrliniiforme subsp. tephranthelum

Hieracium norrliniiforme subsp.

tephranthelum, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments.

The plant has serrated leaves and produces yellow flower heads that are clustered in a flat-topped arrangement. It is characterized by its hairy stems and small, inconspicuous flowers.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
