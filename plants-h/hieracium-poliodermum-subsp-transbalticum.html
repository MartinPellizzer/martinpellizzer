Hieracium poliodermum subsp. transbalticum

Hieracium poliodermum subsp.

transbalticum, commonly known as the Baltic hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 50 cm.

The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its flowering stems are often hairy, and the leaves are usually glandular-dotted, which is a characteristic feature of the genus Hieracium.

This subspecies is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions and is often found in temperate climates.
