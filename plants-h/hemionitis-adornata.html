Hemionitis adornata

Hemionitis adornata, commonly known as the velvetleaf fern, is a unique aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety fronds that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in shallow, still waters and is often found in swampy or marshy habitats. Its rhizomes form a dense network that helps stabilize the substrate and supports a diverse ecosystem.

Hemionitis adornata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and ability to thrive in moist environments.
