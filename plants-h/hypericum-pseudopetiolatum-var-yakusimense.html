Hypericum pseudopetiolatum var. yakusimense

Hypericum pseudopetiolatum var. yakusimense, commonly known as the Yakusimense St. John's Wort, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its stems are often reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
