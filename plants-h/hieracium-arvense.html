Hieracium arvense

Hieracium arvense, commonly known as field hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and can form dense colonies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium arvense is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
