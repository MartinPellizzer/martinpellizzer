Homoioceltis

Homoioceltis, commonly known as the sea holly, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flower heads, which are surrounded by spiny, leaf-like bracts. The plants typically grow in coastal areas and are adapted to salty, windy environments. They have a rosette growth habit with narrow, silvery leaves.

Homoioceltis species are often used in gardens for their ornamental value and hardiness.
