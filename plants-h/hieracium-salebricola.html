Hieracium salebricola

Hieracium salebricola, commonly known as the medicinal hawk's-beard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf structures. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are usually rough and hairy, with a basal rosette formation, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a composite inflorescence.

Hieracium salebricola has been historically used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and healing properties.
