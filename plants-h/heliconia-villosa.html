Heliconia villosa

Heliconia villosa, commonly known as the hairy heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its large, colorful inflorescences that resemble a bird's nest. The plant features broad, waxy leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically orange or red, with a distinctive three-lobed structure.

Heliconia villosa is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and ability to thrive in humid, warm climates.
