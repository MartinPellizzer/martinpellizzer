Hieracium aepymetes

Hieracium aepymetes, commonly known as the tall hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. Native to Europe, it is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides.

It is considered a herbaceous perennial that can spread through rhizomes and is sometimes invasive in certain regions.
