Hieracium sylvularum

Hieracium sylvularum, commonly known as the wood hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and along stream banks. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with serrated edges, and the flowers are arranged in a single head.

Hieracium sylvularum is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich soils and is often found in areas with high humidity.
