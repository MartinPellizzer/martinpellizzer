Hieracium alatum subsp. chondroseoides

Hieracium alatum subsp.

chondroseoides, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It features a rosette of soft, hairy, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine white pubescence.

The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
