Hibiscus amazonicus

Hibiscus amazonicus, commonly known as the Amazonian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are broad and ovate, often with a rough texture, and its flowers bloom throughout the wet season.

Hibiscus amazonicus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
