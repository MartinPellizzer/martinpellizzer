Hedyotis psychotrioides

Hedyotis psychotrioides, commonly known as the blue false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct scent. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Hedyotis psychotrioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
