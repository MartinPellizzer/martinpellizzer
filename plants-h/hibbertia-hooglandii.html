Hibbertia hooglandii

Hibbertia hooglandii, commonly known as the Sturt's Desert Pea, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive crimson or orange flowers with a black or purple central blotch, which are borne on slender, hairy stems. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is adapted to survive in harsh, low-nutrient soils. Hibbertia hooglandii is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters.

It plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing nectar for native pollinators.
