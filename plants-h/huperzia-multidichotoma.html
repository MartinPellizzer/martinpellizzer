Huperzia multidichotoma

Huperzia multidichotoma, commonly known as the forked moss huperzia, is a species of fern-like plant belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit and its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. The plant features small, scale-like leaves that are often arranged in a dense, mat-forming structure. It is native to regions of Southeast Asia, including countries like China, India, and Indonesia.

Huperzia multidichotoma plays a role in forest ecosystems by contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for microorganisms.
