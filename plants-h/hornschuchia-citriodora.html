Hornschuchia citriodora

Hornschuchia citriodora, commonly known as the lemon-scented hornschuchia, is a unique member of the Ericaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves that emit a citrus-like fragrance. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is native to specific regions in South America.
