Heteropogon contortus var. hirtus

Heteropogon contortus var. hirtus, commonly known as the hairy basket grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy stems, which give it its common name. The plant produces dense, cylindrical inflorescences that are often used in floral arrangements. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

This variety is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.
