Heracleum pyrenaicum subsp. cordatum

Heracleum pyrenaicum subsp.

cordatum, commonly known as the Pyrenean cow parsley, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbellate inflorescences and aromatic foliage.

The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of silky hairs. Its flowers are white or pale yellow, forming flat-topped clusters that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is known for its strong scent and has been used historically in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
