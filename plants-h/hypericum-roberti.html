Hypericum roberti

Hypericum roberti, commonly known as the Robert's St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, often in clusters at the ends of its stems. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate with a slightly hairy texture.

Hypericum roberti is often found in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in pine flatwoods.
