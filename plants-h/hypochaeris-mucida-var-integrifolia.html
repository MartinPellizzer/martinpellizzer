Hypochaeris mucida var. integrifolia

Hypochaeris mucida var. integrifolia, commonly known as the hairy false dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are typically lance-shaped and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often found in disturbed soils and open areas, thriving in a variety of climates.

Its growth habit is herbaceous, with a low-growing form that spreads by stolons.
