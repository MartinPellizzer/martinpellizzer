Hippophae rhamnoides subsp. carpatica

Hippophae rhamnoides subsp.

carpatica, commonly known as the Carpathian sea buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Carpathian Mountains. It is characterized by its yellow-orange berries, which are rich in vitamins and antioxidants.

The plant has small, oval leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers in the spring. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role and has been used in traditional medicine and as a source of edible fruits.
