Heuchera rubescens var. truncata

Heuchera rubescens var. truncata, commonly known as the red-leaf heuchera, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features striking, heart-shaped leaves that are typically red or maroon in color, with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered white or pink flowers in the spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for shade gardens.

Its compact growth habit and vibrant foliage make it a versatile and eye-catching addition to woodland or border plantings.
