Hieracium lanatum var. andryaloides

Hieracium lanatum var. andryaloides, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is native to Europe and has been introduced to other regions, including North America. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its fibrous root system and rapid growth make it a common weed in various habitats.
