Hosta pulchella

Hosta pulchella, commonly known as the white-flowered hosta, is a perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a silvery underside. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers in late summer, which are borne on tall, slender stems. Hosta pulchella thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

It is a popular ornamental plant in gardens due to its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
