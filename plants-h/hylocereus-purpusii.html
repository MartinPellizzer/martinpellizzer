Hylocereus purpusii

Hylocereus purpusii, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a climbing cactus native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its fleshy, segmented stems and its ability to climb using tendrils. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night, a feature that attracts nocturnal pollinators. Its fruit, known as the dragon fruit, is edible and has a distinctive appearance with bright colors and a sweet flavor.

The plant is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
