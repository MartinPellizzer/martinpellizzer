Hieracium krafftianum

Hieracium krafftianum, commonly known as the Krafft's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, thriving in rocky and well-drained soils. Its leaves are basal, rough, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head.

It is often found in mountainous areas where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
