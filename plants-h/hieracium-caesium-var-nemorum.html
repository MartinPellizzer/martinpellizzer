Hieracium caesium var. nemorum

Hieracium caesium var. nemorum, commonly known as mouse-ear hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

This variant is often found in temperate regions of Europe and is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and seeds.
