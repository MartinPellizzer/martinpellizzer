Hibbertia stricta var. hirtiflora

Hibbertia stricta var. hirtiflora, commonly known as the hairy-flowered hibbertia, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces yellow flowers with prominent, showy stamens, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

Hibbertia stricta var. hirtiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
