Huperzia engleri

Huperzia engleri, commonly known as the Engler's clubmoss, is a species of evergreen fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is native to regions in Southeast Asia, including countries like China, India, and Thailand. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its structure consists of a short, upright stem with spirally arranged leaves that are often lance-shaped.

Huperzia engleri is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in horticulture for its unique foliage.
