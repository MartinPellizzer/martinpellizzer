Habenaria cirrhata

Habenaria cirrhata, commonly known as the pink lady's tresses, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, spirally arranged leaves and its striking pink or purple floral spikes. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its flowers are borne in a loose raceme and are often visited by bees and other pollinators.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
