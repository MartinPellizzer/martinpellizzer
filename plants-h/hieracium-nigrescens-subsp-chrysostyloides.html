Hieracium nigrescens subsp. chrysostyloides

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

chrysostyloides, commonly known as the golden-stem hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellowish-green leaves.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

Its subspecies status reflects subtle differences in morphology, particularly in the color and structure of its flowering stems.
