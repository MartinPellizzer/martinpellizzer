Hieracium laevigatum subsp. jutlandicum

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

jutlandicum, commonly known as the Jutland hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern Europe. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sandy soils.

The plant has dark green, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in late summer. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in coastal regions.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the genus Hieracium.
