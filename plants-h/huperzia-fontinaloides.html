Huperzia fontinaloides

Huperzia fontinaloides, commonly known as the rock huperzia, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its simple, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. This plant is often found growing on rocky substrates in moist, shaded environments. Huperzia fontinaloides is notable for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and its preference for humid climates.

Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are located on the underside of the leaves, contributing to its unique botanical appearance.
