Heliconia spathocircinata subsp. spathocircinata

Heliconia spathocircinata subsp.

spathocircinata, commonly known as the red-spotted heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America. It is characterized by its large, colorful bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its inflorescence is a prominent feature, with a long, fleshy spadix surrounded by vibrant, often red or orange bracts.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates.
