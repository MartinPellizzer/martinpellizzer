Hieracium levicaule subsp. oligochristum

Hieracium levicaule subsp.

oligochristum, commonly known as the narrowleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and lanceolate, with a rough texture, and its flowers are yellow, forming dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

This subspecies is known for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
