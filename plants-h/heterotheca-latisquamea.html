Heterotheca latisquamea

Heterotheca latisquamea, commonly known as the broad-scaled coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a prominent central cone.

Heterotheca latisquamea is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats, where it plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
