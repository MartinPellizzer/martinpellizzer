Hordeanthos tripaleus

Hordeanthos tripaleus, commonly known as the three-lobed sandbloom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive three-lobed leaves and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas. It is a perennial species that produces a compact rosette of foliage.

Hordeanthos tripaleus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
