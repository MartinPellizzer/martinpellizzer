Helichrysum parviflorum var. latifolium

Helichrysum parviflorum var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf everlasting, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The foliage and flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their long-lasting nature.
