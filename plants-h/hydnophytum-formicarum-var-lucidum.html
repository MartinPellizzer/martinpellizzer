Hydnophytum formicarum var. lucidum

Hydnophytum formicarum var. lucidum, commonly known as the ant plant, is a tropical epiphytic species belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring hollow stems that serve as shelters for ants. The plant forms a symbiotic relationship with ants, providing them with shelter in exchange for protection against herbivores and assistance in seed dispersal. This species is native to Southeast Asia and is often found growing on tree trunks in humid, shaded environments.
