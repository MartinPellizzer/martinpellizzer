Helictotrichon desertorum var. planitierum

Helictotrichon desertorum var. planitierum, commonly known as the desert needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce minute, wind-dispersed seeds. The plant is adapted to harsh, drought-prone environments, with deep root systems that allow it to access water from lower soil layers. It plays a vital role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing forage for wildlife and livestock.

Its botanical features reflect its evolutionary adaptation to extreme desert conditions.
