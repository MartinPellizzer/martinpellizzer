Hybanthus hespericlivus

Hybanthus hespericlivus, commonly known as the white-flowered hybanthus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a small, herbaceous annual that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, white, tubular flowers. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological role.

The plant's botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for study in tropical botany.
