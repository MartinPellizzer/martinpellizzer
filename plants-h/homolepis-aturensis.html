Homolepis aturensis

Homolepis aturensis, commonly known as the golden star fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually bright green with a yellowish or golden hue on the underside, giving it its common name.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in hanging baskets or as a decorative foliage plant.
