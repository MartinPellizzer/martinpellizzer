Hypericum hypericoides var. multicaule

Hypericum hypericoides var. multicaule, commonly known as the clustered St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces bright yellow flowers in late summer. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Its medicinal properties, similar to other Hypericum species, have been studied for their potential anti-inflammatory and antidepressant effects.
