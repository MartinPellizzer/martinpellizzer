Hypericum rubropunctatum

Hypericum rubropunctatum, commonly known as the red-spotted St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the family Hypericaceae and is characterized by its erect, herbaceous growth habit. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, surrounded by distinctive red or orange glands. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and covered with fine hairs.

Hypericum rubropunctatum is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
