Heterospathe sphaerocarpa

Heterospathe sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the globular spathed plant, is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its unique floral structure.

It features a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe, which is typically brightly colored to attract pollinators. The plant is native to tropical regions and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The fruit is a fleshy, spherical structure that contains multiple seeds, aiding in its dispersal by animals.
