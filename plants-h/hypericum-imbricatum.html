Hypericum imbricatum

Hypericum imbricatum, commonly known as the creeping St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters along the stems. The plant has opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or woodland habitats.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used for various therapeutic purposes.
