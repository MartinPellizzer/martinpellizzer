Hieracium saxifragum subsp. venustum

Hieracium saxifragum subsp.

venustum, commonly known as the showy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats.

The plant features a rosette of basal leaves and produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its inflorescence consists of a single, showy flower head with ray florets.

The subspecies is distinguished by its larger, more vibrant flowers compared to other members of the species.
