Hieracium rubicundiforme

Hieracium rubicundiforme, commonly known as the red-veined hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with red veins, which are typically arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. The plant has hairy, upright stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grasslands.

Hieracium rubicundiforme is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
