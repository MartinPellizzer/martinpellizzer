Hibbertia intermedia

Hibbertia intermedia, commonly known as the yellow wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and slender, woody stems. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds.

Hibbertia intermedia is often found in coastal and inland regions of southern Australia.
