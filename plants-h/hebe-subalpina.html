Hebe subalpina

Hebe subalpina, commonly known as the alpine hebe, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a member of the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically produces blue or purple flowers that bloom in spring and early summer. Hebe subalpina thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its hardy nature allows it to withstand colder temperatures, making it a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.
