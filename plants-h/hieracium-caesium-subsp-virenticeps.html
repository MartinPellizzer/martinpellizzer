Hieracium caesium subsp. virenticeps

Hieracium caesium subsp.

virenticeps, commonly known as the green-tipped hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow to orange composite flowers.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are hairy and often reddish in color. This subspecies is distinguished by its elongated, green-tipped bracts that give it its common name.

It thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.
