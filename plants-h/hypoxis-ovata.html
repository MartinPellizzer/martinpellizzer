Hypoxis ovata

Hypoxis ovata, commonly known as the False Shamrock, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a waxy coating. The plant produces yellow flowers in umbel-like clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value and ease of cultivation have made it a popular choice among gardeners.
