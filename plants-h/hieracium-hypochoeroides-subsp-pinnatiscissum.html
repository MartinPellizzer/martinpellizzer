Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp. pinnatiscissum

Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp.

pinnatiscissum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring moist to moderately dry conditions. Its leaves are narrow and pinnately divided, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are visited by various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
