Hieracium sabinum atrocrinitum

Hieracium sabinum atrocrinitum, commonly known as the black-horned hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features dark green, broadly lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and prominent veins. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and bloom from late spring to early autumn.

It thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies.
