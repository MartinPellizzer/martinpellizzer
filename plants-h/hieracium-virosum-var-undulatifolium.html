Hieracium virosum var. undulatifolium

Hieracium virosum var. undulatifolium, commonly known as the wavy-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellowish-orange composite flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped with a wavy or undulating margin, giving it its specific epithet. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grasslands.

This plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
