Harrisia tortuosa var. uruguayensis

Harrisia tortuosa var. uruguayensis, commonly known as the Uruguay prickly pear, is a cacti species native to South America.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces edible fruits that are often used in local cuisine. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and agricultural purposes in its native range.
