Helia alata

Helia alata, commonly known as the sunflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flower heads surrounded by green bracts. The plant grows up to 3 meters tall with a robust stem and broad, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

The seeds produced are widely used for food and oil production.
