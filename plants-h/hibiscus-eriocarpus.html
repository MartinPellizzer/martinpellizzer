Hibiscus eriocarpus

Hibiscus eriocarpus, commonly known as the woolly hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink or red. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that are often covered in a fine, woolly texture, giving it its common name. Hibiscus eriocarpus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
