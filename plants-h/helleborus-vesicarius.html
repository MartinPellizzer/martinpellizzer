Helleborus vesicarius

Helleborus vesicarius, commonly known as the bladder hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its dark green, waxy leaves that are often marked with distinctive white veins. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodlands and rocky areas.

Helleborus vesicarius is noted for its toxic properties, containing various alkaloids that can be harmful if ingested.
