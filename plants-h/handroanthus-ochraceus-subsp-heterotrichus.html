Handroanthus ochraceus subsp. heterotrichus

Handroanthus ochraceus subsp.

heterotrichus, commonly known as the yellow trumpet tree, is a tropical plant native to South America. It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling trumpets.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are typically ovate and glossy, contributing to its ornamental value.

The species is valued for both its aesthetic appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
