Hepetis dendroidea

Hepetis dendroidea, commonly known as the tree hepatica, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmately lobed leaves and showy, early spring blooms that are typically blue or purple. The plant thrives in moist, wooded habitats and is often found in forest understories. Its flowers have five petals and are often accompanied by five sepals that are similar in color.

Hepetis dendroidea plays an important role in early pollination, attracting bees and other pollinators.
