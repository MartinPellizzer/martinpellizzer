Hyssopus seravschanicus

Hyssopus seravschanicus, commonly known as the Afghan hyssop, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant features square stems and opposite leaves that are often hairy and fragrant. Its small, tubular flowers are typically blue or violet, arranged in whorled clusters.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
