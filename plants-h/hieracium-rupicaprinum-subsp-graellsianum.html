Hieracium rupicaprinum subsp. graellsianum

Hieracium rupicaprinum subsp.

graellsianum, commonly known as the Graellsian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in rocky or limestone-rich soils, often in alpine or subalpine regions.

The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are usually grayish-green with a woolly texture. It produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
