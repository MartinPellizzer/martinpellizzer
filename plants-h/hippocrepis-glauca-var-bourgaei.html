Hippocrepis glauca var. bourgaei

Hippocrepis glauca var. bourgaei, commonly known as the Bourgai's blue gentian, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it grows in rocky or limestone soils. It is a member of the Gentianaceae family, known for its showy, often blue or purple flowers.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
