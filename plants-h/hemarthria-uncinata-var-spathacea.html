Hemarthria uncinata var. spathacea

Hemarthria uncinata var. spathacea, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, hairless blades and compact inflorescences that produce small, spike-like clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and wetlands, and is adapted to thrive in nutrient-rich soils. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense stands, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

The species is also valued for its potential in erosion control and as a forage crop for livestock.
