Huperzia biformis

Huperzia biformis, commonly known as the two-lobed huperzia, is a species of moss-like fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its simple, strap-shaped leaves that are often divided into two lobes, giving it its common name. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Huperzia biformis is known for its ability to thrive in humid climates and is often found in temperate regions.

Its structure and growth habits make it an important component of certain ecosystems.
