Hibiscus partitus

Hibiscus partitus, commonly known as the white hibiscus, is a species native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are usually broad and smooth. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Hibiscus partitus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
