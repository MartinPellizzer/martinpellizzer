Heliopsis helianthoides

Heliopsis helianthoides, commonly known as the blazing star, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright, branching stems. The plant produces showy, bright yellow to orange-red flower heads that resemble sunflowers, composed of numerous small, tubular florets. Its leaves are lance-shaped, alternate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Heliopsis helianthoides thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens and pollinator habitats.
