Hieracium patagiarium

Hieracium patagiarium, commonly known as the Patagonian hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open areas, thriving in temperate climates. It has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy, and its yellow flowers bloom in summer.

Hieracium patagiarium is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
