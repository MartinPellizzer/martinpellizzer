Heliopsis laevis

Heliopsis laevis, commonly known as the smooth heliopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its daisy-like composite flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands and along stream banks, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its yellow-orange flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Heliopsis laevis is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological benefits and adaptability.
