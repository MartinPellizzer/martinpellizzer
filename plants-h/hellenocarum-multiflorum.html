Hellenocarum multiflorum

Hellenocarum multiflorum, commonly known as the Greek bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems covered in narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of delicate, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators.
