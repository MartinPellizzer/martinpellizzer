Hypolytrum latifolium var. minus

Hypolytrum latifolium var. minus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland or swampy habitats, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. It has fibrous roots and is often found in association with other wetland flora.

The species is noted for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
