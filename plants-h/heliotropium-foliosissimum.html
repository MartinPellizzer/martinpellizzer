Heliotropium foliosissimum

Heliotropium foliosissimum, commonly known as the many-leaved heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of small, fragrant purple flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are opposite and often deeply lobed, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Heliotropium foliosissimum is often cultivated for its attractive appearance and is used in gardens for its visual interest and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
