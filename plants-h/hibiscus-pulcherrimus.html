Hibiscus pulcherrimus

Hibiscus pulcherrimus, commonly known as the Hawaiian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, with five to seven petals. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight. It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 feet tall, with dark green, heart-shaped leaves.

Hibiscus pulcherrimus is also known for its rapid growth and ability to bloom throughout the year in suitable conditions.
