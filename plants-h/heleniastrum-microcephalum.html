Heleniastrum microcephalum

Heleniastrum microcephalum, commonly known as the small-headed heleniastrum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
