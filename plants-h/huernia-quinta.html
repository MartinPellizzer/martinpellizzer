Huernia quinta

Huernia quinta, commonly known as the Ghost Plant, is a succulent belonging to the Asclepiadaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly Namibia and South Africa, where it grows in arid and rocky environments. The plant is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers. Its unique appearance, resembling ghostly shapes, gives it its common name.

Huernia quinta is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a popular choice for xerophytic gardens.
