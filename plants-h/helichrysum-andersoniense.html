Helichrysum andersoniense

Helichrysum andersoniense, commonly known as the Anderson's everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellow to orange flowers that are often arranged in dense, compact clusters. The plant is known for its long-lasting flowers, which retain their color and shape even after drying, making it popular in floral arrangements. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its scientific name reflects its unique botanical features and geographic origin.
