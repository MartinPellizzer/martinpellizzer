Helianthus dissectifolius

Helianthus dissectifolius, commonly known as the dissectedleaf sunflower, is a species native to the southern United States.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its tall, branching stems and large, daisy-like yellow flowers. The leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, feathery appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
