Hugonia tomentosa

Hugonia tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly hugonia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, or tomentose, stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a broad, ovate shape.

Hugonia tomentosa is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
