Hieracium atroadenioides

Hieracium atroadenioides, commonly known as the Atroadene-like hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and sunny exposures. Its leaves are basal, rough, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disc.

Hieracium atroadenioides is known for its resilience to cold climates and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
