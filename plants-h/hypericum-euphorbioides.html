Hypericum euphorbioides

Hypericum euphorbioides, commonly known as the yellow-stemmed St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its stems are distinctly yellow and become woody with age.

Hypericum euphorbioides is often found in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and streambanks.
