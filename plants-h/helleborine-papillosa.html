Helleborine papillosa

Helleborine papillosa, commonly known as the common helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and produces striking, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive hooded structure. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist, shaded woodlands. It thrives in woodland floors and is known for its ability to form dense colonies.

Helleborine papillosa is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
