Hieracium laggeri subsp. orthorhizon

Hieracium laggeri subsp.

orthorhizon, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe. It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

The plant features a rosette of basal leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in late summer. Its stems are usually hairy and can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and is often found in rocky or grassy alpine meadows.
