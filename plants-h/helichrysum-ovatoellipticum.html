Helichrysum ovatoellipticum

Helichrysum ovatoellipticum, commonly known as the oval-elliptic immortelle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Helichrysum ovatoellipticum is also valued for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
