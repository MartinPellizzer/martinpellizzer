Habracanthus xanthothrix

Habracanthus xanthothrix, commonly known as the yellow-hair grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with long, narrow leaves and small, yellowish-green inflorescences. Native to southern Africa, this plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. Its fibrous roots help it retain moisture in dry conditions, making it well adapted to its native habitat.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and for its ornamental value in landscaping.
