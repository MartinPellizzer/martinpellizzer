Helictotrichon pubescens proles anatolicum

Helictotrichon pubescens proles anatolicum, commonly known as the Anatolian hairy grass, is a perennial grass species native to Turkey.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy leaves, which are covered in fine pubescence. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous florets.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the study of Mediterranean flora and grassland ecosystems.
