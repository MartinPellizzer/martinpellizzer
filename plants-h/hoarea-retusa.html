Hoarea retusa

Hoarea retusa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved horehound, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Hoarea retusa is often found in rocky or dry soils, thriving in well-drained environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological studies and traditional herbal practices.
