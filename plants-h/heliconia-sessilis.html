Heliconia sessilis

Heliconia sessilis, commonly known as the upright heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its large, colorful bracts that resemble petals. The plant produces inflorescences that are typically upright, hence its common name, and these structures house small, inconspicuous flowers. Its leaves are broad and sword-shaped, often with a waxy texture, and the plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Heliconia sessilis thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
