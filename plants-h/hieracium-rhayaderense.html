Hieracium rhayaderense

Hieracium rhayaderense, commonly known as the Rhayader hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and sometimes endangered species.
