Hemizonia increscens subsp. villosa

Hemizonia increscens subsp.

villosa, commonly known as the hairy hemizonia, is a perennial herb native to South America. It typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils.

The plant features slender, upright stems covered in fine, hairy hairs, which give it its subspecies name. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or purple, blooming in late summer. Hemizonia increscens subsp.

villosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
