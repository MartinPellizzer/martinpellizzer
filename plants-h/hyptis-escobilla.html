Hyptis escobilla

Hyptis escobilla, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically blue or purple, arranged in dense clusters, and bloom during the summer months.

Hyptis escobilla is often grown as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and fragrant properties.
