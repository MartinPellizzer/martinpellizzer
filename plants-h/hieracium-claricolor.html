Hieracium claricolor

Hieracium claricolor, commonly known as the clear-colored hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features bright yellow ray florets surrounded by a ring of pale yellow disk florets, giving it a striking appearance. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.

Hieracium claricolor is often found in open habitats and can spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in some areas.
