Hypericum chilense

Hypericum chilense, commonly known as the Chilean St. John's Wort, is a perennial shrub native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from August to October. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Hypericum chilense is also valued for its ornamental appeal and mild medicinal properties.
