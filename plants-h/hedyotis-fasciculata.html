Hedyotis fasciculata

Hedyotis fasciculata, commonly known as the small flame vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a trailing or climbing herb that produces small, bright yellow flowers clustered in fascicles, which are dense, cylindrical clusters. The plant has slender, wiry stems and lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Hedyotis fasciculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
