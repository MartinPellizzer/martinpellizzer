Helianthemum mathezii

Helianthemum mathezii, commonly known as the Mathez's rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub with woody stems and small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited to arid environments.

Helianthemum mathezii is often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions where it contributes to the local flora diversity.
