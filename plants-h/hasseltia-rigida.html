Hasseltia rigida

Hasseltia rigida, commonly known as the rigid hasseltia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright, branching stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique structure and ornamental flowers make it a valuable addition to native plant collections.
