Hieracium rivulare

Hieracium rivulare, commonly known as the water buttercup, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with a central disc and surrounding ray florets. The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as stream banks and wet meadows. It has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Hieracium rivulare is known for its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils, making it a common species in riparian zones.
