Hesperis linifolia

Hesperis linifolia, commonly known as sweet rocket, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces showy clusters of pink or purple flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

Hesperis linifolia is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
