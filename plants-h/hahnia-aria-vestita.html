Hahnia aria vestita

Hahnia aria vestita, commonly known as the velvet tamarisk, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its deep root system allows it to thrive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water.

Hahnia aria vestita is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
