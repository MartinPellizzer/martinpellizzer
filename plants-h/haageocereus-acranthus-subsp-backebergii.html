Haageocereus acranthus subsp. backebergii

Haageocereus acranthus subsp.

backebergii, commonly known as the Backeberg candelabra cactus, is a unique succulent native to South Africa. It is characterized by its branched, cylindrical stems that form a tree-like structure, often with a single main stem and multiple offshoots.

The plant produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers during the winter months, adding ornamental value. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its slow growth rate and striking appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
