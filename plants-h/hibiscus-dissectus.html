Hibiscus dissectus

Hibiscus dissectus, commonly known as the dissected hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The leaves are deeply lobed and have a delicate, fern-like appearance, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This species is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and as a cut flower.
