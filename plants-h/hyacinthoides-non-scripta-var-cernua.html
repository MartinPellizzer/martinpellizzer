Hyacinthoides non-scripta var. cernua

Hyacinthoides non-scripta var. cernua, commonly known as the nodding bluebell, is a flowering plant native to western Europe.

It is characterized by its drooping, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in woodland floors and thrives in partial shade with well-drained soil. It produces slender, grass-like leaves and has a lifespan of several years.
