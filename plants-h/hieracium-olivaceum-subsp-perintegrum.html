Hieracium olivaceum subsp. perintegrum

Hieracium olivaceum subsp.

perintegrum, commonly known as the olive hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish-green foliage and small, daisy-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas and disturbed soils, exhibiting a low-growing habit. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy surface, while its flowers are arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in meadows and along roadsides.
