Hibiscus ternatus var. halophilus

Hibiscus ternatus var. halophilus, commonly known as the salt marsh hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of China and Korea.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves and showy, pink to red flowers that bloom during the summer months. This variant is adapted to saline environments, showcasing its halophilic nature. The plant thrives in brackish water and muddy soils, making it a common sight in estuaries and tidal flats.

Its ability to tolerate high salt concentrations makes it an important species in coastal ecosystems.
