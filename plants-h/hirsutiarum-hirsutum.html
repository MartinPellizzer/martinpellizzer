Hirsutiarum hirsutum

Hirsutiarum hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or violet in color. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds.

Hirsutiarum hirsutum is native to Europe and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
