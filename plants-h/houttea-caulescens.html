Houttea caulescens

Houttea caulescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed houttea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that often display a reddish hue, especially when young. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and usually have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Houttea caulescens is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks.
