Hieracium trilacense

Hieracium trilacense, commonly known as the three-lobed hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are heart-shaped with three distinct lobes, and it produces yellow ray flowers surrounded by a dense array of disk flowers.

Hieracium trilacense is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in various habitats.
