Hamamelis japonica var. japonica

Hamamelis japonica var. japonica, commonly known as the Japanese witch hazel, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late autumn or early winter. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a serrated edge.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant in temperate climates.
