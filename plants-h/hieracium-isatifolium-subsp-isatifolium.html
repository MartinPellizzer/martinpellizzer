Hieracium isatifolium subsp. isatifolium

Hieracium isatifolium subsp.

isatifolium, commonly known as the blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, with blue to purple flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is notable for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
