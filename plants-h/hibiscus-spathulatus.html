Hibiscus spathulatus

Hibiscus spathulatus, commonly known as the broadleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range from pink to deep red, with a distinctive spatulate-shaped calyx. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often hairy on the underside. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Hibiscus spathulatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
