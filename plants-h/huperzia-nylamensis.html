Huperzia nylamensis

Huperzia nylamensis, commonly known as the Nylam fern, is a species of clubmoss found in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves that are often arranged in a compact, rosette-like pattern. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are typically clustered on the underside of the leaves. Huperzia nylamensis thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptability make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
