Hippocratea rubiginosa var. minor

Hippocratea rubiginosa var. minor, commonly known as the lesser red-hued hippocratea, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep red. The plant has elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces distinctive, fragrant blooms during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
