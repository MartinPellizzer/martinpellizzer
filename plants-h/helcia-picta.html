Helcia picta

Helcia picta, commonly known as the painted helcia, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that display a range of hues, including red, orange, and yellow, which intensify with age. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Helcia picta thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is a sought-after plant in specialized botanical gardens and among horticultural enthusiasts.
