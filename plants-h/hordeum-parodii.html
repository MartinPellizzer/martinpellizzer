Hordeum parodii

Hordeum parodii, commonly known as the Parodi's barley, is a species of grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, elongated leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are typically greenish or brownish in color. Hordeum parodii is often found in arid or semi-arid regions, growing in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

It is not widely cultivated for agricultural purposes but is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptability.
