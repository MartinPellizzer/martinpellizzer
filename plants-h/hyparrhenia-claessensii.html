Hyparrhenia claessensii

Hyparrhenia claessensii, commonly known as the African grass or red grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are typically reddish in color, especially when young. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Hyparrhenia claessensii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop in some regions.
