Hibiscus capillipes

Hibiscus capillipes, commonly known as the slender hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and large, showy flowers that typically range from pink to red in color. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Hibiscus capillipes thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
