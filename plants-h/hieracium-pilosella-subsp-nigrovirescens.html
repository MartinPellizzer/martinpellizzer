Hieracium pilosella subsp. nigrovirescens

Hieracium pilosella subsp.

nigrovirescens, commonly known as the blackish-green hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are hairy and lance-shaped, while the flowers are yellow with a central disc.

It is often considered a weed in agricultural areas due to its rapid growth and ability to spread easily.
