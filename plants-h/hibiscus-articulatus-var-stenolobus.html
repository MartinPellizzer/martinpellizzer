Hibiscus articulatus var. stenolobus

Hibiscus articulatus var. stenolobus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in wet, swampy areas and thrives in humid climates. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, flat seeds.

Hibiscus articulatus var. stenolobus is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
