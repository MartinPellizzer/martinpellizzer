Helichrysum cespitosum var. longifolium

Helichrysum cespitosum var. longifolium, commonly known as the longleaf helichrysum, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color and arranged in dense tufts. The plant produces small, yellow to golden flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a drought-tolerant choice for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular ornamental plant in horticulture.
