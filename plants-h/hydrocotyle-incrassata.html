Hydrocotyle incrassata

Hydrocotyle incrassata, commonly known as the broadleaf marsh penny, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland areas in North America.

It features a rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common sight in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Hydrocotyle incrassata plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
