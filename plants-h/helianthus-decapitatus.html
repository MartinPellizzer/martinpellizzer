Helianthus decapitatus

Helianthus decapitatus, commonly known as the dwarf sunflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features a compact growth habit with upright, hairy stems and palmate leaves that are deeply lobed. The plant produces clusters of yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and native garden landscapes.
