Hoffmannia aroensis

Hoffmannia aroensis, commonly known as the Aro Mountains hoffmannia, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Aro Mountains in Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing a robust root system adapted to nutrient-poor soils. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in clusters.

Hoffmannia aroensis is valued for its potential traditional uses in local medicine.
