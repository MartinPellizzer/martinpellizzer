Helichrysum obtusifolium

Helichrysum obtusifolium, commonly known as the woolly sunflower or yellow everlasting, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It features compact, mat-forming growth with dense clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that resemble sunflowers. The leaves are typically gray-green, covered in fine, soft hairs, giving the plant a woolly appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

The plant is also valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional medicine.
