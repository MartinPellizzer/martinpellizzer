Hyptis

Hyptis, commonly known as blue hyssop, is a genus of flowering plants in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

These plants are native to the Americas and are characterized by their aromatic leaves and showy, often blue or purple flowers. Many species of Hyptis are shrubs or herbs, often growing in tropical and subtropical regions. They are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes used in traditional medicine.

The leaves are often used in teas and have a slightly minty, camphor-like scent.
