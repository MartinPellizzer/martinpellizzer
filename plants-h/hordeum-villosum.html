Hordeum villosum

Hordeum villosum, commonly known as hairy barley, is a wild grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted inflorescence and hairy stems, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Hordeum villosum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil.
