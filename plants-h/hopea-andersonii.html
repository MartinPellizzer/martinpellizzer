Hopea andersonii

Hopea andersonii, commonly known as the Andes hopea, is a species of flowering plant in the family Dipterocarpaceae.

It is native to the western slopes of the Andes in South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall and is characterized by its buttressed roots and smooth, gray bark. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a leathery texture with a prominent midrib.

The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers followed by woody, three-lobed fruits that contain a single seed.
