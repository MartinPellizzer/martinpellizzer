Heuchera gracilis

Heuchera gracilis, commonly known as the slender coral bells, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white. The plant has finely cut, serrated leaves that are typically green or variegated with red or purple hues. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from both its vibrant foliage and delicate floral display.
