Huperzia thwaitesii

Huperzia thwaitesii, commonly known as the mountain huperzia, is a species of clubmoss native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and distinctive, spirally arranged leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its sporophylls are arranged in whorls and produce spores for reproduction.

Huperzia thwaitesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
