Hosackia heermannii var. orbicularis

Hosackia heermannii var. orbicularis, commonly known as the roundleaf prairie clover, is a flowering plant native to the central United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its orbicular, or rounded, leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink to reddish in color. It thrives in prairie and open woodland habitats, often in well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its use in native plant landscaping.
