Hypericum microlicioides

Hypericum microlicioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged alternately along its stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and herbal practices.
