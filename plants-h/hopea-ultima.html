Hopea ultima

Hopea ultima, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall, with a straight trunk and broad, leathery leaves that are dark green in color. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, while its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

Hopea ultima plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
