Heuchera ciliata

Heuchera ciliata, commonly known as the velvetleaf alumroot, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, velvety foliage that ranges in color from deep burgundy to silver-green, depending on the variety. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the spring. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Heuchera ciliata is valued for its ornamental leaves and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
