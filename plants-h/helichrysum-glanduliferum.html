Helichrysum glanduliferum

Helichrysum glanduliferum, commonly known as the golden crown or honeycomb bush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers that resemble tiny sunbursts. The plant has aromatic, hairy leaves that emit a distinctive scent when crushed. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
