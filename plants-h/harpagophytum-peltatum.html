Harpagophytum peltatum

Harpagophytum peltatum, commonly known as the bowstring hemp, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems, trifoliate leaves, and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces distinctive, hooked fruits that aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Harpagophytum peltatum is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its anti-inflammatory and pain-relieving compounds.
