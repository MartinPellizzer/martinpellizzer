Helianthus tephrodes

Helianthus tephrodes, commonly known as the sandplain sunflower, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its robust stem and large, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in full sunlight. Its leaves are alternate, rough, and covered in fine hairs, while its flower heads are surrounded by a series of ray flowers.

Helianthus tephrodes plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in open, disturbed habitats.
