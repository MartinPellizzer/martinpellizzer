Hieracium pulveraceum

Hieracium pulveraceum, commonly known as the dusty hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are usually rough and hairy, giving it a dusty appearance, and its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is an annual or biennial plant that produces a large number of small seeds, which are dispersed by the wind.
