Hieracium clinoglossum

Hieracium clinoglossum, commonly known as the bent-tongue hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It features basal leaves that are lanceolate and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

Hieracium clinoglossum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
