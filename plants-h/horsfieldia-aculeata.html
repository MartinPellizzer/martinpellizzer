Horsfieldia aculeata

Horsfieldia aculeata, commonly known as the spike orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, often pendulous growth habit. The plant features a short, thick rhizome with a dense covering of short, stiff hairs. Its leaves are usually fleshy and arranged in a rosette, and it produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters.

Horsfieldia aculeata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
