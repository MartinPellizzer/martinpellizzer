Hedyotis congesta

Hedyotis congesta, commonly known as the clustered dotweed, is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features opposite, small, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a reddish or purple underside. Its flowers are tiny, white or pale yellow, and are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Hedyotis congesta is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
