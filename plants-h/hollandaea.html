Hollandaea

Hollandaea, commonly known as the bluebonnet, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

These plants are native to the Americas and are characterized by their showy, butterfly-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. Hollandaea species are typically herbaceous perennials, often growing in moist, well-drained soils. They produce small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or water.

The genus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, such as nitrogen fixation in soil.
