Homalium multiflorum

Homalium multiflorum, commonly known as the many-flowered homalium, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, compound leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by bright red, fleshy fruits.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
