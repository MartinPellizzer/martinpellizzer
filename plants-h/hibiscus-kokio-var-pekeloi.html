Hibiscus kokio var. pekeloi

Hibiscus kokio var. pekeloi, commonly known as the Hawaiian hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It features large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, with a prominent central pistil. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves and produces edible, tart fruits known as "noni" in some regions. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The varietal form, pekeloi, is distinguished by its unique flower shape and coloration.
