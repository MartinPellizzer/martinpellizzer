Huperzia urbanii

Huperzia urbanii, commonly known as the mountain huperzia, is a species of clubmoss belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is a small, evergreen fern-like plant that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant features simple, linear leaves arranged in whorls around the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. Huperzia urbanii is characterized by its spore-producing structures called strobila, which are located at the tips of the branches.

This species is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
