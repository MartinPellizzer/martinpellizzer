Halophytum ameghinoi

Halophytum ameghinoi, commonly known as the Ameghino's halophyte, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves adapted to store water. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it a halophyte, or salt-tolerant species.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is of interest to botanists studying desert flora and plant resilience.
