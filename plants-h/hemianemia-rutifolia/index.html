Hemianemia Rutifolia

Hemianthera rutifolia is a striking member of the Malvaceae family known for its vibrant pink flowers and unique leaf structure. Native to the southeastern united states, this plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and woodland edges. Its name reflects both its floral and foliage characteristics, with "rutifolia" referring to its hairy leaves. Often overlooked due to its modest size, it plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and wildlife. Conservation efforts are increasingly important as habitat loss threatens its survival in the wild.
