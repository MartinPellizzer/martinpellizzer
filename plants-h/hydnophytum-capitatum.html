Hydnophytum capitatum

Hydnophytum capitatum, commonly known as the umbrella plant, is a hemi-epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique structure, with a short, thick stem that supports a dense cluster of leaves and flowers. The plant possesses a specialized root system that allows it to absorb moisture from the air and substrates. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in a compact inflorescence, and it produces a distinctive, cup-shaped fruit.

Hydnophytum capitatum is often found growing on trees in humid, tropical environments.
