Hypericum scabrellum

Hypericum scabrellum, commonly known as rough-stemmed St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to North America.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its woody stems and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves with a rough texture, and its flowers are often clustered in terminal racemes. Hypericum scabrellum is part of the genus Hypericum, which includes many species known for their medicinal properties.

It is often found in open woodlands and along roadsides, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
