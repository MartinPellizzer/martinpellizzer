Hyptis bisdentata

Hyptis bisdentata, commonly known as the Brazilian hyptis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or white flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Hyptis bisdentata is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
