Helianthemum procumbens

Helianthemum procumbens, commonly known as creeping sunflower, is a low-growing, mat-forming shrub native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in dry environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens or coastal areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in Mediterranean ecosystems.
