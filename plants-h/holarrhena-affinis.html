Holarrhena affinis

Holarrhena affinis, commonly known as the African angel's trumpet, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by woody, orange-red fruits containing flat, winged seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of dye.
