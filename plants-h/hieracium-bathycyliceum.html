Hieracium bathycyliceum

Hieracium bathycyliceum, commonly known as the deep-cup hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces yellow flowers in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained, nutrient-poor soils. Its flowers are clustered in a single head, and the plant is often found in open, sunny areas at high altitudes.
