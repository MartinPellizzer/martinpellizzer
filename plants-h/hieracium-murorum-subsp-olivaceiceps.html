Hieracium murorum subsp. olivaceiceps

Hieracium murorum subsp.

olivaceiceps, commonly known as the olive-headed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and a flowering head of yellowish-orange composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and waste areas. It has hairy stems and leaves that are often reddish or purplish in color.

The subspecies is distinguished by its olive-green bracts beneath the flower head, giving it its common name.
