Hieracium glaucinum subsp. glauciniforme

Hieracium glaucinum subsp.

glauciniforme, commonly known as the glaucous hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a bluish-green hue, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
