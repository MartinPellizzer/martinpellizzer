Hyperanthera peregrina

Hyperanthera peregrina, commonly known as the wandering stamen plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring showy, bright yellow petals and numerous stamens that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and sandy soils, thriving in full sunlight. Its flowers bloom during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
