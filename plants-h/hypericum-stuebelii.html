Hypericum stuebelii

Hypericum stuebelii, commonly known as Stuebel's St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features narrow, dark green leaves and produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late summer. The plant is characterized by its erect growth habit and is often found in moist, sandy soils along stream banks.

It is a member of the Hypericaceae family and is known for its medicinal properties, though it contains compounds that can be toxic if ingested in large quantities.
