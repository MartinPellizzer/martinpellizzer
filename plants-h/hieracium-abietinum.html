Hieracium abietinum

Hieracium abietinum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, erect stems and basal rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense clusters, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes, often in mountainous environments.

Hieracium abietinum is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
