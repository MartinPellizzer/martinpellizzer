Hieracium aethiadenium

Hieracium aethiadenium, commonly known as the Ethiopian hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the high altitudes of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer, surrounded by pale green to white ray florets. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cool, temperate climates.

Hieracium aethiadenium is often found in alpine meadows and is notable for its ornamental value.
