Howardia glaucescens

Howardia glaucescens, commonly known as the blue ghost plant, is a member of the Aizoaceae family, native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal watering, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its striking appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
