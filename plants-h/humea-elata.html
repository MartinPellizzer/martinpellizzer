Humea elata

Humea elata, commonly known as the slender huckleberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, dark blue berries that are edible and often used in local cuisine. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are elliptical with a smooth texture and a slightly serrated edge.

Humea elata plays an important ecological role by providing food for wildlife and contributing to forest biodiversity.
