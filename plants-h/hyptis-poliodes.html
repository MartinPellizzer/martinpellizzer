Hyptis poliodes

Hyptis poliodes, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture. Its flowers are typically blue or purple, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

Hyptis poliodes is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
