Hypericum siamense

Hypericum siamense, commonly known as the Siam St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its medicinal properties have been studied for their potential antidepressant and anti-inflammatory effects.

Hypericum siamense is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
