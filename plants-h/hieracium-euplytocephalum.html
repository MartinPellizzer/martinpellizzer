Hieracium euplytocephalum

Hieracium euplytocephalum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and basal rosettes of lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish to reddish composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Due to its aggressive growth, it can become invasive in certain regions.
