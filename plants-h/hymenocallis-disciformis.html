Hymenocallis disciformis

Hymenocallis disciformis, commonly known as the disc-shaped spider lily, is a striking tropical plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, white, funnel-shaped flowers with a distinctive yellow throat and a prominent, spiky central column. The plant produces narrow, strap-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers well-drained soil.

Hymenocallis disciformis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a popular choice in tropical gardens.
