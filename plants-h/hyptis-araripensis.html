Hyptis araripensis

Hyptis araripensis, commonly known as the Araripe hyptis, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
