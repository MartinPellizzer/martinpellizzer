Hyptis tweediei

Hyptis tweediei, commonly known as silverleaf hyptis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its gray-green, velvety foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.

Its aromatic leaves and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens and landscape design.
