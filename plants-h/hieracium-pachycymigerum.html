Hieracium pachycymigerum

Hieracium pachycymigerum, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf structures. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are covered in dense, woolly hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, contributing to its role in pollinator habitats.
