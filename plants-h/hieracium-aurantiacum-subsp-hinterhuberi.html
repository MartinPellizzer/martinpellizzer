Hieracium aurantiacum subsp. hinterhuberi

Hieracium aurantiacum subsp.

hinterhuberi, commonly known as the Alpine Oxeye, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe. It features showy, bright yellow flowers with a distinctive red-orange center, typically blooming in late summer.

The plant has a robust, hairy stem and basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountain meadows and slopes.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators in alpine ecosystems.
