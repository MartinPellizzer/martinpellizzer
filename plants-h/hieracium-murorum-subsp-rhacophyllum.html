Hieracium murorum subsp. rhacophyllum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

rhacophyllum, commonly known as the mouse-ear hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

The plant has basal leaves that are spoon-shaped and hairy, with flowering stems that bear yellowish to reddish disc flowers. Its inflorescence consists of a single flower head surrounded by several rows of bracts.

The species is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, and it is often found in sandy or gravelly soils.
