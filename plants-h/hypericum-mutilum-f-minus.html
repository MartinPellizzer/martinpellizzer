Hypericum mutilum f. minus

Hypericum mutilum f. minus, commonly known as the dwarf St. John's wort, is a compact, low-growing shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its compact form and ornamental flowers make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
