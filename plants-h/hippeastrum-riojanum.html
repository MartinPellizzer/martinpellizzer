Hippeastrum riojanum

Hippeastrum riojanum, commonly known as the Rioja amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, large, showy flowers that come in various colors such as red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces strap-like leaves that are typically broad and strap-shaped, emerging from a bulbous stem. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements.

The flowers bloom in the spring and can last for several weeks, making it a popular choice for gardeners and florists alike.
