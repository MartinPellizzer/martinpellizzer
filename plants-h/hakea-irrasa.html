Hakea irrasa

Hakea irrasa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces cylindrical, red or yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system for water retention. Hakea irrasa is often found in heathlands and coastal regions, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
