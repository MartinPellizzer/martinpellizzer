Helichrysum armenium

Helichrysum armenium, commonly known as Armenian immortelle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its vibrant yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are often grouped in dense clusters, giving it a fluffy appearance. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, and the plant is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited to rocky and arid environments.

Helichrysum armenium is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
