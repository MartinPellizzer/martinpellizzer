Hieracium magyaricum subsp. substoloniferum

Hieracium magyaricum subsp.

substoloniferum, commonly known as the Hungarian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in a composite inflorescence, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and protected species in some regions.
