Helianthemum oelandicum subsp. pourretii

Helianthemum oelandicum subsp.

pourretii, commonly known as the Oeland thyme, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to southern Europe. It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring.

The plant has needle-like leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white wool, which helps to reduce water loss. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate coastal conditions.
