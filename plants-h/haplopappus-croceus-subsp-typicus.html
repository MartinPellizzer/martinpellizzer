Haplopappus croceus subsp. typicus

Haplopappus croceus subsp.

typicus, commonly known as the yellow pappus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and finely divided, feathery leaves.

The plant produces small, wind-dispersed seeds enclosed in a pappus, which aids in its reproduction. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in open grasslands and rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and local biodiversity.
