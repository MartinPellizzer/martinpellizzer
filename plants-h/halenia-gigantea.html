Halenia gigantea

Halenia gigantea, commonly known as giant halenia, is a striking plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of blue or violet. The plant belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is known for its lance-shaped leaves and tall, branching stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
