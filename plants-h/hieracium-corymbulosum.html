Hieracium corymbulosum

Hieracium corymbulosum, commonly known as the clustered hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 centimeters, with basal leaves that are often heart-shaped or lanceolate. Its flowering period occurs from late spring to early autumn, producing yellow flowers that attract various pollinators.

Hieracium corymbulosum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed sites, thriving in a variety of soil types.
