Hieracium florentinum subsp. albidobracteum

Hieracium florentinum subsp.

albidobracteum, commonly known as the white bract hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows.

The plant features basal leaves that are oblanceolate and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, with white bracts that give it its common name.

It is part of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its composite flower heads.
