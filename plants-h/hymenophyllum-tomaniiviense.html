Hymenophyllum tomaniiviense

Hymenophyllum tomaniiviense, commonly known as the Toamani fern, is a small, epiphytic fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, moss-like fronds that are typically less than 5 centimeters in length. The plant has a unique structure with narrow, linear leaves that are covered in a dense layer of tiny, hair-like structures called rhizoids. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or rocks in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a sensitive indicator species for undisturbed ecosystems.
