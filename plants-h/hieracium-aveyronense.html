Hieracium aveyronense

Hieracium aveyronense, commonly known as Aveyron hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Aveyron region of southern France.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer.

Hieracium aveyronense is known for its hairy stems and leaves, which help it retain moisture in its native environment.
