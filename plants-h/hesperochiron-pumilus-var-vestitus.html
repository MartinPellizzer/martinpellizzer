Hesperochiron pumilus var. vestitus

Hesperochiron pumilus var. vestitus, commonly known as the hairy dwarf bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its tiny, bladder-like structures used for capturing small aquatic organisms. The plant typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and thrives in nutrient-rich environments. Its leaves are finely divided and often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize new areas quickly.
