Hieracium tanylepis

Hieracium tanylepis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. Its leaves are narrow and often hairy, and the flowers are yellow with ray florets.

It is an herbaceous perennial that can spread through rhizomes, forming dense colonies in suitable environments.
