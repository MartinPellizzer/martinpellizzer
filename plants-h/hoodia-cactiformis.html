Hoodia cactiformis

Hoodia cactiformis, commonly known as the desert apple or hoodia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, minimizing water loss through transpiration.

Hoodia cactiformis is also notable for containing a substance called P57, which has been studied for its potential appetite-suppressing properties.
