Hieracium toscoemilianum

Hieracium toscoemilianum, commonly known as the Tuscan hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellowish or reddish composite flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Hieracium toscoemilianum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
