Hydrophyllum virginianum f. simplicifolium

Hydrophyllum virginianum f. simplicifolium, commonly known as the simple-leaved blueweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its blue to purple flowers and simple, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

The inflorescence consists of small, clustered flower heads that bloom in late summer.
