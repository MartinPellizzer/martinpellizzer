Hieracium primulaceum

Hieracium primulaceum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a flowering head composed of numerous small yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring moist to moderately dry conditions. It has hairy stems and leaves that are often glandular, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
