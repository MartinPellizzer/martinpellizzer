Hieracium norvegicum subsp. norvegicum

Hieracium norvegicum subsp.

norvegicum, commonly known as Norwegian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern Europe. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands, heaths, and rocky soils.

The plant features bright yellow composite flowers that bloom from June to August, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

This subspecies is characterized by its robust growth and ability to thrive in cooler climates.
