Hibiscus boscai

Hibiscus boscai, commonly known as the Sierra Madre hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. Hibiscus boscai thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
