Hypericum ovalifolium subsp. hisauchii

Hypericum ovalifolium subsp.

hisauchii, commonly known as the Japanese St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to East Asia. It is characterized by its narrow, oval-shaped leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in woodland areas and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments. It has a compact growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Its medicinal properties are similar to those of other Hypericum species, though it is less commonly used in herbal medicine.
