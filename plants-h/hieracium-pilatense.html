Hieracium pilatense

Hieracium pilatense, commonly known as the Pilate's hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. It produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are usually hairy and alternate along the stem.

Hieracium pilatense is often found in open, sunny areas and is adapted to withstand cold and harsh environmental conditions.
