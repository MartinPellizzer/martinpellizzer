Halostachys caspica

Halostachys caspica, commonly known as the Caspian sandthrift, is a perennial flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is well adapted to harsh, saline environments and thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and often covered with a fine layer of salt crystals, which help reduce water loss.

Halostachys caspica plays an important ecological role in stabilizing desert soils and providing habitat for various desert organisms.
