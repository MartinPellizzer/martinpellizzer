Hieracium mapirense

Hieracium mapirense, commonly known as the Mapire hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile.

The plant typically grows in grasslands and disturbed soils, exhibiting a low-growing habit with hairy stems and serrated leaves. Its flowers are yellow, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets, and it produces small, oval-shaped achenes as its seeds.

Hieracium mapirense is often considered a weed in agricultural areas due to its rapid spread and adaptability.
