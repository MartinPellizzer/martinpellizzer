Hesperis sophia

Hesperis sophia, commonly known as the sweet William or garden hesperis, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Hesperis sophia is a biennial or short-lived perennial, often grown for its ornamental value in gardens.

Its foliage consists of pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges, and it is relatively low maintenance once established.
