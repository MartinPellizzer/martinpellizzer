Hieracium insuetum

Hieracium insuetum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and disturbed soils. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and its flowers are typically arranged in a single head.

Hieracium insuetum is a perennial plant that can spread through rhizomes and is considered invasive in some regions.
