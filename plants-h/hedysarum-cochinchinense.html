Hedysarum cochinchinense

Hedysarum cochinchinense, commonly known as the Vietnamese vetch, is a perennial leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces cylindrical pods that contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Hedysarum cochinchinense is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability and is used in ecological restoration projects.
