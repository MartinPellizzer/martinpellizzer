Hieracium abscissum

Hieracium abscissum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and its bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions, including North America. It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils such as roadsides and meadows.

Hieracium abscissum is a perennial herb that can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
