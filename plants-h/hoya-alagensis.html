Hoya alagensis

Hoya alagensis, commonly known as the "Koh Samui hoya," is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and waxy, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pink in color. Hoya alagensis is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
