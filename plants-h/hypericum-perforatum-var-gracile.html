Hypericum perforatum var. gracile

Hypericum perforatum var. gracile, commonly known as slender St. John's wort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and distinctive leaves with translucent dots. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny environments such as meadows and woodland edges. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
