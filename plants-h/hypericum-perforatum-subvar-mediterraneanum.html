Hypericum perforatum subvar. mediterraneanum

Hypericum perforatum subvar. mediterraneanum, commonly known as the Mediterranean St. John's Wort, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer, and its distinctive leaves with translucent dots. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are often dotted with glands, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
