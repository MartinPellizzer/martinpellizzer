Heliopsis pulchra

Heliopsis pulchra, commonly known as the showy heliopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and prairies. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Heliopsis pulchra is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
