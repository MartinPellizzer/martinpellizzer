Hydrangea oblongifolia

Hydrangea oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves that are often arranged in whorls along the stems. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

Hydrangea oblongifolia is valued for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in humid climates.
