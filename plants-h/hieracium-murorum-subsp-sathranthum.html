Hieracium murorum subsp. sathranthum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

sathranthum, commonly known as the creeping hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas, along roadsides, and in disturbed soils, exhibiting a creeping growth habit with rhizomes. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat head.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability and is often found in moist, temperate climates.
