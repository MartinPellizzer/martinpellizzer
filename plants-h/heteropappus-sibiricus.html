Heteropappus sibiricus

Heteropappus sibiricus, commonly known as Siberian heteropappus, is a flowering plant native to the cold climates of Central Asia and Siberia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive bracts. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid to semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in harsh conditions.

The flowers, which appear in the summer, are yellow and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
