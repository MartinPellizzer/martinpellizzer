Hydriastele wendlandiana var. microcarpa

Hydriastele wendlandiana var. microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf hydriastele, is a striking bromeliad native to the humid forests of Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant produces vibrant, long-lasting inflorescences with bright orange flowers that attract pollinators. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical landscapes.
