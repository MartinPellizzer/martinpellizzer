Hippocratea meizantha

Hippocratea meizantha, commonly known as the purple-flowered orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

The flowers are fragrant and attract various pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.
