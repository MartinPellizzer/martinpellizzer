Hyptis coriacea

Hyptis coriacea, commonly known as rough hyptis, is a perennial shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Hyptis coriacea is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
