Hydrangea opuloides f. caerulea

Hydrangea opuloides f. caerulea, commonly known as the blue hydrangea, is a cultivar of the hydrangea genus native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that typically range from blue to pink, depending on soil pH. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens. Its foliage is dark green and glossy, adding visual interest throughout the growing season.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, providing a striking display that can be further enhanced through proper care and pruning.
