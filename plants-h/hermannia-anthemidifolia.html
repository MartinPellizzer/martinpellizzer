Hermannia anthemidifolia

Hermannia anthemidifolia, commonly known as the dwarf hermannia, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a low-growing habit with dense clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Hermannia anthemidifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a ground cover.
