Heteranthera seubertiana

Heteranthera seubertiana, commonly known as the Brazilian water hyacinth, is a floating aquatic plant native to South America.

It features whorled leaves that are typically dark green and have a waxy texture, adapted for life in water. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or violet in color. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize the plant in water, while its rapid growth rate makes it an invasive species in some regions.

Heteranthera seubertiana is often used in water gardens and as a decorative plant due to its aesthetic appeal.
