Hieracium bocconeiforme

Hieracium bocconeiforme, commonly known as the Boccone's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a flowering stem that bears yellow composite flowers. Native to Europe, it thrives in grassy areas and disturbed soils. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

It is often found in meadows and along roadsides, where it can spread aggressively through its rhizomes.
