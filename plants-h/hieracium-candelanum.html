Hieracium candelanum

Hieracium candelanum, commonly known as the candlestick hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and meadows, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers consist of a central yellow disk surrounded by ray florets.

Hieracium candelanum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in disturbed soils.
