Hedyotis monocephala

Hedyotis monocephala, commonly known as the single-headed jewelweed, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant features small, delicate yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Hedyotis monocephala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant foliage and flowering display.
