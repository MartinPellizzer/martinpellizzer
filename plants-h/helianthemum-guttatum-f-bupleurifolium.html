Helianthemum guttatum f. bupleurifolium

Helianthemum guttatum f. bupleurifolium, commonly known as the bupleurifolium stonecrop, is a low-growing succulent with fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating.

It produces small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer, adding a subtle splash of color to its rocky or gravelly habitat. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a distinctive yellow or white glandular marking, giving it a unique appearance.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
