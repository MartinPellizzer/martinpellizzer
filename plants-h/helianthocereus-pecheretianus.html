Helianthocereus pecheretianus

Helianthocereus pecheretianus, commonly known as the Pechera cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a small, spiny cactus that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months, often at night. Its structure is characterized by cylindrical stems and clusters of sharp spines.

It is well adapted to drought conditions and requires minimal water to survive.
