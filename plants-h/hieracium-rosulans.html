Hieracium rosulans

Hieracium rosulans, commonly known as the clustered hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually basal, rough, and hairy, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a cluster.

Hieracium rosulans is often found in temperate regions and can sometimes be invasive in non-native habitats.
