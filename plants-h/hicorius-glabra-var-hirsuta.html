Hicorius glabra var. hirsuta

Hicorius glabra var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy smooth hibiscus, is a variant of the smooth hibiscus native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which provide a distinct texture compared to the smooth variety. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red, often with a prominent central pistil. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The hairy foliage and vibrant blooms make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking unique and colorful plants.
