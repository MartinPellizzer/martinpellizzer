Hieracium florentinum subsp. deseglisei

Hieracium florentinum subsp.

deseglisei, commonly known as the Florentine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellowish-green flowering heads.

The plant typically produces small, dandelion-like flowers that are borne in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
