Hieracium symphytaceum subsp. pseudorotgesianum

Hieracium symphytaceum subsp.

pseudorotgesianum, commonly known as the false rotgesian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to orange-red flowering heads.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. It has basal leaves that are usually heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Its flowers are arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family, and it is often found in disturbed habitats.
