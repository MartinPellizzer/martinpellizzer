Hieracium porrectiforme

Hieracium porrectiforme, commonly known as the upright hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely divided, grass-like leaves and produces seeds in a fluffy, wind-dispersed form. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it is often found in meadows and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural and garden settings.
