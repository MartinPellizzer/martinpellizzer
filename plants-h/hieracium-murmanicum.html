Hieracium murmanicum

Hieracium murmanicum, commonly known as the Murman hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering heads that produce yellow ray flowers. Native to northern regions of Europe and Asia, it thrives in moist, sandy or peaty soils. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, bogs, and wetlands.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of alpine and arctic environments.
