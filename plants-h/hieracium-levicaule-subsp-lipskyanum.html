Hieracium levicaule subsp. lipskyanum

Hieracium levicaule subsp.

lipskyanum, commonly known as the Lipsky's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant has basal and upright stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with rough textures. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped cluster, and are typically visited by various pollinators.

The subspecies is distinguished by its specific morphological traits and geographic distribution.
