Hedeoma bracteolata

Hedeoma bracteolata, commonly known as the narrowleaf hedeoma, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in whorls along the upper stem. Hedeoma bracteolata is typically found in dry, rocky habitats and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Its aromatic leaves and flowers contribute to its ecological and cultural significance in arid regions.
