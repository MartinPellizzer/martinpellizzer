Hypopitys lanuginosa

Hypopitys lanuginosa, commonly known as the silky pinyon, is a rare and unique coniferous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds. The plant has a distinctive morphology, with a short trunk and dense, sprawling branches. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a conservation concern.
