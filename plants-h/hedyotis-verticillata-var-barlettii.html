Hedyotis verticillata var. barlettii

Hedyotis verticillata var. barlettii, commonly known as the Barlett's Indian mallow, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is notable for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
