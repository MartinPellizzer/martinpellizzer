Habenaria auriculiloba

Habenaria auriculiloba, commonly known as the ear-shaped orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that resemble ears, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its inflorescence consists of several pale yellow or greenish-white flowers with a distinctive lip.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
