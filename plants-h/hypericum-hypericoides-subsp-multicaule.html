Hypericum hypericoides subsp. multicaule

Hypericum hypericoides subsp.

multicaule, commonly known as the clustered St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to eastern North America. It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and features small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, red or orange berries that are attractive to birds. It is characterized by its multiple stems, which give it its subspecies name "multicaule.".

The plant contains hypericin and other compounds, which have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.
