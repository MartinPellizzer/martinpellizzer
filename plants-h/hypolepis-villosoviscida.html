Hypolepis villosoviscida

Hypolepis villosoviscida, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, pinnate fronds with a dense covering of short, soft hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its rhizomes are often covered in fine, velvety hairs, contributing to its common name.

The fronds are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, making it a distinctive and ornamental plant in horticulture.
