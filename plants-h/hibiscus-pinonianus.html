Hibiscus pinonianus

Hibiscus pinonianus, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a smooth texture.

Hibiscus pinonianus is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
