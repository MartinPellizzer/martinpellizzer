Hieracium bifidum subsp. pinnigerum

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

pinnigerum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm.

The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flower heads composed of many small florets. It is characterized by its finely divided leaves and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower structures and medicinal uses in traditional herbal practices.
