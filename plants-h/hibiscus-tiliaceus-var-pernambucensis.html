Hibiscus tiliaceus var. pernambucensis

Hibiscus tiliaceus var. pernambucensis, commonly known as the Pernambucan hibiscus, is a species of flowering plant in the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink or white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in coastal and humid environments. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and can tolerate saline conditions, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

The wood of this plant is also valued for its strength and is used in traditional crafts and construction.
