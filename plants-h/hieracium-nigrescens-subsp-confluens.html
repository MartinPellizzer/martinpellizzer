Hieracium nigrescens subsp. confluens

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

confluens, commonly known as the creeping hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

The plant features basal rosettes of leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while the stems are often reddish in color.

It is known for its aggressive spreading habit due to its ability to form dense colonies.
