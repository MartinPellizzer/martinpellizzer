Hypericum nigricans

Hypericum nigricans, commonly known as black-stemmed St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, reddish-brown fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

Hypericum nigricans is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for pollinators and wildlife.
