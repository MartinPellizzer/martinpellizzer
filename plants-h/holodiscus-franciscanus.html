Holodiscus franciscanus

Holodiscus franciscanus, commonly known as the Franciscan daisy, is a flowering plant native to California.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 15 inches. The plant features small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters, which bloom from spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in fine, silky hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.
