Hieracium pseudoroborascens

Hieracium pseudoroborascens, commonly known as the false hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium pseudoroborascens is known for its ability to spread aggressively through rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural and natural settings.
