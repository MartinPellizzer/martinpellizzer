Hieracium carpathicum subsp. subindutoides

Hieracium carpathicum subsp.

subindutoides, commonly known as the Carpathian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Carpathian Mountains. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming basal leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It has a fibrous root system and produces numerous flowering stems that bear small, daisy-like inflorescences.

Its leaves are usually covered with a dense layer of fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.
