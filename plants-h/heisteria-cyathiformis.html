Heisteria cyathiformis

Heisteria cyathiformis, commonly known as the cup-shaped heister, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is a member of the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive cup-shaped bracts that enclose the flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.
