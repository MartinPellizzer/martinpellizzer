Heliosperma pusillum subsp. pusillum

Heliosperma pusillum subsp.

pusillum, commonly known as the small heliosperma, is a small herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are succulent and fleshy, adapted to store water in arid environments.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in harsh, sun-exposed conditions, making it a resilient member of its ecosystem.
