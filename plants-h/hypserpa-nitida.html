Hypserpa nitida

Hypserpa nitida, commonly known as the silverleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to aquatic environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery or whitish bloom, giving it its common name. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually clustered in spikes and are pollinated by wind or insects.

Hypserpa nitida plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic and semi-aquatic organisms.
