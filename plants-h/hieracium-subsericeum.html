Hieracium subsericeum

Hieracium subsericeum, commonly known as the silky hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the daisy family (Asteraceae).

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. The plant has a creeping growth habit with rhizomes, allowing it to spread readily.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and potentially invasive in certain regions.
