Haplopappus falcatus

Haplopappus falcatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf pappus, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a bluish-gray hue, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Haplopappus falcatus is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.
