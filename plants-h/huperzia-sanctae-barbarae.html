Huperzia sanctae-barbarae

Huperzia sanctae-barbarae, commonly known as the sacred barbara huperzia, is a species of clubmoss native to the mountainous regions of Central America and northern South America.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves that form dense, compact clusters. The plant produces sporangia in specialized structures called sporangium clusters, which are typically located at the base of the leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests and rocky slopes.

Huperzia sanctae-barbarae is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
