Hepatica acutiloba

Hepatica acutiloba, commonly known as the sharp-lobed hepatica, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and prefers partial to full shade. Its flowers range in color from white to blue or purple, depending on the variety.

Hepatica acutiloba is valued for its early blooming habit and is often found in conservation areas and botanical gardens.
