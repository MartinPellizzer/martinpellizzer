Helianthus × multiflorus

Helianthus × multiflorus, commonly known as the swamp sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a hybrid species that results from the cross between Helianthus angustifolius and Helianthus maximiliani. The plant typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. It produces showy yellow flowers with a central disk, and its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture.

This species is valued for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various wildlife.
