Helminthotheca aculeata subsp. maroccana

Helminthotheca aculeata subsp.

maroccana, commonly known as the Moroccan horseweed, is a flowering plant native to North Africa. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

This subspecies is notable for its resilience and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
