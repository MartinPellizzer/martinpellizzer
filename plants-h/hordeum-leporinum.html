Hordeum leporinum

Hordeum leporinum, commonly known as wild barley, is a species of grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical spikes that bear small, pale yellow to brown flowers. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as meadows, riverbanks, and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green hue.

Hordeum leporinum plays a role in ecological systems by providing habitat and food for various insects and wildlife.
