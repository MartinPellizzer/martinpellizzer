Hitoa

Hitoa, commonly known as the "ghost flower," is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, translucent petals that appear to shimmer in the sunlight, giving it its ethereal appearance. The plant has a unique growth habit, often growing in dense clusters along rocky outcrops and forest floors. Its flowers bloom only during specific lunar phases, making it a subject of fascination for botanists and nature enthusiasts.

Due to its limited distribution and sensitivity to environmental changes, it is considered a vulnerable species.
