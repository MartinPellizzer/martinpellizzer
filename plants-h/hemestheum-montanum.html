Hemestheum montanum

Hemestheum montanum, commonly known as the mountain hemestheum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink, which bloom in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine layer of hair to reduce water loss.

Hemestheum montanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
