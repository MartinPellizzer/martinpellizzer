Hoodia albispina

Hoodia albispina, commonly known as the white-spined hoodia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems and small, white flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems. It produces small, yellowish-green fruits that contain seeds.

Hoodia albispina is notable for its ability to thrive in harsh, dry conditions with minimal water requirements.
