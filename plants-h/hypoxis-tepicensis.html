Hypoxis tepicensis

Hypoxis tepicensis, commonly known as the Mexican star grass, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Hemerocallidaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Hypoxis tepicensis is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in suitable climates.

Its ornamental value and ease of cultivation have made it a popular choice for gardeners in temperate regions.
