Hieracium arctogenum

Hieracium arctogenum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky areas, thriving in cool, high-altitude environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disc.

Hieracium arctogenum is known for its resilience to harsh climatic conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its natural habitat.
