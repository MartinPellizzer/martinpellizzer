Hieracium humadense

Hieracium humadense, commonly known as the Humadense hawkweed, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in a single head. Native to southern Europe, it thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant has a taproot system and is known for its ability to colonize disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticulture.
