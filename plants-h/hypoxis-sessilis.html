Hypoxis sessilis

Hypoxis sessilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved star grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a rhizomatous root system and produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers with six petals. The leaves are narrow, grass-like, and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
