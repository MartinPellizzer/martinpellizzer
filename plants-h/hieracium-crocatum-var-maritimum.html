Hieracium crocatum var. maritimum

Hieracium crocatum var. maritimum, commonly known as the sea hawkweed, is a variant of the hawkweed species native to coastal regions.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its rosette growth habit and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils along the seashore, adapting to salty and windy environments.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing dune soils and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
