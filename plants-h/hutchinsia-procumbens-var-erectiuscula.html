Hutchinsia procumbens var. erectiuscula

Hutchinsia procumbens var. erectiuscula, commonly known as the erect dwarf hutchinsia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It features dense, mat-forming foliage with small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish-green flowers during the spring and summer months. It is adapted to hot, dry conditions and thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils.

Due to its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and as a ground cover in xeriscaped landscapes.
