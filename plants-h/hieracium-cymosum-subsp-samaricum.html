Hieracium cymosum subsp. samaricum

Hieracium cymosum subsp.

samaricum, commonly known as the Samara hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering heads.

The plant produces yellow ray florets surrounded by tubular disc florets, typical of the genus Hieracium. It prefers moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is sometimes grown in gardens.
