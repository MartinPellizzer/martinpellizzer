Hieracium bottnicum

Hieracium bottnicum, commonly known as the Baltic hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas and wetlands. Its yellowish-orange flowers bloom in late summer, and it produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

Hieracium bottnicum is known for its aggressive growth and can become invasive in certain environments.
