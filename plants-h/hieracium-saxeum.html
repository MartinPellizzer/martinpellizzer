Hieracium saxeum

Hieracium saxeum, commonly known as the Saxony hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Central and Eastern Europe and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a creeping growth habit, often forming dense mats on disturbed soils. It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of habitats, including meadows, woodland edges, and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and a potential invasive species in certain regions.
