Hieracium pseudargentinense

Hieracium pseudargentinense, commonly known as the Argentine hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow central disks and white to pale yellow ray florets. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open areas, thriving in temperate climates. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and it produces numerous seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Due to its invasive potential in some regions, it is often considered a weed in agricultural and natural settings.
