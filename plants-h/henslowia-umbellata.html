Henslowia umbellata

Henslowia umbellata, commonly known as the umbrella fern, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, umbrella-like inflorescence, which consists of multiple small white or yellow flowers clustered together. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are usually compound, with elongated leaflets that give it a feathery appearance.

Henslowia umbellata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
