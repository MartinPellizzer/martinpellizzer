Hedysarum micropterum

Hedysarum micropterum, commonly known as the small-winged vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial legume characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces slender, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Hedysarum micropterum plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native environment.
