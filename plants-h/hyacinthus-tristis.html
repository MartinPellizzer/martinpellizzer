Hyacinthus tristis

Hyacinthus tristis, commonly known as the weeping hyacinth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that are typically violet or blue in color. The plant has strap-like leaves that are often bluish-green in appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Hyacinthus tristis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
