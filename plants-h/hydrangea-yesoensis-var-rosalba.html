Hydrangea yesoensis var. rosalba

Hydrangea yesoensis var. rosalba, commonly known as the Japanese rose hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that typically range from white to pink, depending on soil pH. The plant produces opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green and often have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by the ability to change flower color, adding versatility to its aesthetic appeal.
