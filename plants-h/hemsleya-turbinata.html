Hemsleya turbinata

Hemsleya turbinata, commonly known as the Turban orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, turban-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow with a prominent, hairy lip. The plant grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, often found on trees or rocks in humid tropical environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Hemsleya turbinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated for its distinctive floral display.
