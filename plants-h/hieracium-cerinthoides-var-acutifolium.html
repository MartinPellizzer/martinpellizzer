Hieracium cerinthoides var. acutifolium

Hieracium cerinthoides var. acutifolium, commonly known as the sharp-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant features upright stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are sharply pointed, giving it its common name. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a single head, and are surrounded by leaf-like bracts.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
