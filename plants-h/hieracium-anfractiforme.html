Hieracium anfractiforme

Hieracium anfractiforme, commonly known as the zigzag hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture, and the flowers are yellow with a distinctive zigzag pattern along the central stem.

Hieracium anfractiforme is often found in open habitats and can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
