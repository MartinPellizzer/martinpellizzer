Heterochroa violacea

Heterochroa violacea, commonly known as the violet tamarisk, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has slender, woody stems that often grow in a shrub-like form. Its leaves are small, narrow, and usually covered in fine hairs.

It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
