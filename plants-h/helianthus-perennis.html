Helianthus perennis

Helianthus perennis, commonly known as the perennial sunflower, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features a robust stem that can grow up to 2 meters tall, covered with rough, hairy leaves. The plant produces large, showy yellow flower heads with a dark central disk, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its flowers are composite, consisting of many small florets arranged in a radiate pattern.

Helianthus perennis is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
