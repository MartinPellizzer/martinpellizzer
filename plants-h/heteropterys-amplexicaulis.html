Heteropterys amplexicaulis

Heteropterys amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clinging leaf tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that often have a waxy coating, and its clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
