Hemerocallis fulva f. variegata

Hemerocallis fulva f. variegata, commonly known as the orange daylily, is a hybrid form of the common daylily.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated leaves that display shades of green, white, and yellow. The plant produces vibrant orange flowers with a yellow throat, blooming in late spring to early summer. It is a hardy perennial that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate a variety of growing conditions.
