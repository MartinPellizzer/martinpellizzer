Hyoscyamus niger proles agrestis

Hyoscyamus niger proles agrestis, commonly known as the black henbane, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and alternate, ovate leaves. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or violet in color. Its berries are toxic and contain tropane alkaloids, which have been historically used in medicine and as a poison.

Due to its toxic properties, it is considered a noxious weed in many regions.
