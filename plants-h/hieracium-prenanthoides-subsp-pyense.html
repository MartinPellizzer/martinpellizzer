Hieracium prenanthoides subsp. pyense

Hieracium prenanthoides subsp.

pyense, commonly known as the Pyrenean hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and sunny to partially shaded conditions.

The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are typically hairy and have a rough texture. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in rocky or grassy slopes.
