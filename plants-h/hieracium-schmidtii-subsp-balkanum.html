Hieracium schmidtii subsp. balkanum

Hieracium schmidtii subsp.

balkanum, commonly known as the Balkan hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and flowering stems that bear yellow disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and is adapted to mountainous and alpine environments. It has small, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces and produces seeds in a composite flower head.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endemic species in the region.
