Hieracium crassicaule

Hieracium crassicaule, commonly known as the broad-stemmed hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are basal and alternate, with a rough, hairy texture, and the flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disk.

Hieracium crassicaule is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
