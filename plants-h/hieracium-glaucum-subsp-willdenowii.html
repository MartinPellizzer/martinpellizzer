Hieracium glaucum subsp. willdenowii

Hieracium glaucum subsp.

willdenowii, commonly known as the blue hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its blue to purple flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while the flowers are arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

It is often found in open habitats and is considered a common weed in agricultural areas.
