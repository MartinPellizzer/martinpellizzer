Hieracium bifidum subsp. subtenue

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

subtenue, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while the flowers are yellow with ray florets.

It is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in some habitats.
