Hymenophyllum smithii

Hymenophyllum smithii, commonly known as the silver moss, is a fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically covered in a dense layer of trichomes, giving it a silvery appearance. This plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surfaces of trees and rocks without deriving nutrients from them. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its small size and intricate structure make it a unique and visually striking component of forest undergrowth.
