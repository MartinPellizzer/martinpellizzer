Humularia purpureocoerulea

Humularia purpureocoerulea, commonly known as the purple-blue bellflower, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that range from deep purple to a striking sky blue. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy. Its flowering period occurs during the spring and early summer, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.
