Hieracium incisum subsp. seniliforme

Hieracium incisum subsp.

seniliforme, commonly known as the old man's beard, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery seed heads that resemble an old man's beard, hence its common name.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. It produces yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in the summer months.

The subspecies seniliforme is distinguished by its elongated, drooping flower heads and finely divided leaves.
