Haplopappus nahuelbutae

Haplopappus nahuelbutae, commonly known as the Andean pappus, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of Argentina and Chile.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and woolly, hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments, adapting to the harsh conditions of its mountainous habitat. Its small, yellowish flowers are followed by fluffy, white pappus structures that aid in seed dispersal by the wind.

Haplopappus nahuelbutae is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in high-altitude ecosystems.
