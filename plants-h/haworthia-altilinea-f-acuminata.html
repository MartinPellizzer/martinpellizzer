Haworthia altilinea f. acuminata

Haworthia altilinea f. acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-tipped Haworthia, is a compact, low-growing succulent native to South Africa.

It features dense rosettes of fleshy, triangular leaves that are typically pale green with distinct white or pale markings. The leaves are pointed at the tips, giving it its common name, and are covered in a fine layer of white wax. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the spring and summer months.

This plant is well-suited for rock gardens or as a container plant due to its compact size and ornamental appeal.
