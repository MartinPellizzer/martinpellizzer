Hieracium corymbosum var. pycnotrichum

Hieracium corymbosum var. pycnotrichum, commonly known as the dense-hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy structure. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are broadly lance-shaped with a hairy texture, and the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

This variant is distinguished by its dense, dark green foliage and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
