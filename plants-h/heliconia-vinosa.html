Heliconia vinosa

Heliconia vinosa, commonly known as the red heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its vibrant, crimson bracts that resemble flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered within the colorful bracts, which serve to attract pollinators. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense clumps in moist, shaded environments.

Heliconia vinosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
