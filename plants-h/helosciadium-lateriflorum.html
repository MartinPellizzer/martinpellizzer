Helosciadium lateriflorum

Helosciadium lateriflorum, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features a low-growing habit with creeping stems that root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, often with a bluish-green hue.

Helosciadium lateriflorum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassy areas.
