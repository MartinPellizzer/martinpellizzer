Hypericum japonicum var. rupestre

Hypericum japonicum var. rupestre, commonly known as Japanese St. John's Wort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has woody stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
