Hieracium blyttianum

Hieracium blyttianum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grasslands and disturbed soils. It has hairy stems and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

The flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.
