Hockinia montana

Hockinia montana, commonly known as the mountain hock, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions.

Hockinia montana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
