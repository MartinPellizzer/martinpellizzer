Hieracium vollmannii subsp. silvaticiforme

Hieracium vollmannii subsp.

silvaticiforme, commonly known as the woodland hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is a low-growing plant that forms dense mats, contributing to soil stabilization in its native environment.
