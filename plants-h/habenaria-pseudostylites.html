Habenaria pseudostylites

Habenaria pseudostylites, commonly known as the narrow-leafed marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in wet, marshy habitats and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, often with a subtle fragrance. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its delicate appearance and ecological significance in its native environment.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a species of conservation concern in some regions.
