Hieracium praeclarum

Hieracium praeclarum, commonly known as the showy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in the summer, and the plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves.

Hieracium praeclarum is often considered a weed but can be found in gardens and natural habitats.
