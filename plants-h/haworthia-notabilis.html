Haworthia notabilis

Haworthia notabilis, commonly known as the Zulu bulb, is a succulent native to South Africa.

It features compact, rosette-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green with white or pale markings. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for rock gardens or indoor cultivation.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
