Hieracium guinaudi

Hieracium guinaudi, commonly known as Guinaudi's hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and a flowering stem that bears yellow, dandelion-like flowers. The plant is native to southern Europe and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides. It has hairy stems and leaves, and its flowers are typically arranged in a single inflorescence at the top of the stem.

Hieracium guinaudi is a perennial herb that thrives in temperate climates.
