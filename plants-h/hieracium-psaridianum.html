Hieracium psaridianum

Hieracium psaridianum, commonly known as the Greek weed or Psaridianum hawkweed, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its yellowish to reddish flowers and basal rosette of leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces numerous small seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often considered a weed in agricultural and natural settings.
