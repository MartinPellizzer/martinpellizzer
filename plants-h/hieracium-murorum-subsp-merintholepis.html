Hieracium murorum subsp. merintholepis

Hieracium murorum subsp.

merintholepis, commonly known as the mouse-ear hawkweed, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish to reddish composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is found in meadows, pastures, and rocky areas. It has a fibrous root system and is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

The subspecies merintholepis is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology and floral structure compared to other Hieracium species.
