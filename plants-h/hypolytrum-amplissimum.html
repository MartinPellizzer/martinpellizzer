Hypolytrum amplissimum

Hypolytrum amplissimum, commonly known as the giant swamp lily, is a striking tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a subtle pink or red hue at the base. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soil. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often reaching several feet in length, and its stems are tall and sturdy.

Hypolytrum amplissimum is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
