Hieracium subargentinense

Hieracium subargentinense, commonly known as the Argentine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in grasslands and disturbed soils, exhibiting a creeping growth habit with rhizomatous stems. Its yellow flowers bloom in summer, and the leaves are lanceolate with a rough, hairy texture.

It is often found in regions with temperate climates and is adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor environments.
