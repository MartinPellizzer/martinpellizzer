Hymenoxys floribunda var. arizonica

Hymenoxys floribunda var. arizonica, commonly known as Arizona sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers and hairy, branching stems. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as rocky soils and open plains. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
