Haplopappus multifolius subsp. baccharidiformis

Haplopappus multifolius subsp.

baccharidiformis, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as deserts and sandy soils.

The plant features a basal rosette of gray-green, woolly leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its small, wind-dispersed seeds are adapted to the harsh conditions of its habitat.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in low-nutrient soils and withstand drought.
