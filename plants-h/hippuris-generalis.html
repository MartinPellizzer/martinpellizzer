Hippuris generalis

Hippuris generalis, commonly known as the water shield, is a aquatic plant that thrives in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes.

It has distinctive broad, shield-shaped leaves that float on the water surface, with a central vein that extends downward into the water. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically borne on stalks above the water. Its leaves are covered in a waxy layer that helps reduce water loss and provides buoyancy.

Hippuris generalis plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by offering habitat and food for various water organisms.
