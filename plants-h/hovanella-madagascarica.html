Hovanella madagascarica

Hovanella madagascarica, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a unique flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, showy blooms. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its roots are specialized for absorbing nutrients from the nutrient-poor soils of its habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
