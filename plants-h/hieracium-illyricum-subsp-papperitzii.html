Hieracium illyricum subsp. papperitzii

Hieracium illyricum subsp.

papperitzii, commonly known as the Illyrian hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellowish to reddish flowering heads.

The plant typically features basal leaves that are broadly ovate to lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are borne in composite inflorescences, consisting of both ray and disk florets.

It thrives in open, often disturbed habitats such as meadows and grasslands.
