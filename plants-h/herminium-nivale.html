Herminium nivale

Herminium nivale, commonly known as the snow twayblade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces delicate white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

Herminium nivale is often found in association with other orchids and is known for its subtle, ephemeral beauty.
