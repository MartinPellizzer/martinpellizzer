Hamamelis afra

Hamamelis afra, commonly known as the African witch hazel, is a deciduous shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Hamamelidaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, winged seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have serrated edges.

Hamamelis afra is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
