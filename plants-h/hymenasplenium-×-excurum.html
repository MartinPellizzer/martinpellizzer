Hymenasplenium × excurum

Hymenasplenium × excurum, commonly known as the hybrid bird's nest fern, is a rare and intriguing plant resulting from the cross between Hymenasplenium pellucidum and Hymenasplenium excursum.

It is characterized by its unique, umbrella-like fronds that resemble a bird's nest, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive system involves spore production, which is released from the specialized structures on the underside of its leaves.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and is of interest to researchers studying plant hybridization.
