Hedyotis bourdillonii

Hedyotis bourdillonii, commonly known as the mountain jewelweed, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Hedyotis bourdillonii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
