Hieracium inuloides subsp. lycopifrons

Hieracium inuloides subsp.

lycopifrons, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom in clusters.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in alpine ecosystems, supporting various pollinators and contributing to soil stabilization.
