Hydnocarpus macrocarpus subsp. burmanicus

Hydnocarpus macrocarpus subsp.

burmanicus, commonly known as Burman's horse chestnut, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its large, palmately compound leaves and produces distinctive, spore-bearing fruits known as hydnocarps.

The plant is notable for its unique fruit structure, which contains a central cavity lined with a sponge-like tissue. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is also valued for its ornamental features and has traditional uses in local medicine.
