Hordeum rothii

Hordeum rothii, commonly known as Roth's barley, is a species of wild barley native to Central Asia.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky environments and has small, spike-like inflorescences. Its seeds are small and hardy, adapted to survive in arid conditions.

While not widely cultivated, it plays a role in ecological studies due to its resilience and genetic diversity.
