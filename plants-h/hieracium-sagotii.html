Hieracium sagotii

Hieracium sagotii, commonly known as the Sagot's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

Hieracium sagotii is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
