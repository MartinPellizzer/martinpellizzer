Heliconia obscura subsp. fusca

Heliconia obscura subsp.

fusca, commonly known as the dark heliconia, is a member of the Heliconiaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its striking, elongated inflorescences that are typically dark red or purple in color, with a dense cluster of small, brightly colored flowers.

The plant has strap-like, waxy leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, providing a dense foliage structure. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests and secondary growth areas.

The flowers are primarily pollinated by birds, particularly hummingbirds, due to their shape and nectar-rich structure.
