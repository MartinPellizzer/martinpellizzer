Hormogyne altissima

Hormogyne altissima, commonly known as the giant hormogony, is a striking plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 10 meters, making it one of the tallest flowering plants in its habitat. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in dense clusters at the top of the stem. Its leaves are elongated and narrow, with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss in humid environments.

Hormogyne altissima plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various insect and bird species.
