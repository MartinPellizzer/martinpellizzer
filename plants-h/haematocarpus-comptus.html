Haematocarpus comptus

Haematocarpus comptus, commonly known as the narrowleaf burrgrass, is a perennial grass native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences that mature into hard, spiny fruits called achenes. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Due to its aggressive growth, it can become a weed in agricultural settings.
