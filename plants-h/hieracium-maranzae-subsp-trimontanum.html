Hieracium maranzae subsp. trimontanum

Hieracium maranzae subsp.

trimontanum, commonly known as the Montenegrin hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish-orange composite flowers and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that attract pollinators such as bees.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its range.
