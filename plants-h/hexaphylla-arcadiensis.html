Hexaphylla arcadiensis

Hexaphylla arcadiensis, commonly known as the Arcadian hexaphyll, is a rare and unique plant species native to the humid lowland forests of the southern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, spiral-shaped leaves that are often a vibrant shade of green with subtle variegation. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by distinctive, elongated seed pods. Its roots are fibrous and capable of absorbing nutrients from the rich, moist soil in which it thrives.

Due to its limited habitat and slow growth rate, Hexaphylla arcadiensis is considered a conservation concern.
