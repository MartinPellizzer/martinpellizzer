Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp. crinifrons

Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp.

crinifrons, commonly known as the curlyhead hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are hairy and lobed, while its flowers consist of both ray and disk florets.

It is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and ability to spread easily.
