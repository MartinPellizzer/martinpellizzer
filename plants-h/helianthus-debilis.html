Helianthus debilis

Helianthus debilis, commonly known as the swamp sunflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, dark green leaves and produces yellow, daisy-like flowers with a central disk. The plant thrives in wet or moist soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along waterways. Its flowering period typically occurs from late spring to early summer.

Helianthus debilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
