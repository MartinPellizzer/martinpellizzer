Hieracium crebridens

Hieracium crebridens, commonly known as the clustered hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a dense cluster of yellowish-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its flowers are composed of both ray and disk florets, typical of the daisy family.

Hieracium crebridens is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in various environments.
