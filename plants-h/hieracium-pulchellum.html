Hieracium pulchellum

Hieracium pulchellum, commonly known as the pretty hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its bright yellow flowers, which appear in midsummer, are surrounded by pale green leaves with a rough texture.

Hieracium pulchellum is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
