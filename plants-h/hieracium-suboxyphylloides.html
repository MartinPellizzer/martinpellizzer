Hieracium suboxyphylloides

Hieracium suboxyphylloides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in meadows and grassy areas.

It is a perennial species that spreads through rhizomes and is sometimes considered invasive in certain regions.
