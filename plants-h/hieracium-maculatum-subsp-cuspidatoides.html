Hieracium maculatum subsp. cuspidatoides

Hieracium maculatum subsp.

cuspidatoides, commonly known as the spotted hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow disc flowers.

The leaves are typically lanceolate with hairy surfaces and are often marked with dark spots. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and can form dense colonies, making it an invasive species in some regions.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties, with historical use in treating skin conditions and respiratory ailments.
