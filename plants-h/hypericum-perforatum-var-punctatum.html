Hypericum perforatum var. punctatum

Hypericum perforatum var. punctatum, commonly known as the spotted St. John's wort, is a variant of the common St. John's wort.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. The leaves are distinctive for their translucent dots, which are actually small glands containing yellow oils. It produces yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in summer.

This plant is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils.
