Hieracium nigrocreperum

Hieracium nigrocreperum, commonly known as the black-veined hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with dark veins.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
