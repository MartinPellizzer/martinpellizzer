Hebe urvilleana

Hebe urvilleana, commonly known as the mountain hebe, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Hebe urvilleana is also valued for its hardiness and ability to withstand coastal conditions.
