Hedeoma purpurea

Hedeoma purpurea, commonly known as purple hedeoma, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy to the touch. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable plant for ecological gardens.
