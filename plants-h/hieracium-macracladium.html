Hieracium macracladium

Hieracium macracladium, commonly known as the tall hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows to a height of 50 to 100 centimeters, with upright stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy. Its flowers are yellow and bloom from late spring to early autumn, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hieracium macracladium is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, and it can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
