Hibiscus fulvus

Hibiscus fulvus, commonly known as the yellow wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive yellowish-green hue and produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Hibiscus fulvus is part of the Malvaceae family, which includes other hibiscus species.

Its flowers bloom throughout the growing season and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
