Hieracium renidescens

Hieracium renidescens, commonly known as the creeping hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with numerous flowering stems that bear yellow daisy-like flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, short hairs, while the stems are often reddish in color. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks.

The plant is known for its ability to spread rapidly through both seeds and rhizomes, making it an aggressive colonizer in its native habitats.
