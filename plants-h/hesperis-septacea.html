Hesperis septacea

Hesperis septacea, commonly known as the narrow-leaved candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 cm, with linear, grass-like leaves and bright white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Hesperis septacea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its delicate, fragrant blossoms.
