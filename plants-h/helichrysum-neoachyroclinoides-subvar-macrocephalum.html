Helichrysum neoachyroclinoides subvar. macrocephalum

Helichrysum neoachyroclinoides subvar. macrocephalum, commonly known as the Cape gold flower, is a South African plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its golden-yellow, sun-like flower heads that resemble small suns, which are composed of numerous tiny florets. The plant is perennial and typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in fine hairs, and it is drought-tolerant once established.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
