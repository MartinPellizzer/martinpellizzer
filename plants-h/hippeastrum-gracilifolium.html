Hippeastrum gracilifolium

Hippeastrum gracilifolium, commonly known as the slender-leaf amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It features narrow, strap-like leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces tall, erect stems bearing clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or red. It is well-suited for container gardening and thrives in well-drained soil with ample sunlight.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for both ornamental and horticultural purposes.
