Heptapleurum altigenum

Heptapleurum altigenum, commonly known as the Japanese laurel, is a broadleaf evergreen shrub native to East Asia.

It features dark green, leathery leaves that are elliptical in shape and arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a hedging plant.
