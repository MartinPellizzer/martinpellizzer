Hieracium bauhini subsp. albaecclesiense

Hieracium bauhini subsp.

albaecclesiense, commonly known as the white-flowered hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and produces showy, white to pale yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is found in temperate regions of Europe. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, elongated achenes as its seeds.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and locally endemic species.
