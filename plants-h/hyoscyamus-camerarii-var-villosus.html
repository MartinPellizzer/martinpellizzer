Hyoscyamus camerarii var. villosus

Hyoscyamus camerarii var. villosus, commonly known as the woolly thornapple, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that contain toxic seeds, making it potentially harmful to animals and humans.
