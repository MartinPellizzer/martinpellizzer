Helichrysum acutatum

Helichrysum acutatum, commonly known as the Cape gold or everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers, which are typically tubular and arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Helichrysum acutatum is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant color and long-lasting blooms.
