Horsfieldia confertiflora

Horsfieldia confertiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers, which bloom during the wet season. Its leaves are typically broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its natural habitat.

Horsfieldia confertiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid environments.
