Hibiscus macrogonus f. macrogonus

Hibiscus macrogonus f. macrogonus, commonly known as the large-flowered hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, large, and often brightly colored flowers that can reach up to 15 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces five to seven petals that are typically pale pink to red in color, with a prominent central pistil and stamens. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while the stems are woody and covered in fine hairs.

This species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.
