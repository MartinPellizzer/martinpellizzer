Hypericum virgatum var. acutifolium

Hypericum virgatum var. acutifolium, commonly known as sharp-leaved St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are sharply pointed, giving it its common name. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and valuable addition to native plant gardens.
