Hieracium fuscocinereum subsp. fuscocinereum

Hieracium fuscocinereum subsp.

fuscocinereum, commonly known as the dark yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clustered, yellowish to orange flower heads.

The plant features basal and cauline leaves that are typically lanceolate or ovate, with hairy surfaces. Its flowers are borne in dense, flat-topped clusters called capitula, and it produces small, oval-shaped achenes as its seeds.

It thrives in moist meadows and woodland edges, often forming dense colonies.
