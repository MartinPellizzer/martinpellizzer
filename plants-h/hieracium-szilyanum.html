Hieracium szilyanum

Hieracium szilyanum, commonly known as the Hungarian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Central and Eastern Europe, including parts of Hungary, Romania, and the Czech Republic. The plant features basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in late summer. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Hieracium szilyanum is characterized by its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in agricultural areas.
