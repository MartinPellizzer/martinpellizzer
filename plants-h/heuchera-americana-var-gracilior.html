Heuchera americana var. gracilior

Heuchera americana var. gracilior, commonly known as the red veined heuchera, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, branched stems with clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The foliage is distinctive, with deep green leaves that have striking red or purple veins. It thrives in partial to full shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental foliage and ability to attract pollinators.
