Hydrangea opuloides var. japonica

Hydrangea opuloides var. japonica, commonly known as the Japanese hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from white to pink, depending on soil pH. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features broad, ovate leaves that are dark green in color. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a striking visual element to the landscape.
