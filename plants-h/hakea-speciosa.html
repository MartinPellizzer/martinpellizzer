Hakea speciosa

Hakea speciosa, commonly known as the showy hakea, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy yellow flowers that appear in late winter to early spring. The plant has needle-like leaves that are covered in a fine, white bloom, giving it a silvery appearance. Hakea speciosa is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is drought-tolerant.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
