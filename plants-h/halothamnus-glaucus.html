Halothamnus glaucus

Halothamnus glaucus, commonly known as the silver sagebrush, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of the Mediterranean and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, wax-like hairs that help reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a subtle floral display to its habitat. It is well adapted to poor, rocky soils and can tolerate extreme temperature fluctuations.

Halothamnus glaucus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various desert-dwelling species.
