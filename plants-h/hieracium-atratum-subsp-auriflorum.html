Hieracium atratum subsp. auriflorum

Hieracium atratum subsp.

auriflorum, commonly known as the goldenflower hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and stems that bear yellow to orange flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its flowers are arranged in a single head, with ray florets that are often golden in color.

The species is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
