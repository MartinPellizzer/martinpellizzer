Herpestis polyantha

Herpestis polyantha, commonly known as the many-flowered herbaceous plant, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered white flowers in the spring. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a tendency to form dense colonies.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
