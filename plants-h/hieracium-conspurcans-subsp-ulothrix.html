Hieracium conspurcans subsp. ulothrix

Hieracium conspurcans subsp.

ulothrix, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and waste areas, and is often found in temperate climates. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer.

The subspecies ulothrix is distinguished by its more elongated leaves and slightly different flower structure compared to other subspecies of Hieracium.
