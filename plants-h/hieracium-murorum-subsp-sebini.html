Hieracium murorum subsp. sebini

Hieracium murorum subsp.

sebini, commonly known as the mouse-ear hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellowish to orange flowers.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. It is a herbaceous species with a low-growing habit, often forming dense mats.

Its botanical features include hairy stems and small, daisy-like flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer.
