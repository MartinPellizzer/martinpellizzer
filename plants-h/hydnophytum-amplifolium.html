Hydnophytum amplifolium

Hydnophytum amplifolium, commonly known as the giant spider plant, is a hemiparasitic plant that derives some of its nutrients from its host through specialized root structures called haustoria.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and is often found growing in dense forests with high humidity. The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length, contributing to its striking appearance. It produces small, greenish flowers that are typically pollinated by insects.

Despite its parasitic nature, it plays a role in the ecological balance of its habitat by influencing host plant growth and diversity.
