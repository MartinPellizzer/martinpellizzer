Hedera sinensis var. leucotricha

Hedera sinensis var. leucotricha, commonly known as the white-haired ivy, is a variant of the common ivy characterized by its distinctive white or silver hairs on the stems and leaves.

This evergreen climbing plant is native to China and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a glossy, dark green surface with a pale underside. It thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

The plant is known for its vigorous growth and ability to climb walls or trellises.
