Hieracium vulgatum var. sejunctum

Hieracium vulgatum var. sejunctum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering stems that bear yellow, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as meadows and roadsides. It has finely divided, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, oblong achenes as its seeds.

The species is native to Europe and has been introduced to other regions, often acting as an invasive species in some areas.
