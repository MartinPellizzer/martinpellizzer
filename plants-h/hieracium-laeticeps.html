Hieracium laeticeps

Hieracium laeticeps, commonly known as the cheerful hawkweed, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually hairy as well.

Hieracium laeticeps is a perennial plant that spreads through rhizomes, forming dense colonies in suitable habitats.
