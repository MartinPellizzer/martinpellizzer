Helianthus dissectifolius subsp. brevilobus

Helianthus dissectifolius subsp.

brevilobus, commonly known as the narrow-lobed sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers with a dark center, and it typically grows in moist, sandy soils.

The plant features narrow, dissected leaves that are often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other sunflowers and wildflowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
