Huperzia tauri

Huperzia tauri, commonly known as the Taurian clubmoss, is a species of primitive vascular plant belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and small, scale-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Huperzia tauri is notable for its spore-producing structures called strobili, which are clustered at the tips of its branches.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its ancient lineage and its role in studying the evolution of early land plants.
