Halogeton jaubertianus

Halogeton jaubertianus, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a halophyte native to arid regions of North America.

It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, showcasing its adaptation to extreme environmental conditions. The plant has a compact, cushion-like growth form that helps reduce water loss and withstand wind erosion. Its leaves are small and scale-like, minimizing surface area exposed to the harsh desert climate.

Halogeton jaubertianus plays a crucial role in stabilizing sandy soils and supporting local biodiversity.
