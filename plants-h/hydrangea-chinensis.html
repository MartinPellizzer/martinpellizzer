Hydrangea chinensis

Hydrangea chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from white to pink, blue, or purple depending on soil pH. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a serrated margin.

Hydrangea chinensis prefers partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
