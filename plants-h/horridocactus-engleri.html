Horridocactus engleri

Horridocactus engleri, commonly known as the Engler's horrid cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that are covered in dense, white to pale green spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and cactus enthusiasts alike.
