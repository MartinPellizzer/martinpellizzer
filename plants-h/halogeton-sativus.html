Halogeton sativus

Halogeton sativus, commonly known as the saltbush, is a halophytic plant adapted to thrive in saline environments.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water and nutrients. The plant's roots can absorb and tolerate high concentrations of salt, making it suitable for arid and coastal regions. Halogeton sativus is also valued for its potential as a forage crop in dryland agriculture.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptability contribute to its importance in both environmental and agricultural contexts.
