Hieracium scorzonerifolium subsp. polybracteum

Hieracium scorzonerifolium subsp.

polybracteum, commonly known as the many-bracted hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, exhibiting a rosette of basal leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers are yellow, with a distinctive involucral bract arrangement that gives it its common name.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
