Hoya purpureofusca

Hoya purpureofusca, commonly known as the Purple-leaved Waxplant, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark purple to almost black in color. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale pink. It is a trailing plant that thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil.

Hoya purpureofusca is popular among indoor gardeners for its ornamental foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
