Hieracium murorum subsp. atropaniculatum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

atropaniculatum, commonly known as the black-tailed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a single terminal head, which are typically surrounded by bracts. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine, glandular hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows.
