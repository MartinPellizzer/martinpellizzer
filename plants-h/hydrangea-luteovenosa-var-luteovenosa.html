Hydrangea luteovenosa var. luteovenosa

Hydrangea luteovenosa var. luteovenosa, commonly known as the yellow-veined hydrangea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that typically range in color from white to pink or blue, depending on soil pH. The leaves are ovate to elliptical, with prominent yellow veins that give the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to adapt to various garden conditions.
