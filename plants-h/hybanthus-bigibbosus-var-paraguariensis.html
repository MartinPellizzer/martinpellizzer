Hybanthus bigibbosus var. paraguariensis

Hybanthus bigibbosus var. paraguariensis, commonly known as the Paraguayan hybanthus, is a species native to South America, particularly found in Paraguay and northern Argentina.

It is a climbing plant with tendrils that allow it to ascend trees and shrubs in its natural habitat. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in clusters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
