Hesperaloe engelmannii

Hesperaloe engelmannii, commonly known as Engelmann's agave, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color and arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces tall, branched flower stalks that bear small, white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Hesperaloe engelmannii is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appearance.
