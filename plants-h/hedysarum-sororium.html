Hedysarum sororium

Hedysarum sororium, commonly known as sweetclover, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, and it is often found in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields. Hedysarum sororium has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion and can thrive in a variety of soil types.

It is also known for its high nutritional value and has been used in forage and as a cover crop.
