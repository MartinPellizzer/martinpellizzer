Hibiscus todayensis

Hibiscus todayensis, commonly known as the African rose, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. Hibiscus todayensis thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various climates.
