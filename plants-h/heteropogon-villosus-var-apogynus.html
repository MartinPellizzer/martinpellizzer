Heteropogon villosus var. apogynus

Heteropogon villosus var. apogynus, commonly known as the silky grass or silky bluemink, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted, soft, and silky foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical spikelet that is often covered in fine hairs.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
