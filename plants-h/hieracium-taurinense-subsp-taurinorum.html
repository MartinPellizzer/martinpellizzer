Hieracium taurinense subsp. taurinorum

Hieracium taurinense subsp.

taurinorum, commonly known as the Taurine hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats.

The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellowish flowers arranged in a single head. Its inflorescence is usually surrounded by a series of bracts, and the flower heads are composed of small, daisy-like florets.

This subspecies is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in open, sunny environments.
