Heteropterys megaptera f. glandulifera

Heteropterys megaptera f. glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular-leafed bellflower, is a variant of the bellflower plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive, glandular-dotted leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological studies.
