Hesperis bakhtiarica

Hesperis bakhtiarica, commonly known as the Bakhtiar sweet rocket, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with lance-shaped leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Hesperis bakhtiarica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms and ecological benefits to pollinators.
