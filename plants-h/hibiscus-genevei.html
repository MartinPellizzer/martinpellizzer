Hibiscus genevei

Hibiscus genevei, commonly known as the Malabar hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Hibiscus genevei thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Its flowers bloom throughout the year, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.
