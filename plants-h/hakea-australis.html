Hakea australis

Hakea australis, commonly known as the Coastal Hakea, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves and distinctive cylindrical or spiny flower spikes. The plant is well adapted to coastal environments, thriving in sandy soils and salt-laden air. It produces yellow or orange flowers that attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.

Hakea australis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping.
