Hesperis nubigena

Hesperis nubigena, commonly known as the cloud-bright scabious, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

Hesperis nubigena is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
