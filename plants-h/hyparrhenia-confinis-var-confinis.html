Hyparrhenia confinis var. confinis

Hyparrhenia confinis var. confinis, commonly known as the African grass or African silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, silver-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open savannas and woodland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and landscapes.
