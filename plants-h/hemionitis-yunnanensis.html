Hemionitis yunnanensis

Hemionitis yunnanensis, commonly known as the Yunnan hemionitis, is a rare and unique fern species native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fan-shaped fronds that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in mountainous regions with cool, humid climates. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are clustered in specialized clusters called sori, which are located on the underside of the fronds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Hemionitis yunnanensis is considered a species of conservation concern.
