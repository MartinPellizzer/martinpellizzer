Hosackia indica

Hosackia indica, commonly known as the Indian vetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Hosackia indica is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
