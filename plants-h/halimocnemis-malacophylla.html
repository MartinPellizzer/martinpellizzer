Halimocnemis malacophylla

Halimocnemis malacophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sandmat, is a small, mat-forming perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It has narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its root system is extensive and adapted to store water, allowing it to survive prolonged droughts.

Halimocnemis malacophylla is often found in sandy or rocky soils and plays a role in stabilizing soil in degraded landscapes.
