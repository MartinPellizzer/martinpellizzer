Heptacodium miconioides

Heptacodium miconioides, commonly known as the Chinese fringeflower, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that appear in late summer and are often described as resembling a fringe or veil. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are typically gray-green in color and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its unique floral display.
