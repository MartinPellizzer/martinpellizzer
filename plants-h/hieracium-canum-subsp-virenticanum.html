Hieracium canum subsp. virenticanum

Hieracium canum subsp.

virenticanum, commonly known as the green-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are broadly lanceolate with a bright green color, and it produces yellow flowers in clusters.

It is often found in disturbed habitats and can be invasive in certain regions.
