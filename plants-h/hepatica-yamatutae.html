Hepatica yamatutae

Hepatica yamatutae, commonly known as the Japanese hepatica, is a herbaceous perennial native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves and showy, spurred flowers. The plant typically blooms in early spring, producing blue to purple flowers with five petals. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form colonies in moist, woodland habitats.

Hepatica yamatutae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with partial shade.
