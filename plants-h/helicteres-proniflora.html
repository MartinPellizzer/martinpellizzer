Helicteres proniflora

Helicteres proniflora, commonly known as the silky senna, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and clusters of yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, oblong pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Botanically, it is notable for its unique floral structure and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
