Hieracium truttae

Hieracium truttae, commonly known as the Italian hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range in color from yellow to reddish-orange.

Hieracium truttae is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in agricultural and natural settings.
