Hippeastrum chionedyanthum

Hippeastrum chionedyanthum, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a bulbous plant native to South America.

It produces striking, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink with a distinctive red or purple throat. The plant is known for its ability to bloom during the holiday season, making it a popular ornamental choice. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often appearing after the flowers have bloomed.

The bulbs are perennial and can be propagated through division or seeds.
