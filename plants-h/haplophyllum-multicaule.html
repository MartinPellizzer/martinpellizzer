Haplophyllum multicaule

Haplophyllum multicaule, commonly known as the clustered haplophyllum, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and slender, branched stems that bear small, greenish-white flowers. Native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa, it has adapted to thrive in low-nutrient soils. The plant's simple, alternate leaves are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Its flowers are typically clustered in inflorescences and bloom during the warmer months.
