Hieracium murorum subsp. xystophorum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

xystophorum, commonly known as the creeping hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy stems.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and meadows, and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

This subspecies is known for its aggressive growth and ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes.
