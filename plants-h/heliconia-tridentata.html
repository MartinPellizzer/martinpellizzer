Heliconia tridentata

Heliconia tridentata, commonly known as the red heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its large, brightly colored inflorescences that resemble a torch. The plant features long, strap-like leaves that form a dense rosette around the central stem. Its flowers are typically orange to red in color, with three prominent bracts that enclose the actual floral parts.

Heliconia tridentata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
