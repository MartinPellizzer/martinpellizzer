Helianthemum tauricum

Helianthemum tauricum, commonly known as the Caucasian sunflower or Tauric rockrose, is a low-growing shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It features small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain water in its arid habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly areas.

Helianthemum tauricum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
