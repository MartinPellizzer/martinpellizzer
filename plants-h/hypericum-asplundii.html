Hypericum asplundii

Hypericum asplundii, commonly known as the Asplund's St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces bright yellow flowers with prominent orange stamens. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture.
