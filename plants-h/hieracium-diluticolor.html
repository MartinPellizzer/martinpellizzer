Hieracium diluticolor

Hieracium diluticolor, commonly known as the pale hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, reaching heights of up to 30 cm. Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer, and the plant is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes.

Hieracium diluticolor is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of light conditions.
