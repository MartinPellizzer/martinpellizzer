Hemicarpha micrantha

Hemicarpha micrantha, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It typically grows in wet or seasonally wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are often greenish or reddish in color. It is characterized by its robust rhizomes and is often found in dense stands.

Hemicarpha micrantha plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
