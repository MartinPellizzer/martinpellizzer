Helichrysum geminatum

Helichrysum geminatum, commonly known as the twinleaf everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, bright yellow to orange flowers that are often used in floral arrangements due to their long-lasting nature. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are linear and often arranged in pairs, giving it the "geminatum" name, which refers to the paired leaves.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
