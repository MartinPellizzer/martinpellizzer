Hemigraphis palauana

Hemigraphis palauana, commonly known as the Polynesian nettle, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and bright red veins. It produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that bloom in clusters.

Hemigraphis palauana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
