Hieracium bifidum subsp. prasinescens

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

prasinescens, commonly known as the greenish hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It features a basal rosette of leaves that are hairy and have a bluish-green hue.

The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a distinct central vein.

It thrives in moist, grassy habitats and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.
