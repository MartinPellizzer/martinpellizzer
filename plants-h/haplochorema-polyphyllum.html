Haplochorema polyphyllum

Haplochorema polyphyllum, commonly known as the many-leaved flax, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and numerous linear leaves that are often arranged in two rows along the stem. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically blooming during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
