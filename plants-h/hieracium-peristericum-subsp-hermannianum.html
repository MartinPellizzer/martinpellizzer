Hieracium peristericum subsp. hermannianum

Hieracium peristericum subsp.

hermannianum, commonly known as the Hermann's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
