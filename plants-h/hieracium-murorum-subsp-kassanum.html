Hieracium murorum subsp. kassanum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

kassanum, commonly known as the Kassan hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and waste areas, and thrives in full sun.

The plant has erect, hairy stems that can reach up to 60 cm in height, with basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head, surrounded by pale green bracts.

This subspecies is characterized by its distinct morphology and is often found in dry, open habitats.
