Helianthemum ledifolium f. vulgare

Helianthemum ledifolium f. vulgare, commonly known as the common rockrose, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to southern Europe.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that are adapted to arid conditions, allowing it to store water efficiently. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
