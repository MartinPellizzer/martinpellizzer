Hypericum japonicum var. gramineum

Hypericum japonicum var. gramineum, commonly known as Japanese St. John's Wort, is a low-growing perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are alternate and linear, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Its medicinal properties have been historically utilized in traditional herbal remedies.
