Hibbertia persquamata subsp. ampliata

Hibbertia persquamata subsp.

ampliata, commonly known as the broad-leafed hibbertia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with red markings and narrow, linear leaves.

The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or heathland environments. It is a perennial shrub that produces small, woody fruits containing seeds. Hibbertia persquamata subsp.

ampliata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
