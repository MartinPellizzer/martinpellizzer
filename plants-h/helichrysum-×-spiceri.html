Helichrysum × spiceri

Helichrysum × spiceri, commonly known as the golden marguerite or everlasting, is a hybrid shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bright yellow or golden-orange flowers that remain intact even after drying, giving it the common name "everlasting.". The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces fluffy, spherical seed heads that are often used in floral arrangements. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Helichrysum × spiceri is also valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
