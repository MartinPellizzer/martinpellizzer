Hieracium villiferum subsp. lanosum

Hieracium villiferum subsp.

lanosum, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves that are covered in dense, white, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a single head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, often in sunny to partially shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
