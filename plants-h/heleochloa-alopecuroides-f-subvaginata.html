Heleochloa alopecuroides f. subvaginata

Heleochloa alopecuroides f. subvaginata, commonly known as the narrow sheath grass, is a perennial grass species characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance.

It typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and wet meadows, and is native to regions of Asia and Australia. The plant features narrow, elongated leaves with a distinct sheathing base, and its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a loose panicle. It has small, inconspicuous flowers and is often found in riparian zones or areas with high soil moisture.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and supporting local biodiversity.
