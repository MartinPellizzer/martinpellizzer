Hieracium folldalicum

Hieracium folldalicum, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head.

Hieracium folldalicum is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual appeal.
