Hordeum vulgare var. nudinipponicum

Hordeum vulgare var. nudinipponicum, commonly known as the Japanese barley, is a variant of barley characterized by its unique botanical features.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is native to East Asia, particularly Japan. This variety is distinguished by its elongated, slender spikelets and reduced glumes, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and produces small, pale yellow to brown flowers.

It is often cultivated for its grain, which is used in traditional Japanese cuisine and brewing.
