Hieracium magyaricum subsp. obscuribracteum

Hieracium magyaricum subsp.

obscuribracteum, commonly known as the Hungarian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dark bracts and compact inflorescence. It typically grows in grasslands and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
