Hibiscus welshii

Hibiscus welshii, commonly known as the Welsh hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range from pink to red in color. The plant features palmately lobed leaves and produces capsules as its fruit, which contain numerous seeds. Hibiscus welshii is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in suitable climates.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its appeal among horticulturists and botanists alike.
