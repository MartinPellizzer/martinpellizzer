Harrisia platygona

Harrisia platygona, commonly known as the pink hedgehog cactus, is a species of cactus native to southern Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are covered in dense, spiny areoles. The plant produces vibrant pink or red flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, and it thrives in arid or semi-arid environments.

Harrisia platygona is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
