Hieracium habradenium

Hieracium habradenium, commonly known as the purple hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf structures. The plant features basal leaves that are broadly ovate with serrated margins, and its flowering stems bear showy purple to pinkish flower heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
