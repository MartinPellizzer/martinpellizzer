Harrisia brookii

Harrisia brookii, commonly known as the red harrisia, is a species of cactus native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems and clusters of vibrant red flowers that bloom during the rainy season. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often consumed by local wildlife and humans. It thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
