Haplopappus tweediei var. pilosus

Haplopappus tweediei var. pilosus, commonly known as the hairy leaf desert parsley, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silvery-green, hairy leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to dry environments, with a deep root system that helps it survive in harsh, sandy soils.
