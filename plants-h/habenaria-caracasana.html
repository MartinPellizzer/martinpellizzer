Habenaria caracasana

Habenaria caracasana, commonly known as the Caracas orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellowish, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
