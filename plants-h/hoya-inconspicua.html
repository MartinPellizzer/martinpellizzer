Hoya inconspicua

Hoya inconspicua, commonly known as the spider plant hoya, is a tropical evergreen vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features waxy, dark green leaves that are slightly pointed and arranged in a spiral pattern along its stems. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom in clusters during the summer months. It is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Hoya inconspicua is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
