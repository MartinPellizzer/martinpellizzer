Heliotropium filiflorum

Heliotropium filiflorum, commonly known as the silky heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its delicate, thread-like flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing in shades of pink or purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a distinctive, fern-like appearance.

Heliotropium filiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
