Habenaria corcovadensis

Habenaria corcovadensis, commonly known as the coral orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in racemes and often have a distinctive, coral-like appearance.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral display.
