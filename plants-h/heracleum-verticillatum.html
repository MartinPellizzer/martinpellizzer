Heracleum verticillatum

Heracleum verticillatum, commonly known as cow parsnip, is a tall biennial plant native to North America.

It features a stout, hollow stem that is often hairy and can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant has large, umbrella-shaped compound leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in dense, spherical umbels, typical of the carrot family.

The plant is known for its irritant sap, which can cause skin irritation upon contact.
