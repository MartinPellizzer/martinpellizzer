Helleborine pseudocordigera

Helleborine pseudocordigera, commonly known as the narrow-leaf helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark green, narrow leaves and produces striking, showy flowers with a greenish-yellow color and intricate patterns. This plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and forest floors. It thrives in temperate climates and is often associated with decaying organic matter in the soil.

Helleborine pseudocordigera is notable for its ability to form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi, enhancing its nutrient uptake in nutrient-poor soils.
