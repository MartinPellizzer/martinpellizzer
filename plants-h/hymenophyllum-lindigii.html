Hymenophyllum lindigii

Hymenophyllum lindigii, commonly known as the Lindig's filmy fern, is a small, delicate fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its thin, translucent fronds that are often covered in a layer of fine, hair-like structures called rhizomes. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and release spores for propagation.

Hymenophyllum lindigii is often found growing on rocks or tree trunks, where it benefits from high humidity and indirect light.
