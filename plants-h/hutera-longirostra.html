Hutera longirostra

Hutera longirostra, commonly known as the long-nosed hutera, is a unique flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and striking, tubular flowers that are often brightly colored. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is typically found in dense forest undergrowth. Hutera longirostra has a shallow root system that allows it to absorb nutrients from the forest floor efficiently.

Its flowers are pollinated primarily by specific species of bees and moths, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
