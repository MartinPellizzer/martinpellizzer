Hieracium reticulatum subsp. pruiniferum

Hieracium reticulatum subsp.

pruiniferum, commonly known as the Pruiniferous Hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are generally hairy and have a rough texture, while its stems are often covered in a waxy, powdery coating.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bright, daisy-like blooms.
