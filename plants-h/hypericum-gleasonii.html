Hypericum gleasonii

Hypericum gleasonii, commonly known as the Gleason's St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the genus Hypericum, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats. Its foliage is characterized by opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer, are clustered in yellow-orange inflorescences and are followed by bright red berries.
