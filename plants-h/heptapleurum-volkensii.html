Heptapleurum volkensii

Heptapleurum volkensii, commonly known as the African nettle, is a perennial shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are usually pubescent and have a serrated margin. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions.
