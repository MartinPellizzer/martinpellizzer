Hydrolea paniculata

Hydrolea paniculata, commonly known as the waterleaf or swamp milkweed, is a tropical plant native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, reflecting its affinity for moist soil conditions. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often with a waxy texture.

Hydrolea paniculata is also noted for its ability to thrive in shaded areas, making it a versatile addition to humid gardens.
