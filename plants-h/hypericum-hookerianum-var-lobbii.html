Hypericum hookerianum var. lobbii

Hypericum hookerianum var. lobbii, commonly known as the Hooker's St. John's Wort, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in late summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is often found in rocky or alpine environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Hypericaceae family.
