Helichrysum thapsus

Helichrysum thapsus, commonly known as the everlasting or summer helen, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces bright yellow or orange flowers, which are often used in floral arrangements due to their long-lasting nature. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is typically found in dry, sunny environments. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Helichrysum thapsus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
