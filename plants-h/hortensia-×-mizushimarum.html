Hortensia × mizushimarum

Hortensia × mizushimarum, commonly known as the Japanese hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy flower clusters that can range in color from pink to blue, depending on soil pH. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and produces attractive foliage that turns red in the fall. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained, acidic soil.

Hortensia × mizushimarum is widely cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
