Haemanthus katharinae

Haemanthus katharinae, commonly known as the Natal lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features striking, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy, blue-green sheen. The plant produces clusters of bright red flowers with yellow stamens, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
