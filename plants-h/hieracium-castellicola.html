Hieracium castellicola

Hieracium castellicola, commonly known as the castle dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, producing yellow composite flowers that attract pollinators.

Hieracium castellicola is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
