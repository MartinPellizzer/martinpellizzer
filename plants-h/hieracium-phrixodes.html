Hieracium phrixodes

Hieracium phrixodes, commonly known as the golden hawkweed, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers and hairy, lobed leaves. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal, aiding in its spread.

The plant is often found in open habitats and is relatively hardy, tolerating various soil conditions.
