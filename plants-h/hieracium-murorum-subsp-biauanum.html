Hieracium murorum subsp. biauanum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

biauanum, commonly known as the alpine groundsel, is a perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments.

The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in the summer. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in high-altitude ecosystems.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and sensitive species.
