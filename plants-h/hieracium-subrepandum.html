Hieracium subrepandum

Hieracium subrepandum, commonly known as the creeping hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with slender stems that often spread along the ground. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from June to August. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Hieracium subrepandum is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
