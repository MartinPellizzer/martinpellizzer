Helichrysum nudifolium var. oxyphyllum

Helichrysum nudifolium var. oxyphyllum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and its striking, yellow-orange flowers that resemble small suns. The plant produces small, scale-like leaves that are typically hairless, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

The flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their long-lasting nature and vibrant color.
