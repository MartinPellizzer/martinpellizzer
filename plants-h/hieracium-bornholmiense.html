Hieracium bornholmiense

Hieracium bornholmiense, commonly known as the Bornholm hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Baltic region, particularly the island of Bornholm.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and sandy soils. The plant produces small, yellowish-green composite flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Hieracium bornholmiense is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its historical significance in botanical studies.
