Hieracium pirinicola subsp. pirinicola

Hieracium pirinicola subsp.

pirinicola, commonly known as the Pirinean hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the Pyrenean region. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils. It has hairy stems and leaves with a rough texture, and its flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species in its native habitat.
