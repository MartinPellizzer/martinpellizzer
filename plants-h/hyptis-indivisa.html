Hyptis indivisa

Hyptis indivisa, commonly known as the velvetleaf hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in shaded or partially shaded areas.

Hyptis indivisa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
