Helianthemum racemosum var. farinosum

Helianthemum racemosum var. farinosum, commonly known as the sticky rockrose, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has glandular hairs that produce a sticky substance, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a suitable plant for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
