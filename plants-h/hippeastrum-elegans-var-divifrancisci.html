Hippeastrum elegans var. divifrancisci

Hippeastrum elegans var. divifrancisci, commonly known as the Divifrancisci amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, typically in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces strap-like leaves that are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native arid environment. It blooms in late winter to early spring, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

The bulbs are perennial, and the plant is relatively easy to care for with proper drainage and light conditions.
