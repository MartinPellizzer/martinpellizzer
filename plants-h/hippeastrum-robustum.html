Hippeastrum robustum

Hippeastrum robustum, commonly known as the robusta amaryllis, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, typically in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces thick, fleshy leaves that are often used as a food source by various animals. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Hippeastrum robustum is a popular ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
