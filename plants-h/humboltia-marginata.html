Humboltia marginata

Humboltia marginata, commonly known as the coastal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the western coast of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in the morning and close in the afternoon. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds. Humboltia marginata thrives in coastal areas with sandy soils and is often found near the shoreline.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in coastal ecosystems.
