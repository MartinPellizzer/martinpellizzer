Hieracium pannosum subsp. amphithales

Hieracium pannosum subsp.

amphithales, commonly known as the broad-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its rosette of broad, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters.

The plant thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It has a taproot system and is often found in nutrient-rich soils.

Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer.
