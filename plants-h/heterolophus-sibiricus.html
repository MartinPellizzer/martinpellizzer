Heterolophus sibiricus

Heterolophus sibiricus, commonly known as the Siberian lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cold regions of Siberia and northern Asia.

It features grass-like leaves and produces striking, showy flowers with three petals and three sepals. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate to subarctic climates. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat.

Heterolophus sibiricus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler regions.
