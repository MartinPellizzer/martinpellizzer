Horsfieldia lauterbachii

Horsfieldia lauterbachii, commonly known as the Lauterbach's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial herb with a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often with a distinctive greenish tinge. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, organic soil.

Horsfieldia lauterbachii is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
