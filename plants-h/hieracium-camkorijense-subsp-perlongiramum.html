Hieracium camkorijense subsp. perlongiramum

Hieracium camkorijense subsp.

perlongiramum, commonly known as the long-stemmed hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and elongated stems that bear numerous yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a lanceolate shape, while its flowers are arranged in a single head.

This subspecies is native to Central Asia and is noted for its ecological adaptability and ornamental value.
