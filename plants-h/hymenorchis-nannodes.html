Hymenorchis nannodes

Hymenorchis nannodes, commonly known as the small-flowered twayblade, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with a short, erect stem and two opposite leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in a spike, typical of the Orchidaceae family. Hymenorchis nannodes is notable for its lack of showy petals, with the sepals and petals being similar in size and shape.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
