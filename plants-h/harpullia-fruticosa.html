Harpullia fruticosa

Harpullia fruticosa, commonly known as the red-fruited hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red in color. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

Harpullia fruticosa is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
