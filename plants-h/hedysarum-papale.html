Hedysarum papale

Hedysarum papale, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a perennial leguminous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces striking violet to purple flowers in racemes. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and is often found in rocky or dry habitats. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes many nitrogen-fixing species.

Hedysarum papale is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
