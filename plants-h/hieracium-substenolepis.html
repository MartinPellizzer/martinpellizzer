Hieracium substenolepis

Hieracium substenolepis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

Hieracium substenolepis is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it is considered a perennial species.
