Habenaria xanthocheila

Habenaria xanthocheila, commonly known as the yellow-tongued orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow and green floral structures, which are adapted for specific pollination strategies. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are often clustered in loose racemes, with each bloom featuring a distinctive, elongated lip.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its native habitats.
