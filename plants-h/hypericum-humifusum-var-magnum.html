Hypericum humifusum var. magnum

Hypericum humifusum var. magnum, commonly known as the large-stemmed St. John's wort, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features upright, woody stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, adorned with opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in late summer, which are rich in hypericin and other bioactive compounds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soils, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and potentially useful in herbal medicine.
