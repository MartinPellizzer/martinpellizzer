Hieracium pseudalbinum

Hieracium pseudalbinum, commonly known as the white hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are pale yellow to white in color.

Hieracium pseudalbinum is often found in open habitats and can form dense colonies, contributing to its ecological significance in grassland ecosystems.
