Haplophyllum latifolium

Haplophyllum latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf haplophyllum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features basal leaves that are broad and lance-shaped, often with a silvery underside. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters during the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping.
