Hieracium nigrescens subsp. lamprodes

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

lamprodes, commonly known as the dark-centred hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and yellow flowers that often have a dark central disc.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and open woodlands.

This subspecies is notable for its distinct coloration and is considered a native plant in parts of Europe and Asia.
