Hieracium virenticanum

Hieracium virenticanum, commonly known as the greenleaf hawkweed, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough, hairy texture, while the flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium virenticanum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in agricultural and natural areas.
