Hieracium transiens subsp. autranii

Hieracium transiens subsp.

autranii, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow flower heads composed of numerous small florets.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cold, sunny conditions. Its leaves are basal, lanceolate, and covered with fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in mountainous environments.
