Hieracium pubenticeps

Hieracium pubenticeps, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It is often found in meadows and grassy areas, thriving in temperate climates.

Hieracium pubenticeps is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in some regions.
