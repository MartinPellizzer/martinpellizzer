Hibiscus micranthus var. sanguineus

Hibiscus micranthus var. sanguineus, commonly known as the red hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from deep red to pink. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces fruits that are capsule-like in structure. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Hibiscus micranthus var. sanguineus is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant blooms.
