Hemigraphis natunensis

Hemigraphis natunensis, commonly known as the velvetleaf sorrel, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, arranged in clusters at the leaf axils. Its leaves are known for their aromatic properties and are sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Hemigraphis natunensis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests or along stream banks.
