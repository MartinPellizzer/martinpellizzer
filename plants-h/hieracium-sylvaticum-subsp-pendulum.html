Hieracium sylvaticum subsp. pendulum

Hieracium sylvaticum subsp.

pendulum, commonly known as the hanging hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its drooping, yellowish-orange flower heads that are typically borne on long, slender stems.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides.

Its subspecific name, *pendulum*, refers to the pendulous nature of its flowering stems.
