Hieracium cincinnatum subsp. syreistschikovii

Hieracium cincinnatum subsp.

syreistschikovii, commonly known as the Siberian hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the cold regions of Siberia and Central Asia. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow flowers in early summer. Its leaves are usually covered with fine hairs, and the flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

This subspecies is characterized by its compact growth habit and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
