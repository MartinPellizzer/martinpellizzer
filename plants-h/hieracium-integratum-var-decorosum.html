Hieracium integratum var. decorosum

Hieracium integratum var. decorosum, commonly known as the showy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow ray florets and dense, upright growth habit. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough texture and a distinctive hairy appearance.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
