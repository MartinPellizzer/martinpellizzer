Hemipilia bulleyi

Hemipilia bulleyi, commonly known as the golden thread orchid, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pendulous flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, fleshy roots that help it store water.

Hemipilia bulleyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticultural gardens.
