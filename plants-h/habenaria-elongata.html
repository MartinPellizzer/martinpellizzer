Habenaria elongata

Habenaria elongata, commonly known as the long-leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and long, narrow leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale green with intricate patterns. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
