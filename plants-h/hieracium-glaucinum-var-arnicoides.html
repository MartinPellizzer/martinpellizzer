Hieracium glaucinum var. arnicoides

Hieracium glaucinum var. arnicoides, commonly known as the glaucous hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. Its flowering stems are usually unbranched and bear yellow disk flowers surrounded by white ray florets.

It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along riverbanks.
