Hologymne californica

Hologymne californica, commonly known as the California ghost flower, is a rare and unique plant native to the coastal regions of California.

It is characterized by its translucent, waxy leaves that appear almost ghostly in appearance, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and requires a specific microclimate to survive.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
