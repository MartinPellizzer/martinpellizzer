Hieracium caesioides subsp. lobophyton

Hieracium caesioides subsp.

lobophyton, commonly known as the purple meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a hairy surface, and the flowers are usually purple or pink, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
