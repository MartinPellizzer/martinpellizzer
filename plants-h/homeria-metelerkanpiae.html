Homeria metelerkanpiae

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and rugged, twisted trunk. The plant exhibits a unique adaptation to its coastal habitat, with a thick, waxy cuticle that helps reduce water loss in the arid environment. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by native bees, and its fruit is a small, round samara that disperses via wind.
