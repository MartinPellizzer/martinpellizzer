Hypericum transsilvanicum

Hypericum transsilvanicum, commonly known as the Transylvanian St. John's Wort, is a perennial shrub native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the family Hypericaceae and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has opposite, simple, and usually hairy leaves that are oval to elliptic in shape. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and open meadows.

Its medicinal properties have been studied, though it is less commonly used than other Hypericum species.
