Hieraciodes divaricata

Hieraciodes divaricata, commonly known as the variable hill everlet, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, rocky habitats at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are small and clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences, adding to its ornamental appeal.
