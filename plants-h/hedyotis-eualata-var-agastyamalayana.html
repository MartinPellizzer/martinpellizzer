Hedyotis eualata var. agastyamalayana

Hedyotis eualata var. agastyamalayana, commonly known as the Malabar bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a herbaceous perennial with slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the monsoon season. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
