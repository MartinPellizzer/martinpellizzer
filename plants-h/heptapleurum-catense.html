Heptapleurum catense

Heptapleurum catense, commonly known as the African nettle, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round, fleshy fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant has a fibrous root system and is known for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions.
