Heliotropium sennii

Heliotropium sennii, commonly known as the velvetleaf heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety foliage and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a distinctive waxy texture.

Heliotropium sennii is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in open woodland or along roadsides.
