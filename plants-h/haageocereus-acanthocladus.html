Haageocereus acanthocladus

Haageocereus acanthocladus, commonly known as the spiny hedge cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in sharp, spiny tubercles, providing protection from herbivores. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne in clusters at the ends of the stems. It is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptations, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in arid gardens.
