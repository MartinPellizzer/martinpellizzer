Hypochaeris parvifolia

Hypochaeris parvifolia, commonly known as the small-flowered hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, upright leaves and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers with five petals. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads.

Hypochaeris parvifolia is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
