Hieracium dentatum subsp. subruncinatiforme

Hieracium dentatum subsp.

subruncinatiforme, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. Its leaves are narrow and toothed, with a distinctive basal rosette structure.

It produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, dry fruits called achenes.
