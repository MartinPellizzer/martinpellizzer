Hordeum vulgare var. micrurum

Hordeum vulgare var. micrurum, commonly known as dwarf barley, is a variant of barley characterized by its shorter stature and compact growth habit.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is adapted to temperate climates. The plant produces small, hard grains that are typically used in animal feed or traditional brewing. Its botanical features include narrow leaves and a flowering head that consists of spikelets.

This variety is valued for its resilience to environmental stress and its role in ecological studies.
