Heliconia × mantenensis

Heliconia × mantenensis, commonly known as the red-veined heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its large, brightly colored inflorescences that resemble a bird's beak. The plant features elongated, strap-like leaves that are often green with prominent red veins, adding to its ornamental appeal. Its flowers are typically orange or red, with a unique structure that attracts pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.

Heliconia × mantenensis is widely cultivated in warm climates for its aesthetic value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
