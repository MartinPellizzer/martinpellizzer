Hieracium nigrescens subsp. lygistodon

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

lygistodon, commonly known as the dark-hued hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters at the end of its flowering stems.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its flowers are composed of numerous small florets arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family.

The subspecies is distinguished by its darker coloration and specific morphological traits compared to other members of the Hieracium genus.
