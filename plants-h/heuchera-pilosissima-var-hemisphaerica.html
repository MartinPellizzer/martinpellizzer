Heuchera pilosissima var. hemisphaerica

Heuchera pilosissima var. hemisphaerica, commonly known as the velvetleaf coralbells, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves that range in color from deep red to purple and green, depending on the season and light conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white during the summer months. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental foliage and is often used in woodland gardens.
