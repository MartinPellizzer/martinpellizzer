Hieracium rectiflorum

Hieracium rectiflorum, commonly known as the straight-flowered hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 20 to 60 centimeters, with hairy stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often lobed. Its flowers are yellow, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets, and it blooms from late spring to early autumn.

Hieracium rectiflorum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
