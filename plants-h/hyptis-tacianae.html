Hyptis tacianae

Hyptis tacianae, commonly known as the Brazilian hyptis, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. Hyptis tacianae is often found in open woodlands and savannas, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
