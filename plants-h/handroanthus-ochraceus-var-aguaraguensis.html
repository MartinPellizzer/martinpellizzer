Handroanthus ochraceus var. aguaraguensis

Handroanthus ochraceus var. aguaraguensis, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a species native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, lowland areas. The plant is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing during the dry season. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The plant has a shallow root system and is adapted to the nutrient-poor soils of its native habitat.
