Hippocrepis multisiliquosa subsp. constricta

Hippocrepis multisiliquosa subsp.

constricta, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed ladies' tresses, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear dense clusters of pale pink to white flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and grasslands. Its flowers are arranged in a dense, spike-like inflorescence, and it is known for its delicate, fragrant blooms.

It is a rare and protected species in many regions due to habitat loss and environmental changes.
