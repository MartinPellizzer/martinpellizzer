Hyphaene multiformis subsp. trigibba

Hyphaene multiformis subsp.

trigibba, commonly known as the African fan palm, is a dioecious plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of large, fan-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by clustered, fibrous fruits. Its leaves are used for thatching and construction in local communities.

The species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions.
