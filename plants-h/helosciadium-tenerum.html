Helosciadium tenerum

Helosciadium tenerum, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, blooming in early summer. The plant has hairy, succulent leaves that help it retain moisture in its arid habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly areas.

Helosciadium tenerum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
