Hieracium subtilipilosum

Hieracium subtilipilosum, commonly known as the slender hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are hairy and often have a serrated edge, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is a perennial species that can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
