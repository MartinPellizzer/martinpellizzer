Hyptis pyriformis

Hyptis pyriformis, commonly known as the horned hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy purple or white flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in dry forests and savannas.

Hyptis pyriformis is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
