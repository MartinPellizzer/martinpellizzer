Hopea lowii

Hopea lowii, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall, with a straight trunk and broad, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its flowers are small and yellowish, and it produces woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

Hopea lowii plays an important role in the ecological systems of its native habitats, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
