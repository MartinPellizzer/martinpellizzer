Hakea granulosa

Hakea granulosa, commonly known as the rough hakea, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its thick, often prickly leaves and distinctive red or yellow flowers that appear in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, coastal environments. Its seed pods are woody and contain numerous small, hard seeds.

Hakea granulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
