Hierochloe longifolia

Hierochloe longifolia, commonly known as long-leaved bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green blades and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and meadows. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle bearing small, spikelet-like flowers.

Hierochloe longifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
