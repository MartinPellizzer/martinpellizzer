Huperzia mexiae

Huperzia mexiae, commonly known as the Mexican clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and is native to the mountainous regions of Mexico. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually arranged in whorls and are often scale-like in appearance.

Huperzia mexiae is of interest to botanists due to its primitive features and its role in ecological systems.
