Hedysarum polymorphum var. pumilum

Hedysarum polymorphum var. pumilum, commonly known as the dwarf yellow vetch, is a low-growing perennial legume native to the western United States.

It features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in well-drained soils, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. It is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

This variety is also used in restoration projects due to its hardiness and adaptability.
