Hugelia filifolia var. floccosa

Hugelia filifolia var. floccosa, commonly known as the silky hugelia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, fibrous root system and its ability to thrive in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky bloom, giving it its common name. It produces small, white or pale green flowers that appear in clusters, typically during the summer months.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing coastal and sandy environments.
