Heliotropium tenuifolium var. exsertum

Heliotropium tenuifolium var. exsertum, commonly known as the slender-leaved heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thin, elongated leaves and clusters of small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along the edges of swamps and wetlands. It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in partial to full sun and is often found in coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for wetland ecosystems and a subject of study in plant ecology.
