Hieracium florentinum subsp. rauracorum

Hieracium florentinum subsp.

rauracorum, commonly known as the Rauracan hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe. It typically grows in meadows, pastures, and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It is characterized by its hairy stems and small, dandelion-like flowers.

This subspecies is often found in disturbed habitats and is considered a characteristic species of certain ecological communities.
