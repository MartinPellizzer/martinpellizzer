Hedysarum brahuicum

Hedysarum brahuicum, commonly known as the Brahui pea, is a perennial leguminous plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

Hedysarum brahuicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it ecologically significant.
