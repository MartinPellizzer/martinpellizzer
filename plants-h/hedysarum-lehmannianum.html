Hedysarum lehmannianum

Hedysarum lehmannianum, commonly known as Lehmann's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in clusters. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, which help reduce water loss.

Hedysarum lehmannianum is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinators.
