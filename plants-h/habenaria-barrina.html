Habenaria barrina

Habenaria barrina, commonly known as the Barrin's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, preferring well-drained soil. The plant produces slender, upright stems that bear a single, showy flower with a distinctive, spurred lip. Its flowers are usually pale pink or white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by insects.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral structure.
