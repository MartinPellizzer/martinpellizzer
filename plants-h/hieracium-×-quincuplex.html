Hieracium × quincuplex

Hieracium × quincuplex, commonly known as the five-rayed hawkweed, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which typically consist of five ray florets and a central disk. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. It is native to Europe and has been introduced to other regions, where it can become invasive.

Due to its hybrid origin, it exhibits a wide range of morphological variations.
