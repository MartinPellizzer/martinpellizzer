Hyparrhenia anemopaegma

Hyparrhenia anemopaegma, commonly known as the silverleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, silvery-gray leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Hyparrhenia anemopaegma is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
