Hydrangea hydrangeoides var. yakushimensis

Hydrangea hydrangeoides var. yakushimensis, commonly known as the Yakushimen hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy flower heads that range from white to pink, depending on soil pH. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into round, white or pink bracts. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to change flower color, adding visual interest throughout the growing season.
