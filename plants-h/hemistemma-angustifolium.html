Hemistemma angustifolium

Hemistemma angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaf hemistemma, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are white to pale blue and are arranged in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
