Hieracium oppositidens

Hieracium oppositidens, commonly known as the opposite-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves that are opposite in arrangement, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that are typically clustered in a single head. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas.

Hieracium oppositidens is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in disturbed habitats.
