Hymenidium chloroleucum

Hymenidium chloroleucum, commonly known as the white bladderwort, is a flowering plant native to wetland and marshy habitats.

It is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the early spring. The plant has slender, branched stems that are typically submerged or floating in water. Its leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it produces tiny, bladder-like organs that aid in trapping small aquatic organisms.

Hymenidium chloroleucum plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and providing habitat for microorganisms.
