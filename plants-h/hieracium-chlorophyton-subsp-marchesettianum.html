Hieracium chlorophyton subsp. marchesettianum

Hieracium chlorophyton subsp.

marchesettianum, commonly known as the Marchesettian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped, dark green leaves with a hairy texture.

The plant produces small, yellowish composite flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in meadows and wetlands.

This subspecies is notable for its limited distribution and is considered a rare plant in certain regions of Europe.
