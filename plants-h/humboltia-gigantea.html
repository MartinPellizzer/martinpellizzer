Humboltia gigantea

Humboltia gigantea, commonly known as the giant hummingbird vine, is a climbing plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and long, woody stems that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers that attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

Humboltia gigantea is often found in humid, tropical environments and thrives in well-drained soil.
