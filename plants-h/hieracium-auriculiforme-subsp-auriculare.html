Hieracium auriculiforme subsp. auriculare

Hieracium auriculiforme subsp.

auriculare, commonly known as the auriculate hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are hairy and often have a distinctive auriculate (ear-shaped) base.

The yellow flowers are arranged in a flat-topped cluster and bloom in late summer.
