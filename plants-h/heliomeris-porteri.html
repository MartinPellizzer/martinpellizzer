Heliomeris porteri

Heliomeris porteri, commonly known as the desert honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its adaptations to arid environments include deep root systems and the ability to store water in its leaves.

Heliomeris porteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting desert wildlife.
