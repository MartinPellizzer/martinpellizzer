Herminium plantagineum

Herminium plantagineum, commonly known as the common twayblade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its lack of leaves, with photosynthesis occurring through its fleshy, leaf-like stems. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically arranged in a spike, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Herminium plantagineum is a hemiparasitic plant, meaning it partially depends on other plants for nutrients through its root system.

Its unique botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and botanical study.
