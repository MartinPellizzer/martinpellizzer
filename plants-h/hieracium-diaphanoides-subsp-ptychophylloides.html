Hieracium diaphanoides subsp. ptychophylloides

Hieracium diaphanoides subsp.

ptychophylloides, commonly known as the translucent hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and small, yellowish to reddish disc flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It is native to Europe and has been introduced to other regions, where it can become invasive in certain areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for ecological studies and botanical gardens.
