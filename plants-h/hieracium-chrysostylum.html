Hieracium chrysostylum

Hieracium chrysostylum, commonly known as the golden hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flower heads, which are composed of numerous small florets arranged in a radiate pattern. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Hieracium chrysostylum is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it plays a role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
