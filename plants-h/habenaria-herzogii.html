Habenaria herzogii

Habenaria herzogii, commonly known as the Herzog's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant typically grows in open, sandy or rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its roots are fibrous, and it produces a single, upright stem with several lance-shaped leaves.

The flowers are usually white or pale pink with a greenish throat, and they are clustered in a loose raceme.
