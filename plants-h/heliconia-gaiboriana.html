Heliconia gaiboriana

Heliconia gaiboriana, commonly known as the Guiana heliconia, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescences that resemble a bird's beak. The plant features large, elongated bracts that enclose numerous small, clustered flowers. Its leaves are broad and waxy, with a distinctive zigzag pattern along the midrib.

Heliconia gaiboriana is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant appearance and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
