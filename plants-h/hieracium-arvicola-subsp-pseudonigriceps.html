Hieracium arvicola subsp. pseudonigriceps

Hieracium arvicola subsp.

pseudonigriceps, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect growth habit and hairy stems.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, adapted to the harsh alpine environment.

It thrives in rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes, often forming dense mats in its native habitat.
