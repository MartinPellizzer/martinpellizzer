Hordeum distichon var. erythraeum

Hordeum distichon var. erythraeum, commonly known as the red barley, is a variant of the barley species native to temperate regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish coloration in the grains, which is due to the presence of anthocyanin pigments. The plant typically grows in grasslands and disturbed soils, showing adaptability to various environmental conditions. Its botanical features include slender stems and spike-like inflorescences with two-rowed spikelets.

This variety is of interest in agricultural and horticultural studies for its potential as a nutritious grain crop.
