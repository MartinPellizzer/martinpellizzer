Hepetis echinata

Hepetis echinata, commonly known as the spiked ironweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stem and clusters of small, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Hepetis echinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
