Haplopappus hirtellus var. limarensis

Haplopappus hirtellus var. limarensis, commonly known as the Limar Valley hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flower heads that are clustered in dense, spherical shapes. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine and arid environments.

Its botanical structure makes it well-adapted to harsh, high-altitude conditions.
