Henslowia

Henslowia, commonly known as the yellow-eyed grass, is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes grasses.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in spikelets, giving it a distinctive appearance. Henslowia typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
