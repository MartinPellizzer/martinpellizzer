Hieracium neocerinthoides

Hieracium neocerinthoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are narrow and often hairy, with a rough texture, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is an herbaceous perennial that blooms from late spring to early summer.
