Halothamnus iliensis

Halothamnus iliensis, commonly known as the Ili sandthorn, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia, particularly found in the arid regions of Kazakhstan and China.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh, dry environments and often grows in sandy or rocky soils. Its leaves are typically reduced to scales, and it produces small, dry fruits that contain seeds.

Halothamnus iliensis plays a role in stabilizing desert soils and is of interest for its ecological and potential medicinal properties.
