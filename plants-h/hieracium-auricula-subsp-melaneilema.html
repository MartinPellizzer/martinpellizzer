Hieracium auricula subsp. melaneilema

Hieracium auricula subsp.

melaneilema, commonly known as the black-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, hairy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dark, almost black leaves, which give it its common name. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and grassy areas.

This plant is often found in temperate regions and is noted for its ornamental value in horticulture.
