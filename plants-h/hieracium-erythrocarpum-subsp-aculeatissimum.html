Hieracium erythrocarpum subsp. aculeatissimum

Hieracium erythrocarpum subsp.

aculeatissimum, commonly known as the red-fruited hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its distinctive, bright red, elongated achenes that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered with glandular dots, and its flowering stems bear small, yellowish flower heads.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species in many regions.
