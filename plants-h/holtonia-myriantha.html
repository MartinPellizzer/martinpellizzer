Holtonia myriantha

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils in woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slight hairy texture.
