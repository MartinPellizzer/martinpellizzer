Hippolytia tomentosa

Hippolytia tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly hippolytia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves and stems, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The woolly hippolytia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
