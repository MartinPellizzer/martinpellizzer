Hieracium depilatum

Hieracium depilatum, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are hairy and grayish-green, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is often found in temperate regions and can be an invasive species in some areas.
