Hieracium taurinense subsp. neoprenanthes

Hieracium taurinense subsp.

neoprenanthes, commonly known as the Taurine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

The plant features bright yellow flowers arranged in a single, terminal head, with ray florets that are usually longer than the disk florets. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is characterized by its robust growth and is often found in disturbed or marginal habitats.
