Hibiscus manuripiensis

Hibiscus manuripiensis, commonly known as the Manuripe hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in countries like Colombia and Ecuador.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, tropical environments. The plant produces large, showy flowers with five petals, often in shades of red, pink, or white. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and often covered in fine hairs.

Hibiscus manuripiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
