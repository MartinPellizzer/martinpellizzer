Helleborine viridiflora

Helleborine viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and its striking greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Native to parts of Europe and Asia, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its flowers are typically arranged in a spike and have a unique structure with three sepals and three petals, one of which is often hooded.

The plant is noted for its ability to form dense colonies and is sometimes considered invasive in certain regions.
