Helichrysum montanum

Helichrysum montanum, commonly known as mountain everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow to orange flowers that resemble small sunflowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, mountainous environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and gray-green, and it produces small, persistent seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Helichrysum montanum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
