Helianthus quinquenervis

Helianthus quinquenervis, commonly known as the five-veined sunflower, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its large, yellow ray flowers and a central disk composed of numerous small florets. The leaves are typically lance-shaped with five distinct veins, giving the plant its scientific name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in open fields and along roadsides.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
