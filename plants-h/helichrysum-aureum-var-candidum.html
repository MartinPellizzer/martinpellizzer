Helichrysum aureum var. candidum

Helichrysum aureum var. candidum, commonly known as the white everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that resemble tiny sunflowers. The plant has silver-green, needle-like leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

The flowers are long-lasting when dried, which contributes to its popularity in floral arrangements.
