Habenaria lagunae-sanctae

Habenaria lagunae-sanctae, commonly known as the Saint Helena orchid, is a rare and endemic species found exclusively on the island of Saint Helena.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive violet lip. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its survival is threatened by habitat loss and limited genetic diversity.

Conservation efforts are crucial to preserving this unique botanical species.
