Hedyotis wightii

Hedyotis wightii, commonly known as the Indian bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including India, Nepal, and Sri Lanka.

It is a low-growing herb with slender, ascending stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Hedyotis wightii is typically found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and rocky areas.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
