Hieracium eupiletum

Hieracium eupiletum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and basal rosettes of leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, flat-topped clusters called capitula. It is native to Europe and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Hieracium eupiletum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
