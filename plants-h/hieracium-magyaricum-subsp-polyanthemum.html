Hieracium magyaricum subsp. polyanthemum

Hieracium magyaricum subsp.

polyanthemum, commonly known as the Hungarian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to orange flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded conditions. It has basal and stem leaves that are usually lance-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces.

The subspecies polyanthemum is distinguished by its larger, more showy flowers compared to other members of the species.
