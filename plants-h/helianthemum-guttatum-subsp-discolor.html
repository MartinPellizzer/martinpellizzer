Helianthemum guttatum subsp. discolor

Helianthemum guttatum subsp.

discolor, commonly known as the pink rockrose, is a low-growing shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and clusters of pink to magenta flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its subspecies distinction is notable for its distinct coloration and growth form compared to other members of the species.

It is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
