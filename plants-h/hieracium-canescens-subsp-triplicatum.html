Hieracium canescens subsp. triplicatum

Hieracium canescens subsp.

triplicatum, commonly known as the three-lobed hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and road sides. It features basal leaves that are hairy and often lobed, with a flowering period in late spring to early summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its three-lobed leaves and is often found in moist, calcareous soils.
