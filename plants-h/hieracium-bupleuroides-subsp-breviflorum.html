Hieracium bupleuroides subsp. breviflorum

Hieracium bupleuroides subsp.

breviflorum, commonly known as the short-flowered hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded conditions. Its leaves are broadly lance-shaped with a rough, hairy texture, and the flowers are yellow with ray florets.

It is a perennial herb that spreads through rhizomes and is often considered a weed in agricultural areas.
