Hieracium pallidum subsp. ceratodon

Hieracium pallidum subsp.

ceratodon, commonly known as the pale hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It plays a role in supporting pollinators and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
