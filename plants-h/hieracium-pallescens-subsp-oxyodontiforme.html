Hieracium pallescens subsp. oxyodontiforme

Hieracium pallescens subsp.

oxyodontiforme, commonly known as the pale hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typically hairy foliage.

The plant features yellowish to greenish flower heads with pale yellow ray florets, and its leaves are generally lance-shaped with a rough texture. It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides, and is often found in temperate climates.

The subspecies oxyodontiforme is distinguished by its elongated, pointed leaf shapes and slightly different flowering patterns compared to other members of the species.
