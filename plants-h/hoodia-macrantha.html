Hoodia macrantha

Hoodia macrantha, commonly known as the desert cabbage, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom during the rainy season, typically in late summer. Its leaves are reduced to spines, minimizing water loss through transpiration.

Hoodia macrantha is also noted for containing a compound called P57, which has been studied for its potential appetite-suppressing properties.
