Hypericum tetrapterum var. tetrapterum

Hypericum tetrapterum var. tetrapterum, commonly known as the four-winged St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are opposite and often slightly hairy. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late summer. Its distinctive four-winged seeds are a notable botanical feature, aiding in wind dispersal.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
