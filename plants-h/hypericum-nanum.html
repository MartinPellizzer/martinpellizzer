Hypericum nanum

Hypericum nanum, commonly known as dwarf St. John's wort, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves and produces red-orange berries that persist into winter. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its compact size and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscape design.
