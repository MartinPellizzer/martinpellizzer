Hieracium villosum subsp. glabratellum

Hieracium villosum subsp.

glabratellum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features dark green, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and its flowers are yellow, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ability to produce a large number of seeds.

The subspecies glabratellum is distinguished by its reduced hairy coverage compared to other subspecies of H. villosum.
