Hemizonia pauciflora

Hemizonia pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered hemizonia, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbel-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its small, white to pale yellow flowers bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hemizonia pauciflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
