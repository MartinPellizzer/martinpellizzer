Hieracium transiens subsp. transiens

Hieracium transiens subsp.

transiens, commonly known as the wandering beggar-ticks, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in disturbed soils such as roadsides and waste areas.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are usually alternate, with a rough texture and often hairy surfaces.

It is a perennial species that spreads through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in various habitats.
