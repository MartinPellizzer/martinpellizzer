Hypericum olympicum f. tenuifolium

Hypericum olympicum f. tenuifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved St. John's wort, is a compact, low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The leaves are narrow and linear, giving the plant its distinguishing appearance. It thrives in well-drained, sunny soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its historical use in herbal medicine.
