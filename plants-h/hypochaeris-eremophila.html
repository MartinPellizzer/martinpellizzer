Hypochaeris eremophila

Hypochaeris eremophila, commonly known as the desert hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand periods of drought. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, and its leaves are narrow and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Hypochaeris eremophila is often found in disturbed areas and can spread readily through its seed production.
