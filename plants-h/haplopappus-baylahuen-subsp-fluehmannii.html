Haplopappus baylahuen subsp. fluehmannii

Haplopappus baylahuen subsp.

fluehmannii, commonly known as the Andean pappus, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or arid soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh habitat.
