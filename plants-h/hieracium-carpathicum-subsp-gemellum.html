Hieracium carpathicum subsp. gemellum

Hieracium carpathicum subsp.

gemellum, commonly known as the Carpathian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Carpathian Mountains. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and forest edges. It has a relatively compact growth habit and is known for its ability to form dense colonies.

The subspecies gemellum is distinguished by its smaller, more clustered flower heads compared to other members of the species.
