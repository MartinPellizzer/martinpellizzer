Hymenophyllum paucicarpum

Hymenophyllum paucicarpum, commonly known as the slender wall moss, is a small, delicate fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are typically less than 2 centimeters in width. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on rock surfaces or tree trunks in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures, known as sporangia, are clustered in small groups called sori, which are typically located on the underside of the leaves.

Hymenophyllum paucicarpum plays a role in maintaining soil moisture and providing habitat for microorganisms in its native ecosystems.
