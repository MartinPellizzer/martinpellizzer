Hymenospron molle

Hymenospron molle, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and dense, glossy green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, pod-like fruits containing sweet, sticky pulp. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Hymenospron molle is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in tropical forests.
