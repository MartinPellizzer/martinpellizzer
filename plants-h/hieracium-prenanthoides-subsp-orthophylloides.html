Hieracium prenanthoides subsp. orthophylloides

Hieracium prenanthoides subsp.

orthophylloides, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.

The plant has basal leaves that are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, with flowering stems that bear yellow composite flowers. Its inflorescence consists of a single, elongated flower head with tubular florets surrounded by ray florets.

The species is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and is often found in temperate regions.
