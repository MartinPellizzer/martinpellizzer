Hypericum transsilvanicum f. perglandulosum

Hypericum transsilvanicum f. perglandulosum, commonly known as the Transylvanian St. John's Wort, is a botanical variant of the genus Hypericum.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and distinctive glandular leaves, which contribute to its unique chemical profile. This plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, often in mountainous regions. It is native to parts of Europe, particularly Romania and the Carpathian Mountains.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
