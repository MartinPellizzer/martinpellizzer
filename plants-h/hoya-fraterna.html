Hoya fraterna

Hoya fraterna, commonly known as the friendship hoya, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and waxy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom during the summer months. Hoya fraterna is a popular houseplant due to its ease of care and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

It is also valued for its air-purifying properties and ornamental appeal.
