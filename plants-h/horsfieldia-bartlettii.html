Horsfieldia bartlettii

Horsfieldia bartlettii, commonly known as the Bartlett's orchid, is a species of flowering plant in the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stem and showy, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-yellow with red or orange markings. Its roots are adapted to grow in epiphytic conditions, attaching to tree trunks in its natural habitat.

Horsfieldia bartlettii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
