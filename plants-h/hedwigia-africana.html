Hedwigia africana

Hedwigia africana, commonly known as the African marsh hollyhock, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant thrives in wet or marshy environments, often growing along the edges of water bodies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a hairy texture, while its flowers are arranged in dense spikes.

Hedwigia africana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with moist soil conditions.
