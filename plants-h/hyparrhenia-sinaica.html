Hyparrhenia sinaica

Hyparrhenia sinaica, commonly known as the Sinai grass, is a perennial grass native to the arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. Hyparrhenia sinaica is adapted to survive in hot, dry environments with poor soil conditions, making it a hardy and resilient species.

Its ability to thrive in such conditions makes it an important plant for stabilizing soils in arid landscapes.
