Hibbertia fruticosa subsp. pilligaensis

Hibbertia fruticosa subsp.

pilligaensis, commonly known as the Pilliga hibbertia, is a shrub native to the Pilliga Forest in New South Wales, Australia. It is characterized by its prickly, hairy stems and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sandy soils and is adapted to the dry, open habitats of the region. Hibbertia fruticosa subsp.

pilligaensis is an important component of the local flora and is often found in heathlands and woodland edges.
