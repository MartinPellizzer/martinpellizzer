Hibbertia tasmanica

Hibbertia tasmanica, commonly known as the Tasmanian sandverbena, is a flowering plant native to Tasmania.

It is a low-growing shrub with slender, woody stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in spring. Hibbertia tasmanica is adapted to sandy soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

It is an important species for local biodiversity and is protected in some areas due to habitat loss.
