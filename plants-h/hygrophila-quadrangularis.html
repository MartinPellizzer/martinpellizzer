Hygrophila quadrangularis

Hygrophila quadrangularis, commonly known as water hyssop, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves, which are typically lance-shaped and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually blue or purple in color, arranged in whorled clusters. It thrives in moist environments, such as wetlands, ponds, and slow-moving streams.

Hygrophila quadrangularis is often used in aquatic gardens and as a decorative plant in water features.
