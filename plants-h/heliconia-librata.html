Heliconia librata

Heliconia librata, commonly known as the red heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its large, colorful bracts that resemble flowers. The plant produces inflorescences with long, narrow, red bracts that surround a cluster of small, white, fragrant flowers. Heliconia librata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical landscapes.

Its structure supports a variety of pollinators, including hummingbirds and bees, making it an important component of its ecosystem.
