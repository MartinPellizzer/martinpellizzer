Hieracium tauschii subsp. vinetorum

Hieracium tauschii subsp.

vinetorum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

The plant is native to Central and Eastern Europe and thrives in moist, grassy habitats. It has been studied for its potential role in ecological succession and as a source of genetic material for cultivated daisies.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both natural and agricultural ecosystems.
