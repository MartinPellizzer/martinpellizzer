Hemionitis delicatessa

Hemionitis delicatessa, commonly known as the delicate hemionitis, is a small, perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. The plant features delicate, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers during the summer months. It is known for its ability to thrive in harsh, arid environments with limited nutrients.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its range.
