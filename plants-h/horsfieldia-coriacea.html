Horsfieldia coriacea

Horsfieldia coriacea, commonly known as the rough bark orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with reddish-brown markings, and it produces small, fleshy fruits.

Horsfieldia coriacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
