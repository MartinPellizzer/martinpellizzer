Hieracium echioides subsp. freynii

Hieracium echioides subsp.

freynii, commonly known as the Freyn's Hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to North America. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and a cluster of small yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky areas. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence.
