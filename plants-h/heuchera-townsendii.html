Heuchera townsendii

Heuchera townsendii, commonly known as Townsend's coral bells, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, pinkish-white flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green foliage that often develops red or purple margins, adding visual interest throughout the growing season. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Heuchera townsendii is valued for its ornamental foliage and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
