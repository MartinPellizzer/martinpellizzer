Haplopappus lanceolatus

Haplopappus lanceolatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf horseweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and yellowish.

It is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions and is often found in the western United States.
