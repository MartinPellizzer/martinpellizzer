Hymenanthes catawbiensis

Hymenanthes catawbiensis, commonly known as the Catawba plum, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin.

Hymenanthes catawbiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
