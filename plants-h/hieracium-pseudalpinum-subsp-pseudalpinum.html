Hieracium pseudalpinum subsp. pseudalpinum

Hieracium pseudalpinum subsp.

pseudalpinum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head.

This subspecies is known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand harsh mountain environments.
