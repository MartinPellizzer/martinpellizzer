Haplopappus rosulatus

Haplopappus rosulatus, commonly known as the clustered pappus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a low-growing, mat-forming structure with narrow, linear leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in shades of yellow or green.

It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe environments.
