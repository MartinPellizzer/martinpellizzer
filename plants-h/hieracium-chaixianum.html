Hieracium chaixianum

Hieracium chaixianum, commonly known as the Chinese hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, rough, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

It is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
