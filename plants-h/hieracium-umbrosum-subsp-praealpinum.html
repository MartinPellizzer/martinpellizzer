Hieracium umbrosum subsp. praealpinum

Hieracium umbrosum subsp.

praealpinum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disc florets.

The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy alpine habitats, preferring cool, moist environments. It has dark green, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
