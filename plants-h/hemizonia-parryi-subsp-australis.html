Hemizonia parryi subsp. australis

Hemizonia parryi subsp.

australis, commonly known as the Desert Evening Primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening.

The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in arid, well-drained soils. It is adapted to hot, dry climates and is often found in desert scrub and rocky habitats.

Its flowers attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths and bats.
