Hemigraphis parabolica

Hemigraphis parabolica, commonly known as the velvetleaf or red-veined nettle, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that often display striking red or purple veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers arranged in spikes, which are typically borne on slender stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Hemigraphis parabolica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in partial shade.
