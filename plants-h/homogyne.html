Homogyne

Homogyne, commonly known as the white-flowered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Homogyne is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and easy care requirements.
