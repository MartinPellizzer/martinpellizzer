Horsfieldia splendida

Horsfieldia splendida, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments, and its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air. Horsfieldia splendida produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a pleasant fragrance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
