Hieracium grovesianum subsp. pseuditalicum

Hieracium grovesianum subsp.

pseuditalicum, commonly known as the false Italian hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and clusters of yellowish to brownish composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has finely divided, hairy leaves and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its ornamental appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
