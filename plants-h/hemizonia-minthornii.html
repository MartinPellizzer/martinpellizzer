Hemizonia minthornii

Hemizonia minthornii, commonly known as the silverleaf hemizonia, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family, commonly referred to as the ice plant family, and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are covered in a fine layer of white, powdery wax, which helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

Hemizonia minthornii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
