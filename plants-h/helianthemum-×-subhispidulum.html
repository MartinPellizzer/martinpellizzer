Helianthemum × subhispidulum

Helianthemum × subhispidulum, commonly known as the hybrid rock rose, is a low-growing shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that are covered in a fine, white pubescence, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
