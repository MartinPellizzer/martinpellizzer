Heliotropium limbatum var. confertifolium

Heliotropium limbatum var. confertifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple, resembling those of the common heliotrope. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The species is notable for its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed areas, making it both a useful ornamental and a potential invasive species in certain regions.
