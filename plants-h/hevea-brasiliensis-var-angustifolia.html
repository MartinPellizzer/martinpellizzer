Hevea brasiliensis var. angustifolia

Hevea brasiliensis var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf rubber tree, is a variant of the rubber tree native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and is primarily cultivated for latex production. The plant is a tropical evergreen tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a dense canopy. Its rubber latex is harvested from the bark, making it economically significant in the rubber industry.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity in its native habitat.
