Helianthemum piliferum f. laxum

Helianthemum piliferum f. laxum, commonly known as the loose-leaved rockrose, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has compact, upright stems covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its leaves are narrow, leathery, and covered in a similar coating of fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
