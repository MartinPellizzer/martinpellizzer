Hieracium sajambrense

Hieracium sajambrense, commonly known as the Sajambrense hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. It produces yellow to orange ray florets surrounding a dense center of disk florets.

Hieracium sajambrense is known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
