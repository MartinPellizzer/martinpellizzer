Hieracium pseudopersonatum

Hieracium pseudopersonatum, commonly known as the purple meadowhawk, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. It produces showy purple to reddish flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
