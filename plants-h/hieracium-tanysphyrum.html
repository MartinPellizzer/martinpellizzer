Hieracium tanysphyrum

Hieracium tanysphyrum, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head.

Hieracium tanysphyrum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions and its role in supporting pollinators.
