Hydrangea arborescens f. acarpa

Hydrangea arborescens f. acarpa, commonly known as the smooth hydrangea, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, rounded clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant's foliage is typically heart-shaped and turns a striking red or orange in the fall. It prefers partial to full shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it ideal for woodland gardens.

Its compact size and attractive flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
