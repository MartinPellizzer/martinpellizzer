Hyparrhenia altissima

Hyparrhenia altissima, commonly known as the tall flatsedge, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are typically green with a slight bluish tinge. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered at the ends of the stems. Hyparrhenia altissima is adapted to a variety of soil types but thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands, wetlands, and along water bodies.
