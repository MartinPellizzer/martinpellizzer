Hieracium bifidum subsp. develatum

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

develatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow flowering heads.

The plant typically has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It produces small, composite flowers that develop into achene seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats such as grasslands and road sides.
