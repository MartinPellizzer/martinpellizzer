Hildegardia cubensis

Hildegardia cubensis, commonly known as the Cuban hildegardia, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
