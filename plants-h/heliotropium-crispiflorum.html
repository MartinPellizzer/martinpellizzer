Heliotropium crispiflorum

Heliotropium crispiflorum, commonly known as the curled flower heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple and pink. The plant has slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or near water sources.

Heliotropium crispiflorum is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
