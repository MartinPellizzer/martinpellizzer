Hieracium pseudoprenanthoides

Hieracium pseudoprenanthoides, commonly known as the false purple hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its rosette growth habit and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster, which bloom in the summer months. It is native to Europe and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Due to its ornamental appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
