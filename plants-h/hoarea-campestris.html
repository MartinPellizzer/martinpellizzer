Hoarea campestris

Hoarea campestris, commonly known as the field horehound, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with hairy, sometimes glandular, stems and leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and disturbed soils.

Hoarea campestris is known for its aromatic leaves and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
