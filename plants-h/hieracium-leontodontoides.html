Hieracium leontodontoides

Hieracium leontodontoides, commonly known as the leopard's bane, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive purple center.

It is often found in shaded or partially sunny environments and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
