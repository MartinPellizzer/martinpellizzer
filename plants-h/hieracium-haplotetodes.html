Hieracium haplotetodes

Hieracium haplotetodes, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and is typically found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers arranged in composite inflorescences, which are typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Hieracium haplotetodes is often considered an invasive species in certain regions due to its rapid spread and competitive growth.
