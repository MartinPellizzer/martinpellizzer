Hieracium ardaricum

Hieracium ardaricum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It produces numerous small, yellow disc flowers that are surrounded by pale yellow to brown ray florets.

Hieracium ardaricum is often considered a weed due to its aggressive growth and ability to spread rapidly.
