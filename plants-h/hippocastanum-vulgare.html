Hippocastanum vulgare

Hippocastanum vulgare, commonly known as the common horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, white or pinkish flowers that appear in spring. The tree produces spiny, capsule-like fruits containing shiny, chestnut-like seeds. Its bark is smooth and greyish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

The plant is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
