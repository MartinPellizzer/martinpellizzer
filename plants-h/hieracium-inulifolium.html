Hieracium inulifolium

Hieracium inulifolium, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has a creeping growth habit with rhizomes that allow it to spread aggressively in suitable habitats. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Hieracium inulifolium is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils across Europe and parts of Asia.
