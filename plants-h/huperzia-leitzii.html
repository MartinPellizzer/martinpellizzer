Huperzia leitzii

Huperzia leitzii, commonly known as the leitzii clubmoss, is a species of fern-like plant belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Its leaves are arranged in whorls and are often slightly curved, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Huperzia leitzii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid, low-light conditions.
