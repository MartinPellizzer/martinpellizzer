Hieracium brachiatum subsp. muranense

Hieracium brachiatum subsp.

muranense, commonly known as the Muran Mountain hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats at high altitudes. It has a hairy stem and leaves with a serrated edge.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
