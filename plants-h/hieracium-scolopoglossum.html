Hieracium scolopoglossum

Hieracium scolopoglossum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are basal, rough, and hairy, while its stems are upright and often branched.

The yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
