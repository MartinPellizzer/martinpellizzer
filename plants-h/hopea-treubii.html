Hopea treubii

Hopea treubii, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth or slightly serrated margin. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in spikes, and its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

Hopea treubii is often found in lowland and hill dipterocarp forests, where it plays an important ecological role.
