Helichrysum rogersianum

Helichrysum rogersianum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, golden-yellow inflorescences that resemble small flowers. The plant is perennial, often growing in a compact shrub form with narrow, gray-green leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its long-lasting blooms make it a favorite in floral arrangements and horticultural displays.
