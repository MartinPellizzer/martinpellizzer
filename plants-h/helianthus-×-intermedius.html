Helianthus × intermedius

Helianthus × intermedius, commonly known as the Teddy Bear Sunflower, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Helianthus annuus (common sunflower) and Helianthus tuberosus (globe thistle).

It is a perennial plant characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers and robust, upright growth habit. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 meters, with broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, producing blooms that are both ornamental and attractive to pollinators.

The species is valued in horticulture for its adaptability and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
