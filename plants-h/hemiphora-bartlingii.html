Hemiphora bartlingii

Hemiphora bartlingii, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and striking plant native to the arid regions of southeastern Australia.

It is a perennial herb with a distinctive appearance, featuring small, pale green to white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a unique morphology, with a short, thick stem and narrow, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in its harsh environment. Its flowers are often described as resembling ghostly apparitions, which gives it its common name.

Hemiphora bartlingii is of interest to botanists due to its specialized adaptations and limited distribution.
