Hevea lutea var. pilosula

Hevea lutea var. pilosula, commonly known as the hairy rubber vine, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems, which give it its common name. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture with prominent veins. It produces small, inconspicuous green flowers followed by spherical, yellowish fruits.

This species is valued for its potential in rubber production and ornamental use in shaded gardens.
