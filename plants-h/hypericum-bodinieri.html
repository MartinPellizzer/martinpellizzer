Hypericum bodinieri

Hypericum bodinieri, commonly known as the golden st. john's wort, is a flowering plant native to China.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and opposite, elliptical leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include yellow-orange flowers and red-tinged stems, making it a striking ornamental plant.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine.
