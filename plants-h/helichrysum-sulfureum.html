Helichrysum sulfureum

Helichrysum sulfureum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that remain colorful even after drying, making it popular in floral arrangements. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its aromatic, serrated leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or sandy environments.

Its resilience and ornamental value make it a favored choice for gardens and horticultural displays.
