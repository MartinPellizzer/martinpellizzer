Hesperis khorasanica

Hesperis khorasanica, commonly known as the Persian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a purple or violet hue at the base. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and produces small, oval-shaped seeds in its fruiting stage. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Hesperis khorasanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
