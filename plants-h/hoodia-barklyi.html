Hoodia barklyi

Hoodia barklyi, commonly known as the desert candy plant, is a succulent shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, branching stems and small, pale green or yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in extreme drought conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy leaves. Its unique chemical composition includes compounds that have been studied for their potential appetite-suppressing properties.

Hoodia barklyi plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing food and shelter for various desert animals.
