Hieracium laevigatum subsp. rigidum

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

rigidum, commonly known as the rigid hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems.

The plant produces yellow flowers in dense clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.
