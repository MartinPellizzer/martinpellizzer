Hymenochilus bicolor

Hymenochilus bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-toned petals and sepals, which often feature a combination of white and purple or pink hues. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are relatively small but showy, with a unique structure that includes a spurred labellum.

Hymenochilus bicolor is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or shaded gardens.
