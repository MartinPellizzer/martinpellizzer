Huperzia phlegmaria var. tardieuae

Huperzia phlegmaria var. tardieuae, commonly known as the slow-growing clubmoss, is a variant of the Huperzia phlegmaria species.

It is a small, evergreen fern-like plant that belongs to the family Huperziaceae. The plant is characterized by its compact, dense growth habit and its ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and arranged in whorls around the stem.

This variety is noted for its slow growth rate and is often found in rocky or forested areas with high humidity.
