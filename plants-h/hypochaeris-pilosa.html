Hypochaeris pilosa

Hypochaeris pilosa, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a single head with ray florets.

Hypochaeris pilosa is an annual or biennial plant that produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.
