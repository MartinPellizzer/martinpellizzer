Hieracium rohacsense subsp. moncalense

Hieracium rohacsense subsp.

moncalense, commonly known as the Alpine groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. It has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces yellow flowers in the summer months.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
