Hypseochloa

Hypseochloa, commonly known as the needlegrass genus, is a group of grasses native to various regions of the world, particularly in temperate and arid climates.

These plants are characterized by their slender, often needle-like leaves and compact growth habit. Many species within this genus are drought-tolerant, making them well-suited for marginal soils and harsh environments. Hypseochloa species typically produce small, spike-like inflorescences that contain minute, wind-pollinated flowers.

Their botanical features make them valuable in ecological studies and as potential forage plants in certain agricultural contexts.
