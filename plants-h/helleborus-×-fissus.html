Helleborus × fissus

Helleborus × fissus, commonly known as the Christmas rose, is a perennial plant native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and striking, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 15–30 cm and prefers moist, well-drained soil in partial shade. It is known for its ability to thrive in woodland environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Helleborus × fissus is also valued for its medicinal properties, though its toxicity requires caution in handling.
