Hydnophytum sumatranum

Hydnophytum sumatranum, commonly known as the sticky bladderwort, is a unique aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its specialized structures called bladder cells, which are used to trap small aquatic organisms. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of other plants or objects in water. Its root system is adapted for anchoring in submerged environments.

Hydnophytum sumatranum plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and providing habitat for microorganisms.
