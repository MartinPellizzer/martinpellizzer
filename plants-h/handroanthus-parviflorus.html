Handroanthus parviflorus

Handroanthus parviflorus, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble small bells, which bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
