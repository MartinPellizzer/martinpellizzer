Hemionitis atreyu

Hemionitis atreyu, commonly known as the Atreyu's hemionitis, is a rare and specialized fern native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments, typically found in cloud forests at high altitudes. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, a common feature among ferns.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, Hemionitis atreyu is considered endangered, making it a subject of conservation interest.
