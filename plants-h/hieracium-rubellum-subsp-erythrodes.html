Hieracium rubellum subsp. erythrodes

Hieracium rubellum subsp.

erythrodes, commonly known as the red hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.

The plant features basal rosettes of dark green, hairy leaves and produces clusters of bright red, dandelion-like flowers. Its flowering period occurs from late spring to early summer, and it is characterized by its showy, elongated inflorescences.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.
