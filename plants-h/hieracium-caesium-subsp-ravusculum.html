Hieracium caesium subsp. ravusculum

Hieracium caesium subsp.

ravusculum, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and basal rosettes of lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers that are typically arranged in dense, flat-topped clusters. It is native to Europe and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Due to its hairy foliage and robust growth, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
