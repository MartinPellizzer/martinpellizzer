Helichrysum pallidum

Helichrysum pallidum, commonly known as the pale immortelle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellow to orange flowers that are often arranged in dense, spherical clusters. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is drought-tolerant. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

Helichrysum pallidum is also known for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
