Hibiscus meraukensis

Hibiscus meraukensis, commonly known as the Javan hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Indonesia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a woody stem that can grow up to several meters in height. Hibiscus meraukensis thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
