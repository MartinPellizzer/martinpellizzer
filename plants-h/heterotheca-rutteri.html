Heterotheca rutteri

Heterotheca rutteri, commonly known as the Rutter's golden aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its yellow to orange-pink flowers that bloom in the summer. Heterotheca rutteri has narrow, linear leaves and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
