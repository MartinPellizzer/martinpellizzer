Helicia bullata

Helicia bullata, commonly known as the silver flame tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and striking clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces woody, capsule-like fruits that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Helicia bullata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a landscape plant.
