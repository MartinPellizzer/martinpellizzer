Hieracium onosmoides subsp. furcellatum

Hieracium onosmoides subsp.

furcellatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features basal leaves that are lanceolate to linear, with a hairy surface, and produces yellow composite flowers in clusters. Its flowering period occurs from late spring to early summer, and it is often found in disturbed or nutrient-rich habitats.

The species is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and is part of the Asteraceae family.
