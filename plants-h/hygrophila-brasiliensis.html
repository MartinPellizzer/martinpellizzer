Hygrophila brasiliensis

Hygrophila brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian water hyssop, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to South America.

It typically grows in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in clusters.

Hygrophila brasiliensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in aquatic ecosystems.
