Helianthus plantagineus

Helianthus plantagineus, commonly known as the swamp sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features large, showy yellow flowers with a distinctive black center, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and often covered in fine, silky hairs.

The swamp sunflower plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
