Hypolepis endlicheriana

Hypolepis endlicheriana, commonly known as the common rock fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves with a distinctive, often serrated margin. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are thick and creeping, allowing it to spread vegetatively.

Hypolepis endlicheriana is valued for its ornamental foliage and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
