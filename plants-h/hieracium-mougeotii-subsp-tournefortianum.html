Hieracium mougeotii subsp. tournefortianum

Hieracium mougeotii subsp.

tournefortianum, commonly known as the Tournefort's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and its leaves are usually lance-shaped with hairy surfaces.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its potential use in traditional herbal medicine.
