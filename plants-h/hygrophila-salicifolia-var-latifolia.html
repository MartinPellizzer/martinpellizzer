Hygrophila salicifolia var. latifolia

Hygrophila salicifolia var. latifolia, commonly known as broad-leaved hygrophila, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features long, narrow leaves that are often broader than the standard variety, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, contributing to its ornamental value.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for water gardens and pond ecosystems.
