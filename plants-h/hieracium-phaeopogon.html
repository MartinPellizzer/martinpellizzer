Hieracium phaeopogon

Hieracium phaeopogon, commonly known as the purple hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, purple to reddish flowers. The plant has hairy leaves and stems, which are typical features of many species in the genus Hieracium. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grasslands.

The species is known for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
