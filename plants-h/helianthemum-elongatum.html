Helianthemum elongatum

Helianthemum elongatum, commonly known as the long-stemmed rockrose, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a vibrant touch to rocky or sandy soils. The plant has hairy, evergreen leaves that are typically narrow and pointed, adapted to withstand dry conditions. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

Helianthemum elongatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
