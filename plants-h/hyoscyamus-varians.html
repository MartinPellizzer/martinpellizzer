Hyoscyamus varians

Hyoscyamus varians, commonly known as the variable henbane, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces distinctive yellow flowers with prominent purple veins, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough, hairy texture.

Hyoscyamus varians contains various alkaloids, making it both toxic and historically significant in herbal medicine.
