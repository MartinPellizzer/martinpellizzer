Holosteum praeumbellatum

Holosteum praeumbellatum, commonly known as the praeumbellate holosteum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a loose umbel, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowering stems are hairy and can grow up to 30 cm in height.

Holosteum praeumbellatum is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
