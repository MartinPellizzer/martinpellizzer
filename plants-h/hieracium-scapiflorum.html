Hieracium scapiflorum

Hieracium scapiflorum, commonly known as the scapiflorous hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf structures. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rough, hairy texture, and the flowers are yellow, arranged in a single head.

It is known for its ability to thrive in various soil types and is often found in disturbed or nutrient-rich environments.
