Humiriastrum glaziovii var. glaziovii

Humiriastrum glaziovii var. glaziovii, commonly known as the Glaziov's coneflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its cylindrical, spiny flower heads are surrounded by narrow, grass-like leaves.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
