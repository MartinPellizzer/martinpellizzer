Huperzia reflexa

Huperzia reflexa, commonly known as the zigzag huperzia, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, horizontally branching structure and small, needle-like leaves that are often curved or twisted. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Americas. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in forests or along stream banks.

Huperzia reflexa is notable for its ability to survive in low-light conditions and its unique morphology that sets it apart from other fern species.
