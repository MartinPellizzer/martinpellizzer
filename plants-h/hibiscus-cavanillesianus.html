Hibiscus cavanillesianus

Hibiscus cavanillesianus, commonly known as the yellow hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers with five petals and a prominent central pistil. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a rough texture and a strong, aromatic scent.

Hibiscus cavanillesianus thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and humid lowlands.
