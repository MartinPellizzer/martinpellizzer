Hibbertia astrophylla

Hibbertia astrophylla, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in open forests and heathlands.

Hibbertia astrophylla is a valuable plant for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
