Hufelandia sulcata

Hufelandia sulcata, commonly known as the African violet, is a tropical plant native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and clusters of showy, long-lasting flowers. The plant thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. It is often cultivated as a popular houseplant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.

Hufelandia sulcata is also known for its ability to bloom throughout the year under proper conditions.
