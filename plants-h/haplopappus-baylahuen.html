Haplopappus baylahuen

Haplopappus baylahuen, commonly known as the Chilean pappus, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. Its small, yellowish flowers are surrounded by a pappus, which aids in wind dispersal of its seeds.

Haplopappus baylahuen is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
