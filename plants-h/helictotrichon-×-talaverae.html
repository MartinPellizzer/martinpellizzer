Helictotrichon × talaverae

Helictotrichon × talaverae, commonly known as the Talavera bluegrass, is a hybrid plant species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and slender, grass-like appearance. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small blue to purple flowers.

Helictotrichon × talaverae is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in arid landscapes.
