Hieracium lagganense

Hieracium lagganense, commonly known as the Laganese hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces yellow to orange ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, typical of the family. It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, preferring well-drained soils.

Hieracium lagganense is known for its low-growing habit and is often found in high-altitude environments.
