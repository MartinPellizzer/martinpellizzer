Heliotropium undulatum var. crispum

Heliotropium undulatum var. crispum, commonly known as the curly-leaved heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, wavy, and often curled leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
