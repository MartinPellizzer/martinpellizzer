Halogeton alopecuroides var. papillosa

Halogeton alopecuroides var. papillosa, commonly known as the sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth form and gray-green leaves covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. It is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots and a high tolerance for drought and salt.

This species is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
