Hieracium florentinum var. acutifolium

Hieracium florentinum var. acutifolium, commonly known as the sharp-leaved oxeye daisy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, sharply pointed leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers with white ray florets, blooming from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade.

Its morphology and growth habits make it a characteristic species of open, sunny habitats.
