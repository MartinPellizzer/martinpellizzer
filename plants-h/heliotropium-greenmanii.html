Heliotropium greenmanii

Heliotropium greenmanii, commonly known as greenman's heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple, resembling those of true heliotropes. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Heliotropium greenmanii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and gardens.
