Hieracium nigrescens subsp. excretum

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

excretum, commonly known as the black hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a distinctive, slightly hairy appearance.

The species is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, forming dense colonies that can dominate vegetation in certain environments.
