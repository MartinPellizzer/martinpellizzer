Hieracium pilosella subsp. subnivescens

Hieracium pilosella subsp.

subnivescens, commonly known as the snow-white hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and forest edges.

The plant features upright stems covered in dense, white to pale yellow hairs, giving it a frosty appearance. Its basal leaves are lobed and hairy, while the upper leaves are narrower and often reduced.

It produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer.
