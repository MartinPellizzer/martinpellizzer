Hallia argentea

Hallia argentea, commonly known as silverleaf lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of South Africa.

It features striking silver-white foliage with a velvety texture, which helps reflect sunlight and reduce water loss. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Hallia argentea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to purify water in aquatic environments.
