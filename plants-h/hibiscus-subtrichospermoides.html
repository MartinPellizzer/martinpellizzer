Hibiscus subtrichospermoides

Hibiscus subtrichospermoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom during the rainy season and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hibiscus subtrichospermoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
