Hieracium paddoense

Hieracium paddoense, commonly known as the Paddock hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and pasturelands. It has rough, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

Hieracium paddoense is often considered a weed due to its rapid growth and ability to spread easily.
