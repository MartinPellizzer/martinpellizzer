Heliotropium astrotrichum

Heliotropium astrotrichum, commonly known as the star-hair heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or arid environments.

Heliotropium astrotrichum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
