Hieracium mallopodum

Hieracium mallopodum, commonly known as the devil's-bit scabious, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are blue or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hieracium mallopodum is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
