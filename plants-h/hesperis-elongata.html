Hesperis elongata

Hesperis elongata, commonly known as the slender candytuft, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with small, four-petaled flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, and it blooms in late spring to early summer.

Hesperis elongata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, fragrant blossoms.
