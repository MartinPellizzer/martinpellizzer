Hieracium lomnicense

Hieracium lomnicense, commonly known as the Lomnica hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are yellow and daisy-like, with ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers.

Hieracium lomnicense is often found in moist environments and can spread through rhizomes, forming dense colonies.
