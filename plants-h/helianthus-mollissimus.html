Helianthus mollissimus

Helianthus mollissimus, commonly known as the softhead sunflower, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial with hairy, soft foliage and yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and produces daisy-like blooms with a central disk surrounded by ray florets. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive texture.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
