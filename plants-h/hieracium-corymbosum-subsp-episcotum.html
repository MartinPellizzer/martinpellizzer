Hieracium corymbosum subsp. episcotum

Hieracium corymbosum subsp.

episcotum, commonly known as the clustered hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and clusters of yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, grassy habitats and has a fibrous root system. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a distinctive lobed or serrated edge.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
