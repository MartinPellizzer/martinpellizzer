Hieracium psepharocephalum

Hieracium psepharocephalum, commonly known as the sand sedge hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in dry, open habitats. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
