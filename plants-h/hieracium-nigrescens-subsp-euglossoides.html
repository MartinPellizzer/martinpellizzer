Hieracium nigrescens subsp. euglossoides

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

euglossoides, commonly known as the black-centred hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disc florets.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a dark central disc.

This subspecies is known for its distinctive dark central flower head, which contrasts with the bright yellow outer petals.
