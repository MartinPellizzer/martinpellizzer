Hieracium scabiosum subsp. virgultorum

Hieracium scabiosum subsp.

virgultorum, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow to orange composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowering period occurs from late spring to early autumn.

It is often found in open habitats and is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens with well-drained soil.
