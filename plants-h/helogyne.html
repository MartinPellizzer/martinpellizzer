Helogyne

Helogyne, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to various regions in South America, particularly the Andes, where it thrives in high-altitude environments. The plants are characterized by their compact growth habit and showy, often fragrant flowers that come in a range of colors. Helogyne species typically have pseudobulbs and are epiphytic or lithophytic, meaning they grow on trees or rocks.

Their unique botanical features make them popular among orchid enthusiasts and botanists alike.
