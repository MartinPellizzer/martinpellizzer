Hieracium macranthoides

Hieracium macranthoides, commonly known as the giant hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers arranged in a single head, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a rough, hairy texture.

Hieracium macranthoides is known for its aggressive growth and is considered an invasive species in some regions.
