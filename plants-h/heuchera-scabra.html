Heuchera scabra

Heuchera scabra, commonly known as the roughleaf plantain, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves that are typically green, red, or purple in color. The plant produces small, white to greenish flowers arranged in a spike during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Heuchera scabra is valued for its ornamental foliage and its ability to stabilize soil in wet habitats.
