Hydrophyllum canadense

Hydrophyllum canadense, commonly known as Canadian water hemp, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is typically found in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and roadside ditches. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct scent when crushed. Its small, white to pale blue flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom from June to September.

Hydrophyllum canadense plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland areas and provides habitat for various insects.
