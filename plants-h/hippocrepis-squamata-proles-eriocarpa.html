Hippocrepis squamata proles eriocarpa

Hippocrepis squamata proles eriocarpa, commonly known as the woolly squamata hawthorn, is a shrub characterized by its dense, woolly foliage.

The plant features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The fruit is a small, dry capsule that contains several seeds.

This species is often found in rocky or dry habitats across southern Europe and parts of North Africa.
