Hepatica nobilis f. obtusa

Hepatica nobilis f. obtusa, commonly known as the broad-leaf hepatica, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves and showy, early spring blooms. The flowers, which range in color from white to blue or purple, are typically borne on short stalks above the foliage. This plant thrives in moist, shaded woodland environments and has a long history of use in traditional herbal medicine.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for both ecological and horticultural interest.
