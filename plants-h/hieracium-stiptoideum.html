Hieracium stiptoideum

Hieracium stiptoideum, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the dandelion family (Compositae) and is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and basal rosette of leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flower heads that are typically clustered in a single inflorescence at the top of the stem. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a slightly rough texture.

Hieracium stiptoideum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
