Hedyotis diantha

Hedyotis diantha, commonly known as the silverweed or white mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It features small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from spring to early summer. The plant has thin, wiry stems and small, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Hedyotis diantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
