Hieracium besenellum

Hieracium besenellum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features bright yellow flowers with a central disk surrounded by ray florets, and it typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while the stems are erect and covered in fine hairs.

Hieracium besenellum is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
