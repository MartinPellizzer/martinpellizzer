Hardenbergia bimaculata var. cordata

Hardenbergia bimaculata var. cordata, commonly known as the wattle or native acacia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and bright yellow spherical flower heads that appear in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and dry climates. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

The variety cordata distinguishes itself with its slightly heart-shaped leaflets, adding a unique visual appeal.
