Homalium surinamensis

Homalium surinamensis, commonly known as the Surinam hibiscus, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Malvaceae, which includes hibiscus and cotton plants. The plant is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. It produces woody capsules containing numerous small seeds, typical of the Malvaceae family.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to humid, warm climates.
