Hieracium tenacicaule

Hieracium tenacicaule, commonly known as the sticky hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features upright, hairy stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with a central disc surrounded by ray florets.

Hieracium tenacicaule is known for its sticky secretions on the leaves and stems, which can make it challenging to handle.
