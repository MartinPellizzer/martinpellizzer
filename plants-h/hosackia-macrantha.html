Hosackia macrantha

Hosackia macrantha, commonly known as the large-flowered false indigo, is a herbaceous perennial native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features palmately compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces showy, violet-blue flowers in late spring. Hosackia macrantha thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and woodland edges.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators and wildlife.
