Hippophae rhamnoides var. procera

Hippophae rhamnoides var. procera, commonly known as the tall sea buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the coastal regions of western Europe and parts of northern Africa.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange berries, which are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, green flowers in the spring. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in maritime climates.

Its hardy nature makes it a valuable species for coastal erosion control and ecological restoration.
