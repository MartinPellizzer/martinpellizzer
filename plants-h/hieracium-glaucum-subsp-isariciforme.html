Hieracium glaucum subsp. isariciforme

Hieracium glaucum subsp.

isariciforme, commonly known as the Isariciform yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

It is often found in alpine and subalpine regions, where it thrives in cooler, temperate climates.
