Hieracium pseudorionii

Hieracium pseudorionii, commonly known as the Greek hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Greece.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing a low-growing, mat-forming structure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium pseudorionii is often found in alpine and subalpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
