Hierochloe australis var. galba

Hierochloe australis var. galba, commonly known as the southern hairgrass, is a grass species native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and its ability to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, spikelet-based flowers. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, contributing to its common name.

This variety is often found in open, disturbed habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in native gardens.
