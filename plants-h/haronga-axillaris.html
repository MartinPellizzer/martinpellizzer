Haronga axillaris

Haronga axillaris, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of East Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pentagonal flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant features serrated, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. Haronga axillaris thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscapes.
