Hieracium ekmanii

Hieracium ekmanii, commonly known as the Ekman's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and sunny exposures. Its leaves are basal, hairy, and often have a serrated margin, while its flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer.

Hieracium ekmanii is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
