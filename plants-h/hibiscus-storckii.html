Hibiscus storckii

Hibiscus storckii, commonly known as the yellow hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers with five petals and a prominent central stamen. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate and have a rough texture, while its bark is smooth and grayish in color.

Hibiscus storckii thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
