Helianthemum paniculatum

Helianthemum paniculatum, commonly known as the creeping snowberry, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and landscaping.
