Hieracium punctillum

Hieracium punctillum, commonly known as the dotted hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disk.

The species is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative reproduction.
