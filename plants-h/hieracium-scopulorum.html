Hieracium scopulorum

Hieracium scopulorum, commonly known as the rock hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium scopulorum is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
