Herpetacanthus neesianus

Herpetacanthus neesianus, commonly known as the New Guinea orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of New Guinea.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, intricate floral structures. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its flowers are often brightly colored, with a complex arrangement of petals and sepals that attract specific pollinators.

Due to its unique beauty and habitat requirements, it is a subject of interest in botanical research and conservation efforts.
