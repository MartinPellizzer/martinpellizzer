Hyparrhenia coleotricha

Hyparrhenia coleotricha, commonly known as the hairy grass or African silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, silver-green blades that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and savannas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated for erosion control and landscaping purposes.
