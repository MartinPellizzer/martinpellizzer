Hieracium canotectum

Hieracium canotectum, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges across Europe and parts of Asia. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rough texture.

Hieracium canotectum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
