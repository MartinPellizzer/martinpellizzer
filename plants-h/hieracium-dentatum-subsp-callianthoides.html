Hieracium dentatum subsp. callianthoides

Hieracium dentatum subsp.

callianthoides, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disc florets.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, meadows, and waste areas. It has serrated, hairy leaves and produces yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in summer.

Its botanical features make it identifiable by its rough texture and distinctive leaf morphology.
