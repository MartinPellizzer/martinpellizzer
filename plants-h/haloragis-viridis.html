Haloragis viridis

Haloragis viridis, commonly known as green waterwheel plant, is a perennial aquatic plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its bright green, waxy leaves that are often submerged or floating on the water surface. The plant has small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, arranged in clusters. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes.

Haloragis viridis plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and fish.
