Hieracium alpinum var. adspersum

Hieracium alpinum var. adspersum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats at high altitudes, where it tolerates cold and poor soil conditions. Its yellow flowers bloom in summer, and the leaves are often covered in fine, white hairs.

This variety is known for its compact growth habit and is sometimes cultivated as a ornamental plant in alpine gardens.
