Hieracium murorum microgene cardiophyllum

Hieracium murorum microgene cardiophyllum, commonly known as the mouse-ear hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and yellow flower heads composed of numerous tiny florets. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. It has a creeping growth habit with rhizomes that allow it to spread vegetatively.

Due to its adaptability, it is considered a common weed in many regions.
