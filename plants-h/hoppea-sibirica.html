Hoppea sibirica

Hoppea sibirica, commonly known as Siberian hoppea, is a deciduous shrub native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern China.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves. The plant produces bell-shaped, white or pale pink flowers in late spring, which are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Hoppea sibirica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
