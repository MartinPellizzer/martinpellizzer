Hemerocallis lilioasphodelus var. nana

Hemerocallis lilioasphodelus var. nana, commonly known as dwarf daylily, is a compact variety of the daylily plant native to eastern North America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, with each flower lasting only one day. It is a hardy perennial that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its small stature and vibrant blooms make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, ornamental plant.
