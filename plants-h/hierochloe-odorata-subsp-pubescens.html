Hierochloe odorata subsp. pubescens

Hierochloe odorata subsp.

pubescens, commonly known as sweet grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its aromatic, silky foliage that emits a pleasant scent when crushed.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks. Its slender, upright stems bear narrow, grass-like leaves that are densely covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional use in floral arrangements and ceremonial practices.
