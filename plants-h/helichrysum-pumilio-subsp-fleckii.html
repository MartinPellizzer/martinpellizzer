Helichrysum pumilio subsp. fleckii

Helichrysum pumilio subsp.

fleckii, commonly known as the dwarf golden geranium, is a small, herbaceous plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom throughout the warmer months.

The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, with a grayish-green coloration. This subspecies is well adapted to arid and rocky environments, thriving in full sun and well-drained soil.

It is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
