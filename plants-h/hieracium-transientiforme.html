Hieracium transientiforme

Hieracium transientiforme, commonly known as the transient hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange composite flowers, which are typically arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. The plant has slender, hairy stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats, often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Hieracium transientiforme is known for its short life cycle and ability to spread rapidly through vegetative reproduction.
