Hieracium vasconicum

Hieracium vasconicum, commonly known as the Basque hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces yellow flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
