Hieracium alfacarensis

Hieracium alfacarensis, commonly known as the Alfacar hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its native range.
