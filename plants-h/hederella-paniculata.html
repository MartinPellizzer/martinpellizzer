Hederella paniculata

Hederella paniculata, commonly known as the panicle ivy, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers arranged in panicles, which bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Its rapid growth habit makes it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover in shaded areas.

Hederella paniculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid environments.
