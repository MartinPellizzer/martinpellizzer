Helianthemum vulgare var. petraeum

Helianthemum vulgare var. petraeum, commonly known as the rock rose, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to southern Europe.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow, typically blooming in spring. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
