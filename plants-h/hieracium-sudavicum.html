Hieracium sudavicum

Hieracium sudavicum, commonly known as the Southern Marsh Hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, meadows, and riverbanks. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are usually hairy and have a rough texture. Its flowering stems bear clusters of small, yellowish to brownish flowers, which are arranged in a composite head.

Hieracium sudavicum is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich soils and is often found in areas with high water tables.
