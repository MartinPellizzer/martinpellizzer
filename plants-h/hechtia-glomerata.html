Hechtia glomerata

Hechtia glomerata, commonly known as the clustered hechtia, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is native to Mexico.

It is a terrestrial bromeliad that typically grows in shaded, humid environments within cloud forests. The plant features rosettes of narrow, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of trichomes, giving them a velvety texture. It produces striking inflorescences with showy, violet-blue flowers that emerge from the center of the rosette.

Hechtia glomerata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
