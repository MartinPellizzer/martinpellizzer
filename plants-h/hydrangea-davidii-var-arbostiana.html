Hydrangea davidii var. arbostiana

Hydrangea davidii var. arbostiana, commonly known as the mountain hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range from white to pink or blue, depending on soil pH. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features broad, serrated leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to the landscape.
