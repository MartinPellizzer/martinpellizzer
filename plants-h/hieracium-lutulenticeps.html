Hieracium lutulenticeps

Hieracium lutulenticeps, commonly known as the sticky-haired hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant is notable for its hairy, often sticky leaves and stems, which give it its common name. It typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows.

Its flowers are yellow and bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
