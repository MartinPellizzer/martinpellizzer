Haplopappus angustifolius subsp. saxatilis

Haplopappus angustifolius subsp.

saxatilis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America. It typically features wiry, succulent stems and small, fleshy leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments.

The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in dense, spherical heads. It is often found in rocky soils and gravelly habitats, showcasing its adaptability to poor, well-drained conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and resilient species suitable for xeriscaping and ecological restoration projects.
