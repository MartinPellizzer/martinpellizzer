Hieracium pseudoalejandrei

Hieracium pseudoalejandrei, commonly known as the false yellow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and basal rosette of leaves. Native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, disc-shaped flower heads.

It is often considered an invasive species in some regions due to its rapid growth and ability to spread easily.
