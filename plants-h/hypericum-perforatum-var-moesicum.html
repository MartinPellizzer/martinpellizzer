Hypericum perforatum var. moesicum

Hypericum perforatum var. moesicum, commonly known as the Moesian St. John's Wort, is a variant of the common St. John's Wort.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its yellow flowers and distinctive leaves with translucent dots. Native to parts of Europe and Asia, this plant thrives in sunny, well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, and it produces small, orange-red berries.

The plant contains hypericin and other compounds, which have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.
