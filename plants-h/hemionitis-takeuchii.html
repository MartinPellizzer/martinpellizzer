Hemionitis takeuchii

Hemionitis takeuchii, commonly known as the Japanese rock fern, is a perennial herb native to Japan and parts of Asia.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, humid environments. The plant has delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish bloom, giving it a unique appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by spore-bearing capsules.

Hemionitis takeuchii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in challenging garden conditions.
