Hieracium inuloides subsp. lasiophyton

Hieracium inuloides subsp.

lasiophyton, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features a rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white to grayish hairs.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typically blooming from June to August. Its leaves and stems are densely pubescent, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.
