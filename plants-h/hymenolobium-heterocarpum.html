Hymenolobium heterocarpum

Hymenolobium heterocarpum, commonly known as the broad-leafed acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive pod fruits that are typically hairy and curved. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry climates.

Its ecological importance includes nitrogen fixation and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
