Hieracium abbrevians

Hieracium abbrevians, commonly known as the short-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves and produces yellow composite flowers. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It has hairy stems and is often found in sunny, open habitats.

The plant is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
