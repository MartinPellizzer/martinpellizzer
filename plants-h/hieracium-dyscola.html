Hieracium dyscola

Hieracium dyscola, commonly known as the rough hawksbeard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun. Its leaves are rough and hairy, with a serrated edge, and its yellow flowers bloom from June to August.

Hieracium dyscola is often found in temperate regions and is known for its resilience in various soil conditions.
