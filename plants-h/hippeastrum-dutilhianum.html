Hippeastrum dutilhianum

Hippeastrum dutilhianum, commonly known as the Dutilhian amaryllis, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a bulbous plant characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces large, showy inflorescences that can reach up to 30 cm in length, making it a popular ornamental plant. Its foliage consists of broad, strap-like leaves that are often used in landscape design.

Hippeastrum dutilhianum thrives in well-drained soil and requires a period of dormancy to produce blooms.
