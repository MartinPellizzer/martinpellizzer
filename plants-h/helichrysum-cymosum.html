Helichrysum cymosum

Helichrysum cymosum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces dense clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers. The plant is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils. Its foliage is often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

The flowers are typically long-lasting when dried, making it a popular choice for dried floral arrangements.
