Hilaria cenchroides var. texana

Hilaria cenchroides var. texana, commonly known as Texas bluegrass, is a native grass species found primarily in the southern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green foliage and forms dense, low-growing turf. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
