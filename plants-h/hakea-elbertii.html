Hakea elbertii

Hakea elbertii, commonly known as the Grey Hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, greyish foliage and cylindrical, woody flower spikes that appear in spring. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers, which are followed by woody, cone-like seed pods. Hakea elbertii is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland heathlands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
