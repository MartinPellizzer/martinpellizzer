Hyacinthoides hispanica

Hyacinthoides hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish bluebell, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It produces striking clusters of bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers in the spring. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that emerge from a basal rosette. It thrives in woodland environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soils.

The Spanish bluebell is also known for its fragrant blooms and is a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
