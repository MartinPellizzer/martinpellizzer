Hyssopus officinalis f. albus

Hyssopus officinalis f. albus, commonly known as white hyssop, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are lanceolate to ovate in shape. The plant produces spikes of small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale blue, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

The plant is valued for its aromatic foliage and historical use in herbal medicine.
