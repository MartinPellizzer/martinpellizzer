Hydrophyllum fendleri var. albifrons

Hydrophyllum fendleri var. albifrons, commonly known as the white-fringed hyssop, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along stream banks. Its small, tubular flowers are typically white or pale blue, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The varietal name "albifrons" refers to its distinctive white-fringed flower petals.
