Hydrangea hortensia

Hydrangea hortensia, commonly known as the bigleaf hydrangea, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy clusters of sterile flowers that range in color from white to pink, blue, or purple depending on soil pH. The plant produces showy bracts that surround the small, true flowers, which are typically white. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Hydrangea hortensia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
