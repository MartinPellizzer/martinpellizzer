Hieracium bicolor

Hieracium bicolor, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, with ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Hieracium bicolor is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and seeds, making it a common weed in agricultural and natural areas.
