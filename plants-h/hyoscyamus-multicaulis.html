Hyoscyamus multicaulis

Hyoscyamus multicaulis, commonly known as the many-stemmed henbane, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late summer. Its fruit is a dry, ribbed capsule that contains numerous small, oblong seeds.

Hyoscyamus multicaulis is known for containing toxic alkaloids, making it a plant of both botanical interest and caution.
