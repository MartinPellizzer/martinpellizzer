Hesperis matronalis var. glabra

Hesperis matronalis var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth white campion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a biennial or short-lived perennial that produces showy clusters of pink or white flowers in its second year. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, fragrant blooms.
