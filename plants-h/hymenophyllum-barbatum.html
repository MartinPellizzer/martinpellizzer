Hymenophyllum barbatum

Hymenophyllum barbatum, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a small, epiphytic fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided leaves that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures, giving it a velvety appearance. This fern thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found on tree trunks or rocks in tropical and subtropical regions. Its fronds are usually less than 10 centimeters in length and have a distinctive, shield-like shape.

The plant's spore-producing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the leaves, contributing to its reproductive strategy.
