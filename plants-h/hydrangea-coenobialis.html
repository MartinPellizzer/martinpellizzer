Hydrangea coenobialis

Hydrangea coenobialis, commonly known as the evergreen hydrangea, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, broad, evergreen foliage that remains green throughout the year. The plant produces showy, rounded flower clusters that range in color from white to pink, depending on soil pH. It thrives in partial shade and requires consistent moisture and well-draining soil.

Hydrangea coenobialis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to form dense, shrub-like growth.
