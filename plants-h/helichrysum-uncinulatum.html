Helichrysum uncinulatum

Helichrysum uncinulatum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellow to orange-colored flowers that resemble tiny sunflowers. The plant produces bract-like leaves that are often mistaken for petals, giving it a unique appearance. It is a hardy perennial that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Helichrysum uncinulatum is also valued for its ornamental use in gardens and as a source of dried floral arrangements.
