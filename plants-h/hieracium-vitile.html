Hieracium vitile

Hieracium vitile, commonly known as the pale hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium vitile is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain habitats.
