Hieracium nigrescens subsp. atratum

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

atratum, commonly known as the black hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in a composite head.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring temperate climates. Its subspecies status highlights subtle differences in morphology compared to other hieracium species.

It is notable for its rapid growth and potential to become invasive in certain environments.
