Hieracium murorum subsp. johanssonii

Hieracium murorum subsp.

johanssonii, commonly known as Johansson's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow flower heads.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and grasslands. It has small, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces and produces numerous tiny seeds in its composite flower heads.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and sometimes endangered species.
