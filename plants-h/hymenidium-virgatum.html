Hymenidium virgatum

Hymenidium virgatum, commonly known as the Virgin's tears, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features delicate, white flowers that bloom in early spring, often in clusters at the tips of its slender stems. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or woodland settings.

Hymenidium virgatum is notable for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
