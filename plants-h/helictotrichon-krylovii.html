Helictotrichon krylovii

Helictotrichon krylovii, commonly known as Krylov's bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky soils. Its inflorescence consists of a narrow spike of small, spikelets that are often tinged with purple.

Helictotrichon krylovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
