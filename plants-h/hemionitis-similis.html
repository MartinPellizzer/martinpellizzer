Hemionitis similis

Hemionitis similis, commonly known as the rock fern, is a unique plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a small, evergreen fern that thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and low moisture. Its fronds are typically narrow and leathery, with a distinctive appearance that helps it blend into its harsh environment. The plant reproduces via spores and is often found growing on limestone or sandstone surfaces.

Due to its specialized habitat, Hemionitis similis is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
