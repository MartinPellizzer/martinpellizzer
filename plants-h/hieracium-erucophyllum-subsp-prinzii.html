Hieracium erucophyllum subsp. prinzii

Hieracium erucophyllum subsp.

prinzii, commonly known as the Prinz's hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features basal leaves that are hairy and lobed, with a rosette structure that helps it conserve moisture. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and it produces small, oval seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is characterized by its distinct morphology and ecological adaptations to arid environments.
