Helianthemum salicifolium var. salicifolium

Helianthemum salicifolium var. salicifolium, commonly known as the willow-leaved sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, willow-like leaves that are usually covered in a fine layer of white hair, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in sunny, well-drained environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in marginal garden conditions.
