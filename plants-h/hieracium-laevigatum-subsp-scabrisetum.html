Hieracium laevigatum subsp. scabrisetum

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

scabrisetum, commonly known as roughleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant has serrated, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are characterized by their rough, scabrous texture, giving it its subspecies name.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
