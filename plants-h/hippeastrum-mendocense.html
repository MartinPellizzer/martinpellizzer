Hippeastrum mendocense

Hippeastrum mendocense, commonly known as the Mendoza amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in late summer or early autumn. The plant produces large, trumpet-shaped blooms in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often with a contrasting green or white throat. Its leaves are strap-like, broad, and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Hippeastrum mendocense is cultivated for its beauty and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil with adequate sunlight.
