Hieracium willeanum

Hieracium willeanum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and clusters of small, yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils across Europe and parts of Asia. It has hairy stems and leaves that are often glandular, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Hieracium willeanum is a perennial herb that can spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain habitats.
