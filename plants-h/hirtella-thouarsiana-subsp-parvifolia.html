Hirtella thouarsiana subsp. parvifolia

Hirtella thouarsiana subsp.

parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved hirtella, is a species native to Madagascar. It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, elliptical leaves.

The plant produces clusters of white to pale yellow flowers, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. Its botanical features include a compact growth habit and a preference for humid, shaded environments.

It is often found in primary rainforest undergrowth, where it contributes to the understory vegetation.
