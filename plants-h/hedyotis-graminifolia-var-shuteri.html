Hedyotis graminifolia var. shuteri

Hedyotis graminifolia var. shuteri, commonly known as the Japanese spurge, is a low-growing perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features small, opposite, ovate leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in shaded gardens.
