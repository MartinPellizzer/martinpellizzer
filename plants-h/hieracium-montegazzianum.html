Hieracium montegazzianum

Hieracium montegazzianum, commonly known as the Montegazza's hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy structure. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine meadows, where it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head.

This species is often found in high-altitude environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
