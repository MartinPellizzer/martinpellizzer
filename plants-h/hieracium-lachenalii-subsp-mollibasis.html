Hieracium lachenalii subsp. mollibasis

Hieracium lachenalii subsp.

mollibasis, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to orange flowers, which are typically arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

The plant has basal and upright leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its roots are fibrous and rhizomatous. It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain regions.
