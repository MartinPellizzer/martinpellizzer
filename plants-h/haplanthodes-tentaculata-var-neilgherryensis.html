Haplanthodes tentaculata var. neilgherryensis

Haplanthodes tentaculata var. neilgherryensis, commonly known as the Neilgherry thornapple, is a climbing shrub native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white, five-petaled flowers. The plant produces small, round, green fruits that turn red when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in dense undergrowth.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the study of tropical flora.
