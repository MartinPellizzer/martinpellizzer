Heracleum pyrenaicum f. setosum

Heracleum pyrenaicum f. setosum, commonly known as the hairy cow parsnip, is a variant of the cow parsnip species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a tall biennial or short-lived perennial plant, typically reaching heights of up to 2 meters. The plant features large, heart-shaped basal leaves with a distinctive hairy texture, and its flowering stems are also covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in umbels, producing a characteristic yellowish-green color.

The plant is often found in moist meadows, stream banks, and other wet habitats.
