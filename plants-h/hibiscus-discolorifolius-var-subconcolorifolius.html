Hibiscus discolorifolius var. subconcolorifolius

Hibiscus discolorifolius var. subconcolorifolius, commonly known as the variegated hibiscus, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking foliage, which features a mix of green, white, and sometimes pink or red veins. The leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and have a velvety texture. It produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red, which bloom during the warmer months.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and thrives in moist, well-drained soil with partial to full sunlight.
