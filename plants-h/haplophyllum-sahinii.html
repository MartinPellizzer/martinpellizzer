Haplophyllum sahinii

Haplophyllum sahinii, commonly known as the Turkish saxifrage, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Turkey.

It belongs to the family Saxifragaceae and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. It has a compact growth habit and is well adapted to harsh, alpine environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
