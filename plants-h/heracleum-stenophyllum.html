Heracleum stenophyllum

Heracleum stenophyllum, commonly known as narrow-leaved cowparley, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring a stout, hollow stem that is often covered in fine hairs. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are usually divided into three parts, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are arranged in umbels and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in full sun to partial shade.
