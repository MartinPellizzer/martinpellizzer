Hieracium knappianum

Hieracium knappianum, commonly known as Knapp's hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe and is characterized by its yellowish to reddish flower heads, which consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. It has a low-growing habit with upright, hairy stems and basal leaves that are usually lance-shaped.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in some regions.
