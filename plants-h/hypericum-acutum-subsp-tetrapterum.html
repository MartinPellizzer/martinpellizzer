Hypericum acutum subsp. tetrapterum

Hypericum acutum subsp.

tetrapterum, commonly known as the four-winged St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer.

The leaves are opposite, simple, and have a distinctive four-lobed appearance, giving the plant its subspecific name. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
