Hibiscus furcellatus var. genuinus

Hibiscus furcellatus var. genuinus, commonly known as the Florida hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in wetlands and along the edges of ponds and swamps. The plant features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a prominent yellow throat. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are covered in fine hairs.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and its ability to thrive in moist, humid environments.
