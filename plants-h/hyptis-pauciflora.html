Hyptis pauciflora

Hyptis pauciflora, commonly known as Mexican mint or blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small, clustered in whorls, and typically have a bluish or purplish hue.

It is often grown as an ornamental plant for its fragrant foliage and attractive floral display.
