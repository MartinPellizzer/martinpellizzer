Hieracium dentatum subsp. trentanum

Hieracium dentatum subsp.

trentanum, commonly known as the Trentanum hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with serrated edges, and the flowers are small, yellowish, and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is known for its rapid spread and is considered invasive in some regions due to its competitive growth habit.
