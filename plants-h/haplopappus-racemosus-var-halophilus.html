Haplopappus racemosus var. halophilus

Haplopappus racemosus var. halophilus, commonly known as saltbush, is a halophytic shrub adapted to saline environments.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and hairy foliage. The plant thrives in arid and coastal regions where salt tolerance is essential for survival. Its leaves are often succulent, helping to store water and reduce water loss.

This species plays a vital role in stabilizing soils in salt-affected areas.
