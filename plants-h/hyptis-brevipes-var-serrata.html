Hyptis brevipes var. serrata

Hyptis brevipes var. serrata, commonly known as the serrated hyptis, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

Its medicinal properties have been studied for their potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.
