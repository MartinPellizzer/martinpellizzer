Heteropappus subserratus

Heteropappus subserratus, commonly known as the serrated pappus plant, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disc florets. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with serrated edges, contributing to its common name.

Heteropappus subserratus is adapted to thrive in sandy soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
