Hieracium leucothyrsoides

Hieracium leucothyrsoides, commonly known as the white thistle.

It is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae, characterized by its spiny, hairy stems and pale yellow to white flowers. Native to Europe, it thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant has basal leaves that are often lobed and covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are typically arranged in a composite head.

It is a perennial species that produces achenes as its seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
