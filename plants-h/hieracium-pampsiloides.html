Hieracium pampsiloides

Hieracium pampsiloides, commonly known as the prairie hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as prairies and roadsides. It features basal rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters.

Hieracium pampsiloides is often found in regions with dry, sandy soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.
