Hypechusa lutea var. laevigata

Hypechusa lutea var. laevigata, commonly known as the smoothleaf yellowbell, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, waxy leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The species is valued for both its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
