Hymenophyllum haematochroum

Hymenophyllum haematochroum, commonly known as the blood-stained moss, is a species of liverwort belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like appearance and is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rock crevices. The plant has small, closely spaced leaves that are usually less than 1 centimeter in length. Its name derives from the reddish hue of its leaves, which can appear blood-red under certain conditions.

This moss is often used in horticultural settings for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
