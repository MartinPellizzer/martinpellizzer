Hieracium parietale

Hieracium parietale, commonly known as the wall groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or disturbed soils, often found on walls, cliffs, and roadsides. It has hairy, rough leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Hieracium parietale is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environments and is often considered a pioneer species in ecological succession.
