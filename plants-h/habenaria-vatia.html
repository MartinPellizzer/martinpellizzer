Habenaria vatia

Habenaria vatia, commonly known as the violet orchid, is a species of terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and showy violet or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky areas. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in a raceme.

Habenaria vatia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
