Hordeum pamiricum

Hordeum pamiricum, commonly known as the Pamir barley, is a wild cereal grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, spikelet-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its grains are small and hard, often used in traditional local cuisines or as forage for livestock.

Hordeum pamiricum plays a significant role in the ecological balance of its native habitats.
