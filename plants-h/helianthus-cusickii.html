Helianthus cusickii

Helianthus cusickii, commonly known as Cusick's sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its vibrant yellow petals and dark center. It has narrow, linear leaves and can reach heights of up to three feet.

Helianthus cusickii is often found in open areas such as grasslands and canyons, where it thrives in full sun.
