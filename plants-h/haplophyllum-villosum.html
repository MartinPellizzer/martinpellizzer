Haplophyllum villosum

Haplophyllum villosum, commonly known as the silky rockrose, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its succulent stems and leaves help it retain moisture in arid environments.

It is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
