Hieracium calycinum

Hieracium calycinum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette, and are covered in fine hairs that help reduce water loss.

Hieracium calycinum produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
