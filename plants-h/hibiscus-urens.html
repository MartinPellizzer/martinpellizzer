Hibiscus urens

Hibiscus urens, commonly known as the yellow wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, often found in moist, shaded environments. Hibiscus urens has a fibrous root system and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural and garden settings.
