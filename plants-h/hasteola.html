Hasteola

Hasteola, commonly known as the wild oat, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce numerous tiny grains. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks. Its leaves are linear and slightly hairy, and it has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil.

Hasteola is an important forage plant for livestock and is also used in ecological restoration projects.
