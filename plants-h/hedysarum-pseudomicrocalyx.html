Hedysarum pseudomicrocalyx

Hedysarum pseudomicrocalyx, commonly known as the slender vetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces cylindrical, dehiscent pods that contain several elliptical seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Hedysarum pseudomicrocalyx is valued for its nitrogen-fixing abilities and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
