Haplopappus angustifolius subsp. angustifolius

Haplopappus angustifolius subsp.

angustifolius, commonly known as narrowleaf hawksbeard, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a rough, hairy texture, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slender leaves and smaller flower heads compared to other subspecies within the species.
