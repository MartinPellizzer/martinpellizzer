Habenaria maitlandii

Habenaria maitlandii, commonly known as the Maitland's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and linear to lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a loose raceme, often with a distinct lip that is adorned with intricate patterns. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is typically found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
