Hieracium diaphanoides subsp. bastardianum

Hieracium diaphanoides subsp.

bastardianum, commonly known as the bastardian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in a single, terminal head.

The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grasslands. It has a taproot system and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smaller flower heads and slightly different leaf morphology compared to other Hieracium species.
