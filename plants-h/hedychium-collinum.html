Hedychium collinum

Hedychium collinum, commonly known as the mountain ginger or golden torch ginger, is a tropical perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy yellow-orange flowers that resemble torches. The plant produces fragrant, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. Hedychium collinum thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forested areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
