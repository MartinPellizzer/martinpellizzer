Hericinia

Hericinia, commonly known as the rose of Jericho, is a unique succulent plant native to the deserts of North Africa and the Middle East.

It has a distinctive appearance with its spiny, fleshy stems and small, waxy leaves that resemble rose petals. This plant is known for its remarkable ability to survive extreme drought conditions by entering a dormant state. When water becomes available, it can quickly revive and bloom.

Its botanical features make it a fascinating subject for study in arid ecosystems.
