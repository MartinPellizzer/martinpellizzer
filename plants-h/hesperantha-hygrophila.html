Hesperantha hygrophila

Hesperantha hygrophila, commonly known as the water lily of the genus Hesperantha, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant features attractive, showy flowers that are usually pink or white, with a distinctive, showy spathe that surrounds the smaller, true flowers. Its leaves are broad and floating, contributing to its aesthetic appeal in aquatic settings.

Hesperantha hygrophila is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to provide habitat for aquatic life.
