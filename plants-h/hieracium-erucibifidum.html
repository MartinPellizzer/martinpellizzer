Hieracium erucibifidum

Hieracium erucibifidum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble clusters of small flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are narrow and often have a serrated edge, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is known for its ability to spread rapidly through rhizomes, making it an invasive species in some regions.
