Hemerocallis flava var. coreana

Hemerocallis flava var. coreana, commonly known as the Korean yellow daylily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Korea.

It belongs to the Hemerocallidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features strap-like, grass-like leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that last only one day. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

This variety is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
