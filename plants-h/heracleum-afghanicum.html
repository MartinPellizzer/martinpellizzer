Heracleum afghanicum

Heracleum afghanicum, commonly known as Afghan cowparley, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to regions of Central Asia, including Afghanistan and Pakistan. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small white to greenish-yellow flowers arranged in compound umbels. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional herbal remedies for various ailments.
