Haplopappus decurrens

Haplopappus decurrens, commonly known as the creeping pappus, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, succulent stems and small, clustered leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, followed by small, wind-dispersed seeds enclosed in a pappus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and gravelly slopes.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is considered a valuable plant for stabilizing soil in arid environments.
