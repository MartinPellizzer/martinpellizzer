Heuchera brevistaminea

Heuchera brevistaminea, commonly known as the short-stamined alumroot, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from deep red to purple and green. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Heuchera brevistaminea is valued for its ornamental foliage and its ability to bloom reliably in cooler climates.
