Harpullia petiolaris var. decidens

Harpullia petiolaris var. decidens, commonly known as the golden glory tree, is a deciduous species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that turn yellow before falling in autumn. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive addition to botanical gardens and landscapes.
