Hydrolea

Hydrolea, commonly known as the waterleaf, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Amaranthaceae.

These plants are native to various regions in Africa, South America, and parts of Asia. Hydrolea species are typically herbaceous, with alternate, often succulent leaves that can store water, aiding in their survival in arid environments. They produce small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Some species of Hydrolea are used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
