Hymenoxys canescens var. nevadensis

Hymenoxys canescens var. nevadensis, commonly known as the Nevada hymenoxys, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in arid environments. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, often in clusters at the ends of its stems. Its leaves are linear and covered in a fine, white pubescence, giving it a dusty appearance.

This variety is adapted to withstand drought and extreme temperature fluctuations, making it well-suited to its native habitat.
