Helianthus mexicanus

Helianthus mexicanus, commonly known as Mexican sunflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico and the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant orange to red ray florets and yellow disk florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowering period occurs from summer to fall, producing numerous blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
