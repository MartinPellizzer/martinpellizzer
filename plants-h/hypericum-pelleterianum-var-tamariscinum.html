Hypericum pelleterianum var. tamariscinum

Hypericum pelleterianum var. tamariscinum, commonly known as the golden-stemmed St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its medicinal properties have been historically noted, though it contains phototoxic compounds that require caution in use.
