Hymenaea stigonocarpa var. olfersiana

Hymenaea stigonocarpa var. olfersiana, commonly known as the African padauk, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets. The tree produces yellow to orange flowers that are borne in racemes, followed by woody pods containing flat, brown seeds. It is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction.

The plant also has cultural and medicinal significance in various African communities.
