Hesperis frutescens

Hesperis frutescens, commonly known as sweet rocket, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and has pinnately lobed leaves. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Sweet rocket is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a mild, peppery scent.
