Hippocrepis balearica subsp. grosii

Hippocrepis balearica subsp.

grosii, commonly known as the Balearic bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Balearic Islands. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers.

The flowers are typically pale blue or purple, and they bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lanceolate leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils.

It is a rare and endemic species, making it of interest to botanists and conservationists.
