Hedychium convexum

Hedychium convexum, commonly known as the golden torch ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its striking, orange-red bracts that resemble a torch, which are actually modified leaves. Its flowers are fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hedychium convexum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
