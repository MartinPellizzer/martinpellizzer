Hiraea acutifolia

Hiraea acutifolia, commonly known as the sharp-leaved hiraea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that are often branched and covered in fine hairs. The leaves are lance-shaped, with sharp tips and a rough texture, contributing to its common name. It produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months.

This plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
