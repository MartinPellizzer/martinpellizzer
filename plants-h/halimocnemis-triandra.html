Halimocnemis triandra

Halimocnemis triandra, commonly known as the African snowberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, white, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces round, blue-black berries that are edible but have a mild, slightly bitter taste. Halimocnemis triandra is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
