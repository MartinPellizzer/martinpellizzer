Hypoxis sobolifera

Hypoxis sobolifera, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a rhizomatous root system and produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers that bloom at night. The plant has lance-shaped, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscapes.

Its unique nocturnal blooming habit and medicinal properties make it a valuable plant in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
