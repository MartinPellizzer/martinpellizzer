Hieracium basispodium

Hieracium basispodium, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist grasslands and meadows, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers have ray florets that are usually yellow.

Hieracium basispodium is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in disturbed soils.
