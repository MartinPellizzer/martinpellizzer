Hieracium cappadocicum

Hieracium cappadocicum, commonly known as Cappadocian hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

It is often considered a weed in agricultural areas due to its rapid growth and ability to spread easily.
