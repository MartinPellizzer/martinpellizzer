Hyptis fruticosa

Hyptis fruticosa, commonly known as Brazilian hyssop, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves and spikes of blue or purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Hyptis fruticosa is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional remedies.
