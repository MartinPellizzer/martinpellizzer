Hieracium murorum subsp. percrenatum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

percrenatum, commonly known as the creeping hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its low-growing habit and yellow flowers.

The plant typically has a mat-forming growth pattern, with numerous flowering stems arising from a central rosette of leaves. Its leaves are usually basal, lanceolate, and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are arranged in dense clusters.

This subspecies is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain habitats.
