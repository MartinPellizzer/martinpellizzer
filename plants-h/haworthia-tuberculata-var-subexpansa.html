Haworthia tuberculata var. subexpansa

Haworthia tuberculata var. subexpansa, commonly known as the Zebra Haworthia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-shaped foliage with distinctive white or pale green tubercles that resemble a zebra's stripes. The leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted for storing water in arid environments. It produces small, white flowers on slender stalks during the warmer months.

This variety is popular among succulent enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
