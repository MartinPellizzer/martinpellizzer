Habenaria simillima

Habenaria simillima, commonly known as the similar orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically pale green or white, with a distinctive three-lobed lip. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found growing in forest understories.

Due to its delicate appearance and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its range.
