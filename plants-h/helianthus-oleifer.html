Helianthus oleifer

Helianthus oleifer, commonly known as the oil sunflower, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to the southern United States.

This plant is characterized by its large, yellow, sun-like flowers and robust, hairy stems. It produces a high amount of oil-rich seeds, which are used in both culinary and industrial applications. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention.

Helianthus oleifer thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy and adaptable species.
