Hieracium sherwalii

Hieracium sherwalii, commonly known as the Sherwal's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range in color from yellow to reddish-orange.

Hieracium sherwalii is noted for its hardy nature and ability to adapt to harsh environmental conditions.
