Hieracium Subsericeum Subsp. Adenodontum

Hieracium subsericeum subsp. adenodontum is a lesser-known member of the dandelion family known for its delicate yellow flowers and hairy stems. Found primarily in moist, grassy habitats across northern regions, this plant thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its scientific name highlights its classification within the broader Hieracium genus, which includes many common and widespread species. Though often mistaken for its more familiar relatives, this subspecies possesses unique characteristics that set it apart. Due to its limited distribution and subtle appearance, it remains a subject of interest for botanists and nature enthusiasts alike.
