Hieracium lipocolope

Hieracium lipocolope, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. It has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

Hieracium lipocolope is a perennial species that can spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
