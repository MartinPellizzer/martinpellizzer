Hieracium transiens subsp. winklerianum

Hieracium transiens subsp.

winklerianum, commonly known as the Winkler's hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, disturbed habitats such as meadows and streambanks.

Its subspecific classification highlights its distinct morphological and genetic traits compared to other hawkweed species.
