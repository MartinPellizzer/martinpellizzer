Hymenopappus corymbosus

Hymenopappus corymbosus, commonly known as the clustered hymenopappus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and linear, arranged in a basal rosette, and the stems are often hairy.

Hymenopappus corymbosus is adapted to arid environments, with features that help it conserve water and withstand drought conditions.
