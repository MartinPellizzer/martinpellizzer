Hieracium villosiceps subsp. comatulum

Hieracium villosiceps subsp.

comatulum, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, rough texture.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It is native to Europe and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
