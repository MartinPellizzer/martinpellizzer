Hieracium mirificissimum

Hieracium mirificissimum, commonly known as the magnificent hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
