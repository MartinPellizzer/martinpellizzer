Hueblia calycina

Hueblia calycina, commonly known as the scarlet cup, is a striking member of the Cupuliferae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant red, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy texture.

The fruit is a dry, three-lobed capsule that releases tiny seeds when mature.
