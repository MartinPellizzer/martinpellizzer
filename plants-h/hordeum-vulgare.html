Hordeum vulgare

Hordeum vulgare, commonly known as barley, is a grass species native to the temperate regions of Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical spikes that bear small, three-lobed grains. Barley is an important cereal crop, valued for its high nutritional content and versatility in food and industrial applications. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with leaves that are narrow and parallel-veined.

Its flowering occurs in the summer, and it is often cultivated for its grains, which are used in brewing, animal feed, and human consumption.
