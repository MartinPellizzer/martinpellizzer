Hieracium pallescens subsp. gelmianum

Hieracium pallescens subsp.

gelmianum, commonly known as the pale hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils.

The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in late spring to early summer. It has a taproot system and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves.

This subspecies is often found in arid or semi-arid environments, showing some adaptability to drier conditions.
