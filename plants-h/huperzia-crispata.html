Huperzia crispata

Huperzia crispata, commonly known as the curly huperzia, is a species of fern-like plant belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, wiry, and often curling fronds that give it a unique appearance. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its sporophylls are arranged in a spiral pattern, and it reproduces through spores rather than seeds.

Huperzia crispata is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can be found in various botanical gardens and collections.
