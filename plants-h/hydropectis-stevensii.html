Hydropectis stevensii

Hydropectis stevensii, commonly known as the Florida waterleaf, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features alternate, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to moist, well-drained soils.

Hydropectis stevensii is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.
