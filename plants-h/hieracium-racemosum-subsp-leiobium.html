Hieracium racemosum subsp. leiobium

Hieracium racemosum subsp.

leiobium, commonly known as the clustered hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a flowering stem that bears a cluster of yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers have five petals and are arranged in a raceme.

This subspecies is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
