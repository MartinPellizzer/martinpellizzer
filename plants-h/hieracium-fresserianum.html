Hieracium fresserianum

Hieracium fresserianum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its flowering stems are usually unbranched and bear several flower heads.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
