Haloragis acutangula f. dentata

Haloragis acutangula f. dentata, commonly known as the toothed willow-herb, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland or aquatic environments. The plant features slender, often branched stems and small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spikes. Its leaves are usually narrow and may have serrated edges, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in riparian zones and supports local wildlife.
