Hieracium murorum subsp. distichanthelum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

distichanthelum, commonly known as the two-flowered hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow flower heads.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky soils. It has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces numerous small, clustered flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique floral arrangement and is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological and genetic traits.
