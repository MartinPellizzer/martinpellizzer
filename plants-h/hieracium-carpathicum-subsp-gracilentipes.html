Hieracium carpathicum subsp. gracilentipes

Hieracium carpathicum subsp.

gracilentipes, commonly known as the Carpathian hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the Carpathian region of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain insect species.
