Hedyotis purpurea var. floridana

Hedyotis purpurea var. floridana, commonly known as Florida bluetail, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with small, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny, bright purple flowers. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and a capsule-like fruit that releases tiny seeds.

It plays a role in supporting local wildlife and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
