Hieracium porrigens subsp. virenticeps

Hieracium porrigens subsp.

virenticeps, commonly known as the green-topped hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow flowering heads.

The subspecies is distinguished by its greenish-tipped flower heads and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It typically thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats such as meadows and roadsides.

The plant produces numerous seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.
