Hoarea appendiculatum

Hoarea appendiculatum, commonly known as the mountain holly or the white holly, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows at high altitudes, often in rocky or alpine environments. The plant is characterized by its small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a delicate and ornamental appearance. Its leaves are usually narrow and leathery, adapted to the harsh conditions of its native habitat.

Hoarea appendiculatum is valued for its resilience and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
