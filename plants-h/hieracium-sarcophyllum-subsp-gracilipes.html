Hieracium sarcophyllum subsp. gracilipes

Hieracium sarcophyllum subsp.

gracilipes, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, preferring partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are lanceolate, with a hairy surface, and the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

The subspecies gracilipes is distinguished by its slender stems and relatively small, delicate flowers compared to other members of the species.
