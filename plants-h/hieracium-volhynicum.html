Hieracium volhynicum

Hieracium volhynicum, commonly known as Volhynian hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring well-drained conditions. Its leaves are basal, rough, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium volhynicum is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain habitats.
