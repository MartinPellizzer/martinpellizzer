Hieracium woloszczakii

Hieracium woloszczakii, commonly known as Woloszczak's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
