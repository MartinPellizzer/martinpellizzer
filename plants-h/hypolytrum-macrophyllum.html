Hypolytrum macrophyllum

Hypolytrum macrophyllum, commonly known as the broad-leaved spikerush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical inflorescence that bears small, greenish flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often clustered at the base, forming a dense rosette. It thrives in shallow water or moist soils and is often found in marshes and swamps.

Hypolytrum macrophyllum plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
