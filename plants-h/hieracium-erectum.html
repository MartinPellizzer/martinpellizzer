Hieracium erectum

Hieracium erectum, commonly known as the upright hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 100 centimeters, featuring basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster, with ray florets that are often shorter than the central disk florets.

Hieracium erectum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
