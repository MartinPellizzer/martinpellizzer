Helianthemum × rigualii

Helianthemum × rigualii, commonly known as the hybrid rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Helianthemum nummularium and Helianthemum italicum. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a vibrant touch to its natural habitat.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
