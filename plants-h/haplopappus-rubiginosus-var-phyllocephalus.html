Haplopappus rubiginosus var. phyllocephalus

Haplopappus rubiginosus var. phyllocephalus, commonly known as the red-stemmed pappus plant, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, while its flowers are small and clustered in spherical heads.

It produces a distinctive pappus, a feathery structure that aids in wind dispersal of its seeds.
