Helenium ooclinium

Helenium ooclinium, commonly known as the orange hawkweed, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, orange-red ray flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Helenium ooclinium is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
