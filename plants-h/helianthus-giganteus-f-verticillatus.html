Helianthus giganteus f. verticillatus

Helianthus giganteus f. verticillatus, commonly known as the giant sunflower variant with whorled leaves, is a striking plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its showy yellow ray flowers and robust composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to heights of 3 to 6 feet, featuring broad, lance-shaped leaves arranged in whorls around the stem. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, producing large, sun-like blooms that attract pollinators.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
