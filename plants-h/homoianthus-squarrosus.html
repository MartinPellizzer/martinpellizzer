Homoianthus squarrosus

Homoianthus squarrosus, commonly known as the woolly phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly covering on its stems and leaves, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming during the rainy season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
