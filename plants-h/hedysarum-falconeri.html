Hedysarum falconeri

Hedysarum falconeri, commonly known as the Falconeri's vetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to harsh alpine conditions.

Hedysarum falconeri plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native environment.
