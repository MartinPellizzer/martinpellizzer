Hieracium nigrisquameum

Hieracium nigrisquameum, commonly known as the black-scaled hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and foliage with a rough, hairy texture. The plant features yellow to orange flowering heads surrounded by bracts that are often darkly colored. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, stiff hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Hieracium nigrisquameum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
