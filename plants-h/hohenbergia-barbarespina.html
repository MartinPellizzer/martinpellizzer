Hohenbergia barbarespina

Hohenbergia barbarespina, commonly known as the spiny hohenbergia, is a striking bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in stiff, spiny bristles, giving it a unique and somewhat intimidating appearance. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red. Its foliage is often variegated with white or silver markings, adding to its ornamental value.

Hohenbergia barbarespina prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
