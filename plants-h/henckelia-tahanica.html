Henckelia tahanica

Henckelia tahanica, commonly known as the Tahanica tree, is a species native to the island of Tahan in the Philippines.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in lowland rainforests. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered, often white or pale yellow, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local communities.
