Heliconia aureorosea

Heliconia aureorosea, commonly known as the golden rose heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its striking, pendulous inflorescences. The flowers are typically orange to yellow in color, with a distinctive, bract-like structure that encloses the true flowers. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for tropical gardens and botanical collections.
