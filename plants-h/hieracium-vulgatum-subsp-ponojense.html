Hieracium vulgatum subsp. ponojense

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

ponojense, commonly known as the Pannonian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm.

The plant features basal rosettes of lanceolate leaves and produces yellowish to reddish flowers in a single terminal head. Its inflorescence is composed of ray florets surrounded by a dense cluster of disc florets.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are often glandular and can produce a mild irritant when touched.
