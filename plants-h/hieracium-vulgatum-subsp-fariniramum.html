Hieracium vulgatum subsp. fariniramum

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

fariniramum, commonly known as the farinose hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm.

The plant features basal leaves that are broadly lanceolate and covered with a fine, powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a single head, with ray florets that are often reflexed.

The species is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its adaptability in various environmental conditions.
