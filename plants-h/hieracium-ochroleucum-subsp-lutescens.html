Hieracium ochroleucum subsp. lutescens

Hieracium ochroleucum subsp.

lutescens, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm.

The plant features bright yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, with hairy stems and leaves that are usually lance-shaped. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is characterized by its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower structure.
