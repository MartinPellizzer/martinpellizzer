Hieracium anisolobum

Hieracium anisolobum, commonly known as the unequal-lobed hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the daisy family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with rough textures, and it produces small, elongated flower heads.

Hieracium anisolobum is often considered a weed but can be found in various ecological settings across its native range.
