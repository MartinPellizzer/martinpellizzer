Hieracium bifidum subsp. hemilonchodes

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

hemilonchodes, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in grassy habitats and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and often have a distinctive serrated edge, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a cluster.

It is a member of the genus Hieracium, which includes many species of hawkweeds known for their ecological importance in various ecosystems.
