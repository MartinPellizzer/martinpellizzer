Hymenopappus nudatus

Hymenopappus nudatus, commonly known as the smooth hymenopappus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and feathery pappus structures. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as rocky soils and open plains. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and bloom in the spring.

The plant's adaptability to harsh conditions makes it an important component of its native ecosystems.
