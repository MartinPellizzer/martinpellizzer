Hymenolobium complicatum

Hymenolobium complicatum, commonly known as the warty acacia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in small, warty tubercles. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
