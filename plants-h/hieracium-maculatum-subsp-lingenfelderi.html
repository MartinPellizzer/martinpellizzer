Hieracium maculatum subsp. lingenfelderi

Hieracium maculatum subsp.

lingenfelderi, commonly known as the Lingenfelder's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to central and eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to orange-yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a hairy texture, and it produces a basal rosette of foliage.

The subspecies is distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and subtle morphological differences from other hawkweed species.
