Helichrysum homilochrysum

Helichrysum homilochrysum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its dense, yellow-orange inflorescences that resemble small, delicate flowers. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically clustered in compact, spherical heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

The leaves are usually narrow and linear, with a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
