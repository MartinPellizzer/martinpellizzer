Hyptis reticulata var. longiflora

Hyptis reticulata var. longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered hyptis, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by aromatic leaves and showy flowers.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
