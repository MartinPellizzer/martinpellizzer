Hieracium argutisquamum

Hieracium argutisquamum, commonly known as the roughleaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and is characterized by its hairy, serrated leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped with rough textures, and it produces numerous small florets arranged in a composite head.

The species is often found in open habitats and is adapted to temperate climates.
