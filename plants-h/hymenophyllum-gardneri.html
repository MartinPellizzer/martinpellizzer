Hymenophyllum gardneri

Hymenophyllum gardneri, commonly known as the Gardner's spikemoss, is a small, evergreen fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, moss-like appearance and is typically found in shaded, humid environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has slender, branching stems covered with small, overlapping leaves that are usually less than 1 centimeter in length. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are clustered on the underside of the leaves and produce spores for propagation.

This species is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist, shaded conditions.
