Hippeastrum brevitubum

Hippeastrum brevitubum, commonly known as the short-stemmed amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant produces large, strap-shaped leaves that are typically broad and dark green. It is a bulbous plant that thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate sunlight.

Hippeastrum brevitubum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
