Hieracium protractifrons

Hieracium protractifrons, commonly known as the long-beaked hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features bright yellow ray florets surrounded by a dense cluster of tubular disk florets. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Hieracium protractifrons is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
