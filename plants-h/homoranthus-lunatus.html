Homoranthus lunatus

Homoranthus lunatus, commonly known as the moonflower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white, night-blooming flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers open in the evening and close by dawn, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique blooming pattern.
