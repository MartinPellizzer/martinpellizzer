Hymenidium stellatum

Hymenidium stellatum, commonly known as the starflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped flowers, which are typically white or pale yellow and bloom in late spring. The plant has lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. Hymenidium stellatum thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
