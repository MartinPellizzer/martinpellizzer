Hibbertia dissitiflora

Hibbertia dissitiflora, commonly known as the scattered-flower hibbertia, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia.

It features slender, wiry stems and produces small, yellow flowers with five petals. The leaves are usually narrow and arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native environment.
