Hypericum henryi subsp. uraloides

Hypericum henryi subsp.

uraloides, commonly known as the Ural goldthread, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with numerous small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces red, berry-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and herbal practices.
