Hymenopus arachicarpa

Hymenopus arachicarpa, commonly known as the rock fig, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and unique, fig-like fruits. The plant thrives in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its fruit, which is edible, has a sweet, fig-like flavor and is often consumed by both humans and animals.

The species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing food and shelter for various wildlife.
