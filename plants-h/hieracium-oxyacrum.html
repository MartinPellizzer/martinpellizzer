Hieracium oxyacrum

Hieracium oxyacrum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disk.

Hieracium oxyacrum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is considered a weed in some agricultural areas.
