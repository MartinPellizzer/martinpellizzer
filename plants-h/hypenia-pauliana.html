Hypenia pauliana

Hypenia pauliana, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, star-shaped fruits. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly hairy surface.

The plant is adapted to thrive in humid, tropical climates with well-drained soil.
