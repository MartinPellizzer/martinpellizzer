Hieracium branae

Hieracium branae, commonly known as the bracken hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and stems that bear yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. It is a perennial species that can spread through both seeds and vegetative reproduction.

Due to its adaptability, it is considered a common weed in agricultural and disturbed areas.
