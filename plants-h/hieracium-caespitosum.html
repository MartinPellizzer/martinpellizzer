Hieracium caespitosum

Hieracium caespitosum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant features upright, hairy stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow, with a distinctive central disk surrounded by ray florets, and it produces seeds in a fluffy, wind-dispersed form.

Hieracium caespitosum is known for its aggressive growth and can become invasive in certain regions.
