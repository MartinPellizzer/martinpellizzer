Habranthus concordiae

Habranthus concordiae, commonly known as the Concordia grass-of-parnassus, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy white to pale blue flower heads that resemble a crown. The plant produces small, tubular florets surrounded by pale bracts, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and unique morphology, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
