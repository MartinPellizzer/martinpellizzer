Hesperis capensis

Hesperis capensis, commonly known as the Cape bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant features showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

Hesperis capensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is adapted to well-drained soils.
