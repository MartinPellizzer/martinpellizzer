Hypericum geminiflorum subsp. geminiflorum

Hypericum geminiflorum subsp.

geminiflorum, commonly known as the twin-flower St. John's wort, is a shrub native to the western United States. It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters of two, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental plant in native gardens.
