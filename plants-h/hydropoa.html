Hydropoa

Hydropoa, commonly known as the water lily, is a genus of aquatic flowering plants that thrive in freshwater environments.

These plants are characterized by their floating leaves and showy, waterborne flowers that bloom in the early morning. Hydropoa species typically have a rhizomatous root system that anchors them in shallow water or muddy substrates. Their leaves are often broad and rounded, with a waxy surface to repel water.

The flowers are usually pink, white, or yellow, and they play a crucial role in providing habitat for aquatic life.
