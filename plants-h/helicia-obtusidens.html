Helicia obtusidens

Helicia obtusidens, commonly known as the broad-leaved helenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Theaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often clustered at the base of the plant. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible, orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
