Hyphaene ventricosa subsp. benguelensis

Hyphaene ventricosa subsp.

benguelensis, commonly known as the Benguela palm, is a species of palm native to coastal regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional construction.

The plant thrives in sandy soils and is adapted to withstand coastal conditions such as salt spray and strong winds. It produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clustered, fibrous fruits.

The botanical features of this subspecies make it an important component of coastal ecosystems and a valuable resource for local communities.
