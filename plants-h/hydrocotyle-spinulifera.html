Hydrocotyle spinulifera

Hydrocotyle spinulifera, commonly known as the spiny marsh mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a herbaceous perennial with a rosette growth habit, featuring heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, spiny hairs. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters during the warm season. Its leaves are typically succulent and can be used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Hydrocotyle spinulifera thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.
