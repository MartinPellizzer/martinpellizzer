Haematorchis

Haematorchis, commonly known as the blood orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, often red or purple-colored flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

The botanical features of Haematorchis make it a visually distinctive and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
