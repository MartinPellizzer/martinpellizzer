Hibiscus rockii

Hibiscus rockii, commonly known as the rock hibiscus, is a shrub native to China, particularly found in rocky and arid environments.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically blooming in summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils. Hibiscus rockii is drought-tolerant and prefers full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in dry regions.
