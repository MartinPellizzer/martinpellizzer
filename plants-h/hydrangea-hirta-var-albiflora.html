Hydrangea hirta var. albiflora

Hydrangea hirta var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered hydrangea, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flower clusters that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its foliage is dark green and often has a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to add a striking visual element to gardens.
