Hyacinthus orientalis subsp. albulus

Hyacinthus orientalis subsp.

albulus, commonly known as the white hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its clusters of fragrant, six-petaled flowers that are typically white or pale blue in color.

The plant produces strap-shaped leaves that are grass-like in appearance and are often arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and its ability to bloom early in the spring.
