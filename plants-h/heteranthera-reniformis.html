Heteranthera reniformis

Heteranthera reniformis, commonly known as the broadleaf water hyssop, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features oval, dark green leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or violet, arranged in spikes at the ends of the stems. It thrives in wet environments such as marshes and pond edges, and is often used in water gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
