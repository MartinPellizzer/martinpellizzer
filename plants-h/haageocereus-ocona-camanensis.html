Haageocereus ocona-camanensis

Haageocereus ocona-camanensis, commonly known as the Ocona cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus with a tall, cylindrical stem that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant features prominent areoles covered with white, woolly hair, and it produces large, showy yellow flowers during the spring. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

The plant is adapted to withstand drought and temperature extremes, making it a hardy species for desert landscapes.
