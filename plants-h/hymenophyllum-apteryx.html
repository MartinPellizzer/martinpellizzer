Hymenophyllum apteryx

Hymenophyllum apteryx, commonly known as the silver fern, is a small, evergreen fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are covered in a fine layer of silvery hair, giving it its common name. This plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments, such as rock crevices and forest floors, where it thrives in high humidity. The fronds are usually less than 10 centimeters in length and have a distinctive, lace-like appearance.

Hymenophyllum apteryx is known for its ability to retain moisture and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
