Hymenoxys olivacea

Hymenoxys olivacea, commonly known as the olive sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its yellow-orange flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hymenoxys olivacea is known for its drought tolerance and is often found in desert landscapes.
