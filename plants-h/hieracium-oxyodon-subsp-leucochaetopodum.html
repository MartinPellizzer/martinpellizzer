Hieracium oxyodon subsp. leucochaetopodum

Hieracium oxyodon subsp.

leucochaetopodum, commonly known as the white-haired hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and basal rosettes of narrow, hairy leaves.

The plant produces yellow ray florets surrounded by a dense cluster of tubular disc florets. It is native to Europe and is often found in grassy areas and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain regions.
