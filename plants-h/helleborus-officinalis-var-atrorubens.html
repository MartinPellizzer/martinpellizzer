Helleborus officinalis var. atrorubens

Helleborus officinalis var. atrorubens, commonly known as the purple-stemmed hellebore, is a perennial plant native to central and southern Europe.

It features dark green, waxy leaves with a distinctive purple or reddish stem, and it produces dark purple to black flowers in late winter or early spring. The plant is part of the Ranunculaceae family and is known for its medicinal properties, though it contains toxic compounds.

It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
