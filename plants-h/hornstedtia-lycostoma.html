Hornstedtia lycostoma

Hornstedtia lycostoma, commonly known as the horned orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, horn-like floral structures. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
