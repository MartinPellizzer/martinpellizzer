Hirtella hispidula

Hirtella hispidula, commonly known as the hairy hirtella, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its small, oval leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a rough texture. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink and are clustered in small inflorescences.

Hirtella hispidula is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
