Hypoxis rigidula var. rigidula

Hypoxis rigidula var. rigidula, commonly known as the rigid false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellowish to orange flowers with a distinctive, honey-scented aroma. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and rocky habitats. It is a perennial species that blooms from late spring to early autumn.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
