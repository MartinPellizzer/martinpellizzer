Hieracium nigrescens var. subrotundum

Hieracium nigrescens var. subrotundum, commonly known as the black hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically lanceolate to oblanceolate, with a rough, hairy texture. This variant is distinguished by its subrotundate leaf shape, which sets it apart from other members of the species.

It is native to Europe and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.
