Hypericum selaginella

Hypericum selaginella, commonly known as the narrowleaf St. John's wort, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and rocky slopes, reaching heights of up to 30 inches. The plant features opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its flowering stems are often branched and bear small, clustered flowers.

Hypericum selaginella is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
