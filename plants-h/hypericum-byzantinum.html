Hypericum byzantinum

Hypericum byzantinum, commonly known as the Byzantine St. John's Wort, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Hypericum byzantinum is well adapted to rocky and dry soils, making it a hardy plant in Mediterranean climates.

It is also noted for its medicinal properties, historically used for its purported healing benefits.
