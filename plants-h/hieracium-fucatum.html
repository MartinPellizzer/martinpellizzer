Hieracium fucatum

Hieracium fucatum, commonly known as the black hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are dark green, rough, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive blackish-purple center.

Hieracium fucatum is often found in disturbed habitats and can form dense colonies, making it an invasive species in some regions.
