Heliconia hirsuta var. cannoidea

Heliconia hirsuta var. cannoidea, commonly known as the red-veined heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, colorful inflorescences that consist of brightly colored bracts surrounding a central spadix. The plant's leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.

The flowers are primarily pollinated by birds, particularly hummingbirds, making it an important species in ecological studies.
