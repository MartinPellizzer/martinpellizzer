Hedysarum wrightianum

Hedysarum wrightianum, commonly known as Wright's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces cylindrical, hairy pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.

Hedysarum wrightianum plays a role in nitrogen fixation and is a valuable forage plant for wildlife.
