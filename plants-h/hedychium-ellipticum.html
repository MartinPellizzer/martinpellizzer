Hedychium ellipticum

Hedychium ellipticum, commonly known as the elliptic ginger or yellow flame ginger, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, with a distinctive, narrow floral tube. Hedychium ellipticum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden areas.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which can extend from late spring through early autumn.
