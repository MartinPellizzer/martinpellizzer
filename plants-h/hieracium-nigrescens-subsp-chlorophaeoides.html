Hieracium nigrescens subsp. chlorophaeoides

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

chlorophaeoides, commonly known as the greenish hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.

The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. Its leaves are often covered with fine hairs, and the stems are usually hairy as well.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
