Hopea splendida

Hopea splendida, commonly known as the golden banyan, is a large evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and large, glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, three-lobed fruits containing seeds. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its hard, durable wood.

The plant also has cultural significance in some regions and is used in traditional medicine.
