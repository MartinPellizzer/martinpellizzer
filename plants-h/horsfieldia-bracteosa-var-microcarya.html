Horsfieldia bracteosa var. microcarya

Horsfieldia bracteosa var. microcarya, commonly known as the small-fruited orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various forest animals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, organic-rich substrates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
