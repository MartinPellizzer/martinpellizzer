Horkelia fusca subsp. parviflora

Horkelia fusca subsp.

parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered horkelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit.

The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically linear to lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and slopes.
