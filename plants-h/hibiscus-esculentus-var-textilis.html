Hibiscus esculentus var. textilis

Hibiscus esculentus var. textilis, commonly known as okra, is a tropical annual herb native to Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant produces edible, fibrous pods that are widely used in cuisines around the world. Its botanical features include a robust stem with soft, hairy surfaces and a system of veins that support its large leaves.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both its nutritional value and its fibrous material used in traditional textiles.
