Helichrysum picardii subsp. virescens

Helichrysum picardii subsp.

virescens, commonly known as the green everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant produces small, cylindrical flower heads that are often used in dried floral arrangements due to their long-lasting nature. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its botanical features include narrow, linear leaves and a compact growth habit.
