Hakea teretifolia subsp. teretifolia

Hakea teretifolia subsp.

teretifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hakea, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are arranged in dense clusters.

The plant produces yellow flowers in summer, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
