Hibiscus furcellatus var. diodon

Hibiscus furcellatus var. diodon, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be found in gardens and natural habitats.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
