Hesperis planchoniana

Hesperis planchoniana, commonly known as the Alpine honesty, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with characteristic cruciform flowers. The plant produces small, white flowers with four petals, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Hesperis planchoniana is often found in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
