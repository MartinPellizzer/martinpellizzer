Hyalolaena paniculata

Hyalolaena paniculata, commonly known as the silver grass, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
