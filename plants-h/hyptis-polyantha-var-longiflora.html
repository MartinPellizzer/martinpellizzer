Hyptis polyantha var. longiflora

Hyptis polyantha var. longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered hyptis, is a species of flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
