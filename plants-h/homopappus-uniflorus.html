Homopappus uniflorus

Homopappus uniflorus, commonly known as the single-flower sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers with prominent central disk flowers surrounded by ray florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and visually appealing species within its native habitat.
