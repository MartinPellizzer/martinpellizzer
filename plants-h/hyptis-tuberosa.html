Hyptis tuberosa

Hyptis tuberosa, commonly known as Mexican mint marigold, is a perennial herb native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, hairy, and often glandular leaves that are aromatic when crushed. Its flowers are typically blue to purple, clustered in dense whorls at the ends of the branches.

Hyptis tuberosa is known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
