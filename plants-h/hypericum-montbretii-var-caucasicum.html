Hypericum montbretii var. caucasicum

Hypericum montbretii var. caucasicum, commonly known as the Caucasian St. John's Wort, is a perennial shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique and ornamental addition to horticultural collections.
