Hieracium lachenalii subsp. subampliatum

Hieracium lachenalii subsp.

subampliatum, commonly known as the broad-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe. It is characterized by its basal rosette of broadly ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
