Hieracium murorum subsp. chattum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

chattum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of broadly lanceolate leaves and produces yellow flowers in a single terminal head.

The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. It has a fibrous root system and is known for its rapid spread through vegetative reproduction.

The subspecies chattum is distinguished by its smaller, more compact growth compared to other subspecies of Hieracium murorum.
