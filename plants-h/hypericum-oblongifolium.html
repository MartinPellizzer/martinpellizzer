Hypericum oblongifolium

Hypericum oblongifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved St. John's wort, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features upright, branched stems covered in yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, with a distinct reddish hue on the underside. This plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in dry meadows and rocky areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
