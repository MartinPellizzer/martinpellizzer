Hieracium erythrocarpum subsp. microtricholepium

Hieracium erythrocarpum subsp.

microtricholepium, commonly known as the red-fruited hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow to orange composite flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its small, glandular trichomes on the leaves and stems. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

The plant produces small, red or orange fruits that are adapted for wind dispersal.
