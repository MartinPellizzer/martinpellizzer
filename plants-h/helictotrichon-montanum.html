Helictotrichon montanum

Helictotrichon montanum, commonly known as mountain bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green blades and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, prairies, and rocky slopes. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue to greenish flowers.

Helictotrichon montanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in disturbed areas.
