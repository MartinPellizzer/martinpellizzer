Helianthus utahensis

Helianthus utahensis, commonly known as Utah thistle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its spiny leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as deserts and sagebrush steppe. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in dry conditions.

Utah thistle plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in disturbed soils.
