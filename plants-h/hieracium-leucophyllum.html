Hieracium leucophyllum

Hieracium leucophyllum, commonly known as the white-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually white or pale green, covered in a fine layer of silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators.
