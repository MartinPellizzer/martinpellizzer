Hieracium toftense

Hieracium toftense, commonly known as Toft's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
