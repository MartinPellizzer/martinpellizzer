Hordeum elongatum

Hordeum elongatum, commonly known as the slender barley, is a wild grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. Hordeum elongatum is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates.

It plays a role in ecological succession and is sometimes used in horticultural or ornamental settings.
