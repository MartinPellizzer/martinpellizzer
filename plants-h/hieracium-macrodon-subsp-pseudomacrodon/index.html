Hieracium Macrodon Subsp. Pseudomacrodon

Hieracium macrodon subsp. pseudomacrodon is a lesser-known member of the dandelion family known for its striking appearance and ecological significance. This perennial plant is commonly found in grasslands and open woodlands across parts of Europe and Asia. Its large, showy yellow flowers and distinctive foliage make it a notable sight in the wild. Despite its beauty, it is often overlooked due to its similarity to other hawkweed species. Understanding this subspecies is key to appreciating the biodiversity of its native habitats.
