Hieracium mestocephalum

Hieracium mestocephalum, commonly known as the gloomy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf structures. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring moist to moderately dry conditions. Its leaves are basal, lanceolate, and often covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of habitats, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
