Hippeastrum formosissimum

Hippeastrum formosissimum, commonly known as the Brazilian fire lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces a tall, leafless stem that bears numerous flowers, each with six petals and a prominent central column. Its bulbs are perennial, and it thrives in warm, humid conditions with well-draining soil.

Hippeastrum formosissimum is popular as a ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
