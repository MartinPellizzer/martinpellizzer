Hieracium subramosum var. rhomboides

Hieracium subramosum var. rhomboides, commonly known as the rhomboid hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped or rhomboid in form, giving the species its common name.

The flowers are yellow and bloom from late spring to early autumn, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
