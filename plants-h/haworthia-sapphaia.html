Haworthia sapphaia

Haworthia sapphaia, commonly known as the purple firetail, is a compact, slow-growing succulent native to South Africa.

It features a rosette of fleshy, bluish-green leaves with a distinctive purple or red tinge along the edges, especially when exposed to bright light. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers on slender stems during the spring or summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for rock gardens or indoor cultivation.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
