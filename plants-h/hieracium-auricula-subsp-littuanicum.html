Hieracium auricula subsp. littuanicum

Hieracium auricula subsp.

littuanicum, commonly known as the Lithuanian cow parsley, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Eastern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides, preferring moist and well-drained soils. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette, and are usually hairy and lobed.

This subspecies is distinguished by its slightly smaller flowers and shorter peduncles compared to other members of the *Hieracium* genus.
