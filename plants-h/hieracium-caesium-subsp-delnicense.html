Hieracium caesium subsp. delnicense

Hieracium caesium subsp.

delnicense, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It has a fibrous root system and produces numerous flowering stems that bear small, daisy-like flowers.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
