Hesperis foliosa

Hesperis foliosa, commonly known as the leafy bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are alternate and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Hesperis foliosa thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
