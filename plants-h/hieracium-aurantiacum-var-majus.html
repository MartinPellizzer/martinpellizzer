Hieracium aurantiacum var. majus

Hieracium aurantiacum var. majus, commonly known as the greater golden hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in late summer, and it produces small, oval seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The leaves are usually hairy and alternate along the stem, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
