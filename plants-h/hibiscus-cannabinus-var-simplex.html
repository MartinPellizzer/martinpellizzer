Hibiscus cannabinus var. simplex

Hibiscus cannabinus var. simplex, commonly known as wild hibiscus or cannabis hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to deep pink. The plant produces fibrous stalks that have been historically used for fiber production. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often with a rough texture.

This variety is closely related to the cannabis plant and shares some chemical similarities.
