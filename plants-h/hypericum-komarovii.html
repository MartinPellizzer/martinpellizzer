Hypericum komarovii

Hypericum komarovii, commonly known as Komarov's St. John's Wort, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the genus Hypericum, which includes over 400 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer, and its woody stems covered in small, orange-red glands. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in alpine or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
