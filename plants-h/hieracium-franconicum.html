Hieracium franconicum

Hieracium franconicum, commonly known as Franconian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces, and its yellow flowers bloom from June to August.

Hieracium franconicum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in garden settings.
