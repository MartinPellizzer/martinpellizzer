Habenaria disoides

Habenaria disoides, commonly known as the orchid with a unique floral structure, is a terrestrial orchid species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in a raceme, with a distinctive, elongated lip. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
