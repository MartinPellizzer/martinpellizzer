Heterospathe elata var. guamensis

Heterospathe elata var. guamensis, commonly known as the Guam fern, is a species native to the tropical regions of the Pacific, particularly Guam.

It is a member of the Polypodiaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that often display a striking contrast of colors. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually bipinnate, with small, linear pinnae that contribute to its ornamental appeal.

This fern is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
