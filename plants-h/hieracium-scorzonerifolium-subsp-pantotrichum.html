Hieracium scorzonerifolium subsp. pantotrichum

Hieracium scorzonerifolium subsp.

pantotrichum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems, which give it its common name.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
