Hieracium florentinum subsp. basiphyllum

Hieracium florentinum subsp.

basiphyllum, commonly known as the Florentine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. It has a fibrous root system and can spread vegetatively through stolons.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
