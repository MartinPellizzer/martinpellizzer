Hosackia oblongifolia var. angustifolia

Hosackia oblongifolia var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf prairie clover, is a perennial herb native to the central United States.

It is characterized by its slender, angular stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinct appearance. This plant thrives in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators and maintaining native plant communities.
