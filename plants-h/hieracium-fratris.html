Hieracium fratris

Hieracium fratris, commonly known as the brother's dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and serrated leaves, typical of the genus Hieracium. Native to Europe, it thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant is often found in temperate regions and is known for its ability to spread through wind-dispersed seeds.

Its botanical features make it a common sight in open habitats across its native range.
