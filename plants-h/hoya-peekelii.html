Hoya peekelii

Hoya peekelii, commonly known as the Peckel's waxplant, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and waxy, elliptical leaves that are often variegated with white or cream markings. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in spring or early summer. Hoya peekelii is a succulent plant that thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

It is popular among houseplant enthusiasts due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
