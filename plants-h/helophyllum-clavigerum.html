Helophyllum clavigerum

Helophyllum clavigerum, commonly known as the clustered spirea, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescences of white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds.

Helophyllum clavigerum is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
