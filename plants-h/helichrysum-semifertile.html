Helichrysum semifertile

Helichrysum semifertile, commonly known as the golden marguerite, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that resemble daisies. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Helichrysum semifertile is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
