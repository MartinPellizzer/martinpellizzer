Hydrostachys decaryi

Hydrostachys decaryi, commonly known as the African waterwheel, is a flowering plant native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along riverbanks. The plant features whorled leaves that are often submerged or floating, and it produces small, white or pale yellow flowers. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to waterlogged soils, aiding in its survival in aquatic habitats.

Hydrostachys decaryi is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in water gardens.
