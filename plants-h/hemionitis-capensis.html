Hemionitis capensis

Hemionitis capensis, commonly known as the Cape hemionitis, is a small, perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Hemionitidaceae and is characterized by its rosette-like growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Hemionitis capensis is noted for its drought tolerance and ability to survive in harsh environmental conditions.
