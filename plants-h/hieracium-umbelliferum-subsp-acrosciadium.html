Hieracium umbelliferum subsp. acrosciadium

Hieracium umbelliferum subsp.

acrosciadium, commonly known as the umbrella hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

It is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain habitats.
