Helichrysum amplectens

Helichrysum amplectens, commonly known as the golden marguerite, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, silver-gray leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Helichrysum amplectens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
