Hydrangea brevipes

Hydrangea brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed hydrangea, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and prefers moist, well-drained soil in partial to full shade. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape, with a smooth margin and a glossy green color.

Hydrangea brevipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
