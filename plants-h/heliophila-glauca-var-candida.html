Heliophila glauca var. candida

Heliophila glauca var. candida, commonly known as the white waterplantain, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland and marsh habitats.

It features slender, erect stems that bear small, white, showy flowers arranged in dense clusters. The leaves are lance-shaped, smooth, and often covered in a waxy coating that aids in water retention. This plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires consistently moist soil conditions.

It is valued for its ecological role in supporting aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
