Hieracium canum subsp. polianthes

Hieracium canum subsp.

polianthes, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features basal rosettes of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in a dense, flat-topped inflorescence. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is often found in disturbed or nutrient-rich habitats.

The species is characterized by its finely divided leaves and is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
