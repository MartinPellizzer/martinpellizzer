Haplopappus lanceolatus var. strictus

Haplopappus lanceolatus var. strictus, commonly known as the narrowleaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
