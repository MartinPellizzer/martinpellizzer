Hieracium lehmannii

Hieracium lehmannii, commonly known as Lehmann's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
