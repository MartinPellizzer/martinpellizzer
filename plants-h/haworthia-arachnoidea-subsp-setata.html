Haworthia arachnoidea subsp. setata

Haworthia arachnoidea subsp.

setata, commonly known as the spider haworthia, is a compact, succulent plant native to South Africa. It features fleshy, triangular leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs, giving it a unique, almost otherworldly appearance.

The leaves are typically green with distinct markings, and the plant produces small, white flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it an ideal choice for succulent enthusiasts.

Its slow growth rate and striking appearance make it a popular ornamental plant in arid and semi-arid environments.
