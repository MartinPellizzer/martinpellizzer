Hosta tsushimensis f. albiflora

Hosta tsushimensis f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered hosta, is a cultivar of the hosta plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are a deep green with a waxy texture. The plant produces striking white flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a unique visual contrast to its foliage. It thrives in partial to full shade and requires moist, well-drained soil.

This cultivar is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
