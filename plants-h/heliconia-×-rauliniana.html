Heliconia × rauliniana

Heliconia × rauliniana, commonly known as the red heliconia, is a hybrid plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble flowers. The plant produces long, segmented inflorescences with bright red or orange hues, which serve as an attractant for pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are large, broad, and often have a waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

Heliconia × rauliniana is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is used in tropical landscaping and as a cut flower.
