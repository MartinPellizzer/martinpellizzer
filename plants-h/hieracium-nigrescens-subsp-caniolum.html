Hieracium nigrescens subsp. caniolum

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

caniolum, commonly known as the black-centred hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its dark green, hairy leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has a taproot system and produces numerous flowering stems that bear small, clustered florets.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dark purple to black central disc florets and its tendency to form dense colonies.
