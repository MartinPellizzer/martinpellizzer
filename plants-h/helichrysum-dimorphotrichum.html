Helichrysum dimorphotrichum

Helichrysum dimorphotrichum, commonly known as the Cape gold or yellow ragwort, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, branched hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in gardens, though it can be invasive in some regions.
