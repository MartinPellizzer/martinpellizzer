Hieracium pilosissimum subsp. barbatiforme

Hieracium pilosissimum subsp.

barbatiforme, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is often found in temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
