Hieracium ulocomum

Hieracium ulocomum, commonly known as the lesser hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and features basal rosettes of narrow, hairy leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, flat-topped inflorescences, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Hieracium ulocomum is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
