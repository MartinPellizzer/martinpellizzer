Helicteres semiglabra var. flagellaris

Helicteres semiglabra var. flagellaris, commonly known as the silky senna, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, slender pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
