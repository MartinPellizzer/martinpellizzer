Hieracium rugelii

Hieracium rugelii, commonly known as Rugel's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring well-drained conditions. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rough, hairy texture, and the flowers are yellow, forming a flat-topped inflorescence.

Rugel's hawkweed is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
