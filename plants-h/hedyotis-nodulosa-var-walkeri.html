Hedyotis nodulosa var. walkeri

Hedyotis nodulosa var. walkeri, commonly known as the nodular inchplant, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches a height of about 10-20 centimeters. The plant features small, bright green leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and flowering habit.
