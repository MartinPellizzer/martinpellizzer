Hieracium lanceolatum var. transalpinum

Hieracium lanceolatum var. transalpinum, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine soils, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in clusters.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in alpine ecosystems and is often found in meadows and grasslands at high altitudes.
