Hieracium decolorans

Hieracium decolorans, commonly known as the common yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 50 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while the flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Hieracium decolorans is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and can form dense colonies in suitable habitats.
