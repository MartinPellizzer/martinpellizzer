Hordeum murinum subsp. setariurum

Hordeum murinum subsp.

setariurum, commonly known as mouse barley, is a grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

This subspecies is adapted to thrive in harsh conditions, making it a resilient and widespread weed.
