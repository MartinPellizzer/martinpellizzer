Hebe cockayneana

Hebe cockayneana, commonly known as the Stewart Island hebe, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

Hebe cockayneana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
