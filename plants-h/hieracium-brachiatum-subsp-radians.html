Hieracium brachiatum subsp. radians

Hieracium brachiatum subsp.

radians, commonly known as the radiate hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and stems that bear showy yellow flowers with ray florets.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It has hairy stems and leaves that are often glandular, which can aid in reducing water loss.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
