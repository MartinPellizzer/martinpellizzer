Haplopappus monactis

Haplopappus monactis, commonly known as the single-actinotaxis, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. Its seeds are surrounded by a pappus, which aids in wind dispersal.

Haplopappus monactis is typically found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.
