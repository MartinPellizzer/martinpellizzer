Helopus trichopus

Helopus trichopus, commonly known as the hairy helopus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in rocky and well-drained soils, preferring full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
