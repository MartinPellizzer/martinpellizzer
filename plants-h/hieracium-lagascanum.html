Hieracium lagascanum

Hieracium lagascanum, commonly known as the Lagascan hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers consist of yellow disk flowers surrounded by pale yellow to white ray florets.

Hieracium lagascanum is often found in disturbed habitats and can spread rapidly, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
