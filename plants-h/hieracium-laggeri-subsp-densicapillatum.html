Hieracium laggeri subsp. densicapillatum

Hieracium laggeri subsp.

densicapillatum, commonly known as the dense-haired hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Central Europe. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and basal rosettes of hairy, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers arranged in a single head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky soils.

Its subspecies name, *densicapillatum*, refers to the dense, hairy covering on its stems and leaves.
