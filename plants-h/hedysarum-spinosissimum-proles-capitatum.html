Hedysarum spinosissimum proles capitatum

Hedysarum spinosissimum proles capitatum, commonly known as the prickly milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and trifoliate leaves, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that are clustered in heads, giving it its specific epithet "proles capitatum.". It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
