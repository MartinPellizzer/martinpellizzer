Hyoscyamus agrestis var. integrifolius

Hyoscyamus agrestis var. integrifolius, commonly known as the rough hyoscyamus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features a robust taproot and produces erect, branched stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height. The leaves are broadly ovate with coarsely toothed margins, and the flowers are yellowish-green with a distinctive blue or purple tinge. It blooms from late spring to early autumn, producing small, clustered flowers that are followed by spherical, greenish-brown fruits.

This plant is often found in dry, disturbed soils and is considered a member of the nightshade family, Solanaceae.
