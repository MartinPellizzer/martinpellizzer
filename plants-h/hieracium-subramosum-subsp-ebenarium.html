Hieracium subramosum subsp. ebenarium

Hieracium subramosum subsp.

ebenarium, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn, and it has a rosette of basal leaves with hairy surfaces. Its stems are usually reddish or purple-hued and often bear short, branched hairs.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaf arrangement.
