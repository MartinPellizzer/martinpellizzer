Humboltia macrocardia

Humboltia macrocardia, commonly known as the large-leafed hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, flat seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Humboltia macrocardia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
