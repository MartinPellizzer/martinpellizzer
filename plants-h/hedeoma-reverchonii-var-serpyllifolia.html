Hedeoma reverchonii var. serpyllifolia

Hedeoma reverchonii var. serpyllifolia, commonly known as hairy tansy, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, branched clusters. It thrives in dry, sunny environments such as rocky slopes and open woodlands.

Its aromatic foliage and unique morphology make it a notable species in its ecological community.
