Hemionitis bonariensis

Hemionitis bonariensis, commonly known as the Brazilian fern, is a unique species of fern native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically light green and finely divided. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce new growth from the base, allowing it to spread gradually.

Hemionitis bonariensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
