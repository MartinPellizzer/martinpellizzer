Hieracium subelongatum subsp. sertigense

Hieracium subelongatum subsp.

sertigense, commonly known as the long-stemmed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow ray florets that surround a central disk of tubular flowers.

The plant typically has slender, hairy stems and basal leaves that are lanceolate in shape. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the hawkweed genus, with specific adaptations to its ecological niche.
