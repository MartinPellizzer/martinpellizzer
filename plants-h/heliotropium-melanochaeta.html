Heliotropium melanochaeta

Heliotropium melanochaeta, commonly known as the black-haired heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Heliotropium melanochaeta is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
