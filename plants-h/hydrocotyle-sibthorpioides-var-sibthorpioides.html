Hydrocotyle sibthorpioides var. sibthorpioides

Hydrocotyle sibthorpioides var. sibthorpioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water shield, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Africa.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. The plant has heart-shaped, floating leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. Its small, yellow flowers appear in clusters and are pollinated by insects.

The plant plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
