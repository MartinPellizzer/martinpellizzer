Hieracium sylvaticum var. angustifolium

Hieracium sylvaticum var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and meadows, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters. Its flowering period occurs from late spring to early summer, and it is characterized by its hairy stems and involucral leaves.

It is often found in association with other woodland flora, contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
