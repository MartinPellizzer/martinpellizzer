Hieracium scabratum

Hieracium scabratum, commonly known as rough hawksbeard, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils. The plant features upright stems covered in rough, bristly hairs, and its yellow flowers are arranged in a composite head with ray florets. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, contributing to its common name.

It is an annual or biennial plant that blooms from late spring to early autumn.
