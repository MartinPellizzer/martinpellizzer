Hexadica cochinchinensis

Hexadica cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese orchid or Vietnamese violet, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, herbaceous growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and purple, often arranged in a spike. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
