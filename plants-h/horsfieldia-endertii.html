Horsfieldia endertii

Horsfieldia endertii, commonly known as the Endert's orchid, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often display vibrant colors. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Horsfieldia endertii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or conservatories.
