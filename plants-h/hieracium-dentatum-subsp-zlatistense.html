Hieracium dentatum subsp. zlatistense

Hieracium dentatum subsp.

zlatistense, commonly known as the Zlatistense Hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
