Hierochloe odorata subsp. dahurica

Hierochloe odorata subsp.

dahurica, commonly known as sweetgrass, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of Asia and North America. It is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and emits a mild, sweet aroma when crushed.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks. Its slender, tufted growth habit and delicate appearance make it a popular ornamental plant.

It plays a role in traditional cultural practices and is also valued for its use in herbal remedies.
