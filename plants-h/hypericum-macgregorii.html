Hypericum macgregorii

Hypericum macgregorii, commonly known as the MacGregor's St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches a height of about 15 to 30 centimeters. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are oval-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and the stems are often reddish in color.

Hypericum macgregorii is adapted to moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.
