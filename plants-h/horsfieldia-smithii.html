Horsfieldia smithii

Horsfieldia smithii, commonly known as the Bornean orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, showy flowers that often display vibrant colors. The plant typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments, using its roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. Its flowers are usually small to medium in size and may have a sweet fragrance.

Horsfieldia smithii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
