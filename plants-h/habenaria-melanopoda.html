Habenaria melanopoda

Habenaria melanopoda, commonly known as the black-veined orchid, is a species of orchid native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, dark green veins. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically pale green or yellow with darker markings. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices.

Its botanical features make it a unique and relatively rare orchid species within its native range.
