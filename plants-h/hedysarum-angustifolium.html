Hedysarum angustifolium

Hedysarum angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved sweetclover, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish-white flowers in clusters. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Hedysarum angustifolium is often used in forage and as a cover crop due to its hardy nature and nutritional value.
