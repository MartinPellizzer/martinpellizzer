Hemicarpha subsquarrosa var. drummondii

Hemicarpha subsquarrosa var. drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's silverleaf, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in desert or semi-arid environments.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
