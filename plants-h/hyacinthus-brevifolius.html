Hyacinthus brevifolius

Hyacinthus brevifolius, commonly known as the short-leaved hyacinth, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features grass-like, narrow leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, violet, or white. The plant is known for its early spring blooming habit, often appearing before many other spring flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance, it is widely cultivated in gardens across temperate climates.
