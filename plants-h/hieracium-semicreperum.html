Hieracium semicreperum

Hieracium semicreperum, commonly known as the creeping hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed sites. It has a creeping growth habit with rhizomes and produces yellow flowers that bloom from June to August.

The leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
