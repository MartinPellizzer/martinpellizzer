Hieracium inuloides subsp. vetteri

Hieracium inuloides subsp.

vetteri, commonly known as the Vetter's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring temperate climates. It has finely divided leaves and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
