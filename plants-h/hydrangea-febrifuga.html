Hydrangea febrifuga

Hydrangea febrifuga, commonly known as the feverfew hydrangea, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with broad, ovate leaves that are often dark green in color. It prefers partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
