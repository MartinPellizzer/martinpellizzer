Hymenetron linguiflora

Hymenetron linguiflora, commonly known as the broad-leafed hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically have five petals and a prominent central stigma. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Hymenetron linguiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
