Hieracium crocatum subsp. phrissoides

Hieracium crocatum subsp.

phrissoides, commonly known as the Phrissoid hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow flowering heads.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, with flowers that develop into small, dry fruits called achene.

This subspecies is often found in calcareous habitats and is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological and taxonomic significance.
