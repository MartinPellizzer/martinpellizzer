Hieracium murorum subsp. prolixiceps

Hieracium murorum subsp.

prolixiceps, commonly known as the long-headed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clustered flowering heads.

The plant features basal and stem leaves that are lanceolate to ovate in shape, with rough, hairy surfaces. Its yellow flowers are typically arranged in dense, flattened clusters, and it produces small, elongated achenes as its fruit.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.
