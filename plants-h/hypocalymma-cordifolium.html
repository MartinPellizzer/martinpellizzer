Hypocalymma cordifolium

Hypocalymma cordifolium, commonly known as the heart-leaved hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves, which are usually narrow and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Hypocalymma cordifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
