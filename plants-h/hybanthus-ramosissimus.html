Hybanthus ramosissimus

Hybanthus ramosissimus, commonly known as the hairy hybanthus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its branched stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has hairy foliage, which helps it retain moisture in its native habitat. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in shaded areas within forests.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive species within the Hybanthus genus.
