Hieracium gletschense

Hieracium gletschense, commonly known as the Gletscherhawk's tongue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky and nutrient-poor soils, thriving in high-altitude environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a distinctive, elongated shape that resembles a hawk's tongue.

Hieracium gletschense is adapted to cold climates and is often found in mountainous areas across the Alps and Scandinavia.
