Hieracium dentatum subsp. euryphylloides

Hieracium dentatum subsp.

euryphylloides, commonly known as the broad-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have serrated edges, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.
