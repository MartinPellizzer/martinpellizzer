Hugelia filifolia var. sparsiflora

Hugelia filifolia var. sparsiflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered hugelia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in whorls along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers clustered in terminal spikes, which bloom during the warmer months. Its woody structure and adaptability make it suitable for ornamental landscaping in humid climates.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun exposure for optimal growth.
