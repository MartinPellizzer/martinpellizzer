Hesperis arvensis

Hesperis arvensis, commonly known as the field gentian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its flowering period occurs from June to August, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Hesperis arvensis is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
