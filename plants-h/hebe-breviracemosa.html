Hebe breviracemosa

Hebe breviracemosa, commonly known as the short-stemmed hebe, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, tubular flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, often with a silvery or greyish underside. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
