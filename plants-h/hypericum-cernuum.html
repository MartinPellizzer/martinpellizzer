Hypericum cernuum

Hypericum cernuum, commonly known as creeping St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to North America.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, typically in clusters along its stems. The plant has opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its botanical name reflects its association with the traditional herbal medicine of the same name.
