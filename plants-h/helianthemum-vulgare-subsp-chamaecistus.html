Helianthemum vulgare subsp. chamaecistus

Helianthemum vulgare subsp.

chamaecistus, commonly known as the rock rose, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically blooming in spring and summer.

The plant has succulent, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, helping it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
