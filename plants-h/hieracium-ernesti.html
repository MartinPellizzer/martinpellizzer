Hieracium ernesti

Hieracium ernesti, commonly known as the Erneste's hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Hieracium ernesti is an herbaceous perennial that produces achenes as its seed form, contributing to its spread in suitable habitats.
