Hieracium hozense

Hieracium hozense, commonly known as the Hozens alpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, high-altitude environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium hozense is known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
