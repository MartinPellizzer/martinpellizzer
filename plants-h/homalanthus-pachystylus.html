Homalanthus pachystylus

Homalanthus pachystylus, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its trunk is often covered in a rough, bark-like texture. It thrives in humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also known for its rapid growth and ability to tolerate poor soil conditions.
