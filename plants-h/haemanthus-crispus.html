Haemanthus crispus

Haemanthus crispus, commonly known as the Blood Lily, is a striking plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bright red, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, often surrounded by narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant has a short, stocky stem and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Haemanthus crispus is also known for its ability to thrive in coastal areas and is sometimes used in coastal landscaping.
