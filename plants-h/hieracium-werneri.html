Hieracium werneri

Hieracium werneri, commonly known as the Werneri's hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers and hairy, basal leaves. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, elongated seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread across suitable habitats.

Hieracium werneri is often found in temperate regions and is considered a short-lived perennial.
