Hypericum balfourii

Hypericum balfourii, commonly known as the Balfour's St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, orange-red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

Hypericum balfourii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
