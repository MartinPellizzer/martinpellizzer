Hemerocallis lilioasphodelus var. thunbergii

Hemerocallis lilioasphodelus var. thunbergii, commonly known as the yellow daylily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features strap-like, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has sword-shaped, grass-like leaves that are typically green with a slight reddish tinge. It is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
