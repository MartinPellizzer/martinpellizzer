Helichrysum tinctum

Helichrysum tinctum, commonly known as the golden marguerite or everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that retain their color even after drying, making it popular in floral arrangements. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic, feathery foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its resilience and ornamental value make it a favored choice for gardens and dried flower crafts.
