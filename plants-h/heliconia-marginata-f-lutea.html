Heliconia marginata f. lutea

Heliconia marginata f. lutea, commonly known as the yellow-edge heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is characterized by its large, brightly colored inflorescences. The plant features elongated, banana-like leaves that are often tinged with yellow or orange at the edges, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically orange or yellow, clustered in a spadix surrounded by a brightly colored bract.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower due to its vibrant appearance.
