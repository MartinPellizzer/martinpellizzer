Helleborus purpurascens f. subdivisus

Helleborus purpurascens f. subdivisus, commonly known as the divided hellebore, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy foliage and striking, deep pink to purple flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant is noted for its unique leaf structure, which is divided into narrow, serrated segments. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

This variety is relatively low maintenance and can tolerate poor soil conditions, making it a versatile choice for woodland gardens.
