Hypolaena crispata

Hypolaena crispata, commonly known as the curly hypolaena, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often curled or crinkled. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as woodlands and heathlands.

Hypolaena crispata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
