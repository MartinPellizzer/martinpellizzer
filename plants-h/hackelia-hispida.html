Hackelia hispida

Hackelia hispida, commonly known as stickyweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hackelia hispida is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.
