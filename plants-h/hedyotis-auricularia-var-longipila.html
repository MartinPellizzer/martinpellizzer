Hedyotis auricularia var. longipila

Hedyotis auricularia var. longipila, commonly known as the long-leaf spurred gentian, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. The plant has slender, trailing stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
