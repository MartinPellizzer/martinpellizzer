Hieracium arevacorum

Hieracium arevacorum, commonly known as the Arevacorum hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. It has a rosette of basal leaves that are usually hairy and serrated.

The flowering stems bear yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.
