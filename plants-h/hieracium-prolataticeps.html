Hieracium prolataticeps

Hieracium prolataticeps, commonly known as the creeping hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit with numerous stems that spread along the ground. The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Hieracium prolataticeps is often found in disturbed soils and open areas, making it a common weed in various habitats.
