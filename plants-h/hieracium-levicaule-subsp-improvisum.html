Hieracium levicaule subsp. improvisum

Hieracium levicaule subsp.

improvisum, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while the flowers are yellow with a central disc.

It is a perennial species that spreads through rhizomes and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
