Helenium microcephalum

Helenium microcephalum, commonly known as the dwarf sunflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear bright orange to red ray florets surrounded by yellow disk florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has finely dissected, fern-like leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics include showy composite flowers and a fibrous root system.
