Humiriastrum cuspidatum var. subhirtellum

Humiriastrum cuspidatum var. subhirtellum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright, branched growth habit. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical heads. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant adapted to arid environments.
