Hexaglottis lewisiae subsp. secunda

Hexaglottis lewisiae subsp.

secunda, commonly known as the coastal lupine, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers.

The plant has compound leaves with multiple small leaflets, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils along coastal areas and is adapted to salt spray and strong winds.

This subspecies is often found in maritime habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing dune environments.
