Homopappus inuloides

Homopappus inuloides, commonly known as the desert groundsel, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual with yellow flowers and is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils. The plant has a basal rosette of leaves and produces small, hairy seeds that are dispersed by the wind. Its botanical features include a taproot system and a lack of true stems, which help it conserve water in harsh environments.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a characteristic species of desert scrub habitats.
