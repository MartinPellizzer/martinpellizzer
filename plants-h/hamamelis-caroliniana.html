Hamamelis caroliniana

Hamamelis caroliniana, commonly known as the Carolina witch hazel, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late autumn or early winter. The plant has narrow, dark green leaves that turn reddish in the fall. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, and it produces small, red berries that persist through the winter.

Hamamelis caroliniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
