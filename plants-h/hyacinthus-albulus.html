Hyacinthus albulus

Hyacinthus albulus, commonly known as the white hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces clusters of fragrant, white flowers in early spring, which are often used in floral arrangements. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is closely related to other hyacinth species. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Hyacinthus albulus is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticulture.
