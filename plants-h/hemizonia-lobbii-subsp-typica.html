Hemizonia lobbii subsp. typica

Hemizonia lobbii subsp.

typica, commonly known as the Cape mountain heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit.

The plant features small, leathery leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale pink flowers. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.
