Helichrysum obovatum var. longifolium

Helichrysum obovatum var. longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved everlasting, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, elongated leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs, giving them a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish to golden flowers arranged in dense, compact clusters. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its name derives from the Greek words "helios" meaning sun and "chrysos" meaning gold, referencing its sunny habitat and golden blooms.
