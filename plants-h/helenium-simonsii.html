Helenium simonsii

Helenium simonsii, commonly known as the western coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features showy, daisy-like yellow to orange flower heads with a prominent central cone, surrounded by narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom from late summer into early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Helenium simonsii is well adapted to dry, well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.
