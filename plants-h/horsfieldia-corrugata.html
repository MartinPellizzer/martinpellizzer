Horsfieldia corrugata

Horsfieldia corrugata, commonly known as the wrinkled orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments within lowland forests. The plant features a slender, leafy stem with a distinctive corrugated or wrinkled appearance on its surface. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and usually pale yellow or white, arranged in a raceme.

Horsfieldia corrugata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
