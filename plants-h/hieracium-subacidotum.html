Hieracium subacidotum

Hieracium subacidotum, commonly known as the acidic meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its yellowish-orange composite flowers and hairy stems, often found in moist, calcareous soils. The plant typically grows in meadows and along stream banks, preferring temperate climates. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Hieracium subacidotum is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor environments, making it a common sight in various parts of Europe.
