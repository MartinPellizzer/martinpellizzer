Hedyotis pinifolia

Hedyotis pinifolia, commonly known as the pineleaf stellaria, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale pink flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Hedyotis pinifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
