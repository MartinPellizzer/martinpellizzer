Hugelia virgata var. pygmaea

Hugelia virgata var. pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf umbrella plant, is a compact evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features dense, dark green foliage that forms a rounded shape, with small, leathery leaves arranged in a whorled pattern. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in umbrella-like clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
