Hoodia pilifera subsp. pilifera

Hoodia pilifera subsp.

pilifera, commonly known as the "cork plant," is a succulent shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and spiny leaves, which help it conserve water in its harsh environment.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to anchor itself in sandy or rocky soils. Hoodia pilifera subsp.

pilifera is adapted to extreme drought conditions, making it a resilient species in its natural habitat.
