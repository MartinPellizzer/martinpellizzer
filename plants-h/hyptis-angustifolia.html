Hyptis angustifolia

Hyptis angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf hyptis, is a perennial herb native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs.

Hyptis angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
