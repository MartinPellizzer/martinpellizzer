Herpestis polyadena

Herpestis polyadena, commonly known as the Indian devil's backbone, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.
