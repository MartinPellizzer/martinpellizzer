Hondbesseion calycinum

Hondbesseion calycinum, commonly known as the golden bellvista, is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, resembling small bells. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a smooth, waxy texture that helps it retain moisture in its arid habitat. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.
