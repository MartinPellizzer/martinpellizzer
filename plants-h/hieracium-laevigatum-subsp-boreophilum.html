Hieracium laevigatum subsp. boreophilum

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

boreophilum, commonly known as the northern hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern regions of Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and rocky slopes.

The plant has yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, with ray florets that are usually shorter than the central disk florets. Its leaves are basal, lanceolate, and often covered with fine hairs.

It is characterized by its robust growth and adaptability to cooler climates.
