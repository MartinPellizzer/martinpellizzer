Hippochaete praealta var. intermedia

Hippochaete praealta var. intermedia, commonly known as the tall silver bush, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
