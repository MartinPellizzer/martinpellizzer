Hydrocotyle wilsonii

Hydrocotyle wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's spurge-leaf, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It features a rosette of heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive addition to ornamental gardens and ecological restoration projects.
