Hieracium murorum subsp. savonicum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

savonicum, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow flowering heads.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in full sun. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rough, hairy texture, and the flowers are arranged in a composite head.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh mountain environments.
