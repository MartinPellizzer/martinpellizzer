Hedera helix var. floribunda

Hedera helix var. floribunda, commonly known as the golden ivy, is a climbing evergreen vine native to Europe.

It features dark green, leathery leaves that often have a golden or yellowish variegation. This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in landscaping and as a houseplant. It thrives in partial shade and requires well-draining soil.

The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in late spring, followed by dark blue berries that are attractive to birds.
