Hieronyma macrocarpa var. moritziana

Hieronyma macrocarpa var. moritziana, commonly known as the Moritz's Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It features elliptical to ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green, with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by orange-red, fleshy berries that are edible for some animals. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in coastal and lowland regions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the Malvaceae family.
