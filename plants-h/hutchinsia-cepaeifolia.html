Hutchinsia cepaeifolia

Hutchinsia cepaeifolia, commonly known as the Cape ivy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale blue flowers. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Hutchinsia cepaeifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
