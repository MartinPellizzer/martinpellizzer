Helianthemum raynaudii

Helianthemum raynaudii, commonly known as the Raynaud's rockrose, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has woody stems and produces small, leathery leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white hair. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to sunny, arid environments.

Helianthemum raynaudii is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
