Hieracium leptophyton subsp. busocense

Hieracium leptophyton subsp.

busocense, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, pastures, and woodland edges.

The plant has hairy, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, characteristic of the dandelion family.

It is noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
