Herminium clavigerum

Herminium clavigerum, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption from the soil.

Herminium clavigerum is part of the Orchidaceae family and exhibits typical orchid morphology with specialized pollination mechanisms.
