Hydrophyllum macrophyllum var. occidentale

Hydrophyllum macrophyllum var. occidentale, commonly known as western sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. It has a taproot system and produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Its botanical features make it an important species for pollinators and a valuable component of native plant communities.
