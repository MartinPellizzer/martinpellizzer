Hieracium polyadenum

Hieracium polyadenum, commonly known as the many-seeded hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces numerous small, yellowish to reddish flowers arranged in a single terminal head. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It has hairy stems and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

The species is also notable for its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
