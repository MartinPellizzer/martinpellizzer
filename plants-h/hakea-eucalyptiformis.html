Hakea eucalyptiformis

Hakea eucalyptiformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hakea, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces distinctive cylindrical or spiky flower spikes, often in shades of pink or red, which bloom in spring. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resistance to drought and poor soil conditions.

Hakea eucalyptiformis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
