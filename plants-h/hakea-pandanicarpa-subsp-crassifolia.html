Hakea pandanicarpa subsp. crassifolia

Hakea pandanicarpa subsp.

crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are typically broad and lance-shaped.

The plant produces distinctive cylindrical inflorescences that bear small, yellowish flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in woody, boat-shaped follicles, a feature typical of the Hakea genus.

This subspecies is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
