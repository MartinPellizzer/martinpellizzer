Hieracium atripileolum

Hieracium atripileolum, commonly known as the black hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its dark green, hairy leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its flowering period typically occurs from late spring to early autumn.

The plant is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
