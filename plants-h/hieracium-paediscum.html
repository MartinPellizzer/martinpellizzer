Hieracium paediscum

Hieracium paediscum, commonly known as the lesser hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are alternate, rough to the touch, and have a distinctive serrated edge.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
