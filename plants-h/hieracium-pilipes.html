Hieracium pilipes

Hieracium pilipes, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster, typical of the daisy family. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grasslands.

Hieracium pilipes is an herbaceous perennial that can spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in some areas.
