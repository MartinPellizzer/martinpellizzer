Hierochloe japonica

Hierochloe japonica, commonly known as Japanese hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wet meadows. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-bearing flowers.

Hierochloe japonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in humid, cool climates.
