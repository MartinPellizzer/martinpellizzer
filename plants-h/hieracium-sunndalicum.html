Hieracium sunndalicum

Hieracium sunndalicum, commonly known as the Swedish hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to northern Europe.

It belongs to the daisy family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
