Hyptis velutina var. deminuata

Hyptis velutina var. deminuata, commonly known as velvetleaf hyptis, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, depending on the variety. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
