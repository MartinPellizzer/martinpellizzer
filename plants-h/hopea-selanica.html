Hopea selanica

Hopea selanica, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a broad, spreading crown. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a glossy green surface.

Hopea selanica plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
