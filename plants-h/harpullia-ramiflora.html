Harpullia ramiflora

Harpullia ramiflora, commonly known as the flame tree or flame of the forest, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange-red flowers that appear in clusters, often resembling flames. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, serrated leaflets and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. Harpullia ramiflora thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
