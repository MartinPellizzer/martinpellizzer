Hieracium diaphanoides subsp. phaedrophyllum

Hieracium diaphanoides subsp.

phaedrophyllum, commonly known as the pale hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This subspecies is native to parts of Europe and is often found in grassy or disturbed habitats.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological and morphological features.
