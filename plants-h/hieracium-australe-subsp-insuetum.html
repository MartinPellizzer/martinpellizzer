Hieracium australe subsp. insuetum

Hieracium australe subsp.

insuetum, commonly known as the Australian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Australia. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and heathlands.

The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are hairy and often have a bluish-green hue. Its flowering stems bear yellow daisy-like flowers with ray florets and a central disk of tubular flowers.

It is characterized by its robust growth and ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
