Hibiscus ahlensis

Hibiscus ahlensis, commonly known as the African hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant features large, showy flowers with five petals, typically in shades of red, pink, or white. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it produces capsules containing numerous seeds.

Hibiscus ahlensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
