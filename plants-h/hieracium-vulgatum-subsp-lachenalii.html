Hieracium vulgatum subsp. lachenalii

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

lachenalii, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats such as roadsides and meadows. It has a creeping rootstock that allows it to spread vegetatively.

Its leaves are usually hairy and have a distinctive lobed or toothed margin.
