Hippoglossum

Hippoglossum, commonly known as the sea hare, is a marine gastropod mollusk belonging to the family Aplysiidae.

It is characterized by its soft, often colorful body and the presence of a distinctive, elongated mantle that can resemble a leaf or a ribbon. The plant-like appearance of the sea hare is due to its ability to secrete a mucus that can form a gelatinous layer, which it uses for protection and camouflage. While not a true plant, it plays an important role in marine ecosystems by grazing on algae and contributing to nutrient cycling.

Its unique botanical-like features have made it a subject of interest in both ecological and scientific studies.
