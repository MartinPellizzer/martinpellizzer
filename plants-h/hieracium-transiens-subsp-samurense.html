Hieracium transiens subsp. samurense

Hieracium transiens subsp.

samurense, commonly known as the Samur hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in composite heads, which are typically arranged in a single terminal inflorescence. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species in its native habitat.
