Hemiboea albiflora

Hemiboea albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered hemiboea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal or inland regions. The plant features small, showy white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually narrow and succulent, adapted to conserve water in arid environments.

Hemiboea albiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
