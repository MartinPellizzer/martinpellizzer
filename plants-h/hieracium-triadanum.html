Hieracium triadanum

Hieracium triadanum, commonly known as the three-toothed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped with three distinct teeth, giving it its common name.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, producing small seeds that are dispersed by the wind.
