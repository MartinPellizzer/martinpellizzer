Hylotelephium cauticola

Hylotelephium cauticola, commonly known as the purple stonecrop, is a succulent perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It features fleshy, thick leaves that are typically blue-green to purple in color, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or purple during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant foliage and long-lasting blooms.
