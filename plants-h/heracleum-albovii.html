Heracleum albovii

Heracleum albovii, commonly known as white-flowered cow parsnip, is a member of the Apiaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves and showy white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in wetlands and along stream banks. It has a stout, hollow stem that is often covered with fine hairs.

The fruit is a small, dry schizocarp that splits into two mericarps when mature.
