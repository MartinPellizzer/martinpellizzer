Hibiscus subreniformis

Hibiscus subreniformis, commonly known as the broad-leaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its flowers bloom in summer and are often visited by bees and other pollinators.

Hibiscus subreniformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
