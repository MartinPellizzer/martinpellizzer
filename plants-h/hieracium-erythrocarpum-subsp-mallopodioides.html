Hieracium erythrocarpum subsp. mallopodioides

Hieracium erythrocarpum subsp.

mallopodioides, commonly known as the red-fruited hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and yellow disk flowers.

The plant produces distinctive reddish-brown, spherical fruits that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it identifiable within the Hieracium genus, though it is often confused with other similar species.
