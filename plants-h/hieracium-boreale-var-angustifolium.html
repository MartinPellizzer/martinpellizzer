Hieracium boreale var. angustifolium

Hieracium boreale var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to northern regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and gravelly soils. Its leaves are narrow and often have a rough texture, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is known for its ability to thrive in cooler climates and is often found in alpine or subarctic environments.
