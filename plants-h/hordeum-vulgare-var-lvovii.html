Hordeum vulgare var. lvovii

Hordeum vulgare var. lvovii, commonly known as the Lvov barley, is a botanical variant of barley characterized by its unique morphological features.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is native to the temperate regions of Eastern Europe. This variety typically exhibits a compact growth habit with shorter, more robust stems compared to common barley. Its leaves are usually narrower and have a distinct glume structure, which is a key identifying feature.

The plant produces small, two-rowed spikelets that are adapted for efficient seed dispersal in its native habitat.
