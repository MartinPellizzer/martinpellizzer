Hieracium magistri

Hieracium magistri, commonly known as the lesser hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It has hairy stems and leaves that are often lobed or toothed.

Hieracium magistri is a perennial species that can spread through rhizomes, making it somewhat invasive in certain environments.
