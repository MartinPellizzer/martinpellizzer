Hoya meliflua

Hoya meliflua, commonly known as the honey-scented wax plant, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that emit a subtle, sweet fragrance. The plant belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions. Its flowers typically bloom in shades of pink and white, and it is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.

Hoya meliflua is also noted for its slow growth and ease of care, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
