Halimium chihuahuense

Halimium chihuahuense, commonly known as the Chihuahua holly or desert holly, is a flowering plant native to the Chihuahua region of Mexico.

It is a low-growing shrub that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh desert conditions. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically small, fleshy, and covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Halimium chihuahuense is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in desert ecosystems.
