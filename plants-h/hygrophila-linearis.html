Hygrophila linearis

Hygrophila linearis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hygrophila, is a semi-aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, linear leaves that are often used in aquariums due to their aesthetic appeal and ease of maintenance. The plant thrives in shallow waters and can tolerate a range of water conditions. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or yellow.

Hygrophila linearis is valued for its ability to provide oxygenation and habitat for aquatic life.
