Hieracium bifidum subsp. melaxum

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

melaxum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are narrow and often have a distinctive lobed or notched appearance, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
