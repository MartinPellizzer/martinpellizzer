Hieracium adriaticum subsp. fallaciniforme

Hieracium adriaticum subsp.

fallaciniforme, commonly known as the Adriatic hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and flowering stems that bear yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has a creeping rootstock and can form dense colonies, making it an invasive species in some regions.

The leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while the flowers are arranged in a single head.
