Helianthus dalyi

Helianthus dalyi, commonly known as Daly's sunflower, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features bright yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of smaller, tubular flowers, which are adapted for pollination by bees and other insects. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, aiding in water conservation.

Daly's sunflower is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
