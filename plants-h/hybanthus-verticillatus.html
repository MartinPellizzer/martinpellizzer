Hybanthus verticillatus

Hybanthus verticillatus, commonly known as the clustered hybanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers clustered in groups of three to five. Its flowers are often white or pale yellow and are pollinated by insects.

Hybanthus verticillatus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
