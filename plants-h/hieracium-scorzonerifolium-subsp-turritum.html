Hieracium scorzonerifolium subsp. turritum

Hieracium scorzonerifolium subsp.

turritum, commonly known as the Turritous hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow flowering heads.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring sunny to partially shaded conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while the flowers consist of both ray and disk florets.

This subspecies is distinguished by its slightly curved flower heads, giving it a unique appearance among other hawkweed species.
