Hermione

Hermione, commonly known as the common tansy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and fern-like leaves. The plant is often found growing in moist meadows and along stream banks. It has a long history of use in traditional medicine, though it is considered toxic in large quantities.

Its botanical name is *Tanacetum corymbosum*.
