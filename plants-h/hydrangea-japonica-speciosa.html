Hydrangea japonica speciosa

Hydrangea japonica speciosa, commonly known as the Japanese hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from blue to pink depending on soil pH. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a glossy green surface that turns red in the fall.

Hydrangea japonica speciosa thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
