Hesperis meyeriana

Hesperis meyeriana, commonly known as the meyer's candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and produces slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are alternate, linear to lanceolate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Hesperis meyeriana blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies with its nectar-rich flowers.
