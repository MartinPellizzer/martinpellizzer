Hedysarum fruticulosum

Hedysarum fruticulosum, commonly known as the creeping yellow vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant has trifoliate leaves and is often found in dry, sandy soils. It is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment.

Hedysarum fruticulosum is also sometimes used in ornamental gardening due to its delicate appearance.
