Hieracium hryniewieckii

Hieracium hryniewieckii, commonly known as the Hryniewiecki's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in a compact inflorescence. Native to Central Europe, this species thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. The plant is notable for its ability to spread through rhizomes, forming dense colonies.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native habitat.
