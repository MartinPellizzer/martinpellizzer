Heteropteris formosa

Heteropteris formosa, commonly known as the golden fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Polypodiaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, frond-like structures. The plant features a creeping rhizome with clustered, fan-shaped leaves that are often golden or yellowish in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
