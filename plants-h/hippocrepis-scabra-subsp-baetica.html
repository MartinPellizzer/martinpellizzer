Hippocrepis scabra subsp. baetica

Hippocrepis scabra subsp.

baetica, commonly known as the rough hawksbeard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine, stiff hairs, contributing to its rough texture.

It is part of the family Brassicaceae, which includes many other flowering plants with similar floral structures.
