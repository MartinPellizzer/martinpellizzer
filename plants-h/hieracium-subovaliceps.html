Hieracium subovaliceps

Hieracium subovaliceps, commonly known as the suboval-headed hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white ray florets. The plant typically has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in wetlands.

Hieracium subovaliceps is a perennial species that spreads through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
