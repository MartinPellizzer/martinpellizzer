Hypodiscus alternans

Hypodiscus alternans, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its velvety, heart-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen perennial, often cultivated for its ornamental value. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular houseplant.

Its blooming period extends throughout the year under optimal conditions.
