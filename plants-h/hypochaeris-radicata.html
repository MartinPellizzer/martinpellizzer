Hypochaeris radicata

Hypochaeris radicata, commonly known as common mouse-ear chickweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a low-growing herb with basal rosettes of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming from spring to early summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and gardens.

Hypochaeris radicata is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in many regions.
