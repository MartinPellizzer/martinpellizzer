Hieracium indutiforme

Hieracium indutiforme, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant is typically found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are rough and hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium indutiforme is often considered a weed due to its rapid growth and ability to spread easily.
