Hymenophyllum trichophorum

Hymenophyllum trichophorum, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a small, epiphytic fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, leathery fronds covered in fine, hair-like structures. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or rocks. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and release spores for propagation.

The intricate morphology of Hymenophyllum trichophorum makes it a unique subject of study in botanical research.
