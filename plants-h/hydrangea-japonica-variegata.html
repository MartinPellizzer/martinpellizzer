Hydrangea japonica variegata

Hydrangea japonica variegata, commonly known as the variegated Japanese hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from white to pink, blue, or purple depending on soil pH. The plant features broad, ovate leaves with striking variegation of white, green, and yellow. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its ability to change flower color based on environmental conditions adds to its visual appeal and botanical interest.
