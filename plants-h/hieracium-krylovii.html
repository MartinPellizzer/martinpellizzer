Hieracium krylovii

Hieracium krylovii, commonly known as Krylov's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky slopes. It features a rosette of basal leaves and produces yellow flower heads that bloom in summer.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
