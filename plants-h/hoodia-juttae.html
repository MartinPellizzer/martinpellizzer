Hoodia juttae

Hoodia juttae, commonly known as the desert cucumber, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant is adapted to extreme drought conditions, storing water in its thick stems and leaves. Hoodia juttae is also notable for its unique chemical composition, which includes compounds like hoodia glycosides.

These compounds have been studied for their potential appetite-suppressing properties.
