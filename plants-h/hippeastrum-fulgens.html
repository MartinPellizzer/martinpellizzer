Hippeastrum fulgens

Hippeastrum fulgens, commonly known as the red hurricane, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, typically displaying vibrant red or orange hues with a white throat. The plant features strap-like, dark green leaves that are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native arid environments. It produces underground bulb-like structures called corms, which are essential for its growth and propagation.

Hippeastrum fulgens is a popular ornamental plant due to its dramatic blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
