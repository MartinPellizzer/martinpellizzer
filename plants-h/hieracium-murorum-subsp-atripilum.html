Hieracium murorum subsp. atripilum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

atripilum, commonly known as the black-veined hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow composite flowers.

The leaves are typically basal, with a rough texture and a distinctive dark green color. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

The plant is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is often found in disturbed soils.
