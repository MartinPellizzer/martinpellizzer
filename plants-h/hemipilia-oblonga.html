Hemipilia oblonga

Hemipilia oblonga, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hemipilia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually pale green to white, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination by insects.

Hemipilia oblonga is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in specialty gardens.
