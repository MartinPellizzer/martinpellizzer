Hypericum cordiforme var. genuinum

Hypericum cordiforme var. genuinum, commonly known as the heart-shaped St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the genus Hypericum, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their medicinal properties. It thrives in woodland habitats and prefers partial to full sun with well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
