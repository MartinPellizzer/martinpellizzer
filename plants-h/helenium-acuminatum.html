Helenium acuminatum

Helenium acuminatum, commonly known as the spearhead rayflower, is a perennial plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features striking, bright orange to red ray flowers that surround a central disk, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Helenium acuminatum thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for garden landscapes due to its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
