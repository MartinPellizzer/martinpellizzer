Hieracium rapunculoides subsp. tridentatiflorum

Hieracium rapunculoides subsp.

tridentatiflorum, commonly known as the three-toothed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks.

The plant features dark green, basal leaves that are often lobed or toothed, and its flowers are yellow with five petals. It produces achenes (small, dry fruits) that are dispersed by wind.

This species is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
