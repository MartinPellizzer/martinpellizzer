Hibiscus sagittatus

Hibiscus sagittatus, commonly known as the arrowleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for warm climates. Hibiscus sagittatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be found in gardens and as a houseplant.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.
