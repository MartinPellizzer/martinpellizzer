Hieracium cinerascens subsp. vratnikense

Hieracium cinerascens subsp.

vratnikense, commonly known as the Vratnik hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to southeastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and basal rosettes of leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in composite inflorescences, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in dry, grassy habitats and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
