Hancea beccariana

Hancea beccariana, commonly known as the Beccarian's hancea, is a rare and distinctive plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its thick, woody stems and compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by fleshy, berry-like fruits. Hancea beccariana is notable for its unique morphology and is often found in the understory of dense, humid forests.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is of particular interest to botanists and conservationists.
