Hibiscus trionum var. ternatus

Hibiscus trionum var. ternatus, commonly known as the yellow wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy yellow or orange flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Hibiscus trionum var. ternatus is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
