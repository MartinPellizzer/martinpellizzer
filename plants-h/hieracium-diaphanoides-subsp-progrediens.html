Hieracium diaphanoides subsp. progrediens

Hieracium diaphanoides subsp.

progrediens, commonly known as the translucent hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pale yellow to white flowers arranged in a composite head.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This subspecies is native to parts of Europe and is often found in meadows and grassy areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Hieracium genus.
