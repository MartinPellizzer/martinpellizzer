Heteromera philaenorum

Heteromera philaenorum, commonly known as the Philippine spider orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its unique, spider-like appearance, with long, slender petals and a distinctive, elongated spur. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests. Its flowers are pollinated by specific insects, showcasing a specialized ecological relationship.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
