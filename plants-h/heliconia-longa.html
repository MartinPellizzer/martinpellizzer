Heliconia longa

Heliconia longa, commonly known as the red heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, brightly colored bracts that resemble flowers. The plant produces long, pendulous inflorescences with numerous small, clustered flowers. Its leaves are large, elongated, and often have a waxy texture.

Heliconia longa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant appearance and ability to thrive in humid, warm climates.
