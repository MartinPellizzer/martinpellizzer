Hypericum marahuacanum

Hypericum marahuacanum, commonly known as the Mexican St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the genus Hypericum, which includes over 400 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its yellow flowers and woody stems, typically growing in dry, sunny habitats. It has small, opposite leaves and produces bright yellow to orange flowers during the summer months.

Hypericum marahuacanum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
