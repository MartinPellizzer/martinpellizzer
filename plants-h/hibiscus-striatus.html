Hibiscus striatus

Hibiscus striatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hibiscus, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its distinctive, narrow leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Hibiscus striatus thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its flowers bloom throughout the growing season, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
