Heliconia augusta

Heliconia augusta, commonly known as the red heliconia, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescences that consist of long, curved bracts resembling a bird's beak. The plant belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower. Its leaves are large, lance-shaped, and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

Heliconia augusta thrives in humid, shaded environments and is adapted to grow in well-drained, nutrient-rich soils.
