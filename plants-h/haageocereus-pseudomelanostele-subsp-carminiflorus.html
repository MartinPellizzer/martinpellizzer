Haageocereus pseudomelanostele subsp. carminiflorus

Haageocereus pseudomelanostele subsp.

carminiflorus, commonly known as the Crimson Flowered Cactus, is a rare and striking member of the Cactaceae family. It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white to pale blue spines, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

The plant produces vibrant pink to red flowers during the summer months, adding a dramatic visual contrast to its otherwise muted foliage. It is native to a very limited area in southern Africa, specifically in Namibia and Angola.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a threatened species in its native range.
