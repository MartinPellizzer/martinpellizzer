Hallia bupleurifolia

Hallia bupleurifolia, commonly known as the leafy bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, pale blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils. It blooms in the summer months and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Hallia bupleurifolia is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
