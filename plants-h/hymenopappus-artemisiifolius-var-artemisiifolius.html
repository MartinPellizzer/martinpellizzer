Hymenopappus artemisiifolius var. artemisiifolius

Hymenopappus artemisiifolius var. artemisiifolius, commonly known as the Artemisia-leaved Hymenopappus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and feathery pappus structures. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lanceolate and aromatic, with a distinctive silvery-green appearance.

The species is known for its showy yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall.
