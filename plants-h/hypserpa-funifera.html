Hypserpa funifera

Hypserpa funifera, commonly known as the sea hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red. The plant thrives in coastal areas and is often found in sandy or saline soils. It has a woody stem that can grow up to several meters tall, and its flowers bloom throughout the year in warm climates.

Hypserpa funifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand salt spray and wind.
