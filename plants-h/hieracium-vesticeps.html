Hieracium vesticeps

Hieracium vesticeps, commonly known as the purple meadowhawk, is a flowering plant belonging to the sunflower family (Asteraceae).

It is native to Europe and is characterized by its hairy, erect stems and basal rosette of leaves. The plant produces yellowish to orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils.

Hieracium vesticeps is often found in open habitats and is relatively easy to grow in gardens with proper conditions.
