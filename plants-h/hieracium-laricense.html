Hieracium laricense

Hieracium laricense, commonly known as the Larice hill hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and often have a serrated edge, while its flowers are yellow and bloom in summer.

Hieracium laricense is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh, high-altitude conditions.
