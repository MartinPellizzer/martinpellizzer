Hieracium stenolepidoides

Hieracium stenolepidoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in some regions.
