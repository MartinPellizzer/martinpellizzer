Hydrocotyle araucana var. patagonica

Hydrocotyle araucana var. patagonica, commonly known as the Patagonian water shield, is a perennial aquatic plant native to South America.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as lakes and streams. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that float on the water surface, with submerged leaves that are narrower and more elongated. Its flowers are small and white, often appearing in clusters, and it produces small, round fruits.

This species is valued for its ecological role in maintaining water quality and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
