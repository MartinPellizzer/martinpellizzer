Hieracium limiticola

Hieracium limiticola, commonly known as the coastal hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along shorelines, tolerating salt spray and wind. It has basal leaves that are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing yellow petals that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
