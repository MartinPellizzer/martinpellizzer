Hyssaria lehmanniana

Hyssaria lehmanniana, commonly known as Lehmann's hyssop, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal properties. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are usually grayish-green in color. Its small, tubular flowers are typically purple or blue, arranged in whorled clusters.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history of use in traditional herbal medicine.
