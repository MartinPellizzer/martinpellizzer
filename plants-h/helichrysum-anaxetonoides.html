Helichrysum anaxetonoides

Helichrysum anaxetonoides, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, bright yellow to orange flowers that resemble daisies. The plant produces bract-like leaves that are often mistaken for petals, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

The flowers are typically short-lived, but the plant is known for its ability to retain its color and form even after drying.
