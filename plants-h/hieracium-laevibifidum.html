Hieracium laevibifidum

Hieracium laevibifidum, commonly known as the smooth-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features upright, hairy stems and basal leaves that are broadly ovate with smooth, wavy edges. Its flowers are typically yellow, with ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers.

Hieracium laevibifidum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
