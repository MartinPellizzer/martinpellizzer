Hypochaeris eurylepis

Hypochaeris eurylepis, commonly known as the broadleaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in open areas such as meadows and disturbed soils, and it produces yellow flowers with prominent central disks. Hypochaeris eurylepis is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in many regions.

Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
