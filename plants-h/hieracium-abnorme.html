Hieracium abnorme

Hieracium abnorme, commonly known as the giant hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are typically rough and covered in fine hairs. Its stems are upright and can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, supporting the dense inflorescence.

Hieracium abnorme is known for its aggressive growth and has been classified as an invasive species in some regions.
