Hieracium flagelliferum subsp. flagelliferum

Hieracium flagelliferum subsp.

flagelliferum, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright, leafy stems and clusters of small, yellowish to reddish flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

It is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain habitats.
