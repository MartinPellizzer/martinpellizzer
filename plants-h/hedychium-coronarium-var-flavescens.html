Hedychium coronarium var. flavescens

Hedychium coronarium var. flavescens, commonly known as the golden flame ginger, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a vibrant splash of color to gardens. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are glossy and arranged in a fan-like pattern. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
