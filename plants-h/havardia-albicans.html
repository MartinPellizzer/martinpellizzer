Havardia albicans

Havardia albicans, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of white, fluffy flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.

The tree is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
