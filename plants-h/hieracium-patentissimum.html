Hieracium patentissimum

Hieracium patentissimum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Hieracium patentissimum is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in agricultural areas.
