Hedysarum madagascariense

Hedysarum madagascariense, commonly known as the Madagascar sweet clover, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shrubland and forest edges, thriving in well-drained soils. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.

Its unique morphology and ecological significance make it an important species in its native habitat.
