Hieracium oxycerinthe

Hieracium oxycerinthe, commonly known as the oxycerinthe hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves and produces yellow composite flowers with ray florets. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant has hairy stems and is often found in moist to dry habitats.

It is considered a weed in some areas due to its aggressive growth habit.
