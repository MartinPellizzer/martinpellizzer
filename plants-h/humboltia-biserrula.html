Humboltia biserrula

Humboltia biserrula, commonly known as the coastal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of California and Baja California.

It is a herbaceous annual that grows up to 1-2 meters tall, featuring slender stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, blooming in the morning. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal dunes and scrub habitats.

Humboltia biserrula is adapted to withstand salt spray and is an important species in coastal ecosystems.
