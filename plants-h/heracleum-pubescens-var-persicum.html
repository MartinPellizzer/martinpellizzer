Heracleum pubescens var. persicum

Heracleum pubescens var. persicum, commonly known as Persian cow parsley, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It features a robust, branched stem that can grow up to 2 meters in height, covered with fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbrella-like inflorescences, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its leaves are deeply lobed, with a distinctive hairy texture, and the plant is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
