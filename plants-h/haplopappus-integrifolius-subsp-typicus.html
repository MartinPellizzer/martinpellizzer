Haplopappus integrifolius subsp. typicus

Haplopappus integrifolius subsp.

typicus, commonly known as the smoothleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typical herbaceous growth habit.

The plant features narrow, smooth, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It produces small, yellowish to brownish composite flowers that are typically visited by various pollinators.

This subspecies is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.
