Hieracium sabaudum subsp. quercetorum

Hieracium sabaudum subsp.

quercetorum, commonly known as the oak-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and basal rosettes of lanceolate leaves.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in dry, calcareous soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
