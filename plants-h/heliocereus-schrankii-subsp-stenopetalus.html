Heliocereus schrankii subsp. stenopetalus

Heliocereus schrankii subsp.

stenopetalus, commonly known as the narrow-petaled night blooming cactus, is a species native to Mexico. It is a type of cactus belonging to the Cactaceae family, characterized by its spiny stems and nocturnal flowering habit.

The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night and remain open for only a few hours. Its flowers are often visited by nocturnal pollinators such as moths and bats.

The plant is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
