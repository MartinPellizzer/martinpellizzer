Hemiandra pungens var. hispida

Hemiandra pungens var. hispida, commonly known as the rough hemiandra, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and distinctive floral structures. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that are often visited by insects. Its foliage is typically covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving it a rough texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
