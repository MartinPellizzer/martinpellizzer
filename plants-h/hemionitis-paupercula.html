Hemionitis paupercula

Hemionitis paupercula, commonly known as the poor man's fern, is a unique vascular plant belonging to the family Hemionitidaceae.

It is characterized by its small, delicate fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. This plant is native to arid regions of Central Asia and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its reproductive structures, including spore-bearing sporangia, are clustered in small, compact clusters on the underside of its leaves.

Hemionitis paupercula is often found growing in rocky or sandy soils, where it forms dense, low-growing mats.
