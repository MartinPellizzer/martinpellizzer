Hieracium jankae subsp. penzesii

Hieracium jankae subsp.

penzesii, commonly known as the Penzes' hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky soils. It has lance-shaped leaves with rough textures and produces yellow flowers in the summer months.
