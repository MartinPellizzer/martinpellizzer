Helichrysum polycladum

Helichrysum polycladum, commonly known as the golden marguerite or sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that produces small, yellow to orange flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Helichrysum polycladum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a cut flower.
