Hieracium innsbruckense

Hieracium innsbruckense, commonly known as the Innsbruck groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Alps, particularly in Austria and Italy.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy habitats and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central purple disc.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for certain insects.
