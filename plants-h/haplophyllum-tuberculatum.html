Haplophyllum tuberculatum

Haplophyllum tuberculatum, commonly known as the warty leaf hakea, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, warty, and often hairy leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
