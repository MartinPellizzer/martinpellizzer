Hirtella racemosa var. racemosa

Hirtella racemosa var. racemosa, commonly known as the clustered hirtella, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of white to pale yellow flowers, which are arranged in racemes and attract various pollinators. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitats.
