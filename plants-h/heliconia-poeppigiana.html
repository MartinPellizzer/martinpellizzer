Heliconia poeppigiana

Heliconia poeppigiana, commonly known as the red heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, boat-shaped inflorescences that are typically red or orange in color. The plant features broad, waxy leaves that form a rosette pattern, providing a lush, tropical appearance. Its flowers are not only ornamental but also serve as a habitat for various pollinators, including hummingbirds and bees.

Heliconia poeppigiana is often cultivated in warm climates for its aesthetic value and is sometimes used in floral arrangements.
