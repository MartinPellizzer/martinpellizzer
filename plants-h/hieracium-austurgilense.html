Hieracium austurgilense

Hieracium austurgilense, commonly known as the Austurian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy, often hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its flowers are yellow, with ray florets that are shorter than the central disk, and it produces numerous small achenes for seed dispersal.

Hieracium austurgilense is known for its resilience in harsh environments and its ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
