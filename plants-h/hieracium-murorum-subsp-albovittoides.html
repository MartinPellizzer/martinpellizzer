Hieracium murorum subsp. albovittoides

Hieracium murorum subsp.

albovittoides, commonly known as the white-banded hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered with fine, soft hairs. This subspecies is often found in meadows and disturbed soils across Europe.

It is noted for its distinctive white veins on the leaves, which give it its common name.
