Heracleum alpinum subsp. palmatum

Heracleum alpinum subsp.

palmatum, commonly known as the alpine cow parsnip, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

The plant features a thick, fleshy rhizome and large, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and yellowish-green, arranged in an umbel, and it produces a distinctive, aromatic scent.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
