Heteropterys megaptera f. eglandulosa

Heteropterys megaptera f. eglandulosa, commonly known as the glandular-leafed evergreen huito, is a variant of the species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are often covered with small, glandular dots, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
