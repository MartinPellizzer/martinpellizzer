Hechtia perotensis

Hechtia perotensis, commonly known as the Mexican hechtia, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick and fleshy. The leaves are typically blue-green in color with a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in its arid habitat. It produces striking red or orange flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer.

This plant is well-suited for rock gardens or as a container plant due to its compact size and low maintenance requirements.
