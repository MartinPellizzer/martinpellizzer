Hieracium penninum subsp. vulpianum

Hieracium penninum subsp.

vulpianum, commonly known as the foxglove hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers arranged in a composite head.

The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils.

This subspecies is distinguished by its slightly larger flowers and more robust growth compared to other Hieracium species.
