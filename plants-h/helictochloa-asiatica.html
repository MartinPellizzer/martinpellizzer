Helictochloa asiatica

Helictochloa asiatica, commonly known as the Asian hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is characterized by its fine, hairy texture and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal.

Helictochloa asiatica plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small invertebrates.
