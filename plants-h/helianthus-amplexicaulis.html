Helianthus amplexicaulis

Helianthus amplexicaulis, commonly known as the curled leaf sunflower, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spirally twisted leaves that wrap around the stem, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers with prominent central disks, typical of the sunflower genus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Helianthus amplexicaulis is an important plant for pollinators and has been studied for its ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
