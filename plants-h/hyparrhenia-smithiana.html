Hyparrhenia smithiana

Hyparrhenia smithiana, commonly known as the African grass or red grass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, dense growth and distinctive reddish coloration in its leaves and inflorescences. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often used in floral arrangements. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Hyparrhenia smithiana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
