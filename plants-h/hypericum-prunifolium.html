Hypericum prunifolium

Hypericum prunifolium, commonly known as rosehip or st. john's wort, is a perennial shrub native to North America.

It features opposite, elliptic to ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a reddish or yellowish underside. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its stems are woody and covered in small, scale-like structures.

Hypericum prunifolium is often found in open woods, along stream banks, and in disturbed soils.
