Hedychium gandasulium

Hedychium gandasulium, commonly known as the golden ginger, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fragrant, yellow-orange flower spikes that bloom in summer. The plant produces showy, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by broad, waxy bracts. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Hedychium gandasulium is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
