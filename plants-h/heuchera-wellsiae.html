Heuchera wellsiae

Heuchera wellsiae, commonly known as the Wells' alumroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking foliage, which features deep purple, bronze, or red leaves that vary in color depending on the season and light exposure. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in early spring, which are borne on upright, branching stems. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Heuchera wellsiae is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
