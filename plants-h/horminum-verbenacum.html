Horminum verbenacum

Horminum verbenacum, commonly known as sweet woodruff, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage that emits a distinctive vanilla-like scent. The plant features slender, creeping stems that often form a mat-like growth pattern on the ground. Its small, white flowers appear in clusters during late spring to early summer.

Horminum verbenacum is often used in traditional herbal remedies and as a decorative plant in gardens.
