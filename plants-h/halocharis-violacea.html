Halocharis violacea

Halocharis violacea, commonly known as the violetleaf halocharis, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a splash of color to its natural habitat. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, providing a dense foliage that helps it retain moisture.

Halocharis violacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping.
