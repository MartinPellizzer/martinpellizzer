Hieracium caesium subsp. sublancifolium

Hieracium caesium subsp.

sublancifolium, commonly known as the slender-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

The plant features upright stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are elongated and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface. It produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is characterized by its slender foliage and is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads.
