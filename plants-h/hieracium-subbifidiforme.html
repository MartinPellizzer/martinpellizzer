Hieracium subbifidiforme

Hieracium subbifidiforme, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough, hairy texture.

It produces yellow flowers from late spring to early autumn, contributing to its ecological role in pollinator habitats.
