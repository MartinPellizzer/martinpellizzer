Haemanthus coarctatus

Haemanthus coarctatus, commonly known as the Blood Lily, is a striking South African plant with striking red-orange flowers.

It is a perennial bulbous plant that produces clusters of tubular blooms during the summer months. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its unique appearance and ease of cultivation make it a favorite among gardeners and botanists alike.
