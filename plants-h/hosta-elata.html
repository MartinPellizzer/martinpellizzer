Hosta elata

Hosta elata, commonly known as the Giant Hosta, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 inches in length. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in late summer, typically in shades of white or pale yellow. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Hosta elata is a popular ornamental plant in gardens for its striking foliage and long blooming period.
