Hieracium bifidum subsp. hollei

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

hollei, commonly known as Holle's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along riverbanks, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features bright yellow composite flowers, which are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and its leaves are usually lance-shaped with serrated edges. It is characterized by its robust growth and ability to thrive in a variety of light conditions.

Botanically, it is distinguished by its specific subspecies traits, including its flower structure and leaf morphology.
