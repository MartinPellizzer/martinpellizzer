Hordeum vulgare var. sikangense

Hordeum vulgare var. sikangense, commonly known as the Tibetan barley, is a variant of barley native to the high-altitude regions of Tibet.

This plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, hard grains that are adapted to thrive in cold and arid conditions. It possesses a robust root system that enables it to survive in nutrient-poor soils. The leaves are typically narrow and hairy, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Botanically, it is classified as a member of the Poaceae family, sharing features with other grasses.
