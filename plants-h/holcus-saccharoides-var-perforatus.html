Holcus saccharoides var. perforatus

Holcus saccharoides var. perforatus, commonly known as the perforate grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, elongated leaves with a distinctive pale green color. The inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed spikelets. Its leaves often exhibit a unique perforation or gap near the base, giving it its common name.

This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks.
