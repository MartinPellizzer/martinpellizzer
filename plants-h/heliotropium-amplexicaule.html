Heliotropium amplexicaule

Heliotropium amplexicaule, commonly known as the hanging heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and opposite leaves that often wrap around the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Heliotropium amplexicaule is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
