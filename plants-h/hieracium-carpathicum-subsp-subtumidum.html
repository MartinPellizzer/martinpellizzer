Hieracium carpathicum subsp. subtumidum

Hieracium carpathicum subsp.

subtumidum, commonly known as the Carpathian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Carpathian Mountains. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and forest edges, preferring well-drained soils. It has hairy stems and lanceolate leaves with serrated edges.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
