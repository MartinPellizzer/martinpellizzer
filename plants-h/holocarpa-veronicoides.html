Holocarpa veronicoides

Holocarpa veronicoides, commonly known as the false heath, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, preferring full sun exposure. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, which are often pink or white in color. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and the plant is known for its compact growth habit.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Ericaceae, which includes other heath and related plants.
