Hieracium bifidum subsp. vensicum

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

vensicum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are typically arranged in dense, flat-topped clusters.

The leaves are generally basal, with a rough, hairy texture and a distinctive lobed or divided appearance. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along roadsides.

This subspecies is known for its adaptability and can be invasive in certain regions.
