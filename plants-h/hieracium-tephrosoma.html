Hieracium tephrosoma

Hieracium tephrosoma, commonly known as the tephrosoma hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, leafy stems and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in moist, disturbed soils such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its flowers are arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

The plant is often found in open habitats and can sometimes form dense colonies.
