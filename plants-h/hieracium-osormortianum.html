Hieracium osormortianum

Hieracium osormortianum, commonly known as the Osormortian hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous tiny florets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range in color from yellow to reddish-orange.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain insect species.
