Helichrysum mutisiifolium

Helichrysum mutisiifolium, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellow-orange flowers that resemble daisies, which are often used in floral arrangements due to their longevity. The plant has hairy, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Helichrysum mutisiifolium is also valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
