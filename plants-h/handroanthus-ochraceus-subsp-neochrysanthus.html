Handroanthus ochraceus subsp. neochrysanthus

Handroanthus ochraceus subsp.

neochrysanthus, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly Brazil. It is characterized by its striking, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season.

The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental species.

Its rapid growth and vibrant floral display make it a notable species in botanical gardens and landscapes.
