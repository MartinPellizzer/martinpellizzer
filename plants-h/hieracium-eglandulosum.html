Hieracium eglandulosum

Hieracium eglandulosum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows to a height of 10 to 50 centimeters, with basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, often blooming from June to September.

Hieracium eglandulosum is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.
