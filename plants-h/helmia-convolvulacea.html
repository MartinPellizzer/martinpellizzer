Helmia convolvulacea

Helmia convolvulacea, commonly known as the scarlet pimpernel, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a trailing perennial that produces small, showy flowers in shades of red and yellow. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shady habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.

Helmia convolvulacea is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through runners, making it a popular choice for ground cover.
