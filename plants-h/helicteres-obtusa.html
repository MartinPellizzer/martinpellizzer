Helicteres obtusa

Helicteres obtusa, commonly known as the broad-leaved hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to yellow. The plant belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other hibiscus species and cotton. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
