Hedyotis coreana

Hedyotis coreana, commonly known as Korean ironweed, is a perennial herb native to Korea and parts of East Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. The plant features small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom from summer to autumn. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Hedyotis coreana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
