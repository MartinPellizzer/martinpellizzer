Hieracium subsagittatum

Hieracium subsagittatum, commonly known as the sagittate hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and the flowers are yellow with ray florets.

It is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is considered a weed in some agricultural areas.
