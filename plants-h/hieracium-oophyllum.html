Hieracium oophyllum

Hieracium oophyllum, commonly known as the golden hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. The plant typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Hieracium oophyllum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common weed in many regions.
