Hartwegiella nasturtioides

Hartwegiella nasturtioides, commonly known as the Mexican watercress, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Central America.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. The plant has finely divided, feather-like leaves that are submerged or floating, and it produces small, yellowish flowers. Its leaves are edible and have a peppery flavor similar to watercress.

It is valued for its nutritional content and traditional use in local cuisines.
