Halimus sesuvioides var. angustifolium

Halimus sesuvioides var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved halimus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It is often found in areas with high salinity and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in coastal ecosystems.
