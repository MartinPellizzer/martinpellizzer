Hermione leucojifolia

Hermione leucojifolia, commonly known as the white-flowered bellwether, is a striking perennial herb native to the temperate regions of southern Africa.

It features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental value make it a favorite among horticulturists and garden enthusiasts.
