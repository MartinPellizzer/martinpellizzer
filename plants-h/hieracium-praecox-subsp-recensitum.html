Hieracium praecox subsp. recensitum

Hieracium praecox subsp.

recensitum, commonly known as the early hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its flowering period extends from June to August, and it produces seeds in small, fluffy structures that aid in wind dispersal.

The species is often found in temperate regions and is considered a common weed in agricultural areas.
