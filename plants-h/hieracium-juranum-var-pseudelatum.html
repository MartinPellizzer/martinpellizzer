Hieracium juranum var. pseudelatum

Hieracium juranum var. pseudelatum, commonly known as the false yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 50 cm. The plant features basal and alternate leaves that are lanceolate to ovate in shape, with rough, hairy surfaces. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and are produced from late spring to early autumn.

It is characterized by its fibrous root system and the presence of glandular hairs on its stems and leaves.
