Holboellia pterocaulis

Holboellia pterocaulis, commonly known as the African cypress, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are followed by woody, cone-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Holboellia pterocaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
