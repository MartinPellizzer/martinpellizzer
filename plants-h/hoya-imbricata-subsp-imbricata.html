Hoya imbricata subsp. imbricata

Hoya imbricata subsp.

imbricata, commonly known as the "Coral Hoya," is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its waxy, dark green leaves that are often marked with subtle veins and a distinctive coral-like bloom.

The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pink in color. It is a climbing or trailing plant that thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil. Hoya imbricata subsp.

imbricata is popular among houseplant enthusiasts for its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
