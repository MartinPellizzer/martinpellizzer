Hieracium atronitens

Hieracium atronitens, commonly known as the black-centred hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range in color from yellow to reddish-brown.

Hieracium atronitens is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in many regions.
