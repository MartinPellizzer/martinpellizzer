Hedychium flavescens

Hedychium flavescens, commonly known as the golden ginger, is a tropical perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has strap-like, dark green leaves and a rhizomatous root system. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Hedychium flavescens is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
