Hieracium leiopogon subsp. gariodianum

Hieracium leiopogon subsp.

gariodianum, commonly known as the Gariod's hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and foliage with serrated edges.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are basal and lanceolate, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head.

This subspecies is distinguished by its slightly rounded leaf bases and is often found in high-altitude environments.
