Hedyotis acutangula

Hedyotis acutangula, commonly known as the sharp-leaved bedstraw, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers. Its flowering period usually occurs in the spring and early summer.

Hedyotis acutangula is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
