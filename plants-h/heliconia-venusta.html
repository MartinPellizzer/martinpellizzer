Heliconia venusta

Heliconia venusta, commonly known as the crimson heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its large, brightly colored inflorescences. The plant produces striking, spiral-shaped clusters of flowers that are typically red or orange in color. Its leaves are broad and waxy, providing a lush, tropical appearance.

Heliconia venusta is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in warm climates due to its vibrant blooms and unique structure.
