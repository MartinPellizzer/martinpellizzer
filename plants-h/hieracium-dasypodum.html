Hieracium dasypodum

Hieracium dasypodum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features upright, hairy stems and basal leaves that are typically lance-shaped. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hieracium dasypodum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
