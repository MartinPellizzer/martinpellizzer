Hypericum coadunatum var. atlanticum

Hypericum coadunatum var. atlanticum, commonly known as the Atlantic St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and narrow, linear leaves. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal dunes and cliffs. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to harsh coastal environments and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
