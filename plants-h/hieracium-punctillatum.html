Hieracium punctillatum

Hieracium punctillatum, commonly known as the dotted hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

The species is notable for its small, dot-like markings on the leaves, which give it its common name.
