Hieracium cylindraceum

Hieracium cylindraceum, commonly known as the cylindrical hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy stems. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a dense, cylindrical cluster.

Hieracium cylindraceum is known for its ability to thrive in various soil types and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
