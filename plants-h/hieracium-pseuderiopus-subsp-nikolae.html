Hieracium pseuderiopus subsp. nikolae

Hieracium pseuderiopus subsp.

nikolae, commonly known as the Nikolai's hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering stems that bear yellow ray florets and numerous disk florets.

This subspecies is native to specific regions in Europe, particularly in the Carpathian Basin. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and has been the subject of botanical studies due to its limited distribution and unique morphology.
