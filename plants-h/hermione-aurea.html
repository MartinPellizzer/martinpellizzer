Hermione aurea

Hermione aurea, commonly known as the golden Hermione, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ease of care.
