Herpetophytum nigricans

Herpetophytum nigricans, commonly known as the black orchid, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its dark purple to black flowers, which are often speckled with white or yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air, and it thrives in high humidity and warm temperatures.

The unique coloration of its flowers makes it a popular specimen among orchid enthusiasts.
