Hibiscus meyeri-johannis

Hibiscus meyeri-johannis, commonly known as the Meyer's hibiscus, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring broad, heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the summer months. Its flowering period makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
