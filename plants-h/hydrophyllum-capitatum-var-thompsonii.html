Hydrophyllum capitatum var. thompsonii

Hydrophyllum capitatum var. thompsonii, commonly known as Thompson's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its blue to violet flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into cylindrical seed heads.

This variety is distinguished by its unique floral structure and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.
