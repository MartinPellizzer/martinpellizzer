Hieracium muraltii

Hieracium muraltii, commonly known as the Muralt's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom from late spring to early summer.

Hieracium muraltii is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
