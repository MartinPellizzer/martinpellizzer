Heteromeris polifolia

Heteromeris polifolia, commonly known as the featherleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands and marshy areas of South America.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are finely divided, giving it a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in moist to waterlogged soils and is often found in riparian zones.

Heteromeris polifolia plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic and semi-aquatic organisms.
