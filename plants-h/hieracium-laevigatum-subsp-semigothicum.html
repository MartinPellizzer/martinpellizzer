Hieracium laevigatum subsp. semigothicum

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

semigothicum, commonly known as the smooth-haired hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf structures.

The plant typically features basal leaves that are broadly ovate to lanceolate in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and are accompanied by bracts that are often glandular.

This subspecies is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, where it thrives in full sun to partial shade.
