Hieracium uniflorum

Hieracium uniflorum, commonly known as the singleflower hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces a single, showy yellow flower per stem during the summer months. Its flowers are characterized by their prominent central disc surrounded by ray florets.

Hieracium uniflorum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
