Hieracium pospichalii subsp. pospichalii

Hieracium pospichalii subsp.

pospichalii, commonly known as the Pospichal's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow flower heads composed of numerous small florets.

The plant typically grows in calcareous grasslands and open habitats, preferring well-drained soils. It is native to the Central European region, including parts of Austria, Czech Republic, and Germany.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in many areas.
