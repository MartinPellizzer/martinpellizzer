Hieracium rubriflorum

Hieracium rubriflorum, commonly known as the red-flowered hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy, bright red flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically basal, with a rough, hairy texture, and the stems are often reddish in color.

Hieracium rubriflorum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it can spread aggressively through rhizomes.
