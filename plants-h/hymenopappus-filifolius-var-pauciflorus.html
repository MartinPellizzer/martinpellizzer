Hymenopappus filifolius var. pauciflorus

Hymenopappus filifolius var. pauciflorus, commonly known as the spiny sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, bract-like structures called pappus that aid in wind dispersal of its seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions.
