Hirculus trinervius

Hirculus trinervius, commonly known as the three-veined hirculus, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is characterized by its small, clustered inflorescences known as cyathia, which are typical of the genus Euphorbia. The leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a prominent venation pattern with three main veins. The plant produces yellowish-orange flowers that are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.
