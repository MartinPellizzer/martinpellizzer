Hoarea × colvillii

Hoarea × colvillii, commonly known as the hybrid saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Hoarea tenuis and Hoarea tenuifolia, both of which are endemic to South America. The plant features compact, rosette-like growth with hairy, fleshy leaves adapted to arid and cold environments. Its flowers are typically small and white or pink, appearing in early spring.

Hoarea × colvillii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils.
