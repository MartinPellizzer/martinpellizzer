Hypericum affine

Hypericum affine, commonly known as upright St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are oval to elliptic in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in meadows and woodland edges.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used for their anti-inflammatory and antidepressant effects.
