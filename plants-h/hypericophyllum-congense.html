Hypericophyllum congense

Hypericophyllum congense, commonly known as the Congo hypericum, is a flowering plant native to the Democratic Republic of the Congo.

It belongs to the family Hypericaceae, which includes various species of St. John's wort. The plant is characterized by its erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with four petals and numerous stamens.

It thrives in moist, forested areas and is of interest for its potential medicinal properties.
