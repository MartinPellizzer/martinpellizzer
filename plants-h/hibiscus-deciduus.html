Hibiscus deciduus

Hibiscus deciduus, commonly known as the African hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features large, showy flowers with five petals, often in shades of red, pink, or white. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Hibiscus deciduus thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.
