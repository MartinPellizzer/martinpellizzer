Hakea reptans

Hakea reptans, commonly known as the rock hakea, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, woody stems and small, needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces vibrant red or orange flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. Hakea reptans is adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it a hardy species suitable for coastal and dry garden settings.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for landscaping in regions with limited water resources.
