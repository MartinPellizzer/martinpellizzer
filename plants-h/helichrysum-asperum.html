Helichrysum asperum

Helichrysum asperum, commonly known as rough helichrysum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and hairy, rough-textured leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments. Its flowers are often used in traditional medicine and as ornamental plants due to their vibrant color and long-lasting blooms.

Helichrysum asperum is also valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in perfumes and herbal remedies.
