Hieracium basifloccum subsp. subglabrisquamum

Hieracium basifloccum subsp.

subglabrisquamum, commonly known as the smooth-squamate hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow flower heads.

The leaves are typically lanceolate and covered with sparse hairs, while the stems are usually glabrous. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This subspecies is distinguished by its reduced leaf pubescence and subtle morphological differences from other members of the species.
