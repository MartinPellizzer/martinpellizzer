Hesperis angustifolia

Hesperis angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a slightly hairy texture.

Hesperis angustifolia is often found in dry, disturbed soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
