Hedypnois arenaria var. maximiliana

Hedypnois arenaria var. maximiliana, commonly known as the sand dune spurge, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in sandy, well-drained soils, often found in dune ecosystems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring, and its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs. It plays a role in stabilizing sand dunes and preventing erosion.

Due to its specialized habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically important species.
