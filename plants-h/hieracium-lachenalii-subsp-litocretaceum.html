Hieracium lachenalii subsp. litocretaceum

Hieracium lachenalii subsp.

litocretaceum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette-forming growth habit and yellow flowering heads.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine soils, thriving in harsh, high-altitude environments. Its leaves are usually hairy and lance-shaped, while its flowers are clustered in dense, disc-like inflorescences.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to extreme conditions and its role in alpine ecosystems.
