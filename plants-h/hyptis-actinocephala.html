Hyptis actinocephala

Hyptis actinocephala, commonly known as the star-headed hyptis, is a perennial herb native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal properties. The plant is characterized by its hairy, square stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a serrated margin. It produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hyptis actinocephala is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
