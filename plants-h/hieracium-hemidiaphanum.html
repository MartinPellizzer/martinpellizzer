Hieracium hemidiaphanum

Hieracium hemidiaphanum, commonly known as the translucent hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosette of lanceolate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Native to Europe, it thrives in moist meadows and woodland edges.

Its translucent, waxy leaves are a distinctive feature, contributing to its unique botanical appearance.
