Hoya amboinensis

Hoya amboinensis, commonly known as the Wax Plant or the Porcelain Flower, is a popular epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often waxy and covered in a fine layer of white pubescence. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped, waxy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, depending on the variety. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Hoya amboinensis is prized for its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance, making it a favorite among indoor gardeners.
