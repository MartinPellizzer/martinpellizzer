Helictotrichon filifolium subsp. cantabricum

Helictotrichon filifolium subsp.

cantabricum, commonly known as the Cantabrian hairgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the Iberian Peninsula. It features slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wet meadows and stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting biodiversity in its native habitat.
