Humata trifoliata

Humata trifoliata, commonly known as the three-leaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red in color. Humata trifoliata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
