Hieracium pseudobifidum subsp. transsylvaniciflorum

Hieracium pseudobifidum subsp.

transsylvaniciflorum, commonly known as the Transylvanian Hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features bright yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, with hairy stems and leaves that are often lobed or deeply divided. Its leaves are basal and alternate, with a rough texture, and the flowers bloom from late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal potential in traditional herbal practices.
