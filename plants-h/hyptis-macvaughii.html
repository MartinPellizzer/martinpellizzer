Hyptis macvaughii

Hyptis macvaughii, commonly known as the Mexican blue sage, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and spikes of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. It is often found in forest understories and along stream banks, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its habitat.

Hyptis macvaughii is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
