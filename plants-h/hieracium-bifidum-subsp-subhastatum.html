Hieracium bifidum subsp. subhastatum

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

subhastatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm.

The plant features basal leaves that are lanceolate and often have a reddish or purplish hue, while the upper leaves are narrower and less conspicuous. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and the plant produces small, oval seeds that are dispersed by wind.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems.
