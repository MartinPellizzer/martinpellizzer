Heliocereus schrankii var. elegantissimus

Heliocereus schrankii var. elegantissimus, commonly known as the elegant night bloomer, is a cactus species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces large, fragrant, white flowers that open at night and remain open for only one night. These flowers are typically borne at the tips of the stems.

It is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.
