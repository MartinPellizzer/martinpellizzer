Halfordia drupifera

Halfordia drupifera, commonly known as the tropical fig tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, pear-shaped fruits. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth, glossy surface.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
