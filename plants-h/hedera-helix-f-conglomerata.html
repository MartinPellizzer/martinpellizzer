Hedera helix f. conglomerata

Hedera helix f. conglomerata, commonly known as the Variegated Ivy, is a cultivar of the common ivy plant.

It is characterized by its distinctive variegated foliage, featuring shades of white, yellow, and green on its leaves. This plant is a climbing evergreen vine that thrives in shady to partially shaded environments. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by dark blue berries.

The Variegated Ivy is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to cover walls or trellises.
