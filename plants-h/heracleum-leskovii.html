Heracleum leskovii

Heracleum leskovii, commonly known as the Leskov's cow parsnip, is a tall biennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbellate inflorescence and aromatic foliage. The plant can grow up to 2 meters in height, with large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in complex umbels, and it produces small, dry fruits known as schizocarps.

The plant contains various bioactive compounds and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
