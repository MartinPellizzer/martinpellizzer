Hieracium phylladinum

Hieracium phylladinum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom from June to August.

Hieracium phylladinum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
