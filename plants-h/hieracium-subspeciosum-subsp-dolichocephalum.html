Hieracium subspeciosum subsp. dolichocephalum

Hieracium subspeciosum subsp.

dolichocephalum, commonly known as the long-headed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. It has alternate, heart-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces, and its stems are usually reddish in color.

This subspecies is noted for its ecological importance as a food source for various pollinators.
