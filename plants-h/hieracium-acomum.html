Hieracium acomum

Hieracium acomum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features bright yellow flowers with a central disk surrounded by ray florets, and it often grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. Hieracium acomum has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette.

It is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
