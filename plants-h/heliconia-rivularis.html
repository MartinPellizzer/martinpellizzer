Heliconia rivularis

Heliconia rivularis, commonly known as the river heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as riverbanks and forest understories. Its inflorescence consists of a spiral arrangement of flowers surrounded by large, fleshy bracts.

Heliconia rivularis is also valued for its ornamental qualities and plays a role in supporting local biodiversity.
