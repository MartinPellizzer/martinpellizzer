Hymenophyllum fucoides var. calodictyon

Hymenophyllum fucoides var. calodictyon, commonly known as the mossy fern, is a small, epiphytic fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically less than 5 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or rocks in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are covered in a dense layer of tiny, hair-like structures called rhizoids, which aid in water retention.

The spore-producing structures, or sporangia, are borne on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its unique reproductive biology.
