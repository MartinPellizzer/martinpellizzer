Hedysarum boreale f. niveum

Hedysarum boreale f. niveum, commonly known as the white-flowered field vetch, is a perennial legume native to northern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a food source for various insects and wildlife.
