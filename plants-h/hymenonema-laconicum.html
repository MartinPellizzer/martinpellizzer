Hymenonema laconicum

Hymenonema laconicum, commonly known as the Laconic Hymenonema, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by small, round berries that are edible but often tart. Its compact growth habit and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Hymenonema laconicum is well adapted to dry, sunny conditions and thrives in well-drained soils.
