Hymenorebutia aurea

Hymenorebutia aurea, commonly known as the golden crown cactus, is a species of cactus native to Bolivia.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and vibrant yellow flowers. The plant features cylindrical stems covered in small, white spines, and its leaves are typically reduced to tubercles. It thrives in arid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Hymenorebutia aurea is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.
