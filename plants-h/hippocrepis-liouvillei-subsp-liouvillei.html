Hippocrepis liouvillei subsp. liouvillei

Hippocrepis liouvillei subsp.

liouvillei, commonly known as the Liouville's bugleweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in sunny, open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.

The plant features wiry stems and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
