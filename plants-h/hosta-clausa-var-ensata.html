Hosta clausa var. ensata

Hosta clausa var. ensata, commonly known as the Japanese hosta, is a compact, ornamental plant native to Japan.

It features broad, waxy, dark green leaves that are often adorned with striking white or light green variegation. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white or pale blue, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is prized for its foliage and is a popular choice for rock gardens and shaded borders.
