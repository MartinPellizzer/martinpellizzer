Hieracium ciliatissimum

Hieracium ciliatissimum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are hairy and often have a serrated edge, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium ciliatissimum is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in some areas.
