Hippomarathrum crispum

Hippomarathrum crispum, commonly known as the curly cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically clustered at the ends of the stems. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating a high degree of drought tolerance.

Its unique appearance and adaptive features make it a notable species within the Cactaceae family.
