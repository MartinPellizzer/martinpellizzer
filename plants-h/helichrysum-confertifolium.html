Helichrysum confertifolium

Helichrysum confertifolium, commonly known as the dense-leaved everlasting.

It is a perennial shrub native to South Africa, characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its leaves are typically narrow and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems.

Helichrysum confertifolium is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
