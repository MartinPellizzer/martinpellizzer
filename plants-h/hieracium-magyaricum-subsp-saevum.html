Hieracium magyaricum subsp. saevum

Hieracium magyaricum subsp.

saevum, commonly known as the Hungarian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to orange composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It has basal and sometimes stem leaves that are usually hairy and lanceolate in shape.

Its flowering period occurs from late spring to early autumn, contributing to its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
