Hieracium bangii subsp. austroboliviense

Hieracium bangii subsp.

austroboliviense, commonly known as the Bolivian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America, particularly Bolivia and Argentina. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Its foliage is generally hairy and has a distinctive, somewhat aggressive growth habit.
