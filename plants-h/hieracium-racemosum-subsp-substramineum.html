Hieracium racemosum subsp. substramineum

Hieracium racemosum subsp.

substramineum, commonly known as the creeping hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with creeping stems that form dense mats.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming from June to August. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in moist, disturbed soils.

It is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along riverbanks.
