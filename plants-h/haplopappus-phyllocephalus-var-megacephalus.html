Haplopappus phyllocephalus var. megacephalus

Haplopappus phyllocephalus var. megacephalus, commonly known as the large-headed goatweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and cylindrical, spiny stems. The plant typically grows from 30 to 60 centimeters tall, with narrow, gray-green leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stem. Its most distinctive feature is its large, showy, yellow-orange flower heads, which are composed of numerous tiny florets.

This species is adapted to harsh environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and efficient water use strategies.
