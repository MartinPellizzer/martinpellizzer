Hymenoxys cooperi var. canescens

Hymenoxys cooperi var. canescens, commonly known as the grayleaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish foliage and yellow ray flowers that resemble those of the common sunflower. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, dark seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation.

The botanical features of this species make it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
