Helminthia lusitanica var. subinervis

Helminthia lusitanica var. subinervis, commonly known as the Lusitanian helminthia, is a rare and endemic plant species native to parts of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its delicate, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or along stream banks.
