Helleborine palustris lusus tricolor

Helleborine palustris lusus tricolor, commonly known as the three-colored marsh helleborine, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as wetlands and forest floors. The plant features dark green, strap-like leaves and produces striking flowers with three distinct colors—white, green, and purple—on the same flower spike. It is characterized by its underground rhizomes and is often found in temperate regions of Europe.

This orchid is notable for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and its unique floral structure.
