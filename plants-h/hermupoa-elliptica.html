Hermupoa elliptica

Hermupoa elliptica, commonly known as the elliptic euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, elliptical leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a cup-shaped inflorescence, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Hermupoa elliptica is valued for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements in drought-tolerant gardens.
