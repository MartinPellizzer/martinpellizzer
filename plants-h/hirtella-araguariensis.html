Hirtella araguariensis

Hirtella araguariensis, commonly known as the Araguari hirtella, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil, specifically the states of Pará and Maranhão, where it grows in humid tropical forests. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and hairy, ovate leaves. It typically reaches a height of about 2 to 3 meters and is often found in understory vegetation.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
