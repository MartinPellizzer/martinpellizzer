Huperzia boliviana

Huperzia boliviana, commonly known as the Bolivian clubmoss, is a species of evergreen fern-like plant belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its creeping, rhizomatous growth habit and small, scale-like leaves that form dense mats. Native to the Andean regions of South America, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. The plant produces sporangia on specialized structures called strobila, which are typically located at the base of the leaves.

Huperzia boliviana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
