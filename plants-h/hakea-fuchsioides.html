Hakea fuchsioides

Hakea fuchsioides, commonly known as the scarlet hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of bright red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has needle-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal areas.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native birds and insects.
