Hieracium austrotatricum

Hieracium austrotatricum, commonly known as the Austro-Tatricum Hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. It has a taproot system and produces numerous flowering stems that bear small, daisy-like blooms.

Hieracium austrotatricum is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its aggressive growth habit.
