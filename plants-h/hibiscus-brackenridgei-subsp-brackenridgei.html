Hibiscus brackenridgei subsp. brackenridgei

Hibiscus brackenridgei subsp.

brackenridgei, commonly known as the Brackenridge's hibiscus, is a species native to the island of Guam in the Mariana Islands. It is a shrub characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red.

The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces fruits that are capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in coastal habitats and is adapted to the tropical climate of its native region.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
