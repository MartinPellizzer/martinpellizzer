Hieracium laggeri subsp. chaetobium

Hieracium laggeri subsp.

chaetobium, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to orange composite flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its hairy stems and leaves, which provide a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade.
