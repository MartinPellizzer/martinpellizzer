Hippocrepis scabra f. fruticulosa

Hippocrepis scabra f. fruticulosa, commonly known as the rough hawksbeard, is a compact, shrub-like perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features gray-green, hairy leaves that are often lobed and covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces yellow, rayless flowers arranged in a single, elongated head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and coastal areas.

Its distinctive morphology and hardy nature make it a notable addition to rock gardens and wildflower displays.
