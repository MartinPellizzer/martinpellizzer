Hieracium obovalifrons

Hieracium obovalifrons, commonly known as the oval-fronded hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this species thrives in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and woodland edges. The plant is often found in disturbed habitats and can form dense colonies.

Its flowers are typically visited by various pollinators, including bees and flies.
