Hibbertia lepidocalyx subsp. lepidocalyx

Hibbertia lepidocalyx subsp.

lepidocalyx, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hibbertia, is a species native to southeastern Australia. It is a low-growing, woody shrub with distinctive yellow-orange flowers that bloom in spring.

The leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a textured appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable plant for rock gardens or coastal areas.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural displays.
