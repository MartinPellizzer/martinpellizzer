Hypericum grisebachii

Hypericum grisebachii, commonly known as the yellow-stemmed St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit, with slender, woody stems that often turn reddish or yellowish with age. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and have a slightly hairy surface.

Hypericum grisebachii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained, sunny conditions.
