Helicia incisa

Helicia incisa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed helenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Theaceae, which includes camellias and other ornamental plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
