Haloragis odontocarpa f. pterocarpa

Haloragis odontocarpa f. pterocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved waterwheel, is a succulent plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Haloragaceae family and is characterized by its rosette growth habit and fleshy, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in whorls along the stems, giving it a distinctive wheel-like appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal environments.

This variety is notable for its unique morphology and adaptation to its native habitat.
