Hoya pseudovalifolia

Hoya pseudovalifolia, commonly known as the "Lace Leaf Hoya," is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming during the summer months. Hoya pseudovalifolia is a popular houseplant due to its low maintenance and ability to thrive in indirect light.

Its unique foliage and elegant blossoms make it a sought-after addition to many indoor gardens.
