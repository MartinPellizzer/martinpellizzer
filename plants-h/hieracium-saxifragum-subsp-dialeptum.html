Hieracium saxifragum subsp. dialeptum

Hieracium saxifragum subsp.

dialeptum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.

The plant has basal rosettes of leaves and produces yellow flowers in late summer. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a distinctive lobed shape.

This subspecies is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some related species.
