Hesperis oblongifolia

Hesperis oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hesperis, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are elongated and narrow, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
