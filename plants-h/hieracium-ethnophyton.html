Hieracium ethnophyton

Hieracium ethnophyton, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as meadows and roadsides. It has hairy stems and produces seeds in small, elongated achenes.

Hieracium ethnophyton is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in many regions.
