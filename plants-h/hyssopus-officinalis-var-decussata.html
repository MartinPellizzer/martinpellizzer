Hyssopus officinalis var. decussata

Hyssopus officinalis var. decussata, commonly known as creeping hyssop, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features small, tubular blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has aromatic, gray-green leaves with a slightly fuzzy texture, and it produces small, four-lobed fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its medicinal properties include antiseptic and expectorant qualities, historically used in traditional herbal medicine.
