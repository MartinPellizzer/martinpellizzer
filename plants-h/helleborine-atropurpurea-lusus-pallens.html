Helleborine atropurpurea lusus pallens

Helleborine atropurpurea lusus pallens, commonly known as the purple-flowered helleborine, is a species of orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking purple and green streaked flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist woodland areas, preferring rich, humusy soil. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy texture, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the forest floor.

This orchid is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
