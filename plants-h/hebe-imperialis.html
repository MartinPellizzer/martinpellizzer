Hebe imperialis

Hebe imperialis, commonly known as the imperial hebe, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of violet or blue flowers that bloom in spring and early summer. Hebe imperialis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for landscaping.
