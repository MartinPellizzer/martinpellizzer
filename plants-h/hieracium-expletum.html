Hieracium expletum

Hieracium expletum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellowish to reddish flowers and is native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Hieracium expletum is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it an invasive species in some regions.
