Hieracium piloselliforme

Hieracium piloselliforme, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble clusters of tiny flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
