Horminum splendens

Horminum splendens, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Horminum splendens is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
