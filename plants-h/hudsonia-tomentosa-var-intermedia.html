Hudsonia tomentosa var. intermedia

Hudsonia tomentosa var. intermedia, commonly known as the woolly mountain-mahoe, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, white-tomentose (fuzzy) foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a leathery texture, and the stems are covered in a thick layer of fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
