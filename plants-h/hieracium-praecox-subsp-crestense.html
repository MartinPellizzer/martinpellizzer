Hieracium praecox subsp. crestense

Hieracium praecox subsp.

crestense, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. It has hairy stems and leaves with a rough texture, and its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in rocky and high-altitude environments.
