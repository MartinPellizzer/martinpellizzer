Hieracium racemosum subsp. pyramoides

Hieracium racemosum subsp.

pyramoides, commonly known as the pyramidal hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers form a dense, pyramid-like cluster of yellow disk flowers.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
