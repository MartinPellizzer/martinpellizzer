Hieracium murorum subsp. oigocladum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

oigocladum, commonly known as the mouse-ear hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves and slender, upright flowering stems.

The flowers are typically yellow, arranged in a single head, and are surrounded by involucral bracts. It thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows.

This subspecies is notable for its compact growth habit and is often found in temperate regions of Europe.
