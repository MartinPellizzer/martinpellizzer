Henophyton zygarrhenum

Henophyton zygarrhenum, commonly known as the golden crown fern, is a unique and rare plant species characterized by its intricate frond structure.

It features a tall, slender stem that supports a crown of delicate, fan-shaped leaves with a golden hue. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical rainforests. Its reproductive structures are concealed within specialized leaf sheaths, which protect the spore-producing organs.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
