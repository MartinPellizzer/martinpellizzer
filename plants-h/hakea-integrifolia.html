Hakea integrifolia

Hakea integrifolia, commonly known as the coastal hakea, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces cylindrical, woody cones that contain numerous small, edible seeds. It thrives in coastal environments, often growing on sand dunes and rocky shores.

Hakea integrifolia is well adapted to salt spray and sandy soils, making it a hardy and resilient species.
