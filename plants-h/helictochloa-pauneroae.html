Helictochloa pauneroae

Helictochloa pauneroae, commonly known as the Patagonian needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the arid regions of southern South America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. This plant is well adapted to harsh, dry environments, with deep root systems that help it survive in nutrient-poor soils. It plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for local wildlife.

Helictochloa pauneroae is also of interest to botanists due to its unique morphological features and ecological significance in its native habitat.
