Halimium scabrosum var. canescens

Halimium scabrosum var. canescens, commonly known as the hairy rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its woody stems and small, fleshy leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow to orange flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and hardy addition to Mediterranean-style gardens.
