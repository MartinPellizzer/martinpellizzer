Hypoxis galpinii

Hypoxis galpinii, commonly known as the Galpin's yellowstar, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Hypoxidaceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces a cluster of bright yellow flowers at the center of the rosette, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Hypoxis galpinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and as a ground cover.
