Hibiscus cisplatinus

Hibiscus cisplatinus, commonly known as the platinum hibiscus, is a rare flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from deep red to pale yellow, depending on the species. The plant thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often with a glossy surface that reflects light.

Hibiscus cisplatinus is also noted for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.
