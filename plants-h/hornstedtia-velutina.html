Hornstedtia velutina

Hornstedtia velutina, commonly known as the velvetleaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green foliage and striking, pendulous flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air, and it thrives in high light conditions with consistent humidity.

Hornstedtia velutina is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and unique growth habit.
