Hieracium cineriticeps

Hieracium cineriticeps, commonly known as the ash-gray hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring well-drained conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive grayish hue.

Hieracium cineriticeps is often found in open habitats and can thrive in a range of soil types.
