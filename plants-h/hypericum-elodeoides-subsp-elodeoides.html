Hypericum elodeoides subsp. elodeoides

Hypericum elodeoides subsp.

elodeoides, commonly known as water St. John's wort, is a semi-aquatic plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats such as ponds, ditches, and wetlands.

The plant has slender, branching stems that often float on the water surface, with small, yellowish-green leaves. Its flowers are bright yellow, with four petals and numerous stamens, and bloom in summer.

It is known for its ability to tolerate a range of water conditions and is often used in wetland restoration projects.
