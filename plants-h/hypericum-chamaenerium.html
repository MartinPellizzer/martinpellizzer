Hypericum chamaenerium

Hypericum chamaenerium, commonly known as creeping St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
