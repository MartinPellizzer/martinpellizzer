Holothrix aspera

Holothrix aspera, commonly known as the rough holothrix, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its woody stems and dense clusters of small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are typically arranged in terminal clusters, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The leaves and flowers are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
