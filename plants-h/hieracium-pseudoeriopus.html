Hieracium pseudoeriopus

Hieracium pseudoeriopus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough, hairy texture, and its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
