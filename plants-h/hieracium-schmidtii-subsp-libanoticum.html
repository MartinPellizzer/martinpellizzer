Hieracium schmidtii subsp. libanoticum

Hieracium schmidtii subsp.

libanoticum, commonly known as the Lebanese hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Eastern Mediterranean. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and daisy-like yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, demonstrating a preference for well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually alternate and lance-shaped, with a rough texture.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
