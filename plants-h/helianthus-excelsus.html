Helianthus excelsus

Helianthus excelsus, commonly known as the swamp sunflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright stems with rough, hairy foliage and produces large, showy yellow-orange flowers with a central disk. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. Its botanical characteristics include lance-shaped leaves and a flowering period that spans from summer to fall.

Helianthus excelsus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
