Hieracium rectum subsp. pseudoeriophorum

Hieracium rectum subsp.

pseudoeriophorum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
