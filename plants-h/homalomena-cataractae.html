Homalomena cataractae

Homalomena cataractae, commonly known as the waterfall caladium, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that come in shades of green, red, and purple. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It belongs to the Araceae family, which includes other ornamental plants like philodendrons and dieffenbachias.

Homalomena cataractae requires well-draining soil and consistent humidity to flourish.
