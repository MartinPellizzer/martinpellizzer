Haworthia ryderiana

Haworthia ryderiana, commonly known as the Zulu bulb, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rosette-shaped leaves that are typically green with white or pale markings. The plant produces small, white flowers on tall stems during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or indoor cultivation.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
