Homalium oubanguiense

Homalium oubanguiense, commonly known as the Oubangui beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Africa.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are ovate and have a rough texture, while its bark is thick and fissured.

Homalium oubanguiense thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.
