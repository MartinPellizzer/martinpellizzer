Hypericum olympicum f. majus

Hypericum olympicum f. majus, commonly known as the greater St. John's wort, is a variant of the St. John's wort species native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features opposite, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowering period typically occurs from July to August, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in well-drained, sunny conditions.
