Hieracium sabaudum subsp. sabaudum

Hieracium sabaudum subsp.

sabaudum, commonly known as the Sabaudian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. It has lance-shaped leaves with rough textures and produces numerous small, yellow disk flowers in clusters.

This subspecies is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
