Hieracium subumbellatiforme

Hieracium subumbellatiforme, commonly known as the clustered hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and a flowering head that resembles a small umbel, giving it its specific epithet. The plant produces yellow flowers that are typically arranged in a tight cluster, and it has hairy stems and leaves. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Due to its aggressive growth, it can become invasive in some regions.
