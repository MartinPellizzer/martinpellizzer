Hippeastrum cuzcoense

Hippeastrum cuzcoense, commonly known as the Cuzco amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It features large, showy flowers that can range in color from white to deep red, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces strap-like leaves that are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native arid environment. Its blooming period typically occurs in the spring or early summer, making it a popular ornamental plant.

The bulb is essential for the plant's survival, allowing it to go dormant during dry seasons and regrow when conditions are favorable.
