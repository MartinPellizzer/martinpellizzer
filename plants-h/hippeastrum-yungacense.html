Hippeastrum yungacense

Hippeastrum yungacense, commonly known as the Yungas giant amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to the humid Andean cloud forests of Bolivia and Peru.

It features large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, typically in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces strap-like, dark green leaves that are often mistaken for grass due to their appearance. It thrives in warm, moist environments with partial shade and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Hippeastrum yungacense is prized for its ornamental value and is a popular choice for tropical gardens and indoor cultivation.
