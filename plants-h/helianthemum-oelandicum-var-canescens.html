Helianthemum oelandicum var. canescens

Helianthemum oelandicum var. canescens, commonly known as the grey stonecress, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to parts of Europe.

It features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer, typically from June to August. The leaves are usually grey-green and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, sunny environments.

This variety is part of the Cistaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh conditions.
