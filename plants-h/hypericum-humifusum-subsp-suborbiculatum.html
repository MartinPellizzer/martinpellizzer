Hypericum humifusum subsp. suborbiculatum

Hypericum humifusum subsp.

suborbiculatum, commonly known as the broad-leaf St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the eastern United States. It features woody stems that are often covered with reddish or orange bark, and its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, typically with a smooth or slightly toothed margin.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
