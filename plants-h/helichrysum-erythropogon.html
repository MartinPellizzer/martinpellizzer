Helichrysum erythropogon

Helichrysum erythropogon, commonly known as the golden everlasting or the red top everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that are often used in dried floral arrangements due to their long-lasting nature. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and has a compact, bushy growth habit. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

Helichrysum erythropogon is also valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
