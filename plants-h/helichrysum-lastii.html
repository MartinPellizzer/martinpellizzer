Helichrysum lastii

Helichrysum lastii, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that resemble tiny suns. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces seed heads that remain intact for a long time, giving it the common name "everlasting.". It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Helichrysum lastii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
