Helleborus multifidus subsp. bocconei

Helleborus multifidus subsp.

bocconei, commonly known as the Boccone hellebore, is a perennial evergreen herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features dark green, leathery leaves that are deeply lobed and often have a distinctive wavy margin.

The plant produces showy, nodding flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to bloom during the colder months.
