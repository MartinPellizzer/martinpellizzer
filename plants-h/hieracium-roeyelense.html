Hieracium roeyelense

Hieracium roeyelense, commonly known as the Roeyelens hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium roeyelense is often found in temperate climates and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
