Hieracium clinocladium

Hieracium clinocladium, commonly known as the bent-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

Hieracium clinocladium is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
