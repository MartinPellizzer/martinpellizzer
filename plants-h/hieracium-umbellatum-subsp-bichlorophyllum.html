Hieracium umbellatum subsp. bichlorophyllum

Hieracium umbellatum subsp.

bichlorophyllum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features basal rosettes of leaves and produces flowering heads that are typically yellow or orange in color.

The plant has a fibrous root system and is characterized by its flat, disc-shaped flower heads with yellow ray florets. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

This subspecies is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
