Hieracium crenulentum

Hieracium crenulentum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in meadows and grasslands. The plant has a taproot system and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

It is considered an invasive species in some regions due to its rapid spread and competitive growth.
