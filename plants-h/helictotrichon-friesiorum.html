Helictotrichon friesiorum

Helictotrichon friesiorum, commonly known as the Prairie Dropseed, is a grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and produces fluffy, feathery seed heads that are visually striking. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies, meadows, and disturbed soils. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a hardy choice for native landscaping.

Its botanical features make it an important component of ecological restoration projects.
