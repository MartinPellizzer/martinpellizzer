Hieracium budoschiense

Hieracium budoschiense, commonly known as the Bodoschiense hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disc.

Hieracium budoschiense is noted for its hardy nature and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
