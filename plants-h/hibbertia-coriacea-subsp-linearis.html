Hibbertia coriacea subsp. linearis

Hibbertia coriacea subsp.

linearis, commonly known as the linear hibbertia, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its woody stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a rough, textured surface.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers with prominent, showy petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
