Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp. leptotephrum

Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp.

leptotephrum, commonly known as the slender-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of tubular and ray florets.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are elongated and often covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is a perennial herb that can spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in certain regions.
