Himeranthus erosus

Himeranthus erosus, commonly known as the pink morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep red. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces showy blooms that open in the morning. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Himeranthus erosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
