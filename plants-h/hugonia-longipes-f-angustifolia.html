Hugonia longipes f. angustifolia

Hugonia longipes f. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hugonia, is a botanical variant of the species Hugonia longipes.

It is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States, particularly found in wetland habitats. The plant features elongated, narrow leaves that are often submerged or floating, adapting to aquatic environments. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in late summer to early autumn.

The species is characterized by its ability to thrive in marshy conditions, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
