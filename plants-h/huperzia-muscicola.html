Huperzia muscicola

Huperzia muscicola, commonly known as the mossy huperzia, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is a small, evergreen plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rock crevices. The plant has a short, upright stem with clustered, leathery leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of moss-like structures. It reproduces through spores rather than seeds, and its rhizomes help it anchor to rocky substrates.

Huperzia muscicola is valued for its unique morphology and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
