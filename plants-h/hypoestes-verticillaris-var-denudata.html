Hypoestes verticillaris var. denudata

Hypoestes verticillaris var. denudata, commonly known as the naked hypoestes, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which are often mistaken for flowers. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and container planting.

Its compact growth habit and long blooming season make it an attractive addition to both indoor and outdoor landscapes.
