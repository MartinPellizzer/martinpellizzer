Hormathophylla purpurea

Hormathophylla purpurea, commonly known as the purple moss rose, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and vibrant purple to pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has fleshy, succulent leaves that help it retain moisture in arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to produce numerous small, showy blooms throughout the growing season.
