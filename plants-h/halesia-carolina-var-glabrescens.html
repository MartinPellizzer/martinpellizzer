Halesia carolina var. glabrescens

Halesia carolina var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth-flowered southern halesia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and clusters of fragrant, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy, dark green upper surface with a lighter underside. The plant produces small, woody capsules that contain several elliptical seeds.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.
