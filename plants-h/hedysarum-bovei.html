Hedysarum bovei

Hedysarum bovei, commonly known as the Bove's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow or orange flowers in early summer. Hedysarum bovei is part of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with bacteria.

Its ecological significance lies in its role as a forage plant for livestock and its potential in ecological restoration projects.
