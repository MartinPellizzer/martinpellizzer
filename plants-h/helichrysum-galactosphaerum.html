Helichrysum galactosphaerum

Helichrysum galactosphaerum, commonly known as the golden ball everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces small, yellowish-orange flower heads that resemble tiny balls. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils. It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its long-lasting flowers and attractive appearance.

The species is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
