Hedysarum conjugatum

Hedysarum conjugatum, commonly known as the sweet vetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, bean-like pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and along roadsides.

Hedysarum conjugatum is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
