Horsfieldia warburgiana

Horsfieldia warburgiana, commonly known as the Warburg's orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its epiphytic growth habit, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange with intricate patterns. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Horsfieldia warburgiana is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in its natural habitat.
