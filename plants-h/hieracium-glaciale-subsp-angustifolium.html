Hieracium glaciale subsp. angustifolium

Hieracium glaciale subsp.

angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to alpine regions of Europe. It features a low-growing habit with upright, hairy stems that bear several yellow flowers.

The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a wavy margin, and are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes, where it is adapted to cold and harsh conditions.

This subspecies is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some related species.
