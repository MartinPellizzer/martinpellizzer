Hypericum triplinerve

Hypericum triplinerve, commonly known as the three-veined St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a low-growing perennial with woody stems and opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are often reddish underneath. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in late summer, which are followed by orange-red berries. Hypericum triplinerve is known for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.
