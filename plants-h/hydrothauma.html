Hydrothauma

Hydrothauma, commonly known as the water lily, is a genus of aquatic plants that thrives in freshwater environments.

These plants are characterized by their floating leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom above the water surface. The leaves are broad and often have a waxy coating to prevent water from penetrating them. Hydrothauma species play an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Their roots help stabilize the sediment, contributing to water clarity and ecological balance.
