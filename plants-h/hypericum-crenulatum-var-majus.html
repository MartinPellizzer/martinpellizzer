Hypericum crenulatum var. majus

Hypericum crenulatum var. majus, commonly known as the large-stemmed St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, woody stems that are often branched and covered in a dense layer of yellowish-orange glandular dots. The plant produces showy, bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and have a slightly serrated edge.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained, sunny conditions.
