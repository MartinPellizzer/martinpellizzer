Hypericum naudinianum

Hypericum naudinianum, commonly known as the Naudin's St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and woody stems, which are typical of the Hypericaceae family. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
