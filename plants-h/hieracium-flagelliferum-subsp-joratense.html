Hieracium flagelliferum subsp. joratense

Hieracium flagelliferum subsp.

joratense, commonly known as the Jorat hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to reddish flowers arranged in a single, terminal head.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are generally lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy and somewhat glandular.

This subspecies is noted for its distinctive floral morphology and its role in local ecosystems.
