Hieracium lachenalii subsp. aspernatum

Hieracium lachenalii subsp.

aspernatum, commonly known as the aspernatum hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features upright stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are yellow, with ray florets and a central disc, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its finely pubescent surfaces and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.
