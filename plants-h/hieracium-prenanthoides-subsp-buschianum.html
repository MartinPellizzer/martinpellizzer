Hieracium prenanthoides subsp. buschianum

Hieracium prenanthoides subsp.

buschianum, commonly known as the Busch's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
