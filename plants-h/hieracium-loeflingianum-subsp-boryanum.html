Hieracium loeflingianum subsp. boryanum

Hieracium loeflingianum subsp.

boryanum, commonly known as the Boryanum hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow flowers arranged in a composite head.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and its flowers bloom from June to August.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
