Heisteria humilis

Heisteria humilis, commonly known as the dwarf heister, is a low-growing perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, or white. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or container planting.

Heisteria humilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
