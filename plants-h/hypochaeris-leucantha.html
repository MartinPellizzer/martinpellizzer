Hypochaeris leucantha

Hypochaeris leucantha, commonly known as white weed or yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in open areas such as meadows, roadsides, and disturbed soils, and it thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers range from white to yellow in color.

Hypochaeris leucantha is often considered an invasive species in certain regions due to its rapid growth and ability to spread easily.
