Helianthus maximiliani var. asperrimus

Helianthus maximiliani var. asperrimus, commonly known as the rough sunflower, is a perennial plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces large, showy yellow flower heads with dark centers, typical of the sunflower genus. It prefers full sun and well-drained soil, often growing in prairies and open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and native plant gardens.
