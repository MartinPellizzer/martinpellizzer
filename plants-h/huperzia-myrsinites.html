Huperzia myrsinites

Huperzia myrsinites, commonly known as the myrtle huperzia, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage composed of small, ovate to lanceolate leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and rocky crevices, where it thrives in humid conditions. Its spore-producing structures, called sporangia, are clustered on the underside of the leaves, a common feature in ferns.

Huperzia myrsinites is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its lush, compact growth habit.
