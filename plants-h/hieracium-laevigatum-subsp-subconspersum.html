Hieracium laevigatum subsp. subconspersum

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

subconspersum, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are broadly ovate with a rough texture, and the flowers are arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

It is often found in open habitats and can sometimes become invasive in certain regions.
