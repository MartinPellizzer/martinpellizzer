Hieracium schmidtii subsp. acrocrocydotum

Hieracium schmidtii subsp.

acrocrocydotum, commonly known as the Schmidt's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy basal rosette.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its flowering stems are usually unbranched, bearing yellow disk flowers surrounded by pale yellow to orange ray florets.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
