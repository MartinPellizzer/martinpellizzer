Hordeum flexicaule

Hordeum flexicaule, commonly known as slender barley, is a species of grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves, which are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny grains, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
