Hesperocyparis sargentii

Hesperocyparis sargentii, commonly known as Sargent's incense-cedar, is a species of conifer native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its reddish-brown bark and scale-like leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs. The plant produces small, spherical cones that mature to a dark purple color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Hesperocyparis sargentii is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
