Hibiscus racemosus

Hibiscus racemosus, commonly known as the clustered hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a rough texture.

Hibiscus racemosus thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
