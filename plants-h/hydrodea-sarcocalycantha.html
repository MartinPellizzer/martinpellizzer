Hydrodea sarcocalycantha

Hydrodea sarcocalycantha, commonly known as the desert waterplant, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
