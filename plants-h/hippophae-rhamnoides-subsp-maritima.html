Hippophae rhamnoides subsp. maritima

Hippophae rhamnoides subsp.

maritima, commonly known as sea buckthorn, is a shrub native to coastal regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its yellow-orange berries, which are rich in vitamins and antioxidants.

The plant thrives in saline and sandy soils, often found in dunes and coastal areas. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, with a rough texture, and it produces small, yellow flowers in the spring.

The subspecies maritima is particularly adapted to withstand harsh maritime conditions, making it an important plant for coastal stabilization.
