Hieracium murorum subsp. spilophyllum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

spilophyllum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.

The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are arranged in a single, terminal head, and the plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads.
