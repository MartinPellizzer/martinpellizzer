Hemerocallis fulva var. maculata

Hemerocallis fulva var. maculata, commonly known as the spotted daylily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Asia.

It features showy, orange-yellow flowers with distinctive red or brown spots on the outer petals. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that are typically green with a slight reddish tinge. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is known for its vigorous growth and long blooming period.

The variety is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and hardiness.
