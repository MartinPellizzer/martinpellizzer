Haloragis teucrioides var. elata

Haloragis teucrioides var. elata, commonly known as the tall haloragis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features upright, branching stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences, and it produces small, three-lobed fruits.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
