Hidalgoa

Hidalgoa, commonly known as the desert bell, is a genus of flowering plants native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

The plants typically have hairy stems and leaves, adapted to conserve water in harsh environments. Hidalgoa species are often found in rocky soils and can thrive in full sun, making them well-suited for desert landscapes.
