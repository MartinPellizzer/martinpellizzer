Hieracium diaphanoides subsp. subtenellifrons

Hieracium diaphanoides subsp.

subtenellifrons, commonly known as the translucent hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is found in temperate regions of Europe. It has a creeping growth habit with hairy stems and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
