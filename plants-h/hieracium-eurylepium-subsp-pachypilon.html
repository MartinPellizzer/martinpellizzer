Hieracium eurylepium subsp. pachypilon

Hieracium eurylepium subsp.

pachypilon, commonly known as the broad-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its robust growth habit and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically has basal and stem leaves that are lanceolate to ovate in shape, with hairy surfaces. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the genus Hieracium.
