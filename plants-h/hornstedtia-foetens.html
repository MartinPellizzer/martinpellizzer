Hornstedtia foetens

Hornstedtia foetens, commonly known as the stinking bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and somewhat woody.

The species is known for its strong, unpleasant odor, which is most noticeable when the flowers are in full bloom.
