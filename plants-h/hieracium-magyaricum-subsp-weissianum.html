Hieracium magyaricum subsp. weissianum

Hieracium magyaricum subsp.

weissianum, commonly known as the Hungarian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. It has small, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowering period occurs from late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its potential use in horticulture.
