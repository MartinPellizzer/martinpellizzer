Helichrysum ephelos

Helichrysum ephelos, commonly known as the golden marguerite, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has wiry, hairy stems and narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Helichrysum ephelos is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
