Hieracium variicolor var. suborbicans

Hieracium variicolor var. suborbicans, commonly known as the orange hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant features bright orange to yellow flowers with a distinctive, orb-shaped center, which gives it its common name. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides, often forming dense colonies.
