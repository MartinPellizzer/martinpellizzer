Hieracium dispalatum var. macrodon

Hieracium dispalatum var. macrodon, commonly known as the large-headed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are typically lanceolate and covered in fine hairs. Its showy yellow flowers are arranged in a single terminal head, and it produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.
