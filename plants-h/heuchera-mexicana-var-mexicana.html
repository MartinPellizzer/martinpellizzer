Heuchera mexicana var. mexicana

Heuchera mexicana var. mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican alumroot, is a perennial herb native to the high elevations of central Mexico.

It features dense, basal rosettes of heart-shaped, velvety leaves that vary in color from silver to deep red. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple during the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its foliage and early blooming habit.
