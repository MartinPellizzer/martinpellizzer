Heliconia sylvestris

Heliconia sylvestris, commonly known as the red heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to the wetlands and forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, crimson inflorescences that resemble a spiky, fleshy structure known as a "heliconia crown.". The plant belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is known for its large, paddle-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its flowers are pollinated by birds, particularly hummingbirds, which are attracted to its bright colors and nectar.

Heliconia sylvestris is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
