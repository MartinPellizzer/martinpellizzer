Hesperis circinnata

Hesperis circinnata, commonly known as the sweet rocket, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant features bright yellow flowers with four petals arranged in a cross shape, typical of the family. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Hesperis circinnata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and meadows.
