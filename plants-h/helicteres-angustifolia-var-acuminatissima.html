Helicteres angustifolia var. acuminatissima

Helicteres angustifolia var. acuminatissima, commonly known as the narrowleaf arrowroot, is a variant of the arrowroot plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and showy, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces edible tubers similar to those of its parent species, which are used in culinary applications. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

This variant is often cultivated for both ornamental and nutritional purposes.
