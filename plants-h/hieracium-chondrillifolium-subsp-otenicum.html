Hieracium chondrillifolium subsp. otenicum

Hieracium chondrillifolium subsp.

otenicum, commonly known as the Otenic hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. It has a creeping growth habit with rhizomes that allow it to spread vegetatively.

The subspecies is distinguished by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and its ability to thrive in disturbed habitats.
