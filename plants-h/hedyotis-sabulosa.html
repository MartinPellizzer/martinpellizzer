Hedyotis sabulosa

Hedyotis sabulosa, commonly known as sand pennywort, is a small, herbaceous plant native to sandy and coastal regions.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as dunes, roadsides, and sandy soils. The plant has opposite, linear to elliptic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, white flowers appear in clusters and bloom from late spring to early summer.

Hedyotis sabulosa is known for its ability to thrive in harsh, nutrient-poor environments.
