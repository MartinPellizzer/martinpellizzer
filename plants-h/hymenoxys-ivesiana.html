Hymenoxys ivesiana

Hymenoxys ivesiana, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow-orange ray florets and dark purple disk florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

Hymenoxys ivesiana is an annual herb that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking colorful, low-maintenance blooms.
