Huperzia bifida

Huperzia bifida, commonly known as the split-leaf huperzia, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, dichotomous branching pattern and its lanceolate, often lobed leaves. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Huperzia bifida has a robust rhizome system that allows it to thrive in various soil types.

Its spores are released from sporangia that are clustered on the underside of the leaves, contributing to its reproductive strategy.
