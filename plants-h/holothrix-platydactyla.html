Holothrix platydactyla

Holothrix platydactyla, commonly known as the flat-fingered holothurian, is a species of sea cucumber belonging to the family Holothuriidae.

It is characterized by its elongated, flexible body and a unique arrangement of tube feet that aid in locomotion and feeding. This marine invertebrate is typically found in shallow coastal waters, where it burrows into sandy or muddy substrates. Its respiratory tree, a network of branched tubes, allows for efficient gas exchange in its aquatic environment.

The plant plays a role in marine ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and maintaining sediment stability.
