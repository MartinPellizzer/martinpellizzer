Hieracium juranum subsp. juraniforme

Hieracium juranum subsp.

juraniforme, commonly known as the Jura hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to orange flower heads, which are typically surrounded by pale green ray florets.

The plant has basal leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, while the stem is usually hairy and somewhat branched. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along riverbanks.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in several regions.
