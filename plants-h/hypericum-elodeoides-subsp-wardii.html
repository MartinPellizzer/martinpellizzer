Hypericum elodeoides subsp. wardii

Hypericum elodeoides subsp.

wardii, commonly known as the Ward's goldenstar, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its dense clusters of bright yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to aquatic environments. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, often with a slight reddish tinge, and it has a rhizomatous root system that aids in its spread.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
