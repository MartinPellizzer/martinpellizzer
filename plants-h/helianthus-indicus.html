Helianthus indicus

Helianthus indicus, commonly known as the Indian sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the warmer regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and other sunflowers. The plant features showy yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of smaller, tubular flowers. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Helianthus indicus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.
