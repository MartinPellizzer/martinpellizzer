Hierochloe nashii

Hierochloe nashii, commonly known as Nash's hairgrass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan. This perennial grass is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, wiry leaves. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Hierochloe nashii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, grass-like appearance.
