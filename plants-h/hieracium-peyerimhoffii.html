Hieracium peyerimhoffii

Hieracium peyerimhoffii, commonly known as the Peyer's hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and has basal leaves that are lance-shaped with hairy surfaces.

It produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, and its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread in suitable habitats.
