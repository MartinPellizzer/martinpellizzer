Heliconia mathiasiae

Heliconia mathiasiae, commonly known as the Mathias's heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to the lowland rainforests of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its large, colorful bracts that resemble flowers, though they are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that attract various pollinators, including hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are broad and waxy, adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Heliconia mathiasiae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and plays a role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
