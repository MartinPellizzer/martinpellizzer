Hierochloe grandiflora

Hierochloe grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant features slender, upright stems and produces small, blue-green flowers in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish tint.

Hierochloe grandiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
