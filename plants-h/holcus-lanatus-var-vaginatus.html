Holcus lanatus var. vaginatus

Holcus lanatus var. vaginatus, commonly known as the velvet grass, is a variant of the common velvetgrass species.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its dense, tufted growth and soft, hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadside areas, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of spikelets, which are often covered in fine hairs.

This variant is notable for its ecological adaptability and role in supporting local insect populations.
