Hieracium laurinum subsp. subhispidum

Hieracium laurinum subsp.

subhispidum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow-orange composite flowers.

The leaves are typically basal, with a rough, hairy texture, and the stems are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes.

The plant is also notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
