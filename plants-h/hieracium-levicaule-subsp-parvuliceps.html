Hieracium levicaule subsp. parvuliceps

Hieracium levicaule subsp.

parvuliceps, commonly known as the small-headed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow flowers in a single head at the end of each stem. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while the flowers have tubular corollas with five lobes.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and wind-pollinated flowers.
