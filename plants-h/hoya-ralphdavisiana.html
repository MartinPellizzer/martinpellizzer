Hoya ralphdavisiana

Hoya ralphdavisiana, commonly known as the Ralph Davis Hoya, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically green with a slight variegation. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale pink in color. It is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its low maintenance requirements and ability to thrive in low light conditions.

Hoya ralphdavisiana is also known for its slow growth rate and ability to develop aerial roots, which can be used for propagation.
