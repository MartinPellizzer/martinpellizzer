Hieracium caerulaceum

Hieracium caerulaceum, commonly known as blue hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its blue to purple flowers are surrounded by yellow ray florets, and it produces small, oval-shaped achenes as fruits.

Hieracium caerulaceum is often used in ornamental gardening due to its vibrant color and delicate appearance.
