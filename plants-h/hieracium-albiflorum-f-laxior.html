Hieracium albiflorum f. laxior

Hieracium albiflorum f. laxior, commonly known as the white-flowered hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its pale yellow to white flowers, which are typically arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are lance-shaped with serrated edges. Native to Europe, it thrives in meadows and grasslands, often in moist or calcareous soils.

It is a perennial species that produces small, oval seeds dispersed by wind.
