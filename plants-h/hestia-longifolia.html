Hestia longifolia

Hestia longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf hestia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the spring and summer seasons. Hestia longifolia typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to gardens and a subject of interest in horticultural studies.
