Hieracium auropurpureum

Hieracium auropurpureum, commonly known as the golden weed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features basal leaves that are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow to orange in color, with a central disc surrounded by ray florets.

Hieracium auropurpureum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade.
