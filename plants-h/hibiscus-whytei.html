Hibiscus whytei

Hibiscus whytei, commonly known as the Whyte's hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, yellow, or orange. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Hibiscus whytei thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to flourish.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
