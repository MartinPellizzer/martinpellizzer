Haylockia pseudocolchicum

Haylockia pseudocolchicum, commonly known as the purple waxy plant, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its vibrant purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features a bulbous stem covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in its arid native environment. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often mistaken for those of other bulbous plants.

Haylockia pseudocolchicum is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
