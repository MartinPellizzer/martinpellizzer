Hieracium abditum

Hieracium abditum, commonly known as the hidden hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous tiny florets. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy habitats and has dark green, heart-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

Hieracium abditum is known for its ability to thrive in shaded environments, making it a common sight in woodland areas.
