Homalium hypolasium

Homalium hypolasium, commonly known as the low-hanging euphorbia, is a tropical shrub belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite, and usually elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, clustered yellowish-green flowers, which are often surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is native to parts of Southeast Asia.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
