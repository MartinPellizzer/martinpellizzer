Hiraea bahiensis var. paraguariensis

Hiraea bahiensis var. paraguariensis, commonly known as yerba mate, is a species of flowering plant in the family Polygonaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, Paraguay, and Argentina. The plant is characterized by its upright stems and heart-shaped leaves, with small, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters. Yerba mate is well-known for its stimulant properties due to the presence of caffeine and other xanthines.

It is often cultivated for its leaves, which are used to make a traditional infusion consumed in many parts of South America.
