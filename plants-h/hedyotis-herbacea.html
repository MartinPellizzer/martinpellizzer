Hedyotis herbacea

Hedyotis herbacea, commonly known as the prostrate mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats on the ground, often in moist, shaded habitats. The plant features small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and slightly hairy. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other damp environments.

Hedyotis herbacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various pollinators and wildlife.
