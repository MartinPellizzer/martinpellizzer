Hunteria fascicularis

Hunteria fascicularis, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow, pendulous flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a feathery pattern. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for warm climates.

The golden rain tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to provide shade.
