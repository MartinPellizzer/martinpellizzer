Hedychium spanogheanum

Hedychium spanogheanum, commonly known as the golden ginger, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a dramatic and ornamental appearance. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, contributing to its visual appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

Hedychium spanogheanum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical landscapes.
