Hordeum vulgare var. colonicum

Hordeum vulgare var. colonicum, commonly known as wild barley, is a wild variety of barley native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a herbaceous annual plant with slender stems and spike-like inflorescences composed of small, two-lobed spikelets. The plant produces both male and female flowers, with the female florets enclosed in lemmas that are often hairy. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

This species is important in ecological studies due to its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for wildlife.
