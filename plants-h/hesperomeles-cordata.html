Hesperomeles cordata

Hesperomeles cordata, commonly known as the wax apple or wax pear, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, yellow or orange fruits with a waxy coating.

The fruit is often used in culinary applications and has a sweet, tangy flavor, making it a valuable plant in both horticulture and gastronomy.
