Hieracium vogesiacum subsp. benthamianum

Hieracium vogesiacum subsp.

benthamianum, commonly known as the Vogesian hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

Native to central and southern Europe, this subspecies thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant has fibrous roots and is often found in disturbed or marginal environments.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the genus Hieracium.
