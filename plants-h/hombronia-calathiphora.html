Hombronia calathiphora

Hombronia calathiphora, commonly known as the flame flower, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often resembling flames. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green.

Hombronia calathiphora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant due to its exotic appearance.
