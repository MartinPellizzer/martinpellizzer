Hopkirkia hirsuta

Hopkirkia hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy hopkirkia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments. Its unique morphology and habitat make it an interesting subject for botanical study.

Hopkirkia hirsuta is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
