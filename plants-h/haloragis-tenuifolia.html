Haloragis tenuifolia

Haloragis tenuifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved waterwheel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often near water bodies. It belongs to the family Haloragaceae, which includes several species adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic environments.

Haloragis tenuifolia is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in moist conditions.
