Humulus lupulus var. acrocarpus

Humulus lupulus var. acrocarpus, commonly known as the acrocarpus hop.

It is a variant of the hop plant, which is widely used in brewing beer. This variety is characterized by its unique morphology, including elongated cones and a distinct chemical composition. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The plant's botanical features make it an important subject in both agricultural and ecological studies.
