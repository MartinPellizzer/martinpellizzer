Hypericum verticillatum

Hypericum verticillatum, commonly known as the whorled St. John's wort, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear whorled clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant has opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy. It typically blooms from June to August and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils.

Hypericum verticillatum is known for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
