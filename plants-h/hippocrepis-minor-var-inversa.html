Hippocrepis minor var. inversa

Hippocrepis minor var. inversa, commonly known as the inverted bellflower, is a rare and unusual variant of the bellflower genus.

It is characterized by its distinctive inverted flower shape, which sets it apart from typical members of the genus. This plant is native to specific regions in Europe, particularly in the Mediterranean area. It thrives in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is considered a botanical curiosity and of interest to specialists in plant taxonomy and conservation.
