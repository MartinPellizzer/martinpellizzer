Hyptis violacea

Hyptis violacea, commonly known as violet hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
