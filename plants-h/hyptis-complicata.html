Hyptis complicata

Hyptis complicata, commonly known as Mexican mint or hyptis, is a perennial herb native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs such as mint and basil. Hyptis complicata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found in tropical and subtropical regions.
