Halgania tuberculosa

Halgania tuberculosa, commonly known as the African buttercup, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and palmate leaves with a waxy texture. The plant grows in grasslands and open woodlands, often in well-drained soils. It produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals.

Halgania tuberculosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
