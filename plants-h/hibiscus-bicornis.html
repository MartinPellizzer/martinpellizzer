Hibiscus bicornis

Hibiscus bicornis, commonly known as the two-horned hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink or red. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces fruits that are capsule-like in structure. Hibiscus bicornis thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in wetlands or near water bodies.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
