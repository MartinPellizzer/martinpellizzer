Helianthus parviflorus var. attenuatus

Helianthus parviflorus var. attenuatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sunflower, is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellow flowers with a distinctive, elongated shape. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy habitats, preferring moist, sandy or loamy soils. Its botanical characteristics include a branched stem and showy, composite flower heads that attract pollinators.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
