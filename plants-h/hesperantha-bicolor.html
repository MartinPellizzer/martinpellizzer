Hesperantha bicolor

Hesperantha bicolor, commonly known as the rainbow lily, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, brightly colored flowers that display a bicolor pattern, typically featuring a white or pale pink base with a vivid purple or red upper lip. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped blooms that are often fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that are narrow and evergreen, providing an elegant backdrop to its vibrant flowers.

Hesperantha bicolor is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil with moderate sunlight.
