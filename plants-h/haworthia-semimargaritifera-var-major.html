Haworthia semimargaritifera var. major

Haworthia semimargaritifera var. major, commonly known as the Zebra Haworthia, is a compact, rosette-forming succulent native to South Africa.

It features striking, translucent blue-gray leaves with distinctive white to light green tubercles that resemble a zebra's stripes. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for rock gardens or container cultivation.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
