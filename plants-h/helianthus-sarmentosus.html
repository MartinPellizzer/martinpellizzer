Helianthus sarmentosus

Helianthus sarmentosus, commonly known as the creeping sunflower, is a species of flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant produces small, hairy leaves and has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in various soil types. Its flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.

Helianthus sarmentosus is often found in wetlands, along stream banks, and in disturbed areas, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
