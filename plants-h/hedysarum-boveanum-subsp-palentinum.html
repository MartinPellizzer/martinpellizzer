Hedysarum boveanum subsp. palentinum

Hedysarum boveanum subsp.

palentinum, commonly known as the Palentine milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant produces cylindrical pods containing small, round seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, temperate climates. Hedysarum boveanum subsp.

palentinum is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats and is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation.
