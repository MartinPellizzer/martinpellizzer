Helichrysum hypnoides

Helichrysum hypnoides, commonly known as the silverleaf everlasting or African dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments. Its fibrous roots help prevent soil erosion, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The plant's name comes from the Greek words "helios" (sun) and "chrysos" (gold), referring to its sun-like appearance and golden flowers.
