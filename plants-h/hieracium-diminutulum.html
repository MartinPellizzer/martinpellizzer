Hieracium diminutulum

Hieracium diminutulum, commonly known as the small hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer, and its leaves are usually lance-shaped with hairy surfaces.

Hieracium diminutulum is often found in open habitats and is relatively easy to grow in gardens with well-drained soil.
