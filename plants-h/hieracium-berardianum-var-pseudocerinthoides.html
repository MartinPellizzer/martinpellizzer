Hieracium berardianum var. pseudocerinthoides

Hieracium berardianum var. pseudocerinthoides, commonly known as the false cerinthoid hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, producing small, wind-dispersed seeds.

The species is notable for its distinct leaf morphology and its role in local ecosystems.
