Hieracium umbrosum subsp. pseudocrepidineum

Hieracium umbrosum subsp.

pseudocrepidineum, commonly known as the shadowy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow composite flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its narrow, linear leaves and shorter flower stems compared to other members of the species. It thrives in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for ecological studies and botanical gardens.
