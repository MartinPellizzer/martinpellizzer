Helmiopsis rigida

Helmiopsis rigida, commonly known as the rigid helmiopsis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring upright, branching stems covered in fine, grayish hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Helmiopsis rigida is adapted to survive in harsh conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
