Hydrangea hortensis var. rosalba

Hydrangea hortensis var. rosalba, commonly known as the Rosalba hydrangea, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from blue to pink, depending on soil pH. The plant produces white to pale blue flowers in late summer, which gradually fade to a pinkish hue. Its foliage is dark green and remains attractive throughout the growing season.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
