Hoya mindorensis subsp. lagyoensis

Hoya mindorensis subsp.

lagyoensis, commonly known as the Mindanao Waxplant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asclepiadaceae. It is native to the Philippines, specifically the island of Mindanao, where it grows in humid, shaded environments.

The plant is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and waxy, ovate leaves that are often variegated with white or cream markings. Its flowers are clustered in umbels and feature a unique, star-shaped appearance with prominent nectar glands.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
