Heliconia mucilagina

Heliconia mucilagina, commonly known as the sticky heliconia, is a tropical plant belonging to the Heliconiaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, colorful, and often fleshy bract-like leaves that enclose its inflorescence. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically orange or red in color. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in rainforests.

Heliconia mucilagina is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its role in supporting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bats.
