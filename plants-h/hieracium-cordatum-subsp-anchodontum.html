Hieracium cordatum subsp. anchodontum

Hieracium cordatum subsp.

anchodontum, commonly known as the heart-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
