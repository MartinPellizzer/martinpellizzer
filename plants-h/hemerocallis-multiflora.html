Hemerocallis multiflora

Hemerocallis multiflora, commonly known as the many-flowered daylily, is a perennial plant native to Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces multiple flower clusters along its arching stems, which are often fragrant. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

Hemerocallis multiflora is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of climates.
