Harpalyce villosa

Harpalyce villosa, commonly known as the hairy harpalyce, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodlands. The plant features slender, hairy stems and small, trifoliate leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are pale blue to purple and are arranged in dense, terminal clusters.

Harpalyce villosa is noted for its ecological value, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various insects.
