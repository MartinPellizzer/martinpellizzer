Heliconia fernandezii

Heliconia fernandezii, commonly known as the Fernandez's heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its large, colorful bracts that resemble flowers, though they are actually modified leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, red flowers that attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its foliage consists of long, narrow, and often brightly colored leaves, which contribute to its ornamental appeal.

Heliconia fernandezii thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in botanical gardens.
