Hieracium dalicum

Hieracium dalicum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually hairy and lance-shaped, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium dalicum is known for its ability to spread rapidly, sometimes becoming invasive in certain environments.
