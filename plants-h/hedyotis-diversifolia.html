Hedyotis diversifolia

Hedyotis diversifolia, commonly known as the variable dotweed, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a low-growing herb with opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from spring to summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common species in tropical and subtropical regions.
