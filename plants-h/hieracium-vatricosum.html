Hieracium vatricosum

Hieracium vatricosum, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows. Its leaves are rough to the touch and have a distinctive lobed shape, while its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

Hieracium vatricosum is often found in moist soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
