Heracleum tenuevittatum

Heracleum tenuevittatum, commonly known as slender-striated cowparley, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate flowers.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and small white or greenish flowers arranged in umbrella-shaped clusters. Its stem is usually slender and hairy, with distinct, pale green longitudinal stripes.

The species is notable for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
