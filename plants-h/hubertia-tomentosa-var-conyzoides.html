Hubertia tomentosa var. conyzoides

Hubertia tomentosa var. conyzoides, commonly known as the woolly hubertia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique texture and ability to store water make it an attractive and low-maintenance addition to gardens.
