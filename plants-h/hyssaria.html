Hyssaria

Hyssaria, commonly known as hyssop, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its small, tubular flowers are typically blue or purple, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Hyssaria is often used in herbal medicine and as a ornamental plant in gardens.
