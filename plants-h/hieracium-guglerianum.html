Hieracium guglerianum

Hieracium guglerianum, commonly known as the Gugler's hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces numerous yellowish to orange flower heads, which are composed of many small florets arranged in a composite flower structure. It is typically found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils across Europe.

Hieracium guglerianum is a perennial species that can spread vegetatively through its rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
