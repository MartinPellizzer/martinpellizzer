Huperzia darwiniana

Huperzia darwiniana, commonly known as the Darwin's huperzia, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, unbranched stems and lanceolate, serrated leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant is native to various regions in South America, particularly in Brazil, Colombia, and Peru. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Huperzia darwiniana is notable for its unique reproductive structures, including sporangia that are clustered in strobili at the tips of the leaves.
