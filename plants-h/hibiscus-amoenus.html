Hibiscus amoenus

Hibiscus amoenus, commonly known as the yellow hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and often have a reddish hue.

Hibiscus amoenus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
