Hieracium sagittatum subsp. subexpallidum

Hieracium sagittatum subsp.

subexpallidum, commonly known as the slender spearhead hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features slender, upright stems with alternate, lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and are produced in late summer.

This subspecies is characterized by its pale yellow flowers and reduced leaf pubescence compared to other members of the species.
