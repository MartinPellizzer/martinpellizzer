Hieracium vulgatum subsp. austrobavaricum

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

austrobavaricum, commonly known as the Australian hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Australia. It typically grows in open grasslands and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies.

The plant features basal leaves that are lanceolate and hairy, with flowering stems that bear yellow disk flowers surrounded by yellow to orange ray florets. It is characterized by its robust growth habit and ability to thrive in a range of environmental conditions.

The subspecies is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
