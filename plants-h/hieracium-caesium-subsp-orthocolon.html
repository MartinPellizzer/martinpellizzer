Hieracium caesium subsp. orthocolon

Hieracium caesium subsp.

orthocolon, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in summer, are clustered in a flat-topped inflorescence.

The leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive botanical appearance.
