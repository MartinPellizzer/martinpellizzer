Hieracium porphyriticum

Hieracium porphyriticum, commonly known as the purple hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, reddish stems and clustered purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring well-drained conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while the flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

Hieracium porphyriticum is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
