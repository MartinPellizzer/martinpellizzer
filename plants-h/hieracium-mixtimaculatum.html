Hieracium mixtimaculatum

Hieracium mixtimaculatum, commonly known as the spotted hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of tubular and ray florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are basal, alternate, and often have a rough texture with hairy surfaces.

The showy yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
