Horsfieldia macilenta

Horsfieldia macilenta, commonly known as the slender horsfieldia, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, pendulous flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its leaves are elongated and leathery, adapted to thrive in the humid, shaded environments of its natural habitat.

Horsfieldia macilenta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical botanical gardens.
