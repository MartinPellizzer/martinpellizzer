Hieracium tommasinii subsp. setosissimum

Hieracium tommasinii subsp.

setosissimum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering stems that bear yellow composite flowers.

The plant has rough, hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and other open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
