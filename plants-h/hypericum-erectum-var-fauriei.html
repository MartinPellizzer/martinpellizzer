Hypericum erectum var. fauriei

Hypericum erectum var. fauriei, commonly known as the upright St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, woody stems that bear opposite, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in late summer, which are characteristic of the Hypericaceae family. Its flowers have five petals and numerous stamens, contributing to its ornamental value.

It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often used in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
