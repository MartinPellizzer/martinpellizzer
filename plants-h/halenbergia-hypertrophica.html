Halenbergia hypertrophica

Halenbergia hypertrophica, commonly known as the giant halenbergia, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its large, waxy leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length, and its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from violet to red. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and somewhat challenging species to grow outside its native range.
