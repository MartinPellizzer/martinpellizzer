Hieracium borsanum

Hieracium borsanum, commonly known as the Borsano's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a single head, surrounded by bracts, and bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Hieracium borsanum is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed environments and is often found along roadsides and in meadows.
