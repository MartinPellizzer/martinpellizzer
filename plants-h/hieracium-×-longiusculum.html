Hieracium × longiusculum

Hieracium × longiusculum, commonly known as the long-leaved hawkweed, is a hybrid species within the genus Hieracium, which belongs to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its elongated, grass-like leaves and yellow composite flowers that consist of both ray and disk florets. This plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks, preferring temperate climates. It is often found in regions of Europe and western Asia, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a range of morphological variations, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
