Hieracium murorum subsp. subsilvularum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

subsilvularum, commonly known as the forest dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

The plant has heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. It is characterized by its robust root system and ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

This subspecies is often found in association with other woodland plants, contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
