Higginsia discolor

Higginsia discolor, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round, red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Higginsia discolor thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
