Hevea guianensis var. cuneata

Hevea guianensis var. cuneata, commonly known as the Guianan rubber tree, is a species native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and produces latex, which is used in the rubber industry. The plant typically grows as a tall tree, reaching heights of up to 30 meters. Its bark is smooth and grayish in color, and it thrives in humid, tropical climates.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting diverse rainforest biodiversity.
