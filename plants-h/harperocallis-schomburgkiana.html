Harperocallis schomburgkiana

Harperocallis schomburgkiana, commonly known as the Schomburgk's golden lily, is a rare and striking flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its elegant, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow to orange, often with a contrasting purple or red throat. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and is of interest to horticulturists and plant enthusiasts.
