Hypericum hedgei

Hypericum hedgei, commonly known as the creeping St. John's wort, is a low-growing shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, yellow, four-petaled flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has woody stems and dark green, oval-shaped leaves. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in rocky or dry soils.

Hypericum hedgei is also valued for its medicinal properties, though it contains compounds that can be toxic if ingested.
