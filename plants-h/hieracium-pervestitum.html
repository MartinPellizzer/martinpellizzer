Hieracium pervestitum

Hieracium pervestitum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped with rough textures, and its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Hieracium pervestitum is often considered a weed due to its aggressive growth and ability to colonize various habitats.
