Hieracium murorum subsp. cirritoidiforme

Hieracium murorum subsp.

cirritoidiforme, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow composite flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its elongated, narrow leaves and a flowering period that typically occurs in late spring to early summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and other open habitats.

This plant is often considered a weed but plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
