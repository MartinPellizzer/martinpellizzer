Hakea deflexa

Hakea deflexa, commonly known as the drooping hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical flower spikes that typically bloom in spring. The plant has woody stems and produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by distinctive, woody, urn-shaped seed pods. Its leaves are usually narrow and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems.

Hakea deflexa is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in coastal and sandy soils.
