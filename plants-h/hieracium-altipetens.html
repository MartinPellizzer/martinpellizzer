Hieracium altipetens

Hieracium altipetens, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats at high elevations, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette, and are usually covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are yellow, with a distinctive central disc surrounded by petal-like bracts, contributing to its ornamental appearance.
