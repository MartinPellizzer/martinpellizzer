Heptacodium miconioides subsp. jasminoides

Heptacodium miconioides subsp.

jasminoides, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its clusters of small, dark purple fruits that appear in the fall.

The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that turn red in autumn, adding visual interest. It produces fragrant, white flowers in late spring, which are often mistaken for jasmine.

This species is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
