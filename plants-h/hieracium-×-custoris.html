Hieracium × custoris

Hieracium × custoris, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering stems that bear yellow composite flowers. The plant is native to Europe and has been introduced to other regions, often becoming invasive in some areas. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and wastelands.

Its botanical features include rough, hairy leaves and a fibrous root system that aids in its spread.
