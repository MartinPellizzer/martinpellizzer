Helicia brevipetiolata

Helicia brevipetiolata, commonly known as the short-stemmed helicia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Theaceae and is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by spherical, woody fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
