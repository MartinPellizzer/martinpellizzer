Haplopappus integrifolius

Haplopappus integrifolius, commonly known as the smooth pappus plant, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typical sunflower-like structure. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and entire, giving it its scientific name. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Haplopappus integrifolius is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
