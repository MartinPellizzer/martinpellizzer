Haplopappus bloomeri var. angustatus

Haplopappus bloomeri var. angustatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf haplopappus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to dry conditions such as reduced leaf size and deep root systems. Its small, yellowish flowers bloom in summer, and the plant produces fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.

It is often found in rocky soils and is considered a species of conservation concern due to habitat loss.
