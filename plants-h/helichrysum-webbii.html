Helichrysum webbii

Helichrysum webbii, commonly known as the golden marguerite, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that resemble miniature sunflowers. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces numerous small, seed-filled bracts that persist after flowering. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Helichrysum webbii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
