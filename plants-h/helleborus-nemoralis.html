Helleborus nemoralis

Helleborus nemoralis, commonly known as the white hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Ranunculaceae and is characterized by its evergreen, leathery leaves and showy, waxy flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist woodland habitats and has a rhizomatous root system. Its flowers are usually white or pale green, with five to seven petals and numerous stamens, and it is known for its toxic properties due to the presence of cardiac glycosides.

Helleborus nemoralis is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and historical medicinal uses.
