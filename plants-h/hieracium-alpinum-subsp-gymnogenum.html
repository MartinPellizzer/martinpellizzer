Hieracium alpinum subsp. gymnogenum

Hieracium alpinum subsp.

gymnogenum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces yellow flowers in clusters.

The plant has finely divided, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the dandelion family, Asteraceae.
