Hieracium subflexicaule

Hieracium subflexicaule, commonly known as the bent-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

It is often found in association with other plants in open habitats, contributing to local biodiversity.
