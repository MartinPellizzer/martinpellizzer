Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp. herculanum

Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp.

herculanum, commonly known as the Herculanum hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has a taproot system and produces achenes as its seed type.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
