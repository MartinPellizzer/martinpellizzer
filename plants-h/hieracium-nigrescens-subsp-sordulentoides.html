Hieracium nigrescens subsp. sordulentoides

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

sordulentoides, commonly known as the blackened hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a dark center.

The subspecies is distinguished by its darker pigmentation and slightly different morphology compared to other members of the Hieracium genus.
