Hieracium wettsteinianum subsp. atroviolascentifrons

Hieracium wettsteinianum subsp.

atroviolascentifrons, commonly known as the black-veined hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that have distinctive dark veins. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species within its range.
