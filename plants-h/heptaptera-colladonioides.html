Heptaptera colladonioides

Heptaptera colladonioides, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to the island of Socotra.

It is characterized by its distinctive coral-like inflorescences, which are composed of numerous small, brightly colored flowers. The plant has a woody stem with a branching pattern that resembles a coral structure, giving it its common name. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Heptaptera colladonioides is notable for its unique floral morphology and is considered a key species in the flora of Socotra.
