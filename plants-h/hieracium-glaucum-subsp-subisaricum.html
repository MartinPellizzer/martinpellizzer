Hieracium glaucum subsp. subisaricum

Hieracium glaucum subsp.

subisaricum, commonly known as the glaucous hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. It has a creeping growth habit with rhizomes, allowing it to spread vegetatively.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural and horticultural settings.
