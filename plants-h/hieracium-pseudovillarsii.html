Hieracium pseudovillarsii

Hieracium pseudovillarsii, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky soils, preferring cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central dark center.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats and is of interest to botanists studying alpine flora.
