Hemizonia parryi

Hemizonia parryi, commonly known as Parry's hemizonia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Hemizonia parryi produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
