Hystrix bigeloviana

Hystrix bigeloviana, commonly known as the bigelovian hystrix, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of many small florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss.

Hystrix bigeloviana produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting various pollinators.
