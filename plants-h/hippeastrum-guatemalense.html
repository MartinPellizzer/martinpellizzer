Hippeastrum guatemalense

Hippeastrum guatemalense, commonly known as the Guatemalan amaryllis, is a bulbous plant native to Central America.

It produces striking, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in the spring or early summer. The plant has a thick, fleshy stem that stores water and nutrients, aiding its survival in dry conditions. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, arranged in a rosette pattern around the base of the plant.

Hippeastrum guatemalense is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
