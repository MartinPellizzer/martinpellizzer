Hieracium laevigatum subsp. chaubardianum

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

chaubardianum, commonly known as the Chaubard's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets.

The plant typically features a basal rosette of leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. Its yellow flowers appear in clusters and bloom from late spring to early summer.

It is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and waste areas, and is considered a weed in some regions.
