Hesperis laciniata subsp. spectabilis

Hesperis laciniata subsp.

spectabilis, commonly known as the showy lady's mantle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with distinctive cruciform flowers.

The plant features basal leaves that are deeply lobed and hairy, and it produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
