Hieracium leiocephalum subsp. leiocephalum

Hieracium leiocephalum subsp.

leiocephalum, commonly known as the smoothhead hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, often forming dense mats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in many temperate regions.
