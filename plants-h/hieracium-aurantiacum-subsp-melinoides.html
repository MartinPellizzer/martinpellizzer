Hieracium aurantiacum subsp. melinoides

Hieracium aurantiacum subsp.

melinoides, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, typically arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

The plant has a robust root system and hairy stems, which help it thrive in disturbed soils and open habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other sunny, well-drained areas.
