Hyptis pectinata

Hyptis pectinata, commonly known as the white hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

The flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
