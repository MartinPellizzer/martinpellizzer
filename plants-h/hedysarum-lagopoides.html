Hedysarum lagopoides

Hedysarum lagopoides, commonly known as the goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers arranged in dense, feathery clusters, which are characteristic of the genus Hedysarum. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are often hairy and lobed.

Hedysarum lagopoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive floral display.
