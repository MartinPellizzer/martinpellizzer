Hierochloe occidentalis

Hierochloe occidentalis, commonly known as western silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
