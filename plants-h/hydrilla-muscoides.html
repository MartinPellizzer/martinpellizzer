Hydrilla muscoides

Hydrilla muscoides, commonly known as the musk hydrilla, is a submerged aquatic plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its branching, ribbon-like leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often submerged, and it can form dense mats in freshwater environments. Hydrilla muscoides is known for its rapid growth rate, which can lead to significant ecological impacts in ponds and lakes.

It is often considered an invasive species in regions outside its native range.
