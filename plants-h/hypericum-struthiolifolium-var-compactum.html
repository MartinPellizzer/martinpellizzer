Hypericum struthiolifolium var. compactum

Hypericum struthiolifolium var. compactum, commonly known as the compact golden St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a compact shrub with upright, branched stems and small, dark green leaves. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its medicinal properties, like those of other Hypericum species, have been historically noted for their potential therapeutic uses.
