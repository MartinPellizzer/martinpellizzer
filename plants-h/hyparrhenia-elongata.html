Hyparrhenia elongata

Hyparrhenia elongata, commonly known as the narrowleaf horse-tail grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a dense, tufted growth pattern. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands, grasslands, and disturbed areas.

Hyparrhenia elongata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature and ability to tolerate various environmental conditions.
