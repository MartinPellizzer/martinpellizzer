Hieracium hyperadenium

Hieracium hyperadenium, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant produces yellow ray flowers and a central disk of tubular flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.

Hieracium hyperadenium is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes, making it both a common weed and a resilient wildflower.
