Hopea martinicensis

Hopea martinicensis, commonly known as the Martinique ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, dark brown heartwood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall, with a broad, spreading canopy and elliptical leaves that are glossy on top and pale underneath. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces woody fruits that contain one or two seeds.

Hopea martinicensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
