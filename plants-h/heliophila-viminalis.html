Heliophila viminalis

Heliophila viminalis, commonly known as water mint, is a herbaceous plant native to moist environments in Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in wet meadows, stream banks, and marshy areas, thriving in waterlogged soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and emit a strong minty fragrance when crushed.

Heliophila viminalis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported calming and digestive properties.
