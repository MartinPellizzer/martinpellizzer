Hibiscus aethiopicus var. ovatus

Hibiscus aethiopicus var. ovatus, commonly known as the woolly hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, velvety leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Hibiscus aethiopicus var. ovatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in warm climates.
