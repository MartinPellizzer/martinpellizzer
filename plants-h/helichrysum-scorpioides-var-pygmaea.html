Helichrysum scorpioides var. pygmaea

Helichrysum scorpioides var. pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf golden weed, is a small, herbaceous perennial native to South Africa.

It features delicate, yellow-orange flowers that resemble tiny sunflowers, often appearing in clusters. The plant has wiry, branched stems and narrow, linear leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its drought tolerance and long-lasting blooms make it a valuable addition to xeriscaped gardens.
