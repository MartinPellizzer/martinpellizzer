Heterotoma flexuosa

Heterotoma flexuosa, commonly known as the wavy lily, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Liliaceae and is characterized by its slender, often curling stems and showy, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a wavy or undulating edge, contributing to its common name.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
