Haworthia reinwardtii var. pulchra

Haworthia reinwardtii var. pulchra, commonly known as the "Cape tulip," is a compact, rosette-forming succulent native to South Africa.

It features dense, fleshy leaves that are typically a pale green to bluish-gray, with white or pale markings. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with small, white flowers during the summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy leaves.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for indoor and rock garden cultivation.
