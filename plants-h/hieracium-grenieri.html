Hieracium grenieri

Hieracium grenieri, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky soils, thriving in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

Hieracium grenieri is often found in high-altitude environments where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
