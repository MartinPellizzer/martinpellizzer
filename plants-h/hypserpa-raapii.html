Hypserpa raapii

Hypserpa raapii, commonly known as the Red-spotted Orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red petals with distinctive black spots, which help in attracting pollinators. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories where it can receive indirect sunlight. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil, and it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, Hypserpa raapii is considered endangered and is protected in many countries.
