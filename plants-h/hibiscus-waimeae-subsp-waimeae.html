Hibiscus waimeae subsp. waimeae

Hibiscus waimeae subsp.

waimeae, commonly known as the Waimea hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands. It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red.

The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to the humid, tropical climate of Hawaii. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This subspecies is of particular interest to botanists due to its unique ecological role and limited distribution.
