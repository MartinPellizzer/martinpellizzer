Hordeum vulgare var. hangaicum

Hordeum vulgare var. hangaicum, commonly known as the Hangai barley, is a variant of barley native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and resilience to harsh environmental conditions. The plant typically produces small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences, with each spikelet containing two to three rows of grains. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and potential use in both traditional and modern agricultural systems.
