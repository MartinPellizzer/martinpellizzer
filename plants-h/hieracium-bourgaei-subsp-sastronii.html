Hieracium bourgaei subsp. sastronii

Hieracium bourgaei subsp.

sastronii, commonly known as the Sastronii hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are usually alternate, rough, and covered with fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
