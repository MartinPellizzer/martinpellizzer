Helicteres barbadensis

Helicteres barbadensis, commonly known as the sweet sarsaparilla, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically divided into several leaflets, and its clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces distinctive, spiny fruits that contain seeds, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
