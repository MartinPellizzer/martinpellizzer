Hieracium sclerophaeum

Hieracium sclerophaeum, commonly known as the rigid hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and foliage with serrated edges. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in clusters.

Hieracium sclerophaeum is known for its robust growth and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
