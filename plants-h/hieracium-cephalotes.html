Hieracium cephalotes

Hieracium cephalotes, commonly known as the hairy spearthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the dandelion family (Compositae).

It is characterized by its spiny, hairy stems and yellow composite flowers that resemble dandelions. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky areas. It has lance-shaped leaves with coarse teeth and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Hieracium cephalotes is native to Europe and parts of Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates.
