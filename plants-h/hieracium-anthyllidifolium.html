Hieracium anthyllidifolium

Hieracium anthyllidifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. It features narrow, elongated leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
