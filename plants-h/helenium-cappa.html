Helenium cappa

Helenium cappa, commonly known as the yellow helenium, is a flowering plant native to the United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, orange to yellow ray florets. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stem is erect and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

Helenium cappa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
