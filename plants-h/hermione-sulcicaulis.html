Hermione sulcicaulis

Hermione sulcicaulis, commonly known as the zigzag orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental value make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
