Hypericum glaucum

Hypericum glaucum, commonly known as the whitish St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, whitish bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Hypericum glaucum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
