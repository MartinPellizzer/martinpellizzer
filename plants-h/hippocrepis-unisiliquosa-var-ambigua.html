Hippocrepis unisiliquosa var. ambigua

Hippocrepis unisiliquosa var. ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous single-seed hawthorn, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is found in Mediterranean climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slight pubescence.

The species is notable for its limited distribution and is considered a rare and specialized plant.
