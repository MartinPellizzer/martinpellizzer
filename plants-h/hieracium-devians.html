Hieracium devians

Hieracium devians, commonly known as the devil's bits, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its clustered, yellowish to reddish-orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped with a rough texture and may have a slight reddish tinge.

Hieracium devians is a perennial species that can spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it somewhat invasive in certain habitats.
