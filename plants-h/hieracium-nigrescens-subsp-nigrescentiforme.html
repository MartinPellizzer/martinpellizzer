Hieracium nigrescens subsp. nigrescentiforme

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

nigrescentiforme, commonly known as the blackening hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its dark green, hairy leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in the summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its tendency for the leaves and stems to darken as they mature, giving it its common name. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

This plant is often found in temperate regions of Europe and is noted for its ornamental value and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
