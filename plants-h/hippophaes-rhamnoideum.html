Hippophaes rhamnoideum

Hippophaes rhamnoideum, commonly known as the sea buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to temperate regions of Asia and Europe.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange berries, which are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. The plant has small, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found near coastal areas.

Its ecological importance includes soil stabilization and providing food for wildlife.
