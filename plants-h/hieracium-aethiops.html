Hieracium aethiops

Hieracium aethiops, commonly known as Ethiopian hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and it produces yellow flowers in clusters. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Hieracium aethiops is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain regions.
