Hypoxis oblonga

Hypoxis oblonga, commonly known as the African potato or yellow star grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellow, star-shaped flowers during the summer months. The plant has a tuberous root system that stores nutrients, contributing to its medicinal use in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Hypoxis oblonga is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
