Hyalea leuzeoides

Hyalea leuzeoides, commonly known as the silky rock heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen shrub form with dense, leathery foliage. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is cultivated in gardens in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
