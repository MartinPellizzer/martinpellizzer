Hosackia pinnata

Hosackia pinnata, commonly known as the narrowleaf false indigo, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves with a pinnate structure. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and along stream banks.

Hosackia pinnata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for ecological restoration projects.
