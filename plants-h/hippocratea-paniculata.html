Hippocratea paniculata

Hippocratea paniculata, commonly known as the panicle orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, branching inflorescence that bears numerous small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination by insects.

Hippocratea paniculata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
