Hulsea carnosa

Hulsea carnosa, commonly known as the woolly hulsea, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly white or grayish hairs covering its stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, such as slopes and open woodlands.

Hulsea carnosa is often found in USDA hardiness zones 4 through 8, thriving in well-drained soils.
