Hedycaryopsis madagascariensis

Hedycaryopsis madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar nutmeg, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen shrub form. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by woody fruits containing aromatic seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough texture, while its bark is thick and fissured.

The plant is valued for its unique fragrance and has traditional uses in local medicine and as a spice.
