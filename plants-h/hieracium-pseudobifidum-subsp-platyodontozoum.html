Hieracium pseudobifidum subsp. platyodontozoum

Hieracium pseudobifidum subsp.

platyodontozoum, commonly known as the broad-lobed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering heads.

The plant produces yellow flowers that are typically arranged in a single inflorescence at the end of each stem. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a rough texture, while the stems are hairy and often reddish in color.

This subspecies is often found in disturbed soils and can form dense colonies in open areas.
