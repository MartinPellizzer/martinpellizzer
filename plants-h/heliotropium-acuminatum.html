Heliotropium acuminatum

Heliotropium acuminatum, commonly known as the velvetleaf or sweet William, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit and hairy, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple, often resembling those of the true heliotropes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Heliotropium acuminatum is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional remedies.
