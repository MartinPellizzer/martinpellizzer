Hypochaeris aurantiaca

Hypochaeris aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden weed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas. It produces yellow flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn, and its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
