Hieracium pilosella subsp. dasycephaloides

Hieracium pilosella subsp.

dasycephaloides, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands.

The plant features a rosette of basal leaves covered in dense, white hairs, giving it a woolly appearance. Its flowering heads are composed of numerous small yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
