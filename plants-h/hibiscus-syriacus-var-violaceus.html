Hibiscus syriacus var. violaceus

Hibiscus syriacus var. violaceus, commonly known as the purple rose of Sharon, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from pale lavender to deep purple, blooming in late summer. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves that provide a striking contrast to its vibrant flowers. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

The botanical name highlights its classification within the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton plants.
