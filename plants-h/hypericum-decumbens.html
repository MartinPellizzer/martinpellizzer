Hypericum decumbens

Hypericum decumbens, commonly known as creeping St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to North America.

It features prostrate stems that spread along the ground, often rooting at the nodes to form dense mats. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Hypericum decumbens is often found in sandy or rocky soils in open areas such as meadows and woodland edges.
