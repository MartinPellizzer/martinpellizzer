Hypoestes depauperata

Hypoestes depauperata, commonly known as the "Snowflake" plant, is a flowering species native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, colorful bracts that resemble small flowers. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of red, yellow, and orange, which are surrounded by showy, petal-like bracts. It thrives in well-draining soil and prefers bright, indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Its unique appearance and ease of care contribute to its popularity among gardeners and plant enthusiasts.
