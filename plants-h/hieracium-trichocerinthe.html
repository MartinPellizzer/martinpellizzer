Hieracium trichocerinthe

Hieracium trichocerinthe, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and yellowish-orange composite flowers that consist of both ray and disk florets. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal, aiding in its spread across various habitats.

The plant's botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and botanical gardens.
