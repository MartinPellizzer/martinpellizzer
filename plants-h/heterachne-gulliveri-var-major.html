Heterachne gulliveri var. major

Heterachne gulliveri var. major, commonly known as the giant marsh grass, is a variant of the species Heterachne gulliveri, which belongs to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, robust stalks and elongated, narrow leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. This plant thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and swamps, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms. Its flowering structures consist of spikelets that produce small, inconspicuous flowers, typical of many grasses.

The plant is adapted to tolerate waterlogged conditions, making it an important component of aquatic and semi-aquatic ecosystems.
