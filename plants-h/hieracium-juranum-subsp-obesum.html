Hieracium juranum subsp. obesum

Hieracium juranum subsp.

obesum, commonly known as the Fat Hen's Eye, is a perennial herb native to Central Europe. It typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are broadly ovate with wavy margins and a hairy surface. Its flowering stems bear yellow composite flowers, which are made up of numerous small florets arranged in a disc.

This species is often found in open, sunny habitats and is characteristic of calcareous grasslands.
