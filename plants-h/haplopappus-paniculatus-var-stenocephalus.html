Haplopappus paniculatus var. stenocephalus

Haplopappus paniculatus var. stenocephalus, commonly known as the narrow-headed eupatorium, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features slender, branched stems with opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowering heads are small and clustered, producing pale yellow to white florets.

The plant is characterized by its reduced pappus, which is a feature typical of the genus Haplopappus.
