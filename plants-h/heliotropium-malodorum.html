Heliotropium malodorum

Heliotropium malodorum, commonly known as the stinking heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from purple to pink, and its distinctive, often pungent odor. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in saline environments. Its leaves are usually opposite and have a rough, hairy texture.

Heliotropium malodorum is also known for its ability to tolerate drought and salt spray, making it a hardy species in coastal ecosystems.
