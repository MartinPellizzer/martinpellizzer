Hieracium paraguayense subvar. glabrescens

Hieracium paraguayense subvar. glabrescens, commonly known as the Paraguay hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It features a hairy stem that becomes glabrous with age, and its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its potential use in horticulture.
