Heliconia schumanniana

Heliconia schumanniana, commonly known as the Schumann's heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its inflorescence consists of a long, spiral arrangement of brightly colored bracts that enclose the actual small, clustered flowers.

Heliconia schumanniana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates.
