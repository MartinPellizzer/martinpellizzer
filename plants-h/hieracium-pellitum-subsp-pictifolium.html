Hieracium pellitum subsp. pictifolium

Hieracium pellitum subsp.

pictifolium, commonly known as the painted hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and distinctive, often intricately patterned leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is found in various parts of Europe. It has a rosette of basal leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
