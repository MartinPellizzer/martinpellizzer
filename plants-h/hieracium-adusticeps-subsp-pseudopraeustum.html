Hieracium adusticeps subsp. pseudopraeustum

Hieracium adusticeps subsp.

pseudopraeustum, commonly known as the dry-haired hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and yellow composite flowers that consist of both ray and disk florets.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered with fine, glandular hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This subspecies is native to Europe and is often found in grassy habitats and disturbed soils.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain insect species.
