Hydrogaster

Hydrogaster, commonly known as the water bladder, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its spongy, balloon-like structure.

It thrives in shallow, still waters where it can absorb nutrients from the surrounding environment. The plant's porous surface allows it to retain water, making it well adapted to submerged conditions. It lacks true roots and instead relies on its expanded stem for buoyancy and nutrient uptake.

Hydrogaster plays a role in stabilizing sediments and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
