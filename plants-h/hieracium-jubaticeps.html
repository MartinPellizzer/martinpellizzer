Hieracium jubaticeps

Hieracium jubaticeps, commonly known as the benthead hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and basal rosettes of narrow, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is often found in meadows and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
