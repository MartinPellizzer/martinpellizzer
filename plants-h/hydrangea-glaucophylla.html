Hydrangea glaucophylla

Hydrangea glaucophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from white to pink and blue, depending on soil pH. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery-white fuzz, giving it its common name. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Hydrangea glaucophylla is also valued for its ability to provide seasonal interest with its changing flower colors and attractive foliage.
