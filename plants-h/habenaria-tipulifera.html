Habenaria tipulifera

Habenaria tipulifera, commonly known as the mosquito orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom during the rainy season. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are linear and often covered in a fine, hairy texture.

The orchid is notable for its ability to thrive in humid conditions and its unique floral structure that resembles the appearance of a mosquito.
