Hemipilia trifurcata

Hemipilia trifurcata, commonly known as the three-branched orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette.

Hemipilia trifurcata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
