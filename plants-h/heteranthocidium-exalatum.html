Heteranthocidium exalatum

Heteranthocidium exalatum, commonly known as the flame violet, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange and yellow. The plant typically features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Its unique floral structure and vibrant colors make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
