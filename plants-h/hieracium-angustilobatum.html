Hieracium angustilobatum

Hieracium angustilobatum, commonly known as narrow-lobed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads made up of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands, and it has a rosette of basal leaves that are narrow and lobed. Its flowering stems bear yellow disk flowers surrounded by pale green to white ray florets.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed soils, making it an invasive species in some regions.
