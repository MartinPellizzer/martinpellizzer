Helianthus orgyalis

Helianthus orgyalis, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in dry, sunny environments. The plant produces vibrant orange to red flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

Helianthus orgyalis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
