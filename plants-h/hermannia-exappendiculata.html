Hermannia exappendiculata

Hermannia exappendiculata, commonly known as the Cape rose or red-stemmed catchfly, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and clusters of small, pink or red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces distinctive, inflated seed pods that split open to release seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Hermannia exappendiculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
