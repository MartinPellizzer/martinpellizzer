Homalocenchrus lenticularis

Homalocenchrus lenticularis, commonly known as the lens-shaped grasshopper beetle, is a species of beetle belonging to the family Chrysomelidae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flattened, and often brightly colored body, which is adapted for life among grasses and other herbaceous plants. The beetle's elongated, lens-shaped pronotum gives it its common name and aids in camouflage within its natural habitat. It is primarily found in arid and semi-arid regions, where it feeds on various grasses and leaves.

Due to its specific ecological niche, it plays a role in the ecological balance of its habitat by contributing to nutrient cycling through its feeding habits.
