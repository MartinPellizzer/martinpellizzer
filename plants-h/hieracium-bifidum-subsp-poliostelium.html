Hieracium bifidum subsp. poliostelium

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

poliostelium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically has narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy, and it produces small, clustered flower heads. It thrives in grassy areas and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
