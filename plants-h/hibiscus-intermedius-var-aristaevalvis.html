Hibiscus intermedius var. aristaevalvis

Hibiscus intermedius var. aristaevalvis, commonly known as the African sand hibiscus, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features large, showy flowers with five deeply notched petals that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in opposite pairs along the stem. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

The flowers bloom throughout the growing season, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
