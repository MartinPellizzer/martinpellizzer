Hieracium fritzei subsp. uechtritzianum

Hieracium fritzei subsp.

uechtritzianum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Central Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow flower heads.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. It has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces numerous ray florets.

Due to its restricted habitat, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
