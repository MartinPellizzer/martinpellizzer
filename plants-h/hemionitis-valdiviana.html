Hemionitis valdiviana

Hemionitis valdiviana, commonly known as the Valdivian fern, is a rare and ancient plant species native to the temperate rainforests of southern South America.

It is a small, evergreen fern characterized by its delicate, leathery fronds and a unique, compact growth habit. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests where humidity is high. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of its fronds, typical of many fern species.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Hemionitis valdiviana is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
