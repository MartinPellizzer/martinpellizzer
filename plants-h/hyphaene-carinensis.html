Hyphaene carinensis

Hyphaene carinensis, commonly known as the African palm or the false date palm, is a member of the Arecaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that develop into edible, sweet fruits resembling dates. Its roots form a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.

Hyphaene carinensis is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of food and fiber.
