Hypolepis rugosula subsp. formosa

Hypolepis rugosula subsp.

formosa, commonly known as the Formosa shield fern, is a fern species native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and pinnately compound fronds with a distinctive, rugged texture.

The fronds are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, with serrated margins. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
