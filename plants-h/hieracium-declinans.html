Hieracium declinans

Hieracium declinans, commonly known as the drooping hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

Hieracium declinans is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it both a common weed and a resilient wildflower.
