Homalomena portae-inferni

Homalomena portae-inferni, commonly known as the Hell's Gate arum, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence composed of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves with a deep green color and prominent veins. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically surrounded by a showy, petal-like structure.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a somewhat ominous common name due to its association with the underworld in some mythologies.
