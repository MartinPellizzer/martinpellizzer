Helichrysum gnaphaloides

Helichrysum gnaphaloides, commonly known as the Cape goldfluff, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, fluffy yellow or golden inflorescences. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically grouped together in a spherical or ovoid shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.
