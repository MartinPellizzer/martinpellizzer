Hieracium lasiophyllum var. planifolium

Hieracium lasiophyllum var. planifolium, commonly known as the flat-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features basal rosettes of broad, spoon-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. It produces yellow flowers in summer, which are followed by small, dry fruits called achenes.

This species is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
