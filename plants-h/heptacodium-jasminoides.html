Heptacodium jasminoides

Heptacodium jasminoides, commonly known as the fragrant bluemouth, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive, clustered, bell-shaped flowers that range from white to pink, which appear in late summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that turn red in the fall, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Heptacodium jasminoides is valued in horticulture for its unique foliage and fragrant blooms.
