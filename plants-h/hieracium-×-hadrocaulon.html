Hieracium × hadrocaulon

Hieracium × hadrocaulon, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces yellow flowers arranged in dense, flat-topped clusters. The plant typically has basal leaves that are heart-shaped and hairy, while the upper leaves are narrower and often have a reddish or purplish hue. It thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Due to its aggressive growth, it can become invasive in certain regions.
