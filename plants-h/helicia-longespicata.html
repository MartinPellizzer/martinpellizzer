Helicia longespicata

Helicia longespicata, commonly known as the long-spined helenia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Theaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Helicia longespicata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a specimen plant due to its attractive appearance and adaptability.
