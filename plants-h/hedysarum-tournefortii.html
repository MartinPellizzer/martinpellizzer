Hedysarum tournefortii

Hedysarum tournefortii, commonly known as the Turkish clover, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas.

Hedysarum tournefortii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
