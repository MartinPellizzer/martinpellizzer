Holothrix rupicola

Holothrix rupicola, commonly known as the rock holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its ability to store water in its thick leaves allows it to survive in arid conditions.

Holothrix rupicola is often cultivated for its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
