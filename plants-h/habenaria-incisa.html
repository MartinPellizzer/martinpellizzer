Habenaria incisa

Habenaria incisa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and swampy areas. Its flowers are usually pale green to white, with a distinctive three-lobed lip.

Habenaria incisa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
