Halimocnemis longifolia

Halimocnemis longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf halimocnemis, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in terminal spikes, which bloom during the dry season. Its root system is extensive and adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, drought-prone environments.

Halimocnemis longifolia is often found in rocky soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
