Hedysarum ussuriense f. pilosum

Hedysarum ussuriense f. pilosum, commonly known as the Ussuri vetch, is a variant of the Ussuri vetch native to eastern Asia.

It is a perennial leguminous plant characterized by its hairy stems and trifoliate leaves. The flowers are typically pink to purple, arranged in racemes, and are adapted for pollination by insects. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest edges or along riverbanks.

This plant plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its natural habitat.
