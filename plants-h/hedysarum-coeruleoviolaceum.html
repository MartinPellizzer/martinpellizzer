Hedysarum coeruleoviolaceum

Hedysarum coeruleoviolaceum, commonly known as the blue-violet peavine, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial legume characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from blue to violet, which are typically borne in clusters. Hedysarum coeruleoviolaceum has small, stipitate pods that contain several elliptical seeds.
