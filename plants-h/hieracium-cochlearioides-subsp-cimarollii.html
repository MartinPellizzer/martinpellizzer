Hieracium cochlearioides subsp. cimarollii

Hieracium cochlearioides subsp.

cimarollii, commonly known as the Cimarolli's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open grasslands.

The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in a single head. Its inflorescence is characterized by a single, compact flower head with ray florets.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators and its adaptability to arid environments.
