Hypericum esquirolii

Hypericum esquirolii, commonly known as the Sierra hypericum, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and reddish stems, which are typical of the Hypericaceae family. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as cliffs and slopes. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often covered in fine hairs.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soils.
