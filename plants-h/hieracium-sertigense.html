Hieracium sertigense

Hieracium sertigense, commonly known as the clustered hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Hieracium sertigense is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain regions.
