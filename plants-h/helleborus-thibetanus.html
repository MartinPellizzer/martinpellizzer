Helleborus thibetanus

Helleborus thibetanus, commonly known as the Tibetan hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Tibet and neighboring areas.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and striking, waxy flowers that range from white to purple. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, thriving in cool, moist, and shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in late winter to early spring, making it one of the earliest flowering plants in its habitat.

Helleborus thibetanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
