Haplopappus angustifolius

Haplopappus angustifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaved pappus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. Its seeds are surrounded by a pappus, which aids in wind dispersal.

It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.
