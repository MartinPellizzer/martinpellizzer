Helleborine longifolia

Helleborine longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved helleborine, is a species of orchid native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and its striking, hooded flowers that typically bloom in late spring. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks. Its flowers are usually violet or purple in color and have a distinctive, spurred lip.

Helleborine longifolia is a perennial herb that thrives in cool, humid climates and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
