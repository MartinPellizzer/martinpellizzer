Heracleum moritzianum

Heracleum moritzianum, commonly known as Moritz's cow parsnip, is a tall biennial or perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbellate inflorescence and aromatic foliage. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves with a hairy surface and can grow up to 2 meters in height. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in complex umbels, and it produces smooth, greenish-brown seeds.

The species is often found in moist meadows, riverbanks, and disturbed sites, and it is known for its strong, often unpleasant odor.
