Hippochaete praealta var. affinis

Hippochaete praealta var. affinis, commonly known as the tall goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and yellowish-orange inflorescences. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a pioneer species in disturbed environments.
