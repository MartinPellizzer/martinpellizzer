Hieracium praematurum var. integrellum

Hieracium praematurum var. integrellum, commonly known as the early common dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves and a flowering stem that produces a single, yellow flower head. The plant is native to Europe and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It blooms early in the spring, typically from March to May, and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

The species is also noted for its medicinal uses, though it can be invasive in some regions.
