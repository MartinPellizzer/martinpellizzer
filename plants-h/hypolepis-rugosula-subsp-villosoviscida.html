Hypolepis rugosula subsp. villosoviscida

Hypolepis rugosula subsp.

villosoviscida, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a fern species native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive, leathery, and often hairy fronds that give it its common name.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its fronds are pinnately compound, with a rough, textured surface.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
