Helichrysum bakeri

Helichrysum bakeri, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that remain vibrant even after drying. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Helichrysum bakeri is also valued for its use in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms.
