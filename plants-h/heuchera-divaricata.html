Heuchera divaricata

Heuchera divaricata, commonly known as the silver leaf heuchera, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage that provides a unique visual contrast in gardens. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or red during the spring and early summer. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Heuchera divaricata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate a range of growing conditions.
