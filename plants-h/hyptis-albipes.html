Hyptis albipes

Hyptis albipes, commonly known as whiteleaf hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in clusters. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks.

Hyptis albipes is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
