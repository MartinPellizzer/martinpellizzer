Hypericum canariense var. floribundum

Hypericum canariense var. floribundum, commonly known as the Canary goldenberry, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are edible but have a tart, acidic flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant flowers and compact growth habit.
