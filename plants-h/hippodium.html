Hippodium

Hippodium, commonly known as the "spear plant," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, daisy-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow or orange. The plant is native to various regions in the Americas and thrives in sunny, well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are often hairy as well.

Hippodium is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
