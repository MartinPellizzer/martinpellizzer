Hieracium × abortivum

Hieracium × abortivum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves and yellow flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows. It is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in various regions.

Due to its adaptability and invasive tendencies, it is often considered a noxious plant in agricultural and natural areas.
