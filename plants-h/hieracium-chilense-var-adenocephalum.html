Hieracium chilense var. adenocephalum

Hieracium chilense var. adenocephalum, commonly known as the Chilean hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in temperate regions, often in grasslands or disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a composite head.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory effects.
