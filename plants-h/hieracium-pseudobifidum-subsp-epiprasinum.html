Hieracium pseudobifidum subsp. epiprasinum

Hieracium pseudobifidum subsp.

epiprasinum, commonly known as the green-centred hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe. It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and woodland edges, preferring moist and well-drained soils.

The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer, with a distinctive greenish center. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it has a creeping rhizomatous growth habit.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
