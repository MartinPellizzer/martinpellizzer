Hieracium radicatum

Hieracium radicatum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant features bright yellow flowers arranged in a cluster, with ray florets that resemble dandelion petals. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

Hieracium radicatum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common weed in many regions.
