Hydrocotyle muriculata

Hydrocotyle muriculata, commonly known as the waxy begonia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features rounded, waxy leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and sometimes pink. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white or pale pink, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden areas.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and delicate floral display.
