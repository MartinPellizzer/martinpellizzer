Helianthemum greenei subsp. sandiegense

Helianthemum greenei subsp.

sandiegense, commonly known as the San Diego rockrose, is a low-growing shrub native to coastal regions of southern California. It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, typically appearing in clusters along the stems.

The plant has aromatic, glandular leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with mild winters and warm, dry summers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in rocky habitats along the coastline.
