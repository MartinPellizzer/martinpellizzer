Hydrangea stenophylla

Hydrangea stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has simple, ovate to elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Hydrangea stenophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of garden settings.
