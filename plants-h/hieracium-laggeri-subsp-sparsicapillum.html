Hieracium laggeri subsp. sparsicapillum

Hieracium laggeri subsp.

sparsicapillum, commonly known as the sparse-hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It typically grows in grassy areas and disturbed soils, exhibiting a low-growing habit with basal leaves.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers arranged in a single head, surrounded by pale green to white ray florets. Its leaves are covered in sparse, stiff hairs, giving it its subspecific name.

It is part of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive floral structure and hirsute foliage.
