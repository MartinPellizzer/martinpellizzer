Hypericum deidesheimense

Hypericum deidesheimense, commonly known as the Deidesheim St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Hypericaceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and red-orange berries. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and along stream banks, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers consist of four petals and numerous stamens.

Hypericum deidesheimense is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
