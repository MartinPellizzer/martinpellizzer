Hymenophyllum imbricatum

Hymenophyllum imbricatum, commonly known as the rock fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is a small, epiphytic or lithophytic fern that typically grows on rocks or tree trunks in humid, shaded environments. The plant has delicate, wiry fronds that are usually less than 10 centimeters in length. Its leaves are narrow, with a smooth, glossy surface and a distinctive, overlapping arrangement.

Hymenophyllum imbricatum is known for its ability to thrive in moist, nutrient-poor conditions, making it a characteristic species of tropical and subtropical forests.
