Hieracium prussicum subsp. eisbergense

Hieracium prussicum subsp.

eisbergense, commonly known as the Eisberg hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish-green flowers and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense colonies.

Due to its ecological role and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
