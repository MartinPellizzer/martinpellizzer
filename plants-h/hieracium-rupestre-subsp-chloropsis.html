Hieracium rupestre subsp. chloropsis

Hieracium rupestre subsp.

chloropsis, commonly known as the greenleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in dry, sunny environments.

The plant features basal leaves that are elongated and sometimes hairy, with a distinctive greenish hue. Its flowers are yellow and borne in dense, flat-topped clusters, adding to its ornamental appeal.

It is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
