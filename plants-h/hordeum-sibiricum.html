Hordeum sibiricum

Hordeum sibiricum, commonly known as Siberian barley, is a grass species native to the colder regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous or alpine environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces edible grains that have been used traditionally in some cultures.

Hordeum sibiricum is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
