Hepatica triloba var. calycina

Hepatica triloba var. calycina, commonly known as the California liverleaf, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features distinctive heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, soft hairs. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in early spring, which are followed by small, greenish berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
