Hibiscus kavinae

Hibiscus kavinae, commonly known as the Kava hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture. Hibiscus kavinae thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires plenty of sunlight.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in the regions where it is found.
