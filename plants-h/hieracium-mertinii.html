Hieracium mertinii

Hieracium mertinii, commonly known as the Mertin's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in late spring to early summer. Native to Europe, it thrives in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils. The plant has a taproot system and is often found in regions with temperate climates.

It is considered an invasive species in some areas due to its rapid spread and competitive growth habits.
