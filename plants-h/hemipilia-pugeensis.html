Hemipilia pugeensis

Hemipilia pugeensis, commonly known as the Puge hemipilia, is a rare and endemic orchid species native to China.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant has slender, hairy stems and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip. It thrives in moist, shaded forest environments at high altitudes.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
