Hirculus densifoliatus

Hirculus densifoliatus, commonly known as the denseleaf hircus, is a flowering plant characterized by its dense, overlapping foliage.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or along stream banks. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged closely together, giving it a compact appearance. Its flowers are small and clustered, often in terminal inflorescences, and are usually white or pale green in color.

Hirculus densifoliatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
