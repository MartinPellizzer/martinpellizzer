Hieracium callimorphoides subsp. pseudoschultesiiforme

Hieracium callimorphoides subsp.

pseudoschultesiiforme, commonly known as the Schultesii-like hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its yellowish to orange-yellow composite flowers and basal rosettes of serrated leaves.

The subspecies is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, where it thrives in alpine and subalpine habitats. Its morphology includes a hairy stem and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to soil stability in rocky environments.
