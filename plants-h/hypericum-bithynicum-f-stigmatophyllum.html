Hypericum bithynicum f. stigmatophyllum

Hypericum bithynicum f. stigmatophyllum, commonly known as the spotted St. John's wort, is a horticultural form of the genus Hypericum.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with red stamens that bear small, dark spots. This plant typically grows as a shrub with woody stems and opposite, oval-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
