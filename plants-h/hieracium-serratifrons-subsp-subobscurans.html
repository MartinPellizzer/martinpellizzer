Hieracium serratifrons subsp. subobscurans

Hieracium serratifrons subsp.

subobscurans, commonly known as the serrated hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, serrated leaves.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers that are typically arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
