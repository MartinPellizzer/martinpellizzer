Heliotropium pubescens

Heliotropium pubescens, commonly known as the hairy heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to purple, often blooming in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and waste areas.

Heliotropium pubescens is also known for its use in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant.
