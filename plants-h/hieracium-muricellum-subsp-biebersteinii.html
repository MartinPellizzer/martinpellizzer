Hieracium muricellum subsp. biebersteinii

Hieracium muricellum subsp.

biebersteinii, commonly known as the Bieberstein's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowering heads and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually covered with fine hairs, and the flower heads consist of both ray and disc florets.

It is often found in temperate regions and is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens with well-drained soil.
