Heliophytum lineare

Heliophytum lineare, commonly known as the linear heliophyte, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. Heliophytum lineare thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun exposure to flourish.

Its unique morphology and drought tolerance make it an interesting subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
