Hymenachne pseudointerrupta

Hymenachne pseudointerrupta, commonly known as the narrow-leafed hymenachne, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It typically grows in wetland and marshy environments, where it forms dense, rhizomatous stands. The plant has narrow, linear leaves with a smooth surface and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowering period occurs during the wet season, and it relies on water for seed dispersal.

Hymenachne pseudointerrupta plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
