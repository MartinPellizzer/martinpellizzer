Helianthus reindutus

Helianthus reindutus, commonly known as the roughleaf sunflower, is a species of flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, rugged appearance. The plant produces yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Helianthus reindutus is a perennial plant that plays a role in supporting local ecosystems and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
