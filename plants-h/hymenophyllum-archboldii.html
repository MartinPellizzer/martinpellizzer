Hymenophyllum archboldii

Hymenophyllum archboldii, commonly known as the Hawaiian fern, is a small, epiphytic fern native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, moss-like fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or rocks in tropical rainforests. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a rare and ecologically significant species.

Conservation efforts are crucial due to its vulnerability to habitat loss and environmental changes.
