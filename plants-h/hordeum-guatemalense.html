Hordeum guatemalense

Hordeum guatemalense, commonly known as Guatemalan barley, is a wild grass species native to Central America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that bear small, two-lobed florets.

Hordeum guatemalense is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its potential role in ecological restoration and its genetic diversity.
