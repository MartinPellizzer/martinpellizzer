Hieracium canadense f. lepagei

Hieracium canadense f. lepagei, commonly known as the Lepage's Canadian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. It has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence.

This subspecies is distinguished by its slightly smaller flower heads and shorter peduncles compared to the typical form of H. canadense.
