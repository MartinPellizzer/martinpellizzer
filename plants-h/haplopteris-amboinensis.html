Haplopteris amboinensis

Haplopteris amboinensis, commonly known as the Amboina shield fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Haplopteraceae and is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually leathery and have a rugged texture, with a deep green color.

Haplopteris amboinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
