Hypochaeris pontana

Hypochaeris pontana, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies. Its yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer, are accompanied by linear, grass-like leaves.

Hypochaeris pontana is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
