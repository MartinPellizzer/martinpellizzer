Hieracium glaucinum subsp. subpinicola

Hieracium glaucinum subsp.

subpinicola, commonly known as the subalpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in alpine and subalpine meadows, where it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are usually hairy and have a bluish-green hue. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth form and is often found in high-altitude environments.
