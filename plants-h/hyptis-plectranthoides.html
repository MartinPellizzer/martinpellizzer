Hyptis plectranthoides

Hyptis plectranthoides, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, tubular flowers. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
