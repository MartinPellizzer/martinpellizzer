Hippolytia delavayi

Hippolytia delavayi, commonly known as the Delavay's fairy lantern, is a species of flowering plant in the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from orange to red. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens. Its delicate appearance and unique floral structure make it a popular choice among horticultural enthusiasts.
