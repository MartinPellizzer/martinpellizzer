Heliconia scarlatina

Heliconia scarlatina, commonly known as the red heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, brightly colored inflorescences that resemble a torch, with vibrant red bracts surrounding clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy texture, while the stems are stout and segmented.

Heliconia scarlatina thrives in warm, humid climates and is typically found in lowland tropical forests.
