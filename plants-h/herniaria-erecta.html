Herniaria erecta

Herniaria erecta, commonly known as the rock scurfpea, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It thrives in rocky, arid environments and is characterized by its small, rounded leaves that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating. The plant produces tiny, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by small, bead-like fruits. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to withstand harsh, saline conditions.

Herniaria erecta is also known for its ability to form dense mats, which help prevent soil erosion in its natural habitat.
