Heisteria acuminata subsp. intermedia

Heisteria acuminata subsp.

intermedia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heister, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
