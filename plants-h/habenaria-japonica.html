Habenaria japonica

Habenaria japonica, commonly known as the Japanese butterfly orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that resemble butterflies, typically in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has a slender, upright stem with several oval-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

Its blooming period occurs in late spring to early summer, making it a notable ornamental plant in horticulture.
