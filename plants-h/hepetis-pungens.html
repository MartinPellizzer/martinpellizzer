Hepetis pungens

Hepetis pungens, commonly known as the spiked gentian, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Hepetis pungens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
