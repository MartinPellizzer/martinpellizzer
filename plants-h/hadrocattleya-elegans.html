Hadrocattleya elegans

Hadrocattleya elegans, commonly known as the Elegant Cattleya, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, yellow, and pink. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to thrive in humid, warm environments.

The flower structure includes a prominent lip, which is often marked with intricate patterns and serves to attract pollinators.
