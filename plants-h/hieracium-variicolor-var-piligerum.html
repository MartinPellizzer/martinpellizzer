Hieracium variicolor var. piligerum

Hieracium variicolor var. piligerum, commonly known as the hairy yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, which contribute to its common name.

The flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
