Hylotelephium malacophyllum f. roseum

Hylotelephium malacophyllum f. roseum, commonly known as the pink rose-centered stonecrop, is a compact, low-growing succulent with fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically green with a rosy or pinkish hue along the edges.

It produces clusters of showy, pinkish-red flowers in late summer, adding a striking visual element to gardens. Native to rocky and alpine regions, this plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. Its foliage is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover due to its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.

The cultivar is prized for its vibrant coloration and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
