Hieracium murorum subsp. trebevicicola

Hieracium murorum subsp.

trebevicicola, commonly known as the Balkan hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to orange flowers that bloom in the summer months.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has basal leaves that are lanceolate and often covered with fine hairs, and its stems are upright with some branching.

This subspecies is distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and morphological traits that set it apart from other members of the Hieracium genus.
