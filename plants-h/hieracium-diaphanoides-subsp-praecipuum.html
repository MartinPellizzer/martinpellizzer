Hieracium diaphanoides subsp. praecipuum

Hieracium diaphanoides subsp.

praecipuum, commonly known as the translucent hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

The species is notable for its translucent, waxy leaves, which give it its common name.
