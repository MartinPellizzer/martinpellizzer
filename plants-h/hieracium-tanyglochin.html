Hieracium tanyglochin

Hieracium tanyglochin, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features hairy, rough stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. Its flowers are typically yellow or pale green, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets.

Hieracium tanyglochin is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
