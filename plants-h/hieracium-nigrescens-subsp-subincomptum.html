Hieracium nigrescens subsp. subincomptum

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

subincomptum, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features upright stems that are often hairy, with basal leaves that are lance-shaped and gradually smaller towards the top. Its flowers are yellow, with ray florets that are usually shorter than the central disk florets.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ability to produce a large number of seeds.
