Helianthemum apenninum subsp. croceum

Helianthemum apenninum subsp.

croceum, commonly known as the yellow rockrose, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain water in its arid habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found on rocky slopes or in coastal areas.

Its compact growth habit and vibrant floral display make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
