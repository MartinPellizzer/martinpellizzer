Hierochloe magellanica

Hierochloe magellanica, commonly known as Magellanic hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and is often found in moist, temperate regions. The plant typically grows in dense mats or clumps, forming a low-growing vegetation cover. Its botanical features include slender stems and spikelets that are usually arranged in a compact panicle.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
