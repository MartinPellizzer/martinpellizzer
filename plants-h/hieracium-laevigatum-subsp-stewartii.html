Hieracium laevigatum subsp. stewartii

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

stewartii, commonly known as Stewart's hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and grasslands.

The plant features a basal rosette of leaves that are hairy and lance-shaped, with a rough texture. Its flowering stems bear clusters of small, yellowish to orange flowers, which are typically arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

The species is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, and it is often found in regions with temperate climates.
