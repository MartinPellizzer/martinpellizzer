Hieracium Murorum Subsp. Albovittoides

Hieracium murorum subsp. albovittoides is a lesser-known member of the dandelion family known for its distinctive appearance and ecological significance. This plant is commonly found in disturbed soils and open habitats across Europe and parts of Asia. Its white-veined leaves and yellow flowers make it easily identifiable among other hawkweeds. Despite its modest size, it plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and soil stability. Conservation efforts are increasingly focusing on protecting this subspecies from habitat loss and invasive species.
