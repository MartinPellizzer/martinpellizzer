Hypericum atomarium subsp. degenii

Hypericum atomarium subsp.

degenii, commonly known as the narrowleaf St. John's wort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellow flowers with four petals, characteristic of the Hypericaceae family. It is known for its medicinal properties, containing hypericin and pseudohypericin, which have been studied for their potential antidepressant effects.

However, due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
