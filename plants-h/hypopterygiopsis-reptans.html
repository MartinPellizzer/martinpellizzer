Hypopterygiopsis reptans

Hypopterygiopsis reptans, commonly known as the creeping hypopterygiopsis, is a small, mat-forming fern that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It belongs to the family Hypopterygiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, elongated fronds that often lie close to the ground. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sorus, which are typically located on the underside of the fronds. Its rhizomes are creeping and help it spread across the forest floor, forming dense colonies.

Hypopterygiopsis reptans is often found in temperate regions with high humidity and is an indicator species for undisturbed, moist habitats.
