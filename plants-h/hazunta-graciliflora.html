Hazunta graciliflora

Hazunta graciliflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered hazunta, is a rare and delicate plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It features elongated, narrow leaves that are covered in a fine layer of silvery hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid environment. The plant produces small, pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is shallow but extensive, allowing it to absorb nutrients from the thin, rocky soil.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Hazunta graciliflora is considered vulnerable to environmental changes and habitat destruction.
