Hieracium × bifurcum subsp. castellanum

Hieracium × bifurcum subsp.

castellanum, commonly known as the Castellano's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lanceolate leaves and produces numerous yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

The plant is native to southern Europe and is often found in dry, sunny grasslands and rocky areas. It has a taproot system and exhibits a mat-forming growth habit, which helps it survive in harsh environmental conditions.

Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, contributing to its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
