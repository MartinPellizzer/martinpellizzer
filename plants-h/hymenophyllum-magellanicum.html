Hymenophyllum magellanicum

Hymenophyllum magellanicum, commonly known as the Magellanic haircap, is a small, epiphytic fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, moss-like appearance with finely divided, hair-like leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or rocks in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are covered in a layer of fine, transparent cells that help reduce water loss and enhance light absorption.

Hymenophyllum magellanicum is known for its unique reproductive structures, including small, spore-producing capsules that are often protected by a hood-like structure.
