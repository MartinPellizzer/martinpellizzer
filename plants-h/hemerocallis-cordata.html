Hemerocallis cordata

Hemerocallis cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf daylily, is a perennial plant native to Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, heart-like leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Hemerocallis cordata is also known for its ability to naturalize and spread through rhizomes, creating dense foliage.
