Hibiscus venustus

Hibiscus venustus, commonly known as the velvetleaf hibiscus, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are often visited by bees and other pollinators.

Hibiscus venustus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage and floral display.
