Haemanthus capensis

Haemanthus capensis, commonly known as the red hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, sword-shaped leaves and vivid red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is part of the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking visual contrast between its green foliage and fiery blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its unique appearance and long-lasting flowers make it a notable ornamental plant in horticulture.
