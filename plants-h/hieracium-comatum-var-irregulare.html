Hieracium comatum var. irregulare

Hieracium comatum var. irregulare, commonly known as the irregular hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with a rough texture, and the flowers are yellow, often appearing in clusters.

It is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is considered a weed in some agricultural areas.
