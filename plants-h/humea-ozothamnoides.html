Humea ozothamnoides

Humea ozothamnoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved huckleberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and streamside areas. The plant has alternate, oval-shaped leaves that are often densely covered with fine hairs. Its small, bell-shaped flowers are usually white or pale pink and appear in clusters.

The plant produces edible berries that are dark blue to black in color, often used in local foraging traditions.
