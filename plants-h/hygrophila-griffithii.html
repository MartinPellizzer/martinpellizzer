Hygrophila griffithii

Hygrophila griffithii, commonly known as the water hyssop, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming during the rainy season. Hygrophila griffithii thrives in wet or marshy environments, making it a common sight in ponds and along waterways.

It is often used in aquatic gardens and as a ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to wet conditions.
