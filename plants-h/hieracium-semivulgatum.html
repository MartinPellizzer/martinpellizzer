Hieracium semivulgatum

Hieracium semivulgatum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Hieracium semivulgatum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
