Hemionitis jonesii

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to extreme environmental conditions. The plant has a distinctive appearance with narrow, leathery fronds that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of many fern species.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Hemionitis jonesii is considered a sensitive indicator of ecological health in its native range.
