Hieracium sclerotrichum

Hieracium sclerotrichum, commonly known as the rough hawksbeard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lance-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces, and the flowers are yellow with prominent central disk florets.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
