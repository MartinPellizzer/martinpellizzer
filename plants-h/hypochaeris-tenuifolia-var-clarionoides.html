Hypochaeris tenuifolia var. clarionoides

Hypochaeris tenuifolia var. clarionoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils, and it produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a hairy texture, and it is often found in regions with a Mediterranean climate.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
