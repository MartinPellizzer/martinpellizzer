Hymenopappus parvulus

Hymenopappus parvulus, commonly known as the dwarf hymenopappus, is a small annual plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive bracts. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are small and yellow, often appearing in clusters, and it produces achenes with pappus hairs for wind dispersal.

Hymenopappus parvulus is often found in disturbed areas and is considered a pioneer species in ecological succession.
