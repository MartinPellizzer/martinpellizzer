Heptapleurum farinosum var. farinosum

Heptapleurum farinosum var. farinosum, commonly known as the glossy bamboo or farinosum heptapleurum, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and stout, bamboo-like stems. The leaves are typically leathery, elliptical, and have a glossy appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by red, fleshy berries.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is valued in horticulture for its hardiness and adaptability.
