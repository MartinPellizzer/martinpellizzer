Hoya medinae

Hoya medinae, commonly known as the "Medina's Waxplant," is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom in late spring or early summer. Hoya medinae is a trailing plant that thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.

It is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
