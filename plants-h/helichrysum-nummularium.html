Helichrysum nummularium

Helichrysum nummularium, commonly known as the golden weed, is a small, flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and produces small, yellow to golden-orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has tiny, rounded leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Helichrysum nummularium is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
