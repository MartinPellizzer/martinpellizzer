Hemianthus trisetosus

Hemianthus trisetosus, commonly known as the three-bristled needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, thread-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences that are typically arranged in panicle-like clusters. Hemianthus trisetosus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
