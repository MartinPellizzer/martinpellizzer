Hymenorchis serrulata

Hymenorchis serrulata, commonly known as the serrated helmet orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, helmet-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow with purple markings. The plant produces a single, upright spike bearing several flowers, each with a long, narrow spur. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in shaded, moist environments.

Hymenorchis serrulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
