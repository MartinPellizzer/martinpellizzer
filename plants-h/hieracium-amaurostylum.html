Hieracium amaurostylum

Hieracium amaurostylum, commonly known as the black-hued hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its dark purple to black flower heads are distinctive, and the leaves are usually lance-shaped with hairy surfaces.

Hieracium amaurostylum is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh, high-altitude habitats.
