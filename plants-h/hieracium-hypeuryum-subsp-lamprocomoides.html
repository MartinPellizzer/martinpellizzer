Hieracium hypeuryum subsp. lamprocomoides

Hieracium hypeuryum subsp.

lamprocomoides, commonly known as the silverleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a silvery-white underside, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers are yellow and are arranged in a flat-topped cluster, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
