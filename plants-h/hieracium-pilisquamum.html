Hieracium pilisquamum

Hieracium pilisquamum, commonly known as the scaly hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant has hairy, often reddish stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped with a rough texture. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster, producing small seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Hieracium pilisquamum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it can sometimes be invasive in certain regions.
