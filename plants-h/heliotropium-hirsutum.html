Heliotropium hirsutum

Heliotropium hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces small, fragrant flowers typically in shades of purple or pink. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas.

Heliotropium hirsutum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
