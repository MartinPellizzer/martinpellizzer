Hieracium sparsum subsp. neiceffianum

Hieracium sparsum subsp.

neiceffianum, commonly known as the Neiceffian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in the summer.

Native to southern Europe, this subspecies thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its flowers are typically arranged in a single head, and the plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread vegetatively.

The species is also notable for its chemical composition, which includes various sesquiterpene lactones.
