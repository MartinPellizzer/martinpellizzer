Hieracium subramosum subsp. concretiforme

Hieracium subramosum subsp.

concretiforme, commonly known as the concrete-form hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rough, hairy texture, and the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is notable for its compact growth form and is often found in disturbed soils.
