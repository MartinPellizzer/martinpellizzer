Hemizonia laevis

Hemizonia laevis, commonly known as the smooth hemizonia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are smooth and lance-shaped, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disc.

Hemizonia laevis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil.
