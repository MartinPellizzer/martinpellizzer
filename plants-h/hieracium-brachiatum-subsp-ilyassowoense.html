Hieracium brachiatum subsp. ilyassowoense

Hieracium brachiatum subsp.

ilyassowoense, commonly known as the Ilyassowian hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and tall flowering stems that bear yellow flowers in clusters.

The plant has rough, hairy leaves and is often found in dry, rocky or sandy soils. It is part of the daisy family (Asteraceae) and has a compact growth habit.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
