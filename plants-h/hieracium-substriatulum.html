Hieracium substriatulum

Hieracium substriatulum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinctive pale green color with a waxy coating.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
