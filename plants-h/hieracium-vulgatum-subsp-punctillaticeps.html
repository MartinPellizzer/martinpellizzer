Hieracium vulgatum subsp. punctillaticeps

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

punctillaticeps, commonly known as the spotted hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower.

The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

The subspecies is distinguished by its finely dotted leaves and a unique pattern of bracts beneath the flower heads.
