Hieracium apiculatiforme

Hieracium apiculatiforme, commonly known as the hooked hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of sparsely hairy, lanceolate leaves and produces numerous yellow flowers arranged in a composite inflorescence. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grasslands and disturbed soils. It has hairy stems and the flowers are typically surrounded by a pale green involucral bracts.

Hieracium apiculatiforme is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in many regions.
