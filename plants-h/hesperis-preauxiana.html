Hesperis preauxiana

Hesperis preauxiana, commonly known as the Mediterranean candytuft, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

Hesperis preauxiana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
