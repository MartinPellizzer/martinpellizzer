Habenaria digitata var. travancorica

Habenaria digitata var. travancorica, commonly known as the Travancore butterfly orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves and distinctive, fragrant flowers that resemble butterflies. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments, preferring well-drained soil. Its flowers are usually pale green to white with a yellow lip, and it blooms during the monsoon season.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
