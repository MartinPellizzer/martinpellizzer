Hieracium bifidum subsp. uncinatulum

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

uncinatulum, commonly known as the hooked hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with hairy surfaces, and the flowers are arranged in a single, terminal head.

The subspecies is distinguished by its hooked bracts and the presence of glandular hairs on the foliage.
