Hyptis jacobi

Hyptis jacobi, commonly known as the Jacob's hyptis, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite leaves that are often hairy and grayish in appearance. Its small, tubular flowers are typically arranged in spikes and can range in color from white to purple.

Hyptis jacobi is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
