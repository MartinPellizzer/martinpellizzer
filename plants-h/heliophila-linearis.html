Heliophila linearis

Heliophila linearis, commonly known as the linear willow herb, is a flowering plant belonging to the Onagraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in moist, sunny environments such as wet meadows and stream banks. Its root system is fibrous, allowing it to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in wetland habitats.

The plant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and aesthetic value.
