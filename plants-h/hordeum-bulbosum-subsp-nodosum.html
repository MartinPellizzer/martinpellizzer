Hordeum bulbosum subsp. nodosum

Hordeum bulbosum subsp.

nodosum, commonly known as the nodose barley, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and cylindrical, spikelet-bearing inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

The subspecies is distinguished by its nodular structures on the stems, which are a key botanical feature.
