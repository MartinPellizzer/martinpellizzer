Hieracium javasevii

Hieracium javasevii, commonly known as the Java hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and typically hairy foliage. The plant has alternate, rough, and often serrated leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers that are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

Hieracium javasevii is typically found in moist, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and forest edges.
