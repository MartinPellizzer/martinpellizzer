Hieracium incisodentatum

Hieracium incisodentatum, commonly known as the serrated hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow composite flowers that attract pollinators. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and damp woodlands. Its leaves are deeply lobed and often have serrated edges, giving it its common name.

Hieracium incisodentatum is native to Europe and parts of Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates.
