Hookera volubilis

Hookera volubilis, commonly known as the creeping lily, is a member of the Araceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vine-like growth habit, with trailing stems that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces striking, spatulate-shaped leaves that are often dark green with a waxy texture. Its flowers are clustered in a spathe and spadix inflorescence, typical of the Araceae family, and are usually white or pale green.

Hookera volubilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in humid, shaded environments.
