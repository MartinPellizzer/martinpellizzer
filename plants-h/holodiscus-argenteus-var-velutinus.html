Holodiscus argenteus var. velutinus

Holodiscus argenteus var. velutinus, commonly known as the velvetleaf holodiscus, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, silvery-white pubescence, giving it a distinctive, velvety appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its fibrous roots and woody stems make it a hardy species adaptable to various soil types.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
