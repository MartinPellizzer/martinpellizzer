Hieracium solitudinis

Hieracium solitudinis, commonly known as the solitary hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its yellowish-orange composite flowers and hairy, often reddish stems. Native to Europe, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant has serrated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette.

It plays a role in supporting pollinators and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
