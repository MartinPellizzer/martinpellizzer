Heisteria vageleri

Heisteria vageleri, commonly known as the velvetleaf heister, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring simple, opposite, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, usually white or pale yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are characterized by their velvety texture and dark green color, while its stems are covered in fine hairs.

Heisteria vageleri is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and along stream banks.
