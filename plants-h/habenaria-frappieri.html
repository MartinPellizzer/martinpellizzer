Habenaria frappieri

Habenaria frappieri, commonly known as the Frappieri's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers with pale pink or white petals and a distinctive, elongated lip. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some horticultural gardens.
