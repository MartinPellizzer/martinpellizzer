Hibiscus esculentus var. praecox

Hibiscus esculentus var. praecox, commonly known as the African eggplant or okra, is a tropical plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible pods, which are rich in nutrients. The botanical features of this plant make it well-suited for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
