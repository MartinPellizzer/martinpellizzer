Hymenophyllum jimenezii

Hymenophyllum jimenezii, commonly known as the Jimenez's haircap, is a small, moss-like fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture, giving it a unique appearance. This species thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the leaves, and they produce spores for propagation.

Hymenophyllum jimenezii is often found growing on rocks or tree trunks, where it can access the moisture and nutrients it requires for survival.
