Henckelia meeboldii

Henckelia meeboldii, commonly known as the Meebold's henckelia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It typically grows at high altitudes, thriving in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil. The plant features compact, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually appear in clusters, displaying shades of pink or purple.

Henckelia meeboldii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate frosty conditions.
