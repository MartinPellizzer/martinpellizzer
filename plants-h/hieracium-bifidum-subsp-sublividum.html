Hieracium bifidum subsp. sublividum

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

sublividum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to orange flower heads.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinctive reddish or purplish underside.

It is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
