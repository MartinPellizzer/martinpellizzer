Haemodorum condensatum

Haemodorum condensatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf everlasting pepper.

It is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia, characterized by its small, dark red to black berries that remain on the plant for extended periods. The plant typically grows in wet sclerophyll forests and rainforests, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, with a glossy appearance, and it produces delicate yellow flowers during the spring.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional Aboriginal medicine.
