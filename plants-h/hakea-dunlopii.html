Hakea dunlopii

Hakea dunlopii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and produces distinctive, cylindrical flower spikes. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought-resistant features. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects.

Hakea dunlopii is often found in heathlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
