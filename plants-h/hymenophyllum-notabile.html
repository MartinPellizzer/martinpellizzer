Hymenophyllum notabile

Hymenophyllum notabile, commonly known as the silver fern, is a small, epiphytic fern native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are often covered in a fine layer of silvery hair. The plant's leaves are typically narrow and elongated, with a distinct midrib and a dense arrangement of tiny, scale-like structures. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found growing on tree trunks or rocks.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a popular subject in botanical research and conservation efforts.
