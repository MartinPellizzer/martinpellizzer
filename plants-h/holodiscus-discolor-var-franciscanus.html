Holodiscus discolor var. franciscanus

Holodiscus discolor var. franciscanus, commonly known as the San Francisco thimbleweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, small, disc-like flowers that are typically white or pale green. The plant has a basal rosette of leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its leaves are alternate and have a lanceolate shape with serrated edges.

This variety is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.
