Hebecoccus falcatus

Hebecoccus falcatus, commonly known as the sickle leaf cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its unique, falcate (sickle-shaped) leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with a thick, woody stem that stores water. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, and they appear in clusters during the warmer months.

Hebecoccus falcatus is adapted to arid environments and is often found in dry forests and scrublands.
