Hieracium striatulum

Hieracium striatulum, commonly known as the striped hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture, and the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

The species is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
