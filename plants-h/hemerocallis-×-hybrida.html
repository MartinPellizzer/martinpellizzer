Hemerocallis × hybrida

Hemerocallis × hybrida, commonly known as the daylily, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Hemerocallidaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically last only one day, hence the name "daylily.". The plant is native to East Asia but has been widely cultivated around the world for its ornamental value. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is drought-tolerant, making it a popular choice for gardens.

The foliage is broad and grass-like, often forming dense clumps that can spread through rhizomes.
