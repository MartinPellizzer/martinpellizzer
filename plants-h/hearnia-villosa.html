Hearnia villosa

Hearnia villosa, commonly known as the woolly heath, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes shrubs and small trees adapted to acidic soils. The plant is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in well-drained environments.

Hearnia villosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique texture and resilience.
