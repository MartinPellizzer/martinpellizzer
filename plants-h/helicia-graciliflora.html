Helicia graciliflora

Helicia graciliflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered helicia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Theaceae and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments and can reach heights of up to 15 meters. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow and are arranged in clusters, while its fruits are woody capsules containing several seeds.

Helicia graciliflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
