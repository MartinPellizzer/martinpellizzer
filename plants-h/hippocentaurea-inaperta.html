Hippocentaurea inaperta

Hippocentaurea inaperta, commonly known as the Alaskan sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Alaska and northern Canada.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, blue to violet flower heads. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in sandy or gravelly soils along the shoreline. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
