Hoarea pilosellifolia

Hoarea pilosellifolia, commonly known as the woolly groundsel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which bloom in spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Hoarea pilosellifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique texture and color.
