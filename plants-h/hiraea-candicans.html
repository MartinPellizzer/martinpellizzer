Hiraea candicans

Hiraea candicans, commonly known as the candleplant, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, candle-like inflorescence composed of numerous small, white to yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Hiraea candicans thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
