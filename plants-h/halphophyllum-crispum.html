Halphophyllum crispum

Halphophyllum crispum, commonly known as the crinkled hawthorn, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are often crinkled or curled, giving it its common name. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Halphophyllum crispum is often found in shrubland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
