Hedyotis acuminata f. obovata

Hedyotis acuminata f. obovata, commonly known as the broad-leafed blushing creeper, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its obovate leaves that are glossy and often have a reddish or purple hue on the underside. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender pedicels. Its stems are usually prostrate or trailing, making it suitable for ground cover or hanging baskets.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its attractive foliage and flowers.
