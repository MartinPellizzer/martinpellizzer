Hibiscus archboldianus

Hibiscus archboldianus, commonly known as the Archbold's hibiscus, is a species native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and have a distinct, rough texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas or near freshwater habitats.

Hibiscus archboldianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
