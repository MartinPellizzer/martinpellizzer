Heracleum sumatranum

Heracleum sumatranum, commonly known as the Greater Bitterroot, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and umbellate flowers.

Its leaves are pinnately divided, and the flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in umbrella-like inflorescences.

The plant is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value due to its distinctive foliage and unique flowering structure.
