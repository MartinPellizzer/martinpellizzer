Heliopsis helianthoides var. fischeri

Heliopsis helianthoides var. fischeri, commonly known as the Fischer's false sunflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features bright yellow, daisy-like flowers with a central disk and ray florets, typically blooming from late spring to early fall. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces seeds in a distinctive, spiky structure. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for pollinators and ecological restoration projects.
