Heymassoli spinosa

Heymassoli spinosa, commonly known as the spiny heymassoli, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems covered in sharp, spiny structures that provide protection against herbivores. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are typically small, narrow, and adapted to reduce water loss through transpiration.

Heymassoli spinosa is often found in rocky soils and is well-suited to drought conditions.
