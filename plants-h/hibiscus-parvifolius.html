Hibiscus parvifolius

Hibiscus parvifolius, commonly known as the small-leaved hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, small leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Hibiscus parvifolius is a perennial shrub that can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

Its flowers bloom in summer and are known for their ornamental value in gardens.
