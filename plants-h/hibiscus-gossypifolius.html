Hibiscus gossypifolius

Hibiscus gossypifolius, commonly known as the cottonleaf hibiscus, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that resemble cotton balls, giving it its common name. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom throughout the summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for Mediterranean climates.

Hibiscus gossypifolius is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
