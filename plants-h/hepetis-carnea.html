Hepetis carnea

Hepetis carnea, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, violet-pink flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Hepetis carnea is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
