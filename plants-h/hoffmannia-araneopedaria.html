Hoffmannia araneopedaria

Hoffmannia araneopedaria, commonly known as the spider-veined hoffmannia, is a striking member of the Araceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with intricate, spider-like veins that give it its common name. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe, typical of many aroid plants.

Hoffmannia araneopedaria is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
