Hypericum ascyron var. longistylum

Hypericum ascyron var. longistylum, commonly known as the long-stemmed St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, woody stems that can grow up to several feet in height, often with a reddish hue. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and elliptic with a slightly serrated margin.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
