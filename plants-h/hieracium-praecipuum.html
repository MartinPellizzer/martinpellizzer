Hieracium praecipuum

Hieracium praecipuum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are basal and alternate, with a rough texture and serrated edges.

The flowers are yellow, and the plant produces achenes as its fruit, which are dispersed by wind.
