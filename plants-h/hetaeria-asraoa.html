Hetaeria asraoa

Hetaeria asraoa, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in shaded, forested areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Hetaeria asraoa is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
