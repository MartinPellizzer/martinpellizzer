Hieracium charadreum

Hieracium charadreum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and flowering stems that bear yellow daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite inflorescences.

Hieracium charadreum is adapted to cold climates and is often found at high altitudes where few other plants can survive.
