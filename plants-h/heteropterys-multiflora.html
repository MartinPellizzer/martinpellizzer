Heteropterys multiflora

Heteropterys multiflora, commonly known as the rosewood tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and small, clustered white or pink flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, round, leathery fruits that contain several seeds. Its leaves are typically alternate, simple, and have a rough texture with a strong scent.

Heteropterys multiflora is valued for its hard, dark wood, which is used in furniture and musical instruments.
