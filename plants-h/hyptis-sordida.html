Hyptis sordida

Hyptis sordida, commonly known as the sordid hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the Americas, particularly found in regions of Brazil, Mexico, and Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple. It often grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

Hyptis sordida is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
