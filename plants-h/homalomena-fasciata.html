Homalomena fasciata

Homalomena fasciata, commonly known as the zebra plant, is a tropical evergreen perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It features broad, arrowhead-shaped leaves with distinctive white to light green vertical stripes, giving it its unique appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of yellow or white, followed by red berries. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.

Its foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
