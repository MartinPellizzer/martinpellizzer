Hemigraphis hirsuta

Hemigraphis hirsuta, commonly known as the velvetleaf or hairy iron plant, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its thick, velvety foliage that ranges from green to purple, often with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the leaf axils. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Hemigraphis hirsuta is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
