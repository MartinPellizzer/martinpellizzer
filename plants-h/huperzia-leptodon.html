Huperzia leptodon

Huperzia leptodon, commonly known as the slender tooth huperzia, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and spore-producing structures called sporangia. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its leaves are often arranged in a rosette pattern and feature a distinctive, tooth-like margin.

Huperzia leptodon is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
