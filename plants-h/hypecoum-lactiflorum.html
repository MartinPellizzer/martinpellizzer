Hypecoum lactiflorum

Hypecoum lactiflorum, commonly known as the milkflower, is a striking flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has a woody stem and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Hypecoum lactiflorum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
