Helicia glabriflora var. conjunctiflora

Helicia glabriflora var. conjunctiflora, commonly known as the clustered-flowered helicia, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its dense, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or yellowish flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule containing several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
