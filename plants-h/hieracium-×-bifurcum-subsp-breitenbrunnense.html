Hieracium × bifurcum subsp. breitenbrunnense

Hieracium × bifurcum subsp.

breitenbrunnense, commonly known as the Breitenbrunn hawkweed, is a hybrid perennial plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and basal rosettes of serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and forest edges, preferring well-drained soils. It exhibits a compact growth habit with hairy stems and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Due to its hybrid origin, it shows variability in morphology, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
