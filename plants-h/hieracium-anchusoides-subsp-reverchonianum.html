Hieracium anchusoides subsp. reverchonianum

Hieracium anchusoides subsp.

reverchonianum, commonly known as the Reverchona hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and clustered, yellowish to reddish flower heads.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at elevations ranging from 1,000 to 2,500 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are arranged in a composite head.

This subspecies is relatively rare and is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations.
