Hippeastrum monanthum

Hippeastrum monanthum, commonly known as the naked Lady, is a bulbous plant native to South America.

It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, typically blooming in winter or early spring. The plant has a fleshy, underground bulb that stores nutrients, supporting its growth and flowering cycle. Its leaves are strap-like and often emerge after the flowers have faded.

Hippeastrum monanthum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation in gardens and indoor settings.
