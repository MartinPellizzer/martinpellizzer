Handroanthus chrysanthus subsp. pluvicola

Handroanthus chrysanthus subsp.

pluvicola, commonly known as the yellow trumpet tree, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil. It is characterized by its striking yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant typically grows in moist, tropical environments and is often found in riverine forests. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, with a smooth surface and a slightly glossy appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
