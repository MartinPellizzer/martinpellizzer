Hieracium nigrescens subsp. varangerense

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

varangerense, commonly known as the Varanger hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern regions of Europe and parts of Russia. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering stems that bear yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in alpine or subalpine habitats. It has hairy stems and leaves, and its flowers are usually arranged in a single head at the tip of each stem.

This subspecies is notable for its restricted distribution and is often found in specific ecological niches.
