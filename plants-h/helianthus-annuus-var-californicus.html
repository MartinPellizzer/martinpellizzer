Helianthus annuus var. californicus

Helianthus annuus var. californicus, commonly known as the California sunflower, is a variant of the common sunflower native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its large, yellow-orange flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces, and it produces numerous seeds in its composite flower head.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
