Hypericum inodorum

Hypericum inodorum, commonly known as sweet woodruff, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves that emit a pleasant, vanilla-like scent when crushed. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Hypericum inodorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
