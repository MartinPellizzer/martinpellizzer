Haplochilus flavus

Haplochilus flavus, commonly known as the yellow sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with succulent, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a vibrant touch to its desert habitat. Its root system is adapted to absorb moisture from deep within the soil, allowing it to survive in nutrient-poor conditions.

Haplochilus flavus plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and supporting local biodiversity.
