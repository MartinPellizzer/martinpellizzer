Helie lanceolata

Helie lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates. Its ornamental value comes from its delicate foliage and prolonged blooming period.

Helie lanceolata is also valued for its use in traditional medicine in some regions.
