Hoya vacciniiflora

Hoya vacciniiflora, commonly known as the red-veined hoya, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and waxy, dark green leaves with prominent red veins. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Hoya vacciniiflora is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil.
