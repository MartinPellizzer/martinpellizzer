Hosackia oblongifolia

Hosackia oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of blue or purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous and can form dense colonies, making it a hardy plant that thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Hosackia oblongifolia is often found in wet meadows, prairies, and along streambanks.
