Hyphaene spaerulifera

Hyphaene spaerulifera, commonly known as the desert palm or the African fan palm, is a flowering plant species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of narrow, fan-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear yellowish-white flowers, followed by edible, fleshy fruits. Its root system is adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various desert animals.
