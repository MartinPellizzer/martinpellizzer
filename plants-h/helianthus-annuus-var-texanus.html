Helianthus annuus var. texanus

Helianthus annuus var. texanus, commonly known as the Texas sunflower, is a variant of the common sunflower native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its large, yellow, daisy-like flowers and robust, upright stems. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and covered in a fine layer of hairs.

The Texas sunflower is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
