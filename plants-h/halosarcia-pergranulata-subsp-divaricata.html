Halosarcia pergranulata subsp. divaricata

Halosarcia pergranulata subsp.

divaricata, commonly known as the branched halosarcia, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, branching stems that are covered in a thick layer of white, powdery bloom.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by round, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
