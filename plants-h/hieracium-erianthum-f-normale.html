Hieracium erianthum f. normale

Hieracium erianthum f. normale, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellowish to orange-red composite flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn. The plant typically has basal and cauline leaves that are lanceolate to ovate in shape, with hairy surfaces. It thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats, often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its reproductive strategy involves wind pollination, and it produces achenes with pappus for seed dispersal.
