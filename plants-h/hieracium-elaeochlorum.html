Hieracium elaeochlorum

Hieracium elaeochlorum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Hieracium elaeochlorum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
