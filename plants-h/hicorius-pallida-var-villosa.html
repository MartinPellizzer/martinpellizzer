Hicorius pallida var. villosa

Hicorius pallida var. villosa, commonly known as the white-hued, hairy hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its pale white to cream-colored flowers, which are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The leaves are typically ovate with a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its overall appearance. This variety thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking floral display.
