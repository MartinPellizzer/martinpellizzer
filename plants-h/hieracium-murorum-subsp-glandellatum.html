Hieracium murorum subsp. glandellatum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

glandellatum, commonly known as the glandular hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.

The plant features basal rosettes of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in late summer. Its subspecies is distinguished by the presence of glandular hairs on the leaves and stems, which contribute to its common name.

The plant is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
