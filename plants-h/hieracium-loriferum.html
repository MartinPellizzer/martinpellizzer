Hieracium loriferum

Hieracium loriferum, commonly known as the showy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features bright yellow to orange flowers that are arranged in a single, showy inflorescence at the top of the stem. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

Hieracium loriferum often grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.
