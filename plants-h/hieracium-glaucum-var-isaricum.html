Hieracium glaucum var. isaricum

Hieracium glaucum var. isaricum, commonly known as the Isar Hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. It has hairy stems and leaves that are often glandular, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Its flowers are arranged in a single capitulum, typical of the Asteraceae family, and bloom from late spring to early summer.
