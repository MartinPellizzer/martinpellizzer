Handroanthus pedicellatus

Handroanthus pedicellatus, commonly known as the yellow trumpet tree, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, featuring a straight trunk and smooth, grayish bark. The plant is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters at the ends of branches, typically during the dry season. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
