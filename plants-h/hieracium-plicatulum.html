Hieracium plicatulum

Hieracium plicatulum, commonly known as the zigzag hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a distinctive zigzag pattern along the edges, giving it its common name.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
