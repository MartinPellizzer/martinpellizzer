Helicia vestita var. longipes

Helicia vestita var. longipes, commonly known as the long-stalked holly.

It is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage and distinctive, elongated inflorescences that bear small, white flowers. Its leaves are typically elliptical with a leathery texture, and the plant often grows in shaded, humid environments.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in tropical climates.
