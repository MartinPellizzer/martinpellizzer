Hieracium fuscovillosulum

Hieracium fuscovillosulum, commonly known as the woolly yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system. The plant features upright, hairy stems and basal leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
