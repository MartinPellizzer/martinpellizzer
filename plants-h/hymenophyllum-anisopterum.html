Hymenophyllum anisopterum

Hymenophyllum anisopterum, commonly known as the silver moss, is a species of liverwort belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically covered in a fine layer of silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on tree trunks and rocks in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are small and closely spaced, and they possess a unique structure with a well-developed midrib.

Hymenophyllum anisopterum plays an important ecological role in forest ecosystems by contributing to soil stability and providing microhabitats for various invertebrates.
