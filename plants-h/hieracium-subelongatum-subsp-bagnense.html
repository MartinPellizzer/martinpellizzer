Hieracium subelongatum subsp. bagnense

Hieracium subelongatum subsp.

bagnense, commonly known as the Bagnes Centaury, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish to reddish flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptation to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and ecological specificity.
