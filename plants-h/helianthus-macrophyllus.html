Helianthus macrophyllus

Helianthus macrophyllus, commonly known as the swamp sunflower, is a tall, herbaceous perennial native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features large, showy yellow flowers with dark centers, typically blooming in late summer to early fall. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its robust root system helps stabilize soil in moist environments.

It is a valuable plant for pollinators and is often used in wetland restoration projects.
