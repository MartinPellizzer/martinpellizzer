Hymenophyllum badium

Hymenophyllum badium, commonly known as the silver fern, is a species of moss belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are often covered in a fine, silvery coating of trichomes. This moss thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are narrow and closely spaced, forming a dense, carpet-like growth.

Hymenophyllum badium plays an important ecological role in maintaining soil moisture and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
