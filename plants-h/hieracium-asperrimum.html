Hieracium asperrimum

Hieracium asperrimum, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features rough, hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with ray florets surrounding a central disc of tubular flowers.

Hieracium asperrimum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it can sometimes be invasive in certain regions.
