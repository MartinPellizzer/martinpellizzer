Hebe venustula

Hebe venustula, commonly known as the pretty hebe, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, or white, typically blooming from spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Hebe venustula is relatively low maintenance and resistant to pests and diseases, contributing to its widespread cultivation.
