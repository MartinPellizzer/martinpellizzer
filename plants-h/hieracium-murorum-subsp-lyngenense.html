Hieracium murorum subsp. lyngenense

Hieracium murorum subsp.

lyngenense, commonly known as the Lyngen hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellowish to orange flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in mountainous or alpine regions. It has basal leaves that are lanceolate and hairy, and its stems are often reddish in color.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in high-altitude environments.
