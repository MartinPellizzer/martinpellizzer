Helicteres procumbens

Helicteres procumbens, commonly known as creeping hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with red centers, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are ovate and covered in fine hairs, and the plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often found in wetlands and along stream banks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
