Hypoxis muhilensis subsp. muhilensis

Hypoxis muhilensis subsp.

muhilensis, commonly known as the Mexican false shamrock, is a perennial herb native to Mexico. It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming foliage composed of narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and delicate flowers.
