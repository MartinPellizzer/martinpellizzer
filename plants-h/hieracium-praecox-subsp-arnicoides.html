Hieracium praecox subsp. arnicoides

Hieracium praecox subsp.

arnicoides, commonly known as the early hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its upright, herbaceous growth habit and features yellowish to orange-red flower heads composed of numerous small florets.

The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rough texture and a serrated edge, while the stems are hairy and often reddish in color.

This subspecies is native to Europe and parts of Asia, where it plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
