Heynea connaroides

Heynea connaroides, commonly known as African mahogany, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Connaraceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by oval, woody fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its valuable timber, it is also considered a threatened species in some regions.
