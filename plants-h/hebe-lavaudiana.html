Hebe lavaudiana

Hebe lavaudiana, commonly known as the Kauri hebe, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its narrow, upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of purple or pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Hebe lavaudiana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
