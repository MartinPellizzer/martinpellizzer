Hieracium graecum subsp. graecum

Hieracium graecum subsp.

graecum, commonly known as Greek hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, and it produces yellow flowers in a flat-topped inflorescence. It has a fibrous root system and is characterized by its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is known for its composite flower heads and diverse ecological roles.
