Helianthemum origanifolium var. marifolium

Helianthemum origanifolium var. marifolium, commonly known as the marjoram-leaved sunflower, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in dense clusters. The plant has aromatic leaves that resemble those of marjoram, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
