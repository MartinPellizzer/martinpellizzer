Helianthemum versicolor

Helianthemum versicolor, commonly known as the variable rockrose, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub with woody stems and small, evergreen leaves. The plant produces showy, brightly colored flowers that range from yellow to red, depending on the variety and environmental conditions. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Helianthemum versicolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh, sunny conditions.
