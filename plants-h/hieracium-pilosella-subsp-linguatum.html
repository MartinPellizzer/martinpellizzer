Hieracium pilosella subsp. linguatum

Hieracium pilosella subsp.

linguatum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
