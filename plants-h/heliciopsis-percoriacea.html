Heliciopsis percoriacea

Heliciopsis percoriacea, commonly known as the glossy ash-wood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage with a striking glossy appearance. The plant has a compact growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruits are woody capsules that split open to release seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant.
