Hoya verticillata var. verticillata

Hoya verticillata var. verticillata, commonly known as the wax plant or porcelain plant, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and clusters of waxy, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical, with a smooth, glossy surface. This plant is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

It is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.
