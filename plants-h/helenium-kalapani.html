Helenium kalapani

Helenium kalapani, commonly known as the Kalapani helenium, is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its showy, orange to red flower heads.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in the island of Kalapani in the Andaman Sea. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces daisy-like flowers with a central disk surrounded by ray florets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Helenium kalapani is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its vibrant colors and ecological significance.
