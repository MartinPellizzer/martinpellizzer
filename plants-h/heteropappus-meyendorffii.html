Heteropappus meyendorffii

Heteropappus meyendorffii, commonly known as the mountain dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typical sunflower-like structure. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually basal, alternate, and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head.

It produces a pappus of bristles that aids in wind dispersal of its seeds.
