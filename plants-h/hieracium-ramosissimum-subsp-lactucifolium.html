Hieracium ramosissimum subsp. lactucifolium

Hieracium ramosissimum subsp.

lactucifolium, commonly known as the milkleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves that are usually hairy and have a milky sap when damaged.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinct central vein, while the stems are upright and branching.

It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.
