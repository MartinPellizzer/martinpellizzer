Hypericum chamaemyrtus subsp. chamaemyrtus

Hypericum chamaemyrtus subsp.

chamaemyrtus, commonly known as creeping St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has opposite, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
