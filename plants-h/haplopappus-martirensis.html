Haplopappus martirensis

Haplopappus martirensis, commonly known as the Mexican hairgrass, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its foliage is grass-like, with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Haplopappus martirensis plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
