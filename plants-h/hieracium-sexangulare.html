Hieracium sexangulare

Hieracium sexangulare, commonly known as the six-angled hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and small, yellowish flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough texture, often with a distinctive serrated edge. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.

This species is often considered a weed but has ecological significance in supporting pollinators.
