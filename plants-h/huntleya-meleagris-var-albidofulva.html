Huntleya meleagris var. albidofulva

Huntleya meleagris var. albidofulva, commonly known as the white-flowered huntleya, is a variant of the huntleya species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive white to pale yellow flowers, which contrast with its green foliage. The plant typically grows in shrubland habitats and thrives in well-drained soils. Its botanical features include simple, alternate leaves and a compact growth habit.

It is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique coloration and potential ornamental value.
