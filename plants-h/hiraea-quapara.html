Hiraea quapara

Hiraea quapara, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of South America.

It features a low-growing, sprawling habit with hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are usually purple or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are berries that turn from green to yellow or red as they mature.

Hiraea quapara is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and forest understories.
