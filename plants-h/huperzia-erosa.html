Huperzia erosa

Huperzia erosa, commonly known as the slender clubmoss, is a species of vascular plant belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is a small, evergreen fern-like plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant has simple, linear leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. Huperzia erosa is characterized by its spore-producing structures called strobila, which are located at the tips of its branches.

This plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also of interest in botanical studies due to its ancient lineage and unique reproductive features.
