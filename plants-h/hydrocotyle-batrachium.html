Hydrocotyle batrachium

Hydrocotyle batrachium, commonly known as the frog's-bit, is a small aquatic plant native to the Americas.

It typically grows in shallow, still or slow-moving freshwater habitats such as ponds and marshes. The plant has floating leaves that are divided into lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Hydrocotyle batrachium is often used in water gardens and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
