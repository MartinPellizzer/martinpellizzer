Hovea longifolia var. normalis

Hovea longifolia var. normalis, commonly known as the long-leaved hovea, is a flowering plant native to parts of Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and pod-like fruits. The plant typically features compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink or white. The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
