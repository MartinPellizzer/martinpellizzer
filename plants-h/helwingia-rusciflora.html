Helwingia rusciflora

Helwingia rusciflora, commonly known as the silverleaf helwingia, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits in the fall. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas at high altitudes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
