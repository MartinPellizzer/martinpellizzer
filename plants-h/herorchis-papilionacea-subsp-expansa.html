Herorchis papilionacea subsp. expansa

Herorchis papilionacea subsp.

expansa, commonly known as the broadleaf orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and showy, butterfly-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its floral structure includes three sepals and three petals, with the latter often modified into a lip.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
