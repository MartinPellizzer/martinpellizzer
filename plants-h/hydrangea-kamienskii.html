Hydrangea kamienskii

Hydrangea kamienskii, commonly known as the Korean hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to Korea.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from blue to pink depending on soil pH. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and features serrated, ovate leaves that are dark green in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding ornamental value to gardens and natural landscapes.
