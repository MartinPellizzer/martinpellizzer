Hibiscus rosa-sinensis albovariegatus

Hibiscus rosa-sinensis albovariegatus, commonly known as the white-veined hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range from white to pink and red, with distinctive white veins. The leaves are typically green with white or pale green variegation, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun.

This plant is often cultivated for its beauty in gardens and as a houseplant.
