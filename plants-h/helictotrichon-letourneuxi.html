Helictotrichon letourneuxi

Helictotrichon letourneuxi, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and tufted growth habit, often forming dense mats on rocky soils. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous narrow, spikelet-like flowers. It thrives in full sun and is adapted to dry, well-drained environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of alpine and subalpine ecosystems.
