Hieracium cordatum subsp. leptocoleum

Hieracium cordatum subsp.

leptocoleum, commonly known as the slender-stemmed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant has hairy, upright stems and basal leaves that are heart-shaped, with a rough texture. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and are produced from late spring to early autumn.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some traditional uses.
