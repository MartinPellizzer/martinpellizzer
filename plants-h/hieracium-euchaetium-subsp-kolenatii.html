Hieracium euchaetium subsp. kolenatii

Hieracium euchaetium subsp.

kolenatii, commonly known as the Kolenati's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and flowering heads composed of yellow disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows. It has a fibrous root system and produces numerous seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
