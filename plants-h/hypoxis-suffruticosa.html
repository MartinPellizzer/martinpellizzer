Hypoxis suffruticosa

Hypoxis suffruticosa, commonly known as the African star-of-Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect, woody stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or yellowish flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Hypoxis suffruticosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural settings.
