Hibiscus micranthus var. rigidus

Hibiscus micranthus var. rigidus, commonly known as the rigid hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of the Middle East.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming from summer to autumn. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Hibiscus micranthus var. rigidus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
