Hypolytrum sphaerostachyum

Hypolytrum sphaerostachyum, commonly known as the globe-headed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and a dense cluster of spikelets at the top of its flowering stems. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its inflorescence consists of spherical or globose clusters of small, pale brown flowers, which are often surrounded by bracts.

Hypolytrum sphaerostachyum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
