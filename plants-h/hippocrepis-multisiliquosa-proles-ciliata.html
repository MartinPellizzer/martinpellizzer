Hippocrepis multisiliquosa proles ciliata

Hippocrepis multisiliquosa proles ciliata, commonly known as the ciliated many-seedling rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and features small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its leaves are usually pinnately divided, and the fruit is a long, slender silique with a hairy or ciliate edge.
