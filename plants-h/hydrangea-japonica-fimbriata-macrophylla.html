Hydrangea japonica fimbriata-macrophylla

Hydrangea japonica fimbriata-macrophylla, commonly known as the Japanese hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from blue to pink depending on soil pH. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
