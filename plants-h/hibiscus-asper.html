Hibiscus asper

Hibiscus asper, commonly known as the rough hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and large, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate in shape, with a rough texture, and the flowers are usually pink to red in color.

Hibiscus asper is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
