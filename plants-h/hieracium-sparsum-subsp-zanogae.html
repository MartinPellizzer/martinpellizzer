Hieracium sparsum subsp. zanogae

Hieracium sparsum subsp.

zanogae, commonly known as the Zanogae hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of northern Africa. It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, disturbed soils, and rocky areas.

The plant has erect, hairy stems with alternate leaves that are usually lanceolate or ovate in shape. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and are borne on long pedicels.

It is characterized by its finely divided leaves and is often found in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
