Helichrysum xylocladum

Helichrysum xylocladum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its vibrant yellow-orange flowers that remain intact even after drying. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its resilience and ornamental value make it a popular choice in horticulture.
