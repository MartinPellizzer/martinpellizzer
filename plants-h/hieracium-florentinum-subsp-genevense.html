Hieracium florentinum subsp. genevense

Hieracium florentinum subsp.

genevense, commonly known as the Dutch hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to orange-red composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. It has alternate, heart-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces and produces achenes as its fruits.

This subspecies is often found in the Netherlands and parts of Germany, where it is part of the local flora.
