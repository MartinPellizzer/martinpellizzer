Hieracium elisaeanum subsp. balearicum

Hieracium elisaeanum subsp.

balearicum, commonly known as the Balearic hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the Balearic Islands. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish-orange composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas. It has rough, hairy leaves and produces small, inconspicuous florets arranged in a composite head.

This subspecies is relatively rare and is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique ecological adaptations.
