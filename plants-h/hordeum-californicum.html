Hordeum californicum

Hordeum californicum, commonly known as California barley, is a species of grass native to the western United States.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as hillsides and coastal areas. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers, which are often tinged with red or purple hues. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Hordeum californicum is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing food for native wildlife.
