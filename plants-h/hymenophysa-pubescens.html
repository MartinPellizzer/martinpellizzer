Hymenophysa pubescens

Hymenophysa pubescens, commonly known as the silky mallow, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the stems are covered in fine, pubescent hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
