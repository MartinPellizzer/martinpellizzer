Houstonia patens var. pusilla

Houstonia patens var. pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf blue-eyed grass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, delicate blue or purple flowers in late spring. The plant typically thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded woodland areas. Its compact size and floral display make it a desirable ornamental plant in gardens.

It is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
