Hypericum pseudoperforatum

Hypericum pseudoperforatum, commonly known as St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer, and its opposite, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in sunny, well-drained soils and can reach heights of up to 1 meter. It is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in the treatment of mild depression.

However, it contains compounds that can interact with various medications, requiring caution in its use.
