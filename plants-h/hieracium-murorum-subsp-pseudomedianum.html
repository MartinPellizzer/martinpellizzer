Hieracium murorum subsp. pseudomedianum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

pseudomedianum, commonly known as the False Common Hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and waste areas, and is often found in temperate climates. It has hairy stems and leaves that are often glandular, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The subspecies pseudomedianum is distinguished from other members of the species by its specific leaf morphology and flowering characteristics.
