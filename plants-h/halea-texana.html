Halea texana

Halea texana, commonly known as Texas halea, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Halea texana is often found in grasslands and shrublands, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
