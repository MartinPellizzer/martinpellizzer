Hesperis assoana

Hesperis assoana, commonly known as the Alaskan candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of white or pale pink blooms.

Hesperis assoana is often found in alpine tundra and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
