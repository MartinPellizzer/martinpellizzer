Hippeastrum tweedieanum

Hippeastrum tweedieanum, commonly known as the Amazonian belladonna lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It features large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, typically in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces a single, elongated flower stem that rises from a basal cluster of leaves. Its bulbs are adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments with well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in warm climates.
