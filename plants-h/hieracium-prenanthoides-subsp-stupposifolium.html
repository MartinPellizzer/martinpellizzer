Hieracium prenanthoides subsp. stupposifolium

Hieracium prenanthoides subsp.

stupposifolium, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, reaching heights of up to 30 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

It is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a common weed in disturbed habitats.
