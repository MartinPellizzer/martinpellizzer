Hymenaea altissima

Hymenaea altissima, commonly known as the Brazil nut tree, is a large evergreen tree native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its thick, flaky bark and pinnately compound leaves. The tree produces woody, pendulous pods that contain the edible Brazil nuts. Its flowers are small and yellowish-green, arranged in dense, terminal clusters.

The plant plays a vital role in the ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various species.
