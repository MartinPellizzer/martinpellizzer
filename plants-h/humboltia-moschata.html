Humboltia moschata

Humboltia moschata, commonly known as the sweet-smelling nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems, lance-shaped leaves, and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. Its fruit is a fleshy, yellow or orange berry that contains several seeds.

Humboltia moschata is often cultivated for its aromatic foliage and ornamental value.
