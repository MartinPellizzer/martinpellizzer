Hieracium planilingua

Hieracium planilingua, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while its yellow flowers are arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

Hieracium planilingua is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it an invasive species in some regions.
