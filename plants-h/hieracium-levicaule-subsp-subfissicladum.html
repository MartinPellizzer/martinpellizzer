Hieracium levicaule subsp. subfissicladum

Hieracium levicaule subsp.

subfissicladum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and leaves with a distinctive lobed structure.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows. Its leaves are linear to lanceolate, often with a prominent central vein, and the flowers are yellow, arranged in a single head.

It is a member of the genus Hieracium, which includes many species of hawkweeds known for their aggressive growth and ecological impact.
