Hieracium carnosum subsp. pseudodollineri

Hieracium carnosum subsp.

pseudodollineri, commonly known as the Dolomites hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of the Dolomites in Italy. It is characterized by its hairy, often reddish stems and basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish composite flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in rocky, nutrient-poor soils at high altitudes, showcasing adaptations to harsh mountain environments.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species in alpine flora.
