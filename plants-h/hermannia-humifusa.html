Hermannia humifusa

Hermannia humifusa, commonly known as the groundsel or groundsel plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories or along stream banks. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, with prominent central disks. Its leaves are usually alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Hermannia humifusa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
