Hieracium stenotaeniceps

Hieracium stenotaeniceps, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy structures. The plant typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring temperate climates. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

Hieracium stenotaeniceps is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
