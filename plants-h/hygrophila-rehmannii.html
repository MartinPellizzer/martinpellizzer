Hygrophila rehmannii

Hygrophila rehmannii, commonly known as the water hyssop, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It thrives in moist or aquatic environments, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of streams. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy, and it produces small, clustered flowers in shades of blue or purple. Hygrophila rehmannii is known for its ability to tolerate varying water conditions, making it a popular choice for water gardens and wetland restoration projects.

Its rapid growth and adaptability contribute to its ecological importance in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic life.
