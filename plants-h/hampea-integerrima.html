Hampea integerrima

Hampea integerrima, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to blue, often blooming in the early morning. The plant is a climbing vine with hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves, and it produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses worldwide.
