Hoffmannseggella × feldmanniana

Hoffmannseggella × feldmanniana, commonly known as the hybrid hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe.

It is a natural hybrid between Crataegus monogyna (common hawthorn) and Crataegus laevigata (smooth hawthorn), resulting in a plant with distinctive botanical features. The species exhibits characteristics of both parent plants, including thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruits are small, red berries that mature in autumn, and the plant is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

Hoffmannseggella × feldmanniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting wildlife.
