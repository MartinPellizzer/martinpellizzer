Helianthus tuberosus var. alexandri

Helianthus tuberosus var. alexandri, commonly known as the Alexander's sunflower, is a variant of the sunflower plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers and robust, hairy stems. The plant produces edible tubers that are rich in starch and have been historically used by indigenous peoples. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often growing in prairies and open fields.

This variety is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its agricultural potential.
