Hedysarum lappaceum

Hedysarum lappaceum, commonly known as the alpine peavine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Hedysarum lappaceum thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
