Hibiscus grossularifolius

Hibiscus grossularifolius, commonly known as the roughleaf hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with a rough, textured surface. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to deep red, depending on the variety. Hibiscus grossularifolius thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
