Hymenostephium woronowii

Hymenostephium woronowii, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that resemble bells, which bloom in clusters. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. It is typically found in moist, tropical environments and can grow up to several meters in height.

The bark is smooth and often exhibits a reddish or brownish hue.
