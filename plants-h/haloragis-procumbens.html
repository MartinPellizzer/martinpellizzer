Haloragis procumbens

Haloragis procumbens, commonly known as the prostrate water starwort, is a flowering plant native to parts of Australia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically thrives in wet or waterlogged soils, often found in swampy areas or along the edges of water bodies. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale blue, which bloom during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are narrow and arranged in a prostrate manner, allowing it to spread across the ground.

Haloragis procumbens plays a role in stabilizing soil in aquatic environments and provides habitat for various small invertebrates.
