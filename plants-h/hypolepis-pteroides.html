Hypolepis pteroides

Hypolepis pteroides, commonly known as the rock fern or cliff fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and its ability to thrive in rocky or shaded environments. The plant has pinnate fronds with a distinctive, finely divided structure, and its spores are produced on the undersides of the leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and moderate moisture, making it suitable for rock gardens and shaded landscapes.

Hypolepis pteroides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to colonize difficult terrain.
