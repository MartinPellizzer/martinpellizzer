Hordeum vulgare var. ismailii

Hordeum vulgare var. ismailii, commonly known as the Ismailii barley, is a variety of barley characterized by its unique botanical features.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is native to regions of Central Asia. This variety typically exhibits robust growth with tall, sturdy stalks and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The grains are usually small and hard, adapted to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.

Its genetic makeup reflects adaptations to specific climatic conditions, making it a subject of interest in agricultural research.
