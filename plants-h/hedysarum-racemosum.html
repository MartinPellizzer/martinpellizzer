Hedysarum racemosum

Hedysarum racemosum, commonly known as the clustered sweetclover, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in racemes. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility. It thrives in a variety of soils but prefers well-drained conditions.

Hedysarum racemosum is often found in grasslands, woodland edges, and disturbed sites.
