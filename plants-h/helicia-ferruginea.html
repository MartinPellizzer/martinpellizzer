Helicia ferruginea

Helicia ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty helicia, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Theaceae and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Helicia ferruginea is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.
