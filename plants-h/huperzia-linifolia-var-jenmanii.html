Huperzia linifolia var. jenmanii

Huperzia linifolia var. jenmanii, commonly known as the Jenman's clubmoss, is a rare and ancient species belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, scale-like leaves that form dense, low-growing mats. This variety is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia, where it thrives in shaded, moist forest environments. The plant exhibits a unique reproductive structure known as a strobilus, which contains the sporangia for spore production.

Its conservation status is of concern due to habitat loss and limited distribution.
