Hieracium sparsum subsp. campanelliforme

Hieracium sparsum subsp.

campanelliforme, commonly known as the bell-shaped hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces, and the flowers are yellow with a distinctive bell-like shape.

It is known for its ornamental value and is often found in gardens and natural habitats.
