Hirtella thouarsiana subsp. abrupte-acuminata

Hirtella thouarsiana subsp.

abrupte-acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-pointed hirtella, is a species native to tropical regions of South America. It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that contains several seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in shaded garden environments.
