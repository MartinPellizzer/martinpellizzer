Hemizonia corymbosa var. macrocephala

Hemizonia corymbosa var. macrocephala, commonly known as the large-headed hemizonia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like inflorescences. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture for its vibrant floral display.
