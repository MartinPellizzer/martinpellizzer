Hieracium valdepilosum subsp. willdenowianum

Hieracium valdepilosum subsp.

willdenowianum, commonly known as the willdenow's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to orange flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, and it produces small, daisy-like flowers in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is often found in disturbed habitats and is considered a common weed in agricultural areas.
