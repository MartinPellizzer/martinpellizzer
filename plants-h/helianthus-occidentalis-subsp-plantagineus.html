Helianthus occidentalis subsp. plantagineus

Helianthus occidentalis subsp.

plantagineus, commonly known as the western sunflower, is a perennial plant native to the western United States. It features large, showy yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of smaller, tubular flowers.

The plant typically grows up to 3 meters tall, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and ornamental addition to gardens.
