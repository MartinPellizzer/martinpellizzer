Hieracium canadense var. fasciculatum

Hieracium canadense var. fasciculatum, commonly known as Canadian hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks. It has a creeping rootstock that allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense mats.

Its flowers bloom from June to September, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
