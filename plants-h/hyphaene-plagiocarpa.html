Hyphaene plagiocarpa

Hyphaene plagiocarpa, commonly known as the African palm or the Natal palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of long, feather-like leaves. The plant produces yellowish flowers that develop into large, woody fruits containing a single seed. It is native to eastern and southern Africa, where it often grows in coastal areas and sandy soils.

Hyphaene plagiocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally for its fibers and as a source of food.
