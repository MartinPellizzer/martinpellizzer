Hakea ulicina var. carinata

Hakea ulicina var. carinata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hakea, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces dense clusters of bright yellow flowers, which appear in late winter to early spring. It has a woody structure with persistent, spiny branches.

This variety is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.
