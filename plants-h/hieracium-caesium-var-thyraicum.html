Hieracium caesium var. thyraicum

Hieracium caesium var. thyraicum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun. It has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy, with a rosette formation.

The flowering stems are usually erect and can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters.
