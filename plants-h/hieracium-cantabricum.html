Hieracium cantabricum

Hieracium cantabricum, commonly known as the Cantabrian hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosette of leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Hieracium cantabricum is adapted to temperate climates and is relatively resilient to environmental stressors.
