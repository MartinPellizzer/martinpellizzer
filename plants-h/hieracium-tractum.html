Hieracium tractum

Hieracium tractum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant features upright, hairy stems and basal leaves that are typically heart-shaped or ovate. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, attracting various pollinators.

Hieracium tractum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it can spread aggressively through rhizomes.
