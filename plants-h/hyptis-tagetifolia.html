Hyptis tagetifolia

Hyptis tagetifolia, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, blue or violet flowers. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its foliage is frequently used in herbal teas and as a natural remedy for various ailments.
