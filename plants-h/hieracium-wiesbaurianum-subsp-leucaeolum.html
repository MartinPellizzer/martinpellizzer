Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp. leucaeolum

Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp.

leucaeolum, commonly known as the white-centred hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its bright yellow ray florets and a central disk that is often white or pale yellow.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. It has basal leaves that are usually lance-shaped and may have hairy surfaces.

This subspecies is notable for its distinctive coloration and is found primarily in Europe and parts of Asia.
