Huperzia pflanzii

Huperzia pflanzii, commonly known as the Pflanz's clubmoss, is a species of evergreen fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems that produce erect, spore-bearing cones at the tips. Native to regions of Southeast Asia, including China, India, and the Himalayas, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically linear and arranged in whorls, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Huperzia pflanzii is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
