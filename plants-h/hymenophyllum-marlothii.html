Hymenophyllum marlothii

Hymenophyllum marlothii, commonly known as the Marloth's filmy fern, is a small, epiphytic fern native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically less than 10 cm in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on trees or rocks in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds and release spores for propagation.

The plant's unique morphology and ecological niche make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
