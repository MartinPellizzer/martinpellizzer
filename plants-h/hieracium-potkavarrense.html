Hieracium potkavarrense

Hieracium potkavarrense, commonly known as the Potkavaran hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus, particularly in Georgia. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in late summer. It thrives in rocky and well-drained soils, often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
