Hexachlamys humilis var. tomentosa

Hexachlamys humilis var. tomentosa, commonly known as the hairy hexachlamys, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
