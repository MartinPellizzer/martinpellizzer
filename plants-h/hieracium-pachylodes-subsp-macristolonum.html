Hieracium pachylodes subsp. macristolonum

Hieracium pachylodes subsp.

macristolonum, commonly known as the Macristolonum hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features a rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences. It is characterized by its robust, fleshy stems and is often found in disturbed or semi-arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and locally endemic species.
