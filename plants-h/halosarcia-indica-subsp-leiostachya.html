Halosarcia indica subsp. leiostachya

Halosarcia indica subsp.

leiostachya, commonly known as the coastal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa. It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to saline environments.

The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes.
