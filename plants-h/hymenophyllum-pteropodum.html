Hymenophyllum pteropodum

Hymenophyllum pteropodum, commonly known as the silver moss, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically covered in a dense layer of silky, white trichomes, giving it a silvery appearance. This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found on rocks or tree trunks in tropical and subtropical regions. Its small, inconspicuous spores are released from the underdeveloped sporangia located on the underside of its fronds.

Hymenophyllum pteropodum plays an important ecological role in maintaining moisture and providing habitat for microorganisms in its native ecosystems.
