Hieracium murorum subsp. marginelliceps

Hieracium murorum subsp.

marginelliceps, commonly known as the marginated hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems.

The subspecies is distinguished by its distinctive leaf margins, which are often lobed or toothed. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade.

The plant has a fibrous root system and produces numerous small, inconspicuous seeds that are dispersed by the wind.
