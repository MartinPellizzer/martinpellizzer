Hedysarum ulconburulicum

Hedysarum ulconburulicum, commonly known as the narrowleaf sweet clover, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a deep root system, allowing it to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to cold climates.

Hedysarum ulconburulicum plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for wildlife.
