Hibiscus palmatilobus

Hibiscus palmatilobus, commonly known as the broad-lobed hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming during the wet season. Hibiscus palmatilobus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
