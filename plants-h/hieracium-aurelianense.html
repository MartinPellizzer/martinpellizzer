Hieracium aurelianense

Hieracium aurelianense, commonly known as the Aurelian Groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its yellow-orange flower heads consist of numerous small florets, and it produces achenes as its seeds.

The species is often found in open habitats and is relatively common in its native range.
