Hypechusa pannonica

Hypechusa pannonica, commonly known as the Pannonian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along riverbanks, preferring well-drained soil. Its leaves are lanceolate and arranged alternately along the stem.
