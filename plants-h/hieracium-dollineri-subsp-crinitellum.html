Hieracium dollineri subsp. crinitellum

Hieracium dollineri subsp.

crinitellum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to orange-red flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in some regions.
