Hordeum hirsutum

Hordeum hirsutum, commonly known as wild barley, is a species of grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and loose clusters of spikelets that contain both male and female flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. Its leaves are narrow and have a rough, hairy texture, which contributes to its common name.

Hordeum hirsutum plays a role in ecological systems and has been studied for its potential in agriculture and genetic research.
