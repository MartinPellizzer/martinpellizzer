Hieracium lachenalii subsp. purpureoviolascens

Hieracium lachenalii subsp.

purpureoviolascens, commonly known as the purple-violet hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and tall, branched stems.

The flowers are typically yellow to orange, with ray florets that may have a purplish tinge, giving it its common name. It thrives in grassy areas and open habitats, often found in disturbed soils.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
