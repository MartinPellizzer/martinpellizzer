Hedyotis centranthoides f. diffusa

Hedyotis centranthoides f. diffusa, commonly known as the spreading dotweed, is a low-growing herbaceous perennial native to parts of Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly rough texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along streambanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
