Hieracium diabolinum subsp. sublongifolium

Hieracium diabolinum subsp.

sublongifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges.

The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in summer. Its leaves are often covered with fine hairs, and the stems are upright with a slightly rough texture.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaf arrangement.
