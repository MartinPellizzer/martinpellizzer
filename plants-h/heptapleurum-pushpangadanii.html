Heptapleurum pushpangadanii

Heptapleurum pushpangadanii, commonly known as the Indian nettle, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
