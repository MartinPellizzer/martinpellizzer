Halleria tetragona

Halleria tetragona, commonly known as the four-angled halleria, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth surface and a distinctive four-sided stem. Its flowers are small, clustered in inflorescences, and are usually white or pale pink in color.

Halleria tetragona is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
