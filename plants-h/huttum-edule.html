Huttum edule

Huttum edule, commonly known as the edible nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant features hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slightly rough texture. Its flowers are usually violet or purple, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible when ripe.

The plant is valued for its nutritional content and has been used in traditional cuisines for its mild, slightly sweet flavor.
