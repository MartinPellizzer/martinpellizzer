Hepetis punicea

Hepetis punicea, commonly known as the scarlet beakviolet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bright red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has slender, upright stems and heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are tubular and have a distinctive beak-like structure, which gives it its common name.

Hepetis punicea thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
