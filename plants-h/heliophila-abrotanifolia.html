Heliophila abrotanifolia

Heliophila abrotanifolia, commonly known as sweetroot or white heliophila, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, while the roots are fibrous and aromatic.

Heliophila abrotanifolia is valued in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
