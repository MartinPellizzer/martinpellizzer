Hoya viridiflora

Hoya viridiflora, commonly known as the Emerald Flower Hoya, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy, ovate leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, waxy, greenish-white flowers that are borne on terminal inflorescences. It is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

Hoya viridiflora is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
