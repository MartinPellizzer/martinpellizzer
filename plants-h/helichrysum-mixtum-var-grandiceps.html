Helichrysum mixtum var. grandiceps

Helichrysum mixtum var. grandiceps, commonly known as the golden marguerite, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces abundant yellow-orange flowers that resemble daisies. The plant features silver-gray, aromatic foliage with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

The flowers are long-lasting and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
