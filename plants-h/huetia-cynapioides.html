Huetia cynapioides

Huetia cynapioides, commonly known as the Cuban onion, is a herbaceous plant native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its strap-like, aromatic leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. Its underground bulb is edible and has a mild onion-like flavor.

Huetia cynapioides thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental and culinary plant in tropical regions.
