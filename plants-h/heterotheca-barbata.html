Heterotheca barbata

Heterotheca barbata, commonly known as the hairy coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the central and southern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Heterotheca barbata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
