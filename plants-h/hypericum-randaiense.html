Hypericum randaiense

Hypericum randaiense, commonly known as the Japanese St. John's Wort, is a shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Hypericaceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and woody stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are opposite and oval-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Hypericum randaiense is noted for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
