Hieracium morii

Hieracium morii, commonly known as the Morii hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and a single, erect stem that bears yellowish to reddish flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has small, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Hieracium morii is native to Europe and is considered a perennial herb with a relatively short flowering period.
