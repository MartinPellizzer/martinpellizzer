Haworthia mucronata f. inermis

Haworthia mucronata f. inermis, commonly known as the Zulu bulb, is a compact, slow-growing succulent native to South Africa.

It features a rosette of thick, fleshy, bluish-green leaves with white or pale green markings. The plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers on tall, slender stems during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for rock gardens or container cultivation.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
