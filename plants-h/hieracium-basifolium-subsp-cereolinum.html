Hieracium basifolium subsp. cereolinum

Hieracium basifolium subsp.

cereolinum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are basal, with a rough texture, and the flowers are yellow, often with a distinctive central disc.

It is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
