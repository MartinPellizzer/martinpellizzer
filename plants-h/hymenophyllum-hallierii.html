Hymenophyllum hallierii

Hymenophyllum hallierii, commonly known as the silver fern, is a small, epiphytic fern native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on rocks or tree trunks. Its leaves are typically less than 10 centimeters in length and have a distinctive, intricate structure.

Hymenophyllum hallierii is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
