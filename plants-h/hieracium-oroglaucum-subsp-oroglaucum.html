Hieracium oroglaucum subsp. oroglaucum

Hieracium oroglaucum subsp.

oroglaucum, commonly known as alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and tall, slender flowering stems that bear yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually covered with fine hairs, and the flowers consist of both ray and disk florets.

It is often found in mountainous areas and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
