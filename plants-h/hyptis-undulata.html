Hyptis undulata

Hyptis undulata, commonly known as the curlyleaf hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its serrated, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Hyptis undulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
