Hosackia oblongifolia var. cuprea

Hosackia oblongifolia var. cuprea, commonly known as the coppery false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a slight bluish tinge. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of purple to pink, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system consists of long, fibrous roots that help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
