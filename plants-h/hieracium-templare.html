Hieracium templare

Hieracium templare, commonly known as the Temple Hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are rough and hairy, with a distinctive serrated edge, and its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium templare is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
