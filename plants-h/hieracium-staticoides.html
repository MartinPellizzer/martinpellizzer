Hieracium staticoides

Hieracium staticoides, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. It has a creeping growth habit with a central stem that produces multiple flowering shoots.

Hieracium staticoides is often found in temperate regions of Europe and is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed habitats.
