Hypericum montanum var. athoum

Hypericum montanum var. athoum, commonly known as the alpine St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly serrated, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

It is known for its medicinal properties, containing hypericin and other compounds with potential therapeutic uses.
