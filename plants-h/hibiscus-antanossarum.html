Hibiscus antanossarum

Hibiscus antanossarum, commonly known as the Madagascar hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, featuring thick, woody stems and large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, or yellow, which bloom during the rainy season. Its flowers are followed by capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Hibiscus antanossarum is adapted to the tropical climate of Madagascar and is often found in moist, forested areas.
