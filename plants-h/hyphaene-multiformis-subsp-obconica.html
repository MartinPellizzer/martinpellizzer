Hyphaene multiformis subsp. obconica

Hyphaene multiformis subsp.

obconica, commonly known as the African fan palm, is a tropical plant native to parts of Africa. It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves and a robust, trunk-like structure.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clustered, edible fruits. Its growth habit is typically solitary, with a tall, slender stem.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
