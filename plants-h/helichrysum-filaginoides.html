Helichrysum filaginoides

Helichrysum filaginoides, commonly known as the golden crown bush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, yellow-orange inflorescences that resemble small suns. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its foliage is aromatic and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is also valued for its ornamental use in gardens and its potential medicinal properties.
