Haageocereus chryseus

Haageocereus chryseus, commonly known as the golden night bloomer, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems and is known for its striking yellow flowers that open at night. The plant has a spiny exterior and is adapted to arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. Its flowers are typically short-lived but emit a strong fragrance to attract nocturnal pollinators.

Haageocereus chryseus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming behavior.
