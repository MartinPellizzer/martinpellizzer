Helianthemum oelandicum subsp. glabratum

Helianthemum oelandicum subsp.

glabratum, commonly known as the smooth rockrose, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Europe. It features small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has compact, evergreen foliage with thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in dry conditions. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or Mediterranean climates.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to harsh environments, contributing to its resilience and ornamental value.
