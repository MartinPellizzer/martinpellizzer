Humiria amplexicaulis

Humiria amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping swamp orchid, is a unique flowering plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its basal leaves that wrap around the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which bloom during the wet season. Humiria amplexicaulis thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in marshy areas.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
