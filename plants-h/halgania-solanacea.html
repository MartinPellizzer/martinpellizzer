Halgania solanacea

Halgania solanacea, commonly known as the purple morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the nightshade family (Solanaceae).

It is native to tropical regions of the Americas and is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that open in the morning. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces small, berry-like fruits that contain seeds. Its botanical features include hairy stems and showy, trumpet-shaped blossoms that attract pollinators.

Halgania solanacea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
