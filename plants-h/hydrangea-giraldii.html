Hydrangea giraldii

Hydrangea giraldii, commonly known as the mountain hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that range from white to pink and blue, depending on soil pH. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or mountainous areas. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with serrated edges, and it produces small, white flowers in late summer.

Hydrangea giraldii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
