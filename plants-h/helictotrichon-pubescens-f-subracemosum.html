Helictotrichon pubescens f. subracemosum

Helictotrichon pubescens f. subracemosum, commonly known as the hairy bluegrass, is a variant of the common bluegrass species.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems. The leaves are typically narrow, with a hairy texture that gives the plant its common name. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny blue to purple flowers.

This plant is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and roadsides, and it is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
