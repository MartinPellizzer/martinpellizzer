Hartiana caroliniana

Hartiana caroliniana, commonly known as the Carolina lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, white to pale pink flowers with a distinctive, sweet fragrance. The plant typically grows in wetlands and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its blooming period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hartiana caroliniana is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
