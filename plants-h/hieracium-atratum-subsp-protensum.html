Hieracium atratum subsp. protensum

Hieracium atratum subsp.

protensum, commonly known as the dark hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features basal rosettes of dark green, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences. Its flowers are typically tubular with five lobes, and the plant is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.

It is also a host plant for several species of Lepidoptera.
