Hyphaene multiformis

Hyphaene multiformis, commonly known as the African date palm, is a species of palm native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach several meters in diameter. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clustered fruits resembling dates. Its fibrous leaves are used in traditional crafts, while the fruit is edible and contains a sweet, starchy pulp.

Hyphaene multiformis is adapted to arid environments and plays an important ecological role in its native habitats.
