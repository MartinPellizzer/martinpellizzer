Hibiscus tiliaceus var. tortuosus

Hibiscus tiliaceus var. tortuosus, commonly known as the mangrove hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to coastal regions of the Indo-Pacific.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in saline environments, often growing in mangrove swamps and estuaries. Its roots are adapted to tolerate waterlogged and brackish conditions, making it an important component of coastal ecosystems.

It also serves as a habitat and food source for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
