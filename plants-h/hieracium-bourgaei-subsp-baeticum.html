Hieracium bourgaei subsp. baeticum

Hieracium bourgaei subsp.

baeticum, commonly known as the Baetican hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish-orange composite flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
