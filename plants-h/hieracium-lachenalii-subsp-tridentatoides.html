Hieracium lachenalii subsp. tridentatoides

Hieracium lachenalii subsp.

tridentatoides, commonly known as the three-toothed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and southern Europe. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering stems that bear yellow composite flowers.

The leaves are typically lobed and have a rough, hairy texture, while the flower heads consist of numerous small disc flowers surrounded by ray florets. This subspecies is often found in grasslands and open habitats, preferring well-drained soils.

It is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
