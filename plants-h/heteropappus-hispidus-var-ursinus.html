Heteropappus hispidus var. ursinus

Heteropappus hispidus var. ursinus, commonly known as the roughleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is an annual herb characterized by its hairy stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are typically arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it an important species for pollinators and a subject of ecological study.
