Hedyotis auricularia subsp. venosa

Hedyotis auricularia subsp.

venosa, commonly known as the slender mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees. It has small, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are small, white or pale green, arranged in clusters. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature.

It is native to parts of Asia and is often found in forested areas where it can attach to suitable hosts.
