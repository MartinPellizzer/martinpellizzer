Hieracium levicaule subsp. lucens

Hieracium levicaule subsp.

lucens, commonly known as the luminous hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The subspecies lucens is distinguished by its more elongated leaves and slightly larger flowers compared to other members of the species.
