Hibiscus cupularis

Hibiscus cupularis, commonly known as the cupular hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink or red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsules that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Hibiscus cupularis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
