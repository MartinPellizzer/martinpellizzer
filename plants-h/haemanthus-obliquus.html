Haemanthus obliquus

Haemanthus obliquus, commonly known as the purple fireplant, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, strap-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color, with a distinctive purple sheen. The plant produces clusters of bright red or orange flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its unique appearance and seasonal blooming make it a notable ornamental plant in horticulture.
