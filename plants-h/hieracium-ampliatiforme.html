Hieracium ampliatiforme

Hieracium ampliatiforme, commonly known as the broad-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, spoon-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in dense clusters. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grasslands. It has hairy stems and is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils.

Its flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn, attracting various pollinators.
