Humboltia crocodiliceps

Its most distinctive feature is its large, showy flowers, which are typically orange or red with a prominent, curling lip that resembles a crocodile's mouth. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, toxic seeds, a trait common among members of its family.

Humboltia crocodiliceps thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found growing in the understory of tropical rainforests.
