Hemigraphis glandulosa

Hemigraphis glandulosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf sorrel, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Hemigraphis glandulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its aesthetic qualities.
