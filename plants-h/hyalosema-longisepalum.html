Hyalosema longisepalum

Hyalosema longisepalum, commonly known as the long-sepaled orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, elongated sepals and petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its flowers are often fragrant and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
