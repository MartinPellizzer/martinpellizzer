Hemionitis lumholtzii

Hemionitis lumholtzii, commonly known as the desert shield fern, is a unique fern species native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It thrives in harsh, dry environments, often growing on rocky substrates or in crevices where moisture is scarce. The plant has a compact, rosette-like growth form with small, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss. Its fronds are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited water availability.

Hemionitis lumholtzii plays a vital ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing microhabitats for desert flora and fauna.
