Hakea tricruris

Hakea tricruris, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hakea, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces striking, red or orange flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Hakea tricruris is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.
