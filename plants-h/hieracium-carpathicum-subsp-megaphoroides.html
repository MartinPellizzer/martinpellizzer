Hieracium carpathicum subsp. megaphoroides

Hieracium carpathicum subsp.

megaphoroides, commonly known as the Carpathian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Carpathian Mountains. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and forest edges. It has hairy stems and leaves with serrated margins, and its flowers bloom from June to August.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its potential use in traditional medicine.
