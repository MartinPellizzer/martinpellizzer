Heliophila lepidioides

Heliophila lepidioides, commonly known as the silverweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. The plant features small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Heliophila lepidioides is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued in ecological landscaping for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
