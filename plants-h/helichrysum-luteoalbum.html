Helichrysum luteoalbum

Helichrysum luteoalbum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that remain intact even after drying. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, narrow leaves are arranged in a compact, bushy form.

Helichrysum luteoalbum is often used in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting, vibrant appearance.
