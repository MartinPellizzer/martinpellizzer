Hordeum brevisubulatum subsp. turkestanicum

Hordeum brevisubulatum subsp.

turkestanicum, commonly known as the Turkestan barley, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its compact growth habit and short, stiff leaves.

The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with pale green to brownish flowers. Its seeds are often used in traditional agriculture and can be processed into flour.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
