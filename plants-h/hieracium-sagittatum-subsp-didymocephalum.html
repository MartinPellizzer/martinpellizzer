Hieracium sagittatum subsp. didymocephalum

Hieracium sagittatum subsp.

didymocephalum, commonly known as the two-headed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy basal rosettes.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a distinctive, elongated inflorescence, with two prominent heads that give it its common name.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
