Hedysarum spinosissimum subsp. capitatum

Hedysarum spinosissimum subsp.

capitatum, commonly known as the prickly vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region. It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant is characterized by its spiny stems and is often found in dry, rocky habitats. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.
