Hieracium sublineolatum

Hieracium sublineolatum, commonly known as the narrow-ribbed hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. It has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom from June to August.

Hieracium sublineolatum is often found in moist, sandy soils and can form dense colonies in suitable environments.
