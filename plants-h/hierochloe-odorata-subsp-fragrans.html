Hierochloe odorata subsp. fragrans

Hierochloe odorata subsp.

fragrans, commonly known as sweet grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves that emit a pleasant, spicy scent when crushed.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks. Its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, silver-green foliage make it visually distinctive.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used traditionally in various cultural practices.
