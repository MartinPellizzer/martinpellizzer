Hieracium attenuatum

Hieracium attenuatum, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

Hieracium attenuatum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in disturbed soils.
