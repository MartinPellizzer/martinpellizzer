Haloragis racemosa

Haloragis racemosa, commonly known as the clustered willow herb, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland or swampy habitats. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense racemes, which bloom during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Haloragis racemosa is adapted to waterlogged soils and plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
