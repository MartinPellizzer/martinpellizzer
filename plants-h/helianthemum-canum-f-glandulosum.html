Helianthemum canum f. glandulosum

Helianthemum canum f. glandulosum, commonly known as the glandular rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its small, fleshy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant's stems and leaves are covered in glandular hairs, which give it its specific epithet "glandulosum.". It thrives in rocky or well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
