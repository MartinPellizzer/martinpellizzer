Helictotrichon convolutum subsp. heldreichii

Helictotrichon convolutum subsp.

heldreichii, commonly known as the Heldreich's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, blue-green foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its leaves are narrow and often curling, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
