Hydrangea aspera var. robusta

Hydrangea aspera var. robusta, commonly known as the rough hydrangea, is a species native to eastern Asia, particularly China.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and large, showy inflorescences that can range from white to pink or blue depending on soil pH. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically broad, ovate, and have a rough texture, giving the plant its common name.

Hydrangea aspera var. robusta is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
