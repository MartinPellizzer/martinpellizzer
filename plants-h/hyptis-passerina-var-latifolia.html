Hyptis passerina var. latifolia

Hyptis passerina var. latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for horticultural and traditional use.
