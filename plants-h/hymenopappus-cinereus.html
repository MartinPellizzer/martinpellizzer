Hymenopappus cinereus

Hymenopappus cinereus, commonly known as the gray-hair dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and wind-dispersed seeds. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into fluffy, white pappus structures, aiding in seed distribution. Its leaves are typically gray-green and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Hymenopappus cinereus thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open habitats.
