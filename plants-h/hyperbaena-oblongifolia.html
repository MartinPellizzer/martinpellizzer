Hyperbaena oblongifolia

Hyperbaena oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed hyperbaena, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often yellow or orange in color, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in warm climates.
