Helianthus grosseserratus var. hypoleucus

Helianthus grosseserratus var. hypoleucus, commonly known as the whiteleaf sunflower, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, yellow, daisy-like flowers and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine white down. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its botanical features include a robust stem with rough, hairy surfaces and a flowering period that occurs in the summer months.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
