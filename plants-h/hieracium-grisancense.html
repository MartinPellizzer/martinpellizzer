Hieracium grisancense

Hieracium grisancense, commonly known as the gray-haired hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typically hairy stems. The plant features basal rosettes of leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, grayish hairs, contributing to its common name.

Hieracium grisancense is often found in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, thriving in temperate climates.
