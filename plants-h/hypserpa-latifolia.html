Hypserpa latifolia

Hypserpa latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf hypserpa, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically green and have a smooth surface. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are often borne on long pedicels. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Hypserpa latifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
