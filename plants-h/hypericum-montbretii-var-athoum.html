Hypericum montbretii var. athoum

Hypericum montbretii var. athoum, commonly known as the golden st. john's wort, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Europe.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are glossy and arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its botanical characteristics include compact growth and a long flowering period, contributing to its ornamental value.
