Hippeastrum minimum

Hippeastrum minimum, commonly known as the dwarf amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact size and striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of red, white, and pink. The plant produces a basal rosette of broad, fleshy leaves that store water, a feature adapted to its natural habitat. Its bulbs are underground storage organs that support the plant's growth and flowering cycle.

Hippeastrum minimum is popular as a houseplant due to its ease of care and vibrant blooms.
