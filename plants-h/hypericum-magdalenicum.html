Hypericum magdalenicum

Hypericum magdalenicum, commonly known as Magdalene's St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces bright yellow flowers with five petals. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Hypericum magdalenicum is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
