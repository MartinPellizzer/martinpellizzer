Helianthemum salicifolium var. macrosepalum

Helianthemum salicifolium var. macrosepalum, commonly known as the large-sepal rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a low-growing shrub with evergreen foliage, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, bright yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its flowers have prominent, broad sepals that are often mistaken for petals, giving it its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
