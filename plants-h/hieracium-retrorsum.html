Hieracium retrorsum

Hieracium retrorsum, commonly known as the hooked hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it can reach heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a distinctive hooked appearance on the underside, giving it its common name.

The flowers are yellow and bloom from late spring to early autumn, attracting various pollinators.
