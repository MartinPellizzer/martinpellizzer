Hyacinthus dubius

Hyacinthus dubius, commonly known as the Dutch hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers. The plant produces strap-shaped leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Hyacinthus dubius is known for its early blooming period, often flowering in late winter or early spring.
