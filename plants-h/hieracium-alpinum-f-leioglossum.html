Hieracium alpinum f. leioglossum

Hieracium alpinum f. leioglossum, commonly known as the smooth-tongued alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in open, rocky habitats at high elevations, where it thrives in cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while the flowers are yellow with a distinctive smooth, tongue-like appearance.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to harsh mountain environments and its role in local ecosystems.
