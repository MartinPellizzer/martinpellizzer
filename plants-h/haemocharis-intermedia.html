Haemocharis intermedia

Haemocharis intermedia, commonly known as the red-veined orchid, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers with red veins. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often dark green with a waxy texture.

The flowers bloom in clusters and are known for their vibrant colors, making it a popular ornamental plant.
