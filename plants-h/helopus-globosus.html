Helopus globosus

Helopus globosus, commonly known as the globe sedge, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in moist soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower spikes that bloom in the summer. Its botanical classification places it in the family Cyperaceae, commonly known as the sedge family.

Helopus globosus is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support aquatic ecosystems.
