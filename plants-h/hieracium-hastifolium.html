Hieracium hastifolium

Hieracium hastifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium hastifolium is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
