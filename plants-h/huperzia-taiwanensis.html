Huperzia taiwanensis

Huperzia taiwanensis, commonly known as the Taiwan huperzia, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen morphology with closely spaced, needle-like leaves. Native to Taiwan, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Its sporophylls are arranged in a whorled pattern, and it produces spores in specialized structures called sporangia.

The plant's unique botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological and taxonomic studies.
