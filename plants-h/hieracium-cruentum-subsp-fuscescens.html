Hieracium cruentum subsp. fuscescens

Hieracium cruentum subsp.

fuscescens, commonly known as the purple hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow to orange flower heads.

The subspecies fuscescens is distinguished by its darker, more reddish flower color compared to the typical purple hue of H. cruentum. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.

This plant is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other sunny, well-drained areas.
