Hosackia crassifolia var. crassifolia

Hosackia crassifolia var. crassifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has palmately divided, heart-shaped leaves that are broad and robust, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Hosackia crassifolia var. crassifolia is often used in native plant gardens for its ecological benefits and aesthetic appeal.
