Hyptis tetracephala

Hyptis tetracephala, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and spikes of blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

The flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
