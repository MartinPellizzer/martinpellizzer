Hieracium hirticollum

Hieracium hirticollum, commonly known as the hairy-husked hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and woodland edges, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features a basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in a dense cluster at the top of the stem. Its flowers are surrounded by leaf-like bracts, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Hieracium hirticollum is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads.
