Hieracium tynnoglochin

Hieracium tynnoglochin, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and yellow-orange composite flowers. Native to Europe, it thrives in meadows and grasslands, often in moist or shaded areas. The plant produces small, achene-like seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and a subject of botanical study.
