Hieracium knappii

Hieracium knappii, commonly known as Knapp's hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy slopes, thriving in cool, high-altitude environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disc.

Hieracium knappii is known for its ability to colonize disturbed sites and is often found in mountainous habitats across the Alps.
