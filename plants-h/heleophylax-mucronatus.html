Heleophylax mucronatus

Heleophylax mucronatus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. The plant features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. Its flowers are small and delicate, usually white or pale yellow, with three sepals and three petals.

The species is notable for its unique pollination mechanism, which involves specific insect species.
