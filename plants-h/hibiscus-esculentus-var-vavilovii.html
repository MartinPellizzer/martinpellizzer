Hibiscus esculentus var. vavilovii

Hibiscus esculentus var. vavilovii, commonly known as the African eggplant or okra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, showy flowers that are typically pink or red in color. The plant produces edible capsules that contain numerous seeds, which are often used in culinary applications. Its botanical structure includes a robust stem, hairy foliage, and a unique fruit morphology that sets it apart from other hibiscus species.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its nutritional benefits.
