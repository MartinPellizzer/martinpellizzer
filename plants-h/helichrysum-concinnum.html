Helichrysum concinnum

Helichrysum concinnum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that remain intact even after drying. The plant is typically shrub-like, with small, narrow leaves and can grow up to 30 centimeters in height. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.
