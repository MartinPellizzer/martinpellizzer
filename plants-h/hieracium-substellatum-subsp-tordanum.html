Hieracium substellatum subsp. tordanum

Hieracium substellatum subsp.

tordanum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, pastures, and woodland edges.

The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs. Its flowering heads are composed of yellow disk flowers surrounded by ray florets, which are usually pink or reddish in color.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed or nutrient-rich environments.
