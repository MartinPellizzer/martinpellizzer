Hookerochloa eriopoda

Hookerochloa eriopoda, commonly known as the silky hookergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, silky hairs that cover the stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in arid and semi-arid regions. Its slender, erect growth habit and small, pale green leaves make it well adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

Hookerochloa eriopoda is primarily valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in degraded landscapes.
