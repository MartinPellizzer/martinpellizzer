Hymenolobium excelsum

Hymenolobium excelsum, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, pendulous yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets and produces woody pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree's rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical regions.
