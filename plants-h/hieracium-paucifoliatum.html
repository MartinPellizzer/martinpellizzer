Hieracium paucifoliatum

Hieracium paucifoliatum, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a hairy texture, and it produces yellow flowers in clusters.

Hieracium paucifoliatum is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in various environments.
