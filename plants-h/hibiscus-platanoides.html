Hibiscus platanoides

Hibiscus platanoides, commonly known as the giant hibiscus, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 15 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom in the summer. Hibiscus platanoides thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and wildlife habitats.
