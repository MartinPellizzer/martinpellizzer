Heterotheca cryptocephala

Heterotheca cryptocephala, commonly known as the narrowleaf blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, while its blue to purple flowers bloom in late summer.

Heterotheca cryptocephala is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
