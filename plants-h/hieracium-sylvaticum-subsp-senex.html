Hieracium sylvaticum subsp. senex

Hieracium sylvaticum subsp.

senex, commonly known as the old man's beard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow-orange flower heads.

The plant typically grows in open woodland and grassland habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces, and it produces a showy inflorescence of composite flowers.

This subspecies is notable for its longevity and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
