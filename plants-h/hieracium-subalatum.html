Hieracium subalatum

Hieracium subalatum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are basal, with a rough texture and a serrated margin, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium subalatum is often found in mountainous areas and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
