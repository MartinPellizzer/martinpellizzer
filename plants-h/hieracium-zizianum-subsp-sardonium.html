Hieracium zizianum subsp. sardonium

Hieracium zizianum subsp.

sardonium, commonly known as the golden hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer, arranged in a single head atop a hairy stem. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with serrated edges, and the plant has a fibrous root system.

It is characterized by its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its ornamental value in garden settings.
