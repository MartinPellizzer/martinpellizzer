Hieracium erythrocarpum subsp. samurense

Hieracium erythrocarpum subsp.

samurense, commonly known as the Samur redfruit hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of yellow flowers.

The plant produces small, reddish-brown fruits that are distinctive and often used for identification purposes. It thrives in rocky or disturbed soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
