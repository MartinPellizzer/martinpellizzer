Heliophila parviflora

Heliophila parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered heliophyte, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It features slender, erect stems that bear small, white to pale blue flowers clustered in dense inflorescences. The leaves are typically narrow and linear, arranged alternately along the stem. This species thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in temperate regions.

It plays a vital role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small aquatic organisms.
