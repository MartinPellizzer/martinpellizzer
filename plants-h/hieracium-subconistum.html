Hieracium subconistum

Hieracium subconistum, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features hairy, rough stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves with a waxy texture. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers.

Hieracium subconistum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
