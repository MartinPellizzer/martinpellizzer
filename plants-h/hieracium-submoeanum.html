Hieracium submoeanum

Hieracium submoeanum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium submoeanum is often found in open habitats and can spread readily through its rhizomatous growth.
