Helleborus corsicus

Helleborus corsicus, commonly known as the Corsican hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, waxy leaves and showy, nodding flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in shaded to partially sunny conditions. Its flowers bloom in late winter to early spring, making it one of the earliest flowering plants in its habitat.

Helleborus corsicus is also noted for its toxic properties, containing various alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
