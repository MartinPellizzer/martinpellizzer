Hieracium lachenalii subsp. aurulentiforme

Hieracium lachenalii subsp.

aurulentiforme, commonly known as the golden-leaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its inflorescence consists of ray flowers surrounding a central disk.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant appearance.
