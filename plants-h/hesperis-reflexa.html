Hesperis reflexa

Hesperis reflexa, commonly known as the sweet rocket, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants such as cabbages and mustards. The plant is characterized by its showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnately lobed, and the plant often grows in meadows and along roadsides.

Hesperis reflexa is known for its pleasant, somewhat spicy fragrance, which attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.
