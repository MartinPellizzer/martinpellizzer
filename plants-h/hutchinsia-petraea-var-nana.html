Hutchinsia petraea var. nana

Hutchinsia petraea var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf hutchinsia, is a low-growing succulent native to South Africa.

It features dense clusters of small, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a bluish tint. The plant produces delicate, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and white during the winter months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
