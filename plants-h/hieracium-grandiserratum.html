Hieracium grandiserratum

Hieracium grandiserratum, commonly known as the large-toothed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features upright, hairy stems and basal leaves that are deeply lobed with serrated edges. Its showy yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

Hieracium grandiserratum is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade.
