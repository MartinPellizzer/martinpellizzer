Heliotropium eritrichioides

Heliotropium eritrichioides, commonly known as the "Mexican heliotrope," is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 feet, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, violet or blue-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers have a distinctive scent reminiscent of vanilla, which attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Heliotropium eritrichioides thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental plants with a fragrant allure.
