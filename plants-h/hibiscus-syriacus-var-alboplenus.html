Hibiscus syriacus var. alboplenus

Hibiscus syriacus var. alboplenus, commonly known as the white-flowered Chinese hibiscus, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers with a distinctive yellow center, which bloom in late summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are dark green and velvety to the touch. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
