Hieracium suborphotrichum

Hieracium suborphotrichum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and ragweed, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers consist of yellow disk florets surrounded by pale green to yellow ray florets.

Hieracium suborphotrichum is often found in open habitats and can spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
