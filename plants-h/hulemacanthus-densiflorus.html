Hulemacanthus densiflorus

Hulemacanthus densiflorus, commonly known as the denseflower hulemacanthus, is a shrub native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, flowering clusters that give it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical environments, often in moist, shaded areas. Its leaves are usually ovate with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The flowers are small and fragrant, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
