Heliotropium incanescens

Heliotropium incanescens, commonly known as the white heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, often found in open, disturbed habitats. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a rough texture, while its stems are slender and branching.

Heliotropium incanescens is also noted for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
