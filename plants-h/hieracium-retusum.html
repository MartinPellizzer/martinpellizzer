Hieracium retusum

Hieracium retusum, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are rough to the touch, hence the common name, and its yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Hieracium retusum is often found in temperate regions and can sometimes be invasive in certain areas.
