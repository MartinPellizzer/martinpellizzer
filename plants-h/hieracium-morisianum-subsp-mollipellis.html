Hieracium morisianum subsp. mollipellis

Hieracium morisianum subsp.

mollipellis, commonly known as the soft-hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It is typically found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows.

This subspecies is native to parts of Europe and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
