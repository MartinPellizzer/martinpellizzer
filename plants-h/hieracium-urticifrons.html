Hieracium urticifrons

Hieracium urticifrons, commonly known as the stinging hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant features showy yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, surrounded by leaf-like bracts. Its leaves are hairy and can cause skin irritation, contributing to its common name.

It plays a role in supporting pollinators and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
