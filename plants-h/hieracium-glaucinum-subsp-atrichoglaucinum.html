Hieracium glaucinum subsp. atrichoglaucinum

Hieracium glaucinum subsp.

atrichoglaucinum, commonly known as the glaucous hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. It has a hairy stem and leaves that are often covered with a whitish, powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in several regions.
