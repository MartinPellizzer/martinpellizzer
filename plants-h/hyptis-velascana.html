Hyptis velascana

Hyptis velascana, commonly known as Mexican blue sage, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it both visually appealing and ecologically valuable.
