Hieracium micranthum

Hieracium micranthum, commonly known as the small-flowered hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and produces numerous small, yellowish to reddish flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is found in various habitats across Europe and parts of Asia. It has hairy stems and leaves, and its flowers are often accompanied by bracts.

Hieracium micranthum is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in some environments.
