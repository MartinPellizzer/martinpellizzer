Hieracium glabratum subsp. trichocephalum

Hieracium glabratum subsp.

trichocephalum, commonly known as the hairyhead hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the daisy family. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while the subspecies is distinguished by its dense, hairy growth at the base of the stem.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.
