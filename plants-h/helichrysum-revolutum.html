Helichrysum revolutum

Helichrysum revolutum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, golden-yellow flowers that remain open for extended periods. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Helichrysum revolutum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
