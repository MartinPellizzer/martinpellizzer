Hemionitis moritziana

Hemionitis moritziana, commonly known as the mountain holly or Moritz's holly, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the high altitudes of Central America.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests. Its leaves are typically glossy and have a distinctive, almost triangular shape.

Hemionitis moritziana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in shaded garden settings.
