Hieracium brachycomum subsp. amplisquamum

Hieracium brachycomum subsp.

amplisquamum, commonly known as the broadscale hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, hairy foliage.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters, which bloom in late summer. It is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed sites, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
