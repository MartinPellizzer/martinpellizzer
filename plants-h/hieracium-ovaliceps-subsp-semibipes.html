Hieracium ovaliceps subsp. semibipes

Hieracium ovaliceps subsp.

semibipes, commonly known as the two-bitted hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as meadows and roadsides. It has a taproot system and produces numerous flowering stems that bear small, clustered flower heads.
