Hesperis adzharica

Hesperis adzharica, commonly known as the Caucasian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a yellow center. It typically grows in meadows and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soil.

Hesperis adzharica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
