Hostana coerulea

Hostana coerulea, commonly known as the blue hosta, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asparagaceae.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, and is characterized by its striking blue-green foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant, white or blue flowers that bloom in summer. Hostana coerulea thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its foliage and flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a visually appealing and low-maintenance plant.
