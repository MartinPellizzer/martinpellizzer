Habenaria iantha

Habenaria iantha, commonly known as the pink lady's tresses, is a terrestrial orchid belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and elongated, spiraling inflorescence that bears numerous small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its roots are fibrous and often form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi.

The species is native to parts of Asia and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance.
