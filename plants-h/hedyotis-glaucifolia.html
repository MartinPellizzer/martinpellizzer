Hedyotis glaucifolia

Hedyotis glaucifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf strobilanthes, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the summer months. The plant has slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the Rubiaceae family.
