Hovenia trichocarpa var. trichocarpa

Hovenia trichocarpa var. trichocarpa, commonly known as the Chinese raisin tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically three to five leaflets, and its small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible and have a sweet, raisin-like flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or scrub habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
