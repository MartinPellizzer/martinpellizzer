Hypericum emarginatum

Hypericum emarginatum, commonly known as the margined St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and features bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has small, oval leaves with a distinctive white or yellow margin. Its stems are woody and often have a reddish hue, and it is known for its medicinal properties.

Hypericum emarginatum is a valuable plant in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
