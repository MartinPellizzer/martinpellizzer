Hieracium spanocomum

Hieracium spanocomum, commonly known as the Spanish hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in sunny, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are alternate, rough, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium spanocomum is known for its ability to spread via rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
