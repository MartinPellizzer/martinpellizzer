Hygrophila bequaertii var. reducta

Hygrophila bequaertii var. reducta, commonly known as the dwarf hygrophila, is a compact aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically bright green and slightly textured. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a delicate floral touch to its appearance. It thrives in freshwater environments with moderate to high light conditions.

Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for aquariums and pond landscaping.
