Hieracium mysaetense

Hieracium mysaetense, commonly known as the Mysaetense hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and is characterized by its erect growth habit and yellowish to reddish flowers. The plant typically has basal leaves that are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in meadows and open habitats, often in moist or calcareous soils.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
