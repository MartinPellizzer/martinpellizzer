Hilsenbergia comorensis

Hilsenbergia comorensis, commonly known as the Comorian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Comoros Islands in the Indian Ocean.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring broad, waxy leaves with a dark green hue. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Hilsenbergia comorensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in its native region.
