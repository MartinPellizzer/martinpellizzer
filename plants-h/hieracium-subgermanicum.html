Hieracium subgermanicum

Hieracium subgermanicum, commonly known as the German hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a composite head.

Hieracium subgermanicum is often considered a weed due to its aggressive growth and ability to spread rapidly.
