Hieracium atratum subsp. conscissum

Hieracium atratum subsp.

conscissum, commonly known as the black hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its dark green, hairy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, grassy habitats such as meadows and riverbanks. It is known for its distinctive, compact inflorescence and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
