Hypericum brasiliense var. punctulatum

Hypericum brasiliense var. punctulatum, commonly known as the Brazilian St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and woody stems, typical of the Hypericaceae family. The plant features small, elliptical leaves with a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its medicinal properties have been studied for potential use in treating various ailments.
