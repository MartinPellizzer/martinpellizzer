Hieracium auriculiforme subsp. tenuiscapum

Hieracium auriculiforme subsp.

tenuiscapum, commonly known as the slender-stemmed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade.

The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, spoon-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters at the ends of its slender stems. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a distinct auriculate base, giving it a characteristic appearance.

It is known for its ability to spread via rhizomes and is considered a common weed in agricultural areas.
