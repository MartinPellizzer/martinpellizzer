Humboltia decurrens

Humboltia decurrens, commonly known as the Humboldt's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the coastal regions of South America.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along the shores of Chile and Peru. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers in dense clusters. It is adapted to tolerate salt spray and strong coastal winds.

Humboltia decurrens is often found in maritime environments and is an important part of the local coastal flora.
