Habenaria transnokoensis

Habenaria transnokoensis, commonly known as the Transnoko orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms in late winter or early spring, producing white or pale pink flowers with a distinctive lip. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
