Hieracium rhaebozum

Hieracium rhaebozum, commonly known as the Rhaebozum hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. It features a basal rosette of lanceolate leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
