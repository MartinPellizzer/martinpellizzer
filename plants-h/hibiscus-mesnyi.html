Hibiscus mesnyi

Hibiscus mesnyi, commonly known as the red hibiscus, is a striking flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, orange, or yellow. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are often hairy. Hibiscus mesnyi thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
