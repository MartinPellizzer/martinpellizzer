Hedypnois cretica subsp. ramosa

Hedypnois cretica subsp.

ramosa, commonly known as the Mediterranean thyme, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its compact, aromatic foliage with small, needle-like leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small pink or purple flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Hedypnois cretica subsp.

ramosa is also valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
