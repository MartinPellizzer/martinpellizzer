Helichrysum chamaethamnos

Helichrysum chamaethamnos, commonly known as the Cape gold or golden bush, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It features dense, compact growth with small, bright yellow flowers that resemble miniature sunflowers. The plant has aromatic, hairy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its drought tolerance and long-lasting flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
