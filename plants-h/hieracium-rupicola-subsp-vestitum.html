Hieracium rupicola subsp. vestitum

Hieracium rupicola subsp.

vestitum, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in well-drained environments. Its leaves are usually hairy and arranged in a basal rosette, which helps it retain moisture.

This subspecies is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its showy inflorescences and medicinal potential.
