Humboltia incurva

Humboltia incurva, commonly known as the hooked hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.
