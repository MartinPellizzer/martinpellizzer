Homeria glauca

Homeria glauca, commonly known as the bluebells of the genus Homeria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, showing a preference for well-drained environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming dense clumps.

Homeria glauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
