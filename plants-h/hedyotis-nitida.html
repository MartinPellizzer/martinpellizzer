Hedyotis nitida

Hedyotis nitida, commonly known as the silverweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers clustered in racemes, which bloom during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its overall ornamental value.

Hedyotis nitida is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks.
