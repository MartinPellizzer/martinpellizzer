Hieracium serratifrons subsp. pleuroleucum

Hieracium serratifrons subsp.

pleuroleucum, commonly known as the serrated hawkweed, is a member of the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its hairy, herbaceous growth habit and is native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

The plant features leaves that are deeply lobed and serrated, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically yellow and arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family.

It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows.
