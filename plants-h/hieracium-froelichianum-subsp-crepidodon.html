Hieracium froelichianum subsp. crepidodon

Hieracium froelichianum subsp.

crepidodon, commonly known as the creeping hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, grassy areas and has a creeping growth habit with stolons that root at the nodes. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators.

The subspecies is distinguished by its shorter stature and more compact growth compared to other Hieracium species.
