Hieracium glaucinum subsp. fraternum

Hieracium glaucinum subsp.

fraternum, commonly known as the glaucous hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands, meadows, and rocky soils.

The plant features bright yellow composite flowers that are clustered in a head, with bracts forming a distinctive involucral structure. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rough, hairy texture and a bluish-green hue.

The species is characterized by its ability to thrive in poor soils and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
