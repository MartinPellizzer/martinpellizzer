Hoodia marlothii

Hoodia marlothii, commonly known as the desert cabbage, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its leaves are reduced to spines, reducing water loss in harsh environments.

Hoodia marlothii is also noted for its unique chemical composition, which has been studied for potential medicinal properties.
