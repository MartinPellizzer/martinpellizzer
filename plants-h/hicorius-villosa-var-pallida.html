Hicorius villosa var. pallida

Hicorius villosa var. pallida, commonly known as the pale hairy hibiscus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features soft, hairy stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The leaves are typically ovate or heart-shaped with a velvety texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
