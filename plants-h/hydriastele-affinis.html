Hydriastele affinis

Hydriastele affinis, commonly known as the slender hydriastele, is a species of flowering plant in the family Bromeliaceae.

It is native to the wet tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in shaded, humid environments. The plant is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a water-holding cup, a feature common among bromeliads. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, often in shades of white or pale pink.

Hydriastele affinis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to adapt to a variety of indoor growing conditions.
