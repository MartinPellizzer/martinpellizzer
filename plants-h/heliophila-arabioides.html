Heliophila arabioides

Heliophila arabioides, commonly known as the desert bellflower, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in clusters. The plant has a compact growth habit with silver-gray foliage adapted to withstand high temperatures and low water availability. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy environments.

Heliophila arabioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to bloom in harsh, desert-like conditions.
