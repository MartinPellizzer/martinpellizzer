Hieracium loscosianum subsp. brachypus

Hieracium loscosianum subsp.

brachypus, commonly known as the short-stemmed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. It produces yellow flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer.

Its leaves are usually basal, with a rough, hairy texture, and the stems are upright and somewhat bristly.
