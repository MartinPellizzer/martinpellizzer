Hutchinsia procumbens var. crassifolia

Hutchinsia procumbens var. crassifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf hutchinsia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, thick leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
