Hymenochilus rubenachii

Hymenochilus rubenachii, commonly known as the red-veined orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, brightly colored flowers with prominent red veins. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually broad and glossy, and it produces small, fragrant blooms during the warmer months.

Hymenochilus rubenachii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
