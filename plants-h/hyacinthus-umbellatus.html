Hyacinthus umbellatus

Hyacinthus umbellatus, commonly known as the Dutch hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces dense clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers in early spring. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that emerge from a bulbous base. Its flowers are typically arranged in a loose umbel, giving it its scientific name.

Hyacinthus umbellatus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and as cut flowers.
