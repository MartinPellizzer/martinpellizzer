Hedyotis schlechtendahliana var. opaca

Hedyotis schlechtendahliana var. opaca, commonly known as the opaca dotweed, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features small, bright green, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white, four-petaled flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural interest.
