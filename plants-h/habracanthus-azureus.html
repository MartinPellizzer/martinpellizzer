Habracanthus azureus

Habracanthus azureus, commonly known as the Azure Habracanthus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers, which bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating, aiding in moisture retention. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections worldwide.
