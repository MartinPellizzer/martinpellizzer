Hippocrepis emerus

Hippocrepis emerus, commonly known as the Erythronium, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often showy flowers that are typically white or pale yellow with a prominent central stigma. The plant features basal leaves that are deeply lobed or divided, and its stems are usually unbranched. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

The flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
