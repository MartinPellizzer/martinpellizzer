Holosepalum

Holosepalum, commonly known as the "garden orchid," is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are characterized by their unique floral structure, where the sepals and petals are fused into a single structure called the labellum. Holosepalum species typically have small, delicate flowers with intricate patterns and vibrant colors. They are native to tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

The genus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
