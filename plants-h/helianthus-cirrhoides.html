Helianthus cirrhoides

Helianthus cirrhoides, commonly known as the curly top sunflower, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its hairy, branched stems and yellow, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine, white hairs.

Helianthus cirrhoides plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
