Hedyotis venosa

Hedyotis venosa, commonly known as the red-rooted or red-veined geranium, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It features small, delicate flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive red or purple vein in the petals. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, and its root system is characterized by red pigmentation. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Hedyotis venosa is valued in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
