Hieracium lachenalii subsp. lupimontis

Hieracium lachenalii subsp.

lupimontis, commonly known as the mountain hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellowish to orange-red composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. It has basal leaves that are lanceolate and often covered with fine hairs, and its stems are usually unbranched.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in high-altitude environments.
