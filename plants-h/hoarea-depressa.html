Hoarea depressa

Hoarea depressa, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It features a rosette of fleshy, blue-green leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy seed heads that disperse seeds by wind. Hoarea depressa is adapted to survive in poor soils and extreme temperatures, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important plant for soil stabilization and as a food source for desert wildlife.
