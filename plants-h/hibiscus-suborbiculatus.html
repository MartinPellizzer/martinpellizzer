Hibiscus suborbiculatus

Hibiscus suborbiculatus, commonly known as the golden hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers with a prominent orange center, which bloom in the summer months. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Hibiscus suborbiculatus is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
