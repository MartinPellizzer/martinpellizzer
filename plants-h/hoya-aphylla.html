Hoya aphylla

Hoya aphylla, commonly known as the naked leaf hoya, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, waxy, and often fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has simple, ovate leaves that are usually hairless, giving it its common name. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Hoya aphylla is popular among plant enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
