Hieracium ilicetorum

Hieracium ilicetorum, commonly known as the creeping hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium ilicetorum is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in disturbed habitats.
