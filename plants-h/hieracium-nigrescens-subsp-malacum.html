Hieracium nigrescens subsp. malacum

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

malacum, commonly known as the black hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement.

The plant typically grows in moist, grassy habitats and has dark green, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in a flat-topped inflorescence, blooming in late summer.

The subspecies malacum is distinguished by its darker pigmentation and slightly different leaf morphology compared to other variants of Hieracium nigrescens.
