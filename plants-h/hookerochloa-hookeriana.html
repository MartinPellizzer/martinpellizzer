Hookerochloa hookeriana

Hookerochloa hookeriana, commonly known as Hooker's grass, is a perennial grass species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spikelets. Hookerochloa hookeriana thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or riparian habitats.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
