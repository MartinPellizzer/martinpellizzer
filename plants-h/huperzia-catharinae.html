Huperzia catharinae

Huperzia catharinae, commonly known as the Catina's clubmoss, is a species of spore-bearing plant in the family Huperziaceae.

It is a small, evergreen fern-like plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its spirally arranged, needle-like leaves that form a dense, cushion-like structure. Huperzia catharinae is characterized by its simple, unbranched stems and the presence of microphylls, which are small, primitive leaves.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
