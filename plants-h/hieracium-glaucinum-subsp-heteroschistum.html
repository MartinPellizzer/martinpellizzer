Hieracium glaucinum subsp. heteroschistum

Hieracium glaucinum subsp.

heteroschistum, commonly known as the glaucous hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features basal leaves that are broadly lanceolate and covered in a dense layer of white to pale green hairs. Its flowers are yellow, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets, and it produces small, oval-shaped achenes as fruits.

It is often found in open habitats and is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads.
