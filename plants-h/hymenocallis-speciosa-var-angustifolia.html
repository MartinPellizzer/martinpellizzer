Hymenocallis speciosa var. angustifolia

Hymenocallis speciosa var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spider lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to the southern United States.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often with a contrasting red or orange throat. The plant has narrow, strap-like leaves that are typically arranged in a fan shape, contributing to its elegant appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
