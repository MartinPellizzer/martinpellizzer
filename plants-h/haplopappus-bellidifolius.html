Haplopappus bellidifolius

Haplopappus bellidifolius, commonly known as the desert groundcherry, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and wind-dispersed seeds. The plant has small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, followed by orange to red berries that contain the seeds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.

Haplopappus bellidifolius is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is well-suited to drought conditions.
