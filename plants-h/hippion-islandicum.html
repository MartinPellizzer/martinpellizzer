Hippion islandicum

Hippion islandicum, commonly known as the Island Spurge, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant has adapted to arid conditions with thick, water-storing leaves and a deep root system. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or coastal environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often found in local botanical gardens and conservation areas.
