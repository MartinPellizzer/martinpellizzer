Haworthia mucronata var. altilinea

Haworthia mucronata var. altilinea, commonly known as the tall-tipped Haworthia, is a compact, succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with white or pale markings. The leaves are often adorned with small, pointed teeth along the edges, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers on tall, slender stems during the summer months.

This variety is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low water requirements and ability to thrive in bright, indirect light.
