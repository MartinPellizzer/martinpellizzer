Howardia barbata

Howardia barbata, commonly known as the zigzag cactus, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiral-shaped stems that form a dense, bushy structure. The plant features small, spiny leaves that are arranged in a zigzag pattern, giving it its common name. It produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage.

Howardia barbata is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
