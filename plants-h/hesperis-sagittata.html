Hesperis sagittata

Hesperis sagittata, commonly known as the common candytuft, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, and it typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and rocky areas. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slight hairy texture.

Hesperis sagittata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
