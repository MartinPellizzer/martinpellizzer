Hesperis andina

Hesperis andina, commonly known as the Andean hesperis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many edible and ornamental plants. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale purple, blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

Hesperis andina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
