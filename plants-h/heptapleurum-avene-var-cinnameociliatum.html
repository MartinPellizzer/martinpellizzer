Heptapleurum avene var. cinnameociliatum

Heptapleurum avene var. cinnameociliatum, commonly known as the cinnamon-leaf heptapleurum, is a variant of the heptapleurum genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is a evergreen shrub or small tree characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves that have a distinctive cinnamon-colored edge. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its foliage is valued for its ornamental qualities and is used in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.
