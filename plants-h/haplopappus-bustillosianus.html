Haplopappus bustillosianus

Haplopappus bustillosianus, commonly known as the Mexican hairgrass, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to dry, hot climates.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapes and native plant gardens.
