Hieracium grisellescens

Hieracium grisellescens, commonly known as the gray-haired hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are usually hairy and grayish in appearance, giving it its common name.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
