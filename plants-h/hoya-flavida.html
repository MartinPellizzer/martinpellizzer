Hoya flavida

Hoya flavida, commonly known as the golden hoya, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its waxy, thick leaves and clusters of star-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, fragrant blooms in shades of yellow and white, often appearing in late spring to early summer. Hoya flavida is a semi-succulent plant that thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.

It is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
