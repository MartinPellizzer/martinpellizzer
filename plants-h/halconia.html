Halconia

Halconia, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and elusive flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, delicate appearance with small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. Halconia has a complex root system that helps it absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in many regions.
