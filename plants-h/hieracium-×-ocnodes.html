Hieracium × ocnodes

Hieracium × ocnodes, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a hybrid plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellow to orange flowering heads, which are composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. It has basal leaves that are usually heart-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a range of morphological variations, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
