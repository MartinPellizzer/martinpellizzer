Hypericum hyssopifolium var. racemosum

Hypericum hyssopifolium var. racemosum, commonly known as the clustered St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, typically from June to August. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to gardens.
