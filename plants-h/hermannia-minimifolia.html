Hermannia minimifolia

Hermannia minimifolia, commonly known as the dwarf hermannia, is a small, herbaceous plant native to Madagascar.

It features delicate, clustered leaves and produces tiny, bright red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is characterized by its low-growing habit and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is popular among succulent and bromeliad enthusiasts.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and sought-after specimen in botanical collections.
