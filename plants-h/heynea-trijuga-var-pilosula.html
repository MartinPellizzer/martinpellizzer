Heynea trijuga var. pilosula

Heynea trijuga var. pilosula, commonly known as the hairy trijuga, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
