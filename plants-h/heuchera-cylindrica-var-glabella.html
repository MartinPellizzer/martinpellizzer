Heuchera cylindrica var. glabella

Heuchera cylindrica var. glabella, commonly known as the smoothleaf alumroot, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, cylindrical flower spikes that bear small, white to pale pink flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, smooth-textured leaves that are often variegated with red or purple margins. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and delicate flowers, which add interest throughout the growing season.
