Hyptis meridionalis

Hyptis meridionalis, commonly known as Brazilian hyptis, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Hyptis meridionalis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
