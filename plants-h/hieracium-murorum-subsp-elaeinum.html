Hieracium murorum subsp. elaeinum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

elaeinum, commonly known as the mouse ear hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is sometimes grown in gardens.
