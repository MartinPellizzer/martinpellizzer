Heptaca

Heptaca, commonly known as the seven-leaf plant, is a unique species characterized by its distinct leaf arrangement.

It typically features seven leaves radiating from a single point, giving it a striking and symmetrical appearance. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly serrated edge.

Heptaca is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
