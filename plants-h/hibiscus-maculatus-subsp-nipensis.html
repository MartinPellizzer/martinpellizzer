Hibiscus maculatus subsp. nipensis

Hibiscus maculatus subsp.

nipensis, commonly known as the Japanese hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Japan and parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a distinctive dark red or purple markings.

The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a smooth texture, while the flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn. Hibiscus maculatus subsp.

nipensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
