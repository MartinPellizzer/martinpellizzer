Hieracium diaphanoides subsp. deductiforme

Hieracium diaphanoides subsp.

deductiforme, commonly known as the translucent hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its delicate appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
