Hieracium velenovskyanum

Hieracium velenovskyanum, commonly known as the Velenovsky's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its flowers are yellow, with a distinctive ray floret structure, and it produces small, oval-shaped achenes as its seeds.

Hieracium velenovskyanum is known for its rapid spread and can become invasive in certain regions.
