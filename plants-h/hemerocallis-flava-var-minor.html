Hemerocallis flava var. minor

Hemerocallis flava var. minor, commonly known as the lesser yellow daylily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.

This variety is less commonly found in cultivation compared to other daylily species.
