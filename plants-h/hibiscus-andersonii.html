Hibiscus andersonii

Hibiscus andersonii, commonly known as the Anderson's hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a dark red throat. Its leaves are ovate and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Hibiscus andersonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
