Huperzia sydowiorum

Huperzia sydowiorum, commonly known as the Sydow's huperzia, is a species of moss belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is a terrestrial plant characterized by its erect, spicate shoots and is typically found in moist, shaded environments. The plant has a distinctive morphology with elongated, spirally arranged leaves that are often covered in a layer of rhizoids. It produces spores in specialized structures called sporangia, which are clustered at the tips of the shoots.

Huperzia sydowiorum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
