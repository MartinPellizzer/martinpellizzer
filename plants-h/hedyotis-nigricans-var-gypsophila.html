Hedyotis nigricans var. gypsophila

Hedyotis nigricans var. gypsophila, commonly known as the white geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has opposite, simple, and usually hairy leaves that are elliptical in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
