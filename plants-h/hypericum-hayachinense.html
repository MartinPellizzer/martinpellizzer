Hypericum hayachinense

Hypericum hayachinense, commonly known as the Japanese St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the genus Hypericum, which includes over 400 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its yellow flowers and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to horticultural collections and ecological studies.
