Haemocharis symplocoides

Haemocharis symplocoides, commonly known as the golden trumpet plant, is a tropical flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a compact, shrub-like growth habit with dark green, glossy leaves. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Haemocharis symplocoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
