Hieracium subcordatum

Hieracium subcordatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with a distinctive basal rosette, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

Hieracium subcordatum is often found in open habitats and can be an indicator of nutrient-rich soils.
