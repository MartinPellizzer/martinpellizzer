Helichrysum pandurifolium

Helichrysum pandurifolium, commonly known as the golden everlasting or African immortelle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, yellow to orange flowers that resemble tiny suns. The plant has aromatic, hairy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Its name derives from the Greek words "helios" meaning sun and "chrysum" meaning gold, reflecting its vibrant floral display.
