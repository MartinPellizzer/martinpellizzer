Helenium hispidum

Helenium hispidum, commonly known as the rough helenium, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its striking orange to red flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in woodland or swampy areas. Its foliage is covered in fine hairs, giving it a rough texture.

Helenium hispidum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
