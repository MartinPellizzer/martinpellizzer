Hebe salicifolia

Hebe salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaved hebe, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like leaves that are lance-shaped and have a soft, silvery-green appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, or white, typically blooming in spring. Hebe salicifolia thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a favorite among gardeners in temperate climates.
