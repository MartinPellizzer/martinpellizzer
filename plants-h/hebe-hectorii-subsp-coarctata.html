Hebe hectorii subsp. coarctata

Hebe hectorii subsp.

coarctata, commonly known as the Stewart Island hebe, is a shrub native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its narrow, upright growth habit and small, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of tubular, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens with appropriate conditions.
