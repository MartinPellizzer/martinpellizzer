Hippeastrum minutum

Hippeastrum minutum, commonly known as the dwarf amaryllis, is a compact, flowering plant native to South America.

It produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, often with a yellow or orange throat. The plant has strap-like, narrow leaves that are typically a deep green color. It prefers well-draining soil and bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Hippeastrum minutum is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
