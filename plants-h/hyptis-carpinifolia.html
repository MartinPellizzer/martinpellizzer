Hyptis carpinifolia

Hyptis carpinifolia, commonly known as caraway hyptis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and spikes of small, pale blue to purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded areas.

Hyptis carpinifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
