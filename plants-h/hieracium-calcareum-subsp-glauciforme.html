Hieracium calcareum subsp. glauciforme

Hieracium calcareum subsp.

glauciforme, commonly known as the limestone hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to calcareous soils in Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems.

The subspecies glauciforme is distinguished by its glaucous (bluish-gray) appearance, particularly on the leaves and stems. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky areas.

This plant is often found in regions with alkaline or limestone-rich soils.
