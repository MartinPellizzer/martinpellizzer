Haageocereus laredensis var. longispinus

Haageocereus laredensis var. longispinus, commonly known as the Laredo long-spined cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its spiny, ribbed stems and a tendency to form clumps or small colonies. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically short-lived but attract pollinators such as bees and moths. Its adaptations include deep root systems and a waxy cuticle to minimize water loss in its harsh environment.

The varietal name "longispinus" refers to its long, sharp spines, which provide protection against herbivores.
