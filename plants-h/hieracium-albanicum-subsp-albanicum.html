Hieracium albanicum subsp. albanicum

Hieracium albanicum subsp.

albanicum, commonly known as the Albanian hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

The plant features upright stems with alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and is often found in calcareous soils.
