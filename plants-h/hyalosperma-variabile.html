Hyalosperma variabile

Hyalosperma variabile, commonly known as the variable glassy grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Hyalosperma variabile is often found in sandy or gravelly soils in arid and semi-arid regions.

Its ability to thrive in harsh environments makes it an important component of local ecosystems.
