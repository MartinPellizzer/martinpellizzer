Hypericum silenoides

Hypericum silenoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf St. John's wort, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has opposite, narrow leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Hypericum silenoides is part of the genus Hypericum, which includes many species with medicinal and ornamental value.
