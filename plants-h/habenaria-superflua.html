Habenaria superflua

Habenaria superflua, commonly known as the showy orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, pale pink to white flowers with a distinctive yellow-green lip. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine in some regions.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its intricate floral structure and adaptability to humid climates.
