Hymenophyllum tomentosum var. fusugasugense

Hymenophyllum tomentosum var. fusugasugense, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a small, epiphytic fern that thrives in humid, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety moss-like foliage, which gives it its common name. The fronds are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft trichomes that contribute to its unique texture. This variety is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including the Philippines and Indonesia.

It is often found growing on tree trunks or rocks in tropical rainforests, where it plays a role in the ecosystem by providing microhabitats for small organisms.
