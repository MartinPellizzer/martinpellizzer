Helictotrichon sedenense subsp. teretifolium

Helictotrichon sedenense subsp.

teretifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of North America. It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and prairies.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native grassland ecosystems.
