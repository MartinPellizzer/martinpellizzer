Haemanthus crassipes

Haemanthus crassipes, commonly known as the red-stemmed haemanthus, is a striking bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features striking red or purple stems that contrast with its broad, waxy leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright red flowers in late winter to early spring. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its unique appearance and seasonal blooming make it a notable addition to botanical collections.
