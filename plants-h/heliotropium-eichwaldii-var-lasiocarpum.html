Heliotropium eichwaldii var. lasiocarpum

Heliotropium eichwaldii var. lasiocarpum, commonly known as the woolly fruit heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant produces capsule fruits that are covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, giving it its varietal name "lasiocarpum.". It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
