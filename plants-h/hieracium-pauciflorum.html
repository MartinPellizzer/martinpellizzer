Hieracium pauciflorum

Hieracium pauciflorum, commonly known as the few-flowered hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has basal rosettes of dark green, hairy leaves and produces small, yellow flowers in clusters.

Hieracium pauciflorum is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in areas with moderate moisture.
