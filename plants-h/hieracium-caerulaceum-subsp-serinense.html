Hieracium caerulaceum subsp. serinense

Hieracium caerulaceum subsp.

serinense, commonly known as the blue hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
