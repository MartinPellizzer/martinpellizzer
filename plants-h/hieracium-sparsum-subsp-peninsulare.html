Hieracium sparsum subsp. peninsulare

Hieracium sparsum subsp.

peninsulare, commonly known as the Peninsular Hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

The plant has bright yellow composite flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, with numerous small florets. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough, hairy texture.

It is characterized by its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain regions.
