Hieracium piloselloides subsp. floccosum

Hieracium piloselloides subsp.

floccosum, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems, which give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.
