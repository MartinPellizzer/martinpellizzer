Hieracium paragogum subsp. oblongifolium

Hieracium paragogum subsp.

oblongifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 50 cm. Its leaves are broadly ovate to elliptical in shape, with a rough, hairy texture.

The flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the genus Hieracium.
