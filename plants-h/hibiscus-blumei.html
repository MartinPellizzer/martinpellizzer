Hibiscus blumei

Hibiscus blumei, commonly known as the Hawaiian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring large, showy flowers with five petals. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers range in color from white to pink and red. Hibiscus blumei thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
