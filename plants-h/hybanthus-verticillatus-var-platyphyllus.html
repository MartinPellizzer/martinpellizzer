Hybanthus verticillatus var. platyphyllus

Hybanthus verticillatus var. platyphyllus, commonly known as the broadleaf hybanthus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, clustered inflorescences and small, white to yellowish flowers. The leaves are typically broad and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. This species is often found in humid, tropical environments, growing in shaded or semi-shaded areas.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
