Hieracium lachenalii subsp. pseudoconsociatiforme

Hieracium lachenalii subsp.

pseudoconsociatiforme, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in rocky and sunny habitats, often growing at high altitudes.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
