Helichrysum stoechas

Helichrysum stoechas, commonly known as the "Golden Everlasting," is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellow-orange flower heads that resemble miniature sunflowers. The plant produces bract-like leaves that are often mistaken for flowers, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Helichrysum stoechas is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
