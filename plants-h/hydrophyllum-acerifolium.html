Hydrophyllum acerifolium

Hydrophyllum acerifolium, commonly known as the mapleleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that resemble maple leaves, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and are usually pink to red in color.

The species is part of the buckwheat family (Polygonaceae) and is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance.
