Helichroa

Helichroa, commonly known as the sunflower or helichrysum, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

These plants are characterized by their showy, daisy-like flowers and are native to various regions across Africa, Australia, and the Mediterranean. Many species of Helichroa are cultivated for their ornamental value due to their vibrant colors and long-lasting blooms. The leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are often upright and sturdy.

Some species are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
