Hieracium hapalochaetum

Hieracium hapalochaetum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. It has slender stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Its flowers are yellow and occur in clusters, contributing to its role in supporting pollinators.
