Hordeum vulgare var. gobicum

Hordeum vulgare var. gobicum, commonly known as goatgrass, is a wild cereal grass native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can thrive in arid or semi-arid environments. Its seeds are small and often used in traditional agriculture or as forage for livestock.

Hordeum vulgare var. gobicum is also of interest in plant breeding due to its genetic diversity and adaptability.
