Hakea longispina

Hakea longispina, commonly known as the long-spined hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves and distinctive, woody spikes that bear showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, red or black fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous coating. Hakea longispina is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

It is an important component of local ecosystems and is also cultivated for its ornamental value.
