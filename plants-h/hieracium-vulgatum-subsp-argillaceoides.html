Hieracium vulgatum subsp. argillaceoides

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

argillaceoides, commonly known as the clay-centred hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, clay-rich soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed areas.

The plant features basal rosettes of lanceolate leaves and produces yellow composite flowers with ray florets. It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

This subspecies is distinguished by its preference for heavy soils and its relatively compact growth habit.
