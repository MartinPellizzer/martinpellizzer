Hieracium caesium subsp. helsingicum

Hieracium caesium subsp.

helsingicum, commonly known as the Helsingian hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and western Russia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to orange flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has basal and erect stems with rough, hairy leaves that are lance-shaped.

Its flowering heads are surrounded by a single row of phyllaries, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.
