Hieracium × bifurcum subsp. pachycladum

Hieracium × bifurcum subsp.

pachycladum, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and clusters of small, yellowish to reddish flowers.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, white to grayish hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as meadows and roadsides.

This subspecies is often found in temperate climates and is known for its resilience and adaptability.
