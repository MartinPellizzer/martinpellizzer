Hyparrhenia gracilescens

Hyparrhenia gracilescens, commonly known as slender flat sedge, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to aquatic environments. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often submerged or floating. It plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects.
