Hesperis heteromalla

Hesperis heteromalla, commonly known as the narrowleaf sweet rocket, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and open woodlands.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native gardens and ecological restoration projects.
