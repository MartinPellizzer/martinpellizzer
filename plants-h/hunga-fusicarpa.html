Hunga fusicarpa

Hunga fusicarpa, commonly known as the red-fruited hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red, fleshy fruits that resemble small, round tomatoes. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom throughout the warm season. Its leaves are broadly ovate with a smooth texture and a slightly hairy underside.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant appearance.
