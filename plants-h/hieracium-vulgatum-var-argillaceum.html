Hieracium vulgatum var. argillaceum

Hieracium vulgatum var. argillaceum, commonly known as the clay cinquefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils, often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks. The plant features a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in a loose, terminal cluster. Its flowers have five petals and are often accompanied by yellow stamens.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and adaptability to various soil conditions.
