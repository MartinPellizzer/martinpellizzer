Hieracium lamyi

Hieracium lamyi, commonly known as the Lamy's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range from yellow to reddish in color.

Hieracium lamyi is often found in open habitats and can sometimes form dense colonies.
