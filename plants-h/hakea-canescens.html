Hakea canescens

Hakea canescens, commonly known as the silver hakea, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces cylindrical, woody flower spikes that range in color from yellow to red, depending on the species. It is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system for water retention.

Hakea canescens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
