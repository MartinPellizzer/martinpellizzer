Hieracium pseudohapalotrichum

Hieracium pseudohapalotrichum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and disturbed soils, exhibiting a low-growing habit with hairy stems and leaves. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in many temperate regions.
