Hoffmannia rosei

Hoffmannia rosei, commonly known as the desert holly, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted for water storage and reducing transpiration. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its compact size and drought tolerance make it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Hoffmannia rosei is valued for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements in dry climates.
