Hieracium koepingense

Hieracium koepingense, commonly known as the Koping's hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. It has basal leaves that are rough and hairy, with a yellowish-green color.

The flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a flat, disk-like structure, and bloom from late spring to early autumn.
