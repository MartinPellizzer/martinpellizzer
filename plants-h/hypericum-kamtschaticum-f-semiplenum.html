Hypericum kamtschaticum f. semiplenum

Hypericum kamtschaticum f. semiplenum, commonly known as the pale-stemmed St. John's wort, is a variant of the species native to the Caucasus region.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and pale green to almost white stems. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are oval-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

This form is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
