Helichrysum auronitens

Helichrysum auronitens, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, golden-yellow flowers that resemble tiny suns. The plant is known for its long-lasting blooms, which retain their color and form even after drying, making it popular in floral arrangements. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Helichrysum auronitens thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, often growing in rocky or sandy habitats.
