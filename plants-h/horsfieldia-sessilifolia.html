Horsfieldia sessilifolia

Horsfieldia sessilifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or branches, drawing moisture and nutrients from the air. Its flowers, which are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, bloom in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees and wasps.

Horsfieldia sessilifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded, humid environments.
