Habenaria eburnea

Habenaria eburnea, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and captivating orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, white, spider-like flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling a ghostly apparition. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or other plants without deriving nutrients from them. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it relies on specific symbiotic relationships with fungi for nutrient absorption.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is listed as endangered in many areas.
