Hieracium nigrescens subsp. stenopiforme

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

stenopiforme, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and lanceolate leaves.

The subspecies stenopiforme is distinguished by its narrow, elongated leaves and reduced flower heads compared to other members of the species. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade.

The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
