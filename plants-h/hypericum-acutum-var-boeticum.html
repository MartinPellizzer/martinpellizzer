Hypericum acutum var. boeticum

Hypericum acutum var. boeticum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically narrow and slightly serrated. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its medicinal properties have historically been valued for their potential health benefits.
