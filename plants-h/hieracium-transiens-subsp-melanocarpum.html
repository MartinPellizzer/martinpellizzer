Hieracium transiens subsp. melanocarpum

Hieracium transiens subsp.

melanocarpum, commonly known as the dark-fruited hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dark, almost black, achene fruits, which set it apart from other hawkweed species.
