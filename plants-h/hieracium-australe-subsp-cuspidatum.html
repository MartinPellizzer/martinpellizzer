Hieracium australe subsp. cuspidatum

Hieracium australe subsp.

cuspidatum, commonly known as the Australian hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Australia. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and heathlands.

The plant features erect stems covered in fine hairs and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, aiding in water retention.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some related species.
