Hymenatherum concinnum

Hymenatherum concinnum, commonly known as the pink wattle, is a small evergreen shrub native to eastern Australia.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are usually covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of delicate, pink or red flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry forest habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
