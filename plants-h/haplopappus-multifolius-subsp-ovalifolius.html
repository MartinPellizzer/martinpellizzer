Haplopappus multifolius subsp. ovalifolius

Haplopappus multifolius subsp.

ovalifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and herbaceous growth habit.

The plant features linear, often hairy leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its yellow disk flowers are surrounded by pale green to white ray florets, typical of the genus Haplopappus.

It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments with minimal water requirements.
