Helichrysum taylorii

Helichrysum taylorii, commonly known as Taylor's immortelle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that resemble small sunflowers. The plant produces small, papery bracts that give it a distinctive appearance, often mistaken for flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Helichrysum taylorii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
