Hieracium atraticeps

Hieracium atraticeps, commonly known as the black-centred hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black central disc flowers, which contrast with its yellow to brown ray florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils across Europe and parts of Asia. It has a creeping rootstock and produces numerous flowering stems that can reach up to 30 cm in height.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
