Hedyotis acuminata f. hirticalyx

Hedyotis acuminata f. hirticalyx, commonly known as the hairy-leafed dotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with five petals and prominent stamens. The leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptical, and covered in fine, hairy glands, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

The plant has a creeping rootstock and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.
