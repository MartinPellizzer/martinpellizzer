Heuchera parviflora var. puberula

Heuchera parviflora var. puberula, commonly known as the velvetleaf alumroot, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features basal leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in a fine, velvety pubescence, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or red during the spring. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value lies in its attractive foliage and delicate floral display.
