Hydrilla dentata

Hydrilla dentata, commonly known as the toothed hydrilla, is a submerged aquatic plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It has long, branching stems that often form dense mats in freshwater environments. The leaves are narrow, ribbon-like, and have serrated edges, giving it its scientific name. It produces small, green flowers that are typically hidden beneath the water's surface.

This plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of water conditions.
