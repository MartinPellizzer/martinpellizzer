Hieracium plecophyllum

Hieracium plecophyllum, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy stems. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are generally hairy and arranged in a rosette pattern, while its flowers are yellow and daisy-like.

Hieracium plecophyllum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting pollinators.
