Helianthus apricus var. camporum

Helianthus apricus var. camporum, commonly known as the Camp's sunflower, is a variant of the sunflower species native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its large, yellow-orange flower heads and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces abundant seeds that are a food source for various birds and small mammals.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
