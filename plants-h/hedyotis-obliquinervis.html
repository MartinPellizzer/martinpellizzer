Hedyotis obliquinervis

Hedyotis obliquinervis, commonly known as the narrow-veined Indian bedstraw, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant has slender, creeping stems and small, opposite leaves with a distinctive, narrow vein pattern. Its flowers are tiny, white or pale yellow, and are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

Hedyotis obliquinervis is known for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
