Hemionitis truncata

Hemionitis truncata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed hemionitis, is a rare and unique fern species native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth form and its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments. The plant features finely divided, linear leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help reduce water loss. Hemionitis truncata is notable for its distinctive reproductive structures, including small, spore-producing cones that grow directly on the leaf surfaces.
