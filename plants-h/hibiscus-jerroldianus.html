Hibiscus jerroldianus

Hibiscus jerroldianus, commonly known as the Jerrold's hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. Hibiscus jerroldianus thrives in humid environments and is often found near water sources such as rivers and swamps.

Its botanical features make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.
