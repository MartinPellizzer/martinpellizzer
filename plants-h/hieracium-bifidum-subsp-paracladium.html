Hieracium bifidum subsp. paracladium

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

paracladium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrowly lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in summer.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. It has a taproot system and is often found in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural and natural areas.
