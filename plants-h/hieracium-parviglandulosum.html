Hieracium parviglandulosum

Hieracium parviglandulosum, commonly known as the small glandular hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped with hairy surfaces, and the flowers are yellow with ray florets.

It is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
