Hieracium crocatum var. vinaceum

Hieracium crocatum var. vinaceum, commonly known as the red-hued hawkweed, is a variant of the hawkweed genus characterized by its distinctive reddish pigmentation.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Europe, typically found in meadows and grassy areas. The plant features basal rosettes of lanceolate leaves and produces yellow disk flowers with ray florets. Its botanical significance lies in its unique coloration and role in local ecosystems.

It is also noted for its potential in ecological studies due to its adaptability to various soil conditions.
