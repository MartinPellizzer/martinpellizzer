Helianthemum × serranicum

Helianthemum × serranicum, commonly known as the hybrid rock rose, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in spring and summer. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, aiding in water retention. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and prolonged flowering period.
