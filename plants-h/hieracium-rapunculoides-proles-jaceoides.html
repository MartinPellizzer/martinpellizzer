Hieracium rapunculoides proles jaceoides

Hieracium rapunculoides proles jaceoides, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia and thrives in moist, grassy habitats. It has hairy stems and leaves that are often glandular, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in disturbed soils.
