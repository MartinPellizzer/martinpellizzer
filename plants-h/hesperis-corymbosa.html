Hesperis corymbosa

Hesperis corymbosa, commonly known as the sweet rocket, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbages and mustards. The plant produces showy clusters of pink or purple flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with a slightly hairy texture.

Hesperis corymbosa is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
