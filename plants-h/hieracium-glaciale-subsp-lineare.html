Hieracium glaciale subsp. lineare

Hieracium glaciale subsp.

lineare, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in the summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats at high elevations. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
