Hieracium laevigatum subsp. sublineolatum

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

sublineolatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 40 cm.

The plant has basal leaves that are broadly ovate to elliptic in shape, with a hairy surface and a slightly wavy margin. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and are borne on long, slender stems.

The species is characterized by its finely toothed leaves and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
