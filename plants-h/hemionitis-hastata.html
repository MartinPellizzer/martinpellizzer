Hemionitis hastata

Hemionitis hastata, commonly known as the rock fern, is a unique plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a small, delicate fern that thrives in rocky and gravelly soils, often found in desert environments. The plant has a compact, creeping growth habit with small, lance-shaped fronds that are usually covered in a fine layer of white hairs. Its reproductive structures are inconspicuous, with spores produced in clusters on the undersides of its leaves.

Hemionitis hastata is well adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited water availability, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
