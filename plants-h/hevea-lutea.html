Hevea lutea

Hevea lutea, commonly known as the yellow rubber tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale yellow bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant is notable for its latex-producing properties, which are harvested for industrial use. It thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

Hevea lutea is often cultivated for its economic value in rubber production.
