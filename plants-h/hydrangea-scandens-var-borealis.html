Hydrangea scandens var. borealis

Hydrangea scandens var. borealis, commonly known as the northern climbing hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that range from white to pink depending on soil pH. The plant features arching stems that can climb or trail, making it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, with serrated edges, and turn vibrant red or orange in the fall.

This variety thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for shaded garden areas.
