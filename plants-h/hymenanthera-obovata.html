Hymenanthera obovata

Hymenanthera obovata, commonly known as the broad-leafed honeywood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its obovate, glossy, dark green leaves that are often arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which attract pollinators such as birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Hymenanthera obovata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
