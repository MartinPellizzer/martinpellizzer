Helichrysum cremnophilum

Helichrysum cremnophilum, commonly known as the mountain everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, bright yellow to orange flowers that resemble daisies, and its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like. The plant is often found growing in rocky or mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained soils. It is a perennial species that produces fluffy, seed-bearing structures known as cymes.

Helichrysum cremnophilum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its drought tolerance and long-lasting blooms.
