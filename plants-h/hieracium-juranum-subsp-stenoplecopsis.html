Hieracium juranum subsp. stenoplecopsis

Hieracium juranum subsp.

stenoplecopsis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm.

The plant features basal and cauline leaves that are lanceolate to linear, with a hairy surface. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and it produces small, oval achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and is often found in moist, nutrient-rich habitats.
