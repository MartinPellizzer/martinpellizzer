Helichrysum rutilans

Helichrysum rutilans, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their long-lasting nature and vibrant color.
