Hieracium murorum subsp. subbifurcatum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

subbifurcatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow composite flowers.

The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a hairy surface. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

The plant is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
