Hieracium majorcanum

Hieracium majorcanum, commonly known as the Majorcan hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Majorca.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosettes of lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and coastal areas.

Hieracium majorcanum is a member of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
