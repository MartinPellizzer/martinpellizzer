Hieracium epimedium subsp. beauverdianum

Hieracium epimedium subsp.

beauverdianum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellowish to orange-red flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are upright and branching.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
