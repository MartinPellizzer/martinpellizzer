Hieracium oenovallis

Hieracium oenovallis, commonly known as the meadowhawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are basal, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

It plays a role in supporting pollinators and is often found in open habitats.
