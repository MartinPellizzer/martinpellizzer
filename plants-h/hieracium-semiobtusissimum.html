Hieracium semiobtusissimum

Hieracium semiobtusissimum, commonly known as the broad-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun. Its leaves are broadly ovate with serrated edges, and the flowers are yellow with a central disc.

It is a perennial species that can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
