Hypericum hircinum var. albimontanum

Hypericum hircinum var. albimontanum, commonly known as the alpine St. John's wort, is a variant of the European St. John's wort.

It is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its yellow flowers and woody stems. This plant typically thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, preferring well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties, though it is less commonly used than other Hypericum species.
