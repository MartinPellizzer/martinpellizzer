Hedysarum roezlianum

Hedysarum roezlianum, commonly known as the Turkish vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Hedysarum roezlianum is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
