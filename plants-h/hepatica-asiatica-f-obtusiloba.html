Hepatica asiatica f. obtusiloba

Hepatica asiatica f. obtusiloba, commonly known as the Japanese liverwort, is a unique plant species belonging to the Pterobranchia order.

It is characterized by its delicate, leaf-like structures that resemble the shape of a liver, hence its common name. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or along stream banks. Its reproductive structures are specialized, with a distinct pattern of branching that aids in its propagation.

The plant plays a vital role in its ecosystem by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
