Hexadesmia falcata

Hexadesmia falcata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed daisy.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often in rocky or sandy soils. Its flowering period usually occurs during the warmer months, and it is often found in regions of southern Africa.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
