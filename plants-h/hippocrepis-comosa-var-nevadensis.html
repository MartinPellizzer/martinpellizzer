Hippocrepis comosa var. nevadensis

Hippocrepis comosa var. nevadensis, commonly known as the Nevada hoary vetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
