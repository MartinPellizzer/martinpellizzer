Homoranthus biflorus

Homoranthus biflorus, commonly known as the two-flowered homoranthus, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are typically clustered in pairs, giving it its species name "biflorus.". Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slight aromatic scent.

Homoranthus biflorus is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathland environments.
