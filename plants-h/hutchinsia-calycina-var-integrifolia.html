Hutchinsia calycina var. integrifolia

Hutchinsia calycina var. integrifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leaved hutchinsia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its delicate, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant features smooth, entire leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a compact appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
