Halimium lepidotum proles multiflorum

Halimium lepidotum proles multiflorum, commonly known as the multiflorous sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in spring. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas or dry grasslands.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
