Hieracium caesiiflorum

Hieracium caesiiflorum, commonly known as the blue flowering hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features upright, hairy stems that bear alternate, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its most distinctive feature is the blue to violet flower heads, which consist of numerous tiny florets arranged in a disc-like pattern.

Hieracium caesiiflorum typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
