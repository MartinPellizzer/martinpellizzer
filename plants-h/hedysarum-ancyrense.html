Hedysarum ancyrense

Hedysarum ancyrense, commonly known as the Turkish vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and is often found in rocky or dry habitats. It has a fibrous root system and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

Hedysarum ancyrense is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
