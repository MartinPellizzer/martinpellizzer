Hippeastrum salinarum

Hippeastrum salinarum, commonly known as the Salina amaryllis, is a species native to the coastal regions of Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces large, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in its arid environment. Hippeastrum salinarum thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its unique adaptations make it well-suited for coastal climates with high salinity and strong winds.
