Hypochaeris aethnensis subsp. depressa

Hypochaeris aethnensis subsp.

depressa, commonly known as the Etna hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the volcanic slopes of Mount Etna in Sicily. It is a low-growing perennial herb with basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers that are arranged in a single head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to the harsh conditions of high-altitude environments.

Due to its restricted habitat, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
