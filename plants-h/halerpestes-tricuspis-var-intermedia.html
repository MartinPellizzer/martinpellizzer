Halerpestes tricuspis var. intermedia

Halerpestes tricuspis var. intermedia, commonly known as the African blackwood, is a species native to parts of Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows in dry, wooded areas. The plant is characterized by its dark, dense wood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic qualities. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are usually elliptical in shape.

The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by small, round fruits that contain three seeds.
