Haemanthus albiflos var. burchellii

Haemanthus albiflos var. burchellii, commonly known as the white-flowered haemanthus, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features a basal rosette of sword-shaped, evergreen leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces tall, upright stems bearing clusters of white, star-shaped flowers in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to bloom reliably in suitable conditions.
