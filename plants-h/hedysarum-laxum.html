Hedysarum laxum

Hedysarum laxum, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers in clusters. Its root system is fibrous and can form dense colonies, contributing to soil stabilization.

Hedysarum laxum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in arid conditions.
