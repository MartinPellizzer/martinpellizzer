Hieracium obscuratum subsp. pedatifolium

Hieracium obscuratum subsp.

pedatifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of broadly ovate to lanceolate leaves.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom from June to August, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
