Hieracium grecescui subsp. basiplatyodontum

Hieracium grecescui subsp.

basiplatyodontum, commonly known as the Greek hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southeastern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosettes of leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in composite inflorescences, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Its subspecies status reflects subtle morphological differences from other Hieracium species.
