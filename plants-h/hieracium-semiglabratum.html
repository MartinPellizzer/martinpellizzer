Hieracium semiglabratum

Hieracium semiglabratum, commonly known as the smooth-haired hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows to a height of 20 to 60 centimeters, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often glandular. Its flowers are yellow, with ray florets surrounding a central disk, and it produces small, oval achenes as fruits.

Hieracium semiglabratum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
