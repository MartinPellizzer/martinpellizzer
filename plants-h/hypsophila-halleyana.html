Hypsophila halleyana

Hypsophila halleyana, commonly known as the Halley's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, delicate flowering heads that appear in clusters. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slight bluish-green hue, and it produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
