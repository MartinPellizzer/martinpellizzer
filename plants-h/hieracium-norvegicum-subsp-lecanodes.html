Hieracium norvegicum subsp. lecanodes

Hieracium norvegicum subsp.

lecanodes, commonly known as the Norwegian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and roadsides. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and its flowers are arranged in a single head.

It is considered an invasive species in some regions due to its rapid spread and competitive growth.
