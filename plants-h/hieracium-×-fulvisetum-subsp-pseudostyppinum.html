Hieracium × fulvisetum subsp. pseudostyppinum

Hieracium × fulvisetum subsp.

pseudostyppinum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm.

The plant features basal rosettes of dark green, hairy leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with five petals. Its flowers are usually borne on elongated stems and bloom from late spring to early summer.

The species is characterized by its hybrid origin, resulting from crosses between different Hieracium species.
