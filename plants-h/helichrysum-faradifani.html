Helichrysum faradifani

Helichrysum faradifani, commonly known as the Faradifan golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that retain their color even after drying. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Helichrysum faradifani is also valued for its ornamental use and historical use in traditional medicine.
