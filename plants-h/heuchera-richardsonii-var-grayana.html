Heuchera richardsonii var. grayana

Heuchera richardsonii var. grayana, commonly known as the Gray's alumroot, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Appalachian Mountains.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant has heart-shaped, palmate leaves that are typically silver or gray-green in color, which give it its common name. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

The leaves and flowers are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported astringent properties.
