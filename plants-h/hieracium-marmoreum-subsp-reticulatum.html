Hieracium marmoreum subsp. reticulatum

Hieracium marmoreum subsp.

reticulatum, commonly known as the reticulate hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and yellowish to orange flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges. It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds and has a shallow root system.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular garden plant, though it can become invasive in some regions.
