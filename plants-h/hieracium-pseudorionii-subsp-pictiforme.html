Hieracium pseudorionii subsp. pictiforme

Hieracium pseudorionii subsp.

pictiforme, commonly known as the painted hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and bright yellow or orange flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique leaf patterns and the presence of red or purple markings on its leaves. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and slopes.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some areas.
