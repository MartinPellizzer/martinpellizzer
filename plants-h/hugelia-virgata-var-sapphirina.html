Hugelia virgata var. sapphirina

Hugelia virgata var. sapphirina, commonly known as the Sapphire Hugelia, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that displays a rich, bluish-green hue, which becomes even more intense in full sun. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and lavender, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden beds or as a container plant.

Its compact growth habit and vibrant color make it a popular choice for adding visual interest to tropical and subtropical landscapes.
