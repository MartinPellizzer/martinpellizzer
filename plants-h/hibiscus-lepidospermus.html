Hibiscus lepidospermus

Hibiscus lepidospermus, commonly known as the Hawaiian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its woody stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, with a prominent yellow throat. It thrives in coastal and lowland areas, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

Hibiscus lepidospermus is an important part of Hawaii's native flora and is often used in traditional Hawaiian culture.
