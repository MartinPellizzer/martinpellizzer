Herorchis champagneuxii f. mesomelana

Herorchis champagneuxii f. mesomelana, commonly known as the Champagne orchid, is a unique orchid species native to specific regions in South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated floral structure with a prominent, showy lip that is often a vibrant pink or purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring or early summer, adding a dramatic visual element to its habitat.

Due to its rarity and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in its native range.
