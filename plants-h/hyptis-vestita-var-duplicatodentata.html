Hyptis vestita var. duplicatodentata

Hyptis vestita var. duplicatodentata, commonly known as the double-toothed hyptis, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive double-toothed margin. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale blue, arranged in dense spikes.
