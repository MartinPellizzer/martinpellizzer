Hesperis coronopifolia

Hesperis coronopifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Hesperis coronopifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
