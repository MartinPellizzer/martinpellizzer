Helianthemum violaceum var. arabinianum

Helianthemum violaceum var. arabinianum, commonly known as the Armenian rock rose, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of the Eastern Mediterranean.

It features small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early summer, adding a striking visual contrast to its gray-green foliage. The plant is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing its drought tolerance and resilience. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
