Helichrysum dichoon

Helichrysum dichoon, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers that are tightly packed and resemble tiny suns. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often used in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

The species is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
