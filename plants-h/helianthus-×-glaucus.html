Helianthus × glaucus

Helianthus × glaucus, commonly known as the bluegrass sunflower, is a hybrid species native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue-green foliage and yellow-orange flower heads that resemble those of other sunflowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it a unique appearance.

This species is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
