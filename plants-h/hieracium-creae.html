Hieracium creae

Hieracium creae, commonly known as the Crete hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads made up of many small florets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat, composite head.

Hieracium creae is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its resilience to drought conditions.
