Hyptis pycnocephala

Hyptis pycnocephala, commonly known as Spanish needle or blue hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to blue. Its leaves are often hairy and have a strong, minty scent.

Hyptis pycnocephala is often used in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and aromatic properties.
