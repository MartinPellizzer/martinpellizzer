Hypochaeris brasiliensis

Hypochaeris brasiliensis, commonly known as Brazilian everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant produces small, yellow disk flowers surrounded by white to pale yellow ray florets. It is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and fields.

Hypochaeris brasiliensis is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common weed in many regions.
