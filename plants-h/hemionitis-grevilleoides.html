Hemionitis grevilleoides

Hemionitis grevilleoides, commonly known as the Greville's hemionitis, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features narrow, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires partial to full shade.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in shaded gardens and rockeries.
