Hevea brasiliensis var. subconcolor

Hevea brasiliensis var. subconcolor, commonly known as the rubber tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon basin.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern. The plant is renowned for its latex-producing capabilities, which are harvested for natural rubber. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, with both male and female parts present on the same plant.

The tree thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
