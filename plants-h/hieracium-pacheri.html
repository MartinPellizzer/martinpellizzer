Hieracium pacheri

Hieracium pacheri, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and a tall, branching stem that bears numerous yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, thriving in temperate climates. Its flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological role.

Hieracium pacheri is often found in Europe and parts of Asia, where it plays a part in local biodiversity.
