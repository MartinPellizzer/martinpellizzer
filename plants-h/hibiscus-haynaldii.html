Hibiscus haynaldii

Hibiscus haynaldii, commonly known as the Mexican hibiscus, is a species native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually pink or red, with five petals and a prominent central stamen. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and often branched.

Hibiscus haynaldii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
