Hymenachne aurita

Hymenachne aurita, commonly known as the silky flat sedge, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces small, three-lobed fruits.

Hymenachne aurita plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
