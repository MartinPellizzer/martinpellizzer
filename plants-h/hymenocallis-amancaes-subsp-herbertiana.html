Hymenocallis amancaes subsp. herbertiana

Hymenocallis amancaes subsp.

herbertiana, commonly known as Herbert's spider lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to Mexico. It features showy, white to pale yellow flowers with a distinctive, recurved spatula-shaped lip.

The plant produces a single, tall stem bearing several blooms, often with a strong, sweet fragrance. Its leaves are strap-like and grass-like, typically emerging after the flowers have faded.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
