Helianthocereus grandiflorus

Helianthocereus grandiflorus, commonly known as the large-flowered night bloomer cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus with smooth, pale green stems that can grow up to several meters in height. Its most striking feature is its large, showy flowers that open at night, typically in shades of yellow to orange. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor.

It thrives in arid environments and requires well-draining soil and full sun exposure.
