Heuchera lancipetala

Heuchera lancipetala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved alumroot, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are typically green or variegated, with a hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white or pink flowers in early spring. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Heuchera lancipetala is valued for its ornamental foliage and early blooming habit.
