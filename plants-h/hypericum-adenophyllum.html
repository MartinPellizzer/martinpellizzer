Hypericum adenophyllum

Hypericum adenophyllum, commonly known as the sticky St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy, glandular stems that give it a sticky texture. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, elliptical leaves and has a compact growth habit.

Hypericum adenophyllum is a member of the Hypericaceae family and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
