Homalomena kiahii f. maculata

Homalomena kiahii f. maculata, commonly known as the spotted umbrella plant, is a tropical evergreen perennial belonging to the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped, dark green leaves with distinctive white or light green spots. The plant produces striking, spatheous inflorescences with a bright yellow spathe and a spadix that bears small, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This ornamental plant is popular in tropical gardens and as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
