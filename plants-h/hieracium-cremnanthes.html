Hieracium cremnanthes

Hieracium cremnanthes, commonly known as the Sierra hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It features basal rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellow flowers in late summer.

Its botanical structure supports its adaptation to arid environments, making it a resilient member of its ecosystem.
