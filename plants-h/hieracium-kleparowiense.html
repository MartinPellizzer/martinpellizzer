Hieracium kleparowiense

Hieracium kleparowiense, commonly known as Kleparow's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and bloom from June to August.

It is considered a low-growing plant, often forming dense mats that can compete with other vegetation.
