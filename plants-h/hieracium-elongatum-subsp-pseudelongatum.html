Hieracium elongatum subsp. pseudelongatum

Hieracium elongatum subsp.

pseudelongatum, commonly known as the elongated hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy basal rosettes.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
