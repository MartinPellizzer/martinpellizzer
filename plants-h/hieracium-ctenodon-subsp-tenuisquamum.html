Hieracium ctenodon subsp. tenuisquamum

Hieracium ctenodon subsp.

tenuisquamum, commonly known as the narrow-scale hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its upright stems and basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves.

The flowers are typically yellow, arranged in a composite head with phyllaries that are narrow and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

This subspecies is known for its subtle floral structure and is relatively uncommon in the wild.
