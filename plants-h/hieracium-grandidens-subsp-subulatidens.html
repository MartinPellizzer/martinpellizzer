Hieracium grandidens subsp. subulatidens

Hieracium grandidens subsp.

subulatidens, commonly known as the needleleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves and yellowish composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, temperate climates. It has a taproot system and produces small, elongated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is notable for its distinctive morphology and ecological adaptation to alpine environments.
