Hoya diversifolia

Hoya diversifolia, commonly known as the variegated wax plant, is a tropical species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pink, which bloom in the summer months. Hoya diversifolia is a popular houseplant due to its ability to thrive in low light conditions and its attractive foliage.

It is also known for its slow growth and ease of care, making it ideal for indoor gardening.
