Halothamnus seravschanicus

Halothamnus seravschanicus, commonly known as the Central Asian spurge.

It is a low-growing, succulent perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. The plant has thick, fleshy stems and leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. It produces small, yellowish flowers during the growing season.

Halothamnus seravschanicus is adapted to sandy soils and is often found in steppe and desert ecosystems.
