Hyssopus officinalis proles montanus

Hyssopus officinalis proles montanus, commonly known as mountain hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, upright stems covered in grayish-white hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces spikes of blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

Its aromatic leaves and flowers have been used historically in herbal medicine and as a culinary herb.
