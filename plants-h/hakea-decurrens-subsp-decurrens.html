Hakea decurrens subsp. decurrens

Hakea decurrens subsp.

decurrens, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hakea, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces bright red or orange flowers in dense, cylindrical spikes, which appear in spring. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is drought-tolerant once established.

This species is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
