Hirtella tomentella

Hirtella tomentella, commonly known as the woolly hirtella, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a soft, fuzzy appearance. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and has small, elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, round berries that are edible.

Hirtella tomentella is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
