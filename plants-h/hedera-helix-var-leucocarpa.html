Hedera helix var. leucocarpa

Hedera helix var. leucocarpa, commonly known as the white-berried ivy, is a variant of the common ivy characterized by its distinctive white berries.

This plant is a climbing evergreen vine native to Europe and is part of the Araliaceae family. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a glossy, dark green surface. The white berries, which are non-toxic, appear in clusters and are a notable feature of this variety.

It thrives in shaded areas and is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal.
