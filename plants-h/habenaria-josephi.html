Habenaria josephi

Habenaria josephi, commonly known as the Joseph's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer or early autumn, producing pink to purple flowers with a distinctive three-lobed lip. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Habenaria josephi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
