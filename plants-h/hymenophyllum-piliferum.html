Hymenophyllum piliferum

Hymenophyllum piliferum, commonly known as the hairy maidenhair fern, is a small, epiphytic fern that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds covered in fine, hair-like structures called trichomes. The plant belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and Australia. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and release spores for propagation.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in greenhouses or terrariums.
