Hieracium dermophyllum subsp. juranopsis

Hieracium dermophyllum subsp.

juranopsis, commonly known as the Jura hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring calcareous soils.

The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are densely hairy and have a distinctive yellowish-green color. Its flowering stems bear numerous yellow disk flowers surrounded by ray florets, which attract pollinators.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
