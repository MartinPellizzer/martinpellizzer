Hymenochilus tristis

Hymenochilus tristis, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers with a distinctive white throat. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its flowers are often fragrant and bloom during the warmer months.

The botanical features of Hymenochilus tristis make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts and horticulturists.
