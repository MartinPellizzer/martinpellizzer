Hoya lucardenasiana

Hoya lucardenasiana, commonly known as the "Lucardenas' Waxy Wreath," is a rare and striking epiphytic plant native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy, and often variegated leaves, which are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale pink, emerging from the leaf axils. Hoya lucardenasiana thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is highly sought after by collectors and enthusiasts of rare tropical plants.
