Hypericum anagalloides var. cymosum

Hypericum anagalloides var. cymosum, commonly known as the creeping St. John's wort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features prostrate or ascending stems that often root at the nodes, allowing it to spread along the ground. The plant produces small, yellow, four-petaled flowers that bloom in summer, typically from June to August. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
