Helenium mexicanum

Helenium mexicanum, commonly known as Mexican sneezeweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its striking orange to red flowers bloom in late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Helenium mexicanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
