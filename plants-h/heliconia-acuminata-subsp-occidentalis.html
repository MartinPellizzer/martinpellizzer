Heliconia acuminata subsp. occidentalis

Heliconia acuminata subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the Western spike heliconia, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and northern South America. It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble flowers.

The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its inflorescence consists of a spiral of brightly colored, fleshy bracts that enclose the actual flowers.

The plant is well adapted to humid, warm climates and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.
