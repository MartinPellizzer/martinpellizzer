Hyptis cinerea var. stenophylla

Hyptis cinerea var. stenophylla, commonly known as silverleaf hyptis, is a herbaceous perennial native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale blue, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping and ornamental gardening.

Its aromatic leaves and adaptability to various climates contribute to its ecological and horticultural significance.
