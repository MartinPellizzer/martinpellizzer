Hieracium rizense

Hieracium rizense, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinct central disc.

Hieracium rizense is an important component of alpine ecosystems, supporting various pollinators and contributing to biodiversity.
