Hypericum confertum subsp. stenobotrys

Hypericum confertum subsp.

stenobotrys, commonly known as the narrow-leaved St. John's wort, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in sunny, well-drained habitats such as rocky soils or dry woodlands. It produces small, red-orange fruits that contain seeds dispersed by birds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
