Hieracium aequalifolium

Hieracium aequalifolium, commonly known as the equal-leaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 50 centimeters. Its leaves are basal, alternate, and have a rough, hairy texture, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head.

It is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in various habitats.
