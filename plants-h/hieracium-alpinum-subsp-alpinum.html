Hieracium alpinum subsp. alpinum

Hieracium alpinum subsp.

alpinum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to alpine regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or grassy areas at high elevations, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.

The plant has basal leaves that are broadly ovate to lanceolate in shape, with hairy surfaces. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a single head, surrounded by phyllaries that are often tinged with red or purple.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and fibrous root system.
