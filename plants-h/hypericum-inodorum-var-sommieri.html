Hypericum inodorum var. sommieri

Hypericum inodorum var. sommieri, commonly known as the sweet woodruff, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Hypericaceae and is characterized by its fragrant, small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, red-orange berries that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a mild, pleasant aroma, which contributes to its popularity in gardens and traditional herbal medicine.

It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often used as a ground cover or in shaded garden beds.
