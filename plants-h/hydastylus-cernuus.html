Hydastylus cernuus

Hydastylus cernuus, commonly known as the hanging water plant, is a unique aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, thread-like leaves that dangle from long, slender stems, giving it a feathery appearance. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and streams, where it can anchor itself to submerged surfaces. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically appearing in clusters near the water's surface.

Hydastylus cernuus plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and small aquatic life.
