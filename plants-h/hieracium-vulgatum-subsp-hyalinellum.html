Hieracium vulgatum subsp. hyalinellum

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

hyalinellum, commonly known as the translucent hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow composite flowers.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with fine hairs, while the subspecies is distinguished by its translucent, waxy coating on the leaf surfaces. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade.

This plant is often found in temperate climates and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
