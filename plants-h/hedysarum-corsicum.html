Hedysarum corsicum

Hedysarum corsicum, commonly known as Corsican vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers in late spring. Its pods are elongated and contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Hedysarum corsicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soils in arid environments.
