Hieracium incisum subsp. tricephalophorum

Hieracium incisum subsp.

tricephalophorum, commonly known as the three-headed hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique inflorescence, which consists of three separate flower heads. It is native to Europe and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

This plant is considered an invasive species in some regions due to its rapid growth and ability to spread aggressively.
