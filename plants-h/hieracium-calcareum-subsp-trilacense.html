Hieracium calcareum subsp. trilacense

Hieracium calcareum subsp.

trilacense, commonly known as the limestone hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to calcareous grasslands and rocky soils in Europe. It features basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces yellow composite flowers with ray florets.

The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor, alkaline environments. It is often found in open habitats such as meadows and forest edges.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
