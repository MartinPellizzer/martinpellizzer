Horsfieldia odorata

Horsfieldia odorata, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a tropical plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its pendant, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a sweet, spicy aroma. Its leaves are typically broad and strap-like, and the plant thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Horsfieldia odorata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is appreciated for its unique fragrance.
