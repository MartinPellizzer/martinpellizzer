Hieracium wysokae

Hieracium wysokae, commonly known as the highland hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

Hieracium wysokae is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
