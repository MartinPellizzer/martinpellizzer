Heracleum sphondylium subsp. sibiricum

Heracleum sphondylium subsp.

sibiricum, commonly known as Siberian cowparley, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern regions of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats.

The plant features a tall, erect stem with finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic when crushed. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are arranged in umbrella-like clusters, typical of the Apiaceae family.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
