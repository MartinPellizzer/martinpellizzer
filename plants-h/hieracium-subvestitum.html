Hieracium subvestitum

Hieracium subvestitum, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant features hairy, glandular leaves that are typically gray-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, and the plant often grows in moist meadows and along riverbanks.

Hieracium subvestitum is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in supporting local insect populations.
