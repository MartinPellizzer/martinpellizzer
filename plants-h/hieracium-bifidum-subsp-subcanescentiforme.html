Hieracium bifidum subsp. subcanescentiforme

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

subcanescentiforme, commonly known as the hairy yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a rough texture.

The subspecies subcanescentiforme is distinguished by its slightly hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its unique appearance.
