Hoya lanotooensis

Hoya lanotooensis, commonly known as the Lanotooensis Waxy Philodendron, is a rare and unique species native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with shades of white and green. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in clusters. Hoya lanotooensis is a climbing plant that thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-draining soil.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is highly sought after by collectors and horticulturists.
