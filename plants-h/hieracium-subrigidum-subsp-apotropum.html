Hieracium subrigidum subsp. apotropum

Hieracium subrigidum subsp.

apotropum, commonly known as the rigid hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright stems and clustered, yellowish-green flowering heads.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a rough texture. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
