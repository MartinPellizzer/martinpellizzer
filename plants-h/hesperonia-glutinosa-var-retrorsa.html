Hesperonia glutinosa var. retrorsa

Hesperonia glutinosa var. retrorsa, commonly known as the sticky hesperonia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its distinctive, sticky resinous secretions that cover its stems and leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
