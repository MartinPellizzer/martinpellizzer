Helianthus niveus subsp. tephrodes

Helianthus niveus subsp.

tephrodes, commonly known as the whiteleaf sunflower, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its white to pale green leaves and yellow ray flowers that surround a central disk of tubular florets.

The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often in rocky soils. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, and it produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The species is noted for its drought tolerance and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
