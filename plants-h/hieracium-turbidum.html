Hieracium turbidum

Hieracium turbidum, commonly known as the foggy hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy areas and has dark green, hairy leaves. Its flowers are yellow with a central disk, and it produces small, achene-like seeds.

Hieracium turbidum is often found in meadows and along stream banks, where it thrives in humid conditions.
