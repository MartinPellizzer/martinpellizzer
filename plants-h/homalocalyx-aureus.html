Homalocalyx aureus

Homalocalyx aureus, commonly known as the golden homalocalyx, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in lowland rainforests. The plant is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture.

The fruit is a fleshy drupe that contains a single seed, making it an important food source for various wildlife species.
