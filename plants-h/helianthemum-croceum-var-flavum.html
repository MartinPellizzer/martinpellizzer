Helianthemum croceum var. flavum

Helianthemum croceum var. flavum, commonly known as the yellow rockrose, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain water in its arid habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its compact form and vibrant blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
