Hieracium platycerinthe

Hieracium platycerinthe, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are narrow and often have a waxy coating, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain habitats.
