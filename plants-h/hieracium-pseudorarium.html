Hieracium pseudorarium

Hieracium pseudorarium, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, with a basal rosette structure, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

Hieracium pseudorarium is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions and its role in supporting pollinators.
