Hannoa ferruginea

Hannoa ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty thistle, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, hairy stems and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces purple to pink flower heads that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves and stems are covered in a fine, rusty-colored down, giving it its common name.

Hannoa ferruginea is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
