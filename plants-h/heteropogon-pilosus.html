Heteropogon pilosus

Heteropogon pilosus, commonly known as the hairy panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to various regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and disturbed soils, demonstrating a degree of adaptability to different environmental conditions. Its inflorescence consists of a narrow, cylindrical spikelet that is often tinged with purple or brown hues.

Heteropogon pilosus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native ecosystems.
