Hieracium spiculatiforme

Hieracium spiculatiforme, commonly known as the spiky hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features hairy, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in a dense, spiky cluster at the top of the stem.

Hieracium spiculatiforme is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it plays a role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
