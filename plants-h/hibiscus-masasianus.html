Hibiscus masasianus

Hibiscus masasianus, commonly known as the Malayan hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red, with a prominent central pistil. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, often with a slight waxy coating. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring moist, well-drained soil.

Hibiscus masasianus is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking floral display.
