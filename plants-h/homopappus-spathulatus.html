Homopappus spathulatus

Homopappus spathulatus, commonly known as the broadleaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows from 30 to 60 centimeters tall, with broad, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its yellow flowers appear in clusters and bloom from late spring to early summer.

The plant is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.
