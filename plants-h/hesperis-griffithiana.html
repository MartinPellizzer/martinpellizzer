Hesperis griffithiana

Hesperis griffithiana, commonly known as the Kashmir wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces showy, bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high elevations.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens in temperate climates.
