Hieracium bifidum subsp. loennbohmii

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

loennbohmii, commonly known as the narrowleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed sites, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters.

The plant features alternate, narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy surface, and its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. It produces small, oval seeds that are dispersed by wind, and it is often found in open, sunny habitats.

This subspecies is characterized by its distinct leaf morphology and is part of the Asteraceae family.
