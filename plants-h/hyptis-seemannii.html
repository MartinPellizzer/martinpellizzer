Hyptis seemannii

Hyptis seemannii, commonly known as Brazilian hyptis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Hyptis seemannii is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
