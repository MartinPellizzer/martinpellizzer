Helianthus atrorubens subsp. radula

Helianthus atrorubens subsp.

radula, commonly known as the redstem sunflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking red-tinged stems, which are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a rough texture with prominent veins.

This subspecies is adapted to moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
