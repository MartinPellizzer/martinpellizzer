Haplopappus whitneyi var. discoideus

Haplopappus whitneyi var. discoideus, commonly known as the desert hawksbeard, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and bract-like structures. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations such as reduced leaf size and deep root systems. Its small, yellowish flower heads consist of numerous tiny florets arranged in a disk-like structure.

The variety *discoideus* is distinguished by its unique morphological features, including the shape and arrangement of its pappus and bracts.
