Helianthemum apenninum subsp. apenninum

Helianthemum apenninum subsp.

apenninum, commonly known as the Sicilian rock rose, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features compact, succulent leaves that are typically gray-green in color, adapted to arid conditions.

The plant produces showy, bright yellow flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for Mediterranean-style gardens.
