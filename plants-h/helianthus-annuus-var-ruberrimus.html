Helianthus annuus var. ruberrimus

Helianthus annuus var. ruberrimus, commonly known as the scarlet sunflower, is a variant of the common sunflower characterized by its vibrant red petals and yellow center.

It is native to the United States and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. The plant produces large, showy flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its botanical features include sturdy stems, lobed leaves, and a robust root system.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and landscapes.
