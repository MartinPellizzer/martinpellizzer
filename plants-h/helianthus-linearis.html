Helianthus linearis

Helianthus linearis, commonly known as the linear sunflower, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its slender, linear leaves and yellow, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Helianthus linearis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
