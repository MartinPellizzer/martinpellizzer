Hesperis minutiflora

Hesperis minutiflora, commonly known as the narrow-flowered candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun, often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its delicate appearance and early blooming period make it a notable species in botanical gardens and wildflower meadows.

Hesperis minutiflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
