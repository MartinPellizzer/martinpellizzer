Hybanthus enneaspermus var. nyassensis

Hybanthus enneaspermus var. nyassensis, commonly known as the African yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape with a smooth margin. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain multiple seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
