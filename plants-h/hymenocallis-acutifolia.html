Hymenocallis acutifolia

Hymenocallis acutifolia, commonly known as the sharpleaf spider lily, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and showy, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from white to yellow and orange. The plant produces a single, tall stem that bears several blooms, often with a distinctive greenish hue at the base. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens in warmer climates.

Hymenocallis acutifolia is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral display.
