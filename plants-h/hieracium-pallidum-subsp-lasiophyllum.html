Hieracium pallidum subsp. lasiophyllum

Hieracium pallidum subsp.

lasiophyllum, commonly known as the pale hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.

The plant features upright stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and are followed by oval, hairy seeds.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which distinguish it from other hawkweed species.
