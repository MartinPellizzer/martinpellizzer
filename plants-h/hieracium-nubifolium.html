Hieracium nubifolium

Hieracium nubifolium, commonly known as the cloudless hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides, and it thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators.
