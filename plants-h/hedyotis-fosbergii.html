Hedyotis fosbergii

Hedyotis fosbergii, commonly known as Fosberg's moneywort, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It typically forms dense mats of small, oval leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors.

Hedyotis fosbergii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
