Hypericum ternatum

Hypericum ternatum, commonly known as lesser St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its stems are typically reddish or purplish in color, and it thrives in moist, woodland habitats.

It is often used in traditional medicine and has been studied for its potential therapeutic properties.
