Hieracium murorum subsp. olympicola

Hieracium murorum subsp.

olympicola, commonly known as Olympic Hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and produces yellow flowers in the summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and forest edges. It has a hairy stem and leaves with a serrated margin.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
