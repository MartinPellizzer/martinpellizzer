Hyoseris grumosa

Hyoseris grumosa, commonly known as the roughleaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy, lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Hyoseris grumosa produces small, spiky fruits known as achenes, which are dispersed by wind.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
