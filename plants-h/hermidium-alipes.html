Hermidium alipes

Hermidium alipes, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, strap-like leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, with a subtle scent that attracts specific pollinators.

Hermidium alipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
