Hieracium cordatum subsp. legrandianum

Hieracium cordatum subsp.

legrandianum, commonly known as the heartleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has a fibrous root system and produces numerous flowering stems that bear small, clustered flowers.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
