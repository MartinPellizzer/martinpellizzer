Hypericum roeperianum var. schimperi

Hypericum roeperianum var. schimperi, commonly known as the Schimper's St. John's Wort, is a perennial shrub native to the highlands of East Africa.

It features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its medicinal properties have been traditionally used in local communities for their anti-inflammatory and antiseptic qualities.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or alpine garden settings.
