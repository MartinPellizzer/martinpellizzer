Hieracium leptocladum

Hieracium leptocladum, commonly known as the slender-stemmed hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typical sunflower-like structure. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy surface, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

Hieracium leptocladum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
