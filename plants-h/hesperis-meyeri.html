Hesperis meyeri

Hesperis meyeri, commonly known as the Meyer's candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Hesperis meyeri is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
