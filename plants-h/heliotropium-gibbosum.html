Heliotropium gibbosum

Heliotropium gibbosum, commonly known as the globe mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which are often covered in fine, sticky hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in disturbed habitats.

Heliotropium gibbosum is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
