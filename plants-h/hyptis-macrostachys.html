Hyptis macrostachys

Hyptis macrostachys, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and spikes of blue or purple flowers. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

Hyptis macrostachys thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or coastal areas.
