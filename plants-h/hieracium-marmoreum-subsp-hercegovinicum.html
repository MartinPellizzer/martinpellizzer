Hieracium marmoreum subsp. hercegovinicum

Hieracium marmoreum subsp.

hercegovinicum, commonly known as the Hercegovina hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellowish to orange flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has a fibrous root system and produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in parts of its range.
