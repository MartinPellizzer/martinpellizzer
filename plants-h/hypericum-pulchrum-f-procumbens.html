Hypericum pulchrum f. procumbens

Hypericum pulchrum f. procumbens, commonly known as the prostrate St. John's wort, is a low-growing, flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It features prostrate or ascending stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are yellow, with five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Hypericaceae family.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
