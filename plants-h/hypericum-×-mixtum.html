Hypericum × mixtum

Hypericum × mixtum, commonly known as the hybrid St. John's wort, is a cross between Hypericum perforatum and Hypericum patulum.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and alternate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late summer. Its botanical characteristics include the presence of glandular dots on the leaves and a distinctive, aromatic fragrance.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
