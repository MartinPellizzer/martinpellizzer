Hieracium subspeciosum subsp. patulum

Hieracium subspeciosum subsp.

patulum, commonly known as the showy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies.

The plant typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and the plant has hairy stems and leaves.

It is often found in open habitats and is relatively common in its native range.
