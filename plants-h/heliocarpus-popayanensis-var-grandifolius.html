Heliocarpus popayanensis var. grandifolius

Heliocarpus popayanensis var. grandifolius, commonly known as the grandifolius variant of the Popayan burr palm, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces distinctive, woody fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and conservation efforts.
