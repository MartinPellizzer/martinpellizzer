Huanaca

Huanaca, commonly known as the "Andean moonflower," is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant produces small, round berries that contain the seeds. Huanaca is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in Andean cultures.

Its botanical features make it a unique and culturally significant species in the region.
