Hieracium maccoshiana

Hieracium maccoshiana, commonly known as the MacCosh's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, and its leaves are usually lance-shaped with a rough texture.

Hieracium maccoshiana is often found in rocky or grassy habitats at high elevations.
