Hyptis marrubioides

Hyptis marrubioides, commonly known as wild basil or lemon verbena, is a perennial herb native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and culinary uses. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a fragrant, slightly hairy surface. Its small, tubular flowers are typically pale blue to violet, arranged in whorled clusters.

Hyptis marrubioides is valued for its essential oils, which contain compounds like citral and limonene, contributing to its strong lemony scent and potential therapeutic properties.
