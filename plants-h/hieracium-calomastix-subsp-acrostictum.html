Hieracium calomastix subsp. acrostictum

Hieracium calomastix subsp.

acrostictum, commonly known as the purple hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped with hairy surfaces, and the flowers are yellow with purple-tinged bracts.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
