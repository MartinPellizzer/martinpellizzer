Hedypnois mauritanica

Hedypnois mauritanica, commonly known as the Mauritania thistle, is a flowering plant native to North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and spiny leaves. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The flowers are yellow, appearing in clusters and attracting various pollinators.
