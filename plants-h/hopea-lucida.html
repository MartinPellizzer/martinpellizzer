Hopea lucida

Hopea lucida, commonly known as the white ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall, with a broad, umbrella-shaped crown and dark green, leathery leaves. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically yellow-green in color, and it produces woody capsules that contain several seeds.

Hopea lucida plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
