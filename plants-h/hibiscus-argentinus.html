Hibiscus argentinus

Hibiscus argentinus, commonly known as the Argentine hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and red. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Hibiscus argentinus grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
