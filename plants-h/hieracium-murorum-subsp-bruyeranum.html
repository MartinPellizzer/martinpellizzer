Hieracium murorum subsp. bruyeranum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

bruyeranum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to orange-yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showing adaptations to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rough texture and a serrated margin.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in alpine ecosystems and is often found in meadows and grasslands.
