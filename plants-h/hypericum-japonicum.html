Hypericum japonicum

Hypericum japonicum, commonly known as Japanese St. John's Wort, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features erect, branching stems covered in small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its roots are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

The plant is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
