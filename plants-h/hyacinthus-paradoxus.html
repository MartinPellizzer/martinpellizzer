Hyacinthus paradoxus

Hyacinthus paradoxus, commonly known as the Persian hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East.

It produces striking clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is valued for its ornamental beauty in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Hyacinthus paradoxus is also known for its historical significance in Persian culture and horticulture.
