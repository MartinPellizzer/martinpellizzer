Hieracium × raripilum

Hieracium × raripilum, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a hybrid species within the genus Hieracium, characterized by its low-growing habit and yellow flowers.

It is native to Europe and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant typically has slender, hairy stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped. Its flowers are arranged in a single head, with ray florets that are usually yellow and tubular florets in the center.

Hieracium × raripilum is a perennial plant that spreads through both seeds and rhizomes, making it somewhat invasive in certain regions.
