Hamamelis japonica f. obtusata

Hamamelis japonica f. obtusata, commonly known as the Japanese witch hazel.

This cultivar is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late autumn. The leaves are oval-shaped with a smooth margin, and the plant produces small, red-brown capsules that contain the seeds. It is a deciduous shrub that thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
