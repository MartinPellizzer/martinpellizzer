Haageocereus limensis

Haageocereus limensis, commonly known as the Limen Cereus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces large, white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and well-draining soil.

Its structure and flowering habits make it a unique and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
