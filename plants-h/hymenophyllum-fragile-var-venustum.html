Hymenophyllum fragile var. venustum

Hymenophyllum fragile var. venustum, commonly known as the silver maidenhair fern, is a delicate and rare fern species native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silvery-green fronds that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments, typically found in rocky crevices or on stream banks. Its small, spore-producing structures, called sori, are arranged in clusters on the underside of the fronds.

This variety is distinguished from the typical Hymenophyllum fragile by its more pronounced silvery appearance and slightly different habitat preferences.
