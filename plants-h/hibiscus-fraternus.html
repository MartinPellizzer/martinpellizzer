Hibiscus fraternus

Hibiscus fraternus, commonly known as the swamp hibiscus, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetlands and marshy areas in the southeastern United States.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soil. Hibiscus fraternus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide habitat for pollinators.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for wetland restoration projects.
