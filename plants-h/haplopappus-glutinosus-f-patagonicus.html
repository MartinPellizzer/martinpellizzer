Haplopappus glutinosus f. patagonicus

Haplopappus glutinosus f. patagonicus, commonly known as the Patagonian sticky hawksbeard, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant is distinguished by its glandular, sticky leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its unique morphology and adaptability to arid environments make it a notable species in its ecological niche.
