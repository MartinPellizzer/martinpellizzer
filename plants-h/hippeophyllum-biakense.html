Hippeophyllum biakense

Hippeophyllum biakense, commonly known as the Biak Begonia, is a species of flowering plant native to the Indonesian island of Biak.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often colorful leaves and showy bracts. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial herb, often found in humid, shaded forest environments. Its botanical features include fleshy stems, heart-shaped leaves, and small, inconspicuous flowers that are often surrounded by vibrant, petal-like bracts.

This species is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique morphology and potential ornamental value.
