Hypericum verrucosum

Hypericum verrucosum, commonly known as the rough-stemmed St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and rough, hairy stems, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are opposite and have a slightly serrated edge, while its flowers bloom from June to August.

It is often found in open habitats such as woodlands, meadows, and along roadsides.
