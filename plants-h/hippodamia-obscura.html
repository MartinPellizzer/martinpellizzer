Hippodamia obscura

Hippodamia obscura, commonly known as the obscure ladybird, is a species of ladybird beetle native to various parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped body and a distinctive black or dark red coloration with yellow or orange markings. The plant it is associated with is typically a host for its larvae, such as certain species of grasses and weeds. Its larvae feed on aphids, making it an important natural pest controller in agricultural settings.

The beetle's life cycle includes four stages: egg, larva, pupa, and adult, with the adult stage being the most commonly observed.
