Henribaillonia

Henribaillonia, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are upright and branching. Henribaillonia thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in various horticultural settings.
