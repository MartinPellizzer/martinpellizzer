Hieracium pilosella subsp. virescens

Hieracium pilosella subsp.

virescens, commonly known as the green hairgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and small, greenish-yellow flower heads that resemble dandelions.

The subspecies is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The plant produces seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread across various habitats.
