Hesperis matronalis subsp. oblongifolia

Hesperis matronalis subsp.

oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sweet rocket, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe. It features upright stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and have a slightly serrated edge.

The plant produces clusters of bright pink to purple flowers with four petals, blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long flowering period and attractive foliage.
