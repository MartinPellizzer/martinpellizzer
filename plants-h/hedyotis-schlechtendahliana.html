Hedyotis schlechtendahliana

Hedyotis schlechtendahliana, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the wet season. The plant has slender, ascending stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens in warm climates.
