Hypericum quadrangulum f. undulatum

Hypericum quadrangulum f. undulatum, commonly known as the wavy-stemmed St. John's wort, is a compact shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are typically oval with a slightly wavy margin, giving it its botanical name. The plant has four-sided stems that are often hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
