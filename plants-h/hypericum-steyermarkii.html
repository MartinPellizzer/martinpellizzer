Hypericum steyermarkii

Hypericum steyermarkii, commonly known as the Steyermark's St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in the summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is typically found in moist, open habitats such as swamps and wetlands. It belongs to the family Hypericaceae and is closely related to other species of St. John's wort.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
