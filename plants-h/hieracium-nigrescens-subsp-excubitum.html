Hieracium nigrescens subsp. excubitum

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

excubitum, commonly known as the black knapweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It has a taproot system and is often found in regions with temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
