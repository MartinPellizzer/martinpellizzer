Hyacinthus dichrous

Hyacinthus dichrous, commonly known as the double hyacinth, is a bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that come in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant produces two layers of petals, giving it its name "dichrous," which means "two-faced" in Greek. It typically blooms in early spring and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Hyacinthus dichrous is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
