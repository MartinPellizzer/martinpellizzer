Hackelia macrantha

Hackelia macrantha, commonly known as the showy hackelia, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pink to purple, with five petals and prominent stamens, making it a notable ornamental plant.

Hackelia macrantha is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
