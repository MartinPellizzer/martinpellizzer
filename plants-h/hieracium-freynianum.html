Hieracium freynianum

Hieracium freynianum, commonly known as Freyn's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

Its flowering period occurs in the summer, producing yellow disk flowers surrounded by pale green to reddish ray florets.
