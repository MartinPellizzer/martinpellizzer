Hieracium murorum subsp. monochroum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

monochroum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

It is often found in disturbed areas and can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
