Hieracium subauriculoides

Hieracium subauriculoides, commonly known as the auriculate hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are hairy and have a distinctive auriculate (ear-like) base, giving it its common name.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators.
