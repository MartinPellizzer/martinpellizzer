Heuchera hemisphaerica

Heuchera hemisphaerica, commonly known as the red-veined heuchera, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking foliage, which features deep green leaves with prominent red veins. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers in the spring. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value lies primarily in its foliage, though it also adds visual interest with its delicate blooms.
