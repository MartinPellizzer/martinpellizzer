Hebe astonii

Hebe astonii, commonly known as the Stewart Island hebe, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a shrub that typically grows in the understory of forests, particularly on the Stewart Island. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are usually a deep green color. It produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple during the spring and summer months.

Hebe astonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and aesthetic value.
