Helleborine latifolia f. montana

Helleborine latifolia f. montana, commonly known as the broad-leaved helleborine, is a hybrid orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of greenish-white flowers with a distinctive, spurred lip. The plant is a perennial herb that thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in woodlands and rocky areas. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.

Its growth habit and foliage make it a unique addition to botanical gardens and natural habitats.
