Hieracium querianum subsp. maladettae

Hieracium querianum subsp.

maladettae, commonly known as the Maladetta hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained, rocky soils. It features wiry stems and basal leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Its flowers bloom in late summer, producing yellowish to reddish hues that attract various pollinators.
