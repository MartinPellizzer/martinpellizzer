Hieracium rectum subsp. dipsacifolium

Hieracium rectum subsp.

dipsacifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 50 centimeters.

The plant features basal leaves that are linear to lanceolate, with a hairy surface, and upright flowering stems bearing yellow disk flowers surrounded by pale yellow to reddish ray florets. Its inflorescence is a composite head, characteristic of the Asteraceae family, and it produces small, oval achenes as fruits.

The plant is adapted to temperate climates and is often found in sunny, well-drained habitats.
