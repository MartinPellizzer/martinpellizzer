Hordeum vulgare var. nutans

Hordeum vulgare var. nutans, commonly known as wild barley, is a wild species of the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and spikelets that hang downward. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers, which are typically green or pale yellow. Its seeds are enclosed in lemma and palea, typical of the grass family structure.

Wild barley is often found in disturbed soils and is an important forage plant for livestock in certain regions.
