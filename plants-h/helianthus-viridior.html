Helianthus viridior

Helianthus viridior, commonly known as greenleaf sunflower, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial with upright, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically green to blue-green in color. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with a central disk, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
