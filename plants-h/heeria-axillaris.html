Heeria axillaris

Heeria axillaris, commonly known as the creeping heeria, is a small, evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It features dense, rounded foliage with small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white, fragrant flowers during the summer months. It is often found growing in rocky or sandy soils in coastal and inland regions.

Heeria axillaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in gardens and as a ground cover.
