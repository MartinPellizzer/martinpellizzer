Helichrysum backhousei var. oreophilum

Helichrysum backhousei var. oreophilum, commonly known as the mountain everlasting, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces small, golden-yellow flowers that resemble tiny sunflowers. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is drought-tolerant once established.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and as a border plant.
