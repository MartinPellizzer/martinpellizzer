Hieracium dentatum subsp. coloratifolium

Hieracium dentatum subsp.

coloratifolium, commonly known as the colorful leaf groundsel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Its foliage is notable for its vivid, often variegated coloration, which contributes to its ornamental value.
