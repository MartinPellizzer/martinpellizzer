Heteropogon buchneri

Heteropogon buchneri, commonly known as the Buchner's grass, is a perennial grass species native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, which are typical of grasses.

Heteropogon buchneri is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for landscaping purposes.
