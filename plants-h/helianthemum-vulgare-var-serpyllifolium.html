Helianthemum vulgare var. serpyllifolium

Helianthemum vulgare var. serpyllifolium, commonly known as the common rockrose, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features small, clustered flowers that range from white to pale yellow, typically blooming in spring and early summer. The plant has hairy, often succulent leaves that are adapted to arid conditions, and its stems are usually prostrate or ascending. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

This variety is closely related to other members of the Cistaceae family, sharing similar ecological and morphological traits.
