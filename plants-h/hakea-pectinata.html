Hakea pectinata

Hakea pectinata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hakea, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and distinctive cylindrical or spiny flower spikes. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. Hakea pectinata is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system for water storage.

It is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ornamental value.
