Hippocratea malifolia

Hippocratea malifolia, commonly known as the sweet-scented orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has strap-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that emit a sweet aroma. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
