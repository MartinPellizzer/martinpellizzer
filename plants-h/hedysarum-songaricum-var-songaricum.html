Hedysarum songaricum var. songaricum

Hedysarum songaricum var. songaricum, commonly known as the Songaric sweetvetch, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces elongated pods containing several seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in steppe and semi-arid environments.

This species is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability and has been used in ecological restoration projects.
