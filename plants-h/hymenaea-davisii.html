Hymenaea davisii

Hymenaea davisii, commonly known as the Davis's ironwood, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dense, hard wood and compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by elongated pods containing flat, kidney-shaped seeds. Hymenaea davisii is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Its ecological importance includes providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
