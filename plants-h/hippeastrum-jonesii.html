Hippeastrum jonesii

Hippeastrum jonesii, commonly known as the Jones' amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It features large, showy flowers that can range in color from white to deep red, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces a tall, sturdy stem that bears multiple blooms, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening. Its bulbs are perennial, capable of surviving through winter dormancy and regrowing each spring.

The foliage consists of broad, strap-like leaves that emerge from the base of the bulb.
