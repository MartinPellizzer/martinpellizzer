Hygrophila oligosperma

Hygrophila oligosperma, commonly known as water hyssop, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Hygrophila oligosperma thrives in moist or aquatic environments, making it a common species in wetlands and marshes.

It is often used in ornamental gardening and has traditional medicinal applications in some cultures.
