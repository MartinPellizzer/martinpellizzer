Hieracium procerum subsp. buhsei

Hieracium procerum subsp.

buhsei, commonly known as the Buhse's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its rosette-forming basal leaves and bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils. It has small, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its range.
