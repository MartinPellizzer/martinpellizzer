Hedychium glabrum

Hedychium glabrum, commonly known as the white ginger lily, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces large, showy white flowers with a strong, spicy fragrance, which are clustered in inflorescences at the top of the stems. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a smooth, glabrous surface.

Hedychium glabrum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
