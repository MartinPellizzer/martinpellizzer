Helichrysum guilelmi

Helichrysum guilelmi, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that remain vibrant even after drying. The plant produces small, tubular flowers surrounded by ray florets, which give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic foliage and long-lasting blooms make it a popular choice for both ornamental and medicinal uses.
