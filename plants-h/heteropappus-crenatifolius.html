Heteropappus crenatifolius

Heteropappus crenatifolius, commonly known as the roughleaf heteropappus, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive bracts. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are usually rough and serrated, contributing to its common name.

The species is known for its yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators.
