Hieracium rigidum subsp. epacroides

Hieracium rigidum subsp.

epacroides, commonly known as the rigid hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade.

The plant features upright, hairy stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped with serrated edges. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a composite head, and are surrounded by pale green to white bracts.

It is characterized by its rigid, upright growth habit and is often found in temperate regions.
