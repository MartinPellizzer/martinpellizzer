Hieracium dimorphophyllum

Hieracium dimorphophyllum, commonly known as the two-form leaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy basal rosette. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows. Its leaves are usually alternate and have a distinctive lobed or toothed appearance.

The flowers are yellow and are arranged in a single inflorescence at the top of the stem.
