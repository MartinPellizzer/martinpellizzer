Haworthia pygmaea var. argenteomaculosa

Haworthia pygmaea var. argenteomaculosa, commonly known as the silver-spotted haworthia, is a compact, slow-growing succulent native to South Africa.

It features a rosette of fleshy, triangular leaves that are typically a pale green with distinctive silver or white spots. The leaves are covered in fine, translucent hairs, giving the plant a unique, almost glass-like appearance. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is prized for its striking appearance and ease of care, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
