Hunteria eburnea

Hunteria eburnea, commonly known as the white hunteria, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces a milky sap that is toxic, containing cardiac glycosides. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in rocky areas.

Hunteria eburnea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
