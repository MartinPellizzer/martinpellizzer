Hemigenia appressa

Hemigenia appressa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hemigenia, is a small, evergreen shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils in coastal and inland regions.

Hemigenia appressa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its fragrance and visual interest.
