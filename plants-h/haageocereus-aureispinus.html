Haageocereus aureispinus

Haageocereus aureispinus, commonly known as the golden spine cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in bright yellow spines, which provide protection from herbivores and intense sunlight. The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its greenish-gray foliage. It is a low-maintenance plant that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in desert gardens.
