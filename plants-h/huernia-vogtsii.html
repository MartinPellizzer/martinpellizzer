Huernia vogtsii

Huernia vogtsii, commonly known as the Vogtsii moon cactus, is a succulent native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and distinctive, spiky flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss, and it produces yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months.

Huernia vogtsii is popular among succulent enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
