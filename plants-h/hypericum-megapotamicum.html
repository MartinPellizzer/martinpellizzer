Hypericum megapotamicum

Hypericum megapotamicum, commonly known as the Andean St. John's Wort, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in the Andes region.

It belongs to the genus Hypericum, which includes numerous species known for their yellow flowers and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, thriving in temperate to subtropical climates. Its leaves are opposite and oval-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers are bright yellow with orange stamens.

The plant is noted for its potential use in traditional medicine, though its pharmacological properties require further scientific investigation.
