Hieracium glomeratum

Hieracium glomeratum, commonly known as the clustered hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in dense clusters.

Hieracium glomeratum is often considered a weed but has historical uses in herbal medicine.
