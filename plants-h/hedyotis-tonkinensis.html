Hedyotis tonkinensis

Hedyotis tonkinensis, commonly known as Vietnamese knotweed, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features slender, creeping stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, white to pinkish flowers are clustered in racemes and bloom in the summer months.

Hedyotis tonkinensis is valued in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
