Hieracium pilosum subsp. leucopogon

Hieracium pilosum subsp.

leucopogon, commonly known as the white-haired hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies.
