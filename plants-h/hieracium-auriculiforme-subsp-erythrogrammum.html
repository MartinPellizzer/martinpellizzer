Hieracium auriculiforme subsp. erythrogrammum

Hieracium auriculiforme subsp.

erythrogrammum, commonly known as the red-stemmed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in meadows, pastures, and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features upright stems that are often reddish in color, especially when mature. Its basal leaves are lance-shaped and often have hairy surfaces, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, composite flower heads.
