Haageocereus pseudomelanostele subsp. acanthocladus

Haageocereus pseudomelanostele subsp.

acanthocladus, commonly known as the spiny-leaved cactus, is a species native to Mexico. It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are typically ribbed and covered in spines.

The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat preferences, it is a subject of interest in botanical studies and cactus cultivation.
