Hechtia argentea

Hechtia argentea, commonly known as silver hechtia, is a striking plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of white, powdery wax. The leaves are typically narrow and pointed, with a silver-gray appearance that gives the plant its common name. It produces striking inflorescences with bright orange or red flowers that bloom in the spring.

This plant is well adapted to dry environments and is often found in rocky or limestone soils.
