Hydnophytum borneense

Hydnophytum borneense, commonly known as the Bornean mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees in the rainforests of Borneo.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while also producing its own carbohydrates through photosynthesis using the limited light it receives. The plant has a distinctive appearance with small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of white or yellow flowers. Its seeds are dispersed by birds that feed on its fleshy, red berries.

Hydnophytum borneense plays a unique ecological role in its habitat, contributing to the biodiversity of the rainforest canopy.
