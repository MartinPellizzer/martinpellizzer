Heracleum latifolium

Heracleum latifolium, commonly known as broadleaf cowparn.

It is a tall biennial or short-lived perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features a robust, hollow stem that is often covered in fine hairs. Its large, compound leaves can reach up to 30 cm in length, with deeply lobed or pinnate divisions.

The flowers are small and yellow-green, arranged in umbrella-like clusters called umbels, which are characteristic of theApiaceae family.
