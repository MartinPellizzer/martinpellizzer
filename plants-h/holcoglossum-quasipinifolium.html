Holcoglossum quasipinifolium

Holcoglossum quasipinifolium, commonly known as the pine-leaf holly fern, is a rare and striking member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern China and is characterized by its elegant, needle-like leaves that resemble pine needles. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow spore-bearing structures called soruses on the underside of its fronds. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in shaded, forested areas.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a valuable species in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
