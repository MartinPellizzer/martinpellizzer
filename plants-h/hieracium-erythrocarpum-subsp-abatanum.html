Hieracium erythrocarpum subsp. abatanum

Hieracium erythrocarpum subsp.

abatanum, commonly known as the red-fruited hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf structures.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped with rough textures, and the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

The subspecies is notable for its red-tinged, oval-shaped fruits, which contribute to its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
