Hydrophyllum virginianum var. patens

Hydrophyllum virginianum var. patens, commonly known as the spreading blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its blue to purple flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, prairies, and along stream banks. Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer.

The species is noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various insects.
