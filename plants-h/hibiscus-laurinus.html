Hibiscus laurinus

Hibiscus laurinus, commonly known as the velvetleaf hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It features large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a prominent yellow center. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are velvety to the touch, giving it its common name. Hibiscus laurinus thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
