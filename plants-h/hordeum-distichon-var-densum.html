Hordeum distichon var. densum

Hordeum distichon var. densum, commonly known as the dense two-row barley.

This variety is characterized by its compact, dense arrangement of spikelets along the stem, which distinguishes it from other barley types. It is a grass species native to temperate regions and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves and a cylindrical inflorescence.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both agricultural and ecological studies.
