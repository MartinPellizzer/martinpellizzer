Hyoscyamus ceratophyllus

Hyoscyamus ceratophyllus, commonly known as the horned toad plant, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny, horn-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and contains toxic alkaloids.

Due to its ornamental value and toxicity, it is often grown in controlled environments.
