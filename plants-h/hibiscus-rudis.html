Hibiscus rudis

Hibiscus rudis, commonly known as the sea hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink or red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in coastal areas where it can tolerate salt spray. Hibiscus rudis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sunlight to flourish.
