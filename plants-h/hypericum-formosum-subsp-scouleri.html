Hypericum formosum subsp. scouleri

Hypericum formosum subsp.

scouleri, commonly known as Scouler's St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky slopes.

The plant features small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and often branched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
