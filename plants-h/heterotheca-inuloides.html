Heterotheca inuloides

Heterotheca inuloides, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant is known for its showy, blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its foliage is gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Heterotheca inuloides is drought-tolerant and often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
