Hypanthera

Hypanthera, commonly known as the moonflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, white, night-blooming flowers that emit a strong fragrance. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its leaves are broad and glossy, while its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds.
