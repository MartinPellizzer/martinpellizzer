Hieracium wettsteinianum

Hieracium wettsteinianum, commonly known as the Wettstein's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a dense, flat head.

It is often found in temperate regions and is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed habitats.
