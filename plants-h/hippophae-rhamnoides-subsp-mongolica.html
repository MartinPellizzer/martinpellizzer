Hippophae rhamnoides subsp. mongolica

Hippophae rhamnoides subsp.

mongolica, commonly known as the Mongolian sea buckthorn, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its small, yellow-orange berries that are rich in vitamins and antioxidants.

The plant has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in poor soils and withstand harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, with a rough texture, and it produces yellow flowers in the spring.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in preventing soil erosion and its economic importance in traditional medicine and nutrition.
