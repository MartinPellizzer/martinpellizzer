Hieracium atratiforme subsp. basipellitum

Hieracium atratiforme subsp.

basipellitum, commonly known as the black-centred hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its dark purple to black central disk in the flower heads.

The plant typically grows in dry grasslands and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a rough texture, while its stems are erect and hairy.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and distribution.
