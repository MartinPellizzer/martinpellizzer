Helianthemum chamaecistus f. multiplex

Helianthemum chamaecistus f. multiplex, commonly known as the multiflorous rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub that thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer, adding a vibrant touch to its surroundings. Its leaves are typically gray-green, covered in a dense layer of fine hairs that help reduce water loss.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
