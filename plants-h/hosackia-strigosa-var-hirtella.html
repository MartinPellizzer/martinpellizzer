Hosackia strigosa var. hirtella

Hosackia strigosa var. hirtella, commonly known as the hairy prairie clover, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as prairies and sandy soils.

This variety is often found in the central and eastern United States.
