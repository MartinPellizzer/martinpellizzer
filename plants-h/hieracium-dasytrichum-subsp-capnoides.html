Hieracium dasytrichum subsp. capnoides

Hieracium dasytrichum subsp.

capnoides, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems.

The plant produces yellow flowers that are typically arranged in dense clusters at the top of the stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine, stiff hairs, contributing to its rough texture.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other open habitats.
