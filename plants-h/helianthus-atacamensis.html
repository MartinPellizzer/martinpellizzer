Helianthus atacamensis

Helianthus atacamensis, commonly known as the Atacama sunflower, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and robust, hairy stems. This plant has adapted to extreme environmental conditions, including high salinity and low water availability. Its leaves are typically small and covered in fine hairs to reduce water loss.

Helianthus atacamensis plays a role in stabilizing desert soils and provides nectar for pollinators in its native habitat.
