Hayata tabiyahanensis var. merrillii

Hayata tabiyahanensis var. merrillii, commonly known as the Philippine violet, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, soft hair, while its stems are slender and upright.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
