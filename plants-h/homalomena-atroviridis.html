Homalomena atroviridis

Homalomena atroviridis, commonly known as the black-veined arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves with dark green veins. The plant produces striking green to white spatheal inflorescences, which are typical of the arum family. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often grown as an ornamental plant.

Its foliage is prized for its aesthetic appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of indoor conditions.
