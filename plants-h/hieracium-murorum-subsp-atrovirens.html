Hieracium murorum subsp. atrovirens

Hieracium murorum subsp.

atrovirens, commonly known as the blackleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring moist to moderately dry conditions.

The plant features dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. Its flowers are typically visited by various pollinators, including bees and flies.

The subspecies is distinguished by its darker foliage and slightly different flower structure compared to other members of the Hieracium genus.
