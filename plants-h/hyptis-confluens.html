Hyptis confluens

Hyptis confluens, commonly known as the Brazilian hyptis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite leaves that are often hairy and lance-shaped. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in dense clusters.

Hyptis confluens is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
