Hieracium erectifrons

Hieracium erectifrons, commonly known as the upright-fronded hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. It has basal leaves that are usually lance-shaped and hairy, with a stem that is upright and often reddish in color.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
