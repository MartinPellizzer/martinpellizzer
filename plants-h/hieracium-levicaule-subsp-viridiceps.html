Hieracium levicaule subsp. viridiceps

Hieracium levicaule subsp.

viridiceps, commonly known as the green-headed hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets.

The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a distinctive greenish central disc.

It is a perennial species that spreads through rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
