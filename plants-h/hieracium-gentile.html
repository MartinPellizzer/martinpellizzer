Hieracium gentile

Hieracium gentile, commonly known as the noble hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has basal and stem leaves that are typically lance-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Hieracium gentile is known for its ornamental value and has been cultivated for its vibrant, daisy-like appearance.
