Hieracium fuscocinereum subsp. philanthrax

Hieracium fuscocinereum subsp.

philanthrax, commonly known as the black-capped hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its leaves are lanceolate, with a rough, hairy texture, and the flowers are yellow with a central disc.

It is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.
