Hieracium hyperdoxum

Hieracium hyperdoxum, commonly known as the noble hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers with prominent central disk florets, often surrounded by ray florets. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Hieracium hyperdoxum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant appearance.
