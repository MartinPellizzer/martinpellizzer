Hemerocallis maculata

Hemerocallis maculata, commonly known as the spotted daylily, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red, often with distinctive spots or streaks. The plant produces numerous narrow, grass-like leaves and is typically grown for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

Hemerocallis maculata is a perennial that blooms for a short period, usually in summer, and is known for its resilience in various soil types.
