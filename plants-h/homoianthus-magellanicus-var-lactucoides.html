Homoianthus magellanicus var. lactucoides

Homoianthus magellanicus var. lactucoides, commonly known as the South American lettuce plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southern regions of South America.

It features basal rosettes of elongated, fleshy leaves that are often lobed or toothed. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a single terminal head, which is a characteristic feature of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in grasslands and along stream banks.

Its leaves are edible and have a mild, slightly bitter taste, making it a potential forage plant for both humans and livestock.
