Hieracium inuloides subsp. reticulatum

Hieracium inuloides subsp.

reticulatum, commonly known as the reticulate hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and cylindrical stems.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture, and its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
