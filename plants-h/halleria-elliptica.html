Halleria elliptica

Halleria elliptica, commonly known as the red hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers, which bloom in clusters and are often used in traditional medicine. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas or near water sources.

Halleria elliptica is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its medicinal properties.
