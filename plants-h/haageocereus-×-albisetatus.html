Haageocereus × albisetatus

Haageocereus × albisetatus, commonly known as the White-Flowered Hedgehog Cactus, is a hybrid cactus species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white, bristly spines and clusters of white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soil. It is a popular choice for xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
