Hedysarum bordzilovskyi

Hedysarum bordzilovskyi, commonly known as the Karakachan milkvetch, is a perennial leguminous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit, trifoliate leaves, and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces cylindrical, dehiscent pods that contain several elliptical seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to temperate to cold climates.

Hedysarum bordzilovskyi plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
