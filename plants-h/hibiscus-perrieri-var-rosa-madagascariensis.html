Hibiscus perrieri var. rosa-madagascariensis

Hibiscus perrieri var. rosa-madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar rose hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forested areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated for its striking floral display.
