Hibiscus pseudodiplocrater

Hibiscus pseudodiplocrater, commonly known as the African hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and its stems are woody and branched. Hibiscus pseudodiplocrater thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
