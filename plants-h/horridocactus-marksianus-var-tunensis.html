Horridocactus marksianus var. tunensis

Horridocactus marksianus var. tunensis, commonly known as the Tunisian devil's tongue, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to North Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne at the base of the stems. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for rock gardens and succulent collections.
