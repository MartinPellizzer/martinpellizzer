Heliconia maculata

Heliconia maculata, commonly known as the spotted heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that enclose its small, clustered flowers. The plant belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its leaves are large, elongated, and often have a waxy texture.
