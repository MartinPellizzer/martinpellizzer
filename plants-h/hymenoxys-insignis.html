Hymenoxys insignis

Hymenoxys insignis, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its bright orange to yellow flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hymenoxys insignis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
