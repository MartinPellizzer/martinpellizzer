Haemocharis aromatica

Haemocharis aromatica, commonly known as the fragrant arrowroot or sweet arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its showy, spadix-like inflorescence surrounded by a colorful spathe. The plant produces a starchy root that is used in various culinary applications. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
