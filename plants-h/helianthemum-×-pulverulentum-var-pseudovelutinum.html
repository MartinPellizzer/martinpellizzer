Helianthemum × pulverulentum var. pseudovelutinum

Helianthemum × pulverulentum var. pseudovelutinum, commonly known as the woolly rockrose, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features dense, compact foliage with greyish-white, woolly hairs that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, typically in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained, sunny conditions and is often found in rocky or coastal environments.

Its hardy nature makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscaping.
