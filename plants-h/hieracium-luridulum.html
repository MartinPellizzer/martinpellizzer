Hieracium luridulum

Hieracium luridulum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. It has basal leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its stems are usually unbranched.

Hieracium luridulum is native to Europe and has been introduced to other parts of the world, where it can sometimes become invasive.
