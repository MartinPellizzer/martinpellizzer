Hieracium pleiastrum

Hieracium pleiastrum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and ragworts, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 50 centimeters. Its leaves are alternate, rough to the touch, and have a distinctive lobed shape.

The flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, often with a central disk surrounded by ray florets.
