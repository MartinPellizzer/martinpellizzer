Hieracium ariphyllum

Hieracium ariphyllum, commonly known as the showy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Hieracium ariphyllum is often found in open habitats and can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
