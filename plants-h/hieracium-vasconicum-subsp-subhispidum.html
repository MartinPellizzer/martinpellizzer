Hieracium vasconicum subsp. subhispidum

Hieracium vasconicum subsp.

subhispidum, commonly known as the Pyrenean hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pyrenees region. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine meadows, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are hairy and often have a reddish or purplish hue, while the flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in mountainous regions.
