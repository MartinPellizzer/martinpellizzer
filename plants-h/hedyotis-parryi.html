Hedyotis parryi

Hedyotis parryi, commonly known as Parry's dotweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and streambanks. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Hedyotis parryi is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
