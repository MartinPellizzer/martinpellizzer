Hieracium badiellum

Hieracium badiellum, commonly known as the Badiell's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring well-drained conditions. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

It is often found in temperate climates and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.
