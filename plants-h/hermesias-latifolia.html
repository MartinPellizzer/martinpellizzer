Hermesias latifolia

Hermesias latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf fern, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
