Halimium rhiphaeum

Halimium rhiphaeum, commonly known as the red sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and arid environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. Its succulent leaves are adapted to store water, making it drought-tolerant.

Halimium rhiphaeum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
