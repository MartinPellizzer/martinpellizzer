Hyptis inodora

Hyptis inodora, commonly known as sweet hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or perennial herb, often found in moist, sunny habitats. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
