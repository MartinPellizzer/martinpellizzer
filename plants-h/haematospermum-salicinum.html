Haematospermum salicinum

Haematospermum salicinum, commonly known as the red-flowered rock orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking red and purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor environments.

The species is noted for its unique floral structure and is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
