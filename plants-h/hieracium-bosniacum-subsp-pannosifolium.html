Hieracium bosniacum subsp. pannosifolium

Hieracium bosniacum subsp.

pannosifolium, commonly known as the Bosnian hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe, particularly Bosnia and Herzegovina. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems.

The subspecies pannosifolium is distinguished by its broad, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and other open habitats.

This plant is typically perennial and has a low-growing habit.
