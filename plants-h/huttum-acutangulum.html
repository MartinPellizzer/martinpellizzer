Huttum acutangulum

Huttum acutangulum, commonly known as the sharp-leaved huttum, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, pointed leaves that are often arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots are fibrous and can be used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Huttum acutangulum thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
