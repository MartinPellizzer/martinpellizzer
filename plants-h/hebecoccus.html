Hebecoccus

Hebecoccus, commonly known as the rock cress, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Brassicaceae.

These plants are typically small, herbaceous perennials that thrive in rocky or sandy soils. They are characterized by their basal leaves and small, clustered flowers that often have four petals. Hebecoccus species are native to various regions, including Europe, Asia, and North America.
