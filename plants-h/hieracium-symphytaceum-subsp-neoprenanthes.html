Hieracium symphytaceum subsp. neoprenanthes

Hieracium symphytaceum subsp.

neoprenanthes, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils, preferring sunny conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often have a hairy surface, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
