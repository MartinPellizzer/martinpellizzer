Hyssopus officinalis var. ruber

Hyssopus officinalis var. ruber, commonly known as red hyssop, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features square, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces spikes of showy, tubular red or purplish flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

The leaves and flowers are often used in herbal remedies and culinary applications.
