Hieracium dissimile subsp. hoidalicum

Hieracium dissimile subsp.

hoidalicum, commonly known as the Hoidalic Hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers and hairy, basal leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. It is native to parts of Europe and has been introduced to other regions.

Due to its invasive potential, it is often considered a weed in agricultural and natural areas.
