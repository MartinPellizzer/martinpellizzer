Hoya gutierrezii

Hoya gutierrezii, commonly known as the "Coral Hoya," is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and clusters of waxy, coral-pink flowers. The plant produces star-shaped blooms that are often fragrant, attracting pollinators such as moths and bees. Hoya gutierrezii is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

Its unique appearance and ease of care make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
