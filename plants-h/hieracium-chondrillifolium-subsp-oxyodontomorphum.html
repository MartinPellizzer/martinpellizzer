Hieracium chondrillifolium subsp. oxyodontomorphum

Hieracium chondrillifolium subsp.

oxyodontomorphum, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the dandelion family. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
