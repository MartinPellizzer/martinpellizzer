Hypochaeris philippii var. glabrescens

Hypochaeris philippii var. glabrescens, commonly known as the glabrous false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and herbaceous growth habit. The plant typically features yellow flowers with a central disk surrounded by ray florets, and it produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in grasslands and open areas.

The variety glabrescens is distinguished by its reduced hair coverage compared to other varieties of Hypochaeris.
