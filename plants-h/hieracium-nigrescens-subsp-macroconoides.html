Hieracium nigrescens subsp. macroconoides

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

macroconoides, commonly known as the large-headed hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has basal and stem leaves that are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
