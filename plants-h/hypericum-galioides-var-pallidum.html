Hypericum galioides var. pallidum

Hypericum galioides var. pallidum, commonly known as the pale St. John's wort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a glandular surface.

It is part of the Hypericaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and medicinal properties.
