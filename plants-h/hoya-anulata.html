Hoya anulata

Hoya anulata, commonly known as the "Rosy Wreath," is a tropical evergreen vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems and clusters of waxy, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink to red. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This plant is popular among indoor gardeners for its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
