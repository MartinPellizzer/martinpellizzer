Hyssopus polycladus

Hyssopus polycladus, commonly known as creeping hyssop, is a perennial herb native to Europe, North Africa, and western Asia.

It is characterized by its square, branched stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Hyssopus polycladus is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
