Hyptis mutabilis var. micrantha

Hyptis mutabilis var. micrantha, commonly known as the purple hyptis, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its strong, spicy scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its medicinal properties have also been studied for their potential health benefits.
