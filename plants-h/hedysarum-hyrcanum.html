Hedysarum hyrcanum

Hedysarum hyrcanum, commonly known as the Hyrcanian vetch, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in steppe and semi-desert environments.

Hedysarum hyrcanum plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
