Heterotheca subaxillaris

Heterotheca subaxillaris, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodlands, and it thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with hairy surfaces, and it produces showy purple to pink flowers in late summer.

Heterotheca subaxillaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
