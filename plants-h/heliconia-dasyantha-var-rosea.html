Heliconia dasyantha var. rosea

Heliconia dasyantha var. rosea, commonly known as the rose heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, brightly colored bracts that range from pink to red, which are often mistaken for flowers. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are surrounded by these vibrant, modified leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Heliconia dasyantha var. rosea is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
