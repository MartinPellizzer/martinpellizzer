Hieracium bifidum subsp. oedocephalum

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

oedocephalum, commonly known as the broad-headed hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a distinctive broad, rounded head.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
