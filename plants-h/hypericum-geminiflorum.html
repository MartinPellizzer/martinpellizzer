Hypericum geminiflorum

Hypericum geminiflorum, commonly known as the twin-flower St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces orange-red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and have a slightly rough texture.

Hypericum geminiflorum is often found in moist, woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
