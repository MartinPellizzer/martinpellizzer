Hieracium ovatifrons

Hieracium ovatifrons, commonly known as the broad-fruited hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and basal rosette of leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a dense, flat-topped inflorescence. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grasslands.

Hieracium ovatifrons is known for its hairy stems and leaves, which help it retain moisture in dry environments.
