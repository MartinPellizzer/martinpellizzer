Himantoglossum hircinum var. praealtum

Himantoglossum hircinum var. praealtum, commonly known as the alpine bell heather, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its upright, slender stems and small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats. It is adapted to cold climates and can tolerate high altitudes and harsh weather conditions.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
