Hypericum bulgaricum

Hypericum bulgaricum, commonly known as the Bulgarian St. John's Wort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the genus Hypericum, which includes over 400 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its yellow flowers with orange stamens and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and rocky areas.

Its medicinal properties have been studied for their potential anti-inflammatory and antidepressant effects.
