Hyphaene multiformis subsp. morogorensis

Hyphaene multiformis subsp.

morogorensis, commonly known as the Morogorensis date palm, is a subspecies native to eastern Africa, particularly Tanzania. It is a small, multi-trunked palm characterized by its robust, clustered stems and pinnate leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that yield edible dates, though its fruit is less commercially significant than other date palms. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil and requires moderate to high levels of sunlight.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to adapt to various environmental conditions.
