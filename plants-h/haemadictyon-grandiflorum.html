Haemadictyon grandiflorum

Haemadictyon grandiflorum, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad, floating leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, providing a unique habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant's showy, fragrant flowers, which bloom in the early morning, are typically pink or red and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots are submerged, anchoring it in the muddy bottom of still or slow-moving water bodies.

The leaves have a waxy surface that repels water, helping to maintain buoyancy and reduce evaporation.
