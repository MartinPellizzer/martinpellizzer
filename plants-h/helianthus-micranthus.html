Helianthus micranthus

Helianthus micranthus, commonly known as the dwarf sunflower, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its short stature and bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are composite, consisting of a central disk surrounded by ray florets.

Helianthus micranthus thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.
