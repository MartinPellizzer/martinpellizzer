Hibiscus zonatus

Hibiscus zonatus, commonly known as the zoned hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically feature a distinctive zoned pattern on the petals. The plant grows as an herbaceous perennial or shrub, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a rough texture and a serrated margin.

Hibiscus zonatus thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
