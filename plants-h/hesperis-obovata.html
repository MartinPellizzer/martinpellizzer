Hesperis obovata

Hesperis obovata, commonly known as the sweet rocket, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant features showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in racemes. Its leaves are ovate to obovate in shape, with a slightly hairy texture.

Hesperis obovata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and naturalized areas.
