Hieracium intertextum

Hieracium intertextum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant has rough, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn. It often grows in disturbed soils and can form dense colonies, making it a common weed in meadows and along roadsides.

Its botanical features include a stout stem, alternate leaves, and a basal rosette of foliage.
