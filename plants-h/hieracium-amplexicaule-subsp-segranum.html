Hieracium amplexicaule subsp. segranum

Hieracium amplexicaule subsp.

segranum, commonly known as the creeping hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering stems that bear yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and riverbanks. It has hairy stems and leaves with a distinct basal rosette structure.

The subspecies segranum is distinguished by its shorter stature and more compact growth compared to other varieties of Hieracium.
