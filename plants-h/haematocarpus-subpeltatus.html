Haematocarpus subpeltatus

Haematocarpus subpeltatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved burrgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and produces distinctive burr-like fruits that are adapted for seed dispersal by attachment to animals. The plant thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland and woodland habitats. It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is known for its resilience in arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora in its native range.
