Helianthus argenteus

Helianthus argenteus, commonly known as the silverleaf sunflower, is a species of flowering plant native to the United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sunlight. Its height can range from 1 to 3 feet, making it a suitable addition to gardens and natural landscapes.

Helianthus argenteus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
