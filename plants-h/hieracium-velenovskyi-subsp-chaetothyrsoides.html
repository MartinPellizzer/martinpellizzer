Hieracium velenovskyi subsp. chaetothyrsoides

Hieracium velenovskyi subsp.

chaetothyrsoides, commonly known as the hairy thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and spiny, lobed leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense, spherical heads. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in steppe and mountainous regions.

Due to its distinctive appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
