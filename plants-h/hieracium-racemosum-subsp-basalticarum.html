Hieracium racemosum subsp. basalticarum

Hieracium racemosum subsp.

basalticarum, commonly known as the basaltic hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and daisy-like blooms.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

This subspecies is distinguished by its adaptation to rocky and nutrient-poor environments, showcasing resilience to harsh conditions.
