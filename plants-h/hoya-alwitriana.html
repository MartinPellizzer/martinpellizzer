Hoya alwitriana

Hoya alwitriana, commonly known as the Wandering Nurse, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asclepiadaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions of Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and waxy, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. Its flowers are small, star-shaped, and typically white or cream-colored, arranged in clusters.

Hoya alwitriana is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ornamental appeal and ease of care.
