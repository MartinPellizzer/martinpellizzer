Hieracium laevigatum subsp. schwarzianum

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

schwarzianum, commonly known as the smooth hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are broadly ovate with a smooth texture, and the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is a herbaceous species that does not form a woody stem, and it is often found in temperate climates.
