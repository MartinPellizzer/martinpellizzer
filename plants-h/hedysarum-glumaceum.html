Hedysarum glumaceum

Hedysarum glumaceum, commonly known as the glume peavine, is a perennial leguminous plant native to central and eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves with rounded leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes, followed by pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along streambanks.

Hedysarum glumaceum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
