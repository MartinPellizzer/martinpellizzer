Hieracium diaphanoides subsp. diaphanoides

Hieracium diaphanoides subsp.

diaphanoides, commonly known as the translucent hawkweed, is a flowering plant species belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, yellowish to greenish composite flowers.

The leaves are typically alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs. This subspecies is native to parts of Europe and is often found in meadows and grassy areas.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique floral structure and distribution patterns.
