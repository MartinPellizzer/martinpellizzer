Hagioseris galilaea

Hagioseris galilaea, commonly known as the Galilee hagioseris, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Israel and Palestine.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptation to harsh conditions. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Hagioseris galilaea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
