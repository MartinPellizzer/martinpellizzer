Hieracium racemosum subsp. caramanicum

Hieracium racemosum subsp.

caramanicum, commonly known as the Caramanicum hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its clustered, yellowish to orange-yellow flowering heads.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils, often in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its leaves are lanceolate and hairy, with a distinct basal rosette structure.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smaller flower heads and shorter peduncles compared to other members of the *Hieracium* genus.
