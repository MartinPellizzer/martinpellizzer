Hymenosicyos parviflorus

Hymenosicyos parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered bottle gourd, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It produces unique, bottle-shaped fruits that are often used in traditional cuisine and crafts. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and useful species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
