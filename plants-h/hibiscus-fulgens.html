Hibiscus fulgens

Hibiscus fulgens, commonly known as the torch hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange, with a distinctive yellow throat. The plant has palmate leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Hibiscus fulgens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
