Hieracium ammobium subsp. ammobium

Hieracium ammobium subsp.

ammobium, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. It has hairy stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped with serrated edges.

The subspecies ammobium is distinguished by its compact growth habit and smaller, more clustered flower heads compared to other Hieracium species.
