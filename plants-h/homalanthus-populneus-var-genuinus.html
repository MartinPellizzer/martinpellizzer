Homalanthus populneus var. genuinus

Homalanthus populneus var. genuinus, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, orange-red flowers that appear in clusters, giving it a striking visual appearance. The plant has pinnate leaves with numerous small, lance-shaped leaflets. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The fruit is a three-lobed capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
