Hoya pseudoleytensis

Hoya pseudoleytensis, commonly known as the Lemon Hoya, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green ovate leaves and clusters of waxy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions. It produces small, fleshy berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Hoya pseudoleytensis is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ornamental value and ease of care.
