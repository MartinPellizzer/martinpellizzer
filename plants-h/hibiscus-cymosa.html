Hibiscus cymosa

Hibiscus cymosa, commonly known as the clustered hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers that typically range in color from white to pink or red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds. Hibiscus cymosa thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in wetlands or along streams.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
