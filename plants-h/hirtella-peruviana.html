Hirtella peruviana

Hirtella peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian hirtella, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and other aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically white or pale pink and are clustered in inflorescences.

Hirtella peruviana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
