Heterotheca fastigiata

Heterotheca fastigiata, commonly known as the stiffstem goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its stiff, upright stems and hairy foliage distinguish it from other species in the genus.

Heterotheca fastigiata is often found in open grasslands and along canyon walls, where it thrives in full sun.
