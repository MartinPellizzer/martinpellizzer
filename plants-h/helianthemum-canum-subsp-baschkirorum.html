Helianthemum canum subsp. baschkirorum

Helianthemum canum subsp.

baschkirorum, commonly known as the Baskhiri stonecrop, is a low-growing, perennial plant native to the Caucasus region. It features compact, mat-forming growth with fleshy, hairy leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Crassulaceae family.
