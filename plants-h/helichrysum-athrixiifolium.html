Helichrysum athrixiifolium

Helichrysum athrixiifolium, commonly known as the golden everlasting or Cape gold.

It is a small, evergreen shrub native to South Africa, characterized by its dense clusters of yellow, daisy-like flowers that remain vibrant for extended periods. The plant features narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates.

The flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their long-lasting color and ornamental appeal.
