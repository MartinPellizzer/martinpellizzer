Hipposelinum

Hipposelinum, commonly known as love-lies-bleeding, is a flowering plant belonging to the carrot family (Apiaceae).

It is characterized by its striking, red, feathery flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant produces small, round, orange fruits called "hips," which are distinctive and ornamental. Hipposelinum thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display and historical use in herbal medicine.
