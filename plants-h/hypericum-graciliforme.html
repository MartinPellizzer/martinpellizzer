Hypericum graciliforme

Hypericum graciliforme, commonly known as slender St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky areas.

Hypericum graciliforme is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators.
