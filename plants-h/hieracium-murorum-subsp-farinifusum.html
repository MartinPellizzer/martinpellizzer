Hieracium murorum subsp. farinifusum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

farinifusum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm. Its leaves are hairy and often have a whitish coating, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
