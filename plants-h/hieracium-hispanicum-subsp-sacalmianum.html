Hieracium hispanicum subsp. sacalmianum

Hieracium hispanicum subsp.

sacalmianum, commonly known as the Spanish hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It has a taproot system and produces achenes as its reproductive structures.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
