Huperzia staudtii

Huperzia staudtii, commonly known as the Staudt's clubmoss, is a species of evergreen fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and its distinctive, club-shaped sporophylls. Native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant produces spores rather than seeds, and its reproductive structures are closely clustered at the base of the stems.

Huperzia staudtii is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and its ability to form dense, carpet-like foliage.
