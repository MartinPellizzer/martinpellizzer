Heptapleurum farinosum var. maiorilimbum

Heptapleurum farinosum var. maiorilimbum, commonly known as the glossy-leaf heptapleurum, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments within tropical and subtropical forests. Its leaves are leathery and have a distinct aromatic scent when crushed.

It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by dark, elliptical berries.
