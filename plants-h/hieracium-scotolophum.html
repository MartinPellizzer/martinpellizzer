Hieracium scotolophum

Hieracium scotolophum, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf structures. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are rough and hairy, with a serrated margin, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat head.

Hieracium scotolophum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
