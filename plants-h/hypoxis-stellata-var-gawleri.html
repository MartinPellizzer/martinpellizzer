Hypoxis stellata var. gawleri

Hypoxis stellata var. gawleri, commonly known as the star-shaped false arum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves with a prominent central stem that bears clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or open habitats. Its flowers are typically borne in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and flies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
