Haworthia devriesii

Haworthia devriesii, commonly known as the Zulu beauty, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically fleshy and covered in a waxy coating. The leaves are usually green with white or pale blue markings, and the plant produces small, white flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

This species is popular among succulent enthusiasts due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
