Hibiscus microphyllus

Hibiscus microphyllus, commonly known as the rock hibiscus, is a shrub native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping and coastal gardens. Its flowers bloom throughout the growing season, adding visual interest and attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Hibiscus microphyllus is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to withstand salty air, making it a hardy choice for coastal environments.
