Helianthemum lavandulifolium var. thibaudii

Helianthemum lavandulifolium var. thibaudii, commonly known as the Thibaudii rockrose, is a low-growing shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often appearing in clusters. The plant has aromatic, narrow leaves that resemble those of lavender, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained, sunny conditions and is often used in rock gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
