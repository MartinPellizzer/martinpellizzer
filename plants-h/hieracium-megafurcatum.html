Hieracium megafurcatum

Hieracium megafurcatum, commonly known as the giant forked hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, surrounded by a ring of pale green to white ray florets. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and the stems are often reddish in color.

Hieracium megafurcatum is known for its robust growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
