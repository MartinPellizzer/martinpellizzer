Hieracium subflocciferum

Hieracium subflocciferum, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a basal rosette of hairy, dark green leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its common name refers to the fluffy, wool-like appearance of its leaves and flower heads.
