Hieracium falcatum subsp. glaucoides

Hieracium falcatum subsp.

glaucoides, commonly known as the glaucous hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in composite inflorescences, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
