Helichrysum siculum

Helichrysum siculum, commonly known as the Sicilian everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces small, bright yellow to orange flowers that are often arranged in dense clusters. The plant is known for its aromatic foliage, which contains essential oils with potential medicinal properties. Its name derives from the Greek words "helios" meaning sun and "chrysm" meaning golden, referring to its sunny appearance and golden flowers.

Helichrysum siculum is often used in herbal remedies and is also cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive, long-lasting flowers.
