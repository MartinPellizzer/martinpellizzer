Hymenaea aurea

Hymenaea aurea, commonly known as the yellow miracle-berry, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, light-colored bark and large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit, which is small and round, has a unique sour taste that becomes sweet when eaten with certain foods.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and its role in traditional medicine.
