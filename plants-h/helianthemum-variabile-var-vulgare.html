Helianthemum variabile var. vulgare

Helianthemum variabile var. vulgare, commonly known as the variable sandmat, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to southern Europe.

It features small, fleshy, greenish-yellow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it well-suited for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
