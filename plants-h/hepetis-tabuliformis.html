Hepetis tabuliformis

Hepetis tabuliformis, commonly known as the broad-leafed heath, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often hairy and have a waxy coating. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and native plant gardens.
