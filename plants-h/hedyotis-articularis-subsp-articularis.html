Hedyotis articularis subsp. articularis

Hedyotis articularis subsp.

articularis, commonly known as the jointed bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Asia. It typically features slender stems that are often branched and covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.
