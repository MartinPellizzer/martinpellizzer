Hibiscus pernambucensis

Hibiscus pernambucensis, commonly known as the Pernambuco hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range from pink to red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Hibiscus pernambucensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
