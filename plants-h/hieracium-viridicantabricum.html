Hieracium viridicantabricum

Hieracium viridicantabricum, commonly known as the green-veined hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped with prominent veins, and its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

It is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
