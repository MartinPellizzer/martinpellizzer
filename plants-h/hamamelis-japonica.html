Hamamelis japonica

Hamamelis japonica, commonly known as Japanese witch hazel, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late autumn or early winter. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that turn reddish in the fall. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, and it produces small, woody capsules that contain seeds.

Hamamelis japonica is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in traditional medicine for its astringent properties.
