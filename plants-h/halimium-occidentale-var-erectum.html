Halimium occidentale var. erectum

Halimium occidentale var. erectum, commonly known as the upright sandmat, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit with woody stems and small, fleshy leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments.

This species is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
