Hibiscus aethiopicus var. diversifolius

Hibiscus aethiopicus var. diversifolius, commonly known as the variegated hibiscus, is a striking tropical plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a prominent yellow center. The leaves are notable for their variegated patterns, featuring shades of green, white, and sometimes yellow. This plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil and ample sunlight.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant blooms and unique foliage.
