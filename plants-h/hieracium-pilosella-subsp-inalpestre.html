Hieracium pilosella subsp. inalpestre

Hieracium pilosella subsp.

inalpestre, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to alpine and subalpine regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.

The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are hairy and often covered in a fine layer of white wax. Its flowers are yellow, forming small, daisy-like inflorescences with ray florets.

This subspecies is adapted to harsh mountain environments and is often found in meadows and open slopes.
