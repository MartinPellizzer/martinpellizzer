Hieracium lanceiferum

Hieracium lanceiferum, commonly known as the lance-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Hieracium lanceiferum is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
