Huperzia juniperistachya

Huperzia juniperistachya, commonly known as the juniper-stalked huperzia, is a species of clubmoss belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and upright growth habit, often forming compact, cushion-like structures. The plant produces strobili, or cone-like structures, that contain sporangia for spore dispersal. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in temperate regions with cool, humid climates.

Huperzia juniperistachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a ground cover in shaded landscapes.
