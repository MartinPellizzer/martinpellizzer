Hyalis latifolia

Hyalis latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf hyalis, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Hyalis latifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate conditions.
