Hydrocotyle triloba

Hydrocotyle triloba, commonly known as marsh pennywort, is a flowering plant native to wetland areas in the Americas.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense mats. The plant has rounded, waxy leaves that are usually divided into three lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the summer months.

Hydrocotyle triloba is known for its ability to thrive in aquatic environments and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
