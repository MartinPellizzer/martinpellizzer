Harrisia fragrans subsp. aboriginum

Harrisia fragrans subsp.

aboriginum, commonly known as the desert night bloomer, is a species of cactus native to Mexico. It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered in spines.

The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its succulent structure allows it to store water, enabling it to thrive in arid environments.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique floral scent and specific geographic distribution within its native range.
