Heliotropium Tuberculosum

Heliotropium tuberculosum is a unique flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East. It is known for its striking purple flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a vibrant touch to its harsh environment. This species thrives in sandy soils and can withstand extreme temperatures, making it a resilient choice for dry landscapes. Its name comes from the Greek words for sun and turning, reflecting its tendency to follow the sun's movement. Despite its beauty, it remains a lesser-known plant in the world of horticulture and botany.
