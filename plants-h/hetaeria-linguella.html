Hetaeria linguella

Hetaeria linguella, commonly known as the tongue lichen, is a species of lichen that belongs to the family Pilocarpaceae.

It is a foliose lichen, characterized by its leaf-like thallus structure with a distinct upper and lower surface. The upper surface is typically pale green to yellowish, while the lower surface is more deeply colored and often covered with rhizines. It grows on tree trunks and branches, particularly in humid and shaded environments.

The lichen is known for its ability to tolerate pollution, making it an indicator species for environmental conditions.
