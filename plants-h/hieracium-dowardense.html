Hieracium dowardense

Hieracium dowardense, commonly known as the Downy Hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and ragworts, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are hairy and serrated, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

It is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and ability to spread easily.
