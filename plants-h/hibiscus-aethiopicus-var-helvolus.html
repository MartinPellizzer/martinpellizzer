Hibiscus aethiopicus var. helvolus

Hibiscus aethiopicus var. helvolus, commonly known as the woolly hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a prominent yellow center. The plant has hairy, rough-textured leaves and stems, which give it its common name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Hibiscus aethiopicus var. helvolus is also known for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions and partial shade.
