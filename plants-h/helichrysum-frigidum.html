Helichrysum frigidum

Helichrysum frigidum, commonly known as the alpine everlasting, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellow to orange flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant produces bract-like leaves that are often mistaken for flowers, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Helichrysum frigidum is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
