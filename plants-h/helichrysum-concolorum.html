Helichrysum concolorum

Helichrysum concolorum, commonly known as the yellow everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and vibrant yellow flowers that remain open for extended periods. The plant produces small, daisy-like blooms with yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Helichrysum concolorum is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
