Hieracium demutatum

Hieracium demutatum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early autumn. The plant has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas.

Hieracium demutatum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting pollinators.
