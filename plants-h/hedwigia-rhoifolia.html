Hedwigia rhoifolia

Hedwigia rhoifolia, commonly known as the desert hedgehog cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that form a compact, shrub-like structure. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. Its adaptations to desert conditions include thick, fleshy stems for water storage and reduced leaves to minimize water loss.

Hedwigia rhoifolia is often used in xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and ability to thrive in hot, dry environments.
