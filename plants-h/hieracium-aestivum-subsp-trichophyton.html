Hieracium aestivum subsp. trichophyton

Hieracium aestivum subsp.

trichophyton, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features basal rosettes of leaves that are hairy and often reddish in color, with a distinctive yellow-orange flower head composed of ray florets. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its common name.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions and is often found in sunny to partially shaded environments.
