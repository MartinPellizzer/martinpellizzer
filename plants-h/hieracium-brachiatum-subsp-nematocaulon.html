Hieracium brachiatum subsp. nematocaulon

Hieracium brachiatum subsp.

nematocaulon, commonly known as the narrowleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting pollinator populations.
