Herminium albovirens

Herminium albovirens, commonly known as the white-veined orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces delicate, greenish-white flowers with a distinctive veined pattern, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Herminium albovirens is often found growing in colonies, and it is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
