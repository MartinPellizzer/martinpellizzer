Hydnophytum montis-kani

Hydnophytum montis-kani, commonly known as the mountain mist vine, is a unique epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and the presence of specialized structures called haustoria, which it uses to absorb nutrients from its host tree. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by capsule fruits containing minute seeds. Hydnophytum montis-kani thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in the canopies of primary rainforests.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as an indicator of undisturbed forest ecosystems and its potential ecological interactions with other flora and fauna.
