Hieracium nigrescens subsp. dolichadenium

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

dolichadenium, commonly known as the long-stemmed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils.

The plant features basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in late summer. Its inflorescence consists of a single, elongated flower head with ray florets.

The subspecies is distinguished by its longer peduncles and more robust growth compared to other members of the species.
