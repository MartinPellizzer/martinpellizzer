Hemianemia tomentosa

Hemianemia tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly hemianthus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that covers its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its unique texture and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
