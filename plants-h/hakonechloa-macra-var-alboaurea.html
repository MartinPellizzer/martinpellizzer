Hakonechloa macra var. alboaurea

Hakonechloa macra var. alboaurea, commonly known as the golden thread fern, is a cultivar of the Hakonechloa macra species, which is native to Japan.

It is a low-growing, evergreen grass-like plant that forms dense, mat-like clumps with slender, arching leaves. The leaves of this variety are characterized by their bright yellow margins and a deep green center, creating a striking visual contrast. It thrives in partial to full shade and is prized for its ornamental value in garden settings.

This plant is relatively easy to care for and is well-suited for rock gardens or as a ground cover in shaded areas.
