Hesperis sibirica

Hesperis sibirica, commonly known as Siberian wallflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers with four petals. The plant has pinnately divided, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Hesperis sibirica is known for its long blooming period, typically from late spring to early summer.
