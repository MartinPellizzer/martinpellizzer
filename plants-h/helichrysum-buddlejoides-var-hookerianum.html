Helichrysum buddlejoides var. hookerianum

Helichrysum buddlejoides var. hookerianum, commonly known as the Hooker's everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, yellow-orange inflorescences that resemble small daisies. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their long-lasting nature and vibrant color.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
