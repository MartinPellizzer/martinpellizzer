Hedysarum basineri

Hedysarum basineri, commonly known as the Armenian vetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, arranged in racemes. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Hedysarum basineri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
