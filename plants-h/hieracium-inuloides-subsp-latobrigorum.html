Hieracium inuloides subsp. latobrigorum

Hieracium inuloides subsp.

latobrigorum, commonly known as the southern marsh hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in wet meadows, marshes, and along riverbanks, preferring moist, nutrient-rich soils.

The plant features upright stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and wind-pollinated reproductive structures.
