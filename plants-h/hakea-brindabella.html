Hakea brindabella

Hakea brindabella, commonly known as the Christmas bush, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its striking, bright red flowers that bloom during the summer months. The plant has needle-like leaves that are typically green, with a waxy coating, and it produces woody, cone-like fruits that contain the seeds. Hakea brindabella is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to coastal and dry climates.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
