Huperzia serrata subsp. javanica

Huperzia serrata subsp.

javanica, commonly known as the Javan huperzia, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae. It is characterized by its simple, linear leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its spores are produced in clusters on the underside of the leaves, a feature common among ferns.

This subspecies is native to Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and Malaysia.
