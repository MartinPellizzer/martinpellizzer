Hebe stricta var. egmontiana

Hebe stricta var. egmontiana, commonly known as the Egmont hebe, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to New Zealand.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green color, often with a slight silver or blue tinge on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, or white, which bloom in spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is known for its hardiness and ability to tolerate coastal conditions.
