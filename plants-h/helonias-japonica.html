Helonias japonica

Helonias japonica, commonly known as Japanese marsh orchid, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its terrestrial growth habit. The plant typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, thriving in moist, acidic soils. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive spurred shape that aids in pollination by insects.

Helonias japonica is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
