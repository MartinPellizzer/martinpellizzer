Heliotropium bottae

Heliotropium bottae, commonly known as the pink heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant, pink or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or near water bodies.

Heliotropium bottae is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
