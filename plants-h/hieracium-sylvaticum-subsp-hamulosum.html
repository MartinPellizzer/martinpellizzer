Hieracium sylvaticum subsp. hamulosum

Hieracium sylvaticum subsp.

hamulosum, commonly known as the forest hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering stems that bear yellow ray florets and tubular disk florets.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces, and the flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn.

It is a common species in temperate regions of Europe and is often found in association with other woodland flora.
