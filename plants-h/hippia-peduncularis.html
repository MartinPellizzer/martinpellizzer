Hippia peduncularis

Hippia peduncularis, commonly known as the clustered leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and clustered foliage. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often arranged in a raceme. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, rich soil to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
