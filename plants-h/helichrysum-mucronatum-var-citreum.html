Helichrysum mucronatum var. citreum

Helichrysum mucronatum var. citreum, commonly known as the golden marguerite or lemon scented everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has aromatic, narrow leaves that emit a citrus-like scent when crushed. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its long-lasting flowers make it a popular choice for dried floral arrangements.
