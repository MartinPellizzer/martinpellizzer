Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp. rivulicola

Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp.

rivulicola, commonly known as the river hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils along riverbanks and wet meadows.

The plant has upright stems with hairy leaves that are lance-shaped and serrated. Its flowers are yellow, with ray florets and a central disc, and it blooms from June to August.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
