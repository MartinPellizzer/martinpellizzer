Hieracium murorum subsp. subbifidiforme

Hieracium murorum subsp.

subbifidiforme, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and serrated leaves.

The subspecies is distinguished by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and a more compact growth habit compared to other hawkweed species. It typically thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade.

Its floral structure consists of both ray and disk flowers, which attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
