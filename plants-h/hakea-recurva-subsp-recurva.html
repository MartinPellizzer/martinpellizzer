Hakea recurva subsp. recurva

Hakea recurva subsp.

recurva, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hakea, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs.

The plant produces cylindrical, woody flower spikes that bloom in spring, typically in shades of pink to red. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, Mediterranean-type climates. Hakea recurva subsp.

recurva is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowering display.
