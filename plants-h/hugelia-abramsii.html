Hugelia abramsii

Hugelia abramsii, commonly known as the sandplain huckleberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, dark purple to black berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils, such as those found in sandplain habitats. It has simple, alternate leaves and produces small, white flowers in the spring.

Hugelia abramsii is adapted to full sun and is often found in open, disturbed areas.
