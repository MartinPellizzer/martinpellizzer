Hieracium pseudobifidum subsp. sphaerophyllum

Hieracium pseudobifidum subsp.

sphaerophyllum, commonly known as the globe hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of a single, globe-shaped flower head, which gives it its common name.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some related species.
