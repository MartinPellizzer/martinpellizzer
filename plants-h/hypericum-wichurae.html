Hypericum wichurae

Hypericum wichurae, commonly known as the yellow-stemmed St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with orange stamens, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Hypericum wichurae is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
