Hebe insularis

Hebe insularis, commonly known as the Stewart Island hebe, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in spring. Hebe insularis thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions.

It is a hardy species that is well adapted to the temperate climate of its native habitat.
