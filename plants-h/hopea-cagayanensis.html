Hopea cagayanensis

Hopea cagayanensis, commonly known as the Cagayan hopea, is a species of flowering plant in the family Dipterocarpaceae.

It is native to the Philippines, particularly found in the mountainous regions of northern Luzon. The plant is a large evergreen tree that can reach heights of up to 40 meters, with a straight trunk and a broad crown. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptic in shape, with a leathery texture and dark green color.

The flowers are small and clustered, and the tree produces woody fruits that contain one or two seeds.
