Haplopappus racemosus subsp. typicus

Haplopappus racemosus subsp.

typicus, commonly known as the clustered pappus plant, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant features a low-growing, mat-forming structure with narrow, linear leaves. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and are followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open habitats.
