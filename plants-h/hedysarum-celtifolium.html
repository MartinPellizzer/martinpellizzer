Hedysarum celtifolium

Hedysarum celtifolium, commonly known as the Celtic leaf peavine, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and can be used in traditional herbal medicine.

Hedysarum celtifolium plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its natural environment.
