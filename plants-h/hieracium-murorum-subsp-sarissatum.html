Hieracium murorum subsp. sarissatum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

sarissatum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and waste areas, and can form dense colonies. Its leaves are usually alternate, with a rough, hairy texture, and its stems are often reddish in color.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common weed in many regions.
