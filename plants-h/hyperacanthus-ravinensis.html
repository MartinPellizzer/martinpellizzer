Hyperacanthus ravinensis

Hyperacanthus ravinensis, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a robust, upright stem that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers with five petals, which bloom in the summer months. Its roots are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in its arid habitat.

Hyperacanthus ravinensis is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils.
