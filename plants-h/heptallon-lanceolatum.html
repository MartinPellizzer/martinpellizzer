Heptallon lanceolatum

Heptallon lanceolatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf heptallon, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged alternately along its stem. Its flowers are small and clustered in panicles, usually appearing in late spring to early summer.

Heptallon lanceolatum is characterized by its fibrous root system and its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich soils.
