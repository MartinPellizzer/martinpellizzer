Hieracium epacroides

Hieracium epacroides, commonly known as the golden hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its yellow-orange flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and the leaves are lance-shaped with a hairy texture.

Hieracium epacroides is often found in temperate climates and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
