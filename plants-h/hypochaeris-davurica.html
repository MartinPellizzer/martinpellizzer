Hypochaeris davurica

Hypochaeris davurica, commonly known as the Siberian hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Asia and Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Hypochaeris davurica is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in agricultural and natural areas.
