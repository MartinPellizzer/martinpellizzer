Habenaria pectinata var. arietina

Habenaria pectinata var. arietina, commonly known as the red-tipped orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It features slender, upright stems with elliptic to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers with a distinctive red or pinkish tip on the petals. Its blooms typically appear in late spring to early summer, and it prefers moist, shady habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
