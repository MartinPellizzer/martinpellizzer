Hieracium pallidum subsp. melaphyrogenes

Hieracium pallidum subsp.

melaphyrogenes, commonly known as the pale hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, pastures, and roadsides.

The plant features a basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces, and its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. It has a taproot system and produces seeds in small, dry fruits.

The species is characterized by its morphological variation and is often found in association with other members of the Asteraceae family.
