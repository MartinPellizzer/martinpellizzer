Hepetis iridiflora

Hepetis iridiflora, commonly known as the blue bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slight hairy texture.

Hepetis iridiflora is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
