Hyalocalyx

Hyalocalyx, commonly known as the glassy calyx plant, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique calyx, which is translucent and often resembles glass, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has small, delicate flowers. Its botanical classification places it within the family Acanthaceae, where it shares features with other members of the family.

The hyalocalyx is valued in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
