Hieracium subambiguum

Hieracium subambiguum, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and ragworts, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat, disc-like structure.

It is often found in temperate regions and is considered a weed in some agricultural areas.
