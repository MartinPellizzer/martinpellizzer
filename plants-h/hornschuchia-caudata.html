Hornschuchia caudata

Hornschuchia caudata, commonly known as the long-tailed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, rocky soils and is often found in forested areas. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually blue or purple in color, blooming in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, contributing to its rugged appearance.

Hornschuchia caudata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
