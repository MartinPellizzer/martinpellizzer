Hypopitys racemosa

Hypopitys racemosa, commonly known as the western redcedar, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and reddish-brown bark that peels in thin, papery layers. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by cylindrical cones that contain winged seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Hypopitys racemosa has a long history of use by indigenous peoples for its durable wood and medicinal properties.
