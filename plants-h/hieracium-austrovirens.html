Hieracium austrovirens

Hieracium austrovirens, commonly known as the green-haired hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough, hairy texture, and the flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Hieracium austrovirens is often found in temperate regions and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
