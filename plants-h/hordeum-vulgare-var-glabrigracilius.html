Hordeum vulgare var. glabrigracilius

Hordeum vulgare var. glabrigracilius, commonly known as the slender naked barley, is a variant of barley characterized by its reduced awns and slender spikelets.

It is an annual grass that typically grows in temperate regions, often found in disturbed soils or agricultural fields. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences. Its grains are generally smaller and less hardy compared to typical barley varieties, making it less common in cultivation.

This variety is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphological traits and potential for genetic research.
