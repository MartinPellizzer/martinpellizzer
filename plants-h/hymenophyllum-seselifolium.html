Hymenophyllum seselifolium

Hymenophyllum seselifolium, commonly known as the silky haircap moss, is a small, epiphytic moss belonging to the family Hypnaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, hair-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky coating. Its spores are released from small, cup-shaped structures called calyptras, which are positioned at the tips of the leaves.

Hymenophyllum seselifolium plays a role in forest ecosystems by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for microorganisms.
