Hopea sublanceolata

Hopea sublanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hopea, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall and produces small, yellowish flowers that are often borne in clusters. Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture-making.

The plant also plays an important role in the ecological systems of its native habitats.
