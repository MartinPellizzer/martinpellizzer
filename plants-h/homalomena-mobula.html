Homalomena mobula

Homalomena mobula, commonly known as the broad-leafed arum.

It is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and striking inflorescence. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which is known for its unique floral structures. Its leaves are typically a deep green with a waxy texture, and the plant thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to indoor conditions.
