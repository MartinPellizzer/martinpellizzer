Hamelia lucida

Hamelia lucida, commonly known as the flamebeard or redroot, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, tubular red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, woody stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Hamelia lucida is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
