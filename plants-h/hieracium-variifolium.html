Hieracium variifolium

Hieracium variifolium, commonly known as the variable hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring moist and sunny environments. It has lance-shaped to ovate leaves that vary in size and shape, contributing to its common name.

Hieracium variifolium is often found in temperate regions and is known for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
