Hieracium incisum subsp. porrectiforme

Hieracium incisum subsp.

porrectiforme, commonly known as the narrowleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in composite heads, which are typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and is often found in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is sometimes grown in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
