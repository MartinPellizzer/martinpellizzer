Hymenothrix wislizeni

Hymenothrix wislizeni, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb with a distinctive, showy inflorescence composed of numerous white to pinkish flowers arranged in a dense, rounded cluster. The plant has a fibrous root system adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. Its leaves are narrow and covered in a thick, waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

The flowers bloom in spring and are pollinated primarily by insects, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
