Hymenorebutia napina

Hymenorebutia napina, commonly known as the napina torch cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a small, columnar cactus with a distinctive red or orange coloration, especially during the flowering season. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It has a thick, ribbed stem that helps store water, adapting to its harsh environment.

Hymenorebutia napina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in succulent gardens.
