Hymenophyllum merrillii

Hymenophyllum merrillii, commonly known as the silver moss, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided leaves that are often covered in a silvery or whitish coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. This plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually small and clustered, with a leathery texture that helps it retain moisture.

Hymenophyllum merrillii plays an important ecological role in nutrient cycling and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
