Hesperis matronalis var. sibirica

Hesperis matronalis var. sibirica, commonly known as Siberian wallflower, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically reaches a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and has lance-shaped, hairy leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
