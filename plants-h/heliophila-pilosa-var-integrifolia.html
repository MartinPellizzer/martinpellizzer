Heliophila pilosa var. integrifolia

Heliophila pilosa var. integrifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf heliophyte, is a flowering plant native to wetland and marshy environments in Europe and Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear small, delicate white or pale blue flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth, entire margin, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in shallow water or along the edges of ponds.

This species is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and support aquatic ecosystems.
