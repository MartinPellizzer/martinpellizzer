Hovea pannosa

Hovea pannosa, commonly known as the hairy hovea, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant has a woody stem with a distinctive hairy texture, and its leaves are often covered in fine, soft hairs. Hovea pannosa is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species found in various regions of Australia.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for native insects and animals.
