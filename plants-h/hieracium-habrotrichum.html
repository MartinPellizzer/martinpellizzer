Hieracium habrotrichum

Hieracium habrotrichum, commonly known as the silky hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and daisy-like appearance. The plant features dark green, hairy leaves that are often lobed or serrated, and it produces yellow flowers with prominent central disks. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
