Heleochloa juncea

Heleochloa juncea, commonly known as the slender flat sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spikelet-bearing inflorescences, which are typical of the Cyperaceae family. The plant thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense vegetation, contributing to soil stabilization.

Heleochloa juncea plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wetland organisms.
