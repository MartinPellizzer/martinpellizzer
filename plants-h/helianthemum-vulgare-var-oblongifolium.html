Helianthemum vulgare var. oblongifolium

Helianthemum vulgare var. oblongifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf rockrose, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Europe.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring and summer, typically arranged in clusters. The leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape, with a grayish-green hue and a hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

This plant is known for its hardiness and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
