Haplopappus paniculatus

Haplopappus paniculatus, commonly known as the desert hawksbeard, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is well adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its small, yellowish flowers bloom in summer, and the plant produces fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.

Haplopappus paniculatus is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats, contributing to the biodiversity of these ecosystems.
