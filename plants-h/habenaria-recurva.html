Habenaria recurva

Habenaria recurva, commonly known as the recurved orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and wetlands. Its rhizome is fibrous, and it produces small, lance-shaped leaves.

The orchid is notable for its unique flower structure, which includes a spurred lip and a distinct column.
