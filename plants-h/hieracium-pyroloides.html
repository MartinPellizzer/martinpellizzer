Hieracium pyroloides

Hieracium pyroloides, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. The plant has basal leaves that are usually hairy and lance-shaped, with a dense cluster of yellow flowers at the top. Its flowers are arranged in a compact inflorescence and bloom in early summer.

Hieracium pyroloides is characterized by its ability to tolerate harsh conditions, making it a resilient species in high-altitude ecosystems.
