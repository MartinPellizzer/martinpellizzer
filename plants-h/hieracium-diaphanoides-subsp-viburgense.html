Hieracium diaphanoides subsp. viburgense

Hieracium diaphanoides subsp.

viburgense, commonly known as the translucent hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, yellowish-green flowers.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine pubescence, which helps reduce water loss. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

The plant is notable for its delicate appearance and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
