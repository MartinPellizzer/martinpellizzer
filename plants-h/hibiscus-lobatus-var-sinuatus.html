Hibiscus lobatus var. sinuatus

Hibiscus lobatus var. sinuatus, commonly known as the sinuate hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and red. The leaves are heart-shaped with a distinctive wavy edge, contributing to its ornamental appeal. This species thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
