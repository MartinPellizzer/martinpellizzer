Helichrysum boormanii var. typicum

Helichrysum boormanii var. typicum, commonly known as the Boorman's everlasting.

It is a perennial herb native to South Africa, characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, golden-yellow flowers. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Its flowers are typically arranged in a spherical or globular shape, giving it a unique and ornamental look.

The species is valued in horticulture for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
