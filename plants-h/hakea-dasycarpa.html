Hakea dasycarpa

Hakea dasycarpa, commonly known as the dense-fruited hakea, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its woody structure and dense, cylindrical inflorescences that bear small, urn-shaped flowers. The plant produces thick, fleshy fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a hard, woody capsule. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Hakea dasycarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
