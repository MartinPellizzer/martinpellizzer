Holopeira villosa

Holopeira villosa, commonly known as the silky holly or silky elder, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems covered in soft, silvery-white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits in the fall. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy texture.

Holopeira villosa is typically found in moist, shaded woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
