Hieracium Hypochoeroides Subsp. Plauense

Hieracium hypochoeroides subsp. plauense is a lesser-known member of the daisy family found in specific regions of europe. it is characterized by its hairy stems and yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. this subspecies thrives in moist, grassy habitats and is often overlooked due to its modest appearance. despite its simplicity, it plays an important role in local ecosystems by supporting pollinators. conservation efforts are needed to protect this plant from habitat loss and environmental changes.
