Hieracium suecicum subsp. cochleatum

Hieracium suecicum subsp.

cochleatum, commonly known as the Swedish hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head.

This subspecies is distinguished by its slightly curled flower heads, giving it a unique appearance among other hawkweed species.
