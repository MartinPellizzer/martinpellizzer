Hibiscus atroviolaceus

Hibiscus atroviolaceus, commonly known as the dark violet hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from deep violet to almost black. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. Hibiscus atroviolaceus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
