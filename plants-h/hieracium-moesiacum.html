Hieracium moesiacum

Hieracium moesiacum, commonly known as the Moesia hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium moesiacum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resilience to environmental stress.
