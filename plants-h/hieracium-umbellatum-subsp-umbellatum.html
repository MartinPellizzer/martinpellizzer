Hieracium umbellatum subsp. umbellatum

Hieracium umbellatum subsp.

umbellatum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its yellowish to reddish-orange flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster called an umbel.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed areas, preferring well-drained soils. It has basal leaves that are heart-shaped and often have hairy surfaces.

The species is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common and sometimes invasive weed in various regions.
