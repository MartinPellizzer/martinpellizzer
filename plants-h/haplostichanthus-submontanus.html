Haplostichanthus submontanus

Haplostichanthus submontanus, commonly known as the mountain holly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a leathery texture and a fragrant scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
