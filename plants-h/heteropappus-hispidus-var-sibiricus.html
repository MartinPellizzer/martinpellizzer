Heteropappus hispidus var. sibiricus

Heteropappus hispidus var. sibiricus, commonly known as the Siberian horseweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia and parts of Eastern Europe.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and covered in fine, stiff hairs.

The flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, contributing to its role in pollination and seed dispersal.
