Hieracium sparsum subsp. subsparsiflorum

Hieracium sparsum subsp.

subsparsiflorum, commonly known as the sparseflower hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and basal rosettes of lance-shaped leaves.

The flowers are yellow and arranged in a single, terminal inflorescence, with ray florets that are shorter than the disk florets. It thrives in sunny, dry habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

The plant is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
