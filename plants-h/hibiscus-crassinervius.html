Hibiscus crassinervius

Hibiscus crassinervius, commonly known as the broad-veined hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and red. The plant features palmately lobed leaves with prominent veins, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded habitats.

Hibiscus crassinervius is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
