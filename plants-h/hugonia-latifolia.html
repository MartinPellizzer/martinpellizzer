Hugonia latifolia

Hugonia latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf hugonia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It features rhizomatous growth and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Hugonia latifolia is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and forest understories, where it contributes to the local biodiversity.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland conservation efforts.
