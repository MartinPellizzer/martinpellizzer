Hibiscus incanus

Hibiscus incanus, commonly known as the white hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pale pink flowers that bloom during the summer months. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. Hibiscus incanus thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
