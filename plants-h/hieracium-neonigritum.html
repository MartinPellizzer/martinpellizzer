Hieracium neonigritum

Hieracium neonigritum, commonly known as the blackened hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a cluster of small flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are usually yellow or reddish in color.

Hieracium neonigritum is often found in open habitats and can sometimes be invasive in certain regions.
