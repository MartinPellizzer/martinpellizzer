Hymenaea courbaril

Hymenaea courbaril, commonly known as the balloon tree, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated seed pods that resemble balloons, which give it its common name. The tree produces showy yellow flowers and has a rough, fissured bark. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and is used in furniture and construction.

The plant also has medicinal uses in traditional medicine.
