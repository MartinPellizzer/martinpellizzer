Hieracium melamphorum

Hieracium melamphorum, commonly known as the black hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a distinctive dark center.

It is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is considered a potential weed in some regions.
