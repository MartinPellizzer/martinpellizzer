Hesperantha pilosa subsp. latifolia

Hesperantha pilosa subsp.

latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved hesperantha, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family native to South Africa. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and unique floral display, it is widely cultivated in horticulture.
