Helleborine atropurpurea var. dilatata

Helleborine atropurpurea var. dilatata, commonly known as the purple helleborine, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple to blackish leaves and its showy, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive purple lip. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky areas. It is a perennial herb that produces aerial rhizomes and has a complex root system.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
