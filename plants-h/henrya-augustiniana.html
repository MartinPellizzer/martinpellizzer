Henrya augustiniana

Henrya augustiniana, commonly known as the silver-leaved henrya, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with a silvery-blue hue. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in spring. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.
