Haloragis tetragyna var. serrata

Haloragis tetragyna var. serrata, commonly known as the serrated willow herb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in small, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in dense spikes, often appearing in late summer to early autumn.

It plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
