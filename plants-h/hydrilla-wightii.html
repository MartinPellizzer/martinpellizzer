Hydrilla wightii

Hydrilla wightii, commonly known as the water plantain, is a submerged aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It has long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually found in clusters near the water surface. Hydrilla wightii thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

It is often found in ponds, lakes, and canals, where it can form dense mats that affect water quality and aquatic life.
