Hieracium niveicuspis

Hieracium niveicuspis, commonly known as the snow-haired hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk flowers. The plant is notable for its white to pale yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. Its leaves are typically hairy and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its hardy nature in alpine environments.

Hieracium niveicuspis is often found in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in cool, moist conditions.
