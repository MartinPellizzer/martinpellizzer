Helichrysum stuhlmannii

Helichrysum stuhlmannii, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its striking yellow to orange flowers that resemble small suns. The plant produces small, papery bracts that persist after the flowers have faded, giving it a long-lasting appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its resilience and ornamental value make it a favored plant in both horticulture and ecological landscaping.
