Hieracium tubulascens subsp. valdetubulosum

Hieracium tubulascens subsp.

valdetubulosum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It features basal rosettes of dark green, hairy leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in summer.

The plant is characterized by its tubular flowers, which are typically arranged in a dense inflorescence. It thrives in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, preferring well-drained soils.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its range.
