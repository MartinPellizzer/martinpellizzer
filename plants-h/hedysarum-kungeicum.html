Hedysarum kungeicum

Hedysarum kungeicum, commonly known as the Kungei sweetvetch, is a perennial leguminous plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in steppe and semi-arid environments.

Hedysarum kungeicum plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitats.
