Hyssopus cuspidatus var. albiflorus

Hyssopus cuspidatus var. albiflorus, commonly known as white-flowered hyssop, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, erect stems covered in grayish-blue hairy foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and aromatic, often used in traditional herbal medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
