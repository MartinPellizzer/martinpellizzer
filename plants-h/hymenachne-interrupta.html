Hymenachne interrupta

Hymenachne interrupta, commonly known as the interrupted hymenachne, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often slightly hairy. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with spikelets that are loosely arranged. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks.

Hymenachne interrupta plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
