Helianthus lodicatus

Helianthus lodicatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sunflower, is a species native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual plant that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces yellow flowers with a central disk surrounded by ray florets. Its seeds are small and oval, often used as a food source for birds and small mammals.

Helianthus lodicatus is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
