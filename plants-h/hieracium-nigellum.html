Hieracium nigellum

Hieracium nigellum, commonly known as the black hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its dark green, hairy leaves and yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in clusters. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant produces small, elongated achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

It is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
