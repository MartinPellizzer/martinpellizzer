Hydrangea serrata f. acuminata

Hydrangea serrata f. acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-toothed hydrangea, is a botanical variant of the common hydrangea.

It is characterized by its serrated, lance-shaped leaves and conical flower clusters. The plant typically blooms with large, showy inflorescences that range in color from blue to pink, depending on soil pH. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to temperate climates.
