Hoya benguetensis

Hoya benguetensis, commonly known as the Benguet Waxplant, is a species of flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and waxy, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Hoya benguetensis is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil.
