Hypochaeris claryi

Hypochaeris claryi, commonly known as yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with a central disc surrounded by ray florets, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Hypochaeris claryi is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and fields.

It is a perennial herb that can spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in certain regions.
