Haplopappus pinea

Haplopappus pinea, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. Its leaves are typically gray-green and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in harsh environments.

Haplopappus pinea is well adapted to sandy soils and can thrive in areas with limited rainfall.
