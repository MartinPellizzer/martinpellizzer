Hieracium laevigatum subsp. acroscotoides

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

acroscotoides, commonly known as the smooth meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and yellow disc flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and sandy soils across Europe and parts of Asia. It produces numerous seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
