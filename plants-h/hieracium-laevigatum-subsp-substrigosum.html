Hieracium laevigatum subsp. substrigosum

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

substrigosum, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow ray flowers and deeply lobed, hairy leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a creeping rootstock and can form dense colonies, making it a persistent weed in agricultural areas.

Its botanical features include glandular hairs and a distinctive, often branched stem.
