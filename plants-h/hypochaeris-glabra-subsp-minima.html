Hypochaeris glabra subsp. minima

Hypochaeris glabra subsp.

minima, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mouse-ear, is a small flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces small yellow flowers with ray florets.

This subspecies is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and disturbed soils. It thrives in temperate climates and is known for its ability to tolerate a variety of soil conditions.

The plant is often considered a weed but has ecological value as a food source for pollinators.
