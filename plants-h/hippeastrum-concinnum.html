Hippeastrum concinnum

Hippeastrum concinnum, commonly known as the red-veined amaryllis, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically range from white to deep red, with prominent purple veins. The plant produces large, strap-like leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Hippeastrum concinnum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
