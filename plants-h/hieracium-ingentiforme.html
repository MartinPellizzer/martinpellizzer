Hieracium ingentiforme

Hieracium ingentiforme, commonly known as the giant hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features bright yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically rough and hairy, with a dark green color and a slightly lobed shape.

Hieracium ingentiforme is known for its aggressive growth and is considered an invasive species in some regions.
