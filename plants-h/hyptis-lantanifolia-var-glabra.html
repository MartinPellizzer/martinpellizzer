Hyptis lantanifolia var. glabra

Hyptis lantanifolia var. glabra, commonly known as velvetleaf hyptis, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically in shades of white or pale purple. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for horticultural use.
