Hyptis monticola

Hyptis monticola, commonly known as mountain hyptis, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and basil. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a mild, mint-like fragrance.

Hyptis monticola is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
