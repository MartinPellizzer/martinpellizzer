Hibiscus syriacus var. micranthus

Hibiscus syriacus var. micranthus, commonly known as the small-flowered bush hibiscus, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features upright, branching stems and heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are typically 5-8 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy, single or double flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its flowers are characterized by their small, tightly packed petals and a prominent central pistil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of garden conditions.
