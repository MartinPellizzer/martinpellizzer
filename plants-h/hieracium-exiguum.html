Hieracium exiguum

Hieracium exiguum, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of tubular and ray florets. The plant typically features hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats, often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Its flowers bloom in the summer, producing a yellow coloration that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
