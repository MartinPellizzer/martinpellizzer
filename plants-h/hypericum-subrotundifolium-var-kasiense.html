Hypericum subrotundifolium var. kasiense

Hypericum subrotundifolium var. kasiense, commonly known as Kasi's golden strobile.

This plant is a flowering shrub native to Japan, characterized by its compact growth habit and bright yellow flowers. It features opposite, elliptical to ovate leaves with smooth margins, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. The flowers are typically clustered in small, cylindrical inflorescences, and the plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are attractive to birds.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in well-drained soil.
