Hieracium prussicum subsp. tephrantheloides

Hieracium prussicum subsp.

tephrantheloides, commonly known as the Prussian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in dry grasslands, meadows, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant has erect, hairy stems that bear basal and alternate leaves, which are usually lanceolate and hairy as well. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and it produces small, hairy achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

It is part of the Asteraceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
