Hieracium erythrocarpum subsp. petrohanicum

Hieracium erythrocarpum subsp.

petrohanicum, commonly known as the Red-fruited Hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellowish to orange flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its red or orange-pink fruits, which are distinctive and contribute to its ornamental value. It typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and heaths.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and threatened species in some areas.
