Hyptis pinetorum

Hyptis pinetorum, commonly known as mountain hyptis, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Hyptis pinetorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
