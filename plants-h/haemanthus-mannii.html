Haemanthus mannii

Haemanthus mannii, commonly known as the Natal yellow rain lily, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It produces striking yellow flowers in late summer, which are borne on long, narrow stalks. The plant features strap-like, grassy leaves that are typically green with a reddish tinge. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Haemanthus mannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
