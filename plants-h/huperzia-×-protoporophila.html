Huperzia × protoporophila

Huperzia × protoporophila, commonly known as the hybrid huperzia, is a fern-like plant belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is a sterile hybrid resulting from the cross between Huperzia squamosa and Huperzia phlegmaria, two species native to the eastern United States. The plant typically grows as a low, creeping mat with small, scale-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors, and is often found in the southeastern United States.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for studies on hybridization and plant morphology.
