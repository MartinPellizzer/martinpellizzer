Hieracium oxyodon subsp. pseudamauradenium

Hieracium oxyodon subsp.

pseudamauradenium, commonly known as the false yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with serrated margins, and its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is often considered an invasive species in some regions due to its rapid spread and competitive growth.
