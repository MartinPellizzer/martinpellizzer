Hedyotis caerulea

Hedyotis caerulea, commonly known as blue false indigo, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, erect stems that are often hairy and bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive blue-green hue. The plant produces small, clustered white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are typically borne on short pedicels and are arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

Hedyotis caerulea is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
