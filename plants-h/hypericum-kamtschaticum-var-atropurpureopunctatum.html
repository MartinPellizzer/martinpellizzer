Hypericum kamtschaticum var. atropurpureopunctatum

Hypericum kamtschaticum var. atropurpureopunctatum, commonly known as the dark purple-spotted St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters along the stems. The leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive dark purple or reddish-pink markings, especially on the underside. This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.
