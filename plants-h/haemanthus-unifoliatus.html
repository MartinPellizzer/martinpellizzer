Haemanthus unifoliatus

Haemanthus unifoliatus, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking South African plant characterized by its dense, spiky red bracts that resemble flames.

It is a bulbous perennial that produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes. Its unique appearance and ability to attract pollinators like birds and insects make it a valuable addition to native plant collections.

Haemanthus unifoliatus is also noted for its resilience to drought and coastal conditions.
