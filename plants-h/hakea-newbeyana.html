Hakea newbeyana

Hakea newbeyana, commonly known as the Newbey's hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical or club-shaped flowers, which are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant has needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces woody, spine-like structures that serve as protective features. It thrives in sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Hakea newbeyana is often found in coastal heathlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
