Hypolytrum punctatum

Hypolytrum punctatum, commonly known as the spotted flat sedge, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, flat leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, such as swampy areas and along riverbanks. Its leaves are often marked with distinct spots or lines, giving it its common name.

Hypolytrum punctatum plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments and provides habitat for various invertebrates.
