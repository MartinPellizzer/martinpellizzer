Helichrysum decaryi

Helichrysum decaryi, commonly known as the golden crown, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow to golden-orange flowers that resemble small sunbursts. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid environments. Its foliage is usually gray-green, with finely divided leaves that help reduce water loss.

Helichrysum decaryi is also known for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
