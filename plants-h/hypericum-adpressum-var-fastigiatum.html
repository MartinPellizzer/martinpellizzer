Hypericum adpressum var. fastigiatum

Hypericum adpressum var. fastigiatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf St. John's wort, is a compact, low-growing perennial shrub native to Europe.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, upright leaves that give it a neat, tidy appearance, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, preferring dry to moderately moist conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
