Hieracium gudbrandii

Hieracium gudbrandii, commonly known as Gudbrand's hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Scandinavia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, nutrient-rich soils in meadows and grasslands. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

It is generally low-growing, with hairy stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped.
