Hyptis diaphora

Hyptis diaphora, commonly known as the silverleaf hyptis, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs, and its small, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Hyptis diaphora is also used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
