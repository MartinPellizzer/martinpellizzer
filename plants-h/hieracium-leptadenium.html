Hieracium leptadenium

Hieracium leptadenium, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and have a distinct basal rosette, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head.

Hieracium leptadenium is often found in temperate regions and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.
