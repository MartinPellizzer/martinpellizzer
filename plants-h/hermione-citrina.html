Hermione citrina

Hermione citrina, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, hairy hairs. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to orange. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly serrated, with a soft, velvety texture.

Hermione citrina thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
