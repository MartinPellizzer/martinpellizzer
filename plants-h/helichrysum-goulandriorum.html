Helichrysum goulandriorum

Helichrysum goulandriorum, commonly known as the Goulandri's everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flower heads, which resemble miniature sunflowers. The plant produces small, tubular florets surrounded by a ring of ray florets, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to retain its color when dried.

Helichrysum goulandriorum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
