Hieracium cabicola

Hieracium cabicola, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Hieracium cabicola is often found in meadows and grasslands at high altitudes.
