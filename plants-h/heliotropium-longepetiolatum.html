Heliotropium longepetiolatum

Heliotropium longepetiolatum, commonly known as the long-petioled heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with long petioles, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple, often with a faint scent. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and waste areas.

Heliotropium longepetiolatum is a perennial herb that can grow up to 1 meter in height, making it a noticeable component of its native ecosystems.
