Hieracium marginellum

Hieracium marginellum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

It is considered a perennial herb, often found in open habitats where it can spread through rhizomes and seeds.
