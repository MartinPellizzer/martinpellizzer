Hieracium leiopogon subsp. hyposericum

Hieracium leiopogon subsp.

hyposericum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

The plant has basal and upright stems, and its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow, with a distinctive ray floret structure, and it produces small, oval seeds in a disk-like arrangement.

This subspecies is often found in high-altitude environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
