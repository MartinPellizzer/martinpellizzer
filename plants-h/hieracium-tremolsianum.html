Hieracium tremolsianum

Hieracium tremolsianum, commonly known as the Tremolsian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disc.

Hieracium tremolsianum is often found in meadows and grasslands at high altitudes, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
