Hieracium transbalticum

Hieracium transbalticum, commonly known as the Trans-Baltic hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Eastern Europe and western Russia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy foliage. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, pastures, and roadsides. Its leaves are lanceolate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are yellow, arranged in a single head.

It is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is considered an invasive species in some regions.
