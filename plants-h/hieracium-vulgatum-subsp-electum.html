Hieracium vulgatum subsp. electum

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

electum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features basal and upright stems with alternate, heart-shaped to lanceolate leaves. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and are produced in late spring to early summer.

It has a taproot system and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
