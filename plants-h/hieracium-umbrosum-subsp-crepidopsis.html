Hieracium umbrosum subsp. crepidopsis

Hieracium umbrosum subsp.

crepidopsis, commonly known as the shadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in composite flower heads, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its subspecific name, *crepidopsis*, refers to its resemblance to the closely related *Hieracium crepidatum*.
