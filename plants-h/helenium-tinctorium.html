Helenium tinctorium

Helenium tinctorium, commonly known as the American feverfew, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its bright orange to red flower heads, which resemble those of the sunflower genus. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its botanical name, Helenium, is derived from the Greek word "helene," meaning "to protect," likely referring to its historical use in herbal medicine.

Helenium tinctorium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties.
