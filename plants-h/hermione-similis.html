Hermione similis

Hermione similis, commonly known as the mimic orchid, is a striking plant native to the temperate regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its intricate, showy flowers that closely resemble those of other orchid species, a trait that aids in its pollination by misleading pollinators. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest understories, where it thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil. Its leaves are elongated and lance-shaped, with a waxy surface that helps retain moisture.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing a fragrant scent that attracts specific insect pollinators.
