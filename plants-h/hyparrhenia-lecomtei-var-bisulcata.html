Hyparrhenia lecomtei var. bisulcata

Hyparrhenia lecomtei var. bisulcata, commonly known as the African grass or narrow-leaf blady grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, blade-like leaves that are often ribbed or channelled, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Hyparrhenia lecomtei var. bisulcata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional practices in some regions.
