Hibiscus pedunculatus

Hibiscus pedunculatus, commonly known as the rock hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dry seeds. Hibiscus pedunculatus thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking blooms.
