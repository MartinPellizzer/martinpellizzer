Humboltia cochlearis

Humboltia cochlearis, commonly known as the California sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of California and Baja California.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spiny fruits that contain seeds. Humboltia cochlearis thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal scrub habitats.

It is a hardy, drought-tolerant species that plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes.
