Hypericum × caesariense

Hypericum × caesariense, commonly known as the golden st. john's wort, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, dark green leaves and a compact growth habit, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.

Its botanical name reflects its origin from the genus Hypericum, which includes several species of flowering plants known for their medicinal properties.
