Hedyotis arenarioides

Hedyotis arenarioides, commonly known as sandstone bluemink, is a perennial herb native to arid and rocky habitats in southern Africa.

It features small, narrow leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces clusters of tiny, blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to anchor firmly in loose, sandy soils.

Hedyotis arenarioides is often found in coastal and inland sandstone formations, contributing to the biodiversity of these unique ecosystems.
