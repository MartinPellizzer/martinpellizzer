Hymenopappus arenosus

Hymenopappus arenosus, commonly known as the sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms a dense mat of foliage, adapted to thrive in sandy and rocky soils. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its unique seed structure includes a pappus, which aids in wind dispersal, allowing the plant to colonize new areas effectively.

Hymenopappus arenosus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various desert organisms.
