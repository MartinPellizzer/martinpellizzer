Hieracium bifidum subsp. subcyaneum

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

subcyaneum, commonly known as the blue hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosette of leaves, which are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine pubescence.

The plant produces small, blue to purple composite flowers arranged in dense clusters, which attract various pollinators. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

This subspecies is distinguished by its more intense blue flower color compared to other members of the species.
