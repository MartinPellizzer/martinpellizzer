Holodiscus dumosus var. glabrescens

Holodiscus dumosus var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smoothleaf holodiscus, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and small, elliptical leaves that are typically smooth and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along streambanks.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to a range of environmental conditions.
