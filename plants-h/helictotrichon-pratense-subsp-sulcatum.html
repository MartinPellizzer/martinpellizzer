Helictotrichon pratense subsp. sulcatum

Helictotrichon pratense subsp.

sulcatum, commonly known as the hairy bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America. It is characterized by its fine, hairy leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats in meadows and open woodlands.

The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers in late spring, which are clustered in spikelets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist to mesic habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
