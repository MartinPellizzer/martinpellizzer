Hyptis pulchella

Hyptis pulchella, commonly known as the pink hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy, pinkish-purple flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are clustered in whorls at the ends of the stems.

Hyptis pulchella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
