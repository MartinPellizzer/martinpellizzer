Hermannia ternifolia

Hermannia ternifolia, commonly known as the three-leaf herb, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that typically range in color from white to pink. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Hermannia ternifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
