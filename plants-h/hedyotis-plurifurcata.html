Hedyotis plurifurcata

Hedyotis plurifurcata, commonly known as the branched dotweed, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a low-growing herbaceous plant with small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny white or pale yellow flowers, which are typically arranged in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Hedyotis plurifurcata is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
