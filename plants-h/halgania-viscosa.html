Halgania viscosa

Halgania viscosa, commonly known as the sticky thornapple, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems covered in a sticky resin, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by distinctive, spiny fruits. Its leaves are narrow and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Halgania viscosa is adapted to survive in harsh environments and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
