Hedysarum clandestinum

Hedysarum clandestinum, commonly known as the "secret honeysuckle," is a perennial leguminous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces elongated, bean-like pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Hedysarum clandestinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
