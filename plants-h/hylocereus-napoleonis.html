Hylocereus napoleonis

Hylocereus napoleonis, commonly known as the sweet star apple, is a climbing cactus belonging to the Cactaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its cylindrical stems and spiny areoles. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths. Its fruit, which is typically green or yellow when mature, is edible and has a sweet, juicy flavor.

The plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and for its unique fruit.
