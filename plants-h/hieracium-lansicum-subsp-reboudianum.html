Hieracium lansicum subsp. reboudianum

Hieracium lansicum subsp.

reboudianum, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes. It has a taproot system and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in alpine flora studies.
