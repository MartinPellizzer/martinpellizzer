Hieracium sylvaticum subsp. subreniforme

Hieracium sylvaticum subsp.

subreniforme, commonly known as the rough meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are rough to the touch, with a serrated margin, and its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

It is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments, thriving in nutrient-rich soils.
