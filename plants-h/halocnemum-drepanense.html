Halocnemum drepanense

Halocnemum drepanense, commonly known as the sea bladder, is a halophytic plant adapted to thrive in saline environments.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, salt marshes, and other saline habitats where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually greenish or reddish and are pollinated by wind.

Halocnemum drepanense is also used in traditional medicine and has been studied for its potential ecological and economic benefits.
