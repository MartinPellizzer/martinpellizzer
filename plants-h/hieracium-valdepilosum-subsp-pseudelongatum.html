Hieracium valdepilosum subsp. pseudelongatum

Hieracium valdepilosum subsp.

pseudelongatum, commonly known as the long-stemmed hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems.

The subspecies is distinguished by its elongated stem and leaf structure compared to other members of the species. It typically grows in meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant is often found in open habitats and can be an indicator of disturbed or nutrient-rich environments.
