Hieracium murorum var. flavescens

Hieracium murorum var. flavescens, commonly known as yellow mouse-ear hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by pale green to white ray florets. It thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Due to its invasive tendencies, it is considered a weed in some regions.
