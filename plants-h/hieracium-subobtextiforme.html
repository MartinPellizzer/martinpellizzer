Hieracium subobtextiforme

Hieracium subobtextiforme, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and often hairy, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
