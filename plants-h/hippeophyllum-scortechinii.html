Hippeophyllum scortechinii

Hippeophyllum scortechinii, commonly known as the desert nightshade, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in rocky soils and desert environments. The plant produces small, purple to violet flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its fruit is a fleshy, red berry that contains several seeds.

The species is adapted to survive in harsh conditions with minimal water and high temperatures.
