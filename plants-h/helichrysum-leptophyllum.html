Helichrysum leptophyllum

Helichrysum leptophyllum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, golden-yellow flowers that remain intact for a long time after blooming. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

The flowers are also used in floral arrangements due to their long-lasting nature.
