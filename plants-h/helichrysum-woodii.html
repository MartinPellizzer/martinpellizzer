Helichrysum woodii

Helichrysum woodii, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and produces small, yellow to orange flowers that persist for a long time after blooming. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Helichrysum woodii is also valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine.
