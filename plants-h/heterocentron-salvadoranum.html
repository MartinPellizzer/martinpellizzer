Heterocentron salvadoranum

Heterocentron salvadoranum, commonly known as the Salvadoran water lily, is a species of aquatic plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves and showy white flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its roots anchor it to the substrate, while its leaves provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.

The flowers emit a mild fragrance, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
