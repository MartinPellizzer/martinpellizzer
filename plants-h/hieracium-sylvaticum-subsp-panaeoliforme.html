Hieracium sylvaticum subsp. panaeoliforme

Hieracium sylvaticum subsp.

panaeoliforme, commonly known as the woodland hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow to orange composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

It is a relatively small plant, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.
