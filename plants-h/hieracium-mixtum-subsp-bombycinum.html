Hieracium mixtum subsp. bombycinum

Hieracium mixtum subsp.

bombycinum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture.

The inflorescence consists of a single flower head surrounded by several involucral bracts.
