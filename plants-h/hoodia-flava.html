Hoodia flava

Hoodia flava, commonly known as the desert apple, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant is well adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, storing water in its thick leaves and stems. Hoodia flava is also notable for its ability to produce a milky sap, which has been used traditionally by indigenous peoples.

Its unique botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and ethnobotanical studies.
