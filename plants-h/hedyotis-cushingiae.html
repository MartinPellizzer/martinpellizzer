Hedyotis cushingiae

Hedyotis cushingiae, commonly known as the Cushing's spurge, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories and rocky crevices. The plant features small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of tiny, yellowish flowers. Its flowering period usually occurs in the spring and early summer.

Hedyotis cushingiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
