Hymenolaena candollei

Hymenolaena candollei, commonly known as the silverleaf grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery bloom, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale blue or purple flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along riverbanks.

Hymenolaena candollei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
