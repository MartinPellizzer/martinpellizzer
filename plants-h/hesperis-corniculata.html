Hesperis corniculata

Hesperis corniculata, commonly known as the sweet William, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with showy blooms. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, four-petaled flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, and the plant grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters.

Hesperis corniculata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
