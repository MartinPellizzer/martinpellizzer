Hibiscus rhodopetalus f. angustisectus

Hibiscus rhodopetalus f. angustisectus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf woods rose, is a variant of the native Hawaiian hibiscus.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, and is adapted to the tropical climate of Hawaii. It is a shrub that can reach heights of up to 3 meters, with woody stems and persistent leaves.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its beauty and resilience in humid conditions.
