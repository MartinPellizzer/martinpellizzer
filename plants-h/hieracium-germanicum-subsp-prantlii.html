Hieracium germanicum subsp. prantlii

Hieracium germanicum subsp.

prantlii, commonly known as the German hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It has finely divided, serrated leaves and produces numerous small, rayless flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
