Hieracium villosiceps subsp. catognense

Hieracium villosiceps subsp.

catognense, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and forest edges.

The plant features a basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its inflorescence consists of numerous small, daisy-like flowers with yellow ray florets and brown disk florets.

The species is characterized by its distinctive hairy foliage and is often found in nutrient-poor soils.
