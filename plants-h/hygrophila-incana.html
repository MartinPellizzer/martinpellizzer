Hygrophila incana

Hygrophila incana, commonly known as the woolly hygrophila, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. It produces small, white flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Hygrophila incana is often used in water gardens and aquariums due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist conditions.
