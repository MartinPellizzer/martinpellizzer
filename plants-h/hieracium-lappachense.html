Hieracium lappachense

Hieracium lappachense, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils at high elevations. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium lappachense is known for its ability to form dense colonies, often acting as a pioneer species in disturbed or barren environments.
