Hieracium cymosum subsp. lanipedicellum

Hieracium cymosum subsp.

lanipedicellum, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe. It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are covered in dense, white to grayish woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowering stems bear clusters of small, yellowish to brownish composite flowers, which are arranged in cymes.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dense, short, and branched pedicels, contributing to its specific epithet "lanipedicellum.".
