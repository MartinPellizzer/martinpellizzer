Hypericum mairei

Hypericum mairei, commonly known as the Maire's St. John's Wort, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, dark green leaves and produces small, orange-red berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Hypericum mairei is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
