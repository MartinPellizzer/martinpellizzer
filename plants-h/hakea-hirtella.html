Hakea hirtella

Hakea hirtella, commonly known as the hairy hakea, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Hakea hirtella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
