Heteromorpha laxiflora var. alticola

Heteromorpha laxiflora var. alticola, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive yellow or green throat. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climates.
