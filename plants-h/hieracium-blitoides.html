Hieracium blitoides

Hieracium blitoides, commonly known as the blighty hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and ability to spread easily.
