Hemigenia

Hemigenia, commonly known as the honey bush, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Lamiaceae.

These plants are native to Australia and are characterized by their aromatic leaves and showy flowers. Hemigenia species typically have square stems and opposite leaves, with small, tubular flowers that are often clustered in whorls. They are valued for their fragrant properties and are sometimes used in herbal medicine.

The genus includes several species, such as Hemigenia amara and Hemigenia hylandii, which are adapted to various habitats across Australia.
