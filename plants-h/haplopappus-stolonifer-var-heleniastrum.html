Haplopappus stolonifer var. heleniastrum

Haplopappus stolonifer var. heleniastrum, commonly known as the Helen's milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial with slender, branching stems that often form dense mats. The plant produces small, yellow flowers clustered in dense, spherical heads, which are surrounded by pale yellow to white bracts. Its leaves are narrow, slightly hairy, and arranged alternately along the stems.

It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.
