Hordeum korshinskianum

Hordeum korshinskianum, commonly known as Korshinsky's barley, is a wild grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical spikelets and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers and is often found in mountainous regions.

Hordeum korshinskianum is of interest to botanists due to its potential for genetic research and its role in ecological studies.
