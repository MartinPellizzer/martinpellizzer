Hakea kimberleyensis

Hakea kimberleyensis, commonly known as Kimberley hakea, is a shrub native to the Kimberley region of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical or club-shaped flower spikes that are typically pink to red in color. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the arid and semi-arid climates of its native habitat.

Hakea kimberleyensis is often found in rocky outcrops and open woodlands, where it contributes to the local biodiversity.
