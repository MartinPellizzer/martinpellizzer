Hieracium horridellum

Hieracium horridellum, commonly known as the rough hawksbeard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun. Its leaves are rough and hairy, with a serrated edge, and its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

Hieracium horridellum is often found in temperate regions and can sometimes be considered a weed in agricultural settings.
