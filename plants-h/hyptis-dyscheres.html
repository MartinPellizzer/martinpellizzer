Hyptis dyscheres

Hyptis dyscheres, commonly known as the Mexican blue sage, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces spikes of blue to purple flowers. Its leaves are typically gray-green and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Hyptis dyscheres is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
