Hemionitis × prototinaei

Hemionitis × prototinaei, commonly known as the hybrid fern, is a rare and unique plant resulting from the cross between Hemionitis arifolia and Hemionitis arifolia var. prototinaei.

It is a member of the Hemionitidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that exhibit a distinctive pattern of pinnae. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its botanical significance lies in its role as a hybrid species, offering insights into genetic diversity and adaptation in ferns.

Due to its rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value and scientific interest.
