Heptapleurum longifolium var. incurvum

Heptapleurum longifolium var. incurvum, commonly known as the curved-leaf heptapleurum, is a variant of the evergreen shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are often curled or rolled at the edges, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest undergrowth, where it thrives in partial to full shade. Its foliage is dense and leathery, with a glossy texture that enhances its ornamental value.

The species is valued in horticulture for its compact growth habit and ability to tolerate a range of soil conditions.
