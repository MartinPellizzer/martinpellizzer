Hieracium phyllophyton

Hieracium phyllophyton, commonly known as the leafy hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features basal and alternate leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its yellow flowers appear in clusters and bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Hieracium phyllophyton is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, where it thrives in partial shade to full sun.
