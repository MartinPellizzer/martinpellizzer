Hieracium umbellatum var. paniculatum

Hieracium umbellatum var. paniculatum, commonly known as the panicled hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its inflorescence consists of a single, branched flower head.

The species is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in agricultural and natural areas.
