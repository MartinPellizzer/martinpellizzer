Hymenophyllum schomburgkii

Hymenophyllum schomburgkii, commonly known as the silver moss, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided, and often silvery-green fronds that are covered with a dense layer of minute scales. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and are protected by a thin, membranous covering.

Hymenophyllum schomburgkii is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is valued for its ability to thrive in humid, low-light conditions.
