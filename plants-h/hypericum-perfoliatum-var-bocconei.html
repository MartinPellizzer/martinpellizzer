Hypericum perfoliatum var. bocconei

Hypericum perfoliatum var. bocconei, commonly known as the Boccone's St. John's Wort, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its stems are typically woody and can become somewhat succulent.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
