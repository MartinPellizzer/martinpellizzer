Hieracium livescens

Hieracium livescens, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosettes of lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Hieracium livescens is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
