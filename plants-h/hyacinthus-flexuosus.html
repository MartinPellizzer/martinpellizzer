Hyacinthus flexuosus

Hyacinthus flexuosus, commonly known as the drooping hyacinth, is a bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers in early spring. The plant has narrow, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its bulbs are perennial, capable of surviving through winter dormancy.

Hyacinthus flexuosus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and pleasant aroma.
