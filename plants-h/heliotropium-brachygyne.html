Heliotropium brachygyne

Heliotropium brachygyne, commonly known as the short-stemmed heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a low-growing herbaceous species that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, often resembling those of the common heliotrope. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Heliotropium brachygyne is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including gardens and roadsides.
