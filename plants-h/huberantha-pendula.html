Huberantha pendula

Huberantha pendula, commonly known as the weeping tamarind, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its drooping clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces elongated, bean-like pods containing edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. Its leaves are compound, with trifoliate or pinnate arrangements, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

The weeping tamarind is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some medicinal uses in local herbal practices.
