Hakea phillipsiana

Hakea phillipsiana, commonly known as the coastal hakea, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its rigid, spine-tipped leaves and dense clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant thrives in sandy soils and is well adapted to coastal environments. It plays a role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for native wildlife.

Hakea phillipsiana is often used in coastal landscaping due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
