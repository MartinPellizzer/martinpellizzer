Hopea argentea

Hopea argentea, commonly known as silver hopea, is a species of flowering plant in the family Dipterocarpaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its smooth, silver-gray bark and elliptical leaves that are glossy on top and pale underneath. It produces small, pale yellow flowers and is often found in lowland dipterocarp forests.

Hopea argentea is valued for its timber, which is used in construction and furniture making.
