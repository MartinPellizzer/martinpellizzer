Howardia febrifuga

Howardia febrifuga, commonly known as the feverfew plant, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its fleshy, gray-green leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in winter. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments. Its common name derives from its traditional use in treating fever and inflammatory conditions.

Howardia febrifuga is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
