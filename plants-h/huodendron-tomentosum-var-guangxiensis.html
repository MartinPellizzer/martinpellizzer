Huodendron tomentosum var. guangxiensis

Huodendron tomentosum var. guangxiensis, commonly known as Guangxi hairy hollies, is a rare evergreen shrub native to southern China.

It is characterized by its dense, grayish-white pubescent foliage and small, dark red berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are elliptical with a leathery texture and a prominent midrib.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
