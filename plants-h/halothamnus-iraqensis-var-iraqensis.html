Halothamnus iraqensis var. iraqensis

Halothamnus iraqensis var. iraqensis, commonly known as the Iraqi halothamnus, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves adapted to water conservation. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or inland desert environments.

Due to its resilience, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects in degraded landscapes.
