Hesperis arabica

Hesperis arabica, commonly known as the Arabica sweet rocket, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with four-petaled flowers. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a slightly rough texture.

Hesperis arabica is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
