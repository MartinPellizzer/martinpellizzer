Humboltia laurifolia

Humboltia laurifolia, commonly known as the California laurel, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a fragrant aroma when crushed. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. Humboltia laurifolia thrives in coastal and inland regions with well-drained soil and moderate to high humidity.

It is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats, contributing to local biodiversity.
