Hieracium ocriodes

Hieracium ocriodes, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Hieracium ocriodes is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
