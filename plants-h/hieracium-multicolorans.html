Hieracium multicolorans

Hieracium multicolorans, commonly known as the multicolored hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, often forming dense mats due to its rhizomatous root system. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with ray florets that may change color as they age.

Hieracium multicolorans is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
