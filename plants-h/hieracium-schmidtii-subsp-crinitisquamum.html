Hieracium schmidtii subsp. crinitisquamum

Hieracium schmidtii subsp.

crinitisquamum, commonly known as the silky-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant thrives in alpine meadows and rocky soils, preferring cool, moist environments. Its subspecies name, *crinitisquamum*, refers to the silky, scaly appearance of its foliage.

This plant plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing soil in its habitat.
