Hyptis recurvata var. grandifolia

Hyptis recurvata var. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-leaved hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped, serrated leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Its aromatic properties make it a popular choice for ornamental and medicinal purposes.
