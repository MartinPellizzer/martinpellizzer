Helianthemum font-queri

Helianthemum font-queri, commonly known as the rock rose of the Pyrenees, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, showy yellow flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine conditions. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Helianthemum font-queri is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in difficult garden conditions.
