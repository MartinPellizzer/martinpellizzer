Hyacinthus orientalis subsp. provincialis

Hyacinthus orientalis subsp.

provincialis, commonly known as the Dutch hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Netherlands. It produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, violet, and white.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements. Its bulbs are typically planted in the fall and bloom in early spring.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticulture.
