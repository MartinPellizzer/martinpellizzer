Hyptianthera bracteata

Hyptianthera bracteata, commonly known as the pink-flowered hyptianthera, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and pairs of opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in clusters at the leaf axils. These flowers are followed by small, woody fruits that contain several seeds.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
