Hyptis acuta

Hyptis acuta, commonly known as acute hyptis, is a species of flowering plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of the Americas, particularly Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or blue. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Hyptis acuta is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
