Hibiscus rogersii

Hibiscus rogersii, commonly known as the Rogers' hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and often branched.

Hibiscus rogersii is a member of the Malvaceae family, which includes other hibiscus species and cotton.
