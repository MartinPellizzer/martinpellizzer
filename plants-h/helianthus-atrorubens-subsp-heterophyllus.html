Helianthus atrorubens subsp. heterophyllus

Helianthus atrorubens subsp.

heterophyllus, commonly known as the redstem sunflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, and has adapted to saturated soil conditions. Its leaves are usually alternate and have a rough texture, often with lobed or serrated edges.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family and plays a role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
