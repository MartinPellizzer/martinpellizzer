Hypochaeris adscendens

Hypochaeris adscendens, commonly known as the ascending weed or yellow oxalis, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits known as cypsela. Native to South America, it is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in some regions.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural and garden settings.
