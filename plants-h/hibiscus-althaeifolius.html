Hibiscus althaeifolius

Hibiscus althaeifolius, commonly known as the African hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red, with a prominent central pistil. The plant has palmate leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Hibiscus althaeifolius is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
