Hippeastrum berteroi

Hippeastrum berteroi, commonly known as the Brazilian amaryllis, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It features large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. The plant has strap-like, dark green leaves that emerge from a bulbous base, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Hippeastrum berteroi is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms and is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
