Helianthemum pulchellum

Helianthemum pulchellum, commonly known as the pink sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fleshy leaves and produces clusters of pink or purple flowers in early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas. It is drought-tolerant and has a compact growth habit, making it suitable for rock gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive and resilient addition to Mediterranean-style landscapes.
