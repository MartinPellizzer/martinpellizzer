Hieracium pachylodes subsp. eucomoides

Hieracium pachylodes subsp.

eucomoides, commonly known as the European oxeye daisy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flower heads surrounded by white ray florets.

The plant typically grows in open grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun. Its leaves are basal, rough, and covered in fine hairs, while the stem is upright and often hairy.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its cheerful appearance.
