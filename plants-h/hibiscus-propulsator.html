Hibiscus propulsator

Hibiscus propulsator, commonly known as the rocket hibiscus, is a unique species characterized by its rapid growth and distinctive floral structure.

It features large, showy flowers with five petals that often display vibrant hues of red, pink, or yellow. The plant's leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. Hibiscus propulsator thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ability to propagate quickly makes it both a valuable ornamental plant and a potential invasive species in certain environments.
