Hieracium murorum subsp. gigantolobum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

gigantolobum, commonly known as the giant lobed hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy structure.

The plant features large, lobed leaves that are typically hairy and have a rough texture. Its showy yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

It thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found in meadows, roadsides, and other open habitats.
