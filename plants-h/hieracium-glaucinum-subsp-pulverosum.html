Hieracium glaucinum subsp. pulverosum

Hieracium glaucinum subsp.

pulverosum, commonly known as the powdered hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its finely divided, gray-green leaves and small yellow flowers.

The plant is typically found in dry meadows, rocky soils, and open woodlands. Its subspecies name, *pulverosum*, refers to the powdery texture of its leaves, which are covered in fine hairs.

It is often considered a weed in agricultural areas due to its aggressive growth habit.
