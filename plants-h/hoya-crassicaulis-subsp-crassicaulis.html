Hoya crassicaulis subsp. crassicaulis

Hoya crassicaulis subsp.

crassicaulis, commonly known as the thick-leaved wax plant, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia. It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy texture, adapted to store water in its native humid environments.

The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white or pink, which bloom in response to specific light and temperature conditions. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This species is popular among succulent and tropical plant enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
