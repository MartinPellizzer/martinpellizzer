Hieracium transiens subsp. incomptum

Hieracium transiens subsp.

incomptum, commonly known as the wandering hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural and natural settings.
