Hibbertia spicata subsp. leptotheca

Hibbertia spicata subsp.

leptotheca, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hibbertia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia. It is a shrub characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with prominent, showy bracts that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, typically found in coastal and tableland regions.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important component of its native habitat.
