Hibiscus longifilus

Hibiscus longifilus, commonly known as the longleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in summer. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves and can grow as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its flowers are usually pink to red in color, with five petals and a prominent central stigma.

Hibiscus longifilus is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking blooms and adaptability to various soil types.
