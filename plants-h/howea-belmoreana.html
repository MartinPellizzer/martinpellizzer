Howea belmoreana

Howea belmoreana, commonly known as the Norfolk Island palm, is a striking tropical plant native to Norfolk Island in the South Pacific.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange-red fruits containing seeds. Howea belmoreana thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a valuable addition to botanical gardens and private collections.
