Hieracium perfulvescens

Hieracium perfulvescens, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its yellow flowers are followed by fluffy, seed-bearing structures that aid in wind pollination.

Hieracium perfulvescens is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize open areas, making it both a common weed and a valued wildflower.
