Hieracium arnicoides

Hieracium arnicoides, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are rough and hairy, with a serrated edge, and its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium arnicoides is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
