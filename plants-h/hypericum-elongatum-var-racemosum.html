Hypericum elongatum var. racemosum

Hypericum elongatum var. racemosum, commonly known as the fragrant St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers with prominent stamens, and its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

The plant is noted for its pleasant, spicy fragrance and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
