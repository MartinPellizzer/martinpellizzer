Hibiscus baidoensis

Hibiscus baidoensis, commonly known as the Baidoensis hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and red. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Hibiscus baidoensis is part of the Malvaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as cotton and okra.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and potential ecological roles in its native habitats.
