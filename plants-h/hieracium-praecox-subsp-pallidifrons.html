Hieracium praecox subsp. pallidifrons

Hieracium praecox subsp.

pallidifrons, commonly known as the pale-browed hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and a basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are often covered in fine, white hairs, giving them a silvery appearance.

This subspecies is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
