Hieracium araeocladum

Hieracium araeocladum, commonly known as the mountain hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typical daisy-like appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium araeocladum is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh, high-altitude environments.
