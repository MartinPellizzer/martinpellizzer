Hygrophila petiolata

Hygrophila petiolata, commonly known as the slender water hyssop, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its opposite, narrow, and often hairy leaves that are adapted to wet environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in whorled clusters. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common species in wetlands and along water edges.

Hygrophila petiolata is often used in aquatic gardening and as a decorative plant in water features.
