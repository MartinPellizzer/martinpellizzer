Hieracium gaudryi subsp. gaudryi

Hieracium gaudryi subsp.

gaudryi, commonly known as the Gaudry's hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster, which bloom from spring to summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
