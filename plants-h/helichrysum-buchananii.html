Helichrysum buchananii

Helichrysum buchananii, commonly known as the Cape goldflax, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, bract-like leaves that are often mistaken for petals, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Helichrysum buchananii is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
