Hieracium pulchellum subsp. seusanum

Hieracium pulchellum subsp.

seusanum, commonly known as the Italian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and produces yellow flowers in late summer.

The plant is native to southern Europe and is often found in grassy areas and disturbed soils. It has hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
