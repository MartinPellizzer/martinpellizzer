Hecatea oppositifolia

Hecatea oppositifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed hecatea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its opposite leaf arrangement and large, showy flowers that are often brightly colored. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are usually broad and elliptical, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Hecatea oppositifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
