Hieraciodes schultzii

Hieraciodes schultzii, commonly known as the Schultze's hairvetch, is a perennial legume native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and nitrogen-fixing ability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
