Holosteum umbellatum subsp. tenerrimum

Holosteum umbellatum subsp.

tenerrimum, commonly known as the slender marsh bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetland and marsh habitats in eastern North America. It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in umbels.

The plant has slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating in water. Its flowers bloom from June to September, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic environments and plays a role in wetland ecosystems.
