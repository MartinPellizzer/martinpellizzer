Hieracium limburgense

Hieracium limburgense, commonly known as the Limburg hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring well-drained conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
