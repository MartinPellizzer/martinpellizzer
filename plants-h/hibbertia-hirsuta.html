Hibbertia hirsuta

Hibbertia hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy hibbertia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a shrub characterized by its erect or spreading growth habit and hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers with five petals, typically appearing in spring. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a rough texture and often have a reddish or purplish tinge.

Hibbertia hirsuta is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.
