Hieracium sylvaticum subsp. cuspidellum

Hieracium sylvaticum subsp.

cuspidellum, commonly known as the woodland hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its yellowish to orange flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its elongated, pointed bracts and slightly hairy leaves. It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

This plant plays a role in supporting local pollinators and contributes to the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
