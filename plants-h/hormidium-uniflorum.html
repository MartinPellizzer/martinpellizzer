Hormidium uniflorum

Hormidium uniflorum, commonly known as the single-flower hormidium, is a unique species of flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces a single, showy flower per stem. The plant's leaves are typically small, oval, and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to white in color, with a distinctive, elongated shape.

Hormidium uniflorum is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.
