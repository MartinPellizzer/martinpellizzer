Hydrangea opuloides

Hydrangea opuloides, commonly known as the mountain hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to the forests of eastern North America.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from white to pink, depending on soil pH. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, often found in moist, woodland habitats.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
