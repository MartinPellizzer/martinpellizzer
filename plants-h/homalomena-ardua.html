Homalomena ardua

Homalomena ardua, commonly known as the mountain ginger or giant arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and striking inflorescence. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are broad and glossy, with a waxy texture, and it produces clusters of small white flowers followed by orange berries.

Homalomena ardua is valued for its ornamental appeal and has also been used in traditional medicine.
