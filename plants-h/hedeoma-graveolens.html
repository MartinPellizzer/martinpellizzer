Hedeoma graveolens

Hedeoma graveolens, commonly known as Mexican oregano, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and small, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its fragrant oils and medicinal properties. Its leaves are often used in cooking and traditional medicine for their pungent, peppery flavor.

Hedeoma graveolens thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy plant in suitable climates.
