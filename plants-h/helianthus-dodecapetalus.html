Helianthus dodecapetalus

Helianthus dodecapetalus, commonly known as the twelve-petaled sunflower, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flower heads with numerous ray florets arranged in a circular pattern. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and hairy.

The twelve-petaled sunflower is a herbaceous perennial that blooms in the summer months.
