Hypserpa ponapensis

Hypserpa ponapensis, commonly known as the Ponape Island orchid, is a rare flowering plant native to the tropical Pacific island of Pohnpei.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, white or pale yellow blossoms that bloom during the dry season. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species and is of significant botanical interest due to its unique ecological role.
