Heracleum sphondylium var. elegans

Heracleum sphondylium var. elegans, commonly known as the slender hogweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features tall, hollow stems that are covered in fine, white hairs, and it produces umbrella-like clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant has large, deeply lobed leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length, with a distinctive silvery underside. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant is noted for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
