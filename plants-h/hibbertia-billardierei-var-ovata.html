Hibbertia billardierei var. ovata

Hibbertia billardierei var. ovata, commonly known as the oval-leaf hibbertia, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, ovate-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, which are typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or heathlands.

Hibbertia billardierei var. ovata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
