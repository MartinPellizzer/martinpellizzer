Hieracium pallescens subsp. maledictum

Hieracium pallescens subsp.

maledictum, commonly known as the pale hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy, lanceolate leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. It has a creeping rhizome system that allows it to spread vegetatively, making it a persistent weed in some regions.

Its botanical features include basal rosettes of leaves and a height that ranges from 10 to 30 centimeters.
