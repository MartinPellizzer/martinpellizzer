Hieracium lachenalii subsp. ramosifolium

Hieracium lachenalii subsp.

ramosifolium, commonly known as the branchedleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and basal rosettes of narrowly lanceolate leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, flat-topped inflorescences, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically hairy and have a distinctive branching pattern, especially on the lower parts.

This subspecies is adapted to rocky and alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils.
