Hieracium acclinifolium

Hieracium acclinifolium, commonly known as the bent-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Compositae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems, with leaves that are typically recurved or bent at the edges. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas.

Hieracium acclinifolium is a perennial species that can spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in some habitats.
