Hyptis conferta var. excelsa

Hyptis conferta var. excelsa, commonly known as the silverleaf hyptis, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its grayish-white, hairy leaves that give it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or disturbed sites.

Hyptis conferta var. excelsa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
