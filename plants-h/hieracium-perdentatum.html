Hieracium perdentatum

Hieracium perdentatum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils across temperate regions. Its leaves are hairy and often have a serrated edge, while its flowering heads consist of numerous small florets.

Hieracium perdentatum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is sometimes considered invasive in certain areas.
