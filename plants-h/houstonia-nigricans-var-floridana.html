Houstonia nigricans var. floridana

Houstonia nigricans var. floridana, commonly known as Florida bluebells, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland areas. The plant features slender, erect stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Its most distinctive characteristic is the clusters of small, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring.

The species is part of the Gentianaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
