Hieracium brachycomum subsp. macrocephalum

Hieracium brachycomum subsp.

macrocephalum, commonly known as the large-headed hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas and disturbed soils, exhibiting a rosette of basal leaves with hairy surfaces. Its showy, yellow-orange flower heads consist of numerous small florets arranged in a disc-like structure.

It is an herbaceous perennial that thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.
