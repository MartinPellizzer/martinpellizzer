Hieracium pavichii var. multiglandulosum

Hieracium pavichii var. multiglandulosum, commonly known as the multiglandular hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a serrated margin, while the stems are erect and often branched.

The flowers are yellow, with the ray florets being longer than the disk florets, contributing to its ornamental appearance.
