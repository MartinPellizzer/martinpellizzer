Helianthus hastatus

Helianthus hastatus, commonly known as the stiff sunflower, is a species of flowering plant native to the United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, stiff, hairy leaves. The plant produces bright yellow ray flowers surrounded by a disk of smaller, tubular florets. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Helianthus hastatus is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other sunflowers and daisies.
