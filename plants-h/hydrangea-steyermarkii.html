Hydrangea steyermarkii

Hydrangea steyermarkii, commonly known as the Sierra Madre hydrangea, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in rocky or open woodland habitats. The plant produces large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from white to pink depending on soil pH. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a glossy surface and serrated edges.

Hydrangea steyermarkii is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to arid conditions.
