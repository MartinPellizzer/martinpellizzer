Heliotropium parviflorum

Heliotropium parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial species with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple, with a distinctive scent reminiscent of vanilla. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Heliotropium parviflorum is also known for its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
