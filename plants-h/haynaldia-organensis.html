Haynaldia organensis

Haynaldia organensis, commonly known as the Organ Mountains hollyhock, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and large, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species adapted to arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to xeriscapic gardens and native plant landscapes.
