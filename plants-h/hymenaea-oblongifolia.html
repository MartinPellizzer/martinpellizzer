Hymenaea oblongifolia

Hymenaea oblongifolia, commonly known as the Brazil nut tree, is a large evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody pods containing the edible Brazil nuts. Its seeds are encased in a hard, fibrous shell and require specific conditions to germinate.

The plant plays a crucial role in the ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
