Halianthus

Halianthus, commonly known as the sunflower, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its large, showy composite flowers that consist of a central disk surrounded by ray florets. The plants are native to various regions of the Americas and are known for their ability to thrive in sunny, well-drained soils. Halianthus species often have rough, hairy stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves.

They are widely cultivated for their ornamental value and for the production of oil from their seeds.
