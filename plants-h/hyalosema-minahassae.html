Hyalosema minahassae

Hyalosema minahassae, commonly known as the Minahassa hyacinth, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Indonesia.

It belongs to the Hyacinthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are slender and erect.

Hyalosema minahassae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
