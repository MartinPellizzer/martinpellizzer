Helianthemum croceum subsp. rothmaleri

Helianthemum croceum subsp.

rothmaleri, commonly known as the Rothmaler's sandthorn, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is a low-growing shrub with yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

The plant has small, leathery leaves and is adapted to dry, rocky environments. It is often found in coastal areas and sandy soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for xerophytic conditions and it is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
