Hypericum radfordiorum

Hypericum radfordiorum, commonly known as the Radford's St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Hypericaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces orange-red seed pods that split open to release its seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
