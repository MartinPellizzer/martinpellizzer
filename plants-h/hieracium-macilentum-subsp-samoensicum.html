Hieracium macilentum subsp. samoensicum

Hieracium macilentum subsp.

samoensicum, commonly known as the Samoan hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Islands. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a composite head. It has a fibrous root system and is adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

Its botanical structure reflects its evolutionary adaptation to the tropical and subtropical climates of its native range.
