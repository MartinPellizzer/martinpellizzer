Hybridella globosa var. globosa

Hybridella globosa var. globosa, commonly known as the globe lily, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, spherical flower heads that resemble small globes, typically ranging from white to pale yellow in color. The plant features slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticultural enthusiasts.
