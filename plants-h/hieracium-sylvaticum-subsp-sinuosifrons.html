Hieracium sylvaticum subsp. sinuosifrons

Hieracium sylvaticum subsp.

sinuosifrons, commonly known as the sinuous-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and basal rosettes of leaves.

The leaves are typically lobed and have a distinctive sinuous or wavy margin, giving the plant its subspecific name. The flowers are yellow and arranged in composite heads, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
