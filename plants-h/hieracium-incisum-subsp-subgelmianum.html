Hieracium incisum subsp. subgelmianum

Hieracium incisum subsp.

subgelmianum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands, rocky slopes, and wastelands.

The plant has basal and upright stems, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are yellow, with a distinctive central disc surrounded by ray florets, and it blooms from late spring to early autumn.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its finely divided leaves and showy inflorescence.
