Hymenophyllum berteroi

Hymenophyllum berteroi, commonly known as the silver fern, is a small, epiphytic fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically growing on rocks or tree trunks in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are typically narrow and linear, with a very short, spore-bearing structure called a sorus.

Hymenophyllum berteroi is often found in humid, shaded habitats and is valued for its ornamental beauty in horticulture.
