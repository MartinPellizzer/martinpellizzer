Hieracium furvulescens

Hieracium furvulescens, commonly known as the brown hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and meadows. It has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Hieracium furvulescens is often found in temperate regions of Europe and is considered a common weed in agricultural settings.
