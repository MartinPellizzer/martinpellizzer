Hypericum erectum var. lutchuense

Hypericum erectum var. lutchuense, commonly known as the Lutchuense St. John's Wort, is a variant of the St. John's Wort species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow flowers. The plant typically features opposite, simple, and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in moist woodland areas.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique geographic distribution and potential medicinal properties.
