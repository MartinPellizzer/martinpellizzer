Heliotropium microsalsoloides

Heliotropium microsalsoloides, commonly known as the saltbush heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically thrives in saline soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from pink to purple, often appearing in clusters at the ends of its branches. Its leaves are usually small, fleshy, and covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Heliotropium microsalsoloides is also notable for its ability to stabilize soils and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
