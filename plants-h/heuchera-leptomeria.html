Heuchera leptomeria

Heuchera leptomeria, commonly known as the narrow-leaved coral bells, is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and its clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant features narrow, dark green to reddish foliage that adds visual interest throughout the growing season. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Heuchera leptomeria is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
