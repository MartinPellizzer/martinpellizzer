Hieracium sublucidum

Hieracium sublucidum, commonly known as the pale hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped, rough to the touch, and often have a bluish or purplish hue.

The flowers are yellow, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets, and it produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.
