Hieracium pycnothyrsum

Hieracium pycnothyrsum, commonly known as the dense-hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are generally basal, with a rough, hairy texture, and the stems are often reddish in color.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing yellow petals that attract various pollinators.
