Hieracium olivaceum subsp. valerianifolium

Hieracium olivaceum subsp.

valerianifolium, commonly known as the Valerian-leaved Hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and basal rosette of leaves.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a distinctive, slightly hairy texture. This subspecies is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it a notable plant in ecological studies and botanical gardens.
