Helichrysum nervicinctum subsp. megistocephalum

Helichrysum nervicinctum subsp.

megistocephalum, commonly known as the Cape goldcup, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, yellow-orange inflorescences that resemble small cups, which are composed of numerous tiny, tubular flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for coastal and arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, and the plant is known for its drought tolerance.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
