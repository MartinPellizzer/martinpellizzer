Hyssopus officinalis subsp. borealis

Hyssopus officinalis subsp.

borealis, commonly known as northern hyssop, is a perennial herb native to northern regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces spikes of blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its aromatic leaves and historical use in herbal medicine.
