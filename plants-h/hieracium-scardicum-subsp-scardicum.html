Hieracium scardicum subsp. scardicum

Hieracium scardicum subsp.

scardicum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm.

The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, with a flowering stem that bears yellow composite flowers. Its inflorescence consists of a single head of disk flowers surrounded by pale yellow ray flowers.

The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, and it is often found in open, sunny habitats.
