Hippeastrum brasiliense

Hippeastrum brasiliense, commonly known as the Brazilian amaryllis, is a striking ornamental plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features broad, strap-like leaves that are typically green and emerge from a bulbous stem. Hippeastrum brasiliense thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to bright light for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and indoor cultivation.
