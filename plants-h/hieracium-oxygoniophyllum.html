Hieracium oxygoniophyllum

Hieracium oxygoniophyllum, commonly known as the blunt-leaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, broadly ovate to lanceolate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in dense, flat-topped clusters called capitula. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas.

Hieracium oxygoniophyllum is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in full sun.
