Hosta laevigata

Hosta laevigata, commonly known as the smooth-leaf hosta, is a perennial plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its broad, smooth, dark green leaves that are often slightly wavy in texture. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped white flowers in late summer. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Hosta laevigata is a popular choice for shady garden landscapes due to its ornamental foliage and ease of care.
