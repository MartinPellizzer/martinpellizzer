Hildegardia macrophylla

Hildegardia macrophylla, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to two meters in diameter, floating on the water's surface. The plant's showy white flowers, which resemble those of water lilies, bloom at night and emit a fragrant scent to attract pollinators. Its roots are submerged, anchoring the plant in shallow waters and absorbing nutrients from the sediment.

Hildegardia macrophylla plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen to water organisms.
