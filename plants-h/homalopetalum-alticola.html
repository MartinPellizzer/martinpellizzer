Homalopetalum alticola

Homalopetalum alticola, commonly known as the Sierra datura, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces capsule fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous coating.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often found in desert and mountainous regions.
