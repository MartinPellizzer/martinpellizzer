Helianthocereus pseudocandicans var. flaviflorus

Helianthocereus pseudocandicans var. flaviflorus, commonly known as the yellow-flowered night-blooming cactus, is a species native to southern Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its smooth, green stems and spines. The plant produces large, showy flowers that open at night, typically in shades of yellow or orange. These flowers emit a sweet fragrance to attract nocturnal pollinators like moths.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
