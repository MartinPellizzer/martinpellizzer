Helicia oblongifolia var. subpetiolata

Helicia oblongifolia var. subpetiolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved helicia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its fruits are small, round drupes that turn red when mature.

This species is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as mountain forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
