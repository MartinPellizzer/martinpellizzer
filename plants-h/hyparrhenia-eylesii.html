Hyparrhenia eylesii

Hyparrhenia eylesii, commonly known as the African grass or red grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that often have a reddish tint, especially when young. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Hyparrhenia eylesii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
