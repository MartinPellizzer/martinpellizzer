Hakea maconochieana

Hakea maconochieana, commonly known as the Macdonnell Range hakea, is a shrub native to Australia, particularly found in the arid regions of the Northern Territory.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like foliage and distinctive, cylindrical flower spikes that bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant is adapted to harsh, dry environments, with thick leaves that reduce water loss and deep roots that access groundwater. It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for native wildlife.

Hakea maconochieana is also valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
