Hiraea adenophora

Hiraea adenophora, commonly known as the sticky nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that produce a sticky substance, giving it its common name. It produces small, white or purple flowers and berries that are typically toxic to animals and humans.

Due to its invasive nature, it is often considered a weed in agricultural and natural settings.
