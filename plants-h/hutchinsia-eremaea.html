Hutchinsia eremaea

Hutchinsia eremaea, commonly known as the desert hutchinsia, is a perennial shrub native to arid regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with low rainfall.

Its growth form and foliage make it well-suited for xeriscaping and ecological restoration projects in semi-arid landscapes.
