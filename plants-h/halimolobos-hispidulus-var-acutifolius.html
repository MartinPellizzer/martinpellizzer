Halimolobos hispidulus var. acutifolius

Halimolobos hispidulus var. acutifolius, commonly known as the hairy halimolobos, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves with a pointed, acute shape, which gives it its specific epithet *acutifolius*. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open grasslands or along riverbanks.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
