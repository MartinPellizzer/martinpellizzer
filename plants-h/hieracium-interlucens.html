Hieracium interlucens

Hieracium interlucens, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring moist and sunny conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with prominent central disks.

Hieracium interlucens is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
