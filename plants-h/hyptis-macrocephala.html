Hyptis macrocephala

Hyptis macrocephala, commonly known as giant hyptis, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in dense, clustered inflorescences. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic and may contain essential oils. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Hyptis macrocephala is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses in some indigenous cultures.
