Hypoxis decumbens

Hypoxis decumbens, commonly known as the African potato, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, white waxy coating. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it adaptable to various garden environments.

The rhizomes of Hypoxis decumbens have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.
