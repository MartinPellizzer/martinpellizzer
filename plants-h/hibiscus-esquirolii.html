Hibiscus esquirolii

Hibiscus esquirolii, commonly known as the Esquiroli hibiscus, is a species native to Madagascar.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features large, showy flowers with five petals, often in shades of red, pink, or white. Its leaves are ovate and have a waxy texture, while its stems are covered in fine hairs.

Hibiscus esquirolii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
