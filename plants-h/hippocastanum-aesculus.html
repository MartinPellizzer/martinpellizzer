Hippocastanum aesculus

Hippocastanum aesculus, commonly known as the horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, white or pinkish flowers that appear in spring. The tree produces distinctive, spiny capsules containing glossy, chestnut-like seeds. Its bark is smooth and gray, with vertical fissures, and it can grow up to 25 meters tall.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine.
