Hibiscus purpureus

Hibiscus purpureus, commonly known as the purple hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from pink to deep purple. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a soft, velvety texture.

Hibiscus purpureus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
