Huperzia buesii

Huperzia buesii, commonly known as the Bues's huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, segmented stems and small, scale-like leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Central and South America. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Huperzia buesii is notable for its ability to form dense colonies, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
