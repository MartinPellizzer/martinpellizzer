Hieracium alatum subsp. neodoronicum

Hieracium alatum subsp.

neodoronicum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems.

The plant produces yellow disk flowers surrounded by pale yellow to brown ray florets, typically blooming from June to September. It thrives in moist meadows, pastures, and woodland edges, often forming dense colonies.

Due to its aggressive growth, it can become invasive in certain regions, impacting native plant communities.
