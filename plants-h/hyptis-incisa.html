Hyptis incisa

Hyptis incisa, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a perennial herb native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, blue-violet flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. Hyptis incisa is often found in moist, sunny environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
