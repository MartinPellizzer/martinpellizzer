Hildmannia mitis

Hildmannia mitis, commonly known as the softleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wet, swampy areas and along riverbanks. The plant features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually pink or red, with five petals and a prominent central column. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and covered in fine, soft hairs.

Hildmannia mitis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in moist environments.
