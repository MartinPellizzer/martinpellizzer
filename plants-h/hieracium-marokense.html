Hieracium marokense

Hieracium marokense, commonly known as Moroccan hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to North Africa, particularly Morocco.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and ragworts, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It features a basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
