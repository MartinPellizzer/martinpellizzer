Helianthemum guttatum var. lipopetalum

Helianthemum guttatum var. lipopetalum, commonly known as the pink-stemmed rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and showy pink to red flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are typically covered in fine, glandular hairs, giving them a slightly sticky texture. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in Mediterranean climates.
