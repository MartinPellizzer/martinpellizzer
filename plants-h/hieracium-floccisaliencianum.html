Hieracium floccisaliencianum

Hieracium floccisaliencianum, commonly known as the fluffier hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to certain regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces fluffy, white to pale yellow flower heads that are composed of numerous tiny florets. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features include serrated leaves and a basal rosette structure, making it identifiable in its native habitat.
