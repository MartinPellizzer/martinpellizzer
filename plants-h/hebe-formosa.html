Hebe formosa

Hebe formosa, commonly known as the Canterbury bells, is a perennial shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green and are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Hebe formosa is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
