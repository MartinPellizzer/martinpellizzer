Heuchera calycosa

Heuchera calycosa, commonly known as the calycosa alumroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, palmate leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from green to red, purple, and bronze. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale pink flowers in the spring, which are borne on slender stems. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in woodland habitats.

Heuchera calycosa is valued for its ornamental foliage and its role in supporting local pollinators.
