Hieracium neoserratifrons

Hieracium neoserratifrons, commonly known as the serrated hawkweed, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with serrated edges, contributing to its common name.

Hieracium neoserratifrons is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
