Hieracium ferrarii

Hieracium ferrarii, commonly known as the Ferrari's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in sunny, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It features upright stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters.

Hieracium ferrarii is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.
