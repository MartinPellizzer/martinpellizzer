Heteropogon villosus subvar. selloanus

Heteropogon villosus subvar. selloanus, commonly known as the Sello's hairy grass, is a grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy, sometimes pubescent leaves. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet-like cluster of small, two- to three-flowered spikelets.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological adaptability and is often found in disturbed or marginal habitats.
