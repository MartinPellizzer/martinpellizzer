Haemocharis tomentosa

Haemocharis tomentosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf arrowroot.

It is a tropical plant native to South America, characterized by its striking, waxy, green leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its foliage and floral structure make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in suitable climates.
