Hyssopus officinalis f. roseus

Hyssopus officinalis f. roseus, commonly known as rose hyssop, is a hybrid cultivar of the hyssop plant.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves with a slight fuzzy texture. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and mild, aromatic scent.

This variety is well-suited for rock gardens, borders, and containers due to its drought tolerance and ease of care.
