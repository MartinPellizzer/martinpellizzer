Hieracium picroides subsp. picroides

Hieracium picroides subsp.

picroides, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides, reaching heights of up to 50 centimeters.

The plant features upright, hairy stems with alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow, clustered in dense heads, and bloom from June to August.

It is characterized by its ability to form dense colonies, making it a common weed in disturbed soils.
