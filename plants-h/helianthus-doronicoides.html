Helianthus doronicoides

Helianthus doronicoides, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It features vibrant orange to red flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has rough, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with toothed margins. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
