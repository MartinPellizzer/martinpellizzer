Hieracium polytrichum

Hieracium polytrichum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are hairy and alternate, while the flower heads consist of both ray and disk flowers.

Hieracium polytrichum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
