Halophila major

Halophila major, commonly known as the seagrass species found in coastal regions, is a marine flowering plant belonging to the Alismataceae family.

It thrives in shallow, warm, tropical waters and is often found in sandy or muddy substrates. Its ability to tolerate high salinity makes it an important component of seagrass meadows in coastal ecosystems.
