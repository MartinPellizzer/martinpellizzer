Hieracium integrellum

Hieracium integrellum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It has hairy stems and produces seeds in a disc-like arrangement surrounded by ray florets.

Hieracium integrellum is a perennial plant that can spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in various habitats.
