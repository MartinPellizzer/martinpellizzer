Hymenophyllum lineare var. brasiliense

Hymenophyllum lineare var. brasiliense, commonly known as the Brazilian toothed moss, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is a small, delicate plant characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often toothed or serrated along the edges. This variety is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. Its leaves are typically a vibrant green and are covered with a fine layer of rhizomes that help anchor it to surfaces.

The plant is known for its ability to retain moisture, making it well adapted to its moist, tropical habitat.
