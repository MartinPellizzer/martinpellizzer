Hicorius glabra var. megacarpa

Hicorius glabra var. megacarpa, commonly known as the giant smooth senna, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces large, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates for its vibrant floral display.
