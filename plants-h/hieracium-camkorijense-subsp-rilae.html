Hieracium camkorijense subsp. rilae

Hieracium camkorijense subsp.

rilae, commonly known as the Rilae Hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the genus Hieracium. It thrives in rocky and dry habitats, often found in alpine and subalpine zones.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its range.
