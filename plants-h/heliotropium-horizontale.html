Heliotropium horizontale

Heliotropium horizontale, commonly known as the horizontal heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a low-growing herb with prostrate or ascending stems that often lie close to the ground. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, with five petals. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

Heliotropium horizontale is often found in moist, sandy soils and is adapted to thrive in coastal or disturbed habitats.
