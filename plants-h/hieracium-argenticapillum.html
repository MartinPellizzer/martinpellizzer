Hieracium argenticapillum

Hieracium argenticapillum, commonly known as silver-capped hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine white hairs, giving them a silvery appearance.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
