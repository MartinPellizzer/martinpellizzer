Heracleum thomsonii

Heracleum thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's cow parsley, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbellate inflorescence and aromatic foliage.

It is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Afghanistan and Pakistan. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
