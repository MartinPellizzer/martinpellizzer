Hyptis flabellata

Hyptis flabellata, commonly known as the blue hyptis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Hyptis flabellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
