Hieracium fastuosum subsp. trichopicris

Hieracium fastuosum subsp.

trichopicris, commonly known as the showy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with fine hairs, while its stems are upright and somewhat branched.

The subspecies trichopicris is distinguished by its hairy leaves and more robust growth compared to other members of the species.
