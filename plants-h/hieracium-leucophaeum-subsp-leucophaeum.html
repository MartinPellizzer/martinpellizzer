Hieracium leucophaeum subsp. leucophaeum

Hieracium leucophaeum subsp.

leucophaeum, commonly known as the white-haired hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy, often reddish stems.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The subspecies leucophaeum is notable for its pale, almost white, floral bracts that contrast with its yellow flower heads.
