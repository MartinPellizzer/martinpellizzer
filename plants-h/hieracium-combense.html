Hieracium combense

Hieracium combense, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces numerous small, clustered flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Hieracium combense is often considered a weed due to its rapid spread and ability to colonize various habitats.
