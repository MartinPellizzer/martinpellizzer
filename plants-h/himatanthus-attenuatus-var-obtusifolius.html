Himatanthus attenuatus var. obtusifolius

Himatanthus attenuatus var. obtusifolius, commonly known as the African snowball, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its clusters of white, waxy flowers that resemble small balls, which bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are slightly rounded at the ends, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates.

This variety is often cultivated for its unique appearance and ability to provide winter interest in gardens.
