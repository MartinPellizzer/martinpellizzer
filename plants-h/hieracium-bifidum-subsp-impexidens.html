Hieracium bifidum subsp. impexidens

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

impexidens, commonly known as the two-lobed hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrowly lance-shaped leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The species is native to Europe and is often found in grassy habitats and disturbed soils. It has small, hairy stems and produces numerous ray florets that bloom in the summer months.

This subspecies is typically short-lived and is considered a perennial herb.
