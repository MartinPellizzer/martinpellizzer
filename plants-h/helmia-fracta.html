Helmia fracta

Helmia fracta, commonly known as the fragrant helmiace, is a flowering plant belonging to the Helmiaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant features small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters along the stems. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a slightly waxy appearance.

Helmia fracta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique aromatic qualities.
