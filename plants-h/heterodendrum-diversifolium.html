Heterodendrum diversifolium

Heterodendrum diversifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf holly, is a species of flowering plant in the family Ericaceae.

It is native to the southeastern United States, where it thrives in acidic, well-drained soils. The plant features a shrub-like growth habit with alternate, elliptic to ovate leaves that are often pubescent. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in umbels, typical of the Ericaceae family.

The plant is adapted to woodland and rocky habitats, often forming dense thickets in its native range.
