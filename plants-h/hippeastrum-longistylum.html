Hippeastrum longistylum

Hippeastrum longistylum, commonly known as the Ecuadorian amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Ecuador.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant produces large, strap-like leaves that are typically broad and dark green. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires moderate to bright light for optimal growth.

Hippeastrum longistylum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is popular in tropical and subtropical gardens.
