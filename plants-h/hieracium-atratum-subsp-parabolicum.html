Hieracium atratum subsp. parabolicum

Hieracium atratum subsp.

parabolicum, commonly known as the black hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its dark green, hairy leaves and showy yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks. It has a rosette growth habit with basal leaves and produces a single, terminal flower head.

The species is noted for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its historical use in traditional medicine.
