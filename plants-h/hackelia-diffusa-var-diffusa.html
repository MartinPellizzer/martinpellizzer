Hackelia diffusa var. diffusa

Hackelia diffusa var. diffusa, commonly known as stickyweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It features upright stems covered in fine, sticky hairs, which give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland and rocky areas.
