Hyptis mutabilis var. spicata

Hyptis mutabilis var. spicata, commonly known as the blue hyptis, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and showy, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in dry to moist habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional herbal medicine.
