Hypericum montanum var. scaberulum

Hypericum montanum var. scaberulum, commonly known as rough-stemmed St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It is characterized by its woody stems that are covered in rough, glandular hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.
