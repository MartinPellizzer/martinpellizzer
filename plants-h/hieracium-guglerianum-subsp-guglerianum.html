Hieracium guglerianum subsp. guglerianum

Hieracium guglerianum subsp.

guglerianum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its upright, herbaceous growth habit and produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in mountainous environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is well adapted to cold climates and can survive in harsh alpine conditions.
