Hygrophila phlomoides

Hygrophila phlomoides, commonly known as water hyssop, is a semi-aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are lance-shaped with serrated edges. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in clusters.

It is often used in aquariums and as a decorative plant in water gardens.
