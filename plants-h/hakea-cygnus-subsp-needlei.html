Hakea cygnus subsp. needlei

Hakea cygnus subsp.

needlei, commonly known as the Needle's hakea, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in dense clusters.

The plant produces cylindrical, yellow-green flowers that bloom in spring, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it a notable species within its native habitat.
