Heliconiopsis

Heliconiopsis, commonly known as the flame flower, is a tropical plant belonging to the Heliconiaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy inflorescences that resemble torches, with bright red, orange, or yellow bracts. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are large, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

Heliconiopsis thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
