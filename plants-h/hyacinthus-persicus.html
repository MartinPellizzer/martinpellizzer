Hyacinthus persicus

Hyacinthus persicus, commonly known as the Persian hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces underground bulbs that store nutrients for the next growing season. Its flowers are often used in ornamental gardening for their vibrant color and pleasant scent.

The Persian hyacinth is also valued for its historical and cultural significance in horticulture.
