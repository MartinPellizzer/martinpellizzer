Halimione verrucifera

Halimione verrucifera, commonly known as the bladderwrack, is a perennial seaweed belonging to the family Fucaceae.

It is characterized by its dark brown, leathery fronds that are often covered in small, bladder-like structures called pneumatocysts. This plant thrives in intertidal zones, particularly in the northern Atlantic and Pacific Oceans, where it attaches itself to rocks. Its structure allows it to float and absorb nutrients from the water, contributing to coastal ecosystems.

Halimione verrucifera plays a vital role in marine environments by providing habitat and food for various marine organisms.
