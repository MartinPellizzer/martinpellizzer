Hieracium coronarium

Hieracium coronarium, commonly known as the crownflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in open areas such as meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stem is often hairy as well.

Hieracium coronarium is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
