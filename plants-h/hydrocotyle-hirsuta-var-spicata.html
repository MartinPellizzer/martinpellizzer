Hydrocotyle hirsuta var. spicata

Hydrocotyle hirsuta var. spicata, commonly known as the hairy water pennywort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland and aquatic environments.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy textures, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces tiny, white flowers that grow in clusters, typically above the water surface. It thrives in shallow, still or slow-moving freshwater habitats.

Its presence is often an indicator of healthy, undisturbed aquatic ecosystems.
