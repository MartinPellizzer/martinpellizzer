Hemianemia elegans

Hemianemia elegans, commonly known as the elegant hemianthus, is a species of aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, ribbon-like leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in length. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and streams, where it forms dense, carpet-like growths. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually borne on slender stems and are pollinated by insects.

Hemianemia elegans is often cultivated in aquariums due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
