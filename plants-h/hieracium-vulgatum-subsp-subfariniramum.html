Hieracium vulgatum subsp. subfariniramum

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

subfariniramum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed sites, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a slightly bitter taste, while its flowers are arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

It is often considered an invasive species in some regions due to its rapid spread and competitive growth.
