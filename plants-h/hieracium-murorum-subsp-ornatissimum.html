Hieracium murorum subsp. ornatissimum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

ornatissimum, commonly known as the showy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands, roadsides, and waste areas. It has alternate, rough-hairy leaves that are often lance-shaped, and its stems are erect and branched.

This subspecies is distinguished by its ornate, showy inflorescence, which contributes to its common name.
