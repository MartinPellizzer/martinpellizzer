Hieracium murorum subsp. orbicans

Hieracium murorum subsp.

orbicans, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems, which are typical features of the genus Hieracium.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other open areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
