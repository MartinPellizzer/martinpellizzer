Hieracium murorum subsp. atrocinereum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

atrocinereum, commonly known as the black-centred hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to orange flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dark purple or black central disc florets, which contrast with the lighter outer ray florets. It typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats such as meadows and roadsides.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are often glandular, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
