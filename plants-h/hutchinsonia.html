Hutchinsonia

Hutchinsonia, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers and thick, fleshy leaves. The plants typically grow as compact shrubs with a rosette of leaves that store water, adapting to arid environments. Hutchinsonia species are popular houseplants due to their ornamental value and ease of care.

They thrive in bright, indirect light and require well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
