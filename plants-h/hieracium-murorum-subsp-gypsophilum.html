Hieracium murorum subsp. gypsophilum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

gypsophilum, commonly known as the rock hawk's-beak, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in open habitats such as cliffs, slopes, and dry meadows.

The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, silvery-hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its inflorescence consists of a single, elongated flower with a distinctive beak-like structure.

This subspecies is characterized by its adaptability to harsh environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.
