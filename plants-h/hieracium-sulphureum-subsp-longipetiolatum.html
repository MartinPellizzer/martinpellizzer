Hieracium sulphureum subsp. longipetiolatum

Hieracium sulphureum subsp.

longipetiolatum, commonly known as the sulphur hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its yellowish-orange flowers, which bloom in early summer, and its basal rosette of leaves.

The leaves are typically long-petioled, hence the subspecific name, and are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

The plant is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
