Holoschoenus subarticulatus

Holoschoenus subarticulatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flax, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in coastal and wetland habitats, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
