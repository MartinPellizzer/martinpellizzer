Hieracium talariscodes

Hieracium talariscodes, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky soils, thriving in cool, high-altitude environments. Its leaves are generally lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often hairy.

The flowers bloom in late summer, producing yellowish-orange petals that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
