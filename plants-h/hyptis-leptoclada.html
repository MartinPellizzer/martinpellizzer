Hyptis leptoclada

Hyptis leptoclada, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a perennial shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

Hyptis leptoclada is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
