Hirculus peplidifolius

Hirculus peplidifolius, commonly known as the silverleaf hirculus, is a unique flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-green, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in dry conditions.

Hirculus peplidifolius is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
