Hieracium brevifolioides

Hieracium brevifolioides, commonly known as the short-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and have a distinctive basal rosette arrangement.

The flowers are yellow, with ray florets and a central disk, making it a common sight in early summer.
