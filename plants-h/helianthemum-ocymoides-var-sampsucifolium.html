Helianthemum ocymoides var. sampsucifolium

Helianthemum ocymoides var. sampsucifolium, commonly known as the redleaf sandthrift, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to southern Europe.

It features small, fleshy leaves that are typically reddish or purplish in color, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or Mediterranean climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
