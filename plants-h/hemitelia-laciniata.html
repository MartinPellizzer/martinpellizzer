Hemitelia laciniata

Hemitelia laciniata, commonly known as the lace-leaf hemitelia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate, lace-like appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, often with a slight fragrance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, commonly found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Hemitelia laciniata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
