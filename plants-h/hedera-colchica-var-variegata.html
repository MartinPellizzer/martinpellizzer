Hedera colchica var. variegata

Hedera colchica var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated Persian ivy, is a climbing evergreen vine native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves with striking white and yellow variegation. This plant thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
