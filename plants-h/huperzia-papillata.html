Huperzia papillata

Huperzia papillata, commonly known as the hairy huperzia, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its small, clustered growth habit and its distinctive, hairy appearance on the fronds. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments. The fronds are typically simple and have a leathery texture, with spores produced on the underside.

It is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
