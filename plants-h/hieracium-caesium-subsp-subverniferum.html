Hieracium caesium subsp. subverniferum

Hieracium caesium subsp.

subverniferum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 50 centimeters.

The plant features basal leaves that are broadly lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while the upper leaves are more narrow. Its flowers are yellow, with a distinctive central disc surrounded by ray florets, and it blooms from June to August.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
