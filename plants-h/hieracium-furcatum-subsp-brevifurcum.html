Hieracium furcatum subsp. brevifurcum

Hieracium furcatum subsp.

brevifurcum, commonly known as the short-horned hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering heads that produce yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has hairy stems and leaves with a distinctive lobed or toothed margin.

The subspecies brevifurcum is distinguished by its shorter, more compact inflorescence compared to other subspecies of Hieracium furcatum.
