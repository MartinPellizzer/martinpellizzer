Helichrysum paniculatum var. intermedium

Helichrysum paniculatum var. intermedium, commonly known as the golden marguerite, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that resemble daisies. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its foliage is aromatic and often used in traditional medicine.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
