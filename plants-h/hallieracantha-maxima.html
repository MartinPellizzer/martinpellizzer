Hallieracantha maxima

Hallieracantha maxima, commonly known as the giant bellflower, is a striking plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy bell-shaped flowers that can reach up to 15 centimeters in length. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of broad, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating.

Hallieracantha maxima is a perennial herb that is prized for its ornamental value in botanical gardens and collections.
