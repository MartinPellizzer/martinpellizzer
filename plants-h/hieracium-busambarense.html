Hieracium busambarense

Hieracium busambarense, commonly known as the Busambare hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of East Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain insects.
