Hieracium pseudoplicatum

Hieracium pseudoplicatum, commonly known as the false pearly everlasting, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers and hairy, often lobed leaves. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant's stems are usually erect and can grow up to 30 centimeters tall.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the hawkweed genus, with a preference for well-drained soils and full sunlight.
