Homalomena sarawakensis

Homalomena sarawakensis, commonly known as the Sarawak arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by bright red berries, which are edible. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its rhizomes are valued for their starchy content and have been used in local cuisines.
