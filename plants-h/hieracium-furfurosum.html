Hieracium furfurosum

Hieracium furfurosum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in the summer, and the leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture.

It is known for its ability to spread rapidly through rhizomes, making it an invasive species in some regions.
