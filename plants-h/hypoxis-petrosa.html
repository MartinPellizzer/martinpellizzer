Hypoxis petrosa

Hypoxis petrosa, commonly known as the star-shaped false shamrock, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of star-shaped yellow flowers. The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun conditions. It is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments.

Hypoxis petrosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
