Hylotelephium ewersii

Hylotelephium ewersii, commonly known as the Ewers' stonecrop, is a perennial succulent native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features fleshy, upright stems covered in dense, woolly hairs, and its leaves are typically blue-green in color, with a slightly wrinkled texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers during the summer months, which are arranged in a compact inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Hylotelephium ewersii is also known for its hardiness and ability to tolerate cold temperatures.
