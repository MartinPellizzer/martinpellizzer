Helianthus glaucophyllus

Helianthus glaucophyllus, commonly known as the silverleaf sunflower, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial with upright stems and large, showy yellow flowers surrounded by green bracts. The leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and covered in a dense layer of fine, silvery-white hairs, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
