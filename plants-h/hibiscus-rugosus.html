Hibiscus rugosus

Hibiscus rugosus, commonly known as the rough hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to deep pink or red. The plant has rough, hairy leaves and sturdy, woody stems, which help it thrive in humid environments. Hibiscus rugosus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
