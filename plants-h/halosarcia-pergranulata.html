Halosarcia pergranulata

Halosarcia pergranulata, commonly known as the granulated halosarcia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. Its leaves are typically reduced to small, scale-like structures, a common adaptation in succulents to minimize water loss.

Halosarcia pergranulata is often found growing in rocky soils and is well-suited for cultivation in dry, well-drained gardens.
