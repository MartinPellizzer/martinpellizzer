Hemionitis recurvata

Hemionitis recurvata, commonly known as the rock fern, is a unique plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and its ability to thrive in harsh, rocky environments. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss. It produces sporangia that are clustered in strobila, typical of ferns, and it reproduces through spores rather than seeds.

Hemionitis recurvata is well adapted to extreme conditions, making it an important component of desert ecosystems.
