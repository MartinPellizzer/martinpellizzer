Humata mutata

Humata mutata, commonly known as the variable hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can vary in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical environments and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often with a slightly rough texture.

Humata mutata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
