Haloragis trifida

Haloragis trifida, commonly known as the narrowleaf waterwheel, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive whorled arrangement of narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in wet or damp environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along waterways.

Haloragis trifida is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
