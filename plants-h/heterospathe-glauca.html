Heterospathe glauca

Heterospathe glauca, commonly known as the blue spathephyllum, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a vibrant touch to its foliage. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating, giving them a glossy appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
