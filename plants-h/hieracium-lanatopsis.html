Hieracium lanatopsis

Hieracium lanatopsis, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a hairy, upright stem and basal leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow or brown, arranged in a disk-like structure with surrounding ray florets.

Hieracium lanatopsis thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and is often found in temperate climates.
