Haplophyllum affine

Haplophyllum affine, commonly known as the narrow-leafed hollyhock, is a member of the Malvaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that are clustered in racemes, blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Haplophyllum affine is native to regions of Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
