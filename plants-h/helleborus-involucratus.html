Helleborus involucratus

Helleborus involucratus, commonly known as the Christmas rose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It features striking dark green, glossy leaves that are heart-shaped and often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Helleborus involucratus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
