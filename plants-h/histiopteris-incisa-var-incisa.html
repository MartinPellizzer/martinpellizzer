Histiopteris incisa var. incisa

Histiopteris incisa var. incisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water fern, is a variant of the water fern species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a fern that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors. The plant has delicate, feather-like fronds that are often divided into narrow, serrated segments. Its rhizomes are submerged or partially submerged, anchoring it in aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appearance and its role in aquatic ecosystems.
