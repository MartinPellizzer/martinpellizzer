Hydrangea sinensis

Hydrangea sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flower clusters that can range in color from blue to pink, depending on soil pH. The plant produces white or pale pink bracts that surround the small, true flowers. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Hydrangea sinensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
