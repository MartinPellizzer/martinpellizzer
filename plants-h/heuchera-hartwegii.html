Heuchera hartwegii

Heuchera hartwegii, commonly known as Hartweg's coral bells, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, matted foliage that ranges from deep red to purple and green hues. The plant produces upright spikes of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and red during the summer months. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant foliage and delicate blooms.
