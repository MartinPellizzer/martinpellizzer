Hydrangea seemannii

Hydrangea seemannii, commonly known as the Japanese hydrangea, is a species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range from white to pink, blue, or purple depending on soil pH. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with broad, ovate leaves that are dark green in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Hydrangea seemannii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its seasonal floral displays.
