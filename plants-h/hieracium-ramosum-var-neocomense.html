Hieracium ramosum var. neocomense

Hieracium ramosum var. neocomense, commonly known as the Neocomense hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and clustered, yellowish to reddish flower heads. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as meadows and woodland edges. It has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, bracteate flowers in the summer.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and sometimes endangered species in certain regions.
