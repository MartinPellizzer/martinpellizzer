Hutchinsonia cymigera

Hutchinsonia cymigera, commonly known as the mountain hutchinsonia, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and often aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

The flowers are small and clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences, giving the plant its species name "cymigera," derived from the Greek words for "cluster" and "branch.".
