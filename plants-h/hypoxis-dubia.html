Hypoxis dubia

Hypoxis dubia, commonly known as the yellow star grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Hypoxidaceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, typically arranged in a cluster at the center of the rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Hypoxis dubia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
