Helichrysum rupestre var. melitense

Helichrysum rupestre var. melitense, commonly known as the Maltese everlasting or Maltese helichrysum, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compact, silver-gray foliage and produces small, yellow-orange flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their long-lasting nature.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
