Himantoglossum jankae subsp. calcaratum

Himantoglossum jankae subsp.

calcaratum, commonly known as the Carpathian helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Carpathian Mountains. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, hooded flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color.

The plant has rhizomatous growth and produces showy, spurred flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and rocky slopes, and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and protected species in many regions.
