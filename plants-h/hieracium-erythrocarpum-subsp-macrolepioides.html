Hieracium erythrocarpum subsp. macrolepioides

Hieracium erythrocarpum subsp.

macrolepioides, commonly known as the red-fruited hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow to orange composite flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the fruit is a small, hairy achene.

It plays a role in supporting pollinators and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
