Hordeum vilmorianum

Hordeum vilmorianum, commonly known as the Vilmoriana barley, is a wild grass species native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences that are typically greenish or brownish in color. Hordeum vilmorianum thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open fields, along roadsides, and in ruderal habitats.

It is of interest to botanists due to its ecological adaptability and potential for genetic research.
