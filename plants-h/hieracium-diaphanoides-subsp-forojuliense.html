Hieracium diaphanoides subsp. forojuliense

Hieracium diaphanoides subsp.

forojuliense, commonly known as the transparent hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer.

The leaves are typically alternate, with a rough texture and a slightly lobed shape. This subspecies is native to the mountainous regions of southeastern Europe, where it thrives in rocky and dry habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and sometimes endangered plant species.
