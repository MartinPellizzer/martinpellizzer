Hedyotis corymbosa var. caespitosa

Hedyotis corymbosa var. caespitosa, commonly known as the creeping bluemink, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically forms dense mats of foliage. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, elliptic to ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks.
