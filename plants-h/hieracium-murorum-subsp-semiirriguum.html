Hieracium murorum subsp. semiirriguum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

semiirriguum, commonly known as the semi-irrigated hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and somewhat woody at the base.

It is a common weed in agricultural areas and can form dense stands in suitable conditions.
