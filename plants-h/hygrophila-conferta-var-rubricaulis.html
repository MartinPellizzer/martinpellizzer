Hygrophila conferta var. rubricaulis

Hygrophila conferta var. rubricaulis, commonly known as the red-stemmed hygrophila, is a striking aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, bushy growth habit and vibrant red stems that contrast beautifully with its narrow, dark green leaves. This variety thrives in slow to moderately flowing freshwater environments, making it a popular choice for aquariums and ponds. Its foliage is often used in aquascaping to create a lush, natural appearance.

The plant is also valued for its ability to provide shelter for aquatic life and improve water clarity through biological filtration.
