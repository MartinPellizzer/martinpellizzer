Holoschoenus

Holoschoenus, commonly known as the swamp wallaby grass, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family, Poaceae.

It is native to Australia and is typically found in wetland and swampy environments. The plants are characterized by their tufted growth habit and grass-like foliage. Holoschoenus species often have narrow, erect leaves and produce small, spike-like inflorescences.
