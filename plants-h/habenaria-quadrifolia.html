Habenaria quadrifolia

Habenaria quadrifolia, commonly known as the four-leaf orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and is characterized by its delicate, greenish-white flowers. The plant has a slender stem with several leaves, and its flowers are arranged in a loose spike. It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is noted for its small, fragrant blooms.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered threatened in some regions and is often protected in conservation efforts.
