Hieracium angustifrons

Hieracium angustifrons, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture, and it produces yellow flowers in clusters.

Hieracium angustifrons is often found in open habitats and can spread vegetatively through rhizomes.
