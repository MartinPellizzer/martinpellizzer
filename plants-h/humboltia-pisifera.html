Humboltia pisifera

Humboltia pisifera, commonly known as the sea hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in saline and coastal environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind and water.

Humboltia pisifera is well adapted to withstand salt spray and strong coastal winds.
