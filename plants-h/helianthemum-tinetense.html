Helianthemum tinetense

Helianthemum tinetense, commonly known as the Canary Islands rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a low-growing shrub with woody stems and small, fragrant yellow flowers. The plant thrives in rocky and coastal environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh, sunny conditions. Its leaves are typically gray-green, covered in a fine layer of white hairs that help reduce water loss.

Helianthemum tinetense plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting local insect populations and contributing to the region's biodiversity.
