Hibiscus elongatifolius

Hibiscus elongatifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved hibiscus, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming during the wet season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Hibiscus elongatifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
