Homalanthus fastuosus

Homalanthus fastuosus, commonly known as the Hawaiian beauty leaf, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy bracts that resemble petals. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a vivid display of red, orange, or yellow hues.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in tropical gardens but is considered invasive in some regions outside its native habitat.
