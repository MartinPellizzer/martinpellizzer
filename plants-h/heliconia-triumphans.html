Heliconia triumphans

Heliconia triumphans, commonly known as the red heliconia, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, vivid red bracts that surround a cluster of small, yellowish flowers. The plant belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower. Its leaves are large, broad, and lance-shaped, contributing to its lush appearance.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to flourish.
