Humata sessilifolia

Humata sessilifolia, commonly known as the sessile-leaf humata, is a perennial herb native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, clustered leaves that are sessile, meaning they lack stalks. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale green, often arranged in clusters. Its foliage is aromatic, with a subtle, mint-like scent, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its unique appearance and fragrance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
