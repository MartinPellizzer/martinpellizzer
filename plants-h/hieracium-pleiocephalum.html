Hieracium pleiocephalum

Hieracium pleiocephalum, commonly known as the showy hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges, thriving in well-drained soil. It has a rosette of basal leaves and a flowering stem that can reach up to 40 cm in height.

Hieracium pleiocephalum is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in disturbed habitats.
