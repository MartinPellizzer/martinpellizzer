Helianthemum rotundifolium subsp. rotundifolium

Helianthemum rotundifolium subsp.

rotundifolium, commonly known as the round-leaved rockrose, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, typically arranged in clusters along the stems.

The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that are rounded and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for Mediterranean climates.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and ornamental plant often used in rock gardens.
