Hieracium sylvaticum subsp. lackschewitzii

Hieracium sylvaticum subsp.

lackschewitzii, commonly known as the forest hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It is known for its ability to thrive in shaded environments, making it a common sight in forest understories.

This subspecies is distinguished by its slightly smaller flowers and more compact growth compared to other members of the Hieracium genus.
