Herniaria maskatensis

Herniaria maskatensis, commonly known as the desert carpetweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, pale green, fleshy leaves and produces tiny, white or pink flowers that bloom in summer. It is well adapted to harsh environments, with thick, succulent tissues that store water.

Herniaria maskatensis is often found in rocky soils and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
