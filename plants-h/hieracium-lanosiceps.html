Hieracium lanosiceps

Hieracium lanosiceps, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy inflorescences. The plant features a robust root system and upright, hairy stems that can grow up to 60 cm in height. Its leaves are lance-shaped, rough to the touch, and covered in fine, white hairs.

The flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in a flat-topped cluster, and bloom from late spring to early autumn.
