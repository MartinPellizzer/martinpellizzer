Hibiscus bancroftianus

Hibiscus bancroftianus, commonly known as the white hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically green and glossy. It thrives in humid environments and requires well-drained soil.

Hibiscus bancroftianus is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance.
