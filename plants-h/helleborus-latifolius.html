Helleborus latifolius

Helleborus latifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features dark green, broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with white or purple veins. The plant produces clusters of pale green to white flowers with prominent, showy spathes during late winter to early spring. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is adapted to calcareous soils.

Helleborus latifolius is noted for its toxic properties, containing various alkaloids that can be harmful if ingested.
