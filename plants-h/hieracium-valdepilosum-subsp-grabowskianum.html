Hieracium valdepilosum subsp. grabowskianum

Hieracium valdepilosum subsp.

grabowskianum, commonly known as the Grabowskian hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. It has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered with fine pubescence.

The subspecies is distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and subtle morphological differences from other members of the *Hieracium* genus.
