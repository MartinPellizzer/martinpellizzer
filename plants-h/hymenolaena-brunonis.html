Hymenolaena brunonis

Hymenolaena brunonis, commonly known as the silverleaf lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its small, spike-like inflorescence produces numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Hymenolaena brunonis is often used in ornamental gardening for its drought tolerance and aesthetic appeal.
