Hieracium nestleri

Hieracium nestleri, commonly known as the Nestler's hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, dry conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

Hieracium nestleri is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
