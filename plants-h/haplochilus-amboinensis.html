Haplochilus amboinensis

Haplochilus amboinensis, commonly known as the Amboina spider orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, spider-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its flowers are usually small and fragrant, with a unique structure that aids in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its natural range.
