Hesperastragalus oxyrhynchus

Hesperastragalus oxyrhynchus, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
