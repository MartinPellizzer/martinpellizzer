Hibiscus divaricatus var. genuinus

Hibiscus divaricatus var. genuinus, commonly known as the scarlet hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from red to pink and orange. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and the stems are often hairy.

Hibiscus divaricatus var. genuinus thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.
