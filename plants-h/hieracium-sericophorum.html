Hieracium sericophorum

Hieracium sericophorum, commonly known as the silky hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant is known for its finely hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, velvety appearance. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies.

The flowers are yellow and bloom from late spring to early autumn, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
