Horsfieldia irya

Horsfieldia irya, commonly known as the white orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its showy, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments, utilizing its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients. Its leaves are usually elongated and arranged in a rosette pattern, facilitating water retention.

Horsfieldia irya is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity in its native habitats.
