Hieracium praecox var. foetidum

Hieracium praecox var. foetidum, commonly known as the stinking hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are basal, rough, and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a strong, unpleasant odor.

The species is known for its distinctive scent, which is thought to attract certain pollinators.
