Hamosa montereyensis

Hamosa montereyensis, commonly known as the Monterey cypress, is a coniferous tree native to the coastal regions of central California.

It is characterized by its slender, columnar form and scale-like leaves that are tightly pressed against its branches. The tree produces small, woody cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in coastal climates with mild temperatures and moderate rainfall.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and landscapes for its ornamental value.
