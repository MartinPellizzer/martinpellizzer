Habenaria schlechteri

Habenaria schlechteri, commonly known as the Schlechter's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are fibrous, and it produces small, greenish-white or pale yellow flowers with three sepals and three petals.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
