Houstonia minor var. pusilla

Houstonia minor var. pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf blue-eyed grass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features delicate, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, sky-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically reaches a height of only a few inches, making it ideal for rock gardens or woodland settings. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

This variety is a member of the gentian family (Gentianaceae) and is characterized by its compact growth habit and ornamental value.
