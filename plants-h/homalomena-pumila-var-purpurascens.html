Homalomena pumila var. purpurascens

Homalomena pumila var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-leafed caladium, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that display vibrant shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or yellow flowers that appear in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

This variety is popular as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
