Hieracium bauhini var. viscidulum

Hieracium bauhini var. viscidulum, commonly known as sticky hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in composite flower heads, which are typical of the daisy family. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, streambanks, and woodland edges.

Due to its sticky secretions, it is sometimes referred to as the "sticky weed.".
