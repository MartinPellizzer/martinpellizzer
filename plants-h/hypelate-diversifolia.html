Hypelate diversifolia

Hypelate diversifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf forest plant, is a species native to tropical rainforests.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves that vary in shape and size across different parts of the plant. The plant typically grows as an understory species, thriving in shaded environments with high humidity. Its flowers are small and clustered, often producing a fragrant scent to attract pollinators.

Hypelate diversifolia plays a vital role in its ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
