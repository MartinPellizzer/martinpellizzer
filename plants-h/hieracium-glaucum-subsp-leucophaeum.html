Hieracium glaucum subsp. leucophaeum

Hieracium glaucum subsp.

leucophaeum, commonly known as the white-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces yellow flowers in a single terminal capitulum.

The plant is typically found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows. It has a fibrous root system and is often invasive in certain regions.

Its botanical features make it distinguishable from other hawkweed species by its pale foliage and specific floral structure.
