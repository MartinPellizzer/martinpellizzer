Hieracium pseudocirritum

Hieracium pseudocirritum, commonly known as the false sand everlasting, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers with ray florets. It is known for its long-lasting bloom, which can persist for several weeks, making it a popular choice for dried flower arrangements.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural studies.
