Hierochloe alpina var. submutica

Hierochloe alpina var. submutica, commonly known as the Alpine hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, hairy leaves that are often covered with a dense layer of short, soft hairs. The plant typically thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils at high elevations. Its inflorescence consists of a narrow, spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing alpine soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
