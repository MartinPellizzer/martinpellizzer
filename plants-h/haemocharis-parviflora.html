Haemocharis parviflora

Haemocharis parviflora, commonly known as the pink shower plant, is a tropical flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or red in color. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant blooms that resemble tiny showers, hence its common name. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Haemocharis parviflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
