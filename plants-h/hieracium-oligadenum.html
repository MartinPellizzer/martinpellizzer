Hieracium oligadenum

Hieracium oligadenum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Hieracium oligadenum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
