Hieracium convergentipes

Hieracium convergentipes, commonly known as the convergent hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a cluster of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and disturbed soils. It has hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
