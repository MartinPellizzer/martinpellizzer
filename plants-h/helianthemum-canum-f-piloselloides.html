Helianthemum canum f. piloselloides

Helianthemum canum f. piloselloides, commonly known as the woolly rockrose, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compact, mat-forming growth with small, fleshy, blue-green leaves covered in a dense layer of white to grayish hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months, adding a bright contrast to its foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This cultivar is valued for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal in arid environments.
