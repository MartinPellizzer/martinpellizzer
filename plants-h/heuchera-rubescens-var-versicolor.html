Heuchera rubescens var. versicolor

Heuchera rubescens var. versicolor, commonly known as the red-leaf heuchera, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant, variegated foliage that ranges from deep red to bronze and green. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white during the summer months. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value lies in its striking leaves and long blooming period.
