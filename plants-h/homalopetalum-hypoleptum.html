Homalopetalum hypoleptum

Homalopetalum hypoleptum, commonly known as the pink coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to lavender petals and a distinctive, conical flower cluster. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Homalopetalum hypoleptum is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in xeriscaped gardens.
