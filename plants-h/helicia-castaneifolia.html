Helicia castaneifolia

Helicia castaneifolia, commonly known as the Chinese holly or holly tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Theaceae.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, where it grows in humid, temperate forests. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape. It produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, followed by spiny, orange-red fruits that are edible for some bird species.

Helicia castaneifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
