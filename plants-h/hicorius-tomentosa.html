Hicorius tomentosa

Hicorius tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often covered in a dense layer of fine, white pubescence.

Hicorius tomentosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
