Hapalochilus kermesinus

Hapalochilus kermesinus, commonly known as the Kermesinus lichen, is a unique lichen species that exhibits a symbiotic relationship between a fungus and a green alga or cyanobacterium.

It typically grows on bark, stone, or soil surfaces, forming dense, crust-like colonies that can vary in color from green to brown. The plant lacks true roots, stems, and leaves, relying instead on its symbiotic partners for nutrients and photosynthesis. It is often found in temperate and tropical regions, thriving in humid environments with adequate light.

Due to its slow growth and specialized habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant organism.
