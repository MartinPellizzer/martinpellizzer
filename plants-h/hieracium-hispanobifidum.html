Hieracium hispanobifidum

Hieracium hispanobifidum, commonly known as the Spanish two-lobed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped with hairy surfaces, and the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is often found in Mediterranean climates and is considered a minor weed in some agricultural settings.
