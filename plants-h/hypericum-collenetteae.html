Hypericum collenetteae

Hypericum collenetteae, commonly known as the Collenette's St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the family Hypericaceae and is characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are opposite and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and branched.

Hypericum collenetteae is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological niche and potential medicinal properties.
