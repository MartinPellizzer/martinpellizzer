Harmsiopanax ingens

Harmsiopanax ingens, commonly known as the giant arrowleaf, is a remarkable plant native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It is characterized by its massive size, with some specimens reaching heights of over 50 meters, making it one of the tallest flowering plants in the world. The plant features a single, unbranched stem and large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 3 meters in length. Its leaves are thick and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in the humid, tropical environment.

Harmsiopanax ingens plays a crucial role in its ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various species of animals.
