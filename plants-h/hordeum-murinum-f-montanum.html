Hordeum murinum f. montanum

Hordeum murinum f. montanum, commonly known as the mountain wild barley, is a grass species native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

This subspecies is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological adaptability and potential role in plant succession in disturbed environments.
