Hieracium pseudopubescens

Hieracium pseudopubescens, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soil. Its leaves are hairy and grayish-green, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is often considered a weed but has ecological value in supporting pollinators.
