Hymenaea reticulata

Hymenaea reticulata, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree has a straight trunk and a rounded crown, with compound leaves that are alternate and pinnate. Its flowers are small and yellowish, and it produces woody pods containing edible seeds.

The plant is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it an important species in its native ecosystems.
