Haloragis

Haloragis, commonly known as the "sea spurge" or "haloaragon," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Haloragaceae.

These plants are typically found in coastal and saline environments, showcasing their adaptability to harsh conditions. They are characterized by their succulent leaves and small, often yellow or greenish flowers. Many species are used in horticulture for their ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soils.

The genus includes both annual and perennial species, with some being invasive in certain regions.
