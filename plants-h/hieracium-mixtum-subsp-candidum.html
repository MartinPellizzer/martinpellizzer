Hieracium mixtum subsp. candidum

Hieracium mixtum subsp.

candidum, commonly known as the white hawk's beard, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow to orange flowers with white centers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are hairy and alternate, with a serrated edge, and it produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

This subspecies is often found in open habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
