Hieracium prediliense subsp. crucimontis

Hieracium prediliense subsp.

crucimontis, commonly known as the Alpine Groundsel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust root system.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it withstands harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rough texture and a distinctive silvery-blue hue.

The flowers, which bloom in late summer, are yellow and arranged in a flat, disc-like structure typical of the genus Hieracium.
