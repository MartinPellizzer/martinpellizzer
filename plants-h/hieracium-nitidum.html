Hieracium nitidum

Hieracium nitidum, commonly known as the shining hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular florets.

Hieracium nitidum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, where it thrives in full sun to partial shade.
