Heliotropium carpentariae

Heliotropium carpentariae, commonly known as the Christmas flower, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces small, violet-colored flowers arranged in clusters. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are opposite and lance-shaped. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Its blooming period typically occurs during the winter months, giving it its common name.
