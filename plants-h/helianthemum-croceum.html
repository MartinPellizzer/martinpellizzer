Helianthemum croceum

Helianthemum croceum, commonly known as the yellow rockrose, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are evergreen, leathery, and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

Helianthemum croceum thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.
