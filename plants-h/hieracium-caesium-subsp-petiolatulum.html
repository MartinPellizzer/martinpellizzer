Hieracium caesium subsp. petiolatulum

Hieracium caesium subsp.

petiolatulum, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flower heads composed of numerous small florets.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring temperate climates. It is native to Europe and has been introduced to other regions, often becoming invasive in some areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and horticultural interest.
