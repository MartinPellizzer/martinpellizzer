Hieracium eubalium

Hieracium eubalium, commonly known as the Balkan hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

Hieracium eubalium is often found in mountainous regions and is adapted to thrive in temperate climates.
