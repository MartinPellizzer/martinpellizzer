Hieracium aracioides

Hieracium aracioides, commonly known as the giant hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disk.

Hieracium aracioides is known for its aggressive growth and has been introduced to other regions, sometimes becoming invasive.
