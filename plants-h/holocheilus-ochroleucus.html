Holocheilus ochroleucus

Holocheilus ochroleucus, commonly known as the golden-leafed orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow and green foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of white and pale yellow. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered and is protected in several countries.
