Hypochaeris angustifolia f. setulifera

Hypochaeris angustifolia f. setulifera, commonly known as the slenderleaf common groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows. Its inflorescence consists of a single central flower surrounded by ray florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

The subspecies f. setulifera is distinguished by its finely hairy stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance.
