Hieracium lachenalii subsp. subirriguifrons

Hieracium lachenalii subsp.

subirriguifrons, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, demonstrating a preference for well-drained and sunny environments.

Its morphology and habitat make it an important component of alpine ecosystems.
