Hieracium chevrolatii

Hieracium chevrolatii, commonly known as the Chevrolat's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators.

Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
