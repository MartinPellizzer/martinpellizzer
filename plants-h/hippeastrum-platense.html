Hippeastrum platense

Hippeastrum platense, commonly known as the Brazilian rain lily, is a bulbous perennial plant native to South America.

It features striking, showy flowers that are typically white or pink with a red or purple throat. The plant produces a cluster of strap-shaped leaves that are often variegated with green and white patterns. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Hippeastrum platense is well-suited for tropical and subtropical climates, making it a popular choice for gardeners in those regions.
