Heliotropium steudneri var. oppositifolium

Heliotropium steudneri var. oppositifolium, commonly known as the opposite-leaf heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its opposite leaf arrangement, where leaves grow in pairs along the stem. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from pink to purple, often resembling those of the common heliotrope. Its stems are typically hairy, and the leaves are usually lance-shaped with a rough texture.

This variety is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of light conditions.
