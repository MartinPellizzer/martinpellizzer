Hieracium wolczankense

Hieracium wolczankense, commonly known as the Wolczanki hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium wolczankense is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain habitats.
