Hesperis matronalis subsp. oblongipetala

Hesperis matronalis subsp.

oblongipetala, commonly known as the oblong-petaled sweet rocket, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its elongated, lance-shaped petals and a compact, upright growth habit. It typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of fragrant, pale yellow to pink flowers.

This plant prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and borders.
