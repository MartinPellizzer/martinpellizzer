Hyoseris radiata var. pusilla

Hyoseris radiata var. pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf radiate hyoseris, is a small flowering plant native to regions of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces small, yellowish to brownish composite flowers. It is often found in rocky or grassy habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Due to its compact size, it is suitable for rock gardens and container planting.
