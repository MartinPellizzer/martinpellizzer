Hiraea ternifolia var. eglandulosa

Hiraea ternifolia var. eglandulosa, commonly known as the glandular hiraea, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet having a glandular surface that secretes a sticky substance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its unique glandular features make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
