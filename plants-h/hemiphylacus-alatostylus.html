Hemiphylacus alatostylus

Hemiphylacus alatostylus, commonly known as the slender-stemmed orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and delicate, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in loose clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
