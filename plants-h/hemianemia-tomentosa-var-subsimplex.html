Hemianemia tomentosa var. subsimplex

Hemianemia tomentosa var. subsimplex, commonly known as the woolly hemianthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that covers the stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its unique texture and foliage.
