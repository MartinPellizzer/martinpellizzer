Heliotropium longiflorum subsp. longiflorum

Heliotropium longiflorum subsp.

longiflorum, commonly known as the long-flowered heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is a herbaceous perennial with opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
