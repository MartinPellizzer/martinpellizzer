Hieracium lepidotum subsp. caligatum

Hieracium lepidotum subsp.

caligatum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, grassy habitats and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
