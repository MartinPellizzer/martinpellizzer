Hyacinthus peruvianus

Hyacinthus peruvianus, commonly known as the Peruvian hyacinth, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a bulbous perennial that produces clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is valued for its ornamental beauty in gardens. It thrives in cool, moist conditions and requires well-drained soil.

Hyacinthus peruvianus is also noted for its ability to naturalize and spread easily in suitable climates.
