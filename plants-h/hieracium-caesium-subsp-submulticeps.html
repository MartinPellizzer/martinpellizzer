Hieracium caesium subsp. submulticeps

Hieracium caesium subsp.

submulticeps, commonly known as the clustered hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and tall, branching stems that bear yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are composed of both ray and disk florets.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental uses in traditional herbal practices.
