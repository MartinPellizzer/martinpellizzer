Hieracium sagaroyense

Hieracium sagaroyense, commonly known as the Sagaroye hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its habitat.
