Hieracium pseudopaltinae subsp. pseudopaltinae

Hieracium pseudopaltinae subsp.

pseudopaltinae, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it forms dense mats or low shrubs. Its leaves are linear to lanceolate, often with a hairy texture, and its yellow flowers bloom in clusters.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
