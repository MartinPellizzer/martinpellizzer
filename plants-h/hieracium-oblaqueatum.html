Hieracium oblaqueatum

Hieracium oblaqueatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, exhibiting a low-growing habit with slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow, with a central disc surrounded by petal-like rays, and it produces small achenes as its fruits.

Hieracium oblaqueatum is often found in open habitats and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
