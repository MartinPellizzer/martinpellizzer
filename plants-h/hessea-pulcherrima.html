Hessea pulcherrima

Hessea pulcherrima, commonly known as the red hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from red to pink and yellow. The plant has smooth, waxy leaves and produces capsules as its fruit, which contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Hessea pulcherrima is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
