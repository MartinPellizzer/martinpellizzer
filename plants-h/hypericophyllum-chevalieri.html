Hypericophyllum chevalieri

Hypericophyllum chevalieri, commonly known as the Corsican hypericum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Hypericaceae and is characterized by its erect growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
