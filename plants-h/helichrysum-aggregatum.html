Helichrysum aggregatum

Helichrysum aggregatum, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, yellow to golden-orange flowers that remain open for a long period. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, cylindrical seed heads that are often used in floral arrangements. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
