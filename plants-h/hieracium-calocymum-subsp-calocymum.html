Hieracium calocymum subsp. calocymum

Hieracium calocymum subsp.

calocymum, commonly known as the golden hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and meadows, and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer, and the leaves are lance-shaped with hairy surfaces.

It is often found in temperate climates and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
