Hedera × cazorlensis

Hedera × cazorlensis, commonly known as the Cazorla ivy, is a hybrid species of ivy that originates from the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a climbing plant characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that are typically heart-shaped or ovate. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds. Hedera × cazorlensis thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to cover vertical surfaces.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a hardy, decorative climbing plant.
