Hypericum buschianum

Hypericum buschianum, commonly known as the Buschian St. John's Wort, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers, which bloom in summer, and its woody stems covered in small, glandular dots. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Hypericum buschianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
