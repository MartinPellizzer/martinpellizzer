Hieracium leucolegnodes

Hieracium leucolegnodes, commonly known as the white-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a white center.

It is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is considered a potential weed in some regions.
