Hieracium conspersum

Hieracium conspersum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and ragworts, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators.
