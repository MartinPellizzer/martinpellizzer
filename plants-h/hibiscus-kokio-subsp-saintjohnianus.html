Hibiscus kokio subsp. saintjohnianus

Hibiscus kokio subsp.

saintjohnianus, commonly known as the Hawaiian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands. It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a strikingly contrasting yellow center.

The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. Its leaves are heart-shaped, glossy, and have a smooth texture.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
