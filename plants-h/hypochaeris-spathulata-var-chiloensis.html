Hypochaeris spathulata var. chiloensis

Hypochaeris spathulata var. chiloensis, commonly known as the Chilean oxeye, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers with a dark center. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It is an annual or biennial herb that can spread through seeds dispersed by wind.

The species is often used in ecological restoration due to its ability to thrive in various environmental conditions.
