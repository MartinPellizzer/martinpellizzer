Hypericum sechmenii

Hypericum sechmenii, commonly known as the Schmied's St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs. It is part of the genus Hypericum, which includes many species known for their medicinal properties.

This particular species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
