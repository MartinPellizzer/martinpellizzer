Hibanobambusa tranquillans

Hibanobambusa tranquillans, commonly known as the quiet bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Bambusoideae subfamily and is characterized by its slender, erect culms that often grow in dense clusters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically green and smooth on both surfaces. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream sides.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
