Haemaria discolor var. rhodoneura

Haemaria discolor var. rhodoneura, commonly known as the red-veined nettle, is a variant of the common nettle found in temperate regions.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its distinctive red veins on the undersides of its leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it easily identifiable and ecologically significant in its native habitats.
