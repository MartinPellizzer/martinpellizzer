Hieracium graciliscapum

Hieracium graciliscapum, commonly known as slender-stemmed hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

It is often found in temperate climates and is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens with well-drained soil.
