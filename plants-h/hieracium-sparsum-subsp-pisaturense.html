Hieracium sparsum subsp. pisaturense

Hieracium sparsum subsp.

pisaturense, commonly known as the creeping hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

The plant features a low-growing rosette of basal leaves with a rough, hairy texture. Its flowers are yellow and occur in clustered inflorescences, often with a distinctive central stem.

It is characterized by its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in disturbed areas.
