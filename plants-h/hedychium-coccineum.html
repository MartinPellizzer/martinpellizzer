Hedychium coccineum

Hedychium coccineum, commonly known as the red flame ginger, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, ornamental stems that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces large, showy inflorescences with bright red bracts that resemble flames, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically fragrant and bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hedychium coccineum thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
