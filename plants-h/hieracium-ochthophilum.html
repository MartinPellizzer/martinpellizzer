Hieracium ochthophilum

Hieracium ochthophilum, commonly known as the purple hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. Its leaves are basal, rough, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with purple markings.

It is a perennial species that produces a large number of seeds, contributing to its ability to spread and colonize new areas.
