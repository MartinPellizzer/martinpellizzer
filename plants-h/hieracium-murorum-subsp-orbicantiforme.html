Hieracium murorum subsp. orbicantiforme

Hieracium murorum subsp.

orbicantiforme, commonly known as the round-leaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The leaves are orbiculate, meaning they are broadly rounded, and often have a hairy texture. It typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats such as roadsides and meadows.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other regions outside its native range.
