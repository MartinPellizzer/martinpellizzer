Hyptis pumila

Hyptis pumila, commonly known as the white hyptis, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the warm climates of the Americas.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green leaves and clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight.

Its aromatic leaves and flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and traditional herbal use.
