Hieracium schmidtii subsp. rotenburgense

Hieracium schmidtii subsp.

rotenburgense, commonly known as the Schmidt's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has a hairy stem and narrow, linear leaves that are often tinged with reddish hues.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
