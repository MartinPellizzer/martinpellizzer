Hosta helonioides f. albopicta

Hosta helonioides f. albopicta, commonly known as the White-Faced Hosta, is a varietal form of the Hosta genus native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are predominantly dark green with distinctive white or light green margins. The plant produces small, fragrant, white flowers during the summer months, adding an ornamental touch to its foliage. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is prized for its unique leaf coloration and is a popular choice in shady garden settings.
