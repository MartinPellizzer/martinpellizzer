Hueblia

Hueblia, commonly known as the Mexican bellflower, is a genus of flowering plants in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to Mexico and Central America, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plants typically have showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Hueblia species are often shrubs or small trees, with smooth, sometimes hairy stems and simple, alternate leaves.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and are occasionally cultivated in gardens for their vibrant blooms.
