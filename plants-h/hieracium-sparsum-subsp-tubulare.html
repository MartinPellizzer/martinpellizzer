Hieracium sparsum subsp. tubulare

Hieracium sparsum subsp.

tubulare, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and tubular flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in many temperate regions.
