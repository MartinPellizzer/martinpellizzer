Henrya laxa

Henrya laxa, commonly known as the silver leaf henrya, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It features dense, compact foliage with silvery-white to grayish-blue leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white or pale pink during late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its ornamental value lies in its attractive foliage and delicate floral display.
