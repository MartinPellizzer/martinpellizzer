Hibiscus palmatifidus var. sakalava

Hibiscus palmatifidus var. sakalava, commonly known as the Sakalava hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in diameter. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in dense forests.

Its unique morphology and striking flowers make it a distinctive species within the Malvaceae family.
