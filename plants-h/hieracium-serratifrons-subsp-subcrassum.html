Hieracium serratifrons subsp. subcrassum

Hieracium serratifrons subsp.

subcrassum, commonly known as the broad-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its serrated leaves and yellow composite flowers, which are typical of the genus Hieracium.

This subspecies is native to Europe and is often found in meadows and grassy areas. It has a creeping growth habit and produces numerous small flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
