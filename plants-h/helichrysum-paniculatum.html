Helichrysum paniculatum

Helichrysum paniculatum, commonly known as the golden marguerite or everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, branched inflorescences composed of small, golden-yellow to orange flowers. The plant is perennial, often growing in open grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a slightly hairy surface.

Helichrysum paniculatum is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
