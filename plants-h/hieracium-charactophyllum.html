Hieracium charactophyllum

Hieracium charactophyllum, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

Hieracium charactophyllum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
