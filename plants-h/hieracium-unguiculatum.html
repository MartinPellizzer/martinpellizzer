Hieracium unguiculatum

Hieracium unguiculatum, commonly known as the hooked hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and hairy, while its flowers consist of both ray and disk florets.

Hieracium unguiculatum is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in many regions.
