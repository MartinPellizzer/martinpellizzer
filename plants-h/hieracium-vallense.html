Hieracium vallense

Hieracium vallense, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along riverbanks, preferring moist to moderately dry soils. It produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Hieracium vallense is often found in disturbed soils and can spread through rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
