Hieracium basinudum

Hieracium basinudum, commonly known as the basin-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded conditions. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette, and are often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are yellow, with ray florets that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
