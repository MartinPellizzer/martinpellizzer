Hieracium xystolepium subsp. brachypodum

Hieracium xystolepium subsp.

brachypodum, commonly known as the short-stemmed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and typical sunflower-like appearance.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are basal, rough, and hairy, while its flowers are yellow and clustered in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is notable for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain habitats.
