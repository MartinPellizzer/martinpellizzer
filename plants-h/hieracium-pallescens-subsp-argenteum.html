Hieracium pallescens subsp. argenteum

Hieracium pallescens subsp.

argenteum, commonly known as silver hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. It has a creeping rootstock and can form dense colonies, making it a persistent weed in some areas.

Its foliage is often covered with fine, white hairs that give it a distinctive silvery appearance.
