Hibiscus setifer

Hibiscus setifer, commonly known as the hairy hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range from pink to red in color. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. Hibiscus setifer thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be found in gardens and natural habitats alike.
