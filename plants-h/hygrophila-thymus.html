Hygrophila thymus

Hygrophila thymus, commonly known as water thyme, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its dense, floating growth habit in aquatic environments. The plant features small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of small, tubular flowers. Hygrophila thymus is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in still or slow-moving water.

It is often used in water gardens and as a biofiltration plant in ponds.
