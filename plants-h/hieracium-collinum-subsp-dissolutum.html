Hieracium collinum subsp. dissolutum

Hieracium collinum subsp.

dissolutum, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and yellow flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its elongated, slender leaves and the way its flowers are arranged in a single, terminal head. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, often in mountainous areas.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized plant.
