Hieracium frondiferum var. wologdense

Hieracium frondiferum var. wologdense, commonly known as the Wologdense hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and forest edges. Its leaves are lanceolate to ovate, with a hairy surface, and it produces yellow flowers in late summer.

It is often considered a weed due to its rapid growth and ability to colonize various environments.
