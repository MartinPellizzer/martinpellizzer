Hygrophila megalantha

Hygrophila megalantha, commonly known as the giant hygrophila, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, ribbon-like leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant typically thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spikes, often emerging above the water surface.

Hygrophila megalantha is often cultivated in aquariums due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to provide habitat for aquatic life.
