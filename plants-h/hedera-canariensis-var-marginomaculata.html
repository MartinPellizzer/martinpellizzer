Hedera canariensis var. marginomaculata

Hedera canariensis var. marginomaculata, commonly known as the Canary ivy, is a variant of the common ivy characterized by its distinctive leaf patterns.

Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a waxy texture and exhibit a striking combination of variegation, often featuring white and yellow margins. The plant is native to the Canary Islands and thrives in shaded, humid environments. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds.

This variety is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to climb or trail in various garden settings.
