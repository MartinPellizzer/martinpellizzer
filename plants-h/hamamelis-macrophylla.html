Hamamelis macrophylla

Hamamelis macrophylla, commonly known as the large-flowered witch hazel, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its striking orange-yellow flowers that bloom in late autumn or early winter. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that turn reddish in the fall. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, with a distinctive peeling texture.

Hamamelis macrophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its unique flowering habit.
