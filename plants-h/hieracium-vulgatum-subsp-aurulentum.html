Hieracium vulgatum subsp. aurulentum

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

aurulentum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped with hairy surfaces, and the flowers are bright yellow with a central disk.

It is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
