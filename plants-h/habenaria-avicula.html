Habenaria avicula

Habenaria avicula, commonly known as the bird's-beak orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its unique floral structure includes a distinctive, bird-like beak-shaped lip that aids in pollination.

Habenaria avicula is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by supporting pollinator species.
