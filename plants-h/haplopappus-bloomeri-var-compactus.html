Haplopappus bloomeri var. compactus

Haplopappus bloomeri var. compactus, commonly known as the compact pappus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical features include narrow, linear leaves and a unique, compact inflorescence structure.

It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
