Hyparrhenia lepida

Hyparrhenia lepida, commonly known as the slender grass or African silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often silver-white in color due to a dense covering of fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Hyparrhenia lepida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional practices in some regions.
