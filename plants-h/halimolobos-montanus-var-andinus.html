Halimolobos montanus var. andinus

Halimolobos montanus var. andinus, commonly known as the Andean halimolobos, is a perennial shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and hairy foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
