Hieracium pilosella subsp. melanocephalum

Hieracium pilosella subsp.

melanocephalum, commonly known as the black-headed hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow flowers that are typically surrounded by a dark, velvety area at the base.

The plant has finely divided, grass-like leaves and is often found in disturbed soils in temperate regions. It is a perennial herb that can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and sometimes invasive species in certain ecosystems.
