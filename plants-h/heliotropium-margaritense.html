Heliotropium margaritense

Heliotropium margaritense, commonly known as the sea hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant thrives in saline and sandy soils, often found in dune environments. Its leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture.

Heliotropium margaritense is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize coastal soils.
