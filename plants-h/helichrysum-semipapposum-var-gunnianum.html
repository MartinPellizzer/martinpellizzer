Helichrysum semipapposum var. gunnianum

Helichrysum semipapposum var. gunnianum, commonly known as the Kangaroo Island everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Australia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, yellow to golden-orange flowers that remain open for an extended period. The plant produces bracts that are often mistaken for petals, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in garden landscapes.
