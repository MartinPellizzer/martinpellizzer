Hieracium erythrocarpum

Hieracium erythrocarpum, commonly known as the red-fruited hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf morphology. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, while the fruit is a reddish, dry capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Hieracium erythrocarpum is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
