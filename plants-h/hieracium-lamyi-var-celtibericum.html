Hieracium lamyi var. celtibericum

Hieracium lamyi var. celtibericum, commonly known as the Celtiberian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typical basal rosette growth habit. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers with ray florets. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky areas.

Its botanical structure reflects adaptations to its native environment, supporting its ecological role in local flora.
