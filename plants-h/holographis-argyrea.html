Holographis argyrea

Holographis argyrea, commonly known as silverleaf honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves and fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has a climbing or trailing growth habit. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
