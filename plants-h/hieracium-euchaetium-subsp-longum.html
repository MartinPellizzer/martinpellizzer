Hieracium euchaetium subsp. longum

Hieracium euchaetium subsp.

longum, commonly known as the long-stemmed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

It is noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
