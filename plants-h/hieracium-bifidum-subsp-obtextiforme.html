Hieracium bifidum subsp. obtextiforme

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

obtextiforme, commonly known as the broad-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring well-drained conditions. Its leaves are broadly ovate to lanceolate, with a rough texture and often have a distinctive basal rosette.

The flowers are yellow, with ray florets that are usually shorter than the central disk florets.
