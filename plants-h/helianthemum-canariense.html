Helianthemum canariense

Helianthemum canariense, commonly known as the Canary Island rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its fleshy, evergreen leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the arid climate of its native habitat. It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is closely related to other species in the genus Helianthemum.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in Mediterranean climates.
