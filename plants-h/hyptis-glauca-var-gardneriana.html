Hyptis glauca var. gardneriana

Hyptis glauca var. gardneriana, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features aromatic, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is often found in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
