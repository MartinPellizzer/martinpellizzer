Hedychium trilobum

Hedychium trilobum, commonly known as the flame ginger, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers with a distinctive red throat, which bloom in clusters at the top of the stem. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a smooth texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Hedychium trilobum thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
