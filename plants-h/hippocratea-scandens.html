Hippocratea scandens

Hippocratea scandens, commonly known as the climbing hippocratea, is a tropical plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its woody vines and trifoliate leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
