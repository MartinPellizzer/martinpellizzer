Humularia multifoliolata

Humularia multifoliolata, commonly known as the many-leaved bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are palmately lobed, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Humularia multifoliolata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
