Hieracium subbifurcatum

Hieracium subbifurcatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically less than 5 cm in length. The plant produces small, yellow flower heads that are clustered in a single inflorescence at the tip of each stem. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas.

Hieracium subbifurcatum is a perennial herb that thrives in temperate climates and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.
