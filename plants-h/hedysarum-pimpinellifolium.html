Hedysarum pimpinellifolium

Hedysarum pimpinellifolium, commonly known as the "cherry blossom pea," is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, rounded leaflets resembling those of the cherry blossom, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its role in supporting pollinators and providing nectar-rich blooms.
