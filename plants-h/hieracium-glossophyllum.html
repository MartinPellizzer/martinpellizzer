Hieracium glossophyllum

Hieracium glossophyllum, commonly known as the glossary hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

Hieracium glossophyllum is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in agricultural and natural settings.
