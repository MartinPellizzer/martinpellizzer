Helictotrichon desertorum subsp. centroasiaticum

Helictotrichon desertorum subsp.

centroasiaticum, commonly known as the Central Asian needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems in its native habitat.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for survival in low-nutrient, drought-prone environments.
