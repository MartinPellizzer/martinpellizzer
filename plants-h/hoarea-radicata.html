Hoarea radicata

Hoarea radicata, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted, grass-like foliage and daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and white. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, open habitats. It is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters.

Hoarea radicata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
