Haplopappus hirsutus

Haplopappus hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy pappus, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its leaves are generally hairy, providing a textured appearance, while the small yellow flowers bloom in summer.

The plant is often found in rocky soils and is well-suited to xerophytic conditions.
