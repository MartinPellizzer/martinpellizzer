Hieracium villosiceps subsp. laniceps

Hieracium villosiceps subsp.

laniceps, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
