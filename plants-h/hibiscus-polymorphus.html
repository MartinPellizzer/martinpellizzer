Hibiscus polymorphus

Hibiscus polymorphus, commonly known as the Hawaiian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink or red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces fruits that are capsule-like in structure. Hibiscus polymorphus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and rainforests.

It plays an important role in the local ecosystem and is also valued for its ornamental beauty.
