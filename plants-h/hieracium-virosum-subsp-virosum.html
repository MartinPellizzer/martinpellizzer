Hieracium virosum subsp. virosum

Hieracium virosum subsp.

virosum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features basal rosettes of lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers arranged in dense, flat-topped clusters.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its flowering period occurs from late spring to early autumn, and it is often found in open habitats with well-drained soil.

The species is characterized by its fibrous root system and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
