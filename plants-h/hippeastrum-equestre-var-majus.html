Hippeastrum equestre var. majus

Hippeastrum equestre var. majus, commonly known as the Giant Arum, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its large, showy inflorescence.

It features a single, waxy, violet-colored spathe that encloses a spadix, which bears numerous small, clustered flowers. The plant is native to the Caribbean and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-draining soil. It produces large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a reddish tint.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
