Hypericum wightianum subsp. axillare

Hypericum wightianum subsp.

axillare, commonly known as the purple-stemmed St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to parts of Asia, including India and Nepal. It is characterized by its slender, woody stems that often display a distinctive purple hue, especially in cooler weather.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
