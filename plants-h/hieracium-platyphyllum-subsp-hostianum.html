Hieracium platyphyllum subsp. hostianum

Hieracium platyphyllum subsp.

hostianum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow flower heads that are typically grouped in clusters.

The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its historical use in traditional medicine.
