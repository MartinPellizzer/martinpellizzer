Hieracium subpatagiarium

Hieracium subpatagiarium, commonly known as the Patagonian hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for certain herbivores.
