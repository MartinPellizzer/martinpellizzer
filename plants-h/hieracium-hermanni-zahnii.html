Hieracium hermanni-zahnii

Hieracium hermanni-zahnii, commonly known as the Hermann's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow composite flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer. Native to central and eastern Europe, this plant thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It has a taproot system and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
