Hyptis lanceifolia

Hyptis lanceifolia, commonly known as lanceleaf hyptis, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its aromatic foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Hyptis lanceifolia is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
