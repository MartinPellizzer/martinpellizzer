Hieracium asterinum

Hieracium asterinum, commonly known as the star dandelion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, spoon-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are typically arranged in a compact cluster, giving the plant its common name.

Hieracium asterinum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in many temperate regions.
