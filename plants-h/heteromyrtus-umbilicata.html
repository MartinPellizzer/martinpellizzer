Heteromyrtus umbilicata

Heteromyrtus umbilicata, commonly known as the Christmasberry, is a flowering plant native to the island of Christmas Island in the Indian Ocean.

It is a small shrub that produces distinctive red berries, which are an important food source for native wildlife. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a glossy surface, and it flowers throughout the year.

The species is also notable for its role in the ecosystem and its unique adaptation to the island's environment.
