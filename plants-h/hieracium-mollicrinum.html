Hieracium mollicrinum

Hieracium mollicrinum, commonly known as the soft-hair hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, often glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers that are typically clustered in a head-like inflorescence. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Hieracium mollicrinum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
