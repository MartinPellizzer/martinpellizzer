Heliotropium samoliflorum

Heliotropium samoliflorum, commonly known as the purple heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or violet. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or disturbed habitats.

Heliotropium samoliflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
