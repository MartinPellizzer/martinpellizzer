Hieracium pseudofestiviforme

Hieracium pseudofestiviforme, commonly known as the showy hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
