Hieracium montis-florum

Hieracium montis-florum, commonly known as the mountain dandelion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Native to alpine regions of Europe, it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils at high elevations. The plant has a short flowering period and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is considered a symbol of resilience in harsh mountain environments.
