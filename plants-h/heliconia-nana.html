Heliconia nana

Heliconia nana, commonly known as the dwarf heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact stature, typically growing to a height of less than one meter. The plant features striking, colorful bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a spiral of small, fragrant flowers surrounded by vibrant red or orange bracts.

Heliconia nana thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in tropical gardens.
