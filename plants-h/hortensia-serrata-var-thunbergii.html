Hortensia serrata var. thunbergii

Hortensia serrata var. thunbergii, commonly known as the Japanese hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from pink to blue, depending on soil pH. The plant produces white, sterile flowers that are clustered in rounded or conical heads. Its leaves are serrated and lobed, typically dark green with a slightly rough texture.

It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
