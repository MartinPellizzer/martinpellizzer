Hieracium paragogum subsp. biforme

Hieracium paragogum subsp.

biforme, commonly known as the two-form hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a cluster of small flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are basal and lobed, with a rough texture, and its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is an herbaceous perennial that spreads through rhizomes, forming dense colonies.
