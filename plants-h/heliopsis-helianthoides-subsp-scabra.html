Heliopsis helianthoides subsp. scabra

Heliopsis helianthoides subsp.

scabra, commonly known as rough heliopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is a perennial herb with erect, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces bright yellow, daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and ray florets, blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators make it a valuable plant in native landscapes.
