Hedysarum microcalyx

Hedysarum microcalyx, commonly known as the small-flowered vetch, is a perennial leguminous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces cylindrical, hairy pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Hedysarum microcalyx is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
