Hieracium spathophyllum subsp. diatentum

Hieracium spathophyllum subsp.

diatentum, commonly known as the white-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disc.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
