Hicriopteris glaucoides

Hicriopteris glaucoides, commonly known as the glaucous water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to parts of Asia.

It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and streams, and is characterized by its delicate, floating fronds. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a waxy, bluish coating that helps reduce water loss. This plant plays a role in maintaining water quality by filtering impurities and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.

Its unique morphology and ecological significance make it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
