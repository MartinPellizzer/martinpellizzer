Hancornia monosperma

Hancornia monosperma, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a deciduous shrub that can grow up to 3 meters in height, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white to pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a bright purple berry that develops in the fall, containing a single seed.

Hancornia monosperma is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
