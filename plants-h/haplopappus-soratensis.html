Haplopappus soratensis

Haplopappus soratensis, commonly known as the narrow-pappus sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and thistles. The plant is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and small, bristle-like pappus structures that aid in wind pollination. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
