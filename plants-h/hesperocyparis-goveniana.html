Hesperocyparis goveniana

Hesperocyparis goveniana, commonly known as the Pacific silver fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, columnar growth habit and dark green, needle-like foliage. The tree produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. Its bark is thick, scaly, and often fissured, providing a distinctive appearance.

Hesperocyparis goveniana is adapted to moist, cool climates and is often found in mountainous regions.
