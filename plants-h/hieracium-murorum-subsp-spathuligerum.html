Hieracium murorum subsp. spathuligerum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

spathuligerum, commonly known as the broadleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually broad and lanceolate, with a hairy surface, and its yellow flowers appear in clusters.

The subspecies is distinguished by its broader leaf shape compared to other hawkweed species.
