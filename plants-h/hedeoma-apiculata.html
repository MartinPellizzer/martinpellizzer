Hedeoma apiculata

Hedeoma apiculata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed ironweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and conical flower heads. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. Hedeoma apiculata typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil, making it ecologically significant in wetland ecosystems.
