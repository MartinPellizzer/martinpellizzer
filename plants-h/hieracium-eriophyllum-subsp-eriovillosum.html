Hieracium eriophyllum subsp. eriovillosum

Hieracium eriophyllum subsp.

eriovillosum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed sites, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually hairy and lance-shaped, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is a perennial species that produces achenes as its reproductive structures.
