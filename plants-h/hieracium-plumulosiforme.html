Hieracium plumulosiforme

Hieracium plumulosiforme, commonly known as the downy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring temperate climates. It features basal leaves that are often hairy and elongated, with yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer.

Hieracium plumulosiforme is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain habitats.
