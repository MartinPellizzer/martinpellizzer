Hieracium dentatum subsp. aechmetes

Hieracium dentatum subsp.

aechmetes, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of rough, hairy leaves and flowering stems that bear yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It has a fibrous root system and produces numerous short, upright shoots.

Its distribution spans across parts of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates.
