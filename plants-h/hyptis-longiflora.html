Hyptis longiflora

Hyptis longiflora, commonly known as the long-flower hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a fragrant, minty scent when crushed.

Hyptis longiflora is often used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
