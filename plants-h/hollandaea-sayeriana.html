Hollandaea sayeriana

Hollandaea sayeriana, commonly known as the purple passion vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
