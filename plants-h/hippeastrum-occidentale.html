Hippeastrum occidentale

Hippeastrum occidentale, commonly known as the Amazonian tiger lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, typically in shades of red, orange, and yellow, with intricate patterns resembling tiger stripes. The plant produces a tall, sturdy stem that can grow up to 6 feet high, supporting its dramatic blooms. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Hippeastrum occidentale is valued in horticulture for its vibrant flowers and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and indoor settings.
