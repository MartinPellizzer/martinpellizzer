Hippeastrum procerum

Hippeastrum procerum, commonly known as the tall amaryllis, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that can be white, pink, or red, often with a contrasting stripe. The plant produces large, strap-like leaves that are broad and strap-shaped, growing from a fleshy underground bulb. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Hippeastrum procerum is popular as a ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
