Hesperis kurdica var. unguiculata

Hesperis kurdica var. unguiculata, commonly known as the Kurdish horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its distinctive four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in early summer, and the leaves are usually lance-shaped with a slightly hairy surface.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
