Hebe saxicola

Hebe saxicola, commonly known as the rock hebe, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white during the spring and summer months. Hebe saxicola thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal environments.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.
