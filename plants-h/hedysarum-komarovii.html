Hedysarum komarovii

Hedysarum komarovii, commonly known as Komarov's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Hedysarum komarovii is also valued for its potential in soil stabilization and as a forage plant.
