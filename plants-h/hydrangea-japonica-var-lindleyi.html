Hydrangea japonica var. lindleyi

Hydrangea japonica var. lindleyi, commonly known as the mountain hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from white to pink, depending on soil pH. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, with serrated margins and a dark green color.

The flowers bloom in late summer, producing a dramatic visual impact in gardens and natural landscapes.
