Henckelia insignis

Henckelia insignis, commonly known as the yellow bell tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture.

Henckelia insignis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
