Heracleum sibiricum f. latifolium

Heracleum sibiricum f. latifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved Siberian cowparsley, is a variant of the Siberian cowparsley, characterized by its wider leaves compared to the typical form.

It is a biennial or perennial herb native to parts of Asia, including Siberia and Central Asia. The plant features a tall, hollow stem with large, fern-like leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. Its flowers are small, white to greenish-yellow, and arranged in umbrella-like clusters called umbels.

This plant is often found in moist meadows, riverbanks, and other damp habitats.
