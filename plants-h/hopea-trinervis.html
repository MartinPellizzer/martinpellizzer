Hopea trinervis

Hopea trinervis, commonly known as the three-veined hopea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, elliptical leaves with three distinct veins. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, three-lobed fruits containing one or two seeds. Hopea trinervis is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
