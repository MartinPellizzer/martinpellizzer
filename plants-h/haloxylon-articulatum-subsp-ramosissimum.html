Haloxylon articulatum subsp. ramosissimum

Haloxylon articulatum subsp.

ramosissimum, commonly known as the desert date or desert shrub, is a drought-resistant plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East. It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, spiny branches that help reduce water loss and deter herbivores.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible, fleshy fruits. Its deep root system allows it to access groundwater, making it well adapted to harsh desert environments.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing shelter for wildlife in its native habitat.
