Holcus annuus subsp. setiger

Holcus annuus subsp.

setiger, commonly known as the hairy yellow oat, is a grass species characterized by its slender, erect stems and fine, hairy leaves. It typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural areas or along roadsides.

The plant produces small, yellowish inflorescences that contain numerous tiny grains, which are dispersed by wind. It is an annual species, completing its life cycle within a single growing season.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in many temperate regions.
