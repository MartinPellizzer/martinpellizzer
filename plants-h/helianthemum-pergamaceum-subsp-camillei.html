Helianthemum pergamaceum subsp. camillei

Helianthemum pergamaceum subsp.

camillei, commonly known as Camille's rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a low-growing shrub with greyish, succulent leaves that are adapted to arid environments.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and drought tolerance.
