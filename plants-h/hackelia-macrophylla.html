Hackelia macrophylla

Hackelia macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved honeysuckle, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct serrated margin. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

Hackelia macrophylla is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
