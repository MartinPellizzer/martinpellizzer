Hieracium villosum subsp. chloropsis

Hieracium villosum subsp.

chloropsis, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The leaves are typically hairy and lance-shaped, with a rough texture. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

This subspecies is often mistaken for other hawkweed species due to its similar appearance.
