Heliotropium inundatum var. chacoense

Heliotropium inundatum var. chacoense, commonly known as the Chaco heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in the Chaco region of Paraguay and Argentina.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, adapting to periodically flooded conditions. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple, blooming during the warmer months. Its leaves are often succulent, allowing it to retain moisture in its native habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
