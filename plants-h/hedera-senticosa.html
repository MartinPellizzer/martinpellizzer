Hedera senticosa

Hedera senticosa, commonly known as the sensitive ivy, is a climbing evergreen vine native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dark green, glossy leaves that are typically heart-shaped with a pointed tip. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Hedera senticosa is also noted for its ability to climb walls and trees, making it a popular choice for vertical gardening.
