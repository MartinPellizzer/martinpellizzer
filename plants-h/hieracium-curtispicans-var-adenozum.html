Hieracium curtispicans var. adenozum

Hieracium curtispicans var. adenozum, commonly known as the腺叶蒲公英, is a variant of the common dandelion species.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that develop into fluffy seed heads, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats, often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant plant in its native range.
