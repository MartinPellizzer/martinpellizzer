Hemisphaera kryophila

Hemisphaera kryophila, commonly known as the snowbank lily, is a rare alpine plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive hemispherical-shaped flower that resembles a small bowl, hence its genus name. The plant thrives in cold, moist environments and is adapted to survive in harsh, snow-covered conditions. Its flowers are typically white or pale blue, and it blooms in early summer when the snow begins to melt.
