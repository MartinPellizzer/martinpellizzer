Hypericum assyriacum

Hypericum assyriacum, commonly known as the Armenian crocus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Turkey.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking yellow flowers with orange stamens, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or well-drained soils. It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to grow in temperate climates.

Hypericum assyriacum is also known for its historical use in traditional medicine.
