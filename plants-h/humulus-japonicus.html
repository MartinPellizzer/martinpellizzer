Humulus japonicus

Humulus japonicus, commonly known as Japanese hop, is a climbing perennial plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Cannabaceae family and is characterized by its vigorous growth and hairy stems. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a distinctive serrated edge.

Humulus japonicus is often cultivated for its potential medicinal properties and ornamental value.
