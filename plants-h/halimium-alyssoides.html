Halimium alyssoides

Halimium alyssoides, commonly known as the alpine catchfly, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features small, hairy leaves and produces clusters of pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, thriving in harsh alpine environments. It is often found in meadows and open slopes at high elevations.

Halimium alyssoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
