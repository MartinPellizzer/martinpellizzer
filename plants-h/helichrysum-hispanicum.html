Helichrysum hispanicum

Helichrysum hispanicum, commonly known as the Spanish immortelle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that remain colorful even when dried, giving it its common name. The plant grows in rocky soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are typically hairy.

Helichrysum hispanicum is valued for its ornamental use in gardens and for its historical use in herbal medicine.
