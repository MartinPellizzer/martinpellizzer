Hieracium arpadianum subsp. plessidicum

Hieracium arpadianum subsp.

plessidicum, commonly known as the Plessidic hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in meadows, pastures, and rocky soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments.

The plant features basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces yellow composite flowers with ray florets. Its inflorescence is a single, showy flower head that blooms in late spring to early summer.

The subspecies is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and is often found in calcareous soils.
