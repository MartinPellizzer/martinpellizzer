Hieracium remotiflorum

Hieracium remotiflorum, commonly known as the distant-flowered hawkweed, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, and it has hairy, upright stems with alternate leaves. It is a perennial herb that can grow up to 30 centimeters tall, and its flowers are often clustered in a single inflorescence at the top of the stem.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
