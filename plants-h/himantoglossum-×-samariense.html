Himantoglossum × samariense

Himantoglossum × samariense, commonly known as the hybrid hare's-foot clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
