Hieracium Chondrillifolium Subsp. Pseudosixtinum

Hieracium chondrillifolium subsp. pseudosixtinum is a lesser-known member of the daisy family found in specific alpine regions. It is characterized by its delicate, hairy leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. This subspecies is often mistaken for other similar plants due to its subtle appearance. Despite its modest size, it plays an important role in its ecosystem. Its rarity and specific habitat requirements make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
