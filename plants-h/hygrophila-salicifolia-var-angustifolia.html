Hygrophila salicifolia var. angustifolia

Hygrophila salicifolia var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf waterweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant thrives in aquatic or semi-aquatic environments, often found in slow-moving freshwater habitats. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it is often used in aquariums due to its ornamental value and ease of care.

Its rapid growth rate makes it both a useful aquatic plant and a potential invasive species in certain areas.
