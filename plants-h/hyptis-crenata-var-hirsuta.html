Hyptis crenata var. hirsuta

Hyptis crenata var. hirsuta, commonly known as hairy hyptis, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in moist or wetland habitats.

Its aromatic leaves and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens and traditional medicine.
