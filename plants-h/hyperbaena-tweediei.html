Hyperbaena tweediei

Hyperbaena tweediei, commonly known as the Texas bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, aiding in its propagation.

The Texas bluebell is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
