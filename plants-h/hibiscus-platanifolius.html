Hibiscus platanifolius

Hibiscus platanifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth. Hibiscus platanifolius is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its rapid growth and ability to adapt to various climates make it a popular choice for gardeners and landscapers.
