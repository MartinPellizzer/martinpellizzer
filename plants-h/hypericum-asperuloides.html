Hypericum asperuloides

Hypericum asperuloides, commonly known as rough-stemmed St. John's wort, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the genus Hypericum, which includes many species of shrubs and herbs known for their yellow flowers. The plant features woody stems covered in rough, bristly hairs, giving it its common name. Its small, clustered yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in dry, open habitats like meadows and woodland edges.
