Habenaria dilatata f. chlorantha

Habenaria dilatata f. chlorantha, commonly known as the green-flowered marsh orchid, is a rare orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and its distinctive greenish-yellow floral bracts. The plant typically grows in wet, shaded environments such as marshes and forest floors. Its flowers are small and clustered, with a unique structure that includes a spurred lip.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its botanical and ecological significance.
