Helicteres baruensis var. ovata

Helicteres baruensis var. ovata, commonly known as the Brazilian false arrowhead, is a shrub native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its palmate leaves with five to seven lobes. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
