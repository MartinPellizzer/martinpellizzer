Helleborus viridis var. grandiflorus

Helleborus viridis var. grandiflorus, commonly known as the green hellebore, is a perennial plant native to parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its evergreen, dark green, leathery leaves that remain throughout the year. The plant produces striking, waxy flowers in shades of white, green, and pink, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in shady, moist environments and prefers well-drained, humus-rich soil.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and its ability to bloom even in cold conditions.
