Henckelia vulcanica

Henckelia vulcanica, commonly known as the volcanic heather, is a flowering plant native to the volcanic regions of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to the harsh conditions of volcanic terrain. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss.

Henckelia vulcanica is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to its extreme environment.
