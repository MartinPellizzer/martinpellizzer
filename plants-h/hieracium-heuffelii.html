Hieracium heuffelii

Hieracium heuffelii, commonly known as the Heuffel's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellowish to reddish flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring well-drained conditions. It produces small, composite flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and sometimes endangered species in certain regions.
