Hibiscus capitatus

Hibiscus capitatus, commonly known as the desert hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has hairy, woody stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in appearance. It thrives in arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Hibiscus capitatus is often found in rocky soils and is a common sight in desert landscapes.
