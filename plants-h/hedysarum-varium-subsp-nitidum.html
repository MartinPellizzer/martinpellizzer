Hedysarum varium subsp. nitidum

Hedysarum varium subsp.

nitidum, commonly known as the smooth vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region. It features trifoliate leaves with slender, hairy stipules and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes.

The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in well-drained soils, often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas. Its small, elliptical pods contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Hedysarum varium subsp.

nitidum is valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
