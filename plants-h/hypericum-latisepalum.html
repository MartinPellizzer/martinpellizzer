Hypericum latisepalum

Hypericum latisepalum, commonly known as the broad-sepal St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along its stems. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five to seven broad, showy sepals that resemble petals. It blooms from June to September, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hypericum latisepalum is often found in moist, woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
