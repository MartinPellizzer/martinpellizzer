Huernia khalidbinsultanii

Huernia khalidbinsultanii, commonly known as the Khalid bin Sultan's moon cactus, is a unique succulent native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its swollen, fleshy stems that resemble a moon, hence its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers during the warmer months, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. Its leaves are typically reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it thrives in well-draining soil with minimal water.

Huernia khalidbinsultanii is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor collections due to its low maintenance and ornamental appeal.
