Hypericum meridense

Hypericum meridense, commonly known as the southern St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in wetlands and moist woodlands, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a reddish or purple tint beneath. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, blooming from late spring to early summer.

It is characterized by its ability to thrive in wet conditions and its historical use in traditional medicine.
