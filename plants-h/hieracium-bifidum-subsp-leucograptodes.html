Hieracium bifidum subsp. leucograptodes

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

leucograptodes, commonly known as the white-stippled hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and yellowish to orange flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped with a hairy surface and often have a distinct white or pale green stippling. This subspecies is native to southern Europe and is often found in meadows and grassy areas.

It is a perennial plant that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.
