Hemerocallis altissima

Hemerocallis altissima, commonly known as the tall daylily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems that can reach heights of up to 6 feet. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are evergreen in warmer climates.

Hemerocallis altissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
