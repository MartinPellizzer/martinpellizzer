Hedyotis albidopunctata

Hedyotis albidopunctata, commonly known as the white-punctured bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a distinctive white or pale spot on the underside.

The species is characterized by its fibrous roots and its ability to thrive in humid, temperate climates.
