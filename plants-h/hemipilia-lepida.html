Hemipilia lepida

Hemipilia lepida, commonly known as the slender hemipilia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern China.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often clustered in loose racemes. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Hemipilia lepida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
