Helianthemum mucronatum

Helianthemum mucronatum, commonly known as the sharp-tipped sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry grasslands. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a bright touch to its habitat. Its leaves are usually succulent and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in arid conditions.

Helianthemum mucronatum is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
