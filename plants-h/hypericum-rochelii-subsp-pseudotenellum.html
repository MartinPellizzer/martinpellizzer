Hypericum rochelii subsp. pseudotenellum

Hypericum rochelii subsp.

pseudotenellum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved St. John's wort, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a glossy green surface. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
