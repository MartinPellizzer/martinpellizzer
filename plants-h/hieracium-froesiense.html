Hieracium froesiense

Hieracium froesiense, commonly known as the Froese's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous tiny florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are rough and hairy, with a serrated edge, and its flowers are yellow, forming a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is often found in moist habitats and is considered a characteristic species of certain ecological zones.
