Hibiscus chinensis

Hibiscus chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to deep red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsules that contain numerous seeds. Hibiscus chinensis thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
