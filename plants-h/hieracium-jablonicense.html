Hieracium jablonicense

Hieracium jablonicense, commonly known as the Jablonicek hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. Native to Central Europe, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant has small, elongated bracts that surround the flower heads, and its flowering period typically occurs from June to August.

It is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.
