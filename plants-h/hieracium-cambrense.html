Hieracium cambrense

Hieracium cambrense, commonly known as Welsh hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to western Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous tiny florets. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

Hieracium cambrense is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of habitats, making it a common sight in the British Isles.
