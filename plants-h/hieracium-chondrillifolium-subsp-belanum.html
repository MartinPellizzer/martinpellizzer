Hieracium chondrillifolium subsp. belanum

Hieracium chondrillifolium subsp.

belanum, commonly known as the Balkan hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe. It typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring temperate climates.

The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces yellow flowers in clusters. It is part of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its basal rosette growth habit.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
