Hieracium elutum

Hieracium elutum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium elutum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in agricultural and natural areas.
