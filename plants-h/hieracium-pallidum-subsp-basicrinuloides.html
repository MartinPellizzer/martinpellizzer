Hieracium pallidum subsp. basicrinuloides

Hieracium pallidum subsp.

basicrinuloides, commonly known as the pale hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to reddish flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while the inflorescence consists of a flat-topped cluster of small, daisy-like flowers.

This subspecies is noted for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its potential use in traditional herbal medicine.
