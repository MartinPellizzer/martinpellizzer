Heliotropium curassavicum

Heliotropium curassavicum, commonly known as the Caribbean heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is an annual herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy, and it produces clusters of small, pink or purple flowers. Its flowers are often fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and pleasant scent.
