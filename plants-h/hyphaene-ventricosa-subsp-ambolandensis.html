Hyphaene ventricosa subsp. ambolandensis

Hyphaene ventricosa subsp.

ambolandensis, commonly known as the Amboland palm, is a robust, fan-shaped palm native to eastern Africa. It features a thick, trunk-like stem covered with persistent leaf bases, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces large, fan-like leaves with long, flexible leaflets that are arranged in a circular pattern. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing at the base of the leaves, and it produces edible fruits that are important in local ecosystems.

This subspecies is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
