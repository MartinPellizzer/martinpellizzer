Hastingia scandens

Hastingia scandens, commonly known as the creeping hastingia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its succulent, trailing stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is popular among succulent enthusiasts.
