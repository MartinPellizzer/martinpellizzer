Hieracium dacicum

Hieracium dacicum, commonly known as the Dacian hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to southeastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring well-drained conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Hieracium dacicum is often found in the Carpathian region and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
