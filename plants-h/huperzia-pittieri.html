Huperzia pittieri

Huperzia pittieri, commonly known as the Mexican shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that are typically dark green and leathery in texture. Native to Mexico and parts of Central America, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and it is valued for its ornamental appearance.

Huperzia pittieri plays an important ecological role in its native habitats by contributing to soil stability and providing microhabitats for various organisms.
