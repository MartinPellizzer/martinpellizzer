Horridocactus paucicostatus

Horridocactus paucicostatus, commonly known as the spiny barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem that is covered in sharp, spiny tubercles, which provide protection from herbivores. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its rugged structure. It is adapted to thrive in harsh, dry environments with minimal rainfall.

Its unique morphology and resilience make it a notable example of desert flora.
