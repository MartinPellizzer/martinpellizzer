Hemigenia podalyrina

Hemigenia podalyrina, commonly known as the narrow-leafed honey mint, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. Hemigenia podalyrina is often found in moist, shaded areas such as rainforests and along stream banks.

It is valued for its fragrant foliage and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
