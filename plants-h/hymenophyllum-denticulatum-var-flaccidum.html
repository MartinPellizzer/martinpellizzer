Hymenophyllum denticulatum var. flaccidum

Hymenophyllum denticulatum var. flaccidum, commonly known as the flaccid toothed moss, is a delicate fern-like liverwort that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its small, elongated leaves with finely serrated margins, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows on rocks or tree trunks, preferring cool and humid conditions. Its reproductive structures, known as sporophytes, are hidden beneath the leafy layers, typical of many liverworts.

This variant is notable for its soft, flexible leaves that can appear somewhat limp, giving it its common name.
