Helianthus strumosus var. mollis

Helianthus strumosus var. mollis, commonly known as the softstem sunflower, is a variant of the sunflower genus characterized by its soft, hairy stems and leaves.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States, where it thrives in moist, sunny habitats. The plant produces large, yellow composite flowers with a central disk surrounded by ray florets, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its foliage is typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is noted for its adaptability to wetland environments and its role in supporting local pollinators.
