Haworthia mcmurtryi

Haworthia mcmurtryi, commonly known as the Zulu bulb, is a species of succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rosette-shaped leaves that are often covered in a white powdery coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers on tall, slender stems during the warmer months. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its fleshy leaves.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
