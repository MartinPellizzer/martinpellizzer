Hibiscus paramutabilis var. paramutabilis

Hibiscus paramutabilis var. paramutabilis, commonly known as the variable hibiscus, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can vary in color from white to pink and even red. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and the flowers are borne on long, slender pedicels.

Hibiscus paramutabilis var. paramutabilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
