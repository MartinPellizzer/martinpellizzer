Hoodia picta

Hoodia picta, commonly known as the painted hoodia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, spineless stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves and stems. It produces small, red berries that are edible and contain a high concentration of nutrients.

Hoodia picta is also noted for its potential medicinal properties, though more research is needed to confirm its therapeutic uses.
