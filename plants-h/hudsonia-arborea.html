Hudsonia arborea

Hudsonia arborea, commonly known as the tree hudsonia, is a unique species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a small, slow-growing tree that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 15 feet. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a glossy green surface. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in dense inflorescences, which bloom in the spring.

Hudsonia arborea is adapted to moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade.
