Hieracium ravidum var. exaltifrons

Hieracium ravidum var. exaltifrons, commonly known as the tall hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate, rough-hairy leaves. The plant typically grows up to 1 meter in height, with yellow-orange flower heads that bloom from June to September. Its leaves are lance-shaped, often with a serrated edge, and it produces a milky sap when damaged.

This species is often found in grassy areas, along roadsides, and in disturbed soils, showing a preference for temperate climates.
