Hakea glaucina

Hakea glaucina, commonly known as the silver hakea, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage that provides a striking visual contrast. The plant produces cylindrical, woody cones that contain numerous seeds, typical of the Proteaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for coastal and dryland environments.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
