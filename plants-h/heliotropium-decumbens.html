Heliotropium decumbens

Heliotropium decumbens, commonly known as the prostrate heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a low-growing annual herb with succulent stems and opposite leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, violet or purple flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in response to sunlight. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and gardens.

Heliotropium decumbens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
