Hibbertia coriacea subsp. monticola

Hibbertia coriacea subsp.

monticola, commonly known as the mountain hibbertia, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in open woodlands and heathlands. It is adapted to dry conditions and can withstand periods of drought. Hibbertia coriacea subsp.

monticola is a species of interest for its ecological role and ornamental value.
