Habenaria trichosantha

Habenaria trichosantha, commonly known as the hairy orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial herb with a rhizomatous root system and produces erect, branching stems. The plant features showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, with three sepals and three petals. Its leaves are usually lanceolate and arranged in a rosette pattern around the base of the plant.

Habenaria trichosantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
