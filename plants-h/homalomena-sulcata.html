Homalomena sulcata

Homalomena sulcata, commonly known as the zigzag caladium, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, wavy, and often variegated leaves that display a range of colors, including green, red, and white. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which is known for its unique inflorescence structure called a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. Homalomena sulcata thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage.
