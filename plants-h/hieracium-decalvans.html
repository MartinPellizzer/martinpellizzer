Hieracium decalvans

Hieracium decalvans, commonly known as the naked hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with fine hairs, while the stems are hairy and somewhat erect.

Hieracium decalvans produces yellow flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
