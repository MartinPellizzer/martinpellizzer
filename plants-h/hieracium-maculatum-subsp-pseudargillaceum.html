Hieracium maculatum subsp. pseudargillaceum

Hieracium maculatum subsp.

pseudargillaceum, commonly known as the spotted hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves with a distinctive reddish or purplish hue, especially on the underside. It is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and can form dense colonies.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Asteraceae and is known for its ability to spread through both vegetative reproduction and seeds.
