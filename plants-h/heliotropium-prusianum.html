Heliotropium prusianum

Heliotropium prusianum, commonly known as Prussian heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant, pinkish-purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

The flowers emit a sweet, vanilla-like scent, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
