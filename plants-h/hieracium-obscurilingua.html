Hieracium obscurilingua

Hieracium obscurilingua, commonly known as the dark-lipped hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe and is characterized by its yellow daisy-like flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flower heads consist of both ray and disk flowers.

Hieracium obscurilingua is often found in temperate regions and can sometimes be considered a weed in agricultural settings.
