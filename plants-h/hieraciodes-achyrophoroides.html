Hieraciodes achyrophoroides

Hieraciodes achyrophoroides, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to anchor it in loose, sandy soils.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapes and rock gardens.
