Hydrostachys

Hydrostachys, commonly known as water hyssop, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The flowers are typically small and clustered in whorls, often with a purple or blue coloration. Hydrostachys species are often found in wetland or aquatic environments, thriving in moist or submerged conditions.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
