Hieracium sanguineum

Hieracium sanguineum, commonly known as the bloodwort, is a flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and woodland edges. Its leaves are broadly lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are usually reddish in color.

The flowers are composed of both ray and disk florets, and the plant is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
