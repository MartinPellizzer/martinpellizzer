Hoya sigillatis

Hoya sigillatis, commonly known as the "Wax Plant" or "Porcelain Flower," is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with white or cream markings. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pink in color. Hoya sigillatis is a popular houseplant due to its low maintenance and ability to thrive in indirect light.

Its unique appearance and ease of care make it a favored choice for indoor gardening.
