Hysterionica scaposa

Hysterionica scaposa, commonly known as the scapose bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, violet, or white, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Hysterionica scaposa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation in temperate climates.
