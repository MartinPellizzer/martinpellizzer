Hieracium juranum subsp. retifolium

Hieracium juranum subsp.

retifolium, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, and the flower heads are borne on long, slender stalks.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to harsh mountain environments and its role in local ecosystems.
