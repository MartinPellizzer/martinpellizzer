Halocnemum americanum

Halocnemum americanum, commonly known as American sea blite, is a halophytic plant adapted to grow in saline environments.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial that thrives in coastal and inland salt marshes. The plant has small, scale-like leaves and produces yellow flowers in spring. It is often found in association with other salt-tolerant species in arid and semi-arid regions.

Its ability to accumulate salts in its tissues makes it an important component of saltland ecosystems.
