Hypochaeris andina var. laciniata

Hypochaeris andina var. laciniata, commonly known as the Andean cutleaf sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lobed, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers with ray florets. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
