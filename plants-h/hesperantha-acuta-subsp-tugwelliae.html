Hesperantha acuta subsp. tugwelliae

Hesperantha acuta subsp.

tugwelliae, commonly known as the Tugwell's bluebell, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family native to South Africa. This perennial herb features slender, grass-like leaves and produces striking blue to violet bell-shaped flowers.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas. The plant is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.

It requires well-drained soil and moderate watering to flourish.
