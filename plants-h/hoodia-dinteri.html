Hoodia dinteri

Hoodia dinteri, commonly known as the desert apple, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in extreme drought conditions, storing water in its thick leaves and stems. Hoodia dinteri is also noted for its unique ability to produce a sweet, apple-like flavor when its fruits ripen.

Due to its resilience and ecological significance, it plays an important role in the desert ecosystems of its native habitat.
