Hypoestes forskaolii subsp. hildebrandtii

Hypoestes forskaolii subsp.

hildebrandtii, commonly known as the flame violet, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its striking, brightly colored bracts that resemble small flowers, which are actually modified leaves.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or herbaceous plant, often with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

The species is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
