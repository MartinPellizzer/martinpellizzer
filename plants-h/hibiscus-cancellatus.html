Hibiscus cancellatus

Hibiscus cancellatus, commonly known as the white hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers with a distinctive yellow center. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Hibiscus cancellatus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
