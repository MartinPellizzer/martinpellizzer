Helenium altissimum

Helenium altissimum, commonly known as the American feverfew, is a striking perennial plant native to North America.

It features showy, daisy-like yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has a robust, upright stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.
