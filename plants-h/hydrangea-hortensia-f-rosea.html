Hydrangea hortensia f. rosea

Hydrangea hortensia f. rosea, commonly known as the roseate hydrangea, is a compact, flowering shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that range from white to pink and blue, depending on soil pH. The plant produces sterile flowers, which are typically pink or red, and it thrives in partial shade with moist, well-drained soil. It is prized for its ornamental value and ability to change flower color, making it a popular choice in gardens.

The foliage remains lush and green throughout the growing season, adding visual interest year-round.
