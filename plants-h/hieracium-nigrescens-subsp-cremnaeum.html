Hieracium nigrescens subsp. cremnaeum

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

cremnaeum, commonly known as the creeping hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and thrives in meadows, woodland edges, and along riverbanks.

The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are broadly lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow, with ray florets that are usually shorter than the central disk florets.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
