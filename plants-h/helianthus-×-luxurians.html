Helianthus × luxurians

Helianthus × luxurians, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a hybrid species native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow-orange flowers and robust, upright growth habit. The plant typically reaches heights of 3 to 6 feet and produces abundant blooms throughout the summer months. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is a hardy annual that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, low-maintenance ornamental plants.
