Homalobus debilis

Homalobus debilis, commonly known as the weakly branching orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a small, epiphytic plant native to tropical regions of South America. The plant features slender, wiry stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and typically white or pale yellow, arranged in a raceme.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, loose soil for optimal growth.
