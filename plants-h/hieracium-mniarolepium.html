Hieracium mniarolepium

Hieracium mniarolepium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow ray florets surrounded by tubular disc florets. Native to Europe, it thrives in moist, disturbed soils such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant has fibrous roots and can form dense colonies, often spreading through rhizomes.

Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
