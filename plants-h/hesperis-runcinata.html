Hesperis runcinata

Hesperis runcinata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant features delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Hesperis runcinata is typically found in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and coastal areas.
