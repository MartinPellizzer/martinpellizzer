Hieracium schultzianum

Hieracium schultzianum, commonly known as the Schultze's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
