Hieracium cordatum subsp. conaticum

Hieracium cordatum subsp.

conaticum, commonly known as the heartleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. It typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and rocky soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters.

The plant features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the upper leaves are more lanceolate. Its flowering stems bear dense clusters of small, yellow flowers with ray florets, which bloom from June to August.

The species is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.
