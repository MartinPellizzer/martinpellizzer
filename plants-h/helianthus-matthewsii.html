Helianthus matthewsii

Helianthus matthewsii, commonly known as the Matthew's sunflower, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to the United States, specifically found in Arizona and New Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its yellow ray flowers and a central disk of smaller florets, typical of the genus Helianthus. It has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Helianthus matthewsii is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
