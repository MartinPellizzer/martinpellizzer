Hyptis mutabilis var. rostrata

Hyptis mutabilis var. rostrata, commonly known as the variable hyptis or rostrata hyptis, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or violet flowers. It often grows in moist, shaded areas and is known for its medicinal properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
