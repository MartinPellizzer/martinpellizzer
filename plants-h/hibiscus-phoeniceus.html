Hibiscus phoeniceus

Hibiscus phoeniceus, commonly known as the Jerusalem sage, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom in the summer months. Its flowers have a distinctive fragrance and are often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Hibiscus phoeniceus is known for its adaptability to coastal and rocky environments.
