Hieracium chalcidicum subsp. thessalum

Hieracium chalcidicum subsp.

thessalum, commonly known as the Thessalian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has rough, hairy leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that attract various pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species in certain regions of Greece.
