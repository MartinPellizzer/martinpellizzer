Hieracium phyllobracteum

Hieracium phyllobracteum, commonly known as the leaf-bract hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat, disc-like structure.

It is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in various habitats.
