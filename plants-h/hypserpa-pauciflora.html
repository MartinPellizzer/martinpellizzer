Hypserpa pauciflora

Hypserpa pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered hypserpa, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale pink. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Hypserpa pauciflora thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
