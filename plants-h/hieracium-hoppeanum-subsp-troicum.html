Hieracium hoppeanum subsp. troicum

Hieracium hoppeanum subsp.

troicum, commonly known as the Hoppe's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and its flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

The subspecies troicum is distinguished by its specific morphological traits, including the size and shape of its leaves and flowers.
