Hieracium caesioides subsp. bordigherae

Hieracium caesioides subsp.

bordigherae, commonly known as the Bordighera hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Italy. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along the Mediterranean coast, preferring sunny to partially shaded locations. It has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and its stems are erect with few branches.

This subspecies is notable for its limited distribution and is considered a rare flora species in its native habitat.
