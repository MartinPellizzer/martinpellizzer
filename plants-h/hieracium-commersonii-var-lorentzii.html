Hieracium commersonii var. lorentzii

Hieracium commersonii var. lorentzii, commonly known as the Lorentz's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces numerous small, composite flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
