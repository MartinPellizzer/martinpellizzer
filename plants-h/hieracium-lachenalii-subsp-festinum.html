Hieracium lachenalii subsp. festinum

Hieracium lachenalii subsp.

festinum, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils at high elevations. It features basal and stem leaves that are generally lanceolate to linear, with hairy surfaces.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, contributing to its ecological role in pollinator support.
