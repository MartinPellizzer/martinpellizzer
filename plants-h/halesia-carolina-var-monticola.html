Halesia carolina var. monticola

Halesia carolina var. monticola, commonly known as the Appalachian halesia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and its showy, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a smooth margin and a pointed tip. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
