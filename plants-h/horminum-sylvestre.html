Horminum sylvestre

Horminum sylvestre, commonly known as the wild orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its flowering stem bears a single, showy flower.

Horminum sylvestre is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique botanical features.
