Hemizonia balsamifera

Hemizonia balsamifera, commonly known as the balsam-flowered hemizonia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, resinous leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, disc-like flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. Its leaves are glandular and emit a balsamic scent, which is a notable botanical feature.

Hemizonia balsamifera is often found in rocky or sandy soils in arid environments.
