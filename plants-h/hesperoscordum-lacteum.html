Hesperoscordum lacteum

Hesperoscordum lacteum, commonly known as the milk thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple flowers and lobed, silvery-green leaves. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers. Its most notable feature is the milky sap that exudes from the stems and leaves when they are broken.

Hesperoscordum lacteum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
