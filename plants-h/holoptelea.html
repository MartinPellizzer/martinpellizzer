Holoptelea

Holoptelea, commonly known as the African sandthorn, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It consists of several species, predominantly found in arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East. The plants are shrubs or small trees with small, clustered leaves and yellow flowers. They are adapted to harsh environments, often having deep root systems to access water.

The fruits are small, fleshy berries that may be edible, though some species have toxic seeds.
