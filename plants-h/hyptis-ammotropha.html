Hyptis ammotropha

Hyptis ammotropha, commonly known as the purple hyptis, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It often grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and streambanks.

Hyptis ammotropha is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
