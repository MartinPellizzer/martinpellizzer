Haplopappus radiatus

Haplopappus radiatus, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is a low-growing perennial that features linear, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant has a fibrous root system that helps it survive in dry, sandy soils. Its flowers are typically arranged in a radiate pattern, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Haplopappus radiatus is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited water availability.
