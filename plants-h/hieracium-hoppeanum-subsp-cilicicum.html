Hieracium hoppeanum subsp. cilicicum

Hieracium hoppeanum subsp.

cilicicum, commonly known as the Cilician hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and locally endemic species.
