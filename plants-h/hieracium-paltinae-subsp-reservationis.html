Hieracium paltinae subsp. reservationis

Hieracium paltinae subsp.

reservationis, commonly known as the Reservation Hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of the United States. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and sensitive species.
