Hieracium sabaudum subsp. scabiosum

Hieracium sabaudum subsp.

scabiosum, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features basal leaves that are rough and hairy, with lanceolate shapes, and flowering stems that bear yellow composite flowers. Its inflorescence consists of a flat, disc-like structure with numerous small florets.

It is characterized by its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting pollinators.
