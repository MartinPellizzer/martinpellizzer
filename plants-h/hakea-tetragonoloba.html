Hakea tetragonoloba

Hakea tetragonoloba, commonly known as the four-angle hakea, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, needle-like leaves that are often arranged in four-sided clusters. The plant produces bright red or orange flowers in winter, which are followed by woody, spherical seed pods. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resistance to drought and salt.

Hakea tetragonoloba is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
