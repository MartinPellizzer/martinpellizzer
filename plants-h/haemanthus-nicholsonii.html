Haemanthus nicholsonii

Haemanthus nicholsonii, commonly known as the Natal grassblades, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. During the flowering season, it produces tall, leafless stems topped with clusters of bright red, lantern-shaped flowers. The plant is adapted to grow in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
