Hieracium laevigatum subsp. gothicum

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

gothicum, commonly known as the Gothic hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
