Hieracium soczavae

Hieracium soczavae, commonly known as the Soczava's hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and yellow flower heads composed of numerous small florets. Native to Central and Eastern Europe, this species thrives in meadows and disturbed soils. It has been classified as a rare and endangered plant due to habitat loss and limited distribution.

The plant's ornamental value and ecological significance make it an important subject for conservation efforts.
