Hieracium vulgatum subsp. castuanum

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

castuanum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features basal rosettes of leaves that are usually hairy and have a rough texture. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped cluster, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

The species is characterized by its ability to produce numerous seeds, contributing to its widespread distribution.
