Hebecarpa myrtilloides

Hebecarpa myrtilloides, commonly known as the purple-fruited hebecarpa, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and dark purple, edible berries. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or swampy areas.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
