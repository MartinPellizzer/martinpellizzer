Hydrangea borealis

Hydrangea borealis, commonly known as the northern hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from white to pink and blue, depending on soil pH. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptical, with a serrated margin and a dark green upper surface.

Hydrangea borealis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
