Hoya desvoeuxensis

Hoya desvoeuxensis, commonly known as the Desvoeux's waxplant, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and clusters of star-shaped, fragrant flowers. The plant belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions. Hoya desvoeuxensis is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.

It produces flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, depending on the variety.
