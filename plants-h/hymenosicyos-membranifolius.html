Hymenosicyos membranifolius

Hymenosicyos membranifolius, commonly known as the African eggplant, is a climbing vine native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves with a hairy underside and produces distinctive, egg-like fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or orange when mature. The plant thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and potential medicinal properties.

The fruits are edible when ripe and are used in various culinary and traditional applications across its native regions.
