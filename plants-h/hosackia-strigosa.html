Hosackia strigosa

Hosackia strigosa, commonly known as the woolly false indigo, is a flowering plant native to the United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in prairies and open woodlands. The plant features dark green, hairy leaves and produces clusters of blue to violet flowers. It is part of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

Hosackia strigosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
