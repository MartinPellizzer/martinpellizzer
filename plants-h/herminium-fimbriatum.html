Herminium fimbriatum

Herminium fimbriatum, commonly known as the fringed lady's tresses, is a perennial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and its distinctive, fragrant, white to pale pink flower spikes that resemble a tresses of hair. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in a loose spiral along the stem.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
