Hieracium pratense subsp. rawaruskanum

Hieracium pratense subsp.

rawaruskanum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of yellow flowers.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its alpine habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and rocky slopes at higher altitudes.

This subspecies is noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators in its native environment.
