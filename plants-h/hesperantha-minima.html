Hesperantha minima

Hesperantha minima, commonly known as the dwarf rain lily, is a small, herbaceous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often with a violet or purple streak. The plant typically grows to a height of only a few inches, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Hesperantha minima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a container plant.
