Hyoscyamus albus var. exserens

Hyoscyamus albus var. exserens, commonly known as the white henbane, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that contain toxic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a waxy coating.

Hyoscyamus albus var. exserens is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and contains various alkaloids.
