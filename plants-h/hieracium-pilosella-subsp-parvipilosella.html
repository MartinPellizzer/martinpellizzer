Hieracium pilosella subsp. parvipilosella

Hieracium pilosella subsp.

parvipilosella, commonly known as the small-hairy hawkweed, is a low-growing perennial herb belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small yellow flowers arranged in clusters.

Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this subspecies thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with fine, white hairs, and it produces numerous seeds dispersed by wind.

The plant is often found in open habitats and is relatively common in temperate regions.
