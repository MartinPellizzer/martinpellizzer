Hebe pauciramosa

Hebe pauciramosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hebe, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, or white, typically in spring. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
