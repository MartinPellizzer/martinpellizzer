Hedera helix var. helvetica

Hedera helix var. helvetica, commonly known as the Swiss ivy, is a climbing evergreen vine native to Europe.

It features dark green, leathery leaves that are typically heart-shaped with a deep, prominent vein. This variety is known for its vigorous growth and ability to thrive in shaded environments. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark blue berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

The plant is often used in horticulture for ground cover and as a decorative climbing plant.
