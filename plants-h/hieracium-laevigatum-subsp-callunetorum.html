Hieracium laevigatum subsp. callunetorum

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

callunetorum, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features upright stems covered in dense, white to pale yellow hairs, giving it a woolly appearance. Its basal leaves are broadly lance-shaped with toothed margins, and the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets.
