Hieracium arrectum subsp. grausonicum

Hieracium arrectum subsp.

grausonicum, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically features rough, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a pioneer species in ecological succession.
