Hamelia suaveolens

Hamelia suaveolens, commonly known as the scarlet lantana, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bright orange to red flowers that are highly attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens in warm climates.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to attract wildlife.
