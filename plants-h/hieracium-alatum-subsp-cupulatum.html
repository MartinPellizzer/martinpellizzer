Hieracium alatum subsp. cupulatum

Hieracium alatum subsp.

cupulatum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems, which give it its common name.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming from June to August. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.
