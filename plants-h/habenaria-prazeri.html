Habenaria prazeri

Habenaria prazeri, commonly known as the Prazzer's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its rhizomes are fibrous, and it produces showy, lance-shaped petals that are usually a mix of white, yellow, and green.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
