Hibiscus floridanus

Hibiscus floridanus, commonly known as the Florida hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in wetlands, swamps, and along the edges of ponds, preferring moist, acidic soils. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and often covered in fine hairs.

Hibiscus floridanus blooms from late spring to early fall, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat.
