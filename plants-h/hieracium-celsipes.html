Hieracium celsipes

Hieracium celsipes, commonly known as the tall hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features upright, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its showy yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Hieracium celsipes is often found in meadows, woodlands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
