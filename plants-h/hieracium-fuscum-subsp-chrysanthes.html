Hieracium fuscum subsp. chrysanthes

Hieracium fuscum subsp.

chrysanthes, commonly known as the golden hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features hairy stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often have a reddish or purplish tinge. Its flowers are yellow, with a distinctive central disc surrounded by ray florets, giving it a dandelion-like appearance.

It is notable for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in areas with low nutrient content.
