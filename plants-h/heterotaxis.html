Heterotaxis

Heterotaxis, commonly known as the "twisted stem" plant, is a unique species characterized by its spiral arrangement of leaves and branches.

This growth pattern is a form of phyllotaxis, where the plant's parts are arranged in a logarithmic spiral, often following the Fibonacci sequence. The botanical significance of heterotaxis lies in its efficient use of space and sunlight, allowing for optimal photosynthesis. Its stems exhibit a helical twist, which can aid in structural stability and water transport.

This plant is often studied for its mathematical patterns in nature and its ecological adaptations.
