Hedyosmum domingense var. domingense

Hedyosmum domingense var. domingense, commonly known as the Mexican moonseed, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, simple, ovate to elliptic leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences, followed by elongated, fleshy fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
