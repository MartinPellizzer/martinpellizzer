Hoya linavergarae

Hoya linavergarae, commonly known as the "Coral Hoya," is a species of flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its glossy, waxy leaves and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements. Hoya linavergarae thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for indoor growing conditions.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental value make it a popular choice among succulent and tropical plant enthusiasts.
