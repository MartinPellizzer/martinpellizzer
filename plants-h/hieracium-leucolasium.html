Hieracium leucolasium

Hieracium leucolasium, commonly known as the white-haired hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its pale yellow to white flower heads, which are surrounded by a dense layer of bracts that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. It has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Hieracium leucolasium is known for its ability to thrive in temperate climates and is considered a common wildflower in parts of Europe and North America.
