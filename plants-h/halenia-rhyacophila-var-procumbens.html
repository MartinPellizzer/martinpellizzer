Halenia rhyacophila var. procumbens

Halenia rhyacophila var. procumbens, commonly known as the creeping halenia, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, spreading habit and features small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. The plant has lanceolate to elliptic leaves that are often hairy on the underside, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
