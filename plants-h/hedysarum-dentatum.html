Hedysarum dentatum

Hedysarum dentatum, commonly known as the toothed milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, characterized by its compound leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Hedysarum dentatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
