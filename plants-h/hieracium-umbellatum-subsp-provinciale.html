Hieracium umbellatum subsp. provinciale

Hieracium umbellatum subsp.

provinciale, commonly known as the provincial cow-wheat, is a perennial herb native to Europe. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its yellowish-orange flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows. It has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, inconspicuous seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is often found in the western parts of Europe, including the British Isles.
