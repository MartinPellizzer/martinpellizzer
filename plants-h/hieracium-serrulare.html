Hieracium serrulare

Hieracium serrulare, commonly known as the serrated hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are serrated and alternate, with a rough texture, and its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Hieracium serrulare is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in agricultural and natural areas.
