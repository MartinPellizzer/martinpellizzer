Haworthia mucronata f. subglauca

Haworthia mucronata f. subglauca, commonly known as the blue-tipped Haworthia, is a compact, slow-growing succulent native to South Africa.

It features rosettes of fleshy, triangular leaves that are typically a mix of green and blue-gray, with a powdery coating that gives it a distinctive, waxy appearance. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white or pale yellow during the warmer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for indoor cultivation.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
