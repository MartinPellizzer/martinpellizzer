Hypericum boeticum

Hypericum boeticum, commonly known as the lesser St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom from June to August, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Hypericum boeticum is often found in dry, sunny habitats like grasslands and rocky soils.
