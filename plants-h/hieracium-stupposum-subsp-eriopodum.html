Hieracium stupposum subsp. eriopodum

Hieracium stupposum subsp.

eriopodum, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads made up of numerous small florets.

The plant features a basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, typically blooming from June to August.

It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands, and is often found in disturbed soils.
