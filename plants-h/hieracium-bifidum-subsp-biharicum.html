Hieracium bifidum subsp. biharicum

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

biharicum, commonly known as the Bihar hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces yellow composite flowers with ray florets.

The plant is native to the Himalayas and is often found in alpine and subalpine regions. It has a fibrous root system and is adapted to grow in rocky and well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and naturalized in certain areas.
