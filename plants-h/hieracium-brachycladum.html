Hieracium brachycladum

Hieracium brachycladum, commonly known as the short-stemmed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat, disk-like structure.

Hieracium brachycladum is known for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions, making it a common sight in high-altitude ecosystems.
