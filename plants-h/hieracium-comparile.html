Hieracium comparile

Hieracium comparile, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems, typically growing in meadows and disturbed soils. The plant has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, oval seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Hieracium comparile is an invasive species in many regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
