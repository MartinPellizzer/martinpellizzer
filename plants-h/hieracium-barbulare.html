Hieracium barbulare

Hieracium barbulare, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, rugged appearance. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Its growth habit and morphology make it a common sight in meadows and along roadsides.
