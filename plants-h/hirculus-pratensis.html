Hirculus pratensis

Hirculus pratensis, commonly known as the meadow hirculus, is a herbaceous plant native to temperate regions.

It typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Hirculus pratensis plays a role in supporting local insect populations and is often found in ecological restoration projects.
