Hieracium erythrocarpum subsp. caloprasinum

Hieracium erythrocarpum subsp.

caloprasinum, commonly known as the red-fruited hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers and reddish, fleshy fruits.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while the stems are upright and usually unbranched.

This subspecies is notable for its distinctive fruit morphology and is often found in association with other members of the Hieracium genus.
