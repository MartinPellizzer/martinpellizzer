Hieracium cruentum subsp. naegelii

Hieracium cruentum subsp.

naegelii, commonly known as the blood dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its bright orange to red flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has basal and stem leaves that are typically heart-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in open woodlands.

This subspecies is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
