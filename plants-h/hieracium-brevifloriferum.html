Hieracium brevifloriferum

Hieracium brevifloriferum, commonly known as the short-flowered hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head.

It is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
