Hesperis odoratissima

Hesperis odoratissima, commonly known as the sweet rocket, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers that typically bloom in early summer. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and slender, branching stems that bear clusters of bright yellow or white flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers emit a subtle, sweet fragrance.

Hesperis odoratissima is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
