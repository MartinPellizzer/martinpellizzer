Homalomena rosea

Homalomena rosea, commonly known as the red-veined begonia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with deep red veins against a dark green background. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires indirect light to flourish.

Due to its attractive foliage, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
