Hoppea puranii

Hoppea puranii, commonly known as the Himalayan hoppea, is a small evergreen tree native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Theaceae and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces woody capsules that contain numerous elliptical seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters.

Hoppea puranii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
