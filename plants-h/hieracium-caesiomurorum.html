Hieracium caesiomurorum

Hieracium caesiomurorum, commonly known as the blue mouse ear, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are hairy and heart-shaped, while its flowers are small and blue or purple in color.

Hieracium caesiomurorum is often found in alpine environments and is adapted to cool, moist conditions.
