Handroanthus impetiginosus var. lepidotus

Handroanthus impetiginosus var. lepidotus, commonly known as the golden trumpet tree, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, featuring a straight trunk and a broad, spreading crown. The plant is characterized by its striking yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a leathery texture with a glossy surface.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
