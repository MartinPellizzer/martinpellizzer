Hepetis kalbreyeri

Hepetis kalbreyeri, commonly known as the Kalbreyer's heath, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in native plant gardens.
