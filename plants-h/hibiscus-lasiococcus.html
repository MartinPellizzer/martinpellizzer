Hibiscus lasiococcus

Hibiscus lasiococcus, commonly known as the woolly hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers bloom in summer and are often visited by bees and other pollinators.

Hibiscus lasiococcus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
