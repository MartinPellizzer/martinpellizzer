Hibiscus rostellatus var. congolanus

Hibiscus rostellatus var. congolanus, commonly known as the Congo hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and red. The plant features heart-shaped, broad leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in moist forest understories.

The flowers are known to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
