Hakea molyneuxii

Hakea molyneuxii, commonly known as the Molyneux's hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are arranged in spikes. It is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for dry landscapes.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
