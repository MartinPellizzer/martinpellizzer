Habenaria silvatica

Habenaria silvatica, commonly known as the forest orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and woodlands. The plant features a slender, erect stem with a single or few leaves, and it produces small, greenish or pale yellow flowers. Its roots are often mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to enhance nutrient uptake.

The botanical characteristics of Habenaria silvatica make it well adapted to its specific ecological niche.
