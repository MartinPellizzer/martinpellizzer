Hieracium nigriceps subsp. floridum

Hieracium nigriceps subsp.

floridum, commonly known as the Florida blackhead hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, and has a rosette of basal leaves with hairy surfaces. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a dense cluster, and it produces achenes as its primary seed dispersal method.

The species is notable for its adaptability to moist environments and its role in local ecosystems.
