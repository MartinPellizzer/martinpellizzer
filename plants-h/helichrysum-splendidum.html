Helichrysum splendidum

Helichrysum splendidum, commonly known as the golden marguerite or the Cape goldcup, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that resemble small sunbursts. The plant has narrow, silvery-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Helichrysum splendidum is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
