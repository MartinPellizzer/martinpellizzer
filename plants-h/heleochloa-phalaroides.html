Heleochloa phalaroides

Heleochloa phalaroides, commonly known as the narrowleaf false brome, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and stream banks. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are usually green to reddish in color.

Heleochloa phalaroides plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
