Hakea inconspicua

Hakea inconspicua, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hakea, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, cylindrical flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant.

Hakea inconspicua is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.
