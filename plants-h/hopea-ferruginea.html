Hopea ferruginea

Hopea ferruginea, commonly known as the red ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard, and heavy wood, which is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay. The tree has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical and often have a reddish or brownish hue. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits containing winged seeds.

Hopea ferruginea plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
