Hypoxis tomentosa

Hypoxis tomentosa, commonly known as the wooly false aloe, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a short, thick rhizome and produces rosettes of narrow, gray-green, hairy leaves. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers arranged in a dense, spherical inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for dry garden environments.

The leaves and flowers are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
