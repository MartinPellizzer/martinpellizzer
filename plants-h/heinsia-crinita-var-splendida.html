Heinsia crinita var. splendida

Heinsia crinita var. splendida, commonly known as the golden heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant yellow blooms, which appear in late summer.

It is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a container plant due to its compact growth habit.
