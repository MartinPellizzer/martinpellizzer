Hylophila rubra

Hylophila rubra, commonly known as the red-stemmed waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It has slender, branching stems that are typically reddish in color, which help it absorb light in shallow waters. Its small, opposite leaves are usually oval or lance-shaped and have a smooth surface. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, followed by small, round fruits.

Hylophila rubra plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
