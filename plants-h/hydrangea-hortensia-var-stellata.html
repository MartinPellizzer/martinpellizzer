Hydrangea hortensia var. stellata

Hydrangea hortensia var. stellata, commonly known as the star hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its conical or spherical clusters of sterile flowers that range in color from white to pink, depending on soil pH. The plant produces showy, lacecap blooms in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, dark green leaves that turn yellow in the fall.

It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
