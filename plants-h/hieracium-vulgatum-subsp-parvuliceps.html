Hieracium vulgatum subsp. parvuliceps

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

parvuliceps, commonly known as the small-headed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.

The plant features basal rosettes of leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in clusters. Its flowering period occurs from late spring to early summer, and it is characterized by its small, rounded flower heads.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
