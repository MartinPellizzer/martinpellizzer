Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp. tanypterum

Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp.

tanypterum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering stems that bear yellow-orange composite flowers.

The leaves are typically lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, while the flower heads consist of both ray and disc florets. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting pollinator populations.
