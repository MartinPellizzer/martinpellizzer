Hieracium erythrocarpum subsp. amphitephrodes

Hieracium erythrocarpum subsp.

amphitephrodes, commonly known as the red-fruited hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems.

The subspecies is distinguished by its red or orange-colored fruiting structures, which are unique among the Hieracium species. It typically grows in grassy areas and open habitats, preferring well-drained soils.
