Hetaeria callosa

Hetaeria callosa, commonly known as the woolly hetaeria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a waxy coating.

Hetaeria callosa is adapted to arid environments and is often found in open woodlands and sagebrush steppe.
