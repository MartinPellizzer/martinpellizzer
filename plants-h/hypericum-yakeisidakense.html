Hypericum yakeisidakense

Hypericum yakeisidakense, commonly known as the Yakeisidakense St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the family Hypericaceae and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and woody stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas and is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually oval-shaped with a slightly serrated edge, and it produces small, orange-red berries in the fall.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique habitat and potential medicinal properties.
