Helleborus purpurascens

Helleborus purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-leaf hellebore, is a perennial plant native to southern and eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen, leathery leaves that are typically dark green with a purple tinge. The plant produces clusters of striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical medicinal uses.

Helleborus purpurascens is also noted for its toxicity, containing various alkaloids that can be harmful if ingested.
