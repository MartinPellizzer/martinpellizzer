Hydrangea viburnifolia

Hydrangea viburnifolia, commonly known as the panicle hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves that are opposite and ovate, with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, cone-shaped flower clusters that range in color from white to pink, depending on soil pH. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its adaptability and seasonal interest make it a valuable addition to native plant landscapes.
