Helia alba

Helia alba, commonly known as the white helia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and have a smooth texture.

Helia alba is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
