Hordeum vulgare var. zeocrithideficiens

Hordeum vulgare var. zeocrithideficiens, commonly known as the zeocrithideficiens barley, is a variant of barley characterized by its unique botanical features.

It is a grass species belonging to the Poaceae family, typically growing in temperate climates. The plant exhibits reduced chlorophyll content, leading to a lighter green coloration compared to standard barley varieties. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, with a prominent midrib, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

This variant is of interest in botanical studies due to its genetic and physiological traits.
