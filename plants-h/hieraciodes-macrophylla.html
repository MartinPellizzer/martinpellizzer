Hieraciodes macrophylla

Hieraciodes macrophylla, commonly known as the broad-leafed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and can grow up to 6 inches in length. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
