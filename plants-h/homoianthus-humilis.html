Homoianthus humilis

Homoianthus humilis, commonly known as the dwarf bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in spring. The plant has slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its compact size and ornamental flowers, it is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and ease of cultivation.
