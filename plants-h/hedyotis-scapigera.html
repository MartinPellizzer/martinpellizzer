Hedyotis scapigera

Hedyotis scapigera, commonly known as the scarlet root or red root, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, including China, India, and Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant has slender, often hairy stems that are usually less than 30 cm in height. Its small, bright red flowers are clustered in racemes and bloom from spring to early summer.

The roots are tuberous and have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.
