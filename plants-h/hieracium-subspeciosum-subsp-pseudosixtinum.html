Hieracium subspeciosum subsp. pseudosixtinum

Hieracium subspeciosum subsp.

pseudosixtinum, commonly known as the showy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. It has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and its flowering stems are usually unbranched.

This subspecies is distinguished by its slightly larger flowers and more elongated bracts compared to other members of the subspecies.
