Hypericum japonicum var. accumbens

Hypericum japonicum var. accumbens, commonly known as Japanese St. John's Wort, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to East Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant has opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its medicinal properties have been studied for their potential anti-inflammatory and antidepressant effects.
