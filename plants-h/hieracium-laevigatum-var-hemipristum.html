Hieracium laevigatum var. hemipristum

Hieracium laevigatum var. hemipristum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 40 cm. The plant features basal leaves that are narrow and linear, with a rough texture, and produces small yellow flowers in clusters. Its flowering period occurs from late spring to early autumn, and it is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some traditional uses.
