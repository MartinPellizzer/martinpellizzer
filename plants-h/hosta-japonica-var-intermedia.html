Hosta japonica var. intermedia

Hosta japonica var. intermedia, commonly known as the Japanese hosta, is a perennial plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a silvery-blue margin. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in late summer, usually in shades of white or pale blue. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental foliage and is commonly used in shade gardens.
