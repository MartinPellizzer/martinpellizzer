Heterotheca sessiliflora subsp. fastigiata

Heterotheca sessiliflora subsp.

fastigiata, commonly known as the stiff-stemmed goldenweed, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of yellow ray flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and sessile, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
