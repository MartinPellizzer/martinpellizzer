Hygrophila oblongifolia var. laxior

Hygrophila oblongifolia var. laxior, commonly known as the broadleaf hygrophila, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along its stem. The plant thrives in slow-moving or still freshwater environments, such as ponds and marshes. Its flowering spikes bear small, clustered flowers that are often pink or white in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide habitat for aquatic life.
