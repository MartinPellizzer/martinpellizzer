Hippeastrum blumenavia

Hippeastrum blumenavia, commonly known as the Brazilian belladonna lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It features large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a distinctive orange or yellow throat. The plant produces sword-shaped, strap-like leaves that are broad and smooth. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Hippeastrum blumenavia is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family, which includes other popular ornamental plants like amaryllis.
