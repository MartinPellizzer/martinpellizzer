Hieracium pseudarnelli

Hieracium pseudarnelli, commonly known as the false hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped with serrated edges. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grasslands.

Hieracium pseudarnelli is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and can sometimes become invasive in certain regions.
