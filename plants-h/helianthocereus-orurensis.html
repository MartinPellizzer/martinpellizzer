Helianthocereus orurensis

Helianthocereus orurensis, commonly known as the yellow night-blooming cereus, is a cactus species native to South America.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and produces large, showy yellow flowers that open at night. The plant is a succulent, adapted to arid environments with thick, fleshy stems that store water. Its flowers have a strong, sweet scent to attract nocturnal pollinators like moths.

The plant is relatively easy to care for, requiring minimal water and well-draining soil.
