Hibiscus denudatus var. involucellatus

Hibiscus denudatus var. involucellatus, commonly known as the woolly hibiscus, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features hairy, glandular leaves that are often covered in a dense, white pubescence. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink to red in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along riverbanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental species in horticulture.
