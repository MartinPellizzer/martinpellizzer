Hydrotrophus echinospermus

Hydrotrophus echinospermus, commonly known as the spiny-seed water-lover, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its specialized adaptations for water environments.

It possesses elongated, ribbon-like leaves that maximize surface area for photosynthesis in low-light aquatic conditions. The plant's most distinctive feature is its spiky, seed-bearing structure, which aids in dispersal by attaching to the fur of animals or the hulls of boats. It thrives in slow-moving freshwater habitats, such as ponds and wetlands, where it can anchor itself to substrates.

Due to its rare occurrence, it is considered a valuable indicator of water quality and ecological health.
