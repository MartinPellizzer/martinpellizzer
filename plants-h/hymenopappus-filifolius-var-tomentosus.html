Hymenopappus filifolius var. tomentosus

Hymenopappus filifolius var. tomentosus, commonly known as the silky hymenopappus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green foliage and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a compact growth habit and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. Its leaves are typically linear and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often found in rocky or arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
