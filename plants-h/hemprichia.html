Hemprichia

Hemprichia, commonly known as the sea holly, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its striking, spiny leaves and showy, often violet or red flowers. These plants are typically found in coastal areas and saline environments, showcasing their adaptability to harsh conditions. Hemprichia species are often used in ornamental gardening due to their unique appearance.

The genus includes several species, with Hemprichia tetrandra being one of the most well-known.
