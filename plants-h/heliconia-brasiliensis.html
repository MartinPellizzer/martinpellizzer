Heliconia brasiliensis

Heliconia brasiliensis, commonly known as the red heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its large, brightly colored bracts that resemble flowers. The plant's inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by long, fleshy bracts in shades of red, orange, and yellow. Heliconia brasiliensis thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its unique structure makes it an important plant for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
