Hyssopus caucasicus

Hyssopus caucasicus, commonly known as Caucasian hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a strong, mint-like scent.

Hyssopus caucasicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
