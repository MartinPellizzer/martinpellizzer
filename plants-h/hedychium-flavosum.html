Hedychium flavosum

Hedychium flavosum, commonly known as the golden ginger, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and large, fragrant, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant's leaves are broad and lance-shaped, contributing to its lush appearance. Hedychium flavosum thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
