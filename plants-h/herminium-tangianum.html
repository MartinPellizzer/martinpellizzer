Herminium tangianum

Herminium tangianum, commonly known as the Tangian orchid, is a rare and endangered species native to the mountainous regions of Namibia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and requires a cool, moist environment to thrive. Its unique floral structure and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss and climate change, it faces significant threats to its survival.
