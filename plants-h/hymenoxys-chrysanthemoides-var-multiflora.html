Hymenoxys chrysanthemoides var. multiflora

Hymenoxys chrysanthemoides var. multiflora, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, yellow-green leaves and produces numerous small, yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes. It is characterized by its hairy stems and showy, composite-like inflorescences.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
