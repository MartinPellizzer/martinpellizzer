Hieracium murorum subsp. submundulum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

submundulum, commonly known as the lesser spearthistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typically hairy foliage.

The plant has narrow, upright stems and produces small, yellowish to brownish composite flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and wastelands.
