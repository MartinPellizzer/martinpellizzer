Hieracium chloroleucolepium

Hieracium chloroleucolepium, commonly known as the white-veined hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences. The plant is native to Europe and is often found in meadows and grassy areas. It has small, glandular hairs on its leaves and stems, which contribute to its distinctive appearance.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
