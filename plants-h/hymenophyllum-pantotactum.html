Hymenophyllum pantotactum

Hymenophyllum pantotactum, commonly known as the broad-leaved spikemoss, is a fern that belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its small, delicate, and often overlapping leaves that form dense mats on the forest floor. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures, known as sporangia, are clustered in spore-bearing clusters called sori.

Hymenophyllum pantotactum plays an important ecological role in maintaining soil moisture and providing habitat for microorganisms.
