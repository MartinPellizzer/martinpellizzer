Habenaria ambigua

Habenaria ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its erect, flowering stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, often with a distinctive lip that is broader than the other petals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and orchid enthusiasts.
