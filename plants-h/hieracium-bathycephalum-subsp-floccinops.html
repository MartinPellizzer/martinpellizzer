Hieracium bathycephalum subsp. floccinops

Hieracium bathycephalum subsp.

floccinops, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its dense, fluffy white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are typically hairy and grayish-green in color. It thrives in dry, sunny environments such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and often invasive species in its native habitats.
