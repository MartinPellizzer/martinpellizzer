Hoarea ornithopifolia

Hoarea ornithopifolia, commonly known as the golden cholla, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spineless, cylindrical stems that are covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its green, fleshy structure. Its ability to store water in its stems allows it to thrive in harsh, dry environments.

Hoarea ornithopifolia is often used in xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and drought tolerance.
