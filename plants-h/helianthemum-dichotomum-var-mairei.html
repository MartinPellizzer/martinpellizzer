Helianthemum dichotomum var. mairei

Helianthemum dichotomum var. mairei, commonly known as the Maire's prickly rockrose, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, typically in clusters at the ends of the stems. The plant has greyish, hairy leaves that are often divided into two parts, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in harsh conditions.
