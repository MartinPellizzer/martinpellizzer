Hemionitis cyclophylla

Hemionitis cyclophylla, commonly known as the cyclophyll hemionitis, is a rare and specialized fern native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring small, delicate fronds with a distinctive, spirally arranged structure. The plant thrives in rocky, dry habitats and is adapted to withstand extreme environmental conditions. Its reproductive structures are highly specialized, with spore-producing sorus clusters located on the underside of the fronds.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, Hemionitis cyclophylla is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
