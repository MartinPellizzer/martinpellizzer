Heliconia psittacorum

Heliconia psittacorum, commonly known as the parrot flower, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, brightly colored bracts that resemble parrots, hence its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers inside the brightly colored bracts. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

Heliconia psittacorum thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-draining soil and bright, indirect light.
