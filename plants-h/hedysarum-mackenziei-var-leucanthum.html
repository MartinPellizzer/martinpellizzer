Hedysarum mackenziei var. leucanthum

Hedysarum mackenziei var. leucanthum, commonly known as the white-flowered sweetvernal, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, showy white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system is well-developed, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun conditions.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
