Hopea glabrifolia

Hopea glabrifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leaved hopea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark-brown bark and large, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, oblong fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, lowland tropical forests and is valued for its durable timber.

Hopea glabrifolia plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, supporting various forms of wildlife.
