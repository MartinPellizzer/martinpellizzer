Hakea ceratophylla var. tricuspis

Hakea ceratophylla var. tricuspis, commonly known as the three-lobed hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, three-lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and fire resistance.

The species is of interest in horticulture for its ornamental value and ecological importance in native ecosystems.
