Hieracium juranum subsp. oleicoloriforme

Hieracium juranum subsp.

oleicoloriforme, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow to orange-yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while the stems are upright and often branched.

The subspecies is distinguished by its vividly colored flowers and is of interest to botanists due to its ecological and genetic traits.
