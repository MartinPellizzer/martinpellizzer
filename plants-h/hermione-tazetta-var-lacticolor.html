Hermione tazetta var. lacticolor

Hermione tazetta var. lacticolor, commonly known as the milk-colored narcissus, is a variant of the daffodil species native to southern Europe.

It features striking white petals with a pale yellow center, giving it a soft, milky appearance. The plant produces a single, upright flower stem with several blooms, each surrounded by a cup-shaped perianth. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking early spring blooms.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its fragrance, which adds a subtle aroma to the garden.
