Hymenophyllum krauseanum

Hymenophyllum krauseanum, commonly known as the Krause's spore moss, is a small, epiphytic fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, narrow, and often hairy leaves that are typically less than 2 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or rocks in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the leaves and are protected by a specialized covering known as a indusium.

This species is notable for its intricate leaf anatomy and is often found in humid, high-altitude habitats.
