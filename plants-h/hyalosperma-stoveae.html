Hyalosperma stoveae

Hyalosperma stoveae, commonly known as the Stove's glassy grass, is a member of the Poaceae family, characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia, where it thrives in well-drained soils. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and often covered with a fine layer of wax, which helps reduce water loss in harsh environments.

Hyalosperma stoveae is adapted to survive in low-nutrient soils and can enter a dormant state during periods of drought.
