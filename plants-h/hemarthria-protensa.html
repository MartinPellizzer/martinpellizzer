Hemarthria protensa

Hemarthria protensa, commonly known as common hairgrass, is a perennial grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted blades and dense, sod-forming growth habit. The plant has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Hemarthria protensa is often used in restoration projects due to its adaptability and ecological benefits.
