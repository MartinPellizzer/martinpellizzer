Hibiscus striatus subsp. striatus

Hibiscus striatus subsp.

striatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf hibiscus, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves with prominent veins and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or near freshwater sources. Hibiscus striatus subsp.

striatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
