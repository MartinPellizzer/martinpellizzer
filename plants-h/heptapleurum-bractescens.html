Heptapleurum bractescens

Heptapleurum bractescens, commonly known as the broadleaf holly, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant features opposite, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green on top, with a lighter underside. It produces small, white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by red berries that are edible for humans but toxic to livestock.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
