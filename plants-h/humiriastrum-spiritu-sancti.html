Humiriastrum spiritu-sancti

Humiriastrum spiritu-sancti, commonly known as the sacred spirit lily, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family, native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting a variety of pollinators. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
