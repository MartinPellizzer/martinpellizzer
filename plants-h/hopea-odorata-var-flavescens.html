Hopea odorata var. flavescens

Hopea odorata var. flavescens, commonly known as the yellow-flowered hopea, is a species of flowering plant in the family Dipterocarpaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value. Its leaves are typically alternate, elliptical, and have a glossy surface.

This variant is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is also valued for its timber in local communities.
