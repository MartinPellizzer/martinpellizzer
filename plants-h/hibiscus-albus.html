Hibiscus albus

Hibiscus albus, commonly known as the white hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Africa, particularly found in regions like Sudan and Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers with five petals and a prominent yellow stamen. The plant is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, with smooth, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in wetlands or along riverbanks.

The flowers are not only ornamental but also have traditional uses in herbal medicine.
