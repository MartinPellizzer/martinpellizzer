Hydnophytum bracteatum

Hydnophytum bracteatum, commonly known as the mistletoe fern, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of trees in tropical regions.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while still performing some photosynthesis using its green leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often pollinated by insects. Its fruit develops into a capsule that releases tiny seeds, which can adhere to the bark of host trees.

Hydnophytum bracteatum plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat by influencing host tree growth and providing food for certain wildlife species.
