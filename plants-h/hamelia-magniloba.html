Hamelia magniloba

Hamelia magniloba, commonly known as the Mexican flame bush, is a deciduous shrub native to Central America and northern South America.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular red flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and velvety to the touch. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

Hamelia magniloba is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract hummingbirds and other pollinators.
