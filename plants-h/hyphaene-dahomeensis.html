Hyphaene dahomeensis

Hyphaene dahomeensis, commonly known as the African palm or the oil palm, is a species of palm native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach several meters in diameter. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clustered fruits containing edible seeds. Its fibrous leaves are used for thatching and basket weaving, while the seeds are processed into oil.

Hyphaene dahomeensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
