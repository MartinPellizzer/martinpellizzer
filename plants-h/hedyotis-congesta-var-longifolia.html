Hedyotis congesta var. longifolia

Hedyotis congesta var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved blushing root, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, wiry stems that often grow in dense clusters, with small, opposite leaves that are usually ovate to lanceolate in shape. The flowers are tiny, white or pale yellow, and are clustered in dense inflorescences, giving the plant a delicate appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

The plant is noted for its medicinal properties, with various traditional uses in herbal medicine.
