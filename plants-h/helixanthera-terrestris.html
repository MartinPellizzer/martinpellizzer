Helixanthera terrestris

Helixanthera terrestris, commonly known as the creeping helixanthera, is a low-growing perennial plant native to certain regions of southern Africa.

It features small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color, arranged in clusters along its prostrate stems. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain water in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its unique floral structure attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
