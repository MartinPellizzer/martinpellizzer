Hedera helix var. crenata

Hedera helix var. crenata, commonly known as the baby's breath ivy, is a compact, trailing evergreen vine native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It features small, rounded, dark green leaves with a waxy texture and a slightly curled margin, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring, followed by dark blue berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for hanging baskets, rock gardens, or as a ground cover.

Due to its slow growth and dense foliage, it is often used in decorative landscaping for its ornamental value.
