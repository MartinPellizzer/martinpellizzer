Hieracium flagellare var. cernuiforme

Hieracium flagellare var. cernuiforme, commonly known as the weeping hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features slender, often drooping stems and bright yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along roadsides.
