Hieracium schmidtii subsp. curcicianum

Hieracium schmidtii subsp.

curcicianum, commonly known as the Armenian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its restricted distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
