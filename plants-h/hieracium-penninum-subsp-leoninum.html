Hieracium penninum subsp. leoninum

Hieracium penninum subsp.

leoninum, commonly known as the lion's head hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe. It features bright yellow flowers arranged in a single, showy capitulum, often with a distinctive reddish-orange center.

The plant has rough, hairy leaves that are typically lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in sunny, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands, and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural gardens.
