Halothamnus heptapotamicus var. hispidulus

Halothamnus heptapotamicus var. hispidulus, commonly known as the hispid halothamnus, is a halophytic shrub adapted to saline and arid environments.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant exhibits a low-growing habit with dense, hairy foliage that helps reduce water loss. It thrives in coastal and inland salt marshes, often forming dense stands in disturbed or saline soils.

Its reproductive structures are small and clustered, typical of many halophytes, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in its harsh habitat.
