Heliconia pogonantha var. holerythra

Heliconia pogonantha var. holerythra, commonly known as the red-tongued heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, boat-shaped inflorescences. The plant features long, narrow leaves that form a rosette, and its flowers are typically orange-red with a distinctive red throat. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

The species is also valued for its role in supporting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.
