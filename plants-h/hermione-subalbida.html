Hermione subalbida

Hermione subalbida, commonly known as the white-flowered orchid, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, white petals and a subtle fragrance. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often near streams or in dense undergrowth. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the forest floor.

Hermione subalbida is prized in horticulture for its beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
