Hydrochloa carolinensis

Hydrochloa carolinensis, commonly known as Carolina waterpennywort, is a small aquatic plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. The plant has narrow, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water surface, often forming dense mats. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by insects.

Hydrochloa carolinensis plays a role in stabilizing water ecosystems by providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
