Homalomena latifrons

Homalomena latifrons, commonly known as the broad-leafed arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves that are broad and often have a waxy texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its rhizomes are also used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.
