Hieracium galactinum

Hieracium galactinum, commonly known as the milk thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple to pink flowers and spiny, lobed leaves. The plant contains a milky sap, which is rich in compounds like silymarin, known for its potential medicinal properties. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and wastelands.

Hieracium galactinum is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
