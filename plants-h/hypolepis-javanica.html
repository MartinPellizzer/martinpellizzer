Hypolepis javanica

Hypolepis javanica, commonly known as the Java shield fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, shield-shaped fronds that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant has a short, creeping rhizome and produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Hypolepis javanica is valued for its ornamental foliage and is commonly cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
