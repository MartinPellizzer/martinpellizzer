Hieracium incisum var. montafonense

Hieracium incisum var. montafonense, commonly known as the Montafon hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy slopes, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a rough, hairy texture, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

This variant is distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and morphological traits adapted to its high-altitude habitat.
