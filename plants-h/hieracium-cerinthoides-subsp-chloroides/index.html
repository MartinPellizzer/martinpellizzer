Hieracium Cerinthoides Subsp. Chloroides

Hieracium cerinthoides subsp. chloroides is a lesser-known member of the dandelion family known for its delicate yellow flowers and unique leaf structure. Found primarily in the mountainous regions of Europe, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its scientific name reflects its close relation to other hawkweeds, yet it stands out with its distinct greenish-yellow hue. Despite its beauty, it remains underappreciated in botanical circles. This article explores its characteristics, habitat, and ecological significance.
