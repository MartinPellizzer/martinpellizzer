Hieracium amphitrichum

Hieracium amphitrichum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are usually hairy, and the flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disk.

Hieracium amphitrichum is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in some regions.
