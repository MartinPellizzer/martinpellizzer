Hieracium nesiotes

Hieracium nesiotes, commonly known as the island groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, showing a preference for well-drained soils. It has hairy, lobed leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Hieracium nesiotes is often found in fragmented habitats, making it a species of interest for conservation efforts.
