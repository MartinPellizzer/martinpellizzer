Hieracium glabratum subsp. acrovillosum

Hieracium glabratum subsp.

acrovillosum, commonly known as the acrovillosum hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of yellow disk flowers surrounded by ray florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It has a fibrous root system and produces seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread in suitable environments.
