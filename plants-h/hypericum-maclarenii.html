Hypericum maclarenii

Hypericum maclarenii, commonly known as the MacLaren's St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, characterized by its woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in the summer, which are composed of numerous small, showy petals. Its botanical features include the presence of yellow, glandular dots on the stems and leaves, which are characteristic of the Hypericaceae family.

Hypericum maclarenii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
