Herorchis morio subsp. picta

Herorchis morio subsp.

picta, commonly known as the painted orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that often display intricate patterns and vibrant hues.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually grass-like and arranged in a rosette formation.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in botanical gardens.
