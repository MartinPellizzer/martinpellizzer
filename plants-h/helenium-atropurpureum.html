Helenium atropurpureum

Helenium atropurpureum, commonly known as the bloodroot helenium, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, cone-shaped flower heads. The plant features upright, grass-like foliage and produces vibrant orange to red blooms in late summer. Its botanical name reflects its dark purple stem and showy flowers.

Helenium atropurpureum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
