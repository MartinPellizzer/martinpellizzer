Hamamelis japonica var. flavopurpurascens

Hamamelis japonica var. flavopurpurascens, commonly known as the Japanese witch hazel with yellowish-purple flowers.

This variant is native to Japan and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in late autumn. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth margin and a glossy green color. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a hardy ornamental shrub.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscapes for its seasonal interest.
