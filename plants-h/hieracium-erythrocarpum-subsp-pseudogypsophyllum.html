Hieracium erythrocarpum subsp. pseudogypsophyllum

Hieracium erythrocarpum subsp.

pseudogypsophyllum, commonly known as the red-fruited hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement.

The plant typically grows in dry, calcareous soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are usually lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat, disc-like structure.

The subspecies is distinguished by its red-tinged fruits and specific ecological adaptations to its habitat.
