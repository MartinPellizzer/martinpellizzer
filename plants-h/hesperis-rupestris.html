Hesperis rupestris

Hesperis rupestris, commonly known as the rock scabious, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Hesperis rupestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators in its native ecosystems.
