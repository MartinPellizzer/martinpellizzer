Hieracium laevigatum subsp. faeroense

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

faeroense, commonly known as the Faeroese hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Faeroe Islands. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous grasslands and coastal areas. It has hairy stems and leaves that are often covered with glandular dots.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and protected species in its native range.
