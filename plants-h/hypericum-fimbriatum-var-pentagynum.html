Hypericum fimbriatum var. pentagynum

Hypericum fimbriatum var. pentagynum, commonly known as the five-lobed St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its opposite, elliptic to ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces yellow flowers with five stamens, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are typically arranged in cymes and are often visited by bees and other pollinators.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties, though it contains compounds that can be toxic if ingested in large quantities.
