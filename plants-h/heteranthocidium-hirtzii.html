Heteranthocidium hirtzii

Heteranthocidium hirtzii, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually blue or violet in color. Its leaves are hairy and lance-shaped, contributing to its common name.

Heteranthocidium hirtzii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.
