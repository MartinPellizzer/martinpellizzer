Hieracium stiriacum

Hieracium stiriacum, commonly known as the Carpathian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, forest edges, and along riverbanks, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium stiriacum plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in habitats with well-drained, nutrient-rich soils.
