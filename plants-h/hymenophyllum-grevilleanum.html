Hymenophyllum grevilleanum

Hymenophyllum grevilleanum, commonly known as the Greville's filmy fern, is a small, epiphytic fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent fronds that are often covered in a fine, waxy layer, which helps reduce water loss. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found on tree trunks or rocks in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and are protected by a specialized covering.

This species is notable for its intricate morphology and ecological adaptation to moist, nutrient-poor habitats.
