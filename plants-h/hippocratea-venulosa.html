Hippocratea venulosa

Hippocratea venulosa, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow petals and intricate floral structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are highly fragrant and attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and moths.
