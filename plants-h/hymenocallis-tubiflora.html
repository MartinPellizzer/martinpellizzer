Hymenocallis tubiflora

Hymenocallis tubiflora, commonly known as the tuberous ginger lily, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from white to yellow and orange. The plant has a pseudostem composed of tightly packed leaves, and it produces edible tubers underground. Its foliage is broad and sword-like, and it thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

Hymenocallis tubiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
