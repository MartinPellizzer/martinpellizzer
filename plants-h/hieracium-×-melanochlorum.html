Hieracium × melanochlorum

Hieracium × melanochlorum, commonly known as the blackleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in open woodlands. It is an invasive species in some regions due to its rapid spread and ability to outcompete native vegetation.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and weed management programs.
