Hedysarum callichroum

Hedysarum callichroum, commonly known as the yellow peavine, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compound leaves with three to five oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers in clusters. The plant is characterized by its twining habit and is often found in dry, rocky soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Hedysarum callichroum is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
