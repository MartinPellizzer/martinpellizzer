Hieracium procerum subsp. macrochaetium

Hieracium procerum subsp.

macrochaetium, commonly known as the tall hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems, which are often more pronounced in this subspecies.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is often found in grassy habitats and along roadsides, where it thrives in disturbed soils.
