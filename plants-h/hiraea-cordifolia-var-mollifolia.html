Hiraea cordifolia var. mollifolia

Hiraea cordifolia var. mollifolia, commonly known as the soft-leaved heartleaf hiraea, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are soft and velvety to the touch. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are small and clustered, often in greenish-white or pale yellow hues.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in shaded garden environments.
