Hierochloe hookeri

Hierochloe hookeri, commonly known as Hooker's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. Its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow leaves are adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Hierochloe hookeri plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small wildlife in its native range.
