Hibiscus serratifolius

Hibiscus serratifolius, commonly known as the serrated-leaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are often visited by bees and other pollinators.

Hibiscus serratifolius is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
