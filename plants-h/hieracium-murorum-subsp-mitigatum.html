Hieracium murorum subsp. mitigatum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

mitigatum, commonly known as the lesser mouse-ear hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with hairy stems and basal leaves that are often heart-shaped.

The plant produces small, yellowish to orange flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

This subspecies is distinguished by its reduced leaf size and more compact growth compared to other members of the species.
