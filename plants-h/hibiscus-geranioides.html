Hibiscus geranioides

Hibiscus geranioides, commonly known as the rock hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Hibiscus geranioides is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and blooms.

It is also valued for its ability to thrive in hot, dry conditions with minimal water requirements.
