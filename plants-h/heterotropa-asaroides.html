Heterotropa asaroides

Heterotropa asaroides, commonly known as the Indian nettle, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including India and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Urticaceae, which includes many stinging plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its stinging hairs, like those of other members of the Urticaceae family, can cause skin irritation upon contact.
