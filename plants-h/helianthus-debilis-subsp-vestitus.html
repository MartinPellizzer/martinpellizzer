Helianthus debilis subsp. vestitus

Helianthus debilis subsp.

vestitus, commonly known as the silverleaf blanketflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and vibrant yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping. Its low-growing habit and drought tolerance make it an attractive choice for native gardens.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
