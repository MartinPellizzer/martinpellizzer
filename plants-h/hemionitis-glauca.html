Hemionitis glauca

Hemionitis glauca, commonly known as the blue hemionite, is a rare and delicate fern native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silvery-green fronds that give it a distinctive, almost ethereal appearance. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to its sensitivity to environmental changes, Hemionitis glauca is considered endangered in many parts of its range.
