Hyphaene ventricosa subsp. aurantiaca

Hyphaene ventricosa subsp.

aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden fan palm, is a distinctive plant native to Madagascar. It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant yellow or golden color, giving it its common name.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers followed by elongated, finger-like fruits. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in coastal areas or near freshwater sources.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
