Hieracium macilentum subsp. staui

Hieracium macilentum subsp.

staui, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a flowering stem that bears yellow disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and roadsides. It has a taproot system and produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and weed management.
