Habenaria vaginata

Habenaria vaginata, commonly known as the hooded orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, fragrant flowers that resemble a hood, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and marshes. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom during the rainy season.

Habenaria vaginata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
