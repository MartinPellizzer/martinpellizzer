Hieracium vulgatum subsp. ochanskiense

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

ochanskiense, commonly known as the Ochanski's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and the leaves are usually hairy and have a serrated margin.

This subspecies is distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and subtle morphological differences from other hawkweed species.
