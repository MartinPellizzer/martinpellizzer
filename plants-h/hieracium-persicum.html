Hieracium persicum

Hieracium persicum, commonly known as Persian hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils across temperate regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowering heads are composed of numerous small florets.

Hieracium persicum is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions and is often found in areas with moderate to high sunlight exposure.
