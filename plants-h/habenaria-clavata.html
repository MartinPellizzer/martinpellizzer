Habenaria clavata

Habenaria clavata, commonly known as the clustered lady's tresses, is an orchid species native to various parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spike-like inflorescence and small, pale green to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil efficiently.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
