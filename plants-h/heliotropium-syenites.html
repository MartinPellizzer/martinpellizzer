Heliotropium syenites

Heliotropium syenites, commonly known as the desert heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a low-growing shrub with small, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces clusters of purple or pink flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its stems are often covered in fine hairs, which provide additional protection against intense sunlight and drought.

Heliotropium syenites is adapted to thrive in poor soils and can be found in rocky or sandy habitats.
