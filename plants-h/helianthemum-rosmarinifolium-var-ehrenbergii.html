Helianthemum rosmarinifolium var. ehrenbergii

Helianthemum rosmarinifolium var. ehrenbergii, commonly known as the Ehrenberg's rockrose, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub with aromatic, evergreen leaves that resemble rosemary. The plant produces clusters of small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its resilience and ornamental value make it a popular choice for Mediterranean landscapes.
