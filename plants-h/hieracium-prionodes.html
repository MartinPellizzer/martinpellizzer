Hieracium prionodes

Hieracium prionodes, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers and hairy, often lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils across Europe and parts of Asia. It has a creeping rootstock and can form dense colonies, making it an invasive species in some regions.

Its botanical features include alternate leaves and a basal rosette structure.
