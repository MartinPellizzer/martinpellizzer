Hydrangea briquetii

Hydrangea briquetii, commonly known as the mountain hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from white to pink, depending on soil pH. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

The flowers bloom in late summer, adding vibrant color to forested landscapes.
