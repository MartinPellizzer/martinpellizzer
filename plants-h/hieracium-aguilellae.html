Hieracium aguilellae

Hieracium aguilellae, commonly known as the Italian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as meadows and roadsides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

Hieracium aguilellae is known for its ability to thrive in various environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
