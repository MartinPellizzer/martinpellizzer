Hymenochilus clivicola

Hymenochilus clivicola, commonly known as the cliff orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, intricate flowers that typically bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. Its flowers are usually pale green or white with a distinctive lip that is adorned with intricate patterns.

Hymenochilus clivicola thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found growing on rocky slopes or in forest understories.
