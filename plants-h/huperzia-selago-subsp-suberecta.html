Huperzia selago subsp. suberecta

Huperzia selago subsp.

suberecta, commonly known as the upright clubmoss, is a small evergreen fern-like plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It belongs to the family Huperziaceae and is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit.

The plant features dense, linear leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rock crevices. Huperzia selago subsp.

suberecta is notable for its ability to form dense colonies, which help stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
