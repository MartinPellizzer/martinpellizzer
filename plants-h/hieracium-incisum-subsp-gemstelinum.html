Hieracium incisum subsp. gemstelinum

Hieracium incisum subsp.

gemstelinum, commonly known as the St. Leonard's groundsel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow flower heads.

The leaves are typically lobed and have a rough texture, while the flowers consist of both ray and disk florets. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
