Hypolytrum buchholzianum

Hypolytrum buchholzianum, commonly known as the Buchholz's grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale yellow flowers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open grasslands or along stream banks.

Hypolytrum buchholzianum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded conditions.
