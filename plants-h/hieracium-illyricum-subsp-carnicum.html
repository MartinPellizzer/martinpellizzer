Hieracium illyricum subsp. carnicum

Hieracium illyricum subsp.

carnicum, commonly known as the Carpathian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along riverbanks. It has hairy stems and leaves with a rough texture, and its flowers bloom from June to August.

This subspecies is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
