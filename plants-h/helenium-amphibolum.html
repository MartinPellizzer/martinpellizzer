Helenium amphibolum

Helenium amphibolum, commonly known as the swamp helenium, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetland environments in North America.

It features upright, branched stems that bear showy, ray-like flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ability to attract pollinators.

Helenium amphibolum is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.
