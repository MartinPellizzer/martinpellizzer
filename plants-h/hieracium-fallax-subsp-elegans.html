Hieracium fallax subsp. elegans

Hieracium fallax subsp.

elegans, commonly known as the elegant hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowering stems bear numerous small florets.

It is a relatively low-maintenance plant that spreads through rhizomes and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
