Hyphaene multiformis subsp. ambigua

Hyphaene multiformis subsp.

ambigua, commonly known as the African date palm, is a distinctive plant native to parts of Africa. It is a tall, palm-like tree characterized by its robust trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that develop into edible fruits resembling dates. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a unique, branching structure that supports its growth in arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional agriculture.
