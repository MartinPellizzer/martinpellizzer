Hieracium amygdalinum var. subhispidum

Hieracium amygdalinum var. subhispidum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the daisy family. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.
