Hearnia balansae

Hearnia balansae, commonly known as the Hearn's orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, greenish-white or pale yellow blossoms that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Hearnia balansae thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, humid conditions.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
