Hieracium subglaucicolor

Hieracium subglaucicolor, commonly known as the bluish-hairy hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring well-drained conditions. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a bluish-green hue, contributing to its common name.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
