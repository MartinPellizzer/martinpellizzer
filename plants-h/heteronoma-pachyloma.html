Heteronoma pachyloma

Heteronoma pachyloma, commonly known as the thick-stemmed spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae, which is characterized by its milky sap and diverse morphological features. The plant typically has smooth, ribbed stems that are covered in a thick, waxy cuticle, aiding in water retention. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a distinctive yellowish-green coloration.

The flowers are small and clustered in cyathia, a unique structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.
