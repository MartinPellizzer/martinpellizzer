Holcus elatior

Holcus elatior, commonly known as the greater purplegrass, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit. The plant produces dense clusters of purple to blue-violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Holcus elatior is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a pioneer species in ecological succession.
