Hieracium juraniforme subsp. juranopsis

Hieracium juraniforme subsp.

juranopsis, commonly known as the Juran Island hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing adaptability to harsh environments. Its subspecies distinction is based on morphological differences, particularly in leaf shape and flower structure.

It is considered a rare and endemic species, highlighting its ecological significance in its native region.
