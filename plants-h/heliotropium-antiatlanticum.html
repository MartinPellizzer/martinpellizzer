Heliotropium antiatlanticum

Heliotropium antiatlanticum, commonly known as the Atlantic heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Atlantic Ocean.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along shorelines. The plant produces small, violet or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually opposite and have a slightly hairy texture.

Heliotropium antiatlanticum is adapted to saline environments and can tolerate moderate levels of drought.
