Hackelia cusickii

Hackelia cusickii, commonly known as the Sierra fairybells, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in open, rocky areas and meadows. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Hackelia cusickii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, bell-like flowers.
