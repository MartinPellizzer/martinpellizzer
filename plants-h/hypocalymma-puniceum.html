Hypocalymma puniceum

Hypocalymma puniceum, commonly known as the red-stemmed bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive red stems and clusters of bright red, brush-like flowers. The plant typically grows in heathland and dry sclerophyll forests, thriving in well-drained soils. Its unique appearance makes it a popular ornamental plant, while its ecological role supports various pollinators.

The leaves are narrow and leathery, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.
