Hieracium neocerinthe subsp. lagascanum

Hieracium neocerinthe subsp.

lagascanum, commonly known as the Lagascan hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and foliage with serrated edges.

The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. It produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster, and its leaves are usually lance-shaped with hairy surfaces.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bright floral display.
