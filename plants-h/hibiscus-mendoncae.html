Hibiscus mendoncae

Hibiscus mendoncae, commonly known as Mendenhall's hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Hibiscus mendoncae is a perennial species that blooms from summer to early fall.
