Hieracium sabaudum subsp. vagum

Hieracium sabaudum subsp.

vagum, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring well-drained conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is known for its ornamental value and has been introduced to various regions outside its native range.
