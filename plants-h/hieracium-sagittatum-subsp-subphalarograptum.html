Hieracium sagittatum subsp. subphalarograptum

Hieracium sagittatum subsp.

subphalarograptum, commonly known as the arrowhead hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along riverbanks, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant has basal rosettes of narrow, lanceolate leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters at the ends of its stems. Its flowers are typically tubular with five lobes, and the plant is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.

It is also a host plant for certain butterfly species, contributing to local biodiversity.
