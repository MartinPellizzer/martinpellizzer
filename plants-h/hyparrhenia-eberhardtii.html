Hyparrhenia eberhardtii

Hyparrhenia eberhardtii, commonly known as the African grass or Eberhardt's grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers.

Hyparrhenia eberhardtii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a forage plant.
