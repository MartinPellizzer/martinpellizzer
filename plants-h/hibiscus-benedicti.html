Hibiscus benedicti

Hibiscus benedicti, commonly known as the sacred hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it thrives in warm, humid climates. Hibiscus benedicti is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in various regions.

Its flowers are used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dye.
