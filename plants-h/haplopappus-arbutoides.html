Haplopappus arbutoides

Haplopappus arbutoides, commonly known as the roughleaf pappus, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has small, hairy leaves. Its flowers are yellow and occur in clusters, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

It is often found in arid and semi-arid regions, showcasing adaptations to harsh environmental conditions.
