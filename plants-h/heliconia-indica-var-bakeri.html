Heliconia indica var. bakeri

Heliconia indica var. bakeri, commonly known as the Baker's heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its large, colorful bracts that resemble petals. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its inflorescence is a key feature, consisting of a spiral arrangement of bracts surrounding the actual flowers.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses due to its vibrant appearance and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
