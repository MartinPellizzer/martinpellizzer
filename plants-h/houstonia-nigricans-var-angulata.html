Houstonia nigricans var. angulata

Houstonia nigricans var. angulata, commonly known as the angular blue-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its delicate, grass-like appearance. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a subtle yet striking visual element to its habitat. It typically grows in moist, acidic soils in open woodlands and along stream banks.

The variety angulata is distinguished by its angular leaf shape, contributing to its unique botanical identity.
