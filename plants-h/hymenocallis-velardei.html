Hymenocallis velardei

Hymenocallis velardei, commonly known as the Velarde's spider lily, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers with a distinctive yellow throat and long, narrow petals. The plant produces bulbous rhizomes that store nutrients, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that emerge from the base of the plant.

Hymenocallis velardei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to full sun to partial shade conditions.
