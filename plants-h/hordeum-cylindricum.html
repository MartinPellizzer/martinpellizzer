Hordeum cylindricum

Hordeum cylindricum, commonly known as the cylindrical barley, is a species of grass native to Eurasia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical spikelets that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows and riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while its stems are hollow and jointed.

Hordeum cylindricum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
