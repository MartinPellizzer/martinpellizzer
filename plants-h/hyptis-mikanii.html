Hyptis mikanii

Hyptis mikanii, commonly known as the Mexican hyptis, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of purple or white. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

Hyptis mikanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
