Hieracium alpinum subsp. dissquamatum

Hieracium alpinum subsp.

dissquamatum, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in harsh, cold climates. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a rough texture, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

This subspecies is known for its resilience and is often found in mountainous areas across the Alps and adjacent regions.
