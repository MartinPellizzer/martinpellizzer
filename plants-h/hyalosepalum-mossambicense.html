Hyalosepalum mossambicense

Hyalosepalum mossambicense, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a unique member of the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, white, star-shaped flowers that open at night, emitting a sweet fragrance. The plant is native to southern Africa and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are small, leathery, and often covered in a fine, waxy coating.

Despite its beauty, it is relatively rare and requires specific conditions to flourish.
