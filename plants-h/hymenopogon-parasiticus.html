Hymenopogon parasiticus

Hymenopogon parasiticus, commonly known as the parasitic hairgrass, is a rare and specialized plant that exhibits hemiparasitic behavior, deriving some of its nutrients from host plants while also performing photosynthesis.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in association with other vegetation in forest understories. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are adapted for wind pollination. Its root system includes haustoria, specialized structures that allow it to penetrate the roots of host plants and extract water and minerals.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, Hymenopogon parasiticus is considered a sensitive indicator of undisturbed, nutrient-rich habitats.
