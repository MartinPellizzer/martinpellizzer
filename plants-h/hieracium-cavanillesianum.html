Hieracium cavanillesianum

Hieracium cavanillesianum, commonly known as the Cavanilles' hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and wet meadows. It has basal leaves that are often hairy and lance-shaped, with a flowering period that occurs in the spring and summer months.

Due to its ornamental value and invasive potential in some regions, it is sometimes cultivated or considered a weed.
