Herpetica rumphiana

Herpetica rumphiana, commonly known as the Rumphius's orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree bark in humid forest environments. Its leaves are broad and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in its native habitat.

The flowers display a range of colors, including white, yellow, and pink, and are often used in traditional herbal medicine.
