Hieracium hoppeanum subsp. pilisquamum

Hieracium hoppeanum subsp.

pilisquamum, commonly known as the rough-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets.

The plant has rough, hairy leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered with small, scale-like structures. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, and its roots are fibrous and shallow.

This subspecies is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, where it thrives in full sun.
