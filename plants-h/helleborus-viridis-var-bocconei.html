Helleborus viridis var. bocconei

Helleborus viridis var. bocconei, commonly known as the green hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features dark green, waxy leaves that are typically heart-shaped and have a distinctive veining pattern. The plant produces clusters of pale green to white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is relatively hardy, though it is toxic to humans and animals.
