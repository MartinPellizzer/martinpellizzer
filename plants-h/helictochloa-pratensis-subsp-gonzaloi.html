Helictochloa pratensis subsp. gonzaloi

Helictochloa pratensis subsp.

gonzaloi, commonly known as the Guatemalan blue grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central America. It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and produces dense clusters of small, blue-green leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in shaded to partially shaded environments. It is often found in cloud forests and along stream banks, where it contributes to soil stabilization.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in some horticultural settings.
