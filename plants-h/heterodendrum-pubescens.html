Heterodendrum pubescens

Heterodendrum pubescens, commonly known as the hairy tree fern, is a unique plant characterized by its distinctive frond structure and hairy appearance.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia. The plant features a slender, unbranched trunk covered in a fibrous, hairy layer, which gives it its common name. Its fronds are pinnately compound, with a long, narrow rachis and broad, overlapping pinnae.

Heterodendrum pubescens is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
