Hieracium symphytaceum subsp. sarracenicum

Hieracium symphytaceum subsp.

sarracenicum, commonly known as the "sarracenic hawkweed," is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces yellow flower heads that are typically clustered in a flat-topped arrangement.

The leaves are basal and broadly lanceolate, often with a hairy texture. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is commonly found in meadows and along riverbanks.

This subspecies is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
