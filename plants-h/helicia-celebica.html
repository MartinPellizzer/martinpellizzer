Helicia celebica

Helicia celebica, commonly known as the Celebes helenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Indonesian archipelago.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in mountainous regions with well-drained soil. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant produces small, woody capsules containing several seeds.

Helicia celebica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
