Hieracium niphobioides

Hieracium niphobioides, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming basal leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant thrives in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained, rocky soils. It is adapted to cold climates and can withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Hieracium niphobioides is often found in meadows and open slopes at high elevations.
