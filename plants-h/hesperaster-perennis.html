Hesperaster perennis

Hesperaster perennis, commonly known as the everlasting flower, is a species of flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. Hesperaster perennis is known for its long-lasting blooms, which can persist for several weeks under favorable conditions.

It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
