Hymenocallis chiapasiana

Hymenocallis chiapasiana, commonly known as the Chiapas spider lily, is a striking tropical plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers with a distinctive yellowish-green throat and long, narrow petals. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its elegant appearance.

Hymenocallis chiapasiana is typically grown in containers or in shaded garden beds where it can receive adequate moisture.
