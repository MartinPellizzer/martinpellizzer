Hesperis ilamica

Hesperis ilamica, commonly known as the Persian sweetroot, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its biennial growth cycle. The plant features basal leaves that are lobed and hairy, with small, white or pale purple flowers arranged in racemes. It produces round, fleshy fruits known as silique, containing several seeds.

Hesperis ilamica is valued for its edible roots and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
