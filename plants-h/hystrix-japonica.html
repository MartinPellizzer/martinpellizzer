Hystrix japonica

Hystrix japonica, commonly known as the Japanese hedgehog cactus, is a species of cactus native to Japan.

It is characterized by its spiny, segmented stems that resemble a hedgehog, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. Hystrix japonica is adapted to grow in rocky or sandy soils and requires well-drained conditions.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
