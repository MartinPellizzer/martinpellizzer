Hedyotis centranthoides

Hedyotis centranthoides, commonly known as the Indian bedstraw, is a small flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including India, China, and Japan.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. The plant features slender, creeping stems with opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and are clustered in dense, branched inflorescences.

Hedyotis centranthoides is valued in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
