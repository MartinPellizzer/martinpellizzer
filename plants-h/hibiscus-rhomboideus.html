Hibiscus rhomboideus

Hibiscus rhomboideus, commonly known as the diamondleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive rhomboid-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

Hibiscus rhomboideus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
