Hieracium incisiceps

Hieracium incisiceps, commonly known as the cut-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems that often branch near the base. The leaves are deeply lobed and usually have a serrated or incised margin, giving the plant its common name. The flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the daisy family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grassy areas and disturbed soils.
