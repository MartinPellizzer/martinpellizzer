Hieracium murorum subsp. cinereostriatum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

cinereostriatum, commonly known as the grey-striped hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands, roadsides, and waste areas.

The plant has basal leaves that are usually greyish-green with a distinctive white or pale stripe along the midrib. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, characteristic of the Asteraceae family.

It is often found in calcareous soils and can form dense mats, sometimes becoming invasive in certain regions.
