Halimocnemis rarifolia

Halimocnemis rarifolia, commonly known as the rareleaf sandmat, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a low-growing, mat-forming structure with fleshy, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the rainy season, which are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its succulent leaves store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions.

Halimocnemis rarifolia is well adapted to sandy soils and can thrive in full sun.
