Hortia longifolia

Hortia longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf hortia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

Hortia longifolia is often found in tropical rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
