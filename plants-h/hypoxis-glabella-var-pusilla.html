Hypoxis glabella var. pusilla

Hypoxis glabella var. pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf starflower, is a small, herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers. Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking low-maintenance, drought-tolerant plants.

It is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
