Helia viscosa

Helia viscosa, commonly known as the sticky helia or woolly helia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its unique morphology, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
