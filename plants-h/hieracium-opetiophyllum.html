Hieracium opetiophyllum

Hieracium opetiophyllum, commonly known as the giant hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, which are typically visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered with fine hairs.

Hieracium opetiophyllum is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.
