Holodiscus velutinus

Holodiscus velutinus, commonly known as the velvetleaf holly.

It is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its evergreen leaves that are soft to the touch and covered in fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in some regions for landscaping purposes.
