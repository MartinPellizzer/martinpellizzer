Hoya parviflora

Hoya parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered wax plant, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and waxy, dark green leaves that are often variegated with white or cream markings. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, clustered in umbels. Hoya parviflora is a succulent that thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.

It is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
