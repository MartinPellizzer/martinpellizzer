Haworthia jansenvillensis var. mollis

Haworthia jansenvillensis var. mollis, commonly known as the Zulu bulb, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped foliage composed of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with white or pale markings. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers on slender stems during the growing season. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or container cultivation.

Its compact size and unique appearance make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
