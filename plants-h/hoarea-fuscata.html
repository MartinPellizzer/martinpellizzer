Hoarea fuscata

Hoarea fuscata, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit, with small, fleshy, gray-green leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color, and it is adapted to thrive in harsh, sandy environments.

Hoarea fuscata is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil in desert ecosystems.
