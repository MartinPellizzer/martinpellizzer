Hesperis balansae subsp. scabrida

Hesperis balansae subsp.

scabrida, commonly known as the rough-hesperis, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, giving the plant a distinctive, rugged appearance.

It is often found in dry, well-drained habitats and is valued for its ornamental value in gardens.
