Haronga paniculata var. oblongifolia

Haronga paniculata var. oblongifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf haronga, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, elongated leaves that are typically arranged in a whorled pattern along the stems. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its root system is fibrous and well-developed, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
