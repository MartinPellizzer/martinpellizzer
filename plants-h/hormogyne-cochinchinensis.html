Hormogyne cochinchinensis

Hormogyne cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in the spring. The plant is a terrestrial orchid with a short, erect stem and linear leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat-specific requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
