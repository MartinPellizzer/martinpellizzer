Hedyotis laevigata

Hedyotis laevigata, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed bedstraw, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, ascending stems covered with small, opposite leaves that are usually smooth or slightly hairy. Its flowers are small, white or pink, and are clustered in dense racemes, giving it a delicate appearance.

Hedyotis laevigata is often found in wetland habitats and is known for its ability to thrive in poorly drained soils.
