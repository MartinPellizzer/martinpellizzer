Hieracium dolorosum

Hieracium dolorosum, commonly known as the sorrowful hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring moist to moderately dry conditions. Its flowers are yellow to orange in color, with ray florets that resemble petals, and it produces small, bristly seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Hieracium dolorosum is often found in open habitats and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
