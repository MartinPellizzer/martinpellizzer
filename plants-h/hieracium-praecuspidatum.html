Hieracium praecuspidatum

Hieracium praecuspidatum, commonly known as the spiked hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant features upright stems that are often hairy, with basal leaves that are lance-shaped and serrated. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Hieracium praecuspidatum is typically found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it plays a role in supporting pollinators.
