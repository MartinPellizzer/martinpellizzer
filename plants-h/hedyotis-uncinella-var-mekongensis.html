Hedyotis uncinella var. mekongensis

Hedyotis uncinella var. mekongensis, commonly known as the Mekong shieldflower, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers. Its stems are often hairy, and it has a compact growth habit.

This variant is distinguished by its unique floral structure and ecological adaptations to its native habitat.
