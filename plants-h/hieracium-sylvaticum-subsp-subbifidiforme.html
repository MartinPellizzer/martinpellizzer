Hieracium sylvaticum subsp. subbifidiforme

Hieracium sylvaticum subsp.

subbifidiforme, commonly known as the woodland hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow flowers arranged in a composite head.

The subspecies is distinguished by its finely divided, bilobed leaves, which give it a delicate appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded woodlands and is often found in areas with rich, loamy soil.

The plant plays a role in supporting local pollinators and contributes to the biodiversity of its habitat.
