Heliotropium crispatum

Heliotropium crispatum, commonly known as the hairy heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit and is often found in sandy or disturbed soils. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple, which bloom in clusters. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a distinctive crinkled or wavy edge.

Heliotropium crispatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in coastal or arid environments.
