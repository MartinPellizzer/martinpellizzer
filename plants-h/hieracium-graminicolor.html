Hieracium graminicolor

Hieracium graminicolor, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium graminicolor is often found in moist soils and can form dense colonies in suitable environments.
