Hieracium mougeotii subsp. attractum

Hieracium mougeotii subsp.

attractum, commonly known as the Showy Hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom from June to August.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while the flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

This subspecies is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant appearance.
