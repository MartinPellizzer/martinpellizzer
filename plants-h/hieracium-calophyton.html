Hieracium calophyton

Hieracium calophyton, commonly known as the showy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring well-drained conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Hieracium calophyton is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
