Himantophyllum

Himantophyllum, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and elusive orchid species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, delicate appearance, with long, slender leaves and a single, intricate flower that resembles a ghostly figure. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its flowers are typically pale green or white, with a distinctive, elongated shape.

Due to its habitat specificity and conservation status, Himantophyllum is considered a vulnerable species.
