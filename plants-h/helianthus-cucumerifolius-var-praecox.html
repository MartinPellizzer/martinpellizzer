Helianthus cucumerifolius var. praecox

Helianthus cucumerifolius var. praecox, commonly known as the early prickly sunflower, is a variant of the prickly sunflower species native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces yellow composite flowers with a central disk surrounded by ray florets. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to drought conditions and full sun exposure.
