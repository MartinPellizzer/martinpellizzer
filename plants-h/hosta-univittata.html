Hosta univittata

Hosta univittata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hosta, is a species native to eastern Asia, particularly China.

It is a perennial plant belonging to the Asparagaceae family, characterized by its clumping growth habit and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The foliage is typically a deep green with a slight white margin, and the plant produces small, fragrant, white flowers in late summer.

It thrives in partial to full shade and is well-suited for moist, well-drained soil in woodland gardens.
