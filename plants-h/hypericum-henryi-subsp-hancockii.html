Hypericum henryi subsp. hancockii

Hypericum henryi subsp.

hancockii, commonly known as the Hancock's St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to China. It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in summer. Its leaves are opposite and oval-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional medicinal uses in Chinese herbal medicine.
