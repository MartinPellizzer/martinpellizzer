Hieracium magyaricum subsp. hispidissimum

Hieracium magyaricum subsp.

hispidissimum, commonly known as the Hungarian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has basal and stem leaves that are usually hairy, contributing to its common name "hispidissimum," which refers to the rough texture. It thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
