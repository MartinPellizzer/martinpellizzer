Hieracium tendinum subsp. tinctum

Hieracium tendinum subsp.

tinctum, commonly known as the purple hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces striking purple to reddish flowers in late summer.

The plant is characterized by its tall, erect stems and is often found in meadows and grasslands. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its showy composite flowers.

The subspecies tinctum is distinguished by its more intensely colored inflorescences compared to other varieties.
