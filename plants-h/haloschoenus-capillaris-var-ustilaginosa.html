Haloschoenus capillaris var. ustilaginosa

Haloschoenus capillaris var. ustilaginosa, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences that bear small, clustered flowers. This variety is typically found in wetland environments, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread vegetatively and form dense colonies.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
