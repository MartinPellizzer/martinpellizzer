Halesia ternata

Halesia ternata, commonly known as the bellflower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has smooth, grayish-brown bark and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.

Halesia ternata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for various wildlife species.
