Hieracium semisagittatum

Hieracium semisagittatum, commonly known as the semi-sagittate hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, preferring temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a serrated edge, and the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is often found in open habitats and can form dense colonies, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
