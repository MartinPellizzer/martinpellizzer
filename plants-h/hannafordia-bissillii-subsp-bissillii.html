Hannafordia bissillii subsp. bissillii

Hannafordia bissillii subsp.

bissillii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa. It typically forms dense mats of small, fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern.

The plant produces tiny, white to pale pink flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in branched inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in coastal or inland dune environments.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
