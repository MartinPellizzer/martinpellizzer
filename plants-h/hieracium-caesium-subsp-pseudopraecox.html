Hieracium caesium subsp. pseudopraecox

Hieracium caesium subsp.

pseudopraecox, commonly known as the early yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features basal leaves that are broadly lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, while the upper leaves are narrower and progressively smaller. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a composite head, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

The species is characterized by its early flowering habit and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
