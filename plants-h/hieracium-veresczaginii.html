Hieracium veresczaginii

Hieracium veresczaginii, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium veresczaginii is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy and resilient nature.
