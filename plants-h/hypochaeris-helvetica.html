Hypochaeris helvetica

Hypochaeris helvetica, commonly known as Swiss groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Europe.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It produces yellow flowers with a central disk surrounded by ray florets, and its leaves are usually hairy and alternate.

Hypochaeris helvetica is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in various habitats.
