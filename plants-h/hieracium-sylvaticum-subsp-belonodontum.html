Hieracium sylvaticum subsp. belonodontum

Hieracium sylvaticum subsp.

belonodontum, commonly known as the woodland hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and along streambanks.

The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in late spring to early summer. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The subspecies is distinguished by its finely divided leaves and smaller, more clustered flower heads compared to other Hieracium species.
