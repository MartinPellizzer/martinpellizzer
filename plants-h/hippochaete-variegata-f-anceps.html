Hippochaete variegata f. anceps

Hippochaete variegata f. anceps, commonly known as the variegated hibiscus, is a hybrid form of the hibiscus plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, which features a combination of green, white, and yellow colors. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This ornamental plant is popular in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
