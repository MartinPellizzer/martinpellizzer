Hieracium praecox var. horripilum

Hieracium praecox var. horripilum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are hairy and have a serrated edge, while its yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is a perennial species that can spread through rhizomes, making it somewhat invasive in certain environments.
