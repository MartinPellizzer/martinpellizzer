Hypericum monadenum

Hypericum monadenum, commonly known as the lesser St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb with yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is often found in woodlands and grassy areas. It contains hypericin and pseudohypericin, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Its historical use in treating mood disorders and skin conditions is well-documented.
