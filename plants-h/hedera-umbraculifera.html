Hedera umbraculifera

Hedera umbraculifera, commonly known as the umbrella ivy, is a species of climbing plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in a distinctive umbrella-like shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found growing on rocky surfaces or in woodland areas.

Hedera umbraculifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to climb and cover vertical surfaces.
