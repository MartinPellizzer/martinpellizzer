Helia tenuifolia

Helia tenuifolia, commonly known as the slenderleaf helia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and herbaceous growth habit. The plant typically features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow to orange. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
