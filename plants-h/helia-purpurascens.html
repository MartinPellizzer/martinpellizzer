Helia purpurascens

Helia purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-flowered helia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Helia purpurascens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
