Habenaria armondiana

Habenaria armondiana, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that emit a subtle fragrance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and marshy areas. Its leaves are linear and often covered in a fine, hairy texture.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
