Heliconia cannoidea

Heliconia cannoidea, commonly known as the red heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous inflorescences composed of bright red bracts that enclose numerous small, yellowish flowers. The plant belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is closely related to ginger and bananas. Its foliage consists of broad, waxy leaves that are often used in floral arrangements.

Heliconia cannoidea thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests and along riverbanks.
