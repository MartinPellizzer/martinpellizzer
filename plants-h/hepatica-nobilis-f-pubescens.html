Hepatica nobilis f. pubescens

Hepatica nobilis f. pubescens, commonly known as the downy hepatica, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves and showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in woodland habitats, thriving in moist, shaded environments with well-drained soil. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological importance.

The downy hairs on its stems and leaves give it the common name "downy hepatica.".
