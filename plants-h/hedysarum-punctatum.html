Hedysarum punctatum

Hedysarum punctatum, commonly known as the spotted bartsia, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, trifoliate leaves with distinctive white spots. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, which are clustered in racemes. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Hedysarum punctatum is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to arid environments.
