Hesperis breviscapa

Hesperis breviscapa, commonly known as the short-capped bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with four-petaled flowers. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it prefers well-drained, calcareous soils.

Hesperis breviscapa is often found in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in full sun to partial shade.
