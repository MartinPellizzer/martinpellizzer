Hamamelis androgyna

Hamamelis androgyna, commonly known as the hybrid witch hazel, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late autumn or early winter. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that turn reddish in the fall. Its botanical features include smooth, grayish bark and clusters of fragrant, spathulate flowers.

Hamamelis androgyna is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
