Hibiscus varians

Hibiscus varians, commonly known as the variable hibiscus, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Hibiscus varians typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
