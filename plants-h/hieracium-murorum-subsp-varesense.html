Hieracium murorum subsp. varesense

Hieracium murorum subsp.

varesense, commonly known as the mouse-ear hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its hairy, basal rosette of leaves and small yellow flowers arranged in composite heads.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as meadows and roadsides. It is native to Europe and has been introduced to other parts of the world.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural and urban environments.
