Hypericum lusitanicum

Hypericum lusitanicum, commonly known as the Portuguese St. John's Wort, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and opposite, simple leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats. It belongs to the genus Hypericum, which includes many species known for their medicinal properties.

The plant's flowering period occurs in summer, and it is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
