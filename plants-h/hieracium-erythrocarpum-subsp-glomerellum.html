Hieracium erythrocarpum subsp. glomerellum

Hieracium erythrocarpum subsp.

glomerellum, commonly known as the red-fruited hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and showy yellow flowers.

The plant produces small, clustered fruits called cypsela, which are typically reddish in color. It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Hieracium genus, known for its variability and adaptability.
