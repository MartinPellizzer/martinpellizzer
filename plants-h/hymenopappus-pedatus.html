Hymenopappus pedatus

Hymenopappus pedatus, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, adapting well to harsh, dry environments. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs to reduce water loss.

The species is characterized by its unique pappus, a structure that aids in seed dispersal by the wind.
