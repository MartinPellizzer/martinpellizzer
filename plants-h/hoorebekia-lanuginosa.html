Hoorebekia lanuginosa

Hoorebekia lanuginosa, commonly known as the wooly bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, woolly leaves and stems, which give it its common name. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Hoorebekia lanuginosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
