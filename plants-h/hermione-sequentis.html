Hermione sequentis

Hermione sequentis, commonly known as the silverleaf moonflower, is a rare flowering plant native to the misty highlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking white petals that emit a faint bioluminescent glow at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. The plant's leaves are broad and silvery, reflecting moonlight, which gives it its common name. It thrives in cool, moist environments and requires partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
