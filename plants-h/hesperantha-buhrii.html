Hesperantha buhrii

Hesperantha buhrii, commonly known as the Buhri's orchid, is a South African endemic plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial herb with a short, stocky rhizome and produces striking, fragrant, violet-blue flowers during the summer months. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils in forested areas. Its flowers are borne on slender, erect stems and are characterized by their intricate, spurred structure.

Hesperantha buhrii is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique and delicate appearance.
