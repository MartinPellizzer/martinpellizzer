Hedyotis brachypetala

Hedyotis brachypetala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bedstraw, is a small herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. The plant features opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters. Its flowers are often followed by small, berry-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Hedyotis brachypetala is often found in wetland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
