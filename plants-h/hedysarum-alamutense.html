Hedysarum alamutense

Hedysarum alamutense, commonly known as the Alamut milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in rocky and arid environments, often found in alpine and steppe habitats. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
