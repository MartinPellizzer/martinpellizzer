Hedera helix subsp. caucasigena

Hedera helix subsp.

caucasigena, commonly known as the Caucasian ivy, is a climbing vine native to the Caucasus region. It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a glossy surface and serrated edges.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in shaded to partially sunny conditions and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Due to its rapid growth, it is often considered an invasive species in non-native regions.
