Hieracium umbellatum f. praelongum

Hieracium umbellatum f. praelongum, commonly known as the long-stemmed wood sorrel, is a variant of the common wood sorrel.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its upright stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow, umbellate flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to native plant gardens.
