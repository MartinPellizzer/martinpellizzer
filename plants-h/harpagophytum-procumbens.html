Harpagophytum procumbens

Harpagophytum procumbens, commonly known as wild quinine or devil's claw, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow-orange flowers and its unique, claw-like seed pods. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils. Its roots contain bioactive compounds, making it valued in traditional medicine.

Harpagophytum procumbens is also cultivated for its medicinal properties and ornamental value.
