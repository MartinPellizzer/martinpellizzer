Hieracium nigrescens subsp. chlorophaeum

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

chlorophaeum, commonly known as the greenish hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Its subspecies distinction is based on the coloration of its leaves and flowers, which tend to be more greenish in appearance compared to other members of the species.
