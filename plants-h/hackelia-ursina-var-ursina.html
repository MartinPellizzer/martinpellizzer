Hackelia ursina var. ursina

Hackelia ursina var. ursina, commonly known as wooly blue curls, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of bright blue to purple, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and rocky areas.

Hackelia ursina var. ursina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
