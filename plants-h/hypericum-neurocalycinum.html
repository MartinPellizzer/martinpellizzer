Hypericum neurocalycinum

Hypericum neurocalycinum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typically from June to August. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky habitats.

Its medicinal properties have been historically valued, though it contains compounds that can interact with certain medications.
