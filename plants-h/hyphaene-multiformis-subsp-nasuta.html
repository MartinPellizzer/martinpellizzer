Hyphaene multiformis subsp. nasuta

Hyphaene multiformis subsp.

nasuta, commonly known as the African fan-palm, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that form a dense crown, and it produces clustered, spiny fruits.

The plant has a robust trunk with a fibrous outer layer, and its roots are adapted to thrive in sandy or well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in some African communities.

The subspecies nasuta is distinguished by its specific leaf and fruit morphology compared to other subspecies of Hyphaene multiformis.
