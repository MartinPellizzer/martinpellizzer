Heliotropium pilosum var. albatum

Heliotropium pilosum var. albatum, commonly known as the silky heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, velvety appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from purple to pink, often resembling those of the common heliotrope. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
