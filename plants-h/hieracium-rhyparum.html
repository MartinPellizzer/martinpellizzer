Hieracium rhyparum

Hieracium rhyparum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering heads that produce yellow or orange disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. It has a fibrous root system and can spread vegetatively through rhizomes.

Due to its adaptability, it is often considered an invasive species in certain regions.
