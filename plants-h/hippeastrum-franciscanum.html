Hippeastrum franciscanum

Hippeastrum franciscanum, commonly known as the Francisco's amaryllis, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant produces large, strap-like leaves that are often used for ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers a warm, sunny environment.

Hippeastrum franciscanum is typically cultivated for its vibrant blooms and is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
