Hydnocarpus crassifolius

Hydnocarpus crassifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf hydnocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces distinctive, irregularly shaped fruits that contain a single, hard, woody seed. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its timber and medicinal properties.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting various wildlife species.
