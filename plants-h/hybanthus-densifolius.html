Hybanthus densifolius

Hybanthus densifolius, commonly known as the dense-leaved hybanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and is often found in humid, lowland environments. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. Its leaves are dense and leathery, providing a textured appearance.

Hybanthus densifolius is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded conditions.
