Hieracium chnootum

Hieracium chnootum, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head.

Hieracium chnootum is known for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions and its role in stabilizing soil in high-altitude ecosystems.
