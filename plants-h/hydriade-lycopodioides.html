Hydriade lycopodioides

Hydriade lycopodioides, commonly known as the creeping clubmoss, is a species of lycophyte belonging to the family Lycopodiales.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and its ability to form dense mats on the forest floor. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky crevices. Its sporophytes produce spores in specialized structures called sporangia, which are clustered in strobila.

Hydriade lycopodioides plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
