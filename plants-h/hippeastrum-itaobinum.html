Hippeastrum itaobinum

Hippeastrum itaobinum, commonly known as the Itaobinum amaryllis, is a species native to the Amazon rainforest of Brazil.

It is a bulbous plant characterized by its striking, large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces a single, tall stem that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, supporting several large, trumpet-shaped blooms. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often appearing after the flowers have faded.

Hippeastrum itaobinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
