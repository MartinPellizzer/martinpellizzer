Hypericum atomarium var. angustifolium

Hypericum atomarium var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaf St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit, with slender, angular stems that often branch near the base. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers with four petals, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

It is often found in alpine environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
