Helianthus grosseserratus

Helianthus grosseserratus, commonly known as the roughleaf sunflower, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to the southeastern United States and is characterized by its yellow, daisy-like flowers and rough, serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, thriving in full sun and well-drained soil. Its botanical features include a hairy stem, lance-shaped leaves with coarsely toothed margins, and composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The roughleaf sunflower is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
