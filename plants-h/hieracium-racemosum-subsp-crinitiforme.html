Hieracium racemosum subsp. crinitiforme

Hieracium racemosum subsp.

crinitiforme, commonly known as the hairy yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies.

The plant features upright stems covered in fine, hairy texture, which gives it its common name. Its yellow flowers bloom in summer, and the leaves are typically lance-shaped with serrated edges.

It is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
