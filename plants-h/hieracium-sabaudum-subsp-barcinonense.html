Hieracium sabaudum subsp. barcinonense

Hieracium sabaudum subsp.

barcinonense, commonly known as the Pyrenean hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pyrenees region. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering heads that produce small yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils. It has hairy stems and leaves, which help protect it from harsh mountain conditions.

This subspecies is notable for its limited distribution and is considered a rare plant in its native habitat.
