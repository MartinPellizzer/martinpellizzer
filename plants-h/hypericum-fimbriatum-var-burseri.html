Hypericum fimbriatum var. burseri

Hypericum fimbriatum var. burseri, commonly known as the Burseri St John's Wort, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its opposite, narrow, and often hairy leaves that are typically green on top and paler underneath. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, ellipsoid seeds.

This species is often found in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in wetlands.
