Hydrangea macrophylla f. maculata

Hydrangea macrophylla f. maculata, commonly known as the spotted hydrangea, is a cultivar characterized by its large, showy inflorescences.

The plant features broad, ovate leaves that are typically green with a distinctive red or purple underside. Its flowers, which can be blue, pink, or white depending on soil pH, are composed of sterile flowers (petals) and fertile flowers (sepals). It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

The variegated foliage adds visual interest, enhancing its aesthetic appeal in landscape designs.
