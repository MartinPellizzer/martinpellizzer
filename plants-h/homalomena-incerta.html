Homalomena incerta

Homalomena incerta, commonly known as the uncertain arrowhead, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by red berries, which are edible but often used in traditional medicine.

Homalomena incerta is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
