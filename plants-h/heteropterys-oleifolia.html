Heteropterys oleifolia

Heteropterys oleifolia, commonly known as the oilleaf soursop, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves that emit a mild oil scent when crushed. The plant produces clusters of small, green, inedible fruits that turn yellow when ripe. Its flowers are typically greenish-white with five petals and are pollinated by insects.

Heteropterys oleifolia is often grown for its ornamental value and has traditional medicinal uses in some regions.
