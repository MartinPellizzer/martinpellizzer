Hypoxis minuta

Hypoxis minuta, commonly known as the dwarf false arrowhead, is a small, herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Hemerocallidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, grass-like foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Hypoxis minuta thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or containers.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
