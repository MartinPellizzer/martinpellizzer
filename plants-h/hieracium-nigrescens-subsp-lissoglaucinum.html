Hieracium nigrescens subsp. lissoglaucinum

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

lissoglaucinum, commonly known as the glaucous hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosettes of lanceolate, glaucous leaves.

The flowers are yellow and borne in a single head, with a distinctive involucre of bracts. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as steppes and rocky slopes.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage.
