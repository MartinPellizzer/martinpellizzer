Hieracium virgultorum subsp. lugdunense

Hieracium virgultorum subsp.

lugdunense, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn.

This subspecies is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats at high altitudes.
