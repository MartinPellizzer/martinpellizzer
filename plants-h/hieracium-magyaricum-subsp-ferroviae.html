Hieracium magyaricum subsp. ferroviae

Hieracium magyaricum subsp.

ferroviae, commonly known as the Hungarian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow flowers arranged in a composite head.

This subspecies is native to Hungary and surrounding regions, often found in dry meadows and grasslands. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, and its flowers bloom from June to August.

It is considered a rare and localized species, with limited distribution and specific habitat requirements.
