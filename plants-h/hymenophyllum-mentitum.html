Hymenophyllum mentitum

Hymenophyllum mentitum, commonly known as the silver moss, is a small, delicate fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silvery-green fronds that are often covered with a dense layer of trichomes, giving it a distinctive appearance. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. It has a unique reproductive structure called a sorus, which contains the spore-producing organs.

Hymenophyllum mentitum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is valued for its ability to thrive in humid, low-light conditions.
