Habenaria boadanensis

Habenaria boadanensis, commonly known as the Boadan's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the soil.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral appearance.
