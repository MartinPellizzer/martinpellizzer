Hieracium oxyodon subsp. isaricimorphum

Hieracium oxyodon subsp.

isaricimorphum, commonly known as the Isar Valley hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowering heads and basal rosettes of leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along riverbanks, preferring calcareous soils. Its leaves are lanceolate and hairy, while its flowers are arranged in composite inflorescences.

It is a relatively rare subspecies with limited distribution, making it of interest to botanists and conservationists.
