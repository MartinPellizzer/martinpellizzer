Hyptis petiolaris

Hyptis petiolaris, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are clustered in whorled inflorescences.

Hyptis petiolaris is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
