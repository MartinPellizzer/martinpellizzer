Hyparrhenia podotricha

Hyparrhenia podotricha, commonly known as the African grass or red grass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves with a distinctive reddish coloration. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain minute, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Hyparrhenia podotricha is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to withstand drought conditions.
