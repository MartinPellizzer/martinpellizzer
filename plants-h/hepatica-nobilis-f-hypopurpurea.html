Hepatica nobilis f. hypopurpurea

Hepatica nobilis f. hypopurpurea, commonly known as the pale-purple hepatica, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is native to the woodlands of eastern North America, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, early spring flowers that range from white to pale purple. Its flowers typically bloom in late winter to early spring, making it one of the first wildflowers to appear.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance and historical significance in herbal medicine.
