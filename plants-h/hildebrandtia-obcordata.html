Hildebrandtia obcordata

Hildebrandtia obcordata, commonly known as the obcordate orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and purple. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Hildebrandtia obcordata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
