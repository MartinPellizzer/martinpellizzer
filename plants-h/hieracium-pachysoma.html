Hieracium pachysoma

Hieracium pachysoma, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in grassy areas and open woodlands. The plant features a basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in clusters. It is characterized by its robust growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
