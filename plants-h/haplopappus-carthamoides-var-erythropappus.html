Haplopappus carthamoides var. erythropappus

Haplopappus carthamoides var. erythropappus, commonly known as the red pappus hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering stems that bear yellow flowers. The plant has a taproot system and produces small, bristly seeds that are dispersed by wind. Its foliage is typically covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads.
