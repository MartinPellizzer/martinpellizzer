Hibiscus campylochlamys

Hibiscus campylochlamys, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hibiscus, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hibiscus campylochlamys is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
