Hieracium collinum subsp. sudetorum

Hieracium collinum subsp.

sudetorum, commonly known as the Sudetic hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and its flowers are arranged in a single head at the end of each stem.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its presence in conservation areas.
