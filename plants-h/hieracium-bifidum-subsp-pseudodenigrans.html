Hieracium bifidum subsp. pseudodenigrans

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

pseudodenigrans, commonly known as the blackened hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features basal leaves that are broadly lance-shaped with toothed margins, and its flowering stems bear yellow composite flowers arranged in a single head. It is characterized by its distinctive dark purple or blackish stem and leaves, which give it its common name.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its showy inflorescences and medicinal potential in traditional herbal practices.
