Hydrangea scandens var. yakushimensis

Hydrangea scandens var. yakushimensis, commonly known as the Japanese climbing hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its vigorous, twining growth habit and can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant produces large, showy inflorescences of white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is typically dark green, turning red or orange in the fall.

It prefers partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for vertical gardening and trellis coverage.
