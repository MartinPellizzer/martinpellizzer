Homalomena terajaensis

Homalomena terajaensis, commonly known as the Terajaensis arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and inflorescence structure. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a deep green color and a smooth, waxy texture. Its flowers are typically small and clustered in a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe.

It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.
