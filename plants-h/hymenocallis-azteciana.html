Hymenocallis azteciana

Hymenocallis azteciana, commonly known as the Aztec lily, is a striking tropical plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy white to pale yellow flowers that resemble lilies. The plant features strap-like, grassy leaves and produces a tall, branched stem that bears multiple flower clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Hymenocallis azteciana is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
