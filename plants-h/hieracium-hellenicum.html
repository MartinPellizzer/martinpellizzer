Hieracium hellenicum

Hieracium hellenicum, commonly known as the Greek hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has basal leaves that are rough and hairy, and its stems are usually hairy as well. It thrives in sunny, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Hieracium hellenicum is often found in areas with well-drained soil and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.
