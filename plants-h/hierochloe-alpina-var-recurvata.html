Hierochloe alpina var. recurvata

Hierochloe alpina var. recurvata, commonly known as alpine bentgrass, is a flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like foliage and small, spike-like inflorescences. This variety is typically found in alpine and subalpine regions, where it thrives in rocky and well-drained soils. The plant is perennial and exhibits a low-growing, mat-forming habit.

Its botanical features make it an important component of alpine ecosystems, contributing to soil stabilization and habitat for various invertebrates.
