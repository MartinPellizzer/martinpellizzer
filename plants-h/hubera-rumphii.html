Hubera rumphii

Hubera rumphii, commonly known as the Rumphii orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the tropical regions of Indonesia.

It is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family, characterized by its elegant, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories, where it thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped, and the flowers are borne in clusters, often in shades of white, pink, or purple.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
