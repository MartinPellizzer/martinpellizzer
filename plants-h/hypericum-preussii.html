Hypericum preussii

Hypericum preussii, commonly known as the Preuss's St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Hypericum preussii is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
