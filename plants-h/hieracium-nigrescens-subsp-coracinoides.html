Hieracium nigrescens subsp. coracinoides

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

coracinoides, commonly known as the blackening hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows. Its leaves are lanceolate, with a rough texture, and the flowers are yellow, arranged in a single head.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
