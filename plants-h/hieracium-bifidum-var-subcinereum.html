Hieracium bifidum var. subcinereum

Hieracium bifidum var. subcinereum, commonly known as the pale hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to reddish flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

This variant is distinguished by its pale coloration compared to other varieties of Hieracium.
