Homoianthus rigidus

Homoianthus rigidus, commonly known as the rigid flame flower, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, woody stem and clusters of vibrant red, flame-like flowers that bloom during the warm season. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough, textured surface. This plant thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its unique appearance and seasonal blooming make it a standout in botanical collections.
