Hieracium lachenalii subsp. erythropodum

Hieracium lachenalii subsp.

erythropodum, commonly known as the red-hued hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, yellow to orange flower heads.

The subspecies is distinguished by its reddish stem and leaves, which give it its common name. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
