Helianthus annuus var. convolutus

Helianthus annuus var. convolutus, commonly known as the twisted sunflower, is a variant of the common sunflower characterized by its unique, spiraled inflorescence.

This plant typically grows in open, sunny areas and is native to the western United States. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture. The flowers are yellow with a central disk, and the plant produces oblong, ridged seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
