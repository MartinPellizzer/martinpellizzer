Heliconia lutea

Heliconia lutea, commonly known as the yellow heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescence composed of brightly colored bracts. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically hidden within the bracts, which serve as a protective structure. Heliconia lutea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

Its foliage is broad and sword-shaped, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
