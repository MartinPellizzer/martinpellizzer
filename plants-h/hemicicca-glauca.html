Hemicicca glauca

Hemicicca glauca, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fleshy, gray-green leaves that are covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, blooming during the cooler evening hours. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive prolonged droughts.

Hemicicca glauca is well adapted to desert conditions, with a deep root system that enables it to access underground water sources.
