Hieracium mougeotii subsp. lustratum

Hieracium mougeotii subsp.

lustratum, commonly known as the glossy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges.

The plant features basal rosettes of lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters at the top of its stem. Its leaves are often glossy and have a distinctive, waxy texture.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and wind-pollinated flowers.
