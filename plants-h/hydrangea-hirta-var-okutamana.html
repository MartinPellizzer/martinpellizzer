Hydrangea hirta var. okutamana

Hydrangea hirta var. okutamana, commonly known as the Japanese mountain hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that range in color from white to pink and blue, depending on soil pH. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and thrives in partial shade with well-drained soil. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a hairy texture that gives it its species name "hirta.".

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
