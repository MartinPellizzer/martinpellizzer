Holcus sorghum var. drummondii

Holcus sorghum var. drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's sorghum, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered panicles of florets that are typically green to reddish in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairie and woodland habitats.

Drummond's sorghum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
