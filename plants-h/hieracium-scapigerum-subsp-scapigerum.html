Hieracium scapigerum subsp. scapigerum

Hieracium scapigerum subsp.

scapigerum, commonly known as the upright hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its erect, often hairy stems and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant produces yellowish to reddish flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grasslands.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional herbal uses.
