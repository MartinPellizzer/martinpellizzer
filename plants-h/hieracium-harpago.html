Hieracium harpago

Hieracium harpago, commonly known as the hooked hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that consist of a central disk surrounded by ray florets. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Hieracium harpago is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is considered a weed in some regions.
