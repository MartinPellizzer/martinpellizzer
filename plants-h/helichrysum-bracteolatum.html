Helichrysum bracteolatum

Helichrysum bracteolatum, commonly known as the golden everlasting or Cape gold, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its yellow, daisy-like flowers that remain vibrant even after drying, making it popular in dried floral arrangements. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with narrow, gray-green leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Helichrysum bracteolatum is also valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine.
