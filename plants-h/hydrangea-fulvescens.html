Hydrangea fulvescens

Hydrangea fulvescens, commonly known as the red-tinted hydrangea, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that range in color from pink to red, depending on soil pH. The plant produces clusters of small, white to cream-colored flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically large, ovate, and have a glossy texture.

Hydrangea fulvescens thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
