Hieracium murorum subsp. radomense

Hieracium murorum subsp.

radomense, commonly known as the Radom Meadow Hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. It has finely divided, lance-shaped leaves and produces numerous small, yellow florets arranged in a composite head.

This subspecies is often found in moist or calcareous soils and is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
