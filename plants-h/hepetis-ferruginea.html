Hepetis ferruginea

Hepetis ferruginea, commonly known as the rust-hairy tansy, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in dry, sunny grasslands and rocky soils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in compact clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and often have a reddish or rust-colored hue, giving it its common name.

Hepetis ferruginea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
