Hieracium murorum subsp. chloroprasinoides

Hieracium murorum subsp.

chloroprasinoides, commonly known as the green-yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellowish to greenish composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry grasslands and open habitats, preferring well-drained soils. It has hairy stems and leaves that are often glandular, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is noted for its relatively small size and its role in local ecosystems as a food source for various insects.
