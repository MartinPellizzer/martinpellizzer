Hieracium davosense

Hieracium davosense, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky areas, thriving in cool, moist environments. It has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and its stems are usually unbranched.

Hieracium davosense is often found at elevations above 1,500 meters, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil in high-altitude ecosystems.
