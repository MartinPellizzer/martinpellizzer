Heuchera parvifolia var. typica

Heuchera parvifolia var. typica, commonly known as the dwarf red heuchera, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features dense, clump-forming growth with heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep red or bronze color. The plant produces small, bell-shaped white flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental foliage and is commonly used in woodland gardens.
