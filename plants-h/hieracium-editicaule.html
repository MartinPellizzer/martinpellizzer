Hieracium editicaule

Hieracium editicaule, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant features bright yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, with a basal rosette structure that helps it survive in various environmental conditions.

Hieracium editicaule is also known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in many regions.
