Hordeum glaucum

Hordeum glaucum, commonly known as wild barley, is a species of grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical spikelets and bluish-green glumes, which give it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks. Its seeds are small and oval, often used in traditional agriculture and as a forage crop.

Hordeum glaucum plays a role in ecological succession and is also studied for its genetic diversity and adaptation to various environmental conditions.
