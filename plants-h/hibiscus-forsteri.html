Hibiscus forsteri

Hibiscus forsteri, commonly known as the Forster's hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red, with a prominent central pistil and five petals. The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and prefers well-drained soil. Its foliage consists of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy in appearance.

Hibiscus forsteri is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant blooms and attractive foliage.
