Hyparrhenia newtonii

Hyparrhenia newtonii, commonly known as the African silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves that are often silvery-white in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland and grassland habitats.

Hyparrhenia newtonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and as a source of thatching.
