Helianthus annuus var. lenticularis

Helianthus annuus var. lenticularis, commonly known as the lentil-seed sunflower, is a variant of the common sunflower characterized by its unique, lens-shaped seeds.

This variety is native to North America and is cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its seeds. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 feet, featuring large, yellow-orange flower heads with dark centers. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are hairy and sturdy.

The lentil-seed sunflower is well-suited for gardens and can thrive in full sun with well-drained soil.
