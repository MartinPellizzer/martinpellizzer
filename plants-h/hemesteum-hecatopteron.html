Hemesteum hecatopteron

Hemesteum hecatopteron, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow and orange inflorescences, which resemble flames, hence its common name. The plant produces numerous small, fragrant flowers arranged in a dense spike, typical of many orchid species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often growing in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is highly sought after by botanists and horticulturists alike.
