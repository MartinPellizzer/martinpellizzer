Hieracium murorum subsp. subplacerum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

subplacerum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed habitats and is often found in open, sunny areas.
