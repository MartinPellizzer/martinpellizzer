Helenium macrolepis

Helenium macrolepis, commonly known as the narrowleaf helenium, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its striking, orange-red ray flowers surrounded by yellow disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist woodlands and prairies, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture, and its stems are hairy.

Helenium macrolepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
