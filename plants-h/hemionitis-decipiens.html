Hemionitis decipiens

Hemionitis decipiens, commonly known as the false spikerush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in the eastern United States.

It has slender, grass-like leaves that form dense tufts, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown. The plant thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in association with other wetland vegetation. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and colonize new areas effectively.

Hemionitis decipiens plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
