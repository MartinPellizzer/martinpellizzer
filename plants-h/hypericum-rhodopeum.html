Hypericum rhodopeum

Hypericum rhodopeum, commonly known as the Rhodope rosehip, is a flowering plant native to the Rhodope Mountains in Bulgaria.

It is a member of the Hypericaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are rich in antioxidants and have been used in traditional medicine. Hypericum rhodopeum thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to sunny, arid environments.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it a notable species in its native habitat.
