Hesperis violaria

Hesperis violaria, commonly known as the sweet violet, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in woodland and grassland habitats. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in herbal medicine.
