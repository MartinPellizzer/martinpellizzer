Helicia rufescens

Helicia rufescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed holly.

It is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its attractive, glossy evergreen leaves and clusters of small, red berries that appear in late summer. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in lowland forests or on rocky slopes.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Ericaceae, which includes other familiar plants like heathers and blueberries.
