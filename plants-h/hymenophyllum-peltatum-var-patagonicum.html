Hymenophyllum peltatum var. patagonicum

Hymenophyllum peltatum var. patagonicum, commonly known as the Patagonian mossy fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to South America.

It belongs to the Hymenophyllaceae family and is characterized by its small, intricate, and often hairy leaves. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in cool, moist forests. Its fronds are usually narrow and have a distinctive, moss-like appearance.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species.
