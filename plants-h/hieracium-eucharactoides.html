Hieracium eucharactoides

Hieracium eucharactoides, commonly known as the golden hawkweed, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to southern Europe and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun. It has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces numerous small seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
