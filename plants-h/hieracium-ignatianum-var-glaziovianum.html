Hieracium ignatianum var. glaziovianum

Hieracium ignatianum var. glaziovianum, commonly known as the Glaziov's hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellowish to reddish composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It has basal and sometimes upright stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves.

Its flowers are arranged in a discoid head, with both ray and disc florets, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
