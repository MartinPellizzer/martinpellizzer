Hieracium parvimaculatum

Hieracium parvimaculatum, commonly known as the small-spotted hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial species belonging to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its small, yellow-orange flowers appear in summer, and the leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture.

It is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
