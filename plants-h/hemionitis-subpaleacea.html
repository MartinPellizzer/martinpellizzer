Hemionitis subpaleacea

Hemionitis subpaleacea, commonly known as the pale leaf hemionitis, is a rare and delicate fern native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, delicate fronds that are typically pale green to white in color. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded, rocky habitats. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of many fern species.

Due to its limited distribution and sensitivity to environmental changes, it is considered a vulnerable species.
