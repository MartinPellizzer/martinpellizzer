Haloxylon ammodendron var. aphyllum

Haloxylon ammodendron var. aphyllum, commonly known as the hairless haloxylon, is a shrub native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, woody stems and reduced leaves, which help it conserve water in harsh desert environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are adapted for pollination by wind or insects. Its seeds are encased in a hard, fibrous coat, allowing them to remain viable in the soil for extended periods.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing shelter for desert wildlife.
