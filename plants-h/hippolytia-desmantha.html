Hippolytia desmantha

Hippolytia desmantha, commonly known as the Cape honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has linear, succulent leaves that help it conserve water in arid environments. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract bees and birds, contributing to local pollination and biodiversity.
