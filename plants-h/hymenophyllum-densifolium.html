Hymenophyllum densifolium

Hymenophyllum densifolium, commonly known as the dense-leaved filmy fern, is a delicate, epiphytic fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, leathery fronds that are often covered in a dense layer of tiny, scale-like structures. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spore-producing structures, called sorocarps, are borne on the underside of the fronds.

Due to its intricate morphology and habitat requirements, it is a popular subject in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
