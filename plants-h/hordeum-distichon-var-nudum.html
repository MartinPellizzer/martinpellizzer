Hordeum distichon var. nudum

Hordeum distichon var. nudum, commonly known as the naked barley, is a wild grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, spike-like inflorescence and reduced glumes, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its adaptability and potential role in ecological restoration.
