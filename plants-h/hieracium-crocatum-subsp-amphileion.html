Hieracium crocatum subsp. amphileion

Hieracium crocatum subsp.

amphileion, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky and open habitats, often at high elevations.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
