Helianthemum spartioides

Helianthemum spartioides, commonly known as the rock rose, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant gardening.
