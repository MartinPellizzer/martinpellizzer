Hieracium reidigense

Hieracium reidigense, commonly known as the Reidigense hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering stems that bear yellow composite flowers. Native to Europe, it thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. The plant has serrated leaves and produces seeds in small, dry capsules.

It is considered a minor weed in agricultural settings but has ecological significance in its native habitats.
