Hydnophytum petiolatum var. petiolatum

Hydnophytum petiolatum var. petiolatum, commonly known as the climbing bladderwort, is a unique aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bladder-like structures, which are used to trap small aquatic organisms. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of slow-moving water bodies. Its leaves are usually reduced to small, scale-like structures, while its stems are often climbing or trailing.

This species plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and providing habitat for microorganisms.
