Heuchera curtisii

Heuchera curtisii, commonly known as Curtis' alumroot, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features basal leaves that are typically heart-shaped with a waxy texture and deep green to reddish-purple hues. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale pink flowers in the spring, which are borne on slender, erect stems. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental foliage and early blooming habit contribute to its horticultural value.
