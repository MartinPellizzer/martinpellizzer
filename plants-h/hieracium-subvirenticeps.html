Hieracium subvirenticeps

Hieracium subvirenticeps, commonly known as the green-headed hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow to greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has rough, hairy leaves that are typically lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and other open areas.

Hieracium subvirenticeps is a perennial herb that spreads through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in various habitats.
