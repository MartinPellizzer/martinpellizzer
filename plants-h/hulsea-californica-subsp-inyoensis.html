Hulsea californica subsp. inyoensis

Hulsea californica subsp.

inyoensis, commonly known as Inyo hulsea, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped and have a serrated margin.

The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This subspecies is adapted to arid conditions and is an important component of its native ecosystem.
