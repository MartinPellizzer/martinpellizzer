Hibiscus sturtii var. campylochlamys

Hibiscus sturtii var. campylochlamys, commonly known as the narrow-leafed hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep red. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It is a perennial shrub that can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

The flowers bloom during the warmer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.
