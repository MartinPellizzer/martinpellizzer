Helenium collinum

Helenium collinum, commonly known as the prairie sneezeweed, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies, meadows, and disturbed soils. Its vibrant orange to yellow flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Helenium collinum is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
