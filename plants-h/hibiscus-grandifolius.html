Hibiscus grandifolius

Hibiscus grandifolius, commonly known as the large-leaf hibiscus, is a species native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with a yellow throat. Hibiscus grandifolius thrives in moist, well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
