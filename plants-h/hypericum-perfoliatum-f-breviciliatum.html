Hypericum perfoliatum f. breviciliatum

Hypericum perfoliatum f. breviciliatum, commonly known as the short-stalked St. John's wort, is a compact variant of the common St. John's wort.

It features small, yellowish-orange flowers with prominent stamens, typical of the Hypericaceae family. The plant has opposite, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This form is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its compact growth and flowering habit.
