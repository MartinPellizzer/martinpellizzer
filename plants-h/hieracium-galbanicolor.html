Hieracium galbanicolor

Hieracium galbanicolor, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features bright yellow flowers with a central disk surrounded by ray florets, and it often grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Hieracium galbanicolor is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
