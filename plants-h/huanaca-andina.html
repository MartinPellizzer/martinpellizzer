Huanaca andina

Huanaca andina, commonly known as the Andean moonflower, is a climbing plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant produces small, round berries that contain seeds used in traditional Andean medicine. Huanaca andina thrives in cool, humid climates and is often found in cloud forests.

Its botanical features make it a unique and ecologically significant species in the region.
