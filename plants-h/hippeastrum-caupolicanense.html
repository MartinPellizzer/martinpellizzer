Hippeastrum caupolicanense

Hippeastrum caupolicanense, commonly known as the Caupolican lily, is a species of amaryllid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 cm in diameter, typically in shades of white, pink, or red. The plant produces striking, strap-like leaves that are often variegated with green and white markings. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Hippeastrum caupolicanense is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in garden settings.
