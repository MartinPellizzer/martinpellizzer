Hoseanthus lobbii

Hoseanthus lobbii, commonly known as the Lobb's honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Hoseanthus lobbii thrives in humid environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.
