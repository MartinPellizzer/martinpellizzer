Helichrysum cymosum subsp. fruticosum

Helichrysum cymosum subsp.

fruticosum, commonly known as the coastal helichrysum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions.

The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that are often arranged in dense, rounded clusters. Its foliage is usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it is known for its drought tolerance.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture.
