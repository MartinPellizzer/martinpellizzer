Hemigenia benthamii

Hemigenia benthamii, commonly known as the mountain hemigenia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are typically purple or pink, arranged in whorls along the stem, and bloom during the spring and summer months.

Hemigenia benthamii is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
