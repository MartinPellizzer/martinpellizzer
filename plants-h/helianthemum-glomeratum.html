Helianthemum glomeratum

Helianthemum glomeratum, commonly known as the clustered rockrose, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has aromatic, glandular leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Helianthemum glomeratum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
