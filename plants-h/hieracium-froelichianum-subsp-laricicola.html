Hieracium froelichianum subsp. laricicola

Hieracium froelichianum subsp.

laricicola, commonly known as the mountain hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and forest edges. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptation to high-altitude environments and its role in local ecosystems.
