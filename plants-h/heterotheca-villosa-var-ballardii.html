Heterotheca villosa var. ballardii

Heterotheca villosa var. ballardii, commonly known as the Ballard's hairy goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its yellow flowers bloom in the summer, adding a splash of color to its native habitat.

It is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in drought-tolerant gardens.
