Hiraea ternifolia var. granatensis

Hiraea ternifolia var. granatensis, commonly known as the Granatense Three-leafed Hiraea, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically glossy and have a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the spring and summer months, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
