Hieracium juranum subsp. tournefortianum

Hieracium juranum subsp.

tournefortianum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, temperate climates. It produces yellow-orange flower heads that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and often have a reddish or purple tinge, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
