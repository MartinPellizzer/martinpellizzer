Homalomena johorensis

Homalomena johorensis, commonly known as the Johore arrowroot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant features broad, arrowhead-shaped leaves that are often a deep green with a glossy texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically followed by berries containing toxic seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and traditional uses.
