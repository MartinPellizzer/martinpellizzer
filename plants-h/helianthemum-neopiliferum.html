Helianthemum neopiliferum

Helianthemum neopiliferum, commonly known as the rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and showy yellow or orange flowers. The plant thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to arid environments. Its flowers typically bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping.
