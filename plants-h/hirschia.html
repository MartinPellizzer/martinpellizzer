Hirschia

Hirschia, commonly known as the "pincushion flower," is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its unique, spiky, and often colorful flower heads that resemble a pincushion, hence its common name. Hirschia species are typically found in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils. The plants are perennial, with some species producing showy blooms in shades of yellow, orange, and red.

Their botanical features make them popular in xeriscaping and native gardens due to their drought tolerance and ornamental value.
