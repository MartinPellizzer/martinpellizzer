Hygrophila conferta

Hygrophila conferta, commonly known as the water hyssop, is a semi-aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in ponds, streams, and wetlands. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. Its small, tubular flowers are usually blue or purple and bloom in clusters.

Hygrophila conferta is often used in aquariums and water gardens due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in aquatic environments.
