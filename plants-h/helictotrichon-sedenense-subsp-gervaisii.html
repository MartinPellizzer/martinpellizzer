Helictotrichon sedenense subsp. gervaisii

Helictotrichon sedenense subsp.

gervaisii, commonly known as the Gervais' needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a loose, open panicle bearing small, spikelet-like flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
