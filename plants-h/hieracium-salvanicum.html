Hieracium salvanicum

Hieracium salvanicum, commonly known as the Salvan's hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. It has basal leaves that are broadly lance-shaped and hairy, with a rosette formation.

Hieracium salvanicum is often found in temperate regions of Europe and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
