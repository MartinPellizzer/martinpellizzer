Hieracium levicaule subsp. macrocentrum

Hieracium levicaule subsp.

macrocentrum, commonly known as the large-centred hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and tall, branched stems that bear yellow ray flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its flowers are typically visited by various pollinators, including bees and flies.

The subspecies is distinguished by its larger, more prominent flower heads compared to other members of the species.
