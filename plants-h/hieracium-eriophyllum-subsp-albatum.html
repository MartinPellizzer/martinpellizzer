Hieracium eriophyllum subsp. albatum

Hieracium eriophyllum subsp.

albatum, commonly known as the albatross hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it tolerates cold and harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in alpine ecosystems.
