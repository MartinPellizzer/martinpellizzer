Helianthus californicus var. parishii

Helianthus californicus var. parishii, commonly known as the Parish's common sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its yellow, daisy-like flowers and linear, grass-like leaves. This variety thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces numerous seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread.

The plant is adapted to arid conditions, making it a resilient member of its ecosystem.
