Hieracium alatum subsp. seguierianum

Hieracium alatum subsp.

seguierianum, commonly known as the Seguier's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically has hairy stems and leaves that are lanceolate in shape. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
