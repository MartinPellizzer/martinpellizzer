Hermione anceps

Hermione anceps, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, pale green leaves and clusters of violet-blue flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, round berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle it with care.
