Hydrangea mathewsii

Hydrangea mathewsii, commonly known as the Hainan hydrangea, is a species native to the island of Hainan in China.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous small, sterile flowers that are often blue or pink in color. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape, with a smooth margin and a dark green upper surface.

Hydrangea mathewsii prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas with partial shade.
