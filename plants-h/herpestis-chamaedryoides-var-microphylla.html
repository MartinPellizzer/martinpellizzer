Herpestis chamaedryoides var. microphylla

Herpestis chamaedryoides var. microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf moonflower, is a low-growing, evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are usually less than 1 inch in length, giving it a delicate appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its compact size and fragrant blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
