Helianthemum variabile var. racemosum

Helianthemum variabile var. racemosum, commonly known as the variable rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub with woody stems and small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne on long, raceme-like inflorescences. It thrives in rocky or dry soils and is well adapted to coastal and arid environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
