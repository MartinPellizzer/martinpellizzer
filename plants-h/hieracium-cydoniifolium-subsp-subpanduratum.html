Hieracium cydoniifolium subsp. subpanduratum

Hieracium cydoniifolium subsp.

subpanduratum, commonly known as the golden hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and distinctive, broad, heart-shaped leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has a robust root system and can spread through both seeds and vegetative means.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Asteraceae family.
