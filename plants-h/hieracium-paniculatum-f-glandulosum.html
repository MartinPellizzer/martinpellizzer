Hieracium paniculatum f. glandulosum

Hieracium paniculatum f. glandulosum, commonly known as the glandular hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy, glandular leaves that give it its specific epithet. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has a creeping rootstock and produces numerous flowering stems.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural collections.
