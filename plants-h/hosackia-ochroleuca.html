Hosackia ochroleuca

Hosackia ochroleuca, commonly known as the white prairie clover, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in open prairies and disturbed soils, and it plays a role in nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with rhizobia bacteria.

Its botanical features include a slender stem, small heart-shaped leaves, and pods that contain several seeds.
