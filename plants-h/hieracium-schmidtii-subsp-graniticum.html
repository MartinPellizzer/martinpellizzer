Hieracium schmidtii subsp. graniticum

Hieracium schmidtii subsp.

graniticum, commonly known as the granitic hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, dark green leaves and produces small, yellowish to reddish composite flowers.

The plant thrives in rocky and well-drained soils, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. It is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including cold temperatures and limited soil nutrients.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endemic species in its native range.
