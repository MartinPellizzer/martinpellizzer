Hieracium confertum

Hieracium confertum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and numerous yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils across temperate regions of Europe and Asia. It has hairy stems and leaves that are often glandular, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Hieracium confertum is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common and sometimes invasive species in certain habitats.
