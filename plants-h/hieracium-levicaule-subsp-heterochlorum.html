Hieracium levicaule subsp. heterochlorum

Hieracium levicaule subsp.

heterochlorum, commonly known as the variable hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering stems that bear yellowish to reddish composite flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its variable leaf coloration, ranging from green to reddish or purplish hues. It typically grows in moist meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils.

The plant has finely toothed leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that attract various pollinators.
