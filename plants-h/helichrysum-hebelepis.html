Helichrysum hebelepis

Helichrysum hebelepis, commonly known as the Cape goldcup, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, yellow-orange inflorescences that resemble small cups, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Helichrysum hebelepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscapes for its drought tolerance and long-lasting blooms.
