Hieracium murorum subsp. umbelliferum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

umbelliferum, commonly known as the common wood hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats such as roadsides and meadows.

The plant features upright stems with alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of yellowish flowers arranged in an umbel.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
