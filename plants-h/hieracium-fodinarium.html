Hieracium fodinarium

Hieracium fodinarium, commonly known as the lesser hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its yellowish to orange-red flowers and hairy, often reddish stems. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Hieracium fodinarium is a perennial species, often found across Europe and parts of Asia.
