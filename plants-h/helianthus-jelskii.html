Helianthus jelskii

Helianthus jelskii, commonly known as the Jelski's sunflower, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its bright yellow ray florets and a central disk of smaller florets. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 feet and produces daisy-like flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Helianthus jelskii is adapted to moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
