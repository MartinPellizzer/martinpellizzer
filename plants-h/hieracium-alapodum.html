Hieracium alapodum

Hieracium alapodum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cold, high-altitude environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium alapodum is known for its resilience to harsh climatic conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
