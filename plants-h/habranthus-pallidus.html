Habranthus pallidus

Habranthus pallidus, commonly known as the pale habranthus, is a flowering plant native to South America, specifically found in regions of Brazil, Paraguay, and Argentina.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along riverbanks.

Its flowers are usually pale yellow to white, with a distinctive hooded shape that attracts pollinators like bees and moths.
