Hariota teres

Hariota teres, commonly known as the mountain holly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which consists of glossy, dark green leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns red when mature, attracting various bird species.

Hariota teres is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.
