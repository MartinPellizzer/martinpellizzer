Hymenasplenium subnormale

Hymenasplenium subnormale, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water clover, is a perennial aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern on the water's surface. The plant produces tiny, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in shallow, still water and is often found in ponds and wetlands.

Its botanical structure supports both aquatic and terrestrial ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various organisms.
