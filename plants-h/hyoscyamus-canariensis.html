Hyoscyamus canariensis

Hyoscyamus canariensis, commonly known as the Canary Islands angel's trumpet, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its large, pendulous, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant produces toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals. It thrives in warm, dry climates and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
