Hieracium auriculigenum

Hieracium auriculigenum, commonly known as the auriculate hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a distinctive auriculate base, giving it its common name.

The yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
