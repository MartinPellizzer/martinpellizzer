Hieracium wolgense

Hieracium wolgense, commonly known as the Wolgian hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are hairy and alternate, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium wolgense is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
