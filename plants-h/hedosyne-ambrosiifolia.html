Hedosyne ambrosiifolia

Hedosyne ambrosiifolia, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange inflorescences that resemble clouds or mist, hence its common name. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in prairies, open woodlands, and along roadsides.

Hedosyne ambrosiifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
