Hydrangea hortensia f. variegata

Hydrangea hortensia f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated hydrangea, is a cultivar characterized by its striking variegated foliage.

The leaves exhibit a mix of green, white, and sometimes pink, creating a visually appealing contrast. This variety thrives in partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil. It produces large, cone-shaped flower clusters that range from white to pink, depending on soil pH.

The plant is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to add color and texture to landscapes.
