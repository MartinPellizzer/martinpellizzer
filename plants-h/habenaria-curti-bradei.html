Habenaria curti-bradei

Habenaria curti-bradei, commonly known as the Curti-Brade's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a raceme, which are typically fragrant. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
