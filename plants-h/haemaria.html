Haemaria

Haemaria, commonly known as the red lily or red-flowered lily, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Liliaceae.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink flowers, which are typically bell-shaped and borne on slender stems. The leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to the plant's ornamental appeal. Haemaria species are native to regions of southern Africa, where they thrive in well-drained soils and full sun.

These plants are valued for their beauty and are often cultivated in gardens for their vibrant floral display.
