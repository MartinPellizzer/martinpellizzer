Hedysarum pedunculatum

Hedysarum pedunculatum, commonly known as the sweet clover, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow or white flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural settings. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

Hedysarum pedunculatum is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
