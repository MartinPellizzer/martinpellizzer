Hyphaene ventricosa subsp. plagiocarpa

Hyphaene ventricosa subsp.

plagiocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed date palm, is a species native to the arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, clustered growth habit and long, narrow leaves that are often used for thatching.

The plant produces large, clustered inflorescences that bear edible, sweet fruits similar to dates. It has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in dry, sandy soils.

This subspecies is valued both for its ecological role and its utility in traditional agriculture.
