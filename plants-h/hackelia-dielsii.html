Hackelia dielsii

Hackelia dielsii, commonly known as the Sierra hackelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing herbaceous perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and its most striking characteristic is its clusters of pink to purple tubular flowers. It thrives in rocky soils and open habitats, often found in mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
