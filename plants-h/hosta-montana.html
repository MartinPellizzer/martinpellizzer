Hosta montana

Hosta montana, commonly known as the mountain hosta, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically variegated with white and green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of blue or purple during the summer months. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Hosta montana is valued for its ornamental foliage and is a popular choice for woodland gardens.
