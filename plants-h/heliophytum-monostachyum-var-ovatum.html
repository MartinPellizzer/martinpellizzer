Heliophytum monostachyum var. ovatum

Heliophytum monostachyum var. ovatum, commonly known as the golden bell orchid, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that resemble small bells. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sunlight. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces a single, elongated inflorescence with clustered flowers.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens with well-draining soil and adequate light.
