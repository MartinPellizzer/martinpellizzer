Hieracium psammogenes subsp. megalotomum

Hieracium psammogenes subsp.

megalotomum, commonly known as the giant sandweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in coastal or arid environments. It produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is distinguished by its larger flowers and robust growth compared to other Hieracium species.
