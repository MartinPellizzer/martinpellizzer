Hordeum jubatum subsp. brachyantherum

Hordeum jubatum subsp.

brachyantherum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wild barley, is a member of the Poaceae family. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescence.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and fields. Its flowers are small and wind-pollinated, with pale to greenish-yellow glumes.

This subspecies is often found in temperate regions of Europe and Asia, where it plays a role in ecological succession and seed dispersal.
