Huperzia pseudophlegmaria

Huperzia pseudophlegmaria, commonly known as the false phlegmaria, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, dense growth habit and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant produces sporophytes with spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are typically clustered in dense clusters on the underside of the leaves. Huperzia pseudophlegmaria is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptability make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
