Hieracium inuloides subsp. subinuloides

Hieracium inuloides subsp.

subinuloides, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are lanceolate, with a hairy surface, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

It is a common weed in agricultural and urban environments, often spreading through rhizomes and vegetative reproduction.
