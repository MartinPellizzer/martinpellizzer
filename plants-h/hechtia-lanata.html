Hechtia lanata

Hechtia lanata, commonly known as the woolly hechtia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Bromeliaceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are densely covered with soft, woolly hairs. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers that emerge from the center of the rosette. Hechtia lanata thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
