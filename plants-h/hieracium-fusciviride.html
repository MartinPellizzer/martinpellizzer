Hieracium fusciviride

Hieracium fusciviride, commonly known as the greenish hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dark green, hairy leaves. The plant produces yellowish to orange flowers arranged in a composite head, which are typically visited by various pollinators. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
