Hieracium murorum subsp. oxlaense

Hieracium murorum subsp.

oxlaense, commonly known as the Oxlaen hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe. It typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

The plant has dark green, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and ragworts.

Its subspecies status highlights its distinct morphological and genetic characteristics compared to other Hieracium species.
