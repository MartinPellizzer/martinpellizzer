Haloxylon scoparium

Haloxylon scoparium, commonly known as the saxaul, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots and a thick, waxy coating on its leaves to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, green or yellowish flowers that are pollinated by insects, followed by the development of small, dry fruits containing seeds. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing shelter for wildlife.

Haloxylon scoparium is also valued for its hardy nature and has been used in traditional medicine and as a source of firewood.
