Hieracium regillatiforme

Hieracium regillatiforme, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow to orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. It is native to Europe and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
