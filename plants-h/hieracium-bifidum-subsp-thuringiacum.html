Hieracium bifidum subsp. thuringiacum

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

thuringiacum, commonly known as the Thuringian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to reddish composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. It has alternate, lanceolate leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often hairy.

This subspecies is distinguished by its distinctive flower structure and habitat preferences.
