Hirtella latifolia

Hirtella latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf hirtella, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, broad leaves and small, clustered white flowers that bloom throughout the year. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
