Hypericum lanuginosum subsp. gracile

Hypericum lanuginosum subsp.

gracile, commonly known as the woolly St. John's wort, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of its branches.

The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the Hypericaceae family, valued for both its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
