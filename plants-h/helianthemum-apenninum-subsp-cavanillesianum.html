Helianthemum apenninum subsp. cavanillesianum

Helianthemum apenninum subsp.

cavanillesianum, commonly known as the Sicilian rock rose, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters along the stems.

The plant has greyish-green, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in its arid habitat. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
