Hieracium caesium subsp. similifolium

Hieracium caesium subsp.

similifolium, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, preferring well-drained conditions. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are arranged in a composite head.

The species is noted for its adaptability and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
