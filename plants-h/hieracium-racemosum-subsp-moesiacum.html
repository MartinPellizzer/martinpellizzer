Hieracium racemosum subsp. moesiacum

Hieracium racemosum subsp.

moesiacum, commonly known as the Moesian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 50 centimeters.

The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces racemes of yellow flowers with prominent central disks. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flower heads are surrounded by pale yellow ray florets.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower structure and ability to produce numerous seeds.
