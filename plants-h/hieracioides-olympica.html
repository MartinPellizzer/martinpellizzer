Hieracioides olympica

Hieracioides olympica, commonly known as Olympic mountain everlasting, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high elevations of the Olympic Peninsula in Washington State.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and foliage with a silvery or grayish hue. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cool, moist climates.
