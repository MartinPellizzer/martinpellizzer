Hieracium nigrescens subsp. gymnogeniforme

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

gymnogeniforme, commonly known as the black-centred hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a dark center.

This subspecies is notable for its distinctive coloration and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
