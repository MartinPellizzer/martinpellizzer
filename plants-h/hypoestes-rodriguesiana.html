Hypoestes rodriguesiana

Hypoestes rodriguesiana, commonly known as the Madagascar flame flower, is a strikingly ornamental shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly hairy underside. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and limited natural range, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in tropical gardens.
