Helianthemum croceum var. tananicum

Helianthemum croceum var. tananicum, commonly known as the Tananian sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It thrives in rocky, arid, and coastal environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to its habitat. Its leaves are typically succulent, helping it retain moisture in dry climates.

This variety is part of the rockrose family (Cistaceae) and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils.
