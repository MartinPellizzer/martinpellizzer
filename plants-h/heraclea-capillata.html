Heraclea capillata

Heraclea capillata, commonly known as the slender hellebore, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves that are typically lance-shaped. The plant produces a spadix surrounded by a pale green spathe, which is typical of the genus Heraclea. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Heraclea capillata is noted for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
