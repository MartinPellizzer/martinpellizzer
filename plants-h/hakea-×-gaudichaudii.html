Hakea × gaudichaudii

Hakea × gaudichaudii, commonly known as the coastal hakea, is a hybrid plant native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its woody, shrub-like structure with dense, needle-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces striking, cylindrical flower spikes that range in color from yellow to red, depending on the species. It is adapted to coastal conditions, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils.

Hakea × gaudichaudii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in coastal landscapes.
