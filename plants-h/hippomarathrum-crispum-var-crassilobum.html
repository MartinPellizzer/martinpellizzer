Hippomarathrum crispum var. crassilobum

Hippomarathrum crispum var. crassilobum, commonly known as the broad-leaved sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves that are broad and slightly lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal and inland deserts.

Its adaptations to arid environments include deep root systems and a short growing season.
