Hippeastrum mexicanum

Hippeastrum mexicanum, commonly known as the Mexican amaryllis, is a bulbous plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It produces striking, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant features strap-like, strap-shaped leaves that are broad and grass-like, emerging from a fleshy underground bulb. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to bright light for optimal growth.

Hippeastrum mexicanum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant flowers and relatively easy care requirements.
