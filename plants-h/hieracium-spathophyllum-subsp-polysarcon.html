Hieracium spathophyllum subsp. polysarcon

Hieracium spathophyllum subsp.

polysarcon, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy structure.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
