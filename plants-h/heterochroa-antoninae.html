Heterochroa antoninae

The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as forest understories, where it relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its leaves are narrow and strap-like, adapted to conserve moisture in its natural habitat.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, Heterochroa antoninae is considered endangered, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
