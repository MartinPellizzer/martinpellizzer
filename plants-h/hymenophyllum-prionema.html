Hymenophyllum prionema

Hymenophyllum prionema, commonly known as the silver fern, is a delicate and intricate moss-like plant belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like leaves that are often covered in a silvery or pale green coating, giving it its common name. This species thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are rich in chlorophyll and contain specialized structures called rhizoids that anchor it to surfaces.

Hymenophyllum prionema plays a significant ecological role in maintaining soil moisture and supporting microfauna in its habitat.
