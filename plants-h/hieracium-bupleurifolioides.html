Hieracium bupleurifolioides

Hieracium bupleurifolioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy structures. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it is known for its basal rosette of leaves. Its leaves are lanceolate to linear, with a hairy surface, and the flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is often found in temperate climates and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
