Hydrangea integerrima

Hydrangea integerrima, commonly known as the mountain hydrangea, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that range in color from white to pink and blue, depending on soil pH. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are large, ovate, and often have a serrated edge.

Hydrangea integerrima is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to bloom repeatedly throughout the growing season.
