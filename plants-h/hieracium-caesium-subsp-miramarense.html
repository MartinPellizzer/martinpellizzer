Hieracium caesium subsp. miramarense

Hieracium caesium subsp.

miramarense, commonly known as the Miramare hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features basal rosettes of dark green, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It has a taproot system and is characterized by its slender, branched stems.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinator populations.
