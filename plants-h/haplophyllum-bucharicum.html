Haplophyllum bucharicum

Haplophyllum bucharicum, commonly known as the Bukharan holly, is a low-growing shrub native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, dark green leaves. The plant produces white or pale pink flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, red berries. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
