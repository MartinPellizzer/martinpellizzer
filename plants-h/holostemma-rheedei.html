Holostemma rheedei

Holostemma rheedei, commonly known as the Indian star jasmine, is a climbing shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its fragrant, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Holostemma rheedei thrives in warm, humid climates and is adaptable to various soil types.
