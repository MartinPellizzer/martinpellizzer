Hypericum repens var. vulgare

Hypericum repens var. vulgare, commonly known as creeping St. John's wort, is a low-growing evergreen perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, linear leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used for their anti-inflammatory and antidepressant effects.
