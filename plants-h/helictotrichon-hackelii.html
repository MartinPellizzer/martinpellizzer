Helictotrichon hackelii

Helictotrichon hackelii, commonly known as the Hackel's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet-bearing panicle, with small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Helictotrichon hackelii plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife in its native range.
