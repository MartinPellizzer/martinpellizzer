Hieracium magyaricum subsp. armeniacum

Hieracium magyaricum subsp.

armeniacum, commonly known as Armenian hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the Carpathian Basin and parts of Eastern Europe. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and forest edges.

The plant has yellow composite flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, with small, thread-like ray florets. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and hairy, and it produces numerous seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is characterized by its robust growth and adaptability to various soil conditions.
