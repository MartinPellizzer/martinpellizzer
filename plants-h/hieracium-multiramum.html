Hieracium multiramum

Hieracium multiramum, commonly known as the many-branched hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and is characterized by its dense, hairy stems and yellow ray flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Hieracium multiramum is often found in open habitats and is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
