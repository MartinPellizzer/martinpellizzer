Hepatica asiatica f. yuseongii

Hepatica asiatica f. yuseongii, commonly known as the Yuseong hepatica, is a rare and unique variant of the hepatica plant native to South Korea.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves and showy, five-petaled blue or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in forest understories or along stream banks. Its flowers often have a waxy texture and a subtle fragrance, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered an important species for botanical research and conservation efforts.
