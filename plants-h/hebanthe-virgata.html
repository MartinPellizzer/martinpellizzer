Hebanthe virgata

Hebanthe virgata, commonly known as the silverleaf hebanthe, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a silvery underside, giving it its common name. It produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months.

Hebanthe virgata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in shaded garden settings.
