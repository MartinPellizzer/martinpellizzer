Hieracium galactobaptum

Hieracium galactobaptum, commonly known as the milk thistle, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of western Asia. The plant is characterized by its distinctive purple flowers and spiny, lobed leaves. Its most notable feature is the milky sap that exudes from the stems and leaves when damaged.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is known for its potential medicinal properties.
