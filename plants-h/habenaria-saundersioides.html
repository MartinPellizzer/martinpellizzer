Habenaria saundersioides

Habenaria saundersioides, commonly known as the Saunders' orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to the wet tropical forests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, greenish-yellow to white flowers with three sepals and three petals, often with a distinctive lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable in some regions.
