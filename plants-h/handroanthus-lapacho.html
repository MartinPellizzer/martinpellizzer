Handroanthus lapacho

Handroanthus lapacho, commonly known as the pink trumpet tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking pink or purple tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. The tree has a straight, smooth trunk and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy surface.

The plant is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
