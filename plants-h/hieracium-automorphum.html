Hieracium automorphum

Hieracium automorphum, commonly known as the autumn hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are alternate, rough, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

The plant is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
