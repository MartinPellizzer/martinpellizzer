Hieracium sagittatum subsp. lanuginosum

Hieracium sagittatum subsp.

lanuginosum, commonly known as the woolly spearhead hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers that are typically arranged in a flat-topped cluster. It thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, often in temperate climates.

Its subspecific name, *lanuginosum*, refers to the soft, woolly texture of its foliage.
