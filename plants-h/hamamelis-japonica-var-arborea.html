Hamamelis japonica var. arborea

Hamamelis japonica var. arborea, commonly known as the Japanese witch hazel tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its fragrant, orange-yellow flowers that bloom in late autumn or early winter. The plant has opposite, elliptic leaves that turn yellow in the fall. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
