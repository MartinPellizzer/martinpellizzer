Hemionitis bolborrhiza

Hemionitis bolborrhiza, commonly known as the ghost fern, is a unique plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is a small, delicate fern that thrives in harsh, dry environments, often growing on rocky surfaces or in sandy soils. Its fronds are narrow and delicate, with a pale green color that gives it a ghostly appearance. The plant has a short, creeping rhizome and produces small, inconspicuous spore-bearing structures.

It is well adapted to survive in low-nutrient, drought-prone conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
