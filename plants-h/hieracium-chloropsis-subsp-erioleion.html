Hieracium chloropsis subsp. erioleion

Hieracium chloropsis subsp.

erioleion, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The subspecies erioleion is distinguished by its dense, woolly foliage and its smaller, more clustered flower heads compared to other members of the species.
