Hieracium lanugineum

Hieracium lanugineum, commonly known as the downy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and woodland edges, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in clusters, producing small seeds that are dispersed by the wind.
