Huernia somalica

Huernia somalica, commonly known as the Somalian moon cactus, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of Somalia and Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems that store water. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red, typically during the warmer months. Its leaves are often reduced to spines, which help reduce water loss in harsh environments.

Huernia somalica is well adapted to dry climates and requires minimal watering, making it a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
