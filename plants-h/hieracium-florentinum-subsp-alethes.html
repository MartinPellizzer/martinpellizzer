Hieracium florentinum subsp. alethes

Hieracium florentinum subsp.

alethes, commonly known as the Florentine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowering heads and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats such as roadsides and meadows. It has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
