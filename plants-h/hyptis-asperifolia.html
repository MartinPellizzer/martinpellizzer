Hyptis asperifolia

Hyptis asperifolia, commonly known as roughleaf hyptis, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, often attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to semi-shaded environments.

Hyptis asperifolia is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
