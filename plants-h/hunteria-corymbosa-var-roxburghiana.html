Hunteria corymbosa var. roxburghiana

Hunteria corymbosa var. roxburghiana, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its attractive, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces small, round, yellow fruits that contain seeds. Its wood is highly valued for its density and aromatic properties.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
