Hymenophyllum ferrugineum subsp. berteroi

Hymenophyllum ferrugineum subsp.

berteroi, commonly known as the rusty toothed hymenophyllum, is a small, epiphytic fern that thrives in moist, shaded environments. It is characterized by its finely divided, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of hair-like structures.

The fronds are typically dark green with a reddish or rusty hue on the underside, giving it its common name. It belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae and is native to various regions in Asia and Australia.

This species is often found on tree trunks and rocks in humid, tropical or subtropical climates.
