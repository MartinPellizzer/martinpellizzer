Heracleum lanatum f. dissectum

Heracleum lanatum f. dissectum, commonly known as the giant hogweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, umbrella-like inflorescence and finely dissected, fern-like leaves. The plant can grow up to 5 meters tall, making it one of the tallest members of its family. Its stems are covered with coarse, hairy ridges and are often reddish in color.

The flowers are small and white, arranged in dense clusters, and the plant is known for its toxic sap, which can cause severe skin irritation.
