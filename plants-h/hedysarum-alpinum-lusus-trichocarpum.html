Hedysarum alpinum lusus trichocarpum

Hedysarum alpinum lusus trichocarpum, commonly known as the alpine milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellow to orange flowers in clusters. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds.

Hedysarum alpinum lusus trichocarpum is part of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic relationships with bacteria.
