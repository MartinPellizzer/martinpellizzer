Hippia gracilis

Hippia gracilis, commonly known as the slender hippia, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features delicate, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually greenish-white and bloom in the spring.

Hippia gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
