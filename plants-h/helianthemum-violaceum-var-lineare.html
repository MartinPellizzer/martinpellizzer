Helianthemum violaceum var. lineare

Helianthemum violaceum var. lineare, commonly known as the linear rockrose, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub with slender, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, giving it a distinctive appearance that is well adapted to rocky and arid environments.

It is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
