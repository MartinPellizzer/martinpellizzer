Habenaria chidori

Habenaria chidori, commonly known as the Japanese lady's tresses, is a terrestrial orchid native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and dense clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in a loose raceme. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it produces a single, elongated spur at the base of each flower.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
