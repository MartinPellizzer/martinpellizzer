Huetia pumila

Huetia pumila, commonly known as the little pumice plant, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America. This plant is characterized by its compact, rosette-shaped foliage that resembles the appearance of pumice stone. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often hidden within the leaf axils.

Huetia pumila thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on tree trunks or rocks in its natural habitat.
