Hakea glabella

Hakea glabella, commonly known as the smooth hakea, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, smooth, grey-green stems and dense clusters of small, needle-like leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in winter, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with deep root systems to access water.

Hakea glabella is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
