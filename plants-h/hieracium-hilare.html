Hieracium hilare

Hieracium hilare, commonly known as the cheerful hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early autumn. The plant has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stem is upright and often branched. Native to Europe, it thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
