Hedyotis gracilis

Hedyotis gracilis, commonly known as slender mistletoe, is a small evergreen shrub native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are often consumed by birds, aiding in seed dispersal. Hedyotis gracilis thrives in shaded, moist environments, typically found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
