Hypoxis triflora

Hypoxis triflora, commonly known as the starry false shamrock, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, star-shaped flowers and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white, three-lobed bracts that surround the flower cluster. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ease of cultivation.

Hypoxis triflora is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
