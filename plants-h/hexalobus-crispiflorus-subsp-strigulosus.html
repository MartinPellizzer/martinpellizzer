Hexalobus crispiflorus subsp. strigulosus

Hexalobus crispiflorus subsp.

strigulosus, commonly known as the hairy leaf hexalobus, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia. It features elliptical to ovate leaves that are covered in fine, appressed hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance.

The flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, typically displaying shades of white or pale yellow. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
