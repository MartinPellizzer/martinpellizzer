Hyptis lavandulacea

Hyptis lavandulacea, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, often resembling lavender. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Hyptis lavandulacea is also valued for its medicinal properties, used traditionally in some cultures for its calming and anti-inflammatory effects.
