Helichrysum whyteanum

Helichrysum whyteanum, commonly known as the Cape goldring, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

The flowers are also used in traditional medicine and are known for their aromatic properties.
