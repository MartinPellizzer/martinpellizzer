Hedera angularis

Hedera angularis, commonly known as the angular ivy, is a species of climbing plant in the Araliaceae family.

It is native to Europe and is characterized by its distinctive, angular leaves that are typically heart-shaped with a notched base. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in shaded environments and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Hedera angularis is also valued for its ability to climb and cover walls or trellises effectively.
