Hexalectris spicata f. albolabia

Hexalectris spicata f. albolabia, commonly known as the white-lipped false dragonhead, is a flowering plant belonging to the gentian family (Gentianaceae).

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as sandy soils and rocky areas. The plant features showy, tubular flowers that are usually pink to purple in color, with a distinctive white lip that distinguishes this form from other varieties. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant adaptations.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and hardiness.
