Hieracium pilosella subsp. pilifolium

Hieracium pilosella subsp.

pilifolium, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in late spring to summer. Its leaves are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
