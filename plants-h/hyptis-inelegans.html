Hyptis inelegans

Hyptis inelegans, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or lavender flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

Hyptis inelegans is also valued as a ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and fragrant aroma.
