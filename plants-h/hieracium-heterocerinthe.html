Hieracium heterocerinthe

Hieracium heterocerinthe, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Hieracium heterocerinthe is a perennial species that can spread through rhizomes, forming dense colonies in suitable habitats.
