Habenaria macvaughiana

Habenaria macvaughiana, commonly known as the MacVaugh's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as pine flatwoods and wetlands. The plant features a slender, upright stem with several elongated, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, greenish-white with purple veins, and are arranged in a loose raceme.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate floral structure and specialized pollination mechanisms.
