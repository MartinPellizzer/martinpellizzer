Hemizonia arida

Hemizonia arida, commonly known as the desert hemizonia, is a perennial shrub native to arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves that reduce water loss and help it survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a subtle ornamental value. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in nutrient-poor soils.

Hemizonia arida is adapted to extreme temperatures and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
