Hardwickia mannii

Hardwickia mannii, commonly known as the African wattle, is a leguminous tree native to Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow floral clusters, typical of the Fabaceae family. The plant has a deep root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often used in reforestation projects.

Its seeds are edible and have been traditionally used in local cuisines.
