Hippeastrum lunaris

Hippeastrum lunaris, commonly known as the moonflower amaryllis, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that open at night, emitting a sweet fragrance to attract nocturnal pollinators. The plant features strap-like, evergreen leaves that form a rosette at the base. Its bulbs are fleshy and store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions.

Hippeastrum lunaris is popular as a houseplant due to its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
