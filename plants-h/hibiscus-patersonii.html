Hibiscus patersonii

Hibiscus patersonii, commonly known as the Paterson's hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually pink to red in color, with five distinct petals. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and upright.

Hibiscus patersonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators.
