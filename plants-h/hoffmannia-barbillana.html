Hoffmannia barbillana

Hoffmannia barbillana, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a deciduous shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces woody pods that contain elliptical seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Hoffmannia barbillana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
