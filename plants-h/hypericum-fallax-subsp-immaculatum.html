Hypericum fallax subsp. immaculatum

Hypericum fallax subsp.

immaculatum, commonly known as the white-stemmed St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its slender, often glabrous stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are elliptical, with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy appearance.

This subspecies is notable for its unique coloration and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
