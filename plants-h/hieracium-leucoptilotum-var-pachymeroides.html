Hieracium leucoptilotum var. pachymeroides

Hieracium leucoptilotum var. pachymeroides, commonly known as the broad-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands, and is native to parts of Europe and Asia.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and botanical gardens.
