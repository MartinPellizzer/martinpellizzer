Helichrysum roseum

Helichrysum roseum, commonly known as the rose gold everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that are often arranged in clusters, giving it a fluffy appearance. Its foliage is typically gray-green and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

Helichrysum roseum is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is often used in dried floral arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms.
