Hieracium glabratum subsp. macroglabrescens

Hieracium glabratum subsp.

macroglabrescens, commonly known as the broad-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are broad and slightly hairy, while the flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is often found in disturbed habitats and can spread through rhizomes, making it an invasive species in some regions.
