Hypericum aciferum

Hypericum aciferum, commonly known as the yellow hypericum, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and alternate leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are composed of five petals and numerous stamens. Its botanical features include a distinctive leaf arrangement and the presence of glandular dots on the leaves, which are characteristic of the Hypericaceae family.

The plant is often found in moist, open habitats such as woodland edges and stream banks.
