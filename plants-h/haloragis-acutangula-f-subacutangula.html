Haloragis acutangula f. subacutangula

Haloragis acutangula f. subacutangula, commonly known as the narrow-leaved willowherb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wetland or riparian habitats, thriving in moist to aquatic environments. The plant features slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic and terrestrial invertebrates.
