Hieracium kultukense

Hieracium kultukense, commonly known as the Kultuk hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky areas. It features a basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow or orange flowers during the summer months.

Hieracium kultukense is known for its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
