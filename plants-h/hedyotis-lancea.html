Hedyotis lancea

Hedyotis lancea, commonly known as the narrowleaf inchplant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to various regions of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, white or yellow flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in the summer months.

Hedyotis lancea is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits and is also used as a ground cover in ornamental gardening.
