Homalomena rostrata

Homalomena rostrata, commonly known as the arrow leaf elephant's ear, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 inches in length. The plant produces striking, hooded flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens with suitable conditions.
