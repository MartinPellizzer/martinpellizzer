Habenaria trimeropetala

Habenaria trimeropetala, commonly known as the three-lobed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in summer. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern around the stem.

The flowers are usually greenish-yellow with distinctive purple or reddish markings, making it a visually striking species.
