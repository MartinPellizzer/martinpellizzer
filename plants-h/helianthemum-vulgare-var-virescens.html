Helianthemum vulgare var. virescens

Helianthemum vulgare var. virescens, commonly known as the green-flowered rock rose, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Europe.

It features dense clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically gray-green in color, adapted to dry environments. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in gardens.
