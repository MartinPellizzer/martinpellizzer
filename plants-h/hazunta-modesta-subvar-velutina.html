Hazunta modesta subvar. velutina

Hazunta modesta subvar. velutina, commonly known as the velvetleaf hazunta, is a perennial plant characterized by its velvety, pubescent foliage.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland undergrowth and stream banks. The leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and have a distinct waxy texture. It produces small, clustered flowers in early spring, which are usually pale green to white in color.

This subvariety is notable for its ability to thrive in humid climates and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
