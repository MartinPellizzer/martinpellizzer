Herminium biporosum

Herminium biporosum, commonly known as the two-pored orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its flowers are clustered in a spike.

Herminium biporosum is notable for its unique floral structure, featuring two small pores on each petal, which is a distinguishing feature of the genus.
