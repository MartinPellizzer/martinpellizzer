Hypericum rumeliacum var. blepharophyllum

Hypericum rumeliacum var. blepharophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland edges or stream banks. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces bright yellow flowers with five petals. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

The species is characterized by its compact growth habit and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
