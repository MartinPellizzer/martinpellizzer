Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp. jenzigense

Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp.

jenzigense, commonly known as the Jenzig's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow-orange composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. It has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, inconspicuous seeds dispersed by wind.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its range.
