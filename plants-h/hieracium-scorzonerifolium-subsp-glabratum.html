Hieracium scorzonerifolium subsp. glabratum

Hieracium scorzonerifolium subsp.

glabratum, commonly known as the glabrate hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
