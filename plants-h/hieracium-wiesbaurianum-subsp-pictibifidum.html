Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp. pictibifidum

Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp.

pictibifidum, commonly known as the painted false dandelion, is a perennial herb native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often hairy, yellowish-green leaves.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically clustered in a composite inflorescence. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its ornamental value has made it a subject of horticultural interest in some regions.
