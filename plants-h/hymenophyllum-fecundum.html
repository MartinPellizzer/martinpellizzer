Hymenophyllum fecundum

Hymenophyllum fecundum, commonly known as the rich fern, is a delicate, epiphytic fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its small, narrow, and often densely packed fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant features a unique structure with a single, short, and often unbranched stipe, and its leaves are covered in tiny, scale-like structures called trichomes. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on rocks or tree bark in tropical and subtropical regions.

Hymenophyllum fecundum is notable for its ability to reproduce through spores, which are released from specialized structures called sorus.
