Heliconia robusta

Heliconia robusta, commonly known as the robust heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is characterized by its large, colorful bracts that resemble petals. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

Heliconia robusta is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
