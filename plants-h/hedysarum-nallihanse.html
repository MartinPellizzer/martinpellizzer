Hedysarum nallihanse

Hedysarum nallihanse, commonly known as the Nallihan sweet vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Native to Central Asia, it thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and nitrogen-fixing ability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for soil improvement.
