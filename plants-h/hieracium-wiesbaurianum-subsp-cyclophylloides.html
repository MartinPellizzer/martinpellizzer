Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp. cyclophylloides

Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp.

cyclophylloides, commonly known as the cyclophylloides hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. Its leaves are usually alternate, with a rough, hairy texture, and the stems are often reddish in color.

It is a relatively small plant, usually reaching heights of less than 30 centimeters.
