Heuchera americana var. minima

Heuchera americana var. minima, commonly known as the dwarf coral bells, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features dense, compact foliage with vibrant, often variegated leaves that range in color from silver to deep red and purple. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white during the summer months. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its compact size and ornamental leaves make it an excellent ground cover or container plant.
