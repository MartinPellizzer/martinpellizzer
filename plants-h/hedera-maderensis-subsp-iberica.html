Hedera maderensis subsp. iberica

Hedera maderensis subsp.

iberica, commonly known as the Iberian ivy, is a climbing evergreen vine native to the Iberian Peninsula. It features dark green, lobed leaves that are typically heart-shaped with a velvety texture.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange-red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forests and rocky areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden use, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
