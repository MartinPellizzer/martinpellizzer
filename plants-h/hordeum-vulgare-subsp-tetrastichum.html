Hordeum vulgare subsp. tetrastichum

Hordeum vulgare subsp.

tetrastichum, commonly known as the slender wild barley, is a wild grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves.

The inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in two rows, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, two-lobed glumes and spikelet-like flowers that are typically wind-pollinated.

This subspecies is often found in disturbed soils and is of interest in agricultural and ecological studies.
