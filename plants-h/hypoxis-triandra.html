Hypoxis triandra

Hypoxis triandra, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a tuberous root system and produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom at night. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a smooth texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full to partial sunlight.

Hypoxis triandra is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
