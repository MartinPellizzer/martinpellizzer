Hieracium nervorum

Hieracium nervorum, commonly known as the narrow-veined hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features upright, hairy stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped with prominent veins. Its flowers are typically yellow, with ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers.

Hieracium nervorum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
