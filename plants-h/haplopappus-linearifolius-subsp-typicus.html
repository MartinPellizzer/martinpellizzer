Haplopappus linearifolius subsp. typicus

Haplopappus linearifolius subsp.

typicus, commonly known as the slender leafed goatweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and cylindrical stems.

The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to drought conditions with its succulent leaves. Its small, yellowish flowers are arranged in clusters and are pollinated by insects.

The subspecies typicus is distinguished by its linear, narrow leaves and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
