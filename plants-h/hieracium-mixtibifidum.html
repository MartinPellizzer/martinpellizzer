Hieracium mixtibifidum

Hieracium mixtibifidum, commonly known as the mixed-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and flowering heads that produce yellow ray florets. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring temperate climates. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
