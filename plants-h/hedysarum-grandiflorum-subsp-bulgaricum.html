Hedysarum grandiflorum subsp. bulgaricum

Hedysarum grandiflorum subsp.

bulgaricum, commonly known as the Bulgarian sweet clover, is a perennial legume native to southeastern Europe. It is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil fertility.

Its subspecific classification highlights its distinct ecological and morphological traits compared to other members of the Hedysarum genus.
