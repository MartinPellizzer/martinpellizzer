Hyacinthella pycnantha

Hyacinthella pycnantha, commonly known as the mountain hyacinth, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the eastern Himalayas.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring, producing a striking display of blue to violet-colored flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Hyacinthella pycnantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in alpine gardens.
