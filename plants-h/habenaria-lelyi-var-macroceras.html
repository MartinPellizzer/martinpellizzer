Habenaria lelyi var. macroceras

Habenaria lelyi var. macroceras, commonly known as the large-fruited helmet orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, helmet-shaped flower that is typically greenish-yellow with a distinctive red or purple lip. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it blooms during the rainy season.

This orchid is often found growing on trees or rocks in forested areas, where it relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.
