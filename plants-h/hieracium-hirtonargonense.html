Hieracium hirtonargonense

Hieracium hirtonargonense, commonly known as the Armenian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant features basal and alternate leaves that are lanceolate to ovate in shape, with hairy surfaces. Its flowering stems bear yellow composite flowers, characteristic of the Asteraceae family.

It is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in areas with moderate to high sunlight exposure.
