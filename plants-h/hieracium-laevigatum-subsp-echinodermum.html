Hieracium laevigatum subsp. echinodermum

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

echinodermum, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant features upright stems covered in rough, hairy texture, and its leaves are usually lance-shaped with serrated edges. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, and the plant is often found in meadows and grassy areas.

Due to its hairy texture and robust growth, it is considered a hardy weed in some regions.
