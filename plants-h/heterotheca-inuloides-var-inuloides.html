Heterotheca inuloides var. inuloides

Heterotheca inuloides var. inuloides, commonly known as the narrowleaf blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, blue to purple composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely divided, aromatic leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid environments and suitable for xeriscaping.
