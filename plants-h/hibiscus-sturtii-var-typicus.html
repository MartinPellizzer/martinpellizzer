Hibiscus sturtii var. typicus

Hibiscus sturtii var. typicus, commonly known as the Sturt's desert rose, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, large, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a contrasting white throat. The plant has thick, waxy leaves that help it conserve water in its harsh environment. Its stems are typically woody and covered in a layer of fine, white hairs.

Hibiscus sturtii var. typicus is well adapted to drought and can thrive in poor, sandy soils.
