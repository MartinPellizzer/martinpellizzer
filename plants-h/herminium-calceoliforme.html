Herminium calceoliforme

Herminium calceoliforme, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb with a rhizomatous root system and produces showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette, and its inflorescence consists of a single spike bearing several flowers. Herminium calceoliforme thrives in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks, and it is often found in calcareous soils.

Its unique floral structure and striking coloration make it a notable species within the orchid family.
