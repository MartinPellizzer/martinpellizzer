Hippeastrum miradorense

Hippeastrum miradorense, commonly known as the Mirador lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces vibrant, trumpet-shaped blooms in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often with a contrasting white or yellow throat. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are broad, strap-like, and can grow up to 60 centimeters in length, contributing to its lush appearance.
