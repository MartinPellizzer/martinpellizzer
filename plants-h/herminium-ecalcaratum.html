Herminium ecalcaratum

Herminium ecalcaratum, commonly known as the spurred orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive spurred flowers that resemble small horns. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces showy, fragrant blooms in late spring to early summer.

Herminium ecalcaratum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
