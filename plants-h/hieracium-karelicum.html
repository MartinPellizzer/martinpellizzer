Hieracium karelicum

Hieracium karelicum, commonly known as Karelian hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to northern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and forest clearings. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium karelicum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is considered a persistent weed in some regions.
