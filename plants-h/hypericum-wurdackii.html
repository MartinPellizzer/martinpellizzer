Hypericum wurdackii

Hypericum wurdackii, commonly known as the Wurdack's St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to Central and Southern Europe.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are composed of five petals and numerous stamens. Its botanical characteristics include small, elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy surface and a strong, aromatic scent.

Hypericum wurdackii is often found in sunny, well-drained soils in woodland or grassland habitats.
