Hildegardia erythrosiphon var. urschiana

Hildegardia erythrosiphon var. urschiana, commonly known as the red-flowered hildegardia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, covered with soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, showy red flowers with prominent yellow stamens, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Hildegardia erythrosiphon var. urschiana prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or alpine settings.
