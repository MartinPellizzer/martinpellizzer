Hieracium panteblaston

Hieracium panteblaston, commonly known as the Panteblaston hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces yellow to orange composite flowers. Native to specific regions in Europe, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant is typically perennial, with a fibrous root system that helps it anchor in various soil types.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in local ecosystems.
