Hirtella ulei

Hirtella ulei, commonly known as the Brazilian hirtella, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces small, spherical fruits that are edible.

Hirtella ulei is often found in shaded, moist environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
