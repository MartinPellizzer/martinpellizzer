Hermannia procumbens

Hermannia procumbens, commonly known as the creeping hermannia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and colonize new areas effectively.
