Habenaria chirensis

Habenaria chirensis, commonly known as the Chirensis orchid, is a species of orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments, often on tree trunks or rocks. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the humid air, and it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

The orchid's delicate structure and unique blooming pattern make it a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists.
