Haplophyllum canaliculatum

Haplophyllum canaliculatum, commonly known as the narrow-ribbed rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of fragrant, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its botanical features include a compact growth habit and a high degree of drought tolerance.

Haplophyllum canaliculatum is often found in coastal and inland areas with well-drained soils.
