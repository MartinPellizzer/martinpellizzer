Herminium spiranthiforme

Herminium spiranthiforme, commonly known as the spiral orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of its floral bracts. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple, with a unique spiral pattern on the petals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in high-altitude grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
