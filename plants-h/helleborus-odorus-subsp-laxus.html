Helleborus odorus subsp. laxus

Helleborus odorus subsp.

laxus, commonly known as the fragrant hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It features dark green, leathery leaves that are typically palmately lobed, and its flowers are usually purple or pink, with a distinctive fragrance. The plant thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom in late winter or early spring.
