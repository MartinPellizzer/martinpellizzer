Hieracium carpathicum subsp. engleri

Hieracium carpathicum subsp.

engleri, commonly known as the Carpathian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe. It has lanceolate to ovate leaves with a hairy surface and produces small, oval seeds in a composite head.

This subspecies is noted for its ecological significance in stabilizing soil in alpine and subalpine habitats.
