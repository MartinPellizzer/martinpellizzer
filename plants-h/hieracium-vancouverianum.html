Hieracium vancouverianum

Hieracium vancouverianum, commonly known as Vancouver hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a cluster of small flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as meadows, roadsides, and gravelly soils. It has basal leaves that are broadly lance-shaped and rough in texture.

The showy yellow flowers appear in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
