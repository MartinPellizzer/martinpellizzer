Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp. subcinereum

Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp.

subcinereum, commonly known as the pale meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering heads.

The plant produces yellow disk flowers surrounded by pale green to white ray florets. It typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the hawkweed group, with specific morphological traits that aid in its identification.
