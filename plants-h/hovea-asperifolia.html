Hovea asperifolia

Hovea asperifolia, commonly known as the rough hovea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often trifoliate leaves. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, clustered yellow flowers. It is typically found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Hovea asperifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
