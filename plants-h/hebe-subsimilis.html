Hebe subsimilis

Hebe subsimilis, commonly known as the silver hebe, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, silver-gray leaves. The plant produces clusters of blue or purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Hebe subsimilis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
