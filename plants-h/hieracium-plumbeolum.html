Hieracium plumbeolum

Hieracium plumbeolum, commonly known as the blue hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features basal rosettes of gray-green, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
