Heinekenia peliorhyncha

Heinekenia peliorhyncha, commonly known as the golden saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb with a rosette of basal leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often found in alpine meadows and is considered a species of conservation concern in some areas.
