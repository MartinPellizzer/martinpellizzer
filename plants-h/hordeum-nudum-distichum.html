Hordeum nudum-distichum

Hordeum nudum-distichum, commonly known as the slender wild barley, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, two-rowed florets. Hordeum nudum-distichum is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in fields, thriving in a variety of soil types.

It plays a role in ecological systems as a food source for various insects and small mammals.
