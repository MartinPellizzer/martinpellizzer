Humiria laurina

Humiria laurina, commonly known as the sweet shrub, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, reddish berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Humiria laurina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
