Hieracium subtriangulare

Hieracium subtriangulare, commonly known as the triangular hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium subtriangulare is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in various habitats.
