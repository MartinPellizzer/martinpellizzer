Helianthemum ledifolium f. dissitiflorum

Helianthemum ledifolium f. dissitiflorum, commonly known as the dwarf stonecress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming in early summer. The plant has a compact growth habit with succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and attractive addition to rock gardens and alpine plantings.
