Hibbertia priceana

Hibbertia priceana, commonly known as the golden wattle.

It is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southeastern Australia. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Hibbertia priceana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
