Hieracium parcicrinoides

Hieracium parcicrinoides, commonly known as the small-flowered hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped, rough to the touch, and often have a hairy texture.

The flowers are small, yellowish, and bloom from late spring to early autumn.
