Heliconia estherae

Heliconia estherae, commonly known as Esther's heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescences that resemble a crown, composed of brightly colored bracts. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pollinated by birds, particularly hummingbirds. Its leaves are large, broad, and often have a waxy texture, adapting to the humid environments where it thrives.

Heliconia estherae is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical landscapes.
