Homostylium

Homostylium, commonly known as the homostylous condition, refers to a floral structure in certain plants where all the stamens and pistils are of equal length.

This arrangement facilitates cross-pollination by ensuring that pollen from one flower can easily reach the stigma of another. Homostylium is often observed in plants that rely on insect pollination, as it enhances the chances of successful fertilization. The term is used in botany to describe the symmetry and positioning of reproductive organs within the flower.

This characteristic is in contrast to heterostylium, where stamens and pistils are of different lengths.
