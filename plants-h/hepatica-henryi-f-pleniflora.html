Hepatica henryi f. pleniflora

Hepatica henryi f. pleniflora, commonly known as the pleniflora hepatica, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its early spring blooming habit, producing showy, nodding flowers in shades of blue, purple, or white. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white down. Native to the mountainous regions of China, it thrives in cool, moist woodland environments.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and role in early pollination dynamics.
