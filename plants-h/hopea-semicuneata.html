Hopea semicuneata

Hopea semicuneata, commonly known as the Sunda hopea, is a species of flowering plant in the family Dipterocarpaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including parts of Malaysia, Indonesia, and Thailand. The tree is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in panicle inflorescences. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several winged seeds, aiding in wind dispersal.

The species is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture making.
