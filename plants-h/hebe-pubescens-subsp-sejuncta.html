Hebe pubescens subsp. sejuncta

Hebe pubescens subsp.

sejuncta, commonly known as the silver-haired hebe, is a shrub native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in dense, soft pubescence.

The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically in spring. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
