Hemigraphis latebrosa var. heyneana

Hemigraphis latebrosa var. heyneana, commonly known as the velvetleaf sorrel, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that range in color from deep red to bronze, often with contrasting lighter veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow, which appear in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its foliage.

Due to its unique appearance, it is popular in both indoor and outdoor gardens.
