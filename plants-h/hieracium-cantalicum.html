Hieracium cantalicum

Hieracium cantalicum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats at high elevations, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer.

Hieracium cantalicum is often found in meadows and mountainous areas, contributing to the biodiversity of its alpine ecosystem.
