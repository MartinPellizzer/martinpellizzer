Heliconia rhodantha

Heliconia rhodantha, commonly known as the red heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its vibrant, boat-shaped inflorescences that are predominantly red or orange in color. The plant produces numerous small, fragrant flowers clustered around a central stem, which eventually develop into fleshy, edible berries. Its leaves are large, broad, and often have a waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Heliconia rhodantha is often cultivated in gardens and used in floral arrangements due to its bold, colorful appearance.
