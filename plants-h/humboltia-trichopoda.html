Humboltia trichopoda

Humboltia trichopoda, commonly known as the hummingbird flower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that are typically bright red or orange, designed to attract hummingbirds for pollination. The plant has a woody stem and produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually opposite in arrangement. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Humboltia trichopoda is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinator populations.
