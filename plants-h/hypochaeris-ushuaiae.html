Hypochaeris ushuaiae

Hypochaeris ushuaiae, commonly known as the Usuana hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, temperate climates. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hypochaeris ushuaiae is known for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions, making it a hardy species in its native habitat.
