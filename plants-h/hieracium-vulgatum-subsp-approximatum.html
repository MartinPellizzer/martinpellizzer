Hieracium vulgatum subsp. approximatum

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

approximatum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering stems that bear yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces, and the flowers are arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

It is often found in temperate regions and is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
