Hydrangea paniculata var. debilis

Hydrangea paniculata var. debilis, commonly known as the smooth hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its large, cone-shaped flower clusters that range in color from white to pink, depending on soil pH. The plant produces showy, sterile flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its foliage is dark green and turns reddish in the fall, adding ornamental value throughout the growing season.

It prefers partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
