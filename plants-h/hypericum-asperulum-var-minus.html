Hypericum asperulum var. minus

Hypericum asperulum var. minus, commonly known as lesser rough-stemmed St. John's wort, is a low-growing shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters along the stems. The plant has rough, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
