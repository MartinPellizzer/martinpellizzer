Habranthus brachyandrus

Habranthus brachyandrus, commonly known as the short-stalked spider orchid, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale green, with a distinctive spider-like appearance due to the elongated sepals.

Habranthus brachyandrus is a perennial herb that blooms in late spring to early summer.
