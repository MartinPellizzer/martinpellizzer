Hippeastrum iguapense

Hippeastrum iguapense, commonly known as the Iguapense amaryllis, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom in shades of red, white, and pink. The plant produces strap-like leaves that are typically broad and dark green, forming a rosette at the base. Hippeastrum iguapense thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and floral arrangements.
