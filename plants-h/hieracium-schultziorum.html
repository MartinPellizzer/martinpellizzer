Hieracium schultziorum

Hieracium schultziorum, commonly known as the Schultze's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf structures. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring temperate climates. Its leaves are alternate, rough, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain Lepidoptera species.
