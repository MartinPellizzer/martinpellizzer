Hookera brevipes

Hookera brevipes, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of blue or violet flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid environment. Hookera brevipes thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its unique appearance and ability to adapt to harsh mountain conditions make it a notable species in alpine botany.
