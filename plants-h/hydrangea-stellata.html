Hydrangea stellata

Hydrangea stellata, commonly known as the star hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white or pink flowers that resemble stars, hence its common name. The plant produces showy inflorescences that change color depending on soil pH, ranging from blue to pink. Its leaves are ovate and have a serrated edge, while its stems are typically green or red.

Hydrangea stellata thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil.
