Heliconia latispatha

Heliconia latispatha, commonly known as the broad-leaf heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its large, colorful bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces long, pendulous inflorescences with numerous small, tubular flowers that are pollinated by birds, particularly hummingbirds. Its leaves are broad and often have a waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

Heliconia latispatha is also valued for its role in supporting local ecosystems and its use in traditional medicine.
