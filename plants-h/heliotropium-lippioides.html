Heliotropium lippioides

Heliotropium lippioides, commonly known as the slender heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers have a strong, sweet fragrance, which attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Heliotropium lippioides is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance and ease of cultivation.
