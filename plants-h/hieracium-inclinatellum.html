Hieracium inclinatellum

Hieracium inclinatellum, commonly known as the leaning hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium inclinatellum is known for its ability to spread via rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain habitats.
