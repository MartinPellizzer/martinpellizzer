Huperzia firma

Huperzia firma, commonly known as the firm huperzia, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is a terrestrial plant characterized by its evergreen, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its leaves, which are clustered in structures called sori. Huperzia firma is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including China, Japan, and India.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices.
