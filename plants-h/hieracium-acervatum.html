Hieracium acervatum

Hieracium acervatum, commonly known as the clustered hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and ragworts, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its flowers are yellow, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets, and it produces seeds in a capsule.

Hieracium acervatum is often found in open habitats and can form dense colonies, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
