Hymenostephium angustifolium

Hymenostephium angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hymenostephium, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showing a high degree of adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it blooms during the spring and early summer.

Hymenostephium angustifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
