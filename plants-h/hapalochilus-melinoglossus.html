Hapalochilus melinoglossus

Hapalochilus melinoglossus, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. The plant has compound leaves with narrow, linear leaflets and is often found in dry, sunny habitats. It plays an important role in the ecological systems of its native region.

The golden wattle is also the floral emblem of Australia, symbolizing the country's natural heritage.
