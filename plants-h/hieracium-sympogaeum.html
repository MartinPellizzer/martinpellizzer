Hieracium sympogaeum

Hieracium sympogaeum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils across Europe and parts of Asia. It has a taproot system and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
