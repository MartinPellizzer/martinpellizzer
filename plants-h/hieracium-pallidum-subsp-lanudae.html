Hieracium pallidum subsp. lanudae

Hieracium pallidum subsp.

lanudae, commonly known as the pale hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaf arrangement.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its yellow flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is a low-growing plant that spreads by rhizomes, forming dense mats in suitable environments.
