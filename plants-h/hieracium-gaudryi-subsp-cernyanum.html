Hieracium gaudryi subsp. cernyanum

Hieracium gaudryi subsp.

cernyanum, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or grassy slopes at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.

The plant features bright yellow composite flowers, which are arranged in a daisy-like pattern, and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. Its stems are usually erect and covered with fine hairs, and it produces small, oval-shaped achenes as its seeds.

This subspecies is known for its ecological importance in alpine ecosystems, supporting various pollinators and contributing to soil stabilization.
