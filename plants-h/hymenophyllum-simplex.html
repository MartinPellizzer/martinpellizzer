Hymenophyllum simplex

Hymenophyllum simplex, commonly known as the silver fern, is a small, delicate fern that belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silky-hair-like fronds that give it a distinctive, almost metallic appearance. This species is typically found in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors and rock crevices, where it thrives in high humidity. The plant's leaves are closely spaced and have a unique structure with a single layer of cells, which helps it retain moisture.

Hymenophyllum simplex is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is a popular choice for terrariums and shaded garden settings.
