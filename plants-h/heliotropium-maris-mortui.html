Heliotropium maris-mortui

Heliotropium maris-mortui, commonly known as the sea heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in the early spring. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, thriving in saline environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and succulent, adapted to withstand coastal conditions.

Heliotropium maris-mortui is also notable for its ability to tolerate high levels of salt spray and wind.
