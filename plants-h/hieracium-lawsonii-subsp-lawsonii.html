Hieracium lawsonii subsp. lawsonii

Hieracium lawsonii subsp.

lawsonii, commonly known as Lawson's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and rocky slopes.

The plant features basal rosettes of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in late spring to early summer. Its flowering stems are usually unbranched and bear several flower heads.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and is often found in dry, sunny environments.
