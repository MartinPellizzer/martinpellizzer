Hevea benthamiana f. huberiana

Hevea benthamiana f. huberiana, commonly known as the rubber plant, is a variant of the rubber tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves and can grow into a tall shrub or small tree. The plant is notable for its ability to produce latex, which is used in the rubber industry. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ornamental and industrial purposes.
