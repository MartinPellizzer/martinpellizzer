Hieracium pampsilum

Hieracium pampsilum, commonly known as the prairie hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as prairies, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium pampsilum is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and is often found in grassland ecosystems.
