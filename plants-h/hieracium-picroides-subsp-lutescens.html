Hieracium picroides subsp. lutescens

Hieracium picroides subsp.

lutescens, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks.

The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are hairy and often have a yellowish or reddish hue. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, with petals that are narrow and pointed.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and wind-pollinated flowers.
