Humiria balsamifera var. pilosa

Humiria balsamifera var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy balsam tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces a resinous sap that has been traditionally used in folk medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a unique subject of study in plant taxonomy and ecological research.
