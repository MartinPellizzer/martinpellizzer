Helichrysum armenium var. lacteum

Helichrysum armenium var. lacteum, commonly known as the Armenian immortelle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers, which are tightly packed in dense, rounded clusters. The plant produces small, tough, and often long-lasting bracts that give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is drought-tolerant.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
