Hieracium amplisquamum

Hieracium amplisquamum, commonly known as the broad-scaled hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces, and the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium amplisquamum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
