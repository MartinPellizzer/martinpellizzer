Hariota lumbricalis

Hariota lumbricalis, commonly known as the slender earthworm, is a species of annelid that plays a crucial role in soil health.

It is characterized by its long, segmented body and the ability to burrow through soil, aiding in aeration and nutrient cycling. This plant-like organism lacks true leaves and flowers, instead relying on its digestive system to break down organic matter. While not a flowering plant, it contributes significantly to the ecosystem through its decomposition activities.

Hariota lumbricalis is typically found in moist, temperate environments where it thrives in rich, organic soil.
