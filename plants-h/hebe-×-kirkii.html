Hebe × kirkii

Hebe × kirkii, commonly known as the mountain hebe, is a hybrid plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, violet, or white, typically during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Hebe × kirkii is also valued for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
