Hearnia cumingiana

Hearnia cumingiana, commonly known as the velvet tunicate, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, velvety leaves and striking, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or alpine habitats. Its foliage is covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, giving it a soft, almost fuzzy appearance.
