Hieracium vulgatum subsp. ramosifolium

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

ramosifolium, commonly known as the bristly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, often hairy foliage.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered with fine, bristly hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows.
