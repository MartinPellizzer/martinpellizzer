Hieracium infrapilellosum

Hieracium infrapilellosum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are narrow and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers are yellow and bloom from June to September.

Hieracium infrapilellosum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
