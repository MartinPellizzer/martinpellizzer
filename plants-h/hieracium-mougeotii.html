Hieracium mougeotii

Hieracium mougeotii, commonly known as Mougeot's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous tiny florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Hieracium mougeotii is often found in temperate climates and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
