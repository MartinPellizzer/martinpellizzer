Hedyotis divaricata var. divaricata

Hedyotis divaricata var. divaricata, commonly known as the variable inchplant, is a low-growing perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers with four petals, typically arranged in clusters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is commonly found in forest understories or along stream banks.
