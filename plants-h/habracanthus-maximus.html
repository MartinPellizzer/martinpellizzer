Habracanthus maximus

Habracanthus maximus, commonly known as the giant habracanthus, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, waxy leaves that can reach up to two meters in length. The plant produces vibrant orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
