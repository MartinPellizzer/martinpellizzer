Hypericum sikokumontanum

Hypericum sikokumontanum, commonly known as the Sierra hypericum, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer, and its small, elliptical leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils. It is part of the genus Hypericum, which includes many species with medicinal properties.

The Sierra hypericum is relatively rare and is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations.
