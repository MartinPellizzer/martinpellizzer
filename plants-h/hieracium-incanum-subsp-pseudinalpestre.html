Hieracium incanum subsp. pseudinalpestre

Hieracium incanum subsp.

pseudinalpestre, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is a perennial herb characterized by its hairy, upright stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces yellow flowers in dense clusters, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine meadows and slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
