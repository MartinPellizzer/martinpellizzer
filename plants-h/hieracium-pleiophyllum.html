Hieracium pleiophyllum

Hieracium pleiophyllum, commonly known as the showy hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. It has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and its flowering stems are often branched.

Hieracium pleiophyllum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant appearance.
