Hesperis ruderalis

Hesperis ruderalis, commonly known as the common whitlow grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, white or pale yellow flowers. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in racemes.

Hesperis ruderalis is an annual plant that produces small, spherical seed pods containing numerous seeds.
