Hieracium lasiophorum

Hieracium lasiophorum, commonly known as the rough hawksbeard, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are rough and hairy, with a distinctive lobed shape, while the flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Hieracium lasiophorum is known for its adaptability and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
