Hymenophyllum thomassetii

Hymenophyllum thomassetii, commonly known as the silver maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically covered in a layer of fine, silky hairs. This plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or rocks without being parasitic. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique morphology and habitat make it a striking addition to botanical collections and a subject of interest in ecological studies.
