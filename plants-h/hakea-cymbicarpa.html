Hakea cymbicarpa

Hakea cymbicarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hakea, is a shrub native to southwestern Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and produces cylindrical, woody fruits that are typically red or purple in color. The plant is adapted to arid environments, featuring a deep root system and water-storing structures. It is often found in sandy soils and is part of the Proteaceae family.

Hakea cymbicarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
