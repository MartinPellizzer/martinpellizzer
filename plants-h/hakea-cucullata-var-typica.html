Hakea cucullata var. typica

Hakea cucullata var. typica, commonly known as the hooded hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, urn-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red in color. The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves that are covered in fine hairs, aiding in water conservation. It produces woody, cone-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by animals.

This species is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient component of native vegetation.
