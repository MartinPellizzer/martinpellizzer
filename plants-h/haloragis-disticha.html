Haloragis disticha

Haloragis disticha, commonly known as the twinleaf, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet, boggy environments. The plant has small, opposite leaves that are often covered in a fine, white waxy coating. Its flowers are clustered in dense, greenish-white inflorescences and bloom in spring.

Haloragis disticha is well adapted to its wetland habitat and plays a role in supporting local ecosystems.
