Hieracium villosum subsp. pamphilii

Hieracium villosum subsp.

pamphilii, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and bright yellow flowers.

The subspecies pamphilii is distinguished by its more robust growth and slightly larger flowers compared to the typical form of H. villosum. It thrives in moist, often calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

The plant is known for its showy inflorescences and is of interest to botanists and gardeners alike.
