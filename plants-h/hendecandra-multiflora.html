Hendecandra multiflora

Hendecandra multiflora, commonly known as the African star grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted, grass-like appearance with slender, erect stems and small, narrow leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pale blue flowers that bloom in summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

Hendecandra multiflora is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
