Hieracium peruanum var. ayavacanum

Hieracium peruanum var. ayavacanum, commonly known as the Ayavacanum hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the high Andean regions of Peru.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

This variety is noted for its ecological significance in its native habitat, supporting local insect populations.
