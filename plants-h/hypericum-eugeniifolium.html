Hypericum eugeniifolium

Hypericum eugeniifolium, commonly known as the golden-stemmed St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has woody stems covered in fine, hairy hairs and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. Its flowers are composed of five petals and are often visited by bees and other pollinators.

Hypericum eugeniifolium is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky soils.
