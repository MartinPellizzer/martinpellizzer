Habenaria hatschbachii

Habenaria hatschbachii, commonly known as the Hatschbach's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It features a slender, upright stem with a single or few erect flowers that are typically pale yellow to greenish-white in color. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and its roots are fibrous and often covered in a dense layer of mycorrhizal fungi. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires high humidity for optimal growth.

The species is noted for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value.
