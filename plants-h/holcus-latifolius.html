Holcus latifolius

Holcus latifolius, commonly known as broadleaf quackgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its wide, flat leaves and tufted inflorescence that resembles a panicle. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread aggressively, making it both a weed and a forage plant.

The flowers are wind-pollinated, and the seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its wide distribution.
