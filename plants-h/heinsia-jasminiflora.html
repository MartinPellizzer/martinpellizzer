Heinsia jasminiflora

Heinsia jasminiflora, commonly known as the sweet shrub or sweet tea plant, is a flowering shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to yellowish flowers that resemble jasmine, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soil and full sun. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Heinsia jasminiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
