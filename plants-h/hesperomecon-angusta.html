Hesperomecon angusta

Hesperomecon angusta, commonly known as narrowleaf tansy, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
