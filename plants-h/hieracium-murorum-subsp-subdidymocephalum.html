Hieracium murorum subsp. subdidymocephalum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

subdidymocephalum, commonly known as the lesser hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering heads that produce yellow disc flowers surrounded by pale yellow to white ray florets.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands, roadsides, and waste areas. It has a taproot system and is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, forming dense colonies.

The species is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it is often found in temperate climates.
