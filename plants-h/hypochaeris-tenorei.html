Hypochaeris tenorei

Hypochaeris tenorei, commonly known as the Italian hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features basal leaves that are lanceolate in shape. Its yellow flowers bloom from spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hypochaeris tenorei is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, showcasing its adaptability to diverse environments.
