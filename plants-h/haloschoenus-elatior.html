Haloschoenus elatior

Haloschoenus elatior, commonly known as the salt marsh grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It thrives in saline and brackish environments, often found in estuaries and tidal marshes. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to withstand high salt concentrations and periodic flooding. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers.

Haloschoenus elatior plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
