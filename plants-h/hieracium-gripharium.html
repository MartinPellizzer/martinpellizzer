Hieracium gripharium

Hieracium gripharium, commonly known as the greater hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and is often found in grasslands and open areas. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that are typically clustered in a flat-topped inflorescence. Its leaves are hairy and lance-shaped, contributing to its common name.

Hieracium gripharium is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
