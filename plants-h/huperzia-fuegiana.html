Huperzia fuegiana

Huperzia fuegiana, commonly known as the Magellanic huperzia, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and is native to southern South America, particularly Chile and Argentina. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its sporophylls are arranged in dense clusters, and it produces spores in specialized structures called sporangia.

Huperzia fuegiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
