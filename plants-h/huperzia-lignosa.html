Huperzia lignosa

Huperzia lignosa, commonly known as the lignous huperzia, is a species of clubmoss native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical formations called strobila. Huperzia lignosa is a perennial plant that thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found on forest floors or rocky outcrops.

Its slow growth and unique morphology make it an interesting subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
