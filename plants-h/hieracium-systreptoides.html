Hieracium systreptoides

Hieracium systreptoides, commonly known as the spiral hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a flowering stem that bears yellow composite flowers. The plant is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in open, disturbed habitats. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a single head.

Hieracium systreptoides is known for its distinctive spiral arrangement of its flower heads, which gives it its common name.
