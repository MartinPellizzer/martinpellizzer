Hieracium baeticum subsp. praecocifolioides

Hieracium baeticum subsp.

praecocifolioides, commonly known as the early-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its native range.
