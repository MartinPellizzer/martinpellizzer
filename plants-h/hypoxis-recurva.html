Hypoxis recurva

Hypoxis recurva, commonly known as the star grass or moonflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a short, creeping rhizome and produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom at night. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats.

Hypoxis recurva is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
