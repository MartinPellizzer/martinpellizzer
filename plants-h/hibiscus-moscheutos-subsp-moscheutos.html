Hibiscus moscheutos subsp. moscheutos

Hibiscus moscheutos subsp.

moscheutos, commonly known as the swamp hibiscus, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States. It features large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with a distinctive yellow center.

The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in wet, swampy environments. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by water.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
