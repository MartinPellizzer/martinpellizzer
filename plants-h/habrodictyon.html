Habrodictyon

Habrodictyon, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and unusual plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, waxy, and often translucent leaves that resemble ghostly shapes, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, pale flowers that bloom in the early morning and close by midday. It is adapted to survive in extreme drought conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy leaves.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is highly valued in botanical collections and conservation efforts.
