Hieracium diaphanoides subsp. faucium

Hieracium diaphanoides subsp.

faucium, commonly known as the translucent hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, yellowish to greenish composite flowers.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine pubescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This subspecies is native to Europe and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphological features.
