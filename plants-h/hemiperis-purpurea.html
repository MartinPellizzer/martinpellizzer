Hemiperis purpurea

Hemiperis purpurea, commonly known as purple fringed bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, with distinctive fringed petals. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Hemiperis purpurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
