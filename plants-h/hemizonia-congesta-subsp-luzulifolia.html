Hemizonia congesta subsp. luzulifolia

Hemizonia congesta subsp.

luzulifolia, commonly known as the blue-eyed grass, is a member of the Poaceae family. It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, tufted foliage.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
