Hemicicca flexuosa

Hemicicca flexuosa, commonly known as the desert holly, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a subtle visual contrast to its green foliage. Its deep root system allows it to access groundwater, making it highly drought-tolerant.

Hemicicca flexuosa plays an important role in stabilizing desert soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
