Hieracium bellidiastrum

Hieracium bellidiastrum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers and is native to Europe, particularly in regions like the British Isles and parts of southern Scandinavia. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies. Its leaves are broadly lance-shaped with a rough texture, and the stems are usually erect and hairy.

Hieracium bellidiastrum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
