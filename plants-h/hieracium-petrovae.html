Hieracium petrovae

Hieracium petrovae, commonly known as the Petrow's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and leaves arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, thriving in well-drained soils and cold climates. Its flowers are yellow, with ray florets that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hieracium petrovae is known for its hardy nature and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
