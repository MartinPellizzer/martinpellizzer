Hoodia rustica

Hoodia rustica, commonly known as desert cabbage, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, spineless stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments by storing water in its thick, fleshy leaves. Its roots can extend deep into the soil to access moisture, and it often grows in rocky or sandy soils.

Hoodia rustica is also noted for its ability to thrive in extreme temperatures, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
