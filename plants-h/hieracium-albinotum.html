Hieracium albinotum

Hieracium albinotum, commonly known as the white-stemmed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are yellow and bloom from June to August, attracting various pollinators.

Hieracium albinotum is known for its distinctive white, hairy stems and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
