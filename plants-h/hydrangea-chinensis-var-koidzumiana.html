Hydrangea chinensis var. koidzumiana

Hydrangea chinensis var. koidzumiana, commonly known as the Chinese mountain hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from white to pink or blue depending on soil pH. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

The flowers are produced in late summer and can persist into early autumn.
