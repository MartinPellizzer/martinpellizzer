Hieracium transiens subsp. abatanum

Hieracium transiens subsp.

abatanum, commonly known as the variable hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. It has small, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces numerous seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is known for its variability in leaf shape and flower size, making it a subject of taxonomic interest.
