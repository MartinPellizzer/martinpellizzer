Hibiscus pachmarhicus

Hibiscus pachmarhicus, commonly known as the Malvaviscus, is a species of flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch and are arranged oppositely along the stem. Hibiscus pachmarhicus thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and prefers well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
