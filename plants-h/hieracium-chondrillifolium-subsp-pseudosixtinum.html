Hieracium chondrillifolium subsp. pseudosixtinum

Hieracium chondrillifolium subsp.

pseudosixtinum, commonly known as the six-leaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and bright yellow flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different leaf morphology compared to other members of the species. It typically grows in meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain habitats.
