Habenorkis sigillahenis

Habenorkis sigillahenis, commonly known as the sigil-marked orchid, is a rare and exotic plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate floral patterns that resemble ancient sigils, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to the bark of trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually a vibrant mix of purple and white, with a unique fragrance that attracts specific pollinators.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is highly sought after in horticultural circles.
