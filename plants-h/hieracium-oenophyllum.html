Hieracium oenophyllum

Hieracium oenophyllum, commonly known as the wine-leek hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium oenophyllum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in agricultural and natural settings.
