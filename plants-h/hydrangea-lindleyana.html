Hydrangea lindleyana

Hydrangea lindleyana, commonly known as the mountain hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from white to pink, blue, or purple depending on soil pH. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its foliage consists of large, heart-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy.

Hydrangea lindleyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to bloom reliably in woodland gardens.
