Hieracium subcaesiiforme subsp. hypocyprium

Hieracium subcaesiiforme subsp.

hypocyprium, commonly known as the purple hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and often have a reddish or purplish hue, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The flowers are yellow with a reddish tinge, and the plant produces small, oval-shaped achenes as its seeds.
