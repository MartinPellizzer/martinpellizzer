Hieracium echiocephalum

Hieracium echiocephalum, commonly known as the echiocephalum hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium echiocephalum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting pollinators.
