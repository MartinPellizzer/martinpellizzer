Hydrobryum nutans

Hydrobryum nutans, commonly known as the nodding hydrobryum, is a small, delicate aquatic plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes. The plant features slender, branching stems that are often submerged, with small, pale green leaves arranged in whorls. Its most distinctive characteristic is its nodding inflorescence, which consists of tiny, greenish-white flowers that droop downward.

Hydrobryum nutans plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates.
