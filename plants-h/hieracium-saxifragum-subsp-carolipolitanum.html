Hieracium saxifragum subsp. carolipolitanum

Hieracium saxifragum subsp.

carolipolitanum, commonly known as the Carolipolitan hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Carpathian Basin. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and yellow disc flowers surrounded by pale yellow to brown ray florets.

The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, often in open habitats such as grasslands and slopes. It is a perennial herb with basal and alternate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Hieracium genus, which includes many other hawkweed species.
