Hieracium sylvaticum subsp. siliginellum

Hieracium sylvaticum subsp.

siliginellum, commonly known as the woodland hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It has hairy stems and leaves that are often glandular, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich soils and is often found in association with other herbaceous plants in temperate regions.
