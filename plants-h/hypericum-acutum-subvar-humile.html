Hypericum acutum subvar. humile

Hypericum acutum subvar. humile, commonly known as the dwarf St. John's wort, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typically from June to August. The plant has narrow, upright stems and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its medicinal properties, similar to other Hypericum species, have been historically used in herbal remedies.
