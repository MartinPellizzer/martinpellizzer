Helianthemum canum subsp. piloselloides

Helianthemum canum subsp.

piloselloides, commonly known as the woolly rockrose, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its hairy, grey-green foliage and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry habitats. It is drought-tolerant and prefers full sun, making it a hardy species well adapted to Mediterranean climates.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
