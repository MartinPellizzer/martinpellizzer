Hieracium atrocinereum

Hieracium atrocinereum, commonly known as the black-centred hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and ragworts, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its dark purple to black central disc is surrounded by yellow ray florets, giving it a striking appearance.

Hieracium atrocinereum is often found in sunny to partially shaded areas and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
