Hieracium nargonense

Hieracium nargonense, commonly known as the Nargon hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine meadows, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are basal, rough, and covered with fine hairs, while the stems are upright and often branched.

The flowers bloom in late summer, producing yellowish-orange inflorescences that attract various pollinators.
