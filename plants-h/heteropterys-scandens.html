Heteropterys scandens

Heteropterys scandens, commonly known as the climbing holly or velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a woody vine that can grow up to 10 meters in length, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a waxy, glossy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by spherical, red berries that contain numerous seeds.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and it is also known for its ability to thrive in disturbed habitats.
