Horkelia fusca subsp. tenella

Horkelia fusca subsp.

tenella, commonly known as the slender horkelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, hairy stems and small, clustered leaves.

The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pale blue to white in color. It thrives in rocky, arid environments and is often found in open grasslands and sagebrush steppe.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions.
