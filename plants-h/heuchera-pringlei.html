Heuchera pringlei

Heuchera pringlei, commonly known as the Pringle's coral bells, is a perennial plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that range in color from deep red to purple and green, depending on the variety. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white during the summer months. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Heuchera pringlei is valued for its ornamental foliage and its ability to add texture and color to shaded landscapes.
