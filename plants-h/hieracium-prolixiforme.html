Hieracium prolixiforme

Hieracium prolixiforme, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems, which are typical features of the genus Hieracium. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are arranged in a composite head.

Hieracium prolixiforme is known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it an invasive species in many regions.
