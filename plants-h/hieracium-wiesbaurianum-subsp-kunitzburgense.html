Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp. kunitzburgense

Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp.

kunitzburgense, commonly known as the Kunitzburg hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and flowering heads that produce yellow ray florets.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. It has a hairy stem and is often found in regions with temperate climates.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and locally endemic species.
