Hessea cinnabarina

Hessea cinnabarina, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to the coastal areas of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has a woody stem and produces small, fleshy fruits that contain several seeds. Hessea cinnabarina thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
