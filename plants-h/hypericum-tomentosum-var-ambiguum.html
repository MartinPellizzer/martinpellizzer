Hypericum tomentosum var. ambiguum

Hypericum tomentosum var. ambiguum, commonly known as the fuzzy St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape and covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, often found in woodland edges and rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
