Helichrysum forsythii

Helichrysum forsythii, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of bright yellow, papery flowers that remain colorful even after drying. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is often used in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its aromatic foliage and striking appearance make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable.
