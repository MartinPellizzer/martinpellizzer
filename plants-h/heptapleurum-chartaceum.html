Heptapleurum chartaceum

Heptapleurum chartaceum, commonly known as the paper-leaf holly, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elongated, and often lobed leaves that are typically pale green and have a papery texture. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and landscape design.
