Hieracium magyaricum var. laconicum

Hieracium magyaricum var. laconicum, commonly known as the Hungarian hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun. It produces yellow flowers with ray florets and has a creeping root system that allows it to spread vegetatively.

Its leaves are usually alternate, rough to the touch, and have a distinctive silvery or whitish underside.
