Hieracium rigidum proles gracilipes

Hieracium rigidum proles gracilipes, commonly known as the slender-stemmed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

It is often found in moist or well-drained soils and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.
