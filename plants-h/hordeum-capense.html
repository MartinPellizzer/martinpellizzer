Hordeum capense

Hordeum capense, commonly known as Cape barley, is a species of grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences, which are typical of the Poaceae family. Hordeum capense is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora and a subject of study in ecological and agricultural research.
