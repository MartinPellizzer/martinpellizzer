Hydrobryopsis sessilis

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as on rocks or soil near streams and wetlands. It lacks true leaves and roots, relying instead on its thallus for absorption of water and nutrients.

Hydrobryopsis sessilis is an important indicator species for wetland ecosystems due to its specific environmental requirements.
