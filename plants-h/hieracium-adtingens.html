Hieracium adtingens

Hieracium adtingens, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, hairy leaves and produces bright yellow flowers with ray florets. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in wetlands.

Hieracium adtingens is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is considered an invasive species in some regions.
