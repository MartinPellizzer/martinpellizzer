Haplopappus viridialbus

Haplopappus viridialbus, commonly known as the greenleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
