Heterostega festuciformis

Heterostega festuciformis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown, arranged in spikelets. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, bogs, and wet meadows.

Heterostega festuciformis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates in its native habitats.
