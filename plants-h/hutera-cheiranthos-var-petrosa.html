Hutera cheiranthos var. petrosa

Hutera cheiranthos var. petrosa, commonly known as the rock hibiscus, is a perennial shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It features woody stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom from spring to summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is drought-tolerant.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
