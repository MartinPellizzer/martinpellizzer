Hyptis albicans

Hyptis albicans, commonly known as white hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves and small, white to pale blue flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded areas.

Hyptis albicans is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic effects.
