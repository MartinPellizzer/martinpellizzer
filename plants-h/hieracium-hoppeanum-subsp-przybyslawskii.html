Hieracium hoppeanum subsp. przybyslawskii

Hieracium hoppeanum subsp.

przybyslawskii, commonly known as the Polish hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats such as meadows and roadsides. It has a fibrous root system and produces numerous flowering stems that bloom in late summer.
