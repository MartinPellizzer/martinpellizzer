Hieracium insuetum subsp. cuspidatum

Hieracium insuetum subsp.

cuspidatum, commonly known as the prickly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to reddish flowers and hairy, sometimes spiny leaves.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It has a rosette of basal leaves that are usually covered in stiff, bristly hairs.

Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, and it produces small, composite flower heads that attract pollinators.
