Hieracium costeanum

Hieracium costeanum, commonly known as the Costeanum hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and upright stems that bear yellow flowers in the summer months. Native to Europe, it thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, dandelion-like flowers that attract pollinators.

It is considered a herbaceous plant with a fibrous root system and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
