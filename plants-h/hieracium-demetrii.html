Hieracium demetrii

Hieracium demetrii, commonly known as the Demetrius' hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring well-drained conditions. Its yellow flower heads consist of numerous small florets, and it produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

Hieracium demetrii is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid spread and adaptability.
