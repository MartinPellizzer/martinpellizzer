Hieracium macranthiforme

Hieracium macranthiforme, commonly known as the giant hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring rough, hairy stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are yellow, with prominent central disk florets surrounded by petal-like structures.

Hieracium macranthiforme is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and wastelands.
