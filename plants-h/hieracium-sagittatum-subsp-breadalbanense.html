Hieracium sagittatum subsp. breadalbanense

Hieracium sagittatum subsp.

breadalbanense, commonly known as the Scottish dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the British Isles. It features basal leaves that are lanceolate and often covered in fine hairs, and it produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands, roadsides, and woodland edges. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and is often found in moist or calcareous soils.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different leaf morphology and flowering habits compared to other members of the species.
