Hieracium ramondii subsp. odontocerinthe

Hieracium ramondii subsp.

odontocerinthe, commonly known as the toothed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves.

The plant produces yellow disk flowers with ray florets, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands.

Its subspecific name, *odontocerinthe*, refers to the toothed appearance of its leaves.
