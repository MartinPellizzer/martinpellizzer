Hieracium dasytrichum subsp. dasytrichum

Hieracium dasytrichum subsp.

dasytrichum, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features basal leaves that are rough and hairy, with a rosette structure, and elongated stems that bear yellow composite flowers. Its inflorescence consists of numerous small florets arranged in a flat-topped cluster, typical of the Asteraceae family.

The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name.
