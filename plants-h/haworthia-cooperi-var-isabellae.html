Haworthia cooperi var. isabellae

Haworthia cooperi var. isabellae, commonly known as the Isabellae Haworthia, is a compact, rosette-forming succulent native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically pale green with distinct, translucent windows, which aid in photosynthesis. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white or pale yellow during the warmer months. It prefers well-draining soil and bright, indirect light, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.

Due to its slow growth and attractive appearance, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
