Hieracium nigrescens subsp. imbutum

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

imbutum, commonly known as the black hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dark purple to black disc florets and a hairy, glandular stem.
