Helichrysum filifolium

Helichrysum filifolium, commonly known as the everlasting dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its yellow, dandelion-like flowers that are often used in floral arrangements due to their longevity. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind. Helichrysum filifolium thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in arid environments.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in its native habitat.
