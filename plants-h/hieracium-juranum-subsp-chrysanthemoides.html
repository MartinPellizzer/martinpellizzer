Hieracium juranum subsp. chrysanthemoides

Hieracium juranum subsp.

chrysanthemoides, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant features yellowish to orange composite flowers that resemble chrysanthemums, hence its common name. It has basal leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

This species is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
