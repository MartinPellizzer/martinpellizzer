Hypericum nitidum subsp. exile

Hypericum nitidum subsp.

exile, commonly known as the slender St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features slender, wiry stems that are often covered with fine hairs, and its leaves are small, elliptical, and slightly hairy.

The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in short inflorescences. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem and opposite leaf arrangement, typical of the Hypericaceae family.

It is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands.
