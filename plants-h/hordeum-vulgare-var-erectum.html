Hordeum vulgare var. erectum

Hordeum vulgare var. erectum, commonly known as stiff barley, is a variant of barley characterized by its upright growth habit.

It is a member of the grass family, Poaceae, and is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves and compact spikelets that contain the seeds. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a culm that is stiff and upright, distinguishing it from other barley varieties.

This variant is often studied for its potential in agricultural and ecological contexts due to its hardy nature and adaptability.
