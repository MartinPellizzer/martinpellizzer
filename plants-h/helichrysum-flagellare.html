Helichrysum flagellare

Helichrysum flagellare, commonly known as the golden scabious, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Helichrysum flagellare is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in dried flower arrangements.
