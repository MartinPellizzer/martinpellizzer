Hicorius pallida

Hicorius pallida, commonly known as the pale hicorius, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and rocky soils, preferring full sun exposure. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a rosette at the base.

Hicorius pallida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
