Halothamnus cinerascens

Halothamnus cinerascens, commonly known as the gray sand verbena, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody stems and narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers during the summer months, which are arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Halothamnus cinerascens is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in arid environments.
