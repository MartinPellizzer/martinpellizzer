Hoarea erythrophylla

Hoarea erythrophylla, commonly known as the red-flowered ironwood, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woody structure and small, leathery leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces clusters of bright red flowers, which are a striking feature and attract various pollinators. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep roots that allow it to access groundwater.

Hoarea erythrophylla is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
