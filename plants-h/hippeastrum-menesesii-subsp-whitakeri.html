Hippeastrum menesesii subsp. whitakeri

Hippeastrum menesesii subsp.

whitakeri, commonly known as the Whitaker's amaryllis, is a tropical plant native to the Andes region of South America. It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow.

The plant produces large, strap-like leaves that are often used as ornamental foliage. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This subspecies is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens with suitable climate conditions.
