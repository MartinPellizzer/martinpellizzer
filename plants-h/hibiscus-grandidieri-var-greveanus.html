Hibiscus grandidieri var. greveanus

Hibiscus grandidieri var. greveanus, commonly known as the Madagascar hibiscus, is a unique variant native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink, with a prominent central pistil. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinct appearance. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
