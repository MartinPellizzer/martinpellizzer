Hieracium hirsutulum

Hieracium hirsutulum, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are hairy and lance-shaped, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium hirsutulum is often considered a weed but can provide nectar for pollinators.
