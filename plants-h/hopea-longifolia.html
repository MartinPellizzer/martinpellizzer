Hopea longifolia

Hopea longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf hopea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture-making.

Hopea longifolia also plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
