Helianthemum apenninum var. viridifolium

Helianthemum apenninum var. viridifolium, commonly known as the greenleaf rockrose, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has compact, upright stems covered in fine, white hairs, and its leaves are narrow, leathery, and often a vibrant green. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ornamental appeal in dry, sunny environments.
