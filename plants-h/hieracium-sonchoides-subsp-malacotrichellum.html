Hieracium sonchoides subsp. malacotrichellum

Hieracium sonchoides subsp.

malacotrichellum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 50 cm.

The plant features basal and stem leaves that are lance-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a flat-topped cluster, and the plant produces small, oblong achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

It is characterized by its yellow coloration and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
