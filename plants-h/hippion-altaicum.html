Hippion altaicum

Hippion altaicum, commonly known as the Altai bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its showy blue or violet petals and is often found in alpine and rocky habitats. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has hairy stems and leaves. It blooms in the summer months, producing clusters of bell-shaped flowers.

Hippion altaicum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
