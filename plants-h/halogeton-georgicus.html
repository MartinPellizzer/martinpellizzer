Halogeton georgicus

Halogeton georgicus, commonly known as the desert saltbush, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the western United States and northern Mexico.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are succulent and fleshy, which helps it store water and survive in drought conditions.
