Hyptis pachycephala

Hyptis pachycephala, commonly known as the thick-headed hyptis, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit and grayish-green foliage. Its small, tubular flowers are typically clustered in dense inflorescences, often with a purple or violet hue.

Hyptis pachycephala is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
