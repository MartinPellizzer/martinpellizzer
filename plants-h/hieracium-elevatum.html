Hieracium elevatum

Hieracium elevatum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow or orange composite flowers that consist of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils across Europe and parts of Asia. Its leaves are alternate, rough to the touch, and often have a hairy texture.

Hieracium elevatum is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common and sometimes invasive species in certain regions.
