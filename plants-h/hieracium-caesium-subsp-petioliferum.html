Hieracium caesium subsp. petioliferum

Hieracium caesium subsp.

petioliferum, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring moist to moderately dry conditions. Its leaves are petiolate, meaning they have distinct leaf stalks, and the flowers are arranged in a single, terminal head.

It is often found in temperate regions and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.
