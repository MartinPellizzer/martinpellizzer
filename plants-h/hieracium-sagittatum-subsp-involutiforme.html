Hieracium sagittatum subsp. involutiforme

Hieracium sagittatum subsp.

involutiforme, commonly known as the curled leaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The subspecies is distinguished by its involute (curled) leaf morphology, which gives it its common name. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides.

The plant produces yellow ray florets and is often found in temperate climates.
