Harpochilus trimerocalyx

Harpochilus trimerocalyx, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by dark, round berries. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests and secondary growth areas.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic and practical properties.
