Hippocastanum rubicundum

Hippocastanum rubicundum, commonly known as the crimson horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that turn a vibrant red in the fall. The tree produces showy, white to pink flowers in spring, followed by spiny, orange-red fruits. Its bark is smooth and gray, with a distinctive, flaky texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been cultivated in gardens for its striking seasonal display.
