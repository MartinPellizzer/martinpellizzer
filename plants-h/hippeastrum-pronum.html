Hippeastrum pronum

Hippeastrum pronum, commonly known as the Brazilian peace lily, is a tropical plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its broad, strap-shaped leaves and striking, funnel-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant produces berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Hippeastrum pronum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
