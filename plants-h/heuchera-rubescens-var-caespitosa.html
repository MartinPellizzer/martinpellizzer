Heuchera rubescens var. caespitosa

Heuchera rubescens var. caespitosa, commonly known as the red-leaf heuchera, is a compact, clump-forming perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features striking, heart-shaped leaves that range from deep red to purplish hues, with a velvety texture. The plant produces upright, spikelike clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its foliage remains colorful throughout the growing season, offering ornamental value from spring to fall.
