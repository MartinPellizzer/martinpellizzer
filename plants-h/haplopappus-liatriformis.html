Haplopappus liatriformis

Haplopappus liatriformis, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are usually basal, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters.

Haplopappus liatriformis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators in its native habitat.
