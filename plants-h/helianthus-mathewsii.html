Helianthus mathewsii

Helianthus mathewsii, commonly known as Mathews' sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers and robust, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Helianthus mathewsii is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
