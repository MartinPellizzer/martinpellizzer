Hippocrepis elongatula

Hippocrepis elongatula, commonly known as the elongated bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The flowers are typically pale blue or purple and bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has small, linear leaves and produces small, elongated pods that contain seeds.
