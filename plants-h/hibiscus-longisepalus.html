Hibiscus longisepalus

Hibiscus longisepalus, commonly known as the long-sepaled hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically have five to seven sepals, which are longer than the petals. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a rough texture.

Hibiscus longisepalus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
