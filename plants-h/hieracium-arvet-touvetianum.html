Hieracium arvet-touvetianum

Hieracium arvet-touvetianum, commonly known as the Toulouet's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. It produces yellow flowers with a central disk, and its leaves are usually lance-shaped with a rough texture.

Hieracium arvet-touvetianum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain habitats.
