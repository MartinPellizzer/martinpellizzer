Hackelia bella

Hackelia bella, commonly known as fairy bells, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial shrub that produces clusters of bell-shaped, pink or white flowers. The plant has slender, upright stems and small, oval-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its showy flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, making it a popular choice for native gardens.
