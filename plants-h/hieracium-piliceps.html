Hieracium piliceps

Hieracium piliceps, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the sunflower family (Asteraceae).

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems, which give it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it is often found in meadows and grassy areas.
