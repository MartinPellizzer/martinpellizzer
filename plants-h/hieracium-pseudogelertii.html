Hieracium pseudogelertii

Hieracium pseudogelertii, commonly known as the pseudogelert hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a cluster of small flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are basal, hairy, and often have a lobed or toothed margin.

The showy yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
