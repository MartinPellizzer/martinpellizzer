Hyoscyamus arabicus

Hyoscyamus arabicus, commonly known as the Greater Celandine, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and yellowish flowers. The plant produces globose, ribbed berries that contain the seeds. It is known for its toxic properties and has been used historically in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and pharmacological studies.
