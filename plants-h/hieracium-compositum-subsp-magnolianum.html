Hieracium compositum subsp. magnolianum

Hieracium compositum subsp.

magnolianum, commonly known as the magnolia hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of many small florets.

The plant typically has hairy stems and leaves that are lance-shaped with serrated edges. Its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
