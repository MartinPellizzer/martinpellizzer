Hermannia procumbens subsp. myrrhifolia

Hermannia procumbens subsp.

myrrhifolia, commonly known as the red-veined strobilanthes, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks.

The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves with distinctive red veins, and it produces clusters of tiny, tubular flowers that are usually greenish or reddish in color. It is often found in the understory of rainforests and requires high humidity and well-drained soil to thrive.

The subspecies myrrhifolia is characterized by its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
