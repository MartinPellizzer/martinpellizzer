Hieracium blepharidiphyllum

Hieracium blepharidiphyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are narrow and often have a rough, hairy texture, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in various environments.
