Haumaniastrum suberosum subsp. kibarense

Haumaniastrum suberosum subsp.

kibarense, commonly known as the Kibari tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Africa. It is characterized by its thick, flaky bark and small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits that split open to release seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to seasonal droughts.

The species is of ecological importance in its native range, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
