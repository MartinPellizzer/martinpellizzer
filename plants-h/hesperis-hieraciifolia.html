Hesperis hieraciifolia

Hesperis hieraciifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
