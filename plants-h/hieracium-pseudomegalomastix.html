Hieracium pseudomegalomastix

Hieracium pseudomegalomastix, commonly known as the giant false dandelion, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its large, yellow-orange composite flowers and robust, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is native to parts of Europe and western Asia. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its roots are fibrous and adaptive to wet environments.

This species is often found in meadows, riverbanks, and other damp habitats.
