Hydrangea ampla

Hydrangea ampla, commonly known as the mountain hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from white to pink, depending on soil pH. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are broadly ovate with serrated edges, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in late spring.

Hydrangea ampla is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and natural landscapes.
