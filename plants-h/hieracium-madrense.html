Hieracium madrense

Hieracium madrense, commonly known as Madren's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

Hieracium madrense is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions and is often found in disturbed soils.
