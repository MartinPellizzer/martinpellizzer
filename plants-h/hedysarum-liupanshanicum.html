Hedysarum liupanshanicum

Hedysarum liupanshanicum, commonly known as the Liupanshan milkvetch, is a perennial leguminous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes, which are typically visited by pollinators such as bees. Its seeds are encased in pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind or animals.

Hedysarum liupanshanicum is often found in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions.
