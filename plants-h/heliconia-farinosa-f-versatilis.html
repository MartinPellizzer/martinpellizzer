Heliconia farinosa f. versatilis

Heliconia farinosa f. versatilis, commonly known as the variable heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its large, brightly colored bracts that resemble flowers. The plant has a tall, leafy stem with broad, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its inflorescence consists of clusters of small, spadix-like flowers surrounded by modified leaves called bracts.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
