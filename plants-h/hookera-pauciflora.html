Hookera pauciflora

Hookera pauciflora, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in shades of blue or violet, adapting to the harsh, high-altitude environments where it thrives. Its leaves are fleshy and succulent, helping it retain moisture in arid conditions.

Hookera pauciflora is often found in rocky or alpine meadows, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
