Houstonia calycosa

Houstonia calycosa, commonly known as the pink rock violet, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Violaceae family and is characterized by its small, showy pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils and thrives in partial shade. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.
