Haworthia mirabilis subsp. badia

Haworthia mirabilis subsp.

badia, commonly known as the Badian haworthia, is a compact, rosette-forming succulent native to South Africa. It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with white or pale markings, giving it a striking appearance.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the growing season. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for rock gardens or container cultivation.

Its unique foliage and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
