Hydriastele gracilis

Hydriastele gracilis, commonly known as the slender hydriastele, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

This evergreen plant is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central water-holding structure, typical of many bromeliads. The leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss. It produces small, white or pale pink flowers that emerge from the center of the rosette, adding a delicate ornamental feature.

Due to its ability to thrive in humid environments, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
