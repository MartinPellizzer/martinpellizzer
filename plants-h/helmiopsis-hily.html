Helmiopsis hily

Helmiopsis hily, commonly known as the silverleaf helmiopsis, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features succulent, lance-shaped leaves covered in a dense layer of white, waxy hair, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the cooler months. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Helmiopsis hily is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
