Huperzia dichotoma

Huperzia dichotoma, commonly known as the forked huperzia, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen rhizome and its distinctive, dichotomous branching pattern of fronds. The fronds are typically short and linear, often with a leathery texture and a dark green color. This plant is native to various regions in Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is often found in rocky or forested areas, where it contributes to the understory vegetation.
