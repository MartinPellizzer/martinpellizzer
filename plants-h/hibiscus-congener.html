Hibiscus congener

Hibiscus congener, commonly known as the common hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically have five petals and a prominent central stigma. The plant produces fibrous, woody stems and has heart-shaped, serrated leaves. Hibiscus congener thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its flowers bloom in summer and are often used in ornamental gardening and traditional medicine.
