Hieracium glaucopsis proles inclinatum

Hieracium glaucopsis proles inclinatum, commonly known as the leaning blue hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its blue-green foliage and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as meadows and roadsides. It has a creeping growth habit with prostrate stems that often lean over, giving it its common name.

Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
