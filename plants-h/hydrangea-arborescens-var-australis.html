Hydrangea arborescens var. australis

Hydrangea arborescens var. australis, commonly known as the Australian snowball bush, is a deciduous shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features large, round clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves that turn reddish in autumn. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
