Hyptis lantanifolia var. costaricensis

Hyptis lantanifolia var. costaricensis, commonly known as the Costa Rican hyptis, is a herbaceous plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are often hairy and have a strong, minty scent, which is a common trait among species in its genus.

It is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
