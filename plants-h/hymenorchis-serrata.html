Hymenorchis serrata

Hymenorchis serrata, commonly known as the serrated twayblade, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with a distinctive appearance, featuring a slender, leafless stem and a single, showy flower. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with intricate patterns, and it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

Hymenorchis serrata is noted for its delicate structure and is often found in rocky or humus-rich soils.
