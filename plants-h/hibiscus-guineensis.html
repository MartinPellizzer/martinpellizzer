Hibiscus guineensis

Hibiscus guineensis, commonly known as the African rose or Guinea hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to West Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, with simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. The flowers are large, showy, and typically pink or red, with five petals and a prominent central stigma. The plant produces capsules that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind or water.

Hibiscus guineensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
