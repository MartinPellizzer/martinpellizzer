Heuchera obtusissima

Heuchera obtusissima, commonly known as the velvet leaf heuchera, is a perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink or red, depending on the variety. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Heuchera obtusissima is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and ability to add color and texture to shaded landscapes.
