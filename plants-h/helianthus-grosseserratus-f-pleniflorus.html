Helianthus grosseserratus f. pleniflorus

Helianthus grosseserratus f. pleniflorus, commonly known as the thickleaf sunflower, is a variant of the thickleaf sunflower native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its robust, hairy stems and broad, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine, stiff hairs. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers with a prominent central disk, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its foliage is often glandular and can emit a mild, aromatic scent.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and hardy nature.
