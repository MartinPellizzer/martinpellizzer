Heuchera utahensis

Heuchera utahensis, commonly known as Utah alumroot, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features basal leaves that are typically green or red-tinged, with a hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and early blooming habit.
