Hypericum annulatum

Hypericum annulatum, commonly known as the yellow wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Hypericum annulatum is part of the genus Hypericum, which includes many species known for their medicinal and ornamental value.
