Haplostachys haplostachya var. angustifolia

Haplostachys haplostachya var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved false indigo, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers in late spring, which are clustered in racemes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
