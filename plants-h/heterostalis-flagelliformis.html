Heterostalis flagelliformis

Heterostalis flagelliformis, commonly known as the silky flagelliform, is a unique plant species characterized by its long, slender, and fibrous stems.

It is native to certain regions of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is notable for its ability to produce a type of fibrous material that is lightweight yet strong, making it valuable in traditional crafts. Its growth pattern involves a creeping habit, with the stems often trailing along the ground or climbing over other vegetation.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Asteraceae, and its structure reflects adaptations to its specific ecological niche.
