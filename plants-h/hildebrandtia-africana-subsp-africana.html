Hildebrandtia africana subsp. africana

Hildebrandtia africana subsp.

africana, commonly known as the African hildebrandtia, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
