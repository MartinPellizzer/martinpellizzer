Halimium hirsutissimum

Halimium hirsutissimum, commonly known as the woolly catchfly, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for rock gardens and drought-tolerant landscapes.

Halimium hirsutissimum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
