Helictotrichon pubescens f. subtricolor

Helictotrichon pubescens f. subtricolor, commonly known as the multicolored bluegrass, is a grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, hairy leaves and compact growth habit. The plant displays a striking color variation, with leaves that can range from blue-green to silver-white. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

This ornamental grass is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is used in landscaping and conservation efforts.
