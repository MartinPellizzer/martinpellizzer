Heuchera parvifolia var. major

Heuchera parvifolia var. major, commonly known as the 'Coral Bells' plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features dense, mounding foliage that varies in color from deep red to purple and green, depending on the season and light conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white during the summer months. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant foliage and delicate floral display.
