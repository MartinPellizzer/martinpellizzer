Hypericum impunctatum

Hypericum impunctatum, commonly known as the smooth St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has slender, woody stems and lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and woodlands.

Hypericum impunctatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
