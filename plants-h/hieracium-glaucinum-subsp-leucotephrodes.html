Hieracium glaucinum subsp. leucotephrodes

Hieracium glaucinum subsp.

leucotephrodes, commonly known as the white-topped hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and occur in clusters, with the subspecies distinguished by its white-tipped bracts.

It is part of the Asteraceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
