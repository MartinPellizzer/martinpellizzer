Hemionitis engywookii

Hemionitis engywookii, commonly known as the desert shield fern, is a unique plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is a member of the Hemionitidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth form. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to extreme temperature fluctuations.

Due to its specialized habitat, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
