Hierochloe novae-zelandiae

Hierochloe novae-zelandiae, commonly known as New Zealand hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open habitats such as grasslands and streambanks. Its flowering stems bear small, spikelet-like inflorescences, and it produces wind-dispersed seeds.

Hierochloe novae-zelandiae plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
