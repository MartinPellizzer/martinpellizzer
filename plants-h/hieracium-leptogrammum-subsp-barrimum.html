Hieracium leptogrammum subsp. barrimum

Hieracium leptogrammum subsp.

barrimum, commonly known as the slender-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. It has slender, hairy stems and leaves with a distinctive, serrated margin.

Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer, contributing to its role in pollinator habitats.
