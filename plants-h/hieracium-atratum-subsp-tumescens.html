Hieracium atratum subsp. tumescens

Hieracium atratum subsp.

tumescens, commonly known as the purple hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and often have a bluish-green hue, while its flowers are purple to reddish-purple in color.

The species is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain habitats.
