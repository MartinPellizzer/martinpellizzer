Hieracium umbrolympicum

Hieracium umbrolympicum, commonly known as the shadowy hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium umbrolympicum is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions, making it a common sight in forested areas.
