Hypochaeris tenuifolia var. eurylepis

Hypochaeris tenuifolia var. eurylepis, commonly known as the slenderleaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, while the flowers are yellow with a central disc.

It is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize new areas, making it both a weed and a pioneer species in ecological succession.
