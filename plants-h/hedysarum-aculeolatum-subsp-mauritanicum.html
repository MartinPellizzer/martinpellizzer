Hedysarum aculeolatum subsp. mauritanicum

Hedysarum aculeolatum subsp.

mauritanicum, commonly known as the Mauritania burr vetch, is a flowering plant native to North Africa and the Middle East. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant features compound leaves with small, trifoliate leaflets and produces yellow to orange pea-like flowers. Its fruit is a legume that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is adapted to drought conditions and plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.
