Hakea gariwerdensis

Hakea gariwerdensis, commonly known as the Gariwerden hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical or cylindrical-branching stems and its unique, woody, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, urn-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red in color. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

Hakea gariwerdensis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing the plant's resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
