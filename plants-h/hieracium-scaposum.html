Hieracium scaposum

Hieracium scaposum, commonly known as the tall hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. The plant features basal rosettes of leaves and a single, erect stem that bears yellow flowers arranged in a composite head. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and the flowers are surrounded by a series of bracts.

Hieracium scaposum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
