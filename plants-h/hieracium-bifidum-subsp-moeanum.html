Hieracium bifidum subsp. moeanum

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

moeanum, commonly known as the Moean hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow flowering heads.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded conditions. Its flowers are surrounded by bracts and are visited by various pollinators, contributing to its ecological role in supporting biodiversity.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly divided leaves and specific geographic distribution.
