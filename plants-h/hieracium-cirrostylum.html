Hieracium cirrostylum

Hieracium cirrostylum, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and basal rosettes of lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some regions.
