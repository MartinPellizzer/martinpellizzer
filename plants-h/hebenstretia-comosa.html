Hebenstretia comosa

Hebenstretia comosa, commonly known as the woolly hebenstretia, is a herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, woolly leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Hebenstretia comosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in garden settings.
