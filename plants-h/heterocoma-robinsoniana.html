Heterocoma robinsoniana

Heterocoma robinsoniana, commonly known as the Robinson's heterocoma, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has small, narrow leaves. It produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer.

Heterocoma robinsoniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
