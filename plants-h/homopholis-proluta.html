Homopholis proluta

Homopholis proluta, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow environments.

The flowers, which bloom in late summer, range in color from white to pale blue, attracting various pollinators.
