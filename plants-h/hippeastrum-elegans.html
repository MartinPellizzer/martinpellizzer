Hippeastrum elegans

Hippeastrum elegans, commonly known as the Amazonian belladonna lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, often displaying vibrant shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces trumpet-shaped blooms that are typically borne on a single stem, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, emerging from a fleshy bulb that stores nutrients for flowering.

Hippeastrum elegans thrives in warm, humid conditions and is often cultivated for its dramatic floral display.
