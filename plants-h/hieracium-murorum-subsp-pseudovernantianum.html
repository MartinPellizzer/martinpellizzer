Hieracium murorum subsp. pseudovernantianum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

pseudovernantianum, commonly known as the false mouse-ear hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows. It has finely divided, grass-like leaves and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

This subspecies is often found in temperate regions of Europe and is considered a perennial herb.
