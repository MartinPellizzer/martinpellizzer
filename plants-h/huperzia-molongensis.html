Huperzia molongensis

Huperzia molongensis, commonly known as the Molong fern, is a species of clubmoss native to Australia.

It is characterized by its erect, segmented stems and pinnately compound leaves that are often covered in small, rounded teeth. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and rocky areas. It produces sporangia in clusters on the undersides of its leaves, which are responsible for spore dispersal.

Huperzia molongensis is valued for its ornamental appearance and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its habitat.
