Heliotropium compactum

Heliotropium compactum, commonly known as the compact heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, bushy growth habit and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or pink. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Heliotropium compactum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
