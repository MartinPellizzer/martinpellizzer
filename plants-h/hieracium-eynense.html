Hieracium eynense

Hieracium eynense, commonly known as the Eyne hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are basal, spoon-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head.

Hieracium eynense is often found in mountainous areas of Austria, Switzerland, and Italy, where it contributes to the biodiversity of alpine ecosystems.
