Hordeum vulgare var. sinicum

Hordeum vulgare var. sinicum, commonly known as Chinese barley, is a variant of the common barley species native to Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical spikes and small, hard grains that are typically brown or yellow in color. This plant thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value and use in traditional foods. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and narrow, grass-like leaves.

It is also valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in sustainable agriculture.
