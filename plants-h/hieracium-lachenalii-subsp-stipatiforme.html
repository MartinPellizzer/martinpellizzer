Hieracium lachenalii subsp. stipatiforme

Hieracium lachenalii subsp.

stipatiforme, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and often hairy, while its yellow flowers are arranged in clusters.

It is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
