Humata multifida

Humata multifida, commonly known as the broad-leafed fern, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, pinnately divided leaves that give it a feathery appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its fronds are typically dark green and can grow up to several feet in length.

Humata multifida is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
