Hydrangea stellata var. prolifera

Hydrangea stellata var. prolifera, commonly known as the star hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, cone-shaped flower clusters that range in color from white to pink depending on soil pH. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is typically dark green, and it thrives in partial shade with moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens for its elegant appearance.
