Hieracium murorum subsp. viridescens

Hieracium murorum subsp.

viridescens, commonly known as the greenish hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and clustered in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is known for its ability to thrive in temperate climates and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
