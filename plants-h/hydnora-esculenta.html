Hydnora esculenta

Hydnora esculenta, commonly known as the meat flower, is a parasitic plant native to southern Africa.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on its host plant for nutrients, typically growing on the roots of Euphorbia species. The plant produces a fleshy, reddish structure that resembles meat, which attracts flies for pollination. Its flowers are odorless but emit a strong, decaying flesh smell.

Hydnora esculenta is notable for its unique morphology and specialized ecological role in its habitat.
