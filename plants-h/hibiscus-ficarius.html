Hibiscus ficarius

Hibiscus ficarius, commonly known as the sand hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red, often with a distinctive dark throat. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in drought conditions.

Hibiscus ficarius is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
