Hieracium pseudotommasinii subsp. stupposiceps

Hieracium pseudotommasinii subsp.

stupposiceps, commonly known as the tall-headed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces a single, tall flowering stem.
