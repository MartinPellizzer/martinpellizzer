Hieracium vulgatum subsp. chlorophyllum

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

chlorophyllum, commonly known as the green hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellowish to orange flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are upright and covered in fine hairs.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more compact growth habit and the presence of chlorophyll-rich, greenish-yellow flower heads.
