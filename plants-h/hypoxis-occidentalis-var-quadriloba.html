Hypoxis occidentalis var. quadriloba

Hypoxis occidentalis var. quadriloba, commonly known as the four-lobed star grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves with distinctive four-lobed margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate foliage and subtle floral display.
