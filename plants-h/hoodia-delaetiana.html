Hoodia delaetiana

Hoodia delaetiana, commonly known as the desert hedgehog cactus, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, segmented stems that store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are reduced to spines, which help reduce water loss and deter herbivores.

Hoodia delaetiana is also noted for its potential medicinal properties, though further research is needed to confirm its efficacy.
