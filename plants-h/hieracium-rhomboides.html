Hieracium rhomboides

Hieracium rhomboides, commonly known as the rhomboid hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Hieracium rhomboides is known for its rhombic-shaped leaves and its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
