Hieracium elongatifolium

Hieracium elongatifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. Its leaves are elongated and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a composite head.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is often found in temperate climates.
