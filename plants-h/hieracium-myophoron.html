Hieracium myophoron

Hieracium myophoron, commonly known as the purple meadow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range from yellow to purple, depending on the variety.

Hieracium myophoron is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
