Hepatica triloba var. acuta

Hepatica triloba var. acuta, commonly known as the sharp-lobed hepatica, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves and early spring blooms. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial shade. Its flowers, which can be white, blue, or purple, are followed by small, green berries that mature in the summer.

The leaves are sharply lobed, giving the plant its common name and contributing to its ornamental value in woodland gardens.
