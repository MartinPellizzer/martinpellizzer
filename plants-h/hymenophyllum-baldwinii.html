Hymenophyllum baldwinii

Hymenophyllum baldwinii, commonly known as the Baldwin's filmy fern, is a small, delicate fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, often translucent fronds that give it a lace-like appearance. This epiphytic plant typically grows on tree trunks or rocks in moist, shaded environments, particularly in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spores are released from tiny, clustered structures called sorangia, which are embedded in the undersides of its fronds.

The plant's unique morphology and habitat requirements make it a subject of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
