Helianthus angustifolius subsp. giganteus

Helianthus angustifolius subsp.

giganteus, commonly known as the giant narrow-leaf sunflower, is a tall, herbaceous perennial native to the southwestern United States. It features narrow, linear leaves and large, showy yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk of tubular florets.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the sunflower family, Asteraceae.
