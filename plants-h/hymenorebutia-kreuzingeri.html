Hymenorebutia kreuzingeri

Hymenorebutia kreuzingeri, commonly known as the silver moon cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to South America.

It is a compact, low-growing plant with a distinctive appearance featuring a swollen, globular stem and a dense covering of white, velvety hairs. The plant produces showy, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
