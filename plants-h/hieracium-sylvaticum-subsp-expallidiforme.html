Hieracium sylvaticum subsp. expallidiforme

Hieracium sylvaticum subsp.

expallidiforme, commonly known as the pale-leaf wood hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow flowering heads.

The leaves are typically lanceolate and have a rough, hairy texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain habitats.
