Hyptis quadrialata

Hyptis quadrialata, commonly known as the four-leaf hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its small, opposite leaves and clusters of purple or pink tubular flowers. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
