Heracleum leskovii var. angustilaciniatum

Heracleum leskovii var. angustilaciniatum, commonly known as narrow-leaf cow parsnip, is a variant of the cow parsnip species native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a biennial plant characterized by its tall, hollow stems and large, deeply lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in umbrella-like clusters, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its roots are thick and fleshy, and while not as commonly used as other parsnips, they can be edible when properly prepared.
