Hordeum pubiflorum var. intermedium

Hordeum pubiflorum var. intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate wild barley, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and spikelet-based inflorescences, with florets that are typically awned. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers, and its seeds are encased in a hull that is often removed during processing. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

This species is of interest in agricultural and ecological studies due to its adaptability and potential for use in crop improvement programs.
