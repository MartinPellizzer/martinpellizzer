Hieracium subpratense

Hieracium subpratense, commonly known as meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, and its leaves are lance-shaped with hairy surfaces.

Hieracium subpratense is often found in open habitats and can form dense colonies, contributing to the biodiversity of its environment.
