Hieracium flocciferum subsp. subflocciferum

Hieracium flocciferum subsp.

subflocciferum, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and yellow flower heads.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are broadly ovate with a hairy texture, and the flowers are arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It is often found in open habitats and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.
