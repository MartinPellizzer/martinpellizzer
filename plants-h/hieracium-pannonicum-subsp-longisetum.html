Hieracium pannonicum subsp. longisetum

Hieracium pannonicum subsp.

longisetum, commonly known as the long-hair hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features bright yellow composite flowers, with a distinct ray floret structure surrounded by a dense disc of tubular flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and often have a rough texture.

The subspecies is characterized by its long, stiff bristles on the flower heads and stems, contributing to its common name.
