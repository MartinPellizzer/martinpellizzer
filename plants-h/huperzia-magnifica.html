Huperzia magnifica

Huperzia magnifica, commonly known as the magnolia fern, is a species of clubmoss native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and its distinctive, large, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of tiny, scale-like structures. The plant produces spore-bearing cones at the base of its fronds, which are typically clustered in small, compact clusters. Huperzia magnifica thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
