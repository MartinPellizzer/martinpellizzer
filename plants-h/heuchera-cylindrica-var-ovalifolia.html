Heuchera cylindrica var. ovalifolia

Heuchera cylindrica var. ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf alumroot, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale pink. The foliage is characterized by broad, oval-shaped leaves that are typically green or variegated with red or purple hues. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This plant is valued for its ornamental foliage and early spring bloom.
