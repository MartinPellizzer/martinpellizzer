Heliotropium macrolimbe

Heliotropium macrolimbe, commonly known as the African heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with hairy surfaces, and its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences. The flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive central disc, and they emit a sweet, vanilla-like fragrance.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.
