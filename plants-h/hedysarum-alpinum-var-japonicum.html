Hedysarum alpinum var. japonicum

Hedysarum alpinum var. japonicum, commonly known as the Japanese alpine vetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves and produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and compound fruit pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
