Herorchis picta

Herorchis picta, commonly known as the painted orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant, often colorful flowers that display intricate patterns, which are adapted for attracting specific pollinators. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air. Its leaves are usually broad and fleshy, aiding in water storage in its natural habitat.

The species is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity.
