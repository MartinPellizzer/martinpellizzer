Hyptis viscidula

Hyptis viscidula, commonly known as sticky hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a sticky texture. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color.

Hyptis viscidula is often found in moist, shaded areas and is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
