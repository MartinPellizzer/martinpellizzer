Hermione tazetta var. cupularis

Hermione tazetta var. cupularis, commonly known as the cupularis daffodil, is a variant of the common daffodil characterized by its unique, cup-like floral structure.

It is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia, typically blooming in early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and a single, showy flower with a trumpet-shaped corona. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking early-season color.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and botanically interesting for study.
