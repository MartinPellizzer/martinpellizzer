Heteropogon fischerianus

Heteropogon fischerianus, commonly known as Fischer's grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The leaves are narrow, grass-like, and typically have a bluish-green coloration. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in regions with limited rainfall.
