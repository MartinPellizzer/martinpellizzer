Herpetacanthus melancholicus

Herpetacanthus melancholicus, commonly known as the sorrowful bellflower, is a rare flowering plant native to the humid rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet petals and heart-shaped leaves, which are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the wet season. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
