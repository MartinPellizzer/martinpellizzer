Hieracium caesium subsp. sublustre

Hieracium caesium subsp.

sublustre, commonly known as the glossy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
