Hancornia laxa

Hancornia laxa, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, featuring a straight trunk and a broad, rounded crown. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a smooth surface and a slightly waxy texture. Its flowers are small, clustered in panicles, and typically have a yellowish or greenish color with red or orange stamens.

The plant produces woody, elongated fruits that contain a single seed, and it is valued for its hard, durable wood.
