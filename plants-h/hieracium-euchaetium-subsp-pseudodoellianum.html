Hieracium euchaetium subsp. pseudodoellianum

Hieracium euchaetium subsp.

pseudodoellianum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough, hairy texture, while its stems are upright and can reach heights of up to 60 cm.

This subspecies is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
