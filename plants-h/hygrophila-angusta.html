Hygrophila angusta

Hygrophila angusta, commonly known as narrow-leaved hygrophila, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its small, white flowers are arranged in spikes and bloom during the summer months.

Hygrophila angusta is often used in aquatic gardening and as a decorative plant in water features.
