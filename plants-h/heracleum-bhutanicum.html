Heracleum bhutanicum

Heracleum bhutanicum, commonly known as Bhutanese cow parsnip, is a member of the Apiaceae family and is native to the Himalayan region.

It is a tall herbaceous plant that can grow up to 2 meters in height, featuring large, compound leaves with a distinctive fern-like appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in umbels, which are typical of the carrot family. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
