Hieracium lysanum

Hieracium lysanum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and woodland edges, thriving in moist to moderately dry soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range in color from yellow to orange.

Hieracium lysanum is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common yet sometimes invasive species in certain regions.
