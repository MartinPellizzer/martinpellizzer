Hieracium umbrosum subsp. pseudopollichiae

Hieracium umbrosum subsp.

pseudopollichiae, commonly known as the shadow hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. It has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, clustered flower heads.

This subspecies is noted for its ecological importance in supporting pollinators and maintaining biodiversity in its native habitats.
