Hesperis matronalis var. meyeriana

Hesperis matronalis var. meyeriana, commonly known as the Meyer's sweet rocket, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It produces clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its botanical features include a short, stout stem and small, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge.
