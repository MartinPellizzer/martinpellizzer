Helia tetragona

Helia tetragona, commonly known as the four-angled helia, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and has hairy, four-angled stems. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are yellow and produce small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.
