Humata grandissima

Humata grandissima, commonly known as the giant humata, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, waxy, and often colorful leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom during the wet season. Humata grandissima is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its dramatic foliage.

It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.
