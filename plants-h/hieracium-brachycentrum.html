Hieracium brachycentrum

Hieracium brachycentrum, commonly known as the short-centred hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium brachycentrum is known for its ability to spread rapidly through rhizomes, making it an invasive species in some regions.
