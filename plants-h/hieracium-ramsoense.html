Hieracium ramsoense

Hieracium ramsoense, commonly known as the Ramsons hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows. The plant features a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. It has a taproot system and is often found in association with other shade-tolerant flora.

The species is characterized by its distinctive, slightly hairy leaves and its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich soils.
