Helianthemum nummularium subsp. lycaonicum

Helianthemum nummularium subsp.

lycaonicum, commonly known as the Lycaon's penny-cress, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, rounded, silver-green leaves that are densely packed, giving it a mat-like appearance.

The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
