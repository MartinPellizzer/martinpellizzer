Hygrophila angustifolia var. quadrivalvis

Hygrophila angustifolia var. quadrivalvis, commonly known as the narrowleaf water hyssop, is a variant of the water hyssop species native to tropical regions.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, tubular flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds, where it can absorb excess moisture. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is also valued in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in aquatic conditions.
