Hieracium cerinthoides subsp. gymnocerinthe

Hieracium cerinthoides subsp.

gymnocerinthe, commonly known as the pale hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets.

The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils, and it has a rosette of basal leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowering stems bear yellow to orange composite flowers, and it is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

The subspecies gymnocerinthe is distinguished by its reduced leaf hairs and pale coloration compared to other members of the species.
