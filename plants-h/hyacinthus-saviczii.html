Hyacinthus saviczii

Hyacinthus saviczii, commonly known as the Persian hyacinth, is a bulbous plant native to Central Asia.

It produces striking clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is closely related to the common hyacinth. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and historical significance in horticulture.
