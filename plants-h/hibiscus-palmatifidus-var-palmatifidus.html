Hibiscus palmatifidus var. palmatifidus

Hibiscus palmatifidus var. palmatifidus, commonly known as the broadleaf hibiscus, is a tropical plant characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves.

It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, with five petals and a prominent central column. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can grow as a shrub or small tree.

Hibiscus palmatifidus var. palmatifidus is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
