Heracleum hirsutum

Heracleum hirsutum, commonly known as cow parsnip, is a tall biennial or short-lived perennial plant native to North America.

It features a robust, hollow stem that is often covered with fine hairs, and its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and deeply lobed. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its roots are thick and fleshy, and the plant is known for its strong, aromatic scent.

Cow parsnip is often found in moist meadows, stream banks, and disturbed areas.
