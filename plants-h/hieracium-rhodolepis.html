Hieracium rhodolepis

Hieracium rhodolepis, commonly known as the red-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually reddish or purple in color, which distinguishes it from other hawkweed species.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
