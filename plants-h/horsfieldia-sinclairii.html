Horsfieldia sinclairii

Horsfieldia sinclairii, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking red petals and greenish-yellow sepals. The plant typically grows on tree branches, drawing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its flowers are fragrant and attract various pollinators, including bees and birds.

Horsfieldia sinclairii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses and botanical gardens.
