Hieracium achlyopum

Hieracium achlyopum, commonly known as the pale hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters at the ends of its stems. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its flowers are typically visited by a variety of pollinators, including bees and flies.

The plant is also known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
