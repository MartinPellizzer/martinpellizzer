Hypericum norysca

Hypericum norysca, commonly known as the yellow hypericum, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green in color. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months, which are followed by small, red-orange berries. Hypericum norysca is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
