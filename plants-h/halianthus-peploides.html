Halianthus peploides

Halianthus peploides, commonly known as the silky goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is often found in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are covered in fine, silky hairs, which help reduce water loss and reflect sunlight.

Halianthus peploides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
