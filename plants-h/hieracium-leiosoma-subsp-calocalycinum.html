Hieracium leiosoma subsp. calocalycinum

Hieracium leiosoma subsp.

calocalycinum, commonly known as the smoothleaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and southern Europe. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.

The plant features basal leaves that are broadly ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth, often glabrous surface. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and are accompanied by bracts that are usually shorter than the flower heads.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and asexual reproduction through rhizomes.
