Hypericum acutum var. corsicum

Hypericum acutum var. corsicum, commonly known as Corsican St. John's Wort, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters along the stems. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal areas.

Its medicinal properties have been studied for potential use in treating mild depression and skin conditions.
