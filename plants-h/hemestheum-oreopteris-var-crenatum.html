Hemestheum oreopteris var. crenatum

Hemestheum oreopteris var. crenatum, commonly known as the crenate shield fern, is a unique variant of the shield fern species.

It is characterized by its distinctive, crenate (notched) fronds that give it a serrated appearance. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it a notable specimen in both horticultural and ecological studies.
