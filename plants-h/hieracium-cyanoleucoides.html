Hieracium cyanoleucoides

Hieracium cyanoleucoides, commonly known as the blue-white hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features basal leaves that are typically hairy and lance-shaped, with a rough texture. Its flowers are blue to white in color, with ray florets that are shorter than the central disk florets.

Hieracium cyanoleucoides is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
