Hebe flavida

Hebe flavida, commonly known as the golden hebe, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the plantain family (Plantaginaceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in spring, which are often tinged with orange or red, adding vibrant color to gardens. Hebe flavida thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its foliage is typically dark green and densely packed, providing a striking contrast to its colorful blooms.
