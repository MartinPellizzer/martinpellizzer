Hieracium ligulellum

Hieracium ligulellum, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and daisy-like appearance. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and woodland edges, preferring moist and well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

Hieracium ligulellum is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain habitats.
