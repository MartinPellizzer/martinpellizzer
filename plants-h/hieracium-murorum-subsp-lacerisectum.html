Hieracium murorum subsp. lacerisectum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

lacerisectum, commonly known as the lacerate hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas.

The plant features a basal rosette of leaves that are deeply lobed and often have a rough, hairy texture. Its flowering stems bear small, yellowish to reddish flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

The subspecies is distinguished by its distinctive leaf morphology and growth habit compared to other members of the Hieracium genus.
