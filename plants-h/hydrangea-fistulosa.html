Hydrangea fistulosa

Hydrangea fistulosa, commonly known as the mountain hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that range from white to pink or blue depending on soil pH. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with serrated margins and a glossy texture.

Hydrangea fistulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its seasonal blooms.
