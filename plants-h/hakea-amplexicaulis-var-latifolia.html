Hakea amplexicaulis var. latifolia

Hakea amplexicaulis var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with broad, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces striking, cylindrical flower spikes that are typically pink to red in color, appearing in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and dryland gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
