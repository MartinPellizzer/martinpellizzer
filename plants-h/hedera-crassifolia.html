Hedera crassifolia

Hedera crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved ivy, is a species of flowering plant in the Araliaceae family.

It is native to eastern Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves. Hedera crassifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
