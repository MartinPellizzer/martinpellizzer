Hyacinthus brachypodus

Hyacinthus brachypodus, commonly known as the dwarf hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of the Middle East.

It produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, violet, or white, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a basal rosette, and its bulbs are relatively small compared to other hyacinth species. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its beauty and fragrance.
