Hymenophyllum boschii

Hymenophyllum boschii, commonly known as the Bosch's filmy fern, is a rare and delicate epiphytic fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its small, thin, and translucent fronds that are typically less than 5 cm in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or rocks in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spores are produced in tiny, cup-like structures called sorus, which are located on the underside of the fronds.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, Hymenophyllum boschii is often found in protected, undisturbed areas such as cloud forests.
