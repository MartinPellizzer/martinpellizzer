Hieracium similigerum

Hieracium similigerum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Hieracium similigerum is an annual or biennial plant, producing seeds that are dispersed by wind.
