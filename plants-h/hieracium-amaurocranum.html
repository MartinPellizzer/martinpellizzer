Hieracium amaurocranum

Hieracium amaurocranum, commonly known as the black-veined hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of northern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are hairy and have a distinctive dark green color with prominent veins.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators.
