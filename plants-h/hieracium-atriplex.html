Hieracium atriplex

Hieracium atriplex, commonly known as the black hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and can grow up to 60 cm tall. Hieracium atriplex produces dark purple to black flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

It is typically found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
