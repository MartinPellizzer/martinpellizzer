Hieracium sylvaticum subsp. amaurocymum

Hieracium sylvaticum subsp.

amaurocymum, commonly known as the dark-centred hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in moist meadows, woodland edges, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy, with a central stem that bears flowering heads. Its flowers are yellow, with a dark center, and are surrounded by pale yellow ray florets.

It is part of the Asteraceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
