Halmoorea trispatha

Halmoorea trispatha, commonly known as the three-pathed fern, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that emerge from a central stem, giving it a lacy appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom during the wet season. Halmoorea trispatha thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in dense forest understories.
