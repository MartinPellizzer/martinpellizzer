Hedera willsiana

Hedera willsiana, commonly known as the Chinese ivy, is a species of climbing evergreen vine native to eastern Asia.

It features dark green, shiny, ovate leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically not showy, followed by bright red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Hedera willsiana is valued for its adaptability and ability to climb surfaces such as walls and trees.
