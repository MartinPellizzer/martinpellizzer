Hibbertia incompta

Hibbertia incompta, commonly known as the yellow wattle, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in spring and summer. The plant has slender, wiry stems and linear, narrow leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and heathland environments.

Hibbertia incompta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
