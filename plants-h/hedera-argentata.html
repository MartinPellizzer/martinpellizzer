Hedera argentata

Hedera argentata, commonly known as silver ivy, is a species of climbing plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray variegated leaves, which are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, pale green berries that are typically not edible for humans but may be consumed by certain wildlife. It thrives in shaded environments and is often used as a decorative ground cover or trailing plant in gardens.

Hedera argentata is relatively easy to care for, requiring moderate watering and well-draining soil.
