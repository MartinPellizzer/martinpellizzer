Hieracium strictiforme

Hieracium strictiforme, commonly known as the upright hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium strictiforme is often found in temperate climates and can sometimes become invasive in certain regions.
