Hieracium incisum subsp. oligomeres

Hieracium incisum subsp.

oligomeres, commonly known as the narrowleaf hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lanceolate and usually have a serrated or lobed margin, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
