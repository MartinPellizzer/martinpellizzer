Haworthia viscosa f. subobtusa

Haworthia viscosa f. subobtusa, commonly known as the woolly Haworthia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are typically arranged in a rosette shape and can grow to a height of about 10 to 15 centimeters. This plant thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.

It produces small, pale green flowers on long, slender stems during the warmer months.
