Hieracium murorum subsp. ellipsophyllum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

ellipsophyllum, commonly known as the elliptic hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, meadows, and waste areas.

The plant features basal rosettes of hairy, lanceolate leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in late summer. Its leaves are usually elliptic in shape, giving it its subspecies name.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal potential in traditional herbal use.
