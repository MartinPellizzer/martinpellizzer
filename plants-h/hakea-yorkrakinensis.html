Hakea yorkrakinensis

Hakea yorkrakinensis, commonly known as the York River hakea, is an evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish bloom, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions. The plant produces striking, cylindrical flower spikes that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. It is well adapted to sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal dunes.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is a species of interest for conservation efforts in its native habitat.
