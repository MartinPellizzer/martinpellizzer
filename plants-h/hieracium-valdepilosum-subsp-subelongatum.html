Hieracium valdepilosum subsp. subelongatum

Hieracium valdepilosum subsp.

subelongatum, commonly known as the elongated hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems.

The subspecies is distinguished by its elongated leaves and flowering stems. It typically grows in meadows and grasslands, preferring moist and well-drained soils.

The plant is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, forming dense colonies.
