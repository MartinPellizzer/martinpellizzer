Heisteria acuta

Heisteria acuta, commonly known as the sharp heister, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive pointed tip. Its small, clustered flowers are usually white or pale blue, blooming in early spring.

Heisteria acuta is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
