Helichrysum eximium

Helichrysum eximium, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its showy, yellow-orange inflorescences that resemble small suns. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that are typically clustered in dense, spherical heads. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The leaves are usually linear and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
