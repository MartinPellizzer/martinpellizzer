Helianthus dowellianus

Helianthus dowellianus, commonly known as the Dowell's sunflower, is a species of flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly found in Florida and Georgia. The plant is characterized by its large, bright yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk of smaller florets. It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.

Helianthus dowellianus is an herbaceous perennial that thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often used in wetland restoration projects.
