Hieracium canum subsp. melanoxanthum

Hieracium canum subsp.

melanoxanthum, commonly known as the black-yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow to orange composite flowers and hairy stems.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered with fine pubescence, while the plant often grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It is a member of the genus Hieracium, which includes many species of hawkweeds known for their medicinal and ornamental uses.

The subspecies melanoxanthum is distinguished by its darker, more intense flower color compared to other members of the species.
