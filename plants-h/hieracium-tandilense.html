Hieracium tandilense

Hieracium tandilense, commonly known as the Tandil hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in grasslands and disturbed soils, exhibiting a herbaceous growth habit. Its leaves are alternate, hairy, and often have a serrated edge, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium tandilense is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in its native habitat.
