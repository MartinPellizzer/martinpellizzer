Helia gracilis

Helia gracilis, commonly known as the slender helia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated stems and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, contributing to its graceful appearance.

Helia gracilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
