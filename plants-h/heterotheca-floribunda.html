Heterotheca floribunda

Heterotheca floribunda, commonly known as the blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its blue to violet flower heads, which are composed of small, tubular florets surrounded by pale yellow ray flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has hairy stems and leaves that are usually gray-green in color.

Heterotheca floribunda is a member of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal potential in some traditional uses.
