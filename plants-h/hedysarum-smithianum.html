Hedysarum smithianum

Hedysarum smithianum, commonly known as Smith's vetch, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow or orange flowers. The plant has a deep root system that helps it thrive in dry, rocky soils. It is often found in alpine and subalpine environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.

Hedysarum smithianum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
