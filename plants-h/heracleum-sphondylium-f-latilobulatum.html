Heracleum sphondylium f. latilobulatum

Heracleum sphondylium f. latilobulatum, commonly known as the broad-lobed cow parsley, is a botanical variant of the common cow parsley.

It is characterized by its broader, more lobed leaves compared to the typical form, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides.
