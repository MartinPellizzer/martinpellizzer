Hieracium sartorianum var. lucanicum

Hieracium sartorianum var. lucanicum, commonly known as the Lucanian hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow ray flowers and robust, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats such as meadows and roadsides. It has alternate, heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture.

Its botanical features make it identifiable in ecological studies and botanical gardens.
