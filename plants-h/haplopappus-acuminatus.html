Haplopappus acuminatus

Haplopappus acuminatus, commonly known as the slender pappus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and wind-dispersed seeds. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and poor soil conditions. Its small, daisy-like flowers are usually yellow or white, and it produces a pappus, a feathery structure that aids in seed dispersal.

Haplopappus acuminatus is often found in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in regions with low precipitation.
