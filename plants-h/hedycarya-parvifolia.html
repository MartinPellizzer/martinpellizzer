Hedycarya parvifolia

Hedycarya parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved hedycarya, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical in shape and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually white or pale yellow, and are followed by small, spherical fruits.

Hedycarya parvifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and floral display.
