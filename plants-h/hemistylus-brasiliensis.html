Hemistylus brasiliensis

Hemistylus brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit, often found growing on trees in humid forests. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, typically blooming during the rainy season. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrates.

Hemistylus brasiliensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
