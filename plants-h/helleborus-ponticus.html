Helleborus ponticus

Helleborus ponticus, commonly known as the Caucasian hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features dark green, waxy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces clusters of dark purple to black flowers with prominent white markings, which bloom in late winter or early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Helleborus ponticus is known for its toxicity, containing various alkaloids that can be harmful if ingested.
