Hoya kwangsiensis

Hoya kwangsiensis, commonly known as the Guangxi Wisteria, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its waxy, showy flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has thick, fleshy stems and dark green, heart-shaped leaves. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

Hoya kwangsiensis is also known for its slow growth and preference for well-draining soil.
