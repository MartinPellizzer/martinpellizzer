Hieracium albopellitum

Hieracium albopellitum, commonly known as the white-haired hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, producing small seeds that are dispersed by the wind.
