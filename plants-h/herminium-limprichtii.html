Herminium limprichtii

Herminium limprichtii, commonly known as the Limpricht's orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. Its flowers are often clustered at the top of the stem and have a subtle fragrance.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Herminium limprichtii is considered a sensitive indicator species for ecological health.
