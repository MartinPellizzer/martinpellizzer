Helleborus fumarioides

Helleborus fumarioides, commonly known as the white hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It features dark green, leathery leaves that are typically oval to rhombic in shape. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive spurred shape. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
