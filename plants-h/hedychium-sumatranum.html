Hedychium sumatranum

Hedychium sumatranum, commonly known as the white ginger lily, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, elegant stems that can reach up to 3 meters in height, adorned with large, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces clusters of long, narrow leaves and has a rhizomatous root system that helps it thrive in moist, shaded environments. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Hedychium sumatranum prefers well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners in tropical and subtropical regions.
