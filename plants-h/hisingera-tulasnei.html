Hisingera tulasnei

Hisingera tulasnei, commonly known as the narrow-leafed horkelia, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of delicate, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. Hisingera tulasnei typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its low-growing, hardy nature.
