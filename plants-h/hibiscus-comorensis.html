Hibiscus comorensis

Hibiscus comorensis, commonly known as the Comoros hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Comoros Islands in the Indian Ocean.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring broad, heart-shaped leaves with a smooth texture. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are usually pink to purple in color, with five petals and a prominent central stigma. Hibiscus comorensis is known for its ability to thrive in tropical climates and well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in its native region.
