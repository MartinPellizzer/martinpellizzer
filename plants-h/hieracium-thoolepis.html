Hieracium thoolepis

Hieracium thoolepis, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. The plant has basal rosettes of narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in late summer. Its inflorescence consists of a single flower per stem, which is surrounded by several bracts.

Hieracium thoolepis is known for its adaptability to harsh environments and its role in stabilizing soil in high-altitude ecosystems.
