Hibiscus diversifolius var. partitus

Hibiscus diversifolius var. partitus, commonly known as the variegated wood hibiscus, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves that exhibit a variegated pattern of green, white, and sometimes red. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming from summer to fall. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

This ornamental plant is valued for its unique foliage and vibrant floral display in gardens and landscapes.
