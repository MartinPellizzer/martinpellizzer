Hydatica cuneifolia

Hydatica cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hydatica, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to withstand salt spray and drought conditions.

Hydatica cuneifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
