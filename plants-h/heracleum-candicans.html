Heracleum candicans

Heracleum candicans, commonly known as cow parsnip, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbrella-shaped flower clusters and aromatic foliage. The plant can grow up to 2 meters tall, with large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are white to greenish-yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The roots are thick and tuberous, often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
