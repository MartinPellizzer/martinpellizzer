Hieracium horotrophum

Hieracium horotrophum, commonly known as the creeping hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, along roadsides, and in disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Hieracium horotrophum is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a common weed in many regions.
