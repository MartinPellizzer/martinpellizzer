Hieracium circumvietum

Hieracium circumvietum, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and typical sunflower-like appearance. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it thrives in well-drained soils and cooler climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in dense clusters.

Hieracium circumvietum is known for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions, making it a common sight in high-altitude ecosystems.
