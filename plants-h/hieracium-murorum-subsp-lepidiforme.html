Hieracium murorum subsp. lepidiforme

Hieracium murorum subsp.

lepidiforme, commonly known as the smooth-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smooth, lance-shaped leaves and yellow disc flowers surrounded by pale yellow to white ray florets. It typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats such as meadows and roadsides.

This plant is often found in temperate regions and is relatively uncommon in the wild.
