Hieracium × moechiadium

Hieracium × moechiadium, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a hybrid plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which typically bloom from late spring to early autumn. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks, often forming dense colonies.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a range of morphological variations, making it a common and widespread species in temperate regions.
