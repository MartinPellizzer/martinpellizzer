Helianthemum rhodanthum

Helianthemum rhodanthum, commonly known as the pink rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its small, fleshy leaves and showy, pink to rose-colored flowers. The plant thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to arid environments. Its flowers typically bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping.
