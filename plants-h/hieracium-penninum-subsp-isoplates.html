Hieracium penninum subsp. isoplates

Hieracium penninum subsp.

isoplates, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and rocky or gravelly substrates.

The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow flowers in late summer. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a hairy texture, while the stems are erect and hairy as well.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal potential in traditional herbal practices.
