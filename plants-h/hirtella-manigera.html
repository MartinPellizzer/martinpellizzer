Hirtella manigera

Hirtella manigera, commonly known as the hairy hirtella, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other notable plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink and are clustered in small inflorescences.

Hirtella manigera is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
