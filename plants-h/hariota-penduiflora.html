Hariota penduiflora

Hariota penduiflora, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Hariota penduiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
