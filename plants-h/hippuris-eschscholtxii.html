Hippuris eschscholtxii

Hippuris eschscholtxii, commonly known as the water pennywort, is a semi-aquatic plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in shallow water or damp soil, often found in streams, ponds, and wetlands. The plant has small, opposite leaves that are oval to lance-shaped, with a waxy surface that helps it float. It produces tiny, greenish flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it is often used in water gardens for its ornamental value.
