Hopea anomala

Hopea anomala, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, heavy timber that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a broad, umbrella-shaped crown. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, with a glossy green upper surface and a pale underside.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing winged seeds.
