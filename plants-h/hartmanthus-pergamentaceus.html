Hartmanthus pergamentaceus

Hartmanthus pergamentaceus, commonly known as the paperleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, papery leaves that are often covered in a fine, white waxy coating. The plant produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by fluffy seed heads that resemble those of a dandelion. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is considered a valuable plant for ecological studies and conservation efforts.
