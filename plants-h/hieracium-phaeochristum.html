Hieracium phaeochristum

Hieracium phaeochristum, commonly known as the golden hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features upright, hairy stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets, and it blooms from late spring to early autumn.

Hieracium phaeochristum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
