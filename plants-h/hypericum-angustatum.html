Hypericum angustatum

Hypericum angustatum, commonly known as narrowleaf St. John's wort, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in open woodlands. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, with a slightly hairy texture.

Hypericum angustatum is part of the genus Hypericum, which includes many species known for their medicinal and ornamental value.
