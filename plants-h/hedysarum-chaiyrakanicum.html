Hedysarum chaiyrakanicum

Hedysarum chaiyrakanicum, commonly known as the Karakum milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant produces small, lens-shaped pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in steppes and rocky soils.

Hedysarum chaiyrakanicum plays a role in soil stabilization and is part of the broader Hedysarum genus, which includes several species valued for their ecological and agricultural significance.
