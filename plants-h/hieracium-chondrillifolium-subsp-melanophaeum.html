Hieracium chondrillifolium subsp. melanophaeum

Hieracium chondrillifolium subsp.

melanophaeum, commonly known as the black-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded conditions. Its leaves are dark green and often have a rough, hairy texture, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a composite head.

It is noted for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its potential use in traditional herbal medicine.
