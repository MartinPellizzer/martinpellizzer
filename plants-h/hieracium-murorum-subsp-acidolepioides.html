Hieracium murorum subsp. acidolepioides

Hieracium murorum subsp.

acidolepioides, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering heads that produce yellow disc florets.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
