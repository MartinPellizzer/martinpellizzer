Helictotrichon desertorum var. centroasiaticum

Helictotrichon desertorum var. centroasiaticum, commonly known as the Central Asian feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.

Its ecological role includes soil stabilization and providing forage for grazing animals in its native habitat.
