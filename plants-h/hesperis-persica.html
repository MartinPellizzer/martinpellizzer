Hesperis persica

Hesperis persica, commonly known as the Persian sweet rocket, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to regions of Central Asia and the Middle East, including parts of Iran and Turkey. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Hesperis persica is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and for its historical use in traditional medicine.
