Helianthemum rubellum f. eglandulosum

Helianthemum rubellum f. eglandulosum, commonly known as the glandless rock rose, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features dense clusters of small, bright orange-red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are typically covered in a fine, woolly pubescence. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and full sun, making it well-suited for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and vibrant flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
