Hieracium pseudepimedium

Hieracium pseudepimedium, commonly known as the false mouse-ear hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

Its leaves and flowers are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance.
