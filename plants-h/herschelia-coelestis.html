Herschelia coelestis

Herschelia coelestis, commonly known as the sky-blue star, is a striking flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, star-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions with well-drained soil. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

The flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer, range in color from pale blue to deep violet, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
