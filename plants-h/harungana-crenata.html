Harungana crenata

Harungana crenata, commonly known as the African snowball tree, is a flowering plant native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows up to 8 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of white flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy coating, while its bark is smooth and grayish in color.

Harungana crenata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
