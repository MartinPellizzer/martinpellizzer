Heliconia choconiana

Heliconia choconiana, commonly known as the red heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble large, fleshy flowers. The plant produces long, narrow inflorescences with bright red or orange bracts that enclose its small, true flowers. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.

The leaves are large, elongated, and often have a waxy texture, contributing to its lush appearance.
