Hyptis woodii

Hyptis woodii, commonly known as the Mexican mint marigold, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, fragrant leaves that emit a mint-like scent. Its flowers are typically pale blue to purple and are clustered in dense spikes.

Hyptis woodii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
