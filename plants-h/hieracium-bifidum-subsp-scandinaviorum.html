Hieracium bifidum subsp. scandinaviorum

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

scandinaviorum, commonly known as the Scandinavian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern Europe. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, pastures, and woodland edges.

The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in a single head at the end of each stem. Its flowers are surrounded by a series of bracts, and the plant is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.
