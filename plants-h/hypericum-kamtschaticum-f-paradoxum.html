Hypericum kamtschaticum f. paradoxum

Hypericum kamtschaticum f. paradoxum, commonly known as the paradoxical St. John's wort, is a rare and unique variant of the St. John's wort genus.

It is characterized by its distinctive floral morphology, including elongated, tubular flowers that differ from the typical yellow flowers of the species. The plant is native to the Caucasus region and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. It is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats, where it adapts to harsh environmental conditions.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
