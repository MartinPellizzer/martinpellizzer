Hieracium maciatum

Hieracium maciatum, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf structures. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, preferring moist to moderately dry soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a hairy surface, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Hieracium maciatum is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common but sometimes invasive species in certain regions.
