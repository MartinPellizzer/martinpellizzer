Hieracium diaphanoides subsp. vorarlbergense

Hieracium diaphanoides subsp.

vorarlbergense, commonly known as the Vorarlberg hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers and hairy, basal rosette leaves.

The plant thrives in rocky and open habitats at high altitudes, often found in the Swiss Alps and adjacent regions. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in late summer.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endemic species in certain areas.
