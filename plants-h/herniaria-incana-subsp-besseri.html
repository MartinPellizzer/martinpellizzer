Herniaria incana subsp. besseri

Herniaria incana subsp.

besseri, commonly known as the coastal horned poppy, is a low-growing perennial plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean. It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green leaves and distinctive, small, pinkish-white flowers.

The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to salt spray and strong coastal winds. It is often found in dunes and rocky shores, where it plays a role in stabilizing the landscape.

Its unique morphology and ecological niche make it an important component of coastal flora.
