Hibiscus margeriae

Hibiscus margeriae, commonly known as the Mexican hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers. The flowers are usually bright red or pink, with a prominent yellow center, and bloom during the summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

The plant is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
