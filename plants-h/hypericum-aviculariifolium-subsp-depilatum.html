Hypericum aviculariifolium subsp. depilatum

Hypericum aviculariifolium subsp.

depilatum, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, woody stems that are often covered with a fine, smooth layer of hair.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and pointed, with a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.
