Helichrysum persicum

Helichrysum persicum, commonly known as Persian immortelle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers that retain their color when dried. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is usually gray-green with a silvery appearance, and it produces small, narrow leaves.

Helichrysun persicum is often used in dried floral arrangements due to its long-lasting, vibrant color.
