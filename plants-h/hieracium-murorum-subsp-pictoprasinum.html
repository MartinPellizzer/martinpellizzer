Hieracium murorum subsp. pictoprasinum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

pictoprasinum, commonly known as the painted green hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and ragworts, and is characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides, and can reach heights of up to 30 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a greenish tinge, giving it its common name.

It is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and is relatively common in its native range.
