Heterocoma pedunculata

Heterocoma pedunculata, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble dandelions. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and high temperatures. Its leaves are succulent and fleshy, storing water to survive in harsh conditions.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
