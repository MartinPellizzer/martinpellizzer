Helichrysum stilpnocephalum

Helichrysum stilpnocephalum, commonly known as the golden everlasting or Cape goldflame, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its showy, golden-yellow inflorescences that resemble small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically arranged in a flat-topped or rounded cluster. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.

The leaves are usually narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
