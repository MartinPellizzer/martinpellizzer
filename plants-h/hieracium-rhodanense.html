Hieracium rhodanense

Hieracium rhodanense, commonly known as the Rhodanese hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils. It features upright stems with basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

The showy yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
