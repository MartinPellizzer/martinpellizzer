Hypericum kemaliyense

Hypericum kemaliyense, commonly known as the Kashmir yellow hypericum, is a shrub native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in summer and are typically arranged in clusters. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
