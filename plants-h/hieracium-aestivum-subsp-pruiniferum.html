Hieracium aestivum subsp. pruiniferum

Hieracium aestivum subsp.

pruiniferum, commonly known as the summer hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and often covered with a waxy coating, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
