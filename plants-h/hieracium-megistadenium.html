Hieracium megistadenium

Hieracium megistadenium, commonly known as the giant hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It has a rosette of basal leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its stems are hairy as well.

The flowers are yellow, and the plant is known for its ability to spread rapidly through both seeds and rhizomes.
