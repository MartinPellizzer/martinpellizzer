Hepatica angulosa var. parviflora

Hepatica angulosa var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered hepatica, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, woodsy areas and is characterized by its distinctive, waxy blue or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often mottled with white or green patterns. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is known for its early blooming habit, making it a symbol of spring in its native habitat.

The varietal name 'parviflora' refers to its smaller flowers compared to other hepatica species.
