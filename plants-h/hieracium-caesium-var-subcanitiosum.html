Hieracium caesium var. subcanitiosum

Hieracium caesium var. subcanitiosum, commonly known as the white hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its flowers are yellow, with a distinctive white margin, and it produces numerous small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

This variant is distinguished by its finely hairy stems and leaves, which give it a silvery appearance.
