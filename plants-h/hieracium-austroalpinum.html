Hieracium austroalpinum

Hieracium austroalpinum, commonly known as the southern alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

Hieracium austroalpinum is often found in meadows and grasslands at high altitudes.
