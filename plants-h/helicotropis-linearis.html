Helicotropis linearis

Helicotropis linearis, commonly known as the slender heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits called follicles, which contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Helicotropis linearis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
