Huthia coerulea

Huthia coerulea, commonly known as the blue huthia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue flowers, which are typically arranged in dense clusters. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Huthia coerulea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
