Hieracium paltinae subsp. paltinae

Hieracium paltinae subsp.

paltinae, commonly known as the Paltinae hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in grasslands and meadows.

The plant has basal and stem leaves that are generally lance-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf morphology.
