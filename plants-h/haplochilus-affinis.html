Haplochilus affinis

Haplochilus affinis, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations such as hairy leaves to reduce water loss. The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
