Hieracium sylvaticum subsp. submarginellum

Hieracium sylvaticum subsp.

submarginellum, commonly known as the woodland hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems.

The subspecies is distinguished by its finely toothed leaves and a distinctive yellow-orange flower head. It typically grows in moist, shady woodlands and thrives in nutrient-rich soils.

This plant plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in undisturbed natural habitats.
