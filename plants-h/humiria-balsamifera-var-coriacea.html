Humiria balsamifera var. coriacea

Humiria balsamifera var. coriacea, commonly known as the rough-hair balsam, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, coriaceous (leathery) leaves that are often glandular and emit a strong, aromatic odor. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique texture and fragrance make it a distinctive species within the Myrtaceae family.
