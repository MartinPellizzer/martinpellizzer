Hackelia patens var. harrisonii

Hackelia patens var. harrisonii, commonly known as the Harrison's blue curl.

It is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States, characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, open habitats. It has slender, branching stems with opposite leaves that are usually hairy.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
