Hieracium pulliceps

Hieracium pulliceps, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in meadows and grassy areas. It has a creeping growth habit and produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in some agricultural settings.
