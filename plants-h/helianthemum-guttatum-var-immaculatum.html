Helianthemum guttatum var. immaculatum

Helianthemum guttatum var. immaculatum, commonly known as the white rockrose, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features dense clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate floral display to its compact form. The plant has gray-green, fuzzy leaves that are often covered in fine white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, sunny conditions and is often found in rocky or limestone soils.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate coastal environments.
