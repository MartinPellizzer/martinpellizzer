Hedysarum gmelinii subsp. setigerum

Hedysarum gmelinii subsp.

setigerum, commonly known as the Mongolian milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and trifoliate leaves with small, rounded leaflets.

The plant produces yellow to orange flowers in racemes, which are typically borne on the upper parts of the stems. It has small, hairy pods that contain several seeds.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in steppes and grasslands.
