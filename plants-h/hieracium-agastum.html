Hieracium agastum

Hieracium agastum, commonly known as the violet hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and ragworts, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded conditions. Its flowers are usually yellow to brownish in color, and the leaves are broadly lance-shaped with a rough texture.

Hieracium agastum is often found in temperate regions and can sometimes be considered a weed in agricultural settings.
