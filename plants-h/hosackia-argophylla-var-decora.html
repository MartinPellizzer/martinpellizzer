Hosackia argophylla var. decora

Hosackia argophylla var. decora, commonly known as the showy milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking purple to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces elongated seed pods.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and native plant gardens.
