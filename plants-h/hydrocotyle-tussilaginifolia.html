Hydrocotyle tussilaginifolia

Hydrocotyle tussilaginifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf tussilaginifolia, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands and marshes of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white, waxy hair, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in shallow water or along the edges of ponds.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a popular subject for botanists and gardeners interested in wetland flora.
