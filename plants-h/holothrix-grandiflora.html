Holothrix grandiflora

Holothrix grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora holothrix, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, oval leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Holothrix grandiflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
