Hieracium glaucinum subsp. runcinatolobatum

Hieracium glaucinum subsp.

runcinatolobatum, commonly known as the blue hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, blue-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
