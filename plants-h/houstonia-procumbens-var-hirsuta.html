Houstonia procumbens var. hirsuta

Houstonia procumbens var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy blue-eyed grass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, creeping stems that produce small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered with fine hairs, giving the plant its "hirsuta" designation. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is typically found in woodlands and wetlands.

The plant's delicate appearance and unique floral structure make it a notable species in its native habitat.
