Hieracium sparsum subsp. nigrovirenticeps

Hieracium sparsum subsp.

nigrovirenticeps, commonly known as the green-veined hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a distinctive greenish-yellow venation, and the flowers are yellow with a central disc.

It is often found in temperate climates and is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens.
