Helichrysum luzulifolium subsp. luzulifolium

Helichrysum luzulifolium subsp.

luzulifolium, commonly known as the purple everlasting or mountain everlasting, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit and dense clusters of small, vibrant purple to pink flowers.

The leaves are typically narrow, linear, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
