Homalium cochinchinense var. pseudopaniculatum

Homalium cochinchinense var. pseudopaniculatum, commonly known as the panicle-flowered huito, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically odd-pinnate with elliptical to lanceolate leaflets. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers arranged in panicles, which bloom during the wet season. Its fruit is a drupe, usually red or orange when mature, containing a single seed.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
