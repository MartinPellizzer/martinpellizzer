Hakea candolleana var. campylorrhyncha

Hakea candolleana var. campylorrhyncha, commonly known as the narrow-lobed hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features narrow, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. The plant produces spikes of small, yellowish-green flowers, which are followed by distinctive, woody, urn-shaped seed pods. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
