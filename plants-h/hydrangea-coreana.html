Hydrangea coreana

Hydrangea coreana, commonly known as the Korean hydrangea, is a flowering shrub native to Korea.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from blue to pink, depending on soil pH. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features broad, serrated leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Hydrangea coreana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its seasonal blooms.
