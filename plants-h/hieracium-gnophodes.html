Hieracium gnophodes

Hieracium gnophodes, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellow to orange composite flowers and is native to Europe, including parts of the United Kingdom. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. It has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

Hieracium gnophodes is often mistaken for other hawkweed species due to its similar appearance.
