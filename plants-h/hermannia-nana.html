Hermannia nana

Hermannia nana, commonly known as dwarf hermannia, is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows to a height of less than 10 centimeters, with compact, rosette-like foliage. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are usually pink or white, often clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Hermannia nana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and subtle floral display.
