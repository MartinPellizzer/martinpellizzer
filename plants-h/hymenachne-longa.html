Hymenachne longa

Hymenachne longa, commonly known as the long-leaved hymenachne, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and dense, clump-forming growth habit. The plant typically thrives in wetland or marshy environments, where it forms extensive mats. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are loosely arranged, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Hymenachne longa plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
