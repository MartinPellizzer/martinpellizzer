Hieracium miarellum

Hieracium miarellum, commonly known as the showy hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, preferring temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and somewhat hairy.

Hieracium miarellum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
