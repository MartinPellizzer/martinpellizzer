Haloragis serpyllifolia

Haloragis serpyllifolia, commonly known as common sandspurry, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It has small, fleshy, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during late spring to early summer. The plant typically thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in dry, sandy or gravelly habitats. Its leaves are alternate, narrow, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are prostrate and often root at the nodes.

Haloragis serpyllifolia is part of the Haloragaceae family, which includes other species with similar adaptations to arid environments.
