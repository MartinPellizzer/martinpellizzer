Hydrangea hortensia var. azisai

Hydrangea hortensia var. azisai, commonly known as the Japanese hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can range in color from pink to blue, depending on soil pH. The plant produces sterile flowers, with the showy blooms consisting of many small, white petals. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its foliage turns vibrant shades of red or orange in the fall, adding seasonal interest to the landscape.
