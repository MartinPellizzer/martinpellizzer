Hyptis macrocephala var. villosa

Hyptis macrocephala var. villosa, commonly known as hairy hyptis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often grayish-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers, which are arranged in dense, terminal inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded areas within tropical forests.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
