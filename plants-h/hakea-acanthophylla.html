Hakea acanthophylla

Hakea acanthophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hakea, is a shrub native to southwestern Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, woody structure and linear, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces cylindrical, yellowish flowers that are typically arranged in clusters, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. Hakea acanthophylla is adapted to arid conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Its ecological significance includes providing habitat and food for local wildlife.
