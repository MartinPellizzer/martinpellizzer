Hallia cordata

Hallia cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in late spring, which are clustered in terminal racemes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive and relatively easy-to-grow plant for gardeners interested in native flora.
