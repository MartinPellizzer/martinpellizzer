Hibiscus tricuspis

Hibiscus tricuspis, commonly known as the three-lobed hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, or white. The leaves are heart-shaped with three distinct lobes, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Hibiscus tricuspis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
