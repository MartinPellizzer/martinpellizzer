Hieracium gustavianum

Hieracium gustavianum, commonly known as the Swedish hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and is native to Europe, particularly Scandinavia. The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, preferring moist, well-drained soils. It has hairy stems and leaves with a rough texture, and its flowers are arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

Hieracium gustavianum is often considered a weed in agricultural areas due to its rapid growth and ability to spread easily.
