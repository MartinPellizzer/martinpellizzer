Homalomena aromatica

Homalomena aromatica, commonly known as the sweet flag or fragrant arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves and vibrant, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense clumps in moist, shady environments.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
