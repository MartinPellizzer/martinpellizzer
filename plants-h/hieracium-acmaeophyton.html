Hieracium acmaeophyton

Hieracium acmaeophyton, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and a flowering stem that bears yellow disk flowers surrounded by ray florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring moist to moderately dry conditions. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it can be found in various habitats across these regions.

Hieracium acmaeophyton is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its aggressive growth habit.
