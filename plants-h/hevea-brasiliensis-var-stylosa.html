Hevea brasiliensis var. stylosa

Hevea brasiliensis var. stylosa, commonly known as the stylosa rubber tree, is a variant of the rubber tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender leaves and a unique growth habit that differs from the more common varietal forms. This plant is known for its latex-producing capabilities, which are essential for natural rubber production. The stylosa variant is often cultivated for its economic value in rubber harvesting.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and industrial contexts.
