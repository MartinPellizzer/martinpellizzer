Henslowia reinwardtiana

Henslowia reinwardtiana, commonly known as the slender glassworts, is a member of the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a halophyte, meaning it thrives in saline or brackish environments, often found in coastal areas and salt marshes. The plant has succulent, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. Its small, scale-like leaves are typically clustered at the base, and it produces tiny, greenish flowers in dense clusters.

Henslowia reinwardtiana plays an important role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various small organisms.
