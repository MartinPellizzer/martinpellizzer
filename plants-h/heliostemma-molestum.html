Heliostemma molestum

Heliostemma molestum, commonly known as the hairy heliostemmatum, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Malvaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
