Helianthus speciosus

Helianthus speciosus, commonly known as the showy sunflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy yellow-orange flower heads. The plant produces both ray and disk flowers, with the outer rays resembling petals. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and ecological restoration projects.
