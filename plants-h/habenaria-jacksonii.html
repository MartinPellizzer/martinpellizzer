Habenaria jacksonii

Habenaria jacksonii, commonly known as Jackson's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of several showy, spurred flowers that are often pink or purple in color.

The species is noted for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
