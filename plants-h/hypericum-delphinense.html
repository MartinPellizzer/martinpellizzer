Hypericum delphinense

Hypericum delphinense, commonly known as blue hypericum, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its attractive blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Hypericum delphinense is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
