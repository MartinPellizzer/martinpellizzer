Haageocereus pseudomelanostele var. clavatus

Haageocereus pseudomelanostele var. clavatus, commonly known as the Clavate Hedgehog Cactus, is a unique cactus species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in dense, white spines that provide protection from herbivores and intense sunlight. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are pollinated by various insects. Its stems are segmented and can store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments.

This variety is distinguished by its thick, club-shaped spines, giving it a distinctive appearance among other cacti.
