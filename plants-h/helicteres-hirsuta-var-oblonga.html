Helicteres hirsuta var. oblonga

Helicteres hirsuta var. oblonga, commonly known as the hairy helicteres, is a variant of the helicteres hirsuta species.

It is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas, particularly in areas with moist, warm climates. The plant is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruits are covered in fine hairs and contain several seeds.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
