Heteropogon messanensis

Heteropogon messanensis, commonly known as the Messanense grass, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit with slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, two-lobed florets. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slight sheen, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
