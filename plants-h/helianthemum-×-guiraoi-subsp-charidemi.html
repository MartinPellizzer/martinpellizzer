Helianthemum × guiraoi subsp. charidemi

Helianthemum × guiraoi subsp.

charidemi, commonly known as the Alboraya rock rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are small, gray-green, and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its hardiness and attractive appearance.
