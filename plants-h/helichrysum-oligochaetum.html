Helichrysum oligochaetum

Helichrysum oligochaetum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that are often clustered in dense heads, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its ornamental value.

Helichrysum oligochaetum is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
