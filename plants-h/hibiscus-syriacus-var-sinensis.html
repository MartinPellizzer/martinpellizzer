Hibiscus syriacus var. sinensis

Hibiscus syriacus var. sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese rose or red hibiscus, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its showy, single-layered flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a prominent yellow or orange center. The plant has palmate leaves that are typically 5-9 lobes, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its blooming period occurs in summer, adding vibrant color to landscapes.
