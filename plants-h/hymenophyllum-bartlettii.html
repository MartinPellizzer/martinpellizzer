Hymenophyllum bartlettii

Hymenophyllum bartlettii, commonly known as the Bartlett's spore moss, is a delicate, epiphytic fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, bright green leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of tiny, hair-like structures called trichomes. This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found on the bark of trees in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the leaves and produce spores for propagation.

Hymenophyllum bartlettii is notable for its intricate morphology and is often studied for its ecological role in forest ecosystems.
