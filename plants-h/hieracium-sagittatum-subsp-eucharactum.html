Hieracium sagittatum subsp. eucharactum

Hieracium sagittatum subsp.

eucharactum, commonly known as the showy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It features upright, hairy stems that bear alternate, lance-shaped leaves with toothed margins.

The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with a distinctive, elongated shape, typical of the genus Hieracium. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and riverbanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and botanical gardens.
