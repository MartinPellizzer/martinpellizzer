Hordeum vulgare var. mandshuricum

Hordeum vulgare var. mandshuricum, commonly known as Siberian barley, is a perennial grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical inflorescence and awned lemmas. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments and is adapted to cold climates. Its seeds are small and often used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

It plays a role in ecological systems by providing forage for wildlife and stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
