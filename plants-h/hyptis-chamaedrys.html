Hyptis chamaedrys

Hyptis chamaedrys, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a perennial herb native to the warm regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and ornamental uses. The plant features square stems and opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its small, tubular flowers are typically blue or purple, arranged in dense clusters that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Hyptis chamaedrys is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported calming and anti-inflammatory effects.
