Hibiscus rhodopetalus f. typica

Hibiscus rhodopetalus f. typica, commonly known as the Hawaiian hibiscus, is a tropical flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range from white to pink or red, with five broad, waxy petals. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight. Hibiscus rhodopetalus f. typica is a member of the Malvaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

It is also culturally significant in Hawaiian traditions and ecology.
