Hieracium brevipes

Hieracium brevipes, commonly known as the short-footed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typically hairy stems. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, and its leaves are usually lance-shaped with a rough texture. Hieracium brevipes is often found in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is considered a common weed in some areas but also holds ecological value for pollinators.
