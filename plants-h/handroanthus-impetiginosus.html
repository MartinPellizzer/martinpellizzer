Handroanthus impetiginosus

Handroanthus impetiginosus, commonly known as the yellow trumpet tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters at the ends of its branches. The plant has a woody stem and produces small, green, fleshy fruits that contain seeds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
