Hieracium bupleuroides subsp. pseudotatrae

Hieracium bupleuroides subsp.

pseudotatrae, commonly known as the Tatra hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its clustered growth habit and yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine meadows, where it thrives in well-drained, nutrient-poor soils. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while its stem is erect and often branched.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological significance in high-altitude ecosystems.
