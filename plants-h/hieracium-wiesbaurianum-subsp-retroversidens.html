Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp. retroversidens

Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp.

retroversidens, commonly known as the reverse-leaf hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and flowering heads.

The leaves are typically lanceolate and often have a hairy surface, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a composite head. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique leaf orientation, which is reversed compared to other members of the species.
