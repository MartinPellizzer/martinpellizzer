Halimium ocymoides

Halimium ocymoides, commonly known as the yellow sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are fleshy and succulent, adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils.

Halimium ocymoides is often found in rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
