Helianthus cultus

Helianthus cultus, commonly known as the prairie sunflower, is a North American wildflower native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual plant that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring a sturdy stem and large, yellow, daisy-like flowers. The plant has rough, hairy leaves that are lance-shaped and often have a distinct, wavy margin. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Helianthus cultus is well adapted to prairie and open woodland habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.
