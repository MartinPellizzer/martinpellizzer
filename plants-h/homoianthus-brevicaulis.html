Homoianthus brevicaulis

Homoianthus brevicaulis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cycad, is a unique plant belonging to the Cycadaceae family.

It is characterized by its short, stout trunk and a crown of feathery, narrow leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. Native to parts of South America, particularly Brazil, it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant produces cones rather than flowers, a feature typical of cycads, and has a slow growth rate.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
