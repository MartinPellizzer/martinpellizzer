Hieracium lividum

Hieracium lividum, commonly known as the bluish hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and ragworts, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium lividum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting pollinators.
