Hedysarum varium subsp. leucocladum

Hedysarum varium subsp.

leucocladum, commonly known as the white-headed vetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is a perennial herb with slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves.

The flowers are typically pink to purple, with a distinctive white keel, and appear in racemes. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.
