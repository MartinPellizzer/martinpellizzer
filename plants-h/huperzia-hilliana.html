Huperzia hilliana

Huperzia hilliana, commonly known as the hill's huperzia, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its simple, linear leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its sporophytes produce spores on the underside of the leaves, which are dispersed by wind.

Huperzia hilliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
