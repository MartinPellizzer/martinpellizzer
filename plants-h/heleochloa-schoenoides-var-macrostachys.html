Heleochloa schoenoides var. macrostachys

Heleochloa schoenoides var. macrostachys, commonly known as the giant hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, grass-like foliage that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces fluffy, feathery inflorescences that are often used in floral arrangements. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water gardens and landscape design.
