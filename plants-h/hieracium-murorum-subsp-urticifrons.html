Hieracium murorum subsp. urticifrons

Hieracium murorum subsp.

urticifrons, commonly known as the stinging hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant has stinging hairs on its stems and leaves, which can cause irritation upon contact. It typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats such as meadows and roadsides.

Its subspecific classification highlights its distinct morphological and ecological traits compared to other Hieracium species.
