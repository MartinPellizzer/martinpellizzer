Hieracium pseudauriculiforme

Hieracium pseudauriculiforme, commonly known as the false mouse ear or yellow hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow daisy-like flowers with ray florets and a central disk, blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in disturbed soils and gardens.
