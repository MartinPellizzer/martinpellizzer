Hicorius × laneyi var. chateaugayensis

Hicorius × laneyi var. chateaugayensis, commonly known as the Chateaugay hibiscus, is a hybrid plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in wetlands.
