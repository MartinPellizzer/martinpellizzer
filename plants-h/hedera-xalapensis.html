Hedera xalapensis

Hedera xalapensis, commonly known as the Mexican ivy, is a climbing evergreen vine native to Mexico.

It features dark green, glossy leaves that are typically heart-shaped or ovate in form. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by orange-red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often used in landscaping for its dense, trailing growth habit.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor ornamental purposes.
