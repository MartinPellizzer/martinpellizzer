Helichrysum barbellatum

Helichrysum barbellatum, commonly known as the golden crown, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in spring. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its name derives from the Greek words "helios" meaning sun and "chryso" meaning gold, reflecting its sunny appearance.
