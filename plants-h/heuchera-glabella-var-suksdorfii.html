Heuchera glabella var. suksdorfii

Heuchera glabella var. suksdorfii, commonly known as the Suksdorf's coral bells, is a perennial plant native to the Appalachian Mountains of the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking foliage, which ranges from deep purple to bronze and red hues. The plant produces upright, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white during the summer months. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
