Hyssopus catariifolius

Hyssopus catariifolius, commonly known as the Sierra hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or alpine soils. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually violet or blue in color, arranged in spikes.

It is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
