Hieracium taurinense subsp. lenitum

Hieracium taurinense subsp.

lenitum, commonly known as the soft hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and rocky areas, preferring temperate climates. It has a hairy stem and basal leaves that are often lobed or toothed.

This subspecies is distinguished by its softer, more pubescent foliage compared to other members of the species.
