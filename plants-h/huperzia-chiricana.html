Huperzia chiricana

Huperzia chiricana, commonly known as the mountain huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its simple, linear leaves that are often clustered at the base of the plant. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. It has a rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Huperzia chiricana is notable for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and its role in maintaining ecological balance in its habitat.
