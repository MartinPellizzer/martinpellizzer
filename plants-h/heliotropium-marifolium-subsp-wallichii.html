Heliotropium marifolium subsp. wallichii

Heliotropium marifolium subsp.

wallichii, commonly known as the Himalayan heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas. It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple, often resembling those of the common heliotrope.

The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine regions, thriving in rocky and well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually hairy and alternate, and the stems are often prostrate or ascending.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
