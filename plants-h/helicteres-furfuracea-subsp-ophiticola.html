Helicteres furfuracea subsp. ophiticola

Helicteres furfuracea subsp.

ophiticola, commonly known as the hairy helicteres, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Asia. It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins and small, clustered yellow flowers.

The plant produces smooth, elongated fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
