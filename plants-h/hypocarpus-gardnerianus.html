Hypocarpus gardnerianus

Hypocarpus gardnerianus, commonly known as the Gardner's hypocraterium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features a tall, leafy stem with large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach several feet in length. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe, typical of the Araceae family.

Hypocarpus gardnerianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
