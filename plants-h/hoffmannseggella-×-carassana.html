Hoffmannseggella × carassana

Hoffmannseggella × carassana, commonly known as the hybrid hawthorn, is a naturally occurring hybrid between *Crataegus monogyna* and *Crataegus laevigata*.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically edible but often have a sour taste. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

The botanical features of Hoffmannseggella × carassana make it an important species in ecological studies and horticulture.
