Heliconia wagneriana

Heliconia wagneriana, commonly known as the Wagner's heliconia, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its large, brightly colored bracts that resemble flowers. The plant has long, strap-like leaves and produces clusters of small, clustered flowers inside its inflorescence. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
