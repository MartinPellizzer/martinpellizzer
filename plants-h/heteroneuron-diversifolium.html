Heteroneuron diversifolium

Heteroneuron diversifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved heteroneuron, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, broad, lance-shaped leaves that vary in size and shape across different individuals. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its unique texture and appearance.

Heteroneuron diversifolium thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or riparian habitats.
