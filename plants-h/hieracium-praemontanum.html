Hieracium praemontanum

Hieracium praemontanum, commonly known as the mountain hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Hieracium praemontanum is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.
