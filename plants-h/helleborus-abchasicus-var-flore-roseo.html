Helleborus abchasicus var. flore-roseo

Helleborus abchasicus var. flore-roseo, commonly known as the Abchazian helleborus, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It features dark green, velvety leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces striking, rose-pink flowers with a nodding habit, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is adapted to cool, temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
