Heuchera suksdorfii

Heuchera suksdorfii, commonly known as the Saksdorf's coral bell, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It features dense, dark green foliage with a velvety texture, and its flowers are small, bell-shaped, and typically pink or red. The plant thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens. It is valued for its ornamental leaves and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Heuchera suksdorfii is relatively low maintenance and resistant to pests and diseases.
