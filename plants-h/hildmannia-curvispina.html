Hildmannia curvispina

Hildmannia curvispina, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hildmannia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tubular, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially sunny habitats.

Hildmannia curvispina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
