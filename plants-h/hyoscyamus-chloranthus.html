Hyoscyamus chloranthus

Hyoscyamus chloranthus, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped. It produces small, berry-like fruits that contain toxic seeds, making it a plant to handle with care.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though its medicinal properties are limited.
