Hieracium djimilense subsp. galatinum

Hieracium djimilense subsp.

galatinum, commonly known as the Galatinum hawkweed, is a flowering plant species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow disc florets and white ray florets.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, preferring well-drained soils. It has basal and stem leaves that are usually hairy and have a lobed or toothed margin.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
