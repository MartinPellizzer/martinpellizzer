Hesperis crucigera

Hesperis crucigera, commonly known as the sweet rocket, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants. The plant features showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in racemes. Its leaves are pinnately divided, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

Hesperis crucigera is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed habitats.
