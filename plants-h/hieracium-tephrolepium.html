Hieracium tephrolepium

Hieracium tephrolepium, commonly known as the white hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and ragworts, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are yellow with white ray florets, and it produces small, oval-shaped seeds.

It is often found in disturbed habitats and is considered a weed in some agricultural areas.
