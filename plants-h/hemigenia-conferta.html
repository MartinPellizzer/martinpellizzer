Hemigenia conferta

Hemigenia conferta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved honey mint, is a perennial herb native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Hemigenia conferta is valued for its fragrant foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
