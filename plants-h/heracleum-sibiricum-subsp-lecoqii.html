Heracleum sibiricum subsp. lecoqii

Heracleum sibiricum subsp.

lecoqii, commonly known as Siberian cowparsley, is a member of the Apiaceae family and is native to regions of Central Asia and Siberia. It is a biennial or short-lived perennial plant that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters.

The plant features hollow, ridged stems and compound leaves with a fern-like appearance. Its flowers are small, white to greenish-yellow, and arranged in an umbrella-like inflorescence called a umbel.

The plant is known for its strong, often unpleasant odor and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
