Heliotropium anchusifolium var. latifolium

Heliotropium anchusifolium var. latifolium, commonly known as broadleaf heliotropium, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its opposite, broad, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from purple to pink, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

This species is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and blooms.
