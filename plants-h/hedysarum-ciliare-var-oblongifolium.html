Hedysarum ciliare var. oblongifolium

Hedysarum ciliare var. oblongifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It features compound leaves with elongated, oblong leaflets that are hairy along the edges. The plant produces yellow to orange pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil health.
