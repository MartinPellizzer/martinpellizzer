Hemerocallis fulva var. variegata

Hemerocallis fulva var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated daylily, is a perennial plant native to Asia.

It features strap-like, variegated leaves with yellow and green patterns, and its flowers are typically orange to red with a yellow throat. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It produces showy blooms from midsummer to early fall, and the flowers last only one day, hence its common name.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and hardiness in various garden settings.
