Hybanthus travancoricus

Hybanthus travancoricus, commonly known as the Travancore hybanthus, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a small, herbaceous shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. Its flowers are often fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hybanthus travancoricus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
