Hieracium athesinum

Hieracium athesinum, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are basal, rough, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and clustered in a single head.

Hieracium athesinum is often found in mountainous areas and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
