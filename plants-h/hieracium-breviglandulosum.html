Hieracium breviglandulosum

Hieracium breviglandulosum, commonly known as the short-glanded hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped with sparse, short glands, and it produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer.

It is a herbaceous plant with a fibrous root system and is often found in temperate climates.
