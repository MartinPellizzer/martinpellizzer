Hydrocotyle ulei

Hydrocotyle ulei, commonly known as the water pennywort, is a small aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. The plant has floating and submerged leaves, with the floating leaves often appearing as small, round, and glossy. Its flowers are small and white, usually appearing in clusters, and it produces small, dark seeds.

Hydrocotyle ulei is often used in ornamental ponds and wetland restoration projects due to its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
