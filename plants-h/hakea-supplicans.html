Hakea supplicans

Hakea supplicans, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces striking, bottlebrush-shaped flowers that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Hakea supplicans is also known for its ability to form dense, hardy vegetation in coastal and inland regions.
