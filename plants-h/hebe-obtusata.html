Hebe obtusata

Hebe obtusata, commonly known as the woolly hebe, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. Hebe obtusata thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a favored plant in horticultural settings.
