Hieracium nigrescens subsp. glandulatum

Hieracium nigrescens subsp.

glandulatum, commonly known as the glandular hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are hairy and often have glandular dots, which contribute to its common name.

The flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
