Hieracium caesioides subsp. caesioides

Hieracium caesioides subsp.

caesioides, commonly known as the blue hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features basal leaves that are spoon-shaped and hairy, with stems that are often reddish in color. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense heads, and are typically blue or purple in color.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and wind-pollinated flowers.
