Hypoxis baurii

Hypoxis baurii, commonly known as the star of Bethlehem, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a short, fibrous rootstock and produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves at the base. The plant is most notable for its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Hypoxis baurii is also valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
