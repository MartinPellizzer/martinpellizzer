Hieracium subramosum subsp. duplicatum

Hieracium subramosum subsp.

duplicatum, commonly known as the double-stemmed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in summer. Its subspecies distinction is primarily based on floral morphology, including the presence of two rows of florets.

It is noted for its low-growing habit and is often found in nutrient-rich soils.
