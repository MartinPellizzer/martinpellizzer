Hemigraphis ledermannii

Hemigraphis ledermannii, commonly known as the silver velvetleaf, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features striking, velvety foliage with a silvery-blue hue on the upper surface and a darker green underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value comes from its unique leaf coloration and texture, making it a popular choice for shaded garden landscapes.
