Huberodendron

Huberodendron, commonly known as the African sandthorn, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, where it thrives in dry, sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its small, spiny leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Huberodendron species are typically shrubs or small trees, often growing in coastal or inland savanna habitats.

They play a role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
