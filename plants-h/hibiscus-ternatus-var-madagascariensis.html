Hibiscus ternatus var. madagascariensis

Hibiscus ternatus var. madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Madagascar.

It features large, showy flowers with five deeply notched petals, typically in shades of red, pink, or white. The plant has hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or forested areas.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
