Helichrysum ledifolium subsp. purpurascens

Helichrysum ledifolium subsp.

purpurascens, commonly known as the purpleleaf everlasting, is a member of the Asteraceae family. It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit.

The plant produces small, yellowish to orange flowers that are surrounded by pale green bracts. Its leaves are narrow, leathery, and often have a reddish or purplish hue, especially under stress.

It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
