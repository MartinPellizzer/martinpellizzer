Humboltia gratiosa

Humboltia gratiosa, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a striking flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds. Humboltia gratiosa thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing along rocky outcrops or in sandy coastal habitats.

Its unique floral structure and vibrant color make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native range.
