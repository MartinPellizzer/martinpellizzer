Hydrangea macrophylla var. amagiana

Hydrangea macrophylla var. amagiana, commonly known as the Japanese mountain hydrangea, is a variant of the bigleaf hydrangea native to Japan.

This plant is characterized by its large, showy flower heads that can range in color from blue to pink, depending on soil pH. It typically grows in woodland areas and thrives in partial shade with moist, well-drained soil. The leaves are broad and ovate, often with a waxy texture, and they provide a lush green backdrop to the colorful blooms.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
