Hermesias neglecta

Hermesias neglecta, commonly known as the neglected hermit beetle plant, is a species of flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. It has a relatively short growing season and is often overlooked due to its inconspicuous appearance.

Despite its modest size, Hermesias neglecta plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for small invertebrates.
