Hieracium sparsiflorum subsp. nikolovii

Hieracium sparsiflorum subsp.

nikolovii, commonly known as the Balkan hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species within its native range.
