Hibiscus membranaceus

Hibiscus membranaceus, commonly known as the membranous hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink or red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and covered in fine, delicate hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Hibiscus membranaceus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
