Halothamnus ferganensis var. hispidulus

Halothamnus ferganensis var. hispidulus, commonly known as the Fergana hairy halothamnus, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, wiry stems covered with short, stiff hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by narrow, linear leaves that are adapted to arid conditions. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in high-altitude environments.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for survival in harsh, semi-arid climates.
