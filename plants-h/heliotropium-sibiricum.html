Heliotropium sibiricum

Heliotropium sibiricum, commonly known as Siberian heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the cold climates of Siberia and Central Asia.

It is a low-growing herb with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand harsh winter conditions.

Heliotropium sibiricum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
