Hexaglochin maritima

Hexaglochin maritima, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue or violet flowers and spiky, silver-gray leaves that are adapted to salty, windy environments. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dunes or along shorelines. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hexaglochin maritima is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize coastal soils.
