Helichrysum quinquenerve

Helichrysum quinquenerve, commonly known as the five-veined everlasting flower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, bushy growth habit and bright yellow to golden-orange flowers that remain open for extended periods. The plant has finely divided, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

The species is also valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
