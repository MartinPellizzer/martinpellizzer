Hieracium amplexicaule var. denticulatum

Hieracium amplexicaule var. denticulatum, commonly known as the toothed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy rosettes. The plant features basal leaves that are broadly ovate to lanceolate, often with serrated or toothed margins, which give it its common name. Its flowering stems bear small, yellowish to reddish composite flowers that bloom in summer.

The species is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, where it spreads via its rhizomes and seeds.
