Hyptis helophila

Hyptis helophila, commonly known as marsh hyptis, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or marshy environments. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a strong, aromatic scent. Its flowers are small, tubular, and clustered in spikes, often displaying shades of purple or blue.

Hyptis helophila is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
