Hieracium murorum subsp. protractifolium

Hieracium murorum subsp.

protractifolium, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are elongated and often have a hairy surface, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a cluster.

It is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain environments.
