Hieracium subbauhiniiflorum

Hieracium subbauhiniiflorum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces small, yellow to orange flowers arranged in composite heads. The leaves are typically narrow and linear, with a rough or hairy texture. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas.

This plant is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
