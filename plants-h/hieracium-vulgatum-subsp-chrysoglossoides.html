Hieracium vulgatum subsp. chrysoglossoides

Hieracium vulgatum subsp.

chrysoglossoides, commonly known as the golden-leaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while the flowers are yellow with a central disc.

It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds, contributing to its ability to spread across various habitats.
