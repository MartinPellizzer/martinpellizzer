Haplopappus leverichii

Haplopappus leverichii, commonly known as the desert pappus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and adaptations to harsh environments. The plant has small, narrow leaves and produces fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds in its capitula. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is well adapted to drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it a resilient and ecologically significant species in desert ecosystems.
