Hieracium murorum subsp. regiomontani

Hieracium murorum subsp.

regiomontani, commonly known as the Regiomontan hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features upright stems with narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in late summer. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
