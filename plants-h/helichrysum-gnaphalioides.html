Helichrysum gnaphalioides

Helichrysum gnaphalioides, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellow to orange flowers that resemble tiny suns. The plant has hairy, aromatic leaves and produces numerous tiny seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Helichrysum gnaphalioides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
