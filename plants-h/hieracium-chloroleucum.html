Hieracium chloroleucum

Hieracium chloroleucum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and the leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly hairy surface.

It is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is considered invasive in some regions.
