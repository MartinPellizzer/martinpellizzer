Hymenachne phalarioides

Hymenachne phalarioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf marsh grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, erect stems that are often covered with fine hairs, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like in appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the Poaceae family.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
