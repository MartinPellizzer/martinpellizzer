Homalopetalum kienastii

Homalopetalum kienastii, commonly known as the Kienast's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in dry environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple during the spring and summer months. Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in well-draining soil and full sun exposure.
