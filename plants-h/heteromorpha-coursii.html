Heteromorpha coursii

Heteromorpha coursii, commonly known as the desert daisy, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to yellow. The plant has a succulent stem and leaves adapted to store water, allowing it to thrive in harsh, dry environments. Its flowers bloom in spring and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Heteromorpha coursii is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
