Hieracium sparsum subsp. korabense

Hieracium sparsum subsp.

korabense, commonly known as the Korab hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan region. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flower heads and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in open, rocky, or grassy habitats at elevations ranging from 1,000 to 2,500 meters. Its leaves are basal and often have a rough, serrated texture, while its flowers are arranged in a composite head.

The species is noted for its ecological adaptability and is often found in disturbed or marginal environments.
