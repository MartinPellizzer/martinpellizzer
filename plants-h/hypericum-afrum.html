Hypericum afrum

Hypericum afrum, commonly known as African St. John's Wort, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features upright, branched stems covered in small, glandular dots that exude a sticky substance. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Hypericum afrum is often found in grasslands and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
