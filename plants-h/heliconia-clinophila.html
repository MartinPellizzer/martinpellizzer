Heliconia clinophila

Heliconia clinophila, commonly known as the red-veined heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, boat-shaped inflorescences. The plant features broad, waxy leaves that are often bright green with red veins, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, surrounded by long, colorful bracts.

Heliconia clinophila is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant foliage and unique floral structure.
