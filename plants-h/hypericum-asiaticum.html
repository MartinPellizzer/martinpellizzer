Hypericum asiaticum

Hypericum asiaticum, commonly known as the garden gold or St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to southern and eastern Europe.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, oval-shaped leaves. Its flowers are composed of five petals and numerous stamens, giving them a distinctive appearance.

Hypericum asiaticum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
