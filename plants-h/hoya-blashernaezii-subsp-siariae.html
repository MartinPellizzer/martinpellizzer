Hoya blashernaezii subsp. siariae

Hoya blashernaezii subsp.

siariae, commonly known as the Siariae Waffle Plant, is a unique member of the Apocynaceae family. It is native to the Philippines and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that resemble waffles, hence its common name.

The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its rarity and specific care requirements, it is considered a sought-after specimen among plant enthusiasts.
