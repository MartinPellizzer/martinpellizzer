Hypericum alpigenum var. umbellatiforme

Hypericum alpigenum var. umbellatiforme, commonly known as the alpine St. John's wort, is a perennial flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, demonstrating its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are generally upright and branched.

This variant is distinguished by its umbellate inflorescence, which gives it its specific epithet.
