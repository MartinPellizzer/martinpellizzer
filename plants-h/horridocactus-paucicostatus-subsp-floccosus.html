Horridocactus paucicostatus subsp. floccosus

Horridocactus paucicostatus subsp.

floccosus, commonly known as the woolly hedgehog cactus, is a unique cactus species native to Mexico. It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems covered in dense, white, woolly hairs that give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, adding a striking contrast to its fuzzy foliage. It thrives in arid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized species within the cactus family.
