Hydrangea strigosa var. angustifolia

Hydrangea strigosa var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces clusters of sterile, showy flowers in shades of blue, pink, or white, depending on soil pH. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to adapt to a range of garden conditions.
