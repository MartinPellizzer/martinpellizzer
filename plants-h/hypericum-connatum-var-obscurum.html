Hypericum connatum var. obscurum

Hypericum connatum var. obscurum, commonly known as the dark-stemmed St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often purplish stems and small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically narrow and opposite, with a slightly hairy texture. This variety is distinguished by its darker stem color compared to other Hypericum species.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
