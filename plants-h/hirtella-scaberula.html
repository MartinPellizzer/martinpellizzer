Hirtella scaberula

Hirtella scaberula, commonly known as the rough hirtella, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage, which gives it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are typically elliptical and have a rough, scabrous texture, contributing to its common name.

Hirtella scaberula is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical rainforests.
