Hieracium hemipoliodes

Hieracium hemipoliodes, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically has finely divided, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Native to Europe, it thrives in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is sometimes grown in gardens.
