Hieracium hians

Hieracium hians, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its yellow flowers bloom from June to September, and the leaves are usually lance-shaped with a rough texture.

Hieracium hians is often found in sunny habitats and can form dense colonies, sometimes becoming invasive in certain regions.
