Hedera canariensis var. maculata

Hedera canariensis var. maculata, commonly known as the variegated English ivy, is a hybrid ivy plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its distinctive variegated leaves, which feature white and yellow markings on a dark green background. The plant has a climbing habit and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in shaded environments and is often used as a ground cover or climbing plant in gardens.

Due to its invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a weed in certain regions.
