Hedyotis robinsonii

Hedyotis robinsonii, commonly known as the Robinson's bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Hedyotis robinsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
