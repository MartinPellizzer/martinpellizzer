Hieracium bifidum subsp. psepharum

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

psepharum, commonly known as the purple hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems.

The plant features basal and alternate leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in dense, flat-topped clusters, known as capitula.

It thrives in moist meadows and woodland edges, often forming dense colonies.
