Hieraciodes crocifolia

Hieraciodes crocifolia, commonly known as the crocus-leaved hieracium, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that resemble those of the genus Crocus, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy areas and is native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are usually arranged in a single head and bloom in late spring to early summer.
