Hieracium arrectipes

Hieracium arrectipes, commonly known as the upright hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features basal leaves that are often lobed or toothed, and its flowering stems bear small, yellow flowers arranged in a single head. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows and disturbed soils, and is often found in temperate regions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
