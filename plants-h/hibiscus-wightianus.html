Hibiscus wightianus

Hibiscus wightianus, commonly known as the Wight's hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red, with a prominent yellow center. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are usually green and slightly hairy. Hibiscus wightianus thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
