Hippochaete × nelsonii

Hippochaete × nelsonii, commonly known as the Nelson's woollybush, is a hybrid plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a range of morphological variations.
