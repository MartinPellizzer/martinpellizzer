Hieracium succisoides

Hieracium succisoides, commonly known as the creeping hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant has a low-growing, mat-forming habit with hairy, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers.

Hieracium succisoides is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
