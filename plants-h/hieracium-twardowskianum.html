Hieracium twardowskianum

Hieracium twardowskianum, commonly known as the Twardowski's hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a cluster of small flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring moist to moderately dry conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

This species is often found in open habitats and is relatively hardy, though it can be sensitive to environmental changes.
