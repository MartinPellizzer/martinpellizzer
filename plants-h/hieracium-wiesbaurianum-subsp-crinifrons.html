Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp. crinifrons

Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp.

crinifrons, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered with fine, soft hairs.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
