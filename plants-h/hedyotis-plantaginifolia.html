Hedyotis plantaginifolia

Hedyotis plantaginifolia, commonly known as the small green herb or lesser bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of Asia and Europe.

It typically features slender, creeping stems covered in fine, soft hairs. The leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape, and often have a slightly serrated edge. Small white or pale yellow flowers appear in clusters during the summer months.

This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.
