Herschelia lacera

Herschelia lacera, commonly known as the rock sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually clustered in spikes. It is adapted to withstand drought conditions due to its deep root system and succulent leaves.

Herschelia lacera is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging landscapes.
