Hyptis brevipes var. glabrior

Hyptis brevipes var. glabrior, commonly known as the hairy hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture.

The flowers are small, clustered in whorls, and often display shades of purple or blue, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
