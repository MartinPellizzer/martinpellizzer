Hordeum tanaiticum

Hordeum tanaiticum, commonly known as the Tanaitic barley, is a species of wild barley native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is a small annual grass that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous short, awned lemmas. Its seeds are generally small and have a hard, protective hull.

Hordeum tanaiticum is of interest to botanists and agricultural researchers due to its potential for genetic diversity and adaptability to harsh climates.
