Hieracium laevigatum subsp. keldii

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

keldii, commonly known as the Keld’s hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are broadly ovate to lanceolate, often with a hairy surface, and the flowers are yellow with ray florets.

It is a relatively small plant, usually reaching heights of 10–30 cm, and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
