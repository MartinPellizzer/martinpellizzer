Hieracium divisum var. sublaciniatum

Hieracium divisum var. sublaciniatum, commonly known as the finely lobed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant features finely divided, serrated leaves that give it a distinctive, fern-like appearance. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.

Its flowers are yellow and bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
