Hippochaete laevigata var. caespitica

Hippochaete laevigata var. caespitica, commonly known as the tufted dwarf dandelion, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features a compact, low-growing habit with a rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically covered in fine, white pubescence, giving them a woolly appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.
