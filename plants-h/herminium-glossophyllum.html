Herminium glossophyllum

Herminium glossophyllum, commonly known as the showy lady's tresses, is a species of orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and its striking, fragrant, white to pale pink flower spikes that resemble a tresses of hair. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and it produces small, round seeds within its pods.

Herminium glossophyllum is a perennial herb that spreads through rhizomes, forming colonies in suitable habitats.
