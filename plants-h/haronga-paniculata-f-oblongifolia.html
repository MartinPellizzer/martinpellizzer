Haronga paniculata f. oblongifolia

Haronga paniculata f. oblongifolia, commonly known as the oblong-leaf haronga, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces clusters of small, daisy-like yellow flowers, which bloom in the spring and summer months. It is native to certain regions of Africa and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for both horticultural and ecological studies.
