Helianthus frondosus

Helianthus frondosus, commonly known as the fronded sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers and robust, branching stems. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. Its leaves are lobed and often have a frond-like appearance, giving it its common name.

Helianthus frondosus is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
