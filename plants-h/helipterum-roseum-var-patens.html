Helipterum roseum var. patens

Helipterum roseum var. patens, commonly known as the spreading helipterum, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant features showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its native environment.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
