Haplopappus ferrisiae

Haplopappus ferrisiae, commonly known as the desert pappus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and adaptive features for survival in harsh environments. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and has a low, sprawling growth habit to minimize water loss. Its small, yellowish flowers are followed by fluffy pappus structures that aid in wind dispersal of its seeds.

Haplopappus ferrisiae is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.
