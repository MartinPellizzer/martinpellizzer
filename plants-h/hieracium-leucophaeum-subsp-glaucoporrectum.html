Hieracium leucophaeum subsp. glaucoporrectum

Hieracium leucophaeum subsp.

glaucoporrectum, commonly known as the glaucous hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette growth habit and hairy, glaucous leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the genus Hieracium. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Its subspecies distinction is based on subtle differences in leaf morphology and pubescence compared to other members of the species.
