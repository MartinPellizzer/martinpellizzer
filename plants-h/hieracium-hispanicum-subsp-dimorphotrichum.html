Hieracium hispanicum subsp. dimorphotrichum

Hieracium hispanicum subsp.

dimorphotrichum, commonly known as the Spanish hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellowish to orange flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has alternate, lanceolate leaves with hairy surfaces, and its stems are often branched and covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is distinguished by its dimorphic trichomes, which are a key feature in its taxonomic classification.
