Hyptis cymulosa

Hyptis cymulosa, commonly known as the purple hyptis, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and clusters of small, violet or purple flowers. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Hyptis cymulosa is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
