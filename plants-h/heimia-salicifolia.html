Heimia salicifolia

Heimia salicifolia, commonly known as the "Mexican willow," is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features lance-shaped, deciduous leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Heimia salicifolia is often found in moist, well-drained soils near streams or in forest understories.
