Hieracium spatholepideum

Hieracium spatholepideum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disk.

Hieracium spatholepideum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
