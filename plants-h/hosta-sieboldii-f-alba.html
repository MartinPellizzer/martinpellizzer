Hosta sieboldii f. alba

Hosta sieboldii f. alba, commonly known as the White Siebold's Hosta, is a cultivar of the Hosta plant known for its striking white-variegated leaves.

It is a perennial plant native to East Asia, specifically Japan, and thrives in shady, moist environments. The foliage is broad, lance-shaped, and often features a creamy white margin with a dark green center. This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to add visual interest throughout the growing season.

It is relatively low maintenance and suitable for USDA hardiness zones 3 to 8.
