Hieracium sparsiflorum subsp. subsparsiflorum

Hieracium sparsiflorum subsp.

subsparsiflorum, commonly known as the sparseflower hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm.

The plant features basal and alternate leaves that are usually lanceolate to ovate in shape. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single, terminal head, with ray florets that are often shorter than the disk florets.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
