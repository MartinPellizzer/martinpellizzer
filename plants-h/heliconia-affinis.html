Heliconia affinis

Heliconia affinis, commonly known as the red heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescences. The plant features long, narrow leaves that form a rosette, and its flowers are clustered in a spiral arrangement. Heliconia affinis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
