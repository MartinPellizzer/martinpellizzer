Heuchera viscida

Heuchera viscida, commonly known as sticky alumroot, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features attractive, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, sticky resin, giving the plant its common name. The foliage ranges in color from deep red to purple and bronze, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens. It produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white or pale yellow flowers in early summer.

This plant thrives in partial to full shade and moist, well-drained soil, often found in woodland environments.
