Hieracium atricapitulum

Hieracium atricapitulum, commonly known as the black-headed hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its dark purple to black central disk contrasts with its yellow ray florets, making it visually distinctive.

The species is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative reproduction, contributing to its success in various habitats.
