Hieracium florentinum subsp. nivigemmum

Hieracium florentinum subsp.

nivigemmum, commonly known as the snowy false dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, thriving in temperate climates.

The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and borne in a single head, often with a distinctive reddish or purplish tinge.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and is often mistaken for other similar dandelion species.
