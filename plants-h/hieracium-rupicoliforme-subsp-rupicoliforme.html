Hieracium rupicoliforme subsp. rupicoliforme

Hieracium rupicoliforme subsp.

rupicoliforme, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

The plant features a rosette of basal leaves and produces yellow flowers with a distinctive ray floret structure. It has a compact growth habit and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
