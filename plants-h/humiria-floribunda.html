Humiria floribunda

Humiria floribunda, commonly known as the Brazilian violet, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of small, violet-colored flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical regions.

Humiria floribunda is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
