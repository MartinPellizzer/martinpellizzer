Hieracium largum var. pallonianum

Hieracium largum var. pallonianum, commonly known as the Pallonian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is known for its aggressive growth and can become invasive in certain environments.
