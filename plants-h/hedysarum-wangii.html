Hedysarum wangii

Hedysarum wangii, commonly known as the Wang's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, which are clustered in racemes. Hedysarum wangii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest for its potential in ecological restoration projects.
