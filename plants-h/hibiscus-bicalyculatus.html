Hibiscus bicalyculatus

Hibiscus bicalyculatus, commonly known as the two-lobed hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink or red. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Hibiscus bicalyculatus thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking floral display.
