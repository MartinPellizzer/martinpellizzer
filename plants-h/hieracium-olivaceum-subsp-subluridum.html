Hieracium olivaceum subsp. subluridum

Hieracium olivaceum subsp.

subluridum, commonly known as the olive hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, silver-green leaves and yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

The plant thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ability to produce a large number of seeds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
