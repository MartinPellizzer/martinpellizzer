Heliconia brachyantha

Heliconia brachyantha, commonly known as the red heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, brightly colored bracts that form a spiral-like structure around the inflorescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are large, broad, and often have a waxy texture.

Heliconia brachyantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical landscapes.
