Hieracium blepharophyllum

Hieracium blepharophyllum, commonly known as the white-leaved hawkweed, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive white to pale green leaves, which are often covered in a fine, silky hair. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas.

Due to its ornamental appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
