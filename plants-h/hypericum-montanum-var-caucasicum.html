Hypericum montanum var. caucasicum

Hypericum montanum var. caucasicum, commonly known as Caucasian St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It features small, yellow, four-petaled flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters along the stems. The plant has woody stems and alternate, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in rocky or well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties, with compounds similar to those found in other Hypericum species.
