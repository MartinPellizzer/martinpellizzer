Haworthia namaquensis

Haworthia namaquensis, commonly known as the Namaqua bulbous, is a species of succulent native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-shaped growth habit and fleshy, thick leaves that are often marked with distinctive white or silver markings. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor collections.
