Hieracium subulisquamum

Hieracium subulisquamum, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leaf-like bracts. The plant features hairy, serrated leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in dense clusters at the top of the plant.

Hieracium subulisquamum is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, where it thrives in full sun to partial shade.
