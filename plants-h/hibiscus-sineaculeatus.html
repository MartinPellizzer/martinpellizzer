Hibiscus sineaculeatus

Hibiscus sineaculeatus, commonly known as the white hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers with five petals and a prominent central stigma. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in humid conditions. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Hibiscus sineaculeatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
