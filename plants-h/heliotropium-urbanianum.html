Heliotropium urbanianum

Heliotropium urbanianum, commonly known as the coastal heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in coastal areas and saline soils. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, violet or purple flowers. It is adapted to withstand salt spray and sandy soils, making it a hardy species.

Its blooming period usually occurs during the warmer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
