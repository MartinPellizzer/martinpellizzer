Hesperis matronalis subsp. laciniata

Hesperis matronalis subsp.

laciniata, commonly known as the cut-leaved white campion, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe. It features basal leaves that are deeply lobed or cut, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long flowering period and ability to attract pollinators.
