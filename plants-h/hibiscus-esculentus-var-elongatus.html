Hibiscus esculentus var. elongatus

Hibiscus esculentus var. elongatus, commonly known as the okra or lady's finger, is a tropical flowering plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically pink or white in color. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture.

This variety is cultivated for its edible fruits, which are widely used in various cuisines around the world.
