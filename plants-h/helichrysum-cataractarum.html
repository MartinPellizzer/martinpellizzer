Helichrysum cataractarum

Helichrysum cataractarum, commonly known as the waterfall everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers that resemble tiny suns, which bloom in spring and summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found growing in rocky or sandy soils. It is a perennial shrub that thrives in full sun and is drought-tolerant.

Its common name reflects its tendency to grow in areas with water flow, such as near streams or waterfalls.
