Hieracium tephrosoma subsp. deruptorum

Hieracium tephrosoma subsp.

deruptorum, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower.

The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are rough and hairy, with a distinctive yellowish-green color, and it produces small, yellow flowers in clusters.

This subspecies is often found in open habitats and is adapted to a variety of soil types.
