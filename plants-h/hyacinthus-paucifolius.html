Hyacinthus paucifolius

Hyacinthus paucifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hyacinth, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a basal rosette, and its bulbs are adapted for storing nutrients. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Hyacinthus paucifolius is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize over time.
