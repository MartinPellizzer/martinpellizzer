Hieracium apatorium

Hieracium apatorium, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosette of leaves, which are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and roadside areas.

Hieracium apatorium is often found in temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.
