Hippeastrum advenum

Hippeastrum advenum, commonly known as the Brazilian belladonna lily, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 centimeters in diameter, typically in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces a tall, leafless stem that bears a single flower at the top, which opens in a bell-like shape. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often emerging from the base of the stem.

Hippeastrum advenum is a bulbous plant that thrives in warm, humid environments and is popular as a decorative ornamental plant.
