Hydnophytum montanum var. latifolia

Hydnophytum montanum var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while still performing some photosynthesis using its broad, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers, followed by fleshy, red berries that attract birds for seed dispersal. It is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, often in lowland forests.

Its unique ecological role makes it an important component of forest ecosystems.
