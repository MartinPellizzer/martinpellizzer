Heliotropium genovefae

Heliotropium genovefae, commonly known as the Genovefa's heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly found in the Bahamas and the Turks and Caicos Islands.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually violet or purple in color, which bloom in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

Heliotropium genovefae is adapted to salt spray and can tolerate moderate drought conditions.
