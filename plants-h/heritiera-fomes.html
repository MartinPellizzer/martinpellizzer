Heritiera fomes

Heritiera fomes, commonly known as the Indian mangrove or mangrove fig, is a tropical tree native to the coastal regions of the Indian subcontinent.

It is a member of the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fibrous roots that help stabilize coastal soils. The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its propagation in wetland environments.
