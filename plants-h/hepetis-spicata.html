Hepetis spicata

Hepetis spicata, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, sky-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Hepetis spicata is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
