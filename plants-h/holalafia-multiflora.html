Holalafia multiflora

Holalafia multiflora, commonly known as the Hawaiian mistflower, is a perennial shrub native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland habitats. Its leaves are elliptical and have a leathery texture, while its stems are woody and branched.

Holalafia multiflora plays an important role in the local ecosystem, providing habitat and nectar for native pollinators.
