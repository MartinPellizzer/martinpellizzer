Hamamelis vernalis f. tomentella

Hamamelis vernalis f. tomentella, commonly known as the woolly witch hazel, is a variant of the witch hazel plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers in the fall, which are followed by reddish-brown capsules. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a rough, textured surface.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in gardens for its winter interest and hardy nature.
