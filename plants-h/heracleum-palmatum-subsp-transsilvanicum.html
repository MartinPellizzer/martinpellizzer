Heracleum palmatum subsp. transsilvanicum

Heracleum palmatum subsp.

transsilvanicum, commonly known as the Transylvanian cow parsley, is a member of the Apiaceae family and is native to Central and Eastern Europe. It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, umbrella-like compound umbels of white flowers.

The leaves are pinnately divided, with a distinctive silvery-green coloration on the underside. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and forest edges, and is often found in areas with calcareous soils.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting pollinators.
