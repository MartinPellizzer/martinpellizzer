Honckenya peploides var. oblongifolia

Honckenya peploides var. oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It has small, linear to oblanceolate leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Due to its drought tolerance and unique morphology, it is an important species for stabilizing soils in arid landscapes.
