Hieracium morisianum subsp. padolanum

Hieracium morisianum subsp.

padolanum, commonly known as the Morisian Hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, and its leaves are usually lance-shaped with a rough texture.

This subspecies is often found in moist environments and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.
