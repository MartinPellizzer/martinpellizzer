Hibiscus rosa-sinensis schizopetalus

Hibiscus rosa-sinensis schizopetalus, commonly known as the Hawaiian hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from red to yellow, with a prominent central pistil and five to seven stamens. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are dark green and arranged alternately along the stems. It produces fruits that are capsule-like, containing numerous seeds.

Hibiscus rosa-sinensis schizopetalus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.
