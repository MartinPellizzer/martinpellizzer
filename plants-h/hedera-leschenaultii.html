Hedera leschenaultii

Hedera leschenaultii, commonly known as the Leschenault's ivy, is a species of climbing plant native to the mountainous regions of southern and eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that are typically heart-shaped with a pointed tip. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest areas.

Hedera leschenaultii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its hardiness and adaptability.
