Hansenia pinnatiinvolucellata

Hansenia pinnatiinvolucellata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sandwort, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant features small, fleshy leaves arranged in a pinnate pattern, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Its flowers are typically pink or white, with four petals, and bloom in early spring.

Hansenia pinnatiinvolucellata is adapted to withstand drought and extreme temperatures, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
