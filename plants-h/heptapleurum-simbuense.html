Heptapleurum simbuense

It is native to the mountainous regions of New Guinea and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and compact growth habit. The plant typically features broad, leathery leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in cup-like structures called cyathia, a common feature in the Euphorbiaceae family.

Heptapleurum simbuense is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to shaded environments.
