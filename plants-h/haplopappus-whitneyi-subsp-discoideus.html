Haplopappus whitneyi subsp. discoideus

Haplopappus whitneyi subsp.

discoideus, commonly known as the desert pappus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and woolly, silvery foliage.

The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as sandy soils and rocky slopes. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually yellow or pale green, and it produces papery, parachute-like seeds for wind dispersal.

The species is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient component of desert ecosystems.
