Hieracium mediofurcum

Hieracium mediofurcum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering stems that bear yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a single head.

Hieracium mediofurcum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes and is often considered a weed in agricultural areas.
