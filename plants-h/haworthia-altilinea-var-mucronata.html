Haworthia altilinea var. mucronata

Haworthia altilinea var. mucronata, commonly known as the sharp-tipped Haworthia, is a compact, rosette-forming succulent native to South Africa.

It features narrow, fleshy leaves that are typically green with white or pale blue markings, often with a distinct white tip. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the growing season. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy leaves.

Due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for indoor and rock garden cultivation.
