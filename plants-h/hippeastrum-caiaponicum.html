Hippeastrum caiaponicum

Hippeastrum caiaponicum, commonly known as the Brazilian fire lily, is a species of amaryllid native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking red and orange flowers, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant produces large, strap-like leaves that are typically broad and dark green. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for both garden beds and container cultivation.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice among horticulturists and garden enthusiasts.
