Hyptis glauca

Hyptis glauca, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a perennial herb native to the Americas.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and spikes of small, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

The genus Hyptis includes over 600 species, many of which are valued for their ornamental and medicinal uses.
