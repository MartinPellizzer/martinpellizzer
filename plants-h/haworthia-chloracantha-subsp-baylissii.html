Haworthia chloracantha subsp. baylissii

Haworthia chloracantha subsp.

baylissii, commonly known as the Bayliss's Haworthia, is a compact, low-growing succulent native to South Africa. It features a rosette of fleshy, dark green leaves with a waxy coating that aids in water retention.

The leaves are adorned with white to pale green tubercles, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange during the warmer months.

This species is well-suited for rock gardens and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
