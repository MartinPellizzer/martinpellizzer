Heliotropium paniculatum

Heliotropium paniculatum, commonly known as the clustered heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, violet or purple flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Heliotropium paniculatum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
