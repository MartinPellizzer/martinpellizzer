Hieracium caesium subsp. laciniifolium

Hieracium caesium subsp.

laciniifolium, commonly known as the cut-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosettes of leaves.

The leaves are deeply lobed or dissected, giving the plant its distinctive, fern-like appearance. It produces yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences, which bloom from late spring to early autumn.

This subspecies is often found in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
