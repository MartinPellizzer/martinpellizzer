Hydrocotyle quinqueloba var. quadrata

Hydrocotyle quinqueloba var. quadrata, commonly known as the broadleaf water shield, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. The plant has floating leaves that are divided into five lobes, with a distinctive quadrilateral shape in its varietal form. Its small, white flowers bloom in the summer, and it produces small, round fruits that float on the water surface.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
