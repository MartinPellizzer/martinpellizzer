Hyssopus lophanthus

Hyssopus lophanthus, commonly known as the mountain hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, arranged in whorled clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

Hyssopus lophanthus is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
