Hesperis secundiflora

Hesperis secundiflora, commonly known as the lesser candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Hesperis secundiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
