Hyphaene obovata

Hyphaene obovata, commonly known as the African date palm, is a large, fan-shaped palm native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its robust trunk and large, feather-like leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, date-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Hyphaene obovata is also valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in many African communities.
