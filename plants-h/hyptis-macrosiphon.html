Hyptis macrosiphon

Hyptis macrosiphon, commonly known as Mexican mint marigold, is a perennial herb native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal properties. The plant features opposite, hairy leaves that are typically lance-shaped and have a strong, mint-like fragrance. Its flowers are small, tubular, and clustered in whorls, often displaying shades of white, purple, or pink.

Hyptis macrosiphon is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
