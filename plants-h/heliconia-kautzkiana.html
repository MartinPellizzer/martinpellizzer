Heliconia kautzkiana

Heliconia kautzkiana, commonly known as the Kautzki's heliconia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is characterized by its large, brightly colored bracts that resemble flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often forming clumps with tall, sturdy stems. Its leaves are broad and elliptical, often with a waxy texture, and the inflorescence consists of a spiral arrangement of colorful, fleshy bracts.

Heliconia kautzkiana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates.
