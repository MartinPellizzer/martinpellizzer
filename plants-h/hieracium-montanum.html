Hieracium montanum

Hieracium montanum, commonly known as mountain hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, preferring well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range from yellow to reddish-orange.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in mountainous areas and serves as a food source for certain pollinators.
