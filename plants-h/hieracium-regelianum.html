Hieracium regelianum

Hieracium regelianum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its yellow flowers, which bloom in summer, are surrounded by pale green bracts, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Hieracium regelianum is also known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in agricultural areas.
