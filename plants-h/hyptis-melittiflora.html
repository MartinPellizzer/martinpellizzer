Hyptis melittiflora

Hyptis melittiflora, commonly known as honeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the warm regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and clusters of small, tubular flowers that are rich in nectar. Its leaves are typically aromatic and often used in traditional medicine.

Hyptis melittiflora is particularly attractive to bees and other pollinators due to its abundant floral resources.
