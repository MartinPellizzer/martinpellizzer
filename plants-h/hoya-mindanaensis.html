Hoya mindanaensis

Hoya mindanaensis, commonly known as the Mindanao Wax Plant, is a species of flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and clusters of star-shaped, waxy flowers. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic, typically in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces a milky sap that can be toxic if ingested.

Hoya mindanaensis is popular among succulent and tropical plant enthusiasts due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
