Hieracium murorum subsp. gilvocaniceps

Hieracium murorum subsp.

gilvocaniceps, commonly known as the yellow-hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, meadows, and wastelands.

The plant features upright stems covered in fine, yellowish hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its basal leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while the upper leaves are smaller and more lobed.

The flowers are yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and are produced in late spring to early summer.
