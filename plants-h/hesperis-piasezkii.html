Hesperis piasezkii

Hesperis piasezkii, commonly known as the Crimean bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus and parts of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and segmented fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pale pink, bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Hesperis piasezkii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
