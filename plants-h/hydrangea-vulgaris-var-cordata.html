Hydrangea vulgaris var. cordata

Hydrangea vulgaris var. cordata, commonly known as the panicle hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and large, cone-shaped flower clusters that range from white to pink or red depending on soil pH. The plant produces showy inflorescences that transition in color as the season progresses. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a serrated margin.

It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
