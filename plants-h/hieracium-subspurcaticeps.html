Hieracium subspurcaticeps

Hieracium subspurcaticeps, commonly known as the spotted hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy, rough leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed areas, preferring well-drained soils. It is native to Europe and has been introduced to other regions, including North America.

Hieracium subspurcaticeps is known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it an invasive species in some areas.
