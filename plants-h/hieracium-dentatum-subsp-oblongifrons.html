Hieracium dentatum subsp. oblongifrons

Hieracium dentatum subsp.

oblongifrons, commonly known as the broad-fruited hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features a basal rosette of leaves that are broadly ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated margins.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers in late spring to early summer, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, and the stem is hairy as well, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is often found in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, showcasing its adaptability to various environments.
