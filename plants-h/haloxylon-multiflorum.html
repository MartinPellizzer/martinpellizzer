Haloxylon multiflorum

Haloxylon multiflorum, commonly known as the desert date tree, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is well adapted to extreme drought conditions, with thick, fleshy leaves that store water and reduce transpiration. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and early summer. Its hardy nature makes it an important component of desert ecosystems, providing shelter and food for various wildlife.

Haloxylon multiflorum is also valued for its hard, heavy wood, which has been used historically for construction and tool-making.
