Hartleya inopinata

Hartleya inopinata, commonly known as the Mexican firecracker, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is a member of the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, often covered in a fine, silky hair.

Hartleya inopinata is prized by horticulturists for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
