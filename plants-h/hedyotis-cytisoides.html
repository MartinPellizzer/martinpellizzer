Hedyotis cytisoides

Hedyotis cytisoides, commonly known as the golden saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, bright yellow flowers clustered in dense inflorescences. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for rock gardens.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
