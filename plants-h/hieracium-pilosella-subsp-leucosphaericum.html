Hieracium pilosella subsp. leucosphaericum

Hieracium pilosella subsp.

leucosphaericum, commonly known as the whitehead hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

The plant features small, yellowish to white flowers arranged in a composite head, which is characteristic of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are usually hairy and lance-shaped, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The subspecies is distinguished by its white to pale yellow flower heads, which set it apart from other members of the Hieracium genus.
