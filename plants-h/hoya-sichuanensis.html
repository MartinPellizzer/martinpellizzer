Hoya sichuanensis

Hoya sichuanensis, commonly known as the Sichuan Waxplant, is a species of flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and clusters of star-shaped flowers. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant. Its flowers, which are typically white or pale pink, bloom in summer and emit a subtle fragrance.

Hoya sichuanensis is also valued for its air-purifying properties and ease of care.
