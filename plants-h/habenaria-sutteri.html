Habenaria sutteri

Habenaria sutteri, commonly known as the Sutter's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and pine flatwoods. The plant features a slender, upright stem with a single or few flowers that are usually pale green to white in color. Its flowers have three sepals and three petals, with the lateral petals often curved backward.

The species is notable for its delicate appearance and is often found growing in association with mycorrhizal fungi, which aid in nutrient absorption.
