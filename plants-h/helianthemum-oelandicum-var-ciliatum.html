Helianthemum oelandicum var. ciliatum

Helianthemum oelandicum var. ciliatum, commonly known as the Oeland thyme, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to southern Europe.

It features small, rounded leaves with ciliate margins, giving it a distinctive fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
