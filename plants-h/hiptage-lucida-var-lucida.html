Hiptage lucida var. lucida

Hiptage lucida var. lucida, commonly known as the sacred lotus of Sri Lanka, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of southern Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and woody stems, making it well adapted to moist, tropical environments.

The plant is often found in wetlands, riverbanks, and other waterlogged areas where it thrives in partial to full shade.
