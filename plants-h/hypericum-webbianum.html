Hypericum webbianum

Hypericum webbianum, commonly known as the narrowleaf St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded or partially shaded areas.

Hypericum webbianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for pollinators.
