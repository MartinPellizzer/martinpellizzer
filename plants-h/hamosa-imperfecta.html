Hamosa imperfecta

Hamosa imperfecta, commonly known as the imperfect hamosa, is a member of the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a shrub-like plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its leaves are succulent and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native habitat.

Hamosa imperfecta is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
