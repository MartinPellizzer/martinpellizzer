Hieracium levicaule subsp. tephrellum

Hieracium levicaule subsp.

tephrellum, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges.

The plant features upright stems that are often hairy, with basal leaves that are lance-shaped and have a rough texture. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, and it produces tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

This subspecies is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
