Heliophila dregeana

Heliophila dregeana, commonly known as the African sunflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows up to 1 meter in height, featuring slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow, daisy-like flowers with a central disk surrounded by ray florets, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy surface.

Heliophila dregeana thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
