Hieracium incisum subsp. pachysphaericum

Hieracium incisum subsp.

pachysphaericum, commonly known as the broad-leafed hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features basal and stem leaves that are broadly ovate to lanceolate, with serrated margins. Its flowers are yellow, clustered in a flat-topped inflorescence, and are surrounded by bracts.

It is characterized by its robust growth habit and distinctive leaf morphology.
