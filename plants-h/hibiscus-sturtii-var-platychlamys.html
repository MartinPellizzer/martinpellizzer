Hibiscus sturtii var. platychlamys

Hibiscus sturtii var. platychlamys, commonly known as the Sturt's desert rose, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, large, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant has a woody stem and produces broad, ovate leaves with a hairy texture. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

The flowers are typically nocturnal, opening in the evening and closing during the day.
