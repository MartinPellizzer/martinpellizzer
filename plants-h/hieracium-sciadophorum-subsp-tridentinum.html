Hieracium sciadophorum subsp. tridentinum

Hieracium sciadophorum subsp.

tridentinum, commonly known as the three-lobed hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are deeply lobed, often with three distinct lobes, and the flowers are yellow with a central disc.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
