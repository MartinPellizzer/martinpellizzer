Hieracium maculatum subsp. subtenuiflorum

Hieracium maculatum subsp.

subtenuiflorum, commonly known as the spotted hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster, and its leaves are typically hairy and serrated.

The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grasslands. It has small, rounded leaves with a distinctive purple or reddish blotch on the underside.

This subspecies is known for its ornamental value but can also be invasive in certain regions.
