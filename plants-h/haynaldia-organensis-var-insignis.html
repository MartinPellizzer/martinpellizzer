Haynaldia organensis var. insignis

Haynaldia organensis var. insignis, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, green leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by pale green to yellowish seed capsules. It is often found in rocky soils and along dry streambeds, thriving in low-nutrient conditions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is considered a pioneer species in disturbed or degraded habitats.
