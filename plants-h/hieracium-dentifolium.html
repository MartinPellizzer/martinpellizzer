Hieracium dentifolium

Hieracium dentifolium, commonly known as the tooth-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features basal leaves that are deeply lobed and serrated, giving it its common name. It produces small yellow flowers arranged in a cluster, typically blooming from late spring to early summer.

Hieracium dentifolium is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
