Hedyotis pruinosa

Hedyotis pruinosa, commonly known as the silverweed or bluish silverweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to regions of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are usually ovate to elliptic in shape and have a slightly hairy surface.

Hedyotis pruinosa is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest floors or rocky areas.
