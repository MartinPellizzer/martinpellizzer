Hyssopus subulifolius

Hyssopus subulifolius, commonly known as the needleleaf hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves that are arranged in opposite pairs along the stem. The plant produces spikes of blue or purple flowers in the summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in horticulture.
