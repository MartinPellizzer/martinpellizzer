Hyptis brunnescens

Hyptis brunnescens, commonly known as Brazilian hyptis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic and medicinal plants. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. Its small, clustered flowers are typically purple or violet, attracting various pollinators.

Hyptis brunnescens is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
