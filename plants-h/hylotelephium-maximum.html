Hylotelephium maximum

Hylotelephium maximum, commonly known as the tall stonecrop, is a perennial succulent native to rocky habitats in Europe and Asia.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically gray-green in color, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of pink, red, or purple during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its hardy nature and ability to tolerate drought conditions contribute to its adaptability in various climates.
