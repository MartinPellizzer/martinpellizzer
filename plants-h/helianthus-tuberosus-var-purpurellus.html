Helianthus tuberosus var. purpurellus

Helianthus tuberosus var. purpurellus, commonly known as the purpleleaf sunflower, is a cultivar of the sunflower family characterized by its striking violet-tinged foliage.

This variety features large, showy yellow flowers with a prominent dark center, typical of the Helianthus genus. It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often reaching heights of 4 to 6 feet. The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes grown for its edible tubers, though these are less prominent than in other varieties.

Its unique leaf color makes it a popular choice in gardens for its visual appeal.
