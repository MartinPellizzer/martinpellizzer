Hosta sieboldiana var. bracteata

Hosta sieboldiana var. bracteata, commonly known as the Siebold's Hosta, is a hardy perennial plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its broad, dark green leaves that are often adorned with a striking white or light green margin. The plant produces fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in late summer, typically in shades of white or pale blue. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is a popular choice in shady garden settings.
