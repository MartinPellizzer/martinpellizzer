Hardenbergia comptoniana

Hardenbergia comptoniana, commonly known as the red-flowered everlasting pea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive bright red or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has compound leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well adapted to dry, sandy soils.

Hardenbergia comptoniana is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, benefiting soil fertility.
