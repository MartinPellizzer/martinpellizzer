Hakea argyrophylla

Hakea argyrophylla, commonly known as silver-leaf hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces distinctive cylindrical or club-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Hakea argyrophylla is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
