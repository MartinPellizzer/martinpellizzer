Hibiscus reflexus

Hibiscus reflexus, commonly known as the reflexed hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetlands and swampy areas. The plant features showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture.

Hibiscus reflexus is notable for its ability to thrive in moist, acidic soils and its role in supporting local wildlife.
