Habranthus jujuyensis

Habranthus jujuyensis, commonly known as the Jujuy bellflower, is a striking plant native to the Andes mountains of Argentina.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers. The plant belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.
