Helianthus atrorubens var. normalis

Helianthus atrorubens var. normalis, commonly known as the redstem sunflower, is a species native to the central and southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow flowers with dark red or purplish ray florets. The plant typically reaches heights of 1 to 3 meters and features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The redstem sunflower is often found in dry, open habitats like prairies and roadsides.
