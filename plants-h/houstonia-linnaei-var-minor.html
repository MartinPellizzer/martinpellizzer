Houstonia linnaei var. minor

Houstonia linnaei var. minor, commonly known as the lesser blue-eyed grass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, grass-like stems and small, showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is closely related to other species in the genus Houstonia, sharing similar ecological and morphological traits.
