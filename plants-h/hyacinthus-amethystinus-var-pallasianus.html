Hyacinthus amethystinus var. pallasianus

Hyacinthus amethystinus var. pallasianus, commonly known as the Pallasian hyacinth, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features striking clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, round bulbs that multiply over time. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its elegant appearance and long-lasting blooms.
