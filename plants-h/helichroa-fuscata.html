Helichroa fuscata

Helichroa fuscata, commonly known as the brown helichrysum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers that are often arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are narrow, leathery, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

Helichroa fuscata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
