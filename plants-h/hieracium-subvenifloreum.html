Hieracium subvenifloreum

Hieracium subvenifloreum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy stems, which are typical features of the genus Hieracium. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring temperate climates. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It plays a role in supporting pollinators and is often found in open habitats across Europe and parts of Asia.
