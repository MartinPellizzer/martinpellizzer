Hoffmannia triosteoides subsp. triosteoides

Hoffmannia triosteoides subsp.

triosteoides, commonly known as the Cape goldcup, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers.

The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal gardens. Its foliage is evergreen, with narrow, grass-like leaves that contribute to its ornamental appeal.

The species is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
