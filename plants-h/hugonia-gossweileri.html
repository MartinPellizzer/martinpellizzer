Hugonia gossweileri

Hugonia gossweileri, commonly known as the African waterwheel, is a rare and striking aquatic plant native to southern Africa.

It features whorled leaves that resemble the spokes of a wheel, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shallow, still waters such as ponds and marshes. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in dense clusters at the base of the stems.

Hugonia gossweileri is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in water gardens.
