Heteropappus altaicus var. hirsutus

Heteropappus altaicus var. hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy leaf horseweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and feathery pappus. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its hairy leaves and yellow flowers make it easily identifiable in its native habitat.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock in certain regions.
