Hypechusa sericocarpa

Hypechusa sericocarpa, commonly known as the silky-fruited honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, followed by small, berry-like fruits that are covered in a silky, hairy coating. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
