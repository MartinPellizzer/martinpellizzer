Hippocrepis unisiliquosa subsp. unisiliquosa

Hippocrepis unisiliquosa subsp.

unisiliquosa, commonly known as the single-seed lady's mantle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It features basal leaves that are broadly ovate to rhombic in shape, with a hairy texture.

The plant produces umbellate inflorescences of small, white to pale yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are follicles that mature into showy, persistent seed pods.

It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.
