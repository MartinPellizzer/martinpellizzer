Hosta plantaginea f. aphrodite

Hosta plantaginea f. aphrodite, commonly known as the Aphrodite Hosta, is a varietal form of the plantaginea species, characterized by its striking, broad, heart-shaped leaves.

The foliage is typically a deep, rich green with a waxy texture, and it often exhibits a subtle variegation pattern. This plant is native to eastern Asia and thrives in shaded, moist environments. It produces small, fragrant flowers in late summer, though the foliage is the main ornamental feature.

The Aphrodite Hosta is valued for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to garden settings.
