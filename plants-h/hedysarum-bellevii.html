Hedysarum bellevii

Hedysarum bellevii, commonly known as the sweet clover, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural settings. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along roadsides.

Hedysarum bellevii is also cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive blooms.
