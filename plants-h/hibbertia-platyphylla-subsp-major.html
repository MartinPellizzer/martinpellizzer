Hibbertia platyphylla subsp. major

Hibbertia platyphylla subsp.

major, commonly known as the broad-leaved hibbertia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia. It is a shrub characterized by its woody stems and opposite, broad, ovate leaves.

The plant produces bright yellow, tubular flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences at the ends of branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry inland regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
