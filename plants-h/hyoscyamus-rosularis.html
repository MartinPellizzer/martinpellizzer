Hyoscyamus rosularis

Hyoscyamus rosularis, commonly known as the rose-leaved henbane, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, kidney-shaped seeds that are often dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Due to its toxic properties, it is considered a hazardous plant to handle without proper protection.
