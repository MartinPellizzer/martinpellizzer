Hedysarum viridiflorum

Hedysarum viridiflorum, commonly known as green-flowered sainfoin, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and steppe environments.

Hedysarum viridiflorum is valued for its forage qualities and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
