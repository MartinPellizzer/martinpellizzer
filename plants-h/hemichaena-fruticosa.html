Hemichaena fruticosa

Hemichaena fruticosa, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow-orange tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are adapted for dispersal by animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to native plant gardens.
