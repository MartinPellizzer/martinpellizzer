Helleborine nephrocardia

Helleborine nephrocardia, commonly known as the kidney orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark green, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Helleborine nephrocardia is known for its ability to grow in nutrient-poor soils and is often found in temperate regions of Europe and Asia.
