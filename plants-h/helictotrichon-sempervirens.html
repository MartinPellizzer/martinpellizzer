Helictotrichon sempervirens

Helictotrichon sempervirens, commonly known as blue grama, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green blades and compact growth habit, making it well-suited for drought-prone environments. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and grasslands.

Its dense growth helps prevent soil erosion and supports a variety of wildlife.
