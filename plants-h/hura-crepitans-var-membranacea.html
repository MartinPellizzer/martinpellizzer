Hura crepitans var. membranacea

Hura crepitans var. membranacea, commonly known as the tropical silk tree, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, yellowish-white flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces distinctive, woody fruits that split open to release silky, fibrous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
