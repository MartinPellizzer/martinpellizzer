Hieracium tauschii subsp. psammotrophum

Hieracium tauschii subsp.

psammotrophum, commonly known as the sand-loving hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, thriving in arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in early summer. It is characterized by its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.

Its morphology and ecological preferences make it an important species in its native habitat.
