Huernia ingeae

Huernia ingeae, commonly known as the Zulu moon cactus, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water. The plant produces striking, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the warmer months. Its leaves are often reduced to small, spiny structures, and it thrives in well-draining soil with bright, indirect sunlight.

Huernia ingeae is popular among succulent enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
