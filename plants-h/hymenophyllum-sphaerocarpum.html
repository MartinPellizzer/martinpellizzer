Hymenophyllum sphaerocarpum

Hymenophyllum sphaerocarpum, commonly known as the globular earthmoss, is a small, epiphytic fern that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, hair-like structures. The plant produces small, spherical sporocarps that are typically greenish-brown in color. Its reproductive structures are concealed within the leaf axils, and it reproduces through spores rather than seeds.

Hymenophyllum sphaerocarpum is often found growing on tree trunks or rocks in tropical and subtropical regions.
