Hemizonia corymbosa subsp. macrocephala

Hemizonia corymbosa subsp.

macrocephala, commonly known as the large-headed hemizonia, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers.

The plant produces distinctive, elongated seed pods that split open to release its seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
