Huperzia schlechtendalii

Huperzia schlechtendalii, commonly known as the Schlechtendal's huperzia, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, scale-like leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern. The plant produces sporangia on specialized structures called synangia, which are clustered at the base of the leaves. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including countries like China, India, and Thailand.

Huperzia schlechtendalii is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices.
