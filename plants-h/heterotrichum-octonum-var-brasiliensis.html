Heterotrichum octonum var. brasiliensis

Heterotrichum octonum var. brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian feather moss, is a variant of the heterotrichum genus within the family Dicranaceae.

It is a small, cryptogamic plant that forms dense, cushion-like mats on forest floors, typically in moist, shaded environments. The plant exhibits a distinctive morphology with elongated, branching stems and dense, overlapping leaves that are often covered in a thick layer of moss. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in areas with high humidity and consistent moisture.

This moss plays an important ecological role in soil stabilization and nutrient cycling in its native habitats.
