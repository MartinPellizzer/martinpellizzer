Hieracium pictum subsp. inclinatiforme

Hieracium pictum subsp.

inclinatiforme, commonly known as the leaning form of the painted hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with a red center.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are upright and often slightly bent. This subspecies is known for its unique growth form, with stems that curve downward, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in meadows and grasslands, often in sunny, well-drained soils.
