Helichrysum tysonii

Helichrysum tysonii, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that retain their color even after drying. The plant produces small, tubular florets surrounded by ray florets, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Helichrysum tysonii is also valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in herbal remedies.
