Hieracium cernuiforme subsp. amoeniceps

Hieracium cernuiforme subsp.

amoeniceps, commonly known as the nodding hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow-orange flower heads that resemble dandelions.

The subspecies is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped with hairy surfaces, and the plant produces numerous small, bristly seeds.

It is considered a perennial herb that thrives in temperate climates.
