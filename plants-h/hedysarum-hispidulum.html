Hedysarum hispidulum

Hedysarum hispidulum, commonly known as the hairy vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of western Asia.

It is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable cover crop in agricultural settings. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often used for erosion control due to its extensive root system.

Hedysarum hispidulum is also sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and blooms.
