Hexacadica corymbosa

Hexacadica corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of blue and violet. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Hexacadica corymbosa is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and delicate floral display.
