Hieracium sericophyllum subsp. leucosphyrum

Hieracium sericophyllum subsp.

leucosphyrum, commonly known as the white-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish to white flowers arranged in a single head, which are typically surrounded by leaf-like bracts. It thrives in moist, disturbed habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and protected species in some regions.
