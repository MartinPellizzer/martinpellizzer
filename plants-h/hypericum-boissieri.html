Hypericum boissieri

Hypericum boissieri, commonly known as the Boissier's St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub with woody stems and small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Hypericum boissieri is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
