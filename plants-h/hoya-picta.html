Hoya picta

Hoya picta, commonly known as the variegated wax plant, is a tropical species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with shades of white, green, and pink. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink. Hoya picta is a trailing plant that thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.

It is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
