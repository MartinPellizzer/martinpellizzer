Huanaca morenonis

Huanaca morenonis, commonly known as the Andean moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its large, white, night-blooming flowers. The plant is typically found in rocky, well-drained soils at elevations between 3,000 and 5,000 meters. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is adapted to the cool, dry conditions of its native habitat and is often used in traditional Andean medicine.
