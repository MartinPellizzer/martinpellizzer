Hippophae gyantsensis subsp. linearifolia

Hippophae gyantsensis subsp.

linearifolia, commonly known as the Tibetan sandthorn, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of the Tibetan Plateau. It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the summer.

The plant produces small, orange berries that are rich in vitamin C and other nutrients. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky or sandy soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and its potential for use in traditional medicine.
