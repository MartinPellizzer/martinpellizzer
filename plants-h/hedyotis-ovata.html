Hedyotis ovata

Hedyotis ovata, commonly known as the small yellow toadflax, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, yellow, four-petaled flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Hedyotis ovata is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
