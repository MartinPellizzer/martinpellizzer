Hibiscus tiliaceus f. immaculatus

Hibiscus tiliaceus f. immaculatus, commonly known as the immaculate hibiscus, is a variant of the common hibiscus native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive white or pale pink flowers, which contrast with its broad, waxy leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, often found near coastal areas and in humid environments. Its leaves are usually smooth and have a glossy appearance, while the stems are sturdy and somewhat woody.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and horticultural settings.
