Hieracium laevigatum subsp. trichocalyx

Hieracium laevigatum subsp.

trichocalyx, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 50 cm.

The plant features basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in a single terminal head. Its inflorescence consists of ray florets surrounded by a dense involucre of bracts.

The subspecies is characterized by its distinctive hairy leaves and stems, as well as its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
