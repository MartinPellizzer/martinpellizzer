Hydrocotyle galapagensis

Hydrocotyle galapagensis, commonly known as the Galápagos spurge, is a flowering plant native to the Galápagos Islands.

It is a herbaceous perennial with a rosette of heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine white hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, typically blooming from late winter to early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed habitats.

Hydrocotyle galapagensis plays a role in the local ecosystem by providing food and shelter for various insects and small animals.
