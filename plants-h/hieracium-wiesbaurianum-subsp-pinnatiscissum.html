Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp. pinnatiscissum

Hieracium wiesbaurianum subsp.

pinnatiscissum, commonly known as the pinnate-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant features a basal rosette of leaves that are pinnately divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically visited by various pollinators.

This subspecies is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
