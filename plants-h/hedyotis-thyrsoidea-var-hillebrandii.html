Hedyotis thyrsoidea var. hillebrandii

Hedyotis thyrsoidea var. hillebrandii, commonly known as the Hillebrand's bedstraw, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features slender, wiry stems and small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
