Hopea sarawakensis

Hopea sarawakensis, commonly known as the Sarawak hopea, is a species of flowering plant in the family Dipterocarpaceae.

It is native to the tropical rainforests of Borneo, particularly in Sarawak, Malaysia. This tree can grow up to 40 meters tall and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elongated, elliptical leaves. The flowers are small and yellowish-green, arranged in catkin-like inflorescences, and the fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

Hopea sarawakensis plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
