Hippeastrum gracile

Hippeastrum gracile, commonly known as the slender amaryllis, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, strap-like leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of bright red or white flowers during the warm season. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to bright light for optimal growth.

Hippeastrum gracile is often cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its striking floral display.
