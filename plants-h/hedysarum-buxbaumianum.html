Hedysarum buxbaumianum

Hedysarum buxbaumianum, commonly known as the Buxbaum's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a robust root system that helps it thrive in arid and rocky soils. It is often found in alpine and steppe habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.

Hedysarum buxbaumianum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
