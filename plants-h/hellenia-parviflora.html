Hellenia parviflora

Hellenia parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered helene, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, producing pale yellow to white blooms.

Hellenia parviflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a drought-tolerant plant.
