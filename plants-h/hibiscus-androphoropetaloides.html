Hibiscus androphoropetaloides

Hibiscus androphoropetaloides, commonly known as the African hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy, pentagonal flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces capsules as its fruit.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
