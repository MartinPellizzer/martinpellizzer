Herorchis picta var. albanica

Herorchis picta var. albanica, commonly known as the Albanian painted orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the Balkan region.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful flowers that display intricate patterns, often in shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas, preferring well-drained soils and partial shade. Its flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern in its native range.
