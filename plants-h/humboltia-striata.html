Humboltia striata

Humboltia striata, commonly known as the California buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to coastal regions of California and Baja California.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters. The plant is characterized by its smooth, reddish bark and bright red, fleshy berries that ripen in the fall. Humboltia striata thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal sage scrub habitats.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and stabilizing soil in coastal areas.
