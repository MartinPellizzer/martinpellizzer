Hordeum × intermedium

Hordeum × intermedium, commonly known as intermediate barley, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between barley (Hordeum vulgare) and wild barley (Hordeum spontaneum).

It is characterized by its intermediate morphology, combining traits from both parent species, such as growth habit and grain structure. This plant is typically found in temperate regions and thrives in a variety of soil types, showing some degree of adaptability. Its botanical features include slender stalks, spikelet clusters, and seeds that range in color from pale yellow to brown.

Hordeum × intermedium is of interest to researchers due to its potential for genetic diversity and its role in agricultural breeding programs.
