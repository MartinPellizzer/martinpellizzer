Helianthus kentuckiensis

Helianthus kentuckiensis, commonly known as the Kentucky sunflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the central and eastern United States.

It features a straight, hairy stem that can grow up to 6 feet tall, supporting large, yellow-orange composite flowers with prominent brown central disks. The plant has lance-shaped, rough, and hairy leaves that are alternate along the stem. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in open woods, prairies, and along roadsides.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
