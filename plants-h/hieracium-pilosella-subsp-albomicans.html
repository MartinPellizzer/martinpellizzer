Hieracium pilosella subsp. albomicans

Hieracium pilosella subsp.

albomicans, commonly known as the white-hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its hairy, pale green stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in meadows and grassy areas.

This subspecies is often found in disturbed soils and is considered an invasive species in some regions.
