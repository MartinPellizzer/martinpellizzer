Hieracium coracolaenum

Hieracium coracolaenum, commonly known as the black-leaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, pastures, and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a dark green to almost black coloration, which gives it its common name.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
