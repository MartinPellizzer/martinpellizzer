Hieracium atratum subsp. pereffusum

Hieracium atratum subsp.

pereffusum, commonly known as the black hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its dark green, hairy leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It has a rosette of basal leaves and produces numerous flowering stems that bear small, clustered flowers.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
