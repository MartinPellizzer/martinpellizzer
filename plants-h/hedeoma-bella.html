Hedeoma bella

Hedeoma bella, commonly known as the silverleaf mint, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Hedeoma bella is valued for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
