Hovea planifolia

Hovea planifolia, commonly known as the flat-leaf hovea, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

Hovea planifolia is adapted to a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions.
