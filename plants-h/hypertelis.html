Hypertelis

Hypertelis, commonly known as the false dandelion, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It consists of several species, primarily found in South America, particularly in Brazil and Paraguay. The plants are herbaceous and often have hairy stems and leaves. Their flowers are typically yellow and arranged in a composite head, similar to other members of the daisy family.

Hypertelis species are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
