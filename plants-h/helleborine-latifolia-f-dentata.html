Helleborine latifolia f. dentata

Helleborine latifolia f. dentata, commonly known as the broad-leaved helleborine with toothed leaves, is a hybrid orchid species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that often have distinct toothed margins, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces clusters of greenish-yellow to brownish flowers, which are typically borne on tall, erect stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and forest floors.

This orchid is known for its ability to hybridize with other Helleborine species, contributing to its genetic diversity.
