Hymenocallis expansa

Hymenocallis expansa, commonly known as the broadleaf spider lily, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pale pink flowers that resemble spider-like blooms, which are typically borne on sturdy, upright stems. The plant features broad, strap-like leaves that are often arranged in a fan shape at the base. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Hymenocallis expansa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in floral arrangements.
