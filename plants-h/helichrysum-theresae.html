Helichrysum theresae

Helichrysum theresae, commonly known as the Cape gold or golden everlasting, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that resemble golden stars. The plant features silver-gray foliage with finely toothed leaves, which contribute to its ornamental appeal. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their long-lasting nature and vibrant color.
