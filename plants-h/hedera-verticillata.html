Hedera verticillata

Hedera verticillata, commonly known as the ivy creeper, is a climbing plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are arranged in a whorl at the ends of its stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in shaded environments and is often found growing on walls, trees, and rocky surfaces.

Hedera verticillata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to cover vertical spaces effectively.
