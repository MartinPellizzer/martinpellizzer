Haemocharis subintegerrima

Haemocharis subintegerrima, commonly known as the golden torch ginger, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow bracts that surround small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are large, lance-shaped, and often have a reddish or purple tint along the veins.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
