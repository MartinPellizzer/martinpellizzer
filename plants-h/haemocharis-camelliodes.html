Haemocharis camelliodes

Haemocharis camelliodes, commonly known as the golden flame lily, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that resemble small sunbursts. The plant has strap-like, dark green leaves that are often variegated with white or cream markings. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Haemocharis camelliodes is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
