Hypericum morarense

Hypericum morarense, commonly known as the Moravian St. John's Wort, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are opposite, simple, and elliptical in shape. It is often found in open woodlands, meadows, and along roadsides.

The plant contains flavonoids and hypericin, which have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.
