Hymenophyllum asplenioides

Hymenophyllum asplenioides, commonly known as the silver fern, is a small, delicate fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-green fronds that are often covered in a thin, waxy layer, which helps reduce water loss. This species is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as rock crevices and forest floors, where it thrives in high humidity. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and are protected by a unique, kidney-shaped indusium.

Hymenophyllum asplenioides is a prized epiphytic or lithophytic plant among fern enthusiasts due to its ornamental appearance and ecological significance.
