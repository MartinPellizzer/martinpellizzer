Hibiscus molochinus

Hibiscus molochinus, commonly known as the African hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically have five petals and a prominent central pistil. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, hard seeds. Hibiscus molochinus thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

It is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
