Hibiscus lambertianus var. glabrus

Hibiscus lambertianus var. glabrus, commonly known as the smooth hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink or red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy but can become smooth in certain conditions. It thrives in wet, swampy areas and is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks.

The flowers bloom in summer and are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.
