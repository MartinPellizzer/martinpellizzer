Hippocratea excelsa

Hippocratea excelsa, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Hippocratea excelsa is noted for its unique morphology and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
