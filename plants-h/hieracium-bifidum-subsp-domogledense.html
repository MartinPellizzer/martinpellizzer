Hieracium bifidum subsp. domogledense

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

domogledense, commonly known as the Domogleden hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Carpathian region. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering heads.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species in its native habitat.
