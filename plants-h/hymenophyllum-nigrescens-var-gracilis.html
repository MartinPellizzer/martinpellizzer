Hymenophyllum nigrescens var. gracilis

Hymenophyllum nigrescens var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender black moss, is a rare and delicate fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and often turn blackish when dry. This species thrives in moist, shaded environments, such as rocky crevices or forest floors, where it can maintain the high humidity it requires. Its small, inconspicuous sporangia are borne on the underside of the leaves, a common feature in many ferns.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
