Helianthemum croceum f. nudicaule

Helianthemum croceum f. nudicaule, commonly known as the naked-stemmed rockrose, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features woody stems that are often leafless, giving it its characteristic "nudicaule" appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically small, scale-like, and arranged in a dense, compact manner.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland areas with mild climates.
