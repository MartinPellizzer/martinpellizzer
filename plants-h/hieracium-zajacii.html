Hieracium zajacii

Hieracium zajacii, commonly known as Zajac's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed sites, reaching heights of up to 30 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

Hieracium zajacii is often found in calcareous soils and is considered a characteristic species of certain habitats.
