Hieracium ecrinense

Hieracium ecrinense, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and flowering stems that bear yellow disk flowers surrounded by ray florets. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, preferring well-drained soils. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ability to thrive in cold, high-altitude environments.

Hieracium ecrinense is often found in areas with limited nutrient availability, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
