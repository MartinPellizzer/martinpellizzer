Hieracium × johnsenii

Hieracium × johnsenii, commonly known as the Johnsen's hawkweed, is a hybrid plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically has hairy stems and leaves that are lance-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and other open habitats.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a range of morphological variations, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
