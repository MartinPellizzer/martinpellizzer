Hieracium subinquilinum

Hieracium subinquilinum, commonly known as the sand dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and other composite flowers. The plant is characterized by its upright stems and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in sandy or disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats such as coastal dunes.

Its foliage is typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
