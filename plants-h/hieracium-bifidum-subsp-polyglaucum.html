Hieracium bifidum subsp. polyglaucum

Hieracium bifidum subsp.

polyglaucum, commonly known as the greenish hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

The plant features upright stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish pubescence. Its flowers are yellow, with ray florets and a central disk, and it produces small, oval-shaped seeds.

It is characterized by its distinctive greenish-blue coloration on the underside of its leaves, which gives it its common name.
