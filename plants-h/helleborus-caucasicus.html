Helleborus caucasicus

Helleborus caucasicus, commonly known as the Caucasian hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features dark green, leathery leaves that are typically heart-shaped or ovate in outline. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in late winter or early spring. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Helleborus caucasicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
