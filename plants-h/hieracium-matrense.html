Hieracium matrense

Hieracium matrense, commonly known as Matrena's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to central and eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a hairy texture.

Hieracium matrense is often found in habitats with partial shade and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.
