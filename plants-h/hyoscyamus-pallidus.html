Hyoscyamus pallidus

Hyoscyamus pallidus, commonly known as the pale henbane, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, globose fruits that contain toxic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Due to its toxic properties, it is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
