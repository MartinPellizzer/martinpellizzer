Hypericum maculatum subsp. desetangsiiforme

Hypericum maculatum subsp.

desetangsiiforme, commonly known as the spotted St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive red-stemmed growth and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant features opposite, simple leaves that are often covered in small glands, giving them a slightly sticky texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a suitable candidate for wetland restoration projects.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various pollinators.
