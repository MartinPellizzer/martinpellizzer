Hieracium obversum

Hieracium obversum, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellow to orange composite flowers and is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are broadly lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Hieracium obversum is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in many regions.
