Hieracium sulphureum subsp. violascentipes

Hieracium sulphureum subsp.

violascentipes, commonly known as the sulphur hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It features bright yellow flower heads that are typically composed of numerous small, daisy-like florets.

The plant has a basal rosette of leaves that are hairy and serrated, with a rough texture. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Its flowering period occurs from late spring to early summer, contributing to its ecological role in pollinator support.
