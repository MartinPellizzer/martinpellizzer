Hagioseris

Hagioseris, commonly known as the sacred thistle, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

These plants are characterized by their spiny leaves and showy composite flowers, which consist of both ray and disk florets. Native to the Mediterranean region, Hagioseris species often thrive in rocky or arid environments. They are perennial herbs that typically grow to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters.

The genus includes several species, such as Hagioseris scabiosa and Hagioseris sphaerocephala, which are valued for their ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
