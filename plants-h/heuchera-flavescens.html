Heuchera flavescens

Heuchera flavescens, commonly known as the golden alumroot, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom in early spring. Its foliage tends to change color throughout the seasons, ranging from green to red and purple.

Heuchera flavescens thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
