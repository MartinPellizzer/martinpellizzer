Hieracium carpathicum subsp. percnophyllum

Hieracium carpathicum subsp.

percnophyllum, commonly known as the Carpathian hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Carpathian Mountains. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette-forming basal leaves and yellow flowers arranged in a composite head.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and forest edges, preferring well-drained soils. It has hairy stems and leaves that are often glandular, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its range.
