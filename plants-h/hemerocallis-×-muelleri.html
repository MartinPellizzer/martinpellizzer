Hemerocallis × muelleri

Hemerocallis × muelleri, commonly known as the yellow daylily, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Hemerocallidaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange. The plant is a perennial herb with sword-shaped, grass-like leaves that form a dense clump. It produces numerous blooms over a short period, usually in summer, and is known for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil conditions.

Hemerocallis × muelleri is widely cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in full sun.
