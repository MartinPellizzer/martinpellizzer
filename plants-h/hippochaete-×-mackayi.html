Hippochaete × mackayi

Hippochaete × mackayi, commonly known as the Mackay's hibiscus, is a hybrid plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to deep pink. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and grows as an upright shrub or small tree. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
