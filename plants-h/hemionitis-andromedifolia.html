Hemionitis andromedifolia

Hemionitis andromedifolia, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-tomentose leaves that give it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Hemionitis andromedifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
