Hakea bathurstensis

Hakea bathurstensis, commonly known as the Bathurst hakea, is a shrub native to the south-western region of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces striking, cylindrical flower spikes that range in color from yellow to red, depending on the species. It is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

Hakea bathurstensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
