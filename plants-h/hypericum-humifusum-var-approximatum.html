Hypericum humifusum var. approximatum

Hypericum humifusum var. approximatum, commonly known as the creeping St. John's wort, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to North America.

It features prostrate or ascending stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, oval, and slightly hairy, with a reddish underside.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in woodland and rocky habitats.
