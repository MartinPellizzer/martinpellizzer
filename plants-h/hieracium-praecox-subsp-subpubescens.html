Hieracium praecox subsp. subpubescens

Hieracium praecox subsp.

subpubescens, commonly known as the early hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers arranged in a single, showy head.

The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance.

It is known for its early flowering season, often blooming in late spring to early summer.
