Hieracium graniticum subsp. pseudocyaneum

Hieracium graniticum subsp.

pseudocyaneum, commonly known as the granitic hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and southern Europe. It thrives in rocky and calcareous soils, often found in alpine and subalpine regions.

The plant produces yellow flowers with a distinctive ray floret structure, typically blooming from June to August. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
