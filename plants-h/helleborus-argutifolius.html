Helleborus argutifolius

Helleborus argutifolius, commonly known as the hollyhock hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features dark green, palmately lobed leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of pale green to white flowers with prominent, showy spathes, which bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

H. argutifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
