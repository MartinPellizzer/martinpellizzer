Hieracium gracile subvar. densifloccum

Hieracium gracile subvar. densifloccum, commonly known as the dense floccose hawkweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom from June to August. The leaves are typically basal, linear, and covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.

This subspecies is distinguished by its dense, fluffy flower heads and is considered a member of the dandelion family, Asteraceae.
