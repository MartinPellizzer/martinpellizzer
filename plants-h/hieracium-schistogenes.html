Hieracium schistogenes

Hieracium schistogenes, commonly known as the rock hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, thriving in well-drained, sunny environments. Its leaves are generally rough and hairy, with a distinctive silvery or greyish underside.

The flowers are yellow, appearing in late summer to early autumn, and are followed by small, elongated achenes that are dispersed by wind.
