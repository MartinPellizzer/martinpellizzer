Hieracium griseum

Hieracium griseum, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its yellow flowering heads, which are composed of numerous small florets arranged in a composite structure. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are upright and branching.

Hieracium griseum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bright, daisy-like appearance.
