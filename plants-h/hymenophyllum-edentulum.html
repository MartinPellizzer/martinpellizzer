Hymenophyllum edentulum

Hymenophyllum edentulum, commonly known as the toothless fern, is a small, delicate epiphytic fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, hair-like structures. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found on tree trunks or rocks in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the leaves and are protected by a specialized covering.

Hymenophyllum edentulum is known for its unique morphology and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
