Hieracium glaucinum subsp. pseudobounophilum

Hieracium glaucinum subsp.

pseudobounophilum, commonly known as the glaucous hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in late spring to early summer. Its inflorescence consists of a single, showy flower head with both ray and disk florets.

The species is characterized by its glaucous (bluish-gray) leaf surfaces and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
