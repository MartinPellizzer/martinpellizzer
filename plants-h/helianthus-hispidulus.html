Helianthus hispidulus

Helianthus hispidulus, commonly known as the roughleaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other sunflower and daisy species.

Helianthus hispidulus is often used in native plant gardens due to its ecological value and ornamental appeal.
