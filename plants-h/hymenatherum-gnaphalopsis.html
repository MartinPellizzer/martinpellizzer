Hymenatherum gnaphalopsis

Hymenatherum gnaphalopsis, commonly known as the silver leaf tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Hymenatherum gnaphalopsis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
