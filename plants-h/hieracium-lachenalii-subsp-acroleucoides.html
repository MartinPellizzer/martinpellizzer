Hieracium lachenalii subsp. acroleucoides

Hieracium lachenalii subsp.

acroleucoides, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellowish to orange-yellow flowering heads.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while the stems are upright and often hairy.

This subspecies is distinguished by its smaller, more compact flower heads compared to other members of the Hieracium genus.
