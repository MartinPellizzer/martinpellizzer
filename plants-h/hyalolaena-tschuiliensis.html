Hyalolaena tschuiliensis

Hyalolaena tschuiliensis, commonly known as the KwaZulu-Natal hyalolaena, is a rare and endemic grass species found primarily in the subtropical regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and forest margins. Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species.

Its unique morphology and restricted range make it an important subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
