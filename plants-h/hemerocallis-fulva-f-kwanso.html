Hemerocallis fulva f. kwanso

Hemerocallis fulva f. kwanso, commonly known as the Kwanso daylily, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to China.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, orange-red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is notable for its vigorous growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

The foliage tends to yellow and die back in winter, making it a hardy plant suitable for temperate climates.
