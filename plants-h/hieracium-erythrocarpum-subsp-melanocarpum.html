Hieracium erythrocarpum subsp. melanocarpum

Hieracium erythrocarpum subsp.

melanocarpum, commonly known as the black-fruited hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its most distinctive feature is its dark, almost black, achene fruits, which give it its common name.

It is often found in open habitats and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.
