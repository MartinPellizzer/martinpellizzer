Hamamelis japonica subsp. obtusata

Hamamelis japonica subsp.

obtusata, commonly known as the Japanese witch hazel, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan. It is characterized by its small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late autumn or early winter.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and oval-shaped with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

This species is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique flowering period and hardy nature.
