Hibbertia glomerata subsp. wandoo

Hibbertia glomerata subsp.

wandoo, commonly known as wandoo, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that appear in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is well adapted to dry conditions and is often found in coastal and sandy environments. Hibbertia glomerata subsp.

wandoo is a popular ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and hardiness.
