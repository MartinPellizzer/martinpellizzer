Habenaria hookeri var. oblongifolia

Habenaria hookeri var. oblongifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in wet, shaded environments such as marshes and swamps. The plant features slender, erect stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often broader than those of the typical species. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellow, with three sepals and three petals, and are arranged in a spike.

This variety is noted for its adaptability to moist conditions and its role in local ecosystems.
