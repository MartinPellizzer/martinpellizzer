Hieracium alatum subsp. benthamianum

Hieracium alatum subsp.

benthamianum, commonly known as the bentham's hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy yellow composite flowers that attract pollinators.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which are typically lance-shaped and serrated. It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

This subspecies is native to southern Europe and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
