Hibiscus abutiloides

Hibiscus abutiloides, commonly known as the velvetleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom throughout the growing season. Hibiscus abutiloides is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage and vibrant blooms.

It prefers full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it adaptable to a variety of garden environments.
