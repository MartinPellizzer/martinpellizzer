Hedysarum inaequale

Hedysarum inaequale, commonly known as the unequal vetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets, and the plant has a climbing habit, often using its tendrils to support itself.

Hedysarum inaequale is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
