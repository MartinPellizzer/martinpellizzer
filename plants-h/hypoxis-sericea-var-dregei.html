Hypoxis sericea var. dregei

Hypoxis sericea var. dregei, commonly known as the silverleaf false shamrock, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, silver-velvet leaves that reflect light, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in a loose cluster, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a popular choice among gardeners in arid regions.
