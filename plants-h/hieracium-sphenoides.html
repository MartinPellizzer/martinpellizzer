Hieracium sphenoides

Hieracium sphenoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and often have a waxy coating, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive, flat disk.

Hieracium sphenoides is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in various habitats.
