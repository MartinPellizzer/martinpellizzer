Hieracium incisum subsp. melanocinereum

Hieracium incisum subsp.

melanocinereum, commonly known as the black-centred hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leaves with serrated edges.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dark purple or black central disc in the flower head, contrasting with the yellow ray florets. It typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded conditions.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves, and it produces achenes as its dry, seed-like fruits.
