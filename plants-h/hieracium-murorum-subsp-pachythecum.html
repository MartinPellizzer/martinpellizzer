Hieracium murorum subsp. pachythecum

Hieracium murorum subsp.

pachythecum, commonly known as the meadow hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in late summer.

The subspecies is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This plant plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
