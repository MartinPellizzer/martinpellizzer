Harpagophytum zeyheri subsp. schijffii

Harpagophytum zeyheri subsp.

schijffii, commonly known as the Namibian devil's claw, is a member of the Fabaceae family. It is native to southern Africa, particularly Namibia and South Africa, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and spiny, claw-like fruits that enclose its seeds. It has adapted to survive in harsh conditions with deep root systems and reduced leaf surfaces.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is also valued for its potential health benefits.
