Hygrophila pinnatifida

Hygrophila pinnatifida, commonly known as water wisteria, is a semi-aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely serrated, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in a whorled pattern along its stems. The plant thrives in shallow freshwater environments, such as ponds and slow-moving streams, where it can receive ample sunlight. Its rapid growth rate makes it a popular choice for aquatic gardening and pond decoration.

Hygrophila pinnatifida is also known for its ability to provide habitat and shelter for small aquatic organisms.
