Hieracium cordifolium subsp. vernicosum

Hieracium cordifolium subsp.

vernicosum, commonly known as the heart-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. It has a hairy stem and is often found in open habitats with moderate light exposure.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and botanical gardens.
