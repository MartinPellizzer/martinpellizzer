Hieracium erosulum

Hieracium erosulum, commonly known as the lesser hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and its clusters of small, yellowish to reddish flower heads. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as meadows and roadsides. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, and it is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.

Hieracium erosulum is considered an invasive species in some regions due to its competitive nature and adaptability.
