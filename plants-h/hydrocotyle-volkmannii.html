Hydrocotyle volkmannii

Hydrocotyle volkmannii, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features a rosette of smooth, waxy leaves that are typically green with a slight bluish tinge. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in umbels, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Hydrocotyle volkmannii is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
