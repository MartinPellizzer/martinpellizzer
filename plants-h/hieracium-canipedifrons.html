Hieracium canipedifrons

Hieracium canipedifrons, commonly known as the broad-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and its yellow flowers appear in clusters.

Hieracium canipedifrons is often found in open habitats and can spread aggressively through its rhizomes.
