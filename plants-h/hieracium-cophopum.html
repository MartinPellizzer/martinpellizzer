Hieracium cophopum

Hieracium cophopum, commonly known as the New Zealand buttercup, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Hieracium cophopum plays an important role in its native ecosystem, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various invertebrates.
