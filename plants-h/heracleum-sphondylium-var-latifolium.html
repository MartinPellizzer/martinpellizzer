Heracleum sphondylium var. latifolium

Heracleum sphondylium var. latifolium, commonly known as broadleaf cowparsley, is a biennial or short-lived perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, umbrella-shaped compound umbels that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. It grows up to 1.5 meters tall and prefers full sun to partial shade in well-drained soils.

This species is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides, waste areas, and along riverbanks.
