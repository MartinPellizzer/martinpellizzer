Hydrolea spinosa f. longifolia

Hydrolea spinosa f. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved hydrolea, is a unique variant of the Hydrolea spinosa species.

It is a shrub native to South Africa, characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its botanical features make it well-suited for rocky or sandy soils and full sun exposure.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
