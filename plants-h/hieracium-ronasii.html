Hieracium ronasii

Hieracium ronasii, commonly known as the Rosan's hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and alpine meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence.

Hieracium ronasii is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
