Helleborus abruzzicus

Helleborus abruzzicus, commonly known as the Abruzzi hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Apennine Mountains of central Italy.

It belongs to the family Ranunculaceae and is characterized by its dark green, waxy leaves and striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments, often found in woodland areas and rocky soils. Helleborus abruzzicus is also notable for its toxicity, containing cardiac glycosides that can be harmful if ingested.

Its unique botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.
