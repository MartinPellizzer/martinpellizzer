Hebe lycopodioides var. patula

Hebe lycopodioides var. patula, commonly known as the silver spruce hebe, is a perennial shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its silver-green, needle-like leaves that are densely packed along its stems. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of purple or pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in temperate climates.
