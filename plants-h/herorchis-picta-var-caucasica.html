Herorchis picta var. caucasica

Herorchis picta var. caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian painted orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful flowers that display intricate patterns, often in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or mountainous regions. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a waxy surface that helps retain moisture.

The orchid's unique floral structure and striking appearance make it a subject of interest for botanists and plant enthusiasts alike.
