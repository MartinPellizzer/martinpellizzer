Hieracium hirsutum subsp. chevallieri

Hieracium hirsutum subsp.

chevallieri, commonly known as the Chevallier's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually hairy and lance-shaped, while the flowers are yellow and clustered in a flat-topped inflorescence.

It is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
