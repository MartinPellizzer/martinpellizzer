Hieracium phlomoides subsp. hastile

Hieracium phlomoides subsp.

hastile, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance.

It is often found in temperate regions and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
