Haloschoenus tenellus

Haloschoenus tenellus, commonly known as the slender flat sedge, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland or swampy areas, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. It plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.

Haloschoenus tenellus is often found in the coastal regions of southern Australia.
