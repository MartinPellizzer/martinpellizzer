Hypericum scopulorum

Hypericum scopulorum, commonly known as the rock St. John's wort, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Hypericum scopulorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
