Hieracium longifolium subsp. wahlenbergianum

Hieracium longifolium subsp.

wahlenbergianum, commonly known as the Wahlenberg's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

This subspecies is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
