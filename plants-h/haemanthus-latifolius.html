Haemanthus latifolius

Haemanthus latifolius, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, upright stems that bear dense clusters of bright orange-red flowers, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green with a reddish tinge along the edges. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and unique foliage structure.
