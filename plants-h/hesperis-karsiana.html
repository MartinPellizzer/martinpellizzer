Hesperis karsiana

Hesperis karsiana, commonly known as the Karsian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants, and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its blooming period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it produces small, elongated seed pods that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Hesperis karsiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
