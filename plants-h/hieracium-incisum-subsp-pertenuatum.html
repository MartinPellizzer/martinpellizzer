Hieracium incisum subsp. pertenuatum

Hieracium incisum subsp.

pertenuatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are narrow and serrated, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

It is a low-growing plant, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.
