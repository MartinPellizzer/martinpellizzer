Hoya aurantiaca

Hoya aurantiaca, commonly known as the flame hoya, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces showy, star-shaped blooms in shades of red, orange, and pink, which are particularly striking when they appear in the spring. Hoya aurantiaca is a trailing plant that can be grown as a houseplant or in hanging baskets, and it thrives in bright, indirect light.

Its ability to produce aerial roots makes it suitable for climbing or cascading displays.
