Hoffmannia congesta

Hoffmannia congesta, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a low-growing shrub, with leaves that are broad and heart-shaped. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires indirect sunlight to flourish.

Hoffmannia congesta is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.
