Hyptis stachydifolia

Hyptis stachydifolia, commonly known as blue hyptis, is a flowering plant native to the Americas, particularly found in regions of Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Hyptis stachydifolia is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

Its aromatic leaves and flowers also make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
