Hieracium pallescens subsp. conferticeps

Hieracium pallescens subsp.

conferticeps, commonly known as the woolly hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green leaves and yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. It has a creeping growth habit with a central stem that branches near the base.

Its subspecies status reflects subtle differences in morphology, particularly in the structure of its leaves and flower heads.
