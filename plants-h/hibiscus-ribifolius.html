Hibiscus ribifolius

Hibiscus ribifolius, commonly known as the Florida hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in wetland habitats, such as swamps and marshes. The plant features heart-shaped, waxy leaves and produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, or white. Hibiscus ribifolius is known for its ability to thrive in humid, moist environments with partial to full sunlight.

It plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators and shelter for various wildlife species.
