Helichrysum tardentii

Helichrysum tardentii, commonly known as the Cape golden bush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, yellow-orange inflorescences that resemble small daisies. The plant is evergreen, with narrow, needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Helichrysum tardentii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
