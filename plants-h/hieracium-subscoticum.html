Hieracium subscoticum

Hieracium subscoticum, commonly known as the lesser spearthistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and wastelands. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a dense, spherical cluster.

Hieracium subscoticum is an annual or biennial plant, depending on the climate, and it plays a role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
