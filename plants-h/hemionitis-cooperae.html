Hemionitis cooperae

Hemionitis cooperae, commonly known as the desert shield fern, is a unique and rare fern species native to arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth form and its ability to thrive in extreme desert conditions. The plant features small, leathery, lance-shaped leaves that are adapted to minimize water loss. Hemionitis cooperae is notable for its ability to survive in low-nutrient soils and high temperatures.

Its botanical significance lies in its ecological role and its potential for studying plant adaptations to harsh environments.
